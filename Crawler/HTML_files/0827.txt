<p><b>Joanna Lauren Christie</b> (born 10 April 1982)<sup id="cite_ref-fresh_face_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fresh_face-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Actress" class="mw-redirect" title="Actress">actress</a> and <a href="/wiki/Singer" class="mw-redirect" title="Singer">singer</a>. She is noted for her work in the play <i><a href="/wiki/Equus_(play)" title="Equus (play)">Equus</a></i> (2007) alongside <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Radcliffe" title="Daniel Radcliffe">Daniel Radcliffe</a>, but is best known for her role as Girl in the Broadway musical <i><a href="/wiki/Once_(musical)" title="Once (musical)">Once</a></i>. Christie played Connie Murphy, one of the lead female characters, in the hit <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Narcos" title="Narcos">Narcos</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joanna_Christie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Christie was born and raised,<sup id="cite_ref-role_in_the_hay_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-role_in_the_hay-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> until the age of 13, when she was awarded a music scholarship to study flute, piano, and voice<sup id="cite_ref-huddersfield_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huddersfield-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  at <a href="/wiki/Oundle_School" title="Oundle School">Oundle School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire" title="Northamptonshire">Northamptonshire</a>. Five years later, she moved to <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> to attend <a href="/wiki/Drama_school" title="Drama school">drama school</a> at <a href="/wiki/Mountview_Academy_of_Theatre_Arts" title="Mountview Academy of Theatre Arts">Mountview Academy of Theatre Arts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-role_in_the_hay_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-role_in_the_hay-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She joined the <a href="/wiki/National_Youth_Theatre" title="National Youth Theatre">National Youth Theatre</a> at 17, where she made her breakthrough stage role in <i>Immaculate Conceit</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Play_(theatre)" title="Play (theatre)">play</a> about a <a href="/wiki/Stripper" title="Stripper">stripper</a> who is pregnant with the next <a href="/wiki/Messiah" title="Messiah">messiah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fresh_face_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fresh_face-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-how_we_met_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-how_we_met-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She then found small roles in television,<sup id="cite_ref-smalldarkplaces_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smalldarkplaces-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> before joining <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Spacey" title="Kevin Spacey">Kevin Spacey</a>'s <i>Old Vic, New Voices</i> <a href="/wiki/Collective" title="Collective">collective</a> for a year in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-how_we_met_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-how_we_met-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ovnv_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ovnv-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She was delighted to be part of the collective of actors taking part in the 24-hour plays at <a href="/wiki/The_Old_Vic" title="The Old Vic">The Old Vic</a>. The group received its name because Spacey, who selected the actors, believed them to be the best new actors around.
</p><p>After a five-month tour of <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> performing <a href="/wiki/Shakespeare" class="mw-redirect" title="Shakespeare">Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" title="A Midsummer Night&#39;s Dream">A Midsummer Night's Dream</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Jaipur_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Jaipur District">Jaipur</a> in 2006, she returned to Britain,<sup id="cite_ref-close_up_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-close_up-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> but endured a lean year and nearly gave up acting altogether when she landed the role of Jill Mason in her <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End</a> debut in <i><a href="/wiki/Equus_(play)" title="Equus (play)">Equus</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Gielgud_Theatre" title="Gielgud Theatre">Gielgud Theatre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-oh_harry_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oh_harry-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She beat over 300 other actresses to the role opposite <i><a href="/wiki/Harry_Potter" title="Harry Potter">Harry Potter</a></i> star <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Radcliffe" title="Daniel Radcliffe">Daniel Radcliffe</a> in <a href="/wiki/Thea_Sharrock" title="Thea Sharrock">Thea Sharrock</a>'s high-profile revival of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Shaffer" title="Peter Shaffer">Peter Shaffer</a>'s award-winning play.<sup id="cite_ref-role_in_the_hay_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-role_in_the_hay-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-smh_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smh-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joanna_Christie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Christie lives in New York and her hobbies include yoga, running, kickboxing, and scuba diving.<sup id="cite_ref-cue&amp;a_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cue&amp;a-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-anglophenia_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-anglophenia-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> She used to be part of her brother's band called <i>Luke J Christie &amp; The Held Breath</i> until she moved to New York City to star as Girl in <i><a href="/wiki/Once_(musical)" title="Once (musical)">Once</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Road_to_Broadway">Road to Broadway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joanna_Christie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Road to Broadway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Christie said that being on Broadway is "every dream [she's] ever had come true."<sup id="cite_ref-carriebradshaw_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-carriebradshaw-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  However, right before she landed the role in <i>Once</i>, she was about to give up. "I felt like I was at my lowest, and I decided to go back to university to do anthropology,"<sup id="cite_ref-lovelorntroubadors_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lovelorntroubadors-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> she said in an interview with broadway.com. Her audition process was rather strenuous, having to learn two full songs on the piano and three scenes in a Czech accent in a mere 24 hours.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_projects">Music projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joanna_Christie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Christie recorded a song called "This Gift" with Glen Hansard, who composed many of the songs in <i>Once</i>. The song was released on his solo album, <i>Rhythm and Repose</i>. She says that it is "one of the biggest honours of my life so far...I still can't quite believe it!"<sup id="cite_ref-cue&amp;a_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cue&amp;a-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Inspired by her move to Broadway last year, she is scheduled to release an album entitled <i>The Year of Joy &amp; Tears</i> set for release later this year.
</p><p>On 20 April 2014, she collaborated with her <i>Once</i> cast members and contributed her piano and vocal talents for a live-album recording of the Adam Brown and Friends performance at the Bowery Poetry Club.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joanna_Christie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Christie's mother, Sue, in addition to conducting for the Millhouse Green Male Voice choir in Penistone, sings with The Mastersingers.<sup id="cite_ref-huddersfield_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huddersfield-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Her father Paul, who died in 2012, was a property developer.<sup id="cite_ref-huddersfield_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huddersfield-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Her brother Luke is also a musician, singer, and songwriter. The pair have a band together called Luke J Christie &amp; The Held Breath.<sup id="cite_ref-cue&amp;a_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cue&amp;a-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She was inspired by her parents, who taught her that she "could do anything [she] wanted in life and to always be [her]self and follow [her] own path."<sup id="cite_ref-cue&amp;a_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cue&amp;a-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She has been highly influenced by her parents her whole life. "I owe all my success to my parents for giving me the opportunities to get to this point in my career and I would like to pay tribute to them...I'm just so grateful to my mum for giving me the gift of music and to both her and my dad for always supporting and encouraging me," she explained in a 2013 interview with the <i>Huddersfield Daily Examiner</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-huddersfield_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huddersfield-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
