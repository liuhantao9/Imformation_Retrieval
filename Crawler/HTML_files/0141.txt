<p><b>Johann Niklaus Schneider-Ammann</b> (born <b>Schneider</b>; 18 February 1952) is a Swiss businessman and a politician.  Schneider-Ammann is married, has two children, and lives in <a href="/wiki/Langenthal" title="Langenthal">Langenthal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Bern" title="Canton of Bern">Canton of Bern</a>, Switzerland.
</p><p>The son of a <a href="/wiki/Veterinarian" title="Veterinarian">veterinarian</a> born in <a href="/wiki/Sumiswald" title="Sumiswald">Sumiswald</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, Schneider-Ammann graduated as an <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineer</a> from the <a href="/wiki/ETH_Z%C3%BCrich" class="mw-redirect" title="ETH Zürich">ETH Zürich</a> in 1977, and obtained a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Business_Administration" title="Master of Business Administration">Master of Business Administration</a> from <a href="/wiki/INSEAD" title="INSEAD">INSEAD</a> in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-Le_Matin_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Le_Matin-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1990 to 2010, Schneider-Ammann acted as the head of his family's <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">mechanical engineering</a> company, <a href="/wiki/Ammann_Group" title="Ammann Group">Ammann Group</a>, in the 4th generation, when he passed control over the company to his two children.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Johann_Schneider-Ammann&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1999, Schneider-Ammann was elected to the Swiss National Council, and is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Free_Democratic_Party_of_Switzerland" title="Free Democratic Party of Switzerland">Free Democratic Party</a>.
</p><p>From 1999, Schneider-Ammann chaired the corporate union <a href="/wiki/Swissmem" title="Swissmem">Swissmem</a>. Schneider-Ammann was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_National_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Swiss National Council">National Council</a> in <a href="/wiki/1999_Swiss_federal_election" title="1999 Swiss federal election">1999</a>, and re-elected in <a href="/wiki/2003_Swiss_federal_election" title="2003 Swiss federal election">2003</a> and <a href="/wiki/2007_Swiss_federal_election" title="2007 Swiss federal election">2007</a>.
</p><p>In the context of the <a href="/wiki/2008_financial_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 financial crisis">2008 financial crisis</a>, Schneider-Ammann took a critical stance on bonuses awarded to the finance industry.<sup id="cite_ref-Egenter_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Egenter-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, Schneider-Ammann's company moved substantial funds to a <a href="/wiki/Tax_haven" title="Tax haven">tax haven</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/2010_Swiss_Federal_Council_election" title="2010 Swiss Federal Council election">22 September 2010</a>, Schneider-Ammann was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Federal_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Swiss Federal Council">Swiss Federal Council</a>, as the successor to <a href="/wiki/Hans-Rudolf_Merz" title="Hans-Rudolf Merz">Hans-Rudolf Merz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Schneider-Ammann is the head of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Department_of_Economic_Affairs,_Education_and_Research" title="Federal Department of Economic Affairs, Education and Research">Federal Department of Economic Affairs, Education and Research</a>—the Swiss <a href="/wiki/Commerce_minister" title="Commerce minister">commerce minister</a> (formerly the Federal Department of Economic Affairs)--taking office on 1 November 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-ATS-2010_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ATS-2010-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
Schneider-Ammann had previously announced his intention to step down from his corporate responsibilities as well as various board memberships if elected.<sup id="cite_ref-Le_Matin_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Le_Matin-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
