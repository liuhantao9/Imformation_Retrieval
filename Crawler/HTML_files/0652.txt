<p><b>North 1 East</b> is the sixth tier of the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union_in_England" title="Rugby union in England">English rugby union</a> <a href="/wiki/English_rugby_union_system" title="English rugby union system">domestic competition</a>, formed in 1987 using the name North Division 2, involving clubs from the north of the country.  There was also division known as <a href="/wiki/North_East_1" title="North East 1">North East 1</a> that began in 1987 for clubs based in the north-east but this was a seventh (later eighth) tier league.  Currently, the division is made up of teams from around the <a href="/wiki/North_East_England" title="North East England">North Eastern area of England</a>, principally <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="East Yorkshire">East Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northumberland" title="Northumberland">Northumberland</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyne_and_Wear" title="Tyne and Wear">Tyne and Wear</a>, and <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, who play home and away matches throughout a winter season.
</p><p>The league champions are automatically promoted to the <a href="/wiki/North_Premier" title="North Premier">North Premier</a>, whilst the second placed team enters into a play-off match with the second placed team in the equivalent division <a href="/wiki/North_1_West" title="North 1 West">North 1 West</a>. The bottom three teams are relegated to either the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_1" title="Yorkshire 1">Yorkshire Division One</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Durham/Northumberland_1" title="Durham/Northumberland 1">Durham/Northumberland 1</a> leagues, the seventh tier of the English domestic rugby union competition.  Following the play-off game, depending on the result, a team from the division with fifteen teams are transferred to the division with thirteen teams to level up the number of teams in each division.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_Division_1">North Division 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_1_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: North Division 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top six teams from <a href="/wiki/North_Premier" title="North Premier">North Division One</a> and the top six from <a href="/wiki/Midlands_Premier" title="Midlands Premier">Midland Division One</a> were combined to create <a href="/wiki/Courage_League_Division_5" title="Courage League Division 5">National 5 North</a>.  This meant that tier 6 was renamed North Division 1 for the years that National 5 North was active.  This would lead to a new <a href="/wiki/North_East_1" title="North East 1">North Division 2</a> was formed at tier 7 between 1994-95.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion_play-offs">Promotion play-offs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_1_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Promotion play-offs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since the 2000–01 season there has been a play-off between the runners-up of North 1 East and <a href="/wiki/North_1_West" title="North 1 West">North 1 West</a> for the third and final promotion place to <a href="/wiki/North_Premier" title="North Premier">North Premier</a>. The team with the superior league record has home advantage in the tie.  At the end of the 2018–19 season the North 1 East have been the most successful with thirteen wins to the North 1 West teams six; and the home team has won promotion on fifteen occasions compared to the away teams five.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=North_1_East&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">
<div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns"><ol class="references">
<li id="cite_note-6"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-6">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Despite finishing bottom <a href="/wiki/New_Brighton_F.C._(rugby_union)" title="New Brighton F.C. (rugby union)">New Brighton</a> avoided relegation with 10th placed <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlise_Rugby_Club&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carlise Rugby Club (page does not exist)">Carlisle</a> going down instead.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-7"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-7">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">It seems likely that <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Rugby_Union_Football_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Rugby Union Football Club">Huddersfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Rugby_Club" title="Manchester Rugby Club">Manchester</a> were the promoted sides this season according to league table records from the <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale_R.U.F.C." title="Wharfedale R.U.F.C.">Wharfedale</a> website as neither had been in North 1 the previous season and were not relegated from a higher division.  However, as of yet it is unsure as to which club finished as champions.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-8"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-8">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Due to RFU restructuring of the English league structure there would be no relegation this season.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-49"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-49">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Two of Bradford &amp; Bingley's titles was won when league was a single division known as North Division 2.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-50"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-50">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Wharfedale's 1989–90 title was won when North 1 East was known as North 2 and was a tier 6 league.  Having secured promotion into North 1 they won that division in 1993–94, but due to RFU restructuring of the leagues that year due to the introduction of <a href="/wiki/Courage_League_Division_5" title="Courage League Division 5">Courage League Division 5</a>, North 1 went from being a tier 5 to tier 6 league in this time frame, with North 2 becoming tier 7.  This explains why Wharfedale has won 2 titles at tier 6.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-51"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-51">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Darlington Mowden Park's title was won when league was a single division known as North Division 2.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-52"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-52">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Doncaster's title was won when league was a single division known as North Division 2.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-53"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-53">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Manchester's title was won when league was a single division known as North Division 1.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-54"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-54">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Northern's title was won when league was a single division known as North Division 2.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-55"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-55">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Stockton's title was won when league was a single division known as North Division 2.</span>
