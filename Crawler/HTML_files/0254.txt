<p><b>Cambodian names</b> usually consist of two elements including a <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a>, which serves as a common <a href="/wiki/Family_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Family name">family name</a> for siblings, followed by a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-huffman_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffman-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-short_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-short-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Surname then given name (<a href="/wiki/Eastern_name_order" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern name order">Eastern name order</a>). An example is famous singer <a href="/wiki/Sin_Sisamouth" class="mw-redirect" title="Sin Sisamouth">Sin Sisamouth</a>, his surname (last name) is Sin and his given name (first name) is Sisamouth. (In <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">Western</a> sources, the two are sometimes reversed.)<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Traditionally, the <a href="/wiki/Khmer_people" title="Khmer people">Khmer</a> do not have Western-style family names shared by multiple generations or entire families. Instead, Khmer children use their father's given name as their family name.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Other <a href="/wiki/Austroasiatic_languages" title="Austroasiatic languages">Austroasiatic</a> indigenous <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_Cambodia" title="Ethnic groups in Cambodia">people groups within Cambodia</a> have similar naming customs, while the <a href="/wiki/Sino-Khmer" class="mw-redirect" title="Sino-Khmer">Sino-Khmer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_Cambodians" title="Vietnamese Cambodians">Viet-Khmer</a> may follow <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_name" title="Vietnamese name">Vietnamese</a> naming patterns, respectively. <a href="/wiki/Chams" title="Chams">Chams</a> in Cambodia may have either Khmer or Cham names or a combination of both. Cham name order is the reverse of the Khmer; the given name is followed by the father's given name.<sup id="cite_ref-Ueki_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ueki-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>23</span></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Given_names">Given names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodian_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Given names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Generally, women are given names relating to beauty, while men are given names of virtues.<sup id="cite_ref-struggling_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-struggling-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Some Cambodian given names are <a href="/wiki/Unisex_names" class="mw-redirect" title="Unisex names">unisex names</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Surnames">Surnames</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodian_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Surnames">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Surnames are usually taken from the surname or the given name of the father<sup id="cite_ref-huffman_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffman-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and are generally monosyllabic.<sup id="cite_ref-fong_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fong-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Cambodian surnames are sometimes identical to <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese</a> or <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_name" title="Vietnamese name">Vietnamese</a> surnames.<sup id="cite_ref-fong_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fong-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Women keep their <a href="/wiki/Maiden_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Maiden name">maiden names</a> after marriage.<sup id="cite_ref-struggling_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-struggling-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_and_meanings">Origin and meanings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodian_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Origin and meanings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The meanings of Cambodian names are generally very simple and reference positive attributes.  Cambodian people are called by their given names without a title (informal) or by their given names with a title (formal); the full name, including both family name and given name is often used.<sup id="cite_ref-short_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-short-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-struggling_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-struggling-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> (Surnames are used as a form of address, however, in the case of names that originated as <a href="/wiki/Khmer_Rouge" title="Khmer Rouge">revolutionary</a> aliases.)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (December 2009)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Different naming traditions exist among ethnic groups other than the <a href="/wiki/Khmer_people" title="Khmer people">Khmer</a> majority. The Cambodian population is 90% <a href="/wiki/Buddhist" class="mw-redirect" title="Buddhist">Buddhist</a> and names are often taken from <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhism</a>. Among the <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> minority, <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> names are often used as family names.<sup id="cite_ref-huffman_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffman-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pronunciation">Pronunciation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodian_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Pronunciation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Khmer names are usually pronounced with the stress (emphasis) placed on the last syllable.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Khmer_phonology" class="mw-redirect" title="Khmer phonology">Khmer</a> uses a <a href="/wiki/Glottal_stop" title="Glottal stop">glottal stop</a> (the Cockney stop in "ten green bo'les") and other <a href="/wiki/Stop_consonant" title="Stop consonant">stops</a>: <i><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">p</span></i>, <i><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">t</span></i>, <i><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">c</span></i> and <i><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">k</span></i> which may or may not occur with <a href="/wiki/Aspiration_(phonetics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Aspiration (phonetics)">aspiration</a>. In romanizations of <a href="/wiki/Khmer_script" title="Khmer script">Khmer script</a>, aspiration (i.e., a breath sound) is usually marked with an <i>h</i>. Final <i>r</i>, <i>d</i>, <i>g</i>, <i>s</i>, <i>b</i>, and <i>z</i> sounds are not heard: Ngor is pronounced <i>Ngow</i>. Some final consonants are written but not pronounced.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_some_family_names">List of some family names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodian_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: List of some family names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Khmer "family" names are usually simply their father's given name, and as such change with each generation. Other ethnic groups, particularly Chinese-Cambodians and Vietnamese-Cambodians, may have a family name that is taken by each generation, in which case the name is pronounced similarly to the language of origin but within the bounds of Khmer phonology. Below is a list of some common family names,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> some of which are also found as given names.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Compound_names">Compound names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cambodian_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Compound names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At times, many families combine shorter names to create a longer name. This happens often among the wealthier class of Cambodians.
</p>
<ul><li>Sovanna (Combined "So" and "<a href="/wiki/Vanna" title="Vanna">Vanna</a>") means gold in Sanskrit</li>
<li>Somally (Combined "So" and "Maly")</li>
<li>Chandarith (Combined "Chanda" and "Rith")</li></ul>
