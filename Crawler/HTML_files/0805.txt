<p><b>Lena Kathren Headey</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/iː/: &#39;ee&#39; in &#39;fleece&#39;">iː</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span><span title="/ə/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">ə</span></span> <wbr /><span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;h&#39; in &#39;hi&#39;">h</span><span title="/iː/: &#39;ee&#39; in &#39;fleece&#39;">iː</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span><span title="/i/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;happy&#39;">i</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">LEE</span>-nə <span style="font-size:90%">HEE</span>-dee</i></a>; born 3 October 1973)<sup id="cite_ref-tvg_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> is an English actress. She is best known for her portrayal of <a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei Lannister</a> on the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">Game of Thrones</a></i> (2011–2019), for which she has received five <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Drama_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series">Primetime Emmy Award</a> nominations and a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actress_%E2%80%93_Series,_Miniseries_or_Television_Film" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress – Series, Miniseries or Television Film">Golden Globe</a> nomination.
</p><p>Headey made her film debut in <i><a href="/wiki/Waterland_(film)" title="Waterland (film)">Waterland</a></i> (1992), and continued to work steadily in British and American films and on television, before finding fame with her lead performances in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Brothers_Grimm_(film)" title="The Brothers Grimm (film)">The Brothers Grimm</a></i> (2005) and <i><a href="/wiki/300_(film)" title="300 (film)">300</a></i> (2006). Her other film credits include <i><a href="/wiki/The_Remains_of_the_Day_(film)" title="The Remains of the Day (film)">The Remains of the Day</a></i> (1993), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jungle_Book_(1994_film)" title="The Jungle Book (1994 film)">The Jungle Book</a></i> (1994), <i><a href="/wiki/Ripley%27s_Game_(film)" title="Ripley&#39;s Game (film)">Ripley's Game</a></i> (2002), <i><a href="/wiki/Dredd" title="Dredd">Dredd</a></i> (2012), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Purge_(2013_film)" title="The Purge (2013 film)">The Purge</a></i> (2013), and <i><a href="/wiki/300:_Rise_of_an_Empire" title="300: Rise of an Empire">300: Rise of an Empire</a></i> (2014).
</p><p>Outside of film, Headey played <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Connor_(Terminator)" title="Sarah Connor (Terminator)">Sarah Connor</a> in the spin-off television series <i><a href="/wiki/Terminator:_The_Sarah_Connor_Chronicles" title="Terminator: The Sarah Connor Chronicles">Terminator: The Sarah Connor Chronicles</a></i> (2008–2009). She provided voices for the <a href="/wiki/Action_role-playing_game" title="Action role-playing game">role-playing</a> video game <i><a href="/wiki/Risen_(video_game)" title="Risen (video game)">Risen</a></i> (2009) and the video game <a href="/wiki/Tie-in" title="Tie-in">tie-in</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Kingsglaive:_Final_Fantasy_XV" title="Kingsglaive: Final Fantasy XV">Kingsglaive: Final Fantasy XV</a></i> (2016), as well as the animated series <i><a href="/wiki/Danger_Mouse_(2015_TV_series)" title="Danger Mouse (2015 TV series)">Danger Mouse</a></i> (2015–) and <i><a href="/wiki/Trollhunters" class="mw-redirect" title="Trollhunters">Trollhunters</a></i> (2017–2018).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lena_Headey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Headey was born in <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_Bermuda" title="Hamilton, Bermuda">Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, to English parents, Sue and John Headey.<sup id="cite_ref-tvg_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tvg-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Her father, a Yorkshire police officer, was stationed there at the time in the <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_Police_Service" title="Bermuda Police Service">Bermuda Police Service</a>. She has one younger brother, Tim.<sup id="cite_ref-menshealth_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-menshealth-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She is of part <a href="/wiki/Irish_people" title="Irish people">Irish</a> descent.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The family moved to <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a> when she was five, and then to <a href="/wiki/Highburton" title="Highburton">Highburton, near Huddersfield, West Yorkshire</a>, when Headey was 11.<sup id="cite_ref-menshealth_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-menshealth-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She took ballet lessons as a child.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Headey had her first experience of acting when she was a pupil at <a href="/wiki/Shelley_College" title="Shelley College">Shelley College</a>; she was noticed at age 17, when performing in a school production at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">Royal National Theatre</a>, and was picked for a role in the 1992 film <i><a href="/wiki/Waterland_(film)" title="Waterland (film)">Waterland</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lena_Headey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Beginnings_.281992.E2.80.932004.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Beginnings_(1992–2004)">Beginnings (1992–2004)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lena_Headey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Beginnings (1992–2004)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the age of 17, Headey performed in a one-off show and afterwards a casting agent took a photo and asked her to audition. She eventually obtained a supporting role in the drama <i><a href="/wiki/Waterland_(film)" title="Waterland (film)">Waterland</a></i> (1992), in which she had the opportunity to work with actors who had been in the business several years before her. She also had a small role in the critically acclaimed film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Remains_of_the_Day_(film)" title="The Remains of the Day (film)">The Remains of the Day</a></i>, which received eight <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Awards</a> nominations. Her career would continue to grow in England throughout the decade, and see larger parts in bigger motion pictures.
</p><p>Headey played Kitty Brydon, the childhood friend and romantic interest of <a href="/wiki/Mowgli" title="Mowgli">Mowgli</a>, in Disney's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jungle_Book_(1994_film)" title="The Jungle Book (1994 film)">The Jungle Book</a></i> (1994). <a href="/wiki/James_Berardinelli" title="James Berardinelli">James Berardinelli</a> praised the cast's "solid performances"<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> as part of a positive critical reception, and the film found moderate commercial success in theaters.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She appeared opposite <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Redgrave" title="Vanessa Redgrave">Vanessa Redgrave</a> in the 1997 romantic drama <i><a href="/wiki/Mrs_Dalloway_(film)" title="Mrs Dalloway (film)">Mrs Dalloway</a></i>, portraying the closest friend of a housewife, who is now wife of a self-made millionnaire and mother of five. She was then cast in the drama <i><a href="/wiki/Onegin_(film)" title="Onegin (film)">Onegin</a></i> (1999), a film based on the 19th-century Russian novel <i><a href="/wiki/Eugene_Onegin" title="Eugene Onegin">Eugene Onegin</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Pushkin" title="Alexander Pushkin">Alexander Pushkin</a>, in which she portrayed the fiancée of an aspiring poet and appeared with <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Fiennes" title="Ralph Fiennes">Ralph Fiennes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liv_Tyler" title="Liv Tyler">Liv Tyler</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-filmography_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-filmography-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The film was critically and financially unsuccessful.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2000, Headey played a newly promoted lawyer with no apparent emotional attachments in the romantic comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_(2000_film)" title="Aberdeen (2000 film)">Aberdeen</a></i>, receiving the Silver <a href="/wiki/Iris_Award" title="Iris Award">Iris Award</a> for Best Actress at the 2001 Brussels European Film Festival, and also starred as a troubled college student in the psychological drama <i><a href="/wiki/Gossip_(2000_American_film)" title="Gossip (2000 American film)">Gossip</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Kate_Hudson" title="Kate Hudson">Kate Hudson</a>. In 2001's comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Parole_Officer" title="The Parole Officer">The Parole Officer</a></i>, Headey took on the role of a police officer, alongside <a href="/wiki/Steve_Coogan" title="Steve Coogan">Steve Coogan</a> in his first film role. While the film was warmly received, <i>ViewLondon</i> remarked: "The only disappointment is Lena Headey, who, despite being fantastically sexy (she's given both a nude scene and a 'dressed as a prostitute' scene), smirks her way through the entire film, even at the most inappropriate moments".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2002, she appeared as a mousey Victorian lesbian artist with <a href="/wiki/Gwyneth_Paltrow" title="Gwyneth Paltrow">Gwyneth Paltrow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Eckhart" title="Aaron Eckhart">Aaron Eckhart</a> in the mystery drama <i><a href="/wiki/Possession_(2002_film)" title="Possession (2002 film)">Possession</a></i>, based on the 1990 <a href="/wiki/Possession:_A_Romance" class="mw-redirect" title="Possession: A Romance">novel of the same name</a> by British author <a href="/wiki/A._S._Byatt" title="A. S. Byatt">A. S. Byatt</a>, and as the wife of a law-abiding art framer dying of <a href="/wiki/Leukemia" title="Leukemia">leukemia</a> in the thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Ripley%27s_Game_(film)" title="Ripley&#39;s Game (film)">Ripley's Game</a></i>, adapted from the 1974 <a href="/wiki/Ripley%27s_Game" title="Ripley&#39;s Game">novel of the same name</a>. In its review for the latter, <i>Eye for Film</i> noted: "Whilst this is very much a male-centered film, Lena Headey turns in a powerful performance as Jonathan's wife, creating a sense of balance and normality against which other events are contrasted".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Headey appeared in the comedy <i><a href="/wiki/The_Actors" title="The Actors">The Actors</a></i> (2003), opposite <a href="/wiki/Dylan_Moran" title="Dylan Moran">Dylan Moran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Caine" title="Michael Caine">Michael Caine</a>, portraying the love interest of a struggling actor.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Rise_to_prominence_.282005.E2.80.932010.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Rise_to_prominence_(2005–2010)">Rise to prominence (2005–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lena_Headey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rise to prominence (2005–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Headey found much wider recognition when she starred with <a href="/wiki/Matt_Damon" title="Matt Damon">Matt Damon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heath_Ledger" title="Heath Ledger">Heath Ledger</a> in <a href="/wiki/Terry_Gilliam" title="Terry Gilliam">Terry Gilliam</a>'s adventure fantasy film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Brothers_Grimm_(film)" title="The Brothers Grimm (film)">The Brothers Grimm</a></i> (2005), as Angelika, whose woodsman father was transformed into a werewolf by the <a href="/wiki/Evil_Queen" title="Evil Queen">Evil Queen</a>. She was drawn to the "tomboy" nature of her character, on which she stated: "She lives and grows up and survives in the forest. Terry and I talked about how her instincts are almost animalistic and she can see 360 degrees around her. She is aware of what is going on. That is how she is grounded. She is of the earth".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Brothers Grimm</i> received mixed reviews and made US$105.3 million worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, Headey also starred with actress <a href="/wiki/Piper_Perabo" title="Piper Perabo">Piper Perabo</a> in the films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cave_(film)" title="The Cave (film)">The Cave</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Imagine_Me_%26_You" title="Imagine Me &amp; You">Imagine Me &amp; You</a></i>. The horror film <i>The Cave</i> saw the actress play a member of a group of divers who become trapped in an underwater cave network. While critical response was negative,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> the film managed to turn a profit at the box office.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In the romantic dramedy <i>Imagine Me &amp; You</i>, she took on the role of a woman who becomes infatuated with a newlywed bride, causing a stir among the bride's family and friends. The film found a limited release in theaters, but Mick LaSalle from the <i><a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Chronicle" title="San Francisco Chronicle">San Francisco Chronicle</a></i> stated that the actress "has a forthright, irresistible appeal and a face and especially a smile that suggest intelligence, integrity and lots of fun".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Headey,_Lena_(2007).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Headey%2C_Lena_%282007%29.jpg/170px-Headey%2C_Lena_%282007%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Headey%2C_Lena_%282007%29.jpg/255px-Headey%2C_Lena_%282007%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Headey%2C_Lena_%282007%29.jpg/340px-Headey%2C_Lena_%282007%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="853" data-file-height="1280" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Headey,_Lena_(2007).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Headey at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Scream_Awards" title="Scream Awards">Scream Awards</a></div></div></div>
<p>Her most known film role came perhaps in 2007, when Headey played <a href="/wiki/Gorgo,_Queen_of_Sparta" title="Gorgo, Queen of Sparta">Queen Gorgo</a> in <a href="/wiki/Zack_Snyder" title="Zack Snyder">Zack Snyder</a>'s epic war film <i><a href="/wiki/300_(film)" title="300 (film)">300</a></i>, based on the <a href="/wiki/300_(comics)" title="300 (comics)">1998 comic series</a> of the same name by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Miller_(comics)" title="Frank Miller (comics)">Frank Miller</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lynn_Varley" title="Lynn Varley">Lynn Varley</a>, a fictionalized retelling of the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Thermopylae" title="Battle of Thermopylae">Battle of Thermopylae</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Greco-Persian_Wars" title="Greco-Persian Wars">Persian Wars</a>. Her character has a much larger role in the film than she does in the comic book, where she only appears in the beginning,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and required her to film naked for one scene. "It's always weird the thought of taking your clothes off in front of 20 people and then to have it projected in front of many more", she said during an interview. "I think it was necessary because we only get that scene to establish their relationship. It is a very obvious moment but I think it does it in quite a beautiful way."<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The film received mixed reviews, but was a box office success, grossing over US$450 million.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, she also appeared as the stuffy Miss Dickinson in the sixth release of the <i><a href="/wiki/St._Trinian%27s_(film)" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Trinian&#39;s (film)">St. Trinian's</a></i> film series.
</p><p>Headey starred as Fembot in Austin Powers, as well as <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Connor_(Terminator)" title="Sarah Connor (Terminator)">Sarah Connor</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Terminator:_The_Sarah_Connor_Chronicles" title="Terminator: The Sarah Connor Chronicles">Terminator: The Sarah Connor Chronicles</a></i>, a television spin-off of <a href="/wiki/James_Cameron" title="James Cameron">James Cameron</a>'s popular <i><a href="/wiki/Terminator_(franchise)" title="Terminator (franchise)">Terminator</a></i> franchise. The show ran for 31 episodes in two seasons, from January 2008 to April 2009. <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> praised "Headey's gritty performance as Sarah —managing to be smart, resourceful and tough, yet melancholy and vulnerable as well".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> For her performance, she was nominated twice for the <a href="/wiki/Saturn_Award_for_Best_Actress_on_Television" title="Saturn Award for Best Actress on Television">Saturn Award for Best Actress on Television</a>. In 2008, she starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Matthias_Schweigh%C3%B6fer" title="Matthias Schweighöfer">Matthias Schweighöfer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Fiennes" title="Joseph Fiennes">Joseph Fiennes</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Red_Baron_(2008_film)" title="The Red Baron (2008 film)">The Red Baron</a></i>, a biographical film of the legendary <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> fighter pilot <a href="/wiki/Manfred_von_Richthofen" title="Manfred von Richthofen">Manfred von Richthofen</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> appeared in <a href="/wiki/Ridley_Scott" title="Ridley Scott">Ridley Scott</a>-produced sci-fi drama <i><a href="/wiki/Tell-Tale_(film)" title="Tell-Tale (film)">Tell-Tale</a></i>, a film based on the short story by <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Allan_Poe" title="Edgar Allan Poe">Edgar Allan Poe</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and played a radiologist in the horror film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Broken_(film)" title="The Broken (film)">The Broken</a></i>, which premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Sundance_Film_Festival" title="Sundance Film Festival">Sundance Film Festival</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> While the three aforementioned films went largely unnoticed by audiences, some critics praised Headey's performance in <i>The Broken</i>, including Kim Koynar, of <i>Cinematical</i>, who wrote that Headey "largely carries the film, and does so quite ably".<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Headey played an ill-fated character in the slasher film <i><a href="/wiki/Laid_to_Rest_(film)" title="Laid to Rest (film)">Laid to Rest</a></i>, which received a DVD release,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> had a part in a short film titled <i>The Devil's Wedding</i>, and also provided her voice for an episode of the <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network" title="Cartoon Network">Cartoon Network</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Super_Hero_Squad_Show" title="The Super Hero Squad Show">The Super Hero Squad Show</a></i>, playing <a href="/wiki/Black_Widow_(comics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Black Widow (comics)">Black Widow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mystique_(comics)" title="Mystique (comics)">Mystique</a>. She briefly appeared in the independent comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Pete_Smalls_Is_Dead" title="Pete Smalls Is Dead">Pete Smalls Is Dead</a></i> (2010).
</p>
<h3><span id="Mainstream_success_.282011.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mainstream_success_(2011–present)">Mainstream success (2011–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lena_Headey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mainstream success (2011–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Beginning in April 2011, Headey portrayed queen regent <a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei Lannister</a> on the <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">Game of Thrones</a></i>, based on <a href="/wiki/George_R._R._Martin" title="George R. R. Martin">George R. R. Martin</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> series of novels.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> She was cast in the role after her friend and eventual co-star, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Dinklage" title="Peter Dinklage">Peter Dinklage</a>, suggested her casting to producers.<sup id="cite_ref-UKComplex_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UKComplex-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Her performance as the ruthless queen has received critical acclaim,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> earning a <a href="/wiki/Scream_Awards" title="Scream Awards">Scream Award</a> nomination for Best Fantasy Actress for the role in 2011<sup id="cite_ref-scream_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scream-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Award</a> nominations for <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Drama_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series">Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series</a> in 2014, 2015, 2016, and 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-huffpost_2014-07_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffpost_2014-07-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-latimes_2014-07_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-latimes_2014-07-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-variety_2015-07_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety_2015-07-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lena_Headey_Primetime_Emmy_Awards_2014.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Lena_Headey_Primetime_Emmy_Awards_2014.jpg/170px-Lena_Headey_Primetime_Emmy_Awards_2014.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Lena_Headey_Primetime_Emmy_Awards_2014.jpg/255px-Lena_Headey_Primetime_Emmy_Awards_2014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Lena_Headey_Primetime_Emmy_Awards_2014.jpg/340px-Lena_Headey_Primetime_Emmy_Awards_2014.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1023" data-file-height="1537" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lena_Headey_Primetime_Emmy_Awards_2014.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Headey in 2014</div></div></div>
<p>After guest-starring in the <i><a href="/wiki/White_Collar_(TV_series)" title="White Collar (TV series)">White Collar</a></i> episode "<a href="/wiki/List_of_White_Collar_episodes#ep37" title="List of White Collar episodes">Taking Account</a>" in 2011, Headey took on the role of the leader of a drug dealing gang and the primary villain in the <a href="/wiki/3D_film" title="3D film">3D</a> sci-fi action film <i><a href="/wiki/Dredd" title="Dredd">Dredd</a></i> (2012), alongside <a href="/wiki/Karl_Urban" title="Karl Urban">Karl Urban</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olivia_Thirlby" title="Olivia Thirlby">Olivia Thirlby</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Her performance was inspired by punk-rock singer <a href="/wiki/Patti_Smith" title="Patti Smith">Patti Smith</a>, and on the character, Headey asserted: "I think of [Ma-Ma] like an old great white shark who is just waiting for someone bigger and stronger to show up and kill her&#160;[...]&#160;she's ready for it. In fact, she can't wait for it to happen&#160;[...]&#160;She's an addict, so she's dead in that way, but that last knock just hasn't come". Despite a positive critical response, the film flopped at the box office, seeing greater success following its home release; it has since been recognised as a <a href="/wiki/Cult_film" title="Cult film">cult film</a>.
</p><p>Headey joined again with <a href="/wiki/Ethan_Hawke" title="Ethan Hawke">Ethan Hawke</a> to star in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Purge_(2013_film)" title="The Purge (2013 film)">The Purge</a></i> (2013), a "micro-budget" horror film, in which she took on the role of the matriarch of a family who find themselves endangered by a gang of murderers during the annual Purge, a night during which all crime, even murder, is temporarily legal. The film at number one position in the United States, grossing over US$36&#160;million over the weekend;<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> it eventually made US$89.3 million worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> She next played shadowhunter <a href="/wiki/List_of_The_Mortal_Instruments_characters#Jocelyn_Fairchild/Morgenstern" title="List of The Mortal Instruments characters">Jocelyn Fray/Jocelyn Fairchild</a> in<i><a href="/wiki/The_Mortal_Instruments:_City_of_Bones" title="The Mortal Instruments: City of Bones">The Mortal Instruments: City of Bones</a></i> (also 2013), based on the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bones_(Clare_novel)" title="City of Bones (Clare novel)">first book</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mortal_Instruments" title="The Mortal Instruments">The Mortal Instruments</a></i> series by <a href="/wiki/Cassandra_Clare" title="Cassandra Clare">Cassandra Clare</a>, and opposite <a href="/wiki/Lily_Collins" title="Lily Collins">Lily Collins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Campbell_Bower" title="Jamie Campbell Bower">Jamie Campbell Bower</a>. The film flopped at the box office, and as a result, plans for sequels were eventually canceled.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the success of <i><a href="/wiki/300_(film)" title="300 (film)">300</a></i> (2007), Headey reprised her role as Queen Gorgo in <i><a href="/wiki/300:_Rise_of_an_Empire" title="300: Rise of an Empire">300: Rise of an Empire</a></i>, which was released in 2014. Like the first film, <i>Rise of an Empire</i> received mixed reviews, but was a major commercial success, grossing over US$337 million worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, she also starred in the fantasy adventure film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Adventurer:_The_Curse_of_the_Midas_Box" title="The Adventurer: The Curse of the Midas Box">The Adventurer: The Curse of the Midas Box</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> and the biographical film <i><a href="/wiki/Low_Down" title="Low Down">Low Down</a></i>, which detailed the life of jazz pianist <a href="/wiki/Joe_Albany" title="Joe Albany">Joe Albany</a>. In 2015, she played the wife of a federal prosecutor running for office who cannot stop himself from sleeping with high-class escorts in the <a href="/wiki/Political_thriller" title="Political thriller">political thriller</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Zipper_(film)" title="Zipper (film)">Zipper</a></i>, opposite <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Wilson_(American_actor)" title="Patrick Wilson (American actor)">Patrick Wilson</a>.
</p><p>In the historical action comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Pride_and_Prejudice_and_Zombies_(film)" title="Pride and Prejudice and Zombies (film)">Pride and Prejudice and Zombies</a></i>, Headey appeared as <a href="/wiki/Lady_Catherine_de_Bourgh" title="Lady Catherine de Bourgh">Lady Catherine de Bourgh</a>, with her former <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">Game of Thrones</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Century_(film)" title="Century (film)">Century</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Contractor_(2007_film)" title="The Contractor (2007 film)">The Contractor</a></i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Charles_Dance" title="Charles Dance">Charles Dance</a>. Andrew Barker, of <i>Variety</i>, found her to be "excessively diverting" in what he considered a "tolerable, but not handsome enough" film.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, Headey appeared as a "predictably hard-boiled boss" in the crime thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Thumper_(film)" title="Thumper (film)">Thumper</a></i>, which premiered at the <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Film_Festival" title="Tribeca Film Festival">Tribeca Film Festival</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> and provided the voiceover for <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_A-Class" title="Mercedes-Benz A-Class">Mercedes A-Class</a> television advertisement and Morgana in <i><a href="/wiki/Trollhunters:_Tales_of_Arcadia" title="Trollhunters: Tales of Arcadia">Trollhunters</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_image">Public image</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lena_Headey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Public image">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She has appeared on the covers of <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Guide" title="TV Guide">TV Guide</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/G3_(British_magazine)" title="G3 (British magazine)">G3</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_Mirror" title="Sunday Mirror">Sunday Mirror</a></i> and Germany's <i>Filmstar</i>. She ranked No. 64 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Maxim_(magazine)" title="Maxim (magazine)">Maxim</a></i> magazine Hot 100 of 2007 list. She was listed at No. 4, No. 10 and No. 3 in <a href="/wiki/AfterEllen" class="mw-redirect" title="AfterEllen">AfterEllen</a>'s list of the Hot 100 in 2007, 2008 and 2009, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lena_Headey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:LenaHeadeyMar07.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/LenaHeadeyMar07.jpg/170px-LenaHeadeyMar07.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="284" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/LenaHeadeyMar07.jpg/255px-LenaHeadeyMar07.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/LenaHeadeyMar07.jpg/340px-LenaHeadeyMar07.jpg 2x" data-file-width="614" data-file-height="1024" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:LenaHeadeyMar07.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Headey in 2007</div></div></div>
<p>Headey was vegetarian; however, in a 2011 interview she stated she now eats meat again, albeit rarely.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Headey has a number of tattoos. Her tattoos include a large floral design on her back<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> as well as a <a href="/wiki/Pema_Ch%C3%B6dr%C3%B6n" title="Pema Chödrön">Pema Chödrön</a> quote on her ribs.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> She told <i><a href="/wiki/Esquire_(magazine)" title="Esquire (magazine)">Esquire</a></i>: "I love tattoos. I find it calming."<sup id="cite_ref-esquire1_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-esquire1-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Her tattoos often require covering when Headey works, although she has stated, "you can usually get away with it by keeping your clothes on."<sup id="cite_ref-esquire1_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-esquire1-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships_and_family">Relationships and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lena_Headey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Relationships and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Headey married musician <a href="/w/index.php?title=Peter_Loughran_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Peter Loughran (musician) (page does not exist)">Peter Loughran</a> in May 2007;<sup id="cite_ref-peopledivorce_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-peopledivorce-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> their son, Wylie Loughran, was born on 31 March 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> She has spoken about suffering from <a href="/wiki/Postpartum_depression" title="Postpartum depression">postpartum depression</a> following Wylie's birth.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Headey and Loughran separated in 2011, and she filed for divorce in the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_County_Superior_Court" title="Los Angeles County Superior Court">Los Angeles County Superior Court</a> on 20 July 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-peopledivorce_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-peopledivorce-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> The divorce was finalised on 26 December 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> She was linked to <i>Game of Thrones</i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Jerome_Flynn" title="Jerome Flynn">Jerome Flynn</a>. The relationship allegedly ended badly some time before March 2014, resulting in the two being kept apart on set.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> However, Flynn rebuffed the claims in 2019, saying the two had worked in a scene together, while adding "And the last time I saw Lena we were speaking, so I wouldn’t believe everything you read, and like I said, [the media] can get pretty desperate for stories."<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 10 July 2015, Headey gave birth to her second child, a daughter named Teddy, with director <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dan_Cadan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dan Cadan (page does not exist)">Dan Cadan</a> (her childhood friend, who also worked with Headey on his short film <i>The Devil's Wedding</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-theknot_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theknot-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> They had separated by June 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian2019_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian2019-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Headey has been close friends with actress <a href="/wiki/Piper_Perabo" title="Piper Perabo">Piper Perabo</a> since they starred together in the 2005 films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cave_(film)" title="The Cave (film)">The Cave</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Imagine_Me_%26_You" title="Imagine Me &amp; You">Imagine Me &amp; You</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> She is also good friends with her <i>Game of Thrones</i> co-stars <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Pascal" title="Pedro Pascal">Pedro Pascal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Dinklage" title="Peter Dinklage">Peter Dinklage</a>, the latter of whom first suggested to producers on <i>Game of Thrones</i> that she be cast as <a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei Lannister</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UKComplex_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UKComplex-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Activism">Activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lena_Headey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Headey has campaigned on behalf of <a href="/wiki/Animal_rights" title="Animal rights">animal rights</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> In 2008, she spoke out against animal abuse in an advertisement for animal welfare group <a href="/wiki/PETA" class="mw-redirect" title="PETA">PETA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Headey supports the <a href="/wiki/LGBT_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="LGBT rights">LGBT rights</a> organisation <a href="/wiki/NOH8" class="mw-redirect" title="NOH8">NOH8</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> and in April 2015 appeared on a T-shirt designed by them and sold through <a href="/wiki/Represent.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Represent.com">Represent.com</a> to raise funds for the cause.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> She has been involved with humanitarian organization the <a href="/wiki/International_Rescue_Committee" title="International Rescue Committee">International Rescue Committee</a> (IRC), advocating for migrants languishing in Greece during the <a href="/wiki/European_migrant_crisis" title="European migrant crisis">European migrant crisis</a>, saying that in the face of rising <a href="/wiki/Populism" title="Populism">populism</a>, and the "lost humanity in leadership", people should continue to "fight for the good".<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, Headey did a <a href="/wiki/Voice-over" title="Voice-over">voice-over</a> for an advertisement by the charity <a href="/wiki/Alzheimer%27s_Research_UK" title="Alzheimer&#39;s Research UK">Alzheimer's Research UK</a>, which launched ahead of World Alzheimer's Day (21 September).<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
