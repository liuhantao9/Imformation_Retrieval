<p>The <b>Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire</b> was an <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">infantry</a> <a href="/wiki/Regiment" title="Regiment">regiment</a> of the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Division" title="King&#39;s Division">King's Division</a>. It was created in 1958 by the amalgamation of the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Regiment" title="West Yorkshire Regiment">West Yorkshire Regiment (Prince of Wales's Own) (14th Foot)</a> and the <a href="/wiki/East_Yorkshire_Regiment" title="East Yorkshire Regiment">East Yorkshire Regiment (Duke of York's Own) (15th Foot)</a>. After 48 years service, the regiment was amalgamated again, this time with two other Yorkshire regiments. It became the 1st Battalion of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Regiment" title="Yorkshire Regiment">Yorkshire Regiment (14th/15th, 19th, 33rd and 76th Foot)</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Green_Howards" title="Green Howards">Green Howards (19th Foot)</a> forming the 2nd Battalion and the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Wellington&#39;s Regiment">Duke of Wellington's Regiment (33rd/76th Foot)</a> forming the 3rd Battalion on 6 June 2006. Following further merges, in 2012, the battalion was redesignated as the new 2nd Battalion (2Yorks) .<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation_and_name">Formation and name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_of_Wales%27s_Own_Regiment_of_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation and name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire was formed on 25 April 1958 by the amalgamation of The <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Regiment" title="West Yorkshire Regiment">West Yorkshire Regiment</a> (The Prince of Wales's Own) (14th Foot) and The <a href="/wiki/East_Yorkshire_Regiment" title="East Yorkshire Regiment">East Yorkshire Regiment</a> (The Duke of York's Own) (15th Foot).<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_of_Wales%27s_Own_Regiment_of_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first overseas posting for the regiment was from September 1958 to June 1959 in the <a href="/wiki/Colony_of_Aden" title="Colony of Aden">Colony of Aden</a> (present day, part of <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a>), followed by a posting to <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a> from June 1959 to June 1961.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The battalion served in <a href="/wiki/Wuppertal" title="Wuppertal">Wuppertal</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a> from 1961 to 1964, Berlin 1964-1965, then again in Aden from September 1965 until September 1966.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The regiment returned to England in 1966 and was based in <a href="/wiki/Colchester" title="Colchester">Colchester</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1967, operational elements were deployed to <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a> to help with the environmental clean-up after the <a href="/wiki/Torrey_Canyon_oil_spill" title="Torrey Canyon oil spill"><i>Torrey Canyon</i> oil spill</a>. An emergency deployment back to Aden in June following the <a href="/wiki/Aden_Emergency" title="Aden Emergency">mutinies by the South Arabian Federation Army and Yemen Federal Police</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Aden saw the deaths in action of two soldiers from the regiment, Pte F langrick and Sgt WS Saville, as well as the awards of one <a href="/wiki/Military_Cross" title="Military Cross">Military Cross</a>, one <a href="/wiki/Military_Medal" title="Military Medal">Military Medal</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Mention_In_Despatches" class="mw-redirect" title="Mention In Despatches">Mention In Despatches</a>. The battalion returned to Colchester in late 1967.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 1969, the battalion was deployed to <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, as part of the UK Government's response to terrorist attacks on the Government infrastructure in the province. The regiment's presence and constant patrolling of vital points initially stabilised the situation in their operational area of <a href="/wiki/County_Down" title="County Down">County Down</a>. On 12 July, sectarian violence led to a rapid redeployment to Derry. Decisive action by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Ulster_Constabulary" title="Royal Ulster Constabulary">Royal Ulster Constabulary</a> again calmed the situation down and the battalion moved into a fixed tactical base at Magilligan's Point, north of <a href="/wiki/Limavady" title="Limavady">Limavady</a> on the shores of <a href="/wiki/Lough_Foyle" title="Lough Foyle">Lough Foyle</a>. On 12 August, the violence erupted in a concerted campaign, which, after nearly three days of street battles, saw the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Ulster_Constabulary" title="Royal Ulster Constabulary">Royal Ulster Constabulary</a> (RUC) totally exhausted. On 14 August 1969, the regiment took over responsibility for the security of <a href="/wiki/Derry" title="Derry">Derry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From February 1970 to October 1972, the battalion was based in <a href="/wiki/Akrotiri_and_Dhekelia" title="Akrotiri and Dhekelia">Cyprus</a>, then returned to the UK, for another tour in Northern Ireland, until November 1973. From November 1973 until May 1974, the regiment was stationed in Dover, Kent.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> From 1974 to 1978, the regiment was stationed in <a href="/wiki/Celle" title="Celle">Celle</a> (formerly in West Germany), as part of the <a href="/wiki/7th_Armoured_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="7th Armoured Brigade (United Kingdom)">7th Armoured Brigade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The battalion completed another four month emergency tour in Derry during 1975. During this tour, 18-year-old Pte David Wray was shot and killed by a sniper whilst on a foot patrol in the <a href="/wiki/Creggan,_Derry" title="Creggan, Derry">Creggan</a> housing estate. From 1978 to 1983, the regiment served as part of the <a href="/wiki/ACE_Mobile_Force_(Land)" class="mw-redirect" title="ACE Mobile Force (Land)">Allied Mobile Force</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 1983, the battalion started a two-year tour as part of the <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Brigade" title="Berlin Brigade">Berlin Brigade</a>, where in 1984 it was presented with new regimental <a href="/wiki/Colours,_standards_and_guidons" class="mw-redirect" title="Colours, standards and guidons">Colours</a>. In April 1985, the battalion redeployed to <a href="/wiki/Abercorn_Barracks" title="Abercorn Barracks">Abercorn Barracks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ballykinler" title="Ballykinler">Ballykinler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> and was heavily committed in domestic duties, maintaining law and order, particularly in the terrorist plagued countryside of <a href="/wiki/South_Armagh_(Northern_Ireland_Parliament_constituency)" title="South Armagh (Northern Ireland Parliament constituency)">South Armagh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 1987, the battalion moved to Catterick, <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>, to join <a href="/wiki/24th_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="24th Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">24 Infantry Brigade</a>. Equipped with <a href="/wiki/Saxon_(vehicle)" title="Saxon (vehicle)">Saxon</a> armoured personnel carriers, this was the battalion's first tour in Yorkshire since their antecedent regiments amalgamation. In 1988, the battalion re-roled as an airmobile unit in line with 24 Infantry Brigade's conversion to 24 Airmobile Brigade.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 1990, the battalion moved to <a href="/wiki/Osnabr%C3%BCck" title="Osnabrück">Osnabrück</a> in West Germany to take up a Mechanised Infantry role as part of <a href="/wiki/12th_Mechanised_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="12th Mechanised Brigade (United Kingdom)">12 Armoured Brigade</a>. During the First <a href="/wiki/Gulf_War" title="Gulf War">Gulf War</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Operation_Granby" title="Operation Granby">Operation Granby</a> (later known as Desert storm), the battalion deployed formed units that included a Milan Section and a <a href="/wiki/Mortar_(weapon)" title="Mortar (weapon)">Mortar</a> section to the 1st battalion of the <a href="/wiki/Staffordshire_Regiment" title="Staffordshire Regiment">Staffordshire Regiment</a>, many men deployed on an individual basis reinforcing units. Some 149 personnel of all ranks were deployed to the gulf.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During Winter 1991, the battalion was once again in Northern Ireland, as the west Belfast <a href="/wiki/Roulement" title="Roulement">roulement</a> battalion.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of the 1992 <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Defence_Review" title="Strategic Defence Review">Strategic Defence Review</a>, the then government of <a href="/wiki/John_Major" title="John Major">John Major</a> decided to bring <a href="/wiki/Territorial_Army_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="Territorial Army (United Kingdom)">Territorial Army</a> (TA) and <a href="/wiki/Regular_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Regular Army">Regular Army</a> units closer together; as a result, the 2nd Battalion The Yorkshire Volunteers became 3rd Battalion The Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire (Yorkshire Volunteers). The 3rd Battalion was based at <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>. 3PWO lasted until 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1993, the regiment deployed to <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia-Herzegovina</a> as part of <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Protection_Force" title="United Nations Protection Force">UNPFOR</a>, with companies in <a href="/wiki/Gornji_Vakuf-Uskoplje" title="Gornji Vakuf-Uskoplje">Gornji Vakuf</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitez" title="Vitez">Vitez</a>. They picked up the unofficial nickname of "ShootBat" due to the 'robustness' of the battalion's actions.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1994, the battalion moved to the <a href="/wiki/British_Army_Training_Unit_Suffield" title="British Army Training Unit Suffield">British Army Training Unit Suffield</a>, Canada followed by a move to Warminster as the Infantry Demonstration Battalion at the Land Warfare Centre. 1997 saw the battalion stationed in Chester with deployments to Ulster, Sierra Leone (Operation Basilica) support to <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Agriculture,_Fisheries_and_Food_(United_Kingdom)" title="Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food (United Kingdom)">Ministry of Agriculture, Fisheries and Food</a> during the foot and mouth outbreak. Training exercises took troops to Kenya, Jamaica, Italy and Oman. By 2001-2003, 1PWO was in <a href="/wiki/Omagh" title="Omagh">Omagh</a> followed by a move to <a href="/wiki/Catterick_Garrison" title="Catterick Garrison">Catterick Garrison</a> as an <a href="/wiki/Air_Assault" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Assault">Air Assault</a> Battalion, with deployments to <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Federation of Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia</a>, as part of <a href="/wiki/SFOR" class="mw-redirect" title="SFOR">SFOR</a> and a training exercise to Belize in 2005. 
</p><p>The regiment amalgamated with its sister battalions of The <a href="/wiki/Green_Howards" title="Green Howards">Green Howards</a> and The <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Wellington&#39;s Regiment">Duke of Wellington's Regiment</a> to form The <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Regiment" title="Yorkshire Regiment">Yorkshire Regiment</a> on 6 June 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-bau_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bau-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regimental_museum">Regimental museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_of_Wales%27s_Own_Regiment_of_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Regimental museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The York Army Museum (for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dragoon_Guards" title="Royal Dragoon Guards">Royal Dragoon Guards</a>, Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire and the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Regiment" title="Yorkshire Regiment">Yorkshire Regiment</a>) is based at the <a href="/wiki/Tower_Street_drill_hall,_York" title="Tower Street drill hall, York">Tower Street drill hall</a> in <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
