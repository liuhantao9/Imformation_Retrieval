	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Colne Valley</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the valley in West Yorkshire. For other, see <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Colne Valley (disambiguation)">Colne Valley (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>Colne Valley</b> is a steep sided valley on the east flank of the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennine Hills</a> in the English county of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. It takes its name from the <a href="/wiki/River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Colne, West Yorkshire">River Colne</a> which rises above the town of <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a> and flows eastward  towards Huddersfield.
</p><p>The name is used to describe that section of the valley between its source and <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> at the point where the <a href="/wiki/River_Holme" title="River Holme">River Holme</a> joins the Colne. The name can describe the whole valley of the Colne, including the section through Huddersfield its confluence with the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a> at Cooper Bridge. Using the more common definition, the Colne Valley includes the towns and villages of <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slaithwaite" title="Slaithwaite">Slaithwaite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linthwaite" title="Linthwaite">Linthwaite</a>, Milnsbridge and <a href="/wiki/Golcar" title="Golcar">Golcar</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry_and_economy">Industry and economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colne_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Industry and economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Colne Valley played a significant role in the development of the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>. Most of the population had been hand loom weavers for generations but when water-powered <a href="/wiki/Textile_manufacture_during_the_Industrial_Revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="Textile manufacture during the Industrial Revolution">textile</a> mills were built on the  streams and rivers, the area was set to become a major producer of <a href="/wiki/Textile" title="Textile">textiles</a>. The Colne and the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Narrow_Canal" title="Huddersfield Narrow Canal">Huddersfield Narrow Canal</a> provided transport links which were added to by <a href="/wiki/A62_road" title="A62 road">road</a> and railway links to Huddersfield and <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> to the east and <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> to the west.
</p><p>By the end of the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>, the importance of the Colne Valley as a centre of textile production had begun to decrease. The area is now primarily residential, its transport links making it ideal for commuters. Tourism is promoted: the Huddersfield Narrow Canal was reopened in 2001 and boat trips into <a href="/wiki/Standedge_Tunnels" title="Standedge Tunnels">Standedge Canal Tunnel</a> are available; the <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Places_of_Historic_Interest_or_Natural_Beauty" title="National Trust for Places of Historic Interest or Natural Beauty">National Trust</a> advertises walks on <a href="/wiki/Marsden_Moor_Estate" title="Marsden Moor Estate">Marsden Moor Estate</a>; and television programmes such as <i><a href="/wiki/Last_of_the_Summer_Wine" title="Last of the Summer Wine">Last of the Summer Wine</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Where_the_Heart_Is_(UK_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Where the Heart Is (UK TV series)">Where the Heart Is</a></i> have been filmed there.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government_and_politics">Government and politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Colne_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Government and politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Colne Valley was an <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a> of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a> between 1937 and 1974. It was created by the merger of <a href="/wiki/Golcar" title="Golcar">Golcar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linthwaite" title="Linthwaite">Linthwaite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scammonden" title="Scammonden">Scammonden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slaithwaite" title="Slaithwaite">Slaithwaite</a> urban districts, and had a population of about 20,000. The <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> saw it merged into the metropolitan borough of <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-visbrit_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-visbrit-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> At the 2011 Census it was a ward of Kirlees Council. The population at the census was 17,369.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Colne Valley (UK Parliament constituency)">Colne Valley parliamentary constituency</a> preserves the Colne Valley name, although the constituency also includes the <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley" title="Holme Valley">Holme Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meltham" title="Meltham">Meltham</a> and some outskirts of Huddersfield. Notable former <a href="/wiki/Members_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Members of Parliament">MPs</a> for the constituency include <a href="/wiki/Victor_Grayson" title="Victor Grayson">Victor Grayson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Wainwright_(politician)" title="Richard Wainwright (politician)">Richard Wainwright</a>.
</p>
