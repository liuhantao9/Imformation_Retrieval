	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Save Huddersfield NHS</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>

<b>Save Huddersfield NHS</b> is a minor <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> registered in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-register_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-register-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The party campaigns against a proposed reorganisation of <a href="/wiki/National_Health_Service" title="National Health Service">National Health Service</a> facilities in the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> area and is led by Dr. Jackie Grunsell, a local <a href="/wiki/General_practitioner" title="General practitioner">general practitioner</a> and member of the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_(England_and_Wales)" title="Socialist Party (England and Wales)">Socialist Party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tsuc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tsuc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The party contested elections to <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a> Council, West Yorkshire, England in 2006–2008.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Save_Huddersfield_NHS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The organisation was initially formed as a pressure group after a public meeting in February 2006 against the Kirklees and Calderdale <a href="/wiki/National_Health_Service" title="National Health Service">NHS</a> Trust Board's proposals to close St. Lukes Hospital and transfer services (including maternity care) from <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> to <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The group organised a petition signed by over 50,000 people opposing the changes and three protest marches.<sup id="cite_ref-win2006_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-win2006-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On 21 March 2006 Save Huddersfield NHS was registered as a political party with the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_Commission_(United_Kingdom)" title="Electoral Commission (United Kingdom)">Electoral Commission</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-register_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-register-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electoral_contests">Electoral contests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Save_Huddersfield_NHS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Electoral contests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The party nominated three candidates to contest the <a href="/wiki/2006_United_Kingdom_local_elections" title="2006 United Kingdom local elections">elections</a> to Kirklees Borough Council in May 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Dr. Grunsell, the party leader, was elected for Crosland Moor and Netherton ward with a majority of 807, defeating the sitting <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats_(UK)" title="Liberal Democrats (UK)">Liberal Democrat</a> councillor.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She claimed her election was due to the electorate's disillusion with "mainstream parties" and warned that "this sends a very clear message to all those parties that you need to fight for the people you represent if you want to maintain your positions – or people who are prepared to stand and fight will take those positions from you." She went to explain that her party would not limit themselves to health issues, but would "fight on other issues".<sup id="cite_ref-win2006_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-win2006-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the following year the party stood a single candidate, again contesting the Crosland Moor and Neteherton ward. Like Grunsell, the candidate was an active Socialist Party member.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He failed to be elected.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/2008_United_Kingdom_local_elections" title="2008 United Kingdom local elections">2008 local elections</a> were the last contested by the party: the same candidate contested the same ward as in the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The party slipped into third place, behind the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> parties.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Loss_of_council_seat">Loss of council seat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Save_Huddersfield_NHS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Loss of council seat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although still listed as leader of Save Huddersfield NHS in April 2010,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Grunsell defended her seat on Kirklees Council at the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_Kingdom_local_elections" title="2010 United Kingdom local elections">2010 elections</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Trade_Unionist_and_Socialist_Coalition" title="Trade Unionist and Socialist Coalition">Trade Unionist and Socialist Coalition</a> candidate. She was defeated, falling to fourth place with 1472 votes.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_developments">Recent developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Save_Huddersfield_NHS&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recent developments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For a number of reasons, both personal and political, the broad coalition that gave rise to Save Huddersfield NHS fragmented after the 2010 election. However, in 2014 a group of local activists, several of whom had been involved in the original 2006 crusade, and all of whom were incensed by the coalition government's plans to restructure the health service, as well as proposals to close Accident and Emergency Services at Calderdale Royal Hospital, founded a Huddersfield chapter of the national campaign group, <a href="/wiki/Keep_Our_NHS_Public" title="Keep Our NHS Public">Keep Our NHS Public</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  At the moment, it is not clear whether the new grouping harbours electoral ambitions. Its primary aim, according to the secretary, Richard Murgatroyd, is to alert "the public about what is going on and why it matters to local people."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
