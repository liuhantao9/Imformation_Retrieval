<p>A <b>national personification</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Anthropomorphism" title="Anthropomorphism">anthropomorphic</a> <a href="/wiki/Personification" title="Personification">personification</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Nation" title="Nation">nation</a> or its people. It may appear in <a href="/wiki/Political_cartoon" title="Political cartoon">political cartoons</a> and <a href="/wiki/Propaganda" title="Propaganda">propaganda</a>.  As a personification it cannot be a real person, of the <a href="/wiki/Father_of_the_Nation" title="Father of the Nation">Father of the Nation</a> type, or one from ancient history who is believed to have been real.
</p><p>Some early personifications in the Western world tended to be national manifestations of the majestic wisdom and war goddess <a href="/wiki/Minerva" title="Minerva">Minerva</a>/<a href="/wiki/Athena" title="Athena">Athena</a>, and often took the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> name of the ancient <a href="/wiki/Roman_province" title="Roman province">Roman province</a>. Examples of this type include <a href="/wiki/Britannia_(emblem)" class="mw-redirect" title="Britannia (emblem)">Britannia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germania_(personification)" title="Germania (personification)">Germania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hibernia_(personification)" title="Hibernia (personification)">Hibernia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helvetia" title="Helvetia">Helvetia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Polonia_(personification)" title="Polonia (personification)">Polonia</a>. Examples of personifications of the <a href="/wiki/Liberty_(goddess)" class="mw-redirect" title="Liberty (goddess)">Goddess of Liberty</a> include <a href="/wiki/Marianne" title="Marianne">Marianne</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Statue_of_Liberty" title="Statue of Liberty">Statue of Liberty</a> (<i>Liberty Enlightening the World</i>), and many examples of United States coinage. Another ancient model was <a href="/wiki/Roma_(mythology)" title="Roma (mythology)">Roma</a>, a female <a href="/wiki/Deity" title="Deity">deity</a> who personified the city of <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> and more broadly, the Roman state, and who was revived in the 20th Century as the personification of <a href="/wiki/Benito_Mussolini" title="Benito Mussolini">Mussolini</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/New_Roman_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="New Roman Empire">New Roman Empire</a>". Examples of representations of the <a href="/wiki/Everyman" title="Everyman">everyman</a> or citizenry in addition to the nation itself are <a href="/wiki/Deutscher_Michel" title="Deutscher Michel">Deutscher Michel</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Bull" title="John Bull">John Bull</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uncle_Sam" title="Uncle Sam">Uncle Sam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Polandball" title="Polandball">Polandball</a>, a contemporary form of national personification in which countries are drawn <a href="/wiki/User-generated_content" title="User-generated content">by Internet users</a> as stereotypic balls and <a href="/wiki/Internet_meme" title="Internet meme">shared</a> as <a href="/wiki/Web_comic" class="mw-redirect" title="Web comic">comics</a> on <a href="/wiki/Online_communities" class="mw-redirect" title="Online communities">online communities</a>.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Hetalia:_Axis_Powers" title="Hetalia: Axis Powers">Hetalia: Axis Powers</a></i>, an <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> about personified countries interacting, mostly taking place within the World Wars.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mural_crown" title="Mural crown">Mural crown</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_animal" class="mw-redirect" title="National animal">National animal</a>, often personifies a nation in cartoons.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_emblem" title="National emblem">National emblem</a>, for other metaphors for nations.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_god" title="National god">National god</a>, a deity that embodies a nation.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_patron_saint" class="mw-redirect" title="National patron saint">National patron saint</a>, a Saint that embodies a nation.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Afghanis-tan" title="Afghanis-tan">Afghanis-tan</a>, a manga originally published as a <a href="/wiki/Webcomic" title="Webcomic">webcomic</a> about Central Asia with personified countries.</li></ul>
