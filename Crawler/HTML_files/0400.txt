<p>A <b>post town</b> is a required part of all postal addresses in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and a basic unit of the postal delivery system.<sup id="cite_ref-royal_mail_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royal_mail-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Including the correct post town in the address increases the chance of a letter or parcel being delivered on time. Post towns in general originated as the location of delivery offices. As of 2004<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Post_town&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, their main function is to distinguish between localities or street names in addresses not including a postcode.<sup id="cite_ref-code_2004_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-code_2004-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Post_town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are approximately 1,500 post towns which are organised by <a href="/wiki/Royal_Mail" title="Royal Mail">Royal Mail</a> subject to its policy only to impose changes where it has a proven, economic and practical benefit to the organisation, covering its own cost.<sup id="cite_ref-code_2004_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-code_2004-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Each post town usually corresponds to one or more <a href="/wiki/UK_postcodes" class="mw-redirect" title="UK postcodes">postal districts (the 'outward' part of the postcode, before the space)</a> therefore each post town can cover an area comprising many towns, urban districts and villages. Post towns rarely correspond exactly to administrative boundaries and their associated physical features.  As such they often group a small minority of neighbourhoods, streets or houses together with a main settlement in a different <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Counties of the United Kingdom">county</a>, area of local government or administration (including healthcare trust), <a href="/wiki/List_of_United_Kingdom_Parliament_constituencies" class="mw-redirect" title="List of United Kingdom Parliament constituencies">constituency</a>, <a href="/wiki/NUTS_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="NUTS of the United Kingdom">European statistical region</a> and/or traditional <a href="/wiki/Ecclesiastical_parish" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecclesiastical parish">parish</a>.  This was a main reason why <a href="/wiki/Postal_county" class="mw-redirect" title="Postal county">postal counties</a> were abolished (but not prohibited) in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-pem_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pem-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In some places several post towns cover a single postal district (with each post town corresponding to one or more of its postcode sectors). There are anomalies where post towns and postcode sectors have a more unusual co-relation. For example, the postcode sector EH14 5 is split between three post towns: <a href="/wiki/Juniper_Green" title="Juniper Green">Juniper Green</a>, <a href="/wiki/Currie" title="Currie">Currie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Balerno" title="Balerno">Balerno</a>. Its other postcode sectors are generally one of these, Balerno.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">Usage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Post_town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Usage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Royal Mail states that the post town must be included on all items and should be clearly written in capitals.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PAFcode_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAFcode-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dd>1 Vallance Road</dd>
<dd><b>LONDON</b></dd>
<dd>E2 1AA</dd></dl>
<p>The use of postcodes means that it is no longer necessary to include the former <a href="/wiki/Postal_county" class="mw-redirect" title="Postal county">postal county</a> in a postal address.  Some post towns, known as <a href="/wiki/Postal_counties_of_the_United_Kingdom#Special_post_towns" title="Postal counties of the United Kingdom">special post towns</a>, never required the inclusion of a postal county, either because the town was large or because it gave its name to the county.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Locality">Locality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Post_town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Locality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In most places, additional locality information such as a village or suburb name is optionally added above the post town, giving a more specific location: the two largest 'post town' cities named, for example, have many roads of the same name in different localities and the additional locality information is therefore essential if the postcode is wrongly recorded or unknown.
</p><p>Where this is a disambiguating feature, Royal Mail terms this the "dependent locality". For a limited number of addresses a "double dependent locality" line is also required, preceding the dependent locality line.
</p><p>Locality information other than the post town is not always part of the official postal address. In particular, within the <a href="/wiki/London_postal_district" title="London postal district">London</a> post town, each postcode district name (which can conflict with administrative boundaries, see above) corresponds to a numbered postcode district and is therefore not required in the postal address whenever the postcode is used. For example, "<a href="/wiki/Bethnal_Green" title="Bethnal Green">Bethnal Green</a>" is the name of the "<a href="/wiki/E_postcode_area#List_of_postcode_districts" title="E postcode area">E2</a>" postcode district and is optional in the following address:
</p>
<dl><dd>1 Vallance Road</dd>
<dd>Bethnal Green</dd>
<dd>LONDON</dd>
<dd>E2 1AA</dd></dl>
<p>If no valid postcode is provided, or if the sorting machine rejects the letter, the use of optional locality or county information may assist manual sorting.  In the absence of a full valid postcode, locality often prevents ambiguity where there is more than one street with the same name covered by a post town or postcode district, or where post towns in different counties have the same name.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Via">Via</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Post_town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Via">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Traditionally only, where a place such as a village was served by a post town entirely distinct from its location, the word "Via" or "Near" ("Nr.") was added before the post town. For example:
</p>
<dl><dd>1 High Street</dd>
<dd>Sewardstone</dd>
<dd>Via London</dd>
<dd>E4 1AA</dd></dl>
<p>However, the Royal Mail discourages this usage<sup id="cite_ref-royal_mail_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-royal_mail-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> because their <a href="/wiki/Optical_character_recognition" title="Optical character recognition">optical character recognition</a> technology and <a href="/wiki/Mailsort" title="Mailsort">Mailsort</a> lookup tables check for the post town at the beginning of a line if the postcode is missing, unreadable or incorrect. Additionally, "Near" and "Nr." can be confused with "North".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ambiguous_post_town_names">Ambiguous post town names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Post_town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ambiguous post town names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_post_towns_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of post towns in the United Kingdom">List of post towns in the United Kingdom</a></div>
<p>Post town names are unique within each <a href="/wiki/Former_postal_county" class="mw-redirect" title="Former postal county">former postal county</a> and each <a href="/wiki/List_of_postcode_areas_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of postcode areas in the United Kingdom">postcode area (the initial letter or two letters of the post code)</a>. But across the UK, some post towns have identical or similar names. For <a href="/wiki/Mailsort" title="Mailsort">Mailsort</a> purposes, post towns in unpostcoded addresses can be pre-sorted only if the first 10 characters of the post town name correspond unambiguously to only one post town. In addition, the following post towns have shared sorting routes or cover such large locations that the town name is insufficient for determining the relevant delivery area without reference to the postcode or further locality information:
</p>
<ul><li>Barnsley (S)</li>
<li>Belfast (BT)</li>
<li>Birmingham (B)</li>
<li>Bolton (BL)</li>
<li>Cardiff (CF)</li>
<li>Chesterfield (S)</li>
<li>Glasgow (G)</li>
<li>Leeds (LS)</li>
<li>London (E, EC, N, NW, SE, SW, W, WC)</li>
<li>Manchester (M)</li>
<li>Mansfield (NG)</li>
<li>Nottingham (NG)</li>
<li>Redditch (B)</li>
<li>Salford (M)</li>
<li>Sheffield (S)</li>
<li>Chester (CH)</li></ul>
