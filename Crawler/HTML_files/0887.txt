<p><b>Batley</b> is a town in the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Metropolitan Borough of Kirklees</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. It lies 7 miles (11&#160;km) south-east of <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, 7 miles (11&#160;km) south-west of <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and 1 mile (2&#160;km) north  of <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a>, near the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62 motorway</a>. <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, in 2011 its two wards (Batley East and Batley West) had a combined population of 38,573.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>   Other nearby towns include: <a href="/wiki/Morley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Morley, West Yorkshire">Morley</a> to the north-east, <a href="/wiki/Ossett" title="Ossett">Ossett</a> to the south-east and <a href="/wiki/Brighouse" title="Brighouse">Brighouse</a> west-south-west. Batley is part of a special <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">EU</a> transformation zone.
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hanging_Heaton,_St_Paul%27s_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226725.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Hanging_Heaton%2C_St_Paul%27s_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226725.jpg/220px-Hanging_Heaton%2C_St_Paul%27s_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226725.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="277" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Hanging_Heaton%2C_St_Paul%27s_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226725.jpg/330px-Hanging_Heaton%2C_St_Paul%27s_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226725.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a6/Hanging_Heaton%2C_St_Paul%27s_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226725.jpg 2x" data-file-width="343" data-file-height="432" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hanging_Heaton,_St_Paul%27s_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226725.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St Paul's Church in the Hanging Heaton area of Batley</div></div></div>
<p>Batley is recorded in the <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i> as 'Bateleia'. After the <a href="/wiki/Norman_conquest_of_England" title="Norman conquest of England">Norman conquest</a>, the manor was granted to <a href="/wiki/De_Lacy#Ilbert_de_Lacy" title="De Lacy">Elbert de Lacy</a> and in 1086 was within the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_(county_division)" title="Hundred (county division)">wapentake</a> of <a href="/wiki/Agbrigg_and_Morley" title="Agbrigg and Morley">Morley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It subsequently passed into the ownership of the de Batleys, and by the 12th century had passed by marriage to the Copley family. Their residence at Batley Hall was held directly from <a href="/wiki/The_Crown" title="The Crown">the Crown</a>; at this time the district fell within the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Lancaster" title="Duchy of Lancaster">Duchy of Lancaster</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  The population at this time was 30 to 40 people. By the late 14th century, the population has increased to around 100.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AllSaintsChurch-Batley.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/AllSaintsChurch-Batley.jpg/220px-AllSaintsChurch-Batley.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/AllSaintsChurch-Batley.jpg/330px-AllSaintsChurch-Batley.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/AllSaintsChurch-Batley.jpg/440px-AllSaintsChurch-Batley.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1182" data-file-height="1012" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AllSaintsChurch-Batley.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The church of All Saints</div></div></div>
<p>There has been a church in Batley since the 11th century. Batley Parish Church was built in the reign of <a href="/wiki/Henry_VI_of_England" title="Henry VI of England">Henry VI</a> (1422–1461), and parts of the original remain. Despite Batley being an ancient settlement, this is all that remains of any great antiquity.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Howley_Hall" title="Howley Hall">Howley Hall</a> in Soothill was built during the 1580s by Sir John Savile, a member of the great Yorkshire landowners, the Savile family. The house was besieged during the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a> in 1643 before the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Adwalton_Moor" title="Battle of Adwalton Moor">Battle of Adwalton Moor</a> but appears to have sustained no serious damage. It continued to be occupied during the 17th century but fell into disrepair. Howley Hall was demolished in 1730. Many ruins exist including the cellars of its great hall.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Batley_Grammar_School" title="Batley Grammar School">Batley Grammar School</a> was founded in 1612 by the Rev. William Lee and is still in existence.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodism</a> came to Batley in the 1740s and took a strong hold in the town which continued into the 20th century. John Nelson from <a href="/wiki/Birstall,_West_Yorkshire" title="Birstall, West Yorkshire">Birstall</a> was a leading lay preacher in the early Methodist movement. Areas of the town, such as Mount Pleasant, were noted for their absence of <a href="/wiki/Public_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Public house">pubs</a> due to the Methodist beliefs of the populations.
</p><p>During the late 18th century the main occupations in the town were <a href="/wiki/Farming" class="mw-redirect" title="Farming">farming</a> and <a href="/wiki/Weaving" title="Weaving">weaving</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a> reached Batley in 1796 with the arrival of its first water powered mills for carding and spinning. During the next half century the population grew rapidly, from around 2,500 at the start of the 19th century to 9,308 at the 1851 <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a>. The parish of Batley at this point included <a href="/wiki/Morley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Morley, West Yorkshire">Morley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Churwell" title="Churwell">Churwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gildersome" title="Gildersome">Gildersome</a>, with a total population of 17,359.
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Toll_road" title="Toll road">toll road</a> built in 1832 between <a href="/wiki/Gomersal" title="Gomersal">Gomersal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a> had a branch to Batley (the present day Branch Road) which allowed for "the growing volumes of wool, cloth and coal" to be transported. Until then there had only been foot and cart tracks. Around the same time there were <a href="/wiki/Strike_action" title="Strike action">strikes</a> in the mills, which led to an influx of <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> workers who settled permanently. Initially this led to antagonism from residents, due to the lower wages demanded by the Irish workers and general anti-<a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> sentiment, but this faded in time. By 1853 Catholic services were held regularly in the town; its first Roman Catholic church, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.stmarybatley.co.uk/">St Mary of the Angels</a>, was not built until 1870 and is still in existence.
</p><p>By 1848 there was a <a href="/wiki/Batley_railway_station" title="Batley railway station">railway station</a> in Batley, and in 1853 Batley <a href="/wiki/Town_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Town Hall">Town Hall</a> was erected. It was enlarged in 1905, and is in the <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">Neoclassical style</a>, with a corbelled <a href="/wiki/Parapet" title="Parapet">parapet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pilaster" title="Pilaster">pilasters</a> rising to a centre <a href="/wiki/Pediment" title="Pediment">pediment</a>. In 1868 Batley was incorporated as a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a>, the former <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Birstall,_West_Yorkshire" title="Birstall, West Yorkshire">Birstall</a> was added to it in 1937.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>1853 also saw the establishment of a small <a href="/wiki/Confectionery" title="Confectionery">confectionery</a> shop by Michael Spedding. His business expanded, moving to larger premises in 1927 becoming <a href="/wiki/Fox%27s_Biscuits" title="Fox&#39;s Biscuits">Fox's Biscuits</a>. Today, along with <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a>, it is one of the largest employers in the town.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Central_Methodist_Church_-_Commercial_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_487177.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Central_Methodist_Church_-_Commercial_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_487177.jpg/220px-Central_Methodist_Church_-_Commercial_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_487177.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="175" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Central_Methodist_Church_-_Commercial_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_487177.jpg/330px-Central_Methodist_Church_-_Commercial_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_487177.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Central_Methodist_Church_-_Commercial_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_487177.jpg/440px-Central_Methodist_Church_-_Commercial_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_487177.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="508" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Central_Methodist_Church_-_Commercial_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_487177.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Central Methodist Church - Zion Chapel</div></div></div>
<p>During the late 19th century, Batley was the centre of the <a href="/wiki/Shoddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Shoddy">shoddy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_textile_manufacturing#Mungo" title="Glossary of textile manufacturing">mungo</a> trade in which <a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">wool</a> rags and clothes were recycled by reweaving them into blankets, carpets and uniforms. In 1861 there were at least 30 shoddy mills in Batley. The owners of the recycling businesses were known as the "shoddy barons". There was a "shoddy king" and a "shoddy temple", properly known as the Zion Chapel. This imposing building in the town centre was opened in 1870, and reflected the popularity of the Methodist movement. The chapel is still active today.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> At the close of the 19th century, growth in population changed the form of governmental institutions above the parish of Batley; the Morley division of the wapentake of <a href="/wiki/Agbrigg_and_Morley" title="Agbrigg and Morley">Agbrigg and Morley</a> was disused as special purpose districts were formed.
</p><p>The library was built in 1907 with funds donated by the <a href="/wiki/Philanthropist" class="mw-redirect" title="Philanthropist">philanthropist</a> <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Carnegie" title="Andrew Carnegie">Andrew Carnegie</a>. The library has been modernised, with a new microfilm viewer, and reels of the Batley News dating back over 120 years. The newspaper was founded by James Fearnsides – a local printer. His grandson Clement, later became the mayor of Batley. There was coal mining in Batley at this time. The first records of <a href="/wiki/Coal_mining" title="Coal mining">coal mining</a> in Batley date back to the 16th century at White Lee; the last pit in the town closed in 1973.
</p><p>From the end of the 1950s, the need for cheap labour in the town's textile industries drew in migrant labourers from <a href="/wiki/Gujarat" title="Gujarat">Gujarat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punjab_region" class="mw-redirect" title="Punjab region">Punjab</a> and other parts of modern-day <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.
</p><p>In 1974 responsibility for local government passed to <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees Metropolitan Council</a>, with its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From the end of the 1950s, the need for cheap labour in the town's textile industries drew in migrant labourers from Gujarat, Punjab and other parts of modern-day Pakistan and India. The South Asian population of Batley is now around 33% in Batley West and 54% in Batley East.<sup id="cite_ref-census_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-census-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shopping">Shopping</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Shopping">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Commercial_Street,_Batley.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Commercial_Street%2C_Batley.jpg/220px-Commercial_Street%2C_Batley.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Commercial_Street%2C_Batley.jpg/330px-Commercial_Street%2C_Batley.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Commercial_Street%2C_Batley.jpg/440px-Commercial_Street%2C_Batley.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Commercial_Street,_Batley.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Commercial Street</div></div></div>
<p>Batley is home to a modest commercial and retail centre along Commercial Street. The largest shop here is <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a> which is linked to the main shopping street by a footbridge. There are several <a href="/wiki/Chain_stores" class="mw-redirect" title="Chain stores">chain stores</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Boots_the_Chemist" class="mw-redirect" title="Boots the Chemist">Boots the Chemist</a> closed in May 2015. Before the chain collapsed, Batley also had a <a href="/wiki/Woolworths_Group" title="Woolworths Group">Woolworth's</a> store, which has now become a small <a href="/wiki/Variety_store" title="Variety store">pound shop</a> called JBM bargains.
</p><p>Batley Shopping Centre is a small shopping-centre which has become somewhat run-down since being acquired by Tesco. The supermarket signpost the shopping centre as a thoroughfare to their supermarket. The main retailers in the shopping centre are <a href="/wiki/Iceland_(retailer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Iceland (retailer)">Iceland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asda" title="Asda">Asda</a>. They are currently surrounded by many vacant shops.
</p><p>Renovated former textile mills form the major part of shopping in Batley. The Mill, Batley<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> is one of the UK's largest retail mills and is a <a href="/wiki/Factory_outlet" class="mw-redirect" title="Factory outlet">factory outlet</a> or shopping village with many national and independent fashion and homeware operators. The opening of The Mill, Batley, in 2000, provided a significant contribution to the area's employment and economic status. Redbrick Mill houses many top-end furniture retailers such as <a href="/wiki/Heal%27s" title="Heal&#39;s">Heal's</a> and Multi York and local textile manufacturers Skopos. Shackletons occupy one of the many former mills along Bradford Road. This 'golden mile' of retail is collectively known as the Yorkshire Mill Mile.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Batley_bus_station">Batley bus station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Batley bus station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Batleybusstation999.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Batleybusstation999.JPG/220px-Batleybusstation999.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Batleybusstation999.JPG/330px-Batleybusstation999.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Batleybusstation999.JPG/440px-Batleybusstation999.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Batleybusstation999.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Batley bus station</div></div></div>
<p><b>Batley bus station</b> serves the town and is owned and maintained by <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Passenger_Transport_Executive" class="mw-redirect" title="West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive">Metro (West Yorkshire PTE)</a>.
</p><p>The bus station is situated in Batley town centre and can be accessed from Bradford Road and St. James's Street. It was re-built by Metro in April 2005 replacing the previously owned <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Yorkshire" title="Arriva Yorkshire">Arriva Yorkshire</a> site. There are six stands and a real-time information board at the bus station. Arriva Yorkshire is the main operator.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The main operator from Batley bus station is Arriva Yorkshire. Buses run from the bus station around <a href="/wiki/Heavy_Woollen_District" title="Heavy Woollen District">Heavy Woollen District</a> (North Kirklees area) and go as far as <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_bus_station" title="Huddersfield bus station">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange" title="Bradford Interchange">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_bus_station" title="Leeds City bus station">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_bus_station" title="Wakefield bus station">Wakefield</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport_and_culture">Sport and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sport and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fox%27s_Biscuits_Stadium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Fox%27s_Biscuits_Stadium.jpg/220px-Fox%27s_Biscuits_Stadium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="292" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Fox%27s_Biscuits_Stadium.jpg/330px-Fox%27s_Biscuits_Stadium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Fox%27s_Biscuits_Stadium.jpg/440px-Fox%27s_Biscuits_Stadium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2340" data-file-height="3111" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fox%27s_Biscuits_Stadium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Batley_Bulldogs" title="Batley Bulldogs">Batley Bulldogs</a>' <a href="/wiki/Fox%27s_Biscuits_Stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="Fox&#39;s Biscuits Stadium">Mount Pleasant</a> in 2016</div></div></div>
<p>The town is home to the professional <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> club <a href="/wiki/Batley_Bulldogs" title="Batley Bulldogs">Batley RLFC</a> and junior football club Batley Juniors F.C. (formerly Carlinghow Boys F.C.) Carlinghow is also located in Batley.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Wilton_Park,_Batley" title="Wilton Park, Batley">Wilton Park</a> (Batley Park) is a large <a href="/wiki/Park" title="Park">park</a> between the town centre and <a href="/wiki/Birstall,_West_Yorkshire" title="Birstall, West Yorkshire">Birstall</a>. In its grounds are the Milner K. Ford <a href="/wiki/Observatory" title="Observatory">Observatory</a> (built in 1966 and home to the Batley &amp; Spenborough Astronomical Society) and <a href="/wiki/Bagshaw_Museum" title="Bagshaw Museum">Bagshaw Museum</a>. The museum is located in a house built by the "shoddy baron", George Sheard, and features local history, <a href="/wiki/Natural_history" title="Natural history">natural history</a>, curios from around the world, and an <a href="/wiki/History_of_ancient_Egypt" title="History of ancient Egypt">Ancient Egyptian</a> exhibition. The museum (originally the Wilton Park Museum) is named after its first <a href="/wiki/Curator" title="Curator">curator</a> Walter Bagshaw, a Batley councillor and extensive traveller.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bagshaw_Museum.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Bagshaw_Museum.JPG/220px-Bagshaw_Museum.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Bagshaw_Museum.JPG/330px-Bagshaw_Museum.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Bagshaw_Museum.JPG/440px-Bagshaw_Museum.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1920" data-file-height="2560" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bagshaw_Museum.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bagshaw Museum, with its distinctive copper tower, was originally the home of the Sheard family</div></div></div>
<p>The Yorkshire Motor Museum had a small but varied collection of cars dating back to 1885, and reflecting local car makers as well as more famous marques. The museum closed in 2010.
</p><p>Batley Art Gallery, in the Batley Library building, features contemporary <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a>, <a href="/wiki/Craft" title="Craft">craft</a> and <a href="/wiki/Photography" title="Photography">photography</a>.
</p><p>Between 1966 and 1977 the <a href="/wiki/Batley_Variety_Club" title="Batley Variety Club">Batley Variety Club</a> was frequented by many notable acts including <a href="/wiki/Louis_Armstrong" title="Louis Armstrong">Louis Armstrong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Mathis" title="Johnny Mathis">Johnny Mathis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eartha_Kitt" title="Eartha Kitt">Eartha Kitt</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Bee_Gees" title="Bee Gees">Bee Gees</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roy_Orbison" title="Roy Orbison">Roy Orbison</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Hollies" title="The Hollies">the Hollies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Richard" title="Cliff Richard">Cliff Richard</a> among others. For a brief period it was named <i>the Crumpet</i>, after which it was closed for four years surviving numerous applications to have the building demolished. The club was then known as the Frontier nightclub from the late 1970s onwards. The Frontier was sold to businessmen in April 2005 and continued to operate as a nightclub whilst hosting variety shows and sporting events such as boxing, snooker and darts. The Frontier closed its doors for the final time in 2016 and following a £2 million refurbishment was successfully transformed into JD gym. 
</p><p><br />
In <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a>, Batley has several local teams, and is also part of the cricket association for the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_Woollen_District" title="Heavy Woollen District">Heavy Woollen District</a>. The original definition of the latter area was to within a six-mile radius of Batley Town Hall. The Heavy Woollen Cup can now be entered by any team within 18 miles of Batley, but there is an upper limit of 64 teams.
</p><p>A dramatic society was founded in October 1913 at Shelton's café at 53 Commercial Street to present dramatic works to raise funds for Batley and District Hospital. On 8 January 1914 at a meeting in the Temperance Hall, it was decided that it would be known as the “Batley Amateur Thespian Society” and it became affiliated with the <a href="/wiki/National_Operatic_and_Dramatic_Association" title="National Operatic and Dramatic Association">National Operatic and Dramatic Association</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Batley includes the districts of <a href="/wiki/Batley_Carr" title="Batley Carr">Batley Carr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlinghow" title="Carlinghow">Carlinghow</a>, Cross Bank, <a href="/wiki/Hanging_Heaton" title="Hanging Heaton">Hanging Heaton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Healey,_Kirklees" title="Healey, Kirklees">Healey</a>, Lamplands, Carlton Grange <a href="/wiki/Mount_Pleasant,_Batley" title="Mount Pleasant, Batley">Mount Pleasant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soothill" title="Soothill">Soothill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Staincliffe" title="Staincliffe">Staincliffe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upper_Batley" title="Upper Batley">Upper Batley</a> and White Lee.
</p><p>As Batley shares boundaries with both <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heckmondwike" title="Heckmondwike">Heckmondwike</a>, parts of Batley Carr, Hanging Heaton and Staincliffe are part of Dewsbury, while part of White Lee is in <a href="/wiki/Heckmondwike" title="Heckmondwike">Heckmondwike</a>. There is an area of Ossett known as <a href="/wiki/Healey,_Ossett" title="Healey, Ossett">Healey</a>, which is identical in name to the Batley district of Healey; the Ossett area is sometimes referred to as "Healey Mills" due to the very large congregation of mills that once existed in that area.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Monty_Python%27s_Flying_Circus" title="Monty Python&#39;s Flying Circus">Monty Python's Flying Circus</a></i> had a series of recurring sketches in which the members of the Batley Ladies Townswomen's Guild would present famous plays or musicals, or re-enact various historical battles (such as the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">Battle of Pearl Harbour</a>), by charging at each other, swinging handbags and wrestling in the mud.
</p><p>Batley is also the setting<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> for the children's television programme <i><a href="/wiki/My_Spy_Family" title="My Spy Family">My Spy Family</a></i>.
</p><p>Batley was used for location filming of the fictional town of Barfield in the 1955 film <i><a href="/wiki/Value_for_Money" title="Value for Money">Value for Money</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/John_Gregson" title="John Gregson">John Gregson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Diana_Dors" title="Diana Dors">Diana Dors</a>.
</p><p>Wilton Park was used for scenes in the 1997 film <i><a href="/wiki/My_Son_the_Fanatic_(film)" title="My Son the Fanatic (film)">My Son the Fanatic</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Om_Puri" title="Om Puri">Om Puri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rachel_Griffiths" title="Rachel Griffiths">Rachel Griffiths</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stellan_Skarsg%C3%A5rd" title="Stellan Skarsgård">Stellan Skarsgård</a>.
</p>
