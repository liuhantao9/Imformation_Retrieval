<p><b>River Island</b> is a London-headquartered high street fashion brand, which operates in a number of worldwide markets. Set up in 1948, by <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Lewis_(entrepreneur)" title="Bernard Lewis (entrepreneur)">Bernard Lewis</a> and his brothers in London, River Island is a <a href="/wiki/Private_company" class="mw-redirect" title="Private company">private company</a> owned by the Lewis family.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:River_Island_at_Castlepoint.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/River_Island_at_Castlepoint.jpg/220px-River_Island_at_Castlepoint.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/River_Island_at_Castlepoint.jpg/330px-River_Island_at_Castlepoint.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/River_Island_at_Castlepoint.jpg/440px-River_Island_at_Castlepoint.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="1944" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:River_Island_at_Castlepoint.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Store front at <a href="/wiki/Castlepoint_Shopping_Centre" title="Castlepoint Shopping Centre">Castlepoint Shopping Centre</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>In 1948, Bernard Lewis started selling <a href="/wiki/Grocery_store" title="Grocery store">fruit and vegetables</a>, and then <a href="/wiki/Knitting" title="Knitting">knitting</a> <a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">wool</a> from a bombed-out site in the <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East End of London</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Telg8392014_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telg8392014-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Joined by his three brothers,<sup id="cite_ref-Telg8392014_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telg8392014-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the chain expanded to nine stores under the title Lewis Separates.<sup id="cite_ref-TelgHist_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TelgHist-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By 1965 Lewis Separates had a 70 store empire in the UK and it was decided that the brand needed a new identity. Lewis Separates was re-branded to Chelsea Girl,<sup id="cite_ref-TelgHist_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TelgHist-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> the name chosen because at the time the <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Road" title="King&#39;s Road">King's Road</a> in <a href="/wiki/Chelsea,_London" title="Chelsea, London">Chelsea</a> was the hub of fashion, music and popular culture.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Chelsea Girl became the first UK fashion boutique chain.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In the late 1970s, two of the brothers left the business,<sup id="cite_ref-TelgHist_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TelgHist-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> leaving Bernard, David and their families in charge. They formed the Lewis Trust Group, for which David ran the other businesses covering <a href="/wiki/Hotels" class="mw-redirect" title="Hotels">hotels</a>, property and investments, leaving Bernard to run the retail operation. This allowed the company to expand into menswear in 1982 with the launch of Concept Man stores,<sup id="cite_ref-TelgHist_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TelgHist-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> before they were merged with Chelsea Girl to become one retail brand under the name of River Island in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-TelgHist_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TelgHist-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1993, the brand opened its first shop in Republic of Ireland. Since then it has expanded into a dozen markets including Russia, Poland, and various countries in the Middle East.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2010, River Island launched Kidswear. In 2011, Chelsea Girl was re-introduced as a capsule collection within River Island stores.<sup id="cite_ref-Telg8392014_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telg8392014-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stores">Stores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stores">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>River Island has over 350 stores in the UK, Ireland, Asia, the Middle East and Europe alongside 6 online websites that operates in 4 currencies, which ships to over 100 countries worldwide. <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.riverisland.com/inside-river-island/about-us">[1]</a>
</p><p>As of 2012, River Island no longer has physical shops in Singapore. In 2013, online fashion retailer Zalora began acting as a bridge between River Island and most of South-East Asia.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In spring / autumn 2015 River Island will open stores in <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>.
