	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Longroyd Bridge</h1>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Longroyd_Bridge_Huddersfield.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Longroyd_Bridge_Huddersfield.jpg/400px-Longroyd_Bridge_Huddersfield.jpg" decoding="async" width="400" height="214" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Longroyd_Bridge_Huddersfield.jpg/600px-Longroyd_Bridge_Huddersfield.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Longroyd_Bridge_Huddersfield.jpg/800px-Longroyd_Bridge_Huddersfield.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="642" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Longroyd_Bridge_Huddersfield.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Longroyd Bridge showing route of A62 over the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Narrow_Canal" title="Huddersfield Narrow Canal">Canal</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Colne, West Yorkshire">River Colne</a></div></div></div>
<p><b>Longroyd Bridge</b> is a suburb approximately 1200 yds (1.1&#160;km) to the southwest of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> town centre, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. The area is composed of industrial and commercial units. There is little housing in the commercial area, though within a few hundred yards are the housing areas of <a href="/wiki/Thornton_Lodge,_Huddersfield" class="mw-redirect" title="Thornton Lodge, Huddersfield">Thornton Lodge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paddock,_Huddersfield" title="Paddock, Huddersfield">Paddock</a>.
</p><p>The dominating feature of the area is the <a href="/wiki/Viaduct" title="Viaduct">viaduct</a> carrying the <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>—Huddersfield <a href="/wiki/Penistone_Line" title="Penistone Line">Penistone Line</a> railway over the valley of the <a href="/wiki/River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Colne, West Yorkshire">River Colne</a> from <a href="/wiki/Lockwood_railway_station" title="Lockwood railway station">the station at Lockwood</a> to Springwood Junction and tunnel where the line continues under Greenhead to arrive at <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield railway station</a>.
</p><p>Longroyd Bridge is where the <a href="/wiki/A62_road" title="A62 road">A62</a>, Manchester Road, route to <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> passes over the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Narrow_Canal" title="Huddersfield Narrow Canal">Huddersfield Narrow Canal</a> and the River Colne, it then travels down through the <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley" title="Colne Valley">Colne Valley</a> passing <a href="/wiki/Milnsbridge" title="Milnsbridge">Milnsbridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linthwaite" title="Linthwaite">Linthwaite</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Slaithwaite" title="Slaithwaite">Slaithwaite</a> to <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a> before crossing the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennine</a> hills to <a href="/wiki/Oldham" title="Oldham">Oldham</a> and Manchester.
</p><p>The Huddersfield Narrow canal has been extensively reconstructed over the last few years, with long infilled stretches dug out again. In places this has required major civil engineering work to route the canal under buildings that had been constructed over the canal when it became disused. Also to replace newly constructed lock gates.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Within the first year after re-opening the canal drained out, unexpectedly, leaving some narrowboats and leisure craft stranded for several days. Then again in early 2009 there was a leak, which again stranded boats. <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
