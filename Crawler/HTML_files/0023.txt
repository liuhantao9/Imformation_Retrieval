<p>The <b>Association for the Advancement of Artificial Intelligence</b> (<b>AAAI</b>) is an international scientific society devoted to promote research in, and responsible use of, <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a>. AAAI also aims to increase public understanding of artificial intelligence (AI), improve the teaching and training of AI practitioners, and provide guidance for research planners and funders concerning the importance and potential of current AI developments and future directions.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_the_Advancement_of_Artificial_Intelligence&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The organization was founded in 1979 under the name "American Association for Artificial Intelligence" and changed its name in 2007 to "Association for the Advancement of Artificial Intelligence". It has in excess of 4,000 members worldwide<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. In its early history, the organization was presided over by notable figures in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Allen_Newell" title="Allen Newell">Allen Newell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Feigenbaum" title="Edward Feigenbaum">Edward Feigenbaum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marvin_Minsky" title="Marvin Minsky">Marvin Minsky</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_McCarthy_(computer_scientist)" title="John McCarthy (computer scientist)">John McCarthy</a>.  The current<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (November 2018)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> president is Subbarao Kambhampati, and the president elect is <a href="/wiki/Yolanda_Gil" title="Yolanda Gil">Yolanda Gil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conferences_and_publications">Conferences and publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_the_Advancement_of_Artificial_Intelligence&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conferences and publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The AAAI provides many services to the Artificial Intelligence community.  The AAAI sponsors many conferences and symposia each year as well as providing support to 14 journals in the field of artificial intelligence.  AAAI produces a quarterly publication, <i><a href="/wiki/AI_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="AI Magazine">AI Magazine</a></i>, which seeks to publish significant new research and literature across the entire field of artificial intelligence and to help members to keep abreast of research outside their immediate specialties. The magazine has been published continuously since 1980.
</p><p>AAAI organises the "AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence",<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which is considered to be one of the top conferences in the field of artificial intelligence.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In addition to <a href="/wiki/AAAI_Fellow" title="AAAI Fellow">AAAI Fellowship</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-faaai_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-faaai-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> the AAAI grants several other awards:

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ACM-AAAI_Allen_Newell_Award">ACM-AAAI Allen Newell Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_the_Advancement_of_Artificial_Intelligence&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: ACM-AAAI Allen Newell Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The ACM-AAAI Allen Newell Award is presented to an individual selected for career contributions that have breadth within computer science, or that bridge computer science and other disciplines. This endowed award is accompanied by a prize of $10,000, and is supported by the Association for the Advancement of Artificial Intelligence (AAAI), <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a> (ACM), and by individual contributions.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span id="AAAI.2FEAAI_Outstanding_Educator_Award"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="AAAI/EAAI_Outstanding_Educator_Award">AAAI/EAAI Outstanding Educator Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Association_for_the_Advancement_of_Artificial_Intelligence&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: AAAI/EAAI Outstanding Educator Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The annual AAAI/EAAI Outstanding Educator Award was created in 2016 to honor a person (or group of people) who has made major contributions to AI education that provide long-lasting benefits to the AI community.
