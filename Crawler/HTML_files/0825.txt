<p><b>Fraizer Lee Campbell</b> (born 13 September 1987) is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Striker_(association_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Striker (association football)">striker</a> for <a href="/wiki/EFL_Championship" title="EFL Championship">Championship</a> club <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a>. He has previously played for <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Antwerp_F.C." title="Royal Antwerp F.C.">Royal Antwerp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a>.
</p><p>A product of Manchester United's youth academy, Campbell progressed to their first team in the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Manchester_United_F.C._season" title="2006–07 Manchester United F.C. season">2006–07 season</a>. He made four appearances without scoring in his tenure at the club. He had a <a href="/wiki/Loan_(sports)" title="Loan (sports)">loan</a> spell at Belgian club Royal Antwerp, where he scored 24 goals in 38 appearances. He also had loan periods with Hull City and Tottenham Hotspur, where he scored 15 goals in 37 matches and three goals in 22 appearances respectively. He signed for Sunderland at the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Sunderland_A.F.C._season" title="2009–10 Sunderland A.F.C. season">2009–10 season</a> for £3.5&#160;million. His involvement at Sunderland was limited due to an <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament_injury" title="Anterior cruciate ligament injury">anterior cruciate ligament injury</a> he sustained in his <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Sunderland_A.F.C._season" title="2010–11 Sunderland A.F.C. season">second season</a> at the club, as well as a recurrence of the same problem later in the season. He moved to Cardiff in January 2013 on a three-and-a-half-year deal, and then Crystal Palace 18 months later.
</p><p>Having previously represented England from under-16 to under-21 level, Campbell earned his first cap for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">senior team</a> in 2012.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, West Yorkshire, Campbell grew up in a Manchester United-supporting household and studied at <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Grammar_School" title="Huddersfield Grammar School">Huddersfield Grammar School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As a child Campbell had a short spell at <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a>'s Centre of Excellence, but he was scouted by Manchester United at the age of 10.<sup id="cite_ref-programme_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-programme-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He also played for Stile Common.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Manchester_United">Manchester United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Manchester United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_career">Youth career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Youth career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Campbell,_Fraizer.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Campbell%2C_Fraizer.jpg/170px-Campbell%2C_Fraizer.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="113" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Campbell%2C_Fraizer.jpg/255px-Campbell%2C_Fraizer.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/7c/Campbell%2C_Fraizer.jpg 2x" data-file-width="300" data-file-height="200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Campbell,_Fraizer.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Campbell with <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C._Reserves_and_Academy" title="Manchester United F.C. Reserves and Academy">Manchester United reserves</a> in 2007</div></div></div>
<p>Having been in their youth schemes since the age of 10, Campbell signed for the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C._Reserves_and_Academy" title="Manchester United F.C. Reserves and Academy">Manchester United Academy</a> on 1 July 2004.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He made an immediate impact, scoring 14 goals in 22 starts for the Under-18s in the 2004–05 season.<sup id="cite_ref-programme_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-programme-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> His performances for the youth team also led to him playing five times for the reserves, for whom he scored once.<sup id="cite_ref-programme_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-programme-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He signed his first professional contract with Manchester United on 22 March 2006,<sup id="cite_ref-programme_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-programme-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and he was named as a <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> for <a href="/wiki/Roy_Keane" title="Roy Keane">Roy Keane</a>'s testimonial match at <a href="/wiki/Old_Trafford" title="Old Trafford">Old Trafford</a> on 9 May 2006, in which he was a 75th minute replacement for <a href="/wiki/Kieran_Richardson" title="Kieran Richardson">Kieran Richardson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Later that summer, he scored his first goal for the club after coming on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Rooney" title="Wayne Rooney">Wayne Rooney</a> in a friendly against <a href="/wiki/Macclesfield_Town_F.C." title="Macclesfield Town F.C.">Macclesfield Town</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="Royal_Antwerp_.28loan.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Royal_Antwerp_(loan)">Royal Antwerp (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Royal Antwerp (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Campbell joined Manchester United's Belgian partner club <a href="/wiki/Royal_Antwerp_F.C." title="Royal Antwerp F.C.">Royal Antwerp</a> for the duration of the 2006–07 season, where his goal-scoring exploits resulted in the fans giving him the nickname "Super Campbell".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> His 21 goals in 31 starts helped Antwerp to a place in the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Second_Division" title="Belgian Second Division">Belgian Second Division</a> play-offs.<sup id="cite_ref-programme_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-programme-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After returning to Manchester United from his loan deal, Campbell scored a volley against <a href="/wiki/Glentoran_F.C." title="Glentoran F.C.">Glentoran</a> on 8 August 2007 in a pre-season friendly.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He made his senior debut for United on 19 August 2007 in the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_derby" title="Manchester derby">Manchester derby</a>, after coming on in the 73rd minute for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Carrick" title="Michael Carrick">Michael Carrick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="Hull_City_.28loan.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Hull_City_(loan)">Hull City (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Hull City (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fraizer_Campbell_(2).jpg" class="image"><img alt="Fraizer Campbell playing for Hull City in 2008" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Fraizer_Campbell_%282%29.jpg/170px-Fraizer_Campbell_%282%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="204" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Fraizer_Campbell_%282%29.jpg/255px-Fraizer_Campbell_%282%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Fraizer_Campbell_%282%29.jpg/340px-Fraizer_Campbell_%282%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1781" data-file-height="2135" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fraizer_Campbell_(2).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Campbell (left) playing for <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> in 2008</div></div></div>
<p>Campbell was sent out on loan again in the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Hull_City_A.F.C._season" title="2007–08 Hull City A.F.C. season">2007–08 season</a>, this time at <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> in the <a href="/wiki/EFL_Championship" title="EFL Championship">Championship</a>. He initially joined Hull in October 2007 on loan until January 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and scored twice on his home debut in a 3–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Following his match-winning display against <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> on <a href="/wiki/Boxing_Day" title="Boxing Day">Boxing Day</a> 2007, in which he scored one goal and assisted another,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Hull expressed their interest in extending Campbell's loan until the end of the season. The loan extension was completed on 28 December 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Campbell finished the season as Hull's top scorer, with 15 goals in 32 appearances.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On 24 May 2008, he played in the Hull City team that achieved promotion to the top flight for the first time in their 104-year history. Campbell provided the assist for <a href="/wiki/Dean_Windass" title="Dean Windass">Dean Windass</a>' goal in a 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Bristol_City_F.C." title="Bristol City F.C.">Bristol City</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2008_Football_League_Championship_play-off_Final" title="2008 Football League Championship play-off Final">Championship play-off Final</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PO2008_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PO2008-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hull expressed a strong desire to retain Campbell's services for the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Hull_City_A.F.C._season" title="2008–09 Hull City A.F.C. season">2008–09</a> season, with Hull chairman, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Duffen" title="Paul Duffen">Paul Duffen</a>, describing Campbell as "too good for the Championship".<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Following his successes leading to their promotion, Hull were hoping to secure either a permanent move or another season-long loan, depending on Manchester United's willingness to let him go.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> However, Campbell repeatedly expressed the desire to return to Old Trafford and try to break into the first team, saying "Now I'm back at United, the plan is to try to force my way into the first team." He added, "I'm going to continue to work hard and try to do enough to stay here and go on from there."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Manchester_United">Return to Manchester United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Return to Manchester United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In July 2008, Campbell was selected for the pre-season tour of South Africa with the Manchester United first team, and scored the team's fourth goal in a 4–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Kaizer_Chiefs_F.C." title="Kaizer Chiefs F.C.">Kaizer Chiefs</a> in the final of the 2008 Vodacom Challenge.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He also scored the winning goal in <a href="/wiki/Ole_Gunnar_Solskj%C3%A6r" title="Ole Gunnar Solskjær">Ole Gunnar Solskjær</a>'s testimonial match against <a href="/wiki/RCD_Espanyol" title="RCD Espanyol">Espanyol</a> on 2 August 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> After the match, United manager <a href="/wiki/Alex_Ferguson" title="Alex Ferguson">Alex Ferguson</a> indicated that Campbell would remain at the club for the duration of the season, stating "Fraizer's future is here".<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Campbell earned his first winner's medal when he came on as a substitute in United's penalty shoot-out win over <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2008_FA_Community_Shield" title="2008 FA Community Shield">2008 FA Community Shield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FACS2008_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FACS2008-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On 17 August 2008, Campbell started alongside Wayne Rooney for the opening match of the season in a 1–1 draw against <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tottenham_Hotspur">Tottenham Hotspur</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tottenham Hotspur">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fraizer_Campbell_(1).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Fraizer_Campbell_%281%29.jpg/170px-Fraizer_Campbell_%281%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="316" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Fraizer_Campbell_%281%29.jpg/255px-Fraizer_Campbell_%281%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Fraizer_Campbell_%281%29.jpg/340px-Fraizer_Campbell_%281%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="720" data-file-height="1340" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fraizer_Campbell_(1).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Campbell playing for <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> in 2008</div></div></div>
<p>On 1 September 2008, <a href="/wiki/Transfer_window" title="Transfer window">transfer deadline day</a>, Hull City bid a club record fee of £7&#160;million for Campbell.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> However, he instead signed for <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> on a season-long loan, as part of the transfer of <a href="/wiki/Dimitar_Berbatov" title="Dimitar Berbatov">Dimitar Berbatov</a> to Manchester United for £30.75&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> This was contradictory to Ferguson's earlier claim that Campbell would be staying at United for the season. He explained the deal saying; "Tottenham insisted that he be part of the deal and Fraizer's signed his own agreement to go there for the year so we're happy with that."<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> He made his Tottenham debut on 18 September 2008, coming on as a 56th-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Lennon" title="Aaron Lennon">Aaron Lennon</a> in a <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_UEFA_Cup" title="2008–09 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> first-round first-leg match against <a href="/wiki/Wis%C5%82a_Krak%C3%B3w" title="Wisła Kraków">Wisła Kraków</a>. Within 15 minutes of coming on, Campbell provided <a href="/wiki/Darren_Bent" title="Darren Bent">Darren Bent</a> with the assist for the winning goal.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Campbell scored his first two goals for Tottenham in a 4–2 victory over <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> in the <a href="/wiki/EFL_Cup" title="EFL Cup">League Cup</a> fourth round, as well as setting up his strike partner <a href="/wiki/Roman_Pavlyuchenko" title="Roman Pavlyuchenko">Roman Pavlyuchenko</a> for the match's opening goal.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On 15 November 2008, Campbell scored his first ever <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> goal late in a 2–1 defeat to <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hull City refused to give up on Campbell and in June 2009 they returned with a £6&#160;million bid for Campbell, which was accepted by Manchester United.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> However, the striker said he would not decide on his future until completion of <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England</a>'s participation in the <a href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2009 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sunderland">Sunderland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sunderland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 July 2009, Campbell signed a four-year contract with <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> after Manchester United had accepted a £3.5&#160;million bid (potentially rising to £6&#160;million) after successfully passing a medical earlier that same day.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club in a 2–0 League Cup victory over <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> On 28 December 2009, Campbell partnered Darren Bent in attack against <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> at <a href="/wiki/Ewood_Park" title="Ewood Park">Ewood Park</a> in the 2–2 draw.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Campbell scored twice in the <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> against non-League team <a href="/wiki/Barrow_A.F.C." title="Barrow A.F.C.">Barrow</a> on 2 January 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On 9 March 2010, Campbell scored his first Premier League goal for Sunderland, scoring against <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton</a> after just 41 seconds in a 4–0 win.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The striker also scored in league matches against <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> and Birmingham.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Campbell began the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Sunderland_A.F.C._season" title="2010–11 Sunderland A.F.C. season">2010–11 season</a> in goalscoring form in pre-season, scoring four goals in a friendly against Hull,<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> and goals against <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_F.C." title="Leicester City F.C.">Leicester City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brighton_%26_Hove_Albion_F.C." title="Brighton &amp; Hove Albion F.C.">Brighton &amp; Hove Albion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Having forced his way into manager <a href="/wiki/Steve_Bruce" title="Steve Bruce">Steve Bruce</a>'s plans, he started the opening match of the new Premier League season, playing the full 90 minutes against Birmingham at the <a href="/wiki/Stadium_of_Light" title="Stadium of Light">Stadium of Light</a> in a 2–2 draw.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> He also played the full 90 minutes in Sunderland's next league outing against <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> at <a href="/wiki/The_Hawthorns" title="The Hawthorns">the Hawthorns</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> However, he sustained an <a href="/wiki/Anterior_cruciate_ligament_injury" title="Anterior cruciate ligament injury">anterior cruciate ligament injury</a> in Sunderland's 1–0 win against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> on 29 August 2010, which sidelined him for an expected six months.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Steve Bruce said "It's a tragedy. But Fraizer will be back. He's a larger-than-life character. He was just starting to show what he was about. But make no mistake he'll be back and he'll be firing again." regarding the injury.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Campbell made his return to training in March 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Despite having seemingly recovered from the injury, Campbell suffered a recurrence of the ligament injury in the same knee in preparation for a match against Manchester City on 3 April 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> After undergoing surgery on 20 April 2011, it was learned that Campbell would be out of action for 12 months.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> This injury ruled Campbell out of action for the remainder of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Premier_League" title="2010–11 Premier League">2010–11 season</a> and much of the <a href="/wiki/2011-12_Premier_League" class="mw-redirect" title="2011-12 Premier League">2011–12 season</a>. Though he was proposed to return in March 2012, Steve Bruce revealed that Campbell was in the frame to return to action around Christmas time – three months ahead of schedule.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Campbell scored on his return for Sunderland on 29 January 2012, coming on at half time for <a href="/wiki/Connor_Wickham" title="Connor Wickham">Connor Wickham</a> against <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> in an FA Cup 4th Round tie where he scored the equaliser in a 1–1 draw at the Stadium of Light.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Campbell made his Premier League return on 1 February 2012 against <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a>, opening the scoring and providing an assist in a 3–0 home win.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cardiff_City">Cardiff City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cardiff City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fraizer_Campbell_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Fraizer_Campbell_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Fraizer_Campbell_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="154" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Fraizer_Campbell_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Fraizer_Campbell_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Fraizer_Campbell_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Fraizer_Campbell_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1022" data-file-height="924" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fraizer_Campbell_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Campbell playing for <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> in 2013</div></div></div>
<p>On 21 January 2013, Campbell signed for <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> on a three-and-a-half-year deal for a fee believed to be in the region of £650,000.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut on 2 February 2013 against <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a>, where he scored the only goal of the match in the 64th minute after coming on as a substitute just two minutes before.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> On 16 February, Campbell scored two goals on his home debut against Bristol City in the <a href="/wiki/Severnside_derby" title="Severnside derby">Severnside derby</a>, which was then followed by another two against Wolverhampton Wanderers the following week.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Campbell was named as the Championship's Player of the Month for February 2013 after scoring all of Cardiff's five goals during the month.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> On the last day of the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Football_League_Championship" title="2012–13 Football League Championship">Championship title-winning</a> season, Campbell scored in a 2–2 draw with his former club Hull, taking his final season tally for the Cardiff to seven goals in twelve appearances.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In the second match of the 2013–14 season on 25 August 2013, Campbell scored twice in a famous 3–2 victory at Cardiff against Manchester City, with both goals coming from headers off corner kicks.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Crystal_Palace">Crystal Palace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Crystal Palace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cardiff accepted two bids for Campbell, one from Leicester City and one from <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> On 24 July 2014, Crystal Palace completed the signing of Campbell on a three-year deal after matching the release clause in his contract, believed to be in the region of £900,000.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> He was released from his contract on 30 June 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Hull_City">Return to Hull City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Return to Hull City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 19 July 2017, Campbell re-signed for newly relegated Championship club Hull City on a two-year contract.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut on the opening day of the season, 5 August 2017, away at Aston Villa, in a 1–1 draw.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club on 23 September 2017, in an away match against <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a> that was drawn 1–1.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> He left Hull at the end of the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Hull_City_A.F.C._season" title="2018–19 Hull City A.F.C. season">2018–19 season</a> when his contract expired.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Huddersfield_Town">Huddersfield Town</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Huddersfield Town">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Campbell signed for newly relegated Championship club Huddersfield Town on 12 August 2019 on a two-year contract.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut on 21 August as an 84th-minute substitute in a 2–1 defeat away to Cardiff City, his former club.<sup id="cite_ref-sb1920_70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sb1920-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Campbell was capped at various youth levels for England, playing three times for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-16_football_team" title="England national under-16 football team">under-16s</a>, six times for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-17_football_team" title="England national under-17 football team">under-17s</a> and once for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-18_football_team" title="England national under-18 football team">under-18s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During his loan spell at Hull City, Campbell received his first call-up to the England under-21 team, coming on as a substitute in a match against <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Poland national under-21 football team">Poland</a> on 25 March 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first international goal on 18 November 2008 in a 2–0 victory over the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic_national_under-21_football_team" title="Czech Republic national under-21 football team">Czech Republic Under-21s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup> He also scored in the second group match of the 2009 UEFA European Under-21 Championship against Spain, in a 2–0 victory on 18 June 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> He was sent off in the semi-final of the championship against <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_under-21_football_team" title="Sweden national under-21 football team">Sweden</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> Following his under-21 experience, Campbell's Sunderland manager Steve Bruce urged <a href="/wiki/Fabio_Capello" title="Fabio Capello">Fabio Capello</a> to consider Campbell for promotion to the senior squad saying, "the England manager should have a look at him", also saying "As for England, why not? Why not go with up-and-coming young talent?"<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 23 February 2012, despite only scoring six league goals in four years, Campbell received his first call-up to the England squad for the friendly versus the Netherlands. Interim England manager <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Pearce" title="Stuart Pearce">Stuart Pearce</a> had stated his ambitions to select untried young players for the friendly which would take place on 29 February. Pearce had given Campbell all 14 of his under-21 caps.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> Campbell made his debut as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Danny_Welbeck" title="Danny Welbeck">Danny Welbeck</a> in the 80th minute, as England lost 3–2 due to an injury-time game-winning goal by <a href="/wiki/Arjen_Robben" title="Arjen Robben">Arjen Robben</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fraizer_Campbell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Campbell has two children with his wife, Emma. He missed the birth of his first child, as he was making his debut for <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>. His daughter was born at almost exactly the same time as he came on the pitch as a second-half substitute.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His younger brother Ashford was a contestant on <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_8)" class="mw-redirect" title="The X Factor (UK series 8)"><i>The X Factor</i> 2011</a> as part of boy band <a href="/wiki/The_Risk" class="mw-redirect" title="The Risk">The Risk</a> until they were voted out in Week 5.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
