<p>The <b>Football League First Division</b> is a former division of the <a href="/wiki/English_Football_League" title="English Football League">Football League</a> and from <a href="/wiki/1888%E2%80%9389_Football_League" title="1888–89 Football League">1888</a> to <a href="/wiki/1991%E2%80%9392_Football_League" title="1991–92 Football League">1992</a>, it was the top tier division in the <a href="/wiki/English_football_league_system" title="English football league system">English football league system</a>. Following the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>, it became the second tier division. It was rebranded as the Football League Championship in <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Football_League" title="2004–05 Football League">2004</a> and in <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_EFL_Championship" title="2016–17 EFL Championship">2016</a> adopted its current name of the <a href="/wiki/EFL_Championship" title="EFL Championship">EFL Championship</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Football_League_First_Division&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Football_in_England#History_of_English_football" title="Football in England">Football in England §&#160;History of English football</a></div>
<p>The Football League was founded in 1888 by <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa" class="mw-redirect" title="Aston Villa">Aston Villa</a> director <a href="/wiki/William_McGregor_(football)" title="William McGregor (football)">William McGregor</a>. It originally consisted of a single division of 12 clubs (<a href="/wiki/Accrington_F.C." title="Accrington F.C.">Accrington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Bolton Wanderers F.C.">Bolton Wanderers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Notts_County_F.C." title="Notts County F.C.">Notts County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Preston_North_End_F.C." title="Preston North End F.C.">Preston North End</a>, Stoke (now <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a>), <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C.">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a>), known as The Football League. When the League admitted additional members from the rival <a href="/wiki/Football_Alliance" title="Football Alliance">Football Alliance</a> in 1892, it was split into two divisions.
</p><p>For the next 100 years, the First Division was the top professional league in English football. Then, in 1992 the 22 clubs making up the First Division elected to resign from the Football League and set up the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>. The Football League was consequently re-organised, with the Second, Third and Fourth Divisions now renamed the First, Second and Third respectively. Thus, the First Division, while still the top level of the Football League, now became the second level of the entire <a href="/wiki/English_football_league_system" title="English football league system">English football league system</a>.
</p><p>The First Division was renamed as the <b>Football League Championship</b> prior to the start of the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_Football_League" title="2004–05 Football League">2004–05 season</a>, as part of a league-wide rebrand. The Football League rebranded itself as the English Football League prior to the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_English_Football_League" title="2016–17 English Football League">2016–17 season</a>, with its top level becoming the <b>EFL Championship</b> at that time.
</p><p>Liverpool were the most frequent winners of the First Division when it was the top flight of English football, winning it a total of 18 times.
</p>
<h2><span id="Trophy_and_players.27_medals"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Trophy_and_players'_medals">Trophy and players' medals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Football_League_First_Division&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Trophy and players&#039; medals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Football League First Division trophy was first awarded in 1891, and was presented to the winners through to 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of the <a href="/wiki/1947%E2%80%9348_Football_League" title="1947–48 Football League">1947–48 season</a> making 7 appearance for their club during the season was not enough for a player to qualify for a winners medal.<sup id="cite_ref-afc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-afc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of the <a href="/wiki/1975%E2%80%9376_Football_League" title="1975–76 Football League">1975–76 season</a> players had to make 14 appearances for their club during the season in order to qualify for a winners medal.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
