<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crosland_Moor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It begins 1&#160;mile (1.6&#160;km) to the south west of Huddersfield town centre. Crosland Moor begins at the junction of the Manchester Road <a href="/wiki/A62_road" title="A62 road">A62</a> and Blackmoorfoot Road the main thoroughfare. The area rises up the hillside to overlook the areas of <a href="/wiki/Milnsbridge" title="Milnsbridge">Milnsbridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Golcar" title="Golcar">Golcar</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley" title="Colne Valley">Colne Valley</a>. The name is derived from the local landowners the Crosland family prominent local land owners who owned much of the area in 15th and 16th centuries. Their home <a href="/w/index.php?title=Crosland_Lodge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Crosland Lodge (page does not exist)">Crosland Lodge</a>, and business holdings at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Crosland_Moor_Mill&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Crosland Moor Mill (page does not exist)">Crosland Moor Mill</a> were situated in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Walpole and Balmoral Avenue <a href="/wiki/Housing_estates" class="mw-redirect" title="Housing estates">housing estates</a> are also a part of Crosland Moor. The area (including <b>Crosland Hill</b> and <b>Beaumont Park</b>) is bordered by <a href="/wiki/Lockwood,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Lockwood, West Yorkshire">Lockwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherton,_Kirklees" title="Netherton, Kirklees">Netherton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linthwaite" title="Linthwaite">Linthwaite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milnsbridge" title="Milnsbridge">Milnsbridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paddock" title="Paddock">Paddock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thornton_Lodge" title="Thornton Lodge">Thornton Lodge</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political">Political</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crosland_Moor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The electoral ward of Crosland Moor and <a href="/wiki/Netherton,_Huddersfield" class="mw-redirect" title="Netherton, Huddersfield">Netherton</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley" title="Colne Valley">Colne Valley</a> constituency. The area of Crosland Moor includes <a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Park" title="Beaumont Park">Beaumont Park</a>, Crosland Hill and Walpole and has a population of 9,085 according to the 2001 census. The ward was renamed Crosland Moor and Netherton in time for the 2011 Census. Its population at the Census was 18,723.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="St_Luke.27s_Hospital"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="St_Luke's_Hospital">St Luke's Hospital</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crosland_Moor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: St Luke&#039;s Hospital">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The former <a href="/wiki/St_Luke%27s_Hospital,_Huddersfield" title="St Luke&#39;s Hospital, Huddersfield">St. Luke's Hospital</a> was situated alongside Blackmoorfoot Road. It was originally a <a href="/wiki/Workhouse" title="Workhouse">workhouse</a> for the poor and needy of the area in the 19th century. This ended up being a hospital that included the headquarters for the Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield NHS Trust, a psychiatric unit and also  other services such as <a href="/wiki/Physiotherapy" class="mw-redirect" title="Physiotherapy">physiotherapy</a>. The hospital closed in December 2010<sup id="cite_ref-examiner.co.uk_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-examiner.co.uk-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and over time, all the buildings on that site have been demolished and the site has been fenced off. However, in 2015 a plan to regenerate the hospital site with up to 200 homes, a retail unit and a small neighbourhood centre have been approved by councillors.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crosland_Moor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moor End Academy (formally Moor End Technology College) is Crosland Moor's secondary school based on Dryclough Road near Beaumont Park. Crosland Moor Junior  School and the Crosland Moor and Dryclough Infant Schools are also on Dryclough Road.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Beaumont_Park">Beaumont Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crosland_Moor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Beaumont Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the end of Dryclough Road, <a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Park" title="Beaumont Park">Beaumont Park</a>, Huddersfield's first park, begins. Opened in 1883, the park mainly consists of woodland and is on a hillside that looks over the <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley" title="Holme Valley">Holme Valley</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Lockwood,_Huddersfield" title="Lockwood, Huddersfield">Lockwood</a> railway viaduct. It is currently being restored to its former glory by the Friends of Beaumont Park. Housing around the park is some of the most expensive in Huddersfield.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="William_Horsfall_and_the_Luddites">William Horsfall and the Luddites</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crosland_Moor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: William Horsfall and the Luddites">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Along Blackmoorfoot Road beyond the junction with Dryclough Road was the spot where in April 1812 mill owner William Horsfall was shot and killed by the <a href="/wiki/Luddite" title="Luddite">Luddites</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> They objected to the introduction of machinery Horsfall's mill in <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a>. A side street where the incident occurred was named after Horsfall (William Horsfall Street).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airfield">Airfield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crosland_Moor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Airfield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Just off Blackmoorfoot Road beyond the Crosland Heath Golf Course is the <a href="/wiki/Crosland_Moor_airfield" class="mw-redirect" title="Crosland Moor airfield">Crosland Moor airfield</a> which was established by the David Brown Engineering Works based in <a href="/wiki/Lockwood,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Lockwood, West Yorkshire">Lockwood</a>. Even further up the road is Huddersfield's Observatory which is situated high over the town for good views of the sky at night.
</p><p>Before the airfield is the Wellfield quarry where Crosland Hill Sandstone comes from. According to the <a href="/wiki/Building_Research_Establishment" title="Building Research Establishment">Building Research Establishment</a> it is a durable stone not affected by acid rain or air pollution.
</p>
