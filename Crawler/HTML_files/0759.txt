<p><b>Tokyo Industries Limited</b> is a private company, based in the North of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>,  with an estate of over 30 venues, including Nightclubs, Hotels, Bars and Restaurants.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tokyo_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tokyo industries was founded in 1997 in Greater Manchester by Aaron Mellor, opening their first venue, Tokyo Project, in <a href="/wiki/Oldham" title="Oldham">Oldham</a>. A second venue opened shortly afterwards in <a href="/wiki/Ashton-under-Lyne" title="Ashton-under-Lyne">Ashton-under-Lyne</a> followed by a third, The Castle, in Oldham.
</p><p>In 2013, the 150 year old premises The Castle was bought by <a href="/wiki/Transport_for_Greater_Manchester" title="Transport for Greater Manchester">Transport for Greater Manchester</a> with plans to demolish the site due to the ongoing <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Metrolink" title="Manchester Metrolink">Manchester Metrolink</a> construction.<sup id="cite_ref-tfgmOldham_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tfgmOldham-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In October 2016, Tokyo Industries opened its venue Church Leeds through a joint venture.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Tokyo Industries Group MD/CEO was found guilty of putting the lives of hundreds of staff and revellers at risk in 2013. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.yorkpress.co.uk/news/13805666.York_nightclub_boss_hit_with___173_000_court_bill_for_fire_safety_lapses_that_risked_hundreds_of_lives/">Aaron Mellor</a>, 44, appeared at Lincoln Magistrates Court between April 21 and 24, and was found guilty on May 6 of 11 offences under the <a href="/wiki/Regulatory_Reform_(Fire_Safety)_Order_2005" title="Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005">Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005</a> and was fined £173,000.
</p>
<h2><span id="Venues_.26_Brands"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Venues_&amp;_Brands">Venues &amp; Brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tokyo_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Venues &amp; Brands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company operates the Stein Bier Keller, Brew Haus, Get Baked and Tokyo brands, in addition to a number of one off venues and events. They are well known for using <a href="/wiki/Funktion-One" title="Funktion-One">Funktion-One</a> sound systems at almost all of their sites.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Night_Clubs">Night Clubs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tokyo_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Night Clubs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><b>Factory 251</b>&#160;– The former headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Factory_Records" title="Factory Records">Factory Records</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, opened as a nightclub in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The venue featured prominently in the 2002 film <a href="/wiki/24_Hour_Party_People" title="24 Hour Party People">24 Hour Party People</a>.</li>
<li><b>Digital</b>&#160;– Digital opened in 2005 in the center of <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle upon Tyne</a>. The award-winning venue<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> was rated as the 11th best nightclub in the world by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ Magazine">DJ Magazine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>South</b>&#160;– Originally opened in Manchester in 1995 and re-designed by <a href="/wiki/Ben_Kelly_(designer)" title="Ben Kelly (designer)">Ben Kelly (designer)</a>, the award-winning designer of <a href="/wiki/The_Ha%C3%A7ienda" title="The Haçienda">The Haçienda</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Church</b>&#160;– A grade 2 listed church in <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, converted into a music venue for live bands and club nights.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals">Festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tokyo_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><b>Lost Village</b>&#160;– Boutique summer festival in woodland outside <a href="/wiki/Lincoln,_England" title="Lincoln, England">Lincoln</a> started in 2015 in collaboration with Moda Black owners, Jaymo and Andy George.</li>
<li><b>Obonjan</b>&#160;– Three month island festival held on a private island near the coast of <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a></li></ul>
