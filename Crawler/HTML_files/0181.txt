<p><b>Arati Ankalikar-Tikekar</b> (born January 27, 1963 in <a href="/wiki/Vijayapura" title="Vijayapura">Vijayapura</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>) is a two-time National Award-winning <a href="/wiki/Indian_people" title="Indian people">Indian</a> classical singer who is active mostly in Marathi, Konkani and Hindi film Industry; known for high-pitch singing and style, earned in <a href="/wiki/Agra" title="Agra">Agra</a> as well as Gwalior and Jaipur.
</p><p>Arati has been credited by audio cassettes and CD recordings. She was the main playback singer for the <a href="/wiki/Shyam_Benegal" title="Shyam Benegal">Sham Benegal</a>'s film, <i><a href="/wiki/Sardari_Begum" title="Sardari Begum">Sardari Begum</a></i>. She is known for her albums <i>Tejomay Nadbrahm</i>, <i>Raag-Rang</i>, and, playback for the movies <i><a href="/wiki/Antarnaad" title="Antarnaad">Antarnaad</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/De_Dhakka" title="De Dhakka">De dhakka</a></i>, <i>Savlee</i>, and the hit <i><a href="/wiki/Sardari_Begum" title="Sardari Begum">Sardari Begum</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Ek_Hazarachi_Note_(2014_Marathi_film)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ek Hazarachi Note (2014 Marathi film)">Ek Hazarachi Note</a></i>. Her daughter <a href="/wiki/Swanandi_Tikekar" title="Swanandi Tikekar">Swanandi Tikekar</a> who is in her mid-twenties dabbles in acting as well.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accomplishments_and_awards">Accomplishments and awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arati_Ankalikar-Tikekar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Accomplishments and awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She received her first <a href="/wiki/National_Film_Award_for_Best_Female_Playback_Singer" title="National Film Award for Best Female Playback Singer">National Film Award for Best Female Playback Singer</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Konkani_cinema" title="Konkani cinema">Konkani cinema</a>, <i>Anternaad</i>, based on the life of a classical singer for the year <a href="/wiki/54th_National_Film_Awards" title="54th National Film Awards">2006</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She received <a href="/wiki/Maharashtra_State_Film_Awards" title="Maharashtra State Film Awards">Maharashtra State Film Awards</a>, for the Marathi Film, <i><a href="/wiki/De_Dhakka" title="De Dhakka">De Dhakka</a></i>. Later, in 2013, she was awarded with <a href="/wiki/National_Film_Award_for_Best_Female_Playback_Singer" title="National Film Award for Best Female Playback Singer">National Film Award for Best Female Playback Singer</a> for the second time in the Marathi movie, <i><a href="/wiki/Samhita_(film)" title="Samhita (film)">Samhita</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She is married to Indian film actor, <a href="/wiki/Uday_Tikekar" title="Uday Tikekar">Uday Tikekar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
