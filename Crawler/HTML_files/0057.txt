	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">LIBRIS</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>
<b>LIBRIS</b> (<i>Library Information System</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> national <a href="/wiki/Union_catalogue" class="mw-redirect" title="Union catalogue">union catalogue</a> maintained by the <a href="/wiki/National_Library_of_Sweden" title="National Library of Sweden">National Library of Sweden</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ne_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ne-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is possible to freely search about 6.5 million titles nationwide.<sup id="cite_ref-libris-help_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-libris-help-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to bibliographic records, one for each book or publication, LIBRIS also contains an <a href="/wiki/Authority_file" class="mw-redirect" title="Authority file">authority file</a> of people. For each person there is a record connecting name, birth and occupation with a unique identifier.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="These records are linked by VIAF but not accessible via the LIBRIS user interface, so it would be useful to have a reliable source mentioning them. (August 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/MARC_standards" title="MARC standards">MARC Code</a> for the Swedish Union Catalog is SE-LIBR, normalized: selibr.<sup id="cite_ref-LOC-MARC_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LOC-MARC-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The development of LIBRIS can be traced to the mid-1960s.<sup id="cite_ref-Olsson_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Olsson-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> While rationalization of libraries had been an issue for two decades after World War II, it was in 1965 that a government committee published a report on the use of computers in research libraries.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The government budget of 1965 created a research library council (<i>Forskningsbiblioteksr√•det</i>, FBR).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> A preliminary design document, <i>Biblioteksadministrativt Information System (BAIS)</i> was published in May 1970, and the name LIBRIS, short for Library Information System, was used for a technical subcommittee that started on 1 July 1970.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The newsletter <i>LIBRIS-meddelanden</i> (<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886058088">.mw-parser-output cite.citation{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .citation
