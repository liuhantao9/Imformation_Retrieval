<p><b>Linthwaite</b> (known as <i>Linfit</i> in the local community)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a village in <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, it is situated 4&#160;miles (6&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/A62_road" title="A62 road">A62</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley" title="Colne Valley">Colne Valley</a>. The village together with <b>Blackmoorfoot</b> had a population of 3,835 according to the 2001 census.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Colne, West Yorkshire">River Colne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Narrow_Canal" title="Huddersfield Narrow Canal">Huddersfield Narrow Canal</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Line">Huddersfield to Manchester railway line</a> and <a href="/wiki/A62_road" title="A62 road">A62</a> main road all pass near to the village. After they were constructed, textile mills were then built to produce cloth making use of the river. This led to the growth of the village. Linthwaite Hall on Linfit Fold was built around 1600.
</p><p>Currently, it is a busy village with five pubs, including the Sair Inn, a traditional pub, formerly known as the 'New Inn'  and now one of the few 'own brew' pubs still in the country. It won the <a href="/wiki/Campaign_for_Real_Ale" title="Campaign for Real Ale">CAMRA</a> National Pub of the Year Award in 1997.
</p><p>Linthwaite is believed to be where the surname Dyson started in 1316.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Blackmoorfoot Reservoir, at the top of Gilroyd Lane, is a wintering site for migrating <a href="/wiki/Anatidae" title="Anatidae">wildfowl</a> and wading birds.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linthwaite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are two primary schools and a secondary in the village: Linthwaite Ardron Memorial Junior School and the Linthwaite Clough Junior Infant and Early Years School for local children; and <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley_High_School" title="Colne Valley High School">Colne Valley High School</a>, whose catchment area is described in the name.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linthwaite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In sport the village has two teams in the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Cricket_League" title="Huddersfield Cricket League">Huddersfield District Cricket League</a>: Broad Oak (on the top road) and Linthwaite (in the valley).
</p><p>Linthwaite Badgers are the current Huddersfield District Division One champions and Barlow Cup winners. Under the leadership of Alan 'Barcode' Wadsworth they have become the best team in Huddersfield.
</p><p>The Badgers Mob can regularly be heard chatting 'slawit dont like it - up the badgers' at their beloved home Badger Cove.
</p><p>Linthwaite also has football teams playing in the RCD Junior Football League from under-8s right up to under-18s. They also have a group of 5-to-7 year olds.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Linthwaite&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The largest employer in the village is <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharmaceutical">pharmaceutical drug</a> manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Thornton_%26_Ross" title="Thornton &amp; Ross">Thornton &amp; Ross</a>, the producer of Covonia cough medicine. The company, which was founded by Nathan Thornton and Phillip Ross in 1922, was acquired by <a href="/wiki/STADA_Arzneimittel" class="mw-redirect" title="STADA Arzneimittel">STADA Arzneimittel</a> in a £221 million deal in August 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
