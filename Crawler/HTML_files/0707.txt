<p><b>Evile</b> are an English <a href="/wiki/Thrash_metal" title="Thrash metal">thrash metal</a> band from <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, formed in 1999. The band's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Enter_the_Grave" title="Enter the Grave">Enter the Grave</a></i>, was produced by <a href="/wiki/Flemming_Rasmussen" title="Flemming Rasmussen">Flemming Rasmussen</a> at Sweet Silence Studios in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a>, Denmark and was released worldwide in 2007 by <a href="/wiki/Earache_Records" title="Earache Records">Earache Records</a> to critical acclaim by fans and critics alike. The band's first reviews included a hail by <i><a href="/wiki/Kerrang!" title="Kerrang!">Kerrang!</a></i> as "Carrying the genre's whole 'revival' on their shoulders."<sup id="cite_ref-d_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-d-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Their second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Infected_Nations" title="Infected Nations">Infected Nations</a></i>, was released on 21 September 2009. It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Russ_Russell" title="Russ Russell">Russ Russell</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> with artwork from <a href="/wiki/Michael_Whelan" title="Michael Whelan">Michael Whelan</a>. Evile's third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Five_Serpent%27s_Teeth" title="Five Serpent&#39;s Teeth">Five Serpent's Teeth</a></i> was released in 2011, also on Earache Records. A fourth album, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Skull_(album)" title="Skull (album)">Skull</a></i>, was released in the late spring of 2013 on Earache. Evile are currently working on a new album.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Pre-Evile_.281999.E2.80.932004.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-Evile_(1999–2004)">Pre-Evile (1999–2004)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-Evile (1999–2004)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1999, former school friends <a href="/wiki/Matt_Drake" title="Matt Drake">Matt Drake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ben_Carter_(musician)" title="Ben Carter (musician)">Ben Carter</a> came together over a mutual love for <a href="/wiki/Thrash_metal" title="Thrash metal">thrash metal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> and formed a cover band named Metal Militia (primarily playing <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a> classics)<sup id="cite_ref-mm_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mm-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Matt's brother, <a href="/wiki/Ol_Drake" title="Ol Drake">Ol Drake</a>, joined as the lead guitarist, and after <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>-born bassist <a href="/wiki/Mike_Alexander_(musician)" title="Mike Alexander (musician)">Mike Alexander</a> responded to the band's advert in a local guitar store looking for a bassist influenced by bands such as <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sepultura" title="Sepultura">Sepultura</a> and <a href="/wiki/Testament_(band)" title="Testament (band)">Testament</a>, the lineup was complete.
</p><p>At the band's first live show, at the Ukrainian Club in <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, an audience member had a brick thrown at their face by area locals.<sup id="cite_ref-halifax_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-halifax-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Birth_of_Evile_.282004.E2.80.932006.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Birth_of_Evile_(2004–2006)">Birth of Evile (2004–2006)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Birth of Evile (2004–2006)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2004, the band ceased playing covers and dedicated their time to writing original material. Soon after, they independently recorded and released two EPs in limited quantities (250–500 units): <i>All Hallows Eve EP</i> (2004) and <i>Hell Demo</i> (2006). Evile toured extensively throughout the UK (2004–2006) and Netherlands (2006), played as the support act for <a href="/wiki/Exodus_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Exodus (band)">Exodus</a> and eventually signed to <a href="/wiki/Earache_Records" title="Earache Records">Earache Records</a>. Though Earache had received the band's <i>Hell Demo</i>, it was after label head <a href="/wiki/Digby_Pearson" title="Digby Pearson">Digby Pearson</a> witnessed Evile's set at <a href="/wiki/Bloodstock_Open_Air" title="Bloodstock Open Air">Bloodstock Open Air</a> in 2006 that he contacted the band and signed them to the label. In an interview Matt Drake said that the band was originally supposed to be called "Exile", but there were "a million other bands that called themself that" so the "X" in the band's name was changed to a "V," and the band became known as Evile.<sup id="cite_ref-digevile_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-digevile-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Enter_the_Grave_.282007.E2.80.932009.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Enter_the_Grave_(2007–2009)"><i>Enter the Grave</i> (2007–2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Enter the Grave (2007–2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Evile_live_in_SB.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Evile_live_in_SB.JPG/300px-Evile_live_in_SB.JPG" decoding="async" width="300" height="225" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Evile_live_in_SB.JPG/450px-Evile_live_in_SB.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Evile_live_in_SB.JPG/600px-Evile_live_in_SB.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Evile_live_in_SB.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Evile live in Saarbrücken 2010</div></div></div>
<p>Prior to the album's recording, <a href="/wiki/Matt_Drake" title="Matt Drake">Matt Drake</a> contacted producer <a href="/wiki/Flemming_Rasmussen" title="Flemming Rasmussen">Flemming Rasmussen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morbid_Angel" title="Morbid Angel">Morbid Angel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blind_Guardian" title="Blind Guardian">Blind Guardian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Artillery_(band)" title="Artillery (band)">Artillery</a>) by doing a search for his name on <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> and enquired as to whether or not he would like to hear Evile's material. Flemming responded positively, prompting Matt to send him some Evile <a href="/wiki/Mp3" class="mw-redirect" title="Mp3">mp3s</a> via e-mail. Supposedly, Flemming never commented on the mp3s, causing Matt to presume that he "hated them",<sup id="cite_ref-flemhate_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flemhate-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> but when <a href="/wiki/Earache_Records" title="Earache Records">Earache Records</a> contacted Flemming to request his skills as producer for Evile's debut, he swiftly accepted the offer.<sup id="cite_ref-flemhate_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flemhate-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Evile recorded their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Enter_the_Grave" title="Enter the Grave">Enter the Grave</a>,</i> at Sweet Silence Studios in <a href="/wiki/Copenhagen" title="Copenhagen">Copenhagen</a> with producer <a href="/wiki/Flemming_Rasmussen" title="Flemming Rasmussen">Flemming Rasmussen</a> and assistant engineer Anders H Mortensen. The album reached No.&#160;33 on the UK rock album charts, and <i><a href="/wiki/Kerrang!" title="Kerrang!">Kerrang!</a></i> magazine awarded the album 4 out of 5 stars while dubbing Evile as "Carrying the genre's whole 'revival' on their shoulders".<sup id="cite_ref-d_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-d-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Generally, the album was received positively and received highly rated reviews, including those from <a href="/wiki/Terrorizer_(magazine)" title="Terrorizer (magazine)"><i>Terrorizer</i> Magazine</a> (9/10),<sup id="cite_ref-terreview_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-terreview-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i>Metal Revolution</i> (100/100)<sup id="cite_ref-MRreview_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MRreview-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Classic_Rock_(magazine)" title="Classic Rock (magazine)">Classic Rock</a> (the 38th best album of 2007).<sup id="cite_ref-CR2007_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CR2007-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The band played many shows with class-acts such as <a href="/wiki/Machine_Head_(band)" title="Machine Head (band)">Machine Head</a>, and other bands such as <a href="/wiki/Sabbat_(English_band)" title="Sabbat (English band)">Sabbat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sanctity_(band)" title="Sanctity (band)">Sanctity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gama_Bomb" title="Gama Bomb">Gama Bomb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Severe_Torture" title="Severe Torture">Severe Torture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Desecration_(band)" title="Desecration (band)">Desecration</a>. On 11 December 2007, Evile participated in and won<sup id="cite_ref-GITR2_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GITR2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metal_Hammer" title="Metal Hammer">Metal Hammer</a>'s Get in the Ring 2, at the <a href="/wiki/Kentish_Town_Forum" class="mw-redirect" title="Kentish Town Forum">Kentish Town Forum</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. Four bands (Evile, <a href="/wiki/Engel_(band)" title="Engel (band)">Engel</a>, Viking Skull and Goat the Head) performed thirty-minute sets inside a boxing ring and the public selected their favorite via <a href="/wiki/Short_message_service" class="mw-redirect" title="Short message service">SMS texts</a>. The show was compered by <a href="/wiki/Oderus_Urungus" class="mw-redirect" title="Oderus Urungus">Oderus Urungus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gwar" title="Gwar">Gwar</a> and the judges included <a href="/wiki/Ville_Valo" title="Ville Valo">Ville Valo</a> of <a href="/wiki/HIM_(Finnish_band)" title="HIM (Finnish band)">HIM</a>. Valo commented that Evile were "really tight and rhythmic"<sup id="cite_ref-GITR2_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GITR2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> but they were not his "cup of tea." The competition was rebroadcast on <a href="/wiki/Rockworld_TV" title="Rockworld TV">Rockworld TV</a> on <a href="/wiki/British_Sky_Broadcasting" class="mw-redirect" title="British Sky Broadcasting">SKY</a> Channel 368 throughout January and February 2008 and included an hour-long supplement dedicated to Evile's GITR2 set.<sup id="cite_ref-gitrair_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gitrair-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February and March 2008, Evile supported thrash metal band <a href="/wiki/Megadeth" title="Megadeth">Megadeth</a> on the European leg of their Tour of Duty and the UK leg of their <a href="/wiki/Gigantour" title="Gigantour">Gigantour</a>, opening for American <a href="/wiki/Deathcore" title="Deathcore">deathcore</a> band <a href="/wiki/Job_for_a_Cowboy" title="Job for a Cowboy">Job for a Cowboy</a>. The band was received warmly throughout the respective tours,<sup id="cite_ref-blive_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blive-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and on the final date, they briefly joined Megadeth on stage at the Hugennottenhalle in <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a> to sing the heavy metal anthem "<a href="/wiki/Peace_Sells" title="Peace Sells">Peace Sells</a>". In March 2008, Evile's track "Thrasher" was featured on the <a href="/wiki/Wii" title="Wii">Wii</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a>, and <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> video game <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_Band_(video_game)" title="Rock Band (video game)">Rock Band</a></i> as downloadable content (part of the "Earache Thrash Pack").<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In April 2008, the band embarked on a European tour alongside thrash veterans <a href="/wiki/Exodus_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Exodus (band)">Exodus</a>. During the tour, they allowed a 14-year-old fan to join them on stage to perform "Enter the Grave",<sup id="cite_ref-fourteen_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fourteen-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and on a few occasions they joined Exodus in backing vocals on "<a href="/wiki/The_Toxic_Waltz_(song)" title="The Toxic Waltz (song)">The Toxic Waltz</a>," which was followed by the members of Exodus handing their instruments over to Evile to close the set.<sup id="cite_ref-exoinst_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-exoinst-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 8 October the release of Evile's first official Music Video. The video was "Thrasher", a song from their debut album, and was directed by Dan Fernbach (who directed bands such as <a href="/wiki/Bullet_For_My_Valentine" class="mw-redirect" title="Bullet For My Valentine">Bullet For My Valentine</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-ThrashVid_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ThrashVid-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Also on 8 October the re-release of Evile's <i><a href="/wiki/Enter_the_Grave" title="Enter the Grave">Enter the Grave</a></i> via <a href="/wiki/Earache_Records" title="Earache Records">Earache Records</a>. Included in the package was a lengthy DVD, Evile guitar pick (with three alternate designs), A woven patch and three bonus tracks.<sup id="cite_ref-etgredux_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-etgredux-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The band later announced they would provide support to <a href="/wiki/Satyricon_(band)" title="Satyricon (band)">Satyricon</a> on their European "Tour of Nero" in November/December 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-SatyTour_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatyTour-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Unfortunately, On 19 November 2008, while on the tour, lead guitarist <a href="/wiki/Ol_Drake" title="Ol Drake">Ol Drake</a> reportedly collapsed due to health issues. His jaw was fractured and the under-side of his chin was cut open. Evile were forced to cancel their appearances on the remainder of the tour.<sup id="cite_ref-olfallblab_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-olfallblab-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-olinjuryreport_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-olinjuryreport-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He then had to have his jaw wired shut for two weeks for healing.<sup id="cite_ref-oljaw_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oljaw-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Beginning in April 2009, Evile began the UK/Ireland "We Who Are About to Tour" tour along with UK death metal act <a href="/wiki/Trigger_the_Bloodshed" title="Trigger the Bloodshed">Trigger the Bloodshed</a>, and London Thrash band Mutant. Warpath, who were originally scheduled for the tour, pulled out two days prior to the start of the tour (though the unfortunate injury occurred a week before the tour<sup id="cite_ref-warpathpull_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warpathpull-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>) leaving Evile two days to find a replacement band.<sup id="cite_ref-hawthorneinjury_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hawthorneinjury-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-triggerreplace_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-triggerreplace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Evile played two brand new songs which would be featured on their second album; one entitled "Demolition" and one without a title, which on a nightly basis was open to being named by the audience.<sup id="cite_ref-newsongs_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newsongs-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Infected_Nations_and_the_death_of_Mike_Alexander_.282009.E2.80.932010.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Infected_Nations_and_the_death_of_Mike_Alexander_(2009–2010)"><i>Infected Nations</i> and the death of Mike Alexander (2009–2010)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Infected Nations and the death of Mike Alexander (2009–2010)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mike_Alexander_(Evile),_Tuska_2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Mike_Alexander_%28Evile%29%2C_Tuska_2009.jpg/220px-Mike_Alexander_%28Evile%29%2C_Tuska_2009.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="422" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Mike_Alexander_%28Evile%29%2C_Tuska_2009.jpg/330px-Mike_Alexander_%28Evile%29%2C_Tuska_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Mike_Alexander_%28Evile%29%2C_Tuska_2009.jpg/440px-Mike_Alexander_%28Evile%29%2C_Tuska_2009.jpg 2x" data-file-width="621" data-file-height="1191" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mike_Alexander_(Evile),_Tuska_2009.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mike Alexander (22 June 1977 – 5 October 2009)</div></div></div>
<p>In February 2009 Evile began the pre-production process for their second album with <a href="/wiki/Russ_Russell" title="Russ Russell">Russ Russell</a> (who has worked with bands such as <a href="/wiki/Napalm_Death" title="Napalm Death">Napalm Death</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Exploited" title="The Exploited">The Exploited</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dimmu_Borgir" title="Dimmu Borgir">Dimmu Borgir</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-preproa2_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preproa2-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> May and June saw Evile enter Parlour Studios in <a href="/wiki/Kettering" title="Kettering">Kettering</a> with Russell to record the album. The band would post three video updates whilst in the studio,<sup id="cite_ref-a2vid1_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-a2vid1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-a2vid2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-a2vid2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-a2vid3_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-a2vid3-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> and give fans a chance to "Ask Evile" questions via their Myspace blog.<sup id="cite_ref-askevilea2_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-askevilea2-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On an Evile studio tour hosted by Ben the working titles of songs to be on the album could be seen written on a whiteboard.<sup id="cite_ref-tourofstudio_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tourofstudio-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 22 June, Evile revealed that the title of the second album would be <i><a href="/wiki/Infected_Nations" title="Infected Nations">Infected Nations</a></i>, with artwork created by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Whelan" title="Michael Whelan">Michael Whelan</a> (who also worked with the likes of <a href="/wiki/Sepultura" title="Sepultura">Sepultura</a>, <a href="/wiki/Obituary_(band)" title="Obituary (band)">Obituary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a>). A video was posted online on the same day of Ol talking fans through the concept of the art, stood in front of a large poster print of the cover.<sup id="cite_ref-oltalkscover_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oltalkscover-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The album was released on 21 September in Europe and 22 September in USA.<sup id="cite_ref-album2news_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-album2news-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> <i>Infected Nations</i> jumped into the UK top 100 chart at number 91 following its first-day sales.
</p><p>The band released three tracks publicly prior to the album's release. These tracks were "Infected Nation", "Now Demolition" and "Nosophoros". Evile released a music video for the track "Infected Nation" which, like "Thrasher" was directed by Dan Fernbach and Static Films once again.<sup id="cite_ref-INvideo_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-INvideo-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Also, like "Thrasher", displays the band playing a dynamically aggressive song in a very confined space.
</p><p>In September 2009, <a href="/wiki/Schmier" class="mw-redirect" title="Schmier">Schmier</a> of the German thrash metal classics <a href="/wiki/Destruction_(band)" title="Destruction (band)">Destruction</a> asked lead guitarist, Ol Drake, to stand in for them on guitar. This was prompted by Destruction's guitarist, Mike Sifringer, breaking his fingers forcing them to find a temporary replacement.
</p><p>On 5 October 2009, tragedy struck the band as their bass player, Mike Alexander, died whilst being rushed to hospital while the band were on tour in <a href="/wiki/Lule%C3%A5" title="Luleå">Luleå</a>, Sweden.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> He died from a <a href="/wiki/Pulmonary_embolism" title="Pulmonary embolism">pulmonary embolism</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Blood_clot" class="mw-redirect" title="Blood clot">blood clot</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Lung" title="Lung">lungs</a>. Evile unveiled a tribute site &lt;<a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://www.mikealexander.co.uk">http://www.mikealexander.co.uk</a>&gt; to their fallen member.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
In early December 2009 the remaining members of Evile held two memorial nights for Mike. One was held in Leeds Rios and the other in the Camden Underworld, London. The night consisted of a covers set of some of Mike's favourite songs and bands, with members of the support bands (<a href="/wiki/Gama_Bomb" title="Gama Bomb">Gama Bomb</a>, Mutant and Seregon) on the night stepping in on various instruments. <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Barker" title="Nicholas Barker">Nick Barker</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dimmu_Borgir" title="Dimmu Borgir">Dimmu Borgir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cradle_of_Filth" title="Cradle of Filth">Cradle of Filth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Testament_(band)" title="Testament (band)">Testament</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exodus_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Exodus (band)">Exodus</a>) also took to the stage on both nights to play drums for several tracks. Both shows saw each remaining member of Evile take to the stage to read out messages of thanks from Mike's girlfriend, children and Mother. The Leeds show saw Mike's mother take to the stage and express her thanks to Evile's fans.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Evile were asked, by UK Magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Metal_Hammer" title="Metal Hammer">Metal Hammer</a></i>, to cover a <a href="/wiki/Pantera" title="Pantera">Pantera</a> track for their <a href="/wiki/Dimebag_Darrell" title="Dimebag Darrell">Dimebag Darrell</a> tribute Issue. The band chose to cover "Cemetery Gates" with it being their favourite Pantera track. The track would go on to receive positive feedback, be released via <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a> and get substantial airplay in the United States. This would unfortunately be the last track recorded with Mike Alexander.<sup id="cite_ref-EvilePantera_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EvilePantera-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> A raffle was also organized prior to the nights to raise funds for Mike's children and family. Evile and Mike's family were overwhelmed to receive support from people/bands such as <a href="/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne" title="Ozzy Osbourne">Ozzy Osbourne</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Sharon_Osbourne" title="Sharon Osbourne">Sharon Osbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Megadeth" title="Megadeth">Megadeth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slayer" title="Slayer">Slayer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Machine_Head_(band)" title="Machine Head (band)">Machine Head</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exodus_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Exodus (band)">Exodus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Overkill_(band)" title="Overkill (band)">Overkill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trivium_(band)" title="Trivium (band)">Trivium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andy_Sneap" title="Andy Sneap">Andy Sneap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Annihilator_(band)" title="Annihilator (band)">Annihilator</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benediction_(band)" title="Benediction (band)">Benediction</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hirax" title="Hirax">Hirax</a> and more. The winners of the Raffle Prizes were announced via <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> by Matt and Ol.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Evile started auditions for a new bass player in late November/December 2009. They then announced dates for a 2010 UK/Europe tour.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> On 16 December it was reported that Joel Graham (formerly of <a href="/wiki/Rise_to_Addiction" title="Rise to Addiction">Rise to Addiction</a>) is the band's new bass player.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In December Evile announced their first shows in <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" class="mw-redirect" title="United States of America">USA</a>/<a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. One tour supporting <a href="/wiki/Kreator" title="Kreator">Kreator</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Voivod_(band)" title="Voivod (band)">Voivod</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kataklysm" title="Kataklysm">Kataklysm</a> and more.<sup id="cite_ref-kreatorNAtour_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kreatorNAtour-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The other tour being in support to <a href="/wiki/Overkill_(band)" title="Overkill (band)">Overkill</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Vader_(band)" title="Vader (band)">Vader</a>, <a href="/wiki/God_Dethroned" title="God Dethroned">God Dethroned</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warbringer" title="Warbringer">Warbringer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-overkilltour_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overkilltour-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2010 Evile would pay tribute to their late bassist, Pantera and Dimebag Darrell by performing their cover of "Cemetery Gates" in the latter's birthplace of <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> while on tour with <a href="/wiki/Overkill_(band)" title="Overkill (band)">Overkill</a> in North America. Also in April 2010, it was announced that Evile would be returning to <a href="/wiki/Bloodstock_Open_Air" title="Bloodstock Open Air">Bloodstock Open Air</a>, playing main stage on Saturday 14 August.<sup id="cite_ref-bloodstockopenair_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bloodstockopenair-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Evile will also be playing <a href="/wiki/Hellfest_Summer_Open_Air" class="mw-redirect" title="Hellfest Summer Open Air">Hellfest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wacken_Open_Air" title="Wacken Open Air">Wacken Open Air</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sonisphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Sonisphere">Sonisphere</a> and many more European Festivals.
</p><p>Evile's track "Bathe in Blood" was used in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Ondine_(film)" title="Ondine (film)">Ondine</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Neil_Jordan" title="Neil Jordan">Neil Jordan</a> and starring <a href="/wiki/Colin_Farrell" title="Colin Farrell">Colin Farrell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-evileondine_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-evileondine-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From September 2010 to the end of December the band toured extensively throughout North America. The band had several headline dates as well as tours with veterans such as <a href="/wiki/Overkill_(band)" title="Overkill (band)">Overkill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dirty_Rotten_Imbeciles" title="Dirty Rotten Imbeciles">D.R.I.</a> and fellow thrash-band <a href="/wiki/Gama_Bomb" title="Gama Bomb">Gama Bomb</a>. Also in February, Ol Drake took part in a "Game with Fame" session via <a href="/wiki/Xbox_Live" title="Xbox Live">Xbox Live</a>, inviting gamers to play the videogame <i><a href="/wiki/Rockband_3" class="mw-redirect" title="Rockband 3">Rockband 3</a></i> with him. The event was advertised on the Xbox Live Dashboard and took place for 3 hours (5pm-8pm EST).<sup id="cite_ref-evilegwfxbox_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-evilegwfxbox-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-eviledashboard_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-eviledashboard-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Around this time the entire <i>Infected Nations</i> album became available via the <a href="/wiki/Rock_Band_Network" title="Rock Band Network">Rock Band Network</a>, as well as the band achieving "Artist of the Month".<sup id="cite_ref-aotmrbnevile_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aotmrbnevile-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-evilerbnsongs_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-evilerbnsongs-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Five_Serpent.27s_Teeth_.282010.E2.80.932012.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Five_Serpent's_Teeth_(2010–2012)"><i>Five Serpent's Teeth</i> (2010–2012)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Five Serpent&#039;s Teeth (2010–2012)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 31 May, Evile revealed that they'd begun working on their third studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-evileinterview_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-evileinterview-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Ol Drake confirmed this via TwitLonger on 9 November and told that "Album 3 is well on the way and will be out on Earache Records in mid 2011".<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> In February 2011 Evile embarked on a short UK tour to say goodbye to their "Infected Nations" album, while also showcasing a brand new track with the working title "Bitch". Matt also announced the third album will have the initials "FST".<sup id="cite_ref-evileuk2011_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-evileuk2011-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> In March 2011 it was announced that Evile had entered Parlour Studios to begin recording their 3rd album with producer <a href="/wiki/Russ_Russell" title="Russ Russell">Russ Russell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Periodically, the band have been posting excerpts of the album's recording process on their official <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> page.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> On 25 April, the band announced that their 3rd album was fully mixed and awaiting mastering.<sup id="cite_ref-album3done_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-album3done-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2011 Evile announced that they would pay tribute to their fallen band mate, Mike Alexander, on the latest album. The track was entitled "In Memoriam".<sup id="cite_ref-mikememoriam_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mikememoriam-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Ol Drake commented:
</p>
<blockquote><p>It goes without saying that there would be some element of the new album which was for Mike alone. We had a few ideas floating around which were out of the ordinary for Evile, and when Mike passed away those ideas just started to make more and more sense. "In Memoriam"'s initial intention was for Mike and Mike alone. All of us in the band have different views and thoughts on what the song means and who it is for, but it was born out of a want to include something special for Mike, his family and ourselves. As the song grew it started to take on a life of its own, so to speak. It is for anyone who's lost anyone close to them. There is a lot of meaning behind this song, and I expect a few people will question Evile doing such a song, but if the sentiment isn't understood, I couldn't care less. As our producer, Russ Russell said: "It's a brilliant song for a brilliant guy."</p></blockquote>
<p>It has been revealed through hard rock/heavy metal magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Metal_Hammer" title="Metal Hammer">Metal Hammer</a></i> that the title for their third album would be <i><a href="/wiki/Five_Serpent%27s_Teeth" title="Five Serpent&#39;s Teeth">Five Serpent's Teeth</a></i> to be released 26 September 2011 in Europe and 18 October 2011 in North America through Earache Records. On 6 June, the band posted a new song, titled "Eternal Empire", on their YouTube channel. On 12 September 2011 the album's first single/music video, "Cult", was released. The video saw a different, more modern approach.<sup id="cite_ref-cultrelease_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cultrelease-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> In September/October 2011 the band set out on a 15-date headline tour of the UK with label-mates <a href="/wiki/Savage_Messiah_(band)" title="Savage Messiah (band)">Savage Messiah (band)</a> as support.<sup id="cite_ref-evileuktoursep2011_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-evileuktoursep2011-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> The band were then asked by <i><a href="/wiki/Kerrang!" title="Kerrang!">Kerrang!</a></i> Magazine to cover a <a href="/wiki/Nirvana_(band)" title="Nirvana (band)">Nirvana</a> song for <i><a href="/wiki/Nevermind" title="Nevermind">Nevermind</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s 20th Anniversary Tribute Issue. Evile covered the track "Lounge Act".<sup id="cite_ref-evilenirvana_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-evilenirvana-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Ol Drake commented:
</p>
<blockquote><p>When we got the offer, all I could see was fun to be had, and what an honor it was to be included. I was a big fan of Nirvana when I was a young teen, so it's an extra honor in that way for me. I wondered what we could do to the track to make it our own, so we approached it like we would one of our own songs. That bass riff is brilliant and I wanted to make it more a core element of the track guitar-wise and just metal it up in general. I wanted to respect the approach of the song but also alter Kurt's [Cobain] chord approach to a style more suited to EVILE, and that's a chug-athon!</p></blockquote>
<p>In October Evile celebrated the release of <i>Five Serpent's Teeth</i> by taking part in in-store signing sessions in select Blue Banana stores as well as a short tour of Release Shows in the UK; London, Birmingham and Holmfirth.<sup id="cite_ref-BlueBanana_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BlueBanana-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> On 17 October The band were featured in a Live Session on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a>'s <i>Rock Show</i> with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_P._Carter" title="Daniel P. Carter">Daniel P. Carter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fstbbc1_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fstbbc1-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> The set was recorded at <a href="/wiki/Maida_Vale_Studios" title="Maida Vale Studios">Maida Vale Studios</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. In November Evile announced a 24-date Headlining tour of Europe with Portrait and Dr. Living Dead as supports.<sup id="cite_ref-fsteurotour_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fsteurotour-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 11 February 2012 Evile took part in, and headlined, <a href="/wiki/HMV_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="HMV Group">HMV Group</a>'s "Next Big Thing" at the HMV Ritz in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, UK along with <a href="/wiki/Cerebral_Bore" title="Cerebral Bore">Cerebral Bore</a> and Savage Messiah. After the death of <a href="/wiki/Woods_of_Ypres" title="Woods of Ypres">Woods of Ypres</a> mastermind David Gold, the band decided to make the first live debut of "In Memoriam" a dedication to him.<sup id="cite_ref-memoriamlivea_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-memoriamlivea-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-memoriamliveb_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-memoriamliveb-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> "In Memoriam" was initially written as a tribute to their fallen band mate, Mike Alexander. In March 2012, Evile's lead guitarist Ol Drake was nominated for the "Dimebag Darrell Shredder" Award as part of <a href="/wiki/Metal_Hammer_Golden_Gods_Awards" title="Metal Hammer Golden Gods Awards">Metal Hammer Golden Gods Awards</a>. Also up for the award were <a href="/wiki/Devin_Townsend" title="Devin Townsend">Devin Townsend</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dino_Cazares" title="Dino Cazares">Dino Cazares</a> (of <a href="/wiki/Fear_Factory" title="Fear Factory">Fear Factory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russ_Parrish" title="Russ Parrish">Russ Parrish</a>),  Satchel of <a href="/wiki/Steel_Panther" title="Steel Panther">Steel Panther</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Adam_Dutkiewicz" title="Adam Dutkiewicz">Adam Dutkiewicz</a> of <a href="/wiki/Killswitch_Engage" title="Killswitch Engage">Killswitch Engage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-oldimebag_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oldimebag-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> In April 2012 Evile announced they would start filming a music video for the track "In Memoriam".<sup id="cite_ref-annoucememoriam_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-annoucememoriam-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> Ol Drake commented:
</p>
<blockquote><p>The initial idea was to release the "Cult" video, as we did, then immediately get onto recording the video for "In Memoriam"; this was easier said than done. It's been well documented why the song exists. When it came to getting the "right"/"appropriate" treatment for the video, it was a lengthy process. The song is so close to all of us that we wanted to make sure the video complemented the song and its subject matter, and that all four of us were happy with how the song will be portrayed visually. If we'd have settled on the first idea after "Cult", we wouldn't have been happy, so amidst touring and gigging, we've been working hard on getting this 100% before we move on to the next video. The song is basically about loss, which we can all relate to, so we wanted to express that in the video, while staying respectful to why the song exists. We can't wait to get this filmed and out there for all to enjoy!</p></blockquote>
<h3><span id="Skull_and_the_departure_of_Ol_Drake_.282013.E2.80.932015.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Skull_and_the_departure_of_Ol_Drake_(2013–2015)"><i>Skull</i> and the departure of Ol Drake (2013–2015)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Skull and the departure of Ol Drake (2013–2015)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 31 January 2013, Ol Drake announced via his Tumblr that Evile were to begin recording their fourth album the following weekend. He also confirmed that the band would again utilise producer Russ Russell.<sup id="cite_ref-olskull_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-olskull-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> On 25 March, Ben Carter and Earache Records released the album name, cover and release date of their fourth album, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Skull_(album)" title="Skull (album)">Skull</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-skulltum_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-skulltum-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-skullblog_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-skullblog-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Guitarist Ol Drake announced his departure from Evile in August 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2014, Evile announced that Piers Donno-Fuller (formerly of Fallen Fate) would be joining them as lead guitarist, and that they "have something in the pipeline for March".<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="New_album_and_member_changes_.282016.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="New_album_and_member_changes_(2016–present)">New album and member changes (2016–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: New album and member changes (2016–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of March 2016, Evile have been working on their fifth studio album.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 19 March 2018, Evile announced the departure of Piers due to personal commitments. 
</p><p>On April 24, the band announced Oliver Drake had rejoined the band.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_style_and_influences">Musical style and influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Musical style and influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Evile's sound is primarily influenced by classic <a href="/wiki/Thrash_metal" title="Thrash metal">thrash metal</a>, citing bands such as <a href="/wiki/Overkill_(band)" title="Overkill (band)">Overkill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Megadeth" title="Megadeth">Megadeth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slayer" title="Slayer">Slayer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exodus_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Exodus (band)">Exodus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Testament_(band)" title="Testament (band)">Testament</a>, <a href="/wiki/Annihilator_(band)" title="Annihilator (band)">Annihilator</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevermore" title="Nevermore">Nevermore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pantera" title="Pantera">Pantera</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sepultura" title="Sepultura">Sepultura</a>, though they are also influenced by <a href="/wiki/Death_metal" title="Death metal">death metal</a> bands like <a href="/wiki/Obituary_(band)" title="Obituary (band)">Obituary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-myspaceinfluences_73-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-myspaceinfluences-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
