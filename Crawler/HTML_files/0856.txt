<p><b>Shollen Quarshie</b> (born 1985),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> known by his stage name <b>DJ Q</b>, is an English <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a> and record producer. He forms part of the group TQD alongside <a href="/wiki/Flava_D" title="Flava D">Flava D</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal-T" title="Royal-T">Royal-T</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Q&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>DJ Q’s musical journey started from a young age at The Nook in <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth" title="Holmfirth">Holmfirth</a>, listening to music played by his parents. It was at the age of 12 when 'Q' first got involved with the <a href="/wiki/UK_garage" title="UK garage">UK garage</a> scene, buying vinyls and tape packs. When his vinyl collection grew larger Q finally got a pair of record decks in his bedroom. After teaching himself to mix, Q started to get bookings at local parties at the age of 15, before learning to produce music the next year at college.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Q&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Q's break on <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a> came in 2004, when a friend working at <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1Xtra" title="BBC Radio 1Xtra">BBC Radio 1Xtra</a> informed him that the station were looking for a new <a href="/wiki/DJ" class="mw-redirect" title="DJ">DJ</a> on the pulse of the rising <a href="/wiki/Bassline_(music_genre)" title="Bassline (music genre)">bassline</a> scene in the North of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. He submitted a mix <a href="/wiki/CD" class="mw-redirect" title="CD">CD</a>, and Q eventually became the youngest DJ at <a href="/wiki/1Xtra" class="mw-redirect" title="1Xtra">1Xtra</a>, hosting the fortnightly UKG Mix Show. 
<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was around the same time Q released his first vinyl, "Love Like This (The Reasons)" which was a big hit in the <a href="/wiki/Bassline_(music_genre)" title="Bassline (music genre)">bassline</a> scene. This brought him more bookings at bassline events and as time passed, Q produced more hits such as "Tea Bag", "Dirty" and later "You Wot!", which was signed and released via <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Sound" title="Ministry of Sound">Ministry of Sound</a>. "You Wot!" made the <a href="/wiki/UK_Top_50" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Top 50">UK Top 50</a> upon its release in summer 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-Mendoza_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mendoza-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> which led to remix work for the likes of <a href="/wiki/Amy_Winehouse" title="Amy Winehouse">Amy Winehouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dizzee_Rascal" title="Dizzee Rascal">Dizzee Rascal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keri_Hilson" title="Keri Hilson">Keri Hilson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Katy_B" title="Katy B">Katy B</a> to name a few.
<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> 
<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> 
<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Q continued to host his <a href="/wiki/BBC_1Xtra" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC 1Xtra">BBC 1Xtra</a> show – by now a weekly <a href="/wiki/Broadcast" class="mw-redirect" title="Broadcast">broadcast</a> - throughout his mainstream success, and frequently appeared on sister station <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a> for guest <a href="/wiki/DJ_mix" title="DJ mix">mixes</a>, before his departure in 2012 after 8 years at the station. He soon began a new monthly show on <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Sound_Radio" title="Ministry of Sound Radio">Ministry of Sound Radio</a>, presenting the Essential Garage show.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Recent years have seen an increase in new productions from Q, with over 10 digital single and EP releases since 2012 that cross a broad spectrum of <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance music</a>. These include "Say to You" (released under the alias Classified),<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> the <i>Sound Boy Connection</i> EP via US label Party Like Us,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and three singles from his debut album via Local Action Records.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During his years in the <a href="/wiki/Music_industry" title="Music industry">music industry</a>, Q has also played music in countries across the globe and Europe particularly, with sets in the likes of <a href="/wiki/Ibiza" title="Ibiza">Ibiza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">America</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_with_TQD">Work with TQD</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=DJ_Q&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Work with TQD">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>TQD</b> (often stylised as <b>t q d</b> or <b>tqd</b>) is a collaborative act that was formed in 2015, comprising <a href="/wiki/Royal-T" title="Royal-T">Royal-T</a>, DJ Q and <a href="/wiki/Flava_D" title="Flava D">Flava D</a>, with the name TQD being an amalgamation of the last letter of each of their names. All three artists were already signed to the <a href="/wiki/Butterz" title="Butterz">Butterz</a> record label and thus all TQD releases have also been released on Butterz.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The act play and produce the genres of music that the individuals do (such as UK garage, bassline and grime), however they have released experimental tracks that cross into many other genres.
</p><p>In 2015, Royal-T and DJ Q were collaborating on their first project together and they asked Flava D if she wanted to join in on the project too. This would then go on to be their debut single "Day and Night", released on Butterz on the 18 April 2015, both digitally and as a limited 500 copy vinyl pressing.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> This track was released as "Day and Night (Day Mix)", however an alternate mix of the single was also released on the B-side as "Day and Night (Night Mix)". Due to their solo efforts and differences in location however, TQD releases were sparse with their second track titled "Ghosts" being previewed digitally over a year later in 2016 and then being released on their second vinyl release <i>Only One/Ghosts</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The trio gained ample attention due to their releases and increasingly popular live sets and thus began working on an album together. This was formally announced at the beginning of 2017 to be the debut album, <i>ukg</i> and would be released on March 17, 2017. <sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>. The album contains features from <a href="/wiki/P_Money" title="P Money">P Money</a>, Swindle and Skilliam and was met with generally positive reviews. <i><a href="/wiki/Pitchfork_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pitchfork (magazine)">Pitchfork</a></i> called the track "Vibsing Ting" "strange and outstanding"<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Mixmag" title="Mixmag">Mixmag</a></i> said it consisted of "10 hard-hitting face melters."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> However, <a href="/wiki/Clash_(magazine)" title="Clash (magazine)"><i>Clash</i> magazine</a> wrote that the album was "disappointingly short" and that "there’s a feeling that t q d aren’t really a case for three heads being better than one."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Bandcamp" title="Bandcamp">Bandcamp</a> release of <i>ukg</i> contained three extra tracks that were not present on the vinyl or physical CD releases.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Though they have not made any official releases since their debut album as a trio, each member continues to produce and release music individually and the act still performs shows in many countries around the world, including the United Kingdom, Spain and Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
