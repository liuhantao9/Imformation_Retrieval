<p>Sir <b>Albert Kaye Rollit</b> (1842 – 12 August 1922) was a <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">British</a> politician, <a href="/wiki/Lawyer" title="Lawyer">lawyer</a>, and businessman.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Rollit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Hull</a>, he became a solicitor and went on to become president of the <a href="/wiki/Law_Society_of_England_and_Wales" title="Law Society of England and Wales">Law Society</a>. He later became a shipowner. He was <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mayors_of_Kingston_upon_Hull" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Mayors of Kingston upon Hull">Mayor of Hull</a> from 1883-1885. In 1886, he was elected as a <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Islington_South_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Islington South (UK Parliament constituency)">South Islington constituency</a>. In 1892 he put forward a <a href="/wiki/Private_Member%27s_Bill" class="mw-redirect" title="Private Member&#39;s Bill">private member's bill</a> in favour of <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_suffrage" title="Women&#39;s suffrage">women's suffrage</a>, which failed narrowly. Having opposed <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Chamberlain" title="Joseph Chamberlain">Chamberlain</a>'s Tariff Reform proposals, he was defeated in the 1906 general election, and failed to get elected as a <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal</a> in <a href="/wiki/Epsom_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Epsom (UK Parliament constituency)">Epsom</a> in 1910. As a businessman he was well known on the Continent of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and acted as consul-general for <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> from 1911 until his death. He was also a <a href="/wiki/Magistrate" title="Magistrate">magistrate</a> in <a href="/wiki/Berkshire" title="Berkshire">Berkshire</a>, where he resided at Sutherland Grange at <a href="/wiki/Dedworth" title="Dedworth">Dedworth</a>, adjoining <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Berkshire" title="Windsor, Berkshire">Windsor</a>, with his second wife.
</p><p>He received the <a href="/wiki/Honorary_degree" title="Honorary degree">honorary degree</a> <a href="/wiki/Legum_Doctor" title="Legum Doctor">Doctor of Laws</a> (LL.D.) from the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_University_of_Manchester" title="Victoria University of Manchester">Victoria University of Manchester</a> in February 1902, in connection with the 50th jubilee celebrations of the establishment of the university.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was elected a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Horticultural_Society" title="Royal Horticultural Society">Royal Horticultural Society</a> in 1902.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1902, Rollin received the decoration Officer of the <a href="/wiki/Legion_d%27honeur" class="mw-redirect" title="Legion d&#39;honeur">Legion d'honeur</a> from the French Republic in recognition of his services towards the development of commercial relations between the United Kingdom and France, and especially for his services in connection with the Universal Exhibition of 1900.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Rollit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Thinktank_Birmingham_-_Rollit_A.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Thinktank_Birmingham_-_Rollit_A.jpg/220px-Thinktank_Birmingham_-_Rollit_A.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="280" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Thinktank_Birmingham_-_Rollit_A.jpg/330px-Thinktank_Birmingham_-_Rollit_A.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Thinktank_Birmingham_-_Rollit_A.jpg/440px-Thinktank_Birmingham_-_Rollit_A.jpg 2x" data-file-width="805" data-file-height="1024" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Thinktank_Birmingham_-_Rollit_A.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rollit</div></div></div>
<p>Rollit married twice. His second wife was Mary, <a href="/wiki/Dowager" title="Dowager">dowager</a> <a href="/wiki/George_Sutherland-Leveson-Gower,_3rd_Duke_of_Sutherland" title="George Sutherland-Leveson-Gower, 3rd Duke of Sutherland">Duchess of Sutherland</a>. He was her third husband. In 1898 her jewellery, then valued at £30,000, was stolen by international jewel-thief William Johnson, known as 'Harry the Valet'.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  Johnson stole the jewellery while she was travelling by train from <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> to <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> with her husband, (Rollit), her brother, his wife and the Duchess's footman and maid. The case was investigated by <a href="/wiki/Police_inspector" class="mw-redirect" title="Police inspector">Inspectors</a> <a href="/wiki/Walter_Dew" title="Walter Dew">Walter Dew</a>, Walter Dinnie and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Froest" title="Frank Froest">Frank Froest</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Albert_Rollit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Steam_Pumping_Engine_Rollit.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Steam_Pumping_Engine_Rollit.JPG/220px-Steam_Pumping_Engine_Rollit.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="254" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Steam_Pumping_Engine_Rollit.JPG/330px-Steam_Pumping_Engine_Rollit.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Steam_Pumping_Engine_Rollit.JPG/440px-Steam_Pumping_Engine_Rollit.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3112" data-file-height="3600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Steam_Pumping_Engine_Rollit.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Rollit engine at <a href="/wiki/Thinktank,_Birmingham_Science_Museum" title="Thinktank, Birmingham Science Museum">Thinktank, Birmingham Science Museum</a></div></div></div>
<p>The Rollit steam pumping engine, built in 1883, and which formerly pumped sewage at Hull, was named in his honour. It is now preserved at <a href="/wiki/Thinktank,_Birmingham_Science_Museum" title="Thinktank, Birmingham Science Museum">Thinktank, Birmingham Science Museum</a>, and carries a brass plaque bearing the name.
</p>
