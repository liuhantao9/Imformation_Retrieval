<p><b>Earith</b> is a village and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cambridgeshire" title="Cambridgeshire">Cambridgeshire</a>, England.<sup id="cite_ref-OS142_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OS142-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Earith lies approximately 10 miles (16&#160;km) east of <a href="/wiki/Huntingdon" title="Huntingdon">Huntingdon</a>. Earith is situated within <a href="/wiki/Huntingdonshire" title="Huntingdonshire">Huntingdonshire</a> which is a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">non-metropolitan district</a> of Cambridgeshire as well as being a <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">historic county</a> of England. At Earith, two artificial diversion channels of the <a href="/wiki/River_Great_Ouse" title="River Great Ouse">River Great Ouse</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Old_Bedford_River" title="Old Bedford River">Old Bedford River</a> and the <a href="/wiki/New_Bedford_River" title="New Bedford River">New Bedford River</a>, leave the river on a course to <a href="/wiki/Denver,_Norfolk" title="Denver, Norfolk">Denver Sluice</a> near <a href="/wiki/Downham_Market" title="Downham Market">Downham Market</a>, where they rejoin the Great Ouse in its tidal part. At the 2001 census, the parish had a population of 1,677,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> reducing to 1,606 at the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:UK_Earith.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/UK_Earith.jpg/170px-UK_Earith.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="366" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/UK_Earith.jpg/255px-UK_Earith.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/UK_Earith.jpg/340px-UK_Earith.jpg 2x" data-file-width="776" data-file-height="1672" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:UK_Earith.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Signpost in Earith</div></div></div>

<p>A <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> bronze statue was found to the west of the village at Bury Fen in 1826. It is now in the <a href="/wiki/British_Museum" title="British Museum">British Museum</a>.
</p><p>To the north east of the village, between the <a href="/wiki/Old_Bedford_River" title="Old Bedford River">Old Bedford River</a> and the <a href="/wiki/New_Bedford_River" title="New Bedford River">New Bedford River</a> lies the remains of an <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a> fort known as Earith Bulwark. It is believed to have been constructed as early as 1643, to fortify the crossing point of the <a href="/wiki/River_Great_Ouse" title="River Great Ouse">River Great Ouse</a>. Many fenland inhabitants resented the <a href="/wiki/Cavalier" title="Cavalier">Royalists</a> because they believed that the King's drainage projects undertaken by Dutch engineer <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Vermuyden" title="Cornelius Vermuyden">Cornelius Vermuyden</a>, had damaged their livelihoods (his <a href="/wiki/Forty_Foot_Drain" title="Forty Foot Drain">Forty Foot Drain</a> had been completed in 1637). Today, the fort is visible as a series of earthworks only, showing a <a href="/wiki/Bastion_fort" title="Bastion fort">bastion fort</a> design with ditches and corner <a href="/wiki/Bastions" class="mw-redirect" title="Bastions">bastions</a>. It is similar in design to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Nassau_(Netherlands)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fort Nassau (Netherlands) (page does not exist)">Fort Nassau</a> at <a href="/wiki/Aardenburg" title="Aardenburg">Aardenburg</a> in the Netherlands. During the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, a machine gun turret was built on the south bastion.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 17 January, 1942 a number of RAF serviceman were killed in a crash involving a <a href="/wiki/Hawker_Hurricane" title="Hawker Hurricane">Hawker Hurricane</a> from 61 Operational Training Unit and a <a href="/wiki/Short_Stirling" title="Short Stirling">Short Stirling</a> bomber. The Stirling was flying from <a href="/wiki/RAF_Oakington" title="RAF Oakington">RAF Oakington</a> with 7 Squadron and the accident occurred just north of Earith Bridge.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Winter_of_1946%E2%80%9347_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Winter of 1946–47 in the United Kingdom">Winter of 1946–47</a>, there was the "Battle of Earith Gap". Melting snow had built up in the <a href="/wiki/The_Fens" title="The Fens">Fenland</a> waterways, unable to be released into the sea because of high tides. On 17 March a 50 yards (46&#160;m) gap was torn in the river bank, and water poured out to flood the Fen farms for five days. Eventually the breach was blocked with amphibious vehicles while more orthodox repair work was carried out.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a civil parish, Earith has a <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">parish council</a>. The parish council is elected by the residents of the parish who have registered on the <a href="/wiki/Electoral_roll" title="Electoral roll">electoral roll</a>; the parish council is the lowest tier of government in England. A parish council is responsible for providing and maintaining a variety of local services including allotments and a cemetery; grass cutting and tree planting within public open spaces such as a village green or playing fields . The parish council reviews all planning applications that might affect the parish and makes recommendations to Huntingdonshire District Council, which is the <a href="/wiki/Local_planning_authority" title="Local planning authority">local planning authority</a> for the parish. The parish council also represents the views of the parish on issues such as local transport, policing and the environment. The parish council raises its own tax to pay for these services, known as the parish precept, which is collected as part of the <a href="/wiki/Council_Tax" title="Council Tax">Council Tax</a>. The parish council consists of eleven councillors and normally meets on the first Thursday of the month at the village hall.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Earith was in the historic and <a href="/wiki/Administrative_county" title="Administrative county">administrative county</a> of Huntingdonshire until 1965. From 1965, the village was part of the new administrative county of <a href="/wiki/Huntingdon_and_Peterborough" title="Huntingdon and Peterborough">Huntingdon and Peterborough</a>. Then in 1974, following the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, Earith became a part of the county of Cambridgeshire. 
</p><p>The second tier of local government is <a href="/wiki/Huntingdonshire_District_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Huntingdonshire District Council">Huntingdonshire District Council</a> which is a <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">non-metropolitan district</a> of Cambridgeshire and has its headquarters in Huntingdon. Huntingdonshire District Council has 52 councillors representing 29 <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Wards of the United Kingdom">district wards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HDC1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HDC1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Huntingdonshire District Council collects the <a href="/wiki/Council_tax" class="mw-redirect" title="Council tax">council tax</a>, and provides services such as building regulations, local planning, environmental health, leisure and tourism.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Earith is a district ward and is represented on the district council by two councillors.<sup id="cite_ref-OSelec_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OSelec-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HDC1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HDC1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> District councillors serve for four year terms following <a href="/wiki/Huntingdonshire_District_Council_elections" title="Huntingdonshire District Council elections">elections to Huntingdonshire District Council</a>.
</p><p>For Earith the highest tier of local government is <a href="/wiki/Cambridgeshire_County_Council" title="Cambridgeshire County Council">Cambridgeshire County Council</a> which has administration buildings in Cambridge. The county council provides county-wide services such as major road infrastructure, fire and rescue, education, social services, libraries and heritage services.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Cambridgeshire County Council consists of 69 councillors representing 60 <a href="/wiki/Electoral_divisions_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Electoral divisions (UK)">electoral divisions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CCC1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CCC1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Earith is part of the electoral division of <i>Somersham and Earith</i> <sup id="cite_ref-OSelec_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OSelec-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and is represented on the county council by one councillor.<sup id="cite_ref-CCC1_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CCC1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At Westminster Earith is in the parliamentary constituency of <a href="/wiki/North_West_Cambridgeshire_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="North West Cambridgeshire (UK Parliament constituency)">North West Cambridgeshire</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-OSelec_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OSelec-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and elects one <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> (MP) by the <a href="/wiki/First_past_the_post" class="mw-redirect" title="First past the post">first past the post</a> system of election. Earith is represented in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="House of Commons of the United Kingdom">House of Commons</a> by <a href="/wiki/Shailesh_Vara" title="Shailesh Vara">Shailesh Vara</a> (<a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a>). Shailesh Vara has represented the constituency since 2005. The previous member of parliament was <a href="/wiki/Brian_Mawhinney" title="Brian Mawhinney">Brian Mawhinney</a> (Conservative) who represented the constituency between 1997 and 2005. For the <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a> Earith is part of the <a href="/wiki/East_of_England_(European_Parliament_constituency)" title="East of England (European Parliament constituency)">East of England constituency</a> which elects seven <a href="/wiki/Members_of_the_European_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Members of the European Parliament">MEPs</a> using the <a href="/wiki/D%27Hondt_method" title="D&#39;Hondt method">d'Hondt method</a> of <a href="/wiki/Party-list_proportional_representation" title="Party-list proportional representation">party-list proportional representation</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_community">Culture and community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Culture and community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village has a post office, a Tandoori Takeaway, a Hair Salon, one public house (<i>The Crown</i>), a part-time surgery and a school. In June 2013 the Riverview Pub was converted into Earith Tearooms. The village does not have a parish church.
</p><p>A business now occupies what was once the National School, and the <a href="/wiki/Wesleyan" class="mw-redirect" title="Wesleyan">Wesleyan</a> chapel, erected in 1828, is now a private house.The village is home to a <a href="/wiki/Marina" title="Marina">marina</a>, and during winter months <a href="/wiki/Ice_skating" title="Ice skating">ice skating</a> is popular at nearby Bury Fen. Both activities are depicted in the village sign.
</p><p>Two large lakes were formed to the north of the village during gravel quarrying in the 1960s. Today the site is home to a <a href="/wiki/Fishery" title="Fishery">fishery</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village was connected to the railway network in 1865, with a <a href="/wiki/Earith_Bridge_railway_station" title="Earith Bridge railway station">railway station</a> located to the east of the village, in an area named <a href="/wiki/Earith_Bridge_railway_station" title="Earith Bridge railway station">Earith Bridge</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/Ely_and_St_Ives_Railway" title="Ely and St Ives Railway">Ely and St Ives Railway</a>. Passenger services were discontinued in 1931, and the Earith section of railway was closed in 1958. 
</p><p>The long distance footpath the <a href="/wiki/Ouse_Valley_Way" title="Ouse Valley Way">Ouse Valley Way</a> links the village with <a href="/wiki/Stretham" title="Stretham">Stretham</a> (11 miles (18&#160;km)) and <a href="/wiki/St_Ives,_Cambridgeshire" title="St Ives, Cambridgeshire">St. Ives</a> (6 miles (10&#160;km)). 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_in_Huntingdonshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Stagecoach in Huntingdonshire">Stagecoach in Huntingdonshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Whippet_Coaches" class="mw-redirect" title="Whippet Coaches">Whippet Coaches</a> operate bus routes that serve Earith. Whippet Coaches routes 21 and 22A, and the Busway A service of Stagecoach in Huntingdonshire, connect Earith to <a href="/wiki/Somersham" title="Somersham">Somersham</a> and St Ives. At peak times, the Stagecoach route continues via the <a href="/wiki/Cambridgeshire_Guided_Busway" title="Cambridgeshire Guided Busway">Cambridgeshire Guided Busway</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
