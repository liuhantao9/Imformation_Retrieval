<p><b>Brighouse railway station</b> serves the town of <a href="/wiki/Brighouse" title="Brighouse">Brighouse</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The station lies on the <a href="/wiki/Calder_Valley_line" title="Calder Valley line">Calder Valley line</a> running west from <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>. The station reopened in 2000 and is managed by <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> but is also served by <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brighouse_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brighouse railway station was first opened on 5 October 1840, as a main line station operated by the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_and_Leeds_Railway" title="Manchester and Leeds Railway">Manchester and Leeds Railway</a>. The station was initially known as Brighouse for <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, as no stations had yet been built in Bradford itself. Similarly, <a href="/wiki/Elland_railway_station" title="Elland railway station">Elland station</a> served Halifax, and Huddersfield was served by the station at <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Bridge_railway_station" title="Cooper Bridge railway station">Cooper Bridge</a>.
</p><p>A lithograph was produced by <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Fitzwilliam_Tait" title="Arthur Fitzwilliam Tait">Arthur Fitzwilliam Tait</a> showing the station in 1845.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1847 Brighouse station came under the control of the <a href="/wiki/Lancashire_and_Yorkshire_Railway" title="Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway">Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway</a>, when the M&amp;L was incorporated into that company. The station remained under L&amp;Y operation until it was incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/London,_Midland_and_Scottish_Railway" title="London, Midland and Scottish Railway">London, Midland and Scottish Railway</a> in 1923. The station's importance had gradually declined because other lines connecting <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> and Leeds were built via Halifax and Huddersfield, bypassing Brighouse.
</p><p>Brighouse station passed into <a href="/wiki/British_Railways" class="mw-redirect" title="British Railways">British Railways</a> ownership upon nationalisation in 1948,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and was operated as part of the <a href="/wiki/North_Eastern_Region_of_British_Railways" title="North Eastern Region of British Railways">North Eastern Region</a>. The decline in passenger numbers continued, and the station was eventually closed by <a href="/wiki/British_Rail" title="British Rail">British Rail</a> on 5 January 1970, remaining closed for thirty years. The line remained open throughout that time as a freight-only one, but it was also used as a diversionary route for passenger trains when other lines were closed.
</p><p>Brighouse station reopened on Sunday 28 May 2000. It also serves the town of <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a> some 2 miles (3&#160;km) away. Plans for a station in Elland to open at the same time as Brighouse were cancelled due to lack of funds.<sup id="cite_ref-Rail_722_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rail_722-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On opening the station was served by one train per hour running to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> via <a href="/wiki/Halifax_railway_station_(West_Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Halifax railway station (West Yorkshire)">Halifax</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradford Interchange railway station">Bradford</a>, and one train per hour in the other direction to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield</a>.  Trains called every two hours on Sundays.
</p><p>From 27 April 2008 a bus service <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://archive.is/20130209160943/http://www.wymetro.com/dc4de7a262cb43cc83bf86b050122e2d">MC4</a> provided a connection with most Leeds via Dewsbury trains to/from Elland. This was replaced in early 2010 by a revised service <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://archive.is/20130209160943/http://www.wymetro.com/dc4de7a262cb43cc83bf86b050122e2d">E8</a>.
</p><p>In December 2008, the service was supplemented by an hourly Leeds - <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_railway_station" title="Dewsbury railway station">Dewsbury</a> - <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Bridge_railway_station" title="Hebden Bridge railway station">Hebden Bridge</a> - <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Victoria">Manchester Victoria</a> - <a href="/wiki/Southport_railway_station" title="Southport railway station">Southport</a> stopping service (Monday - Saturday daytime only, no late evening or Sunday service). This provided a considerable service improvement providing both a twice-hourly frequency for journeys to/from Leeds and a reduction in journey time taking only 35 minutes to travel to Leeds via <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_railway_station" title="Dewsbury railway station">Dewsbury</a> rather than 50 minutes via Halifax. Even without this important improvement in services, usage of the station has increased year on year since the reopening. Some services from Leeds terminate at Brighouse and start back from here.
</p><p>In January 2009, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a> had their application for <a href="/wiki/Train_path" title="Train path">train paths</a> to run a <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange" title="Bradford Interchange">Bradford Interchange</a> (via <a href="/wiki/Halifax_railway_station_(West_Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Halifax railway station (West Yorkshire)">Halifax</a>) to London service accepted by the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Rail_Regulation" class="mw-redirect" title="Office of Rail Regulation">Office of Rail Regulation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This service commenced on 23 May 2010.
</p><p>In May 2018, the Sunday service from Leeds to Huddersfield via Brighouse was doubled in frequency to one per hour in each direction.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brighouse_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of May 2018 Brighouse is regularly served by two trains per hour to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>, one train per hour to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield</a> and one train per hour to <a href="/wiki/Southport_railway_station" title="Southport railway station">Southport</a> via <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Bridge_railway_station" title="Hebden Bridge railway station">Hebden Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Victoria railway station">Manchester Victoria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Leeds service includes a faster journey via <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_railway_station" title="Dewsbury railway station">Dewsbury</a> and a slower journey via <a href="/wiki/Halifax_railway_station_(West_Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Halifax railway station (West Yorkshire)">Halifax</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradford Interchange railway station">Bradford Interchange</a>.
</p><p>On Sundays there is one train per hour to Leeds (via Halifax and Bradford) and one train per hour to Huddersfield, where passengers can change for services to Manchester.
</p><p>In addition to the regular service pattern, there are four direct return services per day to <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a> via <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Kirkgate_railway_station" title="Wakefield Kirkgate railway station">Wakefield Kirkgate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_railway_station" title="Doncaster railway station">Doncaster</a>, including Sundays.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_facilities">Station facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brighouse_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Station facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station has two platforms.
</p>
<ul><li>Platform 1: served by services towards Huddersfield and Leeds (via Dewsbury).</li>
<li>Platform 2: served by services towards Manchester Victoria, and Leeds (via Halifax and Bradford Interchange).</li></ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Passenger_Transport_Executive" class="mw-redirect" title="West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive">Metro</a> secured joint funding from the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Transport" title="Department for Transport">Department for Transport</a>'s Access for All – Small Schemes initiative to provide a new Customer Information System at the station.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>New LCD style customer information screens with real-time information have been installed on both platforms linked into an improved public address system providing accurate, reliable audio/visual train running information to passengers.
</p><p>In addition; Northern has announced plans to replace the station's HelpPoint intercom system with a more updated reliable model. This will enable passengers to speak to a member of staff in Northern's control centre for information or to summon assistance in emergency situations.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
