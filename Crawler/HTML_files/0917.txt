<p><b>Wetherby</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;w&#39; in &#39;wind&#39;">w</span><span title="/ɛ/: &#39;e&#39; in &#39;dress&#39;">ɛ</span><span title="/ð/: &#39;th&#39; in &#39;thy&#39;">ð</span><span title="/ər/: &#39;er&#39; in &#39;letter&#39;">ər</span><span title="&#39;b&#39; in &#39;buy&#39;">b</span><span title="/i/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;happy&#39;">i</span></span>/</a></span></span>) is a <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> within the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a>, in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England, close to the county’s border with <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>. The town stands on the <a href="/wiki/River_Wharfe" title="River Wharfe">River Wharfe</a>, and for centuries has been a crossing place and staging post on the <a href="/wiki/A1_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A1 road (Great Britain)">Great North Road</a> midway between London and Edinburgh.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> a part of the <a href="/wiki/Claro_Wapentake" title="Claro Wapentake">Claro Wapentake</a> (as part of the parish of <a href="/wiki/Spofforth,_North_Yorkshire" title="Spofforth, North Yorkshire">Spofforth</a>) within the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, Wetherby is mentioned in the <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i> of 1086 as <i>Wedrebi</i>, thought to derive from <i>wether-</i> or <i>ram-farm</i> or else meaning "settlement on the bend of a river".
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Bridge" title="Wetherby Bridge">Wetherby Bridge</a>, which spans the River Wharfe, is a <a href="/wiki/Scheduled_Ancient_Monument" class="mw-redirect" title="Scheduled Ancient Monument">Scheduled Ancient Monument</a> and a Grade II listed structure.<sup id="cite_ref-wyaas_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wyaas-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The course of the <a href="/wiki/A1_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A1 road (Great Britain)">Old Great North Road</a> passes through the town and, as result of its situation on the road, a large number of <a href="/wiki/Coaching_inn" title="Coaching inn">coaching inns</a> were established in Wetherby which are still used by travellers today.
</p><p>The town was listed in the 2018 <i>Sunday Times</i> report on Best Places to Live in northern England.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It sits in the <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_(ward)" title="Wetherby (ward)">Wetherby</a> ward of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elmet_and_Rothwell_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Elmet and Rothwell (UK Parliament constituency)">Elmet and Rothwell</a> <a href="/wiki/UK_Parliament_constituencies" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Parliament constituencies">parliamentary constituency</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Wetherby" title="History of Wetherby">History of Wetherby</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Victorian_Bishopgate.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Victorian_Bishopgate.jpg/220px-Victorian_Bishopgate.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="138" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Victorian_Bishopgate.jpg/330px-Victorian_Bishopgate.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Victorian_Bishopgate.jpg/440px-Victorian_Bishopgate.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="313" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Victorian_Bishopgate.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bishopgate, a former <a href="/wiki/Slum" title="Slum">slum</a> area in Wetherby</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wetherby_-_Bridge.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Wetherby_-_Bridge.jpg/220px-Wetherby_-_Bridge.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="159" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Wetherby_-_Bridge.jpg/330px-Wetherby_-_Bridge.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Wetherby_-_Bridge.jpg/440px-Wetherby_-_Bridge.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="463" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wetherby_-_Bridge.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Bridge" title="Wetherby Bridge">Wetherby Bridge</a></div></div></div>
<p>In the 12th and 13th centuries the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Templar" title="Knights Templar">Knights Templar</a> and later the <a href="/wiki/Knights_Hospitallers" class="mw-redirect" title="Knights Hospitallers">Knights Hospitallers</a> were granted land and properties in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>. The local preceptory founded in 1217 was at Ribston Park. In 1240 the Knights Templar were granted by Royal Charter of <a href="/wiki/Henry_III_of_England" title="Henry III of England">Henry III</a> the right to hold a market in Wetherby<sup id="cite_ref-weth-aps_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weth-aps-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (known then as Werreby). on Thursdays and a yearly fair was permitted lasting three days over the day of <a href="/wiki/Saint_James_the_Great" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint James the Great">St James the Apostle</a>.
</p><p>From 1318 to 1319 the North of England suffered many raids from the Scots. After the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bannockburn" title="Battle of Bannockburn">Battle of Bannockburn</a> Wetherby was burned and many people were taken and killed. According to the blue plaque at the entrance to the lane, <i>Scott Lane</i> could be named after the Scottish raiders in 1318 or the 18th-century drovers who used Wetherby as a watering place.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a> in 1644, before marching to <a href="/wiki/Tadcaster" title="Tadcaster">Tadcaster</a> and on to <a href="/wiki/Marston_Moor" class="mw-redirect" title="Marston Moor">Marston Moor</a>, the Parliamentarians spent two days in Wetherby joining forces with the Scots.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the heyday of the coaching era, Wetherby had up to forty inns and <a href="/wiki/Alehouse" class="mw-redirect" title="Alehouse">alehouses</a>. The first recorded mail coach arrived in Wetherby in 1786.<sup id="cite_ref-wyaas_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wyaas-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1824, <a href="/wiki/William_Cavendish,_6th_Duke_of_Devonshire" title="William Cavendish, 6th Duke of Devonshire">William Cavendish, 6th Duke of Devonshire</a> sold the town of Wetherby (except one house) to finance work at <a href="/wiki/Chatsworth_House" title="Chatsworth House">Chatsworth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-weth-aps_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weth-aps-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Wetherby provides the setting for the novel <i>Oldbury</i> (1869) by <a href="/wiki/Annie_Keary" title="Annie Keary">Annie Keary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 20th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First World War">First World War</a>, many Wetherby men served with either the 5th or 9th Battalion, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Regiment" title="West Yorkshire Regiment">West Yorkshire Regiment</a>, which had great losses in <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a>. A war memorial designed by E. F. Roslyn was dedicated on 22 April 1922.<sup id="cite_ref-wol_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wol-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1918, residents contributed to support the crew of the <a href="/wiki/Racecourse_class_minesweeper" class="mw-redirect" title="Racecourse class minesweeper">Racecourse class minesweeper</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Racecourse_class_minesweeper#Improved_Racecourse_type" class="mw-redirect" title="Racecourse class minesweeper">HMS Wetherby</a></i> despite hardship and shortages caused by the war.<sup id="cite_ref-wol_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wol-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the Second World War, nearby <a href="/wiki/RAF_Tockwith" class="mw-redirect" title="RAF Tockwith">RAF Tockwith</a> was renamed <a href="/wiki/RAF_Marston_Moor" title="RAF Marston Moor">RAF Marston Moor</a> to avoid confusion with <a href="/wiki/RAF_Topcliffe" title="RAF Topcliffe">RAF Topcliffe</a>. Part of the airfield is now a driver training centre and the old control tower is used as the offices. Parts of the runways can still be seen.<sup id="cite_ref-wol_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wol-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
<a href="/wiki/Clark_Gable" title="Clark Gable">Clark Gable</a> was stationed at Marston Moor, during the Second World War, as a member of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">USAAF</a> ground staff, with the rank of captain. He was transferred to <a href="/wiki/RAF_Polebrook" title="RAF Polebrook">RAF Polebrook</a> in Northamptonshire. <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> offered a reward to anyone who was able to catch the airman.<sup id="cite_ref-wol_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wol-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
<a href="/wiki/Group_Captain" class="mw-redirect" title="Group Captain">Group Captain</a> <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Cheshire" title="Leonard Cheshire">Leonard Cheshire</a> was stationed at Marston Moor for a short while before leaving to become commander of the <a href="/wiki/617_Squadron" class="mw-redirect" title="617 Squadron">617 Dam Buster squadron</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wol_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wol-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wetherby had the only <a href="/wiki/Stone_frigate" title="Stone frigate">stone frigate</a> north of London, built on <i>Hallfield Lane</i> in 1942 (it later became the local secondary school), named in turn; HMS <i>Cabot</i>, <i>Demetrius</i>, <i>Rodney</i> and <i>Ceres</i>. The base was closed in 1958 and transferred to <a href="/wiki/Chatham,_Medway" class="mw-redirect" title="Chatham, Medway">Chatham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wol_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wol-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Throughout the 1960s the town council deliberated over how best to enlarge the town centre to cope with the needs of a growing population and to provide the town with a purpose built supermarket. Plans were put forward to enlarge the town over the <a href="/wiki/Ings" title="Ings">ings</a>, or to develop the town centre into a pedestrian precinct. In the end it was decided to build a purpose built <a href="/wiki/Shopping_precinct" class="mw-redirect" title="Shopping precinct">shopping precinct</a>, which was built in the 1970s and underwent a significant redevelopment throughout 2003.  By 2006 the remaining open parts of the Horsefair Centre were enclosed under a glass canopy roof.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="see Leeds City Library (June 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wetherbycoatofarms.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/0f/Wetherbycoatofarms.jpg/220px-Wetherbycoatofarms.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="322" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/0/0f/Wetherbycoatofarms.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="256" data-file-height="375" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wetherbycoatofarms.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> of the former <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Rural_District" title="Wetherby Rural District">Wetherby Rural District Council</a>, the first such arms to be granted by the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Arms" title="College of Arms">College of Arms</a> to a <a href="/wiki/Rural_district" title="Rural district">rural district</a> council, on 7 January 1938. The motto translates as "One government within another."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Access_to_King_Georges_field.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Access_to_King_Georges_field.jpg/220px-Access_to_King_Georges_field.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Access_to_King_Georges_field.jpg/330px-Access_to_King_Georges_field.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Access_to_King_Georges_field.jpg/440px-Access_to_King_Georges_field.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="1600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Access_to_King_Georges_field.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>King George's Field, <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Ings" title="Wetherby Ings">Wetherby Ings</a></div></div></div>
<p>Since 2010 Wetherby has been in the <a href="/wiki/Elmet_and_Rothwell_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Elmet and Rothwell (UK Parliament constituency)">Elmet and Rothwell</a> constituency, a <a href="/wiki/Marginal_seat" title="Marginal seat">marginal seat</a>.  The town's MP is <a href="/wiki/Alec_Shelbrooke" title="Alec Shelbrooke">Alec Shelbrooke</a> (<a href="/wiki/Conservative" class="mw-redirect" title="Conservative">Conservative</a>) who has his constituency offices in the town.  <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_(ward)" title="Wetherby (ward)">Wetherby</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Electoral_ward" class="mw-redirect" title="Electoral ward">electoral ward</a> of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a> and has a town council responsible for amenities such as parks.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions_and_suburbs">Divisions and suburbs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Divisions and suburbs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Micklethwaite,_Wetherby" title="Micklethwaite, Wetherby">Micklethwaite</a></b> was a village in its own right but its identity as a separate place has disappeared since the Micklethwaite Farm's buildings were demolished in the 2000s and replaced by 150 dwellings known as 'Micklethwaite'.  It is situated south of the <a href="/wiki/River_Wharfe" title="River Wharfe">River Wharfe</a> and contains the <a href="/wiki/Police_station" title="Police station">police station</a>, magistrates court, the <a href="/wiki/Ramada_Jarvis" title="Ramada Jarvis">Ramada Jarvis</a> Hotel and the town's Leisure Centre and Swimming Baths.  Wetherby Athletic and Wetherby Bulldogs RLFC play on the Wetherby <a href="/wiki/Ings" title="Ings">ings</a>, while Wetherby RUFC and Wetherby CC play at Grange Park.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Ainsty,_Wetherby" title="Ainsty, Wetherby">Ainsty</a></b> is in the north of Wetherby, off the B1224 Deighton Road. Its earliest buildings date from the 1940s made up of <a href="/wiki/Council_housing" class="mw-redirect" title="Council housing">council housing</a> and private housing.  Much of the area was built by developer <a href="/wiki/Norman_Ashton" class="mw-redirect" title="Norman Ashton">Norman Ashton</a> in the 1960s.  Its amenities have declined leaving only three shops on the estate, a <a href="/wiki/Co-op" class="mw-redirect" title="Co-op">Co-op</a>, a dog grooming shop and a decorating shop.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Hallfield" title="Hallfield">Hallfield</a></b> in the southeast is a large council estate and has some houses built by the prison service and some sheltered housing. The area is home to <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_High_School" title="Wetherby High School">Wetherby High School</a>, St James' Primary School, the cemetery, the Church on the Corner and Mason House Community Centre. A new medical centre has been built on the edge of the estate on the site of the demolished Hallfield Mansion.</li>
<li><b>Deighton Bar</b> is situated in the northeast bordering Ainsty and Sandbeck and the village of <a href="/wiki/Kirk_Deighton" title="Kirk Deighton">Kirk Deighton</a> in North Yorkshire, as is one street in Deighton Bar, Autumn Avenue. The oldest houses are in a row of terrace houses on Deighton Road. The area is home to Deighton Gates primary school between Ainsty and Deighton Bar. Most housing in Deighton Bar was built in the late 1970s by <a href="/wiki/Barratt_Developments" title="Barratt Developments">Barratts</a>.</li>
<li><b>Barleyfields</b> – Barleyfields is a residential area of housing in central Wetherby. Barleyfields' oldest houses are large Victorian terraces on Sandringham Terrace and the former quarry workers' cottages behind Prospect Villas.  The area situated in the middle of a large triangular dismantled railway junction.  The area is home to St Joseph's Primary School, Crossley Street Primary School and Barleyfields Community Centre.</li>
<li><b>Sandbeck</b> is home to the Sandbeck Industrial Estate, some 1960s Norman Ashton Houses and some 1970's council houses. The industrial estate is home to West Yorkshire Steel, <a href="/wiki/Goldenfry_Foods" class="mw-redirect" title="Goldenfry Foods">Goldenfry Foods</a>, Mondipack, Swift Research, Dunwood Polymer Services, John Lanham Watts Carpets, Value Self Store and Arvilles Textiles, Tracsis Traffic Data Ltd and the ICC group's Head Office. The former Wetherby &amp; District Social Club is based on the industrial estate, now a pub and events space called The Wetherby.</li>
<li><b>Linton Park View</b> an affluent area of private houses, mostly built in the 1970s between Spofforth Hill and Linton Lane in the northwest of Wetherby.</li>
<li><b>Spofforth Hill</b> named after the road that passes through is an affluent area off the A661.  It contains many large detached houses from Victorian times onwards.  In the 1980s and 1990s the area was expanded after Shepherd Homes built a housing estate on former agricultural land, the Glebe Field Estate.</li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Weather_and_climate">Weather and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Weather and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Wetherby has a cool, fairly moist climate with changeable weather year-round. Liquid equivalent precipitation totals about 630mm per year and is fairly well distributed through the seasons.
</p><p>Cloudy weather tends to predominate, but settled, sunny spells occur at times, as well.
</p><p>Winter temperatures average just above freezing for lows, with highs about 5-9 C.
</p><p>Frost and snow are not uncommon, but are rarely severe or prolonged. Temperatures very rarely drop as low as -10 C.
</p><p>Summers are mild, with lows mostly 10-15 and highs 15-25, with a few hot days approaching 30.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/2001_UK_Census" class="mw-redirect" title="2001 UK Census">2001 UK Census</a>, the Wetherby ward has a population of 26,473<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and the immediate town has a population of 11,155.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, since this was taken, the immediate town area has grown considerably. 150 new dwellings were built in one development in <a href="/wiki/Micklethwaite,_Wetherby" title="Micklethwaite, Wetherby">Micklethwaite</a>, then a further 20 were added, flats have also appeared at the former <a href="/wiki/Motorworld" class="mw-redirect" title="Motorworld">Motorworld</a>, La Locanda Restaurant, Deighton Road car garage, Fields Works and the cattle market.  In the late 2010s, two new large scale greenfield housing developments started to be developed; one in the Sandbeck area and another in the Spofforth Hill area.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Goldenfry_Factory.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Goldenfry_Factory.jpg/220px-Goldenfry_Factory.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Goldenfry_Factory.jpg/330px-Goldenfry_Factory.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Goldenfry_Factory.jpg/440px-Goldenfry_Factory.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Goldenfry_Factory.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Goldenfry" title="Goldenfry">Goldenfry</a> factory</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale_Brewery" title="Wharfedale Brewery">Wharfedale Brewery</a> became Oxley's mineral water factory during the inter-war years.<sup id="cite_ref-weth-aps_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weth-aps-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was demolished in the 1950s and redeveloped as the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Road_Car_Company" title="West Yorkshire Road Car Company">West Yorkshire Road Car Company</a> bus depot and bus station, and has been further redeveloped to include shops, offices, and a restaurant and the bus station. The site of the watermill by the weir, is now occupied by riverside flats.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Wetherby has a manufacturing presence in the town and on the <a href="/wiki/Thorp_Arch_Trading_Estate" title="Thorp Arch Trading Estate">Thorp Arch Trading Estate</a>. Many residents work in Leeds or on the Sandbeck industrial estate, major retailers in the town centre or at Thorp Arch. Large employers include the <a href="/wiki/British_Library" title="British Library">British Library</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morrisons" title="Morrisons">Morrisons</a>, Goldenfry Foods and Moores Furniture.
</p><p>2017 saw the return of brewing to Wetherby when the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby_Brew_Co&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wetherby Brew Co (page does not exist)">Wetherby Brew Co</a> established a new microbrewery and taproom on the York Road Industrial Estate <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Present_day">Present day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Present day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Goldenfry" title="Goldenfry">Goldenfry</a>, which started as a <a href="/wiki/Fish_and_chip_shop" title="Fish and chip shop">fish and chip shop</a> but which now makes other products including own-brand gravy for every UK supermarket, is situated on Sandbeck Way.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Inspirepac has a factory on Sandbeck Lane.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The ICC Group is a multi national UK IT reseller and service provider which started in 1998. ICC has its HQ at Sandbeck Lane. Supporting over 10% of the FTSE100, it specialises in HP/IBM and Dell products and associated IT services.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Premier_Farnell" title="Premier Farnell">Farnell</a> opened its first factory in 1956 on the York Road Industrial Estate, leasing some former W.D. <a href="/wiki/Nissen_hut" title="Nissen hut">nissen huts</a>. By 1963 it required new premises and moved to the Sandbeck Industrial Estate.<sup id="cite_ref-apshistory_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apshistory-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 1997 <a href="/w/index.php?title=GSM_Valtech_Industries_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="GSM Valtech Industries Limited (page does not exist)">GSM Valtech Industries Limited</a> GSM purchased the metalwork fabrication site of Farnell Electronics. GSM Valtech’s operations were transferred to the Wetherby site, increasing the manufacturing area to 28,000 sq ft and gaining staff with 40 years’ experience in manufacturing electronic enclosures. Substantial investment followed.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The company specialises in the manufacture and wholesale distribution of electrical, <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">electronic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Instrumentation" title="Instrumentation">measurement, control and instrumentation</a> equipment. In the 1990s the company moved to offices in <a href="/wiki/Armley" title="Armley">Armley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-apshistory_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-apshistory-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Horsefair_Centre,_Wetherby.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Horsefair_Centre%2C_Wetherby.jpg/220px-Horsefair_Centre%2C_Wetherby.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Horsefair_Centre%2C_Wetherby.jpg/330px-Horsefair_Centre%2C_Wetherby.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Horsefair_Centre%2C_Wetherby.jpg/440px-Horsefair_Centre%2C_Wetherby.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Horsefair_Centre,_Wetherby.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>North Street, from the Garden of Rest. Showing the main entrance to the Horsefair Centre.</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Forensic_Science_Service" title="Forensic Science Service">Forensic Science Service</a> had a laboratory in Wetherby on Sandbeck Way. This closed in 2012 and has been demolished, with a new housing estate now on the site. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/HM_Prison_Wetherby" title="HM Prison Wetherby">HM Prison Wetherby</a> is located on York Road.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>The Horsefair Centre</i> has a <a href="/wiki/Morrisons" title="Morrisons">Morrisons</a> supermarket, <a href="/wiki/Boots_the_Chemist" class="mw-redirect" title="Boots the Chemist">Boots the Chemist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Superdrug" title="Superdrug">Superdrug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Specsavers" title="Specsavers">Specsavers</a> and other shops. It was built in the 1970s It was redeveloped between 2002 and 2005. 
<a href="/wiki/Kwiksave" class="mw-redirect" title="Kwiksave">Kwiksave</a> closed and reopened as a <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury&#39;s">Sainsbury's</a>, the fourth supermarket, competing with the <a href="/wiki/Co-op" class="mw-redirect" title="Co-op">Co-op</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marks_and_Spencers" class="mw-redirect" title="Marks and Spencers">Marks and Spencers</a> and the largest, Morrisons.  An <a href="/wiki/Aldi" title="Aldi">Aldi</a> has opened in 2017 on Sandbeck Lane.
</p><p>Since the closure and demolition of the cattle market in 2001, the site has been redeveloped as a Marks &amp; Spencer Food store, with apartments above.
</p><p>In August 2008 the <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_services" title="Wetherby services">Wetherby service station</a>, operated by <a href="/wiki/Moto_Hospitality" title="Moto Hospitality">MOTO</a>, opened on the B1224, <a href="/wiki/A1_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A1 road (Great Britain)">A1(M)</a> intersection.
</p><p>On 1 October 2008 the healthcare centre on Hallfield Lane was opened by <a href="/wiki/Colin_Burgon" title="Colin Burgon">Colin Burgon</a> Labour Member of Parliament for <a href="/wiki/Elmet_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Elmet (UK Parliament constituency)">Elmet</a>. It was completed in May 2008 and services have been provided from the building since June 2008.  The centre has a range of services these include <a href="/wiki/Podiatry" title="Podiatry">podiatry</a> <a href="/wiki/Physiotherapy" class="mw-redirect" title="Physiotherapy">physiotherapy</a> and a baby clinic.
</p><p>Wetherby is the home to the <a href="/wiki/Martin_House_Hospice" title="Martin House Hospice">Martin House Hospice</a>, which provides care and support to families of children with a short life expectancy.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For many years from 1959, the town's bypass started at a roundabout near a <a href="/wiki/Forte_Group" title="Forte Group">Forte Group</a> Posthouse hotel until July 1988 when the A1 was diverted at a cost of £11.5 million. On 18 December 2004, the northern section of the bypass was diverted to a new section of the A1(M), bypassing Kirk Deighton, after construction work had begun in August 2003. The upgrade of the section between Bramham and Wetherby started in July 2007 and was scheduled to be completed in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-gnn_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gnn-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  The upgrading of the A1 included the construction of <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="Wetherby Services">Wetherby Services</a> at the Wetherby North Junction.  The upgrading of the <a href="/wiki/A1_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A1 road (Great Britain)">A1(M)</a> in Wetherby was the final development after 50 years of gradual improvement to motorway standard. A new road links all routes in and out of the town with the A1(M).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Wetherby_bus_station" title="Wetherby bus station">Wetherby bus station</a> in the Market Place was redeveloped in 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-wypta_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wypta-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Since the partial <a href="/wiki/Pedestrianisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Pedestrianisation">pedestrianisation</a> of the Market Place in 2007 capacity at the bus station has been reduced. Services are operated by <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express</a> coach services, <a href="/wiki/First_West_Yorkshire" title="First West Yorkshire">First West Yorkshire</a> run buses to Leeds via <a href="/wiki/Roundhay" title="Roundhay">Roundhay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_Bus_Company" title="Harrogate Bus Company">Harrogate Bus Company</a> runs to Leeds via Boston Spa and <a href="/wiki/Seacroft" title="Seacroft">Seacroft</a> and to <a href="/wiki/Harrogate" title="Harrogate">Harrogate</a> via <a href="/wiki/Spofforth,_North_Yorkshire" title="Spofforth, North Yorkshire">Spofforth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connexionsbuses" title="Connexionsbuses">Connexionsbuses</a> run to Harrogate and <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a> via <a href="/wiki/Tockwith" title="Tockwith">Tockwith</a>, Eddie Brown run to <a href="/wiki/Bickerton,_North_Yorkshire" title="Bickerton, North Yorkshire">Bickerton</a>, Arriva buses run to <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a> via <a href="/wiki/Garforth" title="Garforth">Garforth</a> and Sandla Transport run to <a href="/wiki/Knaresborough" title="Knaresborough">Knaresborough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tadcaster" title="Tadcaster">Tadcaster</a> and <a href="/wiki/Otley" title="Otley">Otley</a>. The daily footfall is estimated at 1634.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Local passenger services between Leeds, Wetherby, and Harrogate, and between Wetherby and <a href="/wiki/Church_Fenton" title="Church Fenton">Church Fenton</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Cross_Gates%E2%80%93Wetherby_line" title="Cross Gates–Wetherby line">Cross Gates–Wetherby line</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Harrogate%E2%80%93Church_Fenton_line" title="Harrogate–Church Fenton line">Harrogate–Church Fenton line</a> were withdrawn on 6 January 1964, involving closure of <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_(Linton_Road)_railway_station" title="Wetherby (Linton Road) railway station">Wetherby railway station</a>, one of the very first stations to be closed as part of the <a href="/wiki/Beeching_cuts" title="Beeching cuts">Beeching Axe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-davidhey_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-davidhey-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The closest mainline station is <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds railway station</a>. The nearest local stations are <a href="/wiki/Pannal_railway_station" title="Pannal railway station">Pannal railway station</a>  to the west and <a href="/wiki/Cattal_railway_station" title="Cattal railway station">Cattal railway station</a> to the north.
</p><p>The closest airport is <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds Bradford International Airport">Leeds Bradford International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yeadon,_West_Yorkshire" title="Yeadon, West Yorkshire">Yeadon</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wetherbyhighschool.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Wetherbyhighschool.jpg/220px-Wetherbyhighschool.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Wetherbyhighschool.jpg/330px-Wetherbyhighschool.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Wetherbyhighschool.jpg/440px-Wetherbyhighschool.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wetherbyhighschool.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Wetherby_High_School" title="Wetherby High School">Wetherby High School</a>.</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_High_School" title="Wetherby High School">Wetherby High School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_College" title="Leeds City College">Leeds City College</a></div>
<p>There are four primary schools situated in Wetherby and one secondary school, <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_High_School" title="Wetherby High School">Wetherby High School</a> (formerly Wetherby Secondary Modern School). There is Crossley Street Primary School, Deighton Gates Primary School, St Josephs Primary School and St James C of E School.  There is a further secondary school serving Wetherby situated in <a href="/wiki/Boston_Spa" title="Boston Spa">Boston Spa</a>. The local college in Wetherby is <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_College" title="Leeds City College">Leeds City College</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wetherby_Racecourse_(31st_March_2013)_008.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Wetherby_Racecourse_%2831st_March_2013%29_008.jpg/220px-Wetherby_Racecourse_%2831st_March_2013%29_008.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Wetherby_Racecourse_%2831st_March_2013%29_008.jpg/330px-Wetherby_Racecourse_%2831st_March_2013%29_008.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Wetherby_Racecourse_%2831st_March_2013%29_008.jpg/440px-Wetherby_Racecourse_%2831st_March_2013%29_008.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3872" data-file-height="2176" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wetherby_Racecourse_(31st_March_2013)_008.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Racecourse" title="Wetherby Racecourse">Wetherby Racecourse</a></div></div></div>
<p>The town is home to several sporting clubs: Wetherby Athletic AFC, Wetherby Bulldogs RLFC and Wetherby RUFC, all playing at a higher amateur level. Wetherby Athletic play in the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_League" class="mw-redirect" title="West Yorkshire League">West Yorkshire League</a>. The club was formed in 1949, when previous club Wetherby United folded due to lack of players.<sup id="cite_ref-Wetherby_Athletic_FC_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wetherby_Athletic_FC-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The club groundshare with Wetherby Bulldogs RLFC at the newly refurbished ground on <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Ings" title="Wetherby Ings">Ings</a>. Three-time <a href="/wiki/List_of_English_football_champions" title="List of English football champions">English champions</a> <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a>'s training ground is located just outside Wetherby.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wetherby Bulldogs <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> team play at the Ings with Wetherby Athletic. The club plays in the <a href="/wiki/Pennine_League" title="Pennine League">Pennine League</a> Division Four. The club was formed in 1983 playing in the York and District Sunday League. In the 1997/98 season, Wetherby won the league without losing a game, and won the White Rose Cup, playing the final at <a href="/wiki/Featherstone_Rovers" title="Featherstone Rovers">Featherstone Rovers</a>' <a href="/wiki/Post_Office_Road" title="Post Office Road">Post Office Road</a> ground. By 1999 the club was in Yorkshire League Division One.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town's <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> club plays at <a href="/wiki/Grange_Park,_Wetherby" title="Grange Park, Wetherby">Grange Park</a> sharing with Kirk Deighton Rangers Junior Football Club<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and also with the town's cricket and bowls clubs where it has a clubhouse and floodlit pitches.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wetherby Cricket Club plays at Grange Park (adjacent to the South Wetherby <a href="/wiki/A1_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A1 road (Great Britain)">A1(M)</a>/<a href="/wiki/A661_road" title="A661 road">A661</a> intersection). It plays in the Wetherby League and the Whixley Evening League fielding two senior and junior sides ranging from U-9s to U-17s.
</p><p>Wetherby Bowling Club was established in 1986 at Grange Park Sports Centre between the cricket club and the rugby union club. It has crown and flat greens and takes part in a floodlit mini league. The club has six crown green teams (playing in the Harrogate and Tadcaster Leagues) and three flat green teams. 
Formed in October 2004, Wetherby Runners Athletic Club is based at Wetherby Sports Association with a membership of over 160. A junior section competes in West Yorks Track &amp; Field &amp; cross country Leagues. It competes throughout the region in Harrogate Road League,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Yorkshire Dales Race Series,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> West Yorks Cross Country League and takes part in cross country, fell, road races and marathons.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The club organises the Wetherby 10k Run on the second Sunday in September at Wetherby Racecourse.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wetherby Golf Club has an 18-hole golf course, constructed in two loops of 9-holes along Wetherby Ings where, 100 years ago, steeplechase racing was the major sporting activity. The course is almost 6,700 yards with five variable tee positions.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wetherby Castlegarth Tennis Club has had a presence in the town since 1904.
</p><p>The route of <a href="/wiki/The_White_Rose_Way" title="The White Rose Way">The White Rose Way</a>, a long distance walk from Leeds to <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_North_Yorkshire" title="Scarborough, North Yorkshire">Scarborough</a> passes through the town.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Racecourse" title="Wetherby Racecourse">Wetherby Racecourse</a> was originally located at the Ings before moving to York Road. The course is a left hand oval with easy bends.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The racecourse has three stands, one constructed in the 1930s with football style terracing, a two-tier seated stand constructed in the 1970s and the new Millennium Stand which opened in 1999 providing executive facilities.
</p><p>Wetherby Racecourse is the starting point for the Great Yorkshire Bike Ride an annual event held in June. The 70 mile ride ends in <a href="/wiki/Filey" title="Filey">Filey</a>. The event has raised nearly £2 million for charity since its inception in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Crime_and_law_enforcement">Crime and law enforcement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Crime and law enforcement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The nearest police station of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Police" title="West Yorkshire Police">West Yorkshire Police</a> is in Boston Road.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Church6.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Church6.jpg/220px-Church6.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Church6.jpg/330px-Church6.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Church6.jpg/440px-Church6.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="1600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Church6.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/St_James%27_Church,_Wetherby" class="mw-redirect" title="St James&#39; Church, Wetherby">St James' Church in Wetherby</a>, Wetherby's largest church.</div></div></div>
<p>There are five churches in Wetherby.<sup id="cite_ref-churchestog_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-churchestog-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
The parish church is dedicated to <a href="/wiki/St_James%27_Parish_Church,_Wetherby" title="St James&#39; Parish Church, Wetherby">St James</a><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> and its daughter church <i>The Church on the Corner</i> meets in the old Cemetery Chapel on Hallfield Lane.
</p><p>There is a Methodist church on Bank Street, a Baptist church and the Community Church of the Salvation Army.
St Joseph's Roman Catholic Church's building was opened in 1986 and won the Leeds award for Architecture in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-stjoseph_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stjoseph-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>  Two smaller Catholic churches in <a href="/wiki/Bardsey,_West_Yorkshire" title="Bardsey, West Yorkshire">Bardsey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sicklinghall" title="Sicklinghall">Sicklinghall</a> operate as satellite churches to the Wetherby one and do not have their own ministers.
</p><p>The Baptist Church was originally Anglican and was known as Barleyfields Church. Early in 2009 it became part of the Baptist Union of Great Britain. It originally met in the Barleyfields Centre, but moved to Deighton Gates School in September 2009.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_media">Culture and media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Culture and media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1989 the 'Wetherby in Bloom' committee was set up and has charitable status. The town won the Entente Florale gold award in 1999 and an International Communities in Bloom award in 2005. It achieved success in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Horticultural_Society" title="Royal Horticultural Society">RHS</a> <a href="/wiki/Britain_in_Bloom" title="Britain in Bloom">Britain in Bloom</a> competition in 1998, 2002 and 2010, along with numerous regional gold awards over the last 15 years.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wetherby_Cinema.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Wetherby_Cinema.jpg/220px-Wetherby_Cinema.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Wetherby_Cinema.jpg/330px-Wetherby_Cinema.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Wetherby_Cinema.jpg/440px-Wetherby_Cinema.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wetherby_Cinema.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Cinema" class="mw-redirect" title="Wetherby Cinema">Wetherby Cinema</a></div></div></div>
<p>The annual Wetherby Arts Festival<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> is sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby_Town_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Wetherby Town Council (page does not exist)">Wetherby Town Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> It promotes the arts by providing a platform for local groups to perform and to bring in other performers and art forms.
</p><p>The local newspaper is the Wetherby News<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> and the Lifestyle magazine is the Excelle magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The regional radio station is <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Leeds" title="BBC Radio Leeds">BBC Radio Leeds</a>. <a href="/wiki/Tempo_FM" title="Tempo FM">Tempo FM</a> is Wetherby's very own 100% volunteer run community radio station, with studios located in the old council offices in Westgate.  <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Film_Theatre" class="mw-redirect" title="Wetherby Film Theatre">Wetherby Film Theatre</a> is an independently owned, traditional single screen cinema on Caxton Street, which, although opened in 1915 as a cinema, had been used for some time as a bingo and social club, before being re-opened as a cinema in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Housing">Housing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Housing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:York_Place_Flats,_Wetherby_(12th_October_2013).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/York_Place_Flats%2C_Wetherby_%2812th_October_2013%29.JPG/220px-York_Place_Flats%2C_Wetherby_%2812th_October_2013%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/York_Place_Flats%2C_Wetherby_%2812th_October_2013%29.JPG/330px-York_Place_Flats%2C_Wetherby_%2812th_October_2013%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/York_Place_Flats%2C_Wetherby_%2812th_October_2013%29.JPG/440px-York_Place_Flats%2C_Wetherby_%2812th_October_2013%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3872" data-file-height="2176" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:York_Place_Flats,_Wetherby_(12th_October_2013).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Medium-rise blocks of flats in York Place</div></div></div>
<p>In 1914, 100 dwellings in Wetherby were considered unfit for habitation. This and previous reports under the <a href="/wiki/Housing_and_Town_Planning_Act_1909" class="mw-redirect" title="Housing and Town Planning Act 1909">Housing and Town Planning Act 1909</a> led to the building of many 'villas'. There are many surviving examples of these, such as Park Villas, York Place, Grosvenor Terrace and Sandringham Terrace. Landlords found these hard to let due to exorbitant rents and many remained empty for years. This also led to the demolition of the town's Bishopgate Area. In 1910 the parish council started a programme to install street lighting in the hope of bettering the standard of living and reducing crime.
It was not until the post war years the large <a href="/wiki/Housing_estates" class="mw-redirect" title="Housing estates">housing estates</a> appeared throughout Wetherby. From the 1940s until the 1980s, many large estates were built from scratch. Both the local corporation and the private sector built many houses to satisfy the huge demand for homes in Wetherby. Developer <a href="/wiki/Norman_Ashton" class="mw-redirect" title="Norman Ashton">Norman Ashton</a>'s company <a href="/wiki/Ashtons" title="Ashtons">Ashtons</a> were responsible for much of the housing in Wetherby, particularly around the Ainsty Estate, Hall Orchards and Templar Gardens area.  Most housing in the town is from these years. There is a wide variety of housing types in Wetherby, including waterside penthouses, <a href="/wiki/Council_housing" class="mw-redirect" title="Council housing">council flats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maisonette_(apartment)" class="mw-redirect" title="Maisonette (apartment)">maisonettes</a>, large <a href="/wiki/Detached_houses" class="mw-redirect" title="Detached houses">detached houses</a>, small <a href="/wiki/Terraced_housing" class="mw-redirect" title="Terraced housing">terraces</a> and probably the most common, the three bedroomed twentieth century <a href="/wiki/Semi-detached" title="Semi-detached">semi-detached</a> home.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_houses">Public houses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wetherby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Public houses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Blackbullwetherby2003.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Blackbullwetherby2003.jpg/220px-Blackbullwetherby2003.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Blackbullwetherby2003.jpg/330px-Blackbullwetherby2003.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Blackbullwetherby2003.jpg/440px-Blackbullwetherby2003.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Blackbullwetherby2003.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Black Bull in 2003, before refurbishment</div></div></div>
<p>In its hey-day Wetherby had seventeen <a href="/wiki/Pubs" class="mw-redirect" title="Pubs">pubs</a> in its town centre. Only eleven now remain, of which ten are still open. The town's oldest surviving pub the 'Brunswick Hotel' closed in 2003 and reopened as Harris' Bar, and in 2012 reopened again as The Brunswick after refurbishment by <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_Inns" class="mw-redirect" title="Enterprise Inns">Enterprise Inns</a>.  The Three Legs public house closed in 2007 and became "bar Thr3" (Wetherby's first non smoking pub).<sup id="cite_ref-thr3_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thr3-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, The Angel public house served German and Italian <a href="/wiki/Prisoners_of_war" class="mw-redirect" title="Prisoners of war">prisoners of war</a> from the nearby camps and, being the only pub in the town to do so, attracted some controversy as a result.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Angel was sold in 2010 and re-opened as Sant Angelo Italian Restaurant and Ice Cream Parlour. The pub was recreated on the first floor and has become one of the town's busiest weekend drinking venues.
</p><p>Wetherby Sports Association (WSA) came about in 1999 as the brain child of Wetherby Bulldogs and Wetherby Athletic who identified a need for a “home” they could call their own after years of playing out of concrete bunkers by the river and holding meetings in local pubs – the outcome of which was the Lottery/FA funded building on Lodge Lane which is used extensively by the local community today.
</p><p>WSA is a registered charity which provides a community hub for sports clubs and societies in Wetherby that would otherwise struggle to fund a place of their own. WSA has seen its ranks grow over the years and in addition to Wetherby Bulldogs and Wetherby Athletic is now home to Wetherby Runners, Wetherby Wheelers, Wetherby Petanque, the local branch of the Military Vehicles society, Wetherby &amp; District Angling Club, Pilates, Zumba, Wetherby Park Run, Gym Gems and many more.
</p><p>There is also a similar club at Grange Park, serving members of the town's <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bowls" title="Bowls">bowls</a> clubs as well as members of its committee and association.
</p><p>The town's main <a href="/wiki/Working_men%27s_club" title="Working men&#39;s club">Working Men's Club</a>, Wetherby and District Social Club is based on the Sandbeck Industrial Estate. This burnt down in a fire in 2002. The fire was initially treated as suspicious by <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Police" title="West Yorkshire Police">West Yorkshire Police</a>, however investigations were taken no further. The club was rebuilt in its original location. As was the case in <a href="/wiki/Belle_Isle,_Leeds" title="Belle Isle, Leeds">Belle Isle</a> the rebuilt club was significantly larger than its predecessor and offered its members far superior facilities. In 2018, this was taken over by new owners and opened as a pub and event space called ‘The Wetherby’.
</p>
