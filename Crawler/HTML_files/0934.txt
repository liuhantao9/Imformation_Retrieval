<p><b>MusicBrainz</b> is a project that aims to create a collaborative music database that is similar to the <a href="/wiki/Freedb" title="Freedb">freedb</a> project. MusicBrainz was founded in response to the restrictions placed on the <a href="/wiki/CDDB" title="CDDB">Compact Disc Database</a> (CDDB), a database for software applications to look up audio CD (<a href="/wiki/Compact_disc" title="Compact disc">compact disc</a>) information on the Internet. MusicBrainz has expanded its goals to reach beyond a compact disc <a href="/wiki/Metadata_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Metadata (computing)">metadata</a> (this is information about the performers, artists, songwriters, etc.) storehouse to become a structured online database for music.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>MusicBrainz captures information about artists, their recorded works, and the relationships between them. Recorded works entries capture at a minimum the album title, track titles, and the length of each track. These entries are maintained by volunteer editors who follow community written <a href="http://musicbrainz.org/doc/Style_Guideline" class="extiw" title="musicbrainz:Style Guideline">style guidelines</a>. Recorded works can also store information about the release date and country, the CD ID, <a href="/wiki/Cover_art" title="Cover art">cover art</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_fingerprint" title="Acoustic fingerprint">acoustic fingerprint</a>, free-form annotation text and other metadata. As of 21&#160;September&#160;2018<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=MusicBrainz&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, MusicBrainz contained information about roughly 1.4 million artists, 2 million releases, and 19 million recordings.<sup id="cite_ref-statistics_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statistics-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> End-users can use software that communicates with MusicBrainz to add <a href="/wiki/ID3_tag" class="mw-redirect" title="ID3 tag">metadata tags</a> to their digital media files, such as <a href="/wiki/FLAC" title="FLAC">FLAC</a>, <a href="/wiki/MP3" title="MP3">MP3</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ogg_Vorbis" class="mw-redirect" title="Ogg Vorbis">Ogg Vorbis</a> or <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Audio_Coding" title="Advanced Audio Coding">AAC</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_Art_Archive">Cover Art Archive</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MusicBrainz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cover Art Archive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MusicBrainz allows contributors to upload cover art images of releases to the database; these images are hosted by <b>Cover Art Archive</b> (CAA), a joint project between <a href="/wiki/Internet_Archive" title="Internet Archive">Internet Archive</a> and MusicBrainz started in 2012. Internet Archive provides the bandwidth, storage and legal protection for hosting the images, while MusicBrainz stores metadata and provides public access through the web and via an <a href="/wiki/Application_programming_interface" title="Application programming interface">API</a> for third parties to use. As with other contributions, the MusicBrainz community is in charge of maintaining and reviewing the data.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Cover art is also provided for items on sale at <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon.com</a> and some other online resources, but CAA is now preferred because it gives the community more control and flexibility for managing the images.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fingerprinting">Fingerprinting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MusicBrainz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fingerprinting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Besides collecting metadata about music, MusicBrainz also allows looking up recordings by their <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_fingerprint" title="Acoustic fingerprint">acoustic fingerprint</a>. A separate application, such as <a href="/wiki/MusicBrainz_Picard" title="MusicBrainz Picard">MusicBrainz Picard</a>, must be used for this.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Proprietary_services">Proprietary services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MusicBrainz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Proprietary services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2000, MusicBrainz started using <a href="/w/index.php?title=Relatable&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Relatable (page does not exist)">Relatable</a>'s patented TRM (a <a href="/wiki/Recursive_acronym" title="Recursive acronym">recursive acronym</a> for TRM Recognizes Music) for acoustic fingerprint matching. This feature attracted many users and allowed the database to grow quickly. However, by 2005 TRM was showing scalability issues as the number of tracks in the database had reached into the millions. This issue was resolved in May 2006 when MusicBrainz partnered with MusicIP (now <a href="/wiki/AmpliFIND" title="AmpliFIND">AmpliFIND</a>), replacing TRM with MusicDNS.<sup id="cite_ref-musicdns_announcement_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-musicdns_announcement-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> TRMs were phased out and replaced by MusicDNS in November 2008.
</p><p>In October 2009 MusicIP was acquired by <a href="/wiki/AmpliFIND" title="AmpliFIND">AmpliFIND</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Some time after the acquisition, the MusicDNS service began having intermittent problems.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AcoustID_and_Chromaprint">AcoustID and Chromaprint</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MusicBrainz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: AcoustID and Chromaprint">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the future of the free identification service was uncertain, a replacement for it was sought. The Chromaprint acoustic fingerprinting algorithm, the basis for <a href="/wiki/AcoustID" title="AcoustID">AcoustID</a> identification service, was started in February 2010 by a long-time MusicBrainz contributor Lukáš Lalinský.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> While AcoustID and Chromaprint are not officially MusicBrainz projects, they are closely tied with each other and both are open source. Chromaprint works by analyzing the first two minutes of a track, detecting the strength in each of 12 <a href="/wiki/Pitch_class" title="Pitch class">pitch classes</a>, storing these 8 times per second. Additional post-processing is then applied to compress this fingerprint while retaining patterns.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The AcoustID search server then searches from the database of fingerprints by similarity and returns the AcoustID identifier along with MusicBrainz recording identifiers if known.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Licensing">Licensing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MusicBrainz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Licensing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2003,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> MusicBrainz's core data (artists, recordings, releases, and so on) are in the <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">public domain</a>, and additional content, including moderation data (essentially every <a href="/wiki/Threshold_of_originality" title="Threshold of originality">original content</a> contributed by users and its elaborations), is placed under the <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons" title="Creative Commons">Creative Commons</a> <a href="/wiki/CC-BY-NC-SA" class="mw-redirect" title="CC-BY-NC-SA">CC-BY-NC-SA</a>-2.0 license.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  The <a href="/wiki/Relational_database_management_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Relational database management system">relational database management system</a> is <a href="/wiki/PostgreSQL" title="PostgreSQL">PostgreSQL</a>. The server software is covered by the <a href="/wiki/GNU_General_Public_License" title="GNU General Public License">GNU General Public License</a>.
The MusicBrainz client <a href="/wiki/Library_(computing)" title="Library (computing)">software library</a>, <i>libmusicbrainz</i>, is licensed under the <a href="/wiki/GNU_Lesser_General_Public_License" title="GNU Lesser General Public License">GNU Lesser General Public License</a>, which allows use of the code by proprietary software products. In December 2004, the MusicBrainz project was turned over to the <a href="/wiki/MetaBrainz_Foundation" title="MetaBrainz Foundation">MetaBrainz Foundation</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Non-profit" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-profit">non-profit</a> group, by its creator Robert Kaye.<sup id="cite_ref-metabrainz_announcement_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metabrainz_announcement-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On 20 January 2006, the first commercial venture to use MusicBrainz data was the <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, Spain-based <a href="/w/index.php?title=Linkara_(Social_networking_service)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Linkara (Social networking service) (page does not exist)">Linkara</a> in their <a href="/w/index.php?title=Linkara_M%C3%BAsica&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Linkara Música (page does not exist)">Linkara Música</a> service.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On 28 June 2007, BBC announced that it has licensed MusicBrainz's live data feed to augment their music Web pages. The BBC online music editors will also join the MusicBrainz community to contribute their knowledge to the database.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On 28 July 2008, the beta of the new BBC Music site was launched, which publishes a page for each MusicBrainz artist.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Client_software">Client software</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=MusicBrainz&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Client software">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
