	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Harold Percival Himsworth</h1>

<b>Sir Harold Percival</b> (Harry) <b>Himsworth</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Bath" title="Order of the Bath">KCB</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Fellow of the Royal Society">FRS</a><sup id="cite_ref-frs_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-frs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (19 May 1905 &#8211; 1 November 1993) was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> scientist, best known for his <a href="/wiki/Medical_research" title="Medical research">medical research</a> on <a href="/wiki/Diabetes_mellitus" class="mw-redirect" title="Diabetes mellitus">diabetes mellitus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harold_Percival_Himsworth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, the son of Joseph Arnold Himsworth and educated at the local Spring Grove School and <a href="/wiki/King_James%27s_Grammar_School,_Almondbury" class="mw-redirect" title="King James&#39;s Grammar School, Almondbury">King James's Grammar School, Almondbury</a>. He married Charlotte Gray in 1932: they had two sons.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medical_career">Medical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harold_Percival_Himsworth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Medical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He studied <a href="/wiki/Medicine" title="Medicine">medicine</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a> and trained in <a href="/wiki/University_College_Hospital" title="University College Hospital">University College Hospital</a> (UCH). His early involvement in medical research (especially of diabetes and later of <a href="/wiki/Liver_disease" title="Liver disease">liver disease</a>) would lead to an important 1936 paper in <a href="/wiki/The_Lancet" title="The Lancet">The Lancet</a>, distinguishing the two main types of diabetes.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He delivered the <a href="/wiki/Goulstonian_Lecture" title="Goulstonian Lecture">Goulstonian Lecture</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Physicians" title="Royal College of Physicians">Royal College of Physicians</a> in 1939 entitled <i>Mechanism of diabetes mellitus</i>
</p><p>He was appointed Professor of Medicine at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a> and the Secretary of the <a href="/wiki/Medical_Research_Council_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Medical Research Council (UK)">Medical Research Council</a> (MRC) for the very long period of 1949-1968.
</p><p>He was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Knight_Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_Bath" class="mw-redirect" title="Knight Commander of the Order of the Bath">KCB</a> in the <a href="/wiki/British_honours_system" class="mw-redirect" title="British honours system">New Year honours</a> of 1952 and in 1953 was appointed Honorary Physician to the Queen. he was elected a <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Fellow of the Royal Society">fellow of the Royal Society</a> in March, 1955.<sup id="cite_ref-frs_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-frs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Prime Minister of the day saw Sir Harold  Himsworth's report about the 'committee considering the effects of ..... nuclear radiation'  The Prime Minister's comment was "A pity, but we cannot help it". (dated 16 Nov 1955). From the office of AERE Directors Office.
</p><p>His archives are being held by the <a href="/wiki/Wellcome_Library" title="Wellcome Library">Wellcome Library</a>, London.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dieting">Dieting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Harold_Percival_Himsworth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dieting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Himsworth recommended a high-carbohydrate diet to treat diabetes. Professor Edwin Gale has noted:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>He demonstrated that injected insulin produced a greater hypoglycaemic response in individuals treated with the high carbohydrate diet, thus demonstrating that diet could influence insulin sensitivity. The high carbohydrate diet worked because it allowed the flow of glucose to the tissues to be maintained at a lower head of pressure by making people more sensitive to their own insulin.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>
