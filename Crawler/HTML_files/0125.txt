	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Manuel Alegre</h1>

<p><b>Manuel Alegre de Melo Duarte</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Liberty" title="Order of Liberty">GCL</a> (born 12 May 1936) is a Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Poet" title="Poet">poet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politician</a>, member of the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_(Portugal)" title="Socialist Party (Portugal)">Socialist Party</a>, and a candidate to the <a href="/wiki/2006_Portuguese_presidential_election" title="2006 Portuguese presidential election">2006 Portuguese presidential election</a>. He was a presidential candidate, in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Portuguese_presidential_election" title="2011 Portuguese presidential election">2011 presidential election</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> this time being backed up by the <a href="/wiki/Left_Bloc" title="Left Bloc">Left Bloc</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and the Socialist Party.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
Alegre  was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Cam%C3%B5es_Prize" title="Camões Prize">Camões Prize</a> in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Alegre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is the son of Francisco José de Faria e Melo Ferreira Duarte, brother of <a href="/wiki/Sportsperson" class="mw-redirect" title="Sportsperson">sportsman</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=M%C3%A1rio_Duarte&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mário Duarte (page does not exist)">Mário Duarte</a>, son of the 1st <a href="/wiki/Baroness" class="mw-redirect" title="Baroness">Baroness</a> of <i>a</i> Recosta, maternal grandson of the 1st <a href="/wiki/Baron" title="Baron">Baron</a> of Cadoro and matrilineal great-grandson of the 1st <a href="/wiki/Viscount" title="Viscount">Viscount</a> of <i>o</i> <a href="/wiki/Barreiro_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Barreiro (city)">Barreiro</a>, and wife Maria Manuela Alegre. His sister Maria Teresa Alegre de Melo Duarte is also a <a href="/wiki/Deputy_(legislator)" title="Deputy (legislator)">Deputy</a> and is the widow of another Deputy, António Jorge Moreira Portugal (1931–1994). Their son is <a href="/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a> Manuel Alegre Portugal. As he once stated, his ancestors were <a href="/wiki/Hanged" class="mw-redirect" title="Hanged">hanged</a> and <a href="/wiki/Decapitation" title="Decapitation">beheaded</a> at the Praça Nova, <a href="/wiki/Porto" title="Porto">Porto</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Wars" title="Liberal Wars">Liberal Wars</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manuel_Alegre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Communist_Party" title="Portuguese Communist Party">Portuguese Communist Party</a> from his youth until the Soviet invasion of <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a>, which he staunchly opposed, in 1968. Today he's usually considered one of the most leftist members of the Portuguese <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_(Portugal)" title="Socialist Party (Portugal)">Socialist Party</a>. He voted against all the revisions of the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="Portuguese Constitution">Portuguese Constitution</a> of 1976, and abstained at a commemorative vote for the 10th anniversary of the fall of <a href="/wiki/Berlin_Wall" title="Berlin Wall">Berlin Wall</a>, in 1999.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>While studying <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">law</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Coimbra" title="University of Coimbra">University of Coimbra</a>, Alegre was noticed for his opposition to <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Oliveira_Salazar" title="António de Oliveira Salazar">António de Oliveira Salazar</a>'s dictatorial government - the <a href="/wiki/Estado_Novo_(Portugal)" title="Estado Novo (Portugal)">Estado Novo</a> regime. He was <a href="/wiki/Conscript" class="mw-redirect" title="Conscript">conscripted</a>, and sent to the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a> and later to <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Angola" title="Portuguese Angola">Portuguese Angola</a>, where his involvement in an attempt to military rebellion led to his imprisonment. After serving his prison term in <a href="/wiki/Luanda" title="Luanda">Luanda</a>, he returned to <a href="/wiki/Coimbra" title="Coimbra">Coimbra</a>, before going into exile in 1964. As a student at the University of Coimbra he was a very active figure of the <a href="/wiki/Associa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Acad%C3%A9mica_de_Coimbra" class="mw-redirect" title="Associação Académica de Coimbra">Associação Académica de Coimbra</a>, the university's student's union, while member of the governing body, athlete and cultural agent (poetry and theatre). He would never graduate in law.
</p><p>He would live the next ten years in <a href="/wiki/Algiers" title="Algiers">Algiers</a>, where he was one of the main voices of a radio station directed to Portugal, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Voz_da_Liberdade&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Voz da Liberdade (page does not exist)">Voz da Liberdade</a> (<i>Freedom's Voice</i>), also called <i>Rádio Argel</i>, from where he reportedly led a series of activities supporting African forces opposing the Portuguese military intervention in the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Colonial_War" title="Portuguese Colonial War">Portuguese Colonial War</a>, including by airing privileged information regarding Portuguese strategy in the theater of war. The distribution of his first books was forbidden by Salazar's government, so they circulated in <a href="/wiki/Samizdat" title="Samizdat">samizdat</a> form. Alegre returned to Portugal in 1974, one week after the <a href="/wiki/Carnation_Revolution" title="Carnation Revolution">Carnation Revolution</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>He joined the Socialisty Party almost immediately, and was elected to Parliament in every election since 1975. He is currently one of the vice-presidents of Parliament, and sits in the President's advisory Council of State.
</p><p>Several of his poems were made into songs, sung among others by <a href="/wiki/Zeca_Afonso" title="Zeca Afonso">Zeca Afonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adriano_Correia_de_Oliveira" title="Adriano Correia de Oliveira">Adriano Correia de Oliveira</a>, and played by <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Paredes" title="Carlos Paredes">Carlos Paredes</a>.
</p><p>One of his poems <i>Uma flor de verde pinho</i> won 1976's <i>Festival RTP da Canção</i>, who represented Portugal in <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest" title="Eurovision Song Contest">Eurovision Song Contest</a>.
</p><p>In 2004, he lost to <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_S%C3%B3crates" title="José Sócrates">José Sócrates</a> a bid for the party leadership.
</p><p>In 2005, a statue in his honour was erected in <a href="/wiki/Coimbra" title="Coimbra">Coimbra</a>.
</p><p>On 24 September 2005, he announced that he would be a candidate in the <a href="/wiki/2006_Portuguese_presidential_election" title="2006 Portuguese presidential election">2006 Portuguese presidential election</a>, despite his party's official support for former president <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1rio_Soares" title="Mário Soares">Mário Soares</a> as a candidate. On the elections held 22 January 2006, he ended up collecting 20.7% of the valid votes (the second largest amount after the elected President, <a href="/wiki/An%C3%ADbal_Cavaco_Silva" title="Aníbal Cavaco Silva">Cavaco Silva</a>, and ahead of his party's official candidate Mário Soares).
</p><p>He is also a Member of the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Council_of_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Portuguese Council of State">Portuguese Council of State</a>, elected by the <a href="/wiki/Assembly_of_the_Republic_(Portugal)" title="Assembly of the Republic (Portugal)">Assembly of the Republic</a>.
</p>
