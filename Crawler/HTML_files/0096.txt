<p><b>Dame Kristin Ann Scott Thomas</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:100%;"><a href="/wiki/Dame_Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire">DBE</a></span> (born 24 May 1960) is an English actress.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Five times a <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Film_Awards" title="British Academy Film Awards">BAFTA Award</a> nominee and five-times <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier_Award" title="Laurence Olivier Award">Olivier Award</a> nominee, she won the <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Supporting_Role" title="BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role">BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Supporting Role</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Four_Weddings_and_a_Funeral" title="Four Weddings and a Funeral">Four Weddings and a Funeral</a></i> (1994) and the <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Laurence Olivier Award for Best Actress">Olivier Award for Best Actress</a> in 2008 for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Court_Theatre" title="Royal Court Theatre">Royal Court</a> revival of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Seagull" title="The Seagull">The Seagull</a></i>. She was also nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="Academy Award for Best Actress">Academy Award for Best Actress</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/The_English_Patient_(film)" title="The English Patient (film)">The English Patient</a></i> (1996).
</p><p>Scott Thomas made her film debut in the <a href="/wiki/Prince_(musician)" title="Prince (musician)">Prince</a>-directed <i><a href="/wiki/Under_the_Cherry_Moon" title="Under the Cherry Moon">Under the Cherry Moon</a></i> (1986),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and won the <a href="/wiki/Evening_Standard_British_Film_Awards#1988_Winners" title="Evening Standard British Film Awards">Evening Standard Film Award for Most Promising Newcomer</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/A_Handful_of_Dust_(film)" title="A Handful of Dust (film)">A Handful of Dust</a></i> (1988). She has worked in <a href="/wiki/French_cinema" class="mw-redirect" title="French cinema">French cinema</a>, including in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Valet" title="The Valet">The Valet</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Tell_No_One" title="Tell No One">Tell No One</a></i> (2007), <i><a href="/wiki/Leaving_(2009_film)" title="Leaving (2009 film)">Leaving</a></i> (2009), <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Crime" title="Love Crime">Love Crime</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Sarah%27s_Key" title="Sarah&#39;s Key">Sarah's Key</a></i> (2010), and won the <a href="/wiki/European_Film_Award_for_Best_Actress" title="European Film Award for Best Actress">European Film Award for Best Actress</a> for <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Claudel" title="Philippe Claudel">Philippe Claudel</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/I%27ve_Loved_You_So_Long" title="I&#39;ve Loved You So Long">I've Loved You So Long</a></i> (2008). Her other films include <i><a href="/wiki/Bitter_Moon" title="Bitter Moon">Bitter Moon</a></i> (1992), <i><a href="/wiki/Mission:_Impossible_(film)" title="Mission: Impossible (film)">Mission: Impossible</a></i> (1996), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Horse_Whisperer_(film)" title="The Horse Whisperer (film)">The Horse Whisperer</a></i> (1998), <i><a href="/wiki/Gosford_Park" title="Gosford Park">Gosford Park</a></i> (2001), <i><a href="/wiki/Nowhere_Boy" title="Nowhere Boy">Nowhere Boy</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Only_God_Forgives" title="Only God Forgives">Only God Forgives</a></i> (2013), <i><a href="/wiki/Darkest_Hour_(film)" title="Darkest Hour (film)">Darkest Hour</a></i> (2017), and <i><a href="/wiki/Tomb_Raider_(2018_film)" class="mw-redirect" title="Tomb Raider (2018 film)">Tomb Raider</a></i> (2018).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kristin_Scott_Thomas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scott Thomas was born in <a href="/wiki/Redruth" title="Redruth">Redruth</a>, Cornwall. Her mother, Deborah (n√©e Hurlbatt), was brought up in Hong Kong and Africa, and studied drama before marrying Kristin's father,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Commander" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant Commander">Lieutenant Commander</a> Simon Scott Thomas, a pilot in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fleet_Air_Arm" title="Fleet Air Arm">Fleet Air Arm</a> who died in a flying accident when Kristin was aged five.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NZ_Herald_10496540_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NZ_Herald_10496540-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She is the elder sister of actress <a href="/wiki/Serena_Scott_Thomas" title="Serena Scott Thomas">Serena Scott Thomas</a>, the niece of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Thomas_(Royal_Navy_officer)" title="Richard Thomas (Royal Navy officer)">Admiral Sir Richard Thomas</a> (a former <a href="/wiki/Black_Rod" title="Black Rod">Black Rod</a>, the parliamentary ceremonial officer in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and the great-great-niece of the ill-fated explorer <a href="/wiki/Robert_Falcon_Scott" title="Robert Falcon Scott">Captain Scott</a>, who lost the race to the South Pole.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Scott Thomas was brought up as a Roman Catholic.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Her childhood home was in <a href="/wiki/Trent,_Dorset" title="Trent, Dorset">Trent</a>, Dorset, England. Her mother remarried, to another <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> pilot, Lt Cdr Simon Idiens (of <a href="/wiki/Simon%27s_Sircus" title="Simon&#39;s Sircus">Simon's Sircus</a> aerobatic team flying <a href="/wiki/Sea_Vixen" class="mw-redirect" title="Sea Vixen">Sea Vixens</a>) who also died in a flying accident, flying a <a href="/wiki/Phantom_FG1" class="mw-redirect" title="Phantom FG1">Phantom FG1</a> off the North Cornwall coast, six years after the death of her father. Scott Thomas was educated at <a href="/wiki/Cheltenham_Ladies%27_College" title="Cheltenham Ladies&#39; College">Cheltenham Ladies' College</a> and <a href="/wiki/St_Antony%27s_Leweston" class="mw-redirect" title="St Antony&#39;s Leweston">St Antony's Leweston</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sherborne" title="Sherborne">Sherborne</a>, Dorset, both independent schools. On leaving school in 1978<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>, she moved to <a href="/wiki/Hampstead" title="Hampstead">Hampstead</a>, London, and worked in a department store. She began training to become a drama teacher at the <a href="/wiki/Central_School_of_Speech_and_Drama" class="mw-redirect" title="Central School of Speech and Drama">Central School of Speech and Drama</a>, enrolling on a BEd in Speech and Drama.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> During her time at the school, she requested to switch degree courses to acting but was refused.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> After a year at Central, speaking French fluently, she decided to move to Paris as an au-pair<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and studied acting at the <i>√âcole nationale sup√©rieure des arts et techniques du th√©√¢tre</i> (<a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/ENSATT" class="extiw" title="fr:ENSATT">ENSATT</a>). When she was 25, she was cast as Mary Sharon in the  film <i><a href="/wiki/Under_the_Cherry_Moon" title="Under the Cherry Moon">Under the Cherry Moon</a></i> (1986).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kristin_Scott_Thomas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kristin_Scott_Thomas_Cannes.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Kristin_Scott_Thomas_Cannes.jpg/220px-Kristin_Scott_Thomas_Cannes.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="298" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Kristin_Scott_Thomas_Cannes.jpg/330px-Kristin_Scott_Thomas_Cannes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Kristin_Scott_Thomas_Cannes.jpg/440px-Kristin_Scott_Thomas_Cannes.jpg 2x" data-file-width="446" data-file-height="604" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kristin_Scott_Thomas_Cannes.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Scott Thomas at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="2009 Cannes Film Festival">2009 Cannes Film Festival</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Her breakthrough role was in a 1988 adaptation of <a href="/wiki/Evelyn_Waugh" title="Evelyn Waugh">Evelyn Waugh</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/A_Handful_of_Dust_(film)" title="A Handful of Dust (film)">A Handful of Dust</a></i>, where she won an <i><a href="/wiki/Evening_Standard" title="Evening Standard">Evening Standard</a></i> British Film Award for most promising newcomer. This was followed by roles opposite <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Grant" title="Hugh Grant">Hugh Grant</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Bitter_Moon" title="Bitter Moon">Bitter Moon</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Four_Weddings_and_a_Funeral" title="Four Weddings and a Funeral">Four Weddings and a Funeral</a></i> where she won a BAFTA for Best Supporting Actress.
</p><p>In 1994, she starred in the Romanian-French film <i><a href="/wiki/An_Unforgettable_Summer" title="An Unforgettable Summer">An Unforgettable Summer</a></i>, in which she played Marie-Th√©r√®se Von Debretsy. Rather than learn Romanian for the part, she read her lines phonetically.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> She had all the lines translated into French, which she speaks fluently, so she knew what she was saying.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In a 2002 interview for <a href="/wiki/World_Entertainment_News_Network" title="World Entertainment News Network">WENN</a>, Kristin stated that this is the film that she is most proud of and that she views it as the peak of her career.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (May 2017)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In an interview for Gloucester Citizen on 22 March 2015, she cited <i>An Unforgettable Summer</i> as one of the films that she's most proud of alongside <i>The English Patient</i> and <i>Only God Forgives</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>1996 saw the release of her most famous role as Katharine Clifton in <i><a href="/wiki/The_English_Patient_(film)" title="The English Patient (film)">The English Patient</a></i>, which gained her Golden Globe and Oscar nominations as well as critical acclaim. This was followed by a brief period working in Hollywood on films such as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Horse_Whisperer_(film)" title="The Horse Whisperer (film)">The Horse Whisperer</a></i> with Robert Redford and <i><a href="/wiki/Random_Hearts" title="Random Hearts">Random Hearts</a></i> with Harrison Ford. However, growing disillusioned with Hollywood, she took a year off to give birth to her third child.
</p><p>She returned to the stage in 2001 when she played the title role in a French theatre production of Racine's <i>Berenice</i> and on screen as Lady Sylvia McCordle in Robert Altman's critically acclaimed <i><a href="/wiki/Gosford_Park" title="Gosford Park">Gosford Park</a></i>. This started a critically acclaimed second career on stage, in which she has received four nominations for a <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Olivier_Award" title="Laurence Olivier Award">Laurence Olivier Award</a> for Best Actress, including one win, for her performance of Arkadina in a <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">London West End</a> production of <a href="/wiki/Anton_Chekhov" title="Anton Chekhov">Anton Chekhov</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Seagull" title="The Seagull">The Seagull</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> She reprised the role in New York in September 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In summer 2011, Scott Thomas returned to <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">London's West End</a> to star as Emma in <a href="/wiki/Harold_Pinter" title="Harold Pinter">Harold Pinter</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Betrayal_(play)" title="Betrayal (play)">Betrayal</a></i> at the <a href="/wiki/Comedy_Theatre" class="mw-redirect" title="Comedy Theatre">Comedy Theatre</a>. The revival was directed by <a href="/wiki/Ian_Rickson" title="Ian Rickson">Ian Rickson</a>. In January 2013, she starred in another Pinter play, <i><a href="/wiki/Old_Times" title="Old Times">Old Times</a></i>, again directed by Ian Rickson. In 2014, she appeared at <a href="/wiki/The_Old_Vic" title="The Old Vic">The Old Vic</a> in the title role of <a href="/wiki/Sophocles" title="Sophocles">Sophocles</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Electra" title="Electra">Electra</a></i>.
