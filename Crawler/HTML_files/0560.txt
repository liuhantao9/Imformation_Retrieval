<p><b>Asian people</b><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> or <b>Asiatic people</b><sup id="cite_ref-MESH_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MESH-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> are people who descend from a portion of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/List_of_Asian_countries_by_population" title="List of Asian countries by population">population</a>.
</p><p>A variety of definitions and geographical data are presented by organizations and individuals for classifying the <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_Asia" title="Ethnic groups in Asia">ethnic groups in Asia</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Definitions_by_country">Definitions by country</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anglophone_Africa_and_Caribbean">Anglophone Africa and Caribbean</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Asian_South_Africans" title="Asian South Africans">Asian South Africans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indo-Caribbean" class="mw-redirect" title="Indo-Caribbean">Indo-Caribbean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Caribbean" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinese Caribbean">Chinese Caribbean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indo-African" title="Indo-African">Indo-African</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Afro-Asians" title="Afro-Asians">Afro-Asians</a></div>
<p>In parts of <a href="/wiki/English-speaking_world" title="English-speaking world">anglophone</a> <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">East Africa</a> and in parts of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, the term "Asian" is more commonly associated with people of <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asian</a> origin, particularly <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistanis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladeshis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lankans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In South Africa the term Asian is used in the pan-continental sense. Due to the high number of Indians in South Africa, in official documentation the designation "Indian" is used to refer to both South- and East-Asians. <sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="This claim needs references to better sources. (October 2017)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Asian_Australians" title="Asian Australians">Asian Australians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indian_Australians" title="Indian Australians">Indian Australians</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Australian_Census" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian Census">Australian Census</a> includes <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Census_in_Australia" title="Census in Australia">Australian Census</a> includes four regions of Asia in its official definition. Defined by the 2006–2011 Australian Census, three <i>broad groups</i> have the word Asian included in their name: <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia"><i>Central</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia"><i>Southern Asian</i></a>, <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia"><i>South-East Asian</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia"><i>North-East Asian</i></a>.  Russians are classified as <i>Southern and Eastern Europeans</i> while Middle Easterners are classified as <i>North African and Middle Easterners</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Asian_Canadians" title="Asian Canadians">Asian Canadians</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indo-Canadians" title="Indo-Canadians">Indo-Canadians</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Census_in_Canada" title="Census in Canada">Canadian Census</a> uses the term 'Asian' pan-continentally. In its presentation of the "ethnic origin" results of the 2016 census, <a href="/wiki/Statistics_Canada" title="Statistics Canada">Statistics Canada</a> under the category "Asian origins" includes: West Central Asian and Middle Eastern (includes "Arab, not otherwise specified"), South Asian, East and Southeast Asian, and "other" Asian origins.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Asian_New_Zealanders" title="Asian New Zealanders">Asian New Zealanders</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>'s census undertaken by <a href="/wiki/Statistics_New_Zealand" title="Statistics New Zealand">Statistics New Zealand</a> defines the Asian to include people of <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indian_people" title="Indian people">Indian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">Korean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Filipino_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Filipino people">Filipino</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_people" title="Vietnamese people">Vietnamese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Sri_Lanka" title="Demographics of Sri Lanka">Sri Lankan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_Cambodia" title="Demographics of Cambodia">Cambodian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thai_people" title="Thai people">Thai</a> ancestries.<sup id="cite_ref-StatNewZealand_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatNewZealand-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In less formal contexts, the term <i>Asian</i> often does not include <a href="/wiki/South_Asian_ethnic_groups" title="South Asian ethnic groups">South Asian people</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Norway">Norway</span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Statistics_Norway" title="Statistics Norway">Statistics Norway</a> uses the term 'Asian' pan-continentally and considers people of Asian background to be people from all Asian countries.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sweden">Sweden</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Asian_in_Sweden" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian in Sweden">Asian in Sweden</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Statistics_Sweden" title="Statistics Sweden">Statistics Sweden</a> uses the term 'Asian' to refer to immigrants of Asian background from all <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> countries, including the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/British_Asian" title="British Asian">British Asian</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Asians_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="East Asians in the United Kingdom">East Asians in the United Kingdom</a>, and <a href="/wiki/British_Indo-Caribbean_people" title="British Indo-Caribbean people">British Indo-Caribbean people</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sadiq_Khan.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Sadiq_Khan.png/200px-Sadiq_Khan.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="280" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Sadiq_Khan.png/300px-Sadiq_Khan.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/19/Sadiq_Khan.png 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="559" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sadiq_Khan.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sadiq_Khan" title="Sadiq Khan">Sadiq Khan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_London" title="Mayor of London">Mayor of London</a>, is a <a href="/wiki/British_Pakistani" class="mw-redirect" title="British Pakistani">British Pakistani</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, the term "Asian" is more commonly associated with people of <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asian</a> origin, particularly <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistanis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladeshis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lankans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated5_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated5-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Aspinall_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aspinall-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>  The UK usage of the term "Asian" is reflected in the "ethnic group" section of <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">UK census forms</a>, which treat <a href="/wiki/British_Asian" title="British Asian">"Asian"</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Chinese" title="British Chinese">"Chinese"</a> as separate (see <a href="/wiki/British_Asian" title="British Asian">British Asian</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-StatUK_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatUK-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>  Most respondents to the UK 2001 Census of non-Chinese East Asian and Southeast Asian descent chose to write-in their ethnicity in the "Other Ethnic Group" category rather than the "Other Asian" category, reflecting the association of the word Asian in the UK with South Asian.<sup id="cite_ref-ONS_who_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ONS_who-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Despite there being a strong presence of <a href="/wiki/East_Asians_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="East Asians in the United Kingdom">East Asians in the United Kingdom</a> there are considerably more <a href="/wiki/British_Asian" title="British Asian">South Asians</a>, for example the 2001 Census recorded 1.05 million people of Indian origin and 247,000 of Chinese origin in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-ONS_ethnicity_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ONS_ethnicity-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Peter_J._Aspinall" title="Peter J. Aspinall">Peter J. Aspinall</a> of the Centre for Health Services Studies, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kent" title="University of Kent">University of Kent</a>, recommends privileging the term "South Asian" over the term "Asian", since the term "Asian" is a "contested term".<sup id="cite_ref-Aspinall_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aspinall-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In 1968, an Asian activist conference decided on favoring the name "<a href="/wiki/Asian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian American">Asian American</a>" over the competing terms—"<a href="/wiki/Yellow_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Yellow people">yellow</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Mongoloid_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Mongoloid race">Mongoloid</a>", "Asiatic", and "<a href="/wiki/Orient" title="Orient">Oriental</a>"—since the <a href="/wiki/Filipino_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Filipino people">Filipinos</a> at the meeting thought they were "<a href="/wiki/Brown_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Brown people">brown</a>" rather than "yellow" and the conference thought the term "Oriental" was <a href="/wiki/Eurocentrism" title="Eurocentrism">Eurocentric</a>, since they originate from lands "east" only from Europe's standpoint and, since the term "Oriental" suggested to them "passivity".<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Earlier Census forms from 1980 and prior listed particular Asian ancestries as separate groups along with <i>White</i> and <i>Black or Negro</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Previously, Asian Americans were classified as "other".<sup id="cite_ref-GordonLee_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GordonLee-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>  But the <a href="/wiki/1980_United_States_Census" title="1980 United States Census">1980 Census</a> marked the first general analyses of Asians as a group, combining several individual ancestry groups into "Asian or Pacific Islander."  By the <a href="/wiki/1990_United_States_Census" title="1990 United States Census">1990 Census</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Asian_Pacific_American" title="Asian Pacific American">Asian or Pacific Islander</a> (API)</i> was included as an explicit category, although respondents had to select one particular ancestry.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 2000 and 2010 U.S. Census Bureau definition of the Asian <a href="/wiki/Race_(human_classification)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (human classification)">race</a> is: "people having origins in any of the original peoples of the <a href="/wiki/Far_East" title="Far East">Far East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>, or the <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">Indian subcontinent</a> (for example, <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Philippine Islands">Philippine Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>)".<sup id="cite_ref-USCensus2000Asian_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USCensus2000Asian-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1930 and 1940, <i><a href="/wiki/Indian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian American">Indian Americans</a></i> were identified as a separate race, <i><a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">Hindu</a></i>, and in 1950 and 1960 they were racially classified as <i>Other Race</i>, and then in 1970 they were classified as <i>White</i>. Since 1980, Indians and all other South Asians have been classified as part of the <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian ethnic group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>  Sociologist Madhulika Khandelwal described how "<i>....as a result of <a href="/wiki/Activism" title="Activism">activism</a>, South Asians came to be included as 'Asians' in the census only in the 80's. Prior to that many South Asians had been checking 'Caucasian' or 'Other'.</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Respondents can also report their specific ancestry, e.g.: <a href="/wiki/Ryukyuan_people" title="Ryukyuan people">Okinawan</a>, etc.  Someone reporting these ancestries but no race would be classified as "Asian". Unlike South Asians, <a href="/wiki/American_Jews" title="American Jews">Jewish Americans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israeli_Americans" title="Israeli Americans">Israeli Americans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arab_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab American">Arab Americans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Armenian American">Armenian Americans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Assyrian American">Assyrian Americans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kurdish_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Kurdish American">Kurdish Americans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Georgian American">Georgian Americans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chechen_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Chechen American">Chechen Americans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkish_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Turkish American">Turkish Americans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijani_Americans" title="Azerbaijani Americans">Azerbaijani Americans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iranian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Iranian American">Iranian Americans</a> and Central Asian Americans have not lobbied to be included as Asians by the <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">U.S. Census Board</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In normal American usage Asian does not refer to the people from the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Islands">Pacific Islands</a> who are usually called <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islanders" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Islanders">Pacific Islanders</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>  The term "Asians and Pacific Islanders" or "Asia/Pacific" was used on the <a href="/wiki/1990_US_Census" class="mw-redirect" title="1990 US Census">1990 US Census</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
However, in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_2000_Census" class="mw-redirect" title="United States 2000 Census">2000 US Census</a>,  the Asian or Pacific Islander category was separated into two categories, "Asian" and "Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander".<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arab_States_of_the_Persian_Gulf">Arab States of the Persian Gulf</span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Arab_states_of_the_Persian_Gulf" title="Arab states of the Persian Gulf">Arab states of the Persian Gulf</a>, the term "Asian" generally refers to people of <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asian</a> descent due to the large <a href="/wiki/Non-resident_Indian_and_person_of_Indian_origin" title="Non-resident Indian and person of Indian origin">Indian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Pakistani" title="Overseas Pakistani">Pakistani</a> and <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Filipinos" title="Overseas Filipinos">Filipino</a> expatriate population in these countries.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> However, there are instances where the term is used solely to refer to those of South Asian descent.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>A 2009 book about <a href="/wiki/Forensic_anthropology" title="Forensic anthropology">forensic anthropology</a> said that the "<i>leading handbook in human <a href="/wiki/Osteology" title="Osteology">osteology</a></i>" has kept on using the "<i>traditional terms</i>"' of "<i>caucasoid, mongoloid, negroid</i>", but "<i>one of the most popular recent forensic anthropology textbooks</i>" has changed to the "<i>more current, politically correct terminology of Asian, white, black, Native American</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Eugénia Maria Guedes Pinto Antunes da Cunha of the Department of Anthropology, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Coimbra" title="University of Coimbra">University of Coimbra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, said there has been a modern trend in "most of the <a href="/wiki/Forensic_anthropology" title="Forensic anthropology">forensic anthropology</a> literature" to "rename" the term "<a href="/wiki/Mongoloid_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Mongoloid race">Mongoloid</a>", a term in which she includes the "North American Indian", with the term "Asian" or "Asiatic".<sup id="cite_ref-Cunha_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cunha-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Antunes da Cunha said that, even though the "terminology" has changed, the "underlying assumptions are the same".<sup id="cite_ref-Cunha_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cunha-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Karen_T._Taylor" title="Karen T. Taylor">Karen T. Taylor</a> <a href="/wiki/Forensic_arts" title="Forensic arts">forensic art</a> professor at the <a href="/wiki/FBI_Academy" title="FBI Academy">FBI Academy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quantico,_Virginia" title="Quantico, Virginia">Quantico, Virginia</a>, said that the term "Asian-derived" is a modern-day <a href="/wiki/Euphemism" title="Euphemism">euphemism</a> for the "<a href="/wiki/Mongoloid_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Mongoloid race">Mongoloid race</a>" and it includes "Native Americans" and "various Asian groups".<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Kyung-Ran Jung et al. (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>:전경란) of the Department of Laboratory Medicine, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Ulsan" title="University of Ulsan">University of Ulsan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> used the term "Asian populations" for the group he also referred to as the "Asian-<a href="/wiki/Mongoloid_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Mongoloid race">Mongoloid</a>" in which he included <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korean_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Korean people">Korean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thai_people" title="Thai people">Thai</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Meta-analysis" title="Meta-analysis">meta-analysis</a> of <a href="/wiki/Allele" title="Allele">alleles</a> in relation to <a href="/wiki/Rheumatoid_arthritis" title="Rheumatoid arthritis">rheumatoid arthritis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dr. <a href="/wiki/Marta_Miraz%C3%B3n_Lahr" title="Marta Mirazón Lahr">Marta Mirazón Lahr</a> of the Department of Biological Anthropology at <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge University">Cambridge University</a> said "all" "Asian populations" are "grouped under the name <a href="/wiki/Mongoloid_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Mongoloid race">Mongoloid</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Masniari Novita of the Biomedical Department of <a href="/wiki/List_of_universities_in_Indonesia" title="List of universities in Indonesia">Jember University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jember_Regency" title="Jember Regency">Jember</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, said "Asiatics" are part of the "<a href="/wiki/Mongoloid_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Mongoloid race">Mongoloid</a>" race while "<a href="/wiki/South_Asian_ethnic_groups" title="South Asian ethnic groups">Asians from the Indian Subcontinent</a>" are part of the "<a href="/wiki/Caucasoid_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Caucasoid race">Caucasian</a>" race.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Matt_Cartmill" title="Matt Cartmill">Matt Cartmill</a> of the Department of Biological Anthropology and Anatomy, <a href="/wiki/Duke_University" title="Duke University">Duke University</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, said "geography has little to do with the race concept in its actual application", since "Asian individuals [can be] born in the same geographical region" as other races.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Bamshad et al. of the Department of Human Genetics, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Utah" title="University of Utah">University of Utah</a>, found that "107 <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_African" class="mw-redirect" title="Sub-Saharan African">sub-Saharan African</a>, 67 <a href="/wiki/East_Asian" class="mw-redirect" title="East Asian">East Asian</a> and 81 <a href="/wiki/Western_European" class="mw-redirect" title="Western European">Western European</a>" individuals genetically clustered with "ancestry from a single population" at levels of "almost 100%", but among "263 individuals from <a href="/wiki/South_India" title="South India">South India</a>" the "proportion of ancestry shared with Europeans and Asians varies widely".<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sandra Soo-Jin Lee (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>:이수진) of the Department of Cultural and Social Anthropology at <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" class="mw-redirect" title="United States of America">United States of America</a>, said that the reasoning behind "Asian" being a "race" as defined by the US Census is "difficult to determine" because it includes "<a href="/wiki/South_Asian_ethnic_groups" title="South Asian ethnic groups">South Asians</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Willett Enos Rotzell professor of Botany and Zoology at the <a href="/wiki/Drexel_University_College_of_Medicine#History" title="Drexel University College of Medicine">Hahnemann Medical College</a> used the term "Asian" "race" to refer to the race he alternatively called the "<a href="/wiki/Yellow_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Yellow people">Yellow</a> or <a href="/wiki/Mongoloid_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Mongoloid race">Mongolian</a> race".<sup id="cite_ref-Rotzell_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rotzell-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A writing <a href="/wiki/Style_guide" title="Style guide">style guide</a> published in 2011 by two professors at universities in the United States of America recommends using the term "Asian" to refer to people living in Asian countries such as "China, Japan, Korea, Vietnam, etc." unless a given situation makes using a more specific nationality term more appropriate than using the broader term "Asian".<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a 2014 article about Asian <a href="/wiki/Rhinoplasty" title="Rhinoplasty">rhinoplasty</a>, in an <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">academic journal</a> about <a href="/wiki/Plastic_surgery" title="Plastic surgery">plastic surgery</a>, Clyde H. Ishii, the article's author, said, "<i>Although many countries use the term 'Asian' referring to people living on that continent, in reality, Asian usually refers to peoples of <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast</a>, and <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>. Inhabitants of <a href="/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">Southwest Asia</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Arabs" title="Arabs">Arabs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iranian_peoples" title="Iranian peoples">Iranians</a>, are more commonly designated <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_the_Middle_East" title="Ethnic groups in the Middle East">Middle Easterners</a>.<sup>3</sup> The term Asian as used in this article refers to people from Asia who have a <a href="/wiki/Mongoloid" title="Mongoloid">Mongoloid</a> background (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_people" title="Chinese people">Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">Koreans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_people" title="Vietnamese people">Vietnamese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Filipinos" title="Filipinos">Filipinos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesians" title="Indonesians">Indonesians</a>, and others).</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><sup><small>1</small></sup> The U.S. Census Bureau definition of Asians refers to a person having origins in any of the original peoples of the Far East, Southeast Asia, or the Indian subcontinent.
<sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20090831085310/http://quickfacts.census.gov/qfd/meta/long_68178.htm">[2]</a></sup><sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://www.census.gov/quickfacts/meta/long_RHI125213.htm">[3]</a></sup><sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://www.census.gov/mso/www/training/pdf/race-ethnicity-onepager.pdf">[4]</a></sup>
</p><p><sup><small>2</small></sup> The United States Government classified <a href="/wiki/Kalmyk_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Kalmyk people">Kalmyks</a> as Asian until 1951, when <a href="/wiki/Kalmyk_Americans" title="Kalmyk Americans">Kalmyk Americans</a> were reclassified as <a href="/wiki/White_Americans" title="White Americans">White Americans</a>.<sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://books.google.com/books?id=_r9Ni6_u0JEC&amp;lpg=PA434&amp;ots=9CIOAhwtBV&amp;dq=Kalmyks%20reclassified%20European&amp;pg=PA434#v=onepage&amp;q=Kalmyks%20reclassified%20European&amp;f=false">[5]</a></sup> 
</p><p><sup><small>3</small></sup> The U.S. Census Bureau reclassifies anyone identifying as "Tibetan American" as "Chinese American".<sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://www.census.gov/prod/cen1990/cph-r/cph-r-1b.pdf">[6]</a></sup>
</p><p><sup><small>4</small></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bengali_Americans" title="Bengali Americans">Bengali Americans</a> may be classified as Bangladeshi or Indian.<sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://books.google.com/books?id=YMUola6pDnkC&amp;lpg=PA459&amp;ots=cu71YR15ky&amp;dq=%22Bengali%20American%22%20Indian%20Bangladeshi&amp;pg=PA459#v=onepage&amp;q=%22Bengali%20American%22%20Indian%20Bangladeshi&amp;f=false">[7]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Punjabi_Americans" title="Punjabi Americans">Punjabi Americans</a> may be classified as Indian or Pakistani.<sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://books.google.com/books?id=-0sEJ_0vV1QC&amp;lpg=PA1022&amp;ots=w6RwVXyCZ0&amp;dq=%22Punjabi%20American%22%20Indian%20Pakistani&amp;pg=PA1022#v=onepage&amp;q=%22Punjabi%20American%22%20Indian%20Pakistani&amp;f=false">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<sup><small>5</small></sup> Those of <a href="/wiki/West_Asian" class="mw-redirect" title="West Asian">West Asian</a> extraction (i.e. the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a>) are classified as White under the U.S. Census.<sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://www.census.gov/mso/www/training/pdf/race-ethnicity-onepager.pdf">[9]</a></sup></div></div></div></td></tr></tbody></table></div>
