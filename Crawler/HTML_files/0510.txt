<p><b>Deighton</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span><span title="/iː/: &#39;ee&#39; in &#39;fleece&#39;">iː</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/ən/: &#39;on&#39; in &#39;button&#39;">ən</span></span>/</a></span></span> is a district of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. It is situated 2 miles (3.2&#160;km) north east of the town centre and lies off the <a href="/wiki/A62_road" title="A62 road">A62 Leeds Road</a>.
</p><p>Deighton was formerly known as <b>East Bradley</b>, and <a href="/wiki/Bradley,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradley, West Yorkshire">Bradley</a> was called <b>West Bradley</b>. The name changed when the Deighton family bought the area stretching from Screamer Woods (near the Deighton Fields) to <a href="/wiki/Sheepridge,_Huddersfield" title="Sheepridge, Huddersfield">Sheepridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brackenhall" title="Brackenhall">Brackenhall</a>.
</p><p>Deighton has a <a href="/wiki/Deighton_railway_station" title="Deighton railway station">railway station</a> on the <b><a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Line">Huddersfield Line</a></b> for services to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>. The typical journey time to Huddersfield is usually 7 minutes, to Wakefield Westgate 31 minutes and to Leeds 34 minutes.
</p><p>The Deighton Centre is a place for educational, training and leisure activities.  It was formerly a high school for Deighton, Bradley and <a href="/wiki/Brackenhall" title="Brackenhall">Brackenhall</a> students. Deighton High School closed on 31 August 1992, and most students and some staff were relocated to <a href="/wiki/Fartown_High_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Fartown High School">Fartown High School</a>. The centre was refurbished as a sports/music venue and the Deighton Arena houses a gym, squash court and basketball court and is a venue for <a href="/wiki/Dancehall" title="Dancehall">dancehall</a> music. In March 2016 the Deighton Centre was demolished.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The chemical company, <b><a href="/wiki/Syngenta" title="Syngenta">Syngenta</a></b> has a large plant off the <a href="/wiki/A62_road" title="A62 road">A62</a> Leeds Road.
</p><p>Leeds Road Playing Fields has <a href="/wiki/Soccer" class="mw-redirect" title="Soccer">football</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> and all-weather pitches, a sports hall and an <a href="/wiki/Sport_of_athletics" title="Sport of athletics">athletics</a> track. Home to Kirklees Ladies FC.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deighton_Carnival">Deighton Carnival</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Deighton,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Deighton Carnival">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Deighton is a multi-racial district and home to Deighton Carnival in late June every year organised by Fresh Horizons - A community based social enterprise group based in Deighton. The carnival started in 2002 and attracts 3,000 people from Deighton and the rest of Huddersfield. It starts with a procession of floats playing <a href="/wiki/Dancehall" title="Dancehall">Reggae Dancehall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bassline" title="Bassline">Bassline</a>, <a href="/wiki/RnB" class="mw-redirect" title="RnB">RnB</a>, <a href="/wiki/UK_Garage" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Garage">Garage</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">Reggae</a> on the road, costumed bands make up more of the procession including <i>Suga Brown</i> dance group, <i>"Whitacre TRA"</i> and Bradley Junior and Infant and Ashbrow Junior and Infant schools.
</p>
