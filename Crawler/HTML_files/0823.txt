<p><b>Charles Thomas Clough</b> MA, LLD, FGS, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Edinburgh" title="Royal Society of Edinburgh">FRSE</a> (23 December 1852 – 27 August 1916) was a prominent British <a href="/wiki/Geologist" title="Geologist">geologist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cartographer" class="mw-redirect" title="Cartographer">mapmaker</a>. The Edinburgh Geological Society named the <b>Clough Medal</b> in his honour.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Clough_(geologist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Charles Clough was born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the fifth of six children to the lawyer Thomas William Clough and Amelia Jane Ibeson. He attended <a href="/wiki/Rugby_School" title="Rugby School">Rugby School</a> from 1867 to 1871, and in 1871 was accepted at St John's College, Cambridge, to study Natural Sciences. He graduated in 1878 but was working from 1875, being employed as an Assistant Geologist on the national Geological Survey.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He initially worked in the <a href="/wiki/Teesdale" title="Teesdale">Teesdale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cheviot_Hills" title="Cheviot Hills">Cheviot</a> districts of <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a>, under H.H. Howell. In 1884 he was transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> office, in Scotland. Here his fame within his field grew for his work in the <a href="/wiki/North_West_Highlands" class="mw-redirect" title="North West Highlands">North West Highlands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hebrides" title="Hebrides">Hebrides</a>. In 1896 he was promoted to full Geologist and, on the death of William Gunn,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> in 1902 to District Geologist.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geological_Society_awards">Geological Society awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Clough_(geologist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geological Society awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1906 the Geological Society of London awarded him the <a href="/wiki/Murchison_Medal" title="Murchison Medal">Murchison Medal</a>. In 1908 he was elected President of the <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Geological_Society" title="Edinburgh Geological Society">Edinburgh Geological Society</a>, a post he held until 1910. 
</p><p>In the summer of 1916 <a href="/wiki/St_Andrews_University" class="mw-redirect" title="St Andrews University">St Andrews University</a> awarded him an Honorary degree as a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Laws" class="mw-redirect" title="Doctor of Laws">Doctor of Laws</a> (LLD). In the same year he was elected <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society_of_Edinburgh" class="mw-redirect" title="Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh">Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh</a>, his proposers were <a href="/wiki/John_Horne" title="John Horne">John Horne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Peach" title="Ben Peach">Benjamin Neeve Peach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kidston" title="Robert Kidston">Robert Kidston</a> and Sir <a href="/wiki/John_Smith_Flett" class="mw-redirect" title="John Smith Flett">John Smith Flett</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Clough_(geologist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1881 he married Anne Mary Usher, daughter of Thomas Durham Usher. They had a son and two daughters. They lived at St Ann’s Mount on <a href="/wiki/Polton" title="Polton">Polton</a>, just south of <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> His wife died in 1935 and was buried with Charles in <a href="/wiki/Lasswade" title="Lasswade">Lasswade</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Clough_(geologist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_grave_of_Charles_Thomas_Clough,_Lasswade_Cemetery.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/The_grave_of_Charles_Thomas_Clough%2C_Lasswade_Cemetery.jpg/170px-The_grave_of_Charles_Thomas_Clough%2C_Lasswade_Cemetery.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/The_grave_of_Charles_Thomas_Clough%2C_Lasswade_Cemetery.jpg/255px-The_grave_of_Charles_Thomas_Clough%2C_Lasswade_Cemetery.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/The_grave_of_Charles_Thomas_Clough%2C_Lasswade_Cemetery.jpg/340px-The_grave_of_Charles_Thomas_Clough%2C_Lasswade_Cemetery.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2056" data-file-height="3088" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_grave_of_Charles_Thomas_Clough,_Lasswade_Cemetery.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The grave of Charles Thomas Clough, <a href="/wiki/Lasswade" title="Lasswade">Lasswade</a> Cemetery</div></div></div>
<p>On 23 August 1916 Clough was studying rocks in a narrow railway cutting, near Manuel House, south of <a href="/wiki/Bo%27ness" title="Bo&#39;ness">Bo'ness</a>, <a href="/wiki/Falkirk" title="Falkirk">Falkirk</a>, when he was struck by a train as he crossed the line and severely injured, necessitating the amputation of both legs, at Edinburgh Infirmary. He died of <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a> in hospital, four days later, on Sunday 27 August.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He was buried on 30 August, near the centre of the western section of <a href="/wiki/Lasswade" title="Lasswade">Lasswade</a> Cemetery.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Principal_accomplishments">Principal accomplishments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Charles_Clough_(geologist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Principal accomplishments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Completion of the 1 inch map of England and Wales</li>
<li>Survey of the <a href="/wiki/Cowal" title="Cowal">Cowal</a> District in western Scotland</li>
<li>Aiding in the survey of the North-West Highlands</li>
<li>Survey and Mapping of large areas of Sutherland</li>
<li>Survey and mapping of <a href="/wiki/Loch_Maree" title="Loch Maree">Loch Maree</a>.</li>
<li>Survey of Ross-shire</li>
<li>Survey of North Argyllshire and <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Mull" title="Isle of Mull">Mull</a></li>
<li>Survey of the coalfields in the Lothians, Lanarkshire and North Ayrshire</li>
<li>See<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
