<p><b>Luciano Cruz-Coke Carvallo</b> (born July 1, 1970  in <a href="/wiki/Santiago,_Chile" class="mw-redirect" title="Santiago, Chile">Santiago, Chile</a>) is a Chilean actor, and was the <a href="/wiki/National_Council_of_Culture_and_the_Arts" title="National Council of Culture and the Arts">Minister of Arts and Culture</a> under the 35th President <a href="/wiki/Sebasti%C3%A1n_Pi%C3%B1era" title="Sebastián Piñera">Sebastián Piñera</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luciano_Cruz-Coke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cruz-Coke is the son of lawyer and university professor Carlos Cruz-Coke Ossa and Lucía Carvallo Arriagada.  He brother is Carlos Cruz-Coke Carvallo, <a href="/wiki/Councillor" title="Councillor">Councilman</a> of Vitacura in Santiago.
</p><p>Cruz-Coke studied in the Colegio de los Sagrados Corazones de Manquehue. He went on to study law in the Universidad Finis Terrae, later changing his major to Architecture and finally became an actor.  He studied acting at the <a href="/wiki/Lee_Strasberg_Theatre_and_Film_Institute" title="Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute">Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. He holds a degree in Documentary Filming from the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Christian_Humanism_University" title="Academy of Christian Humanism University">Academy of Christian Humanism University</a> and a master's degree in Mass Communications from University of Chile.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luciano_Cruz-Coke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cruz-Coke has a fifteen-year acting career in film, theater and television. He is also the founder and director of the <a href="/wiki/Non-profit_organization" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-profit organization">non-profit organization</a> Teatro Lastarria 90, which caters to activities in film, theater and visual arts.  Lastarria 90 has become a model innovator, attracting both private and public supporters, and which has received recognition en the world of the arts and critique.
</p><p>He was spotted by an executive of <a href="/wiki/Canal_13_(Chile)" title="Canal 13 (Chile)">Canal 13</a> in a pub while he was singing. His first television role was in 1995 in the series El Amor Esta de Moda, where he became known as the "romeo" of <a href="/wiki/Telenovela" title="Telenovela">telenovela</a>. He would go on to play further roles in other series. He won the award Apes in 2004 for his part in a telenovela Destinos Cruzados.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luciano_Cruz-Coke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cruz-Coke was the founder of the Comisión Cultura de los Grupos Tantauco (Cultural Commission of the Tantauco Groups).  During the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Chilean_presidential_election" title="2009–10 Chilean presidential election">2009-2010 Chilean Presidential elections</a> he openly supported the current President-elect Sebastián Piñera, whom would later nominate him to the Ministry of Arts and Culture.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luciano_Cruz-Coke&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cruz-Coke has <a href="/wiki/Spanish_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish people">Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_people" title="French people">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basques" title="Basques">Basque</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danes" title="Danes">Danish</a> ancestry.
</p><p>Cruz-Coke is a practicing <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>.
</p><p>Cruz-Coke is married to Javiera García-Huidobro Aninat and is the father of five children.
</p>
