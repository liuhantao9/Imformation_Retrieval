<p><b>Gordon Fitzgerald Kaye</b> (7 April 1941&#160;&#8211;&#32;23 January 2017), known as <b>Gorden Kaye</b>, was an English actor and singer, best known for playing womanizing café owner <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Artois" title="René Artois">René Artois</a> in the British comedy series <i><a href="/wiki/%27Allo_%27Allo!" title="&#39;Allo &#39;Allo!">'Allo 'Allo!</a></i>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorden_Kaye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kaye was born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, an only child.<sup id="cite_ref-indy_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-indy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His mother, Gracie, was 42 when he was born.<sup id="cite_ref-examiner_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-examiner-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His father, Harold, was a lorry driver,<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/Air_Raid_Precautions" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Raid Precautions">ARP</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, and at other times worked as an engineer in a tractor factory.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When young, Kaye played <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> for <a href="/wiki/Moldgreen" title="Moldgreen">Moldgreen</a> ARLFC before studying at <a href="/wiki/King_James%27s_Grammar_School,_Almondbury" class="mw-redirect" title="King James&#39;s Grammar School, Almondbury">King James's Grammar School, Almondbury</a>, Huddersfield.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He worked in <a href="/wiki/Hospital_radio" title="Hospital radio">hospital radio</a> in Huddersfield (interviewing <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">the Beatles</a> in 1965),<sup id="cite_ref-examiner_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-examiner-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and worked in textile mills, a wine factory,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (September 2017)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and a tractor factory.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorden_Kaye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:142px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gordon_Kaye.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Gordon_Kaye.jpg/140px-Gordon_Kaye.jpg" decoding="async" width="140" height="176" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Gordon_Kaye.jpg/210px-Gordon_Kaye.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Gordon_Kaye.jpg/280px-Gordon_Kaye.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2210" data-file-height="2773" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gordon_Kaye.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kaye in London, 1974</div></div></div>
<p>Kaye had appeared in a radio play directed by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Ayckbourn" title="Alan Ayckbourn">Alan Ayckbourn</a> and also in a television play from Manchester. Ayckbourn suggested that he audition for the <a href="/wiki/Octagon_Theatre,_Bolton" title="Octagon Theatre, Bolton">Bolton Octagon Theatre</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-examiner_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-examiner-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> he was offered a contract and his roles there included Pishchik in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cherry_Orchard" title="The Cherry Orchard">The Cherry Orchard</a></i> followed by roles in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Homecoming" title="The Homecoming">The Homecoming</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Imaginary_Invalid" title="The Imaginary Invalid">The Imaginary Invalid</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Luther_(play)" title="Luther (play)">Luther</a>,</i> and a double-bill of <i><a href="/wiki/Oedipus_Rex" title="Oedipus Rex">Oedipus</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Cyclops_(play)" title="Cyclops (play)">Cyclops</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-TheBoltonNews_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheBoltonNews-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He made his TV debut as a railway guard in the BBC's Yorkshire mill drama <i><a href="/wiki/Champion_House" title="Champion House">Champion House</a></i> (1968) and played small roles in such films as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Party%27s_Over_(1965_film)" title="The Party&#39;s Over (1965 film)">The Party's Over</a></i> (1965), starring <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Reed" title="Oliver Reed">Oliver Reed</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Having been seen by <a href="/wiki/Pat_Phoenix" title="Pat Phoenix">Pat Phoenix</a> in <i>Little Malcolm</i> by <a href="/wiki/David_Halliwell" title="David Halliwell">David Halliwell</a> at Bolton, he was cast as Bernard Butler, the nephew of <a href="/wiki/Elsie_Tanner" title="Elsie Tanner">Elsie Tanner</a> (Phoenix), in the soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Coronation_Street" title="Coronation Street">Coronation Street</a></i> in 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He later made an impression on producer/writer <a href="/wiki/David_Croft_(TV_producer)" title="David Croft (TV producer)">David Croft</a> following guest roles in <i><a href="/wiki/It_Ain%27t_Half_Hot_Mum" title="It Ain&#39;t Half Hot Mum">It Ain't Half Hot Mum</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Come_Back_Mrs._Noah" title="Come Back Mrs. Noah">Come Back Mrs. Noah</a></i>. 
</p><p>He appeared in the 1978 comedy short <i>The Waterloo Bridge Handicap</i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Rossiter" title="Leonard Rossiter">Leonard Rossiter</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and featured as Dines in the feature film version of <i><a href="/wiki/Porridge_(film)" title="Porridge (film)">Porridge</a></i> (1979) alongside <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Barker" title="Ronnie Barker">Ronnie Barker</a>. He also appeared in the drama series <i><a href="/wiki/All_Creatures_Great_and_Small_(TV_series)" title="All Creatures Great and Small (TV series)">All Creatures Great and Small</a></i> and in the private detective series <i><a href="/wiki/Shoestring_(TV_series)" title="Shoestring (TV series)">Shoestring</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 1981, Kaye appeared as Frank Broadhurst in the children's drama serial <i><a href="/wiki/Codename_Icarus" title="Codename Icarus">Codename Icarus</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kaye appeared in three episodes of Croft's British department store sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Are_You_Being_Served%3F" title="Are You Being Served?">Are You Being Served?</a></i> and was later offered the lead role in a series he had written called <i><a href="/wiki/Oh_Happy_Band!" title="Oh Happy Band!">Oh Happy Band!</a></i>, but Kaye was unavailable and the part went to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Worth" title="Harry Worth">Harry Worth</a>. <i>Oh Happy Band!</i> lasted one series.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Kaye had a small part in <a href="/wiki/Terry_Gilliam" title="Terry Gilliam">Terry Gilliam</a>'s film <i><a href="/wiki/Brazil_(1985_film)" title="Brazil (1985 film)">Brazil</a></i> as desk clerk M.O.I. Lobby Porter and appeared in Gilliam's 1977 film <i><a href="/wiki/Jabberwocky_(film)" title="Jabberwocky (film)">Jabberwocky</a></i> as Sister Jessica.<sup id="cite_ref-gazette_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gazette-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He played Dr Grant in a television adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Mansfield_Park_(1983_TV_serial)" title="Mansfield Park (1983 TV serial)">Mansfield Park</a></i> and Lymoges, Duke of Austria in the 1984 <a href="/wiki/BBC_Television_Shakespeare#The_Life_and_Death_of_King_John" title="BBC Television Shakespeare">BBC production of <i>King John</i></a> by Shakespeare.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He also toured in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">National Theatre</a> production of <i><a href="/wiki/As_You_Like_It" title="As You Like It">As You Like It</a></i>, as Touchstone.<sup id="cite_ref-gazette_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gazette-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>Also in 1984 he appeared as hard man Sammy, an <a href="/wiki/Mob_enforcer" title="Mob enforcer">enforcer</a> employed by <a href="/wiki/Agoraphobia" title="Agoraphobia">agoraphobic</a> <a href="/wiki/Bookmaker" title="Bookmaker">bookmaker</a> Albert Wendle in the <i><a href="/wiki/Minder_(TV_series)" title="Minder (TV series)">Minder</a></i> episode <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_Minder_episodes#Series_4" title="List of Minder episodes">Get Daley!</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1990, Kaye played the fictional local television presenter Maynard Lavery in an edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Last_of_the_Summer_Wine" title="Last of the Summer Wine">Last of the Summer Wine</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
In the early 1990s he made a guest appearance in a Christmas special of <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Fortunes" title="Family Fortunes">Family Fortunes</a></i>, in which he served as team captain and placed host <a href="/wiki/Les_Dennis" title="Les Dennis">Les Dennis</a> on a special "Double Big Money" round for Dennis to score more than one hundred points to double the charity prize money, which he did.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id=".27Allo_.27Allo.21"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="'Allo_'Allo!">'<i>Allo 'Allo!</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorden_Kaye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: &#039;Allo &#039;Allo!">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1982, David Croft sent Kaye the script for the pilot episode of <i><a href="/wiki/%27Allo_%27Allo!" title="&#39;Allo &#39;Allo!">'Allo 'Allo!</a></i> inviting him to play the central character of <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Artois" title="René Artois">René Artois</a>. He accepted and appeared in all 84 episodes (the main series ran from 1984, two years after the pilot, until 1992) and 1,200 performances of the stage version.<sup id="cite_ref-Examiner_lifetime_award_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Examiner_lifetime_award-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kaye was the subject of <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_Your_Life_(UK_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="This Is Your Life (UK TV series)">This Is Your Life</a></i> in 1986 when he was surprised by <a href="/wiki/Eamonn_Andrews" title="Eamonn Andrews">Eamonn Andrews</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Curtain_call" title="Curtain call">curtain call</a> of the West End stage version of '<i>Allo 'Allo!</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales_Theatre" title="Prince of Wales Theatre">Prince of Wales Theatre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kaye returned as Rene Artois in a 2007 one-off television <a href="/wiki/The_Return_of_%27Allo_%27Allo!" title="The Return of &#39;Allo &#39;Allo!">revival</a> of '<i>Allo 'Allo!</i> and in a stage show in <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, at the <a href="/wiki/Twelfth_Night_Theatre" title="Twelfth Night Theatre">Twelfth Night Theatre</a> in June and July, alongside <a href="/wiki/Sue_Hodge" title="Sue Hodge">Sue Hodge</a> as <a href="/wiki/Mimi_Labonq" class="mw-redirect" title="Mimi Labonq">Mimi Labonq</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guy_Siner" title="Guy Siner">Guy Siner</a> as <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Gruber" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant Gruber">Lieutenant Gruber</a>. The other characters were portrayed by Australian actors, including <a href="/wiki/Katy_Manning" title="Katy Manning">Katy Manning</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steven_Tandy" title="Steven Tandy">Steven Tandy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chloe_Dallimore" title="Chloe Dallimore">Chloe Dallimore</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jason_Gann" title="Jason Gann">Jason Gann</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorden_Kaye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kaye's autobiography, <i>René and Me: An Autobiography</i> (co-written with <a href="/wiki/Hilary_Bonner" title="Hilary Bonner">Hilary Bonner</a>), was published in 1989. In the book, he described his experiences as a shy, gay, overweight, typecast youth.<sup id="cite_ref-biography_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-biography-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The unusual spelling of his name (usually spelt "Gordon") was the result of a <a href="/wiki/British_Actors%27_Equity_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="British Actors&#39; Equity Association">British Actors' Equity Association</a> typing error.<sup id="cite_ref-biography_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-biography-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kaye suffered serious head injuries in a car accident whilst driving his Honda CRX, during the <a href="/wiki/Burns%27_Day_storm" class="mw-redirect" title="Burns&#39; Day storm">Burns' Day storm</a> on 25 January 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Although he could not remember any details of the incident, he retained a scar on his forehead from a piece of wooden advertising hoarding that had smashed through the car windscreen. Writing in his memoirs, <i><a href="/wiki/%27Allo_%27Allo!" title="&#39;Allo &#39;Allo!">'Allo 'Allo!</a></i> co-writer <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Lloyd" title="Jeremy Lloyd">Jeremy Lloyd</a> said he visited Kaye in hospital, adding, "I believe part of his recovery was due to his agent getting a video and showing reruns of '<i>Allo 'Allo!</i> to remind him who he was."<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> While recovering in hospital from emergency brain surgery to treat injuries sustained in the accident, Kaye was photographed and interviewed by <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_Sport" title="Sunday Sport">Sunday Sport</a></i> journalist Roger Ordish. He sued the newspaper, but the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Appeal_of_England_and_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="Court of Appeal of England and Wales">Court of Appeal</a> held, in <i><a href="/wiki/Kaye_v_Robertson" title="Kaye v Robertson">Kaye v Robertson</a></i>, that there was no remedy in <a href="/wiki/English_law" title="English law">English law</a> for an invasion of privacy.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorden_Kaye&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kaye died in a care home on 23 January 2017, at the age of 75 in <a href="/wiki/Knaresborough" title="Knaresborough">Knaresborough</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> His funeral was held at <a href="/wiki/St_Peter%27s_Church,_Huddersfield" title="St Peter&#39;s Church, Huddersfield">Huddersfield Parish Church</a> on 17 February 2017. His co-stars from '<i>Allo 'Allo!</i> <a href="/wiki/Vicki_Michelle" title="Vicki Michelle">Vicki Michelle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sue_Hodge" title="Sue Hodge">Sue Hodge</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Hartman" title="Kim Hartman">Kim Hartman</a> attended, as did <a href="/wiki/Ken_Morley" title="Ken Morley">Ken Morley</a> from <i>Coronation Street</i>, who gave a tribute. The unofficial Yorkshire anthem "<a href="/wiki/On_Ilkla_Moor_Baht_%27at" title="On Ilkla Moor Baht &#39;at">On Ilkla Moor Baht 'at</a>" was sung at the service.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
