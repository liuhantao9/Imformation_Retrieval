<p><b>Micheline Anne-Marie Calmy-Rey</b> (born 8 July 1945) is a Swiss politician. She was member of the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Federal_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Swiss Federal Council">Swiss Federal Council</a> and became Switzerland's <a href="/wiki/Foreign_minister" title="Foreign minister">foreign minister</a> as head of the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Department_of_Foreign_Affairs" title="Federal Department of Foreign Affairs">Federal Department of Foreign Affairs</a> from 2003 to 2011. She was Vice President of the Confederation in 2006 and 2010 and President in 2007 and 2011. She resigned her office as member of the Federal Council on 31 December 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micheline_Calmy-Rey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Calmy-Rey was born in <a href="/wiki/Sion,_Switzerland" title="Sion, Switzerland">Sion</a> in the canton of <a href="/wiki/Valais" class="mw-redirect" title="Valais">Valais</a> on 8 July 1945 to Charles and Adeline Rey. She received her diploma in 1963 in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Maurice,_Valais" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint-Maurice, Valais">St. Maurice</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Licence" class="mw-redirect" title="Licence">Licence</a> degree in  political science at the <a href="/wiki/Graduate_Institute_of_International_Studies" class="mw-redirect" title="Graduate Institute of International Studies">Graduate Institute of International Studies</a>, Geneva, in 1968. While studying she married André Calmy, and they have two children.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micheline_Calmy-Rey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Calmy-Rey established a small enterprise in the book distribution business. From 1981 to 1997 Calmy-Rey served as a representative in the Grand Conseil of the <a href="/wiki/Canton_of_Geneva" title="Canton of Geneva">canton of Geneva</a> as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Switzerland" title="Social Democratic Party of Switzerland">Social Democratic Party (PSS/SPS)</a>, and was president of the assembly during 1992–1993. She was president of the Geneva section of the party from 1986 to 1990 and again from 1993 to 1997. In 1997, Calmy-Rey was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Conseil_d%27Etat_(Switzerland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Conseil d&#39;Etat (Switzerland)">Conseil d'Etat</a> of Geneva. In 2001, she became head of the Finance Department and president of the Conseil d'Etat of the canton.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She was elected on 4 December 2002 to the Federal Council, heading the Federal Department of Foreign Affairs from 2003 to 2011. Calmy-Rey is the fourth woman elected to the Federal Council in history. She was also elected Vice-President of Switzerland, a post she held for the calendar year of 2006 and the calendar year of 2010.
</p><p>Calmy-Rey supports Switzerland joining the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> and she is an Eminent Member of the <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Vieira_de_Mello_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="Sergio Vieira de Mello Foundation">Sergio Vieira de Mello Foundation</a>. The project <a href="/wiki/SAFFA#Saffa_2020" class="mw-redirect" title="SAFFA"><i>SAFFA 2020</i></a> is also under her patronage and of the councillors (<i>Bundesrat</i>) <a href="/wiki/Doris_Leuthard" title="Doris Leuthard">Doris Leuthard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simonetta_Sommaruga" title="Simonetta Sommaruga">Simonetta Sommaruga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eveline_Widmer-Schlumpf" title="Eveline Widmer-Schlumpf">Eveline Widmer-Schlumpf</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2020-patronat_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2020-patronat-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2016, Calmy-Rey has been a Member of the <a href="/wiki/Global_Leadership_Foundation" title="Global Leadership Foundation">Global Leadership Foundation</a> (chaired by <a href="/wiki/FW_de_Klerk" class="mw-redirect" title="FW de Klerk">FW de Klerk</a>), an organisation that works to support democratic leadership, prevent and resolve conflict through mediation and promote good governance in the form of democratic institutions, open markets, human rights and the rule of law. It does so by making available, discreetly and in confidence, the experience of former leaders to today’s national leaders. It is a not-for-profit organisation composed of former heads of government, senior governmental and international organization officials who work closely with heads of government on governance-related issues of concern to them.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Presidency">Presidency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micheline_Calmy-Rey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Presidency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 1 January 2007, Calmy-Rey became the second female President of the Confederation in history, the first having been her predecessor on the Federal Council, <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Dreifuss" title="Ruth Dreifuss">Ruth Dreifuss</a>. She was elected as President on 13 December 2006 by 147 votes.  However, by Swiss tradition, it was a foregone conclusion she would be elected. She had been the longest-serving councillor not to have been President, and had served as Vice-President for 2006.
</p><p>As President of the Confederation, she presided over meetings of the Federal Council and carried out certain representative functions that would normally be handled by a <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> in other democracies, (though in Switzerland, the Federal Council as a whole is regarded as the head of state).  She was also the highest-ranking official in the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_order_of_precedence" title="Swiss order of precedence">Swiss order of precedence</a>, and had the power to act on behalf of the whole Council in emergency situations.  However, in most cases she was merely <i><a href="/wiki/Prima_inter_pares" class="mw-redirect" title="Prima inter pares">prima inter pares</a></i>, with no power above and beyond her six colleagues.
</p><p>She had already handled most official visits abroad since being elected to the Federal Council; the head of the Department of Foreign Affairs traditionally carries out such visits.
</p><p>Calmy-Rey was chosen as vice president for 2010, serving alongside <a href="/wiki/Doris_Leuthard" title="Doris Leuthard">Doris Leuthard</a>. On 8 December 2010, she was chosen, for the second time, as President for 2011 (by 106 votes out of 189, this being the smallest margin in Swiss history) – the first time two women held the post in succession.
</p>

<p>Calmy-Rey is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Women_World_Leaders" title="Council of Women World Leaders">Council of Women World Leaders</a>, an International network of current and former women presidents and prime ministers whose mission is to mobilize the highest-level women leaders globally for collective action on issues of critical importance to women and equitable development.
</p><p>She announced in September 2011 that she would resign from the government in the following December.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_after_politics">Life after politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micheline_Calmy-Rey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Life after politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 2012 to 2013, Calmy-Rey was a member of the High-Level Advisory Council of the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="President of the United Nations General Assembly">President</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sixty-seventh_session_of_the_United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="Sixty-seventh session of the United Nations General Assembly">67th Session of the United Nations General Assembly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>'s then foreign minister <a href="/wiki/Vuk_Jeremi%C4%87" title="Vuk Jeremić">Vuk Jeremić</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micheline_Calmy-Rey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012, Micheline Calmy-Rey was awarded with the Medal of Honor of the Republic of Armenia.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micheline_Calmy-Rey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, Calmy-Rey was "widely criticised" for putting on a headscarf to meet Iranian President <a href="/wiki/Mahmoud_Ahmadinejad" title="Mahmoud Ahmadinejad">Mahmoud Ahmadinejad</a> on 19 March 2008, even though it was acknowledged that she was simply following proper social protocol.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Her appearance together with the Iranian president sparked a negative reaction from Socialist MP <a href="/wiki/Maria_Roth-Bernasconi" title="Maria Roth-Bernasconi">Maria Roth-Bernasconi</a>. In response, Calmy-Rey stated that she was following protocol out of respect, and that had she not done so, she would not have been able to attend the meeting, which has been widely acknowledged.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Calmy-Rey also prompted controversy by attending the signing of a multibillion-dollar <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">natural gas</a> deal of a Swiss energy supply company with Iran.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The United States had complained that Switzerland was sending the wrong message when Tehran was subject to UN sanctions. Calmy-Rey pointed out that gas exports were not subject to the UN sanctions. Both the <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israeli</a> government and international Jewish groups such as the <a href="/wiki/World_Jewish_Congress" title="World Jewish Congress">World Jewish Congress</a> strongly criticised the deal.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
