<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming_convention">Naming convention</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sindhi_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Naming convention">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most Sindhi family names are a modified form of a <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> and typically end with the suffix -ani, which is used to denote descent from a common male ancestor. One explanation states that the -ani suffix is a <a href="/wiki/Sindhi_language" title="Sindhi language">Sindhi</a> variant of 'anshi', derived from the <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a> word 'ansh', which means 'descended from'. The first part of a Sindhi surname is usually derived from the name or location of an ancestor. In northern Sindh, surnames ending in 'ja' (meaning 'of') are also common. A person's surname would consist of the name of his or her native village, followed by 'ja'. Sindhis generally add the suffix ‘ani’ to the name of a great grandfather and adopt the name as a family name.there many tribe belong to sindh like jokhio. <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_Jokhio_tribe">History of Jokhio tribe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sindhi_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History of Jokhio tribe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The existence of <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a> tribe witnessed in early 12th <a href="/wiki/Century" title="Century">century</a>, but history revealed their powerful existence in 13th century when they become the major part of ruling struggle between then rulers <a href="/wiki/King" title="King">King</a> Dari vs. <a href="/wiki/Kalhora" title="Kalhora">Kalhora</a>, later throughout the era of rule of Mirs (1800). The <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a> tribe is one of original tribe of Sindh and exists through centuries. <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a> tribe supposed to migrate from Hangore (Blochistan) to Sindh particularly in the vicinity of Shah Norani Toms towards north side). 800 years old historical “Chukhandi” <a href="/wiki/Graveyard" class="mw-redirect" title="Graveyard">graveyard</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a> tribe on national highway proved that this tribe is one oldest one in the <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">province</a> of Sindh after “Mirbahes”. Among the many converts to <a href="/wiki/Mahomedanism" class="mw-redirect" title="Mahomedanism">Mahomedanism</a> is <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">Sindh</a> were the Semma, or Soomera family or tribe of <a href="/wiki/Rajpoots" class="mw-redirect" title="Rajpoots">Rajpoots</a>, whose princes are mentioned in the Introductory Sketch of the History of <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">Sindh</a>. They were Jams of Tattah, and reigned until deposed by the Mahomedans, having, during their incumbency, embraced Mahomedanism. This branch of the family remained in <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">Sindh</a>, while the Hindoo branch is represented at present by the chiefs of Cutch, who bear the title of Jam. Of the Sindh Jams the head is (or was) Mihr Ali, an unquiet old man of seventy, who possess large estates in land, of which he has relinquished some portions paid from the treasury .The estate is badly managed and yields comparatively little revenue. The Jokhyas represented belong to the police, and are armed in the usual manner. It will be seen at a glance how widely their features differ from those of real Mahmedan tribes, how they utterly lack the comeliness and dignity of the Belochees. It is very probable that they intermarry in their own tribe exclusively, and thus the ancient Hindoo physiognomy, in this case a remarkably ugly type, has been preserved. <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a> tribe always has played tremendous role in political, social and economical uplift of the area in the larger interest of local community. This effort kept up their separate identity of Jokhio tribe either at local, provincial or national level. This tribe always maintained their status of independent, loyalty and patriotism. This brave and enough <a href="/wiki/Populated" class="mw-redirect" title="Populated">populated</a> <a href="/wiki/Tribe" title="Tribe">tribe</a> set some peaceful and positive examples. Since the independence of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> many Governments sworn in but <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a> <a href="/wiki/Tribe" title="Tribe">tribe</a> have uphold their standing of neutrality .these <b>government</b> either political or non-political due to their community strength always considered <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a> <a href="/wiki/Tribe" title="Tribe">tribe</a> by them and listed in the tribes, who supported and worked for progress, prosperity, peace and security of Pakistan . COMMUNITY INFORMATION: Leading to 0.500 million Jokhio tribesman living throughout Sindh province. Out of them 0.25 million living in rural part of Karachi viz Malir and Karachi west. Also recognized and followed by other communities like Memon, Khaskhali, Sarhandi, Jamote and Mirbehar of Thatta, Malir, Gadap town Karachi .About0125 million peoples belongs to these communities reside in Malir district. There are about 370 plus villages of Jokhio tribesmen existing in the province. Out of them about 325 exist in district Thatta, Malir and Karachi west where 0.4 million population live. The remaining situated in the interior Sindh where about 0.1 million populations reside. About 50% of the population of rural Karachi is Jokhio tribesmen. Social Background: Besides being a chief of Jokhio Supreme Council <b>Jam Bijar Khan</b> supporting to Tanzeem-e-Itehad Jokhia Sindh and other NGO’s of <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a> Community working in <a href="/wiki/Thatta" title="Thatta">Thatta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malir" class="mw-redirect" title="Malir">Malir</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a> west district. Supporting to NGO’s of other then <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a> community engaged of 4r uplift of their community. Political Background Became elected MPA of Sindh in 1985 in non party elections from district <a href="/wiki/Thatta" title="Thatta">Thatta</a>. Supported to then MNA Haji Usman Jalbani who was elected MNA in 1985 from <a href="/wiki/District" title="District">district</a> <a href="/wiki/Thatta" title="Thatta">Thatta</a>. Social and political support was continued in all forthcoming elections held after 1985 for provincial, national and local bodies to the dignities, tribesmen etc. This support leaded the Jokhio tribesmen in the election of local bodies held in the past and succeeds to hold the office of <a href="/wiki/District" title="District">district</a> council Karachi by six members, one member in ZMC East, one member in district council <a href="/wiki/Thatta" title="Thatta">Thatta</a>. Whereas chairmanship of three union councils of district <a href="/wiki/Thatta" title="Thatta">Thatta</a>, two union councils of district <a href="/wiki/Malir" class="mw-redirect" title="Malir">Malir</a> and one union council of <a href="/wiki/District" title="District">district</a> west remained with <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a> tribesmen. There were also many councilors in the councils of <a href="/wiki/Thatta" title="Thatta">Thatta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">Karachi</a> <a href="/wiki/Rural" class="mw-redirect" title="Rural">rural</a>. Current Elected Representative of <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a> tribe’s new local Govt sestem2001. Nazim and Naib-Nazim three union councils are Bin Qasim Town viz Rehri and Gaghar about 60 councilors of various categories. Nazim and Naib-Nazim of four union councils are Gadap Town viz Murad <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mamen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mamen (page does not exist)">Mamen</a> uc Darsano chhano uc, Songal uc, <a href="/wiki/Gadap" title="Gadap">Gadap</a> uc and Gajro uc about 120 councilors of various categories <a href="/wiki/Jokhio" class="mw-redirect" title="Jokhio">Jokhio</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jokhia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jokhia (page does not exist)">Jokhia</a> or Jokhiya (Urdu:جوکھيو) is the Samma tribe settled in <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">Sindh</a> and Balochistan, Pakistan.
