<p><b>Sir Walter Parratt</b> <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Knight_Commander_of_the_Royal_Victorian_Order" class="mw-redirect" title="Knight Commander of the Royal Victorian Order">KCVO</a></span> (10 February 1841&#160;&#8211;&#32;27 March 1924) was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Organist" title="Organist">organist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Composer" title="Composer">composer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Walter_Parratt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, son of a parish organist, Parratt began to play the <a href="/wiki/Pipe_organ" title="Pipe organ">pipe organ</a> from an early age, and held posts as an organist while still a child.  He was child prodigy: on one occasion he played <a href="/wiki/Johann_Sebastian_Bach" title="Johann Sebastian Bach">Bach</a>'s complete <i><a href="/wiki/The_Well-Tempered_Clavier" title="The Well-Tempered Clavier">The Well-Tempered Clavier</a></i> by heart, without notice, at age ten.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1854 to 1861 he was an organist at St Paul's Church in his native town and, as successor to <a href="/wiki/John_Stainer" title="John Stainer">John Stainer</a>, in 1872 at <a href="/wiki/Magdalen_College,_Oxford" title="Magdalen College, Oxford">Magdalen College, Oxford</a>, where he remained for ten years. From 1882 he held the post of organist of <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s_Chapel,_Windsor_Castle" class="mw-redirect" title="St. George&#39;s Chapel, Windsor Castle">St. George's Chapel, Windsor Castle</a>. He became <a href="/wiki/Heather_Professor_of_Music" title="Heather Professor of Music">Heather Professor of Music</a> at <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxford University">Oxford University</a> in 1908, taking over from <a href="/wiki/Hubert_Parry" title="Hubert Parry">Hubert Parry</a>.
</p><p>He became one of the foremost organ teachers of his day, with many important posts in Britain being filled by his students. He was president of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_Organists" title="Royal College of Organists">Royal College of Organists</a>.
</p><p>Parratt was also a distinguished <a href="/wiki/Chess" title="Chess">chess</a> player, and was able to simultaneously play chess and a complex organ piece—at first sight.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He served for a few months as president of the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Chess_Club" title="Oxford University Chess Club">Oxford University Chess Club</a> and for two years was captain of the eight chosen to play against Cambridge.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Walter_Parratt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was <a href="/wiki/Knight" title="Knight">knighted</a> in 1892.  In 1893 he was appointed <a href="/wiki/Master_of_the_Queen%27s_Music" title="Master of the Queen&#39;s Music">Master of the Queen's Musick</a> to <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>, and afterward held the same office under Kings <a href="/wiki/Edward_VII_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward VII of the United Kingdom">Edward VII</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/George_V_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="George V of the United Kingdom">George V</a>.
</p><p>Later honours included: Member (MVO, 1901), Commander (CVO, 1917), and Knight Commander (KCVO, 1921) of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Victorian_Order" title="Royal Victorian Order">Royal Victorian Order</a>.
</p><p>After Parratt's death in 1924 a monument to him was erected in the grounds of Huddersfield Parish Church. There is also a monument to him in St George's Chapel, Windsor Castle, next to the entrance to King George VI Memorial Chapel where King <a href="/wiki/George_VI" title="George VI">George VI</a> and the Queen mother are buried.
</p>
