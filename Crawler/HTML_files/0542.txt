<p><b>Newsome</b> is a village situated approximately 1 mile south of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. It is in the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Metropolitan Borough of Kirklees</a>. The village lies at the centre of <a href="/wiki/Newsome_(ward)" title="Newsome (ward)">Newsome Ward</a>  to which it gives its name.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newsome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Newsome stands on a shoulder of land below <a href="/wiki/Castle_Hill,_Huddersfield" title="Castle Hill, Huddersfield">Castle Hill</a> and above the valley where the <a href="/wiki/River_Holme" title="River Holme">River Holme</a> meets the <a href="/wiki/River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Colne, West Yorkshire">River Colne</a>. The village lies between <a href="/wiki/Berry_Brow" title="Berry Brow">Berry Brow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hall_Bower" title="Hall Bower">Hall Bower</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Hill,_Huddersfield" title="Taylor Hill, Huddersfield">Taylor Hill</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newsome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village has both housing and industrial units, the latter mostly within the site of the former Newsome Mill, which used to produce <a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">woollen</a> <a href="/wiki/Textile" title="Textile">textiles</a>.
</p><p>Newsome used to have a large bakehouse, (which made award winning pork pies), but these industrial units, (across from, now damaged, Newsome Mill), now hold businesses. The <i><a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Examiner" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Examiner">Huddersfield Examiner</a></i> announced in February 2013 the closure to the Huddersfield shops, as well as the bakehouse.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Schools">Schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newsome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There is a secondary school, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.newsomehigh.co.uk/">Newsome High School</a> to the south west of the village centre.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The school's assistant head teacher was named West Yorkshire Teacher of the Year 2006 for an award sponsored by Pulse Radio.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian</a> infant and nursery school 'Stile Common Infant and Nursery School', close to the village centre, was closed down and replaced by newly built Hillside Primary School.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> A specialist school, Castle Hill school, for children aged 3 – 19, with Severe Learning Difficulties and Profound and Multiple Learning Disabilities<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> is located to the south west of the village, by Berry Brow, to support children from within the Huddersfield <a href="/wiki/Catchment_area_(human_geography)" class="mw-redirect" title="Catchment area (human geography)">catchment area</a>. The <i><a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Examiner" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Examiner">Huddersfield Examiner</a></i> announced on 11 July 2009 that the specialist school in Newsome had one confirmed case of <a href="/wiki/Swine_influenza" title="Swine influenza">swine flu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newsome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Newsome FC are an open age amateur <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club. The club play their home fixtures at the former Primrose Hill Cricket Ground in Primrose Hill now called the Westgate Barbers Arena. They field teams in the Premier Division of the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Association_Football_League" title="West Yorkshire Association Football League">West Yorkshire Association Football League</a> and Division One of the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_and_District_Association_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield and District Association League">Huddersfield and District Association League</a> and are affiliated to the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_County_Football_Association" title="West Riding County Football Association">West Riding County Football Association</a>. The  third team are Champions of HDAFL Reserve Division 1 and holders of the Richardson Cup.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Newsome_Panthers" title="Newsome Panthers">Newsome Panthers</a>, an amateur <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> club, are based here.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The club fields ten junior teams and an open age team in several leagues operated by <a href="/wiki/British_Amateur_Rugby_League_Association" title="British Amateur Rugby League Association">British Amateur Rugby League Association</a>. The club plays their home games at the secondary school and the committee meets in one of the village's public houses, The Fountain Inn.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newsome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The nearest Mainline railway station is in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield</a>. <a href="/wiki/Berry_Brow_railway_station" title="Berry Brow railway station">Berry Brow</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Penistone_Line" title="Penistone Line">Penistone Line</a> is the closest local railway station. There are several bus service routes, run by <a href="/wiki/First_Calderdale_%26_Huddersfield" title="First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield">First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger#Tiger_Blue" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Tiger Blue</a>, through the village.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
