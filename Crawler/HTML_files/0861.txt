	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Sally Wainwright</h1>

<p><b>Sally A Wainwright</b> (born 1963)<sup id="cite_ref-FamilySearch-EnglandWalesBirth-1963_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FamilySearch-EnglandWalesBirth-1963-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an English television writer, producer, and director from <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SquareChapel-HappyValley-S1-2015_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SquareChapel-HappyValley-S1-2015-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Guardian-EdinburghTVAward-2016_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-EdinburghTVAward-2016-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Early in her career Wainwright worked as a playwright, and as a scriptwriter on the long-running radio serial drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Archers" title="The Archers">The Archers</a></i>. In the 1990s Wainwright began her television career and in 2000 created her first original drama series <i><a href="/wiki/At_Home_with_the_Braithwaites" title="At Home with the Braithwaites">At Home with the Braithwaites</a></i> (2000–2003).
</p><p>She won the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Television_Society" title="Royal Television Society">Royal Television Society</a>'s Writer of the Year Award for the 2009 mini-series <a href="/wiki/Unforgiven_(TV_series)" title="Unforgiven (TV series)"><i>Unforgiven</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RTS-Unforgiven-2009_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RTS-Unforgiven-2009-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Wainwright is known for her creation of the <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Scott_%26_Bailey" title="Scott &amp; Bailey">Scott &amp; Bailey</a></i> (2011–2016<i>), <a href="/wiki/Last_Tango_in_Halifax" title="Last Tango in Halifax">Last Tango in Halifax</a></i> (2012–2016), and <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_Valley_(TV_series)" title="Happy Valley (TV series)">Happy Valley</a></i> (2014–present).<sup id="cite_ref-Independent-HValley-2014_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-HValley-2014-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WSJ-HValley-2014_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-HValley-2014-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <i>Last Tango in Halifax</i> won the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_Television_Award_for_Best_Drama_Series" title="British Academy Television Award for Best Drama Series">British Academy Television Award for Best Drama Series</a> in 2013, whilst <i>Happy Valley</i> won the same award in both 2015 and 2017.
</p><p>Sally is the creator of the 2019 <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> and <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> television series <i><a href="/wiki/Gentleman_Jack_(TV_series)" title="Gentleman Jack (TV series)">Gentleman Jack</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Suranne_Jones" title="Suranne Jones">Suranne Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sophie_Rundle" title="Sophie Rundle">Sophie Rundle</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sally_Wainwright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wainwright was born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, to Harry Wainwright and Dorothy Wainwright (née Crowther).<sup id="cite_ref-BBC-LastTango-S1-2012_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-LastTango-S1-2012-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Wainwright was brought up in <a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge" title="Sowerby Bridge">Sowerby Bridge</a> where she attended Triangle C of E Primary School and <a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge_High_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Sowerby Bridge High School">Sowerby Bridge High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-YorkshirePost-ScottBailey-2011_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YorkshirePost-ScottBailey-2011-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> She attended the <a href="/wiki/University_of_York" title="University of York">University of York</a>, reading English and Related Literature.<sup id="cite_ref-WainwrightTT_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WainwrightTT-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She has an older sister Diane Hilton, a housing professional with The Guinness Partnership.
</p><p>Wainwright said that she had always wanted to write, and had started writing when she was young, from the time she was 9 years old, and wanted to write for <i><a href="/wiki/Coronation_Street" title="Coronation Street">Coronation Street</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-BBC-WritersRoom-2012_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-WritersRoom-2012-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She said that when she was 16 years old in 1980 she saw a play called <i>Bastard Angel</i> by playwright <a href="/wiki/Barrie_Keeffe" title="Barrie Keeffe">Barrie Keeffe</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Shakespeare_Company" title="Royal Shakespeare Company">Royal Shakespeare Company</a> and was deeply interested in the short sentences and naturalistic approach to dialogue.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC-WritersRoom-LastTangoS1-2015_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-WritersRoom-LastTangoS1-2015-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sally_Wainwright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_York" title="University of York">University of York</a>, Wainwright took an original play called <i>Hanging On</i> to the <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Edinburgh Festival">Edinburgh Festival</a> and found an agent, Meg Davis, for her writing in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-WainwrightTT_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WainwrightTT-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, she worked as a bus driver.<sup id="cite_ref-WainwrightTT_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WainwrightTT-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> When she was 24, she left the driving job after she started writing for the Radio 4 series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Archers" title="The Archers">The Archers</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-BBC-WritersRoom-2012_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-WritersRoom-2012-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> One of her contributions was to write an atypical story for the long running radio soap in which the village shop was robbed. After that she wrote for <i><a href="/wiki/Coronation_Street" title="Coronation Street">Coronation Street</a></i>, developing her writing skills, from 1994 to 1999. She has since said that working on continuing drama was a great education in discipline and a lesson that great stories are hard work.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> She was mentored by <a href="/wiki/Kay_Mellor" title="Kay Mellor">Kay Mellor</a> who encouraged her to stop writing for soaps and to concentrate instead on original work. She created the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/At_Home_with_the_Braithwaites" title="At Home with the Braithwaites">At Home with the Braithwaites</a></i> about a woman who had secretly won the lottery. The programme was nominated for many awards.<sup id="cite_ref-did_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-did-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2006 she wrote the drama series <a href="/wiki/Jane_Hall_(TV_series)" title="Jane Hall (TV series)">Jane Hall</a>, which depicts the life of a female bus driver in London. Wainwright drew on her own experiences in scripting the series.<sup id="cite_ref-bus_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bus-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She won the 2009 Writer of the Year Award given by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Television_Society" title="Royal Television Society">RTS</a> in 2009 for <a href="/wiki/Unforgiven_(TV_series)" title="Unforgiven (TV series)"><i>Unforgiven</i></a> which took several awards including best TV series.<sup id="cite_ref-RTS-Unforgiven-2009_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RTS-Unforgiven-2009-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wainwright says that her strong female flawed characters are almost real to her and arrive fully formed in her imagination. She likes to control the television that is created and has done some directing and production of her own work<sup id="cite_ref-did_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-did-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> partly to ensure the scenery and dialogue reflects Yorkshire.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian-HappyValley-S1-2014_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-HappyValley-S1-2014-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011 she wrote <i><a href="/wiki/Scott_%26_Bailey" title="Scott &amp; Bailey">Scott &amp; Bailey</a></i>, a series about two female police officers. The idea for the series came from the actresses <a href="/wiki/Suranne_Jones" title="Suranne Jones">Suranne Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sally_Lindsay" title="Sally Lindsay">Sally Lindsay</a> and former Detective Inspector Diane Taylor, who assisted with bringing the series to the air.<sup id="cite_ref-cop_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cop-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wainwright based the plot of her series <i><a href="/wiki/Last_Tango_in_Halifax" title="Last Tango in Halifax">Last Tango in Halifax</a></i> on the story of her mother who was widowed in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-WainwrightTT_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WainwrightTT-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Her mother, Dorothy, moved to Oxfordshire to live with her daughter and rediscovered a lost love, Alec Walker, via <a href="/wiki/Friends_Reunited" title="Friends Reunited">Friends Reunited</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WainwrightTT_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WainwrightTT-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> With her mother's permission, Wainwright developed the story of how she remarried so rapidly, publishing series extracts to her mother before broadcast.<sup id="cite_ref-did_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-did-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When she told the story to <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Shindler" title="Nicola Shindler">Nicola Shindler</a>, she suggested she turn her mother's experience into a television series. Shindler became the series' executive producer.<sup id="cite_ref-WainwrightDM_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WainwrightDM-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Both <i>Last Tango in Halifax</i> and her crime series <i>Scott &amp; Bailey</i> were turned down by both the BBC and ITV before both were accepted respectively. The former was voted by <a href="/wiki/BAFTA" class="mw-redirect" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a> to be best series in 2012 and Wainwright was given the award for best writer.<sup id="cite_ref-award_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-award-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Happy_Valley_(TV_series)" title="Happy Valley (TV series)">Happy Valley</a>,</i> which was shot in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>'s picturesque upper <a href="/wiki/Upper_Calder_Valley" title="Upper Calder Valley">Upper Calder Valley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Bridge" title="Hebden Bridge">Hebden Bridge</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Holdsworth-HValley-2014_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Holdsworth-HValley-2014-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HalifaxCourier-HValley-2013_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HalifaxCourier-HValley-2013-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> stars <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Lancashire" title="Sarah Lancashire">Sarah Lancashire</a>, whom Wainwright had in mind as she wrote the role.<sup id="cite_ref-WalesOnline-HValley-2014_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline-HValley-2014-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Wainwright made her directorial debut with episode 4 of the first series.<sup id="cite_ref-WalesOnline-HValley-2014_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WalesOnline-HValley-2014-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Wainwright has said that she is willing to write a third series of Happy Valley, but she has previous commitments to work on other projects. Producer Nicola Shindler has indicated that the third series would not air until 2018 at the earliest.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, Wainwright was made a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Television_Society" title="Royal Television Society">Royal Television Society</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wainwright wrote and directed a two-hour drama special for <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> entitled <i><a href="/wiki/To_Walk_Invisible" title="To Walk Invisible">To Walk Invisible: The Bronte Sisters</a>,</i> which aired on BBC One in 2016 and in the US in 2017. Its subject is the <a href="/wiki/Bront%C3%AB_family" title="Brontë family">Brontë family</a>, particularly the relationship the three sisters, <a href="/wiki/Anne_Bront%C3%AB" title="Anne Brontë">Anne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emily_Bront%C3%AB" title="Emily Brontë">Emily</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Bront%C3%AB" title="Charlotte Brontë">Charlotte</a>, had with their brother, <a href="/wiki/Branwell_Bront%C3%AB" title="Branwell Brontë">Branwell</a>. While working on the drama, Wainwright said "I am thrilled beyond measure that I've been asked by the BBC to bring to life these three fascinating, talented, ingenious Yorkshire women."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Wainwright's <i><a href="/wiki/Gentleman_Jack_(TV_series)" title="Gentleman Jack (TV series)">Gentleman Jack</a></i>, a drama about the 18th-century Yorkshire landowner, diarist, and open lesbian, <a href="/wiki/Anne_Lister" title="Anne Lister">Anne Lister</a>, premiered on both BBC One in the U.K. and HBO in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sally_Wainwright&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1990, Wainwright married Ralph "Austin" Sherlaw-Johnson, an antiquarian <a href="/wiki/Sheet_music" title="Sheet music">sheet music</a> dealer.<sup id="cite_ref-FamilySearch-EnglandWalesMarriage-1990_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FamilySearch-EnglandWalesMarriage-1990-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph-HappyValley-S2-2016_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-HappyValley-S2-2016-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> They have two sons and own two <a href="/wiki/Maine_Coon" title="Maine Coon">Maine Coon</a> cats.<sup id="cite_ref-did_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-did-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Guardian-HappyValley-S2-Titan-2016_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-HappyValley-S2-Titan-2016-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wainwright lives in <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a>.
</p>
