<p><b>Sports Direct International plc</b> is a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> retailing group. Established in 1982 by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Ashley_(businessman)" title="Mike Ashley (businessman)">Mike Ashley</a>, the company is the United Kingdom's largest sports-goods retailer and operates roughly 670 stores worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The company owns many sporting brands and trades predominantly under the SportsDirect.com brand. Other retailers owned by the company include <a href="/wiki/House_of_Fraser" title="House of Fraser">House of Fraser</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flannels_(retail)" title="Flannels (retail)">Flannels</a>, <a href="/wiki/USC_(clothing_retailer)" title="USC (clothing retailer)">USC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lillywhites" title="Lillywhites">Lillywhites</a>. The company operates under low margins.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sporting and fashion brands owned include <a href="/wiki/Donnay_Sports" class="mw-redirect" title="Donnay Sports">Donnay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slazenger" title="Slazenger">Slazenger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Firetrap" title="Firetrap">Firetrap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Everlast_(boxing)" title="Everlast (boxing)">Everlast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kangol" title="Kangol">Kangol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karrimor" title="Karrimor">Karrimor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lonsdale_(clothing)" title="Lonsdale (clothing)">Lonsdale</a>, among others.
</p><p>Mike Ashley has continued to hold a majority stake in the business, and his holding has been 61.7 percent since October 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It is listed on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a> and it is a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/FTSE_250_Index" title="FTSE 250 Index">FTSE 250 Index</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sports_Direct&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sports_Direct&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sports_Direct,_The_Core,_Leeds_(11th_April_2011).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Sports_Direct%2C_The_Core%2C_Leeds_%2811th_April_2011%29.jpg/220px-Sports_Direct%2C_The_Core%2C_Leeds_%2811th_April_2011%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Sports_Direct%2C_The_Core%2C_Leeds_%2811th_April_2011%29.jpg/330px-Sports_Direct%2C_The_Core%2C_Leeds_%2811th_April_2011%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Sports_Direct%2C_The_Core%2C_Leeds_%2811th_April_2011%29.jpg/440px-Sports_Direct%2C_The_Core%2C_Leeds_%2811th_April_2011%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2736" data-file-height="3648" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sports_Direct,_The_Core,_Leeds_(11th_April_2011).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sports Direct shop in 2011, at the former Lillywhite's site on <a href="/wiki/The_Headrow" title="The Headrow">the Headrow</a> in <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a></div></div></div>
<p>The company was founded by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Ashley_(businessman)" title="Mike Ashley (businessman)">Mike Ashley</a> in 1982 as a single store in <a href="/wiki/Maidenhead" title="Maidenhead">Maidenhead</a> trading under the name of <i>Mike Ashley Sports</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1984, Preston Sports shop was opened in London and by 1992 the company had 12 stores.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 1995/96 saw the stores change to Sports Soccer along with the re-location of head office and warehouse to a 100,000 sq. foot unit in <a href="/wiki/Dunstable" title="Dunstable">Dunstable</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The number of retail stores had now increased to 50, along with the acquisition of the Donnay tennis and golf brand.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Ashley incorporated the business in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-billion_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billion-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By 2000, the business had 80 stores, and had formed a joint venture in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, with 22 stores trading as Disport.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Lillywhites" title="Lillywhites">Lillywhites</a> chain of 10 stores and the Lonsdale brand were acquired in 2002 and by 2003, the number of retail stores had increased to 150.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-billion_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billion-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2005 the company began a major rebrand of the stores, changing the fascias to Sports World.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated3_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Going_public">Going public</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sports_Direct&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Going public">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late November 2006, a number of business newspapers reported that Ashley was looking at an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">IPO</a> of Sports World International. He hired <a href="/wiki/Merrill_Lynch" title="Merrill Lynch">Merrill Lynch</a>, who valued the group at up to £2.5bn ahead of a possible flotation on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The group debuted on the exchange on 27 February 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_finance_and_mergers">Corporate finance and mergers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sports_Direct&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Corporate finance and mergers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2006, it was revealed that Sports Direct had built up a 29.4% stake in <a href="/wiki/Blacks_Outdoor_Retail" title="Blacks Outdoor Retail">Blacks Leisure Group</a>, the owner of Millets.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In May 2007, it was also revealed that Ashley had held talks with John Hargreaves, founder of <a href="/wiki/Matalan" title="Matalan">Matalan</a> on both taking a 25% stake in the troubled retail business and installing mezzanine floors in larger Matalan stores, on which SportsDirect.com outlets could be operated.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In June 2007, the company acquired Everlast for £84&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2008, it was disclosed that Sports Direct also held a 12.3% holding in the John David Group, parent of <a href="/wiki/JD_Sports" title="JD Sports">JD Sports</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The stake is currently 11.9% of JD Sports as of November 2013. Sports Direct formerly held 5% of <a href="/wiki/Amer_Sports" title="Amer Sports">Amer Sports</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It was announced on 1 October 2012, that Sports Direct had purchased rival retailer JJB's brand name, website, 20 stores and all of their stock in a deal for approximately £24m. The deal saved around 550 jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2013, after fashion retailer <a href="/wiki/Republic_(retailer)" title="Republic (retailer)">Republic</a> went into administration, Sports Direct bought 116 Republic stores, the brand name and the company's head office from the administrator for an undisclosed sum.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In July 2013, more than 2,000 full-time staff were awarded around £70,000 each under the company's bonus share scheme.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On 13 January 2014, Sports Direct bought 4.6% of <a href="/wiki/Debenhams" title="Debenhams">Debenhams</a> shares. The stock market purchase of 56.8&#160;million shares (worth around £46m) was made without the prior knowledge of the Debenhams board. Sports Direct stated at the time it intended to be a supportive share holder. The Debenhams board responded by stating they were open-minded with regard to exploring operational opportunities to improve its performance.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Sports Direct sold its shares on 16 January 2014, although they took out an option to buy further shares up to a total of 6.6%.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2016, Sports Direct agreed to sell the remaining international rights to its Dunlop brand to <a href="/wiki/Sumitomo_Rubber_Industries" title="Sumitomo Rubber Industries">Sumitomo Rubber Industries</a> for £112 million ($137.5 million).<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Sumitomo already own the rights to the brand in Japan, South Korea and Taiwan. The sale is due to be completed by May 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In July 2017, the company acquired a 26% stake in <a href="/wiki/Game_(retailer)" title="Game (retailer)">Game Digital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Employee_conditions_and_legal_breaches">Employee conditions and legal breaches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sports_Direct&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Employee conditions and legal breaches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 2013, it was revealed that around 90% of the company's employees are employed on <a href="/wiki/Zero-hours_contract" class="mw-redirect" title="Zero-hours contract">zero-hours contracts</a>, an issue that some of their current and former employees have taken the company to court over.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From January 2013 to December 2014, 76 ambulances or paramedic cars were sent to the postcode for Sports Direct's distribution centre, according to a Freedom of Information request by the BBC.The ambulance service received three calls about women experiencing pregnancy difficulties, including one who gave birth in the site's toilets.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
A Channel 4 Dispatches documentary branded Sports Direct a 'sweatshop' with working conditions compared to the Victorian era and bosses were accused of punishing employees if they talk.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Labour MP <a href="/wiki/John_Mann_(British_politician)" title="John Mann (British politician)">John Mann</a> claimed English speakers were snubbed for positions with the chain despite 3,000 people working there. He said, 'British workers living in his constituency near Sports Direct's 800,000 square foot Shirebrook warehouse were snubbed for jobs at the Derbyshire site'. The warehouse is called the '<a href="/wiki/Gulag" title="Gulag">gulag</a>' among local residents.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2015, the chief executive of Sports Direct, David Forsey, was charged with a criminal offence for consultation failures over <a href="/wiki/USC_(clothing_retailer)" title="USC (clothing retailer)">USC</a> staff who only had 15 minutes notice of redundancy.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian-20151009_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-20151009-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-personneltoday-20151021_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-personneltoday-20151021-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In December 2015, an investigation by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> found that the company fines staff for late clocking on, does not award overtime for late clocking off, relies on <a href="/wiki/Zero_hour_contract" class="mw-redirect" title="Zero hour contract">zero hour contracts</a>, and regularly makes staff wait unpaid for a security check at the end of shifts. A union official suggested that these practices were illegal as they brought workers' earnings below the <a href="/wiki/Minimum_wage" title="Minimum wage">minimum wage</a>. The company responded by saying there were unspecified inaccuracies in the reports.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> A representative from the charity <a href="/wiki/ShareAction" title="ShareAction">ShareAction</a> claimed that workers are "jeopardising their health" for fear of being dismissed while another shareholder said the company's reputation as an employer was "atrocious".<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Late in December 2015 Sports Direct announced a 15 pence per hour increase for staff currently receiving less than minimum wage, taking them above minimum wage,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> the annual cost of this was said in the announcement to be ~£10 Million (GBP); however it was immediately noted that £0.15p × 37.5 hours × 19,000 staff × 52 weeks = 5,557,500 (~£5.5&#160;million), this and other factors resulted in many (including <a href="/wiki/Unite_the_Union" title="Unite the Union">Unite</a>) calling it a "PR Stunt".<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Workers on zero-hours contracts are not included in the rise and neither are those already paid more than minimum wage (management/supervisors etc.) therefore the 19,000 staff above is actually substantially fewer.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2016, Sports Direct admitted breaking the law and will disburse unlawfully withheld wages totalling about £1m to the affected workers.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> As of March 2017, some Sports Direct workers are yet to receive backpay for their time worked, because of a disagreement regarding how contracts changing between employment agencies should be handled.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> In November 2016, six <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="House of Commons of the United Kingdom">MPs</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Business,_Innovation_and_Skills_Committee" class="mw-redirect" title="Business, Innovation and Skills Committee">Business and Skills Committee</a> visited Sports Direct, and reported that while there, Sports Direct attempted to place them under <a href="/wiki/Surveillance" title="Surveillance">surveillance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In February 2017, it was reported that Sports Direct had failed to inform its workforce of a data breach of their personal information after an attacker gained access to its internal systems in September 2016. The <a href="/wiki/Information_Commissioner%27s_Office" title="Information Commissioner&#39;s Office">Information Commissioner's Office</a> stated it was aware of "an incident from 2016 involving Sports Direct" and would "be making enquiries."<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions">Acquisitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sports_Direct&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Acquisitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dunlop_acquisition">Dunlop acquisition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sports_Direct&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Dunlop acquisition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2004, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Dunlop_Slazenger" title="Dunlop Slazenger">Dunlop Slazenger</a> for around £40 million, which included the <a href="/wiki/Dunlop_Sport" title="Dunlop Sport">Dunlop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slazenger" title="Slazenger">Slazenger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Sports" title="Carlton Sports">Carlton</a> brands.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> This was followed by the acquisitions of outdoor gear manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Karrimor" title="Karrimor">Karrimor</a> in March for a reported £5&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> In 2006 he acquired <a href="/wiki/Kangol" title="Kangol">Kangol</a> for an estimated £12&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Most of these brands were bought from distressed sellers. After looking at a takeover,<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Sports Direct took a £9&#160;million stake and signed a lucrative long-term deal in August 2005 with troubled brand <a href="/wiki/Umbro" title="Umbro">Umbro</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> which was subsequently sold to <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The brands themselves are an increasingly important part of the business, and Sports Direct made £10 million, from selling the intellectual-property rights to the Slazenger Golf brand to arch-rival JJB in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-billion_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billion-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_of_Dunlop_brands_by_Sumitomo_Rubber">Acquisition of Dunlop brands by Sumitomo Rubber</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sports_Direct&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Acquisition of Dunlop brands by Sumitomo Rubber">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, <a href="/wiki/Sumitomo_Rubber_Industries" title="Sumitomo Rubber Industries">Sumitomo Rubber Industries</a>, a global tyre, sports goods, and industrial rubber products manufacturing company based in Kobe, Japan, filed for regulatory approval before the <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Competition_Commission" title="Philippine Competition Commission">Philippine Competition Commission</a> in connection with its planned acquisition of Dunlop-related wholesale, manufacturing, and licensing business from Sports Direct.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sumitomo Rubber intended to acquire the entire issued share capital of Dunlop Brands Limited, Dunlop Slazenger 1902 Limited, and Dunlop Australia Limited, and the Dunlop-related business of Dunlop Sports Group Americas, Inc. which are subsidiaries of Sports Direct.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Philippine Competition Commission approved the regulatory filing for the said acquisition. The acquisition allowed Sumitomo Rubber to consolidate the Dunlop brand across various products including sports goods worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_50-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_of_House_of_Fraser">Acquisition of House of Fraser</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sports_Direct&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Acquisition of House of Fraser">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 10 August 2018, the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Fraser" title="House of Fraser">House of Fraser</a> entered <a href="/wiki/Administrator_(law)" title="Administrator (law)">administration</a>. Later that day, Sports Direct agreed to buy all House of Fraser UK stores, the House of Fraser brand, and all of the stock in the business for £90 million in cash. Prior to the company entering administration, Sports Direct's Mike Ashley held an 11% stake in the company.<sup id="cite_ref-purchasebySportsDirect_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-purchasebySportsDirect-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_of_Evans_Cycles">Acquisition of Evans Cycles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sports_Direct&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Acquisition of Evans Cycles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 30 October 2018, <a href="/wiki/Evans_Cycles" title="Evans Cycles">Evans Cycles</a> entered <a href="/wiki/Administrator_(law)" title="Administrator (law)">administration</a>, and was purchased by Sports Direct in a pre-pack administration.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_of_Jack_Wills">Acquisition of Jack Wills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sports_Direct&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Acquisition of Jack Wills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 5 August 2019, Sports Direct purchased <a href="/wiki/Jack_Wills" title="Jack Wills">Jack Wills</a> out of administration for £12.7 million after winning a competition against <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Woollen_Mill" title="Edinburgh Woollen Mill">Edinburgh Woollen Mill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
