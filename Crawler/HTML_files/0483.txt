<p><b>Electronics</b> comprises the physics, engineering, technology and applications that deal with the emission, flow and control of <a href="/wiki/Electron" title="Electron">electrons</a> in <a href="/wiki/Vacuum" title="Vacuum">vacuum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matter" title="Matter">matter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Electronics is widely used in <a href="/wiki/Information_processing" title="Information processing">information processing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telecommunication" title="Telecommunication">telecommunication</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Signal_processing" title="Signal processing">signal processing</a>.  The ability of electronic devices to act as <a href="/wiki/Switch" title="Switch">switches</a> makes digital information-processing possible. Interconnection technologies such as <a href="/wiki/Circuit_board" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit board">circuit boards</a>, electronics packaging technology, and other varied forms of communication infrastructure complete circuit functionality and transform the mixed <a href="/wiki/Electronic_component" title="Electronic component">electronic components</a> into a regular working <a href="/wiki/System" title="System">system</a>, called an <b>electronic system</b>; examples are <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computers</a> or <a href="/wiki/Control_system" title="Control system">control systems</a>. An electronic system may be a component of another <a href="/wiki/Systems_engineering" title="Systems engineering">engineered system</a> or a standalone device. As of 2018<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> most electronic devices<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> use <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">semiconductor</a> components to perform electron control.
</p><p>The identification of the electron in 1897, along with the invention of the <a href="/wiki/Vacuum_tube" title="Vacuum tube">vacuum tube</a>, which could <a href="/wiki/Amplifier" title="Amplifier">amplify</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rectifier" title="Rectifier">rectify</a> small electrical signals,  inaugurated the field of electronics and the electron age.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Commonly, electronic devices contain circuitry consisting primarily or exclusively of active <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">semiconductors</a> supplemented with passive elements; such a circuit is described as an <a href="/wiki/Electronic_circuit" title="Electronic circuit">electronic circuit</a>. Electronics deals with <a href="/wiki/Electrical_circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Electrical circuit">electrical circuits</a> that involve <a href="/wiki/Active_component" class="mw-redirect" title="Active component">active electrical components</a> such as vacuum tubes, <a href="/wiki/Transistor" title="Transistor">transistors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diode" title="Diode">diodes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuit" title="Integrated circuit">integrated circuits</a>, <a href="/wiki/Optoelectronics" title="Optoelectronics">optoelectronics</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sensor" title="Sensor">sensors</a>, associated <a href="/wiki/Passivity_(engineering)" title="Passivity (engineering)">passive electrical components</a>, and interconnection technologies.The <a href="/wiki/Nonlinear" class="mw-redirect" title="Nonlinear">nonlinear</a> behaviour of active components and their ability to control electron flows makes amplification of weak signals possible.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">Electrical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electromechanics" title="Electromechanics">electromechanical</a> science and technology deals with the generation, distribution, switching, storage, and conversion of electrical energy to and from other energy forms (using <a href="/wiki/Wire" title="Wire">wires</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electric_motor" title="Electric motor">motors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electric_generator" title="Electric generator">generators</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battery_(electricity)" class="mw-redirect" title="Battery (electricity)">batteries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switch" title="Switch">switches</a>, <a href="/wiki/Relay" title="Relay">relays</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transformer" title="Transformer">transformers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Resistor" title="Resistor">resistors</a>, and other <a href="/wiki/Passive_component" class="mw-redirect" title="Passive component">passive components</a>). This distinction started around 1906 with the invention by <a href="/wiki/Lee_De_Forest" class="mw-redirect" title="Lee De Forest">Lee De Forest</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Triode" title="Triode">triode</a>, which made electrical <a href="/wiki/Amplifier" title="Amplifier">amplification</a> of weak <a href="/wiki/Radio_signal" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio signal">radio signals</a> and audio signals possible with a non-mechanical device.  Until 1950, this field was called "radio technology" because its principal application was the design and theory of radio <a href="/wiki/Transmitter" title="Transmitter">transmitters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Receiver_(radio)" class="mw-redirect" title="Receiver (radio)">receivers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vacuum_tube" title="Vacuum tube">vacuum tubes</a>.
</p><p>The term "<a href="/wiki/Solid-state_electronics" title="Solid-state electronics">solid-state electronics</a>" emerged after the first working <a href="/wiki/Transistor" title="Transistor">transistor</a> was invented by <a href="/wiki/William_Shockley" title="William Shockley">William Shockley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walter_Houser_Brattain" title="Walter Houser Brattain">Walter Houser Brattain</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Bardeen" title="John Bardeen">John Bardeen</a> at <a href="/wiki/Bell_Labs" title="Bell Labs">Bell Labs</a> in 1947. The <a href="/wiki/MOSFET" title="MOSFET">MOSFET</a> (MOS transistor) was later invented by <a href="/wiki/Mohamed_Atalla" title="Mohamed Atalla">Mohamed Atalla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dawon_Kahng" title="Dawon Kahng">Dawon Kahng</a> at Bell Labs in 1959. The MOSFET was the first truly compact transistor that could be miniaturised and mass-produced for a wide range of uses, revolutionizing the <a href="/wiki/Electronics_industry" title="Electronics industry">electronics industry</a>, and playing a central role in the <a href="/wiki/Microelectronics" title="Microelectronics">microelectronics</a> revolution and <a href="/wiki/Digital_Revolution" title="Digital Revolution">Digital Revolution</a>. The MOSFET has since become the basic element in most modern electronic equipment, and is the most widely used electronic device in the world.
</p><p>The study of <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor_devices" class="mw-redirect" title="Semiconductor devices">semiconductor devices</a> and related technology is considered a branch of <a href="/wiki/Solid-state_physics" title="Solid-state physics">solid-state physics</a>, whereas the design and construction of <a href="/wiki/Electronic_circuit" title="Electronic circuit">electronic circuits</a> to solve practical problems come under <a href="/wiki/Electronics_engineering" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronics engineering">electronics engineering</a>.  This article focuses on <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> aspects of electronics.
</p>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Electronic_component" title="Electronic component">Electronic component</a></div>
<p>An electronic component is any physical entity in an <a href="/wiki/Electronic_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic system">electronic system</a> used to affect the <a href="/wiki/Electron" title="Electron">electrons</a> or their associated fields in a manner consistent with the intended function of the electronic system. Components are generally intended to be connected together, usually by being soldered to a <a href="/wiki/Printed_circuit_board" title="Printed circuit board">printed circuit board</a> (PCB), to create an electronic circuit with a particular function (for example an <a href="/wiki/Amplifier" title="Amplifier">amplifier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radio_receiver" title="Radio receiver">radio receiver</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Electronic_oscillator" title="Electronic oscillator">oscillator</a>). Components may be packaged singly, or in more complex groups as <a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuit" title="Integrated circuit">integrated circuits</a>. Some common electronic components are <a href="/wiki/Capacitor" title="Capacitor">capacitors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inductor" title="Inductor">inductors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Resistor" title="Resistor">resistors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diode" title="Diode">diodes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transistor" title="Transistor">transistors</a>, etc. Components are often categorized as active (e.g. transistors and <a href="/wiki/Thyristor" title="Thyristor">thyristors</a>) or <a href="/wiki/Passivity_(engineering)" title="Passivity (engineering)">passive</a> (e.g. resistors, diodes, inductors and capacitors).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_electronic_components">History of electronic components</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History of electronic components">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_electronic_engineering" title="History of electronic engineering">History of electronic engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_electrical_and_electronic_engineering" title="Timeline of electrical and electronic engineering">Timeline of electrical and electronic engineering</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Vacuum_tube" title="Vacuum tube">Vacuum tubes</a> (Thermionic valves) were among the earliest electronic components.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  They were almost solely responsible for the electronics revolution of the first half of the twentieth century.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  They allowed for vastly more complicated systems and gave us radio, television, phonographs, radar, long-distance telephony and much more.  They played a leading role in the field of microwave and high power transmission as well as television receivers until the middle of the 1980s.<sup id="cite_ref-Okamura1994_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Okamura1994-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  Since that time, <a href="/wiki/Solid-state_electronics" title="Solid-state electronics">solid-state</a> devices have all but completely taken over. Vacuum tubes are still used in some specialist applications such as <a href="/wiki/Valve_RF_amplifier" title="Valve RF amplifier">high power RF amplifiers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cathode_ray_tube" class="mw-redirect" title="Cathode ray tube">cathode ray tubes</a>, specialist audio equipment, <a href="/wiki/Guitar_amplifiers" class="mw-redirect" title="Guitar amplifiers">guitar amplifiers</a> and some <a href="/wiki/Cavity_magnetron" title="Cavity magnetron">microwave devices</a>.
</p><p>The first working <a href="/wiki/Point-contact_transistor" title="Point-contact transistor">point-contact transistor</a> was invented by <a href="/wiki/William_Shockley" title="William Shockley">William Shockley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walter_Houser_Brattain" title="Walter Houser Brattain">Walter Houser Brattain</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Bardeen" title="John Bardeen">John Bardeen</a> at Bell Labs in 1947.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In April 1955, the <a href="/wiki/IBM_608" title="IBM 608">IBM 608</a> was the first <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> product to use <a href="/wiki/Transistor" title="Transistor">transistor</a> circuits without any vacuum tubes and is believed to be the first all-transistorized <a href="/wiki/Calculator" title="Calculator">calculator</a> to be manufactured for the commercial market.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The 608 contained more than 3,000 <a href="/wiki/Germanium" title="Germanium">germanium</a> transistors.  <a href="/wiki/Thomas_J._Watson_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Thomas J. Watson Jr.">Thomas J. Watson Jr.</a> ordered all future IBM products to use transistors in their design. From that time on transistors were almost exclusively used for computer logic and peripherals. However, early <a href="/wiki/Junction_transistor" class="mw-redirect" title="Junction transistor">junction transistors</a> were relatively bulky devices that were difficult to manufacture on a <a href="/wiki/Mass-production" class="mw-redirect" title="Mass-production">mass-production</a> basis, which limited them to a number of specialised applications.<sup id="cite_ref-Moskowitz_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moskowitz-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/MOSFET" title="MOSFET">MOSFET</a> (MOS transistor) was invented by <a href="/wiki/Mohamed_Atalla" title="Mohamed Atalla">Mohamed Atalla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dawon_Kahng" title="Dawon Kahng">Dawon Kahng</a> at Bell Labs in 1959.<sup id="cite_ref-computerhistory_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-computerhistory-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lojek_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lojek-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-computerhistory-transistor_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-computerhistory-transistor-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-triumph_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-triumph-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The MOSFET was the first truly compact transistor that could be miniaturised and mass-produced for a wide range of uses.<sup id="cite_ref-Moskowitz_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moskowitz-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Its advantages include <a href="/wiki/MOSFET_scaling" class="mw-redirect" title="MOSFET scaling">high scalability</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> affordability,<sup id="cite_ref-computerhistory-digital_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-computerhistory-digital-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> low power consumption, and <a href="/wiki/Large-scale_integration" class="mw-redirect" title="Large-scale integration">high density</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It revolutionized the <a href="/wiki/Electronics_industry" title="Electronics industry">electronics industry</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Chan_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chan-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Grant_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grant-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> becoming the most widely used electronic device in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-computerhistory-transistor_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-computerhistory-transistor-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Golio_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Golio-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-computerhistory2018_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-computerhistory2018-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The MOSFET is the basic element in most modern electronic equipment,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Colinge2016_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colinge2016-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and has been central to the electronics revolution,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Microelectronics" title="Microelectronics">microelectronics</a> revolution,<sup id="cite_ref-Zimbovskaya_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zimbovskaya-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Digital_Revolution" title="Digital Revolution">Digital Revolution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-triumph_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-triumph-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Raymer_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raymer-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Wong_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wong-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The MOSFET has thus been credited as the birth of modern electronics,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> and possibly the most important invention in electronics.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_circuits">Types of circuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Types of circuits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Circuits and components can be divided into two groups: analog and digital. A particular device may consist of circuitry that has one or the other or a mix of the two types.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Analog_circuits">Analog circuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Analog circuits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Analog_electronics" class="mw-redirect" title="Analog electronics">Analog electronics</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:HitachiJ100A.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/HitachiJ100A.jpg/220px-HitachiJ100A.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="256" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/HitachiJ100A.jpg/330px-HitachiJ100A.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/HitachiJ100A.jpg/440px-HitachiJ100A.jpg 2x" data-file-width="659" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:HitachiJ100A.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hitachi J100 adjustable frequency drive chassis</div></div></div>
<p>Most <a href="/wiki/Analog_signal" title="Analog signal">analog</a> electronic appliances, such as <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a> receivers, are constructed from combinations of a few types of basic circuits. <a href="/wiki/Analog_circuits" class="mw-redirect" title="Analog circuits">Analog circuits</a> use a continuous range of voltage or current as opposed to discrete levels as in digital circuits.
</p><p>The number of different analog circuits so far devised is huge, especially because a 'circuit' can be defined as anything from a single component, to systems containing thousands of components.
</p><p>Analog circuits are sometimes called <a href="/wiki/Linear_circuit" title="Linear circuit">linear circuits</a> although many non-linear effects are used in analog circuits such as mixers, modulators, etc. Good examples of analog circuits include vacuum tube and transistor amplifiers, operational amplifiers and oscillators.
</p><p>One rarely finds modern circuits that are entirely analog. These days analog circuitry may use digital or even microprocessor techniques to improve performance. This type of circuit is usually called "mixed signal" rather than analog or digital.
</p><p>Sometimes it may be difficult to differentiate between analog and digital circuits as they have elements of both linear and non-linear operation. An example is the comparator which takes in a continuous range of voltage but only outputs one of two levels as in a digital circuit. Similarly, an overdriven transistor amplifier can take on the characteristics of a controlled <a href="/wiki/Switch" title="Switch">switch</a> having essentially two levels of output.  In fact, many digital circuits are actually implemented as variations of analog circuits similar to this example&#160;– after all, all aspects of the real physical world are essentially analog, so digital effects are only realized by constraining analog behavior.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Digital_circuits">Digital circuits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Digital circuits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Digital_electronics" title="Digital electronics">Digital electronics</a></div>
<p>Digital circuits are electric circuits based on a number of discrete voltage levels. Digital circuits are the most common physical representation of <a href="/wiki/Boolean_logic" class="mw-redirect" title="Boolean logic">Boolean algebra</a>, and are the basis of all digital computers. To most engineers, the terms "digital circuit", "digital system" and "logic" are interchangeable in the context of digital circuits.
Most digital circuits use a binary system with two voltage levels labeled "0" and "1". Often logic "0" will be a lower voltage and referred to as "Low" while logic "1" is referred to as "High".  However, some systems use the reverse definition ("0" is "High") or are current based.  Quite often the logic designer may reverse these definitions from one circuit to the next as he sees fit to facilitate his design.  The definition of the levels as "0" or "1" is arbitrary.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ternary_computer" title="Ternary computer">Ternary</a> (with three states) logic has been studied, and some prototype computers made.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">Computers</a>, electronic <a href="/wiki/Quartz_clock" title="Quartz clock">clocks</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Programmable_logic_controller" title="Programmable logic controller">programmable logic controllers</a> (used to control industrial processes) are constructed of <a href="/wiki/Digital_data" title="Digital data">digital</a> circuits. <a href="/wiki/Digital_signal_processor" title="Digital signal processor">Digital signal processors</a> are another example.
</p><p>Building blocks:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Metal-oxide-semiconductor_field-effect_transistor" class="mw-redirect" title="Metal-oxide-semiconductor field-effect transistor">Metal-oxide-semiconductor field-effect transistor</a> (MOSFET)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Logic_gate" title="Logic gate">Logic gates</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Adder_(electronics)" title="Adder (electronics)">Adders</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Flip-flop_(electronics)" title="Flip-flop (electronics)">Flip-flops</a></li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Processor_register" title="Processor register">Registers</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Multiplexer" title="Multiplexer">Multiplexers</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Schmitt_trigger" title="Schmitt trigger">Schmitt triggers</a></li></ul>
<p>Highly integrated devices:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Memory_chip" class="mw-redirect" title="Memory chip">Memory chip</a></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Heat_dissipation_and_thermal_management">Heat dissipation and thermal management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Heat dissipation and thermal management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Thermal_management_of_electronic_devices_and_systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Thermal management of electronic devices and systems">Thermal management of electronic devices and systems</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Heat" title="Heat">Heat</a> generated by electronic circuitry must be dissipated to prevent immediate failure and improve long term reliability. Heat dissipation is mostly achieved by passive conduction/convection. Means to achieve greater dissipation include <a href="/wiki/Heat_sink" title="Heat sink">heat sinks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_fan" class="mw-redirect" title="Mechanical fan">fans</a> for air cooling, and other forms of <a href="/wiki/Computer_cooling" title="Computer cooling">computer cooling</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Water_cooling" title="Water cooling">water cooling</a>. These techniques use <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">convection</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heat_conduction" class="mw-redirect" title="Heat conduction">conduction</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Radiation" title="Radiation">radiation</a> of heat energy.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Noise">Noise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Noise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Electronic_noise" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic noise">Electronic noise</a></div>
<p>Electronic noise is defined<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> as unwanted disturbances superposed on a useful signal that tend to obscure its information content. Noise is not the same as signal distortion caused by a circuit. Noise is associated with all electronic circuits. Noise may be electromagnetically or thermally generated, which can be decreased by lowering the <a href="/wiki/Operating_temperature" title="Operating temperature">operating temperature</a> of the circuit. Other types of noise, such as <a href="/wiki/Shot_noise" title="Shot noise">shot noise</a> cannot be removed as they are due to limitations in physical properties.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electronics_theory">Electronics theory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Electronics theory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_methods_in_electronics" title="Mathematical methods in electronics">Mathematical methods in electronics</a></div>
<p>Mathematical methods are integral to the study of electronics. To become proficient in electronics it is also necessary to become proficient in the mathematics of circuit analysis.
</p><p>Circuit analysis is the study of methods of solving generally linear systems for unknown variables such as the voltage at a certain <a href="/wiki/Node_(circuits)" title="Node (circuits)">node</a> or the current through a certain <a href="/wiki/Path_(graph_theory)" title="Path (graph theory)">branch</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Graph_(discrete_mathematics)" title="Graph (discrete mathematics)">network</a>. A common analytical tool for this is the <a href="/wiki/SPICE" title="SPICE">SPICE</a> circuit simulator.
</p><p>Also important to electronics is the study and understanding of <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_field" title="Electromagnetic field">electromagnetic field</a> theory.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electronics_lab">Electronics lab</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Electronics lab">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Electronic_circuit_simulation" title="Electronic circuit simulation">Electronic circuit simulation</a></div>
<p>Due to the complex nature of electronics theory, laboratory experimentation is an important part of the development of electronic devices.  These experiments are used to test or verify the engineer's design and detect errors.  Historically, electronics labs have consisted of electronics devices and equipment located in a physical space, although in more recent years the trend has been towards electronics lab simulation software, such as <a href="/wiki/CircuitLogix" title="CircuitLogix">CircuitLogix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Multisim" class="mw-redirect" title="Multisim">Multisim</a>, and <a href="/wiki/PSpice" class="mw-redirect" title="PSpice">PSpice</a>.
</p>
<h2><span id="Computer_aided_design_.28CAD.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Computer_aided_design_(CAD)">Computer aided design (CAD)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Computer aided design (CAD)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Electronic_design_automation" title="Electronic design automation">Electronic design automation</a></div>
<p>Today's electronics engineers have the ability to <a href="/wiki/Circuit_design" title="Circuit design">design</a> <a href="/wiki/Electronic_circuit" title="Electronic circuit">circuits</a> using premanufactured building blocks such as <a href="/wiki/Power_supply" title="Power supply">power supplies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">semiconductors</a> (i.e. semiconductor devices, such as <a href="/wiki/Transistor" title="Transistor">transistors</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuit" title="Integrated circuit">integrated circuits</a>. <a href="/wiki/Electronic_design_automation" title="Electronic design automation">Electronic design automation</a> software programs include <a href="/wiki/Schematic_capture" title="Schematic capture">schematic capture</a> programs and <a href="/wiki/Printed_circuit_board" title="Printed circuit board">printed circuit board</a> design programs. Popular names in the EDA software world are NI Multisim, Cadence (<a href="/wiki/ORCAD" class="mw-redirect" title="ORCAD">ORCAD</a>), <a href="/wiki/EAGLE_(program)" title="EAGLE (program)">EAGLE</a> PCB and Schematic, Mentor (PADS PCB and LOGIC Schematic), Altium (Protel), LabCentre Electronics (Proteus), gEDA, KiCad and many others.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Packaging_methods">Packaging methods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Packaging methods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Electronic_packaging" title="Electronic packaging">Electronic packaging</a></div>
<p>Many different methods of connecting components have been used over the years. For instance, early electronics often used <a href="/wiki/Point-to-point_construction" title="Point-to-point construction">point to point wiring</a> with components attached to wooden breadboards to construct circuits. <a href="/wiki/Printed_circuit_board#&quot;Cordwood&quot;_construction" title="Printed circuit board">Cordwood construction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wire_wrap" title="Wire wrap">wire wrap</a> were other methods used. Most modern day electronics now use printed circuit boards made of materials such as <a href="/wiki/FR-4" title="FR-4">FR4</a>, or the cheaper (and less hard-wearing) Synthetic Resin Bonded Paper (<a href="/wiki/SRBP" class="mw-redirect" title="SRBP">SRBP</a>, also known as Paxoline/Paxolin (trade marks) and FR2) – characterised by its brown colour. Health and environmental concerns associated with electronics assembly have gained increased attention in recent years, especially for products destined to the European Union, with its <a href="/wiki/Restriction_of_Hazardous_Substances_Directive" title="Restriction of Hazardous Substances Directive">Restriction of Hazardous Substances Directive</a> (RoHS) and <a href="/wiki/Waste_Electrical_and_Electronic_Equipment_Directive" title="Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Directive">Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment Directive</a> (WEEE), which went into force in July 2006.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electronic_systems_design">Electronic systems design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Electronics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Electronic systems design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Systems_engineering" title="Systems engineering">Systems engineering</a></div>
<p>Electronic systems design deals with the multi-disciplinary design issues of complex electronic devices and systems, such as <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone" title="Mobile phone">mobile phones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computers</a>. The subject covers a broad spectrum, from the design and development of an electronic system (<a href="/wiki/New_product_development" title="New product development">new product development</a>) to assuring its proper function, service life and <a href="/wiki/Recycling" title="Recycling">disposal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lienig_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lienig-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Electronic systems design is therefore the process of defining and developing complex electronic devices to satisfy specified <a href="/wiki/Requirement_analysis" class="mw-redirect" title="Requirement analysis">requirements</a> of the user.
</p>
