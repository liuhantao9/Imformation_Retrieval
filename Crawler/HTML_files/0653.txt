<p><b>Kirklees Stadium</b> (currently known due to <a href="/wiki/Naming_rights" title="Naming rights">sponsorship</a> as the <b>John Smith's Stadium</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-history_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> is a multi-use stadium in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. Since 1994, it has been the home ground of <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> side <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Giants" title="Huddersfield Giants">Huddersfield Giants</a>, both of whom moved from <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Road" title="Leeds Road">Leeds Road</a>.
</p><p>The stadium was a venue at the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="Rugby League World Cup">Rugby League World Cup</a> in 1995, 2000 and 2013, in addition to the <a href="/wiki/1999_Rugby_World_Cup" title="1999 Rugby World Cup">1999 Rugby World Cup</a>. It is owned by both clubs, as well as <a href="/wiki/Kirklees_Council" title="Kirklees Council">Kirklees Council</a>. Its naming rights have passed from constructors <a href="/wiki/Alfred_McAlpine" title="Alfred McAlpine">Alfred McAlpine</a> to pharmaceutical company <a href="/wiki/Galpharm_International" title="Galpharm International">Galpharm International</a> in 2004, then to <a href="/wiki/John_Smith%27s_Brewery" title="John Smith&#39;s Brewery">John Smith's Brewery</a> eight years later.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stadium">Stadium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Stadium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During planning and construction, the stadium was referred to as the <b>Kirklees Stadium</b>. It was built by <a href="/wiki/Alfred_McAlpine" title="Alfred McAlpine">Alfred McAlpine</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> designed by Populous and was awarded the <a href="/wiki/RIBA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIBA">RIBA</a> Building of the Year award for 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The decision to build a new stadium for Huddersfield Town was made in August 1992. Construction began the following year and it was completed in time for the <a href="/wiki/1994-95_in_English_football" class="mw-redirect" title="1994-95 in English football">1994–95 season</a>, enabling the club to move to its new base after 86 years at <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Road" title="Leeds Road">Leeds Road</a>.
</p><p>When the stadium opened only the two side stands (the Riverside and Kilner Bank stands) were ready. The South Stand was opened in December 1994. Construction on the North (Panasonic) Stand began in 1996 and it was completed in 1998, bringing the overall capacity of the stadium to approximately 24,500. The estimated cost of construction was £40&#160;million.
</p><p>A ski slope will be built next to the stadium.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ownership">Ownership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ownership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Initially, the stadium was owned by a consortium made up of <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a> Metropolitan Council, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town A.F.C.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Giants" title="Huddersfield Giants">Huddersfield Giants</a> in a 40:40:20 proportion. Following the purchase of Huddersfield Town A.F.C. from the <a href="/wiki/Administrator_(law)" title="Administrator (law)">administrators</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Davy" title="Ken Davy">Ken Davy</a> became chairman of both sports clubs, which are owned by companies he controls.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The present ownership of the stadium is Kirklees Metropolitan Council 40%, Huddersfield Town FC 40%, and Huddersfield Giants 20%. The current Managing director of the stadium company is Gareth Davis<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> who succeeded <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Rimmer" title="Ralph Rimmer">Ralph Rimmer</a> in 2010. On 24 December 2009, Huddersfield Town announced that 40% of the shares owned by Huddersfield Sporting Pride would be transferred to the football club, owned by current chairman <a href="/wiki/Dean_Hoyle" title="Dean Hoyle">Dean Hoyle</a>, but the deal was delayed due to a rent dispute between Davy and Hoyle. The deal was finally completed on 3 September 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sponsorship">Sponsorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sponsorship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1994 until 2004 the stadium was known as the Alfred McAlpine Stadium. <a href="/wiki/Alfred_McAlpine" title="Alfred McAlpine">Alfred McAlpine</a> had been the main construction contractor and its name was part of the payment contract for ten years. The company elected not to renew its sponsorship which was taken up by <a href="/wiki/Galpharm_International" title="Galpharm International">Galpharm Healthcare</a>, leading to the new name. Individual stands are sponsored by local businesses. On 19 July 2012, it was announced that the brewery firm, <a href="/wiki/Heineken_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Heineken International">Heineken</a> had bought the sponsorship rights for the stadium using their domestic <a href="/wiki/John_Smith%27s_Brewery" title="John Smith&#39;s Brewery">John Smith's Brewery</a> as the beneficiary, and as such the stadium was renamed as The John Smith's Stadium on 1 August as part of a five-year deal. In December 2016, this was extended for a further five years.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_Stand">North Stand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: North Stand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Capacity –   (seating)</b><br />
The North Stand, known as the Fantastic Media Stand for sponsorship reasons, is located behind the goal post at the north end of the ground. It has two tiers and houses 16 hospitality boxes and is completely seated. The lower tier contains temporary seats that can be removed for concerts or other events.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Stand">East Stand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: East Stand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Capacity – 7,000 (seating)</b><br />
The East Stand known as the Kilner Bank Stand or the Britannia Rescue Stand for sponsorship reasons is a large single tier stand that runs along the side of the pitch and is completely covered seating. The stand also holds the TV gantry.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Stand">South Stand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: South Stand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Capacity – 4,054 (seating)</b><br />
The John Smiths South Stand as it is known for sponsorship reasons is an all seated stand built into a bank and is completely covered as well as having the big screen. The stand used to be completely for away fans but it is now shared with home fans with segregation netting. From 2017–18 a new segregation system was implemented with away fans given 2,500 tickets instead of 2,000.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="West_Stand">West Stand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: West Stand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Capacity –  (seating)</b><br />
The West stand is the main stand and is known as the Revell Ward Stand for sponsorship or Riverside Stand. It has two tiers and 26 hospitality boxes and incorporates the player changing rooms, tunnel, dug out, ticket office and club shop.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Football">Football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It hosted its first match on 20 August 1994 when Huddersfield Town lost 1–0 to <a href="/wiki/Wycombe_Wanderers_F.C." title="Wycombe Wanderers F.C.">Wycombe Wanderers</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1994%E2%80%9395_Football_League_Second_Division" class="mw-redirect" title="1994–95 Football League Second Division">Second Division</a>. Only the two touchline stands had been completed, and 13,334 spectators attended. <a href="/wiki/Simon_Garner" title="Simon Garner">Simon Garner</a> scored the goal for the visitors.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 4 June 1999, it hosted <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England under-21</a> in a <a href="/wiki/2000_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship_qualification#Group_5" title="2000 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification">2000 UEFA European Under-21 Championship qualification Group 5</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Sweden_national_under-21_football_team" title="Sweden national under-21 football team">Sweden</a>, a 3–0 win for England.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The record for a football match is 24,129 for an <a href="/wiki/FA_Cup" title="FA Cup">FA Cup</a> fifth round match between Huddersfield Town and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> on 18 February 2017, a goalless draw.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On 20 August, it held its first <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> game, with Huddersfield's <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Mooy" title="Aaron Mooy">Aaron Mooy</a> scoring the only goal of a 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United" class="mw-redirect" title="Newcastle United">Newcastle United</a> in front of a crowd one spectator lower than the record.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby_League">Rugby League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Rugby League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:England_v_Ireland_2013_RLWC_(1).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/England_v_Ireland_2013_RLWC_%281%29.jpg/220px-England_v_Ireland_2013_RLWC_%281%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/England_v_Ireland_2013_RLWC_%281%29.jpg/330px-England_v_Ireland_2013_RLWC_%281%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/England_v_Ireland_2013_RLWC_%281%29.jpg/440px-England_v_Ireland_2013_RLWC_%281%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4912" data-file-height="3264" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:England_v_Ireland_2013_RLWC_(1).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The England–Ireland game at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="2013 Rugby League World Cup">2013 Rugby League World Cup</a> set the stadium's attendance record</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="Rugby League World Cup">Rugby League World Cup</a> matches were held at the stadium in <a href="/wiki/1995_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="1995 Rugby League World Cup">1995</a>, <a href="/wiki/2000_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="2000 Rugby League World Cup">2000</a> and <a href="/wiki/2013_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="2013 Rugby League World Cup">2013</a>. Seven <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_national_rugby_league_team" title="Great Britain national rugby league team">Great Britain</a> rugby league internationals have been held at the ground, from 1998 to 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Since the Great Britain team was split into home nations, it has hosted four <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_league_team" title="England national rugby league team">England</a> rugby league internationals, including a 42–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Ireland_rugby_league_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Ireland rugby league team">Ireland</a> in the group stage of the 2013 World Cup, in front of 24,375 spectators. It was the ground's first sell-out crowd,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and its record attendance.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 29 January 2019, it was announced that the John Smith's Stadium would play host to a quarter-final of the <a href="/wiki/2021_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="2021 Rugby League World Cup">2021 Rugby League World Cup</a>.

<p>The stadium has held semi-finals of the <a href="/wiki/Challenge_Cup" title="Challenge Cup">Challenge Cup</a> and the finals of the now defunct <a href="/wiki/League_Cup_(rugby_league)" title="League Cup (rugby league)">Regal Trophy</a> competition were held there in 1995 and 1996. <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Warriors" title="Wigan Warriors">Wigan Warriors</a> won on both occasions. The stadium has also hosted three of <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Bulls" title="Bradford Bulls">Bradford Bulls</a>' <a href="/wiki/World_Club_Challenge" title="World Club Challenge">World Club Challenge</a> matches, from 2002 to 2006. The Huddersfield Giants attendance record at the venue stands at 15,629 for a match against rivals <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Rhinos" title="Leeds Rhinos">Leeds Rhinos</a> on 10 February 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby_Union">Rugby Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Rugby Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite Huddersfield not being a strong rugby union area, the stadium has been used for four full cap international rugby union matches. It hosted three qualifying matches for the <a href="/wiki/1999_Rugby_World_Cup" title="1999 Rugby World Cup">1999 Rugby World Cup</a> and one match in the pool.  Two of the matches saw teams pass 100 points: England beat the Netherlands 110–0 in a qualifier and New Zealand defeated Italy 101–3 in the pool.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Concerts">Concerts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees_Stadium&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Concerts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first concerts at the stadium were on 25 and 26 July 1995 by American band <a href="/wiki/R.E.M." title="R.E.M.">R.E.M.</a>, attended by around 80,000 people and providing a £3&#160;million boom to local businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Eagles_(band)" title="Eagles (band)">Eagles</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Adams" title="Bryan Adams">Bryan Adams</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Beautiful_South" title="The Beautiful South">The Beautiful South</a> also performed there in the 1990s,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> followed four years later by <a href="/wiki/Bon_Jovi" title="Bon Jovi">Bon Jovi</a> in June 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After three years with no performances, <a href="/wiki/Blue_(English_band)" title="Blue (English band)">Blue</a> played at the stadium on the 31 July 2004 with <a href="/wiki/Girls_Aloud" title="Girls Aloud">Girls Aloud</a> and <a href="/wiki/Darius_Campbell" title="Darius Campbell">Darius</a> supporting, in an event to reveal Galpharm's new sponsorship of the venue.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The following two summers, <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lulu_(singer)" title="Lulu (singer)">Lulu</a> in 2005, and Bryan Adams again in 2006, performed.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> After a gap of twelve years, <a href="/wiki/Little_Mix" title="Little Mix">Little Mix</a> were booked to perform in July 2018 followed by <a href="/wiki/Take_That" title="Take That">Take That</a> in June 2019 and <a href="/wiki/Green_Day" title="Green Day">Green Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Weezer" title="Weezer">Weezer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fall_Out_Boy" title="Fall Out Boy">Fall Out Boy</a> in June 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
