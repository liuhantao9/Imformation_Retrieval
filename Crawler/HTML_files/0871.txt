<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Movinggrate.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Movinggrate.jpg/200px-Movinggrate.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="109" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Movinggrate.jpg/300px-Movinggrate.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Movinggrate.jpg/400px-Movinggrate.jpg 2x" data-file-width="701" data-file-height="381" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Movinggrate.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Moving Grate Furnace</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Kirklees EfW</b> is a major moving grate <a href="/wiki/Incineration" title="Incineration">incineration</a> plant in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a>, England. The incinerator is owned and operated by <a href="/wiki/Suez_Recycling_and_Recovery_UK" title="Suez Recycling and Recovery UK">Suez Recycling and Recovery UK</a> who signed a 25-year contract with Kirklees Council in 1998<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> with an option to increase the time period to 2028.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The plant is integral to the waste strategy and Unitary Development plan of Kirklees Council, treating 150,000 tonnes (170,000 tons) of locally generated <a href="/wiki/Municipal_waste" class="mw-redirect" title="Municipal waste">municipal waste</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which when incinerated, will produce enough electricity to power 15,000 homes.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Only 136,000 tonnes (150,000 tons) of waste is actually incinerated, the other tonnages permitted are recovered materials such as metals (for recycling) and <a href="/wiki/Incinerator_bottom_ash" title="Incinerator bottom ash">Incinerator bottom ash</a> (IBA) and <a href="/wiki/Fly_ash" title="Fly ash">Fly ash</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The incinerator has been operational since 2002 and was one of the first waste projects financed by <a href="/wiki/Private_Finance_Initiative" class="mw-redirect" title="Private Finance Initiative">PFI</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. The plant, which employs 29 staff and operates 24 hours a day, cost £35 million.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
