<p>The <b>River Colne</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span><span title="/oʊ/: &#39;o&#39; in &#39;code&#39;">oʊ</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span></span>/</a></span></span> is a river in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> formed by a confluence at the foot of the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a> close to the village of <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Course">Course</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Course">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Numerous brooks formed by rainwater high (between 300 and 480 metres <a href="/wiki/AMSL" class="mw-redirect" title="AMSL">AMSL</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, flow down the hillsides through the small valleys (known locally as <i>Cloughs</i>) to feed two <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Water" title="Yorkshire Water">Yorkshire Water</a> reservoirs; <a href="/w/index.php?title=March_Haigh_Reservoir&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="March Haigh Reservoir (page does not exist)">March Haigh Reservoir</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Redbrook_Reservoir&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Redbrook Reservoir (page does not exist)">Redbrook Reservoir</a>. Thereafter, Haigh Brook and Redbrook continue the journey down the slopes, again being fed by numerous tributaries, until the two streams converge at a scenic spot called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Close_Gate_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Close Gate Bridge (page does not exist)">Close Gate Bridge</a>. This confluence forms the River Colne.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The river flows from west to east through the <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley" title="Colne Valley">Colne Valley</a> passing through the villages of <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slaithwaite" title="Slaithwaite">Slaithwaite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Milnsbridge" title="Milnsbridge">Milnsbridge</a> to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and then on to Cooper Bridge where it feeds the larger <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Its tributaries include <a href="/wiki/Wessenden_Valley" title="Wessenden Valley">Wessenden Brook</a>, Bradley Brook, Crimble Brook, Mag Brook, Fenay Brook, New Mill Dike and the <a href="/wiki/River_Holme" title="River Holme">River Holme</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Colne Valley was famous for the production of woollen and cotton cloth regarded as some of the finest quality produced anywhere and all due to the soft acidic waters of the River Colne and its brooks running down through the side valleys (cloughs) from the peat moors above.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Infrastructure">Infrastructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Infrastructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Narrow_Canal" title="Huddersfield Narrow Canal">Huddersfield Narrow Canal</a> follows the course of the river through the valley, as does the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Line">Huddersfield Line</a> railway, and the <a href="/wiki/A62_road" title="A62 road">A62 road</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The river itself is too shallow and rocky to be navigable by any watercraft.
</p><p>There are numerous factories, warehouses and plants along the river providing goods, services and jobs, but a few of these cause pollution to the local environment and waterways.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In order to help tackle pollution, the <a href="/wiki/Environment_Agency" title="Environment Agency">Environment Agency</a> demand that certain standards be met by local companies.
</p><p>On 24 May 2010, part of a chemical factory was accidentally set ablaze and caused waste to be emptied into the river and the canal causing the deaths of many fish.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
