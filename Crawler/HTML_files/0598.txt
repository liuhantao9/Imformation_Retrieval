<p>The <b>M62</b> is a 107-mile-long (172&#160;km) west–east <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">trans-Pennine</a> motorway in <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a>, connecting <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Hull</a> via <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>; 7 miles (11&#160;km) of the route <a href="/wiki/Concurrency_(road)" title="Concurrency (road)">is shared</a> with the <a href="/wiki/M60_motorway" title="M60 motorway">M60 orbital motorway</a> around Manchester. The road is part of the unsigned <a href="/wiki/International_E-road_network" title="International E-road network">Euroroutes</a> <a href="/wiki/European_route_E20" title="European route E20">E20</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shannon,_County_Clare" title="Shannon, County Clare">Shannon</a> to <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a>) and <a href="/wiki/European_route_E22" title="European route E22">E22</a> (<a href="/wiki/Holyhead" title="Holyhead">Holyhead</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ishim,_Tyumen_Oblast" title="Ishim, Tyumen Oblast">Ishim</a>).
</p><p>The motorway, which was first proposed in the 1930s, and conceived as two separate routes, was opened in stages between 1971 and 1976, with construction beginning at <a href="/wiki/Pole_Moor" title="Pole Moor">Pole Moor</a> and finishing at that time in <a href="/wiki/Tarbock" title="Tarbock">Tarbock</a> on the outskirts of Liverpool. The motorway absorbed the northern end of the <a href="/wiki/Stretford" title="Stretford">Stretford</a>-<a href="/wiki/Eccles,_Greater_Manchester" title="Eccles, Greater Manchester">Eccles</a> bypass, which was built between 1957 and 1960. Adjusted for inflation to 2007, its construction cost approximately £765&#160;million. The motorway has an average daily traffic flow of 144,000 vehicles in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, and has several areas prone to <a href="/wiki/Gridlock" title="Gridlock">gridlock</a>, in particular, between Leeds and <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and the M60 section around <a href="/wiki/Eccles,_Greater_Manchester" title="Eccles, Greater Manchester">Eccles</a>. The <a href="/wiki/M62_coach_bombing" title="M62 coach bombing">M62 coach bombing</a> of 1974 and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Heck_rail_crash" title="Great Heck rail crash">Great Heck rail crash</a> of 2001 are the largest incidents to have occurred on the M62.
</p><p>Stott Hall Farm, situated between the carriageways on the Pennine section has become one of the best-known sights on the motorway. The M62 has no junctions numbered 1, 2 or 3, or even an officially numbered 4, because it was intended to start in Liverpool proper, not in its outskirts.
</p><p>Between Liverpool and Manchester, and east of Leeds, the terrain along which the road passes is relatively flat. Between Manchester and Leeds it traverses the Pennines and its foothills, rising to 1,221 feet (372&#160;m) above sea level slightly east of junction 22 in <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a>, not far from the boundary between <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a> and West Yorkshire.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Planning">Planning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Planning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The motorway's origins are found in the 1930s, when the need for a route between <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> had been agreed after discussion by their county highway authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-themotorwayarchive1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-themotorwayarchive1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At the same time, it was envisaged that a route between Liverpool and Hull was needed to connect the ports to industrial Yorkshire.<sup id="cite_ref-themotorwayarchive2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-themotorwayarchive2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Transport" title="Secretary of State for Transport">Minister of Transport</a> appointed engineers to inspect road standards between the <a href="/wiki/A580_road" title="A580 road">A580 road</a> (<i>East Lancashire Road</i>) in <a href="/wiki/Swinton,_Greater_Manchester" title="Swinton, Greater Manchester">Swinton</a> and the <a href="/wiki/A1_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A1 road (Great Britain)">A1 road</a> near <a href="/wiki/Selby" title="Selby">Selby</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-themotorwayarchive2_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-themotorwayarchive2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The 1949 Road Plan for South Lancashire identified the need to upgrade the A580 to <a href="/wiki/Dual_carriageway" title="Dual carriageway">dual carriageway</a> with <a href="/wiki/Grade_separation" title="Grade separation">grade separation</a> and provide bypasses at <a href="/wiki/Huyton" title="Huyton">Huyton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cadishead" title="Cadishead">Cadishead</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-themotorwayarchive3_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-themotorwayarchive3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1952, the route for a trans-Pennine motorway, the Lancashire–Yorkshire Motorway, was laid down, with <a href="/wiki/Ferrybridge" title="Ferrybridge">Ferrybridge</a> at the eastern terminus rather than Selby.<sup id="cite_ref-themotorwayarchive2_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-themotorwayarchive2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> By the 1960s, the proposed A580 upgrade to dual carriageway was considered inadequate, and there was an urgent need to link Liverpool to the motorway network.<sup id="cite_ref-themotorwayarchive3_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-themotorwayarchive3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The route of the Lancashire-Yorkshire motorway was considered inadequate as it failed to cater for several industrial towns in Yorkshire.<sup id="cite_ref-themotorwayarchive2_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-themotorwayarchive2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> When James Drake visited the United States in 1962, his experience of the <a href="/wiki/Interstate_Highway_System" title="Interstate Highway System">Interstate Highway System</a> led him to conclude that the Merseyside Expressway, planned to run between Liverpool and the <a href="/wiki/M6_motorway" title="M6 motorway">M6</a>, would need to be extended to the <a href="/wiki/M63_motorway" title="M63 motorway">Stretford-Eccles Bypass</a> and beyond, to create a continuous motorway between Liverpool and Ferrybridge (a link between Ferrybridge and Hull was not considered until 1964).<sup id="cite_ref-archive_ouse_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-archive_ouse-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Initially the plans were unpopular and not supported by the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Transport" title="Department for Transport">Ministry of Transport</a>, but the scheme was added to the Road Plan in 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-themotorwayarchive3_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-themotorwayarchive3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction">Construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liverpool_to_Worsley">Liverpool to Worsley</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Liverpool to Worsley">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It was the intention to build an urban motorway in Liverpool.<sup id="cite_ref-simmons_consultancy_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-simmons_consultancy-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The M62 was intended to terminate at Liverpool's Inner Motorway, which was not built. The proposed route would have followed the railway into Liverpool as far as <a href="/wiki/Edge_Hill_railway_station" title="Edge Hill railway station">Edge Hill</a>, with junctions at Rathbone Road and Durning Road where it would drop to two lanes before terminating at the Islington Radial.<sup id="cite_ref-urbanm62_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urbanm62-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Difficulties arose building the Liverpool urban motorway resulting in delays, with the section between Tarbock and Liverpool the last to be completed in 1976. In total, two <a href="/wiki/Viaduct" title="Viaduct">viaducts</a>, ten bridges and seven underpasses were constructed to secure the structural integrity of the surrounding residential areas. The motorway was constructed only as far as the <a href="/wiki/A5058_road" title="A5058 road">Queens Drive inner ring road</a>, which is junction 4.<sup id="cite_ref-themotorwayarchive3_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-themotorwayarchive3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The section west of Manchester was intended to be a separate motorway, the M52 to link Liverpool and Salford, but a continuous motorway between Leeds and Liverpool was deemed more feasible,<sup id="cite_ref-pathetic_m52_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pathetic_m52-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-m602_cbrd_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-m602_cbrd-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Construction between Liverpool and Manchester started in 1971, with the construction of a link between the <a href="/wiki/M57_motorway" title="M57 motorway">M57</a> and M6 motorways.<sup id="cite_ref-themotorwayarchive3_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-themotorwayarchive3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Simultaneously, a contract to link the M6 with Manchester was underway, which required land drainage and the removal of unsuitable earth. This section was completed in August 1974, creating a continuous link between Ferrybridge and Tarbock.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_Greater_Manchester">In Greater Manchester</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In Greater Manchester">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/M60_motorway" title="M60 motorway">M60 motorway</a>, <a href="/wiki/M602_motorway" title="M602 motorway">M602 motorway</a>, and <a href="/wiki/M63_motorway" title="M63 motorway">M63 motorway</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:M62_construction_in_Milnrow.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/M62_construction_in_Milnrow.jpg/220px-M62_construction_in_Milnrow.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/M62_construction_in_Milnrow.jpg/330px-M62_construction_in_Milnrow.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/M62_construction_in_Milnrow.jpg/440px-M62_construction_in_Milnrow.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="481" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:M62_construction_in_Milnrow.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Construction starting in <a href="/wiki/Milnrow" title="Milnrow">Milnrow</a>, 1968</div></div></div>
<p>Two motorways were planned, the M52 from Liverpool to Salford and the M62 to link Pole Moor with the Stretford–Eccles Bypass.<sup id="cite_ref-pathetic_m52_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pathetic_m52-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The first part of the M62 to be built was the Stretford–Eccles Bypass, which is now the section between Junctions&#160;7 to 13 of the <a href="/wiki/M60_motorway" title="M60 motorway">M60</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pathetic_stretford_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pathetic_stretford-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Construction started in 1957, and the motorway opened in 1960. It was originally built as a 2-lane motorway only. It was later re-numbered M63.<sup id="cite_ref-seb_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seb-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The section between the interchange with the Stretford-Eccles Bypass and Salford is now occupied by the <a href="/wiki/M602_motorway" title="M602 motorway">M602 motorway</a>.
</p><p>The Eccles–Pole Moor section opened in 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-cbrd_m62_timeline_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbrd_m62_timeline-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cbrd_m60_timeline_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbrd_m60_timeline-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Between <a href="/wiki/Eccles,_Greater_Manchester" title="Eccles, Greater Manchester">Eccles</a> and Pole Moor, 67 motorway crossings were required, including seven viaducts and eight junctions.<sup id="cite_ref-themotorwayarchive3_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-themotorwayarchive3-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Much of the Worsley Braided Interchange was built on undeveloped <a href="/wiki/Bog" title="Bog">mossland</a> where deep <a href="/wiki/Peat" title="Peat">peat</a> deposits had been covered with waste. Between <a href="/wiki/Worsley" title="Worsley">Worsley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Milnrow" title="Milnrow">Milnrow</a>, some underlying coal seams were still actively worked when the motorway was constructed and allowances had to be made to counteract possible future subsidence.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJohnson19724_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJohnson19724-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The motorway crosses the <a href="/wiki/Irwell_Valley" title="Irwell Valley">Irwell Valley</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pendleton_Fault" title="Pendleton Fault">Pendleton Fault</a><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJohnson197210_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJohnson197210-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> on a 200-foot (61&#160;m) single-span bridge 65 feet (20&#160;m) above the <a href="/wiki/River_Irwell" title="River Irwell">river</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJohnson197211_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJohnson197211-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Milnrow_to_Outlane">Milnrow to Outlane</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Milnrow to Outlane">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:M62_Summit_sign_29_July_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/M62_Summit_sign_29_July_2017.jpg/220px-M62_Summit_sign_29_July_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="223" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/ec/M62_Summit_sign_29_July_2017.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="323" data-file-height="328" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:M62_Summit_sign_29_July_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>M62 Summit sign</div></div></div>
<p>Surveying for the Pennine section began in November 1961 and its route was determined in July 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJohnson19727_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJohnson19727-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Construction between Windy Hill and Pole Moor was difficult through inhospitable hilly terrain, <a href="/wiki/Bog" title="Bog">peat bogs</a>, and in undesirable weather conditions. The motorway's highest point, 1,221 feet (372&#160;m) above sea level at <a href="/wiki/Windy_Hill_(Pennines)" title="Windy Hill (Pennines)">Windy Hill</a> near <a href="/wiki/Denshaw" title="Denshaw">Denshaw</a> (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=M62_motorway&amp;params=53.62982_N_2.018561_W_region:GB_type:landmark&amp;title=Windy+Hill"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">53°37′47″N</span> <span class="longitude">2°01′07″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="vcard"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">53.62982°N 2.018561°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">53.62982; -2.018561</span></span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; (<span class="fn org">Windy Hill</span>)</span></span></span></a></span>) is the highest point of any motorway in England.<sup id="cite_ref-panoramio1_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-panoramio1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A notable structure between junctions 21 and 22 on the uphill section towards Windy Hill is the <a href="/wiki/Rakewood_Viaduct" title="Rakewood Viaduct">Rakewood Viaduct</a> which carries the road over the <a href="/wiki/Longden_End_Brook" title="Longden End Brook">Longden End Brook</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEJohnson19728_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEJohnson19728-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first section of the motorway in Yorkshire was completed between the county boundary at Windy Hill and <a href="/wiki/Outlane" title="Outlane">Outlane</a> in 1970.<sup id="cite_ref-mwdb_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mwdb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-polemoor_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-polemoor-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
To build this section, 12,000,000 cubic yards (9,200,000&#160;m<sup>3</sup>) of material was moved, 8,000,000 cubic yards (6,100,000&#160;m<sup>3</sup>) of which was solid rock and 650,000 cubic yards (500,000&#160;m<sup>3</sup>) of peat which had to be cut from the <a href="/wiki/Stratum" title="Stratum">rock strata</a> and deposited on adjacent hillsides. The geology of the moors resulted in the engineers splitting the carriageways for <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>4</sub></span> mile (1.2&#160;km) in the middle of this section, sparing Stott Hall Farm from demolition.<sup id="cite_ref-polemoor_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-polemoor-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc_fastlane_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_fastlane-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The motorway crosses <a href="/wiki/Scammonden_Reservoir" title="Scammonden Reservoir">Scammonden Dam</a> on an embankment between junctions 22 and 23. Preparatory work in the Deanhead Valley began in August 1964 and the dam in 1966. The motorway's opening on 20 December 1970 was dependent on completion of the dam.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Two other notable constructions on the Pennine section are the pedestrian bridge carrying the <a href="/wiki/Pennine_Way" title="Pennine Way">Pennine Way</a>, which is curved downwards with 85-foot (26&#160;m) long <a href="/wiki/Cantilever" title="Cantilever">cantilevers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Scammonden_Bridge" title="Scammonden Bridge">Scammonden Bridge</a>, the longest single-span non-suspension bridge in the world when it was built.<sup id="cite_ref-polemoor_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-polemoor-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Scammonden_Activity_Centre_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scammonden_Activity_Centre-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> It carries a <a href="/wiki/B_roads_in_Great_Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="B roads in Great Britain">B road</a> 120 feet (37&#160;m) above the motorway.<sup id="cite_ref-polemoor_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-polemoor-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The 1-mile (1.6&#160;km) section between Pole Moor and Outlane suffered fewer problems as the summer weather was satisfactory.<sup id="cite_ref-pmtool_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pmtool-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tnone" style="margin:0 auto;overflow:hidden;width:auto;max-width:1808px"><div class="thumbinner"><div class="noresize" style="overflow:auto"><a href="/wiki/File:M62_Scammonden_Bridge_to_Windy_Hill.jpg" class="image" title="Southerly view from Booth Moor, Calderdale, of the M62 motorway from Scammonden Bridge to Windy Hill. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Outlane_to_Lofthouse">Outlane to Lofthouse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Outlane to Lofthouse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Stopped_on_the_M1_Lofthouse_interchange_the_bridges_carry_the_M62_-_geograph.org.uk_-_722126.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Stopped_on_the_M1_Lofthouse_interchange_the_bridges_carry_the_M62_-_geograph.org.uk_-_722126.jpg/220px-Stopped_on_the_M1_Lofthouse_interchange_the_bridges_carry_the_M62_-_geograph.org.uk_-_722126.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="118" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Stopped_on_the_M1_Lofthouse_interchange_the_bridges_carry_the_M62_-_geograph.org.uk_-_722126.jpg/330px-Stopped_on_the_M1_Lofthouse_interchange_the_bridges_carry_the_M62_-_geograph.org.uk_-_722126.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Stopped_on_the_M1_Lofthouse_interchange_the_bridges_carry_the_M62_-_geograph.org.uk_-_722126.jpg/440px-Stopped_on_the_M1_Lofthouse_interchange_the_bridges_carry_the_M62_-_geograph.org.uk_-_722126.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="342" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Stopped_on_the_M1_Lofthouse_interchange_the_bridges_carry_the_M62_-_geograph.org.uk_-_722126.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Stationary traffic at Lofthouse Interchange</div></div></div> 
<p>The section of the motorway between <a href="/wiki/Gildersome" title="Gildersome">Gildersome</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lofthouse,_West_Yorkshire" title="Lofthouse, West Yorkshire">Lofthouse</a> was built at the same time, resulting in the demolition of a significant proportion of the village of <a href="/wiki/Tingley" title="Tingley">Tingley</a> to build the eponymous interchange.<sup id="cite_ref-mwdb_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mwdb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lofthouse,_West_Yorkshire" title="Lofthouse, West Yorkshire">Lofthouse</a> Interchange was built between 1965 and 1967. <a href="/wiki/Owen_Williams_(engineer)" title="Owen Williams (engineer)">Owen Williams</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Babtie,_Shaw_and_Morton" title="Babtie, Shaw and Morton">Babtie Group</a> were the engineers. Located where the M62 crosses above the <a href="/wiki/M1_motorway" title="M1 motorway">M1 motorway</a>, it is a complex three-level junction with eight bridges including a roundabout supported by four long curved bridges on 12-metre <a href="/wiki/Pier_(architecture)" title="Pier (architecture)">piers</a> above both motorways. The roundabout's north and south bridges have spans of 28 metres and the east and west have spans of 21 metres. The M62 overbridge has a <a href="/wiki/Reinforced_concrete" title="Reinforced concrete">reinforced concrete</a> multi-cellular deck of four spans. Two other pre-stressed reinforced concrete bridges carry slip roads over Longthorpe Lane, the B6135. Another bridge with no motorway access carries Longthorpe Lane over the M1.<sup id="cite_ref-ET_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ET-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two skew tunnels were constructed beneath the original junction between 1996 and 1999. The tunnels, constructed using the <a href="/wiki/Tunnel#Cut-and-cover" title="Tunnel">cut-and-cover</a> method, are for traffic travelling between the M1 south-bound and the M62 west. The tunnel under the M62 is 147 metres in length.<sup id="cite_ref-ET_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ET-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="East_of_Lofthouse">East of Lofthouse</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: East of Lofthouse">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ouse_bridge.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Ouse_bridge.jpg/220px-Ouse_bridge.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="109" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Ouse_bridge.jpg/330px-Ouse_bridge.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Ouse_bridge.jpg/440px-Ouse_bridge.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="318" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ouse_bridge.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/River_Ouse,_Yorkshire" title="River Ouse, Yorkshire">Ouse</a> Bridge, from the floodplain adjacent to the river</div></div></div>
<p>Two contracts were awarded for the section between Lofthouse and Ferrybridge in 1972, and both were completed in 1974.<sup id="cite_ref-m62lofthope_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-m62lofthope-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hopeferr_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hopeferr-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On the first contract, care was needed at the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a> crossing due to the <a href="/wiki/Alluvial" class="mw-redirect" title="Alluvial">alluvial</a> bedrock. On the second contract precautions were taken as the length was built on old <a href="/wiki/Coal_mining" title="Coal mining">coal mine</a> workings.
</p><p>The section between Ferrybridge and <a href="/wiki/North_Cave" title="North Cave">North Cave</a> was the last to be planned<sup id="cite_ref-archive_ouse_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-archive_ouse-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and built.<sup id="cite_ref-cbrd_m62_timeline_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbrd_m62_timeline-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ouse_Bridge_(M62)" title="Ouse Bridge (M62)">Ouse Bridge</a>, across the <a href="/wiki/River_Ouse,_Yorkshire" title="River Ouse, Yorkshire">River Ouse</a> west of <a href="/wiki/Goole" title="Goole">Goole</a>, commenced in January 1973 and is nearly 1 mile (1.6&#160;km) long and rises to 98.4 feet (30.0&#160;m) above ground level. Completion of the bridge was delayed due to "steel supplies [being] a chronic headache" and a partial collapse of the framework caused by bolts joining a cross-beam to a trestle shearing.<sup id="cite_ref-archive_ouse_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-archive_ouse-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Problems with the bridge delayed the opening of the section east of Goole to May 1976.<sup id="cite_ref-archive_ouse_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-archive_ouse-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cbrd_m62_timeline_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbrd_m62_timeline-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:A1(M)_and_M62_interchange.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/A1%28M%29_and_M62_interchange.jpg/220px-A1%28M%29_and_M62_interchange.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/A1%28M%29_and_M62_interchange.jpg/330px-A1%28M%29_and_M62_interchange.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/A1%28M%29_and_M62_interchange.jpg/440px-A1%28M%29_and_M62_interchange.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="479" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:A1(M)_and_M62_interchange.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Junction 32a during construction in September 2005</div></div></div>
<p>In 1987, the Department of Transport proposed a parallel relief road to combat congestion around Manchester. It would have been restricted to long distance traffic, and the current route, part of the Manchester Outer Ring Road (later the M60), used for local traffic. The proposal suggested the closure of junction&#160;13. The proposal was designated a "long term" improvement in 1994, and cancelled on 23 November 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-pathetic_relief_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pathetic_relief-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2000, the section between Eccles Interchange and Simister Interchange (junctions&#160;12 to 18) was designated the M60.<sup id="cite_ref-cbrd_m60_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbrd_m60-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Since then, two junctions were opened—in December 2002, the missing junction 8 was opened to allow access to the <a href="/wiki/A574_road" title="A574 road">A574</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Omega_Development_Site" title="Omega Development Site">Omega Development Site</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> while in January 2006, junction 32a was opened to link to the upgraded <a href="/wiki/A1_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A1 road (Great Britain)">A1(M)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hwa_newsletter_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hwa_newsletter-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The UK's first motorway <a href="/wiki/High-occupancy_vehicle_lane" title="High-occupancy vehicle lane">high-occupancy vehicle lane</a> was opened at junction&#160;26 in 2008 for eastbound traffic from the <a href="/wiki/M606_motorway" title="M606 motorway">M606</a> with more than one occupant.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Smart_motorway">Smart motorway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Smart motorway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pre-2009 proposals to widen the motorway between junctions 25 and 28 to four lanes were withdrawn in January 2009 and replaced by a project to install hard shoulder running and a <a href="/wiki/Smart_motorway" title="Smart motorway">smart motorway</a> system between junctions 25 and 30.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Work started in 2014 to install the system around the M62 – M60 section.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traffic">Traffic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Traffic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Update plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Update" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="Ambox current red.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/42px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png" decoding="async" width="42" height="34" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/63px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/84px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="360" data-file-height="290" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section needs to be <b>updated</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please update this article to reflect recent events or newly available information.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">December 2015</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:M62_traffic_statistics.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/M62_traffic_statistics.svg/220px-M62_traffic_statistics.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/M62_traffic_statistics.svg/330px-M62_traffic_statistics.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/M62_traffic_statistics.svg/440px-M62_traffic_statistics.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="840" data-file-height="520" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:M62_traffic_statistics.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1999–2006 traffic flow graph</div></div></div>
<p>The section between junctions 18 (with the <a href="/wiki/M60_motorway" title="M60 motorway">M60</a>) and 29 (with the <a href="/wiki/M1_motorway" title="M1 motorway">M1</a>) through Greater Manchester and West Yorkshire has been identified as one of the most <a href="/wiki/Traffic_congestion" title="Traffic congestion">congested</a> roads in Britain.<sup id="cite_ref-m62rbs_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-m62rbs-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The motorway provides a direct link between three of the five largest metro areas in England, and is the most practical route for HGVs and other commercial traffic between Manchester and Leeds. There are a significant amount of warehouses in these urban areas, which require commercial delivery to the ports at <a href="/wiki/Merseyside" title="Merseyside">Merseyside</a> and around the <a href="/wiki/Humber" title="Humber">Humber</a>, all of which are transported via the M62.<sup id="cite_ref-m62rbs_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-m62rbs-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Annual_average_daily_traffic" title="Annual average daily traffic">Annual average daily traffic</a> flows of 100,000 cars were recorded east of the Pennines (junction 22) in 2006 and 78,000 cars west of the Pennines.<sup id="cite_ref-dft_2006_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dft_2006-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The figures were increases from 90,000 and 70,000 respectively in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-dft_2003_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dft_2003-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> By way of comparison, the UK's busiest motorway, the <a href="/wiki/M25_motorway" title="M25 motorway">M25</a> carried 144,000 cars between junctions 7 and 23 in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-dft_2006_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dft_2006-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Stott_Hall_Farm">Stott Hall Farm</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Stott Hall Farm">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Stott_Hall_Farm_(crop).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Stott_Hall_Farm_%28crop%29.JPG/220px-Stott_Hall_Farm_%28crop%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="153" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Stott_Hall_Farm_%28crop%29.JPG/330px-Stott_Hall_Farm_%28crop%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Stott_Hall_Farm_%28crop%29.JPG/440px-Stott_Hall_Farm_%28crop%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1537" data-file-height="1071" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Stott_Hall_Farm_(crop).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Stott Hall Farm, viewed from moorland above westbound carriageway</div></div></div>
<p>Stott Hall Farm, (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=M62_motorway&amp;params=53.641599_N_1.952222_W_region:GB_type:landmark&amp;title=Stott+Hall+Farm"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">53°38′30″N</span> <span class="longitude">1°57′08″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="vcard"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">53.641599°N 1.952222°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">53.641599; -1.952222</span></span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; (<span class="fn org">Stott Hall Farm</span>)</span></span></span></a></span>) the only farm in the UK situated in the middle of a motorway,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> was built in the 18th century on Moss Moor. It lies south of <a href="/wiki/Booth_Wood_Reservoir" title="Booth Wood Reservoir">Booth Wood Reservoir</a> where the carriageways are separated between junctions 22 and 23.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_fastlane_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_fastlane-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-life_fastlane_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-life_fastlane-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The road divides for much of its length between the Windy Hill and Deanhead cuttings because of the surrounding geography but a <a href="/wiki/Urban_legend" title="Urban legend">myth</a><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> persists that it was split because the owners Ken and Beth Wild refused to sell.<sup id="cite_ref-life_fastlane_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-life_fastlane-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sally_Boazman" title="Sally Boazman">Sally Boazman</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_2" title="BBC Radio 2">BBC Radio 2</a>'s traffic reporter and CB radio users nicknamed it the <a href="/wiki/Little_House_on_the_Prairie" title="Little House on the Prairie">Little House on the Prairie</a>. It is separated from the motorway by crash barriers and a fence to keep livestock in and prevent out-of-control vehicles crashing onto the property.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_fastlane_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_fastlane-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mitm_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mitm-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> It is one of the ten best-known sights on the motorway network.<sup id="cite_ref-gravelly_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gravelly-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mitm_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mitm-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> It was the subject of a <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a> documentary, a <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Yorkshire Television">Yorkshire Television</a> documentary<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> and a short film.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_incidents">Major incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Major incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 4 February 1974, <a href="/wiki/M62_coach_bombing" title="M62 coach bombing">a bomb was detonated on a coach</a> travelling between Chain Bar (junction&#160;26) and Gildersome (junction&#160;27). The coach was transporting off-duty <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">army personnel</a> and their family members. Twelve people were killed and 38 were injured. <a href="/wiki/Hartshead_Moor_services" title="Hartshead Moor services">Hartshead Moor services</a> was used as a makeshift hospital and base for investigations.<sup id="cite_ref-coach_new_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coach_new-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Provisional_Irish_Republican_Army" title="Provisional Irish Republican Army">Provisional Irish Republican Army</a> was deemed responsible.<sup id="cite_ref-coach_new_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coach_new-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-coach_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coach-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> A memorial to the victims was erected at Hartshead Moor services in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Great_Heck_rail_crash" title="Great Heck rail crash">Great Heck rail crash</a> happened on 28 February 2001, at 06:13 after Gary Hart, a <a href="/wiki/Sleep_deprivation" title="Sleep deprivation">sleep-deprived</a> driver, swerved off the M62 onto the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Main_Line" title="East Coast Main Line">East Coast Main Line</a> near <a href="/wiki/Selby" title="Selby">Selby</a>. While he was calling the <a href="/wiki/Emergency_service" title="Emergency service">emergency services</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Great_North_Eastern_Railway" title="Great North Eastern Railway">GNER</a> southbound train collided with his <a href="/wiki/Land_Rover" title="Land Rover">Land Rover</a> and derailed into the path of an oncoming <a href="/wiki/Rail_freight_transport" title="Rail freight transport">goods train</a>. Ten people were killed, including the drivers of both trains, and 82 others were injured.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 March 2018, a multi-vehicle crash in severe weather conditions (<a href="/wiki/2018_Great_Britain_and_Ireland_cold_wave" title="2018 Great Britain and Ireland cold wave">the beast from the East</a>) caused up to 3,500 vehicles to become trapped on the eastbound Pennine section between Junctions 20 and 24. Up to 200 people, including a baby, spent the night in their vehicles. The military, <a href="/wiki/Mountain_rescue_in_England_and_Wales" title="Mountain rescue in England and Wales">mountain rescue</a>, fire services and <a href="/wiki/Highways_England" title="Highways England">Highways Agency</a> worked alongside the police through the night to ensure people's safety. A barrier between the carriageways was removed to facilitate moving most of the vehicles. The road remained closed the next day due to the weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=M62_motorway&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_of_the_M62_motorway_-_section.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Map_of_the_M62_motorway_-_section.svg/220px-Map_of_the_M62_motorway_-_section.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="83" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Map_of_the_M62_motorway_-_section.svg/330px-Map_of_the_M62_motorway_-_section.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Map_of_the_M62_motorway_-_section.svg/440px-Map_of_the_M62_motorway_-_section.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="443" data-file-height="168" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_of_the_M62_motorway_-_section.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The M62's route in relation to the four major cities it serves: Liverpool, Manchester, Leeds and Hull</div></div></div>
<p>In addition to passing <a href="/wiki/Warrington" title="Warrington">Warrington</a>, Manchester, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, Leeds and <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>, the towns of Huyton, <a href="/wiki/St_Helens,_Merseyside" title="St Helens, Merseyside">St Helens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Widnes" title="Widnes">Widnes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bury,_Greater_Manchester" title="Bury, Greater Manchester">Bury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rochdale" title="Rochdale">Rochdale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oldham" title="Oldham">Oldham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pontefract" title="Pontefract">Pontefract</a>, Selby and Goole are designated <a href="/wiki/Roads_in_the_United_Kingdom#Primary_destinations" title="Roads in the United Kingdom">primary destinations</a> along the road. The M62 is a terminus to two motorways: the M57 near <a href="/wiki/Prescot" title="Prescot">Prescot</a> and the <a href="/wiki/M18_motorway_(Great_Britain)" title="M18 motorway (Great Britain)">M18</a> near <a href="/wiki/Rawcliffe,_East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="Rawcliffe, East Riding of Yorkshire">Rawcliffe</a>; and has four spur routes: the <a href="/wiki/M602_motorway" title="M602 motorway">M602</a>, which serves Manchester, the <a href="/wiki/A627(M)_motorway" title="A627(M) motorway">A627(M)</a>, which serves Oldham and Rochdale, the <a href="/wiki/M606_motorway" title="M606 motorway">M606</a>, which serves Bradford, and the <a href="/wiki/M621_motorway" title="M621 motorway">M621</a>, which serves Leeds. Despite Hull being listed as a primary destination,<sup id="cite_ref-Streetview_-_J30_ADS_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Streetview_-_J30_ADS-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> the motorway downgrades near North Cave, 16 miles (26&#160;km) to the west.<sup id="cite_ref-mwdb_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mwdb-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Streetview_-_End_Of_Motorway_Sign_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Streetview_-_End_Of_Motorway_Sign-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
The motorway starts at Queen's Drive, on Liverpool's middle ring road from where it runs eastward to the outer ring road, the <a href="/wiki/M57_motorway" title="M57 motorway">M57</a>. The route has four exits for Warrington: junction 7, an interchange with the <a href="/wiki/A57_road" title="A57 road">A57 road</a>, junction 8, which also houses <a href="/wiki/IKEA" title="IKEA">IKEA</a>, junction 9, which interchanges with the <a href="/wiki/A49_road" title="A49 road">A49 road</a>, which was intended to be a motorway, and junction 11. Between these is junction 10, which is a <a href="/wiki/Partial_cloverleaf_interchange#Clover-stack" title="Partial cloverleaf interchange">cloverstack interchange</a> with the M6. The M62 crosses <a href="/wiki/Chat_Moss" title="Chat Moss">Chat Moss</a> before interchanging with the <a href="/wiki/M60_motorway" title="M60 motorway">M60 motorway</a>. Owing to the original plan to extend this section of the motorway into Manchester, motorists must turn off to stay on (a <a href="/wiki/TOTSO" class="mw-redirect" title="TOTSO">TOTSO</a>) the route into Yorkshire.
</p><p>In Greater Manchester, the motorway shares seven junctions, 12 to 18, with the M60 motorway. Junction 13, signposted <a href="/wiki/Swinton,_Greater_Manchester" title="Swinton, Greater Manchester">Swinton</a>, is situated <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>2</sub></span> mile (0.80&#160;km) from junction 12, leaving exiting motorists the hazard of crossing the still-merging M62 traffic.<sup id="cite_ref-simunj13_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-simunj13-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
Worsley Braided Interchange serves Junctions 14 and 15 and Junctions 1 to 3 of the <a href="/wiki/M61_motorway" title="M61 motorway">M61</a> which terminates to <a href="/wiki/Preston,_Lancashire" title="Preston, Lancashire">Preston</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:M62_motorway,_Calderdale.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/M62_motorway%2C_Calderdale.jpg/220px-M62_motorway%2C_Calderdale.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/M62_motorway%2C_Calderdale.jpg/330px-M62_motorway%2C_Calderdale.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/M62_motorway%2C_Calderdale.jpg/440px-M62_motorway%2C_Calderdale.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="428" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:M62_motorway,_Calderdale.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>M62 passes Scammonden Water in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a></div></div></div>
<p>Between junction 21 and junction 22, the motorway has four lanes eastbound to climb Windy Hill,<sup id="cite_ref-Streetview_-_lane_gain_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Streetview_-_lane_gain-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> before crossing the border into Yorkshire and interchanging with the rural A672 road, reaching the highest point of any motorway in England 1,221 feet (372&#160;m).<sup id="cite_ref-panoramio1_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-panoramio1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> There is then a 7-mile (11&#160;km) travel through the Pennines to the next junction, passing <a href="/wiki/Scammonden_Reservoir" title="Scammonden Reservoir">Scammonden Reservoir</a> and Stott Hall Farm. The next junction is 23, which is accessible only for westbound traffic. After this, the road dips through a valley to junction 24 and drops slowly before interchanging with the A644 road at junction 25. Between junctions 22 and 25, the road is used as a border between the metropolitan boroughs of <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cmbc_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cmbc-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At junction 26, Chain Bar, the motorway interchanges with several roads: the <a href="/wiki/M606_motorway" title="M606 motorway">M606</a>, a spur into Bradford, the <a href="/wiki/A58_road" title="A58 road">A58 road</a>, which runs between <a href="/wiki/Prescot" title="Prescot">Prescot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wetherby" title="Wetherby">Wetherby</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/A638_road" title="A638 road">A638 road</a>, which runs to <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a>, then follows the old route of the A1 through <a href="/wiki/Bawtry" title="Bawtry">Bawtry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Retford" title="Retford">Retford</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Markham_Moor" title="Markham Moor">Markham Moor</a> where it rejoins the A1. The next junction also serves a spur route: the M621 motorway, before bypassing Leeds to the south to the interchange with the M1 motorway, Lofthouse Interchange, at junction 29. East of Leeds, the motorway serves Wakefield at junction 30 and crosses by the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a>. At junction 32a, the road is crossed by the <a href="/wiki/A1(M)_motorway" title="A1(M) motorway">A1(M) motorway</a>, which also runs parallel to it for a short distance. The next junction 33 serves the A162 and A1 roads, and Ferrybridge service station. After Ferrybridge, the motorway becomes relatively flat, except for a 1-mile (1.6&#160;km) bridge that crosses the <a href="/wiki/River_Ouse,_Yorkshire" title="River Ouse, Yorkshire">River Ouse</a>. For approximately 10 miles (16&#160;km) after this, the road runs towards Hull, serving <a href="/wiki/Howden" title="Howden">Howden</a> and North Cave, before downgrading to the <a href="/wiki/A63_road" title="A63 road">A63 road</a>.
</p>
