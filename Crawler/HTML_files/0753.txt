<p><b>Wm Morrison Supermarkets plc</b>, trading as <b>Morrisons</b>, is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_supermarket_chains_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of supermarket chains in the United Kingdom">fourth largest chain</a> of <a href="/wiki/Supermarkets" class="mw-redirect" title="Supermarkets">supermarkets</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Founded in 1899 by <a href="/wiki/William_Morrison_(businessman)" title="William Morrison (businessman)">William Morrison</a>, hence the abbreviation Wm Morrison, it began as an egg and butter stall in Rawson Market, Bradford, England. Until 2004, Morrisons store locations were primarily focused in the <a href="/wiki/North_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="North of England">North of England</a>, but with the takeover of <a href="/wiki/Safeway_plc" class="mw-redirect" title="Safeway plc">Safeway</a> in that year, the company's presence increased significantly in the <a href="/wiki/South_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="South of England">South of England</a>, Wales and Scotland. As of 2016 the company had 498 superstores across England, Wales and Scotland,<sup id="cite_ref-stores_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stores-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> as well as one in <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a>, which is the chain's only store outside of <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of April 2019, Morrisons employs 110,000 employees and serves around 11 million customers each week.<sup id="cite_ref-aboutus_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aboutus-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Morrisons' market share in August 2017 was 10.14%, down 0.86% from 2015<sup id="cite_ref-business-reporter1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-business-reporter1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>—behind <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a> (27.8%), <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury&#39;s">Sainsbury's</a> (15.8%) and <a href="/wiki/Asda" title="Asda">Asda</a> (15.3%) but ahead of <a href="/wiki/Aldi" title="Aldi">Aldi</a> (7%).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Snapshot_of_the_market_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snapshot_of_the_market-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The company is listed on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a> and is part of the <a href="/wiki/FTSE_100_Index" title="FTSE 100 Index">FTSE 100 Index</a> of companies. The Morrison family currently owns around 10% of the company.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding">Founding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Founding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The company was founded by <a href="/wiki/William_Morrison_(businessman)" title="William Morrison (businessman)">William Morrison</a> in 1899 who started the business as an egg and butter merchant in Rawson Market, <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, England, operating under the name of <i>Wm Morrison Limited</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-student_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-student-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His son <a href="/wiki/Ken_Morrison" title="Ken Morrison">Ken Morrison</a> took over the company in 1952, aged 21. In 1958, Morrisons opened a small shop in the city centre.<sup id="cite_ref-student_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-student-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It was the first self-service store in Bradford, the first store to have prices on its products, and it had three checkouts. The company opened its first supermarket, "Victoria", in the Girlington district of Bradford in 1961.<sup id="cite_ref-student_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-student-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1967, Morrisons became a <a href="/wiki/Public_limited_company" title="Public limited company">public limited company</a> listed on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-student_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-student-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_of_Safeway">Acquisition of Safeway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acquisition of Safeway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Morrisons_Supermarket_-_geograph.org.uk_-_209625.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Morrisons_Supermarket_-_geograph.org.uk_-_209625.jpg/220px-Morrisons_Supermarket_-_geograph.org.uk_-_209625.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="164" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Morrisons_Supermarket_-_geograph.org.uk_-_209625.jpg/330px-Morrisons_Supermarket_-_geograph.org.uk_-_209625.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Morrisons_Supermarket_-_geograph.org.uk_-_209625.jpg/440px-Morrisons_Supermarket_-_geograph.org.uk_-_209625.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="477" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Morrisons_Supermarket_-_geograph.org.uk_-_209625.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Morrisons supermarket in <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes" title="Milton Keynes">Milton Keynes</a> (formerly a Safeway store)</div></div></div>
<p>In March 2004 Morrisons, acquired <a href="/wiki/Safeway_(UK)" title="Safeway (UK)">Safeway</a>, a British supermarket chain which owned 479 stores, allowing Morrisons to have a larger presence in southern England.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The company was purchased for £3.3 billion, comprising 1 new Morrisons share (enabling Safeway shareholders to have a 40% stake in the enlarged group and reducing the Morrison family's shareholding to 18%), plus 60 pence in cash (paid for by the divestment of 52 overlapping stores) for each Safeway share held. The acquisition quickly ran into difficulties caused in part by Safeway UK's outgoing management changing that chain's accounting systems six weeks before the transaction was completed.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The result was a series of profit warnings being issued by Morrisons, poor financial results<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and a reversion to manual systems.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The programme of store conversions from Safeway to Morrisons was the largest of its kind in British retail history, focusing initially on the retained stores which were <a href="/wiki/Freehold_(real_property)" class="mw-redirect" title="Freehold (real property)">freehold</a>, over 25,000&#160;sq&#160;ft (2,300&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) with separate car parks. Within a few weeks, Safeway carrier bags were replaced by those of Morrisons and Morrisons own-brand products began to appear in Safeway stores.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Originally 52 shops were to be compulsorily divested after the takeover, but this was reduced to 50 after one Safeway store in <a href="/wiki/Sunderland,_Tyne_and_Wear" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunderland, Tyne and Wear">Sunderland</a> was destroyed by fire and the lease ended on another in <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> city centre. <a href="/wiki/John_Lewis_Partnership" title="John Lewis Partnership">John Lewis Partnership</a> purchased 19 to be part of its <a href="/wiki/Waitrose" class="mw-redirect" title="Waitrose">Waitrose</a> chain,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/J_Sainsbury_plc" class="mw-redirect" title="J Sainsbury plc">J Sainsbury plc</a> purchased a further 14,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and Tesco bought 10 in October 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-UK_Business_Park_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UK_Business_Park-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
At the time Morrisons chose not to move into the <a href="/wiki/Convenience_store" title="Convenience store">convenience store</a> sector (although it has since done so with its <a href="/wiki/Morrisons#Convenience_stores" title="Morrisons">M Local</a> stores). Further to this policy decision, it was announced in late 2004 that the 114 smaller 'Safeway Compact' stores would be sold off to rival supermarket chain <a href="/wiki/Somerfield" title="Somerfield">Somerfield</a> in a two-part deal worth £260.2&#160;million in total.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> Morrisons sold the <a href="/wiki/Safeway_Stores_(Ireland)" title="Safeway Stores (Ireland)">Safeway</a> stores to Asda. This included a store in <a href="/wiki/Bangor,_County_Down" title="Bangor, County Down">Bangor</a> that opened after the Morrisons takeover.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>One of the largest single purchases in 2005 was that of five stores by Waitrose.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On 18 July 2006, a further six stores from the 'Rump' format were sold to Waitrose, including the former Safeway store in <a href="/wiki/Hexham" title="Hexham">Hexham</a>, Northumberland, which became England's most northerly Waitrose branch.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2005, Morrisons announced the closure of Safeway's joint venture convenience store/petrol station format with <a href="/wiki/BP" title="BP">BP</a>. Under the deal, the premises had been split 50/50 between the two companies.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Morrisons also sold Safeway's <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a> stores, in <a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Guernsey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a>, to <a href="/wiki/Sandpiper_CI" title="Sandpiper CI">CI Traders</a> where the stores continued to trade as <a href="/wiki/Safeway_(Channel_Islands)" title="Safeway (Channel Islands)">Safeway</a>, although the products they sold carried the brand names of chains such as <a href="/wiki/Iceland_(supermarket)" title="Iceland (supermarket)">Iceland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, Sandpiper CI/CI Traders sold the Channel Island Safeway stores to <a href="/wiki/Waitrose" class="mw-redirect" title="Waitrose">Waitrose</a> and the Safeway brand disappeared from the Channel Islands.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>, the Douglas store was sold to <a href="/wiki/Shoprite_(Isle_of_Man)" title="Shoprite (Isle of Man)">Shoprite</a> and the Ramsey store was sold to <a href="/wiki/The_Co-operative_Food" class="mw-redirect" title="The Co-operative Food">The Co-operative Food</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a> store was originally marketed for sale, but has now been converted under the 'Rump' format. In November 2006, plans were submitted for the extension and redevelopment of the store in order to introduce the full Morrisons format.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2005 the company announced the closure of former Safeway depots in Kent, Bristol and Warrington with the loss of 2,500 jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The Kent depot has since been sold to upmarket rival <a href="/wiki/Waitrose" class="mw-redirect" title="Waitrose">Waitrose</a>, whilst Warrington was sold to frozen food rival <a href="/wiki/Iceland_(supermarket)" title="Iceland (supermarket)">Iceland</a>. Part of the Bristol depot has been sold off to Gist.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
The store conversion process was completed on 24 November 2005 when the final Safeway fascia disappeared from the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Optimisation_Plan">Optimisation Plan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Optimisation Plan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the acquisition of Safeway, Morrisons encountered a number of difficulties. The company had issued five profits warnings since the acquisition, and it was felt that the original Morrisons northern format did not work as well in some of the former Safeway stores in the south. To reinvigorate its new national image, Morrisons appointed the Dutchman <a href="/wiki/Marc_Bolland" title="Marc Bolland">Marc Bolland</a> (the Chief Operating Officer of <a href="/wiki/Heineken_N.V." title="Heineken N.V.">Heineken</a>), as its new Chief Executive.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement_of_Sir_Ken_Morrison">Retirement of Sir Ken Morrison</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Retirement of Sir Ken Morrison">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 13 March 2008 <a href="/wiki/Ken_Morrison" title="Ken Morrison">Sir Ken Morrison</a> retired as Chairman after 55 years at the company and was made Honorary President.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Purchase_of_Co-op_and_Somerfield_stores">Purchase of Co-op and Somerfield stores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Purchase of Co-op and Somerfield stores">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When the <a href="/wiki/Co-operative_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Co-operative Group">Co-operative Group</a> completed its takeover of the <a href="/wiki/Somerfield" title="Somerfield">Somerfield</a> supermarket chain in March 2009, it was required to sell a number of stores by the <a href="/wiki/Competition_Commission" title="Competition Commission">Competition Commission</a>. Morrisons purchased 35 stores from the combined group, mostly trading under the Somerfield fascia.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> These new stores were the first of more than 100 identified by Morrisons for expansion into smaller supermarkets as it aims to have a store within 15 minutes of every UK home.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Multi-channel_diversification">Multi-channel diversification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Multi-channel diversification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Following <a href="/wiki/Marc_Bolland" title="Marc Bolland">Marc Bolland</a>'s departure to become the CEO of <a href="/wiki/Marks_%26_Spencer" title="Marks &amp; Spencer">Marks &amp; Spencer</a> in December 2009, <a href="/wiki/Dalton_Philips" title="Dalton Philips">Dalton Philips</a> was appointed in a surprise move as his replacement in January 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC8483863_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC8483863-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, Morrisons signed a deal with budget retailer <a href="/wiki/Peacocks_(clothing)" title="Peacocks (clothing)">Peacocks</a>, the first concession store opened as part of a refurbishment at the retailer's store in Idle, Bradford.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The Peacocks section was rolled out into other stores before launching its own childrenswear brand 'Nutmeg' into 85 stores on 21 March 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first Morrisons M local store opened in <a href="/wiki/Ilkley" title="Ilkley">Ilkley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> in 2011. However, all M Local stores were later rebranded under the short-lived "<a href="/wiki/My_Local" title="My Local">My Local</a>" chain in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2013, Morrisons announced a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Ocado" title="Ocado">Ocado</a> to use its technology systems and distribution infrastructure to help launch its own online service.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Richard Pennycook, who had joined Morrisons in October 2005,<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> was replaced as Chief Financial Officer at Morrisons in June 2013 by Trevor Strain, previously Finance Director Corporate.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Potential_private_equity_takeover_bid">Potential private equity takeover bid</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Potential private equity takeover bid">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2014, it emerged that younger members of the founding Morrison family, who own 10% of the company and who are thought to include two of Honorary President Sir <a href="/wiki/Ken_Morrison" title="Ken Morrison">Ken Morrison</a>'s children, William Morrison Junior and Andrea Shelley, along with Sir Ken Morrison's niece and her husband, Susan and Nigel Pritchard, had approached a number of <a href="/wiki/Private_equity_firm" title="Private equity firm">private equity firms</a> about taking the company private. They were said to be extremely unhappy about the company's disastrous financial performance, and the corporate strategy being undertaken by <a href="/wiki/Dalton_Philips" title="Dalton Philips">Dalton Philips</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_criticism_by_the_Morrison_family">Public criticism by the Morrison family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Public criticism by the Morrison family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following a new three-year corporate strategy revealed in March 2014 aimed at recovering sales and market share, at Morrisons Annual General Meeting in June 2014, Morrisons former chairman Sir <a href="/wiki/Ken_Morrison" title="Ken Morrison">Ken Morrison</a> blasted Dalton Philips and his new board of directors for destroying the company he inherited from his father; Morrison remarked on Philips's strategy to save the failing supermarket from the pressures of <a href="/wiki/Aldi" title="Aldi">Aldi</a> and other discounter stores, stating "<i>When I left work and started working as a hobby, I chose to raise cattle. I have something like 1,000 bullocks and, having listened to your presentation, Dalton, you've got a lot more bull **** than me."</i><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Morrison's comments were backed up by his nephew Chris Blundell, who controls most of the remaining family stake in the supermarket, who also told the board it needed rescuing, and welcomed the decision by chairman Sir Ian Gibson to leave the business next year (in June 2015) after months of pressure.<sup id="cite_ref-Independent_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Independent-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Restructuring_plans">Restructuring plans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Restructuring plans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2014, Morrisons announced that there are plans put in place to cut 2,600 jobs as a result of changes to its management structure.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Morrisons stated that it had trialled the new structure and believed that better performance was achieved via these methods. However, these cuts would primarily affect department manager and supervisory positions. Morrisons would create 1,000 jobs in Morrisons M local convenience stores and 3,000 in new supermarkets. Following this, Morrisons sold its distribution centre in Kent to a real estate investment company for £97.8&#160;million. In turn, the depot in Kemsley, will be immediately leased back to the supermarket chain on a 25-year agreement with a £5.4&#160;million rental fee per annum.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Change_of_leadership_again">Change of leadership again</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Change of leadership again">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following a 3.1% drop in like-for-like sales in the Christmas 2014 trading period, Morrisons announced the widely expected resignation of the heavily publicly criticised Chief Executive of five years, Dalton Philips, to become effective in March 2015. In addition, the Chairman <a href="/wiki/Ian_Gibson_(businessman)" title="Ian Gibson (businessman)">Sir Ian Gibson</a> would stand down six months early to be replaced by former <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a> Chief Financial Officer <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Higginson_(businessman)" title="Andrew Higginson (businessman)">Andrew Higginson</a> at the end of January 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Five additional executives exited the company in March 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Morrisons also announced the closure of 10 small loss-making stores (eight former <a href="/wiki/Netto_UK" title="Netto UK">Netto UK</a> stores and two former <a href="/wiki/Somerfield" title="Somerfield">Somerfield</a> stores, bought under Philips's leadership) in <a href="/wiki/Cramlington" title="Cramlington">Cramlington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Accrington" title="Accrington">Accrington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ravensthorpe,_West_Yorkshire" title="Ravensthorpe, West Yorkshire">Ravensthorpe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bransholme" title="Bransholme">Bransholme</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Hull</a>), <a href="/wiki/Telford" title="Telford">Telford</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich" title="West Bromwich">West Bromwich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wallasey" title="Wallasey">Wallasey</a> (<a href="/wiki/Seacombe" title="Seacombe">Seacombe</a>—store pictured on the right), <a href="/wiki/Newton_le_Willows" class="mw-redirect" title="Newton le Willows">Newton le Willows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rugby,_Warwickshire" title="Rugby, Warwickshire">Rugby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crawley" title="Crawley">Crawley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> In addition, six unprofitable convenience stores would close, and the roll-out of the convenience store chain would be slowed, as a batch of 40 sites would no longer be bought.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>On 25 February 2015, Morrisons named former Tesco director <a href="/wiki/David_Potts_(businessman)" title="David Potts (businessman)">David Potts</a> as its new chief executive.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2015, Morrisons cut the price of 200 'everyday items' by up to 33%<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> The store chain's like-for-like sales had fallen by 2.9% in the first three months of 2015—after falling 2.6% in the last three months of 2014. The company responded by deciding to 'simplify' its head office in <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>—at the cost of 720 jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2015, Morrisons announced the sale of its 140 M Local stores to Mike Greene and <a href="/wiki/Greybull_Capital" title="Greybull Capital">Greybull Capital</a>, to be re-branded <a href="/wiki/My_Local" title="My Local">My Local</a>, for £25&#160;million<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> and that it planned to close 11 supermarkets, costing a reported 900 jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Following on from this in January 2016 Morrisons bosses announced that a further 7 stores would be closing to help optimise their existing assets and address areas of underperformance.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of February 2016, Morrisons has 498 superstores<sup id="cite_ref-stores_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stores-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, including those it retained following its purchase of Safeway plc. </p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Store_formats">Store formats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Store formats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The traditional format of Morrisons superstores is called <i>Market Street</i>. The meat is near or next to the butcher's counter, the <a href="/wiki/Delicatessen" title="Delicatessen">delicatessen</a> being traditionally named <i>Provisions</i> with cheese fridge nearby and a rottisserie counter named <i>Oven Fresh</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_retail">Online retail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Online retail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, the group launched its first retail website called "Morrisons Cellar" selling wine from around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Unlike its major competitors, Morrisons has only recently branched towards offering an online shopping service. In May 2013 Morrisons announced a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Ocado" title="Ocado">Ocado</a> to use its technology systems and distribution infrastructure to help launch its own online service.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> Morrisons also signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">Amazon</a> to supply products for their <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Prime_Pantry" title="Amazon Prime Pantry">Prime Pantry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Market_share">Market share</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Market share">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In mid-2015, Morrisons was still the smallest of the 'big four' supermarkets—with a market share of 11%, down 0.3% from 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-business-reporter1_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-business-reporter1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to <a href="/wiki/CACI" title="CACI">CACI</a>, as of 2006, Morrisons had market dominance in 10 postcode areas; <a href="/wiki/SY_postcode_area" title="SY postcode area">SY</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shrewsbury" title="Shrewsbury">Shrewsbury</a>), <a href="/wiki/LD_postcode_area" title="LD postcode area">LD</a> (<a href="/wiki/Llandrindod_Wells" title="Llandrindod Wells">Llandrindod Wells</a>), <a href="/wiki/WS_postcode_area" title="WS postcode area">WS</a> (<a href="/wiki/Walsall" title="Walsall">Walsall</a>),
<a href="/wiki/TS_postcode_area" title="TS postcode area">TS</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cleveland,_England" title="Cleveland, England">Cleveland</a>), <a href="/wiki/TD_postcode_area" title="TD postcode area">TD</a> (<a href="/wiki/Hawick" title="Hawick">Hawick</a>), <a href="/wiki/BD_postcode_area" title="BD postcode area">BD</a> (<a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>), <a href="/wiki/HG_postcode_area" title="HG postcode area">HG</a> (<a href="/wiki/Harrogate" title="Harrogate">Harrogate</a>), <a href="/wiki/LS_postcode_area" title="LS postcode area">LS</a> (<a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>), <a href="/wiki/WF_postcode_area" title="WF postcode area">WF</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>) and <a href="/wiki/HD_postcode_area" title="HD postcode area">HD</a> (<a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vertical_integration">Vertical integration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Vertical integration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Unlike the other UK supermarkets, Morrisons manufacture a substantial volume of their own food products at 16 sites in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_operations">Former operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Former operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kiddicare">Kiddicare</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Kiddicare">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, Morrisons bought children's retailer <a href="/wiki/Kiddicare" title="Kiddicare">Kiddicare</a> for £70m to give it the knowledge to sell clothing and homewares online.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> In 2012 10 former <a href="/wiki/Best_Buy_Europe" title="Best Buy Europe">Best Buy</a> stores from the <a href="/wiki/Carphone_Warehouse" title="Carphone Warehouse">Carphone Warehouse</a> were acquired to expand Kiddicare into retail stores.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> In March 2014 Morrisons CEO Dalton Phillips announced the company's intention of selling Kiddicare. The company was sold to the Endless private equity firm for £2 million in July 2014, only to be sold on to Worldstores two months later (in September 2014) for an undisclosed sum.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="FreshDirect">FreshDirect</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: FreshDirect">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Morrisons purchased a 10% stake in New York-based online grocer <a href="/wiki/FreshDirect" title="FreshDirect">FreshDirect</a> for £31 million in 2011. After having sent a team to New York to learn from the business ahead of the predicted launch in 2013, Morrisons began a home delivery initiative in January 2014. In March 2014 Morrisons CEO Dalton Phillips announced the company had agreed to sell its stake in FreshDirect due to financial difficulties the company was facing and, as it had set up its own online shopping site, it no longer needed FreshDirect.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> The sale was completed in August 2016 for £45 million.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Convenience_stores">Convenience stores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Convenience stores">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Morrisons_M_Local_store,_Church_Street,_Liverpool.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Morrisons_M_Local_store%2C_Church_Street%2C_Liverpool.jpg/220px-Morrisons_M_Local_store%2C_Church_Street%2C_Liverpool.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Morrisons_M_Local_store%2C_Church_Street%2C_Liverpool.jpg/330px-Morrisons_M_Local_store%2C_Church_Street%2C_Liverpool.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Morrisons_M_Local_store%2C_Church_Street%2C_Liverpool.jpg/440px-Morrisons_M_Local_store%2C_Church_Street%2C_Liverpool.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4608" data-file-height="3456" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Morrisons_M_Local_store,_Church_Street,_Liverpool.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Morrisons M local, Church Street, <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a></div></div></div>
<p>The company operated a number of smaller stores called "Morrisons M Local" in major places such as <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bristol" title="Bristol">Bristol</a>. These stores had a similar format to small <a href="/wiki/Tesco_Express" class="mw-redirect" title="Tesco Express">Tesco Express</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s_Local" title="Sainsbury&#39;s Local">Sainsbury's Local</a> stores, but included a wider range of ready-to-eat hot food such as pastries, coffee, rotisserie, porridge and also a salad bar. Items are stocked from nearby superstores and shoppers can also order foods in including fresh meat and fish.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A distribution centre in <a href="/wiki/Feltham" title="Feltham">Feltham</a>, West London was acquired to provide a distribution network to the stores in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_East_England" title="South East England">the South East</a> where there are few superstores.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Around 70 stores were opened by the end of 2013, which was boosted by the purchase of 7 <a href="/wiki/Jessops" title="Jessops">Jessops</a> and 49 <a href="/wiki/Blockbuster_LLC" title="Blockbuster LLC">Blockbuster</a> stores from administrators.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> On 26 February 2013, a further 6 <a href="/wiki/HMV" title="HMV">HMV</a> stores were acquired from administrators.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> The M Local chain was sold to a private equity group in 2015 and rebranded <a href="/wiki/My_Local" title="My Local">My Local</a>, but entered administration itself less than a year later.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing_and_branding">Marketing and branding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Marketing and branding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Logos_and_slogans">Logos and slogans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Logos and slogans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 15 March 2007, Morrisons announced that it would cease to use its existing branding and instead use a "more modern brand image". Their lower price option brand, <i>Bettabuy</i>, was also changed to a "more modern" brand called the <i>Morrisons Value</i> range.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The change saw the replacement of the old yellow and black logo, with the 'More Reasons To Shop At Morrisons' strap line replaced with 'Fresh Choice For You'. In 2010 this was replaced by 'Eat Fresh. Pay Less'. This was later changed again in 2013 to 'More Of What Matters'. It also involved the replacement of external signage, with the previous <i>Morrisons</i> signs being retained alongside the new logo, as well as changes to product packaging, point of sale, advertising, staff uniforms (replacing the old blue ties and bows with green ones) and distribution vehicles. The rationale behind the decision was the need for Morrisons to attract a wider national customer base, capitalising on its expanded geographical spread following the acquisition of Safeway.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, Morrisons released a new logo and slogan 'Morrisons Makes It' to try and draw on the brand's heritage, with the new logo being installed on all store signs as well as new uniforms and new in-store looks. As well as this, the capital M, as seen on the top of the other logos, is removed and instead replaced at the bottom, 'Since 1899' and above the I, are yellow flower petals. As well as this, all of the letters, apart from the M, are all turned into lowercase letters.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loyalty_card">Loyalty card</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Loyalty card">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Morrisons More card price matched the chain's customers' comparable grocery shopping in store and online with <a href="/wiki/Aldi" title="Aldi">Aldi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lidl" title="Lidl">Lidl</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury&#39;s">Sainsbury's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asda" title="Asda">Asda</a>. If a customer spent £15 or more and could have paid less for their comparable groceries, Morrisons automatically gave them the difference in points on their card at the checkout. For 1p difference in the cost of shopping, customers got 10 Match points—and for £1 difference they got 1,000 points. The difference was calculated at the checkout on national brands and comparable own label products and fresh food, even those that are on promotion elsewhere.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> In 2016 the Match &amp; More loyalty cards were rebranded as the "More" loyalty card and all customers were issued a new card in line with the rebranding.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Product_ranges">Product ranges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Product ranges">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Morrisons stocks thousands of lines which are sold as their "Own Brand" goods. These include M Savers, an economy brand which sells items ranging from food and drink to toiletries, currently the UK's fastest growing grocery brand.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>In 2005, Morrisons purchased part of the collapsed <a href="/wiki/Rathbones_Bakeries" title="Rathbones Bakeries">Rathbones Bakeries</a>, which supplied Morrisons bread, for £15.5&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Morrisons opened a new Distribution Centre in Swindon<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup> and announced that it had bought a new site on Junction 23 of the M5 in <a href="/wiki/Bridgwater" title="Bridgwater">Bridgwater</a> in <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>, for redevelopment as a fresh produce packing facility.<sup id="cite_ref-UK_Business_Park_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UK_Business_Park-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, Morrisons opened a new 767,500 sq/foot distribution centre in Bridgwater as part of the £11&#160;million redevelopment project. This project also created 1,200 new jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup> The opening of the new distribution centre meant that the Swindon depot was no longer required and it was closed in December 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">&#91;85&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Potentially_harmful_dog_feeding">Potentially harmful dog feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Potentially harmful dog feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2012 a television advertising campaign which showed a child giving a dog pieces of Christmas pudding was criticised by the British Veterinary Association and the Kennel Club. Christmas pudding contains ingredients which can be harmful to dogs which led to concern that the behaviour in the clip could be copied with detrimental consequences for animals. A spokesman for Morrisons stated that it had sought veterinary advice before the shoot, at which a vet was present. Advice given was that "...there would be minimal, if any, risk to a dog of serious toxic reaction should a small amount, in relation to its body weight, of Christmas cake or pudding be consumed on a one-off basis."<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">&#91;86&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inaccurate_claim_of_support_for_British_farmers">Inaccurate claim of support for British farmers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Inaccurate claim of support for British farmers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following a well publicised crash in UK milk prices Morrisons launched UK flag branded milk labelled 'For Farmers' promising an extra 23p per carton. In fact the extra money equivalent to 10p a litre went to <a href="/wiki/Arla_Foods" title="Arla Foods">Arla Foods</a>, a Danish milk co-operative group to be divided among certain countries with only one quarter of the 23p going to UK farmers.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">&#91;87&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lawley_Village_Community">Lawley Village Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morrisons&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Lawley Village Community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Morrisons have caused controversy with the local community in the Shropshire Town of <a href="/wiki/Telford_and_Wrekin" title="Telford and Wrekin">Telford and Wrekin</a>: Morrisons has sought to install an <a href="/wiki/Automatic_number-plate_recognition" title="Automatic number-plate recognition">ANPR</a> camera scheme which will penalise other local businesses that use a community designated car park.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">&#91;88&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
