<p><b>Bradford Interchange</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Transport_hub" title="Transport hub">transport interchange</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, which consists of a <a href="/wiki/Railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Railway station">railway station</a> and combined <a href="/wiki/Bus_station" title="Bus station">bus and coach station</a> adjacent. The Interchange, which was designed in 1962, was hailed as a showpiece of European design and was opened on 14 January 1973. It is served by the majority of bus services in the city centre along with <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express Coaches</a>, while the railway station, which is one of two in the city centre (along with  <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Forster_Square_railway_station" title="Bradford Forster Square railway station">Bradford Forster Square</a>), is served by <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> and is also the terminus for <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a> services to <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Layout_and_facilities">Layout and facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bradford_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Layout and facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bradford_Interchange._-_geograph.org.uk_-_419820.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Bradford_Interchange._-_geograph.org.uk_-_419820.jpg/220px-Bradford_Interchange._-_geograph.org.uk_-_419820.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Bradford_Interchange._-_geograph.org.uk_-_419820.jpg/330px-Bradford_Interchange._-_geograph.org.uk_-_419820.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Bradford_Interchange._-_geograph.org.uk_-_419820.jpg/440px-Bradford_Interchange._-_geograph.org.uk_-_419820.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bradford_Interchange._-_geograph.org.uk_-_419820.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The bus station concourse at Bradford Interchange</div></div></div>
<p>The main entrance with the taxi rank and car park is on a lower level, while the train platforms and bus/coach stops are on a split upper level, both separate with pedestrian access. Downstairs, in the central concourse, there are a few shops, a <a href="/wiki/Newsagent" class="mw-redirect" title="Newsagent">newsagent</a>, a cafe and sandwich shop and a fast food outlet on the train platforms, where hot drinks are also available. Toilets are located off the main concourse.
</p><p>There is also a <a href="/wiki/British_Transport_Police" title="British Transport Police">British Transport Police</a> office and lost luggage desk, provided for passengers' concern and safety at the railway station, with a separate <a href="/wiki/Security" title="Security">security</a> and lost-luggage unit for bus travellers, on the <a href="/wiki/Bus" title="Bus">bus</a> <a href="/wiki/Concourse" title="Concourse">concourse</a>. A smoking ban is observed in all parts of Bradford interchange, and <a href="/wiki/Closed-circuit_television" title="Closed-circuit television">CCTV</a> is also in operation with security officers and police regularly patrolling the station.
</p><p>The railway station has four platforms and a short bay that was previously used for the Red Star parcels facility. Platform 1 has a run-round facility for locomotive-hauled trains (mainly freight services). The track layout and associated signalling was remodelled during the course of a week-long engineering blockade from 25 October to 3 November 2008, to permit higher speeds on both routes into the station, and also allow trains to approach the station from both Leeds and Halifax simultaneously (something that was not possible with the old track configuration).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ticket_offices">Ticket offices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bradford_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ticket offices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bradford Interchange has separate bus and train <a href="/wiki/Ticket_(admission)" title="Ticket (admission)">ticket</a> outlets. The bus and Metro office, which also deals with <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express</a> coach enquiries from a separate desk, is located in the central concourse. The train ticket office is next to the pedestrian entrance to the train platforms and is open seven days a week (except for late evenings).  Escalators and lifts link the two levels and there is step-free access to all platforms.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bradford_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:42072_Bradford_Exchange.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/42072_Bradford_Exchange.jpg/220px-42072_Bradford_Exchange.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="160" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/42072_Bradford_Exchange.jpg/330px-42072_Bradford_Exchange.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/42072_Bradford_Exchange.jpg/440px-42072_Bradford_Exchange.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="583" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:42072_Bradford_Exchange.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/LMS_Fairburn_2-6-4T" title="LMS Fairburn 2-6-4T">LMS Fairburn 2-6-4T</a> 42072 at Bradford Exchange, 1966-67</div></div></div>

Note <a href="/wiki/Keighley_Bus_Company" title="Keighley Bus Company">Keighley &amp; District</a>, <a href="/wiki/First_West_Yorkshire" title="First West Yorkshire">First Bradford Traveller</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Metro" title="West Yorkshire Metro">WYPTE</a> liveried buses.</div></div></div>
<p>The original railway station, named <i>Bradford Exchange</i>, was opened by the joint efforts of the <a href="/wiki/Lancashire_and_Yorkshire_Railway" title="Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway">Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_Railway_(Great_Britain)" title="Great Northern Railway (Great Britain)">Great Northern Railway</a> on 9 May 1850.<sup id="cite_ref-subritbradex_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-subritbradex-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 1867, the <a href="/wiki/Leeds,_Bradford_and_Halifax_Junction_Railway" title="Leeds, Bradford and Halifax Junction Railway">Leeds, Bradford and Halifax Junction Railway</a>, which had previously used <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Adolphus_Street_railway_station" title="Bradford Adolphus Street railway station">Bradford Adolphus Street</a>, built a link to the tracks into Exchange station to join the two existing companies; Adolphus Street station was then closed to passenger use.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The railway station was completely rebuilt on the same site in 1880 with ten bay platforms and two <a href="/wiki/Arched" class="mw-redirect" title="Arched">arched</a> roofs. Constructed of <a href="/wiki/Wrought_iron" title="Wrought iron">wrought iron</a>, these rested at the outer sides on plain stone walls and <a href="/wiki/Classical_architecture" title="Classical architecture">classical</a> <a href="/wiki/Corinthian_order" title="Corinthian order">corinthian</a> style columns down the middle. Glass covered the middle half and timber (inside)/ slate (outside) covered the outer quarters of each span. The four end screens were glazed in a fan pattern with decorative timber outer edging. The dimensions were a length of 450 feet (140&#160;m), a width of 100 feet (30&#160;m) for each arch and a height of 80 feet (24&#160;m), track to apex. The railway station never had a formal frontage; instead, passengers entered by an opening in the northwest side.
</p><p>In its 1920s heyday, it served routes to <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Westgate_railway_station" title="Wakefield Westgate railway station">Wakefield Westgate</a> via <a href="/wiki/Ardsley_railway_station" title="Ardsley railway station">Ardsley</a> (used by many of the city's through trains to <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a>), <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Kirkgate_railway_station" title="Wakefield Kirkgate railway station">Wakefield Kirkgate</a> via <a href="/wiki/Batley_railway_station" title="Batley railway station">Batley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ossett" title="Ossett">Ossett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keighley_railway_station" title="Keighley railway station">Keighley</a> &amp; Halifax via <a href="/wiki/Queensbury_railway_station" title="Queensbury railway station">Queensbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mirfield_railway_station" title="Mirfield railway station">Mirfield</a> via <a href="/wiki/Cleckheaton_Central_railway_station" title="Cleckheaton Central railway station">Cleckheaton</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Spen_Valley_Line" title="Spen Valley Line">Spen Valley Line</a>) and to Leeds via the <a href="/wiki/Pudsey_Loop" title="Pudsey Loop">Pudsey Loop</a> in addition to the current lines. These however had all closed by the end of 1966 - most having fallen victim to the <a href="/wiki/Beeching_Axe" class="mw-redirect" title="Beeching Axe">Beeching Axe</a>.
</p><p>By 1973, the railway station with its 10 platforms<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> was deemed too large and was again rebuilt, this time on a different site slightly further south. The old Exchange station was demolished soon afterwards and was used for a time as a car park; the site now houses Bradford <a href="/wiki/Crown_Court" title="Crown Court">Crown Court</a><sup id="cite_ref-subritbradex_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-subritbradex-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and is due to be developed as a 'Justice Quarter' with new <a href="/wiki/Magistrates%27_Court_(England_and_Wales)" class="mw-redirect" title="Magistrates&#39; Court (England and Wales)">Magistrates'</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coroner%27s_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="Coroner&#39;s Court">Coroner's Courts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1977, a bus station was built alongside, and, in 1983, the station was renamed <i>Bradford Interchange</i> to link buses and trains in a covered environment.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The bus station featured a large ridge and furrow design of overall roof, which was subsequently demolished in 1999 to allow for a rebuilding of the bus station, which was opened in 2001. This was paid for partly by the sale of some adjacent land to the south of the site and some now-surplus land on the old bus station site.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
During the 1970s and 1980s, the station was considered the mainline station for Bradford with express services to <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a>, Trans-Pennine services to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Lime_Street_railway_station" title="Liverpool Lime Street railway station">Liverpool</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_railway_station" title="Newcastle railway station">Newcastle</a> and summer Saturday services to the South-West. The Inter-city services were moved to <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Forster_Square_railway_station" title="Bradford Forster Square railway station">Forster Square station</a> in 1992 when the line was electrified. The station also had an adjacent <a href="/wiki/Red_Star_Parcels" title="Red Star Parcels">Red Star Parcels</a> terminus but, like most other mainline stations following the <a href="/wiki/Privatisation_of_British_Rail" title="Privatisation of British Rail">privatisation of British Rail</a>, it lost this facility during the 1990s.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bradford_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus_and_coach">Bus and coach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bradford_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Bus and coach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The bus station is managed by <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Metro" title="West Yorkshire Metro">Metro</a>. The main operators at the bus station include <a href="/wiki/First_West_Yorkshire" title="First West Yorkshire">First West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Yorkshire" title="Arriva Yorkshire">Arriva Yorkshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Yorkshire Tiger</a> with other services run by <a href="/wiki/Geldards_Coaches" title="Geldards Coaches">Geldards Coaches</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keighley_Bus_Company" title="Keighley Bus Company">Keighley Bus Company</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_Yorkshire" title="Stagecoach Yorkshire">Stagecoach Yorkshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/TLC_Travel" title="TLC Travel">TLC Travel</a>. <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express</a> coaches run nationwide from the station, Bharat Coaches run coach services to <a href="/wiki/Derby" title="Derby">Derby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leicester" title="Leicester">Leicester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slough" title="Slough">Slough</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southall" title="Southall">Southall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Megabus_(Europe)" title="Megabus (Europe)">Megabus</a> runs services to <a href="/wiki/Burnley" title="Burnley">Burnley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skipton" title="Skipton">Skipton</a> and <a href="/wiki/East_Midlands_Parkway_railway_station" title="East Midlands Parkway railway station">East Midlands Parkway railway station</a> (with train connections to <a href="/wiki/St_Pancras_railway_station" title="St Pancras railway station">London St Pancras</a>) as part of its <a href="/wiki/Megabus_(Europe)#Megabusplus" title="Megabus (Europe)">Megabusplus</a> service.
</p><p>Local bus services run to many destinations, including <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harrogate" title="Harrogate">Harrogate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ilkley" title="Ilkley">Ilkley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keighley" title="Keighley">Keighley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Otley" title="Otley">Otley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>, as well as services within the Bradford area, such as <a href="/wiki/Shipley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Shipley, West Yorkshire">Shipley</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bradford_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bradford Interchange is on the <a href="/wiki/Caldervale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Caldervale Line">Caldervale Line</a> and is one of the two railway stations serving the city of <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>. The other station is <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Forster_Square_railway_station" title="Bradford Forster Square railway station">Forster Square</a>, a 10 minutes walk away.
</p><p>Monday to Saturday during the daytime, services run every 15 minutes between the Interchange and Leeds and hourly onwards to <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York</a>. Regular through running ended to Selby at the May 2014 timetable change, was reinstated at the corresponding change in May 2018 and removed again in December 2018. On evenings and Sundays, there are usually three services to Leeds each hour with one extended to York.
</p><p>In the other direction, there is a train roughly every 15 minutes to <a href="/wiki/Halifax_railway_station_(West_Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Halifax railway station (West Yorkshire)">Halifax</a>, with two trains an hour continuing to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria_station" title="Manchester Victoria station">Manchester Victoria</a> (one limited stop, the other serving all stations to <a href="/wiki/Todmorden_railway_station" title="Todmorden railway station">Todmorden</a>, then <a href="/wiki/Rochdale_railway_station" title="Rochdale railway station">Rochdale</a> only), one to <a href="/wiki/Preston_railway_station" title="Preston railway station">Preston</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_North_railway_station" title="Blackpool North railway station">Blackpool North</a> via <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_railway_station" title="Blackburn railway station">Blackburn</a> and one to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  Since the summer 2019 timetable update, one of the two Manchester trains extends to <a href="/wiki/Warrington_Bank_Quay_railway_station" title="Warrington Bank Quay railway station">Warrington Bank Quay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chester_railway_station" title="Chester railway station">Chester</a>.
</p><p>Sundays, there are three services each hour to Halifax - these continuing to one of Manchester Victoria, Blackpool North via Preston or Huddersfield (hourly to each).
</p><p>Because of the geography of Bradford, the station was built as a terminus, with the lines in a 'Y'-formation, so trains must reverse out of the station to continue their journey.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="London_services">London services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bradford_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: London services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The station now also sees regular services to <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a> via Halifax, Brighouse, Mirfield, Wakefield, Pontefract and Doncaster. In January 2009, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a> had its application for <a href="/wiki/Train_path" title="Train path">train paths</a> to run a Bradford Interchange to London service accepted by the <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Rail_and_Road" title="Office of Rail and Road">Office of Rail Regulation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Four trains per day operate, now that full approval for the service has been granted;<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> these use <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_180" title="British Rail Class 180">Class 180</a> units and started running from 23 May 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ongoing_improvements">Ongoing improvements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bradford_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ongoing improvements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bus platforms were once more plentiful and originally featured a large 'ridge and furrow' glass roof,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> but this was demolished in the 1990s, following the sale of some land for an office development. The bus station was completely rebuilt in 2001.
</p><p>Metro are currently considering improvements to the bus and rail platforms, including better access between facilities and pedestrian access between the bus concourse and the rail platforms, to save walking down and up the escalators.
</p><p>The information displays were replaced in early 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> following a modest facelift in autumn 2008, which included new signage and a repaint. In January 2010, automatic <a href="/wiki/Turnstile" title="Turnstile">ticket barriers</a> were installed by <a href="/wiki/Northern_Rail_(Serco-Abellio)" title="Northern Rail (Serco-Abellio)">Northern Rail</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Further improvements under the National Station Improvement Plan are proposed, which include refurbished canopies, new flooring, more lighting and <a href="/wiki/CCTV" class="mw-redirect" title="CCTV">CCTV</a>, a new waiting room and extra seating.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Under <a href="/wiki/Network_Rail" title="Network Rail">Network Rail</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hub" title="Northern Hub">Northern Hub</a> development, the Northern franchise, which commenced in April 2016, will reintroduce services to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Lime_Street_railway_station" title="Liverpool Lime Street railway station">Liverpool</a> and new services to <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Nottingham railway station">Nottingham</a> via <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield railway station">Sheffield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Airport railway station">Manchester Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chester_railway_station" title="Chester railway station">Chester</a> have been announced.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>  These will form part of the new "Northern Connect" network and use new-build DMUs once these have been delivered in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Network Rail upgraded the track and signalling infrastructure on the Calder Valley line in October 2018.  This saw the <a href="/wiki/Signal_box" class="mw-redirect" title="Signal box">signal box</a> at Mill Lane Junction closed (along with those at Halifax, Milner Royd Junction and Hebden Bridge), new signals installed and route control passed to the <a href="/wiki/Rail_Operating_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Rail Operating Centre">Rail Operating Centre</a> at <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York</a>.  The same scheme has also seen track and line speed improvements carried out, in order to reduce journey times to Manchester and Preston.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Network Rail and Transport for North are currently working on plans for a high-speed rail stop in Bradford either in the city centre or a parkway station. This has been campaign as part of the Northern Powerhouse Rail Project.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
