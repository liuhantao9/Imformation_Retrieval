It was created in 1313 for Robert de Willoughby. Since 1983, the title has been held by <a href="/wiki/Jane_Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby,_28th_Baroness_Willoughby_de_Eresby" title="Jane Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby, 28th Baroness Willoughby de Eresby">Jane Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby, 28th Baroness Willoughby de Eresby</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Willoughby_de_Eresby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The title of <b>Baron Willoughby</b> was created <a href="/wiki/Hereditary_peer#Writs_of_summons" title="Hereditary peer">by writ</a> in 1313 for Robert de Willoughby, <a href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_manor" title="Lord of the manor">lord of the manor</a> of Eresby in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Spilsby" title="Spilsby">Spilsby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a>. He was the son of Sir William de Willoughby and Alice, daughter of <a href="/wiki/John_Beke,_1st_Baron_Beke" title="John Beke, 1st Baron Beke">John Beke, 1st Baron Beke</a>  of Eresby.  The writ was addressed to "Roberto de Wylghby," and the suffix of <b>de Eresby</b> was added to the title between 1350-1360 to distinguish it from other members of the de Willoughby family.<sup id="cite_ref-burke_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-burke-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The fourteenth Baron was created <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Lindsey" title="Earl of Lindsey">Earl of Lindsey</a> in 1626. His great-grandson, the fourth Earl and seventeenth Baron, was created Marquess of Lindsey in 1706 and Duke of Ancaster and Kesteven in 1715. On the death of the first Duke's great-grandson, the fourth Duke, the Dukedom, Marquessate and Earldom were inherited by his uncle, while the Barony of Willoughby de Eresby fell into abeyance between the late Duke's sisters Lady Priscilla and Lady Georgiana. In 1780, the title was called out of abeyance in favour of Priscilla. She was the wife of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Burrell,_1st_Baron_Gwydyr" title="Peter Burrell, 1st Baron Gwydyr">Peter Burrell, 1st Baron Gwydyr</a>. Their son Peter inherited both Baronies. On the death of Peter's only son Albyric, the Barony of Gwydyr was passed on to a cousin, while the Barony of Willoughby de Eresby fell into abeyance between his sisters Clementina Drummond-Willoughby, wife of <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_John_Heathcote,_1st_Baron_Aveland" class="mw-redirect" title="Gilbert John Heathcote, 1st Baron Aveland">Gilbert John Heathcote, 1st Baron Aveland</a> and Charlotte, wife of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Carrington,_2nd_Baron_Carrington" title="Robert Carrington, 2nd Baron Carrington">Robert Carrington, 2nd Baron Carrington</a>. In 1871 the abeyance was terminated in favour of Clementina. She was succeeded by her and Lord Aveland's son Gilbert, 2nd Baron Aveland and 25th Baron Willoughby de Eresby. In 1892 he was created <b>Earl of Ancaster</b>, a revival of the Ancaster title created for his maternal ancestor in 1715. On the death of his grandson, the third Earl, in 1983, the Earldom and Barony of Aveland became extinct (while the Baronetcy also held by the Earl was passed on to a distant relative), while the Barony of Willoughby de Eresby was inherited by the late Earl's daughter, Nancy, the present holder of the title.
</p><p>Since 1626, the Barony of Willoughby de Eresby has been associated with office of <a href="/wiki/Lord_Great_Chamberlain" title="Lord Great Chamberlain">Lord Great Chamberlain</a>. In that year, the first Earl of Lindsey inherited the Great Chamberlainship. Upon the death of the fourth Duke of Ancaster and Kesteven, it was divided between his sisters Priscilla and Georgiana (who was later <a href="/wiki/Marchioness_of_Cholmondeley" class="mw-redirect" title="Marchioness of Cholmondeley">Marchioness of Cholmondeley</a>). Thereafter, the barony of Willoughby de Eresby has been associated with the senior share of the Lord Great Chamberlainship. However, it has not been associated with the highest share. The share belonging to Lady Cholmondeley has been passed intact to her heirs, the <a href="/wiki/Marquess_of_Cholmondeley" title="Marquess of Cholmondeley">Marquesses of Cholmondeley</a>, but Lady Willoughby de Eresby's share has been split between many heirs. As of 2004, only one-fourth of the Lord Great Chamberlainship is possessed by the holder of the barony.
</p><p>The peerage has been held by a woman six times, more than any other peerage except that of <a href="/wiki/Baron_de_Ros" title="Baron de Ros">Baron de Ros</a>.
</p><p>The family seats are <a href="/wiki/Grimsthorpe_Castle" title="Grimsthorpe Castle">Grimsthorpe Castle</a> in Edenham, near <a href="/wiki/Bourne,_Lincolnshire" title="Bourne, Lincolnshire">Bourne, Lincolnshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drummond_Castle" title="Drummond Castle">Drummond Castle</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Crieff" title="Crieff">Crieff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perthshire" title="Perthshire">Perthshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, originally the family seat of the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Perth" title="Earl of Perth">Drummond earls of Perth</a>.
</p>

<ul><li>Married Lucy le Strange and <a href="/wiki/Joan_Holland" title="Joan Holland">Joan Holland</a>. His daughter Margery, by Lucy,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> married William FitzHugh, 4th <a href="/wiki/Baron_FitzHugh" title="Baron FitzHugh">Lord FitzHugh</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ravensworth" title="Ravensworth">Ravensworth</a> and had a son Henry who became the 5th Lord FitzHugh. Two of the 5th Lord FitzHugh's daughters included <a href="/wiki/Alice_FitzHugh" title="Alice FitzHugh">Alice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_FitzHugh,_Baroness_Vaux_of_Harrowden" class="mw-redirect" title="Elizabeth FitzHugh, Baroness Vaux of Harrowden">Elizabeth FitzHugh</a>. Elizabeth, from her first marriage, was the mother of <a href="/wiki/Sir_Thomas_Parr" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Thomas Parr">Sir Thomas Parr</a> and thus paternal grandmother of Queen consort <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Parr" title="Catherine Parr">Katherine Parr</a>.</li></ul></li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Peregrine_Bertie,_2nd_Duke_of_Ancaster_and_Kesteven" title="Peregrine Bertie, 2nd Duke of Ancaster and Kesteven">Peregrine Bertie, 2nd Duke of Ancaster and Kesteven, 18th Baron Willoughby de Eresby</a> (1686–1742)</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby,_2nd_Earl_of_Ancaster" title="Gilbert Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby, 2nd Earl of Ancaster">Gilbert Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby, 2nd Earl of Ancaster, 26th Baron Willoughby de Eresby</a> (1867–1951)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby,_3rd_Earl_of_Ancaster" class="mw-redirect" title="Gilbert Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby, 3rd Earl of Ancaster">Gilbert James Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby, 3rd Earl of Ancaster, 27th Baron Willoughby de Eresby</a> (1907–1983)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jane_Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby,_28th_Baroness_Willoughby_de_Eresby" title="Jane Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby, 28th Baroness Willoughby de Eresby">(Nancy) Jane Marie Heathcote-Drummond-Willoughby, 28th Baroness Willoughby de Eresby</a> (b. 1934)</li></ul>
<p>The co-heirs presumptive are Sebastian St Maur Miller (b. 1965) and <a href="/wiki/Sir_John_Aird,_4th_Baronet" title="Sir John Aird, 4th Baronet">Sir John Aird, 4th Baronet</a> (b. 1940). The former is the son of Carola Ramsden, the only child of the 26th Baron's elder daughter, Lady Catherine. The latter is the eldest child and only son of the 26th Baron's younger daughter, Lady Priscilla.
</p>
