<p><b>Marquess of Londonderry</b>, of the County of <a href="/wiki/County_Londonderry" title="County Londonderry">Londonderry</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/ʌ/: &#39;u&#39; in &#39;cut&#39;">ʌ</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span><span title="/ən/: &#39;on&#39; in &#39;button&#39;">ən</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span><span title="/ər/: &#39;er&#39; in &#39;letter&#39;">ər</span><span title="/i/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;happy&#39;">i</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">LUN</span>-dən-dər-ee</i></a>),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is a title in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Ireland" title="Peerage of Ireland">Peerage of Ireland</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquess_of_Londonderry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The title was created in 1816 for <a href="/wiki/Robert_Stewart,_1st_Marquess_of_Londonderry" title="Robert Stewart, 1st Marquess of Londonderry">Robert Stewart, 1st Earl of Londonderry</a>. He had earlier represented <a href="/wiki/County_Down" title="County Down">County Down</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Irish_House_of_Commons" title="Irish House of Commons">Irish House of Commons</a>. Stewart had already been created <b>Baron Londonderry</b> in 1789,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  <b>Viscount Castlereagh</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Castlereagh_(County_Down_barony)" title="Castlereagh (County Down barony)">Castlereagh</a> in the County of Down,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-parlgaz_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parlgaz-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> in 1795 and <b>Earl of Londonderry</b>, of the County of Londonderry, in 1796.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> These titles are also in the Peerage of Ireland.
</p><p>He was the son of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Stewart_(1699%E2%80%931781)" title="Alexander Stewart (1699–1781)">Alexander Stewart</a>, who had married Mary Cowan, sister and heiress of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Cowan_(governor)" title="Robert Cowan (governor)">Robert Cowan</a>, who gained great wealth as <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Bombay" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor of Bombay">Governor of Bombay</a> from 1729 to 1737. Alexander was from Ballylawn, a <a href="/wiki/Townland" title="Townland">townland</a> at the south-west corner of <a href="/wiki/Inishowen" title="Inishowen">Inishowen</a> in the north of <a href="/wiki/County_Donegal" title="County Donegal">County Donegal</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Ireland" title="Counties of Ireland">county</a> located in the west of <a href="/wiki/Ulster" title="Ulster">Ulster</a> in the northern part of <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. However, much of the Stewart family wealth was based on the estates which came into the family through this marriage.
</p><p>The 1st Marquess was one of the few people to become a Marquess without inheriting any titles prior to the creation. He sat in the <a href="/wiki/British_House_of_Lords" class="mw-redirect" title="British House of Lords">British House of Lords</a> as one of the twenty-eight original <a href="/wiki/Representative_peer" title="Representative peer">Irish Representative Peers</a> from 1800 to 1821. He was succeeded by his son from his first marriage to Lady Sarah Seymour. The 2nd Marquess, better known as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Castlereagh" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Castlereagh">Lord Castlereagh</a> (he went by the <a href="/wiki/Courtesy_title" title="Courtesy title">courtesy title</a> of Viscount Castlereagh from 1796 to 1821), was a noted statesman and diplomat. Castlereagh is best remembered for his tenure as <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Secretary_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Foreign Secretary of the United Kingdom">Foreign Secretary of the United Kingdom</a> from 1812 to 1822 and played an important role at the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Congress of Vienna</a> of 1814 to 1815. He committed suicide in 1822, one year after succeeding his father in the marquessate. The 2nd Marquess did not follow his father as a Representative Peer into the House of Lords, this permitted him to continue fulfilling his roles in the House of Commons.
</p><p>Castlereagh was succeeded by his half-brother, the 3rd Marquess. He was the only son from the 1st Marquess's second marriage to Lady Frances Pratt, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pratt,_1st_Earl_Camden" title="Charles Pratt, 1st Earl Camden">Charles Pratt, 1st Earl Camden</a>. He was a General in the Army and like his elder half-brother a prominent politician and diplomat. Lord Londonderry served as <a href="/wiki/Under-Secretary_of_State_for_War_and_the_Colonies" title="Under-Secretary of State for War and the Colonies">Under-Secretary of State for War and the Colonies</a> from 1807 to 1809, fought in the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a> and was <a href="/wiki/British_Ambassador_to_Austria" class="mw-redirect" title="British Ambassador to Austria">Ambassador to Austria</a> from 1814 to 1823. In 1814 he was created <b>Baron Stewart</b>, of Stewart's Court and Ballylawn in County Donegal, in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Peerage of the United Kingdom">Peerage of the United Kingdom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 1819 Londonderry married as his second wife <a href="/wiki/Frances_Anne_Vane,_Marchioness_of_Londonderry" class="mw-redirect" title="Frances Anne Vane, Marchioness of Londonderry">Lady Frances Anne Vane-Tempest</a> (died 1865), daughter and wealthy heiress of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Vane-Tempest" title="Henry Vane-Tempest">Sir Henry Vane-Tempest</a>, 2nd <a href="/wiki/Baronet" title="Baronet">Baronet</a>, through which marriage substantial estates in <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a> came into the Stewart family. He assumed the additional surname of Vane on his marriage and in 1823 he was created <b>Viscount Seaham</b>, of Seaham in the County Palatine of Durham, and <b>Earl Vane</b>, with remainder to the male issue of his second marriage. These titles are in the Peerage of the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lord Londonderry was succeeded in the viscountcy of Seaham and earldom of Vane according to the special remainder by his eldest son from his second marriage while he was succeeded in the Irish titles and the barony of Stewart by his son from his first marriage to Lady Catherine Bligh, the 4th Marquess. He died childless in 1872 and was succeeded by his half-brother, the 5th Marquess, who had already succeeded his father as second Earl Vane in 1854. He represented <a href="/wiki/Durham_North_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" class="mw-redirect" title="Durham North (UK Parliament constituency)">Durham North</a> in the <a href="/wiki/British_House_of_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="British House of Commons">House of Commons</a> and served as <a href="/wiki/Lord-Lieutenant_of_County_Durham" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord-Lieutenant of County Durham">Lord-Lieutenant of County Durham</a>. In 1851 the 5th Marquess assumed by Royal licence the additional surname of Tempest. On his death the titles passed to his eldest son, the 6th Marquess. He was a <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> politician and held office in the administrations of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Gascoyne-Cecil,_3rd_Marquess_of_Salisbury" title="Robert Gascoyne-Cecil, 3rd Marquess of Salisbury">Lord Salisbury</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Balfour" title="Arthur Balfour">Arthur Balfour</a> as <a href="/wiki/Lord-Lieutenant_of_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord-Lieutenant of Ireland">Lord-Lieutenant of Ireland</a>, as <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Postmaster_General" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Postmaster General">Postmaster General</a>, as <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Board_of_Education" class="mw-redirect" title="President of the Board of Education">President of the Board of Education</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Privy_Seal" title="Lord Privy Seal">Lord Privy Seal</a> and as <a href="/wiki/Lord_President_of_the_Council" title="Lord President of the Council">Lord President of the Council</a>. In 1885 he assumed by Royal licence the additional and principal surname of Stewart.
</p><p>He was succeeded by his son, the 7th Marquess, who was also a Conservative politician. He had a career in both Irish and British politics but is best known for his role as <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Air" title="Secretary of State for Air">Secretary of State for Air</a> from 1931 to 1935. Lord Londonderry subsequently gained notoriety for his informal diplomatic contacts with senior members of the German government. He made six visits to Nazi Germany between January 1936 and September 1938 meeting <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> on a number of occasions and sympathising with some of his viewpoints.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> His wife <a href="/wiki/Edith_Vane-Tempest-Stewart,_Marchioness_of_Londonderry" title="Edith Vane-Tempest-Stewart, Marchioness of Londonderry">Edith Vane-Tempest-Stewart, Marchioness of Londonderry</a>, was an influential society hostess remembered for her close friendship with <a href="/wiki/Ramsay_MacDonald" title="Ramsay MacDonald">Ramsay MacDonald</a>. Lord Londonderry was succeeded by his son, the 8th Marquess. He represented <a href="/wiki/County_Down_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" class="mw-redirect" title="County Down (UK Parliament constituency)">County Down</a> in the House of Commons as a Conservative from 1931 to 1945.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Heir_apparent" title="Heir apparent">heir apparent</a> to the marquessate is styled <i>Viscount Castlereagh</i>, although the Marquess is also the Earl Vane, and the heir apparent's heir apparent, when such exists, is styled <i>Lord Stewart"<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></i>
</p><p>Between 1823 and 1854 and between 1872 and 1999, the Marquesses of Londonderry sat in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a> as <i>The Earl Vane</i>. Between 1822 and 1823 and between 1854 and 1872, they sat as <i>The Lord Stewart</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Residences">Residences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquess_of_Londonderry&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Residences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The family seat was <a href="/wiki/Mount_Stewart" title="Mount Stewart">Mount Stewart</a> near <a href="/wiki/Newtownards" title="Newtownards">Newtownards</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Down" title="County Down">County Down</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>. The other estates were the <a href="/wiki/Wynyard_Park,_County_Durham" title="Wynyard Park, County Durham">Wynyard Park</a> estate in <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seaham_Hall" title="Seaham Hall">Seaham Hall</a> in County Durham, as well as <a href="/wiki/Londonderry_House" title="Londonderry House">Londonderry House</a> on <a href="/wiki/Park_Lane" title="Park Lane">Park Lane</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> (where the <a href="/wiki/London_Hilton" class="mw-redirect" title="London Hilton">London Hilton</a> hotel is now) and <a href="/wiki/Plas_Machynlleth" title="Plas Machynlleth">Plas Machynlleth</a> in mid-Wales.
</p>

<p>The 4th Marquess did not inherit the Seaham viscountcy and Vane earldom as he was not issue by the 3rd Marquess' second wife. The remainder only applies to the male issue of the 3rd Marquess' second marriage.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Heir_presumptive" title="Heir presumptive">heir presumptive</a> is his brother Lord Reginald Alexander Vane-Tempest-Stewart (born 1977).<br />
The heir presumptive's heir apparent is his son Robin Gabriel Vane-Tempest-Stewart (born 2004).
</p>
