	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Macedonian onomastics</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Macedonian onomastics</b> (<a href="/wiki/Macedonian_language" title="Macedonian language">Macedonian</a>: <span lang="mk">Македонска ономастика</span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Macedonian" title="Romanization of Macedonian">romanized</a>:&#160;</small><i lang="mk-Latn" title="Macedonian-language romanization">Makedonska onomastika</i>) is part of the <a href="/wiki/Macedonistics" class="mw-redirect" title="Macedonistics">Macedonistics</a> that studies the names, surnames and nicknames of the <a href="/wiki/Macedonian_language" title="Macedonian language">Macedonian language</a>. This is relatively new linguistic discipline. In Macedonia, and in the Macedonistics in general, it developed during the 19th century, where the first few research results have been provided. The <a href="/wiki/Onomastics" title="Onomastics">onomastics</a> for a long period of time has been considered as part of various scientific disciplines, such as <a href="/wiki/Geography" title="Geography">geography</a>, <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">history</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ethnography" title="Ethnography">ethnography</a>, until it became a discipline on its own in the 20th century. The Macedonian Onomastics, generally speaking, is divided into <i>toponomastics</i> and <i>anthroponomastics</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-општа_граматика_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-општа_граматика-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Division">Division</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macedonian_onomastics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Division">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The onomastics is divided into two large categories, which in turn include several different subcategories or disciplines. The two basic categories of the Macedonian onomastics are:<sup id="cite_ref-македонска_граматика_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-македонска_граматика-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Toponomastics" class="mw-redirect" title="Toponomastics">Toponomastics</a></i></li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Anthroponomastics" class="mw-redirect" title="Anthroponomastics">Anthroponomastics</a></i></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Toponomastics">Toponomastics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macedonian_onomastics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Toponomastics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The toponomastics is a branch of the onomastics that studies the <a href="/wiki/Toponymy" title="Toponymy">toponymies</a>, or in other words the names of the places, geographical terms, their meaning and origin. Depending on the nature of the toponymy, it can be divided into the following subcategories:<sup id="cite_ref-општа_граматика_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-општа_граматика-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Hydronym" title="Hydronym">Hydronomastics</a></i>, a scientific discipline that studies the names of the <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a> bodies. Some hydronyms are: <i><a href="/wiki/Vardar" title="Vardar">Vardar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Treska" title="Treska">Treska</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crna_River_(Vardar)" title="Crna River (Vardar)">Crna Reka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ohrid_Lake" class="mw-redirect" title="Ohrid Lake">Ohrid Lake</a></i> etc.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Oronymy" class="mw-redirect" title="Oronymy">Oronomastics</a></i>, a scientific discipline that studies the names of the <a href="/wiki/Mountain" title="Mountain">mountains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hill" title="Hill">hills</a>, plains, <a href="/wiki/Valley" title="Valley">valleys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mountain_peak" class="mw-redirect" title="Mountain peak">peaks</a>, fields and other geographic components. Some examples of the Macedonian oronymies are: <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0ar_Mountains" title="Šar Mountains">Šar Mountains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polog" title="Polog">Polog</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pelagonia" title="Pelagonia">Pelagonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Osogovo" title="Osogovo">Osogovo</a> etc.</li>
<li><i>Onomastics of places</i>, a scientific discipline that studies the names of the settlements, towns, villages, cities. Some examples are: <a href="/wiki/Tetovo" title="Tetovo">Tetovo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kumanovo" title="Kumanovo">Kumanovo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skopje" title="Skopje">Skopje</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kon%C4%8De" title="Konče">Konče</a>, <a href="/wiki/Med%C5%BEitlija" title="Medžitlija">Medžitlija</a> etc.</li></ul>
<p>The toponomastics for its researches uses the <i>toponomastic stratigraphy</i>. The research of the toponymies is done via analysis of the different names of one particular item. The names are direct consequence of the different people, languages and cultures that existed on that particular area. The main principle that is taken intro account is the fact that even though the languages and people that lived in that area disappeared (via migration or assimilation), the names of the places, their meanings and origin remain. Those names later on will be acquired by the new people that came to that area and they will become part of their active lexicon.<sup id="cite_ref-македонска_граматика_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-македонска_граматика-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Anthroponomastics">Anthroponomastics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macedonian_onomastics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Anthroponomastics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The anthroponomastics is the second large discipline of the Onomastics. The Anthroponomastics studies the meaning and origin of the personal names. Moreover, it deals with:<sup id="cite_ref-македонска_граматика_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-македонска_граматика-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Name" title="Name">Personal names</a></i>. The origin of the Macedonian names varies. Most of them are of Macedonian Slavic origin, but there are popular names of foreign origin as well. Some names can be simple, such as <i>Драги (Dragi)</i>, and some can be complex, such as <i>Драгомир (Dragomir)</i>. The names are given according to people's will, way of living and the conditions where people live. They are quite important in the Macedonian society since they serve as an identification and they are basis for development of the Macedonian surnames and nicknames.
<ul><li>Many personal names unique to Macedonian were historically <a href="/wiki/Hypocorism" title="Hypocorism">hypocorisms</a>: ex. <i>Гоце</i> (Goce) (for <i>Георгија</i> (Georgija) and variants) which is now a name in its own right, while others have remained hypocorisms such as <i>Зоки</i> (Zoki) for <i>Зоран</i> (Zoran).</li></ul></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Surnames">Surnames</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macedonian_onomastics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Surnames">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surnames</a> in Macedonian are predominantly made by adding the <a href="/wiki/Possessive_suffix" class="mw-redirect" title="Possessive suffix">possessive suffix</a> <i>-ски</i> (-ski), usually with an additional possessive suffix  to a personal name <i>-ев</i> (-ev), title, occupation, etc. <a href="/wiki/Elision" title="Elision">Elision</a> is common in <a href="/wiki/Spoken_Macedonian" title="Spoken Macedonian">Spoken Macedonian</a> and this has resulted in forms such as <i>-оски</i> (-oski) and <i>-ески</i> (-eski). An example is the surname <i>Милевски</i> (Milevski) which is made up of the personal name <i>Mile</i> and the possessive suffixes <i>-ев</i> (-ev) and <i>-ски</i> (-ski). There are some surnames without suffixes such as <i>Темелко (Temelko)</i>, <i>Насто (Nasto)</i>, <i>Митре (Mitre)</i>, <i>Никола (Nikola)</i>, <i>Ристо (Risto)</i>, <i>Терпо (Terpo)</i> and <i>Трајко (Trajko)</i> among the <a href="/wiki/Macedonians_of_Albania" class="mw-redirect" title="Macedonians of Albania">Macedonians of Albania</a>. There are also some names whose <a href="/wiki/Etymology" title="Etymology">etymology</a> is unknown. Female surnames mirror those for men, but <a href="/wiki/Inflection" title="Inflection">inflect</a> for <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_gender" title="Grammatical gender">grammatical gender</a>: <i>-ова</i> (-ova), <i>-евa</i> (-eva), <i>-ска</i> (-ska). Consequently, the feminine form of the surname <i>Milevski</i> is <i>Milevska</i> (elided forms would be <i>Mileski</i> and <i>Mileska</i>, respectively).</li></ul>
<p>The most common suffixes used for surnames and the most common (top 6) surnames by <a href="/wiki/Statistical_regions_of_Macedonia" class="mw-redirect" title="Statistical regions of Macedonia">statistical region</a> are as follows:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Eastern</b>, -<i>ов</i>/-<i>ев</i>: Стојанов, Николов, Митев, Јованов, Атанасов, Иванов.</li>
<li><b>Northeastern</b>, -<i>овски</i>/-<i>евски</i>: Јовановски, Стојановски, Трајковски, Спасовски, Николовски, Илиевски.</li>
<li><b>Pelagonia</b>, -<i>о(в)ски</i>/-<i>е(в)ски</i>: Ристески, Ристевски, Стојановски, Талевски, Петровски, Петрески.</li>
<li><b>Polog</b>, an Albanian-majority region. Top 6 surnames are Albanian.</li>
<li><b>Skopje</b>, -<i>овски</i>/-<i>евски</i>: Стојановски, Николовски, Трајковски, Јовановски, Петровски, Крстевски.</li>
<li><b>Southeastern</b>, -<i>ов</i>/-<i>ев</i>: Стојанов, Ристов, Митев, Атанасов, Јованов, Ѓоргиев.</li>
<li><b>Southwestern</b>, -<i>о(в)ски</i>/-<i>е(в)ски</i>: Ристески, Наумоски, Петрески, Стојаноски.</li>
<li><b>Vardar</b>, -<i>ов</i>/-<i>ев</i>: Стојанов, Петров, Јованов, Ристов, Николов, Димов.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nicknames">Nicknames</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Macedonian_onomastics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Nicknames">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">Nicknames</a></i>. In Macedonian, usually, the nickname is given according to some physical characteristic of the person, but also they can reflect person's profession, personal characteristics, mental capabilities or disabilities. Some Macedonian nicknames are: <i>буцко (bucko, "fatso")</i>, <i>зборко (zborko, "chatterbox")</i>, <i>женкар (ženkar, "ladies' man")</i> and many others.</li></ul>
