<p><b>Mirfield</b> is a small town and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, it is on the <a href="/wiki/A644_road_(Great_Britain)" class="mw-redirect" title="A644 road (Great Britain)">A644</a> road between <a href="/wiki/Brighouse" title="Brighouse">Brighouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a> . At the 2011 census it had a population of 19,563.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town is served by <a href="/wiki/Mirfield_railway_station" title="Mirfield railway station">Mirfield railway station</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1894 to 1974, Mirfield was an <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a> until it was merged into the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Metropolitan Borough">Metropolitan Borough</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a>. In 1988 a <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">parish council</a> was formed, one of five in Kirklees, the others being:- <a href="/wiki/Denby_Dale" title="Denby Dale">Denby Dale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meltham" title="Meltham">Meltham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kirkburton" title="Kirkburton">Kirkburton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley" title="Holme Valley">Holme Valley</a>. As a parish council an additional tax precept to the Kirklees Metropolitan Borough Council tax is levied on the town's residents. It is made up of 16 councillors who serve for a fixed four-year term, and represent wards within the parish.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The members elect a town mayor, who serves for a fixed one-year term.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Mirfield_Show" title="Mirfield Show">Mirfield Show</a> is an annual agricultural event held on the third Sunday in August at Mirfield showground. It is organised and run by the Mirfield Agricultural Society (MAS) as a non-profit making event for the families of Mirfield and district.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Local residents introduced the Mirfield Food &amp; Craft Fayre in April 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> scheduled to be run the last Saturday of each month and "<i>help raise the profile of Mirfield, be a benefit to local traders, businesses, organisations and charities, and add more destination events to the Yorkshire calendar</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are two secondary schools in Mirfield: <a href="/wiki/The_Mirfield_Free_Grammar" title="The Mirfield Free Grammar">Mirfield Free Grammar and Sixth Form</a> and <a href="/wiki/Castle_Hall_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Castle Hall School">Castle Hall School</a>.
</p><p>Primary schools include Battyeford CE Primary School, Crossley Fields, Old Bank, Hopton Primary School and <a href="/wiki/Crowlees_Junior_and_Infant_School" title="Crowlees Junior and Infant School">Crowlees Junior and Infant School</a>, all of which were assessed by <a href="/wiki/Office_for_Standards_in_Education" class="mw-redirect" title="Office for Standards in Education">Ofsted</a> as 'Grade 1 – Outstanding' in the March 2007 inspection.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Old_and_new_church,_Mirfield._(3402662366).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Old_and_new_church%2C_Mirfield._%283402662366%29.jpg/220px-Old_and_new_church%2C_Mirfield._%283402662366%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="308" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Old_and_new_church%2C_Mirfield._%283402662366%29.jpg/330px-Old_and_new_church%2C_Mirfield._%283402662366%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Old_and_new_church%2C_Mirfield._%283402662366%29.jpg/440px-Old_and_new_church%2C_Mirfield._%283402662366%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2568" data-file-height="3599" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Old_and_new_church,_Mirfield._(3402662366).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St Mary's Church</div></div></div>
<p>The 13th century St Mary's Church was rebuilt in 1826 but proved too small for the growing population and was regarded as too minor for the growing district. A new church, designed by Sir <a href="/wiki/George_Gilbert_Scott" title="George Gilbert Scott">George Gilbert Scott</a>, was built a few yards to the northwest, on the site of Castle Hall, a mansion then home to the families of Mirfields (sometimes spelled Mirfin), Hetons and Beaumonts.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> At Scott's suggestion, the tower of the earlier church, which retains some medieval work, was retained.
</p><p>St. Mary's Church was also the boyhood church of <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Stewart" title="Patrick Stewart">Sir Patrick Stewart</a>, of <a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">Star Trek</a> fame.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/College_of_the_Resurrection" title="College of the Resurrection">College of the Resurrection</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> theological college. There, <a href="/wiki/Rowan_Williams" title="Rowan Williams">Rowan Williams</a>, later the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Canterbury" title="Archbishop of Canterbury">Archbishop of Canterbury</a>, lectured from 1975 for two years and Archbishop <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Huddleston" title="Trevor Huddleston">Trevor Huddleston</a> spent his last days.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Navigation">Navigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Navigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the 18th century, a canal was constructed through the town linking the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a> with other rivers in the area. The canal is part of the <a href="/wiki/Calder_and_Hebble_Navigation" title="Calder and Hebble Navigation">Calder and Hebble Navigation</a>. Its construction resulted in many industries in Mirfield, such as the textiles and <a href="/wiki/Marina" title="Marina">boat yards</a>. The canal is still in use for recreational users, with a high popularity of the pastime "duck feeding" being present along the waters edge.
</p><p>Mirfield is the base of the <a href="/wiki/National_Community_Boats_Association" title="National Community Boats Association">Safe Anchor Trust</a>, a charity founded in 1995 to provide canal boat trips for vulnerable and special needs people.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Princess_Royal" title="Anne, Princess Royal">Princess Anne</a> commissioned a new boat for the Trust.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are many national businesses based in Mirfield including John Cotton Group Ltd and Furniture Choice.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town has a variety of local independent shops as well as national chains such as <a href="/wiki/Lidl" title="Lidl">Lidl</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bargain_Booze" title="Bargain Booze">Bargain Booze</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Co-op_Food" title="Co-op Food">Co-op Food</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mirfield is the home of Mirfield <a href="/wiki/Petanque" class="mw-redirect" title="Petanque">Petanque</a> Club who currently play in the West Yorkshire Petanque League.
</p>
