<p><b>Golcar</b> (pronounced 'Goker' or 'Golker': the 'L' is silent in local pronunciation) is a village on a hillside crest above the <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley" title="Colne Valley">Colne Valley</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England, 2.5 miles (4&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, and just north of the <a href="/wiki/River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Colne, West Yorkshire">River Colne</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Narrow_Canal" title="Huddersfield Narrow Canal">Huddersfield Narrow Canal</a>. At the time of the 2011 Census the population had increased to 18,033.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The main transport access is from the <a href="/wiki/A62_road" title="A62 road">A62</a> (Manchester Road), through <a href="/wiki/Milnsbridge" title="Milnsbridge">Milnsbridge</a> in the valley bottom or via <a href="/wiki/Scapegoat_Hill" title="Scapegoat Hill">Scapegoat Hill</a> from the <a href="/wiki/A640_road" title="A640 road">A640</a> (New Hey Road) at the top of the hill.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Township_(England)" title="Township (England)">township</a> of Golcar consisted of Bolster Moor, Golcar, Leymoor, <a href="/wiki/Pole_Moor" title="Pole Moor">Pole Moor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scapegoat_Hill" title="Scapegoat Hill">Scapegoat Hill</a> and Town End.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golcar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Named after <a href="/wiki/Guthlac" class="mw-redirect" title="Guthlac">St Guthlac</a>, who preached in the area during the 8th century, its name is recorded in the <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i> as <i>Goullakarres</i>. During the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a> Golcar became an important centre for <a href="/wiki/Weaving" title="Weaving">weaving</a>.  Pharmacologist James Burrows grew up in the area.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Colne_Valley_Museum">Colne Valley Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golcar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Colne Valley Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley_Museum" title="Colne Valley Museum">Colne Valley Museum</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:ColneValleyMuseum(RLH).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/ColneValleyMuseum%28RLH%29.JPG/170px-ColneValleyMuseum%28RLH%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="170" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/ColneValleyMuseum%28RLH%29.JPG/255px-ColneValleyMuseum%28RLH%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/ColneValleyMuseum%28RLH%29.JPG/340px-ColneValleyMuseum%28RLH%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3203" data-file-height="2728" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:ColneValleyMuseum(RLH).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Colne Valley Museum</div></div></div>
<p>Three 1840s <a href="/wiki/Weavers%27_cottage" title="Weavers&#39; cottage">weavers' cottages</a> were converted into the <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley_Museum" title="Colne Valley Museum">Colne Valley Museum</a> in 1970. The museum has several restored period rooms which are used to show temporary exhibitions. The museum was extended in 2008 following the purchase of the adjoining former fish and chip shop.
</p><p>Exhibits include a handloom and a <a href="/wiki/Spinning_jenny" title="Spinning jenny">spinning jenny</a>, invented by <a href="/wiki/James_Hargreaves" title="James Hargreaves">James Hargreaves</a>. In the loom chamber, spinning room, weavers sitting room and 'gas-lit' cloggers shop, the volunteer helpers give demonstrations of the type of crafts that would have existed during the 19th century.
</p><p>The museum is run by volunteers and opens at weekends and bank holidays. Various craft and working weekends are held through the year.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_facilities">Community facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Golcar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Community facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Golcar supports two <a href="/wiki/The_Scout_Association" title="The Scout Association">scout groups</a>: the 4th Golcar Scout Group, which was founded in 1951<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and the 39th Parkwood Scout Group, founded in 1908.
</p><p>The village has several sporting teams including football and cricket teams.
</p><p>The vllage has three churches, the Anglican Parish Church of St John the Evangelist opened in 1829 <sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and is a good example of a <a href="/wiki/Waterloo_church" class="mw-redirect" title="Waterloo church">Waterloo church</a>,  Golcar Baptist Church (<a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20120317164606/http://www.golcarbc.co.uk/">[1]</a>), founded in 1835 and Golcar Providence Methodist Church founded in 1876.
</p><p>Each year in early May the village hosts the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Golcar_Lily_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Golcar Lily Day (page does not exist)">Golcar Lily Day</a>.
</p><p>Golcar Brass Band (<a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20170917124720/http://golcarband.co.uk/">[2]</a>) was reformed in 1977 and practises at the Baptist Church on a Monday night.
</p>
