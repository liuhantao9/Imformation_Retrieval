<p><b>Morley</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> within the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a>, in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England.
</p><p>It lies approximately 5 miles (8&#160;km) south-west of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_city_centre" title="Leeds city centre">Leeds city centre</a>, built on seven hills: Scatcherd Hill, Dawson Hill, Daisy Hill, Chapel Hill, Hunger Hill, Troy Hill and Banks Hill. In 2011, the town and civil parish had populations of 44,440 and 27,738 respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town is split between the <a href="/wiki/Morley_North_(ward)" title="Morley North (ward)">Morley North</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morley_South_(ward)" title="Morley South (ward)">Morley South</a> <a href="/wiki/Electoral_ward" class="mw-redirect" title="Electoral ward">wards</a> (containing the town centre) of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a>, both making up the western half of the <a href="/wiki/Morley_and_Outwood_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Morley and Outwood (UK Parliament constituency)">Morley and Outwood</a> <a href="/wiki/UK_Parliament_constituencies" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Parliament constituencies">parliamentary constituency</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morley,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Morley is first mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a> in 1086 as <i>Morelege</i>, <i>Morelei</i> and <i>Moreleia</i>.  Morley means "open ground by a moor", from <a href="/wiki/Old_English_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Old English language">Old English</a> <i>mōr</i> "<a href="/wiki/Moorland" title="Moorland">moor</a>, clearing, pasture" + <i>lēah</i> "open ground, clearing". It also gave its name to <i>Morelei Wapentac</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Wapentake" class="mw-redirect" title="Wapentake">wapentake</a> which probably met at <a href="/wiki/Tingley" title="Tingley">Tingley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town was later famous for its textile industry, notably the cloth, <a href="/wiki/Shoddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Shoddy">shoddy</a>, which was worn by both sides in the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morley,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Morley_Map.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Morley_Map.jpg/220px-Morley_Map.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Morley_Map.jpg/330px-Morley_Map.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Morley_Map.jpg/440px-Morley_Map.jpg 2x" data-file-width="978" data-file-height="653" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Morley_Map.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Street map of Morley</div></div></div>
<p>Historically, Morley was the centre of one of two divisions of the <a href="/wiki/Wapentake" class="mw-redirect" title="Wapentake">wapentake</a> of <a href="/wiki/Agbrigg_and_Morley" title="Agbrigg and Morley">Agbrigg and Morley</a>. Morley became a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Municipal Borough">Municipal Borough</a> in 1889 and under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, was incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">Metropolitan District</a>. Morley is represented on <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a> by three wards (namely <a href="/wiki/Morley_North_(ward)" title="Morley North (ward)">Morley North</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morley_South_(ward)" title="Morley South (ward)">Morley South</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ardsley_and_Robin_Hood_(ward)" title="Ardsley and Robin Hood (ward)">Ardsley and Robin Hood</a>) each with three councillors. At the 2010 general election, Morley and Outwood was won by Ed Balls of the Labour Party, who had been MP for Normanton since 2005, and served as Labour's Shadow Chancellor of the Exchequer from 2011 until 2015. Balls narrowly lost the seat at the 2015 general election to Conservative Andrea Jenkyns.
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Town_council" title="Town council">town council</a> was established in 2000, though it does not cover <a href="/wiki/Drighlington" title="Drighlington">Drighlington</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gildersome" title="Gildersome">Gildersome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tingley" title="Tingley">Tingley</a> and <a href="/wiki/East_Ardsley" title="East Ardsley">East</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Ardsley" title="West Ardsley">West Ardsley</a> - areas formerly part of the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a>.
</p><p>Following a review of parliamentary representation in West Yorkshire, the <a href="/wiki/Morley_and_Rothwell_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Morley and Rothwell (UK Parliament constituency)">Morley and Rothwell</a> parliamentary constituency was abolished at the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2010 United Kingdom general election">2010 election</a> and replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Morley_and_Outwood_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Morley and Outwood (UK Parliament constituency)">Morley and Outwood</a> constituency.
</p><p>The town's <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> featured the symbolic principal industries of the municipal borough: <a href="/wiki/Textile_manufacturing" title="Textile manufacturing">textile manufacturing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coal_mining" title="Coal mining">coal mining</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quarry" title="Quarry">quarrying</a>.
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Morley_Town_Hall" title="Morley Town Hall">Morley Town Hall</a> is sometimes used for music recordings. Television programmes, <i><a href="/wiki/Heartbeat_(UK_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Heartbeat (UK TV series)">Heartbeat</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Emmerdale" title="Emmerdale">Emmerdale</a></i> have used its disused magistrates court and a <a href="/wiki/Cobblestone" title="Cobblestone">cobblestoned</a> street to one side. It hosts <a href="/wiki/Concert" title="Concert">concerts</a> by local schools and performances by the Morley Amateur Operatic Society, whose <a href="/wiki/Pantomime" title="Pantomime">pantomimes</a> have taken place at the Alexandra Hall for many years.
</p><p>Morley annually holds one of the largest St George's Day parades in the country and has been named "the most patriotic town in England".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Morley Market has been a feature since the town was formed. The market building has a large trading hall split up into units housing, <a href="/wiki/Butchers" class="mw-redirect" title="Butchers">butchers</a>, fashion shops and a <a href="/wiki/Caf%C3%A9" class="mw-redirect" title="Café">café</a>.
</p><p>Supermarkets in Morley include Morrisons, Wilkinsons, Home Bargains and B &amp; M in the town centre and there is also a 24-hour ASDA superstore.
</p><p>Scatcherd Park in the centre of Morley, by the Morley Leisure Centre, has a large playing field, a skate park, children's park, bowling green and memorial gardens. Events are held on the adjoining field in the summer months.
</p><p>On 21 February 2010, a statue of <a href="/wiki/Ernie_Wise" title="Ernie Wise">Ernie Wise</a> was erected outside Morley Post Office to divided opinion<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and unveiled by his widow, Doreen.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Wise had performed in the old nearby cinema, just around from the Post Office, of which is no longer in use and now derelict.
</p><p>On 25 June 2012, the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Torch" class="mw-redirect" title="Olympic Torch">Olympic Torch</a> paused at <a href="/wiki/The_Morley_Academy" title="The Morley Academy">the Morley Academy</a> on its tour of Britain ahead of the <a href="/wiki/London_2012" class="mw-redirect" title="London 2012">London Olympic Games 2012</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morley,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Morley_railway_station" title="Morley railway station">Morley railway station</a> is half a mile (800 m) from the town centre on the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Line">Huddersfield Line</a>. There is a service seven days a week to and from <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>, but on Sundays, the service is less frequent.
</p><p>Buses go to Leeds, Bradford, Wakefield and other West Yorkshire towns from bus stops near the Town Hall.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/M621_motorway" title="M621 motorway">M621 motorway</a> runs to the west of the town and the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62 motorway</a> to the south. Junctions 27 and 28 of the M62 are closest to Morley.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morley,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are numerous primary schools in Morley including Morley Newlands Academy, Churwell Primary, Seven Hills Primary School, Morley Victoria Primary School, Asquith Primary, Fountain Primary and St Francis Catholic Primary School. It also has an independent preparatory school; Queenswood School.
</p><p>Three secondary schools serve pupils from Morley: <a href="/wiki/The_Morley_Academy" title="The Morley Academy">The Morley Academy</a> (formerly Morley High School and Morley Grammar), <a href="/wiki/Bruntcliffe_Academy" title="Bruntcliffe Academy">Bruntcliffe Academy</a> and  <a href="/wiki/Woodkirk_High_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Woodkirk High School">Woodkirk High School</a> (technically in <a href="/wiki/West_Ardsley" title="West Ardsley">West Ardsley</a> but has a large proportion of students from Morley due to its catchment area).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morley,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Morley_R.F.C." title="Morley R.F.C.">Morley R.F.C.</a> was founded in 1878. When the Northern Union clubs broke away from the RFU to form the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a>, the Morley representatives missed the train to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> due to being in the pub. The club's heyday was in the 1970s when it won the Yorkshire Cup on five occasions. In April 2005 the club won the Powergen Intermediate Cup at <a href="/wiki/Twickenham" title="Twickenham">Twickenham</a>.
</p><p>Morley CC <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> club plays in the Bradford League, Morley Town AFC (currently of Yorkshire FA Premier Division) and Morley Amateur FC are the towns <a href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> club and Morley Borough play rugby league.
</p><p><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.leeds.gov.uk/sports/Pages/Morley-leisure-centre.aspx">Morley Leisure Centre</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> is newly built after undergoing a £33&#160;million joint project with Armley Leisure Centre completed 22 June 2010 boasting a main 25-metre swimming pool, 10-metre learner pool, 150-station Bodyline gym, numerous sports halls, a dance studio and a cafe.
</p><p>Independent Wrestling company Dynamic Pro Wrestling (DPW) is based in Morley and put on shows at Ackroyd Street Working Mens Club and around West Yorkshire until its closure in 2011.
</p><p>Morley was the home of seven times World Cycling Champion <a href="/wiki/Beryl_Burton" title="Beryl Burton">Beryl Burton</a>.
</p><p>Scatcherd Park Bowling club compete in 9 leagues
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Filmography">Filmography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Morley,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Filmography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Morley is a setting for <a href="/wiki/David_Peace" title="David Peace">David Peace</a>'s <i>Red Riding Quartet</i> novel and <a href="/wiki/Red_Riding" title="Red Riding">2009 television series</a> which explore West Yorkshire police corruption during the 1970s, and 1980s. <i>Emmerdale</i> often use Morley for filming court and wedding scenes in the town hall.
</p>
