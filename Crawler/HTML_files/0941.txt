<p><b>South Cambridgeshire</b> is a mostly rural <a href="/wiki/Non-metropolitan_district" title="Non-metropolitan district">local government district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cambridgeshire" title="Cambridgeshire">Cambridgeshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> with a population of 148,755 at the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  It was formed on 1 April 1974 by the merger of <a href="/wiki/Chesterton_Rural_District" title="Chesterton Rural District">Chesterton Rural District</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Cambridgeshire_Rural_District" title="South Cambridgeshire Rural District">South Cambridgeshire Rural District</a>.  It completely surrounds the city of <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">Cambridge</a>, which is administered separately from the district by <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_City_Council,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge City Council, England">Cambridge City Council</a>.
</p><p><i>Southern Cambridgeshire</i>, including both the district of South Cambridgeshire and the city of Cambridge, has a population of over 281,000 (including students) and an area of 1,017.28&#160;km square.
</p><p>On the abolition of <a href="/wiki/South_Herefordshire" title="South Herefordshire">South Herefordshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hereford" title="Hereford">Hereford</a> districts to form the unitary <a href="/wiki/Herefordshire" title="Herefordshire">Herefordshire</a> in 1998, South Cambridgeshire remained the only English district to completely encircle another. As the other parts of Britain consists of district-equivalents, none of them are shaped like a doughnut.
</p><p>The district's <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> contains a reference to the coat of arms of <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">Cambridge University</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  The motto, <i lang="nl" title="Dutch language text">Niet Zonder Arbyt</i>, means "Not Without Work" in old <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>; it was originally the motto of <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Vermuyden" title="Cornelius Vermuyden">Cornelius Vermuyden</a> who drained <a href="/wiki/The_Fens" title="The Fens">The Fens</a> in the 17th century.  The district council's headquarters moved from Cambridge to <a href="/wiki/Cambourne,_Cambridgeshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambourne, Cambridgeshire">Cambourne</a> in 2004.
</p><p>South Cambridgeshire has scored highly on the best places to live, according to <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>, which ranked South Cambridgeshire as the fifth best place to live in 2006.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> A Halifax survey rated South Cambridgeshire the best place to live in rural Britain.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Local_government">Local government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Cambridgeshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Local government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/South_Cambridgeshire_District_Council" title="South Cambridgeshire District Council">South Cambridgeshire District Council</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Cambridgeshire_local_elections" class="mw-redirect" title="South Cambridgeshire local elections">South Cambridgeshire local elections</a></div>
<p>South Cambridgeshire District Council's headquarters are located in South Cambridgeshire Hall in the Cambourne Business Park in <a href="/wiki/Cambourne" title="Cambourne">Cambourne</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The council consists of 45 councillors (reduced from 57 in 2018), representing 26 electoral wards (reduced from 34 in 2018). The <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Democrats_(UK)" title="Liberal Democrats (UK)">Liberal Democrats</a> now have a strong majority on the council, having gained control in the 2018 local elections which saw a major shift in voting; the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservatives</a> having had control since 2007. Prior to that, no party had overall control of the council.
</p>
