<p><b>Manchester Airport station</b> is a railway, tram, bus and coach station at <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport" title="Manchester Airport">Manchester Airport</a>, England which opened at the same time as the second air terminal in 1993. The station is <span class="frac nowrap">9<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>4</sub></span> miles (15.7&#160;km) south of Manchester Piccadilly, situated at the end of a short branch from the <a href="/wiki/Styal_Line" title="Styal Line">Styal Line</a> which is accessed via a triangular junction between <a href="/wiki/Heald_Green_railway_station" title="Heald Green railway station">Heald Green</a> and <a href="/wiki/Styal_railway_station" title="Styal railway station">Styal</a> stations. <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Metrolink" title="Manchester Metrolink">Manchester Metrolink</a> tram services were extended to the airport in November 2014 and operate to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria_station" title="Manchester Victoria station">Manchester Victoria</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Airport_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is <span class="frac nowrap">9<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>4</sub></span> miles (15.7&#160;km) south of <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Piccadilly_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Piccadilly railway station">Manchester Piccadilly</a> at the end of a short branch from the <a href="/wiki/Styal_Line" title="Styal Line">Styal Line</a> constructed by <a href="/wiki/British_Rail" title="British Rail">British Rail</a> in 1993. A branch of <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Metrolink" title="Manchester Metrolink">Manchester Metrolink</a> runs into it. It is accessed via a triangular junction located between <a href="/wiki/Heald_Green_railway_station" title="Heald Green railway station">Heald Green</a> and <a href="/wiki/Styal_railway_station" title="Styal railway station">Styal</a>. The station platforms are connected by escalator, lift, ramps and an elevated covered walkway (known as the "Sky Link") to the airport terminal buildings in which is a manned railway ticket office. Throughout the airport complex, the railway station is known as "The Station" and is signposted as such.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Airport_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_platforms">New platforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Airport_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: New platforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2008, a third platform opened after a year of construction and a total cost of £15 million. The following year <a href="/wiki/Network_Rail" title="Network Rail">Network Rail</a> noted that it had "greatly increased operational flexibility at the station, and reduced the number of times that late-running trains had had to be terminated at Manchester Piccadilly."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, Network Rail stated that the creation of the third platform has meant that the capacity at Manchester Airport will become constrained by the layover of the trains and congestion at the throat. To solve this issue they have recommended building a line underneath the airport towards Northwich in the 2019 to 2024 period.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2012, support for a fourth railway platform was announced by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Government of the United Kingdom">Government</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The current platforms are eight carriage lengths long, so each can accommodate two trains of four carriages. A fourth platform will allow greater use of longer trains of six carriages or more.  This had been recommended as part of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hub" title="Northern Hub">Northern Hub</a> scheme of rail improvements around Manchester by the 2010 Manchester Hub Rail Study and the 2011 Northern Route Utilisation Strategy, with an estimated cost of £23 million.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2014, construction commenced on a new £20m fourth platform at the station. The start of construction was attended by the <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_Exchequer" title="Chancellor of the Exchequer">Chancellor of the Exchequer</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Osborne" title="George Osborne">George Osborne</a>. Construction of the new platform was completed in May 2015 - three years earlier than the 2018 estimate given in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This was to allow engineering work to overlap with that being done on the Manchester Metrolink extension to the airport (see below) so that the fourth platform could be used for the extra traffic generated by the <a href="/wiki/Ordsall_Chord" title="Ordsall Chord">Ordsall Chord</a> (opened 10 December 2017), while the fourth platform itself opened to passengers in Autumn 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="HS2">HS2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Airport_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: HS2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Interchange" title="Manchester Interchange">Manchester Interchange</a></div>
<p>In January 2013, the Government announced that Manchester Airport would be included in the second phase of the <a href="/wiki/High_Speed_2" title="High Speed 2">High Speed 2</a> railway line. This will enable Manchester Airport to be reached from <a href="/wiki/London_Euston_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="London Euston railway station">London Euston</a> in 59 minutes. The new line and associated new station will pass to the west of the Airport, away from the existing railway station and transport interchange, giving the airport two disconnected railways stations approximately 1 mile apart. <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Rail_services">National Rail services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Airport_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: National Rail services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Manchester_airport_ts2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Manchester_airport_ts2.jpg/200px-Manchester_airport_ts2.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Manchester_airport_ts2.jpg/300px-Manchester_airport_ts2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Manchester_airport_ts2.jpg/400px-Manchester_airport_ts2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Manchester_airport_ts2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_185" title="British Rail Class 185">Class 185</a> on platform 2 (was 1a) arriving from <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Piccadilly_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Piccadilly railway station">Manchester Piccadilly</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Greater_Manchester_Railways_map.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Greater_Manchester_Railways_map.svg/220px-Greater_Manchester_Railways_map.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="159" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Greater_Manchester_Railways_map.svg/330px-Greater_Manchester_Railways_map.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Greater_Manchester_Railways_map.svg/440px-Greater_Manchester_Railways_map.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="926" data-file-height="669" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Greater_Manchester_Railways_map.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map showing heavy rail and Metrolink access to the airport</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a>, <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> and <a href="/wiki/Transport_for_Wales_Rail" class="mw-redirect" title="Transport for Wales Rail">Transport for Wales</a> are the three current franchise holders that serve Manchester Airport. Services between the airport and <a href="/wiki/Crewe_railway_station" title="Crewe railway station">Crewe</a> (for connections to <a href="/wiki/Southern_England" title="Southern England">Southern England</a>); Manchester Piccadilly; various destinations throughout <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a> and, since December 2007, to <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_Central_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Glasgow Central railway station">Glasgow Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Waverley_railway_station" title="Edinburgh Waverley railway station">Edinburgh Waverley</a> via <a href="/wiki/Carlisle_railway_station" title="Carlisle railway station">Carlisle</a>.
</p><p>There is a frequent service from the airport to Manchester Piccadilly. Services also operate during the night to Manchester Piccadilly and beyond including <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>; <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York</a>;  <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_railway_station" title="Doncaster railway station">Doncaster</a>; <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield railway station">Sheffield</a>; <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_North_railway_station" title="Blackpool North railway station">Blackpool North</a>.
</p><p>There are currently nine trains per hour to Manchester Piccadilly. There are no long distance services to the airport from the south; a <a href="/wiki/Central_Trains" title="Central Trains">Central Trains</a> service from Manchester Airport to <a href="/wiki/Skegness_railway_station" title="Skegness railway station">Skegness</a> via <a href="/wiki/Derby_railway_station" title="Derby railway station">Derby</a> was cut back to <a href="/wiki/Crewe_railway_station" title="Crewe railway station">Crewe</a> in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>,there was also Virgin Crosscountry services from Birmingham New Street to Edinburgh or Glasgow, the Birmingham Services were cut in 2003 and the Scotland services were transferred to TransPennine Express in 2007.
</p><p>In May 2016, <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Trains_Wales" title="Arriva Trains Wales">Arriva Trains Wales</a> began operating an hourly (off peak) Monday - Saturday service to <a href="/wiki/Llandudno_railway_station" title="Llandudno railway station">Llandudno</a> via <a href="/wiki/Chester_railway_station" title="Chester railway station">Chester</a>. It now operates 57 services a week compared to the previous 17. <a href="/wiki/First_TransPennine_Express" title="First TransPennine Express">First TransPennine Express</a> previously ran the service to Blackpool North but this was passed on to the new <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern franchise</a> in April 2016.
</p><p>It was planned that infrastructure developments that are part of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hub" title="Northern Hub">Northern Hub</a> system of schemes such as the <a href="/wiki/Ordsall_Chord" title="Ordsall Chord">Ordsall Chord</a> should enable direct trains to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Victoria railway station">Manchester Victoria</a> and on towards <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange" title="Bradford Interchange">Bradford</a> from May 2019. However the introduction of the May 2018 timetable, one of the most radical in decades, caused punctuality through Manchester to drop sharply and it became common for TransPennine Express to terminate delayed services at Manchester Piccadilly or Victoria rather than continue onto the final destination at Manchester Airport.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Any future additional services to the Airport are in doubt without further infrastructure works; unresolved issues surround the lack of new 'through' platforms at Manchester Piccadilly which have been shelved by the government and the <a href="/wiki/Styal_Line" title="Styal Line">Styal Line</a> to Manchester Airport operating at full capacity with little resilience to absorb delays.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Metrolink" title="Manchester Metrolink">Manchester Metrolink</a> light rail network was extended from St Werburgh's Road to Manchester Airport as part of the Phase 3 expansion project.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-2006-map_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-2006-map-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The Metrolink station has been built adjacent to the airport station.<sup id="cite_ref-metro-geog-map_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metro-geog-map-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It is the terminus of the <a href="/wiki/Airport_Line_(Manchester_Metrolink)" title="Airport Line (Manchester Metrolink)">Airport Line</a>.
</p><p>Following a period of uncertainty due to <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester_Transport_Innovation_Fund" title="Greater Manchester Transport Innovation Fund">funding problems</a>, the plans were finally given the go-ahead in May 2009. The confirmed route would not complete the full airport loop as proposed, but trams would run along the northern route via <a href="/wiki/Wythenshawe" title="Wythenshawe">Wythenshawe</a>. Completion of the western side of the loop is subject to further funding in a later project.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC-backontrack_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-backontrack-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MEN-130509-2_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MEN-130509-2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> An opening date of Summer 2016 had originally been indicated for opening of the Airport line<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> but the line opened early on 3 November 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Transport_for_Greater_Manchester_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Transport_for_Greater_Manchester-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Manchester_Airport_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Trams run between Manchester Airport and <a href="/wiki/Victoria_tram_stop" class="mw-redirect" title="Victoria tram stop">Victoria</a> every 12 minutes. An early morning service runs from 03:19–06:00 am every 20 minutes.
