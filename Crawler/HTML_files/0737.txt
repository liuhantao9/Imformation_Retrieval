<p><b>JD Sports Fashion plc</b>, more commonly known as just <b>JD Sports</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is a sports-<a href="/wiki/Fashion" title="Fashion">fashion</a> retail company based in <a href="/wiki/Bury,_Greater_Manchester" title="Bury, Greater Manchester">Bury, Greater Manchester</a>, England with shops throughout the United Kingdom, Ireland and the United States. It is listed on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a> and is a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/FTSE_100_Index" title="FTSE 100 Index">FTSE 100 Index</a>. It is a subsidiary of the <a href="/wiki/Pentland_Group" title="Pentland Group">Pentland Group</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=JD_Sports&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The letters JD in JD Sports stand for the initials of the founders of the company, John &amp; David. In 1981, the company was established by John Wardle and David Makin, trading from a single shop in <a href="/wiki/Bury,_Greater_Manchester" title="Bury, Greater Manchester">Bury</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tele_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tele-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 1983, the company opened a store in the <a href="/wiki/Arndale_Centre" title="Arndale Centre">Arndale Centre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1989, the first London store opened on <a href="/wiki/Oxford_Street" title="Oxford Street">Oxford Street</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. In October 1996, the company was first listed on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-history_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2001 it acquired nearly 200 further stores with the acquisition of First Sport from <a href="/wiki/Blacks_Outdoor_Retail" title="Blacks Outdoor Retail">Blacks Leisure Group</a>. In October 2005 it bought 70 stores from the administrators of Allsports, who entered administration in September 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-history_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In May 2005, <a href="/wiki/Pentland_Group" title="Pentland Group">Pentland Group</a> bought Wardle's and Makin's shares for £44.6M; the pair later resigned from the board.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In December 2007, the company bought out Bank Stores, which sold fashion clothing, such as Firetrap, Alu, Henleys &amp; Adidas Originals for around £19M.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>JD Sports is the official supplier and sponsor of numerous <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">association football</a> teams, players and associations. In August 2008, JD Sports announced sponsorship deals with <a href="/wiki/A.F.C._Bournemouth" title="A.F.C. Bournemouth">Bournemouth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlton_Athletic_F.C." title="Charlton Athletic F.C.">Charlton Athletic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dundee_United_F.C." title="Dundee United F.C.">Dundee United</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_F.C." title="Blackpool F.C.">Blackpool</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luton_Town_F.C." title="Luton Town F.C.">Luton Town</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Oldham_Athletic_A.F.C." title="Oldham Athletic A.F.C.">Oldham Athletic</a>. In May 2009, JD Sports acquired <i>Chausport</i>, which operated 75 small stores in France. In addition, JD acquired the rugby heritage brands 'Canterbury' and '<a href="/wiki/Canterbury_of_New_Zealand" title="Canterbury of New Zealand">Canterbury of New Zealand</a>' as well as 'The Duffer of St. George' and 'Kooga Rugby' brands.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2011, JD Sports acquired Champion Sports for €19.6M,
In January 2012, JD Sports purchased the troubled <a href="/wiki/Blacks_Outdoor_Retail" title="Blacks Outdoor Retail">Blacks Leisure Group</a> from administration for a total of £20 million.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In February 2012, JD Sports acquired streetwear clothing brand FLY53, for an undisclosed sum.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The same year JD Sports acquired a 40% stake in fashion retailer <a href="/wiki/Tessuti" title="Tessuti">Tessuti</a>, before acquiring the brand in full in 2016. In February 2013, it purchased <a href="/wiki/Cloggs_Online_limited" class="mw-redirect" title="Cloggs Online limited">Cloggs</a>, a shoe retailer, out of administration however closed it in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2016, JD Sports opened its first store in Malaysia.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:JD_Sports,_Westfield_Carousel,_December_2018.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/JD_Sports%2C_Westfield_Carousel%2C_December_2018.jpg/220px-JD_Sports%2C_Westfield_Carousel%2C_December_2018.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/JD_Sports%2C_Westfield_Carousel%2C_December_2018.jpg/330px-JD_Sports%2C_Westfield_Carousel%2C_December_2018.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/JD_Sports%2C_Westfield_Carousel%2C_December_2018.jpg/440px-JD_Sports%2C_Westfield_Carousel%2C_December_2018.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4048" data-file-height="3036" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:JD_Sports,_Westfield_Carousel,_December_2018.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A JD Sports store in <a href="/wiki/Westfield_Carousel" title="Westfield Carousel">Westfield Carousel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perth" title="Perth">Perth</a></div></div></div>
<p>In November 2016, JD Sports acquired GO Outdoors for £112 million.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In late 2016, JD Sports Fashion acquired an 80% stake in the Australian retailer Next Athleisure for A$6.6 million.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The first Australian JD Sports store opened in Melbourne in April 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017 the company saw 30 per cent rise in sales<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> which was credited partially to growing trends in <a href="/wiki/Athleisure" title="Athleisure">athleisure</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2018, JD Sports reached a deal to acquire <a href="/wiki/Finish_Line,_Inc." title="Finish Line, Inc.">Finish Line</a> for $558 million.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, JD Sports continued its expansion in the Asia Pacific region. It opened its first store in South Korea on 13 April 2018,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> its first Singapore store on 24 May 2018<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and its first store in Thailand on 9 November 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016 and again in 2019 JD Sports was the subject of accusations of mistreatment of its UK warehouse staff, with comparisons being made to Victorian 'dark satanic mills' and 'prison' conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
