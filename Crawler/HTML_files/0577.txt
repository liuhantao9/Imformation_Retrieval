	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Victorian architecture</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg/250px-St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="211" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg/375px-St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg/500px-St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg 2x" data-file-width="8466" data-file-height="7140" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Pancras_Railway_Station_2012-06-23.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St. Pancras railway station and Midland Hotel in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, opened in 1868</div></div></div>
<p><b>Victorian architecture</b> is a series of <a href="/wiki/Architectural_style" title="Architectural style">architectural revival styles</a> in the mid-to-late 19th century. <i>Victorian</i> refers to the reign of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> (1837–1901), called the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian era</a>, during which period the styles known as Victorian were used in construction. However, many elements of what is typically termed "Victorian" architecture did not become popular until later in Victoria's reign. The styles often included interpretations and <a href="/wiki/Eclecticism_in_art" title="Eclecticism in art">eclectic</a> revivals of historic styles. The name represents the British and French custom of naming architectural styles for a reigning monarch. Within this naming and classification scheme, it followed <a href="/wiki/Georgian_architecture" title="Georgian architecture">Georgian architecture</a> and later <a href="/wiki/Regency_architecture" title="Regency architecture">Regency architecture</a>, and was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Edwardian_architecture" title="Edwardian architecture">Edwardian architecture</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Victorian_architecture_in_the_United_Kingdom">Victorian architecture in the United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Victorian_architecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Victorian architecture in the United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Selwyn_College_Gatehouse_Tower,_Cambridge,_UK_-_Diliff.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Selwyn_College_Gatehouse_Tower%2C_Cambridge%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg/220px-Selwyn_College_Gatehouse_Tower%2C_Cambridge%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="217" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Selwyn_College_Gatehouse_Tower%2C_Cambridge%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg/330px-Selwyn_College_Gatehouse_Tower%2C_Cambridge%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Selwyn_College_Gatehouse_Tower%2C_Cambridge%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg/440px-Selwyn_College_Gatehouse_Tower%2C_Cambridge%2C_UK_-_Diliff.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4148" data-file-height="4089" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Selwyn_College_Gatehouse_Tower,_Cambridge,_UK_-_Diliff.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Selwyn_College,_Cambridge" title="Selwyn College, Cambridge">Selwyn College, Cambridge</a></div></div></div>
<p>During the early 19th century, the romantic <a href="/wiki/Medieval" class="mw-redirect" title="Medieval">medieval</a> <a href="/wiki/Gothic_revival" class="mw-redirect" title="Gothic revival">Gothic revival</a> style was developed as a reaction to the <a href="/wiki/Symmetry" title="Symmetry">symmetry</a> of <a href="/wiki/Palladian_architecture" title="Palladian architecture">Palladianism</a>, and such buildings as <a href="/wiki/Fonthill_Abbey" title="Fonthill Abbey">Fonthill Abbey</a> were built. By the middle of the 19th century, as a result of new <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>, construction was able to incorporate <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a> as a building component; one of the greatest exponents of this was <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Paxton" title="Joseph Paxton">Joseph Paxton</a>, architect of <a href="/wiki/The_Crystal_Palace" title="The Crystal Palace">the Crystal Palace</a>. Paxton also continued to build such houses as <a href="/wiki/Mentmore_Towers" title="Mentmore Towers">Mentmore Towers</a>, in the still popular <a href="/wiki/English_Renaissance" title="English Renaissance">English Renaissance</a> styles. New methods of construction were developed in this era of prosperity, but ironically the architectural styles, as developed by such architects as <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Pugin" title="Augustus Pugin">Augustus Pugin</a>, were typically retrospective.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, the architect <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Thomson" title="Alexander Thomson">Alexander Thomson</a> who practiced in <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a> was a pioneer of the use of <a href="/wiki/Cast_iron" title="Cast iron">cast iron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a> for commercial buildings, blending neo-classical conventionality with Egyptian and oriental themes to produce many truly original structures. Other notable Scottish architects of this period are <a href="/wiki/Archibald_Simpson" title="Archibald Simpson">Archibald Simpson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Marshall_Mackenzie" title="Alexander Marshall Mackenzie">Alexander Marshall Mackenzie</a> whose stylistically varied work can be seen in the <a href="/wiki/Architecture_of_Aberdeen" title="Architecture of Aberdeen">architecture of Aberdeen</a>.
</p><p>While Scottish architects pioneered this style it soon spread right across the United Kingdom and remained popular for another 40 years. Its architectural value in preserving and reinventing the past is significant. Its influences were diverse but the Scottish architects who practiced it were inspired by unique ways to blend architecture, purpose, and everyday life in a meaningful way.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_styles_popularised_during_the_period">Other styles popularised during the period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Victorian_architecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Other styles popularised during the period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_spread_of_Victorian_styles">International spread of Victorian styles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Victorian_architecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: International spread of Victorian styles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:China_Merchants_Bank_Building,_Shanghai.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/China_Merchants_Bank_Building%2C_Shanghai.JPG/220px-China_Merchants_Bank_Building%2C_Shanghai.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/China_Merchants_Bank_Building%2C_Shanghai.JPG/330px-China_Merchants_Bank_Building%2C_Shanghai.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/China_Merchants_Bank_Building%2C_Shanghai.JPG/440px-China_Merchants_Bank_Building%2C_Shanghai.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4912" data-file-height="3264" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:China_Merchants_Bank_Building,_Shanghai.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/China_Merchants_Bank_Building,_Shanghai" class="mw-redirect" title="China Merchants Bank Building, Shanghai">China Merchants Bank Building</a> is an example of Victorian architecture found in Shanghai, China</div></div></div>
<p>During the 18th century, a few English architects emigrated to the colonies, but as the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> became firmly established during the 19th century, many architects emigrated at the start of their careers. Some chose the United States, and others went to Canada, Australia and New Zealand. Normally, they applied architectural styles that were fashionable when they left England. By the latter half of the century, however, improving transport and communications meant that even remote parts of the Empire had access to publications such as the magazine <i><a href="/wiki/The_Builder" class="mw-redirect" title="The Builder">The Builder</a></i>, which helped colonial architects keep informed about current fashion. Thus, the influence of English architecture spread across the world. Several prominent architects produced English-derived designs around the world, including <a href="/wiki/William_Butterfield" title="William Butterfield">William Butterfield</a> (<a href="/wiki/St_Peter%27s_Cathedral,_Adelaide" title="St Peter&#39;s Cathedral, Adelaide">St Peter's Cathedral, Adelaide</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Wrey_Mould" title="Jacob Wrey Mould">Jacob Wrey Mould</a> (Chief Architect of Public Works in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>).
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Melbourne_Collins_Street_Architecture.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Melbourne_Collins_Street_Architecture.jpg/220px-Melbourne_Collins_Street_Architecture.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Melbourne_Collins_Street_Architecture.jpg/330px-Melbourne_Collins_Street_Architecture.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Melbourne_Collins_Street_Architecture.jpg/440px-Melbourne_Collins_Street_Architecture.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2288" data-file-height="1712" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Melbourne_Collins_Street_Architecture.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Modern skyscrapers on <a href="/wiki/Collins_Street,_Melbourne" title="Collins Street, Melbourne">Collins Street, Melbourne</a> have been deliberately set back from the street in order to retain Victorian-era buildings.</div></div></div>

There were fifteen styles that predominated:<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEApperlyIrvingReynolds199440-97_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEApperlyIrvingReynolds199440-97-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_America">North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Victorian_architecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alamo_Sq_Painted_Ladies_1,_SF,_CA,_jjron_26.03.2012.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Alamo_Sq_Painted_Ladies_1%2C_SF%2C_CA%2C_jjron_26.03.2012.jpg/220px-Alamo_Sq_Painted_Ladies_1%2C_SF%2C_CA%2C_jjron_26.03.2012.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="154" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Alamo_Sq_Painted_Ladies_1%2C_SF%2C_CA%2C_jjron_26.03.2012.jpg/330px-Alamo_Sq_Painted_Ladies_1%2C_SF%2C_CA%2C_jjron_26.03.2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Alamo_Sq_Painted_Ladies_1%2C_SF%2C_CA%2C_jjron_26.03.2012.jpg/440px-Alamo_Sq_Painted_Ladies_1%2C_SF%2C_CA%2C_jjron_26.03.2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2930" data-file-height="2049" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alamo_Sq_Painted_Ladies_1,_SF,_CA,_jjron_26.03.2012.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Painted_Ladies" class="mw-redirect" title="Painted Ladies">Painted Ladies</a> are an example of Victorian architecture found in San Francisco, California</div></div></div>
<p>In the United States, 'Victorian' architecture generally describes styles that were most popular between 1860 and 1900. A list of these styles most commonly includes <a href="/wiki/Second_Empire_(architecture)" class="mw-redirect" title="Second Empire (architecture)">Second Empire</a> (1855–85), <a href="/wiki/Stick-Eastlake" class="mw-redirect" title="Stick-Eastlake">Stick-Eastlake</a> (1860–ca. 1890), <a href="/wiki/Folk_Victorian" title="Folk Victorian">Folk Victorian</a> (1870-1910), <a href="/wiki/Queen_Anne_Style_architecture_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen Anne Style architecture (United States)">Queen Anne</a> (1880–1910), <a href="/wiki/Richardsonian_Romanesque" title="Richardsonian Romanesque">Richardsonian Romanesque</a> (1880–1900), and <a href="/wiki/Shingle_Style_architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Shingle Style architecture">Shingle</a> (1880–1900). As in the United Kingdom, examples of Gothic Revival and Italianate continued to be constructed during this period, and are therefore sometimes called Victorian. Some historians classify the later years of Gothic Revival as a distinctive Victorian style named High Victorian Gothic. <a href="/wiki/Stick-Eastlake" class="mw-redirect" title="Stick-Eastlake">Stick-Eastlake</a>, a manner of geometric, machine-cut decorating derived from Stick and Queen Anne, is sometimes considered a distinct style. On the other hand, terms such as "<a href="/wiki/Painted_Ladies" class="mw-redirect" title="Painted Ladies">Painted Ladies</a>" or "gingerbread" may be used to describe certain Victorian buildings, but do not constitute a specific style. The names of architectural styles (as well as their adaptations) varied between countries. Many homes combined the elements of several different styles and are not easily distinguishable as one particular style or another.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Victorian_facades_on_16th_Street_in_San_Francisco.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Victorian_facades_on_16th_Street_in_San_Francisco.jpg/220px-Victorian_facades_on_16th_Street_in_San_Francisco.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Victorian_facades_on_16th_Street_in_San_Francisco.jpg/330px-Victorian_facades_on_16th_Street_in_San_Francisco.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Victorian_facades_on_16th_Street_in_San_Francisco.jpg/440px-Victorian_facades_on_16th_Street_in_San_Francisco.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4989" data-file-height="3326" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Victorian_facades_on_16th_Street_in_San_Francisco.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Victorian facades on 16th Street, San Francisco</div></div></div>
<p>In the United States of America, notable cities which developed or were rebuilt largely during this era include <a href="/wiki/Alameda,_California" title="Alameda, California">Alameda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Astoria,_Oregon" title="Astoria, Oregon">Astoria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albany,_New_York" title="Albany, New York">Albany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deal,_New_Jersey" title="Deal, New Jersey">Deal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Troy,_New_York" title="Troy, New York">Troy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_Heights" title="Brooklyn Heights">Brooklyn Heights</a> and <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Flatbush" title="Victorian Flatbush">Victorian Flatbush</a> sections of New York City, <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Ohio" title="Columbus, Ohio">Columbus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eureka,_California" title="Eureka, California">Eureka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galena,_Illinois" title="Galena, Illinois">Galena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">Galveston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Rapids,_Michigan" title="Grand Rapids, Michigan">Grand Rapids</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="Baltimore, Maryland">Baltimore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jersey_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Jersey City">Jersey City</a>/<a href="/wiki/Hoboken,_New_Jersey" title="Hoboken, New Jersey">Hoboken</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_May,_New_Jersey" title="Cape May, New Jersey">Cape May</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milwaukee" title="Milwaukee">Milwaukee</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Paul,_Minnesota" title="Saint Paul, Minnesota">Saint Paul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Midtown_Sacramento" title="Midtown Sacramento">Midtown</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angelino_Heights,_Los_Angeles" title="Angelino Heights, Los Angeles">Angelino Heights</a> and <a href="/wiki/Westlake,_Los_Angeles" title="Westlake, Los Angeles">Westlake</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Angeles, California">Los Angeles</a>. San Francisco is well known for its extensive Victorian architecture, particularly in the <a href="/wiki/Haight-Ashbury" title="Haight-Ashbury">Haight-Ashbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lower_Haight" class="mw-redirect" title="Lower Haight">Lower Haight</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alamo_Square" class="mw-redirect" title="Alamo Square">Alamo Square</a>, <a href="/wiki/Noe_Valley" class="mw-redirect" title="Noe Valley">Noe Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Castro,_San_Francisco" class="mw-redirect" title="The Castro, San Francisco">Castro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nob_Hill" class="mw-redirect" title="Nob Hill">Nob Hill</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Heights" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Heights">Pacific Heights</a> neighborhoods.
</p><p>The extent to which any one is the "largest surviving example" is debated, with numerous qualifications. The <a href="/wiki/Distillery_District" title="Distillery District">Distillery District</a> in Toronto, Ontario contains the largest and best preserved collection of Victorian-era industrial architecture in North America.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> <a href="/wiki/Cabbagetown_(Toronto)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabbagetown (Toronto)">Cabbagetown</a> is the largest and most continuous Victorian residential area in North America.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Other Toronto Victorian neighbourhoods include <a href="/wiki/The_Annex" title="The Annex">The Annex</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parkdale,_Toronto" title="Parkdale, Toronto">Parkdale</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rosedale,_Toronto" title="Rosedale, Toronto">Rosedale</a>. In the US, the <a href="/wiki/South_End,_Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="South End, Boston, Massachusetts">South End</a> of Boston is recognized by the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> as the oldest and largest Victorian neighborhood in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Old_Louisville" title="Old Louisville">Old Louisville</a> in <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, Kentucky</a> also claims to be the nation's largest Victorian neighborhood.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">Richmond, Virginia</a> is home to several large Victorian neighborhoods, the most prominent being <a href="/wiki/Fan_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Fan District">The Fan</a>. The Fan district is best known locally as Richmond's largest and most 'European' of Richmond's neighborhoods and nationally as the largest contiguous Victorian neighborhood in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Old_West_End_District_(Toledo,_Ohio)" title="Old West End District (Toledo, Ohio)">Old West End</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Toledo,_Ohio" title="Toledo, Ohio">Toledo, Ohio</a> is recognized as the largest collection of late Victorian and <a href="/wiki/Edwardian_architecture" title="Edwardian architecture">Edwardian</a> homes in the United States, east of the <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-stine_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stine-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Summit_Avenue_(St._Paul)" title="Summit Avenue (St. Paul)">Summit Avenue</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saint_Paul,_Minnesota" title="Saint Paul, Minnesota">Saint Paul, Minnesota</a> has the longest line of Victorian homes in the country. <a href="/wiki/Over-The-Rhine" class="mw-redirect" title="Over-The-Rhine">Over-The-Rhine</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati,_Ohio" class="mw-redirect" title="Cincinnati, Ohio">Cincinnati, Ohio</a> has the largest collection of early Victorian <a href="/wiki/Italianate_architecture" title="Italianate architecture">Italianate architecture</a> in the United States,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and is an example of an intact 19th-century urban neighborhood.<sup id="cite_ref-otr_historic_signs_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-otr_historic_signs-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The photo album <i>L'Architecture Americaine</i> by <a href="/wiki/Albert_Levy_(photographer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Albert Levy (photographer)">Albert Levy</a> published in 1886 is perhaps the first recognition in Europe of the new forces emerging in North American architecture.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTELewis1975_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTELewis1975-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Preservation">Preservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Victorian_architecture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Preservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Efforts to preserve landmarks of Victorian architecture are ongoing and are often led by the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Society" class="mw-redirect" title="Victorian Society">Victorian Society</a>. A recent campaign the group has taken on is the preservation of Victorian <a href="/wiki/Gas_holder" title="Gas holder">gasometers</a> after utility companies announced plans to demolish nearly 200 of the now-outdated structures.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
