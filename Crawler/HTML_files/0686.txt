<p><b>Superbike racing</b> is a category of <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_racing" title="Motorcycle racing">motorcycle racing</a> that employs highly modified production motorcycles, as opposed to <a href="/wiki/MotoGP" class="mw-redirect" title="MotoGP">MotoGP</a> in which purpose-built motorcycles are used. The <a href="/wiki/Superbike_World_Championship" title="Superbike World Championship">Superbike World Championship</a> is the official world championship series, though national Superbike championships are held in many countries, including the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. Superbike racing is generally popular with manufacturers, since it helps promote and sell their product, as captured by the slogan "Win on Sunday; Sell on Monday".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics_of_Superbike_racing_motorcycles">Characteristics of Superbike racing motorcycles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superbike_racing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics of Superbike racing motorcycles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Superbike racing motorcycles are derived from standard production models, so for a bike to be eligible, the manufacturer must first <a href="/wiki/Homologation" title="Homologation">homologate</a> the model and manufacture the required number of roadgoing machines. While rules vary from series to series, in general the motorcycles must maintain the same profile as their roadgoing counterparts, with the same overall appearance as seen from the front, rear and sides. In addition, the frame cannot be modified. Teams may modify some elements of the bike, including the suspensions, brakes, swingarm, and the diameter and size of the wheels.
</p><p>Superbike racing motorcycles must have <a href="/wiki/Four-stroke_engine" title="Four-stroke engine">four-stroke engines</a> of between 850&#160;cc and 1200&#160;cc for <a href="/wiki/V-twin" class="mw-redirect" title="V-twin">twins</a>, and between 750&#160;cc and 1000&#160;cc for <a href="/wiki/Four_cylinder" class="mw-redirect" title="Four cylinder">four cylinder</a> machines.
</p><p>The restriction to production models distinguishes Superbike racing from <a href="/wiki/MotoGP" class="mw-redirect" title="MotoGP">MotoGP</a> racing, which uses prototype machines that bear little resemblance to production machines. This is somewhat similar to the distinction in car racing between <a href="/wiki/Sports_cars" class="mw-redirect" title="Sports cars">sports cars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> cars, though the performance gap between Superbike and MotoGP racing is much smaller.
</p><p>The world's first 'Superbike' was built by brothers Ross and Ralph Hannan in the mid/late 1970s. First ridden successfully in Australia and overseas, including the Suzuka 8 hour and the Bol d'Or 24-hour endurance races, by <a href="/wiki/Graeme_Crosby" title="Graeme Crosby">Graeme Crosby</a> who went on to international success and was eventually inducted into the NZ sports "Hall of Fame".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Superbike_World_Championship">Superbike World Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superbike_racing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Superbike World Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Toseland-walker.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Toseland-walker.jpg/220px-Toseland-walker.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="142" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Toseland-walker.jpg/330px-Toseland-walker.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Toseland-walker.jpg/440px-Toseland-walker.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="515" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Toseland-walker.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/James_Toseland" title="James Toseland">James Toseland</a> (1) on a <a href="/wiki/Ducati" class="mw-redirect" title="Ducati">Ducati</a> leads <a href="/wiki/Chris_Walker_(motorcycle_racer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chris Walker (motorcycle racer)">Chris Walker</a> (9) on a <a href="/wiki/Kawasaki_motorcycles" title="Kawasaki motorcycles">Kawasaki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yukio_Kagayama" title="Yukio Kagayama">Yukio Kagayama</a> (71) on a <a href="/wiki/Suzuki" title="Suzuki">Suzuki</a> during a 2005 Superbike World Championship race</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Superbike_World_Championship" title="Superbike World Championship">Superbike World Championship</a></div>
<p>Superbike World Championship (also known as SBK) is the premier international superbike Championship. The championship was founded in 1988. It is regulated by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_Motocyclisme" title="Fédération Internationale de Motocyclisme">FIM</a> and managed and promoted by FGSport.
</p><p>Once regarded as the poor cousin to the more glamorous <a href="/wiki/MotoGP" class="mw-redirect" title="MotoGP">MotoGP</a> championship, the Superbike World Championship has grown into a world-class professional racing series. Many of the riders that competed in SBK over the years are household names among motorcycle racing fans. The most successful rider thus far has been England’s <a href="/wiki/Carl_Fogarty" title="Carl Fogarty">Carl Fogarty</a>, who won the championship four times (1994–95, 1998–99). <a href="/wiki/Ducati" class="mw-redirect" title="Ducati">Ducati</a> has been the most successful manufacturer in the series over the years, accumulating 15 manufacturer championships. <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a> has won it 6 times, with <a href="/wiki/Suzuki" title="Suzuki">Suzuki</a> claiming one championship. Australia's <a href="/wiki/Troy_Bayliss" title="Troy Bayliss">Troy Bayliss</a> won the 2006 and 2008 titles riding for Xerox Ducati and <a href="/wiki/James_Toseland" title="James Toseland">James Toseland</a>, from the UK, was the winner of the 2007 championship riding for Hannspree <a href="/wiki/Ten_Kate_Racing" title="Ten Kate Racing">Ten Kate Honda</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Superbike_series">National Superbike series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superbike_racing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: National Superbike series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>National Superbike series vary greatly in challenge and popularity, the most popular being in Britain and North America. Both Japan and Australia have well supported national superbikes series, though they only run for short, 10-race seasons.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Superbike_Championship">British Superbike Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superbike_racing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: British Superbike Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gregorio_Lavilla_BSB_2005.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Gregorio_Lavilla_BSB_2005.jpg/220px-Gregorio_Lavilla_BSB_2005.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="162" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Gregorio_Lavilla_BSB_2005.jpg/330px-Gregorio_Lavilla_BSB_2005.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Gregorio_Lavilla_BSB_2005.jpg/440px-Gregorio_Lavilla_BSB_2005.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="754" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gregorio_Lavilla_BSB_2005.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Gregorio_Lavilla" title="Gregorio Lavilla">Gregorio Lavilla</a> riding for Airwaves Ducati in the 2005 <a href="/wiki/British_Superbike_Championship" title="British Superbike Championship">British Superbike Championship</a> season</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/British_Superbike_Championship" title="British Superbike Championship">British Superbike Championship</a></div>
<p>The British Superbike championship (known to most as "BSB") is the leading <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_sport" title="Motorcycle sport">motorcycle racing</a> championship in the United Kingdom. It is managed and organised by MCRCB-Events. The commercial and television rights have been delegated to MotorSport Vision.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ducati" class="mw-redirect" title="Ducati">Ducati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kawasaki_motorcycles" title="Kawasaki motorcycles">Kawasaki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suzuki" title="Suzuki">Suzuki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Company" title="Yamaha Motor Company">Yamaha</a> all have well supported teams, while <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a> has the only <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_Corporation" title="Honda Racing Corporation">HRC</a> supported superbike team outside Japan. Japanese rider <a href="/wiki/Ryuichi_Kiyonari" title="Ryuichi Kiyonari">Ryuichi Kiyonari</a> won the 2006, 2007 and 2010 titles riding for HM Plant Honda.
</p>
<h3><span id="MotoAmerica_Superbike_Championship_.28previously_AMA_Superbike_Championship.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="MotoAmerica_Superbike_Championship_(previously_AMA_Superbike_Championship)">MotoAmerica Superbike Championship (previously AMA Superbike Championship)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superbike_racing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: MotoAmerica Superbike Championship (previously AMA Superbike Championship)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3><p>
Beginning in 2015 the US National Superbike championship moved to a new organization, MotoAmerica, after several years of decline. The new championship is known as the MotoAmerica Superbike Championship, and incorporates classes similar to those operating at world championship level, and other national series,  i.e. Superbike, Supersport, Superstock 1000, Superstock and KTM Junior cup. </p><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/MotoAmerica" title="MotoAmerica">MotoAmerica</a></div><p>. The aim of the new championship is to reinvigorate motorcycle road racing in North America and ultimately send its riders to the top-level international championships - <a href="/wiki/MotoGP" class="mw-redirect" title="MotoGP">MotoGP</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_Superbike" class="mw-redirect" title="World Superbike">World Superbike</a>. 
</p><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/AMA_Superbike_Championship" title="AMA Superbike Championship">AMA Superbike Championship</a></div>
<p>The AMA Superbike was the premier superbike racing series in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It was part of the AMA Pro Racing series, and was managed by the <a href="/wiki/American_Motorcyclist_Association" title="American Motorcyclist Association">AMA</a> until 2009 when the AMA sold the series to the Daytona MotorSports Group. The series was replaced in 2015 by the MotoAmerica Superbike championship.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="All_Japan_Superbike_Championship">All Japan Superbike Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superbike_racing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: All Japan Superbike Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/All_Japan_Road_Race_Championship" title="All Japan Road Race Championship">All Japan Road Race Championship</a></div>
<p>The All Japan Road Race Championship, also known as MFJ Superbike is the premiere motorcycle road racing championship in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and is run by <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_Federation_of_Japan" title="Motorcycle Federation of Japan">MFJ</a>. The championship started in 1967 and has been running a superbike class since 1994. The series runs a small 7 round schedule but has a large field of Japanese riders and bikes. Atsushi Watanabe won the 2007 championship riding a <a href="/wiki/Pops_Yoshimura" title="Pops Yoshimura">Yoshimura</a> <a href="/wiki/Suzuki" title="Suzuki">Suzuki</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mopar_Canadian_Superbike_Championship">Mopar Canadian Superbike Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superbike_racing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mopar Canadian Superbike Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Superbike_Championship" title="Canadian Superbike Championship">Canadian Superbike Championship</a></div>
<p>The Mopar Canadian Superbike Championship is the Canadian national Superbike series. The series runs from May to September and consists of six to eight rounds per season. Riders from the Canadian series often compete in <a href="/wiki/AMA_Superbike" class="mw-redirect" title="AMA Superbike">AMA Superbike</a> during the Canadian off-season. <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Szoke" title="Jordan Szoke">Jordan Szoke</a> won his 8th title in 2012, riding a <a href="/wiki/BMW_S1000RR" title="BMW S1000RR">BMW S1000RR</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nigeria_Superbike_Road_Race">Nigeria Superbike Road Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Superbike_racing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Nigeria Superbike Road Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nsbrr.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Nsbrr.jpg/220px-Nsbrr.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="121" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Nsbrr.jpg/330px-Nsbrr.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Nsbrr.jpg/440px-Nsbrr.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1099" data-file-height="606" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nsbrr.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>2014 Winner of the Nigeria Superbike Road Race, with the first and Second runners ups</div></div></div>
<p>The Nigeria Superbike Road Race, The Bikers Trophy (The BT) is held annually on the last weekend in November, in Edo State, Nigeria. It is the premier motorcycling sports event in Nigeria and West Africa with participants and spectators from all over the World. Modelled after the Isle of Man TT races, The Bikers Trophy (The BT) races take place in a time trial format over a 32&#160;km street circuits which traverse the 8 communities of Urhonigbe, Urohmehe, Umughun, Ogba, Evbonogbon, Ugo, Ekpokor, and Ugbokirima in Orhiomwon Local Government Area of Edo State.
</p><p>The 2014 event saw podium places go to Jack Affara (Champion), Armstrong Ngugu (2nd Position) and Ikhide Izokpu (3rd Position). Other countries with road races are the Netherlands, Spain, Belgium, Germany, Great Britain, the Czech Republic, Ukraine, New Zealand and Macau.
</p>
