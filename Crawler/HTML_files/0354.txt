	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Award or decoration</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p>An <b>award</b> is something given to a person, a group of people, like a sports team, or an organization in <a href="/wiki/Recognition_(sociology)" title="Recognition (sociology)">recognition</a> of their <a href="/wiki/Excellence" title="Excellence">excellence</a> in a certain field.<sup id="cite_ref-Oxford_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oxford-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> An award may be accompanied by a <a href="/wiki/Trophy" title="Trophy">trophy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Title_of_honor" title="Title of honor">title</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scholarship" title="Scholarship">scholarship</a> certificate, <a href="/wiki/Commemorative_plaque" title="Commemorative plaque">commemorative plaque</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medal" title="Medal">medal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Badge" title="Badge">badge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Award_pin" title="Award pin">pin</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Award_ribbon" class="mw-redirect" title="Award ribbon">ribbon</a>. The accompanying item may vary according to purpose. For instance, awards for sports tournaments often take the form of cups, following a tradition harking back to the ancient Greek tripod given to winners in athletic contests.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Cup" title="Stanley Cup">Stanley Cup</a> is a modern example. In contrast, awards for employee recognition often take the form of plaques or crystal pieces.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>. An award may carry a monetary <a href="/wiki/Prize" title="Prize">prize</a> given to the recipient. For example, the <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize" title="Nobel Prize">Nobel Prize</a> recognizes contributions to society, while the <a href="/wiki/Pulitzer_prize" class="mw-redirect" title="Pulitzer prize">Pulitzer prize</a> honors literary achievements. An award may also simply be a public acknowledgment of excellence, without any tangible token or prize. Finally, an award may recognize participation rather than victory. There is controversy regarding the appropriateness of participation awards for students in United States schools.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A <b>decoration</b> is an object, such as a <a href="/wiki/Medal" title="Medal">medal</a> or an <a href="/wiki/Order_(distinction)" title="Order (distinction)">order</a>'s insignia, that is awarded to honor the recipient (see <a href="/wiki/List_of_prizes,_medals_and_awards" class="mw-redirect" title="List of prizes, medals and awards">List of prizes, medals and awards</a>). It may be awarded by a <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign state</a> (see <a href="/wiki/State_decoration" title="State decoration">state decoration</a>), a <a href="/wiki/Fount_of_honour" title="Fount of honour">fount of honour</a> or an organization.
</p><p>An <i>honorable mention</i> is an award, prize or recognition given to something that does not make it to a higher standing but is worth mentioning in an <a href="/wiki/Honor" class="mw-redirect" title="Honor">honorable</a> way.
</p>
