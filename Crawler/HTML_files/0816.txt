<p><b>Tim Bricheno</b> (born <b>Timothy John Bricheno</b>, 6 July 1963, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, Yorkshire) is an English guitarist, songwriter and <a href="/wiki/Music_education" title="Music education">music teacher</a>. He was a member of several notable English <a href="/wiki/Indie_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Indie (music)">indie</a> bands, including <a href="/wiki/All_About_Eve_(band)" title="All About Eve (band)">All About Eve</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Sisters_of_Mercy" title="The Sisters of Mercy">The Sisters of Mercy</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> XC-NN (originally "CNN") and <a href="/wiki/Tin_Star_(band)" title="Tin Star (band)">Tin Star</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He has also played as a tour guest and recorded with <a href="/wiki/Mission_UK" class="mw-redirect" title="Mission UK">The Mission</a>.
</p><p>Bricheno has written music under several pseudonyms for <a href="/wiki/Musician" title="Musician">recording artists|media compositions</a> including <a href="/wiki/Dusty_Springfield" title="Dusty Springfield">Dusty Springfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gene_Pitney" title="Gene Pitney">Gene Pitney</a>. He currently plays in 'Jok', a band formed with ex XC-NN, and former Tin Star vocalist, David Tomlinson. Moved into media composition during the late 90's alongside brother Toby Bricheno.
</p><p>Tims name appears as songwriting tutor and course author at Middlesex University Hendon, ICMP (Institute of Contemporary Music) and the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Contemporary_Music" title="Academy of Contemporary Music">Academy of Contemporary Music</a> in <a href="/wiki/Guildford" title="Guildford">Guildford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
