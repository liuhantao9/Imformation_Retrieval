<p><b>Edgerton</b> is a suburb of the town of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The suburb is located along the <a href="/wiki/A629_road" title="A629 road">A629 road</a>, also known as Edgerton Road, from Huddersfield to <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>. It is some 1 mile (1.6&#160;km) west of the town centre. It is the location of many of the towns largest and most imposing houses, as well as the town's principal cemetery.<sup id="cite_ref-hdejl_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdejl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-kmbwpg_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kmbwpg-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although the place name has been traced back to the 14th century, Edgerton started its development in the early 19th century, when three substantial <a href="/wiki/Georgian_architecture" title="Georgian architecture">Georgian</a> houses were built in the area. One of these houses, Edgerton Hill, survives today as the town's Ukrainian Club. By 1896, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Corporation_Tramways" title="Huddersfield Corporation Tramways">Huddersfield Corporation Tramways</a> had built one of its pioneering <a href="/wiki/Steam_tram" class="mw-redirect" title="Steam tram">steam tram</a> lines through Edgerton. This was succeeded in turn by <a href="/wiki/Electric_tram" class="mw-redirect" title="Electric tram">electric trams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trolley_bus" class="mw-redirect" title="Trolley bus">trolley buses</a> and motor buses.<sup id="cite_ref-hdejl_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdejl-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Politically, Edgerton forms part of the Greenhead ward of the metropolitan borough of <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a> and is within the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Huddersfield (UK Parliament constituency)">Huddersfield parliamentary constituency</a>. It is bordered to the north by the suburb of <a href="/wiki/Birkby,_Huddersfield" title="Birkby, Huddersfield">Birkby</a>, to the east by the town centre, to the west by <a href="/wiki/Lindley,_Huddersfield" title="Lindley, Huddersfield">Lindley</a> and to the south by <a href="/wiki/Marsh,_Huddersfield" title="Marsh, Huddersfield">Marsh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kmbwpg_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kmbwpg-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
