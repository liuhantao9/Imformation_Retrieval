<p><b>Falih Rıfkı Atay</b> (1894, Istanbul – 20 March 1971, Istanbul) was a <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turkish</a> journalist, writer and politician between 1923 and 1950.<sup id="cite_ref-Bosworth1980_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bosworth1980-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Falih_R%C4%B1fk%C4%B1_Atay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was the son of Halil Hilmi Efendi, an <a href="/wiki/Imam" title="Imam">imam</a>. He was educated in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bosworth1980_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bosworth1980-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Falih began his career as a journalist in the <i><a href="/wiki/Tanin_(newspaper)" title="Tanin (newspaper)">Tanin</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Committee_of_Union_and_Progress" title="Committee of Union and Progress">CUP</a> newspaper.<sup id="cite_ref-Bosworth1980_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bosworth1980-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He later became the private secretary of <a href="/wiki/Talat_Pasha" class="mw-redirect" title="Talat Pasha">Talat Pasha</a>, and during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> accompanied <a href="/wiki/Jamal_Pasha" class="mw-redirect" title="Jamal Pasha">Jamal Pasha</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sinai_and_Palestine_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Sinai and Palestine Campaign">Sinai and Palestine Campaign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bosworth1980_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bosworth1980-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> After the war, he, with three other friends, founded the newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Ak%C5%9Fam" title="Akşam">Akşam</a></i> supporting the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_War_of_Independence" title="Turkish War of Independence">Turkish War of Independence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bosworth1980_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bosworth1980-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 1922, he travelled to the recaptured <a href="/wiki/Izmir" class="mw-redirect" title="Izmir">Izmir</a> to visit <a href="/wiki/Mustafa_Kemal_Atat%C3%BCrk" title="Mustafa Kemal Atatürk">Mustafa Kemal Atatürk</a>. Later, he became an editor-in-chief in the <i><a href="/wiki/Hakimiyet-i_Milliye" title="Hakimiyet-i Milliye">Hakimiyet-i Milliye</a></i>. He entered politics in 1923, and served as deputy of <a href="/wiki/Bolu" title="Bolu">Bolu</a> and later <a href="/wiki/Ankara" title="Ankara">Ankara</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Grand_National_Assembly_of_Turkey" title="Grand National Assembly of Turkey">parliament</a> until the <a href="/wiki/1950_Turkish_general_election" title="1950 Turkish general election">1950 Turkish general election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bosworth1980_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bosworth1980-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is the author of more than 30 works.<sup id="cite_ref-Bosworth1980_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bosworth1980-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Falih Rıfkı Atay died on 20 March 1971 in Istanbul. He was interred at <a href="/wiki/Zincirlikuyu_Cemetery" title="Zincirlikuyu Cemetery">Zincirlikuyu Cemetery</a> in Istanbul.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Falih_R%C4%B1fk%C4%B1_Atay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
