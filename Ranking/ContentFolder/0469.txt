	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Coat of arms of Huddersfield</h1>

<p>The <b>Coat of arms of Huddersfield</b> was the official symbol of the local government of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> (the Corporation of Huddersfield). The borough was abolished in 1974, 106 years after its incorporation in 1868, under the provisions of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> whereby control was ceded to <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees Metropolitan Council</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire Metropolitan County Council</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbolism">Symbolism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Symbolism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>
The arms were granted by the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Arms" title="College of Arms">College of Arms</a> by <a href="/wiki/Letters_patent" title="Letters patent">letters patent</a> dated October 12, 1868 to the Mayor, Aldermen, and Burgesses of the Borough of Huddersfield. The design was based on the arms of the family of Ramsden of Byrom, owners of the manors of <a href="/wiki/Almondbury" title="Almondbury">Almondbury</a> and Huddersfield: </p><blockquote><p>Argent on a chevron between three fleurs-de-lis Sable, as many rams' heads couped at the neck of the first.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>The crest of a ram's head is similar to that granted to the borough of <a href="/wiki/Barrow-in-Furness" title="Barrow-in-Furness">Barrow-in-Furness</a> in the previous year; this also referred to the Ramsden family arms. 
</p><p>The arms feature the motto <i lang="la" title="Latin language text">Juvat Impigros Deus</i> which is Latin for "God helps the diligent," also rendered as "God Helps the Industrious," and the common form, "God Helps Them That Help Themselves."
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Blazon">Blazon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coat_of_arms_of_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Blazon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>
The formal description, or <a href="/wiki/Blazon" title="Blazon">blazon</a>, of the arms is:</p><blockquote><p>
Or on a chevron between three Rams passant Sable as many towers argent. And for the Crest on a Wreath of the Colours a Rams head couped argent armed Or gorged with a collar sable in the mouth A Sprig of the Cotton-tree slipped and fructed proper. Motto: <i lang="la" title="Latin language text">Juvat Impigros Deus</i>.</p></blockquote>
