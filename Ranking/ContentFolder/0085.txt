<p><b>Baron Clinton</b> is a title in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_England" title="Peerage of England">Peerage of England</a>. Created in 1298 for <a href="/wiki/John_de_Clinton,_1st_Baron_Clinton" class="mw-redirect" title="John de Clinton, 1st Baron Clinton">John de Clinton</a>, it is the seventh-oldest barony in England.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation_and_early_history">Creation and early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Clinton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Creation and early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The peerage of Baron Clinton was created in 1298 for Sir <a href="/wiki/John_de_Clinton,_1st_Baron_Clinton" class="mw-redirect" title="John de Clinton, 1st Baron Clinton">John de Clinton, 1st Baron Clinton</a>, a knight who had served in the Scottish and French wars. The peerage was created by <a href="/wiki/Hereditary_peer#Writ_of_summons" title="Hereditary peer">writ</a>, which means that it can descend through both male and female lines.<sup id="cite_ref-burke_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-burke-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is thus one of the most ancient English titles still in existence. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/William_de_Clinton,_1st_Earl_of_Huntingdon" title="William de Clinton, 1st Earl of Huntingdon">William</a>, the younger son of the first Baron, was also summoned to parliament by writ on 6 September 1330 as <b>Baron de Clynton</b>, even though his elder brother, the second Baron, was sitting in parliament under the same title.<sup id="cite_ref-burke_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-burke-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was created <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Huntingdon" title="Earl of Huntingdon">Earl of Huntingdon</a> in 1337.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second Baron, <a href="/w/index.php?title=John_de_Clinton,_2nd_Baron_Clinton&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="John de Clinton, 2nd Baron Clinton (page does not exist)">John</a>, fought with the king's army, which defeated <a href="/wiki/Edward_II_of_England" title="Edward II of England">Edward II</a>'s cousin <a href="/wiki/Thomas,_2nd_Earl_of_Lancaster" title="Thomas, 2nd Earl of Lancaster">Thomas, Earl of Lancaster</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Boroughbridge" title="Battle of Boroughbridge">Battle of Boroughbridge</a> in 1321. He was knighted by 1324. The third Baron fought at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Poitiers_(1356)" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Poitiers (1356)">Battle of Poitiers</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Years%27_War" title="Hundred Years&#39; War">Hundred Years' War</a> and was the Constable of <a href="/wiki/Warwick_Castle" title="Warwick Castle">Warwick Castle</a> 1390-97. He married at least three times. His first wife, Idonea de Saye, was the daughter of Geoffrey de Saye, 2nd Lord Saye. He was succeeded in the barony by their grandson, son of their son Sir William Clinton (<i><a href="/wiki/Decessit_vita_patris" class="mw-redirect" title="Decessit vita patris">DVP</a></i> 25 October 1383).<sup id="cite_ref-burke_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-burke-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1399, upon the death of fourth Baron's cousin, Elizabeth de Saye de Falvesley Heron, Baroness Saye, the fourth Baron succeeded to the Saye lands and styled himself Lord Saye. He married three times; firstly, to Anne Trivett, daughter of Sir Thomas Trivett; secondly, to Alice or Anne FitzWarin, daughter of <a href="/wiki/William_de_Botreaux,_2nd_Baron_Botreaux" title="William de Botreaux, 2nd Baron Botreaux">2nd Baron Botreaux</a> and the widow of <a href="/wiki/Baron_FitzWarin" title="Baron FitzWarin">6th Lord Fitzwarin</a>; and thirdly to Mary Retford, widow of Sir <a href="/wiki/Henry_Redford" title="Henry Redford">Henry Retford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-burke_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-burke-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was succeeded by his son from his second marriage. The fifth Baron fought on the <a href="/wiki/House_of_York" title="House of York">Yorkist</a> side in the <a href="/wiki/Wars_of_the_Roses" title="Wars of the Roses">Wars of the Roses</a>. He was attainted in 1461 but later restored to his title. The sixth Baron was recognised in 1471 as Lord Clinton and Saye, but was not called to Parliament under either title. He married Elisabeth, daughter of Sir <a href="/wiki/Richard_Fiennes,_7th_Baron_Dacre" title="Richard Fiennes, 7th Baron Dacre">Richard Fiennes</a>, and took <a href="/wiki/Fiennes" title="Fiennes">Fiennes</a> as an alias, and Clinton and Saye baronies continued to be linked for several generations. The 10th and 11th Barons were called to Parliament as <b>Baron Clinton de Saye</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-burke_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-burke-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Edward_Clinton,_1st_Earl_of_Lincoln" title="Edward Clinton, 1st Earl of Lincoln">Edward Clinton, 9th Baron Clinton</a> was created <b>Earl of Lincoln</b> in 1572. The titles remained united until 1692 upon the death of his great-great-great-grandson, Edward de Clinton, fifth Earl of Lincoln and 13th Baron Clinton. The earldom was inherited by the late Earl's cousin, the sixth Earl (see the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Lincoln" title="Earl of Lincoln">Earl of Lincoln</a> for later history of this title) while the barony fell into <a href="/wiki/Abeyance" title="Abeyance">abeyance</a> between the issue of his two aunts, Lady Margaret Clinton (d. 1688) and Lady Arabella Clinton, the daughters of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Theophilus_Clinton,_4th_Earl_of_Lincol&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Theophilus Clinton, 4th Earl of Lincol (page does not exist)">Theophilus de Clinton, 4th Earl of Lincoln and 12th Baron Clinton</a>, a brother-in-law of <a href="/wiki/Charles_I_of_England" title="Charles I of England">Charles I</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-burke_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-burke-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Barony_into_first_abeyance">Barony into first abeyance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Clinton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Barony into first abeyance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

Motto: <i>Forte Scutum Salus Ducum</i> ("A Strong Shield is the Salvation of Leaders")<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>The abeyance of 1692 was terminated in 1721 in favour of <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Fortescue,_1st_Earl_Clinton" title="Hugh Fortescue, 1st Earl Clinton">Hugh Fortescue</a> (d. 1751), fourteenth Baron Clinton, the grandson of Lady Margaret Clinton (d. 1688), the eldest daughter of Theophilus de Clinton, 4th Earl of Lincoln and 12th Baron Clinton (d. 1667). Lady Margaret had married <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Boscawen_(1625%E2%80%931701)" title="Hugh Boscawen (1625–1701)">Hugh Boscawen</a> (1625–1701), MP, of Tregothnan in Cornwall, and their daughter Bridget Boscawen (d. 1708) married <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Fortescue_(1665%E2%80%931719)" title="Hugh Fortescue (1665–1719)">Hugh Fortescue</a> (1665–1719) (son of Arthur Fortescue by Barbara Elford) and had issue Sir Hugh Fortescue (d. 1751).<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBurke2003825_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBurke2003825-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1746, he was created <b>Baron Fortescue</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Castle_Hill,_Filleigh" title="Castle Hill, Filleigh">Castle Hill</a> in the County of Devon (with remainder to his half-brother Matthew Fortescue) and <b>Earl Clinton</b>, with remainder to the heirs male of his body. The Earl was childless and on his death the earldom of Clinton became extinct but his two baronies continued. He was succeeded in the barony of Fortescue (according to the special remainder) by his half-brother, the second Baron Fortescue (see <a href="/wiki/Earl_Fortescue" title="Earl Fortescue">Earl Fortescue</a> for later history of this title). The barony of Clinton  was inherited by his second cousin Margaret Rolle, 15th Baroness Clinton, the granddaughter of  Lady Arabella Clinton, the younger daughter of Theophilus de Clinton, 4th Earl of Lincoln and 12th Baron Clinton (d. 1667). Margaret Rolle was the widow of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Walpole,_2nd_Earl_of_Orford" title="Robert Walpole, 2nd Earl of Orford">Robert Walpole, 2nd Earl of Orford</a> (d. 1751) and the  daughter of Samuel Rolle, the son of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Rolle_(died_1660)" title="Robert Rolle (died 1660)">Robert Rolle</a> (d. 1660), MP, of Heanton Satchville, Devon, by his wife Lady Arabella Clinton. She was succeeded by her son, <a href="/wiki/George_Walpole,_3rd_Earl_of_Orford" title="George Walpole, 3rd Earl of Orford">George Walpole, third Earl of Orford and 16th Baron Clinton</a> (d. 1791). He was childless and on his death in 1791 the earldom passed to his uncle, the fourth Earl of Orford (see the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Orford" title="Earl of Orford">Earl of Orford</a> for later history of this title), while the barony of Clinton became dormant.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBurke2003824_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBurke2003824-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Barony_dormant">Barony dormant</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Clinton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Barony dormant">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The barony, which had been dormant since 1791, was successfully claimed in 1794 by the late Earl of Orford's cousin Robert George William Trefusis (1764–1797), who became the 17th Baron Clinton. He was the three-times great-grandson of Lady Arabella Clinton, the younger daughter of the fourth Earl of Lincoln. Lady Arabella's daughter Bridget Rolle had married Francis Trefusis of <a href="/wiki/Trefusis" class="mw-redirect" title="Trefusis">Trefusis</a> in Cornwall, and had issue Samuel Trefusis (1677–1724), whose great-grandson was the 17th Baron Clinton.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBurke2003824-825_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBurke2003824-825-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The 18th Baron served as <i>aide-de-camp</i> to the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington" class="mw-redirect" title="Duke of Wellington">Duke of Wellington</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_War" title="Peninsular War">Peninsular War</a>. He was succeeded by his younger brother, who represented <a href="/wiki/Callington_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Callington (UK Parliament constituency)">Callington</a> in the <a href="/wiki/British_House_of_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="British House of Commons">House of Commons</a>, in which role he was notable. <sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBurke2003825_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBurke2003825-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He was succeeded by his son, the 20th Baron Clinton (1834–1904). He served as <a href="/wiki/Under-Secretary_of_State_for_India" title="Under-Secretary of State for India">Under-Secretary of State for India</a> from 1867 to 1868 in the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Government_1866-1868" class="mw-redirect" title="Conservative Government 1866-1868">Conservative administrations</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Edward_Smith-Stanley,_14th_Earl_of_Derby" title="Edward Smith-Stanley, 14th Earl of Derby">Earl of Derby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Disraeli" title="Benjamin Disraeli">Benjamin Disraeli</a> and was also <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_Devonshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Lieutenant of Devonshire">Lord Lieutenant of Devonshire</a>. In 1867 Baron Clinton assumed by Royal licence the additional surnames of Hepburn-Stuart-Forbes, which were those of his father-in-law. His son, the 21st Baron, held minor office in the coalition government of <a href="/wiki/David_Lloyd_George" title="David Lloyd George">David Lloyd George</a> and served as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Warden_of_the_Stannaries" title="Lord Warden of the Stannaries">Lord Warden of the Stannaries</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBurke2003825_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBurke2003825-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Barony_into_second_abeyance">Barony into second abeyance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Clinton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Barony into second abeyance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the death of the 21st Baron Clinton on 5 July 1957, the barony fell into abeyance between his two daughters: The Hon. Harriet Fane (14 November 1887 – 15 March 1958) (whom she divorced in 1935)<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (September 2019)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>, who married Maj. Henry Nevile Fane, only son of Sir <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Fane" title="Edmund Fane">Edmund Fane</a>; and the Hon. Fenella Bowes-Lyon (19 August 1889 – 19 July 1966), an aunt of Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a> through her marriage to <a href="/wiki/John_Bowes-Lyon" title="John Bowes-Lyon">John Bowes-Lyon</a>, second son of the <a href="/wiki/Claude_Bowes-Lyon,_14th_Earl_of_Strathmore_and_Kinghorne" title="Claude Bowes-Lyon, 14th Earl of Strathmore and Kinghorne">14th Earl of Strathmore and Kinghorne</a>. The abeyance of 1957 was terminated 18 March 1965 in favour of Gerald Neville Mark Fane Trefusis, the 22nd Lord Clinton, who is the great-grandson of the 21st Baron Clinton and grandson of Hon. Harriet Fane. His great-aunt Fenella supported his eight-year legal fight to succeed as baron. Baron Clinton had already in 1958 assumed by <a href="/wiki/Deed_poll" title="Deed poll">deed poll</a> the additional surname of Trefusis and had inherited the 85,000-acre estate in Devon from his great-grandfather.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-burke_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-burke-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Residences">Residences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baron_Clinton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Residences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The family seat is <a href="/wiki/Heanton_Satchville,_Huish" title="Heanton Satchville, Huish">Heanton Satchville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huish,_Devon" title="Huish, Devon">Huish</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Merton,_Devon" title="Merton, Devon">Merton</a> in North Devon, which was built in 1782 as "Innis House" by Sir James Innis, Duke of Roxburgh, and was purchased by the 18th Baron Clinton in about 1805, renamed Heanton Satchville, which burned down in 1935 and was rebuilt.
</p><p>After the death of Hon. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Rolle" title="Mark Rolle">Mark Rolle</a> in 1907, <a href="/wiki/Bicton_House" class="mw-redirect" title="Bicton House">Bicton House</a> became the main residence of the 21st Baron Clinton until his death in 1957,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> where much grand entertaining occurred until the start of World War II and where a herd of 150 deer were kept in the park. The Duke and Duchess of York spent part of their honeymoon there.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 1st Baron resided at <a href="/wiki/Maxstoke_Castle" title="Maxstoke Castle">Maxstoke Castle</a>, Warwickshire, the inheritance of his wife. The 5th Baron exchanged it for lands in Northamptonshire. The 9th Baron's chief seat was in Lincolnshire on an estate which was the inheritance of his wife Elizabeth Blount.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The seat of the 14th Baron Clinton was <a href="/wiki/Castle_Hill,_Filleigh" title="Castle Hill, Filleigh">Castle Hill, Filleigh</a>, rebuilt by him in the Palladian style. The seat of the 15th Baroness was <a href="/wiki/Heanton_Satchville,_Petrockstowe" title="Heanton Satchville, Petrockstowe">Heanton Satchville</a>, Petrockstowe, the ancient Rolle seat, although she spent the later part of her life on the Continent, having left her first husband, and died in <a href="/wiki/Pisa" title="Pisa">Pisa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Her son the 16th Baron largely abandoned that residence in favour of <a href="/wiki/Houghton_Hall" title="Houghton Hall">Houghton Hall</a> in Norfolk. Although he had the legal power to do so, he did not end the <a href="/wiki/Entail" class="mw-redirect" title="Entail">tail-male</a> of the Rolle estates, and the estates thus passed to the Trefusis family not to his own heirs male the Lords Cholmondely, heirs to the Walpole estates, who unsuccessfully claimed the Rolle estates from the 18th Baron Clinton in a lengthy court case.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Heanton_Satchville,_Petrockstowe" title="Heanton Satchville, Petrockstowe">Heanton Satchville</a>, Petrockstowe became the seat of the 17th Baron only for a short time as it burnt down in 1795 and he died two years later. His son the 18th Baron when he came of age  purchased Innes House on the other side of a marshy valley in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Huish,_Devon" title="Huish, Devon">Huish</a> and renamed it Heanton Satchville.
</p>
