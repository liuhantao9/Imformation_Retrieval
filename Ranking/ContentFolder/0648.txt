<p><b>Underbank Rangers</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> team based in <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth" title="Holmfirth">Holmfirth</a> near <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. They play in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_League_Conference_National_Division_1&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rugby League Conference National Division 1 (page does not exist)">National Division</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_Conference" title="Rugby League Conference">Rugby League Conference</a>. The club also competes as <i>Underbank Rangers</i> in division 1 of the <a href="/wiki/Pennine_League" title="Pennine League">Pennine League</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Underbank_Rangers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The club was founded in 1884.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> During the early 1900s Underbank launched the career of rugby league legend <a href="/wiki/Harold_Wagstaff" title="Harold Wagstaff">Harold Wagstaff</a> who would go on to be the first name in the Rugby League’s Hall of Fame.
</p><p>A Holliday Cup and promotion double was achieved in 1981/82 when <a href="/wiki/Neil_Fox_(rugby_league)" title="Neil Fox (rugby league)">Neil Fox</a> was player / coach, this was repeated by the more recent team of 2015 including the likes of Joe Bellas, Tom Stringer, Dayle King and Oliver Moorehouse.
</p><p>Rangers struggled in the late 1980s and early 1990s, as the establishment of the rival Newsome club took its toll. However, the club achieved a two-year revival at the end of the 1992/93 season, culminating in winning the Holliday Cup and gaining promotion to the Pennine Premier Division.
</p><p>Season 1999-2000 witnessed the Rangers finish second, their highest ever position, in the Pennine League Premier division. The following season, 2000-2001, again saw Rangers riding high in the Premier Division and top of the table at Christmas. However, the club suffered badly with the foot and mouth crisis, the ground was closed and the nomadic Rangers won only one of their last nine matches. The next couple of seasons witnessed the Rangers struggle and they couldn’t maintain their Pennine Premier League status.
</p><p>They were founder members of <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_Conference_National_Division" title="Rugby League Conference National Division">National League Three</a> in 2003, in which they competed as <i>Huddersfield Underbank Rangers</i>. They went on to finish sixth out of the ten competing teams. Seasons 2004 to 2006 saw the team miss out on the play-offs.
</p><p>The league was renamed Rugby League Conference National Division and came within the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_Conference" title="Rugby League Conference">Rugby League Conference</a> structure in 2007. Rangers improved and finished fifth and reached the play-offs before losing in the second round at <a href="/wiki/Warrington_Wizards" class="mw-redirect" title="Warrington Wizards">Warrington Wizards</a>. The team again finished fifth in 2008 exiting the competition in the first round of the play-offs after a defeat at <a href="/wiki/Featherstone_Lions" title="Featherstone Lions">Featherstone Lions</a>.
</p><p>They were among nine clubs invited to join the <a href="/wiki/National_Conference_League" title="National Conference League">National Conference League</a> for the 2013 season. They reverted to the original <i>Underbank Rangers</i> name.
</p>
