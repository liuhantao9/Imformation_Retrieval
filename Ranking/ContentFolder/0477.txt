<p>The <b>West Yorkshire Built-up Area</b>, previously known as the <b>West Yorkshire Urban Area</b> is a term used by the <a href="/wiki/Office_for_National_Statistics" title="Office for National Statistics">Office for National Statistics</a> (ONS) to refer to a conurbation in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, based on the cities of <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>, and the large towns of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>. It is the 4th largest urban area in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. However, it excludes towns such as <a href="/wiki/Castleford" title="Castleford">Castleford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pontefract" title="Pontefract">Pontefract</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wetherby" title="Wetherby">Wetherby</a> which though part of the county of West Yorkshire are considered independently. There are substantial areas of agricultural land within the designated area - more than in any other official urban area in England - many of the towns and cities are only just connected with one another by narrow outlying strips of development.
</p>

<p>Three further subdivisions are given<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> with no population numbers as they are present or former industrial areas with no resident population.
</p><p>Rawdon is the subdivision name for Horsforth Vale, on which a former industrial plant was redeveloped for housing from 2010, too late to be recorded for the 2011 census.
</p>
