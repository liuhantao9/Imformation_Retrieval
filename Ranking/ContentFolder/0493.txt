	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Holme Valley</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>

</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Holme_Valley_PC.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/87/Holme_Valley_PC.jpg/220px-Holme_Valley_PC.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="206" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/87/Holme_Valley_PC.jpg/330px-Holme_Valley_PC.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/87/Holme_Valley_PC.jpg/440px-Holme_Valley_PC.jpg 2x" data-file-width="532" data-file-height="499" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Holme_Valley_PC.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Location of Holme Valley within Kirklees</div></div></div>
<p><b>Holme Valley</b> is a large <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Metropolitan Borough of Kirklees</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England.  It has a population of 25,049 (2001 census), increasing to 34,680 for the two wards in the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>   Its administrative centre is in <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth" title="Holmfirth">Holmfirth</a>. Other sizeable settlements in the parish include, <a href="/wiki/Brockholes" title="Brockholes">Brockholes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honley" title="Honley">Honley</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Mill,_West_Yorkshire" title="New Mill, West Yorkshire">New Mill</a>.  It is named from the <a href="/wiki/River_Holme" title="River Holme">River Holme</a> that runs through the parish.
</p>
<div class="center"><div class="thumb tnone"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Upper_Holme_Valley.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Upper_Holme_Valley.JPG/400px-Upper_Holme_Valley.JPG" decoding="async" width="400" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Upper_Holme_Valley.JPG/600px-Upper_Holme_Valley.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Upper_Holme_Valley.JPG/800px-Upper_Holme_Valley.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2281" data-file-height="894" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Upper_Holme_Valley.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Upper Holme Valley, looking north and westerly, from Mount at <a href="/wiki/Jackson_Bridge" title="Jackson Bridge">Jackson Bridge</a> over <a href="/wiki/Totties" title="Totties">Totties</a> &amp; New Mill to Honley &amp; <a href="/wiki/Crosland_Moor" title="Crosland Moor">Crosland Moor</a></div></div></div></div><p>.
</p><p>The parish is the <a href="/wiki/Successor_parish" title="Successor parish">successor</a> to the Holmfirth <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a>.  An urban district covering Holmfirth was created in 1894 by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1894" title="Local Government Act 1894">Local Government Act 1894</a> and then in 1938, under a <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1929" title="Local Government Act 1929">County Review Order</a>, absorbed parts of the <a href="/wiki/Holme,_West_Yorkshire" title="Holme, West Yorkshire">Holme</a>, Honley, New Mill, <a href="/wiki/South_Crosland" title="South Crosland">South Crosland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thurstonland_and_Farnley_Tyas" class="mw-redirect" title="Thurstonland and Farnley Tyas">Thurstonland and Farnley Tyas</a> urban districts, keeping the name.  Under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, the Holmfirth urban district was abolished on 1 April 1974, but its area was retained as a single civil parish with a parish council. The council changed its name from Holmfirth Parish Council to its present Holme Valley Parish Council in 1975.
</p>
