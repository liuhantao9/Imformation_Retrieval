<p><b>BBC Radio 1Xtra</b> (also known simply as <b>1Xtra</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Digital_radio_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Digital radio in the United Kingdom">digital</a> <a href="/wiki/Urban_contemporary" title="Urban contemporary">urban contemporary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Black_music" title="Black music">Black music</a> <a href="/wiki/Radio_broadcasting" title="Radio broadcasting">radio station</a> in the United Kingdom that is owned and operated by the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-inside_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inside-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Launched at 6<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>PM on 16 August 2002, it had been code named <i>Network X</i> during the consultation period and is the <a href="/wiki/Sister_station" title="Sister station">sister station</a> to <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">BBC Radio 1</a>. The station is broadcast from the 8th floor of <a href="/wiki/New_Broadcasting_House" class="mw-redirect" title="New Broadcasting House">New Broadcasting House</a>, shared with Radio 1 and the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Asian_Network" title="BBC Asian Network">Asian Network</a>.
</p><p>Since mid-2017, 1Xtra's management has been merged with that of <a href="/wiki/BBC_Asian_Network" title="BBC Asian Network">BBC Asian Network</a>. Head of BBC Asian Network <a href="/wiki/Markie_Mark" title="Markie Mark">Mark Strippel</a> was given joint control of both stations.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_policy">Music policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Radio_1Xtra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Music policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>BBC Radio 1Xtra's music includes largely British, North American, Caribbean and African <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grime_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Grime (music)">grime</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bassline_(dance_music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bassline (dance music)">bassline</a>,  <a href="/wiki/UK_Garage" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Garage">UK Garage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dubstep" title="Dubstep">dubstep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drum_and_bass" title="Drum and bass">drum and bass</a>, <a href="/wiki/UK_funky" title="UK funky">UK funky</a>, <a href="/wiki/House_music" title="House music">house</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dancehall" title="Dancehall">dancehall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soca_music" title="Soca music">soca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reggae" title="Reggae">reggae</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gospel_music" title="Gospel music">gospel music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bhangra_(music)" title="Bhangra (music)">bhangra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues" title="Rhythm and blues">R&amp;B</a>. It is available on digital radio (<a href="/wiki/Digital_Audio_Broadcasting" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital Audio Broadcasting">DAB</a>), digital satellite television, <a href="/wiki/Digital_terrestrial_television" title="Digital terrestrial television">digital terrestrial television</a> (<a href="/wiki/Freeview_(UK)" title="Freeview (UK)">Freeview</a>), and the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>. The first ever track played on 1Xtra was a specially created track produced by <a href="/wiki/DJ_Skitz" title="DJ Skitz">DJ Skitz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rodney_P" title="Rodney P">Rodney P</a> and featuring <a href="/wiki/Beverley_Knight" title="Beverley Knight">Beverley Knight</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blak_Twang" title="Blak Twang">Blak Twang</a>. The 5-hour show was presented by the Rampage DJ collective and the station's then breakfast show host, KC.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="News_and_speech">News and speech</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Radio_1Xtra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: News and speech">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As part of its <a href="/wiki/Public_broadcasting" title="Public broadcasting">public service broadcasting</a> <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/remit" class="extiw" title="wikt:remit">remit</a>, 1Xtra is required to carry a significant amount of news, information and speech content. 1Xtra had its own news service, 1Xtra News (formerly known as 'TX'), which was operated as a <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> of Radio 1's <a href="/wiki/Newsbeat" title="Newsbeat">Newsbeat</a> operations. The tone and style of the news presentation is in keeping with the station's overall target audience - young and predominantly urban.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Initially, in addition to regular hourly bulletins, TX had a flagship weekday two-hour news, features and discussion show under the title 'TX Unltd' (pronounced 'Unlimited').<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This show - initially broadcast in a 5<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm&#160;&#8211;&#32;7<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm slot - rated poorly, however, and was later absorbed into a mixed music-and-speech format (similar to that used by <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Vine" title="Jeremy Vine">Jeremy Vine</a> on Radio 2) which aired in mid-afternoon (2<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm&#160;&#8211;&#32;4<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm) and was named after its host, Max.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2009, the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Trust" title="BBC Trust">BBC Trust</a> agreed to a further change to the scheduling of news content on 1Xtra, such that it could use the same format successfully operated by Radio 1's Newsbeat: two 15-minute news bulletins, one in the middle of the day and another in the early evening, with other speech features, profiles and social/cultural specials being broadcast on an ad-hoc basis within music-led shows, and with regular hourly news bulletins also continuing. The Trust required that 1Xtra's main bulletins not air at the same time as those on Radio 1.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> When the new bulletins were introduced in late summer 2009, they aired at noon and 5<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm, with Radio 1's bulletins remaining at 12:45<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm and 5:45<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm.
</p><p>As of Summer 2009 it was reported that Radio 1 and 1Xtra were carrying shared news bulletins at weekends;<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> weekday news output remained separate.
</p><p>September 2012 saw a substantial increase in Newsbeat bulletins simulcast with Radio 1. Weekday breakfast bulletins at 6<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am, 7.30<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am, 8<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am, 8.30<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am and 9.30<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am remain bespoke 1Xtra broadcasts. From 10:30<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am, bulletins are shared with Radio 1, including the 15-minute Newsbeat magazines at 12:45<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm and 5:45<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm.
</p><p>In the first quarter of 2011, 1Xtra was part of an efficiency review conducted by <a href="/wiki/John_Myers_(radio_executive)" title="John Myers (radio executive)">John Myers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tel-nov2010_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tel-nov2010-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> His role, according to Andrew Harrison, the chief executive of RadioCentre, was "to identify both areas of best practice and possible savings."<sup id="cite_ref-tel-nov2010_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tel-nov2010-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2017, According to the reports Roundhouse Rising would be partnering with 1Xtra.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Audience_profile">Audience profile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Radio_1Xtra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Audience profile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>1Xtra's typical audience is between 15 and 30 years old.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The upper age range is deliberately lower than sister station Radio 1 which is closer to 35.
</p><p>According to the "Submission to the Secretary of State's review of digital channels" in March 2004, Radio 1Xtra <i>"provides music output 24 hours a day, punctuated by bespoke BBC news bulletins and other speech output designed specifically to be pertinent to the audience."</i>
</p>

<p>Weekday evening shows begin with MistaJam helming a three-hour multi-genre show, followed by six hours of specialist output tailored to a particular genre (e.g. UK Garage, dancehall, etc.) Between October 2009 and spring 2010, the 4<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am&#160;&#8211;&#32;6<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am slot housed a replay of selected weekend specialist programming; this and the one-hour Morning Mix programme were dropped in spring 2010 and a new six-days-a-week 'early breakfast' show (4<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am&#160;&#8211;&#32;7<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am) hosted by <a href="/wiki/Nick_Bright" title="Nick Bright">Nick Bright</a> was introduced. (The Saturday 4<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am replay of Target's Friday night show was also axed, to make room for Bright's sixth show) This has now itself been replaced by a rerun of the previous week's overnight mix show from 4<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am to 6<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am, giving nine hours of specialist output.
</p><p>Weekday overnights (1<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am&#160;&#8211;&#32;3<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am), Saturday overnights (1<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am&#160;&#8211;&#32;4<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am) and Saturday evenings (7<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm&#160;&#8211;&#32;1<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am) are now simulcast entirely with BBC Radio 1 - this allows Radio 1's flagship urban content to air on 1Xtra.
</p>
