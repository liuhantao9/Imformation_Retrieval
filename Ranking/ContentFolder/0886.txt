<p><b>Baildon</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> and town<sup id="cite_ref-TA:CTSA_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TA:CTSA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
in <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a>. 
It is part of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford" title="City of Bradford">Bradford Metropolitan District</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_county" title="Metropolitan county">metropolitan county</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> and within the <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">historic boundaries</a> of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>.
It lies 3 miles (5&#160;km) north of Bradford city centre.
Other nearby suburbs include <a href="/wiki/Shipley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Shipley, West Yorkshire">Shipley</a> to the south and <a href="/wiki/Saltaire" title="Saltaire">Saltaire</a> to the west.
As of the 2011 census, the Baildon ward has a population of 15,360.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baildon is known to have been inhabited for many centuries; several <a href="/wiki/Cup_and_ring_mark" title="Cup and ring mark">cup-and-ring stones</a> on Baildon Moor has shown evidence of <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> inhabitation.<sup id="cite_ref-BV:H_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BV:H-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
Baildon Moor has a number of <a href="/wiki/Gritstone" title="Gritstone">gritstone</a> outcrops with numerous prehistoric <a href="/wiki/Cup_and_ring_marks" class="mw-redirect" title="Cup and ring marks">cup and ring marks</a>.
A denuded and mutilated bank represents the remains of an <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Age</a> settlement known as Soldier's Trench, sometimes mistaken for a Bronze Age <a href="/wiki/Stone_circle" title="Stone circle">stone circle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MP:STSC_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MP:STSC-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
A Bronze Age cup-marked rock is incorporated in the bank.<sup id="cite_ref-P:ST_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P:ST-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Baildon is recorded as <i>Beldone</i> and <i>Beldune</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a>.
In 1066 it belonged to a Gospatric, son of Arnketil, and had passed to Erneis of Buron by 1086.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Baildon had two manor houses: one on Hall Cliffe, the other in lower Baildon.
In the 1960s the Hall Cliffe house was demolished and replaced with the Ian Clough Hall.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>, Baildon developed a woollen industry; Westgate House was built in 1814 by the Ambler family who were prominent in the wool trade and the warehouse part of the building was Feathers Bakery now Nine Café adjacent to the mill which is now the Westgate Bar.<sup id="cite_ref-BV:WH_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BV:WH-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the 19th and early 20th centuries, conditions in Bradford deteriorated and poverty and ill health became widespread; Baildon began developing as a <a href="/wiki/Commuter_town" title="Commuter town">commuter town</a> along with neighbouring Shipley.
In the latter years of the 20th century, the West Riding suffered from economic decline through the gradual closure of its textile and engineering industries.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
Bradford was particularly affected by this; however, Leeds grew as a major administrative and financial centre and Baildon with its railway links to Leeds has become a strategic commuter town.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gypsy_parties">Gypsy parties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Gypsy parties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Baildon was an important location for the British <a href="/wiki/Names_of_the_Romani_people#Gypsy" title="Names of the Romani people">Gypsy</a> community.
A report of 1929 stated that annual Gypsy Parties had started two to three hundred years before – records were said to go back to 1770 when it was reported to be an ancient custom.
In 1881, up to 5,000 people are said to have paid for admission.
Gradually the event was taken over by local residents, who dressed up as Gypsies and formed 'tribes'.
Proceeds went to the local Horticultural Society.
After 1897 the tradition died out, apparently because the 'real Gypsies' had disappeared.
However, in 1929 it was revived to raise funds for Baildon Hospital.
A local resident, John Keen, then contacted the so-called <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Gypsies" title="King of the Gypsies">King of the Gypsies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xavier_Petulengro" title="Xavier Petulengro">Xavier Petulengro</a>, and they re-established large Gypsy gatherings at Baildon, recorded on <a href="/wiki/Pathe_News" class="mw-redirect" title="Pathe News">Pathe News</a> films and shown nationally in cinemas.<sup id="cite_ref-GRTL_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GRTL-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BP_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BP-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
The Gypsy Parties ended with the start of the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> and were never revived.<sup id="cite_ref-GRTL_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GRTL-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
However, in the early 1990s travellers used the Bank Holiday loophole to occupy Shipley Glen in Baildon for a weekend festival.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pleasure_Grounds">Pleasure Grounds</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pleasure Grounds">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Shipley_Glen_Railway_-_geograph.org.uk_-_17666.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Shipley_Glen_Railway_-_geograph.org.uk_-_17666.jpg/190px-Shipley_Glen_Railway_-_geograph.org.uk_-_17666.jpg" decoding="async" width="190" height="143" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Shipley_Glen_Railway_-_geograph.org.uk_-_17666.jpg/285px-Shipley_Glen_Railway_-_geograph.org.uk_-_17666.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Shipley_Glen_Railway_-_geograph.org.uk_-_17666.jpg/380px-Shipley_Glen_Railway_-_geograph.org.uk_-_17666.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Shipley_Glen_Railway_-_geograph.org.uk_-_17666.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Shipley_Glen_Tramway" title="Shipley Glen Tramway">Shipley Glen Tramway</a></div></div></div>
<p>In northern Shipley off Prod Lane there was a small fairground with <a href="/wiki/Bumper_cars" title="Bumper cars">dodgem cars</a>, booths and a historic "Aerial Glide" <a href="/wiki/Suspended_roller_coaster" title="Suspended roller coaster">suspended roller coaster</a> that for a short period had listed building status.<sup id="cite_ref-AAB3_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AAB3-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TPG_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TPG-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
In earlier years the fairground attractions at Shipley Glen were much more extensive.<sup id="cite_ref-TET_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TET-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
The fairground closed and is now dismantled and newly built (2015/6) private housing now covers the site.<sup id="cite_ref-SWHS_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SWHS-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
The only working remnant of the Pleasure Grounds is the <a href="/wiki/Shipley_Glen_Tramway" title="Shipley Glen Tramway">Shipley Glen Cable Tramway</a> a funicular railway carrying passengers from just north of <a href="/wiki/Roberts_Park,_Saltaire" title="Roberts Park, Saltaire">Roberts Park, Saltaire</a> up the hill side through Walker Wood to the location of the Pleasure Grounds at the top of Prod Lane.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baildon lies to the north east of Bradford, and is linked to Bradford and Shipley by the B6151.
To the north of Baildon lies Baildon Moor, a part of <a href="/wiki/Rombalds_Moor" title="Rombalds Moor">Rombalds Moor</a>, with several quarries and underlying strata of coal.
There are the remains of old coal pits.<sup id="cite_ref-WYGT_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WYGT-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Across Baildon Moor is the village of Menston, the town of <a href="/wiki/Ilkley" title="Ilkley">Ilkley</a> and Ilkley Moor.
Baildon is situated on a hill to the north of the <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">River Aire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_and_Liverpool_Canal" title="Leeds and Liverpool Canal">Leeds and Liverpool Canal</a>.
</p><p>Baildon is 9 miles (14&#160;km) from <a href="/wiki/Leeds_city_centre" title="Leeds city centre">Leeds city centre</a> and 3 miles (5&#160;km) from Bradford city centre.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Baildon_Ward_2004.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Baildon_Ward_2004.png/220px-Baildon_Ward_2004.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="210" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Baildon_Ward_2004.png/330px-Baildon_Ward_2004.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Baildon_Ward_2004.png/440px-Baildon_Ward_2004.png 2x" data-file-width="735" data-file-height="703" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Baildon_Ward_2004.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>2004 Boundaries of Baildon Ward</div></div></div>
<dl><dt>History</dt></dl>
<p><a href="/wiki/Boards_of_Guardians" class="mw-redirect" title="Boards of Guardians">Boards of Guardians</a> were formed as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Poor_Law_Amendment_Act_1834" title="Poor Law Amendment Act 1834">Poor Law Amendment Act 1834</a> though the Baildon Local Board of Guardians was formed in 1852.
Elections were held on 16 September 1852 and their first quorate meeting was on 9 October 1852. The last meeting took place on 18 December 1894.
As a result of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1894" title="Local Government Act 1894">Local Government Act 1894</a> Baildon became an <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">Urban District</a> on 1 January 1895 and was then administered by Baildon Urban District Council.
Baildon Urban District Council was established in 1895 and was disbanded when Bradford Metropolitan District Council was established in 1974.
</p>
<dl><dt>Present day</dt></dl>
<p>A group of local residents held an open meeting on 7 May 1997 and as a result the Baildon Community Council came into being on 26 June 1997 as a means of communicating local interest to appropriate authorities.
Residents of Baildon went through the appropriate legal process and as a result Baildon Parish Council was formed which held its first full meeting 14 May 2007.
The civil parish does not cover nearby <a href="/wiki/Esholt" title="Esholt">Esholt</a>, part of the Baildon Ward at District level, but includes the area North of the River Aire along Coach Road and Higher Coach Road (Baildon South West) which is within the <a href="/wiki/Shipley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Shipley, West Yorkshire">Shipley</a> Ward at District level.
</p><p>On Monday 10 June 2013 Baildon officially became a town when Baildon Parish Council resolved that, in accordance with the Local Government Act 1972 s245, the Parish of Baildon shall have the status of a town.
Consequently, the Parish Council was renamed Baildon <i>Town</i> Council.<sup id="cite_ref-BTC_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BTC-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dt>Councillors</dt></dl>
<p>Baildon ward is represented on <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford_Metropolitan_District_Council" title="City of Bradford Metropolitan District Council">Bradford Council</a> by three <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> councillors, Valerie Townend, Mike Pollard and Debbie Davies.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baildon has a modest town centre with most everyday amenities including independent traders, estate agents and family law solicitors.
</p><p>There is a <a href="/wiki/Co-operative_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Co-operative Group">Co-op</a> supermarket and a small <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a> on the outskirts.
The centre is home to many independent shops including Pickles Delicatessen, Westgate General Stores, Seasons Home, Finesse Gifts and Baildon interiors.
There are also several independently owned hair dressers and beauty salons and a variety of restaurants and take-aways.
There are some eight public houses and bars in Baildon.<sup id="cite_ref-PG:B_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PG:B-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Charlestown">Charlestown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Charlestown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Charlestown in south east Baildon is the area of Baildon with the highest concentration of industry.
The area is in the valley bottom between Otley Road and the River Aire, and includes the Acorn Park Industrial Estate and the Butterfield Industrial Estate.Notable companies established in Charlestown include Manor Coating Systems,<sup id="cite_ref-TL:MCS_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TL:MCS-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>and <a href="/wiki/Denso" title="Denso">Denso</a> Marston Radiators.<sup id="cite_ref-TM_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TM-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Further north east in Charlestown there are plans for a 'Baildon Business Park' and hotel on a green field site near Buck Lane, at a cost of £25m but there is opposition to the move.<sup id="cite_ref-TA:BGIB_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TA:BGIB-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
Another industrial area is the Tong Park Industrial Estate off Otley Road in eastern Baildon.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>One&#160;of&#160;the main monuments in Baildon is the Frances Ferrand memorial fountain known locally as the 'potted meat stick'.
This was built by Baron Amphlett of Somerset as a memorial to his mother-in-law, Frances Ferrand.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
It still stands today to the eastern side of Browgate.
In 1925 the monument was put at threat when plans were produced to replace in with a bus terminus.
In the 1960s the monument was removed and dismantled; however, in 1986 the <a href="/wiki/Mechanics_Institute" class="mw-redirect" title="Mechanics Institute">Mechanics Institute</a> raised funds to take it out of storage and restore it.<sup id="cite_ref-PMS_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PMS-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
To the north of Towngate roundabout in front of Glendale House is a concrete paved open area created in the 1960s containing what remains of the stocks and a sandstone pillar thought to be the remains of a mediaeval cross, both Grade II listed.<sup id="cite_ref-BMDC:BSRCA_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BMDC:BSRCA-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
On Glen Road on the edge of Baildon Moor, close to Shipley Glen, is <a href="/wiki/Bracken_Hall_Countryside_Centre_and_Museum" title="Bracken Hall Countryside Centre and Museum">Bracken Hall Countryside Centre and Museum</a>, a free-to-visit children's museum, natural history education centre and nature centre.<sup id="cite_ref-BMAG_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BMAG-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Baildon's War Memorials are situated on Browgate<sup id="cite_ref-BDP:BWM_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BDP:BWM-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
and in Tong Park.<sup id="cite_ref-WW2M:TPWM_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW2M:TPWM-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Churches">Churches</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Churches">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Church of St.&#160;James in Charlestown is a painted <a href="/wiki/Tongue_and_groove" title="Tongue and groove">tongue and groove</a> timber building, now a grade&#160;II listed building.<sup id="cite_ref-BLB-CoSJ_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BLB-CoSJ-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
It was moved to Baildon from <a href="/wiki/Great_Warley" title="Great Warley">Great Warley</a>, Essex in 1905.
The Reverend N.&#160;R.&#160;Bailey, rector of Great Warley, had property in Baildon and hoped to retire there.
However his obituary was published in November 1900 before he retired.
In 2007/2008 the church was moved again, but only by a few yards.
This allowed the surrounding land to be sold by the Diocese for development.
Rotten timbers were replaced and underground <a href="/wiki/Heat_pump" title="Heat pump">heat pump</a> system installed to make the building more eco-friendly.
</p><p>The Church of <a href="/wiki/St._John_the_Evangelist" class="mw-redirect" title="St. John the Evangelist">St. John the Evangelist</a> designed by Mallinson &amp; Healey, was built in 1848 though the south tower was not added until 1928.
The east window designed by Powell &amp; Sons was added in 1870.<sup id="cite_ref-SJEB_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SJEB-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village is served by <a href="/wiki/Baildon_railway_station" title="Baildon railway station">Baildon railway station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Rail_electrification" class="mw-redirect" title="Rail electrification">electrified</a> <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Wharfedale Line">Wharfedale Line</a> with connections to <a href="/wiki/Forster_Square,_Bradford" class="mw-redirect" title="Forster Square, Bradford">Bradford Forster Square</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_railway_station" title="Ilkley railway station">Ilkley</a>.
There is however, no service to Leeds owing to its position on the curve between the two lines.
Passengers must instead change at Shipley or the nearby Guiseley (Shipley having more services).
Baildon is served by the West Yorkshire Metro 626, 649, and 656–659 First and Yorkshire Tiger bus services.<sup id="cite_ref-WYM_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WYM-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
The main road through Baildon is the <a href="/wiki/A6038_road" class="mw-redirect" title="A6038 road">A6038 Otley Road</a> while the B6151 Baildon Road / Brow Gate goes the short distance from Otley Road up to Browgate roundabout.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There were a large number of First and Middle schools in the area, including Fernihurst First School, Tong Park First School, Belmont Middle School &amp; Ladderbanks Middle School, before the 1998 Bradford Education reform which returned the area to a two-tier school system.
Currently, there are four local primary schools: Sandal Primary School to the North on West Lane, Baildon Church of England School to the east off Langley Lane, Hoyle Court Primary school in Charlestown and Glenaire Primary School to the south on Thompson Lane.
</p><p>The nearest secondary school for the area is <a href="/wiki/Titus_Salt_School" title="Titus Salt School">Titus Salt School</a> on Higher Coach Road, overlooking <a href="/wiki/Roberts_Park,_Saltaire" title="Roberts Park, Saltaire">Roberts Park, Saltaire</a>. There is dispute as to which town the school actually is in, some classing it as Baildon and some as <a href="/wiki/Saltaire" title="Saltaire">Saltaire</a><sup id="cite_ref-TSS_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSS-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Within Baildon there are sports clubs for cricket, football, golf, rugby and running.<sup id="cite_ref-SPORT_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPORT-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_events">Culture and events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Culture and events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For nearly a quarter of a century the <a href="/wiki/Bank_holiday" title="Bank holiday">August bank holiday</a> weekend saw over 500 <a href="/wiki/Harley-Davidson" title="Harley-Davidson">Harley-Davidson</a> riders arrive in Baildon as part of the annual UK rally of the Harley-Davidson Riders Club of Great Britain fundraising rally.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
The HDRCGB ran the rally up to 2001 when it moved to Berkshire.
In 2003, Harley-Davidson's Centenary Year, the HDRCGB hosted the event for the last time in Baildon.
The rally is now organised by the Shipley Harley-Davidson Club.
In 2008 430 riders were given a police escort down Browgate towards Hollins Hill, from where most riders travelled to <a href="/wiki/Harewood_House" title="Harewood House">Harewood House</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baildon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Category:People_from_Baildon" title="Category:People from Baildon">Category:People from Baildon</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Zayn_Malik" title="Zayn Malik">Zayn Malik</a> from the band <a href="/wiki/One_Direction" title="One Direction">One Direction</a> who was born at <a href="/wiki/St._Luke%27s_Hospital,_Bradford" title="St. Luke&#39;s Hospital, Bradford">St. Luke's Hospital, Bradford</a>, and grew up in <a href="/wiki/East_Bowling" title="East Bowling">East Bowling</a>, bought his family a house in Baildon in 2013. He stays with his family in Baildon occasionally when he's back in Bradford.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
The late journalist and <i><a href="/wiki/Countdown_(game_show)" title="Countdown (game show)">Countdown</a></i> TV game show presenter <a href="/wiki/Richard_Whiteley" title="Richard Whiteley">Richard Whiteley</a> was a native of Baildon,<sup id="cite_ref-Indep_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indep-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
and <a href="/wiki/Austin_Mitchell" title="Austin Mitchell">Austin Mitchell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">MP</a> for <a href="/wiki/Great_Grimsby" class="mw-redirect" title="Great Grimsby">Great Grimsby</a> since 1977, was actually born in Baildon.<sup id="cite_ref-FOL_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOL-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>

<p><a href="/wiki/Mountaineering" title="Mountaineering">Mountaineer</a> <a href="/wiki/Ian_Clough_(mountaineer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ian Clough (mountaineer)">Ian Clough</a> was born in Baildon.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
After he was killed on an expedition to the <a href="/wiki/Himalaya" class="mw-redirect" title="Himalaya">Himalayan</a> mountain <a href="/wiki/Annapurna" class="mw-redirect" title="Annapurna">Annapurna</a> in 1970, Ian Clough Hall, a meeting-place and arts venue, was established in Baildon in his memory.
In the 1960s, Clough and close friend <a href="/wiki/Chris_Bonington" title="Chris Bonington">Chris Bonington</a> were known to have practised their climbing techniques on Baildon Bank - a 1,000-foot-long (300&#160;m), 50-foot-high (15&#160;m), ex-quarry rock-face that looks out towards <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>.
In 1962, Clough and Bonington were the first Britons to successfully scale the treacherous north face of the <a href="/wiki/Eiger" title="Eiger">Eiger</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Alps" title="Swiss Alps">Swiss Alps</a>.
Former football goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Bert_Hoyle" title="Bert Hoyle">Bert Hoyle</a> was also born in Baildon.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Coventry City">Coventry City</a> football manager <a href="/wiki/Aidy_Boothroyd" title="Aidy Boothroyd">Aidy Boothroyd</a> and veteran sports television commentator <a href="/wiki/John_Helm_(sportscaster)" class="mw-redirect" title="John Helm (sportscaster)">John Helm</a> are from Baildon.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
Ex-Yorkshire &amp; England cricket captain <a href="/wiki/Brian_Close" title="Brian Close">Brian Close</a>, born in nearby <a href="/wiki/Rawdon,_West_Yorkshire" title="Rawdon, West Yorkshire">Rawdon</a>, settled in the village, and died there in 2015 after a long battle with cancer.<sup id="cite_ref-Tel-BC_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tel-BC-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
Ex-<a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_CCC" class="mw-redirect" title="Yorkshire CCC">Yorkshire</a> and England <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> fast bowler <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Hoggard" title="Matthew Hoggard">Matthew Hoggard</a>, though originally from <a href="/wiki/Pudsey" title="Pudsey">Pudsey</a>, has lived in Baildon for some years and is a member of Baildon Cricket Club.<sup id="cite_ref-BW-FP_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BW-FP-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TO_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TO-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
England rugby player, <a href="/wiki/Dan_Scarbrough" title="Dan Scarbrough">Dan Scarbrough</a> currently resides in Baildon.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
