<p>The <i><b>Huddersfield Daily Examiner</b></i> is an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> local daily evening newspaper covering news and sport from <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and its surrounding areas.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_Daily_Examiner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first edition was published as a weekly, starting on 6 September 1851, as the <i><b>Huddersfield &amp; Holmfirth Examiner,</b></i> although the 'Holmfirth' was dropped from the title two years later.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The newspaper has been published as a daily since 28 January 1871<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> when journalists on the title worked all weekend in order to forestall a rival and become the town's first daily paper.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Since 1999 it has been part of the <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Mirror" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity Mirror">Trinity Mirror</a> group, which also owns the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mirror" title="Daily Mirror">Daily Mirror</a></i> and is the largest newspaper publisher in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.
</p><p>The <i>Examiner</i> lays claim to a notable first in regional British journalism, as the first provincial UK newspaper to employ a woman journalist, in 1888.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_developments">Recent developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_Daily_Examiner&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Recent developments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Examiner</i> journalist Adrian Sudbury was given worldwide recognition during his battle with terminal leukaemia between 2006 and 2008. His Sign up for Sudders campaign was aimed at encouraging more people to sign up as bone marrow donors, and education packs about blood, bone marrow and organ donation were sent out to schools across the country. Adrian died at his family home in Sheffield in August 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Roy Wright served as the editor of the <i>Examiner</i> between 2002 and 2017. During this period, the newspaper, along with the local media industry more generally, underwent significant change. One notable shift took place in 2004, when the <i>Examiner</i> swapped its traditional broadsheet format for a tabloid or 'compact' size. At this time, printing of the paper left Huddersfield, to be centralised at <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Mirror" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity Mirror">Trinity Mirror</a>'s Chadderton facility in <a href="/wiki/Oldham" title="Oldham">Oldham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2008, the <i>Examiner</i> switched to overnight printing, making the paper available earlier each day.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The <i>Examiner</i> then moved from its base on Queen Street South to new premises at Pennine Business Park in Bradley.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It maintained a small town centre office for two years, but this closed in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> A daily iPad version of the newspaper began in the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Announcing his departure in 2017, Wright said the <i>Examiner</i> had been transformed from a "black and white on-the-day broadsheet" to a "genuine multimedia newsroom".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <i>Examiner</i> has played a leading role in the campaign to try to prevent the closure of the Accident and Emergency Unit at <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Royal_Infirmary" title="Huddersfield Royal Infirmary">Huddersfield Royal Infirmary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The 'Hands Off HRI' campaign began in January 2016, and journalists at the paper have promised to continue it despite a series of official setbacks.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wayne Ankers was appointed editor of the <i>Examiner</i> in January 2017. He had previously worked for sister paper the <i><a href="/wiki/Manchester_Evening_News" title="Manchester Evening News">Manchester Evening News</a></i> as an associate editor of content.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In the first two years under Ankers' stewardship, circulation fell from 12,046 to 9,019, a drop of 25 percent.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
