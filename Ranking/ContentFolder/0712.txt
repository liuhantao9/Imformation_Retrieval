<p><b>John Matthew McCoy</b> (born c. 1950, in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, England), is an <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar" title="Bass guitar">bass guitarist</a>, who is best known for his work with <a href="/wiki/Ian_Gillan" title="Ian Gillan">Ian Gillan</a> and <i>Mammoth</i> as well as numerous other bands and <a href="/wiki/Session_musician" title="Session musician">sessions</a> since the late 1960s. He currently plays in <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> rock trio <a href="/wiki/Guy_McCoy_Torm%C3%A9" title="Guy McCoy Tormé">Guy McCoy Tormé</a> with former <a href="/wiki/Gillan_(band)" title="Gillan (band)">Gillan</a>/<a href="/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne" title="Ozzy Osbourne">Ozzy</a> guitarist <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Torm%C3%A9" title="Bernie Tormé">Bernie Tormé</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Dickinson" title="Bruce Dickinson">Bruce Dickinson</a>/<a href="/wiki/Sack_Trick" title="Sack Trick">Sack Trick</a> <a href="/wiki/Drummer" title="Drummer">drummer</a> Robin Guy. He is also an accomplished <a href="/wiki/Lead_guitar" title="Lead guitar">guitar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drum" title="Drum">drum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trumpet" title="Trumpet">trumpet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cello" title="Cello">cello</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Double_bass" title="Double bass">double bass</a> player. Nearly as well known as his music is his appearance: he is always pictured wearing sunglasses, with the striking contrast of bald head and robust chin beard. Along with guitarist <a href="/wiki/Vic_Elmes" title="Vic Elmes">Vic Elmes</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=ZZebra&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ZZebra (page does not exist)">ZZebra</a> colleague <a href="/wiki/Liam_Genockey" title="Liam Genockey">Liam Genockey</a> on drums, McCoy can also be heard playing in the intro and end titles theme of the 1970s cult TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Space:_1999" title="Space: 1999">Space: 1999</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_McCoy_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the 1960s, when he was 13, whilst still at school, McCoy began playing as <a href="/wiki/Lead_guitar" title="Lead guitar">lead guitarist</a> with a working <a href="/wiki/Beat_music" title="Beat music">beat</a> group, <i>The Drovers</i>. In 1966 he responded to an advertisement in the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="Yorkshire Post">Yorkshire Post</a> newspaper for a guitarist to join a band called <i>Mamas Little Children</i> who were about to begin touring <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. McCoy went to audition only to find they had just given someone else the position, but still needed a bass player. He auditioned on a spare bass that was there and was given the job. In 1968 he was forced to resign from the band because he was working illegally under age. On his return to Britain he went to <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> where he found work as a <a href="/wiki/Session_musician" title="Session musician">session musician</a> with former <a href="/wiki/The_Drifters" title="The Drifters">Drifters</a> member <a href="/wiki/Clyde_McPhatter" title="Clyde McPhatter">Clyde McPhatter</a> touring the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a>.
</p><p>In 1974, McCoy was playing with London-based band <i>Scrapyard</i> when they recruited Irish-born lead guitarist <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Torm%C3%A9" title="Bernie Tormé">Bernie Tormé</a>. Although Tormé eventually left to form his own <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk rock</a> band, the two were later reunited in former <a href="/wiki/Deep_Purple" title="Deep Purple">Deep Purple</a> <a href="/wiki/Vocals" class="mw-redirect" title="Vocals">singer</a> <a href="/wiki/Ian_Gillan" title="Ian Gillan">Ian Gillan</a>'s band.
</p><p>On 18 July 2009, John McCoy performed at the Furnace in Swindon Wiltshire, England Performing in a group G.M.T with Bernie Tormé (guitar legend formerly with <a href="/wiki/Gillan_(band)" title="Gillan (band)">Gillan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne" title="Ozzy Osbourne">Ozzy Osbourne</a>) Robin Guy (former drummer with Iron Maiden's <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Dickinson" title="Bruce Dickinson">Bruce Dickinson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Faith_No_More" title="Faith No More">Faith No More</a>). More recently John has played with the Tyler Gang, appearing on a live album recorded in Sweden.
</p><p>John McCoy later went on to release, "Trash Basket and Tip-Toe Titters," in a famed attempt to crack into the experimental bass genre.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_with_Gillan">Career with Gillan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_McCoy_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career with Gillan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Gillan_(band)" title="Gillan (band)">Gillan (band)</a></div>
<p>In July 1978, the <a href="/wiki/Jazz_fusion" title="Jazz fusion">jazz-rock fusion</a> <a href="/wiki/Ian_Gillan_Band" title="Ian Gillan Band">Ian Gillan Band</a> were altering direction, under the influence of <a href="/wiki/Keyboard_instrument" title="Keyboard instrument">keyboards</a> player <a href="/wiki/Colin_Towns" title="Colin Towns">Colin Towns</a> in a return to Ian Gillan's <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">hard rock</a> roots. Towns had begun writing new material, and Gillan gave him the task of recruiting the new line-up. Towns recruited session drummer <a href="/wiki/Liam_Genockey" title="Liam Genockey">Liam Genockey</a>, McCoy and guitarist Richard Brampton, who was replaced by Steve Byrd - a former colleague of McCoy's from <a href="/w/index.php?title=ZZebra&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ZZebra (page does not exist)">ZZebra</a> - almost immediately. Within a month of their formation the band had recorded their first album, <i><a href="/wiki/Gillan_(album)" title="Gillan (album)">Gillan</a></i>, and they made their live debut at the <a href="/wiki/Reading_and_Leeds_Festivals" title="Reading and Leeds Festivals">Reading Festival</a> on 16 August 1978. They were originally listed there as the Ian Gillan Band but, in a move away from the jazz-rock connotations, they renamed the band, Gillan.
</p><p>Gillan underwent a further three line-up changes, but McCoy remained as bass player until the band eventually split acrimoniously<sup id="cite_ref-SPLIT_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPLIT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in 1982.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mammoth">Mammoth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_McCoy_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mammoth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Post-Gillan, McCoy recruited session drummer Vinnie "Tubby" Reed, guitarist "Big" Mac Baker and vocalist <a href="/wiki/Nicky_Moore" title="Nicky Moore">Nicky Moore</a> to form a band initially called Dinosaur. The name was already in use by a <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">Californian</a> band, so McCoy renamed his new outfit Mammoth. The name was also a <a href="/wiki/Tongue-in-cheek" title="Tongue-in-cheek">tongue-in-cheek</a> reference to the large size of the band members: McCoy weighed 120&#160;kg (19&#160;st), Reed 140&#160;kg (22&#160;st), Baker 152&#160;kg (23.9&#160;st), and Moore 127&#160;kg (20.0&#160;st).
</p><p>The band toured with <a href="/wiki/Whitesnake" title="Whitesnake">Whitesnake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marillion" title="Marillion">Marillion</a> and were well received by fans. They released one single, "Fatman", and two albums, <i>Mammoth</i> and <i>Larger And Live</i>. In 1988, the entire band appeared in the film <i>Just Ask For Diamond</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> playing the <a href="/wiki/Henchmen" class="mw-redirect" title="Henchmen">henchmen</a>. Musically, commercial success eluded them however and the band eventually split in 1989, with McCoy becoming an independent <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment">Equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_McCoy_(musician)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>McCoy usually uses a traditional <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar#Design_considerations" title="Bass guitar">four string</a> <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar#Frets" title="Bass guitar">fretted</a> <a href="/wiki/Fender_Precision_Bass" title="Fender Precision Bass">Fender Precision</a> guitar and predominantly <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Amplification" title="Marshall Amplification">Marshall</a> <a href="/wiki/Bass_instrument_amplification" class="mw-redirect" title="Bass instrument amplification">amplification</a> in various configurations. Although he has used <a href="/wiki/Trace_Elliot" title="Trace Elliot">Trace Elliot</a>, he has described it as "...a bit clean for my personal taste..." <sup id="cite_ref-AMPS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMPS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Currently he uses a Marshall 200w integrated amp driving a 2x15 <a href="/wiki/Bass_instrument_amplification#Cabinet_design" class="mw-redirect" title="Bass instrument amplification">cab</a> and a Marshall 100w lead amp driving <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Amplification#Marshall_Stack" title="Marshall Amplification">4x12 cabs</a>.
</p><p>His <a href="/wiki/Bass_guitar#Pick_vs._fingers_(or_thumb)" title="Bass guitar">playing style</a> utilises both <a href="/wiki/Guitar_pick" title="Guitar pick">pick</a> and fingers, although he plays mostly with picks, preferring Fender <a href="/wiki/Guitar_pick#Thickness" title="Guitar pick">extra heavy</a> large triangles "...for greater precision and attack."<sup id="cite_ref-AMPS_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMPS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
