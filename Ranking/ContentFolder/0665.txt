<p>The <b>English Football League Championship</b> (often referred to as the <b>Championship</b> for short or the <b>Sky Bet Championship</b> for sponsorship reasons)<sup id="cite_ref-SkyBet_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SkyBet-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is the highest division of the <a href="/wiki/English_Football_League" title="English Football League">English Football League</a> (EFL) and second-highest overall in the <a href="/wiki/English_football_league_system" title="English football league system">English football league system</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a>. The league is contested by 24 clubs. Each season, the two top-finishing teams in the Championship are automatically promoted to the Premier League. The teams that finish the season in 3rd to 6th place enter a playoff tournament, with the winner also gaining promotion to the Premier League. The three lowest-finishing teams in the Championship are relegated to <a href="/wiki/EFL_League_One" title="EFL League One">League One</a>.
</p><p>The Football League Championship, which was introduced for the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_in_English_football" title="2004–05 in English football">2004–05 season</a>, was previously known as the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_First_Division" title="Football League First Division">Football League First Division</a> (1992–2004), and before that was known as Division One (1892–1992). The winners of the Championship receive the Football League Championship trophy, the same trophy as the old First Division champions were handed prior to the Premier League's inception in 1992. Similar to other divisions of professional English football, Welsh clubs can be part of the division, making it a cross-border league.
</p><p>The Championship is the wealthiest non-top flight <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> division in the world and the seventh richest division in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> With an average match attendance for the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_EFL_Championship" title="2016–17 EFL Championship">2016–17 season</a> of 20,130 the Championship ranked second after the German <a href="/wiki/2._Bundesliga" title="2. Bundesliga">2. Bundesliga</a> as the most-watched secondary league in the world.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Barnsley_F.C." title="Barnsley F.C.">Barnsley</a> have spent more seasons at the second level of English football than any other team and on 3 January 2011 became the first club to achieve 1,000 wins in the second level of English football with a 2–1 home victory over <a href="/wiki/Coventry_City_F.C." title="Coventry City F.C.">Coventry City</a>. Barnsley are also the first club to play 3,000 games in second-level league football (W1028, D747, L1224).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At present, <a href="/wiki/Derby_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Derby County">Derby County</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_Forest" class="mw-redirect" title="Nottingham Forest">Nottingham Forest</a> hold the longest tenure in the Championship, last being out of the division in the 2007–08 season.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EFL_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl><dd><i>For history before 2004, see <a href="/wiki/Football_League_First_Division" title="Football League First Division">Football League First Division</a> after 1993 and <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Second_Division" title="Football League Second Division">Football League Second Division</a> before that year</i></dd></dl>
<p>In its inaugural season of <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_in_English_football" title="2004–05 in English football">2004–05</a>, the Football League Championship announced a total attendance (including postseason) of 9.8&#160;million, which it said was the fourth highest total attendance for a European football division, behind the FA Premier League (12.88m), Spain's <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> (11.57m) and Germany's <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a> (10.92m), but beating Italy's <a href="/wiki/Serie_A" title="Serie A">Serie A</a> (9.77m) and France's <a href="/wiki/Ligue_1" title="Ligue 1">Ligue 1</a> (8.17m).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Sunderland won the league in the first season since re-branding, with <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> finishing second to win promotion to the top flight of English football for the first time in their history. They had only been elected to the Football League twenty-seven years previously; playing in the <a href="/wiki/EFL_League_Two" title="EFL League Two">fourth tier</a> as recently as eleven years prior to their promotion. <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> won the first Championship play-off final that season, following a 1–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Preston_North_End_F.C." title="Preston North End F.C.">Preston North End</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Stadium" title="Millennium Stadium">Millennium Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Football_League_Championship" title="2005–06 Football League Championship">2005–06 season</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a> broke the <a href="/wiki/English_Football_League" title="English Football League">Football League</a> points record for a season, finishing on 106 points, exceeding the record set by Sunderland in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sunderland won their second Championship title in three seasons in the <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Football_League_Championship" title="2006–07 Football League Championship">2006–07 season</a>. On 4 May 2007, <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_A.F.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds United A.F.C.">Leeds United</a> became the first side since the re-branding of the division to enter administration; they were deducted 10 points and were relegated as a result.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On 28 May 2007, <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> won the first Championship play-off final at the new <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>, beating <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> 1–0 in front of nearly 75,000 spectators.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> West Brom would go on to win the Championship in the following season.
</p><p>On 30 September 2009, <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> announced they would end their sponsorship deal with <a href="/wiki/English_Football_League" title="English Football League">The Football League</a> (now English Football League) at the end of the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Football_League" title="2009–10 Football League">2009–10 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On 16 March 2010, <a href="/wiki/RWE_npower" class="mw-redirect" title="RWE npower">npower</a> were announced as the new title sponsors of the Football League, and from the start of the 2010–11 Football League season until the end of the 2012–13 season, the Football League Championship was known as the <i>Npower Championship</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 18 July 2013, UK bookmaker <a href="/wiki/Sky_Bet" class="mw-redirect" title="Sky Bet">Sky Bet</a> announced that they signed a five-year agreement to sponsor the league.<sup id="cite_ref-SkyBet_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SkyBet-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 24 May 2014, the Championship play-off final between Derby County and <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> saw the highest crowd for any Championship fixture – 87,348 witnessed a <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Zamora" title="Bobby Zamora">Bobby Zamora</a> stoppage time winner for QPR to win promotion for the London club.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_EFL_Championship" title="2016–17 EFL Championship">2016–17 season</a>, the Football League was re-branded as the English Football League. That season, <a href="/wiki/Rotherham_United_F.C." title="Rotherham United F.C.">Rotherham United</a> recorded the lowest points total in Championship history – winning just 23 points from their 46 matches. The league had an cumulative attendance of more than eleven million – excluding play-off matches – with more than two million watching <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> home fixtures alone; both of whom had been relegated from the Premier League in the previous season. This was included in the highest crowds for the second to fourth tier in England since the <a href="/wiki/1958%E2%80%9359_in_English_football" title="1958–59 in English football">1958–59 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure_of_the_league">Structure of the league</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EFL_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Structure of the league">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The league comprises 24 teams. Over the course of a season, which runs annually from August to the following May, each team plays twice against the others in the league, once at 'home' and once 'away', resulting in each team competing in 46 games in total. Three points are awarded for a win, one for a draw and zero for a loss. The teams are ranked in the league table by points gained, then goal difference, then goals scored and then their head-to-head record for that season. In the event that two or more teams finish the season equal in all these respects, teams are separated by alphabetical order, unless a promotion, relegation or play-off place (see below) is at stake, when the teams are separated by a play-off game, though this improbable situation has never arisen in all the years the rule has existed.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of the season, the top two teams and the winner of the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship_play-offs" class="mw-redirect" title="Football League Championship play-offs">Championship play-offs</a> are promoted to the Premier League and the bottom three teams are relegated to <a href="/wiki/Football_League_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Football League One">Football League One</a>. The Football League Championship play-offs is a knock-out competition for the teams finishing the season in third to sixth place with the winner being promoted to the Premier League. In the play-offs, the third-placed team plays against the sixth-placed team and the fourth-placed team plays against the fifth-placed team in two-legged semi-finals (home and away). The winners of each semi-final then compete in a single match at Wembley  stadium with the prize being promotion to the Premier League and the Championship play-off trophy.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasting_rights">Broadcasting rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EFL_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Broadcasting rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UK_television">UK television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EFL_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: UK television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Highlights were broadcast on <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> from 1994 to 2009, firstly on <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Extra" title="Football League Extra">Football League Extra</a>, and later on <a href="/wiki/The_Championship_(TV_programme)" title="The Championship (TV programme)">The Championship</a> from 2004, until the highlights rights were bought by the BBC in 2009.
From 2001 to 2002, live matches were broadcast on <a href="/wiki/ITV_Digital" title="ITV Digital">ITV Digital</a>, although the company was put into administration in March 2002 and ceased broadcasting after the football season. The broadcast rights were taken over by Sky Sports.
</p><p>From 2009 to 2012, <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a> had the rights to broadcast 65 live matches. Live coverage of both legs of both play-off semi finals and the play-off final are shown live.
Highlights are shown on <a href="/wiki/Quest_(TV_channel)" class="mw-redirect" title="Quest (TV channel)">Quest</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UK_radio">UK radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=EFL_Championship&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: UK radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/TalkSPORT" class="mw-redirect" title="TalkSPORT">talkSPORT</a> hold exclusive national rights to broadcast audio commentary of a selection of Championship matches live to the whole of the United Kingdom; most headline matches are broadcast on either <a href="/wiki/TalkSPORT" class="mw-redirect" title="TalkSPORT">talkSPORT</a> or talkSPORT2. However, <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sport" title="BBC Sport">BBC Sport</a> does have the rights to broadcast audio commentary for <a href="/wiki/BBC_Local_Radio" title="BBC Local Radio">BBC Local Radio</a> in an area with a Championship team.
</p>
