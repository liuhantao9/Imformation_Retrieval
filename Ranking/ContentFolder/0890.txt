<p><b>Castleford</b> is a town in the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a> of <a href="/wiki/City_of_Wakefield" title="City of Wakefield">Wakefield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. It had a population of 40,210 at the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, to the north of the town centre the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a> joins the <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">River Aire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Aire_and_Calder_Navigation" title="Aire and Calder Navigation">Aire and Calder Navigation</a>.
</p><p>The town is the site of a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Britain" title="Roman Britain">Roman</a> settlement. Within the historical Castleford Borough are the suburbs of <a href="/wiki/Airedale,_Castleford" title="Airedale, Castleford">Airedale</a>, Cutsyke, <a href="/wiki/Ferry_Fryston" title="Ferry Fryston">Ferry Fryston</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fryston_Village&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fryston Village (page does not exist)">Fryston Village</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glasshoughton" title="Glasshoughton">Glasshoughton</a>, Half Acres, Hightown, Lock Lane, Townville, Wheldale and Whitwood.
</p><p>Castleford is home to the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a> team <a href="/wiki/Castleford_Tigers" title="Castleford Tigers">Castleford Tigers</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castleford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wheldale_Colliery,_Castleford,_%22the_sunshine_pit%22_-_geograph.org.uk_-_760530.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Wheldale_Colliery%2C_Castleford%2C_%22the_sunshine_pit%22_-_geograph.org.uk_-_760530.jpg/220px-Wheldale_Colliery%2C_Castleford%2C_%22the_sunshine_pit%22_-_geograph.org.uk_-_760530.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="237" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Wheldale_Colliery%2C_Castleford%2C_%22the_sunshine_pit%22_-_geograph.org.uk_-_760530.jpg/330px-Wheldale_Colliery%2C_Castleford%2C_%22the_sunshine_pit%22_-_geograph.org.uk_-_760530.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Wheldale_Colliery%2C_Castleford%2C_%22the_sunshine_pit%22_-_geograph.org.uk_-_760530.jpg/440px-Wheldale_Colliery%2C_Castleford%2C_%22the_sunshine_pit%22_-_geograph.org.uk_-_760530.jpg 2x" data-file-width="594" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wheldale_Colliery,_Castleford,_%22the_sunshine_pit%22_-_geograph.org.uk_-_760530.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Wheldale Colliery was one of the towns collieries until its closure in 1985.</div></div></div>
<p>Castleford's history dates back to <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman times</a>, archaeological evidence points to modern day Castleford being built upon a <a href="/wiki/Roman_army" title="Roman army">Roman</a> army settlement which was called <a href="/wiki/Lagentium" title="Lagentium">Lagentium</a> (thought to mean 'The Place of the Swordsmen').<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Roman funeral urns have been found in modern-day Castleford, giving further evidence to this theory.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> A Roman milestone was unearthed in Beancroft Road, now believed to be in Leeds City Museum.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Queen's Park in Castleford provides evidence of <a href="/wiki/Roundhouse_(dwelling)" title="Roundhouse (dwelling)">Roundhouses</a> used by the <a href="/wiki/Anglo_Saxons" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo Saxons">Anglo Saxons</a>. This was a strategic area due to the views of the entire settlement. The history of the area includes <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell" title="Oliver Cromwell">Oliver Cromwell</a>’s encampment in nearby Knottingley and Ferrybridge (originally thought to be in Castleford itself) whilst his forces laid siege to Pontefract Castle.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hightown,_Castleford,_All_Saints_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226763.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Hightown%2C_Castleford%2C_All_Saints_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226763.jpg/220px-Hightown%2C_Castleford%2C_All_Saints_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226763.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="142" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Hightown%2C_Castleford%2C_All_Saints_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226763.jpg/330px-Hightown%2C_Castleford%2C_All_Saints_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226763.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e0/Hightown%2C_Castleford%2C_All_Saints_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226763.jpg 2x" data-file-width="432" data-file-height="278" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hightown,_Castleford,_All_Saints_Church_-_geograph.org.uk_-_226763.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>All Saints' Church</div></div></div>
<p>In the 19th century, Castleford became a <a href="/wiki/Boomtown" title="Boomtown">boomtown</a> with the population growing from 1,000 to 14,000 as collieries opened around the town; however, these collieries closed in the 20th century. <a href="/wiki/Ferrybridge_Power_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrybridge Power Station">Ferrybridge Power Station</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kellingley_Colliery" title="Kellingley Colliery">Kellingley Colliery</a> have also closed (in 2015 and 2016 respectively) and used to employ Castleford residents.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The newer warehouses and distribution centres in <a href="/wiki/Glasshoughton" title="Glasshoughton">Glasshoughton</a> have brought in many new jobs to the area.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008 <i><a href="/wiki/Grand_Designs" title="Grand Designs">Grand Designs</a></i> presenter <a href="/wiki/Kevin_McCloud" title="Kevin McCloud">Kevin McCloud</a> and <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> led a community regeneration scheme to redevelop Castleford.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2017 Castleford Swimming Pool closed. A new modern leisure facility including a swimming pool is proposed for nearby Pontefract Park.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castleford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Carlton_Street,_Castleford.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Carlton_Street%2C_Castleford.jpg/220px-Carlton_Street%2C_Castleford.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Carlton_Street%2C_Castleford.jpg/330px-Carlton_Street%2C_Castleford.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Carlton_Street%2C_Castleford.jpg/440px-Carlton_Street%2C_Castleford.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Carlton_Street,_Castleford.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Carlton Street is Castleford's main shopping street.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Burberry" title="Burberry">Burberry</a>, the retailer and designer label, has a factory in the town which is due to close when a new facility is built in Leeds.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The new plant will be a combination of the Castleford and Cross Hills plants, but the Brexit vote has stalled the decision, though Burberry insist it will go ahead.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> A large <a href="/wiki/Nestl%C3%A9" title="Nestlé">Nestlé</a> factory, which produced Toffee Crisp and After Eights for 40 years,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> closed in 2012 and demolition starting in 2014 to make way for housing.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Castleford has previously been home to Dunsford and Wesley Textiles, which at peak business times had three factories in Castleford which have all since ceased production.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castleford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
 <div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Reclining_figure,_Castleford_Civic_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_643586.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Reclining_figure%2C_Castleford_Civic_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_643586.jpg/220px-Reclining_figure%2C_Castleford_Civic_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_643586.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Reclining_figure%2C_Castleford_Civic_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_643586.jpg/330px-Reclining_figure%2C_Castleford_Civic_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_643586.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/33/Reclining_figure%2C_Castleford_Civic_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_643586.jpg 2x" data-file-width="427" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Reclining_figure,_Castleford_Civic_Centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_643586.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Castleford Civic Centre</div></div></div><p> Castleford was established as an <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a>, in the administrative county of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a> in 1894 under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1894" title="Local Government Act 1894">Local Government Act 1894</a>, with an urban district council. Whitwood and <a href="/wiki/Glasshoughton" title="Glasshoughton">Glasshoughton</a> were added to the district in the 1930s.  The urban district was incorporated as a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> in 1955.
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, the municipal borough was abolished on 1 April 1974, it then became an <a href="/wiki/Unparished_area" title="Unparished area">unparished area</a> of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Wakefield" title="City of Wakefield">City of Wakefield</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. Castleford is now controlled by <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Council" title="Wakefield Council">Wakefield Council</a>. Three electoral wards cover the town: Airedale and Ferry Fryston; Altofts and Whitwood; and Castleford Central and Glasshoughton.
</p>

<p>The town contains a Junction 32 multi-store shopping village and the <a href="/wiki/Xscape_(building)#Xscape_Yorkshire" title="Xscape (building)">Xscape</a> Leisure Complex, both in the Glasshoughton suburb at the south of the town. In Xscape is Snozone, which used to have Britain's largest indoor real snow slope (but this was edged out by Chillfactore in Manchester)<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and contains <a href="/wiki/Frankie_%26_Benny%27s" title="Frankie &amp; Benny&#39;s">Frankie &amp; Benny's</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nando%27s#Europe" title="Nando&#39;s">Nando's</a> restaurants.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Castleford has an open and an indoor market, a shopping centre on Carlton Street, a retail park, and <a href="/wiki/Asda" title="Asda">Asda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morrisons" title="Morrisons">Morrisons</a>, Aldi and <a href="/wiki/The_Co-operative_Group" title="The Co-operative Group">Co-operative</a> supermarkets.
</p>

<p>In December 2013 the Forum, a library and heritage museum, opened in a new building. The town's library had been in temporary accommodation for two years while the old site was redeveloped. The existing frontage was kept, while the building further back was demolished.  A new three-floor building was then constructed.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town centre has <a href="/wiki/Public_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Public house">public houses</a>, bars, and social clubs, although the number of these has declined in recent years<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (May 2017)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> with leisure becoming focused on the Xscape complex. In 2000 three nightclubs were situated in the town centre. Today none remain.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castleford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ridings_FM" title="Ridings FM">Ridings FM</a> is Castleford's local radio station, Launched in 1999, The station can be heard in the town on 106.8 FM. <a href="/wiki/Ridings_FM" title="Ridings FM">Ridings FM</a> broadcasts to the wider Wakefield District and has a close relationship with the community and is actively involved in local charities fundraising efforts. The town hosts a community radio station called five towns radio available on the internet.
The local newspaper is a weekly called Pontefract and Castleford Express.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castleford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Castleford is only 0.93 miles (1.5&#160;km) away from the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62</a> at Junction 32 via the <a href="/wiki/A656_road" class="mw-redirect" title="A656 road">A656 road</a>. It has other A roads that criss-cross the town and provide access to Junction 31 on the same motorway.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The River Aire and the River Calder have a confluence just north west of the town and also feed the Aire and Calder Navigation. Whilst it is an industrial waterway, it also hosts tourism with moorings on the Castleford Cut of the Navigation.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town has a <a href="/wiki/Castleford_railway_station" title="Castleford railway station">railway station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Hallam_Line" title="Hallam Line">Hallam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pontefract_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Pontefract Line">Pontefract lines</a>. All trains arrive from the west and leave in that same direction.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Trains used to go eastwards to <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York</a> via Burton Salmon, but passenger workings ceased in 1970 with the section retained for freight workings only.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Castleford also had a second service to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> that left eastwards and swung north onto the former <a href="/wiki/Castleford_to_Garforth_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Castleford to Garforth Line">Castleford to Garforth Line</a>, which would see services calling at Ledston and Kippax, before arriving in <a href="/wiki/Garforth_railway_station" title="Garforth railway station">Garforth</a> and continuing to Leeds.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, a new £6&#160;million bus station was opened adjacent to the railway station to allow an integrated travel site for the 12,000 people using the bus station per day.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Glasshoughton" title="Glasshoughton">Glasshoughton</a> area of the town is served by its own <a href="/wiki/Glasshoughton_railway_station" title="Glasshoughton railway station">railway station</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castleford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wakefield_College_Castleford_April_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Wakefield_College_Castleford_April_2017.jpg/220px-Wakefield_College_Castleford_April_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Wakefield_College_Castleford_April_2017.jpg/330px-Wakefield_College_Castleford_April_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Wakefield_College_Castleford_April_2017.jpg/440px-Wakefield_College_Castleford_April_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wakefield_College_Castleford_April_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Wakefield College, Castleford Campus</div></div></div>
<p>The town's primary schools for infants and children are Wheldon Infant School, Castleford Park Junior, Smawthorne Henry Moore, Townville Infant School, Airedale Juniors, Oyster Park Primary, Fairburn View Primary, Three Lane Ends Academy, Acton Pastures and Half Acres.
The town's secondary schools for children aged 11–16 are <a href="/wiki/Castleford_Academy" title="Castleford Academy">Castleford Academy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Airedale_High_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Airedale High School">Airedale Academy</a>. The town has a small further and higher education college on the Castleford Campus of <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_College" title="Wakefield College">Wakefield College</a> in Glasshoughton.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby_League">Rugby League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castleford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Rugby League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Jungle_Castleford_Tigers_Rugby_league_Ground_(geograph_5648191).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/The_Jungle_Castleford_Tigers_Rugby_league_Ground_%28geograph_5648191%29.jpg/220px-The_Jungle_Castleford_Tigers_Rugby_league_Ground_%28geograph_5648191%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/The_Jungle_Castleford_Tigers_Rugby_league_Ground_%28geograph_5648191%29.jpg/330px-The_Jungle_Castleford_Tigers_Rugby_league_Ground_%28geograph_5648191%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/The_Jungle_Castleford_Tigers_Rugby_league_Ground_%28geograph_5648191%29.jpg/440px-The_Jungle_Castleford_Tigers_Rugby_league_Ground_%28geograph_5648191%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4160" data-file-height="2745" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Jungle_Castleford_Tigers_Rugby_league_Ground_(geograph_5648191).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/The_Jungle_(Wheldon_Road)" title="The Jungle (Wheldon Road)">Wheldon Road</a></div></div></div>
<p>Castleford is a <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> town. The local team, <a href="/wiki/Castleford_Tigers" title="Castleford Tigers">Castleford Tigers</a> was formed in 1926, Castleford was one of the twelve founder members of <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a> when the new league format was introduced in 1996. The 'Tigers' nickname and logo were introduced in 1992 and the clubs honours include four <a href="/wiki/Challenge_Cup" title="Challenge Cup">Challenge Cup</a> and one Super League leaders shield.
</p><p>The club have been based at <a href="/wiki/Wheldon_Road" class="mw-redirect" title="Wheldon Road">Wheldon Road</a> since 1927. Castleford also has two other lower league rugby league teams, <a href="/wiki/Castleford_Lock_Lane" title="Castleford Lock Lane">Castleford Lock Lane</a> and Castleford Panthers.
</p><p>Rugby league in the town was originally represented by <a href="/wiki/Castleford_RFC_(1896)" title="Castleford RFC (1896)">Castleford</a>, unrelated to Castleford RUFC, who did not participate in <a href="/wiki/History_of_rugby_league#The_schism_in_England" title="History of rugby league">The schism in English rugby</a> and still exist, nor were they predecessors of the current Castleford Tigers who were founded in 1926. The original Castleford rugby league club played in the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_League" title="Rugby Football League">Northern Union (Rugby Football League)</a> from <a href="/wiki/1896%E2%80%9397_Northern_Rugby_Football_Union_season" title="1896–97 Northern Rugby Football Union season">1896–97</a> to <a href="/wiki/1905%E2%80%9306_Northern_Rugby_Football_Union_season" title="1905–06 Northern Rugby Football Union season">1905–06</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Records_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Records-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and it had one player named <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Cole" title="Isaac Cole">Isaac Cole</a> who won a <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">cap</a> for <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_league_team" title="England national rugby league team">England</a> in 1906 against <a href="/wiki/Other_Nationalities_rugby_league_team" title="Other Nationalities rugby league team">Other Nationalities</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-England_Statistics_at_englandrl.co.uk_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-England_Statistics_at_englandrl.co.uk-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Motorcycle_sport">Motorcycle sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castleford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Motorcycle sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From June 1979 to July 1980, Castleford had its own <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_speedway" title="Motorcycle speedway">Speedway</a> racing team, The Kings, based at the <a href="/wiki/Castleford_Whitwood_Stadium" title="Castleford Whitwood Stadium">Castleford Whitwood Stadium</a>. The circuit was 202 yards and the team never entered the league&#160;– only challenge matches were staged.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Football">Football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castleford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Glasshoughton Welfare play in the Northern Counties East League, Fryston Colliery Welfare were members of this league until they left in 1991 due to ground grading problems. In the 1920s Castleford Town played in the Midland League alongside clubs such as Doncaster Rovers, Chesterfield, Scunthorpe United, Halifax Town, Leeds United (for 1919–20 season only), Lincoln City and Mansfield Town. This league also contained the reserve sides of Sheffield Wednesday, Barnsley, Nottingham Forest, Grimsby Town and Hull City. In 1920 Castleford reached the 2nd round of the FA Cup, losing 2–3 at Bradford Park Avenue. Castleford played at Wheldon Road&#160;– when they shut down the ground was taken over by the rugby league team.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Greyhound_racing">Greyhound racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castleford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Greyhound racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Castleford hosted two greyhound tracks both of which are no longer in existence. The first was in Whitwood and held racing from 1939 until 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The second track was the slightly larger 1,500 capacity <a href="/wiki/Castleford_Sports_Stadium" title="Castleford Sports Stadium">Castleford Sports Stadium</a> located east of Lock Lane sitting on the north bank of the River Aire.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Castleford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Henry_Moore" title="Henry Moore">Henry Moore</a>, sculptor who was born and educated in the town.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Viv_Nicholson" title="Viv Nicholson">Viv Nicholson</a>, born in Castleford and became famous after she and her husband won the football pools in the early 1960s. On winning £152,000 (£3.5&#160;million in 2015), Nicholson claimed that she would "Spend, spend, spend."<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Peter_Robinson_(novelist)" title="Peter Robinson (novelist)">Peter Robinson</a>, crime writer famous for his Detective Inspector Banks novels, set in the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Dales" title="Yorkshire Dales">Yorkshire Dales</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Richard_Stoker" title="Richard Stoker">Richard Stoker</a> composer and actor was born in Castleford, living at Glasshoughton before moving to London.</li></ul>
