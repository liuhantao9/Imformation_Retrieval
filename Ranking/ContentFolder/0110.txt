<p>The <b>Basque Country</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;b&#39; in &#39;buy&#39;">b</span><span title="/æ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;bad&#39;">æ</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span></span>, <wbr /><span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;b&#39; in &#39;buy&#39;">b</span><span title="/ɑː/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;father&#39;">ɑː</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque</a>: <i lang="eu">Euskadi</i> <small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Basque" title="Help:IPA/Basque">[eus̺kadi]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">País Vasco</i> <small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[paˈiz ˈβasko]</a></span>; <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Pays Basque</i>), officially the <b>Basque Autonomous Community</b> (<a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque</a>: <i lang="eu">Euskal Autonomia Erkidegoa</i>, EAE; <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Comunidad Autónoma Vasca</i>, CAV) is an <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">autonomous community</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. It includes the Basque provinces of <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lava" title="Álava">Álava</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biscay" title="Biscay">Biscay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gipuzkoa" title="Gipuzkoa">Gipuzkoa</a>.
</p><p>The Basque Country or Basque Autonomous Community was granted the status of <i><a href="/wiki/Nationalities_and_regions_of_Spain" title="Nationalities and regions of Spain">nationality</a></i> within Spain, attributed by the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Constitution_of_1978" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Constitution of 1978">Spanish Constitution of 1978</a>. The autonomous community is based on the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Autonomy_of_the_Basque_Country" title="Statute of Autonomy of the Basque Country">Statute of Autonomy of the Basque Country</a>, a foundational legal document providing the framework for the development of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Basque people">Basque people</a> on <a href="/wiki/Southern_Basque_Country" title="Southern Basque Country">Spanish soil</a>. Navarre, which had narrowly rejected a joint statute of autonomy with Gipuzkoa, Álava and Biscay in 1932,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was granted a separate statute in 1982.
</p><p>Currently there is no official capital in the autonomous community, but the city of <a href="/wiki/Vitoria-Gasteiz" title="Vitoria-Gasteiz">Vitoria-Gasteiz</a>, in the province of Álava, is the de facto capital as the location of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Parliament" title="Basque Parliament">Basque Parliament</a>, the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Government" title="Basque Government">Basque Government</a>, and the residence of the President of the Basque Autonomous Community (the <a href="/wiki/Ajuria_Enea" title="Ajuria Enea">Palace of Ajuria Enea</a>). The <a href="/wiki/High_Courts_of_Justice_of_Spain" title="High Courts of Justice of Spain">High Court of Justice of the Basque Country</a> has its headquarters in the city of <a href="/wiki/Bilbao" title="Bilbao">Bilbao</a>. Whilst Vitoria-Gasteiz is the largest municipality in area, with 277&#160;km<sup>2</sup> (107 sq mi), Bilbao is the largest in population, with 353,187 people, located in the province of Biscay within a conurbation of 875,552 people.
</p><p>The term <i>Basque Country</i> may also refer to the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(greater_region)" title="Basque Country (greater region)">larger cultural region</a> (Basque: <i>Euskal Herria</i>), the home of the Basque people, which includes the autonomous community.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Physical_geography_of_Euskal_Herria" class="mw-redirect" title="Physical geography of Euskal Herria">Physical geography of Euskal Herria</a></div>
<p>The following <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Spain" title="Provinces of Spain">provinces</a> make up the autonomous community:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/%C3%81lava" title="Álava">Álava</a> (Basque <i>Araba</i>), capital <a href="/wiki/Vitoria-Gasteiz" title="Vitoria-Gasteiz">Vitoria-Gasteiz</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Biscay" title="Biscay">Biscay</a> (Spanish <i>Vizcaya</i>, Basque <i>Bizkaia</i>), capital <a href="/wiki/Bilbao" title="Bilbao">Bilbao</a>-Bilbo</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gipuzkoa" title="Gipuzkoa">Gipuzkoa</a> (Spanish <i>Guipúzcoa</i>), capital <a href="/wiki/Donostia-San_Sebasti%C3%A1n" class="mw-redirect" title="Donostia-San Sebastián">Donostia-San Sebastián</a></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Txindoki_mountain_from_Lazkaomendi.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Txindoki_mountain_from_Lazkaomendi.jpg/220px-Txindoki_mountain_from_Lazkaomendi.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Txindoki_mountain_from_Lazkaomendi.jpg/330px-Txindoki_mountain_from_Lazkaomendi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Txindoki_mountain_from_Lazkaomendi.jpg/440px-Txindoki_mountain_from_Lazkaomendi.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="479" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Txindoki_mountain_from_Lazkaomendi.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Txindoki mountain from Lazkaomendi</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mundaka.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Mundaka.JPG/220px-Mundaka.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Mundaka.JPG/330px-Mundaka.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Mundaka.JPG/440px-Mundaka.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mundaka.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Basque coast near <a href="/wiki/Mundaka" title="Mundaka">Mundaka</a></div></div></div>

<p>The Basque Country borders <a href="/wiki/Cantabria" title="Cantabria">Cantabria</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Burgos_province" class="mw-redirect" title="Burgos province">Burgos province</a> to the west, the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Biscay" title="Bay of Biscay">Bay of Biscay</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nouvelle-Aquitaine" title="Nouvelle-Aquitaine">Nouvelle-Aquitaine</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a> to the east and <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="La Rioja, Spain">La Rioja</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Ebro_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Ebro River">Ebro River</a>) to the south. The territory has three distinct areas, which are defined by the two parallel ranges of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Mountains" title="Basque Mountains">Basque Mountains</a>. The main range of mountains forms the <a href="/wiki/Drainage_divide" title="Drainage divide">watershed</a> between the Atlantic and Mediterranean basins. The highest point of the range is in the <a href="/wiki/Aizkorri" title="Aizkorri">Aizkorri</a> massif (1551 m). The three areas are:
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Atlantic_Basin">Atlantic Basin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Atlantic Basin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Formed by many valleys with short rivers that flow from the mountains to the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Biscay" title="Bay of Biscay">Bay of Biscay</a>, like the <a href="/wiki/Nervi%C3%B3n" title="Nervión">Nervión</a>, <a href="/wiki/Urola" class="mw-redirect" title="Urola">Urola</a> or <a href="/wiki/Oria_(river)" title="Oria (river)">Oria</a>.
The coast is rough, with high cliffs and small inlets. The main features of the coast are the <a href="/wiki/Bilbao_Abra" title="Bilbao Abra">Bilbao Abra</a> Bay and the <a href="/wiki/Estuary_of_Bilbao" title="Estuary of Bilbao">Estuary of Bilbao</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Urdaibai" class="mw-redirect" title="Urdaibai">Urdaibai</a> estuary and the <a href="/wiki/Bidasoa-Txingudi" title="Bidasoa-Txingudi">Bidasoa-Txingudi</a> Bay that forms the border with France.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_section">Middle section</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Middle section">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Between the two mountain ranges, the area is occupied mainly by a high plateau called <i>Llanada Alavesa</i> (the <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lava" title="Álava">Álava</a> Plains), where the capital Vitoria-Gasteiz is located. The rivers flow south from the mountains to the Ebro River. The main rivers are the <a href="/wiki/Zadorra_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Zadorra River">Zadorra River</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bayas_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Bayas River">Bayas River</a>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Ebro_Valley">Ebro Valley</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ebro Valley">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>From the southern mountains to the Ebro is the so-called <i>Rioja Alavesa</i>, which shares the Mediterranean characteristics of other Ebro Valley zones. Some of Spain's production of <a href="/wiki/Rioja_wine" class="mw-redirect" title="Rioja wine">Rioja wine</a> takes place here.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Basque_Mountains" title="Basque Mountains">Basque Mountains</a> form the watershed and also mark the distinct climatic areas of the Basque Country:
The northern valleys, in Biscay and Gipuzkoa and also the valley of Ayala in Álava, are part of <a href="/wiki/Green_Spain" title="Green Spain">Green Spain</a>, where the <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">oceanic climate</a> is predominant, with its wet weather all year round and moderate temperatures. Precipitation average is about 1200&#160;mm.
</p><p>The middle section is influenced more by the <a href="/wiki/Continental_climate" title="Continental climate">continental climate</a>, but with a varying degree of the northern oceanic climate. This gives warm, dry summers and cold, snowy winters.
</p><p>The Ebro valley has a pure continental climate: winters are cold and dry and summers very warm and dry, with precipitation peaking in spring and autumn. Precipitation is scarce and irregular, as low as 300&#160;mm.
</p>

<p>Almost half of the 2,155,546 inhabitants of the Basque Autonomous Community live in <a href="/wiki/Greater_Bilbao" title="Greater Bilbao">Greater Bilbao</a>, Bilbao's metropolitan area. Of the ten most populous cities, six form part of Bilbao's <a href="/wiki/Conurbation" title="Conurbation">conurbation</a> (Bilbao, Barakaldo, Getxo, Portugalete, Santurtzi and Basauri), which is also known as Greater Bilbao.
</p><p>With 28.2% of the Basque population born outside this region,<sup id="cite_ref-elmundo.es_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elmundo.es-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> immigration is crucial to Basque demographics. Over the 20th century most of this immigration came from other parts of Spain, typically from <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a> or <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">Castile and León</a>. Over recent years, sizeable numbers of this population have returned to their birthplaces and most immigration to the Basque country now comes from abroad, chiefly from South America.<sup id="cite_ref-elmundo.es_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elmundo.es-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> There are 151,519 foreigners in the Basque country, 7% of the population.
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism">Roman Catholicism</a> is, by far, the largest religion in the Basque Country. In 2012, the proportion of <a href="/wiki/Basques" title="Basques">Basques</a> that identified themselves as Roman Catholic was 58.6%,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> while it is one of the most secularised communities of Spain: 24.6% were non-religious and 12.3% of Basques were <a href="/wiki/Atheist" class="mw-redirect" title="Atheist">atheist</a>.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque language</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish language</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_Iberian_Peninsula_1030-es.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Map_Iberian_Peninsula_1030-es.svg/300px-Map_Iberian_Peninsula_1030-es.svg.png" decoding="async" width="300" height="208" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Map_Iberian_Peninsula_1030-es.svg/450px-Map_Iberian_Peninsula_1030-es.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Map_Iberian_Peninsula_1030-es.svg/600px-Map_Iberian_Peninsula_1030-es.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="827" data-file-height="574" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_Iberian_Peninsula_1030-es.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>"Spanish Kingdoms in 1030". The first written record in <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque</a> are in the <a href="/wiki/Glosas_Emilianenses" title="Glosas Emilianenses">Glosas Emilianenses</a>. The map shows the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Pamplona" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom of Pamplona">Kingdom of Pamplona</a> between 1029 and 1035</div></div></div>
<p>Spanish and Basque are co-official in all territories of the autonomous community. The Basque-speaking areas in the modern-day autonomous community are set against the wider context of the Basque language, spoken to the east in Navarre and the French Basque Country. The whole Basque speaking territory has experienced both decline and expansion in its history. The Basque language experienced a gradual territorial contraction throughout the last nine centuries,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and very severe deterioration of its sociolinguistic status for much of the 20th century due to heavy immigration from other parts of Spain, the virtual nonexistence of Basque language schooling, and national policies implemented by the different Spanish régimes (see <a href="/wiki/Language_policies_of_Francoist_Spain" title="Language policies of Francoist Spain">Language policies of Francoist Spain</a>). After the advent of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Autonomy_of_the_Basque_Country" title="Statute of Autonomy of the Basque Country">Statute of Autonomy of the Basque Country</a> in 1982 following Franco's death, this reductive trend was gradually reversed thanks to the Basque language schools and the new education system. Basque has always had a strong presence in most of <a href="/wiki/Gipuzkoa" title="Gipuzkoa">Gipuzkoa</a>, central and eastern <a href="/wiki/Biscay" title="Biscay">Biscay</a> and the northern edge of <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lava" title="Álava">Álava</a>, while most Basque speakers in western Biscay and the rest of Álava are second-language speakers.
</p><p>The 2006 sociolinguistic survey<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> of all Basque provinces showed that in 2006 of all people aged 16 and above in the Basque Autonomous Community, 30.1% were fluent Basque speakers, 18.3% passive speakers and 51.5% did not speak Basque. The percentage of Basque speakers was highest in Gipuzkoa (49.1% speakers) and lowest in Álava (14.2%). These results represent an increase on previous years (29.5% in 2001, 27.7% in 1996 and 24.1% in 1991). The highest percentage of speakers was now be found in the 16-24 age range (57.5%), while only 25.0% of those 65 and older reported speaking Basque.
</p><p>Ten years later, the sociolinguistic survey showed that in 2016 of all people aged 16 and above in the Basque Autonomous Community, 33.9% were fluent Basque speakers, 19.1% passive speakers and 47% did not speak Basque. The proportion of Basque speakers was again highest in Gipuzkoa (50.6%) and lowest in Álava (19.2%).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl><dd><i>This section is about the history of the Autonomous Community since 1978. See also <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Basque_people" class="mw-redirect" title="History of the Basque people">History of the Basque people</a></i></dd></dl>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Plaza_de_la_Virgen_Blanca_en_Vitoria-Gasteiz.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Plaza_de_la_Virgen_Blanca_en_Vitoria-Gasteiz.JPG/250px-Plaza_de_la_Virgen_Blanca_en_Vitoria-Gasteiz.JPG" decoding="async" width="250" height="163" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Plaza_de_la_Virgen_Blanca_en_Vitoria-Gasteiz.JPG/375px-Plaza_de_la_Virgen_Blanca_en_Vitoria-Gasteiz.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Plaza_de_la_Virgen_Blanca_en_Vitoria-Gasteiz.JPG/500px-Plaza_de_la_Virgen_Blanca_en_Vitoria-Gasteiz.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1265" data-file-height="826" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Plaza_de_la_Virgen_Blanca_en_Vitoria-Gasteiz.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Monument to the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Vitoria" title="Battle of Vitoria">Battle of Vitoria</a>, part of <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Independence_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Independence War">Spanish Independence War</a> against the French rule.</div></div></div>
<p>The forerunner of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Autonomy_of_the_Basque_Country" title="Statute of Autonomy of the Basque Country">Gernika Statute</a> was the short-lived Statute of Autonomy for Álava, Gipuzkoa and Biscay, which came to be enforced in October 1936 just in Biscay, with the Spanish Civil War already raging, and which was automatically abolished when the Spanish Nationalist troops occupied the territory.
</p><p>Before the Spanish Constitution of 1978 and its system of autonomous communities, these three provinces were known in Spanish as the <i>Provincias Vascongadas</i> <a href="/wiki/1833_territorial_division_of_Spain" title="1833 territorial division of Spain">since 1833</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The political structure of the new autonomous community is defined in the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_Autonomy_of_the_Basque_Country" title="Statute of Autonomy of the Basque Country">Gernika Statute</a>, which was approved by a majority in a <a href="/wiki/Referendum" title="Referendum">referendum</a> held on 25 October 1979. Nowadays it is one of the most decentralised regions in the world; in this regard it has been described as having "<i>more autonomy than just about any other in Europe</i>"<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Churruca_death.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Churruca_death.jpg/220px-Churruca_death.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="109" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Churruca_death.jpg/330px-Churruca_death.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Churruca_death.jpg/440px-Churruca_death.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2952" data-file-height="1462" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Churruca_death.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Churruca's death at the Battle of Trafalgar</i>. Basque navigators key for the navy of <a href="/wiki/Crown_of_Castile" title="Crown of Castile">Castile</a> and later the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Navy" title="Spanish Navy">Spanish Navy</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>As regards the bounds to the Spanish Constitution, <a href="/wiki/Basque_nationalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Basque nationalist">Basque nationalists</a> cite the fact that in the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Constitution_of_1978" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Constitution of 1978">1978 Spanish Constitution</a> referendum, which was passed with a majority of votes and a poor turnout in this area, the Basque Country had the highest <a href="/wiki/Abstention" title="Abstention">abstention</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> (the Basque Nationalist Party had endorsed abstention on the grounds that the Constitution was being forced upon them without any Basque input). To this, the "NO" vote in this referendum was also higher in the Basque Country than in the rest of the state. All in all, many Basques believe that they are not bound to a constitution that they never endorsed.
</p><p>The Statute of Autonomy of the Basque Country is an organic law but powers have been devolved gradually during decades according to re-negotiations between the Spanish and the consecutive Basque regional governments to reach an effective implementation, while the transfer of many powers are still due and has always been a matter of heated political discussion. Basque nationalists often put down this limitation in the devolution of powers to concessions made to appease the military involved in the <a href="/wiki/23-F" class="mw-redirect" title="23-F">23-F</a> coup d'état attempt (1981).
</p><p>In 2003, the governing <a href="/wiki/Basque_Nationalist_Party" title="Basque Nationalist Party">Basque Nationalist Party</a> (PNV) proposed to alter this statute through the <a href="/wiki/Ibarretxe_Plan" title="Ibarretxe Plan">Ibarretxe Plan</a>. The Ibarretxe bill was approved by absolute majority in the Basque Parliament after much discussion, as it was subject to lengthy legal objections&#8212;on the grounds that it contradicts the Spanish Constitution&#8212;that were ultimately overcome. Despite its mandate of the majority of the autonomous Parliament, the main two parties in Spain (PSOE, PP) imposed a blockade on a discussion of the Plan in the Spanish Parliament (<a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> <a href="/wiki/Cortes_Generales" title="Cortes Generales">Cortes Generales</a>), resulting in its rejection for debate by a large majority of that Parliament in January 2005.
</p><p>Since the first autonomic cabinet, the Basque Nationalist Party has held office in the Basque Autonomous Community except for a 2009-2012 term, led by Patxi López (PSE-PSOE). The current Basque prime minister is <a href="/wiki/I%C3%B1igo_Urkullu" title="Iñigo Urkullu">Iñigo Urkullu</a>, also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Nationalist_Party" title="Basque Nationalist Party">Basque Nationalist Party</a>. Despite <a href="/wiki/ETA_(separatist_group)" title="ETA (separatist group)">ETA</a>'s ceasefire in 2011, this autonomous community shows the highest rate of police per 100 inhabitants in Western Europe by 2018. As agreed with the Spanish premier <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Luis_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Zapatero" title="José Luis Rodríguez Zapatero">Zapatero</a> in 2004, Urkullu intends to increase the figure of <a href="/wiki/Ertzaintza" title="Ertzaintza">ertzainas</a>, while the Spanish <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Party_(Spain)" title="People&#39;s Party (Spain)">PP</a>'s Ministry of Interior rejects a pullback of Spanish police bodies, as demanded by the large majority of the political forces in the autonomous parliament, even pointing to an increase of the Guardia Civil in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Governmental_institutions">Governmental institutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Governmental institutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Parlamento_vasco_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Parlamento_vasco_1.jpg/200px-Parlamento_vasco_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Parlamento_vasco_1.jpg/300px-Parlamento_vasco_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Parlamento_vasco_1.jpg/400px-Parlamento_vasco_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Parlamento_vasco_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Basque parliament building in Vitoria-Gasteiz</div></div></div>
<p>The current laws configure the autonomous community as a federation of its present-day three constituent provinces. These western Basque districts kept governing themselves by their own laws and institutions even after the Castilian conquest in 1200. The new king upheld their institutional system issued from the <a href="/wiki/Custom_(law)" class="mw-redirect" title="Custom (law)">consuetudinary law</a> prevalent in Basque and Pyrenean territories. This limited self-government, similar to the one for <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a>, was partially suppressed in 1839 and totally in 1876 in exchange for an agreement on tax-collection and a number of administrative prerogatives. These in turn were <a class="external text" href="https://es.wikisource.org/wiki/Decreto-Ley_de_23_de_junio_de_1937">suspended by Franco</a> for Gipuzkoa and Biscay, but restored by the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Constitution_of_1978" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Constitution of 1978">Spanish Constitution of 1978</a>.
</p><p>The post-Franco Spanish Constitution of 1978 acknowledges historical rights and attempts a compromise in the old conflict between <a href="/wiki/Centralism" class="mw-redirect" title="Centralism">centralism</a> and the different national identities (Basque, Catalan, and Galician). A negotiation between <a href="/wiki/Union_of_the_Democratic_Centre_(Spain)" title="Union of the Democratic Centre (Spain)">UCD</a>'s Suárez in office and PNV led to the establishment of the Basque statute, with its first article stating that the <a href="/wiki/Basque_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Basque people">Basque people</a> (<i>Euskal Herria</i>) takes on an institutional personality in the form of the Basque Autonomous Community; the 2nd article goes on to establish that it may be constituted by Álava, Biscay, Gipuzkoa, as well as <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a> .<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Provincial councils provided with actual relevant attributions (taxation, etc.) were restored to these provinces (called therefore <i>diputación foral</i>), but Navarre <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Basque_people#The_present" class="mw-redirect" title="History of the Basque people">detached from the Basque political process</a>. A specific approach to the national realities in Spain was eventually diffused by a legal provision allowing for the establishment of <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain" title="Autonomous communities of Spain">autonomous administrations and parliaments</a> to any region in Spain (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">Castile and León</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Valencian_Community" title="Valencian Community">Valencian Community</a>, etc.), while the Basques, Catalans, and Galicians were acknowledged historic specificity. 
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vitoria_-_Ajuria_Enea_01.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Vitoria_-_Ajuria_Enea_01.jpg/200px-Vitoria_-_Ajuria_Enea_01.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="137" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Vitoria_-_Ajuria_Enea_01.jpg/300px-Vitoria_-_Ajuria_Enea_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Vitoria_-_Ajuria_Enea_01.jpg/400px-Vitoria_-_Ajuria_Enea_01.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4372" data-file-height="2993" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vitoria_-_Ajuria_Enea_01.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ajuria_Enea" title="Ajuria Enea">Ajuria Enea</a> Palace, official residence of the <a href="/wiki/Lehendakari" title="Lehendakari">Lehendakari</a> in Vitoria-Gasteiz</div></div></div> 
<p>The provinces in the Basque Country still perform tax collection in their respective territories, but with limited margin in decision making under the Spanish and European governments. Under this intricate system, the <i>Diputaciones Forales</i> (Basque: <i>Foru Aldundiak</i>) administer most of each of the provinces but are coordinated by the autonomous <a href="/wiki/Basque_Government" title="Basque Government">Basque Government</a> (Spanish <i>Gobierno Vasco</i>, Basque: <i>Eusko Jaurlaritza</i>). The autonomous community has its own police force (the <a href="/wiki/Ertzaintza" title="Ertzaintza">Ertzaintza</a>), controls <a href="/wiki/Basque_education_system" title="Basque education system">Education</a> and Health Systems, and has a <a href="/wiki/Euskal_Irrati_Telebista" class="mw-redirect" title="Euskal Irrati Telebista">Basque radio/TV station</a>. These and only some of the powers acknowledge in the Gernika Statute have since 1980 been transferred to the Autonomous Community by the <a href="/wiki/Cortes_Generales" title="Cortes Generales">Cortes Generales</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_gernika" class="mw-redirect" title="Statute of gernika">Gernika Statute</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The seats of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Parliament" title="Basque Parliament">Basque Parliament</a> and Government are in <a href="/wiki/Vitoria-Gasteiz" title="Vitoria-Gasteiz">Vitoria-Gasteiz</a>, so this is the capital city <i><a href="/wiki/De_facto" title="De facto">de facto</a></i>, but the Basque Autonomous Community has no capital <i><a href="/wiki/De_iure" class="mw-redirect" title="De iure">de iure</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GernikakoArbola-improved.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/GernikakoArbola-improved.jpg/150px-GernikakoArbola-improved.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/GernikakoArbola-improved.jpg/225px-GernikakoArbola-improved.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/GernikakoArbola-improved.jpg/300px-GernikakoArbola-improved.jpg 2x" data-file-width="749" data-file-height="1147" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GernikakoArbola-improved.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The historic <a href="/wiki/Gernikako_arbola" class="mw-redirect" title="Gernikako arbola">Oak of Gernika</a>, symbol of the Basque institutions.</div></div></div>
<p>The Parliament is composed of 25 representatives from each of the three provinces. The Basque Parliament elects the <a href="/wiki/Lehendakari" title="Lehendakari">Lehendakari</a> (President of the Autonomous Community of the Basque Country) who forms a government following regular parliamentary procedures. Until 2009 all <i>Lehendakaris</i> (even those in 1937 and during the exile) have been members of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Nationalist_Party" title="Basque Nationalist Party">Basque Nationalist Party</a> (Euzko Alderdi Jeltzalea) (moderate and <a href="/wiki/Christian-Democrat" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian-Democrat">Christian-Democrat</a>) since 1978. Despite their continued leadership role, they have not always enjoyed majorities for their party and have needed to form coalitions with either Spain wide parties or left-leaning Basque nationalist parties, often governing in a difficult minority position.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Since 1982 until the late 1990s, <a href="/wiki/Batasuna" title="Batasuna">Basque nationalists</a> ideologically closer to <a href="/wiki/ETA_(separatist_group)" title="ETA (separatist group)">ETA</a> refused to turn out in the Basque parliament, a significant wedge of the parliament. Currently, the Basque Government is headed by <a href="/wiki/I%C3%B1igo_Urkullu" title="Iñigo Urkullu">Iñigo Urkullu</a> (<a href="/wiki/Basque_Nationalist_Party" title="Basque Nationalist Party">PNV</a>).
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:BasqueParliamentDiagram2016.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/BasqueParliamentDiagram2016.svg/220px-BasqueParliamentDiagram2016.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="113" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/BasqueParliamentDiagram2016.svg/330px-BasqueParliamentDiagram2016.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/BasqueParliamentDiagram2016.svg/440px-BasqueParliamentDiagram2016.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="360" data-file-height="185" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:BasqueParliamentDiagram2016.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Summary of the 25 September 2016 <a href="/wiki/Basque_Parliament" title="Basque Parliament">Basque Parliament</a> election results <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Present-day_political_dynamics">Present-day political dynamics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Present-day political dynamics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>ETA's permanent ceasefire (2010-2011) opened the possibility of new governmental alliances and has enabled EH Bildu's electoral success and rise to governmental institutions (<a href="/wiki/Gipuzkoa" title="Gipuzkoa">Gipuzkoa</a>, and capital city <a href="/wiki/Donostia" class="mw-redirect" title="Donostia">Donostia</a>, 2011-2015). In the <a href="/wiki/2012_Basque_parliamentary_election" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Basque parliamentary election">2012 Basque parliamentary election</a>, the PNV obtained a plurality of the votes, followed by the left-wing nationalist coalition <a href="/wiki/EH_Bildu" title="EH Bildu">EH Bildu</a> (<a href="/wiki/Eusko_Alkartasuna" title="Eusko Alkartasuna">Eusko Alkartasuna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sortu" title="Sortu">Sortu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alternatiba,_Basque_political_party" class="mw-redirect" title="Alternatiba, Basque political party">Alternatiba</a>).
</p><p>In 2016 the Basque regional election was held on 25 September to elect the 11th Parliament of the Basque Autonomous Community, which left a <a href="/wiki/Hung_parliament" title="Hung parliament">hung parliament</a>, with the combination of Basque nationalist parties (PNV and <a href="/wiki/EH_Bildu" title="EH Bildu">EH Bildu</a>) representing the largest wedge in the parliament of the Basque Autonomous Community, the main Spanish parties PP and PSOE's branches occupying a 24% of the seats, and <a href="/wiki/Podemos_(Spain)" class="mw-redirect" title="Podemos (Spain)">Podemos - Ahal Dugu</a> accounting for 11 seats (14,66% of the total). However, the leading party PNV renewed its traditional alliance with the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Party_of_the_Basque_Country%E2%80%93Basque_Country_Left" title="Socialist Party of the Basque Country–Basque Country Left">PSE</a> to form government.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/2017_Catalan_independence_referendum" title="2017 Catalan independence referendum">2017 Catalan referendum</a> crisis, the parliament showed its sympathy and support to the Catalan independence referendum and lashed out at the Spanish government's stance on the issue, denouncing any measures it may take against the vote or 'democracy' altogether.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Besides supporting the vote, Basques of this community showed a preference for further self-government (43,5%), with independence and present-day <i>status quo</i> ranking as second and third options (22,6% and 18,9%).<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, the parliament of the autonomous community passed a Police Abuses Act spanning the period between 1978 and 1999; it was shortly after challenged by the public prosecutor and appealed also by virtually all police and Civil Guard unions. Incoming Spanish premier <a href="/wiki/Pedro_S%C3%A1nchez_(politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pedro Sánchez (politician)">Pedro Sanchez</a> agreed to lift the public prosecutor's block on the law in exchange for altering its content.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Territorial_issues">Territorial issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Territorial issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The statute, insofar as it is addressed and provides an administrative framework for the Basque people, provides the mechanisms for neighbouring Navarre to join the three western provinces if it wishes to do so, since at least part of it is ethnically Basque. The Basque Government used the "<a href="/wiki/Laurak_Bat" class="mw-redirect" title="Laurak Bat">Laurak Bat</a>", which included the arms of Navarre, as its symbol for many years. The Navarrese Government protested, and tribunals ruled in their favour. The Basque Government replaced it with an empty red field.
</p><p>Navarre is one of the historical Basque territories and even claimed by the <a href="/wiki/Basque_nationalism" title="Basque nationalism">Basque nationalists</a> as the core of the Basque nation. There are also two <a href="/wiki/Enclave" class="mw-redirect" title="Enclave">enclaves</a> surrounded by Basque territory—<a href="/wiki/Enclave_of_Trevi%C3%B1o" class="mw-redirect" title="Enclave of Treviño">Treviño</a> (Basque: <i>Trebiñu</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Valle_de_Villaverde" title="Valle de Villaverde">Valle de Villaverde</a> (Basque: <i>Villaverde-Turtzioz</i>)—which belong to the fellow neighbouring communities of <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and León">Castile and León</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cantabria" title="Cantabria">Cantabria</a> respectively, for which a legal connection to the Basque Country has become an on-off matter of political discussion.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bilbao_-_Torre_Iberdrola_07.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Bilbao_-_Torre_Iberdrola_07.JPG/220px-Bilbao_-_Torre_Iberdrola_07.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Bilbao_-_Torre_Iberdrola_07.JPG/330px-Bilbao_-_Torre_Iberdrola_07.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Bilbao_-_Torre_Iberdrola_07.JPG/440px-Bilbao_-_Torre_Iberdrola_07.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="1600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bilbao_-_Torre_Iberdrola_07.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Iberdrola_Tower" title="Iberdrola Tower">Iberdrola Tower</a>, headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Iberdrola" title="Iberdrola">Iberdrola</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Bilbao" title="Bilbao">Bilbao</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The Basque Autonomous Community <a href="/wiki/Ranked_lists_of_Spanish_autonomous_communities#GRP_(PPP)" title="Ranked lists of Spanish autonomous communities">ranks first in Spain in terms of per capita income</a>, with a <a href="/wiki/Nominal_gross_domestic_product" class="mw-redirect" title="Nominal gross domestic product">nominal gross domestic product</a> (GDP) per capita (adjusted to purchasing power parity, PPP) being 22% higher than that of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> and 30% higher than Spain's average in 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and at €34,400 in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Industrial activities were traditionally centred on steel and shipbuilding, mainly due to the rich <a href="/wiki/Iron_ore" title="Iron ore">iron ore</a> resources found during the 19th century around Bilbao. The Estuary of Bilbao was the centre of Euskadi's <a href="/wiki/Industrial_revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial revolution">industrial revolution</a> during the 19th and the first half of the 20th century. These activities decayed during the <a href="/wiki/Economic_crisis" class="mw-redirect" title="Economic crisis">economic crisis</a> of the 1970s and 1980s, giving ground for the development of the <a href="/wiki/Tertiary_sector" class="mw-redirect" title="Tertiary sector">services sector</a> and new technologies.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Edinburgh_tram,_5_August_2014_(1).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Edinburgh_tram%2C_5_August_2014_%281%29.jpg/220px-Edinburgh_tram%2C_5_August_2014_%281%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Edinburgh_tram%2C_5_August_2014_%281%29.jpg/330px-Edinburgh_tram%2C_5_August_2014_%281%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Edinburgh_tram%2C_5_August_2014_%281%29.jpg/440px-Edinburgh_tram%2C_5_August_2014_%281%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="2248" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Edinburgh_tram,_5_August_2014_(1).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> tram car assembled in <a href="/wiki/Construcciones_y_Auxiliar_de_Ferrocarriles" title="Construcciones y Auxiliar de Ferrocarriles">CAF</a> <a href="/wiki/Beasain" title="Beasain">Beasain</a> (Gipuzkoa)</div></div></div>
<p>Today, the strongest industrial sectors of the Basque Country's economy are <a href="/wiki/Machine_tool" title="Machine tool">machine tool</a>, present in the valleys of Biscay and Gipuzkoa; <a href="/wiki/Aeronautics" title="Aeronautics">aeronautics</a> in Vitoria-Gasteiz; and <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a>, in Bilbao.
</p><p>The main companies in the Basque Country are: <a href="/wiki/BBVA" class="mw-redirect" title="BBVA">BBVA</a> bank, <a href="/wiki/Iberdrola" title="Iberdrola">Iberdrola</a> energy company (both of them have their headquarters in Bilbao), <a href="/wiki/Mondrag%C3%B3n_Cooperative_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Mondragón Cooperative Corporation">Mondragón Cooperative Corporation</a>&#8212;MCC, the largest cooperative in the world&#8212;<a href="/wiki/Gamesa_Corporaci%C3%B3n_Tecnol%C3%B3gica" class="mw-redirect" title="Gamesa Corporación Tecnológica">Gamesa</a> <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbine" title="Wind turbine">wind turbine</a> producer and <a href="/wiki/Construcciones_y_Auxiliar_de_Ferrocarriles" title="Construcciones y Auxiliar de Ferrocarriles">CAF</a> <a href="/wiki/Rolling_stock" title="Rolling stock">rolling stock</a> producer. MCC's business leadership in the Basque Country hangs in the balance after <a href="/wiki/Fagor" title="Fagor">Fagor</a>, its flagship cooperative of household appliances and goods manufacturing, declared bankruptcy in 2014.
</p><p>Eight out of ten Spanish municipalities with the lowest unemployment rates were found across this autonomous community in 2015, highlighting such towns as <a href="/wiki/Arrasate" class="mw-redirect" title="Arrasate">Arrasate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugalete" title="Portugalete">Portugalete</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barakaldo" title="Barakaldo">Barakaldo</a> with a strong manufacturing industrial make-up.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The Basque Autonomous Community ranked above other communities in Spain in terms of resilience in the face of the economic crisis, going on to become a beacon and a subject of study in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013 the Basque Country outperformed Spain in several parameters, but economic overall figures were highly variable. Spanish figures are subject to conspicuous seasonal fluctuation, relying on its tourist and services sectors, while Basque performance is rather based on mid- and long-term results, according with its more industrial focus. In the last quarter of 2017, unemployment in this autonomous community rose to 11.1%<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> (8.43% in <a href="/wiki/Gipuzkoa" title="Gipuzkoa">Gipuzkoa</a>), second lowest in Spain after <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a>, at a percentage slightly higher than the EU average (10.8%),<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> but still ahead of the Spanish overall unemployment rate of around 16.55%,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> the second highest in the EU.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In regards to GDP performance, 2017 was a remarkably positive year for the Basque Autonomous Community. It underwent an increase in GDP of 3.0%, close to the Spanish increase, 3.1%. In the last term of 2013, the public debt of the Basque Autonomous Community stood at 13.00% of its GDP, totalling 3,753 € per capita,<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> as compared to Spain's overall 93.90%, totalling 20,383 € per capita.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Basque Government's high-ranking officials, as well as Basque-based party leaders and personalities, have protested and voiced their concern over the detrimental effects of austerity measures passed by the Spanish Government as of 2011, overruling Basque taxation powers, may be having on industry and trade, especially export. Basque officials have strongly advocated for participation, along with Navarre, in the <a href="/wiki/Ecofin" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecofin">Ecofin</a>, with a full membership, in order to defend Basque interests in line with Basque reality and fiscal status, and not as a Spanish subsidiary.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Puente_Txonta.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Puente_Txonta.jpg/220px-Puente_Txonta.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="52" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Puente_Txonta.jpg/330px-Puente_Txonta.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Puente_Txonta.jpg/440px-Puente_Txonta.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5960" data-file-height="1413" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Puente_Txonta.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>AP-8 in <a href="/wiki/Eibar" title="Eibar">Eibar</a></div></div></div>
<p>The strategic geographical location of the Basque Country as a link between the northwest and centre of Spain and the rest of Europe makes this territory heavily transited.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Road">Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The main backbones of road transport are the <a href="/wiki/Autopista_AP-8" title="Autopista AP-8">AP-8 motorway</a> which links Bilbao, San Sebastián and the French border and the <a href="/wiki/Autov%C3%ADa_A-1" title="Autovía A-1">A-1 motorway</a> which links San Sebastián and Vitoria-Gasteiz with central Spain. Other important routes include the <a href="/wiki/Autopista_AP-68" title="Autopista AP-68">AP-68 motorway</a> which links Bilbao with the Mediterranean.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eusko_Trenbide_Sarea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Eusko Trenbide Sarea (page does not exist)">Eusko Trenbide Sarea</a> (<i>Basque Railway Network</i>) is the Basque Government-owned company that maintains and creates the railway infrastructure in the autonomous region. <a href="/wiki/EuskoTren" class="mw-redirect" title="EuskoTren">EuskoTren</a> (<i>Basque Train</i>) is the Basque Government-owned <a href="/wiki/Narrow_gauge" class="mw-redirect" title="Narrow gauge">narrow gauge</a> rail company that operates commuter services in Bilbao and San Sebastián, intercity Bilbao-San Sebastián service, and <a href="/wiki/EuskoTran" class="mw-redirect" title="EuskoTran">EuskoTran</a> tram services in Bilbao and Vitoria-Gasteiz.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Metro_Bilbao" title="Metro Bilbao">Metro Bilbao</a> operates two <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">metro</a> lines that serve the Greater Bilbao area while EuskoTren operates a third which opened in 2017. EuskoTren operates a metro service in the San Sebastián area, known as <a href="/wiki/Metro_Donostialdea" title="Metro Donostialdea">Metro Donostialdea</a>.
</p><p>The Spanish government owns two main <a href="/wiki/RENFE" class="mw-redirect" title="RENFE">RENFE</a> <a href="/wiki/Iberian-gauge_railways" title="Iberian-gauge railways">broad gauge</a> lines that link Vitoria-Gasteiz with San Sebastián and Bilbao with central Spain. It also operates <a href="/wiki/Cercan%C3%ADas" title="Cercanías">Cercanías</a> commuter lines in both Bilbao and San Sebastián.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Euskal_Y.PNG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Euskal_Y.PNG/220px-Euskal_Y.PNG" decoding="async" width="220" height="159" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Euskal_Y.PNG/330px-Euskal_Y.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Euskal_Y.PNG/440px-Euskal_Y.PNG 2x" data-file-width="569" data-file-height="411" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Euskal_Y.PNG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Basque Y</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/FEVE" class="mw-redirect" title="FEVE">FEVE</a> narrow gauge rail company operates a commuter line between Bilbao and <a href="/wiki/Balmaseda" title="Balmaseda">Balmaseda</a> and links Bilbao with the rest of northern Spain.
</p><p>A new <a href="/wiki/High-speed_rail" title="High-speed rail">high-speed</a> network (called <a href="/wiki/Basque_Y" title="Basque Y">Basque Y</a>) currently under construction will link the three capitals in 'Y' formation. Because of the rough geography of the territory, most of the network will run through tunnels, with a total estimated cost of up to €10 billion.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The estimated ecological impact of the project has encouraged the formation of a group campaigning against it called <i>AHTrik Ez Elkarlana</i>. The group uses social disobedience to oppose the project and promotes referendums against it in the towns it most affects. In spite of the vocal opposition to the project by this and other community groups (as well as <a href="/wiki/EH_Bildu" title="EH Bildu">EH Bildu</a>), work continues, not without uncertainty. In early 2015, an estimate suggested that the average Basque intercity fare would rise to a non-competitive 25 €, while the Spanish central government's funding has been subject to continuous delays, spurring the irritation of the Basque government in Vitoria-Gasteiz.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Airports">Airports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Airports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aeropuerto_Bilbao_Loiu_01.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Aeropuerto_Bilbao_Loiu_01.jpg/220px-Aeropuerto_Bilbao_Loiu_01.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Aeropuerto_Bilbao_Loiu_01.jpg/330px-Aeropuerto_Bilbao_Loiu_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Aeropuerto_Bilbao_Loiu_01.jpg/440px-Aeropuerto_Bilbao_Loiu_01.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3628" data-file-height="2721" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aeropuerto_Bilbao_Loiu_01.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bilbao Airport</div></div></div>
<p>The three capitals have airports:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Bilbao_Airport" title="Bilbao Airport">Bilbao Airport</a> (BIO) International</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vitoria_Airport" title="Vitoria Airport">Vitoria Airport</a> (VIT)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Airport" title="San Sebastián Airport">San Sebastián Airport</a> (EAS)</li></ul>
<p>Of the three, the most important hub and entry point to the Basque Country is Bilbao Airport, offering many international connections. Nearly 4,600,000 passengers passed through it in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Seaports">Seaports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Seaports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The two most important ports are the <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Bilbao" title="Port of Bilbao">Port of Bilbao</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pasaia" title="Pasaia">Port of Pasaia</a>. There are also minor fishing ports, such as <a href="/wiki/Bermeo" title="Bermeo">Bermeo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ondarroa" title="Ondarroa">Ondarroa</a>.
</p><p>The Port of Bilbao is by far the most important in the Basque Country and the north of Spain, being the fourth most important in Spain with over 38 million tons of traffic.
</p><p>All cruising routes arrive in Bilbao and there is a <a href="/wiki/Ferry" title="Ferry">ferry</a> service linking Bilbao with <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth" title="Portsmouth">Portsmouth</a> (United Kingdom).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Cuisine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Basque_cuisine" title="Basque cuisine">Basque cuisine</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pinchos_txaka_bonito.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Pinchos_txaka_bonito.jpg/220px-Pinchos_txaka_bonito.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="153" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Pinchos_txaka_bonito.jpg/330px-Pinchos_txaka_bonito.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Pinchos_txaka_bonito.jpg/440px-Pinchos_txaka_bonito.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2000" data-file-height="1394" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pinchos_txaka_bonito.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Two sample pintxos</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Basque_cuisine" title="Basque cuisine">Basque cuisine</a> is an important part of <a href="/wiki/Basque_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="Basque culture">Basque culture</a>. According to the chef <a href="/wiki/Ferran_Adri%C3%A0" title="Ferran Adrià">Ferran Adrià</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n" title="San Sebastián">San Sebastián</a> "in terms of the average quality of the food, in terms of what you can get at any place you happen to walk into, maybe it is—probably it is, yes—the best in the world."<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The most popular dishes are seafood, fish (for example <a href="/wiki/Marmitako" class="mw-redirect" title="Marmitako">Marmitako</a>) and "<a href="/wiki/Pintxo" class="mw-redirect" title="Pintxo">Pintxos</a>", bar finger food.
</p><p>During the 1970s, several chefs from the Basque Country, particularly <a href="/wiki/Juan_Mari_Arzak" title="Juan Mari Arzak">Juan Mari Arzak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Subijana" title="Pedro Subijana">Pedro Subijana</a>, led a gastronomic revolution, translating to Spain the principles of French nouvelle cuisine. The first Spanish restaurant to be awarded 3 stars in the Michelin Guide was, in fact, <i>Zalacaín</i>, a Basque restaurant, although located in Madrid. Today, the Basque Country, alongside Catalonia, is the Spanish region with a higher density of stars in the Michelin Guide, and it has become a preferred destination of many gastronomic tourists, both domestic and international. Four restaurants boast 3 stars, the highest possible award: Juan María Arzak (<i>Arzak</i> restaurant), <a href="/wiki/Mart%C3%ADn_Berasategui" title="Martín Berasategui">Martín Berasategui</a> (<i>Berasategui</i> restaurant), Pedro Subijana (chef of <i>Akelarre</i>) and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eneko_Atxa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Eneko Atxa (page does not exist)">Eneko Atxa</a> (<i>Azurmendi</i> restaurant). In the new generation of chefs, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Andoni_Luis_Aduriz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Andoni Luis Aduriz (page does not exist)">Andoni Luis Aduriz</a>, <i>Mugaritz</i> restaurant, is outstanding.
</p><p>The coastal city of <a href="/wiki/San_Sebasti%C3%A1n" title="San Sebastián">San Sebastián</a> is home to the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Culinary_Center" title="Basque Culinary Center">Basque Culinary Center</a>, an academic research institution focused on higher education and research in the areas of gastronomy and nutrition.
</p><p>Basque food is one of the reasons for <a href="/wiki/Tourism_in_the_Basque_Country" class="mw-redirect" title="Tourism in the Basque Country">tourism to the Basque Country</a>, especially the <i>pintxos</i>. A popular way to socialise is "<i>ir de pintxos</i>" or <i>txikiteo</i>, a Basque version of a <a href="/wiki/Pub_crawl" title="Pub crawl">pub crawl</a>, albeit generally more civilised.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sara_JaI_Alai_Lapurdi.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Sara_JaI_Alai_Lapurdi.JPG/220px-Sara_JaI_Alai_Lapurdi.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Sara_JaI_Alai_Lapurdi.JPG/330px-Sara_JaI_Alai_Lapurdi.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Sara_JaI_Alai_Lapurdi.JPG/440px-Sara_JaI_Alai_Lapurdi.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2272" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sara_JaI_Alai_Lapurdi.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Basque_pelota" title="Basque pelota">Pelota</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jai_alai" title="Jai alai">jai alai</a>) court in Sara, Lapurdi</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Basque_rural_sports" title="Basque rural sports">Basque rural sports</a>, known as <i>Herri Kirolak</i> in <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque</a>, are a number of sports competitions rooted in the traditional lifestyles of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Basque people">Basque people</a>, for example <a href="/wiki/Basque_pelota" title="Basque pelota">Basque pelota</a>, the Basque version of the European game family that includes <a href="/wiki/Real_tennis" title="Real tennis">real tennis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Squash_(sport)" title="Squash (sport)">squash</a>. Basque players, playing for either the Spanish or the French teams, dominate international competitions.
</p><p>The Basque country is also home to former national football <a href="/wiki/List_of_Spanish_football_champions" title="List of Spanish football champions">champions</a> <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao" title="Athletic Bilbao">Athletic Club</a> from Bilbao. It has a strict <i><a href="/wiki/Cantera" title="Cantera">Cantera</a></i> policy of <a href="/wiki/Athletic_Bilbao_signing_policy" title="Athletic Bilbao signing policy">employing only players born or trained</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(greater_region)" title="Basque Country (greater region)">Basque Country (greater region)</a>. Athletic's policy does not apply to head coaches, with famous names as <a href="/wiki/Howard_Kendall" title="Howard Kendall">Howard Kendall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jupp_Heynckes" title="Jupp Heynckes">Jupp Heynckes</a> coaching the team at various points. In spite of this, the club shares with worldwide heavyweights <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." class="mw-redirect" title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a> and <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a> the distinction of never being relegated from the top flight.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Another major Basque Country club is <a href="/wiki/Real_Sociedad" title="Real Sociedad">Real Sociedad</a> from San Sebastián, who contest the <a href="/wiki/Basque_derby" title="Basque derby">Basque derby</a> with Athletic. Real Sociedad used to practise the same policy, until they signed Irish <a href="/wiki/Striker_(football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Striker (football)">striker</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Aldridge" title="John Aldridge">John Aldridge</a> in the late 1980s. Since then, Real Sociedad have had many foreign players. <a href="/wiki/Xabi_Alonso" title="Xabi Alonso">Xabi Alonso</a> became the only Basque player to win both the World Cup and the club <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">European Cup</a> and he played in the <a href="/wiki/Real_Sociedad" title="Real Sociedad">Real Sociedad</a>.  The region is also home to other <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> clubs <a href="/wiki/SD_Eibar" title="SD Eibar">SD Eibar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Deportivo_Alav%C3%A9s" title="Deportivo Alavés">Deportivo Alavés</a>.
</p><p>The most renowned Basque footballer of all time is possibly <a href="/wiki/Andoni_Zubizarreta" title="Andoni Zubizarreta">Andoni Zubizarreta</a> who holds the record for appearances in <a href="/wiki/La_Liga" title="La Liga">La Liga</a> with 622 games and has won six league titles and the <a href="/wiki/European_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="European Cup">European Cup</a>. Nowadays, the best known Basque football player is Xabi Alonso, winner of two UEFA European Championships and one <a href="/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup" title="FIFA World Cup">World Cup</a>, who played for Real Sociedad, Liverpool, Real Madrid and <a href="/wiki/Bayern_Munich" class="mw-redirect" title="Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>, but retired in 2017. Other notable Basque players include <a href="/wiki/Mikel_Arteta" title="Mikel Arteta">Mikel Arteta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asier_Illarramendi" title="Asier Illarramendi">Asier Illarramendi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andoni_Iraola" title="Andoni Iraola">Andoni Iraola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aritz_Aduriz" title="Aritz Aduriz">Aritz Aduriz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Xabi_Prieto" title="Xabi Prieto">Xabi Prieto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ander_Herrera" title="Ander Herrera">Ander Herrera</a>. Both Athletic and Real Sociedad have won the Spanish league, including dominating the competition in the early 1980s, with the last title won by a Basque club being Athletic's <a href="/wiki/1983%E2%80%9384_La_Liga" title="1983–84 La Liga">1984 title</a>.
</p><p>At international level, Basque players were especially prominent in <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spanish selections</a> prior to the Civil War, with all of those at the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1928_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_team_squads#Spain" title="Football at the 1928 Summer Olympics – Men&#39;s team squads">1928 Olympics</a>, and the majority of the <a href="/wiki/Football_at_the_1920_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_team_squads#Spain" title="Football at the 1920 Summer Olympics – Men&#39;s team squads">1920 Olympics</a> and <a href="/wiki/1934_FIFA_World_Cup_squads#Spain" title="1934 FIFA World Cup squads">1934 World Cup</a> squads, born in the region. There is an <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_national_football_team" title="Basque Country national football team">unofficial 'national' team</a> which plays occasional friendlies, however its squads pick players from the wider territory including Navarre and the French Basque Country.
</p><p>Cycling as a sport is popular in the Basque Country. <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Olano" title="Abraham Olano">Abraham Olano</a> has won the <a href="/wiki/Vuelta_a_Espa%C3%B1a" title="Vuelta a España">Vuelta a España</a> and the <a href="/wiki/UCI_Road_World_Championships" title="UCI Road World Championships">World Championship</a>. The <a href="/wiki/UCI_World_Tour" title="UCI World Tour">UCI World Tour</a> <a href="/wiki/Movistar_Team_(men%27s_team)" title="Movistar Team (men&#39;s team)">Movistar Team</a> hails from the Basque Country.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The Caisse d'Épargne cycling team traces its history back to the <b>Banesto</b> team that included <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Indur%C3%A1in" title="Miguel Induráin">Miguel Induráin</a>. The region is home to the <a href="/wiki/Tour_of_the_Basque_Country" title="Tour of the Basque Country">Tour of the Basque Country</a> stage race and the <a href="/wiki/Clasica_de_San_Sebastian" class="mw-redirect" title="Clasica de San Sebastian">Clasica de San Sebastian</a> one day race. The <a href="/wiki/Euskaltel%E2%80%93Euskadi" title="Euskaltel–Euskadi">Euskaltel–Euskadi</a> team was also part of the World Tour until its disbandment in 2014. It was an unofficial Basque national team and was partly funded by the Basque Government. Its riders were either Basque, or at least grown up in the Basque cycling culture, and former members of the team have been strong contenders in the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">Tour de France</a> held annually in July and <a href="/wiki/Vuelta_a_Espa%C3%B1a" title="Vuelta a España">Vuelta a España</a> held in September. Team leaders have included riders such as <a href="/wiki/Iban_Mayo" title="Iban Mayo">Iban Mayo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haimar_Zubeldia" title="Haimar Zubeldia">Haimar Zubeldia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samuel_S%C3%A1nchez" title="Samuel Sánchez">Samuel Sánchez</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Etxebarria" title="David Etxebarria">David Etxebarria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Igor_Ant%C3%B3n" title="Igor Antón">Igor Antón</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mikel_Landa" title="Mikel Landa">Mikel Landa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mikel_Nieve" title="Mikel Nieve">Mikel Nieve</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some notable Basque people from this administrative jurisdiction include <a href="/wiki/Francisco_de_Vitoria" title="Francisco de Vitoria">Francisco de Vitoria</a>, philosopher who set the theories of just war, international law and freedom of commerce; <a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano" title="Juan Sebastián Elcano">Juan Sebastián Elcano</a>, completed first circumnavigation of the Earth; <a href="/wiki/Ignatius_of_Loyola" title="Ignatius of Loyola">Ignatius of Loyola</a>, founder of the Jesuits; <a href="/wiki/Don_Juan_de_O%C3%B1ate" class="mw-redirect" title="Don Juan de Oñate">Don Juan de Oñate</a>, explorer of the great plains and Colorado river; <a href="/wiki/Blas_de_Lezo" title="Blas de Lezo">Blas de Lezo</a>, naval strategist, best remembered for his defensive tactics at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cartagena_de_Indias" title="Battle of Cartagena de Indias">Battle of Cartagena de Indias</a>; <a href="/wiki/Jorge_Oteiza" title="Jorge Oteiza">Jorge Oteiza</a> sculptor; <a href="/wiki/Paco_Rabanne" title="Paco Rabanne">Paco Rabanne</a>, fashion designer; <a href="/wiki/Crist%C3%B3bal_Balenciaga" title="Cristóbal Balenciaga">Cristóbal Balenciaga</a>, fashion designer; <a href="/wiki/Xabi_Alonso" title="Xabi Alonso">Xabi Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mikel_Arteta" title="Mikel Arteta">Mikel Arteta</a>, footballers; <a href="/wiki/Edurne_Pasaban" title="Edurne Pasaban">Edurne Pasaban</a>, first woman to climb all of the fourteen eight-thousander peaks in the World; <a href="/wiki/Elena_Arzak" title="Elena Arzak">Elena Arzak</a>, best female chef in the world (2012); <a href="/wiki/Jon_Kortajarena" title="Jon Kortajarena">Jon Kortajarena</a> male model; <a href="/wiki/Jose-Maria_Cundin" title="Jose-Maria Cundin">Jose-Maria Cundin</a>, artist; <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Savater" title="Fernando Savater">Fernando Savater</a> philosopher; <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_Unamuno" title="Miguel de Unamuno">Miguel de Unamuno</a>, essayist, novelist, poet, playwright, philosopher.
</p>
