<p><b>Trevor Ó Clochartaigh</b> (<small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Irish" title="Help:IPA/Irish">[ˈtrɛvər oː ˈklˠɔxaɾˠt̪ˠiː]</a></span> born 14 March 1968) is a former Irish politician who has served as a <a href="/wiki/Seanad_%C3%89ireann" title="Seanad Éireann">Senator</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_Panel" title="Agricultural Panel">Agricultural Panel</a> from 2011 to 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-oireachtas_db_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oireachtas_db-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ó Clochartaigh was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, England.<sup id="cite_ref-nealons_guide_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nealons_guide-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His parents came from the <a href="/wiki/Connemara" title="Connemara">Connemara</a> area of <a href="/wiki/County_Galway" title="County Galway">County Galway</a>. He attended <a href="/wiki/NUI_Galway" title="NUI Galway">NUI Galway</a>, graduating with a Commerce degree. He worked in <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish language</a> theatre, and is a former manager and artistic director of <a href="/wiki/Taibhdhearc_na_Gaillimhe" title="Taibhdhearc na Gaillimhe">An Taiḃḋearc</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nealons_guide_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nealons_guide-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He also worked in television production as producer of <i><a href="/wiki/Fair_City" title="Fair City">Fair City</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ros_na_R%C3%BAn" title="Ros na Rún">Ros na Rún</a></i>, and in community development projects.<sup id="cite_ref-cupan_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cupan-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He first entered politics as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(Ireland)" title="Labour Party (Ireland)">Labour Party</a>, unsuccessfully contesting the Connemara area of <a href="/wiki/Galway_County_Council" title="Galway County Council">Galway County Council</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Irish_local_elections" title="2004 Irish local elections">2004 local elections</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-electionsireland_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-electionsireland-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He subsequently left Labour and joined Sinn Féin, and was the party's candidate at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Irish_local_elections" title="2009 Irish local elections">2009 local elections</a> and for <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A1il_%C3%89ireann" title="Dáil Éireann">Dáil Éireann</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Galway_West_(D%C3%A1il_constituency)" title="Galway West (Dáil constituency)">Galway West</a> constituency at the <a href="/wiki/2011_Irish_general_election" title="2011 Irish general election">2011 general election</a>, but was not elected.<sup id="cite_ref-cupan_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cupan-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-electionsireland_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-electionsireland-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2011, he was elected to <a href="/wiki/Members_of_the_24th_Seanad" title="Members of the 24th Seanad">Seanad Éireann</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_Panel" title="Agricultural Panel">Agricultural Panel</a> <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and was re-elected in April 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2017, he left <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn Féin">Sinn Féin</a>, citing "serious disciplinary issues" within the party.<sup id="cite_ref-Journal_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Journal-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> However Sinn Féin claim that Trevor left the party because "he was worried that a democratic vote of party members in the constituency would not select him to contest the election" and that just days before his leaving he had been advocating that two candidates instead of just one should be nominated at the Party's local selection convention.<sup id="cite_ref-Sinn_Féin.ie_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sinn_Féin.ie-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He resigned from <a href="/wiki/Seanad_%C3%89ireann" title="Seanad Éireann">Seanad Éireann</a> on 5 February 2018, to take up a new role at the television station <a href="/wiki/TG4" title="TG4">TG4</a>.
</p>
