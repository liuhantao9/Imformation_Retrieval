	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Ochoa</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="box-Unreferenced plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This article <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ochoa&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">August 2018</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table><p><b>Ochoa</b> (<a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque</a>: <i lang="eu">Otxoa</i>) is a Spanish<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> surname of Basque origin common throughout <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. It is a surname of <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> origin; it was originally a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spain_in_the_Middle_Ages" title="Spain in the Middle Ages">Medieval Spain</a>.
</p><p>The name originated in the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(greater_region)" title="Basque Country (greater region)">Basque Country</a> and means "the <a href="/wiki/Wolf" title="Wolf">wolf</a>", from the Basque vocabulary word <i>otso</i>/<i>otxo</i> meaning "wolf" (the suffix -a in the <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque language</a> represents the definite article). In <a href="/wiki/Standard_Basque" title="Standard Basque">Standard Basque</a>, the name is spelled <i>otsoa</i> or <i>otxoa</i>. There was also a female given name <i>Ochanda</i> (meaning "female wolf", cf. the elegant tower in the old quarter of <a href="/wiki/Vitoria-Gasteiz" title="Vitoria-Gasteiz">Vitoria-Gasteiz</a> named after Ochanda, proper name of the daughter of a man responsible for revamping the tower in the 16th century) and <i>Ochotorena </i>or<i> Otxotorena</i>, meaning "son of Ochoto" (literally "small wolf"). The Spanish equivalent of this Basque given name was <b><a href="/wiki/Lope_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Lope (disambiguation)">Lope</a></b>, also appearing in the names of <a href="/wiki/Gascony" title="Gascony">Gascon</a> lords in the High Middle Ages.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_distribution">Geographical distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ochoa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geographical distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2014, 32.2% of all known bearers of the surname <i>Ochoa</i> were residents of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> (frequency 1:671), 12.1% of <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a> (1:686), 11.8% of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> (1:5,302), 9.4% of <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> (1:556), 5.8% of <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> (1:949), 5.1% of <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">Guatemala</a> (1:547), 4.8% of <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> (1:1,537), 4.8% of <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> (1:580), 3.1% of <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a> (1:492), 2.5% of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> (1:3,191), 1.6% of <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> (1:651), 1.4% of <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> (1:1,390), 1.4% of <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> (1:1,286) and 1.3% of <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">El Salvador</a> (1:861).
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, the frequency of the surname was higher than national average (1:3,191) in the following autonomous communities:
</p>
<ul><li>1. <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja_(Spain)" title="La Rioja (Spain)">La Rioja</a> (1:310)</li>
<li>2. <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a> (1:438)</li>
<li>3. <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque Country</a> (1:1,261)</li>
<li>4. <a href="/wiki/Cantabria" title="Cantabria">Cantabria</a> (1:1,963)</li>
<li>5. <a href="/wiki/Aragon" title="Aragon">Aragon</a> (1:2,024)</li>
<li>6. <a href="/wiki/Castilla-La_Mancha" class="mw-redirect" title="Castilla-La Mancha">Castilla-La Mancha</a> (1:2,573)</li>
<li>7. <a href="/wiki/Community_of_Madrid" title="Community of Madrid">Community of Madrid</a> (1:2,933)</li></ul>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">Honduras</a>, the frequency of the surname was higher than national average (1:492) in the following departments:<sup id="cite_ref-ochoa2014_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ochoa2014-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>1. <a href="/wiki/Choluteca_Department" title="Choluteca Department">Choluteca</a> (1:147)</li>
