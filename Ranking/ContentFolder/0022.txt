<p><b>The British Academy</b> is the United Kingdom's <a href="/wiki/National_academy" title="National academy">national academy</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Humanities" title="Humanities">humanities</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Social_science" title="Social science">social sciences</a>.
It was established in 1902 and received its <a href="/wiki/Royal_charter" title="Royal charter">royal charter</a> in the same year. It is now a fellowship of more than 1,000 leading scholars spanning all disciplines across the humanities and social sciences and a funding body for research projects across the United Kingdom. The academy is a self-governing and independent registered charity, based at 10–11 <a href="/wiki/Carlton_House_Terrace" title="Carlton House Terrace">Carlton House Terrace</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.
</p><p>The British Academy is funded with an annual grant from the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Business,_Innovation_and_Skills" title="Department for Business, Innovation and Skills">Department for Business, Innovation and Skills</a> (BIS). In 2014/15 the British Academy's total income was £33,100,000, including £27,000,000 from BIS. £32,900,000 was distributed during the year in research grants, awards and charitable activities.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Purposes">Purposes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Purposes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:218px;"><a href="/wiki/File:British_Academy_front.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/dc/British_Academy_front.jpg" decoding="async" width="216" height="250" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="216" data-file-height="250" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:British_Academy_front.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The British Academy's premises on <a href="/wiki/Carlton_House_Terrace" title="Carlton House Terrace">Carlton House Terrace</a></div></div></div>
<p>The academy states that it has five fundamental purposes:
</p>
<ul><li>To speak up for the humanities and the social sciences</li>
<li>To invest in the very best researchers and research</li>
<li>To inform and enrich debate around society's greatest questions</li>
<li>To ensure sustained international engagement and collaboration</li>
<li>To make the most of the Academy's assets to secure the Academy for the future.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:British_Academy_blue_Clio_logo.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/British_Academy_blue_Clio_logo.jpg/220px-British_Academy_blue_Clio_logo.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/31/British_Academy_blue_Clio_logo.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="279" data-file-height="279" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:British_Academy_blue_Clio_logo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The British Academy's royal seal depicts the Greek muse <a href="/wiki/Clio" title="Clio">Clio</a>. She was redrawn by designer and illustrator Debbie Cook in 2008.</div></div></div>
<p>The creation of a "British Academy for the Promotion of Historical, Philosophical and Philological Studies" was first proposed in 1899 in order that Britain could be represented at meetings of European and American academies. The organisation, which has since become simply "the British Academy", was initiated as an unincorporated society on 17 December 1901, and received its Royal Charter from King Edward VII on 8 August 1902.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since then, many of Britain's most distinguished scholars in the humanities and social sciences have been involved in the life of the academy, including <a href="/wiki/John_Maynard_Keynes" title="John Maynard Keynes">John Maynard Keynes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isaiah_Berlin" title="Isaiah Berlin">Isaiah Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/C._S._Lewis" title="C. S. Lewis">C.&#160;S. Lewis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henry_Moore" title="Henry Moore">Henry Moore</a>.
</p><p>Until 1927–28 the academy had no premises. Then it moved to some rooms in No. <a href="/wiki/6_Burlington_Gardens" title="6 Burlington Gardens">6 Burlington Gardens</a>. In 1968 it moved the short distance to <a href="/wiki/Burlington_House" title="Burlington House">Burlington House</a>. It subsequently moved to headquarters near Regent's Park. Then in 1998 the Academy moved to its present headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Carlton_House_Terrace" title="Carlton House Terrace">Carlton House Terrace</a>. Overlooking St James's Park, the terrace was designed by <a href="/wiki/John_Nash_(architect)" title="John Nash (architect)">John Nash</a> and built in the 1820s and 1830s. Number 10 was formerly the London residence of the Ridley family and number 11 was from 1856 to 1875 the home of Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/William_Gladstone" class="mw-redirect" title="William Gladstone">William Gladstone</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>.
</p><p>In March 2010, the academy embarked on a £2.75m project to renovate and restore the public rooms in No. 11, following the departure of former tenant the Foreign Press Association, and link the two buildings together. The work was completed in January 2011 and the new spaces include a new 150-seat Wolfson Auditorium are available for public hire.
</p><p>The history, problems and achievements of the academy have been recorded in works by two of its secretaries. Sir Frederic Kenyon's volume of 37 pages covers the years up to 1951;<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Sir Mortimer Wheeler's volume covers the years 1949 to 1968.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fellowship">Fellowship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fellowship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Election as a Fellow of the British Academy recognises high scholarly distinction in the humanities or social sciences, evidenced by published work. Fellows may use the letters FBA after their names. Fellows are elected into one of the following disciplinary sections:<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dt>Humanities</dt></dl>
<ul><li>Classical Antiquity</li>
<li>Theology and Religious Studies</li>
<li>African and Oriental Studies</li>
<li>Linguistics and Philology</li>
<li>Early Modern Languages and Literatures</li>
<li>Modern Languages, Literatures and other Media</li>
<li>Archaeology</li>
<li>Medieval Studies</li>
<li>Early Modern History to c1800</li>
<li>Modern History from c1800</li>
<li>History of Art and Music</li>
<li>Philosophy</li>
<li>Culture, Media and Performance</li></ul>
<dl><dt>Social Sciences</dt></dl>
<ul><li>Law</li>
<li>Economics and Economic History</li>
<li>Anthropology and Geography</li>
<li>Sociology, Demography and Social Statistics</li>
<li>Political Studies: Political Theory, Government and International Relations</li>
<li>Psychology</li>
<li>Management and Business Studies</li></ul>
<p>There is also an Education 'ginger group'.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Funding_schemes">Funding schemes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Funding schemes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:British_Academy_Schools_Language_Award.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/British_Academy_Schools_Language_Award.jpg/220px-British_Academy_Schools_Language_Award.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="160" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/British_Academy_Schools_Language_Award.jpg/330px-British_Academy_Schools_Language_Award.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/British_Academy_Schools_Language_Award.jpg/440px-British_Academy_Schools_Language_Award.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="364" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:British_Academy_Schools_Language_Award.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The British Academy has awarded prizes of £4,000 to 15 schools across the UK for encouraging innovative and creative foreign language teaching under the Schools Language Awards.</div></div></div>
<p>The British Academy channels substantial public funding into support for individuals and organisations pursuing humanities and social sciences research and scholarship in the UK and overseas. These funding schemes are designed to aid scholars at different stages of their academic career and include postdoctoral fellowships, Wolfson Research Professorships, Leverhulme Senior Research Fellowships, small research grants and British Academy Research Projects.
</p><p>In addition to its main public funds supported by the Department for Business, Innovation and Skills, the academy also draws on private funds arising from gifts, legacies, contributions made by fellows and grants from research foundations to support a further range of research activities. In 2014/15, the academy received around £30m to support research and researchers across the humanities and social sciences. Funds available to the academy were invested in the following main areas: research career development; a portfolio of research grant opportunities, and international engagement. The demand and quality of applications submitted for academy funding remains high. This year the academy received around 3,600 applications and made 588 awards to scholars based in around 100 different universities across the UK&#160;– a success rate of 16 per cent.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_work">International work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Image-Bsa_athens_library.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Image-Bsa_athens_library.jpg/220px-Image-Bsa_athens_library.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Image-Bsa_athens_library.jpg/330px-Image-Bsa_athens_library.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/18/Image-Bsa_athens_library.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="300" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Image-Bsa_athens_library.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/British_School_at_Athens" title="British School at Athens">British School at Athens</a>, one of the six major British overseas research institutes supported by the British Academy.</div></div></div>
<p>In order to promote the interests of UK research and learning around the world, the Academy works to create frameworks to support international networking and collaboration and develop the role of humanities and social sciences research in tackling global challenges.  It draws on expertise from a wide range of sources from within the fellowship and on specialist advice from its seven Area Panels for Africa, China, the Middle East, Europe, South Asia, and Latin America/Caribbean<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>.
</p><p>The Academy also funds and coordinates a network of overseas institutes which provide local expertise, logistical support and often a working base for UK scholars. These include research institutes in Amman, Ankara, Athens, Jerusalem, Nairobi, Rome and Tehran, as well as UK-based specialist learned societies which run strategic research programmes in other parts of the world including Africa, Latin America and South and South East Asia<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Higher_education_and_research">Higher education and research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Higher education and research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As the UK's national voice for the humanities and social sciences, the British Academy seeks to promote and protect the interests and health of these disciplines and their research base. It makes independent representations to the government and other bodies on relevant higher education and research issues, contributes statements and submissions to formal consultations and organises a range of policy events and discussions, liaising regularly with learned societies, universities, national academies and other relevant organisations.
</p>
<h2><span id="British_Academy.27s_policy_work"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Academy's_policy_work">British Academy's policy work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: British Academy&#039;s policy work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

Please discuss further on the <a href="/wiki/Talk:British_Academy" title="Talk:British Academy">talk page</a>.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">June 2018</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The British Academy's Fellowship represents breadth and excellence of expertise across these disciplines, and the Academy's policy work is dedicated to applying that insight to policy issues for public benefit and societal well being. The goal is to enlighten the context, meaning and practicalities of policy challenges. This work is meant to bring independence, authority and objectivity to complex issues, such as public policy, skills, education and research. From reports to small meetings, the British Academy provides a forum for examining issues that are important for the society and the economy.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_events">Public events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Academy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Public events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Where_are_all_the_women%3F_British_Academy_event.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Where_are_all_the_women%3F_British_Academy_event.jpg/220px-Where_are_all_the_women%3F_British_Academy_event.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Where_are_all_the_women%3F_British_Academy_event.jpg/330px-Where_are_all_the_women%3F_British_Academy_event.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Where_are_all_the_women%3F_British_Academy_event.jpg/440px-Where_are_all_the_women%3F_British_Academy_event.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="1697" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Where_are_all_the_women%3F_British_Academy_event.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Three of the speakers at a British Academy panel discussion, "Where are all the women?"</div></div></div>
<p>The British Academy organises a wide-ranging annual programme of more than 50 public lectures, panel discussions, conferences and seminars showcasing new research and debating topical issues. This includes a number of long-established lecture series, such as the Shakespeare Lecture, first given in 1911.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>. Most events are free and most take place at the Academy's headquarters in Carlton House Terrace<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
