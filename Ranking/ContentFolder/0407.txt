<p>The <b>West Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service</b> is the county-wide, <a href="/wiki/Statute" title="Statute">statutory</a> emergency <a href="/wiki/Fire_service_in_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Fire service in the United Kingdom">fire and rescue</a> service for the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_county" title="Metropolitan county">metropolitan county</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is administered by a joint authority of 22 people who are appointed annually from the five <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan boroughs</a> of West Yorkshire, known as the Fire and Rescue Authority.
</p><p>West Yorkshire covers an area of approximately 800 square miles (2,100&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) which includes remote moorland, rural villages, large towns and cities as well as <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds Bradford International Airport">Leeds Bradford International Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  The fire and rescue service's headquarters are located at Oakroyd Hall, Bradford Road, <a href="/wiki/Birkenshaw,_West_Yorkshire" title="Birkenshaw, West Yorkshire">Birkenshaw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> There is also a large training centre at Birkenshaw used by other authorities besides West Yorkshire.
</p><p>In 2006, the service was listed as being the fourth largest in England (behind <a href="/wiki/London_Fire_Brigade" title="London Fire Brigade">London</a>, the <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_Fire_Service" title="West Midlands Fire Service">West Midlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester_Fire_and_Rescue_Service" title="Greater Manchester Fire and Rescue Service">Greater Manchester</a> fire services) with 1,600 wholetime firefighters and 199 retained.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It has 47 fire appliances based at 41 stations, sub divided into five districts; Bradford, Calderdale, Kirklees, Leeds and Wakefield.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Fire_and_Rescue_Service&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The brigade was formed in 1974 when the unitary county of West Yorkshire was created and was an amalgamation of smaller brigades across the county.<sup id="cite_ref-NA_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NA-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> These included the West Riding County Fire Service, Bradford City Fire Brigade, Dewsbury County Borough Fire Brigade, Halifax County Borough Fire Brigade, Huddersfield County Borough Fire, Leeds City Fire Brigade and Wakefield City Fire Brigade.<sup id="cite_ref-NA_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NA-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> At its inception, the WYF&amp;RS had 52 stations across the five districts.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWallington20142_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWallington20142-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The service's headquarters is at Oakroyd Hall in Birkenshaw; the hall is a grade II listed building that has housed the headquarters since 1964 when it was the hq for the West Riding Fire Service (WRFS).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Previous to that, the WRFS hq was at Huddersfield,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> though the WRFS training centre had moved to Birkenshaw in 1961.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWallington201418_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWallington201418-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After a number of firefighter deaths at notable fires in the 1990s (Gillender street in <a href="/wiki/Bow,_East_London" class="mw-redirect" title="Bow, East London">Bow</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_London" title="East London">East London</a> [1991] and the Sun Valley poultry factory fire in <a href="/wiki/Hereford" title="Hereford">Hereford</a> [1993]), coupled with the publishing of the Fennell Report into the <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cross_fire" title="King&#39;s Cross fire">King's Cross fire</a> of 1987, WYFRS developed an incident command policy that encompassed an at-incident dynamic risk assessment and an organisational structure assessment for major fires. This policy was driven forward by central government and after some refinement was adopted in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2014, the service's emergency call response centre was moved from the Birkenshaw site to a location in Leeds which also subsequently became the call centre for the <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Fire_and_Rescue_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="South Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service">South Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service</a>, in the event of a control room failure at SYFRS control in Sheffield.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Wholetime consists of working two days, two nights and four days off. Day crewing is a day orientated shift pattern and close call is where the firefighter works a set number of hours annually, so the shift patterns are managed locally. purposes. Ranks of station manager and above can work a Flexi duty system, with the remainder of the staff being on a retained basis.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
Wholetime 2-2-4 0800-1900hrs (Bingley, Bradford, Cleckheaton, Halifax, Illingworth, Fairweather Green, Shipley, Leeds, Wakefield, Huddersfield, Moortown, Cookridge, Dewsbury, Ossett, Odsal, Pontefract, Rastrick, Stanningley, Killingbeck)
Day Crewed 4-4 0800-1700 - Todmorden, Wetherby 
Day Crewing 2 on, 2 0ff, 3 on, (0800-1700) 1700-0800 respond from home (Garforth, Morley, Rothwell, Rawdon)
Day Crewed Close Call, 2on 2 0ff 3 on (0800-1700) then respond from accomadation that is available  (Csatleford, Normanton and South Kirkby)

<p>In recent years the service has sought to rationalise its stations.  In 2013, <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a> fire station was closed, while in 2015 <a href="/wiki/Gipton" title="Gipton">Gipton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stanks" class="mw-redirect" title="Stanks">Stanks</a> fire stations in East Leeds were replaced with a single fire station  between the two sites at <a href="/wiki/Killingbeck" title="Killingbeck">Killingbeck</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Of the eight firefighters based at Marsden, three left the service, whilst five transferred to the retained station at Slaithwaite. The fire appliance from Marsden was moved as a cover fire engine at Huddersfield.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Haworth fire station, which was staffed by retained firefighters, was closed permanently in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the same year, <a href="/wiki/Batley" title="Batley">Batley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury's</a> fire stations were merged into a single site in Dewsbury and <a href="/wiki/Brighouse" title="Brighouse">Brighouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a> fire stations were also amalgamated into one fire station in <a href="/wiki/Rastrick" title="Rastrick">Rastrick</a> that is nearer to the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> the stations at Otley and Rawdon were due to be closed and replaced with a combined site located in the <a href="/wiki/Menston" title="Menston">Menston</a> area, but these plans were abandoned after a suitable site could not be found.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
