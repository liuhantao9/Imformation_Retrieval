	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Honorary title (academic)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Honorary_degree" title="Honorary degree">Honorary degree</a>.</div>
<p><b>Honorary titles (professor, reader, lecturer)</b> in <a href="/wiki/Academia" class="mw-redirect" title="Academia">academia</a> may be conferred on persons in recognition of contributions by a non-employee or by an employee beyond regular duties. This practice primarily exists in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a> and some <a href="/wiki/Universities" class="mw-redirect" title="Universities">universities</a> and <a href="/wiki/College" title="College">colleges</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, Taiwan, China, New Zealand, Japan, Denmark, and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.
</p><p>Examples of such titles are Honorary <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">Professor</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Honorary <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">Fellow</a>, Honorary Senior <a href="/wiki/Research_Fellow" class="mw-redirect" title="Research Fellow">Research Fellow</a>, Honorary <a href="/wiki/Reader_(academic_rank)" title="Reader (academic rank)">Reader</a>, Honorary <a href="/wiki/Lecturer" title="Lecturer">Lecturer</a> (normally applies to non-teaching staff, who give occasional lectures),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Visiting Fellow (normally applies to students carrying out further studies and research programmes),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Industrial Fellow.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honorary_Professor">Honorary Professor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honorary_title_(academic)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Honorary Professor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Irwin_Jacobs.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Irwin_Jacobs.jpg/150px-Irwin_Jacobs.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Irwin_Jacobs.jpg/225px-Irwin_Jacobs.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Irwin_Jacobs.jpg/300px-Irwin_Jacobs.jpg 2x" data-file-width="429" data-file-height="388" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Irwin_Jacobs.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Dr. Irwin Jacobs received an Honorary Distinguished Chair Professorship from National Tsing Hua University, Taiwan in 2013</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sir-john-meurig-thomas_rare-book-room.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Sir-john-meurig-thomas_rare-book-room.jpg/150px-Sir-john-meurig-thomas_rare-book-room.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="181" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Sir-john-meurig-thomas_rare-book-room.jpg/225px-Sir-john-meurig-thomas_rare-book-room.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Sir-john-meurig-thomas_rare-book-room.jpg/300px-Sir-john-meurig-thomas_rare-book-room.jpg 2x" data-file-width="667" data-file-height="804" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sir-john-meurig-thomas_rare-book-room.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sir_John_Meurig_Thomas" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir John Meurig Thomas">Sir John Meurig Thomas</a> received an Honorary Distinguished Professorship from Osaka Prefecture University, Japan in 2006<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>In the UK, this is the highest title to be awarded to individuals whom the university wish to appoint, honor, and
to work with. These individuals are not university staffs nor employees. An external person is usually recommended by an internal university academic staff, and recommended for approval by the head of department, for which the documents are then forwarded to faculty dean, vice president and president (or deputy vice chancellor) for approval.
</p><p>Examples of UK universities who award honorary professorships are University of Essex, University of Manchester,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
Brunel University,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Middlesex University, University of Bristol,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Leicester University,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> University of Exeter,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> etc. 
Procedures for evaluation and approval are overseen by university personnel or registrar office. Appointment is made formally by an appointment letter, for a fixed period of time (usually three years) and renewal is possible.
Honorary professors are expected to contribute to the department of the university through giving seminars and joint research with university staffs. Requirements vary from university to university but contributions are expected from the appointee.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, more titles are used to recognize different levels of individuals. They are (in descending order of hierarchy):
</p>
<ul><li>Honorary Distinguished Chair Professor</li>
<li>Honorary Chair Professor</li>
<li>Honorary Distinguished Professor</li>
<li>Honorary Professor</li>
<li>Honorary Associate Professor</li>
<li>Honorary Assistant Professor</li></ul>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, top universities like <a href="/wiki/Fudan_University" title="Fudan University">Fudan University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tsinghua_University" title="Tsinghua University">Tsinghua University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peking_University" title="Peking University">Peking University</a> have awarded Honorary Professorships. Recent recipients include Prof. Peter Bruce from Oxford,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Prof. Reinhart Poprawe from Aachen Germany<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and Professor <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Sargent" class="mw-redirect" title="Thomas Sargent">Thomas Sargent</a>, Nobel Memorial Prize winner in Economics in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, Australian Catholic University (ACU),<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> University of Queensland,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> RMIT,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> University of Western Australia,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> University of Wollongong,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> University of Canberra<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and Macquarie University<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> all allow the appointment of honorary professors.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, University of Otago,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> University of Waikato, and University of Auckland<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> also have provisions for the appointment of honorary professors. Recently, Sir Richard Taylor was appointed honorary professor at Massey University.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Prof. Mike Murphy of the Mitochondrial Biology Unit at University of Cambridge was appointed to honorary professor at University of Otago in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, the honorary professor title is conferred in recognition of a person's special contribution to the subject area associated with faculty's activities. Honorary professors are expected to:
</p>
<ul><li>participate in research partnerships</li>
<li>give lectures</li>
<li>participate in PhD co-supervision or PhD examination committee</li></ul>
<p>Although honorary professors are not paid a salary, the following expenses are usually compensated:
</p>
<ul><li>travel and accommodation during visits to university</li>
<li>daily expenses such as cost of meals</li>
<li>a lecture fee</li></ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/Aarhus_University" title="Aarhus University">Aarhus University</a> is an example university in Denmark that confers honorary professorship.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Honorary_Reader.2C_Senior_Lecturer.2C_Lecturer"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Honorary_Reader,_Senior_Lecturer,_Lecturer">Honorary Reader, Senior Lecturer, Lecturer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honorary_title_(academic)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Honorary Reader, Senior Lecturer, Lecturer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Persons of lower prestige and academic achievements are appointed at ranks other than professor. Honorary readers are viewed higher than honorary senior lecturer and honorary lecturer. A person can be promoted to the next higher honorary rank on recommendation by the internal university staff and department. Once approved at the university level, the title is then changed.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honorary_Fellow">Honorary Fellow</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honorary_title_(academic)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Honorary Fellow">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Harry_Shum.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Harry_Shum.jpg/100px-Harry_Shum.jpg" decoding="async" width="100" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Harry_Shum.jpg/150px-Harry_Shum.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/Harry_Shum.jpg/200px-Harry_Shum.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1400" data-file-height="2100" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Harry_Shum.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Dr. Harry Shum received an Honorary Fellowship from the University of Hong Kong, Hong Kong in 2017</div></div></div>
<p>In certain UK universities, the title of Honorary Fellow is awarded to people from industry, whom the university wish to recognize and collaborate with. For example, Imperial College London,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> University College London and Queen Mary College London all have provisions for the award of honorary fellow. The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Hong_Kong" title="University of Hong Kong">University of Hong Kong</a> also awards Honorary Fellow and a recent award was made to <a href="/wiki/Harry_Shum" title="Harry Shum">Harry Shum</a> from Microsoft Corporation.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
Various <a href="/wiki/Professional_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional body">professional bodies</a>, such as the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Institute_of_British_Architects" title="Royal Institute of British Architects">Royal Institute of British Architects</a> and <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology" title="Institution of Engineering and Technology">IET</a> UK also have honorary fellowships.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Visiting_Appointments">Visiting Appointments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honorary_title_(academic)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Visiting Appointments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is often confused with honorary academic titles. A visiting professor or reader or senior lecturer or lecturer is someone who has taken time off his primary institution of employment to visit and collaborate with staff from another university. Hence, the visiting appointment is usually for a short period of time, ranging from 3 months up to 1 year. This is not the same as an honorary appointment held in UK universities. 
However, in Germany, visiting lecturers and private lecturers can be conferred the titles of "Honorarprofessor" or "<a href="/wiki/Au%C3%9Ferplanm%C3%A4%C3%9Figer_Professor" class="mw-redirect" title="Außerplanmäßiger Professor">Außerplanmäßiger Professor</a>" respectively after several semesters of successful teaching.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Benefits">Benefits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Honorary_title_(academic)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Benefits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to the honor and recognition, an honorary title sometimes permits non-employees to enjoy the privileges available to regular staff members, such as access to facilities and libraries, temporary stay in university housing, entitlement to a university business card, an email account, and to receive a parking permit.
</p>
