<p><b>Kirkwood Hospice</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Hospice" title="Hospice">hospice</a> situated in <a href="/wiki/Dalton,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Dalton, West Yorkshire">Dalton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. It provides Specialist <a href="/wiki/Palliative" class="mw-redirect" title="Palliative">palliative</a> care for the terminally ill in <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a>. It was built on the site of the former <b>Mill Hill Isolation Hospital</b>, which closed in 1971
</p><p>Kirwood hospice provided help and assistance in the formation of the Laura Crane Trust.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Finances">Finances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirkwood_Hospice&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Finances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kirkwood is dependent on private donations and charitable gifts. To help raise funds for patient care the hospice organises annual activities such as the Midnight Memory Walk, which has raised in excess of £200,000 for patient care over the last three years<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and an "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_a_Knockout" title="It&#39;s a Knockout">It's a Knockout</a>" style tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Other major fundraising events include <a href="/wiki/Tandem_skydiving" title="Tandem skydiving">tandem skydiving</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Patrons of the hospice include author <a href="/wiki/Joanne_Harris" title="Joanne Harris">Joanne Harris</a>, world-renowned for <a href="/wiki/Bestseller" title="Bestseller">bestsellers</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Chocolat_(novel)" title="Chocolat (novel)">Chocolat</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lollipop_Shoes" title="The Lollipop Shoes">The Lollipop Shoes</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Five_Quarters_of_the_Orange" title="Five Quarters of the Orange">Five Quarters of the Orange</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Runemarks" title="Runemarks">Runemarks</a></i>.
</p>
