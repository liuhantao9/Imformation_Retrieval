	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Viscount Cobham</h1>

<p><b>Viscount Cobham</b> is a title in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Great_Britain" title="Peerage of Great Britain">Peerage of Great Britain</a> that was created in 1718. Owing to its special <a href="/wiki/Remainder_(law)" title="Remainder (law)">remainder</a>, the title has passed through several families. Since 1889, it has been held by members of the <a href="/wiki/Lyttelton_family" title="Lyttelton family">Lyttelton family</a>.
</p><p>From 1750 to 1784, the barony and viscountcy of Cobham were subsidiary titles of the earldom of <a href="/wiki/Earl_Temple_of_Stowe" title="Earl Temple of Stowe">Temple of Stowe</a>, subsidiary titles of the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Buckingham" title="Duke of Buckingham">Marquessate of Buckingham</a> from 1784 to 1822 and of the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Buckingham" title="Duke of Buckingham">Dukedom of Buckingham and Chandos</a> from 1822 to 1889. Since the latter year the Cobham titles have been merged with the titles of <a href="/wiki/Baron_Lyttelton" title="Baron Lyttelton">Baron Lyttelton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baron_Lyttelton" title="Baron Lyttelton">Baron Westcote</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viscount_Cobham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation_of_the_title">Creation of the title</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viscount_Cobham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Creation of the title">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Richard_Temple,_1st_Viscount_Cobham_by_Jean_Baptiste_van_Loo.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Richard_Temple%2C_1st_Viscount_Cobham_by_Jean_Baptiste_van_Loo.jpg/220px-Richard_Temple%2C_1st_Viscount_Cobham_by_Jean_Baptiste_van_Loo.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="267" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Richard_Temple%2C_1st_Viscount_Cobham_by_Jean_Baptiste_van_Loo.jpg/330px-Richard_Temple%2C_1st_Viscount_Cobham_by_Jean_Baptiste_van_Loo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Richard_Temple%2C_1st_Viscount_Cobham_by_Jean_Baptiste_van_Loo.jpg/440px-Richard_Temple%2C_1st_Viscount_Cobham_by_Jean_Baptiste_van_Loo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2400" data-file-height="2913" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Richard_Temple,_1st_Viscount_Cobham_by_Jean_Baptiste_van_Loo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Richard_Temple,_1st_Viscount_Cobham" title="Richard Temple, 1st Viscount Cobham">Richard Temple (1675–1749), 1st Baron Cobham (1714), 1st Baron Cobham (1718), 1st Viscount Cobham (1718)</a></div></div></div>
<p>The viscountcy of Cobham was created in 1718 for <a href="/wiki/Field_Marshal_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="Field Marshal (United Kingdom)">Field Marshal</a> <a href="/wiki/Richard_Temple,_1st_Viscount_Cobham" title="Richard Temple, 1st Viscount Cobham">Sir Richard Temple, 1st Baron Cobham, 4th Baronet</a> of Stowe. He was the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Sir_Richard_Temple,_3rd_Baronet" title="Sir Richard Temple, 3rd Baronet">Sir Richard Temple, 3rd Baronet</a>.
</p><p>During his lifetime, the Field Marshal received three titles in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Great_Britain" title="Peerage of Great Britain">Peerage of Great Britain</a>:
</p>
<ul><li>In 1714 he was made <b>Baron Cobham</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Cobham,_Kent" title="Cobham, Kent">Cobham</a> in the County of Kent, with remainder to heirs male of his body.</li>
<li>In 1718 he was made <b>Baron Cobham</b>, of Cobham in the County of Kent, and <b>Viscount Cobham</b>, with remainder, failing heirs male of his own, to (1) his sister <a href="/wiki/Hester_Grenville,_1st_Countess_Temple" title="Hester Grenville, 1st Countess Temple">Lady Hester</a> and the heirs male of her body and failing which to (2) his third sister Lady Christian, wife of <a href="/wiki/Sir_Thomas_Lyttelton,_4th_Baronet" title="Sir Thomas Lyttelton, 4th Baronet">Sir Thomas Lyttelton, 4th Baronet</a>, of Frankley (see the <a href="/wiki/Baron_Lyttelton" title="Baron Lyttelton">Baron Lyttelton</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>Field Marshal Lord Cobham died childless in 1749, at which time the Cobham barony of 1714 became extinct. His other titles passed to different heirs: The Temple Baronetcy of 1611 passed to his cousin, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sir_William_Temple,_5th_Baronet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sir William Temple, 5th Baronet (page does not exist)">Sir William Temple, 5th Baronet</a>. The barony and viscountcy of 1718 passed, according to the special remainder, to Lord Cobham's sister <a href="/wiki/Hester_Grenville,_1st_Countess_Temple" title="Hester Grenville, 1st Countess Temple">Hester</a>, the widow of Richard Grenville, and her children.
</p>
<h3><span id="Lord_Cobham.27s_heirs"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Lord_Cobham's_heirs">Lord Cobham's heirs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viscount_Cobham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lord Cobham&#039;s heirs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Temple_family">Temple family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viscount_Cobham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Temple family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:TempleArms.PNG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/TempleArms.PNG/200px-TempleArms.PNG" decoding="async" width="200" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/TempleArms.PNG/300px-TempleArms.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/TempleArms.PNG/400px-TempleArms.PNG 2x" data-file-width="1090" data-file-height="1199" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:TempleArms.PNG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Arms of Temple of Stowe: <i>Or, an eagle displayed sable</i></div></div></div>
<p>The Temple family descended from Peter Temple of <a href="/wiki/Burton_Dassett" title="Burton Dassett">Burton Dassett</a>. His younger son Anthony Temple founded the <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Irish</a> branch of the family from whom the <a href="/wiki/Viscount_Palmerston" title="Viscount Palmerston">Viscounts Palmerston</a> descended.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Peter Temple's eldest son, John Temple, acquired the <a href="/wiki/Stowe_House" title="Stowe House">Stowe</a> estate in <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a>. The latter's son <a href="/wiki/Sir_Thomas_Temple,_1st_Baronet,_of_Stowe" title="Sir Thomas Temple, 1st Baronet, of Stowe">Thomas Temple</a> represented <a href="/wiki/Andover_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Andover (UK Parliament constituency)">Andover</a> in Parliament. In 1611 he was created a <b>Baronet</b>, of Stowe in the County of Buckingham, in the <a href="/wiki/Baronetage_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Baronetage of England">Baronetage of England</a>. His son, the second Baronet, represented <a href="/wiki/Buckingham_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Buckingham (UK Parliament constituency)">Buckingham</a> in both the <a href="/wiki/Short_Parliament" title="Short Parliament">Short Parliament</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Long_Parliament" title="Long Parliament">Long Parliament</a>. He was succeeded by his son, the third Baronet. He sat in Parliament for <a href="/wiki/Warwickshire_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Warwickshire (UK Parliament constituency)">Warwickshire</a> and Buckingham. His son succeeded as fourth Baronet in 1697 and received the Cobham titles in 1714 and 1718, respectively. At his death in 1749, the Temple Baronetcy of 1611 passed to his cousin <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sir_William_Temple,_5th_Baronet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sir William Temple, 5th Baronet (page does not exist)">William Temple</a>, the fifth Baronet, who was a great-grandson of Sir John Temple, second son of the first Baronet. It became dormant in 1786 on the death of the seventh Baronet.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Grenville_family">Grenville family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viscount_Cobham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Grenville family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:2ndDukeOfBuckinghamAndChandos.JPG" class="image" title="Richard Temple-Nugent-Brydges-Chandos-Grenville, 2nd Duke of Buckingham and Chandos"><img alt="Richard Temple-Nugent-Brydges-Chandos-Grenville, 2nd Duke of Buckingham and Chandos" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/2ndDukeOfBuckinghamAndChandos.JPG/250px-2ndDukeOfBuckinghamAndChandos.JPG" decoding="async" width="250" height="351" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/2ndDukeOfBuckinghamAndChandos.JPG/375px-2ndDukeOfBuckinghamAndChandos.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/2ndDukeOfBuckinghamAndChandos.JPG/500px-2ndDukeOfBuckinghamAndChandos.JPG 2x" data-file-width="535" data-file-height="752" /></a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Stowe_Armorial.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Stowe_Armorial.jpg/250px-Stowe_Armorial.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="284" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Stowe_Armorial.jpg/375px-Stowe_Armorial.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Stowe_Armorial.jpg/500px-Stowe_Armorial.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1491" data-file-height="1695" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Stowe_Armorial.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The 719 <a href="/wiki/Heraldic_quartering" class="mw-redirect" title="Heraldic quartering">heraldic quarterings</a> of the Temple-Nugent-Brydges-Chandos-Grenville family.</div></div></div>
<p>The Field Marshal's barony and viscountcy of 1718 passed, according to the special remainder, to his sister <a href="/wiki/Hester_Grenville,_1st_Countess_Temple" title="Hester Grenville, 1st Countess Temple">Hester</a>, the widow of Richard Grenville, and her children. In 1751 she was further created <b>Countess Temple</b> in the Peerage of Great Britain, with remainder to the heirs male of her body. Lady Temple's younger son was Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/George_Grenville" title="George Grenville">George Grenville</a>. At her death, she was succeeded by her eldest son, the second Earl. He inherited the Temple estates, including <a href="/wiki/Stowe_House" title="Stowe House">Stowe House</a>, and assumed the additional surname of <i>Temple</i>. He was also involved in politics and held office as <a href="/wiki/First_Lord_of_the_Admiralty" title="First Lord of the Admiralty">First Lord of the Admiralty</a> and as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Privy_Seal" title="Lord Privy Seal">Lord Privy Seal</a>. On his death the titles passed to his nephew, the third Earl, the son of George Grenville. He served as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_Ireland" title="Lord Lieutenant of Ireland">Lord Lieutenant of Ireland</a> between 1782 and 1783 and 1787 and 1789. In 1784 he was created <b>Marquess of Buckingham</b> in the Peerage of Great Britain. Lord Buckingham married Lady Mary Nugent, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Nugent,_1st_Earl_Nugent" title="Robert Nugent, 1st Earl Nugent">Robert Nugent, 1st Earl Nugent</a>. Mary was in 1800 created <b>Baroness Nugent</b> in her own right in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Ireland" title="Peerage of Ireland">Peerage of Ireland</a>, with remainder to her second son George (see the <a href="/wiki/Baron_Nugent" title="Baron Nugent">Baron Nugent</a>). In 1788 Lord Buckingham also succeeded his father-in-law as second <b><a href="/wiki/Earl_Nugent" title="Earl Nugent">Earl Nugent</a></b> according to a special remainder in the <a href="/wiki/Letters_patent" title="Letters patent">letters patent</a>. He assumed by Royal licence the additional surname of <i>Nugent</i> at the same time.
</p><p>He was succeeded by his son, the second Marquess. He served as Joint <a href="/wiki/Paymaster_of_the_Forces" title="Paymaster of the Forces">Paymaster of the Forces</a> from 1806 to 1807. He married Lady Anne Eliza Brydges, the only child of <a href="/wiki/James_Brydges,_3rd_Duke_of_Chandos" title="James Brydges, 3rd Duke of Chandos">James Brydges, 3rd Duke of Chandos</a> (a title which became extinct on his death in 1789), and assumed by Royal licence the additional surnames of <i>Brydges-Chandos</i> in 1799. In 1822 Lord Buckingham was created <b>Earl Temple of Stowe</b>, in the County of Buckingham, <b>Marquess of Chandos</b> and <b>Duke of Buckingham and Chandos</b>, all in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Peerage of the United Kingdom">Peerage of the United Kingdom</a>. The earldom was created with remainder, failing male issue of his own, to (1) the heirs male of the body of his deceased great-grandmother Hester Grenville, 1st Countess Temple, and (2) in default thereof to his granddaughter Lady Anne Eliza Mary Temple-Nugent-Brydges-Chandos-Grenville, daughter of his son Richard, Earl Temple, who succeeded as second Duke. He was a <a href="/wiki/Tory" title="Tory">Tory</a> politician and served as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Privy_Seal" title="Lord Privy Seal">Lord Privy Seal</a> from 1841 to 1842.
</p><p>On his death the titles passed to his son, the third Duke. He was also a prominent politician and served as <a href="/wiki/Lord_President_of_the_Council" title="Lord President of the Council">Lord President of the Council</a> and as <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_the_Colonies" title="Secretary of State for the Colonies">Secretary of State for the Colonies</a>. In 1868 the Duke established his right to the Scottish <a href="/wiki/Lord_Kinloss" title="Lord Kinloss">lordship of Kinloss</a> before the Committee for Privileges of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a>. However, on his death in 1889 without male issue, the dukedom and its subsidiary titles (the marquessate of Buckingham, marquessate of Chandos, earldom of Temple and <a href="/wiki/Earl_Nugent" title="Earl Nugent">earldom of Nugent</a>) became extinct. The lordship of Kinloss passed to his daughter <a href="/wiki/Mary_Morgan-Grenville,_11th_Lady_Kinloss" title="Mary Morgan-Grenville, 11th Lady Kinloss">Mary</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Earl_Temple_of_Stowe" title="Earl Temple of Stowe">earldom of Temple of Stowe</a> passed to his sister's son William Temple-Gore-Langton because the title had been created with a special remainder to her heirs male (see these titles for more information).
</p><p>Another member of the Grenville family was Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/William_Wyndham_Grenville,_1st_Baron_Grenville" class="mw-redirect" title="William Wyndham Grenville, 1st Baron Grenville">William Wyndham Grenville, 1st Baron Grenville</a>. He was the younger son of George Grenville and the younger brother of the first Marquess of Buckingham.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Lyttelton_family">Lyttelton family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Viscount_Cobham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Lyttelton family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Lyttelton_family" title="Lyttelton family">Lyttelton family</a></div>
<p>As the barony and viscountcy of Cobham could only descend to patrilineal descendants of Hester Temple or Christian Lyttelton, the wife of <a href="/wiki/Sir_Thomas_Lyttelton,_4th_Baronet" title="Sir Thomas Lyttelton, 4th Baronet">Sir Thomas Lyttelton, 4th Baronet</a>, they were inherited in 1889 according to the special remainders by the 3rd Duke's distant relative <a href="/wiki/Charles_Lyttelton,_8th_Viscount_Cobham" title="Charles Lyttelton, 8th Viscount Cobham">Charles Lyttelton, 5th Baron Lyttelton</a>. He was the great-great-grandson of the aforementioned Lady Christian and Sir Thomas Lyttelton, 4th Baronet. Before succeeding to his father's peerages, he had represented <a href="/wiki/East_Worcestershire_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="East Worcestershire (UK Parliament constituency)">East Worcestershire</a> in Parliament as a <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/George_Lyttelton,_4th_Baron_Lyttelton" title="George Lyttelton, 4th Baron Lyttelton">4th Baron Lyttelton</a>'s death in 1876 he had already inherited the Lyttelton Baronetcy of Frankley (1611), the <a href="/wiki/Baron_Lyttelton" title="Baron Lyttelton">Westcote barony</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Ireland" title="Peerage of Ireland">Peerage of Ireland</a> (1776) and the <a href="/wiki/Baron_Lyttelton" title="Baron Lyttelton">Lyttelton barony</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Great_Britain" title="Peerage of Great Britain">Peerage of Great Britain</a> (1794).
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Lyttelton_family" title="Lyttelton family">Lyttelton family</a> seat is <a href="/wiki/Hagley_Hall" title="Hagley Hall">Hagley Hall</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Stourbridge" title="Stourbridge">Stourbridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Worcestershire" title="Worcestershire">Worcestershire</a>. Most owners of Hagley Hall are buried at the <a href="/wiki/St_John_the_Baptist_Church,_Hagley" class="mw-redirect" title="St John the Baptist Church, Hagley">parish church of St John the Baptist</a> in the adjacent <a href="/wiki/Hagley_Park,_Worcestershire" title="Hagley Park, Worcestershire">Hagley Park</a>.
</p><p>Since 1889 the holders of the Lyttelton and Cobham titles have chosen to use the style of <b>Viscount Cobham</b>. The eighth Viscount's son, the ninth Viscount, was <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_Worcestershire" title="Lord Lieutenant of Worcestershire">Lord Lieutenant of Worcestershire</a> from 1923 to 1949. He was succeeded by his son, the tenth Viscount. He notably served as <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_New_Zealand" title="Governor-General of New Zealand">Governor-General of New Zealand</a> from 1957 to 1962. As of 2010<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Viscount_Cobham&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> the titles are held by his younger son, the <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Charles_Lyttelton,_12th_Viscount_Cobham" class="mw-redirect" title="Christopher Charles Lyttelton, 12th Viscount Cobham">12th Viscount</a>, who succeeded his elder brother in 2006.
</p>
