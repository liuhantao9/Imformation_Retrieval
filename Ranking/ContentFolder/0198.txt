<p><b>Lexum</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> legal technology firm, specializing in the publishing and management of legal information. Lexum provides turn-key SaaS solutions<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Use_plain_English#Buzzwords" title="Wikipedia:Use plain English"><span title="The material near this tag may use buzzwords designed solely to impress you or obscure meaning. (August 2019)">buzzword</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> designed for the producers and managers of legal information, as well as digitization, conversion and publihing services for legislation, case law, secondary material, and more.
</p><p>The firm is a spin-off from the <a href="/wiki/Universit%C3%A9_de_Montr%C3%A9al" title="Université de Montréal">Université de Montréal</a> LexUM Laboratory. The forerunner of Lexum was founded in 1993 as the Law <a href="/wiki/Gopher_(protocol)" title="Gopher (protocol)">Gopher</a> server at the Université de Montréal.  This site was both, respectively, the first available legal resources site in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>. The Canadian Legal Information Institute (<a href="/wiki/CanLII" title="CanLII">CanLII</a>) is a not-for-profit organization created by the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Law_Societies_of_Canada" title="Federation of Law Societies of Canada">Federation of Law Societies of Canada</a> in 2001 with Lexum technology backing-up the site ever since. In 2018 CanLII acquired Lexum.
</p><p>Lexum is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Free_Access_to_Law_Movement" title="Free Access to Law Movement">Free Access to Law Movement</a>.
</p>
