<p><b>Horsforth</b> is a suburb and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> within the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a>, in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England, lying about five miles north-west of Leeds city centre. <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically within the</a> <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, it has a population of 18,895 according to the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-:popstats:_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:popstats:-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
Horsforth was considered to have the <a href="/wiki/Largest_village_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Largest village in England">largest population of any village in the United Kingdom</a> during the latter part of the 19th century. It became part of the City of Leeds metropolitan borough in 1974 and became a civil parish with <a href="/wiki/Town_council" title="Town council">town council</a> in 1999. The area sits within the <a href="/wiki/Horsforth_(ward)" title="Horsforth (ward)">Horsforth</a> <a href="/wiki/Ward_(electoral_subdivision)" title="Ward (electoral subdivision)">ward</a> of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a>, which also includes the southern part of <a href="/wiki/Rawdon,_West_Yorkshire" title="Rawdon, West Yorkshire">Rawdon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Horsforth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Horsforth_Museum.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Horsforth_Museum.jpg/220px-Horsforth_Museum.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Horsforth_Museum.jpg/330px-Horsforth_Museum.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Horsforth_Museum.jpg/440px-Horsforth_Museum.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Horsforth_Museum.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Horsforth Museum</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:HorsforthHomeFront.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/HorsforthHomeFront.JPG/220px-HorsforthHomeFront.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/HorsforthHomeFront.JPG/330px-HorsforthHomeFront.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/HorsforthHomeFront.JPG/440px-HorsforthHomeFront.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:HorsforthHomeFront.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Home Front: Second World War display in Horsforth Museum</div></div></div>
<p>Horsforth was recorded in the <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i> of 1086 as <i>Horseford</i>, <i>Horseforde</i>, <i>Hoseforde</i>; but late-ninth-century coins with the legend <i>ORSNA FORD</i> and <i>OHSNA FORD</i> may have come from Horsforth. The name derives from Old English <i>hors</i> or, to judge from the coins, *<i>horsa</i> ('horse') in the genitive plural form <i>horsa</i>/<i>horsna</i> + <i>ford</i> '<a href="/wiki/Ford_(river)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ford (river)">ford</a>', thus meaning 'horses' ford'.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This refers to a river crossing on the <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">River Aire</a> that was subsequently used to transport woollen goods to and from <a href="/wiki/Pudsey" title="Pudsey">Pudsey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shipley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Shipley, West Yorkshire">Shipley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>. The original ford was situated off Calverley Lane, but was replaced by a stone footbridge at the turn of the 19th century.
</p><p>The three unnamed <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxons" title="Anglo-Saxons">Saxon</a> <a href="/wiki/Thegns" class="mw-redirect" title="Thegns">thegns</a> that held the land at the <a href="/wiki/Norman_Conquest" class="mw-redirect" title="Norman Conquest">Conquest</a> gave way to the king who granted it to lesser <a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Norman</a> nobles,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> but not long after most of the village came under the control of <a href="/wiki/Kirkstall_Abbey" title="Kirkstall Abbey">Kirkstall Abbey</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Cistercian" class="mw-redirect" title="Cistercian">Cistercian</a> house founded in 1152 on the bank of the River Aire downstream of Horsforth.
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Monasteries" title="Dissolution of the Monasteries">Dissolution of the Monasteries</a> in 1539, Horsforth was partitioned and sold to five families, one of them – the Stanhopes – achieved supremacy and controlled the village for the next 300 years. The estate record of the Stanhopes is regarded as one of the most extensive and important collections of its kind, complementing the extensive medieval record associated with Kirkstall Abbey.
</p><p>Until the mid 19th century, Horsforth was an agricultural community but it expanded rapidly with the growth of the nearby industrial centre of Leeds. A tannery business was founded at Woodside in about 1820 by the Watson family. It was on the eastern edge of their small farm, and memorialised by <i>Tanhouse Hill Lane</i>. The business became a soap manufacturer and moved to Whitehall Road in Leeds in 1861 and under the chairmanship of <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Watson,_1st_Baron_Manton" title="Joseph Watson, 1st Baron Manton">Joseph Watson junior</a>, created <a href="/wiki/Baron_Manton" title="Baron Manton">Baron Manton</a> in 1922, as Joseph Watson &amp; Sons Ltd, became the largest soap supplier to the northeast of England, second in size nationally only to <a href="/wiki/Lever_Brothers" title="Lever Brothers">Lever Brothers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Industrially, Horsforth has a history of producing high-quality stone from its quarries. Not only did it supply <a href="/wiki/Kirkstall_Abbey" title="Kirkstall Abbey">Kirkstall Abbey</a> with building materials and millstones in the medieval period, it provided the stone for <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_North_Yorkshire" title="Scarborough, North Yorkshire">Scarborough's</a> seafront and sent sandstone from Golden Bank Quarry as far afield as <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>. Situated on Horsforth Beck (Oil Mill Beck) were mills serving the textile trade.
</p><p>Between 1861 and 1862, there was an outbreak of <a href="/wiki/Typhoid" class="mw-redirect" title="Typhoid">typhoid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Horsforth was historically a <a href="/wiki/Township_(England)" title="Township (England)">township</a> in the parish of <a href="/wiki/Guiseley" title="Guiseley">Guiseley</a>.  It became a separate civil parish in 1866.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  In the late-19th century it achieved note as the village with the largest population in England. Railways, turnpike roads, tramways and the nearby canal made it a focus for almost all forms of public and commercial transport and it became a dormitory suburb of Leeds. The civil parish became Horsforth <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">Urban District</a> in 1894.  The parish and urban district were abolished in 1974 and merged into the new <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a> metropolitan district. In 1999 Horsforth became a civil parish and a <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">parish council</a> was created, which exercised its right to declare Horsforth a town.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Horsforth Village Museum<sup id="cite_ref-HM_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HM-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> has collections and displays illustrating aspects of life set against the backdrop of the changing role of the village.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a> the £241,000 required to build the corvette HMS <i>Aubrietia</i> was raised entirely by the people of Horsforth. In 2000 the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">US President</a> <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a> acknowledged Horsforth's contribution to the war effort in a letter sent to MP <a href="/wiki/Paul_Truswell" title="Paul Truswell">Paul Truswell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The letter is in the museum.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Horsforth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Horsforth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p><a href="/wiki/Horsforth_railway_station" title="Horsforth railway station">Horsforth railway station</a> is on the <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_line" title="Harrogate line">Harrogate line</a> between <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_railway_station" title="Harrogate railway station">Harrogate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>. The station is just outside the Horsforth parish boundary, on the <a href="/wiki/Cookridge" title="Cookridge">Cookridge</a> side of Moseley Beck.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Newlay_and_Horsforth_railway_station" title="Newlay and Horsforth railway station">Newlay station</a>, which was built by the Midland Railway, was renamed <i>Newlay &amp; Horsforth</i> station in 1889. It was situated south of the <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">River Aire</a> and was accessible from Horsforth on Pollard Lane which connects Horsforth to <a href="/wiki/Bramley,_Leeds" title="Bramley, Leeds">Bramley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The station on the <a href="/wiki/Airedale_line" title="Airedale line">Airedale line</a> was renamed Newlay station in 1961. It closed on 22 March 1965, along with other stations on the Airedale line: <a href="/wiki/Armley_Canal_Road_railway_station" title="Armley Canal Road railway station">Armley Canal Road</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kirkstall_railway_station" title="Kirkstall railway station">Kirkstall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calverley_and_Rodley_railway_station" title="Calverley and Rodley railway station">Calverley &amp; Rodley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Apperley_Bridge_railway_station" title="Apperley Bridge railway station">Apperley Bridge</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Kirkstall_Forge_railway_station" title="Kirkstall Forge railway station">Kirkstall Forge railway station</a> is also located around two miles from Horsforth station. Kirkstall Forge railway station is a suburban station serving the Kirkstall area of Leeds, West Yorkshire, England. It is on the Leeds to Bradford Line between Leeds City and Shipley and was opened on 19 June 2016, near the site of an earlier station with the same name.
</p>

<p>The nearest airport is <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_Airport" title="Leeds Bradford Airport">Leeds Bradford Airport</a>, in neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Yeadon,_West_Yorkshire" title="Yeadon, West Yorkshire">Yeadon</a> approximately 2.6 miles away.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Horsforth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Leeds_Trinity_University" title="Leeds Trinity University">Leeds Trinity University</a>, formerly Leeds Trinity University College, is an independent university after a period as an accredited college of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Leeds" title="University of Leeds">University of Leeds</a>. The residential campus is located off Brownberrie Lane, Horsforth.
</p><p>The further education college <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_College" title="Leeds City College">Leeds City College</a> has a site in Horsforth called the Horsforth Campus. It was previously part of Park Lane College.
</p><p>The main <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school#England_and_Wales" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a> is <a href="/wiki/Horsforth_School" title="Horsforth School">Horsforth School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Horsforth's <a href="/wiki/State_school#United_Kingdom" title="State school">state</a> sector <a href="/wiki/Primary_school#United_Kingdom" title="Primary school">primary schools</a> are West End Lane Primary School, St Margaret's Primary School, Newlaithes Primary School, Westbrook Lane Primary School, Broadgate Lane Primary School, St Mary's Catholic Primary School and Featherbank Primary School.
</p><p>Featherbank School opened in 1911 as a primary school, replacing the Grove Day School. The school's <a href="/wiki/Infant_school" title="Infant school">infant department</a> was moved to the Grove Methodist Church on Stanhope Drive in 1933, but in 1960 transferred to the Featherbank School annexe. In 1972 Featherbank juniors (7–11 years) were allocated places at the newly built Newlaithes Junior School, at which point Featherbank became purely an infants' school (4–7 years).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In September 2011 Featherbank reverted to a full primary school.
</p><p>There is an <a href="/wiki/Independent_school_(United_Kingdom)" title="Independent school (United Kingdom)">independent primary school</a>, The Froebelian School.
</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Scouting" title="Scouting">Scout and Guide</a> hut on New Road Side was requisitioned during the Second World War as an emergency mortuary for the factories based around what is now <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds Bradford International Airport">Leeds Bradford Airport</a> (Yeadon Aerodrome at the time), but it was never needed.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Before being purchased by the scouts and guides<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (May 2012)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>, the building was used as a cafe, a popular stop-off on the way out to Otley, Ilkey and the Dales.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nightlife">Nightlife</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Horsforth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Nightlife">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Given its size, Horsforth has a relatively high number of bars and <a href="/wiki/Pub" title="Pub">pubs</a> of varying types from traditional pubs going back to the 17th century to more modern café bars and lounges. Horsforth Town Street has the highest concentration of these with several bars and pubs within a short distance of each other. Other popular night life areas include Station Road and New Road Side.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
