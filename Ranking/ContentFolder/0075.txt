<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cave-Browne-Cave_baronets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was created on 30 June 1641 for Thomas Cave, a <a href="/wiki/Cavalier" title="Cavalier">Royalist</a> who fought in the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cokayne_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cokayne-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Granted lands in South and <a href="/wiki/North_Cave" title="North Cave">North Cave</a> in Yorkshire by <a href="/wiki/William_the_Conqueror" title="William the Conqueror">William the Conqueror</a>, by the fifteenth century the Caves had moved to Stanford on the boundary of Northamptonshire and Leicestershire to become "a wealthy and powerful clan, foremost among the new men of the age, the <i>nouveaux riches</i>, the shrewd, rapacious, grasping gentry raised up by the Tudor dynasty".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Sir Thomas's aunt Eleanor was married to the diplomat Sir <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Roe" title="Thomas Roe">Thomas Roe</a>; his great-grandmother, Margaret, was a sister of <a href="/wiki/William_Cecil,_1st_Baron_Burghley" title="William Cecil, 1st Baron Burghley">William Cecil, 1st Baron Burghley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen Elizabeth I">Queen Elizabeth I</a>'s Lord High Treasurer; and her husband Roger's uncle Sir <a href="/wiki/Ambrose_Cave" title="Ambrose Cave">Ambrose Cave</a> was <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_Duchy_of_Lancaster" title="Chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster">Chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster</a> under Elizabeth. Roger Cave and Margaret had several daughters including Elizabeth who married Walter Bagot of <a href="/wiki/Blithfield_Hall" title="Blithfield Hall">Blithfield Hall</a> in Staffordshire, their son <a href="/wiki/Hervey_Bagot" title="Hervey Bagot">Hervey Bagot</a> being created a baronet by King Charles I;  Margaret (1565-1594) who married <a href="/wiki/William_Skipwith_(died_1610)" title="William Skipwith (died 1610)">William Skipwith</a>; and Dorothy who married Thomas Hartopp of <a href="/wiki/Freeby" title="Freeby">Freeby</a> in Leicestershire.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Descendants">Descendants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cave-Browne-Cave_baronets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Descendants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sir Thomas Cave's son, the second Baronet, was <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Coventry_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Coventry (UK Parliament constituency)">Coventry</a>. His son, the third Baronet, was Member of Parliament for <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Leicestershire (UK Parliament constituency)">Leicestershire</a>. He married the Hon. Margaret, daughter of <a href="/wiki/John_Verney,_1st_Viscount_Fermanagh" title="John Verney, 1st Viscount Fermanagh">John Verney, 1st Viscount Fermanagh</a>, and a descendant of <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Braye,_1st_Baron_Braye" title="Edmund Braye, 1st Baron Braye">Edmund Braye, 1st Baron Braye</a>. Their elder son, the fourth Baronet, died unmarried in 1734 and the baronetcy devolved on his younger brother, who also sat as Member of Parliament for Leicestershire. His elder son, the sixth Baronet, was a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Fellow of the Royal Society</a> and <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff" class="mw-redirect" title="High Sheriff">High Sheriff</a> of <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire" title="Leicestershire">Leicestershire</a>. His son, the seventh Baronet, sat briefly as Member of Parliament for Leicestershire but died childless at an early age. His sister <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Otway-Cave,_3rd_Baroness_Braye" title="Sarah Otway-Cave, 3rd Baroness Braye">Sarah Otway</a>, the sixth Baronet's only daughter, then inherited the family seat of <a href="/wiki/Stanford_Hall,_Leicestershire" title="Stanford Hall, Leicestershire">Stanford Hall, Leicestershire</a>, and in 1839 became the third Baroness Braye when the <a href="/wiki/Abeyance" title="Abeyance">abeyance</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Baron_Braye" title="Baron Braye">barony of Braye</a> was terminated in her favour (see the Baron Braye for further history of this branch of the family). The seventh Baronet was succeeded by his uncle, the eighth Baronet. He was an unmarried clergyman and on his death in 1810 the line of the third Baronet failed.
</p><p>The late Baronet was succeeded by his second cousin, William Cave-Browne, the ninth Baronet. He was the son of John Cave-Browne (who in 1752 had assumed the additional surname of Browne by Act of Parliament), son of Roger Cave, eldest son of the second marriage of the second Baronet, by his wife Catherine, daughter of William Browne of <a href="/wiki/Stretton_en_le_Field" title="Stretton en le Field">Stretton en le Field</a> in Derbyshire. In 1839 the ninth Baronet's assumption of the additional surname of Cave was confirmed by royal licence. He was succeeded by his son, the tenth Baronet. He was <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff" class="mw-redirect" title="High Sheriff">High Sheriff</a> of <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a> in 1844. His son, the eleventh Baronet, was a <a href="/wiki/Deputy_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Deputy Lieutenant">Deputy Lieutenant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_Peace" class="mw-redirect" title="Justice of the Peace">Justice of the Peace</a> for Derbyshire. He was succeeded by his second but only surviving son, the twelfth Baronet. He was initially a soldier and fought in the <a href="/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion" title="Boxer Rebellion">Boxer Rebellion</a> and <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First World War">First World War</a>, but was later ordained. He died childless and was succeeded by his first cousin, the eldest surviving son of the thirteen children of Ambrose Syned Cave-Browne-Cave, younger son of the tenth Baronet. A <a href="/wiki/Captain_(naval)" title="Captain (naval)">Captain</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> who had served at the bombardment of <a href="/wiki/Alexandria" title="Alexandria">Alexandria</a> in 1882,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> the thirteenth Baronet was childless and was succeeded by his younger brother, the fourteenth Baronet. He died in 1943 without surviving male issue and was succeeded by his nephew, the fifteenth Baronet. He was the son of Edward Lambert Cave-Browne-Cave, the fifth son of the aforementioned Ambrose Syned Cave-Browne-Cave. The title is now held by the fifteenth Baronet's grandson, the seventeenth Baronet, who succeeded his father, the sixteenth Baronet, upon the latter's death in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several other members of the family may also be mentioned. <b>Edward Raban Cave-Brown</b> (1835–1907), son of <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant-Colonel" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant-Colonel">Lieutenant-Colonel</a> Edward Cave-Browne, younger brother of the ninth Baronet, was Accountant-General in the <a href="/wiki/India_Office" title="India Office">India Office</a> from 1893 to 1901 and his son <b>William Cave-Browne</b>(1884–1967) was a <a href="/wiki/Major-General" class="mw-redirect" title="Major-General">Major-General</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Engineers" title="Royal Engineers">Royal Engineers</a>; his grandson <b>John Raban Cave-Browne</b> (1917–1989) was a <a href="/wiki/Brigadier" title="Brigadier">Brigadier</a> in the Royal Engineers.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <b>Sir Thomas Cave-Browne-Cave</b> (1835–1924), third son of Thomas Cave-Browne-Cave, third son of the ninth Baronet, was  Deputy Accountant-General of the Army from 1897 to 1900 and a Commissioner of the Royal Hospital Chelsea from 1899 to 1923. His daughters <a href="/wiki/Frances_Cave-Browne-Cave" title="Frances Cave-Browne-Cave">Frances Cave-Browne-Cave</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beatrice_Mabel_Cave-Browne-Cave" title="Beatrice Mabel Cave-Browne-Cave">Beatrice Mabel Cave-Browne-Cave</a>, were mathematicians; his older son, <b>Thomas Reginald Cave-Browne-Cave</b> (1885–1969), was Professor of Engineering at <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southampton" title="University of Southampton">University College, Southampton</a>, from 1931 to 1950 and Director of Camouflage at the Ministry of Home Security from 1941 to 1945; and his younger son, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Cave-Browne-Cave" title="Henry Cave-Browne-Cave">Henry Meyrick Cave-Browne-Cave</a>, was an <a href="/wiki/Air_Vice-Marshal" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Vice-Marshal">Air Vice-Marshal</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a>. <b>Paul Cave</b> (Paul Astley Cave-Browne-Cave) (1917–2010), the eldest great-grandson of the Rev. William Astley Cave-Browne-Cave, the second son of the ninth Baronet, published and edited <i>Hampshire, The County Magazine</i> <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.hampshiremagazine.com/">[1]</a> for over four decades until 2007. A former Fleet Street journalist with the <a href="/wiki/News_Chronicle" title="News Chronicle">News Chronicle</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Daily_Mirror" title="Daily Mirror">Daily Mirror</a>, he was also a theatrical agent who managed the singer <a href="/wiki/Frankie_Vaughan" title="Frankie Vaughan">Frankie Vaughan</a> for some years <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.dailyecho.co.uk/news/8372548.Ex_newsman_was_a_friend_to_the_stars/">[2]</a>. Paul's  brother <b>Anthony Cave-Browne-Cave</b> (1925–2011) was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order" title="Distinguished Service Order">DSO</a> for distinguishing himself in battle against the Japanese in <a href="/wiki/Burma" class="mw-redirect" title="Burma">Burma</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> when still only in his teens.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <b>Phebe Hyacinth Cave-Browne-Cave</b> (1901–80), <a href="/wiki/MBE" class="mw-redirect" title="MBE">MBE</a>, the only child of the fourteenth Baronet, was a <a href="/wiki/Church_Mission_Society" title="Church Mission Society">Church Mission Society</a> missionary in northern Uganda for over half a century until her death.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Heir_presumptive" title="Heir presumptive">heir presumptive</a> is Paul Cave-Browne-Cave (born 1954), sole son of the aforementioned Paul Cave and, as the great-great-great-grandson of the 9th Baronet, the fourth cousin once removed of the 17th Baronet.
</p>
