<p><b>Wilfred Makepeace Lunn</b> (born 1942 in <a href="/wiki/Rastrick" title="Rastrick">Rastrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England) is an English inventor, <a href="/wiki/Theatrical_property" title="Theatrical property">prop maker</a> and TV presenter. He is best known for his regular appearances on the 1960s and 1970s UK children's television show <i><a href="/wiki/Vision_On" title="Vision On">Vision On</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilf_Lunn&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lunn was born in <a href="/wiki/Rastrick" title="Rastrick">Rastrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England to deaf parents. His upbringing was to later allow him to teach <a href="/wiki/Lip-reading" class="mw-redirect" title="Lip-reading">lip-reading</a> as well as religious education at Odsal House School for the Deaf in <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In the first part of this self-made interview video series "My Best Cellar", available through his website, Lunn claims he was "brought up in a cellar by deaf mutes".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The parents of actor <a href="/wiki/James_Mason" title="James Mason">James Mason</a> lived on the same street as Lunn who met Mason when he was visiting them. Mason introduced Lunn to agent Blanche Marvin. Lunn had already written a television play entitled <i>Benny Rolly</i> which, unusually for the time, was without dialogue and Marvin thought it would be of interest to the deaf. She arranged an interview with Patrick Dowling the producer of BBC's <i>Vision On</i> and Lunn took along some of his models to demonstrate. Dowling was unable to employ Lunn but suggested an exhibition of his cycles. On the opening night of the exhibition, which had been arranged by Marvin, TV presenter <a href="/wiki/Joan_Bakewell" title="Joan Bakewell">Joan Bakewell</a> visited and invited Lunn to appear on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Night_Line-Up" title="Late Night Line-Up">Late Night Line-Up</a></i> that night. This was Lunn's first television appearance.<sup id="cite_ref-lunn_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lunn-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lunn was later invited to appear on the children's TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Magpie_(TV_series)" title="Magpie (TV series)">Magpie</a></i> on which he spoke on a variety of subjects including bottles and the history of the domestic smoothing iron. Meanwhile Dowling approached Lunn with a request to make a 'door-bell machine'. Lunn constructed the device and appeared with it on <i>Vision On</i>. Lunn would go to appear on the programme for many years, with <a href="/wiki/Tony_Hart" title="Tony Hart">Tony Hart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sylvester_McCoy" title="Sylvester McCoy">Sylvester McCoy</a>, demonstrating his latest inventions.<sup id="cite_ref-lunn_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lunn-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He later appeared in several other series, including <i><a href="/wiki/Jigsaw_(UK_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jigsaw (UK TV series)">Jigsaw</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Eureka_(UK_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Eureka (UK TV series)">Eureka</a></i>.
</p><p>Lunn is a prolific constructor of novelty bicycles and an inventor of strange devices, amongst which was a collection formerly to be found in the Inventions Bar, <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle upon Tyne</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Lunn has also appeared as an after-dinner speaker for many functions including the Edinburgh School of Architecture Winter School, and the Society of Industrial Artists Golden Jubilee. He has also been a member of the panel of judges for the World Disco Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-lunn_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lunn-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When asked, in an interview with the <a href="/wiki/B3ta" title="B3ta">b3ta</a> website, "In the event of the end of the world, what would be the last thing you'd do before death?" Lunn answered: "I'm constantly being told at the end I would turn to God. I always point out it was your God that made me an atheist. I have been teetotal for fifteen years, so I would turn to drink."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
