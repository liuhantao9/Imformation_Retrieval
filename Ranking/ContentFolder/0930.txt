	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">High Sheriff of West Yorkshire</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>High Sheriff of West Yorkshire</b> is a current <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff" class="mw-redirect" title="High Sheriff">High Sheriff</a> title which has existed since 1974, the holder is changed annually every March.<sup id="cite_ref-highsheriff_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-highsheriff-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> For around 1,000 years the entire area of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> was covered by a single <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_Yorkshire" title="High Sheriff of Yorkshire">High Sheriff of Yorkshire</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> the title was split to cover several newly created counties, including West Yorkshire.
</p><p>Below is a list of the sheriffs.<sup id="cite_ref-highsheriff_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-highsheriff-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
