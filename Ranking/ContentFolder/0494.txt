<p><b>Holmfirth</b> is a small town on the <a href="/wiki/A6024_road" class="mw-redirect" title="A6024 road">A6024</a> Woodhead Road in the <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley" title="Holme Valley">Holme Valley</a>, within the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Metropolitan Borough of Kirklees</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. Centred upon the <a href="/wiki/Confluence" title="Confluence">confluence</a> of the <a href="/wiki/River_Holme" title="River Holme">Holme</a> and Ribble rivers, Holmfirth is 6 miles (9.7&#160;km) south of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and 13 miles (21&#160;km) northeast of <a href="/wiki/Glossop" title="Glossop">Glossop</a>. It mostly consists of stone-built cottages nestled in the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennine hills</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Peak_District_National_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Peak District National Park">Peak District National Park</a> around <a href="/wiki/Holme_Moss" title="Holme Moss">Holme Moss</a> is 4 miles (6.4&#160;km) to the south of the town.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, Holmfirth was once a centre for pioneering film-making by <a href="/wiki/Bamforth_%26_Co_Ltd" title="Bamforth &amp; Co Ltd">Bamforth &amp; Co.</a>, which later switched to the production of saucy seaside postcards. Between 1973 and 2010 both Holmfirth and the Holme Valley became well known as the filming location of the BBC's situation comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Last_of_the_Summer_Wine" title="Last of the Summer Wine">Last of the Summer Wine</a></i>. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <i>Holmfirth</i> derives from <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i>holegn</i> ('holly'), in the name of <a href="/wiki/Holme,_West_Yorkshire" title="Holme, West Yorkshire">Holme, West Yorkshire</a>, compounded with Middle English <i>frith</i> ('wood'). It thus meant 'the woods at Holme'.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town originally grew up around a corn mill and bridge in the 13th century. Three hundred years later Holmfirth expanded rapidly as the growing cloth trade grew and the production of stone and slates from the surrounding quarries increased. The present <a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a> was built in 1778 after the church built in 1476 was swept away in a flood the previous year. In 1850 <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth_railway_station" title="Holmfirth railway station">Holmfirth railway station</a> opened, on the branch line built by the <a href="/wiki/Lancashire_and_Yorkshire_Railway" title="Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway">Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway</a> Company.
</p><p>Local men who served and died in the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First World War">First</a> and <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World Wars</a> are commemorated on the Holme Valley War Memorial found outside Holme Valley Memorial Hospital.
</p>
<h3><span id="Bamforth_.26_Co"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Bamforth_&amp;_Co">Bamforth &amp; Co</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bamforth &amp; Co">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Holmfirth was the home of <a href="/wiki/Bamforth_%26_Co_Ltd" title="Bamforth &amp; Co Ltd">Bamforth &amp; Co Ltd</a>, who were well known for their <a href="/wiki/Postcard#British_seaside_postcards" title="Postcard">cheeky seaside postcards</a> – although around the time of the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First World War">First World War</a>, they produced postcards of a more sober nature. The printing works on Station Road has now been converted into residential flats.
</p><p>Bamforth's company were early pioneers of film-making, before they abandoned the business in favour of postcards.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During the early 1900s Holmfirth was well known for film making;<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> During the periods 1898–1900 and 1913–1915 Bamforth and Co. produced what the <a href="/wiki/British_Film_Institute" title="British Film Institute">British Film Institute</a> describes as 'a modest but historically significant collection of films'.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flooding">Flooding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flooding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth_floods" title="Holmfirth floods">Holmfirth floods</a></div>
<p>There are a number of instances when <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">flooding</a> has occurred in the Holme Valley affecting Holmfirth and other settlements in the valley. The earliest recorded Holmfirth flood was in 1738<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and the most recent was 1944. The most severe flood occurred early on the morning of 5 February 1852, when the embankment of the Bilberry Reservoir collapsed causing the deaths of 81 people. Following a severe storm in 1777 the <a href="/wiki/River_Holme" title="River Holme">River Holme</a> burst its banks, sweeping away people and property with the loss of three lives; the stone church built in 1476, was also swept away. A storm in 1821 again caused the river to burst its banks. The flooding on the night of 29 May 1944 was not nationally reported and it was then overshadowed by the <a href="/wiki/Normandy_landings" title="Normandy landings">D-Day landings</a> the following week.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Holmfirth (and the surrounding countryside) is the setting for the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>'s long-running comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Last_of_the_Summer_Wine" title="Last of the Summer Wine">Last of the Summer Wine</a></i>. Thousands of tourists flock to the area each year to enjoy scenery and locations familiar from the series. Filming of the TV <a href="/wiki/Slaithwaite" title="Slaithwaite">Slaithwaite</a>-based drama, <a href="/wiki/Where_the_Heart_Is_(1997_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Where the Heart Is (1997 TV series)"><i>Where the Heart Is</i></a>, had also taken place in and around the area.
</p><p>The former Lodge's <a href="/wiki/Supermarket" title="Supermarket">supermarket</a> building had been sitting empty in the heart of the town since the <a href="/wiki/Co-op" class="mw-redirect" title="Co-op">Co-op</a> moved to new premises in Crown Bottom. Lodge's was built in the 1970s by the prominent local grocery company. It was opened by <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_1" title="BBC Radio 1">Radio 1</a> <a href="/wiki/Disc_Jockey" class="mw-redirect" title="Disc Jockey">DJ</a> <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blackburn" title="Tony Blackburn">Tony Blackburn</a> and occupied an unusual location over the River Holme beside the town's small bus station. Lodge's was bought in the 1990s by <a href="/wiki/Co-operative_Retail_Services" title="Co-operative Retail Services">Co-operative Retail Services</a> who eventually closed the store down in 1997, after investing in a brand new £2&#160;million supermarket for the town. Local residents, led by the Holme Valley Business Association, campaigned for its demolition. Their campaign was featured in the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> documentary, <i><a href="/wiki/Demolition_(TV_series)" title="Demolition (TV series)">Demolition</a></i>. The building has since been converted into several smaller shops, with some accommodation on the top floor.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Primary_education">Primary education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Primary education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Holmfirth's only primary school is Holmfirth Junior, Infant, and Nursery School located on Cartworth Road, however there are many other primary schools in villages and hamlets closely surrounding Holmfirth. In 2017, 82% of the school's student were achieving the expected standard for their age and 12% were exceeding the expected standard.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Secondary_education">Secondary education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Secondary education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Holmfirth_High_School" title="Holmfirth High School">Holmfirth High School</a> is a coeducational secondary school that takes in students from the local primary schools named above as well as students from neighboring villages and hamlets. The school has over 1,300 pupils, split over five-year groups from years 7 to 11.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Holmfirth's economy is dominated by rural and tourism activities. A 2013 youth survey<sup id="cite_ref-youth_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youth-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> identified reducing opportunities for young adults in the area and an intention to leave to find employment. The survey resulted in a successful bid for lottery funding to create new opportunities and training to increase employment opportunities in the area. Tourism economic activity is increasing with several accommodation and tourist pursuits developing in the town including booking software to manage and market accommodation. New holiday accommodation includes that linked to the new Winery in Cartworth Moor.
</p><p>Longley Farm, founded in 1948, manufacturer of dairy products, is a significant employer in the town.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 6 July 2014, Stage 2 of the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">Tour de France</a>, from York to Sheffield, passed through the town.<sup id="cite_ref-tdf_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tdf-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The event was televised internationally and attracted huge crowds cheering the riders through the town.
Holmfirth Cycling Club was formed in 2013 and, with over 400 members by 2016 became the fastest growing cycling club in the UK.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth_Branch_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Holmfirth Branch Line">Holmfirth Branch Line</a></div>
<p>Holmfirth used to have its own branch line, off the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> to <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> line (commonly referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Penistone_Line" title="Penistone Line">Penistone Line</a>), this short, 2 mile (3&#160;km), line branched from the mainline just south of <a href="/wiki/Brockholes" title="Brockholes">Brockholes</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Viaduct" title="Viaduct">viaduct</a> took the line across the valley and into <a href="/wiki/Thongsbridge" title="Thongsbridge">Thongsbridge</a> where a station was sited. The line then went along the side of the valley coming to a halt just outside the town centre on Station Road. Plans did exist for the line to be extended up the valley and then tunnel under Black Hill to join the <a href="/wiki/Sheffield,_Ashton-under-Lyne_and_Manchester_Railway" title="Sheffield, Ashton-under-Lyne and Manchester Railway">Sheffield to Manchester line</a> near <a href="/wiki/Woodhead,_Derbyshire" title="Woodhead, Derbyshire">Woodhead</a>. The line closed to passengers in 1959, with goods traffic lasting until 1965. The <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth_railway_station" title="Holmfirth railway station">station building and platform</a> still remain as a private house. Other sections of the line further down the valley have been sold off for private housing and the viaduct, crossing the valley from the A616 (New Mill Road), at Brockholes, over Spring Wood, has been demolished.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Buses">Buses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Buses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Holmbs_777.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Holmbs_777.JPG/220px-Holmbs_777.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="139" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Holmbs_777.JPG/330px-Holmbs_777.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Holmbs_777.JPG/440px-Holmbs_777.JPG 2x" data-file-width="614" data-file-height="389" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Holmbs_777.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Holmfirth bus station</div></div></div>
<p>Holmfirth bus station is located in the centre of Holmfirth from which regular bus services take varying routes around the outlying villages and to Huddersfield's bus and railway stations. Additional services run to the town, from <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a> via <a href="/wiki/Denby_Dale" title="Denby Dale">Denby Dale</a> or <a href="/wiki/Penistone" title="Penistone">Penistone</a>. Most services are operated by <a href="/wiki/First_Calderdale_%26_Huddersfield" title="First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield">First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield</a>, using the town's bus station. Weekend and bank holiday services operate to <a href="/wiki/Glossop" title="Glossop">Glossop</a> in north <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a>. In October 2006, First bus services were re-branded as the 'Holmfirth Connection'.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Holmfirth_Choral_Society">Holmfirth Choral Society</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Holmfirth Choral Society">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Holmfirth Choral Society hold regular classical choral music concerts in Holmfirth Civic Hall<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and the Holme Valley Orchestra plays throughout the year.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town is particularly associated with an unusual choral <a href="/wiki/Folk_song" class="mw-redirect" title="Folk song">folk song</a>, known as the <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth_Anthem" title="Holmfirth Anthem">Holmfirth Anthem</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Holmfirth's Film Festival and Festival of Folk are held every May, and its Arts Festival takes place over two weeks in June.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_festival">Film festival</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Film festival">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The town's cinema, the Picturedrome, which opened in 1912 as the Valley Theatre,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> is now a live music venue and has been nominated for the NME Best Small Venue. It hosts various music events. Acts such as <a href="/wiki/Adam_Ant" title="Adam Ant">Adam Ant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bad_Manners" title="Bad Manners">Bad Manners</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buzzcocks" title="Buzzcocks">Buzzcocks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evile" title="Evile">Evile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fish_(singer)" title="Fish (singer)">Fish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Half_Man_Half_Biscuit" title="Half Man Half Biscuit">Half Man Half Biscuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawkwind" title="Hawkwind">Hawkwind</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Martyn" title="John Martyn">John Martyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ocean_Colour_Scene" title="Ocean Colour Scene">Ocean Colour Scene</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chilli_Pipers" title="Red Hot Chilli Pipers">Red Hot Chilli Pipers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ron_Sexsmith" title="Ron Sexsmith">Ron Sexsmith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saxon_(band)" title="Saxon (band)">Saxon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suzi_Quatro" title="Suzi Quatro">Suzi Quatro</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Beat_(British_band)" title="The Beat (British band)">the Beat</a> have performed.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Art_week">Art week</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Art week">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Holmfirth Art Week, with its July exhibition in the Civic Hall, raises money for <a href="/wiki/Macmillan_Cancer_Support" title="Macmillan Cancer Support">Macmillan Cancer Relief</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Holmfirth_Festival_of_Folk">Holmfirth Festival of Folk</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Holmfirth Festival of Folk">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Holmfirth Festival of Folk takes place in May of each year, featuring a wide selection of <a href="/wiki/Folk_Music" class="mw-redirect" title="Folk Music">Folk Music</a> and <a href="/wiki/Folk_Dance" class="mw-redirect" title="Folk Dance">Folk Dance</a> acts from around the UK. Performances take place in a variety of indoor and outdoor venues throughout the town.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Holmfirth_Arts_Festival">Holmfirth Arts Festival</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Holmfirth Arts Festival">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Holmfirth Arts Festival is a multi-arts festival which celebrates Creativity, Ideas, People and Landscape in the Holme Valley. Its ticketed, community engagement, outdoor arts and arts in the landscape programmes take place throughout the year, culminating in an annual four day festival on the second weekend in June.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brass_bands">Brass bands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Brass bands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Holme Valley <a href="/wiki/Brass_Band" class="mw-redirect" title="Brass Band">Brass Band</a> Contest takes place each year at the civic hall.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Surrounding_villages">Surrounding villages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Holmfirth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Surrounding villages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Holmfirth constitutes a town of its own almost seven&#160;miles (11&#160;km) south of the larger town of Huddersfield. While the town of Holmfirth itself is comparatively small, it is surrounded by several <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(place)" title="Hamlet (place)">hamlets</a> and villages. These neighbouring settlements are often collectively referred to as "Holmfirth" and include:- <a href="/wiki/Austonley" title="Austonley">Austonley</a>, Arrunden, Burnlee, Cinderhills, Cliff, Deanhouse, Netherthong, Gully, Flushhouse, <a href="/wiki/Hade_Edge" title="Hade Edge">Hade Edge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thongsbridge" title="Thongsbridge">Thongsbridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upperthong" title="Upperthong">Upperthong</a> and Washpit. Many of these are located on Cartworth Moor.
</p><p>Other villages and hamlets within the Holmfirth <a href="/wiki/Post_town" title="Post town">post town</a> include:- <a href="/wiki/Brockholes" title="Brockholes">Brockholes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fulstone" class="mw-redirect" title="Fulstone">Fulstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jackson_Bridge" title="Jackson Bridge">Jackson Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hepworth,_West_Yorkshire" title="Hepworth, West Yorkshire">Hepworth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holme,_West_Yorkshire" title="Holme, West Yorkshire">Holme</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holmbridge" title="Holmbridge">Holmbridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honley" title="Honley">Honley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meltham" title="Meltham">Meltham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherthong" title="Netherthong">Netherthong</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Mill,_West_Yorkshire" title="New Mill, West Yorkshire">New Mill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scholes,_Holme_Valley" title="Scholes, Holme Valley">Scholes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Totties,_Holmfirth" class="mw-redirect" title="Totties, Holmfirth">Totties</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thongsbridge" title="Thongsbridge">Thongsbridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upperthong" title="Upperthong">Upperthong</a>, Longley, Hade Edge, Underbank and <a href="/wiki/Wooldale" title="Wooldale">Wooldale</a>.
