	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Saint Thomas Christian names</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>

The <b><a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas_Christians" title="Saint Thomas Christians">Saint Thomas Christians</a></b> of <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">Kerala</a>, in south-west <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, have unique <a href="/wiki/Naming_convention" title="Naming convention">naming conventions</a>. Also known as <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Malabar_Nasrani" class="mw-redirect" title="Syrian Malabar Nasrani">Syrian Malabar Nasranis</a>, they trace their origins to the evangelistic activity of <a href="/wiki/Thomas_the_Apostle" title="Thomas the Apostle">Thomas the Apostle</a> in the 1st century.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Their naming conventions differ from both members of other religions in India and Christians in other regions.
</p><p>Saint Thomas Christian names are <a href="/wiki/Biblical" class="mw-redirect" title="Biblical">biblical</a> in origin and passed on from one generation to the next. Hence male names are usually <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> and female names are usually <a href="/wiki/Matronymic" title="Matronymic">matronymic</a>. That is, usually a person’s name will include the names of their parents and grandparents, or that of a close blood relative.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> These names will not include the names of saints, other religious figures, political leaders or foreign names<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. (September 2019)">vague</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. Family names are also included<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. (September 2019)">vague</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. Unlike Indian Christians of other denominations, foreign<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Vagueness" title="Wikipedia:Vagueness"><span title="This information is too vague. (September 2019)">vague</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> family names are absolutely irrelevant for Saint Thomas Christians as they embraced Christianity long before the arrival of European missionaries in India. So a Nasrani can easily be identified by name, from among other Christians.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Even today, this pattern of giving name is visible in almost all <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas_Christian_Churches" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Thomas Christian Churches">Syrian Christian denominations</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before the arrival of the Portuguese in May 1498, Saint Thomas Christians of Kerala were known to belong to the <i>Margam</i>, a word when translated is ‘The Way’.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1599, Catholic Archbishop Alexio-de-Menezes called a synod at Udayamperoor, in which Christians in and near the kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Cochin" class="mw-redirect" title="Cochin">Cochin</a> attended.  Here he decreed that St. Thomas Christians should abandon their ancient naming conventions. They were specifically told not to use the name <i>Easow</i> because it was  holy.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  But, the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas_Christians" title="Saint Thomas Christians">Saint Thomas Christians</a> ignored this command and had continued with their ancient customs. Even today they do follow this method of naming. Even the Nasranis that did convert to Catholicism (Syrian Catholics) still use the "old margam" names. The Latin names are shunned
</p><p>During the 20th century some names were created by joining two or more syllables. For example, Abey (AB), Aji (AG), Bibi (BB), Biji (BG), Jessy (JC) and so on. Today, several Syrian Christians name their children by Indian names like <a href="/wiki/Deepak" title="Deepak">Deepak</a>, Rahul, Neethu, <a href="/wiki/Asha" title="Asha">Asha</a> etc. But by the 21st century more biblical names began to reappear. Thus names like, <a href="/wiki/Isaac" title="Isaac">Isaac</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joshua" title="Joshua">Joshua</a>, <a href="/wiki/David" title="David">David</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saul" title="Saul">Saul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ezekiel" title="Ezekiel">Ezekiel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Timothy_(name)" class="mw-redirect" title="Timothy (name)">Timothy</a>, appeared on the scene. Generally they still follow the system detailed below.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Standard_form_of_a_name">Standard form of a name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Standard form of a name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A name will include the baptismal name (generally the person is known by that name ) and the name of the father. The practice of appending the first name of father to the child's name instead of family name is also followed by <a href="/wiki/Hindus" title="Hindus">Hindus</a> of <a href="/wiki/South_India" title="South India">South India</a>. Examples are given below.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Male_names">Male names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Male names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Their names traditionally have a threefold structure.
</p>
<dl><dd><dl><dd>Family or house name – Father’s name – Baptismal name</dd></dl></dd></dl>
<p>The first two are usually abbreviated to initials.
</p><p>Another form is that the name will include the baptismal name (generally the person is called by that name) and the name of his father. The practice of appending the first name of father to the child's name instead of family name is also followed by <a href="/wiki/Hindus" title="Hindus">Hindus</a> of <a href="/wiki/South_India" title="South India">South India</a>. Examples are given below.
</p><p>As an example, the name, Thomas Mathew is similar to <i>Shimon bar Jona</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> which means <i>Shimon son of Jona</i>. In the same way, Thomas Mathew means <i>Thomas</i> son of <i>Mathew</i>. Thomas Mathew is to be addressed as Thomas and not by his father’s name.
</p>
<dl><dd><i>Here the correct spelling is ‘’’Mathew’’’ and not ‘’’Matthew’’’ as in English.</i></dd></dl>
<p>Another form of name is Nikhil Thomas Mathew where Nikhil is a name chosen by the parents and they usually call him by that name, Thomas is the biblical and baptismal name and Mathew is his father's name.
</p><p>Their bishop receives a new name on consecration. This also has a threefold structure.
</p>
<dl><dd><dl><dd>His baptismal name (Sometimes in Syriac form) – the title Mar (in East syriac form) or Mor ( in west Syriac form) – an Episcopal title ( a Biblical name or the name of a Christian father).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl></dd></dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Female_names">Female names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Female names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As an example, the name, Rachel Mathew, means Rachel daughter of Mathew. After marriage, father’s name is replaced by the husband’s name. Rachel Mathew is to be addressed as Rachel and not by her father’s name.
</p><p>Another naming pattern is Anita Rachel Mathew where Anita is a formal given name chosen by the parents, Rachel is the biblical and baptismal name and Mathew is father's name. The given first names can be of any origin and many Syrian Christians give Indian names like Neethu, Deepa etc. to their children.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_of_initials">Use of initials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Use of initials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When initials are used, abbreviations of the family name or house name (name of the plot where the parents of the child live at the time of birth) and the father’s name are given before the <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>. For example, the name P.M. Thomas means Palakkappilly (family name), Mathew’s (father’s name) son, Thomas (<a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>). His sister’s name will be P.M. Rachel.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_forms">Other forms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other forms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When family name or house name need to be used, it comes first followed by the <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>. As an example, T.M. Thomas is, THEETAKATTIL Thomas Mathew or THEETAKATTIL T.M.Thomas.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hypocoristic">Hypocoristic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Hypocoristic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hypocoristic" class="mw-redirect" title="Hypocoristic">Hypocoristic</a> (Pet names) are often used in a familiar and friendly manner in informal situations. In more formal situations, the <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> is to be used instead. This alludes to the fact that using a person's pet name betokens familiarity.
Pet names for Syrian Christians can be Hindu, Assyrian, Persian or Biblical in origin.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Order_in_giving_names">Order in giving names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Order in giving names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="For_boys">For boys</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: For boys">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Male names  are <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a>.
</p>
<dl><dd>The first born is given the name of his paternal grandfather.</dd>
<dd>The second born is given the name of his maternal grandfather.</dd>
<dd>The third born is given the name of one of his uncles.</dd></dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="For_girls">For girls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: For girls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Female names are <a href="/wiki/Matronymic" title="Matronymic">matronymic</a>.
</p>
<dl><dd>The first born is given the name of her paternal grandmother.</dd>
<dd>The second born is given the name of her maternal grandmother.</dd>
<dd>The third born is given the name of one of her aunts.</dd></dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Changes">Changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The last name (father’s name) changes with each generation.  The family name would also change if  members who move out of their <a href="/wiki/Consanguineal" class="mw-redirect" title="Consanguineal">consanguineal</a> family homes with the changing ownership of property upon the death of the patriarch decides to adopt a new name. However, several families claim that they are ancient and their family names have remained unchanged for centuries.
</p><p>The Syrian Christians who have migrated to Western nations tend to choose surnames which can either be the family name or the father's name will be used as a surname.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Common_names">Common names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Thomas_Christian_names&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Common names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The mother tongue of Nasranis is Malayalam. So the names given in the following lists are in <a href="/wiki/Phonetic_spelling" class="mw-redirect" title="Phonetic spelling">phonetic spelling</a>. But the first one, given under ‘’Other names,’’ shows how that name is usually written in English. The two lists include the names of a few common names that are in use, and they are not comprehensive. To know the correct pronunciation of these names, see <a href="/wiki/Malayalam_script" title="Malayalam script">Malayalam script</a> or <a href="/wiki/Tiberian_vocalization" title="Tiberian vocalization">Tiberian vocalization</a>.
</p>
