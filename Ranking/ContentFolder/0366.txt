<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom_and_some_other_English-speaking_countries">United Kingdom and some other English-speaking countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecclesiastical_titles_and_styles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United Kingdom and some other English-speaking countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Forms_of_address_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Forms of address in the United Kingdom">Forms of address in the United Kingdom</a></div>
<p>The major difference between U.S. practice and that in several other English-speaking countries is the form of address for archbishops and bishops. In Britain and countries whose Roman Catholic usage it directly influenced:
</p>
<ul><li>Archbishop: <i>the Most Reverend</i> (<i>Most Rev.</i>); addressed as <i>Your Grace</i> rather than <i>His Excellency</i> or <i>Your Excellency</i>.</li>
<li>Bishop: "the Right Reverend" (<i>Rt. Rev.</i>); formally addressed as <i>My Lord</i> rather than <i>Your Excellency</i>. This style is an ancient one, and has been used in the western church for more than a thousand years; it corresponds to, but does not derive from, the Italian <i><a href="/wiki/Monsignore" class="mw-redirect" title="Monsignore">Monsignore</a></i> and the French <i><a href="/wiki/Monseigneur" title="Monseigneur">Monseigneur</a></i>. However, most bishops prefer to be addressed simply as <i>Bishop</i> (<i>Bp.</i>).</li></ul>
<p>In Ireland, and in other countries whose Roman Catholic usage it influenced, all bishops, not archbishops alone, are titled <i>the Most Reverend</i> (<i>Most Rev.</i>).
</p><p>Clergy are often referred to with the title <i>Doctor</i> (<i>Dr.</i>), or have <i>D.D. (Doctor of Divinity)</i> placed after their name, where justified by their possession of such degree.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecclesiastical_titles_and_styles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Similar to, and the source of, most of the U.S. English titles, with some variation:
</p>
<ul><li>Diocesan priest: <i>The Reverend Diocesan</i> (<i>Rev.do.</i>); <i>Don</i>.</li>
<li>Religious priest: <i>Padre</i>; <i>Father</i> (<i>Fr.</i>).</li>
<li>Religious sister: <i>The Reverend Sister</i> (<i>Rev. Sr.</i>).</li>
<li>(Permanent) Deacon: <i>the Reverend Deacon</i> (<i>Rev. Dcn.</i>); may be addressed as <i>Father</i> (<i>Fr.</i>).</li></ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Philippines">The Philippines</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecclesiastical_titles_and_styles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: The Philippines">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the predominantly <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_the_Philippines" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism in the Philippines">Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, ecclesiastical address is adapted from American usage. The titles listed below are only used in the most formal occasions by media or official correspondences, save for the simpler forms of address. Post-nominals that indicate educational attainment or membership in a religious order are usually included.
</p>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a> is always given the title <i>Ang Kaniyáng Kabanalan</i> (<a href="/wiki/Filipino_language" title="Filipino language">Filipino</a> for <a href="/wiki/His_Holiness" title="His Holiness">His Holiness</a>). As such, the Pope is styled, <i>Ang Kaniyáng Kabanalan Papa Francisco</i></li>
<li>A cardinal is formally styled and addressed as <i>Ang Kaniyáng Kabunyian</i> (literally, His Illustriousness; <a href="/wiki/Philippine_English" title="Philippine English">English</a>: <a href="/wiki/His_Eminence" title="His Eminence">His Eminence</a>). Cardinals are informally addressed as "Cardinal &lt;name&gt;". For example, <i>Cardinal Juan</i>. Unlike in the US or Commonwealth countries, a cardinal's name is always written or printed as "&lt;first name&gt; Cardinal &lt;last name&gt;". For example, <i>Juan Cardinal de la Cruz.</i></li>
<li>A bishop is formally titled <i>Ang Mahál na Obispo</i> (<a href="/wiki/Excellency" title="Excellency">His Excellency</a>, the Bishop), and more commonly as <i>Ang Lubháng Kagalanggalang</i> ("The <a href="/wiki/Most_Reverend" class="mw-redirect" title="Most Reverend">Most Reverend</a> &lt;name&gt;"). In the same way, an archbishop is similarly titled <i>Ang Mahál na Arsobispo</i> (His Excellency, the Archbishop). They are often addressed as "Bishop/Archbishop &lt;name&gt;". For example, <i>Bishop Juan de la Cruz</i> or <i>Archbishop Juan de la Cruz.</i></li>
<li>A <a href="/wiki/Monsignor" title="Monsignor">monsignor</a> is formally styled <i>Reberendo Monsenyor</i> (Reverend Monsignor) although if the monsignor holds extra administrative office, he is styled appropriate to his office. Vicars General, <a href="/wiki/Vicar_forane" class="mw-redirect" title="Vicar forane">Forane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_Vicar" class="mw-redirect" title="Episcopal Vicar">Episcopal</a> are styled <i>Very Reverend.</i> Monsignori are colloquially addressed as "Monsignor" (abbreviated "Msgr."). As defined, the written form of address for such a priest is <i>Monsignor (first name) (last name)</i> or <i>The Reverend Monsignor (first name) (last name)</i>, while the spoken form of address is <i>Monsignor (last name)</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESecretary_of_State2000_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTESecretary_of_State2000-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Priests, both diocesan and those belonging to a religious order, are formally titled <i>Reberendo Padre</i> (Reverend Father, abbreviated Rev. Fr.) before their given name and surname. Priests are colloquially addressed <i>Father</i> (abbreviated Fr.) before their name, which may either be a formal name or nickname.</li>
<li>Deacons are formally titled <i>Reberendo</i> (Reverend). For example, <i>Reverend Juan de la Cruz</i>. In the country, deacons are rarely titled  "Deacon &lt;name&gt;" as in the United States. Instead, they are colloquially addressed as <i>Rev</i> as opposed to priests who are addressed as <i>Father.</i></li>
<li>Consecrated persons have different styles and addresses.
<ul><li>Religious sisters are titled as <i>Sister</i> (abbreviated Sr.). Superiors are optionally called <i>Mother</i> (abbreviated Mo.) and are usually addressed formally with the title <i>Reverend Sister/Mother</i> (abbreviated Rev. Sr./Mo.) For example, <i>Rev. Sr. Juana de la Cruz, OP</i>  or <i>Rev. Mo. Juana de la Cruz, OSB.</i> Contemplative nuns are formally and colloquially called <i>Sor</i> (short for Soror which is Latin for Sister). Prioresses and Abbesses are formally addressed as <i>Reverend Mother.</i></li>
<li>Religious brothers who aren't priests are titled and addressed as <i>Brother</i> (abbreviated Br.). For example, <i>Br. Juan de la Cruz, OFM.</i> And like their female counterparts, contemplative monks are addressed and styled as <i>Fra</i> (short for Frater which is Latin for Brother). Sometimes, monks who are priests are also called <i>Fra.</i></li></ul></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_Catholic_clergy">Eastern Catholic clergy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecclesiastical_titles_and_styles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Eastern Catholic clergy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although the styles and titles of Eastern Catholic clergy varies from language to language, in the Greek and Arabic-speaking world the following would be acceptable, but is by no means a full list of appropriate titles. It is notable that surnames are never used except in extra-ecclesial matters or to specify a particular person where many share one Christian name or ordination name. Where not noted, Western titles may be supposed. The following are common in Greek Melkite Catholic usage and in Greek Orthodox usage in the United States.
</p><p>Bishop / Archbishop: In Arabic, a bishop is styled "Sayedna," while in Syriac-tradition churches, he is styled "<a href="/wiki/Mar" class="mw-redirect" title="Mar">Mar</a>". If an Eastern Catholic Archbishop or Patriarch is made a Cardinal by the Pope, he may be addressed as "His Eminence" or use the hybrid title "His Beatitude and Eminence". 
</p><p>Priest: In Arabic, "Abouna", and in Greek "Pappas".
</p><p>Deacon: Identical to a priest in all ways except "Father Deacon" is also heard ("Abouna Shammas" or "Pappas Diakonos").
</p><p>Subdeacon: Reverend Subdeacon in written address, but the Christian name with or without "Brother" is usually used, except some traditions where "Father Subdeacon" is used. In Arabic, this is confused by the word "Shammas" being used for both the subdeaconate and the deaconate, the distinction being a "Deacon of the Letter" and a "Deacon of the Gospel," respectively. Often a Deacon will be addressed as "Father" and the subdeacon as "Brother" to make the distinction clear.
</p><p>Reader: Readers are addressed as "Reader" or "Brother" depending on the preference of the addresser.
</p><p>Seminarians: "Brother" or "Brother Seminarian" is the most common title; the appellation "Father Seminarian" or "Father Student" is not seen outside of rural Greek and Arabic-speaking laity.
</p><p>Tonsured individuals of no title: Brother.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_Orthodox_Church">Eastern Orthodox Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecclesiastical_titles_and_styles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Eastern Orthodox Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Slovo4.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Slovo4.jpg/220px-Slovo4.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Slovo4.jpg/330px-Slovo4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Slovo4.jpg/440px-Slovo4.jpg 2x" data-file-width="777" data-file-height="583" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Slovo4.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An Eastern Orthodox priest blesses his congregation at the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/Divine_Liturgy" title="Divine Liturgy">Divine Liturgy</a></div></div></div>
<p>Usage varies somewhat throughout the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church#Eastern_Orthodox_churches_in_communion" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox Communion</a>, and not every church uses every clerical rank. Surnames are typically not used for archpastors (rank of bishop or above) or monastics.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Ecumenical_Patriarch" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecumenical Patriarch">Ecumenical Patriarch</a> of <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>: Ecumenical Patriarch John II, His All-Holiness, Your All-Holiness</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Patriarch" title="Patriarch">Patriarch</a>: Patriarch John II of Terirem, Patriarch John, His Beatitude, Your Beatitude
<ul><li><i>Note:</i> Some Patriarchs use the honorific "His/Your Holiness"</li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Archbishop" title="Archbishop">Archbishop</a>
<ul><li>of an independent Church: The Most Reverend (Rev.) Archbishop John of Terirem, Archbishop John, His Beatitude, Your Beatitude</li>
<li>of a sub-national Church: The Most Reverend (Rev.) Archbishop John of Terirem, Archbishop John, His Eminence, Your Eminence</li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_bishop" title="Metropolitan bishop">Metropolitan</a>: The Most Reverend (Rev.) Metropolitan John of Terirem, Metropolitan John, His Eminence, Your Eminence
<ul><li>Titular Metropolitan: The Most Reverend (Rev.) Metropolitan John of Terirem, His Excellency, Your Excellency</li>
<li><i>Note:</i> Some Metropolitans use the style "The Very Most Reverend" (V. Most Rev.)</li>
<li><i>Note:</i> A Metropolitan who is the head of an independent Church is addressed as "Beatitude" rather than "Excellency"</li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">Bishop</a>: The Right Reverend (Rt. Rev.) Bishop John of Terirem, Bishop John, His Grace, Your Grace
<ul><li>Titular/Auxiliary Bishop: <i>same as for Bishops, above</i></li>
<li><i>Other Languages:</i> Sayedna (Arabic), Despota (Greek), Vladika (Russian, Serbian)</li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">Priest</a> (<a href="/wiki/Presbyter" title="Presbyter">Presbyter</a>): The Reverend Father (Rev. Fr.) John Smith, Father John
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Protopriest" title="Protopriest">Protopriest</a>: The Very Reverend (V. Rev.) Protopriest John Smith, Father (Fr.) John</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Archpriest" title="Archpriest">Archpriest</a>: The Very Reverend (V. Rev.) Archpriest John Smith, Father (Fr.) John</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Archimandrite" title="Archimandrite">Archimandrite</a>: The Very Reverend (V. Rev.) Archimandrite John, or The Right Reverend (Rt. Rev.) Archimandrite John, Father John</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Protestantism">Protestantism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecclesiastical_titles_and_styles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Protestantism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lutheranism">Lutheranism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecclesiastical_titles_and_styles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Lutheranism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Str%C3%A4ngn%C3%A4s_Cathedral_interior.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Str%C3%A4ngn%C3%A4s_Cathedral_interior.jpg/220px-Str%C3%A4ngn%C3%A4s_Cathedral_interior.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Str%C3%A4ngn%C3%A4s_Cathedral_interior.jpg/330px-Str%C3%A4ngn%C3%A4s_Cathedral_interior.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Str%C3%A4ngn%C3%A4s_Cathedral_interior.jpg/440px-Str%C3%A4ngn%C3%A4s_Cathedral_interior.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2448" data-file-height="3264" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Str%C3%A4ngn%C3%A4s_Cathedral_interior.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Lutheran priest of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Sweden" title="Church of Sweden">Church of Sweden</a> prepares for the celebration of <a href="/wiki/Mass_(liturgy)" title="Mass (liturgy)">Mass</a> in <a href="/wiki/Str%C3%A4ngn%C3%A4s_Cathedral" title="Strängnäs Cathedral">Strängnäs Cathedral</a>.</div></div></div>
<ul><li>Archbishops/Presiding Bishops: <i>the Most Reverend</i> (<i>Most Rev.</i>); <i>Archbishop</i> (<i>Abp.</i>; <i>Arch.</i>; <i>Archbp.</i>)/<i>Presiding Bishop</i> (<i>P.B.</i>).</li>
<li>Bishops: <i>Bishop</i> (<i>Bp.</i>); <i>Reverend Bishop</i> (<i>Rev. Bp.</i>); <i>the Right Reverend</i> (<i>Rt. Rev.</i>).</li>
<li>Pastors: <i>the Reverend</i> (<i>Rev.</i>); <i>Pastor</i> (<i>Pr.</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-Peterson2013_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Peterson2013-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Deacons: <i>Deacon</i> (<i>Dcn.</i>).</li>
<li>Vicars: <i>Vicar</i> (<i>Vic.</i>).</li>
<li>Seminarians: <i>the Reverend Seminarian</i> (<i>Rev. Sem.</i>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ecclesiastical" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecclesiastical">Ecclesiastical</a> Doctors (Dr. eccl.)<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>, e.g., Ph.D. (Doctor of Philosophy), Dr. sc. rel. (Doctor of Religious Sciences/Studies),  Dr. mph. (Doctor of <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a> <a href="/wiki/Metaphysics" title="Metaphysics">Metaphysics</a>), Dr. sc. bs. (Doctor of <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Biblical</a> Studies), et al.: <i>Reverend Doctor</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anglicanism">Anglicanism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecclesiastical_titles_and_styles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Anglicanism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Deacons" class="mw-redirect" title="Deacons">Deacons</a> are styled as <i>The Reverend</i>, <i>The Reverend Deacon</i>, or <i>The Reverend Mr/Mrs/Miss</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Priest" title="Priest">Priests</a> are usually styled as <i>The Reverend</i>, <i>The Reverend Father/Mother</i> (even if not a <a href="/wiki/Religious_(Catholicism)" class="mw-redirect" title="Religious (Catholicism)">religious</a>; abbreviated Fr/Mthr) or <i>The Reverend Mr/Mrs/Miss</i>.</li>
<li>Heads of some women's <a href="/wiki/Religious_orders" class="mw-redirect" title="Religious orders">religious orders</a> are styled as <i>The Reverend Mother</i> (even if not ordained).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Canon_(priest)" title="Canon (priest)">Canons</a> are often styled as <i>The Reverend Canon</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dean_(religion)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dean (religion)">Deans</a> are usually styled as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Very_Reverend" title="The Very Reverend">The Very Reverend</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Archdeacon" title="Archdeacon">Archdeacons</a> are usually styled as <i><a href="/wiki/Venerable" class="mw-redirect" title="Venerable">The Venerable</a></i> (<i>The Ven</i>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Prior" title="Prior">Priors</a> of monasteries may be styled as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Very_Reverend" title="The Very Reverend">The Very Reverend</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Abbot" title="Abbot">Abbots</a> of monasteries may be styled as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Right_Reverend" title="The Right Reverend">The Right Reverend</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">Bishops</a> are styled as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Right_Reverend" title="The Right Reverend">The Right Reverend</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Lord#Ecclesiastical" title="Lord">His Lordship</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Nathan1927_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nathan1927-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Archbishop" title="Archbishop">Archbishops</a> and <a href="/wiki/Primate_(bishop)" title="Primate (bishop)">primates</a> and (for historical reasons) the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Meath_and_Kildare" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishop of Meath and Kildare">Bishop of Meath and Kildare</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> are styled as <i><a href="/wiki/The_Most_Reverend" title="The Most Reverend">The Most Reverend</a></i>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Canterbury" title="Archbishop of Canterbury">Archbishop of Canterbury</a> is styled as <i><a href="/wiki/Grace_(style)" title="Grace (style)">His Grace</a></i>.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Methodism">Methodism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecclesiastical_titles_and_styles&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Methodism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a href="/wiki/File:MethodistPastorvestedwithpreachingbands.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/MethodistPastorvestedwithpreachingbands.jpg/350px-MethodistPastorvestedwithpreachingbands.jpg" decoding="async" width="350" height="196" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/MethodistPastorvestedwithpreachingbands.jpg/525px-MethodistPastorvestedwithpreachingbands.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/MethodistPastorvestedwithpreachingbands.jpg/700px-MethodistPastorvestedwithpreachingbands.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="561" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:MethodistPastorvestedwithpreachingbands.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A <a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodist</a> pastor wearing a <a href="/wiki/Cassock" title="Cassock">cassock</a>, vested with a <a href="/wiki/Surplice" title="Surplice">surplice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stole_(vestment)" title="Stole (vestment)">stole</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Preaching_bands" class="mw-redirect" title="Preaching bands">preaching bands</a> attached to his <a href="/wiki/Clerical_collar" title="Clerical collar">clerical collar</a></div></div></div>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Deacons" class="mw-redirect" title="Deacons">Deacons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elder_(Methodist)" title="Elder (Methodist)">Ordained Elders</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Methodist_local_preacher" title="Methodist local preacher">Licensed ministers/priesters</a> are addressed as <i>Reverend</i>, unless they hold a doctorate, in which case they are often addressed in formal situations as <i>The Reverend Doctor</i>. <i>The Reverend</i>, however, is used in more formal or in written communication, in addition to <i>His/Her Reverence</i> or <i>Your Reverence</i>. In informal situations <i>Reverend</i> is used.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Bishops" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishops">Bishops</a> are styled as <i>Bishop</i> or <i><a href="/wiki/Grace_(style)" title="Grace (style)">Your Grace</a></i>.</li>
<li>Religious brothers and sisters are styled as <i>Br.</i> or <i>Sr.</i>; for example, if their name was <i>John Smith</i> and they belonged to a <a href="/wiki/Religious_order" title="Religious order">religious order</a>, they would be addressed as <i>Brother John Smith</i>.</li></ul>
