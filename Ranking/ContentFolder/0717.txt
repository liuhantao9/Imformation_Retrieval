<p><i><b>The League of Gentlemen</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Television_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Television in the United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy" title="Comedy">comedy</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_series" class="mw-redirect" title="Television series">television series</a> that premiered on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a> in 1999. The show is set in <a href="/wiki/Royston_Vasey" title="Royston Vasey">Royston Vasey</a>, a fictional town in <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a>, originally based on <a href="/wiki/Alston,_Cumbria" title="Alston, Cumbria">Alston, Cumbria</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and follows the lives of <a href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_The_League_of_Gentlemen" class="mw-redirect" title="List of characters in The League of Gentlemen">bizarre characters</a>, most of whom are played by three of the show's four writers—<a href="/wiki/Mark_Gatiss" title="Mark Gatiss">Mark Gatiss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Pemberton" title="Steve Pemberton">Steve Pemberton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Reece_Shearsmith" title="Reece Shearsmith">Reece Shearsmith</a>—who, along with <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Dyson" title="Jeremy Dyson">Jeremy Dyson</a>, formed the League of Gentlemen comedy troupe in 1995. The series originally aired for three series from 1999 until 2002 followed by a film in 2005. A three-part revival mini-series was broadcast in December 2017 to celebrate the 20th anniversary of the group's first appearance on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a>.
</p><p>The series was filmed mainly in <a href="/wiki/Hadfield,_Derbyshire" title="Hadfield, Derbyshire">Hadfield, Derbyshire</a>; other locations include <a href="/wiki/Glossop" title="Glossop">Glossop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gamesley" title="Gamesley">Gamesley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hope_Valley,_Derbyshire" title="Hope Valley, Derbyshire">Hope Valley</a> in <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a>; <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Todmorden" title="Todmorden">Todmorden</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Mottram_in_Longdendale" title="Mottram in Longdendale">Mottram</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-comedymap_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-comedymap-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The series ended in 2002, and was followed by a film <i><a href="/wiki/The_League_of_Gentlemen%27s_Apocalypse" title="The League of Gentlemen&#39;s Apocalypse">The League of Gentlemen's Apocalypse</a></i> and a stage production <i><a href="/wiki/The_League_of_Gentlemen_Are_Behind_You!" title="The League of Gentlemen Are Behind You!">The League of Gentlemen Are Behind You!</a></i> in 2005. Shearsmith and Pemberton later collaborated to create another dark comedy series, <i><a href="/wiki/Psychoville" title="Psychoville">Psychoville</a></i> (2009); Mark Gatiss appeared in one episode. The three also performed together in the fourth series of <i><a href="/wiki/Horrible_Histories_(2009_TV_series)" title="Horrible Histories (2009 TV series)">Horrible Histories</a></i>, in which they play American film producers who hear <a href="/wiki/Pitch_(filmmaking)" title="Pitch (filmmaking)">movie pitches</a> from historical figures.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Shearsmith and Pemberton also wrote and starred in the black comedy <a href="/wiki/Anthology_series" title="Anthology series">anthology series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Inside_No._9" title="Inside No. 9">Inside No. 9</a></i>, which premiered on BBC Two in 2014.
</p><p>The BBC announced in August 2017 that three new episodes would be produced to commemorate the show's 20th anniversary.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> They aired on BBC2 on 18,
19 and 20 December 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_League_of_Gentlemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three of the Four members of the league of gentlemen  (Steve Pemberton, Mark Gattis and Reece Shearsmith) met at Bretton Hall theatre school. They met their final member- Jeremy Dyson- later in their comedy career. He does not act as such in the franchise but does have a few small/ cameo roles throughout the series.
The stage show began in late 1994 and it was not long before the team took as their name the title of a <a href="/wiki/Jack_Hawkins" title="Jack Hawkins">Jack Hawkins</a> movie, <i><a href="/wiki/The_League_of_Gentlemen_(film)" title="The League of Gentlemen (film)">The League of Gentlemen</a></i>.  In 1997 they were awarded the <a href="/wiki/Perrier_Comedy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Perrier Comedy Award">Perrier award</a> for comedy at the <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Festival_Fringe" title="Edinburgh Festival Fringe">Edinburgh Festival Fringe</a> and their <a href="/wiki/Radio_programming" title="Radio programming">radio series</a> <i>On the Town with The League of Gentlemen</i>, debuted on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_4" title="BBC Radio 4">BBC Radio 4</a>.  <i>On the Town</i> was set in the fictional town of Spent.  They won a <a href="/wiki/Sony_Radio_Academy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Sony Radio Academy Awards">Sony Award</a> for this six-episode run. In 1999 the show moved to <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a> and quickly acquired a <a href="/wiki/Cult_following" title="Cult following">cult following</a>; three series were produced, the first airing in 1999, the second in 2000 and the third in 2002. A Christmas Special was broadcast in December 2000, after the airing of the second series. For television, Spent was renamed Royston Vasey - the given name of comedian <a href="/wiki/Roy_Chubby_Brown" title="Roy Chubby Brown">Roy Chubby Brown</a>, who appears in the series, notably as the Mayor of Royston Vasey in series 2. Along with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fast_Show" title="The Fast Show">The Fast Show</a></i>, the series is credited with the revival of the sketch show format in BBC comedy. Its influence can be seen on later series, particularly <i><a href="/wiki/Little_Britain" title="Little Britain">Little Britain</a></i> (the first series of which was directed by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Bendelack" title="Steve Bendelack">Steve Bendelack</a> and script-edited by Gatiss).
</p><p>Filming took place mainly on location in the north <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a> town of <a href="/wiki/Hadfield,_Derbyshire" title="Hadfield, Derbyshire">Hadfield</a> and consequently had no live audience.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-comedymap_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-comedymap-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  A <a href="/wiki/Laugh_track" title="Laugh track">laugh track</a> was added to the first and second series, by inviting a studio audience to watch a playback of the completed episodes as well as the filming of certain interior scenes, such as the Dentons'. The laughter track was dropped from the Christmas Special and Series 3 when shown in the United Kingdom.
</p><p>The group took the show on tour for the first time in 2001, using a mixture of old and new material. In early 2005 a special one-off sketch was broadcast on the BBC for <a href="/w/index.php?title=Comic_Aid&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Comic Aid (page does not exist)">Comic Aid</a>, a charity benefit for the <a href="/wiki/2004_Indian_Ocean_earthquake" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 Indian Ocean earthquake">tsunami disaster</a>. In this, two of the most popular characters, Tubbs and <a href="/wiki/Papa_Lazarou" title="Papa Lazarou">Papa Lazarou</a>, kidnapped <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Richardson" title="Miranda Richardson">Miranda Richardson</a>. A feature-length film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_League_of_Gentlemen%27s_Apocalypse" title="The League of Gentlemen&#39;s Apocalypse">The League of Gentlemen's Apocalypse</a></i>, was released on 3 June 2005. Later in the same year, the League toured the UK with their new pantomime-themed show, <i><a href="/wiki/The_League_of_Gentlemen_Are_Behind_You" class="mw-redirect" title="The League of Gentlemen Are Behind You">The League of Gentlemen Are Behind You</a></i>, which ran from October to mid-December.
</p><p>In September 2006, the unofficial website reported that The League of Gentlemen were to 'reunite' at the beginning of 2007, most likely to plan for the fourth series.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Shearsmith and Pemberton appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Russell_Brand_Show_(TV_series)" title="The Russell Brand Show (TV series)">The Russell Brand Show</a></i> on 22 December 2006. When asked "Will there be any more of <i>The League of Gentlemen</i>?", Shearsmith simply replied "Yes" but was quick to change the subject and not reveal anything about a new series. On the official website, Shearsmith's <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blog</a> entry for 23 May 2007 stated that the troupe had recently met up in London's <a href="/wiki/West_End_of_London" title="West End of London">West End</a>: "We discussed our next project - it seems we have hit upon something. Early days - but exciting nevertheless."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2008, Shearsmith confirmed that although he and Steve would be making <i><a href="/wiki/Psychoville" title="Psychoville">Psychoville</a></i> (broadcast in 2009) without the other members of the League, the League would re-unite in the future. Despite this claim, Gatiss appears in the show as an actor who is murdered by the characters played by Pemberton and Shearsmith.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2010, a one off radio show, <i>The League of Gentlemen's Ghost Chase</i>, was broadcast on 28 October for Halloween.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Unlike other shows, this was not a scripted dark comedy but a documentary of the members spending a night at <a href="/wiki/The_Ancient_Ram_Inn" class="mw-redirect" title="The Ancient Ram Inn">The Ancient Ram Inn</a>, reputedly the most haunted hotel in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Speaking to <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_6_Music" title="BBC Radio 6 Music">BBC Radio 6</a> in October 2016, Mark Gatiss spoke about the desire of the creators to bring back the show in some form with Brexit forming a suitable background to revive it.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2017, both Gatiss and Shearsmith confirmed that the show would be returning for an anniversary special.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The BBC announced in August 2017 that three special new episodes were to be produced, to be aired in December 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Series">Series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_League_of_Gentlemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_The_League_of_Gentlemen_episodes" title="List of The League of Gentlemen episodes">List of The League of Gentlemen episodes</a></div>
<h3><span id="Radio_series_.281997.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_series_(1997)">Radio series (1997)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_League_of_Gentlemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Radio series (1997)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the radio series, the plot involved outsider Benjamin Denton visiting his aunt and uncle in Spent to be interviewed for a job at the local power plant. Not surprisingly, he missed the interview and was forced to stay longer than expected.
</p>
<h3><span id="Series_1_.281999.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_1_(1999)">Series 1 (1999)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_League_of_Gentlemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Series 1 (1999)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the first television series, a sketch show, the story opens by revisiting the arrival of Benjamin Denton in Royston Vasey at the home of Uncle Harvey (who has a preoccupation with toads and domestic cleanliness) and Auntie Val.
</p><p>Another main plot involves a new road being built through Royston Vasey, raising the possibility of a great numbers of strangers visiting the town, a fact which terrifies the owners of the local shop, Edward and Tubbs, who are determined to keep the shop "local" and protect "the precious things". The road development ends when Tubbs and Edward discover that the construction manager is their long lost son, David, and convince him to end construction and live "locally". there is some mirroring on this sketch with the film "The Wickerman"; one of the league's favourite films.
</p><p>A third thread follows Pauline Campbell-Jones, the local <a href="/wiki/Restart_(training_course)" title="Restart (training course)">Restart</a> officer with a pen obsession and utter contempt for the attendees on her Restart course, and her fall from grace at the hands of her malevolent colleague, Ross, who poses as a course attendee.
</p><p>Guests in series 1 include <a href="/wiki/Don_Estelle" title="Don Estelle">Don Estelle</a>, playing the role of the Royston Vasey zookeeper, whose zoo is located on a traffic island (until the animals go missing).
</p>
<h3><span id="Series_2_.282000.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_2_(2000)">Series 2 (2000)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_League_of_Gentlemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Series 2 (2000)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The second series sees a deadly epidemic of nosebleeds grip the town's inhabitants, killing many. The series concludes after some confusion over the cause of the nosebleeds, involving butcher Hilary Briss's "special stuff", Benjamin Denton's escape from his Uncle Harvey and Auntie Val (aided, unexpectedly, by his young cousins, Chloe and Radcliffe), Pauline Campbell-Jones's revenge on Ross, and the murders in the Local Shop.
</p><p>The first episode of this series contains the debut of <a href="/wiki/Papa_Lazarou" title="Papa Lazarou">Papa Lazarou</a>, a character in <a href="/wiki/Blackface" title="Blackface">blackface</a> who (despite only appearing in four episodes) quickly became one of the show's most popular characters. Tubbs and Edward, seeking a partner for their son, David, who they are now keeping captive above the shop, persuade village transsexual taxi driver Barbara to marry him. At the conclusion of the series, the townsfolk, mistaking the source of the "special stuff", free Barbara and burn down Edward and Tubbs' shop.
</p><p>Guests in series 2 include <a href="/wiki/Sian_Gibson" title="Sian Gibson">Sian Gibson</a> (as Sian Foulkes) and the comedian <a href="/wiki/Roy_Chubby_Brown" title="Roy Chubby Brown">Roy Chubby Brown</a>, in the role of the Mayor of Royston Vasey. As noted above, the town of Royston Vasey is actually named in tribute to Roy Chubby Brown, that being his actual birth name.
</p>
<h3><span id="Series_3_.282002.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_3_(2002)">Series 3 (2002)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_League_of_Gentlemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Series 3 (2002)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The third and final series focused on a different character each week but with the overlaps creating a more complex layering of the plot, more akin to a one-off episode of a situation comedy (albeit with intertwined plots) than a traditional sketch show. The end of each episode features a white van swerving to avoid a pedestrian and crashing into a garden wall. Some residents escape unharmed, while others are not so lucky. A red plastic bag (which is viewed as a sign of imminent death) is seen being blown by the wind through the town in every episode, indicating that all the stories occur simultaneously.
</p><p>Guests in series 3 include <a href="/wiki/Sian_Gibson" title="Sian Gibson">Sian Gibson</a> (as Sian Foulkes), the designer <a href="/wiki/Laurence_Llewelyn-Bowen" title="Laurence Llewelyn-Bowen">Laurence Llewelyn-Bowen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Eccleston" title="Christopher Eccleston">Christopher Eccleston</a> and a very young <a href="/wiki/Brooke_Vincent" title="Brooke Vincent">Brooke Vincent</a>, later to become a regular in <a href="/wiki/Coronation_Street" title="Coronation Street">Coronation Street</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Specials">Specials</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_League_of_Gentlemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Specials">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Christmas_Special_.282000.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Christmas_Special_(2000)">Christmas Special (2000)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_League_of_Gentlemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Christmas Special (2000)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 2000 Christmas Special, "Yule Never Leave!" took the slightly different format of three self-contained stories, with three of the characters seeking the help of the <a href="/wiki/Vicar" title="Vicar">vicar</a>, Bernice, on <a href="/wiki/Christmas_Eve" title="Christmas Eve">Christmas Eve</a>.
</p><p>The first story features Charlie and Stella Hull, the warring couple from series 2. The second concerns the backstory of Herr Lipp and his wife, Lotte, in Duisberg, in the years before Herr Lipp visits Royston Vasey. The third is a Victorian tale, telling the story of how Dr. Edmund Chinnery comes to be in Royston Vasey and of the curse that is placed on Chinnery’s ancestor and his descendants. A postscript tells of how vicar Bernice, as a small girl, witnessed her mother be abducted by Papa Lazarou and ends up with the adult Bernice being visited by and kidnapped by Papa Lazarou, on Christmas Eve. This episode was aired between Series 2 and Series 3 and features guest appearances by <a href="/wiki/Liza_Tarbuck" title="Liza Tarbuck">Liza Tarbuck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Jones" title="Freddie Jones">Freddie Jones</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="Anniversary_Specials_.282017.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Anniversary_Specials_(2017)">Anniversary Specials (2017)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_League_of_Gentlemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Anniversary Specials (2017)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2017, the BBC announced that three new episodes would be produced to mark the twentieth anniversary of the show's debut on BBC Radio.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Filming for the specials took place from late September through mid October 2017,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> with guest star <a href="/wiki/David_Morrissey" title="David Morrissey">David Morrissey</a> on set with the cast.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On 24 October 2017, the first official image confirmed the return of Pauline Campbell-Jones, alongside Ross and Mickey. On the same day, it was announced that a special screening of one of the episodes would be taking place at the <a href="/wiki/British_Film_Institute" title="British Film Institute">British Film Institute</a> on 12 December 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The central plot sees Royston Vasey's existence threatened by boundary changes. Bernice, having escaped from Papa Lazarou and now elected as the mayor, launches a campaign to save the town after learning she will lose some of her privileges. One of the councillors behind the move and a local journalist are taken hostage by Tubbs and Edward, who have survived and have set up a shop in <a href="/wiki/Inside_No._9" title="Inside No. 9">flat No. 9</a> of an abandoned block of flats, and demand that no changes are made. The story spreads nationally, with the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister</a> agreeing not to change the boundaries. But Tubbs and Edward are forced to flee when it is revealed that they have cut off the faces of their hostages. Fleeing the police, Tubbs hides in a photo booth with a trap door, which opens and kidnaps people when a photo is taken. Bernice later reveals that the land on which Royston Vasey lies has been sold for <a href="/wiki/Fracking" class="mw-redirect" title="Fracking">fracking</a>, because she was allowed to escape from Papa Lazarou only by agreeing to sell the land to him. The photo booth is revealed to be one of Papa Lazarou's traps, who then appears before Tubbs saying: "It's a wife mine now."
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters">Characters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_League_of_Gentlemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Characters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_The_League_of_Gentlemen_characters" title="List of The League of Gentlemen characters">List of The League of Gentlemen characters</a></div>

<p><i>The League of Gentlemen</i> have played in total nearly a hundred characters, many created in the early stage shows, others during the span of the television series and some especially for the team's film.  Most of the characters live in Royston Vasey.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_and_inspiration">Writing and inspiration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_League_of_Gentlemen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Writing and inspiration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is widely believed that a lot of the characters and indeed the town are based on Pemberton's home town of <a href="/wiki/Chorley" title="Chorley">Chorley</a>, with Royston Vasey based on <a href="/wiki/Adlington,_Lancashire" title="Adlington, Lancashire">Adlington</a>, a village within Chorley Borough.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The character of Herr Lipp is believed to be based on a hospital chaplain Steve Pemberton encountered after suffering a heart attack in Germany and Pauline is primarily based on a restart officer of Reece Shearsmith's.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Similarly, Ollie Plimsolls is based on a community theatre actor that Shearsmith had worked with.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Audio_commentary" title="Audio commentary">DVD commentary</a> on the second series, Pemberton and Shearsmith state that <a href="/wiki/Papa_Lazarou" title="Papa Lazarou">Papa Lazarou</a>'s speech patterns are based on their former landlord, who would phone their flat and insist on speaking only to Steve.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Gatiss has said in interview that the local shop was inspired by a shop in the village of <a href="/wiki/Rottingdean" title="Rottingdean">Rottingdean</a><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and that he was influenced growing up around the former <a href="/wiki/Winterton_Hospital" title="Winterton Hospital">Winterton Hospital</a> asylum near <a href="/wiki/Sedgefield" title="Sedgefield">Sedgefield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The majority of the inhabitants of the village — male and female — are played by <a href="/wiki/Reece_Shearsmith" title="Reece Shearsmith">Reece Shearsmith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Pemberton" title="Steve Pemberton">Steve Pemberton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Gatiss" title="Mark Gatiss">Mark Gatiss</a>, and the script was written by these three, along with <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Dyson" title="Jeremy Dyson">Jeremy Dyson</a>.  Dyson, not an actor like the others, appears only in <a href="/wiki/Cameo_appearance" title="Cameo appearance">cameo</a> roles.  As there are usually only three actors on screen at any one time, the different characters mostly play out their own stories in several serialised sketches, rarely crossing into each other's storylines.  Only rarely do actors "meet themselves". Exceptions include Papa Lazarou facing the Reverend Bernice in the Christmas Special (both Reece Shearsmith), Les McQueen buying a magazine from Pop's son (both Mark Gatiss), and Alvin Steele buying food from Iris at a supermarket checkout in Series 2 (again, both Mark Gatiss).  The idea is taken further in <i><a href="/wiki/The_League_of_Gentlemen%27s_Apocalypse" title="The League of Gentlemen&#39;s Apocalypse">The League of Gentlemen's Apocalypse</a></i>, when the characters meet the actors (especially when Herr Lipp meets his creator, Steve Pemberton). In the live shows, when Pam Doove was auditioning for a part in the Christmas Nativity Play, directed by Ollie Plimsolls, Pam had to audition in front of Ollie's Legz Akimbo colleague Dave (Pemberton), who said that Ollie couldn't make it "for obvious reasons" (Shearsmith plays both Pam and Ollie in the television series).
</p>
