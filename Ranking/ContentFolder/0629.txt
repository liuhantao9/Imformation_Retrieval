<p><b>Scarborough railway station</b>, formerly <b>Scarborough Central</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Grade_II_listed" class="mw-redirect" title="Grade II listed">Grade II listed</a><sup id="cite_ref-Listing_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Listing-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> station serving the seaside town of <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_North_Yorkshire" title="Scarborough, North Yorkshire">Scarborough, North Yorkshire</a>. It lies 42 miles (68&#160;km) east of <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York</a> and is one of the eastern termini on the <a href="/wiki/North_TransPennine" class="mw-redirect" title="North TransPennine">North TransPennine</a> route, operated by <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a>. The station is also at the northern end of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Coast_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Yorkshire Coast Line">Yorkshire Coast Line</a> and is reputed to have the longest station seat in the world at 456-foot (139&#160;m) long.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1907 until 2010 the station approaches were controlled from a 120-lever signal box named Falsgrave (at the outer end of platform 1 and close to the former excursion station at <a href="/wiki/Scarborough_Londesborough_Road_railway_station" title="Scarborough Londesborough Road railway station">Londesborough Road</a>). In its final years Falsgrave box controlled a mixture of colour-light and semaphore signals, including a gantry carrying 11 semaphores. The signal box was decommissioned in September 2010 and the gantry was dismantled and removed in October 2010. Its new home was Grosmont Station, on the North Yorkshire Moors Railway. The new signalling is a relay-based interlocking with two- and three-aspect LED signals controlled from an extension to the existing panel at nearby <a href="/wiki/Seamer_railway_station" title="Seamer railway station">Seamer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Simplification of the track layout and major renewals took place at the same time.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scarborough_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Scarborough station opened on Monday 7 July 1845, following the completion of the line from York. The first train, consisting of 35 coaches, was hauled by two locomotives named 'Hudson' and 'Lion' and arrived in Scarborough at 1:35&#160;p.m., having stopped at Castle Howard, Malton and Ganton, taking just over three hours. All the shops closed, and an estimated ten to fifteen thousand spectators saw it arrive.
</p><p>The original station building was designed by G.T. Andrews. It had a wrought-iron and glazed roof, 348 feet long by 88 feet wide, in two spans and 30 feet from the rails. On the opening day the station was complete except for the overall roof, and the goods shed in the station yard had not been built.
</p><p>The main station building included: a large central booking office, superintendent room, 1st class, 2nd class and Ladies' waiting rooms, toilets, porters' room, storeroom and refreshment room. Above the refreshment room was originally the station master's house, later the station hotel with ten bedrooms.
</p><p>At first there were two platforms connected at the north end, with four tracks in between them, each track having a pair of turntables, one at each end of the platforms. Gradually as traffic increased modifications were made to the station layout.
</p><p>To accommodate excursion traffic two new platforms were added in 1883, now known as platforms 1 &amp; 2. Separate waiting rooms and more facilities were provided. The station clock, built by Potts of Leeds and costing £110, was added in about 1884.  (equivalent to £10,882&#32;in 2018).<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-UK_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UK-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the 1890s and until 1903 goods traffic was moved from the station yard to <a href="/wiki/Gallows_Close_goods_yard" title="Gallows Close goods yard">Gallows Close</a> so that more platforms could be provided. The original goods shed, which was next to the station building, became platforms 6 to 9. Trains for the Forge Valley line often used these four platforms.
</p><p>Platform 1A was cut out of platform 1 for easier access to the Whitby line and opened for the beginning of the summer timetable in 1934.
</p><p>Until 1965 the station also served a line from <a href="/wiki/Scarborough_%26_Whitby_Railway" class="mw-redirect" title="Scarborough &amp; Whitby Railway">Whitby</a> and until 1950 from <a href="/wiki/Forge_Valley_Line" title="Forge Valley Line">Pickering</a>. The station was previously named <b>Scarborough Central</b> to distinguish it from the now closed <a href="/wiki/Scarborough_Londesborough_Road_railway_station" title="Scarborough Londesborough Road railway station">Scarborough Londesborough Road</a> on the <a href="/wiki/York_to_Scarborough_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="York to Scarborough Line">York to Scarborough Line</a>. In the late 1960s most of the roof that covered platforms 1 and 2 was demolished, leaving these platforms outdoors, though the platforms still remain in use today
</p><p>By the early 1980s regular use of platforms 6-9 had declined and following a simplification of the track layout in 1985 they were taken out of use and demolished.  The land they had occupied is now used for car &amp; coach parking, though the overall roofs and brick walls remain.
</p><p>The current <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York</a> to <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_North_railway_station" title="Blackpool North railway station">Blackpool</a> service used to continue to <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_North_Yorkshire" title="Scarborough, North Yorkshire">Scarborough</a> alongside other TransPennine Express services. This was operated by <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Trains_Northern" title="Arriva Trains Northern">Arriva Trains Northern</a> until <a href="/wiki/Northern_Rail_(Serco-Abellio)" title="Northern Rail (Serco-Abellio)">Northern Rail</a> took over the franchise in 2004. This service was usually worked by <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Passenger_Transport_Executive" class="mw-redirect" title="West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive">Metro</a> liveried <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_158" title="British Rail Class 158">Class 158</a> <a href="/wiki/Diesel_Multiple_Unit" class="mw-redirect" title="Diesel Multiple Unit">Diesel Multiple Units</a> and occasionally a <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_155" title="British Rail Class 155">Class 155</a> DMU. There was also a local service from York to Scarborough, usually worked by a <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_144" title="British Rail Class 144">Pacer</a> or a <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_156" title="British Rail Class 156">Class 156</a>.
</p>

<p>Scarborough station has a Travel Centre, ticket office, touch-screen ticket machines and a Pumpkin Cafe. The main building has a small waiting room.
</p><p>Platforms 3–5 are partly covered, as is platform 1, which reputedly features the longest railway bench in the world at 456-foot (139&#160;m) in length.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Outside the station are a taxi rank and several bus shelters/stops where local and longer-distance bus services depart and arrive. These include <a href="/wiki/Arriva_North_East" title="Arriva North East">Arriva North East</a> route X93 to <a href="/wiki/Whitby" title="Whitby">Whitby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a> via the A171; <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Coastliner" class="mw-redirect" title="Yorkshire Coastliner">Yorkshire Coastliner</a> services to West Yorkshire via the <a href="/wiki/A64_road" title="A64 road">A64</a>; and <a href="/wiki/East_Yorkshire_Motor_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="East Yorkshire Motor Services">East Yorkshire Motor Services</a> route 128 (to <a href="/wiki/Pickering,_North_Yorkshire" title="Pickering, North Yorkshire">Pickering</a>/<a href="/wiki/Helmsley" title="Helmsley">Helmsley</a> via the A170) and routes south along the A165 to <a href="/wiki/Filey" title="Filey">Filey</a>/<a href="/wiki/Bridlington" title="Bridlington">Bridlington</a>.
</p><p>Benches are provided throughout the station, which is staffed at all times. The station also has two payphones, a vending machine and luggage trolleys, as well as toilets and cycle racks. Step-free access is available to all platforms.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> operate an hourly service to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Lime_Street_railway_station" title="Liverpool Lime Street railway station">Liverpool Lime Street</a> via York, <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Victoria railway station">Manchester Victoria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> operate an hourly service to <a href="/wiki/Hull_Paragon_Interchange" title="Hull Paragon Interchange">Hull</a> via <a href="/wiki/Filey_railway_station" title="Filey railway station">Filey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bridlington_railway_station" title="Bridlington railway station">Bridlington</a> with some services continuing to <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield railway station">Sheffield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On summer Saturdays <a href="/wiki/East_Midlands_Railway" title="East Midlands Railway">East Midlands Railway</a> operate one return service from and to <a href="/wiki/St_Pancras_railway_station" title="St Pancras railway station">London St Pancras</a> via <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_railway_station" title="Doncaster railway station">Doncaster</a>. This is scheduled to cease in September 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In summer <a href="/wiki/West_Coast_Railways" title="West Coast Railways">West Coast Railways</a> operates steam locomotive hauled <i><a href="/wiki/Scarborough_Spa_Express" title="Scarborough Spa Express">Scarborough Spa Express</a></i> services from York.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Scarborough is also a popular destination for charter services as it has an operational <a href="/wiki/Railway_turntable" title="Railway turntable">turntable</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_of_the_station">Future of the station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scarborough_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Future of the station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three different proposals were put forward at the November 2009 Town Team meeting by SNAP Architects (Hull) with Local Transport Projects (Beverley) on the development of the Scarborough station area. All the proposals focused on different aspects (such as community/green/transport) of development. All three proposed improving the station frontage and opening up an entrance to the south of the station.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_improvements">Service improvements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scarborough_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Service improvements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Update plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Update" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="Ambox current red.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/42px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png" decoding="async" width="42" height="34" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/63px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Ambox_current_red.svg/84px-Ambox_current_red.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="360" data-file-height="290" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section needs to be <b>updated</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please update this article to reflect recent events or newly available information.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">June 2017</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The new TPE and Northern franchises, which started in April 2016, were committed to improved service frequencies and rolling stock on both lines<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> - the York route will have two departures per hour instead of the previous one as of December 2019, both operators offering an hourly service as far as York, whilst the Hull line now has an hourly timetable seven days per week.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The latter was introduced at the May 2019 timetable change.
</p><p>Trains to Liverpool are also diverted west of Stalybridge to travel via <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Victoria railway station">Manchester Victoria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newton-le-Willows_railway_station" title="Newton-le-Willows railway station">Newton-le-Willows</a> (reverting to the route they used prior to May 1989). The improved service saw a new £7&#160;million train servicing depot built in Scarborough to maintain the rolling stock.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accident">Accident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scarborough_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Accident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 10 August 1943 Scarborough station was the scene of an accident between two trains at platform 5. The late-running 09:05 express from <a href="/wiki/Hull_Paragon_Interchange" title="Hull Paragon Interchange">Hull</a> was wrongly routed by the signalman and hit the 11:18 stopping train, which was waiting to depart. Four passengers in the first coach of the stopping train – all soldiers – were killed, eight received serious injuries and a further 22 suffered minor injuries. Nobody was injured on the express train.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
