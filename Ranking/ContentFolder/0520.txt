<p><b>Dalton</b> is a district of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> in England, approximately one mile east of the town centre between <a href="/wiki/Moldgreen" title="Moldgreen">Moldgreen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kirkheaton" title="Kirkheaton">Kirkheaton</a>.
</p><p>Located in a small valley it is mostly housing, with a small number of engineering firms to the northwest, surrounded by farmland used in the production of milk.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Germaine_Lindsay" title="Germaine Lindsay">Germaine Lindsay</a>, one of the suicide bombers involved in the <a href="/wiki/7_July_2005_London_bombings" title="7 July 2005 London bombings">7 July 2005 London bombings</a>, had lived in the area following his arrival from <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> aged five.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dalton,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dalton was listed in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a> of 1086. This tax-motivated survey commissioned by <a href="/wiki/William_the_Conqueror" title="William the Conqueror">William the Conqueror</a> listed landholdings and resources. However, unlike other areas surrounding Huddersfield, Dalton was not listed as `waste', meaning uncultivated or unusable land, and there was economic activity. A plough was being used and the land was worth ten shillings.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
