<p>There are a number of agencies that participate in <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Law enforcement in the United Kingdom">law enforcement in the United Kingdom</a> which can be grouped into three general types:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Territorial police forces</b>, who carry out the majority of policing. These are police forces that cover a <a href="/wiki/Police_area" title="Police area">police area</a> (a particular region) and have an independent <a href="/wiki/Police_authority" title="Police authority">police authority</a>. Current police forces have their grounding in the <a href="/wiki/Police_Act_1996" title="Police Act 1996">Police Act 1996</a> (in <a href="/wiki/England_and_Wales" title="England and Wales">England and Wales</a>), a combination of <a href="/wiki/Police_(Scotland)_Act_1967" title="Police (Scotland) Act 1967">Police (Scotland) Act 1967</a> and <a href="/wiki/Police_and_Fire_Reform_(Scotland)_Act_2012" title="Police and Fire Reform (Scotland) Act 2012">Police and Fire Reform (Scotland) Act 2012</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Police_(Northern_Ireland)_Act_2000" title="Police (Northern Ireland) Act 2000">Police (Northern Ireland) Act 2000</a> (in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>), which prescribe a number of issues such as appointment of a <a href="/wiki/Chief_constable" title="Chief constable">chief constable</a>, jurisdiction and responsibilities.</li>
<li><b>National law enforcement bodies</b>, including the <a href="/wiki/National_Crime_Agency" title="National Crime Agency">National Crime Agency</a> and national police forces that have a specific, non-regional jurisdiction, such as the <a href="/wiki/British_Transport_Police" title="British Transport Police">British Transport Police</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Serious_Organised_Crime_and_Police_Act_2005" title="Serious Organised Crime and Police Act 2005">Serious Organised Crime and Police Act 2005</a> refers to these as '<a href="/wiki/Special_police_force" class="mw-redirect" title="Special police force">special police forces</a>', not including the NCA which is not a police force.  In addition, there are non-police law enforcement agencies, whose officers are not police officers, but still enforce laws, and other bodies with solely investigatory powers.</li>
<li><b>Miscellaneous police forces</b>, mostly having their foundations in older legislation or <a href="/wiki/Common_law" title="Common law">common law</a>. These are responsible for policing specific local areas or activities, such as ports and parks. Before the passing of recent legislation such as the Serious Organised Crime and Police Act 2005, they were often referred to as 'special police forces'; care must therefore be taken in interpreting historical use of that phrase. These constabularies are not within the scope of the legislation applicable to the previously-mentioned organisations but can still be the subject of statutes applicable to, for example, docks, harbours or railways. Until the passing of <a href="/wiki/Railways_and_Transport_Safety_Act_2003" title="Railways and Transport Safety Act 2003">Railways and Transport Safety Act 2003</a>, the British Transport Police was such a force.</li></ul>
<p>The majority of law enforcement in the United Kingdom is carried out by territorial police forces that police the general public and their activities. The other types of agencies are concerned with policing of more specific matters.
</p><p>Over the centuries there has been a wide variation in the number of police forces in the United Kingdom, with a <a href="/wiki/List_of_defunct_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of defunct law enforcement agencies in the United Kingdom">large number now no longer in existence</a>.
</p>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_police_forces_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of police forces of the United Kingdom">List of police forces of the United Kingdom</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="England_and_Wales">England and Wales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: England and Wales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_of_English_and_Welsh_Police_areas_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg" class="image"><img alt="Map of English and Welsh Police areas in the United Kingdom.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Map_of_English_and_Welsh_Police_areas_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg/225px-Map_of_English_and_Welsh_Police_areas_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png" decoding="async" width="225" height="410" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Map_of_English_and_Welsh_Police_areas_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg/338px-Map_of_English_and_Welsh_Police_areas_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Map_of_English_and_Welsh_Police_areas_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg/450px-Map_of_English_and_Welsh_Police_areas_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="560" data-file-height="1020" /></a></div>
<p>Except in Greater London, each <a href="/wiki/Territorial_police_force" title="Territorial police force">territorial police force</a> covers one or more of the <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Local government in the United Kingdom">local government areas</a> (counties) established in the 1974 local government reorganisations (although with subsequent modifications), in an area known in statute as a <a href="/wiki/Police_area" title="Police area">police area</a>. These forces provide the majority of policing services to the public of England and Wales. These forces have been known historically as "<a href="/wiki/Home_Office" title="Home Office">Home Office</a> police forces" due to the central government department, the Home Office, being responsible for and providing the majority of funding these police forces. Despite the implication of the term, all police forces are independent, with operational control resting solely with the chief officer of each force (the <a href="/wiki/Chief_Constable" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Constable">Chief Constable</a> or with regard to the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Police_Service" title="Metropolitan Police Service">Metropolitan Police</a> and <a href="/wiki/City_of_London_Police" title="City of London Police">City of London Police</a> forces, their respective Commissioners); each force was overseen by a <a href="/wiki/Police_authority" title="Police authority">Police authority</a> until these were replaced by <a href="/wiki/Police_and_Crime_Commissioners" class="mw-redirect" title="Police and Crime Commissioners">Police and Crime Commissioners</a> in 2012.
</p><p>The Police Act 1996 is the most recent piece of legislation, which outlines the areas of responsibility for the 43 territorial forces of <a href="/wiki/England_and_Wales" title="England and Wales">England and Wales</a> (found in Schedule 1 of the Act).
</p><p><i>Constable</i> is the lowest rank in the police service, but all officers, whatever their rank are "constables" in terms of legal powers and jurisdiction. Police officers in territorial police forces in England and Wales derive their jurisdiction from Section 30 of the Police Act 1996. This section outlines that such officers have jurisdiction throughout England and Wales and also the adjacent United Kingdom waters. <a href="/wiki/Special_Constable" class="mw-redirect" title="Special Constable">Special Constables</a>, who are part-time, volunteer officers of these forces, used to have a more limited jurisdiction – limited solely to their own force areas and adjacent forces. Since 1 April 2007, however Special Constables of England &amp; Wales have full police powers throughout those two countries. This means that, in contrast to the majority of countries, all UK volunteer police officers now have exactly the same powers as their full-time colleagues.  There are a number of situations in which the jurisdiction of a constable extends to one of the other countries, and constables of one jurisdiction do have reciprocal powers of arrest in each others jurisdictions as a matter of course – see <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_in_the_United_Kingdom#Cross-border_powers" title="Law enforcement in the United Kingdom">the main article</a> for details.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scotland">Scotland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Scotland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_of_Scotland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg" class="image"><img alt="Map of Scotland Police area in the United Kingdom.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Map_of_Scotland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg/225px-Map_of_Scotland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png" decoding="async" width="225" height="410" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Map_of_Scotland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg/338px-Map_of_Scotland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Map_of_Scotland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg/450px-Map_of_Scotland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="560" data-file-height="1020" /></a></div>
<p>Most police powers and functions have been inherited by the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish Government</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament" title="Scottish Parliament">Scottish Parliament</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Office" title="Scottish Office">Scottish Office</a>. Areas for which legislative responsibility remains with the UK Government include national security, terrorism, firearms and drugs. The <a href="/wiki/Police_(Scotland)_Act_1967" title="Police (Scotland) Act 1967">Police (Scotland) Act 1967</a>, as amended, was the basis for the organisation and jurisdiction of the eight former territorial forces in Scotland that were formed in 1975. These forces covered one or more of the areas of the <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Local government in the United Kingdom">local government regions</a> established in the 1975 local government reorganisation (and since abolished), with minor adjustments to align with the post-1996 <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Scotland" title="Subdivisions of Scotland">council area</a> borders. These forces provided the majority of police services to the public of Scotland, although Scottish police officers also have limited jurisdiction throughout the rest of the United Kingdom as required (See above comments under English and Welsh forces).
</p><p>In 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish Government</a> announced that it planned to amalgamate the eight territorial forces in Scotland, along with the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Crime_and_Drug_Enforcement_Agency" title="Scottish Crime and Drug Enforcement Agency">Scottish Crime and Drug Enforcement Agency</a>, into a single agency. The <a href="/wiki/Police_and_Fire_Reform_(Scotland)_Act_2012" title="Police and Fire Reform (Scotland) Act 2012">Police and Fire Reform (Scotland) Act 2012</a>, an Act of the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament" title="Scottish Parliament">Scottish Parliament</a>, codified this amalgamation and brought about the new <a href="/wiki/Police_Scotland" title="Police Scotland">Police Service of Scotland</a> (to be known as "Police Scotland"). The new force was established on 1 April 2013.
</p>
<div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #0086BE; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Police_Scotland" title="Police Scotland">Police Scotland <i>(Poileas Alba, Polis Scotland)</i></a></div>
<p>In 2017, plans were being debated in the Scottish Parliament to merge railway policing with Police Scotland.
</p><p>As of December 2012, police numbers in Scotland were:<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Police officers: 17,436</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Special_Constable" class="mw-redirect" title="Special Constable">Special constables</a>: 1,404</li>
<li>Other staff: 6,168</li></ul>
<p><small>Community Support Officers, commonly referred to as "Police Community Support Officers", were established by Section 38(2) of the Police Reform Act 2002, which applies only to England and Wales.  There are therefore no Community Support Officers in Scotland.</small>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_Ireland">Northern Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Northern Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_of_Northern_Ireland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg" class="image"><img alt="Map of Northern Ireland Police area in the United Kingdom.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Map_of_Northern_Ireland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg/225px-Map_of_Northern_Ireland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png" decoding="async" width="225" height="410" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Map_of_Northern_Ireland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg/338px-Map_of_Northern_Ireland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Map_of_Northern_Ireland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg/450px-Map_of_Northern_Ireland_Police_area_in_the_United_Kingdom.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="560" data-file-height="1020" /></a></div>
<p>County and borough based police forces were not formed in Ireland as they were in Great Britain, with instead a single <a href="/wiki/Royal_Irish_Constabulary" title="Royal Irish Constabulary">Royal Irish Constabulary</a> covering most of Ireland (the exceptions being the <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Metropolitan_Police" title="Dublin Metropolitan Police">Dublin Metropolitan Police</a>, which was responsible for policing in <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Belfast_Town_Police" class="mw-redirect" title="Belfast Town Police">Belfast Town Police</a> force, which was replaced by the RIC in the 1880s). The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Ulster_Constabulary" title="Royal Ulster Constabulary">Royal Ulster Constabulary</a> was formed in 1922 after the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Free_State" title="Irish Free State">Irish Free State</a>, and served until the reforms of the police under the terms established initially by the <a href="/wiki/Good_Friday_Agreement" title="Good Friday Agreement">Good Friday Agreement</a> of 1998 undertaken by the <a href="/wiki/Independent_Commission_on_Policing_for_Northern_Ireland" title="Independent Commission on Policing for Northern Ireland">Patten Commission</a>, which led to the renaming of the RUC in 2001. The <a href="/wiki/Police_(Northern_Ireland)_Act_2000" title="Police (Northern Ireland) Act 2000">Police (Northern Ireland) Act 2000</a> sets out the basis for the organisation and function of the police force in the province. Until 2010, police powers were not transferred to the devolved <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Executive" title="Northern Ireland Executive">Northern Ireland Executive</a>, unlike Scotland, instead remaining with the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Office" title="Northern Ireland Office">Northern Ireland Office</a>. However, in January 2010 agreement was reached between the two largest parties in the Assembly, the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Unionist_Party" title="Democratic Unionist Party">DUP</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sinn_F%C3%A9in" title="Sinn Féin">Sinn Féin</a>, over a course that would see them assume responsibility for policing and justice from April.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #0086BE; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Police_Service_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Police Service of Northern Ireland">Police Service of Northern Ireland</a></div>
<p>As of April 2007 police numbers in Northern Ireland were:<sup id="cite_ref-strengthmap_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-strengthmap-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Police officers: 7,216</li>
<li>Full-time reserve police officers: 335</li>
<li>Part-time police officers: 684</li>
<li>Other staff: 2,265</li></ul>
<p><small>Police in Northern Ireland do not employ Police Community Support Officers</small>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>County police forces traditionally bore the name "<a href="/wiki/Constabulary" title="Constabulary">constabulary</a>" upon their formation (as a derivation of "<a href="/wiki/Constable" title="Constable">constable</a>"). The reorganisation of police forces over the years has seen this name dropped in favour of "police" as a name, as many have decided that the word "constabulary" is confusing for people more used to searching for the word "police".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> However, a number of police forces in the areas overseen by the United Kingdom retain the name "constabulary":
</p>
<ul><li>12 territorial forces in England</li>
<li>1 special police force - <a href="/wiki/Civil_Nuclear_Constabulary" title="Civil Nuclear Constabulary">Civil Nuclear Constabulary</a></li>
<li>1 <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man_Constabulary" title="Isle of Man Constabulary">territorial force</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Crown_dependencies" title="Crown dependencies">crown dependencies</a></li>
<li>1 <a href="/wiki/Kew_Constabulary" title="Kew Constabulary">parks police force</a> (under the terms of the Parks Regulation Act 1872)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_law_enforcement">National law enforcement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: National law enforcement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bodies_with_police_powers">Bodies with police powers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Bodies with police powers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>These bodies operate in more than one county of the United Kingdom. The remit of some of the forces is further limited to the areas that they police, such as railway infrastructure. The <a href="/wiki/Anti-terrorism,_Crime_and_Security_Act_2001" title="Anti-terrorism, Crime and Security Act 2001">Anti-terrorism, Crime and Security Act 2001</a> gave the British Transport Police and Ministry of Defence Police a limited, conditional authority to act outside of their primary jurisdiction if the situation requires urgent police action and the local force are not readily available, or if they believe that there is risk to life or limb, or where they are assisting the local force.
</p>
<dl><dt>Government agencies</dt></dl>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/National_Crime_Agency" title="National Crime Agency">National Crime Agency</a> (NCA) – An agency that leads UK-wide activities to combat high-level crime such as organised crime. In addition, the NCA acts as the UK point of contact for foreign law enforcement agencies. It replaced the <a href="/wiki/Serious_Organised_Crime_Agency" title="Serious Organised Crime Agency">Serious Organised Crime Agency</a> in 2013.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Border_Force" title="Border Force">Border Force</a> is a part of the <a href="/wiki/Home_Office" title="Home Office">Home Office</a>, responsible for frontline border control operations at air, sea and rail ports in the United Kingdom. Employees of Border Force may be <a href="/wiki/Immigration_Officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Immigration Officer">Immigration Officers</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Customs_officer" title="Customs officer">customs officers</a>. They hold certain powers of arrest, detention and search in addition to those available to <i>Any person</i><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> in England and Wales or to any person in Scotland and Northern Ireland. Police-like powers are exercised by border officers and inland immigration enforcement officers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Immigration_Enforcement" title="Immigration Enforcement">Immigration Enforcement</a> The agency also has a specialist criminal investigations directorate.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Revenue_and_Customs" class="mw-redirect" title="Her Majesty&#39;s Revenue and Customs">Her Majesty's Revenue and Customs</a>. Since the creation of the UK Border Agency (now <a href="/wiki/Border_Force" title="Border Force">Border Force</a>), staff of HMRC no longer perform frontline duties at <a href="/wiki/Port_of_entry" title="Port of entry">ports of entry</a>. The remainder of the staff with law enforcement powers employed by <a href="/wiki/HM_Revenue_and_Customs" title="HM Revenue and Customs">HMRC</a> consists of the Criminal Investigation Branch, who, as <a href="/wiki/Customs_officer" title="Customs officer">customs officers</a>, continue to exercise the powers granted under the Customs Management Acts and the Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 including arrest.</li>
<li>Environment Agency Fisheries Enforcement Officers/<a href="/wiki/Water_bailiff" title="Water bailiff">Water bailiffs</a> have powers of a constable under the Salmon and Freshwater Fisheries Act 1975. Officers/bailiffs protect fish and combat related crime (e.g. poaching).</li></ul>
<p><span id="Special_police_forces"></span>
Additionally, the following three government agencies are defined in legislation as "<a href="/wiki/Special_police_force" class="mw-redirect" title="Special police force">special police forces</a>". As these forces are responsible to specific areas of infrastructure, they do not answer to the Home Office, but instead to the government department responsible for the area they police. All three forces do voluntarily submit themselves to <a href="/wiki/HMIC" class="mw-redirect" title="HMIC">HMIC</a> inspection:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Defence_Police" title="Ministry of Defence Police">Ministry of Defence Police</a> – A police force tasked with providing armed security, uniformed policing, and investigative services to Ministry of Defence installations throughout the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Civil_Nuclear_Constabulary" title="Civil Nuclear Constabulary">Civil Nuclear Constabulary</a> – A police force responsible for providing law enforcement and security at or within 5&#160;km of any relevant nuclear site and for nuclear materials in transit within the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/British_Transport_Police" title="British Transport Police">British Transport Police</a> (Great Britain) – A police force responsible for providing law enforcement at certain railways and light-rail systems in Great Britain.<sup id="cite_ref-strengthmap_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-strengthmap-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<dl><dt>Bodies hosted by the Association of Chief Police Officers</dt></dl>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/National_Wildlife_Crime_Unit" title="National Wildlife Crime Unit">National Wildlife Crime Unit</a> – A police unit run by the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Chief_Police_Officers" title="Association of Chief Police Officers">ACPO</a> that gathers intelligence on wildlife crime and provides analytical and investigative support to law enforcement agencies across the United Kingdom.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_Counter_Terrorism_Security_Office" title="National Counter Terrorism Security Office">National Counter Terrorism Security Office</a> – A police unit run by the ACPO, which advises the British government on its counter terrorism strategy.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_Vehicle_Crime_Intelligence_Service" title="National Vehicle Crime Intelligence Service">National Vehicle Crime Intelligence Service</a> – A police unit run by the ACPO, tasked with combating organised vehicle crime and the use of vehicles in crime.</li></ul>
<dl><dt>Bodies hosted by territorial police forces</dt></dl>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/National_Domestic_Extremism_and_Disorder_Intelligence_Unit" title="National Domestic Extremism and Disorder Intelligence Unit">National Domestic Extremism and Disorder Intelligence Unit</a> – A police unit that is part of the Metropolitan Police Service Specialist Operations Directorate, tasked with coordinating police response to domestic extremism across the United Kingdom.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Protection_Command" title="Protection Command">Protection Command</a> – A police unit that is part of the Metropolitan Police Service Specialist Operations Directorate, responsible for providing protective security to the government/diplomatic community and the Royal Family within the United Kingdom.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_Fraud_Intelligence_Bureau" title="National Fraud Intelligence Bureau">National Fraud Intelligence Bureau</a> – A police unit hosted by the City of London Police, tasked with combating economic crime throughout the United Kingdom.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_Ballistics_Intelligence_Service" title="National Ballistics Intelligence Service">National Ballistics Intelligence Service</a> (Great Britain) – A police unit hosted by West Midlands Police, tasked with gathering and disseminating fast time intelligence on the criminal use of firearms across Great Britain.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_Police_Air_Service" title="National Police Air Service">National Police Air Service</a> (England and Wales) – A police aviation service hosted by West Yorkshire Police, that provides centralised air support to the 43 territorial police forces in England and Wales.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bodies_with_limited_executive_powers">Bodies with limited executive powers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Bodies with limited executive powers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>These organisations are not police forces but do have similar powers to that of the police with the exception that they cannot arrest a person nor make forcible entry without a warrant.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Driver_and_Vehicle_Standards_Agency" title="Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency">Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency</a> (Great Britain)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Driver_and_Vehicle_Agency" title="Driver and Vehicle Agency">Driver and Vehicle Agency</a> (Northern Ireland)</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Independent_Police_Complaints_Commission" title="Independent Police Complaints Commission">Independent Police Complaints Commission</a> (England and Wales) investigates complaints against police officers and staff of the police forces in England and Wales,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and staff of <a href="/wiki/HM_Revenue_and_Customs" title="HM Revenue and Customs">HM Revenue and Customs</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Crime_Agency" title="National Crime Agency">National Crime Agency</a> in England and Wales and the <a href="/wiki/Border_Force" title="Border Force">Border Force</a>. Certain investigators of the IPCC, for the purposes of the carrying out of an investigation and all purposes connected with it, have all the powers and privileges of constables throughout England and Wales and the territorial waters.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Miscellaneous_police_forces">Miscellaneous police forces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Miscellaneous police forces">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These police forces generally come under the control of a local authority, public trusts or even private companies; examples include some ports police and the Mersey Tunnels Police. They could have been established by individual <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Parliament" title="Act of Parliament">Acts of Parliament</a> or under common law powers. Jurisdiction is generally limited to the relevant area of private property alone and in some cases (e.g. docks and harbours) the surrounding area. This, together with the small size of the police forces, means they are often reliant on the territorial force for the area under whose jurisdiction they fall to assist with any serious matter. The statutory responsibility for law and order sits with the territorial police forces even if there is a specialist police force in the locality. These police forces do not have independent Police Authorities and their founding statutes (if any) do not generally prescribe their structure and formation.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ports_police">Ports police</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Ports police">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are two types of <a href="/wiki/Port_police" class="mw-redirect" title="Port police">port police</a> in the United Kingdom — most are sworn in under the 1847 Act, but a few have Acts specific to their port.
</p>
<dl><dt>Ports police operating under the Harbours, Docks, and Piers Clauses Act 1847</dt></dl>
<p>For every port/harbour, an individual Act of Parliament (or, more recently, a Harbour (Revision) Order) can incorporate parts of the <a href="/wiki/Harbours,_Docks,_and_Piers_Clauses_Act_1847" class="mw-redirect" title="Harbours, Docks, and Piers Clauses Act 1847">Harbours, Docks, and Piers Clauses Act 1847</a> (HDPCA) and apply them to that specific port/harbour. Officers of port police forces are sworn in as "<a href="/wiki/Special_constable" title="Special constable">special constables</a>" under section 79 of the 1847 Act, as incorporated by the individual local Act. As a result, officers have the full powers of a constable on any land owned by the harbour, dock, or port and at any place within one mile of any owned land.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Marine_Navigation_Act_2013" title="Marine Navigation Act 2013">Marine Navigation Act 2013</a> has potentially enabled ports contables in England &amp; Wales to act as constables beyond this one mile limit, in relation to policing purposes connected with the port only, in a <a href="/wiki/Police_area" title="Police area">police area</a> where consent has been obtained from the relevant Chief Constable.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  This act does not however give general police powers to ports constables beyond their core jurisdiction as set out in the 1847 act, merely in relation to policing purposes connected to the port as set out in the Act.  As of 2014, 3 ports police forces (Dover, Teesport and Bristol) have sought and received consent from the local Chief Constable, with a fourth (Liverpool) in the process of applying for it.  This has enabled these 3 ports forces to act as constables, in relation to policing purposes connected to the port, throughout the police area in which they are geographically located.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  There are 224 constables sworn in under the 1847 Act.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Serious or major incidents or crime generally become the responsibility of the local territorial police force.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parks_police">Parks police</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Parks police">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl><dt>Parks not controlled by local authorities</dt></dl>
<p>These small constabularies are responsible for policing specific land and parks. Officers of these forces have the powers of a constable within their limited jurisdiction. They are not <i>constables</i> as dealt with in the general Police Acts.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Epping_Forest_Keepers" title="Epping Forest Keepers">Epping Forest Keepers</a>
<dl><dd>Current powers derive from regulations made under <a href="/wiki/Epping_Forest_Act_1878" title="Epping Forest Act 1878">Epping Forest Act 1878</a></dd></dl></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kew_Constabulary" title="Kew Constabulary">Kew Constabulary</a> (formerly Royal Botanic Gardens Constabulary)
<dl><dd>Constables of this force have full police powers whilst on land belonging to the Royal Botanical Gardens as per the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Parks_Regulation_Act_1872&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Parks Regulation Act 1872 (page does not exist)">Parks Regulation Act 1872</a> as amended by section 3 (a) of the Parks Regulation (Amendment) Act 1974.</dd></dl></li></ul>
<p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Parks_Regulation_Act_1872&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Parks Regulation Act 1872 (page does not exist)">Parks Regulation Act 1872</a> provides for the <a href="/wiki/Police_Oath" class="mw-redirect" title="Police Oath">attestation</a> of <i>parks constables</i>.
</p>
<dl><dt>Parks controlled by local authorities</dt></dl>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Birmingham_Parks_Police_photo.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Birmingham_Parks_Police_photo.jpg/220px-Birmingham_Parks_Police_photo.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="272" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Birmingham_Parks_Police_photo.jpg/330px-Birmingham_Parks_Police_photo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d9/Birmingham_Parks_Police_photo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="404" data-file-height="500" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Birmingham_Parks_Police_photo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A photograph of officers of the Birmingham Parks Police, taken between c. 1900 and 1910.</div></div></div>
<p>Over history, a number of local authorities outside London have maintained their own parks police forces, the most notable being Liverpool (<a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Parks_Police" title="Liverpool Parks Police">Liverpool Parks Police</a>) and Birmingham (<a href="/wiki/Birmingham_Parks_Police" title="Birmingham Parks Police">Birmingham Parks Police</a>). No local authority parks police forces currently exist outside London, although the legal powers for them to do so (granted by various local Acts of Parliament) survive in a limited number of cases.
</p><p>In London, these constabularies are responsible for enforcing byelaws within the parks and open spaces of their respective local authorities. Members of the constabularies are sworn as <a href="/wiki/Constable#United_Kingdom" title="Constable">constables</a> under article 18 of the Greater London Parks and Open Spaces Order 1967.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Members of the constabularies are constables only in relation to the enforcement of the parks byelaws (which, by definition, apply only in the parks).<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Parks_Police_Service" title="Parks Police Service">Parks Police Service</a>, created through the merger of <a href="/wiki/Hammersmith_and_Fulham_Parks_Constabulary" title="Hammersmith and Fulham Parks Constabulary">Hammersmith and Fulham Parks Constabulary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Royal_Borough_of_Kensington_and_Chelsea_Parks_Police" title="Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea Parks Police">Royal Borough of Kensington and Chelsea Parks Police</a> in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hampstead_Heath_Constabulary" class="mw-redirect" title="Hampstead Heath Constabulary">Hampstead Heath Constabulary</a>, also appointed under the Corporation of London opens spaces act sec 16, 1878 with the full powers and privileges of a Police Constable.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hillingdon_Parks_Patrol_Service" title="Hillingdon Parks Patrol Service">Hillingdon Parks Patrol Service</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wandsworth_Parks_and_Events_Police" title="Wandsworth Parks and Events Police">Wandsworth Parks and Events Police</a></li></ul>
<p>Some of these constables have (or have had) a shared role as security staff for their own local authority's buildings and housing estates with appropriate changes of badges and/or uniform being made when changing to/from park duties.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_police">Service police</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Service police">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Military_police_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Military police of the United Kingdom">Military police of the United Kingdom</a></div>
<p>Each branch of the military has its own police service, though the powers of a service police officer are identical and recipricol across all three services.  The service police is made up of the:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy_Police" title="Royal Navy Police">Royal Navy Police</a></li></ul>
<p>- including the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Marines_Police" title="Royal Marines Police">Royal Marines Police</a>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Royal_Military_Police" title="Royal Military Police">Royal Military Police</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force_Police" title="Royal Air Force Police">Royal Air Force Police</a></li></ul>
<p>In the UK, the service police exercise jurisdiction over those serving in the military in any capacity and those civilians subject to service discipline as defined by the Armed Forces Act 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>  They are not 'constables' and do not have any policing powers in relation to the general public in normal circumstances.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>  In <a href="/wiki/British_Forces_Germany" title="British Forces Germany">British Forces Germany</a>, under the Status Of Forces Act, military police have jurisdiction over British Forces personnel, their families, MOD contractors, and <a href="/wiki/NAAFI" class="mw-redirect" title="NAAFI">NAAFI</a> staff.
</p><p>Service Police are PACE trained and all investigations are PACE compliant.  They make regular use of civilian police facilities often conducting joint investigations where necessary. The Service Police are able to investigate all crime within their jurisdiction, up to and including Murder, however within the UK, offences of murder and sudden deaths are passed to the local police force as per national jurisdiction agreements.
</p><p>Whilst operating in conflict zones the military police will conduct the full range of policing including murder investigations as evidenced by the Sgt Blackman investigation.<sup id="cite_ref-PACE_1984_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PACE_1984-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overseas_law_enforcement_in_the_UK">Overseas law enforcement in the UK</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_law_enforcement_agencies_in_the_United_Kingdom,_Crown_dependencies_and_British_Overseas_Territories&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Overseas law enforcement in the UK">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_in_the_United_Kingdom#Overseas_police_forces_in_the_UK" title="Law enforcement in the United Kingdom">Law enforcement in the United Kingdom §&#160;Overseas police forces in the UK</a></div>
<p>There are certain instances where police forces of other nations operate in a limited degree in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>:
</p>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Garda_S%C3%ADoch%C3%A1na" title="Garda Síochána">Garda Síochána</a></i> – Under an agreement between the <a href="/wiki/British_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="British Government">British Government</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Ireland" title="Government of Ireland">Irish Government</a> and under the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> <a href="/wiki/Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" class="mw-redirect" title="Convention on the Law of the Sea">Convention on the Law of the Sea</a>, the Garda Síochána and the <a href="/wiki/Radiological_Protection_Institute_of_Ireland" title="Radiological Protection Institute of Ireland">Radiological Protection Institute of Ireland</a> are allowed to inspect the <a href="/wiki/Sellafield" title="Sellafield">Sellafield</a> nuclear facility in <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a>.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Direction_centrale_de_la_police_aux_fronti%C3%A8res" title="Direction centrale de la police aux frontières">Police aux Frontières</a></i> – As part of the <a href="/wiki/Channel_Tunnel" title="Channel Tunnel">Channel Tunnel</a> agreement between the British and French governments, the Police aux Frontières maintains a presence at <a href="/wiki/St_Pancras_railway_station" title="St Pancras railway station">St. Pancras International</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ebbsfleet_International_railway_station" title="Ebbsfleet International railway station">Ebbsfleet International</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ashford_International_railway_station" title="Ashford International railway station">Ashford International</a> railway stations and on <a href="/wiki/Eurostar" title="Eurostar">Eurostar</a> trains. The British Transport Police have a reciprocal arrangement at the <a href="/wiki/Gare_du_Nord" title="Gare du Nord">Gare du Nord</a> in Paris.  The Police aux Frontieres also maintain a presence at passport control at the <a href="/wiki/Eurotunnel" class="mw-redirect" title="Eurotunnel">Eurotunnel</a> terminal in Folkestone and at <a href="/wiki/Dover" title="Dover">Dover</a> port, whilst Kent Police maintains a presence at Coquelles on the French side of the tunnel. Similar arrangements allow the <a href="/wiki/Border_Force" title="Border Force">Border Force</a> to operate juxtaposed controls in France and Belgium.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Military_Police" class="mw-redirect" title="Military Police">Military Police</a> of visiting forces while present within the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Visiting_Forces_Act_1952" title="Visiting Forces Act 1952">Visiting Forces Act 1952</a>.</li></ul>
