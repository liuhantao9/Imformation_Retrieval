	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Baron Thurlow</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Edward_Thurlow,_Baron_Thurlow_by_Sir_Thomas_Lawrence.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Edward_Thurlow%2C_Baron_Thurlow_by_Sir_Thomas_Lawrence.jpg/200px-Edward_Thurlow%2C_Baron_Thurlow_by_Sir_Thomas_Lawrence.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="216" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Edward_Thurlow%2C_Baron_Thurlow_by_Sir_Thomas_Lawrence.jpg/300px-Edward_Thurlow%2C_Baron_Thurlow_by_Sir_Thomas_Lawrence.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Edward_Thurlow%2C_Baron_Thurlow_by_Sir_Thomas_Lawrence.jpg/400px-Edward_Thurlow%2C_Baron_Thurlow_by_Sir_Thomas_Lawrence.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2400" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Edward_Thurlow,_Baron_Thurlow_by_Sir_Thomas_Lawrence.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Edward Thurlow, 1st Baron Thurlow</div></div></div>
<p><b>Baron Thurlow</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Great_Thurlow" title="Great Thurlow">Thurlow</a> in the County of Suffolk, is a title in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Great_Britain" title="Peerage of Great Britain">Peerage of Great Britain</a>. It was created in 1792 for the lawyer and politician <a href="/wiki/Edward_Thurlow,_1st_Baron_Thurlow" title="Edward Thurlow, 1st Baron Thurlow">Edward Thurlow, 1st Baron Thurlow</a>, with remainder to his younger brothers and the heirs male of their bodies.
</p><p>Thurlow had already on his appointment as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Chancellor" title="Lord Chancellor">Lord Chancellor</a> in 1778 been created <b>Baron Thurlow</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Ashfield,_Suffolk" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashfield, Suffolk">Ashfield</a> in the County of Suffolk, in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Great_Britain" title="Peerage of Great Britain">Peerage of Great Britain</a>, with remainder to the heirs male of his body. Lord Thurlow never married and on his death in 1806 the barony of 1778 became extinct, while he was succeeded in the barony of 1792 according to the special remainder by his nephew <a href="/wiki/Edward_Hovell-Thurlow,_2nd_Baron_Thurlow" title="Edward Hovell-Thurlow, 2nd Baron Thurlow">Edward, the second Baron</a>. The latter was the son of the Right Reverend <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Thurlow_(bishop)" title="Thomas Thurlow (bishop)">Thomas Thurlow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Durham" title="Bishop of Durham">Bishop of Durham</a> from 1787 to 1791. Lord Thurlow gained a reputation as a minor poet. In 1813, he married the actress <a href="/wiki/Mary_Catherine_Bolton" title="Mary Catherine Bolton">Mary Catherine Bolton</a>, and they had three sons. In 1814 he assumed by royal licence the additional surname of Hovell, in commemoration of his ancestor Sir Richard Hovell.
</p><p>His grandson, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Hovell-Thurlow-Cumming-Bruce,_5th_Baron_Thurlow" title="Thomas Hovell-Thurlow-Cumming-Bruce, 5th Baron Thurlow">the fifth Baron</a> (who succeeded his elder brother), was a <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal</a> politician and served as <a href="/wiki/Paymaster-General" class="mw-redirect" title="Paymaster-General">Paymaster-General</a> in 1886. In 1873 he assumed by royal licence the additional surname of Bruce, which was that of his father-in-law, <a href="/wiki/James_Bruce,_8th_Earl_of_Elgin" title="James Bruce, 8th Earl of Elgin">James Bruce, 8th Earl of Elgin</a>. One year later, Lord Thurlow assumed by royal licence the additional surname of Cumming.
</p><p>In 1952, to pay the death duties of his father, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Hovell-Thurlow-Cumming-Bruce,_6th_Baron_Thurlow" title="Charles Hovell-Thurlow-Cumming-Bruce, 6th Baron Thurlow">the sixth Baron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Hovell-Thurlow-Cumming-Bruce,_7th_Baron_Thurlow" title="Henry Hovell-Thurlow-Cumming-Bruce, 7th Baron Thurlow">Henry, the seventh Baron</a>, sold the family house in Surrey, <a href="/wiki/Baynards_Park" title="Baynards Park">Baynards Park</a>. It was later owned by helicopter entrepreneur <a href="/wiki/Alan_Bristow" title="Alan Bristow">Alan Bristow</a>, under whose ownership the <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">Grade II-listed</a> <a href="/wiki/Elizabethan_architecture" title="Elizabethan architecture">Elizabethan</a> <a href="/wiki/English_country_houses" class="mw-redirect" title="English country houses">country house</a> burnt down in 1980.
</p><p>The title was then held by the seventh Baron, a soldier who won a <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Order" title="Distinguished Service Order">DSO</a> and bar in World War II and served as <a href="/wiki/General_officer_commanding" title="General officer commanding">GOC</a> in Malta. He was succeeded by his younger brother in 1971. The <a href="/wiki/Francis_Hovell-Thurlow-Cumming-Bruce,_8th_Baron_Thurlow" title="Francis Hovell-Thurlow-Cumming-Bruce, 8th Baron Thurlow">eighth Baron Thurlow</a> was a diplomat and notably served as <a href="/wiki/High_Commissioner_to_New_Zealand" class="mw-redirect" title="High Commissioner to New Zealand">High Commissioner to New Zealand</a> and <a href="/wiki/High_Commissioner_to_Nigeria" class="mw-redirect" title="High Commissioner to Nigeria">Nigeria</a> and as <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_the_Bahamas" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor of the Bahamas">Governor of the Bahamas</a>. In 2013 he was succeeded by his son, the <a href="/wiki/Roualeyn_Hovell-Thurlow-Cumming-Bruce,_9th_Baron_Thurlow" title="Roualeyn Hovell-Thurlow-Cumming-Bruce, 9th Baron Thurlow">ninth Baron</a>, who in 2015 was elected by the hereditary peers to sit in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a>.
</p><p><br />
</p>
