<p><b>Simone Greiner-Petter-Memm</b> (born September 15, 1967 in <a href="/wiki/Jena" title="Jena">Jena</a>, as <i>Simone Greiner-Petter</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Cross_country_skier" class="mw-redirect" title="Cross country skier">cross country skier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biathlon" title="Biathlon">biathlete</a> who competed from 1987 to 1997 in <a href="/wiki/Cross_country_skiing" class="mw-redirect" title="Cross country skiing">cross country skiing</a> and from 1992 to 2000 in <a href="/wiki/Biathlon" title="Biathlon">biathlon</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cross_country_skiing_results">Cross country skiing results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simone_Greiner-Petter-Memm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cross country skiing results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a cross country skier at the <a href="/wiki/1988_Winter_Olympics" title="1988 Winter Olympics">1988 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Calgary" title="Calgary">Calgary</a>, Greiner-Petter-Memm finished fifth in the 4 × 5&#160;km relay and 15th in the 20&#160;km event. She also finished 15th in the 20&#160;km event at the <a href="/wiki/1987_FIS_Nordic_World_Ski_Championships" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships">1987 FIS Nordic World Ski Championships</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oberstdorf" title="Oberstdorf">Oberstdorf</a>. Greiner-Petter-Memm's only individual World Cup victory was in a 20&#160;km event in 1988.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biathlon_results">Biathlon results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simone_Greiner-Petter-Memm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Biathlon results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Switching to biathlon in 1992, Greiner-Petter-Memm earned a total of four individual World Cup career victories and had her best overall World cup finish of sixth in the 1993-94 season. At the <a href="/wiki/1994_Winter_Olympics" title="1994 Winter Olympics">1994 Winter Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lillehammer" title="Lillehammer">Lillehammer</a>, she earned a silver medal in the 4 × 7.5&#160;km relay despite blowing a noticeable lead during the standing phase of her leg. Greiner-Petter-Memm missed all five of her targets and missed with her three additional bullets, which results in five 150 m penalty loops afterwards.
</p><p>She also earned gold medals at the <a href="/wiki/Biathlon_World_Championships" title="Biathlon World Championships">Biathlon World Championships</a> in the 4 × 7.5&#160;km relay event (1995–1997, 1999).
</p>

<p>Her husband is <a href="/wiki/Silvio_Memm" title="Silvio Memm">Silvio Memm</a>, a former <a href="/wiki/Nordic_combined" title="Nordic combined">nordic combined</a> skier.
</p>
