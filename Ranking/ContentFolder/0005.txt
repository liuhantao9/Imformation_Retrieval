<p><b>Roger Michael Needham</b>, <a href="/wiki/Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Commander of the Order of the British Empire">CBE</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Fellow of the Royal Society">FRS</a>, <a href="/wiki/FREng" class="mw-redirect" title="FREng">FREng</a> (9 February 1935 &#8211; 1 March 2003)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Needham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He attended <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_Grammar_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Doncaster Grammar School">Doncaster Grammar School for Boys</a> in <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a> (then in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding</a>).
</p><p>Needham began his undergraduate studies at <a href="/wiki/St_John%27s_College,_Cambridge" title="St John&#39;s College, Cambridge">St John's College, Cambridge</a> in 1953, graduating with a B.A. in 1956 in mathematics and philosophy.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph.D.</a> thesis was on applications of digital <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computers</a> to the automatic classification and retrieval of documents. He worked on a variety of key computing projects in <a href="/wiki/Computer_Security" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer Security">security</a>, <a href="/wiki/Operating_systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Operating systems">operating systems</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_architecture" title="Computer architecture">computer architecture</a> (capability systems) and <a href="/wiki/Local_area_network" title="Local area network">local area networks</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Needham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among his theoretical contributions is the development of the <a href="/wiki/Burrows-Abadi-Needham_logic" class="mw-redirect" title="Burrows-Abadi-Needham logic">Burrows-Abadi-Needham logic</a> for <a href="/wiki/Authentication" title="Authentication">authentication</a>, generally known as the <a href="/wiki/BAN_logic" class="mw-redirect" title="BAN logic">BAN logic</a>. His <a href="/wiki/Needham%E2%80%93Schroeder" class="mw-redirect" title="Needham–Schroeder">Needham–Schroeder</a> (coinvented with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schroeder" title="Michael Schroeder">Michael Schroeder</a>) <a href="/wiki/Security_protocol" class="mw-redirect" title="Security protocol">security protocol</a> forms the basis of the <a href="/wiki/Kerberos_(protocol)" title="Kerberos (protocol)">Kerberos</a> <a href="/wiki/Authentication" title="Authentication">authentication</a> and <a href="/wiki/Key_exchange" title="Key exchange">key exchange</a> system. He also codesigned the <a href="/wiki/Tiny_Encryption_Algorithm" title="Tiny Encryption Algorithm">TEA</a> and <a href="/wiki/XTEA" title="XTEA">XTEA</a> <a href="/wiki/Encryption_algorithm" class="mw-redirect" title="Encryption algorithm">encryption algorithms</a>. He pioneered the technique of protecting passwords using a one-way hash function.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-guardian_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He joined Cambridge's <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_University_Computer_Laboratory" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge University Computer Laboratory">Computer Laboratory</a>, then called the Mathematical Laboratory, in 1962, became head of the laboratory in 1980, was made a <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> in 1981 and remained with the laboratory until his retirement in 1995. Needham then set up <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>'s UK-based <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Research" title="Microsoft Research">Research Laboratory</a> in 1997. He was also one of the founding <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">Fellows</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wolfson_College,_Cambridge" title="Wolfson College, Cambridge">University College</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">Cambridge</a>, which became <a href="/wiki/Wolfson_College,_Cambridge" title="Wolfson College, Cambridge">Wolfson College</a>.
</p><p>Needham was elected to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a> in 1985, became a fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Engineering" title="Royal Academy of Engineering">Royal Academy of Engineering</a> in 1993 and received a <a href="/wiki/Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Commander of the Order of the British Empire">CBE</a> for his contributions to <a href="/wiki/Computing" title="Computing">computing</a> in 2001. He also was a longtime and respected member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Association_for_Cryptologic_Research" title="International Association for Cryptologic Research">International Association for Cryptologic Research</a>, the <a href="/wiki/IEEE_Computer_Society" title="IEEE Computer Society">IEEE Computer Society</a> Technical Committee on Security and Privacy and the <a href="/wiki/University_Grants_Committee_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="University Grants Committee (UK)">University Grants Committee</a>. He was made a fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a> in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Needham held honorary doctorate degrees from <a href="/wiki/University_of_Twente" title="University of Twente">University of Twente</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loughborough_University" title="Loughborough University">Loughborough University</a>, and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kent" title="University of Kent">University of Kent</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Needham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Needham married <a href="/wiki/Karen_Sp%C3%A4rck_Jones" title="Karen Spärck Jones">Karen Spärck Jones</a> in 1958. Needham died of cancer in March 2003 at his home in <a href="/wiki/Willingham,_Cambridgeshire" title="Willingham, Cambridgeshire">Willingham, Cambridgeshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-seattletimes_peterson_2003_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seattletimes_peterson_2003-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Roger_Needham_Award">Roger Needham Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Needham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Roger Needham Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/British_Computer_Society" title="British Computer Society">British Computer Society</a>, in 2004, established an annual <i><a href="/wiki/Roger_Needham_Award" title="Roger Needham Award">Roger Needham Award</a></i> in Needham's honour.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="EuroSys_Roger_Needham_PhD_Award">EuroSys Roger Needham PhD Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Roger_Needham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: EuroSys Roger Needham PhD Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A separate prize honoring Roger Needham has been established by <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.eurosys.org/">EuroSys</a>, the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.eurosys.org/awards/needham-award">"EuroSys Roger Needham PhD Award"</a>.  This annual prize awards €2,000 to a PhD student from a European University whose thesis is regarded to be an exceptional, innovative contribution to knowledge in the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://tocs.acm.org/">Computer Systems</a> area.  Past winners have been:
</p>
<ul><li>2018 Dennis Andriesse (<a href="/wiki/Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam" title="Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam">Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam</a>) for his PhD thesis entitled <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://syssec.mistakenot.net/theses/phd-thesis.pdf">Analyzing and Securing Binaries Through Static Disassembly</a></i></li>
<li>2015 Cristiano Giuffrida (<a href="/wiki/Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam" title="Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam">Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam</a>) for his PhD thesis entitled <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://dare.ubvu.vu.nl/handle/1871/52113">Safe and Automatic Live Update</a></i></li>
<li>2014 Torvald Riegel (<a href="/wiki/Technische_Universitaet_Dresden" class="mw-redirect" title="Technische Universitaet Dresden">Technische Universitaet Dresden</a>), for his thesis <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://nbn-resolving.de/urn:nbn:de:bsz:14-qucosa-115596">Software Transactional Memory Building Blocks</a></i></li>
<li>2013 Asia Slowinska (<a href="/wiki/Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam" title="Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam">Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam</a>) for her PhD thesis entitled <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://dare.ubvu.vu.nl/handle/1871/35439">Using Information Flow Tracking to Protect Legacy Binaries</a></i></li>
<li>2012 Derek Murray (<a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>), for his thesis <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/~dgm36/thesis-corrected.pdf">A Distributed Execution Engine Supporting Data-Dependent Control Flow</a></i></li>
<li>2011 Jorrit Herder (<a href="/wiki/Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam" title="Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam">Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam</a>) for <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://hdl.handle.net/1871/16055">Building a Dependable Operating System: Fault Tolerance in MINIX 3</a></i></li>
<li>2010 Willem de Bruijn (<a href="/wiki/Vrije_Universiteit_Amsterdam" title="Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam">Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam</a>) for <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://hdl.handle.net/1871/21639">Adaptive Operating System Design for High Throughput I/O</a></i></li>
