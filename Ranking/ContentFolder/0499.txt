	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">South Pennines</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Spennines.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Spennines.jpg/220px-Spennines.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Spennines.jpg/330px-Spennines.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Spennines.jpg/440px-Spennines.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Spennines.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Footpath across Inchfield Moor</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>South Pennines</b> is a region of <a href="/wiki/Moorland" title="Moorland">moorland</a> and hill country in <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">northern England</a> lying towards the southern end of the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a>. In the west it includes the <a href="/wiki/Rossendale_Valley" title="Rossendale Valley">Rossendale Valley</a> and the <a href="/wiki/West_Pennine_Moors" title="West Pennine Moors">West Pennine Moors</a>. It is bounded by the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a> conurbation in the west and the <a href="/wiki/Forest_of_Bowland" title="Forest of Bowland">Bowland Fells</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Dales" title="Yorkshire Dales">Yorkshire Dales</a> to the north. To the east it is fringed by the towns of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> whilst to the south it is bounded by the <a href="/wiki/Peak_District" title="Peak District">Peak District</a>. The rural <a href="/wiki/South_Pennine_Moors" title="South Pennine Moors">South Pennine Moors</a> constitutes both a <a href="/wiki/Site_of_Special_Scientific_Interest" title="Site of Special Scientific Interest">Site of Special Scientific Interest</a> and <a href="/wiki/Special_Area_of_Conservation" title="Special Area of Conservation">Special Area of Conservation</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_character_area">National character area</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Pennines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: National character area">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Southern Pennines <a href="/wiki/National_character_area" class="mw-redirect" title="National character area">National Character Area</a> defined by <a href="/wiki/Natural_England" title="Natural England">Natural England</a> includes the <a href="/wiki/West_Pennine_Moors" title="West Pennine Moors">West Pennine Moors</a> and is a landscape of broad moorland, flat-topped hills and fields enclosed by <a href="/wiki/Dry_stone" title="Dry stone">dry stone</a> walls. It is formed from the <a href="/wiki/Millstone_Grit" title="Millstone Grit">Millstone Grit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coal_Measures" class="mw-redirect" title="Coal Measures">Coal Measures</a> of the Carboniferous age. Settlements built from local gritstone occupy river valleys with wooded sides. <a href="/wiki/Peat" title="Peat">Peat</a> soils and <a href="/wiki/Blanket_bog" title="Blanket bog">blanket bog</a> on the moors store carbon while high rainfall fills many reservoirs supplying water to the adjacent conurbations. The area is important for recreation having <a href="/wiki/Open_access" title="Open access">open access</a> areas, footpaths and historic <a href="/wiki/Packhorse" title="Packhorse">packhorse</a> routes.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Pennines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Settlements within the South Pennines include <a href="/wiki/Addingham" title="Addingham">Addingham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ilkley" title="Ilkley">Ilkley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Otley" title="Otley">Otley</a> in the north, <a href="/wiki/Bingley" title="Bingley">Bingley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keighley" title="Keighley">Keighley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haworth" title="Haworth">Haworth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxenhope" title="Oxenhope">Oxenhope</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge" title="Sowerby Bridge">Sowerby Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Bridge" title="Hebden Bridge">Hebden Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Todmorden" title="Todmorden">Todmorden</a> in the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">Calder Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a> in the south-east, <a href="/wiki/Darwen" title="Darwen">Darwen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haslingden" title="Haslingden">Haslingden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rawtenstall" title="Rawtenstall">Rawtenstall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>,  and <a href="/wiki/Horwich" title="Horwich">Horwich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ramsbottom" title="Ramsbottom">Ramsbottom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Littleborough,_Greater_Manchester" title="Littleborough, Greater Manchester">Littleborough</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SP_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Moorland in the South Pennines includes <a href="/wiki/Rombalds_Moor" title="Rombalds Moor">Rombalds Moor</a>, Rishworth Moor, Haworth Moor, Turton Moor and Castleshaw Moor.<sup id="cite_ref-SP_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The highest point of the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62 motorway</a>, the highest motorway in England, is at 1,221 feet (372&#160;m) on <a href="/wiki/Windy_Hill_(Pennines)" title="Windy Hill (Pennines)">Windy Hill</a> near Junction 22.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The rivers <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">Aire</a>, Calder and <a href="/wiki/River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Colne, West Yorkshire">Colne</a> drain the area to the east and the <a href="/wiki/River_Roch" title="River Roch">Roch</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Irwell" title="River Irwell">Irwell</a> to the west. The Calder Valley provides a low-level route for road, railway and the <a href="/wiki/Rochdale_Canal" title="Rochdale Canal">Rochdale Canal</a> between Greater Manchester and  Lancashire in the west and <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> to the east.<sup id="cite_ref-SP_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Narrow_Canal" title="Huddersfield Narrow Canal">Huddersfield Narrow Canal</a> and the Manchester to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> railway pass through the <a href="/wiki/Standedge_Tunnels" title="Standedge Tunnels">Standedge Tunnels</a> and the <a href="/wiki/A62_road" title="A62 road">A62 road</a> crosses the moorland at <a href="/wiki/Standedge" title="Standedge">Standedge</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Leeds_and_Liverpool_Canal" title="Leeds and Liverpool Canal">Leeds and Liverpool Canal</a> passes through Bingley and Keighley en route to Skipton.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Natural_England" title="Natural England">Natural England</a> describes the South Pennine moorlands as the <i>Watershed Landscape</i> where the area's high rainfall fills a multitude of reservoirs. The South Pennines and its fringe has a population of more than a million people.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Woodland covers about 4% of the terrain mostly on steep valley sides.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Pennines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The South Pennines provide evidence of <a href="/wiki/Mesolithic" title="Mesolithic">Mesolithic</a>, the late <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a> and  <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Age</a> findings. The <a href="/wiki/Roman_Britain" title="Roman Britain">Romans</a> built roads and built forts in <a href="/wiki/Olicana" class="mw-redirect" title="Olicana">Ilkley</a> and at <a href="/wiki/Castleshaw_Roman_fort" class="mw-redirect" title="Castleshaw Roman fort">Castleshaw</a>. They also dug coal which was further exploited especially during the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a> in the late 18th and early 19th centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Steep-sided valleys with fast flowing streams provided power and water for the area's early mills and factories. Water-powered corn mills and <a href="/wiki/Fulling" title="Fulling">fulling mills</a> were used in <a href="/wiki/Medieval" class="mw-redirect" title="Medieval">medieval</a> times and more fulling mills were built after the mid-16th century as the woollen industry grew. At the end of the 18th century, water powered mills were vital for industrial expansion of the textile industry, initially for spinning cotton, but subsequently for woollens and worsteds.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Pennines&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Walking, mountain-biking and horseriding are common pastimes enjoyed in the area. Numerous walking trails have been established including, amongst many others, the <a href="/wiki/Calderdale_Way" title="Calderdale Way">Calderdale Way</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bronte_Way" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronte Way">Bronte Way</a>. The long-established <a href="/wiki/Pennine_Way" title="Pennine Way">Pennine Way</a> passes through the area.
The South Pennines Walk &amp; Ride Festival which takes place annually in September is a two-week celebration of the area's <a href="/wiki/Landscape" title="Landscape">landscape</a>. Typical events include guided walks, mountain bike rides, horse rides, <a href="/wiki/Orienteering" title="Orienteering">orienteering</a> events and evening talks by noted speakers.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
