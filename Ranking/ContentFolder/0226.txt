		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>This is a <b>list of <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">pseudonyms</a></b>, in various categories.
</p>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable plainlinks">This is a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Lists#Incomplete_lists" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Lists">dynamic list</a> and may never be able to satisfy particular standards for completeness. You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=List_of_pseudonyms&amp;action=edit">expanding it</a> with <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources">reliably sourced</a> entries.</div>
<p>A pseudonym is a name adopted by a person for a particular purpose, which differs from his or her true name. A pseudonym may be used by social <a href="/wiki/Activism" title="Activism">activists</a> or <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">politicians</a> for political purposes or by others for <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religious</a> purposes. It may be a <a href="/wiki/Soldier" title="Soldier">soldier</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Noms_de_guerre" class="mw-redirect" title="Noms de guerre">noms de guerre</a></i> or an <a href="/wiki/Author" title="Author">author</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Pen_name" title="Pen name">nom de plume</a></i>. It may be a performer's <a href="/wiki/Stage_name" title="Stage name">stage name</a> or an alias used by <a href="/wiki/Visual_arts" title="Visual arts">visual artists</a>, <a href="/wiki/Athlete" title="Athlete">athletes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fashion_design" title="Fashion design">fashion designers</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Crime" title="Crime">criminals</a>. Pseudonyms are occasionally used in <a href="/wiki/Fiction" title="Fiction">fiction</a> such as by <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superheroes</a> or other fictional characters.
</p>
