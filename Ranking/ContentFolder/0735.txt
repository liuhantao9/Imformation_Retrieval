<p><b>GAME Digital</b> is the parent company of <b>GAME Retail Limited</b>, a British <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a> company that trades under the <b>GAME</b> brand, stylised as <b>GAME</b>.
</p><p>The company's origins lie in the founding of the Rhino Group by Terry Norris and Bev Ripley in 1992. A number of mergers and acquisitions followed during the 1990s, and in 1999, the company was purchased by Electronics Boutique Limited, which rebranded itself as The Game Group.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The company continued to expand during the 2000s, purchasing several retailers including Gameswizards in Australia.
</p><p>In March 2012, several suppliers, including <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Arts" title="Electronic Arts">Electronic Arts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Capcom" title="Capcom">Capcom</a> refused to supply their latest products due to concerns over Game's creditworthiness.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Game subsequently entered <a href="/wiki/Administration_(law)" title="Administration (law)">administration</a> on 26 March 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-PricewaterhouseCoopers_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PricewaterhouseCoopers-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and was purchased by <a href="/wiki/OpCapita" title="OpCapita">OpCapita</a> the following week.<sup id="cite_ref-Purchase_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Purchase-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Baker Acquisitions was subsequently renamed Game Retail Ltd.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The company operated in the United Kingdom under the GAME and <a href="/wiki/Gamestation" title="Gamestation">Gamestation</a> brands from the acquisition of the latter in May 2007 until the end of 2012, when it was announced that the business would focus solely on the Game brand.
</p><p>GAME Digital plc reported a £10.4 million loss for the 12 month period from July 2016 to July 2017 in its full year results.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the corresponding 2015–2016 period, the company had a net profit of £7.1 million.<sup id="cite_ref-ar_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ar-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2019, Game Digital agreed to a takeover of the company by SportsDirect.com for £52 million.<sup id="cite_ref-takeover_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-takeover-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Game_(retailer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Game_(retailer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The company that went on to become Game was founded by Bev Ripley and Terry Norris as Rhino Group in 1992,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> with stores branded as Future Zone. A year earlier in 1990, a separate company, called Game, was founded by Peter Wickins and Neil Taylor.<sup id="cite_ref-GameTakeover_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GameTakeover-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Rhino Group acquired Virgin Games Stores from <a href="/wiki/W_H_Smith" class="mw-redirect" title="W H Smith">W H Smith</a> in November 1993, increasing the total number of stores to 77.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 1995, the gaming retailer <a href="/wiki/EB_Games" title="EB Games">Electronics Boutique</a> acquired 25% of the Rhino Group, the name of which was changed to <i>Electronics Boutique Limited</i> under license from the business in America, and John Steinbrecher was sent from the United States to manage the chain.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 1999, Electronics Boutique Limited purchased a chain of retail outlets known as Game, from which the current name of the company was derived, for £99 million.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> At the time of the takeover Game had 86 stores.<sup id="cite_ref-GameTakeover_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GameTakeover-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In October 2001, Electronics Boutique Limited went on to purchase the <a href="/wiki/BarrysWorld" title="BarrysWorld">BarrysWorld</a> online gaming service,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> the French retailer <i>ScoreGames</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and the Spanish retailer <i>Centro Mail</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In 2002, Electronics Boutique stores in the United Kingdom and Ireland were rebranded as Game, and the company name was changed to The Game Group. Despite the name change, a 1% sales royalty to Electronics Boutique in the United States continued to be paid until January 2006. Attempts to have this overturned in court in February 2003 were unsuccessful.<sup id="cite_ref-ebregister_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ebregister-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2004, the Game group acquired Gameplay (GB) Limited, an online and mail order retailer of video games, computer software and associated products.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Gameplay (GB) Ltd started trading as Game.co.uk, the group's main online arm in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2006, Game acquired Australian speciality video games retailer <a href="/wiki/Gameswizards" title="Gameswizards">Gameswizards</a> for A$3.8 million and rebranded all Gameswizards outlets as Game stores.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2007, the company announced the acquisition of rival specialist video game chain Gamestation for £74 million. The company stated that they intended to retain the brand.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On 21 April 2010, Lisa Morgan stepped down as CEO, to be replaced by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Bell_(businessman)" title="Christopher Bell (businessman)">Chris Bell</a> as interim CEO.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On 17 June 2010, it was announced that Ian Shepherd had been appointed as CEO, and took up the position on 28 June.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_problems">Financial problems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Game_(retailer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Financial problems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 February 2012 it was announced that both Game and Gamestation would no longer stock new titles from Electronic Arts. This was due to the major games distributor limiting Game's credit terms.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> This affected the pre-orders of <i><a href="/wiki/Mass_Effect_3" title="Mass Effect 3">Mass Effect 3</a></i>, due out the following week.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On 5 March 2012 it was announced that Game and Gamestation stores would not be stocking titles by Capcom. The first title affected by the move was <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Fighter_X_Tekken" title="Street Fighter X Tekken">Street Fighter X Tekken</a></i>. Customers who pre-ordered the special edition from either store were refunded.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early March 2012, Game and Gamestation began heavily discounting stock in an attempt to bring in cash ahead of administration.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 14 March 2012, OpCapita made an unsuccessful bid for the company. The business had been in talks with Game's lenders with the aim of buying its debt and settling its bills with suppliers in full.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The offer was rejected by Game's lenders.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Microsoft and Activision ceased the supply of their products to Game on 15 March.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On 19 March, the company's shares were suspended from the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a> at its own request.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Game_(retailer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GAME_store_closed.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/GAME_store_closed.jpg/220px-GAME_store_closed.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/GAME_store_closed.jpg/330px-GAME_store_closed.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/GAME_store_closed.jpg/440px-GAME_store_closed.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="1944" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GAME_store_closed.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Game, Mill Street, Macclesfield, Cheshire (Closed)</div></div></div>

<p>The company entered administration on 26 March 2012, with <a href="/wiki/PricewaterhouseCoopers" title="PricewaterhouseCoopers">PricewaterhouseCoopers</a> appointed and Ian Shepherd stepping down as CEO. No administrator was appointed for the stores in Ireland and redundancies were only offered to staff in the United Kingdom, resulting in a continuing protest from Irish staff. 277 of Game's 609 stores in the United Kingdom were closed immediately, resulting in 2,104 job losses.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 31 March 2012, Game Group was purchased out of administration by OpCapita.<sup id="cite_ref-Purchase_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Purchase-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> David Hamid was announced as the new executive chairman of Game.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 14 May 2012, TGW Pty Ltd, trading as Game Australia announced it would also enter administration.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On 25 May 2012, Pricewaterhouse Coopers, the administrators of Game Australia made 264 staff redundant and closed 60 stores. An expected further round of redundancies and closures<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> was confirmed on 19 June 2012 when the administrators announced that 16 of the remaining 31 stores would close that day and the remaining 15 would close over the coming weeks, marking the end of Game Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Game's Scandinavian operations were purchased by Nordic Games at the end of May 2012. Nordic Games purchased 55 stores (44 in Sweden and 11 in Norway) and Game's Scandinavian website, and also have a licensing deal that allows continued use of the Game brand.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_listing">Public listing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Game_(retailer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Public listing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 2014, the company announced plans to relist on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> took place in June 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2019, Game Digital agreed to a takeover of the company by SportsDirect.com for £52 million.<sup id="cite_ref-takeover_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-takeover-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Game_(retailer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of 2 April 2013, Game has 328 stores in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Stores traded under the Game and Gamestation brands until the latter was replaced by Game from November 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Stores sell games for all major video game platforms. Games are also sold under a 'preowned' label, where a customer has returned a used game for cash or credit. The company also operates a transactional website, Game.co.uk.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
