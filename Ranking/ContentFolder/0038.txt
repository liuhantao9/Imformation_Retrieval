<p>The <i><b>Journal of Documentation</b></i> is a double-blind <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer-reviewed</a> <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">academic journal</a> covering theories, concepts, models, frameworks, and philosophies in <a href="/wiki/Information_science" title="Information science">information science</a>. The journal publishes scholarly articles, research reports, and critical reviews.
</p><p>The scope of the <i>Journal of Documentation</i> is broadly <a href="/wiki/Information_science" title="Information science">information sciences</a>, encompassing all of the academic and professional disciplines which deal with recorded information. These include, but are not limited to, information science, <a href="/wiki/Library_science" title="Library science">library science</a>, and related disciplines, <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_management" title="Knowledge management">knowledge management</a>, <a href="/wiki/Knowledge_organization" title="Knowledge organization">knowledge organization</a>, <a href="/wiki/Information_seeking" title="Information seeking">information seeking</a>, <a href="/wiki/Information_retrieval" title="Information retrieval">information retrieval</a>, human information behaviour, and <a href="/wiki/Digital_literacy" title="Digital literacy">digital literacy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The main audience for the journal is educators, scholars, researchers, and policy-makers in information-related areas. It published quarterly between 1945 and 1996, expanding to five issues per year between 1997-1999. Since 2000, it is published bimonthly. It is currently edited by David Bawden (<a href="/wiki/City_University_London" class="mw-redirect" title="City University London">City University London</a>.)<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In celebration of the journal's 60th anniversary, a series of review articles were published between 2004-2006, which commented on the significance of eleven articles originally published in the <i>Journal</i> in the previous six decades.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The journal is published by <a href="/wiki/Emerald_Group_Publishing" title="Emerald Group Publishing">Emerald Group Publishing</a>. It is available for subscription singularly and also as part of an online subscription to the Emerald Library and Information Studies Subject Collection.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
