	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Leone Sextus Tollemache</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>
<b>Leone Sextus Denys Oswolf Fraudatifilius Tollemache-Tollemache de Orellana Plantagenet Tollemache-Tollemache</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/uː/: &#39;oo&#39; in &#39;goose&#39;">uː</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="&#39;m&#39; in &#39;my&#39;">m</span><span title="/eɪ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;face&#39;">eɪ</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span></span> <wbr /><span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;t&#39; in &#39;tie&#39;">t</span><span title="/ɒ/: &#39;o&#39; in &#39;body&#39;">ɒ</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="&#39;m&#39; in &#39;my&#39;">m</span><span title="/æ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;bad&#39;">æ</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">TOOL</span>-mayk-<span style="font-size:90%">TOL</span>-mak</i></a>; 10 June 1884 – 20 February 1917) was a <a href="/wiki/Captain_(OF-2)" class="mw-redirect" title="Captain (OF-2)">captain</a> in the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> who died during the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">First World War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He has been stated, incorrectly, to have had the longest English surname on record,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> or the English surname with the most <a href="/wiki/Double-barrelled_name" title="Double-barrelled name">multiple barrels</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  His last name is the <a href="/wiki/Double-barrelled_name" title="Double-barrelled name">double-barrelled</a> "Tollemache-Tollemache"; his other names (including the first instance of "Tollemache-Tollemache") are forenames<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> which have been mistaken by some chroniclers as part of his last name. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Leone_Sextus_Tollemache&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Leone_Tollemache1891_census.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Leone_Tollemache1891_census.jpg/220px-Leone_Tollemache1891_census.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="30" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Leone_Tollemache1891_census.jpg/330px-Leone_Tollemache1891_census.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Leone_Tollemache1891_census.jpg/440px-Leone_Tollemache1891_census.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1406" data-file-height="191" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Leone_Tollemache1891_census.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Extract from 1891 Census return for South Witham, Lincolnshre showing the Tollemache family. Leone is recorded as "Leone S. D. O. F. F. T. T. D. O. P.", in the second line from below.</div></div></div>
<p>Leone was born in <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a>, the sixth son of the eccentric clergyman <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Tollemache-Tollemache" class="mw-redirect" title="Ralph Tollemache-Tollemache">Ralph Tollemache-Tollemache</a>. He was the eighth of Ralph's many children by his second wife, Dora Cleopatra Maria Lorenza de Orellana. In common with his many brothers and sisters, his father gave him an unconventional name.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His surname at birth was "Tollemache-Tollemache", his father having doubled his original surname, "Tollemache", in 1876 after his second marriage. "de Orellana" derives from the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_naming_customs" title="Spanish naming customs">naming customs of Spain</a>, where his mother came from, and is a forename rather than part of his surname. The first "Tollemache-Tollemache" also seems to be an unusual forename. Leone was Ralph's sixth son, hence "Sextus". "Fraudatifilius" is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "son of the <a href="/wiki/Fraud" title="Fraud">defrauded</a> one". "Leone" repeats a pattern seen in the names of his elder brothers and sisters (Lyonel, Lyonesse, Lyulph, Lyona, Leo, Lyonella and Lyonetta). His first five initials, "LSD OF", may include a reference to the divisions of the pre-decimal British currency, <a href="/wiki/%C2%A3sd" title="£sd">£sd</a>, for pounds, shillings, and pence followed by half-pence (<i>ob.</i>, "<a href="/wiki/Obol_(coin)" title="Obol (coin)">obol</a>") and farthings (<i>i.e.</i>, quarter-pence). This may be a reference to a financial dispute between his father Ralph and Ralph's first wife's trustees which had driven him bankrupt in 1863.<sup id="cite_ref-odnb_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odnb-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Similarly, an elder brother was named Lyulph Ydwallo Odin Nestor Egbert Lyonel Toedmag Hugh Erchenwyne Saxon Esa Cromwell Orma Nevill Dysart Plantagenet Tollemache-Tollemache<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>—his first 15 initials spell "LYONEL THE SECOND". In practice, Leone shortened his name to "Leone Sextus Tollemache".
</p><p>He joined the British Army, attending the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Military_Academy_Sandhurst" title="Royal Military Academy Sandhurst">Royal Military College, Sandhurst</a>, in 1902. He was commissioned into the <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire_Regiment" class="mw-redirect" title="Leicestershire Regiment">Leicestershire Regiment</a> in 1903. Before the First World War, he served in <a href="/wiki/British_India" class="mw-redirect" title="British India">British India</a> and at <a href="/wiki/Fermoy" title="Fermoy">Fermoy</a> in Ireland.
</p><p>He married an Irishwoman, Kathleen Mary Mills (daughter of Joseph Mills and Charlotte née Bloomfield), at <a href="/wiki/Acomb,_North_Yorkshire" title="Acomb, North Yorkshire">Acomb, North Yorkshire</a> on 23 April 1914.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> They honeymooned in Fermoy. Their son was born on 12 January 1915 in White House in Acomb; Kathleen died in childbirth, but the son, Denys Herbert George, survived.
</p><p>On the outbreak of the First World War, he was sent to France on the <a href="/wiki/Union-Castle_Line" title="Union-Castle Line">Union-Castle steamer</a> <a href="/wiki/SS_Braemar_Castle" title="SS Braemar Castle">SS <i>Braemar Castle</i></a> in September 1914. He kept a personal diary of his experiences. In 1916, Leone was seconded to serve as <a href="/wiki/Brigade_major" title="Brigade major">brigade major</a> in the <a href="/wiki/3rd_Brigade_(Australia)" title="3rd Brigade (Australia)">3rd Australian Infantry Brigade</a> of the <a href="/wiki/1st_Division_(Australia)" title="1st Division (Australia)">1st Australian Division</a> after it was redeployed from <a href="/wiki/Gallipoli_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Gallipoli Campaign">Gallipoli</a> to <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Somme" title="Battle of the Somme">the Somme</a>. He died on active service on 20 February 1917 from <a href="/wiki/Classification_of_pneumonia" title="Classification of pneumonia">double pneumonia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He is buried in the communal war cemetery in <a href="/wiki/Dernancourt" title="Dernancourt">Dernancourt</a> near <a href="/wiki/Albert,_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Albert, France">Albert</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Leone's elder brother Leo (Quintus Tollemache-Tollemache de Orellana Plantagenet) also served in France, in the 1st Battalion of the <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire_Regiment" class="mw-redirect" title="Lincolnshire Regiment">Lincolnshire Regiment</a>. He went missing, presumed killed, on 1 November 1914 and his body was never found. He is commemorated on the <a href="/wiki/Menin_Gate" title="Menin Gate">Menin Gate</a> memorial.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
