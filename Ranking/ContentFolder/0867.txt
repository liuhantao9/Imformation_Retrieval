	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Haddersfield, Jamaica</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the town in Jamaica. For the similarly pronounced town in England, see <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>.</div>
<p class="mw-empty-elt">

</p><p><b>Haddersfield</b>, also known as <b>Huddersfield</b>, is a small town located in the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Mary,_Jamaica" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Mary, Jamaica">St. Mary parish</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>. It is located at 18°24' N, 77°1' W, close to the small town of 'Retreat' and the mouth of the 'Rio Nuevo', some 10 kilometres east of <a href="/wiki/Ocho_Rios" title="Ocho Rios">Ocho Rios</a>. It lies at an altitude of 164 metres above sea level.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Haddersfield,_Jamaica&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the periods 1822-1832 the <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Wellington&#39;s Regiment">33rd Regiment of Foot</a>, recruited from <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, was stationed in Jamaica. At the end of the tour 142 men chose to remain in Jamaica, having married and raised families, some of which may have originated from <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, thereby originating the name. Over 560 officers and men died and were buried in Jamaica during this period, from <a href="/wiki/Endemic_(epidemiology)" title="Endemic (epidemiology)">endemic</a> diseases. On 18 June 1853 the regiment formally became known as "The 33rd (or Duke of Wellington's Regiment)." The regiment's second battalion was again posted to Jamaica (Newcastle Camp) from 18 March 1891 to 10 April 1893.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
