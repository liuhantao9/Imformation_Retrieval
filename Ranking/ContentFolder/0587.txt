<p>A <b>parish church</b> (or <i>parochial church</i>) in <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Local_church" class="mw-redirect" title="Local church">church</a> which acts as the religious centre of a <a href="/wiki/Parish" title="Parish">parish</a>. In many parts of the world, especially in <a href="/wiki/Rural" class="mw-redirect" title="Rural">rural</a> areas, the parish church may play a significant role in community activities, often allowing its premises to be used for non-religious community events. The <a href="/wiki/Church_architecture" title="Church architecture">church building</a> reflects this status, and there is considerable variety in the size and style of parish churches. Many <a href="/wiki/Village" title="Village">villages</a> in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> have churches that date back to the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, but all periods of architecture are represented.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Role">Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parish_church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, the parish church is the basic administrative unit of <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_polity" title="Episcopal polity">episcopal</a> churches. Nearly every part of England is designated as a parish, and most parishes have an <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England_parish_church" title="Church of England parish church">Anglican parish church</a>, which is <a href="/wiki/Consecration" title="Consecration">consecrated</a>. If there is no parish church, the <a href="/wiki/Bishop" title="Bishop">bishop</a> licenses another building for worship, and may designate it as a <a href="/wiki/Parish_Centre_of_Worship" title="Parish Centre of Worship">parish centre of worship</a>. This  building is not <a href="/wiki/Consecrated" class="mw-redirect" title="Consecrated">consecrated</a>, but is dedicated,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="It is only used by the church? (January 2019)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and for most legal purposes it is deemed to be a parish church.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  In areas of increasing secularisation or shifts in religious belief, centres of worship are becoming more common, and larger churches are sold due to their upkeep costs. Instead the church may use <a href="/wiki/Community_centres" class="mw-redirect" title="Community centres">community centres</a> or the facilities of a local church of another denomination.
</p><p>While smaller villages may have a single parish church, larger towns may have a parish church and other smaller churches in various districts. These churches do not have the legal or religious status of 'parish church' and may be described by a variety of terms, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Chapel_of_ease" title="Chapel of ease">chapel of ease</a></i> or <i>mission church</i>. Often the parish church will be the only one to have a full-time <a href="/wiki/Minister_(Christianity)" title="Minister (Christianity)">minister</a>, who will also serve any smaller churches within the parish.
</p><p>In cities without an Anglican <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a> , the parish church may have administrative functions similar to that of a cathedral. However, the <a href="/wiki/Diocese" title="Diocese">diocese</a> will still have a cathedral.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="By_denomination">By denomination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parish_church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: By denomination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>, as the seat of worship for the parish, this church is the one where the members of the parish must go for <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptisms</a> and weddings, unless permission is given by the <a href="/wiki/Parish_priest" class="mw-redirect" title="Parish priest">parish priest</a> (US '<a href="/wiki/Pastor" title="Pastor">pastor</a>') for celebrating these <a href="/wiki/Sacrament" title="Sacrament">sacraments</a> elsewhere. One sign of this is that the parish church is the only one to have a <a href="/wiki/Baptismal_font" title="Baptismal font">baptismal font</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Scotland" title="Church of Scotland">Church of Scotland</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Established_church" class="mw-redirect" title="Established church">established</a> <a href="/wiki/Presbyterian" class="mw-redirect" title="Presbyterian">Presbyterian</a> church, also uses a system of parish churches, covering the whole of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, towns elected publicly funded parish churches from 1780 until 1834, under the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Massachusetts" title="Constitution of Massachusetts">Constitution of Massachusetts</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Protestant_resurgence">Protestant resurgence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Parish_church&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Protestant resurgence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Toward the end of the 20th century, a new resurgence in interest in "parish" churches emerged across the United States. This has given rise to efforts like the <a href="/wiki/Slow_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Slow Church">Slow Church</a> Movement and The Parish Collective which focus heavily on localized involvement across work, home, and church life.<sup id="cite_ref-Slow_Church_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Slow_Church-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ByFaith_magazine_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ByFaith_magazine-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
