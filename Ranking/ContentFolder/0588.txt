<p><b>Holy Trinity Church</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England_parish_church" title="Church of England parish church">parish church</a> in the town of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It opened in 1819 and is a grade II* <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a>. The church is situated just off Trinity Street, named after the church and forming part of the main <a href="/wiki/A640_road" title="A640 road">A640 road</a> from Huddersfield to <a href="/wiki/Rochdale" title="Rochdale">Rochdale</a>, and is just outside the town centre, in the suburb of <a href="/wiki/Marsh,_Huddersfield" title="Marsh, Huddersfield">Marsh</a>. The parish forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Diocese_of_Leeds" title="Anglican Diocese of Leeds">diocese of Leeds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWebster2012116–117_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWebster2012116–117-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nhle_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nhle-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the early 19th century the town of Huddersfield was growing rapidly as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial revolution">industrial revolution</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Parish_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Parish Church">town's parish church</a> was too small to cope. In order to address this, Holy Trinity was built between 1816 and 1819 as a <a href="/wiki/Chapel_of_ease" title="Chapel of ease">chapel of ease</a>. The new church was funded by Benjamin Haigh Allen, a local banker, and designed by the architect <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Taylor_(architect)" title="Thomas Taylor (architect)">Thomas Taylor</a> of Leeds.
</p><p>Benjamin Haigh Allen invited Henry Maddock (1781-1826) to be Holy Trinity's first Perpetual Curate.  Maddock met Allen during a preaching tour he gave in 1814 along with the Revd. Legh Richmond, on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Church_Mission_Society" title="Church Mission Society">Church Mission Society</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Holy Trinity became a parish church in its own right in 1857, serving Marsh and the adjoining suburbs of <a href="/wiki/Edgerton,_Huddersfield" title="Edgerton, Huddersfield">Edgerton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Springwood,_Huddersfield" title="Springwood, Huddersfield">Springwood</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nhle_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nhle-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
