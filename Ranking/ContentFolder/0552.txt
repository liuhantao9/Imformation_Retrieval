<p><b>Gawthorpe</b>, also known as <i>Gawthorp</i>, is a hamlet in the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a> District, in the English county of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, about 4 miles (6&#160;km) east of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-vision_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vision-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The nearest major road is the <a href="/wiki/A642_road" title="A642 road">A642</a> which passes about 0.3 miles (0.5&#160;km) south of the place. In the 19th century Gawthorpe was listed variously as a village<sup id="cite_ref-vision_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vision-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> or a hamlet<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in Lepton township, part of the parish of Kirkheaton in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>. Coal was mined at Gawthorpe around the middle of the 19th century.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was claimed that <a href="/wiki/William_Gascoigne" title="William Gascoigne">Chief Justice Gascoigne</a> was born here,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> however, he was a native of the abandoned village of Gawthorp near Harewood House.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nearby_settlements">Nearby settlements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gawthorpe,_Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nearby settlements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nearby settlements include the town of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, the villages of <a href="/wiki/Kirkheaton" title="Kirkheaton">Kirkheaton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lepton,_West_Yorkshire" title="Lepton, West Yorkshire">Lepton</a> and the hamlet of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gawthorpe_Green&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gawthorpe Green (page does not exist)">Gawthorpe Green</a> where a dyeworks<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/Carding" title="Carding">scribbling mill</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> were located.
</p>
