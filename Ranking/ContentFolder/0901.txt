<p><b>Ilkley</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Spa_town" title="Spa town">spa town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a>. <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, Ilkley civil parish includes the adjacent village of <a href="/wiki/Ben_Rhydding" title="Ben Rhydding">Ben Rhydding</a> and is a <a href="/wiki/Ward_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ward (politics)">ward</a> within the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford_Metropolitan_District_Council" title="City of Bradford Metropolitan District Council">City of Bradford Metropolitan District Council</a>. Approximately 12 miles (19&#160;km) north of <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a> and 17 miles (27&#160;km) northwest of <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, the town lies mainly on the south bank of the <a href="/wiki/River_Wharfe" title="River Wharfe">River Wharfe</a> in <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale" title="Wharfedale">Wharfedale</a>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Dales" title="Yorkshire Dales">Yorkshire Dales</a>.
</p><p>Ilkley's spa town heritage and surrounding countryside make tourism an important local industry. The town centre is characterised by <a href="/wiki/Victorian_architecture" title="Victorian architecture">Victorian architecture</a>, wide streets and floral displays. <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_Moor" title="Ilkley Moor">Ilkley Moor</a>, to the south of the town, is the subject of a folk song, often described as the unofficial anthem of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, "<a href="/wiki/On_Ilkla_Moor_Baht_%27at" title="On Ilkla Moor Baht &#39;at">On Ilkla Moor Baht 'at</a>". The song's words are written in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_dialect" title="Yorkshire dialect">Yorkshire dialect</a>, its title translated as "On Ilkley Moor without a hat."
</p>

<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cup_and_Ring_markings_above_Ilkley.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Cup_and_Ring_markings_above_Ilkley.jpg/170px-Cup_and_Ring_markings_above_Ilkley.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="219" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Cup_and_Ring_markings_above_Ilkley.jpg/255px-Cup_and_Ring_markings_above_Ilkley.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Cup_and_Ring_markings_above_Ilkley.jpg/340px-Cup_and_Ring_markings_above_Ilkley.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1989" data-file-height="2562" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cup_and_Ring_markings_above_Ilkley.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bronze Age markings at Hangingstone Quarry, above Ilkley</div></div></div>
<p>The earliest evidence of habitation in the Ilkley area is from flint arrowheads or <a href="/wiki/Microlith" title="Microlith">microliths</a>, dating to the <a href="/wiki/Mesolithic" title="Mesolithic">Mesolithic</a> period, from about 11,000&#160;BC onwards.<sup id="cite_ref-Prehistory_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Prehistory-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The area around Ilkley has been continuously settled since at least the early <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a>, around 1800&#160;BC; more than 250 <a href="/wiki/Cup_and_ring_mark" title="Cup and ring mark">cup and ring marks</a>, and a curved <a href="/wiki/Swastika" title="Swastika">swastika</a> carving dating to the period have been found on rock outcrops,<sup id="cite_ref-Beckensall_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beckensall-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and archaeological remains of dwellings are found on <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_Moor" title="Ilkley Moor">Ilkley Moor</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Druid" title="Druid">druidical</a> <a href="/wiki/Stone_circle" title="Stone circle">stone circle</a>, the Twelve Apostles Stone Circle, was constructed 2,000 years ago.<sup id="cite_ref-Prehistory_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Prehistory-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Serious interest in the rock art of Ilkley began after the publication in 1879 of the "Prehistoric Rock Sculptures of Ilkley" by Romilly Allen in the <i><a href="/wiki/British_Archaeological_Association" title="British Archaeological Association">Journal of the British Archaeological Association</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Beckensall_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beckensall-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The remains of a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Britain" title="Roman Britain">Roman</a> fort occupy a site near the town centre. Some authorities believe it is <a href="/wiki/Olicana" class="mw-redirect" title="Olicana">Olicana</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> dating to 79&#160;AD, but the identification is not settled. A number of Roman altars have been discovered from the reigns of <a href="/wiki/Antoninus_Pius" title="Antoninus Pius">Antoninus Pius</a> (138 to 161), and <a href="/wiki/Septimius_Severus" title="Septimius Severus">Septimius Severus</a> and his son <a href="/wiki/Caracalla" title="Caracalla">Caracalla</a> (211 to 217).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Three <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxons" title="Anglo-Saxons">Anglo-Saxon</a> crosses from the 8th&#160;century that stood in the churchyard of All Saints' Church have been moved inside to prevent erosion. The church site, as a centre for Christian worship, extends to 627&#160;AD, and the present mainly <a href="/wiki/Victorian_architecture" title="Victorian architecture">Victorian</a>-era church incorporates <a href="/wiki/Medieval_architecture" title="Medieval architecture">medieval</a> elements.<sup id="cite_ref-AllSaintsChurch_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllSaintsChurch-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i>, of 1086, records Ilkley (<i>Ilecliue/Illecliue/Illiclei/Illicleia</i>) as being in the possession of <a href="/wiki/William_de_Percy,_1st_Baron_Percy" class="mw-redirect" title="William de Percy, 1st Baron Percy">William de Percy</a> <a href="/wiki/Baron_Percy" title="Baron Percy">1st Baron Percy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The land was acquired by the Middelton family of Myddelton Lodge, from about a century after the time of <a href="/wiki/William_I_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="William I of England">William the Conqueror</a>. The family lost possession through a series of land sales and mortgage repossessions over a period of about a hundred years from the early 19th&#160;century.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The agents of William Middelton (1815–1885) were responsible for the design of the new town of Ilkley to replace the village which had stood there before.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 17th and 18th&#160;centuries the town gained a reputation for the efficacy of its water. In the 19th&#160;century it became established as a fashionable <a href="/wiki/Spa_town" title="Spa town">spa town</a>, with the construction of Ben Rhydding Hydro, a <a href="/wiki/Hydropathic_establishment" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydropathic establishment">hydropathic establishment</a> at Wheatley, a mile to the east, between 1843 and 1844.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ShifrinBenRhydding_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ShifrinBenRhydding-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a> underwent hydropathic treatment at Wells House when his book <i><a href="/wiki/On_the_Origin_of_Species" title="On the Origin of Species">On the Origin of Species</a></i> was published on 24 November 1859, whilst staying with his family at North View House (now Hillside Court).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Tourists flocked to 'take the waters' and bathe in the cold-water spring. Wheatley was renamed Ben Rhydding after the Hydro, which has been demolished.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Manor_House,_Ilkley.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/The_Manor_House%2C_Ilkley.jpg/220px-The_Manor_House%2C_Ilkley.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="126" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/The_Manor_House%2C_Ilkley.jpg/330px-The_Manor_House%2C_Ilkley.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/The_Manor_House%2C_Ilkley.jpg/440px-The_Manor_House%2C_Ilkley.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1244" data-file-height="714" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Manor_House,_Ilkley.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Manor House, Ilkley</div></div></div>
<p>Development based on the Hydro movement, and on the establishment of convalescent homes and hospitals, was accelerated in August 1865 by the construction of the Otley and Ilkley Joint Railway, which linked to the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_and_Bradford_Railway" title="Leeds and Bradford Railway">Leeds and Bradford Railway</a> and the <a href="/wiki/North_Eastern_Railway_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="North Eastern Railway (UK)">North Eastern Railway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-butt_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-butt-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-casserley_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-casserley-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Midland_Railway" title="Midland Railway">Midland Railway</a> built a connection to <a href="/wiki/Skipton_railway_station" title="Skipton railway station">Skipton</a> via <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Abbey_railway_station" title="Bolton Abbey railway station">Bolton Abbey</a> in May 1888.<sup id="cite_ref-butt_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-butt-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other Victorian visitors to the town included <a href="/wiki/Madame_Tussaud" class="mw-redirect" title="Madame Tussaud">Madame Tussaud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The only remaining hydro building is the white cottage known as <a href="/wiki/White_Wells" title="White Wells">White Wells</a> House. The cottage can be seen and visited on the edge of the moor overlooking the town.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town has a parish council and although it is a town and has a town hall, the parish council has not exercised its right to be called a town council.  The parish consists of four wards and elects 14 councillors: Ilkley North (3 councillors), Ilkley South (3), Ilkley West (4) and Ben Rhydding (4).<sup id="cite_ref-Parish_Council_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parish_Council-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The parish council precept is collected with the annual <a href="/wiki/Council_Tax" title="Council Tax">Council Tax</a> to fund its running and to aid the development of local projects.<sup id="cite_ref-Parish_Council_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parish_Council-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ilkley is in the <a href="/wiki/Keighley_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Keighley (UK Parliament constituency)">Keighley UK Parliament constituency</a> whose seat is held by John Grogan, Labour MP. He replaced <a href="/wiki/Kris_Hopkins" title="Kris Hopkins">Kris Hopkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> MP,<sup id="cite_ref-MP_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MP-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> in the 2017 election. Kris Hopkins replaced <a href="/wiki/Ann_Cryer" title="Ann Cryer">Ann Cryer</a> and was elected in the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2010 United Kingdom general election">2010 general election</a>. Ann Cryer was elected in the General Election of 1997, and her late husband <a href="/wiki/Bob_Cryer" title="Bob Cryer">Bob Cryer</a> held the seat between 1974 and 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Ilkley is in the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_and_the_Humber_(European_Parliament_constituency)" title="Yorkshire and the Humber (European Parliament constituency)">Yorkshire and the Humber</a> European constituency.<sup id="cite_ref-MP_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MP-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before 1974 Ilkley was an <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a>, a type of <a href="/wiki/Local_government" title="Local government">local government</a> district.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Ilkley Urban District Council shared local government responsibilities with the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding County Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> dissolved urban districts and in 1974 Ilkley adopted its current status as a ward of the metropolitan borough of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford" title="City of Bradford">City of Bradford</a>. Services provided by the urban district council are now run centrally by the City of Bradford Metropolitan District Council.
</p><p>Until 2006 Ilkley civil parish consisted of Ilkley ward, which includes Ben Rhydding, and the north half of Rombalds ward. The latter ward housed the villages of Burley-in-Wharfedale and Menston. The population of the parish in 2001 was therefore considerably higher than it is today, consisting of 24,954 residents. In 2006 Burley-in-Wharfedale and Menston established their own parishes and today Ilkley consists only of Ilkley ward (13,828 residents).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:TheOldBridgeIlkley.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/TheOldBridgeIlkley.jpg/220px-TheOldBridgeIlkley.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/TheOldBridgeIlkley.jpg/330px-TheOldBridgeIlkley.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/TheOldBridgeIlkley.jpg/440px-TheOldBridgeIlkley.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="433" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:TheOldBridgeIlkley.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Old Bridge, Ilkley</div></div></div>

<p>Nearby are the <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a> hamlets of <a href="/wiki/Middleton,_Harrogate" title="Middleton, Harrogate">Middleton</a> (1 mile (1.6&#160;km)), <a href="/wiki/Denton,_North_Yorkshire" title="Denton, North Yorkshire">Denton</a> (2.7 miles (4.3&#160;km)) and <a href="/wiki/Bolton_Abbey" title="Bolton Abbey">Bolton Abbey</a> (6 miles (9.7&#160;km)); the villages of <a href="/wiki/Addingham" title="Addingham">Addingham</a> (3.1 miles (5.0&#160;km)), <a href="/wiki/Burley-in-Wharfedale" class="mw-redirect" title="Burley-in-Wharfedale">Burley-in-Wharfedale</a> (3.8 miles (6.1&#160;km)) and <a href="/wiki/Menston" title="Menston">Menston</a> (5.4 miles (8.7&#160;km)); and the towns of <a href="/wiki/Guiseley" title="Guiseley">Guiseley</a> (7.6 miles (12.2&#160;km)) and <a href="/wiki/Skipton" title="Skipton">Skipton</a> (8 miles (13&#160;km)).<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town is within the travel-to-work radius of <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>. Leeds is 18 miles (29&#160;km) away and Bradford 15.6 miles (25.1&#160;km) by road, with a railway connection offering about 35 trains to each destination per day from <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_railway_station" title="Ilkley railway station">Ilkley railway station</a>. The railway, before the <a href="/wiki/Beeching_axe" class="mw-redirect" title="Beeching axe">Beeching axe</a>, also connected to Addingham, Bolton Abbey and Skipton to the west, and to <a href="/wiki/Otley" title="Otley">Otley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pool-in-Wharfedale" title="Pool-in-Wharfedale">Pool-in-Wharfedale</a>, meeting the main Leeds to <a href="/wiki/Harrogate" title="Harrogate">Harrogate</a> line at <a href="/wiki/Arthington" title="Arthington">Arthington</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town partially straddles the <a href="/wiki/River_Wharfe" title="River Wharfe">River Wharfe</a> in a valley, rising from the river at 230 feet (70&#160;m) to 650 feet (200&#160;m) above sea level, up <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_Moor" title="Ilkley Moor">Ilkley Moor</a> (a bracken and heather covered <a href="/wiki/Moorland" title="Moorland">moorland</a>, with rocky outcrops, to the south) and to 540 feet (160&#160;m) across Middleton Woods in the north. The river runs through the northern extent of the town from west to east, and is crossed by four bridges, in order: a 16th&#160;century three-arched stone bridge, now closed to road traffic; a 19th&#160;century single-span wrought-iron bridge; a <a href="/wiki/Suspension_bridge" title="Suspension bridge">suspension bridge</a> for foot traffic only (a set of concrete stepping stones) and a prefabricated steel arched box-girder bridge. The river is prone to flooding the sports fields (and a few houses) that occupy the <a href="/wiki/Watermeadow" class="mw-redirect" title="Watermeadow">watermeadows</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography">Demography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A person from Ilkley is called an Olicanian which is derived from <a href="/wiki/Olicana" class="mw-redirect" title="Olicana">Olicana</a>, thought to be the name of the <a href="/wiki/Castra" title="Castra">Roman fort</a> Ilkley is built upon. The ethnic make-up of Ilkley's population is 98.02% White, 0.74% mixed, 0.72% Asian, 0.37% Chinese or other ethnic group and 0.14% Black. The largest age group is 45- to 59-year-olds (20.73%).<sup id="cite_ref-2001census_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2001census-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The town is a tourist destination and is used as a base from which to explore the famous moor and the countryside beyond. Ilkley is a shopping town that sells everything from game, fine wine, expensive fashions and fine art. The Victorian parades of the Grove and Brook Street have a selection of speciality shops. The town's original Victorian arcade has been restored as an indoor shopping walkway complete with a fountain and hanging baskets. <a href="/wiki/Bettys_and_Taylors_of_Harrogate" title="Bettys and Taylors of Harrogate">Bettys</a>, the Yorkshire tea room, has a shop in the town.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
Ilkley has little by way of industry or commerce but employers include the <i>Woolmark Company</i>, <i>Spooner Industries</i> and <i>NG Bailey</i>. The town is also home to two breweries, Ilkley Brewery situated on the outskirts and <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale_Brewery" title="Wharfedale Brewery">Wharfedale Brewery</a> which is housed within the grounds of a former 18th&#160;century farmhouse in the town centre.
</p><p>According to the 2001 UK census, the industry of employment of residents aged 16–74 was 18.8% property and business services, 14.8% retail and wholesale, 13.4% education, 13.1% health and social work, 9.6% manufacturing, 5.7% finance, 4.8% construction, 4.8% public administration, 4.4% hotels and restaurants, 4.2% transport and communications, 0.6% energy and water supply, 0.5% agriculture, 0.1% mining, and 5.2% other. Compared with national figures, Ilkley town had a relatively high percentage of residents working in education and health and social work. The town had a relatively low percentage working in transport and communication and manufacturing.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The census recorded the economic activity of residents aged 16–74, 2.2% students were with jobs, 3.7% students without jobs, 5.8% looking after home or family, 2.7% permanently sick or disabled, and 1.8% economically inactive for other reasons.<sup id="cite_ref-employment_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-employment-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Ilkley's 1.8% unemployment rate was low compared with the national rate of 3.3%. The City of Bradford has a relatively low proportion of people who were self-employed compared to the whole of England, but Ilkley's rate of 11.9% was well above both the district and national figures.<sup id="cite_ref-Bradford_economic_activity_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bradford_economic_activity-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bus_Station_-_Station_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_475502.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Bus_Station_-_Station_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_475502.jpg/220px-Bus_Station_-_Station_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_475502.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Bus_Station_-_Station_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_475502.jpg/330px-Bus_Station_-_Station_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_475502.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Bus_Station_-_Station_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_475502.jpg/440px-Bus_Station_-_Station_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_475502.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="437" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bus_Station_-_Station_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_475502.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ilkley bus station</div></div></div>
<p>Ilkley lies on the <a href="/wiki/A65_road" title="A65 road">A65 road</a>, which runs between Leeds and <a href="/wiki/Kendal" title="Kendal">Kendal</a>. The nearest substantial motorways are the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62</a> at Bradford, and the <a href="/wiki/M1_motorway" title="M1 motorway">M1</a> at Leeds, and the <a href="/wiki/A1_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A1 road (Great Britain)">A1(M)</a> at Wetherby. The civil parish is served by two railway stations: <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_railway_station" title="Ilkley railway station">Ilkley</a> terminus<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Ben_Rhydding_railway_station" title="Ben Rhydding railway station">Ben Rhydding</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> These are linked by the <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Wharfedale Line">Wharfedale Line</a> upon which trains run between Ilkley and <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>/<a href="/wiki/Bradford_Forster_Square_railway_station" title="Bradford Forster Square railway station">Bradford Forster Square</a> stations.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From Ilkley bus station services run to Keighley, Leeds, Leeds Bradford Airport, Skipton, Grassington, <a href="/wiki/Malham" title="Malham">Malham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Otley" title="Otley">Otley</a>. The main operators are <a href="/wiki/First_Leeds" title="First Leeds">First Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keighley_Bus_Company" title="Keighley Bus Company">Keighley Bus Company</a> and North Yorkshire County Council.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
Ilkley's nearest airport is <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds Bradford International Airport">Leeds Bradford International Airport</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is a wide variety of sports clubs and facilities in and around Ilkley. Notable clubs include the Ilkley Lawn Tennis and Squash Club, founded in 1880 (member of the <a href="/wiki/Lawn_Tennis_Association" title="Lawn Tennis Association">Lawn Tennis Association</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> which hosts an Ilkley Squash Open that has attracted world-class players such as <a href="/wiki/Peter_Barker" title="Peter Barker">Peter Barker</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_Trophy" title="Ilkley Trophy">Ilkley Trophy</a>, a distinguished <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">tennis</a> tournament for men (an ATP event) as well as women (an ITF W100 event).<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
Furthermore, there are also the <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_Golf_Club" title="Ilkley Golf Club">Ilkley Golf Club</a>, Yorkshire's third oldest golf club founded in 1890,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> and where <a href="/wiki/Colin_Montgomerie" title="Colin Montgomerie">Colin Montgomerie</a> for some years practised the game;<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Ilkley Rugby Club;<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> and Ilkley Harriers Athletics Club.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> There are a number of football, rugby and hockey teams. Ilkley Cricket Club<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> play in the Aire-Wharfe League.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> The nearby Ben Rhydding Sports Club in <a href="/wiki/Ben_Rhydding" title="Ben Rhydding">Ben Rhydding</a> is the home of Ben Rhydding Hockey Club, founded in 1901,<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> and Ben Rhydding Cricket Club. An Ilkley Karate Club is run by former UK champion Mark Outterside.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Public facilities include indoor and outdoor swimming pools, and outdoor tennis courts at Ilkley's <a href="/wiki/Lido_(swimming_pool)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lido (swimming pool)">Lido</a>. Constructed in 1935, it is one of only four public open-air swimming pools in Yorkshire<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> and is a tourist attraction during the summer holiday season.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cycling_races">Cycling races</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cycling races">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The town has seen a number of high-profile international cycle races pass through. The steep climb up to the Cow and Calf rocks at the edge of Ilkley Moor is normally the reason for the races to visit as it presents a short but challenging climb. For three consecutive years between 1994 and 1996, the climb was used as part of the Leeds Classic, which was part of the World Cup series of events.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> In both 2005 and 2007, the town hosted stages of the <a href="/wiki/Tour_of_Britain" title="Tour of Britain">Tour of Britain</a> race, with Ilkley Moor being a categorised climb on each occasion.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, the town had its biggest cycling moment to date when the Tour de France passed through. The route approached the town on the A65 from Otley and passed through the town centre along the A65.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> As a direct consequence of the success of the Tour de France its organisers, ASO, established the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_Yorkshire" title="Tour de Yorkshire">Tour de Yorkshire</a>. Ilkley features in Stage 3 of the inaugural Tour, with the race set to enter from the west along the A65, before turning to climb up past the Cow and Calf.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The annual <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_Yorkshire" title="Tour de Yorkshire">Tour de Yorkshire</a> which is a spin-off from the 2014 Tour de France includes Ilkley as part of the route. In 2018 the stage 2 finish line was at the Cow and Calf rocks, its first ever summit finish.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_attractions">Culture and attractions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Culture and attractions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Ilkley town centre is a tourist attraction with a high number of small independent shops. Of particular note is Lishman's of Ilkley, an award-winning butcher shop whose owner, David Lishman, became one of <a href="/wiki/Rick_Stein" title="Rick Stein">Rick Stein's</a> superheroes in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Ilkley is one of five towns to feature a <a href="/wiki/Bettys_and_Taylors_of_Harrogate" title="Bettys and Taylors of Harrogate">Bettys</a> tearoom and is home to the <a href="/wiki/Michelin_Guide" title="Michelin Guide">Michelin-starred</a> <a href="/wiki/Box_Tree" class="mw-redirect" title="Box Tree">Box Tree</a> restaurant<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> where <a href="/wiki/Marco_Pierre_White" title="Marco Pierre White">Marco Pierre White</a> trained. In 1991 Ilkley won the <a href="/wiki/Entente_Florale" title="Entente Florale">Entente Florale</a> and in 1990 and 2004 the <a href="/wiki/Britain_in_Bloom" title="Britain in Bloom">Britain in Bloom</a> contest in the category of 'Town'. In 2006 Ilkley became a <a href="/wiki/Fairtrade_Town" title="Fairtrade Town">Fairtrade Town</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Manor_House_Museum" title="Manor House Museum">Manor House</a>, one of the town's oldest buildings, houses a museum and art gallery. The museum contains prehistoric artefacts and documents the Roman fort of Olicana – remains of which are exposed at the back of the building – as well as the rise of Ilkley as a Victorian spa town.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_Toy_Museum" title="Ilkley Toy Museum">Ilkley Toy Museum</a> has a collection of toys dating from 350&#160;BC and a collection of English wooden dolls.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ilkley_-_The_Grove_-_September_2007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Ilkley_-_The_Grove_-_September_2007.jpg/220px-Ilkley_-_The_Grove_-_September_2007.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Ilkley_-_The_Grove_-_September_2007.jpg/330px-Ilkley_-_The_Grove_-_September_2007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Ilkley_-_The_Grove_-_September_2007.jpg/440px-Ilkley_-_The_Grove_-_September_2007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="960" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ilkley_-_The_Grove_-_September_2007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Grove, Ilkley's principal shopping street, designed with wide pavements for promenading</div></div></div>
<p>Ilkley's rural surroundings attract walkers and cyclists to the area. The landmark Cow and Calf rocks, which overlook the town on <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_Moor" title="Ilkley Moor">Ilkley Moor</a>, consist of a large outcrop, which allegedly imitates a cow, and a boulder, which imitates a calf. The site is also visited for its <a href="/wiki/Rock_climbing" title="Rock climbing">rock climbing</a> routes.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> The Old Bridge just outside the town centre is the official start to the <a href="/wiki/Dales_Way" title="Dales Way">Dales Way</a>, an 84-mile (135&#160;km) walk through the dales to <a href="/wiki/Bowness-on-Windermere" title="Bowness-on-Windermere">Bowness-on-Windermere</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Lake_District" title="Lake District">Lake District</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Darwin Gardens, to the south of the town, is a <a href="/wiki/Millennium_Green" title="Millennium Green">Millennium Green</a> which commemorates the town's links with English naturalist <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a>. The Green features a maze, whose design was influenced by the <a href="/wiki/Swastika_Stone" title="Swastika Stone">Swastika Stone</a> carving, and includes monuments with an <a href="/wiki/Evolutionary" class="mw-redirect" title="Evolutionary">evolutionary</a> theme.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ilkley is home to the largest and oldest literary festival in the <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">North of England</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_Literature_Festival" title="Ilkley Literature Festival">Ilkley Literature Festival</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> The 1984 British comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/A_Private_Function" title="A Private Function">A Private Function</a></i>, written by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Bennett" title="Alan Bennett">Alan Bennett</a>, was filmed in Ilkley and Ben Rhydding.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> The town was also one of the locations used for the 2003 British comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Calendar_Girls" title="Calendar Girls">Calendar Girls</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> programme <a href="/wiki/Psychoville" title="Psychoville">Psychoville</a> is set, in part, in Ilkley. The <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ilkleygazette.co.uk/">Ilkley Gazette</a></i> is the town's weekly newspaper.
</p><p>A complementary medicine festival has been held in Ilkley since 1988 and is currently held twice a year, attracting 2,000 visitors.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 1969 Ilkley has been <a href="/wiki/Town_twinning" class="mw-redirect" title="Town twinning">twinned</a> with <a href="/wiki/Coutances" title="Coutances">Coutances</a> in France.<sup id="cite_ref-Archant_twinning_3_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Archant_twinning_3-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Schools">Schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A free school was first established in Ilkley by a Mr Marshall who in 1608 bequeathed £100 for its endowment.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> Current schools include four <a href="/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">primary schools</a>, All Saints' C of E Primary School,<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
Ashlands Primary School,<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
Ben Rhydding Primary School,<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup>
Sacred Heart Catholic Primary School, Ben Rhydding;<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup>
three <a href="/wiki/Preparatory_school_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Preparatory school (UK)">preparatory schools</a>,
Moorfield School,<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>
Westville House School, Middleton,<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> and Ghyll Royd School and Pre-School.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> Augustus Wooldbridge Godby founded Ghyll Royd School in 1889. He was Headmaster until his death on 5 November 1922.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup> Formerly an all boys School, Ghyll Royd has been a co-educational preparatory school since 1999, accepting children from two years old until they leave at eleven in Year 6. Ilkley also has one <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a>,
<a href="/wiki/Ilkley_Grammar_School" title="Ilkley Grammar School">Ilkley Grammar School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_education">Further education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Further education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1975, David Gayle founded the Yorkshire Ballet Seminars which brought distinguished names in dance, like <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Markova" title="Alicia Markova">Alicia Markova</a>, to Ilkley College of Education.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup> In 1978, Ilkley College of Education merged with Bingley College of Education to become Ilkley College. In 1982, Ilkley College merged with <a href="/wiki/Bradford_College_(England)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradford College (England)">Bradford College</a> to become Bradford and Ilkley Community College.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup> In 1999, the Ilkley campus of Bradford and Ilkley Community College closed despite opposition. The campus had occupied a 15.64 acres (6.33&#160;ha) site at Wells House, which was sold for housing after its closure.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AllSaintsParishChurch.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/AllSaintsParishChurch.jpg/220px-AllSaintsParishChurch.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/AllSaintsParishChurch.jpg/330px-AllSaintsParishChurch.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/AllSaintsParishChurch.jpg/440px-AllSaintsParishChurch.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AllSaintsParishChurch.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Parish_Church,_Ilkley" title="All Saints&#39; Parish Church, Ilkley">All Saints' Parish Church</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>According to Census 2001, 75.64% of Ilkley residents are Christian. The second largest group are people with no religion who account for 15.53% of residents. 7.48% did not state their religion and 1.34% fall into a variety of other religious groups.<sup id="cite_ref-2001census_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2001census-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_worship">Places of worship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ilkley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Places of worship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ilkley's medieval parish church <a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Parish_Church,_Ilkley" title="All Saints&#39; Parish Church, Ilkley">All Saints'</a>, largely reconstructed in the Victorian era, houses artefacts of the site's ancient Christian heritage including three Saxon crosses<sup id="cite_ref-AllSaintsChurch_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllSaintsChurch-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and today operates within the <a href="/wiki/Anglican_Communion" title="Anglican Communion">Anglican Communion</a>. An expanding congregation in the 19th&#160;century triggered plans in 1873 for a second church which became St Margaret's Church, designed by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Norman_Shaw" title="Richard Norman Shaw">Richard Norman Shaw</a> and completed in 1879.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup> Worship at the church is conducted in the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Catholicism" title="Anglo-Catholicism">Anglo-Catholic</a> tradition<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup> and in 2005 was led by <a href="/wiki/David_Hope,_Baron_Hope_of_Thornes" title="David Hope, Baron Hope of Thornes">David Hope, Baron Hope of Thornes</a>. When the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_York" title="Archbishop of York">Archbishop of York</a> resigned he became vicar at Ilkley until his retirement in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other places of worship in Ilkley include Ben Rhydding Methodist Church (<a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodist</a>), Christchurch (<a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodist</a>/<a href="/wiki/United_Reformed_Church" title="United Reformed Church">United Reformed</a>), Ilkley Baptist Church (<a href="/wiki/Baptist" class="mw-redirect" title="Baptist">Baptist</a>), Kingdom Hall, Ilkley (<a href="/wiki/Jehovah%27s_Witnesses" title="Jehovah&#39;s Witnesses">Jehovah's Witnesses</a>), St John's Parish Church, St Margaret's church, Ben Rhydding (<a href="/wiki/Anglicanism" title="Anglicanism">Anglican</a>), The Church of the Sacred Heart (<a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic</a>) and The Ilkley Society of Friends (<a href="/wiki/Religious_Society_of_Friends" class="mw-redirect" title="Religious Society of Friends">Quaker</a>). <a href="/wiki/Churches_Together_in_England" title="Churches Together in England">Churches Together in Ilkley</a> exists to encourage co-operation amongst member churches.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">&#91;85&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
