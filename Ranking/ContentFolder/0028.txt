	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">BCS Lovelace Medal</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p>The <b>Lovelace Medal</b> was established by the <a href="/wiki/British_Computer_Society" title="British Computer Society">British Computer Society</a> in 1998, and is presented to individuals who have made outstanding contributions to the understanding or advancement of Computing.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is the top award in Computing in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The award is named after <a href="/wiki/Ada_Lovelace" title="Ada Lovelace">Ada Lovelace</a>, who was the daughter of Lord Byron and an English Mathematician, scientist, and writer. She worked with  computer pioneer <a href="/wiki/Charles_Babbage" title="Charles Babbage">Charles Babbage</a> on the proposed mechanical general-purpose computer - The Analytical Engine,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> in 1842 and is often described as the world's first female <a href="/wiki/Computer_programmer" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer programmer">computer programmer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The computer language <a href="/wiki/Ada_(programming_language)" title="Ada (programming language)">Ada</a>, created on behalf of the United States Department of Defense, was named after Lovelace. 
</p><p>The medal is intended to be presented to individuals,  without regard to their countries of domicile, who have made a contribution which is of major significance in the advancement of Computing.
It is generally anticipated that there will be one medalist each year, but the regulation does not preclude either several medalists or no medalist.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medal_Recipients">Medal Recipients</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BCS_Lovelace_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Medal Recipients">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Awardees include:<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>2018 <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Plotkin" title="Gordon Plotkin">Gordon Plotkin</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> - for contributions to semantic framework for programming languages.</li>
<li>2017 <a href="/wiki/Georg_Gottlob" title="Georg Gottlob">Georg Gottlob</a> - for contributions to the logical and theoretical foundations of databases</li>
<li>2016 <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Blake_(scientist)" title="Andrew Blake (scientist)">Andrew Blake</a> - for contributions to the understanding and advancement of Computing as a discipline</li>
<li>2015 <a href="/wiki/Ross_J._Anderson" title="Ross J. Anderson">Ross Anderson</a> - for contributions to building security engineering into a discipline.</li>
<li>2014 <a href="/wiki/Steve_Furber" title="Steve Furber">Steve Furber</a> - for designing the ARM microprocessor architecture and contributions to computer systems</li>
<li>2013 <a href="/wiki/Samson_Abramsky" title="Samson Abramsky">Samson Abramsky</a> - for contributions to domain theory, game semantics and categorical quantum mechanics</li>
<li>2012 <a href="/wiki/Grady_Booch" title="Grady Booch">Grady Booch</a> - for contributions to software architecture, software engineering and collaborative environments</li>
<li>2011 <a href="/wiki/Hermann_Hauser" title="Hermann Hauser">Hermann Hauser</a> - for entrepreneurship and for co-developing the BBC Micro Computer.</li>
<li>2010 <a href="/wiki/John_C._Reynolds" title="John C. Reynolds">John C. Reynolds</a> -  for contributions to logical foundations of programs and programming languages</li>
<li>2009 <a href="/wiki/Yorick_Wilks" title="Yorick Wilks">Yorick Wilks</a> - for contributions to meaning-based understanding of natural language</li>
<li>2008 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tony_Storey&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tony Storey (page does not exist)">Tony Storey</a> - for contributions to Autonomic Computing</li>
<li>2007 <a href="/wiki/Karen_Sp%C3%A4rck_Jones" title="Karen Spärck Jones">Karen Spärck Jones</a> - for contributions to natural language processing</li>
<li>2006 <a href="/wiki/Tim_Berners-Lee" title="Tim Berners-Lee">Sir Tim Berners-Lee</a> - for inventing the World Wide Web</li>
<li>2005 <a href="/wiki/Nick_McKeown" title="Nick McKeown">Nick McKeown</a> - for contributions to router hardware design</li>
<li>2004 <a href="/wiki/John_Warnock" title="John Warnock">John Warnock</a> of <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Adobe Systems">Adobe Systems</a> - for contributions in document processing</li>
<li>2002 <a href="/wiki/Ian_Foster" title="Ian Foster">Ian Foster</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carl_Kesselman" title="Carl Kesselman">Carl Kesselman</a>, for contributions to <a href="/wiki/Grid_computing" title="Grid computing">grid computing</a></li>
<li>2001 <a href="/wiki/Douglas_C._Engelbart" class="mw-redirect" title="Douglas C. Engelbart">Douglas C. Engelbart</a> - for inventing the computer mouse</li>
<li>2000 <a href="/wiki/Linus_Torvalds" title="Linus Torvalds">Linus Torvalds</a> - for creating the <a href="/wiki/Linux_kernel" title="Linux kernel">Linux kernel</a> operating system</li>
<li>1998 <a href="/wiki/Michael_A._Jackson" title="Michael A. Jackson">Michael A. Jackson</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chris_Burton_(information_systems)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chris Burton (information systems) (page does not exist)">Chris Burton</a> - for program design and structured programming</li></ul>
