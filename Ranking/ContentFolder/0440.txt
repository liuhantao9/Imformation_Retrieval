<p><b>Huddersfield New College</b> is a former <a href="/wiki/Grammar_school" title="Grammar school">grammar school</a> and current <a href="/wiki/Sixth_form_college" title="Sixth form college">sixth form college</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Salendine_Nook" title="Salendine Nook">Salendine Nook</a> on the outskirts of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, in the county of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. The current Principal is Angela Williams.
On 17 May 2016 the college was assessed as 'Outstanding' in all 6 inspection domains following an <a href="/wiki/OFSTED" class="mw-redirect" title="OFSTED">OFSTED</a> review.  They are the first <a href="/wiki/Sixth_Form_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Sixth Form College">Sixth Form College</a> to receive such an accolade under the new (September 2016) inspection framework.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Admissions">Admissions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_New_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Admissions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It should not be confused with Huddersfield Technical College, which became <a href="/wiki/Kirklees_College" title="Kirklees College">Kirklees College</a> in 2008. Like most sixth form colleges, good performance at GCSE is required to attend. It is situated to the west of the town, on <i> New Hey Road</i> (<a href="/wiki/A640_road" title="A640 road">A640</a>) less than a mile from junction 23 of the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_New_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Grammar_school">Grammar school</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_New_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Grammar school">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield_New_College_2005.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Huddersfield_New_College_2005.JPG/220px-Huddersfield_New_College_2005.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Huddersfield_New_College_2005.JPG/330px-Huddersfield_New_College_2005.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Huddersfield_New_College_2005.JPG/440px-Huddersfield_New_College_2005.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2160" data-file-height="1440" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield_New_College_2005.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Huddersfield New College in 2005, as viewed from New Hey Road. Both the original brick construction, and the more modern partial replacement can be seen</div></div></div>
<p>The college was founded in 1958 when the existing Huddersfield College (founded in 1839) was merged with Hillhouse Technical School to form a new boys' grammar school at a new campus at Salendine Nook with 950 boys. <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ernest_Atkins" title="Henry Ernest Atkins">Henry Ernest Atkins</a>, the chess master, had been Principal from 1909-36. In 1959, the girls-only Longley Technical High School moved to the campus, with a new school called Huddersfield High School also on New Hey Road with 700 girls run by Huddersfield Education Committee. Princess Margaret opened the girls' school on the campus on 14 November 1958.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The whole site, including Salendine Nook High School, had cost £1 million. Sir <a href="/wiki/Edward_Boyle,_Baron_Boyle_of_Handsworth" title="Edward Boyle, Baron Boyle of Handsworth">Edward Boyle</a> opened Huddersfield New College on 26 March 1958.
</p><p>The last admission of 11-year-olds was in 1972, and the college then began a gradual transition from a boys only grammar school to a co-educational sixth form college. During the dissolution of the grammar schools under Harold Wilson's watch, (an old boy of the nearby Royds Hall Grammar School), he infamously said that <i>grammar schools would be dismantled over his dead body</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However the sixth form college has retained much of the academic-minded ethos of his former school.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sixth_form_college">Sixth form college</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_New_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sixth form college">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It became a sixth form college when the two grammar schools, Huddersfield New College and Huddersfield High School, gradually merged from 1973. In 1974 it was administered by Kirklees Metropolitan Council until 1993 when funded by the <a href="/wiki/Further_Education_Funding_Council_for_England" title="Further Education Funding Council for England">FEFC</a>. In 2001 it was administered by West Yorkshire LSC, whose executive director was Margaret Coleman, a former Principal of the College.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Buildings">Buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_New_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield_New_College_Entrance.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Huddersfield_New_College_Entrance.jpg/220px-Huddersfield_New_College_Entrance.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Huddersfield_New_College_Entrance.jpg/330px-Huddersfield_New_College_Entrance.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Huddersfield_New_College_Entrance.jpg/440px-Huddersfield_New_College_Entrance.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2816" data-file-height="2112" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield_New_College_Entrance.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Huddersfield New College, showing the entrance and English rooms</div></div></div>
<p>More recently, the eastern half of the original 1958 built building has been demolished, and replaced with a modern construction grafted onto the remaining half of the original building.
</p><p>In January 2007 building works commenced to expand the current school to increase capacity. New buildings will house additional classrooms for Geography, Art, Psychology, Textiles, Modern Languages, IT, Media Studies and a new student dining area (known as the IT Café by students). Additional expansion to 'The Boiler House' -  the current performing arts area is also commencing, yielding increased classrooms a recording studio and a new theatre. There has also been expansion to the sports centre, which now houses: Sports Studies, Travel and Tourism, Sociology, Health and Social Care, Children's Learning, Care and Development. Also a large gym with state-of-the-art equipment, and a large sports hall.
</p><p>In September 2012 the college completed the construction of a £100,000 3G AstroTurf pitch which is also used by the neighbouring Salendine Nook High School.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_performance">Academic performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_New_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Academic performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In October 2011 the college was formally inspected by <a href="/wiki/Ofsted" title="Ofsted">Ofsted</a>, who praised the college and rated it as "a good college with outstanding features". Ofsted said that the college was showing a lot of improvement year-on-year and that the quality of teaching across all areas was good. All courses have high success rates, and students enjoy their time at the college.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 17 May 2016 the college was assessed as 'Outstanding' in all 6 inspection domains following an <a href="/wiki/OFSTED" class="mw-redirect" title="OFSTED">OFSTED</a> review.  They are the first <a href="/wiki/Sixth_Form_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Sixth Form College">Sixth Form College</a> to receive such an accolade under the new (September 2016) inspection framework.
</p><p><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
