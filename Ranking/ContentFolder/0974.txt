<p><b>Peter G. Gyarmati</b> (born July 14, 1941) is a <a href="/wiki/Software_engineer" title="Software engineer">software engineer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer_scientist" title="Computer scientist">computer scientist</a>, best known for the development of <a href="/w/index.php?title=OS/360%2BHASP&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="OS/360+HASP (page does not exist)">OS/360+HASP</a> for the <a href="/wiki/System/360" class="mw-redirect" title="System/360">System/360</a>, then later the <a href="/wiki/OS/VS" title="OS/VS">OS/VS</a> for the <a href="/wiki/System/370" class="mw-redirect" title="System/370">System/370</a>, especially the resource allocation <a href="/wiki/System" title="System">system</a>. He introduced here the adaptive allocation strategy based on his earlier engineering works.
</p><p>Born in Budapest, Hungary there he received <a href="/wiki/BSc" class="mw-redirect" title="BSc">BSc</a> (Eng) from the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Budapesti_M%C5%B1szaki_Egyetem&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Budapesti Műszaki Egyetem (page does not exist)">Budapesti Műszaki Egyetem</a>, Hungary and <a href="/wiki/MSc" class="mw-redirect" title="MSc">MSc</a> from <a href="/wiki/Manchester_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester University">Manchester University</a>, England in 1972, and he received a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">PhD</a> in Applied <a href="/wiki/Computer_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer Science">Computer Science</a> from <a href="/wiki/ELTE" class="mw-redirect" title="ELTE">ELTE</a> (Eötvös Lóránd Tudomány Egyetem) in 1981.
</p><p>After their earlier work with <a href="/wiki/Ferranti" title="Ferranti">Ferranti</a>, then the successor <a href="/wiki/International_Computers_Limited" title="International Computers Limited">ICL</a>, in Manchester University he joined for research to <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> from 1972 until 1981, working in <a href="/wiki/Poughkeepsie_(city),_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Poughkeepsie (city), New York">Poughkeepsie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yorktown,_New_York" title="Yorktown, New York">Yorktown, New York</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Delft_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Delft University">Delft University</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.
</p><p>In his PhD work – <i>Adaptive Controls in <a href="/wiki/Operating_Systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Operating Systems">Operating Systems</a></i> — he proposed the so-called ADIOS solution, extension to the System/370 family, with the <a href="/wiki/OS/VS2" title="OS/VS2">OS/VS2</a> software.
</p><p>Actively studied the ALOHA-type networks suggested solution to the collision problem and gave a prove for the radio communication <a href="/wiki/Channel_(communications)" class="mw-redirect" title="Channel (communications)">channel</a> capacity. Now the everywhere used <a href="/wiki/CSMA/CD" class="mw-redirect" title="CSMA/CD">CSMA/CD</a> protocol –an essential for the <a href="/wiki/Ethernet" title="Ethernet">Ethernet</a>—based on his work.
</p><p>Returning to Hungary he turned to the developing world of the <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">PC</a> and microcomputing, where he introduced a forerunner of the <a href="/wiki/Portable_computer" title="Portable computer">portable computer</a>, a portable data collector machine, called MOBI-X, then MOBI-2000. He also designed, created and patented the portable <a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating system</a>, which is now the core of many such machines.
</p><p>Later he worked with networking reliability, security, in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stuttgart" title="Stuttgart">Stuttgart</a> and also in <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a> for BSB, TCC and worked in <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto" class="mw-redirect" title="Palo Alto">Palo Alto</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S.</a> as a guest <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a>, and as emeritus returned to <a href="/wiki/Szentendre" title="Szentendre">Szentendre</a>, where he lives now. He is member of the PhD body of the HAS (Hungarian Academy of Sciences) and of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bolyai_Society_of_Mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bolyai Society of Mathematics (page does not exist)">Bolyai Society of Mathematics</a>.
</p>
