		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Hadersfild</b> (Serbian phonetic spelling of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Serbian_language" title="Serbian language">Serbian</a> <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">film</a> from 2007, directed by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ivan_%C5%BDivkovi%C4%87_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ivan Živković (director) (page does not exist)">Ivan Živković</a>, and the script was written by <a href="/wiki/Uglje%C5%A1a_%C5%A0ajtinac" title="Uglješa Šajtinac">Uglješa Šajtinac</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dejan_Nikolaj_Kralja%C4%8Di%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dejan Nikolaj Kraljačić (page does not exist)">Dejan Nikolaj Kraljačić</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">Plot summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hadersfild&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The story is set in a small town in <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>. Rasha (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Goran_%C5%A0u%C5%A1ljik&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Goran Šušljik (page does not exist)">Goran Šušljik</a>) is thirty-two, lives with his alcoholic father (<a href="/wiki/Josif_Tati%C4%87" title="Josif Tatić">Josif Tatić</a>) and tries, whilst failing, to make ends meet by giving literature lessons to teenage girls and hosting a program on the local radio, presenting new books and interviewing authors. 
</p><p>Ivan (<a href="/wiki/Neboj%C5%A1a_Glogovac" title="Nebojša Glogovac">Nebojša Glogovac</a>), a promising judoist in his teenage years, has since had a history of neurosis, psychotic episodes, hospitalization, being heavily medicated, involvement in various occult groups and practices, prior to having been baptized in the Orthodox Church. 
</p><p>Milla (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Suzana_Luki%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Suzana Lukić (page does not exist)">Suzana Lukić</a>) is very attractive, energetic, resourceful and very straightforward: it immediately becomes clear that apart from having a student/teacher relationship, she and Rasha are lovers. 
</p><p>Dule (<a href="/wiki/Vojin_%C4%86etkovi%C4%87" title="Vojin Ćetković">Vojin Ćetković</a>) is a wannabe yuppie: he works for the local representative of major confectionery brands and is doing his best to act the part of a successful businessman who is well aware of global business trends as he sees them. 
</p><p>The monotony of their lives is interrupted by the arrival of Igor (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Damjan_Kecojevi%C4%87&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Damjan Kecojević (page does not exist)">Damjan Kecojević</a>), who has lived in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> since the beginning of the nineties, and this is the first time he's come to visit since. 
</p><p>They all get together in the evening, and what begins as a cheerful high school reunion party of close friends with a lot of catching up to do, turns into an emotional roller coaster of reminiscing, dark humor, bitterness, uncontrollable laughter, anger and grim soul searching.
</p>

<p>The film has proven to be a hit within Serbia and has played at the <a href="/wiki/International_Thessaloniki_Film_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="International Thessaloniki Film Festival">International Thessaloniki Film Festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, receiving rave reviews from critics.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
