<p><b>Silsden</b> is a town and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England, on the <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">River Aire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leeds_and_Liverpool_Canal" title="Leeds and Liverpool Canal">Leeds and Liverpool Canal</a> between <a href="/wiki/Keighley" title="Keighley">Keighley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skipton" title="Skipton">Skipton</a>, which had a population of 8,268 at the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silsden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Silsden was mentioned in the 1086 <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i> as "Siglesdene", and as the most important village in <a href="/wiki/Craven" title="Craven">Craven</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Generally an agricultural area, industry came with the canal and the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>. The town hosted a number of mills none of which now operate in their original form. There is still industry in the town, some in old mill buildings and some in a new industrial estate between the town and the river. The town retains some manufacturing.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_James%27_Church_-_Kirkgate_-_geograph.org.uk_-_547453.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/St_James%27_Church_-_Kirkgate_-_geograph.org.uk_-_547453.jpg/220px-St_James%27_Church_-_Kirkgate_-_geograph.org.uk_-_547453.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="194" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/St_James%27_Church_-_Kirkgate_-_geograph.org.uk_-_547453.jpg/330px-St_James%27_Church_-_Kirkgate_-_geograph.org.uk_-_547453.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/St_James%27_Church_-_Kirkgate_-_geograph.org.uk_-_547453.jpg/440px-St_James%27_Church_-_Kirkgate_-_geograph.org.uk_-_547453.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="563" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_James%27_Church_-_Kirkgate_-_geograph.org.uk_-_547453.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St. James' Church, Kirkgate</div></div></div>
<p>In 1911 there was a riot in Silsden when the <a href="/wiki/Police_station" title="Police station">police station</a> was attacked.<sup id="cite_ref-DMirror1911_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DMirror1911-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A very unpopular policeman had been too enthusiastic in his duties. Questions were raised in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="House of Commons of the United Kingdom">House of Commons</a> and it was reported in the national press. The <a href="/wiki/Policeman" class="mw-redirect" title="Policeman">policeman</a> was removed from the town and no more trouble occurred.
</p><p>During the 1940s a hostel was built off Howden Road, (now a housing estate) The hostel housed <a href="/wiki/Refugee" title="Refugee">refugees</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prisoner_of_war" title="Prisoner of war">PoW</a> from various countries and various camps. A plaque to commemorate this is located at the bottom of Ings way, at the entrance to the housing estate
</p><p>In 1998 a <a href="/wiki/Silsden_Hoard" title="Silsden Hoard">hoard of 27 gold coins</a> dating back to the 1st century AD were found in the town and subsequently valued at £20,000 by experts appointed by the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Culture,_Media_and_Sport" class="mw-redirect" title="Department of Culture, Media and Sport">Department of Culture, Media and Sport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Guinness_Book_of_World_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Guinness Book of World Records">Guinness Book of World Records</a></i> reported that the biggest onion ever, at 14&#160;lb (6.4&#160;kg), was grown in Silsden in 2010 by Vincent Throup.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> However this has since been beaten.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 6 July 2014, Stage 2 of the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">Tour de France</a> from York to Sheffield, passed through the town.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silsden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The village is part of the <a href="/wiki/Craven_(Bradford_ward)" title="Craven (Bradford ward)">Craven</a> <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Wards of the United Kingdom">ward</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">Metropolitan borough</a> of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford" title="City of Bradford">City of Bradford</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_county" title="Metropolitan county">Metropolitan county</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silsden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Silsden_Fire_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_42864.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Silsden_Fire_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_42864.jpg/220px-Silsden_Fire_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_42864.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Silsden_Fire_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_42864.jpg/330px-Silsden_Fire_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_42864.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Silsden_Fire_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_42864.jpg/440px-Silsden_Fire_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_42864.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Silsden_Fire_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_42864.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Silsden Fire Station</div></div></div>
<p>Silsden has been administered by the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford" title="City of Bradford">City of Bradford Metropolitan District Council</a> since 1974. It also has its own town council.
</p><p>Silsden has four <a href="/wiki/Public_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Public house">public houses</a>: the Robin Hood, the Red Lion, the Punch Bowl, and the King's Arms. There are two members' clubs: Sunnybank Social Club and Twisters Social Club.
</p><p>Whilst Silsden does not have its own railway station, the <a href="/wiki/Steeton_and_Silsden_railway_station" title="Steeton and Silsden railway station">Steeton and Silsden railway station</a> is 1 mile (1.6&#160;km) south in the village of <a href="/wiki/Steeton_(village)" class="mw-redirect" title="Steeton (village)">Steeton</a>. The station provides a link for commuters to the cities of <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sunnybank_Social_Club_-_Hillcrest_Avenue_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1514143.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Sunnybank_Social_Club_-_Hillcrest_Avenue_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1514143.jpg/220px-Sunnybank_Social_Club_-_Hillcrest_Avenue_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1514143.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Sunnybank_Social_Club_-_Hillcrest_Avenue_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1514143.jpg/330px-Sunnybank_Social_Club_-_Hillcrest_Avenue_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1514143.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Sunnybank_Social_Club_-_Hillcrest_Avenue_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1514143.jpg/440px-Sunnybank_Social_Club_-_Hillcrest_Avenue_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1514143.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sunnybank_Social_Club_-_Hillcrest_Avenue_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1514143.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sunnybank Social Club</div></div></div>
<p>Lying between <a href="/wiki/Keighley" title="Keighley">Keighley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ilkley" title="Ilkley">Ilkley</a>, Silsden is served by buses to both these towns. Silsden's public transport benefits from Silsden being part of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> rather than <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>, the border of which runs along one end of the town.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silsden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Lampkin family lived at Silsden during the Second World War. Lampkin brothers <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Lampkin" title="Arthur Lampkin">Arthur</a>, Alan and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Lampkin" title="Martin Lampkin">Martin</a> were, later, national <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_trials" title="Motorcycle trials">motorcycle trials</a> champions. Martin Lampkin won the 1975 <a href="/wiki/FIM_Trial_World_Championship" title="FIM Trial World Championship">FIM Trial World Championship</a> and his son, <a href="/wiki/Dougie_Lampkin" title="Dougie Lampkin">Douglas "Dougie" Lampkin</a>, is a twelve-time trials world champion.<sup id="cite_ref-Arthur_Lampkin_Sr._obituary_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arthur_Lampkin_Sr._obituary-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Wainman family, a second motorsport dynasty from Silsden, compete in <a href="/wiki/BriSCA_Formula_1_Stock_Cars" title="BriSCA Formula 1 Stock Cars">BriSCA Formula 1 Stock Cars</a>. Frankie Wainman was <a href="/wiki/BriSCA_Formula_1_Stock_Cars_World_Championship" title="BriSCA Formula 1 Stock Cars World Championship">World Champion</a> in 1979, while his son <a href="/wiki/Frankie_Wainman_Junior" class="mw-redirect" title="Frankie Wainman Junior">Frankie Wainman Junior</a> is one of the most successful drivers in the history of the sport.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>English rugby league player <a href="/wiki/Jack_Reed_(rugby_league)" title="Jack Reed (rugby league)">Jack Reed</a>, who played for the Brisbane Broncos, was born in Silsden.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Henry_Price_(tailor)" class="mw-redirect" title="Henry Price (tailor)">Henry Price</a> started his first <a href="/wiki/Fifty_Shilling_Tailors" title="Fifty Shilling Tailors">Fifty Shilling Tailors</a> shop in Silsden.<sup id="cite_ref-UDS_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UDS-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> With this fortune, the now Sir Henry Price, bought <a href="/wiki/Wakehurst_Place" title="Wakehurst Place">Wakehurst Place</a>, Ardingly, West Sussex, which is now owned by the <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Places_of_Historic_Interest_or_Natural_Beauty" title="National Trust for Places of Historic Interest or Natural Beauty">National Trust</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Margaret_Wintringham" title="Margaret Wintringham">Margaret Wintringham</a>, née Longbottom, was a British <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal Party</a> politician. She was the second woman take her seat in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="House of Commons of the United Kingdom">House of Commons</a> and lived in Silsden when her father was the head teacher at Bolton Road School.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silsden&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 27 April 1995, a one-off <a href="/wiki/Anthology" title="Anthology">anthology</a> <a href="/wiki/Supernatural" title="Supernatural">supernatural</a> drama titled <i>Chiller</i> aired, in which episode 6, titled "number 6", featured Silsden. Silsden was featured for almost the entire one-hour episode, using locations across the town.
</p><p>Bonaparte's Restaurant, on Kirkgate, was the subject of the first-ever episode of <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Ramsay" title="Gordon Ramsay">Gordon Ramsay</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Ramsay%27s_Kitchen_Nightmares" title="Ramsay&#39;s Kitchen Nightmares">Kitchen Nightmares</a></i> in 2004. After the show aired, Bonaparte's owner Sue Ray threatened to take legal action against Ramsay, <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> and the programme makers, <a href="/wiki/Optomen" title="Optomen">Optomen</a>, after claiming that the show put her £400,000 in debt. Christine Hall, producer of <i>Kitchen Nightmares</i>, refused to accept the blame, stating Ray only had herself to blame.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The programme revisited the restaurant in the second series, but Ray would only talk to Ramsay off-camera.
</p><p>In June 2006, Ramsay won a <a href="/wiki/High_Court_of_Justice" title="High Court of Justice">High Court</a> case against the <i><a href="/wiki/London_Evening_Standard" class="mw-redirect" title="London Evening Standard">London Evening Standard</a></i>, which had alleged, after reports from Ray, that scenes and the general condition of Bonaparte's had been faked. Ramsay was awarded £75,000 plus costs. Ramsay said at the time: "I won't let people write anything they want to about me. We have never done anything in a cynical, fake way."<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2007, the butchers ,barbers and shoe shop located on Bradley Road were used in an episode of ITV's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal" title="The Royal">The Royal</a></i>.
</p>
