<p><b>Lars Løkke Rasmussen</b> <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Dannebrog" title="Order of the Dannebrog">RSKmd</a></span> (<small>Danish:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Danish" title="Help:IPA/Danish">[laːs ˈløkə ˈʁasmusn̩]</a></span>; born 15 May 1964) is a Danish politician who served as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Denmark" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Prime Ministers of Denmark">25th</a> <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Denmark" title="Prime Minister of Denmark">Prime Minister of Denmark</a> from 2009 to 2011 and again 2015 to 2019. He was the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Liberalism" title="Liberalism">liberal</a> <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a> party from 2009 to 2019.
</p><p>Rasmussen has been a member of the <a href="/wiki/Folketing" title="Folketing">Folketing</a> since 21 September 1994. He also served as <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Denmark" title="Counties of Denmark">County Mayor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Frederiksborg_County" title="Frederiksborg County">Frederiksborg County</a> from 1998 to 2001. Subsequently, he was the <a href="/wiki/Interior_and_Health_Minister_(Denmark)" class="mw-redirect" title="Interior and Health Minister (Denmark)">Interior and Health Minister</a> from 27 November 2001 to 23 November 2007 as part of <a href="/wiki/Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen" title="Anders Fogh Rasmussen">Anders Fogh Rasmussen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabinet of Anders Fogh Rasmussen I">first</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabinet of Anders Fogh Rasmussen II">second cabinets</a>, and then <a href="/wiki/Finance_Minister_of_Denmark" class="mw-redirect" title="Finance Minister of Denmark">Minister of Finance</a> from 23 November 2007 to April 2009 as part of Anders Fogh Rasmussen's <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabinet of Anders Fogh Rasmussen III">third cabinet</a>. On 5 April 2009, he succeeded Anders Fogh Rasmussen as Prime Minister following the latter's appointment as <a href="/wiki/Secretary_General_of_NATO" title="Secretary General of NATO">Secretary General of NATO</a>.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/2011_Danish_general_election" title="2011 Danish general election">2011 general election</a>, the government lost its parliamentary majority and Rasmussen tendered the government's resignation to <a href="/wiki/Margrethe_II_of_Denmark" title="Margrethe II of Denmark">Queen Margrethe II</a>. He was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Helle_Thorning-Schmidt" title="Helle Thorning-Schmidt">Helle Thorning-Schmidt</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democrats_(Denmark)" title="Social Democrats (Denmark)">Social Democrats</a> on 3 October 2011. In the <a href="/wiki/2015_Danish_general_election" title="2015 Danish general election">2015 general election</a>, the right-wing parties regained a majority in the Folketing. Rasmussen again became Prime Minister and formed his <a href="/wiki/Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen_II_Cabinet" title="Lars Løkke Rasmussen II Cabinet">second cabinet</a> in the same month. This cabinet was made up exclusively of Venstre members, but in November 2016 he was pressured to also include members of <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Alliance_(Denmark)" title="Liberal Alliance (Denmark)">Liberal Alliance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conservative_People%27s_Party_(Denmark)" title="Conservative People&#39;s Party (Denmark)">Conservative People's Party</a>, forming his third cabinet.
</p><p>On 6 June 2019, he resigned from his position as prime minister after <a href="/wiki/2019_Danish_general_election" title="2019 Danish general election">a general election</a>, in which his government was defeated. However, he continued to lead a caretaker government until a new government was formed and sworn in. This was completed on 27 June 2019 and Rasmussen was succeeded as Prime Minister by <a href="/wiki/Mette_Frederiksen" title="Mette Frederiksen">Mette Frederiksen</a>. On 31 August 2019, he resigned as chairman of Venstre.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lars Løkke Rasmussen was born in <a href="/wiki/Vejle" title="Vejle">Vejle</a> to Jeppe Rasmussen and Lise Løkke Rasmussen (née Løkke). His last name is Rasmussen, while Løkke, his mother's <a href="/wiki/Maiden_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Maiden name">maiden name</a>, is his <a href="/wiki/Middle_name#Scandinavia" title="Middle name">middle name</a>. Unless his full name is used, he is correctly referred to only by the name of Rasmussen, but his middle name is used frequently by the media and in informal usage to distinguish him from <a href="/wiki/Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen" title="Anders Fogh Rasmussen">Anders Fogh Rasmussen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poul_Nyrup_Rasmussen" title="Poul Nyrup Rasmussen">Poul Nyrup Rasmussen</a>, his two immediate predecessors as Prime Minister (to whom he is not related).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He graduated from <a href="/wiki/Gymnasium_(Denmark)" title="Gymnasium (Denmark)">high school</a> in 1983, and was the president of the <a href="/wiki/Venstres_Ungdom" title="Venstres Ungdom">youth branch of Venstre</a> from 1986 to 1989. He graduated with a law degree (<a href="/wiki/Candidatus_juris" class="mw-redirect" title="Candidatus juris">cand. jur</a>) from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Copenhagen" title="University of Copenhagen">University of Copenhagen</a> in 1992. From 1990 to 1995 he worked as a self-employed consultant.
</p><p>Lars Løkke Rasmussen is married to <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B3lrun_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen" title="Sólrun Løkke Rasmussen">Sólrun Løkke Rasmussen</a> (née Sólrun Jákupsdóttir). Together they have three children.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Venstres_Ungdom_chairmanship_and_Afghanistan_mission">Venstres Ungdom chairmanship and Afghanistan mission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Venstres Ungdom chairmanship and Afghanistan mission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lars Løkke Rasmussen served as chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Venstres_Ungdom" title="Venstres Ungdom">youth branch</a> of <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a> from 1986 to 1989. One of his initiatives was to establish an alternative to <i>Operation Dagsværk</i> — an annual one day fundraising campaign by high school students collecting money for third world countries — since <i>Operation Dagsværk</i> at the time was spearheaded by members of the Danish Communist Youth.<sup id="cite_ref-Politiken_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Politiken-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Rasmussen's campaign was supported by the party youth branch, and raised 600,000 <a href="/wiki/Danish_krone" title="Danish krone">DKK</a> which were spent on school equipment<sup id="cite_ref-Politiken_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Politiken-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet</a> <a href="/wiki/Soviet_occupation_of_Afghanistan" class="mw-redirect" title="Soviet occupation of Afghanistan">occupied</a> Afghanistan. Lars Løkke Rasmussen led a Danish delegation to Afghanistan delivering the collected funds, and a photograph taken by photographer <a href="/w/index.php?title=J%C3%B8rn_Stjerneklar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jørn Stjerneklar (page does not exist)">Jørn Stjerneklar</a> shows him and two other delegation members disguised as Afghans. Another photo shows him holding an <a href="/wiki/AK-47" title="AK-47">AK-47</a>, while standing together with three <a href="/wiki/Mujahideen" title="Mujahideen">Mujahideen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The photos have generated a lot of media attention in Denmark, after the Danish participation in the <a href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%93present)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001–present)">war in Afghanistan</a> and especially as Rasmussen moved up the rankings at Venstre.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="County_Mayor_and_deputy_chairman_of_Venstre">County Mayor and deputy chairman of Venstre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: County Mayor and deputy chairman of Venstre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rasmussen was elected deputy chairman of <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a> in 1998, at the same time as <a href="/wiki/Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen" title="Anders Fogh Rasmussen">Anders Fogh Rasmussen</a> assumed the position as party leader after <a href="/wiki/Uffe_Ellemann-Jensen" title="Uffe Ellemann-Jensen">Uffe Ellemann-Jensen</a>. In 1998, he was elected as county mayor of <a href="/wiki/Frederiksborg_County" title="Frederiksborg County">Frederiksborg County</a>, a position he occupied until 2001, when he joined the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabinet of Anders Fogh Rasmussen I">first Fogh Rasmussen cabinet</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minister_of_the_Interior_and_Health">Minister of the Interior and Health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Minister of the Interior and Health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lars Løkke Rasmussen served as Interior and Health Minister between 2001 and 2007 until he was appointed Minister of Finance in 2007. He was responsible for negotiating a 2002 agreement between <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_People%27s_Party_(Denmark)" title="Conservative People&#39;s Party (Denmark)">Conservatives</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democrats_(Denmark)" title="Social Democrats (Denmark)">Social Democrats</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Danish_People%27s_Party" title="Danish People&#39;s Party">Danish People's Party</a> giving patients in public hospitals the right to select a <a href="/wiki/Private_hospital" title="Private hospital">private hospital</a>, provided that the public hospital had been unable to treat the patient within two months.  In 2007, this time limit was lowered to one month. Since 2002, the government has awarded extra funds earmarked at reducing the waiting list at National Health Service hospitals, a grant sometimes referred to by the media as <i>Løkkeposen</i><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> (A pun on 'lykkepose' the Danish word for a goodie bag). He also represented the government during negotiations regarding a reform of the system by which richer municipalities transfer part of their tax incomes to poorer municipalities.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Municipal_reform_of_2007">Municipal reform of 2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Municipal reform of 2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>As <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_the_Interior_and_Health_(Denmark)" class="mw-redirect" title="Minister of the Interior and Health (Denmark)">Minister of the Interior and Health</a>, Lars Løkke Rasmussen spearheaded the <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Denmark#Municipal_Reform_2007" title="Municipalities of Denmark">municipal reform</a> that reduced Denmark's 271 municipalities to 98, and abolished the 14 <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Denmark" title="Counties of Denmark">counties</a> and replaced them with five <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_Denmark" title="Regions of Denmark">regions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Minister_of_Finance">Minister of Finance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Minister of Finance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After then Prime Minister Anders Fogh Rasmussen won his second reelection in 2007 he created his third cabinet in which Lars Løkke Rasmussen was appointed Minister of Finance. This was seen as a clear indicator that Rasmussen was next in line to follow Fogh as leader of Venstre and Prime Minister, when Fogh would leave Danish politics.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
As Finance Minister Lars Løkke Rasmussen led the negotiations concerning funds to banks hit by the <a href="/wiki/Global_financial_crisis_of_2008%E2%80%932009" class="mw-redirect" title="Global financial crisis of 2008–2009">global financial crisis</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tax_reform_of_2009">Tax reform of 2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tax reform of 2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2009, Lars Løkke Rasmussen was the chief negotiator in the political agreement behind a major tax reform, implementing the government's ambition of reducing income tax and increasing taxes on pollution.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The reform was, according to Lars Løkke Rasmussen, the biggest reduction of the <a href="/wiki/Marginal_tax_rate" class="mw-redirect" title="Marginal tax rate">marginal tax rate</a> since the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/Income_tax" title="Income tax">income tax</a> in 1903.<sup id="cite_ref-jp_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jp-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The opposition accused it of being historically skewed in favouring those with high-income jobs and giving very little to those with low-income jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-jp_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jp-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prime_Minister_of_Denmark">Prime Minister of Denmark</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Prime Minister of Denmark">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen_foran_Amalienborg_7_april_2009_(edit).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen_foran_Amalienborg_7_april_2009_%28edit%29.JPG/220px-Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen_foran_Amalienborg_7_april_2009_%28edit%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="233" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen_foran_Amalienborg_7_april_2009_%28edit%29.JPG/330px-Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen_foran_Amalienborg_7_april_2009_%28edit%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen_foran_Amalienborg_7_april_2009_%28edit%29.JPG/440px-Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen_foran_Amalienborg_7_april_2009_%28edit%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="539" data-file-height="572" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen_foran_Amalienborg_7_april_2009_(edit).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rasmussen outside <a href="/wiki/Amalienborg_Palace" class="mw-redirect" title="Amalienborg Palace">Amalienborg Palace</a> immediately after his appointment as Prime Minister by Queen Margrethe.</div></div></div>
<p>On 4 April 2009, <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> decided that Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen" title="Anders Fogh Rasmussen">Anders Fogh Rasmussen</a> would replace <a href="/wiki/Jaap_de_Hoop_Scheffer" title="Jaap de Hoop Scheffer">Jaap de Hoop Scheffer</a> as <a href="/wiki/Secretary_General_of_NATO" title="Secretary General of NATO">Secretary General of NATO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On the same day, Anders Fogh Rasmussen declared that he would resign as Prime Minister on 5 April 2009. As deputy of the largest party in the government, Lars Løkke Rasmussen thus took over the post as Prime Minister of Denmark.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> An opinion poll released on the day of Lars Løkke Rasmussen's takeover revealed that Danes believed that he only beat <a href="/wiki/Helle_Thorning-Schmidt" title="Helle Thorning-Schmidt">Helle Thorning-Schmidt</a> as the person best suited for bringing Denmark through the <a href="/wiki/Global_financial_crisis_of_2008%E2%80%932009" class="mw-redirect" title="Global financial crisis of 2008–2009">financial crisis</a>, and that Thorning-Schmidt would have been better suited to combatting unemployment, reducing hospital waiting lists, securing the welfare society of the future, and representing Denmark internationally.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On 7 April 2009, Lars Løkke Rasmussen announced the new set of ministers in <a href="/wiki/Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen_I_Cabinet" title="Lars Løkke Rasmussen I Cabinet">his Cabinet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="COP15_-_December_2009">COP15 - December 2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: COP15 - December 2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lars Løkke Rasmussen has been sharply criticized from many sides for his handling of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Climate_Change_Conference_2009" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Climate Change Conference 2009">COP15</a> leadership.
</p><p>At the first meeting of the summit high level section, led by Lars Løkke Rasmussen, a number of countries protested the Danish handling of the negotiations. ”We cannot continue to talk about procedure. We must move forward. The World awaits us”, said Lars Løkke Rasmussen responding to criticism of the Danish led negotiations coming from several countries who regarded them as undemocratic.<sup id="cite_ref-politiken1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-politiken1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many developing countries viewed this statement as arrogant. Procedure is a major element in UN negotiations.<sup id="cite_ref-politiken2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-politiken2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> ”This is not about procedure. This is about content. We have stated that the results in Copenhagen must come in two texts. One cannot simply present a text pulled from the clouds”, replied the Chinese delegate in the auditorium.<sup id="cite_ref-politiken1_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-politiken1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Stanislaus Lumumba Di-Aping, chief negotiator for the Developing Nations' organisation <a href="/wiki/Group_of_77" title="Group of 77">G77</a>, cross examined what exactly Rasmussen meant when stating that the chairmen of the negotiating groups should be "people whom we trust".<sup id="cite_ref-politiken2_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-politiken2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Criticism of the Office of the Prime Minister was supported by China, India and Brazil. The last had been regarded as an ally by the Danish delegation.<sup id="cite_ref-politiken2_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-politiken2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The international press, too, has been severe in its criticism of the Prime Minister and the Prime Minister's Office. The BBC's climate correspondent stated: "According to all my sources, the Prime Minister's Office is on the verge of a melt-down. They have no modus operandi, or the diplomatic experience needed to plan one in advance. Ed Miliband, the UK <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Energy_and_Climate_Change" title="Secretary of State for Energy and Climate Change">Secretary of State for Energy and Climate Change</a>, was quoted for stating that "Denmark is doing a reasonable job".<sup id="cite_ref-politiken2_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-politiken2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Budget_cuts">Budget cuts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Budget cuts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dmitry_Medvedev_in_Denmark_28_April_2010-4.jpeg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Dmitry_Medvedev_in_Denmark_28_April_2010-4.jpeg/220px-Dmitry_Medvedev_in_Denmark_28_April_2010-4.jpeg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Dmitry_Medvedev_in_Denmark_28_April_2010-4.jpeg/330px-Dmitry_Medvedev_in_Denmark_28_April_2010-4.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Dmitry_Medvedev_in_Denmark_28_April_2010-4.jpeg/440px-Dmitry_Medvedev_in_Denmark_28_April_2010-4.jpeg 2x" data-file-width="650" data-file-height="434" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dmitry_Medvedev_in_Denmark_28_April_2010-4.jpeg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lars Løkke Rasmussen and Russian President <a href="/wiki/Dmitrij_Medvedev" class="mw-redirect" title="Dmitrij Medvedev">Dmitrij Medvedev</a> in the Prime Minister's office at <a href="/wiki/Christiansborg_Palace" title="Christiansborg Palace">Christiansborg</a> in Copenhagen, Denmark, 28 April 2010.</div></div></div>
<p>In May 2010 Rasmussen's government announced major spending cuts and measures designed to increase revenues, notably to unemployment insurance (cut from a maximum of four years to two), foreign aid (cut from 0.83% of GDP to 0.76%), cuts to child support payments, and miscellaneous tax reforms designed to increase revenues.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The cuts were designed to save the government 24 billion DKK.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_election">2011 election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2011 election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rasmussen led <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Danish_parliamentary_election" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Danish parliamentary election">September 2011 parliamentary election</a>. He sought to renew the mandate of the <a href="/wiki/Rightwing" class="mw-redirect" title="Rightwing">rightwing</a> coalition that had been in power since <a href="/wiki/2001_Danish_parliamentary_election" class="mw-redirect" title="2001 Danish parliamentary election">2001</a>. Although his party gained a seat, the opposition parties combined obtained more seats than the parties supporting the incumbent government. On 16 September 2011, Rasmussen tendered the government's resignation to Queen Margrethe. He remained in office as head of a <a href="/wiki/Caretaker_government" title="Caretaker government">caretaker government</a> until his successor, <a href="/wiki/Helle_Thorning-Schmidt" title="Helle Thorning-Schmidt">Helle Thorning-Schmidt</a>, was appointed on 3 October 2011.
</p>

<p>Rasmussen led <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2015_Danish_general_election" title="2015 Danish general election">June 2015 general election</a>. His Blue Bloc won a tight election in which his party came third overall, winning Rasmussen the ability to form a government.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>All members of Lars Løkke Rasmussen's second Cabinet, composed solely of members of Venstre, were sworn in on 28 June 2015 in the Danish Parliament.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> As of July&#160;2015<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, his Cabinet consists of seventeen ministers.
</p><p>On 28 November 2016 Rasmussen presented <a href="/wiki/Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen_III_Cabinet" title="Lars Løkke Rasmussen III Cabinet">Lars Løkke Rasmussen III Cabinet</a>, composed of members of <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conservative_People%27s_Party_(Denmark)" title="Conservative People&#39;s Party (Denmark)">Conservative People's Party</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Alliance_(Denmark)" title="Liberal Alliance (Denmark)">Liberal Alliance</a>.
</p><p>On 31 May 2018 it was announced that Denmark would be banning full-face veils.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2019_general_election">2019 general election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2019 general election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Though Venstre made the largest gains of any party in the <a href="/wiki/2019_Danish_general_election" title="2019 Danish general election">2019 general election</a>, support for the Danish People's Party and Liberal Alliance collapsed, costing Rasmussen his majority. With the result beyond doubt on election night, Rasmussen conceded defeat to the "red bloc" under the Social Democrats' <a href="/wiki/Mette_Frederiksen" title="Mette Frederiksen">Mette Frederiksen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On 6 June 2019, he announced his resignation. On 31 August 2019, Rasmussen resigned from his position as the chairman of <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre party</a> following weeks of pressure from party members.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rasmussen has on several occasions been accused of spending tax payer money on himself and his family. In the spring of 2008, he was accused by the media - essentially the Danish tabloid <a href="/wiki/Ekstra_Bladet" title="Ekstra Bladet">Ekstra Bladet</a> - of having charged his official accounts with considerable expenses he should have paid himself, e.g. restaurants, cigarettes, taxis, and hotels, both as county mayor<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and as minister. All of this has been well documented, according to several independent media sources, although all charges were dropped and there was never a court trial.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> It was something that was according to the rules of the party <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In May 2007, Rasmussen was again accused by Ekstra Bladet of having his ministry pay for a hotel room in Copenhagen when he privately attended a <a href="/wiki/Paul_McCartney" title="Paul McCartney">Paul McCartney</a> concert in <a href="/wiki/Horsens" title="Horsens">Horsens</a> in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Since the many serious scandals surrounding Rasmussen were brought to the attention of the public, Venstre has suffered in the polls.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Global_Green_Growth_Institute_(GGGI)" class="mw-redirect" title="Global Green Growth Institute (GGGI)">Global Green Growth Institute (GGGI)</a> was criticized by two member countries for its financial management: Norway withheld $10 million in donations, citing excessive spending on flights and food by GGGI former Council Chairman Lars Løkke Rasmussen, and both Norway and Denmark demanded an Audit of the organization's finances before renewing support for 2014. Rasmussen was, as the chairman, accused of being greedy, while the other members of GGGI were not accused.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, Rasmussen's wife was about to get fired and was called to a meeting with the leader of the school where she worked. Rasmussen, as his wife's civil assessor, came to the meeting, along with his bodyguards. His wife was fired at the meeting. The case resulted in criticism because Rasmussen's position as Prime Minister could be perceived as inappropriate pressure on the school leader and in similar meetings it is usually the <a href="/wiki/Union_representative" title="Union representative">union representative</a> that functions as the civil assessor.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
