<p><b>Elland</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a>, in the county of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. It is situated south of <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, by the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Calder_and_Hebble_Navigation" title="Calder and Hebble Navigation">Calder and Hebble Navigation</a>. Elland was recorded as <i>Elant</i> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i>. The town's name is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> meaning 'land by the water, river or land partly or wholly surrounded by water'.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It had a population in <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001</a> of 14,554,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> with the ward being measured at 11,676 in the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-NOMIS_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOMIS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mentioned in the 1086 <i>Domesday Book</i> as <i>Elant</i>, Elland retained continuity of tenure from before the <a href="/wiki/Norman_conquest_of_England" title="Norman conquest of England">Norman Conquest</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, as the Elland family were descended from <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo-Saxon">Anglo-Saxon</a> <a href="/wiki/Thegn" title="Thegn">thegns</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Manorialism" title="Manorialism">Manor</a> of Elland, with <a href="/wiki/Greetland" title="Greetland">Greetland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southowram" title="Southowram">Southowram</a> formed an island of the Honour of <a href="/wiki/Pontefract" title="Pontefract">Pontefract</a> in the surrounding Manor of <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>. In 1350 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sir_John_de_Eland&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sir John de Eland (page does not exist)">Sir John de Eland</a> was murdered, as were his son and grandson in the following year, which extinguished the male line of the family and the manor passed to the Savile family. From this period, the <a href="/wiki/Manor_house" title="Manor house">manor house</a> ceased to be the principal dwelling of a gentry family, as the Saviles had their seat at the moated manor of <a href="/wiki/Thornhill,_West_Yorkshire" title="Thornhill, West Yorkshire">Thornhill</a>. Elland manor house was never completely reconstructed and, when dismantled and excavated in 1975 by the West Yorkshire Archaeology Unit, it was found to incorporate a 13th-century <a href="/wiki/Solar_(room)" title="Solar (room)">solar wing</a> – one of the earliest <a href="/wiki/Secularity" title="Secularity">secular</a> buildings in the county. The manor house stood on a knoll aligned with the bridge over the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a> and was destroyed during the construction of Calderdale Way <a href="/wiki/Bypass_(road)" title="Bypass (road)">bypass</a>. The farm buildings survive.
</p>

<p>At the request of <a href="/wiki/John_de_Warenne,_7th_Earl_of_Surrey" title="John de Warenne, 7th Earl of Surrey">John de Warenne, 7th Earl of Surrey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edward_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward II">Edward II</a> granted a <a href="/wiki/Charter" title="Charter">charter</a>, to John de Eland, for a <a href="/wiki/Free_market" title="Free market">free market</a> on Tuesday at his Manor of Elland, and two <a href="/wiki/Fair" title="Fair">fairs</a>.
</p><p>The town became a centre of wool production. The decline of the <a href="/wiki/Textile_manufacturing" title="Textile manufacturing">woollen industry</a> had a significant effect on the town and many mills were demolished or <a href="/wiki/Mill_conversion" title="Mill conversion">converted</a> to residences.
</p><p>Durable <a href="/wiki/Flagstone" title="Flagstone">flagstones</a>, Elland flags, were quarried near the town and after the canal was constructed, they could be transported economically all over the county.<sup id="cite_ref-dimensionstone7_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dimensionstone7-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-townguideElland_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-townguideElland-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Elland housed the main factory of the manufacturer of <a href="/wiki/Gannex" title="Gannex">Gannex</a> products<sup id="cite_ref-HistoricGannexfactory_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HistoricGannexfactory-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and is the home of the Dobsons sweet factory, which produces traditional <a href="/wiki/List_of_candies#Hard_candy" title="List of candies">boiled sweets</a>. Since 2001, Elland has been home to <a href="/wiki/Suma_(co-operative)" title="Suma (co-operative)">Suma Wholefoods</a>, the largest <a href="/wiki/Worker_cooperative" title="Worker cooperative">workers' co-operative</a> in the United Kingdom.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Elland was historically a <a href="/wiki/Township_(England)" title="Township (England)">township</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Greetland" title="Greetland">Greetland</a>, in the large ancient parish of Halifax.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The township became a <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in 1866, but in 1894 Elland was separated from Greetland and became Elland Urban District (and civil parish). In 1937 Greetland and <a href="/wiki/Stainland" title="Stainland">Stainland</a> were added to the Urban District.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 1974 the urban district and civil parish were abolished and merged into Calderdale Metropolitan Borough.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Landmarks">Landmarks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Landmarks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Mary_the_Virgin,_Elland_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1573006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/St_Mary_the_Virgin%2C_Elland_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1573006.jpg/220px-St_Mary_the_Virgin%2C_Elland_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1573006.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/St_Mary_the_Virgin%2C_Elland_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1573006.jpg/330px-St_Mary_the_Virgin%2C_Elland_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1573006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/St_Mary_the_Virgin%2C_Elland_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1573006.jpg/440px-St_Mary_the_Virgin%2C_Elland_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1573006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="480" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Mary_the_Virgin,_Elland_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1573006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Church_of_St_Mary,_Elland" title="Church of St Mary, Elland">church of St Mary the Virgin</a></div></div></div>
<p>Buildings of interest include the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_St_Mary,_Elland" title="Church of St Mary, Elland">parish church of St Mary the Virgin</a>, the former Rose and Crown Inn in Northgate, the Old Town Hall, Southgate <a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodist Church</a>, the reputedly haunted Fleece Inn at the top of Westgate,<sup id="cite_ref-fleece_inn_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fleece_inn-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> the Rex Cinema and Waxman ceramics on Elland Lane. The remains of the <a href="/wiki/Medieval" class="mw-redirect" title="Medieval">medieval</a> <a href="/wiki/Stocks" title="Stocks">stocks</a> can be found at the junction of Southgate and Elizabeth Street.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Elland_Power_Station">Elland Power Station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Elland Power Station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Former_Elland_Power_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_203659.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Former_Elland_Power_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_203659.jpg/170px-Former_Elland_Power_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_203659.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Former_Elland_Power_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_203659.jpg/255px-Former_Elland_Power_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_203659.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Former_Elland_Power_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_203659.jpg/340px-Former_Elland_Power_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_203659.jpg 2x" data-file-width="480" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Former_Elland_Power_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_203659.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Elland Power Station in 1991</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Elland_Power_Station" title="Elland Power Station">Elland Power Station</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Fossil-fuel_power_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Fossil-fuel power station">coal-fired power station</a> by the River Calder.  It was decommissioned and closed in 1991, in keeping with the trend of generating power at fewer but larger power stations away from towns, and demolished in 1996.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Calder_and_Hebble_Navigation" title="Calder and Hebble Navigation">Calder and Hebble Navigation</a> opened in the late-18th century to serve the growing <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">industrialisation</a> of the Calder Valley. <a href="/wiki/Elland_railway_station" title="Elland railway station">Elland railway station</a> closed in 1962 but the line is still in use as a passenger service for the <a href="/wiki/Caldervale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Caldervale Line">Caldervale Line</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/A643_road" title="A643 road">A643 road</a> begins in <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and ended in Elland. It passes <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_AFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds United AFC">Leeds United AFC</a>'s football ground, <a href="/wiki/Elland_Road" title="Elland Road">Elland Road</a>. It now ends at junction 23 of the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62 motorway</a>.
</p>
