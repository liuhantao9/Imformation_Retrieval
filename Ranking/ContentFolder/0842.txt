<p><b>Nina Hossain</b> (born 15 December 1973) is a British journalist and presenter employed by <a href="/wiki/ITN" title="ITN">ITN</a> as the lead presenter of the <i><a href="/wiki/ITV_Lunchtime_News" title="ITV Lunchtime News">ITV Lunchtime News</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nina_Hossain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hossain was born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, West Yorkshire, England to a Bangladeshi father and an English mother. Her father, Dr Tabarek Hossain (1935–2002), was born in <a href="/wiki/British_India" class="mw-redirect" title="British India">British India</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>) and migrated to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a> in the 1960s (when Bangladesh was still <a href="/wiki/East_Pakistan" title="East Pakistan">East Pakistan</a>). He was a <a href="/wiki/Psychiatry" title="Psychiatry">psychiatrist</a> with a specialism in alcoholism, who set up the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a> Alcohol Advisory Service in 1973 at the town centre, and was the founder and president of Concern For Mental Health in West Yorkshire.<sup id="cite_ref-examiner1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-examiner1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Her father married an English nurse, Pamela from <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a>. Of her Bangladeshi parentage, she states that she has never visited Bangladesh, and does not speak <a href="/wiki/Bengali_language" title="Bengali language">Bengali</a>. She also states that while her father never discussed religion Hossain and her younger sister, Rezina, "were nominally <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_career">Television career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nina_Hossain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Television career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hossain's ambitions to be a journalist began at an early age. She has a degree from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Durham" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Durham">University of Durham</a>, (<a href="/wiki/St_Cuthbert%27s_Society" class="mw-redirect" title="St Cuthbert&#39;s Society">St Cuthbert's Society</a>). She then gained a Postgraduate Diploma in Broadcast Journalism at the department of <a href="/wiki/Journalism,_Media_and_Communication,_University_of_Central_Lancashire" title="Journalism, Media and Communication, University of Central Lancashire">Journalism, Media and Communication, University of Central Lancashire</a>, Preston.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1996 she was chosen for a traineeship at <a href="/wiki/ITV_Border" title="ITV Border">ITV Border</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Lookaround" title="Lookaround">Lookaround</a></i> where she was a news presenter, producer and reporter. In 2000 she was a presenter for The Medical Channel. In 2001 she became a presenter and reporter for <i><a href="/wiki/BBC_London_News" title="BBC London News">BBC London News</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/BBC_London" title="BBC London">BBC London</a>. In March 2004 she became the main presenter on the programme whilst <a href="/wiki/Emily_Maitlis" title="Emily Maitlis">Emily Maitlis</a> took maternity leave.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She was the word pronouncer for Series 1 of <i><a href="/wiki/Hard_Spell" title="Hard Spell">Hard Spell</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> spelling competition for children. She reprised this role in the one-off episode of spin-off <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Spell" title="Star Spell">Star Spell</a></i> (similar to <i>Hard Spell</i>, but with celebrities instead of children), but when <i>Star Spell</i> became a full series, she had joined ITV and so was replaced by BBC newsreader <a href="/wiki/Mishal_Husain" title="Mishal Husain">Mishal Husain</a>, who continued this role through the second series of <i>Hard Spell</i>.
</p><p>In November 2004, Hossain joined <a href="/wiki/ITN" title="ITN">ITN</a> as the main co-presenter for the <i><a href="/wiki/ITV_Evening_News" title="ITV Evening News">ITV Evening News</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>, whilst <a href="/wiki/Mary_Nightingale" title="Mary Nightingale">Mary Nightingale</a> took maternity leave.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Following Nightingale's return she was a relief presenter for <a href="/wiki/ITV_London" title="ITV London">ITV London</a> (<i>ITV News London</i>) and <a href="/wiki/ITV_News" title="ITV News">ITV News</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/ITV_Lunchtime_News" title="ITV Lunchtime News">ITV Lunchtime News</a></i>, <i>ITV Evening News</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/ITV_News_at_Ten" title="ITV News at Ten">ITV News at Ten</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/ITV_News_(weekend_bulletins)" class="mw-redirect" title="ITV News (weekend bulletins)">ITV News</a> weekend bulletins</i>).
</p><p>During April 2010, she was also a guest presenter for <a href="/wiki/GMTV" title="GMTV">GMTV</a>, standing in for <a href="/wiki/Lorraine_Kelly" title="Lorraine Kelly">Lorraine Kelly</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/LK_Today#GMTV_with_Lorraine" class="mw-redirect" title="LK Today">GMTV with Lorraine</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-mirror1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mirror1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2010, she replaced <a href="/wiki/Katie_Derham" title="Katie Derham">Katie Derham</a> as a presenter of <i>London Tonight</i>, initially alongside <a href="/wiki/Donal_MacIntyre" title="Donal MacIntyre">Donal MacIntyre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> However, on 1 November 2010 she became the sole presenter. She also presented the <i>ITV Lunchtime News</i> in a shared role with <a href="/wiki/Alastair_Stewart" title="Alastair Stewart">Alastair Stewart</a>.
</p><p>Hossain continued to be an occasional relief presenter for the <i><a href="/wiki/ITV_Evening_News" title="ITV Evening News">ITV Evening News</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/ITV_News_at_Ten" title="ITV News at Ten">ITV News at Ten</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/ITV_Weekend_News" title="ITV Weekend News">ITV Weekend News</a></i>.
</p><p>On 24 May 2019 it was announced Hossain would leave <i>ITV News London</i> and become the lead presenter of the <i>ITV Lunchtime News</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She presented her final <i>ITV News London</i> 6pm bulletin on 19 June 2019.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_work">Other work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nina_Hossain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004, Hossain appeared in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Trauma_(2004_film)" title="Trauma (2004 film)">Trauma</a></i>, which starred <a href="/wiki/Colin_Firth" title="Colin Firth">Colin Firth</a>.
</p><p>Hossain reads a fake news story at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a> song "The Man Who Stole a Leopard" and performs the voice of the satellite navigation on "<a href="/wiki/Blame_the_Machines" class="mw-redirect" title="Blame the Machines">Blame the Machines</a>", both from the band's 2010 album <i><a href="/wiki/All_You_Need_Is_Now" title="All You Need Is Now">All You Need Is Now</a></i>.
</p><p>She is also the charity ambassitor for Whizz Kidz, doing the 2017 Virgin London Marathon to raise funds for them.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nina_Hossain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In January 2015, she was nominated for the Services to Media award at the <a href="/wiki/British_Muslim_Awards" title="British Muslim Awards">British Muslim Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-asianimage_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-asianimage-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nina_Hossain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hossain now lives between London and the Midlands. She is divorced from her former husband of eight years, <a href="/wiki/Sky_News" title="Sky News">Sky News</a> editor Craig O'Hara.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Hossain has been in a relationship with her current partner, former editor of ITV's <i><a href="/wiki/London_Tonight" class="mw-redirect" title="London Tonight">London Tonight</a></i> regional news programme, Stuart Thomas, since the spring of 2005 and together they have three children.<sup id="cite_ref-thesun_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thesun-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-examiner2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-examiner2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, Hossain drew attention from the media when she was mugged on London's <a href="/wiki/South_Bank" title="South Bank">South Bank</a>, and for going to work the same day, saying that she did not consider it "a big deal".<sup id="cite_ref-mirror2_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mirror2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2007, it was revealed that sex offender David Decoteau from London, 45, had an unhealthy interest in Hossain and that he had also sent letters to BBC newsreader <a href="/wiki/Emily_Maitlis" title="Emily Maitlis">Emily Maitlis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
