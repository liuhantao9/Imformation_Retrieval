<p><b>Ian Berry</b> (previously known as <b>Denimu</b>) is a British born artist based in <a href="/wiki/Poplar,_London" title="Poplar, London">Poplar</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_London" title="East London">East London</a> <sup id="cite_ref-:10_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He creates artwork solely from denim; re-using jeans, jackets, and other denim clothing to create portraits, urbanscapes and other unique works.<sup id="cite_ref-trendhu_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trendhu-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tst_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tst-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-timeout_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeout-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-jd_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jd-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Ian Berry was named as one of <i>Art Business News</i> "30 under 30" influential artists in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ian has dropped using the Denimu name he went by in the early years and just goes by his real name, Ian Berry<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ian was born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> (he was previously based in <a href="/wiki/Landskrona" title="Landskrona">Landskrona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a> <sup id="cite_ref-dailymail_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dailymail-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-huffpo_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffpo-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-times_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-trendhu_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trendhu-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> as well as Sydney, Australia)<sup id="cite_ref-:8_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ian Berry began experimenting with art while at university and then while working at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tullo_Marshall_Warren&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tullo Marshall Warren (page does not exist)">TMW</a> as an art director.<sup id="cite_ref-catto_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-catto-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In advertising he worked on brands like the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">RAF</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nissan" title="Nissan">Nissan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guinness" title="Guinness">Guinness</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Airways" title="British Airways">British Airways</a> and <a href="/wiki/Talisker_distillery" title="Talisker distillery">Talisker</a> whiskey<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While first experimenting with denim at the end of university he carried on while working as an art director in London, he then left to work in Australia. Berry started to work full-time on his artwork when he moved to Sweden from Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-catto_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-catto-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As well as London his artwork has been featured in galleries and Art Fairs across Sweden, Portugal (<a href="/wiki/Calheta,_Madeira" title="Calheta, Madeira">Calheta Madeira</a>), United States (<a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fairmount,_Indiana" title="Fairmount, Indiana">Fairmount,</a> <a href="/wiki/Asbury_Park,_New_Jersey" title="Asbury Park, New Jersey">Asbury Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Hamptons" title="The Hamptons">Hamptons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland</a>, <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paducah,_Kentucky" title="Paducah, Kentucky">Paducah</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-catto_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-catto-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-plave_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-plave-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-shop_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shop-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hab_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hab-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medium">Medium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Medium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ian Berry's works with textile, denim.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He told Selvedge Magazine how 'you can't mix denim like you can paint'<sup id="cite_ref-:3_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His process involves cutting, stitching, and gluing various shades of denim to provide contrast and shadow.<sup id="cite_ref-catto_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-catto-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-juz_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-juz-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Even at touching distance, many viewers don't realise that they are looking at many layers, and shades, of denim jeans.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Many people compare it to <a href="/wiki/Photorealism" title="Photorealism">Photorealism</a>, but just with <a href="/wiki/Denim" title="Denim">denim</a>, not paint.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-chuckievision.com_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chuckievision.com-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Denim_-_Don&#39;t_Just_Wear_It!_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Denim_-_Don&#39;t_Just_Wear_It!-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>'your first impression is that of an indigo coloured oil painting, or a photograph in blue.'<sup id="cite_ref-Denim_-_Don&#39;t_Just_Wear_It!_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Denim_-_Don&#39;t_Just_Wear_It!-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In real life the pieces are very layered and three dimensional, something that gets lost once views online or in print.<sup id="cite_ref-Denim_-_Don&#39;t_Just_Wear_It!_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Denim_-_Don&#39;t_Just_Wear_It!-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Many people can believe they are made of denim.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fellow artist Colin Fraser described Ian's technique as 'Ian uses denim as a painter uses paint, but with a difference: he makes what I call ‘denImages’. The entire surface of these works is made from denim. The scissors are Ian's paint brush and he handles them with virtuosity.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In each work, the artist carefully selects denim samples that he cuts, trims, tears and then glues to create works of depth and space. This depth pushes the boundaries of conventional central perspective by building up layers that reach out from the picture plane and draw us in to look again.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Vice_(magazine)" title="Vice (magazine)">Vice</a> <a href="/wiki/The_Creators_Project" class="mw-redirect" title="The Creators Project">Creators Project</a> wrote he 'develops his work by selecting individual pieces of denim and certifying each is a unique wash. Afterward, all the many denim parts are layered to produce a three-dimensional collage effect. All together, the pieces collate into an established image from afar, bringing together light, color, and many of the elements of classic painting style.'<sup id="cite_ref-:2_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ian has a studio in <a href="/wiki/Poplar,_London" title="Poplar, London">Poplar</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_London" title="East London">East London</a> where he has thousands of pairs of jeans organised in a <a href="/wiki/Color_palette_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect" title="Color palette (disambiguation)">palette</a> from light to dark, he gets many donations from brands, denim mills, as well as looking in charity shops, vintage store yet start with his own jeans, then his friends, then their friends. Now he gets packages from around the world <sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:5_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Siegle_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Siegle-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Idea">The Idea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Idea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The idea came from a simple observation when he was back at his family home in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>. His Mum knew he was never moving home so had cleared and sorted some of his room. There was a pile of <a href="/wiki/Denim" title="Denim">denim</a> where he noticed the different shades of <a href="/wiki/Indigo" title="Indigo">indigo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He started to think of his relationship with denim and remembered a time when he was 14 and had to wear chords at a family party, and when he got there everyone was wearing jeans and even now, it is the only material he feel really comfortable wearing and so it became the only medium he felt comfortable using.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Denim.2C_The_Material_of_Our_Time"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Denim,_The_Material_of_Our_Time">Denim, The Material of Our Time</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Denim, The Material of Our Time">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is often said that denim is the most <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratic</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fabrics" class="mw-redirect" title="Fabrics">fabrics</a>, and he really feel like that he can tap into this, and he can communicate with people as there is something about the denim that draws them in, even if they find the work unusual, it is still something familiar. You don't need to be a connoisseur to enjoy denim, anyone can wear it he says. While it started as a very rural material, 'I feel now it is a very urban one, and the layers of urban life is what interests me and what I portray, so what better material is there to depict our contemporary life, than with the material of our time.' <sup id="cite_ref-:3_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As he portrays the changes in our cities, often depicting places that are closing down or at risk of with using denim he is adding a comment on the fading fabric of the urban environment".<sup><a href="/wiki/Denimu#cite_note-:4-36" title="Denimu">[37]</a><a href="/wiki/Denimu#cite_note-:10-1" title="Denimu">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:4_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Movement">Movement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Movement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While many reports talk about it being made of only denim, Ian said "It is not about it being about denim. It’s just my medium. Yes, it helps, but it’s not about the material foremost. I see it as very much an urban material now. I love urban society and all the layers and depth within it and for me, what better way to portray contemporary life and issues than with the material of our time."<sup id="cite_ref-:2_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ian's pieces are so detailed many talk of his work in terms of <a href="/wiki/Photorealism" title="Photorealism">photorealism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Levi%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="Levi&#39;s">Levis</a> unzipped said 'from a distance, indigo's high contrast and gradient fades create a stunning illusion of depth, light, and ultimately, photorealism.'<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ian Said “Admittedly at the beginning I often used parts like the pockets and seams to kind of say, 'Hey look, it's denim,' but now I use all those parts in a way to not show it's denim. Sometimes, this has been too successful though. [It’s] maybe a compliment when people mistake it for a photo-realistic painting, but often people don’t get to the ‘aha!’ factor when the penny drops—it's denim!"<sup id="cite_ref-:2_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Fiona_MacCarthy" title="Fiona MacCarthy">Fiona McCarthy</a> of the Sunday Times said 'one of a number of artists fusing the slightly loftier world of art with the often (unjustifiably) denigrated world of craft. Like <a href="/wiki/Tracey_Emin" title="Tracey Emin">Tracey Emin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grayson_Perry" title="Grayson Perry">Grayson Perry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Close" title="Chuck Close">Chuck Close</a> before him, Berry is pushing the boundaries of <a href="/wiki/Textile" title="Textile">Textiles</a> as a medium, producing <a href="/wiki/Photorealism" title="Photorealism">photorealistic</a> denim <a href="/wiki/Collage" title="Collage">collages</a> depicting everyday scenes.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Photorealism" title="Photorealism">Photorealism</a> is a movement or <a href="/wiki/Genre" title="Genre">genre</a> that brings together various kinds of art form where the artist recreates an image or photograph (after close observation) as realistically as his skills enable him to. The movement became increasingly popular in the last century, and over the last couple of decades has seen tremendous change with digital machinery making it a near-precise art.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Ian does base his work of photography, photographs that he took and set up the scene.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ian Berry is a <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_art" title="Contemporary art">Contemporary Artist</a> but his work has also been talked about in the context of <a href="/wiki/Arte_Povera" title="Arte Povera">Arte Povera</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Collage" title="Collage">Collage</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Secret_Garden_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Secret_Garden-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> and also with his medium being denim <a href="/wiki/Textile_arts" title="Textile arts">Textile Art</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiber_art" title="Fiber art">Fiber Art,</a> <sup id="cite_ref-:8_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:9_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:3_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> and while not using <a href="/wiki/Quilting" title="Quilting">quilting</a> techniques many quilters follow Ian and he is often mentioned in that context.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ian work has also often been talked about in the context of <a href="/wiki/Eco-art" class="mw-redirect" title="Eco-art">Eco-Art</a>, <a href="/wiki/Upcycling" title="Upcycling">Upcycling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Recycling" title="Recycling">Recycling</a><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:7_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Siegle_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Siegle-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Work">Work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He lives in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and portrays the city he is surrounded by depicting the changing urban life<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:7_60-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> he also did a body of work on <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">USA</a> <a href="/wiki/Urban_culture" title="Urban culture">urban</a> living with the <i>American Jean</i><sup><a href="/wiki/Denimu#cite_note-67" title="Denimu">[67]</a></sup> which was based mainly on <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>. He had seen that in many cities, the places that were meeting places for the community were changing, closing down for bigger development or left empty.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Pubs were closing down, launderettes left empty, diners being replaced. He often depicted the places with scenes of loneliness.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some of his major bodies of work are below.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Behind_Closed_Doors">Behind Closed Doors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Behind Closed Doors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ian Berry's 2016 London solo show was a big success. The gallery had said "it’s easy to get carried away with the novelty of the medium and forget that, first and foremost, Ian Berry is an artist with something to say. Well, in this two-part show he is speaking loud and clear."<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> Art Critic <a href="/wiki/Tabish_Khair" title="Tabish Khair">Tabish Khan</a> wrote it was a top 5 London Exhibition for Fad magazine 'What Berry can do with denim is astonishing. He creates multi-layered paintings, his attention to detail is superb and this exhibition is an impressive feat'<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-chuckievision.com_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chuckievision.com-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The scenes are the ubiquitous life of London residents that form the creative foundation for a series of poignant pieces of art made of azure and bright navy swatches of denim. Framed within the lonely enclaves of home life, the settings of many of the graceful, hyper-detailed portraits are fixated on an isolated interior. Wide, cinematic tableaus of a woman just waking up to bright sunlight is mirrored in two other similarly evocative visuals of a sleeping girl in a rumpled camisole. The collages of day-to-day life filled out in the medium of denim portrayed a perfect life, almost like a page from a <a href="/wiki/Interior_design" title="Interior design">interior design</a> magazine however the lonely figure offered a <a href="/wiki/Melancholia" title="Melancholia">melancholic</a> view that <a href="/wiki/Materialism" title="Materialism">materialistic</a> possession doesn't make a happy life.
</p><p><sup id="cite_ref-:10_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:8_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cattogallery.co.uk_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cattogallery.co.uk-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cattogallery.co.uk_68-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cattogallery.co.uk-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="My_Beautiful_Launderette">My Beautiful Launderette</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: My Beautiful Launderette">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By the end of the 1970s there were around 12,500 in the country. That number has now slumped to 3,000, 450 here in London. On one photoshoot Ian went back to one he had only visited on a recce the week before only to discover it boarded up. Ian portrayed the interior of launderettes in <a href="/wiki/Chrisp_Street_Market" title="Chrisp Street Market">Crisp Street Market</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poplar,_London" title="Poplar, London">Poplar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bromley-by-Bow" title="Bromley-by-Bow">Bow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ladbroke_Grove" title="Ladbroke Grove">Ladbroke Grove</a> and <a href="/wiki/Holloway_Road" title="Holloway Road">Holloway Road</a> in London.
</p><p>The body of work was a comment on the declining launderettes and what they meant to the community they served.
</p><p>The title of the show was borrowed from a film based on the screenplay of <a href="/wiki/Hanif_Kureishi" title="Hanif Kureishi">Hanif Kureishi</a>, shot in the mid 80's <a href="/wiki/My_Beautiful_Laundrette" title="My Beautiful Laundrette">My Beautiful Launderette</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Frears" title="Stephen Frears">Stephen Frears</a> captured a mood London at the time, the cultural tensions and the economic change but central to the film was the <a href="/wiki/Launderette" class="mw-redirect" title="Launderette">launderette</a>, one scene depicts a crowd of customers gathering outside, impatient to be let in. Launderettes are now no longer like this.
</p><p>The 'showstopper'<sup id="cite_ref-chuckievision.com_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chuckievision.com-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> was the installation however. Where people could interact and take photos and selfies and sit down and pretend they were waiting for their laundry. Ian made with set builder Luke Aan de Wiel and whole life sized denim launderette with washing machines, dryers, tiled floors and walls, posters and instructions all made in denim.
</p><p><sup id="cite_ref-:1_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Secret_Garden">Secret Garden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Secret Garden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This interactive installation titled the <a href="/wiki/Secret_garden_(installation)" class="mw-redirect" title="Secret garden (installation)">Secret Garden</a> that you could walk through, on top of a denim path that was filled with various flowers and plants, from <a href="/wiki/Rose" title="Rose">roses</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cactus" title="Cactus">cacti,</a> <a href="/wiki/Wisteria" title="Wisteria">wisteria</a> to <a href="/wiki/Chrysanthemum" title="Chrysanthemum">chrysanthemum</a> that hung down like a <a href="/wiki/Trellis_(architecture)" title="Trellis (architecture)">trellis</a>, was shown at the <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_Museum_of_the_Arts" title="Children&#39;s Museum of the Arts">Children's Museum of the Arts</a> <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a> in 2017.
</p><p>The installation for the Bridge Project was inspired by thinking of childhood. Immediately Ian thought of playing outside at his <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> hometown. He believed children play less outside and interact and look less at the nature around. Children have new technology with iPads. He had stated that while he was making the Behind Closed Doors piece, he was thinking of a woman with a perfect home but the children had grown up, leaving the nest empty. The home depicted in the picture, there was a garden at the back. It made him think of the garden being full of the laughter of kids playing once upon a time and now the empty nest. As children would come to the garden in denim with their parents he wanted them to look at being in a garden differently and as in New York seek out a Community Garden together.
</p><p>He wanted to draw attention to the community gardens as a meeting place and a location to enjoy being outside.
</p><p>Ian also used some of the last fabric that the USA had produced to make the installation. After 112 years of production, Cone Mills, a historic denim mill in America, announced it would close its White Oak plant in <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a>. As the last major manufacturer of <a href="/wiki/Selvage" title="Selvage">selvage</a> denim in the United States, its final products served as relics for newer generations to learn about in this exhibition.
</p><p>It was also used as an example of sustainability. Ian worked with Tonello, the Italian laundry manufacturer and technology company. He used their laser machines in this work to cut many of the pieces made for the trellis, and used the ozone washing to colour the denim. These are now some of the new tools for the denim industry.
</p><p>In the opening to the garden, Ian shows a <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a> plant and explained that this is where the jeans we wear first comes from. The headline was from <i>plants to pants, to plants again.</i>
</p><p><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:9_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Secret_Garden_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Secret_Garden-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-denimsandjeans.com_73-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-denimsandjeans.com-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:10_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_59-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Record_Store">Record Store</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Record Store">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2013 Ian Berry turned the gallery into a vintage <a href="/wiki/Phonograph_record" title="Phonograph record">record</a> store. It was in response to the changing High Street and the loss of a lot of independent <a href="/wiki/Record_shop" title="Record shop">Record Stores</a> in the UK, where many in the <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a> community and like minded people would meet. The whole of the gallery window was turned into a <a href="/wiki/Phonograph_record" title="Phonograph record">vinyl</a> store. It was filled with records, tee shirts, records and framed albums. It wasn't just any albums however, they were all chosen for their connection to the denim story.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">Rock and Roll</a> music and denim have gone together through time.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup> With <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis</a> to <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">Bob Dylan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hippie" title="Hippie">hippies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk</a>, the pioneers of youth music have worn jeans. Many of the most famous album covers of all time feature this artisan fabric: the <a href="/wiki/The_Rolling_Stones" title="The Rolling Stones">Rolling Stones’</a> <a href="/wiki/Sticky_Fingers" title="Sticky Fingers">Sticky Fingers</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen's</a> <a href="/wiki/Born_in_the_U.S.A." title="Born in the U.S.A.">Born in the USA</a> for example. In 1975, the musicians who hung around a New York bar called <a href="/wiki/CBGB" title="CBGB">CBGB</a> started to crop their hair and rip their jeans. The look was spotted by musician and designer <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_McLaren" title="Malcolm McLaren">Malcolm McLaren</a>, who adapted it into punk back in London with the <a href="/wiki/Sex_Pistols" title="Sex Pistols">Sex Pistols</a>. Ian was portraying much of this history. He spoke with <a href="/wiki/Robert_Elms" title="Robert Elms">Robert Elms</a> about the albums, who he had met most of the people and bands depicted in the albums.
</p><p>Warhol said of the denim: “I wish I could invent something like blue jeans. Something to be remembered for.” Of course, he went on to design the jeans close-up cover for the Stones’ Sticky Fingers album.
</p><p><sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018 Ian brought the Record Store back together in the US to make a whole high street, including the launderette (changed to Laundromat) and the Secret Garden (changed to a community garden) together with a gallery showing some of Ian's pieces.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other well known works include the Newsstand installation<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-juxtapoz.com_84-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-juxtapoz.com-84">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">&#91;85&#93;</a></sup> and the CCTV Surveillance piece.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">&#91;86&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:9_48-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">&#91;87&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:10_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Portraits">Portraits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Portraits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ian Berry has made many portraits but has turned down most. He does portraits where the person has to have a connection to denim, and be respected and not just celebrity.<sup id="cite_ref-Himelfield_88-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Himelfield-88">&#91;88&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ayrton_Senna">Ayrton Senna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Ayrton Senna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ian Berry was commissioned to make a portrait of the late <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> racing driver, <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, in 2015 to commemorate the 20th year of his passing. He immortalized Ayrton in denim, including jeans from the family of Ayrton who look after his legacy with the <a href="/wiki/Instituto_Ayrton_Senna" title="Instituto Ayrton Senna">Instituto Ayrton Senna</a>. The piece was unveiled in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> before the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_Grands_Prix" title="List of Formula One Grands Prix">Grand Prix</a> to the press and family and was met with positive reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-Himelfield_88-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Himelfield-88">&#91;88&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">&#91;90&#93;</a></sup> The piece subsequently traveled around the world including to <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> Grand Prix, <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a> at Adplog, at <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a><sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">&#91;92&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Respected Motorsport journalist Maurice Hamilton said 'I'm not in the least surprised that Ayrton's sister, <a href="/wiki/Viviane_Senna" title="Viviane Senna">Viviane</a>, found it difficult to contain her emotions when she saw the work for the first time during an official unveiling last year. And the nice thing is that the entire project has been associated with the <a href="/wiki/Instituto_Ayrton_Senna" title="Instituto Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna Foundation</a>, for which Viviane and the family continue to do such good work in the name of underprivileged children in Brazil. There's a short but moody video on YouTube <sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup> showing the unassuming and hugely talented Berry at work.'<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At one Event in Birmingham there was an emotional showing and presentation of the people by many who knew Ayrton closely like <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, who replaced Ayrton in the team after he died in 1994, <a href="/wiki/Allan_McNish" title="Allan McNish">Allan McNish</a> who had been his test driver as well as a former F1 driver and <a href="/wiki/David_Brabham" title="David Brabham">David Brabham</a> who was in the same <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">Imola</a> race when Ayrton died.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Paul Weaver at the <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">Guardian</a> called it '<i>Berry's finest creation'</i> and called the piece <i>'Stunning'</i>. He also spoke how Ian didn't sign the piece 'as it wasn't about me, its about Ayrton' but Ayrton's mother insisted he signed it.<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ian liked the idea that it was the families jeans that made up his image, as they are the ones that are carrying on his legacy with the charity and also licensing and protecting his image. He also spoke how he had worked with denim brands, and was often seen in denim but what he especially loved is that like denim, Ayrton transcended so many people and demographics, but was ultimately a man of the people and denim represented that well.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Debbie_Harry">Debbie Harry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Debbie Harry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

Ian wanted to do the commission as Debbie Harry was a big part of the CBGB bands down the Bowery in the 70's. It was this group of bands that changed the history of music, but also denim.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">&#91;97&#93;</a></sup> Debbie harry was also one of the first to be seen to wear double denim<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">&#91;98&#93;</a></sup> The portrait got rave reviews and reactions from the media on the opening night.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">&#91;99&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Independent_100-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Independent-100">&#91;100&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Independent_100-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Independent-100">&#91;100&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">&#91;101&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">&#91;102&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">&#91;103&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">&#91;104&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quotes">Quotes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Quotes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Denimu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ian Berry believes in art and creativity in education and has given a lot of time to schools, colleges and universities.<sup id="cite_ref-128" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-128">&#91;128&#93;</a></sup> He has given lectures all around the world and as well as the UK has spoke in the USA<sup id="cite_ref-129" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-129">&#91;129&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a><sup id="cite_ref-130" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-130">&#91;130&#93;</a></sup> Brazil, Holland<sup id="cite_ref-131" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-131">&#91;131&#93;</a></sup> as well as giving workshops<sup id="cite_ref-132" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-132">&#91;132&#93;</a></sup> Many schools do projects on Ian Berry and children are inspired to make works in denim like him.<sup id="cite_ref-133" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-133">&#91;133&#93;</a></sup> Many education magazines have featured Ian's work including <a href="/wiki/Scholastic_Corporation" title="Scholastic Corporation">Scholastic</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-134" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-134">&#91;134&#93;</a></sup> arts&amp;activities,<sup id="cite_ref-135" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-135">&#91;135&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Science_%26_Vie_Junior" title="Science &amp; Vie Junior">Science &amp; Vie</a><sup id="cite_ref-136" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-136">&#91;136&#93;</a></sup> as well as <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic" title="National Geographic">National Geographic</a> in Brazil and <a href="/wiki/Newsround" title="Newsround">BBC Newsround</a> in the UK.
</p><p>Ian was so committed to this cause that he exhibited at the Children's Museum of Art <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a> where he showed the Secret Garden in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_48-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> In 2018 he travelled the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and gave lectures in many schools<sup id="cite_ref-137" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-137">&#91;137&#93;</a></sup> and when he found that many students were inspired to do his work, but didn't have the correct equipment so Ian arranged for many companies to step in and donate tools like scissors, denim and rotary cutters.<sup id="cite_ref-138" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-138">&#91;138&#93;</a></sup> Many Children, parents and teachers send him examples of their work inspired from him.<sup id="cite_ref-denimsandjeans.com_73-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-denimsandjeans.com-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Most of Ian's family are or were teachers.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_48-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
