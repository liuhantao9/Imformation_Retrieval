<p><b>Farsley</b> is a town in the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> 6&#160;miles (9&#160;km) to the west of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_city_centre" title="Leeds city centre">Leeds city centre</a>, and 4&#160;miles (6&#160;km) east of <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>. Farsley could be considered a <a href="/wiki/Commuter_town" title="Commuter town">commuter town</a> as it is situated between the two cities. It was formerly in the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> of nearby <a href="/wiki/Pudsey" title="Pudsey">Pudsey</a> and is part of the City of Leeds <a href="/wiki/Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Wards and electoral divisions of the United Kingdom">ward</a> of Calverley and Farsley which also includes the estate of <a href="/wiki/Swinnow" title="Swinnow">Swinnow</a> and some northern parts of Pudsey.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is mentioned in the 1086 <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i> as <i>Ferselleia</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During the industrial revolution Farsley was a centre for wool processing as there were a number of mills in the area.
</p><p>Farsley is just off the main road between <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and Bradford and just off the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Outer_Ring_Road" title="Leeds Outer Ring Road">A6110 Leeds outer ring road</a>. <a href="/wiki/New_Pudsey_railway_station" title="New Pudsey railway station">New Pudsey railway station</a> is between Farsley and Pudsey providing train services towards Leeds, Bradford, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria_station" title="Manchester Victoria station">Manchester Victoria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_North_railway_station" title="Blackpool North railway station">Blackpool</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Amenities">Amenities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Farsley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Amenities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most of Farsley's amenities are situated on or around Town Street.  There is a <a href="/wiki/Co-operative_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Co-operative Group">Co-operative Group</a> convenience store, a post office, bookmakers, several independent retailers as well as four pubs and a wine bar.  <a href="/wiki/Throstle_Nest" class="mw-redirect" title="Throstle Nest">Throstle Nest</a> football ground also has facilities open to the general public.  The only form of public transport in Farsley is bus, services are available to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_city_centre" title="Leeds city centre">Leeds city centre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pudsey" title="Pudsey">Pudsey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Horsforth" title="Horsforth">Horsforth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seacroft" title="Seacroft">Seacroft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>.  New Pudsey railway station which is midway between Leeds and Bradford is about 1 mile away from the centre of Farsley. There are also several schools in Farsley, as well as further schools in neighbouring Pudsey.  Nearby Pudsey offers a wider range of amenities, which many Farsley residents take advantage of.  The nearest large supermarket is <a href="/wiki/Asda" title="Asda">Asda</a> at the Owlcotes Centre in <a href="/wiki/Stanningley" title="Stanningley">Stanningley</a>, there is also a <a href="/wiki/Marks_and_Spencers" class="mw-redirect" title="Marks and Spencers">Marks and Spencers</a>. Only four of the previously 11 pubs are left in the town. Among them, <i>The Fleece</i> at the bottom of Town Street was the second pub purchased by <a href="/wiki/Tetley%27s_Brewery" title="Tetley&#39;s Brewery">Joshua Tetley's &amp; Son</a> the Leeds Brewer, and the first outside of the brewer's grounds. The other public houses are <i>The Old Hall</i> and <i>The Bay Horse</i>. In addition, The Farsley Village Wine Bar is located in Town Street. Farsley also has an Indian restaurant named <i>Deeva</i> and a Mediterranean restaurant named <i>The Olive and Feta</i>.  There are also three coffee lounges offering food: The Hub, Coopers, and The Mill Kitchen.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Farsley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Farsley there are three primary schools: Farsley Farfield Primary School for 3 to 11 year old children,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Westroyd Primary School and Nursery<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> for 3 to 11 year olds, and Farsley Springbank Primary School<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> for 4 to 11 year old pupils. The local secondary school is <a href="/wiki/Priesthorpe_School" title="Priesthorpe School">Priesthorpe School</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> with about 1100 pupils.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Farsley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Farsley_Celtic_03_2_September_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Farsley_Celtic_03_2_September_2017.jpg/220px-Farsley_Celtic_03_2_September_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Farsley_Celtic_03_2_September_2017.jpg/330px-Farsley_Celtic_03_2_September_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Farsley_Celtic_03_2_September_2017.jpg/440px-Farsley_Celtic_03_2_September_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Farsley_Celtic_03_2_September_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Throstle_Nest" class="mw-redirect" title="Throstle Nest">Throstle Nest</a> football ground</div></div></div>
<p>Farsley is home to the <a href="/wiki/Non-League_football" title="Non-League football">non-League football</a> team <a href="/wiki/Farsley_Celtic_F.C." title="Farsley Celtic F.C.">Farsley Celtic F.C.</a> who play at <a href="/wiki/Throstle_Nest" class="mw-redirect" title="Throstle Nest">Throstle Nest</a>. They were formed to replace <a href="/wiki/Farsley_Celtic_A.F.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Farsley Celtic A.F.C.">Farsley Celtic A.F.C.</a> who played in the <a href="/wiki/Football_Conference" class="mw-redirect" title="Football Conference">Football Conference</a> for several years prior to their winding up in March 2010.
</p><p>Farsley Cricket Club, whose ground is situated in Red Lane, play in the <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Cricket_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradford Cricket League">Bradford League</a> Division 1. <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Illingworth" class="mw-redirect" title="Raymond Illingworth">Raymond Illingworth</a>, former <a href="/wiki/England_cricket_team" title="England cricket team">England cricket captain</a>, is their most notable former player.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Housing">Housing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Farsley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Housing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Marsden_Court,_Farsley_1_September_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Marsden_Court%2C_Farsley_1_September_2017.jpg/220px-Marsden_Court%2C_Farsley_1_September_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Marsden_Court%2C_Farsley_1_September_2017.jpg/330px-Marsden_Court%2C_Farsley_1_September_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Marsden_Court%2C_Farsley_1_September_2017.jpg/440px-Marsden_Court%2C_Farsley_1_September_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2868" data-file-height="2292" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Marsden_Court,_Farsley_1_September_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Marsden Court flats, with low rise to left</div></div></div>
<p>Farsley has a variety of housing. Around Town Street are some older <a href="/wiki/Terrace_houses" class="mw-redirect" title="Terrace houses">terrace houses</a> and smaller cottages. To the west of Town Street is a small <a href="/wiki/Council_estate" class="mw-redirect" title="Council estate">council estate</a>, consisting mainly of <a href="/wiki/Apartment" title="Apartment">flats</a>, the tallest block being twelve stories high.  Towards the outskirts of Farsley there are many large <a href="/wiki/Detached_houses" class="mw-redirect" title="Detached houses">detached houses</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People">People</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Farsley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: People">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The Rev. <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Marsden" title="Samuel Marsden">Samuel Marsden</a> (born in Farsley)<sup id="cite_ref-ADB_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADB-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> was associated with the reformist <a href="/wiki/William_Wilberforce" title="William Wilberforce">William Wilberforce</a> in England, was ordained in 1794, married Elisabeth Fristan, and then sailed to New South Wales, Australia. He arrived in Sydney on 10 March 1794, shortly after the birth of their first child, Anne. By 1795 he was settled in Parramatta, where he became Chaplain, wealthy landowner, farmer and magistrate.  He was known as the "Flogging Parson", because even by the standards of his day, he inflicted severe punishments while acting as a magistrate.<sup id="cite_ref-ADB_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADB-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  This has been attributed to a dislike of Roman Catholics and Irish. Joseph Holt, an Irish priest and activist, left on account of a flogging ordered by Marsden.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  Sheephead Park is a memorial garden dedicated to Marsden and is situated on Farsley Town Street.</li>
<li>Sculptor <a href="/wiki/John_Wormald_Appleyard" title="John Wormald Appleyard">John Wormald Appleyard</a> (1831–1894) grew up in Farsley.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> There is a stained glass window dedicated to him, in St John's Church.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">Rugby league</a> footballer <a href="/wiki/Fred_Farrar" title="Fred Farrar">Fred Farrar</a>, whose nickname was The Farsley Flyer, was a member of <a href="/wiki/Hunslet_R.L.F.C." title="Hunslet R.L.F.C.">Hunslet</a>'s <a href="/wiki/1907%E2%80%9308_Northern_Rugby_Football_Union_season" title="1907–08 Northern Rugby Football Union season">1907–08</a> <a href="/wiki/All_Four_Cups" title="All Four Cups">All Four Cups</a> winning team.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li></ul>
