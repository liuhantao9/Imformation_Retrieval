<p><i><b>Remember Me</b></i> is a British drama television serial that premiered on <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> in autumn 2014, and subsequently made its U.S. debut on <a href="/wiki/Public_Broadcasting_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Broadcasting Service">PBS</a> in summer 2017. Written by <a href="/wiki/Gwyneth_Hughes" title="Gwyneth Hughes">Gwyneth Hughes</a>, the three-part mystery was made by <a href="/wiki/Mammoth_Screen" title="Mammoth Screen">Mammoth Screen</a>. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Palin" title="Michael Palin">Michael Palin</a> plays the lead role, making it his first regular television acting role in 20 years.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remember_Me_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The elderly Tom Parfitt (<a href="/wiki/Michael_Palin" title="Michael Palin">Michael Palin</a>) fakes a fall at his long-term terraced house in bleak Yorkshire, to escape to a care home. However, moments after arriving the social worker that delivered Tom is thrown from the seemingly impenetrable fourth floor window of Tom's room, with only her and the frail Tom inside. This mystifies teenaged care assistant Hannah (<a href="/wiki/Jodie_Comer" title="Jodie Comer">Jodie Comer</a>), although local police, including depressed detective Rob Fairholme (<a href="/wiki/Mark_Addy" title="Mark Addy">Mark Addy</a>) are largely uninterested. One by one, strange things begin happening to those in contact with Tom, including Hannah and her family, and Tom soon vanishes from his hospital bed without explanation. Returning to Tom's home, Hannah starts to piece together several clues that lead her to Scarborough, where a dark secret from Tom's past comes to light and it becomes apparent that the paranormal is moving against them.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remember_Me_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2013, the series was commissioned by <a href="/wiki/Ben_Stephenson" title="Ben Stephenson">Ben Stephenson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Danny_Cohen_(television_executive)" title="Danny Cohen (television executive)">Danny Cohen</a> for <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Filming began in January 2014 in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_North_Yorkshire" title="Scarborough, North Yorkshire">Scarborough</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The series is produced by Chris Carey and directed by Ashley Pearce. Funding was provided by ITV GE and North Light Film Studios.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/British_Film_Institute" title="British Film Institute">British Film Institute</a> issued a tax break on the series.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It started airing Sunday 23 November 2014 at 9pm. The series lasted only one run of three episodes.
</p>
