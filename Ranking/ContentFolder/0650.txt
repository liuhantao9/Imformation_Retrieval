<p><b>National League 2 North</b>, (which before September 2009 was known as National Division Three North) is a level four league in the <a href="/wiki/English_rugby_union_system" title="English rugby union system">English rugby union system</a> and provides mostly amateur competition for teams in the northern half of England. From 2009–10 the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_Union" title="Rugby Football Union">RFU</a> restructured the league system in England, and this league was expanded from fourteen to sixteen teams. Participating clubs are from the <a href="/wiki/English_Midlands" class="mw-redirect" title="English Midlands">English Midlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a>. Each team plays thirty league games on a home and away basis. The champion club is promoted to <a href="/wiki/National_Division_Two" class="mw-redirect" title="National Division Two">National League 1</a> and the runner-up participates in a one-off play-off with the runner-up of <a href="/wiki/National_League_2_South" title="National League 2 South">National League 2 South</a> for promotion. Relegation is to either the <a href="/wiki/Midlands_Premier" title="Midlands Premier">Midlands Premier</a> or <a href="/wiki/North_Premier" title="North Premier">North Premier</a> leagues depending on where the teams are based.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Participating_teams_and_locations">Participating teams and locations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_League_2_North&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Participating teams and locations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eleven of the teams listed below participated in the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_National_League_2_North" title="2018–19 National League 2 North">2018–19 National League 2 North</a> season.  The 2018–19 champions, <a href="/wiki/Hull_Ionians" title="Hull Ionians">Hull Ionians</a>, were promoted into the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_National_League_1" title="2019–20 National League 1">2019–20 National League 1</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Caldy_Rugby_Football_Club" title="Caldy Rugby Football Club">Caldy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Loughborough_Students_RUFC" title="Loughborough Students RUFC">Loughborough Students</a> who were relegated from the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_National_League_1" title="2018–19 National League 1">2018–19 National League 1</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The three teams relegated from National League 2 North the previous season were <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_Lions_RFC" title="Peterborough Lions RFC">Peterborough Lions</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Leicester_RFC" title="South Leicester RFC">South Leicester</a> (both <a href="/wiki/Midlands_Premier" title="Midlands Premier">Midlands Premier</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Macclesfield_R.U.F.C." title="Macclesfield R.U.F.C.">Macclesfield</a> (<a href="/wiki/North_Premier" title="North Premier">North Premier</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The promoted teams include <a href="/wiki/Scunthorpe_Rugby_Club" title="Scunthorpe Rugby Club">Scunthorpe</a> (champions) and <a href="/wiki/Luctonians" title="Luctonians">Luctonians</a> (play-offs) from Midlands Premier, while <a href="/wiki/Hull_RUFC" title="Hull RUFC">Hull</a> come up as champions of North Premier.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  In order to address an imbalance of teams, <a href="/wiki/Leicester_Lions_RFC" title="Leicester Lions RFC">Leicester Lions</a> have been level transferred to the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_National_League_2_South" title="2018–19 National League 2 South">2018–19 National League 2 South</a> as the most appropriate located team in terms of access to the southern sides.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion_play-offs">Promotion play-offs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_League_2_North&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Promotion play-offs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since the 2000–01 season there has been a play-off between the league runners-up of National League 2 North and <a href="/wiki/National_League_2_South" title="National League 2 South">National League 2 South</a> for the third and final promotion place to <a href="/wiki/National_League_1" title="National League 1">National League 1</a>. The team with the superior league record having home advantage in the tie.  As of the end of the 2018–19 season the southern teams have been more successful with fourteen wins to the northern teams four, while the home side has won thirteen teams to the away sides five.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Records">Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_League_2_North&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Note that all records are from 1996-97 season onwards as this is widely held as the dawn of professionalism across the English club game.  It also offers a better comparison between seasons as the division team numbers are roughly equal (for example when league rugby union first started in 1987-88 the northern league had only 11 teams playing 10 games each, compared to 14 teams in 1996-97 playing 26 games (home &amp; away), going up to 16 teams in 2009-10 playing 30 games each).  Attendance records are from 2000 onwards unless otherwise specified.  All records are up to date up till the end of the 2018-19 season.</i>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_League_2_North&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">
<div class="mw-references-wrap"><ol class="references">
<li id="cite_note-10"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-10">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">As no northern teams from <a href="/wiki/National_League_1" title="National League 1">National 3</a> were relegated into the division at the end of the season, there was no relegation.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-11"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-11">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">This year there was a league restructure with a new league (Courage National League 4) becoming the new tier 4.  Courage National 4 would contain the previous season's top teams from National 4 North and National 4 South with the remaining teams joining lower tier teams as part of <a href="/wiki/Courage_League_Division_5" title="Courage League Division 5">Courage League Division 5</a> (which retained the North/South divisions).  This structure would continue for several years before being abolished at the end of 1996 where the league would revert to the old system.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-12"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-12">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text"> The top eight teams were all promoted to the re-organised, sixteen team, <a href="/wiki/National_League_1" title="National League 1">Courage League Division 3</a> for season 1996–97 </span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-39"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-39">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Rotherham's league title was won during the period when tier 4 was a single national league (1993-96).</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-44"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-44">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Note that due to poor attendance keeping by press and online sources means that only seasons from 2004-05 onwards are included (apart from playoff games).</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-45"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-45">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Note that promotion playoff games include stats for northern clubs only.  Southern club attendances will be covered at the <a href="/wiki/National_League_2_South" title="National League 2 South">National League 2 South</a> page.</span>
</li>
<li id="cite_note-46"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-46">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">This attendance is the lowest recorded but may not actually be the lowest as a couple of playoff games involving northern clubs have no attendance figures due to poor coverage.  It is also worth noting than many Stourbridge fans felt the crowd was twice this large but this is the figure given in The Rugby Paper.</span>
</li>
