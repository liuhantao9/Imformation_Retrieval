<p><b>Rothwell</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> in the south-east of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is situated between <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>.
</p><p>It sits in the <a href="/wiki/Rothwell_(ward)" title="Rothwell (ward)">Rothwell</a> ward of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elmet_and_Rothwell_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Elmet and Rothwell (UK Parliament constituency)">Elmet and Rothwell</a> <a href="/wiki/UK_Parliament_constituencies" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Parliament constituencies">parliamentary constituency</a>. Rothwell is also part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Urban_Area" title="West Yorkshire Urban Area">West Yorkshire Urban Area</a>.
</p><p>Rothwell has a population of 21,010,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and the Rothwell ward has an estimated population of 32,365.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At the 2011 Census only the Leeds Metropolitan Ward remained. This had a population of 20,354.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The town has benefited from recent improvements in the transport infrastructure, most notably the nearby <a href="/wiki/A1_road_(Great_Britain)" title="A1 road (Great Britain)">A1</a>/<a href="/wiki/M1_motorway" title="M1 motorway">M1</a> link road. The nearest railway station is <a href="/wiki/Woodlesford_railway_station" title="Woodlesford railway station">Woodlesford</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rothwell,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Holy_Trinity_Church,_Rothwell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_46367.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Holy_Trinity_Church%2C_Rothwell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_46367.jpg/220px-Holy_Trinity_Church%2C_Rothwell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_46367.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="164" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Holy_Trinity_Church%2C_Rothwell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_46367.jpg/330px-Holy_Trinity_Church%2C_Rothwell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_46367.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Holy_Trinity_Church%2C_Rothwell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_46367.jpg/440px-Holy_Trinity_Church%2C_Rothwell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_46367.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="476" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Holy_Trinity_Church,_Rothwell_-_geograph.org.uk_-_46367.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Holy_Trinity_Church,_Rothwell,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Holy Trinity Church, Rothwell, West Yorkshire">Holy Trinity Church</a> in Rothwell.</div></div></div>
<p>Rothwell was mentioned in the Domesday Book as <i>"Rodewelle"</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>One of the royal lodge's documented owners was <a href="/wiki/John_of_Gaunt,_1st_Duke_of_Lancaster" class="mw-redirect" title="John of Gaunt, 1st Duke of Lancaster">John of Gaunt, 1st Duke of Lancaster</a>, who is supposed to have killed the last wild boar in England while hunting nearby<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>; hence a boar's head formed part of the arms of the former Rothwell Urban District Council. The <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England_parish_church" title="Church of England parish church">parish church</a> (Church Street) is dedicated to Holy Trinity and is on the site of an Anglo Saxon predecessor. The current church, which has a ring of eight bells, is of medieval origins but was substantially rebuilt in the 19th century: the tower retains medieval fabric believed to be from the 15th century.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/John_Blenkinsop" title="John Blenkinsop">John Blenkinsop</a> (1783–1831) is buried at Holy Trinity Church. He was a pioneer in the use of steam locomotives on the nearby <a href="/wiki/Middleton_Railway" title="Middleton Railway">Middleton Railway</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rothwell_Jawbone_2016_01.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Rothwell_Jawbone_2016_01.jpg/220px-Rothwell_Jawbone_2016_01.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="264" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Rothwell_Jawbone_2016_01.jpg/330px-Rothwell_Jawbone_2016_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Rothwell_Jawbone_2016_01.jpg/440px-Rothwell_Jawbone_2016_01.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1615" data-file-height="1939" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rothwell_Jawbone_2016_01.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Whale jaws boundary marker</div></div></div>
<p>The town was granted the rights of a <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> in the 15th century and a twice-yearly <a href="/wiki/Fair" title="Fair">fair</a>. The tradition of a fair is maintained by the annual carnival which is organised by the Rothwell Entertainments Committee.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> May Day is celebrated beside the stone cross and on the Pastures on the first Monday Bank Holiday in May, while Rothwell Carnival is held in Springhead Park on the second Saturday of July every year.
</p><p>An arch made of whale jawbones has marked the northern boundary by the junction with Wood Lane and the <a href="/wiki/A61_road" title="A61 road">A61 road</a> for over 100 years.<sup id="cite_ref-jaws_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jaws-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rothwell is part of the historic <a href="/wiki/Rhubarb_Triangle" title="Rhubarb Triangle">Rhubarb Triangle</a>, with the town and surrounding areas famed for having once produced 90% of the world's winter forced <a href="/wiki/Rhubarb" title="Rhubarb">rhubarb</a> from the forcing sheds that were common across the fields there.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century">20th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rothwell,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 20th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Clock_Tower_of_the_old_Workhouse_-_Wood_Lane_-_geograph.org.uk_-_507276.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Clock_Tower_of_the_old_Workhouse_-_Wood_Lane_-_geograph.org.uk_-_507276.jpg/220px-Clock_Tower_of_the_old_Workhouse_-_Wood_Lane_-_geograph.org.uk_-_507276.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="211" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Clock_Tower_of_the_old_Workhouse_-_Wood_Lane_-_geograph.org.uk_-_507276.jpg/330px-Clock_Tower_of_the_old_Workhouse_-_Wood_Lane_-_geograph.org.uk_-_507276.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Clock_Tower_of_the_old_Workhouse_-_Wood_Lane_-_geograph.org.uk_-_507276.jpg/440px-Clock_Tower_of_the_old_Workhouse_-_Wood_Lane_-_geograph.org.uk_-_507276.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="613" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Clock_Tower_of_the_old_Workhouse_-_Wood_Lane_-_geograph.org.uk_-_507276.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Workhouse clock tower</div></div></div>
<p>St George's Hospital was situated off Wood Lane where now exists Castle Lodge Avenue and associated houses. It was built in 1903 to a design by <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> architect Edward J. Dodgshun by the Rothwell, Methley and Hunslet Joint Isolation Hospital Committee which was formed under the Isolation Hospitals Act 1893 by an order of the West Riding County Council 10 January 1900. When first constructed it was known as the New Union <a href="/wiki/Workhouse" title="Workhouse">Workhouse</a> and Infirmary for the Hunslet Union, On being taken over by the Leeds Public Assistance Committee in 1934 it was renamed St George’s Hospital. In 1934 it was transferred to the Leeds Health Committee. In 1948, the hospital was managed by the Leeds Group B <a href="/wiki/Hospital_Management_Committee" class="mw-redirect" title="Hospital Management Committee">Hospital Management Committee</a>. After local government reorganisation in 1974 it was transferred to the Leeds Eastern District and soon after to the Leeds Western District. The hospital was closed in December 1991. From 1934 the hospital provided accommodation for the elderly ill, patients with chronic and acute <a href="/wiki/Mental_illness" class="mw-redirect" title="Mental illness">mental illness</a>, persons with <a href="/wiki/Learning_disability" title="Learning disability">learning disabilities</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Maternity" class="mw-redirect" title="Maternity">maternity</a> ward and a separate <a href="/wiki/Isolation_ward_(medicine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Isolation ward (medicine)">isolation ward</a>. The site was developed for housing at the start of the 21st century but the original tall clock tower remains.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Rothwell_Temperance_Band" title="Rothwell Temperance Band">Rothwell Temperance Band</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Brass_Band_Sections_in_Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="Brass Band Sections in Britain">Championship section</a> <a href="/wiki/Brass_band_(British_style)" class="mw-redirect" title="Brass band (British style)">brass band</a> founded in Rothwell in 1984. Although they do not rehearse in Rothwell itself, they have strong connections with the town and hold many concerts for the local community. They actually rehearse in <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>. The closest Champion Section Brass Band is the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Imperial_Band" title="Yorkshire Imperial Band">Yorkshire Imperial Urquhart Travel Band</a>, formerly of the Yorkshire Imperial Copperworks based in Stourton, from which the band is named. The Imps, as they are more commonly known, merged with the original Rothwell Band (founded 1881) in the 1990s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Rothwell has a long history of coal mining. It was a site of early mining, using a system known as <a href="/wiki/Bell_pit" title="Bell pit">Bell Pits</a>. Coal mining has been carried out in the area for over 600 years, though coal production stopped on 9 December 1983.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> There were many local pits including the Fanny, the Rose and Rothwell Water Haigh. In 1995, Leeds City Council and Leeds Groundwork formed a partnership which, together with local residents and community groups, transformed the former colliery into a 50-hectare country park.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Rothwell was constituted an <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1894" title="Local Government Act 1894">Local Government Act 1894</a>. In 1937 it was expanded by taking in the <a href="/wiki/Methley" title="Methley">Methley</a> urban district and <a href="/wiki/Hunslet" title="Hunslet">Hunslet</a> Rural District.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rothwellaerial.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Rothwellaerial.JPG/220px-Rothwellaerial.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="107" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Rothwellaerial.JPG/330px-Rothwellaerial.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Rothwellaerial.JPG/440px-Rothwellaerial.JPG 2x" data-file-width="840" data-file-height="408" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rothwellaerial.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Aerial map of Rothwell</div></div></div>
<p>It was incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a>, West Yorkshire by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>. Its inclusion in the Leeds district as opposed to the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Wakefield" title="City of Wakefield">Wakefield metropolitan district</a> was controversial<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>: originally planned for the Leeds district, it was added to the Wakefield district at the request of residents, but then moved to the Leeds district by the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Lords" title="House of Lords">House of Lords</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="21st_century">21st century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rothwell,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 21st century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The introduction of Leeds Valley Park in the early 21st century and its subsequent expansions<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> have caused concerns for residents due to the parking problems caused on neighbouring Wood Lane.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> This is despite numerous efforts by local councillors, and the community, to include further parking restrictions.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The issues are having a significant impact on the quality of life for the residents of the town.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rothwell_Urban_District">Rothwell Urban District</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rothwell,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Rothwell Urban District">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Between 1894 and 1972, Rothwell was constituted as an urban district. This district included the areas of Rothwell, <a href="/wiki/Methley" title="Methley">Methley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oulton,_West_Yorkshire" title="Oulton, West Yorkshire">Oulton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Woodlesford" title="Woodlesford">Woodlesford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stourton,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Stourton, West Yorkshire">Stourton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlton,_Rothwell" title="Carlton, Rothwell">Carlton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robin_Hood,_West_Yorkshire" title="Robin Hood, West Yorkshire">Robin Hood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lofthouse,_West_Yorkshire" title="Lofthouse, West Yorkshire">Lofthouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thorpe_on_the_Hill,_West_Yorkshire" title="Thorpe on the Hill, West Yorkshire">Thorpe</a>. The Rothwell Urban District had a total population of around 25,000, but if it still existed today, that figure would be closer to 30,000. In 1972 these areas were taken into the newly formed City of Leeds Metropolitan District, although Thorpe, Lofthouse, Carlton and some parts of Robin Hood have a <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a> postcode.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Town_centre">Town centre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rothwell,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Town centre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rothwell has a town centre which includes high street chains as well as independent boutique shops.
</p><p>Besides the two annual fairs, a Christmas Fayre takes place in the autumn, and a food and drink fayre in early spring.
</p><p>Since 2007, the town centre has experienced a major redevelopment to respect the local area's conservation status,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> pedestrianising and restoring the original route of Commercial Street.
</p><p>Since this renovation, there has been some concern about threats raised against local business by the monolithic Morrisons store.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> However, in 2015 a competing supermarket requested permission to build a store on Marsh Street<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and in 2017 it was confirmed by local councillor, Karen Bruce, that a second supermarket - ALDI - was to open in the Rothwell area, with construction to begin in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Shortly before construction began, the Morrisons store altered its layout to include a new party section – this caused confusion among residents as they were unable to find items. In early 2019, construction of the new ALDI began and is currently ongoing. This has caused mixed feelings among the residents, whilst some are concerned about the disruption to traffic during the construction phase, others are excited that there will be a greater selection of bread available in the local area.
</p>

<p>Rothwell is home to 3 football clubs:
</p>
<ul><li>Rothwell Town J.F.C<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> – who are based at John O'Gaunts Recreation Ground near Rothwell Labour Club</li>
<li>Rothwell Juniors<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> – Currently based at a purpose-built £1.5&#160;million development at Fleet Lane, <a href="/wiki/Woodlesford" title="Woodlesford">Woodlesford</a>. Open age teams known as simply 'Rothwell FC'.</li>
<li>Rothwell Albion<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> – A newly formed club in the area.</li></ul>
<p>There are numerous teams in the wider Rothwell ward, these include; <a href="/wiki/Carlton,_Rothwell" title="Carlton, Rothwell">Carlton</a> Athletic and <a href="/wiki/Robin_Hood,_West_Yorkshire" title="Robin Hood, West Yorkshire">Robin Hood</a> Athletic, among others.
</p>

</p>
