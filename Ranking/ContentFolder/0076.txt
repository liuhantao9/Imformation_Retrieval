<p><b>Earl of Minto</b>, in the County of Roxburgh, is a title in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Peerage of the United Kingdom">Peerage of the United Kingdom</a>. It was created in 1813 for <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound,_1st_Earl_of_Minto" title="Gilbert Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound, 1st Earl of Minto">Gilbert Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound, 1st Baron Minto</a>. The current earl is <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound,_7th_Earl_of_Minto" title="Timothy Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound, 7th Earl of Minto">Gilbert Timothy George Lariston Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound, 7th Earl of Minto</a> (born 1953).
</p><p>The family seat is Minto Park, near <a href="/wiki/Hawick" title="Hawick">Hawick</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Borders" title="Scottish Borders">Scottish Borders</a>. The original family seat, Minto Castle, was demolished some years ago after having been abandoned for some time.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_of_Minto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The family descends from the politician and judge <a href="/wiki/Sir_Gilbert_Elliot,_1st_Baronet,_of_Minto" title="Sir Gilbert Elliot, 1st Baronet, of Minto">Gilbert Elliot</a>, who served as a <a href="/wiki/Lord_of_Session" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord of Session">Lord of Session</a> under the judicial title of Lord Minto. In 1700 he was created a baronet, of Minto in the County of Roxburgh, in the <a href="/wiki/Baronetage_of_Nova_Scotia" class="mw-redirect" title="Baronetage of Nova Scotia">Baronetage of Nova Scotia</a>. He was succeeded by his son, the <a href="/wiki/Sir_Gilbert_Elliot,_2nd_Baronet,_of_Minto" title="Sir Gilbert Elliot, 2nd Baronet, of Minto">second Baronet</a>. He was also a prominent judge and politician and served as a Lord of Session (under the judicial title of Lord Minto) from 1726 to 1733, as a Lord of the Justiciary from 1733 to 1765 and as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Justice_Clerk" title="Lord Justice Clerk">Lord Justice Clerk</a> from 1763 to 1766. His eldest son, the <a href="/wiki/Sir_Gilbert_Elliot,_3rd_Baronet,_of_Minto" title="Sir Gilbert Elliot, 3rd Baronet, of Minto">third Baronet</a>, was a politician and held ministerial office as a <a href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_Admiralty" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord of the Admiralty">Lord of the Admiralty</a> from 1756 to 1762 and as <a href="/wiki/Treasurer_of_the_Navy" title="Treasurer of the Navy">Treasurer of the Navy</a> from 1767 to 1770. He was succeeded by his eldest son, the fourth Baronet. He was a noted diplomat, politician and colonial administrator and served as <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_India" title="Governor-General of India">Governor-General of India</a> from 1807 to 1813. In 1797 he was raised to the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_Great_Britain" title="Peerage of Great Britain">Peerage of Great Britain</a> as <b>Baron Minto</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Minto,_Scottish_Borders" title="Minto, Scottish Borders">Minto</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Roxburghshire" title="Roxburghshire">County of Roxburgh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 1813 he was further honoured when he was made <b>Viscount Melgund</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Melgund_Castle" title="Melgund Castle">Melgund</a> in the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Forfar" class="mw-redirect" title="County of Forfar">County of Forfar</a>, and <b>Earl of Minto</b>, in the County of Roxburgh.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The latter titles are in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Peerage of the United Kingdom">Peerage of the United Kingdom</a>. In 1797 Lord Minto assumed by Royal licence the additional surnames of Murray-Kynynmound after those of Elliot.
</p><p>He was succeeded by his eldest son, the second Earl. He was a diplomat and <a href="/wiki/Whig_Party_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Whig Party (UK)">Whig</a> politician and held office as <a href="/wiki/First_Lord_of_the_Admiralty" title="First Lord of the Admiralty">First Lord of the Admiralty</a> from 1835 to 1841 and as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Privy_Seal" title="Lord Privy Seal">Lord Privy Seal</a> from 1846 to 1852. Lord Minto was succeeded by his eldest son, the third Earl. He sat as a <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal</a> <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Hythe_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Hythe (UK Parliament constituency)">Hythe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greenock_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Greenock (UK Parliament constituency)">Greenock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clackmannanshire_and_Kinross-shire_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Clackmannanshire and Kinross-shire (UK Parliament constituency)">Clackmannanshire</a>. On his death the titles passed to his son, the fourth Earl. He was a prominent colonial administrator and served as <a href="/wiki/Governor_General_of_Canada" title="Governor General of Canada">Governor General of Canada</a> from 1898 to 1904 and as <a href="/wiki/Viceroy_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Viceroy of India">Viceroy of India</a> from 1905 to 1910. As of 2014<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Earl_of_Minto&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> the titles are held by his great-grandson (the titles having descended from father to son), the seventh Earl, who succeeded in 2005.
</p><p>The family seat is <a href="/wiki/Minto,_Scottish_Borders" title="Minto, Scottish Borders">Minto</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Hawick" title="Hawick">Hawick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roxburghshire" title="Roxburghshire">Roxburghshire</a>. In 1992 Minto House was <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed</a> as Category A, and largely demolished within weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Timothy_Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound,_7th_Earl_of_Minto" title="Timothy Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound, 7th Earl of Minto">Gilbert Timothy George Lariston Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound, 7th Earl of Minto</a> (born 1953)</li></ul>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Heir_apparent" title="Heir apparent">heir apparent</a> is the present holder's eldest son Gilbert Francis Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound, Viscount Melgund (born 1984).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_family_members">Other family members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_of_Minto&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other family members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Numerous other members of the family have also gained distinction. 
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/John_Elliot_(colonial_governor)" class="mw-redirect" title="John Elliot (colonial governor)">John Elliot</a>, younger son of the second Baronet, was an <a href="/wiki/Admiral" title="Admiral">admiral</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> and also served as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Lieutenant_Governors_of_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Lieutenant Governors of Newfoundland and Labrador">Commodore Governor of Newfoundland</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Andrew_Elliot" title="Andrew Elliot">Andrew Elliot</a>, another younger son of the second Baronet, was the last <a href="/wiki/List_of_colonial_governors_of_New_York" title="List of colonial governors of New York">colonial governor of New York</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jean_Elliot" title="Jean Elliot">Jean Elliot</a>, daughter of the second Baronet, was a poet and wrote one of the most famous versions of the Scottish folk tune <i><a href="/wiki/The_Flowers_of_the_Forest" class="mw-redirect" title="The Flowers of the Forest">The Flowers of the Forest</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hugh_Elliot" title="Hugh Elliot">Hugh Elliot</a>, second son of the third Baronet, was a diplomat and colonial administrator and served as <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_the_Leeward_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor of the Leeward Islands">Governor of the Leeward Islands</a> from 1808 to 1814. His son <a href="/wiki/Charles_Elliot" title="Charles Elliot">Sir Charles Elliot</a> was an admiral in the Royal Navy and colonial administrator.</li>
<li>The Hon. <a href="/wiki/George_Elliot_(1784%E2%80%931863)" class="mw-redirect" title="George Elliot (1784–1863)">Sir George Elliot</a>, second son of the first Earl, was an admiral in the Royal Navy and also held political office under <a href="/wiki/Charles_Grey,_2nd_Earl_Grey" title="Charles Grey, 2nd Earl Grey">Lord Grey</a> as <a href="/wiki/First_Secretary_to_the_Admiralty" class="mw-redirect" title="First Secretary to the Admiralty">First Secretary to the Admiralty</a> from 1830 to 1834. He was the father of:
<ul><li>1) <a href="/wiki/George_Elliot_(1813%E2%80%931901)" class="mw-redirect" title="George Elliot (1813–1901)">Sir George Augustus Elliot</a> (1812–1901), an admiral in the Royal Navy, and</li>
<li>2) <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Elliot" title="Alexander Elliot">Sir Alexander James Hardy Elliot</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Major-general" class="mw-redirect" title="Major-general">major-general</a> in the army.</li></ul></li>
<li>The Hon. <a href="/wiki/John_Elliot_(1788%E2%80%931862)" title="John Elliot (1788–1862)">John Edmund Elliot</a>, third son of the first Earl, was a Member of Parliament. His grandson Charles Sinclair Elliot (1853–1915) was a <a href="/wiki/Captain_(naval)" title="Captain (naval)">captain</a> in the Royal Navy.</li>
<li>The Hon. <a href="/wiki/Henry_Elliot" title="Henry Elliot">Sir Henry George Elliot</a>, second son of the second Earl, was a noted diplomat and served as <a href="/wiki/British_Ambassador_to_Austria" class="mw-redirect" title="British Ambassador to Austria">Ambassador to Austria</a> from 1877 to 1884. His son Sir Francis Edmund Hugh Elliot (1851–1940) was also a diplomat and served as Minister to Greece.</li>
<li>Sir <a href="/wiki/Charles_Elliot_(Royal_Navy_officer)" title="Charles Elliot (Royal Navy officer)">Charles Gilbert John Brydone Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound</a> (1818–1895), third son of the second Earl, was an <a href="/wiki/Admiral_of_the_Fleet" class="mw-redirect" title="Admiral of the Fleet">Admiral of the Fleet</a>.</li>
<li>The Hon. <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Elliot_(politician)" title="Arthur Elliot (politician)">Arthur Ralph Douglas Elliot-Murray-Kynynmound</a> (known as Arthur Elliott), second son of the third Earl, was a <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> politician and served as <a href="/wiki/Financial_Secretary_to_the_Treasury" title="Financial Secretary to the Treasury">Financial Secretary to the Treasury</a> in 1903.</li>
<li>The Hon. <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Elliot_(MP)" title="Hugh Elliot (MP)">Hugh Frederick Hislop Elliot</a>, third son of the third Earl, sat as Member of Parliament for <a href="/wiki/North_Ayrshire_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="North Ayrshire (UK Parliament constituency)">Ayrshire North</a>.</li></ul>
