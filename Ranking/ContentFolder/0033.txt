<p><b>Evgeniy Gabrilovich</b> is a senior staff research scientist at <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, specializing in Information Retrieval, <a href="/wiki/Machine_Learning" class="mw-redirect" title="Machine Learning">Machine Learning</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Computational_Linguistics" class="mw-redirect" title="Computational Linguistics">Computational Linguistics</a>, and a Senior Member of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (<a href="/wiki/IEEE" class="mw-redirect" title="IEEE">IEEE</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and an <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">ACM</a> Distinguished Scientist. In 2010, he received the <a href="/wiki/Karen_Sp%C3%A4rck_Jones" title="Karen Spärck Jones">Karen Spärck Jones</a> Award from the <a href="/wiki/British_Computer_Society" title="British Computer Society">British Computer Society</a> Information Retrieval Specialist Group.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Evgeniy_Gabrilovich&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2002, Gabrilovich published a research paper documenting the possibility of an <a href="/wiki/IDN_homograph_attack" title="IDN homograph attack">IDN homograph attack</a>, with fellow researcher <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Gontmakher&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alex Gontmakher (page does not exist)">Alex Gontmakher</a>.
</p><p>In 2005, Gabrilovich earned his PhD degree in Computer Science from the <a href="/wiki/Technion_%E2%80%93_Israel_Institute_of_Technology" title="Technion – Israel Institute of Technology">Technion – Israel Institute of Technology</a>. In his Ph.D. thesis, he developed a methodology for using large scale repositories of world knowledge, such as <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a>, as a basis for improvement of text representations.
</p><p>On November 6, 2007, Gabrilovich was granted a US <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a> for a new method for personalizing newsfeeds, via an analysis of information novelty and dynamics. Later that year, Gabrilovich also published, together with Prof. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shaul_Markovitch&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Shaul Markovitch (page does not exist)">Shaul Markovitch</a>, a series of articles suggesting a possible synergy between Wikipedia and <a href="/wiki/Artificial_Intelligence" class="mw-redirect" title="Artificial Intelligence">Artificial Intelligence</a>.
</p>
