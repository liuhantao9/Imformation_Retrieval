<p>This <b>list of academic ranks</b> identifies the hierarchical ranking structure found amongst <a href="/wiki/Scholars" class="mw-redirect" title="Scholars">scholars</a> and <a href="/wiki/Academic_personnel" title="Academic personnel">personnel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Academia" class="mw-redirect" title="Academia">academia</a>. The lists below refer specifically to <a href="/wiki/College" title="College">colleges</a> and <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">universities</a> throughout the world, although other institutions of <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">higher learning</a> may follow a similar schema.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bangladesh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Teachers are categorised in four main classes in Bangladesh at University level. The ascending rank of teacher is Lecturer, Assistant Professor, Associate Professor and Professor. The initial position Lecturer is generally enrolled as the top student of the class. Master's degree is required for normal university level Lecturer. After PhD, the appointment starts with Assistant Professor, then gradually Associate Professor and Professor depending on research/teaching experience. Beside these, professor of Emeritus is given to extraordinary professor after their retirement.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Egypt">Egypt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Egypt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Academic_ranks_in_Egypt" title="Academic ranks in Egypt">Academic ranks in Egypt</a></div>
<p>Teachers are categorized in five main classes in Egypt at University level. The ascending rank of teacher is Demonstrator, Assistant Lecturer, Lecturer, Associate Professor and Professor. The initial position Demonstrator is generally enrolled as the top student of the class. Master's degree is required for university level Assistant Lecturer. After PhD, the appointment starts with Lecturer, then gradually Associate Professor and Professor depending on research/teaching experience. Beside these, professor of Emeritus is given to extraordinary professor after their retirement.
</p>
<ul><li>OSTADH MOTAFAREGH (أستاذ متفرغ), equivalent to professor emeritus.</li>
<li>OSTADH (أستاذ) (the "DH" pronounced like the "TH" in the word "THE"), equivalent to professor (usually after minimum of five years serving as OSTATH MOSAED as well as publishing certain number of research papers)</li>
<li>OSTADH (the "DH" pronounced like the "TH" in the word "THE") MOSAED (أستاذ مساعد), equivalent to associate professor.</li>
<li>MODARRES (مدرس), Lecturer (must have a PhD degree)</li>
<li>MODARRES MOSA'ED (مدرس مساعد), Assistant Lecturer (must have a master's degree)</li>
<li>MOA'ED (معيد), or Teaching Assistant- also called demonstrator- (must have a bachelor's degree; usually graduated top of the class).</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Finland">Finland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Finland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Academic_ranks_in_Finland" title="Academic ranks in Finland">Academic ranks in Finland</a></div>
<p>In Finland, there's no "graduate" or "undergraduate" system; university students may be employed as a research assistant (<i>tutkimusapulainen</i>) before they graduate with a master's degree. A doctoral student must already hold a master's degree, and are typically employed by the university, or enjoy a similar grant, and will be called <i>tohtorikoulutettava</i> (doctoral candidate), <i>tutkija</i> (researcher) or <i>assistentti</i> (teaching assistant). Besides post-doctoral researchers (<i>tutkijatohtori</i>) and senior teaching assistants (<i>yliassistentti</i>), there are several mid-level non-professorial positions such as teaching researcher (<i>opettava tutkija</i>). Senior teachers and researchers may be employed as e.g. <i>erikoistutkija</i> ("special researcher") or <i>yliopistotutkija</i> (university researcher). Docent (<i>dosentti</i>) is a non-professiorial rank and title (for life) awarded to academics qualified as a principal investigator and for supervision of doctoral students; however, they do not belong to the professor corps and may be employed elsewhere. Professors (<i>professori</i>) are understood as research group leaders and managers, and traditionally there's no direct equivalent of an assistant / associate / full professor career path, although <a href="/wiki/Aalto_University" title="Aalto University">Aalto University</a> is introducing these. Qualifications for a professor's position are earned as a docent or in other mid-career positions. In management, professors serve as department heads (<i>laitosjohtaja</i> or <i>osastonjohtaja</i>) and deans (head of faculty, <i>dekaani</i>). The leader of a university is called a rector (<i>rehtori</i>), assisted by multiple vice-rectors (<i>vararehtori</i>). Vice-rectors may retain their professor's positions or work full-time as a vice-rector. Some universities have an even more senior officer called Chancellor (<i>kansleri</i>), which works more with outreach and public relations than daily management.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Iceland">Iceland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Iceland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Icelandic universities, especially <a href="/wiki/The_University_of_Iceland" class="mw-redirect" title="The University of Iceland">The University of Iceland</a>, <i>prófessor</i> is the most senior ranking teaching position. Below <i>prófessor</i> is <i>dósent</i>, then <i>lektor</i>. This three step hierarchy is akin to the US-scale, of full-, associate- and assistant-professors. Until the early 1990s no upward mobility was available in the Icelandic system. Most university teachers were hired as "prófessor." A "dósent" or a "lektor" wishing to ascend to a higher rank had to apply for a new position when it became available. Currently (since the 1990s) much more university teachers are hired as junior rank "lektor" and are promoted to "dósent" and "prófessor" if their work proves worthy of it.
</p>
<ul><li><i>Prófessor</i></li>
<li><i>Dósent</i> (associate-professors)</li>
<li><i>Lektor</i> (assistant-professors)</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Tenured positions (<i>confermato</i>):
</p>
<ul><li><i>Professore Ordinario</i>, or <i>Professore di I fascia</i>. A tenured, full professor position.</li>
<li><i>Professore Associato</i>, or <i>Professore di II fascia</i>. A tenured, associate professor position.</li>
<li><i>Ricercatore confermato</i>. A tenured position as researcher or assistant professor.</li>
<li><i>Professore Aggregato</i>. A 'ricercatore confermato' who is in charge of teaching university classes.</li></ul>
<p>Non-tenured positions (<i>non confermato</i>):
</p>

<li><i>Professore Associato non confermato</i>, also <i>Professore di II fascia</i>. A three-year associate professor appointment leading to tenure at associate level.</li>
<li><i>Ricercatore non confermato</i>. A three-year position at assistant professor/researcher/lecturer level.</li>
<li><i>Assegnista di Ricerca</i>. A research fellow (post-doc) with a fixed-term appointment.</li></ul>
<p>An honorary title as <i>Professore Emerito</i> also exists and is typically appointed to illustrious academics after retirement.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=50" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ranking system in most Mexican public universities is as follows
</p>
<ul><li>Profesor Asociado A (early assistant professor)</li>
<li>Profesor Asociado B (assistant professor)</li>
<li>Profesor Asociado C (early associate professor)</li>
<li>Profesor Titular A (associate professor)</li>
<li>Profesor Titular B (full professor)</li>
<li>Profesor Titular C (senior full professor)</li>
<li>Profesor Emerito (equivalent to distinguished professor, not to be confused with emeritus professor)</li></ul>
<p>Usually Mexican academics are also fellows of the <a href="/wiki/Sistema_Nacional_de_Investigadores" title="Sistema Nacional de Investigadores">Sistema Nacional de Investigadores</a> (SNI) that has four levels (candidate, I, II and III) that more or less correlate (but are not equivalent) to the Asociado and Titular A, B, and C professorships. The rank as professor is determined by the individual's institution while the SNI level is determined by an independent committee that evaluates the researchers nationwide.
</p><p>Many universities and research institutions make a distinction between "Profesores" -Professors- and "Investigadores" -Researchers- (Asociado A, B, C, Titular A, B, C), the main difference is that "Profesores" have a higher load of teaching, while "Investigadores" have a higher load of research, but normally all of them take part in both activities and are considered equivalent.
</p><p>Research rankings are awarded by the Sistema Nacional de Investigadores which depends on the national research council (Conacyt):
</p>
<ul><li>SNI emeritus researcher (not necessarily higher rank but lifetime, requires 15 continuous years as SNI III)</li>
<li>SNI III researcher (about 7% of the researchers in SNI)</li>
<li>SNI II researcher (about 16% of the researchers in SNI)</li>
<li>SNI I researcher (about 59% of researchers in SNI)</li>
<li>SNI candidate researcher (C) (about 18% of researchers in SNI)</li></ul>
<p>All titles, both professors and researchers, can be given in a:
</p>
<ul><li>Full-time, or</li>
<li>Part-time basis</li></ul>
<p>Administrative academic positions:
</p>
<ul><li>Rector</li>
<li>Director of Faculty or College</li>
<li>Coordinator</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pakistan">Pakistan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=56" title="Edit section: Pakistan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The hiring of academic positions in public universities throughout Pakistan is managed by the Higher Education Commission of Pakistan, the requirement for all positions vary with respect to the field of studies e.g. Engineering, IT, Medical, Law, and Arts and Design.
</p><p>There are four faculty ranks lecturer, assistant professor, associate professor, and professor.
</p><p>In engineering public universities, a lecturer requires an M.Sc. or B.Sc. degree and high academic standing in the field (e.g. gold medalist, among top 15 students of graduating class).
An assistant professor position requires a Ph.D. in relevant field with no experience.
An associate professorship can be conducted in the fourth year of employment, although, it is becoming more common for promotion and tenure to be awarded in the sixth year of employment. The review requires a certain number 5/8/10 research publications (with at least 1/2/4<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style#Technical_language" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style"><span title="The material near this tag may be using jargon that limits the article&#39;s accessibility. (August 2013)">jargon</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> publications in the last 5 years) by the calendar years 2007/2008/2012 respectively, in HEC/PEC recognized journals.
</p><p>A professor requires ten years post-PhD teaching/research experience in an HEC recognized university or a post-graduate institution or professional experience in the relevant field in a national or international organization. It requires a minimum of 8/12/15 research publications (with at least 2/3/5 publications in the last 5 years) by the calendar years 2007/2008/2012 respectively,  in HEC/PEC recognized journals
</p>
<ul><li>Professor Emeritus</li>
<li>Meritorious Professor/ Distinguished National Professor</li>
<li>Professor</li>
<li>Associate professor</li>
<li>Assistant professor</li>
<li>Lecturer</li>
<li>Lab Engineer</li>
<li>Research Associate</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Peru">Peru</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=57" title="Edit section: Peru">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After some recent reforms in the University Law (Minedu, 2014),<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> the Peruvian universities (publics and privates) consider these ranks:
</p><p><b>Extraordinary Professors</b>: Principally, this is a category of recognition for his contribution as professor or researcher when he retires or as a professional when he has built a great contribution to society throughout his career. To have 10% as maximum of these professors in any university (public or private) is mandatory.
</p>
<ul><li>Emeritus professor (Profesor Emérito) - For this recognition, the candidate should be faculty member (former ordinary professor in retiring process).</li>
<li>Honorary professor (Profesor Honorario) - For this recognition, the candidate is not related to a faculty member. For instance, in this category is the Doctor Honoris Causa.</li></ul>
<p><b>Research Professors</b>: Principally, full-time research position with few teaching responsibilities. Research professorships are almost always funded by grants or fellowships apart from the regular university budget. He is designated because of its academic excellence. He has a special bonus of fifty percent (50%) of its total payments. The competent authority of each university evaluates every two years, the production of the ordinary professors, for their permanence as a researcher.
</p><p><b>Ordinary Professors</b>: Often full-time (with exclusivity) professors with research competences. Principal faculty. To have 25% as minimum of these professors in any university (public or private) is mandatory.
</p>
<ul><li>Full professor (Profesor Principal) - PhD and MSc required, and former Associate professor or 15 years of experience (minimum) as researcher in the field to apply.</li>
<li>Associate professor (Profesor Asociado) - PhD (postgraduate level) and MSc required, and former Assistant professor or 10 years of experience (minimum) as researcher in the field to apply.</li>
<li>Assistant professor (Profesor Auxiliar) - PhD (postgraduate level) and MSc required, and 5 years of experience (minimum) as researcher in the field to apply.</li></ul>
<p><b>Lecturer (Hired Professors)</b>: Often part-time (with non-exclusivity) professors. Complementary faculty. Generally, the primary employer is not the university with which they have the status. Principally, this kind of professor come from practitioner market and not develop research activities. MSc required. 
</p><p>In addition, the universities developed prior ranks, as junior temporary rank, to get experience and training (with strong motivation to be ordinary professor):
</p>
<ul><li><b>Instructor (Instructor, Jefe de Práctica)</b> - MSc required in Postgraduate level and BSc required in Undergraduate level.</li>
<li><b>Teaching/Research Assistant (Asistente Académico/Investigador)</b> - MSc required in Postgraduate level and BSc required in Undergraduate level.</li></ul>
<p>The lectures are usually hired by private universities as they can offer this type of part-time contract. The instructors are usually hired by public universities as their type of contract has to be full-time. Even if the duties of these two ranks are similar and the terminology for both is the same in Spanish: "Jefe de prácticas", we should consider the differences in their type of contract.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singapore">Singapore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=66" title="Edit section: Singapore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Singapore universities adopt a hybrid of US and British academic ranks. Faculty members on the tenure-track are appointed for sustained excellence in both teaching and research. Assistant Professors are appointed on term contracts, while Associate Professors and Professors may be appointed either with tenure or on term contract. Faculty members on the Educator Track engage in high quality educational activities. They are oriented towards teaching excellence, student learning, and pedagogical research and innovation. Appointments in this Track include Teaching Assistants, Instructors, Lecturers, Senior Lecturers and Associate Professors (Educator Track). They are on term contracts which are usually of 1 – 3 years in the first instance. Research track faculty members focus on conducting fundamental research of high international quality. Appointments on this Track include Research Assistants, Research Fellows, Senior Research Fellows, Associate Professor (Research) and Professor (Research). They are on term contracts of 1 – 3 years in the first instance. Practice Track faculty members are scholar-practitioners with professional skills and expertise in industry to complement the teaching and research enterprise of the University. They also contribute significantly to the outreach activities of the School. Appointments are to Associate Professor (Practice) and Professor (Practice). They are on term contracts of 1 – 3 years in the first instance.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Tenure Track: Assistant Professor; Associate Professor; Professor</li>
<li>Educator Track: Teaching Assistant; Instructor; Lecturer; Senior Lecturer; Associate Professor (Educator Track)</li>
<li>Research Track: Research Assistant; Research Fellow; Senior Research Fellow; Associate Professor (Research); Professor (Research)</li>
<li>Practice Track: Associate Professor (Practice); Professor (Practice)</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Slovakia">Slovakia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=67" title="Edit section: Slovakia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Academic_ranks_in_Slovakia" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic ranks in Slovakia">Academic ranks in Slovakia</a></div>
<ul><li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Profesor</i> (<a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a>), both degree (written <i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">prof.</i> before name) and position. Professors are appointed by the president after a successful accomplishment of the process of awarding a professorship. One of the requirements is an already accomplished <i>docent</i> degree.</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Docent</i> (<a href="/wiki/Associate_professor" title="Associate professor">associate professor</a>), both degree (written <i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">doc.</i> before name) and position. The degree is awarded by the rector after a certain number of years of teaching and after a successful accomplishment of <i>habilitácia</i> (a process concluded by a defense of a reviewed research manuscript and a public lecture).</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Odborný asistent</i> (<a href="/wiki/Lecturer" title="Lecturer">lecturer</a> / <a href="/wiki/Researcher" class="mw-redirect" title="Researcher">researcher</a>), this title covers positions from lecturers to researchers. Requirements for lecturers / researchers vary, usually a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph.D.</a> / <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Theology" title="Doctor of Theology">Th.D.</a> is required</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Asistent</i> (assistant lecturer), at least <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">Master</a> degree is required</li></ul>
<p><b>Special</b>
</p>
<ul><li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Hosťujúci profesor</i> (hosting professor), significant expert with whom a dean with the approval of the scientific council of a faculty concluded employment at the position of professor</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Hosťujúci docent</i> (hosting associate professor), significant expert with whom a dean with the approval of the scientific council of a faculty concluded employment at the position of associate professor</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Mimoriadny profesor</i> (adjunct professor; sometimes also translated as associate professor, but it is not same position as <i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">docent</i>), associate professor temporarily appointed to the position of professor at a faculty while being an expectant to professor degree (written <i>mimoriadny profesor</i> after name)</li></ul>
<p><b>Administrative</b>
</p>
<ul><li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Rektor</i> (<a href="/wiki/Rector_(academia)" title="Rector (academia)">rector</a>), head of the university</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Prorektor</i> (<a href="/wiki/Vice-rector" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice-rector">vice-rector</a>), typically three to five people are vice-rectors; the position is further specified by indicating domain a vice-rector is responsible for (e. g. study-related issues, research, public relationships, international relationships, development, information technology)</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Kvestor</i> (<a href="/wiki/Registrar_(education)" title="Registrar (education)">registrar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bursar" title="Bursar">bursar</a>), senior professional financial administrator of the university</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Dekan</i> (<a href="/wiki/Dean_(education)" title="Dean (education)">dean</a>), head of a faculty</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Prodekan</i> (vice-dean); the position is further specified by indicating domain a vice-den is responsible for (e. g. admissions, study-related issues, research, public relationships, international relationships, information technology)</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Riaditeľ</i> (director), head of a sub-unit at the university or a faculty (institute, research center), e. g. <a href="/wiki/Slovak_University_of_Technology" class="mw-redirect" title="Slovak University of Technology">Institute of Life-Long Learning of the Slovak University of Technology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pavol_Jozef_%C5%A0af%C3%A1rik_University" title="Pavol Jozef Šafárik University">Institute of Physical Education and Sport at the Pavol Jozef Šafárik University</a></li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Vedúci katedry</i> (departmental chairman), head of a <i>katedra</i> (department) – sub-unit at a faculty</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Zástupca vedúceho katedry</i> (deputy departmental chairman), deputizes department chairman if necessary</li></ul>
<p><b>Honorary ranks</b>
</p>
<ul><li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Emeritný profesor</i> (<a href="/wiki/Professor_emeritus" class="mw-redirect" title="Professor emeritus">professor emeritus</a>), may be awarded by the rector on the proposal of the scientific council of the university for significant contributions in the field of science, art or education to professor older than 65 years who is no longer employment at the university, but is still active in research and teaching</li>
<li><i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Doctor honoris causa</i> (<a href="/wiki/Honorary_doctor" class="mw-redirect" title="Honorary doctor">honorary doctor for the sake of the honor</a>), honorary doctorate of the university or a faculty awarded for special merit (written <i lang="sk" title="Slovak language text">Dr.h.c.</i> before name)</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Slovenia">Slovenia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=68" title="Edit section: Slovenia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li><i>Prodekan</i> – vice-dean / head of faculty, academy or school at the university</li>
<li><i>Predstojnik oddelka</i> – head of department; several per faculty, academy or school at the university</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sri_Lanka">Sri Lanka</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=72" title="Edit section: Sri Lanka">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The appointment of professors follows the British system and is governed by the University Grants Commission regulations. A points-based system considers contributions to the research field, national development and institutional development. Several types of professorships exist:
</p>
<ul><li>Professor emeritus (on retirement at the age of 65)</li>
<li>Senior professor</li>
<li>Professor</li>
<li>Associate professor</li></ul>
<p>Professor positions are clearly separated from other junior faculty positions such as, in seniority order: senior lecturer (grade I) (usually PhD and 6+ years service), senior lecturer (grade II) (usually a PhD and 2+ years service), lecturer (usually with PhD), lecturer (probationary), assistant lecturer.
</p>
<ul><li>Professor emeritus (on retirement)</li>
<li>Senior professor</li>
<li>Professor</li>
<li>Associate professor</li>
<li>Senior lecturer</li>
<li>Lecturer</li>
<li>Temporary Assistant lecturer</li>
<li>Instructor/ demonstrator</li>
<li>Student instructor (undergrad)</li></ul>
<p><b>Administrative ranks</b>
</p>
<ul><li>Chancellor (titular)</li>
<li>Vice-chancellor/president</li>
<li>Registrar</li>
<li>Deans of faculties</li>
<li>Heads of departments/schools</li>
<li>Directors of Postgraduate Institutes</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Zimbabwe">Zimbabwe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_academic_ranks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=86" title="Edit section: Zimbabwe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The appointment and ranking of academics is governed by the Minister of Higher and Tertiary Education, Science and Technology Development. A points-based system considers an academic's contributions to research, teaching, and university and community service. The following structure of grades and notches for Academic Staff exist at universities in Zimbabwe:
</p>
<ul><li>Professor/Research Professor (Grade 3b)</li>
<li>Associate Professor/Associate Research Professor (Grade 4)</li>
<li>Senior Lecturer/Senior Research Fellow (Grade 5)</li>
<li>Lecturer/Research Fellow (Grade 6)</li>
<li>Teaching Assistant/Assistant Lecturer/Assistant Research Fellow (Grade 7)</li></ul>
<p><b>University Administrative ranks</b><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Chancellor (President of the Republic of Zimbabwe)<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Vice-chancellors</li>
<li>Registrars</li>
<li>Deans / Executive Deans of faculties</li>
<li>Heads / Chairpersons of departments/schools</li>
<li>Directors of Postgraduate Institutes</li></ul>
<p><b>Description of each Academic Rank</b>
</p><p>Grade 7	- Teaching Assistant/Assistant Lecturer/Assistant Research Fellow
Conditions: First Degree. Annual appointment for a maximum period of three years during which one must acquire a recognized master's degree.
</p><p>Grade 6	- Lecturer/Research Fellow (Master's Degree/PhD Degree)
Conditions: Lecturers may be tenured after three years subject to a minimum of 5 publications in refereed Journals (or assessed equivalent including patents, copyrights, trademarks). Research Fellows may be tenured after three years subject to  a minimum of 7 publications in refereed Journals (or assessed equivalent including patents, copyrights, trademarks).
</p><p>Grade 5	- Senior Lecturer/Senior Research Fellow 
Conditions: Senior Lecturer (may be equivalent to Assistant Professor rank in other countries - PhD/earned Doctorate with 6 - 10 publications in refereed journals (or assessed equivalent including patents, copyrights, trademarks). May be tenured on appointment. Senior Research Fellow - PhD/earned Doctorate with 8 - 12 publications in refereed journals (or assessed equivalent including patents, copyrights, trademarks). May be tenured on appointment.
</p><p>Grade 4	- Associate Professor/Associate Research Professor 
Conditions: Associate Professor - PhD/earned Doctorate with 21 but less than 35 publications in refereed journals (or assessed equivalent including patents, copyrights, trademarks). Tenured on appointment. Associate Research Professor - PhD Degree/earned Doctorate with 24 or more publications in refereed journals (or assessed equivalent including patents, copyrights, trademarks)	Associate Professor. Tenured on appointment.
</p><p>Grade 3b - Professor/Research Professor
Conditions: 
Professor - PhD/earned Doctorate with 35 or more Publications in refereed journals (or assessed equivalent including patents, copyrights, trademarks). Research Professor - PhD/earned Doctorate with 40 or more Publications in refereed journals (or assessed equivalent including patents, copyrights, trademarks).
</p>
