<p><b>Secession</b> (derived from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> term <i><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/secessio" class="extiw" title="wikt:secessio">secessio</a></i>) is the withdrawal of a group from a larger entity, especially a <a href="/wiki/Political_entity" class="mw-redirect" title="Political entity">political entity</a>, but also from any organization, union or military alliance. Threats of secession can be a strategy for achieving more limited goals.<sup id="cite_ref-Buchanan_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Buchanan-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is, therefore, a process, which commences once a group proclaims the act of secession (e.g. declaration of independence).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It could involve a violent or peaceful process but these do not change the nature of the outcome, which is the creation of a new state or entity independent from the group or territory it seceded from.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Secession_theory">Secession theory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Secession theory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is a great deal of theorizing about secession so that it is difficult to identify a consensus regarding its definition.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> There is also a claim that this subject has been neglected by <a href="/wiki/Political_philosophy" title="Political philosophy">political philosophers</a> and that by the 1980s - when it finally generated interest - the discourse concentrated on the moral justifications of the unilateral right to secession.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It was only in the early 1990s when American philosopher <a href="/wiki/Allen_Buchanan" title="Allen Buchanan">Allen Buchanan</a> offered the first systematic account of the subject and contributed to the <a href="/wiki/Normative" title="Normative">normative</a> classification of the literature on secession. In his 1991 book <i>Secession: The Morality of Political Divorce From Fort Sumter to Lithuania and Quebec</i>, Buchanan outlined limited rights to secession under certain circumstances, mostly related to oppression by people of other ethnic or racial groups, and especially those previously conquered by other people.<sup id="cite_ref-BuchananBook_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BuchananBook-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the 2007 book <i>Secession and Security</i> by George Mason political scientist Ahsan Butt, states respond violently to secessionist movements if the potential state would pose a greater threat than a violent secessionist movement would.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  States perceive future war as likely with a potentially new state if the ethnic group driving the secessionist struggle has deep identity division with the central state, and if the regional neighborhood is violent and unstable.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Justifications_for_secession">Justifications for secession</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Justifications for secession">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some theories of secession emphasize a general right of secession for any reason ("Choice Theory") while others emphasize that secession should be considered only to rectify grave injustices ("Just Cause Theory").<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Some theories do both. A list of justifications may be presented supporting the right to secede, as described by Allen Buchanan, Robert McGee, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Birch" title="Anthony Birch">Anthony Birch</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jane_Jacobs" title="Jane Jacobs">Jane Jacobs</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Frances Kendall and Leon Louw,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Leopold_Kohr" title="Leopold Kohr">Leopold Kohr</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kirkpatrick_Sale" title="Kirkpatrick Sale">Kirkpatrick Sale</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and various authors in David Gordon's "Secession, State and Liberty", includes:
</p>
<ul><li>United States <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/James_Buchanan" title="James Buchanan">James Buchanan</a>, Fourth Annual Message to Congress on the State of the Union December 3, 1860: "The fact is that our Union rests upon public opinion, and can never be cemented by the blood of its citizens shed in civil war. If it can not live in the affections of the people, it must one day perish. Congress possesses many means of preserving it by conciliation, but the sword was not placed in their hand to preserve it by force."</li>
<li>Former <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson" title="Thomas Jefferson">Thomas Jefferson</a>, in a letter to <a href="/wiki/William_H._Crawford" title="William H. Crawford">William H. Crawford</a>, <a href="/wiki/US_Secretary_of_War" class="mw-redirect" title="US Secretary of War">Secretary of War</a> under President <a href="/wiki/James_Madison" title="James Madison">James Madison</a>, on June 20, 1816: "In your letter to Fisk, you have fairly stated the alternatives between which we are to choose&#160;: 1, licentious commerce and gambling speculations for a few, with eternal war for the many; or, 2, restricted commerce, peace, and steady occupations for all. If any State in the Union will declare that it prefers separation with the first alternative, to a continuance in union without it, I have no hesitation in saying, 'let us separate'. I would rather the States should withdraw, which are for unlimited commerce and war, and confederate with those alone which are for peace and agriculture."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Economic enfranchisement of an economically oppressed class that is regionally concentrated within the scope of a larger national territory.</li>
<li>The right to <a href="/wiki/Liberty" title="Liberty">liberty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_association" title="Freedom of association">freedom of association</a> and <a href="/wiki/Private_property" title="Private property">private property</a></li>
<li>Consent as important democratic principle; will of majority to secede should be recognized</li>
<li>Making it easier for states to join with others in an experimental union</li>
<li>Dissolving such union when goals for which it was constituted are not achieved</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Self-defense" title="Self-defense">Self-defense</a> when larger group presents lethal threat to minority or the government cannot adequately defend an area</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Self-determination" title="Self-determination">Self-determination</a> of peoples</li>
<li>Preserving culture, language, etc. from assimilation or destruction by a larger or more powerful group</li>
<li>Furthering diversity by allowing diverse cultures to keep their <a href="/wiki/Identity_politics" title="Identity politics">identity</a></li>
<li>Rectifying past injustices, especially past conquest by a larger power</li>
<li>Escaping "discriminatory redistribution", i.e., tax schemes, regulatory policies, economic programs, etc. that distribute resources away to another area, especially in an undemocratic fashion</li>
<li>Enhanced efficiency when the state or empire becomes too large to administer efficiently</li>
<li>Preserving "liberal purity" (or "conservative purity") by allowing less (or more) liberal regions to secede</li>
<li>Providing superior constitutional systems which allow flexibility of secession</li>
<li>Keeping political entities small and <a href="/wiki/Human_scale" title="Human scale">human scale</a> through right to secession</li></ul>
<p>Aleksander Pavkovic,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> associate professor at the Department of Politics and International Studies at <a href="/wiki/Macquarie_University" title="Macquarie University">Macquarie University</a> in Australia and the author of several books on secession describes five justifications for a general right of secession within liberal political theory:<sup id="cite_ref-Pavkovic-Questions_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pavkovic-Questions-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Anarcho-Capitalism: individual liberty to form political associations and private property rights together justify right to secede and to create a "viable political order" with like-minded individuals.</li>
<li>Democratic Secessionism: the right of secession, as a variant of the right of self-determination, is vested in a "territorial community" which wishes to secede from "their existing political community"; the group wishing to secede then proceeds to delimit "its" territory by the majority.</li>
<li>Communitarian Secessionism: any group with a particular "participation-enhancing" identity, concentrated in a particular territory, which desires to improve its members' political participation has a <a href="/wiki/Prima_facie" title="Prima facie">prima facie</a> right to secede.</li>
<li>Cultural Secessionism: any group which was previously in a minority has a right to protect and develop its own culture and distinct national identity through seceding into an independent state.</li>
<li>The Secessionism of Threatened Cultures: if a minority culture is threatened within a state that has a majority culture, the minority needs a right to form a state of its own which would protect its culture.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_secession">Types of secession</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Types of secession">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hashim_Thaci_Joe_Biden_Fatmir_Sejdiu_with_Declaration_of_Independence_of_Kosovo.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Hashim_Thaci_Joe_Biden_Fatmir_Sejdiu_with_Declaration_of_Independence_of_Kosovo.JPG/220px-Hashim_Thaci_Joe_Biden_Fatmir_Sejdiu_with_Declaration_of_Independence_of_Kosovo.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Hashim_Thaci_Joe_Biden_Fatmir_Sejdiu_with_Declaration_of_Independence_of_Kosovo.JPG/330px-Hashim_Thaci_Joe_Biden_Fatmir_Sejdiu_with_Declaration_of_Independence_of_Kosovo.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Hashim_Thaci_Joe_Biden_Fatmir_Sejdiu_with_Declaration_of_Independence_of_Kosovo.JPG/440px-Hashim_Thaci_Joe_Biden_Fatmir_Sejdiu_with_Declaration_of_Independence_of_Kosovo.JPG 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="458" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hashim_Thaci_Joe_Biden_Fatmir_Sejdiu_with_Declaration_of_Independence_of_Kosovo.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>U.S. Vice President <a href="/wiki/Joe_Biden" title="Joe Biden">Joe Biden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hashim_Tha%C3%A7i" title="Hashim Thaçi">Hashim Thaçi</a> with Declaration of Independence of <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a></div></div></div>
<p>Secession theorists have described a number of ways in which a political entity (city, county, canton, state) can secede from the larger or original state:<sup id="cite_ref-Buchanan_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Buchanan-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pavkovic-Questions_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pavkovic-Questions-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Secession from <a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">federation</a> or <a href="/wiki/Confederation" title="Confederation">confederation</a> (political entities with substantial reserved powers which have agreed to join together) versus secession from a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_state" title="Unitary state">unitary state</a> (a state governed as a single unit with few powers reserved to sub-units)</li>
<li>Colonial wars of independence from an imperial state</li>
<li>National (seceding entirely from the national state) versus local (seceding from one entity of the national state into another entity of the same state)</li>
<li>Central or <a href="/wiki/Enclave_and_exclave" title="Enclave and exclave">enclave</a> (seceding entity is completely surrounded by the original state) versus peripheral (along a border of the original state)</li>
<li>Secession by contiguous units versus secession by non-contiguous units (<a href="/wiki/Enclave_and_exclave" title="Enclave and exclave">exclaves</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Separatism" title="Separatism">Separation</a> or <a href="/wiki/Partition_(politics)" title="Partition (politics)">partition</a> (although an entity secedes, the rest of the state retains its structure) versus dissolution (all political entities dissolve their ties and create several new states)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Irredentism" title="Irredentism">Irredentism</a> where secession is sought in order to annex the territory to another state because of common ethnicity or prior historical links</li>
<li>Minority (a minority of the population or territory secedes) versus majority (a majority of the population or territory secedes)</li>
<li>Secession of better off regions versus secession of worse off regions</li>
<li>The threat of secession is sometimes used as a strategy to gain greater autonomy within the original state</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arguments_against_secession">Arguments against secession</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Arguments against secession">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Allen Buchanan, who supports secession under limited circumstances, lists arguments that might be used against secession:<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>"Protecting Legitimate Expectations" of those who now occupy territory claimed by secessionists, even in cases where that land was stolen</li>
<li>"Self Defense" if losing part of the state would make it difficult to defend the rest of it</li>
<li>"Protecting Majority Rule" and the principle that minorities must abide by them</li>
<li>"Minimization of Strategic Bargaining" by making it difficult to secede, such as by imposing an exit tax</li>
<li>"Soft Paternalism" because secession will be bad for secessionists or others</li>
<li>"Threat of Anarchy" because smaller and smaller entities may choose to secede until there is chaos, although this is not the true meaning of the political and philosophical concept.</li>
<li>"Preventing Wrongful Taking" such as the state's previous investment in infrastructure</li>
<li>"Distributive Justice" arguments that wealthier areas cannot secede from poorer ones</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Explanations_for_the_20th_century_explosion_in_secessionism">Explanations for the 20th century explosion in secessionism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Explanations for the 20th century explosion in secessionism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to University of California, Santa Barbara, political scientist Bridget L. Coggins, there are four potential explanations in the academic literature for the drastic increase in state birth during the 20th century:<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Ethnonational mobilization - Ethnic minorities have been increasingly mobilized to pursue states of their own.</li>
<li>Institutional empowerment - The growing inability of empires and ethnic federations to maintain colonies and member states.</li>
<li>Relative strength - Increasingly powerful secessionist movements are more likely to achieve statehood.</li>
<li>Negotiated consent - Home states and the international community increasingly consent to secessionist demands.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Secession_movements">Secession movements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Secession movements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
 <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/List_of_active_autonomist_and_secessionist_movements" class="mw-redirect" title="List of active autonomist and secessionist movements">List of active autonomist and secessionist movements</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_historical_autonomist_and_secessionist_movements" class="mw-redirect" title="List of historical autonomist and secessionist movements">List of historical autonomist and secessionist movements</a></div>
<dl><dd><i><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref">See also: the categories <a href="/wiki/Category:Secession_by_country" title="Category:Secession by country">Secession by country</a> and <a href="/wiki/Category:Secessionist_organizations" title="Category:Secessionist organizations">Secessionist organizations</a>.</div></i></dd></dl>
<p>Movements that work towards political secession may describe themselves as being <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_entity" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous entity">autonomy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Separatist" class="mw-redirect" title="Separatist">separatist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Self-determination" title="Self-determination">self-determination</a>, <a href="/wiki/Partition_(politics)" title="Partition (politics)">partition</a>, <a href="/wiki/Devolution" title="Devolution">devolution</a>, <a href="/wiki/Decentralization" title="Decentralization">decentralization</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">sovereignty</a>, <a href="/wiki/Self-governance" title="Self-governance">self-governance</a> or <a href="/wiki/Decolonization" title="Decolonization">decolonization</a> movements instead of, or in addition to, being secession movements.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Proposals_for_new_Australian_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Proposals for new Australian States">Proposals for new Australian States</a></div>
<p>During the 19th century, the single <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland" title="United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland">British</a> <a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a> in eastern mainland Australia, <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> (NSW) was progressively divided up by the British government as new settlements were formed and spread. <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a> (Vic) in 1851 and Queensland (Qld) in 1859.
</p><p>However, settlers agitated to divide the colonies throughout the later part of the century; particularly in <a href="/wiki/Central_Queensland" title="Central Queensland">central Queensland</a> (centred in <a href="/wiki/Rockhampton,_Queensland" class="mw-redirect" title="Rockhampton, Queensland">Rockhampton</a>) in the 1860s and 1890s, and in <a href="/wiki/North_Queensland" title="North Queensland">North Queensland</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Bowen,_Queensland" title="Bowen, Queensland">Bowen</a> as a potential colonial capital) in the 1870s. Other secession (or territorial separation) movements arose and these advocated the secession of New England in northern central New South Wales, <a href="/wiki/Deniliquin" title="Deniliquin">Deniliquin</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Riverina" title="Riverina">Riverina</a> district also in NSW, and <a href="/wiki/Mount_Gambier,_South_Australia" title="Mount Gambier, South Australia">Mount Gambier</a> in the eastern part of <a href="/wiki/South_Australia" title="South Australia">South Australia</a>.
</p>
<dl><dt>Western Australia</dt></dl>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Secessionism_in_Western_Australia" title="Secessionism in Western Australia">Secessionism in Western Australia</a></div>
<p>Secession movements have surfaced several times in Western Australia (WA), where a <a href="/wiki/1933_Western_Australian_secession_referendum" title="1933 Western Australian secession referendum">1933 referendum</a> for secession from the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Australia" title="Federation of Australia">Federation of Australia</a> passed with a two-thirds majority. The referendum had to be ratified by the <a href="/wiki/British_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="British Parliament">British Parliament</a>, which declined to act, on the grounds that it would contravene the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian Constitution">Australian Constitution</a>.
</p>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Principality_of_Hutt_River" title="Principality of Hutt River">Principality of Hutt River</a> claims to have seceded from Australia in 1970, although its status is not recognised by Australia or any other country. According to a lexicon on nationalist movements across the world, Macau happened to recognise that Principality.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Austria">Austria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Austria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After being liberated by the <a href="/wiki/Red_Army" title="Red Army">Red Army</a> and the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Army">U.S. Army</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> seceded from <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> on April 27, 1945. This took place after seven years of Austria's being part of Adolf Hitler's <a href="/wiki/Third_Reich" class="mw-redirect" title="Third Reich">Third Reich</a> due to the <a href="/wiki/Anschluss" title="Anschluss">Anschluss</a> annexation of Austria into Nazi Germany in March 1938, and could not have taken place without the Third Reich being defeated by the Allies.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Belgium_and_the_Netherlands">Belgium and the Netherlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Belgium and the Netherlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Revolution" title="Belgian Revolution">Belgian Revolution</a></div>
<p>On August 25, 1830, during the reign of <a href="/wiki/William_I_of_the_Netherlands" title="William I of the Netherlands">William I</a>, the nationalistic opera <a href="/wiki/La_muette_de_Portici" title="La muette de Portici">La muette de Portici</a> was performed in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>. Soon after, the Belgian Revolt occurred, which resulted in the Belgian secession from the Netherlands.
</p>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Partition_of_Belgium" title="Partition of Belgium">Partition of Belgium</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brazil">Brazil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Brazil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1825, soon after the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Brazil" title="Empire of Brazil">Empire of Brazil</a> managed to defeat the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Cortes" title="Portuguese Cortes">Cortes-Gerais</a> and Portugal in an <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_War_of_Independence" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazilian War of Independence">Independence War</a>, the platinean nationalists in <a href="/wiki/Cisplatina" title="Cisplatina">Cisplatina</a> declared independence and joined the <a href="/wiki/United_Provinces_of_Rio_de_la_Plata" class="mw-redirect" title="United Provinces of Rio de la Plata">United Provinces</a>, which led to a stagnated war between both, as they were both weakened, without manpower and fragile politically. The peace treaty accepted Uruguay's independence, reasserted the rule of both nations over their land and some important points like free navigation in the Silver River.
</p><p>Three rather disorganized secessionist rebellions happened in <a href="/wiki/Gr%C3%A3o-Par%C3%A1_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Grão-Pará Province">Grão-Pará</a>, Bahia and Maranhão, where the people were unhappy with the Empire (these provinces were Portuguese bastions in the Independence War). The <a href="/wiki/Mal%C3%AA_Revolt" class="mw-redirect" title="Malê Revolt">Malê Revolt</a>, in Bahia, was an Islamic slave revolt. These three rebellions were bloodily crushed by the Empire of Brazil.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/History_of_Pernambuco" title="History of Pernambuco">Pernambuco</a> was one of the most <a href="/wiki/Nativism_(politics)" title="Nativism (politics)">nativist</a> of all Brazilian regions, which in five revolts (1645–1654, 1710, 1817, 1824, 1848), the province <a href="/wiki/Pernambucan_Revolt" class="mw-redirect" title="Pernambucan Revolt">ousted the Dutch West India Company</a>, tried to secede from the Portuguese Empire and from the Brazilian Empire. In the attempts the rebels were crushed, the leaders shot and its territory divided, nevertheless they kept revolting until its territory was a little fraction of what it was before.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Ragamuffin_War" title="Ragamuffin War">Ragamuffin War</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Sul#History" title="Rio Grande do Sul">Province of Rio Grande do Sul</a> was undergoing a (at that time common) liberal vs conservative "cold" war. After the Emperor favoured the conservatives, the liberals took the Capital and declared an <a href="/wiki/Riograndense_Republic" title="Riograndense Republic">independent Republic</a>, fighting their way to the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Catarina_(state)#History" title="Santa Catarina (state)">Province of Santa Catarina</a>, declaring the <a href="/wiki/Juliana_Republic" title="Juliana Republic">Juliana Republic</a>. Eventually they were slowly forced back, and made a reunification peace with the Empire. The war was not a secessionist war, even if it could become if the Empire were defeated, after the Empire agreed to aid its economy by taxing Argentina's products (like dry meat), the rebels reunited with the Empire and even filled its ranks, as the rebels were very good fighters.
</p><p>In modern times, the <a href="/wiki/South_Region,_Brazil" title="South Region, Brazil">South Region of Brazil</a> has been the centre of a secessionist movement led by an organization called <a href="/wiki/The_South_is_My_Country" title="The South is My Country">The South is My Country</a> since the 1990s. Reasons cited for South Region Brazil's secession are taxation due to it being one of the wealthiest regions in the country and political disputes with the northernmost states of Brazil as well as <a href="/wiki/2015-16_protests_in_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="2015-16 protests in Brazil">the recent scandal</a> revolving around the <a href="/wiki/Workers_Party_(Brazil)" class="mw-redirect" title="Workers Party (Brazil)">Workers Party</a> found to be making shady deals with state-owned oil company <a href="/wiki/Petrobras" title="Petrobras">Petrobras</a> and the impeachment of then-<a href="/wiki/President_of_Brazil" title="President of Brazil">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Dilma_Rouseff" class="mw-redirect" title="Dilma Rouseff">Dilma Rouseff</a> additionally there is also an ethnic divide as the South Region is predominately <a href="/wiki/White_Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="White Brazilian">European</a> populated primarily by <a href="/wiki/German_Brazilians" title="German Brazilians">Germans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_Brazilians" title="Italian Brazilians">Italians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Brazilians" title="Portuguese Brazilians">Portuguese</a> and other European countries in  contrast to the rest of Brazil which is a multicultural melting pot "<a href="/wiki/Racial_Democracy" class="mw-redirect" title="Racial Democracy">Racial Democracy</a>". The South Region in 2016 voted in an unofficial referendum called "Plebisul" in which 616,917 (or half a million) voters overwhelming supported secession and the creation of an independent South Region by 95%. Another Brazilian secession movement is based in the state of <a href="/wiki/Sao_Paulo_(state)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sao Paulo (state)">Sao Paulo</a> which seeks to create to make the state an independent country from the rest of Brazil.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cameroon">Cameroon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Cameroon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Anglophone_Crisis" title="Anglophone Crisis">Anglophone Crisis</a></div>
<p>In October 2017, <a href="/wiki/Ambazonia" title="Ambazonia">Ambazonia</a> declared its independence from Cameroon. Less than a month beforehand, tensions had escalated into <a href="/wiki/Anglophone_Crisis" title="Anglophone Crisis">open warfare</a> between separatists and the Cameroonian military.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Secessionist_movements_of_Canada" title="Secessionist movements of Canada">Secessionist movements of Canada</a></div>
<p>Throughout Canada's history, there has been tension between English-speaking and French-speaking Canadians. Under the <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_Act_of_1791" class="mw-redirect" title="Constitutional Act of 1791">Constitutional Act of 1791</a>, the Quebec colony (including parts of what are today Quebec, Ontario and Newfoundland and Labrador) was divided in two: <a href="/wiki/Lower_Canada" title="Lower Canada">Lower Canada</a> (which retained French law and institutions and is now part of the provinces of <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">Newfoundland and Labrador</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Upper_Canada" title="Upper Canada">Upper Canada</a> (a new colony intended to accommodate the many new English-speaking settlers, including the <a href="/wiki/United_Empire_Loyalists" class="mw-redirect" title="United Empire Loyalists">United Empire Loyalists</a>, and now part of <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>). The intent was to provide each group with its own colony. In 1841, the two Canadas were merged into the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Canada" title="Province of Canada">Province of Canada</a>. The union proved contentious, however, resulting in a legislative deadlock between English and French legislators. The difficulties of the union led (amongst other factors) in 1867 to <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Confederation" title="Canadian Confederation">Confederation</a>, the adoption of a <a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">federal</a> system that united the Province of Canada, <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">New Brunswick</a> (later joined by other British colonies in North America). The federal framework did not eliminate all tensions, however, leading to the <a href="/wiki/Quebec_sovereignty_movement" title="Quebec sovereignty movement">Quebec sovereignty movement</a> in the latter half of the 20th century.
</p><p>Other occasional secessionist movements have included anti-Confederation movements in 19th century <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">Atlantic Canada</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Anti-Confederation_Party" title="Anti-Confederation Party">Anti-Confederation Party</a>), the <a href="/wiki/North-West_Rebellion" title="North-West Rebellion">North-West Rebellion</a> of 1885, and various small separatism movements in <a href="/wiki/Alberta" title="Alberta">Alberta</a> particularly (see <a href="/wiki/Alberta_separatism" title="Alberta separatism">Alberta separatism</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Western_Canada" title="Western Canada">Western Canada</a> generally (see, for example, <a href="/wiki/Western_Canada_Concept" title="Western Canada Concept">Western Canada Concept</a>).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Central_America">Central America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Central America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the 1823 collapse of the <a href="/wiki/First_Mexican_Empire" title="First Mexican Empire">First Mexican Empire</a>, the former Captaincy-General of Guatemala was organized into a new <a href="/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Central_America" title="Federal Republic of Central America">Federal Republic of Central America</a>. In 1838 <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">Nicaragua</a> seceded. The Federal Republic was formally dissolved in 1840, all but one of the states having seceded amidst general disorder.
</p>

<ul><li>The <i><a href="/wiki/Anti-Secession_Law" title="Anti-Secession Law">Anti-Secession Law</a></i> against  the <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_independence_movement" title="Taiwan independence movement">Taiwan independence movement</a></li>
<li>Western regions of <a href="/wiki/Xinjiang" title="Xinjiang">Xinjiang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">Tibet</a> are the focus of secessionist calls by the <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_independence_movement" title="Tibetan independence movement">Tibetan independence movement</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkistan_Islamic_Party" title="Turkistan Islamic Party">East Turkestan Islamic movement</a>.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Congo">Congo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Congo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1960 the <a href="/wiki/State_of_Katanga" title="State of Katanga">State of Katanga</a> declared independence from the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a>. <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> troops crushed it in <a href="/wiki/Congo_Crisis#UN_Operation_Grand_Slam_ends_Katanga_secession" title="Congo Crisis">Operation Grand Slam</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cyprus">Cyprus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Cyprus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1974, the Turkish Army invaded northern <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> to protect the interests of the ethnic Turkish minority, who in the following year formed the <i>Turkish Federated State of Cyprus</i> and in 1983 declared independence as the Republic of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Cyprus" title="Northern Cyprus">Northern Cyprus</a>, recognized only by Turkey.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Timor">East Timor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: East Timor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:East_Timor_Demo.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/East_Timor_Demo.jpg/220px-East_Timor_Demo.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/East_Timor_Demo.jpg/330px-East_Timor_Demo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/East_Timor_Demo.jpg/440px-East_Timor_Demo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1800" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:East_Timor_Demo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>September 1999 demonstration for <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_occupation_of_East_Timor" title="Indonesian occupation of East Timor">independence from Indonesia</a></div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/East_Timor" title="East Timor">Democratic Republic of Timor-Leste</a> (also known as East Timor) has been described as having "seceded" from <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> After <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> sovereignty was terminated in 1975, East Timor was occupied by Indonesia. However the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> and the <a href="/wiki/International_Court_of_Justice" title="International Court of Justice">International Court of Justice</a> refused to recognize this incorporation. Therefore, the resulting civil war and eventual 2002 East Timorese vote for complete separation are better described as an <a href="/wiki/Independence" title="Independence">independence</a> movement.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Ethiopia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the 1993 victory of opposition forces against the communist <a href="/wiki/Derg" title="Derg">Derg</a> regime during the <a href="/wiki/Ethiopian_Civil_War" title="Ethiopian Civil War">Ethiopian Civil War</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a> (formerly known as "Bahri Negash" before being renamed to "Eritrea" by Italian colonizers from 1890–1941) seceded in a United Nations referendum with the blessing of the newly formed Ethiopian government.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="European_Union">European Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: European Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Withdrawal_from_the_European_Union" title="Withdrawal from the European Union">Withdrawal from the European Union</a></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_European_Union_membership_referendum,_2016" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016">United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016</a></div>
<p>Before the Treaty of Lisbon entered into force on 1 December 2009 no provision in the treaties or law of the European Union outlined the ability of a state to voluntarily withdraw from the EU. The European Constitution did propose such a provision and, after the failure to ratify the Treaty establishing a Constitution for Europe, that provision was then included in the Lisbon Treaty.
</p><p>The treaty introduces an exit clause for members who wish to withdraw from the Union. This formalises the procedure by stating that a member state may notify the European Council that it wishes to withdraw, upon which withdrawal negotiations begin; if no other agreement is reached the treaty ceases to apply to the withdrawing state two years after such notification.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Finland">Finland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Finland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Finland successfully and peacefully seceded from the newly formed and weak <a href="/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic" title="Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic">Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic</a> in 1917, the latter led by Lenin who had goodwill towards the Finns due to their having helped in his revolutionary struggle. Unsuccessful attempts at greater autonomy or peaceful secession had already been made during the preceding <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> but had been denied by the Russian emperor.
</p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Alsace_independence_movement" title="Alsace independence movement">Alsace independence movement</a></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gran_Colombia">Gran Colombia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Gran Colombia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AGHRC_(1890)_-_Carta_XI_-_Divisi%C3%B3n_pol%C3%ADtica_de_Colombia,_1824.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/AGHRC_%281890%29_-_Carta_XI_-_Divisi%C3%B3n_pol%C3%ADtica_de_Colombia%2C_1824.jpg/220px-AGHRC_%281890%29_-_Carta_XI_-_Divisi%C3%B3n_pol%C3%ADtica_de_Colombia%2C_1824.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="177" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/AGHRC_%281890%29_-_Carta_XI_-_Divisi%C3%B3n_pol%C3%ADtica_de_Colombia%2C_1824.jpg/330px-AGHRC_%281890%29_-_Carta_XI_-_Divisi%C3%B3n_pol%C3%ADtica_de_Colombia%2C_1824.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/AGHRC_%281890%29_-_Carta_XI_-_Divisi%C3%B3n_pol%C3%ADtica_de_Colombia%2C_1824.jpg/440px-AGHRC_%281890%29_-_Carta_XI_-_Divisi%C3%B3n_pol%C3%ADtica_de_Colombia%2C_1824.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4293" data-file-height="3452" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AGHRC_(1890)_-_Carta_XI_-_Divisi%C3%B3n_pol%C3%ADtica_de_Colombia,_1824.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map showing the shrinking territory of <a href="/wiki/Gran_Colombia" title="Gran Colombia">Gran Colombia</a> from 1824 to 1890 (red line). <a href="/wiki/Separation_of_Panama_from_Colombia" title="Separation of Panama from Colombia">Panama separated from Colombia</a> in 1903.</div></div></div>
<p>After a decade of tumultuous federalism, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a> and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> seceded from <a href="/wiki/Gran_Colombia" title="Gran Colombia">Gran Colombia</a> in 1830, leaving the similarly tumultuous <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_Colombia" title="United States of Colombia">United States of Colombia</a>, now the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Colombia" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Colombia">Republic of Colombia</a> which also lost <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> in 1903.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Separatist_movements_of_India" title="Separatist movements of India">Separatist movements of India</a></div>
<p>Pakistan seceded from the British Indian empire in what is known as <a href="/wiki/Partition_of_India" title="Partition of India">the Partition</a>.
Today, the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_India" title="Constitution of India">Constitution of India</a> does not allow Indian states to secede from the Union. The territory of Indian-administered Kashmir has had a violent Muslim nationalist movement against Indian establishment mostly in the Valley of Kashmir since 1989, which continues and is supported by Pakistan. The militancy reached at its peak influence in the decade of 1990. However, it has been fading since start of 21st century. Other secessionist movements in <a href="/wiki/Nagaland" title="Nagaland">Nagaland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assam" title="Assam">Assam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manipur" title="Manipur">Manipur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indian_Punjab" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian Punjab">Punjab</a> (known as the <a href="/wiki/Khalistan_movement" title="Khalistan movement">Khalistan movement</a>), <a href="/wiki/Mizoram" title="Mizoram">Mizoram</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tripura" title="Tripura">Tripura</a> were also formerly active, while <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu" title="Tamil Nadu">Tamil Nadu</a> had a <a href="/wiki/Dravida_Nadu" title="Dravida Nadu">non-violent movement</a> in the 1960s.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> While a violent Maoist <a href="/wiki/Naxalite" title="Naxalite">Naxalite</a> insurgency continues to rage across a wide-swath of eastern rural India, the movement is not considered a secessionist movement because the goal of the Maoists is to overthrow the government of India, although rebel commanders have occasionally called for a Communist republic to be carved out of swaths of India.
The Pakistani Armed organizations is a participant in the Kashmir conflict and strives to establish the merger state of Jammu and Kashmir from secular India to Muslim Pakistan.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Movement_for_the_Independence_of_Sicily" title="Movement for the Independence of Sicily">Movement for the Independence of Sicily</a> (Movimento Indipendentista Siciliano, MIS) has its roots in the <a href="/wiki/Sicilian_Independence_Movement" class="mw-redirect" title="Sicilian Independence Movement">Sicilian Independence Movement</a> of the late 1940s; they have been around for 60 years. Today, the MIS no longer exists, though many other parties have been born. One is <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nation_Sicily&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nation Sicily (page does not exist)">Nation Sicily</a> (Sicilia Nazione), which still believes in the idea that Sicily, due to its deeply personal and ancient history, has to be a sovereign country. Moreover, a common ideology shared by all the Sicilian independentist movements is to fight against <a href="/wiki/Cosa_Nostra" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosa Nostra">Cosa Nostra</a> and all the other Mafia organizations, that have a very deep influence over Sicily's public and private institutions. Also, the Sicilian branch of the <a href="/wiki/Five_Star_Movement" title="Five Star Movement">Five Star Movement</a>, which is according to the polls Sicily's most popular party, has publicly expressed the intention to start working for a possible secession from Italy in the case where the central government would not collaborate in shifting the nation's administrative organization from a unitary country to a <a href="/wiki/Federal_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal state">federal state</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lega_Nord" title="Lega Nord">Lega Nord</a> has been seeking the independence of the so-called region of <a href="/wiki/Padania" title="Padania">Padania</a>, which includes lands along the <a href="/wiki/Po_Valley" title="Po Valley">Po Valley</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. Some organizations separately work for the <a href="/wiki/Venetian_nationalism" title="Venetian nationalism">independence of Venetia</a> or Veneto and the <a href="/wiki/South_Tyrolean_secessionist_movement" class="mw-redirect" title="South Tyrolean secessionist movement">secession or reunification</a> of <a href="/wiki/South_Tyrol" title="South Tyrol">South Tyrol</a> with <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. Lega Nord governing <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a> has expressed a will to turning the region into a sovereign country. Also the island of <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a> is home to a notable <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_nationalism" title="Sardinian nationalism">nationalist movement</a>. In Southern Italy several movements have expressed a will to secede from Italy. This newborn ideology is so-called neo-Bourbonic, because the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Two_Sicilies" title="Kingdom of the Two Sicilies">Kingdom of the Two Sicilies</a> was under the control of the House of Bourbon. The Kingdom of the Two Sicilies was created in 1816 after the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna" title="Congress of Vienna">Congress of Vienna</a>, and it comprised both Sicily and continental Southern Italy. The Kingdom came to an end in 1861, being annexed to the newborn Kingdom of Italy. However, the patriotic feelings shared among the southern Italian population is more ancient, starting in 1130 with the Kingdom of Sicily, which was composed by both the island and south Italy. According to the neo-Bourbonic movements the Italian regions which should secede are <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calabria" title="Calabria">Calabria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basilicata" title="Basilicata">Basilicata</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apulia" title="Apulia">Apulia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Molise" title="Molise">Molise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Campania" title="Campania">Campania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abruzzo" title="Abruzzo">Abruzzo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Latio" class="mw-redirect" title="Latio">Latio</a>'s provinces of <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Rieti" title="Province of Rieti">Rieti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Latina" title="Province of Latina">Latina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Frosinone" title="Province of Frosinone">Frosinone</a>. The major movements and parties which believe in this ideology are <a href="/w/index.php?title=Unione_Mediterranea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Unione Mediterranea (page does not exist)">Unione Mediterranea</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mo!&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mo! (page does not exist)">Mo!</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brigandage_in_Southern_Italy_after_1861" title="Brigandage in Southern Italy after 1861">Briganti</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Iran">Iran</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Iran">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Active secession movements include: <a href="/wiki/Iranian_Azeri" class="mw-redirect" title="Iranian Azeri">Iranian Azeri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_independence_movement" title="Assyrian independence movement">Assyrian independence movement</a>, Bakhtiary <a href="/wiki/Lurs" title="Lurs">lurs</a> movement in 1876, <a href="/wiki/Iranian_Kurdistan" title="Iranian Kurdistan">Iranian Kurdistan</a>; <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Democratic_Party_of_Iran" class="mw-redirect" title="Kurdistan Democratic Party of Iran">Kurdistan Democratic Party of Iran (KDPI)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kh%C5%ABzest%C4%81n_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Khūzestān Province">Khūzestān Province</a> <a href="/wiki/Balochistan" title="Balochistan">Balochistan</a> independence movement for free separated Balochistan, (<i><a href="/wiki/Arab_nationalism" title="Arab nationalism">Arab nationalist</a></i>); Al-Ahwaz Arab People's Democratic Popular Front, Democratic Solidarity Party of Al-Ahwaz (See <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Kh%C5%ABzest%C4%81n_Province#Arab_politics_and_separatism" class="mw-redirect" title="Politics of Khūzestān Province">Politics of Khūzestān Province: Arab politics and separatism</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Balochistan_People%27s_Party" title="Balochistan People&#39;s Party">Balochistan People's Party</a> (BPP) supporting <a href="/wiki/Baloch_people" title="Baloch people">Baloch</a> <a href="/wiki/Baloch_nationalism" title="Baloch nationalism">Separatism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japan">Japan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Japan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ryukyu_independence_movement" title="Ryukyu independence movement">Ryukyu independence movement</a></div>
<p>The Ryukyuan (Okinawan) people had their own state historically (<a href="/wiki/Ryukyu_Kingdom" title="Ryukyu Kingdom">Ryukyu Kingdom</a>) and have sought to become independent from Japan since they were annexed by Japan in 1879, and especially after 1972 when the islands were transferred from U.S. rule to Japan.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Malaysia">Malaysia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Malaysia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When racial and <a href="/wiki/PAP-UMNO_relations" class="mw-redirect" title="PAP-UMNO relations">partisan strife</a> erupted, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> <a href="/wiki/Singapore_in_Malaysia#Expulsion" title="Singapore in Malaysia">was expelled from the Malaysian federation</a> in 1965. Agitation for secession has since been sporadic on the culturally distinct large island of <a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a> in the states of <a href="/wiki/Sabah" title="Sabah">Sabah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sarawak" title="Sarawak">Sarawak</a> although these sentiments have been gaining momentum and support in the past few years following the proliferation of social medias and failure of the central government to fulfill conditions of the Malaysia Agreement 1963.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mexico%27s_Territorial_Evolution.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Mexico%27s_Territorial_Evolution.png/220px-Mexico%27s_Territorial_Evolution.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="200" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Mexico%27s_Territorial_Evolution.png/330px-Mexico%27s_Territorial_Evolution.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Mexico%27s_Territorial_Evolution.png/440px-Mexico%27s_Territorial_Evolution.png 2x" data-file-width="1669" data-file-height="1518" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mexico%27s_Territorial_Evolution.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Territorial_evolution_of_Mexico" title="Territorial evolution of Mexico">Territorial evolution of Mexico</a> after independence, noting losses to the US (red, white and orange) and the secession of <a href="/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Central_America" title="Federal Republic of Central America">Central America</a> (purple)</div></div></div>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Texas" title="Republic of Texas">Texas</a> seceded from Mexico in 1836 (see <a href="/wiki/Texas_Revolution" title="Texas Revolution">Texas Revolution</a>), after animosity between the Mexican government and the American settlers of the <a href="/wiki/Coahuila_y_Tejas" title="Coahuila y Tejas">Coahuila y Tejas State</a>. It was later annexed by the United States in 1845.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Rio_Grande" title="Republic of the Rio Grande">Republic of the Rio Grande</a> seceded from Mexico on January 17, 1840. It rejoined Mexico on November 6 the same year.</li>
<li>After the <a href="/wiki/Federal_government" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal government">federal system</a> was abandoned by <a href="/wiki/President_of_Mexico" title="President of Mexico">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Antonio_L%C3%B3pez_de_Santa_Anna" title="Antonio López de Santa Anna">Santa Anna</a>, the Congress of Yucatán approved in 1840 a declaration of independence, establishing the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Republic of Yucatán">Republic of Yucatán</a>. The Republic rejoined Mexico in 1843.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/South_Island_Independence" class="mw-redirect" title="South Island Independence">South Island Independence</a></div>
<p>Secession movements have surfaced several times in the <a href="/wiki/South_Island" title="South Island">South Island</a> of New Zealand. A <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_New_Zealand" title="Prime Minister of New Zealand">Premier of New Zealand</a>, Sir <a href="/wiki/Julius_Vogel" title="Julius Vogel">Julius Vogel</a>, was amongst the first people to make this call, which was voted on by the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_New_Zealand" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliament of New Zealand">Parliament of New Zealand</a> as early as 1865. The desire for South Island independence was one of the main factors in moving the capital of New Zealand from <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a> to <a href="/wiki/Wellington" title="Wellington">Wellington</a> in the same year.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/NZ_South_Island_Party" title="NZ South Island Party">NZ South Island Party</a> with a pro-South agenda, fielded only five candidates (4.2% of electoral seats) candidates in the <a href="/wiki/1999_New_Zealand_general_election" title="1999 New Zealand general election">1999 General Election</a> but achieved only 0.14% (2622 votes) of the general vote. The reality today is that although "South Islanders" are most proud of their geographic region, secession does not carry any real constituency; the party was not able to field any candidates in the 2008 election due to being unable to enlist 500 paying members, a requirement by the New Zealand Electoral commission. The party is treated more as a "joke" party than any real political force.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nigeria">Nigeria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Nigeria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Starved_girl.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Starved_girl.jpg/170px-Starved_girl.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="258" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Starved_girl.jpg/255px-Starved_girl.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Starved_girl.jpg/340px-Starved_girl.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1177" data-file-height="1788" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Starved_girl.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A girl during the <a href="/wiki/Nigerian_Civil_War" title="Nigerian Civil War">Nigerian Civil War</a> of the late 1960s. <p>Between 1967 and 1970, the state of Biafra (The <a href="/wiki/Biafra" title="Biafra">Republic of Biafra</a>) seceded from Nigeria and fought a war that ended with the state returning to Nigeria.
Later in 1999 at the beginning of a new democratic regime, other secessionist movements emerged, the movement for the Actualization of a Sovereign state of Biafra was formed as a military wing of the Republic of Biafra.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Norway_and_Sweden">Norway and Sweden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Norway and Sweden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_union_between_Norway_and_Sweden_in_1905" class="mw-redirect" title="Dissolution of the union between Norway and Sweden in 1905">Dissolution of the union between Norway and Sweden in 1905</a></div>
<p>Sweden, having left the <a href="/wiki/Kalmar_Union" title="Kalmar Union">Kalmar Union</a> with Denmark and Norway in the 16th century, entered into a loose <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a> with Norway in 1814. Following a <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_crisis" title="Constitutional crisis">constitutional crisis</a>, on June 7, 1905 the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Norwegian Parliament">Norwegian Parliament</a> declared that King <a href="/wiki/Oscar_II_of_Norway" class="mw-redirect" title="Oscar II of Norway">Oscar II</a> had failed to fulfill his constitutional duties. He was therefore no longer King of Norway and because the union depended on the two countries sharing a king, it was thus dissolved. After negotiations Sweden agreed to this on October 26 and on April 14.
</p>

<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Awami_League" title="Awami League">Awami League</a> won the 1970 national elections, negotiations to form a new government floundered, resulting in the <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Liberation_War" title="Bangladesh Liberation War">Bangladesh Liberation War</a> by which the eastern wing of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> seceded, to become <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>. The Balochistan Liberation Army (also Baloch Liberation Army or Boluchistan Liberation army) (BLA) is a <a href="/wiki/Baloch_nationalist" class="mw-redirect" title="Baloch nationalist">Baloch nationalist</a> militant secessionist organization. The stated goals of the organization include the establishment of an independent state of <a href="/wiki/Balochistan_(region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Balochistan (region)">Balochistan</a> free of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistani</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iranian</a> and Afghan Federations . The name Baloch Liberation Army first became public in summer 2000, after the organization claimed credit for a series of bomb attacks in markets and removal of railways lines.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Somalia">Somalia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Somalia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Somaliland" title="Somaliland">Somaliland</a> is an autonomous region,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> which is part of the <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Federal Republic of Somalia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Those who call the area the <i>Republic of Somaliland</i> consider it to be the successor state of the former <a href="/wiki/British_Somaliland" title="British Somaliland">British Somaliland</a> <a href="/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">protectorate</a>. Having established its own local government in Somalia in 1991, the region's self-declared independence remains <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_recognition" title="Diplomatic recognition">unrecognized</a> by any country or international organization.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Soviet_Union">Soviet Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: Soviet Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cold_War_border_changes.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Cold_War_border_changes.png/250px-Cold_War_border_changes.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="182" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Cold_War_border_changes.png/375px-Cold_War_border_changes.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Cold_War_border_changes.png/500px-Cold_War_border_changes.png 2x" data-file-width="599" data-file-height="436" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cold_War_border_changes.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/List_of_national_border_changes_since_World_War_I" title="List of national border changes since World War I">Changes in national boundaries</a> in post-Soviet states</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Constitution of the Soviet Union">Constitution of the Soviet Union</a> guaranteed all <a href="/wiki/Republics_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Republics of the Soviet Union">SSRs</a> the right to secede from the Union. In practice however, the central government wouldn't allow an SSR to secede. In 1990, after free elections, the Lithuanian SSR <a href="/wiki/Act_of_the_Re-Establishment_of_the_State_of_Lithuania" title="Act of the Re-Establishment of the State of Lithuania">declared independence</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Republics_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Republics of the Soviet Union">SSRs</a> soon followed. Despite the Soviet central-government's refusal to recognize the independence of the republics, the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">Soviet Union dissolved</a> in 1991.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa">South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1910, following the British Empire's defeat of the Afrikaners in the <a href="/wiki/Boer_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Boer War">Boer Wars</a>, four self-governing colonies in the south of Africa were merged into the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_South_Africa" title="Union of South Africa">Union of South Africa</a>. The four regions were the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Colony" title="Cape Colony">Cape Colony</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orange_Free_State" title="Orange Free State">Orange Free State</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colony_of_Natal" title="Colony of Natal">Natal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Transvaal_Colony" title="Transvaal Colony">Transvaal</a>. Three other territories, <a href="/wiki/High_Commissioner_for_Southern_Africa" title="High Commissioner for Southern Africa">High Commission Territories</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bechuanaland" class="mw-redirect" title="Bechuanaland">Bechuanaland</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a>), <a href="/wiki/Basutoland" title="Basutoland">Basutoland</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Lesotho" title="Lesotho">Lesotho</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Swaziland" class="mw-redirect" title="Swaziland">Swaziland</a> later became independent states in the 1960s. Following the election of the <a href="/wiki/National_Party_(South_Africa)" title="National Party (South Africa)">Nationalist</a> government in 1948, some <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English-speaking</a> whites in Natal advocated either secession or a loose federation.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> There were also calls for secession, with Natal and the eastern part of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Province" title="Cape Province">Cape Province</a> breaking away.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> following the <a href="/wiki/1960_South_African_republic_referendum" title="1960 South African republic referendum">referendum</a> in 1960 on establishing a <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>, and in 1993, prior to South Africa's first elections under universal suffrage and the end of apartheid, some <a href="/wiki/Zulu_people" title="Zulu people">Zulu</a> leaders in <a href="/wiki/KwaZulu-Natal" title="KwaZulu-Natal">KwaZulu-Natal</a><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> considered secession as did some politicians in the Cape Province.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, a political movement calling for the return to independence of the Cape resurged in the shape of the political organisation, the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Party" title="Cape Party">Cape Party</a>. The Cape Party contested their first elections on 22 April 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Sudan">South Sudan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=39" title="Edit section: South Sudan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>A referendum took place in Southern Sudan from 9 to 15 January 2011, on whether the region should remain a part of Sudan or become independent. The referendum was one of the consequences of the 2005 Naivasha Agreement between the Khartoum central government and the Sudan People's Liberation Army/Movement (SPLA/M).
</p><p>A simultaneous referendum was supposed to be held in Abyei on whether to become part of Southern Sudan but it has been postponed due to conflict over demarcation and residency rights.
</p><p>On 7 February 2011, the referendum commission published the final results, with 98.83% voting in favour of independence. While the ballots were suspended in 10 of the 79 counties for exceeding 100% of the voter turnout, the number of votes were still well over the requirement of 60% turnout, and the majority vote for secession is not in question.
</p><p>The predetermined date for the creation of an independent state was 9 July 2011.
</p>

</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ukraine">Ukraine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=41" title="Edit section: Ukraine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2014-05-11._%D0%A0%D0%B5%D1%84%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%B4%D1%83%D0%BC_%D0%B2_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD%D0%B5%D1%86%D0%BA%D0%B5_014.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/2014-05-11._%D0%A0%D0%B5%D1%84%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%B4%D1%83%D0%BC_%D0%B2_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD%D0%B5%D1%86%D0%BA%D0%B5_014.jpg/220px-2014-05-11._%D0%A0%D0%B5%D1%84%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%B4%D1%83%D0%BC_%D0%B2_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD%D0%B5%D1%86%D0%BA%D0%B5_014.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/2014-05-11._%D0%A0%D0%B5%D1%84%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%B4%D1%83%D0%BC_%D0%B2_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD%D0%B5%D1%86%D0%BA%D0%B5_014.jpg/330px-2014-05-11._%D0%A0%D0%B5%D1%84%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%B4%D1%83%D0%BC_%D0%B2_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD%D0%B5%D1%86%D0%BA%D0%B5_014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/2014-05-11._%D0%A0%D0%B5%D1%84%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%B4%D1%83%D0%BC_%D0%B2_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD%D0%B5%D1%86%D0%BA%D0%B5_014.jpg/440px-2014-05-11._%D0%A0%D0%B5%D1%84%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%B4%D1%83%D0%BC_%D0%B2_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD%D0%B5%D1%86%D0%BA%D0%B5_014.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4928" data-file-height="3264" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2014-05-11._%D0%A0%D0%B5%D1%84%D0%B5%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%B4%D1%83%D0%BC_%D0%B2_%D0%94%D0%BE%D0%BD%D0%B5%D1%86%D0%BA%D0%B5_014.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/2014_Donetsk_and_Luhansk_status_referendums" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 Donetsk and Luhansk status referendums">Donetsk status referendum</a> organized by pro-Russian separatists. A line to enter a polling place, 11 May 2014</div></div></div>
<p>In the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Ukrainian_Revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 Ukrainian Revolution">Ukrainian Revolution</a> of 2014, several regions of Ukraine declared independence:
</p>
<ul><li>In March 2014, the governments of the <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_Republic_of_Crimea" title="Autonomous Republic of Crimea">Autonomous Republic of Crimea</a> and the city of <a href="/wiki/Sevastopol" title="Sevastopol">Sevastopol</a> declared independence from Ukraine and asked to join the Russian Federation.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Donetsk_People%27s_Republic" title="Donetsk People&#39;s Republic">Donetsk People's Republic</a> was declared to be independent from Ukraine on 7 April 2014, comprising the territory of the <a href="/wiki/Donetsk_Oblast" title="Donetsk Oblast">Donetsk Oblast</a>. There have been military confrontations between the Ukrainian Army and the forces of the Donetsk People's Republic when the Ukrainian Government attempted to reassert control over the oblast.</li>
<li>The Lugansk Parliamentary Republic was proclaimed on 27 April 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-itarfedlu_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itarfedlu-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> before being succeeded by the <a href="/wiki/Lugansk_People%27s_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Lugansk People&#39;s Republic">Lugansk People's Republic</a>. The Lugansk forces have successfully occupied vital buildings in <a href="/wiki/Lugansk" class="mw-redirect" title="Lugansk">Lugansk</a> since 8 April, and controlled the City Council, prosecutor's office, and police station since 27 April.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLPRRSA_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLPRRSA-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The Government of the Lugansk Oblast announced its support for a referendum, and granted the governorship to independence leader <a href="/wiki/Valeriy_Bolotov" class="mw-redirect" title="Valeriy Bolotov">Valeriy Bolotov</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kyivpost_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kyivpost-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=42" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>What is now the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a> is the only territory that has withdrawn from the United Kingdom proper. Ireland <a href="/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_Irish_Republic" title="Proclamation of the Irish Republic">declared independence in 1916</a> and, as the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Free_State" title="Irish Free State">Irish Free State</a>, gained independence in 1922. Currently the United Kingdom has a number of secession movements:
</p>
<ul><li>In <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Irish_republicanism" title="Irish republicanism">Irish Republicans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Irish_nationalism" title="Irish nationalism">Nationalists</a> in general have long called for the secession of Northern Ireland from the United Kingdom in order to join the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>. This is opposed by <a href="/wiki/Unionism_in_Ireland" title="Unionism in Ireland">Unionists</a>.<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Easter_1916.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Easter_1916.jpg/200px-Easter_1916.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="300" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Easter_1916.jpg/300px-Easter_1916.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Easter_1916.jpg/400px-Easter_1916.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1273" data-file-height="1909" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Easter_1916.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A mural in <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a> depicting the <a href="/wiki/Easter_Rising" title="Easter Rising">Easter Rising</a> of 1916</div></div></div></li>
<li>In Scotland the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_National_Party" title="Scottish National Party">Scottish National Party</a> (SNP) campaigns for <a href="/wiki/Scottish_independence" title="Scottish independence">Scottish independence</a> and direct Scottish membership in the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>. It has representation at all levels of <a href="/wiki/Politics_in_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Politics in Scotland">Scottish politics</a> and forms the devolved <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish Government</a>. A number of nascent pro-independence parties have enjoyed only limited electoral success. The <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Green_Party" title="Scottish Green Party">Scottish Green Party</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Socialist_Party" title="Scottish Socialist Party">Scottish Socialist Party</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Enterprise_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish Enterprise Party">Scottish Enterprise Party</a> are most widely publicised. However all independence movements/parties are opposed by <a href="/wiki/Unionism_in_Scotland" title="Unionism in Scotland">Unionists</a>. A <a href="/wiki/2014_Scottish_independence_referendum" title="2014 Scottish independence referendum">referendum on independence</a>, in which voters were asked "Should Scotland be an independent country?", took place in September 2014, and resulted in a victory for the "no" campaign as 55.3 percent of voters voted against independence.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In Wales, <a href="/wiki/Plaid_Cymru" title="Plaid Cymru">Plaid Cymru</a> <i>(Party of Wales)</i> stands for <a href="/wiki/Welsh_independence" title="Welsh independence">Welsh independence</a> within the European Union. It is also represented at all levels of <a href="/wiki/Welsh_politics" class="mw-redirect" title="Welsh politics">Welsh politics</a> and is the second largest party in the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="National Assembly of Wales">National Assembly of Wales</a>.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, supporters of <a href="/wiki/Mebyon_Kernow" title="Mebyon Kernow">Mebyon Kernow</a> call for the creation of a <a href="/wiki/Cornish_Assembly" title="Cornish Assembly">Cornish Assembly</a> and separation from England, giving the county significant <a href="/wiki/Cornish_self-government_movement" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornish self-government movement">self-government</a>, whilst remaining within the United Kingdom as a fifth <a href="/wiki/Home_nation" class="mw-redirect" title="Home nation">home nation</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Acting_Witan_of_Mercia" title="Acting Witan of Mercia">Acting Witan of Mercia</a> calls for Mercia to be recognized as distinct from England. Other "<a href="/wiki/Regionalism_(politics)" title="Regionalism (politics)">regionalists</a>" call for similar recognition of other areas of Britain to be restored from <a href="/wiki/Lapsed_power" title="Lapsed power">lapsed states</a>.</li>
<li>In England the now-disbanded <a href="/wiki/Free_England_Party" title="Free England Party">Free England Party</a> (FEP) campaigned for <a href="/wiki/English_independence" title="English independence">English independence</a>.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=43" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Secession_in_the_United_States" title="Secession in the United States">Secession in the United States</a></div>
<p>Discussions and threats of secession often surface in American politics, and secession was declared during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>. However, in 1869 the United States Supreme Court ruled in <i><a href="/wiki/Texas_v._White" title="Texas v. White">Texas v. White</a></i>, 74 U.S. 700 (1869) that unilateral secession was not permitted saying that the union between a state (Texas in the case before the bar) "was as complete, as perpetual, and as indissoluble as the union between the original States. There was no place for reconsideration or revocation, except through revolution or through consent of the States."<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Yemen">Yemen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=44" title="Edit section: Yemen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/North_Yemen" title="North Yemen">North Yemen</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Yemen" title="South Yemen">South Yemen</a> merged in 1990; tensions led to a 1994 southern secession which was crushed in a <a href="/wiki/1994_civil_war_in_Yemen" class="mw-redirect" title="1994 civil war in Yemen">civil war</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Yugoslavia">Yugoslavia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Secession&amp;action=edit&amp;section=45" title="Edit section: Yugoslavia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:A_destroyed_T-34-85_tank_in_Karlovac,_Croatia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/A_destroyed_T-34-85_tank_in_Karlovac%2C_Croatia.jpg/220px-A_destroyed_T-34-85_tank_in_Karlovac%2C_Croatia.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/A_destroyed_T-34-85_tank_in_Karlovac%2C_Croatia.jpg/330px-A_destroyed_T-34-85_tank_in_Karlovac%2C_Croatia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/A_destroyed_T-34-85_tank_in_Karlovac%2C_Croatia.jpg/440px-A_destroyed_T-34-85_tank_in_Karlovac%2C_Croatia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2778" data-file-height="1857" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:A_destroyed_T-34-85_tank_in_Karlovac,_Croatia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A destroyed <a href="/wiki/T-34-85" class="mw-redirect" title="T-34-85">T-34-85</a> tank in Karlovac, <a href="/wiki/Croatian_War_of_Independence" title="Croatian War of Independence">Croatian War of Independence</a>, 1992</div></div></div>
<p>On June 25, 1991, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a> seceded from the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia" title="Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia">Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia</a>. <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Macedonia" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Macedonia">Macedonia</a> also declared independence, after which the federation broke up, causing the separation of the remaining two countries <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a>. Several <a href="/wiki/Yugoslav_Wars" title="Yugoslav Wars">wars</a> ensued between FR Yugoslavia and seceding entities and among other ethnic groups in <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>, and later, <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a>. <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a> peacefully separated from its union with Serbia in 2006.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a> declared de facto independence on February 17, 2008, and was recognized by several dozen countries, but officially remains under <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> administration.
</p>
