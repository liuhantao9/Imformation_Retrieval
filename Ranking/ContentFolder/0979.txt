	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Computer scientist</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Computational_scientist" title="Computational scientist">Computational scientist</a>.</div>
<p>

A <b>computer scientist</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Person" title="Person">person</a> who has acquired the knowledge of <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, the study of the theoretical foundations of information and computation and their application.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Computer scientists typically work on the theoretical side of computer systems, as opposed to the <a href="/wiki/Computer_hardware" title="Computer hardware">hardware</a> side that <a href="/wiki/Computer_engineer" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer engineer">computer engineers</a> mainly focus on (although there is overlap). Although computer scientists can also focus their work and research on specific areas (such as <a href="/wiki/Algorithm" title="Algorithm">algorithm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Data_structure" title="Data structure">data structure</a> development and design, <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Information_theory" title="Information theory">information theory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Database_theory" title="Database theory">database theory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computational_complexity_theory" title="Computational complexity theory">computational complexity theory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Numerical_analysis" title="Numerical analysis">numerical analysis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Programming_language_theory" title="Programming language theory">programming language theory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_graphics" title="Computer graphics">computer graphics</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Computer_vision" title="Computer vision">computer vision</a>), their foundation is the theoretical study of computing from which these other fields derive.<sup id="cite_ref-salary_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-salary-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A primary goal of computer scientists is to develop or validate models, often mathematical in nature, to describe the properties of computer-based systems (<a href="/wiki/Processor_(computing)" title="Processor (computing)">processors</a>, programs, computers interacting with people, computers interacting with other computers, etc.) with an overall objective of discovering designs that yield useful benefits (faster, smaller, cheaper, more precise, etc.).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_scientist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most computer scientists are required to possess a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph.D.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">M.S.</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">B.S.</a> in computer science, or other similar fields like <a href="/wiki/Information_and_Computer_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Information and Computer Science">Information and Computer Science</a> (CIS), or a closely related discipline such as <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a><sup id="cite_ref-salary_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-salary-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Areas_of_specialization">Areas of specialization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_scientist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Areas of specialization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Theoretical_computer_science" title="Theoretical computer science">Theoretical computer science</a> – including data structures and algorithms, <a href="/wiki/Theory_of_computation" title="Theory of computation">theory of computation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Information_theory" title="Information theory">information theory</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coding_theory" title="Coding theory">coding theory</a>, <a href="/wiki/Programming_language_theory" title="Programming language theory">programming language theory</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Formal_methods" title="Formal methods">formal methods</a></li>
<li>Computer systems – including <a href="/wiki/Computer_architecture" title="Computer architecture">computer architecture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer_engineering" title="Computer engineering">computer engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_performance" title="Computer performance">computer performance</a> analysis, <a href="/wiki/Concurrency_(computer_science)" title="Concurrency (computer science)">concurrency</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Distributed_computing" title="Distributed computing">distributed computing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_network" title="Computer network">computer networks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_security" title="Computer security">computer security</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cryptography" title="Cryptography">cryptography</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">databases</a>.</li>
<li>Computer applications – including <a href="/wiki/Computer_graphics_(computer_science)" title="Computer graphics (computer science)">computer graphics</a> and visualization, <a href="/wiki/Human%E2%80%93computer_interaction" title="Human–computer interaction">human–computer interaction</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scientific_computing" class="mw-redirect" title="Scientific computing">scientific computing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">artificial intelligence</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">Software engineering</a> - the application of engineering to software development in a systematic method</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Employment">Employment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Computer_scientist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Employment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Computer scientists are often hired by software publishing firms, scientific research and development organizations where they develop the theories that allow new technologies to be developed. Computer scientists are also employed by educational institutions such as <a href="/wiki/Universities" class="mw-redirect" title="Universities">universities</a>.
</p><p>Computer scientists can follow more practical applications of their knowledge, doing things such as software engineering. They can also be found in the field of <a href="/wiki/Information_technology_consulting" title="Information technology consulting">information technology consulting</a>, and may be seen as a type of <a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematician</a>, given how much of the field depends on mathematics.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Computer scientists employed in industry may eventually advance into managerial or project leadership positions.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Employment prospects for computer scientists are said to be excellent. Such prospects seem to be attributed, in part, to very rapid growth in computer systems design and related services industry, and the software publishing industry, which are projected to be among the fastest growing industries in the U.S. economy.<sup id="cite_ref-salary_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-salary-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
