<p><b>Meltham</b> is a town and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Metropolitan Borough of Kirklees</a>, in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. It lies in the <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley" title="Holme Valley">Holme Valley</a>, below Wessenden Moor, four and a half miles south-west of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> on the edge of the <a href="/wiki/Peak_District" title="Peak District">Peak District National Park</a>. It had a population of 8,089 at the 2001 census,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> which was estimated to have increased to 8,600 by 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The population assessed at the 2011 Census was 8,534.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meltham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The valley has been inhabited since pre-historic times and there are two Iron Age sites overlooking the town. Meltham also includes the small village of <a href="/wiki/Helme,_West_Yorkshire" title="Helme, West Yorkshire">Helme</a> which has its own school and church. Meltham is situated within close reach of several major cities. <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a> is 14 miles to the north, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> is 19 miles north-east, <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> is 19 miles to the south-west and <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> is 21 miles to the south-east.
</p><p>Surrounding villages within the Kirklees area include <a href="/wiki/Crosland_Moor" title="Crosland Moor">Crosland Moor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golcar" title="Golcar">Golcar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honley" title="Honley">Honley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth" title="Holmfirth">Holmfirth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Linthwaite" title="Linthwaite">Linthwaite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherton,_Huddersfield" class="mw-redirect" title="Netherton, Huddersfield">Netherton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slaithwaite" title="Slaithwaite">Slaithwaite</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meltham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Meltham itself contains three <a href="/wiki/Primary_schools" class="mw-redirect" title="Primary schools">primary schools</a>, namely Meltham Church of England (C of E) School, Meltham Moor Primary School and Helme (C of E) junior and infant school. <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">Secondary schools</a> serving Meltham primarily include <a href="/wiki/Honley_High_School" title="Honley High School">Honley High School</a>, Holmfirth High School and Colne Valley High School, all of which are located in neighbouring areas.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meltham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Meltham has active teams in a variety of sports, including <a href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Football (soccer)">football</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a>. The football team, Meltham Athletic FC, currently play in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_County_Amateur_Football_League" title="West Riding County Amateur Football League">West Riding County Amateur Football League</a> Premier Division. Notable honours include the West Riding Challenge Cup in 2005, the Huddersfield and District League on two occasions and the Barlow Cup 4 times (including three in a row 2003-05). The cricket side, Meltham CC, has won the 1st XI Byrom Shield on 7 occasions and the Sykes Cup 8 times.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The 1st XI and 2nd XI currently play in the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Cricket_League" title="Huddersfield Cricket League">Drakes Premierships</a> 1 and 2 respectively. Notable ex-players include England international cricketer <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Sidebottom" title="Ryan Sidebottom">Ryan Sidebottom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dilip_Doshi" title="Dilip Doshi">Dilip Doshi</a> (father of <a href="/wiki/Nayan_Doshi" title="Nayan Doshi">Nayan</a>), <a href="/wiki/Madan_Lal" title="Madan Lal">Madan Lal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shahid_Mahmood" title="Shahid Mahmood">Shahid Mahmood</a>. The rugby league side, Meltham All Blacks ARLFC, currently play in the <a href="/wiki/Pennine_League" title="Pennine League">Pennine League</a> Division 4.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arts">Arts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meltham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Arts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meltham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Meltham is home to the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.melthamband.co.uk/">Meltham and Meltham Mills Band</a>, which was established in 1846 as a brass band. They became the first band to win the British Open Title for three consecutive years, a feat only matched by 5 others.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meltham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Meltham has been used as part of the location for several television projects. The third episode in 1995's <i><a href="/wiki/Coogan%27s_Run" title="Coogan&#39;s Run">Coogan's Run</a></i> was set in and around Meltham Parish Church Hall. In addition scenes of long-running <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> <a href="/wiki/Sitcom" title="Sitcom">sitcom</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Last_of_the_Summer_Wine" title="Last of the Summer Wine">Last of the Summer Wine</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">drama</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Where_the_Heart_Is_(1997_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Where the Heart Is (1997 TV series)">Where the Heart Is</a></i> used some Meltham houses as character's homes. The scout hut has also been used as well as streets for shooting outdoor scenes.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_history">Industrial history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meltham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Industrial history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Brook.28e.29_family"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Brook(e)_family">Brook(e) family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meltham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Brook(e) family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Meltham Mills was the former site of Jonas Brook and Brothers, a <a href="/wiki/Silk" title="Silk">silk</a> mill complex that employed over 1,000 workers during the late 19th century. The Brook family originally came from New House Hall in <a href="/wiki/Sheepridge,_Huddersfield" title="Sheepridge, Huddersfield">Sheepridge</a>, moving to Thickhollins towards the end of the 18th century. William Brook married Martha Smith at Bradford Parish Church - the daughter of a prominent <a href="/wiki/Mirfield" title="Mirfield">Mirfield</a> banker. Their sons Jonas, James and Joseph established their business in Meltham Mills, using a goat's head - the <a href="/wiki/Crest_(heraldry)" title="Crest (heraldry)">crest</a> from the Brook's <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">coat of arms</a> - as their brand. The goat's head can still be seen on the old office building to the mill complex and their arms are emblazoned in St. James' Church, in Meltham Mills (which the family built) - a hawkes lure: motto <i>"en dieu ma foy"</i> (in God my trust). Meltham Mills Band also carry the Brook family coat of arms as their official logo. Edward Brook died in 1904 at <a href="/wiki/Hoddom_Castle" title="Hoddom Castle">Hoddom Castle</a>, the house near <a href="/wiki/Ecclefechan" title="Ecclefechan">Ecclefechan</a>, south-west Scotland, which he had purchased in 1878.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The Brook family were <a href="/wiki/Philanthropist" class="mw-redirect" title="Philanthropist">philanthropists</a> and built housing in Meltham Mills for their employees, including the convalescent home.  They also built Meltham Hall, Helme Church and owned an estate at <a href="/wiki/Enderby,_Leicestershire" title="Enderby, Leicestershire">Enderby</a> in <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire" title="Leicestershire">Leicestershire</a>. William Brook is buried with his wife Martha in Meltham Church, but his descendants are buried in the crypt underneath St. James Church, Meltham Mills.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jonas Brook and Brothers became United Threads in 1890 and Sir <a href="/wiki/Hildred_Carlile" title="Hildred Carlile">Hildred Carlile</a> of Ponsonby Hall, Hertfordshire was a Director of the business. United Threads was closed in 1939 - the business was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Paisley,_Renfrewshire" title="Paisley, Renfrewshire">Paisley</a> as part of J &amp; P Coats - now <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://archive.is/20120719062445/http://www.archiveshub.ac.uk/news/02120302.html">Coats Viyella</a>. The factory site was taken over by David Brown Tractors.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/John_Charles_Brooke" title="John Charles Brooke">John Charles Brooke</a>, who originated from the Silkstone branch of the Newhouse Hall family was <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a> <a href="/wiki/Herald" title="Herald">Herald</a> during the 18th century. He was crushed to death at the <a href="/wiki/Haymarket_Theatre" class="mw-redirect" title="Haymarket Theatre">Haymarket Theatre</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> following the crowd's clamour to see <a href="/wiki/King_George_III" class="mw-redirect" title="King George III">King George III</a> in 1794. He is buried in <a href="/wiki/St_Benet_Paul%27s_Wharf" class="mw-redirect" title="St Benet Paul&#39;s Wharf">St Benet Paul's Wharf</a> in London.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tractor_factory">Tractor factory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meltham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Tractor factory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Meltham Mills was also the former base of the <a href="/wiki/David_Brown_Ltd." title="David Brown Ltd.">David Brown Tractors</a> factory opening in 1939 and closing operations on the site in 1988. The various building have now been converted into a diverse number of industrial units, one housing a <a href="/wiki/Tractor" title="Tractor">Tractor</a> museum and other large sections containing an indoor <a href="/wiki/Kart_racing" title="Kart racing">Kart racing</a> track.
</p><p>Durker Roods, the former home of <a href="/wiki/David_Brown_(entrepreneur)" title="David Brown (entrepreneur)">Sir David Brown</a> was converted into a hotel and the grounds were sold for private housing.
</p><p>The town has its own joint Scouting and Guides Association buildings.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people_from_Meltham">Notable people from Meltham</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meltham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Notable people from Meltham">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Meltham was the birthplace of Lance Sergeant James Taylor (25B/82) E Company 2nd Battalion, <a href="/wiki/24th_Regiment_(2nd_Warwickshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="24th Regiment (2nd Warwickshire)">24th Regiment (2nd Warwickshire)</a>, who fought at, and survived, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_at_Rorke%27s_Drift" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle at Rorke&#39;s Drift">battle at Rorke's Drift</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Zulu_war" class="mw-redirect" title="Zulu war">Zulu war</a>. Although born in the village, to parents also born there, he grew up in Manchester, but can still be regarded as a native of Meltham.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Albert_Craig_(The_Surrey_Poet)" class="mw-redirect" title="Albert Craig (The Surrey Poet)">Albert Craig</a>, dubbed the Surrey Poet, was also born and raised in Meltham. His ditties mostly related to cricket and football.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Australian textile magnate Godfrey Hirst was born at Royd Edge, Meltham in 1857.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  In 1890 he founded the Godfrey Hirst Woollen Mills at <a href="/wiki/Geelong" title="Geelong">Geelong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a>, which in the early 20th century became the largest manufacturer of textiles in Australia. The company still operates, as <a href="/wiki/Godfrey_Hirst_Carpets" title="Godfrey Hirst Carpets">Godfrey Hirst Carpets</a>. Hirst died in 1917.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Dora_Thewlis" title="Dora Thewlis">Dora Thewlis</a>, who gained brief national notoriety as a young suffragette when a photograph of her arrest appeared on the front page of the <i>Daily Mirror</i>, was born on Shady Row, Meltham Mills, in May 1890. Within a few years of her birth, the residents of the row had successfully petitioned to have the boundary redrawn so that the entirety of Meltham Mills was within the township of Meltham.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
