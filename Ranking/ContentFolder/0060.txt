<p><b>WorldCat</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Union_catalog" title="Union catalog">union catalog</a> that itemizes the collections of 17,900 <a href="/wiki/Library" title="Library">libraries</a> in 123 countries and territories<sup id="cite_ref-statistics_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statistics-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> that participate in the <a href="/wiki/Online_Computer_Library_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="Online Computer Library Center">Online Computer Library Center</a> (OCLC) global cooperative. It is operated by OCLC, Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-InsideWorldCat_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-InsideWorldCat-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The subscribing member libraries collectively maintain WorldCat's <a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">database</a>, the world's largest <a href="/wiki/Bibliographic_database" title="Bibliographic database">bibliographic database</a>. OCLC makes WorldCat itself available free to libraries, but the catalog is the foundation for other subscription OCLC services (such as <a href="/wiki/Interlibrary_loan" title="Interlibrary loan">resource sharing</a> and collection management). WorldCat is used by the general public and by librarians for cataloging and research.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WorldCat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/OCLC" title="OCLC">OCLC</a> was founded in 1967 under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Fred_Kilgour" title="Fred Kilgour">Fred Kilgour</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fox_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fox-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> That same year, OCLC began to develop the union catalog technology that would later evolve into WorldCat; the first catalog records were added in 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-Fox_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fox-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-history_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2003, OCLC began the "Open WorldCat" pilot program, making abbreviated records from a subset of WorldCat available to partner web sites and booksellers, to increase the accessibility of its subscribing member libraries' collections.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2005, the OCLC technical staff began a <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">wiki</a> project, WikiD, allowing readers to add commentary and structured-field information associated with any WorldCat record.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> WikiD was later phased out, although WorldCat later incorporated <a href="/wiki/User-generated_content" title="User-generated content">user-generated content</a> in other ways.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, it became possible for anyone to search WorldCat directly at its open website,<sup id="cite_ref-Hane_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hane-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> not only through the subscription FirstSearch interface where it had been available on the web to subscribing libraries for more than a decade before.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Options for more sophisticated searches of WorldCat have remained available through the FirstSearch interface.<sup id="cite_ref-Hane_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hane-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, WorldCat Identities began providing pages for 20 million "identities", predominantly authors and persons who are the subjects of published titles.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of May 2019, WorldCat contained over 450 million bibliographic records in 484 languages, representing over 2.8 billion physical and digital library assets,<sup id="cite_ref-InsideWorldCat_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-InsideWorldCat-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and the WorldCat persons dataset (<a href="/wiki/Data_mining" title="Data mining">mined</a> from WorldCat) included over 100 million people.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Model">Model</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WorldCat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Model">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>WorldCat contains records in MAchine Readable Cataloging (<a href="/wiki/MARC_standards" title="MARC standards">MARC</a>) format contributed by library catalogers worldwide who use OCLC as a cataloging tool, and these MARC format records can also be downloaded into other libraries' local catalog systems. This allows libraries to find and download records for materials they are adding to their local catalog, without having to undergo the lengthy process of creating a new catalog entry from scratch for each new item.
</p><p>WorldCat operates on a <a href="/wiki/Batch_processing" title="Batch processing">batch processing</a> model rather than a real-time model. That is, WorldCat records are synchronized at intermittent intervals with the underlying library catalogs instead of real-time or every day. Consequently:
</p>
<ul><li>WorldCat shows that a particular item is owned by a particular library but does not provide that library's <a href="/wiki/Call_number" class="mw-redirect" title="Call number">call number</a>.</li>
<li>WorldCat does not indicate whether or not an item is currently borrowed, lost, undergoing restoration or repair, or moved to storage not directly accessible to patrons (thereby forcing interested patrons to submit a retrieval request and wait).</li>
<li>Furthermore, WorldCat does not show whether or not a library owns multiple copies of a particular title.</li></ul>
<p>As an alternative, WorldCat allows participating institutions to add direct links from WorldCat to their own catalog entries for a particular item, which enables the user to determine its real-time status.<sup id="cite_ref-whatis_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whatis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> However, this still requires users to open multiple Web pages, each pointing to a different <a href="/wiki/Online_public_access_catalog" title="Online public access catalog">online public access catalog</a> with its own distinctive user interface design (which places item status in a different portion of the Web browser display), until they can locate a catalog entry that shows the item is currently available at a particular library.
</p><p>Library contributions to WorldCat are made via the Connexion computer program, which was introduced in 2001; its predecessor, OCLC Passport, was phased out in May 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
