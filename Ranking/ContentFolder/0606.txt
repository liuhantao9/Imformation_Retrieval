<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trolleybuses_in_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Huddersfield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="infobox vcard" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="fn org" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold">Huddersfield trolleybus system</th></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center"><a href="/wiki/File:British_Trolleybuses_-_Huddersfield_-_geograph.org.uk_-_553408.jpg" class="image"><img alt="British Trolleybuses - <p>The <b>Huddersfield trolleybus system</b> once served the <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. Opened on 4&#160;December&#160;1933<span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="bday dtstart published updated">1933-12-04</span>)</span>,<sup id="cite_ref-joyce-king-newman_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-joyce-king-newman-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Short_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Short-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> it gradually replaced the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Corporation_Tramways" title="Huddersfield Corporation Tramways">Huddersfield tramway network</a>, which closed on Saturday, 29 June 1940.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By the standards of the various now-defunct <a href="/wiki/Trolleybus" title="Trolleybus">trolleybus</a> systems in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, the Huddersfield system was a medium-sized one, with a total of 15 routes and a maximum fleet of 140 trolleybuses.<sup id="cite_ref-Short_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Short-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  It was closed on 13&#160;July&#160;1968<span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="dtend">1968-07-13</span>)</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-joyce-king-newman_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-joyce-king-newman-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Short_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Short-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A notable feature of the system was the <a href="/wiki/Longwood_trolleybus_turntable" title="Longwood trolleybus turntable">Longwood trolleybus turntable</a>, which was one of only four such turntables ever to have been constructed in the UK. The turntable was manually operated, and was in use only in 1939–1940 until wartime conditions forced the introduction of other arrangements. However, it remained <i>in situ</i> until demolished in the 1980s.
</p><p>Three of the former Huddersfield trolleybuses are now preserved, at <a href="/wiki/The_Trolleybus_Museum_at_Sandtoft" title="The Trolleybus Museum at Sandtoft">the Trolleybus Museum at Sandtoft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Zebedee_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zebedee-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Trolleybuses_in_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield_trolleybus_routes.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Huddersfield_trolleybus_routes.JPG/220px-Huddersfield_trolleybus_routes.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Huddersfield_trolleybus_routes.JPG/330px-Huddersfield_trolleybus_routes.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Huddersfield_trolleybus_routes.JPG/440px-Huddersfield_trolleybus_routes.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3248" data-file-height="2203" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield_trolleybus_routes.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1933-1968 trolleybus routes</div></div></div>
<p>The first conversion was the 1.9&#160;mi (3.1&#160;km) <a href="/wiki/Almondbury" title="Almondbury">Almondbury</a> tramway. It was converted in sections with a temporary motor bus link as the road was resealed and electrified.
</p><p>Six different 6-wheel buses were bought to trial on the route with <a href="/wiki/Karrier#Rootes_Group" title="Karrier">Karrier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christchurch_Transport_Board#Ransomes" title="Christchurch Transport Board">Ransomes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sunbeam_Commercial_Vehicles#Trolleybuses" title="Sunbeam Commercial Vehicles">Sunbeam</a> chassis and bodies by several builders. Most later buses were Karrier, though the local factory had closed.

<p>In 1958 Edgar Dyson became general manager. The council then agreed to a closure programme, ending with 5 crowded Outlane buses on 13 July 1968. Rates paid to the county council were among reasons for early closure of the West Vale and Marsden routes.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
