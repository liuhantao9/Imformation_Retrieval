<p>"<b>Wasted Years</b>" is a song by the English <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_band" class="mw-redirect" title="Heavy metal band">heavy metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/Iron_Maiden" title="Iron Maiden">Iron Maiden</a>. It is the band's fourteenth single released and the first from their sixth studio album,  <i><a href="/wiki/Somewhere_in_Time_(Iron_Maiden_album)" title="Somewhere in Time (Iron Maiden album)">Somewhere in Time</a></i> (1986). It is the only song on the album that features no synthesizers. Released in 1986, it was the first single solely written by guitarist <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Smith" title="Adrian Smith">Adrian Smith</a>, who also sings backing vocals. It reached number 18 in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Charts</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synopsis">Synopsis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wasted_Years&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Synopsis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song deals with subject of homesickness and alienation,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> as well as the negative aspects of the band's nearly year long previous tour and personal problems that Smith and other band members were going through in that period.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At the same time the chorus suggests the idea that one should move beyond past troubles and look forward. The original working title was "Golden Years", as can be heard on numerous <a href="/wiki/Somewhere_on_Tour" title="Somewhere on Tour">Somewhere on Tour</a> bootlegs.
</p><p>The cover depicts the band mascot <a href="/wiki/Eddie_(mascot)" title="Eddie (mascot)">Eddie</a>'s point of view as he flies a time machine.<sup id="cite_ref-Popoff_94_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Popoff_94-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Only a part of Eddie's face is seen, as a reflection in a screen.<sup id="cite_ref-Popoff_94_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Popoff_94-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> According to artist <a href="/wiki/Derek_Riggs" title="Derek Riggs">Derek Riggs</a>, this is because the band did not want to reveal the mascot in his new cyborg guise until the album was released a few weeks later.<sup id="cite_ref-Popoff_94_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Popoff_94-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As with the <i><a href="/wiki/Somewhere_in_Time_(Iron_Maiden_album)" title="Somewhere in Time (Iron Maiden album)">Somewhere in Time</a></i> album sleeve,<sup id="cite_ref-Popoff_87_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Popoff_87-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> the single cover also features the <a href="/wiki/TARDIS" title="TARDIS">TARDIS</a> from the BBC series <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">Doctor Who</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Popoff_94_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Popoff_94-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Riggs describes the illustration as "a duffer. It's a technical illustration of a keyboard of a time machine, with Eddie reflected in the window, because it was the only thing we could think of that wouldn't give Eddie away".<sup id="cite_ref-Popoff_94_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Popoff_94-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">promotional video</a> features black-and-white footage of the band playing the song in a studio in <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>, mixed with various scenes from band's previous promotional videos, together with clips of off-show activities during the <a href="/wiki/World_Slavery_Tour" title="World Slavery Tour">World Slavery Tour</a>.
</p><p>"Wasted Years" is featured in the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Rock_Band_(video_game)" title="Rock Band (video game)">Rock Band</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="B-sides">B-sides</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wasted_Years&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: B-sides">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/B-side" class="mw-redirect" title="B-side">b-side</a> "Reach Out" was sung by Adrian Smith, with regular <a href="/wiki/Lead_singer" class="mw-redirect" title="Lead singer">lead singer</a> <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Dickinson" title="Bruce Dickinson">Bruce Dickinson</a> providing backing vocals. This song was previously performed live by <a href="/wiki/Nicko_McBrain" title="Nicko McBrain">Nicko McBrain</a> and Smith's <a href="/wiki/Side_project" title="Side project">side project</a> <a href="/wiki/The_Entire_Population_of_Hackney" title="The Entire Population of Hackney">The Entire Population of Hackney</a>.
</p><p>The demential song "Sheriff of Huddersfield" is about Iron Maiden manager <a href="/wiki/Rod_Smallwood" title="Rod Smallwood">Rod Smallwood</a>'s decision to move to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> and buy a house in the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Hills" title="Hollywood Hills">Hollywood Hills</a>. Apparently he had trouble adjusting to the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">LA</a> lifestyle and often complained to the band about it. Smallwood was unaware of the composition until it was released on the single, as the band had kept it secret from him. The song opens with the spoken line "We're on a mission from Rod", a parody of the tagline for <a href="/wiki/The_Blues_Brothers_(film)" title="The Blues Brothers (film)">The Blues Brothers</a>, "We're on a mission from God". Later, a similar comedy piece called "Bayswater Ain't a Bad Place to Be", again mocking Smallwood, was released as an unlisted untitled b-side ("Space Station No. 5"'s epilogue) in the "<a href="/wiki/Be_Quick_or_Be_Dead" title="Be Quick or Be Dead">Be Quick or Be Dead</a>" single.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wasted_Years&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song is composed in the key of <a href="/wiki/E_minor" title="E minor">E minor</a>. Adrian Smith plays the guitar solo.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wasted_Years&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Wasted Years" is one of three tracks from <i>Somewhere in Time</i> that have been played on more than two concert tours, the other two being the album's second single, "Stranger in a Strange Land", and "Heaven Can Wait". It was featured on <a href="/wiki/Somewhere_On_Tour" class="mw-redirect" title="Somewhere On Tour">Somewhere On Tour</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-somewhere_on_tour_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-somewhere_on_tour-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Seventh_Tour_of_a_Seventh_Tour" title="Seventh Tour of a Seventh Tour">Seventh Tour of a Seventh Tour</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-seventh_tour_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-seventh_tour-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Real_Live_Tour" title="Real Live Tour">Real Live Tour</a> (only on few dates),<sup id="cite_ref-real_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-real-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/The_Ed_Hunter_Tour" title="The Ed Hunter Tour">The Ed Hunter Tour</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-ed_hunter_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ed_hunter-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Somewhere_Back_in_Time_World_Tour" title="Somewhere Back in Time World Tour">Somewhere Back in Time World Tour</a><sup id="cite_ref-back_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-back-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Maiden_England_World_Tour" title="Maiden England World Tour">Maiden England World Tour</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It was the final song of the group's encore set on <a href="/wiki/The_Book_of_Souls_World_Tour" title="The Book of Souls World Tour">The Book of Souls World Tour</a>, the first time it has ever been part of an encore performance.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Janick_Gers" title="Janick Gers">Janick Gers</a> played the solo on Real Live Tour due to Smith leaving Iron Maiden in 1990. He also played all Smith's guitar parts on few dates of Ed Hunter Tour, while Smith was absent. In current Iron Maiden line-up shows (since 1999), Smith's trademark intro riff is doubled by Gers.
</p><p>During Somewhere On Tour years, the band used to lip-sync the track on various TV shows. The most famous recording comes from Germany, where Maiden swapped instruments on stage while the song was "performed", as a humorous protest to lip-syncing.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
