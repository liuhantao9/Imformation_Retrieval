	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Colne Bridge</h1>

<p><b>Colne Bridge</b> is an historic 18th-century bridge near <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. A  <a href="/wiki/Grade_II_listed" class="mw-redirect" title="Grade II listed">Grade II listed</a> stone-built arch bridge, it spans the <a href="/wiki/River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Colne, West Yorkshire">River Colne</a> between <a href="/wiki/Bradley,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradley, West Yorkshire">Bradley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kirkheaton" title="Kirkheaton">Kirkheaton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to Ted Ruddock, Colne Bridge may have been the design inspiration for <a href="/wiki/John_Smeaton" title="John Smeaton">John Smeaton</a>'s work on the <a href="/wiki/Blackfriars_Bridge" title="Blackfriars Bridge">Blackfriars Bridge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
