<p><b>Farnley Tyas</b> is a small village in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England 3 miles (4.8&#160;km) south east of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>. It is located on a hilltop between <a href="/wiki/Almondbury" title="Almondbury">Almondbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castle_Hill,_Huddersfield" title="Castle Hill, Huddersfield">Castle Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thurstonland" title="Thurstonland">Thurstonland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honley" title="Honley">Honley</a>. It is mostly rural and farmland with private housing and some local authority social housing.
</p><p>It has a public house, the Golden Cock Inn, a <a href="/wiki/First_School" class="mw-redirect" title="First School">First School</a> catering for around 50 children, aged from four to ten years old, a <a href="/wiki/Bowling_green" title="Bowling green">bowling club</a>, a small sports field and the Church of St Lucius. It is part of the Parish of Almondbury with Farnley Tyas <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Farnley_Tyas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Farnley Tyas <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Township" title="Township">Township</a> in 1894. First mentioned in the Domesday Book of 1086, it was then called Fereleia. Tyas is a family name from the le Tyeis who held land in the neighbourhood from the 13th century.
</p><p>Extract from Pigot &amp; Co's National Commercial Directory, 1834
</p>
<ul><li><i>FARNLEY TYAS is a township, in the same parish as Honley and Crossland, about three miles from Huddersfield and two from Honley. There are but few manufacturing establishments in this township, and, divested of these, it is a place of little importance.  The Earl of Dartmouth contributes £30 annually for the support of a school, in which thirty children are instructed.  The population of this township has latterly declined: in 1821 it contained 900 inhabitants, and in 1831, 849.</i></li></ul>
<p>In 1925 it was merged with Thurstonland urban district. Farnley Tyas and Thurstonland Urban District was then abolished in 1938 under a <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1929" title="Local Government Act 1929">County Review Order</a>, with the majority of the district merging into the <a href="/wiki/Kirkburton" title="Kirkburton">Kirkburton</a> urban district and the remainder into the <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley" title="Holme Valley">Holmfirth urban district</a>. Although within the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a> district of Huddersfield recent boundary changes will transfer the village from the Westminster Parliamentary district of <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Wakefield (UK Parliament constituency)">Wakefield</a> to  <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Huddersfield (UK Parliament constituency)">Barnsley</a>
</p>
