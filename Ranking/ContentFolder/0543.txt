<p><b>Primrose Hill</b> is a hill of 213 feet (65&#160;m)<sup id="cite_ref-mills_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mills-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> located on the northern side of <a href="/wiki/Regent%27s_Park" title="Regent&#39;s Park">Regent's Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. The name was given also to the surrounding district. The hill summit has a clear view of <a href="/wiki/Central_London" title="Central London">central London</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Hampstead" title="Hampstead">Hampstead</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belsize_Park" title="Belsize Park">Belsize Park</a> to the north  and is adorned by an engraved quotation from <a href="/wiki/William_Blake" title="William Blake">William Blake</a>.  Nowadays it is one of the most exclusive and expensive residential areas in London and is home to many prominent residents.<sup id="cite_ref-Knight_Frank_&amp;_Rutley_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Knight_Frank_&amp;_Rutley-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Primrose Hill district is surrounded by the affluent areas of <a href="/wiki/St_John%27s_Wood" title="St John&#39;s Wood">St John's Wood</a> to the west, <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Cottage" title="Swiss Cottage">Swiss Cottage</a> to the northwest, <a href="/wiki/Belsize_Park" title="Belsize Park">Belsize Park</a> to the north, <a href="/wiki/Chalk_Farm" title="Chalk Farm">Chalk Farm</a> to the northeast, <a href="/wiki/Camden_Town" title="Camden Town">Camden Town</a> to the east and <a href="/wiki/Regent%27s_Park" title="Regent&#39;s Park">Regent's Park</a> itself lies adjacent to the south of the hill itself. The nearest stations to Primrose Hill are <a href="/wiki/Chalk_Farm_tube_station" title="Chalk Farm tube station">Chalk Farm tube station</a> to the northeast and <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Cottage_tube_station" title="Swiss Cottage tube station">Swiss Cottage tube station</a> to the northwest. The defunct <a href="/wiki/Primrose_Hill_railway_station" title="Primrose Hill railway station">Primrose Hill railway station</a> sits on the railway lines that separate the Primrose Hill area from <a href="/wiki/Camden_Town" title="Camden Town">Camden Town</a>.
</p><p>Amenities of the hill include an outdoor gym known as the Hill Trim Trail, a children's playground, and toilets, all located on the south side near Primrose Hill bridge which connects to <a href="/wiki/London_Zoo" title="London Zoo">London Zoo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Regent%27s_Park" title="Regent&#39;s Park">Regent's Park</a>.
</p><p>The park is Grade II listed.<sup id="cite_ref-NHLE_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NHLE-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Primrose_Hill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Primrose_Hill_-_London.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Primrose_Hill_-_London.jpg/220px-Primrose_Hill_-_London.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Primrose_Hill_-_London.jpg/330px-Primrose_Hill_-_London.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Primrose_Hill_-_London.jpg/440px-Primrose_Hill_-_London.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Primrose_Hill_-_London.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Panoramic view of <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> from Primrose Hill</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:View_from_Primrose_Hill_at_night.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/View_from_Primrose_Hill_at_night.jpg/220px-View_from_Primrose_Hill_at_night.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/View_from_Primrose_Hill_at_night.jpg/330px-View_from_Primrose_Hill_at_night.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/View_from_Primrose_Hill_at_night.jpg/440px-View_from_Primrose_Hill_at_night.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5931" data-file-height="3954" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:View_from_Primrose_Hill_at_night.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>View of London Skyline by night from the summit of Primrose Hill.</div></div></div>
<p>Like Regent's Park, Primrose Hill was once part of a great chase appropriated by <a href="/wiki/Henry_VIII_of_England" title="Henry VIII of England">Henry VIII</a>. Later, in 1841, it became Crown property and in 1842 an <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Parliament" title="Act of Parliament">Act of Parliament</a> secured the land as public open space. The built-up part of Primrose Hill comprises mainly Victorian terraces; the name Primrose Hill has been in use since the 15th century - giving the lie to later claims that it was named after <a href="/wiki/Archibald_Primrose" class="mw-redirect" title="Archibald Primrose">Archibald Primrose</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Prime_minister" title="Prime minister">premiership</a> witnessed the rapid expansion of <a href="/wiki/London_Underground" title="London Underground">London underground</a> rail network London.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It has always been one of the more fashionable districts in the urban belt that lies between the core of London and the outer suburbs, and remains expensive and prosperous. Primrose Hill is an archetypal example of a successful London urban village, due to the location and the quality of its socio-historical development.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In October 1678, Primrose Hill was the scene of the mysterious murder of <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Berry_Godfrey" title="Edmund Berry Godfrey">Edmund Berry Godfrey</a> (Greenberry Hill).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1792 the radical Unitarian poet and antiquarian <a href="/wiki/Iolo_Morganwg" title="Iolo Morganwg">Iolo Morganwg</a> (Edward Williams) founded the <a href="/wiki/Gorsedd" title="Gorsedd">Gorsedd</a>, a community of <a href="/wiki/Welsh_people" title="Welsh people">Welsh</a> bards, at a ceremony on 21 June at Primrose Hill.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Famous_graffiti">Famous graffiti</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Primrose_Hill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Famous graffiti">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There is, as in most places in London, graffiti on Primrose Hill. However, the one that seemed to impact the public most was a lyric about Primrose Hill by the band <a href="/wiki/Blur_(band)" title="Blur (band)">Blur</a> (from their single "<a href="/wiki/For_Tomorrow" title="For Tomorrow">For Tomorrow</a>"). The graffiti read 'And the view's so nice' until it was removed in 2012. Some have tried to restore it, but none have yet succeeded.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_buildings_and_residents">Notable buildings and residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Primrose_Hill&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Notable buildings and residents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are seven <a href="/wiki/List_of_English_Heritage_blue_plaques_in_Camden" class="mw-redirect" title="List of English Heritage blue plaques in Camden">English Heritage blue plaques in Primrose Hill</a> commemorating the historic personalities that have lived there.<sup id="cite_ref-EngHetPH_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EngHetPH-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
The plaques mark the residences of poet <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Hugh_Clough" title="Arthur Hugh Clough">Sir Hugh Clough</a>, historian and broadcaster <a href="/wiki/A._J._P._Taylor" title="A. J. P. Taylor">A. J. P. Taylor</a> and painter <a href="/wiki/William_Roberts_(painter)" title="William Roberts (painter)">William Roberts</a> at 11, 13, and 14 St Mark's Crescent respectively, revolutionary socialist and philosopher <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Engels" title="Friedrich Engels">Friedrich Engels</a> at 122 Regent's Park Road, photographer <a href="/wiki/Roger_Fenton" title="Roger Fenton">Roger Fenton</a> at 2 Albert Terrace, poet and novelist <a href="/wiki/Sylvia_Plath" title="Sylvia Plath">Sylvia Plath</a> at 3 Chalcot Square, and poet <a href="/wiki/William_Butler_Yeats" class="mw-redirect" title="William Butler Yeats">William Butler Yeats</a> at 23 Fitzroy Road.<sup id="cite_ref-EngHetPH_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EngHetPH-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Stanley_Johnson_(writer)" title="Stanley Johnson (writer)">Stanley Johnson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lukas_Heller" title="Lukas Heller">Lukas Heller</a> each lived at different times at the 'Rocking Horse House' on Regent's Park Road. <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
<a href="/wiki/Joan_Bakewell" title="Joan Bakewell">Joan Bakewell</a> and actor <a href="/wiki/Derek_Jacobi" title="Derek Jacobi">Derek Jacobi</a> live in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Elliot Square is a grouping of modernist 1960s houses by Douglas Streeter, built as part of the Chalcot Estate on land owned by <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton College</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>During the 1990s Primrose Hill was a popular place to live with some who worked the film, music and fashion industries and who were referred to as the <i><a href="/wiki/Primrose_Hill_set" title="Primrose Hill set">Primrose Hill set</a></i> in British newspapers.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
