<p><b>Michael Schroeder</b> is an American computer scientist. His areas of research include computer security, distributed systems and operating systems and he is perhaps best known as the co-inventor of the <a href="/wiki/Needham%E2%80%93Schroeder_protocol" title="Needham–Schroeder protocol">Needham–Schroeder protocol</a>. In 2001 he co-founded the <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Research_Silicon_Valley" class="mw-redirect" title="Microsoft Research Silicon Valley">Microsoft Research Silicon Valley</a> lab and was the Assistant Managing Director until the lab was disbanded in 2014.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Schroeder&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Schroeder was born in 1945 in Richland, Washington. He did his undergraduate work at Washington State University and went to graduate school at MIT, obtaining his PhD in 1972.
</p><p>Starting in 1976 he has been on the MIT EECS department faculty, at <a href="/wiki/Xerox_PARC" class="mw-redirect" title="Xerox PARC">Xerox PARC</a>, and at the <a href="/wiki/DEC_Systems_Research_Center" title="DEC Systems Research Center">DEC Systems Research Center</a>. At MIT he was involved with <a href="/wiki/Multics" title="Multics">Multics</a>, where his contributions included a seminal work on security architecture for shared information systems.<sup id="cite_ref-SaltzerSchroeder1975_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SaltzerSchroeder1975-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some other systems he has built are Grapevine (distributed system), the filesystem of <a href="/wiki/Mesa_programming_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Mesa programming language">Cedar</a>, Topaz (distributed OS), Autonet (LAN) and Pachyderm (web based email).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Schroeder&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004 he was inducted as a <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">Fellow</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery" title="Association for Computing Machinery">Association for Computing Machinery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006 ACM SIGSAC presented him with the Outstanding Innovations Award "for technical contributions to the field of computer and communication security that have had lasting impact in furthering or understanding the theory and/or development of commercial systems."
</p><p>In 2007 NIST/NSA gave him the National Computer Systems Security Award. In 2008 ACM SIGOPS chose the paper <i>Grapevine: An Exercise in Distributed Computing</i>, which he coauthored, for a Hall of Fame Award "that recognizes the most influential operating systems papers in the peer-reviewed literature at least ten year old."
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gilbert_Munger">Gilbert Munger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Schroeder&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Gilbert Munger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He is a leading expert on the American landscape painter <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Munger" title="Gilbert Munger">Gilbert Munger</a> [1837-1903], for whom he authors a web-based <a href="/wiki/Catalogue_raisonn%C3%A9" title="Catalogue raisonné">catalogue raisonné</a> and archive of period documents. With J. Gray Sweeney of Arizona State University he wrote the book <i>Gilbert Munger: Quest for Distinction</i> (Afton Historical Society Press, 2003).
</p>
