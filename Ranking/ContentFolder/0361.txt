	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Category:Professional titles and certifications</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Professional_titles&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional titles">Professional titles</a>)</span></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Professional titles</b> are used to signify a person's <a href="/wiki/Professional" title="Professional">professional</a> role or to designate membership in a professional society.  Professional titles in the anglophone world are usually used as a <a href="/wiki/Suffix_(name)" title="Suffix (name)">suffix</a> following the person's name, such as <i>John Smith, <a href="/wiki/Esquire#United_States" title="Esquire">Esq.</a></i>, and are thus termed <a href="/wiki/Post-nominal_letters" title="Post-nominal letters">post-nominal letters</a>.  However, many European countries use <a href="/wiki/Prenominal_letters" class="mw-redirect" title="Prenominal letters">prenominal letters</a> such as <a href="/wiki/European_Engineer" title="European Engineer">Eur Ing</a>.  In the UK, many professional titles are 'chartered' such as <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Engineer_(UK)" title="Chartered Engineer (UK)">Chartered Engineer</a> or <a href="/wiki/Institute_of_Physics#Chartered_Physicist" title="Institute of Physics">Chartered Physicist</a>.
Other professional Engineer in the UK is the Incorporated Engineer.
Under the <a href="/wiki/European_professional_qualification_directives" title="European professional qualification directives">European professional qualification directives</a>, holders of professional titles in one member state are entitled to recognition of their title in all other member states.
</p><p>Once a person achieves <b>professional certification</b> in a given area of study, they have undergone examination by some form of <a href="/wiki/Organization" title="Organization">governing body</a> or <a href="/wiki/Professional_association" title="Professional association">professional association</a> that is willing to assure the candidates competence in the given field of knowledge. In most cases, a <a href="/wiki/Professional_certification" title="Professional certification">certificate</a> is issued for a set duration of time, at which the candidate must recertify with the same governing body.
</p>
