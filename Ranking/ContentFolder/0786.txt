	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Joseph Crosland</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Sir Joseph Crosland</b> <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Knight_Bachelor" title="Knight Bachelor">Kt</a>&#32;<a href="/wiki/Deputy_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Deputy Lieutenant">DL</a></span> (24 October 1826 – 27 August 1904)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a British <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> politician who served as <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Huddersfield (UK Parliament constituency)">Huddersfield</a> from 4 February 1893 to 13 July 1895.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He had contested the seat in the previous three general elections, losing each time to the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal</a> candidate.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was made <a href="/wiki/Knight_Batchelor" class="mw-redirect" title="Knight Batchelor">Knight Batchelor</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1889_Birthday_Honours" title="1889 Birthday Honours">1889 Birthday Honours</a>.
</p><p>Joseph was the third son of George Crosland, a wool manufacturer who built several mills in the area around <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, notably in <a href="/wiki/Lockwood,_Huddersfield" title="Lockwood, Huddersfield">Lockwood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Crosland_Moor" title="Crosland Moor">Crosland Moor</a>.
</p><p>Crosland was a Conservative councillor for Lockwood (1869–72), a member of the School Board, and President of the Chamber of Commerce (1872–74). Crosland led the family textile firm, and was chairman of the Huddersfield Banking company from 1876 until 1897, when it was amalgamated with the <a href="/wiki/Midland_Bank" title="Midland Bank">London, City and Midland Bank</a>. He was an <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Anglican</a> for most of his life. His brother, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Crosland" title="Thomas Crosland">Thomas Crosland</a>, represented Huddersfield as MP between 1865 and 1868.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Crosland built Royds Hall in 1866 as a family home; the building is now <a href="/wiki/Royds_Hall_School" title="Royds Hall School">Royds Hall School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
