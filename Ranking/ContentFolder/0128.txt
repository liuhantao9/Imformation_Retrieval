<p><b>Alberto Santos-Dumont</b> (<small>Brazilian Portuguese:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">[awˈbɛɾtu ˈsɐ̃tus duˈmõ]</a></span>; 20 July 1873&#160;&#8211;&#32;23 July 1932) was a Brazilian inventor and <a href="/wiki/Aviation" title="Aviation">aviation</a> pioneer, one of the very few people to have contributed significantly to the development of both <a href="/wiki/Lighter-than-air" class="mw-redirect" title="Lighter-than-air">lighter-than-air</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heavier-than-air" class="mw-redirect" title="Heavier-than-air">heavier-than-air</a> aircraft.
</p><p>The heir of a wealthy family of coffee producers,  Santos-Dumont dedicated himself to aeronautical study and experimentation in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, where he spent most of his adult life.
In his early career he designed, built, and flew <a href="/wiki/Hot_air_balloons" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot air balloons">hot air balloons</a> and early <a href="/wiki/Airship" title="Airship">dirigibles</a>, culminating in his winning the <a href="/wiki/Deutsch_de_la_Meurthe_prize" class="mw-redirect" title="Deutsch de la Meurthe prize">Deutsch de la Meurthe prize</a> on 19 October 1901 for a flight that rounded the <a href="/wiki/Eiffel_Tower" title="Eiffel Tower">Eiffel Tower</a>.
He then turned to heavier-than-air machines, and on 23 October 1906 his <i><a href="/wiki/14-bis" class="mw-redirect" title="14-bis">14-bis</a></i> made the first powered heavier-than-air flight in Europe to be certified by the <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9ro-Club_de_France" title="Aéro-Club de France">Aéro-Club de France</a> and the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_A%C3%A9ronautique_Internationale" title="Fédération Aéronautique Internationale">Fédération Aéronautique Internationale</a>.
His conviction that aviation would usher in an era of worldwide peace and prosperity led him to freely publish his designs and forego <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patenting</a> his various innovations.
</p><p>Santos-Dumont is a national hero in Brazil, where it is popularly held that he preceded the <a href="/wiki/Wright_brothers" title="Wright brothers">Wright brothers</a> in demonstrating a practical airplane.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-smithsonian_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smithsonian-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Countless roads, plazas, schools, monuments, and airports there are dedicated to him, and his name is inscribed on the <a href="/wiki/Tancredo_Neves_Pantheon_of_the_Fatherland_and_Freedom" title="Tancredo Neves Pantheon of the Fatherland and Freedom">Tancredo Neves Pantheon of the Fatherland and Freedom</a>.
He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Academy_of_Letters" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazilian Academy of Letters">Brazilian Academy of Letters</a> from 1931 until his suicide in 1932.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Santos-Dumont&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Childhood">Childhood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Santos-Dumont&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Childhood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Santos-Dumont was born on 20 July 1873 in Cabangu in the Brazilian town of Palmira (today named <a href="/wiki/Santos_Dumont,_Minas_Gerais" title="Santos Dumont, Minas Gerais">Santos Dumont</a>) in the state of <a href="/wiki/Minas_Gerais" title="Minas Gerais">Minas Gerais</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="Southeast Brazil">southeast Brazil</a>. He was the youngest of seven children born to <a href="/wiki/Henrique_Dumont" title="Henrique Dumont">Henrique Dumont</a>, an engineer of French descent, and Francisca de Paula Santos. Santos-Dumont's father managed a coffee <a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">plantation</a> on land owned by his wife's family, and later bought land  in <a href="/wiki/Ribeir%C3%A3o_Preto" title="Ribeirão Preto">Ribeirão Preto</a> on which he established a plantation of his own.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His extensive use of labor-saving inventions earned him a fortune, and he was known for a time as the "<a href="/wiki/Coffee_King" title="Coffee King">Coffee King of Brazil</a>."
</p><p>Santos-Dumont was fascinated by machinery, and while still a child he learned to drive the plantation's <a href="/wiki/Steam_tractor" title="Steam tractor">steam tractors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Locomotive" title="Locomotive">locomotives</a>. He also read a great deal of the works of <a href="/wiki/Jules_Verne" title="Jules Verne">Jules Verne</a>. He wrote in his autobiography that the dream of flying came to him while contemplating the magnificent skies of Brazil from the plantation.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After basic instruction with private tutors, Santos-Dumont studied for a time at the <a href="/wiki/Col%C3%A9gio_Culto_%C3%A0_Ci%C3%AAncia" title="Colégio Culto à Ciência">Colégio Culto à Ciência</a> in <a href="/wiki/Campinas" title="Campinas">Campinas</a>, after which he was sent to the Colégio Morton in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> and the Escola de Minas in Minas Gerais.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Move_to_France">Move to France</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Santos-Dumont&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Move to France">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alberto_Santos_Dumont.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Alberto_Santos_Dumont.jpg/170px-Alberto_Santos_Dumont.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="254" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Alberto_Santos_Dumont.jpg/255px-Alberto_Santos_Dumont.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Alberto_Santos_Dumont.jpg/340px-Alberto_Santos_Dumont.jpg 2x" data-file-width="401" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alberto_Santos_Dumont.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Caricature from <a href="/wiki/Vanity_Fair_caricatures" class="mw-redirect" title="Vanity Fair caricatures">Vanity Fair</a>, 1901</div></div></div>
<p>In 1891 Santos-Dumont's father was partially paralyzed by a fall from a horse. He sold the plantation and went to Europe with his wife and Santos-Dumont in search of treatment.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In Paris, Santos-Dumont contacted a balloonist with the intention of making an ascent. The price quoted was 1,200 francs for a two-hour flight, plus payment for any damage caused and for returning the balloon to Paris. This was a considerable sum of money, and Santos-Dumont decided not to make the flight, reasoning that "If I risk 1,200 francs for an afternoon's pleasure I shall find it either good or bad. If it is bad the money will be lost. If it is good I shall want to repeat it and I shall not have the means."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After this he bought a <a href="/wiki/Peugeot" title="Peugeot">Peugeot</a> automobile, which he took with him when he returned to Brazil with his parents at the end of the year.
</p><p>In 1892 the family returned to Europe, but Henriques felt too ill to continue on to Paris from Lisbon, and Alberto made the journey on his own. His father's health deteriorated and he decided to return to Brazil, where he died on 30 August 1892.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For the next four years Alberto lived in Paris, studying <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">chemistry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mechanics" title="Mechanics">mechanics</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Electricity" title="Electricity">electricity</a> with the help of a private tutor, and returning to Brazil for short holidays. During this period he sold his Peugeot, replacing it with a more powerful and faster <a href="/wiki/De_Dion-Bouton" title="De Dion-Bouton">De Dion</a> motor-tricycle.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 1896 he returned to Brazil for a longer period, but began to miss Paris and so returned to Europe in 1897. Before embarking he had bought a copy of an account of <a href="/wiki/Salomon_August_Andr%C3%A9e" title="Salomon August Andrée">Salomon Andrée</a>'s attempt to fly to the North Pole by balloon, written by the constructors of the balloon, MM. Lachambre and Machuron. In his biography Santos-Dumont describes the book as "a revelation", and resolved to make contact with the balloon constructors when he reached Paris.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aircraft">Aircraft</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Santos-Dumont&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Aircraft">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Santos-Dumont_aircraft" title="List of Santos-Dumont aircraft">List of Santos-Dumont aircraft</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Balloons_and_dirigibles">Balloons and dirigibles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Santos-Dumont&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Balloons and dirigibles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Brazil,_4_julho_1898_-Musee_de_l%27Air_et_de_l%27Espace,_MA_39568-.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Brazil%2C_4_julho_1898_-Musee_de_l%27Air_et_de_l%27Espace%2C_MA_39568-.JPG/170px-Brazil%2C_4_julho_1898_-Musee_de_l%27Air_et_de_l%27Espace%2C_MA_39568-.JPG" decoding="async" width="170" height="241" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Brazil%2C_4_julho_1898_-Musee_de_l%27Air_et_de_l%27Espace%2C_MA_39568-.JPG/255px-Brazil%2C_4_julho_1898_-Musee_de_l%27Air_et_de_l%27Espace%2C_MA_39568-.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Brazil%2C_4_julho_1898_-Musee_de_l%27Air_et_de_l%27Espace%2C_MA_39568-.JPG/340px-Brazil%2C_4_julho_1898_-Musee_de_l%27Air_et_de_l%27Espace%2C_MA_39568-.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4016" data-file-height="5683" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Brazil,_4_julho_1898_-Musee_de_l%27Air_et_de_l%27Espace,_MA_39568-.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Santos-Dumont's first balloon, the <i>Bresil</i></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nacelle_Santos-Dumont.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Nacelle_Santos-Dumont.jpg/170px-Nacelle_Santos-Dumont.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="203" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Nacelle_Santos-Dumont.jpg/255px-Nacelle_Santos-Dumont.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Nacelle_Santos-Dumont.jpg/340px-Nacelle_Santos-Dumont.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1290" data-file-height="1540" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nacelle_Santos-Dumont.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An airship basket of Santos-Dumont's on display in France</div></div></div>
<p>On arrival in Paris Santos-Dumont contacted Lachambre and Machuron and arranged to make a flight, piloted by Alexis Machuron.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Taking off from <a href="/wiki/Vaugirard" class="mw-redirect" title="Vaugirard">Vaugirard</a>, the flight lasted nearly two hours during which the balloon travelled 100&#160;km (62&#160;mi), coming down in the grounds of the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau_de_Ferri%C3%A8res" title="Château de Ferrières">Château de Ferrières</a>.  Enchanted by the experience, during the train journey back to Paris Santos-Dumont told Machuron that he wanted to have a balloon constructed for himself.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  Before this was completed he gained experience by making a number of demonstration flights for Lachambre.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Santos-Dumont's first balloon design, the <i>Brésil</i>, was remarkable for its small size and light weight, with a capacity of only 113&#160;m<sup>3</sup> (4,000&#160;cu&#160;ft). In comparison, the balloon in which he had made his first flight had a capacity of 750&#160;m<sup>3</sup> (26,000&#160;cu&#160;ft).
</p><p>After numerous balloon flights, Santos-Dumont turned to the design of steerable balloons, which became known as <a href="/wiki/Dirigible" class="mw-redirect" title="Dirigible">dirigibles</a>. These could be propelled through the air rather than drifting along with the wind.  A dirigible powered by an electric motor, <i><a href="/wiki/La_France_(airship)" title="La France (airship)">La France</a></i>, capable of flying at around 24&#160;km/h (15&#160;mph) had been successfully flown in 1884 by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Renard" title="Charles Renard">Charles Renard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Krebs" class="mw-redirect" title="Arthur Krebs">Arthur Krebs</a>, but their experiments had not progressed due to a lack of funding.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
His <a href="/wiki/List_of_Santos-Dumont_aircraft#1898" title="List of Santos-Dumont aircraft">first design</a> was wrecked during its second flight on 29 September 1898, and he had even less luck with his second, which was abandoned after his first attempt to fly it on 11 May 1899.
</p><p>A major cause of the accidents to his first two airships had been loss of pressure causing the elongated envelope to lose shape, and for his third design he adopted a much shorter and fatter envelope shape, and towards the end of 1899 made a number of successful flights in it.  Meanwhile, he had an airship shed complete with its own <a href="/wiki/Hydrogen" title="Hydrogen">hydrogen</a> generating plant constructed at the <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9ro-Club_de_France" title="Aéro-Club de France">Aéro-Club de France</a>'s flying grounds in the <a href="/wiki/Parc_Saint_Cloud" class="mw-redirect" title="Parc Saint Cloud">Parc Saint Cloud</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The zenith of his lighter-than-air career came when Santos-Dumont won the <a href="/wiki/Henri_Deutsch_de_la_Meurthe#Deutsch_de_la_Meurthe_prize" title="Henri Deutsch de la Meurthe">Deutsch de la Meurthe prize</a> for the first flight from the Parc Saint Cloud to the <a href="/wiki/Eiffel_Tower" title="Eiffel Tower">Eiffel Tower</a> and back in less than 30 minutes, necessitating an average ground speed of at least 22&#160;km/h (14&#160;mph) to cover the 11&#160;km (6.8&#160;mi) in the allotted time.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Santos-Dumont_flight_around_the_Eiffel_Tower.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Santos-Dumont_flight_around_the_Eiffel_Tower.jpg/170px-Santos-Dumont_flight_around_the_Eiffel_Tower.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="242" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Santos-Dumont_flight_around_the_Eiffel_Tower.jpg/255px-Santos-Dumont_flight_around_the_Eiffel_Tower.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Santos-Dumont_flight_around_the_Eiffel_Tower.jpg/340px-Santos-Dumont_flight_around_the_Eiffel_Tower.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1802" data-file-height="2569" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Santos-Dumont_flight_around_the_Eiffel_Tower.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>No. 5 rounding the Eiffel Tower during an unsuccessful try for the Deutsch Prize</div></div></div>
<p>To win the Deutsch de la Meurthe prize Santos-Dumont decided to build a bigger craft, the No. 5. On 8 August 1901, during one of his attempts, his dirigible began to lose hydrogen, and started to descend and was unable to clear the roof of the Trocadero Hotel. Santos-Dumont was left hanging in the basket from the side of the hotel. With the help of the Paris fire brigade, he climbed to the roof without injury, but the dirigible was a complete loss. He immediately ordered a replacement to be constructed, the <i><a href="/wiki/Santos-Dumont_number_6" title="Santos-Dumont number 6">No.6</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 19 October 1901, after several  more attempts, Santos-Dumont succeeded in making the return flight.  Immediately after he reached Saint-Cloud, a controversy broke out regarding the precise timing of the flight: although he had reached his destination in under 30 minutes there had been a delay of over a minute before his mooring line was picked up.  However a satisfactory compromise was reached, and Santos-Dumont was eventually given the prize, which he announced would be given to the poor of Paris.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>  An additional  125,000 francs along with a gold medal was voted to him  by the government of his native Brazil.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Santos_Dumont_No.9_Paris.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Santos_Dumont_No.9_Paris.jpg/220px-Santos_Dumont_No.9_Paris.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Santos_Dumont_No.9_Paris.jpg/330px-Santos_Dumont_No.9_Paris.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Santos_Dumont_No.9_Paris.jpg/440px-Santos_Dumont_No.9_Paris.jpg 2x" data-file-width="539" data-file-height="365" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Santos_Dumont_No.9_Paris.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>No. 9 in Paris</div></div></div>
<p>Winning the de la Meurthe prize made Santos-Dumont an international celebrity. He would float his <a href="/wiki/List_of_Santos-Dumont_aircraft#1903" title="List of Santos-Dumont aircraft">No. 9 <i>Baladeuse</i></a> along Paris boulevards at rooftop level, sometimes landing at a cafe for lunch.<sup id="cite_ref-About.com_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-About.com-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
Parisians affectionately dubbed Santos-Dumont <i>le petit Santos</i>. The fashionable people of the day copied various aspects of his style of dress, from his high collared shirts to his signature <a href="/wiki/Panama_hat" title="Panama hat">Panama hat</a>.
</p><p>In 1904 Santos-Dumont shipped his new airship No. 7 from Paris to St. Louis to fly at the <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Purchase_Exposition" title="Louisiana Purchase Exposition">Louisiana Purchase Exposition</a>, to compete for the Grand Prize of $100,000 which was to be given to a flying machine (of any sort) that could make three round-trip flights over a 24&#160;km (15&#160;mile) L-shaped course at an average speed of 20&#160;mph (32&#160;km/h), later reduced to 15&#160;mph (24&#160;km/h). It was also necessary for the machine to land undamaged not more than 46&#160;m (150&#160;ft) from the starting point. Because he was the best-known aviator at the time, the Fair committee went to great lengths to ensure his participation, including modifying the rules.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> 
In conjunction with this trip he was invited to the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a> to meet U.S. President <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Roosevelt" title="Theodore Roosevelt">Theodore Roosevelt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, upon arrival in St. Louis, Santos-Dumont found his airship’s envelope to be irreparably damaged. Sabotage, although suspected, was never proven. Santos-Dumont did not participate in the contest after suspicion of the deed, a repeat of a similar incident in London, began to focus on Santos-Dumont himself. He left the Fair and returned immediately to France.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 1904, after Santos-Dumont complained to his friend <a href="/wiki/Louis_Cartier" class="mw-redirect" title="Louis Cartier">Louis Cartier</a> about the difficulty of checking his pocket watch during flight, Cartier created his first men's <a href="/wiki/Watch#Wristwatch" title="Watch">wristwatch</a>, thus allowing Santos-Dumont to check his flight performance while keeping both hands on the controls.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
Cartier still markets a line of Santos-Dumont watches and sunglasses.<sup id="cite_ref-Cartier_sun_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cartier_sun-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Although Santos-Dumont continued to work on non-rigid airships, his primary interest soon turned to <a href="/wiki/Heavier-than-air" class="mw-redirect" title="Heavier-than-air">heavier-than-air</a> aircraft. By 1905, he had finished his first <a href="/wiki/Fixed-wing_aircraft" title="Fixed-wing aircraft">fixed-wing aircraft</a> design, and also a <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a>. Santos-Dumont finally succeeded in flying a heavier-than-air aircraft on 23 October 1906, piloting the <i><a href="/wiki/14-bis" class="mw-redirect" title="14-bis">14-bis</a></i> before a large crowd of witnesses at the grounds of Paris' <a href="/wiki/Ch%C3%A2teau_de_Bagatelle" title="Château de Bagatelle">Château de Bagatelle</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bois_de_Boulogne" title="Bois de Boulogne">Bois de Boulogne</a> for a distance of 60&#160;metres (197&#160;ft) at a height of about five meters (16&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> This was the first flight of a powered heavier-than-air machine in Europe to be certified by the <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9ro-Club_de_France" title="Aéro-Club de France">Aéro-Club de France</a>, and won the Deutsch-Archdeacon Prize for the first officially observed flight of more than 25&#160;meters.  On 12 November 1906 Santos-Dumont set the first world record recognized by the <a href="/wiki/Federation_Aeronautique_Internationale" class="mw-redirect" title="Federation Aeronautique Internationale">Federation Aeronautique Internationale</a>, by flying 220&#160;metres (722&#160;ft) in 21.5&#160;seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On that date he became the first person to be filmed in an airplane in flight.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> As late as 1906 he was reported as stating that the Wright Brothers claim to have flown for thirty miles was mere bluff.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alberto_Santos_Dumont_flying_the_Demoiselle_(1909).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Alberto_Santos_Dumont_flying_the_Demoiselle_%281909%29.jpg/220px-Alberto_Santos_Dumont_flying_the_Demoiselle_%281909%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Alberto_Santos_Dumont_flying_the_Demoiselle_%281909%29.jpg/330px-Alberto_Santos_Dumont_flying_the_Demoiselle_%281909%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9b/Alberto_Santos_Dumont_flying_the_Demoiselle_%281909%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="320" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alberto_Santos_Dumont_flying_the_Demoiselle_(1909).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Flying the Demoiselle over Paris</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Santos-Dumont_flying_the_14_bis.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Santos-Dumont_flying_the_14_bis.jpg/260px-Santos-Dumont_flying_the_14_bis.jpg" decoding="async" width="260" height="163" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Santos-Dumont_flying_the_14_bis.jpg/390px-Santos-Dumont_flying_the_14_bis.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Santos-Dumont_flying_the_14_bis.jpg/520px-Santos-Dumont_flying_the_14_bis.jpg 2x" data-file-width="544" data-file-height="341" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Santos-Dumont_flying_the_14_bis.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Postcard showing the 14-bis</div></div></div>
<p>Santos-Dumont's final design were the <i><a href="/wiki/Santos-Dumont_Demoiselle" title="Santos-Dumont Demoiselle">Demoiselle</a></i> monoplanes (Nos. 19 to 22). These aircraft were used by Dumont for personal transport.  The <a href="/wiki/Fuselage" title="Fuselage">fuselage</a> consisted of three specially reinforced bamboo booms, and the pilot sat a seat between the main wheels of a <a href="/wiki/Tricycle" title="Tricycle">tricycle</a> <a href="/wiki/Landing_gear" title="Landing gear">landing gear</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Santos-Dumont_Demoiselle" title="Santos-Dumont Demoiselle">Demoiselle</a> was controlled in flight by a tail unit that functioned both as elevator and <a href="/wiki/Rudder" title="Rudder">rudder</a>, and by <a href="/wiki/Wing_warping" title="Wing warping">wing warping</a> (No. 20).
</p><p>In 1908 Santos-Dumont started working with <a href="/wiki/Adolphe_Cl%C3%A9ment" class="mw-redirect" title="Adolphe Clément">Adolphe Clément</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Adolphe_Cl%C3%A9ment#Aeroplane_manufacture" class="mw-redirect" title="Adolphe Clément">Clement-Bayard</a> company to mass-produce the <i>Demoiselle No 19</i>. They planned a production run of 100 units, built 50 but sold only 15, for 7,500 francs for each airframe. It was the world's first series production aircraft. By 1909 it was offered with a choice of three engines: Clement 20&#160;hp; Wright 4-cyl 30&#160;hp (Clement-Bayard had the license to manufacture Wright engines) and Clement-Bayard 40&#160;hp designed by Pierre Clerget. The <i>Demoiselle</i> could achieve a  speed of 120&#160;km/h.<sup id="cite_ref-Hydro_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hydro-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <i>Demoiselle</i> could be constructed in only 15 days. Possessing a good performance, flying at a speed of more than 100&#160;km/h, the <i>Demoiselle</i> was the last aircraft designed by Santos-Dumont. The June 1910 edition of the <i>Popular Mechanics</i> magazine published drawings of the <i>Demoiselle</i> and stated, "This machine is better than any other which has ever been built, for those who wish to reach results with the least possible expense and with a minimum of experimenting." American companies sold drawings and parts for <i>Demoiselles</i> for several years afterward.
</p>

<p>Santos-Dumont's final flight as a pilot was made in a <i>Demoiselle</i> on 4 January 1910. The flight ended when a bracing wire snapped at an altitude of about 25&#160;m (80&#160;ft), causing a wing to collapse. Santo-Dumont suffered only bruises.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 1910 Santos-Dumont announced that he was retiring from aviation. He secluded himself in his house and it was rumoured that he was suffering from a nervous breakdown caused by overwork, but it is probable that he was depressed about the <a href="/wiki/Multiple_sclerosis" title="Multiple sclerosis">multiple sclerosis</a> from which he was later known to suffer.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1911 he moved to the French seaside village of <a href="/wiki/Benerville-sur-Mer" title="Benerville-sur-Mer">Bénerville</a> (now Benerville-sur-Mer), where he took up astronomy as a hobby.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> After the outbreak of war in 1914 his German-made telescope and unusual accent led to accusations he was a German spy tracking French naval activity, and his rooms searched by French police. Upset by the allegation and depressed about his illness Santos-Dumont burned all his papers and plans.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>  For this reason there is little direct information available about his designs today. He spent much of the 1920s in Swiss and French sanatoria, though returning to Brazil at times.
</p><p>For his arrival in Brazil on the luxury liner <i><a href="/wiki/Cap_Arcona" class="mw-redirect" title="Cap Arcona">Cap Arcona</a></i> in 1928, a dozen members of the Brazilian scientific community boarded a seaplane with the intention of paying him a flying welcome. The plane crashed with the loss of all on board;<sup id="cite_ref-Hallion_p._93_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hallion_p._93-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Santos-Dumont's growing despondency deepened even further, and he returned to Switzerland.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Santos-Dumont&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1931 Santos-Dumont's nephew went to Switzerland and took him back to Brazil.
Seriously ill and said to be depressed both by his <a href="/wiki/Multiple_sclerosis" title="Multiple sclerosis">multiple sclerosis</a> and also the use of aircraft in warfare during São Paulo's <a href="/wiki/Constitutionalist_Revolution" title="Constitutionalist Revolution">Constitutionalist Revolution</a>, he hanged himself on 23&#160;July 1932, three days after his 59th birthday, in the city of <a href="/wiki/Guaruj%C3%A1" title="Guarujá">Guarujá</a><sup id="cite_ref-Hallion_p._93_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hallion_p._93-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> (although his death certificate gives the cause of death as "cardiac collapse").<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>After lying in state for two days in the crypt of São Paulo Cathedral, his body was taken to <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, where after a state funeral he was buried in the <a href="/wiki/Cemit%C3%A9rio_S%C3%A3o_Jo%C3%A3o_Batista" class="mw-redirect" title="Cemitério São João Batista">São João Batista Cemetery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
His heart is preserved in a golden globe at Brazil's National Air and Space Museum.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Private_life">Private life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Santos-Dumont&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Private life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aida-1903.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Aida-1903.jpg/260px-Aida-1903.jpg" decoding="async" width="260" height="177" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/51/Aida-1903.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="352" data-file-height="240" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aida-1903.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Aida_de_Acosta" title="Aida de Acosta">Aida de Acosta</a> piloting No. 9 in 1903.</div></div></div>
<p>Santos-Dumont, a lifelong bachelor, appeared to have a particular affection for a married Cuban-American woman named <a href="/wiki/Aida_de_Acosta" title="Aida de Acosta">Aida de Acosta</a>, who in 1903 became the only other person that he ever permitted to fly one of his airships&#160;&#8211;&#32;his No. 9. Until the end of his life, he kept a picture of her on his desk alongside a vase of fresh flowers. Nonetheless, there is no indication that Santos-Dumont and Acosta stayed in touch after her flight; upon his death she was reported as saying that she hardly knew him.
</p><p>Santos-Dumont is also known to not only have often used an equal sign (=) between his <a href="/wiki/Double-barreled_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Double-barreled name">two surnames</a> in place of a hyphen, but also seems to have preferred that practice, to display equal respect for his French and Brazilian-Portuguese ethnicities.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In Brazil, during 1918<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Santos-Dumont bought a small lot on the side of a hill in the city of <a href="/wiki/Petr%C3%B3polis" title="Petrópolis">Petrópolis</a>, in the mountains near Rio de Janeiro, and in 1918 built a small house there filled with imaginative mechanical gadgetry including an alcohol-fueled heated shower of his own design. The hill was purposefully chosen because of its great steepness as a proof that ingenuity could make it possible to build a comfortable house in that unlikely site. After building it, he used to spend his summers there to escape the heat in Rio, calling it <i><a href="/wiki/Santos_Dumont_House_Museum" title="Santos Dumont House Museum">A Encantada</a></i> ("The Enchanted") after its street, <i>Rua do Encanto</i>. The treads of the exterior stairs are hollowed alternately on the right and left, to enable people to climb them comfortably. The house is now a museum.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honors_and_legacy">Honors and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alberto_Santos-Dumont&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Honors and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is popularly believed in Brazil that Santos-Dumont preceded the <a href="/wiki/Wright_brothers" title="Wright brothers">Wright brothers</a> in demonstrating a practical airplane.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>1905 – Chevalier de la Légion d'Honneur</li>
<li>1909 – Officier de la Légion d'Honneur<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>1913 – Commandeur de la Légion d'Honneur</li>
<li>1929 – Grand Officier de la Légion d'Honneur<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In 1967, Santos-Dumont was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/International_Air_%26_Space_Hall_of_Fame" title="International Air &amp; Space Hall of Fame">International Air &amp; Space Hall of Fame</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Santos-Dumont_(crater)" title="Santos-Dumont (crater)">Santos-Dumont</a> is a small <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">lunar</a> <a href="/wiki/Impact_crater" title="Impact crater">impact crater</a> that lies in the northern end of the <a href="/wiki/Montes_Apenninus" title="Montes Apenninus">Montes Apenninus</a> range at the eastern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Mare_Imbrium" title="Mare Imbrium">Mare Imbrium</a></li>
<li>The aviator gives his name to the city of <a href="/wiki/Santos_Dumont,_Minas_Gerais" title="Santos Dumont, Minas Gerais">Santos Dumont</a>, in the state of <a href="/wiki/Minas_Gerais" title="Minas Gerais">Minas Gerais</a>, Brazil.  The <a href="/wiki/Cabangu_Museum" title="Cabangu Museum">Cabangu</a> farm, where he was born is in this municipality . The Faculdades Santos Dumont is a group of private higher learning colleges in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The city of <a href="/wiki/Dumont,_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Dumont, São Paulo">Dumont</a>, in the state of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Ribeir%C3%A3o_Preto" title="Ribeirão Preto">Ribeirão Preto</a> is so named because it is located where what used to be one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a> farms in the world, owned by Alberto Santos-Dumont's father. It was sold in 1896 to a British company, the <i>Dumont Coffee Company</i>.</li>
<li>Airports in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Santos_Dumont_Airport" title="Santos Dumont Airport">Santos Dumont Airport</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Paranagu%C3%A1" title="Paranaguá">Paranaguá</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Paranagu%C3%A1_Airport" title="Paranaguá Airport">Paranaguá Airport</a>) are named after him.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Rodovia_Santos_Dumont" title="Rodovia Santos Dumont">Rodovia Santos Dumont</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Highway" title="Highway">highway</a> in the state of São Paulo.</li>
<li>The Brazilian Air Force (Command of Aeronautics) awards the Santos Dumont Medal of Merit to important personalities in the world of aviation. The state government of Minas Gerais has a similar medal.</li>
<li>The <i>Réseau Santos Dumont</i> is a cooperative <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> network between France and Brazil, instituted by the French and Brazilian Ministries of Education in 1994, with 26 universities in each country.</li>
<li>The single <a href="/wiki/Bl%C3%A9riot_5190" title="Blériot 5190">Blériot 5190</a> flying boat, intended to carry out a transatlantic mail service to Brazil, was named <i>Santos-Dumont</i></li>
<li>The American <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Naval_Research" title="Office of Naval Research">Office of Naval Research</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> named one of its research airships the <i>600B Santos Dumont</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The Historic and Cultural Institute of Aeronautics of Brazil has instituted the Santos Dumont Annual Prize of Journalism for the best reports in the media about aeronautics.</li>
<li>The Lycée Polyvalent Santos Dumont is a lyceum in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Cloud" title="Saint-Cloud">Saint-Cloud</a>, France<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Santos-Dumont is mentioned as a pioneer of aviation, specifically in the area of dirigibles, in <a href="/wiki/Robert_A._Heinlein" title="Robert A. Heinlein">Robert A. Heinlein</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Job:_A_Comedy_of_Justice" title="Job: A Comedy of Justice">Job: A Comedy of Justice</a></i> (1984).</li>
<li>The official Brazilian presidential aircraft, an <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A319CJ#A319CJ" class="mw-redirect" title="Airbus A319CJ">Airbus Corporate Jet</a> tail number FAB2101, was named Alberto Santos Dumont.</li>
<li>H.G. Wells' "The Truth About Pyecraft" (1903) refers to Santos-Dumont's skill as an aviator.</li>
<li>The American aviation magazine <a href="/wiki/Flying_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Flying Magazine"><i>Flying</i></a> ranked Santos-Dumont eighth on its list of the "51 Heroes of Aviation" in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The opening ceremony of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympic_Games" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 Summer Olympic Games">2016 Summer Olympic Games</a> in Rio de Janeiro included a recreation of Santos-Dumont's 1906 flight.</li></ul>
