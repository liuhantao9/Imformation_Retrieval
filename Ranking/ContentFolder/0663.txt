<p>The <b>Bass Brewery</b> <span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;b&#39; in &#39;buy&#39;">b</span><span title="/æ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;bad&#39;">æ</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span></span>/</a></span></span> was founded in 1777 by <a href="/wiki/William_Bass_(brewer)" title="William Bass (brewer)">William Bass</a> in <a href="/wiki/Burton-upon-Trent" class="mw-redirect" title="Burton-upon-Trent">Burton-upon-Trent</a>, England.<sup id="cite_ref-history_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The main brand was Bass <a href="/wiki/Pale_ale#Burton_Pale_Ale" title="Pale ale">Pale Ale</a>, once the highest-selling beer in the <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-thisislondon.co.uk_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thisislondon.co.uk-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> By 1877, Bass had become the largest brewery in the world, with an annual output of one million barrels.<sup id="cite_ref-wordpress.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wordpress.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Its pale ale was exported throughout the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>, and the company's distinctive red triangle became the UK's first registered <a href="/wiki/Trade_mark" class="mw-redirect" title="Trade mark">trade mark</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bass took control of a number of other large breweries in the early 20th century, and in the 1960s merged with <a href="/wiki/Charringtons" class="mw-redirect" title="Charringtons">Charrington United Breweries</a> to become the largest UK brewing company, Bass Charrington.<sup id="cite_ref-history_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The brewing operations of the company were bought by <a href="/wiki/Interbrew" title="Interbrew">Interbrew</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch_InBev" title="Anheuser-Busch InBev">Anheuser-Busch InBev</a>) in 2000, while the retail side (hotel and pub holdings) were renamed <a href="/wiki/Six_Continents_plc" class="mw-redirect" title="Six Continents plc">Six Continents plc</a>. The UK government's <a href="/wiki/Competition_Commission" title="Competition Commission">Competition Commission</a> was concerned about the <a href="/wiki/Competition_law" title="Competition law">monopoly</a> implications arising from the deal, and instructed Interbrew to dispose of the brewery and certain brands (<a href="/wiki/Carling" class="mw-redirect" title="Carling">Carling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Worthington_Draught_bitter" class="mw-redirect" title="Worthington Draught bitter">Worthington</a>) to <a href="/wiki/Coors_Brewing_Company" title="Coors Brewing Company">Coors</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Molson_Coors_Brewing_Company" title="Molson Coors Brewing Company">Molson Coors Brewing Company</a>), but allowed Interbrew to retain the rights to the Bass Pale Ale brand.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, it was widely reported that AB-InBev are attempting to sell the rights to the Bass brand in the UK for around £10 million to £15 million.<sup id="cite_ref-thisislondon.co.uk_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thisislondon.co.uk-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Draught Bass (4.4% <a href="/wiki/ABV" class="mw-redirect" title="ABV">ABV</a>) has been brewed under contract in Burton by <a href="/wiki/Marston%27s_plc" class="mw-redirect" title="Marston&#39;s plc">Marston's</a> (formerly a relatively minor competitor) for <a href="/wiki/AB-InBev" class="mw-redirect" title="AB-InBev">AB-InBev</a> since 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Bottled and <a href="/wiki/Keg_beer" class="mw-redirect" title="Keg beer">keg</a> products are brewed at AB-InBev's own brewery in <a href="/wiki/Samlesbury#Samlesbury_brewery" title="Samlesbury">Samlesbury</a> for export, except in the United States and Belgium, where Bass is brewed locally.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Bass Ale is a top ten premium canned ale in the UK, with 16,080 hectolitres sold in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1700s">1700s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1700s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prior to establishing a brewery, <a href="/wiki/William_Bass_(brewer)" title="William Bass (brewer)">William Bass</a> transported ale for brewer Benjamin Printon. Bass sold this <a href="/wiki/Common_carrier" title="Common carrier">carrier</a> business to the <a href="/wiki/Pickfords" title="Pickfords">Pickford</a> family, using the funds to establish Bass &amp; Co Brewery in 1777 as one of the first <a href="/wiki/Brewers_of_Burton" title="Brewers of Burton">breweries in Burton-upon-Trent</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1800s">1800s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1800s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Early in the company's history, Bass was exporting bottled beer around the world, serving the <a href="/wiki/Baltic_sea" class="mw-redirect" title="Baltic sea">Baltic</a> region through the port of <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Hull</a>. Growing demand led his son <a href="/wiki/Michael_Thomas_Bass_(senior)" class="mw-redirect" title="Michael Thomas Bass (senior)">Michael Thomas Bass (senior)</a>, to build a second brewery in Burton in 1799 in  partnership with John Ratcliff. The water produced from local boreholes became popular with brewers, with 30 operating there by the mid-19th century. His son, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Thomas_Bass" title="Michael Thomas Bass">Michael Thomas Bass</a>, succeeded on his father's death in 1827, renewed the Ratcliff partnership, brought in John Gretton, and created 'Bass, Ratcliff and Gretton'.
</p><p>The opening of a railway through Burton in 1839<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> redoubled Burton's preeminence as a brewing town. In the mid-1870s, Bass, Ratcliff and Gretton accounted for one third of Burton's output.  A strong export business allowed Bass to boast their product was available "in every country in the globe".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> By 1877, Bass was the largest brewery in the world, with an annual output of one million barrels.<sup id="cite_ref-wordpress.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wordpress.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Following the death of the second Michael Bass in 1884, his son Michael Arthur Bass, later the <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bass,_1st_Baron_Burton" title="Michael Bass, 1st Baron Burton">1st Baron Burton</a>, took the reins.  Both Michael Thomas Bass and his son Lord Burton were philanthropically inclined, making extensive charitable donations to the towns of Burton and <a href="/wiki/Derby" title="Derby">Derby</a>. The annual <a href="/wiki/The_Bass_Excursions" title="The Bass Excursions">Bass excursions</a>, laid on by the firm for its employees, were the largest operation of its type in the world by a private company. The brewer became a public limited company in 1888.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bass' No. 1 Ale was the first beer to be marketed as <a href="/wiki/Barley_wine" title="Barley wine">barley wine</a>, around 1870.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_1900s">Early 1900s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early 1900s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Early in the 20th century, a declining market closed many Burton breweries, 20 in 1900 falling to eight in 1928. Bass took over Walkers in 1923, and <a href="/wiki/Worthington_Draught_bitter" class="mw-redirect" title="Worthington Draught bitter">Worthington</a> in 1927. They also acquired <a href="/wiki/Salt%27s_Brewery" class="mw-redirect" title="Salt&#39;s Brewery">Thomas Salt</a> in 1927, which was founded in 1774 by Joseph Clay, for over £1,000,000.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mid-1900s">Mid-1900s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mid-1900s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bass was one of the original London Stock Exchange <a href="/wiki/FT_30" title="FT 30">FT 30</a> companies when the listing was established in 1935.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Over the next half-century, Bass maintained its UK dominance through acquiring such brewers as <a href="/wiki/Mitchells_%26_Butlers_Brewery" title="Mitchells &amp; Butlers Brewery">Mitchells &amp; Butlers</a> (1961), <a href="/wiki/Charrington_Brewery" title="Charrington Brewery">Charringtons</a> (1967), Bents-Gartsides (1967), John Joule &amp; Sons (1968), <a href="/wiki/William_Stones_Ltd" class="mw-redirect" title="William Stones Ltd">William Stones Ltd</a> (1968), and <a href="/wiki/Grimsby" title="Grimsby">Grimsby</a>'s Hewitt Brothers Ltd (1969), being variously known as <i>Bass, Mitchells and Butlers</i> or <i>Bass Charrington</i>. Draught Bass ale and Worthingon "E" were merged to become the same product until Bass became preferred as the name of the cask beer and Worthington for keg, although some pubs resisted this distinction. 
</p><p>Bass had been reliant on railways to distribute its beer from Burton and owned the country's largest private rail network within the town linking its various premises. From the 1970's it followed the trend to abandon the use of rail freight which had become notoriously unreliable. The switch to road haulage required local transport depots, and in many places small independent breweries were bought and repurposed as such. At this time, along with the other major brewers which now dominated the industry, Bass were moving away from the production of traditional ales in favour of keg beer and particularly Carling lager at Warrington, ignoring opposition from <a href="/wiki/CAMRA" class="mw-redirect" title="CAMRA">CAMRA</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Late_20th_Century">Late 20th Century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Late 20th Century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1988, Bass acquired the rights to franchise the <a href="/wiki/Holiday_Inn" title="Holiday Inn">Holiday Inn</a> name outside of North and South America and in 1989 went on purchase the Holiday Inn hotel chain from <a href="/wiki/Holiday_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Holiday Corporation">Holiday Corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following decades of closures and consolidation, Bass was left by the end of the 20th century with one of the two large remaining breweries in Burton. The brewing operations of the company were bought by <a href="/wiki/Interbrew" title="Interbrew">Interbrew</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch_InBev" title="Anheuser-Busch InBev">Anheuser-Busch InBev</a>) in 2000, while the retail side (hotel and pub holdings) were renamed <a href="/wiki/Six_Continents_plc" class="mw-redirect" title="Six Continents plc">Six Continents plc</a>. The UK government's <a href="/wiki/Competition_Commission" title="Competition Commission">Competition Commission</a> was concerned about the monopoly implications arising from the deal, and instructed Interbrew to dispose of the brewery and certain brands (<a href="/wiki/Carling" class="mw-redirect" title="Carling">Carling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Worthington_Draught_bitter" class="mw-redirect" title="Worthington Draught bitter">Worthington</a> ) to <a href="/wiki/Coors_Brewing_Company" title="Coors Brewing Company">Coors</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Molson_Coors_Brewing_Company" title="Molson Coors Brewing Company">Molson Coors Brewing Company</a>), but allowed Interbrew to retain the rights to the Bass Pale Ale brand.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mitchells_%26_Butlers" title="Mitchells &amp; Butlers">Mitchells &amp; Butlers</a> lives on as the assignee of the licensed retail outlet business which separated from Six Continents.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Separation_of_brewery_and_beer">Separation of brewery and beer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Separation of brewery and beer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bottles_of_Bass_beer.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Bottles_of_Bass_beer.JPG/220px-Bottles_of_Bass_beer.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="182" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Bottles_of_Bass_beer.JPG/330px-Bottles_of_Bass_beer.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Bottles_of_Bass_beer.JPG/440px-Bottles_of_Bass_beer.JPG 2x" data-file-width="724" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bottles_of_Bass_beer.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bottles of Bass beer for sale at a liquor store in <a href="/wiki/Iizaka,_Fukushima" title="Iizaka, Fukushima">Iizaka</a>, Fukushima, Japan</div></div></div>
<p>Bass plc's brewing business was bought by the Belgian brewer <a href="/wiki/Interbrew" title="Interbrew">Interbrew</a> (now <a href="/wiki/AB-InBev" class="mw-redirect" title="AB-InBev">AB-InBev</a>) in June 2000, its  hotel and pub holdings absorbed and renamed <a href="/wiki/Six_Continents_plc" class="mw-redirect" title="Six Continents plc">Six Continents plc</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Competition_Commission" title="Competition Commission">Competition Commission</a> raised potential monopoly concerns,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Interbrew disposed of Bass Brewers Limited's <a href="/wiki/Carling" class="mw-redirect" title="Carling">Carling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Worthington_Draught_bitter" class="mw-redirect" title="Worthington Draught bitter">Worthington</a> brands to <a href="/wiki/Coors_Brewing_Company" title="Coors Brewing Company">Coors</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Molson_Coors_Brewing_Company" title="Molson Coors Brewing Company">Molson Coors Brewing Company</a>), but retained the rights to the Bass beer brand.
</p><p>The beer was produced under licence by Coors, which acquired the Bass brewery facility. Bass Brewers Limited was renamed <a href="/wiki/Coors_Brewers" title="Coors Brewers">Coors Brewers Limited</a>. When the licence to brew draught Bass came to an end in 2005, it was taken up by <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_%26_Dudley_Breweries_PLC" class="mw-redirect" title="Wolverhampton &amp; Dudley Breweries PLC">Wolverhampton &amp; Dudley Breweries PLC</a>, which started production at Burton's <a href="/wiki/Marston%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="Marston&#39;s">Marston's</a> Brewery. Bottled and keg Bass formerly exported to the USA with a higher alcohol content are now produced there domestically by <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch" title="Anheuser-Busch">Anheuser-Busch</a> at a <a href="/wiki/Baldwinsville,_New_York" title="Baldwinsville, New York">Baldwinsville</a>, New York, facility.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Brewery_museum">Brewery museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Brewery museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/National_Brewery_Centre" title="National Brewery Centre">National Brewery Centre</a></div>
<p>Sited next to the brewery, the Bass Museum of Brewing (later renamed the Coors Visitor Centre &amp; The Museum of Brewing), was Burton-upon-Trent's largest tourist attraction until closed by Coors in June 2008. A steering group was established to investigate re-opening,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and the museum was relaunched in May 2010 as the National Brewery Centre.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Branding">Branding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Branding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Edouard_Manet,_A_Bar_at_the_Folies-Berg%C3%A8re.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Edouard_Manet%2C_A_Bar_at_the_Folies-Berg%C3%A8re.jpg/220px-Edouard_Manet%2C_A_Bar_at_the_Folies-Berg%C3%A8re.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="164" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Edouard_Manet%2C_A_Bar_at_the_Folies-Berg%C3%A8re.jpg/330px-Edouard_Manet%2C_A_Bar_at_the_Folies-Berg%C3%A8re.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Edouard_Manet%2C_A_Bar_at_the_Folies-Berg%C3%A8re.jpg/440px-Edouard_Manet%2C_A_Bar_at_the_Folies-Berg%C3%A8re.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2000" data-file-height="1493" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Edouard_Manet,_A_Bar_at_the_Folies-Berg%C3%A8re.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bottles of Bass on the bar in Manet's 1882 <i><a href="/wiki/A_Bar_at_the_Folies-Berg%C3%A8re" title="A Bar at the Folies-Bergère">A Bar at the Folies-Bergère</a></i></div></div></div>
<p>Bass was a pioneer in international <a href="/wiki/Brand_marketing" class="mw-redirect" title="Brand marketing">brand marketing</a>. "Many years before 1855"<sup id="cite_ref-1899courtcase_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1899courtcase-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Bass applied a red triangle to casks of its Pale Ale. After 1855 the triangles were red, white or blue depending on which of three breweries it came from, but all bottles of Pale Ale had the red triangle from that date.<sup id="cite_ref-1899courtcase_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1899courtcase-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The blue triangle was briefly revived after World War II for Pale Ale that wasn't bottle conditioned. The Bass Red Triangle was the first <a href="/wiki/Trade_mark" class="mw-redirect" title="Trade mark">trade mark</a> to be registered under the UK's <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_trade_mark_law" title="United Kingdom trade mark law">Trade Marks Registration Act 1875</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The Act came into effect on 1 January 1876 and legend has it that a Bass employee queued overnight outside the registrar's office on New Year's Eve in order to be the first in line to register a trade mark the next morning. As a result, Bass, Ratcliff &amp; Gretton Limited received the first two registrations, the Bass Red Triangle for their pale ale and the Bass Red Diamond next for their strong ale.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The trade marks are now owned by Brandbrew SA, an InBev subsidiary based in <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In June 2013 InBev renamed Bass Pale Ale as Bass Trademark No.1.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bottles of Bass with the Red Triangle logo have occasionally appeared in art and literature, including  <a href="/wiki/%C3%89douard_Manet" title="Édouard Manet">Édouard Manet</a>'s 1882 painting <i><a href="/wiki/A_Bar_at_the_Folies-Berg%C3%A8re" title="A Bar at the Folies-Bergère">A Bar at the Folies-Bergère</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> and in over 40 paintings by <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Picasso" title="Pablo Picasso">Picasso</a>, mostly at the height of his <a href="/wiki/Cubism" title="Cubism">Cubist</a> period around 1914.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In the "Oxen of the Sun" episode of <a href="/wiki/James_Joyce" title="James Joyce">James Joyce</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Ulysses_(novel)" title="Ulysses (novel)">Ulysses</a></i>, Bloom observes the Bass logo.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Versions_of_Bass">Versions of Bass</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Versions of Bass">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Draught_Bass">Draught Bass</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Draught Bass">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Draught Bass is a 4.4% ABV <a href="/wiki/Cask_conditioned" class="mw-redirect" title="Cask conditioned">cask conditioned</a> beer. Most prevalent near its <a href="/wiki/Burton_upon_Trent" title="Burton upon Trent">Burton upon Trent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a> heartlands,<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> it is brewed by <a href="/wiki/Marston%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="Marston&#39;s">Marston's</a> in Burton in <a href="/wiki/Brewing_methods#Yorkshire_Square" title="Brewing methods">Yorkshire Squares</a> using English hops and <a href="/wiki/Dry_hopping" class="mw-redirect" title="Dry hopping">dry hopping</a><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> and is described as "a classic ale with a malty, fruity, nutty aroma and a complex, satisfying flavour".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bass_Ale">Bass Ale</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Bass Ale">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This is the exported version of Bass, usually brewed to around 5% ABV.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="UK_keg_ales">UK keg ales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: UK keg ales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><b>Bass Extra Smooth</b> - A 3.6% ABV pasteurised keg version of Bass, brewed to the same recipe, and most popular in the <a href="/wiki/South_West_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="South West of England">South West of England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Bass Mild XXXX</b> - A 3.1% ABV keg mild.<sup id="cite_ref-onestopshop.molsoncoorsdirect.com_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-onestopshop.molsoncoorsdirect.com-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Bass Best Scotch</b> - A 3.4% ABV keg beer in the <a href="/wiki/North_East_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="North East of England">North East of England</a> <a href="/wiki/Scotch_ale" class="mw-redirect" title="Scotch ale">Scotch ale</a> style.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> It was formerly brewed to 3.8% following its launch in 1986.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shandy_Bass">Shandy Bass</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Shandy Bass">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Shandy Bass</b> is a 0.5% <a href="/wiki/ABV" class="mw-redirect" title="ABV">ABV</a> <a href="/wiki/Shandy" title="Shandy">shandy</a> made with Bass beer and lemonade.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Introduced in 1972, it is made by <a href="/wiki/Britvic" title="Britvic">Britvic</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bass_overseas">Bass overseas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Bass overseas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Belgium">Belgium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Belgium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bass Pale Ale has been brewed under licence in Belgium since the Interbrew takeover. It is typically sold in 25cl bottles at 5.2% ABV.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Draught Bass has been exported to America since at least 1966.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> In 2001, 66,500,000 litres of Bass were sold in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Euromonitor,_2011_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Euromonitor,_2011-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> However Bass seems to have suffered under the custodianship of <a href="/wiki/InBev" title="InBev">InBev</a> and later <a href="/wiki/Anheuser-Busch_InBev" title="Anheuser-Busch InBev">Anheuser-Busch InBev</a> as it is undergoing heavy decline in American consumption, with 24,200,000 litres sold in the country in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-Euromonitor,_2011_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Euromonitor,_2011-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> AB InBev have pledged funding to support the Bass brand in America, and since June 2012, Bass has been brewed in <a href="/wiki/Merrimack,_New_Hampshire" title="Merrimack, New Hampshire">Merrimack, New Hampshire</a> at 5% ABV for the American market.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">Elsewhere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bass_Brewery&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1860, Bass was the first foreign beer to be sold in Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
