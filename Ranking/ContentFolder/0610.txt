<p><b>Arriva Yorkshire</b><sup id="cite_ref-company84167_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company84167-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a bus operator providing services in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Riding of Yorkshire</a> and the southern areas of <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>. It is a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Arriva_UK_Bus" title="Arriva UK Bus">Arriva UK Bus</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arriva_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:West_Riding_Buses_bus_252_(C920_FMP),_Showbus_rally_2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/West_Riding_Buses_bus_252_%28C920_FMP%29%2C_Showbus_rally_2009.jpg/220px-West_Riding_Buses_bus_252_%28C920_FMP%29%2C_Showbus_rally_2009.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/West_Riding_Buses_bus_252_%28C920_FMP%29%2C_Showbus_rally_2009.jpg/330px-West_Riding_Buses_bus_252_%28C920_FMP%29%2C_Showbus_rally_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/West_Riding_Buses_bus_252_%28C920_FMP%29%2C_Showbus_rally_2009.jpg/440px-West_Riding_Buses_bus_252_%28C920_FMP%29%2C_Showbus_rally_2009.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:West_Riding_Buses_bus_252_(C920_FMP),_Showbus_rally_2009.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Preserved <a href="/wiki/Leyland_Lynx" title="Leyland Lynx">Leyland Lynx</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_Automobile_Company" title="West Riding Automobile Company">West Riding Buses</a> livery in September 2009</div></div></div>

<p>Arriva Yorkshire was formed as a combination of mergers of previous companies based in West and North Yorkshire.
</p><p>In 1904 Yorkshire (West Riding) Electric Tramways began operating tram services in <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a> followed in 1906 by <a href="/wiki/Castleford" title="Castleford">Castleford</a>. In November 1923 the West Riding Automobile Company began operating bus services in <a href="/wiki/West_Riding" class="mw-redirect" title="West Riding">West Riding</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ArrivaHistory_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ArrivaHistory-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yorkshire Woollen District Transport<sup id="cite_ref-company2284553_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company2284553-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> operated services around <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ArrivaHistory_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ArrivaHistory-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Both companies were acquired by the <a href="/wiki/National_Bus_Company_(UK)" title="National Bus Company (UK)">National Bus Company</a>, along with Selby &amp; District<sup id="cite_ref-company2225132_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company2225132-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> with the companies maintaining separate identities. In 1987 West Riding Automobile and Yorkshire Woollen District were sold in a <a href="/wiki/Management_buyout" title="Management buyout">management buyout</a> to Caldaire,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> under whose ownership they traded as West Riding Buses and Yorkshire Buses respectively. In 1994 South Yorkshire Road Transport, based in <a href="/wiki/Pontefract" title="Pontefract">Pontefract</a>, was purchased.<sup id="cite_ref-ArrivaHistory_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ArrivaHistory-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The four companies were taken over by <a href="/wiki/British_Bus" title="British Bus">British Bus</a> in 1995<sup id="cite_ref-ArrivaHistory_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ArrivaHistory-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> which itself was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Arriva" title="Arriva">Cowie Group</a> in August 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> All were rebranded under the Arriva brand in 1997.
</p><p>Although Arriva Yorkshire trades as one entity, it is formed of three companies: Arriva Yorkshire West<sup id="cite_ref-company2284553_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company2284553-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> (the former Yorkshire Woollen District company), Arriva Yorkshire<sup id="cite_ref-company84167_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company84167-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (the former West Riding and South Yorkshire Road Transport companies) and Arriva Yorkshire North<sup id="cite_ref-company2225132_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company2225132-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> (the former Selby &amp; District company).
</p><p>Since May 2008 there has been a sister company in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>. <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Centrebus Holdings</a>, in which Arriva held a 40% stake, was formed when the Huddersfield operations of <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_Yorkshire" title="Stagecoach Yorkshire">Stagecoach Yorkshire</a> was purchased along with the separate <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger#Tiger_Blue" title="Yorkshire Tiger">K-Line</a> bus company.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In September 2013, Arriva took full ownership of Centrebus Holdings and K-Line with the former rebranded <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Yorkshire Tiger</a> and the latter as <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger#Tiger_Blue" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Tiger Blue</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Brands">Brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arriva_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Brands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Arriva_Yorkshire_Bus_Number_1514.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Arriva_Yorkshire_Bus_Number_1514.jpg/220px-Arriva_Yorkshire_Bus_Number_1514.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Arriva_Yorkshire_Bus_Number_1514.jpg/330px-Arriva_Yorkshire_Bus_Number_1514.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Arriva_Yorkshire_Bus_Number_1514.jpg/440px-Arriva_Yorkshire_Bus_Number_1514.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5328" data-file-height="4000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Arriva_Yorkshire_Bus_Number_1514.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A MAX branded bus in <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a></div></div></div>
<p>Arriva Yorkshire uses several different brands to differentiate their services. <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Sapphire" title="Arriva Sapphire">Sapphire</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> a premium brand, uses buses fitted with E-leather seats, free WiFi access, charging points, and audible and visual stop announcements <sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>, as well as dedicated drivers for each route. <sup id="cite_ref-:0_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Max" title="Arriva Max">MAX</a> is similar to Sapphire, also using buses fitted with WiFi and E-leather seating.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In Yorkshire, routes 126 and 127, from Dewsbury to Wakefield, 202 and 203, from Dewsbury to Leeds, 268, Wakefield to Bradford, and 229, Huddersfield to Leeds are MAX branded,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> whilst routes 110, from Hall Green and Wakefield to Leeds, 163 and 166, from Castleford to Leeds, and route 415, from Selby to York, are branded as Sapphire.
</p><p>The third sub brand in use is Frequenta, which designates high frequency routes. Arriva Yorkshire currently use this on routes 148 and 149, running between Wakefield, Pontefract and Knottingley,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> with services running every 10 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discrimination">Discrimination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arriva_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Discrimination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A widely reported case in 2008 concerned a <a href="/wiki/Goth_subculture" title="Goth subculture">gothic</a> couple, Dani Graves and his fiancée Tasha Maltby, who wears a dog collar and lead.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> A driver had refused them travel and made comments to them, allegedly saying "We don't let freaks and dogs like you on."
</p><p>Arriva Yorkshire has said "We take any allegations of discrimination very seriously" and is investigating the claims and the couple's complaints.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The company confirmed the couple were refused travel on two occasions due to "fears for passenger safety". In a statement the company addressed the issue, claiming that the dog lead was potentially dangerous. Arriva also said they would be writing to Mr Graves "to apologise for any distress caused by the way this matter was handled".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleet">Fleet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arriva_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fleet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of December 2017<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Arriva_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> the fleet consisted of 334 buses.<sup id="cite_ref-Fleetlist_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fleetlist-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
