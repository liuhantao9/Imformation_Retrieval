<p><b>Aussie Rules UK (ARUK)</b> was established in London as a pilot project for the international development of <a href="/wiki/Australian_rules_football" title="Australian rules football">Australian rules football</a>. Commencing in April 2005, ARUK grew rapidly with over 10,000 children participating in its flagship program, Aussie Rules Schools.
</p><p>From 2007 until 2009, Aussie Rules UK also ran a senior competition, entitled the Aussie Rules UK National League. All leagues are now run by, and affiliated to AFL England.
</p><p>It was an initiative of <a href="/wiki/Australian_Football_International" title="Australian Football International">Australian Football International</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_Achievements">Major Achievements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aussie_Rules_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Major Achievements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aussie Rules UK also established many firsts for <a href="/wiki/Australian_rules_football" title="Australian rules football">Australian rules football</a> internationally with major achievements including:
</p>
<ul><li>Inclusion in the <a href="/wiki/London_Youth_Games" title="London Youth Games">London Youth Games</a> (Europe's largest junior sports tournament)</li>
<li>Hosting the inaugural <a href="/wiki/EU_Cup" title="EU Cup">EU Cup</a> (European Championship) in London</li>
<li>1st Women's match in Europe – Anzac Sports Challenge London</li>
<li>1st international junior match in Europe (England v Denmark, Copenhagen)</li>
<li>Establishment of the Dragon Cup (England v Wales)</li>
<li>Establishment of the Kangaroo Cup (England v Irish Exiles)</li>
<li>Aussie Rules Gala Day in London (involving 300 children from 19 schools)</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior_Development">Senior Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aussie_Rules_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Senior Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Aussie Rules UK National League was launched in 2007 featuring three <a href="/wiki/9-a-side_footy" class="mw-redirect" title="9-a-side footy">9-a-side</a> local leagues located in northeastern England, southern England and Wales.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern">Northern</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aussie_Rules_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Northern">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Northern Division began with four teams, the Middlesbrough Hawks, Hartlepool Dockers, Durham Swans and Newcastle Centurions.  In 2009, new team Gateshead Miners entered the league, whilst the Durham club changed its name to the Durham Saints.  The league has now become the AFL Britain North East Division.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Southern">Southern</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aussie_Rules_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Southern">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Southern Division initially featured the Southampton Titans, Bournemouth Demons, Thanet Bombers and two sides affiliated with the London Swans, one as the Brighton Black Swans, and the second keeping the club's original name Sussex Swans and playing in the club's original home of <a href="/wiki/Crawley" title="Crawley">Crawley</a>.  Thanet folded after the 2007 season, but were replaced in 2008 by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chippenham_Redbacks&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Chippenham Redbacks (page does not exist)">Chippenham Redbacks</a>, a new club who were based largely around players from the recently defunct Swindon Devils.  The league gained new side the <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_Pirates" title="Portsmouth Pirates">Portsmouth Pirates</a> for 2009, but lost Brighton.  The league is now the AFL Britain Southern Division. In 2011 the league grew again to six teams with the addition of the <a href="/wiki/Guildford_Crows_Aussie_Rules_FC" title="Guildford Crows Aussie Rules FC">Guildford Crows Aussie Rules FC</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Welsh">Welsh</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aussie_Rules_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Welsh">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Welsh division began with four sides in south Wales and the Swindon Devils from England's <a href="/wiki/West_Country" title="West Country">West Country</a>, but the Welsh sides struggled initially for player numbers in 2007 and games were held irregularly.  2008 saw the league restructure as an organisation called the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_Australian_Rules_Football_League" title="Welsh Australian Rules Football League">Welsh Australian Rules Football League</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Central">Central</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aussie_Rules_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Central">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Central Division began play in 2009, with the <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_Bears" title="Birmingham Bears">Birmingham Bears</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Rams_Aussie_Rules" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Rams Aussie Rules">Huddersfield Rams</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Leeds_Bombers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Leeds Bombers (page does not exist)">Leeds Bombers</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Liverpool_Eagles&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Liverpool Eagles (page does not exist)">Liverpool Eagles</a> competing.  Some of these clubs still compete in the AFL Britain Central &amp; North West Division, together with former BARFL Social League clubs Manchester Mosquitoes and Nottingham Scorpions and new clubs Wolverhampton Wolverines, Hull Mariners and Sheffield Thunder.
</p><p><br />
</p>
