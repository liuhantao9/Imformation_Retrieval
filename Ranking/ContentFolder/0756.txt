<p>The <b>Lawrence Batley Theatre</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Theatre" title="Theatre">theatre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> which offers <a href="/wiki/Drama" title="Drama">drama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dance" title="Dance">dance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Comedy" title="Comedy">comedy</a>.
</p><p>The theatre is named after <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Batley" title="Lawrence Batley">Lawrence Batley</a>, a local entrepreneur and philanthropist, who founded a nationwide cash and carry chain.
</p><p>The building was originally built in 1819 as a <a href="/wiki/Methodism" title="Methodism">Methodist</a> <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">chapel</a>, called the Queen Street Chapel.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The architect is unknown but the chief mason was Joseph Kaye, the man who was also responsible for Huddersfield station.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was opened on 9 July 1819 and the reporter in the Leeds Mercury described it as "one of the most handsome and commodious chapels in the kingdom; being capable of accommodating 3000 persons, and has been erected at an expense of from 8 to £10,000".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The chapel became a <a href="/wiki/Mission_(Christian)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mission (Christian)">mission</a> in 1906 until a decline in numbers saw the mission move out of the building in 1970 to a new building in King Street.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1973 the building was converted into an <a href="/wiki/Arts_centre" title="Arts centre">arts centre</a>.  However serious structural problems were discovered by Kirklees Metropolitan Council in 1975 and the Arts Centre was rehoused into Venn Street Arts Centre and the building remained vacant before being sublet to Novosquash Limited and converted to a <a href="/wiki/Squash_(sport)" title="Squash (sport)">squash club</a> known as The Ridings.  It also housed a <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">restaurant</a> and a The Catacombs <a href="/wiki/Disco" title="Disco">Disco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1989 the Kirkless Theatre Trust was given the go ahead to save the building from deterioration and launch Huddersfield's newest theatre. Building work for the theatre started in September 1992 and took 4 years to complete.
</p>
