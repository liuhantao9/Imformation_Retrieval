<p><i><b>The Independent</b></i> is a British <a href="/wiki/Online_newspaper" title="Online newspaper">online newspaper</a><sup id="cite_ref-BBC_ind_stop_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_ind_stop-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> established in 1986 as a politically independent national morning newspaper published in London. It was controlled by <a href="/wiki/Tony_O%27Reilly" title="Tony O&#39;Reilly">Tony O'Reilly</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Independent_News_%26_Media" title="Independent News &amp; Media">Independent News &amp; Media</a> from 1997 until it was sold to <a href="/wiki/Russian_oligarch" title="Russian oligarch">Russian oligarch</a> <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lebedev" title="Alexander Lebedev">Alexander Lebedev</a> in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-i2010-03-25_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-i2010-03-25-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The last printed edition of <i>The Independent</i> was published on Saturday 26 March 2016, leaving only its digital editions.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_ind_stop_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_ind_stop-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nicknamed the <i>Indy</i>, it began as a <a href="/wiki/Broadsheet" title="Broadsheet">broadsheet</a>, but changed to <a href="/wiki/Tabloid_(newspaper_format)" title="Tabloid (newspaper format)">tabloid</a> format in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Until September 2011, the paper described itself on the banner at the top of every newspaper as "free from party political bias, free from proprietorial influence".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It tends to take a pro-market stance on economic issues.<sup id="cite_ref-Wilby_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wilby-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The daily edition was named <i>National Newspaper of the Year</i> at the 2004 <a href="/wiki/British_Press_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="British Press Awards">British Press Awards</a>.
</p><p>In June 2015, it had an average daily circulation of just below 58,000, 85 per cent down from its 1990 peak, while the Sunday edition had a circulation of just over 97,000.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_ind_stop_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_ind_stop-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1986_to_1990">1986 to 1990</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1986 to 1990">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Launched in 1986, the first issue of <i>The Independent</i> was published on 7 October in <a href="/wiki/Broadsheet" title="Broadsheet">broadsheet</a> format.<sup id="cite_ref-Griffiths_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Griffiths-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It was produced by Newspaper Publishing plc and created by <a href="/wiki/Andreas_Whittam_Smith" title="Andreas Whittam Smith">Andreas Whittam Smith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Glover_(columnist)" title="Stephen Glover (columnist)">Stephen Glover</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Symonds" title="Matthew Symonds">Matthew Symonds</a>. All three partners were former journalists at <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i> who had left the paper towards the end of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Berry,_Baron_Hartwell" title="Michael Berry, Baron Hartwell">Lord Hartwell</a>'s ownership. <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Sieff" class="mw-redirect" title="Marcus Sieff">Marcus Sieff</a> was the first chairman of Newspaper Publishing, and Whittam Smith took control of the paper.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The paper was created at a time of a fundamental change in British newspaper publishing. <a href="/wiki/Rupert_Murdoch" title="Rupert Murdoch">Rupert Murdoch</a> was challenging long-accepted practices of the print unions and ultimately defeated them in the <a href="/wiki/Wapping_dispute" title="Wapping dispute">Wapping dispute</a>. Consequently, production costs could be reduced which, it was said at the time,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (October 2016)">by whom?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> created openings for more competition. As a result of controversy around Murdoch's move to Wapping, the plant was effectively having to function under siege from sacked print workers picketing outside. <i>The Independent</i> attracted some of the staff from the two Murdoch broadsheets who had chosen not to move to his company's new headquarters. Launched with the advertising slogan "It is. Are you?", and challenging both <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> for centre-left readers and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> as the newspaper of record, <i>The Independent</i> reached a circulation of over 400,000 by 1989.
</p><p>Competing in a moribund market, <i>The Independent</i> sparked a general freshening of newspaper design as well as, within a few years, a price war in the market sector.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> When <i>The Independent</i> launched <i>The Independent on Sunday</i> in 1990, sales were less than anticipated, partly due to the launch of the <i><a href="/wiki/Sunday_Correspondent" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunday Correspondent">Sunday Correspondent</a></i> four months prior, although this direct rival closed at the end of November 1990. Some aspects of production merged with the main paper, although the Sunday paper retained a largely distinct editorial staff.
</p>
<h3><span id="1990.E2.80.931999"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1990–1999">1990–1999</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1990–1999">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 1990s, <i>The Independent</i> was faced with price cutting by the Murdoch titles, and started an advertising campaign accusing <i>The Times</i> and <i>The Daily Telegraph</i> of reflecting the views of their proprietors, Rupert Murdoch and <a href="/wiki/Conrad_Black" title="Conrad Black">Conrad Black</a>. It featured spoofs of the other papers' <a href="/wiki/Masthead_(publishing)" title="Masthead (publishing)">mastheads</a> with the words <i>The Rupert Murdoch</i> or <i>The Conrad Black</i>, with <i>The Independent</i> below the main title.
</p><p>Newspaper Publishing had financial problems. A number of other media companies were interested in the paper. <a href="/wiki/Tony_O%27Reilly" title="Tony O&#39;Reilly">Tony O'Reilly</a>'s media group and <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Mirror" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity Mirror">Mirror Group Newspapers</a> (MGN) had bought a stake of about a third each by mid-1994. In March 1995, Newspaper Publishing was restructured with a rights issue, splitting the shareholding into O'Reilly's <a href="/wiki/Independent_News_%26_Media" title="Independent News &amp; Media">Independent News &amp; Media</a> (43%), <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Mirror" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinity Mirror">MGN</a> (43%), and <a href="/wiki/PRISA" title="PRISA">Prisa</a> (publisher of <i><a href="/wiki/El_Pa%C3%ADs" title="El País">El País</a></i>) (12%).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 1996, there was another refinancing, and in March 1998, O'Reilly bought the other shares of the company for £30&#160;million, and assumed the company's debt. Brendan Hopkins headed Independent News, <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Marr" title="Andrew Marr">Andrew Marr</a> was appointed editor of <i>The Independent</i>, and <a href="/wiki/Rosie_Boycott" class="mw-redirect" title="Rosie Boycott">Rosie Boycott</a> became editor of <i>The Independent on Sunday</i>. Marr introduced a dramatic if short-lived redesign which won critical favour but was a commercial failure, partly as a result of a limited promotional budget. Marr admitted his changes had been a mistake in his book, <i>My Trade</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Boycott left in April 1998 to join the <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Express" title="Daily Express">Daily Express</a></i>, and Marr left in May 1998, later becoming the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>'s political editor. <a href="/wiki/Simon_Kelner" title="Simon Kelner">Simon Kelner</a> was appointed as the editor. By this time the circulation had fallen below 200,000. Independent News spent heavily to increase circulation, and the paper went through several redesigns. While circulation increased, it did not approach the level which had been achieved in 1989, or restore profitability. Job cuts and financial controls reduced the morale of journalists and the quality of the product.<sup id="cite_ref-Mad_eyes_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mad_eyes-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2000.E2.80.932009"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000–2009">2000–2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2000–2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ivan Fallon, on the board since 1995 and formerly a key figure at <i>The Sunday Times</i>, replaced Hopkins as head of Independent News &amp; Media in July 2002. By mid-2004, the newspaper was losing £5&#160;million per year. A gradual improvement meant that by 2006, circulation was at a nine-year high.<sup id="cite_ref-Mad_eyes_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mad_eyes-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2008, following further staff cuts, production was moved to Northcliffe House, in Kensington High Street, the headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Associated_Newspapers_Ltd" class="mw-redirect" title="Associated Newspapers Ltd">Associated Newspapers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Northcliffe_move_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Northcliffe_move-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The two newspaper groups' editorial, management and commercial operations remained separate, but they shared services including security, information technology, switchboard and payroll.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932016"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2016">2010–2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010–2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 25 March 2010, Independent News &amp; Media sold the newspaper to <a href="/wiki/Russian_oligarchs" class="mw-redirect" title="Russian oligarchs">Russian oligarch</a> <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lebedev" title="Alexander Lebedev">Alexander Lebedev</a> for a nominal £1 fee and £9.25m over the next 10 months, choosing this option over closing <i>The Independent</i> and <i>The Independent on Sunday</i>, which would have cost £28m and £40m respectively, due to long-term contracts.<sup id="cite_ref-i2010-03-25_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-i2010-03-25-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bintliff_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bintliff-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, Lebedev had bought a controlling stake in the <i><a href="/wiki/London_Evening_Standard" class="mw-redirect" title="London Evening Standard">London Evening Standard</a></i>. Two weeks later, editor <a href="/wiki/Roger_Alton" title="Roger Alton">Roger Alton</a> resigned.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2011, <i>The Independent</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s columnist <a href="/wiki/Johann_Hari" title="Johann Hari">Johann Hari</a> was stripped of the <a href="/wiki/Orwell_Prize" title="Orwell Prize">Orwell Prize</a> he had won in 2008 after claims, to which Hari later admitted,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> of plagiarism and inaccuracy.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In January 2012, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Blackhurst" title="Chris Blackhurst">Chris Blackhurst</a>, editor of <i>The Independent</i>, told the <a href="/wiki/Leveson_inquiry" class="mw-redirect" title="Leveson inquiry">Leveson inquiry</a> that the scandal had "severely damaged" the newspaper's reputation. He nevertheless told the inquiry that Hari would return as a columnist in "four to five weeks".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Hari later announced that he would not return to <i>The Independent</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Foreman_(journalist)" title="Jonathan Foreman (journalist)">Jonathan Foreman</a> contrasted <i>The Independent</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s reaction to the scandal unfavourably with the reaction of American newspapers to similar incidents such as the <a href="/wiki/Jayson_Blair" title="Jayson Blair">Jayson Blair</a> case, which led to resignations of editors, "deep soul-searching", and "new standards of exactitude being imposed".<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The historian <a href="/wiki/Guy_Walters" title="Guy Walters">Guy Walters</a> suggested that Hari's fabrications had been an open secret amongst the newspaper's staff and that their internal inquiry was a "facesaving exercise".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> A proportion of articles are now behind a pay wall, that section is titled, 'Independent Minds'.
</p><p><i>The Independent</i> and <i>The Independent on Sunday</i> endorsed the "Remain" in the <a href="/wiki/2016_United_Kingdom_European_Union_membership_referendum" title="2016 United Kingdom European Union membership referendum">Brexit referendum of 2016</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="From_2016">From 2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: From 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2016 <i>The Independent</i> decided to close its print edition and become an <a href="/wiki/Online_newspaper" title="Online newspaper">online newspaper</a>; the last printed edition was published on Saturday 26 March 2016. <i>The Independent on Sunday</i> published its last print edition on 20 March 2016 and was closed following that.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Format_and_design">Format and design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Format and design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Independent</i> began publishing as a broadsheet, in a series of celebrated designs. The final version was designed by Carroll, Dempsey and Thirkell following a commission by <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Garland" title="Nicholas Garland">Nicholas Garland</a> who, along with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Chancellor" title="Alexander Chancellor">Alexander Chancellor</a>, was unhappy with designs produced by Raymond Hawkey and Michael McGuiness – on seeing the proposed designs, Chancellor had said "I thought we were joining a serious paper". The first edition was designed and implemented by Michael Crozier, who was Executive Editor, Design and Picture, from pre-launch in 1986 to 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From September 2003, the paper was produced in both broadsheet and tabloid-sized versions, with the same content in each. The tabloid edition was termed "compact" to distance itself from the more sensationalist reporting style usually associated with "tabloid" newspapers in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-Bus_Week_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bus_Week-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> After launching in the London area and then in <a href="/wiki/North_West_England" title="North West England">North West England</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Indy_northwest_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indy_northwest-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> the smaller format appeared gradually throughout the UK. Soon afterwards, Rupert Murdoch's <i>Times</i> followed suit, introducing its own tabloid-sized version.<sup id="cite_ref-Brand_Rep_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brand_Rep-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Prior to these changes, <i>The Independent</i> had a daily circulation of around 217,500, the lowest of any major national British daily, a figure that climbed by 15% as of March 2004 (to 250,000). Throughout much of 2006, circulation stagnated at a quarter of a million. On 14 May 2004, <i>The Independent</i> produced its last weekday broadsheet, having stopped producing a Saturday broadsheet edition in January. <i>The Independent on Sunday</i> published its last simultaneous broadsheet on 9 October 2005, and has since followed a compact design.
</p><p>On 12 April 2005, <i>The Independent</i> redesigned its layout to a more European feel, similar to France's <i><a href="/wiki/Lib%C3%A9ration" title="Libération">Libération</a></i>. The redesign was carried out by a Barcelona-based design studio. The weekday second section was subsumed within the main paper, double-page feature articles became common in the main news sections, and there were revisions to the front and back covers.<sup id="cite_ref-Guard_redesign_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guard_redesign-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> A new second section, "Extra", was introduced on 25 April 2006. It is similar to <i>The Guardian</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s "G2" and <i>The Times</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s "Times2", containing features, reportage and games, including <a href="/wiki/Sudoku" title="Sudoku">sudoku</a>. In June 2007, <i>The Independent on Sunday</i> consolidated its content into a news section which included sports and business, and a magazine focusing on life and culture.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On 23 September 2008, the main newspaper became full-colour, and "Extra" was replaced by an "Independent Life Supplement" focusing on different themes each day.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Three weeks after the acquisition of the paper by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Lebedev" title="Alexander Lebedev">Alexander Lebedev</a> and <a href="/wiki/Evgeny_Lebedev" title="Evgeny Lebedev">Evgeny Lebedev</a> in 2010, the paper was relaunched with another redesign on 20 April. The new format featured smaller headlines and a new pullout "Viewspaper" section, which contained the paper's comment and feature articles.<sup id="cite_ref-pgazette_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pgazette-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> From 26 October 2010, the same day as its sister paper, <i>i</i>, was launched, <i>The Independent</i> was printed on slightly thicker paper than before and ceased to be full-colour throughout, with many photographs and pictures (though none of those used in adverts) being printed in black and white only. On 11 October 2011, <i>The Independent</i> unveiled yet another new look, featuring a red, sans-serif masthead. In November 2013, the whole newspaper was overhauled again, including new custom fonts and a vertical masthead in black.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Front_pages">Front pages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Front pages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the 2003 switch in format, <i>The Independent</i> became known for its unorthodox and campaigning front pages, which frequently relied on images, graphics or lists rather than traditional headlines and written news content. For example, following the Kashmir earthquake in 2005, it used its front page to urge its readers to donate to its appeal fund, and following the publication of the <a href="/wiki/Hutton_Inquiry" title="Hutton Inquiry">Hutton Report</a> into the death of British government scientist <a href="/wiki/David_Kelly_(weapons_expert)" title="David Kelly (weapons expert)">David Kelly</a>, its front page simply carried the word "Whitewash?"<sup id="cite_ref-PR_week_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR_week-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> In 2003, the paper's editor, Simon Kelner, was named "Editor of the Year" at the <i><a href="/wiki/What_the_Papers_Say" title="What the Papers Say">What the Papers Say</a></i> awards, partly in recognition of, according to the judges, his "often arresting and imaginative front-page designs".<sup id="cite_ref-Indy_award_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indy_award-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In 2008, however, as he was stepping down as editor, he stated that it was possible to "overdo the formula" and that the style of the paper's front pages perhaps needed "reinvention".<sup id="cite_ref-Guard_Kelner_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guard_Kelner-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Under the subsequent editorship of <a href="/wiki/Chris_Blackhurst" title="Chris Blackhurst">Chris Blackhurst</a>, the campaigning, poster-style front pages were scaled back in favour of more conventional news stories.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_presence">Online presence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Online presence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 23 January 2008, <i>The Independent</i> relaunched its online edition, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.independent.co.uk/">www.independent.co.uk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The relaunched site introduced a new look, better access to the blog service, priority on image and video content, and additional areas of the site including art, architecture, fashion, gadgets and health. The paper launched <a href="/wiki/Podcast" title="Podcast">podcast</a> programmes such as "The Independent Music Radio Show", "The Independent Travel Guides", "The Independent Sailing Podcasts", and "The Independent Video Travel Guides". Since 2009, the website has carried short video news bulletins provided by the <a href="/wiki/Al_Jazeera_English" title="Al Jazeera English">Al Jazeera English</a> news channel.<sup id="cite_ref-Guard_al_Jazeera_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guard_al_Jazeera-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, <i>The Independent</i> launched a sister website, <i>i100</i>, a "shareable" journalism site with similarities to <a href="/wiki/Reddit" title="Reddit">Reddit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Upworthy" title="Upworthy">Upworthy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_views">Political views</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Political views">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When the paper was established in 1986, the founders intended its political stance to reflect the centre of the British political spectrum and thought that it would attract readers primarily from <i>The Times</i> and <i>The Daily Telegraph</i>. It has been seen as leaning to the left wing of the political spectrum, making it more a competitor to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>. However, <i>The Independent</i> tends to take a <a href="/wiki/Classical_liberal" class="mw-redirect" title="Classical liberal">classical liberal</a>, pro-market stance on economic issues.<sup id="cite_ref-Wilby_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wilby-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The <i>Independent on Sunday</i> referred to itself as a "proudly liberal newspaper".<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The paper took a strong editorial position against the <a href="/wiki/2003_Invasion_of_Iraq" class="mw-redirect" title="2003 Invasion of Iraq">2003 Invasion of Iraq</a> and aspects of US and UK foreign policy related to the <a href="/wiki/War_on_Terrorism" class="mw-redirect" title="War on Terrorism">War on Terrorism</a> following the <span class="nowrap"><a href="/wiki/11_September_attacks" class="mw-redirect" title="11 September attacks">11 September attacks</a></span>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In addition, <i>The Independent</i> has highlighted what it refers to as <a href="/wiki/War_crime" title="War crime">war crimes</a> being committed by pro-government forces in the <a href="/wiki/War_in_Darfur" title="War in Darfur">Darfur</a> region of Sudan.<sup id="cite_ref-Indy_Darfur_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indy_Darfur-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The paper has been a strong supporter of <a href="/wiki/Electoral_reform" title="Electoral reform">electoral reform</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lead2010may5_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lead2010may5-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The paper has also taken strong positions on environmental issues, campaigned against the introduction of <a href="/wiki/British_national_identity_card" class="mw-redirect" title="British national identity card">ID cards</a>, and campaigned against the restriction of mass immigration to the UK.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In 1997, <i>The Independent on Sunday</i> launched a campaign for the decriminalisation of cannabis. Ten years later, it reversed itself, arguing that skunk, the cannabis strain  "smoked by the majority of young Britons" in 2007, had become "25 times stronger than <a href="/wiki/Hashish" title="Hashish">resin</a> sold a decade ago".<sup id="cite_ref-Indy_cannabis_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indy_cannabis-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The paper supports the <a href="/wiki/Republicanism_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Republicanism in the United Kingdom">United Kingdom becoming a republic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Originally, it avoided royal stories, Whittam Smith later saying he thought the British press was "unduly besotted" with the Royal Family and that a newspaper could "manage without" stories about the monarchy.<sup id="cite_ref-Whittam_Smith_Royals_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Whittam_Smith_Royals-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Rusbridger" title="Alan Rusbridger">Alan Rusbridger</a>, editor of <i>The Guardian</i>, said of <i>The Independent</i>: "The emphasis on views, not news, means that the reporting is rather thin, and it loses impact on the front page the more you do that".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> In a 12 June 2007 speech, British Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">Tony Blair</a> called <i>The Independent</i> a "viewspaper", saying it "was started as an antidote to the idea of journalism as views not news. That was why it was called the Independent. Today it is avowedly a viewspaper not merely a newspaper".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> <i>The Independent</i> criticised Blair's comments the following day<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> but later changed format to include a "Viewspaper" insert in the centre of the regular newspaper, designed to feature most of the opinion columns and arts reviews.
</p><p>A leader published on the day of the <a href="/wiki/2008_London_mayoral_election" title="2008 London mayoral election">2008 London Mayoral election</a> compared the candidates and said that, if the newspaper had a vote, it would vote first for the <a href="/wiki/Green_Party_of_England_and_Wales" title="Green Party of England and Wales">Green Party</a> candidate, <a href="/wiki/Sian_Berry" class="mw-redirect" title="Sian Berry">Sian Berry</a>, noting the similarity between her priorities and those of <i>The Independent</i>, and secondly, with "rather heavy heart", for the incumbent, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Livingstone" title="Ken Livingstone">Ken Livingstone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-London_endorsement_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-London_endorsement-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>An <a href="/wiki/Ipsos_MORI" title="Ipsos MORI">Ipsos MORI</a> poll estimated that in the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2010 United Kingdom general election">2010 general election</a>, 44% of regular readers voted Liberal Democrat, 32% voted Labour,<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> and 14% voted Conservative, compared to 23%, 29%, and 36%, respectively, of the overall electorate.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> On the eve of the 2010 general election, <i>The Independent</i> supported the Liberal Democrats, arguing that "they are longstanding and convincing champions of civil liberties, sound economics, international co-operation on the great global challenges and, of course, fundamental electoral reform. These are all principles that this newspaper has long held dear. That is why we argue that there is a strong case for progressively minded voters to lend their support to the Liberal Democrats wherever there is a clear opportunity for that party to win".<sup id="cite_ref-lead2010may5_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lead2010may5-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> However, before the <a href="/wiki/2015_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2015 United Kingdom general election">2015 general election</a> <i>The Independent on Sunday</i> desisted from advising its readers how to vote, writing that "this does not mean that we are a bloodless, value-free news-sheet. We have always been committed to social justice", but the paper recognised that it was up the readers to "make up [their] own mind about whether you agree with us or not". Rather than support a particular party, the paper urged all its reader to vote as "a responsibility of common citizenship".<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> On 4 May 2015, the weekday version of <i>The Independent</i> said that a continuation of the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a>–Liberal Democrat coalition after the general election would be a positive outcome.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of July 2018 <i>The Independent</i> led a campaign they called the "Final Say" - a <a href="/wiki/Change.org" title="Change.org">change.org</a> petition by editor <a href="/wiki/Christian_Broughton" title="Christian Broughton">Christian Broughton</a>, for a binding referendum on the <a href="/wiki/Brexit" title="Brexit">Brexit</a> deal between the UK and the European Union.<sup id="cite_ref-People&#39;s_Vote_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-People&#39;s_Vote-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 2019, Independent in Arabic is owned and managed by Saudi Research and Marketing Group (SRMG), a major publishing organisation with close ties to the Saudi royal family.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Independent_on_Sunday"><i>Independent on Sunday</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Independent on Sunday">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Independent on Sunday</i> (<i>IoS</i>) was the Sunday sister newspaper of <i>The Independent</i>. It ceased to exist in 2016, the last edition being published on 20 March; the daily paper ceasing print publication six days later.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_i">The <i>i</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: The i">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/I_(newspaper)" title="I (newspaper)">i (newspaper)</a></div>
<p>In October 2010, the <i>i</i>, a compact sister newspaper, was launched. The <i>i</i> is a separate newspaper but uses some of the same material. It was later sold to regional newspaper company <a href="/wiki/Johnston_Press" title="Johnston Press">Johnston Press</a>, becoming that publisher's flagship national newspaper. The <i>i</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s online presence, <i>i100</i>, was restyled as <i>indy100</i> and retained by Independent News &amp; Media.
</p>
<h3><span id="The_.28RED.29_Independent"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_(RED)_Independent"><i>The (RED) Independent</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: The (RED) Independent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Independent</i> supported U2 lead singer <a href="/wiki/Bono" title="Bono">Bono</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Product_RED" class="mw-redirect" title="Product RED">Product RED</a> brand by creating <i>The (RED) Independent</i>, an occasional edition that gave half the day's proceeds to the charity.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> The first edition was in May 2006. Edited by Bono, it drew high sales.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A September 2006 edition of <i>The (RED) Independent</i>, designed by fashion designer <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Armani" title="Giorgio Armani">Giorgio Armani</a>, drew controversy due to its cover shot, showing model <a href="/wiki/Kate_Moss" title="Kate Moss">Kate Moss</a> in <a href="/wiki/Blackface" title="Blackface">blackface</a> for an article about AIDS in Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_nominations">Awards and nominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Independent&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Awards and nominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p>

<li>"Specialist Journalist of the Year": Michael McCarthy, 2000; Jeremy Laurance, 2011</li>
<li>"Sports Journalist of the Year": James Lawton, 2010</li>
<li>"Young Journalist of the Year": Johann Hari, 2002; Ed Caesar, 2006</li></ul>
