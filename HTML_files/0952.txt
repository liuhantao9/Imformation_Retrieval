<p><b>Port Washington</b> is an affluent <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(New_York)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hamlet (New York)">hamlet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a> (CDP) in <a href="/wiki/Nassau_County,_New_York" title="Nassau County, New York">Nassau County</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a> on the <a href="/wiki/North_Shore_(Long_Island)" title="North Shore (Long Island)">North Shore</a> of <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">Long Island</a>. As of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_2010_Census" class="mw-redirect" title="United States 2010 Census">United States 2010 Census</a>, the community population was 15,846.<sup id="cite_ref-GR2_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Port Washington is a hamlet within and directly governed by the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_New_York#Town" class="mw-redirect" title="Administrative divisions of New York">town</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_Hempstead,_New_York" title="North Hempstead, New York">North Hempstead</a>. With rolling hills and a serpentine coastline in the northwest corner of Nassau County, Port Washington is studded with marinas, parks, yacht clubs and golf courses. The <a href="/wiki/Great_Neck" class="mw-redirect" title="Great Neck">Great Neck</a> peninsula is across <a href="/wiki/Manhasset_Bay" title="Manhasset Bay">Manhasset Bay</a> to the west; <a href="/wiki/Manhasset" class="mw-redirect" title="Manhasset">Manhasset</a> and <a href="/wiki/Plandome" class="mw-redirect" title="Plandome">Plandome</a> are to the south; <a href="/wiki/Roslyn,_New_York" title="Roslyn, New York">Roslyn</a> lies southeast. Besides an unincorporated area of the Town of North Hempstead, Port Washington is home to four incorporated villages: <a href="/wiki/Baxter_Estates" class="mw-redirect" title="Baxter Estates">Baxter Estates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manorhaven" class="mw-redirect" title="Manorhaven">Manorhaven</a>, <a href="/wiki/Port_Washington_North" class="mw-redirect" title="Port Washington North">Port Washington North</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sands_Point,_New_York" title="Sands Point, New York">Sands Point</a>, plus part of the village of <a href="/wiki/Flower_Hill" class="mw-redirect" title="Flower Hill">Flower Hill</a>. 
</p><p>According to <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i>, Port Washington is ranked as the 418th wealthiest place in the United States as of 2017, with a median home sale price of $1,191,865.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Port_Washington,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the 1870s, Port Washington became an important sand-mining town; it had the largest sandbank east of the Mississippi and easy barge access to Manhattan. Some 140 million cubic yards of local sand were used for concrete for New York skyscrapers like the <a href="/wiki/Empire_State_Building" title="Empire State Building">Empire State</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Crysler_Building&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Crysler Building (page does not exist)">Chrysler</a> buildings, according to Jon Kaiman, the Town Supervisor until 2013. In 1998, the sand mines were redeveloped as Harbor Links,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> a golf course for North Hempstead residents.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Dodge_Homestead" title="Thomas Dodge Homestead">Thomas Dodge Homestead</a>, <a href="/wiki/Execution_Rocks_Light" title="Execution Rocks Light">Execution Rocks Light</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gould-Guggenheim_Estate" class="mw-redirect" title="Gould-Guggenheim Estate">Gould-Guggenheim Estate</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Landsberg_House" title="William Landsberg House">William Landsberg House</a>, <a href="/wiki/Main_Street_School" title="Main Street School">Main Street School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monfort_Cemetery" title="Monfort Cemetery">Monfort Cemetery</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sands-Willets_Homestead" title="Sands-Willets Homestead">Sands-Willets Homestead</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_Philip_Sousa_House" title="John Philip Sousa House">John Philip Sousa House</a> are listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-weekly_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weekly-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Knickerbocker_Yacht_Club" title="Knickerbocker Yacht Club">Knickerbocker Yacht Club</a> was a yacht club in Port Washington for over 100 years.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>

<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GoosePondinNovSm.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/GoosePondinNovSm.jpg/220px-GoosePondinNovSm.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/GoosePondinNovSm.jpg/330px-GoosePondinNovSm.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/GoosePondinNovSm.jpg/440px-GoosePondinNovSm.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GoosePondinNovSm.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Baxter Pond, November 2007</div></div></div>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the CDP has a total area of 5.6 square miles (15&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), of which 4.2 square miles (11&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 1.4 square miles (3.6&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) (25.22%) is water.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bar_Beach">Bar Beach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Port_Washington,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Bar Beach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bar_beach.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Bar_beach.jpg/220px-Bar_beach.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Bar_beach.jpg/330px-Bar_beach.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Bar_beach.jpg/440px-Bar_beach.jpg 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="720" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bar_beach.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bar Beach</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bar_Beach_North_Hempstead.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Bar_Beach_North_Hempstead.jpg/220px-Bar_Beach_North_Hempstead.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Bar_Beach_North_Hempstead.jpg/330px-Bar_Beach_North_Hempstead.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Bar_Beach_North_Hempstead.jpg/440px-Bar_Beach_North_Hempstead.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1365" data-file-height="2048" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bar_Beach_North_Hempstead.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bar Beach North Hempstead</div></div></div>
<p>Bar Beach is a beach located on the <a href="/wiki/North_Shore_(Long_Island)" title="North Shore (Long Island)">North Shore (Long Island)</a>, in Port Washington. It is adjacent to <a href="/wiki/Hempstead_Harbor" title="Hempstead Harbor">Hempstead Harbor</a> beach.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A fireworks display sometimes takes place at the beach on <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" title="Memorial Day">Memorial Day</a> but not in 2007. The absence of fireworks has been blamed on the <a href="/wiki/Americana_Manhasset" title="Americana Manhasset">Americana Manhasset</a> and Wheatley Plaza by the Town of North Hempstead.  Fireworks returned to Bar Beach (renamed as North Hempstead Beach Park in 2008) on Memorial Day, with the Town of North Hempstead fully sponsoring the event. In late 2007, the Town assumed management of the neighboring beach, Hempstead Harbor, from Nassau County. After beginning renovations to this portion of the beach, the entire property was re-opened in the spring of 2008 as North Hempstead Beach Park.
</p><p>In addition to the annual Memorial Day fireworks show, the beach is host to "Beachfest", an end of summer festival which is held each September. Beachfest features music, games, food vendors, and attractions for all ages.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Port_Washington,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_Census">2010 Census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Port_Washington,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2010 Census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the 2010 census,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> the population was 82.2% <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a> 74.7% <a href="/wiki/Non-Hispanic_Whites" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-Hispanic Whites">Non-Hispanic White</a>, 2.4% <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.2% <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 8% <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.0% <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 4.8% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 2.4% from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> people of any race were 13.4% of the population. The median income for households in Port Washington, New York is $117,647, while the mean household income is $174,069, excluding the data from the extremely wealthy hamlet of <a href="/wiki/Sands_Point,_New_York" title="Sands Point, New York">Sands Point</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000_Census">2000 Census</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Port_Washington,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2000 Census">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of 2000, there were 15,215 people, 5,521 households, and 4,168 families residing in the CDP. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 3,613.7 per square mile (1,395.4/km²). There were 5,662 housing units at an average density of 1,344.8/sq&#160;mi (519.3/km²). The racial makeup of the CDP was 85.97% <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 2.81% <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.11% <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 6.07% <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.02% <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 3.15% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.86% from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> people of any race were 11.20% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-GR2_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were 5,521 households, out of which 36.3% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 62.9% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, and 24.5% were non-families. 20.4% of all households were made up of individuals, and 10.4% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.73 and the average family size was 3.15.<sup id="cite_ref-GR2_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the CDP, the population was spread out, with 25.3% under the age of 18, 5.0% from 18 to 24, 28.3% from 25 to 44, 26.4% from 45 to 64, and 15.0% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 40 years. For every 100 females, there were 90.9 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 87.5 males.<sup id="cite_ref-GR2_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The median income for a household in the CDP was $105,837 and the median income for a family was $122,646. Males had a median income of $91,024 versus $59,299 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the CDP was $53,815. About 3.1% of families and 4.7% of the population were below the <a href="/wiki/Poverty_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 3.5% of those under age 18 and 5.0% of those age 65 or over.<sup id="cite_ref-GR2_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fire_department">Fire department</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Port_Washington,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Fire department">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Port Washington Fire Department is a not-for-profit private corporation providing contractual public safety services to the area known as the Port Washington Fire Protection District. The Port Washington Fire Protection District is an 11-square-mile (28&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) area on the Northern Peninsula of Nassau County Long Island that includes:
</p>
<ul><li>Incorporated <a href="/wiki/Sands_Point,_New_York" title="Sands Point, New York">Village of Sands Point</a></li>
<li>Incorporated <a href="/wiki/Port_Washington_North,_New_York" title="Port Washington North, New York">Village of Port Washington North</a></li>
<li>Incorporated <a href="/wiki/Manorhaven,_New_York" title="Manorhaven, New York">Village of Manorhaven</a></li>
<li>Incorporated <a href="/wiki/Baxter_Estates,_New_York" title="Baxter Estates, New York">Village of Baxter Estates</a></li>
<li>Portions of the incorporated <a href="/wiki/Plandome_Manor,_New_York" title="Plandome Manor, New York">Village of Plandome Manor</a></li>
<li>Portions of the incorporated <a href="/wiki/Flower_Hill,_New_York" title="Flower Hill, New York">Village of Flower Hill</a></li>
<li>Portions of the unincorporated areas of the <a href="/wiki/Town_of_North_Hempstead,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Town of North Hempstead, New York">Town of North Hempstead</a></li></ul>
<p>The Port Washington Fire Department is a volunteer department composed of four companies.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Ladder Company: Atlantic Hook &amp; Ladder Company #1</li>
<li>Engine Company: Protection Engine Company #1</li>
<li>Engine Company: Flower Hill Hose Company #1</li>
<li>Emergency Medical Service Company: Fire Medic Company #1</li></ul>

<p>The police district provides police protection for the unincorporated area of Port Washington in the Town of North Hempstead as well as the incorporated villages of Baxter Estates and Port Washington North.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Port_Washington,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Port Washington is the terminus of <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Route_101" title="New York State Route 101">New York State Route 101</a> and of the <a href="/wiki/Port_Washington_Branch" title="Port Washington Branch">Port Washington Branch</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Long_Island_Rail_Road" title="Long Island Rail Road">Long Island Rail Road</a> built at the end of the 19th century at <a href="/wiki/Port_Washington_(LIRR_station)" class="mw-redirect" title="Port Washington (LIRR station)">a station of the same name</a>, which opened for passengers in 1898. Anticipating growth due to the railroad, the community was renamed from Cow Neck. Shoreline roads connect it to <a href="/wiki/Manhasset,_New_York" title="Manhasset, New York">Manhasset</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roslyn,_New_York" title="Roslyn, New York">Roslyn, New York</a>. During part of the 1930s, before the opening of New York City's <a href="/wiki/Marine_Air_Terminal" title="Marine Air Terminal">Marine Air Terminal</a>, Port Washington was the New York base of the Yankee Clipper <a href="/wiki/Boeing_314" class="mw-redirect" title="Boeing 314">Boeing 314</a> <a href="/wiki/Seaplane" title="Seaplane">seaplane</a>.
</p><p>The N23 bus operated by <a href="/wiki/Nassau_Inter-County_Express" title="Nassau Inter-County Express">Nassau Inter-County Express</a> from Manorhaven to either Mineola or Hempstead also serves Port Washington, running along Main Street and Port Washington Boulevard.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Port_Washington,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Port Washington is depicted as the area of East Egg in <a href="/wiki/F._Scott_Fitzgerald" title="F. Scott Fitzgerald">F. Scott Fitzgerald</a>'s novel, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Gatsby" title="The Great Gatsby">The Great Gatsby</a></i>.
</p><p>Scenes from the 2000 movie, '<a href="/wiki/Meet_the_Parents" title="Meet the Parents">Meet the Parents</a>' were shot along Main Street in Port Washington.
</p>
