<p><b>Claiborne de Borda Pell</b> <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Order_of_Christ_(Portugal)" title="Order of Christ (Portugal)">GCC</a></span> <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Order_of_Merit_(Portugal)" title="Order of Merit (Portugal)">GCM</a></span> (November 22, 1918 – January 1, 2009) was an American politician and writer who served as a <a href="/wiki/U.S._Senator" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Senator">U.S. Senator</a> from <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a> for six terms from 1961 to 1997. He was the sponsor of the 1972 bill that reformed the Basic Educational Opportunity Grant, which provides <a href="/wiki/Student_financial_aid_in_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Student financial aid in the United States">financial aid</a> funding to American college students; the grant was later given Pell's name in honor of his work in education legislation. A member of the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic Party</a>, he served in the U.S. Senate longer than anyone else from Rhode Island.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Claiborne Pell was born on November 22, 1918, in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the son of Matilda Bigelow and diplomat and congressman <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Pell" title="Herbert Pell">Herbert Pell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pell's family members included <a href="/wiki/John_Francis_Hamtramck_Claiborne" title="John Francis Hamtramck Claiborne">John Francis Hamtramck Claiborne</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Mifflin_Dallas" class="mw-redirect" title="George Mifflin Dallas">George Mifflin Dallas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nathaniel_Herbert_Claiborne" class="mw-redirect" title="Nathaniel Herbert Claiborne">Nathaniel Herbert Claiborne</a>.  He was a direct descendant of mathematician <a href="/wiki/John_Pell" title="John Pell">John Pell</a> and a descendant of Senator <a href="/wiki/William_C._C._Claiborne" title="William C. C. Claiborne">William C. C. Claiborne</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1927 Pell's parents divorced and his mother remarried <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">U.S. Naval</a> Commander <a href="/wiki/Hugo_W._Koehler" title="Hugo W. Koehler">Hugo W. Koehler</a> of <a href="/wiki/St._Louis" title="St. Louis">St. Louis</a>.  Following the First World War, Koehler served as an Office of Naval Intelligence and State Department operative in Russia during its civil war, and later as naval attaché to Poland. Said to be the "richest officer in the Navy" during the 1920s, Koehler was rumored to be the illegitimate son of the Crown Prince of Austria and to have assisted the Romanovs in fleeing Russia following the revolution of 1917, though no evidence of this has been established. Pell was close to his stepfather, who died when Pell was 22. In later years, Pell made a concerted effort to determine the veracity of these rumors surrounding Koehler's past.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pell attended <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s_School,_Newport" title="St. George&#39;s School, Newport">St. George's School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport, Rhode Island">Newport, Rhode Island</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and received an <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Arts" title="Bachelor of Arts">A.B.</a> in history from <a href="/wiki/Princeton_University" title="Princeton University">Princeton University</a> in 1940.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  While at Princeton, he was a member of <a href="/wiki/Colonial_Club" title="Colonial Club">Colonial Club</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Whig-Cliosophic_Society" class="mw-redirect" title="American Whig-Cliosophic Society">American Whig-Cliosophic Society</a>, and played on the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">rugby</a> team.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-college_life">Post-college life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Post-college life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After graduating, Pell worked as an <a href="/wiki/Oil_field" class="mw-redirect" title="Oil field">oil field</a> <a href="/wiki/Roustabout" title="Roustabout">roustabout</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  He then served as private secretary for his father, who was <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_Portugal" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to Portugal">United States Ambassador to Portugal</a>.  At the start of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> he was with his father, who was then <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_Hungary" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to Hungary">United States Ambassador to Hungary</a>.  Claiborne Pell drove trucks carrying emergency supplies to prisoners of war in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, and was detained several times by the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi government</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Mulligan_2009_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mulligan_2009-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Uniformed_service">Uniformed service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Uniformed service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pell enlisted in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Guard" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Coast Guard">U.S. Coast Guard</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Seaman_apprentice" title="Seaman apprentice">seaman second class</a> on August 12, 1941, four months before the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor</a>. Pell served as a ship's cook, was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Seaman_(rank)" title="Seaman (rank)">seaman first class</a> on October 31, and then was commissioned as an <a href="/wiki/Ensign_(rank)#United_States" title="Ensign (rank)">ensign</a> on December 17, 1941.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> During the war, Pell's ships served as <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic" class="mw-redirect" title="North Atlantic">North Atlantic</a> <a href="/wiki/Convoy" title="Convoy">convoy</a> escorts, and also in <a href="/wiki/Amphibious_warfare" title="Amphibious warfare">amphibious warfare</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Allied_invasion_of_Sicily" title="Allied invasion of Sicily">allied invasion of Sicily</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Allied_invasion_of_Italy" title="Allied invasion of Italy">allied invasion of the Italian mainland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pell was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_(junior_grade)" title="Lieutenant (junior grade)">lieutenant (junior grade)</a> on October 1, 1942, and then to <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant#Naval_rank" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a> in May, 1943.  Due to his fluency in Italian, Pell was assigned as a civil affairs officer in <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, where he became ill from drinking unpasteurized milk.  He was sent home for recuperation during the summer of 1944, but returned to active service later in the war.  Pell was discharged from active duty on September 5, 1945.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the end of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, he remained in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Guard_Reserve" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Coast Guard Reserve">U.S. Coast Guard Reserve</a>.  He retired from that service in 1978 with the rank of <a href="/wiki/Captain_(United_States)#U.S._Coast_Guard" title="Captain (United States)">captain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life_and_family">Personal life and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pell married Nuala O'Donnell in December 1944.<sup id="cite_ref-nuala_obit_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nuala_obit-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> They had four children: Herbert Claiborne Pell III, Christopher Thomas Hartford Pell, Nuala Dallas Pell, and Julia Lorillard Wampage Pell.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Herbert (September 11, 1945 – September 24, 1999)<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and Julia (May 9, 1953 – April 13, 2006) predeceased their parents.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> His grandson <a href="/wiki/Clay_Pell" title="Clay Pell">Clay Pell</a> (son of Herbert) was an unsuccessful contender in the <a href="/wiki/2014_Rhode_Island_gubernatorial_election#Democratic_primary" title="2014 Rhode Island gubernatorial election">2014 Democratic primary</a> for <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Rhode_Island" title="Governor of Rhode Island">Governor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Diplomatic_work.2C_further_education"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Diplomatic_work,_further_education">Diplomatic work, further education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Diplomatic work, further education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1945 to 1952, he served in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_State" title="United States Department of State">United States Department of State</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Service_Officer" title="Foreign Service Officer">Foreign Service Officer</a> in <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>  He was fluent in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1945, Pell was a participant in the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Conference_on_International_Organization" title="United Nations Conference on International Organization">United Nations Conference on International Organization</a> in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a> that drafted the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Charter">United Nations Charter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1946 he completed graduate studies in International Relations at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> and received a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts" title="Master of Arts">Master of Arts</a> degree.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-diplomatic_career">Post-diplomatic career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-diplomatic career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1954 Pell was appointed vice president and member of the board of directors of the International Fiscal Corporation.  He also served as a vice president and director of the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Newspaper_Alliance" title="North American Newspaper Alliance">North American Newspaper Alliance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He was also a director of the <a href="/wiki/Roosevelt_Institute" title="Roosevelt Institute">Franklin D. Roosevelt Foundation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fort_Ticonderoga" title="Fort Ticonderoga">Fort Ticonderoga</a> Association, and <a href="/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Donatien_de_Vimeur,_comte_de_Rochambeau" title="Jean-Baptiste Donatien de Vimeur, comte de Rochambeau">General Rochambeau Commission</a> of Rhode Island.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>  He also served as a fundraiser and consultant for the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_National_Committee" title="Democratic National Committee">Democratic National Committee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> He served as Vice President of the <a href="/wiki/International_Rescue_Committee" title="International Rescue Committee">International Rescue Committee</a>. Stationed in <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, he was responsible for assisting refugees from the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Revolution_of_1956" title="Hungarian Revolution of 1956">Hungarian Revolution of 1956</a> to leave the country and resettle.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During Pell's diplomatic career and other international activities in the 1940s and 1950s, he was arrested and jailed at least six times, including detentions by both fascist and communist governments.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mrs._Gorbachev_looking_at_books.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Mrs._Gorbachev_looking_at_books.jpg/220px-Mrs._Gorbachev_looking_at_books.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="143" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Mrs._Gorbachev_looking_at_books.jpg/330px-Mrs._Gorbachev_looking_at_books.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Mrs._Gorbachev_looking_at_books.jpg/440px-Mrs._Gorbachev_looking_at_books.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="664" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mrs._Gorbachev_looking_at_books.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pell watches as <a href="/wiki/Raisa_Gorbachev" class="mw-redirect" title="Raisa Gorbachev">Raisa Gorbachev</a> listens to <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Quayle" title="Marilyn Quayle">Marilyn Quayle</a> at a display of books and other items at the <a href="/wiki/Library_of_Congress" title="Library of Congress">Library of Congress</a></div></div></div>
<p>In 1960, Pell won the seat of retiring U.S. Senator <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Francis_Green" class="mw-redirect" title="Theodore Francis Green">Theodore Francis Green</a>, defeating former <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Rhode_Island" title="Governor of Rhode Island">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Dennis_J._Roberts" title="Dennis J. Roberts">Dennis J. Roberts</a> and former Governor and U.S. Senator <a href="/wiki/J._Howard_McGrath" title="J. Howard McGrath">J. Howard McGrath</a> in the Democratic primary,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and former <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_Republican_Party" title="Rhode Island Republican Party">Rhode Island Republican Party</a> Chairman Raoul Archambault in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_elections,_1960" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Senate elections, 1960">general election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite being called "the least electable man in America" by <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> because of his many odd habits and beliefs,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Pell proved a durable politician.  He won reelection five times, including victories over <a href="/wiki/Ruth_M._Briggs" title="Ruth M. Briggs">Ruth M. Briggs</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_elections,_1966" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Senate elections, 1966">(1966)</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Chafee" title="John Chafee">John Chafee</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_elections,_1972" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Senate elections, 1972">(1972)</a>, James G. Reynolds <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_elections,_1978" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Senate elections, 1978">(1978)</a>, Barbara Leonard <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_elections,_1984" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Senate elections, 1984">(1984)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Claudine_Schneider" title="Claudine Schneider">Claudine Schneider</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_elections,_1990" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Senate elections, 1990">(1990)</a>.
</p><p>Often considered by his opponents to be too easygoing, Pell demonstrated his effectiveness as a campaigner.<sup id="cite_ref-Mulligan_2009_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mulligan_2009-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  During his first campaign, when he was accused of <a href="/wiki/Carpetbagger" title="Carpetbagger">carpetbagging</a>, Pell published newspaper advertisements featuring a photograph of his grand-uncle Duncan Pell, who had served as <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Governor_of_Rhode_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant Governor of Rhode Island">Lieutenant Governor of Rhode Island</a> during the 1860s, thus demonstrating Pell's association with the state.<sup id="cite_ref-Mulligan_2009_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mulligan_2009-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> When Briggs called him a "creampuff" during their 1966 campaign, Pell turned that to his advantage and mocked Briggs by obtaining an endorsement from a local baker's union.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During his first campaign, Pell also used his foreign experience to great advantage, impressing some largely immigrant audiences in person and on the radio by campaigning in their native languages.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personality_and_beliefs">Personality and beliefs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personality and beliefs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pell was known for unusual beliefs and behaviors, including wearing threadbare suits, using public transportation and purchasing cheap used automobiles despite his wealth, and an interest with the paranormal.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He also wore his father's belt as a memento, despite the fact that Herbert Pell was stouter than the rail-thin Claiborne Pell, requiring Claiborne Pell to wrap the belt around his waist twice to make it fit.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arrest_allegation">Arrest allegation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Arrest allegation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1972 author and former lobbyist Robert N. Winter-Berger wrote about Pell's alleged arrest during a raid on a <a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Village" title="Greenwich Village">Greenwich Village</a> homosexual bar in 1964. Pell denied the allegation. There were no police records, witness statements or other sources to corroborate Winter-Berger' allegation. Despite legal advice to sue for defamation, Pell declined, deciding that it would draw undue publicity to the allegations.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pell_education_grants">Pell education grants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Pell education grants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pell was largely responsible for the creation of "Basic Educational Opportunity Grants" in 1973, renamed <a href="/wiki/Pell_Grants" class="mw-redirect" title="Pell Grants">Pell Grants</a> in 1980, to provide financial aid funds to U.S. college students. Pell Grants initially provided for grants for prisoners but Congress later eliminated that provision. For some years there was more money available than was applied for.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was the main sponsor of the bill that created the <a href="/wiki/National_Endowment_for_the_Arts" title="National Endowment for the Arts">National Endowment for the Arts</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Endowment_for_the_Humanities" title="National Endowment for the Humanities">National Endowment for the Humanities</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> and was active as an advocate for mass transportation initiatives and domestic legislation facilitating and conforming to the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea">United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_Senate_career">Later Senate career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Later Senate career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pell served as Chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Senate_Foreign_Relations_Committee" class="mw-redirect" title="Senate Foreign Relations Committee">Senate Foreign Relations Committee</a> from 1987 to 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>  In 1990 he was re-elected to his sixth and last term of the Senate.
</p><p>In 1996, his last year in the Senate, Pell voted against the <a href="/wiki/Defense_of_Marriage_Act" title="Defense of Marriage Act">Defense of Marriage Act</a>, which banned the federal government from legally recognizing <a href="/wiki/Same-sex_marriage_in_the_United_States" title="Same-sex marriage in the United States">same-sex marriage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pell declined to seek re-election in 1996 and retired on January 3, 1997. Pell served in the Senate for thirty-six continuous years, making him the longest serving U.S. Senator in the history of Rhode Island.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>  He was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Jack_Reed_(politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jack Reed (politician)">Jack Reed</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement_and_death">Retirement and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Retirement and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After retirement, Pell lived in Newport and was a communicant of St. Columba's Church in Middletown. He occasionally attended public functions of organizations with which he was affiliated.
Towards the end of his life, he was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Parkinson%27s_disease" title="Parkinson&#39;s disease">Parkinson's disease</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Claiborne Pell died on January 1, 2009. His funeral was performed at Trinity Church in Newport, Rhode Island.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>  In addition to members of his family, Pell was eulogized by former President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a>, Senator <a href="/wiki/Edward_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward Kennedy">Edward Kennedy</a> and then Vice-President elect <a href="/wiki/Joe_Biden" title="Joe Biden">Joe Biden</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>  He was buried at St. Columba's Episcopal Church (Berkeley Memorial Cemetery) in <a href="/wiki/Middletown,_Rhode_Island" title="Middletown, Rhode Island">Middletown, Rhode Island</a>, near the graves of his son Herbert and his daughter Julia, who had predeceased him.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Soon after his death, the newspaper <i>The New York Times</i> termed Pell "the most formidable politician in Rhode Island history."<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Authorship.2C_recognition.2C_organizations"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Authorship,_recognition,_organizations">Authorship, recognition, organizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Authorship, recognition, organizations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Published_works">Published works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Published works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Senator Pell authored two books, <i>Megalopolis Unbound: The Supercity and the Transportation of Tomorrow</i> (1966), and <i>A Challenge of the Seven Seas</i> (1966), (co-author).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honors">Awards and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Awards and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Senator Pell received more than 40 honorary college degrees, including recognition from <a href="/wiki/Johnson_%26_Wales_University" title="Johnson &amp; Wales University">Johnson &amp; Wales University</a>, the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Vermont" title="University of Vermont">University of Vermont</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Massachusetts" title="University of Massachusetts">University of Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1987 Pell was among those selected for the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Environment_Programme" title="United Nations Environment Programme">United Nations Environment Programme's</a> <a href="/wiki/Global_500_Roll_of_Honour" title="Global 500 Roll of Honour">Global 500 Roll of Honour</a>, during the first year that award was established.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1988, Pell received the Foreign Language Advocacy Award from the Northeast Conference on the Teaching of Foreign Languages in recognition of his work in establishing the NEA, the NEH, and the Pell Grant Program.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 14, 1994, Pell was presented with the <a href="/wiki/Presidential_Citizens_Medal" title="Presidential Citizens Medal">Presidential Citizens Medal</a> from <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rhode Island's <a href="/wiki/Claiborne_Pell_Newport_Bridge" title="Claiborne Pell Newport Bridge">Newport Bridge</a> was renamed the "Claiborne Pell Bridge"<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> and the Pell Center of International Relations and Public Policy was established at <a href="/wiki/Salve_Regina_University" title="Salve Regina University">Salve Regina University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pell was a Chevalier of the French <a href="/wiki/Legion_of_Honor" class="mw-redirect" title="Legion of Honor">Legion of Honor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>  He also received the Knight Grand Cross of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Crown_of_Italy" title="Order of the Crown of Italy">Order of the Crown of Italy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His awards for service in the Coast Guard during the Second World War included the <a href="/wiki/American_Defense_Service_Medal" title="American Defense Service Medal">American Defense Service Medal</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Campaign_Medal" title="American Campaign Medal">American Campaign Medal</a>, <a href="/wiki/European-African-Middle_Eastern_Campaign_Medal" class="mw-redirect" title="European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal">European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_War_Two_Victory_Medal" class="mw-redirect" title="World War Two Victory Medal">World War Two Victory Medal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Memberships">Memberships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Claiborne_Pell&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Memberships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pell was a member of the Rhode Island <a href="/wiki/Society_of_the_Cincinnati" title="Society of the Cincinnati">Society of the Cincinnati</a>. Pell was also an honorary life member of the Rhode Island <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Colonial_Wars" class="mw-redirect" title="Society of Colonial Wars">Society of Colonial Wars</a> as well as a member of Spouting Rock Beach Association (<a href="/wiki/Bailey%27s_Beach" title="Bailey&#39;s Beach">Bailey's Beach</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Newport_Reading_Room" title="Newport Reading Room">Newport Reading Room</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
