<p>The <b>11th <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Games" title="Pan American Games">Pan American Games</a></b> were held in <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">Havana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> from August 2 to August 18, 1991. There were 4,519 athletes from 39 countries of the <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Sports_Organization" class="mw-redirect" title="Pan American Sports Organization">PASO</a> community, with events in 33 different sports. The main stadium was the <a href="/wiki/Estadio_Panamericano,_Havana" title="Estadio Panamericano, Havana">Estadio Panamericano</a>, a multi-use stadium in Havana that holds 50,000 people.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Host_city_election">Host city election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Pan_American_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Host city election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Bids_for_the_1991_Pan_American_Games" title="Bids for the 1991 Pan American Games">Bids for the 1991 Pan American Games</a></div>
<p>Havana was the only non-withdrawn bid to host the <b><a class="mw-selflink selflink">1991 Pan American Games</a></b>. At the Pan American Games (ODEPA) Assembly, from November 12 to 14, 1986, in <a href="/wiki/Bridgetown" title="Bridgetown">Bridgetown</a>, Barbados, <a href="/wiki/Mar_del_Plata" title="Mar del Plata">Mar del Plata</a> withdrew its bid, leaving Havana as the winner to host the Games.<sup id="cite_ref-photos_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-photos-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/London,_Ontario" title="London, Ontario">London, Ontario</a>, Canada also submitted a bid, but withdrew from the bid process due to the federal government prohibiting all funding from any international multi-sport events (aside from the <a href="/wiki/1988_Winter_Olympics" title="1988 Winter Olympics">1988 Calgary Winter Olympics</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mascot">Mascot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Pan_American_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mascot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:217px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1991_Pan_American_Games_Mascot.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/2/2f/1991_Pan_American_Games_Mascot.jpg" decoding="async" width="215" height="288" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="215" data-file-height="288" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1991_Pan_American_Games_Mascot.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mascotte Tocopan Pan Am Games 1991</div></div></div>
<p>The 1991 Games' mascot named <i>Tocopan</i>, was a combination of the country's national bird "Tocororo" and the first three letters of Panamerican.
