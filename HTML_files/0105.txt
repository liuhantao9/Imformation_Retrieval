<p><b>María Isabel Preysler Arrastía</b> (born February 18, 1951), better known as <b>Isabel Preysler</b>,  is a <a href="/wiki/Filipina" class="mw-redirect" title="Filipina">Filipina</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-R._Arce_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R._Arce-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Socialite" title="Socialite">socialite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Television_host" class="mw-redirect" title="Television host">television host</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isabel_Preysler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Preysler was born in <a href="/wiki/Manila" title="Manila">Manila</a>, Philippines, the third of six children to a wealthy family. She attended a private <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> school; Her father, Carlos Preysler y Pérez de Tagle, was the executive director of <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Airlines" title="Philippine Airlines">Philippine Airlines</a> and on the Board of Directors for the Banco Español de Manila (Spanish Bank of Manila),<sup id="cite_ref-Isabel_Preysler_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Isabel_Preysler-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> while her mother, María Beatriz Arrastia y Reynares, was the owner of a real estate company in Manila.<sup id="cite_ref-Isabel_Preysler_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Isabel_Preysler-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She is the niece of actress <a href="/wiki/Neile_Adams" title="Neile Adams">Neile Adams</a>, who is her mother's half-sister. She is also a first cousin once removed of American actor <a href="/wiki/Steven_R._McQueen" title="Steven R. McQueen">Steven R. McQueen</a>, who is Neile's grandson.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Pérez de Tagle family, a Filipino <a href="/wiki/Cadet_branch" title="Cadet branch">cadet branch</a> of the Spanish <a href="/wiki/Dynasty" title="Dynasty">dynasty</a> that has held the <a href="/wiki/Marquisate_of_Altamira" class="mw-redirect" title="Marquisate of Altamira">marquisate of Altamira</a> since the 1600s, produced <a href="/wiki/Abac%C3%A1" title="Abacá">abacá</a> and <a href="/wiki/Copra" title="Copra">copra</a>. <i>Abacá</i> was the main ingredient in ropes and other products prior to the invention of <a href="/wiki/Nylon" title="Nylon">nylon</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The Arrastía family is from the province of <a href="/wiki/Pampanga" title="Pampanga">Pampanga</a>. Their old town was <a href="/wiki/Lubao,_Pampanga" title="Lubao, Pampanga">Lubao</a>, site of the ancient San Nicolas Tolentino Cathedral.  The Arrastías used to own one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Hacienda" title="Hacienda">haciendas</a> there, most of which were utilized to plant rice and sugarcane.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isabel_Preysler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During her youth, Preysler was a <a href="/wiki/Model_(person)" title="Model (person)">model</a> who participated in beauty pageants and charity events for the <a href="/wiki/Sheraton_Hotels_and_Resorts" title="Sheraton Hotels and Resorts">Sheraton Hotels and Resorts</a> in Manila and went on to win titles in several events. At the age of 16, she migrated to <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> to live with her uncle and aunt and to study at Mary Ward College, an Irish Catholic University in Spain, where she studied accounting.
</p><p>Preysler began working as a journalist for Spanish celebrity-news magazine <i><a href="/wiki/%C2%A1Hola!" title="¡Hola!">¡Hola!</a></i> in 1970, and her first interviewee was her future husband Julio Iglesias. In 1984, she hosted a Spanish lifestyle television programme, <i>Hoy en Casa</i>, and has hosted and appeared in various programs since. In May 2001, she was Prince <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Charles</a>' guest of honour for the opening of his Spanish Garden at the <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Flower_Show" title="Chelsea Flower Show">Chelsea Flower Show</a> in England. She was his guest of honor again in 2005 at a garden party during a holiday to Spain by the Royal Crown. In 2004, Preysler became Spain's welcoming host for <a href="/wiki/David_Beckham" title="David Beckham">David</a> and <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Beckham" title="Victoria Beckham">Victoria Beckham</a> when she hosted a welcoming party at her house for the celebrity couple. She became close friends with Victoria and was often photographed shopping with her during their stay in Madrid.
</p><p>Preysler continues to be the national spokesmodel for <a href="/wiki/Ferrero_Rocher" title="Ferrero Rocher">Ferrero Rocher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Su%C3%A1rez" class="mw-redirect" title="Suárez">Suárez</a> jewelry, <a href="/wiki/Manolo_Blahnik" title="Manolo Blahnik">Manolo Blahnik</a> shoes, <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler</a> cars and <a href="/wiki/Porcelanosa" title="Porcelanosa">Porcelanosa</a> tiles, for which <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> Hollywood actor <a href="/wiki/George_Clooney" title="George Clooney">George Clooney</a> recently worked with her in 2006 to represent the brand in an advertising campaign.
</p><p>Readers at <i>Hola magazine</i> voted Preysler as the most elegant and best-dressed woman in Spain for 1991, 2002, 2006 and 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, Preysler was also honored along with <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yoko_Ono" title="Yoko Ono">Yoko Ono</a> among others with the Women Together Award, which honors women for their philanthropical contributions to the United Nations in New York, making her the first woman of Filipino descent in history to win the award.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, she and her daughters were invited by <a href="/wiki/Prince_Charles" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Charles">Prince Charles</a> to be guests of honour at his London home, <a href="/wiki/Clarence_House" title="Clarence House">Clarence House</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rob10dec_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rob10dec-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isabel_Preysler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1970, Preysler was introduced to a retired football player named <a href="/wiki/Julio_Iglesias" title="Julio Iglesias">Julio Iglesias</a>, who just signed a recording contract to become a singer. Iglesias invited her to watch a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Juan_Pardo_(singer)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Juan Pardo (singer) (page does not exist)">Juan Pardo</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_Pardo_(cantante)" class="extiw" title="es:Juan Pardo (cantante)">es</a>&#93;</span> concert.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The couple was married seven months later on January 29, 1971. They were married for seven years and the couple had three children, <a href="/wiki/Chabeli_Iglesias" title="Chabeli Iglesias">María Isabel</a> (b. 1971), <a href="/wiki/Julio_Iglesias,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Julio Iglesias, Jr.">Julio José</a> (b. 1973) and <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Iglesias" title="Enrique Iglesias">Enrique Miguel</a> (b. 1975). They divorced in 1979. After they had divorced and moved on, in 1982 she sent her children away to Miami to live with their father because of kidnapping threats she was receiving.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Preysler married the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marquis_of_Gri%C3%B1%C3%B3n&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Marquis of Griñón (page does not exist)">Marquis of Griñón</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carlos_Falc%C3%B3&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carlos Falcó (page does not exist)">Carlos Falcó</a> on March 23, 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt23may_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt23may-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The couple had one daughter, Tamara Isabel Falcó (b. 1981).
</p><p>Her third marriage was to the former Spanish finance minister <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Boyer" title="Miguel Boyer">Miguel Boyer</a> (died September 29, 2014), the couple had one daughter, Ana Isabel Boyer (b. 1989).<sup id="cite_ref-nyt23may_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt23may-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She currently is in a relationship with Peruvian <a href="/wiki/Nobel_Prize_in_Literature" title="Nobel Prize in Literature">Nobel laureate</a> writer, journalist, and politician <a href="/wiki/Mario_Vargas_Llosa" title="Mario Vargas Llosa">Mario Vargas Llosa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-elpais17nov_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elpais17nov-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1987, her two sisters immigrated to Spain with their families to be closer to Isabel. She holds dual citizenship in both the Philippines and Spain.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
