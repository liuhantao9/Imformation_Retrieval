<p><b>Emley Association Football Club</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> club based in <a href="/wiki/Emley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Emley, West Yorkshire">Emley, West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. They are currently members of <a href="/wiki/North_West_Counties_Football_League" title="North West Counties Football League">North West Counties League</a> Division One North and play at the Welfare Ground.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emley_A.F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_F.C." title="Wakefield F.C.">Emley club</a> had been members of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Football_League" title="Yorkshire Football League">Yorkshire League</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Counties_East_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Counties East League">Northern Counties East League</a>, before winning promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Premier_League" title="Northern Premier League">Northern Premier League</a> in 1989. In 2000, new ground grading regulations introduced by the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Premier_League" title="Northern Premier League">Northern Premier League</a> forced Emley to relocate to <a href="/wiki/Belle_Vue_(Wakefield)" title="Belle Vue (Wakefield)">Belle Vue</a> in Wakefield, although the reserves continued to play at the Welfare Ground. In 2002 the club was renamed Wakefield &amp; Emley, and when the reserve team was disbanded in 2005, supporters of the original club decided to establish a new club based in Emley under the name A.F.C. Emley.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The following year Wakefield &amp; Emley was renamed Wakefield,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and remained in the Northern Premier League until disbanding in 2014.
</p><p>The new Emley club joined Division One of the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Football_League" class="mw-redirect" title="West Yorkshire Football League">West Yorkshire League</a> for the 2005–06 season.<sup id="cite_ref-FCHD_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FCHD-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After finishing third in their first season, they were promoted to Division One of the Northern Counties East League.<sup id="cite_ref-FCHD_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FCHD-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_Northern_Counties_East_Football_League" title="2015–16 Northern Counties East Football League">2015–16</a> they finished fourth in Division One, qualifying for the promotion play-offs. After beating <a href="/wiki/Penistone_Church_F.C." title="Penistone Church F.C.">Penistone Church</a> 1–0 in the semi-finals, they lost 4–3 on penalties to <a href="/wiki/Bottesford_Town_F.C." title="Bottesford Town F.C.">Bottesford Town</a> in the final after a 1–1 draw. Striker Ashley Flynn finished the season with 73 goals, a league record.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Northern_Counties_East_Football_League" title="2016–17 Northern Counties East Football League">2016–17</a> the club finished third, missing out on automatic promotion on goal difference; they went on to lose 3–1 to Penistone Church in the play-off semi-finals.
</p><p>In March 2019 it was announced that the club would be renamed Emley A.F.C. from the 2019–20 season, five years after the original club was dissolved.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The club were transferred to Division One North of the <a href="/wiki/North_West_Counties_Football_League" title="North West Counties Football League">North West Counties League</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_North_West_Counties_Football_League" title="2019–20 North West Counties Football League">2019–20 season</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ground">Ground</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Emley_A.F.C.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ground">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>
The club plays at the Welfare Ground in Emley. It has a capacity of 2,000, of which 1,000 is covered and 330 seated.<sup id="cite_ref-NCEL_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NCEL-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> </p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Emleystand.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Emleystand.jpg/220px-Emleystand.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="391" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Emleystand.jpg/330px-Emleystand.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Emleystand.jpg/440px-Emleystand.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2340" data-file-height="4160" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Emleystand.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The seated stand at AFC Emley</div></div></div>
