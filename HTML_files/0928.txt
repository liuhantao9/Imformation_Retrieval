<p><b>Wallingford</b> is a town in <a href="/wiki/New_Haven_County,_Connecticut" title="New Haven County, Connecticut">New Haven County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The population was 45,135 at the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Census_2010_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The urban center of the town is the Wallingford Center <a href="/wiki/Census-designated_place" title="Census-designated place">census-designated place</a>, with a population of 18,209 at the 2010 census.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The community was named after <a href="/wiki/Wallingford,_Oxfordshire" title="Wallingford, Oxfordshire">Wallingford</a>, in England.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wallingford,_Connecticut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_General_Assembly" title="Connecticut General Assembly">Connecticut General Assembly</a> created the town on October 10, 1667. This original plot of land near the <a href="/wiki/Quinnipiac_River" title="Quinnipiac River">Quinnipiac River</a> is now considered Main Street. Starting on May 12, 1670 there were 126 people who lived in temporary housing, and five years later in 1675 there were 40 permanent homes.<sup id="cite_ref-wallingford1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wallingford1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1697 Wallingford was the site of the last <a href="/wiki/Witchcraft_trial" class="mw-redirect" title="Witchcraft trial">witchcraft trial</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a>. Winifred Benham was thrice tried for witchcraft and acquitted all three times.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/1878_Wallingford_tornado" title="1878 Wallingford tornado">1878 Wallingford tornado</a> struck on August 9 of that year. It killed at least 29 and likely 34 people in Wallingford, the most by any tornado event in <a href="/wiki/History_of_Connecticut" title="History of Connecticut">Connecticut history</a>.
</p><p>Wallingford has diversified its commercial and industrial base over the past decade attracting high-technology industries as compared to traditional heavy manufacturing. It is the home of a large variety of industries and major corporations spanning the spectrum of the medical, health care, service, high-tech specialty metal manufacturing and research development. The development of the Barnes Industrial Park, Casimir Pulaski Industrial Park, Wharton Brook Industrial Park, and the South Turnpike Road area have greatly contributed to this transition. The Bristol-Myers Squibb Company, the town's largest taxpayer, has established a research and development facility in Wallingford's MedWay Industrial Park. An Interchange Zone which permits very restrictive commercial development of office parks, research and development centers and hotels has been created at the intersection of <a href="/wiki/Interstate_91_in_Connecticut" class="mw-redirect" title="Interstate 91 in Connecticut">Interstate 91</a> and <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Route_68" title="Connecticut Route 68">Route 68</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-wallingford1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wallingford1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In terms of Wallingford's manufacturing and design history, silver-producing companies like <a href="/wiki/Simpson,_Hall,_Miller_%26_Co." title="Simpson, Hall, Miller &amp; Co.">Simpson, Hall, Miller &amp; Co.</a> and <a href="/wiki/R._Wallace_%26_Sons" title="R. Wallace &amp; Sons">R. Wallace &amp; Sons</a> are of particular note. Simpson, Hall, Miller &amp; Co. as well as Wallingford's Watrous Manufacturing later became part of the <a href="/wiki/International_Silver_Company" title="International Silver Company">International Silver Company</a>, which was headquartered in the neighboring city of <a href="/wiki/Meriden,_Connecticut" title="Meriden, Connecticut">Meriden</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Stern_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stern-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wallingford,_Connecticut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town of Wallingford has both public and private education. The Wallingford Public School System consists of eight elementary schools: Cook Hill, E. C. Stevens, Highland, and Moses Y. Beach Elementary Schools covering Pre-K to second grade and Parker Farms, Pond Hill, Rock Hill, and Mary G. Fritz Elementary Schools covering grades three to five; two middle schools, Dag Hammarskjold and James H. Moran; and two high schools, <a href="/wiki/Lyman_Hall_High_School" title="Lyman Hall High School">Lyman Hall</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_T._Sheehan_High_School" title="Mark T. Sheehan High School">Mark T. Sheehan</a>. Wallingford has a private sector of schools offering the following: <a href="/wiki/Choate_Rosemary_Hall" title="Choate Rosemary Hall">Choate Rosemary Hall</a>, Heritage Baptist Academy, and Holy Trinity School.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wallingford,_Connecticut&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the town has a total area of 39.9&#160;square miles (103.3&#160;km²), of which 39.0&#160;square miles (101.1&#160;km²) is land and 0.9&#160;square miles (2.2&#160;km²), or 2.16%, is water.
</p><p>The town of Wallingford sits astride the <a href="/wiki/Quinnipiac_River" title="Quinnipiac River">Quinnipiac River</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/New_Haven_County,_Connecticut" title="New Haven County, Connecticut">New Haven County</a>. It is 5 miles (8&#160;km) south of <a href="/wiki/Meriden,_Connecticut" title="Meriden, Connecticut">Meriden</a> and about 13 miles (21&#160;km) north of <a href="/wiki/New_Haven,_Connecticut" title="New Haven, Connecticut">New Haven</a>. Towns bordering Wallingford are Cheshire, Durham, Hamden, Meriden, Middlefield, North Branford and North Haven. Situated in the <a href="/wiki/Hartford,_Connecticut" title="Hartford, Connecticut">Hartford</a>-New Haven-<a href="/wiki/Springfield,_Massachusetts" title="Springfield, Massachusetts">Springfield</a> corridor, Wallingford is traversed by <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_5" title="U.S. Route 5">U.S. Route 5</a>, <a href="/wiki/Interstate_91" title="Interstate 91">Interstate 91</a>, and State Highways <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Route_15" title="Connecticut Route 15">Route 15</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wilbur_Cross_Parkway" title="Wilbur Cross Parkway">Wilbur Cross Parkway</a>), <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Route_68" title="Connecticut Route 68">Route 68</a>, <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Route_71" title="Connecticut Route 71">Route 71</a> and <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Route_150" title="Connecticut Route 150">Route 150</a>.
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup id="cite_ref-GR2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> of 2000, there were 43,026 people, 16,697 households, and 11,587 families residing in the town.  The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,102.7 people per square mile (425.7/km²).  There were 17,306 housing units at an average density of 443.5 per square mile (171.2/km²).  The racial makeup of the town was 94.77% <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 1.02% <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.17% <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 1.75% <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 1.16% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 1.14% from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 4.52% of the population.
</p><p>There were 16,697 households out of which 32.3% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 57.3% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 9.0% had a female householder with no husband present, and 30.6% were non-families. Of all households 25.6% were made up of individuals and 10.3% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older.  The average household size was 2.52 and the average family size was 3.07. The median household income in Wallingford is $91,317. The median family income is $101,239. The per capita income in Wallingford is $40,903.
</p><p>In the town, the population was spread out with 24.0% under the age of 18, 6.0% from 18 to 24, 30.7% from 25 to 44, 24.0% from 45 to 64, and 15.2% who were 65 years of age or older.  The median age was 39 years. For every 100 females, there were 93.0 males.  For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 89.7 males.
</p>
