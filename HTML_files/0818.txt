<p>The <b>Solomon Islands campaign</b> was a major <a href="/wiki/Military_campaign" title="Military campaign">campaign</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_War" title="Pacific War">Pacific War</a> of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.  The campaign began with <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japanese</a> landings and occupation of several areas in the <a href="/wiki/British_Solomon_Islands" title="British Solomon Islands">British Solomon Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bougainville_Island" title="Bougainville Island">Bougainville</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Territory_of_New_Guinea" title="Territory of New Guinea">Territory of New Guinea</a>, during the first six months of 1942.  The Japanese occupied these locations and began the construction of several naval and air bases with the goals of protecting the flank of the Japanese offensive in New Guinea, establishing a security barrier for the major Japanese base at <a href="/wiki/Rabaul" title="Rabaul">Rabaul</a> on <a href="/wiki/New_Britain" title="New Britain">New Britain</a>, and providing bases for interdicting supply lines between the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> powers of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_New_Zealand" title="Dominion of New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.
</p><p>The Allies, to defend their communication and supply lines in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">South Pacific</a>, supported a counteroffensive in New Guinea, isolated the Japanese base at Rabaul, and counterattacked the Japanese in the Solomons with landings on <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal" title="Guadalcanal">Guadalcanal</a> (see <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Guadalcanal Campaign">Guadalcanal Campaign</a>) and small neighboring islands on 7 August 1942.  These landings initiated a series of combined-arms battles between the two adversaries, beginning with the Guadalcanal landing and continuing with several battles in the central and northern Solomons, on and around <a href="/wiki/New_Georgia" title="New Georgia">New Georgia</a> Island, and <a href="/wiki/Bougainville_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Bougainville Campaign">Bougainville</a> Island.
</p><p>In a campaign of attrition fought on land, on sea, and in the air, the Allies wore the Japanese down, inflicting irreplaceable losses on Japanese military assets.  The Allies retook some of the Solomon Islands (although resistance continued until the end of the war), and they also isolated and neutralized some Japanese positions, which were then bypassed.  The Solomon Islands campaign then converged with the <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea_campaign" title="New Guinea campaign">New Guinea campaign</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solomon_Islands_campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Strategic_background">Strategic background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solomon_Islands_campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Strategic background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 7, 1941, after failing to resolve a dispute with the United States over Japan's <a href="/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War" title="Second Sino-Japanese War">actions in China</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Indochina" title="French Indochina">French Indochina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">the Japanese attacked</a> the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Pacific_Fleet" title="United States Pacific Fleet">US Pacific fleet</a> at <a href="/wiki/Pearl_Harbor" title="Pearl Harbor">Pearl Harbor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>. The surprise attack crippled most of the U.S. Pacific Fleet's battleships and started a war between the two nations. Attacks on British Empire possessions in the Pacific, beginning with an attack on Hong Kong almost simultaneously with the Pearl Harbor attack, brought the United Kingdom, Australia and New Zealand into the conflict.  The Japanese sought to neutralize the U.S. and Royal navies, seize possessions rich in natural resources, and obtain strategic military bases to defend their far-flung empire.  In the words of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Japanese Navy's</a> <a href="/wiki/Combined_Fleet" title="Combined Fleet">Combined Fleet</a> Secret Order Number One, dated November 1, 1941, the goals of the initial Japanese campaigns in the impending war were to, "(eject) British and American strength from the Netherlands Indies and the Philippines, (and) to establish a policy of autonomous self-sufficiency and economic independence."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Empire of Japan accomplished its initial strategic objectives in the first six months of the war, capturing <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Malaya" title="British Malaya">Malaya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wake_Island" title="Wake Island">Wake Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Britain" title="New Britain">New Britain</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Islands" title="Gilbert Islands">Gilbert Islands</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">Guam</a>.  A Japanese goal was to establish an effective defensive perimeter from <a href="/wiki/British_India" class="mw-redirect" title="British India">British India</a> on the west, through the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a> on the south, and to island bases in the south and central Pacific as its southeastern line of defense.  Anchoring its defensive positions in the South Pacific was the major Japanese army and navy base at <a href="/wiki/Rabaul" title="Rabaul">Rabaul</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Britain" title="New Britain">New Britain</a>, which had been <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Rabaul_(1942)" title="Battle of Rabaul (1942)">captured from the Australians</a> in January 1942.  In March and April, Japanese forces occupied and began constructing an airfield at Buka in northern Bougainville, as well as an airfield and naval base at Buin, in southern Bougainville.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Japanese_advance_into_the_Solomons">Japanese advance into the Solomons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solomon_Islands_campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Japanese advance into the Solomons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 1942, the Japanese Army and Navy together initiated <a href="/wiki/Operation_Mo" title="Operation Mo">Operation Mo</a>, a joint plan to capture <a href="/wiki/Port_Moresby" title="Port Moresby">Port Moresby</a> in New Guinea.  Also part of the plan was a Navy operation to capture <a href="/wiki/Tulagi" title="Tulagi">Tulagi</a> in the southern Solomons.  The objective of the operation was for the Japanese to extend their southern perimeter and to establish bases to support possible future advances to seize <a href="/wiki/Nauru" title="Nauru">Nauru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Banaba_Island" title="Banaba Island">Ocean Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia" title="New Caledonia">New Caledonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a> and thereby cut the supply lines between Australia and the United States, with the goal of reducing or eliminating Australia as a threat to Japanese positions in the South Pacific.  The Japanese Navy also proposed a future invasion of Australia, but the Army answered that it currently lacked enough troops to support such an operation.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Japanese naval forces <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Tulagi_(May_1942)" title="Invasion of Tulagi (May 1942)">captured Tulagi</a> but its invasion of Port Moresby was repulsed at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Coral_Sea" title="Battle of the Coral Sea">Battle of the Coral Sea</a>.  Shortly thereafter, the Japanese Navy established small garrisons on the other northern and central Solomon Islands.  One month later, the Japanese Combined Fleet lost four of its fleet aircraft carriers at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Midway" title="Battle of Midway">Battle of Midway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Allies countered the threats to Australia by a build-up of troops and aircraft,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> with the aim of implementing plans to approach and reconquer the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.  In March 1942 Admiral <a href="/wiki/Ernest_King" title="Ernest King">Ernest King</a>, then Commander-in Chief of the U.S. Fleet, had advocated an offense from <a href="/wiki/New_Hebrides" title="New Hebrides">New Hebrides</a> through the Solomon Islands to the <a href="/wiki/Bismarck_Archipelago" title="Bismarck Archipelago">Bismarck Archipelago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  Following the victory at Midway, General <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">Douglas MacArthur</a>, who had taken command of the <a href="/wiki/South_West_Pacific_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="South West Pacific Area">South West Pacific Area</a>, proposed a lightning offense to retake Rabaul, which the Japanese were fortifying and using as a base of operations. The United States Navy advocated a more gradual approach from New Guinea and up the Solomon Island chain. These competing proposals were resolved by Admiral King and U.S. Army Chief of Staff General <a href="/wiki/George_C._Marshall" class="mw-redirect" title="George C. Marshall">George C. Marshall</a>, who adopted a three-task plan.  Task One was the capture of the island of <a href="/wiki/Tulagi" title="Tulagi">Tulagi</a> in the Solomons.  Task Two was an advance along the New Guinea coast.  Task Three was the capture of Rabaul.  Task One, implemented by a directive of the Joint Chiefs of Staff on 2 July 1942 and named the initial attacks Operation Watchtower,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> became the Solomon Islands campaign.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Course_of_campaign">Course of campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Solomon_Islands_campaign&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Course of campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 7 August 1942 <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">U.S. Marines</a> landed on <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal" title="Guadalcanal">Guadalcanal</a>, beginning the <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Guadalcanal Campaign">Guadalcanal Campaign</a>. The Allies created a combined air formation, the <a href="/wiki/Cactus_Air_Force" title="Cactus Air Force">Cactus Air Force</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;d&#93;</a></sup> establishing air superiority during daylight hours. The Japanese then resorted to nightly resupply missions which they called <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Express" title="Tokyo Express">"Rat Transportation"</a> (and the Allies called "the Tokyo Express") through <a href="/wiki/New_Georgia_Sound" title="New Georgia Sound">New Georgia Sound</a> (a.k.a. "The Slot"). Many pitched battles were fought trying to stop Japanese supplies from getting through. So many ships were lost by both sides during the Guadalcanal campaign that the southern end of New Georgia Sound, the area north of Guadalcanal previously called Savo Sound, became known as "<a href="/wiki/Ironbottom_Sound" title="Ironbottom Sound">Ironbottom Sound</a>".
</p><p>Allied success in the Solomon Islands campaign prevented the Japanese from cutting Australia and New Zealand off from the United States. <a href="/wiki/Operation_Cartwheel" title="Operation Cartwheel">Operation Cartwheel</a> &#8212; the Allied grand strategy for the Solomons and <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea_campaign" title="New Guinea campaign">New Guinea campaigns</a>  &#8212; launched on June 30, 1943, isolated and <a href="/wiki/Pacification_of_Rabaul" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacification of Rabaul">neutralized Rabaul</a> and destroyed much of Japan's sea and air supremacy. This opened the way for Allied forces to <a href="/wiki/Philippines_campaign_(1944%E2%80%9345)" class="mw-redirect" title="Philippines campaign (1944–45)">recapture the Philippines</a> and cut off Japan from its crucial resource areas in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_East_Indies" class="mw-redirect" title="Netherlands East Indies">Netherlands East Indies</a>.
</p><p>The Solomons campaign culminated in the often bitter fighting of the <a href="/wiki/Bougainville_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Bougainville Campaign">Bougainville Campaign</a>, which continued until the end of the war.
</p>
