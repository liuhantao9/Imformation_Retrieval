<p><i><b>Storyteller</b></i> is the fifth studio album recorded by American singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>. It was released on October 23, 2015, by <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Nashville" title="Sony Music Nashville">Sony Music Nashville</a>. Following the release and success of her fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(album)" title="Blown Away (album)">Blown Away</a></i> (2012), Underwood began working on <i>Storyteller</i> in early 2014. However, she tentatively suspended most work on the album because of her pregnancy with her first child. In the midterm, Underwood released her first greatest hits record, <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits:_Decade_Number_1" title="Greatest Hits: Decade Number 1">Greatest Hits: Decade #1</a></i>, on December 9, 2014, to much success. After the birth of her son, she took some additional time off before going back into the studio in early 2015 to finish working on the album.
</p><p>Met with mostly positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>, <i>Storyteller</i> became a commercial success and set multiple records upon its release. By debuting at number two on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, it made Underwood the only country artist in history to have all of her first five studio albums debut at number one or two, and with a number one debut on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Top Country Albums</a> chart, it made her the only artist to score six consecutive number one albums on the chart. <i>Storyteller</i> also performed well internationally upon its release, including a number four debut in <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">Australia</a>, number three in <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Albums_Chart" title="Canadian Albums Chart">Canada</a> and thirteen in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">United Kingdom</a>. As of October 2016, the album has been certified Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-carrieunderwood.fm_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-carrieunderwood.fm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It marks Underwood's sixth consecutive album to go either Platinum or multi-Platinum.
</p><p>The album has produced the back-to-back <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart number-one singles "<a href="/wiki/Heartbeat_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Heartbeat (Carrie Underwood song)">Heartbeat</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Church_Bells_(song)" title="Church Bells (song)">Church Bells</a>", along with the number-two singles "<a href="/wiki/Smoke_Break" title="Smoke Break">Smoke Break</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Dirty_Laundry_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Dirty Laundry (Carrie Underwood song)">Dirty Laundry</a>." "Smoke Break", "Hearbeat" and "Dirty Laundry" also topped the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts#Canadian_charts" title="Billboard charts">Canada Country</a> chart. The first three singles have been certified gold by the RIAA.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Storyteller</i> is Underwood's last album under the Arista Nashville and Sony Music Group, following her new deal with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Nashville" class="mw-redirect" title="Capitol Nashville">Capitol Nashville</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Storyteller_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the release and success of her fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(album)" title="Blown Away (album)">Blown Away</a></i>, in 2012 and its international supporting tour, <a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_Tour" title="Blown Away Tour">Blown Away Tour</a> (2012-2013), Underwood confirmed, in August 2013, that she had begun planning on a new album and would start working on it in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, she released her first <a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits:_Decade_Number_1" title="Greatest Hits: Decade Number 1">greatest hits album</a> in late 2014 to much success, while pregnant with her first child.<sup id="cite_ref-EW_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EW-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After giving birth, in February 2015, Underwood went back into the studio to finish working on the album.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On August 20, 2015, Underwood announced the album's title and release date via live <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> stream.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Underwood has described Storyteller as being more laid-back and more <a href="/wiki/Twang" title="Twang">twangy</a> as opposed to her last studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(album)" title="Blown Away (album)">Blown Away</a></i> (2012).<sup id="cite_ref-EW_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EW-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> For this album, Underwood worked with frequent collaborators such as <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Gorley" title="Ashley Gorley">Ashley Gorley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_DeStefano" title="Chris DeStefano">Chris DeStefano</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Hodges" title="David Hodges">David Hodges</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brett_James" title="Brett James">Brett James</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Bright_(record_producer)" title="Mark Bright (record producer)">Mark Bright</a>, although Underwood also worked with new collaborators such as <a href="/wiki/Liz_Rose" title="Liz Rose">Liz Rose</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zach_Crowell" title="Zach Crowell">Zach Crowell</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jay_Joyce" title="Jay Joyce">Jay Joyce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Joyce produced the album's lead single "Smoke Break", which Underwood wrote with DeStefano and Lindsey.<sup id="cite_ref-Smoke_Break_lyrics_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smoke_Break_lyrics-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Storyteller_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood revealed the album's track listing on September 9, 2015, via her <a href="/wiki/Instagram" title="Instagram">Instagram</a> account. On September 10, 2015, Underwood appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a></i> and also performed "Smoke Break". On September 21, 2015, Underwood performed the song at the <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music_Festival" title="Apple Music Festival">Apple Music Festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-New_Tour_and_London_Performance_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_Tour_and_London_Performance-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 9, 2015, "Heartbeat" was released as a promotional single from the album.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The song features background vocals by country artist <a href="/wiki/Sam_Hunt_(musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sam Hunt (musician)">Sam Hunt</a> . It sold 23,000 copies and debuted at number 26 on <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart for the week ending October 31, 2015. As of October 26, 2015 the song has sold 31,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The song also entered the Scottish Singles Chart at number 80.
</p><p>"Renegade Runaway" was released as the second promotional single on October 16, 2015. It sold 13,000 copies and debuted at number 34 on Hot Country Songs chart. A third and final promotional single, "What I Never Knew I Always Wanted" was released October 19, 2015. It sold 9,000 copies and entered the Hot Country Songs chart at number 38 and reached number 32. As of November 21, 2015, it sold 21,000 copies. All three singles were made available for download on iTunes, Amazon, and other digital retailers with a pre-order of the full <i>Storyteller</i> album.
</p><p>In support of the album, Underwood returned to headline the <a href="/wiki/C2C:_Country_to_Country" title="C2C: Country to Country">C2C: Country to Country</a> festival for the first time since 2013, making her the first artist to headline the festival twice. She performed concerts in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oslo" title="Oslo">Oslo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Storyteller_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Smoke_Break" title="Smoke Break">Smoke Break</a>", the lead single off <i>Storyteller</i>, was released on August 21, 2015. The song set an all-time record for the largest first-week radio adds in the history of <i>Country Aircheck</i>, with 145 <a href="/wiki/Mediabase" title="Mediabase">Mediabase</a> adds and 159 total <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Billboard</a></i> and <i>Country Aircheck</i> reporting stations lined up for the song's official airplay impact date.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-History_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-History-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It reached number four on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart and number two on the <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart.
</p><p>"<a href="/wiki/Heartbeat_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Heartbeat (Carrie Underwood song)">Heartbeat</a>" was released as the second single from <i>Storyteller</i> at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2015" title="American Music Awards of 2015">2015 American Music Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The song made its official impact on country radio on November 30, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-country_adds_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-country_adds-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It has reached number two on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart and number one on the <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart, becoming her 14th leader on the chart, widening her record for the most number-one's among women in the history of the chart.
</p><p>"Chaser" was released as a single exclusively to UK radio stations on April 1, 2016. It marked Underwood's first limited release to European stations.
</p><p>"<a href="/wiki/Church_Bells_(song)" title="Church Bells (song)">Church Bells</a>" is the third single from "Storyteller"  in the US, and was released to country radio on April 11, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> It has reached number two on the Hot Country Songs chart and number one on the Country Airplay chart, becoming Underwood's 15th number one on that chart.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The song was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Solo_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Solo Performance">Best Country Solo Performance</a> at the <a href="/wiki/59th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="59th Annual Grammy Awards">59th Grammy Awards</a>.
</p><p>On August 12, 2016, it was confirmed that "<a href="/wiki/Dirty_Laundry_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Dirty Laundry (Carrie Underwood song)">Dirty Laundry</a>" would be serviced as the fourth single in the United States and Canada, and was released to country radio on September 5, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It peaked at number two on Country Airplay chart and number three on Hot Country Songs chart.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tour">Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Storyteller_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Underwood began the <a href="/wiki/Storyteller_Tour:_Stories_in_the_Round" title="Storyteller Tour: Stories in the Round">Storyteller Tour: Stories in the Round</a> on January 30, 2016, in <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">Jacksonville, Florida</a> to support the album.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The first leg of the tour was completed by May 31, 2016. On July 21, 2016, <i>Billboard</i> published their mid-year touring update for the highest-grossing tours between November 10, 2015 and June 6, 2016 and Storyteller Tour was ranked the tenth highest-grossing tour for that period with a total gross of $29,993,822  and total attendance of 449,396.
</p><p>The tour was concluded on November 28, 2016. It has grossed $51,204,491 and had played to a million fans.
</p>

<p><i>Storyteller</i> received generally positive reviews from critics. On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream publications, the album has an <a href="/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean" title="Weighted arithmetic mean">average</a> score of 74, based on nine reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-MC_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MC-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_Erlewine" title="Stephen Thomas Erlewine">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</a> of <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> said the record "demands attention and it deserves it, too",<sup id="cite_ref-allmusicreview_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allmusicreview-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> while <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boston_Globe" title="The Boston Globe">The Boston Globe</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s Sarah Rodman called it Underwood's best album yet.<sup id="cite_ref-bostonglobereview_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bostonglobereview-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Jewly Hight from <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> wrote, "Unlike newer country acts who can sound like they’re merely co-starring with their own faddish production, Underwood commands the spotlight, balancing the well-established extremes of her on-stage persona -- Midwestern girl-next-door and imperious diva -- within these freshened-up aesthetic frames."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a> wrote in <i><a href="/wiki/Vice_(magazine)" title="Vice (magazine)">Vice</a></i> that the record has more "good tales" on it than Underwood's <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits:_Decade_Number_1" title="Greatest Hits: Decade Number 1">Greatest Hits</a></i> album. "She still oversings sometimes, as idols will", he said, "but finally she's relaxed enough to let the songs narrate for themselves—be they torch-carrying and fuck-you songs, bad girl and justifiable homicide songs, or tonight's-the-night and happily-ever-after songs."<sup id="cite_ref-Christgau_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Christgau-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Times" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i> critic Jon Caramanica was less impressed, finding much of the songwriting devoid of "bite or pulp" while accusing Underwood of singing bombastically throughout the album. "She largely picks songs that serve as launch platforms for her ballistic-missile voice," he wrote, "but they don’t cohere into a whole identity."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> In <i><a href="/wiki/Exclaim!" title="Exclaim!">Exclaim!</a></i>, Stuart Henderson wrote that the record is "more than halfway boring," explaining that "it isn't uninspired or weakly performed. Rather, it is boring in spite of the overwhelming bombast, the booming bass and pounding drums, the huge vocals, the wailing guitars; it is boring because rather than electrifying you, it distances and anaesthetizes."<sup id="cite_ref-exclaim_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-exclaim-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Storyteller_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United States, <i>Storyteller</i> debuted at number two on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> with 177,000 <a href="/wiki/Album-equivalent_unit" title="Album-equivalent unit">album-equivalent units</a>; the album sold 164,000 copies in pure sales, with the remainder of its unit total reflecting the album's streaming and track equivalent album units.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard11-2-2015_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard11-2-2015-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> This made Underwood the only country artist to have his or her first five studio albums debut at number one or number two on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 chart.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard11-1-2015_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard11-1-2015-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In addition, the album debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Top Country Albums</a> chart, earning Underwood another record as the only artist to score six consecutive number one albums on that chart.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard11-1-2015_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard11-1-2015-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> It held the number two position on the <i>Billboard 200</i> in its second week, moving another 81,000 units, including over 73,000 album copies sold.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On the chart dated December 19, it rose back to number one on the Top Country Albums for a second week.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> It was certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> on December 4, 2015 for shipments of over 500,000 copies in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-1milRIAA_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1milRIAA-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> In October 2016, the album was certified Platinum, one year after its release, becoming the sixth album by Underwood to receive a Platinum certification.<sup id="cite_ref-carrieunderwood.fm_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-carrieunderwood.fm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As of April 2016, the album has sold 752,100 copies in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-1milRIAA_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1milRIAA-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Outside of the United States, the album debuted at number three in <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Albums_Chart" title="Canadian Albums Chart">Canada</a>, number four in <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">Australia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-aus_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aus-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> number six in <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Singles_and_Albums_Charts" title="Scottish Singles and Albums Charts">Scotland</a>, number thirteen on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>—becoming her second top twenty album in the region<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>—and charted in another six regions.
</p>
