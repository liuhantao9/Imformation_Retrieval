<p><b>Benson</b> is a city in <a href="/wiki/Cochise_County,_Arizona" title="Cochise County, Arizona">Cochise County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, 45 miles (72&#160;km) east-southeast of <a href="/wiki/Tucson,_Arizona" title="Tucson, Arizona">Tucson</a>. It was founded as a rail terminal for the area, and still serves as such. As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>, the population of the city was 5,105.<sup id="cite_ref-FactFinder_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FactFinder-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benson,_Arizona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city was founded in 1880 when the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Pacific_Railroad" class="mw-redirect" title="Southern Pacific Railroad">Southern Pacific Railroad</a> came through. It was named after Judge William S. Benson, a friend of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Crocker" title="Charles Crocker">Charles Crocker</a>, president of the Southern Pacific.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The railroad, coming overland from California, chose the Benson site to cross the <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_River_(Arizona)" title="San Pedro River (Arizona)">San Pedro River</a>.  Benson then served as a rail junction point to obtain ore and refined metal by wagon, in turn shipping rail freight back to the mines at <a href="/wiki/Tombstone,_Arizona" title="Tombstone, Arizona">Tombstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fairbank,_Arizona" title="Fairbank, Arizona">Fairbank</a>, <a href="/wiki/Contention_City,_Arizona" title="Contention City, Arizona">Contention City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bisbee,_Arizona" title="Bisbee, Arizona">Bisbee</a>. For example, the railhead in Benson was about 25 miles (40&#160;km) from Tombstone, and was the closest rail connection to it until 1882, when a feeder line was laid from Benson to Contention City.
</p><p>The railhead in Benson was founded about a mile from a traditional crossing of the upper San Pedro River (known also as the Middle Crossing), used by the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Emigrant_Trail" title="Southern Emigrant Trail">Southern Emigrant Trail</a> and <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio-San_Diego_Mail_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="San Antonio-San Diego Mail Line">San Antonio-San Diego Mail Line</a>.  It was the site of the San Pedro Station of the <a href="/wiki/Butterfield_Overland_Mail" title="Butterfield Overland Mail">Butterfield Overland Mail</a> and a wagon depot, the San Pedro River Station, run since 1871 by William Ohnesorgen.  In 1878 he had erected a toll bridge over which mining supplies were transported to the new mining camps such as Fairbank and Tombstone. Two years later this bridge marked the location of the railroad bridge that became the terminal site of Benson.
</p><p>The city today is perhaps best known as the gateway to <a href="/wiki/Kartchner_Caverns_State_Park" title="Kartchner Caverns State Park">Kartchner Caverns State Park</a>. It is also home to the acclaimed Singing Wind Bookshop, which specializes in books about the Southwest.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Jay_Six_Ranch">Jay Six Ranch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benson,_Arizona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Jay Six Ranch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 40,000-acre (160&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) Jay Six cattle ranch is located just outside Benson. The ranch played host to political figures like the young brothers <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph P. Kennedy, Jr.">Joseph</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Jack Kennedy">Jack Kennedy</a>, and to senior statesman <a href="/wiki/Barry_Goldwater" title="Barry Goldwater">Barry Goldwater</a>. The Kennedy brothers (Joe age 21 and Jack age 19) were sent out to the ranch in the spring of 1936 so that the brothers could work as ranch hands, and so that Jack Kennedy could recuperate from a recent illness in the dry desert heat. It is reported that ranchman <a href="/wiki/Jack_Speiden" title="Jack Speiden">Jack Speiden</a> worked both brothers "very hard".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benson,_Arizona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Benson is located in western Cochise County at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Benson,_Arizona&amp;params=31_57_10_N_110_18_24_W_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">31°57′10″N</span> <span class="longitude">110°18′24″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">31.95278°N 110.30667°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">31.95278; -110.30667</span></span></span></a></span> (31.95288, -110.30677).<sup id="cite_ref-GR1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, Benson has a total area of 41.5 square miles (107.4&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), of which 0.04 square miles (0.1&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), or 0.12%, is water.<sup id="cite_ref-Census_2010_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the census<sup id="cite_ref-GR2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> of 2000, there were 4,711 people, 2,084 households, and 1,346 families residing in the city.  The population density was 131.9 people per square mile (50.9/km²).  There were 2,822 housing units at an average density of 79.0 per square mile (30.5/km²).  The racial makeup of the city was 89.32% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">White</a> or <a href="/wiki/European_American" class="mw-redirect" title="European American">European American</a>, 1.29% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Native American</a>, 0.72% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Black</a> or <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">African American</a>, 0.47% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Asian</a>, 0.13% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 5.69% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 2.38% from two or more races.  <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">Latino</a> people of any race were 19.85% of the population.
</p><p>There were 2,084 households, out of which 18.9% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 51.5% were married couples living together, 9.2% had a female householder with no husband present, and 35.4% were non-families. 30.0% of all households were made up of individuals and 17.4% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older.  The average household size was 2.22 and the average family size was 2.72.
</p><p>In the city, the population was 19.5% under the age of 18, 6.7% from 18 to 24, 18.0% from 25 to 44, 26.5% from 45 to 64, and 29.3% who were 65 years of age or older.  The median age was 50 years. For every 100 females, there were 95.7 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 91.9 males.
</p><p>The median income for a household in the city was $28,289, and the median income for a family was $36,364. Males had a median income of $34,013 versus $18,964 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the city was $17,315. About 6.2% of families and 13.7% of the population were below the <a href="/wiki/Poverty_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 26.1% of those under age 18 and 9.9% of those age 65 or over.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benson,_Arizona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Similar to other desert plains areas of Arizona, Benson receives little rainfall and is relatively hot. Some snow occasionally falls in the winter.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benson,_Arizona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Benson_station.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Benson_station.jpg/220px-Benson_station.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="122" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Benson_station.jpg/330px-Benson_station.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/15/Benson_station.jpg 2x" data-file-width="344" data-file-height="190" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Benson_station.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Replica of Benson Railroad Station</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Benson_Municipal_Airport_(Arizona)" title="Benson Municipal Airport (Arizona)">Benson Airport</a> is located 5 miles (8&#160;km) northwest of the city center.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Interstate_10" title="Interstate 10">Interstate 10</a> serves the city with four exits; the highway leads northwest 45 miles (72&#160;km) to <a href="/wiki/Tucson,_Arizona" title="Tucson, Arizona">Tucson</a> and northeast 35 miles (56&#160;km) to <a href="/wiki/Willcox,_Arizona" title="Willcox, Arizona">Willcox</a>. <a href="/wiki/Arizona_State_Route_80" title="Arizona State Route 80">Arizona State Route 80</a> leads southeast 24 miles (39&#160;km) to <a href="/wiki/Tombstone,_Arizona" title="Tombstone, Arizona">Tombstone</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Arizona_State_Route_90" title="Arizona State Route 90">Arizona State Route 90</a> leads south 32 miles (51&#160;km) to <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Vista,_Arizona" title="Sierra Vista, Arizona">Sierra Vista</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">Amtrak</a> provides passenger rail service at <a href="/wiki/Benson_(Amtrak_station)" class="mw-redirect" title="Benson (Amtrak station)">105 E. 4th Street</a>; freight railroads serving Benson are the <a href="/wiki/Union_Pacific_Railroad" title="Union Pacific Railroad">Union Pacific Railroad</a> and the <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_and_Southwestern_Railroad" class="mw-redirect" title="San Pedro and Southwestern Railroad">San Pedro and Southwestern Railroad</a>.
</p><p>Benson Area Transit (BAT) is a bus service that covers Benson, <a href="/wiki/St._David,_Arizona" title="St. David, Arizona">St. David</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dragoon,_Arizona" title="Dragoon, Arizona">Dragoon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mescal,_Arizona" title="Mescal, Arizona">Mescal</a>. Benson is also served by <a href="/wiki/Greyhound_Lines" title="Greyhound Lines">Greyhound</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cochise Connection, which is operated by the <a href="/wiki/Douglas,_Arizona" title="Douglas, Arizona">City of Douglas</a>, offers regularly-scheduled shuttle service between Benson, <a href="/wiki/Bisbee,_Arizona" title="Bisbee, Arizona">Bisbee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Vista,_Arizona" title="Sierra Vista, Arizona">Sierra Vista</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huachuca_City,_Arizona" title="Huachuca City, Arizona">City of Huachuca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Douglas,_AZ" class="mw-redirect" title="Douglas, AZ">Douglas</a> with a one-way fare of $3-4.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/The_World_University" title="The World University">The World University</a> is located in Benson.
</p><p>The Benson Unified School District is a K-12 public school serving the Benson area. Their mascot is the Benson Bobcat.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Cochise_College" title="Cochise College">Cochise College</a> has a branch located on Route 90.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_references">Media references</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benson,_Arizona&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Media references">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This city was one of the filming locations for <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount</a>'s 1994 film <i><a href="/wiki/Pontiac_Moon" title="Pontiac Moon">Pontiac Moon</a></i>.</li>
<li>"Benson, Arizona" is the name of the theme song to the 1974 movie <i><a href="/wiki/Dark_Star_(film)" title="Dark Star (film)">Dark Star</a></i>. Dark Star Road in Benson is named in honor of the film.</li>
<li>In a tip of the hat to <i>Dark Star</i>, the <a href="/wiki/Avengers_(comics)" title="Avengers (comics)">Avengers</a> <a href="/wiki/Fictional_crossover" class="mw-redirect" title="Fictional crossover">crossover</a> story <i><a href="/wiki/Operation:_Galactic_Storm" title="Operation: Galactic Storm">Operation: Galactic Storm</a></i>, which is about a war between alien races, commences here.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Spiritual_Conference_for_Radical_Fairies" title="Spiritual Conference for Radical Fairies">Spiritual Conference for Radical Fairies</a> was held August 31–2 September 2, 1979, at the Sri Ram <a href="/wiki/Ashram" title="Ashram">Ashram</a> in Benson, the foundational event that began the <a href="/wiki/Radical_Faeries" title="Radical Faeries">Radical Faeries</a> community.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Rage_(1972_film)" title="Rage (1972 film)">Rage</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/George_C._Scott" title="George C. Scott">George C. Scott</a>, was filmed at the then-new Benson Hospital which had opened two years prior on July, 4th 1970.</li>
<li>"Benson Hedges" by the popular American band <a href="/wiki/Fun." class="mw-redirect" title="Fun.">Fun.</a> references the lead singer and his existential crisis in Benson.</li>
<li>In the short film <i>Thalion LTD</i>, two soldiers remember they know each other from "the counterattack in Arizona" that took place in Benson.</li>
<li>This city was one of the filming locations for the 2005 film <i>Miracle at Sage Creek</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
