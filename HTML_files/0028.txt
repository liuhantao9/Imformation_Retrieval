<p><b>Miranda Leigh Lambert</b> (born November 10, 1983) is an American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> singer and songwriter. In 2003, she finished in third place of the television program <i><a href="/wiki/Nashville_Star" title="Nashville Star">Nashville Star</a>,</i> a singing competition which aired on the <a href="/wiki/USA_Network" title="USA Network">USA Network</a>. Outside her solo career, she is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Pistol_Annies" title="Pistol Annies">Pistol Annies</a> formed in 2011 alongside <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Monroe" title="Ashley Monroe">Ashley Monroe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Angaleena_Presley" title="Angaleena Presley">Angaleena Presley</a>. Lambert has been honored by the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music Awards</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a>.
</p><p>Lambert's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Kerosene_(album)" title="Kerosene (album)">Kerosene</a></i> (2005) was certified <a href="/wiki/Platinum_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Platinum album">Platinum</a> in the United States and produced the singles "Me and Charlie Talking," "<a href="/wiki/Bring_Me_Down_(Miranda_Lambert_song)" title="Bring Me Down (Miranda Lambert song)">Bring Me Down</a>," "<a href="/wiki/Kerosene_(Miranda_Lambert_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kerosene (Miranda Lambert song)">Kerosene</a>" and "New Strings." All four singles reached the top 40 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a>. Her second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Crazy_Ex-Girlfriend_(album)" title="Crazy Ex-Girlfriend (album)">Crazy Ex-Girlfriend</a>,</i> was released in early 2007. Three of its singles ("<a href="/wiki/Famous_in_a_Small_Town" title="Famous in a Small Town">Famous in a Small Town</a>," "<a href="/wiki/Gunpowder_%26_Lead" title="Gunpowder &amp; Lead">Gunpowder &amp; Lead</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/More_Like_Her" title="More Like Her">More Like Her</a>") peaked within the top 20 on the country songs chart, with "Gunpowder &amp; Lead" becoming her first top 10 entry in July 2008. Her third album, <i><a href="/wiki/Revolution_(Miranda_Lambert_album)" title="Revolution (Miranda Lambert album)">Revolution</a>,</i> was released in September 2009. Two of its songs – "<a href="/wiki/The_House_That_Built_Me" title="The House That Built Me">The House That Built Me</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Heart_Like_Mine" title="Heart Like Mine">Heart Like Mine</a>" – topped the Hot Country Songs chart.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>2011's <i><a href="/wiki/Four_the_Record" title="Four the Record">Four the Record</a>,</i> included the singles "<a href="/wiki/Baggage_Claim" title="Baggage Claim">Baggage Claim</a>," "<a href="/wiki/Over_You_(Miranda_Lambert_song)" title="Over You (Miranda Lambert song)">Over You</a>," "<a href="/wiki/Fastest_Girl_in_Town" title="Fastest Girl in Town">Fastest Girl in Town</a>," "<a href="/wiki/Mama%27s_Broken_Heart" title="Mama&#39;s Broken Heart">Mama's Broken Heart</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/All_Kinds_of_Kinds" title="All Kinds of Kinds">All Kinds of Kinds</a>." Lambert released her fifth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Platinum_(Miranda_Lambert_album)" title="Platinum (Miranda Lambert album)">Platinum</a>,</i> in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The record won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Album">Grammy Award for Best Country Album</a>, and the album's lead single "<a href="/wiki/Automatic_(Miranda_Lambert_song)" title="Automatic (Miranda Lambert song)">Automatic</a>" reached top 5 on the Country charts.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Her sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Weight_of_These_Wings" title="The Weight of These Wings">The Weight of These Wings</a>,</i> was released on November 18, 2016, and subsequently certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA).<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_Certification_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA_Certification-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Miranda Leigh Lambert was born November 10, 1983,<sup id="cite_ref-dob_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dob-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> to Rick and Bev (née Hughes) Lambert in <a href="/wiki/Longview,_Texas" title="Longview, Texas">Longview, Texas</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Miranda_Lambert_Biography_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Miranda_Lambert_Biography-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and was raised in <a href="/wiki/Lindale,_Texas" title="Lindale, Texas">Lindale, Texas</a>.  She was named after her great-grandmother, Lucy Miranda.<sup id="cite_ref-usmagazine.com_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usmagazine.com-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> She has one younger brother, Luke Lambert.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> She has English, Irish and Native American ancestry.<sup id="cite_ref-usmagazine.com_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usmagazine.com-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Her parents met while Bev was attending camp at <a href="/wiki/Southern_Methodist_University" title="Southern Methodist University">Southern Methodist University</a> and Rick was an on-campus undercover narcotics officer, and they got together a few years later.  Rick is a former <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a> police officer who played in a country-rock group called Contraband in the 1970s.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Rick and Bev later became <a href="/wiki/Private_detectives" class="mw-redirect" title="Private detectives">private detectives</a> and even worked on the high-profile case of the impeachment of President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a>. At the age of 6, Miranda began helping with some of the cases.  After the oil crisis dampened the economy in Texas, her family lost everything.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the downturn, things got better and her parents began a faith-based ministry and offered their home as a shelter for the victims of domestic violence and their children.  Miranda has said that this experience fueled her music and that her song "Gunpowder &amp; Lead" is a reflection of that.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While still in high school, Lambert made her professional singing debut with "The Texas Pride Band."<sup id="cite_ref-Maerz,_Melissa_2011_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maerz,_Melissa_2011-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> She also fronted the <a href="/wiki/House_band" title="House band">house band</a> at the Reo Palm Isle<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Longview,_Texas" title="Longview, Texas">Longview, Texas</a>, a long-running venue that had presented legends such as <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Willie_Nelson" title="Willie Nelson">Willie Nelson</a>, and the place where <a href="/wiki/Brooks_%26_Dunn" title="Brooks &amp; Dunn">Brooks &amp; Dunn</a> started out as the house band.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2000.E2.80.932003:_Career_beginnings.3B_Nashville_Star"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2000–2003:_Career_beginnings;_Nashville_Star">2000–2003: Career beginnings; <i>Nashville Star</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2000–2003: Career beginnings; Nashville Star">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Miranda_Lambert_at_Laughlin_AFB.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Miranda_Lambert_at_Laughlin_AFB.jpg/200px-Miranda_Lambert_at_Laughlin_AFB.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="252" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Miranda_Lambert_at_Laughlin_AFB.jpg/300px-Miranda_Lambert_at_Laughlin_AFB.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c6/Miranda_Lambert_at_Laughlin_AFB.jpg 2x" data-file-width="317" data-file-height="400" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Miranda_Lambert_at_Laughlin_AFB.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lambert performing at Laughlin AFB in 2006</div></div></div>
<p>At 16, Lambert appeared on the Johnnie High Country Music Revue in <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Texas" title="Arlington, Texas">Arlington, Texas</a>, the same talent show that had helped launch the career of <a href="/wiki/LeAnn_Rimes" title="LeAnn Rimes">LeAnn Rimes</a>. Lambert then acquired a recording session in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>, but left the studio after she became frustrated with the "pop" type of music presented to her. She went back to Texas in 2000 and asked her dad to teach her how to play guitar, so she could write her own songs.<sup id="cite_ref-wrangler_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wrangler-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2002, Dusty Meador hired Lambert to perform at Tye Phelps' country music restaurant and venue Love and War in Texas. Lambert continued to perform around the Texas Music Scene opening for Cooder Graw, Kevin Fowler, and Jack Ingram. In 2003, she auditioned for the talent competition <i><a href="/wiki/Nashville_Star" title="Nashville Star">Nashville Star</a></i>, where she earned a third-place finish behind <a href="/wiki/Buddy_Jewell" title="Buddy Jewell">Buddy Jewell</a> and John Arthur Martinez.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> During her 9-week stay on <i>Nashville Star</i>, Lambert caught the attention of the show's judge and Sony Music executive, Tracy Gershon, who later convinced the label to sign her.
</p>
<h3><span id="2004.E2.80.932008:_Kerosene_and_Crazy_Ex-_Girlfriend"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2004–2008:_Kerosene_and_Crazy_Ex-_Girlfriend">2004–2008: <i>Kerosene</i> and <i>Crazy Ex- Girlfriend</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2004–2008: Kerosene and Crazy Ex- Girlfriend">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On September 15, 2003, she signed with <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-about1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Her debut single, "Me and Charlie Talking," co-written by her father and Heather Little, was released in summer of 2004 as the lead single to her debut album, titled <i><a href="/wiki/Kerosene_(album)" title="Kerosene (album)">Kerosene</a>.</i> The album comprised 12 songs, 11 of which she wrote or co-wrote. The album debuted at number one on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Top Country Albums</a> charts,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and eventually gained a Platinum certification by the RIAA for shipments of over one million copies,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> selling more than 930,000 copies up to July 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Overall, the album produced four Top 40 singles on the <i>Billboard</i> country charts, including the title track which was a Top 20 hit. Lambert also toured with <a href="/wiki/Keith_Urban" title="Keith Urban">Keith Urban</a><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/George_Strait" title="George Strait">George Strait</a><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> in early 2006. In 2007, she toured with <a href="/wiki/Dierks_Bentley" title="Dierks Bentley">Dierks Bentley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toby_Keith" title="Toby Keith">Toby Keith</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Miranda_Lambert_3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Miranda_Lambert_3.jpg/200px-Miranda_Lambert_3.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="279" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Miranda_Lambert_3.jpg/300px-Miranda_Lambert_3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Miranda_Lambert_3.jpg/400px-Miranda_Lambert_3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="896" data-file-height="1249" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Miranda_Lambert_3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lambert on stage, in <a href="/wiki/Pontiac,_Michigan" title="Pontiac, Michigan">Pontiac, Michigan</a>, March 31, 2007</div></div></div>
<p>Lambert's second album, <a href="/wiki/Crazy_Ex-Girlfriend_(album)" title="Crazy Ex-Girlfriend (album)"><i>Crazy Ex-Girlfriend</i></a>, was released on May 9, 2007. She wrote eight of the album's 11 tracks,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> including its four singles. Much of the track "Gunpowder &amp; Lead," the album's third single and her highest-charting single, was written while she was taking a concealed handgun class in her home town.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Fady Joudah of <i>The New Yorker</i> said the album proved "she has talent and charisma on a par with <a href="/wiki/Dolly_Parton" title="Dolly Parton">Dolly Parton</a>, another blond beauty who was once underestimated."<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, at the 40th Annual <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music" title="Academy of Country Music">Academy of Country Music</a> Awards in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a>, Lambert won the <a href="/wiki/Cover_Girl" class="mw-redirect" title="Cover Girl">Cover Girl</a> "Fresh Face of Country Music Award." She was also nominated for the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association" title="Country Music Association">Country Music Association</a>'s Horizon Award in 2005; in 2007, Lambert also received a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> nomination for Best Female Country Vocal Performance for her single "Kerosene." She also won the Top New Female Vocalist award at the 2007 ACM (Academy of Country Music) Awards.  At the 2008 ACM (Academy of Country Music) Awards, <i>Crazy Ex-Girlfriend</i> won Album of the Year.
</p>
<h3><span id="2009.E2.80.932011:_Revolution"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2009–2011:_Revolution">2009–2011: <i>Revolution</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2009–2011: Revolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lambert released her third album, <i>Revolution</i>, on September 29, 2009. She co-wrote 11 of the album's 15 tracks; the album also includes co-writes from <a href="/wiki/Dave_Haywood" title="Dave Haywood">Dave Haywood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Kelley" title="Charles Kelley">Charles Kelley</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lady_Antebellum" title="Lady Antebellum">Lady Antebellum</a> and Blake Shelton.<sup id="cite_ref-musicrow.com_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-musicrow.com-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Revolution</i> received significant critical praise on its release.<sup id="cite_ref-Metacritic_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metacritic-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> the album received an <a href="/wiki/Weighted_mean" class="mw-redirect" title="Weighted mean">average</a> score of 85, based on 11 reviews, which indicates "universal acclaim."<sup id="cite_ref-Metacritic_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metacritic-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The album also received positive reviews from <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Globe" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston Globe">Boston Globe</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant Magazine</a></i>. <i>Entertainment Weekly</i> called the release "the best mainstream-country album so far this year."
</p><p>Lambert debuted her new single, "<a href="/wiki/Dead_Flowers_(Miranda_Lambert_song)" title="Dead Flowers (Miranda Lambert song)">Dead Flowers</a>," at the 44th annual Academy of Country Music Awards on April 5, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> It was released on May 4, 2009, and was a minor Top 40 hit on the charts. The album's second single, "<a href="/wiki/White_Liar" title="White Liar">White Liar</a>," was released on August 17, 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> and debuted at No.&#160;50 on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot Country Songs chart. In February 2010, "White Liar" became Lambert's first Top Five hit, reaching a peak of No.&#160;2 on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot Country Songs chart.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/The_House_That_Built_Me" title="The House That Built Me">The House That Built Me</a>, the album's third single, was released on March 8, 2010, and became a No.&#160;1 on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot Country Songs chart. It stayed there for four weeks, and received platinum certification from the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> on July 8, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Only_Prettier" title="Only Prettier">Only Prettier</a>" followed as the album's fourth single in July 2010, and its accompanying music video went viral. The music video for "Only Prettier" featured cameo appearances by fellow country artists <a href="/wiki/Kellie_Pickler" title="Kellie Pickler">Kellie Pickler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laura_Bell_Bundy" title="Laura Bell Bundy">Laura Bell Bundy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Scott" title="Hillary Scott">Hillary Scott</a> of Lady Antebellum. In December 2010, "Only Prettier" reached a peak of number 12 on the U.S. Billboard Hot Country Songs chart, giving Lambert her seventh Top 20 hit. "Heart Like Mine" was released in January 2011 as the fifth and final single from <i>Revolution.</i> It became Lambert's second Number One hit on the country charts for the chart dated May 28, 2011.
</p><p>In promotion of <i>Revolution,</i> Lambert launched a headlining tour; <i>Roadside Bars &amp; Pink Guitars</i> in March 2010, with stops in 22 cities and a performance at the <a href="/wiki/Bonnaroo_Music_Festival" title="Bonnaroo Music Festival">Bonnaroo Music Festival</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-headlining_tour_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-headlining_tour-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 1, 2010, it was announced that Lambert had received a record-setting 9 CMA award nominations. She performed at the 44th Annual Country Music Association Awards on November 10, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> She won the CMA Award for Female Vocalist of the Year, and her <i>Revolution</i> won Album of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Lambert and <a href="/wiki/Sheryl_Crow" title="Sheryl Crow">Sheryl Crow</a> performed "<a href="/wiki/Coal_Miner%27s_Daughter_(song)" title="Coal Miner&#39;s Daughter (song)">Coal Miner's Daughter</a>" as a tribute to country legend <a href="/wiki/Loretta_Lynn" title="Loretta Lynn">Loretta Lynn</a> who also entered the stage to join them and finished the song with Crow and Lambert as backup. Later that night, Lynn presented the Female Vocalist of the Year CMA award to Lambert. On February 13, 2011, Lambert won a Grammy Award in the <i>Best Female Country Vocal Performance</i> category for "The House That Built Me."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2011.E2.80.932013:_Four_the_Record_and_the_Pistol_Annies"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011–2013:_Four_the_Record_and_the_Pistol_Annies">2011–2013: <i>Four the Record</i> and the Pistol Annies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2011–2013: Four the Record and the Pistol Annies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Miranda_Lambert.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Miranda_Lambert.jpg/200px-Miranda_Lambert.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="286" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Miranda_Lambert.jpg/300px-Miranda_Lambert.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Miranda_Lambert.jpg/400px-Miranda_Lambert.jpg 2x" data-file-width="700" data-file-height="1000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Miranda_Lambert.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Miranda Lambert performing in <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, Texas, July 1, 2007</div></div></div>
<p>On April 4, 2011, during the taping of the Academy of Country Music's 'Girls' Night Out' television special in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>, Lambert debuted her new project, girl group <a href="/wiki/Pistol_Annies" title="Pistol Annies">Pistol Annies</a>. The group consists of Lambert, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Monroe" title="Ashley Monroe">Ashley Monroe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Angaleena_Presley" title="Angaleena Presley">Angaleena Presley</a>. They released their single, "Hell on Heels," in May 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> and released their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Hell_on_Heels" title="Hell on Heels">Hell on Heels</a></i>, on August 23, 2011, which debuted at No.&#160;1 on Billboard's country chart. Sasha Frere-Jones of <i>The New Yorker</i> said the trio "is good enough to recall those transcendent moments of vocal harmony the Dixie Chicks used to hit every few months."<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lambert's fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Four_the_Record" title="Four the Record">Four the Record</a></i>, was released on November 1, 2011;<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> her first album for <a href="/wiki/RCA_Nashville" class="mw-redirect" title="RCA Nashville">RCA Nashville</a>, after Sony Music Nashville announced a corporate restructuring.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> <i>Four the Record</i> produced five singles: "<a href="/wiki/Baggage_Claim" title="Baggage Claim">Baggage Claim</a>," "<a href="/wiki/Over_You_(Miranda_Lambert_song)" title="Over You (Miranda Lambert song)">Over You</a>," "<a href="/wiki/Fastest_Girl_in_Town" title="Fastest Girl in Town">Fastest Girl in Town</a>," "<a href="/wiki/Mama%27s_Broken_Heart" title="Mama&#39;s Broken Heart">Mama's Broken Heart</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/All_Kinds_of_Kinds" title="All Kinds of Kinds">All Kinds of Kinds</a>." "<a href="/wiki/Over_You_(Miranda_Lambert_song)" title="Over You (Miranda Lambert song)">Over You</a>," which Lambert and Shelton co-wrote, reached number 1 in early 2012.
</p><p>On February 8, 2012, Lambert made her acting debut on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>'s long-running <a href="/wiki/Legal_drama" title="Legal drama">legal drama</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit">Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit</a></i> in an episode titled "<a href="/wiki/Law_%26_Order:_Special_Victims_Unit_(season_13)#ep13" title="Law &amp; Order: Special Victims Unit (season 13)">Father's Shadow</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Lambert later told <i><a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Country Music Television">CMT News</a></i> she was a big fan of the show: "I never wanted to act. I still don't. I don't want to be an actress. I just wanted to be on that show mainly so I could be a groupie [for their autographs]."<sup id="cite_ref-LambertSVUCMT_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LambertSVUCMT-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Late in 2012, Lambert appeared on Shelton's Christmas album <i><a href="/wiki/Cheers,_It%27s_Christmas" title="Cheers, It&#39;s Christmas">Cheers, It's Christmas</a>,</i> to which she contributed guest vocals on a version of "<a href="/wiki/Jingle_Bell_Rock" title="Jingle Bell Rock">Jingle Bell Rock</a>." The soundtrack was released on October 2, 2012.  On October 23, 2012, Lambert and <a href="/wiki/Dierks_Bentley" title="Dierks Bentley">Dierks Bentley</a> announced the co-headlined 33-show Locked &amp; Reloaded Tour, which began on January 17, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 7, 2013, a second Pistol Annies album, <i><a href="/wiki/Annie_Up" title="Annie Up">Annie Up</a>,</i> was released.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> This album produced the group's first country chart entry with "Hush Hush." The Pistol Annies are also one of many acts featured on Blake Shelton's 2013 single "<a href="/wiki/Boys_%27Round_Here" title="Boys &#39;Round Here">Boys 'Round Here</a>,"<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> which went to number 1 on <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a>. They had previously accompanied Shelton on a rendition of "<a href="/wiki/Blue_Christmas_(song)" title="Blue Christmas (song)">Blue Christmas</a>" on <i>Cheers, It's Christmas.</i><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.932015:_Platinum"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2015:_Platinum">2014–2015: <i>Platinum</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2014–2015: Platinum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Automatic_(Miranda_Lambert_song)" title="Automatic (Miranda Lambert song)">Automatic</a>," the <a href="/wiki/Lead_single" title="Lead single">lead single</a> from Lambert's fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Platinum_(Miranda_Lambert_album)" title="Platinum (Miranda Lambert album)">Platinum</a>,</i> was released on February 5, 2014, to praise from music critics.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The album, containing 16 songs, was released on June 3, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The album's second single, "<a href="/wiki/Somethin%27_Bad" title="Somethin&#39; Bad">Somethin' Bad</a>," is a duet with <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> and was debuted on the <a href="/wiki/2014_Billboard_Music_Awards" title="2014 Billboard Music Awards">2014 <i>Billboard</i> Music Awards</a> on May 18. The album's third single, "<a href="/wiki/Little_Red_Wagon_(Miranda_Lambert_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Little Red Wagon (Miranda Lambert song)">Little Red Wagon</a>," was released to country radio on January 12, 2015. The album's fourth single, "<a href="/wiki/Smokin%27_and_Drinkin%27" title="Smokin&#39; and Drinkin&#39;">Smokin' and Drinkin'</a>" released to country radio on June 22, 2015.
</p>
<h3><span id="2016.E2.80.932018:_The_Weight_of_These_Wings_and_third_Pistol_Annies_album"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–2018:_The_Weight_of_These_Wings_and_third_Pistol_Annies_album">2016–2018: <i>The Weight of These Wings</i> and third Pistol Annies album</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2016–2018: The Weight of These Wings and third Pistol Annies album">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2016, Lambert made her first ever performances outside the US and Canada as one of the headliners of <a href="/wiki/C2C:_Country_to_Country" title="C2C: Country to Country">C2C: Country to Country</a>, Europe's biggest country music festival, which saw Lambert play in England, Scotland and Ireland alongside <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Yoakam" title="Dwight Yoakam">Dwight Yoakam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Rhett" title="Thomas Rhett">Thomas Rhett</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Monroe" title="Ashley Monroe">Ashley Monroe</a>. On July 18, 2016, Lambert released "<a href="/wiki/Vice_(Miranda_Lambert_song)" title="Vice (Miranda Lambert song)">Vice</a>" to country radio and digital outlets. Written by Lambert, <a href="/wiki/Shane_McAnally" title="Shane McAnally">Shane McAnally</a> and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Osborne" title="Josh Osborne">Josh Osborne</a>, it was her first song released since <i>Platinum</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> In September, Lambert announced her sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Weight_of_These_Wings" title="The Weight of These Wings">The Weight of These Wings</a></i>, which was released on November 18, 2016. She was selected as one of 30 artists to perform on "<a href="/wiki/Forever_Country" title="Forever Country">Forever Country</a>", a <a href="/wiki/Mashup_(music)" title="Mashup (music)">mashup track</a> of "<a href="/wiki/Take_Me_Home,_Country_Roads" title="Take Me Home, Country Roads">Take Me Home, Country Roads</a>," "<a href="/wiki/On_the_Road_Again_(Willie_Nelson_song)" title="On the Road Again (Willie Nelson song)">On the Road Again</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/I_Will_Always_Love_You" title="I Will Always Love You">I Will Always Love You</a>" which celebrated 50 years of the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">CMA Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Shortly after the release of <i>The Weight of These Wings</i>, the <a href="/wiki/Highway_Vagabond_Tour" title="Highway Vagabond Tour">Highway Vagabond Tour</a> was announced<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> and began on January 24, 2017. These were Lambert's first live shows since she was put on mandatory <a href="/wiki/Vocal_rest" title="Vocal rest">vocal rest</a> and was forced to cancel her <a href="/wiki/Keeper_of_the_Flame_Tour" title="Keeper of the Flame Tour">Keeper of the Flame Tour</a> in 2016. The tour visited the US, Canada and were her first solo shows in Europe, seeing her return to England, Ireland, Scotland and, for the first time, the Netherlands.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After promotion and touring for <i>The Weight of These Wings</i> ended, the Pistol Annies reformed and released their third studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Interstate_Gospel" title="Interstate Gospel">Interstate Gospel</a></i> on November 2, 2018, which subsequently reached number 1 on the Billboard Country Albums Chart.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2019:_Wildcard">2019: <i>Wildcard</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2019: Wildcard">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lambert announced that the lead-off single from her upcoming seventh studio album will be "<a href="/wiki/It_All_Comes_Out_in_the_Wash" title="It All Comes Out in the Wash">It All Comes Out in the Wash</a>," which was released on July 18, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> The song was co-written by Lambert with <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lori_McKenna" title="Lori McKenna">Lori McKenna</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Liz_Rose" title="Liz Rose">Liz Rose</a> ("Love Junkies") and produced by <a href="/wiki/Jay_Joyce" title="Jay Joyce">Jay Joyce</a>, marking the first time she had worked with the producer.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> The song was released alongside "Locomotive," another track intended for the upcoming album that she had previously debuted on the <a href="/wiki/CMA_Music_Festival" title="CMA Music Festival">CMA Music Festival</a> in June 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Two other tracks—"Mess with My Head" and "Bluebird"—were also previewed ahead of the album in August 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lambert's seventh studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Wildcard_(Miranda_Lambert_album)" title="Wildcard (Miranda Lambert album)">Wildcard</a></i>, will be released on November 1, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-Track_Listing_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Track_Listing-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Endorsements">Endorsements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Endorsements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2009, Lambert and two other singers became the new faces of <a href="/wiki/Cotton_Incorporated" title="Cotton Incorporated">Cotton Inc.'s</a> revived “The Touch, The Feel of Cotton” campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> She has appeared in ads to promote cotton, and the website features a free download of the full version of her song, "Fabric of My Life."<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2014, Lambert's line of shoes and boots were released at <a href="/wiki/Designer_Brands" title="Designer Brands">DSW</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> In November 2014, Lambert's partnership with <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">Chrysler's</a> <a href="/wiki/Ram_Trucks" title="Ram Trucks">Ram trucks</a> was announced. Under the partnership, Ram gave Lambert a customized 1500 Laramie Longhorn which she auctioned off, with proceeds going to Lambert's Muttnation Foundation which betters the lives for shelter animals.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="MuttNation_Foundation">MuttNation Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: MuttNation Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2009, Lambert opened MuttNation Foundation, with her mother, Bev. The organization focuses on rescue animals and shelters, and Lambert has been vocal about the cause being very close to her heart. The core issues MuttNation works on are:<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Adoption drives – held by Lambert at her concerts, CMA music awards, and the Grand Ole Opry</li>
<li>Fill the little red wagon – donation drives</li>
<li>Special circumstances – emergency disaster relief or instances of hoarding</li>
<li>Cause for the Paws – annual fundraising concerts</li>
<li>Mutts Across America – provides funds and support to shelters in all 50 US states.</li>
<li>Ran's Animal Care Express – pet transports</li>
<li>Mutt March – an annual 5k with proceeds going to MuttNation causes</li></ul>
<p>In 2017, in the wake of Hurricane Harvey's devastation, MuttNation rescued dozens of animals as a part of its emergency/natural disasters objective.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Lambert opened Redemption Ranch, a shelter in <a href="/wiki/Tishomingo,_Oklahoma" title="Tishomingo, Oklahoma">Tishomingo, Oklahoma</a>, her adopted home town where she lived with Shelton.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2016, Lambert paid $3.4&#160;million for a 400-plus acre farm, with three residences, near <a href="/wiki/Primm_Springs,_Tennessee" title="Primm Springs, Tennessee">Primm Springs, Tennessee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-variety.com_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety.com-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2006, Lambert began dating singer <a href="/wiki/Blake_Shelton" title="Blake Shelton">Blake Shelton</a>. They got engaged in May 2010,<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-peoplem_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-peoplem-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> and were married on May 14, 2011, at the Don Strange Ranch in <a href="/wiki/Boerne,_Texas" title="Boerne, Texas">Boerne, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-peoplem_68-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-peoplem-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> On July 20, 2015, the couple announced that they were divorcing after four years of marriage.<sup id="cite_ref-freep_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-freep-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2015, it was confirmed that Lambert was dating R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/Anderson_East" title="Anderson East">Anderson East</a>; they met at Nashville's <a href="/wiki/Live_on_the_Green_Music_Festival" title="Live on the Green Music Festival">Live on the Green Music Festival</a> in September of that year.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> In April 2018, it was confirmed that Lambert and East had broken up months earlier after two years of dating.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> Lambert started dating <a href="/wiki/Turnpike_Troubadours" title="Turnpike Troubadours">Turnpike Troubadours</a> frontman, <a href="/wiki/Evan_Felker" title="Evan Felker">Evan Felker</a>, in February 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> His estranged wife, Staci, has alleged that he left her suddenly to be with Lambert.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup> They broke up in August 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 16, 2019, Lambert announced on social media that she married <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department" title="New York City Police Department">New York City Police Department</a> Officer Brendan McLoughlin on January 26, 2019 in <a href="/wiki/Davidson_County,_Tennessee" title="Davidson County, Tennessee">Davidson County, Tennessee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Lambert&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_awards_and_nominations_received_by_Miranda_Lambert" title="List of awards and nominations received by Miranda Lambert">List of awards and nominations received by Miranda Lambert</a></div>
<p>In April 2018 Lambert won the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">ACM Award</a> for Female Vocalist of the Year for the ninth consecutive year, surpassing <a href="/wiki/Reba_McEntire" title="Reba McEntire">Reba McEntire</a> as the most awarded in the category in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-hdd4-3-17_79-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hdd4-3-17-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup> In November 2015 she became the first woman to have won the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a>' Album of the Year twice.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup> She has won two <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Awards</a> out of sixteen nominations.
</p>
