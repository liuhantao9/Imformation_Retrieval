<p>The <b>Republic of Independent Guiana</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">La R√©publique de la Guyane ind√©pendante</i>) commonly referred to by the name of the capital <b>Counani</b> (rendered <b>"Cunani"</b> in Portuguese by the Brazilians), was a short-lived independent state in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Independent_Guiana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Counani</i> was created on 23 July 1886 in the area that was disputed by <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> (as part of <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> in the late nineteenth century.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The state was founded by French settlers and existed from 1886 to 1891. The territory of the former state of Counani is now located in the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Amap%C3%A1" title="Amap√°">Amap√°</a>.
</p>
<h2><span id="Free_State_of_Counani_.281904.E2.80.931912.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_State_of_Counani_(1904‚Äì1912)">Free State of Counani (1904‚Äì1912)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Independent_Guiana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Free State of Counani (1904‚Äì1912)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Some years after, in 1904 a Frenchman named <a href="/wiki/Adolphe_Brezet" title="Adolphe Brezet">Adolphe Brezet</a> self-proclaimed himself "Pr√©sident de l'√âtat libre de Counani".
</p><p>This "special" State had a constitution, a flag and issued some stamps. It was never recognized by Brazil or France, but the <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South African</a> <a href="/wiki/Boer_Republics" title="Boer Republics">Boer Republics</a> opened diplomatic relations with Brezet (who had fought for them previously) during the <a href="/wiki/South_African_Wars_(1879%E2%80%931915)" title="South African Wars (1879‚Äì1915)">Boer wars</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Claimants_of_Head_of_State">Claimants of Head of State</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Republic_of_Independent_Guiana&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Claimants of Head of State">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All claimants of head of state of Counani were Frenchmen:
</p>
<ul><li>Jules Gros (1809-1891) - a French journalist who laid claim as head of state from 1886 to 1891; head was Secretary of the <a href="/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_de_g%C3%A9ographie" class="mw-redirect" title="Soci√©t√© de g√©ographie">Soci√©t√© de g√©ographie</a> in 1883<sup id="cite_ref-listverse.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-listverse.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Adolphe Brezet 1901-1904 <sup id="cite_ref-listverse.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-listverse.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
