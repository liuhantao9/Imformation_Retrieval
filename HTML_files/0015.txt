<p><b>Luke Robert Laird</b> (born May 4, 1978 in <a href="/wiki/Hartstown,_Pennsylvania" title="Hartstown, Pennsylvania">Hartstown, Pennsylvania</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is an American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> <a href="/wiki/Songwriter" title="Songwriter">songwriter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">producer</a>. He has written over 20 #1 <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a> singles, including <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>’s "<a href="/wiki/So_Small" title="So Small">So Small</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Temporary_Home" title="Temporary Home">Temporary Home</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Undo_It" title="Undo It">Undo It</a>"; <a href="/wiki/Blake_Shelton" title="Blake Shelton">Blake Shelton</a>’s "<a href="/wiki/Gonna_(song)" title="Gonna (song)">Gonna</a>"; <a href="/wiki/Sara_Evans" title="Sara Evans">Sara Evans</a>’ "<a href="/wiki/A_Little_Bit_Stronger" title="A Little Bit Stronger">A Little Bit Stronger</a>"; <a href="/wiki/Rodney_Atkins" title="Rodney Atkins">Rodney Atkins</a>’s "<a href="/wiki/Take_a_Back_Road_(song)" title="Take a Back Road (song)">Take a Back Road</a>"; <a href="/wiki/Eric_Church" title="Eric Church">Eric Church</a>’s "<a href="/wiki/Drink_in_My_Hand" title="Drink in My Hand">Drink in My Hand</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Give_Me_Back_My_Hometown" title="Give Me Back My Hometown">Give Me Back My Hometown</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Talladega_(Eric_Church_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Talladega (Eric Church song)">Talladega</a>"; <a href="/wiki/Little_Big_Town" title="Little Big Town">Little Big Town</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Pontoon_(song)" title="Pontoon (song)">Pontoon</a>";<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Luke_Bryan" title="Luke Bryan">Luke Bryan</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/I_See_You_(Luke_Bryan_song)" title="I See You (Luke Bryan song)">I See You</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Fast_(Luke_Bryan_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fast (Luke Bryan song)">Fast</a>"; <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Rhett" title="Thomas Rhett">Thomas Rhett</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/T-Shirt_(Thomas_Rhett_song)" title="T-Shirt (Thomas Rhett song)">T-Shirt</a>"; <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Chesney" title="Kenny Chesney">Kenny Chesney</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/American_Kids" title="American Kids">American Kids</a>"; <a href="/wiki/Lady_Antebellum" title="Lady Antebellum">Lady Antebellum</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Downtown_(Lady_Antebellum_song)" title="Downtown (Lady Antebellum song)">Downtown</a>"; and <a href="/wiki/Jon_Pardi" title="Jon Pardi">Jon Pardi</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Head_Over_Boots" title="Head Over Boots">Head Over Boots</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-Thompson_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thompson-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He has also written and produced songs for <a href="/wiki/Tim_McGraw" title="Tim McGraw">Tim McGraw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rascal_Flatts" title="Rascal Flatts">Rascal Flatts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kacey_Musgraves" title="Kacey Musgraves">Kacey Musgraves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toby_Keith" title="Toby Keith">Toby Keith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ne-Yo" title="Ne-Yo">Ne-Yo</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">John Legend</a>, <a href="/wiki/Darius_Rucker" title="Darius Rucker">Darius Rucker</a>, and many others.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luke_Laird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Laird was born in Hartstown, PA on May 4, 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He wrote songs and learned guitar in elementary school.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After seeing a <a href="/wiki/Randy_Travis" title="Randy Travis">Randy Travis</a> concert in high school, Laird claims he became fascinated with songwriting and production.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He taught himself basic elements of music theory by listening to the radio and dissecting songs.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Laird's parents took him in high school to see <a href="/wiki/Middle_Tennessee_State_University" title="Middle Tennessee State University">Middle Tennessee State University</a> and its recording program,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and he enrolled there in 1997, graduating in 2001 with a degree in Recording Industry Management.<sup id="cite_ref-Murfreesboro_Pulse_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Murfreesboro_Pulse-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After college Laird moved to Nashville. Laird's first job in the industry was assistant tour manager for <a href="/wiki/Brooks_%26_Dunn" title="Brooks &amp; Dunn">Brooks &amp; Dunn</a>, though he spent his weekends writing.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He participated in songwriter nights in Nashville at venues such as <a href="/wiki/Bluebird_Caf%C3%A9" title="Bluebird Café">Bluebird Cafe</a>. In 2002, Chris Oglesby, at <a href="/wiki/Bertelsmann_Music_Group" title="Bertelsmann Music Group">BMG Music</a> at the time, offered Laird a publishing deal.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> BMG was later bought by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Publishing_Group" title="Universal Music Publishing Group">Universal Music Publishing Group</a>, which took over Laird's contract and made him a staff writer in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While working at BMG he met a receptionist, Beth Mason.<sup id="cite_ref-Murfreesboro_Pulse_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Murfreesboro_Pulse-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The two were married in 2010, and went on to co-found Creative Nation in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Murfreesboro_Pulse_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Murfreesboro_Pulse-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luke_Laird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Early in his time at UMPG, Bill Luther took an interest in him and encouraged him as a writer.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Luther brought Laird along with him to write a song with <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The three worked together and named a song around their rapport, called "<a href="/wiki/Painless_(song)" title="Painless (song)">Painless</a>." The song went on to be Laird's first released song by <a href="/wiki/Lee_Ann_Womack" title="Lee Ann Womack">Lee Ann Womack</a> in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Laird and Lindsey went on to work on a number of songs with <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, including his first number one single "<a href="/wiki/So_Small" title="So Small">So Small</a>" in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The song held the number one spot on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Billboard Country</a> charts for three weeks and went platinum.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Since then Laird has co-written 23 singles that reached No. 1 on the Billboard Country charts.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  He is a proponent of the “New Country” style of country music, and has worked with pop artists such as <a href="/wiki/Ne-Yo" title="Ne-Yo">Ne-Yo</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">John Legend</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He has written No. 1 hits with <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blake_Shelton" title="Blake Shelton">Blake Shelton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tim_McGraw" title="Tim McGraw">Tim McGraw</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Chesney" title="Kenny Chesney">Kenny Chesney</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Church" title="Eric Church">Eric Church</a>, among others, and has had hits with artists such as <a href="/wiki/Ingrid_Michaelson" title="Ingrid Michaelson">Ingrid Michaelson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Aldean" title="Jason Aldean">Jason Aldean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sara_Evans" title="Sara Evans">Sara Evans</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kacey_Musgraves" title="Kacey Musgraves">Kacey Musgraves</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was named <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_Music,_Inc." title="Broadcast Music, Inc.">BMI</a>'s Country Songwriter of the Year in 2012, and his song with <a href="/wiki/Rodney_Atkins" title="Rodney Atkins">Rodney Atkins</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Take_a_Back_Road_(song)" title="Take a Back Road (song)">Take a Back Road</a>" was named Song of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He was named ACM's Songwriter of the Year in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has been nominated for three <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Song">Grammys for Best Country Song</a>: twice in 2015, for <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Chesney" title="Kenny Chesney">Kenny Chesney</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/American_Kids" title="American Kids">American Kids</a>" and for <a href="/wiki/Eric_Church" title="Eric Church">Eric Church</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Give_Me_Back_My_Hometown" title="Give Me Back My Hometown">Give Me Back My Hometown</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-:7_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and again in 2016 for <a href="/wiki/Tim_McGraw" title="Tim McGraw">Tim McGraw</a>'s "Diamond Rings and Old Barstools."<sup id="cite_ref-:6_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Although he is primarily interested in songwriting, Laird also produced two <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Album">Grammy nominated albums</a> by <a href="/wiki/Kacey_Musgraves" title="Kacey Musgraves">Kacey Musgraves</a>, the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Same_Trailer_Different_Park" title="Same Trailer Different Park"><i>Same Trailer Different Park</i>–</a>which won<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>–and the 2016 <i><a href="/wiki/Pageant_Material" title="Pageant Material">Pageant Material</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-:6_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Creative_Nation">Creative Nation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Luke_Laird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Creative Nation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2011, Laird and his wife Beth founded <a href="/wiki/Creative_Nation" class="mw-redirect" title="Creative Nation">Creative Nation</a>, a music publishing and management company for country music songwriters and producers. While Beth Laird handles logistics, Luke Laird focuses on songwriting.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The company includes Kassi Ashton, Alec Bailey, Casey Brown, <a href="/wiki/Barry_Dean_(songwriter)" title="Barry Dean (songwriter)">Barry Dean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Hemby" title="Natalie Hemby">Natalie Hemby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lori_McKenna" title="Lori McKenna">Lori McKenna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Moakler" title="Steve Moakler">Steve Moakler</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tyler_Johnson_(musician)" title="Tyler Johnson (musician)">Tyler Johnson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The company has had a partnership with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Publishing_Group" title="Universal Music Publishing Group">Universal Music Publishing Group</a><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and currently has partnerships with <a href="/wiki/Sony/ATV_Music_Publishing" title="Sony/ATV Music Publishing">Sony ATV Music Publishing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scott_Cutler" title="Scott Cutler">Pulse Music Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
