<p>"<b>Before He Cheats</b>" is a song written by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Tompkins" title="Chris Tompkins">Chris Tompkins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Kear" title="Josh Kear">Josh Kear</a> and the third wide-release single from <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>'s debut studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Hearts" title="Some Hearts">Some Hearts</a></i> (2005). It was the fifth release from the album overall. The song tells the story of a woman taking revenge on her potentially unfaithful boyfriend/husband.
</p><p>The song became an enormous <a href="/wiki/Crossover_music" title="Crossover music">crossover</a> success, topping the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart for five consecutive weeks, reaching the top five on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Adult_Top_40" title="Adult Top 40">Adult Top 40</a> chart, and becoming a top ten hit on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Mainstream_Top_40" title="Mainstream Top 40">Mainstream Top 40</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adult_Contemporary_(chart)" title="Adult Contemporary (chart)">Adult Contemporary</a> charts. On the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart, "Before He Cheats" reached number eight and achieved a longevity of 64 consecutive weeks on the chart, making it the sixth longest-charting single in the history of the Hot 100 chart.
</p><p>"Before He Cheats" became the first ever country song to sell over two million digitally and was once the best-selling country song of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It had sold 4,312,000 digital downloads as of May 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and was once the best-selling song by an <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> contestant but is now the third best-selling (behind <a href="/wiki/Phillip_Phillips" title="Phillip Phillips">Phillip Phillips</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Home_(Phillip_Phillips_song)" title="Home (Phillip Phillips song)">Home</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Clarkson" title="Kelly Clarkson">Kelly Clarkson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Stronger_(What_Doesn%27t_Kill_You)" title="Stronger (What Doesn&#39;t Kill You)">Stronger (What Doesn't Kill You)</a>").<sup id="cite_ref-Ask_Billboard_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ask_Billboard-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It has also been certified 5× Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>, Underwood's largest-selling single to date.<sup id="cite_ref-CarrieUnderwoodSSearch_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CarrieUnderwoodSSearch-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song has received a number of accolades. At the <a href="/wiki/50th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="50th Annual Grammy Awards">50th Annual Grammy Awards</a>, Underwood won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Female_Country_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance">Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance</a> and the song's writers won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Song">Grammy Award for Best Country Song</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It received a nomination for the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Song_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Song of the Year">Grammy Award for Song of the Year</a> as well. It was also named the 2007 Single of the Year by the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a>. It ranked on CMT's <i>40 Greatest Songs of the Decade</i> at number 25, along with Underwood's other signature hit, "<a href="/wiki/Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel" title="Jesus, Take the Wheel">Jesus, Take the Wheel</a>", which ranked number four.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Before_He_Cheats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Before He Cheats" tells the story of a woman taking revenge on her potentially unfaithful boyfriend or husband.
</p><p>She imagines him hanging out and flirting with a "bleach-blonde" girl, shooting pool, buying her a <a href="/wiki/Alcoholic_beverage" class="mw-redirect" title="Alcoholic beverage">drink</a>, dancing, and hoping to "<a href="/wiki/Sexual_intercourse" title="Sexual intercourse">get lucky</a>" with her. In retaliation, she vandalizes his customized <a href="/wiki/Four-wheel_drive" title="Four-wheel drive">four-wheel drive</a> vehicle by <a href="/wiki/Vandalism" title="Vandalism">keying</a> the sides, carving her name into its leather seats, smashing the headlights with a <a href="/wiki/Louisville_Slugger" class="mw-redirect" title="Louisville Slugger">Louisville Slugger</a> baseball bat and slashing all four tires. All of these actions are part of her hopes that this will make him "think before he cheats" again.
</p><p>Although the song is about a tale of revenge, according to Carrie Underwood, while she had been cheated on before, "I wouldn't recommend doing any property damage, though. I'm a 'let it go, move on' kind of person."<sup id="cite_ref-billboard_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"Before He Cheats" is set in the key of <a href="/wiki/F-sharp_minor" title="F-sharp minor">F-sharp minor</a>. It has a moderate tempo in <a href="/wiki/Cut_time" class="mw-redirect" title="Cut time">cut time</a> with a "shuffle" beat. The song's main chord pattern is F<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-sharp">&#x266f;</span></span>m-E-D<sub>sus</sub>2-C<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-sharp">&#x266f;</span></span>m, and Underwood's vocals range from F<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-sharp">&#x266f;</span></span>3-C<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-sharp">&#x266f;</span></span>5.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Before_He_Cheats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video for "Before He Cheats" was directed by <a href="/wiki/Roman_White" title="Roman White">Roman White</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cmtvideo_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cmtvideo-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In the beginning of the video Underwood is seen leaving a parking lot brandishing a Louisville Slugger, having just vandalized her husband/boyfriend's truck for cheating on her. In other scenes, the "husband/boyfriend" (portrayed by actor Tabb Shoup) is seen kissing another woman. As the video progresses Underwood takes the stage to sing as various objects behind her, such as lamps, are seen exploding. At one point, a screen shot of the front seat of the truck is seen with Underwood's name carved in it, which she mentions during the chorus of the song. Eventually, she catches up with her cheating husband/boyfriend with the other woman. After looking shocked for a second, she scoffs and drops the keys to his now-demolished truck into his drink. Towards the end Underwood struts down a street, singing along with the song, while different objects are shown flying across the air, glasses are seen shattering, and light bulbs explode, paying homage to <a href="/wiki/Brian_De_Palma" title="Brian De Palma">Brian De Palma</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Carrie_(1976_film)" title="Carrie (1976 film)">Carrie</a></i>. At the very end of the music video, the truck is shown completely destroyed.
</p><p>The early scenes in the video featuring a crowd in a narrow street and Underwood in a parking garage, were filmed in and around <a href="/wiki/Printer%27s_Alley" title="Printer&#39;s Alley">Printer's Alley</a> in downtown <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>. The final scene, featuring the exploding glass and light bulbs, was filmed on Fourth Avenue just north of Church Street in Nashville.
</p><p>"Before He Cheats" made <a href="/wiki/Great_American_Country" title="Great American Country">GAC</a> music video history by debuting at number one. It also marks the third consecutive number-one music video on GAC for Underwood. It made history on CMT's Top Twenty Countdown for being at number one for a consecutive five weeks. In December 2006, "Before He Cheats" was named the best video of 2006 by CMT's Top 20 Countdown. It also finished number two in GAC's Video of the Year for 2006 behind <a href="/wiki/Trace_Adkins" title="Trace Adkins">Trace Adkins</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Honky_Tonk_Badonkadonk" title="Honky Tonk Badonkadonk">Honky Tonk Badonkadonk</a>". Proving its crossover success, the video made a debut at number 15 on the <i><a href="/wiki/VSpot_Top_20_Countdown" class="mw-redirect" title="VSpot Top 20 Countdown">VSpot Top 20 Countdown</a></i>, peaking at number 4.
</p><p>The video premiered on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Total_Request_Live" title="Total Request Live">Total Request Live</a></i> in April 2007, this is the second country music video to be premiered in this program, the first being "<a href="/wiki/These_Boots_Are_Made_for_Walkin%27" title="These Boots Are Made for Walkin&#39;">These Boots Are Made for Walkin'</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Simpson" title="Jessica Simpson">Jessica Simpson</a>.
</p><p>On April 16, 2007, the video for "Before He Cheats" swept the <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Awards" title="CMT Music Awards">CMT Music Awards</a>, winning three categories: Video of the Year, Female Video of the Year, and Director of the Year. Underwood made history by being the first female to win Video of the Year.
</p><p>The video also garnered Carrie a nomination for Music Video of the Year at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association" title="Country Music Association">Country Music Association</a> Awards and a nomination for a <a href="/wiki/2007_MTV_Video_Music_Awards" title="2007 MTV Video Music Awards">2007 MTV Video Music Award</a> in the category of Best New Artist.
</p><p>During an <i><a href="/wiki/NBC_Sunday_Night_Football" title="NBC Sunday Night Football">NBC Sunday Night Football</a></i> telecast of a <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Cowboys" title="Dallas Cowboys">Dallas Cowboys</a>-<a href="/wiki/New_York_Giants" title="New York Giants">New York Giants</a> game, the song was played over the Dallas offensive line-up announcements after Underwood broke up with Dallas QB <a href="/wiki/Tony_Romo" title="Tony Romo">Tony Romo</a>.
</p><p>The music video was ranked number nine on CMT's 100 Greatest Videos.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  It also ranked number one on <a href="/wiki/Great_American_Country" title="Great American Country">GAC</a>'s <i>Top 50 Videos of the 2000s</i>.
</p><p>In 2009, the music video for the song was voted number one by fans as VH1's Greatest Diva Music Video of all-time, before the airing of the annual VH1 Divas Live special.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Before_He_Cheats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Before He Cheats" first appeared on the charts in February 2006. Although it had not at that point been released as a single, many country stations began giving the song unsolicited airplay, leading it to debut on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart at number 59. By the time the song was officially released as <i>Some Hearts</i>&#39; third single in August of that year, it had already racked up 20 non-consecutive weeks on the chart, reaching as high as number 49. After the official release, the song climbed the country charts quickly, reaching number one in November and remaining there for five weeks. This was Underwood's third consecutive number one country single and fourth number one single overall. It also managed to debut on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart at number 92, and by November it had reached number 16 on that chart. By the end of 2006, the song began to slowly descend the charts, and it had appeared the single had peaked.
</p><p>However, pop radio began to take notice of the song in February 2007, around the time of Underwood's <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> wins. As the single increased its top 40 airplay, it began to rebound on the Hot 100 chart. The new airplay, along with attention from numerous award show wins for the song, such as favorite country song at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Choice_Awards" title="People&#39;s Choice Awards">People's Choice Awards</a> in January and Video of the Year at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Country Music Television">CMTs</a> in April, reinvigorated digital sales as well. When it finally peaked at number 8 in May 2007, it had already logged 38 weeks on the chart, making it the longest-trek to the top 10 ever. As pop airplay began dying down, the song got a third life on the <a href="/wiki/Adult_contemporary" class="mw-redirect" title="Adult contemporary">adult contemporary</a> format, which began playing the song in May. "Before He Cheats" spent 64 consecutive weeks on the Hot 100 chart, before finally falling off in late November 2007. The song is one of the longest-charting hits in <i>Billboard</i> history, and was the third longest-running hit of the 2000s decade.
</p><p>"Before He Cheats" was ranked sixth on the 2007 Hot 100 Year-end chart and fifth on the 2007 Hot 100 Airplay Year-end chart by <i>Billboard</i>. It was also ranked tenth on the 2007 Adult Contemporary Year-end chart.
</p><p>The song sold over two million in digital sales in December 2007, and was certified 2× Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> in February 2008, making it the first country song to achieve this feat.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> It was the best-selling single from an <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> contestant until <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Clarkson" title="Kelly Clarkson">Kelly Clarkson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Stronger_(What_Doesn%27t_Kill_You)" title="Stronger (What Doesn&#39;t Kill You)">Stronger (What Doesn't Kill You)</a>" surpassed it in the spring of 2012, and is still Underwood's best-selling single to date. It has sold over one million ringtones. In Canada, it is certified as Platinum. On August 10, 2015, the song was certified 5× Platinum by the RIAA.<sup id="cite_ref-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodBefore_He_CheatssingleCertRef_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodBefore_He_CheatssingleCertRef-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Despite not charting in the United Kingdom, "Before He Cheats" received a Silver certification from the <a href="/wiki/British_Phonographic_Industry" title="British Phonographic Industry">British Phonographic Industry</a> in December 2018 for sales and streams exceeding 200,000; it is Underwood's only certified song in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-ukcert_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ukcert-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song has sold 4,312,000 digital downloads in the United States as of May 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-us_sales_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-us_sales-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and it is the third best-selling song by an <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> contestant in the United States (behind <a href="/wiki/Phillip_Phillips" title="Phillip Phillips">Phillip Phillips</a>' "<a href="/wiki/Home_(Phillip_Phillips_song)" title="Home (Phillip Phillips song)">Home</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Clarkson" title="Kelly Clarkson">Kelly Clarkson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Stronger_(What_Doesn%27t_Kill_You)" title="Stronger (What Doesn&#39;t Kill You)">Stronger (What Doesn't Kill You)</a>").<sup id="cite_ref-Ask_Billboard_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ask_Billboard-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It is also the eighth best-selling country song in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard_jan_2014_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard_jan_2014-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 16, 2014, "Before He Cheats" entered the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Singles_Chart" title="Irish Singles Chart">Irish Singles Chart</a> at number 98, becoming Underwood's first appearance on that chart.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 31, 2015, the song was performed live along with "<a href="/wiki/Smoke_Break" title="Smoke Break">Smoke Break</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Heartbeat_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Heartbeat (Carrie Underwood song)">Heartbeat</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/Dick_Clark%27s_New_Year%27s_Rockin%27_Eve" title="Dick Clark&#39;s New Year&#39;s Rockin&#39; Eve">Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_versions">Cover versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Before_He_Cheats&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cover versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li>In 2010, <a href="/wiki/Adrianne_Leon" class="mw-redirect" title="Adrianne Leon">Adrianne Leon</a> sang a cover version on the American soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/General_Hospital" title="General Hospital">General Hospital</a></i> while portraying <a href="/wiki/Brook_Lynn_Ashton" class="mw-redirect" title="Brook Lynn Ashton">Brook Lynn Ashton</a>.</li>

<li>In 2013, <a href="/wiki/Sasha_Allen" title="Sasha Allen">Sasha Allen</a> performed the song in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Voice_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Voice (U.S. TV series)">The Voice</a></i>.</li>
<li>On her 2014 debut album, <a href="/wiki/Ana_Gasteyer" title="Ana Gasteyer">Ana Gasteyer</a> released a jazz cover of the song.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Pitch_Perfect_2" title="Pitch Perfect 2">Pitch Perfect 2</a></i>, the song is performed during the Riff-off by <a href="/wiki/The_Barden_Bellas" class="mw-redirect" title="The Barden Bellas">the Barden Bellas</a>.</li></ul>
