<p><i><b>Play On</b></i> is the third studio album by American singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>. It was released in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on November 3, 2009, through <a href="/wiki/Arista_Nashville" title="Arista Nashville">Arista Nashville</a>.
</p><p>The album debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> with first week sales of 318,000 copies, which was rated as the highest first-week sales for a female artist in 2009 upon release. It is her second album to debut at number one on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, and her third to debut atop the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Top_Country_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Country Albums">Top Country Albums</a> chart.
</p><p>As of October 2016, the album has been certified 3x Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>, Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Music_Canada" title="Music Canada">CRIA</a> and Gold by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Recording_Industry_Association" title="Australian Recording Industry Association">ARIA</a>. <i>Play On</i> has since sold over three million copies worldwide.
</p><p>The album spawned three consecutive <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> number one singles — "<a href="/wiki/Cowboy_Casanova" title="Cowboy Casanova">Cowboy Casanova</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Temporary_Home" title="Temporary Home">Temporary Home</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Undo_It" title="Undo It">Undo It</a>", all of which certified Multi-Platinum or Platinum by the RIAA. The fourth single, "<a href="/wiki/Mama%27s_Song" title="Mama&#39;s Song">Mama's Song</a>", reached number two on the chart and received a Gold certification from the RIAA. "Cowboy Casanova" achieved one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Billboard_Hot_100_chart_achievements_and_milestones#Biggest_single-week_upward_movements" title="List of Billboard Hot 100 chart achievements and milestones">biggest single-week upwards on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> of all time, when it climbed 96-11 in its second week.
</p><p>"Temporary Home" and "Mama's Song" were both nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a>, while <i>Play On</i> was nominated for Album of the Year at both the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music Awards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a>, and won <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Award_for_Favorite_Country_Album" title="American Music Award for Favorite Country Album">Favorite Country Album</a> at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2010" title="American Music Awards of 2010">2010 American Music Awards</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album was originally to have included some songs that Underwood wrote with <a href="/wiki/Ne-Yo" title="Ne-Yo">Ne-Yo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  One of the tracks entitled "Look at Me" was previously recorded by country singer, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jackson" title="Alan Jackson">Alan Jackson</a>, on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billy:_The_Early_Years_(soundtrack)" title="Billy: The Early Years (soundtrack)">Billy: The Early Years</a></i> soundtrack.  <a href="/wiki/Vince_Gill" title="Vince Gill">Vince Gill</a> sang background vocals on the track.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> "Mama's Song" sold more than 31,000 copies in its debut week.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 16, 2009, Underwood made a promotional stop in Singapore to promote her upcoming album to the Asian media. The album was released on November 2 in Singapore and various parts of Asia, followed by a November 3 release in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Underwood performed on <i>The Late Show with David Letterman</i> on November 2, and performed a special outdoor concert at Lincoln Center on <i>Good Morning America</i> on album release day, and rounded out her week with a visit to <i>Live with Regis and Kelly</i> on November 5.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Underwood also performed another outdoor concert for <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i> at the Sommet Center in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a> on November 11, 2009, with <a href="/wiki/Brad_Paisley" title="Brad Paisley">Brad Paisley</a>.
</p><p>On the November 16, 2009 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Conan_O%27Brien" title="The Tonight Show with Conan O&#39;Brien">The Tonight Show with Conan O'Brien</a></i>, Underwood performed "<a href="/wiki/Temporary_Home" title="Temporary Home">Temporary Home</a>", which is the second official single from <i>Play On</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to the release of <i>Play On</i>, three promotional singles were made available by iTunes. "<a href="/wiki/Mama%27s_Song" title="Mama&#39;s Song">Mama's Song</a>" released on October 12 followed by "Temporary Home" which was released on October 20 and "<a href="/wiki/Undo_It" title="Undo It">Undo It</a>" released on October 27. Billboard.com also posted a 60-second clip of each song being released on iTunes.
</p><p>She debuted several of her singles at the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music" title="Academy of Country Music">Academy of Country Music Awards</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a>.
</p><p>She also appeared on several shows to promote her album including <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">Late Show with David Letterman</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Live_With_Regis_and_Kelly" class="mw-redirect" title="Live With Regis and Kelly">Live With Regis and Kelly</a></i>,  <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_with_Conan_O%27Brien" title="The Tonight Show with Conan O&#39;Brien">The Tonight Show</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards" title="American Music Awards">American Music Awards</a>, <a href="/wiki/CNN_International" title="CNN International">CNN International</a>, and at the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Ole_Opry" title="Grand Ole Opry">Grand Ole Opry</a>.
</p><p>Underwood also performed in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> on October 16, 2009; it is her first public appearance in an Asian country in line of a series of events for the grand opening of the newest shopping attraction in the heart of the city, <a href="/wiki/ION_Orchard" title="ION Orchard">ION Orchard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Cowboy_Casanova" title="Cowboy Casanova">Cowboy Casanova</a>" served as the lead single for the album. It was released on September 14, 2009,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> two months before the album's official release and debuted on the U.S. Billboard <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> at number 26, the fifth highest debut by a female artist on the chart. It jumped from number 96 to number 11 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 in its second week, one of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Billboard_Hot_100_chart_achievements_and_milestones#Biggest_single-week_upward_movements" title="List of Billboard Hot 100 chart achievements and milestones">biggest single-week upwards on the chart</a> of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The song received a nomination for Song of the Year at the 45th <a href="/wiki/ACM_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="ACM Awards">ACM Awards</a>. Underwood was nominated as an artist and a composer. The music video for the song was released in October 2009. It has sold 2,300,000 copies in the United States and has been certified 2x platinum.<sup id="cite_ref-riaaSingles_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riaaSingles-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"<a href="/wiki/Temporary_Home" title="Temporary Home">Temporary Home</a>", the second single, was released to country radio starting the week of December 14, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The song has received multiple forms of promotion, including performances on the <a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">Late Show with David Letterman</a>, the television special "A Home For the Holidays", Underwood's personal CMT Invitation Only performance, Underwood's Christmas variety show entitled "Carrie Underwood: An All-Star Holiday Special", at the 45th <a href="/wiki/ACM_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="ACM Awards">ACM Awards</a>, and on her tour. The song has hit at number one on the U.S. Billboard <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> becoming her ninth number one song in the process. It has also hit number 41 on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. The music video for the song was released in February 2010. For her performance of the song in her personal CMT Invitation Only, Carrie won the CMT award for Best Live Performance at the 2010 CMT Awards.
The song received a nomination at the <a href="/wiki/53rd_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="53rd Grammy Awards">53rd Grammy Awards</a> for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Female_Country_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance">Best Female Country Vocal performance</a>. It has sold 1,093,000 copies and has been certified Platinum by the RIAA.<sup id="cite_ref-riaaSingles_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riaaSingles-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The third single, "<a href="/wiki/Undo_It" title="Undo It">Undo It</a>", was released on May 24, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Underwood performed the song on the season finale of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_9)" title="American Idol (season 9)">American Idol</a></i> and at the 2010 <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Awards" title="CMT Music Awards">CMT Music Awards</a>. The song peaked at number 23 on the Hot 100, giving Underwood her eleventh Top 40 single and reached number one on Hot Country Songs chart. "Undo It" was nominated for the 2010 Teen Choice Award for 'Choice Country Song'. It has sold 1,600,000 copies and has been certified Platinum.<sup id="cite_ref-riaaSingles_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riaaSingles-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The fourth single, "<a href="/wiki/Mama%27s_Song" title="Mama&#39;s Song">Mama's Song</a>", was officially released to radio on September 13, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-allaccess.com_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allaccess.com-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Underwood announced this in an interview after her performance on the Today Show on NBC on July 30. The video for the song premiered on <a href="/wiki/VEVO" class="mw-redirect" title="VEVO">VEVO</a> on September 24, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <i>Mama's Song</i> reached the number two position on the country charts. It has been certified Gold and has sold 443,000 copies. At the <a href="/wiki/54th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="54th Grammy Awards">54th Grammy Awards</a>, the song was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Solo_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Solo Performance">Best Country Solo Performance</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotional_singles">Promotional singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Promotional singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prior to the release of the album, three promotional singles were released exclusively on <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a>'s <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a>. These promotional singles should not be confused with ordinary <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">singles</a>.
</p><p>"<a href="/wiki/Mama%27s_Song" title="Mama&#39;s Song">Mama's Song</a>" was the first promotional single and was released on October 12, 2009. It was released to radio as the fourth official single off the album on September 13, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-allaccess.com_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allaccess.com-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Temporary_Home" title="Temporary Home">Temporary Home</a>" was the second promotional single and was released on October 20, 2009.  It was released to radio as the second official single off the album on December 14, 2009. "<a href="/wiki/Undo_It" title="Undo It">Undo It</a>" was the final promotional single and was released on October 27, 2009.  It was released to radio as the  third official single off the album on May 24, 2010.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tours">Tours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Tours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_American_Tour">North American Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: North American Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 11, 2010, Underwood began the <a href="/wiki/Play_On_Tour" title="Play On Tour">Play On Tour</a>. The tour includes several legs traveling across the globe, with the first and second leg focusing on the United States and Canada. The first began in Reading, Pennsylvania on March 11, and concluded in St. Paul, Minnesota at the Minnesota State Fair on August 31, 2010. The second leg began on September 25, 2010 in Portland, Oregon, and concluded on December 19 in Calgary, Alberta, Canada.  Her opening acts/guest performers for the spring tour were <a href="/wiki/Craig_Morgan_(singer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Craig Morgan (singer)">Craig Morgan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sons_of_Sylvia" title="Sons of Sylvia">Sons of Sylvia</a>. The second leg included Sons of Sylvia and country singer <a href="/wiki/Billy_Currington" title="Billy Currington">Billy Currington</a>.
</p><p>In July 2010, the tour ranked at number fifteen on the Top 100 Tours for the first half of the year. Within four 4 months of the tour's first leg, it raked in an approximate total of $18,400,000, and has sold more than 375,000 tickets.
</p><p>In December 2010, the tour ranked at number 19 for the largest North American tours of 2010,<sup id="cite_ref-pollstarpro1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pollstarpro1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and again at number 31 for the largest tours worldwide for the 2010 touring year.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The tour made a collective total of $38,300,000 <sup id="cite_ref-pollstarpro1_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pollstarpro1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and reached to over one million fans,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> becoming Underwood's largest tour to date.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australian_Tour">Australian Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Australian Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On May 16, 2011, <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music" title="Sony Music">Sony Music</a> and Underwood collectively announced that the Country singer would travel to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> in June 2011 to promote her <i>Play On</i> album. During the tour's duration, Underwood would also release the <i>Play On: Deluxe Edition</i> exclusively to Australia. The album included six previous number one singles of Underwood's, as well as a studio recording of "<a href="/wiki/Home_Sweet_Home_(Motley_Crue_song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Home Sweet Home (Motley Crue song)">Home Sweet Home</a>", a recording in which has never been featured on an album before.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Underwood participated in numerous televised events, media coverage, talk shows, and other miscellaneous appearances to promote the album. This was Underwood's first time headlining an international tour.
</p>

<p><i>Play On</i> received mixed reviews. The album had a score of 54 out of 100 from <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> based on "mixed or average reviews" from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Metacritic_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metacritic-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_Erlewine" title="Stephen Thomas Erlewine">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</a> of <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> gave the album 2.5 out of 5 stars. He argues that "Carrie is still nominally a country artist and sometimes will sing supported by fiddles and steel guitar, but this is crossover pop pure and simple, whether it's the thundering rhythms on the <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain" title="Shania Twain">Shania</a>-styled strut "Cowboy Casanova" or the succession of maudlin melodies on the preponderance of power ballads."<sup id="cite_ref-AllmusicReview_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllmusicReview-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In his Consumer Guide, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a> gave the album a "dud" rating (<span style="white-space:nowrap" title="(dud)"><a href="/wiki/Christgau%27s_Consumer_Guide:_Albums_of_the_%2790s#Grading_key" title="(dud)"><img alt="(dud)" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Rating-Christgau-dud.svg/17px-Rating-Christgau-dud.svg.png" decoding="async" width="17" height="13" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Rating-Christgau-dud.svg/26px-Rating-Christgau-dud.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Rating-Christgau-dud.svg/34px-Rating-Christgau-dud.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="265" data-file-height="207" /></a></span>).<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Leah Greenblatt rated it B in <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i>, saying "If it seems vaguely insulting to call the smashingly successful <i>Idol</i> alum’s material formulaic, know at least that it is, three albums in, one of the most well-honed formulas in the business."<sup id="cite_ref-EWreview_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EWreview-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sean Daly from the <i>St. Petersburg Times</i> argues that <i>Play On</i> is Underwood's "weakest album to date," but predicts that the album will "sell like gangbusters." Daly says that "<a href="/wiki/Cowboy_Casanova" title="Cowboy Casanova">Cowboy Casanova</a>", "co-written by <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> buddy Mike Elizondo, brazenly bites from both 'Before He Cheats' and 'Last Name'." Daly highlights the song "Someday When I Stop Loving You". He says that it "is so good. It has a vague '70s feel, reminiscent of all those great old Chicago songs."<sup id="cite_ref-StPetersburgTimesReview_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StPetersburgTimesReview-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Blake Boldt of Engine 145 (Country Music) graded the album 3 out of 5 stars. He states "Underwood’s third disc, <i>Play On</i>, starts promisingly, with “Cowboy Casanova”, an ode to a hard-to-keep lothario, plows along to a grungy electric guitar laced with a curling pedal steel line. At her best, Underwood excels at handling the finest storytelling that Nashville has to offer. The album’s strongest track is “Someday When I Stop Loving You”, a brooding tune framed beautifully by her delicate performance, while “What Can I Say”, a collaboration with sibling trio Sons of Sylvia, is a simple, elegant declaration of longing. He added that the remaining 45 minutes, though, are more Hallmark than honky tonk. <i>Play On</i> is a tastefully-done pop pleasure, with little of the pretentious production that gutted much of her first two discs. <i>Play On</i>, though saddled with its share of loose songwriting, is a convincing reminder of a blonde, budding talent."<sup id="cite_ref-Engine145Review_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Engine145Review-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The album debuted at number one on the <i>Billboard</i> Top Country Albums Chart, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, with first week sales of 318,000 copies, and rated as the highest first week sales for a female artist in 2009, before being overtaken by <a href="/wiki/Susan_Boyle" title="Susan Boyle">Susan Boyle</a> a few weeks later.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> In its second week, the album slipped to number three with sales of 128,000. <i>Play On</i> spent nine consecutive weeks at number two in Top Country Albums Chart, and stayed in the Top 10 of the <i>Billboard</i> 200 for its first eight weeks of release. By October 2015, the album had sold 2,300,000 copies in the US, and it was certified 3x Platinum by the RIAA on October 24, 2016 for three million units in sales and streams.<sup id="cite_ref-USsales_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USsales-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The album debuted at number 114 on the <a href="/wiki/Oricon" title="Oricon">Japan Oricon Albums Chart</a> and number 93 on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-theofficialcharts.com_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theofficialcharts.com-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> becoming Underwood's first entry on both charts. In Canada, the album debuted at number two on the Canadian charts with 15,000 sales.<sup id="cite_ref-winnipegsun.com_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-winnipegsun.com-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, it debuted at number three on the Top 20 Country Albums chart and at number 80 on the National Album Charts ARIA Top 100 despite very little advertising at the time. In June 2011, Underwood traveled to Australia for a promotional tour for the deluxe edition of <i>Play On</i>. For the chart week following her promotional tour, the album re-entered both the Top 20 Country Albums chart and the National Album Charts ARIA Top 100 at number two and number fourteen, respectively. The following week, it rose to number one on the Top 20 Country Albums chart, giving Underwood her first number one album in Australia, and remained stable on the National Album Charts ARIA Top 100, at number fifteen. It has reached Gold status in Australia and it is her first album to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-countrymusicchannel_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-countrymusicchannel-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> <i>Play On</i> has since sold over three million copies worldwide.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p><i>Play On</i> charted at number seventy three on <i>Billboard</i> 200 and at number 15 on Top Country Albums for the year ending 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> In December 2010, the album  was placed on <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a>'</i>s Year-End Charts, landing at number four for Country Albums, and at number twelve for <i>Billboard</i> 200 Albums. The following year, it charted at number 37 for Country Albums <sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> and number 176 for <i>Billboard</i> 200 Albums.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
