<p>The <b>coat of arms of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a></b> was adopted on November 25, 1975. The motto reads <i>Justitia – Pietas – Fides</i> (“Justice – Piety – Fidelity”). It further consists of two natives who carry a shield. The left half of the shield symbolizes the past, as <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a> were abducted via ship out of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. The right half, the side of the present, shows a <a href="/wiki/Roystonea_regia" title="Roystonea regia">royal palm</a>, also the symbol of a just person ("The just person should blossom like a palm"). The <a href="/wiki/Diamond" title="Diamond">diamond</a> in the middle is the stylized form of the heart, which is regarded as the organ of love. The points of the diamond show the four directions of the <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">wind</a>. Inside the diamond is a five-pointed star. This star symbolizes the five <a href="/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">continents</a> from which the inhabitants of Suriname migrated: Africa, America, Australia, Asia, and Europe.   
</p>
