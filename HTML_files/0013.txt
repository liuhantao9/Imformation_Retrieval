	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Arista Nashville</h1>

<p><b>Arista Nashville</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">record label</a> that serves as a wholly owned division of <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music" title="Sony Music">Sony Music</a>, operated under the <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Nashville" title="Sony Music Nashville">Sony Music Nashville</a> division. Founded in 1989, the label specializes in <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> artists, including <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jackson" title="Alan Jackson">Alan Jackson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brooks_%26_Dunn" title="Brooks &amp; Dunn">Brooks &amp; Dunn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brad_Paisley" title="Brad Paisley">Brad Paisley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>. The label used to operate three sister labels: Career Records, Arista Austin, and Arista Latin. It is a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a> since Arista's relaunch in 2018, but during the dissolution, Arista Nashville was not affected and it remains a Sony Music label.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arista_Nashville&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><a href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg" class="image"><img alt="[icon]" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" decoding="async" width="20" height="14" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="44" data-file-height="31" /></a></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>&#32;with: More history. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Arista_Nashville&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">June 2010</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Arista Nashville was formed in 1989 by <a href="/wiki/Tim_DuBois" title="Tim DuBois">Tim DuBois</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clive_Davis" title="Clive Davis">Clive Davis</a>. <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jackson" title="Alan Jackson">Alan Jackson</a> was the first artist signed to the label. Artists signed to the label within its first five years included <a href="/wiki/Asleep_at_the_Wheel" title="Asleep at the Wheel">Asleep at the Wheel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exile_(American_band)" title="Exile (American band)">Exile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rob_Crosby" title="Rob Crosby">Rob Crosby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Wariner" title="Steve Wariner">Steve Wariner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Wright" title="Michelle Wright">Michelle Wright</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pam_Tillis" title="Pam Tillis">Pam Tillis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brooks_%26_Dunn" title="Brooks &amp; Dunn">Brooks &amp; Dunn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Rio" title="Diamond Rio">Diamond Rio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Roy_Parnell" title="Lee Roy Parnell">Lee Roy Parnell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Radney_Foster" title="Radney Foster">Radney Foster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackhawk_(band)" title="Blackhawk (band)">Blackhawk</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Tractors" title="The Tractors">The Tractors</a>. Brooks &amp; Dunn remained with the label for the entirety of their career.
</p><p>The label founded Arista Texas in 1993, a sub-label specializing in <a href="/wiki/Texas_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Texas music">Texas music</a> artists. In 1997, it was split into Arista Austin, which featured <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Black_(singer-songwriter)" title="Jeff Black (singer-songwriter)">Jeff Black</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Earl_Keen" title="Robert Earl Keen">Robert Earl Keen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Abra_Moore" title="Abra Moore">Abra Moore</a>, and a Spanish-language label called Arista Latin. Foster also moved to Arista Austin from Arista Nashville in 1997.
</p><p>In 1995, Arista Nashville founded a sister label called Career Records. Parnell was transferred to this label upon its foundation, while <a href="/wiki/Brett_James" title="Brett James">Brett James</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tammy_Graham" title="Tammy Graham">Tammy Graham</a> were newly signed to it.<sup id="cite_ref-dubois_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dubois-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> All three artists moved back to Arista Nashville when Career Records was dissolved. While Graham and James were dropped from it soon afterward, James re-signed to Arista Nashville between 2002 and 2003.
</p><p>From 2005 to 2017, Arista Nashville, Arista Records, and 19 Recordings promoted and distributed recordings by <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> winner <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>.
</p><p>In late 2009, <a href="/wiki/Brad_Paisley" title="Brad Paisley">Brad Paisley</a> launched a personal label, Sea Gayle, which is distributed through Arista. The label's first signee was <a href="/wiki/Jerrod_Niemann" title="Jerrod Niemann">Jerrod Niemann</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the years after the dissolution of <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">Arista Records</a> in 2011, Arista Nashville remained <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music" title="Sony Music">Sony Music</a>'s only label with the Arista name<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> until Sony
reopened Arista in July 2018,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> becoming Arista Nashville's parent company again.
</p>
