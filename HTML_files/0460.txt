	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Sir John Ramsden, 4th Baronet</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Sir John Ramsden, 4th Baronet</b> (1755 – 15 July 1839) was an English landowner and <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a>.
<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was born the son of <a href="/wiki/Sir_John_Ramsden,_3rd_Baronet" title="Sir John Ramsden, 3rd Baronet">Sir John Ramsden, 3rd Baronet</a> of <a href="/wiki/Byram,_North_Yorkshire" title="Byram, North Yorkshire">Byram</a>, near Pontefract, Yorkshire, whom he succeeded in 1769, inheriting the Manor of Huddersfield. He was educated at <a href="/wiki/University_College,_Oxford" title="University College, Oxford">University College, Oxford</a> (1774).
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ex-Midland_Railway_viaduct_over_the_Huddersfield_Broad_Canal_-_geograph.org.uk_-_789339.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Ex-Midland_Railway_viaduct_over_the_Huddersfield_Broad_Canal_-_geograph.org.uk_-_789339.jpg/220px-Ex-Midland_Railway_viaduct_over_the_Huddersfield_Broad_Canal_-_geograph.org.uk_-_789339.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="140" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Ex-Midland_Railway_viaduct_over_the_Huddersfield_Broad_Canal_-_geograph.org.uk_-_789339.jpg/330px-Ex-Midland_Railway_viaduct_over_the_Huddersfield_Broad_Canal_-_geograph.org.uk_-_789339.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Ex-Midland_Railway_viaduct_over_the_Huddersfield_Broad_Canal_-_geograph.org.uk_-_789339.jpg/440px-Ex-Midland_Railway_viaduct_over_the_Huddersfield_Broad_Canal_-_geograph.org.uk_-_789339.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="407" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ex-Midland_Railway_viaduct_over_the_Huddersfield_Broad_Canal_-_geograph.org.uk_-_789339.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Huddersfield Broad Canal</div></div></div>
<p>An Act of Parliament (14 George III. Cap. 13) obtained on 9 March 1774, enabled "Sir John Ramsden, Baronet, to make and maintain a navigable Canal from the River Calder, between a Bridge called Cooper's Bridge, and the Mouth of the River Colne to the King's Mill, near the town of Huddersfleld, in the West Riding of the county of York". Completed in 1776 and originally named Sir John Ramsden's Canal, it is now known as the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Broad_Canal" title="Huddersfield Broad Canal">Huddersfield Broad Canal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was elected Member of Parliament (MP) for <a href="/wiki/Grampound_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Grampound (UK Parliament constituency)">Grampound</a> under the patronage of Lord Rockingham in 1780, retiring from politics in 1784. He was made <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_Yorkshire" title="High Sheriff of Yorkshire">High Sheriff of Yorkshire</a> for 1797–98.
</p><p>He died in 1839. He had married the Hon. Louisa Susan Ingram-Shepherd, daughter and coheiress of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Ingram,_9th_Viscount_of_Irvine" title="Charles Ingram, 9th Viscount of Irvine">Charles Ingram, 9th Viscount of Irvine</a>, with whom he had 4 sons and 5 daughters. His eldest son was <a href="/wiki/John_Charles_Ramsden" title="John Charles Ramsden">John Charles Ramsden</a>, MP, who however predeceased him. The baronetcy thus passed to John Charles' son <a href="/wiki/Sir_John_William_Ramsden,_5th_Baronet" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir John William Ramsden, 5th Baronet">Sir John William Ramsden, 5th Baronet</a>.
</p>
