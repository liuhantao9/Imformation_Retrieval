<p><b>Ryan Guno Babel</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:&#32;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Dutch" title="Help:IPA/Dutch">[ˈrɑjɐn ˈbaːbəl]</a></span>; born 19 December 1986)<sup id="cite_ref-LFC_profile_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LFC_profile-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> is a Dutch professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for <a href="/wiki/S%C3%BCper_Lig" title="Süper Lig">Süper Lig</a> club <a href="/wiki/Galatasaray_S.K._(football)" title="Galatasaray S.K. (football)">Galatasaray</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands national team</a>. He can play as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> or <a href="/wiki/Winger_(soccer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Winger (soccer)">left winger</a>.
</p><p>Babel began his career at Ajax in 1998, working his way up through the youth team and into the senior squad. He played three seasons for the first team before he was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> in mid-2007, where under three successive managers he failed to secure a regular first team position. Babel was sold to <a href="/wiki/TSG_1899_Hoffenheim" title="TSG 1899 Hoffenheim">TSG 1899 Hoffenheim</a> in January 2011, for a sum around half of that which Liverpool paid for him. His time at Hoffenheim was marred with disciplinary problems with three managers at the club. In the summer of 2012, Babel bought out the remaining final year of his contract, making him a free agent, and re-signed a one-year contract with Ajax. After this season, Babel went on to sign with Turkish club <a href="/wiki/Kas%C4%B1mpa%C5%9Fa_S.K." title="Kasımpaşa S.K.">Kasımpaşa</a> on 21 June 2013. Babel often was linked to a return to the Premier League during his time in Turkey, before ending rumours by signing for UAE side <a href="/wiki/Al_Ain_FC" title="Al Ain FC">Al Ain FC</a> in July 2015.
</p><p>Babel has been part of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands national team</a> since 2005, and has represented his country at all youth team levels. He played in two World Cups, 2006 and 2010, reaching the final of the latter.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Babel was born in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hugman2010-11_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hugman2010-11-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Inspired by the local footballing talent, including <a href="/wiki/Ruud_Gullit" title="Ruud Gullit">Ruud Gullit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Rijkaard" title="Frank Rijkaard">Frank Rijkaard</a>, Babel played for nearby youth-teams S.V Diemen, then Fortius.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 1997, he attended a youth selection day with <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">AFC Ajax</a>. He made it through the first selection round, but failed to progress any further.<sup id="cite_ref-biography_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-biography-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> However, the next year Ajax accepted him and he played the 1999–2000 season for their D1 team.<sup id="cite_ref-biography_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-biography-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After having graduated through the C1, B1 and A1 teams, Babel signed his first professional contract in January 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-biography_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-biography-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ajax">Ajax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ajax">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Playing as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a>, on 1 February 2004, just one and a half months after his 17th birthday, Babel made his first-team debut in Ajax's 4–0 home win over <a href="/wiki/ADO_Den_Haag" title="ADO Den Haag">ADO Den Haag</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ARCHIVES_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ARCHIVES-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Ajax went on to win the Dutch league title, but Babel didn't feature again that season. Nine months later, on 20 November 2004 Babel scored his first senior goal against <a href="/wiki/De_Graafschap" title="De Graafschap">De Graafschap</a> in a 5–0 victory.<sup id="cite_ref-fifa_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fifa-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2005, Babel signed a new contract with Ajax. He started the new season by scoring the winning goal in Ajax's 2–1 defeat of <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Johan_Cruyff_Shield" title="Johan Cruyff Shield">Johan Cruyff Shield</a>. Babel scored in both legs of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> third qualifying round against <a href="/wiki/Br%C3%B8ndby_IF" title="Brøndby IF">Brøndby</a> as Ajax made it to the group stage. 2005–06 was a tougher season for Babel in general though as he managed only two league goals. He did however continue to feature for the national team, and scored his second goal against <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_football_team" title="Italy national football team">Italy</a> in November. At the end of the season, Babel came on as a second-half <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> in Ajax's 2–1 victory over PSV in the <a href="/wiki/KNVB_Cup" title="KNVB Cup">KNVB Cup</a> final.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Babel marked the start of 2006–07 with another Johan Cruyff Shield medal, as Ajax defeated PSV 3–1. Babel was linked with <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United" class="mw-redirect" title="Newcastle United">Newcastle United</a> during the January transfer window of the <a href="/wiki/2007_in_football_(soccer)" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 in football (soccer)">2006–07 season</a> but no move materialised.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Amidst the transfer speculation, Babel agreed to a new three-year deal with Ajax on 2 February 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-ARCHIVES_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ARCHIVES-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In May, Babel got another Dutch Cup winners medal as Ajax successfully defended the title. The match against AZ went to a penalty shoot-out, but Babel was substituted off after 120 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liverpool">Liverpool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Liverpool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 10 July 2007 it was reported that <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> had made a <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>14&#160;million offer to the Amsterdam club.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On 12 July it was reported that Liverpool and Ajax had agreed a fee in the region of £11.5&#160;million, and later Liverpool confirmed that Babel would sign a five-year contract on 13 July.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He was unveiled on 13 July with <a href="/wiki/Yossi_Benayoun" title="Yossi Benayoun">Yossi Benayoun</a>. Babel was handed the number 19 shirt. He made his debut on 17 July against <a href="/wiki/Werder_Bremen" class="mw-redirect" title="Werder Bremen">Werder Bremen</a> in a friendly. He was due to play in the first round of the <a href="/wiki/Barclays_Asia_Trophy_2007" class="mw-redirect" title="Barclays Asia Trophy 2007">Barclays Asia Trophy 2007</a> but had a delay in the granting of international clearance. This was sorted out in time for the final against Portsmouth.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He made his <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> debut in the 2007–08 Premier League curtain raiser against <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> away after coming on off the bench. A week later he made his <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a> debut, coming off the bench against <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a>. On 1 September Babel scored his first goal for Liverpool against <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Babel scored his first Champions League goal for Liverpool on 6 November against <a href="/wiki/Be%C5%9Fikta%C5%9F_J.K." title="Beşiktaş J.K.">Beşiktaş J.K.</a> after coming on as a substitute. He netted twice in the game and almost completed a hat-trick, with a header from a <a href="/wiki/Harry_Kewell" title="Harry Kewell">Harry Kewell</a> cross, but was denied by the crossbar.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Babel scored the fourth and last goal in Liverpool's <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_UEFA_Champions_League_group_stage" title="2007–08 UEFA Champions League group stage">2007–08 UEFA Champions League group stage</a> match against <a href="/wiki/Olympique_de_Marseille" title="Olympique de Marseille">Olympique de Marseille</a>, which they needed to win.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He came off the bench in the second leg of the quarter final against Arsenal in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">Champions League</a> and won a penalty and scored a goal, with Liverpool winning the game 4–2 (5–3 on aggregate).<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He also came on as a substitute against Chelsea in the semi-final and despite scoring a goal, Liverpool lost 3–2 in extra time.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ryan_Babel_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Ryan_Babel_2010.jpg/220px-Ryan_Babel_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="392" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Ryan_Babel_2010.jpg/330px-Ryan_Babel_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Ryan_Babel_2010.jpg/440px-Ryan_Babel_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="632" data-file-height="1126" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ryan_Babel_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Babel before Jamie Carragher's testimonial match in 2010</div></div></div>
<p>Former Liverpool and <a href="/wiki/Celtic_F.C." title="Celtic F.C.">Celtic</a> player <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Dalglish" title="Kenny Dalglish">Kenny Dalglish</a> has said that Babel has the ability to terrorise defenders in the Premier League with his pace and trickery with the ball.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On 13 September 2008, Babel came off the bench to score the winner for Liverpool in a 2–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester United">Manchester United</a>, his first goal of the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League_2008-09" class="mw-redirect" title="Premier League 2008-09">2008–09 season</a><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Babel scored his second goal of the season in Liverpool's 5–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> on 28 December 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 20 September 2009, against West Ham, Babel came off the bench for Dirk Kuyt. At this stage the score was 2–2. Babel picked up the ball and did well to beat the full-back and sent in a delightful cross for <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Torres" title="Fernando Torres">Fernando Torres</a> to head home the winner. Babel, who has been criticised in the past for his work rate and attitude, earned praise for his efforts in the West Ham game.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
On 27 September 2009, Babel scored two goals against <a href="/wiki/Hull_City_A.F.C." title="Hull City A.F.C.">Hull City</a> coming off the bench for Fernando Torres to round up a match which ended 6–1 for Liverpool.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On 4 November 2009, Babel scored the opening goal against <a href="/wiki/Olympique_Lyonnais" title="Olympique Lyonnais">Lyon</a> in a Champions League game, a strike from 25 yards in a 1–1 draw. Former Liverpool player <a href="/wiki/Alan_Hansen" title="Alan Hansen">Alan Hansen</a> called upon Babel to play like he did against Lyon, as he still possessed the ability to be a 'top player'.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 6 January 2010, it was reported that Liverpool rejected an £8&#160;million offer from <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C" class="mw-redirect" title="Birmingham City F.C">Birmingham City</a> for Babel.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Babel was then disciplined by manager Benitez, over stating on his Twitter page that he had been dropped for the game against <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a>, and was fined two weeks wages of £120,000. He was frequently linked with a move away from Anfield but Rafael Benitez stated his desire for Babel to stay. On 15 March 2010 he scored against <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth_F.C." title="Portsmouth F.C.">Portsmouth</a> in a 4–1 win. On 1 April 2010, he was <a href="/wiki/Sent_off" class="mw-redirect" title="Sent off">sent off</a> for the first time in his Liverpool career in the 30th minute of the first leg of the <a href="/wiki/Europa_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Europa League">Europa League</a> quarter-final against <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">S.L. Benfica</a> after an altercation with <a href="/wiki/Luis%C3%A3o" title="Luisão">Luisão</a>. He scored in Liverpool's 4–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a> at Turf Moor, subsequently relegating Burnley to the Championship.
On 19 August 2010, Babel scored the winner against <a href="/wiki/Trabzonspor" title="Trabzonspor">Trabzonspor</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Europa_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Europa League">Europa League</a> qualifier first leg, it was his first game of the 2010–11 season.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> He started his first match in the Premier League at <a href="/wiki/Anfield" title="Anfield">Anfield</a> against Aston Villa and scored his first goal in the Premier League with a right-footed volley that beat former Liverpool goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Brad_Friedel" title="Brad Friedel">Brad Friedel</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Babel gained further notoriety amongst fans when, on <a href="/wiki/Transfer_window" title="Transfer window">transfer deadline day</a> 31 August 2010, it was reported that the player was travelling by helicopter between Liverpool and an unspecified London location as possible transfer talks with <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham</a> were ongoing, and there was speculation about his true destination.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> No transfer actually occurred and the helicopter story may be apocryphal, but the image stuck and the term "Babelcopter" became a metaphor for players with an uncertain destination on future transfer deadline days, with the player himself promoting the use of the <a href="/wiki/Hashtag" title="Hashtag">hashtag</a> #BabelCopter.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>One of the first Premier League players to use Twitter to communicate with fans, in January 2011, Babel posted a <a href="/wiki/Photoshop" class="mw-redirect" title="Photoshop">photoshopped</a> image on Twitter of referee <a href="/wiki/Howard_Webb" title="Howard Webb">Howard Webb</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester United">Manchester United</a> shirt following Liverpool's 1–0 defeat to United in the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_FA_Cup" title="2010–11 FA Cup">FA Cup</a>. He was subsequently charged by <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">the F.A.</a> with improper conduct and fined £10,000.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 18 January, Liverpool agreed a fee believed to be in the region of £7&#160;million for Babel from <a href="/wiki/TSG_1899_Hoffenheim" title="TSG 1899 Hoffenheim">TSG 1899 Hoffenheim</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On 24 January, <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Dalglish" title="Kenny Dalglish">Kenny Dalglish</a> said that Babel would be staying at Liverpool,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> but the next day Babel flew to Germany to finalise the deal to sign with Hoffenheim.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1899_Hoffenheim">1899 Hoffenheim</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1899 Hoffenheim">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 25 January 2011, it was confirmed that Babel had left Liverpool to join German side TSG 1899 Hoffenheim for a reported fee of £8 million, signing a two-and-a-half-year deal.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His first competitive match was on 26 January 2011 in the <a href="/wiki/DFB-Pokal" title="DFB-Pokal">DFB Cup</a> quarter final match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Energie_Cottbus" title="FC Energie Cottbus">FC Energie Cottbus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He is well known for his 'left little finger out' celebration, in support of disadvantaged youths in Amsterdam.
On 9 April 2011, Babel scored his first goal for Hoffenheim, in a 3–2 loss to <a href="/wiki/SC_Freiburg" title="SC Freiburg">SC Freiburg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
In the 2011–12 Bundesliga season, Babel netted his first league goal of the season on 20 August 2011 against <a href="/wiki/FC_Augsburg" title="FC Augsburg">Augsburg</a> in a 2–0 away win. On 10 September 2011, he scored his first brace for 1899 Hoffenheim in a 4–0 win against <a href="/wiki/1._FSV_Mainz_05" title="1. FSV Mainz 05">Mainz 05</a> and scored again in the next game on 17 September in a 3–1 win over <a href="/wiki/VfL_Wolfsburg" title="VfL Wolfsburg">VfL Wolfsburg</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>After playing for Hoffenheim for 18 months, scoring six goals in 51 matches, he was released by the club on 31 August 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_Ajax">Return to Ajax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Return to Ajax">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After personally buying off the remaining year of his contract with Hoffenheim, he returned to his old club Ajax on a one-year deal. Wearing shirt number 49, the same number he wore when he made his first ever appearance for the first team at <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a>, he made his debut with Ajax for the 2012–13 season on 15 September 2012, in a regular season match against <a href="/wiki/RKC_Waalwijk" title="RKC Waalwijk">RKC Waalwijk</a>. He came on as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Derk_Boerrigter" title="Derk Boerrigter">Derk Boerrigter</a> in the second half, assisting <a href="/wiki/Jody_Lukoki" title="Jody Lukoki">Jody Lukoki</a> on the second goal in the 2–0 home win for the <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> side.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first goal since returning to Ajax in a match against ADO Den Haag on 23 September 2012.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Struggling with a slight injury midway through the season, Babel managed to play a total of 16 league matches for Ajax, scoring four goals in total in the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a>. He also made four appearances in the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2012–13 UEFA Champions League">2012–13 UEFA Champions League</a>, as well as two more appearances in the <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_KNVB_Cup" title="2012–13 KNVB Cup">2012–13 KNVB Cup</a>, where he scored the second goal in the '15-minute of the second round fixture against <a href="/wiki/FC_Utrecht" title="FC Utrecht">FC Utrecht</a>, with the match ending 3–0 for the Amsterdam side prior to his injury. He would return to action after being sidelined to help his side secure their 3rd consecutive national title and 32nd overall.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h3><span id="Kas.C4.B1mpa.C5.9Fa"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Kasımpaşa">Kasımpaşa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Kasımpaşa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Opting not to extend his contract with Ajax in order to make way for the next generation,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Babel decided to join Turkish side <a href="/wiki/Kas%C4%B1mpa%C5%9Fa_S.K." title="Kasımpaşa S.K.">Kasımpaşa</a> where former Ajax player <a href="/wiki/Shota_Arveladze" title="Shota Arveladze">Shota Arveladze</a> was the current manager. He was joined in Turkey by fellow Amsterdam born defender <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Donk" title="Ryan Donk">Ryan Donk</a> who made the transfer from Belgian side <a href="/wiki/Club_Brugge" class="mw-redirect" title="Club Brugge">Club Brugge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Al_Ain">Al Ain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Al Ain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Babel signed a contract with <a href="/wiki/UAE_Arabian_Gulf_League" class="mw-redirect" title="UAE Arabian Gulf League">UAE Arabian Gulf League</a> champions <a href="/wiki/Al_Ain_FC" title="Al Ain FC">Al Ain</a> to replace the outgoing <a href="/wiki/Miroslav_Stoch" title="Miroslav Stoch">Miroslav Stoch</a>. He signed the contract after passing a medical test on 2 July 2015. In December 2015, Al Ain and Babel were involved in a conflict over Babel's disciplinary issues with the club's hierarchy and regarding his use of social media. The club demoted him to the reserves citing poor performance.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Be.C5.9Fikta.C5.9F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Beşiktaş">Beşiktaş</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Beşiktaş">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 2017, Babel joined <a href="/wiki/Be%C5%9Fikta%C5%9F_J.K." title="Beşiktaş J.K.">Beşiktaş</a> on a two-and-a-half-year deal.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Despite only joining the club in January, Babel became a crucial member of the squad that season, making 18 <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_S%C3%BCper_Lig" title="2016–17 Süper Lig">league</a> appearances and scoring 5 goals as he helped Beşiktaş secure their fifteenth league title.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fulham">Fulham</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Fulham">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 January 2019, Babel signed for Premier League club <a href="/wiki/Fulham_F.C." title="Fulham F.C.">Fulham</a> on a contract until the end of the <a href="/wiki/2018%E2%80%9319_Fulham_F.C._season" title="2018–19 Fulham F.C. season">2018–19 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Galatasaray">Galatasaray</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Galatasaray">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2019, Babel agreed to join <a href="/wiki/Galatasaray_S.K._(football)" title="Galatasaray S.K. (football)">Galatasaray</a> on a three-year deal.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Babel_Oranje.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Babel_Oranje.jpg/150px-Babel_Oranje.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="226" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Babel_Oranje.jpg/225px-Babel_Oranje.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Babel_Oranje.jpg/300px-Babel_Oranje.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1647" data-file-height="2476" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Babel_Oranje.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Babel playing for the Netherlands in August 2007</div></div></div>
<p>Babel took part in the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="2005 FIFA World Youth Championship">2005 FIFA World Youth Championship</a>. Babel scored two goals in four games as the Netherlands reached the quarter-finals. There they were defeated by Nigeria, 10–9 in a penalty shoot-out (Babel scored his penalty).<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Later that season, Babel made his international debut on 26 March 2005, away against <a href="/wiki/Romanian_national_football_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Romanian national football team">Romania</a>. He entered the game as a first-half substitute for <a href="/wiki/Arjen_Robben" title="Arjen Robben">Arjen Robben</a> and went on to score the second goal in a 2–0 victory.<sup id="cite_ref-fifa_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fifa-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The goal made Babel the youngest goalscorer in 68 years for the Netherlands<sup id="cite_ref-fifa_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fifa-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and the fourth-youngest of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-MARCO_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCO-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Babel had been a first team regular with Ajax in 2004–05 and finished with seven league goals in 22 appearances.
</p><p>In 2006, <a href="/wiki/Marco_van_Basten" title="Marco van Basten">Marco van Basten</a> included Babel in the Dutch squad for <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">2006 FIFA World Cup</a>. Due to a knee injury however,<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Babel only featured once, as a second-half substitute for <a href="/wiki/Ruud_van_Nistelrooy" title="Ruud van Nistelrooy">Ruud van Nistelrooy</a> in the group match against <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Van Basten has been quoted as saying Babel "has all the potential to become the next <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Henry" title="Thierry Henry">Thierry Henry</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-MARCO_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MARCO-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2007, Babel was part of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-21_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-21 football team">Netherlands under-21 team</a> competing in the <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-21_Football_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship">UEFA Under-21 Championship</a>, being held in the Netherlands. In the group stage, Babel scored a penalty against Portugal, helping the Dutch to secure a semi-final spot and thus qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">2008 Summer Olympics</a> in Beijing. His second tournament goal came during his <a href="/wiki/Man_of_the_match" class="mw-redirect" title="Man of the match">man of the match</a> performance in the final as the Netherlands defeated <a href="/wiki/Serbia_national_under-21_football_team" title="Serbia national under-21 football team">Serbia</a> 4–1 to retain their title.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2008, Babel was selected for the Dutch squad which would compete in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_2008" title="UEFA Euro 2008">UEFA Euro 2008</a> tournament. On 31 May, it was announced that Babel had been withdrawn from the squad after tearing ankle ligaments in training. Van Basten added then-<a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> defender <a href="/wiki/Khalid_Boulahrouz" title="Khalid Boulahrouz">Khalid Boulahrouz</a> to his squad in place of Babel.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_World_Cup">2010 World Cup</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2010 World Cup">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Babel was included in the preliminary squad for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a> in South Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> On 27 May 2010, Netherlands manager <a href="/wiki/Bert_van_Marwijk" title="Bert van Marwijk">Bert van Marwijk</a> announced that the player would be part of the final squad of 23 participating in the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Though the Netherlands reached <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="2010 FIFA World Cup Final">the final</a>, Babel did not appear in any of the matches during the tournament.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Euro_2012">Euro 2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Euro 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Babel returned in Oranje after an absence of almost a year on 11 November 2011 as a starter in the pre Euro 2012 0–0 drawn friendly against Switzerland.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2017-present">2017-present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ryan_Babel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2017-present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 September 2017, Babel was recalled to the Oranje squad after a six-year absence for the 2018 FIFA World Cup qualifiers against Sweden and Belarus.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> The Netherlands failed to qualify for the tournament; despite this, Babel was regularly called up for the upcoming inaugural <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Nations_League" title="UEFA Nations League">UEFA Nations League</a> campaign. He scored the equaliser in a 2-1 away defeat to France<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> in the Netherlands' first Nations League match.
</p>
