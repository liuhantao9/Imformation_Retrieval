<p><b>Otley</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> at a bridging point on the <a href="/wiki/River_Wharfe" title="River Wharfe">River Wharfe</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> a part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, the population was 13,668 at the 2011 census.<sup id="cite_ref-2011_census_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011_census-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is in two portions – south of the river is the historic town of Otley and to the north is <a href="/wiki/Newall,_West_Yorkshire" title="Newall, West Yorkshire">Newall</a>, which was formerly a separate township. The town is in lower <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale" title="Wharfedale">Wharfedale</a> on the <a href="/wiki/A660_road" title="A660 road">A660 road</a> which connects  it to <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>.
</p><p>The town sits in the <a href="/wiki/Otley_and_Yeadon_(ward)" title="Otley and Yeadon (ward)">Otley and Yeadon</a> ward of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_North_West_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Leeds North West (UK Parliament constituency)">Leeds North West</a> <a href="/wiki/UK_Parliament_constituencies" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Parliament constituencies">parliamentary constituency</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toponymy">Toponymy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Toponymy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Otley's name is derived from Otto, Otho, Othe, or Otta, a <a href="/wiki/Saxon" class="mw-redirect" title="Saxon">Saxon</a> personal name and <i>leah</i>, a woodland clearing in <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a>. It was recorded as <i>Ottanlege</i> in 972 and <i>Otelai</i> or <i>Othelia</i> in the <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i> of 1086.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tde_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tde-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The name <a href="/wiki/The_Chevin" title="The Chevin">Chevin</a> has close parallels to the early <a href="/wiki/Brittonic_languages" title="Brittonic languages">Brythonic</a> <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a> term <i>Cefn</i> meaning ridge and may be a survival of the ancient <a href="/wiki/Cumbric" title="Cumbric">Cumbric</a> language.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are pre-historic settlement finds alongside both sides of the River Wharfe and it is believed the valley has been settled at this site since the Bronze Age. There are Bronze Age carvings on rocks situated on top of The Chevin, one such example is the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.stone-circles.org.uk/stone/knottiesstone.htm">Knotties stone</a>. West Yorkshire Geology Trust has reference to Otley Chevin and Caley Crags having a rich history of human settlement stretching back into Palaeolithic times. Flint tools, Bronze Age rock carvings and Iron Age earthworks have been found. In medieval times the forest park was used as common pasture land, as a source of wood and sandstones for buildings and walls.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Saxon_and_Medieval">Saxon and Medieval</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Saxon and Medieval">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The majority of the early development of the town dates from <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon_settlement_of_Britain" title="Anglo-Saxon settlement of Britain">Saxon times</a> and was part of an extensive manor granted by King <a href="/wiki/Athelstan" class="mw-redirect" title="Athelstan">Athelstan</a> to the <a href="/wiki/See_of_York" class="mw-redirect" title="See of York">see of York</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Archbishops_of_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Archbishops of York">Archbishops of York</a> had a residence and were <a href="/wiki/Lords_of_the_manor" class="mw-redirect" title="Lords of the manor">lords of the manor</a>. Their palace was located on the site occupied by the Manor House.<sup id="cite_ref-tde_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tde-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Otley is close to <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and may have formed part of the kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Elmet" title="Elmet">Elmet</a>. Remains of the Archbishop's Palace were found during the construction of St Joseph's School.
</p><p>As in other areas of the north, the <a href="/wiki/Norman_conquest_of_England" title="Norman conquest of England">Norman conquest</a> largely laid waste this area.<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  The Saxon church was replaced by a Norman one, but this contains much Saxon sculpture.<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  Thus in the 11th and 12th century Otley would have been a loose congregation of buildings around the two focal points of the manor house by the bridge and the church.<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> An important reason for the town's location was a water supply, the Calhead Beck (now covered over) which ran down from Otley Chevin over Whitley Croft, a little East of the church and then to the river near the bridge.<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The town grew in the first half of the 13th century when the archbishops laid out <a href="/wiki/Burgage" title="Burgage">burgage</a> (freehold) plots to attract merchants and tradespeople. The burgage plots were on Boroughgate, Walkergate and Kirkgate. This began to create the layout of today,<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> based on a triangle of these plots forming the streets.<sup id="cite_ref-conserve_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conserve-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Bondgate was for the workers: bondsmen and tenants.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A leper hospital was founded on the road to <a href="/wiki/Harewood" title="Harewood">Harewood</a> beyond Cross Green.<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-conserve_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conserve-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As well as farming and use of woodland, important local activities were quarrying stone, and the manufacture of <a href="/wiki/Potash" title="Potash">potash</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bracken" title="Bracken">bracken</a>, used to make a soap which therefore supported a community carrying out <a href="/wiki/Fulling" title="Fulling">fulling</a>, the cleansing and finishing of woollen cloth on Watergate.<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  The Chevin provided stone for building (and millstones) as well as bracken, wood and common grazing, while the river provided reeds for <a href="/wiki/Thatching" title="Thatching">thatching</a> houses.<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_Revolution">Industrial Revolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Industrial Revolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Weir_on_the_River_Wharfe_at_Otley_-_geograph.org.uk_-_45975.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Weir_on_the_River_Wharfe_at_Otley_-_geograph.org.uk_-_45975.jpg/220px-Weir_on_the_River_Wharfe_at_Otley_-_geograph.org.uk_-_45975.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Weir_on_the_River_Wharfe_at_Otley_-_geograph.org.uk_-_45975.jpg/330px-Weir_on_the_River_Wharfe_at_Otley_-_geograph.org.uk_-_45975.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Weir_on_the_River_Wharfe_at_Otley_-_geograph.org.uk_-_45975.jpg/440px-Weir_on_the_River_Wharfe_at_Otley_-_geograph.org.uk_-_45975.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Weir_on_the_River_Wharfe_at_Otley_-_geograph.org.uk_-_45975.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Weir on the River Wharfe at Otley with Garnett's paper mill behind</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Woolen" title="Woolen">woollen</a> industry developed as a cottage industry but during the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a> and the mechanisation of the textile industry, mills were built using water then steam power. A cotton mill and weaving shed for <a href="/wiki/Calico_(textile)" class="mw-redirect" title="Calico (textile)">calicoes</a> were built by the river in the late 18th century. Later woolcombing and worsted spinning were introduced.<sup id="cite_ref-conserve_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conserve-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> By the mid 19th century 500 inhabitants were employed in two worsted-mills, a paper-mill, and other mills.<sup id="cite_ref-tde_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tde-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Tannery" class="mw-redirect" title="Tannery">tannery</a> was established in the 19th century.<sup id="cite_ref-conserve_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conserve-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> At this time the opening of the new Leeds Road and Bradford Road greatly increased access for trade.<sup id="cite_ref-OtleyM_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OtleyM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Many houses were built from the middle of the 19th century onwards, including the first row of terraces by the newly formed Otley Building Society from 1847.<sup id="cite_ref-OtleyM_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OtleyM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Otley_railway_station" title="Otley railway station">Otley railway station</a> opened in 1865 connecting goods and people to Leeds, with a connection to Bradford in 1875.  At its peak it had 50 trains a day, but it was closed in 1965 under the <a href="/wiki/Beeching_cuts" title="Beeching cuts">Beeching cuts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  Kirkgate was the first street to be paved in 1866, followed by sewers in 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-OtleyM_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OtleyM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Otley_Wharfdale_Press_sculpture_7_August_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Otley_Wharfdale_Press_sculpture_7_August_2017.jpg/220px-Otley_Wharfdale_Press_sculpture_7_August_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Otley_Wharfdale_Press_sculpture_7_August_2017.jpg/330px-Otley_Wharfdale_Press_sculpture_7_August_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Otley_Wharfdale_Press_sculpture_7_August_2017.jpg/440px-Otley_Wharfdale_Press_sculpture_7_August_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Otley_Wharfdale_Press_sculpture_7_August_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sculpture of Wharfedale Press in Wharfemeadows Park</div></div></div>
<p>The Wharfedale Printing Machine was developed in Otley by William Dawson and David Payne.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> An early example can be seen in <a href="/wiki/Otley_Museum" title="Otley Museum">Otley Museum</a>. By 1900 the printing machinery trade, with over 2,000 people employed in seven machine shops, was Otley's most important industry.<sup id="cite_ref-conserve_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conserve-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century_onwards">20th century onwards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 20th century onwards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First World War">First World War</a> there was a general shortage of housing in Britain, and much of it was crowded slums.  Otley Council prepared one of the first subsidized housing schemes, commencing with relatively open land in Newall on the North of the river in 1920.<sup id="cite_ref-OtleyM_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OtleyM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  The 1920s also saw the beginnings of the conversion of properties to a sewer drainage system, and electric lighting instead of gas on the streets.<sup id="cite_ref-OtleyM_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OtleyM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Further estates followed and by 1955 there were more than 1000 <a href="/wiki/Council_house" title="Council house">council houses</a>.  Private housing was also expanded during this time, but was greatly reduced the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>. House building revived in the 1960s to 1980s, but industry declined, with many factories closing, including the printing machine works in 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OtleyM_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OtleyM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Historically Otley was a market-town and the centre of a large <a href="/wiki/Ecclesiastical_parish" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecclesiastical parish">ecclesiastical parish</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Wapentake" class="mw-redirect" title="Wapentake">wapentakes</a> of <a href="/wiki/Skyrack" title="Skyrack">Skyrack</a> and <a href="/wiki/Claro_(wapentake)" class="mw-redirect" title="Claro (wapentake)">Claro</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>. The various <a href="/wiki/Chapelry" title="Chapelry">chapelries</a> and <a href="/wiki/Township_(England)" title="Township (England)">townships</a> in the ancient parish became separate civil parishes in 1866.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  The local authority was the lord of the manor until 1864 when Otley Board was formed and many public buildings date from then on.<sup id="cite_ref-conserve_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conserve-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> From 1894 Otley formed an <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">Urban District</a>, and in 1897 and 1903 expanded north of the River Wharfe to include <a href="/wiki/Newall,_West_Yorkshire" title="Newall, West Yorkshire">Newall</a>.  Since local government reorganisation in 1974 Otley has been a civil parish in the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a> of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a>, in the county of West Yorkshire.  The parish council has exercised its option to declare itself a <a href="/wiki/Town_councils_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Town councils in England">town council</a>.
</p><p>Otley lies in the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_North_West_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Leeds North West (UK Parliament constituency)">Leeds North West</a> constituency of the <a href="/wiki/UK_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Parliament">UK Parliament</a> and is represented by MP <a href="/wiki/Alex_Sobel" title="Alex Sobel">Alex Sobel</a> (<a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party (UK)</a>). It is part of the Otley &amp; Yeadon ward on Leeds City Council and is represented by three Liberal Democrat Councillors Ryk Downes, Colin Campbell &amp; Sandy Lay.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It is <a href="/wiki/Twin_towns_and_sister_cities" class="mw-redirect" title="Twin towns and sister cities">twinned</a> with the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> town of <a href="/wiki/Montereau-Fault-Yonne" title="Montereau-Fault-Yonne">Montereau-Fault-Yonne</a>, south of <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>.
</p><p>Otley and Wharfedale <a href="/wiki/Ward_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ward (politics)">ward</a> has a population of 24,000, and Otley itself has a population of 14,348, according to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">Census 2001</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Otley_overview_001.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Otley_overview_001.jpg/220px-Otley_overview_001.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Otley_overview_001.jpg/330px-Otley_overview_001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Otley_overview_001.jpg/440px-Otley_overview_001.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Otley_overview_001.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>View over Otley</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Otley_Bridge_01_7_August_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Otley_Bridge_01_7_August_2017.jpg/220px-Otley_Bridge_01_7_August_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Otley_Bridge_01_7_August_2017.jpg/330px-Otley_Bridge_01_7_August_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Otley_Bridge_01_7_August_2017.jpg/440px-Otley_Bridge_01_7_August_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Otley_Bridge_01_7_August_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Otley Bridge, viewed from the South and West side</div></div></div>
<p>Otley lies 28 miles (45&#160;km) south-west of York, 10 miles (16&#160;km) north-west of Leeds, 10 miles (16&#160;km) north-east of Bradford, and 196 miles (315&#160;km) from London. The town lies in lower <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale" title="Wharfedale">Wharfedale</a>, at a bridging point over the <a href="/wiki/River_Wharfe" title="River Wharfe">River Wharfe</a> where there is a seven-arched medieval bridge, and is surrounded by <a href="/wiki/Arable_land" title="Arable land">arable farmland</a>. The historic town developed on the south bank of the Wharfe, but in the 20th century Otley expanded north of the river, to include new developments at <a href="/wiki/Newall,_West_Yorkshire" title="Newall, West Yorkshire">Newall</a> and the Weston Estate.
</p><p>The south side of the valley is dominated by a <a href="/wiki/Gritstone" title="Gritstone">gritstone</a> <a href="/wiki/Escarpment" title="Escarpment">escarpment</a> overlooking Otley called <a href="/wiki/The_Chevin" title="The Chevin">the Chevin</a> and to the north is Newall Carr.<sup id="cite_ref-tde_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tde-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 1944, Major Le G.G.W. Horton Fawkes of <a href="/wiki/Farnley_Hall_(North_Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Farnley Hall (North Yorkshire)">Farnley Hall</a> donated 263 acres (1.1&#160;km²) of land on the Chevin to the people of Otley. This has been expanded to 700 acres (2.8&#160;km²) and constitutes <i>Chevin Forest Park</i>. It was from a quarry on the Chevin that the foundation stones for the <a href="/wiki/Houses_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Houses of Parliament">Houses of Parliament</a> were hewn.
</p><p>To the east and west of Otley are flooded gravel pits, where sand and gravel have been extracted in the 20th century. The gravel pits to the east at Knotford Nook are a noted <a href="/wiki/Birdwatching" title="Birdwatching">birdwatching</a> site. Those to the west are devoted to <a href="/wiki/Angling" title="Angling">angling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sailing" title="Sailing">sailing</a>.
</p><p>To the west are the villages of <a href="/wiki/Burley-in-Wharfedale" class="mw-redirect" title="Burley-in-Wharfedale">Burley-in-Wharfedale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Menston" title="Menston">Menston</a>. To the east is <a href="/wiki/Pool-in-Wharfedale" title="Pool-in-Wharfedale">Pool-in-Wharfedale</a>. To the south is the towns of <a href="/wiki/Guiseley" title="Guiseley">Guiseley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yeadon,_West_Yorkshire" title="Yeadon, West Yorkshire">Yeadon</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Roman_roads_in_Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman roads in Britain">Roman roads</a> bypassed Otley, South of the Chevin and North of Timble, so most of roads in the area were little better than tracks until the 18th century when efforts were made to facilitate trade.<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  By 1820 there was a regular <a href="/wiki/Post_coach" class="mw-redirect" title="Post coach">post coach</a> to Leeds on 4 days a week and 9 carriers delivering as far as <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>.  In 1840 and 1841 new roads to Leeds and Bradford were opened.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  From 1900 the first motor vehicles appeared in Otley, and in 1912 a motor haulage business started with a vehicle with interchangeable bodies so that it could work as either as lorry or <a href="/wiki/Charabanc" title="Charabanc">charabanc</a>.  By 1930 there were bus companies operating and in 1939 <a href="/wiki/Otley_bus_station" title="Otley bus station">Otley bus station</a> opened.  <a href="/wiki/Trolleybus" title="Trolleybus">Trolleybuses</a> operated by Leeds Corporation Tramways arrived in 1915, but ceased in 1928.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Otley_railway_station" title="Otley railway station">Otley railway station</a> opened in 1865 and closed in March 1965; the town bypass follows the line of the old railway.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The main roads through the town are the <a href="/wiki/A660_road" title="A660 road">A660</a> to the south east, which connects Otley to <a href="/wiki/Bramhope" title="Bramhope">Bramhope</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adel,_Leeds" title="Adel, Leeds">Adel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds city centre</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/A65_road" title="A65 road">A65</a> to the west, which goes to <a href="/wiki/Ilkley" title="Ilkley">Ilkley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Skipton" title="Skipton">Skipton</a>. The A6038 heads to <a href="/wiki/Guiseley" title="Guiseley">Guiseley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shipley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Shipley, West Yorkshire">Shipley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, connecting with the A65.  To <a href="/wiki/Harrogate" title="Harrogate">Harrogate</a>, the A659 heads east to the A658, which is the main Bradford–Harrogate road.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Otley_bus_station" title="Otley bus station">Otley bus station</a> is run by <a href="/wiki/Metro_(West_Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Metro (West Yorkshire)">West Yorkshire Metro</a> and services are operated by <a href="/wiki/First_West_Yorkshire" title="First West Yorkshire">First West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/TLC_Travel" title="TLC Travel">TLC Travel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keighley_Bus_Company" title="Keighley Bus Company">Keighley Bus Company</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Connexionsbuses" title="Connexionsbuses">Connexionsbuses</a>. There are local services connecting the town and outlying areas.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_features">Notable features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Notable features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Otley_Bridge_and_riverside">Otley Bridge and riverside</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Otley Bridge and riverside">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wharfemeadows_Park_along_7_August_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Wharfemeadows_Park_along_7_August_2017.jpg/220px-Wharfemeadows_Park_along_7_August_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Wharfemeadows_Park_along_7_August_2017.jpg/330px-Wharfemeadows_Park_along_7_August_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Wharfemeadows_Park_along_7_August_2017.jpg/440px-Wharfemeadows_Park_along_7_August_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wharfemeadows_Park_along_7_August_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Wharfemeadows Park</div></div></div>
<p>The bridge is a 7 span stone bridge and <a href="/wiki/Scheduled_Ancient_Monument" class="mw-redirect" title="Scheduled Ancient Monument">Scheduled Ancient Monument</a>, dating from 1228, which was rebuilt after the flood of 1673 and widened in 1776.<sup id="cite_ref-conserve_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conserve-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 1957 a concrete cantilevered footwalk was added to separate pedestrians from road traffic.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On the North side Eastwards, 2 miles of the riverbank is Wharfemeadows Park with extensive gardens on land donated to the town in 1924 by the Fawkes family.<sup id="cite_ref-conserve_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conserve-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Welcome_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welcome-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  It originally had an open air swimming pool.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Opposite on the south side is the much smaller Tittybottle park, originally designated Manor Park in 1909 but it acquired its popular (and now official) name for its popularity with mothers and nannies.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Welcome_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welcome-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  On the South side Westward, is Manor Garth Park, formerly part of the land of the manor house.<sup id="cite_ref-Welcome_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welcome-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  About 0.6 miles (1&#160;km) East of the bridge is Gallows Hill, where the medieval <a href="/wiki/Gallows" title="Gallows">gallows</a> stood.<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  Low-lying land by this was formerly a <a href="/wiki/Sewage_works" class="mw-redirect" title="Sewage works">sewage works</a>, but was bought by the Town Council in the 1980s and developed into Gallows Hill <a href="/wiki/Nature_reserve" title="Nature reserve">nature reserve</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Welcome_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Welcome-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Major_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Major-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Market_Place">Market Place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Market Place">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While markets have been held from at least 1227<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> it has only been in the current Market Place from about 1800.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It contains the <a href="/wiki/Buttercross" title="Buttercross">Buttercross</a> (covered area for farm produce, now used for occasional charity events, otherwise rest and shelter for visitors) and the Jubilee Clock, which was erected in 1888 at a cost of £175.  It has two plaques, one in memory to two locals killed in the <a href="/wiki/Transvaal_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Transvaal War">Transvaal War</a> and one expressing the gratitude of Belgian refugees who came to Otley during the <a href="/wiki/First_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="First World War">First World War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  Many of the buildings around are listed.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Otley_Stew_Oyster_7_August_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Otley_Stew_Oyster_7_August_2017.jpg/220px-Otley_Stew_Oyster_7_August_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="173" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Otley_Stew_Oyster_7_August_2017.jpg/330px-Otley_Stew_Oyster_7_August_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Otley_Stew_Oyster_7_August_2017.jpg/440px-Otley_Stew_Oyster_7_August_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3052" data-file-height="2400" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Otley_Stew_Oyster_7_August_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Old Prince Henry's Grammar School</div></div></div>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Grammar_school" title="Grammar school">grammar school</a> was founded by Royal Charter issued to Thomas Cave in 1607 by <a href="/wiki/James_VI_and_I" title="James VI and I">King James VI and I</a>, who named it "The Grammar School of Prince Henry".<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The single storey building was pulled down and rebuilt in the Elizabethan style with two storeys in 1840.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  It closed in 1878 and was used as a court-house<sup id="cite_ref-tde_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tde-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and in recent times has been commercial premises, currently a public house. In 1918 the foundation was re-established in temporary premises and in 1925 <a href="/wiki/Prince_Henry%27s_Grammar_School,_Otley" title="Prince Henry&#39;s Grammar School, Otley">Prince Henry's Grammar School, Otley</a>, in Farnley Lane opened.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Otley has a number of primary schools: Ashfield Primary School, Otley All Saints Primary School, The Whartons Primary School, Westgate Primary School and St. Joseph's Catholic Primary School. Closed schools include Thomas Chippendale Primary School (previously Weston Lane Middle School), All Saints CE Junior School, Ashfield Infant School, Newall Primary School, Otley <a href="/wiki/Secondary_modern" class="mw-redirect" title="Secondary modern">secondary modern</a> school and Otley <a href="/wiki/National_school_(England_and_Wales)" title="National school (England and Wales)">National School</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:All_Saints_Otley_exterior_01_7_August_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/All_Saints_Otley_exterior_01_7_August_2017.jpg/220px-All_Saints_Otley_exterior_01_7_August_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/All_Saints_Otley_exterior_01_7_August_2017.jpg/330px-All_Saints_Otley_exterior_01_7_August_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/All_Saints_Otley_exterior_01_7_August_2017.jpg/440px-All_Saints_Otley_exterior_01_7_August_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:All_Saints_Otley_exterior_01_7_August_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Church,_Otley" title="All Saints&#39; Church, Otley">All Saints Parish Church</a>.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Otley_Navvies_Monument_7_August_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Otley_Navvies_Monument_7_August_2017.jpg/220px-Otley_Navvies_Monument_7_August_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Otley_Navvies_Monument_7_August_2017.jpg/330px-Otley_Navvies_Monument_7_August_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Otley_Navvies_Monument_7_August_2017.jpg/440px-Otley_Navvies_Monument_7_August_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Otley_Navvies_Monument_7_August_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Navvies' Monument</div></div></div>
<p>Otley's first church was built in the early 7th century, made of wood, but was burnt down.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/All_Saints%27_Church,_Otley" title="All Saints&#39; Church, Otley">Parish Church (All Saints)</a> originates from Saxon times and contains the remains of two early <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxons" title="Anglo-Saxons">Anglo-Saxon</a> crosses, one of which has been reproduced for the town's war memorial.  The present building is based on a <a href="/wiki/Norman_architecture" title="Norman architecture">Norman</a> church from the 12th century but little of the original remains, except the north doorway.<sup id="cite_ref-tde_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tde-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gen_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gen-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Substantial changes were made in the 13th, 14th and 18th century, with the Tower Clock dating from 1793.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This church was the centre of an ancient ecclesiastical parish which comprised the chapelries of <a href="/wiki/Baildon" title="Baildon">Baildon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bramhope" title="Bramhope">Bramhope</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burley_in_Wharfedale" title="Burley in Wharfedale">Burley in Wharfedale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denton,_North_Yorkshire" title="Denton, North Yorkshire">Denton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Farnley,_North_Yorkshire" title="Farnley, North Yorkshire">Farnley</a>, and the townships of <a href="/wiki/Esholt" title="Esholt">Esholt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hawksworth,_Guiseley" title="Hawksworth, Guiseley">Hawksworth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lindley,_North_Yorkshire" title="Lindley, North Yorkshire">Lindley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Menston" title="Menston">Menston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newall_with_Clifton" title="Newall with Clifton">Newall with Clifton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pool-in-Wharfedale" title="Pool-in-Wharfedale">Pool-in-Wharfedale</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Little_Timble" class="mw-redirect" title="Little Timble">Little Timble</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Wood_5-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The graveyard contains the "Navvies' Monument", a replica of the entrance to <a href="/wiki/Bramhope_Tunnel" title="Bramhope Tunnel">Bramhope Tunnel</a>, a monument to those killed during its construction.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  Inside the church is the tomb of the grandparents of <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Fairfax,_3rd_Baron_Fairfax_of_Cameron" class="mw-redirect" title="Thomas Fairfax, 3rd Baron Fairfax of Cameron">Thomas Fairfax</a> who commanded Parliament's forces at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Marston_Moor" title="Battle of Marston Moor">Battle of Marston Moor</a> in 1644.
</p><p>What is now the Bridge Church was originally the Salem Chapel, built in 1826, being for many years the Congregational Church but having its present name from 1972 with the formation of the <a href="/wiki/United_Reformed_Church" title="United Reformed Church">United Reformed Church</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  Our Lady and All Saints Roman Catholic Church was opened in 1851.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Methodist" class="mw-redirect" title="Methodist">Methodist</a> preacher <a href="/wiki/John_Wesley" title="John Wesley">John Wesley</a> was a frequent visitor to the town in the 18th century.  Allegedly his horse died in the town and is buried in the grounds of the parish church.  Its grave is marked by an unusual stone, also known locally as the "Donkey Stone".  His Journal for 1761 reads, "6 July Monday; In the evening I preached at Otley and afterwards talked with many of the Society. There is reason to believe that ten or twelve of these are filled with the love of God." Wesley Street is named after him.  A chapel was established on  Walkergate in about 1800, replaced by a larger one on Westgate in 1857: a third Chapel (now Trinity Methodist Church) was built on Boroughate in 1876.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Primitive_Methodist" class="mw-redirect" title="Primitive Methodist">Primitive Methodist</a> Chapel opened on New Market in 1835, and is now the <a href="/wiki/Salvation_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Salvation Army">Salvation Army</a> Citadel: a Primitive Church on Station Road opened in 1874 and closed in 1965 (it is now residences).<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Another Primitive Church on Craven Street opened in 1901 and closed in the early 1950s. It is now the headquarters of 2nd Otley Scouts, and is known as the Chevin Community Centre.<sup id="cite_ref-OtleyMe_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OtleyMe-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>An 1890 <a href="/wiki/Quaker" class="mw-redirect" title="Quaker">Quaker</a> meeting house on Cross Green is now a <a href="/wiki/Gospel_Hall_Assemblies" title="Gospel Hall Assemblies">Gospel Hall</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Otleyco_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Otleyco-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> What was formerly Bethany Chapel on Myers Croft became home to Bethel Evangelical Church in the mid 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-Otleyco_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Otleyco-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Other Christian groups meet in members' homes or rented rooms.<sup id="cite_ref-Otleyco_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Otleyco-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture_and_community">Culture and community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Culture and community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:A_True_Yorkshireman.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/A_True_Yorkshireman.jpg/220px-A_True_Yorkshireman.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="174" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/A_True_Yorkshireman.jpg/330px-A_True_Yorkshireman.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/A_True_Yorkshireman.jpg/440px-A_True_Yorkshireman.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2267" data-file-height="1798" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:A_True_Yorkshireman.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Otley Show in 2009.</div></div></div>
<p>Otley has a diverse range of cultural organisations. It has five active <a href="/wiki/Morris_dance" title="Morris dance">Morris dance</a> sides, the Wharfedale Wayzgoose (<a href="/wiki/Border_Morris" title="Border Morris">Border</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The Buttercross Belles (Ladies Northwest),<sup id="cite_ref-Belles_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Belles-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Flash Company (Border, <a href="/wiki/Molly_dance" title="Molly dance">Molly</a>, Appalachian &amp; <a href="/wiki/Clogging" title="Clogging">Clog</a>), Hellz Bellz (Contemporary) and Kitchen Taps (Appalachian Step).
</p><p>Drama groups include the Otley Community Players, Otley Youth Theatre (OY), and a thriving arts centre in the former courthouse. There is a poetry society, which  meets monthly in the Black Horse Hotel. The town has a <a href="/wiki/Brass_Band" class="mw-redirect" title="Brass Band">Brass Band</a><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> who perform at many events in the town. It is not a regular contesting band, but won first prize in the unregistered section at their first contest at <a href="/wiki/Hardraw_Scar" class="mw-redirect" title="Hardraw Scar">Hardraw Scar</a> in September 2007 and again in 2008. Since then they have competed in the 1st to 3rd section winning Second prize and Best March in 2014.
</p><p>There are regular music performances in pubs encouraging the many talented musicians that live in Otley. Many community events are held at the Woolpack Music Studios—home to the world famous Otley Ukulele Orchestra.
</p><p>Otley hosts the annual Otley <a href="/wiki/List_of_folk_festivals" title="List of folk festivals">Folk Festival</a> in September, a <a href="/wiki/Victorian_architecture" title="Victorian architecture">Victorian</a> Fayre in December, a carnival in June, and, in May, what is reputed to be the oldest one day <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_show" title="Agricultural show">agricultural show</a> in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This celebrated its bicentenary in 2009. There is a <a href="/wiki/Beer_festival" title="Beer festival">beer festival</a>, organised by the church, in November.
Otley has four <a href="/wiki/Scouting" title="Scouting">Scout</a> troops, Otley Parish, Otley Bridge, 2nd Otley, and Otley Methodist Scouts.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Army_Cadet_Force" title="Army Cadet Force">Army Cadet Force</a> detachment is also located in the town.
</p><p>In January 2013 <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> newspaper featured an article in its Weekend section entitled <i>Let's move to Otley, West Yorkshire</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hostelries">Hostelries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Hostelries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Black Horse Hotel (original demolished, current from 1901<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and the Royal White Horse Hotel (the former Barclays Bank (closed 2019), in Manor Square<sup id="cite_ref-OldOtley_7-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OldOtley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>) were the original <a href="/wiki/Post_office#Name" title="Post office">posting houses</a> and many of the others were <a href="/wiki/Coaching_inns" class="mw-redirect" title="Coaching inns">coaching inns</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OtleyM_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OtleyM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  By 1900 there were over 30 inns, and Otley was said to have "a pub on every corner".<sup id="cite_ref-OtleyM_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OtleyM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  This reputation has continued into recent years with BBC Radio 4's statistics programme "More or Less" concluding that it had the greatest number per head of population.<sup id="cite_ref-MoreOrLess_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MoreOrLess-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Today there are 20 <a href="/wiki/Pub" title="Pub">pubs</a> in the town although the <i>Roebuck</i> (formerly known as the <i>Spite</i>), the <i>Chevin</i> and the <i>Royalty</i> are on the outskirts, with the <i>Roebuck</i> located in <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PubClub_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PubClub-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Some of the oldest buildings have been demolished or replaced, but the Red Lion on Kirkgate dates from 1745, the Bowling Green from 1757 (originally a courthouse), the Rose &amp; Crown (originally cottages) 1731.<sup id="cite_ref-OtleyM_8-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OtleyM-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  The Old Grammar School is (as of 2017) the Stew and Oyster pub. The Old Cock on Crossgate (despite its name) has only recently become a pub, but inhabits former cottages from 1757.<sup id="cite_ref-BLBOldCock_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BLBOldCock-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>  These are all Grade II listed buildings.
</p><p>The <i>Black Bull</i> in the Market Place, was allegedly drunk dry by Cromwell's troops on the night before the battle of Marston Moor during the <a href="/wiki/English_Civil_War" title="English Civil War">English Civil War</a> and has a 15th-century well in the beer garden.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Filmography">Filmography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Filmography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Otley is "Hotton" in the <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> television <a href="/wiki/Soap_opera" title="Soap opera">soap opera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Emmerdale" title="Emmerdale">Emmerdale</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and  appears in <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Heartbeat_(UK_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Heartbeat (UK TV series)">Heartbeat</a></i> where Otley Courthouse is the old Police Station.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> ITV's <i><a href="/wiki/DCI_Banks" title="DCI Banks">DCI Banks </a></i> also regularly filmed in the town.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Otley was also the setting for the drama series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chase_(2006_TV_series)" title="The Chase (2006 TV series)">The Chase</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> and the ITV dramatisation of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bad_Mother%27s_Handbook" title="The Bad Mother&#39;s Handbook">The Bad Mother's Handbook</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Otley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Otley Angling Club was formed in 1897 by local land owners. It controls the fishing on the River Wharfe through Otley and a coarse fishing pond on the outskirts of the town. It runs regular fishing matches on the river and junior matches on the club pond.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Otley Athletic Club meets at Otley Cricket Club.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Otley_Cricket_Club" title="Otley Cricket Club">Otley Cricket Club</a>, founded in 1820, play in the Airedale and Wharfedale Senior Cricket League. The club has won the league title 13 times and shared it in 1966. The club has three Senior teams and provides facilities for Juniors from Under 9 to Under 17. The Club play at <a href="/wiki/Cross_Green" title="Cross Green">Cross Green</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cross_Green_rugby_ground,_Otley.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Cross_Green_rugby_ground%2C_Otley.jpg/220px-Cross_Green_rugby_ground%2C_Otley.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Cross_Green_rugby_ground%2C_Otley.jpg/330px-Cross_Green_rugby_ground%2C_Otley.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Cross_Green_rugby_ground%2C_Otley.jpg/440px-Cross_Green_rugby_ground%2C_Otley.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cross_Green_rugby_ground,_Otley.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Cross_Green_rugby_ground" class="mw-redirect" title="Cross Green rugby ground">Cross Green rugby ground</a>, the home ground of <a href="/wiki/Otley_R.U.F.C." title="Otley R.U.F.C.">Otley R.U.F.C.</a></div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Otley_R.U.F.C." title="Otley R.U.F.C.">Otley R.U.F.C.</a> play home matches at Cross Green,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> which was the venue for the Italy v USA fixture in the 1991 <a href="/wiki/Rugby_World_Cup" title="Rugby World Cup">Rugby World Cup</a>. In 1979 Cross Green was the site of a victory by the North of England against the <a href="/wiki/All_Blacks" class="mw-redirect" title="All Blacks">All Blacks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Otley R.U.F.C. finished 5th in <a href="/wiki/National_Division_One" class="mw-redirect" title="National Division One">National Division One</a> in both the 2003-4 and 2004-05 seasons but were relegated to <a href="/wiki/National_Division_Two" class="mw-redirect" title="National Division Two">National Division Two</a> at the end of the 2006–07 season. The club won National Division Two in 2007-08 and returned to National Division One for the 2008–09 season.
</p><p>Otley Town Football Club has teams in the Premier division of the West Yorkshire League, the Premier division of the Harrogate &amp; District League and the reserves division of the West Yorkshire League. It runs junior teams including two girls' teams. The club is a Charter Standard football club run by volunteers. The Sunday League team, Otley Wharfeside AFC, play in the Wharfedale Triangle Football League.
</p><p>Otley Cycle Club was founded on 27 January 1927.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Its patron is <a href="/wiki/Lizzie_Armitstead" class="mw-redirect" title="Lizzie Armitstead">Lizzie Armitstead</a>, an international champion cyclist who was born in the town. It meets regularly and hosts a number of races throughout the year.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 5 July 2014, the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France" title="Tour de France">Tour de France</a> Stage 1 from Leeds to Harrogate passed through the town.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On 3 May 2015, the final stage of the first <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_Yorkshire" title="Tour de Yorkshire">Tour de Yorkshire</a> came through the outskirts of Otley. On 30 April 2016, Otley was the start of the second stage of the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_Yorkshire" title="Tour de Yorkshire">Tour de Yorkshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Chippendale_Born_Here_Otley_7_August_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Chippendale_Born_Here_Otley_7_August_2017.jpg/220px-Chippendale_Born_Here_Otley_7_August_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Chippendale_Born_Here_Otley_7_August_2017.jpg/330px-Chippendale_Born_Here_Otley_7_August_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Chippendale_Born_Here_Otley_7_August_2017.jpg/440px-Chippendale_Born_Here_Otley_7_August_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Chippendale_Born_Here_Otley_7_August_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Thomas Chippendale was born in a cottage which formerly stood here</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Thomas_Chippendale" title="Thomas Chippendale">Thomas Chippendale</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Cabinetmaker" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabinetmaker">cabinetmaker</a>, was born in a cottage at the junction of Boroughgate and Wesley Street<sup id="cite_ref-conserve_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conserve-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and his statue stands next to the old <a href="/wiki/Grammar_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammar School">Grammar School</a> that he once attended in Manor Square. <a href="/wiki/J.M.W._Turner" class="mw-redirect" title="J.M.W. Turner">J.M.W. Turner</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Painting" title="Painting">painter</a>, visited Otley in 1797, aged 22, when commissioned to paint watercolours of the area. He was so attracted to Otley and the surrounding area that he returned time and time again. His friendship with Walter Ramsden Fawkes made him a regular visitor to <a href="/wiki/Farnley_Hall_(North_Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Farnley Hall (North Yorkshire)">Farnley Hall</a>, two miles from Otley. The stormy backdrop of Hannibal Crossing The Alps is reputed to have been inspired by a storm over Otley's <a href="/wiki/The_Chevin" title="The Chevin">Chevin</a> while Turner was staying at Farnley Hall.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
