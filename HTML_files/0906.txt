<p><b>Patricia Helen Kennedy Lawford</b> (May 6, 1924 – September 17, 2006) was an American socialite and the sixth of nine children of <a href="/wiki/Rose_Kennedy" title="Rose Kennedy">Rose</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr." title="Joseph P. Kennedy Sr.">Joseph P. Kennedy Sr.</a> She was a sister of President <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>, Senator <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert F. Kennedy</a>, and Senator <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Ted Kennedy</a>. Pat wanted to be a film producer, a profession not readily open to young women in her time. She married English actor <a href="/wiki/Peter_Lawford" title="Peter Lawford">Peter Lawford</a> in 1954, but they divorced in 1966.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Patricia_Kennedy_Lawford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Patricia Helen Kennedy was born in <a href="/wiki/Brookline,_Massachusetts" title="Brookline, Massachusetts">Brookline, Massachusetts</a>. She attended Roehampton Sacred Heart Convent School in London and Maplehurst Sacred Heart Convent School in <a href="/wiki/Bronxville,_New_York" title="Bronxville, New York">Bronxville, New York</a>. In 1945, she received a bachelor of arts degree from <a href="/wiki/Rosemont_College" title="Rosemont College">Rosemont College</a>, where she was active in both directing and acting in theatrical productions.<sup id="cite_ref-jfk_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jfk-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She was considered the most sophisticated, yet also the most introverted, of her parents' five daughters. Since childhood she had a fascination with travel and <a href="/wiki/Classical_Hollywood_cinema" title="Classical Hollywood cinema">Hollywood</a>. In time she would become a world traveler, so much so that as a young girl she was given assignments by the independent and foreign press to write of her travels. Her ongoing fascination with Hollywood was fueled by her father's stories and adventures there as a movie mogul heading <a href="/wiki/RKO_Pictures" title="RKO Pictures">RKO Pictures</a>. After graduating from Rosemont College, she moved to Hollywood in hopes of becoming a movie producer and director like her father.
</p><p>Her father apparently believed that she could do as much, once saying, "Pat is the one with head for business. She could really run this town if she put her mind to it."<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She worked as a production assistant on patriotic and religious productions such as singer <a href="/wiki/Kate_Smith" title="Kate Smith">Kate Smith</a>'s radio program and Father Peyton's <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Rosary_Crusade" title="Family Rosary Crusade">Family Rosary Crusade</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-jfk_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jfk-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> When she was 22 years old, Lawford was the producer of <i><a href="/wiki/I_Love_to_Eat" title="I Love to Eat">I Love to Eat</a></i>, on <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>-TV; it was the first cooking program on network television.<sup id="cite_ref-rbf_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rbf-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Patricia_Kennedy_Lawford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She met English actor <a href="/wiki/Peter_Lawford" title="Peter Lawford">Peter Lawford</a> through her sister <a href="/wiki/Eunice_Kennedy_Shriver" title="Eunice Kennedy Shriver">Eunice</a> in the 1940s. They met again in 1949, and again in 1953. They courted briefly and officially announced their engagement in February 1954. They married on April 24, 1954,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;note 1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-jfk_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jfk-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> of St. Thomas More in New York City, twelve days before her thirtieth birthday. They settled in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">Santa Monica, California</a>, and often socialized with actress <a href="/wiki/Judy_Garland" title="Judy Garland">Judy Garland</a> and her family. Garland gave birth to her son Joseph at the same hospital and on the same day Kennedy gave birth to her son Christopher.
</p><p>The couple had four children, all born in <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">Santa Monica, California</a>: <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Lawford" title="Christopher Lawford">Christopher Lawford</a> (1955-2018), Sydney Maleia Lawford (b. 1956), Victoria Francis Lawford (b. 1958), and Robin Elizabeth Lawford (b. 1961).
</p><p>Despite the glamorous persona Lawford presented, their relationship suffered strains as early as their brief engagement. Lawford had difficulty adjusting to Kennedy's steadfast Catholicism and her family's larger-than-life image. Kennedy could not tolerate Lawford's heavy drinking, extramarital affairs, and gradual addiction to drugs. Shortly after <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_John_F._Kennedy" title="Assassination of John F. Kennedy">her brother Jack's death</a>, she filed for a legal separation, and the couple was officially divorced in February 1966. She never remarried.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_years">Later years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Patricia_Kennedy_Lawford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:KennedyGorbi.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/KennedyGorbi.jpg/220px-KennedyGorbi.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/KennedyGorbi.jpg/330px-KennedyGorbi.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/KennedyGorbi.jpg/440px-KennedyGorbi.jpg 2x" data-file-width="565" data-file-height="370" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:KennedyGorbi.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>With Lawford (far right) are Senator <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Ted Kennedy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Kennedy_Smith" title="Jean Kennedy Smith">Jean Kennedy Smith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Raisa_Gorbachyova" class="mw-redirect" title="Raisa Gorbachyova">Raisa Gorbachyova</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev" title="Mikhail Gorbachev">Mikhail Gorbachev</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>After her divorce, Kennedy battled alcoholism and suffered from <a href="/wiki/Tongue_cancer" class="mw-redirect" title="Tongue cancer">tongue cancer</a>. She worked with the <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Library" class="mw-redirect" title="John F. Kennedy Library">John F. Kennedy Library and Museum</a>, as well as with the National Center on Addiction, and was a founder of the National Committee for the Literary Arts, for which she arranged a series of author lectures and scholarships.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Patricia_Kennedy_Lawford&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kennedy died of <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a> at age of 82 on September 17, 2006 in her <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> home.  She was buried in <a href="/wiki/Southampton_Cemetery" title="Southampton Cemetery">Southampton Cemetery</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
