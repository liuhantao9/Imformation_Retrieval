<p><i><b>Some Hearts</b></i> is the debut studio album by American singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, released in the United States on November 15, 2005 by <a href="/wiki/Arista_Nashville" title="Arista Nashville">Arista Nashville</a>. The album contains the number one country singles "<a href="/wiki/Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel" title="Jesus, Take the Wheel">Jesus, Take the Wheel</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Forget_to_Remember_Me" title="Don&#39;t Forget to Remember Me">Don't Forget to Remember Me</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Wasted_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Wasted (Carrie Underwood song)">Wasted</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Before_He_Cheats" title="Before He Cheats">Before He Cheats</a>". The North American version contains the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> number one single, "<a href="/wiki/Inside_Your_Heaven" title="Inside Your Heaven">Inside Your Heaven</a>," as a bonus track.
</p><p>Bolstered by the success of its singles, <i>Some Hearts</i> became the best-selling album of 2006 in all genres in the United States. The album was also the best-selling country album in the United States of both 2006 and 2007, making Underwood the first female artist in <i>Billboard</i> history to earn back-to-back honors for Top Country Album. Additionally, it was the best-selling female country album of 2005, 2006 and 2007. <i>Some Hearts</i> has since been certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_Certification" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA Certification">8x platinum</a>  by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA), and is the fastest-selling debut country album in the history of the <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a> era, the best-selling solo female debut album in country music history, the best-selling country album of the last 10 years, and the best-selling album by an <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> alumni in the U.S.
</p><p>It was listed as one of the 100 best-selling albums of all time by the RIAA in 2009. <i>Some Hearts</i> has since sold over 7.45 million copies in the U.S. and over nine million worldwide. In December 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> announced that the album was the biggest-selling country album of the decade, as well as the fourteenth biggest-selling album of any genre.
</p><p>The album and its songs were praised by music critics. It led Underwood to win three <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Awards</a>: <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_New_Artist" title="Grammy Award for Best New Artist">Best New Artist</a> in <a href="/wiki/49th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="49th Grammy Awards">2007</a> and twice <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Female_Country_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance">Best Female Country Vocal Performance</a> — for "Jesus, Take the Wheel", in 2007, and for "Before He Cheats", in <a href="/wiki/50th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="50th Grammy Awards">2008</a>. Additionally, <i>Some Hearts</i> won Album of the Year at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music Awards</a>, while "Jesus, Take the Wheel" and "Before He Cheats" both won Single of the Year at the 2006 Academy of Country Music Awards and 2007 <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a>, respectively.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Some_Hearts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Carrie Underwood's <i>American Idol</i> winning single "<a href="/wiki/Inside_Your_Heaven" title="Inside Your Heaven">Inside Your Heaven</a>", which debuted at number one on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 five months before the release of <i>Some Hearts</i>, is included on the album as a bonus track. The song is Underwood's only number 1 on the Hot 100. The song has sold 880,000 copies.
</p><p>The first single from the album, "<a href="/wiki/Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel" title="Jesus, Take the Wheel">Jesus, Take the Wheel</a>", was No. 1 on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart for six consecutive weeks and reached No. 20 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. In August 2008, "Jesus, Take the Wheel" was reported to have been sold more than 1 million ringtones and was certified Platinum, the first achievement by a country artist for two  Platinum Mastertone ranked songs. As of February 2016, it has sold 2,473,000 copies in the United States and has been certified 3xPlatinum by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>.
</p><p>"Some Hearts", written by <a href="/wiki/Diane_Warren" title="Diane Warren">Diane Warren</a> and originally covered by <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Crenshaw" title="Marshall Crenshaw">Marshall Crenshaw</a> in 1989, was released to <a href="/wiki/Pop_radio" class="mw-redirect" title="Pop radio">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adult_contemporary" class="mw-redirect" title="Adult contemporary">adult contemporary</a> radio only and eventually reached number 12 on the <a href="/wiki/Adult_Contemporary_(Billboard_chart)" class="mw-redirect" title="Adult Contemporary (Billboard chart)">Adult Contemporary chart</a>. As of June 2011 it has sold 207,000 copies.
</p><p>"<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Forget_to_Remember_Me" title="Don&#39;t Forget to Remember Me">Don't Forget to Remember Me</a>" was the third single from the album, and also proved successful, reaching number one on the <i><a href="/wiki/Mediabase" title="Mediabase">Mediabase</a></i> Country Chart, number two on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot Country Songs chart, and number 49 on the Hot 100. The song has sold 403,000 copies in the United States and has been certified Gold by the RIAA as of August 12, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"<a href="/wiki/Before_He_Cheats" title="Before He Cheats">Before He Cheats</a>" was released as the next single, and climbed the charts faster than any of Underwood's other singles, making it into the top twenty of the Hot Country Songs chart before the release of a music video. "Before He Cheats" subsequently peaked at number one on the Hot Country Songs chart for five consecutive weeks, and at number eight on the Hot 100; it proved to be a huge <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Crossover (music)">crossover</a> hit, making appearances in the top 20 of every chart on which it appeared, and it is the biggest hit overall from the album. When it finally peaked at number 8 in May 2007, it had already logged 38 weeks on the chart, making it the longest trek to the top 10 ever. As pop airplay began dying down, the song got a third life on the adult contemporary format, which began playing the song in May. "Before He Cheats" spent 64 consecutive weeks on the Hot 100, before finally falling off in late November 2007. The song is the fifth-longest charting single on the Hot 100, following LeAnn Rimes's "<a href="/wiki/How_Do_I_Live" title="How Do I Live">How Do I Live</a>", Jewel's "<a href="/wiki/You_Were_Meant_for_Me_(Jewel_song)" title="You Were Meant for Me (Jewel song)">You Were Meant for Me</a>"/"<a href="/wiki/Foolish_Games" title="Foolish Games">Foolish Games</a>", <a href="/wiki/Adele" title="Adele">Adele</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Rolling_in_the_Deep" title="Rolling in the Deep">Rolling in the Deep</a>", and Jason Mraz's "<a href="/wiki/I%27m_Yours_(Jason_Mraz_song)" title="I&#39;m Yours (Jason Mraz song)">I'm Yours</a>". It is the second longest running hit so far this decade."Before He Cheats" reached No. 6 on the 2007 Hot 100 Year-End Chart and fifth on the 2007 Hot 100 Airplay Year-End Chart by <i>Billboard</i> magazine. It reached No. 10 on the 2007 Adult Contemporary Year-End Chart. It was named the 2007 Single of the Year by the Country Music Association. It is Underwood's best selling single and had sold 4,312,000 digital downloads as of August 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> making it the third best-selling country song of all time behind Lady Antebellum's "<a href="/wiki/Need_You_Now_(Lady_Antebellum_song)" title="Need You Now (Lady Antebellum song)">Need You Now</a>", with 4.97 million and Taylor Swift's "<a href="/wiki/Love_Story_(Taylor_Swift_song)" title="Love Story (Taylor Swift song)">Love Story</a>", with 4.96 million. It is the third best-selling song by an <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> contestant in the United States (behind <a href="/wiki/Phillip_Phillips" title="Phillip Phillips">Phillip Phillips</a>' <a href="/wiki/Home_(Phillip_Phillips_song)" title="Home (Phillip Phillips song)">Home</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Clarkson" title="Kelly Clarkson">Kelly Clarkson</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Stronger_(What_Doesn%27t_Kill_You)" title="Stronger (What Doesn&#39;t Kill You)">Stronger (What Doesn't Kill You)</a>"), and the fourth longest-charting single in the history of the Hot 100. It also came in on CMT's 40 Greatest Songs of the Decade at number twenty-five, along with Underwood's other signature hit, "Jesus, Take the Wheel", it reached No. 4. It has been certified 5xPlatinum by the RIAA.
</p><p>"<a href="/wiki/Wasted_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Wasted (Carrie Underwood song)">Wasted</a>" also proved another successful hit for Underwood by peaking at number one for three consecutive weeks on the Hot Country Songs chart, and going to number 37 on the Hot 100. The song has sold 705,000 copies in the United States and has been certified Platinum in August 2015.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Dann_Huff" title="Dann Huff">Dann Huff</a> produced tracks 3, 6, 8, 9, 11, and 12, <a href="/wiki/Desmond_Child" title="Desmond Child">Desmond Child</a> produced "Inside Your Heaven", and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Bright_(record_producer)" title="Mark Bright (record producer)">Mark Bright</a> produced the rest.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Some_Hearts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>To promote <i>Some Hearts</i>, Underwood embarked on her very first solo tour, which was called <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood:_Live_2006" class="mw-redirect" title="Carrie Underwood: Live 2006">Carrie Underwood: Live 2006</a>. The tour started April 4, 2006 and ended November 30 of the same year.

<p>The album received positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>. Penny Rondinella of <a href="/wiki/About.com" class="mw-redirect" title="About.com">About.com</a> gave a positive review to the album, awarding it with a four-and-a-half stars out of five and said: "American Idol Season 4 winner Carrie Underwood's debut album is just as expected, a country feel with some pop added to it. Lots of variety in her vocals, which tells me she has true talent."  <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> gave it 4 out of 5 stars and classified the album as "anthemic country pop, ideal for either country or adult contemporary radio, with none of the delightful tackiness of <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain" title="Shania Twain">Shania Twain</a>—and her debut album, Some Hearts, not only hits this mark exactly, it's better than either album Hill has released since Breathe in 1999." It also praised Underwood's vocal performance and found that "she sounds equally convincing on such sentimental fare as 'Jesus, Take the Wheel' as on the soaring pop 'Some Hearts,' and even if she doesn't exactly sound tough on the strutting 'Before He Cheats,' she does growl with a fair amount of passion."
</p><p><i>Some Hearts</i> won Album of the Year at the 2007 <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music Awards</a>, while "Jesus, Take the Wheel" and "Before He Cheats" both won Single of the Year at the 2006 Academy of Country Music Awards and 2007 <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a>, respectively. At the <a href="/wiki/49th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="49th Grammy Awards">49th Grammy Awards</a>, in 2007, Underwood won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_New_Artist" title="Grammy Award for Best New Artist">Grammy Award for Best New Artist</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Female_Country_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance">Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance</a>, for "Jesus, Take the Wheel", which also won <a href="/wiki/Best_Country_Song" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Country Song">Best Country Song</a> for its songwriters, being nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Song_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Song of the Year">Song of the Year</a> as well. The following year, at the <a href="/wiki/50th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="50th Grammy Awards">50th Grammy Awards</a>, Underwood won the Grammy for Best Female Country Vocal Performance for "Before He Cheats". The song won Best Country Song for the songwriters as well as being nominated for Song of the Year.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Some_Hearts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Some Hearts</i> debuted at number two on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, behind <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Confessions_on_a_Dance_Floor" title="Confessions on a Dance Floor">Confessions on a Dance Floor</a></i> and number one on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Country_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Country Albums"><i>Billboard</i> Top Country Albums</a> selling 315,000 copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The large first week sales of <i>Some Hearts</i> made it the biggest debut of any country artist since the advent of the <a href="/wiki/SoundScan" class="mw-redirect" title="SoundScan">SoundScan</a> system in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It was also the fifth best first-week sales for any <i>American Idol</i> contestant (behind Ruben Studdard's <i><a href="/wiki/Soulful_(Ruben_Studdard_album)" title="Soulful (Ruben Studdard album)">Soulful</a></i>, her own <i><a href="/wiki/Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" title="Play On (Carrie Underwood album)">Play On</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Carnival_Ride" title="Carnival Ride">Carnival Ride</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Clay_Aiken" title="Clay Aiken">Clay Aiken</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Measure_of_a_Man_(Clay_Aiken_album)" title="Measure of a Man (Clay Aiken album)">Measure of a Man</a></i>, which sold over 417,000 copies, 318,000 copies, 527,000 copies and 613,000 copies, respectively, in their first weeks). In the week of December 25, 2006, the album's fifty-eighth week on the chart, the album rose back up to number four on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 chart after selling close to 300,000 copies.
</p><p><i>Some Hearts</i> became the best-selling album of 2006 in all genres in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The album was also the best-selling country album in the United States of both 2006 and 2007, making Underwood the first female artist in <i>Billboard</i> history to earn back-to-back honors for Top Country Album.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, it was the best-selling female country album of 2005, 2006 and 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> 200, the album had a run of 137 consecutive weeks on the chart, the second-longest for any album released in 2005, behind <a href="/wiki/Nickelback" title="Nickelback">Nickelback</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/All_the_Right_Reasons" title="All the Right Reasons">All the Right Reasons</a></i>, making it the sixth album with the most weeks spent on such a chart since 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The album spent 10 weeks within the Top 5 of the <i>Billboard</i> 200, a record among American Idol alumni.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On the week dated December 12, 2009, under the changes of <i>Billboard'</i>s rules, the album re-entered the <i>Billboard</i> 200, at number 132.
The album appeared on the <i>Billboard</i> Year-End Chart for 2009, coming in at number 158 for album sales that year.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On the week dated for August 7, 2010, the album climbed back onto the <i>Billboard</i> 200, reaching No. 191.  On the week dated for December 5, 2010, the album once again landed onto the <i>Billboard</i> 200, reaching No. 192.  On the week dated for December 8, 2012, the album once again landed onto the <i>Billboard</i> 200, reaching No. 69.
</p><p><i>Some Hearts</i> has since been certified eight times Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and is the fastest-selling debut country album in the history of the <a href="/wiki/SoundScan" class="mw-redirect" title="SoundScan">SoundScan</a> era,<sup id="cite_ref-cmt20070110_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cmt20070110-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cst_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cst-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> the best-selling solo female debut album in country music history,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> the best-selling Country album of the last 10 years,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and the best-selling album by an American Idol alumni in the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> As of December 2015, it has sold over 7,450,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-USsales_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USsales-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On October 24, 2016, the album was certified 8x Platinum by the RIAA.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By December 2007, <i>Some Hearts</i> had sold over 7 million copies worldwide, according to <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> It has now sold over 9 million copies worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> announced that the album was the biggest-selling country album of the 2000s decade, as well as the fourteenth biggest-selling album of any genre.<sup id="cite_ref-DecadeEnd_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DecadeEnd-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
On August 6, 2016 issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>, <i>Some Hearts</i> reached No. 7 on the <i>All-time Country Albums</i> chart. On <i>All-time Country Songs</i> chart, the album's fourth single, "Before He Cheats", reached No. 64.
</p>
