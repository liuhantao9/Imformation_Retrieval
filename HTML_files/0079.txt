<p>"<b>Two Black Cadillacs</b>" is a song by American recording artist <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, taken from her fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(album)" title="Blown Away (album)">Blown Away</a>.</i> The song served as the album's third single on November 18, 2012, through <a href="/wiki/Arista_Nashville" title="Arista Nashville">Arista Nashville</a>. Written by Underwood, <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Kear" title="Josh Kear">Josh Kear</a>, "Two Black Cadillacs" is a <a href="/wiki/Country_pop" title="Country pop">country pop</a> song with lyrics addressing the story of two women who, when they realize they are both involved with the same man, set aside their differences, take matters into their own hands and decide to kill him. It was produced by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Bright_(record_producer)" title="Mark Bright (record producer)">Mark Bright</a>, and the instrumentation was compared to songs by <a href="/wiki/Dixie_Chicks" title="Dixie Chicks">Dixie Chicks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Lambert" title="Miranda Lambert">Miranda Lambert</a>.
</p><p>Upon its release, "Two Black Cadillacs" was met with positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>, who praised its story and Underwood's versatility as an artist. Commercially, the song was successful. In the United States, it reached number two on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart, and also reached number 41 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a>. "Two Black Cadillacs" was certified Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA), denoting sales of over one million units in the country. The song also charted in Canada at number three on the <a href="/wiki/Canada_Country" class="mw-redirect" title="Canada Country">Country</a> chart and at number 52 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a>.
</p><p>The accompanying music video was directed by <a href="/wiki/P.R._Brown" class="mw-redirect" title="P.R. Brown">P.R. Brown</a>, and filmed in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>. The video draws inspiration from <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/Christine_(novel)" title="Christine (novel)">Christine</a>,</i> and shows how the wife and mistress kill the cheating husband with a black <a href="/wiki/Cadillac" title="Cadillac">Cadillac</a>. It was nominated for Video of the Year at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music" title="Academy of Country Music">Academy of Country Music</a> Awards. Underwood has performed "Two Black Cadillacs" in a number of live appearances, including at the <a href="/wiki/55th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="55th Annual Grammy Awards">55th Annual Grammy Awards</a>, where various images were projected onto the singer's gown, and at the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">2013 Country Music Association Awards</a>, in a medley with "<a href="/wiki/Good_Girl_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Good Girl (Carrie Underwood song)">Good Girl</a>", "<a href="/wiki/See_You_Again_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="See You Again (Carrie Underwood song)">See You Again</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Blown Away (Carrie Underwood song)">Blown Away</a>". It was also performed during the <a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_Tour" title="Blown Away Tour">Blown Away Tour</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_and_composition">Writing and composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Two_Black_Cadillacs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Writing and composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Underwood's <a href="/wiki/Play_On_Tour" title="Play On Tour">Play On Tour</a> ended in December 2010, she started to work on her then-untitled fourth studio album. <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Nashville" title="Sony Music Nashville">Sony Music Nashville</a> chairman/CEO Gary Overton said that the singer "took nearly a year to compile and record the songs."<sup id="cite_ref-usainterview_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usainterview-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Songwriters <a href="/wiki/Chris_Tompkins" title="Chris Tompkins">Chris Tompkins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Kear" title="Josh Kear">Josh Kear</a>, who previously wrote "<a href="/wiki/Before_He_Cheats" title="Before He Cheats">Before He Cheats</a>" for Underwood's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Hearts_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Some Hearts (Carrie Underwood album)">Some Hearts</a>,</i> worked on a track titled "<a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Blown Away (Carrie Underwood song)">Blown Away</a>" and sent it to the singer.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Underwood loved the song and recorded it, also naming her album <i>Blown Away</i> as it was the song that defined the album's darker direction.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She then started writing material with several songwriters, including <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a> and Kear. Together, the trio wrote "Two Black Cadillacs", which is about two women finding out that they both are involved in a relationship with the same man and set their differences aside to kill him. Underwood said that "it was so much fun to write and just be in that room" with Lindsey and Kear, as they "didn't really know what to expect or where we were headed or what we would end up with."<sup id="cite_ref-btsong_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-btsong-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Kear described the writing session:
</p>
<blockquote><p>Carrie had told me in an earlier writing appointment that she was expecting this album to have darker tones than any of her earlier records. So I remember sitting at the piano while writing the song thinking that if Carrie really wanted something dark, this was what she was after. Carrie really got into the story and making sure we didn't give away how the two women got rid of the man the song is about. This kind of song is really tricky because you need enough detail to tell the story and keep it interesting without it becoming so graphic that it's unpleasant to listen to more than once. I think we managed to pull that off. Either way, it was certainly fun choosing which elements of the story to put in and which ones to leave out.<sup id="cite_ref-unc_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unc-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>

<p>Melodically, "Two Black Cadillacs" is written in the <a href="/wiki/Key_(music)" title="Key (music)">key</a> of <a href="/wiki/F_minor" title="F minor">F minor</a>, and is set in the common time with a tempo of 120 <a href="/wiki/Beats_per_minute" class="mw-redirect" title="Beats per minute">beats per minute</a>. Underwood's voice spans from the low note of A<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-flat">&#x266d;</span></span><sup>3</sup> to the high note of <a href="/wiki/C_(musical_note)" title="C (musical note)">C</a><sup>5</sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-mnotes_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mnotes-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Billy Dukes of <a href="/wiki/Townsquare_Media" title="Townsquare Media">Taste of Country</a> noted that the lyrics of the song push "the normally plucky country superstar to the edge of evil. It's as far as she can go without falling off the cliff of believability."<sup id="cite_ref-dukes_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dukes-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Dukes also described the song as a "mini-movie",<sup id="cite_ref-dukes_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dukes-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> in which Underwood sings: "And the preacher said he was a good man / And his brother said he was a good friend / But the women in the two black veils didn't bother to cry / Bye, bye / Yeah they took turns laying a rose down / Threw a handful of dirt into the deep ground / He's not the only one who had a secret to hide / Bye bye, bye bye, bye bye."<sup id="cite_ref-unc_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unc-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Country_pop" title="Country pop">country pop</a> instrumentation includes <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a>, brooding <a href="/wiki/Strings_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Strings (music)">strings</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a>, and has been compared to "<a href="/wiki/Goodbye_Earl" title="Goodbye Earl">Goodbye Earl</a>" by the <a href="/wiki/Dixie_Chicks" title="Dixie Chicks">Dixie Chicks</a> and "<a href="/wiki/Gunpowder_and_Lead" class="mw-redirect" title="Gunpowder and Lead">Gunpowder and Lead</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Lambert" title="Miranda Lambert">Miranda Lambert</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-mnotes_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mnotes-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dukes_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dukes-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Two_Black_Cadillacs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Two_Black_Cadillacs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Carrie_Underwood_Blown_Away_Tour_2012_-_01.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Carrie_Underwood_Blown_Away_Tour_2012_-_01.jpg/150px-Carrie_Underwood_Blown_Away_Tour_2012_-_01.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="291" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Carrie_Underwood_Blown_Away_Tour_2012_-_01.jpg/225px-Carrie_Underwood_Blown_Away_Tour_2012_-_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Carrie_Underwood_Blown_Away_Tour_2012_-_01.jpg/300px-Carrie_Underwood_Blown_Away_Tour_2012_-_01.jpg 2x" data-file-width="810" data-file-height="1571" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Carrie_Underwood_Blown_Away_Tour_2012_-_01.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Underwood performing during the first act of the <a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_Tour" title="Blown Away Tour">Blown Away Tour</a> in which she sang "Two Black Cadillacs"</div></div></div> 
<p>"Two Black Cadillacs" received generally positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>. Billy Dukes of Taste of Country stated that "vocally, Underwood is brilliant as always, and lyrically, she, Hillary Lindsey and Josh Kear have penned a tight story", but went on to say that "compared to Underwood's other hits, this song doesn't stand out after one digests the macabre storyline. So, maybe it'll only sell a million singles and reach No. 2", giving the song three-and-a-half stars.<sup id="cite_ref-dukes_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dukes-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Daryl Addison of <a href="/wiki/Great_American_Country" title="Great American Country">Great American Country</a> praised the song, saying that "even with heavy thematic elements, the songs impressively maintain a pop/crossover shine and accessible sound, courtesy of Carrie's pristine voice and modern delivery."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Writing for Country Universe, Kevin Coyne gave the song an A, complimenting the singer's versatility, saying that "it's a credit to her ambition as an artist. For someone so frequently accused of getting to the top without having to earn it, she continues to work harder than the rest of her peers just to stay there."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Two_Black_Cadillacs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the release of the album, "Two Black Cadillacs" sold 8,000 units.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> After being released as a single, the song debuted at number 55 on the US <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart on the dated week of November 24, 2012, and eventually reached its peak of number 2, spending a total of 21 weeks on the chart.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-countryairplay_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-countryairplay-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> "Two Black Cadillacs" became Underwood's 17th number one on <a href="/wiki/Mediabase" title="Mediabase">Mediabase</a>'s Country Airplay charts.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On the dated week of December 29, 2012, the song debuted at number 90 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, reaching its peak of number 41 following the release of the video.<sup id="cite_ref-acharts_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acharts-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> By May 15, 2013, it had sold 762,000 in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On September 30, 2013, "Two Black Cadillacs" was certified Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA), and as of November 2015, it has sold 1,110,000 copies<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodTwo_Black_CadillacssingleCertRef_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodTwo_Black_CadillacssingleCertRef-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In Canada, the song achieved moderate success, reaching number three on the <a href="/wiki/Canada_Country" class="mw-redirect" title="Canada Country">Country</a> component chart and 52 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-acharts_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acharts-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cancountry_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cancountry-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_miniseries">Television miniseries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Two_Black_Cadillacs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Television miniseries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Two_Black_Cadillacs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The accompanying music video for "Two Black Cadillacs" was directed by <a href="/wiki/P.R._Brown" class="mw-redirect" title="P.R. Brown">P.R. Brown</a> and produced by Steve Lamar for Lamar Brothers.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Filmed in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville, Tennessee</a>, the video draws inspiration from <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/Christine_(novel)" title="Christine (novel)">Christine</a>,</i> which tells the story of a vintage <a href="/wiki/Automobile" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile">automobile</a> apparently possessed by <a href="/wiki/Supernatural" title="Supernatural">supernatural</a> forces.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-countryvideo_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-countryvideo-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> A trailer was released on November 26, 2012, showing Underwood as a widow done wrong, bringing the lyrics of the song to life.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> It premiered on January 23, 2013, on <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Tonight" title="Entertainment Tonight">Entertainment Tonight</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-premiere_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The video shows Underwood driving her black 1964 Cadillac through the countryside, heading towards her destination. Elsewhere, two women are shown attending a funeral devoid of any emotion complete with matching black veils. Intercut are scenes of Underwood's car in a dark alleyway and the cheating husband in its headlights. The video ends with the car running over the man and repairing itself afterwards.<sup id="cite_ref-countryvideo_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-countryvideo-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Two_Black_Cadillacs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>
Underwood performed "Two Black Cadillacs" at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2012" title="American Music Awards of 2012">40th American Music Awards</a> on November 18, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Amy Sciaretto of Taste of Country summarized the performance, writing: </p><blockquote><p>Underwood performed in a lacy, frilly black dress with a full skirt and black leather fingerless gloves, which she paired with smoky black eye makeup and black hoop earrings. The singer was the centerpiece of the performance, and her look was jaw-droppingly gorgeous. [...] There were bright lights above the stage and images of two black Cadillacs on the screens behind her, but that was about the extent of her production. And the simplicity worked for her.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote><p> She performed an acoustic version of the song during the <a href="/wiki/55th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="55th Annual Grammy Awards">55th Annual Grammy Awards</a>. During the performance, the singer's gown became a screen on which images varying from roses to butterflies and fireworks were projected.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Underwood also performed "Two Black Cadillacs" at the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">2013 Country Music Association Awards</a>, in a medley with "<a href="/wiki/Good_Girl_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Good Girl (Carrie Underwood song)">Good Girl</a>", "<a href="/wiki/See_You_Again_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="See You Again (Carrie Underwood song)">See You Again</a>" and "Blown Away".<sup id="cite_ref-cma_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cma-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> "Two Black Cadillacs" was the third song in the medley, and Coti Howell of Taste of Country noted that, in that part, "lighting ripped across the screen and everything went black, except Underwood and her sparkly microphone."<sup id="cite_ref-cma_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cma-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The song was also performed at the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">2013 Academy of Country Music Awards</a> and during the <a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_Tour" title="Blown Away Tour">Blown Away Tour</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
