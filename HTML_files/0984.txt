<p><i><b>Amagiri</b></i><span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span lang="ja">天霧</span><span style="display:none">,</span>&#32;<i>"Heavenly Mist"</i>)</span><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was the 15th of 24 <a href="/wiki/Fubuki-class_destroyer" title="Fubuki-class destroyer"><i>Fubuki</i>-class</a> <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyers</a>, built for the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a> following <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. When introduced into service, these ships were the most powerful destroyers in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> They served as first-line destroyers through the 1930s, and remained formidable weapons systems well into the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_War" title="Pacific War">Pacific War</a>. She is most famous for ramming the <a href="/wiki/Motor_Torpedo_Boat_PT-109" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor Torpedo Boat PT-109"><i>PT-109</i></a> commanded by <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_(junior_grade)" title="Lieutenant (junior grade)">Lieutenant</a> <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>, who would later become the 35th <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_destroyer_Amagiri_(1930)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Construction of the advanced <i>Fubuki</i>-class destroyers was authorized as part of the Imperial Japanese Navy's expansion program from fiscal 1923, intended to give Japan a qualitative edge with the world's most modern ships.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The <i>Fubuki</i> class had performance that was a quantum leap over previous destroyer designs, so much so that they were designated <i>Special Type destroyers</i><span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span lang="ja">特型</span><span style="display:none">,</span>&#32;<i>Tokugata</i>)</span>. The large size, powerful engines, high speed, large radius of action and unprecedented armament gave these destroyers the firepower similar to many <a href="/wiki/Light_cruiser" title="Light cruiser">light cruisers</a> in other navies.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <i>Amagiri</i>, built at the <a href="/wiki/IHI_Corporation" title="IHI Corporation">Ishikawajima Shipyards</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a> was the fifth in an improved series, which incorporated a modified <a href="/wiki/Gun_turret" title="Gun turret">gun turret</a> which could elevate her main battery of <a href="/wiki/Type_3_127_mm_50_caliber_naval_gun" class="mw-redirect" title="Type 3 127 mm 50 caliber naval gun">127&#160;mm (5.0&#160;in)/50 cal Type 3 naval guns</a> to 75° as opposed to the original 40°, thus permitting the guns to be used as <a href="/wiki/Dual_purpose_gun" class="mw-redirect" title="Dual purpose gun">dual purpose guns</a> against aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <i>Amagiri</i> was <a href="/wiki/Keel_laying" title="Keel laying">laid down</a> on 28 November 1928, <a href="/wiki/Ship_launching" class="mw-redirect" title="Ship launching">launched</a> on 27 February 1930 and commissioned on 10 November 1930.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Originally assigned hull designation “Destroyer No. 49”, she was designated <i>Amagiri</i> before her launch.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inter-war_period">Inter-war period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_destroyer_Amagiri_(1930)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Inter-war period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1935, after the <a href="/wiki/IJN_4th_Fleet" class="mw-redirect" title="IJN 4th Fleet">Fourth Fleet Incident</a>, in which a large number of ships were damaged by a <a href="/wiki/Typhoon" title="Typhoon">typhoon</a>, <i>Amagiri</i>, along with her <a href="/wiki/Sister_ship" title="Sister ship">sister ships</a>, were modified with stronger hulls and increased displacement. In 1937, <i>Amagiri</i> covered landing of Japanese forces in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hangzhou" title="Hangzhou">Hangzhou</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War" title="Second Sino-Japanese War">Second Sino-Japanese War</a>. In 1940, she was assigned to patrol and cover landings of Japanese forces in south China, and subsequently participated in the <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_French_Indochina" class="mw-redirect" title="Invasion of French Indochina">Invasion of French Indochina</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_destroyer_Amagiri_(1930)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_operations">Early operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_destroyer_Amagiri_(1930)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the time of the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">attack on Pearl Harbor</a>, <i>Amagiri</i> was assigned to Destroyer Division 20 of Desron 3 of the <a href="/wiki/IJN_1st_Fleet" class="mw-redirect" title="IJN 1st Fleet">IJN 1st Fleet</a>, and had deployed from <a href="/wiki/Kure_Naval_District" title="Kure Naval District">Kure Naval District</a> to the port of Samah on <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan</a> Island. From 4 December 1941, to the end of the year, <i>Amagiri</i> covered the landings of Japanese troops in <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Malaya" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Malaya">Malaya</a>, and was part of the escort in support of <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Sumatra_(1942)" class="mw-redirect" title="Invasion of Sumatra (1942)">"Operation L"</a> (the invasion of <a href="/wiki/Bangka_Island" title="Bangka Island">Banka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palembang" title="Palembang">Palembang</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_East_Indies" class="mw-redirect" title="Netherlands East Indies">Netherlands East Indies</a>. At the end of February, <i>Amagiri</i> covered minesweeping operations around Singapore and <a href="/wiki/Johore" class="mw-redirect" title="Johore">Johore</a>. In March, <i>Amagiri</i> was assigned to <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Sumatra_(1942)" class="mw-redirect" title="Invasion of Sumatra (1942)">"Operation T"</a> (the invasion of northern <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Invasion_and_occupation_of_the_Andaman_Islands_during_World_War_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Invasion and occupation of the Andaman Islands during World War II">"Operation D"</a> (the invasion of the <a href="/wiki/Andaman_Islands" title="Andaman Islands">Andaman Islands</a>). During the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean_raids" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian Ocean raids">Indian Ocean raids</a>, <i>Amagiri</i> — together with the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_cruiser" title="Heavy cruiser">heavy cruisers</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Mogami_(1934)" title="Japanese cruiser Mogami (1934)"><i>Mogami</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Mikuma" title="Japanese cruiser Mikuma"><i>Mikuma</i></a> sank the British passenger ship <i>Dardanus</i>, Steamship <i>Gandara</i> and Merchant ship <i>Indora</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> From 13–22 April, <i>Amagiri</i> returned via Singapore and <a href="/wiki/Camranh_Bay" class="mw-redirect" title="Camranh Bay">Camranh Bay</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kure_Naval_Arsenal" title="Kure Naval Arsenal">Kure Naval Arsenal</a>, for maintenance.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 4–5 June, <i>Amagiri</i> participated in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Midway" title="Battle of Midway">Battle of Midway</a> as was part of the <a href="/wiki/Aleutian_campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Aleutian campaign">Aleutian Invasion force</a> and was subsequently based at <a href="/wiki/Amami-%C5%8Cshima" class="mw-redirect" title="Amami-Ōshima">Amami-Ōshima</a> for patrols of southern waters until mid-July. In July 1942, <i>Amagiri</i> sailed from Amami-Ōshima to <a href="/wiki/Mako_Guard_District" title="Mako Guard District">Mako Guard District</a>, Singapore, <a href="/wiki/Sabang,_Indonesia" class="mw-redirect" title="Sabang, Indonesia">Sabang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mergui" class="mw-redirect" title="Mergui">Mergui</a> for a projected second Indian Ocean raid. The operation was cancelled due to the <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal_campaign" title="Guadalcanal campaign">Guadalcanal campaign</a>, and <i>Amagiri</i> was ordered to <a href="/wiki/Chuuk_Lagoon" title="Chuuk Lagoon">Truk</a> instead, arriving in late August.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Eastern_Solomons" title="Battle of the Eastern Solomons">Battle of the Eastern Solomons</a> on 24 August, <i>Amagiri</i> took on troops from transport ships while at sea, and sailed on to <a href="/wiki/Guadalcanal" title="Guadalcanal">Guadalcanal</a>. During this operation, she was attacked 60&#160;nmi (110&#160;km; 69&#160;mi) north-northeast of <a href="/wiki/Savo_Island" title="Savo Island">Savo Island</a> by <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a> <a href="/wiki/SBD_Dauntless" class="mw-redirect" title="SBD Dauntless">SBD Dauntless</a> <a href="/wiki/Dive_bomber" title="Dive bomber">dive bombers</a> from <a href="/wiki/Henderson_Field_(Guadalcanal)" title="Henderson Field (Guadalcanal)">Henderson Field</a>, which sank her sister ship <a href="/wiki/Japanese_destroyer_Asagiri_(1929)" title="Japanese destroyer Asagiri (1929)"><i>Asagiri</i></a> and severely damaged <a href="/wiki/Japanese_destroyer_Shirakumo_(1927)" title="Japanese destroyer Shirakumo (1927)"><i>Shirakumo</i></a>. After rescuing the <i>Asagiri</i> survivors, she towed <i>Shirakumo</i> to <a href="/wiki/Shortland_Island" title="Shortland Island">Shortlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <i>Amagiri</i> continued to be used on numerous "<a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Express" title="Tokyo Express">Tokyo Express</a>" transport missions to various locations in the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a> in September.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although reassigned to the <a href="/wiki/IJN_8th_Fleet" class="mw-redirect" title="IJN 8th Fleet">IJN 8th Fleet</a> in October, <i>Amagiri</i> continued to be used for "Tokyo Express" missions through the end of the year. After the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Battle_of_Guadalcanal" title="Naval Battle of Guadalcanal">Naval Battle of Guadalcanal</a> from 13–15 November, she assisted destroyer <a href="/wiki/Japanese_destroyer_Mochizuki_(1927)" title="Japanese destroyer Mochizuki (1927)"><i>Mochizuki</i></a> in the rescue of 1,500 survivors of the merchant vessels <i>Canberra Maru</i> and <i>Nagara Maru</i>, and escorted the damaged <i>Sado Maru</i> to the Shortlands. She returned to Kure Naval Arsenal for repairs by mid-January 1943.
</p><p><i>Amagiri</i> returned to Rabaul by March 1943, and resumed its missions as a high speed transport. On 7 April, she was strafed by a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">USAAF</a> <a href="/wiki/B-17_Flying_Fortress" class="mw-redirect" title="B-17 Flying Fortress">B-17 Flying Fortress</a> bomber, killing 10 crewmen. On 5–6 July, during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kula_Gulf" title="Battle of Kula Gulf">Battle of Kula Gulf</a> <i>Amagiri</i> was engaged by United States Navy destroyers and cruisers while attempting a troop transport mission to <a href="/wiki/Kolombangara" title="Kolombangara">Kolombangara</a>. She took five shell hits, which killed 10 crewmen. After the battle she attempted to rescue the survivors of the destroyer <a href="/wiki/Japanese_destroyer_Niizuki" title="Japanese destroyer Niizuki"><i>Niizuki</i></a>, but was driven off by the American destroyers <a href="/wiki/USS_Nicholas_(DD-449)" title="USS Nicholas (DD-449)">USS&#160;<i>Nicholas</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/USS_Radford_(DD-446)" title="USS Radford (DD-446)"><i>Radford</i></a> and returned to Rabaul for repairs.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="John_F._Kennedy_and_PT-109">John F. Kennedy and <i>PT-109</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_destroyer_Amagiri_(1930)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: John F. Kennedy and PT-109">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Motor_Torpedo_Boat_PT-109" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor Torpedo Boat PT-109">Motor Torpedo Boat PT-109</a></div>
<p>On 2 August, while returning from another "Tokyo Express" night reinforcement fast transport mission to <a href="/wiki/Vila,_Solomon_Islands" title="Vila, Solomon Islands">Vila</a>, <i>Amagiri</i> rammed and sank the <i>PT-109</i> torpedo boat, commanded by the future U.S. President, then <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant,_junior_grade" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant, junior grade">Lieutenant, junior grade</a> <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>. It is widely believed that those aboard <i>Amagiri</i> were not even aware of <i>PT-109</i>, which was difficult to see because of its small size and lack of lights. However, <a href="/wiki/Robert_J._Donovan" title="Robert J. Donovan">Robert J. Donovan</a> in his book <i>PT 109: John F. Kennedy in WWII</i>, after interviewing many of the crew, concludes that it was not an accident, and he talked to the man at the wheel who was ordered to steer for a <a href="/wiki/Collision_course" title="Collision course">collision course</a>. <i>Amagiri</i> also engaged other <a href="/wiki/PT_boat" title="PT boat">PT boats</a> in the Blackett Strait south of <a href="/wiki/Kolombangara" title="Kolombangara">Kolombangara</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Commander" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant Commander">Lieutenant Commander</a> Kohei Hanami — who commanded <i>Amagiri</i> at that time — attended President Kennedy's inauguration in 1961.
</p><p>The incident would be publicized in a book, <a href="/wiki/PT_109_(film)" title="PT 109 (film)">movie</a>, and in a <a href="/wiki/PT-109_(song)" title="PT-109 (song)">hit song</a> by <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Dean" title="Jimmy Dean">Jimmy Dean</a>, as "the Jap destroyer in the night, cut the 109 in two", making it probably the only Japanese ship to ever be mentioned in the top ten of the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Billboard Hot 100</a> singles charts.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subsequent_career">Subsequent career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Japanese_destroyer_Amagiri_(1930)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Subsequent career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Amagiri</i> continued to be used on "Tokyo Express" missions through the end of 1943. She engaged United States Navy destroyers in combat at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cape_St._George" title="Battle of Cape St. George">Battle of Cape St. George</a> in late November, and escaped pursuing American destroyers led by <a href="/wiki/Captain_(naval)" title="Captain (naval)">Captain</a> <a href="/wiki/Arleigh_Burke" title="Arleigh Burke">Arleigh Burke</a>. On 7 December, she collided near <a href="/wiki/Kavieng" title="Kavieng">Kavieng</a> with the destroyer <a href="/wiki/Japanese_destroyer_Akikaze" title="Japanese destroyer Akikaze"><i>Akikaze</i></a>, which sheared off her <a href="/wiki/Bow_(ship)" title="Bow (ship)">bow</a>. Sent back to <a href="/wiki/Kure_Naval_Arsenal" title="Kure Naval Arsenal">Kure Naval Arsenal</a> for repairs in January 1944, she was reassigned to the <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Area_Fleet" title="Southwest Area Fleet">Southwest Area Fleet</a> in March and was based in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> to provide escort for transport missions in the western Netherlands East Indies. On 23 April, after departing Singapore with heavy cruiser <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Aoba" title="Japanese cruiser Aoba"><i>Aoba</i></a> and <a href="/wiki/Light_cruiser" title="Light cruiser">light cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_%C5%8Ci" title="Japanese cruiser Ōi"><i>Ōi</i></a> bound for <a href="/wiki/Davao_City" title="Davao City">Davao</a>, <i>Amagiri</i> struck a <a href="/wiki/Naval_mine" title="Naval mine">naval mine</a> in <a href="/wiki/Makassar_Strait" title="Makassar Strait">Makassar Strait</a> 55&#160;nmi (102&#160;km; 63&#160;mi) south of <a href="/wiki/Balikpapan" title="Balikpapan">Balikpapan</a> at position (<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Japanese_destroyer_Amagiri_(1930)&amp;params=02_10_S_116_45_E_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">02°10′S</span> <span class="longitude">116°45′E</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; As she took over two hours to sink, there were few casualties.
</p><p><i>Amagiri</i> was struck from the <a href="/wiki/Navy_List" class="mw-redirect" title="Navy List">Navy List</a> on 10 June 1944.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
