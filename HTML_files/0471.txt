<p>The <b>8th <a href="/wiki/IAAF_World_Championships_in_Athletics" class="mw-redirect" title="IAAF World Championships in Athletics">World Championships in Athletics</a></b>, under the auspices of the <a href="/wiki/International_Association_of_Athletics_Federations" title="International Association of Athletics Federations">International Association of Athletics Federations</a>, were held at <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Stadium_(Edmonton)" title="Commonwealth Stadium (Edmonton)">Commonwealth Stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Edmonton,_Alberta" class="mw-redirect" title="Edmonton, Alberta">Edmonton, Alberta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> between 3 August and 12 August and was the first time the event had visited <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. The music for the Opening and Closing Ceremonies was composed by Canadian composers <a href="/wiki/Jan_Randall" title="Jan Randall">Jan Randall</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Cassius_Khan" title="Cassius Khan">Cassius Khan</a> and the events were televised live to an estimated<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag may use weasel words or too-vague attribution. (February 2018)">by whom?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> viewing audience of 4 billion people in over 200 countries.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The ceremonies also featured a 1000 voice choir, and the <a href="/wiki/Edmonton_Symphony_Orchestra" title="Edmonton Symphony Orchestra">Edmonton Symphony Orchestra</a>.
</p>
