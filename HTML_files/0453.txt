<p><b>Gerald Mervin Vanenburg</b> (born 5 March 1964) is a Dutch retired <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> of <a href="/wiki/Surinamese_people" title="Surinamese people">Surinamese</a> descent who played as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder#Winger" title="Midfielder">right winger</a>.
</p><p>He amassed <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> totals of 372 games and 112 goals for <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> and <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a> combined, winning fifteen major titles between the two clubs, including the <a href="/wiki/1988_European_Cup_Final" title="1988 European Cup Final">1988 European Cup</a> with the latter. Subsequently he played in Japan, France and Germany, in a 20-year professional career.
</p><p>Vanenburg earned more than 40 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a>, appearing at the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990 World Cup</a> and <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1988" title="UEFA Euro 1988">Euro 1988</a> and winning the latter tournament.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Vanenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ajax">Ajax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Vanenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ajax">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Utrecht" title="Utrecht">Utrecht</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Vanenburg finished his football formation with <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">AFC Ajax</a>, and made his <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> debuts exactly one month after his 17th birthday, against <a href="/wiki/ADO_Den_Haag" title="ADO Den Haag">ADO Den Haag</a>. He finished <a href="/wiki/1980%E2%80%9381_Eredivisie" title="1980–81 Eredivisie">his first season</a> with 11 games and three goals, being soon dubbed <i>Vaantje</i> and <i>Geraldinho</i> for his above-average skills.
</p><p>Vanenburg became an undisputed starter for the <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> side shortly after, providing countless <a href="/wiki/Assist_(football)" title="Assist (football)">assists</a> for <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)#Striker" title="Forward (association football)">strikers</a> <a href="/wiki/Marco_van_Basten" title="Marco van Basten">Marco van Basten</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wim_Kieft" title="Wim Kieft">Wim Kieft</a> and adding 30 himself in two seasons combined as the club won back-to-back national championships; before leaving in June 1986, he scored in double digits in two more seasons. Himself, van Basten, Kieft were amongst a steady stream of talented youngsters that also included <a href="/wiki/Frank_Rijkaard" title="Frank Rijkaard">Frank Rijkaard</a> that helped to the conquest of three league titles between 1982 and 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-FH_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FH-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PSV">PSV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Vanenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: PSV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vanenburg signed for <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a> for <a href="/wiki/1986%E2%80%9387_Eredivisie" title="1986–87 Eredivisie">1986–87</a>, netting nine goals in 34 matches in his first season, which ended in league conquest. He was part of the team that won <a href="/wiki/Treble_(association_football)" title="Treble (association football)">the treble</a> <a href="/wiki/1987%E2%80%9388_Eredivisie" title="1987–88 Eredivisie">the following campaign</a>, with the player appearing in <a href="/wiki/1988_European_Cup_Final" title="1988 European Cup Final">the final</a> of the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Champions_League" title="UEFA Champions League">European Cup</a> and converting his <a href="/wiki/Penalty_shoot-out_(association_football)" title="Penalty shoot-out (association football)">penalty shootout</a> attempt against <a href="/wiki/S.L._Benfica" title="S.L. Benfica">S.L. Benfica</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The backbone of this treble winning team was formed by many of his former teammates at Ajax, including <a href="/wiki/Frank_Arnesen" title="Frank Arnesen">Frank Arnesen</a>, Kieft, <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Koeman" title="Ronald Koeman">Ronald Koeman</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%B8ren_Lerby" title="Søren Lerby">Søren Lerby</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FH_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FH-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Having rejected a lucrative move to <a href="/wiki/A.S._Roma" title="A.S. Roma">A.S. Roma</a>, Vanenburg played and scored regularly for PSV in the following five seasons, winning a further three leagues and two <a href="/wiki/KNVB_Cup" title="KNVB Cup">Dutch Cups</a>. He appeared in nearly 500 official games between the two clubs, scoring almost 150 goals. He was also one of five European players to ever achieve the feat of winning four competitions – three with their club and one with the national team – in the same year, the others being teammates <a href="/wiki/Berry_van_Aerle" title="Berry van Aerle">Berry van Aerle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hans_van_Breukelen" title="Hans van Breukelen">Hans van Breukelen</a>, Kieft and Koeman.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Abroad">Abroad</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Vanenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Abroad">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Aged 29, Vanenburg had his first abroad experience, helping <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BAbilo_Iwata" title="Júbilo Iwata">Júbilo Iwata</a> promote to the <a href="/wiki/J1_League" title="J1 League">J1 League</a> in his first year then playing a further two seasons with them. He finished the <a href="/wiki/1996%E2%80%9397_Eredivisie" title="1996–97 Eredivisie">1996–97 campaign</a> back in his country, still being relatively played as hometown's <a href="/wiki/FC_Utrecht" title="FC Utrecht">FC Utrecht</a> ranked in 12th position.
</p><p>Until his retirement in 2000 at the age of 36, Vanenburg played three more years of top flight football, with <a href="/wiki/AS_Cannes" title="AS Cannes">AS Cannes</a> (France) and <a href="/wiki/TSV_1860_Munich" title="TSV 1860 Munich">TSV 1860 Munich</a> (Germany), where he began appearing regularly as a <a href="/wiki/Defender_(association_football)#Sweeper_(libero)" title="Defender (association football)">sweeper</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Vanenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vanenburg made his debut for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> on 14 April 1982 at only 18, playing the full 90 minutes of a 1–0 <a href="/wiki/Exhibition_game" title="Exhibition game">friendly</a> win with <a href="/wiki/Greece_national_football_team" title="Greece national football team">Greece</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Eindhoven" title="Eindhoven">Eindhoven</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RSSSF_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RSSSF-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Vanenburg was a member of the Dutch squad at the <a href="/wiki/1983_FIFA_World_Youth_Championship" title="1983 FIFA World Youth Championship">1983 FIFA World Youth Championship</a>. He was selected for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Euro_1988" title="UEFA Euro 1988">UEFA Euro 1988</a> tournament in <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>, appearing in all the games as the <i>Oranje</i> won the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vanenburg was also picked by manager <a href="/wiki/Leo_Beenhakker" title="Leo Beenhakker">Leo Beenhakker</a> for his <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1990 FIFA World Cup">1990 FIFA World Cup</a> squad, but his contribution consisted of 45 minutes against <a href="/wiki/Egypt_national_football_team" title="Egypt national football team">Egypt</a> (1–1 group stage draw),<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> in an eventual <a href="/wiki/Single-elimination_tournament" title="Single-elimination tournament">round-of-16</a> exit in Italy. His last international appearance came as a substitute in a 2–2 draw to <a href="/wiki/Poland_national_football_team" title="Poland national football team">Poland</a> on 14 October 1992, in <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a> in a <a href="/wiki/1994_World_Cup_qualification" class="mw-redirect" title="1994 World Cup qualification">1994 World Cup qualification</a> match.<sup id="cite_ref-RSSSF_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RSSSF-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Vanenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After leaving 1860 Munich, Vanenburg immediately returned to PSV where he was appointed the youth team's manager but, during that timeframe, also managed former club TSV during three months, starting in April 2004, with the team eventually <a href="/wiki/2003%E2%80%9304_Bundesliga" title="2003–04 Bundesliga">being relegated</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Bundesliga" title="Bundesliga">Bundesliga</a>.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/2006%E2%80%9307_Eerste_Divisie" title="2006–07 Eerste Divisie">2006–07</a>, Vanenburg coached <a href="/wiki/Helmond_Sport" title="Helmond Sport">Helmond Sport</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Eerste_Divisie" title="Eerste Divisie">Eerste Divisie</a>, being fired on 17 February 2007. On 1 January of the following year he was appointed at another club in the category, <a href="/wiki/FC_Eindhoven" title="FC Eindhoven">FC Eindhoven</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gerald_Vanenburg&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vanenburg was the nephew of former <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_football_team" title="Suriname national football team">Surinamese</a> international player and manager <a href="/wiki/Roy_Vanenburg" title="Roy Vanenburg">Roy Vanenburg</a>. The latter was considered one of the greatest footballers in the country's history, having won the <a href="/wiki/SVB_Hoofdklasse" title="SVB Hoofdklasse">SVB Hoofdklasse</a> title six times and the <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF_Champions%27_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="CONCACAF Champions&#39; Cup">CONCACAF Champions' Cup</a> twice with <a href="/wiki/S.V._Transvaal" title="S.V. Transvaal">S.V. Transvaal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
