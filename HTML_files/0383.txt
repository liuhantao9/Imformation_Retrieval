<p>The <b>Kalina</b>, also known as the <b>Caribs</b>, <b>mainland Caribs</b> and several other names, are an <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">indigenous people</a> native to the northern coastal areas of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. Today, the Kalina live largely in villages on the rivers and coasts of <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. They speak a <a href="/wiki/Cariban_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Cariban language">Cariban language</a> known as <a href="/wiki/Carib_language" title="Carib language">Carib</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> They may be related to the <a href="/wiki/Island_Caribs" title="Island Caribs">Island Caribs</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, though their languages are unrelated.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalina_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Traditionally, Westerners have called the Kalina people variants of the Spanish name <i>Caribe</i>, including "Caribs" in English, <i>Galina</i> in French, and <i>Karaïeb</i> in Dutch. However, the speakers call themselves <i>Kalina</i> or <i>Karìna</i> <span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA">[kaɽiɁnʲauɽaŋ]</span>, spelled variously.<sup id="cite_ref-Courtz1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Courtz1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Variants include <i>Kali'na</i>, <i>Cariña</i>, <i>Kariña</i>, <i>Kalihna</i>, <i>Kalinya</i>; other native names include <i>Maraworno</i> and <i>Marworno</i>. Kalina may distinguish themselves as <i>Kali'na tilewuyu</i> ("true Kalina"), partly to differentiate themselves from the mixed <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">Maroon</a>-Kalina inhabitants of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Use of "Kalina" and related variants has become common practice only recently in publications; many sources continue to use "Caribs" or associated names.<sup id="cite_ref-Courtz1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Courtz1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalina_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Kalina.png/300px-Kalina.png" decoding="async" width="300" height="232" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Kalina.png/450px-Kalina.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Kalina.png/600px-Kalina.png 2x" data-file-width="3300" data-file-height="2550" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map indicating the current geographic distribution of the Kali'na population</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Guyana" title="History of Guyana">History of Guyana</a></div>
<p>Lacking a written form of language before the arrival of Europeans, Kali'na history was passed down orally from one generation to the next through tales of myth and legend.
</p><p>For a long time, the few Europeans studying the history of the Amerindian people of this area did not distinguish between the various Caribbean tribes. Once the period of exploration was over, interest in the study of these people diminished greatly and did not re-emerge until the end of the 20th century, when a few French expatriates, notably Gérard Collomb, became interested in the Kali'na, and the Kali'na themselves began to relate their history, in particular Félix Tiouka, president of the Association of Amerindians of French Guiana (AAGF), and his son Alexis.
</p><p>For the reasons given, historical information regarding the Kali'na is rare and incomplete.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-Columbian_era">Pre-Columbian era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalina_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-Columbian era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina_chief_test_amazonia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Kalina_chief_test_amazonia.jpg/200px-Kalina_chief_test_amazonia.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="318" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Kalina_chief_test_amazonia.jpg/300px-Kalina_chief_test_amazonia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Kalina_chief_test_amazonia.jpg/400px-Kalina_chief_test_amazonia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2246" data-file-height="3574" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina_chief_test_amazonia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Drawing of a Kali'na ritual.</div></div></div>
<p>Making up for lack of written records, <a href="/wiki/Archaeology" title="Archaeology">archaeologists</a> have to date uncovered 273 Amerindian archeological sites on only 310&#160;km² of the land recovered from the <a href="/wiki/Sinnamary_River" title="Sinnamary River">Sinnamary River</a> by the Petit-Saut Dam. Some date back as far as two thousand years, establishing the antiquity of the Amerindian presence in this area.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup>,</sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The weak historical clues available indicate that before 1492, the Kali'na inhabited the coast (from the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_River" title="Amazon River">Amazon River</a> to that of the <a href="/wiki/Orinoco" title="Orinoco">Orinoco</a>), dividing their territory with the <a href="/wiki/Arawaks" class="mw-redirect" title="Arawaks">Arawak</a>, against whom they fought during their expansion toward the east and the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_River" title="Amazon River">Amazon River</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup>,</sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>They were prolific travelers even though they weren't nomads. They often traveled by land and by sea as far as the area around the Orinoco river to visit family, trade, and marry. <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> They often went to the area surrounding the Essequibo river (now in Guyana) to collect pebbles of red porphyry (<i>takuwa</i>), which Kali'na women prized for polishing their pottery.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The term <i>takuwa</i> also refers to jade, which was often traded in the Americas in general.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Colonization">Colonization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalina_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Colonization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="The_Palanaki.C5.82i_arrive"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Palanakiłi_arrive">The <i>Palanakiłi</i> arrive</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalina_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The Palanakiłi arrive">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina_weapons_tools.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Kalina_weapons_tools.jpg/200px-Kalina_weapons_tools.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="133" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Kalina_weapons_tools.jpg/300px-Kalina_weapons_tools.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Kalina_weapons_tools.jpg/400px-Kalina_weapons_tools.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1872" data-file-height="1243" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina_weapons_tools.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kali'na weapons and tools.</div></div></div>
<p>In their first contact with Europeans, the Kali'na thought they were dealing with the spirits of the sea, Palanakiłi, a name they use to this day when referring to whites.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>One of the first consequences of the arrival of Europeans, as in the case of many other Native American peoples, was a decrease in population due to violence inflicted by European soldiers <a href="/wiki/Genocide" title="Genocide">genocide</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Columbian_exchange" title="Columbian exchange">diseases brought over by the Europeans</a>. The Kali'na quickly succumbed in large numbers, because their <a href="/wiki/Immune_system" title="Immune system">immune systems</a> were not adapted to the viruses and bacteria of the Old World.
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Amerindians_in_Paris">Amerindians in Paris</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalina_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Amerindians in Paris">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina_man_1892.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Kalina_man_1892.jpg/200px-Kalina_man_1892.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="288" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Kalina_man_1892.jpg/300px-Kalina_man_1892.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Kalina_man_1892.jpg/400px-Kalina_man_1892.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1751" data-file-height="2521" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina_man_1892.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kali'na man in Paris in 1892 holding a <i>putu</i>, or wooden club.</div></div></div>
<p>The second half of the nineteenth century saw the heyday of <a href="/wiki/World%27s_fair" title="World&#39;s fair">World's Fairs</a>, in which European countries were displaying their wealth with colonial "villages" representing the colonized cultures. Although the World's Fairs of Paris did not have "Amerindian villages," public curiosity was such that Kali'na were sent to the capital twice - once in 1882 and again in 1892 - to be exhibited as oddities at the <a href="/wiki/Jardin_d%27Acclimatation" title="Jardin d&#39;Acclimatation">Jardin d'Acclimatation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pau:wa_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pau:wa-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup>,</sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="1882">1882</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalina_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1882">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina_Paris_1892.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Kalina_Paris_1892.jpg/250px-Kalina_Paris_1892.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="107" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Kalina_Paris_1892.jpg/375px-Kalina_Paris_1892.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Kalina_Paris_1892.jpg/500px-Kalina_Paris_1892.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4156" data-file-height="1784" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina_Paris_1892.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Portrait of the Kali'na exhibited at the Jardin d’Acclimatation in Paris in 1892.</div></div></div>
<p>Fifteen Kali’na, all members of one family living in <a href="/wiki/Sinnamary" title="Sinnamary">Sinnamary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iracoubo" title="Iracoubo">Iracoubo</a>, were sent to <i>Pau:wa</i> ("The Land of the Whites") in July 1882.<sup id="cite_ref-Pau:wa_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pau:wa-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Almost nothing is known about them, except their names<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and the fact that they were housed in <a href="/wiki/Hut_(dwelling)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hut (dwelling)">huts</a> on the lawn of the Jardin d'Acclimatation. The trip lasted four months, including three in Paris and a month's journey by boat (round trip). They were accompanied by a Creole who acted as intermediary and, presumably, interpreter.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> There are several portraits of them, taken by photographer <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Petit_(photographer)" title="Pierre Petit (photographer)">Pierre Petit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="The_Kali.27na_today"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Kali'na_today">The Kali'na today</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalina_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: The Kali&#039;na today">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_distribution">Geographic distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalina_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Geographic distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The part of South America where the Kali'na live is very sparsely populated.  However, the people of this ethnic group are such an extreme minority in all of the countries in which they are well established that locally they are a majority only in certain very secluded areas. Their current geographic distribution covers only a small fraction of their Pre-Columbian territory.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:School_girls_Bigi_Poika.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/School_girls_Bigi_Poika.jpg/250px-School_girls_Bigi_Poika.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="164" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/School_girls_Bigi_Poika.jpg/375px-School_girls_Bigi_Poika.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/School_girls_Bigi_Poika.jpg/500px-School_girls_Bigi_Poika.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1777" data-file-height="1168" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:School_girls_Bigi_Poika.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kali'na girls in Suriname in the village of Bigi Poika.</div></div></div>
<ul><li>In <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, they are especially localized in <a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A3o_Jos%C3%A9_dos_Galibi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="São José dos Galibi (page does not exist)">São José dos Galibi</a>, a village founded in 1950 on the right bank of the <a href="/wiki/Oyapock_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Oyapock River">Oyapock River</a> opposite <a href="/wiki/Saint-Georges,_French_Guiana" title="Saint-Georges, French Guiana">Saint-Georges</a> in French Guiana by several families who came from the region of the Mana River. They are also in the capital of <a href="/wiki/Amap%C3%A1" title="Amapá">Amapá</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macap%C3%A1" title="Macapá">Macapá</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Par%C3%A1" title="Pará">Pará</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Bel%C3%A9m" title="Belém">Belém</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>, they are still present in significant numbers in their original territory, the region between the <a href="/wiki/Maroni_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Maroni River">Maroni</a> and the Mana rivers (in particular, the communities of <a href="/wiki/Awala-Yalimapo" title="Awala-Yalimapo">Awala-Yalimapo</a>, the only one where they are a majority, <a href="/wiki/Saint-Laurent-du-Maroni" title="Saint-Laurent-du-Maroni">Saint-Laurent-du-Maroni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mana,_French_Guiana" title="Mana, French Guiana">Mana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iracoubo" title="Iracoubo">Iracoubo</a>), and the Amerindian village of <a href="/wiki/Kourou" title="Kourou">Kourou</a> as well as, in fewer numbers, the island of <a href="/wiki/Cayenne" title="Cayenne">Cayenne</a>.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, they are a strong presence on the left bank of the Maroni River and on the banks of the <a href="/wiki/Coppename_River" title="Coppename River">Coppename River</a>.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, the country where their numbers are the greatest, they can be found in two distinct zones: in the <a href="/wiki/Los_Llanos,_Venezuela" class="mw-redirect" title="Los Llanos, Venezuela">llanos</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Orinoco" title="Orinoco">Orinoco</a> river valley and on the <a href="/wiki/Cuyuni_River" title="Cuyuni River">Cuyuni River</a> valley part of which is in <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>.  See also <a href="/wiki/Chimire,_Venezuela" title="Chimire, Venezuela">Chimire, Venezuela</a>.</li></ul>
<p><br />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalina_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>They use mostly <a href="/wiki/Percussion_instrument" title="Percussion instrument">percussion instruments</a>. Their <i>sanpula</i> (or <i>sambula</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Drum" title="Drum">drum</a> with two skins stretched over either end of the shell by hoops pulled together with cord and is played with a <a href="/wiki/Percussion_mallet" title="Percussion mallet">mallet</a>. They also have two kinds of <a href="/wiki/Maraca" title="Maraca">maracas</a>, called a <i>kalawasi</i> (or <i>kalawashi</i>) and a <i>malaka</i>.
</p><p>Their flute, the <i>kuwama</i>, is still made but is more and more often replaced by the European <a href="/wiki/Flute" title="Flute">flute</a>. There is also a terra cotta horn called a <i>kuti</i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Language">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kalina_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina_boy_pirogue_1892.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Kalina_boy_pirogue_1892.jpg/200px-Kalina_boy_pirogue_1892.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="208" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Kalina_boy_pirogue_1892.jpg/300px-Kalina_boy_pirogue_1892.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Kalina_boy_pirogue_1892.jpg/400px-Kalina_boy_pirogue_1892.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1205" data-file-height="1254" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kalina_boy_pirogue_1892.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kali'na boy in a dugout in Paris in 1892.</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kali%27na_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Kali&#39;na language">Kali'na language</a></div>
<p>They speak <a href="/wiki/Kali%27na_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Kali&#39;na language">Kali'na</a>, belonging to the family of <a href="/wiki/Cariban_languages" title="Cariban languages">Cariban languages</a>, is today still spoken by above 10,000 people in the coastal strip that stretches from Venezuela (5,000 speakers) to Brazil (100) passing through Guyana (475), Suriname (2,500) and French Guiana (3,000 people).
</p><p>Thanks to the relatively significant number of speakers, it is one of the most likely Amazonian tongues to overlive. Some experiments with written transcription were undertaken in Guyana.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Linguistics" title="Linguistics">Linguistic</a> standardization of a Kali'na <a href="/wiki/Writing" title="Writing">writing</a> system however is plagued by the diversity of the many different forms of the written language currently in use, which have been influenced by the languages of the <a href="/wiki/Colonization" title="Colonization">colonists</a> of the countries in which the Kali'na live, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>. Thus, even as far as their <a href="/wiki/Ethnonym" title="Ethnonym">ethnonym</a> is concerned, Kali'na, there are no fewer than nine different writing systems. Kali'na therefore remains a primarily oral language.
</p>
