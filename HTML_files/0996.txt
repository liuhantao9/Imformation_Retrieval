<p>The <b>United States Department of the Navy</b> was established by an <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Congress" title="Act of Congress">Act of Congress</a> on 30 April 1798 (initiated by the recommendation of <a href="/wiki/James_McHenry" title="James McHenry">James McHenry</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-Steiner_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Steiner-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> to provide a government organizational structure to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a> (from 1834 onward) and, when directed by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> (or <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a> during time of war), the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a>, as a service within the Department of the Navy,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> though each remain independent <a href="/wiki/Service_branch" class="mw-redirect" title="Service branch">service branches</a>. The Department of the Navy was an Executive Department and the Secretary of the Navy was a member of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Cabinet" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Cabinet">President's cabinet</a> until 1949, when amendments to the <a href="/wiki/National_Security_Act_of_1947" title="National Security Act of 1947">National Security Act of 1947</a> changed the name of the <i><a href="/wiki/National_Military_Establishment" class="mw-redirect" title="National Military Establishment">National Military Establishment</a></i> to the <i>Department of Defense</i> and made it an Executive Department. The Department of the Navy then became, along with the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_the_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Department of the Army">Department of the Army</a> and <a href="/wiki/Department_of_the_Air_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Department of the Air Force">Department of the Air Force</a>, a <i>Military Department</i> within the Department of Defense: subject to the authority, direction and control of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_Defense" title="United States Secretary of Defense">Secretary of Defense</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership">Leadership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Department_of_the_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Leadership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Seal_of_the_United_States_Department_of_the_Navy_(1879-1957).png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Seal_of_the_United_States_Department_of_the_Navy_%281879-1957%29.png/170px-Seal_of_the_United_States_Department_of_the_Navy_%281879-1957%29.png" decoding="async" width="170" height="170" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Seal_of_the_United_States_Department_of_the_Navy_%281879-1957%29.png/255px-Seal_of_the_United_States_Department_of_the_Navy_%281879-1957%29.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Seal_of_the_United_States_Department_of_the_Navy_%281879-1957%29.png/340px-Seal_of_the_United_States_Department_of_the_Navy_%281879-1957%29.png 2x" data-file-width="2371" data-file-height="2376" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Seal_of_the_United_States_Department_of_the_Navy_(1879-1957).png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Seal of the U.S. Department of the Navy from 1879 to 1957.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DON-org-sec.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/DON-org-sec.png/200px-DON-org-sec.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/DON-org-sec.png/300px-DON-org-sec.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/DON-org-sec.png/400px-DON-org-sec.png 2x" data-file-width="775" data-file-height="562" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DON-org-sec.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Navy Department, mainly the Office of the Secretary, organizational structure (2006.)</div></div></div>
<p>The Department of the Navy is headed by the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_the_Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary of the Navy">Secretary of the Navy</a>, also known as the <i>SECNAV</i> in naval jargon, who has the authority to conduct all of the affairs of the Department, subject to lawful authority, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_Defense" title="United States Secretary of Defense">Secretary of Defense</a>, and the President. The Secretary of the Navy is appointed by the President with the advice and consent of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">Senate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Secretary is assisted by an <a href="/wiki/Under_Secretary_of_the_Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="Under Secretary of the Navy">Under Secretary of the Navy</a>, four <a href="/wiki/Assistant_Secretary_of_the_Navy" title="Assistant Secretary of the Navy">Assistant Secretaries of the Navy</a> and a <a href="/wiki/General_Counsel_of_the_Department_of_the_Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="General Counsel of the Department of the Navy">General Counsel of the Department of the Navy</a>, who are also appointed by the President with the advice and consent of the Senate.
</p><p>The highest ranking <a href="/wiki/Military_officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Military officer">military officers</a> in the Department of the Navy are the <a href="/wiki/Chief_of_Naval_Operations" title="Chief of Naval Operations">Chief of Naval Operations</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Commandant_of_the_Marine_Corps" title="Commandant of the Marine Corps">Commandant of the Marine Corps</a>, who are the  principal military advisors to the Secretary of the Navy. They supervise their respective military services of the Department of the Navy, and in a separate capacity serve as members of the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Chiefs_of_Staff" title="Joint Chiefs of Staff">Joint Chiefs of Staff</a>. They are assisted by a <a href="/wiki/Vice_Chief_of_Naval_Operations" title="Vice Chief of Naval Operations">Vice Chief of Naval Operations</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Assistant_Commandant_of_the_Marine_Corps" title="Assistant Commandant of the Marine Corps">Assistant Commandant of the Marine Corps</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Department_of_the_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Unlike its U.S. Army and U.S. Air Force counterparts, the Department of the Navy comprises two <a href="/wiki/Uniformed_services_of_the_United_States" title="Uniformed services of the United States">uniformed services</a>: the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a> (sometimes collectively called the "naval services" or "sea services").<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Department of the Navy consists of all elements of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a>. According to <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy_Regulations" title="United States Navy Regulations">Navy Regulations</a> Section 0204-2, the term "Navy Department" refers only to the executive offices at the seat of government.
</p><p>The Department of the Navy is composed of the following:<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>All field activities, headquarters, forces, bases, installations, activities, and functions under the control or supervision of the Secretary of the Navy; and</li>
<li>When it is operating as a service in the Navy Department, the Coast Guard. (Ordinarily part of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Homeland_Security" title="United States Department of Homeland Security">Department of Homeland Security</a>, federal law provides that the Coast Guard may be transferred to the Department of the Navy by the President at any time, or by <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a> during time of war)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Headquarters_Marine_Corps" title="Headquarters Marine Corps">Headquarters Marine Corps</a>;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Office_of_the_Chief_of_Naval_Operations" class="mw-redirect" title="Office of the Chief of Naval Operations">Office of the Chief of Naval Operations</a>, also known as OPNAV or the Navy Staff;</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Office_of_the_Secretary_of_the_Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="Office of the Secretary of the Navy">Office of the Secretary of the Navy</a>, also known as the Secretariat;</li>
<li>The entire operating forces of the Navy (including naval aviation) and the Marine Corps, including both the active and <a href="/wiki/Reserve_components_of_the_United_States_armed_forces" class="mw-redirect" title="Reserve components of the United States armed forces">reserve components</a> (the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy_Reserve" title="United States Navy Reserve">Navy Reserve</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps_Reserve" title="United States Marine Corps Reserve">Marine Corps Reserve</a>) of those forces.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_redesignation">Proposed redesignation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=United_States_Department_of_the_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Proposed redesignation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A provision in the initial <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">House of Representatives</a> bill (H.R. 1585) for the fiscal year 2008 <a href="/wiki/National_Defense_Authorization_Act" title="National Defense Authorization Act">national defense authorization</a> would have renamed the Department of the Navy as the "Department of the Navy and Marine Corps". The bill passed in the House on 17 May 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-2008NDAA_USHouse_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2008NDAA_USHouse-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> but encountered opposition among members of the DoD civilian leadership and among senior Navy admirals and Marine Corps generals.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">Senate</a>, the provision was replaced in S. Amdt. 2011, an amendment in the nature of a substitute proposed by Senator <a href="/wiki/Carl_Levin" title="Carl Levin">Carl Levin</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a> on 9 July 2007 and agreed to by <a href="/wiki/Unanimous_consent" title="Unanimous consent">unanimous consent</a> on 1 October 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The amendment removed the renaming provision and also made other changes. The House version including the provision was withdrawn in <a href="/wiki/United_States_congressional_conference_committee" title="United States congressional conference committee">conference committee</a> and so was not included in the final <a href="/wiki/National_Defense_Authorization_Act_for_Fiscal_Year_2008" title="National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2008">National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2008</a>.
</p>
