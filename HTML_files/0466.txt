<p><b>Humphrey August Mijnals</b> (21 December 1930 ‚Äì 27 July 2019)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who played international football for both the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_football_team" title="Suriname national football team">Suriname</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Humphrey_Mijnals&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mijnals began his career with the Surinamese football club <a href="/wiki/S.V._Robinhood" title="S.V. Robinhood">S.V. Robinhood</a> in the 1950s, where he was one of the stars of the team and won four national titles.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Then he played for six months for <a href="/wiki/Am%C3%A9rica_Futebol_Clube_(PE)" class="mw-redirect" title="Am√©rica Futebol Clube (PE)">Am√©rica Futebol Clube (PE)</a> in Brazil. After this he returned to Suriname. Following his transfer in 1956 to <a href="/wiki/USV_Elinkwijk" title="USV Elinkwijk">USV Elinkwijk</a>, he received a signing-on fee of $15,000 while his club received a transfer fee of 3000 guilders. The club contracted four more Surinamese footballers; his brother <a href="/wiki/Frank_Mijnals" title="Frank Mijnals">Frank Mijnals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Erwin_Sparendam" title="Erwin Sparendam">Erwin Sparendam</a>, Michel Kruin and Charly Marbach, collectively known as the "five-leaf clover". He was then transferred to <a href="/wiki/VV_DOS" title="VV DOS">VV DOS</a>, remaining in Utrecht.
</p><p>He debuted in the Dutch football team on 3 April 1960 in a home match against <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria_national_football_team" title="Bulgaria national football team">Bulgaria</a>. Mijnals, nicknamed <i>Minna</i>, is best known for clearing a Bulgarian attempt on goal with an overhead kick.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In total, Mijnals played just three matches for the Netherlands, including a match against Suriname, because he came into conflict with the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Football_Association" title="Royal Dutch Football Association">KNVB</a>. The conflict arose after a training trip to South and Central America; he complained about the game and selection policy to a journalist, which resulted in him being placed on the list of unwanted players by the association.
</p><p>Then he played 45 times in the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_football_team" title="Suriname national football team">Suriname national football team</a>. In 1999, he was elected Surinamese footballer of the century. On 24 August 2008, Mijnals also received Sports Medal of the city of Utrecht.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
