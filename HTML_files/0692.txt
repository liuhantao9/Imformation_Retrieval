<p>The <b>Economic Cooperation Organization</b> or <b>ECO</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> political and economic <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organization" title="Intergovernmental organization">intergovernmental organization</a> which was founded in 1985 in <a href="/wiki/Tehran" title="Tehran">Tehran</a> by the leaders of <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. It provides a platform to discuss ways to improve development and promote trade and investment opportunities. The ECO is an <i><a href="/wiki/Ad_hoc" title="Ad hoc">ad hoc</a></i> organisation under the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Charter">United Nations Charter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The objective is to establish a <a href="/wiki/Single_market" title="Single market">single market</a> for goods and services, much like the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tehrantimes.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tehrantimes.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The ECO's secretariat and cultural department are located in <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, its economic bureau is in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> and its scientific bureau is situated in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>.
</p><p>The nature of the ECO is that it consists of predominantly Muslim-majority states as it is a trade bloc for the Central Asian states connected to the Mediterranean through Turkey, to the Persian Gulf via Iran, and to the Arabian sea via Pakistan. The current framework of the ECO expresses itself mostly in the form of bilateral agreements and arbitration mechanisms between individual and fully sovereign member states. This makes the ECO similar to <a href="/wiki/ASEAN" class="mw-redirect" title="ASEAN">ASEAN</a> in that it is an organisation that has its own offices and bureaucracy for implementation of trade amongst sovereign member states.
</p><p>This consists of the historically integrated agricultural region of the <a href="/wiki/Ferghana_Valley" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferghana Valley">Ferghana Valley</a> which allows for trade and common agricultural production in the border region of Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Uzbekistan and Turkmenistan. Free trade agreements between the industrial nations of Iran and Turkey are due to be signed<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (October 2017)">dubious</span></a>&#32;<span class="metadata"> &#8211; <a href="/wiki/Talk:Economic_Cooperation_Organization#Dubious" title="Talk:Economic Cooperation Organization">discuss</a></span></i>&#93;</sup> in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Likewise the <a href="/wiki/Pakistan-Turkey_Free_Trade_Agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="Pakistan-Turkey Free Trade Agreement">Pakistan-Turkey Free Trade Agreement</a> is due to be signed<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (October 2017)">dubious</span></a>&#32;<span class="metadata"> &#8211; <a href="/wiki/Talk:Economic_Cooperation_Organization#Dubious" title="Talk:Economic Cooperation Organization">discuss</a></span></i>&#93;</sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Pakistan has free trade agreements with both Afghanistan and Iran which are signed and are in the process of implementation, and currently most of Afghanistan trade is through Pakistan. And the <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan-Pakistan_Transit_Trade_Agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="Afghanistan-Pakistan Transit Trade Agreement">Afghanistan-Pakistan Transit Trade Agreement</a> is designed to facilitate trade for goods and services for Central Asia via both Afghanistan and Pakistan.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This is in addition to the <a href="/wiki/Ashgabat_agreement" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashgabat agreement">Ashgabat agreement</a> which is a multi-modal transport agreement between the Central Asian states.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Further cooperation amongst members is planned in the form of the <a href="/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Pakistan_gas_pipeline" title="Iran–Pakistan gas pipeline">Iran–Pakistan gas pipeline</a>, as well as a Turkmenistan–Afghanistan–Pakistan pipeline. Current pipelines include the <a href="/wiki/Tabriz%E2%80%93Ankara_pipeline" title="Tabriz–Ankara pipeline">Tabriz–Ankara pipeline</a> in addition to the planned <a href="/wiki/Persian_Pipeline" title="Persian Pipeline">Persian Pipeline</a>. This is in addition to the transportation of oil and gas from resource rich Central Asian states such as Kazakshtan and Turkmenistan of minerals and agriculture that complements the industrialisation underway in Iran, Pakistan and Turkey. Pakistan plans to diversify its source of oil and gas supplies towards the Central Asian states including petroleum import contracts with Azerbaijan.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Economic Cooperation Organization was the successor organisation of what was the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Cooperation_for_Development" title="Regional Cooperation for Development">Regional Cooperation for Development</a> (RCD), founded in 1964, which ended activities in 1979. In 1985 Iran, Pakistan and Turkey joined to form the ECO. By the fall of 1992, the ECO expanded to include seven new members; <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyzstan" title="Kyrgyzstan">Kyrgyzstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>. The date of the expansion to its present strength, 28 November, is referred to as "ECO Day". The status and power of the ECO is growing. However, the organisation faces many challenges. Most importantly, the member states are lacking appropriate infrastructure and institutions which the Organization is primarily seeking to develop, to make full use of the available resources in the region and provide sustainable development for the member nations. The Economic Cooperation Organisation Trade Agreement (ECOTA) was signed on 17 July 2003 in <a href="/wiki/Islamabad" title="Islamabad">Islamabad</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> ECO Trade Promotion Organization (TPO) is a new organization for trade promotion among member states located in Iran (2009).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Under the agreement reached between ECO members, the common trade market should be established by 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-tehrantimes.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tehrantimes.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Objectives_.26_Principles_of_Cooperation"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Objectives_&amp;_Principles_of_Cooperation">Objectives &amp; Principles of Cooperation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Objectives &amp; Principles of Cooperation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Sustainable economic development of Member States;</li>
<li>Progressive removal of trade barriers and promotion of intraregional trade; the Greater role of ECO region in the growth of world trade; Gradual integration of the economies of the Member States with the world economy;</li>
<li>Development of transport &amp; communications infrastructure linking the Member States with each other and with the outside world;</li>
<li>Economic liberalization and privatization;</li>
<li>Mobilization and utilization of ECO region's material resources;</li>
<li>Effective utilization of the agricultural and industrial potentials of ECO region.</li>
<li>Regional cooperation for drug abuse control, ecological and environmental protection and strengthening of historical and cultural ties among the peoples of the ECO region; and</li>
<li>Mutually beneficial cooperation with regional and international organizations.</li>
<li>Sovereign equality of the Member States and mutual advantage;</li>
<li>Linking of national economic, development plans with ECO's immediate and long-term objectives to the extent possible;</li>
<li>Joint efforts to gain freer access to markets outside the ECO region for the raw materials and finished products of the Member States;</li>
<li>Effective utilization of ECO institutions, agreements and cooperative arrangements with other regional and international organizations including multilateral financial institutions;</li>
<li>Common endeavors to develop a harmonized approach for participation in regional and global arrangements;</li>
<li>Realization of economic cooperation strategy; and Exchanges in educational, scientific, technical and cultural fields</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Council_of_Ministers">Council of Ministers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Council of Ministers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Council of Ministers (COM) is the highest policy and decision-making body and is composed of the various Ministers of Foreign Affairs or such other representatives of the ministerial rank as may be designated by the respective governments. The COM meets at least once a year by rotation among the member states.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Council_of_Permanent_Representatives">Council of Permanent Representatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Council of Permanent Representatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Council of Permanent Representatives (CPR) consists of the Permanent Representatives/Ambassadors of the member states accredited to the Islamic Republic of Iran as well as to the ECO and the Director-General for ECO Affairs of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Islamic Republic of Iran.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_Planning_Council">Regional Planning Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Regional Planning Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Regional Planning Council (RPC) is composed of the Head of the Planning Organization of member states or such other representatives of corresponding authorities.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="General_Secretariat">General Secretariat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: General Secretariat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The General Secretariat (GS) consists of six directorates under the supervision of the Secretary-General and his deputies. Two specialized agencies and six regional institutes are acting under the supervision of the GS.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/w/index.php?title=ECO_Directorate_of_Industry_%26_Agriculture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ECO Directorate of Industry &amp; Agriculture (page does not exist)">ECO Directorate of Industry &amp; Agriculture</a></li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=ECO_Directorate_of_Trade_%26_Investment&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ECO Directorate of Trade &amp; Investment (page does not exist)">ECO Directorate of Trade &amp; Investment</a></li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=ECO_Directorate_of_Energy,_Mineral_%26_Environment&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ECO Directorate of Energy, Mineral &amp; Environment (page does not exist)">ECO Directorate of Energy, Mineral &amp; Environment</a></li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=ECO_Directorate_of_Transport_%26_Communications&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ECO Directorate of Transport &amp; Communications (page does not exist)">ECO Directorate of Transport &amp; Communications</a></li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=ECO_Directorate_of_Economic_Research_%26_Statistics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ECO Directorate of Economic Research &amp; Statistics (page does not exist)">ECO Directorate of Economic Research &amp; Statistics</a></li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=ECO_Directorate_of_Project_Research_%26_Development&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ECO Directorate of Project Research &amp; Development (page does not exist)">ECO Directorate of Project Research &amp; Development</a></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Activities">Activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Activities of ECO are conducted through directorates under the supervision of Secretary-General and his Deputies which considered and evolve projects and programs of mutual benefit in the fields of:
</p>
<ul><li>Trade and Investment</li>
<li>Transport and Telecommunications</li>
<li>Energy, Minerals and Environment</li>
<li>Agriculture, Industry and Tourism</li>
<li>Human Resources &amp; Sustainable Development</li>
<li>Project &amp; Economic Research and Statistics</li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ECO_Chamber_of_Commerce_and_Industry">ECO Chamber of Commerce and Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: ECO Chamber of Commerce and Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>ECO-CCI was established on 10 June 1993. Its objectives are to contribute to enhancing economic cooperation and relations in trade, industry, agriculture, tourism, contracting, engineering and banking sectors as well as to realize joint investments among the Member States. National Chambers of Afghanistan, Azerbaijan, Iran, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Pakistan, Tajikistan, and Turkey are members of ECO-CCI.
The 7th General Assembly Meeting of ECO Chamber of Commerce and Industry (ECO-CCI), held on 20 April 2004 in Kabul, Afghanistan. The participating member states offered some proposals for developing new mechanism and modalities for better interaction between member chambers and to re-activate ECO-CCI.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ECO_Reinsurance_Company">ECO Reinsurance Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: ECO Reinsurance Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 1995, Iran, Pakistan, and Turkey agreed to establish ECO Reinsurance Company. The purpose is to supplement the existing reinsurance services in the region, promote the growth of the national underwriting and retention capacities, minimize the outflow of foreign exchange from the region and to support economic development in the region. The three-member countries decided to form a Trilateral Interim Committee to pave the way for the establishment of this important institution. The Trilateral Interim Committee in its various meetings reviewed the relevant issues such as the development of the business plan and signing of the Articles of Agreement already finalized by a group of Experts from the three founding member countries.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="ECO_Consultancy_.26_Engineering_Company"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="ECO_Consultancy_&amp;_Engineering_Company">ECO Consultancy &amp; Engineering Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: ECO Consultancy &amp; Engineering Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Governments of all the ECO Member States has established a central resource pool in the shape of ECO Consultancy and Engineering Company (Pvt.) Ltd., or ECO-CEC, to assist in the development projects sponsored by the ECO Member States or by its Trade and Development Bank. The founder States are the Islamic Republic of Iran, Islamic Republic of Pakistan, and Republic of Turkey which holds an equal share in ECO-CEC, Turkey being represented by two companies and Iran and Pakistan, by one each.
The Iranian and Turkish Companies specialize mainly in oil and gas pipelines, refineries, petrochemical and industrial engineering, while the Pakistani partner in all other fields of development engineering, including communications, power, urban development public health, telecommunications, water resources development and agriculture. ECO-CEC provides its expertise in the entire range of consultancy operations, starting from conception, project planning and appraisal, through pre-feasibility, feasibility and financial studies, investigation and exploration, site selection to engineering design, material and equipment specifications, construction supervision, contract management, quality control and preparation of technical manuals for the operation and maintenance of the projects.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ECO_Trade_and_Development_Bank">ECO Trade and Development Bank</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: ECO Trade and Development Bank">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Economic Cooperation Organization Trade and Development Bank (ETDB) was established by the three founding members of the Economic Cooperation Organization (ECO) in 2005 which are the Islamic Republic of Iran, Islamic Republic of Pakistan and Republic of Turkey. The Republic of Azerbaijan, the Islamic Republic of Afghanistan and the Kyrgyz Republic became the member of the ETDB in 2013, 2014 and 2015 respectively.
</p><p>As of 31 December 2015, paid in the share capital of the ETDB was SDR 310,870 thousand since Azerbaijan, Afghanistan, and Kyrgyzstan are in process of payment of their paid-in capital contributions.
</p><p>The Bank has successfully started its operations in 2008. Its headquarters is in Istanbul (Turkey) and representative offices are in Karachi (Pakistan) and Tehran (Iran). The primary objective of the Bank is to provide financial resources for projects and programmes in member countries. The Bank offers a range of medium-to-long term products i.e. project finance, corporate finance, trade finance and loans to support small and medium-sized enterprises directly or through financial intermediaries to private and state-owned entities. 11
</p>
<h3><span id="ECO_Cultural_Institute_.28ECI.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="ECO_Cultural_Institute_(ECI)">ECO Cultural Institute (ECI)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: ECO Cultural Institute (ECI)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>ECO Cultural Institute (ECI) is affiliated with ECO and aims at fostering understanding and the preservation of the rich <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage" title="Cultural heritage">cultural heritage</a> of its members through common projects in the field of the <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">media</a>, <a href="/wiki/Literature" title="Literature">literature</a>, <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sport" title="Sport">sport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ECO_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECO-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_other_organizations">Relationship with other organizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Economic_Cooperation_Organization&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Relationship with other organizations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
