<p><b>Brighouse</b> is a town within the <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Metropolitan Borough of Calderdale</a>, in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> within the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, it is situated on the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 4 miles (6.4&#160;km) east of <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>. It is served by Junction 25 of the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62 motorway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brighouse_railway_station" title="Brighouse railway station">Brighouse railway station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Caldervale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Caldervale Line">Caldervale Line</a> and <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Line">Huddersfield Line</a>.  In the town centre is a mooring basin on the <a href="/wiki/Calder_and_Hebble_Navigation" title="Calder and Hebble Navigation">Calder and Hebble Navigation</a>. The <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">United Kingdom Census 2001</a> gave the Brighouse / Rastrick subdivision of the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Urban_Area" title="West Yorkshire Urban Area">West Yorkshire Urban Area</a> a population of 32,360.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Brighouse ward of Calderdale Council gave a population of 11,195 at the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The name Brighouse (or "Bridge House") originates from a building on (or close to) the bridge over the River Calder. In its early history, it was a <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(place)" title="Hamlet (place)">hamlet</a> of the nearby village of <a href="/wiki/Rastrick" title="Rastrick">Rastrick</a>.
</p><p>Brighouse is <a href="/wiki/Sister_cities" class="mw-redirect" title="Sister cities">twinned</a> with <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCdenscheid" title="Lüdenscheid">Lüdenscheid</a> in Germany, the link beginning with an exchange by Brighouse Children's Theatre in 1950 followed by a civic twinning charter in 1960.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brighouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Brighouse_bridge_019.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Brighouse_bridge_019.jpg/220px-Brighouse_bridge_019.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="96" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Brighouse_bridge_019.jpg/330px-Brighouse_bridge_019.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/Brighouse_bridge_019.jpg/440px-Brighouse_bridge_019.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="1596" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Brighouse_bridge_019.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rastrick_Bridge" title="Rastrick Bridge">Rastrick Bridge</a></div></div></div>
<p>There was a <a href="/wiki/Ford_(river)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ford (river)">river crossing</a> called Snake Hill Ford across the Calder – believed to have formed part of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Britain" title="Roman Britain">Roman</a> route between <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>. A wooden structure called <a href="/wiki/Rastrick_Bridge" title="Rastrick Bridge">Rastrick Bridge</a> was recorded in 1275.  The bridge was replaced by another built with timber donated by John Hanson in 1514.  Hanson's son funded a stone replacement for this bridge in 1558. The river provided power for the flour milling industry and the <a href="/wiki/Textile" title="Textile">textile</a> mills. Brighouse's industry received a boost through the <a href="/wiki/Construction" title="Construction">construction</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Calder_and_Hebble_Navigation" title="Calder and Hebble Navigation">Calder and Hebble Navigation</a>, started in 1757 by the engineer <a href="/wiki/John_Smeaton" title="John Smeaton">John Smeaton</a>. The town was incorporated in 1893.<sup id="cite_ref-EB1911_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Halifax and Huddersfield Turnpike Act of 1823 allowed for the building of Calder Bridge, or <a href="/wiki/Brighouse_Bridge" title="Brighouse Bridge">Brighouse Bridge</a>, over the river on what was to become the <a href="/wiki/A_roads_in_Zone_6_of_the_Great_Britain_numbering_scheme" title="A roads in Zone 6 of the Great Britain numbering scheme">A641 road</a>; tolls were abolished on the bridge in 1875 and extensive widening work was undertaken in 1905 and 1999 (both of these latter dates being commemorated in dedication stones on the bridge).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brighouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="center"><div class="thumb tnone"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:602px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Brighouse_%26_Rastrick,_viewed_from_Thornhills_Lane,UK,(RLH).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Brighouse_%26_Rastrick%2C_viewed_from_Thornhills_Lane%2CUK%2C%28RLH%29.JPG/600px-Brighouse_%26_Rastrick%2C_viewed_from_Thornhills_Lane%2CUK%2C%28RLH%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="600" height="228" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Brighouse_%26_Rastrick%2C_viewed_from_Thornhills_Lane%2CUK%2C%28RLH%29.JPG/900px-Brighouse_%26_Rastrick%2C_viewed_from_Thornhills_Lane%2CUK%2C%28RLH%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Brighouse_%26_Rastrick%2C_viewed_from_Thornhills_Lane%2CUK%2C%28RLH%29.JPG/1200px-Brighouse_%26_Rastrick%2C_viewed_from_Thornhills_Lane%2CUK%2C%28RLH%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2044" data-file-height="778" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Brighouse_%26_Rastrick,_viewed_from_Thornhills_Lane,UK,(RLH).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Brighouse, viewed from Thornhills Lane, Clifton</div></div></div></div>
<p>Brighouse was historically part of the <a href="/wiki/Township_(England)" title="Township (England)">township</a> of Hipperholme with Brighouse in the large ancient parish of Halifax.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The township became a <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> in 1866, but was abolished in 1894 and divided:  Brighouse became a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Municipal Borough">Municipal Borough</a> (and civil parish), <a href="/wiki/Hipperholme" title="Hipperholme">Hipperholme</a> became an <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban district</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norwood_Green,_West_Yorkshire" title="Norwood Green, West Yorkshire">Norwood Green</a> and Coley became a civil parish in Halifax Rural District. The borough was expanded in 1915 when Rastrick was absorbed, and in 1937 when Clifton CP, Hipperholme UD, Norwood Green and Coley CP and part of Southowram UD were added.  Brighouse borough and civil parish was abolished in 1974 when it became part of Calderdale Metropolitan Borough.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The mayor's mace was removed in 1974 to the Town Hall, Halifax, where it is displayed in the mayor's parlour to denote the authority of the Halifax mayor.<sup id="cite_ref-Halifax_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Halifax-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Brighouse_Town_Board">Brighouse Town Board</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brighouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Brighouse Town Board">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Calderdale_Metropolitan_Borough_Council" title="Calderdale Metropolitan Borough Council">Calderdale Council</a> established Town Boards to assist with regenerating Calderdale's Town Centres. The boards are made up of local councillors and community representatives. The first <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.calderdale.gov.uk/v2/residents/environment-planning-and-building/regeneration/brighouse-development-board">Brighouse Town Board</a>&#160;meeting (Tuesday, March 28th 2017 ) appointed Councillors from Brighouse, Hipperholme &amp; Lightclifee, Rastrick Wards,&#160;and community members, who represent local business and residents. Brighouse Town Board works with public, private and voluntary organisations, and takes a strategic view of the needs and opportunities of the local area<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>

<p>Brighouse Library is a large building and in addition to its normal lending library services provides facilities for language courses; internet access and fax facilities. Like most public buildings wheelchair access is provided. There is also a small cafe and a lift for access to all floors.
</p><p>The main shopping streets are Commercial Street and Bethel Street. Shops include <a href="/wiki/Wilko_(retailer)" title="Wilko (retailer)">Wilko</a>, <a href="/wiki/W_Boyes_%26_Co" class="mw-redirect" title="W Boyes &amp; Co">Boyes</a>, Czerwik (a specialist cheese and wine shop), Lords Photography, Harrison Lord Gallery &amp; Studio, Domino's Pizza, Just Books, Design 55, Superdrug, Boots, Hallmarks, Thorntons, M&amp;Co and Jack Fulton. In early 2000, a financial agreement made between <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a> and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20080917185626/http://www.bsc-brighouse.co.uk/">Brighouse Sports Club</a>, allowed for a huge new Tesco superstore to be built on the site of the former club, and the old Tesco became Wilkos. The deal also enabled the sports club to build new premises almost a mile away (1.5&#160;km) on land at Russell Way, <a href="/wiki/Bailiff_Bridge" title="Bailiff Bridge">Bailiff Bridge</a>.

Plus a micropub the Market Tavern among others. Restaurants include Meze and Pregos for Mediterranean food. Plus the Royal Delhi for curry.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brighouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Martin%27s_Church,_Brighouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_150077.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/St_Martin%27s_Church%2C_Brighouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_150077.jpg/220px-St_Martin%27s_Church%2C_Brighouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_150077.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="163" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/St_Martin%27s_Church%2C_Brighouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_150077.jpg/330px-St_Martin%27s_Church%2C_Brighouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_150077.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/St_Martin%27s_Church%2C_Brighouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_150077.jpg/440px-St_Martin%27s_Church%2C_Brighouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_150077.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="473" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Martin%27s_Church,_Brighouse_-_geograph.org.uk_-_150077.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St Martin's Church</div></div></div>

<p>St James's Church, which was a <a href="/wiki/Chapel_of_ease" title="Chapel of ease">chapel of ease</a> for the Parish Church of Saint Martin in Brighouse, opened in 1870, with a congregation of 450. An organ was installed in the same year. Two <a href="/wiki/Stained_glass" title="Stained glass">stained glass</a> windows from the <a href="/wiki/William_Morris" title="William Morris">William Morris</a> factory, designed by <a href="/wiki/Edward_Burne-Jones" title="Edward Burne-Jones">Edward Burne-Jones</a> were installed in July 1871.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Further windows were added at later dates by other designers. In 1900 William Camm<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> the organist paid for the fitting of a window in memory of his mother. A further window to commemorate the curate Rev Alban Bodley Mace (1915), who was killed in action at <a href="/wiki/History_of_Thessaloniki#Balkan_Wars_and_World_War_I" title="History of Thessaloniki">Salonika</a> during 1916, serving with the Allied <a href="/wiki/Expeditionary_warfare" title="Expeditionary warfare">Expeditionary Force</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The chancel screen was designed by G. H. Woodhouse of Manchester. St James Church was last used on 27 December 1970; and then demolished, with the land used for residential properties. The stained glass windows were removed and taken into the care of the <a href="/wiki/Cliffe_Castle_Museum" title="Cliffe Castle Museum">Cliffe Castle Museum</a> in <a href="/wiki/Keighley" title="Keighley">Keighley</a> where they can now be viewed. St Joseph's Catholic Church was built in the early 1900s, on Martin Street. In 1879 a <a href="/wiki/Primary_school" title="Primary school">primary school</a> was established, using the church hall, from Monday to Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It remained in use until 4 September 1961 when a new school was built, on Finkel Street.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>  The Central Methodist Church is situated at the west end of Brighouse backing on to the Ludenschied Link with a side entry at the lower part of Parsonage Lane. The frontage overlooks a small car park.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arts_and_media">Arts and media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brighouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Arts and media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smith Art Gallery is in the Brighouse Library and features many paintings from the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian era</a> displayed in the main hall, in addition to a changing programme of exhibitions of artwork by local artists.
</p><p>Brighouse was the original home of the <a href="/wiki/Brighouse_and_Rastrick_Brass_Band" title="Brighouse and Rastrick Brass Band">Brighouse and Rastrick Brass Band</a>, founded in 1881 and associated with neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Rastrick" title="Rastrick">Rastrick</a>. They originally formed under the name the <i>Brighouse and Rastrick Temperance Brass Band</i> taking their current title in the early 20th century.
They are currently based next to Brighouse High School in Hove Edge, just north of Brighouse. In 1977 the band recorded "<a href="/wiki/The_Floral_Dance" title="The Floral Dance">The Floral Dance</a>" which reached Number 2 in the <a href="/wiki/UK_Top_40" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Top 40">UK Top 40</a> and stayed there for 6 weeks. In 1968, the band won the inaugural Brass Band World Championship, and in 1993 became the first Yorkshire team to win the All England Masters Brass Band Championship, a feat repeated in 1998. The band won the National Brass Band Championship Finals in 1946, 1968, 1969, 1973, 1980, 1997, 1998, 2010, 2011 and 2017, and the European Championships in 1981 and 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town is also home to the Yorkshire Co-op Brass band, formed in 1999 by merger of the Jayess '87 and Jayess Queensbury bands.
</p><p>The area is home to rock band <a href="/wiki/Embrace_(English_band)" title="Embrace (English band)">Embrace</a>, who have had three UK number one albums. The town is mentioned in "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_Grim_Up_North" title="It&#39;s Grim Up North">It's Grim Up North</a>" by the <a href="/wiki/The_KLF" title="The KLF">Justified Ancients of Mu Mu</a> (The JAMS).
</p><p>A local drum &amp; bugle corps, Conquest Alliance was formed, in 1985, by the merger of the Northern Skyryders and Brighouse RBL (Legion). The following year, 1986, Conquest Alliance appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Drum_Corps_United_Kingdom" title="Drum Corps United Kingdom">Drum Corp United Kingdom (DCUK)</a> field of competition and were declared co-champions of the DCUK.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In 1987 the corps was placed 1st,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> 5th in 1988,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> 2nd in 1989,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> and 4th in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
The corps disbanded in 1990.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brighouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/List_of_defunct_rugby_league_clubs#England" title="List of defunct rugby league clubs">Brighouse Rangers</a> Amateur <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">Rugby league</a> Club, founded in 1878, are based at the Brighouse Sports Club Complex on Russell Way (Off A641 Bradford Road) between Brighouse town centre and Bailiff Bridge. The club competes at amateur level in the <a href="/wiki/Pennine_League" title="Pennine League">Pennine League</a>'s Second Division and fields a reserve side in Division Six. There is a junior section for under 18's, down to Under 8's, and a ladies side.
</p><p>Before the <a href="/wiki/History_of_rugby_league#The_schism_in_England" title="History of rugby league">1895 schism in English rugby</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Nicholl" title="William Nicholl">William Nicholl</a> played rugby union for <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_union_team" title="England national rugby union team">England</a>, and Brighouse Rangers. After the schism, Brighouse Rangers became a founder member of the Northern Rugby Football Union (now <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_League" title="Rugby Football League">Rugby Football League</a>), and the first Northern Rugby Football Union chairman was Mr. H. H. Waller from the club. Brighouse Rangers played from <a href="/wiki/1895%E2%80%9396_Northern_Rugby_Football_Union_season" title="1895–96 Northern Rugby Football Union season">1895–96</a> to <a href="/wiki/1905%E2%80%9306_Northern_Rugby_Football_Union_season" title="1905–06 Northern Rugby Football Union season">1905–06</a> winning the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_county_leagues" title="Rugby league county leagues">Yorkshire League</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1896%E2%80%9397_Northern_Rugby_Football_Union_season" title="1896–97 Northern Rugby Football Union season">1896–97</a> season. Although finishing last in the 14-club elite league in the <a href="/wiki/1901%E2%80%9302_Northern_Rugby_Football_Union_season" title="1901–02 Northern Rugby Football Union season">1901–02</a> season, the club was not relegated as the league expanded to 18 clubs. In the <a href="/wiki/1902%E2%80%9303_Northern_Rugby_Football_Union_season" title="1902–03 Northern Rugby Football Union season">1902–03</a> season Brighouse finished 18th and was relegated. After finishing bottom of the 31-club recombined league in the <a href="/wiki/1905%E2%80%9306_Northern_Rugby_Football_Union_season" title="1905–06 Northern Rugby Football Union season">1905–06</a> season, the club withdrew from the Northern Union.
</p><p>As of the 2018-19 season, <a href="/wiki/Brighouse_Town_FC" class="mw-redirect" title="Brighouse Town FC">Brighouse Town F.C.</a> play in the Evostik Division One East at level 8 of the <a href="/wiki/National_League_System" title="National League System">National League System</a> (also known as 'The Football Pyramid'). Brighouse has its own Sunday League football competition, which in the 2007–08 season had one division of 10 teams. Junction (Rastrick) were crowned champions on 20 April 2008.
There are many Junior football teams in the area too such as Brighouse Juniors, which was started by parents in 1989, who go from Under 6's up to Under 17's.
</p><p>The town is on the route of the 50 mile <a href="/wiki/Calderdale_Way" title="Calderdale Way">Calderdale Way</a> circular walk around the hills and valleys of Calderdale.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brighouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/A641_road" class="mw-redirect" title="A641 road">A641</a> northward links Brighouse with <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a> and southward with <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>. The <a href="/wiki/A644_road" class="mw-redirect" title="A644 road">A644</a> runs eastward out of Brighouse to Junction 25 of the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a>. In the opposite direction, the A644 leaves Brighouse to the north-west towards <a href="/wiki/Hipperholme" title="Hipperholme">Hipperholme</a>, where it crosses the <a href="/wiki/A58_road" title="A58 road">A58</a> Leeds-Halifax road, and continues through <a href="/wiki/Queensbury,_West_Yorkshire" title="Queensbury, West Yorkshire">Queensbury</a> to meet the <a href="/wiki/A629_road" title="A629 road">A629</a> for <a href="/wiki/Keighley" title="Keighley">Keighley</a>. The <a href="/wiki/A643_road" title="A643 road">A643</a> and <a href="/wiki/A6025_road" class="mw-redirect" title="A6025 road">A6025</a> provide less important local links, although the A643 is an alternative route through to Leeds.
</p><p>A bypass, originally called Elland Road, then renamed <a href="/wiki/L%C3%BCdenscheid" title="Lüdenscheid">Lüdenscheid</a> Link in the 1980s, after Brighouse's twin town in Germany, was constructed in the 1970s to clear heavy traffic from the narrow streets in the town centre.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Brighouse_railway_station" title="Brighouse railway station">Brighouse railway station</a> reopened in 2000 and provides the town with access to the National Rail network. All services are sponsored by Metro and provided by <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a>. The current service is part of both the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Line">Huddersfield Line</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Calder_Valley_line" title="Calder Valley line">Calder Valley line</a>, with services on the Huddersfield Line between Leeds and Manchester Victoria and services on the Caldervale Line between Leeds and Wakefield Westgate via Bradford, Halifax and Huddersfield. Direct services to London Kings Cross are also available, with four services per day provided by <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a>.
</p><p>Bus services operate frequently to Halifax and Huddersfield. Plus Bradford (from where easy connections can be made to the city of Leeds using the X6 and 72 services). These are coordinated by <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Metro" title="West Yorkshire Metro">West Yorkshire Metro</a>. Services are operated by <a href="/wiki/First_Bradford" title="First Bradford">First Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/First_Calderdale_%26_Huddersfield" title="First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield">First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Yorkshire Tiger</a>, TLC Travel and TJ Walsh. In addition there was a daytime <a href="/wiki/Tesco" title="Tesco">Tesco</a>-sponsored ShopHopper service, operated by TJ Walsh, around the town centre, linking the bus station to the superstore, unfortunately, this service was removed a few years ago. <a href="/wiki/Brighouse_bus_station" title="Brighouse bus station">Brighouse bus station</a> has been refurbished with funding from Metro and re-opened in May 2009. The bus station is between Commercial Street and the by-pass. The bus station and some bus stops in the Brighouse area are equipped with Real Time Passenger Information Screens.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Calder_and_Hebble_Navigation" title="Calder and Hebble Navigation">Calder and Hebble Navigation</a> links Brighouse with Wakefield (junction with the <a href="/wiki/Aire_and_Calder_Navigation" title="Aire and Calder Navigation">Aire and Calder Navigation</a>), <a href="/wiki/Mirfield" title="Mirfield">Mirfield</a> (junction with the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Broad_Canal" title="Huddersfield Broad Canal">Huddersfield Broad Canal</a>) and Sowerby Bridge (junction with the <a href="/wiki/Rochdale_Canal" title="Rochdale Canal">Rochdale Canal</a>).
</p>
