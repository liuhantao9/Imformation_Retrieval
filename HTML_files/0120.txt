<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logro%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin_of_the_name">Origin of the name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logro%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origin of the name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The origin of this toponym is, as for many other places, unknown. The name <i>Lucronio</i> is first used in a document from 965 where <a href="/wiki/Garc%C3%ADa_S%C3%A1nchez_I_of_Pamplona" title="García Sánchez I of Pamplona">García Sánchez I of Pamplona</a> donates the so-called place to the <a href="/wiki/Monasteries_of_San_Mill%C3%A1n_de_la_Cogolla" title="Monasteries of San Millán de la Cogolla">Monastery of San Millán</a>. In the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Fuero_of_Logro%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Fuero of Logroño (page does not exist)">fuero</a> from 1095 it appears under the name <i>Logronio</i>, except once when it is called <i>illo Gronio</i>. The most broadly accepted theses seem those which indicate it is a late latinización by prefixing of the article "<i>lo/illo</i>" of the old toponym <i>Gronio</i>/<i>Gronno</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> word of Celtic origin which means <i>the ford</i> or <i>the pass</i>. It is believed that this name was due to the frequent use of this place to cross the Ebro river.
</p><p>Other historians have proposed alternative theories, like a possible derivation of <i>Lucus Brun</i>/<i>Lucus Beronius</i> ("Sacred place in the Beronian forest").<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles">Titles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logro%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Titles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/John_II_of_Castile" title="John II of Castile">John II of Castile</a> granted it 7 February 1431 in <a href="/wiki/Palencia" title="Palencia">Palencia</a> the title of "City" and ratified it the day 20 of the very same month in <a href="/wiki/Valladolid" title="Valladolid">Valladolid</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> thus it stopped being called "Village", despite there is no explicit justification of the reasons for that change. 20 July 1444 the same king adds the titles of "Very noble", <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Muy Noble</i>, and "Very loyal", <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>: <i lang="es">Muy Leal</i>, which up until today appear in the seal of the city. In this case, the reason was the loyalty of the habitants against the homonymous king <a href="/wiki/John_II_of_Aragon" title="John II of Aragon">John II of Aragon</a>, because despite the <i>«long war, and wounds and deaths, and robberies, and fires, and damages and oppresions»</i>, the city remained loyal to the service of the king of Castile.
</p><p>5 July 1523, the king <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles I</a> granted it the three <a href="/wiki/Fleur-de-lis" title="Fleur-de-lis">fleurs-de-lis</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Escutcheon_(heraldry)" title="Escutcheon (heraldry)">shield</a> of the city for its resistance during the french siege in 1521.
</p><p>It also received by Royal Decree the title of "Excellence" 6 December 1854 from <a href="/wiki/Isabella_II_of_Spain" title="Isabella II of Spain">Isabella II</a>, as a reward for its behavior during the <a href="/wiki/Cholera" title="Cholera">cholera</a> epidemic which devastated the city.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logro%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Logroño is located in the northern region of <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja_(autonomous_community)" class="mw-redirect" title="La Rioja (autonomous community)">La Rioja</a>, on the river <a href="/wiki/Ebro" title="Ebro">Ebro</a>, 384 metres (1,260 feet) <a href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" class="mw-redirect" title="Above mean sea level">above sea level</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Way_of_St._James" class="mw-redirect" title="Way of St. James">Camino de Santiago</a> passes through the city. The geographical coordinates of the city are: 42° 27′ N, 2° 29′ W.
</p><p>The city lies 152&#160;km (94&#160;mi) from <a href="/wiki/Bilbao" title="Bilbao">Bilbao</a>, 172&#160;km (107&#160;mi) from <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a>, 336&#160;km (209&#160;mi) from <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> and 468&#160;km (291&#160;mi) from <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>.
</p>

<p>Logroño was an old settlement, first of the Romans, under the name of Vareia,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> a commercial port, and then of the Celts<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (December 2015)">dubious</span></a>&#32;<span class="metadata"> &#8211; <a href="/wiki/Talk:Logro%C3%B1o#Dubious" title="Talk:Logroño">discuss</a></span></i>&#93;</sup>.
From the 10th century, possession of Logroño was disputed between the kings of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Navarre" title="Kingdom of Navarre">Navarre</a> and those of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Castile</a>; the region was finally annexed to <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Castile</a>. The name is a combination of le and Groin, mashed together as Logroño over time.  <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_VI_of_Castile" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfonso VI of Castile">Alfonso VI of Castile</a> granted Logroño in 1095 a charter of rights that served as a model for other Spanish cities. In 1609 and 1610 Logroño was the main seat of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_witch_trials" title="Basque witch trials">Basque witch trials</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Inquisition" title="Spanish Inquisition">Spanish Inquisition</a>.
</p><p>Famous people from Logroño include <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Bret%C3%B3n_de_los_Herreros" title="Manuel Bretón de los Herreros">Manuel Bretón de los Herreros</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fausto_Elh%C3%BAyar" class="mw-redirect" title="Fausto Elhúyar">Fausto Elhúyar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pr%C3%A1xedes_Mateo_Sagasta" title="Práxedes Mateo Sagasta">Práxedes Mateo Sagasta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Azcona" title="Rafael Azcona">Rafael Azcona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ram%C3%B3n_Castroviejo" class="mw-redirect" title="Ramón Castroviejo">Ramón Castroviejo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_J._Ram%C3%ADrez" title="Pedro J. Ramírez">Pedro J. Ramírez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Fern%C3%A1ndez_Navarrete" title="Juan Fernández Navarrete">Navarrete "El Mudo"</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logro%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fa%C3%A7ade_of_Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_de_la_Redonda_in_Logro%C3%B1o.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Fa%C3%A7ade_of_Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_de_la_Redonda_in_Logro%C3%B1o.jpg/170px-Fa%C3%A7ade_of_Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_de_la_Redonda_in_Logro%C3%B1o.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Fa%C3%A7ade_of_Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_de_la_Redonda_in_Logro%C3%B1o.jpg/255px-Fa%C3%A7ade_of_Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_de_la_Redonda_in_Logro%C3%B1o.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Fa%C3%A7ade_of_Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_de_la_Redonda_in_Logro%C3%B1o.jpg/340px-Fa%C3%A7ade_of_Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_de_la_Redonda_in_Logro%C3%B1o.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="2134" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fa%C3%A7ade_of_Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_de_la_Redonda_in_Logro%C3%B1o.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Co-Cathedral_of_Logro%C3%B1o" title="Co-Cathedral of Logroño">Con-catedral de Santa María de la Redonda</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Logroño is the shopping and financial capital of <a href="/wiki/La_Rioja_(autonomous_community)" class="mw-redirect" title="La Rioja (autonomous community)">La Rioja</a>. Its economy is heavily reliant on <a href="/wiki/Wine" title="Wine">wine</a>. It is twinned with <a href="/wiki/Dunfermline" title="Dunfermline">Dunfermline</a>, <a href="/wiki/Darmstadt" title="Darmstadt">Darmstadt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Libourne" title="Libourne">Libourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dax,_Landes" title="Dax, Landes">Dax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rancagua" title="Rancagua">Rancagua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_de_La_Rioja" class="mw-redirect" title="Ciudad de La Rioja">Ciudad de La Rioja</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brescia" title="Brescia">Brescia</a>, <a href="/wiki/El_Hagounia" title="El Hagounia">El Hagounia</a>. The new airport <a href="/wiki/Logro%C3%B1o-Agoncillo_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Logroño-Agoncillo Airport">Logroño-Agoncillo</a> connects the city with Madrid.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Food">Food</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logro%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Food">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are over 50 <i>taperías</i> (tapas restaurants) located within a four-block area near the town center. The traditional tapas restaurants often serve only one tapa [such as <i>seta</i> (mushroom), served as <i>pincho</i> – <i>pintxo</i> in <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque</a> – meaning one serving, or <i>media ración</i> ("half portion"), a small plate of tapas, but offer the <a href="/wiki/Rioja_(wine)" title="Rioja (wine)">Rioja D.O.</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logro%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The weather in Logroño – mostly due to its peculiar location, both in terms of distance to the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlantic">Atlantic</a> coast and in the situation along the course of the <a href="/wiki/Ebro_river" class="mw-redirect" title="Ebro river">Ebro river</a>, is characterized by values ranging from those typically found in temperate <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">oceanic</a> climates to the warmer and drier ones observed in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">mediterranean</a> regions of the river's valley. The average annual temperature is 13.9&#160;°C (57&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AEMET.es:_‘Standard_Climate_Values_for_Logroño,_ES’_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AEMET.es:_‘Standard_Climate_Values_for_Logroño,_ES’-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Although much infrequent, unusually low temperatures during the winter can drop to −5&#160;°C (23&#160;°F), and as low as −10&#160;°C (14&#160;°F); while daily high averages may exceptionally exceed the mark of 35&#160;°C (95&#160;°F) in a particularly hot summer. The average annual precipitation is about 400&#160;mm (15.75&#160;in), regularly spread over the whole year: from 20&#160;mm (0.79&#160;in)–25&#160;mm (0.98&#160;in) in drier periods to the 45&#160;mm (1.77&#160;in)-50&#160;mm (1.97&#160;in) range in the rainiest.<sup id="cite_ref-AEMET.es:_‘Standard_Climate_Values_for_Logroño,_ES’_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AEMET.es:_‘Standard_Climate_Values_for_Logroño,_ES’-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>

<ul><li>Paseo del Príncipe de Vergara (El Espolón): Located in the financial center of the capital and positioned in the center by the statue of General <a href="/wiki/Espartero" class="mw-redirect" title="Espartero">Espartero</a>.</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_life">Social life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logro%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Social life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Calle del Laurel, known as "the path of the elephants" and Calle San Juan are typical streets where various restaurants and tapas bars offer some of the best <i>pinchos</i> and <i>tapas</i> in northern Spain. Calle Portales is the main street in the old town, where people like to walk and sit in the terraces to eat a meal or drink wine. Calle Marqués de San Nicolás (otherwise known as Calle Mayor) is the main area where people spend weekend nights.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasting_stations">Broadcasting stations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logro%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Broadcasting stations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Southwest of Logroño, at 42°26'34"N   2°30'43"W, there is a mediumwave broadcasting station with a transmission power of 20&#160;kW. There may exist a longwave transmitter with 300 metres tall masts at Logroño with 300 metres tall masts transmitting RNE Todo Noticias on 207&#160;kHz.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals_and_traditions">Festivals and traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Logro%C3%B1o&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Festivals and traditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Arco_San_Bernabe_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Arco_San_Bernabe_2015.jpg/220px-Arco_San_Bernabe_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Arco_San_Bernabe_2015.jpg/330px-Arco_San_Bernabe_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Arco_San_Bernabe_2015.jpg/440px-Arco_San_Bernabe_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3096" data-file-height="2322" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Arco_San_Bernabe_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Arch of Saint Barnabas.</div></div></div>
<p>The patron saint of Logroño is <a href="/wiki/Mary,_Mother_of_Jesus" class="mw-redirect" title="Mary, Mother of Jesus">Santa María de la Esperanza</a>.
</p><p>The most important festivals are:
</p>
<ul><li>San Bernabé (<a href="/wiki/Saint_Barnabas" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Barnabas">Saint Barnabas</a>), celebrated on June 11, commemorating the victory and resistance of Logroño against <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French invaders</a> under <a href="/wiki/Francis_I_of_France" title="Francis I of France">Francis I</a> that besieged the city in May and June 1521. During this celebration, fried trout is typically served by the Fish Brotherhood, along with bread and wine, allegedly the only foodstuffs available in Logroño during the siege.</li>
<li>San Mateo, celebrated between September 20 and September 26. Since 2006 the celebrations start the Saturday before September 21 (the day of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Matthew" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Matthew">Saint Matthew</a>) and last for a week.</li></ul>
<p>During the first week of January there is a cultural festival known as "ACTUAL" with <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Theater" class="mw-redirect" title="Theater">theater</a> and <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a>.
</p>
