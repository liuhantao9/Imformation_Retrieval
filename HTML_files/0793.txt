<p><b>Benjamin Atwood Smith II</b> (March 26, 1916&#160;&#8211;&#32;September 26, 1991) was a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senator" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Senator">United States Senator</a> from the state of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> from December 1960 until November 1962.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_and_education">Family and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_A._Smith_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smith, who was named for his grandfather Benjamin A. Smith,<sup id="cite_ref-globe_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> was born in <a href="/wiki/Gloucester,_Massachusetts" title="Gloucester, Massachusetts">Gloucester, Massachusetts</a> to R. Russell and Grace Smith.<sup id="cite_ref-globe_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He married Barbara M. (Mechem) of Lake Forest, Illinois and Annisquam, Mass.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> They had five children, sons, R. Russell Smith II and <a href="/wiki/Ben_Smith_(ice_hockey_coach)" title="Ben Smith (ice hockey coach)">Benjamin A. Smith III</a>, an ice hockey player and coach, and daughters, Barbara (Smith) Ramsey, Susan (Smith) Crotty, and Cathleen Smith.
</p><p>Smith attended the Gloucester public schools.<sup id="cite_ref-globe_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Smith was captain of the 1933 Gloucester High School football team under coach Nate Ross.<sup id="cite_ref-globe_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He later graduated from <a href="/wiki/Governor_Dummer_Academy" class="mw-redirect" title="Governor Dummer Academy">Governor Dummer Academy</a> and  <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>. While at Harvard, Smith played fullback on the football team under coach <a href="/wiki/Dick_Harlow" title="Dick Harlow">Dick Harlow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-globe_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At Harvard, Smith was a roommate of <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_service">Military service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_A._Smith_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Military service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smith served in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> for four years<sup id="cite_ref-globe_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. While he was in the Navy, Smith served in the Pacific as Commander on an anti-submarine, anti-torpedo vessel.<sup id="cite_ref-herald_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-herald-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_service">Public service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_A._Smith_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Public service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smith served as a member of the Gloucester School Committee, the Gloucester City Council and was a trustee of the Addison Gilbert Hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-globe_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Smith served as Mayor of Gloucester from 1954 to 1955; however, at the time Smith was mayor, Gloucester had a Plan E form of government. The office of mayor was a ceremonial position, the mayor was a city councilor chosen by that body and the actual city administration was carried out by a professional city manager.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_career">Business career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_A._Smith_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Business career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For many years Smith was the chief executive of his family's business, the Merchants Box Company in Gloucester.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senate_appointment">Senate appointment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_A._Smith_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Senate appointment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>John F. Kennedy resigned from the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">Senate</a> on December 22, 1960, after he was elected <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>. Kennedy, who had been reelected to a second Senate term of six years in 1958, advised Gov. <a href="/wiki/Foster_Furcolo" title="Foster Furcolo">Foster Furcolo</a> of Massachusetts to appoint Smith to fill the vacated seat "in the interest of promoting party unity." Critics said Smith, a close friend of the Kennedy family, was intended to be a "seat-warmer" until the President-elect's brother <a href="/wiki/Edward_M._Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward M. Kennedy">Edward M. Kennedy</a> turned thirty (the minimum age provided by the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Constitution">U.S. Constitution</a> for eligibility to serve in the Senate). Smith served as Senator until November 6, 1962, when Edward Kennedy, his duly elected successor, was chosen in a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_special_election_in_Massachusetts,_1962" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Senate special election in Massachusetts, 1962">special election</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_ambassador">Special ambassador</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_A._Smith_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Special ambassador">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1963, President Kennedy named Smith as the chairman of the U.S. delegation to the North Pacific Fisheries Conference<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> involving the United States, the Soviet Union, Canada and Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-globe_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_and_burial">Death and burial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_A._Smith_II&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Death and burial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Smith died after a long illness in the Addison Gilbert Hospital in Gloucester and was buried in the Calvary Cemetery in Gloucester.<sup id="cite_ref-globe_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
