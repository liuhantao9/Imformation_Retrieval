<p>"<b>Jesus, Take the Wheel</b>" is a song written by <a href="/wiki/Brett_James" title="Brett James">Brett James</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gordie_Sampson" title="Gordie Sampson">Gordie Sampson</a>, and recorded by American country music artist <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>.  It was released in October 2005 as the first single from Underwood's debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Hearts" title="Some Hearts">Some Hearts</a></i>. The ballad tells of a woman seeking help from <a href="/wiki/Jesus" title="Jesus">Jesus</a> in an emergency, ultimately letting Jesus take control of her life.
</p><p>The country-Christian song became a <a href="/wiki/Crossover_hit" class="mw-redirect" title="Crossover hit">crossover hit</a>, spending six consecutive weeks at number one on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a>, peaking at number four on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Christian_Songs" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Christian Songs">Hot Christian Songs</a> chart and charting high on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Adult_Contemporary_(chart)" title="Adult Contemporary (chart)">Adult Contemporary</a> chart. It also became a top twenty hit on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart.
</p><p>"Jesus, Take the Wheel" won Grammys for <a href="/wiki/Best_Female_Country_Vocal_Performance" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Female Country Vocal Performance">Best Female Country Vocal Performance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Best_Country_Song" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Country Song">Best Country Song</a>, and it won Single of the Year at the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music" title="Academy of Country Music">2005 Academy of Country Music Awards</a>. The song ranked number four on CMT's <i>40 Greatest Songs of the Decade.</i>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The song tells the story of a young mother who lives a hectic life. On a late night Christmas Eve drive on a snow-covered road on her way to <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati,_Ohio" class="mw-redirect" title="Cincinnati, Ohio">Cincinnati, Ohio</a>, the woman begins sorting out her emotions and bemoans not having enough time to do the things that really matter. Then, her car hits a patch of <a href="/wiki/Black_ice" title="Black ice">black ice</a>, causing the woman to lose control of her car. She panics, takes her hands off the steering wheel and cries out to Jesus; shortly thereafter, the car stops spinning and safely stops on the shoulder. After taking stock of the situation (and seeing that her baby has remained fast asleep in the rear seat), the woman decides to let "Jesus take the wheel" of her life.
</p>

<p>Underwood gave her debut performance of the song at the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 23, 2006, she again sang it at the 41st annual <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music Awards</a>, where she won the Single of the Year Award for the song.
</p><p>In April 2006, she sang it on the <a href="/wiki/List_of_CMT_Music_Awards_ceremonies#2000s" title="List of CMT Music Awards ceremonies">CMT Awards</a>, where "Jesus, Take The Wheel" won two major awards, Breakthrough Video of the Year and Female Video of the Year.
</p><p>Underwood performed the song during her debut tour: <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood:_Live_in_Concert" title="Carrie Underwood: Live in Concert">Carrie Underwood: Live in Concert</a>, and has subsequently performed the track in all of her tours as well as during her sets at the <a href="/wiki/CMA_Music_Festival" title="CMA Music Festival">CMA Music Festival</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach" title="Stagecoach">Stagecoach</a>, <a href="/wiki/C2C:_Country_to_Country" title="C2C: Country to Country">C2C: Country to Country</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glastonbury_Festival_2019" title="Glastonbury Festival 2019">Glastonbury</a> festivals.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> was Underwoods's first and was directed by Roman White. It features her singing in various backgrounds such as a living room, through shelves, standing by a wall, and sitting in a chair. A woman, a young couple, and an older couple are all shown through the video trying to ease a baby, fighting over bills and making up, and trying to feed his wife respectively.
</p><p>The music video for it was originally slated for release on November 4, 2005 but was delayed and made available at a later date on <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a> <a href="/wiki/Launchcast" class="mw-redirect" title="Launchcast">Launch</a>.
</p><p>"Jesus, Take The Wheel" was nominated for Music Video of the Year at the 2006 <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association" title="Country Music Association">Country Music Association</a> Awards and was ranked No. 64 on CMT's 100 Greatest Videos.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Charts_and_certifications">Charts and certifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Charts and certifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The song debuted at number 39 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard magazine">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart, and eventually hit number one in January 2006, spending six consecutive weeks at the top.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It proved to be a <a href="/wiki/Crossover_hit" class="mw-redirect" title="Crossover hit">crossover hit</a>, eventually making it to number twenty on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart and selling around 2,470,000 <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital downloads</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sales_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sales-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2008, "Jesus, Take the Wheel" was certified Platinum, making Underwood the first country artist ever to have two songs hit Platinum Mastertone status.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In February 2011, "Jesus, Take the Wheel" was certified 3× Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
