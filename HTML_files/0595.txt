<p>The <b>St Paul's Street drill hall</b> is a military installation in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. It is a Grade II <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-listed_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-listed-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St_Paul%27s_Street_drill_hall,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The building was designed by Captain Willey Cooper as the headquarters of the 2nd Volunteer Battalion, The <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Wellington&#39;s Regiment">Duke of Wellington's Regiment</a> and was built between 1899 and 1901.<sup id="cite_ref-listed_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-listed-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was opened by Field Marshal <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Roberts,_1st_Earl_Roberts" title="Frederick Roberts, 1st Earl Roberts">Lord Roberts</a> in May 1901.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The 2nd Volunteer Battalion, The Duke of Wellington's Regiment evolved to become the 5th Battalion, The Duke of Wellington's Regiment in 1908.<sup id="cite_ref-regiments_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regiments-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The battalion was mobilised at the drill hall in August 1914 before being deployed to the <a href="/wiki/Western_Front_(World_War_I)" title="Western Front (World War I)">Western Front</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-trail_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trail-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 5th Battalion, The Duke of Wellington's Regiment converted to become the 43rd (5th Duke of Wellington's Regiment) Anti-Aircraft Battalion, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Engineers" title="Royal Engineers">Royal Engineers</a> in 1936 and, in the midst of the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a>, it evolved to become the 600th Regiment, <a href="/wiki/Royal_Artillery" title="Royal Artillery">Royal Artillery</a> (5th Battalion The Duke of Wellington's Regiment) in 1944, and, after the war, the 578th (5th Battalion The Duke of Wellington's) Heavy Anti-Aircraft Regiment, Royal Artillery in 1947.<sup id="cite_ref-regiments_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regiments-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It then amalgamated with the 382nd Medium Regiment, Royal Artillery (Duke of Wellington's Regiment) in 1955, a unit which converted back to form the West Riding Battalion, The Duke of Wellington's Regiment (West Riding) at Huddersfield in 1961.<sup id="cite_ref-regiments_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regiments-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the reductions in 1967, the presence at the St Paul's Street drill hall was re-established by C (The Duke of Wellington's Regiment) Company, 3rd Battalion, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Volunteers" title="Yorkshire Volunteers">Yorkshire Volunteers</a> in 1971.<sup id="cite_ref-wrb_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wrb-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> This unit evolved to become C Company, 3rd Battalion, The Duke of Wellington's Regiment (West Riding) (Yorkshire Volunteers) in 1993, Ypres Company (Duke of Wellington's Regiment), The <a href="/wiki/East_and_West_Riding_Regiment" title="East and West Riding Regiment">East and West Riding Regiment</a> in 1999 and Corunna Company, 4th Battalion, The <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Regiment" title="Yorkshire Regiment">Yorkshire Regiment</a> in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The building remains an active <a href="/wiki/Army_Reserve_(United_Kingdom)" title="Army Reserve (United Kingdom)">Army Reserve</a> Centre.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
