<p>The current <b>Constitution of Suriname</b> (Dutch: <i lang="nl">Grondwet van Suriname</i>) was adopted on 30 September 1987, following <a href="/wiki/1987_Surinamese_constitutional_referendum" title="1987 Surinamese constitutional referendum">a referendum</a>. It marked the return to democracy after the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Bouterse military dictatorship</a> of the 1980s.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Constitution_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a> took over the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_(Dutch_colony)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suriname (Dutch colony)">Colony of Suriname</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Suriname" title="Society of Suriname">Society of Suriname</a> in 1795, the Dutch government issued various government regulations for Suriname (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i>Regeringsreglement voor Suriname</i>), establishing the government of the colony. In 1865, a new government regulation replaced the previous regulation of 1832, which theoretically gave Suriname some limited self-rule.<sup id="cite_ref-Paula_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paula-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The colonial elite was given the right to elect a Colonial Council (Dutch: <i>Koloniale Raad</i>) which would co-govern the colony together with the Governor-General appointed by the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_Netherlands" title="Monarchy of the Netherlands">Dutch crown</a>. Among others, the Colonial Council was allowed to decide over the colony's budget, which was subject to approval by the Dutch crown, but which did not see any involvement of Dutch parliament.<sup id="cite_ref-Paula_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paula-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the wake of the 1922 Dutch constitutional revision, in which the term "<a href="/wiki/Colony" title="Colony">colony</a>" was replaced by the term "<a href="/wiki/Overseas_territory" class="mw-redirect" title="Overseas territory">overseas territory</a>", the 1865 government regulation was replaced by the Basic Law of Suriname (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">Staatsregeling van Suriname</i>) on 1 April 1937. This Basic Law renamed the Colonial Council to Estates of Suriname (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">Staten van Suriname</i>) and increased the membership from 13 to 15.
</p><p>After the Second World War, during which the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_government-in-exile" title="Dutch government-in-exile">Dutch government-in-exile</a> had pledged to review the relationship between the Netherlands and its colonies, the Basic Law was extensively revised. In March 1948, revisions to the Basic Law were adopted by Dutch parliament, which introduced <a href="/wiki/Universal_suffrage" title="Universal suffrage">universal suffrage</a> for both men and women, which increased the membership of the Estates from 15 to 21, and which introduced a College of General Government (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">College van Algemeen Bestuur</i>) which was to assist the Governor in the everyday government of the colony, and which was the precursor to the Cabinet of Ministers.<sup id="cite_ref-Suriname.nu_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suriname.nu-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The new constitution took effect in July 1948.
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Charter_for_the_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Charter for the Kingdom of the Netherlands">Charter for the Kingdom of the Netherlands</a> was proclaimed on 15 December 1954, Suriname ceased to be an overseas possession of the Netherlands, and rather became a <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands#Countries" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">constituent country of the Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>, in which the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antilles" title="Netherlands Antilles">Netherlands Antilles</a>, Suriname and the Netherlands itself cooperated on a basis of equality. A new Basic Law that reflected the new constitutional arrangements was adopted by the Estates of Suriname in 1955.
</p><p>The first constitution of independent Suriname was adopted in 1975, and was modeled after the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_the_Netherlands" title="Constitution of the Netherlands">Constitution of the Netherlands</a>.  It was suspended after the <a href="/wiki/1980_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="1980 Surinamese coup d&#39;état">1980 Surinamese coup d'état</a> and was replaced by the current constitution after being approved in <a href="/wiki/1987_Surinamese_constitutional_referendum" title="1987 Surinamese constitutional referendum">a referendum</a> held on 30 September 1987.
</p>
