<p><b>Chinese Surinamese people</b> are <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Surinamese</a> residents of  ethnic <a href="/wiki/Han_Chinese" title="Han Chinese">Chinese</a> origin. Chinese Surinamese are a small part of the <a href="/wiki/Surinamese_people" title="Surinamese people">Surinamese people</a>. There were 7,885 Chinese at the 2012 census,<sup id="cite_ref-auto_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> constituting 1.5% of the total population. They constitute the largest component of the 'other' ethnic category, which makes up 2.3% of the population as per the CIA World Factbook.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The majority of the Chinese Surinamese consider <a href="/wiki/Hakka" class="mw-redirect" title="Hakka">Hakka</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dongguan" title="Dongguan">Dongguan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huiyang" class="mw-redirect" title="Huiyang">Huiyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huizhou" title="Huizhou">Huizhou</a> or <a href="/wiki/Bao%27an_District" title="Bao&#39;an District">Bao'an</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shenzhen" title="Shenzhen">Shenzhen</a>) of <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> as their <a href="/wiki/Ancestral_home_(Chinese)" title="Ancestral home (Chinese)">ancestral homes</a>. There is a small minority of <a href="/wiki/Heshan,_Guangdong" title="Heshan, Guangdong">Heshan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jiangmen" title="Jiangmen">Jiangmen</a> origin Cantonese and Hakkas as well.
</p><p>Many Chinese Surinamese are active in the retail and business community. Six percent of the Chinese in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> migrated from Suriname.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Surinamese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Contract_workers">Contract workers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Surinamese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Contract workers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
 <div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_60012337_Portret_van_een_Chinese_immigrantenfa.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_60012337_Portret_van_een_Chinese_immigrantenfa.jpg/220px-Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_60012337_Portret_van_een_Chinese_immigrantenfa.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_60012337_Portret_van_een_Chinese_immigrantenfa.jpg/330px-Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_60012337_Portret_van_een_Chinese_immigrantenfa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_60012337_Portret_van_een_Chinese_immigrantenfa.jpg/440px-Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_60012337_Portret_van_een_Chinese_immigrantenfa.jpg 2x" data-file-width="700" data-file-height="560" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_60012337_Portret_van_een_Chinese_immigrantenfa.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Southern Chinese migrant family</div></div></div>
<p>In 1853, <a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">planters</a> in Suriname feared a labor shortage when <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> was about to be abolished. They asked the government to recruit other workers from abroad.
</p><p>The government of <a href="/wiki/Java" title="Java">Java</a> recruited a group of 18 Chinese for <a href="/wiki/Contract_labor" class="mw-redirect" title="Contract labor">contract labor</a> in the Catharina Sophia plantation in <a href="/wiki/Saramacca_District" title="Saramacca District">Saramacca</a>. Because of the high acquisition costs, it was decided to get a second group, not from Java, but from <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> instead. In 1858, 500 Chinese laborers were recruited by the Dutch consul in <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>. 
They arrived in Suriname in April, but it turned out that no one wanted to hire people to do work that slaves would do "for free".
</p><p>Because of this, the <a href="/wiki/Contract" title="Contract">contract</a> with the Chinese was changed without their knowledge by <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">Governor</a> Charles Pierre Schimpf, in favor of the employers. The Chinese could now be treated like slaves. When they would revolt against this, they were, without due process and contrary to existing regulations, punished by police with cane strokes, an unlawful act that was repeated again and again.
</p><p>An interpellation (formal request for information) to the Minister of Colonies <a href="/wiki/Jan_Jacob_Rochussen" title="Jan Jacob Rochussen">Jan Jacob Rochussen</a> did not help.
</p><p>In the 1850s and 1860s, about 2,500 Chinese people went to Suriname. Most were employed as contract laborers on the plantations. After their contracts expired, many found opportunities in trade, mostly in food retail. Most of the male laborers were married to non-Chinese women. Those who married Chinese women, mostly married with an imported bride.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_immigrants">Later immigrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Surinamese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later immigrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Other Chinese came to Suriname as free laborers, traders and shop assistants, especially in the 1950s and 1960s. Further large numbers came in the 1990s. In 2007, there were over 70,000 Chinese in Suriname, and the <a href="/wiki/Immigration" title="Immigration">immigration</a> is still ongoing. The rapidly growing demand in China for wood and minerals makes Suriname very attractive to Chinese businesses. The new Chinese migrants from northern China are known in Suriname as "salt-water-Chinese".
</p><p>Since the 1960s, thousands of Chinese have emigrated from Suriname to the Netherlands.
</p><p>The Chinese held a prominent position in small and medium business for a long time, and their mostly well-educated offspring of mixed ancestry or Chinese ancestry can be found in various social sectors. Also, the Surinamese people have adopted several Chinese customs.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chinese_Surinamese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original Chinese settlers were <a href="/wiki/Hakka_people" title="Hakka people">Hakka</a> from the Fuitungon region of <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-Tjonspeakingp199_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tjonspeakingp199-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
Fui<sup>5</sup>tung<sup>1</sup>on<sup>1</sup> means three places: <a href="/wiki/Huiyang" class="mw-redirect" title="Huiyang">Huiyang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huizhou" title="Huizhou">Huizhou</a> or Fui<sup>5</sup>jong<sup>2</sup>, <a href="/wiki/Dongguan" title="Dongguan">Dongguan</a> or tung<sup>1</sup>kon<sup>1</sup>, and <a href="/wiki/Bao%27an_County" title="Bao&#39;an County">Bao'an County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shenzhen" title="Shenzhen">Shenzhen</a> or pau<sup>3</sup>on<sup>1</sup>,<sup id="cite_ref-Tjonspeakingp196_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tjonspeakingp196-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hakka_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Hakka language">Hakka</a> was originally present and became the "old Chinese" of Guyana.<sup id="cite_ref-Tjonspeakingp199_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tjonspeakingp199-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was previously the only spoken <a href="/wiki/Variety_of_Chinese" class="mw-redirect" title="Variety of Chinese">variety of Chinese</a> in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-Tjonspeakingp201_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tjonspeakingp201-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Paul Brendan Tjon Sie Fat of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Amsterdam" title="University of Amsterdam">University of Amsterdam</a> stated that Hakka people in Suriname do not frequently voice negative attitudes towards the fact that Hakka has a lower status than <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> in Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-Tjonspeakingp202_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tjonspeakingp202-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 1970s <a href="/wiki/Cantonese_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Cantonese language">Cantonese</a> was introduced into Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-Tjonspeakingp201_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tjonspeakingp201-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Beginning in the 1990s new migrants from China moved to Suriname,<sup id="cite_ref-Tjonspeakingp196_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tjonspeakingp196-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Putonghua" class="mw-redirect" title="Putonghua">Putonghua</a>, during circa 2004-2014, became the main Chinese <a href="/wiki/Lingua_franca" title="Lingua franca">lingua franca</a> in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-Tjonspeakingp202_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tjonspeakingp202-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There are Mandarin and Cantonese television shows aired in Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-Tjonspeakingp202_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tjonspeakingp202-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Most of the Chinese Surinamese are irreligious,with significant population adhering to Christianity.<a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> are also followed in small number.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
