<p><b>Dewsbury</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Minster_(church)" title="Minster (church)">minster town</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Metropolitan Borough of Kirklees</a>, in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is to the west of <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>, east of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and south of <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>. It lies by the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a> and an arm of the <a href="/wiki/Calder_and_Hebble_Navigation" title="Calder and Hebble Navigation">Calder and Hebble Navigation</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> a part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, after undergoing a period of major growth in the 19th century as a <a href="/wiki/Mill_town" title="Mill town">mill town</a>, Dewsbury went through a period of decline. More recently there has been redevelopment of derelict mills into flats, and regenerating of city areas.
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2011" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2011">2011 census</a> the Dewsbury urban sub-area had a population of 62,945. Dewsbury is the largest town in the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_Woollen_District" title="Heavy Woollen District">Heavy Woollen District</a>, a conurbation of small mill towns.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toponymy">Toponymy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Toponymy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i> of 1086 records the name as <i>Deusberie</i>, <i>Deusberia</i>, <i>Deusbereia</i>, or <i>Deubire</i>, literally
"Dewi's fort", Dewi being an old Welsh name (equivalent to David) and "bury" coming from the old English word "burh", meaning fort.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other, less supported, theories exist as to the name's origin. For example, that it means "dew hill", from <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i>dēaw</i> (genitive <i>dēawes</i>), "<a href="/wiki/Dew" title="Dew">dew</a>", and <i>beorg</i>, "hill" (because Dewsbury is built on a hill). It has been suggested that <i>dēaw</i> refers to the town's proximity to the water of the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Historically other origins were proposed, such as "God's fort", from <a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a> <i>Duw</i>, "God". "Antiquarians supposed the name, Dewsbury, to be derived from the original planter of the village, Dui or Dew, who … had fixed his abode and fortified his "Bury". Another conjecture holds, that the original name is Dewsborough, or God's Town" (1837)<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">Early history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxon" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo-Saxon">Anglo-Saxon</a> times, Dewsbury was a centre of considerable importance. The <a href="/wiki/Ecclesiastical_parish" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecclesiastical parish">ecclesiastical parish</a> of Dewsbury encompassed <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mirfield" title="Mirfield">Mirfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>. Ancient legend records that in 627 <a href="/wiki/Paulinus_of_York" title="Paulinus of York">Paulinus</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_York" title="Archbishop of York">Bishop of York</a>, preached here on the banks of the River Calder. Numerous Anglian graves have been found in Dewsbury and <a href="/wiki/Thornhill,_West_Yorkshire" title="Thornhill, West Yorkshire">Thornhill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_Minster.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Dewsbury_Minster.jpg/220px-Dewsbury_Minster.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Dewsbury_Minster.jpg/330px-Dewsbury_Minster.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Dewsbury_Minster.jpg/440px-Dewsbury_Minster.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_Minster.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_Minster" title="Dewsbury Minster">Dewsbury Minster</a></div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_Minster" title="Dewsbury Minster">Dewsbury Minster</a> lies near the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a>, traditionally on the site where <a href="/wiki/Paulinus_of_York" title="Paulinus of York">Paulinus</a> preached. Some of the visible stonework in the nave is Saxon, and parts of the church also date to the 13th century. The tower houses "Black Tom", a bell which is rung each Christmas Eve, one toll for each year since Christ's birth, known as the "Devil's Knell", a tradition dating from the 15th century. The bell was given by Sir Thomas de Soothill, in penance for murdering a servant boy in a fit of rage. The tradition was commemorated on a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Mail" title="Royal Mail">Royal Mail</a> postage stamp in 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dewsbury market was established in the 14th century for local clothiers. Occurrences of the <a href="/wiki/Bubonic_plague" title="Bubonic plague">plague</a> in 1593 and 1603 closed the market and it reopened in 1741.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Throughout the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, Dewsbury retained a measure of importance in ecclesiastical terms, collecting tithes from as far away as <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a> in the mid-14th century. <a href="/wiki/John_Wesley" title="John Wesley">John Wesley</a> visited the area five times in the mid-18th century, and the first <a href="/wiki/Methodist" class="mw-redirect" title="Methodist">Methodist</a> Society was established in 1746. Centenary Chapel on Daisy Hill commemorates the centenary of this event, and the Methodist tradition remained strong in the town.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_Revolution">Industrial Revolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Industrial Revolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<table class="box-Unreferenced_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. <div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_County_Court.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Dewsbury_County_Court.jpg/220px-Dewsbury_County_Court.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="168" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Dewsbury_County_Court.jpg/330px-Dewsbury_County_Court.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Dewsbury_County_Court.jpg/440px-Dewsbury_County_Court.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="488" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_County_Court.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Dewsbury County Court</div></div></div>
<p>In 1770, a short branch of the <a href="/wiki/Calder_and_Hebble_Navigation" title="Calder and Hebble Navigation">Calder and Hebble Navigation</a> was completed, linking Dewsbury to the <a href="/wiki/Canal" title="Canal">canal</a> system giving access to <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Hull</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>. By the time of the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>, Dewsbury was a centre for the <a href="/wiki/Shoddy" class="mw-redirect" title="Shoddy">shoddy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_textile_manufacturing#Mungo" title="Glossary of textile manufacturing">mungo</a> industries which recycled woollen items by mixing them with new <a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">wool</a> and making heavy blankets and uniforms. The town benefited economically from the canal, its location at the heart of the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_Woollen_District" title="Heavy Woollen District">Heavy Woollen District</a>, and its proximity to <a href="/wiki/Coal_mines" class="mw-redirect" title="Coal mines">coal mines</a>. The railway arrived in 1848 when <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_railway_station" title="Dewsbury railway station">Dewsbury Wellington Road</a> railway station on the <a href="/wiki/London_and_North_Western_Railway" title="London and North Western Railway">London and North Western Railway</a> opened; this is the only station which remains open. Other stations were Dewsbury Central on the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_Railway_(Great_Britain)" title="Great Northern Railway (Great Britain)">Great Northern Railway</a> which closed in 1964 and Dewsbury Market Place on the <a href="/wiki/Lancashire_and_Yorkshire_Railway" title="Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway">Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway</a> which closed in 1930; a fourth goods-only railway station was built in the early 20th century at Savile Town by the <a href="/wiki/Midland_Railway" title="Midland Railway">Midland Railway</a>. In 1985 a bypass road was built on the site of Central Station and its adjacent viaduct, and nothing remains of Market Place railway station. The 19th century saw a great increase in population, rising from 4,566 in 1801 to around 30,000 by 1890.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Steam_engine,_Providence_Mills_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688602.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Steam_engine%2C_Providence_Mills_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688602.jpg/250px-Steam_engine%2C_Providence_Mills_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688602.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Steam_engine%2C_Providence_Mills_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688602.jpg/375px-Steam_engine%2C_Providence_Mills_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688602.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Steam_engine%2C_Providence_Mills_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688602.jpg/500px-Steam_engine%2C_Providence_Mills_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688602.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="428" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Steam_engine,_Providence_Mills_-_geograph.org.uk_-_688602.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Steam engine, Providence Mills, Dewsbury</div></div></div>
<p>The town's rapid expansion and commitment to industrialisation resulted in social instability. In the early 19th century, Dewsbury was a centre of <a href="/wiki/Luddite" title="Luddite">Luddite</a> opposition to mechanisation in which workers retaliated against the mill owners who installed textile machinery and smashed the machines which threatened their way of life. In the 1830s, Dewsbury was a centre of <a href="/wiki/Chartism" title="Chartism">Chartist</a> agitation. In August 1838, after a speech by Chartist leader <a href="/wiki/Feargus_O%27Connor" title="Feargus O&#39;Connor">Feargus O'Connor</a>, a mob of between five and seven thousand people besieged the Dewsbury <a href="/wiki/Poor_Law" class="mw-redirect" title="Poor Law">Poor Law</a> Guardians in the town's Royal Hotel. The mob was dispersed by troops. Trouble flared in 1840 when radical agitators seized control of the town, and troops were stationed to maintain order. This radical tradition left a legacy in the town's political life, its first elected MP in 1867 was John Simon, a <a href="/wiki/Jew" class="mw-redirect" title="Jew">Jewish</a> lawyer from <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal</a>. The tradition of firing the "Ten o'Clock" gun dates from 1815 and was a hangover from the Luddite problems. It was fired from Wormald and Walker's Mill to reassure that all was well. It could be heard all over the area. Eventually the actual gun was replaced with a specially made firework but the tradition was discontinued in 1983 with the closure of the mill.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
The mills were family businesses and continued manufacturing after the wool crisis in 1950–51, which saw Australian <a href="/wiki/Sheep_husbandry" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheep husbandry">sheep farmers</a> begin to charge higher prices.  However, the recovery of the late 1960s was reversed by the <a href="/wiki/1973_oil_crisis" title="1973 oil crisis">1973 oil crisis</a>, and the textile industry in Dewsbury declined, with only bed manufacturing remaining a large scale employer.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Recent history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After 2005, Dewsbury was labelled a troubled town after negative press reports<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and became "the town that dare not speak its name"<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> after high-profile crimes brought it into the media spotlight. In June, a girl of 12 was charged with <a href="/wiki/Grievous_bodily_harm" title="Grievous bodily harm">grievous bodily harm</a> after attempting to hang a five-year-old boy from <a href="/wiki/Chickenley" title="Chickenley">Chickenley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Sidique_Khan" title="Mohammad Sidique Khan">Mohammad Sidique Khan</a>, ringleader of the group responsible for the <a href="/wiki/7_July_2005_London_bombings" title="7 July 2005 London bombings">7 July 2005 London bombings</a>, lived in Lees Holm.
</p><p>On 19 February 2008, <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Matthews" class="mw-redirect" title="Shannon Matthews">Shannon Matthews</a>, a nine-year-old girl from the Moorside Estate, was reported missing. After a 24-day hunt which attracted huge media and public attention nationally, she was found hidden in a flat in the <a href="/wiki/Batley_Carr" title="Batley Carr">Batley Carr</a> area on 14 March 2008. Her mother Karen Matthews, along with Michael Donovan, the uncle of her step-father Craig Meehan, were later found guilty of abduction and false imprisonment and both jailed for eight years, as part of a plot to claim the reward money for her safe return by pretending to have solved her disappearance.
</p><p>In October 2010, the Dewsbury Revival Centre opened, in the refurbished former St Mark's Church on Halifax Road, the church attended by the <i><a href="/wiki/Titanic" class="mw-redirect" title="Titanic">Titanic</a></i> bandmaster <a href="/wiki/Wallace_Hartley" title="Wallace Hartley">Wallace Hartley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2014, Kirklees Council enforced a media ban covering the visit of the <a href="/wiki/Princess_Royal" title="Princess Royal">Princess Royal</a> who was due to deliver a 1-minute and 15 second speech on the importance of restorative justice. Kirklees Council later responded that the highly unusual media ban had been insisted upon by the Royal Household. <a href="/wiki/Buckingham_Palace" title="Buckingham Palace">Buckingham Palace</a> however was mystified over the ban with a Royal spokesman stating: "This visit has been openly listed in the future engagements section on the Royal website for the last eight weeks. There are no restrictions on reporting on the event from the Royal Household."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dewsbury was incorporated as a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> in 1862.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Its first mayor was Dr George Fearnley. The <a href="/wiki/Reform_Act" title="Reform Act">Reform Act</a> of 1868 constituted Dewsbury a <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliamentary borough">parliamentary borough</a>, and Liberal candidate John Simon, serjeant-at-law, was returned as the borough's first <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">MP</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Victorian_architecture" title="Victorian architecture">Victorian</a> <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_Town_Hall" title="Dewsbury Town Hall">town hall</a> standing in front of the old marketplace dates from 1886–89.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Dewsbury's boundaries were expanded to include the <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban districts</a> of Ravensthorpe, Thornhill, and Soothill Nether, and part of Soothill Upper, in 1910, and in 1913 it was elevated to <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county borough</a> status. "Soothill Nether" refers to the current east end of the town, although at that time Chickenley and Chidswell were hamlets, and Earlsheaton contained the bulk of the area's population.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 1974, responsibility for local government passed to <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees Metropolitan Council</a>, with its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>. The population of Dewsbury has remained broadly static over the past century: the 1911 census recorded 53,351 people,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and the 1971 census 51,326 people, making it the fourth least populous county borough in England (after <a href="/wiki/Canterbury" title="Canterbury">Canterbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burton_upon_Trent" title="Burton upon Trent">Burton upon Trent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Great_Yarmouth" title="Great Yarmouth">Great Yarmouth</a>).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_Market_Place.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Dewsbury_Market_Place.jpg/220px-Dewsbury_Market_Place.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Dewsbury_Market_Place.jpg/330px-Dewsbury_Market_Place.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Dewsbury_Market_Place.jpg/440px-Dewsbury_Market_Place.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_Market_Place.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Market Place</div></div></div>
<p>Dewsbury is situated between <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a> 8 miles (13&#160;km) to the north, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> a similar distance to the south west, and Wakefield 6&#160;mi (10&#160;km) east. Its proximity to these major urban centres, the <a href="/wiki/M1_motorway" title="M1 motorway">M1</a> and <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62</a> motorways and its position on the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Line">Huddersfield Line</a>, served by the <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a>, have contributed to its popularity as a <a href="/wiki/Commuter_town" title="Commuter town">commuter town</a>.
</p><p>Dewsbury is part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Urban_Area" title="West Yorkshire Urban Area">West Yorkshire Urban Area</a>, although its natural boundaries are not well defined, with built up areas of the town running into <a href="/wiki/Batley" title="Batley">Batley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heckmondwike" title="Heckmondwike">Heckmondwike</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ossett" title="Ossett">Ossett</a>.
</p><p>Geologically, the town is situated on rocks of the <a href="/wiki/Carboniferous_Period" class="mw-redirect" title="Carboniferous Period">Carboniferous Period</a>, consisting of <a href="/wiki/Coal_measure" class="mw-redirect" title="Coal measure">coal measures</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gritstone" title="Gritstone">gritstones</a>. <a href="/wiki/Quaternary_Period" class="mw-redirect" title="Quaternary Period">Quaternary Period</a> rock, <a href="/wiki/Glacial_till" class="mw-redirect" title="Glacial till">glacial deposits</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gravel" title="Gravel">gravels</a> exist in the <a href="/wiki/Calder_Valley" class="mw-redirect" title="Calder Valley">Calder Valley</a>. Coal, sandstone and gravel have been exploited commercially. <a href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">Average rainfall</a> is 100&#160;cm per annum.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The town is dominated by hills, notably Earlsheaton, Dewsbury Moor, Staincliffe and Thornhill. The town centre is between 130 and 180 feet (40 and 55 metres) above sea level, rising to 360&#160;ft (110&#160;m) at Earlsheaton and Batley Carr, and 755&#160;ft (230&#160;m) at Grange Moor. The approach from Earlsheaton through the Wakefield Road cutting, constructed in 1830<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>, is dramatic with the view of the town centre in the Calder Valley opening up.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Divisions_and_suburbs">Divisions and suburbs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Divisions and suburbs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dewsbury has a number of districts with different geographical and socio-economic patterns, they are, Chickenley, Crackenedge, <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_Moor" title="Dewsbury Moor">Dewsbury Moor</a>, Earlsheaton, Eastborough, Eightlands, Flatts, <a href="/wiki/Ravensthorpe,_Dewsbury" class="mw-redirect" title="Ravensthorpe, Dewsbury">Ravensthorpe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savile_Town" title="Savile Town">Savile Town</a>, Shaw Cross, Scout Hill, Thornhill Lees, Westborough, Westtown. <a href="/wiki/Batley_Carr" title="Batley Carr">Batley Carr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hanging_Heaton" title="Hanging Heaton">Hanging Heaton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Staincliffe" title="Staincliffe">Staincliffe</a> have areas which lie in both Dewsbury and neighbouring Batley. <a href="/wiki/Thornhill,_West_Yorkshire" title="Thornhill, West Yorkshire">Thornhill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Briestfield" title="Briestfield">Briestfield</a> and Whitley are part of Dewsbury. Thornhill was annexed in 1910.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demography_and_economy">Demography and economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Demography and economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_market.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Dewsbury_market.jpg/220px-Dewsbury_market.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Dewsbury_market.jpg/330px-Dewsbury_market.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Dewsbury_market.jpg/440px-Dewsbury_market.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_market.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Dewsbury Market</div></div></div>
<p>From the outset of the industrialisation of the town with its many mills, a large influx of Irish workers arrived in the town, settling in the Westtown area.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> This area has the large and imposing Our Lady and St. Paulinus <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> Church and its school, once run by the nuns of the area. The Irish National Club also is home to <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_Celtic" title="Dewsbury Celtic">Dewsbury Celtic</a> amateur <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> club, with its many age groups of players.
</p><p>The town has a large Asian community. <a href="/wiki/Savile_Town" title="Savile Town">Savile Town</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ravensthorpe,_Dewsbury" class="mw-redirect" title="Ravensthorpe, Dewsbury">Ravensthorpe</a> are populated mainly by <a href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> of <a href="/wiki/British_Indian" title="British Indian">Indian</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Pakistani" class="mw-redirect" title="British Pakistani">Pakistani</a> origin. In recent years, there has also been an immigration of Iraqi <a href="/wiki/British_Kurds" class="mw-redirect" title="British Kurds">Kurds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hungarians_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Hungarians in the United Kingdom">Hungarians</a> into the town.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>  Dewsbury has been accused of having a controversial Shariah arbitration court.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
The <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_Moor" title="Dewsbury Moor">Dewsbury Moor</a>, Ravensthorpe and Chickenley areas are classed among the 10% most deprived areas in the UK.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In contrast to some British towns and cities, the east side of the town is generally more affluent. The majority of houses in the town are in the cheapest band for council tax, for house prices are amongst the lowest in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The local market once consisted of 400 stalls and was one of the busiest<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> in Yorkshire and in years gone by drew large numbers of visitors to the town. Wednesdays and Saturdays are the normal market days with the popular <a href="/wiki/Flea_market" title="Flea market">flea market</a> on Fridays. The town's mills were located just south of the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a> in the town centre. As the mills closed this area became a large <a href="/wiki/Brownfield_site" class="mw-redirect" title="Brownfield site">brownfield site</a>. However, many of the units have been reoccupied and the town's largest employer, <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Cards" title="Carlton Cards">Carlton Cards</a>, is based in this area.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_Bus_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_411829.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Dewsbury_Bus_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_411829.jpg/220px-Dewsbury_Bus_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_411829.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Dewsbury_Bus_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_411829.jpg/330px-Dewsbury_Bus_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_411829.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Dewsbury_Bus_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_411829.jpg/440px-Dewsbury_Bus_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_411829.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_Bus_Station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_411829.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Inside Dewsbury bus station</div></div></div>
<p>Dewsbury bus station serves the town of Dewsbury. The bus station is managed and owned by <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Passenger_Transport_Executive" class="mw-redirect" title="West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive">Metro (West Yorkshire PTE)</a>. The bus station was rebuilt in 1994 with a main passenger concourse and 19 bus stands.
</p><p>The town is served on the railway network by <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_railway_station" title="Dewsbury railway station">Dewsbury railway station</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tetley_stadium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Tetley_stadium.jpg/220px-Tetley_stadium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Tetley_stadium.jpg/330px-Tetley_stadium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Tetley_stadium.jpg/440px-Tetley_stadium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="419" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tetley_stadium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Crown_Flatt" title="Crown Flatt">Crown Flatt stadium</a>, also known as <b>Tetley's Stadium</b> for sponsorship purposes</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_Rams" title="Dewsbury Rams">Dewsbury RLFC</a> play in the Kingstone Press Championship. They play at the <a href="/wiki/Crown_Flatt" title="Crown Flatt">Crown Flatt</a>, on Owl Lane, towards <a href="/wiki/Ossett" title="Ossett">Ossett</a>, on the site of the old Savile &amp; Shaw Cross Colliery. Shaw Cross Sharks is an amateur Rugby League club. The club was founded in 1947 and has produced several players into the professional game, including <a href="/wiki/Mike_Stephenson" title="Mike Stephenson">Mike Stephenson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Stephenson" title="Nigel Stephenson">Nigel Stephenson</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Ward_(rugby_league)" title="David Ward (rugby league)">David Ward</a>. They operate from Shaw Cross Club for Young People and play their home fixtures at the adjacent Paul Lee Hinchcliffe Memorial Playing Fields. The open age first team is the National Conference League. <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_Celtic" title="Dewsbury Celtic">Dewsbury Celtic</a> play in National League 3, their ground is on the west side of the town, in Crow Nest Park. The club's headquarters are at the Dewsbury Irish National Club on Park Parade. Also the home of the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.dewsburyrangers.co.uk">Dewsbury Rangers Football club</a>, with over 300 members from the ages of six through to the old boys' teams it is one of the largest in the area.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_Museum_2004_3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Dewsbury_Museum_2004_3.jpg/220px-Dewsbury_Museum_2004_3.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="238" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Dewsbury_Museum_2004_3.jpg/330px-Dewsbury_Museum_2004_3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Dewsbury_Museum_2004_3.jpg/440px-Dewsbury_Museum_2004_3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1109" data-file-height="1198" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_Museum_2004_3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Dewsbury Museum, in Crow Nest Park</div></div></div>
<p>Dewsbury Museum was located within the mansion house in <a href="/wiki/Crow_Nest_Park" title="Crow Nest Park">Crow Nest Park</a>. The museum closed to the public in November 2016. Nearby attractions include the <a href="/wiki/National_Coal_Mining_Museum_for_England" title="National Coal Mining Museum for England">National Coal Mining Museum for England</a>.
</p><p>Dewsbury Town Hall contains a 700-seater concert hall and regularly hosts concerts, exhibitions, live music, cabaret evenings and weddings.
</p><p>The town also has an annual event called Spirit, a street theatre show every winter which takes place in the town centre.
</p><p>2018 comedy film <a href="/wiki/Destination:_Dewsbury" title="Destination: Dewsbury">Destination: Dewsbury</a> was filmed and part set in the town. The production was shot in 2016 and premiered at the 2018 <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills_Film_Festival" title="Beverly Hills Film Festival">Beverly Hills Film Festival</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_Baptist_Church_-_Manor_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_691185.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Dewsbury_Baptist_Church_-_Manor_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_691185.jpg/220px-Dewsbury_Baptist_Church_-_Manor_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_691185.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="169" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Dewsbury_Baptist_Church_-_Manor_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_691185.jpg/330px-Dewsbury_Baptist_Church_-_Manor_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_691185.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Dewsbury_Baptist_Church_-_Manor_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_691185.jpg/440px-Dewsbury_Baptist_Church_-_Manor_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_691185.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="493" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_Baptist_Church_-_Manor_Street_-_geograph.org.uk_-_691185.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Dewsbury Baptist Church</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_and_District_Technical_School_of_Art_and_Science_-_Halifax_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_707276.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Dewsbury_and_District_Technical_School_of_Art_and_Science_-_Halifax_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_707276.jpg/220px-Dewsbury_and_District_Technical_School_of_Art_and_Science_-_Halifax_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_707276.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Dewsbury_and_District_Technical_School_of_Art_and_Science_-_Halifax_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_707276.jpg/330px-Dewsbury_and_District_Technical_School_of_Art_and_Science_-_Halifax_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_707276.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Dewsbury_and_District_Technical_School_of_Art_and_Science_-_Halifax_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_707276.jpg/440px-Dewsbury_and_District_Technical_School_of_Art_and_Science_-_Halifax_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_707276.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="512" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dewsbury_and_District_Technical_School_of_Art_and_Science_-_Halifax_Road_-_geograph.org.uk_-_707276.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Dewsbury and District Technical School of Art and Science</div></div></div>
<p>Dewsbury had two <a href="/wiki/Grammar_school" title="Grammar school">grammar schools</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kirklees_College" title="Kirklees College">Wheelwright Grammar School for Boys</a> and, further up the hill, <a href="/wiki/Birkdale_High_School" title="Birkdale High School">Wheelwright Grammar School for Girls</a>. The 1970s education reforms converted these two establishments to high schools and they were renamed <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Dewsbury College">Dewsbury College</a> and <a href="/wiki/Birkdale_High_School" title="Birkdale High School">Birkdale High School</a>. Dewsbury College was merged with <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Technical_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Technical College">Huddersfield Technical College</a> to become part of <a href="/wiki/Kirklees_College" title="Kirklees College">Kirklees College</a> in 2008, and is now known by that name.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Birkdale High School closed in July 2011. In the 2005 School League Tables, Dewsbury's Eastborough Junior, Infants and Nursery schools were reported to have the most consistently improved results over the past four years. However, the headteacher of the school, Nicola Roth, has been highly critical of School League Tables in the UK and has been reported to have said, "It would be better if league tables did not exist".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Batley" title="Batley">Batley</a> College of <a href="/wiki/Art_and_Design" class="mw-redirect" title="Art and Design">Art and Design</a>, which is part of Kirklees College (Dewsbury Centre), has a strong reputation<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> for print and textile-based art work, whilst <a href="/wiki/St_John_Fisher_Catholic_Voluntary_Academy" title="St John Fisher Catholic Voluntary Academy">St John Fisher Catholic Voluntary Academy</a> is a specialist Sports College and is one of the few schools in the area with a Sixth Form.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References_in_popular_culture">References in popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: References in popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dewsbury is referenced in <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">the Beatles</a>' 1967 film <i><a href="/wiki/Magical_Mystery_Tour_(film)" title="Magical Mystery Tour (film)">Magical Mystery Tour</a></i>. A line of dialogue in the film has one of the magicians (all portrayed by the Beatles themselves) – who are keeping an eye on the whereabouts of the bus that is taking its passengers on the journey of the film's title – exclaim: "The bus is 10 miles [16&#160;km] north on the Dewsbury road and they're having a lovely time!"<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Dewsbury is also referenced in the 1991 single "<a href="/wiki/It%27s_Grim_Up_North" title="It&#39;s Grim Up North">It's Grim Up North</a>" by the <a href="/wiki/Justified_Ancients_of_Mu_Mu" class="mw-redirect" title="Justified Ancients of Mu Mu">Justified Ancients of Mu Mu</a> (also known as <a href="/wiki/The_KLF" title="The KLF">the KLF</a>).
</p><p>The 1960 book <i><a href="/wiki/A_Kind_of_Loving" title="A Kind of Loving">A Kind of Loving</a></i> is set in a fictional city named "Cressley", but its description was based upon Dewsbury. The author, <a href="/wiki/Stan_Barstow" title="Stan Barstow">Stan Barstow</a>, was born in <a href="/wiki/Horbury" title="Horbury">Horbury</a> and grew up in Ossett – both of which are just to the east of Dewsbury.
</p><p>More recently, the phrase "Dewsbury noir" has been used to describe the violent novels of <a href="/wiki/David_Peace" title="David Peace">David Peace</a>, who was born in Dewsbury but lives in neighbouring Ossett.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Educating_Yorkshire" title="Educating Yorkshire">Educating Yorkshire</a></i> followed the everyday lives of the staff and students of <a href="/wiki/Thornhill_Community_Academy" title="Thornhill Community Academy">Thornhill Community Academy</a>, a secondary school in Dewsbury, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. A Christmas special entitled <i>Educating Yorkshire at Christmas</i> was aired on 19 December 2013. In January 2014, the show won best Documentary Programme at the <a href="/wiki/National_Television_Awards" title="National Television Awards">National Television Awards</a>.
</p>
