<p><b>Beatrix</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> (Beatrix Wilhelmina Armgard, <small>Dutch pronunciation:&#32;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Dutch" title="Help:IPA/Dutch">[ˈbeːjaːtrɪks ˌʋɪlɦɛlˈminaː ˈʔɑrmɡɑrt]</a></span>&#32;<span class="nowrap" style="font-size:85%">(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Beatrix_Wilhelmina_Armgard,_prinses_van_Oranje-Nassau.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" decoding="async" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a></span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/94/Beatrix_Wilhelmina_Armgard%2C_prinses_van_Oranje-Nassau.ogg" class="internal" title="Beatrix Wilhelmina Armgard, prinses van Oranje-Nassau.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</span>; born 31 January 1938) is a princess of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_royal_house" title="Dutch royal house">Dutch royal house</a> who reigned as <a href="/wiki/Queen_of_the_Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen of the Netherlands">Queen of the Netherlands</a> from 30 April 1980 until her abdication on 30 April 2013.
</p><p>Beatrix is the eldest daughter of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Juliana" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen Juliana">Queen Juliana</a> and her husband, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Bernhard_of_Lippe-Biesterfeld" title="Prince Bernhard of Lippe-Biesterfeld">Prince Bernhard of Lippe-Biesterfeld</a>. Upon her mother's accession in 1948, she became <a href="/wiki/Heir_presumptive" title="Heir presumptive">heir presumptive</a>. Beatrix attended a public primary school in Canada during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and then finished her primary and secondary education in the Netherlands in the post-war period. In 1961, she received her <a href="/wiki/Law_degree" title="Law degree">law degree</a> from <a href="/wiki/Leiden_University" title="Leiden University">Leiden University</a>. In 1966, Beatrix married <a href="/wiki/Claus_von_Amsberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Claus von Amsberg">Claus von Amsberg</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Germans" title="Germans">German</a> diplomat, with whom she had three children. When her mother abdicated on 30 April 1980, Beatrix succeeded her as queen.
</p><p>Beatrix's reign saw the country's <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antilles" title="Netherlands Antilles">Caribbean possessions</a> reshaped with <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Status_aparte" title="Status aparte">secession</a> and becoming its own <a href="/wiki/Countries_of_the_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Countries of the Kingdom of the Netherlands">constituent country</a> within the kingdom in 1986. This was followed by the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Netherlands_Antilles" title="Dissolution of the Netherlands Antilles">dissolution of the Netherlands Antilles</a> in 2010, which created the new <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Netherlands" title="Caribbean Netherlands">special municipalities</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bonaire" title="Bonaire">Bonaire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sint_Eustatius" title="Sint Eustatius">Sint Eustatius</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Saba" title="Saba">Saba</a>, and the two new constituent countries of <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a>.
</p><p>On <a href="/wiki/Koninginnedag" class="mw-redirect" title="Koninginnedag">Koninginnedag</a> (<i>Queen's Day</i>), 30 April 2013, Beatrix <a href="/wiki/Abdicated" class="mw-redirect" title="Abdicated">abdicated</a> in favour of her eldest son, <a href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander" class="mw-redirect" title="Willem-Alexander">Willem-Alexander</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At the time of her abdication at age 75, Beatrix was the oldest reigning <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_Netherlands" title="Monarchy of the Netherlands">monarch in the country's history</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beatrix Wilhelmina Armgard was born on 31 January 1938 at the <a href="/wiki/Soestdijk_Palace" title="Soestdijk Palace">Soestdijk Palace</a> in <a href="/wiki/Baarn" title="Baarn">Baarn</a>, Netherlands, as the first child of <a href="/wiki/Princess_Juliana" class="mw-redirect" title="Princess Juliana">Princess Juliana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Bernhard_of_Lippe-Biesterfeld" title="Prince Bernhard of Lippe-Biesterfeld">Prince Bernhard of Lippe-Biesterfeld</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-youth_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youth-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Beatrix was <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptized</a> on 12 May 1938 in the <a href="/wiki/Grote_of_Sint-Jacobskerk_(The_Hague)" title="Grote of Sint-Jacobskerk (The Hague)">Great Church</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Her five godparents were <a href="/wiki/King_Leopold_III_of_Belgium" class="mw-redirect" title="King Leopold III of Belgium">King Leopold III of Belgium</a>; <a href="/wiki/Princess_Alice,_Countess_of_Athlone" title="Princess Alice, Countess of Athlone">Princess Alice, Countess of Athlone</a>; <a href="/wiki/Princess_Elisabeth_of_Waldeck_and_Pyrmont" title="Princess Elisabeth of Waldeck and Pyrmont">Elisabeth, Princess of Erbach-Schönberg</a>; <a href="/wiki/Duke_Adolf_Friedrich_of_Mecklenburg" title="Duke Adolf Friedrich of Mecklenburg">Duke Adolf Friedrich of Mecklenburg</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Allene_Tew" title="Allene Tew">Countess Allene de Kotzebue</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Beatrix's middle names are the first names of her maternal grandmother, the then reigning <a href="/wiki/Wilhelmina_of_the_Netherlands" title="Wilhelmina of the Netherlands">Queen Wilhelmina</a>, and her paternal grandmother, Princess <a href="/wiki/Armgard_of_Sierstorpff-Cramm" class="mw-redirect" title="Armgard of Sierstorpff-Cramm">Armgard of Sierstorpff-Cramm</a>.
</p><p>When Beatrix was one year old, in 1939, her younger sister <a href="/wiki/Princess_Irene_of_the_Netherlands" title="Princess Irene of the Netherlands">Princess Irene</a> was born.<sup id="cite_ref-youth_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youth-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Prinses_Beatrix,_prinses_Irene_en_prinses_Juliana_kijken_naar_voorbij_varende_sc,_Bestanddeelnr_255-7629_(crop).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Prinses_Beatrix%2C_prinses_Irene_en_prinses_Juliana_kijken_naar_voorbij_varende_sc%2C_Bestanddeelnr_255-7629_%28crop%29.jpg/170px-Prinses_Beatrix%2C_prinses_Irene_en_prinses_Juliana_kijken_naar_voorbij_varende_sc%2C_Bestanddeelnr_255-7629_%28crop%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="205" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Prinses_Beatrix%2C_prinses_Irene_en_prinses_Juliana_kijken_naar_voorbij_varende_sc%2C_Bestanddeelnr_255-7629_%28crop%29.jpg/255px-Prinses_Beatrix%2C_prinses_Irene_en_prinses_Juliana_kijken_naar_voorbij_varende_sc%2C_Bestanddeelnr_255-7629_%28crop%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Prinses_Beatrix%2C_prinses_Irene_en_prinses_Juliana_kijken_naar_voorbij_varende_sc%2C_Bestanddeelnr_255-7629_%28crop%29.jpg/340px-Prinses_Beatrix%2C_prinses_Irene_en_prinses_Juliana_kijken_naar_voorbij_varende_sc%2C_Bestanddeelnr_255-7629_%28crop%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1511" data-file-height="1820" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Prinses_Beatrix,_prinses_Irene_en_prinses_Juliana_kijken_naar_voorbij_varende_sc,_Bestanddeelnr_255-7629_(crop).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Beatrix and Irene on board the <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Piet_Hein_(royal_yacht)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Piet Hein (royal yacht) (page does not exist)">Piet Hein</a></i> in 1946</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> broke out in the Netherlands on 10 May 1940 (<a href="/wiki/Westfeldzug" class="mw-redirect" title="Westfeldzug">Westfeldzug</a>). On 13 May, the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Royal_Family" class="mw-redirect" title="Dutch Royal Family">Dutch Royal Family</a> evacuated to <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, United Kingdom. One month later, Beatrix went to <a href="/wiki/Ottawa" title="Ottawa">Ottawa</a>, Ontario, Canada, with her mother Juliana and her sister Irene, while her father Bernhard and maternal grandmother Queen Wilhelmina remained in London.<sup id="cite_ref-youth_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youth-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The family lived at the <a href="/wiki/Stornoway_(residence)" title="Stornoway (residence)">Stornoway residence</a> (now the residence of the <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_the_Opposition" title="Leader of the Opposition">Leader of the Opposition</a> in the Parliament of Canada).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> With bodyguards and ladies in waiting, the family summered at Bigwin Inn on <a href="/wiki/Lake_of_Bays,_Ontario" class="mw-redirect" title="Lake of Bays, Ontario">Lake of Bays, Ontario</a> where four private stone cottages of the resort served as their retreat. While on <a href="/wiki/Bigwin_Island" title="Bigwin Island">Bigwin Island</a>, the constitution of the Netherlands was stored in the safe of Bigwin Inn's Rotunda building. Princess Juliana and her family were remembered for their "down to earth" friendliness, general gratefulness and great reverence for their homeland and people, to whom they paid homage by refraining from all luxuries offered to guests at the resort that was once billed as the largest and most luxurious summer resort in Canada. In order to provide them with a greater sense of security, culinary chefs and staff catered to personal orders at meal time. Upon their departure, the hotel musicians of the Bigwin Inn Orchestra assembled dockside; and at every public performance afterward through to the end of World War II, the <i><a href="/wiki/Wilhelmus" title="Wilhelmus">Wilhelmus</a></i> was played. In the years following the shuttering and neglect of the island resort, the "Juliana" cottages were well maintained and preserved in an informal tribute to Princess Juliana and her family. In thanks for the protection of her and her daughters, Princess Juliana established the custom of the delivery to the Canadian government every spring of tulips, which are the centrepiece of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Tulip_Festival" title="Canadian Tulip Festival">Canadian Tulip Festival</a>.
</p><p>The second sister of Beatrix, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Margriet_of_the_Netherlands" title="Princess Margriet of the Netherlands">Princess Margriet</a>, was born in Ottawa in 1943.<sup id="cite_ref-youth_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youth-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> During their exile in Canada, Beatrix attended nursery and<sup id="cite_ref-education_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-education-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rockcliffe_Park_Public_School" title="Rockcliffe Park Public School">Rockcliffe Park Public School</a>, a primary school where she was known as "Trixie Orange".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 5 May 1945, the German troops in the Netherlands surrendered. The family returned to the Netherlands on 2 August 1945. Beatrix went to the progressive primary school <i>De Werkplaats</i> in <a href="/wiki/Bilthoven" title="Bilthoven">Bilthoven</a>. Her third sister <a href="/wiki/Princess_Christina_of_the_Netherlands" title="Princess Christina of the Netherlands">Princess Christina</a> was born in 1947.<sup id="cite_ref-youth_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-youth-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On 6 September 1948, her mother succeeded her grandmother Wilhelmina as Queen of the Netherlands, and Beatrix became the <a href="/wiki/Heir_presumptive" title="Heir presumptive">heir presumptive</a> to the Dutch throne at the age of ten.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Christmas_-_queen_Juliana_and_princess_Beatrix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Christmas_-_queen_Juliana_and_princess_Beatrix.jpg/220px-Christmas_-_queen_Juliana_and_princess_Beatrix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="212" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Christmas_-_queen_Juliana_and_princess_Beatrix.jpg/330px-Christmas_-_queen_Juliana_and_princess_Beatrix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Christmas_-_queen_Juliana_and_princess_Beatrix.jpg/440px-Christmas_-_queen_Juliana_and_princess_Beatrix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="669" data-file-height="646" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Christmas_-_queen_Juliana_and_princess_Beatrix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Princess Beatrix and <a href="/wiki/Juliana_of_the_Netherlands" title="Juliana of the Netherlands">Queen Juliana</a> in 1960</div></div></div>
<p>In April 1950, Princess Beatrix entered the Incrementum, a part of Baarnsch Lyceum, where, in 1956, she passed her school graduation examinations in the subjects of arts and classics.<sup id="cite_ref-studies_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-studies-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1954, Beatrix served as bridesmaid at the wedding of Baroness van Randwijck and Mr T Boey.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 31 January 1956 Beatrix celebrated her 18th birthday.  From that date, under the Constitution of the Netherlands, she was entitled to assume the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Prerogative" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Prerogative">Royal Prerogative</a>.  At that time, her mother installed her in the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Council_of_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Dutch Council of State">Council of State</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The same year her studies at <a href="/wiki/Leiden_University" title="Leiden University">Leiden University</a>  began. In her first years at the university, she studied <a href="/wiki/Sociology" title="Sociology">sociology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jurisprudence" title="Jurisprudence">jurisprudence</a>, <a href="/wiki/Economics" title="Economics">economics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Political_history" title="Political history">parliamentary history</a> and <a href="/wiki/Constitutional_law" title="Constitutional law">constitutional law</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-studies_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-studies-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In the course of her studies she also attended lectures on the cultures of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antilles" title="Netherlands Antilles">Netherlands Antilles</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Charter_for_the_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Charter for the Kingdom of the Netherlands">Charter for the Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/International_relations" title="International relations">international affairs</a>, <a href="/wiki/International_law" title="International law">international law</a>, history and <a href="/wiki/Law_of_the_European_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="Law of the European Union">European law</a>
</p><p>The princess also visited various European and international organisations in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Strasbourg" title="Strasbourg">Strasbourg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>.  She was also an active member of the VVSL (Female Union for Students in Leiden), now called L.S.V. Minerva, after merging with the Leidsch Studenten Corps (which before then was male-only). In the summer of 1959, she passed her preliminary examination in law, and she obtained her law degree in July 1961.<sup id="cite_ref-studies_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-studies-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Her appearance on the political scene was almost immediately marked by controversy. In 1965, Beatrix became engaged to the German aristocrat <a href="/wiki/Claus_von_Amsberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Claus von Amsberg">Claus von Amsberg</a>, a diplomat working for the German Foreign Office. There was a massive protest on their wedding day in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> on 10 March 1966. Prince Claus had served in the <a href="/wiki/Hitler_Youth" title="Hitler Youth">Hitler Youth</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Wehrmacht" title="Wehrmacht">Wehrmacht</a> and was therefore associated by some of the Dutch population with German <a href="/wiki/Nazism" title="Nazism">Nazism</a>. Protests included such memorable slogans as "<i>Claus 'raus!</i>" (Claus out!) and "<i>Mijn fiets terug</i>" ("Return my bicycle" – a reference to occupying German soldiers confiscating Dutch bicycles during WWII). A group of <a href="/wiki/Provo_(movement)" title="Provo (movement)">Provos</a> threw a smoke bomb  at the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Coach_(Netherlands)" title="Golden Coach (Netherlands)">Golden Coach</a>, resulting in a violent street battle with the <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_in_the_Netherlands" title="Law enforcement in the Netherlands">police</a>. But as time went on, Prince Claus became one of the most popular members of the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_Netherlands" title="Monarchy of the Netherlands">Dutch monarchy</a>, and his death in 2002 was widely mourned.
</p><p>On 25 November 1975, Beatrix and Prince Claus attended the independence ceremony of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, held in the new nation's capital, <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, representing her mother the Queen.
</p><p>There was an even more violent riot <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_coronation_riots" title="Amsterdam coronation riots">on 30 April 1980</a>, during Beatrix's <a href="/wiki/Investiture" title="Investiture">investiture</a> (sovereigns of the Netherlands are not crowned as such). Some people, including <a href="/wiki/Socialist" class="mw-redirect" title="Socialist">socialist</a> <a href="/wiki/Squatters" class="mw-redirect" title="Squatters">squatters</a>, used the occasion to protest against poor housing conditions in the Netherlands and against the monarchy in general, using the slogan "<i>Geen woning; geen Kroning</i>" (No home, no coronation). There were violent clashes with the police and security forces. This event is reflected in contemporary Dutch literature in the books of <a href="/wiki/A.F.Th._van_der_Heijden" class="mw-redirect" title="A.F.Th. van der Heijden">A.F.Th. van der Heijden</a>.
</p><p>As monarch, Beatrix had weekly meetings with the prime minister. She signed all new Acts of Parliament and royal decrees, and until a constitutional change late in her reign, appointed the <i><a href="/wiki/Informateur" class="mw-redirect" title="Informateur">informateur</a></i>, an official who is responsible for chairing coalition talks in the formation of new governments. At the state opening of parliament each September, she delivered the Speech from the Throne, in which the government announces its plans for the coming parliamentary year. As queen, she was president of the Council of State. Her role was largely ceremonial and as a focus of national unity; she did not make legislative or executive decisions.
</p><p>Beatrix is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Bilderberg_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Bilderberg Group">Bilderberg Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-prweb97_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prweb97-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_children">Marriage and children</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Marriage and children">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Troonswisseling,_balkonscene_-_Change_of_throne_balcony_scene_(8638755621).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Troonswisseling%2C_balkonscene_-_Change_of_throne_balcony_scene_%288638755621%29.jpg/220px-Troonswisseling%2C_balkonscene_-_Change_of_throne_balcony_scene_%288638755621%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Troonswisseling%2C_balkonscene_-_Change_of_throne_balcony_scene_%288638755621%29.jpg/330px-Troonswisseling%2C_balkonscene_-_Change_of_throne_balcony_scene_%288638755621%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Troonswisseling%2C_balkonscene_-_Change_of_throne_balcony_scene_%288638755621%29.jpg/440px-Troonswisseling%2C_balkonscene_-_Change_of_throne_balcony_scene_%288638755621%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="846" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Troonswisseling,_balkonscene_-_Change_of_throne_balcony_scene_(8638755621).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Queen Beatrix with <a href="/wiki/Prince_Claus_of_the_Netherlands" title="Prince Claus of the Netherlands">Prince Claus</a> and their children <a href="/wiki/Prince_Constantijn_of_the_Netherlands" title="Prince Constantijn of the Netherlands">Constantijn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander_of_the_Netherlands" title="Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands">King Willem-Alexander</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Friso_of_Orange-Nassau" title="Prince Friso of Orange-Nassau">Friso</a> on the balcony of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Palace,_Amsterdam" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Palace, Amsterdam">Royal Palace</a> in Amsterdam following the abdication of Queen Juliana, 1980.</div></div></div>
<p>On 28 June 1965, the engagement of Princess Beatrix to the German diplomat Claus von Amsberg was announced. Claus and Beatrix had met at the wedding-eve party of <a href="/wiki/Princess_Tatjana_of_Sayn-Wittgenstein-Berleburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Princess Tatjana of Sayn-Wittgenstein-Berleburg">Princess Tatjana of Sayn-Wittgenstein-Berleburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moritz,_Landgrave_of_Hesse" title="Moritz, Landgrave of Hesse">Moritz, Landgrave of Hesse</a>, in summer 1964. Following the consent of Parliament to the marriage, Claus von Amsberg became a Dutch citizen, and upon his marriage became Prince Claus of the Netherlands, Jonkheer van Amsberg.
</p><p>Beatrix married Claus von Amsberg on 10 March 1966 in civil and religious ceremonies.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The bride wore a traditional gown with train in duchesse silk satin, designed by Caroline Bergé-Farwick of Maison Linette, in Den Bosch, and the Württemberg Ornate Pearl Tiara. The senior bridesmaids were the bride’s youngest sister, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Christina_of_the_Netherlands" title="Princess Christina of the Netherlands">Princess Christina of the Netherlands</a>; <a href="/wiki/Princess_Christina,_Mrs._Magnuson" title="Princess Christina, Mrs. Magnuson">Princess Christina of Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Shakerley" title="Geoffrey Shakerley">Lady Elisabeth Anson</a>, Joanna Roëll, Eugénie Loudon and the bridegroom’s sister, Christina von Amsberg. The junior bridesmaids were <a href="/wiki/Jack_Stewart-Clark" title="Jack Stewart-Clark">Daphne Stewart-Clark</a> and Carolijn Alting von Geusau, with page boys Joachim Jencquel and Markus von Oeynhausen-Sierstorpff.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The royal couple travelled to the ceremony together in the gold state carriage.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The civil ceremony was conducted by the Mayor of Amsterdam, <a href="/wiki/Gijsbert_van_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Gijsbert van Hall">Gijsbert van Hall</a>, at Amsterdam City Hall. The marriage blessing took place in the Westerkerk, conducted by the Rev. Hendrik Jan Kater, with a sermon by the Rev. Johannes Hendrik Sillevis Smitt.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The royal couple had three sons:
</p>
<ol><li>King <a href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander_of_the_Netherlands" title="Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands">Willem-Alexander</a> (born 1967)</li>
<li>Prince <a href="/wiki/Prince_Friso_of_Orange-Nassau" title="Prince Friso of Orange-Nassau">Johan Friso</a>, known as "Friso" (1968–2013)<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Prince <a href="/wiki/Prince_Constantijn_of_the_Netherlands" title="Prince Constantijn of the Netherlands">Constantijn</a> (born 1969).</li></ol>
<p>They lived at <a href="/wiki/Drakensteyn_Castle" title="Drakensteyn Castle">Drakensteyn Castle</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lage_Vuursche" title="Lage Vuursche">Lage Vuursche</a> with their children until Beatrix ascended the throne. In 1981, they moved into <a href="/wiki/Huis_ten_Bosch" class="mw-redirect" title="Huis ten Bosch">Huis ten Bosch</a> Palace in The Hague.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reign">Reign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vladimir_Putin_in_the_Netherlands_1_November_2005-2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Vladimir_Putin_in_the_Netherlands_1_November_2005-2.jpg/220px-Vladimir_Putin_in_the_Netherlands_1_November_2005-2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Vladimir_Putin_in_the_Netherlands_1_November_2005-2.jpg/330px-Vladimir_Putin_in_the_Netherlands_1_November_2005-2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Vladimir_Putin_in_the_Netherlands_1_November_2005-2.jpg/440px-Vladimir_Putin_in_the_Netherlands_1_November_2005-2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="334" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vladimir_Putin_in_the_Netherlands_1_November_2005-2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Queen Beatrix and President <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a> during his state visit to the Netherlands in 2005</div></div></div>
<p>On 30 April 1980, Beatrix became the monarch when her mother abdicated. She was <a href="/wiki/Inauguration_of_the_Dutch_monarch" title="Inauguration of the Dutch monarch">sworn-in and inaugurated</a> as monarch during a joint session of the two chambers of the States General at a ceremony held in the <a href="/wiki/Nieuwe_Kerk,_Amsterdam" title="Nieuwe Kerk, Amsterdam">Nieuwe Kerk</a> in Amsterdam later that day.
</p><p>Beatrix's constitutional duties included those typically accorded to a <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a>; this includes having to sign every piece of legislation before it becomes law, formally appointing various officials, receiving and accrediting ambassadors, and awarding honours and medals, among others. Outside of these constitutional duties, her other informal roles included being the highest representative of the kingdom internationally and to be a unifying figure locally.
</p><p>Beatrix was rarely quoted directly in the press during her reign since the government information service (<i>Rijksvoorlichtingsdienst</i>) made it a condition of interviews that she may not be quoted. This policy was introduced shortly after her inauguration, reportedly to protect her from political complications that may arise from "off-the-cuff" remarks. It did not apply to her son <a href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander_of_the_Netherlands" title="Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands">Prince Willem-Alexander</a>.
</p><p>Throughout much of her reign Beatrix had a considerable role in the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_cabinet_formation" title="Dutch cabinet formation">cabinet formation process</a>; notably she appointed the <i><a href="/wiki/Formateur#Informateur" title="Formateur">informateur</a></i>, the person who leads the negotiations that ultimately lead to the formation of a government.  However, this was changed in 2012, and now the largest party in the <a href="/wiki/States_General_of_the_Netherlands" title="States General of the Netherlands">States General</a> appoints a "scout" who then appoints an informateur.
</p><p>On 1 January 1986, <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a> seceded from the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antilles" title="Netherlands Antilles">Netherlands Antilles</a> and became a separate constituent country in the Dutch Kingdom.
</p><p>On 6 October 2002, the Queen's husband, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Claus_of_the_Netherlands" title="Prince Claus of the Netherlands">Prince Claus</a>, died after a long illness. A year and a half later her mother died after long suffering from <a href="/wiki/Dementia" title="Dementia">senile dementia</a>, while her father succumbed to <a href="/wiki/Cancer" title="Cancer">cancer</a> in December 2004.
</p><p>On 8 February 2005, Beatrix received a rare honorary doctorate from <a href="/wiki/Leiden_University" title="Leiden University">Leiden University</a>, an honour the Queen does not usually accept. In her acceptance speech she reflected on the monarchy and her own 25 years as Queen.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The speech was broadcast live.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Beatrix_Prinsjesdag.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Beatrix_Prinsjesdag.jpg/220px-Beatrix_Prinsjesdag.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Beatrix_Prinsjesdag.jpg/330px-Beatrix_Prinsjesdag.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Beatrix_Prinsjesdag.jpg/440px-Beatrix_Prinsjesdag.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1279" data-file-height="963" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Beatrix_Prinsjesdag.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Queen Beatrix and her son, <a href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander_of_the_Netherlands" title="Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands">Willem-Alexander</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Coach_(Netherlands)" title="Golden Coach (Netherlands)">Golden Coach</a> on <a href="/wiki/Prinsjesdag" title="Prinsjesdag">Prinsjesdag</a> 2007, the day she gives the annual <a href="/wiki/Speech_from_the_throne" title="Speech from the throne">speech from the throne</a> outlining the government's agenda for the upcoming parliamentary year.</div></div></div>
<p>On 29 and 30 April 2005, she celebrated the <a href="/wiki/Silver_Jubilee" class="mw-redirect" title="Silver Jubilee">25th anniversary of her reign</a>.  She was interviewed on Dutch television, was offered a concert on <a href="/wiki/Dam_Square" title="Dam Square">Dam Square</a> in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, and a celebration took place in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a>, the country's seat of government.
</p><p>On 31 May 2006 the <i>6th Polish Air Assault Brigade</i> would receive the <i>Militaire Willemsorde der 4e klasse</i> in The Hague. Beatrix was to tie the prestigious medal to the standard of the incumbents of the <a href="/wiki/1st_Independent_Parachute_Brigade_(Poland)" title="1st Independent Parachute Brigade (Poland)">1st Independent Polish Parachute Brigade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 10 October 2010, the Netherlands Antilles were <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Netherlands_Antilles" title="Dissolution of the Netherlands Antilles">formally dissolved</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Netherlands" title="Caribbean Netherlands">new municipalities</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bonaire" title="Bonaire">Bonaire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sint_Eustatius" title="Sint Eustatius">Sint Eustatius</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Saba" title="Saba">Saba</a> and the new constituent countries of <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a> were established in its place. The dissolution ceremony in the Netherlands Antilles' capital, <a href="/wiki/Willemstad" title="Willemstad">Willemstad</a>, was attended by the then-<a href="/wiki/House_of_Orange-Nassau" title="House of Orange-Nassau">Prince and Princess of Orange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Willem-Alexander_of_the_Netherlands" title="Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands">Willem-Alexander</a> and his wife <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1xima_of_the_Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Máxima of the Netherlands">Máxima</a>, representing the Queen.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Attack_on_the_royal_family">Attack on the royal family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Attack on the royal family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2009_attack_on_the_Dutch_Royal_Family" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 attack on the Dutch Royal Family">2009 attack on the Dutch Royal Family</a></div>
<p>On 30 April 2009, the Queen and other members of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Royal_Family" class="mw-redirect" title="Dutch Royal Family">royal family</a> were targeted in a car attack by a man named Karst Tates. Tates crashed his car into a parade in <a href="/wiki/Apeldoorn" title="Apeldoorn">Apeldoorn</a>, narrowly missing a bus carrying the Queen. Five people were killed instantly and two victims and the assailant Tates died later. Other victims of the crash were critically hurt. One week after the attack, another victim succumbed to the injuries he had sustained. The royal party were unharmed, but the Queen and members of her family saw the crash at close range. Within hours, Beatrix made a rare televised address to express her shock and condolences. The man reportedly told police he was deliberately targeting the royal family.<sup id="cite_ref-NOS-doelwit_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOS-doelwit-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Abdication">Abdication</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Abdication">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Beatrix_in_April_2013.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Beatrix_in_April_2013.jpg/220px-Beatrix_in_April_2013.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="278" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Beatrix_in_April_2013.jpg/330px-Beatrix_in_April_2013.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Beatrix_in_April_2013.jpg/440px-Beatrix_in_April_2013.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1177" data-file-height="1488" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Beatrix_in_April_2013.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Beatrix in 2013, prior to her abdication</div></div></div>
<p>In a broadcast on national media on 28 January 2013, Beatrix announced her intention to <a href="/wiki/Abdicate" class="mw-redirect" title="Abdicate">abdicate</a> on 30 April (<a href="/wiki/Koninginnedag" class="mw-redirect" title="Koninginnedag">Queen's Day</a>), when she would have been on the throne for exactly 33 years. Beatrix stated that it was time to "place the responsibility for the country in the hands of a new generation."<sup id="cite_ref-AlJ-abdicate1_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AlJ-abdicate1-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Her <a href="/wiki/Heir_apparent" title="Heir apparent">heir apparent</a> was her eldest son, Prince Willem-Alexander.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCabdicate1_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCabdicate1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> She was the third successive Dutch monarch to abdicate, following her grandmother and her mother.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCabdicate1_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCabdicate1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The broadcast was followed by a statement from Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Mark_Rutte" title="Mark Rutte">Mark Rutte</a> who paid tribute to Beatrix, saying "Since her <a href="/wiki/Investiture" title="Investiture">investiture</a> in 1980, she has applied herself heart and soul to Dutch society."<sup id="cite_ref-AlJ-abdicate1_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AlJ-abdicate1-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The official programme for the abdication and inauguration took place on 30 April 2013. Beatrix signed the instrument of abdication in the Vroedschapkamer (also known as Mozeszaal)<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> in the Royal Palace, Amsterdam at 10:07&#160;a.m. local time. With the completion of her signature, she was no longer monarch. The inauguration of Willem-Alexander as king took place that afternoon in the <a href="/wiki/Nieuwe_Kerk_(Amsterdam)" class="mw-redirect" title="Nieuwe Kerk (Amsterdam)">Nieuwe Kerk</a> in Amsterdam.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_wealth">Personal wealth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal wealth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:King_Willem-Alexander,_Princess_Beatrix_and_Queen_Maxima.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/King_Willem-Alexander%2C_Princess_Beatrix_and_Queen_Maxima.jpg/220px-King_Willem-Alexander%2C_Princess_Beatrix_and_Queen_Maxima.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="238" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/King_Willem-Alexander%2C_Princess_Beatrix_and_Queen_Maxima.jpg/330px-King_Willem-Alexander%2C_Princess_Beatrix_and_Queen_Maxima.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/King_Willem-Alexander%2C_Princess_Beatrix_and_Queen_Maxima.jpg/440px-King_Willem-Alexander%2C_Princess_Beatrix_and_Queen_Maxima.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2389" data-file-height="2585" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:King_Willem-Alexander,_Princess_Beatrix_and_Queen_Maxima.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Beatrix following her abdication with her son and successor and his wife</div></div></div>
<p>In 2009, <i>Forbes</i> estimated her wealth at US$300 million.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-abdication">Post-abdication</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post-abdication">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Princess Beatrix continues to undertake some royal duties and is patron of many organisations. She now lives in the small <a href="/wiki/Moat" title="Moat">moated</a> <a href="/wiki/Drakensteyn_Castle" title="Drakensteyn Castle">Drakensteyn Castle</a> near the village of Lage Vuursche.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_impact">Cultural impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cultural impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beatrix has given her name to a number of facilities in the Netherlands and beyond. These include:
</p>
<ul><li>Streekziekenhuis Koningin Beatrix, regional hospital in Beatrixpark, <a href="/wiki/Winterswijk" title="Winterswijk">Winterswijk</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></li>

<h2><span id="Titles.2C_styles.2C_honours_and_arms"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles,_styles,_honours_and_arms">Titles, styles, honours and arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Titles, styles, honours and arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Royal_Monogram_of_Queen_Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Royal_Monogram_of_Queen_Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands.svg/100px-Royal_Monogram_of_Queen_Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands.svg.png" decoding="async" width="100" height="172" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Royal_Monogram_of_Queen_Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands.svg/150px-Royal_Monogram_of_Queen_Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Royal_Monogram_of_Queen_Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands.svg/200px-Royal_Monogram_of_Queen_Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="128" data-file-height="220" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Royal_Monogram_of_Queen_Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Royal Monogram of Queen Beatrix</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_titles_and_honours_of_Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands" title="List of titles and honours of Beatrix of the Netherlands">List of titles and honours of Beatrix of the Netherlands</a></div>
<ul><li>31 January 1938 – 30 April 1980: Her Royal Highness Princess Beatrix of the Netherlands, Princess of Orange-Nassau, Princess of Lippe-Biesterfeld<sup id="cite_ref-Parlement_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parlement-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>30 April 1980 – 30 April 2013: Her Majesty The Queen of the Netherlands, Princess of Orange-Nassau, Princess of Lippe-Biesterfeld<sup id="cite_ref-Parlement_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parlement-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>30 April 2013 – present: Her Royal Highness Princess Beatrix of the Netherlands, Princess of Orange-Nassau, Princess of Lippe-Biesterfeld<sup id="cite_ref-Parlement_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parlement-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>Princess Beatrix has held titles throughout her life, as a granddaughter or daughter of a monarch, and eventually as the Sovereign. Beatrix's official title was <i>Her Majesty the Queen of the Netherlands, Princess of Orange-Nassau, etc., etc., etc</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Parlement_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parlement-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The triple <i>etc.</i> refers to the monarch's <a href="/wiki/Style_of_the_Dutch_sovereign" title="Style of the Dutch sovereign">many dormant titles</a>. She signed official documents with only "Beatrix". In common parlance she was referred to as <i>The Queen</i> (<i>de koningin</i> or <i>de vorstin</i>) or <i>Her Majesty</i> (Hare Majesteit). But when in conversation with the queen the practice was to initially address her as "Your <a href="/wiki/Majesty" title="Majesty">Majesty</a>" or in <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> as "Uwe Majesteit"<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> and thereafter as "Mevrouw" (ma'am).<sup id="cite_ref-stylepref_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stylepref-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Beatrix has received honours and awards from countries around the world, both during her life as a princess and as a monarch. In her capacity as the Sovereign she was Grand Master of the Military Order of William (<i>Militaire Willemsorde</i>) and the other Dutch orders of merit. She is a Stranger Lady of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Garter" title="Order of the Garter">Most Noble Order of the Garter</a> and the 1,187th <a href="/wiki/Knight" title="Knight">Dame</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Golden_Fleece" title="Order of the Golden Fleece">Order of the Golden Fleece</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, and she has received numerous other medals and decorations.
</p><p>From birth till her inauguration as queen she had the following name and titles, to which she reverted after her abdication: <i>Her Royal Highness Princess Beatrix of the Netherlands, Princess of Orange-Nassau, Princess of Lippe-Biesterfeld</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
