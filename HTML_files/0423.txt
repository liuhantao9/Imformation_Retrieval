<p><b>Surinamese culture</b> is very diverse and dynamic, and has strong <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a>, <a href="/wiki/African_culture" class="mw-redirect" title="African culture">African</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> influences. The population is mainly composed of the contribution of people from the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, as well as indigenous peoples who lived in the area, before the arrival of European settlers. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Culture_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Music_of_Suriname" title="Music of Suriname">Music of Suriname</a></div>
<p>Suriname is a <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South American</a> country, a former colony of the Netherlands. The country is known for its <a href="/wiki/Kaseko" title="Kaseko">kaseko</a> music, and for having an <a href="/wiki/Indo-Caribbean" class="mw-redirect" title="Indo-Caribbean">Indo-Caribbean</a> tradition.
</p><p>The term <i><a href="/wiki/Kaseko" title="Kaseko">kaseko</a></i> is probably derived from the French expression <i>casser le corps</i> (<i>break the body</i>), which was used during slavery to indicate a very swift dance. Kaseko is a fusion of numerous popular and folk styles derived from Europe, Africa and the Americas. It is rhythmically complex, with <a href="/wiki/Percussion_instrument" title="Percussion instrument">percussion instruments</a> including <a href="/wiki/Skratji" class="mw-redirect" title="Skratji">skratji</a> (a very large bass drum) and <a href="/wiki/Snare_drum" title="Snare drum">snare drums</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Saxophone" title="Saxophone">saxophone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trumpet" title="Trumpet">trumpet</a> and occasionally <a href="/wiki/Trombone" title="Trombone">trombone</a>. Singing can be both solo and choir. Songs are typically <a href="/wiki/Call_and_response_(music)" title="Call and response (music)">call-and-response</a>, as are Creole folk styles from the area, such as <a href="/wiki/Kawina_(music)" title="Kawina (music)">kawina</a>.
</p><p>Kaseko emerged from the traditional Surinamese Creole kawina music, which was played since the beginning of 1900 by Creole street musicians in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>. It evolved in the 1930s during festivities that used large bands, especially <a href="/wiki/Brass_band" title="Brass band">brass bands</a>, and was called <i>Bigi Pokoe</i> (big drum music). Following World War II, <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calypso_music" title="Calypso music">calypso</a> and other importations became popular, while <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock and roll</a> soon left its own influence in the form of electrified instruments.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Culture_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cuisine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2015_1001_Chinny%27s_broodje_bakeljauw.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/2015_1001_Chinny%27s_broodje_bakeljauw.jpg/220px-2015_1001_Chinny%27s_broodje_bakeljauw.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/2015_1001_Chinny%27s_broodje_bakeljauw.jpg/330px-2015_1001_Chinny%27s_broodje_bakeljauw.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/2015_1001_Chinny%27s_broodje_bakeljauw.jpg/440px-2015_1001_Chinny%27s_broodje_bakeljauw.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1829" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2015_1001_Chinny%27s_broodje_bakeljauw.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Surinamese <i>"broodje bakeljauw"</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> (bun with shredded and spiced stockfish), with a chili paste made from Madame Jeanette peppers on the side</div></div></div>
<p>Surinamese cuisine is extensive, since the population of Suriname came from many countries.  Surinamese cuisine is a combination of many international cuisines including <a href="/wiki/Indian_cuisine" title="Indian cuisine">Indian</a>, <a href="/wiki/African_cuisine" title="African cuisine">African</a>, <a href="/wiki/Javanese_cuisine" title="Javanese cuisine">Javanese</a> (Indonesian), <a href="/wiki/Chinese_cuisine" title="Chinese cuisine">Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dutch_cuisine" title="Dutch cuisine">Dutch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jewish_cuisine" title="Jewish cuisine">Jewish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_cuisine" title="Portuguese cuisine">Portuguese</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Native_American_cuisine" title="Native American cuisine">Amerindian</a> cuisines. This has ensured that Surinamese cooking has spawned many dishes; the different groups were influenced by each other's dishes and ingredients; this new Surinamese cuisine included <a href="/wiki/Roti" title="Roti">roti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nasi_goreng" title="Nasi goreng">nasi goreng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mie_goreng" title="Mie goreng">bami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pom_(dish)" title="Pom (dish)">pom</a>, <i>snesi foroe</i>, <i>moksi meti</i>, and <i>losi foroe</i>; because of this blending of many cultures, Surinamese cuisine is a unique creation. Basic foods include <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>, plants such as <a href="/wiki/Xanthosoma_sagittifolium" title="Xanthosoma sagittifolium">tayer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cassava" title="Cassava">cassava</a>, and roti. Usually, there is <a href="/wiki/Chicken_(food)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicken (food)">chicken</a> on the menu in many variations of the Chinese <i>snesi foroe</i>, the Indian <i>chicken masala</i> and <i>pom</i>, a very popular party dish of Creole origin. Also, <a href="/wiki/Salted_meat" class="mw-redirect" title="Salted meat">salted meat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stockfish" title="Stockfish">stockfish</a> (<i>bakkeljauw</i>) are widely used. <a href="/wiki/Yardlong_beans" class="mw-redirect" title="Yardlong beans">Yardlong beans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Okra" title="Okra">okra</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eggplant" title="Eggplant">eggplant</a> are examples of <a href="/wiki/Vegetables" class="mw-redirect" title="Vegetables">vegetables</a> in the Surinamese kitchen. For a spicy taste, <a href="/wiki/Madame_Jeanette" title="Madame Jeanette">Madame Jeanette</a> peppers are used.
</p><p>Besides the casserole <i>pom</i>, roti (often served with a filling of <a href="/wiki/Chicken_masala" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicken masala">chicken masala</a>, <a href="/wiki/Potato" title="Potato">potato</a> and vegetables) is also often served on festive occasions with many guests. Other well known dishes are <i>moksi-alesi</i> (mixed boiled rice with <a href="/wiki/Salted_meat" class="mw-redirect" title="Salted meat">salted meat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shrimp_(food)" class="mw-redirect" title="Shrimp (food)">shrimp</a> or <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>, and any vegetable), <a href="/wiki/Rice_and_beans" title="Rice and beans">rice and beans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peanut_soup" title="Peanut soup">peanut soup</a>, battered <a href="/wiki/Fried_plantain" title="Fried plantain">fried plantain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vada_(food)" title="Vada (food)">bara</a> and the original Javanese nasi goreng and <a href="/wiki/Mie_goreng" title="Mie goreng">mie goreng</a>.
</p><p>Desserts include <i>boyo</i>, a sweet cake made with <a href="/wiki/Coconut" title="Coconut">coconut</a> and cassava, and <i>fiadu</i>, a cake containing <a href="/wiki/Raisins" class="mw-redirect" title="Raisins">raisins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zante_currant" title="Zante currant">currants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Almonds" class="mw-redirect" title="Almonds">almonds</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Succade" title="Succade">succade</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Culture_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Religion_in_Suriname" title="Religion in Suriname">Religion in Suriname</a></div>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>: 48.4%</li>
