	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Unitary state</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:372px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_of_unitary_and_federal_states.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Map_of_unitary_and_federal_states.svg/370px-Map_of_unitary_and_federal_states.svg.png" decoding="async" width="370" height="190" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Map_of_unitary_and_federal_states.svg/555px-Map_of_unitary_and_federal_states.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Map_of_unitary_and_federal_states.svg/740px-Map_of_unitary_and_federal_states.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="863" data-file-height="443" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_of_unitary_and_federal_states.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #0000b0;; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;Unitary states</div> <div class="legend" style="-webkit-column-break-inside: avoid;page-break-inside: avoid;break-inside: avoid-column"><span class="legend-color" style="display:inline-block; width:1.5em; height:1.5em; margin:1px 0; border:1px solid black; background-color: #00e000;; color:black; font-size:100%; text-align:center;">&#160;</span>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">Federal</a> states</div></div></div></div>
<p>A <b>unitary state</b> is a <a href="/wiki/State_(polity)" title="State (polity)">state</a> governed as a single power in which the <a href="/wiki/Central_government" title="Central government">central government</a> is ultimately supreme. The central government may create (or abolish) <a href="/wiki/Administrative_division" title="Administrative division">administrative divisions</a> (sub-national units).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Such units exercise only the powers that the central government chooses to delegate. Although <a href="/wiki/Power_(social_and_political)" title="Power (social and political)">political power</a> may be delegated through <a href="/wiki/Devolution" title="Devolution">devolution</a> to regional or <a href="/wiki/Local_government" title="Local government">local governments</a> by <a href="/wiki/Statute" title="Statute">statute</a>, the central government may abrogate the acts of <a href="/wiki/Devolution" title="Devolution">devolved governments</a> or curtail (or expand) their powers. A large majority of the world's states (165 of the 193 <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_United_Nations" title="Member states of the United Nations">UN member states</a>) have a unitary system of government.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Unitary states stand in contrast with <a href="/wiki/Federation" title="Federation">federations</a>, also known as federal states. In federations, the provincial governments share powers with the central government as equal actors through a written <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a>, to which the consent of both is required to make amendments. This means that the sub-national units have a right of existence and powers that cannot be unilaterally changed by the central government.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland</a> is an example of a unitary state. <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> have a degree of autonomous devolved power, but such power is delegated by the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament of the United Kingdom</a>, which may enact laws unilaterally altering or abolishing devolution (<a href="/wiki/Devolved_English_parliament" title="Devolved English parliament">England does not have any devolved power</a>). Similarly in the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Kingdom of Spain</a>, the devolved powers are delegated through the central government.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Many unitary states have no areas possessing a degree of autonomy.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In such countries, sub-national regions cannot decide their own laws. Examples are <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Kingdom of Norway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
