<p>The <b>World War II Victory Medal</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Service_medal" title="Service medal">service medal</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_military" class="mw-redirect" title="United States military">United States military</a> which was established by an Act of <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a> on 6 July 1945 (Public Law 135, 79th Congress) and promulgated by Section V, War Department Bulletin 12, 1945.<sup id="cite_ref-Army_Regulation_600-8-22_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Army_Regulation_600-8-22-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WW2VM_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW2VM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/World_War_I_Victory_Medal_(United_States)" title="World War I Victory Medal (United States)">World War I Victory Medal</a> is the corresponding medal from <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_War_II_Victory_Medal_(United_States)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The World War II Victory Medal was first issued as a <a href="/wiki/Service_ribbon" title="Service ribbon">service ribbon</a> referred to as the “Victory Ribbon.”  The World War II Victory Medal was established by an Act of Congress on 6 July 1945 (Public Law 135, 79th Congress) and promulgated by Section V, War Department Bulletin 12, 1945. The medal was designed by Mr. Thomas H. Jones and approved by the Secretary of War on 5 February 1946. Consequently, it did not transition from a ribbon to a full medal until after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> had ended.
</p><p>The Congressional authorization for the medal specified that it was to be awarded to any member of the United States military, including members of the armed forces of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_the_Philippines" title="Commonwealth of the Philippines">Government of the Philippine Islands</a>, who served on active duty, or as a reservist, between 7 December 1941 and 31 December 1946.<sup id="cite_ref-WW2VM_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW2VM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 8 August 1946, the separate <a href="/wiki/Merchant_Marine_World_War_II_Victory_Medal" title="Merchant Marine World War II Victory Medal">Merchant Marine World War II Victory Medal</a> was established for members of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Merchant_Marine" title="United States Merchant Marine">United States Merchant Marine</a> who served during World War II.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria">Criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_War_II_Victory_Medal_(United_States)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The medal is awarded for service between 7 December 1941 and 31 December 1946, both dates inclusive.<sup id="cite_ref-Army_Regulation_600-8-22_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Army_Regulation_600-8-22-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/National_Personnel_Records_Center" title="National Personnel Records Center">National Personnel Records Center</a> has reported some cases of service members receiving the award for simply a few days of service. As the Second World War ended on 2 September 1945, there may be cases of service members who had enlisted, entered officer candidate school, or had been a cadet or midshipman at the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Military_Academy" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Military Academy">U.S. Military Academy</a>, the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Naval_Academy" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Naval Academy">U.S. Naval Academy</a> or the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Guard_Academy" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Coast Guard Academy">U.S. Coast Guard Academy</a> between 3 September 1945 and any date in 1946, receiving the medal without having been a veteran of World War II. The reason for this late date is that President <a href="/wiki/Harry_S._Truman" title="Harry S. Truman">Harry S. Truman</a> did not declare an official end of hostilities until the last day of 1946.<sup id="cite_ref-WW2VM_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW2VM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As every member of the United States Armed Forces who served from December 7, 1941 to December 31, 1946 was eligible for the medal, there were over 12 million eligible recipients, making the World War II Victory Medal one of the most widely awarded decorations of the United States military.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearance">Appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_War_II_Victory_Medal_(United_States)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bronze medal is 1 &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>4</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span> inches in width. The <a href="/wiki/Obverse" class="mw-redirect" title="Obverse">obverse</a> is a figure of Liberation standing full length with head turned to dexter looking to the dawn of a new day, right foot resting on a war god’s helmet with the hilt of a broken sword in the right hand and the broken blade in the left hand, the inscription <i>WORLD WAR II</i> placed immediately below the center. On the 
<a href="/wiki/Obverse_and_reverse" title="Obverse and reverse">reverse</a> are inscriptions for the <a href="/wiki/Four_Freedoms" title="Four Freedoms">Four Freedoms</a>: <i>FREEDOM FROM FEAR AND WANT</i> and <i>FREEDOM OF SPEECH AND RELIGION</i> separated by a palm branch, all within a circle composed of the words <i>UNITED STATES OF AMERICA 1941 1945</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-WW2VM_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW2VM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The suspension and <a href="/wiki/Service_ribbon" title="Service ribbon">service ribbon</a> of the medal is 1 &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span> inches wide and consists of the following stripes: &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span> inch double rainbow in juxtaposition (blues, greens, yellows, reds (center), yellows greens and blues); &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>32</sub></span> inch White 67101; center &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>9</sup>&#8260;<sub>16</sub></span> inch Old Glory Red 67156; &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>32</sub></span> inch White; and &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span> inch double rainbow in juxtaposition. The rainbow on each side of the ribbon is a miniature of the pattern used in the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I_Victory_Medal_(United_States)" title="World War I Victory Medal (United States)">World War I Victory Medal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WW2VM_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WW2VM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although the World War I Victory Medal included <a href="/wiki/Medal_bar" title="Medal bar">clasps</a>, the World War II Victory Medal did not. This was because <a href="/wiki/Campaign_medal" title="Campaign medal">campaign medals</a> were awarded instead.  
</p>
