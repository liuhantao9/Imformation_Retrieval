<p>The <b>Netherlands women's national cricket team</b>, nicknamed the <b>Lionesses</b>, represents the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> in international <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_cricket" title="Women&#39;s cricket">women's cricket</a>. The team is organised by the <a href="/wiki/Koninklijke_Nederlandse_Cricket_Bond" title="Koninklijke Nederlandse Cricket Bond">Koninklijke Nederlandse Cricket Bond</a> (KNCB), which has been an associate member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Cricket_Council" title="International Cricket Council">International Cricket Council</a> (ICC) since 1966.
</p><p>A Dutch women's team first played an international match in 1937, when <a href="/wiki/Australia_women%27s_national_cricket_team" title="Australia women&#39;s national cricket team">Australia</a> toured on its way to play a series in England. The team regularly played fixtures against English club sides over the following decades, but it was not until the early 1980s that regular international competition commenced.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The Netherlands made its <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_One_Day_International" title="Women&#39;s One Day International">One Day International</a> (ODI) debut in 1984, against <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_women%27s_national_cricket_team" title="New Zealand women&#39;s national cricket team">New Zealand</a>, and made its <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Cricket_World_Cup" title="Women&#39;s Cricket World Cup">World Cup</a> debut at the <a href="/wiki/1988_Women%27s_Cricket_World_Cup" title="1988 Women&#39;s Cricket World Cup">1988 edition</a> of the tournament, in Australia. Considered a top-level team from the late 1980s through to the early 2000s, the Netherlands participated in four consecutive World Cups between 1988 and 2000, and made the quarter-finals of the <a href="/wiki/1997_Women%27s_Cricket_World_Cup" title="1997 Women&#39;s Cricket World Cup">1997 event</a>. Since 2000, the Dutch side has not qualified for either the World Cup or the <a href="/wiki/ICC_Women%27s_World_Twenty20" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC Women&#39;s World Twenty20">World Twenty20</a>, although it retained ODI status until the <a href="/wiki/2011_Women%27s_Cricket_World_Cup_Qualifier" title="2011 Women&#39;s Cricket World Cup Qualifier">2011 World Cup Qualifier</a>. <a href="/wiki/South_African_women%27s_cricket_team_in_the_Netherlands_in_2007" title="South African women&#39;s cricket team in the Netherlands in 2007">In 2007</a>, the team played a one-off <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">Test</a> match against South Africa, joining <a href="/wiki/Ireland_women%27s_cricket_team" title="Ireland women&#39;s cricket team">Ireland</a> as the only associate member of the ICC to play at that level.
</p><p>In April 2018, the ICC granted full <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Twenty20_International" title="Women&#39;s Twenty20 International">Women's Twenty20 International</a> (WT20I) status to all its members. Therefore, all <a href="/wiki/Twenty20" title="Twenty20">Twenty20</a> matches played between the Netherlands women and another international side after 1 July 2018 will be a full WT20I.<sup id="cite_ref-status_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-status-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Netherlands_women%27s_national_cricket_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Netherlands_women%27s_national_cricket_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Netherlands took part in women's international cricket from its earliest years, as early as 1937 they hosted the Australians on the first leg of their first ever Women's Ashes tour, before visiting England late the same year.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980s">1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Netherlands_women%27s_national_cricket_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1980s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Dutch team played their first ODI in 1984 against <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_women%27s_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="New Zealand women&#39;s cricket team">New Zealand</a>. This was 12 years before the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Dutch cricket team">Dutch men's team</a> played their first ODI. They lost that game by 67 runs, and were next seen in international cricket in 1988, playing in their first <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Cricket_World_Cup" title="Women&#39;s Cricket World Cup">World Cup</a>, in which they finished in last place. They finished third in the first <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_European_Cricket_Championship" title="Women&#39;s European Cricket Championship">European Championship</a> in 1989.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1990s">1990s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Netherlands_women%27s_national_cricket_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1990s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>They again finished third in the European Championship in 1990, and finished fourth the following year. The 1993 World Cup was again a disappointment, with another last place finish. 1995 saw them again finish third in the European Championship.
</p><p>1997 was a busy year for the Dutch team, travelling to the <a href="/wiki/Mikkelberg-Kunst-und-Cricket_Center" title="Mikkelberg-Kunst-und-Cricket Center">Mikkelberg-Kunst-und-Cricket Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> to play two ODIs against <a href="/wiki/Danish_women%27s_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Danish women&#39;s cricket team">Denmark</a>, a trip they repeated in 1998. They also visited <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a> for a three match ODI series against <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lankan_women%27s_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Sri Lankan women&#39;s cricket team">the hosts</a>, which they won 2–1, which remains their sole ODI series victory. This was followed by the World Cup, in which they avoided last place by reaching the quarter finals before being knocked out by <a href="/wiki/Australian_women%27s_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian women&#39;s cricket team">Australia</a>.
</p><p>1999 saw another tour to Sri Lanka, where they lost the five match ODI series 5–0. This was followed by a last place finish in the European Championship in <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2000s">2000s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Netherlands_women%27s_national_cricket_team&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2000s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>2000 saw the Dutch team's fourth and, to date, final World Cup appearance, where they again finished last. This was followed in 2001 by a tour to <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> where <a href="/wiki/Pakistani_women%27s_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Pakistani women&#39;s cricket team">the hosts</a> went 4–0 up in the seven match ODI series before the Netherlands won the final three games. Later in the year saw another third-place finish in the European Championship.
</p><p>Their only cricket in 2002 was a three match ODI series against New Zealand, which saw three heavy defeats, two by more than 200 runs. The following year they hosted the <a href="/wiki/2003_IWCC_Trophy" title="2003 IWCC Trophy">2003 IWCC Trophy</a>, the inaugural edition of what is now the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Cricket_World_Cup_Qualifier" title="Women&#39;s Cricket World Cup Qualifier">World Cup Qualifier</a>. They needed to finish in the top two to gain qualification for the 2005 World Cup, but could only manage third place.
</p><p>Their next international engagement was the European Championship in 2005, finishing in fourth place. 2006 saw a two match ODI series against <a href="/wiki/Irish_women%27s_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish women&#39;s cricket team">Ireland</a>, which they lost 2–0. The year did see some good news for them though, as the ICC announced that the top ten women's teams would have Test and ODI status. Their third-place finish in the IWCC Trophy in 2003, meant that the Dutch were included in this top ten. In February 2008 the Dutch women retained their test status for another four years by reaching the semi-final of the Women's Cricket World Cup Qualifier.
</p>
