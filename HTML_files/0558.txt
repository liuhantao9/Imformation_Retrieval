	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Mill town</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For places called Milltown, see <a href="/wiki/Milltown_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Milltown (disambiguation)">Milltown</a>.</div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"Mill village" redirects here. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Mill_Village_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Mill Village (disambiguation)">Mill Village</a>.</div>
<p>A <b>mill town</b>, also known as <b>factory town</b> or <b>mill village</b>, is typically a settlement that developed around one or more <a href="/wiki/List_of_types_of_mill#Manufacturing_facilities" title="List of types of mill">mills</a> or factories, usually <a href="/wiki/Cotton_mill" title="Cotton mill">cotton mills</a> or factories producing textiles.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mill_town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United Kingdom, the term "mill town" usually refers to the 19th century <a href="/wiki/Textile_manufacturing" title="Textile manufacturing">textile manufacturing</a> towns of <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">northern England</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Lowlands" title="Scottish Lowlands">Scottish Lowlands</a>, particularly those in <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> (cotton) and <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> (<a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">wool</a>).
</p><p>Some former mill towns have a symbol of the <a href="/wiki/Textile_industry" title="Textile industry">textile industry</a> in their town badge. Some towns may have statues dedicated to textile workers (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Colne" title="Colne">Colne</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) or have a symbol in the badge of local schools (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Ossett_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Ossett School">Ossett School</a>).

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Poland">Poland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mill_town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Poland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Zyrardow_panorama01.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Zyrardow_panorama01.jpg/220px-Zyrardow_panorama01.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="55" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Zyrardow_panorama01.jpg/330px-Zyrardow_panorama01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Zyrardow_panorama01.jpg/440px-Zyrardow_panorama01.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1800" data-file-height="448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Zyrardow_panorama01.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Zyrardow – winter panorama of main square</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Zyrardow">Zyrardow</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mill_town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Zyrardow">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The town grew out of a textile factory founded in 1833 by the sons of <a href="/wiki/Feliks_Lubienski" class="mw-redirect" title="Feliks Lubienski">Feliks Lubienski</a>, who owned the land where it was built. They brought in a specialist from France and his newly designed machines. He was French inventor, <a href="/wiki/Philippe_de_Girard" title="Philippe de Girard">Philippe de Girard</a> from <a href="/wiki/Lourmarin" title="Lourmarin">Lourmarin</a>. He became a director of the firm.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The factory town developed during the 19th century into a significant textile mill town in <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>.
In honour of Girard, 'Ruda Guzowska' as the original estate was called, was renamed <a href="/wiki/Zyrardow" class="mw-redirect" title="Zyrardow">Żyrardów</a>, a toponym derived of the <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">polonised</a> spelling of Girard's name.
</p><p>Most of Żyrardów's monuments are located in the manufacturing area which dates from the 19th and early 20th centuries. It is widely believed that Żyrardów's textile settlement is the only entire urban industrial complex from the 19th-century to be preserved in Europe.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mill_town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_England">New England</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mill_town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: New England">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Berlin_Panoramic.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Berlin_Panoramic.jpg/250px-Berlin_Panoramic.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="128" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Berlin_Panoramic.jpg/375px-Berlin_Panoramic.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Berlin_Panoramic.jpg/500px-Berlin_Panoramic.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="523" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Berlin_Panoramic.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Androscoggin_River" title="Androscoggin River">Androscoggin River</a> at <a href="/wiki/Berlin,_New_Hampshire" title="Berlin, New Hampshire">Berlin, New Hampshire</a></div></div></div>
<p>Beginning with <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Slater" title="Samuel Slater">Samuel Slater</a> and technological information smuggled out of England by <a href="/wiki/Francis_Cabot_Lowell_(businessman)" class="mw-redirect" title="Francis Cabot Lowell (businessman)">Francis Cabot Lowell</a>, large mills were established in <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> in the early to mid 19th century.  Mill towns, sometimes planned, built and owned as a <a href="/wiki/Company_town" title="Company town">company town</a>, grew in the shadow of the industries. The region became a manufacturing powerhouse along rivers like the <a href="/wiki/Housatonic_River" title="Housatonic River">Housatonic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quinebaug_River" title="Quinebaug River">Quinebaug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shetucket_River" title="Shetucket River">Shetucket</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackstone_Valley" title="Blackstone Valley">Blackstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Merrimack_River" title="Merrimack River">Merrimack</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nashua_River" title="Nashua River">Nashua</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cochecho_River" title="Cochecho River">Cocheco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saco_River" title="Saco River">Saco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Androscoggin_River" title="Androscoggin River">Androscoggin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kennebec_River" title="Kennebec River">Kennebec</a> or <a href="/wiki/Winooski_River" title="Winooski River">Winooski</a>.
</p><p>In the 20th century, alternatives to water power were developed, and it became more profitable for companies to manufacture textiles in southern states where cotton was grown and winters did not require significant heating costs.  Finally, the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a> acted as a catalyst that sent several struggling New England firms into bankruptcy.
</p>
