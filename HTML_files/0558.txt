<p>The <b>Cathedral-Basilica of Saint Peter and Paul</b>  also called <b>Saint Peter and Paul Cathedral</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i>Sint-Petrus-en-Pauluskathedraal</i>) is a wooden <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a> located in the centre of the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. It is the biggest wooden structure in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>, and the seat of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Paramaribo" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Paramaribo">Roman Catholic Diocese of Paramaribo</a>.
</p><p>The Catholic congregation in the city originally used a church adapted in 1826 from a Dutch Jewish theatre built in 1809. As the years passed, the church became too small for the growing number of Catholics in the city. In 1882 the diocese decided to build the <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a>. It was consecrated in 1885, but the towers were not completed until 1901. A major restoration was completed in 2010 and the cathedral was re-opened. The cathedral was designated as a <a href="/wiki/Minor_basilica" title="Minor basilica">minor basilica</a> by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Pope Francis</a> in 2014.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Peter_and_Paul_Cathedral,_Paramaribo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cathedral_Paramaribo.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Cathedral_Paramaribo.jpg/200px-Cathedral_Paramaribo.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="282" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Cathedral_Paramaribo.jpg/300px-Cathedral_Paramaribo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/30/Cathedral_Paramaribo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="390" data-file-height="549" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cathedral_Paramaribo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Cathedral of St. Peter and Paul in Paramaribo</div></div></div>
<p>In 1826 the Roman Catholic Church in Paramaribo adapted <i>De Verrenze Phoenix,</i> a former Dutch Jewish theatre built in 1809, to use as a church. In 1858 it was designated as a cathedral when a diocese was established in Paramaribo and a bishop assigned.
</p><p>When the congregation outgrew the building, the diocese decided to build a new cathedral. It was designed by Frans Harmes. Construction of the cathedral began January 30, 1883, on the site of a former Dutch Jewish <a href="/wiki/Theatre" title="Theatre">theatre</a> <i>De Verrezene Phoenix</i>, built in 1809. The cathedral interior is built of unpainted Surinamese <a href="/wiki/Cedar_wood" title="Cedar wood">cedar</a>. Although it was <a href="/wiki/Consecrated" class="mw-redirect" title="Consecrated">consecrated</a> in 1885, the towers were not completed until 1901.
</p><p>A poorly done restoration was completed in 1977, but the building began to fall into disrepair, encountering problems with tilting and <a href="/wiki/Termites" class="mw-redirect" title="Termites">termites</a>. In 2002 an extensive renovation was completed and the cathedral was returned to usable condition. With help of <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>-funding, formal restoration of the cathedral began in 2007 and, after 3 years, the church was re-opened on November 13, 2010.
</p><p>In 2009 the gate to the cathedral turned 200 years old.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Peter_and_Paul_Cathedral,_Paramaribo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Saint Peter and Paul Cathedral is the largest wooden structure in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">Western Hemisphere</a>. The cathedral has two confession rooms. The Dutch-Surinamese priest <a href="/wiki/Peter_Donders" title="Peter Donders">Peter Donders</a>, who had led the cathedral for years, is buried in here.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Measurements">Measurements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Peter_and_Paul_Cathedral,_Paramaribo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Measurements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>59.1&#160;m (194&#160;ft) long</li>
<li>14.6&#160;m (48&#160;ft) high in the main hall</li>
<li>16.5&#160;m (54&#160;ft) wide</li>
<li>44&#160;m (144&#160;ft) high in the tower up to the bronze cross</li></ul>
<p>This makes the <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a> the biggest and tallest wooden structure in the Western Hemisphere. The cathedral has space for about 900 people and was initially built for <a href="/wiki/Freedmen" class="mw-redirect" title="Freedmen">freedmen</a> and contract laborers.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bells">Bells</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Peter_and_Paul_Cathedral,_Paramaribo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bells">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The building has three bells in the west tower. The name of the smallest bell is <i>Alfhonsus</i> and it weighs 222&#160;kg. The biggest bell is named <i>John</i> and it weighs 827&#160;kg. The middle one is named <i>Rosa,</i> and it weighs 413&#160;kg.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pipe_organ">Pipe organ</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Peter_and_Paul_Cathedral,_Paramaribo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pipe organ">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Pipe_organ" title="Pipe organ">pipe organ</a> was constructed in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and initially contained 1,550 pipes. Many of the pipes have been stolen over the years, leaving the organ with a value of 400 euros. After restoration, it is expected to have a value of around 10 million euros.
</p>
