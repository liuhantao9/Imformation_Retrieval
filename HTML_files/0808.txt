<p><b>Joseph Patrick Kennedy</b> (September 6, 1888 – November 18, 1969) was an American businessman, investor, and politician known for his high-profile positions in United States government and for the political and other achievements of his children.
</p><p>Kennedy was born to a political family in <a href="/wiki/East_Boston" title="East Boston">East Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>. He made a large fortune as a stock market and commodity investor and later rolled over his profits by investing in real estate and a wide range of business industries across the United States. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, he was an assistant <a href="/wiki/General_manager" title="General manager">general manager</a> of a Boston area <a href="/wiki/Bethlehem_Steel" title="Bethlehem Steel">Bethlehem Steel</a> shipyard; through this position, he became acquainted with Franklin D. Roosevelt, who was the Assistant Secretary of the Navy. In the 1920s, Kennedy made huge profits by reorganizing and refinancing several <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a> studios; several acquisitions were ultimately merged into <a href="/wiki/Radio-Keith-Orpheum" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio-Keith-Orpheum">Radio-Keith-Orpheum</a> (RKO) studios.<sup id="cite_ref-Richard_J._Whalen_1964_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Richard_J._Whalen_1964-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy increased his fortune with distribution rights for <a href="/wiki/Scotch_whisky" title="Scotch whisky">Scotch whisky</a>. He owned the largest privately-owned building in the country, Chicago's <a href="/wiki/Merchandise_Mart" title="Merchandise Mart">Merchandise Mart</a>.
</p><p>Kennedy was a leading member of the <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_United_States_Democratic_Party" title="History of the United States Democratic Party">Democratic Party</a> and of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish American">Irish Catholic</a> community. President <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a> appointed Kennedy to be the first chairman of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a> (SEC), which he led from 1934 to 1935. Kennedy later directed the <a href="/wiki/Maritime_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Maritime Commission">Maritime Commission</a>. Kennedy served as the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom">United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom</a> from 1938 until late 1940, when he annoyed Roosevelt by his pessimism about Britain's survival. During the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Britain" title="Battle of Britain">Battle of Britain</a> in November 1940, Kennedy publicly suggested that "Democracy is finished in England. It may be here [in the United States]".<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Following this controversy, Kennedy resigned his position.
</p><p>Kennedy was married to <a href="/wiki/Rose_Kennedy" title="Rose Kennedy">Rose Kennedy</a>. During his later life he was heavily involved in the political careers of his sons. Three of the Kennedys' sons attained distinguished political positions: <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> (1917–1963) served as a U.S. Senator from Massachusetts and as President of the United States, <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert F. Kennedy</a> (1925–1968) served as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Attorney_General" title="United States Attorney General">Attorney General</a> and as a U.S. Senator from New York, and <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Edward M. Kennedy</a> (1932–2009) also served as a U.S. Senator from Massachusetts.
</p>

<h2><span id="Background.2C_early_life.2C_and_education"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Background,_early_life,_and_education">Background, early life, and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background, early life, and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:157px;"><a href="/wiki/File:JPBostonLatin.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Waist high portrait of man in his teens wearing a suit, leaning to his left so his right shoulder is cut off" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c9/JPBostonLatin.jpg" decoding="async" width="155" height="211" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="155" data-file-height="211" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:JPBostonLatin.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kennedy's yearbook photo from Boston Latin School</div></div></div>
<p>Joseph Patrick Kennedy was born in 1888 in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston, Massachusetts</a>. He was the elder son of <a href="/wiki/Mary_Augusta_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Mary Augusta Kennedy">Mary Augusta Kennedy</a> (Hickey) and businessman and politician <a href="/wiki/P._J._Kennedy" title="P. J. Kennedy">Patrick Joseph "P.J." Kennedy</a>. He had a younger brother, Francis (who died young), and two younger sisters, Mary and Margaret. All four of Joe's grandparents had immigrated to <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> in the 1840s to escape the <a href="/wiki/Irish_famine" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish famine">Irish famine</a>. He was born into a highly <a href="/wiki/Sectarianism" title="Sectarianism">sectarian society</a>, where Irish Catholics were excluded by upper-class <a href="/wiki/Boston_Brahmins" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston Brahmins">Boston Brahmins</a>. Boston Irish thus became active in the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic Party</a>, which included P.J. and numerous relatives. P.J. Kennedy was an accomplished businessman. A successful saloon business, investment ventures, and an influential role in local politics enabled him to provide a comfortable lifestyle for his family. His mother encouraged Joe to attend the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Latin_School" title="Boston Latin School">Boston Latin School</a>, where he was a below-average scholar but was popular among his classmates, winning election as class president and playing on the school <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> team.
</p><p>Kennedy followed in the footsteps of elder cousins by attending <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a>. He focused on becoming a social leader, working energetically to gain admittance to the prestigious <a href="/wiki/Hasty_Pudding_Club" title="Hasty Pudding Club">Hasty Pudding Club</a>. While at Harvard he joined the <a href="/wiki/Delta_Upsilon" title="Delta Upsilon">Delta Upsilon</a> International fraternity and played on the baseball team, but he was blackballed from the <a href="/wiki/Porcellian_Club" title="Porcellian Club">Porcellian Club</a>.
</p><p>In 1937, he received an honorary Doctor of Laws degree from <a href="/wiki/Oglethorpe_University" title="Oglethorpe University">Oglethorpe University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 7, 1914, Kennedy married <a href="/wiki/Rose_Kennedy" title="Rose Kennedy">Rose Fitzgerald</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-time.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-time.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> the eldest daughter of Boston Mayor <a href="/wiki/John_F._Fitzgerald" title="John F. Fitzgerald">John F. "Honey Fitz" Fitzgerald</a> and Mary Josephine "Josie" Hannon.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The marriage joined two of the city's most prominent political families.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_career">Business career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Upon graduation from college, Kennedy pursued a career in business and investing. In his mid- to late 20s, he made a large fortune as a stock market and commodity investor; he reinvested in real estate and a wide range of business industries. He did not build a significant business from scratch, but his timing as both buyer and seller was usually excellent.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Various criminals, such as <a href="/wiki/Frank_Costello" title="Frank Costello">Frank Costello</a>, have boasted they worked with Kennedy in mysterious <a href="/wiki/Rum-running" title="Rum-running">bootlegging</a> operations during <a href="/wiki/Prohibition" title="Prohibition">Prohibition</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Okrent_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Okrent-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Scholars dismiss the claims. The most recent and most thorough biographer <a href="/wiki/David_Nasaw" title="David Nasaw">David Nasaw</a> asserts that no credible evidence has been found to link Kennedy to bootlegging activities.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> When <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> magazine published its first list of the <a href="/wiki/Wealthiest_Americans_(1957)" class="mw-redirect" title="Wealthiest Americans (1957)">richest people in the United States</a> in 1957, it placed Kennedy in the $200–400&#160;million group ($1.78&#160;billion–3.57&#160;billion today),<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-US_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> meaning he was somewhere between the ninth- and sixteenth-richest persons in the United States.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_ventures">Early ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Josephpatrickkennedysr.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Young man in his twenties in a suit, seated and looking back toward the camera" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Josephpatrickkennedysr.jpg/240px-Josephpatrickkennedysr.jpg" decoding="async" width="240" height="300" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Josephpatrickkennedysr.jpg/360px-Josephpatrickkennedysr.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Josephpatrickkennedysr.jpg/480px-Josephpatrickkennedysr.jpg 2x" data-file-width="760" data-file-height="951" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Josephpatrickkennedysr.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kennedy in 1914, aged 25, when he claimed to be America's youngest bank president</div></div></div>
<p>After Kennedy graduated from Harvard in 1912 with a bachelor's degree in economics, he took his first job as a state-employed bank examiner; this allowed him to learn a great deal about the banking industry. In 1913, the Columbia Trust Bank, in which his father held a significant share, was under threat of takeover. Kennedy borrowed $45,000 ($1,140,758 today)<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-US_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> from family and friends and bought back control. At the age of 25, he was rewarded by being elected the bank's president. Kennedy told the press he was "the youngest" bank president in America.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy emerged as a highly successful entrepreneur who had an eye for value. For example, he was a real estate investor who turned a handsome profit from ownership of Old Colony Realty Associates, Inc., which bought distressed real estate.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, President <a href="/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson" title="Woodrow Wilson">Woodrow Wilson</a> asked the Intercontinental Rubber Company—owned by <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Baruch" title="Bernard Baruch">Bernard Baruch</a>, <a href="/wiki/J._P._Morgan" title="J. P. Morgan">J. P. Morgan</a> and Kennedy—to grow <a href="/wiki/Guayule" class="mw-redirect" title="Guayule">guayule</a>. Guayule is a plant that produces latex that can be used to make rubber. Many feared the <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German</a> navy might blockade rubber shipments from Asia. As a result, Intercontinental established the Continental Farm and the little town of <a href="/wiki/Continental,_Arizona" title="Continental, Arizona">Continental</a> in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a> in 1916. The guayule-growing operation did not continue long; in 1922 the Continental Farm was sold to Queen <a href="/wiki/Wilhelmina_of_the_Netherlands" title="Wilhelmina of the Netherlands">Wilhelmina of the Netherlands</a>, who rented the fields to cotton farmers for several years.<sup id="cite_ref-Goorian,_pg._51_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Goorian,_pg._51-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although he was skeptical of American involvement in the war, Kennedy sought to participate in wartime production as an assistant general manager of <a href="/wiki/Fore_River_Shipyard" title="Fore River Shipyard">Fore River</a> a major <a href="/wiki/Bethlehem_Steel" title="Bethlehem Steel">Bethlehem Steel</a> shipyard in <a href="/wiki/Quincy,_Massachusetts" title="Quincy, Massachusetts">Quincy, Massachusetts</a>. There he oversaw the production of transports and warships. Through this job, he became acquainted with Assistant Secretary of the Navy <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Delano_Roosevelt" class="mw-redirect" title="Franklin Delano Roosevelt">Franklin Delano Roosevelt</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wall_Street_and_stock_market_investments">Wall Street and stock market investments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wall Street and stock market investments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1919, Kennedy joined the prominent <a href="/wiki/Brokerage_firm" class="mw-redirect" title="Brokerage firm">stock brokerage firm</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hayden,_Stone_%26_Co." title="Hayden, Stone &amp; Co.">Hayden, Stone &amp; Co.</a> where he became an expert dealing in the unregulated <a href="/wiki/Stock_market" title="Stock market">stock market</a> of the day, engaging in tactics that were later considered to be <a href="/wiki/Insider_trading" title="Insider trading">insider trading</a> and <a href="/wiki/Market_manipulation" title="Market manipulation">market manipulation</a>. He happened to be on the corner of Wall and Broad Streets at the moment of the <a href="/wiki/Wall_Street_bombing" title="Wall Street bombing">Wall Street bombing</a> on September 16, 1920, and was thrown to the ground by the force of the blast.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In 1923, he left Hayden and set up his own investment company. Kennedy subsequently became a multi-millionaire during the <a href="/wiki/Bull_market" class="mw-redirect" title="Bull market">bull market</a> of the 1920s and even wealthier as a result of taking "<a href="/wiki/Short_(finance)" title="Short (finance)">short</a>" positions in 1929.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/David_M._Kennedy_(historian)" title="David M. Kennedy (historian)">David M. Kennedy</a> (no relation to this Kennedy), described the Wall Street of the Kennedy era:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>[It] was a strikingly information-starved environment. Many firms whose securities were publicly traded published no regular reports or issued reports whose data were so arbitrarily selected and capriciously audited as to be worse than useless. It was this circumstance that had conferred such awesome power on a handful of investment bankers like J. P. Morgan, because they commanded a virtual monopoly of the information necessary for making sound financial decisions. Especially in the <a href="/wiki/Secondary_market" title="Secondary market">secondary markets</a>, where reliable information was all but impossible for the average investor to come by, opportunities abounded for insider manipulation and wildcat speculation.
</p></blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1929_Wall_Street_Crash">1929 Wall Street Crash</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1929 Wall Street Crash">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kennedy formed alliances with several other Irish-Catholic investors, including <a href="/wiki/Charles_E._Mitchell" title="Charles E. Mitchell">Charles E. Mitchell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_J._Meehan" title="Michael J. Meehan">Michael J. Meehan</a>, and Bernard Smith. He helped establish a "stock pool" to control trading in the stock of glassmaker <a href="/wiki/Libbey-Owens-Ford" title="Libbey-Owens-Ford">Libbey-Owens-Ford</a>. The arrangement drove up the value of the pool operators' holdings in the stock by using insider information and the public's lack of knowledge. Pool operators would bribe journalists to present information in the most advantageous manner. Pool operators tried to corner a stock and drive the price up, or drive the price down with a "<a href="/wiki/Bear_raid" title="Bear raid">bear raid</a>". Kennedy got into a bidding war for control of <a href="/wiki/Yellow_Cab_Company" title="Yellow Cab Company">Yellow Cab Company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy later claimed he understood that the rampant stock speculation <a href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Crash_of_1929" title="Wall Street Crash of 1929">of the late 1920s would lead to a market crash</a>. Supposedly, he said that he knew it was time to get out of the market when he received stock tips from a shoe-shine boy.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy survived the crash "because he possessed a passion for facts, a complete lack of sentiment and a marvelous sense of timing".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>, Kennedy vastly increased his fortune by investing most of his money in real estate. In 1929, Kennedy's fortune was estimated to be $4&#160;million (equivalent to $58.4&#160;million today).<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-US_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> By 1935, his wealth had increased to $180&#160;million (equivalent to $3.29&#160;billion today).<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-US_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Investments_in_entertainment.2C_shipping.2C_and_real_estate"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Investments_in_entertainment,_shipping,_and_real_estate">Investments in entertainment, shipping, and real estate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Investments in entertainment, shipping, and real estate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sadiethompsonlobbycard.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Lobby card for Sadie Thompson with head high portrait of woman in her thirties" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Sadiethompsonlobbycard.jpg/240px-Sadiethompsonlobbycard.jpg" decoding="async" width="240" height="184" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Sadiethompsonlobbycard.jpg/360px-Sadiethompsonlobbycard.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Sadiethompsonlobbycard.jpg/480px-Sadiethompsonlobbycard.jpg 2x" data-file-width="576" data-file-height="442" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sadiethompsonlobbycard.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kennedy, along with fifteen others, signed a telegram warning that the release of <i><a href="/wiki/Sadie_Thompson" title="Sadie Thompson">Sadie Thompson</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Swanson" title="Gloria Swanson">Gloria Swanson</a> would jeopardize the ability of the film industry to censor itself. Swanson needed financing for her film production company, and Kennedy began a three-year affair with her when he met her for lunch in New York after the film's release.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>Kennedy made huge profits from reorganizing and refinancing several Hollywood film studios. Film production in the U.S. was much more decentralized than it is today, with many different movie studios producing film product.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> One small studio was <a href="/wiki/Film_Booking_Offices_of_America" title="Film Booking Offices of America">Film Booking Offices of America</a> (or FBO), which specialized in Westerns produced cheaply. Its owner was in financial trouble, and asked Kennedy to help find a new owner. Kennedy formed his own group of investors and bought it for $1.5&#160;million (about $21.4&#160;million today).<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-US_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 1926, Kennedy moved to Hollywood to focus on running film studios. At that time, film studios were permitted to own exhibition companies, which were necessary to get their films on local screens. With that in mind, in a <a href="/wiki/Hostile_takeover" class="mw-redirect" title="Hostile takeover">hostile buyout</a>, he acquired the <a href="/wiki/Keith-Albee-Orpheum" title="Keith-Albee-Orpheum">Keith-Albee-Orpheum Theaters Corporation</a> (KAO), which had more than 700 <a href="/wiki/Vaudeville" title="Vaudeville">vaudeville</a> theaters across the United States that had begun showing movies. He later purchased another production studio called Pathe Exchange, and merged those two entities with <a href="/wiki/Cecil_B._DeMille" title="Cecil B. DeMille">Cecil B. DeMille</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Producers_Distributing_Corporation" title="Producers Distributing Corporation">Producers Distributing Corporation</a> in March 1927.
</p><p>In August 1928, he unsuccessfully tried to run First National Pictures.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In October 1928, he formally merged his film companies FBO and KAO to form <a href="/wiki/Radio-Keith-Orpheum" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio-Keith-Orpheum">Radio-Keith-Orpheum</a> (RKO) and made a large amount of money in the process. Then, keen to buy the <a href="/wiki/Pantages_Theatre_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Pantages Theatre (disambiguation)">Pantages Theatre</a> chain, which had 63 profitable theaters, Kennedy made an offer of $8&#160;million ($117&#160;million today).<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-US_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It was declined. He then stopped distributing his movies to Pantages. Still, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Pantages" title="Alexander Pantages">Alexander Pantages</a> declined to sell. However, when Pantages was later charged and tried for rape, his reputation took a battering, and he accepted Kennedy's revised offer of $3.5&#160;million ($51.1&#160;million today).<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-US_9-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Pantages, who claimed that Kennedy had "set him up", was later found not guilty at a second trial. The girl who had accused Pantages of rape, Eunice Pringle, confessed on her deathbed that Kennedy was the mastermind of the plot to frame Pantages.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:211px;"><a href="/wiki/File:James_Roosevelt-Harris_%26_Ewing.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/91/James_Roosevelt-Harris_%26_Ewing.jpg" decoding="async" width="209" height="257" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="209" data-file-height="257" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:James_Roosevelt-Harris_%26_Ewing.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/James_Roosevelt" title="James Roosevelt">James Roosevelt</a>, son of President <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a>, helped Kennedy start his liquor business after Prohibition.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>Many estimate that Kennedy made over $5&#160;million ($73&#160;million today)<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-US_9-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> from his investments in Hollywood. During his three-year affair with film star <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Swanson" title="Gloria Swanson">Gloria Swanson</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Beauchamp,_Cari_2009_p_263-5_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beauchamp,_Cari_2009_p_263-5-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> he arranged the financing for her films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Love_of_Sunya" title="The Love of Sunya">The Love of Sunya</a></i> (1927) and the ill-fated <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_Kelly" title="Queen Kelly">Queen Kelly</a></i> (1928). The duo also used Hollywood's famous "body sculptor", masseuse <a href="/wiki/Sylvia_of_Hollywood" title="Sylvia of Hollywood">Sylvia of Hollywood</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Beauchamp,_Cari_2009_p_263-5_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beauchamp,_Cari_2009_p_263-5-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Their relationship ended when Swanson discovered that an expensive gift from Kennedy had been charged to her account.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A recurring rumor alleges that he made money in <a href="/wiki/Rum-running" title="Rum-running">bootlegging</a> illegal liquor during <a href="/wiki/Prohibition_in_the_United_States" title="Prohibition in the United States">Prohibition</a>. Historians have not found credible evidence of this. On the contrary, there is abundant evidence that as the end of prohibition loomed (in 1933), Kennedy invested heavily in Scottish distilleries. As soon as it became legal he imported large shipments of high-priced Scotch and made a large profit. Numerous criminals spread various contradictory "bootlegging" stories, such as Canadian distiller <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Bronfman" title="Samuel Bronfman">Samuel Bronfman</a> and to <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">New England</a> bootlegger <a href="/wiki/Danny_Walsh" title="Danny Walsh">Danny Walsh</a> and his crime syndicate. They did illegally smuggle spirits across the Canada–US border. After Prohibition ended, Bronfman had a bitter rivalry with Kennedy in acquiring North American liquor distribution rights.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> At the start of the Franklin Roosevelt administration in March 1933, Kennedy and future Congressman <a href="/wiki/James_Roosevelt" title="James Roosevelt">James Roosevelt II</a> founded Somerset Importers, an entity that acted as the exclusive American agent for <a href="/wiki/Haig_(whisky)" title="Haig (whisky)">Haig &amp; Haig</a> Scotch, <a href="/wiki/Gordon%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="Gordon&#39;s">Gordon's</a> Dry Gin and <a href="/wiki/Dewar%27s" title="Dewar&#39;s">Dewar's</a> Scotch. Kennedy kept his Somerset company for years.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> (Kennedy himself drank little alcohol. He so disapproved of what he considered a stereotypical Irish vice that he offered his sons $1,000 to not drink until they turned 21.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>)
</p><p>Kennedy invested his profits from alcohol into residential and commercial real estate in New York, the <a href="/wiki/Le_Pavillon" class="mw-redirect" title="Le Pavillon">Le Pavillon</a> restaurant, and the <a href="/wiki/Hialeah_Park_Race_Track" title="Hialeah Park Race Track">Hialeah Park Race Track</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hialeah,_Florida" title="Hialeah, Florida">Hialeah, Florida</a>. In addition, Kennedy purchased spirits-importation rights from <a href="/wiki/Schenley_Industries" title="Schenley Industries">Schenley Industries</a>, a firm in Canada.<sup id="cite_ref-Richard_J._Whalen_1964_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Richard_J._Whalen_1964-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His most important purchase was the largest privately owned building in the country, Chicago's <a href="/wiki/Merchandise_Mart" title="Merchandise Mart">Merchandise Mart</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> which gave his family a grounding in that city and an alliance with the city's Irish-American political leadership.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_career">Political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="SEC_Chairman_.281934.E2.80.931935.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="SEC_Chairman_(1934–1935)">SEC Chairman (1934–1935)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: SEC Chairman (1934–1935)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1932, Kennedy supported <a href="/wiki/Franklin_Delano_Roosevelt" class="mw-redirect" title="Franklin Delano Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a> in his bid for the Presidency. This was his first major involvement in a national political campaign, and he donated, loaned, and raised a substantial amount of money for the campaign.
</p><p>In 1934, Congress established the independent <a href="/wiki/Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Securities and Exchange Commission">Securities and Exchange Commission</a> to end irresponsible market manipulations and dissemination of false information about securities.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> In the 21st century, the SEC remains one of the most powerful government agencies. Its predecessor had been ineffective in 1933–34 as part of another agency and the financial market was dying. Roosevelt named Kennedy to head the SEC clean up of Wall Street. The <a href="/wiki/New_Deal" title="New Deal">New Deal</a> attracted many of the nation's most talented young lawyers. Roosevelt's brain trust drew up a list of recommended candidates for the SEC chairmanship. Kennedy headed the list, which stated he was "the best bet for Chairman because of executive ability, knowledge of habits and customs of business to be regulated and ability to moderate different points of view on Commission."<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy sought out the best lawyers available giving him a hard-driving team with a mission for reform. They included <a href="/wiki/William_O._Douglas" title="William O. Douglas">William O. Douglas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Abe_Fortas" title="Abe Fortas">Abe Fortas</a>, both of whom were later named to the Supreme Court.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>  The SEC had four missions. First and foremost was to restore investor confidence in the securities market which had collapsed on account of its questionability, and the external threats supposedly posed by anti-business elements in the Roosevelt administration. Second, the SEC had to get rid of the penny ante swindles based on false information, fraudulent devices, and <a href="/wiki/Get-rich-quick_scheme" title="Get-rich-quick scheme">get-rich-quick schemes</a>. Thirdly, and much more important than the frauds, the SEC had to end the million-dollar maneuvers in major corporations, whereby insiders with access to much better information about the company knew when to buy or sell their own securities. A crackdown on insider trading was essential. Finally, the SEC had to set up a complex system of registration for all securities sold in America, with a clear set of rules, deadlines and guidelines that all companies had to follow. The main challenge faced by the young lawyers was drafting precise rules. The SEC succeeded in its four missions, as Kennedy reassured the American business community that they would no longer be deceived and taken advantage of by Wall Street. He trumpeted for ordinary investors to return to the market and enable the economy to grow again.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy's reforming work as SEC Chairman was widely praised on all sides, as investors realized the SEC was protecting their interests. He resigned from the SEC in 1935.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chairman_of_U.S._Maritime_Commission">Chairman of U.S. Maritime Commission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Chairman of U.S. Maritime Commission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1937, Kennedy became the first Chairman of the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Maritime_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Maritime Commission">Maritime Commission</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> which built on his wartime experience in running a major shipyard.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_Father_Charles_Coughlin">Relationship with Father Charles Coughlin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Relationship with Father Charles Coughlin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Father <a href="/wiki/Charles_Coughlin" title="Charles Coughlin">Charles Coughlin</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Irish-Canadian" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish-Canadian">Irish-Canadian</a> priest near <a href="/wiki/Detroit,_Michigan" class="mw-redirect" title="Detroit, Michigan">Detroit</a>, became the most prominent Roman Catholic spokesman on political and financial issues in the 1930s, with a radio audience that reached millions every week. Having been a strong supporter of Roosevelt since 1932, in 1934 Coughlin broke with the president, who became a bitter opponent of Coughlin's weekly anti-communist, anti-Semitic, far-right, anti–Federal Reserve and isolationist radio talks. Roosevelt sent Kennedy and other prominent Irish Catholics to try to tone down Coughlin.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coughlin swung his support to <a href="/wiki/Huey_Long" title="Huey Long">Huey Long</a> in 1935 and then to <a href="/wiki/William_Lemke" title="William Lemke">William Lemke</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Union_Party_(United_States)" title="Union Party (United States)">Union Party</a> in 1936. Kennedy strongly supported the New Deal (Father Coughlin believed that the New Deal did not go far enough – indeed that Franklin Roosevelt was a tool of the rich) and reportedly believed as early as 1933 that Coughlin was "becoming a very dangerous proposition" as an opponent of Roosevelt and "an out and out demagogue". In 1936, Kennedy worked with Roosevelt, Bishop <a href="/wiki/Francis_Spellman" title="Francis Spellman">Francis Spellman</a> and Cardinal Eugenio Pacelli (later <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_XII" title="Pope Pius XII">Pope Pius XII</a>) to shut Coughlin down.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> When Coughlin returned to the air in 1940, Kennedy continued to battle against his influence among Irish Americans.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite his public disputes with Coughlin, it has also been acknowledged that Kennedy would also accompany Coughlin whenever the priest visited Roosevelt at Hyde Park.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> A historian with <a href="/wiki/History_News_Network" title="History News Network">History News Network</a> also stated that Coughlin was in fact a friend of Kennedy as well.<sup id="cite_ref-kennedyfailure_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kennedyfailure-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> In a <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston Post">Boston Post</a></i> article of August 16, 1936, Coughlin referred to Kennedy as the "shining star among the dim 'knights' in the [Roosevelt] Administration."<sup id="cite_ref-Maier2009_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maier2009-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Ambassador_to_the_United_Kingdom_.281938.E2.80.931940.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Ambassador_to_the_United_Kingdom_(1938–1940)">Ambassador to the United Kingdom (1938–1940)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Ambassador to the United Kingdom (1938–1940)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Joseph_and_Rose_Kennedy_1940.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Joseph_and_Rose_Kennedy_1940.JPG/240px-Joseph_and_Rose_Kennedy_1940.JPG" decoding="async" width="240" height="213" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Joseph_and_Rose_Kennedy_1940.JPG/360px-Joseph_and_Rose_Kennedy_1940.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Joseph_and_Rose_Kennedy_1940.JPG/480px-Joseph_and_Rose_Kennedy_1940.JPG 2x" data-file-width="614" data-file-height="546" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Joseph_and_Rose_Kennedy_1940.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rose and Joseph Kennedy arrive for dinner at <a href="/wiki/The_Colony_(restaurant)" title="The Colony (restaurant)">The Colony Restaurant</a> in Manhattan, November 1, 1940. At the time, Kennedy was the US Ambassador to the UK.</div></div></div>
<p>In 1938, Roosevelt appointed Kennedy as the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_the_Court_of_St._James%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to the Court of St. James&#39;s">United States Ambassador to the Court of St. James's</a> (<a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a>). Kennedy hoped to succeed Roosevelt in the White House in 1940.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy hugely enjoyed his leadership position in London high society, which stood in stark contrast to his relative outsider status in Boston. On May 6, 1944, his daughter <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington" title="Kathleen Cavendish, Marchioness of Hartington">Kathleen</a> married <a href="/wiki/William_Cavendish,_Marquess_of_Hartington" title="William Cavendish, Marquess of Hartington">William "Billy" Cavendish</a>, the elder son of the <a href="/wiki/Edward_Cavendish,_10th_Duke_of_Devonshire" title="Edward Cavendish, 10th Duke of Devonshire">Duke of Devonshire</a>, the head of one of England's most aristocratic families.
</p><p>Kennedy rejected the belief of <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> that any compromise with <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> was impossible. Instead, he supported Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain" title="Neville Chamberlain">Neville Chamberlain</a>'s policy of <a href="/wiki/Appeasement" title="Appeasement">appeasement</a>. Throughout 1938, while the Nazi persecution of the Jews in Germany intensified, Kennedy attempted to arrange a meeting with <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Shortly before the <a href="/wiki/The_Blitz" title="The Blitz">Nazi bombing of British cities</a> began in September 1940, Kennedy once again sought a personal meeting with Hitler without the approval of the U. S. Department of State, in order to "bring about a better understanding between the United States and Germany".<sup id="cite_ref-Hersh_63_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hersh_63-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy also argued strongly against providing military and economic aid to the United Kingdom. "Democracy is finished in England. It may be here", he stated in the <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Sunday_Globe" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston Sunday Globe">Boston Sunday Globe</a></i> of November 10, 1940. With German troops having overrun <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, and with daily bombings of Great Britain, Kennedy unambiguously and repeatedly stated that the war was not about saving democracy from National Socialism (Nazism) or from Fascism. In an interview with two newspaper journalists, <a href="/wiki/Louis_M._Lyons" title="Louis M. Lyons">Louis M. Lyons</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boston_Globe" title="The Boston Globe">The Boston Globe</a></i>, and Ralph Coghlan of the <i><a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Post-Dispatch" title="St. Louis Post-Dispatch">St. Louis Post-Dispatch</a></i>, Kennedy said:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886047036"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>It's all a question of what we do with the next six months. The whole reason for aiding England is to give us time&#160;... As long as she is in there, we have time to prepare. It isn't that [Britain is] fighting for democracy. That's the bunk. She's fighting for self-preservation, just as we will if it comes to us. ... I know more about the European situation than anybody else, and it's up to me to see that the country gets it.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>
<p>His views were becoming inconsistent and increasingly isolationist. British MP <a href="/wiki/Josiah_Wedgwood,_1st_Baron_Wedgwood" title="Josiah Wedgwood, 1st Baron Wedgwood">Josiah Wedgwood IV</a>, who had himself opposed the British government's earlier appeasement policy, said of Kennedy:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886047036"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>We have a rich man, untrained in diplomacy, unlearned in history and politics, who is a great publicity seeker and who apparently is ambitious to be the first Catholic president of the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>
<p>Kennedy told a British reporter in late 1939 that he was confident that Roosevelt would "fall" in <a href="/wiki/1940_United_States_presidential_election" title="1940 United States presidential election">1940 (i.e. in that year's presidential election)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kennedyfailure_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kennedyfailure-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In British government circles during <a href="/wiki/The_Blitz" title="The Blitz">the Blitz</a>, Kennedy was widely disparaged as a defeatist. He retreated to the countryside during the bombings of London by German aircraft, at a time when the British Royal Family, Prime Minister, government ministers, and other ambassadors chose to stay in London. (This prompted a member of Britain's Foreign Office to say, "I thought my daffodils were yellow until I met Joe Kennedy.")
</p><p>When the White House read his quotes it became clear that Kennedy was completely out of step with Roosevelt's policies. Kennedy returned home. Roosevelt urgently needed his support to hold the Catholic vote and invited him to spend the night at the White House. Kennedy agreed to make a nationwide radio speech to advocate Roosevelt's reelection. Roosevelt was pleased with the speech because, Nasaw says, it successfully "rallied reluctant Irish Catholic voters to his side, buttressed his claims that he was not going to take the nation into war, and emphasized that he alone had the experience to lead the nation in these difficult times." After Roosevelt was reelected, Kennedy submitted his resignation as ambassador.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reduced_influence">Reduced influence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Reduced influence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Throughout the rest of the war, relations between Kennedy and the Roosevelt Administration remained tense, especially when <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_Jr." title="Joseph P. Kennedy Jr.">Joe Jr.</a> vocally opposed President Roosevelt's unprecedented nomination for a third term, which began in 1941. Kennedy may have wanted to run for president himself in 1940 or later. Having effectively removed himself from the national stage, Joe Sr. sat out <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> on the sidelines. Kennedy stayed active in the smaller venues of rallying Irish-American and Roman Catholic Democrats to vote for Roosevelt's re-election for a fourth term in 1944. Former Ambassador Kennedy claimed to be eager to help the war effort, but as a result of his previous gaffes, he was neither trusted nor invited to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to his <a href="/wiki/Philanthropy" title="Philanthropy">philanthropy</a> and a close friendship with <a href="/wiki/Francis_Spellman" title="Francis Spellman">Francis Spellman</a>, Archbishop of New York (later Cardinal), during this time, Kennedy was invested as a knight of the <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_Military_Order_of_Malta" title="Sovereign Military Order of Malta">Sovereign Military Order of Malta</a>, an honor which at that time he shared with just a few dozen Americans.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anti-Semitism">Anti-Semitism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Anti-Semitism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to Harvey Klemmer, who served as one of Kennedy's embassy aides, Kennedy habitually referred to Jews as "<a href="/wiki/Kike" title="Kike">kikes</a> or sheenies". Kennedy allegedly told Klemmer that "[some] individual Jews are all right, Harvey, but as a race they stink. They spoil everything they touch."<sup id="cite_ref-Hersh_63_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hersh_63-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> When Klemmer returned from a trip to Germany and reported the pattern of vandalism and assaults on Jews by Nazis, Kennedy responded, "Well, they brought it on themselves."<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 13, 1938, Kennedy met in London with <a href="/wiki/Herbert_von_Dirksen" title="Herbert von Dirksen">Herbert von Dirksen</a>, the German ambassador to the United Kingdom, who claimed upon his return to Berlin that Kennedy had told him that "it was not so much the fact that we want to get rid of the Jews that was so harmful to us, but rather the loud clamor with which we accompanied this purpose. [Kennedy] himself fully understood our Jewish policy."<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy's main concern with such violent acts against German Jews as <i><a href="/wiki/Kristallnacht" title="Kristallnacht">Kristallnacht</a></i> was that they generated bad publicity in the West for the Nazi regime, a concern that he communicated in a letter to <a href="/wiki/Charles_Lindbergh" title="Charles Lindbergh">Charles Lindbergh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy had a close friendship with <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Astor,_Viscountess_Astor" title="Nancy Astor, Viscountess Astor">Viscountess Astor</a> and their correspondence is replete with anti-Semitic statements.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> According to Edward Renehan:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886047036"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>As fiercely anti-Communist as they were anti-Semitic, Kennedy and Astor looked upon Adolf Hitler as a welcome solution to both of these "world problems" (Nancy's phrase). ... . Kennedy replied that he expected the "Jew media" in the United States to become a problem, that "Jewish pundits in New York and Los Angeles" were already making noises contrived to "set a match to the fuse of the world".<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>
<p>By August 1940, Kennedy worried that a third term for President Roosevelt would mean war. Laurence Leamer in <i>The Kennedy Men: 1901–1963</i> reports: "Joe believed that Roosevelt, Churchill, the Jews, and their allies would manipulate America into approaching <a href="/wiki/Armageddon" title="Armageddon">Armageddon</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Nevertheless, Kennedy supported Roosevelt's third term in return for Roosevelt's promise to support Joseph Kennedy Jr. in a run for Governor of Massachusetts in 1942.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> However, even during the darkest months of World War II, Kennedy remained "more wary of" prominent American Jews, such as Associate Justice <a href="/wiki/Felix_Frankfurter" title="Felix Frankfurter">Felix Frankfurter</a>, than he was of Hitler.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy told the reporter Joe Dinneen:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886047036"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>It is true that I have a low opinion of some Jews in public office and in private life. That does not mean that I. ... believe they should be wiped off the face of the Earth. ... Jews who take an unfair advantage of the fact that theirs is a persecuted race do not help much. ... Publicizing unjust attacks upon the Jews may help to cure the injustice, but continually publicizing the whole problem only serves to keep it alive in the public mind.
</p></blockquote>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Alliances">Alliances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Alliances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kennedy used his wealth and connections to build a national network of supporters that became the base for his sons' political careers. He especially concentrated on the <a href="/wiki/Irish_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish American">Irish American</a> community in large cities, particularly Boston, New York, Chicago, <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a> and several <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> cities.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy also used <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Krock" title="Arthur Krock">Arthur Krock</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>, America's most influential political columnist, for decades as a paid speechwriter and political advisor.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A political conservative (<a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> once described his father as being to "the right of <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Hoover" title="Herbert Hoover">Herbert Hoover</a>"),<sup id="cite_ref-Leuchtenburg2001_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leuchtenburg2001-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy supported <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">Richard Nixon</a>, who had entered Congress with John in 1947. In 1960 Joseph Kennedy approached Nixon, praised his anti-Communism, and said "Dick, if my boy can't make it, I'm for you" for the presidential election that year.<sup id="cite_ref-kakutani19960524_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kakutani19960524-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Alliance_with_Senator_Joseph_McCarthy">Alliance with Senator Joseph McCarthy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Alliance with Senator Joseph McCarthy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Kennedy's close ties with <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> (GOP) Senator <a href="/wiki/Joseph_McCarthy" title="Joseph McCarthy">Joseph McCarthy</a> strengthened his family's position among Irish Catholics, but weakened it among liberals who strongly opposed McCarthy. Even before McCarthy became famous in 1950, Kennedy had forged close ties with the Republican Senator. Kennedy often brought him to his family compound at <a href="/wiki/Hyannis_Port" class="mw-redirect" title="Hyannis Port">Hyannis Port</a> as a weekend house guest in the late 1940s. McCarthy at one point dated <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Patricia Kennedy">Patricia Kennedy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Michael_O_2005_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Michael_O_2005-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When McCarthy became a dominant voice of anti-Communism starting in 1950, Kennedy contributed thousands of dollars to McCarthy, and became one of his major supporters. In the Senate race of 1952, Kennedy apparently worked a deal so that McCarthy, a Republican, would not make campaign speeches for the GOP ticket in Massachusetts. In return, Congressman <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>, running for the Senate seat, would not give any anti-McCarthy speeches that his liberal supporters wanted to hear.<sup id="cite_ref-Michael_O_2005_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Michael_O_2005-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At Kennedy's urging in 1953, McCarthy hired <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Kennedy">Robert Kennedy</a> (aged 27) as a senior staff member of the <a href="/wiki/Permanent_Subcommittee_on_Investigations" class="mw-redirect" title="Permanent Subcommittee on Investigations">Senate's investigations subcommittee</a>, which McCarthy chaired. In 1954, when the Senate was threatening to condemn McCarthy, Senator John Kennedy faced a dilemma. "How could I demand that Joe McCarthy be censured for things he did when my own brother was on his staff?" asked JFK.<sup id="cite_ref-Michael_O_2005_58-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Michael_O_2005-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By 1954 Robert F. Kennedy and McCarthy's chief aide <a href="/wiki/Roy_Cohn" title="Roy Cohn">Roy Cohn</a> had fallen out with each other, and Robert no longer worked for McCarthy. John Kennedy had a speech drafted calling for the censure of McCarthy, but never delivered it. When the Senate voted to censure McCarthy on December 2, 1954, Senator Kennedy was in a hospital and never indicated how he would cast his vote. Joe Kennedy strongly supported McCarthy to the end.<sup id="cite_ref-Michael_O_2005_58-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Michael_O_2005-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Involvement_in_sons.27_political_careers"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Involvement_in_sons'_political_careers">Involvement in sons' political careers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Involvement in sons&#039; political careers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Kennedy's connections and influence were turned into political capital for the political campaigns of sons John, Robert and Ted.
</p><p>Kennedy had been consigned to the political shadows after his remarks during World War II ("Democracy is finished"), and he remained an intensely controversial figure among U.S. citizens because of his suspect business credentials, his Roman Catholicism, his opposition to Roosevelt's foreign policy, and his support for Joseph McCarthy. Although his own ambitions to achieve the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a> were thwarted, Kennedy held out great hope for his eldest son, Joseph P. Kennedy Jr., to seek the presidency. However, Joe Jr., who had become a <a href="/wiki/U.S._Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Navy">U.S. Navy</a> bomber pilot, was killed over the <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a> in August 1944 while undertaking <a href="/wiki/Operation_Aphrodite" title="Operation Aphrodite">Operation Anvil</a>, a high-risk, new way to use heavy bombers to strike German weapon sites in France. After grieving over his dead son, Joe Sr. turned his attention to his second son, John, for a run for the presidency.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Because of his own unpopularity, Kennedy's presence in John's 1960 presidential campaign had to be downplayed. However, Kennedy still drove the campaign behind the scenes. He played a central role in planning strategy, fundraising, and coalition and alliance building. Kennedy almost oversaw the entire operation, supervising spending, helping to select advertising agencies, phoning local and state party leaders, newsmen, and business leaders.
</p><p>When John F. Kennedy was asked about the level of involvement and influence that his father had held in his razor-thin presidential victory over <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">Richard Nixon</a>, he would joke that on the eve of the election his father had asked him the exact number of votes he would need to win: there was no way he was paying "for a landslide". Kennedy was one of four fathers (the other three being <a href="/wiki/George_Tryon_Harding" title="George Tryon Harding">George Tryon Harding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nathaniel_Fillmore" title="Nathaniel Fillmore">Nathaniel Fillmore</a>, and <a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">George Herbert Walker Bush</a>) to live through the entire presidency of a son.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Historian Richard J. Whalen describes Kennedy's influence on John F. Kennedy's policy decisions in his biography of Kennedy. Kennedy was influential in creating the Kennedy Cabinet (which included Robert Kennedy as Attorney General, although he had never argued or tried a case).<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1961, Kennedy suffered a <a href="/wiki/Stroke" title="Stroke">stroke</a> that placed limitations on his influence on his sons' political careers.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_family" title="Kennedy family">Kennedy family</a></div>
<p>Joseph and Rose Kennedy had nine children (see table below).<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> Three of the Kennedys' sons attained distinguished political positions: <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> (1917–1963) served as a U.S. Senator from Massachusetts and as President of the United States, <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert F. Kennedy</a> (1925–1968) served as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Attorney_General" title="United States Attorney General">Attorney General</a> and as a U.S. Senator from New York, and <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Edward M. "Ted" Kennedy</a> (1932–2009) served as a U.S. Senator from Massachusetts. One of the Kennedys' daughters, <a href="/wiki/Eunice_Kennedy_Shriver" title="Eunice Kennedy Shriver">Eunice Kennedy Shriver</a>, founded the Special Olympics,<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> while another, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Kennedy_Smith" title="Jean Kennedy Smith">Jean Kennedy Smith</a>, served as U.S. Ambassador to Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Kennedy's business success expanded, he and his family kept homes in Brookline, Massachusetts (a suburb of Boston); Bronxville, New York (a suburb of New York City); <a href="/wiki/Hyannis_Port,_Massachusetts" title="Hyannis Port, Massachusetts">Hyannis Port, Massachusetts</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach,_Florida" title="Palm Beach, Florida">Palm Beach, Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_59-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy had extramarital relationships with actresses <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Swanson" title="Gloria Swanson">Gloria Swanson</a><sup id="cite_ref-time.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-time.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Marlene_Dietrich" title="Marlene Dietrich">Marlene Dietrich</a><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> and with his secretary, Janet DesRosiers Fontaine.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lobotomy_of_Rosemary_Kennedy">Lobotomy of Rosemary Kennedy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Lobotomy of Rosemary Kennedy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Rosemary_Kennedy#Lobotomy" title="Rosemary Kennedy">Rosemary Kennedy §&#160;Lobotomy</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a href="/wiki/File:TheKennedyFamily1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Family portrait taken outside with children surrounding their parents" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/TheKennedyFamily1.jpg/240px-TheKennedyFamily1.jpg" decoding="async" width="240" height="190" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/TheKennedyFamily1.jpg/360px-TheKennedyFamily1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/66/TheKennedyFamily1.jpg/480px-TheKennedyFamily1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="760" data-file-height="602" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:TheKennedyFamily1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The family at their home in <a href="/wiki/Hyannis_Port,_Massachusetts" title="Hyannis Port, Massachusetts">Hyannis Port, Massachusetts</a>, 1931. Rosemary Kennedy is seated on the far right.</div></div></div>
<p>Kennedy requested that surgeons perform a <a href="/wiki/Lobotomy" title="Lobotomy">lobotomy</a> (one of the earliest in the U.S.) on his eldest daughter <a href="/wiki/Rosemary_Kennedy" title="Rosemary Kennedy">Rosemary</a> in 1941. Various reasons for the operation have been given, but it left her permanently incapacitated.<sup id="cite_ref-Weiss_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weiss-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> She died in 2005, aged 86. Rosemary's name "was never mentioned in the house", according to Janet Des Rosiers, Kennedy's secretary and mistress of nine years.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> Dr. <a href="/wiki/Bertram_S._Brown" title="Bertram S. Brown">Bertram S. Brown</a>, director of the <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Mental_Health" title="National Institute of Mental Health">National Institute of Mental Health</a>, said later that Kennedy called his daughter Rosemary mentally retarded rather than mentally ill in order to protect John's reputation for a presidential run, and that the family's "lack of support for mental illness is part of a lifelong family denial of what was really so".<sup id="cite_ref-Weiss_68-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weiss-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Shorter,_Edward_32–33_73-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shorter,_Edward_32–33-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Murawski_74-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Murawski-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Illness_and_death">Illness and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Illness and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a href="/wiki/File:BirthdayJPK.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Family group portrait in sunroom with flowered drapes and upholstery with all standing behind Joe who is seated, Jackie is kneeling next to him" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/BirthdayJPK.jpg/240px-BirthdayJPK.jpg" decoding="async" width="240" height="237" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/BirthdayJPK.jpg/360px-BirthdayJPK.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/BirthdayJPK.jpg/480px-BirthdayJPK.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="986" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:BirthdayJPK.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kennedy and family celebrate his birthday in Hyannis Port in 1963.</div></div></div>
<p>On December 19, 1961, at the age of 73, Kennedy suffered a stroke. He survived but was left paralyzed on his right side. Thereafter, he suffered from <a href="/wiki/Aphasia" title="Aphasia">aphasia</a>, which severely affected his ability to speak. He remained mentally alert, regained certain functions with therapy, and began walking with a cane. His speech also showed some improvement.<sup id="cite_ref-People:_May_22,_1964_75-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-People:_May_22,_1964-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy began to experience excessive muscular weakness, which eventually required him to use a wheelchair. In 1964, Kennedy was taken to <a href="/wiki/The_Institutes_for_the_Achievement_of_Human_Potential" title="The Institutes for the Achievement of Human Potential">The Institutes for the Achievement of Human Potential</a> in Philadelphia, a medical and rehabilitative center for those who have experienced brain injury.<sup id="cite_ref-People:_May_22,_1964_75-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-People:_May_22,_1964-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy's son Robert was assassinated on June 5, 1968.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> In the aftermath of his son's death, Kennedy made his last public appearance when he, his wife, and son Ted made a filmed message to the country.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He died at home in Hyannis Port the following year on November 18, 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup> He had outlived four of his children.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> He was buried at <a href="/wiki/Holyhood_Cemetery" title="Holyhood Cemetery">Holyhood Cemetery</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brookline,_Massachusetts" title="Brookline, Massachusetts">Brookline, Massachusetts</a>. Kennedy's widow Rose was buried next to him following her death in 1995, as was their daughter Rosemary in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kennedy plays a significant role as a character in <i><a href="/wiki/Winston%27s_War" title="Winston&#39;s War">Winston's War</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dobbs" title="Michael Dobbs">Michael Dobbs</a>' fictionalized account of the rise of Winston Churchill. In <a href="/wiki/Richard_Condon" title="Richard Condon">Richard Condon</a>'s thriller <i><a href="/wiki/Winter_Kills" title="Winter Kills">Winter Kills</a></i>, Pa Keegan is a fictionalized version of Kennedy, and is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/John_Huston" title="John Huston">John Huston</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Winter_Kills_(film)" title="Winter Kills (film)">film version of that novel</a>.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Alternate_history" title="Alternate history">alternate history</a> novel <i><a href="/wiki/Fatherland_(novel)" title="Fatherland (novel)">Fatherland</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Harris_(author)" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Harris (author)">Robert Harris</a>, set in 1964, the senior Kennedy—not his son John F. Kennedy—is president of the United States and about to arrive in Berlin to conclude a treaty with <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>.
</p>
