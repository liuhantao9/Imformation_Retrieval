<p><b>Rose Elizabeth Fitzgerald Kennedy, Countess of the Holy Roman Church</b> (July 22, 1890 – January 22, 1995) was an American philanthropist, socialite, and the matriarch of the <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_family" title="Kennedy family">Kennedy family</a>. She was deeply embedded in the "<a href="/wiki/Lace_curtain_and_shanty_Irish" title="Lace curtain and shanty Irish">lace curtain</a>" <a href="/wiki/Irish_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Catholic">Irish Catholic</a> community in Boston, where her father <a href="/wiki/John_F._Fitzgerald" title="John F. Fitzgerald">John F. Fitzgerald</a> was mayor. Kennedy was the wife of businessman and investor <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr." title="Joseph P. Kennedy Sr.">Joseph P. Kennedy Sr.</a>, who was <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom">United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom</a>, formally known as Ambassador to the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_St_James%27s" title="Court of St James&#39;s">Court of St. James's</a> in the UK. Their nine children included President <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>, Senator <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert F. Kennedy</a>, and longtime Senator <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Ted Kennedy</a>. In 1951 she was ennobled by <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_XII" title="Pope Pius XII">Pope Pius XII</a>, becoming the sixth American woman to be granted the rank of <a href="/wiki/Papal_nobility#Papal_counts_and_countesses" title="Papal nobility">Papal countess</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rose_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rose was born at 4 Garden Court<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in the <a href="/wiki/North_End,_Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="North End, Boston, Massachusetts">North End</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>. She was the eldest of six children born to Boston Mayor <a href="/wiki/John_F._Fitzgerald" title="John F. Fitzgerald">John Francis "Honey Fitz" Fitzgerald</a> and Mary Josephine "Josie" Hannon.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Her siblings were Agnes, Thomas, John Jr., Eunice, and Frederick.
</p><p>As a young child, she lived in an <a href="/wiki/Italianate" class="mw-redirect" title="Italianate">Italianate</a>/<a href="/wiki/Mansard" class="mw-redirect" title="Mansard">Mansard</a>-style home in the Ashmont Hill section of <a href="/wiki/Dorchester,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Dorchester, Massachusetts">Dorchester, Massachusetts</a> and attended the local <a href="/wiki/Girls%27_Latin_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Girls&#39; Latin School">Girls' Latin School</a>. The home later burned down, but a plaque at Welles Avenue and Harley Street proclaims "Rose Fitzgerald Kennedy Square". The plaque was dedicated by her son, Senator <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Ted Kennedy</a>, on her 102nd birthday in 1992.
</p><p>Fitzgerald studied at the convent school Kasteel Bloemendal in <a href="/wiki/Vaals" title="Vaals">Vaals</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, and graduated from Dorchester High School in 1906. She also attended the <a href="/wiki/New_England_Conservatory" class="mw-redirect" title="New England Conservatory">New England Conservatory</a> in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, where she studied piano. After being refused permission by her father to attend <a href="/wiki/Wellesley_College" title="Wellesley College">Wellesley College</a>, Fitzgerald enrolled at the <a href="/wiki/Manhattanville_College" title="Manhattanville College">Manhattanville College of the Sacred Heart</a> (as it was then known) in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, an institution that did not grant degrees at the time. In 1908, Fitzgerald and her father embarked on a tour of Europe. She and Honey Fitz had a private audience with Pope <a href="/wiki/Pius_X" class="mw-redirect" title="Pius X">Pius X</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Apostolic_Palace" title="Apostolic Palace">Vatican</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_family_life">Marriage and family life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rose_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marriage and family life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rose_kennedy.JPG" class="image"><img alt="Waist high portrait of woman in her twenties with a child in front of her" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b1/Rose_kennedy.JPG/170px-Rose_kennedy.JPG" decoding="async" width="170" height="213" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/b1/Rose_kennedy.JPG 1.5x" data-file-width="198" data-file-height="248" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rose_kennedy.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kennedy with her firstborn child, Joe Jr., circa 1919</div></div></div>
<p>On October 7, 1914 at age 24, she married <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr." title="Joseph P. Kennedy Sr.">Joseph Patrick "Joe" Kennedy</a> after a courtship of more than seven years.  The long courtship was partly due to her father's disapproval of Kennedy. He was the elder son of businessman/politician <a href="/wiki/P._J._Kennedy" title="P. J. Kennedy">P. J. Kennedy</a> (political rival of Honey Fitz) and Mary Augusta Hickey. They initially lived in a home in <a href="/wiki/Brookline,_Massachusetts" title="Brookline, Massachusetts">Brookline, Massachusetts</a> that is now the <a href="/wiki/John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy_National_Historic_Site" title="John Fitzgerald Kennedy National Historic Site">John Fitzgerald Kennedy National Historic Site</a>, and later a 15-room vacation home at <a href="/wiki/Hyannis_Port" class="mw-redirect" title="Hyannis Port">Hyannis Port</a> on <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">Cape Cod</a>, which became the Kennedy family's lasting base. Their nine children were <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_Jr." title="Joseph P. Kennedy Jr.">Joseph Jr. (Joe)</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John (Jack)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rosemary_Kennedy" title="Rosemary Kennedy">Rose (Rosemary)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington" title="Kathleen Cavendish, Marchioness of Hartington">Kathleen (Kick)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eunice_Kennedy_Shriver" title="Eunice Kennedy Shriver">Eunice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Kennedy_Lawford" title="Patricia Kennedy Lawford">Patricia (Pat)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert (Bobby)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Kennedy_Smith" title="Jean Kennedy Smith">Jean</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Edward (Ted)</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:197px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rose_Kennedy_with_husband_Joseph_P._Kennedy,_Sr._and_grandchildren.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Rose_Kennedy_with_husband_Joseph_P._Kennedy%2C_Sr._and_grandchildren.jpg/195px-Rose_Kennedy_with_husband_Joseph_P._Kennedy%2C_Sr._and_grandchildren.jpg" decoding="async" width="195" height="195" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Rose_Kennedy_with_husband_Joseph_P._Kennedy%2C_Sr._and_grandchildren.jpg/293px-Rose_Kennedy_with_husband_Joseph_P._Kennedy%2C_Sr._and_grandchildren.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Rose_Kennedy_with_husband_Joseph_P._Kennedy%2C_Sr._and_grandchildren.jpg/390px-Rose_Kennedy_with_husband_Joseph_P._Kennedy%2C_Sr._and_grandchildren.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="1000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rose_Kennedy_with_husband_Joseph_P._Kennedy,_Sr._and_grandchildren.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rose Kennedy (standing, rear) with her husband Joseph P. Kennedy Sr. and grandchildren, 1963. The President is partially in view, holding a <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coke</a> bottle.</div></div></div>
<p>Joseph provided well for their family, but he was <a href="/wiki/Infidelity" title="Infidelity">unfaithful</a>. His affairs included one with <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Swanson" title="Gloria Swanson">Gloria Swanson</a>. When Rose was eight months pregnant with the couple's fourth child, Kathleen, she temporarily went back to her parents, returning to Joseph after her father told her divorce was not an option. In turning a blind eye to her husband's affairs, Rose depended heavily on medication. <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Kessler" title="Ronald Kessler">Ronald Kessler</a> found records for prescription tranquilizers <a href="/wiki/Seconal" class="mw-redirect" title="Seconal">Seconal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Placidyl" class="mw-redirect" title="Placidyl">Placidyl</a>, <a href="/wiki/Librium" class="mw-redirect" title="Librium">Librium</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dalmane" class="mw-redirect" title="Dalmane">Dalmane</a> to relieve Rose's nervousness and stress, and <a href="/wiki/Lomotil" class="mw-redirect" title="Lomotil">Lomotil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bentyl" class="mw-redirect" title="Bentyl">Bentyl</a>, <a href="/wiki/Librax" class="mw-redirect" title="Librax">Librax</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tagamet" class="mw-redirect" title="Tagamet">Tagamet</a> for her stomach.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rose Kennedy was a strict Catholic<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> throughout her life. Even after her 100th birthday, she rarely missed Sunday Mass and maintained an "extremely prudish" exterior. Her strict beliefs often placed her at odds with her children.<sup id="cite_ref-Obituary_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Obituary-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jacqueline_Kennedy_Onassis" title="Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis">Jacqueline Kennedy</a> described her mother-in-law in her correspondence to Father Joseph Leonard, an Irish priest: "I don't think Jack's mother is too bright&#160;– and she would rather say a rosary than read a book."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rose Kennedy stated that she felt completely fulfilled as a full-time homemaker. In her 1974 autobiography, <i>Times to Remember</i>, she wrote, "I looked on child rearing not only as a work of love and a duty, but as a profession that was fully as interesting and challenging as any honorable profession in the world and one that demanded the best I could bring to it..... What greater aspiration and challenge are there for a mother than the hope of raising a great son or daughter?"<sup id="cite_ref-Obituary_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Obituary-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_years">Later years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rose_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Later years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:President_Kennedy_with_his_mother_crop.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/President_Kennedy_with_his_mother_crop.jpg/220px-President_Kennedy_with_his_mother_crop.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="248" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/President_Kennedy_with_his_mother_crop.jpg/330px-President_Kennedy_with_his_mother_crop.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/President_Kennedy_with_his_mother_crop.jpg/440px-President_Kennedy_with_his_mother_crop.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2123" data-file-height="2390" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:President_Kennedy_with_his_mother_crop.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rose Kennedy with her son, President <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> in 1962</div></div></div>
<p>After her son <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John</a> was elected President in 1960, Rose "became a sort of quiet celebrity" and appeared on the <a href="/wiki/International_Best_Dressed_List" class="mw-redirect" title="International Best Dressed List">International Best Dressed List</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Obituary_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Obituary-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Most of her social activities consisted of involvement in charities and women's groups. Rose also took brisk ocean swims outside her Cape Cod house in fifty-degree weather.
</p><p>After suffering a <a href="/wiki/Stroke" title="Stroke">stroke</a> in 1984, she used a wheelchair for the remaining eleven years of her life. She maintained her residence at the <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Compound" title="Kennedy Compound">Kennedy Compound</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hyannis_Port,_Massachusetts" title="Hyannis Port, Massachusetts">Hyannis Port, Massachusetts</a> and was cared for by private nurses and staff.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rose_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On January 22, 1995, Kennedy died from complications from <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a> at age 104 at the Kennedy family compound in Hyannis Port. She was survived by five of her nine children as well as many grandchildren and great-grandchildren. Rose Kennedy was interred with her husband at <a href="/wiki/Holyhood_Cemetery" title="Holyhood Cemetery">Holyhood Cemetery</a> in Brookline, Massachusetts.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rose_Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1951, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Pius_XII" title="Pope Pius XII">Pope Pius XII</a> granted Kennedy the title of <a href="/wiki/Papal_count" class="mw-redirect" title="Papal count">countess</a> in recognition of her "exemplary motherhood and many charitable works."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 1992, when she turned 102, the intersection of Welles Avenue and Harley Street in Boston was proclaimed "Rose Fitzgerald Kennedy Square". The plaque was dedicated by her son, Senator Edward M. Kennedy. Also, the <a href="/wiki/Rose_Kennedy_Greenway" class="mw-redirect" title="Rose Kennedy Greenway">Rose Kennedy Greenway</a> in <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a>&#160;– the park that was created when the city's <a href="/wiki/Central_Artery" title="Central Artery">Central Artery</a> was sunk below ground level in the "<a href="/wiki/Big_Dig" title="Big Dig">Big Dig</a>"&#160;– was named after her on July 26, 2004.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Well known for her <a href="/wiki/Philanthropy" title="Philanthropy">philanthropic</a> efforts and for leading the Grandparents' Parade at age 90 at the <a href="/wiki/Special_Olympics" title="Special Olympics">Special Olympics</a>, Kennedy's life and work are documented in the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Academy Award">Oscar</a>-nominated short documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Rose_Kennedy:_A_Life_to_Remember" title="Rose Kennedy: A Life to Remember">Rose Kennedy: A Life to Remember</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-NY_Times_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NY_Times-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She was a lifelong <a href="/wiki/Autograph" title="Autograph">autograph</a> collector.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The longest bridge in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Rose_Fitzgerald_Kennedy_Bridge" title="Rose Fitzgerald Kennedy Bridge">Rose Fitzgerald Kennedy Bridge</a>, is named after her.
</p>
