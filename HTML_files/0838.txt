<p><b>George Herbert Hirst</b> (7 September 1871&#160;– 10 May 1954) was a professional English <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricketer</a> who played <a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class cricket</a> for <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Yorkshire County Cricket Club">Yorkshire County Cricket Club</a> between 1891 and 1921, with a further appearance in 1929.  One of the best <a href="/wiki/All-rounder" title="All-rounder">all-rounders</a> of his time, Hirst was a left arm <a href="/wiki/Fast_bowling#Categorisation_of_fast_bowling" class="mw-redirect" title="Fast bowling">medium-fast</a> bowler and right-handed batsman.
</p><p>Hirst played in 24 <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">Test matches</a> for <a href="/wiki/England_cricket_team" title="England cricket team">England</a> between 1897 and 1909, touring Australia twice. He completed the <a href="/wiki/Double_(cricket)" title="Double (cricket)">double</a> of 1,000 runs and 100 wickets in an English cricket season 14 times, the second most of any cricketer after his contemporary and team-mate <a href="/wiki/Wilfred_Rhodes" title="Wilfred Rhodes">Wilfred Rhodes</a>. One of the <a href="/wiki/Wisden_Cricketers_of_the_Year" title="Wisden Cricketers of the Year"><i>Wisden</i> Cricketers of the Year</a> for 1901, Hirst scored 36,356 runs and took 2,742 wickets in first-class cricket. In Tests, he made 790 runs and captured 59 wickets.
</p><p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Kirkheaton" title="Kirkheaton">Kirkheaton</a>, Hirst first achieved success for Yorkshire as a bowler who could bat a little. Over his first few seasons, his batting improved at the expense of his bowling until he was regarded mainly as a specialist batsman. Around 1900, his bowling re-emerged when he discovered a method to make the ball <a href="/wiki/Swing_bowling" title="Swing bowling">swing</a> in the air after he released it. He was one of the first bowlers to control the swing of the ball, which batsmen found very difficult to counter, making Hirst's bowling far more successful from then on.
</p><p>From 1903 he achieved 11 consecutive doubles. He set records in 1905, when he scored 341 runs in an innings against <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Leicestershire County Cricket Club">Leicestershire</a>—still the highest total for Yorkshire as of 2015—and in 1906, when he completed an unprecedented and unrepeated double of 2,000 runs and 200 wickets. In many seasons, he battled injury which reduced his effectiveness, but his bowling remained successful until shortly before the First World War. Hirst played in all England's home Test series between 1899 and 1909, but his record for England was less impressive than his record for Yorkshire, and he may have suffered from playing in Australia where match conditions did not suit him.
</p><p>Hirst returned to play for Yorkshire after the war, but became a cricket coach at <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton College</a> in 1920, where he remained until 1938. After making occasional appearances in 1920 and 1921, he retired from regular first-class cricket. He maintained his connections with Yorkshire for the rest of his life, coached young players and established an excellent reputation for developing players of all social backgrounds. A popular player, coach, and personality with cricketers and spectators, Hirst died in 1954, aged 82.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hirst was born on 7 September 1871 in the Brown Cow Inn, <a href="/wiki/Kirkheaton" title="Kirkheaton">Kirkheaton</a>, a village close to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>. He was the last of 10 children born to James Hirst and his wife Sarah Maria Woolhouse. When his father died in 1880, Hirst lived with his sister Mary Elizabeth Woolhouse and her husband John Berry in Kirkheaton.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;note 1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-E76_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E76-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After leaving school at 10 years of age, Hirst first worked for a hand-loom weaver in a local cottage, and then at a dyeing firm.<sup id="cite_ref-T27_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T27-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He played <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">rugby football</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Fullback_(rugby_union)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fullback (rugby union)">full back</a> during winter,<sup id="cite_ref-T27_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T27-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and cricket with his friends and brothers in summer.<sup id="cite_ref-E76_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E76-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> By the age of 15, Hirst was playing regularly for the Kirkheaton cricket team and his batting and bowling performances regularly won prizes from a local newspaper.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> His reputation grew; when he was 18 he was a key player in the Kirkheaton team which won the Lumb Challenge Cup of 1889. In the final, watched by players from <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Yorkshire County Cricket Club">Yorkshire County Cricket Club</a>, he took five wickets for 23 runs.<sup id="cite_ref-E76_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E76-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-coy_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Days later, invited to take part along with another local player, he appeared for Yorkshire against Cheshire in a non-<a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class</a> match in Huddersfield. He scored six runs in his only innings, and took three wickets in the match.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hirst played only intermittently for Yorkshire over the next couple of seasons,<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> but continued to develop as a cricketer, signing as a professional for <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a> Cricket Club for the 1890 season before joining <a href="/wiki/Mirfield" title="Mirfield">Mirfield</a> in 1891.<sup id="cite_ref-Ellam,_p._77_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ellam,_p._77-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> During that season, he made his first-class debut for Yorkshire against <a href="/wiki/Somerset_County_Cricket_Club" title="Somerset County Cricket Club">Somerset</a> in the <a href="/wiki/County_Championship" title="County Championship">County Championship</a>; he scored 15 runs and took two wickets in the game.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="First-class_cricketer">First-class cricketer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: First-class cricketer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hirst_catching_cr.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A cricketer catching" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Hirst_catching_cr.jpg/220px-Hirst_catching_cr.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="282" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Hirst_catching_cr.jpg/330px-Hirst_catching_cr.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Hirst_catching_cr.jpg/440px-Hirst_catching_cr.jpg 2x" data-file-width="559" data-file-height="716" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hirst_catching_cr.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hirst catching in a photograph taken by <a href="/wiki/George_Beldam" title="George Beldam">George Beldam</a> c. 1906</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hirst_bowling_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A cricketer bowling" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hirst_bowling_2.jpg/220px-Hirst_bowling_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="351" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hirst_bowling_2.jpg/330px-Hirst_bowling_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hirst_bowling_2.jpg/440px-Hirst_bowling_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="790" data-file-height="1261" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hirst_bowling_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hirst bowling in a photograph taken by George Beldam in 1906</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_seasons_for_Yorkshire">First seasons for Yorkshire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: First seasons for Yorkshire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the 1892 season, Hirst joined Huddersfield, which played a higher standard of cricket.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Yorkshire also gave him a longer run in the first team. Early in the season, Hirst appeared for Yorkshire against the <a href="/wiki/Marylebone_Cricket_Club" title="Marylebone Cricket Club">Marylebone Cricket Club</a> (MCC). Not considered a good batsman at this stage, he batted at <a href="/wiki/Batting_order_(cricket)#Lower_order_or_tail" title="Batting order (cricket)">number 11</a> in the first innings, scored 20 and 43 <a href="/wiki/Not_out" title="Not out">not out</a> and, as a bowler, took four wickets for 29 runs (four for 29) and two for 58. His bowling performance particularly impressed <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Pardon" title="Sydney Pardon">Sydney Pardon</a>, the editor of <i><a href="/wiki/Wisden_Cricketers%27_Almanack" title="Wisden Cricketers&#39; Almanack">Wisden Cricketers' Almanack</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-timesobit_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesobit-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Yorkshire, needing to fill a vacant place in the team, played Hirst 13 times in first-class matches in 1892.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-H55_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H55-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He did not pass 30 runs in an innings again and <a href="/wiki/Batting_average_(cricket)" title="Batting average (cricket)">averaged</a> 16.15 with the bat.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> With the ball, he took 30 wickets at an <a href="/wiki/Bowling_average" title="Bowling average">average</a> of 20.56 with a best performance of six for 16 against <a href="/wiki/Sussex_County_Cricket_Club" title="Sussex County Cricket Club">Sussex</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <i>Wisden</i> later noted that Hirst, until he tired later in the season and was dropped from the team, "bowled up to a certain point with excellent results".<sup id="cite_ref-coy_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Over the next few seasons, Hirst became a regular member of the Yorkshire side, but although his performances were good enough to keep him in the team, he had few outstanding successes. <i>Wisden</i> noted: "For some time after his first season Hirst's career was one of steady progress rather than of brilliant achievement."<sup id="cite_ref-coy_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Hirst's batting remained undeveloped in 1893; he batted at number 10 and did not pass fifty in any one innings, though he managed some useful scores.<sup id="cite_ref-T28_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T28-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He averaged only 15.04 with the bat,<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> but his bowling continued to make a good impression on critics.<sup id="cite_ref-T28_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T28-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He took 99 wickets at an average of 14.39, placing him third in the Yorkshire averages for the season;<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-T28_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T28-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> that year, the county won the official <a href="/wiki/County_Championship" title="County Championship">County Championship</a>, which had begun in 1890, for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-ccposn_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccposn-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In 1894, Hirst scored his maiden first-class century against <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Gloucestershire County Cricket Club">Gloucestershire</a>, hitting an unbeaten 115 out of a partnership of 176 for the ninth <a href="/wiki/Wicket#Partnership" title="Wicket">wicket</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Although this was his only score over fifty, <i>Wisden</i> recorded how his batting often helped his team out of difficult situations.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In total, he hit 564 runs at an average of 16.58.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> With the ball, his record was similar to the previous season. He took 98 wickets at an average of 15.98,<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and his best figures came in a match against <a href="/wiki/Lancashire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Lancashire County Cricket Club">Lancashire</a>, a feat appreciated by Yorkshire supporters as the fixture<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;note 2&#93;</a></sup> was always highly competitive.<sup id="cite_ref-Ellam,_p._77_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ellam,_p._77-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Thomson,_p._30_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thomson,_p._30-19">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Then in the 1895 season, Hirst passed 100 wickets in the season for the first time, securing 150 wickets at an average of 17.06.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He established himself as an opening bowler for Yorkshire, assuming the role from <a href="/wiki/Ted_Wainwright" title="Ted Wainwright">Ted Wainwright</a> and developing a good partnership with <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Peel" title="Bobby Peel">Bobby Peel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Against <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Leicestershire County Cricket Club">Leicestershire</a>, he took a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick#Cricket" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-E78_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E78-21">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Although primarily regarded as a bowler, and while <i>Wisden</i> later described his batting as being in "temporary decline" in 1895,<sup id="cite_ref-coy_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Hirst also scored 710 runs at an average of 19.18, with three fifties.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leading_all-rounder">Leading all-rounder</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Leading all-rounder">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1896, Yorkshire won their second County Championship. Hirst hit a century against Leicestershire and nine other scores over fifty; this improvement in his batting took him past 1,000 runs for the first time, and his average of 28.05 was substantially higher than he had achieved previously.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> With the ball he took 104 wickets, at the more expensive average of 21.61, to complete <a href="/wiki/Double_(cricket)" title="Double (cricket)">the double</a> of 1,000 runs and 100 wickets for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> However, some critics in Yorkshire were unhappy that his batting had improved, believing that the extra effort involved would diminish his effectiveness as a bowler. They considered a powerful bowling attack to be vital to the team's success, more so than a strong batting line-up, and expressed the opinion that Hirst should concentrate on one discipline rather than dividing his energy.<sup id="cite_ref-Thomson,_p._30_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thomson,_p._30-19">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Over the next few seasons, these fears were proven to some extent, <i>Wisden</i> noting in 1901 that he was a less effective bowler than previously.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 1897, Hirst completed the double again. He scored 1,535 runs at an average of 35.69, with a century and 11 fifties, and took 101 wickets at an average of 23.22.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> These performances were good enough to earn him selection for <a href="/wiki/Gentlemen_v_Players" title="Gentlemen v Players">the Players against the Gentlemen</a> in the prestigious matches at <a href="/wiki/The_Oval" title="The Oval">The Oval</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lord%27s" title="Lord&#39;s">Lord's</a>, in both of which he scored half-centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For the winter of 1897–98, Hirst was selected as part of <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Stoddart" title="Andrew Stoddart">Andrew Stoddart</a>'s team to tour Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-T33_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T33-22">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The team was outplayed and lost the Test matches 4–1, hampered by the poor form of the bowlers and ill health among the team, not least from Stoddart himself who missed several matches following the death of his mother.<sup id="cite_ref-stod_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stod-23">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Hirst, suffering from a strained leg for some of the tour,<sup id="cite_ref-T33_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T33-22">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> was ineffective as a bowler.<sup id="cite_ref-stod_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stod-23">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The hard Australian pitches favoured batsmen and did not suit Hirst's style of bowling.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-E78_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E78-21">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In all the first-class matches on the tour, he took only nine wickets at the very expensive average of 75.77.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> His batting was inconsistent and he scored 338 runs at an average of 21.12.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-T33_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T33-22">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> His best scores came in minor matches.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Nevertheless, he was selected for England in four of the five Test matches. His debut came in the first match, England's only victory, when he scored 62 batting at number six in the <a href="/wiki/Batting_order_(cricket)" title="Batting order (cricket)">batting order</a>. Despite bowling 41 <a href="/wiki/Over_(cricket)" title="Over (cricket)">overs</a> in the game, he failed to take a wicket;<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> his maiden Test match wicket came in the following Test, but he took only two wickets in the series.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the third Test, Hirst scored 85, which remained the highest Test score of his career, but in this series he reached double figures only once more. When the series ended, Hirst had scored 207 runs at an average of 29.57.<sup id="cite_ref-tbat_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbat-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hirst's poor form continued when he returned to England for the 1898 season. Fatigue from continuous cricket and ongoing problems with his leg added to his difficulties and he had a poor season.<sup id="cite_ref-T34_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T34-26">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> His only score over fifty was an innings of 130 against Surrey, and he scored 567 runs at an average of 17.71.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Statistically, he had one of his worst bowling seasons, taking 36 wickets at 25.61 and never taking more than four wickets in an innings.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> For Yorkshire, Hirst's loss of form was offset by the debut of <a href="/wiki/Wilfred_Rhodes" title="Wilfred Rhodes">Wilfred Rhodes</a>, also from Kirkheaton, who took 154 wickets in his first season.<sup id="cite_ref-T34_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T34-26">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In 1899, Hirst showed a big improvement in his batting form, scoring 1,630 runs—his best tally to date—at an average of 35.43.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He increased his number of wickets to 82 at 24.75,<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and his form was good enough to earn selection for his first home Test match, against Australia. However, it was only injuries to three other candidates that secured his place as a fast bowler, and he was not selected in the rest of the series. <i>Wisden</i> noted: "It cannot be said that the experiment was in any way a success. Hirst worked hard for his side, his fielding indeed being perfection, but as a bowler he did not cause the Australians any trouble."<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> When he played the tourists in their game against Yorkshire soon after the Test, Hirst took 13 wickets.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Later in the season, he played for the Players against the Gentlemen, though without any great success.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Discovery_of_.22swerve.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery_of_&quot;swerve&quot;">Discovery of "swerve"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Discovery of &quot;swerve&quot;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Hirst's batting continued to be more successful than his bowling, to the point where commentators regarded him as primarily a batsman, who bowled occasionally.<sup id="cite_ref-E80_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E80-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In 1900, he scored 1,960 runs at an average of 40.83, his best batting record to date,<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> but his total of wickets fell to 62 at an average of 26.90, his worst average until 1914.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> His only representative cricket came in end-of-season festival matches, including a Gentlemen v Players match.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Yorkshire won the County Championship, the first of three consecutive victories in which Hirst played a leading role.<sup id="cite_ref-ccposn_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccposn-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-T36_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T36-29">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> After his achievements in the season, Hirst was selected as one of <i>Wisden's</i> <a href="/wiki/Wisden_Cricketers_of_the_Year" title="Wisden Cricketers of the Year">Cricketers of the Year</a>. The citation described him as a confident batsman who could be relied upon in difficult batting conditions or when his team were under pressure, but noted how his bowling had suffered as he improved as a batsman.<sup id="cite_ref-coy_6-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coy-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> However, around this time, Hirst began to develop his use of <a href="/wiki/Swing_bowling" title="Swing bowling">swing bowling</a>, known at the time as swerve bowling. Previously, the ball occasionally swung through the air without his deliberate intention after he released it, but he now discovered a method to control the "swerve" in certain atmospheric conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-T37_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T37-30">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> He never discussed how he achieved the effect, limiting his comments to "sometimes it works and sometimes it doesn't."<sup id="cite_ref-T37_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T37-30">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 1901 season, Hirst's improvement in bowling brought him the highest total of wickets in his career to date. In a summer of sunny weather which brought a succession of good batting pitches,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> he took 183 wickets, the first time since 1897 he had passed 100 wickets, at an average of 16.38.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> This placed him second in the national averages behind Rhodes.<sup id="cite_ref-T36_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T36-29">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He recorded a series of impressive bowling performances, winning extravagant praise from <i>Wisden</i>; he took five wickets in an innings on 15 occasions and 10 wickets in a match five times, including once against traditional rivals Lancashire.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> His batting continued to be successful, and he completed his second double with 1,950 runs at 42.39,<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> including his first double-century:  214 against <a href="/wiki/Worcestershire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Worcestershire County Cricket Club">Worcestershire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-E80_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E80-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He was selected for the Gentlemen and Players match at Lord's,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and appeared in the equivalent fixture in an end-of-season festival match.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Test_match_regular">Test match regular</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Test match regular">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Success_against_Australia">Success against Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Success against Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Haigh,_Hirst_and_Rhodes.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Three men in dark suits, two sitting on chairs and the third stood behind" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4c/Haigh%2C_Hirst_and_Rhodes.jpg/170px-Haigh%2C_Hirst_and_Rhodes.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="231" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4c/Haigh%2C_Hirst_and_Rhodes.jpg/255px-Haigh%2C_Hirst_and_Rhodes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/4c/Haigh%2C_Hirst_and_Rhodes.jpg/340px-Haigh%2C_Hirst_and_Rhodes.jpg 2x" data-file-width="519" data-file-height="706" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Haigh,_Hirst_and_Rhodes.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>From left to right: The Yorkshire team-mates <a href="/wiki/Schofield_Haigh" title="Schofield Haigh">Schofield Haigh</a>, Hirst and <a href="/wiki/Wilfred_Rhodes" title="Wilfred Rhodes">Wilfred Rhodes</a> at <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a>, 1905</div></div></div>
<p>Although both were invited, neither Hirst nor Rhodes toured Australia in the winter of 1901–02 with <a href="/wiki/Archie_MacLaren" title="Archie MacLaren">Archie MacLaren</a>'s team, as the Yorkshire committee wanted both players to be well rested before the new season.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Hirst was statistically less successful in 1902, but he nevertheless returned to the Test team. The summer was very wet, resulting in many rain-affected pitches, known as <a href="/wiki/Sticky_wicket" title="Sticky wicket">sticky wickets</a>, which favoured slower bowlers and made batting difficult.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Hirst scored 1,413 runs in the season, at an average of 31.11 with two centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He took fewer wickets than the previous year, partly as he was often used for a few overs early in an innings by Yorkshire before giving way to slow bowlers Rhodes and <a href="/wiki/Schofield_Haigh" title="Schofield Haigh">Schofield Haigh</a>, who topped the national first-class bowling averages for the season.<sup id="cite_ref-T39_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T39-36">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> In total, Hirst took 83 wickets at an average of 20.33.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the summer of 1902, Australia toured England, playing five Test matches; Hirst made an impact on the series.<sup id="cite_ref-T39_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T39-36">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Around the time of the first Test, the tourists endured a dispiriting spell of poor form and illness.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In the first Test, England scored 376 for nine wickets. Hirst scored 48 and <i>Wisden</i> described his partnership with <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Tyldesley" title="Johnny Tyldesley">Johnny Tyldesley</a> of 94 in 80 minutes as the turning point of the innings.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Egbaston02_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Egbaston02-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> In reply, Hirst and Rhodes bowled out Australia for 36 in conditions that the umpires considered reasonable to bat in. <i>Wisden</i> described the two Yorkshiremen as bowling very well.<sup id="cite_ref-Egbaston02_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Egbaston02-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Hirst had figures of three for 15 and Rhodes returned seven for 17. However <a href="/wiki/C._B._Fry" title="C. B. Fry">C. B. Fry</a>, who played for England in the match, believed Hirst to be the more difficult to play and that while Rhodes bowled well, the Australian batsmen got themselves out as they "hurried to the other end and tried to hit Rhodes, without success. Well as Rhodes bowled, it was Hirst who was responsible for the debacle. This is the best instance I know of the bowler at the other end getting wickets for his colleague."<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The England side for this match was later described by some critics as the greatest England team in history.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Rain prevented the match from being completed and saved Australia from almost certain defeat.<sup id="cite_ref-Egbaston02_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Egbaston02-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The Australian team's next game was against Yorkshire. Hirst took four for 35 in the tourist's first innings, but in a low-scoring game, Yorkshire were behind after the first innings. After Australia had reached 20 for three wickets in their second innings, Hirst bowled <a href="/wiki/Victor_Trumper" title="Victor Trumper">Victor Trumper</a> with what Hirst believed was the best delivery of his life and the tourists last wickets fell quickly. The team were bowled out for 23, of which Hirst took five wickets for nine runs as he made the ball swing. Yorkshire lost five wickets in scoring the 48 runs they required to win.<sup id="cite_ref-E80_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E80-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second Test was badly affected by weather and Hirst did not bat or bowl, and in the third Test in Sheffield, lost by England, he scored eight runs and did not take any wickets.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Although he failed at Sheffield, Hirst was the leading all-rounder in England and thus unlikely to be left out of the team.<sup id="cite_ref-Gibson_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gibson-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> However, the England captain, Archie MacLaren, was engaged in a dispute with the team selectors over the composition of his side. He was given only 12 players to choose from for the fourth Test. <a href="/wiki/Fred_Tate" title="Fred Tate">Fred Tate</a> was one of the twelve and the selectors probably included him as they believed MacLaren could not possibly choose him in the final eleven over another player and so would be forced to field the side they wanted. MacLaren responded by dropping Hirst to play Tate, out of anger towards the selectors.<sup id="cite_ref-Gibson_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gibson-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The pitch was soft, which would have favoured the bowling of Tate, but <i>Wisden</i> reported that while this offered some justification for dropping Hirst, "it meant playing a bowler pure and simple in preference to a first-rate all-round man, and the result proved anything but happy."<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Tate bowled just 16 overs in the game, dropped a vital catch and was the last man out as Australia won the match by three runs, giving them an unassailable lead in the series; later writers claimed that Tate's performance was responsible for losing the match.<sup id="cite_ref-Gibson_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gibson-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hirst was recalled for the final Test and played a vital role. <i>Wisden</i> said that he bowled as well as he had in 1901 as he brought about an early batting collapse. Although Australia recovered, he had figures of five for 77.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Oval02_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oval02-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> With <a href="/wiki/Len_Braund" title="Len Braund">Len Braund</a>, Hirst then helped England to avoid the <a href="/wiki/Follow-on" title="Follow-on">follow-on</a>, scoring 43 and according to <i>Wisden</i> "hitting with the utmost freedom".<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Oval02_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oval02-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Hirst took a further wicket as Australia were dismissed for 121.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> England, needing 263 to win, were 48 for five at one point but an innings of 104 from <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Jessop" title="Gilbert Jessop">Gilbert Jessop</a> gave England a chance. Hirst scored 58 not out, making an aggressive start but slowing down to score mainly from singles in the later stages of the innings as wickets were lost.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Oval02_45-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oval02-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The ninth wicket fell with 15 needed when Rhodes joined Hirst. It has been claimed that Hirst said to Rhodes, "We'll get 'em in singles",<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> but neither batsman could remember those words being said and not all the runs came in singles.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> The two Yorkshiremen held their nerve to take England to a one-wicket victory. <i>Wisden</i> described Hirst as playing "a great game", noted the coolness of his play under pressure and said that "Hirst's innings was in its way almost as remarkable as Jessop's".<sup id="cite_ref-Oval02_45-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oval02-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In four Tests, Hirst scored 157 runs at an average of 39.25 and took nine wickets at an average of 23.11.<sup id="cite_ref-tbat_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbat-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tbowl_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbowl-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Apart from his Test appearances, Hirst also played twice for the Players against the Gentlemen and played for the same side against the Australians.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_tour_of_Australia">Second tour of Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Second tour of Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pelham_Warner_Vanity_Fair_3_September_1903_(modified).jpg" class="image"><img alt="Caricature of a cricketer with his hands in his pockets" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Pelham_Warner_Vanity_Fair_3_September_1903_%28modified%29.jpg/170px-Pelham_Warner_Vanity_Fair_3_September_1903_%28modified%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="298" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Pelham_Warner_Vanity_Fair_3_September_1903_%28modified%29.jpg/255px-Pelham_Warner_Vanity_Fair_3_September_1903_%28modified%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Pelham_Warner_Vanity_Fair_3_September_1903_%28modified%29.jpg/340px-Pelham_Warner_Vanity_Fair_3_September_1903_%28modified%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1754" data-file-height="3070" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pelham_Warner_Vanity_Fair_3_September_1903_(modified).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Pelham_Warner" title="Pelham Warner">Pelham Warner</a>, the captain of the first MCC tour of Australia</div></div></div>
<p>Early in the 1903 season, Hirst suffered a leg injury. This, coupled with other players' absences, was partly responsible for a poor start to the season by Yorkshire, and although the team recovered, it could only achieve third place in the County Championship. When Hirst returned his bowling speed was reduced, but during the season he rediscovered his swing bowling.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> In completing the first of 10 consecutive doubles, he took 128 wickets at an average of 14.94 and scored 1,844 runs at an average of 47.28.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> For Yorkshire, he topped both the batting and bowling averages and had the third highest batting average in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Hirst played in two end-of-season games for the Players against the Gentlemen; his 124 not out in the second match was his only century in the Players versus Gentlemen series.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1903, <a href="/wiki/Pelham_Warner" title="Pelham Warner">Pelham Warner</a>, who played for <a href="/wiki/Middlesex_County_Cricket_Club" title="Middlesex County Cricket Club">Middlesex</a>, was chosen to captain the first tour of Australia to be sponsored by MCC, in the winter of 1903–04. According to Warner, Hirst and Rhodes were the first two players to be selected, as "the two best bowlers of the present day in this country".<sup id="cite_ref-W7_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W7-51">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Before the tour, critics claimed Hirst would be unsuccessful, citing his failure during his previous tour. However, Warner later wrote: "Hirst, to say nothing of his batting, bowled excellently throughout the tour, and was of much more value as a bowler than his average would suggest".<sup id="cite_ref-W7_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W7-51">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> He further described him as the best all-rounder in England and noted that "the tighter the match, the better he plays".<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> <i>Wisden</i> was less convinced, and the report on the tour said that, as a bowler, "Hirst, though by no means the failure he had been with Mr Stoddart's team six years before, fell far below his English form."<sup id="cite_ref-03-04_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-03-04-53">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> While commenting on his batting record, the <i>Wisden</i> report noted: "The Australian wickets [i.e. pitches] in fine weather are rather too fast to suit the pulling and hook strokes of which he is such a master in England."<sup id="cite_ref-03-04_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-03-04-53">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> As he could not make the ball swing in Australia in the same way it did in England,<sup id="cite_ref-W55_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W55-54">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Hirst used <a href="/wiki/Leg_theory" title="Leg theory">leg theory</a>, bowling at <a href="/wiki/Stump_(cricket)#Part_of_the_wicket" title="Stump (cricket)">leg stump</a> with fielders close by on the <a href="/wiki/Leg_side" title="Leg side">leg side</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his second match of the tour, Hirst scored 92 and used his pull shot more effectively than on other occasions.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> He followed this with 66 in the third match but did not take many wickets before the first Test.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Warner considered he had nevertheless bowled well but was finding it difficult to maintain his energy in the hot conditions; his bowling lost pace and sting later in his bowling spells.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Hirst played in all five Test matches, the first two of which were won by England. In the first game, Hirst took two wickets. In his first innings, he scored a <a href="/wiki/Duck_(cricket)" title="Duck (cricket)">duck</a> and was dropped by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Laver" title="Frank Laver">Frank Laver</a> before he had scored in the second. He survived to score 60 not out and his partnership with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hayward" title="Tom Hayward">Tom Hayward</a> guided England to victory after wickets had fallen early chasing a target of 194.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Hirst scored 11 runs in the second match and took three wickets on a difficult batting pitch, badly affected by rain.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Although this performance was not statistically impressive, Warner thought he bowled very well.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> During the third Test, Hirst took three wickets and, with innings of 58 and 44,<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> was one of the few successful England batsmen in a poor performance which enabled Australia to win the match.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> England won the crucial fourth match to ensure the series victory. Hirst took two wickets and contributed scores of 25 and 18.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Australia achieved a win in the final match in which Hirst scored 0 and 1, but after taking no wickets in the first innings he achieved his best Test figures of the tour in the second, with five for 48.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the Test series, Hirst scored 217 runs at an average of 24.11 and took 15 wickets at an average of 30.06.<sup id="cite_ref-tbat_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbat-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tbowl_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbowl-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> He scored 569 runs at 33.47 and took 36 wickets at 24.50 over all first-class games on the tour.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> His best bowling performance was five for 37 against Tasmania.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Warner described Hirst as a very consistent batsman throughout the tour,<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> and noted that while he was a less effective bowler in Australian conditions than at home, he was often unlucky.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dominant_in_county_cricket">Dominant in county cricket</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Dominant in county cricket">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:George_Hirst_by_Leslie_Ward_(modified).jpg" class="image"><img alt="Caricature of a cricketer holding a ball" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/George_Hirst_by_Leslie_Ward_%28modified%29.jpg/170px-George_Hirst_by_Leslie_Ward_%28modified%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="290" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/George_Hirst_by_Leslie_Ward_%28modified%29.jpg/255px-George_Hirst_by_Leslie_Ward_%28modified%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/George_Hirst_by_Leslie_Ward_%28modified%29.jpg/340px-George_Hirst_by_Leslie_Ward_%28modified%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="566" data-file-height="964" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:George_Hirst_by_Leslie_Ward_(modified).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A caricature of Hirst by <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Ward" title="Leslie Ward">Leslie Ward</a> ("Spy") published in January 1903: the caption reads "Yorkshire".</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Affected_by_injury">Affected by injury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Affected by injury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After returning to England, Hirst completed another double in 1904. Although a leg injury reduced the pace and effectiveness of his bowling for much of the season,<sup id="cite_ref-T50_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T50-64">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> he scored 2,501 runs at an average of 54.36, the highest aggregate and average of his career, and took 132 wickets at 21.09.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He became the first Yorkshire player to achieve the double of 2,000 runs and 100 wickets, a feat previously achieved only by the <a href="/wiki/Gloucestershire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Gloucestershire County Cricket Club">Gloucestershire</a> trio of <a href="/wiki/W._G._Grace" title="W. G. Grace">W. G. Grace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Townsend" title="Charlie Townsend">Charlie Townsend</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Jessop" title="Gilbert Jessop">Gilbert Jessop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-T50_64-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T50-64">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> Most of his eight centuries were either against the strongest counties or in adverse circumstances for the team.<sup id="cite_ref-T51_65-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T51-65">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> In August, Hirst had a <a href="/wiki/Benefit_(sports)" title="Benefit (sports)">benefit match</a> against Lancashire from which he received £3,703,<sup id="cite_ref-T51_65-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T51-65">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> worth around £380,000 as of 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-UKGDP_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UKGDP-66">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> a very high sum for a benefit at the time, and his popularity was reflected in the attendance over three days of 78,792 spectators.<sup id="cite_ref-H56_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H56-67">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yorkshire finished second to Lancashire in the County Championship in 1904 but regained the title in 1905.<sup id="cite_ref-ccposn_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccposn-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Hirst's leg-strain continued to cause him pain throughout the season and again hampered his bowling.<sup id="cite_ref-T52_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T52-68">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> Even so, he took 110 wickets at 19.94 and passed 2,000 runs for the second time, scoring 2,226 runs at an average of 53.95.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Early in the season, Hirst scored 341 against Leicestershire, the highest score of his career and, as of 2015, the record individual innings by a Yorkshire batsman.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> On a good pitch for batting,<sup id="cite_ref-T52_68-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T52-68">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> he began his innings when Yorkshire had scored 22 for three wickets, in reply to Leicestershire's score of 419, batted just under seven hours and hit 53 fours and a six.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> Later that season, he scored another double century, hitting 232 not out against Surrey; only two other players reached double figures in the Yorkshire innings. In the same game, Hirst took five wickets for 43 runs.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hirst's leg injury kept him out of the England side for the first two Test matches against Australia, although he would have been a certain selection had he been fit.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> He was chosen in the squad for the second match but was left out on fitness grounds.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> However, Hirst was not particularly successful in the three Tests he played that season, hitting a highest score of 40 not out; as a bowler he never took more than three wickets in an innings. In the series he scored 105 runs at an average of 35.00 and took 6 wickets at 35.33.<sup id="cite_ref-tbat_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbat-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tbowl_48-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbowl-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> England won the series 2–0, mainly due to the all-round efforts of Hirst's Yorkshire teammate <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Jackson" title="Stanley Jackson">Stanley Jackson</a>, who headed the batting and bowling averages for the series.<sup id="cite_ref-T51_65-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T51-65">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Record_breaking_season_and_Tests_against_South_Africa">Record breaking season and Tests against South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Record breaking season and Tests against South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1906, Hirst completed an unprecedented double of 2,000 runs and 200 wickets.<sup id="cite_ref-Beard_74-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beard-74">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> Cricket writer <a href="/wiki/A._A._Thomson" title="A. A. Thomson">A. A. Thomson</a> noted that both milestones were unusual individually, and that such all-round achievements had only been matched in scope by those of W. G. Grace in the 1870s.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> No other cricketer has come close to matching this particular double.<sup id="cite_ref-E82_76-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E82-76">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup> Hirst reached the 1,000 run–100 wicket double by the end of June, two weeks faster than anyone else in the history of first-class cricket.<sup id="cite_ref-Chalke_77-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chalke-77">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> In total he scored 2,385 first-class runs at an average of 45.86 and took 208 wickets at 16.50.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Hirst's contributions were particularly important in a close race for the County Championship. <a href="/wiki/Kent_County_Cricket_Club" title="Kent County Cricket Club">Kent</a> were eventual champions after Yorkshire lost a close game to Gloucestershire, but Hirst performed well in the two matches against Kent. He scored a century and took 11 wickets as Yorkshire won the first, and took eight wickets and scored a match-saving 93 in the drawn second encounter. Against the other title contenders, Lancashire and Surrey, he was likewise successful with both bat and ball, earning praise from <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> and <i>Wisden</i> for his batting under difficult circumstances in these matches.<sup id="cite_ref-Chalke_77-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chalke-77">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> His captain, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Hawke,_7th_Baron_Hawke" title="Martin Hawke, 7th Baron Hawke">Lord Hawke</a>, said "It was not only what Georgie Hirst did but how he did it, coming off [i. e. "succeeding"] when an effort seemed most necessary and playing his best against the more formidable sides."<sup id="cite_ref-Chalke_77-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chalke-77">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> He scored six centuries, two of which came in a record-equalling performance against Somerset. He scored 111 and 117 not out when batting and took six for 70 and five for 45 with the ball.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He became only the second man after <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Bosanquet_(cricketer)" title="Bernard Bosanquet (cricketer)">Bernard Bosanquet</a> to score two centuries and take 10 wickets in the same first-class match; as of 2015, only one other man, <a href="/wiki/Franklyn_Stephenson" title="Franklyn Stephenson">Franklyn Stephenson</a> in 1988, has achieved the feat.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> Battling a knee injury and exhaustion towards the end of the season, it became increasingly difficult for Hirst to perform.<sup id="cite_ref-E82_76-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E82-76">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Chalke_77-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chalke-77">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> Hirst took his 200th wicket at the end of August and commented that, were his feat to be duplicated in future, "whoever does will be very tired".<sup id="cite_ref-Chalke_77-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chalke-77">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup> He also commented that his injuries only troubled him once the season was over and it "was a triumph of spirit over matter".<sup id="cite_ref-E82_76-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E82-76">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The weather was poor during the 1907 season, resulting in a succession of pitches which suited spin bowling and were difficult to bat on. In these circumstances, Hirst scored fewer runs than in the previous season. He did not score a century, making 1,321 runs at 28.71,<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and the prevalent type of pitches did not suit his bowling pace so that he took fewer wickets.<sup id="cite_ref-T58_80-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T58-80">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> Even so, he was the second highest wicket-taker in the season and came fifth in the bowling averages,<sup id="cite_ref-T58_80-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T58-80">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> with 183 wickets at 15.29.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Hirst remained a first-choice member of the Test team, playing all three matches against  <a href="/wiki/South_African_cricket_team_in_England_in_1907" title="South African cricket team in England in 1907">South Africa</a> that summer in a series which England won 1–0, with the other two games drawn. Although unsuccessful with the bat, achieving 46 runs in five innings with a top-score of 17,<sup id="cite_ref-tbat_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbat-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> in a low-scoring second Test, Hirst was one of the few batsmen to handle the <a href="/wiki/Googly" title="Googly">googlies</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aubrey_Faulkner" title="Aubrey Faulkner">Aubrey Faulkner</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup> As a bowler he took 10 wickets at 18.50;<sup id="cite_ref-tbowl_48-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbowl-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> of these wickets, six were in the final match of the series.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Yorkshire won the County Championship again during the 1908 season.<sup id="cite_ref-ccposn_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccposn-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Hirst completed another double, scoring 1,598 runs at 38.97 and taking 174 wickets at 14.05,<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> but was not selected for any other representative games until the end of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However, he declined an invitation to tour Australia with the <a href="/wiki/English_cricket_team_in_Australia_in_1907%E2%80%9308" title="English cricket team in Australia in 1907–08">MCC team in 1907–08</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Final_Tests">Final Tests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Final Tests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 1909 season, Hirst was less successful. Possibly affected by his heavy workload with bat and ball in previous seasons, his batting disappointed critics. He scored 1,256 runs at 27.30, his lowest batting average since 1898.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-T61_83-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T61-83">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup> With the ball, he took 115 wickets at 20.05; his best performances came in the more important matches such as those against Lancashire and Surrey.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-T61_83-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T61-83">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup> He was selected for the Players against the Gentlemen, taking seven wickets in the game.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the Test series against Australia, which England lost 2–1, Hirst played in the first four matches. England won the first game, in which Hirst took nine wickets. On the first day, bowling throughout the Australian innings with <a href="/wiki/Colin_Blythe" title="Colin Blythe">Colin Blythe</a>, Hirst took four for 28.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The Australians were never comfortable;  <i>Wisden</i> noted that Hirst "[made] the ball swerve in his most puzzling fashion".<sup id="cite_ref-1T1909_84-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1T1909-84">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup> After England established a first innings lead, Australia were bowled out for 151 in their second innings, with Hirst taking five for 58. The English <a href="/wiki/Batting_order_(cricket)#Opening_batsmen" title="Batting order (cricket)">opening batsmen</a> scored the 105 runs required for victory without being separated.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Hirst and Blythe took all the wickets which fell to England in the match, a rare accomplishment, and apart from a brief period, bowled throughout the match without resting.<sup id="cite_ref-1T1909_84-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1T1909-84">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup> However, Hirst was ineffective in the rest of the series, and was left out of the side for the fifth and final Test.<sup id="cite_ref-E83_85-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E83-85">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup> In the series he scored 52 runs at an average of 8.66, with a top-score of 31,<sup id="cite_ref-tbat_25-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbat-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> and took 16 wickets at 21.75.<sup id="cite_ref-tbowl_48-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tbowl-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> This ended his Test career;<sup id="cite_ref-E83_85-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E83-85">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup> his final Test record in 24 matches was 790 runs, with three scores over fifty, at an average of 22.57, and 59 wickets at an average of 30.00.<sup id="cite_ref-Cricinfo_86-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cricinfo-86">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Last_seasons_before_the_First_World_War">Last seasons before the First World War</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Last seasons before the First World War">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yorkshire had one of their worst seasons to date in 1910, finishing eighth in the County Championship and attracting disapproval from critics.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">&#91;85&#93;</a></sup> Hirst led the Yorkshire batting and bowling averages and was the third highest wicket-taker in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">&#91;86&#93;</a></sup> He scored 1,840 runs at 32.85 and took 164 wickets at 14.79.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> One of his best performances came at Lord's, where he scored an unbeaten century to guide Yorkshire to a win against Middlesex. Against Lancashire he took nine for 23, the best bowling figures of his career, <a href="/wiki/Clean_bowled" class="mw-redirect" title="Clean bowled">clean-bowling</a> eight of his victims as Lancashire were dismissed for 61.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-T66_89-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T66-89">&#91;87&#93;</a></sup> Yorkshire continued to struggle in 1911, but Hirst was successful against many of the leading counties, mainly as a bowler.<sup id="cite_ref-T66_89-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T66-89">&#91;87&#93;</a></sup> However, he achieved some large scores; against Sussex, he hit the third double-century of his career and against Lancashire he scored 156 as well as taking six wickets for 83.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">&#91;88&#93;</a></sup> In total, he scored 1,789 runs at 33.12 and took 137 wickets at 20.40.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In 1912 Yorkshire regained the County Championship;<sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup> Hirst's form that season was not as good as in previous years, but he batted well before poor weather and a knee injury interrupted his cricket. He hit one century and his performances with the ball were unspectacular.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">&#91;90&#93;</a></sup> In all he scored 1,133 runs at an average of 25.75 and took 118 wickets at 17.37.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Although both Australia and South Africa toured England, Hirst did not play any Tests and, for the first time since 1904, was not selected for the Players side in any of their matches.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hirst completed the 14th and final double of his career in 1913, leading the Yorkshire batting averages with 1,540 runs at an average of 35.81.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup> His bowling was not as effective and he was no longer Yorkshire's main attacking bowler, as <a href="/wiki/Alonzo_Drake" title="Alonzo Drake">Alonzo Drake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Major_Booth" title="Major Booth">Major Booth</a> headed the averages.<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">&#91;92&#93;</a></sup> Hirst took 101 wickets at 20.13.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> During the following season, the last before the First World War, Hirst was further afflicted by injuries and missed some matches. He bowled far less frequently than in recent seasons and his 43 wickets, which cost 29.81, were his fewest since 1898; it was the first time since 1902 he failed to complete the double.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-T72_95-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T72-95">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup> His batting remained effective and he produced some good performances when his team were in difficult circumstances, although his two centuries were against the weaker bowling attacks of <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Northamptonshire County Cricket Club">Northamptonshire</a> and Somerset.<sup id="cite_ref-T72_95-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T72-95">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup> He scored 1,670 runs at 41.75.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In June, he was selected in a match to celebrate the hundredth anniversary of the current Lord's ground, playing for the Rest of England against the MCC side which had toured South Africa the previous winter.<sup id="cite_ref-T73_96-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T73-96">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup> The outbreak of war brought the season to an early close in August.<sup id="cite_ref-T73_96-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T73-96">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_career">Later career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Later career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Final_playing_years">Final playing years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Final playing years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the war Hirst, along with Rhodes and their team-mate <a href="/wiki/Schofield_Haigh" title="Schofield Haigh">Schofield Haigh</a>, worked in a munitions factory in Huddersfield. Hirst and Rhodes were paid by Yorkshire to play in war-time cricket matches on a certain number of Saturdays.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup> Hirst played in the <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Cricket_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradford Cricket League">Bradford League</a>, and became known among cricketers for accepting minimal fees from financially struggling clubs.<sup id="cite_ref-H56_67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H56-67">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup> When first-class cricket restarted in 1919, Hirst resumed playing for Yorkshire. In the first match of the season he hit an aggressive unbeaten 180 against MCC, to secure a draw for the county; he followed this with two more centuries in the first two weeks of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">&#91;97&#93;</a></sup> Although his form later faltered, he played some substantial innings in difficult circumstances and ended the season with 1,441 runs at an average of 38.94.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-T78_100-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T78-100">&#91;98&#93;</a></sup> He bowled infrequently, taking 18 wickets at 29.27.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> During this season, Hirst accepted the position of coach at <a href="/wiki/Eton_College" title="Eton College">Eton College</a>. In the last match of the season, at Scarborough, he was given a warm reception by the crowd, who did not expect to see him playing for Yorkshire again.<sup id="cite_ref-T78_100-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T78-100">&#91;98&#93;</a></sup> However, he appeared on occasions during the school holidays in the next two seasons, although he did not score any centuries, did not average more than 24 with the bat and took only 21 wickets in total.<sup id="cite_ref-fcbatting_12-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbatting-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fcbowling_13-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fcbowling-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-T78_100-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T78-100">&#91;98&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the 1921 season Hirst retired as a Yorkshire player, and made what was expected to be his final first-class appearance in a <a href="/wiki/Scarborough_Festival" title="Scarborough Festival">Scarborough Festival</a> match, in which he captained the Players against the Gentlemen. On the last day of the match, Hirst's 50th birthday, he took the final two wickets to secure victory for his side. The crowd gathered outside the pavilion and demanded to see him; he gave a farewell speech, and was moved by the reception given to him.<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">&#91;99&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ODNB_102-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODNB-102">&#91;100&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hirst played three more first-class games; in 1921–22 he played two games for the <a href="/wiki/Europeans_cricket_team" title="Europeans cricket team">Europeans cricket team</a> in India, and in 1929, aged 58, he made a final appearance for Yorkshire in a Scarborough Festival match against the MCC.<sup id="cite_ref-figures_8-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-figures-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-T80_103-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T80-103">&#91;101&#93;</a></sup> He scored just one run before <a href="/wiki/Bill_Bowes" title="Bill Bowes">Bill Bowes</a> bowled him; Hirst reportedly commented: "A grand ball that, lad. I couldn't have played that one when I was good."<sup id="cite_ref-T80_103-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T80-103">&#91;101&#93;</a></sup> In all first-class cricket, Hirst played 826 games, scored 36,356 runs at an average of 34.13 with 60 hundreds and took 2,742 wickets at 18.73.<sup id="cite_ref-Cricinfo_86-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cricinfo-86">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following his retirement as a player, Hirst occasionally umpired first-class matches, taking charge of at least one match at every Scarborough Festival between 1922 and 1938. He also umpired two matches on Yorkshire's tour of Jamaica in 1936,<sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">&#91;102&#93;</a></sup> and a Minor Counties match between Yorkshire and Lancashire second teams.<sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105">&#91;103&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coaching_career">Coaching career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Coaching career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1920 to 1938, Hirst was the cricket coach at Eton.<sup id="cite_ref-W133_106-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W133-106">&#91;104&#93;</a></sup> The college's most important match was the <a href="/wiki/Eton_v_Harrow" title="Eton v Harrow">annual match against Harrow at Lord's</a>, and during Hirst's tenure, the team were unbeaten in the fixture, winning the six matches that produced a definite result and drawing the remainder. Following Hirst's retirement, Eton lost to Harrow in 1939 for the first time since the First World War.<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107">&#91;105&#93;</a></sup> In the period of Hirst's coaching, Eton only lost once—in 1920—to Winchester.<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108">&#91;106&#93;</a></sup> A combination of Hirst's technical knowledge, playing experience and empathy with young people made him a very successful coach. He taught technical proficiency, but encouraged his pupils to play their natural game.<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109">&#91;107&#93;</a></sup> His <i>Times</i> obituary noted "his professional capacity earned him the respect of the boys and his natural good humour and good manners gained him the love of all."<sup id="cite_ref-timesobit_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesobit-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> When he retired, a dinner was held in his honour at Eton.<sup id="cite_ref-110" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-110">&#91;108&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While serving as the Eton coach, Hirst also worked with young Yorkshire players during the school holidays, and maintained his coaching connection with the county until shortly before his death.<sup id="cite_ref-timesobit_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesobit-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ODNB_102-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODNB-102">&#91;100&#93;</a></sup> Despite the differences in the backgrounds of the players, Hirst was equally respected at Eton and when he acted as a coach to the Yorkshire team.<sup id="cite_ref-111" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-111">&#91;109&#93;</a></sup> At Yorkshire Hirst worked with young players in the cricket nets at <a href="/wiki/Headingley_Cricket_Ground" title="Headingley Cricket Ground">Headingley</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112">&#91;110&#93;</a></sup> took charge of indoor trials during the winter,<sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-113">&#91;111&#93;</a></sup> and travelled with the team as coach on a tour to Jamaica in 1936.<sup id="cite_ref-114" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-114">&#91;112&#93;</a></sup> Many Yorkshire players, with vastly differing temperaments, came under his influence and improved as players.<sup id="cite_ref-115" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-115">&#91;113&#93;</a></sup> One of Hirst's most notable achievements as a coach was improving the bowling of <a href="/wiki/George_Macaulay" title="George Macaulay">George Macaulay</a> to the point where he became a key member of the Yorkshire team.<sup id="cite_ref-H56_67-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H56-67">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-116" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-116">&#91;114&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bill Bowes, who received coaching by Hirst and went on to play for England, described him as "the finest coach in the world".<sup id="cite_ref-B147_117-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-B147-117">&#91;115&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Len_Hutton" title="Len Hutton">Len Hutton</a>, another who was coached by Hirst, wrote "I shall always think of George Hirst as the ideal coach. He was a 'natural' one, the guide, the philosopher, and friend of every young fellow who has had a trial under him".<sup id="cite_ref-118" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-118">&#91;116&#93;</a></sup> Part of Hirst's success at Eton came from his personality and ability to extract the best from people.<sup id="cite_ref-Gents_119-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gents-119">&#91;117&#93;</a></sup> Bowes noted how his enthusiasm inspired young players,<sup id="cite_ref-Gents_119-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gents-119">&#91;117&#93;</a></sup> and his humour and kindness led boys to worship him. He could pass on technical knowledge in a way that was easy to comprehend, backed up with anecdotes to illustrate his point. Bowes described how "he had a rare skill in noting and demonstrating your faults and no less skill in illustrating the remedies".<sup id="cite_ref-B147_117-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-B147-117">&#91;115&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Style_and_technique">Style and technique</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Style and technique">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hirst_pull.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A cricketer about to hit a ball" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Hirst_pull.jpg/170px-Hirst_pull.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="234" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Hirst_pull.jpg/255px-Hirst_pull.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Hirst_pull.jpg/340px-Hirst_pull.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1073" data-file-height="1476" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hirst_pull.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>George Hirst getting into position to pull a short ball, photographed by <a href="/wiki/George_Beldam" title="George Beldam">George Beldam</a></div></div></div>
<p>Hirst received little coaching as a batsman. Physically brave,<sup id="cite_ref-timesobit_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesobit-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> he was often at his best on pitches which were difficult for batting,<sup id="cite_ref-obit_120-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-120">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup> and when his team faced a crisis.<sup id="cite_ref-W133_106-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W133-106">&#91;104&#93;</a></sup> His usual approach was to bat aggressively. Although he could play defensively if required, he preferred to attack when his team were in difficulties.<sup id="cite_ref-T86_121-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T86-121">&#91;119&#93;</a></sup> He played a variety of strokes, but he favoured the <a href="/wiki/Batting_(cricket)#Drive" title="Batting (cricket)">on drive</a> and particularly the <a href="/wiki/Batting_(cricket)#Pull_and_hook" title="Batting (cricket)">pull and hook shots</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-T86_121-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T86-121">&#91;119&#93;</a></sup> He was able to pull and hook almost any ball delivered to him, making it difficult to place fielders effectively while he was batting.<sup id="cite_ref-timesobit_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesobit-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Only in Australia did this approach prove less successful.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_120-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-120">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup> He also established a reputation as an outstanding fielder at <a href="/wiki/Fielding_(cricket)#Fielding_position_names_and_locations" title="Fielding (cricket)">mid off</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-timesobit_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesobit-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In this position, he took many catches, often from hard drives in an era when batsmen played this shot very well.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_120-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-120">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although Hirst was a right-handed batsman, he bowled with his left arm at <a href="/wiki/Fast_bowling#Categorisation_of_fast_bowling" class="mw-redirect" title="Fast bowling">medium-fast</a> pace.<sup id="cite_ref-Cricinfo_86-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cricinfo-86">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup> He was one of the first bowlers to make the ball swerve through the air in a controlled fashion.<sup id="cite_ref-timesobit_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesobit-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> According to A. A. Thomson, Hirst's development of swing bowling was almost as revolutionary as <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Bosanquet_(cricketer)" title="Bernard Bosanquet (cricketer)">Bernard Bosanquet's</a> invention of the <a href="/wiki/Googly" title="Googly">googly</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-T37_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T37-30">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> His bowling partnership with Rhodes was particularly effective, and established a formidable reputation.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_120-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-120">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup> Hirst could not make the ball swing in every match, nor could he maintain it through a long innings. However, when he could achieve swerve, even the best batsmen found it almost impossible to bat against him. His success was dependent on atmospheric conditions; for example, he could not swing the ball much in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-W55_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W55-54">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> He was particularly effective when bowling into the wind.<sup id="cite_ref-T37_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T37-30">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Woods" title="Sammy Woods">Sammy Woods</a> described facing Hirst when the ball was moving in the air: "How the devil can you play a ball that comes in at you like a hard throw-in from cover-point?"<sup id="cite_ref-T37_30-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-T37-30">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Hirst was not an especially quick bowler,  a little faster than <a href="/wiki/Fast_bowling#Categorisation_of_fast_bowling" class="mw-redirect" title="Fast bowling">medium pace</a>, with a long run-up and a relaxed action.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_120-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-120">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup> He usually bowled <a href="/wiki/Cricket_terminology#O" class="mw-redirect" title="Cricket terminology">over the wicket</a>, meaning he bowled from the right hand side of the wickets and therefore angled the ball across the pitch. After delivery, the ball swung through the air at the last minute and hurried through after pitching, appearing to get faster. A very accurate bowler, he was difficult to score against unless a batsman was prepared to hit him in the air over <a href="/wiki/Fielding_(cricket)#Fielding_position_names_and_locations" title="Fielding (cricket)">mid on</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-timesobit_10-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesobit-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-obit_120-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-120">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup> The main dangers to the batsmen were the risk of being bowled or hitting the ball with a defensive shot and being caught by specially-placed fielders on the leg side. His ability to make the ball swing made him effective on a variety of pitches.<sup id="cite_ref-H55_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H55-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Before Hirst developed his technique, bowlers often rubbed the ball in the dirt to remove the shiny layer of the ball, unaware that this layer helped the ball to swing. Hirst's <i>Wisden</i> obituary records: "Hirst, in fact, has been described as the father of all modern seam and swing bowling."<sup id="cite_ref-obit_120-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-120">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hirst completed the double 14 times, more than any other cricketer except Rhodes.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_120-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-120">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup> Unusually for an all-rounder, for much of his career he was equally successful as a batsman and as a bowler.<sup id="cite_ref-W133_106-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W133-106">&#91;104&#93;</a></sup> Consequently, he was a key member of the Yorkshire team.<sup id="cite_ref-Sw12_122-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sw12-122">&#91;120&#93;</a></sup> His Yorkshire captain Lord Hawke described Hirst as "the greatest county cricketer of all time",<sup id="cite_ref-obit_120-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-120">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup> and journalist <a href="/wiki/Jim_Kilburn" title="Jim Kilburn">Jim Kilburn</a> noted that no cricketer could "capture the heart and the imagination and the affections more firmly than George Herbert Hirst".<sup id="cite_ref-Kilburn_123-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kilburn-123">&#91;121&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/E._W._Swanton" title="E. W. Swanton">E. W. Swanton</a> described his typical style of play as "grafting for victory without heroics".<sup id="cite_ref-Sw12_122-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sw12-122">&#91;120&#93;</a></sup> His record as a Test cricketer was less impressive than his figures in county cricket, owing to some extent to playing conditions during his two tours of Australia which reduced the effectiveness of his bowling and batting.<sup id="cite_ref-ODNB_102-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODNB-102">&#91;100&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hirst gave the impression of enjoying every game he played, and many of the captains under whom he played praised both his personality and his contribution to the team. Lord Hawke said that Hirst's smile "went right round his head and met at the back".<sup id="cite_ref-124" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-124">&#91;122&#93;</a></sup> Warner noted his wit helped the team in difficult situations during the tour of Australia in 1903–04.<sup id="cite_ref-obit_120-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-120">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup> Hirst's <i>Times</i> obituary said: "No why or wherefore, no explanation of his great ability, not even his record which adorns the pages of <i>Wisden</i> can adequately describe to those who had not the fortune to see him play the rich quality of George Hirst, the type of professional cricketer to which all would like to aspire. He played during the golden age of cricket, and he was one of the most illustrious of his time."<sup id="cite_ref-timesobit_10-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesobit-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Hirst was noted for his honesty, sportsmanship, and enthusiasm.<sup id="cite_ref-H56_67-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-H56-67">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gents_119-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gents-119">&#91;117&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-125" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-125">&#91;123&#93;</a></sup> Known as "George Herbert", he was admired and affectionately regarded by his contemporaries and by spectators.<sup id="cite_ref-W133_106-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-W133-106">&#91;104&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sw12_122-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sw12-122">&#91;120&#93;</a></sup> The public worshipped him in a way never replicated for his contemporary and fellow Kirkheaton-born all-rounder Wilfred Rhodes, a much more dour character.<sup id="cite_ref-126" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-126">&#91;124&#93;</a></sup> The two men were never good friends; there may have been  a degree of jealousy between them, and Rhodes did not appreciate Hirst's jovial attitude.<sup id="cite_ref-127" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-127">&#91;125&#93;</a></sup> Rhodes was more tactically astute than Hirst, but Hirst's enthusiasm and personality were more inspirational to the team. Rhodes, when asked about Hirst's ability to swing the ball, replied: "He was very good. But he didn't know how to use it, you know. I had to set the field for him so that he got the best out of it."<sup id="cite_ref-Gents_119-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gents-119">&#91;117&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A plain-speaking man,<sup id="cite_ref-Sw12_122-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sw12-122">&#91;120&#93;</a></sup> Hirst could be firm and even outspoken at times.<sup id="cite_ref-128" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-128">&#91;126&#93;</a></sup> <i>Wisden</i> said: "Cricket was George Hirst's life".<sup id="cite_ref-obit_120-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-obit-120">&#91;118&#93;</a></sup> Bowes wrote that Hirst "was loved as a player, he was worshipped as a coach, revered as a man. His friends numbered thousands. He gave his full life to cricket; cricket gave a full life to him."<sup id="cite_ref-B147_117-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-B147-117">&#91;115&#93;</a></sup> Bowes also commented: "I never hope to meet a better coach or a better man."<sup id="cite_ref-129" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-129">&#91;127&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hirst&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 1 January 1896, Hirst married Emma Kilner in Kirkheaton;  James, their first child, was born on 6 October of the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-E78_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E78-21">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> A second child, Annie, followed in December 1899,<sup id="cite_ref-E80_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E80-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and a third, Molly, in April 1906.<sup id="cite_ref-E82_76-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E82-76">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup> The family first lived in Kirkheaton but later moved to <a href="/wiki/Marsh,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Marsh, West Yorkshire">Marsh</a>, a more affluent area of Huddersfield.<sup id="cite_ref-E78_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E78-21">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-E83_85-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E83-85">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In his later years, Hirst's health declined and he spent time in a nursing home. His wife died in 1953; twelve months later, on 10 May 1954, Hirst died, aged 82. He was cremated at Lawnswood Crematorium, Leeds.<sup id="cite_ref-130" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-130">&#91;128&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
