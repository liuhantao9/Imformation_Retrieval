<p><b>Gum Air</b> is a Surinamese airline based at <a href="/wiki/Zorg_en_Hoop_Airport" title="Zorg en Hoop Airport">Zorg en Hoop Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo,_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="Paramaribo, Suriname">Paramaribo, Suriname</a>. Gum Air cooperates with <a href="/wiki/Trans_Guyana_Airways" title="Trans Guyana Airways">Trans Guyana Airways</a> to provide daily flights between <a href="/wiki/Zorg_en_Hoop_Airport" title="Zorg en Hoop Airport">Zorg en Hoop Airport (ORG)</a> in Paramaribo, Suriname and <a href="/wiki/Ogle_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Ogle Airport">Ogle Airport (OGL)</a> in <a href="/wiki/Georgetown,_Guyana" title="Georgetown, Guyana">Georgetown, Guyana</a>).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gum_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886046874">@media all and (max-width:720px){.mw-parser-output .mobile-float-reset{float:none!important;width:100%!important}}.mw-parser-output .stack-container{box-sizing:border-box}.mw-parser-output .stack-clear-left{float:left;clear:left}.mw-parser-output .stack-clear-right{float:right;clear:right}.mw-parser-output .stack-left{float:left}.mw-parser-output .stack-right{float:right}.mw-parser-output .stack-object{margin:1px;overflow:hidden}</style><div class="mw-stack stack-container stack-clear-right mobile-float-reset"></div>
<p>Gum Air was founded in 1971 by six brothers of the Gummels family. The company they started was named Sky Farmers and focused on national flights.
</p><p>Gum Air has flown various unique types of <a href="/wiki/STOL" title="STOL">STOL</a> Short Take Off &amp; Landing aircraft from Suriname in the past such as <a href="/wiki/Dornier_Do_28" title="Dornier Do 28">Dornier DO 28D-1 Skyservant</a>, <a href="/wiki/GAF_Nomad" title="GAF Nomad">GAF Nomad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Britten-Norman_Islander" class="mw-redirect" title="Britten-Norman Islander">Britten-Norman BN-2 Islander</a> and also helicopter types such as <a href="/wiki/Agusta-Bell_204" class="mw-redirect" title="Agusta-Bell 204">Agusta-Bell 204</a>B &amp; <a href="/wiki/Bell_206" title="Bell 206">Bell 206B Jet Ranger II</a>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gum_Air&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>On 10 February 2001 Gum Air's <a href="/wiki/GAF_Nomad" title="GAF Nomad">GAF Nomad</a> N24A, registered PZ-TBP was written off when it crashed on a flight from Paramaribo – Zanderij <a href="/wiki/Johan_Adolf_Pengel_International_Airport" title="Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport">Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport</a> (PBM/SMJP) to <a href="/wiki/Njoeng_Jacob_Kondre_Airstrip" title="Njoeng Jacob Kondre Airstrip">Njoeng Jacob Kondre Airstrip</a> (ICAO: SMJK). The Nomad plane had lost radio contact. According to personnel at the airstrip in Jacob Kondre it was flying low and crashed into a mountain. All 9 passengers plus the pilot died.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 21 August 2008 a Gum Air's <a href="/wiki/Cessna_206" title="Cessna 206">Cessna 206</a> Stationair 6 ran off the airstrip at <a href="/wiki/Poesoegroenoe_Airstrip" title="Poesoegroenoe Airstrip">Poesoegroenoe Airstrip</a> (ICAO: SMPG) during take-off when engine failure occurred. All 6 people on board survived, with only two slightly injured.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 22 November 2008 Gum Air's <a href="/wiki/Cessna_404_Titan" title="Cessna 404 Titan">Cessna 404 Titan</a>, registered PZ-TVC made a forced landing on the road near Gusterie, Suriname, after engine failures. The aircraft came to rest in the bushes and was reported destroyed. No injuries or fatalities occurred in this accident.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
