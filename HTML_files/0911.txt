class="plainlinks"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.brookline.k12.ma.us/coolidgecorner">Coolidge Corner School</a></td></tr></tbody></table><p><b>The Coolidge Corner School</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  formerly known as the Devotion School or Devo is a public <a href="/wiki/Elementary_school" class="mw-redirect" title="Elementary school">elementary school</a> located at 345 Harvard Street, <a href="/wiki/Brookline,_Massachusetts" title="Brookline, Massachusetts">Brookline, Massachusetts</a>, United States. The school was founded in 1892 on land formerly owned by Edward Devotion (1621-1685) and later by his grandson, another Edward Devotion (1667-1744). The land was purchased by the town from a later owner.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Devotions' <a href="/wiki/Edward_Devotion_House" title="Edward Devotion House">18th-century house</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> is preserved by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Brookline_Historical_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Brookline Historical Society (page does not exist)">Brookline Historical Society</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and stands amidst part of its original gardens in the school's forecourt.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coolidge_Corner_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The school is attended by over 800 students from pre-kindergarten to eighth grade, and is the largest of eight public elementary schools in Brookline. The school emphasizes diversity, with English being a second language to over one third of the student body, among which about 40% are English Language Learners. Roughly 37% of students are non-white or multiracial. <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_life">Student life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coolidge_Corner_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Student life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The school has a <a href="/wiki/Parent-Teacher_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="Parent-Teacher Association">Parent Teacher Organization</a> supporting activities including <a href="/wiki/Field_trip" title="Field trip">field trips</a>, "Arts Council," a <a href="/wiki/Science_fair" title="Science fair">science fair</a>, <a href="/wiki/Math" class="mw-redirect" title="Math">Math</a> night, and an International Night.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Renovation">Renovation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coolidge_Corner_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Renovation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The school was reconstructed and enlarged (completed in August, 2018). <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> For the 2016-2017 and 2017-2018 academic years, the student body was split between two temporary buildings, termed <i>Lower Devotion School</i> (grades K-4) and <i>Upper Devotion School</i> (grades 5-8).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The principals of Lower Devotion were David O'Hara and Jennifer Buller, <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and the principal of Upper Devotion was Monica Crowley.  <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coolidge_Corner_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The school built in 1892 was named for the second Edward Devotion, who decreed in his will that any money left over after the payments of his debt and funeral expenses be given to the town for use "towards Building or Maintaining a School as near the Centre of the said Town as shall be agreed upon by the Town."<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Although the money was long gone by the time school was built, the school was apparently named for him in recognition of his original request, although no records from the 1890s survive discussing the naming.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The school was attended by <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy#Early_life" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> from 1922 to 1924.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Albert Edward Scott, “Scotty,” was a Brookline newsboy and a graduate of the Devotion School. As the US entered World War I, he enlisted in the United States Army, at age fifteen. On July 23rd, 1918, at the age of sixteen, he was killed in action in France. Albert Edward Scott was a member of Company H, 101st United States Infantry, Allied Expeditionary Force. As his citation reads, “For extreme gallantry and risk of life, in combat with an armed enemy force,” Albert Edward Scott was awarded the Distinguished Service Cross, an award second only to the Medal of Honor.
</p><p>Until 2005, the school librarian was award-winning author Norman H. Finkelstein who wrote the book <i>The Other 1492</i><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> (not to be confused with the similarly named professor <a href="/wiki/Norman_Finkelstein" title="Norman Finkelstein">Norman Finkelstein</a>). Another former librarian, Christine McDonnell, is an author of many popular children's books including <i>Dog Wants to Play</i> and  <i>Ballet Bug</i>.  She retired in 2014.
</p><p>In 2018, a citizen-led movement endorsed by Brookline's Town Meeting and School Committee began the process of renaming the school, in recognition of the fact that the estate of Edward Devotion sold for his bequest included a slave.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>  In May 2018 the name was changed to the Coolidge Corner School pending a new name.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coolidge_Corner_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The school is the inspiration for Abigail Adams Junior High School, the fictional school featured in the <a href="/wiki/Beacon_Street_Girls" title="Beacon Street Girls">Beacon Street Girls</a> <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_books" class="mw-redirect" title="Children&#39;s books">children's books</a> series.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. </p>
