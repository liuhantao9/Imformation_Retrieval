<p>The early <b>history of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a></b> dates from 3000 BCE when <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(Americas)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (Americas)">Native Americans</a> first inhabited the area. The Dutch acquired Suriname from the English, and European settlement in any numbers dates from the seventeenth century, when it was a plantation colony utilizing slavery for sugar cultivation. With abolition in the late nineteenth century, planters sought labor from China, Madeira, India, and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, which was also colonized by the Dutch. Although Dutch is Suriname's official language, with such a diverse population, it developed a <a href="/wiki/Creole_language" title="Creole language">Creole language</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sranan" class="mw-redirect" title="Sranan">Sranan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Indigenous_settlement">Indigenous settlement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Indigenous settlement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Suriname was populated millennia before the Europeans by many distinct indigenous cultures. The largest nations at the time of colonialization were the <a href="/wiki/Arawaks" class="mw-redirect" title="Arawaks">Arawaks</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Nomad" title="Nomad">nomadic</a> coastal tribe that lived from <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Kalina_people" title="Kalina people">Caribs</a>. The Caribs conquered the Arawaks along much of the coast, and into the Caribbean, using sailing ships.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> They settled in Galibi (<i>Kupali Yumï</i>, meaning "tree of the forefathers") on the mouth of the <a href="/wiki/Marowijne_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Marowijne River">Marowijne</a> river. While the Arawak and Carib lived off the coast and <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a>, smaller groups of <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Indigenous people">indigenous peoples</a> lived in the <a href="/wiki/Rainforest" title="Rainforest">rainforest</a> inland, such as the <a href="/wiki/Akurio" class="mw-redirect" title="Akurio">Akurio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tri%C3%B3" class="mw-redirect" title="Trió">Trió</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warrau" class="mw-redirect" title="Warrau">Warrau</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wayana" title="Wayana">Wayana</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="European_colonization">European colonization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: European colonization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="English_colonization">English colonization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: English colonization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Guiana%27s.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Guiana%27s.jpg/250px-Guiana%27s.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Guiana%27s.jpg/375px-Guiana%27s.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Guiana%27s.jpg/500px-Guiana%27s.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="751" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Guiana%27s.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Coastline of the Guianas</div></div></div>
<p>The first Europeans who came to Suriname were Spanish explorers and Dutch traders who visited the area along with other parts of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>'s 'Wild Coast.' The first attempts to settle the area by Europeans was in 1630, when <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> settlers led by Captain Marshall attempted to found a colony.<sup id="cite_ref-Marshall&#39;s_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marshall&#39;s-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> They cultivated crops of <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>, but the venture failed financially.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Willoughbyland">Willoughbyland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Willoughbyland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Willoughbyland" title="Willoughbyland">Willoughbyland</a></div>
<p>In 1650, <a href="/wiki/Francis_Willoughby,_5th_Baron_Willoughby_of_Parham" title="Francis Willoughby, 5th Baron Willoughby of Parham">Lord Willoughby</a>, the governor of <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>, furnished out a vessel to settle a colony in Suriname. At his own cost he equipped a ship of 20 guns, and two smaller vessels with things necessary for the support of the plantation.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Major <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Rowse" title="Anthony Rowse">Anthony Rowse</a> settled there in his name. Two years later, for the better settling of the colony, he went in person, fortified and furnished it with things requisite for defence and trade. Willoughbyland consisted of around 30,000 acres (120&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) and a fort. In 1663 most of the work on the 50 or so plantations was done by native Indians and 3,000 African slaves.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> There were around 1,000 whites there, joined by Brazilian Jews, attracted by religious freedom which was granted to all the settlers by the English.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dutch_colonization">Dutch colonization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Dutch colonization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Surinam_(Dutch_colony)" title="Surinam (Dutch colony)">Surinam (Dutch colony)</a></div>
<p>The settlement was invaded by seven <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> ships (from the <a href="/wiki/Zeeland" title="Zeeland">Zeeland</a> region), led by <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Crijnssen" title="Abraham Crijnssen">Abraham Crijnssen</a>, on February 26, 1667. Fort Willoughby was captured the next day after a three-hour fight<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and renamed <a href="/wiki/Fort_Zeelandia_(Paramaribo)" title="Fort Zeelandia (Paramaribo)">Fort Zeelandia</a>. On July 31, 1667, the English and <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> signed the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Breda_(1667)" title="Treaty of Breda (1667)">Treaty of Breda</a>, in which for the time being the status quo was respected: the Dutch could keep occupying Suriname and the British the formerly Dutch colony <a href="/wiki/New_Amsterdam" title="New Amsterdam">New Amsterdam</a> (modern-day <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>). Willoughbyland was renamed <a href="/wiki/Suriname_(Dutch_colony)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suriname (Dutch colony)">Suriname</a>. This arrangement was made official in the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Westminster_(1674)" title="Treaty of Westminster (1674)">Treaty of Westminster</a> of 1674, after the British had regained and again lost Suriname in 1667 and the Dutch regained the colony in 1668. In 1683 the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Suriname" title="Society of Suriname">Society of Suriname</a> was set up, modelled on the ideas of <a href="/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Colbert" title="Jean-Baptiste Colbert">Jean-Baptiste Colbert</a> to profit from the management and defence of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a>'s colony. It had three participants, with equal shares in the society's responsibilities and profits—the city of <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, the family <a href="/wiki/Van_Aerssen_van_Sommelsdijck" class="mw-redirect" title="Van Aerssen van Sommelsdijck">Van Aerssen van Sommelsdijck</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_West_India_Company" title="Dutch West India Company">Dutch West India Company</a>. The Van Aerssen family only managed to sell its share in 1770. The Society came to an end in 1795 when this kind of trade and business was no longer seen as acceptable.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Slavery_and_emancipation">Slavery and emancipation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Slavery and emancipation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:A_Surinam_Planter_in_his_Morning_Dress.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/A_Surinam_Planter_in_his_Morning_Dress.JPG/220px-A_Surinam_Planter_in_his_Morning_Dress.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="301" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/A_Surinam_Planter_in_his_Morning_Dress.JPG/330px-A_Surinam_Planter_in_his_Morning_Dress.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/A_Surinam_Planter_in_his_Morning_Dress.JPG/440px-A_Surinam_Planter_in_his_Morning_Dress.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1584" data-file-height="2167" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:A_Surinam_Planter_in_his_Morning_Dress.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Dutch plantation owner and female slave from <a href="/wiki/William_Blake" title="William Blake">William Blake</a>'s illustrations of the work of <a href="/wiki/John_Gabriel_Stedman" title="John Gabriel Stedman">John Gabriel Stedman</a>, published in 1792-1794.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_3444-7_Begrafenis_bij_plantageslaven2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_3444-7_Begrafenis_bij_plantageslaven2.jpg/250px-Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_3444-7_Begrafenis_bij_plantageslaven2.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="189" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_3444-7_Begrafenis_bij_plantageslaven2.jpg/375px-Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_3444-7_Begrafenis_bij_plantageslaven2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_3444-7_Begrafenis_bij_plantageslaven2.jpg/500px-Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_3444-7_Begrafenis_bij_plantageslaven2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3467" data-file-height="2620" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tropenmuseum_Royal_Tropical_Institute_Objectnumber_3444-7_Begrafenis_bij_plantageslaven2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Funeral at slave plantation, Suriname. <div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Maroon_village,_Suriname_River,_1955.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Maroon_village%2C_Suriname_River%2C_1955.jpg/250px-Maroon_village%2C_Suriname_River%2C_1955.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="170" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Maroon_village%2C_Suriname_River%2C_1955.jpg/375px-Maroon_village%2C_Suriname_River%2C_1955.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Maroon_village%2C_Suriname_River%2C_1955.jpg/500px-Maroon_village%2C_Suriname_River%2C_1955.jpg 2x" data-file-width="652" data-file-height="444" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Maroon_village,_Suriname_River,_1955.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Maroon village, <a href="/wiki/Suriname_River" title="Suriname River">Suriname River</a>, 1955</div></div></div>
<p>In South America, slavery was the norm.  The native people proved to be in limited supply and consequently the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_slave_trade" title="Atlantic slave trade">Atlantic slave trade</a> supplied the workforce for the plantations.  The plantations were producing <a href="/wiki/Sugar" title="Sugar">sugar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">coffee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cocoa_bean" title="Cocoa bean">cocoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cotton" title="Cotton">cotton</a> which were exported for the Amsterdam market. In 1713 for instance most of the work on the 200 plantations was done by 13,000 <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> slaves. Their treatment was horrific, and slaves periodically escaped to the jungle from the start.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> These <i><a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">Maroons</a></i> (also known as "Djukas" or "Bakabusi Nengre") attacked the plantations in order to acquire goods that were in short supply and to free enslaved women. Notable leaders of the Surinam Maroons were <a href="/wiki/Alabi" title="Alabi">Alabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boni_(guerrilla_leader)" title="Boni (guerrilla leader)">Boni</a>, Joli-coeur and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kapitein_Broos&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kapitein Broos (page does not exist)">Broos</a> (Captain Broos). In the 18th century, three of the Maroon people signed a peace treaty, similar to the peace treaty ending the <a href="/wiki/First_Maroon_War" title="First Maroon War">First Maroon War</a> in Jamaica, whereby they were recognised as free people and received a yearly tribute that provided them with the goods they used to "liberate" from the plantations.  A contemporary description of the war between the Maroons and the plantation owners in Suriname can be found in <i>Narrative of a Five Years Expedition Against the Revolted Negroes of Surinam</i> by <a href="/wiki/John_Gabriel_Stedman" title="John Gabriel Stedman">John Gabriel Stedman</a>.
</p><p>Suriname was occupied by the British in 1799, after the Netherlands were incorporated by France, and was returned to the Dutch in 1816, after the defeat of <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>. The Dutch abolished slavery only in 1863, although the British had already abolished it during their short rule. The freed slaves were, however, still required to continue their plantation work on a contract basis and were not released until 1873;<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> up to that date they conducted obligatory but paid work at the plantations. In the meantime, many more workers had been imported from the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a>, mostly Chinese inhabitants of that colony, creating a <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Surinamese" title="Chinese Surinamese">Chinese Surinamese</a> population.  From 1873 to 1916, many laborers were imported from India, creating the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Surinamese" title="Indo-Surinamese">Indo-Surinamese</a>. After 1916, many laborers were again imported from the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/Java_(island)" class="mw-redirect" title="Java (island)">Java</a>, creating the <a href="/wiki/Javanese_Surinamese" title="Javanese Surinamese">Javanese Surinamese</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Twentieth_century">Twentieth century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Twentieth century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 20th century, the natural resources of Suriname, <a href="/wiki/Rubber" class="mw-redirect" title="Rubber">rubber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gold" title="Gold">gold</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a>, were exploited. The US company <a href="/wiki/Alcoa" title="Alcoa">Alcoa</a> had a claim on a large area in Suriname where bauxite, from which aluminum can be made, was found. Given that the peace treaties with the Maroon people granted them title to the lands, there have been international court cases that negated the right of the Surinam government to grant these claims (meaning the right to take the land for themselves).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (April 2010)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> On November 23, 1941, under an agreement with the Netherlands government-in-exile, the United States occupied Dutch Guiana to protect the bauxite mines.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Decolonization">Decolonization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Decolonization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Suriname_(Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands)" title="Suriname (Kingdom of the Netherlands)">Suriname (Kingdom of the Netherlands)</a></div>
<p>In 1945, the first full election was held, with the Netherlands providing aid in health matters.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 1954, <a href="/wiki/Suriname_(Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands)" title="Suriname (Kingdom of the Netherlands)">Suriname gained self-government</a>, with the Netherlands retaining control of defence and foreign affairs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Independence">Independence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Independence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Henck_Arron,_Beatrix,_Johan_Ferrier_1975.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Henck_Arron%2C_Beatrix%2C_Johan_Ferrier_1975.jpg/250px-Henck_Arron%2C_Beatrix%2C_Johan_Ferrier_1975.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="250" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Henck_Arron%2C_Beatrix%2C_Johan_Ferrier_1975.jpg/375px-Henck_Arron%2C_Beatrix%2C_Johan_Ferrier_1975.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Henck_Arron%2C_Beatrix%2C_Johan_Ferrier_1975.jpg/500px-Henck_Arron%2C_Beatrix%2C_Johan_Ferrier_1975.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2016" data-file-height="2016" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Henck_Arron,_Beatrix,_Johan_Ferrier_1975.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Henck_Arron" title="Henck Arron">Henck Arron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beatrix_of_the_Netherlands" title="Beatrix of the Netherlands">Beatrix</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johan_Ferrier" title="Johan Ferrier">Johan Ferrier</a> on November 25, 1975</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a></div>
<p>In 1973 the Dutch government started independence negotiations with the local government, led by the <a href="/wiki/National_Party_of_Suriname" title="National Party of Suriname">NPS</a> (a largely Creole party), which was granted on November 25, 1975. The Dutch instituted an aid programme worth US$1.5 billion to last till 1985. The first President of the country was <a href="/wiki/Johan_Ferrier" title="Johan Ferrier">Johan Ferrier</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Henck_Arron" title="Henck Arron">Henck Arron</a> (leader of the NPS) as Prime Minister. Roughly a third of the population emigrated to the Netherlands prior to independence, fearing that the new country would not be viable.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 1980, the government of Henck Arron <a href="/wiki/1980_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="1980 Surinamese coup d&#39;état">was overthrown in a military coup</a> led by Sergeant-Major <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a>. President Ferrier refused to recognise the new government, appointing <a href="/wiki/Henk_Chin_A_Sen" class="mw-redirect" title="Henk Chin A Sen">Henk Chin A Sen</a> (of the <a href="/wiki/Nationalist_Republican_Party_(Suriname)" title="Nationalist Republican Party (Suriname)">Nationalist Republican Party</a>). Another coup followed five months later, with the army replacing Ferrier with Chin A Sen. These developments were largely welcomed by a population that expected the new army-installed government to put an end to corruption and improve the standard of living. This was despite the fact that the new regime banned opposition parties and became increasingly dictatorial. The Dutch initially accepted the new government; however, relations between Suriname and the Netherlands collapsed when 15 members of the political opposition were killed by the army on December 8, 1982, in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Zeelandia_(Paramaribo)" title="Fort Zeelandia (Paramaribo)">Fort Zeelandia</a>. This event is also known as the <i><a href="/wiki/December_murders" title="December murders">December murders</a></i> (<i>Decembermoorden</i> in Dutch). The Dutch and Americans cut off their aid in protest at the move.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 1985, the ban on opposition parties was lifted, and work began on devising a new constitution. The following year saw the start of an anti-government rebellion of the Maroons in the interior, calling themselves the Jungle Commando and led by <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Brunswijk" title="Ronnie Brunswijk">Ronnie Brunswijk</a>. The Bouterse government violently tried to suppress the insurgency by burning villages and other similar means. Many Maroons fled to <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
