	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Treaty of Breda (1667)</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Breda_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Treaty of Breda (disambiguation)">Treaty of Breda (disambiguation)</a>.</div>

<p>The <b>Treaty of Breda</b> was signed at the Dutch city of <a href="/wiki/Breda_(Netherlands)" class="mw-redirect" title="Breda (Netherlands)">Breda</a>, 31 July (<a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>), 1667, by <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">United Provinces</a> (Netherlands), <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Denmark%E2%80%93Norway" title="Denmark–Norway">Denmark–Norway</a>. It brought a hasty end to the <a href="/wiki/Second_Anglo-Dutch_War" title="Second Anglo-Dutch War">Second Anglo-Dutch War</a> (1665–1667) in favour of the Dutch, as <a href="/wiki/Louis_XIV_of_France" title="Louis XIV of France">Louis XIV</a>'s forces began invading the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Netherlands" title="Spanish Netherlands">Spanish Netherlands</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/War_of_Devolution" title="War of Devolution">War of Devolution</a>, but left many territorial disputes unresolved. It was thus a typical quick <i><a href="/wiki/Uti_possidetis" title="Uti possidetis">uti possidetis</a></i> treaty. In the latter stages of the war, the Dutch had prevailed. <a href="/wiki/Michiel_de_Ruyter" title="Michiel de Ruyter">Lieutenant-Admiral Michiel de Ruyter</a> virtually controlled the seas around the south coast of England, following his successful <a href="/wiki/Raid_on_the_Medway" title="Raid on the Medway">Raid on the Medway</a>, and his presence encouraged English commissioners to <a href="/wiki/Sue_for_peace" class="mw-redirect" title="Sue for peace">sue for peace</a> quickly. Negotiations, which had been long protracted, and had actually begun in Breda before the raid, took only ten days to conclude after resumption of talks.
</p><p>During the negotiations, the English commissioners (<a href="/wiki/Denzil_Holles,_1st_Baron_Holles" title="Denzil Holles, 1st Baron Holles">Denzil Holles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henry_Coventry" title="Henry Coventry">Henry Coventry</a>) offered to return <a href="/wiki/New_Netherland" title="New Netherland">New Netherland</a> in exchange for their sugar factories on the coast of <a href="/wiki/Surinam" class="mw-redirect" title="Surinam">Surinam</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> that had been taken by <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Crijnssen" title="Abraham Crijnssen">Abraham Crijnssen</a> earlier in 1667. The Dutch side declined. In the <a href="/wiki/East_Indies" title="East Indies">East Indies</a>, the Dutch secured a worldwide monopoly on <a href="/wiki/Nutmeg" title="Nutmeg">nutmeg</a> by forcing England to give up their claim on <a href="/wiki/Run_(island)" title="Run (island)">Run</a>, the most remote of the <a href="/wiki/Banda_Islands" title="Banda Islands">Banda Islands</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Navigation" class="mw-redirect" title="Act of Navigation">Act of Navigation</a> was moderated in that the Dutch were now allowed to ship German goods, if imported over the <a href="/wiki/Rhine" title="Rhine">Rhine</a>, to England.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As communications were slow, special dates were established for the different parts of the world, on which legal hostilities would end: 5 September for the <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a> and the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>, 5 October for the other European seas, 2 November for the African coast north of the equator and 24 April 1668 for the rest of the world.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Acadia" title="Acadia">Acadia</a> was returned to France, without specifying what territories were actually involved on the ground. <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Temple" title="Thomas Temple">Thomas Temple</a>, the proprietor, residing in Boston, had been given a charter by <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Cromwell" title="Oliver Cromwell">Cromwell</a>, which was ignored in the Treaty, and the actual handing off was delayed at the site until 1670. In addition, the conquest of <a href="/wiki/New_Netherland" title="New Netherland">New Netherland</a> by the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> was confirmed on 21 July 1667, producing the Colonies of <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">Delaware</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, the island of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Kitts" title="Saint Kitts">Saint Kitts</a> was re-partitioned between English and French forces.
</p><p>The most complete contemporary account of the war was published first in <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>, then in <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> in 1668 as a <i>Description exacte de tout ce qui s'est passé dans les guerres</i>. It contains a list of Dutch vessels and goods lost in North America, an account of the 1664 capture of <a href="/wiki/New_Amsterdam" title="New Amsterdam">New Amsterdam</a> (<a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> today) with the articles of surrender to Governor <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nicolls" title="Richard Nicolls">Richard Nicolls</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michiel_de_Ruyter" title="Michiel de Ruyter">Michiel de Ruyter</a>'s voyage to the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>. The Dutch commemorated the Treaty of Breda with a patriotic engraving.
</p><p>The parties agreed to postpone a discussion of the pawnings of <a href="/wiki/Orkney" title="Orkney">Orkney</a> (1468) and <a href="/wiki/Shetland" title="Shetland">Shetland</a> (1469) until a future occasion. Such a discussion has yet to take place, leading some to argue that this constitutes a legal basis for regarding Orkney and Shetland as direct dependencies of the Scots crown, rather than parts of the <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
