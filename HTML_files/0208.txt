<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:BSAC-arms.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/BSAC-arms.jpg/200px-BSAC-arms.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="160" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/BSAC-arms.jpg/300px-BSAC-arms.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e7/BSAC-arms.jpg 2x" data-file-width="331" data-file-height="265" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:BSAC-arms.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The arms of the British South Africa Company.</div></div></div><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Company created to colonize and trade</div>
<p>A <b>chartered company</b> is an association with investors or <a href="/wiki/Shareholders" class="mw-redirect" title="Shareholders">shareholders</a> and incorporated and granted (often exclusive) rights by <a href="/wiki/Royal_charter" title="Royal charter">royal charter</a> (or similar instrument of government) for the purpose of trade, exploration, and <a href="/wiki/Colonization" title="Colonization">colonization</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
