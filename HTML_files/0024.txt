<p>The <b>Las Vegas Strip</b> is a stretch of <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Boulevard" title="Las Vegas Boulevard">South Las Vegas Boulevard</a> in <a href="/wiki/Clark_County,_Nevada" title="Clark County, Nevada">Clark County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a> that is known for its concentration of resort <a href="/wiki/Hotel" title="Hotel">hotels</a> and <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casinos</a>. <b>The Strip</b> is approximately 4.2 miles (6.8&#160;km) in length,<sup id="cite_ref-Google_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Google-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> located immediately south of the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> city limits in the <a href="/wiki/Unincorporated_towns_in_Nevada" title="Unincorporated towns in Nevada">unincorporated towns</a> of <a href="/wiki/Paradise,_Nevada" title="Paradise, Nevada">Paradise</a> and <a href="/wiki/Winchester,_Nevada" title="Winchester, Nevada">Winchester</a>. However, the Strip is often referred to as being in Las Vegas.
</p><p>Many of the largest hotel, casino, and <a href="/wiki/Resort" title="Resort">resort</a> properties in the world are located on the Strip. The boulevard's cityscape is highlighted by its use of contemporary architecture, lights, and a wide variety of attractions. Its hotels, casinos, restaurants, residential high-rises, entertainment offerings, and skyline have established the Strip as one of the most popular and iconic tourist destinations in the world and is one of the driving forces for Las Vegas' economy.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Most of the Strip has also been designated as an <a href="/wiki/National_Scenic_Byway" title="National Scenic Byway">All-American Road</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and is considered a <a href="/wiki/Scenic_route" title="Scenic route">scenic route</a> at night.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Historically, the casinos that were not in <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Las_Vegas" title="Downtown Las Vegas">Downtown Las Vegas</a> along <a href="/wiki/Fremont_Street" title="Fremont Street">Fremont Street</a> were limited to outside the city limits on Las Vegas Boulevard. In 1959, the <a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_Fabulous_Las_Vegas_sign" title="Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas sign">Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas sign</a> was constructed exactly 4.5 miles (7.2&#160;km) outside the city limits. The sign is currently located in the median just south of Russell Road, across from the now-demolished <a href="/wiki/Klondike_Hotel_and_Casino" title="Klondike Hotel and Casino">Klondike Hotel and Casino</a> and about 0.4 miles (0.64&#160;km) south of the southernmost entrance to <a href="/wiki/Mandalay_Bay" title="Mandalay Bay">Mandalay Bay</a>, which is the Strip's southernmost casino.
</p><p>In the strictest sense, "the Strip" refers only to the stretch of Las Vegas Boulevard that is roughly between <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Avenue" title="Sahara Avenue">Sahara Avenue</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russell_Road_(Las_Vegas)" title="Russell Road (Las Vegas)">Russell Road</a>, a distance of 4.2 miles (6.8&#160;km).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However, the term is often used to refer not only to the road but also to the various casinos and resorts that line the road, and even to properties that are not on the road but are in proximity to it. Phrases such as <i>Strip Area</i>, <i>Resort Corridor</i> or <i>Resort District</i> are sometimes used to indicate a larger geographical area, including properties 1 mile (1.6&#160;km) or more away from Las Vegas Boulevard, such as the <a href="/wiki/Hard_Rock_Hotel_and_Casino_(Las_Vegas)" title="Hard Rock Hotel and Casino (Las Vegas)">Hard Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rio_All_Suite_Hotel_and_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio All Suite Hotel and Casino">Rio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palms_Casino_Resort" title="Palms Casino Resort">Palms</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oyo_Hotel_%26_Casino" title="Oyo Hotel &amp; Casino">Oyo</a> casinos.
</p><p>A long-standing definition considers the Strip's northern terminus as the <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Las_Vegas" title="Sahara Las Vegas">Sahara</a>, though travel guides typically extend it to include the <a href="/wiki/Stratosphere_Las_Vegas" title="Stratosphere Las Vegas">Stratosphere</a> 0.4 miles (0.64&#160;km) to the north. <a href="/wiki/Mandalay_Bay" title="Mandalay Bay">Mandalay Bay</a>, located just north of Russell Road, is the southernmost resort considered to be on the Strip (the <a href="/wiki/Klondike_Hotel_and_Casino" title="Klondike Hotel and Casino">Klondike</a> was the southernmost until 2006, when it was closed, although it was not included in the Strip on some definitions and travel guides). The "Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas" sign is often considered part of the Strip, despite it being 0.4 miles south of the Mandalay Bay and Russell Road.
</p><p>Because of the number and size of the resorts, the resort corridor can be quite wide. <a href="/wiki/Interstate_15_(Nevada)" class="mw-redirect" title="Interstate 15 (Nevada)">Interstate 15</a> runs roughly parallel and 0.5 to 0.8 miles (0.80 to 1.29&#160;km) to the west of Las Vegas Boulevard for the entire length of the Strip. Paradise Road runs to the east in a similar fashion, and ends at St. Louis Avenue. The eastern side of the Strip is bounded by <a href="/wiki/McCarran_International_Airport" title="McCarran International Airport">McCarran International Airport</a> south of Tropicana Avenue.
</p><p>North of this point, the resort corridor can be considered to extend as far east as Paradise Road, although some consider Koval Lane as a less inclusive boundary. Interstate 15 is sometimes considered the western edge of the resort corridor from Interstate 215 to Spring Mountain Road. North of this point, Industrial Road serves as the western edge.
</p><p>Newer <a href="/wiki/Hotel" title="Hotel">hotels</a> and <a href="/wiki/Resort" title="Resort">resorts</a> such as <a href="/wiki/South_Point_Hotel,_Casino_%26_Spa" title="South Point Hotel, Casino &amp; Spa">South Point</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Grandview_at_Las_Vegas" title="The Grandview at Las Vegas">Grandview Resort</a>, and <a href="/wiki/M_Resort" title="M Resort">M Resort</a> are on Las Vegas Boulevard South as distant as 8 miles south of the "Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas" sign. Marketing for these casinos and hotels usually states that they are on southern Las Vegas Boulevard and not "Strip" properties.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Vegas_Strip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Early_years_.281930s.E2.80.931990s.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years_(1930s–1990s)">Early years (1930s–1990s)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Vegas_Strip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early years (1930s–1990s)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first casino to be built on <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_91" title="U.S. Route 91">Highway 91</a> was the <a href="/wiki/Pair-O-Dice" class="mw-redirect" title="Pair-O-Dice">Pair-o-Dice Club</a> in 1931, but the first resort on what is currently the Strip was the <a href="/wiki/El_Rancho_Vegas" title="El Rancho Vegas">El Rancho Vegas</a>, opening on April 3, 1941, with 63 rooms. That casino-resort stood for almost 20 years before being destroyed by a fire in 1960. Its success spawned a second hotel on what would become the Strip, the <a href="/wiki/Hotel_Last_Frontier" class="mw-redirect" title="Hotel Last Frontier">Hotel Last Frontier</a> in 1942. <a href="/wiki/Organized_crime" title="Organized crime">Organized crime</a> figures such as New York's <a href="/wiki/Bugsy_Siegel" title="Bugsy Siegel">Bugsy Siegel</a> took interest in the growing gaming center leading to other resorts such as the <a href="/wiki/Flamingo_Las_Vegas" title="Flamingo Las Vegas">Flamingo</a>, which opened in 1946, and the <a href="/wiki/Desert_Inn" title="Desert Inn">Desert Inn</a>, which opened in 1950. The funding for many projects was provided through the <a href="/wiki/American_National_Insurance_Company" title="American National Insurance Company">American National Insurance Company</a>, which was based in the then notorious <a href="/wiki/Free_State_of_Galveston" title="Free State of Galveston">gambling empire of Galveston, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gas_station_Last_Frontier_hotel.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Gas_station_Last_Frontier_hotel.jpg/220px-Gas_station_Last_Frontier_hotel.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Gas_station_Last_Frontier_hotel.jpg/330px-Gas_station_Last_Frontier_hotel.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Gas_station_Last_Frontier_hotel.jpg/440px-Gas_station_Last_Frontier_hotel.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1534" data-file-height="1038" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gas_station_Last_Frontier_hotel.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Strip in the 1940s. Pictured is the gas station of the <a href="/wiki/Hotel_Last_Frontier" class="mw-redirect" title="Hotel Last Frontier">Hotel Last Frontier</a>, the second hotel on the Strip.</div></div></div>
<p>Las Vegas Boulevard South was previously called <a href="/wiki/Arrowhead_Highway" class="mw-redirect" title="Arrowhead Highway">Arrowhead Highway</a>, or Los Angeles Highway. The Strip was named by <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Police_Department" title="Los Angeles Police Department">Los Angeles police</a> officer and businessman <a href="/wiki/Guy_McAfee" title="Guy McAfee">Guy McAfee</a>, after his hometown's <a href="/wiki/Sunset_Strip" title="Sunset Strip">Sunset Strip</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Caesars_Palace" title="Caesars Palace">Caesars Palace</a> was established in 1966. In 1968, <a href="/wiki/Kirk_Kerkorian" title="Kirk Kerkorian">Kirk Kerkorian</a> purchased the <a href="/wiki/Flamingo_Las_Vegas" title="Flamingo Las Vegas">Flamingo</a> and hired Sahara Hotels Vice President Alex Shoofey as President. Alex Shoofey brought along 33 of Sahara's top executives. The Flamingo was used to train future employees of the International Hotel, which was under construction. Opening in 1969, the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Hilton" class="mw-redirect" title="Las Vegas Hilton">International Hotel</a>, with 1,512 rooms, began the era of mega-resorts. The International is known as Westgate Las Vegas today.The first <a href="/wiki/Bally%27s_Las_Vegas" title="Bally&#39;s Las Vegas">MGM Grand Hotel and Casino</a>, also a Kerkorian property, opened in 1973 with 2,084 rooms. At the time, this was one of the largest hotels in the world by number of rooms. The <a href="/wiki/Rossiya_Hotel" title="Rossiya Hotel">Rossiya Hotel</a> built in 1967 in Moscow, for instance, had 3,200 rooms; however, most of the rooms in the Rossiya Hotel were single rooms of 118 sq. ft (roughly 1/4 size of a standard room at the MGM Grand Resort). On November 21, 1980, the MGM Grand suffered the worst <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Fire" class="mw-redirect" title="MGM Grand Fire">resort fire</a> in the history of Las Vegas as a result of electrical problems, killing 87 people. It reopened eight months later. In 1986, Kerkorian sold the MGM Grand to <a href="/wiki/Bally_Manufacturing" title="Bally Manufacturing">Bally Manufacturing</a>, and it was renamed <a href="/wiki/Bally%27s_Las_Vegas" title="Bally&#39;s Las Vegas">Bally's</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Wet_%27n_Wild_(Las_Vegas)" title="Wet &#39;n Wild (Las Vegas)">Wet 'n Wild water park</a> opened in 1985 and was located on the south side of the Sahara hotel. It closed at the end of the 2004 season and was later demolished. The opening of <a href="/wiki/The_Mirage" title="The Mirage">The Mirage</a> in 1989 set a new level to the Las Vegas experience, as smaller hotels and casinos made way for the larger mega-resorts. The <a href="/wiki/Rio_All_Suite_Hotel_and_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="Rio All Suite Hotel and Casino">Rio</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Excalibur_Hotel_and_Casino" title="Excalibur Hotel and Casino">Excalibur</a> opened in 1990. These huge facilities offer entertainment and <a href="/wiki/Restaurant" title="Restaurant">dining</a> options, as well as gambling and lodging. This change affected the smaller, well-known and now historic hotels and casinos, like the <a href="/wiki/Dunes_(hotel_and_casino)" title="Dunes (hotel and casino)">Dunes</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Sands_Hotel" class="mw-redirect" title="Sands Hotel">Sands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Stardust_Hotel" class="mw-redirect" title="Stardust Hotel">Stardust</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Hotel_and_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="Sahara Hotel and Casino">Sahara</a>.
</p><p>The lights along the Strip have been dimmed in a sign of respect to six performers and one other major Las Vegas figure upon their deaths. They are <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a> (1977), <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Davis_Jr." title="Sammy Davis Jr.">Sammy Davis Jr.</a> (1990),<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dean_Martin" title="Dean Martin">Dean Martin</a> (1995), <a href="/wiki/George_Burns" title="George Burns">George Burns</a> (1996), <a href="/wiki/Frank_Sinatra" title="Frank Sinatra">Frank Sinatra</a> (1998), former <a href="/wiki/UNLV_Runnin%27_Rebels_basketball" class="mw-redirect" title="UNLV Runnin&#39; Rebels basketball">UNLV basketball</a> head coach <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Tarkanian" title="Jerry Tarkanian">Jerry Tarkanian</a> (2015),<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Don_Rickles" title="Don Rickles">Don Rickles</a> (2017).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The Strip lights were dimmed later in 2017 as a memorial to victims of a <a href="/wiki/2017_Las_Vegas_Strip_shooting" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 Las Vegas Strip shooting">mass shooting</a> at a concert held adjacent to the Strip.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  In 2005, Clark County renamed a section of Industrial Road (south of Twain Avenue) as Dean Martin Drive, also as a tribute to the famous <a href="/wiki/Rat_Pack" title="Rat Pack">Rat Pack</a> singer, actor, and frequent Las Vegas entertainer.
</p><p>In an effort to attract families, resorts offered more attractions geared toward youth, but had limited success. The current <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Las_Vegas" title="MGM Grand Las Vegas">MGM Grand</a> opened in 1993 with <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Adventures_Theme_Park" title="MGM Grand Adventures Theme Park">MGM Grand Adventures Theme Park</a>, but the park closed in 2000 due to lack of interest. Similarly, in 2003 <a href="/wiki/Treasure_Island_Hotel_and_Casino" title="Treasure Island Hotel and Casino">Treasure Island</a> closed its own <a href="/wiki/Video_arcade" class="mw-redirect" title="Video arcade">video arcade</a> and abandoned the previous <a href="/wiki/Pirate" class="mw-redirect" title="Pirate">pirate</a> theme, adopting the new <i>ti</i> name.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to the large hotels, casinos and resorts, the Strip is home to many attractions, such as <a href="/wiki/M%26M%27s_World" title="M&amp;M&#39;s World">M&amp;M's World</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adventuredome" title="Adventuredome">Adventuredome</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Fashion_Show_Mall" title="Fashion Show Mall">Fashion Show Mall</a>. Starting in the mid-1990s, the Strip became a popular New Year's Eve celebration destination.
</p>
<h3><span id="Recent_years_.282000.E2.80.93present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_years_(2000–present)">Recent years (2000–present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Vegas_Strip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recent years (2000–present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>With the opening of <a href="/wiki/Bellagio_(resort_and_casino)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bellagio (resort and casino)">Bellagio</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Venetian,_Las_Vegas" class="mw-redirect" title="The Venetian, Las Vegas">Venetian</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Palazzo" title="The Palazzo">Palazzo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wynn_Las_Vegas" title="Wynn Las Vegas">Wynn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Encore_Las_Vegas" title="Encore Las Vegas">Encore</a> resorts, the strip trended towards the luxurious high end segment through most of the 2000s, while some older resorts added major expansions and renovations, including some de-theming of the earlier themed hotels. High end dining, specialty retail, spas and nightclubs increasingly became options for visitors in addition to gambling at most Strip resorts. There was also a trend towards expensive residential condo units on the strip.
</p><p>In 2004, <a href="/wiki/MGM_Mirage" class="mw-redirect" title="MGM Mirage">MGM Mirage</a> announced plans for <a href="/wiki/CityCenter" title="CityCenter">CityCenter</a>, a 66-acre (27&#160;ha), $7&#160;billion multi-use project on the site of the <a href="/wiki/Boardwalk_Hotel_and_Casino" title="Boardwalk Hotel and Casino">Boardwalk</a> hotel and adjoining land. It consists of hotel, casino, condo, retail, art, business and other uses on the site. City Center is currently the largest such complex in the world. Construction began in April 2006, with most elements of the project opened in late 2009. Also in 2006, the Las Vegas Strip lost its longtime status as the world's highest-grossing gambling center, falling to second place behind <a href="/wiki/Macau" title="Macau">Macau</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, the <a href="/wiki/High_Roller_(Ferris_wheel)" title="High Roller (Ferris wheel)">High Roller Ferris wheel</a> and a retail district called <a href="/wiki/The_Linq#The_Linq_Promenade" title="The Linq">The LINQ Promenade</a> broke ground in an attempt to diversify attractions beyond that of casino resorts. Renovations and rebrandings such as <a href="/wiki/The_Cromwell_Las_Vegas" title="The Cromwell Las Vegas">The Cromwell Las Vegas</a> and the <a href="/wiki/SLS_Las_Vegas" class="mw-redirect" title="SLS Las Vegas">SLS Las Vegas</a> continued to transform the Strip in 2014. The <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Festival_Grounds" title="Las Vegas Festival Grounds">Las Vegas Festival Grounds</a> opened in 2015. In 2016, the <a href="/wiki/T-Mobile_Arena" title="T-Mobile Arena">T-Mobile Arena</a>, The Park, and the Park Theatre opened.
</p><p>On October 1, 2017, <a href="/wiki/2017_Las_Vegas_shooting" title="2017 Las Vegas shooting">a mass shooting</a> occurred on the Strip at the <a href="/wiki/Route_91_Harvest" class="mw-redirect" title="Route 91 Harvest">Route 91 Harvest</a> country music festival, adjacent to the <a href="/wiki/Mandalay_Bay" title="Mandalay Bay">Mandalay Bay</a> hotel. 58 people were killed and 851 were injured. This incident became the deadliest mass shooting in modern United States history.<sup id="cite_ref-TheGuardian_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TheGuardian-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, the Monte Carlo Resort and Casino finished a makeover into the <a href="/wiki/Park_MGM" title="Park MGM">Park MGM</a>, while the SLS transitioned back to its Sahara name.
</p>

<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Stratosphere_Las_Vegas" title="Stratosphere Las Vegas">Stratosphere</a> is to be rebranded as <a href="/wiki/The_STRAT_Hotel,_Casino_and_Skypod" class="mw-redirect" title="The STRAT Hotel, Casino and Skypod">The STRAT Hotel, Casino and Skypod</a>, with upgrades to public spaces scheduled for early 2020. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Vegas_Strip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/RTC_Transit" title="RTC Transit">RTC Transit</a> (previously Citizens Area Transit, or CAT) provides bus service on the Strip with <a href="/wiki/Double_decker_bus" class="mw-redirect" title="Double decker bus">double decker buses</a> known as <a href="/wiki/The_Deuce_(transit_bus_service)" title="The Deuce (transit bus service)">The Deuce</a>. The Deuce runs between Mandalay Bay at the southern end of the Strip (and to the Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas sign and South Strip Transfer Terminal after midnight) to the Bonneville Transit Center (BTC) and the Fremont Street Experience in <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Las_Vegas" title="Downtown Las Vegas">Downtown Las Vegas</a>, with stops near every casino. RTC also operates an express bus called the Strip and Downtown Express (SDX). This route connects the Strip to the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Convention_Center" title="Las Vegas Convention Center">Las Vegas Convention Center</a> and <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Las_Vegas" title="Downtown Las Vegas">Downtown Las Vegas</a> to the north, with stops at selected hotels and shopping attractions (Las Vegas Premium Outlets North &amp; South).

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pedestrian_traffic">Pedestrian traffic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Vegas_Strip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Pedestrian traffic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Concerning pedestrian safety and to help alleviate traffic congestion at popular intersections, several pedestrian footbridges were erected in 1990s. Some feature designs that match the theme of the nearby resorts. The <a href="/wiki/Tropicana_%E2%80%93_Las_Vegas_Boulevard_intersection" title="Tropicana – Las Vegas Boulevard intersection">Tropicana – Las Vegas Boulevard</a>
footbridges were the first to be installed, and based on the success of this project additional footbridges have been built on Las Vegas Boulevard at the Flamingo Road intersection connecting <a href="/wiki/Bellagio_(casino)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bellagio (casino)">Bellagio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caesars_Palace" title="Caesars Palace">Caesars Palace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bally%27s" class="mw-redirect" title="Bally&#39;s">Bally's</a>, and <a href="/wiki/The_Cromwell" class="mw-redirect" title="The Cromwell">The Cromwell</a>; between <a href="/wiki/The_Mirage" title="The Mirage">The Mirage</a>/<a href="/wiki/Treasure_Island_Hotel_and_Casino" title="Treasure Island Hotel and Casino">Treasure Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Venetian_(Las_Vegas)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Venetian (Las Vegas)">The Venetian</a>, and at the Las Vegas Boulevard-Spring Mountain and Sands Avenue intersection connecting the <a href="/wiki/Wynn_Las_Vegas" title="Wynn Las Vegas">Wynn</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Fashion_Show_Mall" title="Fashion Show Mall">Fashion Show Mall</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Palazzo" title="The Palazzo">The Palazzo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Treasure_Island" title="Treasure Island">Treasure Island</a>. The latest to be completed connects <a href="/wiki/Planet_Hollywood_Resort_and_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="Planet Hollywood Resort and Casino">Planet Hollywood</a>, <a href="/wiki/CityCenter" title="CityCenter">CityCenter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_of_Las_Vegas" title="Cosmopolitan of Las Vegas">The Cosmopolitan</a> at the Harmon Avenue intersection.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the Las Vegas Convention and Visitors Authority's annual Las Vegas Visitor Profile Study, only 36% of people said they walked around the Strip, a figure that is a drop from 2013 (52%).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Golf">Golf</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Vegas_Strip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Golf">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Las_Vegas,_NV_(The_Wynn,_golf).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Las_Vegas%2C_NV_%28The_Wynn%2C_golf%29.JPG/220px-Las_Vegas%2C_NV_%28The_Wynn%2C_golf%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="141" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Las_Vegas%2C_NV_%28The_Wynn%2C_golf%29.JPG/330px-Las_Vegas%2C_NV_%28The_Wynn%2C_golf%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Las_Vegas%2C_NV_%28The_Wynn%2C_golf%29.JPG/440px-Las_Vegas%2C_NV_%28The_Wynn%2C_golf%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2017" data-file-height="1289" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Las_Vegas,_NV_(The_Wynn,_golf).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Wynn Golf and Country Club</div></div></div>
<p>In 2000, Bali Hai Golf Club opened just south of <a href="/wiki/Mandalay_Bay" title="Mandalay Bay">Mandalay Bay</a> and the Strip.<sup id="cite_ref-golfclub_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-golfclub-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As land values on the Strip have increased over the years, the resort-affiliated golf courses been removed to make way for building projects. The <a href="/wiki/Tropicana_Country_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropicana Country Club">Tropicana Country Club</a> closed in 1990<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Dunes_(hotel_and_casino)" title="Dunes (hotel and casino)">Dunes</a> golf course in the mid-90s. <a href="/wiki/Steve_Wynn" title="Steve Wynn">Steve Wynn</a>, founder of previously owned <a href="/wiki/Mirage_Resorts" title="Mirage Resorts">Mirage Resorts</a>, purchased the <a href="/wiki/Desert_Inn" title="Desert Inn">Desert Inn</a> and golf course for his new company <a href="/wiki/Wynn_Resorts_Limited" class="mw-redirect" title="Wynn Resorts Limited">Wynn Resorts</a> and redeveloped the course as the Wynn Golf Club. This course closed in 2017, but the development planned for the course was cancelled and the course will be renovated and re-opened in late 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Aladdin_(hotel_and_casino)" title="Aladdin (hotel and casino)">Aladdin</a> also had a nine-hole golf course in the 1960s.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2016, a <a href="/wiki/TopGolf" class="mw-redirect" title="TopGolf">TopGolf</a> opened near the Strip.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/The_Shoppes_at_the_Palazzo" class="mw-redirect" title="The Shoppes at the Palazzo">The Shoppes at the Palazzo</a> feature luxury stores including the only <a href="/wiki/Barneys_New_York" title="Barneys New York">Barneys New York</a> department store in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fashion_Show_Mall" title="Fashion Show Mall">Fashion Show Mall</a> is adjacent to <a href="/wiki/Treasure_Island_Hotel_and_Casino" title="Treasure Island Hotel and Casino">Treasure Island</a> and opposite <a href="/wiki/Wynn_Las_Vegas" title="Wynn Las Vegas">Wynn Las Vegas</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Grand_Canal_Shoppes" title="Grand Canal Shoppes">Grand Canal Shoppes</a> is a luxury mall connected to <a href="/wiki/The_Venetian_Las_Vegas" title="The Venetian Las Vegas">The Venetian</a> with canals, gondolas and singing gondoliers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_Linq#The_Linq_Promenade" title="The Linq">The LINQ Promenade</a> is an open-air retail, dining, and entertainment district located between <a href="/wiki/The_Linq" title="The Linq">The Linq</a> and <a href="/wiki/Flamingo_Las_Vegas" title="Flamingo Las Vegas">Flamingo</a> resorts that began a soft open in January 2014. It leads from a Strip-side entrance to the <a href="/wiki/High_Roller_(Ferris_wheel)" title="High Roller (Ferris wheel)">High Roller</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Miracle_Mile_Shops" title="Miracle Mile Shops">Miracle Mile Shops</a> is part of the <a href="/wiki/Planet_Hollywood_Resort_and_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="Planet Hollywood Resort and Casino">Planet Hollywood</a> hotel.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_Forum_Shops_at_Caesars" title="The Forum Shops at Caesars">The Forum Shops at Caesars</a> is a luxury mall connected to <a href="/wiki/Caesars_Palace" title="Caesars Palace">Caesars Palace</a>, with more than 160 shops and 11 restaurants.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/The_Crystals_(Las_Vegas)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Crystals (Las Vegas)">Crystals at CityCenter</a> is a luxury high-fashion mall at <a href="/wiki/CityCenter" title="CityCenter">CityCenter</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Harmon_Corner" title="Harmon Corner">Harmon Corner</a> is a three-story retail center located next to <a href="/wiki/Planet_Hollywood" title="Planet Hollywood">Planet Hollywood</a> with shops and restaurants.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Showcase_Mall" title="Showcase Mall">Showcase Mall</a> is next to <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Las_Vegas" title="MGM Grand Las Vegas">MGM Grand</a>, and displays a 100-foot <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> bottle.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The Park, a short east-west street between the <a href="/wiki/Park_MGM" title="Park MGM">Park MGM</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_York-New_York_Hotel_%26_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="New York-New York Hotel &amp; Casino">New York-New York</a> resorts is a park-like boulevard lined with retail shops and restaurants, leading to <a href="/wiki/T-Mobile_Arena" title="T-Mobile Arena">T-Mobile Arena</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entertainment">Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Vegas_Strip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nevada_National_Guard_patrols_Las_Vegas_Strip_on_New_Years_(2017).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Nevada_National_Guard_patrols_Las_Vegas_Strip_on_New_Years_%282017%29.jpg/220px-Nevada_National_Guard_patrols_Las_Vegas_Strip_on_New_Years_%282017%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Nevada_National_Guard_patrols_Las_Vegas_Strip_on_New_Years_%282017%29.jpg/330px-Nevada_National_Guard_patrols_Las_Vegas_Strip_on_New_Years_%282017%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Nevada_National_Guard_patrols_Las_Vegas_Strip_on_New_Years_%282017%29.jpg/440px-Nevada_National_Guard_patrols_Las_Vegas_Strip_on_New_Years_%282017%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3324" data-file-height="2195" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nevada_National_Guard_patrols_Las_Vegas_Strip_on_New_Years_(2017).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nevada_National_Guard" title="Nevada National Guard">Nevada National Guard</a> assist with New Year's Eve security</div></div></div>
<p>The Las Vegas Strip is well known for its lounges, showrooms, theaters and nightclubs;<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> most of the attractions and shows on the Strip are located on the hotel casino properties. Some of the more popular free attractions visible from the Strip include the water fountains at  <a href="/wiki/Bellagio_(resort)" title="Bellagio (resort)">Bellagio</a>, the volcano at <a href="/wiki/The_Mirage" title="The Mirage">The Mirage</a>, and the Fall of Atlantis and Festival Fountain at <a href="/wiki/Caesars_Palace" title="Caesars Palace">Caesars Palace</a>. There are several <a href="/wiki/Cirque_du_Soleil" title="Cirque du Soleil">Cirque du Soleil</a> shows, such as <i><a href="/wiki/K%C3%A0" title="Kà">Kà</a></i> at the MGM Grand, <i><a href="/wiki/O_(Cirque_du_Soleil)" title="O (Cirque du Soleil)">O</a></i> at Bellagio, <i><a href="/wiki/Myst%C3%A8re_(Cirque_du_Soleil)" title="Mystère (Cirque du Soleil)">Mystère</a></i> at Treasure Island, <i><a href="/wiki/Zumanity" class="mw-redirect" title="Zumanity">Zumanity</a></i> for adults at <a href="/wiki/New_York-New_York_Hotel_and_Casino" title="New York-New York Hotel and Casino">New York-New York</a>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson:_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Michael Jackson: One">Michael Jackson: One</a></i> at <a href="/wiki/Mandalay_Bay" title="Mandalay Bay">Mandalay Bay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many notable artists have performed in Las Vegas, including <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frank_Sinatra" title="Frank Sinatra">Frank Sinatra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Judy_Garland" title="Judy Garland">Judy Garland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Newton" title="Wayne Newton">Wayne Newton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liza_Minnelli" title="Liza Minnelli">Liza Minnelli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dean_Martin" title="Dean Martin">Dean Martin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sammy_Davis_Jr." title="Sammy Davis Jr.">Sammy Davis Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liberace" title="Liberace">Liberace</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-lasvegasweekly_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lasvegasweekly-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> and in more recent years <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barry_Manilow" title="Barry Manilow">Barry Manilow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cher" title="Cher">Cher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bette_Midler" title="Bette Midler">Bette Midler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Donny_and_Marie_Osmond" class="mw-redirect" title="Donny and Marie Osmond">Donny and Marie Osmond</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garth_Brooks" title="Garth Brooks">Garth Brooks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reba_McEntire" title="Reba McEntire">Reba McEntire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain" title="Shania Twain">Shania Twain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Criss_Angel" title="Criss Angel">Criss Angel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olivia_Newton-John" title="Olivia Newton-John">Olivia Newton-John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_%2B_Adam_Lambert" title="Queen + Adam Lambert">Queen + Adam Lambert</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a> have had residencies in the various resorts on the Strip. The only movie theatre directly on the Strip was the 10-screen Regal Showcase Theatre in the Showcase Mall. The theater opened in 1997 and was operated by <a href="/wiki/Regal_Entertainment_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Regal Entertainment Group">Regal Entertainment Group</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> until its closure in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demolished_or_closed_Strip_casinos_and_hotels">Demolished or closed Strip casinos and hotels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Las_Vegas_Strip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Demolished or closed Strip casinos and hotels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Aladdin_(hotel_and_casino)" title="Aladdin (hotel and casino)">Aladdin</a>: Opened in 1962 as the Tallyho, became the King's Crown Tallyho in 1963, the Aladdin in 1966, and was demolished in 1998. A new Aladdin resort opened on the property in 2000, and was renamed <a href="/wiki/Planet_Hollywood_Resort_%26_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="Planet Hollywood Resort &amp; Casino">Planet Hollywood</a> in 2007.</li>
<li>Big Red's Casino: Opened in 1981 and closed in 1982. Property developed for CBS Sports World Casino in 1997. Changed name to Sports World Casino after <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a> threatened to sue.<sup id="cite_ref-cbs_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbs-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Closed in 2001, now a shopping center.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Barbary_Coast_Hotel_and_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbary Coast Hotel and Casino">Barbary Coast Hotel and Casino</a>: Closed in 2007, now <a href="/wiki/The_Cromwell" class="mw-redirect" title="The Cromwell">The Cromwell</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Boardwalk_Hotel_and_Casino" title="Boardwalk Hotel and Casino">Boardwalk Hotel and Casino</a>: Closed on January 6, 2006, demolished May 9, 2006 to make way for <a href="/wiki/CityCenter" title="CityCenter">CityCenter</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Castaways_(casino)" title="Castaways (casino)">Castaways Hotel and Casino</a>: Opened in 1957 as the San Souci Hotel and became the Castaways in 1963 and was demolished in 1987. Now <a href="/wiki/The_Mirage" title="The Mirage">The Mirage</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Desert_Inn" title="Desert Inn">Desert Inn</a>: Closed on August 28, 2000, demolished in 2004, now <a href="/wiki/Wynn_Las_Vegas" title="Wynn Las Vegas">Wynn Las Vegas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Encore_Las_Vegas" title="Encore Las Vegas">Encore Las Vegas</a>; Desert Inn golf course was retained and improved.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dunes_(hotel_and_casino)" title="Dunes (hotel and casino)">Dunes Hotel and Casino</a>: Closed on January 26, 1993, demolished in 1993, now <a href="/wiki/Bellagio_(resort_and_casino)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bellagio (resort and casino)">Bellagio</a>. The Dunes golf course is now occupied by parts of <a href="/wiki/Park_MGM" title="Park MGM">Park MGM</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York-New_York_Hotel_and_Casino" title="New York-New York Hotel and Casino">New York-New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/CityCenter" title="CityCenter">CityCenter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_of_Las_Vegas" title="Cosmopolitan of Las Vegas">Cosmopolitan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/T-Mobile_Arena" title="T-Mobile Arena">T-Mobile Arena</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/El_Rancho_Hotel_and_Casino" title="El Rancho Hotel and Casino">El Rancho (formerly Thunderbird/Silverbird)</a>: Closed in 1992 and demolished in 2000. Now the unfinished <a href="/wiki/The_Drew_Las_Vegas" title="The Drew Las Vegas">The Drew Las Vegas</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/El_Rancho_Vegas" title="El Rancho Vegas">El Rancho Vegas</a>: Burned down in 1960. The <a href="/wiki/Hilton_Grand_Vacations_Club" title="Hilton Grand Vacations Club">Hilton Grand Vacations Club</a> timeshare now exists on the south edge of the site where the resort once stood; the remainder is now the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Festival_Grounds" title="Las Vegas Festival Grounds">Las Vegas Festival Grounds</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hacienda_(resort)" title="Hacienda (resort)">Hacienda</a>: Closed and demolished in 1996, now <a href="/wiki/Mandalay_Bay" title="Mandalay Bay">Mandalay Bay</a>. Until 2015, a separate <a href="/wiki/Hoover_Dam_Lodge" title="Hoover Dam Lodge">Hacienda</a> operated outside <a href="/wiki/Boulder_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Boulder City">Boulder City</a>, formerly the <a href="/wiki/Hoover_Dam_Lodge" title="Hoover Dam Lodge">Gold Strike Inn</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Holy_Cow_Casino_and_Brewery" title="Holy Cow Casino and Brewery">Holy Cow Casino and Brewery</a>: First micro brewery in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a>. Closed in 2002, now a <a href="/wiki/Walgreens" title="Walgreens">Walgreens</a> store.</li>
<li>Jackpot Casino: Closed in 1977, now part of <a href="/wiki/Bonanza_Gift_Shop" title="Bonanza Gift Shop">Bonanza Gift Shop</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Klondike_Hotel_and_Casino" title="Klondike Hotel and Casino">Klondike Hotel and Casino</a>: Closed in 2006, demolished in 2008.</li>
<li>Little Caesars Casino: Opened in 1970 and closed in 1994. <a href="/wiki/Paris_Las_Vegas" title="Paris Las Vegas">Paris Las Vegas</a> now occupies the area.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Money Tree Casino: Closed in 1979, now <a href="/wiki/Bonanza_Gift_Shop" title="Bonanza Gift Shop">Bonanza Gift Shop</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Marina_Hotel_and_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="Marina Hotel and Casino">Marina Hotel and Casino</a>: Closed, adapted into MGM Grand, now the West Wing of the <a href="/wiki/MGM_Grand_Las_Vegas" title="MGM Grand Las Vegas">MGM Grand</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/New_Frontier_Hotel_and_Casino" title="New Frontier Hotel and Casino">New Frontier</a>: Closed July 16, 2007, demolished November 13, 2007. Currently being redeveloped as <a href="/wiki/Wynn_West" class="mw-redirect" title="Wynn West">Wynn West</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Casino_Royale_Hotel_%26_Casino" title="Casino Royale Hotel &amp; Casino">Nob Hill Casino</a>: Opened in 1979 and closed in 1990. Now <a href="/wiki/Casino_Royale_Hotel_%26_Casino" title="Casino Royale Hotel &amp; Casino">Casino Royale</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Riviera_(hotel_and_casino)" title="Riviera (hotel and casino)">Riviera Hotel and Casino</a>: Opened in 1955; Closed in May 2015 to make way for the <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Global_Business_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Las Vegas Global Business District">Las Vegas Global Business District</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sands_Hotel_and_Casino" title="Sands Hotel and Casino">Sands Hotel and Casino</a>: Closed on June 30, 1996, demolished in 1996, now <a href="/wiki/The_Venetian_Las_Vegas" title="The Venetian Las Vegas">The Venetian</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Silver_City_Casino" title="Silver City Casino">Silver City Casino</a>: Closed in 1999, now the Silver City Plaza Shopping Center.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Silver_Slipper" title="Silver Slipper">Silver Slipper</a> Casino: Opened in 1950 and closed and demolished in 1988. It became the parking lot for the <a href="/wiki/New_Frontier_Hotel_and_Casino" title="New Frontier Hotel and Casino">New Frontier</a> until its closure and demolition in 2007.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Stardust_Resort_%26_Casino" class="mw-redirect" title="Stardust Resort &amp; Casino">Stardust Resort &amp; Casino</a>: Closed on November 1, 2006, demolished on March 13, 2007. Currently being redeveloped as <a href="/wiki/Resorts_World_Las_Vegas" title="Resorts World Las Vegas">Resorts World Las Vegas</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vegas_World" title="Vegas World">Vegas World</a>: Opened in 1979 and closed in 1995. Now the <a href="/wiki/Stratosphere_Las_Vegas" title="Stratosphere Las Vegas">Stratosphere Las Vegas</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Westward_Ho_Hotel_and_Casino" title="Westward Ho Hotel and Casino">Westward Ho Hotel and Casino</a>: Closed in 2005, demolished in 2006.</li></ul>
