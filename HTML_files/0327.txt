<p><b>Vietnamese personal names</b> generally consist of three parts: one <a href="/wiki/Patrilineal" class="mw-redirect" title="Patrilineal">patrilineal</a> <a href="/wiki/Family_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Family name">family name</a>, one or more <a href="/wiki/Middle_name" title="Middle name">middle name</a>(s) (one of which may be taken from the mother's family name), and one <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a>, used in that order. The "family name first" order follows the system of <a href="/wiki/Chinese_name" title="Chinese name">Chinese names</a> and is common throughout the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_cultural_sphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Chinese cultural sphere">Chinese cultural sphere</a>. However, it is different from Chinese, <a href="/wiki/Korean_name" title="Korean name">Korean</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Japanese_name" title="Japanese name">Japanese names</a> in the usage of "middle names", as they are less common in China and Korea and do not exist in Japan.  Persons can be referred to by the whole name, the given name or <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_pronouns#Kinship_terms" title="Vietnamese pronouns">a hierarchic pronoun</a>, which usually connotes a degree of family relationship or kinship, in normal usage.
</p><p>Due to the frequency of the major family names such as <a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n" class="mw-redirect" title="Nguyễn">Nguyễn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BA%A7n" title="Trần">Trần</a>, and <a href="/wiki/L%C3%AA" class="mw-redirect" title="Lê">Lê</a>, persons are often referred to by their middle name along with their given name in Vietnamese media and youth culture.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_language" title="Vietnamese language">Vietnamese language</a> is tonal, and so are Vietnamese names. Names with the same spelling (ignoring diacritics) but with different tones are different names, which can confuse non-Vietnamese people when the diacritics are dropped, as is commonly done outside Vietnam.
</p><p>Anyone applying for Vietnamese nationality must adopt a Vietnamese name.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_name">Family name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnamese_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The family name is positioned first and is passed on by the father to his children. It is estimated that there are around 100 family names in common use, but some are far more common than others. The name <i><a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n" class="mw-redirect" title="Nguyễn">Nguyễn</a></i> is estimated to be used by almost 40% of the Vietnamese population.  The top three names are so popular because people tended to take family names of emperors to show their loyalty. Over many generations, family names became permanent.
</p><p>The most common family names among the Vietnamese are the following (the Chinese characters following each name are <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A1n_t%E1%BB%B1" class="mw-redirect" title="Hán tự">Hán tự</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Altogether, the 14 names account for 90% of the people.

<p>In Vietnamese cultural practice, women always keep their family names once they marry, just as in other <a href="/wiki/East_Asian" class="mw-redirect" title="East Asian">East Asian</a> cultures, including <a href="/wiki/Chinese_culture" title="Chinese culture">Chinese culture</a> to the north and the northeast.
</p><p>In formal contexts, people are referred to by their full name. In more casual contexts, people are always on a "first name basis", which involves their given names, accompanied by proper <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_pronouns#Kinship_terms" title="Vietnamese pronouns">kinship terms</a>. There is no such thing as a "last name basis", or family name basis, in Vietnam.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_name">Middle name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnamese_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Middle name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Unreferenced_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vietnamese_name&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">April 2018</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Most Vietnamese have one middle name, but it is quite possible to have two or more of them or to have no middle name at all.
</p><p>In the past, the middle name was selected by parents from a fairly narrow range of options. Almost all women had <i>Thị</i> (<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r867716940">.mw-parser-output .vi-nom{font-family:"Han-Nom Gothic","Nom Na Tong","Han-Nom Ming","Han-Nom Minh","HAN NOM A","HAN NOM B","Han-Nom Kai","Sun-ExtA","Sun-ExtB","Ming-Lt-HKSCS-UNI-H","Ming-Lt-HKSCS-ExtB","HanaMinA","HanaMinB","HanaMin","MingLiU","MingLiU-ExtB","MingLiU HKSCS","MingLiU HKSCS-ExtB","SimSun","SimSun-ExtB","FZKaiT-Extended","FZKaiT-Extended(SIP)",sans-serif}.mw-parser-output .vi-nom .ext{font-family:"Han-Nom Gothic","Han-Nom Ming","Han-Nom Minh","BabelStone Han","Sun-ExtB","MingLiU HKSCS-ExtB","Ming-Lt-HKSCS-ExtB","HanaMinB","Han-Nom Kai",sans-serif}</style><span lang="vi-Hani" title="Vietnamese language text"><span class="vi-nom">氏</span></span>) as their middle name, and many men had <i>Văn</i> (<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r867716940"/><span lang="vi-Hani" title="Vietnamese language text"><span class="vi-nom">文</span></span>). More recently, a broader range of names has been used, and people named <i>Thị</i> sometimes omit their middle name.
</p><p><i>Thị</i> is by far the most common female middle name. That word expresses possession. For example, "Trần Thị Mai Loan" is a person who has the given name of "Mai Loan" and the surname "Trần", and the combination "Trần Thị" means "a female person belonging to the Trần family." The combination is similar to Western surname formation like "Van" in "Van Helsing", "Mac" in "MacCartney", etc. Male middle names include <i>Văn</i> (<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r867716940"/><span lang="vi-Hani" title="Vietnamese language text"><span class="vi-nom">文</span></span>), <i>Hữu</i> (<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r867716940"/><span lang="vi-Hani" title="Vietnamese language text"><span class="vi-nom">友</span></span>), <i>Đức</i> (<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r867716940"/><span lang="vi-Hani" title="Vietnamese language text"><span class="vi-nom">德</span></span>), <i>Thành</i> (<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r867716940"/><span lang="vi-Hani" title="Vietnamese language text"><span class="vi-nom">誠</span></span>), <i>Công</i> (<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r867716940"/><span lang="vi-Hani" title="Vietnamese language text"><span class="vi-nom">公</span></span>), and <i>Quang</i> (<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r867716940"/><span lang="vi-Hani" title="Vietnamese language text"><span class="vi-nom">光</span></span>).
</p><p>The middle name can have three uses:
</p>
<ol><li>To indicate a person's generation. Brothers and sisters share the same middle name, which distinguish them from the generation before them and the generation after them (see <a href="/wiki/Generation_name" title="Generation name">generation name</a>).</li>
<li>To separate branches of a large family: "Nguyễn <i>Hữu</i>", "Nguyễn <i>Sinh</i>", "Trần <i>Lâm</i>" (middle names can be taken from the mother's family name). However, this usage is still controversial<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Accuracy_dispute#Disputed_statement" title="Wikipedia:Accuracy dispute"><span title="The material near this tag is possibly inaccurate or nonfactual. (July 2018)">dubious</span></a>&#32;<span class="metadata"> &#8211; <a href="/wiki/Talk:Vietnamese_name#Dubious" title="Talk:Vietnamese name">discuss</a></span></i>&#93;</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. Some people<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (July 2018)">who?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> consider them to be dual family names, not family name + middle name. Some families may, however, set up arbitrary rules about giving a different middle name to each generation.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (July 2018)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li>
<li>To indicate a person's position (<a href="/wiki/Birth_order" title="Birth order">birth order</a>) in the family. This usage is less common than others.</li></ol>
<p>However, most middle names now do not have those uses. They can have a meaning or only make the full name sound better.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Given_name">Given name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnamese_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Given name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In most cases, the middle name is formally part of the given name. For example, the name "Đinh Quang Dũng" is separated into the surname "Đinh" and the given name "Quang Dũng". In a normal name list, those two parts of the full name are put in two different columns. However, in daily conversation, the last word in a given name with a title before it is used to address a person: "Ông Dũng", "Anh Dũng", etc., with "Ông" and "Anh" being words to address the person and depend on age, social position, etc.
</p><p>The given name is the primary form of address for Vietnamese. It is chosen by parents and usually has a literal meaning in the <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_language" title="Vietnamese language">Vietnamese language</a>. Names often represent beauty, such as bird or flower names, or attributes and characteristics that the parents want in their child, such as modesty (<i>Khiêm</i>, 謙).
</p><p>Typically, Vietnamese will be addressed with their given name, even in formal situations, although an honorific equivalent to "Mr.", "Mrs.", etc. will be added when necessary. That contrasts with the situation in many other cultures in which the family name is used in formal situations, but it is a practice similar to usage in <a href="/wiki/Icelandic_name" title="Icelandic name">Icelandic</a> usage and, to some degree, <a href="/wiki/Polish_name" title="Polish name">Polish</a>. It is similar to the Latin-American and southern European custom of referring to women as "<a href="/wiki/Don_(honorific)" title="Don (honorific)">Doña</a>" and men as "<a href="/wiki/Don_(honorific)" title="Don (honorific)">Don</a>", along with their first name.
</p><p>Addressing someone by the family name is rare. In the past, married women in the north were called by their (maiden) family name, with <i>Thị</i> (氏) as a suffix. In recent years, doctors are more likely than any other social group to be addressed by their family name, but that form of reference is more common in the north than in the south. Some extremely famous people are sometimes referred to by their family names, such as <a href="/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh" title="Ho Chi Minh">Hồ Chí Minh</a> (<i>Bác Hồ</i>—"<i>Uncle Hồ</i><span style="padding-left:.15em;">"</span>) (however, his real surname is Nguyễn), <a href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BB%8Bnh_C%C3%B4ng_S%C6%A1n" title="Trịnh Công Sơn">Trịnh Công Sơn</a> (<i>nhạc Trịnh</i>—"<i>Trịnh music</i><span style="padding-left:.15em;">"</span>), and <a href="/wiki/H%E1%BB%93_Xu%C3%A2n_H%C6%B0%C6%A1ng" title="Hồ Xuân Hương">Hồ Xuân Hương</a> (<i>nữ sĩ họ Hồ</i>—"<i>the poetess with the family name Hồ</i><span style="padding-left:.15em;">"</span>). Traditionally, people in Vietnam, particularly North Vietnam, addressed parents using the first child's name: Mr and Mrs Anh or Master Minh.
</p><p>When being addressed within the family, children are sometimes referred to by their birth number, starting with one in the north but two in the south. That practice is less common recently, especially in the north.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Double_name" title="Double name">Double names</a> are common among females, especially in combination with the name <span lang="vi" style="font-style:normal;" title="Vietnamese language text"><a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Kim#Vietnamese" class="extiw" title="wikt:Kim">Kim</a></span>. For example, <span lang="vi" style="font-style:normal;" title="Vietnamese language text"><a href="/wiki/Phan_Th%E1%BB%8B_Kim_Ph%C3%BAc" class="mw-redirect" title="Phan Thị Kim Phúc">Phan Thị Kim Phúc</a></span> has the given name <span lang="vi" style="font-style:normal;" title="Vietnamese language text">Kim Phúc</span>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">Examples</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnamese_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Examples">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_T%E1%BA%A5n_D%C5%A9ng" title="Nguyễn Tấn Dũng">Nguyễn Tấn Dũng</a> is the former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Vietnam" title="Prime Minister of Vietnam">Prime Minister of Vietnam</a>. <i>Nguyễn</i> is his family name, <i>Tấn</i> is his middle name, and <i>Dũng</i> is his given name. In formal usage, he is referred to by his given name ("Mr. Dũng"), not by his family name ("Mr. Nguyễn").</li>
<li>Likewise, the famous general and military leader, <a href="/wiki/V%C3%B5_Nguy%C3%AAn_Gi%C3%A1p" title="Võ Nguyên Giáp">Võ Nguyên Giáp</a>, is referred to by his given name, i.e. "General Giáp".</li></ul>
<h2><span id="Saint.27s_name"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Saint's_name">Saint's name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnamese_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Saint&#039;s name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church_in_Vietnam" title="Catholic Church in Vietnam">Vietnamese Catholics</a> are given a <a href="/wiki/Saint%27s_name" title="Saint&#39;s name">saint's name</a> at <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptism</a> (<a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_language" title="Vietnamese language">Vietnamese</a>: <i lang="vi">tên thánh</i> or <i lang="vi" title="Vietnamese language text">tên rửa tội</i>). Boys are given male saint's names, while girls are given female saint's names. This name appears first, before the family name, in formal religious contexts. Out of respect, clergy are usually referred to by saint's name. The saint's name also functions as a <a href="/wiki/Posthumous_name" title="Posthumous name">posthumous name</a>, used instead of an individual's given name in prayers after their death. The most common saint's names are taken from the <a href="/wiki/New_Testament" title="New Testament">New Testament</a>, such as <i lang="vi" title="Vietnamese language text">Phêrô</i> (Peter), <i lang="vi" title="Vietnamese language text">Phaolô</i> (Paul), <i lang="vi" title="Vietnamese language text">Gioan</i> (John), <i lang="vi" title="Vietnamese language text">Maria</i> (Mary), and <i lang="vi" title="Vietnamese language text">Anna</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Saint's names are respelled phonetically according to the <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_alphabet" title="Vietnamese alphabet">Vietnamese alphabet</a>. Some more well-known saint's names are derived further into names that sound more Vietnamese.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Near_homonyms_distinguished_by_vowel_or_tones">Near homonyms distinguished by vowel or tones</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnamese_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Near homonyms distinguished by vowel or tones">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some names may appear the same if simplified into a basic <a href="/wiki/ASCII" title="ASCII">ASCII</a> script, as for example on websites, but are different names:
</p>
<ul><li>Emperor <a href="/wiki/L%C3%AA_Hi%E1%BA%BFn_T%C3%B4ng" title="Lê Hiến Tông">Lê Hiến Tông</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sino-Vietnamese_vocabulary" title="Sino-Vietnamese vocabulary">Sino-Vietnamese</a>: 黎憲宗, 1461–1504), vs. emperor <a href="/wiki/L%C3%AA_Hi%E1%BB%83n_T%C3%B4ng" title="Lê Hiển Tông">Lê Hiển Tông</a> (黎顯宗, 1717–1786)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BB%8Bnh_C%C4%83n" title="Trịnh Căn">Trịnh Căn</a> (鄭根, 1633–1709) reformist warlord, vs. <a href="/wiki/Tr%E1%BB%8Bnh_C%C3%A1n" title="Trịnh Cán">Trịnh Cán</a> (鄭檊, 1777–1782) infant heir of warlord Trịnh Sâm</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_Du" title="Nguyễn Du">Nguyễn Du</a> (1765–1820) writer, vs. <a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_D%E1%BB%AF" title="Nguyễn Dữ">Nguyễn Dữ</a> (c.1550) writer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ho%C3%A0ng_T%C3%ADch_Chu" title="Hoàng Tích Chu">Hoàng Tích Chu</a> (1897–1933) journalist, vs.<a href="/wiki/Ho%C3%A0ng_T%C3%ADch_Ch%C3%B9" title="Hoàng Tích Chù">Hoàng Tích Chù</a> (1912–2003) painter</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_V%C4%83n_T%E1%BB%B5" title="Nguyễn Văn Tỵ">Nguyễn Văn Tỵ</a> (1917–1992) painter and poet, vs. <a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_V%C4%83n_T%C3%BD" title="Nguyễn Văn Tý">Nguyễn Văn Tý</a> (1925), composer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Phan_Thanh_H%C3%B9ng" title="Phan Thanh Hùng">Phan Thanh Hùng</a> (1960) football manager, vs. <a href="/wiki/Phan_Thanh_H%C6%B0ng" title="Phan Thanh Hưng">Phan Thanh Hưng</a> (1987), footballer</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_B%C3%ACnh" title="Nguyễn Bình">Nguyễn Bình</a> (1906–1951), vs. <a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_B%C3%ADnh" title="Nguyễn Bính">Nguyễn Bính</a> (1918–1966)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_V%C4%83n_H%C6%B0ng" title="Nguyễn Văn Hưng">Nguyễn Văn Hưng</a> (1958–) representative of the Vietnam National Assembly, vs. <a href="/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_V%C4%83n_H%C3%B9ng_(martial_artist)" title="Nguyễn Văn Hùng (martial artist)">Nguyễn Văn Hùng</a> (1980), martial artist,</li></ul>
<p>Typically, as in the above examples, it is middle or the last personal given name which varies, as almost any Sino-Vietnamese character may be used. The number of family names is limited.
</p><p>Further, some historical names may be written using different Chinese characters (<a href="/wiki/Sino-Vietnamese_vocabulary" title="Sino-Vietnamese vocabulary">Sino-Vietnamese</a>), but are still written the same in the modern Vietnamese alphabet.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Indexing">Indexing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vietnamese_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Indexing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the English-language <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Manual_of_Style" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago Manual of Style">Chicago Manual of Style</a></i>, Vietnamese names are <a href="/wiki/Subject_indexing" title="Subject indexing">indexed</a> according to the final given name and not according to the family name, with a cross-reference placed in regards to the family name. <a href="/wiki/Ng%C3%B4_%C4%90%C3%ACnh_Di%E1%BB%87m" class="mw-redirect" title="Ngô Đình Diệm">Ngô Đình Diệm</a> would be listed as "Diem, Ngô Dinh" and <a href="/wiki/V%C3%B5_Nguy%C3%AAn_Gi%C3%A1p" title="Võ Nguyên Giáp">Võ Nguyên Giáp</a> would be listed as "Giáp, Võ Nguyên".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
