<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Romantic_affair">Romantic affair</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Affair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Romantic affair">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jean-Honor%C3%A9_Fragonard_-_The_Stolen_Kiss.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Jean-Honor%C3%A9_Fragonard_-_The_Stolen_Kiss.jpg/220px-Jean-Honor%C3%A9_Fragonard_-_The_Stolen_Kiss.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="180" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Jean-Honor%C3%A9_Fragonard_-_The_Stolen_Kiss.jpg/330px-Jean-Honor%C3%A9_Fragonard_-_The_Stolen_Kiss.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Jean-Honor%C3%A9_Fragonard_-_The_Stolen_Kiss.jpg/440px-Jean-Honor%C3%A9_Fragonard_-_The_Stolen_Kiss.jpg 2x" data-file-width="7909" data-file-height="6488" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jean-Honor%C3%A9_Fragonard_-_The_Stolen_Kiss.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i><a href="/wiki/The_Stolen_Kiss_(Fragonard)" title="The Stolen Kiss (Fragonard)">The Stolen Kiss</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Honor%C3%A9_Fragonard" title="Jean-Honoré Fragonard">Jean-Honoré Fragonard</a></div></div></div>
<p>A romantic affair, also called an affair of the heart, may refer to sexual liaisons among unwed or wedded parties, or to various forms of <a href="/wiki/Forms_of_nonmonogamy" class="mw-redirect" title="Forms of nonmonogamy">nonmonogamy</a>. Unlike a <a href="/wiki/Casual_relationship" class="mw-redirect" title="Casual relationship">casual relationship</a>, which is a physical and emotional relationship between two people who may <a href="/wiki/Having_sex" class="mw-redirect" title="Having sex">have sex</a> without expecting a more formal romantic relationship, an affair is by its nature romantic.
</p><p>The term "affair" may also describe part of an agreement within an <a href="/wiki/Open_marriage" title="Open marriage">open marriage or open relationship</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Swinging_(sexual_practice)" title="Swinging (sexual practice)">swinging</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dating" title="Dating">dating</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Polyamory" title="Polyamory">polyamory</a>, in which some forms of <a href="/wiki/Extramarital_sex" title="Extramarital sex">sex with one's non-primary partner(s)</a> are permitted and other forms are not. Participants in open relationships, including unmarried couples and polyamorous families, may consider sanctioned affairs the norm, but when a non-sanctioned affair occurs, it is described as <a href="/wiki/Infidelity" title="Infidelity">infidelity</a> and may be experienced as <a href="/wiki/Adultery" title="Adultery">adultery</a>, or a <a href="/wiki/Betrayal" title="Betrayal">betrayal</a> both of <a href="/wiki/Trust_(sociology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Trust (sociology)">trust</a> and <a href="/wiki/Integrity" title="Integrity">integrity</a>, even though to most people it would not be considered "illicit".
</p><p>When romantic affairs lack both overt and covert sexual behavior, yet exhibits intense or enduring emotional intimacy, it may also be referred to as an <a href="/wiki/Emotional_affair" title="Emotional affair">emotional affair</a>, <a href="/wiki/Platonic_love" title="Platonic love">platonic love</a>, or a <a href="/wiki/Romantic_friendship" title="Romantic friendship">romantic friendship</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Extramarital_affair"><span id="extramarital_affair"></span> Extramarital affair</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Affair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Extramarital affair">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Infidelity" title="Infidelity">Infidelity</a>, <a href="/wiki/Legitimacy_(family_law)" title="Legitimacy (family law)">Legitimacy (family law)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adultery" title="Adultery">Adultery</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Extramarital_sex" title="Extramarital sex">Extramarital sex</a></div>
<p>Extramarital affairs are relationships outside of <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">marriage</a> where an illicit <a href="/wiki/Romantic_love" class="mw-redirect" title="Romantic love">romantic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Human_sexuality" title="Human sexuality">sexual</a> relationship or a <a href="/wiki/Romantic_friendship" title="Romantic friendship">romantic friendship</a> or <a href="/wiki/Passion_(emotion)" title="Passion (emotion)">passionate</a> attachment occurs.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>An affair that continues in one form or another for years, even as one of the partners to that affair passes through marriage, divorce and remarriage, could be considered the primary relationship and the marriages secondary to it. Several people claim the reason of extra marital affair as their unsuccessful marriage and that both spouses failed to please each other. This may be serial polygamy or other <a href="/wiki/Forms_of_nonmonogamy" class="mw-redirect" title="Forms of nonmonogamy">forms of nonmonogamy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The ability to pursue serial and clandestine extramarital affairs while safeguarding other secrets and <a href="/wiki/Conflict_of_interest" title="Conflict of interest">conflict of interest</a> inherent in the practice, requires skill in <a href="/wiki/Deception" title="Deception">deception</a> and duplicitous <a href="/wiki/Negotiation_(process)" class="mw-redirect" title="Negotiation (process)">negotiation</a>. Even to hide one affair requires a degree of skill or malicious <a href="/wiki/Gaslighting" title="Gaslighting">gaslighting</a>. All these behaviors are more usually called <a href="/wiki/Lie" title="Lie">lying</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Deception can be defined as the "<a href="/wiki/Covert" class="mw-redirect" title="Covert">covert</a> manipulation of perception to alter thoughts, feelings, or beliefs". The presence of deception may indicate the degree to which the deceiver has breached fundamental conditions of <a href="/wiki/Fidelity" title="Fidelity">fidelity</a>, of reciprocal <a href="/wiki/Vulnerability" title="Vulnerability">vulnerability</a> and of <a href="/wiki/Transparency_(humanities)" class="mw-redirect" title="Transparency (humanities)">transparency</a>. Sometimes these are explicit or assumed pre-conditions of a committed <a href="/wiki/Intimacy" class="mw-redirect" title="Intimacy">intimate</a> relationship.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Individuals having affairs with married men or women can be prosecuted for adultery in some jurisdictions and can be sued by the jilted spouses in others,<sup id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> or named as 'co-respondent' in divorce proceedings. As of 2009, eight U.S.&#160;states permitted such <a href="/wiki/Alienation_of_affections" title="Alienation of affections">alienation of affections</a> lawsuits.<sup id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Online_affair">Online affair</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Affair&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Online affair">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The appearance of <a href="/wiki/Computer-mediated_communication" title="Computer-mediated communication">computer-mediated communication</a> introduces a new type of communication and consequently a new type of "affair". There are various kinds of computer-mediated communication that differ in some significant aspects: one-to-one or group communication formats, interrelating with anonymous or identified people, and communicating in synchronous or asynchronous formats.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Online affairs combine features of close and remote relationships. 
</p><p>Ben Ze'ef argues that an online affair is a unique kind of affair—termed "detached attachment", or just "detachment"—that includes opposing features whose presence in a face-to-face affair would be paradoxical. Like direct, face-to-face affairs, online affairs can be spontaneous and casual and show intensive personal involvement. However, online affairs can also be more of a planned discourse than spontaneous talk; like written letters, online messages can be stored and thus have permanent presence, which is absent from face-to-face affairs.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>People participating in online affairs may be strangers to each other in the sense that they have never actually met each other. However, they are also close to each other since they share intimate information. In online affairs, people try to enjoy the benefits of both close and remote affairs, while avoiding their flaws. People enjoy the highly valued products of close affairs while paying the low cost of remote affairs. As one woman wrote: 'He constantly told me that he can not provide me with what I would want and I would always respond with: "I'm not asking anything from you, but simply enjoy your company"'.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
