<p>The <b>Inter-American Commission on Human Rights</b> (the <b>IACHR</b> or, in the three other official languages&#160;&#8211;&#32; Spanish, French, and <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>&#160;&#8211;&#32; <b>CIDH</b>, <i>Comisión Interamericana de los Derechos Humanos</i>, <i>Commission Interaméricaine des Droits de l'Homme</i>, <i>Comissão Interamericana de Direitos Humanos</i>) is an autonomous organ of the <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_American_States" title="Organization of American States">Organization of American States</a> (OAS).
</p><p>Along with the <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Court_of_Human_Rights" title="Inter-American Court of Human Rights">Inter-American Court of Human Rights</a>, it is one of the bodies that comprise the inter-American system for the promotion and protection of human rights.
</p><p>The IACHR is a permanent body, with headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>, United States, and it meets in regular and special sessions several times a year to examine allegations of human rights violations in the hemisphere.
</p><p>Its <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a> duties stem from three documents:
</p>
<ul><li>the <a href="/wiki/OAS_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="OAS Charter">OAS Charter</a></li>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/American_Declaration_of_the_Rights_and_Duties_of_Man" title="American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man">American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man</a></li>
<li>the <a href="/wiki/American_Convention_on_Human_Rights" title="American Convention on Human Rights">American Convention on Human Rights</a></li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_the_Inter-American_human_rights_system">History of the Inter-American human rights system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History of the Inter-American human rights system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The inter-American system for the protection of human rights emerged with the adoption of the <a href="/wiki/American_Declaration_of_the_Rights_and_Duties_of_Man" title="American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man">American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man</a> in April 1948&#160;&#8211;&#32; the  first international human rights instrument of a general nature, predating the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Declaration_of_Human_Rights" title="Universal Declaration of Human Rights">Universal Declaration of Human Rights</a> by more than six months.
</p><p>The IACHR was created in 1959. It held its first meeting in 1960, and it conducted its first on-site visit to inspect the human rights situation in the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> in 1961.
</p><p>A major step in the development of the system was taken in 1965 when the Commission was expressly authorized to examine specific cases of human rights violations. Since that date the IACHR has received thousands of petitions and has processed in excess of 12,000 individual cases.
</p><p>In 1969, the guiding principles behind the American Declaration were taken, reshaped, and restated in the <a href="/wiki/American_Convention_on_Human_Rights" title="American Convention on Human Rights">American Convention on Human Rights</a>. The Convention defines the human rights that the states parties are required to respect and guarantee, and it also ordered the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Court_of_Human_Rights" title="Inter-American Court of Human Rights">Inter-American Court of Human Rights</a>. It is currently binding on 24 of the OAS's 35 member states.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Functions_of_the_Inter-American_Commission">Functions of the Inter-American Commission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Functions of the Inter-American Commission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The main task of the IACHR is to promote the observance and defense of human rights in the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>.
</p><p>In pursuit of this mandate it:
</p>
<ul><li>Receives, analyzes, and investigates individual petitions alleging violations of specific human rights protected by the American Convention on Human Rights.</li>
<li>Works to resolve petitions in a collaborative way that is amiable to both parties.</li>
<li>Monitors the general human rights situation in the OAS's member states and, when necessary, prepares and publishes country-specific human rights reports.</li>
<li>Conducts on-site visits to examine members' general human rights situation or to investigate specific cases.</li>
<li>Encourages public awareness about human rights and related issues throughout the <a href="/wiki/Western_Hemisphere" title="Western Hemisphere">hemisphere</a>.</li>
<li>Holds conferences, seminars, and meetings with governments, NGOs, academic institutions, etc. to inform and raise awareness about issues relating to the inter-American human rights system.</li>
<li>Issues member states with recommendations that, if adopted, would further the cause of human rights protection.</li>
<li>Requests that states adopt <a href="/w/index.php?title=Precautionary_measures&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Precautionary measures (page does not exist)">precautionary measures</a> to prevent serious and irreparable harm to human rights in urgent cases.<sup id="cite_ref-IACHR_precautionary_measures-2012-06_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IACHR_precautionary_measures-2012-06-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Refers cases to the Inter-American Court of Human Rights, and litigates those same cases before the Court.</li>
<li>Asks the Inter-American Court to provide advisory opinions on matters relating to the interpretation of the Convention or other related instruments.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rapporteurships_and_units">Rapporteurships and units</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rapporteurships and units">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The IACHR has created several rapporteurships, a special rapporteurship and a unit to monitor OAS states' compliance with inter-American human rights treaties in the following areas:
</p>
<ul><li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cidh.org/Migrantes/defaultmigrants.htm">Rapporteurship on Migrant Workers and their Families</a></li>
<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cidh.org/women/Default.eng.htm">Rapporteurship on the Rights of Women</a> (the first rapporteurship created by the IACHR in 1994)</li>
<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cidh.org/Ninez/default_eng.htm">Rapporteurship on the Rights of the Child</a></li>
<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.oas.org/en/iachr/indigenous/default.asp">Rapporteurship on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples</a></li>
<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.oas.org/en/iachr/pdl/default.asp">Rapporteurship on the Rights of Persons Deprived of Liberty</a></li>
<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.oas.org/en/iachr/afro-descendants/default.asp">Rapporteurship on the Rights of Afro-Descendants and against Racial Discrimination</a></li>
<li><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.oas.org/en/iachr/defenders/default.asp">Rapporteurship on Human Rights Defenders</a></li></ul>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Special_Rapporteur_for_Freedom_of_Expression" class="mw-redirect" title="Special Rapporteur for Freedom of Expression">Special Rapporteur for Freedom of Expression</a> is the only special rapporteurship of the IACHR, meaning that it has a rapporteur dedicated full-time to the job. The other rapporteurships are in the hands of the commissioners, who have other functions at the IACHR and also their own jobs in their home countries, since their work as commissioners is unpaid.
</p><p>The <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.oas.org/en/iachr/lgtbi/">Unit on the Rights of Lesbian, Gay, Trans, Bisexual, and Intersex Persons</a> was created in 2011.
</p><p>The IACHR also has a Press and Outreach Office.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Petitions">Petitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Petitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Unreferenced_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">September 2013</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The Commission processes petitions lodged with it pursuant to its <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iachr.org/Basicos/basic16.htm">Rules of Procedure</a>.
</p><p>Petitions may be filed by NGOs or individuals. Unlike most court filings, petitions are confidential documents and are not made public. Petitions must meet three requirements; domestic remedies must have already been tried and failed (exhaustion), petitions must be filed within six months of the last action taken in a domestic system (timeliness), petitions can not be before another court (duplication of procedure).
</p><p>Once a petition has been filed, it follows the  following procedure:
</p>
<ul><li>Petition is forwarded to the Secretariat and reviewed for completeness; if complete, it is registered and is given a case number. This is where the state is notified of the petition.</li>
<li>Petition reviewed for admissibility.</li>
<li>The Commission tries to find a friendly settlement.</li>
<li>If no settlement is found, then briefs are filed by each side on the merits of the case.</li>
<li>The Commission then files a report on the merits, known as an Article 50 report from relevant article of the Convention. This is a basically a ruling by the Commission with recommendations on how to solve the conflict. The Article 50 report is sent to the state. This is a confidential report; the petitioner does not get a full copy of this report.</li>
<li>The state is given two months to comply with the recommendations of the report.</li>
<li>The petitioner then has one month to file a petition asking for the issue to be sent to the Inter-American Court (only applicable if the State in question has recognized the competence of the Inter-American Court).</li>
<li>The Commission has three months, from the date the Article 50 report is given to the state, to either publish the Article 50 report or send the case to the Inter-American Court of Human Rights. Alternatively, the Commission can also choose to monitor the situation. The American Convention establishes that if the report is not submitted to the Court within three months it may not be submitted in the future, but if the State asks for more time in order to comply with the recommendations of the Article 50 report, the Commission might grant it on the condition that the State signs a waiver on this requirement.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticisms">Criticisms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Criticisms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Politicization_and_position_in_debatable_matters">Politicization and position in debatable matters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Politicization and position in debatable matters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Commission's performance has not been always welcomed. Among others, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> has criticized its politicization. Many others criticize the Commission's stress in some rights instead of some others. These criticisms have given rise to what was called the "Strengthening process of the Commission". This process began in 2011, led by the States belonging to the <a href="/wiki/Bolivarian_Alliance_for_the_Americas" class="mw-redirect" title="Bolivarian Alliance for the Americas">Bolivarian Alliance for the Americas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Location_of_its_headquarters">Location of its headquarters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Location of its headquarters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Officers of Ecuador, Argentina, Bolivia, Guatemala, Nicaragua, and Venezuela, supported the motion for moving the Commission's headquarters, which are currently in Washington D.C. These countries suggested moving the IACHR's headquarters to a Member State to the American Convention of Human Rights. Among the suggested countries were Argentina, Costa Rica and Peru.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The IACHR's ranking officers are its seven <b>commissioners</b>.
The commissioners are elected by the <a href="/wiki/General_Assembly_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="General Assembly of the Organization of American States">OAS General Assembly</a>, for four-year terms, with the possibility of re-election on one occasion, for a maximum period in office of eight years.
They serve in a personal capacity and are not considered to represent their countries of origin but rather "all the member countries of the Organization" (Art. 35 of the Convention).
The Convention (Art. 34) says that they must "be persons of high moral character and recognized competence in the field of human rights".
No two nationals of the same member state may be commissioners simultaneously (Art. 37), and commissioners are required to refrain from participating in the discussion of cases involving their home countries.
</p>
