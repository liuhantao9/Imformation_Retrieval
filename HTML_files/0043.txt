<p><b>William George Thompson</b> (born 6 October 1960) is an English <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a> writer, best known for his weekly column in the Technology section of <a href="/wiki/BBC_News_Online" title="BBC News Online">BBC News Online</a> and his appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/Digital_Planet" title="Digital Planet">Digital Planet</a></i>, a radio show on the <a href="/wiki/BBC_World_Service" title="BBC World Service">BBC World Service</a>. He is also an Honorary Senior Visiting Fellow at <a href="/wiki/City_University_London" class="mw-redirect" title="City University London">City University London</a>'s  Journalism Department.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and writes for <a href="/w/index.php?title=BBC_Webwise&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="BBC Webwise (page does not exist)">BBC Webwise</a>.
</p><p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Jarrow" title="Jarrow">Jarrow</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Durham" title="County Durham">County Durham</a>, Thompson grew up in <a href="/wiki/Corby" title="Corby">Corby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire" title="Northamptonshire">Northamptonshire</a>. He graduated from <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">Cambridge University</a> in philosophy and with a <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_Diploma_in_Computer_Science" title="Cambridge Diploma in Computer Science">diploma in computing</a> in 1984 and worked at <a href="/wiki/Acorn_Computers" title="Acorn Computers">Acorn Computers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was a correspondent for the technology programme<i> The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Byte&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Big Byte (page does not exist)">Big Byte</a></i> on <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio" title="BBC Radio">BBC Radio</a>. He began to write for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> in 1990, and in 1994 went to work there, setting up the paper's website. He left in 1996 to work as a freelance writer and consultant. In November 2009 he took on a role as head of partnership development for Archive Development projects at the BBC, working with <a href="/wiki/Tony_Ageh" title="Tony Ageh">Tony Ageh</a> (formerly of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>), the then Controller of Archive Development at the BBC.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Thompson is a trustee of the <a href="/wiki/Britten_Sinfonia" title="Britten Sinfonia">Britten Sinfonia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-BS_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BS-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and a board member of the <a href="/wiki/Writers%27_Centre_Norwich" class="mw-redirect" title="Writers&#39; Centre Norwich">Writers' Centre Norwich</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WCN_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WCN-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, he was nominated for the Prudential Arts and Business Board Member of the year award.<sup id="cite_ref-Carr_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carr-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In October 2016, <a href="/wiki/Anglia_Ruskin_University" title="Anglia Ruskin University">Anglia Ruskin University</a> announced that it would award Thompson an  Honorary Doctor of Arts degree, in a ceremony on the 11th of that month.<sup id="cite_ref-ARU1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ARU1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has a daughter and a son called Max<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>.
</p>
