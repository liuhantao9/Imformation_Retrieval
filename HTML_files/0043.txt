<p>The <b>Big Six wheel</b>, also known simply as <b>The Big Six</b> or <b>Wheel of Fortune</b>, is an unequal <a href="/wiki/Gambling" title="Gambling">game of chance</a>, played using a large vertical <a href="/wiki/Wheel" title="Wheel">wheel</a> that can be spun.
</p><p>Since 13 May 2002, it can be played legally in licensed casinos in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, under The Gaming Clubs (Bankers' Games) (Amendment) Regulations 2002 (<a href="/wiki/Statutory_Instrument" class="mw-redirect" title="Statutory Instrument">Statutory Instrument</a> 2002/1130).
</p><p>The game is also known in casinos in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rules">Rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Six_wheel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The wheel is divided into a number of equal segments separated by spokes or pins. Each segment is associated with a number. The wheel is spun by a dealer, and the winning segment is indicated by a pointer mounted on a flexible piece of <a href="/wiki/Rubber" class="mw-redirect" title="Rubber">rubber</a> or <a href="/wiki/Leather" title="Leather">leather</a>, which also rubs against the pins to impart friction and slow the wheel down. Should the player stop the wheel, the segment at the top is the winner.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Variants">Variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Six_wheel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are a number of variants of the game, that divide the wheel into a different number of segments, use different symbols in the segments, and have different odds if a symbol is selected.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Money_wheel">Money wheel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Six_wheel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Money wheel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This variant is the most common in <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casinos</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The symbols are $1, $2, $5, $10 and $20 bills — and two special symbols, a <a href="/wiki/Joker_(playing_card)" title="Joker (playing card)">joker</a> and the casino <a href="/wiki/Logo" title="Logo">logo</a>.  The $1 bills pay at odds of 1 to 1, the $2 bills at 2 to 1, the $5 bills at 5 to 1, and so on.  The joker and the logo pay at odds of 40 to 1 or 45 to 1, depending on local gaming regulations or the practice of the casino.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/House_advantage" class="mw-redirect" title="House advantage">house advantage</a> or edge (the proportion of the stakes that the <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casino</a> expects to win on average) of this game is one of the highest of most <a href="/wiki/Casino_game" title="Casino game">casino games</a>.  In the United States it ranges from 11.1% on the $1-bill bet to more than 24% on the joker or logo (when it pays at 40 to 1). In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> the house edge is 7.69% on all bets, so that the payouts on a 52-segment wheel are 47:1, 23:1, 11:1, 5:1, 3:1 and 1:1.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dice_wheel">Dice wheel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Six_wheel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dice wheel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The symbols on the wheel represent some of the 216 possible combinations of three <a href="/wiki/Dice" title="Dice">dice</a>. Sometimes the same combination appears in more than one segment. Players wager on the numbers 1 through 6. If the number appears on one of the dice in the winning segment, the dealer pays at 1 to 1; on two of the dice, 2 to 1; on all three of the dice, 3 to 1.
</p><p>One example of a dice wheel, manufactured by <a href="/wiki/H._C._Evans" title="H. C. Evans">H. C. Evans</a> &amp; Co. of <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> (or its successor), is divided into 54 segments. Each of the triples appears four times. The following doubles each appear four times: 2, 1, 1; 2, 2, 1; 4, 3, 3; 5, 4, 4; 6, 5, 5; and 6, 6, 3.  The following combinations each appear three times: 3, 2, 1; and 6, 5, 4.
</p><p>In the example above, there are 54 possible outcomes for a single spin of the wheel.  For a specific number:
</p>
<ul><li>There are 7 possible outcomes where only one die will match the number</li>
<li>There are 4 possible outcomes where only two dice will match</li>
<li>There are 4 possible outcomes where all three dice will match</li></ul>
<p>At odds of 1 to 1, 2 to 1 and 3 to 1 respectively for each of these types of outcome, the expected loss as a percentage of the stake wagered is:
</p><p>1 - [(7/54) * 2 + (4/54) * 3 + (4/54) * 4] = 22.2%
</p><p>The symbolism of the game is redolent of <a href="/wiki/Chuck-a-luck" title="Chuck-a-luck">chuck-a-luck</a> or <a href="/wiki/Sic_bo" title="Sic bo">sic bo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gambling" title="Gambling">games of chance</a> played with three <a href="/wiki/Dice" title="Dice">dice</a>.  However, the <a href="/wiki/House_advantage" class="mw-redirect" title="House advantage">house advantage</a> or edge is greater than for <a href="/wiki/Chuck-a-luck" title="Chuck-a-luck">chuck-a-luck</a>, which itself has a higher <a href="/wiki/House_advantage" class="mw-redirect" title="House advantage">house advantage</a> than other <a href="/wiki/Casino_game" title="Casino game">casino games</a>.
</p><p>This variety is seldom seen in casinos, but frequently seen as a <a href="/wiki/Carnival_game" title="Carnival game">carnival game</a>, or at a <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organization" title="Charitable organization">charity</a> "<a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a> night" fund-raiser. A similar game, the "Big Nine" wheel, has five numbers on each segment, and also three special symbols, appearing on three spaces each, which pay 10:1 odds.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_variants">Other variants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Six_wheel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other variants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Other variants, using different symbols and odds, are relatively rare in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.
</p><p>One variant called "Mississippi Derby" was used for a short time at the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Casino_Gulfport" title="Grand Casino Gulfport">Grand Casino</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gulfport,_Mississippi" title="Gulfport, Mississippi">Gulfport, Mississippi</a>. (The casino was destroyed in 2005 by <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a>.)  The symbols were combinations of three of a number of different horses, arranged to represent a winner, a second-placed horse and a third-placed horse. (The horses were represented in three concentric rings, with the winner on the outer ring.) Players wagered on particular horses to "win", "place" or "show", as with betting in <a href="/wiki/Horse_racing" title="Horse racing">horse racing</a>.  The payoffs varied from horse to horse, depending on how many times and where the horse appeared on the rings.  Odds ranged from 40 to 1 for the "longshot" to win, down to 1-2 for the "favorite" to show.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="References">References</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Big_Six_wheel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: References">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
