<p><b>Soria</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[Ààso…æja]</a></span>) is a municipality and a <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> city, located on the <a href="/wiki/Douro" title="Douro">Douro</a> river in the east of the autonomous community of <a href="/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n" title="Castile and Le√≥n">Castile and Le√≥n</a> and capital of the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Soria" title="Province of Soria">province of Soria</a>. Its population is 38,881 (<a href="/wiki/Instituto_Nacional_de_Estad%C3%ADstica_(Spain)" title="Instituto Nacional de Estad√≠stica (Spain)">INE</a>, 2017), 43.7% of the provincial population. The municipality has a surface area of 271,77&#160;km<sup>2</sup>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> with a density of 144.13 inhabitants/km<sup>2</sup>. Situated at about 1063 metres above sea level, Soria is the second highest provincial capital in Spain.
</p><p>Although there are remains of settlements from the Iron Age and Celtiberian times, Soria itself enters history with its repopulation between 1109 and 1114, by the Aragonese king <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_I_the_Battler" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfonso I the Battler">Alfonso I the Battler</a>. A strategic enclave due to the struggles for territory between the kingdoms of Castile, Navarre and Aragon, Soria became part of Castile definitively in 1134, during the reign of <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_VII" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfonso VII">Alfonso VII</a>. In Soria was born <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_VIII" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfonso VIII">Alfonso VIII</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_X" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfonso X">Alfonso X</a> had his court established when he received the offer to the throne of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>. In Soria, the deposed king <a href="/wiki/James_IV_of_Mallorca" class="mw-redirect" title="James IV of Mallorca">James IV of Mallorca</a> died, and <a href="/wiki/John_I_of_Castile" title="John I of Castile">John I of Castile</a> married. Booming during the Late Middle Ages thanks to its border location and its control over the bovine industry, Soria went into a slow decline over the next few centuries. It was seriously damaged during the <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_War" title="Peninsular War">Peninsular War</a>. The city preserves an important architectural heritage (extensive medieval walls, Renaissance palaces and architecturally distinctive Romanesque churches) and is home to the Numantine Museum (with pieces from the nearby Celtiberian city of <a href="/wiki/Numantia" title="Numantia">Numantia</a>).
</p><p>Today, its population of 38,881 makes Soria the least populated provincial capital of Castile and Le√≥n and the second least populated in Spain (after <a href="/wiki/Teruel" title="Teruel">Teruel</a>). Particularly important in its economy is the agri-food industry, while an increasing number of tourists are attracted by its cultural heritage. Soria was mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Unesco" class="mw-redirect" title="Unesco">UNESCO</a> as a good example when including the Mediterranean diet in its Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is claimed that in Roman times there was a castle called Oria, purportedly named after a Greek knight called Doricus. Based on this folk etymology, some historians guessed that the first inhabitants of this city might have been the <a href="/wiki/Dorians" title="Dorians">Dorians</a>. Archaeology has not confirmed that story. Instead it has suggested that the first inhabitants were the <a href="/wiki/Suebi" title="Suebi">Suebi</a>, whose kings (as reported by Tutor and Malo in their <i>Compedio historial de las dos Numancias</i>) established one of their courts there. These two hypotheses have been abandoned because of lack of evidence. It seems more likely that the name <i>Soria</i> may have its origin in the word <i>dauria</i> from the river <i>Durius</i> (<a href="/wiki/Douro" title="Douro">Douro</a>).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shield">Shield</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Shield">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">shield</a> of Soria has the following heraldic description:<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>In a field of <a href="/wiki/Gules" title="Gules">gules</a> (red), a <a href="/wiki/Castle" title="Castle">castle</a>, of <a href="/wiki/Argent" title="Argent">argent</a>, crenellated with three battlements, lined up and marbled with sabre, rinsed with <a href="/wiki/Azure_(heraldry)" title="Azure (heraldry)">azure</a> (blue) and a king's bust crowned with gold and with its attributes coming out of his homage, in its colour; silver embroidery loaded with the following legend: "Soria Pura Cabeza de Estremadura", written in saber letters.
</p></blockquote>
<p>The king in the coat of arms is <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_VIII" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfonso VIII">Alfonso VIII</a>, born in Soria, and the red field represents the blood shed by the Sorians, particularly in the battles of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Alarcos" title="Battle of Alarcos">Alarcos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Las_Navas_de_Tolosa" title="Battle of Las Navas de Tolosa">Navas de Tolosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aljubarrota" title="Battle of Aljubarrota">Aljubarrota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The oldest preserved example of the coat of arms is found in the high-medieval bell of San Gil, today the church of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_of_Santa_Mar%C3%ADa_la_Mayor_(Soria)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Church of Santa Mar√≠a la Mayor (Soria) (page does not exist)">Santa Mar√≠a de la Mayor</a>, which already reflected the city's motto.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Unlike the current official coat of arms, the king who now appears on the bust of the castle's keep on the castle's bell tower, is represented in the bell of San Gil with his entire body at the foot of the castle, leaving through its door.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Soria" title="History of Soria">History of Soria</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:212px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ermita_El_Mir%C3%B3n2.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Ermita_El_Mir%C3%B3n2.JPG/210px-Ermita_El_Mir%C3%B3n2.JPG" decoding="async" width="210" height="140" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Ermita_El_Mir%C3%B3n2.JPG/315px-Ermita_El_Mir%C3%B3n2.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Ermita_El_Mir%C3%B3n2.JPG/420px-Ermita_El_Mir%C3%B3n2.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3456" data-file-height="2304" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ermita_El_Mir%C3%B3n2.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Madonna del Mir√≥n Hermitage, started to be built in the 6th century by the <a href="/wiki/Suebi" title="Suebi">Suebi</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The area of Soria was inhabited by the <a href="/wiki/Iberians" title="Iberians">Iberians</a>, who merged with the <a href="/wiki/Celts" title="Celts">Celts</a> to form the <a href="/wiki/Celtiberians" title="Celtiberians">Celtiberians</a> around the 4th century BC.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Roman_conquest_of_Iberia" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman conquest of Iberia">Roman conquest of Iberia</a>, Soria was besieged and its population committed a collective suicide in order to escape slavery. After the fall of the <a href="/wiki/Western_Roman_Empire" title="Western Roman Empire">Western Roman Empire</a>, the rebuilt city was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Suebi" title="Suebi">Suebi</a>. Later, after the <a href="/wiki/Arab_conquests" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab conquests">Arab</a> conquest of Spain, it grew in importance due to its proximity to the border of the Christian lands, which in the 8th century had settled along the Duero river.
</p><p>In 869 Soria was the centre of the rebellion of Suleyman ibn-Abus against the <a href="/wiki/Emir_of_C%C3%B3rdoba" class="mw-redirect" title="Emir of C√≥rdoba">emir of C√≥rdoba</a>, who sent his son <a href="/wiki/Hakan" title="Hakan">Hakan</a> to quench it. In the early 12th century the city was conquered by <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_I_the_Battler" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfonso I the Battler">Alfonso I the Battler</a>, being absorbed into the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n" title="Kingdom of Le√≥n">Kingdom of Le√≥n</a> in 1134. Due to its strategic placement at the borders of the Kingdoms of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile" title="Kingdom of Castile">Castile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Aragon" title="Kingdom of Aragon">Aragon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Navarre" title="Kingdom of Navarre">Navarre</a> and Le√≥n, Soria in the Middle Ages was at the centre of several conflicts between them. <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_VIII_of_Castile" title="Alfonso VIII of Castile">Alfonso VIII of Castile</a>, in reward for its support, gave the city several privileges which it maintained until modern times. In 1195 the town was stormed by <a href="/wiki/Sancho_VII_of_Navarre" title="Sancho VII of Navarre">Sancho VII of Navarre</a>, but later recovered and continued to develop its splendour and trades, mostly held by a community of <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a>.
</p><p>Soria lost most of its importance after the unification of Arag√≥n and Castile in 1479, and above all after the decree of exile issued against the Jews in 1492. In the <a href="/wiki/War_of_Spanish_Succession" class="mw-redirect" title="War of Spanish Succession">War of Spanish Succession</a> (early 18th century), Soria sided for <a href="/wiki/Philip_V_of_Spain" title="Philip V of Spain">Philip V</a>. In 1808 it was captured and set on fire by the French troops.
</p><p>The economical and social crisis of Spain in the early 20th century, and the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War" title="Spanish Civil War">Spanish Civil War</a> with <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Franco" title="Francisco Franco">Francisco Franco</a>'s dictatorship which followed, had negative effects on Soria and its neighborhood, which became depopulated due to strong emigration.
</p><p>The policy of the current authorities aims to strengthen the local economy pivoting on Soria's tourism potential, and has also launched a programme of reconstruction for the neighbouring villages.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:249px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sanjuansoria.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Sanjuansoria.jpg/247px-Sanjuansoria.jpg" decoding="async" width="247" height="185" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Sanjuansoria.jpg/371px-Sanjuansoria.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Sanjuansoria.jpg/494px-Sanjuansoria.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1920" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sanjuansoria.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>San Juan's feast day in Soria.</div></div></div>
<p>The poet <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Machado" title="Antonio Machado">Antonio Machado</a> (1875-1939) spent five years in Soria teaching French in a secondary school, before moving to the neighbouring town of <a href="/wiki/Segovia" title="Segovia">Segovia</a>. These years proved significant in his literary development. He married and lost his wife there and discovered much about the nature of the Castilian people ‚Äì a subject the Generation of '98 authors were very interested in. <i>Campos de Soria</i> was a series of poems lamenting his wife's early death. They formed part of a major collection <i>Campos de Castilla</i>.
</p><p>The firewalking rituals performed at the Sorian village of <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_Manrique" title="San Pedro Manrique">San Pedro Manrique</a> every June as part of the festival of San Juan have been declared as a tradition of National Tourist Interest and have attracted global attention through ethnographic and scientific studies and media coverage.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

The <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Soria" title="Province of Soria">province of Soria</a> has a <a href="/wiki/Minor_Basilica" class="mw-redirect" title="Minor Basilica">Minor Basilica</a>, the Marian Bas√≠lica de Nuestra Se√±ora de los Milagros, in <a href="/wiki/%C3%81greda" title="√Ågreda">√Ågreda</a>.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city is served by the <a href="/wiki/Soria_Railway_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Soria Railway Station">Soria Railway Station</a>, with daily services to <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> via <a href="/wiki/Guadalajara,_Castilla-La_Mancha" class="mw-redirect" title="Guadalajara, Castilla-La Mancha">Guadalajara</a>. There are also many bus lines to neighbouring cities. A new highway has reduced the trip to Madrid by car down to 2 hours.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Soria sits on the banks of Douro river, in its upper course. Its municipality is formed by the city, Barrio Las Casas and three locations: <a href="/w/index.php?title=Oteruelos&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Oteruelos (page does not exist)">Oteruelos</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedrajas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pedrajas (page does not exist)">Pedrajas</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Toledillo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Toledillo (page does not exist)">Toledillo</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Soria has got an <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">oceanic climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="K√∂ppen climate classification">K√∂ppen</a>: <i>Cfb</i>) Due to its altitude, winters in Soria are very cold by Spanish standards (3.2&#160;¬∞C (38&#160;¬∞F) in January) with almost 90 frost days per year. Summers are dry and warm (average 20.5&#160;¬∞C (69&#160;¬∞F) in July) with the daytime temperature usually around 29&#160;¬∞C (84&#160;¬∞F). Temperatures above 35&#160;¬∞C (95&#160;¬∞F) are not rare in summer, while lows can go under 10&#160;¬∞C (50&#160;¬∞F) at nights. It has scant rainfall (512&#160;mm per year) and spring is the wettest season. It is the coldest Spanish provincial capital.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city's professional <a href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Football (soccer)">football</a> club is called <a href="/wiki/CD_Numancia" title="CD Numancia">CD Numancia</a>.
</p><p>Two of Spain's foremost runners of the late 20th century lived and trained in Soria: <a href="/wiki/Ferm%C3%ADn_Cacho" title="Ferm√≠n Cacho">Ferm√≠n Cacho</a>, a gold medallist at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Barcelona_Olympics" class="mw-redirect" title="1992 Barcelona Olympics">1992 Barcelona Olympics</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Abel_Ant%C3%B3n" title="Abel Ant√≥n">Abel Ant√≥n</a>, a two-time <a href="/wiki/World_Championships_in_Athletics" class="mw-redirect" title="World Championships in Athletics">World Champion</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Marathon" title="Marathon">marathon</a>. The area in which they trained, Monte Valonsadero, is now host to the annual <a href="/wiki/Cross_Internacional_de_Soria" title="Cross Internacional de Soria">Cross Internacional de Soria</a> meeting ‚Äì one of Spain's most prestigious <a href="/wiki/Cross_country_running" title="Cross country running">cross country running</a> competitions.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Historia_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Historia-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The European Youth Orienteering Championships were held in Soria in July 2010.
</p>
