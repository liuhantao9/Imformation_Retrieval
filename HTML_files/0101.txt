<p>"<b>Praying for Time</b>" is a song written and performed by <a href="/wiki/George_Michael" title="George Michael">George Michael</a>, released on <a href="/wiki/Epic_Records" title="Epic Records">Epic Records</a> in the United Kingdom and <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> in the United States in 1990. It spent one week at number one on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, making it Michael's seventh number one in the US and his last solo single to reach the top of the Hot 100. "Praying for Time" also reached number one in Canada for two weeks, becoming Michael's penultimate number-one solo hit there.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Praying_for_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song was Michael's first single in almost two years, entering the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> in August 1990.
</p><p>A dark and sombre reflection on social ills and injustice, it was hailed by critics, with James Hunter of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> magazine describing the song as "a distraught look at the world's astounding woundedness. Michael offers the healing passage of time as the only balm for physical and emotional hunger, poverty, hypocrisy and hatred."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The single peaked at number six in the UK, but it was his ninth number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">USA</a>. The song remained in the <i>Billboard</i> top 40 for ten weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was the first song of political motivation he had released as a single since his earliest days with <a href="/wiki/Wham!" title="Wham!">Wham!</a>.
</p><p>The song was the first of five released in the UK from the album <i><a href="/wiki/Listen_Without_Prejudice_Vol._1" title="Listen Without Prejudice Vol. 1">Listen Without Prejudice Vol. 1</a></i>, although it was the only one of the quintet to make the UK top 10. As of October 2017, the single sold 140,000 copies in UK.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Praying_for_Time&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While Michael refused to appear in videos to support the album, an experimental video clip directed by Michael Borofsky was released for "Praying for Time", featuring only the lyrics of the song with a blue and black background that, at the end of the clip, reveals itself to be the image on the cover of the album. Some of the written lyrics featured in the video are slightly animated:
</p>
<ul><li>The lines "The rich declare themselves poor", and "And the wounded skies above" have a shimmering quality, as if written on water.</li>
<li>The lines "I guess somewhere along the way", "he must have let us out to play", and, at the start of the second verse, "These are the days of the empty hand", unroll themselves on the screen, as if written on a ribbon.</li>
<li>The single words forming the line "'cause God's stopped keeping score" appear on screen one at a time, in sync with the vocals, on both occurrences of that line.</li>
<li>In the second chorus, the words "love", "hate" and "hope", respectively at the end of the lines "It's hard to love", "There's so much to hate" and "Hanging on to hope", are all left literally hanging on the screen, fading out a few instants later after the other words in the respective lines have faded out.</li>
<li>The two occurrences of the line "when there is no hope to speak of", one in each chorus, feature different treatments of the word "hope". In the first chorus, the line appears without the word "hope", which flashes briefly on screen when it is sung; in the second chours, the word does not appear at all and the line is shown with a gap (i.e. "when there is no ____ to speak of"). Similarly, in the line "This is the year of the guilty man", the word "guilty" flashes on screen only when it is actually sung.</li></ul>
<p>The video quickly became a buzz clip on <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a>, and stayed in rotation on most video networks for weeks. Similarly, the commercial single had no cover photo, only words. Some have speculated that the style of the video was influenced by Prince's similar promo clip for Sign 'O' The Times released three years previously.
</p><p>After Michael's temporary move to <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Records" title="Virgin Records">Virgin</a> in 1996, <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music" title="Sony Music">Sony Music</a> re-made the video in a simplified CGI version, with an uniformed black/grey background and no animations at all. This is the one most commonly available as of 2017, and as such it was released in the 2017 Deluxe Edition of the <i><a href="/wiki/Listen_Without_Prejudice_Vol._1" title="Listen Without Prejudice Vol. 1">Listen Without Prejudice Vol. 1</a></i> album.
</p><p>An alternate music video for this song has also been released, with Michael actually seen recording and performing the lyrics.
</p>

George Michael performed the song during the 2008 <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> finale on 21 May 2008.
