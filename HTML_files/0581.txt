	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Simon Romero</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Simon Romero</b> is an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> journalist who is a National Correspondent for <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>, based in <a href="/wiki/Albuquerque,_New_Mexico" title="Albuquerque, New Mexico">Albuquerque, New Mexico</a>. Previously he was the newspaper's Brazil Bureau Chief, based in Rio Janeiro, from 2011 to 2017. In that assignment, he covered Brazil, Argentina, Chile, Paraguay and Uruguay. Romero was the Andean bureau chief, based in Caracas, Venezuela, from 2006 to 2011. He joined the Times in March 1999.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Romero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Romero was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and graduated from <a href="/wiki/West_Las_Vegas_High_School" title="West Las Vegas High School">West Las Vegas High School</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in San Miguel County, N.M. He subsequently graduated with honors from <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a> with a degree in History and Literature. He also studied for one year in the history department at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="University of São Paulo">University of São Paulo</a> in Brazil.
</p><p>Romero joined the <i>Times</i> as a contract writer in March 1999, covering economic issues from <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo,_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="São Paulo, Brazil">São Paulo, Brazil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He subsequently covered telecommunications for the <i>Times</i> from New York from 2000 to 2003 and was a national financial correspondent based in Houston, Texas, from 2003 to 2006, focusing on the international energy industry. In August 2006, Romero was named the <i>Times</i> bureau chief in Caracas, moving the Andean bureau there from Bogota, Colombia.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Prior to joining the <i>Times</i>, Romero worked in Brazil for <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_News" title="Bloomberg News">Bloomberg News</a>, having launched the company's news bureaus in <a href="/wiki/Brasilia" class="mw-redirect" title="Brasilia">Brasilia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>. He is also a former senior correspondent based in Rio de Janeiro for the pan-regional business magazine <i>America Economia</i>.
</p><p>In October 2007, intruders broke into Romero's home in Caracas, stealing his computer.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Romero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Simon Romero received the <a href="/wiki/Maria_Moors_Cabot_Prize" class="mw-redirect" title="Maria Moors Cabot Prize">Maria Moors Cabot Prize</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> for reporting on Latin America &amp; the Caribbean in 2015. Previously, in 2014, he won the <a href="/wiki/Robert_Spiers_Benjamin_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Spiers Benjamin Award">Robert Spiers Benjamin Award</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> for best reporting in any medium on Latin America.
</p>
