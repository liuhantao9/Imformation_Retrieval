<p>The <b>ICC World Cricket League</b> was a series of international <a href="/wiki/One-day_cricket" class="mw-redirect" title="One-day cricket">one-day</a> <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> tournaments for national teams without <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">Test</a> status (of Associate status), administered by the <a href="/wiki/International_Cricket_Council" title="International Cricket Council">International Cricket Council</a>. All Associate Members of the ICC are eligible to compete in the <a href="/wiki/League_system" title="League system">league system</a>, which features a promotion and relegation structure between divisions. The league system has two main aims: to provide a qualification system for the <a href="/wiki/ICC_Cricket_World_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC Cricket World Cup">World Cup</a> that can be accessed by all Associate Members, and as an opportunity for these sides to play international one-day matches against teams of similar standards.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/ICC_World_Cricket_League_2007%E2%80%9309" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC World Cricket League 2007–09">inaugural ICC World Cricket League 2007–09</a>, teams were allocated into divisions based on their performance in the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup_qualification" title="2007 Cricket World Cup qualification">qualification tournaments for the 2007 World Cup</a>; the six initial teams in Division One were the teams that had qualified for the 2007 World Cup. The initial series began with regional qualifiers and <a href="/wiki/2007_ICC_World_Cricket_League_Division_One" title="2007 ICC World Cricket League Division One">a First Division</a> in 2007, and ended with the <a href="/wiki/2009_ICC_World_Cup_Qualifier" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 ICC World Cup Qualifier">2009 ICC World Cup Qualifier</a>. At this stage, there were only five divisions.
</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/ICC_World_Cricket_League_2009%E2%80%9313" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC World Cricket League 2009–13">second cycle</a> ran from 2009 to 2014, and the third one from 2012 to 2018. The fourth cycle ran from 2017 to 2019. Following the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/2019_ICC_World_Cricket_League_Division_Two" title="2019 ICC World Cricket League Division Two">2019 Division Two tournament</a>, the World Cricket League was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9321_ICC_Cricket_World_Cup_League_2" class="mw-redirect" title="2019–21 ICC Cricket World Cup League 2">ICC Cricket World Cup League</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9321_ICC_Cricket_World_Cup_Challenge_League" title="2019–21 ICC Cricket World Cup Challenge League">ICC Cricket World Cup Challenge League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ICC_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ICC-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CI_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CI-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Cricket_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The initial league began in 2007 with seven tournaments over five global divisions, based upon previous world rankings.<sup id="cite_ref-about_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lyall_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lyall-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This was expanded into eight separate divisions for the 2009–14 edition. In the first cycle, the number of teams in each tournament varied from six to twelve. With the advent of the second cycle, the number of teams was regularised to six for each tournament, with the exception of the lowest division, Division 8, in which eight teams played. As from 2015, the number of divisions was again reduced to just five.
</p><p>When most of the divisions are played, two teams will be promoted, two relegated and two remain for the next instalment (normally two years later). At the end of each cycle, a World Cup Qualifier is played. In 2018, this featured the four lowest teams of those holding ‘Full’ (senior) status, together with six ’Associate’ nations&#160;&#8211;&#32; namely the four who were still in Division One, plus the top two from Division Two. The two last-placed teams in that World Cup Qualifier lost their ODI status and were relegated into Division Two.
</p><p>Regional tournaments, which act as qualifiers for the lowest division of the World League, are administered by the five development regions of the International Cricket Council: <a href="/wiki/World_Cricket_League_Africa_Region" title="World Cricket League Africa Region">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/ICC_Americas_Championship" title="ICC Americas Championship">Americas</a>, <a href="/wiki/ACC_Trophy" title="ACC Trophy">Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Cricket_League_EAP_region" class="mw-redirect" title="World Cricket League EAP region">East Asia-Pacific</a>, and <a href="/wiki/European_Cricket_Championship" title="European Cricket Championship">Europe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-structure_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-structure-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-about_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Associate_one-day_rankings">Associate one-day rankings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Cricket_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Associate one-day rankings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In late 2005, the <a href="/wiki/International_Cricket_Council" title="International Cricket Council">International Cricket Council</a> ranked the top non-Test nations from 11–30 to complement the <a href="/wiki/Test_cricket" title="Test cricket">Test</a> nations' rankings in the <a href="/wiki/ICC_ODI_Championship" title="ICC ODI Championship">ICC ODI Championship</a>. The ICC used the results from the <a href="/wiki/2005_ICC_Trophy" title="2005 ICC Trophy">2005 ICC Trophy</a> and <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup_qualification#World_Cup_Qualifying_Series_Division_2_2005" title="2007 Cricket World Cup qualification">WCQS Division 2 competition</a> (i.e. the primary qualification mechanisms for the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2007 Cricket World Cup">2007 Cricket World Cup</a>) to rank the nations.
</p><p>These rankings were used to seed the initial stage of the global World Cricket League. Teams ranked 11–16 were placed into Division 1; teams 17–20 were placed into Division 2; teams 21–24 were placed into Division 3; the remaining teams were placed into the upper divisions of their respective regional qualifiers.
</p><p>In 2005, six associates were assigned One Day International status, based on their performance at the preceding <a href="/wiki/ICC_World_Cup_Qualifier" title="ICC World Cup Qualifier">World Cup Qualifier</a>. In 2017, Afghanistan and Ireland were both promoted to "Full" (test-match) status, leaving only four associate nations with ODI-status: after mid-March 2018 these were Scotland, Netherlands, UAE, and Nepal. Netherlands, as winners of the <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9317_ICC_World_Cricket_League_Championship" title="2015–17 ICC World Cricket League Championship">2015–17 ICC World Cricket League Championship</a>, have qualified for a place in the <a href="/wiki/2020%E2%80%9322_ICC_Cricket_World_Cup_Super_League" title="2020–22 ICC Cricket World Cup Super League">2020–22 ICC Cricket World Cup Super League</a>.
In May 2009, the ICC added a rankings table for the associate and affiliate members containing both global and regional placings. In 2016 this changed to maintain a global list only for the top teams and a set of regional lists for the remaining teams.
</p>
