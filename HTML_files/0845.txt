<p><b>George Derek Ibbotson</b> <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">MBE</a></span> (17 June 1932 &#8211; 23 February 2017) was an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> runner who excelled in <a href="/wiki/Athletics_(sport)" class="mw-redirect" title="Athletics (sport)">athletics</a> in the 1950s. His most famous achievement was setting a new <a href="/wiki/World_record" title="World record">world record</a> in the mile in 1957.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Derek_Ibbotson&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ibbotson was born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, and studied at <a href="/wiki/King_James%27s_Grammar_School,_Almondbury" class="mw-redirect" title="King James&#39;s Grammar School, Almondbury">King James's Grammar School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Almondbury" title="Almondbury">Almondbury</a>. He was of the generation that included other great British milers such as <a href="/wiki/Roger_Bannister" title="Roger Bannister">Roger Bannister</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Brasher" title="Chris Brasher">Chris Brasher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Chataway" title="Christopher Chataway">Christopher Chataway</a>. He was the junior champion in Britain in 1951.
</p><p>After service in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a>, Ibbotson returned to competition. In 1956 at <a href="/wiki/1956_Summer_Olympics" title="1956 Summer Olympics">Melbourne</a>, he won a <a href="/wiki/Bronze_medal" title="Bronze medal">bronze medal</a> in the 5,000 metres. After the games, Ibbotson focused on the mile, which was probably an error. Ibbotson began the 1957 season running in mile races, as a 5,000m or 3 mile runner would often do in the early part of the racing season - to race at a faster pace than he would need in those longer distances. After he had run a particularly fast mile at a Glasgow meeting, an experienced athletics official told the BBC that while it was a very good time, he and many others felt that Ibbotson’s greater potential was over 5,000m or 3 miles.
In a race dubbed "mile of the century", Ibbotson won, probably because Delaney, the 1,500m champion at Melbourne the previous year, was boxed in at a crucial point on the final lap. The time was a new world record, taking 0.8 of a second off <a href="/wiki/John_Landy" title="John Landy">John Landy</a>'s time of 3.58 min set in 1954. Ibbotson continued competing in mile races throughout the season as well as some longer distance events - and ended the season exhausted. It was not just the number of races but the faster pace at which they were run.
</p><p>Ibbotson never found the same form again. He represented <a href="/wiki/England_at_the_1958_British_Empire_and_Commonwealth_Games" title="England at the 1958 British Empire and Commonwealth Games">England</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Empire_Games" class="mw-redirect" title="Empire Games">Empire Games</a> at <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a> in 1958, finishing tenth in the 3 miles.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Ibbotson soldiered on and in 1960, in a bid to enter the Rome Olympics, he copied <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Pirie" title="Gordon Pirie">Gordon Pirie</a> who, when he was struggling for form, increased his racing and took part in shorter races than usual, because it was good speed training. Ibbotson was not so successful and was not selected.
</p><p>He was appointed <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of the Order of the British Empire">Member of the Order of the British Empire</a> (MBE) in the <a href="/wiki/New_Year_Honours_2008" class="mw-redirect" title="New Year Honours 2008">2008 New Year Honours</a> for services to athletics. In 2011, he was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/England_Athletics_Hall_of_Fame" title="England Athletics Hall of Fame">England Athletics Hall of Fame</a>.
</p><p>Ibbotson died in <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a> on 23 February 2017, aged 84.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
