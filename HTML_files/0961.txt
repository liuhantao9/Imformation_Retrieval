<p>In diplomacy, an <b>attaché</b> is a person who is assigned ("to be attached") to the diplomatic or administrative staff of a higher placed person or another service or agency.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Although a <a href="/wiki/Loanword" title="Loanword">loanword</a> from <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, in English the word is not modified according to <a href="/wiki/French_grammar#Gender" title="French grammar">gender</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>An attaché is normally an official, who serves either as a <a href="/wiki/Diplomat" title="Diplomat">diplomat</a> or as a member of the support staff, under the authority of an <a href="/wiki/Ambassador" title="Ambassador">ambassador</a> or other head of a <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_mission" title="Diplomatic mission">diplomatic mission</a>, mostly in <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organizations" class="mw-redirect" title="Intergovernmental organizations">intergovernmental organizations</a> or <a href="/wiki/International_non-governmental_organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="International non-governmental organisation">international non-governmental organisations or agencies</a>. Attachés monitor various issues related to their area of specialty (see examples below) that may require some action. To this end, attachés may undertake the planning for events to be attended, decisions which will be taken, managing arrangements and agendas, conducting research, and acting as a representative of the interests of their state when necessary, to the types of organizations mentioned above, and also to <a href="/wiki/National_academy" title="National academy">national academies</a> and to industry.
</p><p>Sometimes an attaché has special responsibilities or expertise.  Examples include a <a href="/wiki/Cultural_attach%C3%A9" title="Cultural attaché">cultural attaché</a>, customs attaché, police officer attaché, labor attaché, legal attaché, liaison officer attaché, <a href="/wiki/Military_attach%C3%A9" title="Military attaché">military/defense attaché</a>, <a href="/wiki/Press_agent" class="mw-redirect" title="Press agent">press attaché</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agricultural_attach%C3%A9" title="Agricultural attaché">agricultural attaché</a>, commercial attaché, maritime attaché and <a href="/wiki/Science_attach%C3%A9" title="Science attaché">science attaché</a>.
</p>

<h2><span id="Military_attach.C3.A9"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_attaché">Military attaché</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Attach%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Military attaché">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Military_attach%C3%A9" title="Military attaché">Military attaché</a></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Air_attach%C3%A9" title="Air attaché">Air attaché</a></div>
<p>Typically, a military attaché serves on the diplomatic staff of an embassy or consulate while retaining a military commission.
</p>
<h2><span id="Science_attach.C3.A9"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Science_attaché">Science attaché</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Attach%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Science attaché">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Science_attach%C3%A9" title="Science attaché">science attaché</a></div>
<p>A science attaché advises on issues pertaining to science and technology.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Health_attach.C3.A9"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_attaché">Health attaché</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Attach%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Health attaché">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A health attaché advises on global health issues and may serve multiple functions.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Holy_See">Holy See</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Attach%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Holy See">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The title is also used in reference to diplomacy and in the hierarchical administration of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>, specifically in the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Curia" title="Roman Curia">Roman Curia</a>, in cases where a priest, usually in the diplomatic corps of the Holy See or else released for service to the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a>, serves in a <a href="/wiki/Nunciature" class="mw-redirect" title="Nunciature">nunciature</a> in a given country or to an international or <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organization" title="Intergovernmental organization">intergovernmental organization</a>.  Especially in the latter cases, the official often provides a particular expertise in the service of the Church, thus, legal or otherwise.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Belgium">Belgium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Attach%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Belgium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the ministries of the Belgian federal state the term is used, since 2005 replacing the term <i>adjunct-adviseur</i> (in Dutch) or <i>conseiller-adjoint</i> (in French), normally used for college graduates, one rank under the head of a competence section.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Colombia">Colombia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Attach%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Colombia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Colombia attachés are increasingly being appointed at younger ages.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The attachés are generally appointed from the great families of Colombian history, most recently the <a href="/w/index.php?title=House_of_Restrepo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="House of Restrepo (page does not exist)">House of Restrepo</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Restrepo_(desambiguaci%C3%B3n)" class="extiw" title="es:Restrepo (desambiguación)">es</a>&#93;</span> gaining increasing prominence.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> This policy has been described by former United States <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Secretary of State">Secretary of State</a> <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a> as "bringing fresh ideas into the diplomatic community, by giving a voice to our young and building the great leaders of our future".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. </p>
