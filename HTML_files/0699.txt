	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">2004 South American Summit</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox vevent" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="summary" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold">2004 South American Summit</th></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center"><a href="/wiki/File:Emblem_of_the_Union_of_South_American_Nations.svg" class="image"><img alt="Emblem of the Union of South American Nations.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Emblem_of_the_Union_of_South_American_Nations.svg/220px-Emblem_of_the_Union_of_South_American_Nations.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="176" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Emblem_of_the_Union_of_South_American_Nations.svg/330px-Emblem_of_the_Union_of_South_American_Nations.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Emblem_of_the_Union_of_South_American_Nations.svg/440px-Emblem_of_the_Union_of_South_American_Nations.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="250" data-file-height="200" /></a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Host country</th><td class="plainlist">Peru</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Dates</th><td>7–9 December 2004</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The <b>2004 South American Summit</b> &#8211; the third of its kind, after earlier events in <a href="/wiki/Bras%C3%ADlia" title="Brasília">Brasília</a> (September 2000) and <a href="/wiki/Guayaquil" title="Guayaquil">Guayaquil</a> (July 2002) &#8211; was held in <a href="/wiki/Cusco" title="Cusco">Cuzco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ayacucho" title="Ayacucho">Ayacucho</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, on 7 &#8211;9 December 2004.  Officially it constituted the Extraordinary Meeting of the Andean Presidential Council (<i>Reunión Extraordinaria del Consejo Presidencial Andino</i> <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20051216093430/http://www.comunidadandina.org/cumbre_lima/ante.htm">[1]</a>) and was also billed as the Third Meeting of Presidents of South America (<i>III Reunión de Presidentes de América del Sur</i>).
</p><p>The main item on the agenda was the signature, by heads of state and plenipotentiary representatives of 12 <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South American</a> nations, of the <a href="/wiki/Cuzco_Declaration" class="mw-redirect" title="Cuzco Declaration">Cuzco Declaration</a>, a two-page document containing a preamble to the deed of foundation of the <a href="/wiki/South_American_Community_of_Nations" class="mw-redirect" title="South American Community of Nations">South American Community of Nations</a> (or "South American Union"), uniting the region's two existing free-trade organisations &#8211; <a href="/wiki/Mercosur" title="Mercosur">Mercosur</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Andean_Community" title="Andean Community">Andean Community</a>.  
</p><p>Ayacucho was chosen for symbolic reasons: it was there that <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Jos%C3%A9_de_Sucre" title="Antonio José de Sucre">Antonio José de Sucre</a>, fighting under the banner of  <a href="/wiki/Sim%C3%B3n_Bol%C3%ADvar" title="Simón Bolívar">Simón Bolívar</a> "the Liberator", defeated the last Imperial <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> troops in South America on  9 December 1824.
</p><p>While the organisation's exact nature and functions &#8211; and even its name &#8211; remain unclear, it aspires to evolve along the lines followed by the continental integration efforts of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, rather than becoming a mere free-trade area.  The initiative emerged &#8211; largely at the instigation of Brazil &#8211; in response to the failed negotiations of the <a href="/wiki/Free_Trade_Area_of_the_Americas" title="Free Trade Area of the Americas">Free Trade Area of the Americas</a>. The FTAA process has been stalemated for more than 12 months in the wake of irreconcilable differences, largely along the geopolitical faultlines between <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> on the one hand and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> on the other.
</p>
