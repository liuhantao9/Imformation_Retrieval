	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox vevent" style="width:22em"><caption>American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man</caption><tbody><tr><th scope="row">Signed</th><td>April 1948</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Location</th><td class="location"><a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogotá">Bogotá</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Effective</th><td>2 May 1948</td></tr></tbody></table>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>All men are born free and equal, in dignity and in rights, and, being endowed by nature with reason and conscience, they should conduct themselves as brothers one to another.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite>Preamble, American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man</cite></div>
</blockquote>
<p>The <b>American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man</b>, also known as the <b>Bogota Declaration</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was the world's first <a href="/wiki/International_human_rights_instruments" title="International human rights instruments">international human rights instrument</a> of a general nature, predating the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Declaration_of_Human_Rights" title="Universal Declaration of Human Rights">Universal Declaration of Human Rights</a> by less than a year.
</p><p>The Declaration was adopted by the nations of the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a> at the Ninth <a href="/wiki/International_Conference_of_American_States" class="mw-redirect" title="International Conference of American States">International Conference of American States</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bogot%C3%A1" title="Bogotá">Bogotá</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, on 2 May 1948,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>where we can find most of its travaux préparatoires.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The same meeting that adopted the <a href="/wiki/Charter_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="Charter of the Organization of American States">Charter of the Organization of American States</a> and thereby created the <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_American_States" title="Organization of American States">OAS</a>.
</p><p>Chapter One of the Declaration sets forth a catalogue of <a href="/wiki/Civil_and_political_rights" title="Civil and political rights">civil and political rights</a> to be enjoyed by the citizens of the signatory nations, together with additional <a href="/wiki/Economic,_social,_and_cultural_rights" class="mw-redirect" title="Economic, social, and cultural rights">economic, social, and cultural rights</a> due to them. As a corollary, its second chapter contains a list of corresponding duties. As explained in the preamble:<br />
</p>
<dl><dd>"The fulfillment of duty by each individual is a prerequisite to the rights of all. Rights and duties are interrelated in every social and political activity of man. While rights exalt individual liberty, duties express the dignity of that liberty."</dd></dl>
<p>Although strictly speaking a declaration is not a <a href="/wiki/Soft_law" title="Soft law">legally binding</a> treaty, the <a href="/wiki/Jurisprudence" title="Jurisprudence">jurisprudence</a> of both the <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Court_of_Human_Rights" title="Inter-American Court of Human Rights">Inter-American Court of Human Rights</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights" title="Inter-American Commission on Human Rights">Inter-American Commission on Human Rights</a> holds it to be a source of binding international obligations for the OAS's member states. While largely superseded in the current practice of the inter-American human rights system by the more elaborate provisions of the <a href="/wiki/American_Convention_on_Human_Rights" title="American Convention on Human Rights">American Convention on Human Rights</a> (in force since 18 July 1978), the terms of the Declaration are still enforced with respect to those states that have not ratified the Convention, such as <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
