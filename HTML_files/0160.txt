<p>According to the official data, the <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> population of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> represents about 13.9 percent of the country's total population as of 2012, which is the highest percentages of Muslims on the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">South American continent</a>. Though the majority belong to the <a href="/wiki/Sunni" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunni">Sunni</a> interpretation of Islam and some syncretic sects such as <a href="/wiki/Sufism" title="Sufism">Sufism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Javanese_people" title="Javanese people">Javanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Kejaw%C3%A8n" title="Kejawèn">Kejawèn</a>.
</p><p>Some speculate that Muslims first came to Suriname as slaves from <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West Africa</a> and then were converted to <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> over time, even though there is little proof for these speculations. The ancestors of the actual Muslim population came to the country as indentured laborers from <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, from whom today most Muslims in Suriname are descended.
</p><p>Because <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> came to Suriname with immigrants from Indonesia (Java) and <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a> (today <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>), who brought their local form of Islam to Suriname, it is strongly influenced by these regions. Apart from descent, most Surinamese Muslims also share the same culture and speak the <a href="/wiki/Suriname#Languages" title="Suriname">same languages</a>. Suriname has a small number of <a href="/wiki/Pashtun_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Pashtun people">Afghan</a> Muslims and their native-born children.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Islam_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are 75,053 Muslims in Suriname, according to the 2012 census.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This number is up from 66,307 Muslims in 2004. However, the share of Muslims declined from 19.6% to 13.9% in the last half-century. The main reason for the declining share of Muslims in Suriname is the <a href="/wiki/Religious_conversion" title="Religious conversion">mass conversion</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ahmadiyya" title="Ahmadiyya">Ahmadi</a> <a href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> of <a href="/wiki/Javanese_Surinamese" title="Javanese Surinamese">Javanese descent</a> to <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> in the last years.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Between 1971 and 2012 the share of
Christianity among ethnic Javanese people grew from 9% to 21% (+12%), while that of Javanese Muslims decreased from 85% to 67% (-18%). The share of Muslims of <a href="/wiki/Indo-Surinamese" title="Indo-Surinamese">Indo-Surinamese</a> descent decreased from 17% to 13% in the same period (-4%), mainly because of <a href="/wiki/Emigration" title="Emigration">emigration</a> to <a href="/wiki/The_Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="The Netherlands">the Netherlands</a> and declining <a href="/wiki/Fertility_rate" class="mw-redirect" title="Fertility rate">fertility rates</a>. The share of <a href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> among <a href="/wiki/Maroon_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Maroon people">Maroon people</a> doubled from 0.1% to 0.2%. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Islam_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Suriname (since 1996) and <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> (since 1998) are the only countries in the Americas which are member states of the <a href="/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation" title="Organisation of Islamic Cooperation">Organisation of Islamic Cooperation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
