<p><b>Sören Hans Brad Opti</b> (born 16 May 1997) is a <a href="/wiki/Badminton" title="Badminton">badminton</a> player from <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. He competed at the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics" title="Badminton at the 2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He lost to both <a href="/wiki/Lee_Chong_Wei" title="Lee Chong Wei">Lee Chong Wei</a> of <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Wong_Zi_Liang" class="mw-redirect" title="Derek Wong Zi Liang">Derek Wong Zi Liang</a> of <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> during group play and did not advance further.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Opti was the flagbearer for Suriname during the opening ceremony.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Opti participated at the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2018_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="Badminton at the 2018 Central American and Caribbean Games">2014</a> and <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2018_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="Badminton at the 2018 Central American and Caribbean Games">2018 Central American and Caribbean Games</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After winning many National and Regional junior titles, he became <a href="/wiki/Surinamese_National_Badminton_Championships" title="Surinamese National Badminton Championships">Surinamese National Champion</a> Men's Singles in 2016 and retained this title in 2017 and 2018. In 2016 and 2018 he was runner-up at the <a href="/wiki/Carebaco_International" title="Carebaco International">Carebaco International</a>. In 2016 he reached the semi-finals at the <a href="/wiki/Chile_International" title="Chile International">Chile International</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo_Open" title="Santo Domingo Open">Santo Domingo Open</a>. Opti participated at the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_at_the_2019_Pan_American_Games" title="Suriname at the 2019 Pan American Games">2019 Pan Am Games</a> for Suriname in the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2019_Pan_American_Games_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_singles" title="Badminton at the 2019 Pan American Games – Men&#39;s singles">Men's Singles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2019_Pan_American_Games_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_doubles" title="Badminton at the 2019 Pan American Games – Men&#39;s doubles">Men's Doubles</a> events. In 2019 he reached the semi-finals in the Mens Doubles category with his doubles partner Mitch Nai Chung Tong at the Carebaco International in <a href="/wiki/Bridgetown" title="Bridgetown">Bridgetown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
