<p><b>Brokopondo</b> is a capital town of the <a href="/wiki/Brokopondo_District" title="Brokopondo District">Brokopondo District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. It is located on the west shore of the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_river" class="mw-redirect" title="Suriname river">Suriname river</a>, just north of the <a href="/wiki/Afobaka_dam" class="mw-redirect" title="Afobaka dam">Afobaka dam</a>. Brokopondo can be reached via the road from <a href="/wiki/Paranam" title="Paranam">Paranam</a> to <a href="/wiki/Afobaka" title="Afobaka">Afobaka</a>. Nearby is a granite monument made by sculptor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jo_Rens&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jo Rens (page does not exist)">Jo Rens</a>, displaying two men: one standing with a parrot and one sitting and writing. It is meant to symbolize the past and the future. There is also a large beach next to the Suriname river near the city centre.
</p><p>Demographically, the largest ethnic group of Brokopondo are the <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">Maroons</a>.
</p><p>There is also a large beach next to the Suriname river near the city centre.
</p>
