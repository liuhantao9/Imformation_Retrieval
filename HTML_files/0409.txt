	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Kwinti people</h1>

<p>The <b>Kwinti</b> are a "Maroon" <a href="/wiki/Bushinengue" title="Bushinengue">Bushinengue</a> ethnic group, descendants of runaway African slaves,  living in the forested interior of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> on the bank of the <a href="/wiki/Coppename_River" title="Coppename River">Coppename River</a>, and the eponymous term for their language, which has less than 1,000 speakers. <a href="/wiki/Kwinti_language" title="Kwinti language">Their language</a> is an <a href="/wiki/English-based_creole" class="mw-redirect" title="English-based creole">English-based creole</a> with <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> and other influences. It is similar to the languages spoken by the <a href="/wiki/Aluku" title="Aluku">Aluku</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paramaccan" class="mw-redirect" title="Paramaccan">Paramaccan</a> Maroons.
</p>
