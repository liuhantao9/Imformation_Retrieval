<p>The <b>Navy and Marine Corps Medal</b> is the highest non-combat <a href="/wiki/Military_awards_of_the_United_States_Department_of_the_Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="Military awards of the United States Department of the Navy">decoration</a> awarded for heroism by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_the_Navy" title="United States Department of the Navy">United States Department of the Navy</a> to the members of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a>. The medal was established by an act of Congress on 7 August 1942, and is authorized under <a href="/wiki/Title_10_of_the_United_States_Code" title="Title 10 of the United States Code">10 U.S.C.</a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/6246">§&#160;6246</a>.
</p><p>The Navy and Marine Corps Medal is the equivalent of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">Army</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Soldier%27s_Medal" title="Soldier&#39;s Medal">Soldier's Medal</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">Air Force's</a> <a href="/wiki/Airman%27s_Medal" title="Airman&#39;s Medal">Airman's Medal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">Coast Guard's</a> <a href="/wiki/Coast_Guard_Medal" title="Coast Guard Medal">Coast Guard Medal</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria">Criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navy_and_Marine_Corps_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As the senior non-combat award for heroism, this award hinges on the actual level of personal "life threatening" risk experienced by the awardee. For heroic performance to rise to this level it must be clearly established that the act involved very specific life-threatening risk to the awardee.<sup id="cite_ref-1650.1H_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1650.1H-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the mid-20th century, the Navy and Marine Corps Medal has been awarded instead of the Silver or Gold <a href="/wiki/Lifesaving_Medal" title="Lifesaving Medal">Lifesaving Medal</a>, for sea rescues involving risk to life. This is due primarily to the creation of a variety of additional military decorations that are often considered more prestigious than the Lifesaving Medal.<sup id="cite_ref-award_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-award-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Additional awards of the medal are denoted by gold or silver <a href="/wiki/5/16_inch_star" title="5/16 inch star">&#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>5</sup>&#8260;<sub>16</sub></span> inch stars</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1650.1H_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1650.1H-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Navy and Marine Corps Medal was first bestowed during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearance">Appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navy_and_Marine_Corps_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Navy and Marine Corps Medal is an <a href="/wiki/Octagon" title="Octagon">octagonal</a> bronze medal.  The <a href="/wiki/Obverse_and_reverse" title="Obverse and reverse">obverse</a> depicts an eagle holding a <a href="/wiki/Fouled_anchor" class="mw-redirect" title="Fouled anchor">fouled anchor</a> over a globe.  The word <i>Heroism</i> is inscribed below the globe.<sup id="cite_ref-award_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-award-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The ribbon of the medal is three equal stripes of navy blue, old gold, and apple red.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
