<p><b>York railway station</b> is on the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Main_Line" title="East Coast Main Line">East Coast Main Line</a> in the United Kingdom, serving the city of <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>, North Yorkshire. It is 188&#160;miles 40&#160;chains (303.4&#160;km) north of <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a> and on the main line it is situated between <a href="/wiki/Doncaster_railway_station" title="Doncaster railway station">Doncaster</a> to the south and <a href="/wiki/Thirsk_railway_station" title="Thirsk railway station">Thirsk</a> to the north. As of June&#160;2018<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> the station is operated by <a href="/wiki/London_North_Eastern_Railway" title="London North Eastern Railway">London North Eastern Railway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>York's station is a key junction approximately halfway between London and <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Waverley_railway_station" title="Edinburgh Waverley railway station">Edinburgh</a>. It is approximately five miles north of the point where the <a href="/wiki/Cross_Country_Route" title="Cross Country Route">Cross Country</a> and <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> routes via <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> join the main line, connecting Scotland and the <a href="/wiki/North_East_England" title="North East England">North East</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_West_England" title="North West England">North West</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_Midlands" class="mw-redirect" title="English Midlands">Midlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southern_England" title="Southern England">southern England</a>. The junction was historically a major site for rolling stock manufacture, maintenance and repair.
</p><p>In <i>Britain's 100 Best Railway Stations</i> by <a href="/wiki/Simon_Jenkins" title="Simon Jenkins">Simon Jenkins</a>, the station was one of only ten to be awarded five stars.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first York railway station was a temporary wooden building on Queen Street outside the <a href="/wiki/York_city_walls" title="York city walls">walls of the city</a>, opened in 1839 by the <a href="/wiki/York_and_North_Midland_Railway" title="York and North Midland Railway">York and North Midland Railway</a>.  It was succeeded in 1841, inside the walls, by what is now <a href="/wiki/York_old_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="York old railway station">York old railway station</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In due course, the irksome requirement that through trains between London and Newcastle needed to reverse out of the old York station to continue their journey necessitated the construction of a new through station outside the walls.
</p><p>The present station, designed by the <a href="/wiki/North_Eastern_Railway_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="North Eastern Railway (UK)">North Eastern Railway</a> architects <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Prosser_(architect)" title="Thomas Prosser (architect)">Thomas Prosser</a> and William Peachey, opened on 25 June 1877.  It had 13 platforms and was at that time the largest in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  As part of the new station project, the Royal Station Hotel (now <a href="/wiki/The_Royal_York_Hotel" class="mw-redirect" title="The Royal York Hotel">The Royal York Hotel</a>), designed by Peachey, opened in 1878.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Autocar_at_York_Station.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Autocar_at_York_Station.jpg/220px-Autocar_at_York_Station.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Autocar_at_York_Station.jpg/330px-Autocar_at_York_Station.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Autocar_at_York_Station.jpg/440px-Autocar_at_York_Station.jpg 2x" data-file-width="700" data-file-height="459" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Autocar_at_York_Station.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>York station in the early 20th Century</div></div></div>
<p>In 1909 new platforms were added, and in 1938 the current footbridge was built and the station resignalled.
</p><p>The building was heavily bombed during the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HWWtW_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HWWtW-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On one occasion, on 29 April 1942,<sup id="cite_ref-OP-10489_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-10489-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 800 passengers had to be evacuated from a King's Cross-Edinburgh train which arrived during a bombing raid.<sup id="cite_ref-HWWtW_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HWWtW-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On the same night, two railway workers were killed, one being station foreman William Milner (born 1900<sup id="cite_ref-OP-10489_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-10489-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>), who died after returning to his burning office to collect his <a href="/wiki/First_aid" title="First aid">first aid</a> kit. He was posthumously awarded the King's commendation for gallantry.<sup id="cite_ref-OP-10489_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-10489-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A plaque in his memory has been erected at the station.<sup id="cite_ref-HWWtW_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HWWtW-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OP-10489_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OP-10489-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The station was extensively repaired in 1947.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:York_railway_station_-_DSC07744.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/York_railway_station_-_DSC07744.JPG/220px-York_railway_station_-_DSC07744.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/York_railway_station_-_DSC07744.JPG/330px-York_railway_station_-_DSC07744.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/York_railway_station_-_DSC07744.JPG/440px-York_railway_station_-_DSC07744.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:York_railway_station_-_DSC07744.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The station in 2010</div></div></div>
<p>The station was designated as a Grade II* <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a> in 1968.<sup id="cite_ref-HE1256554_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HE1256554-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The track layout through and around the station was remodelled again in 1988 as part of the resignalling scheme that was carried out prior to the <a href="/wiki/Electrification" title="Electrification">electrification</a> of the ECML shortly afterwards. This resulted in several bay platforms (mainly on the eastern side) being taken out of service and the track to them removed. At the same time a new signalling centre (<a href="/wiki/York_IECC" title="York IECC">York IECC</a>) was commissioned on the western side of the station to control the new layout and also take over the function of several other signal boxes on the main line. The IECC here now supervises the main line from Temple Hirst (near Doncaster) through to <a href="/wiki/Northallerton_railway_station" title="Northallerton railway station">Northallerton</a>, along with sections of the various routes branching from it. It has also (since 2001–2) taken over responsibility for the control area of the former power box at <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> and thus signals trains as far away as <a href="/wiki/Gargrave_railway_station" title="Gargrave railway station">Gargrave</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morley_railway_station" title="Morley railway station">Morley</a>.
</p><p>In 2006–7, to improve facilities for bus, taxi and car users as well as pedestrians and cyclists, the approaches to the station were reorganised.  The former motive power depot and goods station now house the <a href="/wiki/National_Railway_Museum" title="National Railway Museum">National Railway Museum</a>.
</p>

<ul><li>On 31 March 1920, a passenger train was derailed as it entered platform 8.<sup id="cite_ref-Hoole3_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoole3-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 5 August 1958, a passenger train crashed into the buffers at platform 12.<sup id="cite_ref-Hoole3_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoole3-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Layout">Layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All the platforms except 9, 10 and 11 are under the large, curved, glass and iron roof. They are accessed via a long footbridge (which also connects to the <a href="/wiki/National_Railway_Museum" title="National Railway Museum">National Railway Museum</a>) or via lifts and either of two pedestrian tunnels.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>  Between April 1984 and 2011 the old tea rooms housed the <a href="/wiki/Rail_Riders_World" class="mw-redirect" title="Rail Riders World">Rail Riders World</a>/York Model Railway exhibition.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Major_renovation">Major renovation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Major renovation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station was renovated in 2009.  Platform 9 has been reconstructed and extensive lighting alterations were put in place. New automated <a href="/wiki/Turnstile" title="Turnstile">ticket gates</a> (similar to those in <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>) were planned, but the City of York Council wished to avoid spoiling the historic nature of the station. The then operator National Express East Coast planned to appeal the decision but the plans were scrapped altogether upon handover to East Coast.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_developments">Recent developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Recent developments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The southern side of the station has been given new track and signalling systems. An additional line and new junction was completed in early 2011. This work has helped take away one of the bottlenecks on the East Coast Main Line.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The station has also become the site of one of Network Rail's modern <a href="/wiki/York_Rail_Operating_Centre" title="York Rail Operating Centre">Rail Operation Centres</a>, which opened in September 2014 on land to the west of the station<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  This took over the functions of the former <a href="/wiki/York_IECC" title="York IECC">IECC</a> in December 2018 and will eventually control much of the East Coast Main Line from London to the Scottish border and various subsidiary routes across the North East, Lincolnshire and South, North and West Yorkshire.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Platforms">Platforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Platforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:York_station_approach.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/York_station_approach.JPG/220px-York_station_approach.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/York_station_approach.JPG/330px-York_station_approach.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/York_station_approach.JPG/440px-York_station_approach.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4320" data-file-height="3240" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:York_station_approach.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Platforms 4, 5 and 8 seen from the north</div></div></div>
<p>The platforms at York have been renumbered several times, the most recent being in the late 1980s to coincide with a reduction in the number of platforms from 15 to 11. The current use is:<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Platform 1: South-facing <a href="/wiki/Bay_platform" title="Bay platform">bay platform</a> mostly used for services to <a href="/wiki/Hull_Paragon_Interchange" title="Hull Paragon Interchange">Hull</a> or <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield railway station">Sheffield</a> via <a href="/wiki/Moorthorpe_railway_station" title="Moorthorpe railway station">Moorthorpe</a> and for stabling empty stock.</li>
<li>Platform 2: North-facing bay platform connected only to the <a href="/wiki/Scarborough_railway_station" title="Scarborough railway station">Scarborough</a> branch, used mostly for stabling a spare <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> unit (along with the accompanying station siding).</li>
<li>Platform 3: Main southbound platform (but is signalled bi-directionally), accessible directly from the station concourse. Fast and semi-fast southbound <a href="/wiki/London_North_Eastern_Railway" title="London North Eastern Railway">London North Eastern Railway</a> for <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a> generally use this platform. <a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a> services, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a>, and some westbound TransPennine Express services also use it.</li>
<li>Platform 4: Northward continuation of platform 3 connected only to the Scarborough branch, used by TransPennine Express services from Scarborough.</li>
<li>Platform 5: (Split into 5a and 5b) Main northbound platform (but is signalled bi-directionally). Fast northbound London North Eastern Railway services to Scotland use this and generally call at <a href="/wiki/Darlington_railway_station" title="Darlington railway station">Darlington</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_railway_station" title="Newcastle railway station">Newcastle</a> only. Accessible by footbridge or tunnel. Also used by some <a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a> services northbound. North/eastbound TransPennine Express to Scarborough generally use this platform along with summer Saturday-only <a href="/wiki/East_Midlands_Railway" title="East Midlands Railway">East Midlands Railway</a> services to Scarborough. Southbound London North Eastern Railway services also stop here both fast and semi-fast, the latter of which generally call at <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newark_North_Gate_railway_station" title="Newark North Gate railway station">Newark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peterborough_railway_station" title="Peterborough railway station">Peterborough</a> and London King's Cross.</li>
<li>Platform 6: South-facing <a href="/wiki/Bay_platform" title="Bay platform">bay platform</a> used mostly by <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> commuter services to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_North_railway_station" title="Blackpool North railway station">Blackpool North</a> and by terminating London North Eastern Railway services that return south to London King's Cross, and on non-summer Saturdays by <a href="/wiki/East_Midlands_Railway" title="East Midlands Railway">East Midlands Railway</a> services to <a href="/wiki/St_Pancras_railway_station" title="St Pancras railway station">London St Pancras</a>.</li>
<li>Platform 7: South-facing bay platform used mostly by Northern commuter services, London North Eastern Railway services to/from London King's Cross starting/terminating at York, and non-summer Saturday services by East Midlands Railway to London St. Pancras.</li>
<li>Platform 8: North-facing bay platform used almost exclusively by Northern trains on the <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Harrogate Line">Harrogate Line</a>.</li>
<li>Platforms 9, 10, 11: Bi-directional platforms used by semi-fast London North Eastern Railway services heading north to Newcastle and Scotland (but also some fast services), <a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a> services north and southbound via <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> services westbound to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Piccadilly_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Piccadilly railway station">Manchester Piccadilly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Airport railway station">Manchester Airport</a> and northbound to Newcastle and <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_railway_station" title="Middlesbrough railway station">Middlesbrough</a>. Some Northern services to <a href="/wiki/Blackpool_North_railway_station" title="Blackpool North railway station">Blackpool</a> also use this platform.</li></ul>
<p>Platforms 10 and 11 exist outside the main body of the station. Another siding (the former <i>fruit dock</i>) exists opposite Platform 11.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The station is operated by London North Eastern Railway and is used by the following <a href="/wiki/Train_operating_company" title="Train operating company">train operating companies</a>:
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="London_North_Eastern_Railway">London North Eastern Railway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: London North Eastern Railway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>London North Eastern Railway operates to London as well as many services northbound to Newcastle and Edinburgh. In addition, there are infrequent services to <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_Central_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Glasgow Central railway station">Glasgow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_railway_station" title="Aberdeen railway station">Aberdeen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Inverness_railway_station" title="Inverness railway station">Inverness</a>.
The fastest southbound services run non-stop to London, completing the 188 mile journey in 1 hour and 52 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Rolling stock used:</b> <a href="/wiki/Inter-City_225" class="mw-redirect" title="Inter-City 225">Inter-City 225</a> (<a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_91" title="British Rail Class 91">Class 91</a> <a href="/wiki/Electric_locomotive" title="Electric locomotive">electric locomotive</a> and <a href="/wiki/Driving_Van_Trailer" title="Driving Van Trailer">DVT</a>) and <a href="/wiki/InterCity_125" title="InterCity 125">InterCity 125s</a>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: CrossCountry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a> provides a number of services that run across the country, running as far north as <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_railway_station" title="Aberdeen railway station">Aberdeen</a> and south as <a href="/wiki/Penzance_railway_station" title="Penzance railway station">Penzance</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southampton_Central_railway_station" title="Southampton Central railway station">Southampton Central</a> via <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_New_Street_railway_station" title="Birmingham New Street railway station">Birmingham New Street</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
<b>Rolling stock used:</b> <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_220" title="British Rail Class 220">Class 220</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_221" title="British Rail Class 221">Class 221</a> 'Voyager' <a href="/wiki/Diesel_multiple_units" class="mw-redirect" title="Diesel multiple units">diesel multiple units</a> and Inter-City 125 (HST)
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="East_Midlands_Railway">East Midlands Railway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: East Midlands Railway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/East_Midlands_Railway" title="East Midlands Railway">East Midlands Railway</a> provides one weekend return journey between York and London St Pancras via the <a href="/wiki/Midland_Main_Line" title="Midland Main Line">Midland Main Line</a>, as well as one summer Saturday journey to/from Scarborough:<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
<b>Rolling stock used:</b> <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_222" title="British Rail Class 222">Class 222</a> Meridian diesel multiple units, and very rarely on railtours, <a href="/wiki/InterCity_125" title="InterCity 125">InterCity 125s</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="TransPennine_Express">TransPennine Express</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: TransPennine Express">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> provides a number of express services across the north of England (to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Piccadilly_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Piccadilly railway station">Manchester Piccadilly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Lime_Street_railway_station" title="Liverpool Lime Street railway station">Liverpool Lime Street</a>, Newcastle, <a href="/wiki/Scarborough_railway_station" title="Scarborough railway station">Scarborough</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_railway_station" title="Middlesbrough railway station">Middlesbrough</a>):<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
<b>Rolling stock used:</b> <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_185" title="British Rail Class 185">Class 185</a> "Pennine" diesel multiple units.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Grand_Central">Grand Central</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Grand Central">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a> runs an <a href="/wiki/Open_access_operator" class="mw-redirect" title="Open access operator">open access</a> service between Sunderland and London:<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
<b>Rolling stock used:</b> <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_180" title="British Rail Class 180">Class 180</a> "Adelante".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern">Northern</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=York_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Northern">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> operates a most hourly service towards Hull, Blackpool North and Leeds serving most stations on route.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
<b>Rolling stock used:</b> <a href="/wiki/Sprinter_(train)" title="Sprinter (train)">Sprinter</a> (Class 150/153/155/158) and <a href="/wiki/Pacer_(train)" title="Pacer (train)">Pacer</a> (Class 142/144) diesel multiple units.
</p>
