<p>"<b>The Champion</b>" is a song recorded by American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, featuring rapper <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a>. The song was released on January 12, 2018, and was recorded to serve as an opening theme for <a href="/wiki/NBC_Sports" title="NBC Sports">NBC Sports</a>'s television broadcast of <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_LII" title="Super Bowl LII">Super Bowl LII</a>. It was included as a bonus track on Underwood's sixth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Cry_Pretty" title="Cry Pretty">Cry Pretty</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>.
</p><p>The song entered at number 47 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Champion_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song's lyrics revolve around recovery from obstacles. The song was relevant to both <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_LII" title="Super Bowl LII">Super Bowl LII</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2018_Winter_Olympics" title="2018 Winter Olympics">2018 Winter Olympics</a>, which were both being broadcast by <a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a> in the United States; Underwood also performs the theme song for the network's <i><a href="/wiki/NBC_Sunday_Night_Football" title="NBC Sunday Night Football">Sunday Night Football</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Underwood stated that "the main focus was to celebrate athletes at the top of their game, but we also wanted the song to resonate with people in their everyday lives". The song was featured in the intro for NBC's Super Bowl LII broadcast; in the sequence, Carrie Underwood performed on a soundstage with a screen  showing  highlights from past <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl" title="Super Bowl">Super Bowl</a> games.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Champion_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song is set in the key of <a href="/wiki/G-sharp_minor" title="G-sharp minor">G<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-sharp">&#x266f;</span></span> minor</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Tempo" title="Tempo">tempo</a> of 90 beats per minute in <a href="/wiki/Time_signature" title="Time signature">common time</a>. It follows a chord progression of G<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-sharp">&#x266f;</span></span>m–B–F<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-sharp">&#x266f;</span></span>–D<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-sharp">&#x266f;</span></span>, and Underwood's vocals span from G<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-sharp">&#x266f;</span></span><sub>3</sub> to C<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-sharp">&#x266f;</span></span><sub>5</sub>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Champion_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"The Champion" sold 61,000 units in its first week and debuted at number 47 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> becoming that week's Hot Shot Debut.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  Following the video premiere of the song during the <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_LII" title="Super Bowl LII">Super Bowl LII</a>, it saw an increase in streaming—668,000 on-demand streams on February 5, a 110 percent rise compared to what it tallied on February 3.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song was certified Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> on January 24, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> As of May 14, 2018, the single has sold 314,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Champion_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The official music video for the song was released on March 2, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The video, directed by Jimmy Lynch, shows a montage of different embodiments of "champions" encompassing range of athletes, students and soldiers alike. There's also strong references to female empowerment, with clips of shark attack survivor and surfer <a href="/wiki/Bethany_Hamilton" title="Bethany Hamilton">Bethany Hamilton</a> appearing next to images of the <a href="/wiki/Me_Too_movement" title="Me Too movement">Me Too movement</a>. Underwood also appears in the video in various clips of her recording the song in the studio, clips from her tour, as well as on a running trail, and even in footage from her audition for <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_4)" title="American Idol (season 4)">American Idol</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>.
</p><p>The video received three nominations at the 2018 <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Awards" title="CMT Music Awards">CMT Music Awards</a> for "Video of the Year", "Collaborative Video of the Year" and "Female Video of the Year", ultimately winning the last one and extending Underwood's record for most CMT Music Award wins.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Champion_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood and Ludacris performed the song live for the first time at the <a href="/wiki/2018_Radio_Disney_Music_Awards" title="2018 Radio Disney Music Awards">2018 Radio Disney Music Awards</a> on June 23, 2018. The performance was accompanied by the West Los Angeles Children's Choir, who filtered through the audience and made their way to the stage towards the final chorus.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
