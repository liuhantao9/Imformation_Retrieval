<p>"<b>Remind Me</b>" is a song co-written and recorded by American country music singer <a href="/wiki/Brad_Paisley" title="Brad Paisley">Brad Paisley</a>, performed as a duet with <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>. It was released in May 2011 as the third single from Paisley's 2011 album <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_Country_Music" title="This Is Country Music">This Is Country Music</a></i>. The music video for the song premiered on CMT on July 26, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The song was also named Taste of Country's Song of The Year for 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Paisley wrote this song with <a href="/wiki/Kelley_Lovelace" title="Kelley Lovelace">Kelley Lovelace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_DuBois" title="Chris DuBois">Chris DuBois</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remind_Me_(Brad_Paisley_and_Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In an interview at the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> Country Music Summit in June 2011, Underwood described the recording of the song as "the most unorganized thing [she] had ever been a part of" but added that it "all worked out so perfectly."<sup id="cite_ref-boot_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boot-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She and Paisley had previously discussed recording a duet for <i>This Is Country Music</i> but couldn't coordinate their schedules before the album was due to be completed in December 2010. The album was pushed back and Paisley contacted Underwood in February 2011, sending her a work tape of "Remind Me" with "a lot of mumbling" and <a href="/wiki/Sheryl_Crow" title="Sheryl Crow">Sheryl Crow</a> singing the female part.<sup id="cite_ref-cmt_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cmt-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Underwood saw potential in the song and flew from <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> to <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a> to meet with Paisley, where they recorded the song on February 11, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-boot_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boot-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Upon the album's release in May 2011, Paisley expressed his hope that "Remind Me" would be its third single and described the decision to ask Underwood to perform on the song with him as an easy one.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2013, songwriter Amy Bowen filed a $10 million lawsuit against the song's writers, claiming that they had stolen the idea from a song that she wrote in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remind_Me_(Brad_Paisley_and_Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song's topic is about a married couple in seek of rekindling the flame of their relationship they once carried. It is set at a slow tempo of 69 <a href="/wiki/Beats_per_minute" class="mw-redirect" title="Beats per minute">beats per minute</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Musical_key" class="mw-redirect" title="Musical key">key</a> of <a href="/wiki/F_major" title="F major">F major</a>, with a vocal range from F3 to A5 and a primary chord pattern of F-Dm-C-B<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-flat">&#x266d;</span></span>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remind_Me_(Brad_Paisley_and_Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The duet has been met with favorable reviews. Dan Milliken of Country Universe gave the song a B rating, saying the song is "loaded with potential" but "trying too hard to be a big event."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It received a five-out-of-five star rating from Billy Dukes of Taste of Country, who says "Underwood's vocals are pristine, and Paisley does more than enough to keep up."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Bobby Peacock of Roughstock gave the song a 4.5 out of 5 stars, saying that "it's hard to find a good vocal match for Carrie, but no matter how much she tries to dial herself down, her powerhouse voice still makes Brad's very limited range all the more obvious. But that's a small complaint for an otherwise-excellent song."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Zimbio gave the song five stars out five, saying that "You can actually feel the longing and the pain in both Brad and Carrie’s vocals throughout the single. The lyrics are well written, and lets you actually picture the memories they are talking about" and stated the duet is a potential Number One.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remind_Me_(Brad_Paisley_and_Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Remind Me" entered the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> charts at number 36 on the chart dated for the week ending May 26, 2011. The duet also debuted at number 59 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart, for the week of June 16, 2011<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (December 2015)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and debuted at number 78 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a>. The song reached the top of the Hot Country Songs chart. It sold 42,000 digital copies in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> At number 17, it is Paisley's highest ranking song on the Billboard Hot 100. 
</p><p>The song was certified 2× Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> on September 30, 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and has sold 2,152,000 copies as of January 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-us_sales_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-us_sales-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Remind_Me_(Brad_Paisley_and_Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video for Remind Me was filmed on June 27, 2011, at El Mirage Lake, California. Deaton-Flanigen directed the video.
</p><p>It shows Brad Paisley and Carrie Underwood walking towards each other in the desert while they sing the song for each other. She is wearing a floral dress and he is wearing a shirt, jeans and a white cowboy hat. The desert represents their actual relationship like a barrel by walking towards each other is like they are trying to rekindle the relationship. In the end, they are finally face-to-face. This open end leaves up in the air if they will remind each other, just like the song.
</p>
