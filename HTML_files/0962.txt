<p><b>Ensign</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/ɛ/: &#39;e&#39; in &#39;dress&#39;">ɛ</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="/ən/: &#39;on&#39; in &#39;button&#39;">ən</span></span>/</a></span></span>;<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Middle_English#Late_Middle_English" title="Middle English">Late Middle English</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i>enseigne</i> (12c.) "mark, symbol, signal; flag, standard, pennant", from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>insignia</i> (plural)) is a junior rank of a <a href="/wiki/Officer_(armed_forces)#Commissioned_officers" title="Officer (armed forces)">commissioned officer</a> in the armed forces of some countries, normally in the infantry or navy. As the junior officer in an infantry regiment was traditionally the carrier of the <a href="/wiki/Ensign" title="Ensign">ensign flag</a>, the rank acquired the name. This rank has generally been replaced in army ranks by <a href="/wiki/Second_lieutenant" title="Second lieutenant">second lieutenant</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rnza_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rnza-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Ensigns were generally the lowest ranking commissioned officer, except where the rank of <a href="/wiki/Subaltern" title="Subaltern">subaltern</a> existed.<sup id="cite_ref-ae_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ae-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In contrast, the Arab rank of ensign, لواء, <i><a href="/wiki/Liwa_(Arabic)" title="Liwa (Arabic)">liwa'</a></i>, derives from the command of units with an ensign, not the carrier of such a unit's ensign, and is today the equivalent of a <a href="/wiki/Major_general" title="Major general">major general</a>.
</p><p>In Thomas Venn's 1672 <i>Military and Maritime Discipline in Three Books</i>, the duties of ensigns are to include not only carrying the colour but assisting the captain and lieutenant of a company and in their absence, have their authority.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"Ensign" is <i>enseigne</i> in French, and <i><a href="/wiki/Chor%C4%85%C5%BCy" title="Chorąży">chorąży</a></i> in Polish, each of which derives from a term for a flag. The Spanish <i><a href="/wiki/Alf%C3%A9rez_(rank)" title="Alférez (rank)">alférez</a></i> and Portuguese <i><a href="/wiki/Alf%C3%A9rez_(rank)" title="Alférez (rank)">alferes</a></i> is a junior officer rank below lieutenant associated with carrying the flag, and so is often translated as "ensign". Unlike the rank in other languages, its etymology has nothing to do with flags, but instead comes from the <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> for "cavalier" or "knight". <i><a href="/wiki/F%C3%A4hnrich" title="Fähnrich">Fähnrich</a></i> in German comes from an older German military title, <i>Fahnenträger</i> (flag bearer); however, it is an officer cadet rank, not a junior officer – the same applies to the Dutch <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vaandrig&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vaandrig (page does not exist)">vaandrig</a></i>, which has a parallel etymology. In the German <a href="/wiki/Landsknecht" title="Landsknecht">Landsknecht</a> armies (c. 1480), the equivalent rank of <a href="/wiki/Cornet_(military_rank)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornet (military rank)">cornet</a> existed for those men who carried the troop standard (known as a "cornet"). It is still used in the artillery and cavalry units of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> (<i>kornet</i>).
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Naval_officer_ranks#NATO_Rank_Codes" title="Naval officer ranks">NATO rank code</a> is OF-1 (junior).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Argentina">Argentina</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensign_(rank)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Argentina">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Argentina, the rank of ensign is used by both the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Air_Force" title="Argentine Air Force">air force</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_National_Gendarmerie" title="Argentine National Gendarmerie">gendarmerie</a>. It is, however, used differently in the two services. The air force uses the rank for newly qualified officers, while the gendarmerie uses "ensign" ranks as an equivalent for the army's "lieutenant" ranks.

<p>The other <a href="/wiki/Military_of_Argentina" class="mw-redirect" title="Military of Argentina">armed forces of Argentina</a> have ranks equivalent to ensign: <i>subteniente</i> (which can be translated into English as "sublieutenant") in the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Army" title="Argentine Army">army</a> and <i>guardiamarina</i> (midshipman) in the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Navy" title="Argentine Navy">navy</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the army, the most junior sublieutenant in a regiment (or other unit) is also the flag carrier.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="France_and_Canada">France and Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensign_(rank)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: France and Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Grade-enseigne-1classe.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Grade-enseigne-1classe.svg/100px-Grade-enseigne-1classe.svg.png" decoding="async" width="100" height="51" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Grade-enseigne-1classe.svg/150px-Grade-enseigne-1classe.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Grade-enseigne-1classe.svg/200px-Grade-enseigne-1classe.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="636" data-file-height="327" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Grade-enseigne-1classe.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Enseigne de vaisseau de première classe</i></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Grade-enseigne-2classe.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Grade-enseigne-2classe.svg/100px-Grade-enseigne-2classe.svg.png" decoding="async" width="100" height="51" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Grade-enseigne-2classe.svg/150px-Grade-enseigne-2classe.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Grade-enseigne-2classe.svg/200px-Grade-enseigne-2classe.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="636" data-file-height="327" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Grade-enseigne-2classe.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Enseigne de vaisseau de deuxième classe</i></div></div></div>
<p>During the <i><a href="/wiki/Ancien_R%C3%A9gime" title="Ancien Régime">Ancien Régime</a></i> in France, as in other countries, the ensign (<i>enseigne</i>) was the banner of an infantry regiment.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;note 1&#93;</a></sup> As in other countries, the name began to be used for the officers who carried the ensign. It was renamed <a href="/wiki/Sub-lieutenant" title="Sub-lieutenant">sub-lieutenant</a> (<i>sous-lieutenant</i>) at the end of the 18th century.
The Navy used a rank of ship-of-the-line ensign (<i>enseigne de vaisseau</i>), which was the first officer rank. It was briefly renamed ship-of-the-line sub-lieutenant (<i>sous-lieutenant de vaisseau</i>) in the end of the 18th century, but its original name was soon restored.
</p><p>Nowadays, the rank is still used in the <i><a href="/wiki/Marine_Nationale" class="mw-redirect" title="Marine Nationale">Marine Nationale</a></i>: Ship-of-the-line ensign (<i>enseigne de vaisseau</i>) is the name of the two lowest officer ranks (which are distinguished from one another as "first class", equal to an army lieutenant, and "second class", equal to an army sub-lieutenant.) Both ranks of ensign use the style <i>lieutenant</i>.
</p><p>French-speaking <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Forces" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian Forces">Canadian Naval</a> <a href="/wiki/Officer_(armed_forces)" title="Officer (armed forces)">officers</a> also use the terms of <i>enseigne de vaisseau de deuxième classe</i> and <i>de première classe</i> as the French term for acting sub-lieutenant and <a href="/wiki/Sub-lieutenant" title="Sub-lieutenant">sub-lieutenant</a> respectively. However, <a href="/wiki/French-Canadian" class="mw-redirect" title="French-Canadian">French-Canadian</a> sub-lieutenants use the short form of <i>enseigne</i> instead of <i>lieutenant.</i>
</p><p>The rank "ensign" is also used to refer to second lieutenants (NATO OF-1) in household regiments of the Canadian Army.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Austria_and_Germany">Austria and Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensign_(rank)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Austria and Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A4hnrich" title="Fähnrich">Fähnrich</a></div>
<table border="0" align="right">

<tbody><tr>
<td><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:52px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bundeswehr-OR-6-FR.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Bundeswehr-OR-6-FR.png/50px-Bundeswehr-OR-6-FR.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="104" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Bundeswehr-OR-6-FR.png/75px-Bundeswehr-OR-6-FR.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/ce/Bundeswehr-OR-6-FR.png 2x" data-file-width="91" data-file-height="190" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bundeswehr-OR-6-FR.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/German_Army" title="German Army">German Army</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">Air Force</a> rank insignia</div></div></div>
</td>
<td><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:60px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fhr_OF1c-Rockkragen_AT.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/45/Fhr_OF1c-Rockkragen_AT.gif" decoding="async" width="58" height="100" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="58" data-file-height="100" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fhr_OF1c-Rockkragen_AT.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Austrian_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Austrian Army">Austrian Army</a> rank insignia</div></div></div>
</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><i>Fähnrich</i> which has a parallel etymology to ensign, containing the corresponding German word stem of "flag" in it, is a German and an Austrian <a href="/wiki/Officer_cadet" title="Officer cadet">officer cadet</a> rank. The word <i><a href="/wiki/F%C3%A4hnrich" title="Fähnrich">Fähnrich</a></i> comes from an older German military title, <i>Fahnenträger</i> (literally: "flag carrier"), and first became a distinct <a href="/wiki/Military_rank" title="Military rank">military rank</a> on 1 January 1899.
</p><p>The German <i>Fähnrich</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Non-commissioned_officer" title="Non-commissioned officer">non-commissioned officer</a> promoted from the rank of <i><a href="/wiki/Fahnenjunker" title="Fahnenjunker">Fahnenjunker</a></i> (German Army) or <i><a href="/wiki/Seekadett" title="Seekadett">Seekadett</a></i> (German Navy). <i>Fähnrich</i> is equivalent to <i><a href="/wiki/Feldwebel" title="Feldwebel">Feldwebel</a></i> (staff sergeant), but with additional responsibilities as an officer cadet. If successful, he may be promoted to <i>Oberfähnrich</i> (German Army, equivalent to <i>Hauptfeldwebel</i>, or sergeant first class) or to <i>Oberfähnrich zur See</i> (German Navy), before being made an officer. The German rank <i>Fähnrich</i> should not be translated into English as "ensign" to avoid the possible misconception that this would be an officer's rank, and the English navy rank "ensign" should not be translated as "Fähnrich" but as "Leutnant zur See", which is the German equivalent.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Cornet_(rank)" title="Cornet (rank)">Cornet</a>, originally equal in rank to the <i>Fähnrich</i> in the 17th and 18th century, was the lowest grade of commissioned officers.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Estonia">Estonia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensign_(rank)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Estonia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Estonian_Defence_Forces" title="Estonian Defence Forces">Estonian Defence Forces</a> the equivalent of “ensign” is <i>lipnik</i>. It is used mainly as a rank for reserve officers.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensign_(rank)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:72px;"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Zealand_Navy_ensign_insignia.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/New_Zealand_Navy_ensign_insignia.gif/70px-New_Zealand_Navy_ensign_insignia.gif" decoding="async" width="70" height="105" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9b/New_Zealand_Navy_ensign_insignia.gif 1.5x" data-file-width="100" data-file-height="150" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Zealand_Navy_ensign_insignia.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Shoulder insignia of an RNZN ensign</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Royal_New_Zealand_Navy" title="Royal New Zealand Navy">Royal New Zealand Navy</a>, unlike the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> — whose uniforms, insignia, and traditions it inherited — created the ensign grade to equal the lowest commissioned <a href="/wiki/Royal_New_Zealand_Air_Force" title="Royal New Zealand Air Force">RNZAF</a> grade of <a href="/wiki/Pilot_officer" title="Pilot officer">pilot officer</a> and the New Zealand Army grade of <a href="/wiki/Second_lieutenant" title="Second lieutenant">second lieutenant</a>. It ranks above the grade of <a href="/wiki/Midshipman" title="Midshipman">midshipman</a>. Like the grade of pilot officer, it uses a single thin strip of braid.
</p><p>
The fact that the Royal Navy has no real equivalent to the lowest commissioned <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> grades was one of the driving factors behind the RNZN's decision to create the ensign grade. Another was that, at the time, New Zealand was actively involved with the United States Armed Forces, so it made sense to balance the rank system out with that used by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>.</p><div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Norway">Norway</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensign_(rank)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Norway">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:72px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Generic-Navy-O1.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Generic-Navy-O1.svg/70px-Generic-Navy-O1.svg.png" decoding="async" width="70" height="130" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Generic-Navy-O1.svg/105px-Generic-Navy-O1.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Generic-Navy-O1.svg/140px-Generic-Navy-O1.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="100" data-file-height="185" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Generic-Navy-O1.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Norwegian Navy <i>fenrik</i> insignia</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:72px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Army-NOR-OF-01b.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Army-NOR-OF-01b.svg/70px-Army-NOR-OF-01b.svg.png" decoding="async" width="70" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Army-NOR-OF-01b.svg/105px-Army-NOR-OF-01b.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Army-NOR-OF-01b.svg/140px-Army-NOR-OF-01b.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="96" data-file-height="205" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Army-NOR-OF-01b.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Norwegian Army and Home Guard <i>fenrik</i> insignia</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Norwegian_Navy" title="Royal Norwegian Navy">Royal Norwegian Navy</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_Army" title="Norwegian Army">Norwegian Army</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Norwegian_Air_Force" title="Royal Norwegian Air Force">Royal Norwegian Air Force</a>'s equivalent of ensign is <i>fenrik</i> (the rank below <i>lieutenant</i>). It was previously referred to as <i>second lieutenant</i> (Norwegian: <i>sekondløytnant</i>), while the rank of lieutenant was called <i>premier lieutenant</i>.
</p><p>The rank is obtained after attending <i>befalsskolen</i> for one year, from which the candidate emerges as a sergeant, and serving as a sergeant for three additional years. The rank is known to have been temporarily given to soldiers with rank equivalent of non-commissioned officers, indicating skills and performance beyond their rank, in contract based operative service (UN, NATO). This is highly uncommon and the rank is reverted after the contract period ends.
</p><p>A <i>fenrik</i> serves as a NATO (OF-1) second lieutenant, but the function of the rank differs drastically from other armies. Although it is an officer rank, it strongly resembles an NCO rank in practice. <i>Fenrik</i>s are usually former experienced sergeants without officer education, and usually fill such roles as squad leaders and platoon sergeants. This is due to the lack of an NCO-corps in the Norwegian Army.
</p><p>The historical background for this is that Norway's NCO corps was discontinued on 1 July 1975, and the senior NCOs currently serving were given officer ranks. NCOs ranking as <i>oversersjant</i> were given the officer rank of <i>fenrik</i>, NCOs ranking as <i>stabssersjant</i> were given the rank of <i>lieutenant</i>, and - in accordance with "Hærordningen av 1. januar 1977" - the most senior of the NCOs ranking as <i>stabsserjant</i> were given the rank of <i>captain</i>.
</p><p>The corresponding ranks for the Royal Norwegian Navy were <i>overkvartermester</i> (<i>kvartermester I klasse</i>) and <i>flaggkvartermester</i>. The corresponding ranks for the Royal Norwegian Air Force were <i>vingsersjant</i> and <i>stabssersjant</i>.
</p><p>The rank insignia is worn on the sleeves (navy dress uniform only), on the shoulders of service uniforms or - more recently - on the chest. The chest placement is of newer date, and was introduced with the M-2000 uniform, which is worn by both noncommissioned ranks and officers in all branches of the Norwegian Defence.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensign_(rank)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Until 1871, when it was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Second_lieutenant" title="Second lieutenant">second lieutenant</a>, ensign was the lowest rank of <a href="/wiki/Commissioned_officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Commissioned officer">commissioned officer</a> in <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">infantry</a> regiments of the British Army (except <a href="/wiki/Fusilier" title="Fusilier">fusilier</a> and rifle regiments, and the Marines, which always used <a href="/wiki/Second_lieutenant" title="Second lieutenant">second lieutenant</a>). It was the duty of officers of this rank to carry the <a href="/wiki/Regimental_colours" class="mw-redirect" title="Regimental colours">colours of the regiment</a>.  In the 16th century, "ensign" was corrupted into "ancient", and was used in the two senses of a banner and the bearer of the banner. Today, the term "ensign" is still used by the <a href="/wiki/Foot_Guards" class="mw-redirect" title="Foot Guards">Foot Guards</a> regiments, for instance during the ceremony of <a href="/wiki/Trooping_the_colour" class="mw-redirect" title="Trooping the colour">trooping the colour</a>. The equivalent cavalry rank was <a href="/wiki/Cornet_(military_rank)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornet (military rank)">cornet</a>, also being derived from the name of a banner.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ensign_(rank)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ranks of ensign and <a href="/wiki/Cornet_(military_rank)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornet (military rank)">cornet</a> were abolished in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> in the Army Organization Act of 1815.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>, the rank of ensign superseded <a href="/wiki/Passed_midshipman" title="Passed midshipman">passed midshipman</a> in 1862. Ensign is the junior commissioned officer rank in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a>, the <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration_Commissioned_Officer_Corps" class="mw-redirect" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration Commissioned Officer Corps">National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration Commissioned Officer Corps</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Public_Health_Service_Commissioned_Corps" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Health Service Commissioned Corps">PHS Commissioned Corps</a>. This rank is also used in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Maritime_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Maritime Service">U.S. Maritime Service</a> and the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Naval_Sea_Cadet_Corps" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Naval Sea Cadet Corps">U.S. Naval Sea Cadet Corps</a>. Ensign ranks below <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_junior_grade" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant junior grade">lieutenant junior grade</a>, and it is equivalent to a <a href="/wiki/Second_lieutenant" title="Second lieutenant">second lieutenant</a> in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Army" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Army">U.S. Army</a>, the <a href="/wiki/U.S.M.C." class="mw-redirect" title="U.S.M.C.">Marine Corps</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/U.S.A.F." class="mw-redirect" title="U.S.A.F.">Air Force</a>.
</p><p>Where a newly-commissioned ensign is assigned in the Navy is dependent on status as either an unrestricted line, restricted line, or staff corps officer.  For unrestricted line officers, Depending on assignment to which <a href="/wiki/List_of_Naval_Officer_Designators" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Naval Officer Designators">warfare community</a>, prospective Surface Warfare Officers (SWO) will spend 22 weeks at Surface Warfare Officer School followed by assignment to a warship for qualification as a SWO.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Prospective Submarine Warfare Officers will attend Naval Nuclear Power School for 26 weeks, followed by Nuclear Power Training Unit (Prototype) for 24 weeks and Submarine Officer Basic Course for 12 weeks before reporting to their first submarine.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  Prospective Naval Aviators and Naval Flight Officers have a 12 to 18 month flight training track to earn their wings, followed by a six to nine month training track in a Fleet Replacement Squadron before being assigned to fly combat aircraft in a deployable Fleet aviation squadron.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  Sea-Air-Land (SEAL) Special Warfare Officers attend a 6 month Basic Underwater Demolition/SEAL (BUD/S) course followed by a 4 month SEAL Qualification Training (SQT) course before assignment to a SEAL Team.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  Finally, Special Operations Officers, primarily Explosive Ordnance Disposal (EOD) / Diver officers will have a training track similar in length to that of SEAL officers, to include schools for EOD, SCUBA, hard hat diving, airborne (parachutists) and combat arms skills training before assignment to their first operational assignment.     
</p><p>Restricted Line officers, depending on designator, may train, qualify and be assigned as naval intelligence officers, naval cryptographic officers, aircraft maintenance duty officers, meteorologists/oceanographers, information professionals, human resources professionals, public affairs officers, or a host of other specialties.
</p><p>Still others may become staff corps officers in the Supply Corps, Civil Engineering Corps, Nurse Corps, Medical Service Corps, or be law school students or medical or dental school students in the Judge Advocate General's Corps, Medical Corps or Dental Corps, respectively. 
</p><p>While the Coast Guard does not categorize its officers as unrestricted line, restricted line or staff corps, a similar career sorting and training process also takes place, ranging from those in operational fields such as cuttermen aboard Coast Guard cutters, Naval Aviators in Coast Guard Aviation, specialists in maritime safety and inspections, and a host of other Coast Guard officer career fields.
</p><p>All ensigns will become branch officers or division officers in their first operational assignments, responsible for leading a group of <a href="/wiki/Petty_officer" title="Petty officer">petty officers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Enlisted_men" class="mw-redirect" title="Enlisted men">enlisted men</a> in one of the ship's, squadrons, team's or other organization's branches and divisions (for example, engineering, navigation, communications, sensors or weapons aboard a warship, or similar functions in the operations, aircraft maintenance, administrative or safety/NATOPS departments in a flying squadron) while at the same time receiving on-the-job training in leadership, naval systems, programs, and policies from higher-ranking officers and from senior enlisted men and women in the Chief Petty Officer rates.
</p><p>Navy and Coast Guard ensigns wear collar insignia of a single gold bar and because of this share the nickname "butterbars" with Army, Air Force, and Marine Corps second lieutenants, who wear the same insignia.
</p><p>Within the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Public_Health_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Public Health Service">U.S. Public Health Service</a>, those wearing the rank of ensign are part of a commissioned officer student training, and extern program (COSTEP), either junior, for those with more than a year remaining of education in a commissionable degree (JRCOSTEP), or senior, for those within one year of graduating with a commissionable degree (SRCOSTEP).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Some officers may hold a permanent rank of ensign based on their experience and education, but then can hold the temporary rank of lieutenant, junior grade.
</p>
