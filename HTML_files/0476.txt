<p><b>Anthony Conrad Nesty</b> (born November 25, 1967) is a former competition <a href="/wiki/Swimming_(sport)" title="Swimming (sport)">swimmer</a> from <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> who was an Olympic gold medalist in the 100-metre <a href="/wiki/Butterfly_swimming" class="mw-redirect" title="Butterfly swimming">butterfly</a> event in <a href="/wiki/1988_Summer_Olympics" title="1988 Summer Olympics">1988</a>. He is currently the head coach of the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Gators_swimming_and_diving" title="Florida Gators swimming and diving">Florida Gators men's swim team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Florida" title="University of Florida">University of Florida</a>, where he attended school.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthony_Nesty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Anthony Nesty was born in <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidad</a> in 1967,<sup id="cite_ref-sroprofile_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sroprofile-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the youngest of five children in his family.  Nesty's family migrated to Suriname when he was seven months old, and he started swimming at the age of 5.  Nesty trained and competed in Suriname and the Caribbean through the beginning of his teenage years. He represented Suriname along with his sister, Pauline, at the <a href="/wiki/1983_Pan_American_Games" title="1983 Pan American Games">1983 Pan American Games</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-s322_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s322-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  After placing twenty-first in the 100-metre butterfly at the <a href="/wiki/1984_Summer_Olympics" title="1984 Summer Olympics">1984 Summer Olympics</a> in Los Angeles, Nesty enrolled in <a href="/wiki/The_Bolles_School" class="mw-redirect" title="The Bolles School">The Bolles School</a> in Jacksonville, Florida, a prep school with an athletic program known for training elite, world-class swimmers.  While training under Bolles coach <a href="/wiki/Gregg_Troy" title="Gregg Troy">Gregg Troy</a>, Nesty broke the prep school 100-yard butterfly record held by <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Morales" title="Pablo Morales">Pablo Morales</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ishofprofile_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ishofprofile-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  Breaking Morales's record was the beginning of prominent successes for Nesty.  He graduated from the Bolles School in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-bolles_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bolles-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_swimming_career">International swimming career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthony_Nesty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International swimming career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nesty returned to international competition at the <a href="/wiki/1987_Pan-American_Games" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 Pan-American Games">1987 Pan-American Games</a> in Indianapolis, Indiana, winning a gold medal in the 100-metre butterfly and a bronze medal in the 200-metre butterfly.<sup id="cite_ref-ishofprofile_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ishofprofile-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_1988_Summer_Olympics" title="Swimming at the 1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Summer Olympics</a> in Seoul, Korea, Nesty edged American favorite <a href="/wiki/Matt_Biondi" title="Matt Biondi">Matt Biondi</a> by one one-hundredth of a second to win the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_1988_Summer_Olympics_-_Men%27s_100_metre_butterfly" class="mw-redirect" title="Swimming at the 1988 Summer Olympics - Men&#39;s 100 metre butterfly">100-metre butterfly</a>; he finished the event in 53.00 seconds and Biondi in 53.01.<sup id="cite_ref-sroprofile_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sroprofile-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Nesty is the only Olympic medal winner from <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> and after winning his Olympic gold medal, he was unbeaten in the 100-metre butterfly event for three years.  Nesty was the second black athlete to win an individual Olympic medal in swimming following <a href="/wiki/Enith_Brigitha" title="Enith Brigitha">Enith Brigitha</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1976_Summer_Olympics" title="1976 Summer Olympics">1976 Summer Olympics</a>, and only the second South American swimmer to win an Olympic gold medal after <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Zorrilla" title="Alberto Zorrilla">Alberto Zorrilla</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1928_Summer_Olympics" title="1928 Summer Olympics">1928 Summer Olympics</a>.
</p><p>Nesty's victory in Seoul was a momentous social and political event for Afro-Caribbeans.  The Suriname government commemorated his gold-medal performance on a stamp and on gold and silver coins.  A 25-guilders bank note portraying an illustration of a butterfly swimmer was printed in his honor.  <a href="/wiki/Surinam_Airways" title="Surinam Airways">Surinam Airways</a> named one of its planes after Nesty (this plane was <a href="/wiki/Surinam_Airways_Flight_764" title="Surinam Airways Flight 764">destroyed in an accident while operating as Surinam Airways Flight 764</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>, and the indoor stadium in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a> was renamed for him.
</p><p>Nesty won gold medals in the 100-metre butterfly at the <a href="/wiki/Goodwill_Games" title="Goodwill Games">Goodwill Games</a> in 1990 and the <a href="/wiki/FINA_World_Aquatics_Championships" title="FINA World Aquatics Championships">FINA World Aquatics Championships</a> in 1991.  At the 1991 <a href="/wiki/Pan-American_Games" class="mw-redirect" title="Pan-American Games">Pan-American Games</a> in Havana, Cuba, he again won a gold medal in 100-metre butterfly and a silver in the 200-metre butterfly.<sup id="cite_ref-ishofprofile_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ishofprofile-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  He attempted to defend his 1988 Olympic gold medal in the 100-metre butterfly at the <a href="/wiki/Swimming_at_the_1992_Summer_Olympics" title="Swimming at the 1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a> in Barcelona, Spain, but finished with a third-place bronze.<sup id="cite_ref-sroprofile_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sroprofile-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_swimming_career">College swimming career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthony_Nesty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: College swimming career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After winning his gold medal in Seoul, Nesty accepted an athletic scholarship to attend the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Florida" title="University of Florida">University of Florida</a> in Gainesville, Florida, where he enjoyed a successful swimming career with the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Gators_swimming_and_diving" title="Florida Gators swimming and diving">Florida Gators swimming and diving</a> team under coach <a href="/wiki/Randy_Reese" title="Randy Reese">Randy Reese</a> and coach Skip Foster from 1989 to 1992.  During his four years of <a href="/wiki/National_Collegiate_Athletic_Association" title="National Collegiate Athletic Association">National Collegiate Athletic Association</a> (NCAA) competition, he won three consecutive NCAA individual championships in the 100-yard butterfly (1990, 1991, 1992), one in the 200-yard butterfly (1990), and one as a member of the team's 400-yard medley relay team (1991), and received sixteen <a href="/wiki/All-American" class="mw-redirect" title="All-American">All-American</a> honors. Nesty also won eleven <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_Conference" title="Southeastern Conference">Southeastern Conference</a> (SEC) titles—five in individual races and six as a member of Gators relay teams.<sup id="cite_ref-ufmediaguide_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ufmediaguide-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nesty graduated from the University of Florida with his bachelor's degree in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-ufprofile_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ufprofile-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_after_competition_swimming">Life after competition swimming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anthony_Nesty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Life after competition swimming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nesty was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/International_Swimming_Hall_of_Fame" title="International Swimming Hall of Fame">International Swimming Hall of Fame</a> (ISHOF) as an "Honor Swimmer" in 1998,<sup id="cite_ref-ishofprofile_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ishofprofile-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_University_of_Florida_Athletic_Hall_of_Fame_members" title="List of University of Florida Athletic Hall of Fame members">University of Florida Athletic Hall of Fame</a> as a "Gator Great" in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the mid 1990s, Anthony returned to the Bolles School as a member of the swim coaching staff.
</p><p>He was the associate head coach for the Florida Gators men's swimming team.<sup id="cite_ref-ufprofile_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ufprofile-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of June 4, 2018, he is the head coach for the Florida Gators men's swimming teams.
</p>
