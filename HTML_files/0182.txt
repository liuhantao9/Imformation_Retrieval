<p>This article is about the <a href="/wiki/Demographics" class="mw-redirect" title="Demographics">demographic</a> features of the <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_group" title="Ethnic group">ethnicity</a>, education level, health of the populace, economic status, religious affiliations and other aspects of the population.
</p><p>Most <a href="/wiki/Surinamese_people" title="Surinamese people">Surinamese people</a> live in the narrow, northern coastal plain. The population is one of the most ethnically varied in the world. Each ethnic group preserves its own culture and many institutions, including political parties, tend to follow ethnic lines. Informal relationships vary: the upper classes of all ethnic backgrounds mix freely; outside of the elite, social relations tend to remain within ethnic groupings. All groups may be found in the schools and workplace.
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Suriname_demography.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Suriname_demography.png/250px-Suriname_demography.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Suriname_demography.png/375px-Suriname_demography.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Suriname_demography.png/500px-Suriname_demography.png 2x" data-file-width="724" data-file-height="426" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Suriname_demography.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Demographics of Suriname, Data of <a href="/wiki/FAO" class="mw-redirect" title="FAO">FAO</a>, year 2005&#160;; Number of inhabitants in thousands.</div></div></div>
<p>According to the 2017 revision of the World Population Prospects<sup id="cite_ref-UN_WPP_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_WPP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the total population was 558,368 in 2016, compared to only  215,000 in 1950. The proportion of children below the age of 15 in 2010 was 28.6%, 65% was between 15 and 65 years of age, while 6.5% was 65 years or older
.<sup id="cite_ref-WPP_2010_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WPP_2010-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> According to 2012 census, there were 270,629 males and 271,009 females in Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li>The census used self-identification for ethnic classification.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Amerindians are the original inhabitants of Suriname.</li>
<li>East Indians, also known locally as <i><a href="/wiki/Hindustan" title="Hindustan">Hindustanis</a></i>, are those whose ancestors emigrated from northern <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British India</a> in the latter part of the 19th century.</li>
<li>Creoles are descendants from slaves from Africa.  Prior to the 2004 census, mixed-race people were counted as Creoles.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Their ancestors were brought to the country in the 17th and 18th centuries.</li>
<li>The Maroon are descendants from slaves from Africa that escaped to the interior of Suriname. Their proportion has increased considerably during the past decades, from 9% in 1964 to 22% in 2012. Part of this increase is probably caused by interchanging identities of Creole and Maroon, causing a decrease in the proportion of Creole and an increase in the proportion Maroon.</li>
<li>The Javanese are Asians from formerly <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch-ruled Indonesia</a>.</li>
<li>Whites are principally descended from <a href="/wiki/Dutch_people" title="Dutch people">Dutch</a> colonists.</li></ul>
<p>The current population of Suriname will be different to these census figures, as the census records residents, and notes legal visitors, but does not record illegal immigrants.  According to estimates there may be as many as:
</p>
<ul><li>60,000  <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazilian people">Brazilians</a> (estimates varies between 20,000 and 80,000) from <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> There are also other smaller numbers of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South American</a> nationalities.</li>
<li>40,000 Chinese,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> with quite small communities of <a href="/wiki/Koreans" title="Koreans">Koreans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_people" title="Japanese people">Japanese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Filipino" class="mw-redirect" title="Overseas Filipino">Filipinos</a>.</li>
<li>2,000 <a href="/wiki/Arab" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab">Arab</a>/Middle-eastern (mostly <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanese</a> but also <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syrian</a> and <a href="/wiki/State_of_Palestine" title="State of Palestine">Palestinians</a>).</li>
<li>200 <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a> who are identified in ethnoreligious terms.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cherokee_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Cherokee people">Cherokee people</a> - in the late 19th century, Suriname as well <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> attracted tens of thousands of settlers from <a href="/wiki/Indian_Territory" title="Indian Territory">Indian Territory</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, now part of the state of <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>. The exact number of Cherokee descendants is unknown, but Suriname and Guyana each have large American expatriate (mostly retiree) communities (about 50,000 Americans live in Suriname).</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fertility_rate_by_ethnic_group">Fertility rate by ethnic group</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Fertility rate by ethnic group">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Total_fertility_rate" title="Total fertility rate">total fertility rate</a> for Suriname as a whole is 2.53 children per woman aged 15 to 49. <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)"> Maroons</a> have the highest fertility rate, with 4.47 children per woman. On the other hand, <a href="/wiki/Indo-Surinamese" title="Indo-Surinamese">Hindustani's</a> have the lowest fertility with 1.78 children per woman. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Demographics_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> (official), <a href="/wiki/Sranan_Tongo" title="Sranan Tongo">Sranan Tongo</a> (Surinamese Creole, sometimes called Taki-Taki, is native language of Creoles and much of the younger population), <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> (widely spoken), <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Hindustani" title="Caribbean Hindustani">Sarnami Hindustani (Hindi-Urdu)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Javanese_language" title="Javanese language">Javanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> due to cultural influence from <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> due to cultural influence from <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> due to influence from <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, and other <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin American</a> countries. <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a> is also spoken as well.
</p>
