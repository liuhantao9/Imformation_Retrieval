<p><b>Padmaja Phenany Joglekar</b> is a  <a href="/wiki/Hindustani_Classical" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindustani Classical">Hindustani Classical</a> singer.
<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> She is a disciple of <a href="/wiki/Pandit_Jasraj" class="mw-redirect" title="Pandit Jasraj">Pandit Jasraj</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> She was awarded the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Padma_Shri_award_recipients_(2000%E2%80%9309)" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Padma Shri award recipients (2000–09)">Padma Shri Award</a> in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Padmaja_Phenany_Joglekar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brought up in the tradition of <a href="/wiki/Indian_classical_music" title="Indian classical music">Indian classical music</a>, Padmaja a graduate in <a href="/wiki/Microbiology" title="Microbiology">Microbiology</a>, opted for a career in music. Today she sings both <a href="/wiki/Hindustani_Classical" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindustani Classical">Hindustani Classical</a> ( <a href="/wiki/Khayal" class="mw-redirect" title="Khayal">Khayal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thumri" title="Thumri">Thumri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tappa" title="Tappa">Tappa</a> etc.) and the light popular variety ( <a href="/wiki/Gazals" class="mw-redirect" title="Gazals">Gazals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geet_(song)" title="Geet (song)">Geet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bhajans" class="mw-redirect" title="Bhajans">Bhajans</a> etc.).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Padmaja_Phenany_Joglekar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>She is married to Sunil Joglekar and has a son.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Padmaja_Phenany_Joglekar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Joglekar started learning <a href="/wiki/Hindustani_Classical_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindustani Classical music">Hindustani Classical music</a> under the guidance of the vocalist <a href="/wiki/Jasraj" title="Jasraj">Jasraj</a>. She took further training from  the <a href="/wiki/Sarangi" title="Sarangi">Sarangi</a> maestro <a href="/wiki/Pandit_Ram_Narayan" class="mw-redirect" title="Pandit Ram Narayan">Pandit Ram Narayan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Music_director" title="Music director">music director</a> <a href="/wiki/Hridaynath_Mangeshkar" title="Hridaynath Mangeshkar">Pandit Hridaynath Mangeshkar</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Her three albums titled <i>Hee Shubhra Phulanchi Jwala</i>, <i>Rang Bawara Shravan</i> and <i>Ghar Nachale Nachela</i> consist of Marathi poems written by poets like <a href="/wiki/Kusumagraj" title="Kusumagraj">Kusumagraj</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indira_Sant" title="Indira Sant">Indira Sant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vinda_Karandikar" title="Vinda Karandikar">Vinda Karandikar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mangesh_Padgaonkar" title="Mangesh Padgaonkar">Mangesh Padgaonkar</a>, Shankar Ramani, <a href="/wiki/Suresh_Bhat" title="Suresh Bhat">Suresh Bhat</a>, Grace &amp; Shantabai Shelke. She has also released an album of Marathi Gazals and recently two albums  titled <i>Hey Gagana</i> and <i>He Sparsha Chandanyanche</i>. Her latest Marathi album <i>Megha Re</i> was released in Diwali 2009.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In her album titled <i>Geet Naya Gata Hoon</i>, Joglekar sang  poems written by <a href="/wiki/Atal_Bihari_Vajpayee" title="Atal Bihari Vajpayee">Atal Bihari Vajpayee</a>. She has also tuned and sung poems written by former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_India" title="Prime Minister of India">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/V._P._Singh" class="mw-redirect" title="V. P. Singh">Shri V P Singh</a>, album to be released shortly.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>She has released an Gazal Album <b>‘Zindagi ki Zuban’</b> and Hindi album of <a href="/wiki/Bhajan" title="Bhajan">bhajans</a> composed by <a href="/wiki/Anup_Jalota" title="Anup Jalota">Shri Anup Jalota</a> titled <b>“Mangaldeep”</b>.  Another album of Gazals written by such great poets like <a href="/wiki/Daagh_Dehlvi" title="Daagh Dehlvi">Daag</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jigar" title="Jigar">Jigar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ahmed_Faraz" class="mw-redirect" title="Ahmed Faraz">Ahmed Faraz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bashir_Badr" title="Bashir Badr">Dr. Bashir Badr</a> etc. &amp; are also in the making. Her latest album <i>Rang</i> consists of <a href="/wiki/Sufi_music" title="Sufi music">Sufi</a> style songs in <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Concerts">Concerts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Padmaja_Phenany_Joglekar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Concerts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Joglekar has performed at various conferences in India and also had many concerts in the UK, United States, Canada, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> and <a href="/wiki/UAE" class="mw-redirect" title="UAE">UAE</a> including the Bruhan Maharashtra Mandal (BMM) 2013 convention held at <a href="/wiki/Rhodes_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhodes Island">Rhodes Island</a> in July 2013.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<ul><li>"<b>Mangaldeep</b>" a concert of Marathi Bhavgeet &amp; <a href="/wiki/Poems" class="mw-redirect" title="Poems">poems</a>. The concert includes the songs Padmaja has sung in the film <b>Nivdung</b> (<i><b>Kenvha tari Pahate</b></i> &amp; <i><b>Lavlav kari Paat</b></i>), her composition of outstanding poems of <a href="/wiki/Kusumagraj" title="Kusumagraj">Kusumagraj</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indira_Sant" title="Indira Sant">Indira Sant</a>, Shankar Ramani, <a href="/wiki/Suresh_Bhat" title="Suresh Bhat">Suresh Bhat</a> etc from her various albums (<b>Hee shubhra phulanchi Jwala</b>, <b>Ranga Bavara Shravan</b>, <b>Ghara nachale Nachale </b>etc) and some Bandish and associated songs based on that bandish which she has learnt from her guruji <a href="/wiki/Hridaynath_Mangeshkar" title="Hridaynath Mangeshkar">Hridaynath Mangeshkar</a>.</li>
<li>"<b>BhaktiRang</b>", a concert of <a href="/wiki/Devotional_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Devotional music">devotional music</a>. It consists of <a href="/wiki/Bhajans" class="mw-redirect" title="Bhajans">Bhajans</a> by <a href="/wiki/Meera" title="Meera">Meera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kabir" title="Kabir">Kabir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tulsi" class="mw-redirect" title="Tulsi">Tulsi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nanak" class="mw-redirect" title="Nanak">Nanak</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rahim" title="Rahim">Rahim</a> etc. They are mainly in Hindi with a few in Gujarati. She has been performing this concert since last two years and has also performed a few combined concerts with Shri <a href="/wiki/Anup_Jalota" title="Anup Jalota">Anup Jalota</a>  and <b>Padmashri Purshottamdas ji  Jalota</b>. The press report of Chennai concert is available on the Hindu website whose address is <sup id="cite_ref-BhaktiRang_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BhaktiRang-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>"<b>Mehefil -E -Gazal</b>" consisting of <a href="/wiki/Gazals" class="mw-redirect" title="Gazals">gazals</a> of great <a href="/wiki/Shayar_(poet)" title="Shayar (poet)">shayars</a> like <a href="/wiki/Ghalib" title="Ghalib">Ghalib</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daagh_Dehlvi" title="Daagh Dehlvi">Daag</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jigar" title="Jigar">Jigar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bahadurshah_Zafar" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahadurshah Zafar">Bahadurshah Zafar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Faiz_Ahmad_Faiz" title="Faiz Ahmad Faiz">Faiz Ahmad Faiz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Momin_Khan_Momin" title="Momin Khan Momin">Momin</a>, Mir Taki Mir etc &amp; living legends like <a href="/wiki/Ahmad_Faraz" title="Ahmad Faraz">Ahmad Faraz</a>, Dr. Bashir Badr etc. In this concert she sings her own compositions as well as some popular ones.  This concert is a Gazal connoisseurs delight.</li>
<li>"<b>Tere Sur aur mere Geet</b>" - This is the latest concert launched in 2005. It consists of memorable Classics of Hindi Film mainly 50’s to 70’s period consisting of compositions of such great composers like <a href="/wiki/Pandit_Ravi_Shankar" class="mw-redirect" title="Pandit Ravi Shankar">Pandit Ravi Shankar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madan_Mohan_(music_director)" title="Madan Mohan (music director)">Madan Mohan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salil_Choudhary" class="mw-redirect" title="Salil Choudhary">Salil Choudhary</a>, <a href="/wiki/S._D._Burman" title="S. D. Burman">S D Burman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaidev" title="Jaidev">Jaidev</a>, Sham Sundar, <a href="/wiki/Vasant_Desai" title="Vasant Desai">Vasant Desai</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/R._D._Burman" title="R. D. Burman">R D Burman</a>. The songs selected are outstanding &amp; popular but rarely performed live. This is not an orchestra but a Mehfil.</li></ul>
