<p><b>Jeremain Marciano Lens</b> (born 24 November 1987) is a Dutch professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays for Turkish club <a href="/wiki/Be%C5%9Fikta%C5%9F_J.K." title="Beşiktaş J.K.">Beşiktaş</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands national team</a>. He is a versatile attacking player, known for his pace and stamina, primarily being used as a <a href="/wiki/Left_winger" class="mw-redirect" title="Left winger">winger</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Striker_(association_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Striker (association football)">striker</a>, but he can successfully play as a <a href="/wiki/Second_striker" class="mw-redirect" title="Second striker">second striker</a>.
</p><p>Lens began his career at <a href="/wiki/AZ_Alkmaar" title="AZ Alkmaar">AZ</a>, and after a loan at <a href="/wiki/N.E.C._(football_club)" title="N.E.C. (football club)">NEC</a>, played a part in their <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Eredivisie" title="2008–09 Eredivisie">2008–09 Eredivisie</a> triumph. In 2010, he moved to <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a>, where he won the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_KNVB_Cup" title="2011–12 KNVB Cup">2011–12 KNVB Cup</a>, and in 2013 was signed by <a href="/wiki/FC_Dynamo_Kyiv" title="FC Dynamo Kyiv">Dynamo Kyiv</a>. He won the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Cup" title="Ukrainian Cup">Ukrainian Cup</a> in his first season, the <a href="/wiki/Double_(association_football)" title="Double (association football)">double</a> in his second, and signed for Sunderland in July 2015.
</p><p>Lens is a full international for the Netherlands, and has earned 34 caps. In August 2010, he received his first international <a href="/wiki/Cap_(association_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cap (association football)">cap</a> against <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_football_team" title="Ukraine national football team">Ukraine</a> and scored a debut goal. He was part of the Dutch squad which came third at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="AZ">AZ</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: AZ">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lens made his debut into professional football in the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Eredivisie" title="2005–06 Eredivisie">2005–06 season</a>. He played two matches for <a href="/wiki/AZ_Alkmaar" title="AZ Alkmaar">AZ</a> that season. Before that, he played in the youth team of AZ, <a href="/wiki/FC_Omniworld" class="mw-redirect" title="FC Omniworld">Omniworld</a>, <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> and his regional team, Spartaan.
</p><p>In June 2007, AZ reached a deal concerning a loan involving Lens; it meant he would play the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Eredivisie" title="2007–08 Eredivisie">2007–08 season</a> for <a href="/wiki/N.E.C._(football_club)" title="N.E.C. (football club)">NEC</a>, and that he would return to AZ the season afterwards. Lens agreed with the deal, and the player officially joined NEC on loan in the summer of 2007.
</p>
<h4><span id="NEC_.28loan.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="NEC_(loan)">NEC (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: NEC (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After a difficult beginning with his new club NEC before the winter, Lens' as well as his team's situation improved with Lens scoring eight goals in 16 matches. NEC qualified for the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League#Qualification" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> preliminaries. For a long time, it seemed he would join NEC permanently, but he eventually decided to return to AZ.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Return_to_AZ">Return to AZ</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Return to AZ">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In July 2008, Lens had an operation on his left foot, which meant he was out for some time. The <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_Eredivisie" title="2008–09 Eredivisie">2008–09 season</a> was a personal disappointment for Lens, who did not contribute much to AZ's championship win. When head coach <a href="/wiki/Louis_van_Gaal" title="Louis van Gaal">Louis van Gaal</a> left the team for <a href="/wiki/FC_Bayern_Munich" title="FC Bayern Munich">Bayern Munich</a>, Lens saw a chance for himself to play more matches. He grabbed the chance under the new coach, and he played well and scored important goals for AZ.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PSV">PSV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: PSV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jeremain_Lens.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Jeremain_Lens.jpg/220px-Jeremain_Lens.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="247" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Jeremain_Lens.jpg/330px-Jeremain_Lens.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Jeremain_Lens.jpg/440px-Jeremain_Lens.jpg 2x" data-file-width="537" data-file-height="604" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jeremain_Lens.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lens at <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a> in 2010</div></div></div>
<p>On 21 May 2010, <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a> contracted Lens for four years, swapping <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Marcellis" title="Dirk Marcellis">Dirk Marcellis</a> as part of the deal. Lens was given the number 9 shirt, and his first year at the club was a successful one for the player. He was moved from a central position to the flanks, being more utilised on both <a href="/wiki/Left_winger" class="mw-redirect" title="Left winger">wings</a> or working behind strikers as a <a href="/wiki/Second_striker" class="mw-redirect" title="Second striker">second striker</a>. He struggled to score in first few matches, but eventually became used to the new position. He reached the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2010–11 UEFA Europa League">Europa League</a> quarter-finals with the team, scoring 3 goals in 11 appearances, and reached 10 Eredivisie goals in 33 appearances, while the <a href="/wiki/Eindhoven" title="Eindhoven">Eindhoven</a>-based team only finished in third place, despite being the table leaders for the most of the <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Eredivisie" title="2010–11 Eredivisie">2010–11 season</a>.
</p><p>At the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Eredivisie" title="2011–12 Eredivisie">2011–12 season</a>, Lens' jersey number was changed to number 11, which was free since the departure of <a href="/wiki/Nordin_Amrabat" title="Nordin Amrabat">Nordin Amrabat</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kayserispor" title="Kayserispor">Kayserispor</a> in January 2011. On 6 November 2011, Lens came in from the bench against <a href="/wiki/Heracles_Almelo" title="Heracles Almelo">Heracles</a> and scored a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a>, his first for the club.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dynamo_Kyiv">Dynamo Kyiv</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Dynamo Kyiv">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DK-Olborg_(10).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/DK-Olborg_%2810%29.jpg/170px-DK-Olborg_%2810%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="325" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/DK-Olborg_%2810%29.jpg/255px-DK-Olborg_%2810%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/DK-Olborg_%2810%29.jpg/340px-DK-Olborg_%2810%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="404" data-file-height="772" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DK-Olborg_(10).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lens playing for Dynamo Kyiv in 2014</div></div></div>
<p>On 18 June 2013, Lens signed a four-year contract with Ukrainian club <a href="/wiki/FC_Dynamo_Kyiv" title="FC Dynamo Kyiv">Dynamo Kyiv</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On 29 August, he scored his first official goal for Dynamo, opening the score on the ninth minute of a 5–1 victory over Kazakhstani club <a href="/wiki/FC_Aktobe" title="FC Aktobe">Aktobe</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_UEFA_Europa_League" title="2013–14 UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> play-offs second leg, an 8–3 aggregate win. On 6 October, Lens scored his first two goals in the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Premier_League" title="Ukrainian Premier League">Ukrainian Premier League</a> in a crushing 9–1 victory over <a href="/wiki/FC_Metalurh_Donetsk" title="FC Metalurh Donetsk">Metalurh Donetsk</a>. He helped Dynamo win the <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_Cup" title="Ukrainian Cup">Ukrainian Cup</a> in his first season.
</p><p>On 23 April 2015, Lens was sent off for two bookings in the first half, as Dynamo lost 0–2 at <a href="/wiki/ACF_Fiorentina" title="ACF Fiorentina">Fiorentina</a> and were eliminated from the quarter-finals of the Europa League. His second yellow card was when the referee judged that he had dived in the penalty area.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In his second and final season for Dynamo, he managed to help his team win both the Premier League and Ukrainian Cup without suffering a single defeat in both competitions.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sunderland">Sunderland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sunderland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 July 2015, after completing a medical, <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_A.F.C." title="Sunderland A.F.C.">Sunderland</a> signed Lens for an undisclosed fee on a four-year contract. This move reunited him with head coach <a href="/wiki/Dick_Advocaat" title="Dick Advocaat">Dick Advocaat</a> after previously working under him at PSV and AZ.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lens scored his first English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> goal on 29 August 2015, securing a 2–2 draw at <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He scored his second Sunderland goal against <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a> on 3 October 2015, lobbing the ball over goalkeeper <a href="/wiki/Adri%C3%A1n_(footballer)" title="Adrián (footballer)">Adrián</a> to put Sunderland 2–0 ahead. However, Lens was then sent off after receiving two yellow cards for rash tackles, and West Ham went on to earn a 2–2 draw.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The match turned out to be Advocaat's final one in charge of Sunderland, as he left the club the next day.
</p><p>Lens fell out of favour under new manager <a href="/wiki/Sam_Allardyce" title="Sam Allardyce">Sam Allardyce</a>, and was dropped from the squad and fined two weeks wages after refusing to participate in a post-match warm-down following a defeat to <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> on 12 December 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Lens later stated he was unhappy at Sunderland, suggesting he could leave the club in the January transfer window as he "did not come to the Premier League to sit on the bench".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Lens started in Sunderland's <a href="/wiki/2015%E2%80%9316_FA_Cup" title="2015–16 FA Cup">FA Cup</a> third round tie against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>, and scored the opening goal in the match, after which Arsenal came back to win 3–1.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="Fenerbah.C3.A7e_.28loan.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Fenerbahçe_(loan)">Fenerbahçe (loan)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Fenerbahçe (loan)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 30 August 2016, Lens was loaned to <a href="/wiki/Fenerbah%C3%A7e_S.K._(football)" title="Fenerbahçe S.K. (football)">Fenerbahçe</a> on a season-long deal.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Be.C5.9Fikta.C5.9F"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Beşiktaş">Beşiktaş</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Beşiktaş">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 6 August 2017, <a href="/wiki/Be%C5%9Fikta%C5%9F_J.K" class="mw-redirect" title="Beşiktaş J.K">Beşiktaş</a> signed Lens on loan, with the option of a permanent transfer afterwards.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He scored his debut goal for the club in a 2–2 draw against <a href="/wiki/Trabzonspor" title="Trabzonspor">Trabzonspor</a>. Playing as a replacement for <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Quaresma" title="Ricardo Quaresma">Ricardo Quaresma</a>, he managed to find the net in the 58th minute of the match.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On 7 June 2018, it was confirmed that Lens had signed permanently for <a href="/wiki/Be%C5%9Fikta%C5%9F_J.K." title="Beşiktaş J.K.">Beşiktaş</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Suriname">Suriname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Suriname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although Lens previously played for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-21_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-21 football team">Netherlands under-21 team</a>, he chose to play for the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_football_team" title="Suriname national football team">Suriname national team</a>, since he said he felt like he is <a href="/wiki/Surinamese" class="mw-redirect" title="Surinamese">Surinamese</a>. In 2009, Lens was called up for Suriname's squad by national team coach <a href="/wiki/Wensley_Bundel" title="Wensley Bundel">Wensley Bundel</a>, for the <a href="/wiki/Parbo_Bier_Cup" title="Parbo Bier Cup">Parbo Bier Cup</a>. He scored two goals in three matches and helped Suriname finish second in the tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since the tournament was not under the auspices of <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>, Lens was still eligible for a call-up for the Netherlands.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Netherlands">Netherlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jeremain_Lens&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Netherlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 3 May 2010, Lens was called up for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Dutch national team</a>, as a part of the preparations for the <a href="/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2010 FIFA World Cup">2010 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> However, on 27 May 2010, Netherlands manager <a href="/wiki/Bert_van_Marwijk" title="Bert van Marwijk">Bert van Marwijk</a> announced that the player would not be part of the final 23-man squad participating in the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On 11 August 2010, Lens made his debut in a 1–1 <a href="/wiki/Friendly_match" class="mw-redirect" title="Friendly match">friendly</a> draw with <a href="/wiki/Ukraine_national_football_team" title="Ukraine national football team">Ukraine</a>, scoring the only goal for the <i>Oranje</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lens became a regular member of the Dutch team under the management of his former AZ coach Louis van Gaal. He scored five goals and made a further five assists during the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification_%E2%80%93_UEFA_Group_D" title="2014 FIFA World Cup qualification – UEFA Group D">2014 World Cup qualification campaign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lens was named in the Netherlands squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 World Cup</a> in Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He appeared as a substitute in the Netherlands' first two Group B games (a 1–5 win over <a href="/wiki/Spain_national_football_team" title="Spain national football team">Spain</a> and a 2–3 victory against <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_soccer_team" title="Australia national soccer team">Australia</a>) before starting in a 2–0 victory over <a href="/wiki/Chile_national_football_team" title="Chile national football team">Chile</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Lens played the full 120 minutes of the semi-final against <a href="/wiki/Argentina_national_football_team" title="Argentina national football team">Argentina</a>, which Argentina ultimately won on penalties.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
