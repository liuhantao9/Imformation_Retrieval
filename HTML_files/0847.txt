<p><b>Cameron Zishan Rana-Jerome</b> (born 14 August 1986), known as <b>Cameron Jerome</b>, is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">striker</a> for Turkish <a href="/wiki/S%C3%BCper_Lig" title="Süper Lig">Süper Lig</a> club <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6ztepe_S.K." title="Göztepe S.K.">Göztepe</a>. He earned 10 <a href="/wiki/Cap_(sport)" title="Cap (sport)">caps</a> for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England under-21 team</a>.
</p><p>Jerome began his career as a trainee with <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grimsby_Town_F.C." title="Grimsby Town F.C.">Grimsby Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> and <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> before signing a professional contract with <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a> in the summer of 2004. He quickly became a regular at <a href="/wiki/Ninian_Park" title="Ninian Park">Ninian Park</a> and after scoring 20 goals in <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_Cardiff_City_F.C._season" title="2005–06 Cardiff City F.C. season">2005–06</a> he was signed by <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a> for a fee of £3&#160;million. He spent five years at <a href="/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_(stadium)" title="St Andrew&#39;s (stadium)">St Andrew's</a> where he experienced two promotions and two relegations and also helped the side win the <a href="/wiki/2011_Football_League_Cup_Final" title="2011 Football League Cup Final">2011 Football League Cup</a>. He joined <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> in August 2011 for a fee of around £4&#160;million and was mainly used as an impact player by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Pulis" title="Tony Pulis">Tony Pulis</a>. After not figuring in new manager <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hughes" title="Mark Hughes">Mark Hughes</a>' plans Jerome joined <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a> on loan for the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Crystal_Palace_F.C._season" title="2013–14 Crystal Palace F.C. season">2013–14 season</a>. Jerome joined <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a> in August 2014, and after 138 appearances in three-and-a-half years, he signed for <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> in January 2018.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameron_Jerome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jerome was born in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, West Yorkshire,<sup id="cite_ref-PFA_323_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PFA_323-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and is of Grenadian descent.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As a youth, Jerome played for Stile Common, alongside <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Griffith" title="Anthony Griffith">Anthony Griffith</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fraizer_Campbell" title="Fraizer Campbell">Fraizer Campbell</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Reuben_Noble-Lazarus" title="Reuben Noble-Lazarus">Reuben Noble-Lazarus</a>; who all went on to enjoy careers as professional players.<sup id="cite_ref-pals_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pals-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Griffith and Jerome also played for Yorkshire Counties.<sup id="cite_ref-pals_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pals-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Jerome later joined home town club Westend Juniors before moving on to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> as a junior, then moved on via <a href="/wiki/Grimsby_Town_F.C." title="Grimsby Town F.C.">Grimsby Town</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Wednesday_F.C." title="Sheffield Wednesday F.C.">Sheffield Wednesday</a> and <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> to gain a contract at <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_City_F.C." title="Cardiff City F.C.">Cardiff City</a>, after being recommended by reserve team manager <a href="/wiki/Paul_Wilkinson_(footballer)" title="Paul Wilkinson (footballer)">Paul Wilkinson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Jerome played under Wilkinson in Grimsby's youth system, but after Wilkinson left <a href="/wiki/Blundell_Park" title="Blundell Park">Blundell Park</a> to take up his position with Cardiff in October 2003, Jerome was released by replacement <a href="/wiki/Neil_Woods" title="Neil Woods">Neil Woods</a> for being a disruptive influence.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cardiff_City">Cardiff City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameron_Jerome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cardiff City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Described by the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> as "one of Cardiff's rising stars", Jerome made his debut in a 0–0 draw with <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_F.C." title="Leeds United F.C.">Leeds United</a> on 2 October 2004, as a substitute for <a href="/wiki/Andy_Campbell" title="Andy Campbell">Andy Campbell</a>, before going on to score seven goals in 32 appearances in the <a href="/wiki/2004%E2%80%9305_in_English_football" title="2004–05 in English football">2004–05 season</a>, and was in a good run of form in the <a href="/wiki/2005%E2%80%9306_in_English_football" title="2005–06 in English football">2005–06 season</a>, finishing top scorer for Cardiff with 20 goals.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>His hot form earned him a contract with second-city club <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a>. The move not only benefitted Jerome but also helped Cardiff as they used the transfer money to sign <a href="/wiki/Michael_Chopra" title="Michael Chopra">Michael Chopra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_McPhail" title="Stephen McPhail">Stephen McPhail</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Loovens" title="Glenn Loovens">Glenn Loovens</a>. Jerome appeared in an April 2005 <i><a href="/wiki/FourFourTwo" title="FourFourTwo">FourFourTwo</a></i> article which heralded the Yorkshire forward as the eighth best player in the Football League.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Birmingham_City">Birmingham City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameron_Jerome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Birmingham City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:CameronJerome_cropped.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/CameronJerome_cropped.jpg/170px-CameronJerome_cropped.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="218" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/CameronJerome_cropped.jpg/255px-CameronJerome_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/CameronJerome_cropped.jpg/340px-CameronJerome_cropped.jpg 2x" data-file-width="633" data-file-height="813" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:CameronJerome_cropped.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Jerome playing for <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_City_F.C." title="Birmingham City F.C.">Birmingham City</a> in 2008</div></div></div>
<p>Jerome signed for Birmingham City on 31 May 2006, for a <a href="/wiki/Transfer_fee" class="mw-redirect" title="Transfer fee">transfer fee</a> reported as an initial £3&#160;million, potentially rising to £4m.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Jerome made his debut as a 66th-minute <a href="/wiki/Substitute_(association_football)" title="Substitute (association football)">substitute</a> against <a href="/wiki/Colchester_United_F.C." title="Colchester United F.C.">Colchester United</a> at <a href="/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_(stadium)" title="St Andrew&#39;s (stadium)">St Andrew's</a> on 5 August. However, it was a debut to forget, as he was sent off within five minutes of entering play elbowing an opponent in the face.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He netted his first goal for Birmingham against <a href="/wiki/Queens_Park_Rangers_F.C." title="Queens Park Rangers F.C.">Queens Park Rangers</a> at <a href="/wiki/Loftus_Road" title="Loftus Road">Loftus Road</a> on 12 September.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Jerome had a slow first season at St Andrew's by his own admission, scoring just 9 times in 44 games in all competitions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Jerome scored his first <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> goal after 32 seconds of the away match against <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> on 25 August 2007; his second goal of that game confirmed Birmingham's first win of the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Premier_League" title="2007–08 Premier League">2007–08 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He finished the season as Birmingham's second top goal scorer with 7 goals in 33 games as Birmingham were relegated. However, the Blues immediately bounced back in the 2008–09 season by finishing second in the <a href="/wiki/Football_League_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="Football League Championship">Championship</a>. Jerome's 10 goals in 45 appearances were only bettered by veteran partner <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Phillips_(footballer)" title="Kevin Phillips (footballer)">Kevin Phillips</a> in the Birmingham attack. He scored the first goal in the West Midlands derby match against <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton_Wanderers" class="mw-redirect" title="Wolverhampton Wanderers">Wolverhampton Wanderers</a> as Birmingham won 2–0.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In July 2009, Jerome signed a new five-year contract with the club.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He scored a spectacular first goal of the <a href="/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_Premier_League" title="2009–10 Premier League">2009–10 season</a> to give his club a 2–1 lead against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a>, described as "the kind of goal that only the likes of <a href="/wiki/Steven_Gerrard" title="Steven Gerrard">Gerrard</a> usually scores at Anfield";<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> he held off <a href="/wiki/Javier_Mascherano" title="Javier Mascherano">Javier Mascherano</a> before "unleashing a 30-yard thunderbolt that flew beyond the reach of <a href="/wiki/Pepe_Reina" title="Pepe Reina">Reina</a> and dipped under the crossbar".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Jerome hit a purple patch as the Blues notched up a club-record fifteen consecutive games unbeaten in the Premier League, scoring crucial goals against <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> and two versus <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a>. His tenth goal of the season, a header from <a href="/wiki/James_McFadden" title="James McFadden">James McFadden</a>'s cross, came in a 5–1 defeat away to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Two weeks after the final game of the season, Jerome's goals total rose to 11 when he was awarded an extra goal by the <a href="/wiki/Dubious_Goals_Committee" title="Dubious Goals Committee">Dubious Goals Committee</a> for his attempt against <a href="/wiki/Burnley_F.C." title="Burnley F.C.">Burnley</a>, which had originally been given as an <a href="/wiki/Own_goal" title="Own goal">own goal</a> by <a href="/wiki/Brian_Jensen_(footballer,_born_1975)" title="Brian Jensen (footballer, born 1975)">Brian Jensen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WrongStats_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WrongStats-4">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jerome was a 92nd-minute substitute as Birmingham won the <a href="/wiki/2011_Football_League_Cup_Final" title="2011 Football League Cup Final">2011 League Cup</a>, defeating favourites <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> 2–1 at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Stadium" title="Wembley Stadium">Wembley Stadium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2011LC_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011LC-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stoke_City">Stoke City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameron_Jerome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Stoke City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 31 August 2011, the last day of the transfer window, Jerome signed a four-year contract with Premier League club <a href="/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C." title="Stoke City F.C.">Stoke City</a> for an undisclosed fee, believed by <i><a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a></i> to be £4&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He scored on his Stoke City debut in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Europa League</a> against <a href="/wiki/FC_Dynamo_Kyiv" title="FC Dynamo Kyiv">Dynamo Kiev</a> to earn his side a 1–1 draw away from home.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He scored against <a href="/wiki/Maccabi_Tel_Aviv_F.C." title="Maccabi Tel Aviv F.C.">Maccabi Tel Aviv</a> in a 3–0 win but was then sent off for two bookable offences.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Jerome scored his first League goal for Stoke against <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic_F.C." title="Wigan Athletic F.C.">Wigan Athletic</a> on 31 December 2011; it was his first league goal for more than 13 months.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He was used in a squad rotation system by manager <a href="/wiki/Tony_Pulis" title="Tony Pulis">Tony Pulis</a>, something which he admitted he would have to accept.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He continued to make an impact as a substitute, scoring against <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich_Albion_F.C." title="West Bromwich Albion F.C.">West Bromwich Albion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Everton_F.C." title="Everton F.C.">Everton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> However, he began to vent his frustration at his lack of starts.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/2012%E2%80%9313_Stoke_City_F.C._season" title="2012–13 Stoke City F.C. season">2012–13 season</a> began with Jerome again on the bench and almost joining <a href="/wiki/Blackburn_Rovers_F.C." title="Blackburn Rovers F.C.">Blackburn Rovers</a> on loan.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In a rare appearance, against <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> on 28 November 2012 coming on as a substitute, he provided an assist for <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Walters" title="Jonathan Walters">Jonathan Walters</a> and then scored the winning goal.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On 29 December, Jerome "lashed into the top corner from 30 yards" to score Stoke's third goal in a 3–3 draw with <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C." title="Southampton F.C.">Southampton</a> in the final minute of the match.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> He scored his third and final goal of the season in a 2–1 win against <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a> on 9 February 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Stoke ended the season in 13th position with Jerome making 30 appearances of which nine were starts.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the season Tony Pulis was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hughes" title="Mark Hughes">Mark Hughes</a> and Jerome criticised Pulis' lack of squad rotation.&#160;– "There's no guarantees [for places under Hughes] but before in the old regime, there was a guaranteed XI who played. No matter what happened, how you trained or if you came on and did well in the games you were involved in, you were still never going to start."<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sentinel_1_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sentinel_1-37">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 5 August 2013 Jerome admitted an FA charge of breaching betting regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> He was fined £50,000 and was also severely warned as to his future conduct by the FA.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loan_to_Crystal_Palace">Loan to Crystal Palace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameron_Jerome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Loan to Crystal Palace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 2 September 2013 Jerome joined <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_F.C." title="Crystal Palace F.C.">Crystal Palace</a> on loan for the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Crystal_Palace_F.C._season" title="2013–14 Crystal Palace F.C. season">2013–14 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He made his debut for the club on 14 September, coming on as a 64th-minute substitute for <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Gayle" title="Dwight Gayle">Dwight Gayle</a> in a 2–0 defeat away at <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> After a poor start to the season <a href="/wiki/Ian_Holloway" title="Ian Holloway">Ian Holloway</a> left Palace by mutual consent and Tony Pulis was appointed manager on 23 November. Despite Jerome criticising Pulis towards the end of his time at Stoke, he started and scored against his former club Cardiff City on 7 December.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In total Jerome played 29 times for the Eagles scoring twice as they finished in 11th position.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Norwich_City">Norwich City</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameron_Jerome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Norwich City">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Norwich_City_Defensive_Wall.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Norwich_City_Defensive_Wall.jpg/220px-Norwich_City_Defensive_Wall.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="178" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Norwich_City_Defensive_Wall.jpg/330px-Norwich_City_Defensive_Wall.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Norwich_City_Defensive_Wall.jpg/440px-Norwich_City_Defensive_Wall.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4160" data-file-height="3375" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Norwich_City_Defensive_Wall.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Left to right: <a href="/wiki/Jonny_Howson" title="Jonny Howson">Jonny Howson</a>, Jerome, <a href="/wiki/Timm_Klose" title="Timm Klose">Timm Klose</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Murphy" title="Jacob Murphy">Jacob Murphy</a>. Norwich City <a href="/wiki/Defensive_wall" title="Defensive wall">defensive wall</a> v <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic" class="mw-redirect" title="Wigan Athletic">Wigan Athletic</a>, September 2016</div></div></div>
<p>On 20 August 2014 Jerome joined <a href="/wiki/EFL_Championship" title="EFL Championship">Championship</a> club <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a> for an undisclosed fee believed to be around £1.5&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club on 13 September in a 4–2 away victory over Cardiff City. His goal was the fourth of Norwich's second-half goals, in a game which they came from 2–0 behind to win.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Jerome scored twice in a 3–0 win three days later away at <a href="/wiki/Brentford_F.C." title="Brentford F.C.">Brentford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> He scored another two on 20 September against former club Birmingham City, taking his tally to five goals in four league matches.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> He scored his sixth goal on 4 October in a draw with <a href="/wiki/Rotherham_United_F.C." title="Rotherham United F.C.">Rotherham United</a> that kept Norwich top of the table.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He added another two on 31 October as Norwich beat Bolton 2–1.
</p><p>After an impressive first season at Norwich, Jerome was voted third in the club's annual Player of the Season competition.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Norwich finished third, just short of automatic promotion, and qualified for the playoffs. They reached the final against <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a>, and Jerome scored the opening goal in a 2–0 victory for the Canaries.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Jerome had an effective game, frustrating Middlesbrough with his frequent pressing, and was named <a href="/wiki/Man_of_the_match" class="mw-redirect" title="Man of the match">man of the match</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 6 August 2016, Jerome scored in the 4–1 win away to Blackburn Rovers on the opening day of the 2016–17 season.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> On 21 August, he scored Norwich's only goal in the <a href="/wiki/East_Anglian_derby" title="East Anglian derby">East Anglian derby</a>, which ended in a 1–1 draw at <a href="/wiki/Portman_Road" title="Portman Road">Portman Road</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Jerome scored his 16th and last goal of the season in the 7–1 thrashing of <a href="/wiki/Reading_F.C." title="Reading F.C.">Reading</a> on 8 April 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Derby_County">Derby County</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameron_Jerome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Derby County">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 16 January 2018, Jerome joined <a href="/wiki/Derby_County_F.C." title="Derby County F.C.">Derby County</a> on a one-and-a-half-year contract. The fee, officially undisclosed, was reported by <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a> as £1.5&#160;million.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first goal for Derby in a 3–0 home win against <a href="/wiki/Brentford_F.C." title="Brentford F.C.">Brentford</a> on 3 February,<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> and finished the season with 6 from 20 appearances, including a goal in the <a href="/wiki/2018_English_Football_League_play-offs#Championship" title="2018 English Football League play-offs">play-off semi-final</a>, as Derby failed to gain promotion.<sup id="cite_ref-sb1718_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sb1718-58">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="G.C3.B6ztepe"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Göztepe">Göztepe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameron_Jerome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Göztepe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 31 August 2018, Jerome joined Turkish <a href="/wiki/S%C3%BCper_Lig" title="Süper Lig">Süper Lig</a> club <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6ztepe_S.K." title="Göztepe S.K.">Göztepe</a> for an undisclosed fee.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> His first goal for the club opened the scoring in a 3–2 home win against <a href="/wiki/Konyaspor" title="Konyaspor">Konyaspor</a> on 30 September.<sup id="cite_ref-Soccerway_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Soccerway-61">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameron_Jerome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jerome made 10 appearances for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England under-21 team</a> between 2005 and 2008 without scoring.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameron_Jerome&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p>
