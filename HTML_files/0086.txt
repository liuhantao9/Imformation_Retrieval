<p>"<b>Church Bells</b>" is a song recorded by American singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> from her fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Storyteller_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" title="Storyteller (Carrie Underwood album)">Storyteller</a></i>. The song was written by <a href="/wiki/Zach_Crowell" title="Zach Crowell">Zach Crowell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brett_James" title="Brett James">Brett James</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, with production from Mark Bright, and was released as the third single from the album in the United States, being shipped to radio on April 3, 2016, and had an official impact date of April 11, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"Church Bells" topped the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart, becoming Underwood's 15th leader on that chart and thereby extending her record as the female artist with most number-ones in history of that chart. It is her second number one from <i>Storyteller</i> on the Country Airplay chart. Underwood became the first woman in 2016 to have two number-ones on the chart. The song was certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> on August 3, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-riaa.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riaa.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Solo_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Solo Performance">Best Country Solo Performance</a> at the <a href="/wiki/59th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="59th Annual Grammy Awards">59th Grammy Awards</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_Bells_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Church Bells</i> tells the story of Jenny, a poor girl who marries a rich oilman. However, she soon discovers her new husband is an abusive alcoholic. Jenny slips an untraceable poison into his drink and kills him.
</p><p>Underwood said of the song, "It's...like Fancy's little sister", referencing <a href="/wiki/Bobbie_Gentry" title="Bobbie Gentry">Bobbie Gentry</a>'s hit song "<a href="/wiki/Fancy_(Bobbie_Gentry_song)" title="Fancy (Bobbie Gentry song)">Fancy</a>", made famous by <a href="/wiki/Reba_McEntire" title="Reba McEntire">Reba McEntire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> “She’s young, she’s pretty, she’s poor, doing what she can to survive,” Underwood explains, describing her “Church Bells” heroine. “(She) meets a man that has a lot of wealth and is supposed to take care of her.”<sup id="cite_ref-auto1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_Bells_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song received generally positive reviews. In their review of the album, <i>Billboard</i> said of the song, "The first half holds a pair of Southern-gothic blockbusters that are the closest she has come to channeling McEntire’s down-home storytelling: “Choctaw County Affair,” a tale of lovers silencing their would-be blackmailer, and “Church Bells,” a rags-to-riches murder ballad in which a woman quietly offs her abusive husband."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <i>Rolling Stone</i> called it "the most epic domestic-violence-revenge-murder ballad in years" and that it "split the difference between <a href="/wiki/Martina_McBride" title="Martina McBride">Martina McBride</a>'s "Independence Day" and the <a href="/wiki/Dixie_Chicks" title="Dixie Chicks">Dixie Chicks</a> "Goodbye Earl", but without the latter's safety-valve of humor."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a mixed review, website <i><a href="/wiki/Taste_of_Country" class="mw-redirect" title="Taste of Country">Taste of Country</a></i> said, "on “Church Bells” Underwood injects new drama into an oft-told tale. The Dixie Chicks “Goodbye Earl” relies on the same plot points, with Earl’s fate being the same as the oil baron in this song". The review was concluded by saying "expect the song to perform well on country radio, even if it’s not one that pushes her artistry forward as much as others on her most recent studio album".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_Bells_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Church Bells" first charted at number 49 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart, selling 2,300 copies following the release of the parent album. It fell off the chart in the following week. After its release as the third official single and following Underwood's performance at the 51st Annual <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music Awards</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-auto2_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> the song re-entered the Hot Country Songs chart at number 34 and peaked at number two, spending three consecutive weeks at that spot. It debuted at number 50 on the US <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart and peaked at number one, becoming Underwood's 15th number one on that chart and extending her record for most number ones among women on that chart. Underwood became the first female artist of 2016 to have two number one hits on the Country Airplay chart, with "<a href="/wiki/Heartbeat_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Heartbeat (Carrie Underwood song)">Heartbeat</a>" and "Church Bells".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For the week ending May 28, 2016, it debuted at number 90 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a> chart and peaked at number 43.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Canada, the song debuted at number 37 on the <a href="/wiki/Canada_Country" class="mw-redirect" title="Canada Country">Canada Country</a> chart and peaked at number two<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and debuted at number 100 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> chart and peaked at number 64.
</p><p>The song has been certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> on August 3, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-riaa.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riaa.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> As of January 2017, it has sold 456,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_Bells_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video for the single was filmed  during the <a href="/wiki/Storyteller_Tour:_Stories_in_the_Round" title="Storyteller Tour: Stories in the Round">Storyteller Tour</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lincoln,_Nebraska" title="Lincoln, Nebraska">Lincoln, Nebraska</a>. The music video was released on May 10, 2016, and premiered on <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Church_Bells_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood first performed the song live on the first stop of the <a href="/wiki/Storyteller_Tour:_Stories_in_the_Round" title="Storyteller Tour: Stories in the Round">Storyteller Tour</a> in Jacksonville, Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> She has since performed the song numerous times during the tour.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Underwood's first televised performance of the song was at the 51st Annual <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-auto2_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> She again performed the song live at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/American_Country_Countdown_Awards" title="American Country Countdown Awards">American Country Countdown Awards</a>.
Underwood performed the song at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Awards" title="CMT Music Awards">CMT Music Awards</a> on June 8, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Underwood performed this song in a hits medley at the 52nd Annual <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
