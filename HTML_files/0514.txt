<p><b>Gilbert Ramon Yvel</b> (born June 30, 1976) is a Dutch professional <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial artist</a>. A professional competitor since 1997, Yvel competed in the <a href="/wiki/Pride_Fighting_Championships" title="Pride Fighting Championships">PRIDE Fighting Championships</a>, <a href="/wiki/Affliction_Entertainment" title="Affliction Entertainment">Affliction</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Ultimate_Fighting_Championship" title="Ultimate Fighting Championship">UFC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Road_FC" class="mw-redirect" title="Road FC">Road FC</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Resurrection_Fighting_Alliance" class="mw-redirect" title="Resurrection Fighting Alliance">RFA</a>, <a href="/wiki/RINGS" class="mw-redirect" title="RINGS">RINGS</a>, <a href="/wiki/M-1_Challenge" title="M-1 Challenge">M-1 Challenge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cage_Rage_Championships" title="Cage Rage Championships">Cage Rage</a>, <a href="/wiki/K-1" title="K-1">K-1</a>, and Showtime promotions. He is the former RINGS Openweight Champion.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilbert_Yvel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yvel was born in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Surinamese</a> descent. He was raised as an orphan in the notorious neighborhood of the <a href="/wiki/Bijlmermeer" title="Bijlmermeer">Bijlmermeer</a> in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>. He holds a <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_jiu-jitsu" title="Brazilian jiu-jitsu">Brazilian jiu-jitsu</a> purple belt under John Lewis.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mixed_martial_arts_career">Mixed martial arts career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilbert_Yvel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mixed martial arts career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="RINGS">RINGS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilbert_Yvel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: RINGS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yvel came into contact with kickboxing and MMA through his brother, and after only two years of training he made his debut at 17 years of age, representing Vos Gym. Gilbert was off to a flying start winning his first 9 fights, primarily fighting for the <a href="/wiki/Fighting_Network_RINGS" class="mw-redirect" title="Fighting Network RINGS">Fighting Network RINGS</a> promotion, initially only in events hosted by its Dutch brand. One of his first opponents would be <a href="/wiki/Bas_Rutten" title="Bas Rutten">Bas Rutten</a>'s apprentice Leon Dijk, whom he defeated by KO, as well as "dirty" <a href="/wiki/Bob_Schrijber" title="Bob Schrijber">Bob Schrijber</a>. In another of his performances, he squared off against Russian fighter Vyacheslav Kiselyov in an event promoved by RINGS Holland leader <a href="/wiki/Chris_Dolman" title="Chris Dolman">Chris Dolman</a>. This fight would become controversial because Yvel, after getting his leg embraced by Kiselyov in a missed <a href="/wiki/Flying_knee" class="mw-redirect" title="Flying knee">flying knee</a>, grabbed the fence to keep himself vertical and then, still hanging off the cage, delivered several knees and axe kicks on his opponent's head. Yvel would go to win the match by knee strikes shortly after.
</p><p>His first loss came in 1998 in a rematch against Schrijber, being knocked out in the first round. Yvel had actually met his biological mother for the first time two days before the fight. He would follow with a participation representing RINGS Holland in the Russian tournament Pankration European Championship, where he would face RINGS Russia fighter Karimula Barkalaev. Although Yvel dominated the match, he was disqualified after Barkalaev's coach <a href="/wiki/Volk_Han" title="Volk Han">Volk Han</a> noted the referee Yvel had illegally bitten his fighter.
</p><p>In the following years Yvel came to be considered the best Dutch <a href="/wiki/Heavyweight_(MMA)" title="Heavyweight (MMA)">Heavyweight</a> fighter active on the European fighting circuit. He got a victory over teammate <a href="/wiki/Valentijn_Overeem" title="Valentijn Overeem">Valentijn Overeem</a> by TKO, as well as a high level one over RINGS England chief <a href="/wiki/Lee_Hasdell" title="Lee Hasdell">Lee Hasdell</a>. In April 1999, Yvel was finally called to Japan by RINGS, where he fought <a href="/wiki/Tsuyoshi_Kohsaka" class="mw-redirect" title="Tsuyoshi Kohsaka">Tsuyoshi Kohsaka</a>. Through the match, Yvel left himself behind by five points due to multiple fouls, which included grabbing the ropes, hitting the face on the ground, striking against an opponent on the knees and, in spectacular fashion, hanging himself off the turnbuckle in order to land a knee to the face. However, he rallied over in points with a palm strike and a tight <a href="/wiki/Guillotine_choke" title="Guillotine choke">guillotine choke</a>, and finally won the match when it was stopped due to accumulated damage to Kohsaka's face.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He then would fly back to Holland in order to face <a href="/wiki/Semmy_Schilt" title="Semmy Schilt">Semmy Schilt</a> in an interpromotional match. The bout was particularly intriguing because Schilt belonged to <a href="/wiki/Pancrase" title="Pancrase">Pancrase</a>, RINGS's rival promotion in Japan.
</p><p>The fight was met under special rules, with Gilbert keeping his gloves for punching while Schilt preferred to go barehanded and use open palm strikes like it was done in Pancrase. Although Schilt was considered a heavy favourite, sporting a huge advantage in both weight and height and boasting victories over <a href="/wiki/Masakatsu_Funaki" title="Masakatsu Funaki">Masakatsu Funaki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guy_Mezger" title="Guy Mezger">Guy Mezger</a>, Yvel proved himself at the first minutes of the match by scoring a shocking knockdown on Schilt by a flurry of hooks. The Pancrase fighter recovered with knees to the body and took Yvel down repeatedly in an attempt to submit him, but the ruleset's quick stand-ups and Yvel's own defensive acumen impeded any advance in this field. Meanwhile, while Schilt still won the earlier stand-up exchanges, Yvel eventually took over and bloodied Schilt's eye with punches and a possible <a href="/wiki/Eye_gouge" class="mw-redirect" title="Eye gouge">eye gouge</a> which was dismissed by the referee. At the end of several grinding assaults, Yvel overpowered Schilt and landed a long series of unanswered punches, finally knocking Schilt out on his feet for the victory.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his return to Japan, Yvel was scheduled in a rematch with Tsuyoshi Kohsaka. This time, however, Yvel was forced to spend a rope escape by an early <a href="/wiki/Leglock#Toe_hold" title="Leglock">toehold</a>, and shortly after had to defend on the ground after being thrown down by the judo specialist. With Gilbert behind on points, the match was stood up, but then it was suddenly stopped when Kohsaka, trying another judo throw, made both them fall outside the ring and was rendered injured by the fall. The bout was declared no contest, but as Yvel had lost a point while Kohsaka hadn't lost any of them, the Japanese was controversially declared winner by points.
</p><p>In December 1999, Yvel qualified for the King of Kings world tournament. He eliminated karate champion <a href="/wiki/Tariel_Bitsadze" title="Tariel Bitsadze">Tariel Bitsadze</a> by <a href="/wiki/Armbar" class="mw-redirect" title="Armbar">armbar</a> on the first round, and went to avenge his defeat to Kohsaka in the second, stopping him by a cut caused by a grazing punch. Yvel would face eventual winner <a href="/wiki/Dan_Henderson" title="Dan Henderson">Dan Henderson</a> in his block's final match. The American controlled the wrestling and seemed to have an armbar locked, but Yvel rolled out of it and utilized a strategy consistent on trying to strike between takedown and takedown. In one of those instances, he got a yellow card for landing an illegal elbow to Henderson's spine. At the end, although Yvel landed solid shots, he couldn't finish Henderson and was eliminated by unanimous decision.
</p><p>Yvel's last bout in RINGS would be against <a href="/wiki/Kiyoshi_Tamura" title="Kiyoshi Tamura">Kiyoshi Tamura</a> in a match for the RINGS Openweight Championship. Again, Yvel was taken down and positionally controlled on the mat, but he resisted successfully Tamura's scarce submission attempts and knocked him out via strikes. He won the title, but was forced to vacate it shortly after due to signing up with PRIDE.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Affliction">Affliction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilbert_Yvel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Affliction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2008 Yvel signed a three-fight deal with <a href="/wiki/Affliction_Entertainment" title="Affliction Entertainment">Affliction Entertainment</a>, his first and only fight on the contract was against <a href="/wiki/Josh_Barnett" title="Josh Barnett">Josh Barnett</a> at Affliction's January 24, 2009 "<a href="/wiki/Affliction:_Day_of_Reckoning" title="Affliction: Day of Reckoning">Day of Reckoning</a>" event in <a href="/wiki/Anaheim,_California" title="Anaheim, California">Anaheim, California</a> before Affliction abandoned MMA promotion.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
During the fight, Yvel showed great resilience against his <a href="/wiki/Wrestling" title="Wrestling">wrestling</a> opponent and kept an active guard which prevented Barnett from submitting him in the first two rounds, until Yvel finally succumbed to punches at the 3:05 mark of the third round.
</p><p>Yvel was scheduled to fight Chris Gathers at <a href="/wiki/Affliction:_Trilogy" class="mw-redirect" title="Affliction: Trilogy">Affliction: Trilogy</a>, a fight which never materialized due to the folding of the promotion.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ultimate_Fighting_Championship">Ultimate Fighting Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilbert_Yvel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ultimate Fighting Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yvel fought up-and-coming Brazilian Heavyweight <a href="/wiki/Junior_dos_Santos" title="Junior dos Santos">Junior dos Santos</a> at UFC 108<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> replacing <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Gonzaga" title="Gabriel Gonzaga">Gabriel Gonzaga</a> for the bout on January 2.  Yvel lost the fight, with Herb Dean stopping the fight at 2:07 in the first round due to strikes. Yvel protested the stoppage but congratulated Dos Santos for winning the fight moments later.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yvel next faced <a href="/wiki/Ben_Rothwell" title="Ben Rothwell">Ben Rothwell</a> at <a href="/wiki/UFC_115" title="UFC 115">UFC 115: Liddell vs. Franklin</a>. Yvel lost by unanimous decision (30–27, 29–28, and 29–28).
</p><p>Yvel then faced <a href="/wiki/Jon_Madsen" title="Jon Madsen">Jon Madsen</a> on October 23, 2010 at <a href="/wiki/UFC_121" title="UFC 121">UFC 121</a>, replacing an injured <a href="/wiki/Todd_Duffee" title="Todd Duffee">Todd Duffee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Both men circled early on in the fight before Madsen shot for a double-leg <a href="/wiki/Takedown_(grappling)" title="Takedown (grappling)">takedown</a> that downed Yvel after Gilbert slipped when his knee buckled, they ended up against the cage. Madsen then hit Yvel multiple times when Yvel's head was pressed up against the Octagon padding, resulting in the referee stopping the fight. Yvel was subsequently released from his UFC contract after the loss.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_UFC">Post UFC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilbert_Yvel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post UFC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Yvel was scheduled to face former <a href="/wiki/King_of_the_Cage" title="King of the Cage">King of the Cage</a> champion Tony Lopez in a <a href="/wiki/Pride_Fighting_Championships" title="Pride Fighting Championships">PRIDE</a> rules bout at Colosseo Championship Fighting 6: Bushido on July 23.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> However, the event was postponed and was then scheduled take place on September 3.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The event was subsequently canceled.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Gilbert Yvel cut down to 205-pounds to compete in the Light-Heavyweight division for Resurrection Fighting Alliance (RFA).  He was successful in his debut in this weight class, defeating Damian Dantibo at RFA 1 - Pulver vs. Elliott. The event took place on Friday, December 16, 2011 at the Viaero Event Center in Kearney, Nebraska. On March 30, 2012, Yvel faced <a href="/wiki/Houston_Alexander" title="Houston Alexander">Houston Alexander</a> at RFA 2 - Yvel vs. Alexander, winning by first-round knockout.
</p><p>Yvel was scheduled to fight against fellow UFC veteran and <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Jiu-Jitsu" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu">Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu</a> Champion <a href="/wiki/Marcio_Cruz" class="mw-redirect" title="Marcio Cruz">Marcio Cruz</a> on November 2, 2012 at Resurrection Fighting Alliance 4 in Las Vegas, Nevada.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> but he was forced out of the bout due to an injury.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yvel announced his retirement in an interview with Ground and Pound TV. He plans to become a trainer at Agoge MMA in Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yvel also has fought two kickboxing matches, losing to <a href="/wiki/Ray_Sefo" title="Ray Sefo">Ray Sefo</a> and beating <a href="/wiki/Yuji_Sakuragi" title="Yuji Sakuragi">Yuji Sakuragi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bad_conduct_and_subsequent_rehabilitation">Bad conduct and subsequent rehabilitation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gilbert_Yvel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Bad conduct and subsequent rehabilitation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yvel has acquired, through a number of instances, a lingering reputation in the MMA community for his temper and poor sportsmanship in the ring earlier in his career.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-espn_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5oz_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5oz-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his May 1998 bout with Karimula Barkalaev, Yvel was disqualified for biting his opponent.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5oz_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5oz-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his September 2001 match with <a href="/wiki/Don_Frye" title="Don Frye">Don Frye</a>, Yvel repeatedly raked his fingers across Frye's face and <a href="/wiki/Eye_gouge" class="mw-redirect" title="Eye gouge">pushed his thumb</a> into Frye's eyes to avoid being wrestled to the mat. Yvel was disqualified in this match, as well, although the official reason given was for grabbing the ropes.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5oz_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5oz-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During Yvel's November 2004 match with Atte Backman, the referee separated the fighters as they were falling over the ropes in a clinched position. Yvel refused the attempts by the referee to get the fighters to resume the clinched position away from ropes, swatting the referee's hand away when he attempted to guide the fighters back together. After a number of unsuccessful attempts to restart the fight in the clinched position, Yvel suddenly punched the referee in the head, dropping him to the canvas, and then kicked him while he was down. This was Yvel's 3rd disqualification in six years.
</p><p>A scheduled <a href="/wiki/PRIDE_33" class="mw-redirect" title="PRIDE 33">PRIDE 33</a> match in 2007 with <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Kharitonov" title="Sergei Kharitonov">Sergei Kharitonov</a> had to be cancelled when the Nevada State Athletic Commission (NSAC) refused to grant Yvel a license to fight in their state due to his history of poor conduct.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-si_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-si-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yvel's 2009 <a href="/wiki/Affliction_Entertainment" title="Affliction Entertainment">Affliction</a> match-up with <a href="/wiki/Josh_Barnett" title="Josh Barnett">Josh Barnett</a> was scheduled to take place in California. Again, because of his past behaviour, there was debate over whether he should be allowed to have a fight license. After taking the unusual step of arranging a personal meeting between Yvel, Assistant Executive Officer Bill Douglas and Chief Athletic Inspector Dean Lohuis, the <a href="/wiki/California_State_Athletic_Commission" title="California State Athletic Commission">California State Athletic Commission</a> (CSAC) did license him to fight there.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_16-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5oz_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5oz-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-si_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-si-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2009, after a new application, the NSAC again declined to grant Yvel a standard license but did grant him a limited, 1-fight only license to fight in Nevada due to his good conduct since his last instance of poor behaviour in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
