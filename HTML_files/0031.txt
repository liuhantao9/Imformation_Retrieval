	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Blender</i> (magazine)</h1>

<p><i><b>Blender</b></i> was an American <a href="/wiki/Music_magazine" title="Music magazine">music magazine</a> that billed itself as "the ultimate guide to music and more". It was also known for sometimes steamy pictorials of celebrities. It compiled lists of albums, artists, and songs, including both "best of" and "worst of" lists. In each issue, there was a review of an artist's entire <a href="/wiki/Discography" title="Discography">discography</a>, with each album being analyzed in turn.
</p><p><i>Blender</i> was published by <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Publishing" title="Dennis Publishing">Dennis Publishing</a>. The magazine began in 1994 as the first digital <a href="/wiki/CD-ROM" title="CD-ROM">CD-ROM</a> magazine by Jason Pearson, David Cherry, and Regina Joseph, acquired by Felix Dennis/Dennis Publishing, UK it published 15 digital <a href="/wiki/Compact_disc" title="Compact disc">CD</a> issues, and launched on the web in 1997. It started publishing a print edition again in 1999 in its most recent form. Blender CD-ROM showcased the earliest digital editorial formats, as well as the first forms of digital advertising. The first digital advertisers included <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Klein" title="Calvin Klein">Calvin Klein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apple_Computer" class="mw-redirect" title="Apple Computer">Apple Computer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toyota" title="Toyota">Toyota</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a>.
</p><p>In June 2006, the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Tribune" title="Chicago Tribune">Chicago Tribune</a></i> named it one of the top ten English-language magazines, describing it as "the cool kid at the school of rock magazines".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Owner Alpha Media Group closed <i>Blender</i> magazine March 26, 2009, going to an online-only format in a move that eliminated 30 jobs and reduced the company's portfolio of titles to <a href="/wiki/Maxim_(magazine)" title="Maxim (magazine)"><i>Maxim</i></a> alone. <i>Blender</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;s</span> final print issue was the April 2009 issue.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Subscribers to the magazine were sent issues of <i>Maxim</i> magazine to make up for the unsent <i>Blender</i> issues.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Indian_edition">Indian edition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blender_(magazine)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Indian edition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="infobox hproduct" style="width:22em"><caption class="fn"><i>Blender India</i></caption><tbody><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center"><a href="/wiki/File:Priyanka_Chopa_Blender_India_November_2008_cover.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Priyanka Chopa Blender India November 2008 cover.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/78/Priyanka_Chopa_Blender_India_November_2008_cover.jpg/220px-Priyanka_Chopa_Blender_India_November_2008_cover.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="297" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/7/78/Priyanka_Chopa_Blender_India_November_2008_cover.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="272" data-file-height="367" /></a><div><a href="/wiki/Priyanka_Chopra" title="Priyanka Chopra">Priyanka Chopra</a> on the November 2008 cover of the Indian edition of <i>Blender</i></div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Editor in Chief</th><td>Sam Lal<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Categories</th><td><a href="/wiki/Music_magazine" title="Music magazine">Music</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Publisher</th><td>Piyush Sharma<sup id="cite_ref-dmnews1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dmnews1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">First issue</th><td>May&#160;2008<span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="bday dtstart published updated">2008-May</span>)</span></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Company</th><td>Dennis Media Transasia India</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Country</th><td><a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Based in</th><td><a href="/wiki/New_Delhi" title="New Delhi">New Delhi</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Language</th><td><a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> edition of <i>Blender</i> was the title's first venture outside the United States. It commenced publication with its May 2008 issue, which featured <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> on the cover.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The magazine was targeted at educated male city dwellers aged between 18 and 34.<sup id="cite_ref-dmnews1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dmnews1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The magazine was launched through Dennis Media Transasia India, a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> between Dennis Publishing and Media Transasia,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> which also publishes the Asian versions of <i>Blender</i> and <i>Maxim</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The joint venture was based in New Delhi<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> with offices in Bangalore, Chennai, Kolkata and Mumbai.
</p>
