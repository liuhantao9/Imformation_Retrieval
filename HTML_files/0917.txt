<p><b>Riverdale Country School</b> is a co-educational, independent, college-preparatory day school in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> serving <a href="/wiki/Pre-kindergarten" title="Pre-kindergarten">pre-kindergarten</a> through <a href="/wiki/Twelfth_grade" title="Twelfth grade">twelfth grade</a>. It is located on two campuses covering more than 27 acres (110,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) in the <a href="/wiki/Riverdale,_The_Bronx" class="mw-redirect" title="Riverdale, The Bronx">Riverdale</a> section of <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, United States. Started as a school for boys, Riverdale Country School became fully coeducational in 1972. It currently serves 1,140 students. According to <a href="/wiki/Niche_(company)" title="Niche (company)">Niche</a>'s 2018 Private School Rankings, Riverdale is ranked the 5<sup>th</sup> best private high school in New York City and the 28<sup>th</sup> best private high school in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riverdale_Country_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hackett_Hall_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Hackett_Hall_2016.jpg/220px-Hackett_Hall_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Hackett_Hall_2016.jpg/330px-Hackett_Hall_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Hackett_Hall_2016.jpg/440px-Hackett_Hall_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="673" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hackett_Hall_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hackett Hall on the Hill Campus of Riverdale Country School</div></div></div>
<p>Founded in 1907 by Frank Sutliff Hackett (1878–1952) and his first wife, Francis Dean (Allen) Hackett, Riverdale Country School is one of the oldest <a href="/wiki/Country_Day_School_movement" title="Country Day School movement">country day schools</a> in the United States. Originally known as the Riverdale School for Boys, it began with 12 students and four teachers and promised scholarly, intimate teaching amid abundant recreational space.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Describing his school as "an American experiment in education," Hackett later told <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>: "We have tried to transform schools from mere nurseries of the brain into a means of nourishing the whole boy — mind, body, and spirit."<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>An early advocate of outdoor experiences for young people, Hackett started a summer camp known as Camp Riverdale at Long Lake in the Adirondacks (1912–1964) to provide summer recreation for his students.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> By 1920, Hackett had acquired three acres on Fieldston Road for the school and built a classroom building and dormitory. The 100-room dormitory, designed by McKim, Mead, and White,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> later became known as Hackett Hall. In 1924, he started the Neighborhood School for boys and girls from grades one to three, and in 1933, the Riverdale Girls School. By World War II, the school attracted both day students and boarders; one student in six or seven came from another country.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hackett dreamed of expanding the school into an "American World School" and acquired a new site in Riverdale.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 1948, Gen. Dwight Eisenhower, then president of Columbia University, spoke at a dedication ceremony at the site.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Hackett died before his dream was realized, and the property was sold. In 1972, the Boys and Girls Schools were combined.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1985, the Middle and Upper Schools were consolidated on the Hill Campus and the Lower School moved to the River Campus. Hackett was an organizer of the Guild of Independent Schools of New York City, and an organizer and president of the <a href="/wiki/Adirondack_Mountain_Club" title="Adirondack Mountain Club">Adirondack Mountain Club</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/President_John_F._Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="President John F. Kennedy">President John F. Kennedy</a> attended the school from 1927 to 1930 when his family lived in Riverdale. During the 1960 presidential campaign, Kennedy made an appearance in the Bronx. "I said up the street that I was a former resident of the Bronx," he said. "Nobody believes that, but it is true. No other candidate for the presidency can make that statement."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campuses">Campuses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riverdale_Country_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Campuses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mow_Hall_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mow_Hall_2016.jpg/220px-Mow_Hall_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mow_Hall_2016.jpg/330px-Mow_Hall_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mow_Hall_2016.jpg/440px-Mow_Hall_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="673" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mow_Hall_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mow Hall on the Hill Campus of Riverdale Country School</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Upper_Learning_Building,_Riverdale_Country_School.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Upper_Learning_Building%2C_Riverdale_Country_School.jpg/240px-Upper_Learning_Building%2C_Riverdale_Country_School.jpg" decoding="async" width="240" height="179" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Upper_Learning_Building%2C_Riverdale_Country_School.jpg/360px-Upper_Learning_Building%2C_Riverdale_Country_School.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Upper_Learning_Building%2C_Riverdale_Country_School.jpg/480px-Upper_Learning_Building%2C_Riverdale_Country_School.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="673" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Upper_Learning_Building,_Riverdale_Country_School.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Upper Learning Building, completed in 2016, on the River Campus of Riverdale Country School</div></div></div>
<p>Covering 19.5 acres (79,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>), the Hill Campus is located on Fieldston Road, overlooking Van Cortlandt Park. It is home to the Middle School (6<sup>th</sup> to 8<sup>th</sup>) and the Upper School (9<sup>th</sup> to 12<sup>th</sup>). Facilities include Hackett Hall, Mow Hall, Lindenbaum Center for the Arts, the 9/10 Building, Vinik Hall (the Admissions Building), the Weinstein Science Building, the Science Annex, the Day Care, and the P. Gordon B. Stillman Amphitheater. A new Aquatic Center opened in 2017. The Hill Campus has three playing fields (Frankel Field, Alumni Field, and the Frank J. Bertino Memorial field) and tennis courts. The Marc A. Zambetti '80 Athletic Center includes a gymnasium, fencing room, and workout room. It is currently being renovated to add a second gymnasium and an expanded health/fitness center.
</p><p>The River Campus (Pre-K to 5<sup>th</sup>) sits on 8 acres (32,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) along the Hudson River. The buildings on the River Campus are the Early Learning Building (Pre-K through second grade classrooms and the gymnasium), the Senior Building (classrooms for drawing, painting, and sculpture), the Admissions/Junior building (includes the Lower School head's office, the Learning Commons, and Admissions, and the Upper Learning Building. Completed in 2016, the Upper Learning Building includes a theater, cafeteria, classrooms for third through fifth grade, and multi-purpose spaces. Architectural Record noted: "It is a building born of exploration, deep discussions with educators about their needs and desires, and a willingness to tweak on the fly." <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The River Campus also has tennis courts, a playing field, a greenhouse, gardens, and a playground. The land for the River Campus was given to Riverdale by the family of <a href="/wiki/George_Walbridge_Perkins" title="George Walbridge Perkins">George Walbridge Perkins</a>, an early environmentalist and associate of <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Roosevelt" title="Theodore Roosevelt">Theodore Roosevelt</a> and <a href="/wiki/J.P._Morgan" class="mw-redirect" title="J.P. Morgan">J.P. Morgan</a>. The family also donated their adjacent property, <a href="/wiki/Wave_Hill" title="Wave Hill">Wave Hill</a>, to the City of New York for a public garden and cultural center.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academics">Academics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riverdale_Country_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Academics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Becoming Riverdale's sixth head of school in 2007, Dominic A.A. Randolph refined the school’s mission with a focus on “Mind, Character, and Community.” He eliminated courses tied to Advanced Placement tests, and advocated a teaching philosophy centered on building character strengths.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Working with <a href="/wiki/Angela_Duckworth" title="Angela Duckworth">Angela Duckworth</a>, a psychologist at the University of Pennsylvania and Dave Levin, the co-founder of the KIPP network of charter schools, he co-founded Character Lab, an organization dedicated to providing research-based approaches to character education. In 2016, Riverdale Country School launched Plussed, a non-profit designed to "create a dynamic collective of experts on learning designing ways to improve student learning and education." <sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> To encourage innovative teaching, Randolph has advocated design thinking, a methodology used to create and test new ideas. Riverdale teachers collaborated with the design firm, IDEO, on the Design Thinking Toolkit for Educators in 2011. <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> That work led to the formation of The Teachers Guild, in 2015. <sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The Teachers Guild is a partnership between IDEO and Riverdale Country School's Plussed initiative that provides teachers with a forum for sharing innovating teaching strategies.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to college-preparatory courses in math, science, and humanities, Riverdale offers “maker” programs that combine science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics, as well as courses in robotics and coding. Latin, French, Spanish, Chinese, and Japanese also are offered. Interdisciplinary courses in the Lower, Middle, and Upper Schools encourage students to draw connections across disciplines. Constructing America, a required course for juniors, is co-taught by the History and English faculty. Integrated Liberal Studies, a required course for seniors, explores four essential topics: Virtue, the Self, Social Justice, and the Environment. Independent study opportunities allow students to gain in-depth knowledge in subjects of their choosing. The Global Studies program organizes trips around the world. An outdoor educator organizes hiking, camping and rock-climbing trips in the Hudson Valley, trekking in Patagonia, and kayaking in Alaska.
</p><p>Riverdale is chartered by the New York State Board of Regents and is accredited by the New York State Association of Independent Schools.<sup id="cite_ref-stats_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arts_and_activities">Arts and activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riverdale_Country_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Arts and activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Upper School students produce one musical and one play each year in the Jeslo Harris theatre. Riverdale students may participate in the jazz and concert bands, orchestra, chamber music ensembles, chorus, dance team, and the a cappella singing groups, the Rivertones and Testostertones. More than 40 student-led clubs, organizations, and service-learning partnerships are offered.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Associations">Associations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Riverdale_Country_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Associations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Riverdale is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Ivy_Preparatory_School_League" title="Ivy Preparatory School League">Ivy Preparatory School League</a> and the <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Association_of_Independent_Schools" title="New York State Association of Independent Schools">New York State Association of Independent Schools</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nysais_86-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nysais-86">&#91;86&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Riverdale Country School, <a href="/wiki/The_Fieldston_School" class="mw-redirect" title="The Fieldston School">The Fieldston School</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Horace_Mann_School" title="Horace Mann School">Horace Mann School</a> together are known as the "Hill Schools," as all three are located within two miles (3&#160;km) of each other in the neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Riverdale,_The_Bronx" class="mw-redirect" title="Riverdale, The Bronx">Riverdale</a> on a hilly area above <a href="/wiki/Van_Cortlandt_Park" title="Van Cortlandt Park">Van Cortlandt Park</a>.
</p>
