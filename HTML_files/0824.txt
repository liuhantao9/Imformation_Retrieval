<p>A <b>service star</b> is a miniature bronze or silver <a href="/wiki/Five-pointed_star" title="Five-pointed star">five-pointed star</a> &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>16</sub></span> inch (4.8 mm) in diameter that is authorized to be worn by members of the seven <a href="/wiki/Uniformed_services_of_the_United_States" title="Uniformed services of the United States">uniformed services of the United States</a> on medals and ribbons to denote an additional award or service period.<sup id="cite_ref-DODM1348.33V3-SS_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DODM1348.33V3-SS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The service star may also be referred to as a <b>campaign star</b> or <b>battle star</b> depending on which award is authorized the star and the manner in which the device is used for the award.<sup id="cite_ref-DODM1348.33V3-CS_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DODM1348.33V3-CS-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Service stars, campaign stars, and battle stars are worn with one point of the star pointing up on the suspension ribbon of a medal or <a href="/wiki/Service_ribbon" title="Service ribbon">service ribbon</a>. A silver star is worn instead of five bronze stars.<sup id="cite_ref-DODM1348.33V3-SS_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DODM1348.33V3-SS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> A service star is sometimes mistaken for a <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Star_Medal" title="Bronze Star Medal">Bronze Star</a> (Bronze Star Medal) or <a href="/wiki/Silver_Star" title="Silver Star">Silver Star</a> (Silver Star Medal). The service star is also similar to the gold and silver <a href="/wiki/5/16_inch_star" title="5/16 inch star">&#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>5</sup>&#8260;<sub>16</sub></span> Inch Stars</a> which may be authorized to be worn on specific <a href="/wiki/5/16_inch_star#Decorations_and_awards" title="5/16 inch star">individual decorations</a> of certain services to denote additional decorations.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_stars">Service stars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Service_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Service stars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Expeditionary_medals">Expeditionary medals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Service_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Expeditionary medals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Service stars are authorized for these United States expeditionary medals:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_Expeditionary_Medal" title="Armed Forces Expeditionary Medal">Armed Forces Expeditionary Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Navy_Expeditionary_Medal" title="Navy Expeditionary Medal">Navy Expeditionary Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Marine_Corps_Expeditionary_Medal" title="Marine Corps Expeditionary Medal">Marine Corps Expeditionary Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Global_War_on_Terrorism_Expeditionary_Medal" title="Global War on Terrorism Expeditionary Medal">Global War on Terrorism Expeditionary Medal</a> (GWOT-EM) effective February 9, 2015, retroactive to September 11, 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-GWOTEM-SS_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GWOTEM-SS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Each star represents a deployment in support of an approved GWOT operation. Four bronze service stars are authorized for five approved deployment operations (only one GWOT-EM is awarded for each operation). The five GWOT-EM-approved operations by inclusive dates are:<sup id="cite_ref-GWOTEM-SS_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GWOTEM-SS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<dl><dd><a href="/wiki/Operation_Enduring_Freedom" title="Operation Enduring Freedom">Enduring Freedom</a>: Sep. 11, 2001 – TBD (to be determined)</dd>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Operation_Iraqi_Freedom" class="mw-redirect" title="Operation Iraqi Freedom">Iraqi Freedom</a>: Mar. 19, 2003 – Aug. 31, 2010</dd>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Nomad_Shadow" title="Nomad Shadow">Nomad Shadow</a>: Nov. 05, 2007 – TBD</dd>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Operation_New_Dawn_(Iraq,_2010%E2%80%932011)" class="mw-redirect" title="Operation New Dawn (Iraq, 2010–2011)">New Dawn</a>: Sep. 01, 2010 – Dec. 31, 2011</dd>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Operation_Inherent_Resolve" title="Operation Inherent Resolve">Inherent Resolve</a>: Jun. 15, 2014 – TBD</dd></dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_medals">Service medals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Service_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Service medals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Service stars are authorized to denoted additional awards for these United States <a href="/wiki/Service_medals" class="mw-redirect" title="Service medals">service medals</a>:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Prisoner_of_War_Medal" title="Prisoner of War Medal">Prisoner of War Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/National_Defense_Service_Medal" title="National Defense Service Medal">National Defense Service Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Humanitarian_Service_Medal" title="Humanitarian Service Medal">Humanitarian Service Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Air_and_Space_Campaign_Medal" title="Air and Space Campaign Medal">Air and Space Campaign Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_Service_Medal" title="Armed Forces Service Medal">Armed Forces Service Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sea_Service_Ribbon" title="Sea Service Ribbon">Army Sea Duty Ribbon</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Military_Outstanding_Volunteer_Service_Medal" title="Military Outstanding Volunteer Service Medal">Military Outstanding Volunteer Service Medal</a><sup id="cite_ref-DODM1348.33V3-SS_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DODM1348.33V3-SS-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Unit_awards">Unit awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Service_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Unit awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Service stars are authorized for certain unit awards (the service ribbon itself indicates the first award, with a bronze service star being added to indicate the second and subsequent awards. If ever applicable, a silver service star is worn instead of five bronze stars) such as the:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Presidential_Unit_Citation_(United_States)" title="Presidential Unit Citation (United States)">Presidential Unit Citation</a> (Navy and Marine Corps)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Navy_Unit_Commendation" title="Navy Unit Commendation">Navy Unit Commendation</a> (Navy and Marine Corps)</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaign_stars">Campaign stars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Service_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Campaign stars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Campaign stars are authorized for these United States <a href="/wiki/Campaign_medal" title="Campaign medal">campaign medals</a> (bronze and silver campaign stars are worn to denote participation in a designated campaign or campaign phase or period):<sup id="cite_ref-DODM1348.33V3-CS_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DODM1348.33V3-CS-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/World_War_I_Victory_Medal_(United_States)" title="World War I Victory Medal (United States)">World War I Victory Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/American_Defense_Service_Medal" title="American Defense Service Medal">American Defense Service Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/American_Campaign_Medal" title="American Campaign Medal">American Campaign Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Asiatic%E2%80%93Pacific_Campaign_Medal" title="Asiatic–Pacific Campaign Medal">Asiatic–Pacific Campaign Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/European%E2%80%93African%E2%80%93Middle_Eastern_Campaign_Medal" title="European–African–Middle Eastern Campaign Medal">European–African–Middle Eastern Campaign Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Korean_Service_Medal" title="Korean Service Medal">Korean Service Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vietnam_Service_Medal" title="Vietnam Service Medal">Vietnam Service Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Southwest_Asia_Service_Medal" title="Southwest Asia Service Medal">Southwest Asia Service Medal</a>*</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kosovo_Campaign_Medal" title="Kosovo Campaign Medal">Kosovo Campaign Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Afghanistan_Campaign_Medal" title="Afghanistan Campaign Medal">Afghanistan Campaign Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Iraq_Campaign_Medal" title="Iraq Campaign Medal">Iraq Campaign Medal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Inherent_Resolve_Campaign_Medal" title="Inherent Resolve Campaign Medal">Inherent Resolve Campaign Medal</a></li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Battle_stars">Battle stars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Service_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Battle stars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since February 26, 2004, the Global War on Terrorism Expeditionary Medal and the <a href="/wiki/Global_War_on_Terrorism_Service_Medal" title="Global War on Terrorism Service Medal">Global War on Terrorism Service Medal (GWOT-SM)</a> are authorized to be awarded with bronze and silver battle stars for personnel who were engaged in specific battles in combat under circumstances involving grave danger of death or serious bodily injury from enemy action.<sup id="cite_ref-DODM1348.33V3-BtS_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DODM1348.33V3-BtS-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GWOTMs-BS_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GWOTMs-BS-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> However, though authorized for wear, no battle stars have been approved for wear. Only a <a href="/wiki/Combatant_commander" class="mw-redirect" title="Combatant commander">combatant commander</a> can initiate a request for a battle star, and the Chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Joint_Chiefs_of_Staff" title="Joint Chiefs of Staff">Joint Chiefs of Staff</a> is the approving authority,<sup id="cite_ref-GWOTMs-BS_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GWOTMs-BS-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> which since January 2016 has been eliminated by the Department of Defense for the GWOT-SM.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Only one award of the Global War on Terrorism Expeditionary Medal and one award of the Global War on Terrorism Service Medal may be authorized for any individual. No service stars were authorized for the Global War on Terrorism Expeditionary or Service Medal<sup id="cite_ref-GWOTMs-BS_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GWOTMs-BS-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> until February 9, 2015, when the Department of Defense authorized service stars for the Global War on Terrorism Expeditionary Medal retroactive to September 11, 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-GWOTEM-SS_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GWOTEM-SS-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Earlier_service_stars_and_battle_stars">Earlier service stars and battle stars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Service_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Earlier service stars and battle stars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Service stars (were sometimes referred to as campaign stars or battle stars) were also authorized for the <a href="/wiki/World_War_I_Victory_Medal_(United_States)" title="World War I Victory Medal (United States)">World War I Victory Medal</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Defense_Service_Medal" title="American Defense Service Medal">American Defense Service Medal</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Campaign_Medal" title="American Campaign Medal">American Campaign Medal</a>, <a href="/wiki/European%E2%80%93African%E2%80%93Middle_Eastern_Campaign_Medal" title="European–African–Middle Eastern Campaign Medal">European–African–Middle Eastern Campaign Medal</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Asiatic%E2%80%93Pacific_Campaign_Medal" title="Asiatic–Pacific Campaign Medal">Asiatic–Pacific Campaign Medal</a>. The specific manner of wear and symbolism of the stars varied from medal to medal. For example, an <a href="/wiki/American_Campaign_Medal" title="American Campaign Medal">American Campaign Medal</a> with a bronze service star indicated the service member had participated in an antisubmarine campaign. On other medals, bronze service stars were used on the medal's service ribbon for those recipients of medals in possession of authorized campaign clasps for those medals.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Navy_warships">Navy warships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Service_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Navy warships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Historically, during <a href="/wiki/Most_decorated_US_ships_of_World_War_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Most decorated US ships of World War II">World War II</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a>, commendations called "battle stars" were also issued to United States Navy warships for meritorious participation in battle, or for having suffered damage during battle conditions. As an example, the <a href="/wiki/USS_Enterprise_(CV-6)#Awards_and_commendations" title="USS Enterprise (CV-6)">USS <i>Enterprise</i> (CV-6)</a> received 20 Battle Stars for her combat service in World War II. Similarly, during the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a> and afterwards, the <a href="/wiki/Battle_Effectiveness_Award" title="Battle Effectiveness Award">Battle Effectiveness Award</a> ("Battle E") took the place of receiving "battle stars" for superior battle efficiency in place of combat operations.
</p>
