<p><b>ISO 3166</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Standardization" title="Standardization">standard</a> published by the <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">International Organization for Standardization</a> (ISO) that defines <a href="/wiki/Code" title="Code">codes</a> for the names of <a href="/wiki/Country" title="Country">countries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dependent_territory" title="Dependent territory">dependent territories</a>, special areas of geographical interest, and their principal <a href="/wiki/Country_subdivision" class="mw-redirect" title="Country subdivision">subdivisions</a> (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Province" title="Province">provinces</a> or <a href="/wiki/State_(administrative_division)" class="mw-redirect" title="State (administrative division)">states</a>). The official name of the standard is <i><b>Codes for the representation of names of countries and their subdivisions</b></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Parts">Parts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO_3166&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Parts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It consists of three parts:<sup id="cite_ref-iso-info_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iso-info-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-1" title="ISO 3166-1">ISO 3166-1</a></b>, <i>Codes for the representation of names of countries and their subdivisions – Part 1: Country codes</i>, defines codes for the names of countries, dependent territories, and special areas of geographical interest. It defines three sets of <a href="/wiki/Country_code" title="Country code">country codes</a>:
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-1_alpha-2" title="ISO 3166-1 alpha-2">ISO 3166-1 alpha-2</a> – two-letter country codes which are the most widely used of the three, and used most prominently for the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Country_code_top-level_domain" title="Country code top-level domain">country code top-level domains</a> (with a few exceptions).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-1_alpha-3" title="ISO 3166-1 alpha-3">ISO 3166-1 alpha-3</a> – three-letter country codes which allow a better visual association between the codes and the country names than the alpha-2 codes.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-1_numeric" title="ISO 3166-1 numeric">ISO 3166-1 numeric</a> – three-digit country codes which are identical to those developed and maintained by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Statistics_Division" title="United Nations Statistics Division">United Nations Statistics Division</a>, with the advantage of script (<a href="/wiki/Writing_system" title="Writing system">writing system</a>) independence, and hence useful for people or systems using non-<a href="/wiki/Latin_script" title="Latin script">Latin scripts</a>.</li></ul></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-2" title="ISO 3166-2">ISO 3166-2</a></b>, <i>Codes for the representation of names of countries and their subdivisions – Part 2: Country subdivision code</i>, defines codes for the names of the principal subdivisions (e.g., provinces, states, departments, regions) of all countries coded in ISO 3166-1.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/ISO_3166-3" title="ISO 3166-3">ISO 3166-3</a></b>, <i>Codes for the representation of names of countries and their subdivisions – Part 3: Code for formerly used names of countries</i>, defines codes for country names which have been deleted from ISO 3166-1 since its first publication in 1974.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Editions">Editions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO_3166&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Editions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first edition of ISO 3166 was published in 1974, which included only alphabetic country codes. The second edition, published in 1981, also included numeric country codes, with the third and fourth editions published in 1988 and 1993 respectively. The fifth edition, published between 1997 and 1999, was expanded into three parts to include codes for subdivisions and former countries.<sup id="cite_ref-iso-info_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iso-info-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="ISO_3166_Maintenance_Agency">ISO 3166 Maintenance Agency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO_3166&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: ISO 3166 Maintenance Agency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The ISO 3166 standard is maintained by the <b>ISO 3166 Maintenance Agency</b> (<b>ISO 3166/MA</b>), located at the ISO central office in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>. Originally it was located at the <a href="/wiki/Deutsches_Institut_f%C3%BCr_Normung" title="Deutsches Institut für Normung">Deutsches Institut für Normung</a> (DIN) in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>. Its principal tasks are:<sup id="cite_ref-iso-info_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iso-info-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>To add and to eliminate country names (respectively, country subdivision names) and to assign code elements to them;</li>
<li>To publish lists of country names (respectively, country subdivision names) and code elements;</li>
<li>To maintain a reference list of all country code elements and country subdivision code elements used and their period of use;</li>
<li>To issue newsletters announcing changes to the code tables;</li>
<li>To advise users on the application of ISO 3166.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Members">Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ISO_3166&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are fifteen experts with voting rights on the ISO 3166/MA.<sup id="cite_ref-iso-info_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iso-info-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Nine are representatives of national <a href="/wiki/Standards_organization" title="Standards organization">standards organizations</a>:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Association_fran%C3%A7aise_de_normalisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Association française de normalisation">Association française de normalisation</a> (AFNOR) – <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/American_National_Standards_Institute" title="American National Standards Institute">American National Standards Institute</a> (ANSI) – <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/British_Standards_Institution" class="mw-redirect" title="British Standards Institution">British Standards Institution</a> (BSI) – <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Deutsches_Institut_f%C3%BCr_Normung" title="Deutsches Institut für Normung">Deutsches Institut für Normung</a> (DIN) – <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Japanese_Industrial_Standards_Committee" title="Japanese Industrial Standards Committee">Japanese Industrial Standards Committee</a> (JISC) - <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Standards_Australia" title="Standards Australia">Standards Australia</a> (SA) - <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a></li>
<li>Kenya Bureau of Standards (KEBS) - <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Standardization_Administration_of_China" title="Standardization Administration of China">Standardization Administration of China</a> (SAC) - <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Swedish_Standards_Institute" class="mw-redirect" title="Swedish Standards Institute">Swedish Standards Institute</a> (SIS) – <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a></li></ul>
<p>The other six are representatives of major <a href="/wiki/List_of_specialized_agencies_of_the_United_Nations" title="List of specialized agencies of the United Nations">United Nations agencies</a> or other <a href="/wiki/International_organization" title="International organization">international organizations</a> who are all users of ISO 3166-1:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/International_Atomic_Energy_Agency" title="International Atomic Energy Agency">International Atomic Energy Agency</a> (IAEA)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/International_Civil_Aviation_Organization" title="International Civil Aviation Organization">International Civil Aviation Organization</a> (ICAO)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/International_Telecommunication_Union" title="International Telecommunication Union">International Telecommunication Union</a> (ITU)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Internet_Corporation_for_Assigned_Names_and_Numbers" class="mw-redirect" title="Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers">Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers</a> (ICANN)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Universal_Postal_Union" title="Universal Postal Union">Universal Postal Union</a> (UPU)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Economic_Commission_for_Europe" title="United Nations Economic Commission for Europe">United Nations Economic Commission for Europe</a> (UNECE)</li></ul>
<p>The ISO 3166/MA has further associated members who do not participate in the votes but who, through their expertise, have significant influence on the decision-taking procedure in the maintenance agency.
</p>
