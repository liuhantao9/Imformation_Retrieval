</p><p>The <b>Mayo Clinic</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit" class="mw-redirect" title="Nonprofit">nonprofit</a> <a href="/wiki/Medical_centers_in_the_United_States" title="Medical centers in the United States">academic medical center</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_Minnesota" title="Rochester, Minnesota">Rochester, Minnesota</a>, focused on integrated clinical practice, <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_College_of_Medicine_and_Science" title="Mayo Clinic College of Medicine and Science">education</a>, and research.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It employs more than 4,500 physicians and scientists, along with another 58,400 administrative and allied health staff.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mayoinfo_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mayoinfo-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The practice specializes in treating difficult cases through <a href="/wiki/Health_care#Tertiary_care" title="Health care">tertiary care</a> and <a href="/wiki/Medical_tourism#United_States" title="Medical tourism">destination medicine</a>. It is home to the top-ten ranked <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_Alix_School_of_Medicine" title="Mayo Clinic Alix School of Medicine">Mayo Clinic Alix School of Medicine</a> in addition to many of the largest, best regarded residency education programs in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It spends over $660 million a year on research and has more than 3,000 full-time research personnel.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/William_Worrall_Mayo" title="William Worrall Mayo">William Worrall Mayo</a> settled his family in Rochester in 1864 and opened a sole proprietorship medical practice that evolved under his sons, <a href="/wiki/William_James_Mayo" title="William James Mayo">Will</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Horace_Mayo" title="Charles Horace Mayo">Charlie Mayo</a>, along with practice partners Drs. Stinchfield, Graham, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Stanley_Plummer" title="Henry Stanley Plummer">Henry Plummer</a>, Millet, Judd, and <a href="/wiki/Donald_Balfour" title="Donald Balfour">Donald Balfour</a> into Mayo Clinic. Today, in addition to its flagship hospital in Rochester, Mayo Clinic has major campuses in Arizona<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_Florida" title="Mayo Clinic Florida">in Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The Mayo Clinic Health System also operates affiliated facilities throughout <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mayo Clinic is ranked number 1 in the United States for 2019-20<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/U.S._News_%26_World_Report" title="U.S. News &amp; World Report">U.S. News &amp; World Report</a></i> Best Hospitals Honor Roll, maintaining a position at or near the top for more than 27 years.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> It has been on the list of "<a href="/wiki/100_Best_Companies_to_Work_For" title="100 Best Companies to Work For">100 Best Companies to Work For</a>" published by <a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)"><i>Fortune</i> magazine</a> for fourteen consecutive years, and has continued to achieve this ranking through 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;"><a href="/wiki/File:William_Worrall_Mayo.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/William_Worrall_Mayo.jpg/190px-William_Worrall_Mayo.jpg" decoding="async" width="190" height="231" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/William_Worrall_Mayo.jpg/285px-William_Worrall_Mayo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/William_Worrall_Mayo.jpg/380px-William_Worrall_Mayo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="394" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:William_Worrall_Mayo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/William_Worrall_Mayo" title="William Worrall Mayo">William Worrall Mayo</a>, founder of Mayo Clinic</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1863, <a href="/wiki/William_Worrall_Mayo" title="William Worrall Mayo">William Worrall Mayo</a> (1819–1911) came to <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_Minnesota" title="Rochester, Minnesota">Rochester, Minnesota</a>, from Salford in Lancashire, England, as part of his appointment as an examining surgeon for the military draft board during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The city was to his liking, and his wife and children joined him in early 1864; the family served in several leadership roles in the community.  On January 27, 1864, William Worrall Mayo advertised in the <i>Rochester City Post</i> the opening of a private medical partnership "over the Union Drug Store on Third Street" with "all calls answered by day or night".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Both of W.W. Mayo's sons, <a href="/wiki/William_James_Mayo" title="William James Mayo">William James Mayo</a> (1861–1939) and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Horace_Mayo" title="Charles Horace Mayo">Charles Horace Mayo</a> (1865–1939) grew up in Rochester and, when old enough, both attended medical school. William graduated in 1883 and joined his father's practice, with Charles joining after he completed his training in 1888.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 21, 1883, a <a href="/wiki/1883_Rochester_tornado" title="1883 Rochester tornado">tornado</a> struck Rochester, causing at least 37 deaths in the area and over 200 injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> One-third of the town was destroyed, but the Mayo family escaped serious harm. The relief efforts began immediately with a temporary hospital being established at Rommell's Hall, and the doctors Mayo (W.W. and Will) as well as other local doctors, were extensively involved in treating the injured who were brought there for help. <a href="/wiki/Mother_Alfred_Moes" class="mw-redirect" title="Mother Alfred Moes">Mother Alfred Moes</a> (1828–1899) and the <a href="/wiki/Sisters_of_Saint_Francis_of_Rochester,_Minnesota" title="Sisters of Saint Francis of Rochester, Minnesota">Sisters of Saint Francis</a> (a teaching order) were called in to act as nurses despite having been trained as teachers and with little if any medical experience.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg/220px-1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="118" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg/330px-1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg/440px-1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1182" data-file-height="633" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St. Mary's Hospital in 1910</div></div></div>
<p>After the crisis subsided, Moes approached W.W. Mayo about establishing a hospital in Rochester. Mayo agreed to work in the hospital and soon other local doctors agreed as well. On September 30, 1889, <a href="/wiki/Saint_Marys_Hospital_(Rochester)" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Marys Hospital (Rochester)">Saint Mary's Hospital</a> was opened by the Sisters. W.W. Mayo, 70 years old, was one of the consulting physicians at the hospital. His two sons began seeing patients and performing surgeries at the hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-smh_ref_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smh_ref-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_founding_and_development_of_the_Mayo_Clinic">Early founding and development of the Mayo Clinic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early founding and development of the Mayo Clinic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1892, W. W. Mayo asked <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Stinchfield" title="Augustus Stinchfield">Augustus Stinchfield</a>, whom he considered to be the best doctor in the area, to join the practice.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> After Stinchfield agreed, W.W. Mayo retired at the age of 73 and the practice continued to grow. The founders of Mayo Clinic are the Mayo brothers Will and Charlie, Stinchfield, Graham, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Stanley_Plummer" title="Henry Stanley Plummer">Henry Plummer</a>, Millet, Judd, and Balfour. These early founders and partners shared in the profits of the private group practice, while other staff hired by the partners were salaried. W.W. Mayo died in 1911 and in 1919 the remaining founders, with the exception of Graham, created the Mayo Properties Association, and their private practice became a not-for-profit entity. The founders gave the Clinic properties and furnishings to this newly formed association.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The integrated practice model developed primarily by Plummer created a foundation for what would grow into Mayo Clinic.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Growth_and_expansion">Growth and expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Growth and expansion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the private practice grew, it required additional space. In 1914, the partners planned, designed and built a new clinic building.<sup id="cite_ref-Lens_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lens-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Ellerbe Architects are the architect of record for the 1914 Mayo "Red" building.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>  
</p><p>Until 1919 the Mayo Clinic was operated as a for-profit medical practice.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> In 1919, the Mayo brothers donated the clinic property and significant amounts of their wealth to develop the Mayo Properties Association. The Association later became the Mayo Clinic Foundation.<sup id="cite_ref-medicinehistory_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-medicinehistory-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The result of this was that the Mayo Clinic became a non-profit medical practice in 1920. The historic 1914 "Red" Mayo Clinic building, a National Landmark listed on the National Register, was demolished by the Clinic in the 1980s to make way for the Hammel, Green and Abrahamson-designed Siebens building.<sup id="cite_ref-Lens_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lens-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 1986, the Mayo Clinic campus has formally included the Rochester Methodist Hospital and <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_Hospital,_Saint_Mary%27s_Campus" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayo Clinic Hospital, Saint Mary&#39;s Campus">Saint Marys Hospital</a>, as all operations were integrated under one governing board to more efficiently serve the needs of Mayo patients.<sup id="cite_ref-smh_ref_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smh_ref-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, the foundation went before the Supreme Court to argue that medical residents should remain exempt from <a href="/wiki/Social_Security_(United_States)" title="Social Security (United States)">Social Security</a> deductions. In <i><a href="/wiki/Mayo_Foundation_v._United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayo Foundation v. United States">Mayo Foundation v. United States</a></i> the court sided with the <a href="/wiki/Social_Security_Administration" title="Social Security Administration">Social Security Administration</a> and required <a href="/wiki/Federal_Insurance_Contributions_Act_tax" title="Federal Insurance Contributions Act tax">FICA</a> to be deducted going forward.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The same year, Tarek Obaid made a major donation in the name of his family to establish the Essam and Dalal Obaid Center for Reconstructive Transplant Surgery.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, when plans to establish a <a href="/wiki/Proton_therapy" title="Proton therapy">proton beam therapy</a> program and to build new facilities in Rochester and <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix</a> were underway, philanthropist and longtime Mayo Clinic patient Richard O. Jacobson donated $100 million to the nonprofit. At that time, the donation was the largest in the Mayo Clinic's history.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:MayoClinicPlummerBldg16floor.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/MayoClinicPlummerBldg16floor.JPG/200px-MayoClinicPlummerBldg16floor.JPG" decoding="async" width="200" height="267" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/MayoClinicPlummerBldg16floor.JPG/300px-MayoClinicPlummerBldg16floor.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/MayoClinicPlummerBldg16floor.JPG/400px-MayoClinicPlummerBldg16floor.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2304" data-file-height="3072" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:MayoClinicPlummerBldg16floor.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Plummer_Building" title="Plummer Building">Plummer Building</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_Minnesota" title="Rochester, Minnesota">Rochester, Minnesota</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Mayo Clinic is a nonprofit hospital system with campuses in <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_Minnesota" title="Rochester, Minnesota">Rochester, Minnesota</a>; <a href="/wiki/Scottsdale,_Arizona" title="Scottsdale, Arizona">Scottsdale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">Jacksonville, Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Stubbe14_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stubbe14-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Expand_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Expand-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Mayo Clinic employs 63,000 people, including more than 4,500 physicians and scientists and 58,400 administrative and allied health staff, as of 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-Beckers-52Great_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beckers-52Great-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Snowbeck-Retire_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snowbeck-Retire-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Of those, approximately 34,000 are based in Rochester.<sup id="cite_ref-Boese_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boese-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> In addition, Mayo Clinic partially owns and operates the Mayo Clinic Health System, which consists of more than 70 hospitals and clinics across <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Advisory14_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Advisory14-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Mayo Clinic also operates the <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_College_of_Medicine_and_Science" title="Mayo Clinic College of Medicine and Science">Mayo Clinic College of Medicine and Science</a>, a nonprofit college dedicated to training medical and allied health professionals at Mayo Hospitals in Minnesota, Arizona, and Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-USNWR-2018_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USNWR-2018-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The clinic created an independent business subsidiary in London in partnership with Oxford University Clinic, a collaboration between the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Hospitals_NHS_Foundation_Trust" title="Oxford University Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust">Oxford University Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust</a> to operate a clinic starting in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>  The clinic, in the <a href="/wiki/Harley_Street" title="Harley Street">Harley Street</a> Medical Area, will focus on preventative healthcare, with magnetic resonance imaging and <a href="/wiki/CT_scan" title="CT scan">CT scan</a> technology and open in the summer of 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mayo Clinic is led by president and CEO Gianrico Farrugia, M.D.<sup id="cite_ref-Karnowski18_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Karnowski18-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_H._Noseworthy" title="John H. Noseworthy">John H. Noseworthy</a>, M.D. retired as president and CEO in December 2018; his predecessor, Denis A. Cortese, M.D. retired in November 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Samuel_A._DiPiazza,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Samuel A. DiPiazza, Jr.">Samuel A. DiPiazza, Jr.</a>, retired CEO of <a href="/wiki/PricewaterhouseCoopers" title="PricewaterhouseCoopers">PricewaterhouseCoopers</a>, is chairman of Mayo Clinic's governing board of trustees.<sup id="cite_ref-Kiger17_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kiger17-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Core_operations">Core operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Core operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Patient_care">Patient care</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Patient care">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Each year, more than 1.3 million different patients from all 50 states and from more than 150 countries are seen at one of the Mayo Clinic facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-mayoinfo_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mayoinfo-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Mayo Clinic offers highly specialized medical care, and a large portion of the patient population are referrals from smaller clinics and hospitals across the upper Midwest and the United States. Mayo Clinic physicians are paid a fixed salary, which is not linked to patient volume (<a href="/wiki/Relative_value_unit" title="Relative value unit">relative value units</a>) or income from <a href="/wiki/Fee-for-service" title="Fee-for-service">fee-for-service</a> payments.<sup id="cite_ref-Underwood09_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Underwood09-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MacGillis09_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MacGillis09-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Olson15_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Olson15-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> This practice is thought to decrease the monetary motivation to see patients in excessive numbers and increase the incentive to spend more time with individuals.<sup id="cite_ref-Underwood09_57-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Underwood09-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mayo Clinic researchers contribute to the understanding of disease processes, best clinical practices, and translation of findings from the laboratory to the clinical practice. Nearly 600 doctoral level physicians and research scientists are employed, with an additional 3,400 other health personnel and students with appointments in research. In 2015, more than 2,700 research protocols were reviewed by the Mayo Clinic <a href="/wiki/Institutional_Review_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="Institutional Review Board">Institutional review board</a> and 11,000 ongoing human research studies. These research initiatives led to more than 7,300 research publications and review articles in peer-review journals.<sup id="cite_ref-mayoinfo_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mayoinfo-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_College_of_Medicine_and_Science" title="Mayo Clinic College of Medicine and Science">Mayo Clinic College of Medicine and Science</a></div>
<p>The Mayo Clinic College of Medicine and Science (MCCMS), established in 1915, offers educational programs embedded in Mayo Clinic's clinical practice and biomedical research activities.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> MCCMS consists of five accredited schools, including the M.D. degree-granting <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_Alix_School_of_Medicine" title="Mayo Clinic Alix School of Medicine">Mayo Clinic Alix School of Medicine</a> as well as the master's and Ph.D. degree-granting <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_Graduate_School_of_Biomedical_Sciences" title="Mayo Clinic Graduate School of Biomedical Sciences">Mayo Clinic Graduate School of Biomedical Sciences</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_School_of_Health_Sciences" title="Mayo Clinic School of Health Sciences">Mayo Clinic School of Health Sciences</a> offers training for about 50 health sciences career fields. The <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_College_of_Medicine_and_Science#Mayo_Clinic_School_of_Graduate_Medical_Education" title="Mayo Clinic College of Medicine and Science">Mayo Clinic School of Graduate Medical Education</a> runs over 270 residences and fellowships in all medical and surgical specialties. The <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_College_of_Medicine_and_Science#Mayo_Clinic_School_of_Continuous_Professional_Development" title="Mayo Clinic College of Medicine and Science">Mayo Clinic School of Continuous Professional Development</a> delivers continuing education courses aimed at practicing medical professionals.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Innovation">Innovation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Innovation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mayo Clinic has adopted more than 15,000 mobile devices from <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a> for patient care; including the <a href="/wiki/IPad" title="IPad">iPad</a>, <a href="/wiki/IPad_Mini" title="IPad Mini">iPad Mini</a> and <a href="/wiki/IPhone" title="IPhone">iPhone</a>. Mayo Clinic then created an app for these devices called Synthesis Mobile, which integrated hundreds of their health systems. For Mayo Clinic Care Network members, more apps  were created to help patients see their medical records or ask clinicians for assistance.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, Mayo Clinic was developing an app for <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a>'s <a href="/wiki/HealthKit" class="mw-redirect" title="HealthKit">HealthKit</a> to help users maintain healthy lifestyles and warn of certain health signs that need attention.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mayo Clinic, in collaboration with real estate firm Delos Living, launched the <a href="/wiki/Well_Living_Lab" title="Well Living Lab">Well Living Lab</a> in September 2015. This research facility is designed to simulate real-world, non-hospital environments to allow Mayo Clinic researchers to study the interaction between indoor spaces and human health.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Mayo Clinic <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_Center_for_Innovation" title="Mayo Clinic Center for Innovation">Center for Innovation</a>, established in 2008, was one of the pioneers of innovation in healthcare. It has since worked on over 270 projects and is often looked to as a role model for using design in healthcare.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2018, Mayo Clinic and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mytonomy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mytonomy (page does not exist)">Mytonomy</a>, a healthcare education system company, partnered to provide video content for cancer patients. The video content is used to address important questions and answers and designed to aid in the decision-making process between patient and doctor.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Contributions_to_medicine">Contributions to medicine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Contributions to medicine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mayo Clinic has developed many medical and surgical specialities, including cancer research, heart and lung surgery, laboratory techniques and many others.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> Two Mayo Clinic staff members, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Calvin_Kendall" title="Edward Calvin Kendall">Edward Kendall</a>, Ph.D., and <a href="/wiki/Philip_Showalter_Hench" title="Philip Showalter Hench">Philip Hench</a>, M.D., were awarded the Nobel Prize for Medicine or Physiology in 1950 for their discovery of <a href="/wiki/Cortisone" title="Cortisone">cortisone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> Mayo Clinic considers its own most significant contribution to medicine to be the development and implementation of the concept of integrated, multi-specialty physician led group medical practice.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rankings">Rankings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayo_Clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Rankings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2016-17, Mayo Clinic, Rochester, was ranked as the #1 overall hospital in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> by <a href="/wiki/U.S._News_%26_World_Report" title="U.S. News &amp; World Report">U.S. News &amp; World Report</a>. A total of almost 5,000 hospitals were considered and ranked in 16 specialties from cancer and heart disease to respiratory disorders and urology; 153 (just over 3 percent of the total) were ranked in at least one of the 16 specialties. Of the 153 hospitals that are ranked in one or more specialties, 20 qualified for the Honor Roll by earning high scores in at least six specialties. Mayo Clinic, Rochester, was ranked in the top 10 in all but one of 16 specialties, in the top 4 in 13 specialties, and was the #1 ranked hospital in 8 of the 12 data-driven specialties.  This year U.S. News expanded their common procedures and conditions list to 9 individual measures, and Mayo was one of fewer than 70 hospitals to score High Performing in every category.<sup id="cite_ref-Browning_69-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Browning-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>  Additionally, Mayo was the only hospital on the 2016-2017 honor roll to also receive 5 stars from CMS.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>  Every Mayo Clinic hospital received an "A" safety rating from Leapfrog in its April 2017 report.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
