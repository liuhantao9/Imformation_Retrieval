	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Fred Ormskerk</h1>

<p><b>Frederik Ferdinand "Fred" Ormskerk</b> (April 26, 1923 – May 1, 1980) was a <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Surinamese</a> military leader. He was awarded many honors for his military services in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a>. After a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Netherlands_East_Indies_Army" title="Royal Netherlands East Indies Army">KNIL</a> training at Camp Casino, Australia, he arrived in <a href="/wiki/Batavia,_Dutch_East_Indies" title="Batavia, Dutch East Indies">Batavia</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>). Here he became a soldier in the newly formed <a href="/wiki/Korps_Speciale_Troepen" title="Korps Speciale Troepen">Korps Speciale Troepen</a> (KST), led by <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Westerling" title="Raymond Westerling">Captain Westerling</a>. He was nicknamed <i>Bikkel</i>, which means "stud".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 1980, the <a href="/wiki/1980_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="1980 Surinamese coup d&#39;état">coup d'état</a> led by <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a> took place in Suriname. Ormskerk originally wanted to launch a counter-coup on April 30, but failed and he was put in prison, and then tortured and eventually slain.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Decades after this tragedy occurred, the alleged failed coup is still shrouded in fog.
</p>
