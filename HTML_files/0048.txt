<p>The <b>Canadian Hot 100</b> is a music industry <a href="/wiki/Record_chart" title="Record chart">record chart</a> in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> for songs, published weekly by <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a> magazine. The Canadian Hot 100 was launched on the issue dated March 31, 2007, and is currently the standard record chart in Canada; a new chart is compiled and officially released to the public by <i>Billboard</i> every Tuesday.
</p><p>The chart is similar to <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s US-based <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a> in that it combines <a href="/wiki/CD_single" title="CD single">physical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital</a> sales as measured by <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Streaming_media" title="Streaming media">streaming</a> activity data provided by online music sources, and <a href="/wiki/Airplay" title="Airplay">radio airplay</a> as measured by <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_Broadcast_Data_Systems" title="Nielsen Broadcast Data Systems">Nielsen BDS</a>. Canada's radio airplay is the result of monitoring more than 100 stations representing <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adult_contemporary_music" title="Adult contemporary music">adult contemporary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_hit_radio" title="Contemporary hit radio">Top 40</a> genres.<sup id="cite_ref-Launches_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Launches-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first number-one song of the Canadian Hot 100 was "<a href="/wiki/Girlfriend_(Avril_Lavigne_song)" title="Girlfriend (Avril Lavigne song)">Girlfriend</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Avril_Lavigne" title="Avril Lavigne">Avril Lavigne</a> on March 31, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As of the issue for the week ending September 28, 2019, the Canadian Hot 100 has had 142 different number-one songs. The current number-one is "<a href="/wiki/Se%C3%B1orita_(Shawn_Mendes_and_Camila_Cabello_song)" title="Señorita (Shawn Mendes and Camila Cabello song)">Señorita</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Shawn_Mendes" title="Shawn Mendes">Shawn Mendes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Camila_Cabello" title="Camila Cabello">Camila Cabello</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canadian_Hot_100&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The chart was launched on the issue dated March 31, 2007 and was made available for the first time via <i>Billboard</i> online services on June 7, 2007. With this launch, it marked the first time that <i>Billboard</i> created a Hot 100 chart for a country outside the United States. 
</p><p><i>Billboard</i> charts manager Geoff Mayfield announced the premiere of the chart, explaining "the new <i>Billboard</i> Canadian Hot 100 will serve as the definitive measure of Canada's most popular songs, continuing our magazine's longstanding tradition of using the most comprehensive resources available to provide the world's most authoritative music charts."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <i>Billboard</i> Canadian Hot 100 is managed by Paul Tuch, director of Canadian operations for Nielsen BDS, in consultation with Silvio Pietroluongo, <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s associate director of charts and manager of the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-Launches_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Launches-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_achievements">Other achievements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Canadian_Hot_100&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Other achievements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Teenage_Dream_(Katy_Perry_album)" title="Teenage Dream (Katy Perry album)">Teenage Dream</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Scorpion_(Drake_album)" title="Scorpion (Drake album)">Scorpion</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a> are the albums with most number-one singles (4 each).</li>
<li>On the issue dated March 31, 2007, <a href="/wiki/Avril_Lavigne" title="Avril Lavigne">Avril Lavigne</a> became the first Canadian act to top the Canadian Hot 100 with "<a href="/wiki/Girlfriend_(Avril_Lavigne_song)" title="Girlfriend (Avril Lavigne song)">Girlfriend</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/I_Gotta_Feeling" title="I Gotta Feeling">I Gotta Feeling</a>" by <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas" title="The Black Eyed Peas">The Black Eyed Peas</a> holds the record for the longest stay on the chart (76 weeks).<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On the issue dated February 27, 2010, <a href="/wiki/Nikki_Yanofsky" title="Nikki Yanofsky">Nikki Yanofsky</a> became the youngest artist to top the Canadian Hot 100 at 16 years, 19 days old with the song "<a href="/wiki/I_Believe_(Nikki_Yanofsky_song)" title="I Believe (Nikki Yanofsky song)">I Believe</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/Sexy_and_I_Know_It" title="Sexy and I Know It">Sexy and I Know It</a>" by <a href="/wiki/LMFAO" title="LMFAO">LMFAO</a> holds the record for largest gap between turns at #1, falling from the position on the week ending November 12, 2011 and returning the week ending January 7, 2012, a 9-week gap.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On the issue dated October 6, 2012, "<a href="/wiki/Gangnam_Style" title="Gangnam Style">Gangnam Style</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Psy" title="Psy">Psy</a> became the first non-English single to top the Canadian Hot 100.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On the issue dated June 27, 2009, <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas" title="The Black Eyed Peas">The Black Eyed Peas</a> became the first act to simultaneously occupy the top two positions with "<a href="/wiki/Boom_Boom_Pow" title="Boom Boom Pow">Boom Boom Pow</a>" at number one and "<a href="/wiki/I_Gotta_Feeling" title="I Gotta Feeling">I Gotta Feeling</a>" at number two.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On the issue dated October 24, 2009, "<a href="/wiki/3_(Britney_Spears_song)" title="3 (Britney Spears song)">3</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Britney_Spears" title="Britney Spears">Britney Spears</a> broke the record for the biggest jump to number one, leaping from number 86 to number one.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/Counting_Stars" title="Counting Stars">Counting Stars</a>" by <a href="/wiki/OneRepublic" title="OneRepublic">OneRepublic</a> had the longest climb to number one taking 34 weeks to reach that peak.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On the issue dated October 31, 2015, <a href="/wiki/The_Weeknd" title="The Weeknd">The Weeknd</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/The_Hills_(song)" title="The Hills (song)">The Hills</a>" reached the top spot seven weeks after "<a href="/wiki/Can%27t_Feel_My_Face" title="Can&#39;t Feel My Face">Can't Feel My Face</a>", becoming the first time in Canadian Hot 100 history that an album's lead single hit #1 after the second single did.</li>
<li>In 2016, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a> became the first Canadian act to top the Year-End chart with "<a href="/wiki/Sorry_(Justin_Bieber_song)" title="Sorry (Justin Bieber song)">Sorry</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On the issue dated January 28, 2017, <a href="/wiki/Ed_Sheeran" title="Ed Sheeran">Ed Sheeran</a> became the first act to simultaneously debut at the top two positions with "<a href="/wiki/Shape_of_You" title="Shape of You">Shape of You</a>" at number one and "<a href="/wiki/Castle_on_the_Hill" title="Castle on the Hill">Castle on the Hill</a>" at number two.<sup id="cite_ref-january282017_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-january282017-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On the issue dated July 14, 2018, <a href="/wiki/Drake_(musician)" title="Drake (musician)">Drake</a> became the artist with the most simultaneous top 10 singles (6).<sup id="cite_ref-July142018_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-July142018-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On the issue dated July 14, 2018, Drake became the artist with the most simultaneously charted Canadian Hot 100 songs in a single week (27) and the most Canadian Hot 100 debuts in a week (22).<sup id="cite_ref-July142018_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-July142018-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On the issue dated January 12, 2019, "<a href="/wiki/All_I_Want_for_Christmas_Is_You" title="All I Want for Christmas Is You">All I Want for Christmas Is You</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> had the biggest drop out of the Canadian Hot 100, dropping off from #1.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On the issue dated May 25, 2019, "<a href="/wiki/I_Don%27t_Care_(Ed_Sheeran_and_Justin_Bieber_song)" title="I Don&#39;t Care (Ed Sheeran and Justin Bieber song)">I Don't Care</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Ed_Sheeran" title="Ed Sheeran">Ed Sheeran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">Justin Bieber</a> jumped 90 spots to number 2, becoming the biggest single-week jump on the Canadian Hot 100's history.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
