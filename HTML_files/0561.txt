<p>The <b>Neveh Shalom Synagogue</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r886049626"/><span class="script-hebrew" style="font-size: 115%;" dir="rtl">בית הכנסת נווה שלום</span>) is the only <a href="/wiki/Synagogue" title="Synagogue">synagogue</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Ashkenazi_Jews" title="Ashkenazi Jews">Ashkenazi</a> community in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The lot on <a href="/wiki/Keizerstraat" title="Keizerstraat">Keizerstraat</a> 82 was acquired in 1716 by <a href="/wiki/Sephardi_Jews" title="Sephardi Jews">Sephardi Jews</a>. The original building was completed in 1723 and replaced the first Surinamese synagogue in the <a href="/wiki/Jodensavanne" title="Jodensavanne">Jodensavanne</a>, originally built of wood between 1665-1671 (but already rebuilt with bricks). The synagogue was sold to the <a href="/wiki/Ashkenazi_Jews" title="Ashkenazi Jews">Ashkenazim</a> in 1735, and the Sephardim formed a separate community known as <a href="/wiki/Tzedek_ve-Shalom" title="Tzedek ve-Shalom">Tzedek ve-Shalom</a>.  The two communities have since merged, and hold services in alternating buildings and alternating rites.
</p><p>The current synagogue on the Keizerstraat 82 lot, designed by architect J.F. Halfhide, was completed in 1842 or 1843.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Mosque_Keizerstraat" title="Mosque Keizerstraat">Mosque Keizerstraat</a> is adjacent to the synagogue.
</p>
