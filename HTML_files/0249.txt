<p><a href="/wiki/Personal_name" title="Personal name">Personal names</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bengali-speaking" class="mw-redirect" title="Bengali-speaking">Bengali-speaking</a> countries consist of one or several <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given names</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a>.
The given is usually gender-specific. A name is usually cited in the "<a href="/wiki/Name_order" class="mw-redirect" title="Name order">Western order</a>" of "given name, surname". Personal names may depend generally on the person's religion and also have origins from other languages like <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pali" title="Pali">Pali</a>, but they are used and pronounced as according to the native <a href="/wiki/Bengali_language" title="Bengali language">Bengali language</a>.
</p>

<h2><span id="Given_names_.28first_name.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Given_names_(first_name)">Given names (first name)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bengali_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Given names (first name)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many people in <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a> have two given names: a "good name" (<span lang="bn" title="Bengali language text">ভাল নাম</span> <i>bhalo nam</i>), which is used on all legal documents, and a "<a href="/wiki/Call_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Call name">call name</a>"  or "nickname" (<span lang="bn" title="Bengali language text">ডাক নাম</span> <i>đak nam</i>), which is used by family members and close friends.
</p><p>The two names may or may not be at all related; for example, a man named Šumon Saha (<span lang="bn" title="Bengali language text">সুমন সাহা</span>) may be called by his <i>đak nam</i> (e.g. Bubai <span lang="bn" title="Bengali language text">বুবাই</span>) at home and by his <i>bhalo nam</i> (Šumon <span lang="bn" title="Bengali language text">সুমন</span>) elsewhere. Many people also have a shortened version of their <i>bhalo nam</i> (e.g. Dipu for Deepak, Faru for Farhana, etc.) in addition to their full <i>bhalo nam</i> and their <i>dak nam</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Middle_names">Middle names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bengali_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Middle names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Though middle names are very common in Bangladesh, not every individual may have one. Recently, many people have begun to add their <i>dak nam</i> to the middle or end of their full official name, resulting in names like "Saifuddin Kanchon Choudhuri" (<span lang="bn" title="Bengali language text">সাইফুদ্দীন কাঞ্চন চৌধুরী</span>), where "Saifuddin" would be the man's <i>bhalo nam</i>, "Kanchon" would be his <i>dak nam</i> and "Choudhuri" would be his family name. "Saifuddin Kanchon Choudhuri" could also be written as "Saifuddin Choudhuri Kanchon" <i>dak nam</i> in the end, depending on the choice of the person, how he/she displays their name. In these situations, this man would be correctly addressed "Mr. Choudhuri", not "Mr. Kanchon".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_names">Family names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bengali_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Family names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A majority of the people of Bangladesh are adherents of Islam, and because of this, they mostly use names of <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> origins followed by <a href="/wiki/Farsi" class="mw-redirect" title="Farsi">Farsi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bengali_language" title="Bengali language">Bengali</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pali" title="Pali">Pali</a> origins. Among Muslims of Bangladesh, there are several different naming conventions. There is no fixed scheme for the structure of names.<sup id="cite_ref-Understanding_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Understanding-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Many people do not really use a family name, so members of a family can have different last names. The system of usage of different family names in the same family may also be followed by non-Muslims due to the dominating name style of not having same family names in a family by Muslims in Bangladesh. Some family names may be common between all religions like: <span lang="bn" title="Bengali language text">চৌধুরী</span> (Choudhuri / Chowdhury), <span lang="bn" title="Bengali language text">সরকার</span> (Sorkar / Sarker / Sarkar) and <span lang="bn" title="Bengali language text">বিশ্বাস</span> (Bishwas).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Initials_and_prefixes">Initials and prefixes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bengali_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Initials and prefixes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mohammod, Muhammod or <a href="/wiki/Muhammad" title="Muhammad">Muhammad</a> (<span lang="bn" title="Bengali language text">মোহাম্মদ</span>) is a common prefix used before the name of many Muslim males, and it is not considered as the name used to refer to the person. In many cases, the "Muhammad" prefix is shortened to <span lang="bn" title="Bengali language text">মোঃ</span> ("Md.", or "MD."). Other common prefixes are not systematic. The prefix often serves as the first name and the given name appears as the last name/surname.
</p>
