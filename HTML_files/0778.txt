<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">Terminology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sexual_health_clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Terminology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sexual health clinics are also called <i><a href="/wiki/Sexually_transmitted_disease" class="mw-redirect" title="Sexually transmitted disease">sexually transmitted disease</a> (STD) clinics</i>, <i>sexually transmitted infection (STI) clinics</i>, <i>venereal disease (VD) clinics</i>, or <i><a href="/wiki/Genitourinary_medicine" class="mw-redirect" title="Genitourinary medicine">genitourinary medicine</a> (GUM) clinics</i>.
</p><p>Sexual health clinics differ from <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_health" title="Reproductive health">reproductive health</a> and <a href="/wiki/Family_planning" title="Family planning">family planning</a> clinics. Sexual health clinics offer only some reproductive health services. <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_health" title="Reproductive health">Reproductive health</a> clinics, such as <a href="/wiki/Planned_Parenthood" title="Planned Parenthood">Planned Parenthood</a>, offer most of the services of sexual health clinics.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sexual_health_clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sexual health clinics provide some or all of the following:
</p>
<ul><li>Information about <a href="/wiki/Safer_sex" class="mw-redirect" title="Safer sex">safer sex</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birth_control" title="Birth control">birth control</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reproductive_health" title="Reproductive health">reproductive health</a> and general <a href="/wiki/Sex_education" title="Sex education">sex education</a></li>
<li>Providing free <a href="/wiki/Condom" title="Condom">condoms</a></li>
<li>Sexual health <a href="#Examinations">examinations</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/STD_testing" class="mw-redirect" title="STD testing">Tests</a> to detect some <a href="/wiki/Sexually_transmitted_infection" title="Sexually transmitted infection">sexually transmitted infections</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Antibiotic" title="Antibiotic">Antibiotics</a> to <a href="/wiki/Cure" title="Cure">cure</a> <a href="/wiki/Chlamydia_infection" class="mw-redirect" title="Chlamydia infection">chlamydia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gonorrhea" title="Gonorrhea">gonorrhea</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Syphilis" title="Syphilis">syphilis</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Medication" title="Medication">Medications</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Therapy" title="Therapy">treatments</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vaccination" title="Vaccination">Vaccinations</a></li>
<li>Counseling and therapy</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Emergency_contraception" title="Emergency contraception">Emergency contraception</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Urine_test" class="mw-redirect" title="Urine test">Urine test</a> for <a href="/wiki/Pregnancy" title="Pregnancy">pregnancy</a></li>
<li>Referrals for additional information or services</li></ul>
<p>Many clinics provide vaccinations to prevent <a href="/wiki/Infection" title="Infection">infections</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_A" title="Hepatitis A">hepatitis A</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_B_virus" title="Hepatitis B virus">B</a> <a href="/wiki/Introduction_to_viruses" title="Introduction to viruses">viruses</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
Young women may receive vaccinations to prevent infection from some strains of the <a href="/wiki/HPV_vaccine" title="HPV vaccine">human papillomavirus</a> (HPV).
</p><p>Many clinics provide <a href="/wiki/Interpreting" class="mw-redirect" title="Interpreting">interpreting</a> for the hearing impaired or speakers of other languages.
</p><p>Many clinics will help patients tell their sexual contacts if they have a sexually transmitted infection, anonymously if needed.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Public governmental and <a href="/wiki/Non-profit" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-profit">non-profit</a> clinics often provide services for free or adjust the fee based on a patient's ability to pay.
</p><p>Sexual health clinics often offer services without appointments. Some clinics open evenings or weekends.
</p><p>Some clinics have separate hours or facilities for men and women. Some clinics serve only specific populations such as women, men, <a href="/wiki/Men_who_have_sex_with_men" title="Men who have sex with men">MSM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Youths" class="mw-redirect" title="Youths">youths</a>, <a href="/wiki/LGBT" title="LGBT">LGBT</a>, ethnic groups, the poor, or students.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Examinations">Examinations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sexual_health_clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Examinations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Well-woman_examination" title="Well-woman examination">Well-woman examination</a></div>
<p>With the patient's <a href="/wiki/Informed_consent" title="Informed consent">consent</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Clinician" title="Clinician">clinician</a> will inspect the patient visually and by touch. If needed, the clinician will take samples to <a href="/wiki/STD_test" class="mw-redirect" title="STD test">test</a> for <a href="/wiki/Sexually_transmitted_infection" title="Sexually transmitted infection">sexually transmitted infections</a>.
</p><p>In a private room or space, the patient will partially undress.
</p><p>The clinician may inspect the patient's:
</p>
<ul><li>Throat and <a href="/wiki/Lymph_node" title="Lymph node">lymph nodes</a> of the neck for inflammation</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Pubic_hair" title="Pubic hair">Pubic hair</a> for <a href="/wiki/Pubic_lice" class="mw-redirect" title="Pubic lice">lice</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Superficial_inguinal_lymph_nodes" class="mw-redirect" title="Superficial inguinal lymph nodes">Lymph nodes</a> of the groin for swelling</li>
<li>Genitals, <a href="/wiki/Human_anus" title="Human anus">anus</a>, and surrounding areas for sores and <a href="/wiki/Wart" title="Wart">warts</a></li></ul>
<p>The clinician may swab the patient's:
</p>
<ul><li>Throat to test for <a href="/wiki/Gonorrhea" title="Gonorrhea">gonorrhea</a> and possibly <a href="/wiki/Chlamydia_infection" class="mw-redirect" title="Chlamydia infection">chlamydia</a></li>
<li>Cheek, inside, to <a href="/wiki/Diagnosis_of_HIV/AIDS" title="Diagnosis of HIV/AIDS">diagnose HIV</a></li>
<li>Sores of the genitals, <a href="/wiki/Human_anus" title="Human anus">anus</a>, and surrounding areas to test for <a href="/wiki/Herpes" class="mw-redirect" title="Herpes">herpes</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Urethra" title="Urethra">Urethra</a> to test for <a href="/wiki/Gonorrhea" title="Gonorrhea">gonorrhea</a> and possibly <a href="/wiki/Chlamydia_infection" class="mw-redirect" title="Chlamydia infection">chlamydia</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vagina" title="Vagina">Vagina</a> to test for <a href="/wiki/Chlamydia_infection" class="mw-redirect" title="Chlamydia infection">chlamydia</a> and possibly <a href="/wiki/Gonorrhea" title="Gonorrhea">gonorrhea</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Cervix" title="Cervix">Cervix</a> to test for <a href="/wiki/Cervical_intraepithelial_neoplasia" title="Cervical intraepithelial neoplasia">cervical intraepithelial neoplasia</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Pap_test" title="Pap test">Pap test</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rectum" title="Rectum">Rectum</a> to test for <a href="/wiki/Gonorrhea" title="Gonorrhea">gonorrhea</a> and possibly <a href="/wiki/Chlamydia_infection" class="mw-redirect" title="Chlamydia infection">chlamydia</a></li></ul>
<p>The clinician may take small blood samples by <a href="/wiki/Finger_prick" class="mw-redirect" title="Finger prick">pricking a finger</a> or from a <a href="/wiki/Venipuncture" title="Venipuncture">vein</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
to test for <a href="/wiki/HIV" title="HIV">HIV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syphilis" title="Syphilis">syphilis</a>, and possibly <a href="/wiki/Herpes" class="mw-redirect" title="Herpes">herpes</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
and <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_C" title="Hepatitis C">hepatitis C</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The clinician may ask for a small <a href="/wiki/Urine" title="Urine">urine</a> sample, given in private, to test for <a href="/wiki/Chlamydia_infection" class="mw-redirect" title="Chlamydia infection">chlamydia</a> and possibly <a href="/wiki/Gonorrhea" title="Gonorrhea">gonorrhea</a>.
</p><p>The inspections and taking samples don't hurt, but swabbing the urethra and cervix, and a finger prick blood sample feel uncomfortable.
</p><p>Women will often receive a <a href="/wiki/Pelvic_exam" class="mw-redirect" title="Pelvic exam">pelvic exam</a>, both external and internal, but usually less thorough than a reproductive health exam.
</p><p>A patient can choose a female or male clinician if available.
A patient can have a <a href="/wiki/Chaperone_(clinical)" title="Chaperone (clinical)">chaperone</a>.
Some clinics have separate hours or facilities for men and women.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Privacy">Privacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sexual_health_clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Privacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Medical <a href="/wiki/Confidentiality" title="Confidentiality">confidentiality</a> is an important part of the <a href="/wiki/Medical_ethics" title="Medical ethics">medical ethics</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Doctor%E2%80%93patient_relationship" title="Doctor–patient relationship">doctor–patient relationship</a>. Sexual health clinics follow local standards of medical confidentiality to protect the privacy of patients. Some clinics provide anonymous services or protect confidentiality by having a patient use a number or a pseudonym.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Additional privacy protections sometimes apply to matters of sexuality and reproduction, since these areas are sensitive in many cultures. The <a href="/wiki/Diagnosis_of_HIV/AIDS" title="Diagnosis of HIV/AIDS">diagnosis of HIV/AIDS</a> has legal restrictions in patient confidentiality, and some clinics use <a href="/wiki/Diagnosis_of_HIV/AIDS#Rapid_or_point-of-care_tests" title="Diagnosis of HIV/AIDS">rapid antibody tests</a> to provide results to a patient within 30 minutes, without holding the patient's records.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In the United States, clinics receiving federal funding from <a href="/wiki/Medicaid" title="Medicaid">Medicaid</a> or <a href="/wiki/Title_X" title="Title X">Title X</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Public_Health_Service_Act" title="Public Health Service Act">Public Health Service Act</a> must treat all patients confidentially. Thus minors can receive services without parental notification or consent.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, medical records for all patients age 18 and above are strictly confidential under <a href="/wiki/HIPAA" class="mw-redirect" title="HIPAA">HIPAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Consent">Consent</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sexual_health_clinic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Consent">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Medical standards of <a href="/wiki/Informed_consent" title="Informed consent">informed consent</a> apply to sexual health clinics. A patient needs information about the purposes and consequences of examinations, tests, treatments, and other procedures. A patient may then choose whether to consent to these procedures.
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Minor_(law)" title="Minor (law)">minor</a> may consent to receive some or all of the procedures at many sexual health clinics.
