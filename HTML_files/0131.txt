<p><b>Sikiana</b>, or <b>Kashuyana</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Carib_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Carib languages">Carib</a> language that was spoken by 33 people in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and 15 people in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>.  It was spoken in <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a> at one time and is now probably extinct there. The Warikyana dialect became extinct around 2000, and the language frequently goes by the name of the surviving dialect, Sikiana.
</p>
