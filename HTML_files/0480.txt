<p><b>Cummins</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500">Fortune 500</a> corporation that designs, manufactures, and distributes <a href="/wiki/Engines" class="mw-redirect" title="Engines">engines</a>, filtration, and power generation products.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Cummins also services engines and related equipment, including <a href="/wiki/Fuel_injection" title="Fuel injection">fuel systems</a>, <a href="/wiki/Control_system" title="Control system">controls</a>, air handling, <a href="/wiki/Filtration" title="Filtration">filtration</a>, emission control, <a href="/wiki/Electricity_generation" title="Electricity generation">electrical power generation systems</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Trucks" class="mw-redirect" title="Trucks">trucks</a>. Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Indiana" title="Columbus, Indiana">Columbus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, United States, Cummins sells in approximately 190 countries and territories through a network of more than 600 company-owned and independent distributors and approximately 6,000 dealers. Cummins reported net income of $2.19 billion on sales of $23.77 billion in 2018.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:227px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cummins_Diesel_Roadster.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Cummins_Diesel_Roadster.jpg/225px-Cummins_Diesel_Roadster.jpg" decoding="async" width="225" height="158" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Cummins_Diesel_Roadster.jpg/338px-Cummins_Diesel_Roadster.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Cummins_Diesel_Roadster.jpg/450px-Cummins_Diesel_Roadster.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1452" data-file-height="1020" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cummins_Diesel_Roadster.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An early Cummins diesel in a 1950 <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_500" title="Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a> roadster</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:CUMMINS_ENGINE_COMPANY_MAIN_PLANT,_DESIGNED_BY_HARRY_WEESE_-_NARA_-_546542.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/CUMMINS_ENGINE_COMPANY_MAIN_PLANT%2C_DESIGNED_BY_HARRY_WEESE_-_NARA_-_546542.jpg/220px-CUMMINS_ENGINE_COMPANY_MAIN_PLANT%2C_DESIGNED_BY_HARRY_WEESE_-_NARA_-_546542.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/CUMMINS_ENGINE_COMPANY_MAIN_PLANT%2C_DESIGNED_BY_HARRY_WEESE_-_NARA_-_546542.jpg/330px-CUMMINS_ENGINE_COMPANY_MAIN_PLANT%2C_DESIGNED_BY_HARRY_WEESE_-_NARA_-_546542.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/CUMMINS_ENGINE_COMPANY_MAIN_PLANT%2C_DESIGNED_BY_HARRY_WEESE_-_NARA_-_546542.jpg/440px-CUMMINS_ENGINE_COMPANY_MAIN_PLANT%2C_DESIGNED_BY_HARRY_WEESE_-_NARA_-_546542.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="2028" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:CUMMINS_ENGINE_COMPANY_MAIN_PLANT,_DESIGNED_BY_HARRY_WEESE_-_NARA_-_546542.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Columbus main plant (1973)</div></div></div>
<p>The Cummins Engine Company was founded in <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Indiana" title="Columbus, Indiana">Columbus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, in 1919 by <a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Glanton_Irwin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="William Glanton Irwin (page does not exist)">William Glanton Irwin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clessie_Cummins" title="Clessie Cummins">Clessie Cummins</a>, a local mechanic.  It focused on developing the <a href="/wiki/Diesel_engine" title="Diesel engine">diesel engine</a> invented 20 years earlier, but in spite of several well publicized endurance trials, it was not until 1933 that their Model H, used in small railroad switchers, proved successful.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Cummins_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cummins-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Cummins N Series engines became the industry leader in the post-World War II road building boom in the United States, with more than half the heavy duty truck market from 1952 to 1959.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWren1979232_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWren1979232-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In the 1960s, the company opened an assembly plant in <a href="/wiki/Shotts" title="Shotts">Shotts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> (closed in 1996). By 2013, Cummins had operations in 197 countries and territories.<sup id="cite_ref-Cummins_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cummins-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cummins_headquarters.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Cummins_headquarters.jpg/220px-Cummins_headquarters.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Cummins_headquarters.jpg/330px-Cummins_headquarters.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Cummins_headquarters.jpg/440px-Cummins_headquarters.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4096" data-file-height="2304" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cummins_headquarters.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Cummins 1st headquarters</div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_units">Business units</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business units">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Engine_Business">Cummins Engine Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cummins Engine Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cummins Engine Business Unit consists of Aftermarket support, Mid-Range, Heavy-Duty, and High-Power Engines. Cummins manufactures and markets a complete line of diesel and natural gas-powered engines for on-highway and off-highway use.  Its markets include heavy-and medium-duty truck, bus, recreational vehicle (RV), light-duty automotive and a number of industrial uses including, construction, mining, marine, oil and gas, railroad and military equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-10K2017_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10K2017-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For the general public, the most visible Cummins product may be the <a href="/wiki/Cummins_B_Series_engine" title="Cummins B Series engine">5.9-liter in-line six-cylinder engine</a> used in the <a href="/wiki/Dodge_Ram" class="mw-redirect" title="Dodge Ram">Dodge Ram</a> light duty pickups starting in 1988.5.<sup id="cite_ref-allpar_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allpar-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2007.5, a 6.7-liter version of the Cummins straight six engine became optional on the RAM pickup.<sup id="cite_ref-allpar_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allpar-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 2008, Cummins was a named defendant in a class action suit related to 1998-2001 model year Chrysler Dodge Ram trucks, model 2500 or 3500, originally equipped with a Cummins ISB 5.9 liter diesel engine built using a pattern 53 Block.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The case has been settled, but some qualified Chrysler owners may receive $500 for repairs to the block, which was alleged to crack and create a coolant leak.
</p><p>In April 2013, Cummins utilized technology developed by <a href="/wiki/Westport_Innovations" title="Westport Innovations">Westport Innovations</a> to begin shipping large natural gas fueled engines to truck manufacturers in the United States as trucking companies began converting portions of their fleets to natural gas and the natural gas distribution network in the United States began to expand.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT42213_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT42213-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cummins has a Technical Centre in <a href="/wiki/Darlington_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Darlington (UK Parliament constituency)">Darlington UK</a>, where it develops products for the European, Middle Eastern and Asian markets.
</p>
<h3><span id="Cummins_Power_Systems_Business_.28formerly_Cummins_Power_Generation.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Power_Systems_Business_(formerly_Cummins_Power_Generation)">Cummins Power Systems Business (formerly Cummins Power Generation)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Cummins Power Systems Business (formerly Cummins Power Generation)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Shenandoah_National_Park_HQ_Generator.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Shenandoah_National_Park_HQ_Generator.png/220px-Shenandoah_National_Park_HQ_Generator.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Shenandoah_National_Park_HQ_Generator.png/330px-Shenandoah_National_Park_HQ_Generator.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Shenandoah_National_Park_HQ_Generator.png/440px-Shenandoah_National_Park_HQ_Generator.png 2x" data-file-width="884" data-file-height="663" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Shenandoah_National_Park_HQ_Generator.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Cummins Power Solutions unit at the Shenandoah National Park office outside of Luray, Virginia.</div></div></div>
<p>Cummins Power Systems Business Unit consists of Alternators, Automatic Transfer Switches, Commercial Power Systems, Consumer Systems, Engines, and Paralleling Systems. Cummins Power Systems is a global provider of power generation systems, components and services in standby power, distributed power generation, as well as auxiliary power in mobile applications.
</p><p>All of the above solutions<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Use_plain_English#Buzzwords" title="Wikipedia:Use plain English"><span title="The material near this tag may use buzzwords designed solely to impress you or obscure meaning. (June 2019)">buzzword</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> stem from Cummins Onan, of which products are still in service today.
</p><p>This Business Unit was formed recently, following a merge of the Power Generation Unit and High Horsepower Sub-Division.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:227px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cummins_generator.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Cummins_generator.jpg/225px-Cummins_generator.jpg" decoding="async" width="225" height="169" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Cummins_generator.jpg/338px-Cummins_generator.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Cummins_generator.jpg/450px-Cummins_generator.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cummins_generator.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A Cummins generator at the base of a <a href="/wiki/Radio_mast" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio mast">radio mast</a></div></div></div>
<p>On August 22, 2017, <a href="/wiki/United_Rentals" title="United Rentals">United Rentals</a> announced it had acquired all mobile power generation assets from Cummins. To maintain fleet and customer service continuity, a small number of Cummins employees joined United Rentals.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Component_Business">Cummins Component Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Cummins Component Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cummins Component Business Unit consists of Emission Solutions, Filtration (Fleetguard), Fuel Systems, Turbo Technologies (Holset), and Electronics. With regards to Turbo Technologies, Cummins designs and manufactures <a href="/wiki/Turbochargers" class="mw-redirect" title="Turbochargers">turbochargers</a> and related products, on a global scale, for diesel engines above 3 liters. In regard to Emission Solutions, Cummins develops and supplies catalytic exhaust systems and related products to the medium-and heavy-duty commercial diesel engine markets. In regard to Filtration, Cummins designs, manufactures and distributes heavy-duty and light-duty air, fuel, hydraulic and lube filtration, chemicals and exhaust system technology products for diesel and gas-powered equipment. With regards to Electronics, Cummins designs engine control units and sensors for Cummins diesel engines.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Distribution_Business">Cummins Distribution Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cummins Distribution Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cummins Distribution Business consists of Engine and Power Generation Distribution as well as Service and Parts. The distribution unit of Cummins consists of 17 Cummins owned distributors and 10 joint ventures, covering 90 countries and territories through 234 locations.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Turbo_Technologies">Cummins Turbo Technologies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cummins Turbo Technologies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <b>Holset Engineering Co. </b> was a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> company that produced <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbochargers</a>, primarily for <a href="/wiki/Diesel_engine" title="Diesel engine">diesel</a> and heavy duty applications.
</p><p>In 1973 the company was purchased by Cummins after briefly being owned by the Hanson Trust. Holset now operates facilities in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.
</p><p>In 2006, the division officially changed its name to Cummins Turbo Technologies to be identified more closely with its parent company. The turbocharger products still use the Holset brand name.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Power_Systems">Cummins Power Systems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cummins Power Systems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1990 Cummins began acquisition of Onan and completed it in 1992. Since then, Onan has evolved into Cummins Power Generation (now Cummins Power Systems), a wholly invested division of Cummins. The Onan name continues to be used for modern versions of their traditional engine-driven generators for RV, marine, commercial mobility, home standby, and portable use.
</p><p><br />
</p><p>Fun Roads Website and Facebook page will also be rebranded to Cummins RV.
</p><p>Cummins Inc. (NYSE: CMI) announced that it will be unifying its brand strategy across its Power Systems business segment, which provides high-speed engines from 760 – 4400hp and power generation equipment from 2-3,500kW, including standby and prime power gen sets, alternators, switchgear and other components. Currently the portfolio features the Cummins, Cummins Power Generation and Cummins Onan brands. With immediate effect, the branding will be consolidated under the Cummins brand. The Cummins Power Generation and Cummins Onan brands will be retired and the Onan name synonymous with mobile gensets, will be repositioned as a generator product line under the newly unified Cummins brand in the RV market.
</p><p>Cummins Inc. will also be permanently changing all “Fun Roads” branding to Cummins RV moving forward as well and the Fun Roads brand will also be retired. As Onan will now be repositioned as a RV product line, with the new rv.cummins.com website and social media platforms serving as tools for RVers across the country to find relevant information like product specs and our sales and service locator.
</p><p>“Looking to unify Cummins into one cohesive, unified brand, we decided that consolidating both products (engines and generators) into the Cummins RV family only strengthens the brand and more uniformly speaks to our manufacturers and consumers,” said Jodie Wilson. “The brand changing will not affect product or service offerings, but will help us to continue on our promise of delivering dependability across the globe.”
</p><p>Rebranding will take place today globally on all marketing activities, as product branding changes across all manufacturing plants this July.
<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cummins_Emission_Solutions">Cummins Emission Solutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Cummins Emission Solutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Exhaust and emissions after-treatment company <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nelson_Industries&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nelson Industries (page does not exist)">Nelson Industries</a> was purchased in 1999, due to the increasing importance of exhaust after-treatment systems for meeting future emissions standards. The division officially changed its name to <i>Cummins Emission Solutions</i>, in order to be identified more closely with their parent company.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="China_operations">China operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: China operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

</p><p>Other entities were Cummins Beijing, Chongqing Cummins Engine, Xi’an Cummins Engine, Wuxi Newage Alternators, Xiangfan Fleetguard, Wuxi Cummins Turbo Technologies.<sup id="cite_ref-worldwide_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-worldwide-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="India_operations">India operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cummins&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: India operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cummins began its <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> operations on 17 February 1962 in a joint venture with the <a href="/wiki/Kirloskar_Group" title="Kirloskar Group">Kirloskar Group</a>. The ownership structure of the joint venture was divided as follows:
</p>
<ul><li>Cummins - 50%</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kirloskar_Group" title="Kirloskar Group">Kirloskar Group</a> - 25.5%</li>
<li>Retail Investors - 24.5%</li></ul>
<p>In 1996 Cummins Inc. bought Kirloskar shares. Now its Cummins Inc. subsidiary. 
As of 2013, the Cummins group had revenues of over $1.5 billion, 20 factories and 9000 employees in India.
</p><p>Cummins does a significant part of its R&amp;D in India at the Cummins Research and Technology centre that was set up in 2003. Also, Cummins is building an advanced technical centre in <a href="/wiki/Pune" title="Pune">Pune</a> which will house over 2000 engineers.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cummins India has also made significant contributions to local skill development by establishing the <a href="/wiki/MKSSS%27s_Cummins_College_of_Engineering_for_Women" title="MKSSS&#39;s Cummins College of Engineering for Women">MKSSS's Cummins College of Engineering for Women</a>, a women-only engineering college in Pune.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
