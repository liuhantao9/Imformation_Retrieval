<p>The <b>Society of Suriname</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i>SociÃ«teit van Suriname</i>) was a Dutch private company, modelled on the ideas of <a href="/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Colbert" title="Jean-Baptiste Colbert">Jean-Baptiste Colbert</a> and set up on 21 May 1683 to profit from the management and defence of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a>'s colony of <a href="/wiki/Suriname_(Dutch_colony)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suriname (Dutch colony)">Suriname</a>. It had three participants, with equal shares in the costs and benefits of the society; the city of <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, the family <a href="/wiki/Cornelis_van_Aerssen_van_Sommelsdijck" title="Cornelis van Aerssen van Sommelsdijck">Van Aerssen van Sommelsdijck</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_West_India_Company" title="Dutch West India Company">Dutch West India Company</a>. Only through mutual consent could these shareholders withdraw from the society.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although the organization and administration was of the colony was limited to these three shareholders, all citizens of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a> were free to trade with Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Also, the planters were consulted in a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Council_of_Police&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Council of Police (page does not exist)">Council of Police</a>, which was a unique feature among the colonies of Guyana.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Its governors included <a href="/wiki/Cornelis_van_Aerssen_van_Sommelsdijck" title="Cornelis van Aerssen van Sommelsdijck">Cornelis van Aerssen van Sommelsdijck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johan_van_Scharphuizen" title="Johan van Scharphuizen">Johan van Scharphuizen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Paulus_van_der_Veen" title="Paulus van der Veen">Paulus van der Veen</a>.
</p><p>The Society was nationalized by the <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a> in November 1795, as the <a href="/wiki/Patriottentijd" title="Patriottentijd">Patriottentijd</a> deemed the governing of colonies by chartered companies a thing of the past.
</p>
