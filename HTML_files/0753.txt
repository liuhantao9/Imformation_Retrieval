<p>The <b>Pan American Union Building</b> is the headquarters for the <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_American_States" title="Organization of American States">Organization of American States</a>. It is located at 17th Street N.W. between C Street N.W. and <a href="/wiki/Constitution_Avenue" title="Constitution Avenue">Constitution Avenue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northwest,_Washington,_D.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Northwest, Washington, D.C.">Northwest, Washington, D.C.</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pan_American_Union_Building&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On the former site of the <a href="/wiki/John_Peter_Van_Ness" title="John Peter Van Ness">John Peter Van Ness</a> Mansion. The cornerstone was laid on May 11, 1908, by <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Roosevelt" title="Theodore Roosevelt">Theodore Roosevelt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elihu_Root" title="Elihu Root">Elihu Root</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Carnegie" title="Andrew Carnegie">Andrew Carnegie</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and the building was dedicated on April 26, 1910.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1919, the initial meeting of the <a href="/wiki/International_Labour_Organization" title="International Labour Organization">International Labour Organization</a> was held in the building.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1969, the building was added to the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>.
</p>
