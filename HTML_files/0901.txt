<p>The <b>mayor of Boston</b> is the head of the municipal government in <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a>. Boston has a <a href="/wiki/Mayor-council_government" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayor-council government">mayor-council system of government</a>. Boston's mayoral elections are non-partisan (as are all municipal elections in Boston), and elect a mayor to a four-year term; there are no term limits. The mayor's office is in <a href="/wiki/Boston_City_Hall" title="Boston City Hall">Boston City Hall</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Government_Center,_Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Government Center, Boston, Massachusetts">Government Center</a>.
</p><p>The current mayor is <a href="/wiki/Marty_Walsh_(politician)" title="Marty Walsh (politician)">Martin J. "Marty" Walsh</a>. Currently, there is one living former mayor, <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Flynn" title="Raymond Flynn">Raymond Flynn</a>, born <span style="display:none"> (<span class="bday">1939-07-22</span>) </span>July 22, 1939<span class="noprint ForceAgeToShow"> (age&#160;80)</span>. The most recent mayor to die was <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Menino" title="Thomas Menino">Thomas Menino</a>, on October 30, 2014.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayor_of_Boston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to 1822, there was no Mayor of Boston, because Boston was incorporated as a town. In Massachusetts, a town is typically governed by a <a href="/wiki/Town_meeting" title="Town meeting">town meeting</a>, with a <a href="/wiki/Board_of_selectmen" title="Board of selectmen">board of selectmen</a> handling regular business. Boston was the first community in Massachusetts to receive a city charter, which was granted in 1822.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Under the terms of the new charter, the mayor was elected annually. In June 1895, the charter was amended, and the mayor's term was increased to two years.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1909, the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a>-controlled <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_General_Court" title="Massachusetts General Court">state legislature</a> enacted <a href="/wiki/Strong-mayor" class="mw-redirect" title="Strong-mayor">strong-mayor</a> charter changes it hoped would dampen the rising power of <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic</a> <a href="/wiki/Irish_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish American">Irish Americans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Adopted by public vote in the November 1909 <a href="/wiki/General_election" title="General election">general election</a>, changes included extending the mayoral term to four years, and making the post formally non-partisan.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The reforms did not work; the first mayor elected under the new charter was Democrat <a href="/wiki/Honey_Fitz" class="mw-redirect" title="Honey Fitz">John "Honey Fitz" Fitzgerald</a>, and every mayor since Republican <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_Nichols" title="Malcolm Nichols">Malcolm Nichols</a> (1926–1930) has been a Democrat.
</p><p>In a bid to temper the rising power of <a href="/wiki/James_Michael_Curley" title="James Michael Curley">James Michael Curley</a>, the state legislature in 1918 passed legislation barring the Mayor of Boston from serving consecutive terms in office;<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Curley was prevented from running for re-election twice by this law (<a href="/wiki/Boston_mayoral_election,_1925" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston mayoral election, 1925">November 1925</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boston_mayoral_election,_1933" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston mayoral election, 1933">November 1933</a>). The law was repealed in 1939,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> after Curley's political career appeared to be in decline.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Another charter change was enacted in 1949, partly in response to Curley's fourth term (1946–1950), during which he served prison time for crimes committed in an earlier term. Changes included adding a <a href="/wiki/Primary_election" title="Primary election">preliminary election</a> to narrow the field to two mayoral candidates in advance of the general election, changing the <a href="/wiki/Boston_City_Council" title="Boston City Council">Boston City Council</a> from having 22 members (one from each city <a href="/wiki/Ward_(electoral_subdivision)" title="Ward (electoral subdivision)">ward</a>) to having nine members (elected <a href="/wiki/At-large" title="At-large">at-large</a>), and giving the council ability to override some mayoral vetoes.<sup id="cite_ref-cc1949_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cc1949-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> These changes went into effect in 1951, resulting in the first term of <a href="/wiki/John_B._Hynes" class="mw-redirect" title="John B. Hynes">John B. Hynes</a> being shortened to two years.
</p><p>From 1951 through 1991, Boston mayoral elections were held the year before presidential elections (e.g. mayoral election in <a href="/wiki/Boston_mayoral_election,_1951" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston mayoral election, 1951">1951</a>, presidential election in <a href="/wiki/1952_United_States_presidential_election" title="1952 United States presidential election">1952</a>). Starting in 1993, due to the election held following <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Flynn" title="Raymond Flynn">Raymond Flynn</a>'s appointment as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_the_Holy_See" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to the Holy See">United States Ambassador to the Holy See</a>, Boston mayoral elections are held the year following presidential elections (e.g. presidential election in <a href="/wiki/1992_United_States_presidential_election" title="1992 United States presidential election">1992</a>, mayoral election in <a href="/wiki/Boston_mayoral_election,_1993" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston mayoral election, 1993">1993</a>).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Salary">Salary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mayor_of_Boston&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Salary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of 2018, salary for the mayor is $199,000.<sup id="cite_ref-Valencia_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Valencia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Members of the <a href="/wiki/Boston_City_Council" title="Boston City Council">Boston City Council</a> receive a salary that is 50% of that amount, currently $99,500.<sup id="cite_ref-Valencia_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Valencia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Every four years, the Council votes on whether or not to raise the mayor's salary, thereby also raising its own salaries or not.
</p><p>In June 2018, the Council voted to increase the salary of the mayor to $207,000, effective after the next mayoral election of <a href="/w/index.php?title=2021_Boston_mayoral_election&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2021 Boston mayoral election (page does not exist)">November 2021</a> (term starting in January 2022); this will increase the salary of councillors to $103,500, effective after the next council elections of <a href="/wiki/2019_Boston_City_Council_election" title="2019 Boston City Council election">November 2019</a> (terms starting in January 2020).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Valencia_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Valencia-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

Hynes' time acting as Mayor is not numbered; his time as elected Mayor is.
