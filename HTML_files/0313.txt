<p><b>Totness</b> is a town in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, located in the <a href="/wiki/Coronie" class="mw-redirect" title="Coronie">Coronie</a> district, of which it is the capital. Totness once had a <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> settlement. The Suriname-Guyana Submarine Cable System has its landing station in Totness. It connects the telecommunications networks in Suriname with those in <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidad</a> and from Trinidad to the rest of the world.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Totness_Airstrip" title="Totness Airstrip">Totness Airstrip</a> is one of the oldest airport in Suriname, in use since 1953, when the <a href="/wiki/Piper_Cub" class="mw-redirect" title="Piper Cub">Piper Cub</a> (PZ-NAC) of Kappel-van Eyck named "Colibri" landed there from <a href="/wiki/Zorg_en_Hoop_Airport" title="Zorg en Hoop Airport">Zorg en Hoop Airport</a>.
</p>
