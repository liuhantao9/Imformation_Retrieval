<p><b>"Inside Your Heaven"</b> is a song written by <a href="/wiki/Andreas_Carlsson" title="Andreas Carlsson">Andreas Carlsson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pelle_Nyl%C3%A9n" title="Pelle Nylén">Pelle Nylén</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savan_Kotecha" title="Savan Kotecha">Savan Kotecha</a>, and produced by <a href="/wiki/Desmond_Child" title="Desmond Child">Desmond Child</a>. <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bo_Bice" title="Bo Bice">Bo Bice</a>, the final two <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_4)" title="American Idol (season 4)">fourth season</a> contestants on <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i>, each released a version of the song in June 2005.
</p><p>Underwood, the winner of <i>Idol</i>, released her version as a single on June 14, 2005. It debuted at number one on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Singles_Chart" title="Canadian Singles Chart">Canadian Singles Chart</a>, where it remained for one and seven weeks, consecutively, atop the charts. It was the longest running single of 2005 in Canada. Although Underwood's recording was not released to country radio, the song charted at number 52 on <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a>. It was certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Gold</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> and two times platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Music_Canada" title="Music Canada">CRIA</a>, and it was later included on the North American version of her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Hearts" title="Some Hearts">Some Hearts</a></i>. The <a href="/wiki/A-side_and_B-side" title="A-side and B-side">B-side</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Martina_McBride" title="Martina McBride">Martina McBride</a> song "<a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Martina_McBride_song)" title="Independence Day (Martina McBride song)">Independence Day</a>".
</p><p>Bice's version was released the following week on June 21 and peaked at number two on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. It was certified Gold by the RIAA<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and two times platinum by the CRIA. The B-side is a cover of the song "<a href="/wiki/Vehicle_(song)" title="Vehicle (song)">Vehicle</a>" by <a href="/wiki/The_Ides_of_March_(band)" title="The Ides of March (band)">The Ides of March</a>.
</p><p>The song won Underwood the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Music_Award" title="Billboard Music Award"><i>Billboard</i> Music Awards</a> for "Top-Selling Country Single of the Year" and "Top-Selling Hot 100 Song of the Year" (Bice's version was also nominated in this category). It also helped Underwood win the award for "Country Single Sales Artist of the Year".  Carrie Underwood's version sold 880,000 copies, while Bo Bice's version sold 401,000.<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
