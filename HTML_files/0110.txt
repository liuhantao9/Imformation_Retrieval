<p><b>Family name <a href="/wiki/Affix" title="Affix">affixes</a></b> are a clue for <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a> <a href="/wiki/Etymology" title="Etymology">etymology</a> and can sometimes determine the <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_origin" title="Ethnic origin">ethnic origin</a> of a person. This is a partial list of affixes.

<li><b>Ap</b> – (<a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>) "son of"<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>

<li>-<b>aj</b> (<a href="/wiki/Albanian_language" title="Albanian language">Albanian</a>) (pronounced AY; meaning  “of the" ) It denotes the name of the family, which mostly comes from the male founder of the family, but also from a place, as in, Lash-aj (from the village Lashaj of Kastrat, MM, Shkoder).  It is likely that its ancient form, still found in MM, was an [i] in front of the last name, as in ‘Déda i Lékajve’  (Déd of Lekës).  For ease of use, the [i] in front of the last name, and the ending _ve, were dropped.  If the last name ends in [a], then removing the [j] would give the name of the patriarch or the place, as in, Grudaj - j = Gruda (place in MM).  Otherwise, removing the whole ending [aj] yields the name of founder or place of origin, as in Lekaj - aj = Lek(ë).  Since the names are found most commonly in Malsi e Madhe (North) and Labëri (South), it is likely that this linguistic feature is very old.  It must have been lost as a result of foreign influences brought into Albania by the invaders.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
