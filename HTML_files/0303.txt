<p>The <b>Royal Netherlands Navy (RNLN)</b> (Dutch: <i lang="nl">Koninklijke Marine</i> <i>[KM]</i> “Royal Navy”) is the naval force of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Its origins date back to the <a href="/wiki/Eighty_Years%27_War" title="Eighty Years&#39; War">Eighty Years' War</a> (1568–1648), the war of independence from the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Habsburg" title="House of Habsburg">House of Habsburg</a> who ruled over the <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Netherlands" title="Habsburg Netherlands">Habsburg Netherlands</a>.
</p><p>During the 17th century the navy of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a> (1581–1795) was one of the most powerful naval forces in the world and played an active role in wars against <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Dutch_Wars" title="Anglo-Dutch Wars">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franco-Dutch_War" title="Franco-Dutch War">France</a>, Spain and several other European powers. The <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Navy" title="Batavian Navy">navy</a> of the later <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a> (1795–1806) and <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Holland" title="Kingdom of Holland">Kingdom of Holland</a> (1806–1810) played an active role in the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a>, though mostly dominated by French interests. After the establishment of the modern <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a> (founded 1815) it served an important role in protecting <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Empire" title="Dutch Empire">Dutch colonial rule</a>, especially in Southeast Asia, and would play a minor role in World War II, especially against the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">Imperial Japanese Navy</a>.
</p><p>Since World War II the Royal Netherlands Navy has taken part in expeditionary peacekeeping operations.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bases">Bases</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Netherlands_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Bases">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main naval base is located at <a href="/wiki/Den_Helder" title="Den Helder">Den Helder</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Holland" title="North Holland">North Holland</a>.
Secondary naval bases are located at <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vlissingen" title="Vlissingen">Vlissingen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texel" title="Texel">Texel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Willemstad" title="Willemstad">Willemstad</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a>). <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Marine_Corps" title="Netherlands Marine Corps">Netherlands Marine Corps</a> barracks are found in <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a>, Doorn, Suffisant on <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Savaneta" title="Savaneta">Savaneta</a> on <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Officer_training">Officer training</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Netherlands_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Officer training">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Officers of the Nederland Navy are trained at the <i>Koninklijk Instituut voor de Marine</i> ("Royal Naval Institute"), which is part of the <i>Nederlandse Defensie Academie</i> ("Netherlands defence academy") in <a href="/wiki/Den_Helder" title="Den Helder">Den Helder</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
Around 100–150 people start training every year.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ship_prefixes">Ship prefixes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Netherlands_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ship prefixes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>An international prefix for ships of the Royal Netherlands Navy is HNLMS (<i>His/Her Netherlands Majesty's Ship</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The Netherlands navy itself uses the prefixes Zr.Ms. (<i>Zijner Majesteits</i>, <i>His Majesty's</i>) when a king is on the throne, and Hr.Ms. (<i>Harer Majesteits</i>, <i>Her Majesty's</i>) when there is a queen. This happens automatically at the moment of coronation. <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Netherlands_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Naval_history_of_the_Netherlands" title="Naval history of the Netherlands">Naval history of the Netherlands</a></div>
<p>The modern Netherlands Navy dates its founding to a "statute of admiralty" issued by <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Emperor</a> <a href="/wiki/Maximilian_I,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor">Maximilian I</a> on January 8, 1488.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Naval historians trace the origins of an independent Dutch navy to the early stages of the <a href="/wiki/Eighty_Years%27_War" title="Eighty Years&#39; War">Eighty Years' War</a> (1568–1648) while the formation of a "national" navy is dated to the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Republic" title="Dutch Republic">Dutch Republic</a> in 1597.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Netherlands_Golden_Age">Netherlands Golden Age</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Netherlands_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Netherlands Golden Age">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:De_slag_bij_Terheide_-_The_Battle_of_Schevening_-_August_10_1653_(Willem_van_de_Velde_I,_1657).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/De_slag_bij_Terheide_-_The_Battle_of_Schevening_-_August_10_1653_%28Willem_van_de_Velde_I%2C_1657%29.jpg/220px-De_slag_bij_Terheide_-_The_Battle_of_Schevening_-_August_10_1653_%28Willem_van_de_Velde_I%2C_1657%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="128" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/De_slag_bij_Terheide_-_The_Battle_of_Schevening_-_August_10_1653_%28Willem_van_de_Velde_I%2C_1657%29.jpg/330px-De_slag_bij_Terheide_-_The_Battle_of_Schevening_-_August_10_1653_%28Willem_van_de_Velde_I%2C_1657%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/De_slag_bij_Terheide_-_The_Battle_of_Schevening_-_August_10_1653_%28Willem_van_de_Velde_I%2C_1657%29.jpg/440px-De_slag_bij_Terheide_-_The_Battle_of_Schevening_-_August_10_1653_%28Willem_van_de_Velde_I%2C_1657%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="7342" data-file-height="4288" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:De_slag_bij_Terheide_-_The_Battle_of_Schevening_-_August_10_1653_(Willem_van_de_Velde_I,_1657).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Scheveningen" title="Battle of Scheveningen">battle of Scheveningen</a> in 1653 during the <a href="/wiki/First_Anglo-Dutch_War" title="First Anglo-Dutch War">First Anglo-Dutch War</a></div></div></div>
<p>The Netherlands navy was involved in several wars against other European powers from the late 16th century, initially for independence against Spain in European waters, later for shipping lanes, trade and colonies in many parts of the world, notably in four <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Dutch_wars" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo-Dutch wars">Anglo-Dutch wars</a> against <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. During the 17th century the Dutch navy was one of the most powerful navies in the world. As an organization, the navy of the Dutch Republic consisted of five separate admiralties (three of them in <a href="/wiki/Holland" title="Holland">Holland</a>, and one each in <a href="/wiki/Friesland" title="Friesland">Friesland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zeeland" title="Zeeland">Zeeland</a>), each with its own ships, personnel, shipyards, command structures and revenues.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Netherlands_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Piet_de_Jong_in_uniform.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Piet_de_Jong_in_uniform.jpg/220px-Piet_de_Jong_in_uniform.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Piet_de_Jong_in_uniform.jpg/330px-Piet_de_Jong_in_uniform.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Piet_de_Jong_in_uniform.jpg/440px-Piet_de_Jong_in_uniform.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2126" data-file-height="2126" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Piet_de_Jong_in_uniform.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Piet_de_Jong" title="Piet de Jong">Piet de Jong</a>, commanding officer of <a href="/wiki/HNLMS_Gelderland_(D811)" title="HNLMS Gelderland (D811)">HNLMS&#160;<i>Gelderland</i></a> in 1958</div></div></div>
<p>During the Second World War, the Dutch navy was based in <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> countries after the Netherlands was conquered by <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> in a matter of days: the Dutch navy had its headquarters in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, and smaller units in <a href="/wiki/British_Ceylon" title="British Ceylon">Ceylon</a> (modern day <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a>.
</p><p>Around the world Dutch naval units were responsible for transporting troops, for example during <a href="/wiki/Operation_Dynamo" class="mw-redirect" title="Operation Dynamo">Operation Dynamo</a> at Dunkirk and on <a href="/wiki/D-Day" class="mw-redirect" title="D-Day">D-Day</a>, they escorted convoys and attacked enemy targets. During the war the navy suffered heavy losses, especially in defending the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a>, most notably the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Java_Sea" title="Battle of the Java Sea">Battle of the Java Sea</a> in which the commander, Dutchman <a href="/wiki/Karel_Doorman" title="Karel Doorman">Karel Doorman</a>, went down with his fleet along with 1,000 of the ships' crew. Two Dutch <a href="/wiki/Light_cruiser" title="Light cruiser">light cruisers</a> and one <a href="/wiki/Destroyer_leader" title="Destroyer leader">destroyer leader</a> and three <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyers</a> that were under construction were captured in their <a href="/wiki/Shipyard" title="Shipyard">shipyard</a> by <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a>.
</p><p>During the relentless Japanese offensive of February through April 1942 in the Dutch East Indies, the Dutch navy in Asia was virtually annihilated, and it sustained losses of a total of 20 ships (including two of its three light cruisers) and 2,500 sailors killed.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The Dutch navy had suffered from years of underfunding and came ill-prepared to face an enemy with more and heavier ships with better weapons, including the <a href="/wiki/Long_Lance" class="mw-redirect" title="Long Lance">Long Lance</a>-torpedo, with which the cruiser <a href="/wiki/Japanese_cruiser_Haguro" title="Japanese cruiser Haguro"><i>Haguro</i></a> sank the light cruiser <a href="/wiki/HNLMS_De_Ruyter_(1935)" title="HNLMS De Ruyter (1935)">HNLMS&#160;<i>De Ruyter</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A small force of <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarines</a> based in Western Australian sank more Japanese ships in the first weeks after Japan joined the war than the entire British and American navies together during the same period, an exploit which earned <a href="/wiki/Conrad_Emil_Lambert_Helfrich" class="mw-redirect" title="Conrad Emil Lambert Helfrich">Admiral Helfrich</a> the nickname "Ship-a-day Helfrich".<sup id="cite_ref-Dutchman_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dutchman-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The aggressive pace of operations against the Japanese was a contributing factor to both the heavy losses sustained and the greater number of successes scored as compared to the British and Americans in the region.
</p><p>Both British and American forces believed that the Dutch admiral in charge of the joint-Allied force was being far too aggressive. Later in the war, a few Dutch submarines scored some remarkable hits, including one on a <i><a href="/wiki/Kriegsmarine" title="Kriegsmarine">Kriegsmarine</a></i> <a href="/wiki/U-boat" title="U-boat">U-boat</a> <a href="/wiki/German_submarine_U-95_(1940)" title="German submarine U-95 (1940)"><i>U-95</i></a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a>, which was sunk by <a href="/wiki/HNLMS_O_21" title="HNLMS O 21"><i>O 21</i></a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Netherlands_New_Guinea">Netherlands New Guinea</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Netherlands_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Netherlands New Guinea">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the war, the relations between the Netherlands and its colonies changed dramatically. The establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Indonesia" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Indonesia">Republic of Indonesia</a>, two days after the Japanese surrender, thwarted the Dutch plans for restoring colonial authority. After four years of war the Netherlands acknowledged the independence of Indonesia.
</p><p>Part of the Dutch Navy was next stationed in <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_New_Guinea" title="Netherlands New Guinea">Netherlands New Guinea</a> until that, too, was turned over to the <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_government" class="mw-redirect" title="Indonesian government">Indonesian government</a> in 1962. This followed a campaign of infiltrations by the <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_National_Armed_Forces" title="Indonesian National Armed Forces">Indonesian National Armed Forces</a>, supported by modern equipment from the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, that was nevertheless successfully repulsed by the Dutch navy. These infiltrations took place after the order of President <a href="/wiki/Sukarno" title="Sukarno">Sukarno</a> to integrate the territory as an Indonesian province.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="NATO_cooperation">NATO cooperation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Netherlands_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: NATO cooperation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ships_of_Standing_NATO_Maritime_Group_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Ships_of_Standing_NATO_Maritime_Group_1.jpg/220px-Ships_of_Standing_NATO_Maritime_Group_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Ships_of_Standing_NATO_Maritime_Group_1.jpg/330px-Ships_of_Standing_NATO_Maritime_Group_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Ships_of_Standing_NATO_Maritime_Group_1.jpg/440px-Ships_of_Standing_NATO_Maritime_Group_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2400" data-file-height="1574" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ships_of_Standing_NATO_Maritime_Group_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Standing_NRF_Maritime_Group_1" class="mw-redirect" title="Standing NRF Maritime Group 1">Standing NRF Maritime Group 1</a> in 2007 with <a href="/wiki/HNLMS_Evertsen_(F805)" title="HNLMS Evertsen (F805)">HNLMS&#160;<i>Evertsen</i></a> second from the right</div></div></div>
<p>With the creation of the <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Treaty_Organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="North Atlantic Treaty Organisation">North Atlantic Treaty Organisation</a>, the military focus was on the army and air force; it was not until the <a href="/wiki/Korean_War" title="Korean War">Korean War</a> (1950–53) that the navy got more recognition. The government allowed the creation of a balanced fleet consisting of two naval squadrons. Apart from the <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">aircraft carrier</a> <a href="/wiki/HNLMS_Karel_Doorman_(R81)" title="HNLMS Karel Doorman (R81)">HNLMS&#160;<i>Karel Doorman</i></a> the Dutch navy consisted of two <a href="/wiki/Light_cruiser" title="Light cruiser">light cruisers</a> (two <a href="/wiki/De_Zeven_Provinci%C3%ABn-class_cruiser" title="De Zeven Provinciën-class cruiser"><i>De Zeven Provinciën</i>&#x20;class</a>), 12 <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyers</a> (four <a href="/wiki/Holland-class_destroyer" title="Holland-class destroyer"><i>Holland</i>&#x20;class</a>, eight <a href="/wiki/Friesland-class_destroyer" title="Friesland-class destroyer"><i>Friesland</i>&#x20;class</a>), eight <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarines</a>, six <a href="/wiki/Frigate" title="Frigate">frigates</a> (<a href="/wiki/Van_Speijk-class_frigate" title="Van Speijk-class frigate"><i>van Speijk</i>-class&#x20;frigates</a>), and a considerable number of <a href="/wiki/Minesweeper" title="Minesweeper">minesweepers</a>.
</p><p>As a member of <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a>, the Netherlands developed its security policy in close cooperation with other members. The establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Warsaw_pact" class="mw-redirect" title="Warsaw pact">Warsaw pact</a> in 1955 intensified the arms race between West and East. Technical innovations rapidly emerged, the introduction of radar and sonar were followed by <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">nuclear weapon</a> systems and long-range missiles. The geopolitical situation allowed for a fixed military strategy. Beginning in 1965, the Dutch Navy joined certain permanent NATO squadrons like the <a href="/wiki/Standing_Naval_Force_Atlantic" class="mw-redirect" title="Standing Naval Force Atlantic">Standing Naval Force Atlantic</a>.
</p>

<h3><span id="Naval_aviation_.E2.80.93_maritime_helicopters"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Naval_aviation_–_maritime_helicopters">Naval aviation – maritime helicopters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Netherlands_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Naval aviation – maritime helicopters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The total tonnage will be approx. 140,000 tonnes. Next to these ships a lot of other smaller vessels remain in the navy.
</p><p>With these changes the Royal Netherlands Navy will have 10 large oceangoing vessels ranging from medium/low to high combat action ships. The renewed Dutch Navy will be a <a href="/wiki/Green-water_navy" title="Green-water navy">green-water navy</a>, having enough frigates and auxiliaries to operate far out at sea, while depending on land-based air support, and, with the large amphibious squadron, they will have significant <a href="/wiki/Brown-water_navy" title="Brown-water navy">brown-water navy</a> capabilities.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_changes">Future changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Netherlands_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Future changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Women are expected to <a href="/wiki/Women_in_the_military#Women_on_submarines" title="Women in the military">join submarine crews</a> in the Royal Netherlands Navy in 2019, with the addition of shower doors and changing room curtains.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2018, the Dutch Government approved a multi-year investment program and allocated funds for the 2018–2030 period, including;
</p>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Walrus-class_submarine" title="Walrus-class submarine"><i>Walrus</i>-class&#x20;submarines</a> replacement in 2025. The sub's are currently undergoing a Service-life Extension Program (SLEP), including new sonar, new optronic periscope and weapon upgrades for near shore operations. The Royal Dutch Navy is evaluating <a href="/wiki/A26_submarine" class="mw-redirect" title="A26 submarine">Saab/Damen (A-26)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Type_212_submarine" title="Type 212 submarine">TKMS (Upgraded 212)</a>, <a href="/wiki/S-80-class_submarine" class="mw-redirect" title="S-80-class submarine">Navantia S-80</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Barracuda-class_submarine" class="mw-redirect" title="French Barracuda-class submarine">Naval Group (SSK version of Barracuda)</a> proposal, decision is due early 2019.</li>
<li>Upgrading the <a href="/wiki/De_Zeven_Provinci%C3%ABn-class_frigate" title="De Zeven Provinciën-class frigate"><i>De Zeven Provinciën</i>-class LCF frigates</a> Theatre Ballistic Missile Defense, acquisition of SM-3 missiles, a new 127mm canon, ESSM-2 and SLCM integration 2018–2021 with a planned replacement from 2028 onwards.</li>
<li>Replacement of the <a href="/wiki/Karel_Doorman-class_frigate" title="Karel Doorman-class frigate"><i>Karel Doorman</i>-class M frigates</a> in 2025 by initially 4 ships, 2 to be operated by the Belgian Navy and 2 by the Dutch navy, designed &amp; build by Damen Shipyards.<sup id="cite_ref-nieuweschepenmarine_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nieuweschepenmarine-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> See <a href="/wiki/Future_Surface_Combatant_(Koninklijke_Marine)" title="Future Surface Combatant (Koninklijke Marine)">Future Surface Combatant</a> for more information.</li>
<li>Replacement of the 6 <a href="/wiki/Tripartite-class_minehunter" title="Tripartite-class minehunter"><i>Alkmaar</i>-class MCM ships</a> from 2024 including MCM Drones. 6 units will be built for both the Belgian and Dutch navies with a total of 12 ships.<sup id="cite_ref-nieuweschepenmarine_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nieuweschepenmarine-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The contract was won by <a href="/wiki/Naval_Group" title="Naval Group">Naval Group</a> on 15 March 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Increasing the size of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Netherlands_Marine_Corps" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Netherlands Marine Corps">Royal Netherlands Marine Corps</a> to remain highly integrated with the British Royal Marines. In 2017 the Ministry of Defence announced the formation of a Fleet Marine Squadron for the protection of merchant ships.</li>
<li>The German Navy Seebatallion (Marines) will be integrated into the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Netherlands_Marine_Corps" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Netherlands Marine Corps">Royal Netherlands Marine Corps</a>.</li>
<li>Cooperation with the <a href="/wiki/German_Navy" title="German Navy">German Navy</a> regarding Submarine &amp; Amphibious Operations.</li>
<li>Acquisition of a new Combat Support Ship to replace the former Zr.Ms. Amsterdam, designed &amp; build by Damen Shipyards.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> This ship will be based on the JSS Karel Doorman design to improve type commonality (architecture &amp; components) and is expected to be operational in 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The ship will be named Zr.Ms. Den Helder after the city of Den Helder, with the pennant A834.</li>
<li>Acquisition of new LCU's in 2025 with additional capacity to support amphibious operations and the integration of the German Navy Marines (Seebatallion).</li>
<li>Main Naval Weapons will be replaced by acquiring ESSM-2, 127mm canon, Harpoon ASuW replacement, SM-3, SM-2 IIIC SAM, Goalkeeper CIWS replacement, MK 46 &amp; MK 48 Torpedo replacement and SLCM (study).</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theater_ballistic_missile_defense">Theater ballistic missile defense</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Netherlands_Navy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Theater ballistic missile defense">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Together with the United States and several other <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> members, the Dutch Navy is testing and updating its ships for <a href="/wiki/Tactical_ballistic_missile" title="Tactical ballistic missile">Tactical ballistic missile</a> defense capability. Although tests conducted concerning the capability of the APAR (<a href="/wiki/Active_Phased_Array_Radar" class="mw-redirect" title="Active Phased Array Radar">Active Phased Array Radar</a>) have been very successful, in 2018 the Dutch Government decided to acquire the SM-3 missiles for integration into the existing weapon suite of the LCF frigates. Four ships will be fitted out for tactical ballistic missile defense. The four LCFs will be fitted out with only eight SM-3 missiles each, due to the high costs for each missile (approximately $2.5–$5&#160;million).
</p>
