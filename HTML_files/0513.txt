<p><b>Melvin Manhoef</b> (born 11 May 1976) is a <a href="/wiki/Surinamese_people" title="Surinamese people">Surinamese</a>-<a href="/wiki/Dutch_people" title="Dutch people">Dutch</a> professional <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">mixed martial artist</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxer</a>. In kickboxing, he is a former <a href="/wiki/It%27s_Showtime_(kickboxing)" title="It&#39;s Showtime (kickboxing)">It's Showtime Champion</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_arts" title="Mixed martial arts">MMA</a> he was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Cage_Rage_champions#World_Light_Heavyweight_Championship" title="List of Cage Rage champions">Cage Rage Light Heavyweight Champion</a>. In mixed martial arts, he is currently competing in <a href="/wiki/Bellator_MMA" title="Bellator MMA">Bellator</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Middleweight_(MMA)" title="Middleweight (MMA)">Middleweight</a> division<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and has also formerly competed for <a href="/wiki/Strikeforce_(mixed_martial_arts)" title="Strikeforce (mixed martial arts)">Strikeforce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dream_(mixed_martial_arts)" title="Dream (mixed martial arts)">DREAM</a>, <a href="/wiki/RINGS" class="mw-redirect" title="RINGS">RINGS</a>, It's Showtime, <a href="/wiki/K-1" title="K-1">K-1</a>, <a href="/wiki/ONE_Fighting_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="ONE Fighting Championship">ONE FC</a>, and <a href="/wiki/KSW_(mixed_martial_arts)" class="mw-redirect" title="KSW (mixed martial arts)">KSW</a>. 28 of his 30 wins have come via knockout, the highest percentage of any MMA fighter with at least 15 wins.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melvin_Manhoef&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Manhoef was born in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. When he was three years old his family moved to <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a>, the Netherlands. Manhoef played <a href="/wiki/Soccer" class="mw-redirect" title="Soccer">soccer</a> during his youth, and was introduced to <a href="/wiki/Muay_Thai" title="Muay Thai">Muay Thai</a> by his younger brother, Moreno.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mixed_martial_arts_career">Mixed martial arts career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melvin_Manhoef&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mixed martial arts career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melvin_Manhoef&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the age of 18, Manhoef had his first fight, which he won by decision. He made his comeback in September 2001, as part of <a href="/wiki/Chakuriki_Gym" title="Chakuriki Gym">Chakuriki Gym</a>. In 2004, Manhoef entered the <a href="/wiki/Cage_Rage" class="mw-redirect" title="Cage Rage">Cage Rage</a> promotion in London. He became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Cage_Rage_champions#British_Light_Heavyweight_Championship" title="List of Cage Rage champions">British Cage Rage Light Heavyweight Champion</a> by beating Fabio Piamonte at Cage Rage 13. He defended his title for almost two years, until entering the <a href="/wiki/K-1" title="K-1">K-1</a>'s MMA affiliate promotion <a href="/wiki/Hero%27s" title="Hero&#39;s">Hero's</a> in 2006. He won his first fight at <a href="/wiki/Hero%27s" title="Hero&#39;s">Hero's 4</a> against <a href="/wiki/Shungo_Oyama" title="Shungo Oyama">Shungo Oyama</a> by <a href="/wiki/Technical_knockout" class="mw-redirect" title="Technical knockout">technical knockout</a> in first round.
</p><p>Melvin has switched his main focus to MMA over the past couple of years. He lost to <a href="/wiki/Dong-sik_Yoon" class="mw-redirect" title="Dong-sik Yoon">Dong-sik Yoon</a> by submission armbar in <a href="/wiki/K-1_Dynamite!!_USA" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 Dynamite!! USA">K-1 Dynamite!! USA</a>, he defeated <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Ackah" title="Bernard Ackah">Bernard Ackah</a> at <a href="/wiki/List_of_Hero%27s_events#K-1_Hero&#39;s_9_-_Middleweight_Tournament_Opening_Round" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Hero&#39;s events">K-1 Hero's Middleweight Tournament Opening Round</a>, and also got a TKO win against <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A1bio_Silva_(fighter)" title="Fábio Silva (fighter)">Fábio Silva</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Chute_Boxe" class="mw-redirect" title="Chute Boxe">Chute Boxe</a> fighter, by TKO in <a href="/wiki/List_of_Hero%27s_events#K-1_Hero&#39;s_10_-_Middleweight_Tournament_Final" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Hero&#39;s events">K-1 Hero's Middleweight GP Final</a>. At <a href="/wiki/Dream_4" title="Dream 4">Dream 4</a> Melvin fought <a href="/wiki/Kazushi_Sakuraba" title="Kazushi Sakuraba">Kazushi Sakuraba</a>. Manhoef and Sakuraba circled the ring for the first minute of the fight, before Manhoef dropped Sakuraba with a hard right head kick and finished him via TKO (hammerfists) at 90 seconds into Round 1. With the win, Melvin had advanced to the semifinal round of the DREAM Middleweight Grand Prix. At <a href="/wiki/Dream_6" title="Dream 6">Dream 6</a> Melvin fought <a href="/wiki/Gegard_Mousasi" title="Gegard Mousasi">Gegard Mousasi</a> as the tournament dictated. Wary of Manhoef's standup, Mousasi quickly took the fight to the ground despite attempts to shrug him with a sprawl. Mousasi took Manhoef's back until scrambling into a <a href="/wiki/Triangle_choke" title="Triangle choke">triangle choke</a> at 1:28 of the first round, surviving a powerful slam attempt from Manhoef while he had the submission secured. Mousasi went on to win the DREAM Middleweight Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At <a href="/wiki/Dynamite!!_2008" title="Dynamite!! 2008">Dynamite!! 2008</a> Melvin fought <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hunt" title="Mark Hunt">Mark Hunt</a> as a late alternate at heavyweight, despite usually functioning at two weight classes below his opponent. Regardless of the size disadvantage, he knocked down the iron-jawed <a href="/wiki/Samoans" title="Samoans">Samoan</a> in 18 seconds and followed with punches as he was postured over Hunt, until the referee stopped the fight.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It was the first time Hunt was finished by knockout in his <a href="/wiki/Mixed_Martial_Arts" class="mw-redirect" title="Mixed Martial Arts">MMA</a> career. He fought former <a href="/wiki/List_of_WEC_champions#Middleweight_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="List of WEC champions">WEC Middleweight Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Paulo_Filho" title="Paulo Filho">Paulo Filho</a> on 20 July 2009 at <a href="/wiki/Dream_10" title="Dream 10">Dream 10</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Despite early success on the feet with his striking offensive, Manhoef was taken down and submitted via <a href="/wiki/Armbar" class="mw-redirect" title="Armbar">armbar</a> in the first round.
</p><p>Manhoef signed a multi-year fight contract with <a href="/wiki/Strikeforce_(mixed_martial_arts)" title="Strikeforce (mixed martial arts)">Strikeforce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
Manhoef made his debut for the organization against <a href="/wiki/Robbie_Lawler" title="Robbie Lawler">Robbie Lawler</a> on 30 January 2010 at <a href="/wiki/Strikeforce:_Miami" title="Strikeforce: Miami">Strikeforce: Miami</a>. Despite a strong start with heavy leg kicks and punches, he was knocked out at 3:33 in the first round.
</p><p>Manhoef fought <a href="/wiki/Tatsuya_Mizuno" title="Tatsuya Mizuno">Tatsuya Mizuno</a> at <a href="/wiki/Dream_15" title="Dream 15">Dream 15</a> and lost by submission.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Manhoef returned to the United States in March 2011, facing <a href="/wiki/Tim_Kennedy_(fighter)" title="Tim Kennedy (fighter)">Tim Kennedy</a> at <a href="/wiki/Strikeforce:_Feijao_vs._Henderson" title="Strikeforce: Feijao vs. Henderson">Strikeforce: Feijao vs. Henderson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He lost the fight via submission in the first round.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Melvin_manhoef.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Melvin_manhoef.jpg/220px-Melvin_manhoef.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="198" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Melvin_manhoef.jpg/330px-Melvin_manhoef.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Melvin_manhoef.jpg/440px-Melvin_manhoef.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2537" data-file-height="2281" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Melvin_manhoef.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Manhoef in 2012.</div></div></div>
<p>On 31 January 2012 it was announced that Manhoef had signed with <a href="/wiki/ONE_Fighting_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="ONE Fighting Championship">ONE Fighting Championship</a> and would be fighting <a href="/wiki/Renato_Sobral" title="Renato Sobral">Renato Sobral</a> in the main event at <a href="/wiki/ONE_Fighting_Championship_3" class="mw-redirect" title="ONE Fighting Championship 3">ONE Fighting Championship 3</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  However, Sobral pulled out of the fight and Manhoef faced Yoshiyuki Nakanishi.  The fight ended in a No Contest due to an accidental injury to Manhoef's leg in round 1. Manhoef scored a knockout victory on 6 October 2012 against Ryo Kawamura, followed by a quick knockout of Dennis Kang on 31 December 2012. Melvin is now on a three-fight win streak in MMA and has gone 4–0 on New Year's Eve.
</p><p>Recently Manhoef was training with the famous <a href="/wiki/Black_House_(MMA)" title="Black House (MMA)">Black House</a> in the United States. He helped his teammate <a href="/wiki/Lyoto_Machida" title="Lyoto Machida">Lyoto Machida</a> train for a fight with <a href="/wiki/Dan_Henderson" title="Dan Henderson">Dan Henderson</a>. Manhoef openly hopes to sign with the UFC after visiting <a href="/wiki/UFC_156" title="UFC 156">UFC 156</a> in Las Vegas and meeting with <a href="/wiki/Dana_White" title="Dana White">Dana White</a>.
</p><p>In 2013 Manhoef fought UFC veteran <a href="/wiki/Brock_Larson" title="Brock Larson">Brock Larson</a> at <a href="/wiki/ONE_FC" class="mw-redirect" title="ONE FC">ONE FC</a> on 5 April. After controlling the standup and Larson literally running away from him, Manhoef was repeatedly taken down in the last two rounds and Larson won via unanimous decision.  He lost to <a href="/wiki/Mamed_Khalidov" title="Mamed Khalidov">Mamed Khalidov</a> at KSW23 on 8 June 2013.
</p><p>He lost to <a href="/wiki/Zabit_Samedov" title="Zabit Samedov">Zabit Samedov</a> by unanimous decision in the semi-finals of the 93&#160;kg/205&#160;lb kickboxing tournament at <i>Legend 2: Invasion</i> in Moscow, Russia on 9 November 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Manhoef faced <a href="/wiki/Evangelista_Santos" title="Evangelista Santos">Evangelista Santos</a> in a much-anticipated rematch for the Gringo Super Fight Welterweight title on 27 April.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The fight was Melvin's first at 170&#160;lbs. Manhoef won the bout via TKO, early in the first round.
</p><p>It was announced during the <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_15:_Istanbul" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 15: Istanbul">Glory 15: Istanbul</a></i> broadcast that Manhoef would be one of eight fighters competing in the <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_17:_Los_Angeles_-_Last_Man_Standing" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 17: Los Angeles - Last Man Standing">Glory 17: Los Angeles - Last Man Standing</a></i> middleweight tournament in <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood, California</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on June 21, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He lost to <a href="/wiki/Filip_Verlinden" title="Filip Verlinden">Filip Verlinden</a> in the quarter-finals, suffering a head kick knockdown in round one before losing a majority decision.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bellator_MMA">Bellator MMA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Melvin_Manhoef&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bellator MMA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 21 July 2014, <a href="/wiki/Bellator_MMA" title="Bellator MMA">Bellator MMA</a> announced that they signed <a href="/wiki/Paul_Daley" title="Paul Daley">Paul Daley</a> along with Melvin Manhoef.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Manhoef made his Bellator debut against <a href="/wiki/Doug_Marshall" title="Doug Marshall">Doug Marshall</a>, in a middleweight bout, at <a href="/wiki/Bellator_125" class="mw-redirect" title="Bellator 125">Bellator 125</a> on 19 September 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He won the fight via knockout in the first round.
</p><p>Manhoef faced Glory veteran <a href="/wiki/Joe_Schilling" title="Joe Schilling">Joe Schilling</a> on 15 November 2014 at <a href="/wiki/Bellator_131" class="mw-redirect" title="Bellator 131">Bellator 131</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> After a dominant first round that saw Manhoef drop Schilling twice, he lost the fight in the second round due to a knockout.
</p><p>Manhoef next faced <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Shlemenko" title="Alexander Shlemenko">Alexander Shlemenko</a> on 13 February 2015 at <a href="/wiki/Bellator_MMA_in_2015#Bellator133" title="Bellator MMA in 2015">Bellator 133</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He lost via knockout early in the second round. On 17 March 2015 Shlemenko was suspended indefinitely for failing a post fight drug test with elevated testosterone levels. The fight result was changed to a <a href="/wiki/No_contest_(combat_sports)" title="No contest (combat sports)">no contest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Manhoef faced <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Carvalho" title="Rafael Carvalho">Rafael Carvalho</a> on 20 May 2016 for the Bellator Middleweight Championship at <a href="/wiki/Bellator_MMA_in_2016#Bellator_155" title="Bellator MMA in 2016">Bellator 155</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Manhoef lost a controversial split decision. Bellator announcer Jimmy Smith called it one of the worst decisions he had ever seen in the promotion; likewise, 5 of 5 media outlets scored the bout in favor of Manhoef.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>An immediate rematch with Carvalho was scheduled to take place at <a href="/wiki/Bellator_MMA_in_2016#Bellator_168" title="Bellator MMA in 2016">Bellator 168</a>, on 10 December 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> However, Carvalho pulled out of the fight due to injury.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The rematch eventually took place on 8 April 2017 at <a href="/wiki/Bellator_MMA_in_2017#Bellator_176" title="Bellator MMA in 2017">Bellator 176</a>. Manhoef lost the bout via knockout due to a head kick in the fourth round.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Manhoef made a comeback to kickboxing and faced <a href="/wiki/Remy_Bonjasky" title="Remy Bonjasky">Remy Bonjasky</a> for the fourth time, after losing three times, on 29 October 2017. Manhoef won the bout by decision. He announced his retirement from kickboxing and stated that he would focus on his MMA career.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2018 it was revealed that Manhoef and Bellator had come to terms for an exclusive, multi-fight contract.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Manhoef was scheduled to face <a href="/wiki/Chidi_Njokuani" title="Chidi Njokuani">Chidi Njokuani</a> on 30 November 2018 at <a href="/wiki/Bellator_MMA_in_2018#Bellator_210" title="Bellator MMA in 2018">Bellator 210</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> However, on 1 November 2018 it was reported that Manhoef pulled out from the event due to injury and he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/John_Salter" title="John Salter">John Salter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After over two years away from MMA action, Manhoef returned and faced Kent Kauppinen at <a href="/wiki/Bellator_MMA_in_2019#Bellator_London/Bellator_223" title="Bellator MMA in 2019">Bellator 223</a>. He won the fight by unanimous decision.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
