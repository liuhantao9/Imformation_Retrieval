	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Suriname National Army</h1>

<p>After the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Statute_of_the_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Statute of the Kingdom of the Netherlands">Statute of the Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Netherlands_Army" title="Royal Netherlands Army">Royal Netherlands Army</a> was entrusted with the defence of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, while the defence of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antilles" title="Netherlands Antilles">Netherlands Antilles</a> was the responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Netherlands_Navy" title="Royal Netherlands Navy">Royal Netherlands Navy</a>. The army set up a separate <i>Troepenmacht in Suriname</i> (Forces in Suriname, TRIS). Upon independence in 1975, this force was turned into the <i>Surinaamse Krijgsmacht</i> (SKM):, <b>Surinamese Armed Forces</b>. On February 25, 1980, a group of 15 <a href="/wiki/Non-commissioned_officers" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-commissioned officers">non-commissioned officers</a> and one junior SKM officer, under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Sergeant_major" title="Sergeant major">sergeant major</a> <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a>, staged a <a href="/wiki/1980_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="1980 Surinamese coup d&#39;état">coup d'état</a> and overthrew the Government. Subsequently, the SKM was rebranded as <i>Nationaal Leger</i> (NL), <b>National Army</b>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> has provided limited military assistance to the Surinamese armed forces since the election of a democratic government in 1991. In recent years, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> has provided training to military officers and <a href="/wiki/Policy" title="Policy">policymakers</a> to promote a better understanding of the role of the military in a civilian government. Also, since the mid-1990s, the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People&#39;s Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a> has been donating military equipment and logistical material to the Surinamese Armed Forces, as has <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_National_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Suriname's  National Armed Forces are composed of some 2,500 personnel, the majority of whom are deployed in the Army of Suriname.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Surinamearmy.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Surinamearmy.jpg/350px-Surinamearmy.jpg" decoding="async" width="350" height="263" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Surinamearmy.jpg/525px-Surinamearmy.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Surinamearmy.jpg/700px-Surinamearmy.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Surinamearmy.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Suriname Soldiers in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a></div></div></div>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Air_Force">Air Force</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_National_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Air Force">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Roundel_of_Suriname.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Roundel_of_Suriname.svg/150px-Roundel_of_Suriname.svg.png" decoding="async" width="150" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Roundel_of_Suriname.svg/225px-Roundel_of_Suriname.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Roundel_of_Suriname.svg/300px-Roundel_of_Suriname.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="601" data-file-height="601" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Roundel_of_Suriname.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Roundel of the Suriname Air Force</div></div></div>
<p>In 1982 a small air arm was formed within the Suriname defense force called "Surinaamse Luchtmacht" in short also called LUMA. The first military aircraft of the young air force was a <a href="/wiki/Hughes_500" class="mw-redirect" title="Hughes 500">Hughes 500</a> - Model 369D helicopter, simply registered SAF-100 and being used for light observation tasks. Unfortunately the aircraft was written off in March 1982 killing all four occupants,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> but from May of the same year the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_Air_Force" title="Suriname Air Force">Suriname Air Force</a> was being equipped with four (Pilatus) <a href="/wiki/Britten_Norman" class="mw-redirect" title="Britten Norman">Britten Norman</a> BN-2B Defenders. Registered with the numbers SAF-001, SAF-002, SAF-003 and SAF-004. Later on during the decade a <a href="/wiki/Cessna_172" title="Cessna 172">Cessna 172</a> Skyhawk <i>(SAF-007)</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Cessna_206" title="Cessna 206">Cessna 206</a> <i>(SAF-200)</i> and in 1993 a <a href="/wiki/Cessna_303" class="mw-redirect" title="Cessna 303">Cessna T303</a> Crusader <i>(SAF-008)</i> was acquired. The first official Surinam <a href="/wiki/Air_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Air Force">Air Force</a> Commander from 1983 until 1989 was air force <a href="/wiki/Pilot_(aeronautics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pilot (aeronautics)">pilot</a> <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a> Eddie Alenso Savalie Djoe. He was one of the passenger victims of the <a href="/wiki/Surinam_Airways_Flight_764" title="Surinam Airways Flight 764">Surinam Airways Flight 764</a> accident in June 1989, by then he was already promoted to the rank of <a href="/wiki/Major" title="Major">Major</a>.
</p><p>All aircraft of the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_Air_Force" title="Suriname Air Force">Suriname Air Force</a> undertake border patrols, utility transport and SAR (Search &amp; Rescue) missions from the main base at Paramaribo - <a href="/wiki/Zorg_en_Hoop_Airport" title="Zorg en Hoop Airport">Zorg en Hoop</a> and are occasionally detached to Zanderij - <a href="/wiki/Johan_Adolf_Pengel_International_Airport" title="Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport">Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport</a>, Nieuw Nickerie - <a href="/wiki/Majoor_Henry_Fernandes_Airport" title="Majoor Henry Fernandes Airport">Major Fernandes Airfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albina_Airstrip" title="Albina Airstrip">Albina Airstrip</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moengo_Airstrip" title="Moengo Airstrip">Moengo Airstrip</a>. In 1986 anti-government guerrilla activity prompted the government to acquire a pair of <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A9rospatiale_Alouette_III" title="Aérospatiale Alouette III">Aérospatiale SA.316B Alouette III</a> <i>(SAF-400 &amp; SAF-500)</i> helicopters from the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Air_Force" title="Portuguese Air Force">Portuguese Air Force</a> (<i>Portuguese: Força Aérea Portuguesa</i>), formerly registered <i>FAP9350</i> &amp; <i>FAP9386</i>. In the same year also three <a href="/wiki/Pilatus_PC-7" title="Pilatus PC-7">Pilatus PC-7</a>'s <i>(SAF-111, SAF-112 &amp; SAF-113)</i> were ordered in Switzerland for COIN (Counter-Insurgency) missions. One of the Alouettes crashed and both delivered <a href="/wiki/PC-7" class="mw-redirect" title="PC-7">PC-7s</a> were returned to Switzerland but one was later re-delivered. In 1987 a <a href="/wiki/Bell_205" class="mw-redirect" title="Bell 205">Bell 205</a> Iroquois <i>(SAF-300)</i> was acquired from Venezuela and used as a gunship for five years prior to sale to the US as N6594S in 1991. It crashed in July 1987<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> due to a mechanical failure killing the American pilot Billy Pearson, seriously injuring the American mechanic and four other Surinamese crewmembers.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The helicopter was later repaired and back in action.
</p><p>Two <a href="/wiki/CASA_212" class="mw-redirect" title="CASA 212">CASA 212</a>-400s Aviocar transports <i>(SAF-212 &amp; SAF-214)</i> <a href="/wiki/Garrett_TPE331" class="mw-redirect" title="Garrett TPE331">Garret AiResearch TPE331-10HR</a> turbo-prop engined aircraft were delivered in 1999. One of these two Spanish built CASA 212-400s is a Maritime Patrol Aircraft version <i>(SAF-214)</i> which was modified for the maritime patrol role with a Bendix RDR-1500 surveillance radar.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Lack of spares and funding has hampered maintenance and sometimes grounding much of the SAF fleet. In 2012 six experts from Venezuela made an assessment for the Suriname Air Force on the rehabilitation of the Casa 212 airplanes and now the Suriname Air Force has sold them to Fayard Enterprises in the USA.
</p><p>Three single engined Indian <a href="/wiki/HAL_Chetak" class="mw-redirect" title="HAL Chetak">HAL Chetak</a> helicopters were ordered in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In a deal worth US$13.4 million with Hindustan Aeronautics Limited [HAL], facilitated through a line of credit from India. The deal was previous rumoured to include the more modern twin engined HAL Dhruv helicopters, but this proved to be wrong.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On 26 December 2012 ten technical personnel of the Suriname Air Force left to India to be trained to become certified helicopter mechanics.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In 2014 eight helicopter pilots from the Suriname Air Force were trained on operating the HAL Chetak helicopters in Bangalore, India.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2014 it was announced that Suriname's order for helicopters from India was in fact for HAL Chetak and not the HAL Dhruv even as the Chetak production line was planned to be shut down. Rumours aside, by the end of January 2015 the three Indian Chetak helicopters were assembled and delivered in Suriname as SAF-H001 <i>(c/n AH-350)</i>, SAF-H002 <i>(c/n AH-351)</i> &amp; SAF-H003 <i>(c/n AH-352)</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> These registrations later changed to SAF-153, SAF-303 and SAF-811 when operational training started. The plan is to have one helicopter each based at the city of Paramaribo (<a href="/wiki/Zorg_en_Hoop_Airport" title="Zorg en Hoop Airport">Zorg en Hoop Airport</a>), Nickerie (<a href="/wiki/Majoor_Henry_Fernandes_Airport" title="Majoor Henry Fernandes Airport">Majoor Henry Fernandes Airport</a>) and Albina (<a href="/wiki/Albina_Airstrip" title="Albina Airstrip">Albina Airstrip</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Finally on 13 March 2015 the helicopter fleet of three Chetaks was officially handed over by the Indian ambassador Subashini Murgesan to the Minister of Defence of Suriname Lamuré Latour in a ceremony at Zorg en Hoop.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> On the same day and occasion the Minister announced that a de Havilland Canada DHC-6 Twin Otter was about to be added to the airfleet of the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_Air_Force" title="Suriname Air Force">Suriname Air Force</a> shortly, with pilots already in training. Because of the financial crisis in Suriname, the Air Force has had to decommission one of its Chetaks.
</p>

<h3><span id="Navy_.26_Coast_Guard"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Navy_&amp;_Coast_Guard">Navy &amp; Coast Guard</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_National_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Navy &amp; Coast Guard">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1977 the Navy (Marine) of Suriname received three large patrol vessels from the Dutch, built by De Vries Scheepsbouw. With a length of 32 meters each ship had two Paxman 12YHMC diesel engines of 1200HP performing a maximum speed of 20 knots. Delivery was between February 1977 and 1978 and the hull numbers were S-401, S-402 &amp; S-403. Now all three are out of service, the last one S-401 later P-401 is still moored at the Marine Harbor. One was re-built as a luxurious yacht. All ships had their base at the Marine harbor on the Suriname river.
</p><p>Today most of the fleet of the Marine of Suriname are based at Boxel, close to the resort town of <a href="/wiki/Domburg,_Suriname" title="Domburg, Suriname">Domburg</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Wanica_District" title="Wanica District">Wanica District</a> on the Suriname river.
</p><p>In November 2012 the defence &amp; internal affairs Ministry of Surinam bought three patrol vessels from the French company Ocea for the <a href="/wiki/Coast_Guard" class="mw-redirect" title="Coast Guard">Coast Guard</a>. This order was worth 16 million euros. These patrol vessels will be used for fishery protection and to counterattack piracy in Surinamese waters. The first <a href="/wiki/Patrol_Boat" class="mw-redirect" title="Patrol Boat">Fast Patrol Boat</a> (P201), a 32 meters long, 6,3 meters wide FPB 98 type, was delivered in June 2013. The first boat arrived in Paramaribo with a container vessel from the port of Saint-Nazaire, France. The vessels can reach speeds of 30 knots. Delivery of the remaining two vessels (P101 &amp; P102), FPB 72 types (24 meters long), occurred by the end of July, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The Surinamese Government ordered the three vessels, accelerating planning to set up a coast guard for Suriname that will be deployed to conduct patrol duties and fight maritime crime activities like illegal fishing, drug trafficking, and piracy.
</p><p>The coast guard is a branch of the Ministry of Internal Affairs. Soldiers were transferred from the Navy Unit (Marine) of the National Army, to form the initial coast guard staff. The Maritime Authority of Suriname (MAS) is currently training 16 students from the Nature Technical Institute (NaTIn) and Technical Faculty of <a href="/wiki/Anton_de_Kom_University_of_Suriname" title="Anton de Kom University of Suriname">University of Suriname</a> on how to conduct technical maintenance of the vessels. Ocea sent a trainer along with the boats to help conduct a six-month course. Colonel Jerry Slijngaard heads the Government's coast guard committee. While the first three boats will barely be sufficient to patrol Suriname's territorial waters and combat maritime crime activities such piracy, rapid reaction is now possible.
</p><p>The unit has its own base on the banks of the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_River" title="Suriname River">Suriname River</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, with posts at the border with <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> (in western District Nickerie) and  <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a> (in eastern District Marowijne). Legislation on which the coast guard will be founded is almost finished. It will soon be tabled in the Council of Ministers and the Council of State, after which it will head for the National Assembly for approval. The new unit is a civil organisation, with authority to enforce the law in Suriname territorial waters. The Surinamese government does not intend to make cuts to the Navy (Marine), once the Coast Guard is fully operational. The Navy will keep operating in the high seas outside the 100-mile zone.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Command_structure">Command structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_National_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Command structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Surinamearmy17.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Surinamearmy17.jpg/300px-Surinamearmy17.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="162" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Surinamearmy17.jpg/450px-Surinamearmy17.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Surinamearmy17.jpg/600px-Surinamearmy17.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1104" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Surinamearmy17.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>President <a href="/wiki/Bouterse" class="mw-redirect" title="Bouterse">Bouterse</a>, the Supreme Commander-in-Chief of the Armed Forces</div></div></div>
<p>The President of the Republic, <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a>, is the head of the armed forces, with the title of the Supreme Commander-in-Chief of the Armed Forces (<i>Opperbevelhebber van de Strijdkrachten</i>). The President is the supreme authority for all national military matters . The President is assisted by the Minister of Defence in his role over the armed forces, Mr. Lamure Latour. The director of the Ministry of Defence is Mr. John Achong.
</p><p>Beneath the President and Minister of Defense is the Commander of the Armed Forces (<i>Bevelhebber van de Strijdkrachten</i>), <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">Colonel</a> Hedwig Gilaard, whose headquarters is in Paramaribo, was followed-up after three years by <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">Colonel</a> Ronni Benschop, who in turn was promoted to <a href="/wiki/Brigade_General" class="mw-redirect" title="Brigade General">Brigade General</a> in February 2014. This is the highest-ranked officer in the Armed Forces of Suriname ever.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Commander called "Bevelhebber", is the Military Chief, charged with command over the different Military Branches. 
The Military Branches and regional Military Commands report to the Commander.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commanders_of_the_Suriname_Armed_Forces_past_to_present">Commanders of the Suriname Armed Forces past to present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_National_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Commanders of the Suriname Armed Forces past to present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Yngwe_Elstak" title="Yngwe Elstak">Yngwe Elstak</a> (25 November 1975 – 25 February 1980)</li>
<li>Desi Bouterse (July 1980 – 3 December 1992)</li>
<li>Ivan Graanoogst (temporary; 3 December 1992 – 15 May 1993)</li>
<li>Arthy Gorré (15 May 1993 – 30 June 1995)</li>
<li>Glenn Sedney (30 June 1995 – 1 July 2001)</li>
<li>Ernst Mercuur (1 July 2001 – 4 February 2010)</li>
<li>Hedwig Gilaard (4 February 2010 – 10 July 2013)</li>
<li>Ronni Benschop (temporary; 10 July 2013 – February 2014)</li>
<li>Ronni Benschop (February 2014 – August 2015)</li>
<li>Adolf Jardim (acting) (August 2015 March 2019)</li>
<li>Robert Kartodikromo (March 2019 – present)</li></ul>
<p>The Armed Forces is Headquartered in Paramaribo.
</p><p>The task of the national army of Suriname is defending the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Suriname against foreign armed military aggression. That is, the defense of not only the territory but also the territorial waters and the airspace above it.
</p><p>The Ministry of defence consists of the Policy Centre and the operational part (the national army) that makes up the Defence Organization Forms. The Department of Defense has no departments. There are various services and units. The Policy Centre is responsible for the care of the armed forces so that timely and adequate it can perform the duties or missions assigned to it by law in an efficient and effective manner.
Providing assistance to international organisations, if and to the extent that command is given for that purpose by the competent authority. This is e.g. for humanitarian operations of the United Nations. Also providing assistance in the preparation and implementation of projects related to the socio-economic development of Suriname
</p><p>There are several military installations, barracks and detachments in the various districts including the Memre Boekoe Kazerne (Paramaribo), the Naval Marine base (district Wanica), the Air Force Luma base (Zorg en Hoop, Paramaribo), the training centre for recruits namely the Ayoko-barracks and the detachment Zanderij, the eastern border post, the Akontoe Velantie Kazerne at Albina, in Nickerie the western border post, the Professor Dr. Ali Kazerne and on the Kennedy Highway to Concordia the 1st Sgt Martowidjojo Kazerne. There are also various detachments and the so-called small stations throughout Suriname in the districts Sipaliwini, Saramacca, Brokopondo and Para. But also the protection of important objects such as the Afobakkadam or the bridge over the Coppename River belongs to the protective task of the National Army of Suriname.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conflicts">Conflicts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_National_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Conflicts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Contra">Contra</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_National_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Contra">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Armed Forces of Suriname were engaged in a domestic war against a few hundred <a href="/wiki/Resistance_movement" title="Resistance movement">freedom fighters</a> who named themselves "<a href="/wiki/Jungle_Commando" title="Jungle Commando">Jungle Commandos</a>" led by <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Brunswijk" title="Ronnie Brunswijk">Ronnie Brunswijk</a> between 1986 and 1992.
</p><p>Surinamese armed forces also fought against the Resistance Amerindian groups who call themselves "<a href="/w/index.php?title=Tucajana_Amazon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tucajana Amazon (page does not exist)">Tucajana Amazon</a>" and were led by Alex Jubitana and Thomas Sabajo. These Amerindian insurgents fought from 1986 to 1989. They opposed the expropriation of land owned by indigenous people and discrimination by the military regime.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Role">Role</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_National_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Role">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Defend the territorial integrity of Suriname.</li>
<li>Assist the civil power in the maintenance of law and order when required.</li>
<li>Contribute to the economic development of Suriname.</li></ul>
<p>The Army also participated in the Multi-National Force in Haiti in the 90's and were redeployed in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future">Future</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_National_Army&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Future">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With latest procurement of three HAL Chetak helicopters from India for the Air Force and three patrol vessels from France for the new Coast Guard the Armed Forces of Suriname should be better equipped to fulfill its roles in the future.
</p><p>In September 12, 2012. The Suriname defense minister, Lamouré Latour, discussed with the Brazilian defense minister the possibility of the Military of Suriname acquiring from two to four Brazilian Embraer AT-29B <a href="/wiki/Embraer_EMB_314_Super_Tucano" title="Embraer EMB 314 Super Tucano">Super Tucano</a> light attack (COIN) trainer planes, 500 ton light patrol ships and the revitalization of the armoured vehicles supplied from Brazil in 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
