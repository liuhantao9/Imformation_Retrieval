</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guayana_Region,_Venezuela&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The region has a population of 1,383,297 inhabitants and a territory of 458,344&#160;km<sup>2</sup>, slightly over half the area of the whole country. 
</p><p>During the colonial period until the early 18th century, it was known as <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Guiana" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish Guiana">Spanish Guiana</a>. It borders the independent nations of <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> (formerly <a href="/wiki/British_Guiana" title="British Guiana">British Guiana</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.
</p><p>Geologically it is part of the <a href="/wiki/Guiana_Shield" title="Guiana Shield">Guiana Shield</a>, and is also known as the <b>Venezuelan Guayana</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The higher elevation <a href="/wiki/Guayana_Highlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Guayana Highlands">Guayana Highlands</a> and its <a href="/wiki/Tepui" title="Tepui">tepuis</a> (mesas) are in the southwestern section.
</p>
