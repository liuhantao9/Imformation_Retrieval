<p><b>Jules Albert Wijdenbosch</b> (born 2 May 1942 in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Surinamese</a> politician who was <a href="/wiki/President_of_Suriname" title="President of Suriname">President of Suriname</a> from 1996 to 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Democratic_Party_(Suriname)" title="National Democratic Party (Suriname)">National Democratic Party</a>, which held absolute power in Suriname during the 1980s. He was <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister of Suriname">Prime Minister</a> from April 1987 until January 1988, <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_Suriname" title="Vice President of Suriname">Vice President</a> from January 1991 until September 1991,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/President_of_Suriname" title="President of Suriname">President</a> from September 1996 until August 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-timeline_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timeline-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is now the leader of <a href="/wiki/Democratic_National_Platform_2000" title="Democratic National Platform 2000">Democratic National Platform 2000</a>. He left a <a href="/wiki/Jules_Wijdenbosch_Bridge" title="Jules Wijdenbosch Bridge">bridge</a> bearing his name.
</p>
