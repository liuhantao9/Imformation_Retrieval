<p><b>Dutch colonisation of the Guianas</b>—the coastal region between the <a href="/wiki/Orinoco" title="Orinoco">Orinoco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amazon_River" title="Amazon River">Amazon</a> rivers in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>—began in the early 16th century. The <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> originally claimed all of <a href="/wiki/The_Guianas" title="The Guianas">Guiana</a> (also called <i>De wilde kust</i>, the "Wild Coast") but—following attempts to sell it first to Bavaria and then to <a href="/wiki/Hanauish_Indies" class="mw-redirect" title="Hanauish Indies">Hanau</a> and the loss of sections to Portugal, Britain, and France—the section actually settled and controlled by the Netherlands became known as <b>Dutch Guiana</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i>Nederlands-Guiana</i>).
</p><p>The colonies of <a href="/wiki/Essequibo_(colony)" title="Essequibo (colony)">Essequibo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Demerara" title="Demerara">Demerara</a> were controlled by the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_West_India_Company" title="Dutch West India Company">Dutch West India Company</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Berbice" title="Berbice">Berbice</a> and <a href="/wiki/Surinam_(Dutch_colony)" title="Surinam (Dutch colony)">Surinam</a> were controlled by the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Berbice" title="Society of Berbice">Society of Berbice</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Suriname" title="Society of Suriname">Society of Suriname</a>, respectively. <a href="/wiki/Cayenne_(Dutch_colony)" title="Cayenne (Dutch colony)">Cayenne</a> also came under brief periods of Dutch control. After the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a> in 1814, Britain gained control of the three colonies (Demerara, Berbice, and Essequibo) west of the <a href="/wiki/Courantyne_River" title="Courantyne River">Courantyne River</a>, which became <a href="/wiki/British_Guiana" title="British Guiana">British Guiana</a> and then modern <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>. The remaining colony, <a href="/wiki/Surinam_(Dutch_colony)" title="Surinam (Dutch colony)">Suriname</a> (also called "Dutch Guiana"), remained under Dutch control until its independence in 1975.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_colonisation_of_the_Guianas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_colonisation_of_the_Guianas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Guiana_and_Amazon_Region_-_1649.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Guiana_and_Amazon_Region_-_1649.jpg/220px-Guiana_and_Amazon_Region_-_1649.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="168" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Guiana_and_Amazon_Region_-_1649.jpg/330px-Guiana_and_Amazon_Region_-_1649.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Guiana_and_Amazon_Region_-_1649.jpg/440px-Guiana_and_Amazon_Region_-_1649.jpg 2x" data-file-width="19160" data-file-height="14645" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Guiana_and_Amazon_Region_-_1649.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A map of Dutch Guiana by <a href="/wiki/Jan_Janssonius" title="Jan Janssonius">Jan Janssonius</a>, 1649</div></div></div>
<p>The first written reference to the region was in a 1599 map, drawn by <a href="/wiki/Flemish" title="Flemish">Flemish</a> <a href="/wiki/Cartographer" class="mw-redirect" title="Cartographer">cartographer</a> <a href="/wiki/Jodocus_Hondius" title="Jodocus Hondius">Jodocus Hondius</a>. Beginning in 1600, the colonies were settled by Dutch colonists, most of whom came from the province of <a href="/wiki/Zeeland" title="Zeeland">Zeeland</a>. Trading posts were established near various rivers, including the <a href="/wiki/Pomeroon_River" title="Pomeroon River">Pomeroon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Essequibo_River" title="Essequibo River">Essequibo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berbice_River" title="Berbice River">Berbice</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Suriname_River" title="Suriname River">Suriname</a> rivers. Many small commercial establishments, mostly <a href="/wiki/Bartering" class="mw-redirect" title="Bartering">bartering</a> posts, were founded by <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a>, and <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> colonists. Due to the effects of disease and attacks from natives, these colonies rarely lasted long.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Establishment">Establishment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_colonisation_of_the_Guianas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Establishment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Dutch_West_India_Company" title="Dutch West India Company">Dutch West India Company</a> was created in 1621, and given unsupervised control of the colonies in South America. The colony was administered by <a href="/wiki/Abraham_van_Peere" title="Abraham van Peere">Abraham van Peere</a>, a Dutch explorer who had founded the settlement of <a href="/wiki/Berbice" title="Berbice">Berbice</a>. After the <a href="/wiki/Third_Anglo-Dutch_War" title="Third Anglo-Dutch War">Third Anglo-Dutch War</a>, England ceded the <a href="/wiki/Surinam_(Dutch_colony)" title="Surinam (Dutch colony)">colony of Suriname</a>, in exchange for <a href="/wiki/New_Amsterdam" title="New Amsterdam">New Amsterdam</a>.
</p><p>Dutch Guiana was not a political entity, but, rather, a geographical indication. The colonies that formed along Dutch Guiana were, initially, controlled by several entities. <a href="/wiki/Essequibo_(colony)" title="Essequibo (colony)">Essequibo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Demerara" title="Demerara">Demerara</a> were controlled by the Dutch West India Company, while Berbice and Suriname were controlled by the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Berbice" title="Society of Berbice">Society of Berbice</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Suriname" title="Society of Suriname">Society of Suriname</a>, respectively. <a href="/wiki/Cayenne_(Dutch_colony)" title="Cayenne (Dutch colony)">Cayenne</a> (<a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>) was also briefly controlled by the Dutch between 1660 and 1664, and again between 1676 and 1677.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Dissolution">Dissolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_colonisation_of_the_Guianas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dissolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_of_Dutch_Guiana_and_of_Cura%C3%A7ao,_Bonaire,_and_Aruba,_1781.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Map_of_Dutch_Guiana_and_of_Cura%C3%A7ao%2C_Bonaire%2C_and_Aruba%2C_1781.jpg/300px-Map_of_Dutch_Guiana_and_of_Cura%C3%A7ao%2C_Bonaire%2C_and_Aruba%2C_1781.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="240" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Map_of_Dutch_Guiana_and_of_Cura%C3%A7ao%2C_Bonaire%2C_and_Aruba%2C_1781.jpg/450px-Map_of_Dutch_Guiana_and_of_Cura%C3%A7ao%2C_Bonaire%2C_and_Aruba%2C_1781.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Map_of_Dutch_Guiana_and_of_Cura%C3%A7ao%2C_Bonaire%2C_and_Aruba%2C_1781.jpg/600px-Map_of_Dutch_Guiana_and_of_Cura%C3%A7ao%2C_Bonaire%2C_and_Aruba%2C_1781.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4980" data-file-height="3976" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_of_Dutch_Guiana_and_of_Cura%C3%A7ao,_Bonaire,_and_Aruba,_1781.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>"A map of the Dutch settlements of Surinam, Demerary, Issequibo, Berbices, and the islands of <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curassoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bonaire" title="Bonaire">Bonaire</a>, &amp;c."</i> (1781)</div></div></div>
<p>Under the <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a>, much of Dutch Guiana was once again occupied by the British. After the <a href="/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars" title="Napoleonic Wars">Napoleonic Wars</a> in 1814, Britain gained control of the three colonies (Demerara, Berbice, and Essequibo) west of the <a href="/wiki/Courantyne_River" title="Courantyne River">Courantyne River</a>. These three colonies became <a href="/wiki/British_Guiana" title="British Guiana">British Guiana</a>. After 1815, there were five Guianas, referred to by their dominant languages: Spanish Guayana (<a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>), British Guiana, Dutch Guiana, <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>, and Portuguese Guiana (<a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>).
</p><p>The colony that remained was part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a> until 1975, when it became independent as the <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Republic of Suriname</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_colonisation_of_the_Guianas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dutch Guiana covered the majority of the <a href="/wiki/Guiana_Shield" title="Guiana Shield">Guiana Shield</a>, with its borders ranging from the <a href="/wiki/Orinoco_Delta" title="Orinoco Delta">Orinoco Delta</a> in the northwest, the eastern banks of the <a href="/wiki/Caron%C3%AD_River" title="Caroní River">Caroní River</a> in the southwest, to the <a href="/wiki/Maraj%C3%B3" title="Marajó">Marajó</a> island of the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_River" title="Amazon River">Amazon River</a> delta in the southeast.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dutch_Guiana_or_Suriname">Dutch Guiana or Suriname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_colonisation_of_the_Guianas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Dutch Guiana or Suriname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the <a href="/wiki/Surinam_(Dutch_colony)" title="Surinam (Dutch colony)">Dutch colony of Surinam</a> has always been officially known as Surinam or Suriname, in both Dutch<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and English,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the colony was often unofficially and semi-officially referred to as Dutch Guiana (Dutch: <i>Nederlands Guiana</i>) in the 19th and 20th century, in an analogy to <a href="/wiki/British_Guiana" title="British Guiana">British Guiana</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>. Using this term for Suriname is problematic, however, as historically Suriname was only one of many Dutch colonies in <a href="/wiki/The_Guianas" title="The Guianas">the Guianas</a>, others being <a href="/wiki/Berbice" title="Berbice">Berbice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Essequibo_(colony)" title="Essequibo (colony)">Essequibo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Demerara" title="Demerara">Demerara</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pomeroon_(colony)" title="Pomeroon (colony)">Pomeroon</a>, which after being taken over by the United Kingdom in 1814, were united into British Guiana in 1831. Before 1814, the term Dutch Guiana did not describe a distinct political entity, but rather all colonies under Dutch sovereignty taken together.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> While referring to post-1814 governors of Suriname as governors of Dutch Guiana seems harmless, it is problematic doing the same for the pre-1814 governors, as that would imply that they had jurisdiction over the other Dutch colonies in the Guianas, which they had not.
</p>
