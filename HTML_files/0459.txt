	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Sir John Ramsden, 3rd Baronet</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Sir John Ramsden, 3rd Baronet</b>  (1699–1769) of <a href="/wiki/Byram,_North_Yorkshire" title="Byram, North Yorkshire">Byram</a> and Longley Hall, Yorkshire, was a British landowner and Independent Whig politician who sat in the <a href="/wiki/British_House_of_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="British House of Commons">House of Commons</a> from 1727 to 1754.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sir_John_Ramsden,_3rd_Baronet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ramsden was baptised on 21 March 1699, the eldest son of Sir William Ramsden, 2nd Baronet, of Byram and Longley Hall and his wife Elizabeth Lowther, daughter of <a href="/wiki/John_Lowther,_1st_Viscount_Lonsdale" title="John Lowther, 1st Viscount Lonsdale">John Lowther, 1st Viscount Lonsdale</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cokayne_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cokayne-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He was admitted at <a href="/wiki/Clare_College,_Cambridge" title="Clare College, Cambridge">Clare College, Cambridge</a> on 4 April 1718.<sup id="cite_ref-CU_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CU-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He succeeded his father in the <a href="/wiki/Ramsden_baronets" title="Ramsden baronets">baronetcy</a> on 27 June 1736.<sup id="cite_ref-Cokayne_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cokayne-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sir_John_Ramsden,_3rd_Baronet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/1727_British_general_election" title="1727 British general election">1727 British general election</a>, Ramsden was returned as <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Appleby_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Appleby (UK Parliament constituency)">Appleby</a> by his uncle, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Lowther,_3rd_Viscount_Lonsdale" title="Henry Lowther, 3rd Viscount Lonsdale">Henry Lowther, 3rd Viscount Lonsdale</a>. He was an independent Whig and voted with the Government in 1729 on the civil list arrears, in 1733 on the excise bill, and in 1734 on the repeal of the Septennial Act. He was returned at the <a href="/wiki/1734_British_general_election" title="1734 British general election">1734 British general election</a>, and voted against the government on the Spanish convention in 1739. He was considered an opposition member in  1740. He was returned again at the <a href="/wiki/1741_British_general_election" title="1741 British general election">1741 British general election</a> and was put forward though not elected for the secret committee on Walpole in 1742. However he voted against the Government on the Hanoverians in the following December and again in 1744. He was returned again at the <a href="/wiki/1747_British_general_election" title="1747 British general election">1747 British general election</a>  and was classed as a government supporter, although he  remained independent.  He did not stand again in  <a href="/wiki/1754_British_general_election" title="1754 British general election">1754</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HOP_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HOP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life_and_legacy">Later life and legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sir_John_Ramsden,_3rd_Baronet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Later life and legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ramsden married by licence dated 8 August 1748, Margaret Bright, widow of Thomas Liddell Bright of Badsworth, Yorkshire and daughter of William Norton of Sawley, Yorkshire. They had two sons and two daughters. In 1766 he was responsible for the building of the Cloth Hall at <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He died at Byrom on 10 April 1769 and was buried at Brotherton on 17 April. He was succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/Sir_John_Ramsden,_4th_Baronet" title="Sir John Ramsden, 4th Baronet">John</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cokayne_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cokayne-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
