<p><b>Fly All Ways</b> is an airline of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, based in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a> and started operations on January 10, 2016 with the launch of its inaugural flight above Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Its first commercial flight took place on January 22, 2016 to <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Lu%C3%ADs,_Maranh%C3%A3o" title="São Luís, Maranhão">São Luís</a>, capital of the state of <a href="/wiki/Maranh%C3%A3o" title="Maranhão">Maranhão</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. On February 5, 2016 this was followed by the first charter flight to <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Later in February 2016 first flights followed to <a href="/wiki/Willemstad" title="Willemstad">Willemstad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philipsburg,_Sint_Maarten" title="Philipsburg, Sint Maarten">Philipsburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a>. In the same month <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> granted the new airline rights to operate scheduled flights to Guyana with connections to Brazil and the Caribbean.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Fly All Ways is planning to fly to several cities in both the Caribbean and South America.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleet_acquisition">Fleet acquisition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fly_All_Ways&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fleet acquisition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Blue_Wing_Airlines" title="Blue Wing Airlines">Blue Wing Airlines</a>, another Suriname-based aviation company, entered into a sales agreement with <a href="/wiki/KLM_Royal_Dutch_Airlines" class="mw-redirect" title="KLM Royal Dutch Airlines">KLM Royal Dutch Airlines</a> to acquire two ex-KLM cityhopper <a href="/wiki/Fokker_70" title="Fokker 70">Fokker 70</a> twin jets early January 2014. The airline from Suriname took delivery of PH-KZV (msn 11556) and PH-WXA (11570) in 2014. <a href="/wiki/KLM_Cityhopper" title="KLM Cityhopper">KLM Cityhopper</a> Fokker 70 PH-KZV was withdrawn from service at Norwich on January 5, 2014 after arriving as KL1515 from Amsterdam, KLM Cityhopper Fokker 70 PH-WXA was withdrawn from service at Amsterdam in the morning of March 30, 2014 after arriving as KL1486 from Humberside. The aircraft then positioned Amsterdam – Norwich for maintenance early the same afternoon as KL9955 where both aircraft were being prepared for service. The aircraft have subsequently been sold to Fly All Ways, by then a new independent start up airline offering transport to regional destinations. On November 20, 2014 the first aircraft (PH-WXA) landed at the <a href="/wiki/Johan_Adolf_Pengel_International_Airport" title="Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport">Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport</a> of Suriname in Fly All Ways colours.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> One month later the second Fly All Ways aircraft (PH-KZV) arrived in Suriname. These two are now registered as PZ-TFA &amp; PZ-TFB in Suriname and stationed at the <a href="/wiki/Johan_Adolf_Pengel_International_Airport" title="Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport">Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport</a> where a new hangar was built.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transition_from_Regular_schedule_to_charter">Transition from Regular schedule to charter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fly_All_Ways&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Transition from Regular schedule to charter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The airline which was mainly flying between Suriname, Guyana and Barbados suspending its regular flights to reschedule its operations.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Although the Suriname base airlines suspend regular flights on January 15th 2017 charter flights are still available, the airline CEO also said it plans to base the airline in Guyana. However, a confirmed time has not yet been set to restart regular service.
</p>
