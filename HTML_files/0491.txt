<p><b>Iris Widiawatie Jharap</b> (born 1 May 1970) is a former Dutch <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricketer</a> who played twelve <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_One_Day_International_cricket" class="mw-redirect" title="Women&#39;s One Day International cricket">One Day Internationals</a> (ODIs) for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_women%27s_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Netherlands national women&#39;s cricket team">Dutch national side</a>, including at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Women%27s_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2000 Women&#39;s Cricket World Cup">2000 World Cup</a>.
</p><p>Born in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname_(Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands)" title="Suriname (Kingdom of the Netherlands)">Suriname</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Jharap played club cricket for Groen-Greel, KZKC (Klein Zwitserland de Krekels Combinatie) and HV &amp; CV Quick before making her ODI debut for the Netherlands in March 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Her debut came during the Dutch side's tour of <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, in the third ODI played against the <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka_national_cricket_team" title="Sri Lanka national cricket team">Sri Lankan national side</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-odi_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odi-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A right-arm <a href="/wiki/Fast_bowling" class="mw-redirect" title="Fast bowling">fast bowler</a>, her first <a href="/wiki/Wicket" title="Wicket">wicket</a> came in her second match, the fifth and final ODI, when she had Sri Lanka's <a href="/wiki/Captain_(cricket)" title="Captain (cricket)">captain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rasanjali_Silva" title="Rasanjali Silva">Rasanjali Silva</a>, caught by <a href="/wiki/Carolien_Salomons" title="Carolien Salomons">Carolien Salomons</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jharap was subsequently chosen for the Dutch squad at the 2000 World Cup, hosted by <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. She played in four of the Netherlands' seven matches at the tournament, with the Dutch team going winless.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Against <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_women%27s_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="South Africa national women&#39;s cricket team">South Africa</a>, she bowled only 1.2 overs, but took 1/2, her best figures at international level, having <a href="/wiki/Hanri_Strydom" title="Hanri Strydom">Hanri Strydom</a> stumped by <a href="/wiki/Rowan_Milburn" title="Rowan Milburn">Rowan Milburn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Jharap's remaining matches for the Netherlands came in a seven-ODI series against <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_national_women%27s_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Pakistan national women&#39;s cricket team">Pakistan</a> during the 2000–01 season.<sup id="cite_ref-odi_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odi-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  She played in all but the fourth game of the series, and took four wickets at an <a href="/wiki/Bowling_average" title="Bowling average">average</a> of 26.00. Her economy rate of 3.35 runs per over ranked behind only Carolien Salomons and <a href="/wiki/Helmien_Rambaldo" title="Helmien Rambaldo">Helmien Rambaldo</a> for the Dutch.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
