<p><b>Prometheus Global Media</b> was a <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Business-to-business" title="Business-to-business">B2B</a> media company. The company was formed in December 2009, when <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Nielsen Company">Nielsen Company</a> sold its entertainment and media division to a <a href="/wiki/Private_equity" title="Private equity">private equity</a>-backed group led by Pluribus Capital Management and <a href="/wiki/Guggenheim_Partners" title="Guggenheim Partners">Guggenheim Partners</a>. Guggenheim acquired Pluribus's stake in the company in January 2013, giving it full ownership under the division of Guggenheim Digital Media.
</p><p>The company owned and operated a number of major <a href="/wiki/Entertainment_industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Entertainment industry">entertainment industry</a> <a href="/wiki/Trade_publication" class="mw-redirect" title="Trade publication">trade publications</a> and their associated digital properties, including <i><a href="/wiki/Adweek" title="Adweek">Adweek</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Backstage_(magazine)" title="Backstage (magazine)">Backstage</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Film_Journal_International" title="Film Journal International">Film Journal International</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i>.
</p><p>On December 17, 2015, it was announced that Guggenheim would spin out its media properties to a group led by former executive Todd Boehly, known as <a href="/wiki/Eldridge_Industries" title="Eldridge Industries">Eldridge Industries</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prometheus_Global_Media&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding">Founding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prometheus_Global_Media&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Founding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On December 10, 2009, the Nielsen Company announced that it would sell its Business Media division, which included brands such as <i><a href="/wiki/Adweek" title="Adweek">Adweek</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i>, to a new company known as e5 Global Media; a joint venture between <a href="/wiki/Guggenheim_Partners" title="Guggenheim Partners">Guggenheim Partners</a> and Pluribus Capital Management—a company led by James Finkelstein, Matthew Doull, and George Green. Two Nielsen properties, <i><a href="/wiki/Editor_%26_Publisher" title="Editor &amp; Publisher">Editor &amp; Publisher</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Kirkus_Reviews" title="Kirkus Reviews">Kirkus Reviews</a></i>, were not included in the sale, and were to be shut down.<sup id="cite_ref-adage-adweeksold_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adage-adweeksold-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <i>Editor &amp; Publisher</i> would instead be sold to the Duncan McIntosh Company,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and <i>Kirkus Reviews</i> would be sold to <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Simon_(real_estate)" title="Herbert Simon (real estate)">Herbert Simon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_Rich2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Rich2-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The company's first CEO was <a href="/wiki/Richard_Beckman" title="Richard Beckman">Richard Beckman</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> previously an executive and publisher at Condé Nast and Fairchild Publications,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and former publisher of magazines <i>GQ</i> and <i>Vogue</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-adweek-newhome_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adweek-newhome-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Beckman's career suffered a setback in 1999 following "some inappropriate behavior" resulting in injuries to <i>Vogue</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s West Coast advertising director Carol Matthews, while Beckman was Matthews' publisher at Condé Nast.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT-pays_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-pays-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Beckman's first major move was a re-launch of <i>The Hollywood Reporter</i>; with the hiring of <a href="/wiki/Janice_Min" title="Janice Min">Janice Min</a>, formerly of <i><a href="/wiki/Us_Weekly" title="Us Weekly">Us Weekly</a></i>, as editorial director, <i>THR</i> replaced its daily print publication with a weekly magazine, and performed a significant redesign to its website with an increased focus on breaking scoops. The new format was meant to compete against up-and-coming <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blogs</a> focusing on industry news, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Deadline_Hollywood" title="Deadline Hollywood">Deadline Hollywood</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/TheWrap" title="TheWrap">TheWrap</a></i>, along with its then-struggling rival <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i>. The changes had a significant impact on the publication's performance: by 2013, ad sales were up more than 50%, while traffic to the magazine's website had grown by 800%.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In October 2010, the company was renamed Prometheus Global Media; named after the <a href="/wiki/Prometheus" title="Prometheus">Greek mythological figure</a>, Beckman stated in an internal memo that the new name would "[carry] more weight and gravitas in the marketplace."<sup id="cite_ref-folio-name_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-folio-name-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Re-organization_and_acquisition">Re-organization and acquisition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prometheus_Global_Media&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Re-organization and acquisition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In late 2011, Prometheus went through a number of <a href="/wiki/Cost_reduction" title="Cost reduction">cost-cutting</a> measures. In August 2011, <i>Backstage</i> was sold to a group of investors led by John Amato in a transaction funded by Guggenheim, and the following month, Prometheus laid off the staff responsible for the <i>Hollywood Creative Directory</i> and announced it had sold the publication.<sup id="cite_ref-deadline-backstagesold_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deadline-backstagesold-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-thr-backstagesold_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thr-backstagesold-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-deadline-cuts_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deadline-cuts-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2013, Guggenheim Partners acquired the stake in Prometheus owned by Pluribus Capital, giving it full ownership; following the acquisition, former <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a> executive <a href="/wiki/Ross_Levinsohn" title="Ross Levinsohn">Ross Levinsohn</a> was named as CEO of the new Guggenheim Digital Media division, which would oversee Prometheus and other digital assets for Guggenheim companies (such as <a href="/wiki/Dick_Clark_Productions" title="Dick Clark Productions">Dick Clark Productions</a>). In April 2013, Guggenheim re-acquired <i>Backstage</i> (which had also acquired Sonicbids, a platform for allowing musicians to book <a href="/wiki/Gig_(music)" title="Gig (music)">gigs</a> online) and made its CEO John Amato president of the Billboard Group—a new group consisting of <i>Billboard</i>, <i>Backstage</i>, and Sonicbids.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard-group_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-group-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-variety-shakeup_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety-shakeup-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nyp-bound_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyp-bound-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-adweek-guggenbackstage_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adweek-guggenbackstage-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a January 2014 restructuring, Levinsohn was shifted to a business development role and no longer directly manages the Prometheus properties. Additionally, the company was split into two operating groups; an Entertainment Group was formed by merging <i>The Hollywood Reporter</i> into the Billboard Group, with Janice Min becoming co-president and <a href="/wiki/Chief_creative_officer" title="Chief creative officer">chief creative officer</a> of the group alongside Amato. The remaining properties, consisting of <i>Adweek</i> and Film Expo Group, are led by Jeff Wilbur.<sup id="cite_ref-variety-shakeup_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety-shakeup-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-billboard-minthr_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard-minthr-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 29, 2014, Prometheus announced it would acquire the publishing assets of <a href="/wiki/Mecklermedia" title="Mecklermedia">Mediabistro</a>—a network of websites focusing on various aspects of the <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">mass media</a> industry—which includes the media job listing site <i><a href="/wiki/Mediabistro_(website)" title="Mediabistro (website)">Mediabistro</a></i> and its network of blogs such as <i>AgencySpy</i>, <i>FishbowlNY</i>, <i>Lost Remote</i> and <i>TVNewser</i>—for $8 million. The acquisition did not include Mediabistro's expo business, which were retained under the name Mecklermedia.<sup id="cite_ref-adweek-mbbuy_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adweek-mbbuy-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fishbowlny-pgmsell_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fishbowlny-pgmsell-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On January 13, 2015, <i><a href="/wiki/Adweek" title="Adweek">Adweek</a></i> and Film Expo Group were merged into Mediabistro to form a new Prometheus subsidiary, Mediabistro Holdings. At the same time, its blogs were re-launched under the new "Adweek Blog Network" banner, and all of Mediabistro's social media-oriented blogs were merged into <i>SocialTimes</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-adweek-blognetlaunch_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adweek-blognetlaunch-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2015, Guggenheim Partners reported that its president Todd Boehly was exploring the possibility of forming his own company. A representative stated that such a company would "likely be harmonious with Guggenheim, especially since Todd's role for some time has been strategic and transaction-oriented, rather than working in or managing any of our day-to-day businesses."<sup id="cite_ref-variety-bohleycompany_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety-bohleycompany-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On December 17, 2015, in response to losses across Guggenheim Partners, the company announced that it would spin out its media properties to a group led by Boehly, including the Hollywood Reporter-Billboard Media Group, Mediabistro, and <a href="/wiki/Dick_Clark_Productions" title="Dick Clark Productions">Dick Clark Productions</a>, all under their existing leadership.<sup id="cite_ref-deadline-guggensell_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-deadline-guggensell-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-thewrap-todd_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thewrap-todd-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-thr-spinout_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thr-spinout-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The resultant company is known as <a href="/wiki/Eldridge_Industries" title="Eldridge Industries">Eldridge Industries</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
