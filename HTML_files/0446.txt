<p><b>Europa Nostra</b> (<a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "Our Europe") is a pan-European Federation for <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage" title="Cultural heritage">Cultural Heritage</a>, representing citizens’ organisations that work on safeguarding Europe's cultural and <a href="/wiki/Natural_heritage" title="Natural heritage">natural heritage</a>. It is the voice of this movement to relevant international bodies, in particular the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Europe" title="Council of Europe">Council of Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It has consultative status with UNESCO<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and is recognised as an NGO partner.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation_and_objectives">Organisation and objectives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Europa_Nostra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organisation and objectives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Europa Nostra's network covers almost 50 countries across Europe and beyond. It is composed of over 250 member organisations (heritage associations and foundations with a combined membership of more than 5 million people), 150 associated organisations (governmental bodies, local authorities and corporations) and also 1500 individual members who directly support the mission of Europa Nostra.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Europa Nostra's main goal is to place heritage and its benefits in the mainstream of public consciousness and to make heritage a higher priority for public policy both at European and national levels. Its specific objectives are to promote, at a European level, high standards of quality in the fields of <a href="/wiki/Heritage_conservation" class="mw-redirect" title="Heritage conservation">heritage conservation</a>, architecture, urban and rural planning and to advocate a balanced and <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a> of urban and rural, built and natural environment. Europa Nostra also seeks to highlight the importance of cultural heritage as a building block of European identity and as contributing to a strengthening sense of <a href="/wiki/European_citizenship" class="mw-redirect" title="European citizenship">European citizenship</a>.
</p><p>Its activities are coordinated by an International Secretariat based in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a> (Netherlands) headed by Europa Nostra's Secretary General, Sneška Quaedvlieg-Mihailović (Netherlands/Serbia). In several countries, the International Secretariat is assisted in its tasks by country representations.
</p><p>Europa Nostra's main activities are: 
</p>
<ol><li>to act as a representative lobby for cultural heritage in Europe</li>
<li>to celebrate and promote excellence through the <a href="/wiki/European_Union_Prize_for_Cultural_Heritage_/_Europa_Nostra_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="European Union Prize for Cultural Heritage / Europa Nostra Awards">European Union Prize for Cultural Heritage / Europa Nostra Awards</a>;</li>
<li>to save Europe's historic monuments, sites and cultural landscapes which are in danger, and</li>
<li>to animate a network of cultural heritage stakeholders in Europe.</li></ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Europa_Nostra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Europa Nostra was founded in 1963 on the initiative of <a href="/wiki/Italia_Nostra" title="Italia Nostra">Italia Nostra</a>, as a response to the serious threat to the survival of <a href="/wiki/Venice" title="Venice">Venice</a>, caused by regular flooding. In 1991 it merged with the <i>Internationales Burgen Institut</i> (the International Castles Institute), itself created in 1949.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Today, Europa Nostra is presided over by world-renowned opera singer Maestro <a href="/wiki/Pl%C3%A1cido_Domingo" title="Plácido Domingo">Plácido Domingo</a> (Spain), and its Executive President is Denis de Kergorlay (France). Previous presidents have been <a href="/wiki/Infanta_Pilar,_Duchess_of_Badajoz" title="Infanta Pilar, Duchess of Badajoz">the Infanta Doña Pilar de Borbón</a> from Spain (2007–2009), <a href="/wiki/Henrik,_Prince_Consort_of_Denmark" title="Henrik, Prince Consort of Denmark">the Prince Consort of Denmark</a> (1990–2007) and <a href="/wiki/Hans_de_Koster" title="Hans de Koster">Hans de Koster</a> from the Netherlands (1984–1990).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lobby_for_cultural_heritage">Lobby for cultural heritage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Europa_Nostra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lobby for cultural heritage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Acting as a European cultural heritage lobby, Europa Nostra seeks to secure adequate support for <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage" title="Cultural heritage">cultural heritage</a> in various areas of European policy-making and funding. It advocates the need to take cultural heritage into consideration when formulating and implementing all European and national policies which have an impact – direct or indirect – on heritage. Europa Nostra also seeks to highlight the importance and the specific character of cultural heritage within the wider cultural and policy agenda of its international partners, the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Europe" title="Council of Europe">Council of Europe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO</a>.
</p><p>During its European Heritage Congress held in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> (June 2011), Europa Nostra and 27 other European and international networks and organisations active in the wider field of cultural heritage decided to set up the <a href="/wiki/European_Heritage_Alliance_3.3" title="European Heritage Alliance 3.3">European Heritage Alliance 3.3</a>.
</p><p>In March 2010, Europa Nostra opened a liaison office in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, whose task is to coordinate Europa Nostra's lobbying of EU institutions and other European and international bodies based in Brussels, <a href="/wiki/Strasbourg" title="Strasbourg">Strasbourg</a> and Paris.
</p>
<h2><span id="European_Union_Prize_for_Cultural_Heritage.2FEuropa_Nostra_Awards"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="European_Union_Prize_for_Cultural_Heritage/Europa_Nostra_Awards">European Union Prize for Cultural Heritage/Europa Nostra Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Europa_Nostra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: European Union Prize for Cultural Heritage/Europa Nostra Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The European Union Prize for Cultural Heritage/Europa Nostra Awards reward excellence in <a href="/wiki/Cultural_heritage" title="Cultural heritage">cultural heritage</a> <a href="/wiki/Conservation_(cultural_heritage)" class="mw-redirect" title="Conservation (cultural heritage)">conservation</a>, ranging from the restoration of buildings and their adaptation to new uses, to urban and rural landscape rehabilitation, <a href="/wiki/Archaeological_site" title="Archaeological site">archaeological site</a> interpretations, and care for art collections. Moreover, it highlights research, dedicated service to heritage conservation by individuals or organisations, and education projects related to cultural heritage. The awards are supported by the <a href="/wiki/Creative_Europe" title="Creative Europe">Creative Europe</a> programme. It has been organised by Europa Nostra since 2002. 
</p><p>This awards scheme aims to promote high standards and high-quality skills in conservation practice and to stimulate trans-frontier exchange in heritage protection. By spreading the "Power of Example", the awards also aim to encourage further efforts and projects related to heritage throughout Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Europa Nostra is the main partner of "<a href="/wiki/The_Best_in_Heritage" title="The Best in Heritage">The Best in Heritage</a>", an annual presentation of awarded museum, heritage, and conservation projects, which takes place in the second part of September in the world heritage town of <a href="/wiki/Dubrovnik" title="Dubrovnik">Dubrovnik</a> (Croatia).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Heritage_in_Danger">Heritage in Danger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Europa_Nostra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Heritage in Danger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Europa Nostra supports national and international campaigns for the preservation and rescue of Europe's heritage which is in danger. In January 2013 it launched with the <a href="/wiki/European_Investment_Bank" title="European Investment Bank">European Investment Bank Group</a>, represented by the <a href="/wiki/European_Investment_Bank" title="European Investment Bank">EIB</a> Institute, the <a href="/wiki/7_Most_Endangered_Programme" title="7 Most Endangered Programme">7 Most Endangered Programme</a> to identify endangered monuments and sites in Europe and mobilize public and private partners on a local, national and European level to find a future for those sites, working as a catalyst for action.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The 7 Most Endangered sites and monuments for 2013 were selected from a shortlist of 14 prepared by an international panel that reviewed 40 nominations from member organizations from 21 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In recent years, Europa Nostra has raised its voice to save endangered historic monuments, sites or landscapes in Europe such as:
</p>
<ul><li>The area of <a href="/wiki/Rosia_Montana" class="mw-redirect" title="Rosia Montana">Rosia Montana</a> (<a href="/wiki/Transylvania" title="Transylvania">Transylvania</a>, Romania) whose heritage and environment is threatened by a proposed open-cast <a href="/wiki/Gold_mine" class="mw-redirect" title="Gold mine">gold mine</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The Roman archaeological site of <a href="/wiki/Allianoi" title="Allianoi">Allianoi</a> (Turkey) threatened by the construction of an irrigation dam (today, the site has disappeared under water);<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mont_Saint-Michel" class="mw-redirect" title="Mont Saint-Michel">Mont Saint-Michel</a> (<a href="/wiki/Normandy" title="Normandy">Normandy</a>, France) threatened by the proposed construction of giant <a href="/wiki/Wind_turbines" class="mw-redirect" title="Wind turbines">wind-turbines</a> having a negative visual impact on the cultural landscape surrounding the Mont;<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Italian historic cities, mainly in the <a href="/wiki/Emilia_Romagna" class="mw-redirect" title="Emilia Romagna">Emilia Romagna</a> region, affected by the earthquakes in May 2012;<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The Ancient City of <a href="/wiki/L%E2%80%99Aquila" class="mw-redirect" title="L’Aquila">l’Aquila</a> and the surrounding distinctive villages (<a href="/wiki/Abruzzo" title="Abruzzo">Abruzzo</a> Region, Italy) threatened by various degrees of destruction or damage caused by the April 2008 earthquake;</li>
<li>The Ancient City of <a href="/wiki/Famagusta" title="Famagusta">Famagusta</a> (Cyprus) threatened by neglect caused by on-going political conflict;</li>
<li>The remains of the Mediaeval Town Hall of <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a> (Germany) as well as the city's historic gas lanterns both threatened by new infrastructure works;</li>
<li>The Piazza Sant’Ambrogio in <a href="/wiki/Milano" class="mw-redirect" title="Milano">Milano</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a>, Italy) threatened by the proposed construction of an underground car-park.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pan-European_Network">Pan-European Network</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Europa_Nostra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Pan-European Network">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Europa Nostra serves as a platform for exchange for those concerned with <a href="/wiki/Heritage_conservation" class="mw-redirect" title="Heritage conservation">heritage conservation</a>, education, research, communication and <a href="/wiki/Language_interpretation" title="Language interpretation">interpretation</a>. It enables heritage professionals, volunteers and supporters from all over Europe and beyond to meet, debate and inspire each other. It also engages with a wider range of stakeholders, be they policy makers, other European or international networks related to heritage, students and young heritage professionals or the public at large. Europa Nostra is a partner of Wiki Loves Monuments, the Wikipedia photo contest around cultural heritage.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Europa Nostra organises an annual European Heritage Congress, including a public Forum on various heritage subjects that are of relevance to the whole of Europe. It also holds smaller-scale national, regional and local meetings and debates with heritage NGOs in different parts of Europe. In addition to this, Europa Nostra's Scientific Council organises an annual Colloquium to promote and coordinate the scientific study of ancient structures and fortified buildings in Europe.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Membership">Membership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Europa_Nostra&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Membership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Europa Nostra is a non-profit organisation, which is financially supported by membership fees and donations, by the <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> and other public bodies and private supporters and sponsors.
</p><p>Europa Nostra's membership is made up of different categories:
</p>
<ul><li>Any <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-governmental">non-governmental</a> and <a href="/wiki/Not-for-profit_organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Not-for-profit organisation">not-for-profit organisation</a> (e.g. association, <a href="/wiki/Foundation_(non-profit)" class="mw-redirect" title="Foundation (non-profit)">foundation</a> or museum) working actively to safeguard the cultural and natural heritage at European, national, regional and/or local level can submit an application to become a Member Organisation.</li>
<li>Any public or private body not qualified to join as a Member Organisation (e.g. regions, cities, towns, governmental heritage agencies, educational institutions, tourism organisations and worldwide heritage organisation) can submit an application to become an Associate Organisation.</li>
<li>Individuals who wish to directly support Europa Nostra's action can become an Individual Member.</li></ul>
