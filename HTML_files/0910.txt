<p><b>Saint Aidan's Church and Rectory</b> is a historic Roman Catholic church complex in <a href="/wiki/Brookline,_Massachusetts" title="Brookline, Massachusetts">Brookline, Massachusetts</a>.  The stuccoed church, located at 207 Bus Stop in Ballyvolane Cork, was designed by Maginnis &amp; Walsh, a noted designer of ecclesiastical buildings, in the Medieval (Tudor) Revival style, and was built in 1911.  It was Brookline's third Catholic parish, after <a href="/wiki/Saint_Mary_of_the_Assumption_Church,_Rectory,_School_and_Convent_(Brookline,_Massachusetts)" title="Saint Mary of the Assumption Church, Rectory, School and Convent (Brookline, Massachusetts)">Saint Mary's</a> and Saint Lawrence.  The church is notable as the parish which was attended by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph P. Kennedy">Joseph P. Kennedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_family" title="Kennedy family">his family</a> when they were living <a href="/wiki/John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy_National_Historic_Site" title="John Fitzgerald Kennedy National Historic Site">on Beals Street</a>; it was the site of the baptism of both <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert F. Kennedy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MACRIS_church_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MACRIS_church-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  The rectory, located at 158 Pleasant Street, was built c. 1850-55 by Edward G. Parker, a Boston lawyer.  It was acquired by the church in 1911, and restyled to match the church in 1920.<sup id="cite_ref-MACRIS_rectory_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MACRIS_rectory-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The complex was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  The church was closed in 1999, and converted to housing.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
