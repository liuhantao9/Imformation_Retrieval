	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Benjamin Hick</h1>

<p><b>Benjamin Hick</b> (1 August 1790&#160;– 9 September 1842) was a successful English civil and mechanical engineer, <a href="/wiki/Art_collector" class="mw-redirect" title="Art collector">art collector</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patron" class="mw-redirect" title="Patron">patron</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-fine_arts_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fine_arts-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> his improvements to the <a href="/wiki/Steam_engine" title="Steam engine">steam engine</a> and invention of scientific tools were held in high esteem by the engineering profession,<sup id="cite_ref-Catalogue_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Catalogue-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> some of Hick's improvements became public property without claiming the <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a> rights he was entitled to.<sup id="cite_ref-Unknown_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Unknown-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was born at <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Infant_baptism" title="Infant baptism">christened</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Independent_(religion)" title="Independent (religion)">Independent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dissenter" title="Dissenter">dissenter</a>'s chapel at Highfield, his parents moving shortly afterwards to <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> where he was educated. <a href="/wiki/Henry_Venn_(Clapham_Sect)" title="Henry Venn (Clapham Sect)">Henry Venn</a> was vicar of Huddersfield parish until 1771.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Catalogue_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Catalogue-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hick's aptitude for mechanics and passion for drawing led to an apprentiship in 1804 (age 14) as a <a href="/wiki/Draughtsman" class="mw-redirect" title="Draughtsman">draughtsman</a> with <a href="/wiki/Fenton,_Murray_and_Wood" class="mw-redirect" title="Fenton, Murray and Wood">Fenton, Murray and Wood</a><sup id="cite_ref-Catalogue_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Catalogue-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/Round_Foundry" title="Round Foundry">Round Foundry</a> in <a href="/wiki/Holbeck" title="Holbeck">Holbeck</a>. The company made steam engines, textile and other machinery; here he was entrusted with the installation of several large steam engines<sup id="cite_ref-Unknown_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Unknown-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and offered a partnership when his apprenticeship expired.<sup id="cite_ref-Catalogue_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Catalogue-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The offer was declined and Hick moved instead to <a href="/wiki/Bolton" title="Bolton">Bolton</a> in 1810<sup id="cite_ref-Unknown_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Unknown-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> to work for <a href="/w/index.php?title=Smalley,_Thwaites_and_Company&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Smalley, Thwaites and Company (page does not exist)">Smalley, Thwaites and Company</a><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> as manager<sup id="cite_ref-Catalogue_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Catalogue-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> of Rothwell's Union Foundry on Blackhorse Street.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His brother John Hick also trained as an engineer, in 1808 John left Fenton, Murray &amp; Wood to take up the position of "<a href="/wiki/Book-keeper" class="mw-redirect" title="Book-keeper">book-keeper</a> and traveller" for John Sturges and Co. at the <a href="/wiki/Bowling_Iron_Works" title="Bowling Iron Works">Bowling Iron Works</a> near <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Chrimes_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chrimes-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 1820 Hick joined other leading <a href="/wiki/Industrialist" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrialist">industrialists</a> <a href="/wiki/Dobson_%26_Barlow" title="Dobson &amp; Barlow">Isaac Dobson</a>, Thomas Hardcastle and Peter Rothwell together with engineer <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Routledge" title="Joshua Routledge">Joshua Routledge</a> to form the Bolton Gaslight and <a href="/wiki/Coke_(fuel)" title="Coke (fuel)">Coke</a> Company,<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> providing <a href="/wiki/Coal_gas" title="Coal gas">gas</a> for public buildings, <a href="/wiki/Gas_lighting" title="Gas lighting">street lamps</a> and industrial lighting. With the inevitable fire risk of naked flame, the Great Bolton Trustees and a number of mill owners bought horse-drawn fire engines; Hick became a <a href="/wiki/Trustee" title="Trustee">Trustee</a> of <a href="/wiki/Great_Bolton" title="Great Bolton">Great Bolton</a> during the early 1820s, he was an <a href="/wiki/Anglican" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglican">Anglican</a> and prominent member of Bolton's Pitt Club, formed 1809 as a "<a href="/wiki/Political_organisation" title="Political organisation">political organisation</a> composed of the admirers of <a href="/wiki/William_Pitt_the_Younger" title="William Pitt the Younger">William Pitt</a>", that helped Hick move amongst the <a href="/wiki/Middle_class" title="Middle class">middle class</a> <a href="/wiki/Social_elite" class="mw-redirect" title="Social elite">social elite</a> of the time.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pilling3_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pilling3-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Dobsons (Isaac and Benjamin), Hick, Rothwell, <a href="/wiki/John_Kennedy_(manufacturer)" title="John Kennedy (manufacturer)">John Kennedy</a> and others were members of a "prosecution" club (formed 1801), meeting at the Black Horse <a href="/wiki/Pub" title="Pub">pub</a> in Bolton, that in 1824 secured an <a href="/wiki/Annuity_(finance_theory)" class="mw-redirect" title="Annuity (finance theory)">annuity</a> for the inventor and fellow member <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Crompton" title="Samuel Crompton">Samuel Crompton</a>, who also frequented the Inn.<sup id="cite_ref-Pilling3_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pilling3-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-black_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-black-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Hick joined the <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Civil_Engineers" title="Institution of Civil Engineers">Institution of Civil Engineers</a> in the same year,<sup id="cite_ref-Unknown_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Unknown-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> proposed by <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Field_(engineer)" title="Joshua Field (engineer)">Joshua Field</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Farey" title="Joseph Farey">Joseph Farey</a> and James Jones.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Also with Rothwell and the Dobsons, Hick was a prominent member of the Black Horse Club, that met "to discuss not only business matters but the most interesting topics affecting that period".<sup id="cite_ref-Pilling3_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pilling3-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-black_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-black-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Together with many other leading figures from the Bolton area, including those from the Black Horse Club, Hick was a promoter and with Peter Rothwell<sup id="cite_ref-Pilling3_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pilling3-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> an original shareholder of the <a href="/wiki/Bolton_and_Leigh_Railway" title="Bolton and Leigh Railway">Bolton and Leigh Railway</a> that opened 1 August 1828 with the naming of the locomotive <i><a href="/wiki/Lancashire_Witch" title="Lancashire Witch">Lancashire Witch</a></i> by Mrs Hulton, wife of the vilified <a href="/wiki/William_Hulton" title="William Hulton">William Hulton</a> <a href="/wiki/Magistrate_(England_and_Wales)" title="Magistrate (England and Wales)">JP</a>, <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_Lancashire" title="High Sheriff of Lancashire">High Sheriff of Lancashire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Coal_mining" title="Coal mining">collier</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Stephenson" title="Robert Stephenson">Robert Stephenson</a> was driver of the engine he designed and built<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> with chief engineer <a href="/wiki/George_Stephenson" title="George Stephenson">George Stephenson</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> who was a passenger with the other guests.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Following the occasion that gathered crowds of 40-50 thousand people, in October 1828, Hick and Rothwell received <a href="/wiki/Robert_Peel" title="Robert Peel">Robert Peel</a>, then <a href="/wiki/Home_secretary" class="mw-redirect" title="Home secretary">home secretary</a>, as a guest at their foundry.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1837 Hick was, among other local figures including <a href="/wiki/Wallsuches" title="Wallsuches">Thomas Ridgway</a> (1778–1839),<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/William_Bolling_(MP)" class="mw-redirect" title="William Bolling (MP)">Edward Bolling</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Hargreaves_(carrier)" title="John Hargreaves (carrier)">John Hargreaves elder</a> (1780–1860) and <a href="/wiki/John_Hargreaves_(early_railway_operator)" title="John Hargreaves (early railway operator)">Jr</a>, a member of the Provisional <a href="/wiki/Committee" title="Committee">Committee</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Bolton_and_Preston_Railway" title="Bolton and Preston Railway">Bolton and Preston Railway</a>. By 1841 Hick was <a href="/wiki/Deputy_chairman" class="mw-redirect" title="Deputy chairman">Deputy chairman</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Director_(business)" title="Director (business)">Director</a> with chairman John Hargreaves; the two families (Hick and Hargreaves) were linked by marriage in 1836.<sup id="cite_ref-Mary_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mary-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Chief engineer was <a href="/wiki/John_Urpeth_Rastrick" title="John Urpeth Rastrick">John Urpeth Rastrick</a> and resident engineer Alexander James Adie, son of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Adie" title="Alexander Adie">Alexander Adie</a> inventor of the <a href="/wiki/Sympiesometer" title="Sympiesometer">sympiesometer</a>. The line opened 22 June 1843 after Hick's death, following some problems in its construction,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> and merged with the <a href="/wiki/North_Union_Railway" title="North Union Railway">North Union Railway Company</a> 10 May 1844. Hick's <a href="/wiki/Executorial_trustee" title="Executorial trustee">executorial trustee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solicitor" title="Solicitor">solicitor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Banker" class="mw-redirect" title="Banker">banker</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Lever_Rushton&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Thomas Lever Rushton (page does not exist)">Thomas Lever Rushton</a> (1810–1883), by then a director of the railway, was part of the negotiating committee for the merger.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pilling2_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pilling2-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the records of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Beyer" title="Charles Beyer">Charles Beyer</a> and an appraisal by <a href="/wiki/John_Farey_Jr." title="John Farey Jr.">John Farey</a>, Hick was apparently responsible for pioneering the use of <a href="/wiki/Steam_engine#High-pressure_engines" title="Steam engine">high-pressure</a> and <a href="/wiki/Compound_steam_engine" title="Compound steam engine">compound steam engines</a> in <a href="/wiki/Textile_mill" class="mw-redirect" title="Textile mill">textile mills</a>, following the designs of <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Woolf" title="Arthur Woolf">Arthur Woolf</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hills_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hills-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Hick's engines should probably be considered best practice for the time c.1841.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Locomotives built by Hick were of the best quality, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Bury" title="Edward Bury">Edward Bury</a> considered them "extremely well made" and they were used by the <a href="/wiki/London_and_Birmingham_Railway" title="London and Birmingham Railway">London and Birmingham Railway</a> for the very first scheme of <a href="/wiki/Interchangeable_parts" title="Interchangeable parts">standardisation of parts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Rothwell.2C_Hick_and_Rothwell"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Rothwell,_Hick_and_Rothwell">Rothwell, Hick and Rothwell</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_Hick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Rothwell, Hick and Rothwell">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rothwell_Hick_%26_Co_Steam-Engine_Manufactory_and_Iron-Works,_Bolton.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Rothwell_Hick_%26_Co_Steam-Engine_Manufactory_and_Iron-Works%2C_Bolton.jpg/260px-Rothwell_Hick_%26_Co_Steam-Engine_Manufactory_and_Iron-Works%2C_Bolton.jpg" decoding="async" width="260" height="191" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Rothwell_Hick_%26_Co_Steam-Engine_Manufactory_and_Iron-Works%2C_Bolton.jpg/390px-Rothwell_Hick_%26_Co_Steam-Engine_Manufactory_and_Iron-Works%2C_Bolton.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b8/Rothwell_Hick_%26_Co_Steam-Engine_Manufactory_and_Iron-Works%2C_Bolton.jpg 2x" data-file-width="428" data-file-height="315" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rothwell_Hick_%26_Co_Steam-Engine_Manufactory_and_Iron-Works,_Bolton.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>"Steam-Engine Manufactory and Iron-Works, Bolton. To Messrs. Rothwell, Hick &amp; Co. This plate is respectfully inscribed by the publishers. Fisher, Son and Co. of London 1832."</i> Engraving by William Watkins after a drawing by John Harwood. Originally published in <a href="/wiki/William_Henry_Pyne" title="William Henry Pyne">William Henry Pyne</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Partwork" title="Partwork">partwork</a> <i>Lancashire Illustrated, from Original Drawings</i> (1828-1831).<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>By 1821 Hick was managing partner of the Union Foundry, that later became <a href="/wiki/Rothwell,_Hick_and_Rothwell" title="Rothwell, Hick and Rothwell">Rothwell, Hick and Rothwell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Unknown_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Unknown-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Devided_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Devided-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> After Peter Rothwell's death on 2 August 1824, the company continued with Peter Rothwell Jr (1792–1849) as Rothwell, Hick &amp; Co.<sup id="cite_ref-Marshall_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marshall-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> They made <a href="/wiki/Stationary_steam_engine" title="Stationary steam engine">stationary steam engines</a>, (a number of which were featured by <a href="/wiki/John_Farey,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="John Farey, Jr.">John Farey</a> in the second volume of his <i>Treatise on the Steam Engine</i>, 1827) as well as general engineering products including <a href="/wiki/Cast_iron" title="Cast iron">cast iron</a> <a href="/wiki/Dockyard" class="mw-redirect" title="Dockyard">dockyard</a> <a href="/wiki/Crane_(machine)" title="Crane (machine)">cranes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Chrimes_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chrimes-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Rothwell, Hick and Rothwell were listed in <a href="/wiki/Edward_Baines_(1774%E2%80%931848)" title="Edward Baines (1774–1848)">Baines</a>' directory as supplying steam engines, hydraulic presses, weighing machines, <a href="/wiki/Gas_lighting" title="Gas lighting">gas light apparatus</a>, mill machinery, <a href="/wiki/Sugar_mill" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar mill">sugar mills</a> and constructors of fire proof buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1824 when the prodigious and forward looking <a href="/wiki/Swiss_people" title="Swiss people">Swiss</a> engineer <a href="/wiki/Johann_Georg_Bodmer" title="Johann Georg Bodmer">Johann Georg Bodmer</a> (<a href="/wiki/Anglicisation" title="Anglicisation">anglicised</a> to John George Bodmer) developed his patterns and textile machinery near Bolton he made use of the Rothwell, Hick and Rothwell workshops.<sup id="cite_ref-Chrimes_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chrimes-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Here in the late 1820s with the co-operation of an Italian merchant, Philip Novelli and <a href="/w/index.php?title=H._%26_E._Ashworth&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="H. &amp; E. Ashworth (page does not exist)">H. &amp; E. Ashworth</a>, (<a href="/wiki/Henry_Ashworth_(nonconformist)" title="Henry Ashworth (nonconformist)">Henry (1794–1880)</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Edmund_Ashworth&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Edmund Ashworth (page does not exist)">Edmund (1800–1881)</a> Ashworth), they began a project of advanced concept at <a href="/wiki/Egerton,_Greater_Manchester" title="Egerton, Greater Manchester">Egerton</a> Mill<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> to include a spectacular <a href="/wiki/Waterwheel" class="mw-redirect" title="Waterwheel">waterwheel</a><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>  of 62 feet <a href="/wiki/Diameter" title="Diameter">diameter</a> by 12 feet wide<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> and 110–140 <a href="/wiki/Horsepower" title="Horsepower">horsepower</a>, completed by <a href="/wiki/William_Fairbairn_%26_Sons" title="William Fairbairn &amp; Sons">Fairbairn and Lillie</a><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> when Bodmer returned to Europe as a result of ill-health. To aid in the construction it is claimed that Bodmer devised the <a href="/wiki/Gantry_crane" title="Gantry crane"><i>travelling crane</i></a>; the Egerton wheel became a tourist destination during the 1830s and 1840s, it was one of the largest in the United Kingdom attracting visits from industrialists and politicians.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Disraeli" title="Benjamin Disraeli">Benjamin Disraeli</a> was a visitor to the Ashworth mills in 1843.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> By the 1830s Hick had become a highly valued friend of Bodmer, on one occasion <a href="/wiki/Arbitration" title="Arbitration">arbitrating</a> a patent dispute.<sup id="cite_ref-icevirtuallibrary.com_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icevirtuallibrary.com-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rothwell,_Hick_and_Rothwell_low_pressure_steam_engine.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Rothwell%2C_Hick_and_Rothwell_low_pressure_steam_engine.jpg/260px-Rothwell%2C_Hick_and_Rothwell_low_pressure_steam_engine.jpg" decoding="async" width="260" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Rothwell%2C_Hick_and_Rothwell_low_pressure_steam_engine.jpg/390px-Rothwell%2C_Hick_and_Rothwell_low_pressure_steam_engine.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c2/Rothwell%2C_Hick_and_Rothwell_low_pressure_steam_engine.jpg/520px-Rothwell%2C_Hick_and_Rothwell_low_pressure_steam_engine.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1533" data-file-height="979" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rothwell,_Hick_and_Rothwell_low_pressure_steam_engine.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Technical_drawing" title="Technical drawing">Technical drawing</a> of a balance and low pressure steam engine by Rothwell, Hick and Rothwell with architectural details in the <a href="/wiki/Doric_order" title="Doric order">Doric order</a>. <i>Traité Théorique et pratique des moteurs a vapeur</i>, <a href="/wiki/Jacques-Eug%C3%A8ne_Armengaud" title="Jacques-Eugène Armengaud">Jacques-Eugène Armengaud</a> 1862.</div></div></div>
<p>Hick also formed a close friendship with engineer and artist <a href="/wiki/James_Nasmyth" title="James Nasmyth">James Nasmyth</a>, in his autobiography Nasmyth refers to Hick as a "most admirable man... whose judgment in all matters connected with engineering and mechanical construction was held in the very highest regard... ingenious", he "contrived and constructed... one of the most powerful <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_press" title="Hydraulic press">hydraulic presses</a>" in existence. Hick was inventor of the self tightening collar, used universally in hydraulic pressess.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to Nasmyth, Hick and <a href="/wiki/William_Fairbairn" title="William Fairbairn">William Fairbairn</a> were among the most "intelligent and cultivated persons in Lancashire".<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Hick was an accomplished draughtsman and it is stated that he introduced almost a new era of elegance and design for the exterior forms of steam engines and larger works.<sup id="cite_ref-books.google.co.uk_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-books.google.co.uk-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hick and Rothwell built their first locomotive <i>Union</i> in 1830 for the <a href="/wiki/Bolton_and_Leigh_Railway" title="Bolton and Leigh Railway">Bolton and Leigh Railway</a>, they also built <i>Pioneer</i> for the <a href="/wiki/Petersburg_Railroad" title="Petersburg Railroad">Petersburg Railroad</a> in America<sup id="cite_ref-Skeat_and_Marshall_1974_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Skeat_and_Marshall_1974-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/2-2-0" title="2-2-0">2-2-0</a>, <i>the Pontchartrain</i> for the <a href="/wiki/Pontchartrain_Railroad" title="Pontchartrain Railroad">Pontchartrain Railroad</a>, New Orleans in 1832.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture_and_public_works">Architecture and public works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_Hick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Architecture and public works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hick's talent and reputation as a draughtsman lead to requests for his design of public buildings in Bolton:<sup id="cite_ref-Catalogue_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Catalogue-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Public_dispensary" title="Public dispensary">Dispensary</a> (1825), Nelson Square (demolished); Cloth Hall, Market Street (demolished); Gas Works (demolished), Water Works<sup id="cite_ref-Catalogue_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Catalogue-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and possibly the old Town Hall (1826) in <a href="/wiki/Little_Bolton" title="Little Bolton">Little Bolton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Portraits_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Portraits-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> The old Town Hall on All Saints Street,<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Belmont_Reservoir" title="Belmont Reservoir">Waterworks</a> cottage (1824), <a href="/wiki/Belmont,_Lancashire" title="Belmont, Lancashire">Belmont</a><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> remain today. The Dispensary is referred to in <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Pevsner" class="mw-redirect" title="Nicholas Pevsner">Pevsner's</a> <i>South Lancashire: The Industrial and Commercial South</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Allen_(topographer)" title="Thomas Allen (topographer)">Thomas Allen</a>'s, <i>Lancashire Illustrated, from Original Drawings.</i> considers, "The gas and waterworks also are eminently deserving of notice. Bolton will long retain memorials of its numerous obligations to the superior genius, public exertions and enterprising spirit of Mr. Benjamin Hick".<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> Hick designed a Gas <a href="/wiki/Column" title="Column">Pillar</a> and presented it for the opening of Bolton's New Market Place (1826), claimed to be the finest uncovered market in the country; about November 1859 his son <a href="/wiki/John_Hick_(politician)" title="John Hick (politician)">John Hick</a> gifted a circular "<a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Drinking_Fountain_and_Cattle_Trough_Association" title="Metropolitan Drinking Fountain and Cattle Trough Association">cattle fountain</a>" round the base,<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> both <a href="/wiki/Gaslight" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaslight">gaslight</a> and trough remained a feature of the square until 1925,<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> the pillar described in 1825 as "a piece of elegant and <a href="/wiki/Classical_architecture" title="Classical architecture">classical</a> workmanship" and "justly the admiration of persons of taste".<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> Rothwell, Hick and Rothwell contributed elaborate iron work screens with <a href="/wiki/Anthemion" class="mw-redirect" title="Anthemion">anthemion</a> patterns to <a href="/wiki/Preston,_Lancashire" title="Preston, Lancashire">Preston</a> <a href="/wiki/Corn_exchange" title="Corn exchange">Corn Exchange</a> (1822–24).<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hick designed a heating system for <a href="/wiki/Richard_Lane_(architect)" title="Richard Lane (architect)">Richard Lane's</a> <i>The Oaks</i> (demolished), an imposing <a href="/wiki/Ionic_order" title="Ionic order">Ionic</a> villa conceived in 1838, and home of Quaker, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ashworth_(nonconformist)" title="Henry Ashworth (nonconformist)">Henry Ashworth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> Hick's grandson, also Benjamin Hick (1845–1882)<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> was architect of changes made to Lane's Exchange and Library (1825–29), New Market Place (renamed <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria#Diamond_Jubilee" title="Queen Victoria">Victoria Square</a> in 1897)<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> about 1871,<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> his grandfather was among the 80 shareholders of all political and religious persuasions.<sup id="cite_ref-Devided_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Devided-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="B._Hick_and_Sons">B. Hick and Sons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_Hick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: B. Hick and Sons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/B._Hick_and_Sons" title="B. Hick and Sons">B. Hick and Sons</a></div>
<p>In 1814 Benjamin Hick married Elizabeth Routledge (1783–1826) sister of his companion, <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Routledge" title="Joshua Routledge">Joshua Routledge</a> (1773–1829), an  engineer and <a href="/wiki/Ironmonger" class="mw-redirect" title="Ironmonger">ironmonger</a> living in <a href="/wiki/Bolton" title="Bolton">Bolton</a>, and former manager for <a href="/wiki/Fenton,_Murray_and_Wood" class="mw-redirect" title="Fenton, Murray and Wood">Fenton, Murray and Wood</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> Hick's father-in-law, William Routledge, was a <a href="/wiki/Blacksmith" title="Blacksmith">blacksmith</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wesleyan" class="mw-redirect" title="Wesleyan">Wesleyan</a> lay minister<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> at <a href="/wiki/Elvington,_City_of_York" title="Elvington, City of York">Elvington</a>, a village about 8 miles southeast of <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a> in Yorkshire. Joshua Routledge's sons were also engineers; William (1812–1882), a driver of the locomotive <i>Phoenix</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Opening_of_the_Liverpool_and_Manchester_Railway" title="Opening of the Liverpool and Manchester Railway">opening</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_and_Manchester_Railway" title="Liverpool and Manchester Railway">Liverpool and Manchester Railway</a> 1828, in 1852 master engineer at New Bridge <a href="/wiki/Brass" title="Brass">Brass</a> Foundry and partner in Routledge &amp; Ommanney, <a href="/wiki/City_of_Salford" title="City of Salford">Salford</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> 
Henry (1817–1884), manager of Bolton brass founders J. and W. Kirkham in 1882.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Benjamin Hick had five children, two sons John (1815–1894) and Benjamin (1818–1845)<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup> who he trained as engineers; on 10 April <a href="/wiki/1833_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="1833 in the United Kingdom">1833</a><sup id="cite_ref-Skeat_and_Marshall_1974_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Skeat_and_Marshall_1974-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> they set up their own manufactory, <a href="/wiki/B._Hick_and_Sons" title="B. Hick and Sons">B. Hick and Sons</a>, at the Soho <a href="/wiki/Foundry" title="Foundry">Foundry</a>, Crook Street, Bolton. The firm built the locomotive <i>Soho</i> bought by <a href="/wiki/Common_carrier" title="Common carrier">carrier</a> <a href="/wiki/John_Hargreaves_(early_railway_operator)" title="John Hargreaves (early railway operator)">John Hargreaves</a> with six first class carriages for the <a href="/wiki/Bolton_and_Leigh_Railway" title="Bolton and Leigh Railway">Bolton and Leigh Railway</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup> and soon became well known as suppliers to British and foreign railway companies.<sup id="cite_ref-Marshall_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marshall-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Three years later Hick's first child and eldest daughter Mary (1813–1878) married <a href="/wiki/John_Hargreaves_(early_railway_operator)" title="John Hargreaves (early railway operator)">John Hargreaves Jr</a> (1800–1874),<sup id="cite_ref-Mary_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mary-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> manufacturer and operator of the <a href="/wiki/Bolton_and_Leigh_Railway" title="Bolton and Leigh Railway">Bolton &amp; Leigh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kenyon_and_Leigh_Junction_Railway" title="Kenyon and Leigh Junction Railway">Leigh &amp; Kenyon Junction railways</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Marshall_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marshall-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Elizabeth's death he married Hannah Elizabeth Goodyer (c.1791–1862) in 1827 at <a href="/wiki/Garden_Museum#St_Mary-at-Lambeth" title="Garden Museum">St Mary's church</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lambeth" title="Lambeth">Lambeth</a>. Hannah was a daughter of Landon Goodyer who held a position of responsibility in a Fire Office,<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup> and sister of <a href="/wiki/Leicester_City_Police" title="Leicester City Police">Frederick Goodyer</a> who was to become a highly regarded <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Metropolitan_Police_Service" title="History of the Metropolitan Police Service">Metropolitan Police</a> Officer under <a href="/wiki/Home_Secretary" title="Home Secretary">Home Secretary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Peel" title="Robert Peel">Robert Peel</a>. The first daughter by Hick's second wife married the only son of <a href="/wiki/Johann_Georg_Bodmer" title="Johann Georg Bodmer">Johann Georg Bodmer</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup> his youngest daughter married the fourth son of James Bodmer.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hick was also linked by his mother-in-law Hannah Goodyer, née Schwenck, to the Naval surgeon and novelist <a href="/wiki/William_Gilbert_(author)" title="William Gilbert (author)">William Gilbert</a>, father of <a href="/wiki/W._S._Gilbert" title="W. S. Gilbert">William Schwenck Gilbert</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_and_Sullivan" title="Gilbert and Sullivan">Gilbert and Sullivan</a>, his first daughter from his marriage to Hannah Goodyer was named in a similar fashion; Helen Schwenck Hick. Hannah Goodyer's father John Adam Schwenck was treasurer of the congregation of <a href="/wiki/St_Mary_le_Strand" title="St Mary le Strand">St Mary le Strand</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup> formerly partner in Bourdorff and Schwenck, <a href="/wiki/Sugar_refinery" title="Sugar refinery">sugar refiners</a> on <a href="/wiki/Millbank" title="Millbank">Millbank</a> Street, in the parish of <a href="/wiki/St_John%27s,_Smith_Square" title="St John&#39;s, Smith Square">St. John the Evangelist</a>, <a href="/wiki/Westminster" title="Westminster">Westminster</a><sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">&#91;85&#93;</a></sup> and a director of the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Fire_Office" title="Phoenix Fire Office">Phoenix Fire Office</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">&#91;86&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">&#91;87&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Patron_of_the_arts">Patron of the arts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_Hick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Patron of the arts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Benjamin Hick was both a serious <a href="/wiki/Collecting" title="Collecting">collector</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patron" class="mw-redirect" title="Patron">patron</a> of the arts alongside other wealthy <a href="/wiki/Industrialists" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrialists">industrialists</a> and bankers from the <a href="/wiki/North_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="North of England">North of England</a> about mid nineteenth century, including Samuel Ashton, William Bashall of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bashall_%26_Boardman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bashall &amp; Boardman (page does not exist)">Bashall &amp; Boardman</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Chapman_(1810%E2%80%931877)" class="mw-redirect" title="John Chapman (1810–1877)">John Chapman</a>, Henry Cooke, Benjamin <a href="/wiki/Dobson_%26_Barlow" title="Dobson &amp; Barlow">Dobson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sir_John_Gladstone,_1st_Baronet" title="Sir John Gladstone, 1st Baronet">Sir John Gladstone</a>, John Miller, Thomas Miller Jr of <a href="/wiki/Preston,_Lancashire" title="Preston, Lancashire">Preston</a>, John Naylor of <a href="/wiki/Leighton_Hall,_Powys#The_Leyland/Naylor_Family" title="Leighton Hall, Powys">Leyland &amp; Bullins</a> and Peter <a href="/wiki/Rothwell,_Hick_and_Rothwell" title="Rothwell, Hick and Rothwell">Rothwell</a> who favoured <a href="/wiki/English_art" title="English art">English art</a> particularly that depicting nature and history.
</p><p>A man of "acknowledged taste<sup id="cite_ref-Unknown_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Unknown-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and judgment"; Hick's <a href="/wiki/Private_collection" title="Private collection">private collection</a> built over a period of 30 years "valuable, well known and much admired", comprised works of the Italian, Flemish, Dutch and British Masters. He was regarded as "one of the most liberal of the provincial patrons of Art", generous in his support of the British school, in particular a friend of <a href="/wiki/Henry_Liverseege" title="Henry Liverseege">Henry Liverseege</a> who painted several works for Hick, and "on terms of intimacy" with many artists. The collection consisted of antiques, bronzes, engravings by <a href="/wiki/George_Thomas_Doo" title="George Thomas Doo">George Thomas Doo</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Henry_Robinson" title="John Henry Robinson">John Henry Robinson</a> and foreign engravers, marbles, paintings and watercolour drawings.
</p><p>Foreign works included amongst others those of: <a href="/wiki/Canaletto" title="Canaletto">Canaletti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Annibale_Carracci" title="Annibale Carracci">Annibale Carracci</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" title="Gerard Dow">Gerard Dow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anton_Raphael_Mengs" title="Anton Raphael Mengs">Anton Raphael Mengs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jan_Miel" title="Jan Miel">Jan Miel</a>, </p><p>Having the "greatest reverence for <a href="/wiki/Work_of_art" title="Work of art">Works of Art</a> and <a href="/wiki/Books" class="mw-redirect" title="Books">Books</a>", Hick's intention was to establish a <a href="/wiki/Art_museum" title="Art museum">Public Picture Gallery</a> in Bolton, with "the conviction that such Works of Art would tend to greatly improve the taste and cultivate the better feeling of the rising generation". He obtained designs for the project, but did not live to see them through. As a boy he looked forward to becoming an <a href="/wiki/Artist" title="Artist">Artist</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Catalogue_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Catalogue-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Benjamin_Hick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 9 September 1842, Hick died suddenly at Bolton from a "<a href="/wiki/Cardiovascular_disease" title="Cardiovascular disease">disease of the heart</a>", age 52.<sup id="cite_ref-Catalogue_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Catalogue-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Pilling_100-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pilling-100">&#91;100&#93;</a></sup> Following, <a href="/wiki/B._Hick_and_Sons" title="B. Hick and Sons">B. Hick and Son</a> continued under the management of his eldest son, <a href="/wiki/John_Hick_(MP)" class="mw-redirect" title="John Hick (MP)">John Hick</a>.
</p><p>Art works from Benjamin Hick's collection were advertised in January and February 1843 editions of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Art_Journal" title="The Art Journal">The Art-Union</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Athenaeum_(British_magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Athenaeum (British magazine)">Athenaeum</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Literary_Gazette" class="mw-redirect" title="Literary Gazette">Literary Gazette</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">&#91;101&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">&#91;102&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">&#91;103&#93;</a></sup> then <a href="/wiki/Auction" title="Auction">auctioned</a> by Thomas Winstanley &amp; Sons of <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> at the Exchange Gallery in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> between 21 and 24 February 1843. The sale included <a href="/wiki/John_Martin_(painter)" title="John Martin (painter)">John Martin</a>'s pair <i><a href="/wiki/Pand%C3%A6monium_(Paradise_Lost)" title="Pandæmonium (Paradise Lost)">Pandæmonium</a></i> and <i>The Celestial City and the River of Bliss</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-http_89-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-British_Paintings_99-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-British_Paintings-99">&#91;99&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">&#91;104&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105">&#91;105&#93;</a></sup> Both paintings were bought by Hick from the artist following their exhibition at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Arts" title="Royal Academy of Arts">Royal Academy of Arts</a> in 1841; <i>Pandæmonium</i> and its frame designed by Martin<sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106">&#91;106&#93;</a></sup> can be seen at the <a href="/wiki/Louvre" title="Louvre">Louvre</a>. Hick's <a href="/wiki/Obituary" title="Obituary">obituary</a> in the <i>Art-Union</i> appeared with those of <a href="/wiki/John_Varley_(painter)" title="John Varley (painter)">John Varley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maria_Graham" title="Maria Graham">Lady Callcott</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Berney_Crome" title="John Berney Crome">John Berney Crome</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107">&#91;107&#93;</a></sup> Details of the sale were published in the April edition of <i>The Art-Union</i><sup id="cite_ref-SALES_OF_THE_MONTH_91-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SALES_OF_THE_MONTH-91">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup> and May edition of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Gentleman%27s_Magazine" title="The Gentleman&#39;s Magazine">The Gentleman's Magazine</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Gentlemen&#39;s_Magazine_90-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Gentlemen&#39;s_Magazine-90">&#91;90&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Friends of Hick from Lancashire: Robert Barlow, Joseph Beckton, Robert Daglish Jr (son of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Daglish" title="Robert Daglish">Robert Daglish</a>), Jonathan Hardcastle, John Moore, John Mawdsley, <a href="/wiki/Rothwell,_Hick_and_Rothwell" title="Rothwell, Hick and Rothwell">Peter Rothwell</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Lever_Rushton&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Thomas Lever Rushton (page does not exist)">Thomas Lever Rushton</a> formed a committee to see through the production of an <a href="/wiki/Engraving" title="Engraving">engraving</a> from Hick's portrait by <a href="/wiki/George_Patten" title="George Patten">George Patten</a>. The picture was entrusted to <a href="/wiki/John_Clowes_Grundy" title="John Clowes Grundy">John Grundy</a> and Henry Cousins undertook the work in <a href="/wiki/Mezzotint" title="Mezzotint">mezzotint</a>; proofs were then published at a moderate price,<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108">&#91;108&#93;</a></sup> examples can be found today in various museum collections.<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-109">&#91;109&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-110" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-110">&#91;110&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-111" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-111">&#91;111&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Hick was well respected, despite his family's wish the funeral be "strictly private", more than 500 people "including a great number of influential gentlemen of the neighbourhood", attended his grave; employees of B. Hick &amp; Son gathered nearly £200 for a memorial and despite offers of assistance "resolved unanimously to keep this honour for themselves".<sup id="cite_ref-books.google.co.uk_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-books.google.co.uk-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Benjamin Hick's memorial in <a href="/wiki/St_Peter%27s_Church,_Bolton" title="St Peter&#39;s Church, Bolton">Bolton Parish Church</a> reads:
</p>
<blockquote><div class="poem">
<p>This monument is erected by a general subscription of his Workmen,<br />
in remembrance of his Christian character<br />
and to record virtues so rare, for future imitation;<br />
that he, though dead, may live again<br />
in the spirit, action, and conduct of those,<br />
who, guided by his character, and stimulated by his example,<br />
will learn to love their neighbour as themselves<br />
and to do good to all men.<br />
<br />
He was an affectionate husband, <br />
a kind father, and a sincere friend;<br />
alike distinguished by eminent ability,<br />
and uniform integrity;<br />
genius, in whatever art or science displayed,<br />
even found in him a liberal patron;<br />
He was <a href="/wiki/Benefactor_(law)" title="Benefactor (law)">benefactor</a> to this town,<br />
where his worth will be long appreciated;<br />
and his loss deeply deplored.<sup id="cite_ref-Catalogue_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Catalogue-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Unknown_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Unknown-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-112">&#91;112&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
</div></blockquote>
