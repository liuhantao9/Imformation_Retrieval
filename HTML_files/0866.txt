	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Beaumont Street Studios</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

<b>Beaumont Street Studios</b> (<b>BSS</b>) was an English nonprofit community music centre and <a href="/wiki/Radio_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio station">radio station</a>. Founded in 1985 by the Huddersfield West Indian Association, it closed in 2010.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beaumont_Street_Studios&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Beaumont Street Studios was established by the <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit_organization" title="Nonprofit organization">nonprofit organisation</a> Huddersfield West Indian Association in 1985.<sup id="cite_ref-Davison2010-02-08_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Davison2010-02-08-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The organisation was inspired by the black neighborhood's social issues and unemployment.<sup id="cite_ref-Rennie2006_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rennie2006-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2006-10-18_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2006-10-18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Beaumont Street Studios received funding from the <a href="/wiki/New_Deal_(United_Kingdom)" title="New Deal (United Kingdom)">New Deal</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Labour" title="New Labour">New Labour</a> initiatives that aimed to help the unemployed find work.<sup id="cite_ref-Rennie2006_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rennie2006-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During their 1999–2000 budget year, Beaumont Street Studios had revenue of roughly half a million pounds.<sup id="cite_ref-Rennie2006_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rennie2006-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At its inception, Beaumont Street Studios had an eight-track recording facility on Beaumont Street at Hudawi Centre. In 1993, Hudawi Centre was destroyed in a fire. The prior year, they had started building a 24-track studio nearby, which they started using after the fire.<sup id="cite_ref-2006-10-18_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2006-10-18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It later moved to St Peter's Chambers.<sup id="cite_ref-2010-10-05_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010-10-05-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2006-10-18_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2006-10-18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Its recording studios were used by the bands <a href="/wiki/Chumbawamba" title="Chumbawamba">Chumbawamba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Embrace_(English_band)" title="Embrace (English band)">Embrace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Four_Day_Hombre" class="mw-redirect" title="Four Day Hombre">Four Day Hombre</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Mission_(band)" title="The Mission (band)">The Mission</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shed_Seven" title="Shed Seven">Shed Seven</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Sisters_of_Mercy" title="The Sisters of Mercy">The Sisters of Mercy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Terrorvision" title="Terrorvision">Terrorvision</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2006-10-18_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2006-10-18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The organisation closed in 2010 and was <a href="/wiki/Liquidation" title="Liquidation">liquidated</a> through the sale of musical accessories like as <a href="/wiki/Mixing_console" title="Mixing console">mixing consoles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Laptop" title="Laptop">laptops</a>. It had a remaining debt of £112,000.<sup id="cite_ref-2010-10-05_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010-10-05-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Creative FM was a community <a href="/wiki/Radio_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Radio station">radio station</a> based at Beaumont Street Studios in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2006-10-18_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2006-10-18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Broadcasting" title="Broadcasting">broadcasting</a> <a href="/wiki/Frequency" title="Frequency">frequency</a> is 107.1 <a href="/wiki/FM_broadcasting" title="FM broadcasting">FM</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2004-06-17_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2004-06-17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In addition to broadcasting in <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, the station also had broadcasts in <a href="/wiki/Punjabi_language" title="Punjabi language">Punjabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hindi_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindi language">Hindi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gujarati_language" title="Gujarati language">Gujarati</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Urdu_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2004-06-17_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2004-06-17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Creative FM began broadcasting on 14 June 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-2004-06-17_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2004-06-17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The station broadcast under a <a href="/wiki/Restricted_Service_Licence" title="Restricted Service Licence">Restricted Service Licence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Rennie2006_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rennie2006-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On 11 July 2004, Creative FM started broadcasting every day of the week <a href="/wiki/24/7_service" title="24/7 service">24 hours a day</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2004-06-17_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2004-06-17-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
