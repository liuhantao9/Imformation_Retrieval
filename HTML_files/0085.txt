<p>"<b>Heartbeat</b>" is a song recorded by American singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> from her fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Storyteller_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" title="Storyteller (Carrie Underwood album)">Storyteller</a></i>. It was written by Underwood, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Gorley" title="Ashley Gorley">Ashley Gorley</a>, and the song's producer, <a href="/wiki/Zach_Crowell" title="Zach Crowell">Zach Crowell</a>. <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">Country</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Sam_Hunt" title="Sam Hunt">Sam Hunt</a> sings <a href="/wiki/Backing_vocalist" title="Backing vocalist">backing vocals</a> on the track. The song was released to the <a href="/wiki/Country_radio" title="Country radio">radio</a> by <a href="/wiki/Arista_Nashville" title="Arista Nashville">Arista Nashville</a> on November 30, 2015 as the album's second single, after "<a href="/wiki/Smoke_Break" title="Smoke Break">Smoke Break</a>". Musically, "Heartbeat" is a <a href="/wiki/Country_pop" title="Country pop">country pop</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> song with lyrics that relate to the feeling of intimacy experienced by being alone with one's lover. It appears to be inspired by Underwood's marriage to <a href="/wiki/Mike_Fisher_(ice_hockey)" title="Mike Fisher (ice hockey)">Mike Fisher</a>.
</p><p>"Heartbeat" was met with positive reviews from music critics and became a commercial success. It reached number one on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart, becoming Underwood's fourteenth leader on the chart, widening her record for the most number-one's among women in the history of the chart. It also topped the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Canada_Country" class="mw-redirect" title="Canada Country">Canada Country</a> chart.
</p><p>The song received its first televised performance on the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_12)" class="mw-redirect" title="The X Factor (UK series 12)">twelfth season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The X Factor (UK TV series)">The X Factor</a></i> on November 29, 2015. Underwood has since performed the song live on television at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2015" title="American Music Awards of 2015">2015 American Music Awards</a> and at the <a href="/wiki/58th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="58th Grammy Awards">58th Grammy Awards</a> (along with Hunt).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heartbeat_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Heartbeat" is a <a href="/wiki/Country_pop" title="Country pop">country pop</a><sup id="cite_ref-ent_weekly_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ent_weekly-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-billboard_duet_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard_duet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a><sup id="cite_ref-taste_of_country_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taste_of_country-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> song written by Carrie Underwood, <a href="/wiki/Ashley_Gorley" title="Ashley Gorley">Ashley Gorley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Zach_Crowell" title="Zach Crowell">Zach Crowell</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Sentimental_ballad" title="Sentimental ballad">sentimental ballad</a> composed in the key of <a href="/wiki/D-flat_major" title="D-flat major">B<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-flat">&#x266d;</span></span> minor</a> and the sheet music designates a "slow" tempo of 140 <a href="/wiki/Tempo#Beats_per_minute" title="Tempo">BPM</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;A&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The song alternates between "smooth" verses with a relatively low-key musical accompaniment and a "bombastic" chorus.<sup id="cite_ref-E_online_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E_online-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">Country</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Sam_Hunt" title="Sam Hunt">Sam Hunt</a>, who is a frequent collaborator of Crowell's, sings <a href="/wiki/Backing_vocalist" title="Backing vocalist">backing vocals</a> on the track, leading some publications to label the song a <a href="/wiki/Duet" title="Duet">duet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ent_weekly_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ent_weekly-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-billboard_duet_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard_duet-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Hunt provides background vocals on the chorus and bridge, with Underwood's vocals layered over top of his to creating a "warm... echo" effect.<sup id="cite_ref-ent_weekly_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ent_weekly-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Described as "the most real love song" Underwood has recorded, the lyrics of "Heartbeat" relate to the feeling of intimacy experienced by being alone with one's lover.<sup id="cite_ref-taste_of_country_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taste_of_country-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-E_online_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E_online-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Critics noted a thematic similarity to then-recent single "<a href="/wiki/Smoke_Break" title="Smoke Break">Smoke Break</a>" in that the song encourages finding time in a busy life for a private moment of bliss.<sup id="cite_ref-taste_of_country_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taste_of_country-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The song appears to be inspired by Underwood's marriage to <a href="/wiki/Mike_Fisher_(ice_hockey)" title="Mike Fisher (ice hockey)">Mike Fisher</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-taste_of_country_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-taste_of_country-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rolling_stone_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rolling_stone-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heartbeat_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Heartbeat" was met with positive reviews from music critics. Will Hodge of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> opined that, "Underwood is known for creatively blending intimate lyrics with bombastic choruses and the power-ballad boom of "Heartbeat" is no exception. Her molasses-smooth vocal delivery sets the mood, leaving the listener feeling as if they're eavesdropping on someone else's date night." He also complimented Sam Hunt's involvement in the song, stating "Hunt provides the perfect male counterpoint, doubling Underwood's vocals in the chorus."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Renowned_for_Sound" title="Renowned for Sound">Renowned for Sound</a></i> reviewed the song favorably giving it 3.5 stars out of 5 and praised Underwood's as well as Hunt's vocals by saying, "Rather than making waves with its instrumental, the song’s major shining point is Underwood’s vocals. The presence of frequent collaborator Sam Hunt as her backup singer compliments her vocals perfectly, with the pairing of their vocals and the echoing effect it creates adding a deeper element to the song, both in terms of sound and aesthetic. At its core, "Heartbeat" is nothing new; it covers a familiar topic with a familiar sound behind it. However, the sentimental and thematic vocals bring the song to a whole other level."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Markos Papadatos of Digital Journal reviewed the song positively by calling it "absolutely beautiful". He concluded his review by saying, "Carrie Underwood tackles the subject matter in "Heartbeat" with much delicacy and sentimentalism. It garners an A rating, and it should do well for her on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot Country charts."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Country Weekly awarded the song a B+ and commented, "Carrie interprets the message with real conviction and a passion that hits the right emotional notes without going overboard."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heartbeat_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song was first released to digital retailers as a <a href="/wiki/Promotional_single" class="mw-redirect" title="Promotional single">promotional single</a> from <i>Storyteller</i> on October 9, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-E_online_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-E_online-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It sold 23,000 digital copies in its first week, debuting at number five on the Country Digital Songs chart for the week of October 19, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> That same week, it debuted on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)"><i>Billboard</i></a> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart at number 26, and on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Bubbling_Under_Hot_100_Singles" class="mw-redirect" title="Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles">Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles</a> extension chart at number 23.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bubbling_under_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bubbling_under-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was later confirmed, at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2015" title="American Music Awards of 2015">American Music Awards of 2015</a>, that "Heartbeat" would serve as the second single from <i>Storyteller</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It was then sent to country radio at 1:00 AM eastern time on the morning of November 23, 2015, and made its official radio impact on November 30, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-country_adds_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-country_adds-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The official release prompted it to debut at number 41 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart. The song also debuted at number 76 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, becoming that week's "Hot Shot Debut". Following Underwood's performance at the <a href="/wiki/58th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="58th Grammy Awards">58th Grammy Awards</a> on February 15, 2016, the sales of the song increased by 133% and sold 37,000 copies for the week of March 5, 2016 <sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and jumped from number 55 to number 42 on the US <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. On the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart, it reached number two and stayed at that spot for two weeks. "Heartbeat" went on to reach number one on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart for the week of March 26, 2016, becoming Underwood's fourteenth number-one on the chart, widening her lead for the most among women. <a href="/wiki/Reba_McEntire" title="Reba McEntire">Reba McEntire</a> ranks second with 11.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The song was certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> on April 29, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodHeartbeatsingleCertRef_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodHeartbeatsingleCertRef-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> As of May 2016, it sold 348,000 copies in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-us_sales_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-us_sales-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"Heartbeat" entered the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Canadian_Digital_Songs" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Canadian Digital Songs">Hot Canadian Digital Songs</a> chart at number 31.<sup id="cite_ref-canadian_digital_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-canadian_digital-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The song also reached number 60 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> and number one on the <a href="/wiki/Canada_Country" class="mw-redirect" title="Canada Country">Canada Country</a> chart, becoming her third single to do so, and the second from <i>Storyteller</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> It also became her third charting single in Scotland, debuting on the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Singles_Chart" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish Singles Chart">Scottish Singles Chart</a> at number 80.<sup id="cite_ref-scotland_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotland-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heartbeat_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The official music video for the single was released on December 2, 2015. It was directed by Randee St. Nicholas.
</p><p>The video shows Underwood in a sparkly gold flowing dress, walking barefoot through a lush forest, humming the melody of the song. She is seen wandering through the forest and singing the ballad.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Heartbeat_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood performed the song live at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2015" title="American Music Awards of 2015">2015 American Music Awards</a> on November 22, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> She also performed the song on the <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_12)" class="mw-redirect" title="The X Factor (UK series 12)">twelfth season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The X Factor (UK TV series)">The X Factor</a></i> on November 29, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Underwood performed the song along with Sam Hunt at the <a href="/wiki/58th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="58th Grammy Awards">58th Grammy Awards</a> on February 15, 2016. The pair also performed Hunt's song "<a href="/wiki/Take_Your_Time_(Sam_Hunt_song)" title="Take Your Time (Sam Hunt song)">Take Your Time</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
