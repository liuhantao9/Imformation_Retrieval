<p>The <b>Town of Palm Beach</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Incorporated_town" title="Incorporated town">incorporated town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_County,_Florida" title="Palm Beach County, Florida">Palm Beach County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, United States. The <a href="/wiki/Intracoastal_Waterway" title="Intracoastal Waterway">Intracoastal Waterway</a> separates it from several neighboring cities including <a href="/wiki/West_Palm_Beach,_Florida" title="West Palm Beach, Florida">West Palm Beach</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Worth,_Florida" class="mw-redirect" title="Lake Worth, Florida">Lake Worth</a>. As of <a href="/wiki/U.S._Census,_2010" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Census, 2010">2010</a>, Palm Beach had a year-round population of 8,348. In 2018, <a href="/wiki/Bloomberg_News" title="Bloomberg News">Bloomberg</a> ranked Palm Beach as the 27th-wealthiest place in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Palm_Beach,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to being established as a resort by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Morrison_Flagler" class="mw-redirect" title="Henry Morrison Flagler">Henry Morrison Flagler</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-palm_beach_reuters_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-palm_beach_reuters-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  who made the Atlantic coast <a href="/wiki/Barrier_island" title="Barrier island">barrier island</a> accessible via his <a href="/wiki/Florida_East_Coast_Railway" title="Florida East Coast Railway">Florida East Coast Railway</a>, Palm Beach was a sparsely populated part of <a href="/wiki/Lake_Worth,_Florida" class="mw-redirect" title="Lake Worth, Florida">Lake Worth</a>. The nucleus of the community was established by Flagler's two luxury resort hotels, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Poinciana_Hotel" title="Royal Poinciana Hotel">Royal Poinciana Hotel</a> and The <a href="/wiki/Breakers_Hotel" class="mw-redirect" title="Breakers Hotel">Breakers Hotel</a>. <a href="/wiki/West_Palm_Beach,_Florida" title="West Palm Beach, Florida">West Palm Beach</a> was built across Lake Worth as a service town and has become a major city in its own right.
</p><p>Flagler's house lots were bought by the beneficiaries of the <a href="/wiki/Gilded_Age" title="Gilded Age">Gilded Age</a>, and in 1902 Flagler himself built a <a href="/wiki/Beaux-Arts_architecture" title="Beaux-Arts architecture">Beaux-Arts</a> mansion, <a href="/wiki/Flagler_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Flagler Museum">Whitehall</a>, designed by the New York-based firm <a href="/wiki/Carr%C3%A8re_and_Hastings" title="Carrère and Hastings">Carrère and Hastings</a> and helped establish the Palm Beach winter "season" by constantly entertaining. The town was incorporated on 17 April 1911.
</p><p>An area known as the Styx housed the people who built up the island. Workers rented small houses from the landowners. In the early 1900s the landowners agreed to evict all of the residents of the Styx (who moved to <a href="/wiki/West_Palm_Beach,_Florida" title="West Palm Beach, Florida">West Palm Beach, Florida</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Edward_R._Bradley" title="Edward R. Bradley">Edward R. Bradley</a> bought up much of this land.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  The houses were razed, according to the <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_Daily_News" title="Palm Beach Daily News">Palm Beach Daily News</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Palm_Beach,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The wreck of the <i>Providencia</i> is credited with giving Palm Beach its famous name. The <i>Providencia</i> was traveling from <a href="/wiki/Havana,_Cuba" class="mw-redirect" title="Havana, Cuba">Havana</a> to <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A1diz,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Cádiz, Spain">Cádiz, Spain</a> with a cargo of coconuts harvested on the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> <a href="/wiki/Island_nation" class="mw-redirect" title="Island nation">island nation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_%26_Tobago" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinidad &amp; Tobago">Trinidad &amp; Tobago</a>, when the ship wrecked near Palm Beach. Many of the coconut naturalized or were planted along the Palm Beach coast.<sup id="cite_ref-HOPB_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HOPB-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PBTL_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PBTL-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PBName_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PBName-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VF500_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VF500-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> A lush grove of palm trees soon grew on what would later be named Palm Beach. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Palm_Beach,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tnone" style="margin:0 auto;overflow:hidden;width:auto;max-width:1008px"><div class="thumbinner"><div class="noresize" style="overflow:auto"><a href="/wiki/File:Palm_Beach_proper_Florida_photo_by_D_Ramey_Logan.jpg" class="image" title="Palm Beach"><img alt="Palm Beach" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Palm_Beach_proper_Florida_photo_by_D_Ramey_Logan.jpg/1000px-Palm_Beach_proper_Florida_photo_by_D_Ramey_Logan.jpg" decoding="async" width="1000" height="283" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Palm_Beach_proper_Florida_photo_by_D_Ramey_Logan.jpg/1500px-Palm_Beach_proper_Florida_photo_by_D_Ramey_Logan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Palm_Beach_proper_Florida_photo_by_D_Ramey_Logan.jpg/2000px-Palm_Beach_proper_Florida_photo_by_D_Ramey_Logan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3170" data-file-height="898" /></a></div><div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Palm_Beach_proper_Florida_photo_by_D_Ramey_Logan.jpg" title="File:Palm Beach proper Florida photo by D Ramey Logan.jpg"> </a></div>Palm Beach</div></div></div>
<p>Palm Beach is the easternmost town in Florida, located on an 18-mile (29&#160;km) long <a href="/wiki/Barrier_island" title="Barrier island">barrier island</a> between <a href="/wiki/Lake_Worth_Lagoon" title="Lake Worth Lagoon">Lake Worth Lagoon</a> on the west and the Atlantic Ocean on the east. At no point is the island wider than three-quarters of a mile (1.2&#160;km), and in places it is only 500 feet (150 m) wide.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Census_Bureau" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Census Bureau">U.S. Census Bureau</a>, the town has a total area of 10.4 square miles (27 square kilometres). 3.9 square miles (10 square kilometres) of it is land and 6.5 square miles (17 square kilometres) of it is water. The total area is 62.45% water.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Palm_Beach,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a> Palm Beach has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_savanna" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical savanna">tropical savanna climate</a> with hot, humid summers and warm, dry winters. <sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The wet season is from May to October when convective thunderstorms and tropical downpours are common, and weak tropical lows pass nearby. Average high temperatures in Palm Beach are 87 to 93&#160;°F (31 to 34&#160;°C) with lows of 76 to 82 ºF (25 to 28 ºC). During this period, more than half of the summer days bring occasional afternoon thunderstorms and seabreezes that somewhat cool the rest of the day.<sup id="cite_ref-weatherbase.com_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weatherbase.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The winter brings dry, sunny, and much less humid weather. Average high temperatures of 76 to 83&#160;°F (24 to 28&#160;°C) and lows of 67 to 73&#160;°F (19 to 23&#160;°C), and is considered the main tourist season in the Palm Beach area. Occasionally highs drop below 70 ºF while at other times high temperatures occasionally reach 90 ºF in mid winter. In some years, the dry season can become quite dry, and water restrictions are imposed.
</p><p>The annual average precipitation is 65&#160;in (1,700&#160;mm), most of which occurs during the summer season from May through October. In the wet summer season, short-lived heavy afternoon thunderstorms are common. Palm Beach reports more than 2,900 hours of sunshine annually. Although rare, tropical cyclones can impact Palm Beach, with the last direct hit in 1928.<sup id="cite_ref-weatherbase.com_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weatherbase.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a> of 2010, there were 9,091 households, out of which 47.2% were vacant. As of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_2000_Census" class="mw-redirect" title="United States 2000 Census">2000 census</a>, over half the population (52.7%) were 65 years of age or older, with a median age of 67 years. 9.4% were under the age of 18, 1.5% were from 18 to 24, 11.5% were from 25 to 44, and 25.0% from 45 to 64. For every 100 females, there were 79.3 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 77.0 males.
</p><p>In 2000, the <a href="/wiki/Household_income" class="mw-redirect" title="Household income">household income</a> for the town was $109,219. Males had a median income of $71,685 versus $42,875 for females. 5.3% of the population and 2.4% of families were below the <a href="/wiki/Poverty_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>. 4.6% of those under the age of 18 and 2.9% of those 65 and older were living below the poverty line. Palm Beach had a median household income of $124,562 and a median family income of $137,867. The town's affluence and its "abundance of pleasures" and "strong community-oriented sensibility" were cited when it was selected in June 2003 as <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">America's</a> "Best Place to Live" by <i><a href="/wiki/Robb_Report" title="Robb Report">Robb Report</a></i> magazine.
</p><p>In 2000, 7.7% of the households had children under the age of 18 living with them, 48.1% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 3.3% had a female householder with no husband present, and 47.8% were non-families. 42.6% of all households were made up of individuals and 27.6% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 1.81 and the average family size was 2.38.
</p><p>In 2000, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> was the <a href="/wiki/First_language" title="First language">first language</a> of 87.81% of all residents, while <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> comprised 4.48%, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> consisted of 3.65%, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> made up 2.16%, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> speakers made up 0.45%, <a href="/wiki/Yiddish_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Yiddish language">Yiddish</a> made up 0.36%, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a> was at 0.30%, <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a> at 0.25%, and <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a> was the <a href="/wiki/Mother_tongue" class="mw-redirect" title="Mother tongue">mother tongue</a> of 0.24% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2000, Palm Beach had the 40th highest percentage of <a href="/wiki/Russian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Russian people">Russian</a> residents in the U.S., with 10.30% of the populace (tied with <a href="/wiki/Pomona,_New_York" title="Pomona, New York">Pomona, New York</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Township" title="Township">township</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lower_Merion,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Lower Merion, Pennsylvania">Lower Merion, Pennsylvania</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It also had the 26th highest percentage of <a href="/wiki/Austrian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Austrian people">Austrian</a> residents in the US, at 2.10% of the town's population (which tied with 19 other US areas).<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Palm_Beach,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Palm Beach is a generally Republican city. In 2016, Donald Trump received 3,231 votes and Hillary Clinton received 2,612 votes. <sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Palm_Beach,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Worth_Avenue_Vicinity_by_Volkan_Yuksel_2012-04-14_DSC09553.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Worth_Avenue_Vicinity_by_Volkan_Yuksel_2012-04-14_DSC09553.jpg/220px-Worth_Avenue_Vicinity_by_Volkan_Yuksel_2012-04-14_DSC09553.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="86" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Worth_Avenue_Vicinity_by_Volkan_Yuksel_2012-04-14_DSC09553.jpg/330px-Worth_Avenue_Vicinity_by_Volkan_Yuksel_2012-04-14_DSC09553.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Worth_Avenue_Vicinity_by_Volkan_Yuksel_2012-04-14_DSC09553.jpg/440px-Worth_Avenue_Vicinity_by_Volkan_Yuksel_2012-04-14_DSC09553.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4912" data-file-height="1920" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Worth_Avenue_Vicinity_by_Volkan_Yuksel_2012-04-14_DSC09553.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Worth Avenue</div></div></div>
<p>The city is served by <a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">Amtrak</a><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Tri-Rail" title="Tri-Rail">Tri-Rail</a>, which connect Palm Beach with <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_International_Airport" title="Palm Beach International Airport">Palm Beach International Airport</a>, all located in <a href="/wiki/West_Palm_Beach" class="mw-redirect" title="West Palm Beach">West Palm Beach</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Higher-speed_rail" title="Higher-speed rail">higher speed</a> <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Trains_USA" title="Virgin Trains USA">Virgin Trains USA</a> service has a stop in West Palm Beach, as well. Public transportation is available through <a href="/wiki/Palm_Tran" title="Palm Tran">Palm Tran</a>, and connects with the rest of the county.
</p><p>The northern portion of Palm Beach is served by the Route 41 bus which travels from the northernmost portion of Palm Beach at the inlet and then down to Royal Palm Way, across the Royal Park Bridge (<a href="/wiki/Florida_State_Road_704" title="Florida State Road 704">State Road 704</a>) into West Palm Beach and up to the government center, and then follows the same route in reverse.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Private vehicles and taxis are the predominant means of transport in Palm Beach. Profiling of lower-cost cars and minorities has resulted in tense relations between visitors and the Town.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bicycles are a popular mode of transportation on the island, although most areas have no bicycle trails, so safe and comfortable travel is not always assured. The Lake Trail, exclusively for pedestrian and bike traffic, extends from Royal Palm Way (<a href="/wiki/Florida_State_Road_704" title="Florida State Road 704">State Road 704</a>) in the south up to the north end of the island. The trail follows the edge of the <a href="/wiki/Lake_Worth_Lagoon" title="Lake Worth Lagoon">Lake Worth Lagoon</a> (part of the intracoastal waterway) except for a section between the <a href="/wiki/Flagler_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Flagler Museum">Flagler Museum</a> and the Biltmore Condominiums, where the trail follows the streets. Another break occurs to pass around the Sailfish Yacht Club in the north end of the island. The Lake Trail is filled daily with bikers, rollerbladers, runners, and dog-walkers.
</p><p>Traveling by bike along the ocean can be hazardous. Only a short section in the downtown area has sidewalks. The roads along the ocean are narrow and have small or nonexistent shoulders, making biking a potentially dangerous activity in those areas.
</p><p>In the southern end of the island, south of Sloan's Curve, through <a href="/wiki/South_Palm_Beach,_Florida" title="South Palm Beach, Florida">South Palm Beach</a> to East Ocean Avenue (linking to Lantana) is a two-mile (3200 m) long, relatively wide pedestrian path that is popular with walkers, runners, and bikers alike.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Schools">Schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Palm_Beach,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Palm Beach Public Elementary is located on the island and has kindergarten through fifth grade. It has a school grade of A and 477 students attend the school. <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_Day_Academy" title="Palm Beach Day Academy">Palm Beach Day Academy</a> is a private school in the area. It was formed in 2005 from a merger between Palm Beach Day School and the Academy of the Palm Beaches.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
