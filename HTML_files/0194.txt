<p>National Significant Numbers (NSN):
</p>
<ul><li>Minimum number length (excluding the country code): six digits.</li>
<li>Maximum number length (excluding the country code): seven digits.</li></ul>
<p>International dialling format:
</p>
<ul><li>Mobile: +597 XXX XXXX</li>
<li>Fixed: +597 XXX XXX</li></ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> uses a number plan introduced in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Telephone_numbers_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Until 1969, a system was used with 2-digit area codes and local numbers varying from 2 to 5 digits depending on the area. Around 1969/1970 this was changed to a closed numbering system with 5-digit numbers nationwide; a zero had to be dialled before the number. At that time, some areas were not automated yet.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
