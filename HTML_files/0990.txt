<p>The <b>Silver Star Medal</b> is the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">United States Armed Forces</a>'s third-highest <a href="/wiki/Awards_and_decorations_of_the_United_States_military" class="mw-redirect" title="Awards and decorations of the United States military">personal decoration</a> for <a href="/wiki/Courage" title="Courage">valor</a> in combat. The Silver Star Medal is awarded primarily to members of the United States Armed Forces for gallantry in action against an enemy of the United States.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_Star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Silver Star Medal (SSM)<sup id="cite_ref-DoD1348.33v3_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DoD1348.33v3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> is the successor award to the "<a href="/wiki/Citation_Star" title="Citation Star">Citation Star</a>" (&#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>16</sub></span> silver star) which was established by an <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Congress" title="Act of Congress">Act of Congress</a> on July 9, 1918, during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. On July 19, 1932, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_War" title="United States Secretary of War">Secretary of War</a> approved the conversion of the "Citation Star" to the SSM with the original "Citation Star" incorporated into the center of the medal.
</p><p>Authorization for the Silver Star Medal was placed into law by an Act of Congress for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">U.S. Navy</a> on August 7, 1942, and an Act of Congress for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">U.S. Army</a> on December 15, 1942. The current statutory authorization for the medal is <a href="/wiki/Title_10_of_the_United_States_Code" title="Title 10 of the United States Code">Title 10 of the United States Code</a>, <a href="/wiki/Title_10_of_the_United_States_Code" title="Title 10 of the United States Code">10 U.S.C.</a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/3746">§&#160;3746</a> for the U.S. Army,  <a href="/wiki/Title_10_of_the_United_States_Code" title="Title 10 of the United States Code">10 U.S.C.</a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/8746">§&#160;8746</a> for the U.S. Air Force, and <a href="/wiki/Title_10_of_the_United_States_Code" title="Title 10 of the United States Code">10 U.S.C.</a>&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/10/6244">§&#160;6244</a> for the U.S. Navy.
</p><p>The U.S. Army and Air Force award the medal as the "Silver Star". The U.S. Navy, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard continue to award the medal as the "Silver Star Medal". Since 21 December 2016, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Defense" title="United States Department of Defense">Department of Defense</a> (DoD) refers to the decoration as the Silver Star Medal.<sup id="cite_ref-DoD1348.33v3_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DoD1348.33v3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Award_criteria">Award criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_Star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Award criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Silver Star Medal is awarded for gallantry, so long as the action does not justify the award of one of the next higher valor awards: the <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Cross_(United_States)" title="Distinguished Service Cross (United States)">Distinguished Service Cross</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Navy_Cross" title="Navy Cross">Navy Cross</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Air_Force_Cross_(United_States)" title="Air Force Cross (United States)">Air Force Cross</a>, or the <a href="/wiki/Coast_Guard_Cross" title="Coast Guard Cross">Coast Guard Cross</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Section_578.12_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Section_578.12-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The gallantry displayed must have taken place while in action against an enemy of the United States, while engaged in military operations involving conflict with an opposing foreign force, or while serving with friendly foreign forces engaged in an armed conflict against an opposing armed force in which the United States is not a belligerent party.<sup id="cite_ref-DoD1348.33v3_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DoD1348.33v3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Silver Star Medal is awarded for singular acts of valor or heroism over a brief period, such as one or two days of a battle.<sup id="cite_ref-DoD1348.33v3_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DoD1348.33v3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Air Force pilots and combat systems officers and Navy/Marine Corps naval aviators and flight officers flying fighter aircraft, are often considered eligible to receive the Silver Star upon becoming an <a href="/wiki/Flying_ace" title="Flying ace">ace</a> (i.e., having five or more confirmed aerial kills), which entails the pilot and, in multi-seat fighters, the weapons system officer or radar intercept officer, intentionally and successfully risking his life multiple times under combat conditions and emerging victorious.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> However, during the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>, the last conflict to produce U.S. fighter aces: an Air Force pilot and two navigators/weapon systems officers (who were later retrained as Air Force pilots), a naval aviator and a naval flight officer/radar intercept officer who had achieved this distinction, were eventually awarded the <a href="/wiki/Air_Force_Cross_(United_States)" title="Air Force Cross (United States)">Air Force Cross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Navy_Cross" title="Navy Cross">Navy Cross</a>, respectively, in addition to SSMs previously awarded for earlier aerial kills.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<li>Army – <a href="/wiki/Valorous_Unit_Award" title="Valorous Unit Award">Valorous Unit Award</a></li>
<li>Coast Guard – <a href="/wiki/Coast_Guard_Unit_Commendation" title="Coast Guard Unit Commendation">Coast Guard Unit Commendation</a></li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearance">Appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_Star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Silver Star Medal is a gold <a href="/wiki/Five-pointed_star" title="Five-pointed star">five-pointed star</a>, <span class="frac nowrap">1<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>2</sub></span> inches (38&#160;mm) in circumscribing diameter with a <a href="/wiki/Laurel_wreath" title="Laurel wreath">laurel wreath</a> encircling rays from the center and a <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>16</sub></span> inch (4.8&#160;mm) diameter silver star superimposed in the center. The pendant is suspended from a rectangular shaped metal loop with rounded corners. The reverse has the inscription <i>FOR GALLANTRY IN ACTION</i>. The ribbon is <span class="frac nowrap">1<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span> inches (35&#160;mm) wide and consists of the following stripes: <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>7</sup>&#8260;<sub>32</sub></span> inch (5.6&#160;mm) Old Glory red (center stripe); proceeding outward in pairs <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>7</sup>&#8260;<sub>32</sub></span> inch (5.6&#160;mm) white; <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>7</sup>&#8260;<sub>32</sub></span> inch (5.6&#160;mm) ultramarine blue; <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>64</sub></span> inch (1.2&#160;mm) white; and <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>32</sub></span> inch (2.4&#160;mm) ultramarine blue.<sup id="cite_ref-tioh_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tioh-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dt>Ribbon devices</dt></dl>
<p>Second and subsequent awards of the Silver Star Medal are denoted by bronze or silver <a href="/wiki/Oak_leaf_cluster" title="Oak leaf cluster">oak leaf clusters</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">Army</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">Air Force</a> and by gold or silver <a href="/wiki/5/16_inch_star" title="5/16 inch star">&#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>5</sup>&#8260;<sub>16</sub></span> inch stars</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">Navy</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">Marine Corps</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">Coast Guard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DoD1348.33v3_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DoD1348.33v3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-COMDTINSTM1650.25D_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-COMDTINSTM1650.25D-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DoD1348.33v3_5-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DoD1348.33v3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recipients">Recipients</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_Star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Recipients">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cheney_Presents_Silver_Star_to_Spc._Monica_Brown.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Cheney_Presents_Silver_Star_to_Spc._Monica_Brown.jpg/220px-Cheney_Presents_Silver_Star_to_Spc._Monica_Brown.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="274" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Cheney_Presents_Silver_Star_to_Spc._Monica_Brown.jpg/330px-Cheney_Presents_Silver_Star_to_Spc._Monica_Brown.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Cheney_Presents_Silver_Star_to_Spc._Monica_Brown.jpg/440px-Cheney_Presents_Silver_Star_to_Spc._Monica_Brown.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1406" data-file-height="1754" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cheney_Presents_Silver_Star_to_Spc._Monica_Brown.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">Army</a> <a href="/wiki/Specialist_(rank)" title="Specialist (rank)">Specialist</a> <a href="/wiki/Monica_Lin_Brown" title="Monica Lin Brown">Monica Lin Brown</a> receives the Silver Star from then-<a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_United_States" title="Vice President of the United States">Vice President</a> <a href="/wiki/Dick_Cheney" title="Dick Cheney">Dick Cheney</a>, 2008.</div></div></div>
<p>The Department of Defense does not keep extensive records for the Silver Star Medal. Independent groups estimate that between 100,000 and 150,000 SSMs have been awarded since the decoration was established.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Colonel_(United_States)" title="Colonel (United States)">Colonel</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Hackworth" title="David Hackworth">David Hackworth</a> who was awarded ten SSMs while serving in the Army during the Korean War and Vietnam War, is likely to be the person awarded the most SSMs.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Donald H. Russell, a civilian <a href="/wiki/Vought_F4U_Corsair" title="Vought F4U Corsair">Vought F4U Corsair</a> technical support engineer attached to a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">Marine Corps</a> fighter wing, received the SSM for his actions aboard <a href="/wiki/USS_Franklin_(CV-13)" title="USS Franklin (CV-13)">USS&#160;<i>Franklin</i></a> after the carrier was attacked by a Japanese <a href="/wiki/Dive_bomber" title="Dive bomber">dive bomber</a> in March 1945.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Female_recipients">Female recipients</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_Star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Female recipients">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Three Army nurses that served in World War I were cited in 1919 and 1920 with Citation Stars for gallantry in attending to the wounded while under artillery fire in July 1918. In 2007, it was discovered that they had never been awarded their Citation Stars. The three nurses (Army nurses served without rank until 1920) were awarded the Silver Star Medal posthumously:<sup id="cite_ref-auto_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
<sup id="cite_ref-auto_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Jane_Rignel" title="Jane Rignel">Jane Rignel</a> – Mobile Hospital No. 2, <a href="/wiki/42nd_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="42nd Infantry Division (United States)">42nd Division</a>, for gallantry in "giving aid to the wounded under heavy fire" in France on July 15, 1918</li>
<li>Linnie Leckrone – Shock Team No. 134, Field Hospital No. 127, <a href="/wiki/32nd_Infantry_Division_(United_States)" title="32nd Infantry Division (United States)">32nd Division</a>, for gallantry while "attending to the wounded during an artilley bombardment" in France on July 29, 1918</li>
<li>Irene Robar – Shock Team No. 134, Field Hospital No. 127, 32nd Division, for gallantry while "attending to the wounded during an artillery bombardment" in France on July 29, 1918</li></ul>
<p>An unknown number of servicewomen received the award in World War II. Four Army nurses serving in Italy during the war—<a href="/wiki/First_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="First Lieutenant">First Lieutenant</a> Mary Roberts, <a href="/wiki/Second_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Second Lieutenant">Second Lieutenant</a> <a href="/wiki/Elaine_Roe" title="Elaine Roe">Elaine Roe</a>, Second Lieutenant Rita Virginia Rourke, and Second Lieutenant <a href="/wiki/Ellen_Ainsworth" title="Ellen Ainsworth">Ellen Ainsworth</a> (posthumous)—became the first women recipients of the Silver Star, all cited for their bravery in evacuating the 33rd Field Hospital at <a href="/wiki/Anzio" title="Anzio">Anzio</a> on February 10, 1944.<sup id="cite_ref-foxnews_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-foxnews-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Later that same year, Corporal Magdalena Leones, a <a href="/wiki/Filipino_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Filipino American">Filipino American</a>, received the medal for <a href="/wiki/Clandestine_operation" title="Clandestine operation">clandestine</a> activities on <a href="/wiki/Luzon" title="Luzon">Luzon</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> as of 2016<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Silver_Star&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, she is the only female <a href="/wiki/Asian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian American">Asian American</a> to receive a Silver Star.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The next known servicewomen to receive the Silver Star is Army National Guard <a href="/wiki/Sergeant" title="Sergeant">Sergeant</a> <a href="/wiki/Leigh_Ann_Hester" title="Leigh Ann Hester">Leigh Ann Hester</a> in 2005, for gallantry during an insurgent ambush on a convoy in <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a><sup id="cite_ref-foxnews_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-foxnews-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and Army <a href="/wiki/Specialist_(rank)" title="Specialist (rank)">Specialist</a> <a href="/wiki/Monica_Lin_Brown" title="Monica Lin Brown">Monica Lin Brown</a> in March 2008, for extraordinary heroism as a <a href="/wiki/Combat_medic" title="Combat medic">combat medic</a> in the <a href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%93present)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001–present)">War in Afghanistan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-foxnews_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-foxnews-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
