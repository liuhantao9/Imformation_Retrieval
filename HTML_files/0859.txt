<p><b>Tom Sykes</b> (born 19 August 1985 in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, West Yorkshire, England) is a professional <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_racing" title="Motorcycle racing">motorcycle racer</a> who has competed in <a href="/wiki/World_Superbike_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="World Superbike Championship">World Superbike Championship</a> for ten years.
Following his fourth place finish in 2018,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Sykes left the Kawasaki factory racing team and will be racing a <a href="/wiki/BMW_S1000RR#2019" title="BMW S1000RR">2019</a> for <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Muir_Racing" title="Shaun Muir Racing">Shaun Muir Racing</a> in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007 he gained his first ride in British Superbikes, riding a Stobart Vent-Axia <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a>; Sykes finished in sixth position in the championship in his rookie year. Following this success he was signed by the <a href="/wiki/Rizla" title="Rizla">Rizla</a> <a href="/wiki/Suzuki" title="Suzuki">Suzuki</a> team for the 2008 season; he went on to finish 4th in the championship as well as making wildcard World Superbike appearances at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Donington_Park" title="Donington Park">Donington Park</a>, where he impressed strongly, helping him gain a World Superbike ride at Yamaha Motor Italia for the 2009 season.<sup id="cite_ref-londonbikers.com_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-londonbikers.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> For 2010 he raced a <a href="/wiki/Paul_Bird_Motorsport" title="Paul Bird Motorsport">PBM Racing</a> Kawasaki in the series.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The date of the event predicted near this tag has passed. (October 2018)">needs update</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He won his first SBK title in 2013 for Kawasaki.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tom_Sykes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sykes is the grandson of Peter Brooks, who is involved in the manufacture of Kawasaki bikes.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Sykes raced in the British Supersport championship from 2003 to 2006, finishing 8th, 5th, 6th, and as runner-up to <a href="/wiki/Cal_Crutchlow" title="Cal Crutchlow">Cal Crutchlow</a> in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="British_Superbikes_2007.E2.80.932008"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Superbikes_2007–2008">British Superbikes 2007–2008</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tom_Sykes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: British Superbikes 2007–2008">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>2007 was his first season in the <a href="/wiki/British_Superbike" class="mw-redirect" title="British Superbike">British Superbike</a> championship, riding for the Stobart Vent-Axia <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a> team alongside 2003 series champion <a href="/wiki/Shane_Byrne_(motorcycle_racer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Shane Byrne (motorcycle racer)">Shane Byrne</a>. He finished 18 of the first 20 rounds, including a pair of 4th places at <a href="/wiki/Snetterton_Motor_Racing_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Snetterton Motor Racing Circuit">Snetterton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Second on the grid at <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Park" title="Oulton Park">Oulton Park</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and pole at <a href="/wiki/Donington_Park" title="Donington Park">Donington Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Sykes took his first two podiums at the <a href="/wiki/Croft_Circuit" title="Croft Circuit">Croft Circuit</a>, to move up to 6th in the championship, immediately behind Byrne, and immediately ahead of <a href="/wiki/Leon_Camier" title="Leon Camier">Leon Camier</a> on another Honda.
</p><p>Shortly after joining the Rizla Suzuki team for 2008, he was seen testing a Suzuki MotoGP bike.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Sykes started the 2008 with a 6th and an 8th at <a href="/wiki/Thruxton_Circuit" title="Thruxton Circuit">Thruxton</a> (The first races were there as the season opener at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a> was snowed off). He took pole position at <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Park" title="Oulton Park">Oulton Park</a>, but crashed at the aborted start of race 1, before finishing 5th on the restart <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.motorcyclenews.com/MCN/sport/sportresults/mcn/2008/may/5-11/may0508oultonparkbsbraceoneshanebyrnewinsbattlewithleonhaslam/">[1]</a>. He led race 2 until being taken out by <a href="/wiki/Leon_Haslam" title="Leon Haslam">Leon Haslam</a>, who was excluded for the move. Sykes continued to finish towards the front of the field consistently scoring podiums at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a> (2nd), <a href="/wiki/Donington_Park" title="Donington Park">Donington Park</a> (3rd) and <a href="/wiki/Snetterton_Motor_Racing_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Snetterton Motor Racing Circuit">Snetterton</a> (3rd) all in the first of the 2 races. It was at <a href="/wiki/Oulton_Park" title="Oulton Park">Oulton Park</a> where he scored his first two victories, taking the lead from <a href="/wiki/James_Ellison_(motorcycle_racer)" class="mw-redirect" title="James Ellison (motorcycle racer)">James Ellison</a> late in race 1 but leading most of race 2. He followed that up with a third straight win at <a href="/wiki/Knockhill" class="mw-redirect" title="Knockhill">Knockhill</a> in the first race, and collected podiums for the rest of the championship. Sykes finished 4th in the championship with 316 points, 2 points behind <a href="/wiki/Cal_Crutchlow" title="Cal Crutchlow">Cal Crutchlow</a>.
</p>

<p>Sykes made his <a href="/wiki/World_Superbike_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="World Superbike Championship">World Superbike Championship</a> debut at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Wild_card_(sports)" title="Wild card (sports)">wildcard</a>, impressing by qualifying sixth (ahead of three works Suzukis with more powerful engines, including title contender <a href="/wiki/Max_Neukirchner" title="Max Neukirchner">Max Neukirchner</a>). He retired from a strong position in race 1 due to a hole in the radiator by rocks thrown up by Max Biaggi's Sterilgarda Ducati machine, but came back to finish 6th in race 2. He gained a second wildcard meeting at <a href="/wiki/Donington_Park" title="Donington Park">Donington Park</a> in changeable conditions, where he proved even stronger. He again qualified on the second row, but got a flying start in race one and had a comfortable lead when the race was red-flagged due to oil from <a href="/wiki/Noriyuki_Haga" title="Noriyuki Haga">Noriyuki Haga</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Company" title="Yamaha Motor Company">Yamaha</a>. Knowing he had to finish within 4 seconds of <a href="/wiki/Troy_Bayliss" title="Troy Bayliss">Troy Bayliss</a> to win on aggregate, Sykes lead early in race two, before easing off after seeing a white flag with a red cross, which typically means a slippery surface (often with the yellow and red striped oil flag), but racing in British Superbike Championship races, the flag neutralises the race under a full-course caution period with <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> deployment, so Sykes and <a href="/wiki/Leon_Haslam" title="Leon Haslam">Leon Haslam</a> both eased up, anticipating neutralisation, but the time lost here proved costly, as Sykes failed to stay close enough to Bayliss, finishing second behind the three-time series champion. He was less competitive in race two, but his reputation had still been boosted.
</p><p>On 11 September 2008 Sykes signed a contract with the Yamaha Motor Italia World Superbike team for the 2009 season, with an option to extend that contract until 2010 depending on results.<sup id="cite_ref-londonbikers.com_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-londonbikers.com-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Sykes had an average year finishing mostly mid-pack, while his team mate <a href="/wiki/Ben_Spies" title="Ben Spies">Ben Spies</a> was at the front of the field. This led to <a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Company" title="Yamaha Motor Company">Yamaha</a> not offering Sykes a second year, instead hiring fellow Brits <a href="/wiki/James_Toseland" title="James Toseland">James Toseland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cal_Crutchlow" title="Cal Crutchlow">Cal Crutchlow</a>.
</p><p>
Sykes signed for the Paul Bird Motorsport <a href="/wiki/Kawasaki_Heavy_Industries_Motorcycle_%26_Engine" title="Kawasaki Heavy Industries Motorcycle &amp; Engine">Kawasaki</a> team for 2010, as had been rumoured,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> enabling him to stay in the <a href="/wiki/World_Superbike_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="World Superbike Championship">World Superbike</a> class. Sykes said </p><blockquote>
<p>“It’s going to be a challenge, but I’m looking forward to it. I believe that from what is in place in the team we’re going to be able to do a good job.”
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The Kawasaki was relatively uncompetitive in 2010, but Sykes managed a strong fifth place at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale Monza">Monza</a>. He was the team leader for much of the year, with the more experienced <a href="/wiki/Chris_Vermeulen" title="Chris Vermeulen">Chris Vermeulen</a> struggling following a knee injury sustained at the first round at <a href="/wiki/Phillip_Island_Grand_Prix_Circuit" title="Phillip Island Grand Prix Circuit">Phillip Island</a>. Despite speculation as to his future with the team, Sykes flew to Japan to help test the ZX-10R, the bike the team will use for 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He made a wildcard appearance with the team in the <a href="/wiki/2010_British_Superbike_Championship_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 British Superbike Championship season">British Superbike Championship</a> at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tom_Sykes_-_Phillip_Island_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Tom_Sykes_-_Phillip_Island_2017.jpg/220px-Tom_Sykes_-_Phillip_Island_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Tom_Sykes_-_Phillip_Island_2017.jpg/330px-Tom_Sykes_-_Phillip_Island_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Tom_Sykes_-_Phillip_Island_2017.jpg/440px-Tom_Sykes_-_Phillip_Island_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4608" data-file-height="3072" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tom_Sykes_-_Phillip_Island_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sykes at the <a href="/wiki/Phillip_Island_Grand_Prix_Circuit" title="Phillip Island Grand Prix Circuit">Phillip Island</a> 2017 Australian World Superbike round</div></div></div>
<p>On the final weekend of the 2010 World Superbike Championship season, Sykes confirmed that he had signed a one-year contract with Kawasaki that would see him ride in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Superbike_World_Championship_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Superbike World Championship season">2011 Superbike World Championship season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sykes crashed in the chicane at Donington Park shortly after he passed an Aprilia and moved up to 4th place and he was taken out of the race while his teammates managed mid -placings. He finished close to last on race 2, but Lascorz made 6th.
</p><p>Tom Sykes has been crowned the 2013 World Superbike Champion after securing the third-place finish he needed to secure a popular title victory at Jerez, while Eugene Laverty took a dramatic last corner victory over Marco Melandri.
</p><p>On 21 June 2014, Tom Sykes captured his 21st career Superpole at Italy’s Misano World Circuit for round seven of the series.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
