<p><b>Surinamese people</b> are the citizens of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> and their descendants abroad. Suriname is a multi-ethnic nation, home to people of different ethnic and national backgrounds. As a result, Surinamese do not consider their nationality as an ethnicity but as a citizenship with various ethnicities.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethnic_groups">Ethnic groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinamese_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ethnic groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The population of Suriname is a mixture of different ethnic groups:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Indo-Surinamese" title="Indo-Surinamese">East Indians</a> form 27% of the population. They are descendants of 19th-century contract workers from <a href="/wiki/British_India" class="mw-redirect" title="British India">British India</a>. They are from the Indian states of <a href="/wiki/Bihar" title="Bihar">Bihar</a> and Eastern <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a>, in Northern India, along the <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepali</a> border.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Afro-Surinamese" title="Afro-Surinamese">Afro-Surinamese</a> form about 37% of the population, and are usually divided into two groups: the <a href="/wiki/Creole_peoples" title="Creole peoples">Creoles</a> (15.7%), mixed descendants of enslaved Africans and European colonists (mostly Dutch), and <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">Maroons</a> (21.7%), descendants of escaped enslaved Africans. The two main Maroon groups are the <a href="/wiki/Ndyuka_people" title="Ndyuka people">Ndyuka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saramaka" title="Saramaka">Saramaccans</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Javanese_Surinamese" title="Javanese Surinamese">Javanese</a> (descendants of contract workers from the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Java" title="Java">Java</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-javanese_in_suriname_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-javanese_in_suriname-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> form 14% of the population.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_in_Suriname" title="Indigenous peoples in Suriname">Amerindians</a>, the original inhabitants of Suriname, form 3.7% of the population. The main groups are the <a href="/wiki/Akurio_people" title="Akurio people">Akurio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arawak" title="Arawak">Arawak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kalina_people" title="Kalina people">Kalina</a> (Caribs), <a href="/wiki/Tiriy%C3%B3_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiriyó people">Tiriyó</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wayana_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Wayana people">Wayana</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chinese_Surinamese" title="Chinese Surinamese">Chinese</a>, mainly descendants of the earliest 19th-century contract workers. The 1990s and early 21st century saw renewed immigration on a large scale. In the year 2011 there were over 40,000 Chinese in Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-Romero_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Romero-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/White_Surinamese" title="White Surinamese">Europeans</a>, descendants of <a href="/wiki/Dutch_people" title="Dutch people">Dutch</a> 19th-century immigrant farmers, known as "<a href="/wiki/Dutch_Surinamese" title="Dutch Surinamese">Boeroes</a>" (derived from <i>boer</i>, the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> word for "farmer"), <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Surinamese" title="Portuguese Surinamese">Portuguese</a> from <a href="/wiki/Madeira" title="Madeira">Madeira</a> and other European groups.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Levant" title="Levant">Levantines</a>, primarily <a href="/wiki/Maronites" title="Maronites">Maronites</a> from <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Suriname" title="History of the Jews in Suriname">Jews</a>, mainly descendants of <a href="/wiki/Sephardic_Jews" class="mw-redirect" title="Sephardic Jews">Sephardic Jews</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ashkenazi_Jews" title="Ashkenazi Jews">Ashkenazi Jews</a>. In their history, <a href="/wiki/Jodensavanne" title="Jodensavanne">Jodensavanne</a> plays a major role. Many Jews are mixed with other populations.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Multiracial" title="Multiracial">Multiracial</a> people form 13.4% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-statistics-suriname1_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statistics-suriname1-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinamese_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most of the inhabitants live in the north of the country, in the districts of <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wanica_District" title="Wanica District">Wanica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nickerie_District" title="Nickerie District">Nickerie</a>. The least populated county is <a href="/wiki/Sipaliwini_District" title="Sipaliwini District">Sipaliwini</a>, which covers most of the nation's interior and is sparsely inhabited. More than half of the population lives in and around the capital.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emigration">Emigration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinamese_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Emigration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Migration to the Netherlands began during the colonial era. Initially this was mainly the colonial elite but expanded during the 1920s and 1930s to the less fortunate inhabitants looking for better education, employment, or other opportunities.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Approximately 350,000 individuals of Surinamese descent now live in the Netherlands, with mass migration beginning in the years leading up to Suriname's independence in 1975, and continuing in the period immediately after independence and during military rule in the 1980s. Surinamese continued to migrate to the Netherlands throughout the 1990s because of the then tough economic situation in Suriname. Other emigration destinations include French Guiana and the United States.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Languages">Languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinamese_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Suriname, there are no fewer than twenty languages spoken. Most Surinamese are <a href="/wiki/Multilingual" class="mw-redirect" title="Multilingual">multilingual</a>. In terms of numbers of speakers are the main languages in Suriname, successively the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch language</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sranan_Tongo" title="Sranan Tongo">Sranan Tongo</a> (Surinamese Creole), <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Hindustani#Sarnami_Hindustani" title="Caribbean Hindustani">Sarnami</a> (Surinamese Hindustani), <a href="/wiki/Javanese_language" title="Javanese language">Javanese</a>, and different Maroon languages (especially <a href="/wiki/Saramaccan_language" title="Saramaccan language">Saramaccan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ndyuka_language" title="Ndyuka language">Ndyuka</a>). Since most Surinamese people are multilingual (for instance Dutch and Sranan Tongo), it is not easy for people to share in a particular language group.
</p><p>According to the results of the seventh general population and housing census, which was held in 2004, Dutch is the most spoken home language in Suriname. In over 70% of households Dutch is spoken as the first or second language. The <a href="/wiki/Lingua_franca" title="Lingua franca">lingua franca</a> is Sranan Tongo, which literally means "Surinamese language", and is spoken primarily as a second language in 46% of households, along with 22% Sarnami Hindustani and 11% Javanese.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinamese_people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The following religious statistics have been reported as of 2012:<sup id="cite_ref-CENSUS2012_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CENSUS2012-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<ul><li>48.4% <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> (26.8% <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestant</a> and 21.6% <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a>)</li>
<li>22.3% <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a> (Sanatana Dharma, and Arya Samaj)</li>
<li>13.9% <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> (Sunnism, Sufism, and Ahmadiyya)</li>
<li>4.7% Other</li>
<li>10.7% None</li></ul>
