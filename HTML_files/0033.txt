<p>The <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for <b>Best Female Country Vocal Performance</b> was first awarded in 1965, to <a href="/wiki/Dottie_West" title="Dottie West">Dottie West</a>. The award has had several minor name changes:
</p>
<ul><li>From 1965 to 1967 the award was known as <b>Best Country &amp; Western Vocal Performance - Female</b></li>
<li>In 1968 it was awarded as <b>Best Country &amp; Western Solo Vocal Performance, Female</b></li>
<li>From 1969 to 1994 it was awarded as <b>Best Country Vocal Performance, Female</b></li>
<li>From 1995 to 2011 it was awarded as <b>Best Female Country Vocal Performance</b></li></ul>
<p>The award was discontinued after 2011 award season in a major overhaul of Grammy categories. From 2012 to the present, all solo performances (male, female and instrumental) in the country category are recognized in the newly formed <b><a href="/wiki/Best_Country_Solo_Performance" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Country Solo Performance">Best Country Solo Performance</a></b> category.
</p><p>Years reflect the year in which the Grammy Awards were presented, for works released in the previous year.
</p>
