<p><b>Vivek Hallegere Murthy</b> (born July 10, 1977) is an American <a href="/wiki/Physician" title="Physician">physician</a> and was a <a href="/wiki/Vice_Admiral_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Vice Admiral (United States)">vice admiral</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Public_Health_Service_Commissioned_Corps" title="United States Public Health Service Commissioned Corps">Public Health Service Commissioned Corps</a> who served as the 19th Surgeon General of the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Murthy, who founded the nonprofit <a href="/wiki/Doctors_for_America" title="Doctors for America">Doctors for America</a>, succeeded <a href="/wiki/Boris_Lushniak" title="Boris Lushniak">Boris Lushniak</a>, who had been Acting Surgeon General since 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Murthy was the first Surgeon General of Indian descent and, while serving in office, was the youngest active duty <a href="/wiki/Flag_officer" title="Flag officer">flag officer</a> in federal <a href="/wiki/Uniformed_services_of_the_United_States" title="Uniformed services of the United States">uniformed service</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivek_Murthy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Murthy was born in July 10, 1977,<sup id="cite_ref-Straehley141225_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Straehley141225-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, England, to immigrants from <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>, India. When he was three years old, the family relocated to <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, Florida,<sup id="cite_ref-Straehley141225_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Straehley141225-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> where Murthy was raised and completed his early education, graduating as <a href="/wiki/Valedictorian" title="Valedictorian">valedictorian</a> from <a href="/wiki/Miami_Palmetto_Senior_High_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami Palmetto Senior High School">Miami Palmetto Senior High School</a> in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-Wen-Bierman_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wen-Bierman-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He then attended college at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>, where he graduated <i>magna cum laude</i> in 1997 with a bachelor's degree in Biochemical Sciences.<sup id="cite_ref-Wen-Bierman_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wen-Bierman-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2003, Murthy earned an <a href="/wiki/M.D." class="mw-redirect" title="M.D.">MD</a> from <a href="/wiki/Yale_School_of_Medicine" title="Yale School of Medicine">Yale School of Medicine</a> and an <a href="/wiki/MBA" class="mw-redirect" title="MBA">MBA</a> in Health Care Management from <a href="/wiki/Yale_School_of_Management" title="Yale School of Management">Yale School of Management</a>, where he was a recipient of <a href="/wiki/The_Paul_%26_Daisy_Soros_Fellowships_for_New_Americans" title="The Paul &amp; Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans">The Paul &amp; Daisy Soros Fellowships for New Americans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown2003_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown2003-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Soros1998_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Soros1998-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He completed his residency in Internal Medicine in 2006 at <a href="/wiki/Brigham_and_Women%27s_Hospital" title="Brigham and Women&#39;s Hospital">Brigham and Women's Hospital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BWHDir2013_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWHDir2013-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivek_Murthy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While a Harvard freshman in 1995, Murthy co-founded VISIONS Worldwide, which he led for eight years. The nonprofit organization focused on <a href="/wiki/HIV/AIDS" title="HIV/AIDS">HIV/AIDS</a> education in the U.S. and India. In 1997, he co-founded Swasthya Community Health Partnership to train women as <a href="/wiki/Community_health_worker" title="Community health worker">community health workers</a> in rural India.<sup id="cite_ref-Soros1998_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Soros1998-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MurthyBio_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MurthyBio-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Medical_career">Medical career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivek_Murthy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Medical career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Murthy completed his Internal Medicine residency at <a href="/wiki/Brigham_and_Women%27s_Hospital" title="Brigham and Women&#39;s Hospital">Brigham and Women's Hospital</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Medical_School" title="Harvard Medical School">Harvard Medical School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, Massachusetts where he also led and managed medical care teams as a faculty member.<sup id="cite_ref-MurthyBio_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MurthyBio-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He is also a founder and president of <a href="/wiki/Doctors_for_America" title="Doctors for America">Doctors for America</a>, a group of over 15,000 physicians and medical students supporting high quality affordable care for all.<sup id="cite_ref-Kenny131114_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kenny131114-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Reuters131114_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters131114-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> As part of this work, he developed and led national and local initiatives around coverage and prevention.
</p><p>In 2011, Murthy was appointed by U.S. President Barack Obama to serve on the U.S. Presidential Advisory Council on Prevention, Health Promotion, and Integrative and Public Health within the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Health_and_Human_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="Department of Health and Human Services">Department of Health and Human Services</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Gideon131114_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gideon131114-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The group advises the National Prevention Council on developing strategies and partnerships to advance the nation's health through prevention.<sup id="cite_ref-PAG2013_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAG2013-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Murthy is also the co-founder and chairman of TrialNetworks, a cloud-based Clinical Trial Optimization System for <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical" class="mw-redirect" title="Pharmaceutical">pharmaceutical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biotechnology" title="Biotechnology">biotechnology</a> trials that improves the quality and efficiency of clinical trials to bring new drugs to market faster and more safely.<sup id="cite_ref-Larabee131022_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Larabee131022-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TN2013_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TN2013-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He founded the company as Epernicus in 2008 to originally be a collaborative networking web platform for scientists to boost research productivity.<sup id="cite_ref-WhiteHouse131114_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WhiteHouse131114-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Surgeon_General_of_the_United_States">Surgeon General of the United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivek_Murthy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Surgeon General of the United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2013, Murthy was nominated by President Obama for the post of United States Surgeon General.<sup id="cite_ref-WhiteHouse131114_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WhiteHouse131114-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> His nomination met resistance in the Senate by some Democrats, Republicans and the <a href="/wiki/National_Rifle_Association" title="National Rifle Association">National Rifle Association</a> regarding previous comments Murthy made declaring <a href="/wiki/Gun_violence_in_the_United_States" title="Gun violence in the United States">gun violence</a> as a threat to public health.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Murthy's nomination received broad support from over 100 medical and public health organizations in the U.S., including the <a href="/wiki/American_Heart_Association" title="American Heart Association">American College of Physicians, the American Public Health Association, the American Cancer Society, the American Heart Association, and the</a> <a href="/wiki/American_Diabetes_Association" title="American Diabetes Association">American Diabetes Association</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> His nomination was also endorsed by numerous op-eds and editorial boards including the New England Journal of Medicine and The New York Times.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He received the endorsements of two former Surgeons General: <a href="/wiki/David_Satcher" title="David Satcher">David Satcher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Regina_Benjamin" title="Regina Benjamin">Regina Benjamin</a>. Another former Surgeon General, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Carmona" title="Richard Carmona">Richard Carmona</a>, opposed the appointment based on Murthy's age.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 15, 2014, Murthy's appointment as Surgeon General was approved in a 51–43 Senate vote.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As America’s Doctor, Murthy made it a point to travel extensively - from remote fishing villages in Alaska to American Indian reservations in Oklahoma, to schools, workplaces, homes of worship and community centers across the nation - to hear firsthand about people’s experiences, struggles, and hopes.
</p><p>During his tenure, Murthy created initiatives to tackle our country’s most urgent public health issues. In releasing the first-ever <i>Surgeon General’s Report on Alcohol, Drugs, and Health</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Murthy worked with the nation's leading researchers to compile the latest scientific  data on addiction and issued a call to action to the nation to recognize addiction as a chronic illness, not a moral failing. He worked with Republican and Democratic elected officials and hundreds of partner organizations to bring attention to this critical issue and to expand access to prevention and treatment. He also released a public service announcement with the cast of Mom that doubled the number of people who called into a substance use hotline to ask for help.
</p><p>Additionally, he launched the TurnTheTide campaign and sent a personal letter to millions of health care professionals urging them to join a movement to tackle the opioid epidemic.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The unprecedented letter sparked opioid-related initiatives in healthcare settings across the country. Responding to a growing chorus of questions from parents, teachers, and lawmakers, he also released the first <i>Surgeon General’s Report on E-cigarettes Use Among Youth</i> highlighting the health risks of e-cigarette use for youth.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From the beginning of his tenure, Murthy spoke about the importance of creating a culture of prevention in America, one that is grounded in physical activity, nutrition, and emotional well-being. As part of this effort, he issued <i>Step It Up! The Surgeon General’s Call to Action to Promote Walking and Walkable Communities.</i><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> For the one-year anniversary of the Call to Action, he led a two-week public- private partnership with Fitbit called the Step it Up Challenge that engaged over 600,000 people to increase their physical activity with an industry record-setting 60 billion steps. He also partnered with <a href="/wiki/Elmo" title="Elmo">Elmo</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Chef" title="Top Chef">Top Chef</a></i> to inform the country about vaccines and healthy eating, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Murthy has also worked on climate change and its effects on the country's health,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> and has also spoken out against conversion therapy for LGBTQ.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, Murthy focused his attention on chronic stress and emotional well-being as prevalent problems that have profound implications for health, productivity, and happiness. Partnering with the Veterans Health Administration, he led a  convening  that brought together leading thinkers, researchers, and practitioners to identify evidence-based approaches to cultivate emotional well-being thereby enhancing health, productivity, education and satisfaction.
</p><p>In addition to his role as America’s Doctor, as the Vice Admiral of the U.S. Public Health Service Commissioned Corps, Murthy commanded a uniformed service of 6,600 public health officers, serving the most under served and vulnerable populations in over 800 locations domestically and abroad. He worked with thousands of Commissioned Corps  officers  to protect the nation from Ebola and Zika and to respond to the Flint water crisis, major hurricanes, and frequent health care shortages in rural communities. He met with Corps officers across the country and worked to strengthen the ability of the Commissioned Corps to carry out its central mission to protect, promote, and advance the health and safety of the nation.
</p><p>On April 21, 2017, Murthy was relieved of his duties by the newly elected President Trump. His deputy Surgeon General Rear Admiral <a href="/wiki/Sylvia_Trent-Adams" title="Sylvia Trent-Adams">Sylvia Trent-Adams</a> was named acting Surgeon General.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivek_Murthy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Murthy is married to Dr. Alice Chen, an internal medicine physician who trained at Yale, Cornell and UCLA and was the former executive director of <a href="/wiki/Doctors_for_America" title="Doctors for America">Doctors for America</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PERS2015-1_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PERS2015-1-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PERS2015-2_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PERS2015-2-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
