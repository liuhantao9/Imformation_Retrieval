<p><b>Neo</b> was an early <a href="/wiki/New_wave_music" title="New wave music">new wave</a> band which was part of the English musical scene originated by punk in the 1970s.  The group was formed by the American-born singer <a href="/wiki/Ian_North" title="Ian North">Ian North</a>, who was the frontman and the only continuous member from the band formation in 1977 to the end in 1979.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neo_(British_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_days:_Radio">Early days: Radio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neo_(British_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early days: Radio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1976, Ian North (lead vocals, guitar<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) travelled with his band <a href="/wiki/Milk_%27N%27_Cookies" title="Milk &#39;N&#39; Cookies">Milk 'N' Cookies</a> to London to record an album for <a href="/wiki/Island_Records" title="Island Records">Island Records</a>. The record was recorded, but the release delayed, resulting in the group disbanding.
</p><p>North stayed in London, meeting <a href="/wiki/Brian_Eno" title="Brian Eno">Brian Eno</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Gordon" title="Martin Gordon">Martin Gordon</a> (bass), who a few years previously had founded, with ex-<a href="/wiki/John%27s_Children" title="John&#39;s Children">John's Children</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Andy_Ellison" title="Andy Ellison">Andy Ellison</a>, the glam band <a href="/wiki/Jet_(UK_band)" title="Jet (UK band)">Jet</a>. North created a new band called <b>Radio</b> and invited <a href="/wiki/Paul_Simon_(UK_musician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Paul Simon (UK musician)">Paul Simon</a> (drums), who formed part of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Limmie_Funk_Limited&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Limmie Funk Limited (page does not exist)">Limmie Funk Limited</a> (with <a href="/wiki/Limmie_Snell" title="Limmie Snell">Limmie Snell</a>) to join them and completed the line-up. The band was post-punk,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> but after only one gig, supporting <a href="/wiki/Ultravox" title="Ultravox">Ultravox</a>! in November 1976, Gordon dropped out. The band recruited <a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Dyner&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="George Dyner (page does not exist)">George Dyner</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> to replace Gordon. However, in April 1977, North took his place on bass and continuing singing, and Paul's brother <a href="/wiki/Robert_Simon_(guitarist)" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Simon (guitarist)">Robert</a> was introduced on guitar.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to Paul Simon, Radio (now credited as <b>Ian North's Radio</b>) recorded some songs in the studio, which he remastered in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In mid 1977, Paul Simon accompanied Martin Gordon, forming part of Radio Stars and recording with them "Good Personality", a 7-inch single, and other tracks, some of which were eventually released on Radio Stars' compilation <i>Somewhere There's A Place For Us</i>. He was asked to stay with them, but preferred continuing with North in Neo.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Neo">Neo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neo_(British_band)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Neo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a three piece band, Radio immediately transformed to Neo.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> North became the bassist, and in this way the group continued until February/March 1978, when Robert Simon dropped out and formed part of <a href="/wiki/Ultravox_(band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ultravox (band)">Ultravox</a>. North sacked Paul Simon,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and included session players in his project: ex-<a href="/w/index.php?title=ZZebra&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ZZebra (page does not exist)">ZZebra</a> members, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Byrd" title="Steve Byrd">Steve Byrd</a> on guitar and <a href="/wiki/John_McCoy_(musician)" title="John McCoy (musician)">John McCoy</a> on bass, and <a href="/wiki/Bryson_Graham" title="Bryson Graham">Bryson Graham</a> on drums. They recorded an album in <a href="/wiki/Ian_Gillan" title="Ian Gillan">Ian Gillan</a>'s studio, and after Gillan heard them, he hired Byrd and McCoy away from North. Gillan and the ex-Neo musicians formed <a href="/wiki/Gillan_(band)" title="Gillan (band)">Gillan</a>. Only Graham stayed with North.
</p><p>North renewed Neo again with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Steve_Wilkin_(guitarist)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Steve Wilkin (guitarist) (page does not exist)">Steve Wilkin</a> (ex-Wired and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Masterwitch&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Masterwitch (page does not exist)">Masterwitch</a>) on guitar, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_South&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nick South (page does not exist)">Nick South</a> on bass and Graham on drums, but shortly afterwards South and Graham were replaced by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mik_Sweeney&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mik Sweeney (page does not exist)">Mik Sweeney</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Derek_Quinton&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Derek Quinton (page does not exist)">Derek Quinton</a>, respectively. <a href="/wiki/Dan_Black" title="Dan Black">Dan Black</a> completed the line-up as keyboardist. That line-up toured with <a href="/wiki/Magazine_(band)" title="Magazine (band)">Magazine</a>, doing gigs promoting their single "Tran-Sister", released that year.
</p><p>After changes of line-ups, Neo split up in 1979 because North had to return to the US. Sweeney joined The News and, later, <a href="/wiki/Classix_Nouveaux" title="Classix Nouveaux">Classix Nouveaux</a>, Steve Wilkin joined <a href="/wiki/Random_Hold" title="Random Hold">Random Hold</a>, Quinton joined Sector 27, with <a href="/wiki/Tom_Robinson" title="Tom Robinson">Tom Robinson</a>, and Black formed <a href="/wiki/Psychic_TV" title="Psychic TV">Psychic TV</a>.
</p>
