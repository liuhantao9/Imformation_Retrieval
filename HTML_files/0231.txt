<p>A <b>stage name</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">pseudonym</a> used by <a href="/wiki/Performer" class="mw-redirect" title="Performer">performers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Entertainer" class="mw-redirect" title="Entertainer">entertainers</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Comedian" title="Comedian">comedians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singing" title="Singing">singers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Musician" title="Musician">musicians</a>. Such titles are adopted for a wide variety of reasons and may be similar or nearly identical to an individual's <a href="/wiki/Birth_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Birth name">birth name</a>. In some situations, a performer will eventually adopt their title as a <a href="/wiki/Legal_name" title="Legal name">legal name</a>, although this is often not the case. <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">Nicknames</a> are sometimes used as part of a person's professional name.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Motivation_to_use_a_stage_name">Motivation to use a stage name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stage_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Motivation to use a stage name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A performer will often take a stage name because their real name is considered unattractive, dull, or unintentionally amusing, is difficult to pronounce or spell, has been used by another notable individual, or projects an undesired image. Sometimes a performer adopts a name which is unusual or outlandish to attract attention. Other performers use a stage name in order to retain anonymity. The equivalent concept among <a href="/wiki/Writer" title="Writer">writers</a> is called a <a href="/wiki/Nom_de_plume" class="mw-redirect" title="Nom de plume">nom de plume</a> or pen name. In <a href="/wiki/Radio" title="Radio">radio</a>, the term "radio name" or "air name" is used.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_connection">Family connection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stage_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family connection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some individuals who are related to a celebrity take a different last name so they are not perceived to have received <a href="/wiki/Nepotism" title="Nepotism">undue advantage from their family connection</a>. Actor <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Cage" title="Nicolas Cage">Nicolas Cage</a>, born Nicolas Coppola, chose a new last name to avoid comparisons with his uncle, director <a href="/wiki/Francis_Ford_Coppola" title="Francis Ford Coppola">Francis Ford Coppola</a>, who gave him his big break in the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Peggy_Sue_Got_Married" title="Peggy Sue Got Married">Peggy Sue Got Married</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> 
</p><p>Conversely, individuals who wish to receive benefit from their family connections may take that person's first or last name. <a href="/wiki/Lon_Chaney_Sr." class="mw-redirect" title="Lon Chaney Sr.">Lon Chaney Sr.</a>’s son Creighton spent a number of years appearing in minor roles before renaming himself <a href="/wiki/Lon_Chaney,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Lon Chaney, Jr.">Lon Chaney, Jr.</a> <a href="/wiki/Emilio_Estevez" title="Emilio Estevez">Emilio Estevez</a> and his sister <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9e_Estevez" title="Renée Estevez">Renee</a> chose not to take their father <a href="/wiki/Martin_Sheen" title="Martin Sheen">Martin Sheen</a>’s professional name and use their birth names; however, their brother Carlos chose to use their father's professional name, and took the name <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Sheen" title="Charlie Sheen">Charlie Sheen</a>. 
</p><p>Women who achieve fame after marriage often use their married name as part of their professional name, while women who achieved fame before marriage may continue to use their maiden name or a <a href="/wiki/Hyphenated_surname" class="mw-redirect" title="Hyphenated surname">Hyphenated surname</a>.
</p><p>In some cases, the individual may adopt a stage name to avoid confusion with other family members who have similar names. Actor <a href="/wiki/Mark_Harmon" title="Mark Harmon">Mark Harmon</a> (Thomas Mark Harmon) uses his middle name professionally to avoid confusion with his father <a href="/wiki/Heisman_Trophy" title="Heisman Trophy">Heisman Trophy</a> winner and former broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Tom_Harmon" title="Tom Harmon">Tom Harmon</a> (Thomas Dudley Harmon).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guild_and_association_rules">Guild and association rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stage_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Guild and association rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Guilds and associations that represent actors, such as the <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild" title="Screen Actors Guild">Screen Actors Guild</a> (SAG) in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Actors%27_Equity_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="British Actors&#39; Equity Association">British Actors' Equity Association</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, stipulate that no two members may have identical working names. An actor whose name has already been taken must choose a new name. 
</p><p>Notable examples include: <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Lane" title="Nathan Lane">Nathan Lane</a>, whose birth name (Joseph Lane) was already in use; <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Granger" title="Stewart Granger">Stewart Granger</a>, born James Stewart; and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Keaton" title="Michael Keaton">Michael Keaton</a>, born Michael Douglas.  <a href="/wiki/Diane_Keaton" title="Diane Keaton">Diane Keaton</a>, whose birth name is Diane Hall, took her mother's maiden name as a stage name after learning that there was already a registered actress named Diane Hall in the <a href="/wiki/Actors%27_Equity_Association" title="Actors&#39; Equity Association">Actors' Equity Association</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/Ugly_Betty" title="Ugly Betty">Ugly Betty</a></i> actress <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_L._Williams" class="mw-redirect" title="Vanessa L. Williams">Vanessa Williams</a> officially uses "Vanessa L. Williams" due to SAG guidelines, although the other actress with same first and last name (<a href="/wiki/Vanessa_A._Williams" title="Vanessa A. Williams">Vanessa A. Williams</a>) is arguably less notable. Similarly, <a href="/wiki/David_Walliams" title="David Walliams">David Walliams</a> changed one letter in his surname due to there being another "David Williams". <a href="/wiki/Terry_O%27Quinn" title="Terry O&#39;Quinn">Terry O'Quinn</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i> fame changed his surname from Quinn to O'Quinn as another registered actor already had the name Terrance Quinn.  Long-time <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">Simpsons</a></i> writer and <i><a href="/wiki/Futurama" title="Futurama">Futurama</a></i> executive producer <a href="/wiki/David_X._Cohen" title="David X. Cohen">David X. Cohen</a> changed his middle initial from S to X because there was already a David S. Cohen registered with the Writer's Guild of America. 
</p><p>(The rumor that Michael Keaton changed his surname because of an attraction to actress <a href="/wiki/Diane_Keaton" title="Diane Keaton">Diane Keaton</a> is incorrect;<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> he chose Keaton because of an affinity for the <a href="/wiki/Physical_comedy" title="Physical comedy">physical comedy</a> of <a href="/wiki/Buster_Keaton" title="Buster Keaton">Buster Keaton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>)
</p><p>A middle name may be adopted in preference to changing a name. In some cases, attaching a <a href="/wiki/Suffix_(name)" title="Suffix (name)">generational suffix</a> is sufficient for guild rules; broadcaster <a href="/wiki/David_H._Lawrence_XVII" title="David H. Lawrence XVII">David Lawrence</a> is credited as David H. Lawrence XVII as a result of there being sixteen other David Lawrences in show business at the time he received his SAG card.
</p><p>A person hoping to become successful as an <a href="/wiki/Entertainer" class="mw-redirect" title="Entertainer">entertainer</a> who has a name identical to a name already familiar to the public (in any field of endeavor) may change his/her name in order to avoid having his/her name evoke the other person with the same name. Singer <a href="/wiki/Katy_Perry" title="Katy Perry">Katy Perry</a>, born Katheryn Elizabeth Hudson, released her <a href="/wiki/Katy_Hudson_(album)" title="Katy Hudson (album)">self-titled album</a> under the name Katy Hudson, but later used her mother's maiden name to avoid confusion with actress <a href="/wiki/Kate_Hudson" title="Kate Hudson">Kate Hudson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Involuntary_name_changes">Involuntary name changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stage_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Involuntary name changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A performer may also have had their stage name chosen for them by their agent &#8211; such was the case with <a href="/wiki/Barbara_Eden" title="Barbara Eden">Barbara Eden</a>, born Barbara Jean Huffman<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Barbara_Eden" title="Barbara Eden"><span title="Her biography says she was born Barbara Jean Morehead (March 2018)">contradictory</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> &#8211; or, in the heyday of the Hollywood studios, by a movie studio. <a href="/wiki/Joan_Rivers" title="Joan Rivers">Joan Rivers</a> (born Joan Alexandra Molinsky) went one step further and named herself after a former agent, Tony Rivers, after he told her to change her name. In the non English-speaking world, an example is the Taiwanese <a href="/wiki/Mandopop" title="Mandopop">Mandopop</a> girl group <a href="/wiki/S.H.E" title="S.H.E">S.H.E</a> (composed of <a href="/wiki/Selina_Jen" title="Selina Jen">Selina Jen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hebe_Tian" class="mw-redirect" title="Hebe Tian">Hebe Tian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ella_Chen" title="Ella Chen">Ella Chen</a>), whose members' English names were chosen by their manager after taking <a href="/wiki/Personality_test" title="Personality test">personality tests</a>.
</p><p>Former child star <a href="/wiki/Patty_Duke" title="Patty Duke">Patty Duke</a> (whose real name is Anna Marie Duke) had her stage name chosen for her by her first managers. </p><p><a href="/wiki/Cary_Grant" title="Cary Grant">Cary Grant</a> (born Archibald Leach) had his name selected for him by <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a>. He had been using the name "Cary Lockwood", but the studio decided against it, deeming it too similar to another actor working at the time. Cary and the studio eventually settled on "Cary Grant" (Grant thought the letters "C" and "G" to be lucky: they had brought previous success for both <a href="/wiki/Clark_Gable" title="Clark Gable">Clark Gable</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gary_Cooper" title="Gary Cooper">Gary Cooper</a>). <a href="/wiki/Joan_Crawford" title="Joan Crawford">Joan Crawford</a>, born Lucille Fay LeSueur,  had her name changed as a result of a magazine poll organised by her studio, <a href="/wiki/Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer" title="Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer">MGM</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ethnicity">Ethnicity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stage_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ethnicity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the past, a stage name was often used when a performer's real name was considered to denote a specific ethnicity that faced potential discrimination. In other cases, actors have reinvented themselves with a more ethnic identity, when that gave them an advantage in playing "ethnic" roles.<sup id="cite_ref-Picayune_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Picayune-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Steven_Tyler" title="Steven Tyler">Steven Tyler</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">Aerosmith</a> changed his name from Stephen Victor Tallarico "for more promotional appeal".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Historically, <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a> were encouraged to <a href="/wiki/Anglicize" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglicize">anglicize</a> their names to avoid possible discrimination. Examples of such name changes are <a href="/wiki/Danny_Kaye" title="Danny Kaye">Danny Kaye</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mel_Brooks" title="Mel Brooks">Mel Brooks</a>, both of whom were born with the surname Kaminsky, and <a href="/wiki/Woody_Allen" title="Woody Allen">Woody Allen</a> (born Allen Konigsberg). <a href="/wiki/Jon_Stewart" title="Jon Stewart">Jon Stewart</a> did not anglicize his name for career reasons, but because of his estranged relationship with his father.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Israeli-American <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Portman" title="Natalie Portman">Natalie Portman</a> changed her name to protect her privacy.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ramón Estévez changed his name to <a href="/wiki/Martin_Sheen" title="Martin Sheen">Martin Sheen</a> as he felt it affected his job prospects due to racial discrimination and bias, although he maintains his birth name for legal documents such as his passport;<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> his sons made divergent choices: Carlos Irwin Estévez is now <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Sheen" title="Charlie Sheen">Charlie Sheen</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Emilio_Estevez" title="Emilio Estevez">Emilio Estevez</a> left his name unchanged. German-born actor Hans Gudegast adopted the stage name of <a href="/wiki/Eric_Braeden" title="Eric Braeden">Eric Braeden</a>.
</p><p>Actors <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Quinn" title="Anthony Quinn">Anthony Quinn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anne_Bancroft" title="Anne Bancroft">Anne Bancroft</a> were advised to anglicize their names because 'Antonio Rodolfo Quinn Oaxaca' and 'Anna Maria Louisa Italiano', respectively, were considered too 'ethnic' for Hollywood and Broadway at the time. <a href="/wiki/Eydie_Gorme" class="mw-redirect" title="Eydie Gorme">Eydie Gorme</a> (born Edith Garmezano), <a href="/wiki/Sophia_Loren" title="Sophia Loren">Sophia Loren</a> (born Sofia Villani Scicolone), <a href="/wiki/Charles_Bronson" title="Charles Bronson">Charles Bronson</a> (born Charles Dennis Buchinsky), and <a href="/wiki/Rita_Hayworth" title="Rita Hayworth">Rita Hayworth</a> (born Margarita Carmen Cansino), are four more well-known examples of this trend.
</p><p>Broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Dave_Roberts_(broadcaster)" title="Dave Roberts (broadcaster)">Dave Roberts</a> was born David T. Boreanaz but was known professionally as Dave Thomas and later Dave Roberts as ethnic surnames were discouraged when he first began his career during the 1950s; his son, actor <a href="/wiki/David_Boreanaz" title="David Boreanaz">David Boreanaz</a>, chose not to adopt a stage name.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The use of stage names for ethnic purposes may vary widely depending on the <a href="/wiki/Media_market" title="Media market">media market</a> the personality is representing. For example, in <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">Buffalo, New York</a>, a city with a large <a href="/wiki/Polish-American" class="mw-redirect" title="Polish-American">Polish-American</a> population, Polish-American media personalities typically work freely using their birth names.
</p><p>Sicilian-American actor Espera Oscar de Corti, who built his film career portraying <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans</a>, reinvented himself as <a href="/wiki/Iron_Eyes_Cody" title="Iron Eyes Cody">Iron Eyes Cody</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Picayune_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Picayune-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He not only took his stage name as his legal name, but eventually began <a href="/wiki/Passing_(racial_identity)#Passing_as_indigenous_Americans" title="Passing (racial identity)">insisting that he actually was Native American</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Picayune_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Picayune-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Chloe_Bennet" title="Chloe Bennet">Chloe Bennet</a> had used her birth name, Chloe Wang, for her singing career in China. She started using the surname Bennet, after her father's first name, when she failed to be welcomed by Hollywood agents.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ease_of_use">Ease of use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stage_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Ease of use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Another consideration in choosing a stage name is ease of use. The <a href="/wiki/Actors%27_Equity_Association" title="Actors&#39; Equity Association">Actors' Equity Association</a> (AEA) advises performers to select a name that is easy for others to pronounce, spell, and remember. Some performers, while paying great attention to their skills and abilities, give little thought to the difference that a well-thought-out name can make to their career. Often a person or group decides on a different name only after they realize that a poorly chosen name gives a bad impression.
</p><p>Actor <a href="/wiki/Michael_Caine" title="Michael Caine">Michael Caine</a> was born Maurice Micklewhite and chose the name "Michael" because he preferred the sound of it to the less glamorous-sounding "Maurice".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> He reputedly chose the name "Caine" because at that precise instant he saw a cinema marquee for the then-current movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Caine_Mutiny_(film)" title="The Caine Mutiny (film)">The Caine Mutiny</a></i>, and thought that it would make a good last name in conjunction with Michael. ("Had I looked the other direction," he later quipped, "I'd be known as Michael <a href="/wiki/One_Hundred_and_One_Dalmatians" title="One Hundred and One Dalmatians">The One Hundred and One Dalmatians</a>.")<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Similarly, actor <a href="/wiki/Pete_Postlethwaite" title="Pete Postlethwaite">Pete Postlethwaite</a> was advised to adopt a different surname by peers who quipped that his "would never be put <a href="/wiki/Marquee_(sign)" class="mw-redirect" title="Marquee (sign)">up in lights</a> outside theaters because they couldn't afford the electricity" (Postlethwaite rejected the advice).<sup id="cite_ref-Postlethwaitename_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Postlethwaitename-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Conversely, Doris Kappelhoff heeded the suggestion of a bandleader who said that her name would never fit on the marquee; since "Day By Day" had become a signature song for her, she became known to the public as <a href="/wiki/Doris_Day" title="Doris Day">Doris Day</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relevance_to_image">Relevance to image</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stage_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Relevance to image">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ching_Lau_Lauro" title="Ching Lau Lauro">Ching Lau Lauro</a> (flourished 1827–1839) used a Chinese stage name to represent his stage image as a <a href="/wiki/Contortion" title="Contortion">contortionist</a> in Chinese costume. He was probably the first magician to dress in Chinese costume on stage.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Commonly in the music world, and especially those of <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Punk_rock" title="Punk rock">punk rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Industrial_music" title="Industrial music">industrial</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a>, musicians will rename themselves with names more menacing or striking than their birth names. Every member of the punk band <a href="/wiki/Ramones" title="Ramones">Ramones</a> took the pseudonymous "Ramone" surname as part of their collective stage persona. Members of New Zealand art-rock band <a href="/wiki/Split_Enz" title="Split Enz">Split Enz</a> all took their middle names as stage names, so as to keep their private image separate from their public personae.
</p><p>Other performers may assume stage names as a means of distancing themselves from publicly known childhood names that could be considered professionally embarrassing, outlandish, or otherwise inappropriate.  Film director <a href="/wiki/Duncan_Jones" title="Duncan Jones">Duncan Jones</a> (son of singer <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>) was known publicly as a child as Zowie Bowie.
</p><p>Hip-hop artists are known to use stage names, such as <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">Jay-Z</a> (born Shawn Carter), <a href="/wiki/50_Cent" title="50 Cent">50 Cent</a> (born Curtis Jackson), <a href="/wiki/Sean_Combs" title="Sean Combs">Diddy</a> (born Sean Combs), <a href="/wiki/Ludacris" title="Ludacris">Ludacris</a> (born Chris Bridges), <a href="/wiki/Lil_Wayne" title="Lil Wayne">Lil Wayne</a> (born Dwayne Carter Jr.), and <a href="/wiki/Soulja_Boy" title="Soulja Boy">Soulja Boy</a> (born DeAndre Way). At times, these artists will use their real names to make promoted material seem more authentic or personal. <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> has used his real name, Marshall Mathers, in various public events and as an <a href="/wiki/Alter_ego" title="Alter ego">alter ego</a> as his real name gained recognition following the release of his multi-platinum album, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Marshall_Mathers_LP" title="The Marshall Mathers LP">The Marshall Mathers LP</a></i>.  Similarly, <a href="/wiki/LL_Cool_J" title="LL Cool J">LL Cool J</a> released the albums <i><a href="/wiki/Mr._Smith_(album)" title="Mr. Smith (album)">Mr. Smith</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Todd_Smith_(album)" title="Todd Smith (album)">Todd Smith</a></i> in 1995 and 2006, respectively, under his real name Todd Smith. Rapper-singer-actress <a href="/wiki/Queen_Latifah" title="Queen Latifah">Queen Latifah</a> released <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dana_Owens_Album" title="The Dana Owens Album">The Dana Owens Album</a></i> under her own given name, Dana Owens, after changing her musical focus from hip-hop to vocal jazz. Rapper and sometime actor <a href="/wiki/Xzibit" title="Xzibit">Xzibit</a> has been credited by his real name Alvin Joiner in several television shows.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Euphony_and_ease_of_remembrance">Euphony and ease of remembrance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stage_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Euphony and ease of remembrance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some performers and artists may choose to simplify their name to make it easier to spell and pronounce, and easier for others to remember. For instance, <a href="/wiki/Fall_Out_Boy" title="Fall Out Boy">Fall Out Boy</a> vocalist and guitarist <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Stump" title="Patrick Stump">Patrick Stump</a> removed the "h" from his original name, Stumph. It was still pronounced "stump," but the change ensured his audience wouldn't think to pronounce it "stumf."  Singer <a href="/wiki/Jason_Derulo" title="Jason Derulo">Jason Derulo</a> uses the phonetic spelling of his given name, Jason Desrouleaux.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Andy_Warhol" title="Andy Warhol">Andy Warhol</a> dropped an "a" from his original name, Warhola, while couturier <a href="/wiki/Yves_Saint_Laurent_(designer)" title="Yves Saint Laurent (designer)">Yves Mathieu-Saint-Laurent</a> dropped the first of his two surnames. Rodolfo Alfonso Raffaello Piero Filiberto Guglielmi adopted the stage name <a href="/wiki/Rudolph_Valentino" title="Rudolph Valentino">Rudolph Valentino</a> in part because American casting directors found his original surname difficult to pronounce. Singer <a href="/wiki/George_Michael" title="George Michael">George Michael</a> (the son of a Greek Cypriot restaurateur in North London) was born Georgios Kyriacos Panayiotou.
</p><p>Some surnames may carry unfortunate connotations. <a href="/wiki/Hal_Linden" title="Hal Linden">Hal Linden</a>, born Harold Lipshitz, adopted his stage name for fear that the embedded obscenity in his original surname could cost him work. <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Lauren" title="Ralph Lauren">Ralph Lauren</a>'s brother (who was his guardian) changed their family name from Lifshitz for a similar reason: fear of mockery.  <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nick_Rhodes" title="Nick Rhodes">Nick Rhodes</a>, born Nicholas James Bates, changed his name to escape childhood ridicule (as a child, children would often tease him by calling him "<a href="/wiki/Masturbation" title="Masturbation">Master Bates</a>"; he adopted the surname "Rhodes" after a brand of <a href="/wiki/Synthesizer" title="Synthesizer">keyboard</a>). <a href="/wiki/Diana_Dors" title="Diana Dors">Diana Dors</a> was born Diana Fluck (one letter away from <a href="/wiki/Fuck" title="Fuck">a profanity</a>); this prompted her to change her name to a more benign one in order to avoid an accidental obscenity (which could have been exacerbated by her status as a <a href="/wiki/Sex_symbol" title="Sex symbol">sex symbol</a>).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_use">Musical use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stage_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Musical use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some types of music are more associated with stage names than others. For example, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Electronic_dance_music" title="Electronic dance music">EDM</a> artists almost always use stage names, whereas "classical" composers and performers virtually never do.  Classical violinist <a href="/wiki/Amad%C3%A9us_Leopold" title="Amadéus Leopold">Amadéus Leopold</a> (born in South Korea as Yoo Hanbin) and opera singers <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Sills" title="Beverly Sills">Beverly Sills</a> (born Belle Miriam Silverman) and <a href="/wiki/Nellie_Melba" title="Nellie Melba">Nellie Melba</a> (born Helen Porter Mitchell) are rare exceptions.
</p><p>Some Algerian raï musicians use the prefix <a href="/wiki/Cheb_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Cheb (disambiguation)">Cheb</a> (for men) or Chaba (Chebba) for women. Both <a href="/wiki/Arabic" title="Arabic">Arabic</a> words mean "young" (e.g. as in <a href="/wiki/Khaled_(musician)" title="Khaled (musician)">Cheb Khaled</a>, or "Young Khaled").
</p><p>Some performers take a series of different stage names. The British pop singer successful in the 1970s as <a href="/wiki/Alvin_Stardust" title="Alvin Stardust">Alvin Stardust</a> previously went by the stage name of <i>Shane Fenton</i> in the 1960s. He had been born <i>Bernard William Jewry</i>. Some performers will use different names in different settings. Charles Thompson, singer-songwriter for the alternative band the <a href="/wiki/Pixies_(band)" title="Pixies (band)">Pixies</a>, was known in that band as <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Francis" title="Black Francis">Black Francis</a></i>. He was called <i>Frank Black</i> as a solo performer, and again called <i>Black Francis</i> in a reunited Pixies.
</p><p>Many performers refer to their stage name as their "professional name". In some cases, performers subsequently adopt their stage name as their legal name. For instance, the former Robert Allen Zimmerman's legal name has been Robert Dylan (<a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">Bob Dylan</a>) since he changed it in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> <a href="/wiki/New_York_Supreme_Court" title="New York Supreme Court">Supreme Court</a> in August 1962.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a> was born Reginald Kenneth Dwight but changed his name by <a href="/wiki/Deed_poll" title="Deed poll">deed poll</a>, making Elton Hercules John his real name. When he was <a href="/wiki/Knighting" class="mw-redirect" title="Knighting">knighted</a>, he became Sir Elton Hercules John rather than Sir Reginald Kenneth Dwight. <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Costello" title="Elvis Costello">Elvis Costello</a> (born Declan MacManus), who had adopted his professional name as a legal name, changed it back to his birth name in 1986. Another example is Marvin Lee Aday, known by his stage name <a href="/wiki/Meat_Loaf" title="Meat Loaf">Meat Loaf</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In a similar way, actress and singer <a href="/wiki/Miley_Cyrus" title="Miley Cyrus">Miley Cyrus</a> was born Destiny Hope Cyrus but found "Miley" more comfortable, making it her legal name.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Entire musical groups have been known to adopt a common stage <a href="/wiki/Surname" title="Surname">surname</a>, the most notable arguably being the <a href="/wiki/Ramones" title="Ramones">Ramones</a>. Recent examples include <a href="/wiki/Those_Darlins" title="Those Darlins">Those Darlins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Los_Campesinos!" title="Los Campesinos!">Los Campesinos!</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ween" title="Ween">Ween</a>.
</p>
