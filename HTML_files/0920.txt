<p>The <b>Kennedy Compound</b> consists of three houses on six <a href="/wiki/Acre" title="Acre">acres</a> (24,000 m²) of waterfront property on <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">Cape Cod</a> along <a href="/wiki/Nantucket_Sound" title="Nantucket Sound">Nantucket Sound</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hyannis_Port,_Massachusetts" title="Hyannis Port, Massachusetts">Hyannis Port</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, in the United States. It was once the home of an American businessman, investor, politician, and U.S. <a href="/wiki/Ambassador" title="Ambassador">ambassador</a> to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr." title="Joseph P. Kennedy Sr.">Joseph P. Kennedy</a>; his wife, <a href="/wiki/Rose_Kennedy" title="Rose Kennedy">Rose</a>; and their children, including U.S. President <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> and U.S. Senators <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert F. Kennedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edward_M._Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward M. Kennedy">Edward M. Kennedy</a>. As an adult, the youngest son, Edward, lived in his parents' house, and it was his primary residence from 1982 until he died of brain cancer at the compound, in August 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>John F. Kennedy used the compound as a base for his <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_presidential_campaign,_1960" class="mw-redirect" title="John F. Kennedy presidential campaign, 1960">successful 1960 U.S. presidential campaign</a> and later as a <a href="/wiki/Summer_White_Houses" class="mw-redirect" title="Summer White Houses">summer White House</a> and presidential retreat, until <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_John_F._Kennedy" title="Assassination of John F. Kennedy">his assassination</a>, in November 1963.  In 2012, the main house was donated to the <a href="/wiki/Edward_M._Kennedy_Institute_for_the_United_States_Senate" title="Edward M. Kennedy Institute for the United States Senate">Edward M. Kennedy Institute for the United States Senate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However, it was reported in 2019 that Robert Kennedy's widow Ethel was living there.<sup id="cite_ref-compoundrevealed_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-compoundrevealed-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kennedy_Compound&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1926 Joseph P. Kennedy rented a summer <a href="/wiki/Cottage" title="Cottage">cottage</a> at 50 Marchant Avenue in Hyannis Port. Two years later, he purchased the structure, which had been erected in 1904, and enlarged and remodeled it to suit his growing family's needs. In and around this house, their nine children spent their summers, acquiring a lifelong interest in <a href="/wiki/Sailing" title="Sailing">sailing</a> and other competitive activities.
</p><p>In 1956, John bought a smaller home of his own at 111 Irving Avenue,<sup>(<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Kennedy_Compound&amp;params=41.6308_N_70.3035_W_type:landmark_scale:5000"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">41°37′51″N</span> <span class="longitude">70°18′13″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">41.6308°N 70.3035°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">41.6308; -70.3035</span></span></span></a></span>)</sup> not far from his father's home. Subsequently, Edward acquired the residence at 28 Marchant Avenue <sup>(<span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Kennedy_Compound&amp;params=41.63_N_70.303_W_type:landmark_scale:5000"><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">41°37′48″N</span> <span class="longitude">70°18′11″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">41.63°N 70.303°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">41.63; -70.303</span></span></span></a></span>)</sup> adjacent to the other two in 1959 and sold it to Robert and his wife <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Kennedy" title="Ethel Kennedy">Ethel</a> in 1961. Edward lived in the main house at the compound until his death.<sup id="cite_ref-Cape_Cod_Times_-_Berry_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cape_Cod_Times_-_Berry-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_residence">Current residence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kennedy_Compound&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current residence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2019, Robert Kennedy's granddaughter, Saoirse Kennedy Hill, died in a residence at the compound, where her grandmother Ethel Kennedy lives.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Layout">Layout</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kennedy_Compound&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Layout">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All three buildings are white-frame <a href="/wiki/Clapboard_(architecture)" title="Clapboard (architecture)">clapboard</a> structures typical of vacation residences on Cape Cod. Except for specific occasions at the Main House, the buildings are not available for public visitation.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_house">Main house</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kennedy_Compound&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Main house">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Joseph's home, the Main House and the largest of the three, is surrounded by well-tended <a href="/wiki/Lawn" title="Lawn">lawns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Garden" title="Garden">gardens</a> and it commands sweeping views of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">ocean</a> from its long porches.<sup id="cite_ref-nps.gov_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nps.gov-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the main floor are a living room, dining room, sun room, <a href="/wiki/Family_room" title="Family room">television room</a>, the bedroom that John used before he purchased his own house in the compound, the kitchen, and various <a href="/wiki/Pantry" title="Pantry">pantries</a> and <a href="/wiki/Utility_room" title="Utility room">utility rooms</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nps.gov_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nps.gov-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the second floor are six bedrooms, a sewing room, packing room, and four servants' bedrooms. The house has a full <a href="/wiki/Attic" title="Attic">attic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nps.gov_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nps.gov-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Basement" title="Basement">basement</a> contains a <a href="/wiki/Movie_theater" title="Movie theater">motion-picture theater</a> and a hall covered with dolls from all around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-nps.gov_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nps.gov-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> A wine cellar designed after a ship's <a href="/wiki/Hull_(watercraft)" title="Hull (watercraft)">hull</a> and a sipping room<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (August 2009)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  – one of the Kennedy family's favorite hideouts.<sup id="cite_ref-nps.gov_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nps.gov-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The house has changed little, either structurally or in furnishings, since President Kennedy's association with it.
</p><p>In 2012, the main house was donated by the Kennedy family to the <a href="/wiki/Edward_M._Kennedy_Institute_for_the_United_States_Senate" title="Edward M. Kennedy Institute for the United States Senate">Edward M. Kennedy Institute for the United States Senate</a>. On the grounds are an enclosed <a href="/wiki/Swimming_pool" title="Swimming pool">swimming pool</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tennis_court" title="Tennis court">tennis court</a>, a four-car <a href="/wiki/Garage_(house)" class="mw-redirect" title="Garage (house)">garage</a>, and two <a href="/wiki/Guest_house_(lodging)" class="mw-redirect" title="Guest house (lodging)">guest houses</a>.
</p><p>There are two circular driveways with <a href="/wiki/Flagpole" class="mw-redirect" title="Flagpole">flagpoles</a> standing in the middle, a <a href="/wiki/Boathouse" title="Boathouse">boathouse</a> and several large stretches of lawn area where many of the family <a href="/wiki/Touch_football_(American)" title="Touch football (American)">touch football</a> games were played.
</p><p>Other parcels of land that assorted members of the family have purchased remain as well-tended as those of the more prominent homes.
</p>
