<p>The <b>American Defense Service Medal</b> was a <a href="/wiki/United_States_service_medals_of_the_World_Wars" title="United States service medals of the World Wars">military award</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">United States Armed Forces</a>, established by <a href="/wiki/Executive_order_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Executive order (United States)">Executive Order</a> <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Executive_Order_8808" class="extiw" title="s:Executive Order 8808">8808</a>, by President <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a>, on June 28, 1941.<sup id="cite_ref-American_Defense_Service_Medal_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-American_Defense_Service_Medal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ADSM_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADSM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The medal was intended to recognize those military service members who had served on active duty between September 8, 1939 and December 7, 1941.
</p><p>A similar medal, known as the <a href="/wiki/American_Campaign_Medal" title="American Campaign Medal">American Campaign Medal</a>, was established in 1942, for service in the <a href="/wiki/American_Theater_(World_War_II)" title="American Theater (World War II)">American Theater</a> during the World War II era.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Defense_Service_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The American Defense Service Medal was established by Executive Order 8808, on 28 June 1941, by President Franklin D. Roosevelt and announced in War Department Bulletin 17, 1941. The criteria for the medal was announced in Department of the Army Circular 44, on 13 February 1942. The <a href="/wiki/Service_ribbon" title="Service ribbon">service ribbon</a> design was approved by the Secretary of War and the Secretary of Navy on January 7, 1942. The medal was designed by Mr. Lee Lawrie, a civilian sculptor from Easton, Maryland. The model was approved by the Commission of Fine Arts on May 5, 1942.<sup id="cite_ref-ADSM_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADSM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ADSM_Fact_Sheet_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADSM_Fact_Sheet-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria">Criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Defense_Service_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The medal is authorized to military members who served on active duty between President Roosevelt's declaration of a limited national emergency on September 8, 1939 and the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">attack on Pearl Harbor</a> on December 7, 1941.  Members of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a>, to include those in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Reserve" title="United States Army Reserve">Organized Reserve</a> and <a href="/wiki/Army_National_Guard" title="Army National Guard">National Guard</a>, received this medal for any length of service during the eligibility period, provided that they were on orders to active duty for a period of twelve months or longer.<sup id="cite_ref-American_Defense_Service_Medal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-American_Defense_Service_Medal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a> excluded those reservists who were on active duty for less than ten days during the eligibility period, but otherwise the Navy, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> awarded the medal to all personnel who served on active duty at any time during the eligibility period, Regular or Reservist, provided they passed their initial physical examinations, such as in the case of those Reservists called back to extended active duty prior to the December 7, 1941 attack on Pearl Harbor, or those enlisted recruits and officer candidates accessed during the same period.<sup id="cite_ref-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearance">Appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Defense_Service_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bronze medal is <span class="frac nowrap">1<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>4</sub></span> inches (32&#160;mm) in diameter. On the <a href="/wiki/Obverse" class="mw-redirect" title="Obverse">obverse</a> is a female <a href="/wiki/Grecian" class="mw-redirect" title="Grecian">Grecian</a> figure symbolic of defense, holding in her sinister hand an ancient war shield in reverse and her <a href="/wiki/Dexter_and_sinister" title="Dexter and sinister">dexter</a> hand brandishing a sword above her head, and standing upon a conventionalized oak branch with four leaves. Around the top is the lettering "AMERICAN DEFENSE". The <a href="/wiki/Obverse_and_reverse" title="Obverse and reverse">reverse</a> is the wording "FOR SERVICE DURING THE LIMITED EMERGENCY PROCLAIMED BY THE PRESIDENT ON SEPTEMBER 8, 1939 OR DURING THE UNLIMITED EMERGENCY PROCLAIMED BY THE PRESIDENT ON MAY 27, 1941" above a seven-leafed spray of laurel.<sup id="cite_ref-ADSM_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADSM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ADSM_Fact_Sheet_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADSM_Fact_Sheet-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The suspension and <a href="/wiki/Service_ribbon" title="Service ribbon">service ribbon</a> of the medal is <span class="frac nowrap">1<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span> inches (35&#160;mm) wide and consists of the following stripes: <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>16</sub></span>&#160;in (4.8&#160;mm) Golden Yellow 67104; <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span>&#160;in (3.2&#160;mm) triparted Old Glory Blue 67178; White 67101; and Scarlet 67111; center <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>4</sub></span>&#160;in (19&#160;mm) Golden Yellow; <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span>&#160;in (3.2&#160;mm) triparted Scarlet; White; and Old Glory Blue 67178; and <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>16</sub></span>&#160;in (4.8&#160;mm) Golden yellow. The golden yellow color was symbolic of the golden opportunity of the youth of the United States to serve the National colors, represented by the blue, white and red pin stripes on each side.<sup id="cite_ref-ADSM_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADSM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ADSM_Fact_Sheet_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADSM_Fact_Sheet-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Devices">Devices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Defense_Service_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Devices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The American Defense Service Medal was authorized with the following devices:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Campaign_clasp" class="mw-redirect" title="Campaign clasp">Foreign Service Clasp</a>: Issued by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> for military service outside the continental limits of the United States, including service in Alaska. The foreign service clasp is a bronze bar <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span> inch (3.2&#160;mm) in width and <span class="frac nowrap">1<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>2</sub></span>&#160;in (38&#160;mm) in length with the words <i>FOREIGN SERVICE</i>, with a star at each end of the inscription.<sup id="cite_ref-American_Defense_Service_Medal_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-American_Defense_Service_Medal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Campaign_clasp" class="mw-redirect" title="Campaign clasp">Base Clasp</a>: Issued by the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Navy">U.S. Navy</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">United States Marine Corps</a> for service outside the continental limits of the United States (service in either Alaska or Hawaii qualified).<sup id="cite_ref-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Campaign_clasp" class="mw-redirect" title="Campaign clasp">Fleet Clasp</a>: Issued by the Navy, Marine Corps and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a> for service on the high seas while regularly attached to any vessels of the Atlantic, Pacific, or Asiatic fleets as well as vessels of the Naval Transport Service and vessels operating directly under the Chief of Naval Operations.<sup id="cite_ref-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Fleet clasp is a bronze bar <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span> inch (3.2&#160;mm) in width and <span class="frac nowrap">1<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>2</sub></span>&#160;in (38&#160;mm) in length with the words <i>FLEET</i> inscribed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Campaign_clasp" class="mw-redirect" title="Campaign clasp">Sea Clasp</a>: Issued by the Coast Guard for all other vessels and aircraft, not qualifying for the Fleet Clasp, which regularly conducted patrols at sea.<sup id="cite_ref-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/%22A%22_Device" title="&quot;A&quot; Device">"A" Device</a>: Awarded to any member of the Navy who served duty in actual or potential belligerent contact with <a href="/wiki/Axis_Powers" class="mw-redirect" title="Axis Powers">Axis Powers</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> between June 22 and December 7, 1941.<sup id="cite_ref-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  The "A" Device was also worn on the medal's uniform ribbon.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Service_star" title="Service star">Service star</a>: Worn in lieu of clasps when wearing the American Defense Service Medal as a ribbon on a military uniform.<sup id="cite_ref-American_Defense_Service_Medal_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-American_Defense_Service_Medal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ADSM_Fact_Sheet_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADSM_Fact_Sheet-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Navy_American_Defense_Service_Medal-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
