<p><b>Belém</b> (<small>Brazilian Portuguese:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Portuguese" title="Help:IPA/Portuguese">[beˈlẽj]</a></span>;<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> for <b><a href="/wiki/Bethlehem" title="Bethlehem">Bethlehem</a></b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> <a href="/wiki/Cities_of_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="Cities of Brazil">city</a> with 2,491,052 people residing in its Metropolitan Region. The capital city itself has 1,485,732 inhabitants (for more details on its population see in Demographics below). It is the capital and largest city of the state of <a href="/wiki/Par%C3%A1" title="Pará">Pará</a> in the country's <a href="/wiki/North_Region,_Brazil" title="North Region, Brazil">north</a>. It is the gateway to the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_River" title="Amazon River">Amazon River</a> with a busy <a href="/wiki/Port" title="Port">port</a>, airport, and bus/coach station. Belém lies approximately 100&#160;km upriver from the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/Par%C3%A1_River" title="Pará River">Pará River</a>, which is part of the greater Amazon River system, separated from the larger part of the Amazon delta by <i>Ilha de Marajó</i> (<a href="/wiki/Maraj%C3%B3" title="Marajó">Marajo Island</a>). With an estimated population of 1,439,561 people — or 2,249,405, considering its metropolitan area — it is the <a href="/wiki/List_of_largest_cities_in_Brazil" title="List of largest cities in Brazil">11th most populous city</a> in Brazil, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Economy_of_Brazil" title="Economy of Brazil">16th by economic relevance</a>. It is the second largest in the <a href="/wiki/North_Region,_Brazil" title="North Region, Brazil">North Region</a>, second only to <a href="/wiki/Manaus" title="Manaus">Manaus</a>, in the state of <a href="/wiki/Amazonas_(Brazilian_state)" title="Amazonas (Brazilian state)">Amazonas</a>.
</p><p>Founded in 1616 by the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Portugal" title="Kingdom of Portugal">Kingdom of Portugal</a>, Belém was the first <a href="/wiki/Colonial_Brazil" title="Colonial Brazil">European colony</a> on the Amazon but did not become part of Brazil until 1775. The newer part of the city has modern buildings and skyscrapers. The colonial portion retains the charm of tree-filled squares, churches and traditional blue tiles. The city has a rich history and architecture from colonial times. Recently it witnessed a skyscraper boom.
</p><p>Belém is also known as the <i>Metropolis of the Brazilian Amazon region</i> or the <i>Cidade das Mangueiras</i> (City of <a href="/wiki/Mango" title="Mango">Mango</a> Trees) due to the vast number of those trees found in the city. Brazilians often refer to the city as Belém do Pará ("Belém of Pará") rather than just <i>Belém</i>, a reference to an earlier name for the city, Santa Maria de Belém do Grão Pará, and also to differentiate it from  a number of other towns called Belém in Brazil, as well as the Palestinian city of Bethlehem. It is named after <a href="/wiki/Santa_Maria_de_Bel%C3%A9m_(Lisbon)" class="mw-redirect" title="Santa Maria de Belém (Lisbon)">Santa Maria de Belém</a> in Lisbon, also better known by its shortened name, <a href="/wiki/Bel%C3%A9m_(Lisbon)" title="Belém (Lisbon)">Belém</a>.
</p><p>Belém is served by two airports: <a href="/wiki/Val_de_Cans_International_Airport" title="Val de Cans International Airport">Val de Cans International Airport</a>, which connects the city with the rest of Brazil and other cities in South America, North America (USA) and Europe (Lisbon) and <a href="/wiki/Prot%C3%A1sio_de_Oliveira_Airport" title="Protásio de Oliveira Airport">Brig. Protásio de Oliveira Airport (formerly called Júlio César Airport)</a> dedicated to general aviation. The city is also home to the <a href="/wiki/Federal_University_of_Par%C3%A1" title="Federal University of Pará">Federal University of Pará</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Par%C3%A1_State_University" title="Pará State University">Pará State University</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <b>Belém</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> word for <a href="/wiki/Bethlehem" title="Bethlehem">Bethlehem</a>, the town where Christians believe <a href="/wiki/Jesus_Christ" class="mw-redirect" title="Jesus Christ">Jesus Christ</a> was born. The city had other names before eventually becoming Belém.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ver-oPeso-Belem.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Ver-oPeso-Belem.jpg/220px-Ver-oPeso-Belem.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Ver-oPeso-Belem.jpg/330px-Ver-oPeso-Belem.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Ver-oPeso-Belem.jpg/440px-Ver-oPeso-Belem.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="533" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ver-oPeso-Belem.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ver-o-Peso market.</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_Bel%C3%A9m" title="Timeline of Belém">Timeline of Belém</a></div>
<p>In 1615, Portuguese captain-general <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Caldeira_Castelo_Branco" title="Francisco Caldeira Castelo Branco">Francisco Caldeira Castelo Branco</a> of the captaincy of <a href="/wiki/Bahia" title="Bahia">Bahia</a> commanded a military expedition sent by the <a href="/wiki/Governorate_General_of_Brazil" title="Governorate General of Brazil">Governor General of Brazil</a> to check the trading excursions of foreigners (French, Dutch, English) up the river (Amazon) from the Cabo do Norte in Grão Pará.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bel%C3%A9m_(PA).tif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Bel%C3%A9m_%28PA%29.tif/lossy-page1-220px-Bel%C3%A9m_%28PA%29.tif.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="131" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Bel%C3%A9m_%28PA%29.tif/lossy-page1-330px-Bel%C3%A9m_%28PA%29.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Bel%C3%A9m_%28PA%29.tif/lossy-page1-440px-Bel%C3%A9m_%28PA%29.tif.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3114" data-file-height="1854" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bel%C3%A9m_(PA).tif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Belém, 1910s. <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_National_Archives" title="Brazilian National Archives">National Archives of Brazil.</a></div></div></div>
<p>On January 12, 1616, he anchored in what is now known as Guajará Bay, formed by the confluence of the Para and <a href="/wiki/Guam%C3%A1_River" title="Guamá River">Guama</a> Rivers, called by the Tupinambás, "Guaçu Paraná". Caldeira mistook the bay for the main channel, and thirty leagues (178&#160;km) upstream, he built a wooden fort, covered with straw, which he called "Presépio" (nativity scene), now known as "Forte do Castelo". The colony formed by the fort was given the name Feliz Lusitânia, "Happy Lusitania". It was the embryo of the future city of Belém. The fort failed to suppress Dutch and French trading, but did ward off colonization.
</p><p>Feliz Lusitânia was later called Nossa Senhora de Belém do Grão Pará (Our Lady of Bethlehem of Grao-Para) and Santa Maria de Belém (St. Mary of Bethlehem). Belém was given city status in 1655 and was made capital of the State when Pará state was split off from <a href="/wiki/Maranh%C3%A3o" title="Maranhão">Maranhão</a> in 1772. The early decades of the 19th century were marked by political instability. Uprisings and internecine strife finally ended in 1836, after considerable loss of life.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sugar_trade" class="mw-redirect" title="Sugar trade">sugar trade</a> in the Belém region was important up to the end of the 17th century. Thereafter the city's economic importance alternately rose and fell. Cattle ranching supplanted sugar until the 18th century, when cultivation of rice, cotton and coffee became profitable. With the settlement of <a href="/wiki/Southern_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="Southern Brazil">southern Brazil</a>, where such crops could be produced more efficiently, Belém declined again. The city subsequently became the main exporting centre of the Amazon rubber industry, and by 1866 its position was further enhanced by the opening of the Amazon, <a href="/wiki/Tocantins_River" title="Tocantins River">Tocantins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tapaj%C3%B3s" title="Tapajós">Tapajós</a> rivers to navigation. The rubber era ended after the boom of 1910–12, but Belém continued to be the main commercial centre of northern Brazil and the <a href="/wiki/Entrep%C3%B4t" title="Entrepôt">entrepôt</a> for the Amazon valley.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886046874">@media all and (max-width:720px){.mw-parser-output .mobile-float-reset{float:none!important;width:100%!important}}.mw-parser-output .stack-container{box-sizing:border-box}.mw-parser-output .stack-clear-left{float:left;clear:left}.mw-parser-output .stack-clear-right{float:right;clear:right}.mw-parser-output .stack-left{float:left}.mw-parser-output .stack-right{float:right}.mw-parser-output .stack-object{margin:1px;overflow:hidden}</style><div class="mw-stack stack-container stack-right mobile-float-reset"></div>
<p>These include the islands of <a href="/wiki/Mosqueiro" title="Mosqueiro">Mosqueiro</a>, fringed by 14 freshwater beaches, and Caratateua which receive a large number of visitors in summertime. In addition to these and also near Belém, is the island of Tatuoca which is the location of one of the seven <a href="/wiki/Geophysical" class="mw-redirect" title="Geophysical">geophysical</a> stations in the world, and the only station in <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Belém has a <a href="/wiki/Tropical_rainforest_climate" title="Tropical rainforest climate">tropical rainforest climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen</a>: <i>Af</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> more subject to the <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a> than the maritime <a href="/wiki/Trade_wind" class="mw-redirect" title="Trade wind">trade winds</a>, and with no <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">cyclones</a>, that is a true equatorial climate. In all 12 months of the year, the city on average sees more than 60&#160;mm of rainfall, so the city has no true <a href="/wiki/Dry_season" title="Dry season">dry season</a> month. However, Belém features noticeably wetter and drier seasons. The wetter season spans from December through May, while the drier season covers the remaining six months of the year. Like many cities with a tropical rainforest climate, average temperatures vary little throughout the course of the year, generally hovering around 26.5 degrees Celsius. As one would expect, tropical rainforest is the natural vegetation in and around the city.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vegetation">Vegetation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Vegetation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vitregias02.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Vitregias02.jpg/220px-Vitregias02.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Vitregias02.jpg/330px-Vitregias02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Vitregias02.jpg/440px-Vitregias02.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vitregias02.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Vit%C3%B3ria_R%C3%A9gia" class="mw-redirect" title="Vitória Régia">Vitória Régia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paraense_Em%C3%ADlio_Goeldi_Museum" class="mw-redirect" title="Paraense Emílio Goeldi Museum">Paraense Emílio Goeldi Museum</a></div></div></div>
<p>The Amazon represents more than half the planet's remaining rainforests and comprises the largest and most species-rich tract of tropical rainforest in the world. Wet tropical forests are the most species-rich <a href="/wiki/Biome" title="Biome">biome</a>, and tropical forests in the Americas are consistently more species rich than the wet forests in Africa and Asia.<sup id="cite_ref-Turner_2001_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Turner_2001-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>  As the largest tract of tropical rainforest in the Americas, the Amazonian rainforests have unparalleled <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a>. More than one-third of all species in the world live in the Amazon Rainforest.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many valuable products now exported from the Amazon by way of Belém are <a href="/wiki/Aluminium" title="Aluminium">aluminium</a>, iron ore, and other metals.,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> nuts (chiefly <a href="/wiki/Brazil_nuts" class="mw-redirect" title="Brazil nuts">Brazil nuts</a>), pineapples, <a href="/wiki/Cassava" title="Cassava">cassava</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jute" title="Jute">jute</a>, wood veneers, and <a href="/wiki/Hardwoods" class="mw-redirect" title="Hardwoods">hardwoods</a>. <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="Japanese Brazilian">Japanese immigration</a> after the 1930s was an important factor in developing <a href="/wiki/Jute" title="Jute">jute</a> and black pepper, notably at Tomé-Açu, just south of Belém, and near <a href="/wiki/Santar%C3%A9m,_Brazil" class="mw-redirect" title="Santarém, Brazil">Santarém</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Maraj%C3%B3_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Marajó Island">Marajó Island</a>, the largest <a href="/wiki/Fluvial" class="mw-redirect" title="Fluvial">fluvial</a> island in the world, which lies just across the Rio Pará from Belém, has some livestock grazing.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Electricity is provided by the massive <a href="/wiki/Tucuru%C3%AD_Dam" title="Tucuruí Dam">Tucuruí Dam</a>, some 300&#160;km southwest of the city on the Tocantins River.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Institute_of_Geography_and_Statistics" title="Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics">IBGE</a> of 2018, there were 2,491,052 people residing in the Metropolitan Region of Belém. This region is composed by 7 cities: Belém (1,485.732 people), Ananindeua (525,566 people), Marituba (129,321 people), Benevides (61,689 people), Santa Bárbara do Pará (20,704 people), Santa Izabel do Pará (69,746 people), and Castanhal (198,294 people). In 2012, according to IBGE, the capitalcity itself had a population density of 1,337.2 inhabitants per square kilometre (3,463/sq&#160;mi). The last PNAD (National Research for Sample of Domiciles) census revealed the following numbers: 1,379,655 <a href="/wiki/Pardo" title="Pardo">Pardo</a> (brown) people (64.5%), 588,225 <a href="/wiki/White_Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="White Brazilian">White</a> (27.5%), 156,147 <a href="/wiki/Afro-Brazilian" class="mw-redirect" title="Afro-Brazilian">Black</a> (7.3%), 14,973 Asian or Amerindian people (0.7%).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to an autosomal DNA genetic study from 2011, the ancestral composition of the population of Belém is: 68.6% European ancestry, followed by 20.9% Amerindian ancestry and 10.6% African ancestry.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Belem-Cathedral1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Belem-Cathedral1.jpg/220px-Belem-Cathedral1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="196" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Belem-Cathedral1.jpg/330px-Belem-Cathedral1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Belem-Cathedral1.jpg/440px-Belem-Cathedral1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1840" data-file-height="1640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Belem-Cathedral1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">Cathedral</a> of Sé.</div></div></div>
<h3><span id="C.C3.ADrio_de_Nazar.C3.A9"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Círio_de_Nazaré">Círio de Nazaré</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Círio de Nazaré">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the second Sunday in October, Pará celebrates the largest religious event in Brazil: the <a href="/wiki/Procession" title="Procession">procession</a> of the Círio of Nazaré. This tradition started when a farmer and lumberman called Plácido José de Souza found an image of the Virgin and Child on the edge of the Murucutu creek, where the <a href="/wiki/Basilica_of_Our_Lady_of_Nazareth_of_Exile" title="Basilica of Our Lady of Nazareth of Exile">Basilica of Our Lady of Nazareth of Exile</a> stands today. He decided to take the image home. However, the image would mysteriously go back to the place where it was initially found every time he took it home. So Plácido decided to build a small <a href="/wiki/Chapel" title="Chapel">chapel</a> on the edge of the creek. This episode was regarded as miraculous throughout the region. It attracted hundreds of believers to see the image and pay homage to it.
</p><p>Since then, the Círio has been celebrated as a long procession lasting around five hours, in which thousands of people follow the statue through the streets of Belém. The Círio festival is regarded as the "Christmas of the Amazon" because everyone is involved in the arrangements to receive the saint. In early September, minor celebrations take place as a spiritual preparation for the Círio, with thousands of images scattered all over the capital and neighboring cities. 
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cirio_2006_berlinda_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Cirio_2006_berlinda_1.jpg/220px-Cirio_2006_berlinda_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="218" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Cirio_2006_berlinda_1.jpg/330px-Cirio_2006_berlinda_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Cirio_2006_berlinda_1.jpg/440px-Cirio_2006_berlinda_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2043" data-file-height="2022" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cirio_2006_berlinda_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Círio de Nazaré celebration.</div></div></div>
<p>In time it became necessary to incorporate new elements into the tradition due to the people's desire to honor their patron saint. On the Saturday morning prior to the Círio procession the statue is taken to the square of <a href="/wiki/Ananindeua" class="mw-redirect" title="Ananindeua">Ananindeua</a>, a nearby city, to begin a Road Pilgrimage to the Icoaraci pier for a <a href="/wiki/Mass_in_the_Catholic_Church" title="Mass in the Catholic Church">Mass</a>. The river pilgrimage then begins. It was created to honor the "water men" who regard the Virgin of Nazareth as their patron saint. There is a traditional ship contest to award the most originally decorated boat. When the Sacred Image leaves Icoaraci in a Navy <a href="/wiki/Corvette" title="Corvette">Corvette</a>, it is followed by dozens of boats and ships through the waters of Guajará Bay to the pier of Belém, from where it is escorted all the way to the Gentil Bittencourt School. The subsequent candle-lit procession symbolizes the story of the discovery of the Saint and its return to where it was found. The procession follows the Carriage, to which a huge rope is tied, which is carried by the faithful until the procession arrives at the Sé Cathedral.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cirio_2006_corda_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Cirio_2006_corda_1.jpg/220px-Cirio_2006_corda_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="85" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Cirio_2006_corda_1.jpg/330px-Cirio_2006_corda_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Cirio_2006_corda_1.jpg/440px-Cirio_2006_corda_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="1180" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cirio_2006_corda_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Cord and procession.</div></div></div>
<p>During the procession, there is a <a href="/wiki/Fireworks" title="Fireworks">fireworks</a> show, sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Stevedore" title="Stevedore">Stevedores</a>' Union, marking the passage of the Saint until its arrival at the <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">Cathedral</a>. At daybreak of the next day, the faithful start to gather at the Old City, believing that this will bring them closer to the Virgin. At 7 o'clock, the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop" title="Archbishop">archbishop</a> conducts the image to the carriage as bells toll and fireworks explode. The main procession then goes through the streets of the city to the Architectonic Centre of Nazareth, known for its Sanctuary Square. As it arrives at the square, the image is removed from the carriage for the celebration of a Mass and then lifted so that everybody will be blessed by the patron saint of Pará.
</p><p>The festivities last 15 days, with religious celebrations, like the Children's Círio, and its procession, held two Sundays after the Círio. The Re-Círio marks the end of the celebrations, with a shorter course back to the Gentil Bittencourt chapel.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Convention_and_Fair_Centre_of_the_Amazon">Convention and Fair Centre of the Amazon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Convention and Fair Centre of the Amazon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Built in a 23,000-square-metre area, the Hangar Convention and Fair Center of the Amazon has 12 rooms, ticket offices, baggage keeping, press room, and a <a href="/wiki/Food_court" title="Food court">food court</a>, distributed in two big buildings with a parking lot for 800 vehicles. Genuine Amazonian trees will soon be planted in the outdoor area. With Hangar, Belém joins the market for national conventions that take place in a different city each time, such as the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Computer_Society" title="Brazilian Computer Society">Brazilian Computer Society</a> Congress. National conventions had not been held in the Northern region previously.
</p><p>Hangar, whose auditorium has room for 2,160 participants, has some of the best technologies available in the world and is the most modern and functional space for events in the country. According to the Brazilian Association of Convention and Fair Centers (ABRACCEF), there are 17,500 events happening on average throughout the 53 main convention and exhibition centres of Brazil. These activities bring together approximately 28 million participants. Belém has great potential for this type of tourism.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Architecture">Architecture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Architecture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In many ways, Belém's <a href="/wiki/Colonial_architecture" title="Colonial architecture">colonial architecture</a> reflects the seventeenth-century architecture of <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> in Portugal which served as the inspiration for the main housing projects of the time, with the frequent use of tile-hung façades. <a href="/wiki/Neoclassicism" title="Neoclassicism">Neoclassical architecture</a> is also present in buildings such as the <a href="/wiki/Theatro_da_Paz" title="Theatro da Paz">Theatro da Paz</a>, built in 1874. Many of the buildings at the end of the nineteenth century and early twentieth century also echo <a href="/wiki/French_architecture" title="French architecture">French architecture</a>.
</p><p>Belém began with the narrow streets of the Cidade Velha (Old City), a district which still preserves some structures that the Portuguese colonials built here, places like Forte do Castelo, a fort built to defend the region against French, Dutch and British colonization attempts, and one of the first structures in the capital. The Ver-o-pêso market is the biggest open market in Latin America where you can find everything, from the most exotic fruit, to the scented baths.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cuisine">Cuisine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Cuisine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The local <a href="/wiki/Amerindian" class="mw-redirect" title="Amerindian">Amerindian</a> culture makes use of local elements to create the colors and flavors of its cuisine. One such dish, "Cupuaçu", comes from the Cupuaçu tree, found in the Amazonian woods. Cupuaçu is easily identified by its unique smell and sour taste. Its pulp is also extracted to make juices, candies, jellies, liquors, and ice cream. <a href="/wiki/A%C3%A7a%C3%AD_palm" title="Açaí palm">Açaí</a> is a palm tree with a long, thin stem. Açaí, also known as Jussara, is purple in color with a delicious taste. Long prized by the local population, it recently it has also reached the national menu.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Freshwater_crab" title="Freshwater crab">Freshwater crab</a>, a traditional local delicacy, is popular. Found only in swamps, its well-tempered meat can be served in different forms: as a shell, the so-called unha (the claws) or toc-toc.  "<a href="/wiki/Mani%C3%A7oba" title="Maniçoba">Maniçoba</a>" is another highlight of local cuisine. Its preparation is time-consuming and its final appearance is quite surprising for those who have never tried it, due to the dark look of the cooked maniva (ground <a href="/wiki/Manioc" class="mw-redirect" title="Manioc">manioc</a> leaves). But this first impression ends quickly, after you taste the dish with its seemingly awkward ingredients. Maniçoba is often served in ceramic dishes, and can be eaten with rice or with manioc flour and <a href="/wiki/Capsicum" title="Capsicum">capsicum</a>.
</p><p>Belém has been part of the <a href="/wiki/Creative_Cities_Network" title="Creative Cities Network">UNESCO Creative Cities Network</a> in the category of gastronomy since 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The three main football teams in <a href="/wiki/Par%C3%A1" title="Pará">Pará</a> are based in Belém: <a href="/wiki/Clube_do_Remo" title="Clube do Remo">Remo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paysandu_Sport_Club" title="Paysandu Sport Club">Paysandu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tuna_Luso_Brasileira" title="Tuna Luso Brasileira">Tuna Luso</a>. </p><p><a href="/wiki/Uni%C3%A3o_Esportiva" title="União Esportiva">União Esportiva</a> used to be a relatively successful club based in the city but was disbanded in 1965.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Belém has a modern appearance with tree-lined streets, several plazas and public gardens, and many noteworthy buildings. The north's leading educational and cultural centre, it is the seat of a <a href="/wiki/Diocese" title="Diocese">bishopric</a>, and its <a href="/wiki/Cathedral" title="Cathedral">cathedral</a> (Igreja da Sé, founded in 1917) is one of Brazil's largest. Santo Alexandre, the oldest of Belém's churches, was built in 1616. The Museu (museum) Paraense <a href="/wiki/%C3%89mil_Goeldi" title="Émil Goeldi">Emílio Goeldi</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Da_Paz_Theatre" class="mw-redirect" title="Da Paz Theatre">Teatro da Paz</a> (a classical theatre), and the public library and archives are other notable institutions. The Universidade Federal do Pará (1957), a teacher-training school, an agricultural institute, and an institute for research on <a href="/wiki/Tropical_diseases" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical diseases">tropical diseases</a> are also in the city. The Ver-o-Peso (Portuguese: "see the weight") market in the old port centre is a major tourist attraction. The city is also home to a large <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> stadium, the <a href="/wiki/Est%C3%A1dio_Ol%C3%ADmpico_do_Par%C3%A1" title="Estádio Olímpico do Pará">Estádio Olímpico do Pará</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Utinga_State_Park">Utinga State Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Utinga State Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The municipality of Belém contains 99% of the <a href="/wiki/Utinga_State_Park" title="Utinga State Park">Utinga State Park</a>, created in 1993 to protect the metropolitan area's water supply.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
The park provides a space for healthy activities in contact with nature by local people, visitors or tourists.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
It is just a few kilometers from the city center.
There are eight trails, all providing direct contact with various species of flora and fauna.
The most popular is the monkey trail, which takes about 40 minutes and goes from Bolonha lake to the visitor center, where other activities are available.
Visitors can also learn about the water treatment plant that supplies Belém.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Amazon_Biopark_Zoo">Amazon Biopark Zoo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Amazon Biopark Zoo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Located less than 15 kilometres (9.3 miles) from the center of Belém, in the Tenoné neighborhood, the Bioparque Amazônia Safari - Crocodilo Safari Zoo, is surrounded by forests, rivers and streams. Summary of the Amazon landscape with lush flora and fauna, the zoo has about 14 miles (23 kilometres) of trails in an area composed of four interconnected ecosystems.
</p><p>A large attractive Bioparque is the Museum of Paleontology and Malacology where visitors can watch a collection of three thousand pieces of exposed shells and mollusks collected from all continents. On site, you will also see paintings with motifs of cabloco Amazon.
</p><p>Bioparque The Amazon is a private investment, owner Jorge Arthur Aarão Monteiro, authorized and licensed by IBAMA to function as C class zoo, the only category of Pará There are four different species of alligators in a population of thousands of individuals, highlighting açu for alligator, monkeys, anteaters, and the otters, hyacinth macaw, papagaios, tucannos, pacaranas, harpy eagle, among other animals.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> 
Also see the city turistic website here.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Rodrigues_Alves_Wood.E2.80.93Botanical_Garden"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Rodrigues_Alves_Wood–Botanical_Garden">Rodrigues Alves Wood–Botanical Garden</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Rodrigues Alves Wood–Botanical Garden">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Inspired by the <a href="/wiki/Bois_de_Boulogne" title="Bois de Boulogne">Bois de Boulogne</a> Park in Paris, the <a href="/wiki/Francisco_de_Paula_Rodrigues_Alves" title="Francisco de Paula Rodrigues Alves">Rodrigues Alves</a> Wood is a little piece of Amazonia preserved in the middle of the city. Comprising 16 <a href="/wiki/Hectare" title="Hectare">hectares</a>, in the ample Avenida <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Manoel_Barroso_da_Silva" class="mw-redirect" title="Francisco Manoel Barroso da Silva">Almirante Barroso</a>, one of the city's busiest thoroughfares, having 2,500 native species, an orchidary, lakes, caverns, waterfalls and even a replica of a mountain. There you can see small animals characteristic to the region, such as <a href="/wiki/Squirrel_monkey" title="Squirrel monkey">squirrel monkeys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Agouti" title="Agouti">agoutis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Macaw" title="Macaw">macaws</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="The_Esta.C3.A7.C3.A3o_das_Docas_Complex"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Estação_das_Docas_Complex">The Estação das Docas Complex</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: The Estação das Docas Complex">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Estação das Docas Complex reopened the windows of Belém to Guajará Bay. The restoration project covers the area of old warehouses of the Pará Docks Company. Constructed from prefabricated metal structures in England and that were built at the beginning of the twentieth century in Belém.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There are eighteen thousand square metres (193,750 square feet) of urbanised area, with coffee bar services, various restaurants, stores, travel agencies, banks, in addition to an auditorium and two memorials: The Porto Memorial and the Fortaleza de <a href="/wiki/Peter_Nolasco" title="Peter Nolasco">São Pedro Nolasco</a> Memorial There is, also, a fluvial station and extensive external area.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ver-o-Peso_Market">Ver-o-Peso Market</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Ver-o-Peso Market">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Created in 1688, as a result of the Portuguese deciding to levy a tax for everything entering and leaving Amazonia. Despite resembling a large retailer, the mixture of colours, fragrances and objects is very interesting as well as folkloric<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (May 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>.
</p><p>Medicinal herbs, various regional fruits, arts and crafts, domestic utilities, meats, fish and seasonings and spices can be found there. The Market brings together two thousand stalls and traders in every part and is located near to the old Mercado de Ferro (Iron market), on the <a href="/wiki/Quay" class="mw-redirect" title="Quay">quays</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Airports">Airports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Airports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Valdecaes.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Valdecaes.JPG/220px-Valdecaes.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Valdecaes.JPG/330px-Valdecaes.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Valdecaes.JPG/440px-Valdecaes.JPG 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="450" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Valdecaes.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Val_de_C%C3%A3es_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Val de Cães International Airport">Belém International Airport</a>.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Val_de_Cans_International_Airport" title="Val de Cans International Airport">Belém International Airport (Val de Cans)</a> is the major airport serving the city of Belém. The building design uses plane curves on its roof to permit light to enter its entire large terminal hall.
</p><p>The architect <a href="/w/index.php?title=S%C3%A9rgio_Parada&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sérgio Parada (page does not exist)">Sérgio Parada</a> used adopted multiple-use totems integrated with light projectors, a sound system, air conditioning, and public telephones. Currently Belém International Airport serves 2.7 million passengers a year, in a constructed area of 33,255.17 square metres (357,955.67 square feet).<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Traditionally called Val-de-Cães Airport, it is responsible for increasing tourism in the Amazon region, as well as for the outflow of products and attracting new investments. The passenger terminal is fully air conditioned on two levels and has "futuristic" architecture, designed to take advantage of natural lighting. People with special needs have individualized service with own equipment at specific locations to facilitate their access. The terminal's interior is decorated with plants native to the Amazon region and is enclosed by a source able to imitate the sound of the rains that fall every day in the region.
</p><p>The smaller <a href="/wiki/Prot%C3%A1sio_de_Oliveira_Airport" title="Protásio de Oliveira Airport">Brig. Protásio de Oliveira Airport (Júlio César)</a> also administrated by Infraero, is used for general aviation.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Highways">Highways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bel%C3%A9m&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Highways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/BR-316" title="BR-316">BR-316</a> is the major access highway for those coming from the Northeastern Brazil. For visitors from the Southern, Southeastern, and Mid-Eastern Regions, the best route is <a href="/wiki/BR-010" title="BR-010">BR-010</a>, which originates in <a href="/wiki/Bras%C3%ADlia" title="Brasília">Brasília</a> in the South, and also PA-150, a route that links Belém to Southern Pará.
</p>
