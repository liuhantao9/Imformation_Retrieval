<p><b>Tyrone Clinton Spong</b> (born 3 September 1985) is a <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Surinamese</a>-<a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> <a href="/wiki/Professional_boxer" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional boxer">professional boxer</a> and former <a href="/wiki/Kickboxer" class="mw-redirect" title="Kickboxer">kickboxer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mixed_martial_art" class="mw-redirect" title="Mixed martial art">mixed martial artist</a>. He is the current WBC Latino <a href="/wiki/Heavyweight" title="Heavyweight">Heavyweight</a> Champion, current <a href="/wiki/World_Boxing_Organization" title="World Boxing Organization">WBO</a> Latino <a href="/wiki/Heavyweight" title="Heavyweight">Heavyweight</a> Champion, former WFCA <a href="/wiki/Cruiserweight_(boxing)" title="Cruiserweight (boxing)">Cruiserweight</a> champion, former <a href="/wiki/Glory_(kickboxing)" title="Glory (kickboxing)">Glory 95kg Slam Champion</a> and former <a href="/wiki/It%27s_Showtime_(kickboxing)" title="It&#39;s Showtime (kickboxing)">It's Showtime 95MAX World champion</a>. Spong fights out of the Fighting Factory Carbin in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, Netherlands.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyrone_Spong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tyrone Spong was born in Suriname. In 1990 at the age of 5, he moved to the Netherlands with his family. Growing up in the <a href="/wiki/Bijlmermeer" title="Bijlmermeer">Bijlmermeer</a>, he was often involved in <a href="/wiki/Street_fighting" title="Street fighting">street fighting</a> and began training in kickboxing at thirteen when he found a gym by coincidence. After getting his "ass whooped" in his first day of training, he was motivated to improve. He had his first match at fifteen and won by first-round knockout.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Some of his early sparring partners included <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Overeem" title="Alistair Overeem">Alistair</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valentijn_Overeem" title="Valentijn Overeem">Valentijn Overeem</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Yvel" title="Gilbert Yvel">Gilbert Yvel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kickboxing_career">Kickboxing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyrone_Spong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Kickboxing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2003, his first year as a pro, he made his mark winning 12 fights that year. In 2004, he fought against Rafi Zouheir at the Battle of Zaandam and won his first European Muay Thai Title (<a href="/wiki/World_Kickboxing_Network" title="World Kickboxing Network">World Kickboxing Network</a>). In 2004 Spong also visited Japan to compete in Shootboxing where he lost to Ryuji Goto. Goto suplexed him in the final seconds of the fight to earn a well-received unanimous decision. His next fight in April 2005, was against Belgian Mohammed Ouali for another European Title (WPKL). Spong won the fight by unanimous decision.
</p><p>In December, 2005 at the A-1 Combat Cup in <a href="/wiki/Duisburg" title="Duisburg">Duisburg</a>, after three consecutive KO's in one night, Tyrone Spong won his first tournament Championship.
</p><p>In 2006, Spong had two his biggest victories of his young career, KO wins over Muay Thai veteran <a href="/wiki/Joerie_Mes" title="Joerie Mes">Joerie Mes</a> and two weeks later knocking out <a href="/wiki/K-1" title="K-1">K-1</a> superstar <a href="/wiki/Kaoklai_Kaennorsing" title="Kaoklai Kaennorsing">Kaoklai Kaennorsing</a> from Thailand in a first round.
</p><p>In 2007, Spong won the SLAMM world title up to 79&#160;kg by defeating the Thai Yodchai Wor Petchpun via TKO in the first round, and in the following match he defeated the Belarusian <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Shakuta" title="Dmitry Shakuta">Dmitry Shakuta</a>.
</p><p>On January 26, 2008, Spong won the World Full Contact Association (WFCA) World Thaiboxing Cruiserweight Championship by defeating Aurelien Duarte. Following this, he won the <i>KO World Series 2008</i> by defeating Nikos Sokolis via knockout in the first round, and defended his WFCA Muay Thai title by finishing <a href="/wiki/Ondrej_Hutnik" class="mw-redirect" title="Ondrej Hutnik">Ondrej Hutnik</a> with a left liver punch in the second round. On April 26, 2008, Spong faced <a href="/wiki/Azem_Maksutaj" title="Azem Maksutaj">Azem Maksutaj</a> at the <i><a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix_2008_in_Amsterdam" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World Grand Prix 2008 in Amsterdam">K-1 World Grand Prix 2008 in Amsterdam</a></i>, and won by knockout in the second round. He then defeated <a href="/wiki/Gary_Turner_(fighter)" title="Gary Turner (fighter)">Gary Turner</a> via TKO in the first round. On November 29, 2008, he became the first <a href="/wiki/It%27s_Showtime_(kickboxing)" title="It&#39;s Showtime (kickboxing)">It's Showtime 95MAX</a> World champion after beating <a href="/wiki/Zabit_Samedov" title="Zabit Samedov">Zabit Samedov</a> by unanimous decision.
</p><p>On March 28, 2009, Spong participated in the first ever K-1 Heavyweight (-100&#160;kg) Title tournament, held in <a href="/wiki/Yokohama,_Japan" class="mw-redirect" title="Yokohama, Japan">Yokohama, Japan</a>. He was beaten by <a href="/wiki/Gokhan_Saki" class="mw-redirect" title="Gokhan Saki">Gokhan Saki</a> in semifinals by right hook KO of the extra round.<sup id="cite_ref-Maeda_Takes_K-1_Heavyweight_Belt;_Bonjasky_Beats_Overeem_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maeda_Takes_K-1_Heavyweight_Belt;_Bonjasky_Beats_Overeem-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Spong finally met rival <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Corbett" title="Nathan Corbett">Nathan Corbett</a> at Champions of Champions II on June 27, 2009, in what was widely considered a battle between the two best Muay Thai fighters in the world at their weight class for the W.M.C. world title -93&#160;kg.  The fight was a close affair with Spong knocking Corbett down on the second round only for it to end with a controversial decision.  In the third round Corbett managed to knock Spong out with a huge right hook causing the referee to stop the fight.  However, due to the referee's misleading hand signals, Corbett rushed back in to knock Spong down once more, not sure that the fight had been stopped.  After considerable confusion the fight was declared a no contest instead of a Corbett KO win.
</p><p>After defeating the Worlds heavyweight champion, <a href="/wiki/Kyotaro" class="mw-redirect" title="Kyotaro">Kyotaro</a> on December 5, 2009, he fought <a href="/wiki/Jerome_Le_Banner" class="mw-redirect" title="Jerome Le Banner">Jerome Le Banner</a> in April 2010. Similar to the Nathan Corbett fight Spong broke his right hand in the first round and was knocked down. Despite coming back strongly he lost by decision.  At the end of the year he qualified for the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix_2010_Final" title="K-1 World Grand Prix 2010 Final">K-1 World Grand Prix 2010 Final</a>, defeating veteran <a href="/wiki/Ray_Sefo" title="Ray Sefo">Ray Sefo</a> in an elimination match, only to lose to eventual winner <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Overeem" title="Alistair Overeem">Alistair Overeem</a> at the quarter final stage in what was a more competitive fight than most initial thought - as Overeem was much the bigger fighter.  At the start of 2011, Spong had to vacate the It's Showtime 95MAX world title due to a number of issues such as not having had a title defense in the allocated two years and his management stating that he had moved up in weight to fight as a heavyweight.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He knocked out <a href="/wiki/Igor_Mihaljevic" class="mw-redirect" title="Igor Mihaljevic">Igor Mihaljevic</a> with a left knee in the first round at <i>It's Showtime 2011 Lyon</i> on May 14, 2011. He defeated <a href="/wiki/Loren_Javier_Jorge" class="mw-redirect" title="Loren Javier Jorge">Loren Javier Jorge</a> by unanimous decision at <i>It's Showtime Madrid 2011</i> on June 18, 2011.
</p><p>Spong faced <a href="/wiki/Melvin_Manhoef" title="Melvin Manhoef">Melvin Manhoef</a> at <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Showtime_2012_in_Leeuwarden" title="It&#39;s Showtime 2012 in Leeuwarden">It's Showtime 2012 in Leeuwarden</a></i> on January 28, 2012,  and won by unanimous decision. He faced the legendary Dutchman <a href="/wiki/Peter_Aerts" title="Peter Aerts">Peter Aerts</a> at an It's Showtime event in Brussels, Belgium on June 30, 2012, and won by knockout in the third round.
</p><p>He faced <a href="/wiki/Remy_Bonjasky" title="Remy Bonjasky">Remy Bonjasky</a> at <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_5:_London" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 5: London">Glory 5: London</a></i> in London, England on March 23, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  Spong won via one-punch KO with a right hook in the second round.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-davewalsh_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-davewalsh-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-muaythaiauthority.com_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-muaythaiauthority.com-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Spong returned to 95&#160;kg to fight in the <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_9:_New_York_-_2013_95kg_Slam" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 9: New York - 2013 95kg Slam">Glory 9: New York - 2013 95kg Slam</a></i> in New York City, New York, United States on June 22, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He had a scare in the quarter-finals when he was dropped by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Duut" title="Michael Duut">Michael Duut</a> immediately as their bout started, although it was not counted by the referee. Just moments after returning to his feet, he landed a crushing right hand on Duut, sending him crashing to the canvas and ensuring his passage to the semis against <a href="/wiki/Filip_Verlinden" title="Filip Verlinden">Filip Verlinden</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It was a technical battle this time, with Spong outpointing the Belgian and taking a unanimous decision. A match with <a href="/wiki/Danyo_Ilunga" title="Danyo Ilunga">Danyo Ilunga</a> awaited him in the final but it ended in a controversial and anticlimactic fashion. As the fight began, both fighters met in the center of the ring and Spong unloaded a flurry of punches. As Ilunga covered up and prepared to counter, referee Mufadel Elghazaoui jumped in and stopped the fight, giving Spong the TKO win at just sixteen seconds of the first round.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Although, it was later revealed that there was a "no <a href="/wiki/Standing_eight_count" title="Standing eight count">standing eight count</a>" rule in effect at the event as per the <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Athletic_Commission" title="New York State Athletic Commission">New York State Athletic Commission</a>'s regulations, the stoppage was still deemed premature by much of the kickboxing community.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He twice dropped <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Corbett" title="Nathan Corbett">Nathan Corbett</a> with left hooks, winning by second-round TKO in their long-awaited rematch at <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_11:_Chicago_-_Heavyweight_World_Championship_Tournament" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 11: Chicago - Heavyweight World Championship Tournament">Glory 11: Chicago - Heavyweight World Championship Tournament</a></i> in Hoffman Estates, Illinois, United States on October 12, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Competing for the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Glory_(kickboxing)#Glory_Light_Heavyweight_Championship" title="Glory (kickboxing)">Glory Light Heavyweight Championship</a>, he fought in the  <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_15:_Istanbul_-_Light_Heavyweight_World_Championship_Tournament" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 15: Istanbul - Light Heavyweight World Championship Tournament">Glory 15: Istanbul - Light Heavyweight World Championship Tournament</a></i> in Istanbul, Turkey on April 12, 2014. After defeating <a href="/wiki/Saulo_Cavalari" title="Saulo Cavalari">Saulo Cavalari</a> via unanimous decision in the semi-finls, he then faced <a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6khan_Saki" title="Gökhan Saki">Gökhan Saki</a> in a highly anticipated rematch in the final. Midway through the opening round, Spong threw a kick to Saki's left leg. Saki checked the kick, causing Spong's lower right leg to fracture immediately and end the fight via TKO.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Spong underwent surgery to repair his broken leg and returned to training in October 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On 14 April 2016 he stated that he had retired from kickboxing and was looking forward to new challenges.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mixed_martial_arts_career">Mixed martial arts career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyrone_Spong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mixed martial arts career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Series_of_Fighting">World Series of Fighting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyrone_Spong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: World Series of Fighting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Spong made his highly anticipated MMA debut against Travis Bartlett at <a href="/wiki/WSOF_1" class="mw-redirect" title="WSOF 1">World Series of Fighting 1</a> on November 3, 2012, in Las Vegas, Nevada in the <a href="/wiki/Light_heavyweight_(MMA)" title="Light heavyweight (MMA)">light heavyweight (205 lb)</a> division. For his MMA debut he trained with the Blackzilians for over a year with such fighters as K-1 World Grand Prix winner and former Strikeforce heavyweight champion <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Overeem" title="Alistair Overeem">Alistair Overeem</a> and former UFC light heavyweight champions <a href="/wiki/Rashad_Evans" title="Rashad Evans">Rashad Evans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitor_Belfort" title="Vitor Belfort">Vitor Belfort</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Spong knocked out Bartlett at 3:15 of the first round with a straight right.
</p><p>Spong faced Angel DeAnda at <a href="/wiki/World_Series_of_Fighting" class="mw-redirect" title="World Series of Fighting">World Series of Fighting 4</a> on August 10, 2013, in the main event.<sup id="cite_ref-Moraes_Returns_at_WSOF_4_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moraes_Returns_at_WSOF_4-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Spong won via unanimous decision.
</p><p>Spong was scheduled to face Jake Heun on December 18, 2015 at <a href="/wiki/World_Series_of_Fighting_26" class="mw-redirect" title="World Series of Fighting 26">World Series of Fighting 26</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> but pulled out with an injury.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Boxing_career">Boxing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyrone_Spong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Boxing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Spong made his boxing debut on March 6, 2015, beating Gabor Farkas by KO in the first round. Spong defeated Juan Carlos Salas by KO in Round 1 at Brave Warriors in Action PPV event on May 27, 2017 in Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On October 7, 2017, Spong won the <a href="/wiki/World_Boxing_Council" title="World Boxing Council">WBC</a> Latino Heavyweight Championship.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tyrone_Spong&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Spong has four children.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>  He is an avid lover of animals, as is evidenced by the majority of his tattoos.  He is the owner of several pit bull terriers, which he bred himself.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>  Spong has trained in the United States with Floyd Mayweather, Sr.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>  He is friends with mixed martial artists <a href="/wiki/Rashad_Evans" title="Rashad Evans">Rashad Evans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Overeem" title="Alistair Overeem">Alistair Overeem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Muhammed_Lawal" title="Muhammed Lawal">Muhammed Lawal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rodney_Glunder" title="Rodney Glunder">Rodney Glunder</a>.  He has helped all of them prepare for some of their fights.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>  Spong trains his physical conditioning at the ReAT Sports athletic performance facility in Amsterdam, under the Strength &amp; Conditioning specialist Rayen Bindraban.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He has also been decorated with the <a href="/wiki/Honorary_Order_of_the_Yellow_Star" title="Honorary Order of the Yellow Star">Honorary Order of the Yellow Star</a> the highest order of Suriname by President  <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a>.
</p>
