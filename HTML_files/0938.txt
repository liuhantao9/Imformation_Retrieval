<p><b>Convalescence</b> is the gradual recovery of <a href="/wiki/Health" title="Health">health</a> and strength after <a href="/wiki/Illness" class="mw-redirect" title="Illness">illness</a> or <a href="/wiki/Injury" title="Injury">injury</a>. It refers to the later stage of an <a href="/wiki/Infectious_Disease" class="mw-redirect" title="Infectious Disease">infectious disease</a> or illness when the patient recovers and returns to previous health, but may continue to be a source of infection to others even if feeling better.<sup id="cite_ref-Cole_1947_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cole_1947-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In this sense, "<a href="/wiki/Healing" title="Healing">recovery</a>" can be considered a synonymous term. This also sometimes includes <a href="/wiki/Patient_Care" class="mw-redirect" title="Patient Care">patient care</a> after a <a href="/wiki/Major_surgery" class="mw-redirect" title="Major surgery">major surgery</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> under which they are required to visit the <a href="/wiki/Physician" title="Physician">doctor</a> for regular <a href="/wiki/Check-up" class="mw-redirect" title="Check-up">check-ups</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Convalescent care facilities are sometimes recognized by the acronym CCF.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
