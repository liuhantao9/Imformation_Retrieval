<p><b>Barnsley Interchange</b> (formerly <i>Barnsley Exchange</i> station) lies in the centre of the town of <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a>, in <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The station was opened in 1850 and is 16 miles (26&#160;km) north of <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Midland_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield Midland railway station">Sheffield</a>. It is on the <a href="/wiki/Hallam_Line" title="Hallam Line">Hallam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penistone_Line" title="Penistone Line">Penistone Lines</a>, both operated by <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Earlier_history">Earlier history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barnsley_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Earlier history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Barnsley,_Penistone,_Batley_RJD_3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Barnsley%2C_Penistone%2C_Batley_RJD_3.jpg/220px-Barnsley%2C_Penistone%2C_Batley_RJD_3.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Barnsley%2C_Penistone%2C_Batley_RJD_3.jpg/330px-Barnsley%2C_Penistone%2C_Batley_RJD_3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Barnsley%2C_Penistone%2C_Batley_RJD_3.jpg/440px-Barnsley%2C_Penistone%2C_Batley_RJD_3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2861" data-file-height="1890" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Barnsley,_Penistone,_Batley_RJD_3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A 1912 <a href="/wiki/Railway_Clearing_House" title="Railway Clearing House">Railway Clearing House</a> Junction Diagram showing (left) railways in the vicinity of Barnsley. The present station is shown here as <i>L&amp;Y STA</i>, on the line coloured blue.</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Sheffield,_Rotherham,_Barnsley,_Wakefield,_Huddersfield_and_Goole_Railway" title="Sheffield, Rotherham, Barnsley, Wakefield, Huddersfield and Goole Railway">Sheffield, Rotherham, Barnsley, Wakefield, Huddersfield &amp; Goole Railway</a> was formed in 1846 with the aim of providing access to the South Yorkshire coalfield. It was to link the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_and_Leeds_Railway" title="Manchester and Leeds Railway">Manchester and Leeds Railway</a> (M&amp;LR) near <a href="/wiki/Horbury_and_Ossett_railway_station" title="Horbury and Ossett railway station">Horbury</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_and_Rotherham_Railway" title="Sheffield and Rotherham Railway">Sheffield and Rotherham Railway</a> near <a href="/wiki/Brightside_railway_station" title="Brightside railway station">Brightside</a>, by way of Barnsley. Whilst the railway was still at the planning stage, it was split in two at Barnsley, the northern portion being leased to the M&amp;LR and the southern to the <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Railway" title="South Yorkshire Railway">South Yorkshire, Doncaster &amp; Goole Railway</a> (SYD&amp;G). The northern section opened first, and Barnsley station was opened with the line on 1 January 1850. The route of the southern section was changed, and instead, is connected to the SYD&amp;G line near <a href="/wiki/Mexborough_railway_station" title="Mexborough railway station">Mexborough</a>. This section opened on 1 July 1851, and Barnsley then became a through station, although the two sections of line were operated by different railways. On 1 July 1854, the <a href="/wiki/Manchester,_Sheffield_and_Lincolnshire_Railway" title="Manchester, Sheffield and Lincolnshire Railway">Manchester, Sheffield and Lincolnshire Railway</a> (MS&amp;LR) opened a line from <a href="/wiki/Penistone_railway_station" title="Penistone railway station">Penistone</a> to Barnsley.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Butt_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Butt-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Each of these railway companies went through various takeovers and amalgamations, until the early 20th century, when the station at Barnsley was co-owned by the <a href="/wiki/Lancashire_and_Yorkshire_Railway" title="Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway">Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway</a> (LYR, successor to the M&amp;LR), and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Central_Railway" title="Great Central Railway">Great Central Railway</a> (GCR, successor to the MS&amp;LR and SYD&amp;G). At the <a href="/wiki/Railways_Act_1921" title="Railways Act 1921">1923 grouping</a>, the GCR became part of the <a href="/wiki/London_and_North_Eastern_Railway" title="London and North Eastern Railway">London and North Eastern Railway</a> (LNER), whilst the LYR formed part of the new <a href="/wiki/London,_Midland_and_Scottish_Railway" title="London, Midland and Scottish Railway">London, Midland and Scottish Railway</a> (LMS), as did the <a href="/wiki/Midland_Railway" title="Midland Railway">Midland Railway</a> (MR). The LYR and MR each contributed one station in Barnsley to the LMS, and since the ex-MR station was already distinguished as <a href="/wiki/Barnsley_Court_House_railway_station" title="Barnsley Court House railway station">Barnsley Court House</a>, the LMS renamed the former LYR station to <i>Barnsley Low Town</i> on 2 June 1924. Just two months later, on 1 August 1924, it was again renamed, this time to <i>Barnsley Exchange</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Butt_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Butt-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barnsley Court House station closed on 19 April 1960, following the commissioning of a new chord line south of the town near Quarry Junction that linked the former SYR route down the Blackburn Valley with the ex-<a href="/wiki/Midland_Railway" title="Midland Railway">Midland Railway</a> line from Sheffield Midland, allowing services on the latter route to serve the station (and continue northwards to Wakefield &amp; Leeds). Subsequently, on 13 June 1960, <i>Barnsley Exchange</i> was renamed <i>Barnsley</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Butt_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Butt-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barnsley_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Station_sign_at_Barnsley.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Station_sign_at_Barnsley.jpg/220px-Station_sign_at_Barnsley.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Station_sign_at_Barnsley.jpg/330px-Station_sign_at_Barnsley.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Station_sign_at_Barnsley.jpg/440px-Station_sign_at_Barnsley.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4608" data-file-height="3456" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Station_sign_at_Barnsley.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Station sign</div></div></div>
<p>The station is fully staffed throughout the day, with the booking office open from 06:00 to 19:30 Mondays to Saturdays and from 08:45 to 19:00 on Sundays.  There are several self-service ticket machines provided for use outside these times and for collecting advance purchase tickets, located on the platform 2 side of the foot-bridge, and outside the ticket office. In the main building on platform 1, there is a waiting room and toilets.  A separate waiting room (with vending machines for drinks and snacks) is located on platform 2, with a fully accessible footbridge (equipped with lifts) linking them; there is step-free access from the entrance and bus station to all platforms.  Train running information is provided by automated announcements, digital display screens and timetable posters. There are a number of shops a short walk over the footbridge to the bus station, these include a newsagents, Lloyds Pharmacy, Coopland Bakery and a Subway restaurant.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barnsley_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2013 it was used as a filming location in <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>'s cult drama series <i><a href="/wiki/Utopia_(UK_TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Utopia (UK TV series)">Utopia</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Barnsley_Interchange_rail_services">Barnsley Interchange rail services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barnsley_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Barnsley Interchange rail services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Morning_crowds,_Barnsley_station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_219216.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Morning_crowds%2C_Barnsley_station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_219216.jpg/220px-Morning_crowds%2C_Barnsley_station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_219216.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Morning_crowds%2C_Barnsley_station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_219216.jpg/330px-Morning_crowds%2C_Barnsley_station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_219216.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Morning_crowds%2C_Barnsley_station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_219216.jpg/440px-Morning_crowds%2C_Barnsley_station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_219216.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="435" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Morning_crowds,_Barnsley_station_-_geograph.org.uk_-_219216.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The station platforms in 1998.</div></div></div>
<p>Rail services operate frequently through Barnsley Interchange station. On the Hallam line during the day from Monday to Saturday, there are three trains per hour northbound bound to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>. Two are express services, calling only at Wakefield Kirkgate en route to Leeds whilst the third is an all-stations local that runs via <a href="/wiki/Castleford_railway_station" title="Castleford railway station">Castleford</a>. On Sundays, the service reduces to one express service and one stopping service to Leeds each hour. Two northbound Sunday services from <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Nottingham railway station">Nottingham</a> to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> extend to <a href="/wiki/Carlisle_railway_station" title="Carlisle railway station">Carlisle</a> via the <a href="/wiki/Settle-Carlisle_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Settle-Carlisle Line">Settle-Carlisle Line</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the Penistone line, there is an hourly service northbound to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield</a>, now provided seven days a week.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Southbound there are four trains per hour (2 fast and 2 stopping). Two of these services terminate at <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield railway station">Sheffield</a> whilst one fast train carries on to <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Nottingham railway station">Nottingham</a> and the other fast service runs through to <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Central" class="mw-redirect" title="Lincoln Central">Lincoln Central</a> (now laying foundations for the Northern Connect service, cutting out all stops between Sheffield and Worksop to reduce journey times). The service drops to three per hour (one fast &amp; two stopping) on Sundays.
</p><p>From the near future, the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> to <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Central_railway_station" title="Lincoln Central railway station">Lincoln Central</a> via <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield railway station">Sheffield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Retford_railway_station" title="Retford railway station">Retford</a> will transfer to the new <i>Northern Connect</i> brand, using brand new trains with WiFi. Furthermore, services to <a href="/wiki/Nottingham_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Nottingham railway station">Nottingham</a> will also be cut from Barnsley, this service will join Northern Connect and operate a new route via <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Westgate" class="mw-redirect" title="Wakefield Westgate">Wakefield Westgate</a> with less stops on the <a href="/wiki/Erewash_Valley_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Erewash Valley Line">Erewash Valley Line</a>. <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> have stated that the frequency of express services between <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield railway station">Sheffield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> via Barnsley will be maintained when the Nottingham service ends.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 1999, <a href="/wiki/Midland_Mainline" title="Midland Mainline">Midland Mainline</a> commenced a daily weekday service to <a href="/wiki/St_Pancras_railway_station" title="St Pancras railway station">London St Pancras</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The service was withdrawn by <a href="/wiki/East_Midlands_Trains" title="East Midlands Trains">East Midlands Trains</a> on 5 September 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Barnsley_Interchange_bus_services">Barnsley Interchange bus services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barnsley_Interchange&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Barnsley Interchange bus services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The new Barnsley Interchange was opened on 20 May 2007 by <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Transport" title="Secretary of State for Transport">Secretary of State for Transport</a> <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Alexander" title="Douglas Alexander">Douglas Alexander</a>. The new building forms the entire new complex of Barnsley Interchange. Rail and bus users exit the interchange via the new car park or, for the town centre, the new entrance and exit is on Eldon Street. It has 21 stands, with 3 coach stands.
</p><p>The bus station features the latest in technology such as the <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Passenger_Transport_Executive" title="South Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive">South Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive</a> scheme of "Your Next Bus", tracking buses with <a href="/wiki/GPS" class="mw-redirect" title="GPS">GPS</a> to check what time they are due to arrive and depart. Several shops occupy retail units within the interchange, including <a href="/wiki/Lloyd%27s_Pharmacy" class="mw-redirect" title="Lloyd&#39;s Pharmacy">Lloyd's Pharmacy</a>, GT News, Cowpuccino Espresso Bar, <a href="/wiki/Cooplands" title="Cooplands">Cooplands</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Subway_(restaurant)" title="Subway (restaurant)">Subway</a>.
</p><p>Work has since been completed on the link road, <a href="/wiki/Schw%C3%A4bisch_Gm%C3%BCnd" title="Schwäbisch Gmünd">Schwäbisch Gmünd</a> Way (formerly Interchange Way), which was renamed in honour of Barnsley's twin town in Germany. That enables buses to enter or leave the Interchange quickly, without having to use the busy level crossing at Jumble Lane.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_Yorkshire" title="Stagecoach Yorkshire">Stagecoach Yorkshire</a> operate most of the services from the Interchange, and Waterson's also operating services from the station. 
Stagecoach operate local routes throughout Barnsley and also to Wakefield, Pontefract, Doncaster, Rotherham, Sheffield and Leeds. Local routes go as far north as Wooley and as far south as Elsecar.
</p>
