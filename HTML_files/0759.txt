	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Summits of the Americas</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Summits of the Americas</b> (<b>SOA</b>) are a series of international <a href="/wiki/Summit_(meeting)" title="Summit (meeting)">summit meetings</a> bringing together the leaders of countries in the <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_American_States" title="Organization of American States">Organization of American States</a> (OAS). All countries have sent representatives to all meetings except for <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>, who was expelled from the OAS under US pressure after the <a href="/wiki/Cuban_Revolution" title="Cuban Revolution">Cuban Revolution</a> of 1959. Cuba participated in the 7th Summit held in Panama in 2015 and sent its foreign minister to the subsequent 2018 summit.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  In the early 1990s, what were formerly <i>ad hoc</i> summits came to be institutionalized into a regular "Summit of the Americas" based on the principles of democracy and free trade.<sup id="cite_ref-twaddle382_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twaddle382-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The meetings, organized by a number of multilateral bodies led by the <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_American_States" title="Organization of American States">Organization of American States</a>, provide an opportunity for discussions about a variety of issues and topics.
</p>
