	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Afro-American religion</h1>

<p><b>Afro-American religion</b> (also known as <b>African diasporic religions</b>) are a number of related <a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">religions</a> that developed in the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a> in various nations of <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin America</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, and in the state of <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> in the Southern United States. They derive from <a href="/wiki/Traditional_African_religion" class="mw-redirect" title="Traditional African religion">traditional African religions</a> with some influence from other religious traditions, notably <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Afro-American_religion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Afro-American religions involve <a href="/wiki/Veneration_of_the_dead" title="Veneration of the dead">veneration of the dead</a>, and include a <a href="/wiki/Creator_deity" title="Creator deity">creator deity</a> along with a <a href="/wiki/Pantheon_(religion)" title="Pantheon (religion)">pantheon</a> of divine <a href="/wiki/Spirit" title="Spirit">spirits</a> such as the <a href="/wiki/Orisha" title="Orisha">Orisha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loa" title="Loa">Loa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nkisi" title="Nkisi">Nkisi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alusi" title="Alusi">Alusi</a>, among others. In addition to the <a href="/wiki/Religious_syncretism" title="Religious syncretism">religious syncretism</a> of these various African traditions, many also incorporate elements of <a href="/wiki/Folk_Catholicism" title="Folk Catholicism">Folk Catholicism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Native_American_religion" title="Native American religion">Native American religion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spiritism" title="Spiritism">Spiritism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spiritualism" title="Spiritualism">Spiritualism</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_folklore" title="European folklore">European folklore</a>.
</p><p>Various "doctoring" spiritual traditions also exist such as <a href="/wiki/Obeah" title="Obeah">Obeah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hoodoo_(folk_magic)" title="Hoodoo (folk magic)">Hoodoo</a> which focus on spiritual health.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> African religious traditions in the Americas can vary. They can have non-prominent African roots or can be almost wholly African in nature, such as religions like <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_Orisha" title="Trinidad Orisha">Trinidad Orisha</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
