<p><b>Prince Leopold, Duke of Albany</b>, <span class="noexcerpt" style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Knight_of_the_Order_of_the_Garter" class="mw-redirect" title="Knight of the Order of the Garter">KG</a><span style="font-size:14px;">&#44;</span>&#32;<a href="/wiki/Knight_Companion_of_the_Order_of_the_Thistle" class="mw-redirect" title="Knight Companion of the Order of the Thistle">KT</a><span style="font-size:14px;">&#44;</span>&#32;<a href="/wiki/Knight_Grand_Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_Star_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Knight Grand Commander of the Order of the Star of India">GCSI</a><span style="font-size:14px;">&#44;</span>&#32;<a href="/wiki/Knight_Grand_Cross_of_the_Order_of_St_Michael_and_St_George" class="mw-redirect" title="Knight Grand Cross of the Order of St Michael and St George">GCMG</a><span style="font-size:14px;">&#44;</span>&#32;<a href="/wiki/Bailiff_Grand_Cross_of_the_Order_of_St_John" class="mw-redirect" title="Bailiff Grand Cross of the Order of St John">GCStJ</a></span> (Leopold George Duncan Albert; 7 April 1853&#160;&#8211;&#32;28 March 1884) was the eighth child and youngest son of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albert,_Prince_Consort" title="Albert, Prince Consort">Prince Albert</a>. Leopold was later created <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Albany" title="Duke of Albany">Duke of Albany</a>, Earl of Clarence, and Baron Arklow. He had <a href="/wiki/Haemophilia" title="Haemophilia">haemophilia</a>, which led to his death at the age of 30.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany_by_W%26D_Downey,_1868.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Prince_Leopold%2C_Duke_of_Albany_by_W%26D_Downey%2C_1868.png/200px-Prince_Leopold%2C_Duke_of_Albany_by_W%26D_Downey%2C_1868.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="336" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Prince_Leopold%2C_Duke_of_Albany_by_W%26D_Downey%2C_1868.png/300px-Prince_Leopold%2C_Duke_of_Albany_by_W%26D_Downey%2C_1868.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a8/Prince_Leopold%2C_Duke_of_Albany_by_W%26D_Downey%2C_1868.png 2x" data-file-width="366" data-file-height="615" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany_by_W%26D_Downey,_1868.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Prince Leopold in 1868</div></div></div>
<p>Leopold was born on 7 April 1853 at <a href="/wiki/Buckingham_Palace" title="Buckingham Palace">Buckingham Palace</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, the eighth child and youngest son of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen Victoria of the United Kingdom">Queen Victoria of the United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince_Albert_of_Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Albert of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha">Prince Albert of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha</a>. During labour, Queen Victoria chose to use <a href="/wiki/Chloroform" title="Chloroform">chloroform</a> and thus sanctioned the use of <a href="/wiki/Anesthesia" title="Anesthesia">anesthesia</a> in childbirth, recently developed by Professor <a href="/wiki/James_Young_Simpson" title="James Young Simpson">James Young Simpson</a>. The chloroform was administered by <a href="/wiki/John_Snow_(physician)" class="mw-redirect" title="John Snow (physician)">John Snow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As a son of the British sovereign, the newborn was styled <i>His Royal Highness The Prince Leopold</i> at birth. His parents named him Leopold after their common uncle, <a href="/wiki/Leopold_I_of_Belgium" title="Leopold I of Belgium">King Leopold I of Belgium</a>.
</p><p>He was baptised in the Private Chapel of Buckingham Palace on 28 June 1853 by the <a href="/wiki/Archbishop_of_Canterbury" title="Archbishop of Canterbury">Archbishop of Canterbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Bird_Sumner" title="John Bird Sumner">John Bird Sumner</a>. His godparents were his first cousin once removed, <a href="/wiki/George_V_of_Hanover" title="George V of Hanover">King George V of Hanover</a>; his fourth cousin once removed, <a href="/wiki/Augusta_of_Saxe-Weimar-Eisenach" title="Augusta of Saxe-Weimar-Eisenach">Princess William of Prussia</a>; his first cousin once removed, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Mary_Adelaide_of_Cambridge" title="Princess Mary Adelaide of Cambridge">Princess Mary Adelaide of Cambridge</a>; and his maternal uncle by marriage, <a href="/wiki/Ernst_I,_Prince_of_Hohenlohe-Langenburg" title="Ernst I, Prince of Hohenlohe-Langenburg">Prince Ernst of Hohenlohe-Langenburg</a>.
</p><p>Leopold <a href="/wiki/Haemophilia_in_European_royalty" title="Haemophilia in European royalty">inherited the disease haemophilia</a> from his mother, Queen Victoria, and was a delicate child.  There was speculation during his life that Leopold also suffered mildly from epilepsy,<sup id="cite_ref-Nelson_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nelson-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> like his grand-nephew <a href="/wiki/Prince_John_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prince John of the United Kingdom">Prince John</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education_and_career">Education and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Prince's intellectual abilities were evident as a boy; <a href="/wiki/Poet_Laureate" class="mw-redirect" title="Poet Laureate">Poet Laureate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alfred,_Lord_Tennyson" title="Alfred, Lord Tennyson">Alfred, Lord Tennyson</a> and his friend, philosopher <a href="/wiki/James_Martineau" title="James Martineau">James Martineau</a>, were familiar with the Queen's children and had noted that Leopold, who had often "conversed with the eminent Dr. Martineau, was considered to be  a young man of a very thoughtful mind, high aims, and quite remarkable acquirements".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Oxford_University">Oxford University</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Oxford University">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1872, Prince Leopold entered <a href="/wiki/Christ_Church,_Oxford" title="Christ Church, Oxford">Christ Church, Oxford</a>, where he studied a variety of subjects and became <a href="/wiki/List_of_officers_of_the_Oxford_University_Chess_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="List of officers of the Oxford University Chess Club">president</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Chess_Club" title="Oxford University Chess Club">Oxford University Chess Club</a>. On <a href="/wiki/Coming_of_age" title="Coming of age">coming of age</a> in 1874, he had been made a <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">privy councillor</a> and granted an <a href="/wiki/Annuity" title="Annuity">annuity</a> of £15,000.<sup id="cite_ref-EB1911_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He left the university with an honorary doctorate in civil law (DCL) in 1876, then travelled in Europe. In 1880, he toured <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> with his sister, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Louise,_Duchess_of_Argyll" title="Princess Louise, Duchess of Argyll">Princess Louise</a>, whose husband <a href="/wiki/John_Campbell,_9th_Duke_of_Argyll" title="John Campbell, 9th Duke of Argyll">John Campbell, Marquess of Lorne</a>, was <a href="/wiki/Governor_General_of_Canada" title="Governor General of Canada">Governor General of Canada</a>.   He was a prominent patron of chess, and the <a href="/wiki/London_1883_chess_tournament" title="London 1883 chess tournament">London 1883 chess tournament</a> was held under his patronage.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  Incapable of pursuing a military career because of his haemophilia and the need to avoid even minor injuries, Leopold instead became a patron of the arts and literature and served as an unofficial secretary to his mother. "Leopold was the favourite son, and through him her relations with the Government of the day were usually kept up."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>   Later he pursued vice-regal appointments in Canada and the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Colony of Victoria</a>, but his mother refused to appoint him, to his great unhappiness.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Army">British Army</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: British Army">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Despite his inability (through illness) to pursue an active military role, he had an honorary association with the <a href="/wiki/72nd_Regiment,_Duke_of_Albany%27s_Own_Highlanders" title="72nd Regiment, Duke of Albany&#39;s Own Highlanders">72nd Regiment, Duke of Albany's Own Highlanders</a>, and from 1881 served as the first <a href="/wiki/Colonel-in-Chief" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonel-in-Chief">Colonel-in-Chief</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Seaforth_Highlanders" title="Seaforth Highlanders">Seaforth Highlanders</a>, when that regiment was formed through the merger of the 72nd regiment with the <a href="/wiki/78th_(Highlanders)_Regiment_of_Foot" title="78th (Highlanders) Regiment of Foot">78th (Highlanders) Regiment of Foot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A portrait of Prince Leopold in military uniform is held in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Collection" title="Royal Collection">Royal Collection</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The Seaforth Highlanders paraded at Prince Leopold's funeral, a fact recorded by <a href="/wiki/William_McGonagall" title="William McGonagall">William McGonagall</a> in his poem "The Death of Prince Leopold".<sup id="cite_ref-mcg_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mcg-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Freemasonry">Freemasonry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Freemasonry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prince Leopold was an active <a href="/wiki/Freemason" class="mw-redirect" title="Freemason">Freemason</a>, being initiated in the <a href="/wiki/Apollo_University_Lodge" title="Apollo University Lodge">Apollo University Lodge</a>, Oxford, whilst resident at Christ Church. He was proposed for membership by his brother, <a href="/wiki/Edward_VII" title="Edward VII">Albert Edward, Prince of Wales</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> who was at the time the Worshipful Master of the Lodge,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and was initiated in a joint ceremony with Robert Hawthorne Collins, his friend and tutor, who later became <a href="/wiki/Comptroller_of_the_Household" title="Comptroller of the Household">Comptroller</a> of his Household.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He served as Master of the Lodge from 1876-1877, and was later the <a href="/wiki/Provincial_Grand_Master" title="Provincial Grand Master">Provincial Grand Master</a> for Oxfordshire, still holding that office at the time of his death.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Duke_of_Albany">Duke of Albany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Duke of Albany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prince Leopold was created <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Albany" title="Duke of Albany">Duke of Albany</a>, Earl of Clarence and Baron <a href="/wiki/Arklow" title="Arklow">Arklow</a> on 24 May 1881.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-creation_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-creation-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage">Marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prince Leopold, stifled by the desire of his mother, Queen Victoria, to keep him at home, saw marriage as his only hope of independence. Due to his haemophilia, he had difficulty finding a wife.  Heiress <a href="/wiki/Daisy_Greville,_Countess_of_Warwick" title="Daisy Greville, Countess of Warwick">Daisy Maynard</a> was one of the women he considered as a possible bride.<sup id="cite_ref-Zeepvat_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zeepvat-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He was acquainted with <a href="/wiki/Alice_Liddell" title="Alice Liddell">Alice Liddell</a>, the daughter of the Vice-Chancellor of Oxford for whom <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Carroll" title="Lewis Carroll">Lewis Carroll</a> wrote <i><a href="/wiki/Alice%27s_Adventures_in_Wonderland" title="Alice&#39;s Adventures in Wonderland">Alice's Adventures in Wonderland</a></i>, and was godfather of Alice's second son, who was named after him. It has been suggested that he considered marrying her, though others suggest that he preferred her sister Edith (for whom he later served as <a href="/wiki/Pall-bearer" class="mw-redirect" title="Pall-bearer">pall-bearer</a> on 30 June 1876).<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Leopold also considered his second cousin <a href="/wiki/Princess_Frederica_of_Hanover" title="Princess Frederica of Hanover">Princess Frederica of Hanover</a> for a bride; they instead became lifelong friends and confidantes.<sup id="cite_ref-Zeepvat_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zeepvat-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>  Other aristocratic women he pursued included <a href="/wiki/Victoria_of_Baden" title="Victoria of Baden">Victoria of Baden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Princess_St%C3%A9phanie_of_Belgium" title="Princess Stéphanie of Belgium">Princess Stéphanie of Belgium</a>, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Elisabeth_of_Hesse-Kassel" title="Princess Elisabeth of Hesse-Kassel">Princess Elisabeth of Hesse-Kassel</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Princess_Karoline_Mathilde_of_Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Augustenburg" title="Princess Karoline Mathilde of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Augustenburg">Princess Karoline Mathilde of Schleswig-Holstein-Sonderburg-Augustenburg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Zeepvat_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zeepvat-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Leopold was very fond of Mary Baring, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Bingham_Baring,_2nd_Baron_Ashburton" title="Bingham Baring, 2nd Baron Ashburton">Lord Ashburton</a>, but, though she was very fond of him too, at 19, she felt she was too young to marry.<sup id="cite_ref-Zeepvat_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zeepvat-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After rejection from these women, Victoria stepped in to bar what she saw as unsuitable possibilities. Insisting that the children of British monarchs should marry into other reigning <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestant</a> families, Victoria suggested a meeting with <a href="/wiki/Princess_Helena_of_Waldeck_and_Pyrmont" title="Princess Helena of Waldeck and Pyrmont">Princess Helena Friederike</a>, the daughter of <a href="/wiki/George_Victor,_Prince_of_Waldeck_and_Pyrmont" title="George Victor, Prince of Waldeck and Pyrmont">Georg Viktor</a>, reigning Prince of <a href="/wiki/Waldeck_(state)" class="mw-redirect" title="Waldeck (state)">Waldeck-Pyrmont</a>, one of whose daughters had already married <a href="/wiki/William_III_of_the_Netherlands" title="William III of the Netherlands">King William III of the Netherlands</a>. On 27 April 1882, Leopold and Helena were married, at <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s_Chapel,_Windsor_Castle" class="mw-redirect" title="St. George&#39;s Chapel, Windsor Castle">St George's Chapel</a> at <a href="/wiki/Windsor_Castle" title="Windsor Castle">Windsor Castle</a>, and his income was raised by parliament to £25,000.<sup id="cite_ref-EB1911_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EB1911-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Leopold and Helena enjoyed a happy (although brief) marriage. In 1883, Leopold became a father when his wife gave birth to a daughter, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Alice,_Countess_of_Athlone" title="Princess Alice, Countess of Athlone">Alice</a>. He died shortly before the birth of his son, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Edward,_Duke_of_Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha" title="Charles Edward, Duke of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha">Charles Edward</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Illness_and_death">Illness and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Illness and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prince Leopold had <a href="/wiki/Haemophilia" title="Haemophilia">haemophilia</a>, diagnosed in childhood and in early years had various physicians in permanent attendance, including Arnold Royle<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/John_Wickham_Legg" title="John Wickham Legg">John Wickham Legg</a>. In February 1884, Leopold went to <a href="/wiki/Cannes" title="Cannes">Cannes</a> on doctor's orders: joint pain is a common symptom of haemophilia and the winter climate in the United Kingdom was always difficult for him. His wife, pregnant at the time, stayed at home but urged him to go. On 27 March, at his Cannes residence, the 'Villa Nevada', he slipped and fell, injuring his knee and hitting his head. He died in the early hours of the next morning, apparently from a cerebral haemorrhage.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He was buried in the Albert Memorial Chapel at <a href="/wiki/St_George%27s_Chapel_at_Windsor_Castle" class="mw-redirect" title="St George&#39;s Chapel at Windsor Castle">Windsor</a>. The court observed official mourning from 30 March 1884 to 11 May 1884.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> His posthumous son, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Edward,_Duke_of_Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha" title="Charles Edward, Duke of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha">Prince Charles Edward</a>, succeeded him as 2nd <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Albany" title="Duke of Albany">Duke of Albany</a> upon birth four months later.
</p><p>Having died six years after his older sister Alice, Leopold was the second of Queen Victoria's children to die. At 30 years, he was also the shortest-lived of the cohort. His mother outlived him by seventeen years, by which time she had also outlived a third child, Alfred.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Leopold's passing was lamented by the Scottish "poet and tragedian" <a href="/wiki/William_McGonagall" title="William McGonagall">William McGonagall</a> in the poem "The Death of Prince Leopold".<sup id="cite_ref-mcg_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mcg-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1900, Leopold's son, Charles Edward, succeeded his uncle <a href="/wiki/Alfred,_Duke_of_Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha" title="Alfred, Duke of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha">Alfred</a> as <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha" class="mw-redirect" title="Duke of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha">Duke of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha</a>.
</p><p>Through Charles Edward, Leopold was the great-grandfather of <a href="/wiki/Carl_XVI_Gustaf_of_Sweden" title="Carl XVI Gustaf of Sweden">Carl XVI Gustaf</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_Sweden" title="Monarchy of Sweden">current King</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>.
</p><p>The haemophilia gene is carried on the <a href="/wiki/X_chromosome" title="X chromosome">X chromosome</a>, and is normally passed through female descent, as in the past few haemophiliac men survived to beget children. Any daughter of a haemophiliac is a carrier of the gene. Leopold's daughter Alice inherited the haemophilia gene, and passed it to her elder son <a href="/wiki/Rupert_Cambridge,_Viscount_Trematon" title="Rupert Cambridge, Viscount Trematon">Rupert</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arms">Arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1856, at the age of three, Prince Leopold was granted a personal coat of arms — the arms of the kingdom, with an inescutcheon of the shield of Saxony (representing his father), and all differenced by a label argent of three points, the first and third bearing hearts gules, and the second a cross gules.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
