<p><i><b>Carnival Ride</b></i> is the second studio album by American country music recording artist <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>. It was released in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> on October 23, 2007, by <a href="/wiki/Arista_Nashville" title="Arista Nashville">Arista Nashville</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-arista_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arista-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-msnbc01_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-msnbc01-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On this album, Underwood was more involved in the songwriting process; she set up a writers' retreat at Nashville's famed <a href="/wiki/Ryman_Auditorium" title="Ryman Auditorium">Ryman Auditorium</a> to collaborate with such Music Row tunesmiths as <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Craig_Wiseman" title="Craig Wiseman">Craig Wiseman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rivers_Rutherford" title="Rivers Rutherford">Rivers Rutherford</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gordie_Sampson" title="Gordie Sampson">Gordie Sampson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Carnival Ride</i> debuted at number one on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, selling over 527,000 copies and achieving one of the biggest ever first-week sales by a female artist. It was the singer's first album to debut at number one on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 and second to debut atop the <a href="/wiki/Top_Country_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Country Albums">Top Country Albums</a> chart. The album was certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_Certification" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA Certification">quadruple platinum</a>, it has sold 3.4 million copies in the United States, and four million copies worldwide.
</p><p>Five singles were released from the album — "<a href="/wiki/So_Small" title="So Small">So Small</a>", "<a href="/wiki/All-American_Girl_(song)" title="All-American Girl (song)">All-American Girl</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Last_Name_(song)" title="Last Name (song)">Last Name</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Just_a_Dream_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Just a Dream (Carrie Underwood song)">Just a Dream</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/I_Told_You_So_(Randy_Travis_song)" title="I Told You So (Randy Travis song)">I Told You So</a>". The first four of which reached No. 1 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart, thus making Underwood the first solo female artist to pull four number one's from one album since <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain" title="Shania Twain">Shania Twain</a> in 1995-1996. All singles were Top 30 hits on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart, with "I Told You So" climbing to number nine and both "So Small" and "Last Name" reaching the Top 20.
</p><p>The album and its songs were largely praised by music critics. Underwood won two <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a> - one for <a href="/wiki/Best_Female_Country_Vocal_Performance" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Female Country Vocal Performance">Best Female Country Vocal Performance</a> for "Last Name", at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Grammy Awards">2009 Grammy Awards</a>, and one for <a href="/wiki/Best_Country_Collaboration_with_Vocals" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Country Collaboration with Vocals">Best Country Collaboration with Vocals</a> for "I Told You So", at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Grammy Awards">2010 Grammy Awards</a>. <i>Carnival Ride</i> was nominated for Album of the Year at the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music Awards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a> and won the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="American Music Award">American Music Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Award_for_Favorite_Country_Album" title="American Music Award for Favorite Country Album">Favorite Country Album</a>, in 2008.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Ride&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood has explained the meaning behind the album's title and theme, saying:
</p>
<table class="cquote pullquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
<tbody><tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: &#39;Times New Roman&#39;, Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“
</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;"><i>You step onto this ride called life, and it’s a crazy thing you don’t know anything about, but you get on it anyway. You do what you can to lean different directions to try and get it to go where you want it to go, but you can’t stop it&#160;– it just keeps moving. That’s why Carnival Ride works as my album title, because it describes the wonderful craziness that I’ve been through over the past couple years</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-arista_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arista-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</td>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: &#39;Times New Roman&#39;, Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">”
</td></tr>

</tbody></table>
<p>Two of this album's tracks have been previously recorded by other artists. "Flat on the Floor" was previously cut by singer <a href="/wiki/Katrina_Elam" title="Katrina Elam">Katrina Elam</a> on her unreleased 2007 album <i>Turn Me Up</i>, and was a number 52 hit on the country charts for her that year. "<a href="/wiki/I_Told_You_So_(Randy_Travis_song)" title="I Told You So (Randy Travis song)">I Told You So</a>" is a cover of a song previously cut by <a href="/wiki/Randy_Travis" title="Randy Travis">Randy Travis</a> on his 1988 number one album <i><a href="/wiki/Always_%26_Forever_(Randy_Travis_album)" title="Always &amp; Forever (Randy Travis album)">Always &amp; Forever</a></i>. Travis' version of the song was a Number One hit on the country charts that year. Underwood and Travis recently released "I Told You So" as a duet single on iTunes, and they also performed it on the results show of the <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_8)" title="American Idol (season 8)">eighth season of <i>American Idol</i></a> during the Grand Ole Opry week. The duet later went on to win the 52nd Grammy Award for Best Country Collaboration with Vocals.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing_and_promotion">Marketing and promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Ride&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marketing and promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood promoted the record through the Carnival Ride Tour which ran through 2008. It started late January and ended on December 14, 2008. She performed over 137 live shows since the start of the tour, performing to over a million people.
</p><p>She debuted several of her singles at the <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music" title="Academy of Country Music">Academy of Country Music Awards</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a>.
</p><p>She also appeared on several shows to promote her album including <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Oprah_Winfrey_Show" title="The Oprah Winfrey Show">The Oprah Winfrey Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">Saturday Night Live</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Live_With_Regis_and_Kelly" class="mw-redirect" title="Live With Regis and Kelly">Live With Regis and Kelly</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Early_Show" title="The Early Show">The Early Show</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dick_Clark%27s_New_Year%27s_Rockin%27_Eve" title="Dick Clark&#39;s New Year&#39;s Rockin&#39; Eve">Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_View_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The View (U.S. TV series)">The View</a></i>, and at the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Ole_Opry" title="Grand Ole Opry">Grand Ole Opry</a>.
</p><p>A Limited Edition CD/DVD release was made available only at <a href="/wiki/Target_Corporation" title="Target Corporation">Target</a> stores. The <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> includes four live acoustic performances, as well as a four-part interview.
</p><p>Following this a Platinum MusicPass edition was released on January 15, 2008. This release included a previously unreleased track and two music videos.
</p><p>On October 21, 2008, a 2-disc set was released at <a href="/wiki/Wal-Mart" class="mw-redirect" title="Wal-Mart">Wal-Mart</a>. The second CD contains five Christmas tracks, of which all were available for radio download on September 29, 2008. One of the tracks, "<a href="/wiki/Do_You_Hear_What_I_Hear" class="mw-redirect" title="Do You Hear What I Hear">Do You Hear What I Hear</a>", was previously released on the 2007 album <i>Hear Something Country Christmas</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Ride&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/So_Small" title="So Small">So Small</a>" served as the lead single for the album. It was released in mid-September 2007, two months before the album's official release and debuted on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> at number 20, making it the highest chart debut by a solo country female artist in 43 years of Nielsen BDS history. It eventually held the number one spot for 3 consecutive weeks. It also became her fifth top 20 hit when it peaked at number 17 on U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>. "<a href="/wiki/So_Small" title="So Small">So Small</a>" has become a cross-over hit, selling over 1,088,000 downloads,<sup id="cite_ref-billboard.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and being certified Platinum.
</p><p>The second single, "<a href="/wiki/All-American_Girl_(song)" title="All-American Girl (song)">All-American Girl</a>", was released around December 2007. It also became a success by hitting number one on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot Country Songs where it stayed there for 2 weeks and on the Canadian Country Charts where it stayed there for 5 weeks. It also managed to be a top 30 single on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, where it peaked at number 27, making it her sixth top 30. As of 2015, "<a href="/wiki/All-American_Girl_(song)" title="All-American Girl (song)">All-American Girl</a>" sold 1,800,000 copies in the United States. The song was certified 2x Platinum.
</p><p>The third single, "<a href="/wiki/Last_Name_(song)" title="Last Name (song)">Last Name</a>", became Underwood's fastest single to hit number one on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot Country Songs after only 13 weeks of its official release around April 2008. It stayed there for one week. It is also Underwood's sixth top 20 hit, reaching number 19 on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. The song also won Underwood her third consecutive <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy award</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Female_Country_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance">Best Female Country Vocal Performance</a> category. As of November 2015, the song has sold 1,300,000 copies<sup id="cite_ref-billboard.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The fourth single, "<a href="/wiki/Just_a_Dream_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Just a Dream (Carrie Underwood song)">Just a Dream</a>", reached Number One on the country charts for the chart week of November 8, 2008 and stayed there for 2 weeks, thus making Underwood the first solo female artist to pull four number one's from one album since <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain" title="Shania Twain">Shania Twain</a> did it with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Woman_in_Me_(Shania_Twain_album)" title="The Woman in Me (Shania Twain album)">The Woman in Me</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceC_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It became her seventh Number One single on the U.S. Billboard <i>Hot Country Songs</i> chart and eighth Number One country single overall. It also managed to reach number 29 on U.S. <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100, becoming her 10th top 30 there. The song also gave Underwood her fourth consecutive <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy award</a> nomination in the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Female_Country_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance">Best Female Country Vocal Performance</a> category. The song was certified Platinum on the week ending on September 4, 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> giving Underwood her 7th Platinum hit. As of November 2015, it sold 1,280,000 copies.
</p><p>The fifth single, "<a href="/wiki/I_Told_You_So_(Randy_Travis_song)" title="I Told You So (Randy Travis song)">I Told You So</a>" was officially released on February 2, 2009. It became Underwood's fourth top 10 all-genre hit, peaking at number nine on the U.S <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100. On the week of April 10, it climbed to the top of the Canadian Country Charts and stayed there for one week. The song peaked at number two on the Hot Country Songs chart, making it only her second country single to not reach number one on that chart after "<a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Forget_To_Remember_Me" class="mw-redirect" title="Don&#39;t Forget To Remember Me">Don't Forget To Remember Me</a>" which also peaked at number two. Underwood rerecorded the song with original artist <a href="/wiki/Randy_Travis" title="Randy Travis">Randy Travis</a>, and the song won her and Travis the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for Best Country Collaboration with Vocals. As of January 2013, the song has been certified Platinum. It has sold 1,089,000 copies as of November 2015<sup id="cite_ref-billboard.com_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><i>Carnival Ride</i> received mostly positive reviews from music critics. On the music review aggregator Metacritic, it has received an average score of 72 out of 100 based on ten reviews indicating generally favorable reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-Critic_Reviews_for_Carnival_Ride_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Critic_Reviews_for_Carnival_Ride-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first official review of the album from <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> gave it 4 out of 5 stars, on par with what they gave her debut. The site classified the album as "completely contemporary country", and said "the remarkable thing about Carnival Ride is that it's stronger song for song than Some Hearts." They also praised the album for having "the appearance of a genuine heart, something that no other big country-pop album has had since the glory days of <a href="/wiki/Come_On_Over_(Shania_Twain_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Come On Over (Shania Twain album)">Come On Over</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-AllMusic_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> also praised the album for its versatility saying "The songs call for vulnerability (You Won’t Find This), urgency (Flat on the Floor), sympathy (Crazy Dreams, her co-written salute to “the hairbrush singers and dashboard drummers” from whose ranks she sprang), humor (The More Boys I Meet, the tag line of which goes “The more I love my dog”) and extreme role-playing (Last Name’s saga of a bar pickup that turns into an impulsive Vegas marriage). She delivers on all counts."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Herald" title="Boston Herald">Boston Herald</a></i> gave the album a B, and claimed, "Underwood manages enough spunk to occasionally avoid the cookie-cutter, especially with the curious beat-box-meets-banjo arrangement of "Get Out of This Town" and "Just a Dream," a bona fide [tearjerker] about a young war widow."<sup id="cite_ref-Boston_Herald_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boston_Herald-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carnival_Ride&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Carnival Ride</i> became Underwood's first number-one album on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, selling 527,000 copies in its first week of release.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It achieved one of the biggest ever first-week sales by a female artist at that time.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In its second week the album sold less than 190,000 copies. The album also debuted at number 1 on the Top Digital Albums, Top Country Albums, and Top Canadian Albums charts. The album has sold 3,400,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_Album_sales_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_Album_sales-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In October 2016, the album was certified <a href="/wiki/RIAA_Certification" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA Certification">4x platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) for combined sales and <a href="/wiki/Album-equivalent_unit" title="Album-equivalent unit">album-equivalent units</a> of four million units.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_album_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA_album-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It appeared on the <i>Billboard</i> Year-End Charts for 2009 at number 74.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
