	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Legal name</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Legal name</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Name" title="Name">name</a> that identifies a person for legal, administrative and other official purposes. A person's first legal name generally is the name of the person that was given for the purpose of <a href="/wiki/Civil_registry" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil registry">registration of the birth</a> and which then appears on a <a href="/wiki/Birth_certificate" title="Birth certificate">birth certificate</a> (see <i><a href="/wiki/Birth_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Birth name">birth name</a></i>), but may change subsequently. Most <a href="/wiki/Jurisdiction" title="Jurisdiction">jurisdictions</a> require the use of a legal name for all legal and administrative purposes, and some jurisdictions permit or require a <a href="/wiki/Name_change" title="Name change">name change</a> to be recorded at <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">marriage</a>. The legal name may need to be used on various government issued documents (e.g., a court order). The term is also used when an individual changes their first or full name, typically after reaching a certain legal age (usually eighteen or over, though it can be as low as fourteen in several European nations).
</p><p>A person's legal name typically is the same as their <a href="/wiki/Personal_name" title="Personal name">personal name</a>, comprising a <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Family_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Family name">family name</a>. The order varies according to culture and country. There are also country-by-country differences on changes of legal names by marriage. (See <i><a href="/wiki/Married_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Married name">married name</a></i>.)
</p><p>Most countries require by law the registration of a name for newborn children, and some can refuse registration of "undesirable" names. In 1991, a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> couple refused to give their newborn a legal name, in protest of existing naming laws. In 1996, they were fined for not registering a name for their child for five years, after they unsuccessfully tried to register the child's name as <a href="/wiki/Brfxxccxxmnpcccclllmmnprxvclmnckssqlbb11116" class="mw-redirect" title="Brfxxccxxmnpcccclllmmnprxvclmnckssqlbb11116">Brfxxccxxmnpcccclllmmnprxvclmnckssqlbb11116</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and then as "A".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Jurisdiction_by_country">Jurisdiction by country</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Jurisdiction by country">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">Canada</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Canada">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Quebec">Quebec</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Quebec">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Civil_Code_of_Quebec" title="Civil Code of Quebec">Civil Code of Quebec</a></i> states that "Every person exercises his civil rights under the name assigned to him and stated in his act of birth,"<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and spouses retain their legal names upon marriage.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> They can be changed, upon registration, only under prescribed conditions, and only where the person is a Canadian citizen who has been domiciled in Québec for at least one year.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Germany, names are regulated to a large extent. Apart from possibly <a href="/wiki/Married_name#Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Married name">adopting the partner's name</a> upon marriage, German citizens may only change their name for a recognised important reason. Among other reasons, a change of names is permitted when the name can give rise to confusion, ridicule, unusual orthographic difficulties, or stigmatization. In certain situations, children's last names may also be changed to their natural, foster or adoptive parent's last name. Transgender people may change their first names. Foreign names in writing systems that are not based on Latin are transliterated according to rules which may conflict with the system of transcribing or transliterating names that is used in the country of origin. Former titles of nobility became integrated into the last names in 1919 but continue to be adapted according to gender and other circumstances.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom">United Kingdom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: United Kingdom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the UK, businesses that trade under names other than those of the owner or a corporate entity must display the name of owner and an address at which documents may be served, or the name and registered number of the corporate body and its registered address. The requirements apply to sole traders and partnerships, but there are special provisions for large partnerships where listing all partners would be onerous. 
</p><p>The information must be shown on any trading premises where the public have access to trade and in documents such as order forms, receipts and, as of January 2007, corporate websites (to be extended later in 2007 to sole trader websites).
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="England_and_Wales">England and Wales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: England and Wales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In strict English law, if there is such a thing as a "<i>legal</i>&#8202;" surname, it is easily changed. In the words of <i>A dictionary of American and English law</i>, "Any one may take on himself whatever surname or as many surnames as he pleases, without statutory licence".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This does not always seem to have applied to names given in <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptism</a>. As noted by <a href="/wiki/Edward_Coke" title="Edward Coke">Sir Edward Coke</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Institutes_of_the_Lawes_of_England" title="Institutes of the Lawes of England">Institutes of the Lawes of England</a></i>, "a man may have divers names at divers times, but not divers Christian names."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  But in modern practice all names are freely changeable.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Changes of name are usually effected through <a href="/wiki/Deed_of_change_of_name" title="Deed of change of name">deed poll</a>, optionally enrolled either at the <a href="/wiki/High_Court_of_Justice" title="High Court of Justice">High Court of Justice</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> or at the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Arms" title="College of Arms">College of Arms</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-CONCOA_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CONCOA-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> with a notice recorded in <i><a href="/wiki/The_London_Gazette" title="The London Gazette">The London Gazette</a></i>. Changes may also be made by means of a Royal License obtained through the College of Arms, with similar notice.<sup id="cite_ref-CONCOA_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CONCOA-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> These enrolment, licence and notice procedures are useful for having the new name appear in official documents.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Scotland">Scotland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Scotland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Scots Law allows anyone who wishes to do so to change their forename(s) or surname and such changes may be recorded in the official register held by the <a href="/wiki/National_Records_of_Scotland" title="National Records of Scotland">National Records of Scotland</a>.  Technically the Registrar General makes a correction to the entry.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  A correction can be recorded where a birth has been registered in Scotland, or where a person is the subject in Scotland of an entry in the Adopted Children Register, the Parental Order Register or the Gender Recognition Register.<sup id="cite_ref-NRS_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NRS-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The above formalities are not necessary where a spouse/partner assumes the other spouse/partner's surname upon marriage or civil partnership, or reverts back to their original name upon separation, divorce or dissolution of the civil partnership.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Only one change of name is allowed in the register where a person has not yet reached the age of 16, and afterwards only one change of forename and three changes of surname may be granted during a person's lifetime, provided that at least five years have passed between changes of surname.<sup id="cite_ref-NRS_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NRS-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Name changes may also be recorded where:
</p>
<ul><li>a decree or certificate has been granted by the <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lyon_King_of_Arms" title="Lord Lyon King of Arms">Lord Lyon King of Arms</a>,</li>
<li>a certified copy of a <a href="/wiki/Will_(law)" class="mw-redirect" title="Will (law)">will</a>, <a href="/wiki/Settlement_(trust)" title="Settlement (trust)">settlement</a> or <a href="/wiki/Trust_instrument" title="Trust instrument">deed of trust</a> is produced, containing a condition that the person concerned takes a name different from his or her registered name, or</li>
<li>an alternative forename or surname is used where the registered name is not in an English-language form.<sup id="cite_ref-NRS_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NRS-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_Ireland">Northern Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Northern Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Anyone born or adopted in Northern Ireland is able to change their name with the <a href="/wiki/General_Register_Office_(Northern_Ireland)" title="General Register Office (Northern Ireland)">General Register Office of Northern Ireland</a> in the following circumstances:<sup id="cite_ref-GRONI_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GRONI-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>a child's birth registration can be re-registered where its parents have subsequently married, or where a father that had not previously been identified is subsequently entered (whether by declaration, agreement or court order);</li>
<li>a child's name can be changed once, before the age of 18, upon application by the parents (or adoptive parents, or guardian where the parents are deceased, as the case may be);</li>
<li>a person aged 18 or over may apply to register one change of forename and three changes of surname during that person's lifetime, provided that at least five years have passed between changes of surname.</li></ul>
<p>A deed poll can also be used in Northern Ireland for this purpose.<sup id="cite_ref-GRONI_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GRONI-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Legal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most states in the United States follow the <a href="/wiki/Common_law" title="Common law">common law</a> which permits <a href="/wiki/Name_change#United_States" title="Name change">name changing</a> for non-fraudulent purposes. This is actually the most common method, since most women who marry do not petition a court under the statutorily prescribed method, but simply use a new name (typically the husband's, a custom which started under the theory of <a href="/wiki/Coverture" title="Coverture">coverture</a> where a woman lost her identity and most rights when she married).<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Most <a href="/wiki/List_of_courts_of_the_United_States" title="List of courts of the United States">state courts</a> have held that a legally assumed name (i.e., for a non-fraudulent purpose) is a legal name and usable as their true name, though assumed names are often not considered the person's technically true name.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <b>legal name</b> of a business is the name under which the business conducts its operations.
</p>
