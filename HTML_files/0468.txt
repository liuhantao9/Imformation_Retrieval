<p><b>Rudi André Kamperveen</b> (27 September 1924 – 8 December 1982) was a <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Surinamese</a> football player, sports administrator, politician and businessman.
</p><p>During his playing career, the centre forward represented and captained the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_football_team" title="Suriname national football team">Suriname national football team</a> in the 1940s. He played professionally in Brazil (<a href="/wiki/Paysandu_Sport_Club" title="Paysandu Sport Club">Paysandu Sport Club</a>) and Netherlands during his playing career, becoming the first Surinamese player to play professionally in the Netherlands in the process while plying his trade for <a href="/wiki/HFC_Haarlem" title="HFC Haarlem">HFC Haarlem</a>.
</p><p>After his playing career he became Minister for Sport in Suriname. He also helped establish the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Football_Union" title="Caribbean Football Union">Caribbean Football Union</a> which was formed in 1978 and he was selected as the union's first President. He was also a vice-president of <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>.
</p><p>He was killed in 1982 as part of the <a href="/wiki/December_murders" title="December murders">December murders</a>. His body reportedly showed injuries to the jaw and a swollen face, 18 bullet wounds in the chest, a shot wound in the right temple, a fractured femur and a fractured arm.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF" title="CONCACAF">CONCACAF</a> hall of fame.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen_Stadion" title="André Kamperveen Stadion">André Kamperveen Stadion</a> is named in his honour.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Known as Suriname's Man of the People, Kamperveen or 'Ampie' as he was more commonly called was born 27 September 1924 in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Surinam_(Dutch_colony)" title="Surinam (Dutch colony)">Surinam</a>. Having pursued <a href="/wiki/Sport_of_athletics" title="Sport of athletics">athletics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Judo" title="Judo">judo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> was his first love and the sport he was best at. He started playing as an 11-year-old on the Dr. Sophie Redmond Straat and signs of potential were noticeable at an early age. In 1942 he was called up for <a href="/wiki/Military_service" title="Military service">military service</a> at the age of 18.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Association_football_career">Association football career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Association football career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="MVV">MVV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: MVV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Kamperveen joined the military club <a href="/wiki/Sportvereniging_Nationaal_Leger" title="Sportvereniging Nationaal Leger">MVV</a> during his service, competing in the <a href="/wiki/SVB_Hoofdklasse" title="SVB Hoofdklasse">Hoofdklasse</a>, the top flight of football in Suriname. He was a crowd favorite, scoring many goals and helping MVV to win a national championship in 1948.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Paysandu_SC">Paysandu SC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Paysandu SC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 1950, he joined <a href="/wiki/Paysandu_Sport_Club" title="Paysandu Sport Club">Paysandu Sport Club</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bel%C3%A9m" title="Belém">Belém</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> where he signed on a 1-year contract. He became the first professional football player from Suriname, joining a club that was used to winning, this proved to be a big change for Kamperveen, where he learned <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Portuguese" title="Brazilian Portuguese">Brazilian Portuguese</a> in order to communicate with his teammates.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 1952 a Caribbean All-Stars team came to Suriname to play exhibition matches at the <a href="/wiki/Andre_Kamperveen_Stadion" class="mw-redirect" title="Andre Kamperveen Stadion">Cultuurtuinlaan</a>. Kamperveen was called up to play for the Caribbean All-Star team together with Michel Kruin and <a href="/wiki/Humphrey_Mijnals" title="Humphrey Mijnals">Humphrey Mijnals</a> from Suriname. He scored two goals in the opening match for the Caribbean All-Stars against the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_football_team" title="Suriname national football team">Suriname national team</a> in a 2–2 draw. Two more matches were played against the national team, which ended in a draw and a loss for the All-Stars, while two additional matches played were lost to <a href="/wiki/S.V._Robinhood" title="S.V. Robinhood">S.V. Robinhood</a> and <a href="/wiki/SV_Voorwaarts,_Suriname" title="SV Voorwaarts, Suriname">S.V. Voorwaarts</a>. It is worth mentioning that Kamperveen had already played for the Suriname national team prior to being called up for the Caribbean regional team.<sup id="cite_ref-voetbal_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-voetbal-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="HFC_Haarlem">HFC Haarlem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: HFC Haarlem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 1954, Kamperveen relocated to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> to study at the <a href="https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/CIOS" class="extiw" title="nl:CIOS">CIOS</a> in <a href="/wiki/Overveen" title="Overveen">Overveen</a>. Aside from football he took classes in <a href="/wiki/Boxing" title="Boxing">boxing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Judo" title="Judo">judo</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_ju-jutsu" title="German ju-jutsu">jiu-jitsu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">basketball</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After completion of his studies he attended the Sporthochschule in <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> During his period in the Netherlands he signed with <a href="/wiki/HFC_Haarlem" title="HFC Haarlem">HFC Haarlem</a> competing in the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Netherlands Football League Championship</a> and in the <a href="/wiki/KNVB_Cup" title="KNVB Cup">KNVB Cup</a> and becoming the first Surinamese player to compete in the Netherlands.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He scored two goals against <a href="/wiki/RCH_(football_club)" title="RCH (football club)">Racing Club Heemstede</a> on 19 August 1956 in a 2–1 win in the first round of the <a href="/wiki/KNVB_Cup" title="KNVB Cup">KNVB Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Haarlem were however eliminated in the second round by <a href="/wiki/AZ_(football_club)" class="mw-redirect" title="AZ (football club)">Alkmaar '54</a>. That same year, Kamperveen graduated with an A-level diploma from the <a href="/wiki/KNVB" class="mw-redirect" title="KNVB">KNVB</a>, the highest level diploma issued by the Dutch governing body of football. At the end of the season he returned to Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Suriname">Suriname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Suriname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Kamperveen played for the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_football_team" title="Suriname national football team">Suriname national team</a>. He made his debut in 1945 at the age of 21.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Playing as a <a href="/wiki/Forward_(association_football)" title="Forward (association football)">center forward</a>, he earned the captain armband in his first year with the team.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 1946 the national team travelled to <a href="/wiki/Willemstad" title="Willemstad">Willemstad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a> to participate in a tournament with <a href="/wiki/Territory_of_Cura%C3%A7ao_national_football_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Territory of Curaçao national football team">Curaçao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aruba_national_football_team" title="Aruba national football team">Aruba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colombia_national_football_team" title="Colombia national football team">Colombia</a> and Dutch club <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a>. Suriname were able to draw 1–1 with Colombia in their opening match, losing to Curaçao 6–0, and suffering record loses to Aruba and Feyenoord (8–1). The tournament was won by Feyenoord.<sup id="cite_ref-voetbal_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-voetbal-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On 2 February 1947 he scored four goals in a 9–0 win over <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana_national_football_team" title="French Guiana national football team">French Guiana</a>, the record highest win recorded by the Suriname national team.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 1948, Kamperveen travelled to the Netherlands with the Suriname national team, the team's first ever trip to Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-voetbal_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-voetbal-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Kamperveen scored a goal in the opening match, which saw the national team play <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> at the <a href="/wiki/De_Meer_Stadion" title="De Meer Stadion">De Meer Stadion</a> ending in a 2–2 draw.<sup id="cite_ref-voetbal_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-voetbal-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Additional games were played against Feyenoord, Tilburg XI, <a href="/wiki/FC_Dordrecht" title="FC Dordrecht">DFC</a> and Den Haag XI. Kamperveen scored four goals on the tour in total.<sup id="cite_ref-voetbal_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-voetbal-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1951, the Suriname national team played a series of friendly matches against <a href="/wiki/Clube_N%C3%A1utico_Capibaribe" title="Clube Náutico Capibaribe">Náutico</a> from <a href="/wiki/Recife" title="Recife">Recife</a>, Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-voetbal_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-voetbal-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Kamperveen scored once in two matches played.<sup id="cite_ref-voetbal_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-voetbal-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On 1 August 1954 the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> played Suriname at the newly built <a href="/wiki/Andre_Kamperveen_Stadion" class="mw-redirect" title="Andre Kamperveen Stadion">National Stadion</a> in Paramaribo. Kamperveen played the full match in the 4–3 loss at home.<sup id="cite_ref-voetbal_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-voetbal-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Two days later the two teams played again ending in 2–0 loss to the Netherlands for Suriname. In 1957 he played in a friendly match for the national team against visiting <a href="/wiki/SK_Rapid_Wien" title="SK Rapid Wien">SK Rapid Wien</a> from <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> winning the match 3–1 at home.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Managerial_career">Managerial career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Managerial career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Suriname_2">Suriname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Suriname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 1958, Kamperveen took over as manager of the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_football_team" title="Suriname national football team">Suriname national team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On 30 July 1958 the national team suffered its biggest loss under Kamperveen, when they were defeated 9–2 by the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Andre_Kamperveen_Stadion" class="mw-redirect" title="Andre Kamperveen Stadion">National Stadion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He managed the team in their first ever World Cup qualifications ahead of the <a href="/wiki/1962_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1962 FIFA World Cup">1962 FIFA World Cup</a> in Chile.<sup id="cite_ref-voetbal_5-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-voetbal-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="SV_Transvaal">SV Transvaal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: SV Transvaal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>He took over the managerial position at <a href="/wiki/S.V._Transvaal" title="S.V. Transvaal">S.V. Transvaal</a> in 1958 as well, leading the team to the national championship in the <a href="/wiki/SVB_Hoofdklasse" title="SVB Hoofdklasse">Hoofdklasse</a> in 1962.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Basketball_career">Basketball career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Basketball career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kamperveen played many sports apart from football. <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">Basketball</a> was one of his favorites, having joined the basketball team of the <a href="/wiki/Military_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="Military of Suriname">Surinamese military</a> after enlisting.<sup id="cite_ref-basketbal_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-basketbal-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Basketball had arrived in Suriname in 1941 when the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">U.S. military</a> arrived on <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_United_States_military_operations#1940.E2.80.931944" title="Timeline of United States military operations">November 24th</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-basketbal_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-basketbal-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Games were organized between teams of the battalions of both militaries and local community teams.<sup id="cite_ref-basketbal_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-basketbal-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The local basketball teams 'Surinam' and 'Chung Fa Foei Kon', a Chinese basketball club from Suriname and one of the strongest in the sports national history, were among the first teams to register in the country. Kamperveen played <a href="/wiki/Point_Guard_(basketball)" class="mw-redirect" title="Point Guard (basketball)">point guard</a> for the basketball team of the <a href="/wiki/Sportvereniging_Nationaal_Leger" title="Sportvereniging Nationaal Leger">Nationaal Leger</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-basketbal_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-basketbal-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Surinamese Basketball Association was founded in 1947 with Kamperveen competing in the first official season. Kamperveen also played for the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_basketball_team" title="Suriname national basketball team">Suriname national basketball team</a> at the same time that he was playing for the national football team.<sup id="cite_ref-basketbal_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-basketbal-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>29 November 1957 was the first time the national basketball team played inside an arena. The game was between the national teams of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>. Kamperveen officiated the game which ended in an 81–78 loss to Trinidad and Tobago for Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-basketbal_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-basketbal-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Judo_career">Judo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Judo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="infobox vcard" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold;background-color:#efefef"><span class="fn">André Kamperveen</span></th></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center"><a href="/wiki/File:Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen.jpg" class="image"><img alt="André Kamperveen.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen.jpg/220px-Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="375" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/cb/Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="297" data-file-height="506" /></a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Other names</th><td class="nickname">Ampie</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Nationality</th><td><a href="/wiki/Surinamese_people" title="Surinamese people">Surinamese</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Style</th><td><a href="/wiki/Judo" title="Judo">Judo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jujutsu" title="Jujutsu">Jiu-Jitsu</a></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Fighting out of</th><td><a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, Suriname</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Team</th><td class="org">Kodokan (Suriname)</td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Rank</th><td>2nd <i>dan</i> judo<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></td></tr><tr><th scope="row">Years active</th><td>1954–1957</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Kamperveen became the first <a href="/wiki/Black_belt_(martial_arts)" title="Black belt (martial arts)">black belt</a> in the history of <a href="/wiki/Judo" title="Judo">judo</a> in Suriname. While studying in the Netherlands at <a href="https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/CIOS" class="extiw" title="nl:CIOS">CIOS</a>, Kamperveen took up judo graduating to <a href="/wiki/Dan_(rank)" title="Dan (rank)">2nd dan</a> in 1957.<sup id="cite_ref-judo_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-judo-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On 3 May 1957 Kamperveen opened the first <a href="/wiki/Dojo" class="mw-redirect" title="Dojo">dojo</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, known as <b>Kodokan</b> on the Mgr. Wulfingstraat.<sup id="cite_ref-judo_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-judo-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Named after the original <a href="/wiki/Kodokan" class="mw-redirect" title="Kodokan">Kodokan</a> in Tokyo, Japan, the Kodokan dojo is of great importance to the development of the practice in Suriname, organizing the first tournaments, it is the dojo where judoka Eddy Murray was given his first belts. Murray would go on to play a much bigger role in the Sport.<sup id="cite_ref-judo_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-judo-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1964, Kamperveen made an attempt at founding the first national judo/jiu-jitsu association in Suriname. Although the attempt failed it did serve to further promote judo in the outlying districts of Suriname. That year he graduated judoka in <a href="/wiki/Nieuw_Nickerie" title="Nieuw Nickerie">Nieuw Nickerie</a> during half time of a football match as manager of <a href="/wiki/S.V._Transvaal" title="S.V. Transvaal">S.V. Transvaal</a>. In 1968, he founded the Surinamese Judo Association, becoming the first chairman of the organization.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasting_and_Journalism">Broadcasting and Journalism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Broadcasting and Journalism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 15 December 1950 the first edition of 'Sport Ontspannings Kroniek' (also known as SOK) was published as a weekly sports newspaper. Although not the first sports paper in Suriname, the previous publication folded after only two years in existence. Kamperveen and Jules Defares were the directors of the new paper. Initially a weekly, it was eventually published twice a week, becoming a weekly once more by 1955.<sup id="cite_ref-sportjournalistiek_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportjournalistiek-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Kamperveen contributed a great deal to sports journalism in Suriname. He was heavily involved in some of the earliest live radio broadcasting of football matches as well, working with Surinamese radio stations Rapar, Apintie and SRS.<sup id="cite_ref-sportjournalistiek_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportjournalistiek-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He founded the VSJS (<i>Vereniging van Sportjournalisten in Suriname</i> (English, Association of Sports journalists in Suriname), becoming the first chairman of the organization in 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 20 October 1966 the first Surinamese television station went on air, the Surinaamse Televisie Stichting (STVS), and two years later, Kamperveen produced the first weekly sports show in Suriname for the station called 'Sportrevue'.<sup id="cite_ref-sportjournalistiek_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportjournalistiek-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_ABC">Radio ABC</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Radio ABC">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Ampie%27s_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Ampie&#39;s Broadcasting Corporation">Ampie's Broadcasting Corporation</a></div>
<p>On 6 December 1975 Kamperveen's dream, to one day own his own radio station, finally came true. <a href="/wiki/Ampie%27s_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Ampie&#39;s Broadcasting Corporation">Ampies Broadcasting Corporation Suriname</a>, commonly known as <b>ABC, the lovestation</b> was born. After years of working for Apintie, Kamperveen finally owned his own station. Both his Sons, Johnny and Henk Kamperveen joined him to work at the station, Johnny as a host, and Henk as a director. Radio ABC is a popular station in Suriname, blending news, activities, sports, humor and a lot of music.<sup id="cite_ref-sportjournalistiek_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportjournalistiek-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_roles_in_Sports">Administrative roles in Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Administrative roles in Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kamperveen held many administrative roles in regards to recreation, youth and sports throughout his entrepreneurial career. In 1957 he graduated with an A-level diploma from the Royal Dutch Football Association (<a href="/wiki/KNVB" class="mw-redirect" title="KNVB">KNVB</a>), having completed studies at the <a href="https://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/CIOS" class="extiw" title="nl:CIOS">CIOS</a> and attending the Sportshochschule in West Germany. After becoming a board member of the Surinamese Football Association, he held various positions within the board of the <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF" title="CONCACAF">CONCACAF</a>, the continental governing body for association football in North America, Central America and the Caribbean. He also helped form the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Football_Union" title="Caribbean Football Union">Caribbean Football Union</a> becoming the first president of the organization, and was elected vice-president of <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>, the global governing body for football.<sup id="cite_ref-kamperveen_3-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kamperveen-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Administrative positions held over the years include:
</p>
<ul><li>In 1964 Kamperveen became a board member of the <a href="/wiki/Surinamese_Football_Association" title="Surinamese Football Association">Surinamese Football Association</a>.</li>
<li>In 1968, he founded the Surinamese Judo Association, becoming the first chairman of the organization.</li>
<li>In 1973, he was made a member of the <a href="/wiki/CONCACAF" title="CONCACAF">CONCACAF</a> disciplinary committee during the association meeting in Mexico.</li>
<li>In 1974, he organized the country's first national billiards tournaments, setting a precedent of the sport.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In 1975 on 6 August he became a board member of CONCACAF holding a chair on the board for one year.</li>
<li>In 1976, he became the honorary chairman of the Association of Sports journalists in Suriname. That same year he became vice-president of <a href="/wiki/FIFA" title="FIFA">FIFA</a>.</li>
<li>In 1978 on 27 January the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Football_Union" title="Caribbean Football Union">Caribbean Football Union</a> (CFU) was founded with Kamperveen becoming the first president of the organization.</li>
<li>In 1980 on 1 March he became the Minister of Culture, Youth and Sports in Suriname. On 6 June of the same year he became a board member of CONCACAF once more, holding a chair on the board for two years until his untimely death.</li>
<li>In 1982, he stepped down as Minister of Culture, Youth and Sports due to disagreements over the <a href="/wiki/1980_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="1980 Surinamese coup d&#39;état">Sergeant's Coup</a>.</li></ul>
<p>That year Kamperveen as vice-president of FIFA appeared along the center circle at the <a href="/wiki/Santiago_Bernab%C3%A9u_Stadium" title="Santiago Bernabéu Stadium">Santiago Bernabéu Stadium</a> in Madrid, Spain as king <a href="/wiki/Juan_Carlos_I_of_Spain" title="Juan Carlos I of Spain">Juan Carlos I of Spain</a> kicked off the <a href="/wiki/1982_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1982 FIFA World Cup">1982 FIFA World Cup</a> opening ceremony.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Kamperveen would also witness the beginning of Surinamese compatriots <a href="/wiki/Frank_Rijkaard" title="Frank Rijkaard">Frank Rijkaard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ruud_Gullit" title="Ruud Gullit">Ruud Gullit</a>'s International career with the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands</a> in the buildup to the tournament before losing his life in the <a href="/wiki/December_murders" title="December murders">December murders</a>. He would not survive to witness the rise and influence of Surinamese footballers in Dutch football throughout the eighties and nineties.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kamperveen is the father of three children from his first marriage. Two Sons, Johnny and Henk, and a daughter Lilian. In 1976 his Sons joined him at Radio ABC, Johnny became a popular radio host, while Henk became a director of the station. He was married to Lea van Leuvenum, his second wife. They were married in 1980 in New York City following his divorce from his previous marriage, they had no children together.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_activities">Political activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Political activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/1980_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="1980 Surinamese coup d&#39;état">1980 Surinamese coup d'état</a>, Kamperveen was appointed Minister of Culture, Youth and Sports under Minister-president <a href="/wiki/Henk_Chin_A_Sen" class="mw-redirect" title="Henk Chin A Sen">Henk Chin A Sen</a>. Due to disagreements over the political direction of the National Military Council (NMR), he denounced his position and became vocal about his opposition through writing and on live radio on his own ABC station. Kamperveen believed the country should head in a more democratic direction, while <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a> wanted a <a href="/wiki/Military_dictatorship" title="Military dictatorship">military dictatorship</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/December_murders" title="December murders">December murders</a></div>
<p>On 7 December 1982 at 2:00&#160;am, he and his wife were interrupted from their sleep by soldiers of <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a>, the then dictator of Suriname. His guard dogs were shot and Kamperveen was taken away to <a href="/wiki/Fort_Zeelandia_(Paramaribo)" title="Fort Zeelandia (Paramaribo)">Fort Zeelandia</a>, where he and 14 other men who had voiced opposition to the military regime were heard as "suspects in a trial" by Bouterse and other sergeants in a self-appointed court. After these "hearings" they were tortured and shot dead. The circumstances have not yet become completely clear; on 10 December 1982, Bouterse claimed on national television that all of the detainees had been shot dead "in an attempt to flee". That day his Wife went to the hospital to identify the body. He had sustained injuries to the jaw and a swollen face, 18 bullet wounds in the chest, a shot wound in the right temple, a fractured femur and a fractured arm.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>His son Johnny had initially been reported as one of the victims of the murders. He was however able to escape abduction. and the family members fled to the Netherlands. The ABC Radio building had been torched to the ground and completely destroyed on the night of the abduction as well. Kamperveen's funeral was on 13 December 1982, buried at the Annette's Hof cemetery in Paramaribo, his funeral attracted thousands of people and was led by vicar Rudi Polanen of the Moravian Church.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Posthumous">Posthumous</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Posthumous">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Andre_Kamperveen_Standbeeld.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Andre_Kamperveen_Standbeeld.JPG/250px-Andre_Kamperveen_Standbeeld.JPG" decoding="async" width="250" height="187" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Andre_Kamperveen_Standbeeld.JPG/375px-Andre_Kamperveen_Standbeeld.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Andre_Kamperveen_Standbeeld.JPG/500px-Andre_Kamperveen_Standbeeld.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1023" data-file-height="764" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Andre_Kamperveen_Standbeeld.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bronze statue of André Kamperveen at the <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen_Stadion" title="André Kamperveen Stadion">André Kamperveen Stadion</a> in Paramaribo.</div></div></div>
<p>The Kamperveen family have long been involved with the prosecution of Bouterse, working with human rights organization and consulting with the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Human_Rights_Committee" title="United Nations Human Rights Committee">United Nations Human Rights Committee</a> in 1983 along with other victim families.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Henk Kamperveen was also involved in the December murders trial that commenced on 30 November 2007. To this date the case has gone unresolved and Dési Bouterse has long denied guilt and only accepted political responsibility for the murders as of 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1993, Johnny and Henk returned to Paramaribo to rebuild the ABC Radio station, and after 11 years of absence, the love station was back on air in Suriname. In 2003, Johnny died from a viral infection at his home after years as a host on his father's station.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1988, the National Stadium in Paramaribo was renamed the <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Kamperveen_Stadion" title="André Kamperveen Stadion">André Kamperveen Stadion</a> to commemorate the stadiums 35th Anniversary, and on 1 October 2000 a bronze statue of Kamperveen was erected outside the stadium to commemorate the Surinamese Football Association's 80th Anniversary.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
