<p><b>GOL Linhas Aéreas Inteligentes S.A</b> (<b>"GOL Intelligent Airlines S.A."</b> also known as <b>VRG Linhas Aéreas S/A</b>)  <a href="/wiki/B3_(stock_exchange)" title="B3 (stock exchange)">B3</a>:&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.bmfbovespa.com.br/en_us/products/listed-equities-and-derivatives/equities/listed-companies.htm?codigo=19569">GOLL3</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.bmfbovespa.com.br/en_us/products/listed-equities-and-derivatives/equities/listed-companies.htm?codigo=19569">GOLL4</a> / <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">NYSE</a>:&#160;<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.nyse.com/quote/XNYS:GOL">GOL</a> is a <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> low-cost airline based in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/National_Civil_Aviation_Agency_of_Brazil" title="National Civil Aviation Agency of Brazil">National Civil Aviation Agency of Brazil</a> (ANAC), between January and December 2018 GOL had 35.7% of the domestic and 9.6% of the international <a href="/wiki/Market_share" title="Market share">market shares</a> in terms of passengers per kilometer flown,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> making it the largest domestic and third largest international airline in Brazil. GOL competes in Brazil and other <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South American</a> countries with <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chilean</a> <a href="/wiki/LATAM_Airlines_Group" title="LATAM Airlines Group">LATAM Airlines Group</a>, Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Azul_Brazilian_Airlines" title="Azul Brazilian Airlines">Azul</a> and <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Avianca_Holdings" title="Avianca Holdings">Avianca Holdings</a> S.A. It also owns the brand <b>Varig</b>, although now that name refers to what is informally known as the "new" Varig, founded in 2006, not to the extinct <a href="/wiki/Varig" title="Varig">"old" Varig</a> airline, founded in 1927.
</p><p>GOL operates a growing domestic and international scheduled network. Its main hubs are <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo%E2%80%93Guarulhos_International_Airport" title="São Paulo–Guarulhos International Airport">São Paulo–Guarulhos International Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gale%C3%A3o_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Galeão International Airport">Rio de Janeiro–Galeão International Airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tancredo_Neves_International_Airport" title="Tancredo Neves International Airport">Tancredo Neves International Airport</a> near <a href="/wiki/Belo_Horizonte" title="Belo Horizonte">Belo Horizonte</a>. GOL also has focus operations at <a href="/wiki/Santos_Dumont_Airport" title="Santos Dumont Airport">Rio de Janeiro-Santos Dumont Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Congonhas-S%C3%A3o_Paulo_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Congonhas-São Paulo Airport">São Paulo-Congonhas Airport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Salgado_Filho_International_Airport" title="Salgado Filho International Airport">Salgado Filho International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Porto_Alegre" title="Porto Alegre">Porto Alegre</a>
GOL refers to itself as <b>GOL Intelligent Airlines</b><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> (<b>GOL Linhas Aéreas Inteligentes</b> in <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>) as a slogan. The company is traded on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> as "GOL Linhas Aéreas Inteligentes S.A."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The company's name is a <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Portuguese" title="Brazilian Portuguese">Brazilian Portuguese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> word borrowing from the <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> word "goal" from <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">Association football</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Deseret_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Deseret-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GolTicketCounterBrasilia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/GolTicketCounterBrasilia.jpg/220px-GolTicketCounterBrasilia.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="148" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/GolTicketCounterBrasilia.jpg/330px-GolTicketCounterBrasilia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/GolTicketCounterBrasilia.jpg/440px-GolTicketCounterBrasilia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2639" data-file-height="1772" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GolTicketCounterBrasilia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>GOL ticket counter at <a href="/wiki/Brasilia_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Brasilia International Airport">Brasilia International Airport</a></div></div></div>
<p>The airline was established in 2000 and started operations on January 15, 2001 with a flight from Brasília to São Paulo.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It is a subsidiary of the Brazilian conglomerate Grupo Áurea, based in <a href="/wiki/Minas_Gerais" title="Minas Gerais">Minas Gerais</a> <a href="/wiki/States_of_Brazil" title="States of Brazil">state</a>, which has other transportation interests, including Brazil's largest long-distance bus company. Grupo Áurea in turn is owned by the Constantino family. <a href="/wiki/Constantino_de_Oliveira_J%C3%BAnior" title="Constantino de Oliveira Júnior">Constantino Júnior</a> was responsible for building the business next to the vice president of the company, <a href="/wiki/David_Barioni" title="David Barioni">David Barioni</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IstoE_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IstoE-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Exame_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Exame-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 24, 2004 GOL launched simultaneous initial public offerings on the New York and São Paulo stock exchanges. In 2007, GOL was owned by AeroPar Participações (75.5%), Venture (17.6%), American International Group (5.4%) and <a href="/wiki/Air_France-KLM" class="mw-redirect" title="Air France-KLM">Air France-KLM</a> (1,5%).<sup id="cite_ref-FI_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  The growth in GOL's stock price made the Constantino family a member of the <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> magazine billionaire list in 2005.
</p><p>In 2007, GOL was supposed to begin a code-share agreement with <a href="/wiki/TAP_Portugal" class="mw-redirect" title="TAP Portugal">TAP Portugal</a>, opening the European market to the Brazilian airline, and the internal Brazilian market to the Portuguese airline (the largest foreign airline in Brazil). Instead, TAP Portugal chose to cooperate with <a href="/wiki/TAM_Airlines" class="mw-redirect" title="TAM Airlines">TAM Airlines</a> (<i>TAM Linhas Aéreas</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-Avionews_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Avionews-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 24, 2010 GOL announced it was in "advanced talks" to join <a href="/wiki/Oneworld_alliance" class="mw-redirect" title="Oneworld alliance">Oneworld alliance</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> which would allow it to catch up with rival TAM, a recent member of Star Alliance. However, on October 6, 2010, the airline announced a change in position by denying any interest in joining an alliance, preferring to remain independent and to establish a "patchwork of code-sharing agreements."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Following this trend, on September 28, 2011 GOL and <a href="/wiki/Aerol%C3%ADneas_Argentinas" title="Aerolíneas Argentinas">Aerolíneas Argentinas</a> announced the intention to implement a codeshare, feeder and frequent flyer programs agreement on a date yet to be announced and pending approval from the governments of Brazil and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 18, 2010 GOL unveiled the expansion of its maintenance base located at <a href="/wiki/Tancredo_Neves_International_Airport" title="Tancredo Neves International Airport">Belo Horizonte/Confins - Tancredo Neves International Airport</a>. Originally opened in 2006 with the capacity to service 60 aircraft per year (mainly Boeing 737 and 767), the expansion allowed GOL to increase the number to 120.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> GOL later started also using the base to service aircraft from other airlines, and as of 2013 it also serviced airplanes from <a href="/wiki/Azul_Brazilian_Airlines" title="Azul Brazilian Airlines">Azul Brazilian Airlines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Copa_Airlines" title="Copa Airlines">Copa Airlines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-falandodeviagem_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-falandodeviagem-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 23, 2010, GOL Airlines started an operational partnership with Brazilian carrier <a href="/wiki/Passaredo_Linhas_A%C3%A9reas" title="Passaredo Linhas Aéreas">Passaredo Linhas Aéreas</a>. The agreement was rescinded on July 31, 2014, when Passaredo established a similar interline agreement with TAM Airlines instead.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 7, 2011, GOL announced the intention of <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a> to purchase 3% of its shares.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The agreement also includes the creation of mutual code-share flights, alignment of frequent flyer benefits and transfer of GOL's Boeing 767s lease agreements to Delta.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 1, 2012, GOL confirmed a firm order of 60 <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737_MAX" title="Boeing 737 MAX">Boeing 737 MAXs</a>. References did not specify the type of MAX aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 6, 2012, GOL started seasonal operations to <a href="/wiki/Miami_International_Airport" title="Miami International Airport">Miami</a> and <a href="/wiki/Orlando_International_Airport" title="Orlando International Airport">Orlando</a>, available for Smiles account holders and originating in Brazil only. Technically, they are considered charter flights, although they are not necessarily part of an inclusive tour package; the use of miles or miles plus money is mandatory, as well as a minimum 7-day stay at the destination.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2014, <a href="/wiki/Air_France%E2%80%93KLM" title="Air France–KLM">Air France–KLM</a> announced it would invest $100 million in GOL Linhas Aéreas Inteligentes in advance of the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2016_Olympics" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 Olympics">2016 Olympics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some disagreement exists as to whether GOL is a <a href="/wiki/Low-cost_carrier" title="Low-cost carrier">low-cost carrier</a>. In 2014, GOL was ranked the second best low-cost airline in South America after <a href="/wiki/Azul_Brazilian_Airlines" title="Azul Brazilian Airlines">Azul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> GOL refers to itself as a low-cost carrier, but it is increasingly not regarded as such. According to <a href="/wiki/Federal_University_of_Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Federal University of Rio de Janeiro">UFRJ</a> specialist Elton Fernandes, "GOL's costs are not very different from those of <a href="/wiki/TAM_Airlines" class="mw-redirect" title="TAM Airlines">TAM Airlines</a>. People are accustomed to calling GOL low-cost, but GOL is not that. It is not even low-fare anymore."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Purchase_of_Varig">Purchase of Varig</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gol_Transportes_A%C3%A9reos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Purchase of Varig">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Varig_logo.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Varig_logo.svg/220px-Varig_logo.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="31" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Varig_logo.svg/330px-Varig_logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Varig_logo.svg/440px-Varig_logo.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="144" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Varig_logo.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Logo of the "new" VARIG brand</div></div></div>
<p>On March 28, 2007 GOL officially purchased part of the assets of VARIG - VRG Linhas Aéreas, informally known as the "new Varig," a new company that owned the Varig brand, for US$320 million from Volo Group and <a href="/wiki/MatlinPatterson" title="MatlinPatterson">MatlinPatterson Global Opportunies</a> <a href="/wiki/Private_equity" title="Private equity">hedge fund</a>. At that time, the "old Varig" was under bankruptcy protection.
</p><p>GOL announced that the Varig brand would continue <a href="/wiki/D/b/a" class="mw-redirect" title="D/b/a">doing business</a> operating as such, rather than its official name VRG Linhas Aéreas.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The transaction, via its GTI subsidiary, required a US$98 million cash payment, with the balance through the allocation of non-voting shares to <a href="/wiki/VarigLog" class="mw-redirect" title="VarigLog">VARIG Logística</a> and Volo, which had acquired VARIG in June 2006 for US$24 million.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The transaction did not involve the original airline, informally known as "old Varig," which continued to exist until its own bankruptcy in mid-2010 under the name <a href="/wiki/Flex_Linhas_A%C3%A9reas" title="Flex Linhas Aéreas">Flex Linhas Aéreas</a>.
</p><p>In 2009 GOL was merged into VRG Linhas Aéreas. VRG Linhas Aéreas thus became an airline that operates two brands: GOL and Varig, although in reality flights are operated only under GOL flight numbers. Initially, the Varig brand operated to medium-haul scheduled and charter international destinations within South America and to the Caribbean with Boeing 737-700's configured in two classes, and to long-haul charter international destinations in North America, Europe and Africa with <a href="/wiki/Boeing_767" title="Boeing 767">Boeing 767</a>-300ERs configured in economy only. The latter were also used in <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_lease" title="Aircraft lease">wet-lease</a> operations.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> This scheme was later dropped. The brand GOL operates most of the flights of the network, and had aircraft configured in all-economy class, used for scheduled domestic and international operations within South America.
</p><p>Because of contractual obligations, between 2006 and 2009 the "new Varig" (VRG Linhas Aéreas) was obliged to purchase a minimum of 140 hours/month of services from the "old Varig" (Flex Linhas Aéreas). Therefore, at that time, some of VRG Linhas Aéreas flights operated with GOL flight numbers but were actually flown with chartered aircraft from Flex Linhas Aéreas. The agreement ceased before the bankruptcy of Flex on August 20, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span id="Purchase_of_Webjet_Linhas_A.C3.A9reas"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Purchase_of_Webjet_Linhas_Aéreas">Purchase of Webjet Linhas Aéreas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gol_Transportes_A%C3%A9reos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Purchase of Webjet Linhas Aéreas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 9, 2011, VRG Linhas Aéreas, owner of the brands GOL and Varig, announced the intention to purchase full control of <a href="/wiki/WebJet_Linhas_A%C3%A9reas" title="WebJet Linhas Aéreas">WebJet Linhas Aéreas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The purchase contract was signed on August 2, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On October 10, 2012 the purchase received its final approval with some operational restrictions from the Brazilian regulatory agency.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Services were integrated but both companies continued to operate independently for some weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-Andrade_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Andrade-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-O_Globo_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-O_Globo-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Integration started on October 17, 2012 when sales requested via Webjet's web-portal started to be redirected to GOL's site.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> However, on November 23, 2012 Webjet abruptly ceased to operate and all services were incorporated by GOL. The Webjet brand ceased to exist.<sup id="cite_ref-Andrade_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Andrade-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-O_Globo_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-O_Globo-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>  GOL also announced its intention to return all of Webjet's Boeing 737-300s to lessors until the end of the first quarter of 2013. There was no mention to the fate of Webjet's 737-800s.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Partnership_with_TwoFlex">Partnership with TwoFlex</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gol_Transportes_A%C3%A9reos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Partnership with TwoFlex">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On April 12, 2019, Gol announced an adapted <a href="/wiki/Essential_Air_Service" title="Essential Air Service">Essential Air Service</a> partnership with <a href="/wiki/TwoFlex" title="TwoFlex">TwoFlex</a> in which the latter would operate feeder services on behalf of Gol in the States of <a href="/wiki/Amazonas_(Brazilian_state)" title="Amazonas (Brazilian state)">Amazonas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Par%C3%A1" title="Pará">Pará</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mato_Grosso" title="Mato Grosso">Mato Grosso</a> using <a href="/wiki/Cessna_208_Caravan" title="Cessna 208 Caravan">Cessna 208 Caravan</a> aircraft. The flights are marketed by Gol in its reservation platform but operated by TwoFlex and integrated into the network of Gol. This partnership greatly increased the number of Gol destinations in these three States.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Airline_Affinity_Program">Airline Affinity Program</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gol_Transportes_A%C3%A9reos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Airline Affinity Program">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Smiles</b> is GOL/Varig's <a href="/wiki/Frequent-flyer_program" title="Frequent-flyer program">Frequent-flyer program</a> since July 20, 2006. Points can be used for services from GOL/Varig, and partners <a href="/wiki/Aerol%C3%ADneas_Argentinas" title="Aerolíneas Argentinas">Aerolíneas Argentinas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aerom%C3%A9xico" title="Aeroméxico">Aeromexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_Canada" title="Air Canada">Air Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_France" title="Air France">Air France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alitalia" title="Alitalia">Alitalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Copa_Airlines" title="Copa Airlines">Copa Airlines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines" title="Delta Air Lines">Delta Air Lines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emirates_(airline)" title="Emirates (airline)">Emirates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Etihad_Airways" title="Etihad Airways">Etihad</a>,  <a href="/wiki/KLM" title="KLM">KLM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qatar_Airways" title="Qatar Airways">Qatar Airways</a> and <a href="/wiki/TAP_Air_Portugal" title="TAP Air Portugal">TAP Air Portugal</a>, including flights, upgrades, holidays, hotel stays and car rentals. Smiles was part of the "new Varig" package bought by GOL, which honored all miles and eventually became its own frequent-flyer program. Previously GOL had no such program.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On a study conducted in 2011, Smiles ranked third among 24 chosen frequent flyer programs, with 97.1% success of requests made.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Smiles was spun off as an independent company <sup id="cite_ref-SMLERI_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SMLERI-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> eventually leading to its <a href="/wiki/IPO" class="mw-redirect" title="IPO">IPO</a> at <a href="/wiki/BOVESPA" class="mw-redirect" title="BOVESPA">BOVESPA</a> in April 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-EXAME_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EXAME-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents_and_accidents">Incidents and accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Gol_Transportes_A%C3%A9reos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Incidents and accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>On September 29, 2006, <a href="/wiki/Gol_Transportes_A%C3%A9reos_Flight_1907" title="Gol Transportes Aéreos Flight 1907">Flight 1907</a> operated by <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737" title="Boeing 737">Boeing 737-800 SFP</a> registration PR-GTD, disappeared from radar while flying over the <a href="/wiki/Central-West_Region,_Brazil" title="Central-West Region, Brazil">central-western</a> state of <a href="/wiki/Mato_Grosso" title="Mato Grosso">Mato Grosso</a> en route from <a href="/wiki/Eduardo_Gomes_International_Airport" title="Eduardo Gomes International Airport">Manaus</a> to <a href="/wiki/Bras%C3%ADlia_International_Airport" title="Brasília International Airport">Brasília</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro%E2%80%93Gale%C3%A3o_International_Airport" title="Rio de Janeiro–Galeão International Airport">Rio de Janeiro-Galeão</a>. The aircraft collided in mid-air with an <a href="/wiki/Embraer_Legacy_600" title="Embraer Legacy 600">Embraer Legacy 600</a> business jet, registration N600XL, near the town of <a href="/wiki/Matup%C3%A1" title="Matupá">Matupá</a>, 470 miles (760&#160;km) south of <a href="/wiki/Manaus" title="Manaus">Manaus</a>. The GOL aircraft broke up in mid-air and crashed in the Amazon jungle, leaving no survivors among its 154 occupants. The wreckage was found a day later. The Legacy jet landed safely at <a href="/wiki/Cachimbo_Airport" title="Cachimbo Airport">Cachimbo Airport</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Campo_de_Provas_Brigadeiro_Velloso" title="Campo de Provas Brigadeiro Velloso">Brigadeiro Velloso Test Range</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Air_Force" title="Brazilian Air Force">Brazilian Air Force</a>, with damage to the tail and left winglet.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
