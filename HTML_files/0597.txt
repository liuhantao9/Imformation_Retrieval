<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisations">Organisations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Law_enforcement_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Organisations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Korps_Politie_Suriname">Korps Politie Suriname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Law_enforcement_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Korps Politie Suriname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The largest force and the most published in the media is the Korps Politie Suriname (KPS).<sup id="cite_ref-Suriname2014_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suriname2014-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This is a traditional police department model and is responsible for all the policing efforts. Under the main umbrella of the KPS, there are three branches.<sup id="cite_ref-Suriname2014_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suriname2014-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>The city police handle all issues inside the city limits of <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>.</li>
<li>The rural police handle everything outside of Paramaribo.</li>
<li>The Judicial Police are composed of specialised police units, such as forensics, fraud, homicide, etc.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_police">Military police</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Law_enforcement_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Military police">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The second largest military police polices all members of the Surinamese Military and handles border control/immigration functions.<sup id="cite_ref-Suriname2014_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suriname2014-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bureau_of_National_Security">Bureau of National Security</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Law_enforcement_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bureau of National Security">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The third largest law enforcement/security entity are the Surinamese Bureau of National Security. The Bureau is responsible for the Central Intelligence and Security Service (CIVD) and the presidential personal security unit.<sup id="cite_ref-Suriname2014_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suriname2014-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Human_rights">Human rights</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Law_enforcement_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Human rights">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>LGBT individuals have made claims of police mistreatment in Paramaribo.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> A 2012 UN report stated that Suriname's police scored the lowest in the region in communicating respectably with citizens.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
