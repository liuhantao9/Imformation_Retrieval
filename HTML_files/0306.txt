<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Districts_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The country was first divided up into subdivisions by the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> on October 8, 1834, when a Royal Decree declared that there were to be 8 divisions and 2 districts:
</p>
<ul><li>Upper Suriname and Torarica</li>
<li>Para</li>
<li>Upper Commewijne</li>
<li>Upper Cottica and Perica</li>
<li>Lower Commewijne</li>
<li>Lower Cottica</li>
<li>Matapica</li>
<li>Saramacca</li>
<li>Coronie (district)</li>
<li>Nickerie (district)</li></ul>
<p>The divisions were areas near the <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, and the districts were areas further away from the city.
</p><p>In 1927, Suriname's districts were revised, and the country was divided into 7 districts. In 1943, 1948, 1949, 1952 and 1959 further small modifications were made. On October 28, 1966, the districts were redrawn again, into
</p>
<ul><li>Nickerie</li>
<li>Coronie</li>
<li>Saramacca</li>
<li>Brokopondo</li>
<li>Para</li>
<li>Suriname</li>
<li>Paramaribo</li>
<li>Commewijne</li>
<li>Marowijne</li></ul>
<p>These divisions remained until 1980, when yet again, the borders of the districts were redrawn, however, with the following requirements: 
</p>
<ul><li>Changes in the old boundaries were made only if it leads to improved functioning</li>
<li>Each area should be developed</li>
<li>The new boundaries should respect the identities of <a href="/wiki/Amerindian" class="mw-redirect" title="Amerindian">indigenous people</a>.</li></ul>
<p>The districts created in 1980 remain to this day.
</p>
