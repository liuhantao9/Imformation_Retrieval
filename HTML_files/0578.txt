	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">&#160;&#160;(Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=INSEE&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="INSEE">INSEE</a>)</span></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p><p>The <b>National Institute of Statistics and Economic Studies</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i lang="fr">Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques</i>), abbreviated <b>INSEE</b> (<small>French pronunciation:&#160;&#8203;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[inse]</a></span>), is the national statistics bureau of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. It collects and publishes information about the <a href="/wiki/Economy_of_France" title="Economy of France">French economy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Demographics_of_France" title="Demographics of France">people</a> and carries out the periodic national census. Headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, it is the French branch of <a href="/wiki/Eurostat" title="Eurostat">Eurostat</a>. The INSEE was created in 1946 as a successor to the <a href="/wiki/Vichy_France" title="Vichy France">Vichy regime</a>'s National Statistics Service (SNS). It works in close cooperation with the <a href="/wiki/Institut_national_d%27%C3%A9tudes_d%C3%A9mographiques" title="Institut national d&#39;études démographiques">Institut national d'études démographiques</a> (INED).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose">Purpose</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institut_national_de_la_statistique_et_des_%C3%A9tudes_%C3%A9conomiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Purpose">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>INSEE is responsible for the production and analysis of official statistics in France. Its best known responsibilities include:
</p>
<ul><li>Organising and publishing the national census.</li>
<li>Producing various <a href="/wiki/Index_(economics)" title="Index (economics)">indices</a> – which are widely recognised as being of excellent quality –  including an inflation index used for determining the rates of rents and the costs associated with construction.</li>
<li>Eurostat uses INSEE statistics in combination with those of other national statistical agencies to compile comparable statistics for the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> as a whole. It is also widely recognized as representing France on international questions of statistics.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institut_national_de_la_statistique_et_des_%C3%A9tudes_%C3%A9conomiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The INSEE is the responsibility of <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_the_Economy,_Finance_and_Industry_(France)" class="mw-redirect" title="Minister of the Economy, Finance and Industry (France)">MINEFI</a>, the French Ministry of Finance. The current director is Jean-Luc Tavernier. However, Eurostat considers INSEE as an independent body, although its independence is not written in the law.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Teaching_and_research">Teaching and research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institut_national_de_la_statistique_et_des_%C3%A9tudes_%C3%A9conomiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Teaching and research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Research and teaching for the INSEE is undertaken by GENES or Group of the National Schools of the Economy and Statistics (French:  <i>Groupe des Écoles Nationales d'Économie et Statistique</i>) which includes:
</p>
<ul><li>ENSAE (<i>École nationale de la statistique et de l'administration économique</i>), a <i><a href="/wiki/Grande_%C3%A9cole" class="mw-redirect" title="Grande école">grande école</a></i> which trains INSEE administrators and engineers specialized in statistics, the economy, and finance.</li>
<li>ENSAI, (<i>École nationale de la statistique et de l'analyse de l'information</i>), an engineering school.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Codes_and_numbering_system">Codes and numbering system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institut_national_de_la_statistique_et_des_%C3%A9tudes_%C3%A9conomiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Codes and numbering system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/INSEE_code" title="INSEE code">INSEE code</a></div>
<p>INSEE gives numerical indexing codes (French: <i>les Codes INSEE</i>) to various entities in France:
</p>
<ul><li><b>INSEE codes</b> (known as <b>COG</b>) are given to various administrative units, notably the French <a href="/wiki/Commune_in_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Commune in France">communes</a> (they do not coincide with <a href="/wiki/Postal_code" title="Postal code">postcodes</a>). The 'complete' code has 8 digits and 3 spaces within, but there is a popular 'simplified' code with 5 digits and no space within:
<ul><li>2 digits (<i><a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9partement" class="mw-redirect" title="Département">département</a></i>) and 3 digits (commune) for the 96 <i>départements</i> of <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_France" title="Metropolitan France">Metropolitan France</a>.</li>
<li>3 digits (<i>département</i> or collectivity) and 2 digits (commune) for the <a href="/wiki/Overseas_departments" class="mw-redirect" title="Overseas departments">Overseas departments</a>, <a href="/wiki/Territorial_collectivity" title="Territorial collectivity">Overseas Territorial Collectivities</a> and <a href="/wiki/Overseas_Countries_and_Territories" class="mw-redirect" title="Overseas Countries and Territories">Overseas Countries and Territories</a>. <i>See also: <a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Code_INSEE#Code_communal" class="extiw" title="fr:Code INSEE">fr:Code INSEE#Code communal</a>.</i></li></ul></li>
<li><b>INSEE numbers</b> (13 digits + a two-digit key) are <a href="/wiki/National_identification_number" title="National identification number">national identification numbers</a> given to people. The format is as follows: <i>syymmlllllooo kk</i>, where
<ul><li><i>s</i> is 1 for a male, 2 for a female for a permanent number; it is 7 for a male, 8 for a female for a temporary number,</li>
<li><i>yy</i> are the last two digits of the year of birth,</li>
<li><i>mm</i> is the month of birth or a number above 20 if the birthdate is unknown</li>
<li><i>lllll</i> is the COG for the location of birth,</li>
<li><i>ooo</i> is an order number to distinguish people being born at the same place in the same year and month.</li>
<li>'kk' is the "control key", equal to 97-(the rest of the number <a href="/wiki/Modular_arithmetic" title="Modular arithmetic">modulo</a> 97).<br />There are exceptions for people in particular situations.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/SIREN_code" title="SIREN code">SIREN codes</a> are given to businesses and <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit" class="mw-redirect" title="Nonprofit">nonprofit associations</a>, <a href="/wiki/SIRET_code" title="SIRET code">SIRET codes</a> to their establishments and facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institut_national_de_la_statistique_et_des_%C3%A9tudes_%C3%A9conomiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Statistics_in_France_before_INSEE">Statistics in France before INSEE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institut_national_de_la_statistique_et_des_%C3%A9tudes_%C3%A9conomiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Statistics in France before INSEE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>INSEE was preceded by several related statistical agencies in France. In 1833, the Statistics Bureau (French: <i>Bureau de la statistique</i>) was created by <a href="/wiki/Adolphe_Thiers" title="Adolphe Thiers">Adolphe Thiers</a>. In 1840 it was renamed <i>SGF</i> or <i>General Statistics of France</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Under the direction of Lucien March, the SGF expanded its activities. It began an investigation of consumption habits in 1907, following with periodic investigations on retail prices in 1911.
</p><p>In 1920 <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Sauvy" title="Alfred Sauvy">Alfred Sauvy</a> introduced competitive entrance exams for SGF recruitment. However, it was <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Carmille" title="René Carmille">René Carmille</a>, a pioneer of the use of the <a href="/wiki/Calculator" title="Calculator">calculators</a>, who laid the foundations of the modern organization. In 1940, the Demographic Service (<i>Service de la démographie</i>) was created under the Ministry of Finance in order to replace the military recruitment office prohibited by the <a href="/wiki/Armistice_with_France_(Second_Compi%C3%A8gne)" class="mw-redirect" title="Armistice with France (Second Compiègne)">June 1940 Armistice</a> with <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a>. In order to better conceal its undertakings, the Demographic Service absorbed the SGF on 11 October 1941. The new organization was called the <i>SNS</i> or <i>National Statistics Service</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> As part of this reorganization, six new offices were created in the <a href="/wiki/Vichy_France" title="Vichy France">Northern (occupied) zone</a> whose regional structure is maintained today in INSEE.
</p><p>René Carmille created an Applied Sciences School (predecessor of the current <a href="/wiki/Ecole_Nationale_de_la_Statistique_et_de_l%27Administration_Economique" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecole Nationale de la Statistique et de l&#39;Administration Economique">ENSAE</a>) to specially train members for the SNS.
</p><p>Carmille worked for Vichy France but he was actually a double agent for the French underground. From his position in the SNS he sabotaged the Nazi census of France, which saved untold numbers of Jewish people from death camps. He also used his department to help mobilize French resistance in Algeria. He was caught by the Nazis and sent to <a href="/wiki/Dachau_concentration_camp" title="Dachau concentration camp">Dachau</a> where he died in 1945.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation_of_INSEE">Creation of INSEE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Institut_national_de_la_statistique_et_des_%C3%A9tudes_%C3%A9conomiques&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Creation of INSEE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The SNS was finally transformed into INSEE by the law of 27 April 1946, <i>The National Institute of Statistics and Economic Surveys for the metropolis and overseas France</i> (<i>L'Institut national de la statistique et des études économiques pour la métropole et la France d'outre-mer</i>).
</p>
