<p><b>Clintons</b>, previously branded as <b>Clinton Cards</b>, is a chain of stores in the <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a> founded in 1968 by <a href="/wiki/Don_Lewin" title="Don Lewin">Don Lewin</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and known for selling <a href="/wiki/Greeting_cards" class="mw-redirect" title="Greeting cards">greeting cards</a>, together with <a href="/wiki/Soft_toys" class="mw-redirect" title="Soft toys">soft toys</a> and related gift products.
</p><p>It was listed on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a> and was a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/FTSE_Fledgling_Index" title="FTSE Fledgling Index">FTSE Fledgling Index</a>. On 9 May 2012 the company entered administration<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and following the closure of 350 branches, the company was bought by <a href="/wiki/American_Greetings" title="American Greetings">American Greetings</a>.
</p><p>Clintons is managed by Eddie Shepard,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> from American Greetings’ subsidiary, Schurman Retail Group.<sup id="cite_ref-Reinvention_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reinvention-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Starting in July 2012, the company began the gradual introduction of a new store design and logo, which removed the previous ‘Clinton Cards’ branding, replacing it with the new ‘Clintons’ name.<sup id="cite_ref-Reinvention_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reinvention-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Clinton Cards was founded in 1968 when <a href="/wiki/Don_Lewin" title="Don Lewin">Don Lewin</a> OBE opened his first shop in <a href="/wiki/Epping,_Essex" title="Epping, Essex">Epping</a>, <a href="/wiki/Essex" title="Essex">Essex</a>. The business grew to 77 shops by 1988 and was then successfully floated on the <a href="/wiki/London_Stock_Exchange" title="London Stock Exchange">London Stock Exchange</a>. This enabled the company to increase its rate of growth and by the summer of 1994 the business comprised 277 shops. In October 1994 Clintons acquired 83 shops from <a href="/wiki/Hallmark_Cards" title="Hallmark Cards">Hallmark Cards</a> and in September 1995 acquired 112 shops from <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Cards" title="Carlton Cards">Carlton Cards</a>. In October 1998, the entire share capital of GSG Holdings Limited was purchased, adding a further 211 shops to the portfolio. These two important transactions and continuing organic growth were financed without recourse to shareholders.
</p><p>In 2004 the company purchased the <a href="/wiki/Birthdays_(retailer)" title="Birthdays (retailer)">Birthdays</a> chain of card and party shops for £46.4million, but placed the subsidiary into administration in 2009, subsequently buying back 140 of the 332 stores.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2012, the company's main supplier <a href="/wiki/American_Greetings" title="American Greetings">American Greetings</a> bought £36 million of Clintons debt from its main lending banks, and immediately called in the debt for payment.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Clinton Cards was unable to make the payment and entered administration on 9 May, with Peter Saville, Simon Freakley and Anne O’Keefe of Zolfo Cooper LLP, appointed as Joint Administrators of the Company. A week later 350 branches were closed, including all the Birthdays branches, and the remaining 397 stores were purchased in June 2012 by US based American Greetings' subsidiary Lakeshore Lending Limited.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
