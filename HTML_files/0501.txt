	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Surinamese National Badminton Championships</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Surinamese National Badminton Championships</b> is a tournament organized by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinaamse_Badminton_Bond&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Surinaamse Badminton Bond (page does not exist)">Surinaamse Badminton Bond</a> (SBB) the <a href="/wiki/Sport_governing_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Sport governing body">governing body</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Sport" title="Sport">sport</a> of <a href="/wiki/Badminton" title="Badminton">badminton</a> in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, to crown the best <a href="/wiki/Badminton" title="Badminton">badminton</a> players in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>.
</p><p>The tournament started in 1966 and is held every year, except for the years 1971, 1986, 1989, 2002 and 2007 when no National championships were held. Since the year 2000 the National Championships of Suriname is divided between Category A for elite players and Category B for recreational players. Furthermore a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinamese_National_Junior_Badminton_Championships&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Surinamese National Junior Badminton Championships (page does not exist)">National Junior Championships</a> is also organized every year.
</p>
