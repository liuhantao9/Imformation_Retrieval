<p>"<b>Southbound</b>" is a song co-written and recorded by American singer <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, and released as the third single from her sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Cry_Pretty" title="Cry Pretty">Cry Pretty</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The single had an official release day of April 29, 2019. The song was written by Underwood, <a href="/wiki/David_Garcia_(musician)" title="David Garcia (musician)">David Garcia</a> and Josh Miller.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_and_composition">Writing and composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southbound_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Writing and composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>“Southbound” was written by Underwood, co-producer David Garcia and Josh Miller. Underwood said of the writing process, "I was like, 'This sounds like fun, I think. We can do something fun with this.' But then you realize how tricky for some reason it is to be a woman and write kind of a drinking party boat song. You wouldn’t think it would be any different, but for some reason, I feel like a guy could get away with saying this line that we just threw out there, but I can’t for some reason."<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> When talking about particular characters in the song, she added, "I used Katie in the song as an example. So, Katie, we wanted to make her have a little too much fun, but for every line we came out with, it was like, 'Well, we don’t want it to be sad.' We still had to make Katie have a little respect but wanted to just get it out there that some people were having too much fun. It surprisingly took us a while to write it but by the end of it, it was like, 'Well, this is a lot of fun,'" she added. "I feel like everybody kind of gravitated towards that one when we were all done with it."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southbound_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> gave the song favorable reception in their review of the album, writing, "One of <i>Cry Pretty</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s strangest surprises is a Florida Georgia Line-style party song called 'Southbound' that finds her singing about tan lines and 'redneck margaritas.' It's probably the most casual Underwood's ever been, and, sung by a male artist, it would have felt almost impossibly unoriginal. Recorded by a woman who doesn’t exactly make pontoon day drinking a part of her personal brand, it's pleasingly contrarian: perhaps it's Underwood trying to reclaim a style of music that's been co-opted and ridden high on the charts by the genre's leading men."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <i>Sounds Like Nashville</i> similarly praised the song, highlighting it's "propulsive rhythm, sunny guitar riffs and breezy organ accents", also noting that it was "the first time in recent years that the Oklahoma native has offered up such a carefree track, and its good-time lyrics are all about pontoon-boat weekends, two-for-one redneck margaritas, tan lines and cheap sunglasses."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Consequence_of_Sound" title="Consequence of Sound">Consequence of Sound</a></i> gave a less favorable review of the song in their review of the album, writing that it was "a bit of fluffy party music high on southern pride."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southbound_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United States, "Southbound" has so far reached number 12 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart and number 6 on the <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart, <sup id="cite_ref-Country_Airplay_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Country_Airplay-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> becoming Underwood's 28th top 10 hit on that chart.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It debuted at number 96 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart and has since moved to number 66.
</p><p>As of September 2019, it has sold 74,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southbound_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The official music video for the song debuted on Underwood's <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel on June 8, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The video was filmed on <a href="/wiki/Cinco_de_Mayo" title="Cinco de Mayo">Cinco de Mayo</a> and features some of Underwood's friends and family, including her husband <a href="/wiki/Mike_Fisher_(ice_hockey)" title="Mike Fisher (ice hockey)">Mike Fisher</a>. Directed by Jeff Venable, it features behind-the-scenes footage of Underwood's 2019 <a href="/wiki/Cry_Pretty_Tour_360" title="Cry Pretty Tour 360">Cry Pretty Tour 360</a>, intercut with Underwood, her husband, and family and friends having a big celebration at a dock on Lake Travis in <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Southbound_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood gave the first live performance of the song at the 54th <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music awards</a> on April 7, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> She performed the song again on the <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> finale on May 19, 2019. Underwood also used the song to open the <a href="/wiki/Cry_Pretty_Tour_360" title="Cry Pretty Tour 360">Cry Pretty Tour 360</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Underwood also performed it on the 2019 <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Awards" title="CMT Music Awards">CMT Music Awards</a> at The <a href="/wiki/Parthenon_(Nashville)" title="Parthenon (Nashville)">Parthenon</a> on June 5, 2019 in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville.</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> She included the song in her set at <a href="/wiki/Glastonbury_Festival" title="Glastonbury Festival">Glastonbury Festival</a> in June 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
