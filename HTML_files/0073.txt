<p>"<b>Undo It</b>" is a song written by <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kara_DioGuardi" title="Kara DioGuardi">Kara DioGuardi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marti_Frederiksen" title="Marti Frederiksen">Marti Frederiksen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luke_Laird" title="Luke Laird">Luke Laird</a>. It was made available to digital retailers on October 27, 2009, and was released as an official single on May 24, 2010. The song is the third single from Underwood's third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" title="Play On (Carrie Underwood album)">Play On</a></i>.
</p><p>Underwood performed the song at the 2010 <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(Season_9)" class="mw-redirect" title="American Idol (Season 9)">American Idol</a></i> finale, as well as at the 2010 <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Awards" title="CMT Music Awards">CMT Music Awards</a>. "Undo It" has sold 1.6 million copies in the United States, making it the 7th biggest song of her career.<sup id="cite_ref-USsales_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USsales-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was also her first song to chart in Australia, peaking at Number 85. 
</p><p>"Undo It" was ranked number 29 on <i>Billboard</i>'s year-end charts for Top Country Songs.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Underwood told The Boot that the idea for the "Undo It" hook came accidentally. "The guys had their guitars and they were playing something kind of bluesy, and Kara's got such a great R&amp;B sense about her," she said. "And I started, 'Na, na, na-ing' to whatever they were playing. And they were like, 'What if that was the hook?' I said, 'Aw, I don't know?' And before you knew it, we had 'Uh, uh, uh undo it.' I mean, who would have thought? How do you write that down on paper? It was something that it didn't take that long to write and it's so much fun to sing on stage, and people get into it."<sup id="cite_ref-boot_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boot-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undo_It&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song has received generally mixed reviews from music critics. Juli Thanki of Engine 145 gave the song a thumbs-down, calling it "a third-rate tune from <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Clarkson" title="Kelly Clarkson">Kelly Clarkson</a>’s reject pile" and saying that it had "deeply stupid lyrics."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Roughstock critic Matt Bjorke rated the song two-and-a-half stars out of five, saying that it had "catchy repeatable lyrics" but concluded his review by saying "she can do so much better."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In his review for <a href="/wiki/Allmusic" class="mw-redirect" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_Erlewine" title="Stephen Thomas Erlewine">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</a> called the song "strained sassiness."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> critic Deborah Evans Price was more positive in her review, comparing it to the "vengeful Carrie persona" of "<a href="/wiki/Before_He_Cheats" title="Before He Cheats">Before He Cheats</a>" and saying that it had "crisp, compelling" production.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Kevin John Coyne of Country Universe gave the song an A- calling Undo It a short, sweet, and undeniably catchy song which features both “We Will Rock You” drum riffs and twangy fiddle, as if those two things together are as natural as peanut butter and chocolate.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undo_It&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Country Music Television">CMT</a> premiered the video for "Undo It" on May 7, 2010.  The video features shots of Underwood performing the song in concert along with shots of the background video played during the performance of the song in the Play On Tour. The video was directed by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hicky" title="Chris Hicky">Chris Hicky</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Undo_It&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Undo It" debuted at number 43 on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart for the week of May 1, 2010. The song hit number one for the week of August 7, 2010, giving Underwood her tenth number one on this chart. The song debuted at number 86 on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a> before the release of her album <i>Play On</i>, and re-entered the chart at number 95 following its release as an official single. After performing the song on the finale of <i>American Idol</i>, the song rose to a new peak of number 25, becoming the second most successful single from <i>Play On</i>. It then rose to number 23 after performing it at the 2010 CMT Music Awards. The song became her eleventh Top 40 Hot 100 hit. The song re-entered once again at number 67 and stayed there for five more weeks before it dropped off the chart. On the week of June 27, 2011, "Undo It" entered the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Chart" class="mw-redirect" title="ARIA Chart">Australian Singles Chart</a> at number 85, becoming her first entrance to the chart in her career.
</p><p>As of October 2015, "Undo It" has sold 1,600,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
