<p><b>West Yorkshire</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_county" title="Metropolitan county">metropolitan county</a> in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It is an inland and in relative terms upland county having eastward-draining valleys while taking in moors of the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a> and has a population of <a href="/wiki/List_of_ceremonial_counties_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="List of ceremonial counties of England">2.2 million</a>. West Yorkshire came into existence as a metropolitan county in 1974 after the passage of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>West Yorkshire consists of five <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan boroughs</a> (<a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford" title="City of Bradford">City of Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a>, <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/City_of_Wakefield" title="City of Wakefield">City of Wakefield</a>) and is bordered by the counties of <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a> to the south, <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a> to the south-west, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> to the north-west, <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a> to the north and east, and <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a> to the south and south-east.
</p><p>Remnants of strong coal, wool and iron ore industries remain in the county, having attracted people over the centuries, and this can be seen in the buildings and architecture. Major railways and two major motorways traverse the county, which also contains <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds Bradford International Airport">Leeds Bradford International Airport</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_County_Council" title="West Yorkshire County Council">West Yorkshire County Council</a> was abolished in 1986, so its five districts became effectively <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authorities</a>. However, the metropolitan county, which covers an area of 2,029 square kilometres (783&#160;sq&#160;mi), continues to exist in law, and as a geographic frame of reference.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Since 1 April 2014 West Yorkshire has been a <a href="/wiki/Combined_authority" title="Combined authority">combined authority</a> area, with the local authorities pooling together some functions over transport and regeneration as the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Combined_Authority" title="West Yorkshire Combined Authority">West Yorkshire Combined Authority</a>.
</p><p>West Yorkshire includes the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Urban_Area" title="West Yorkshire Urban Area">West Yorkshire Urban Area</a>, which is the biggest and most built-up <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban area</a> within the <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">historic county boundaries</a> of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Leeds" title="History of Leeds">History of Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/History_of_Yorkshire" title="History of Yorkshire">History of Yorkshire</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:KirkstallAbbey.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/KirkstallAbbey.JPG/220px-KirkstallAbbey.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/KirkstallAbbey.JPG/330px-KirkstallAbbey.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/KirkstallAbbey.JPG/440px-KirkstallAbbey.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:KirkstallAbbey.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kirkstall_Abbey" title="Kirkstall Abbey">Kirkstall Abbey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kirkstall" title="Kirkstall">Kirkstall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a></div></div></div>

<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Leeds_Rathaus.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Leeds_Rathaus.jpg/220px-Leeds_Rathaus.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Leeds_Rathaus.jpg/330px-Leeds_Rathaus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Leeds_Rathaus.jpg/440px-Leeds_Rathaus.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1552" data-file-height="1164" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Leeds_Rathaus.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Leeds_Town_Hall" title="Leeds Town Hall">Leeds Town Hall</a></div></div></div>
<p>West Yorkshire was formed as a metropolitan county in 1974, by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, and corresponds roughly to the core of the <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">historic</a> <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding</a> of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> and the county boroughs of Bradford, Dewsbury, Halifax, Huddersfield, Leeds, and Wakefield.
</p><p>West Yorkshire Metropolitan County Council inherited the use of <a href="/wiki/County_Hall,_Wakefield" title="County Hall, Wakefield">West Riding County Hall</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>, opened in 1898, from the West Riding County Council in 1974.  Since 1987 it has been the headquarters of Wakefield City Council.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The county initially had a two-tier structure of local government with a strategic-level county council and five districts providing most services.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 1986, throughout England the metropolitan county councils were abolished. The functions of the county council were devolved to the boroughs; joint-boards covering fire, police and public transport; and to other special joint arrangements.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Organisations such as the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Metro" title="West Yorkshire Metro">West Yorkshire Metro</a> (governed by the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Police_and_Crime_Commissioner" title="West Yorkshire Police and Crime Commissioner">West Yorkshire Police and Crime Commissioner</a>) continue to operate on this basis.
</p><p>Although the county council was abolished, West Yorkshire continues to form a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_and_non-metropolitan_counties_of_England" title="Metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties of England">metropolitan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties_of_England" title="Ceremonial counties of England">ceremonial</a> county with a <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_West_Yorkshire" title="Lord Lieutenant of West Yorkshire">Lord Lieutenant of West Yorkshire</a> and a <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_West_Yorkshire" title="High Sheriff of West Yorkshire">High Sheriff</a>.
</p><p>Wakefield's Parish Church was raised to cathedral status in 1888 and after the elevation of Wakefield to diocese, Wakefield Council immediately sought city status and this was granted in July 1888.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> However the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="Industrial revolution">industrial revolution</a>, which changed West and South Yorkshire significantly, led to the growth of Leeds and Bradford, which became the area's two largest cities (Leeds being the largest in Yorkshire).  Leeds was granted city status in 1893 and Bradford in 1897.  The name of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Town_Hall" title="Leeds Town Hall">Leeds Town Hall</a> reflects the fact that at its opening in 1858 Leeds was not yet a city, while Bradford renamed its Town Hall as City Hall in 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Yorksgeology.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Yorksgeology.jpg/220px-Yorksgeology.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="181" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Yorksgeology.jpg/330px-Yorksgeology.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Yorksgeology.jpg/440px-Yorksgeology.jpg 2x" data-file-width="549" data-file-height="451" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Yorksgeology.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Geology of Yorkshire</div></div></div>
<p>The county borders, going anticlockwise from the west: <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>. The terrain of the county mostly consists of the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a> and its foothills which dominate the west of the county and gradually descend into the <a href="/wiki/Vale_of_York" title="Vale of York">Vale of York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Humberhead_Levels" title="Humberhead Levels">Humberhead Levels</a> in the east. Geologically, it lies almost entirely on rocks of carboniferous age which form the inner Southern Pennine fringes in the west<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and the Yorkshire coalfield further eastwards.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In the extreme east of the metropolitan county there are younger deposits of <a href="/wiki/Magnesian_Limestone" title="Magnesian Limestone">Magnesian Limestone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Areas in the west such as Bradford and Calderdale are dominated by the scenery of the eastern slopes of the <a href="/wiki/South_Pennines" title="South Pennines">South Pennines</a>, dropping from upland in the west down to the east, and dissected by many steep-sided valleys while a small part of the northern <a href="/wiki/Peak_District" title="Peak District">Peak District</a> extends into the south west of Kirklees. Large-scale industry, housing, public and commercial buildings of differing heights, transport routes and open countryside conjoin. The dense network of roads, canals and railways and urban development, confined by valleys creates dramatic interplay of views between settlements and the surrounding hillsides, as shaped the first urban-rural juxtapositions of <a href="/wiki/David_Hockney" title="David Hockney">David Hockney</a>. Where most rural the land crops up in the such rhymes and folklore as <a href="/wiki/On_Ilkley_Moor_Bah_%27Tat" class="mw-redirect" title="On Ilkley Moor Bah &#39;Tat">On Ilkley Moor Bah 'Tat</a>, date unknown, the early 19th century novels and poems of the <a href="/wiki/Bront%C3%AB_family" title="Brontë family">Brontë family</a> often in and around <a href="/wiki/Haworth" title="Haworth">Haworth</a> and long-running light comedy-drama <i><a href="/wiki/Last_of_the_Summer_Wine" title="Last of the Summer Wine">Last of the Summer Wine</a></i> in the 20th century.
</p><p>The carboniferous rocks of the Yorkshire coalfield further east have produced a rolling landscape with hills, escarpments and broad valleys in the outer fringes of the Pennines. In this landscape there is widespread evidence of both current and former industrial activity. There are numerous derelict or converted mine buildings and recently landscaped former spoil heaps.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The scenery is a mixture of built up areas, industrial land with some dereliction, and farmed open country. Ribbon developments along transport routes including canal, road and rail are prominent features of the area although some remnants of the pre industrial landscape and semi-natural vegetation still survive. However, many areas are affected by urban fringe pressures creating fragmented and downgraded landscapes and ever present are urban influences from major cities, smaller industrial towns and former mining villages.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Magnesian_Limestone" title="Magnesian Limestone">Magnesian Limestone</a> belt to the east of the Leeds and Wakefield areas is an elevated ridge with smoothly rolling scenery, dissected by dry valleys. Here, there is a large number of country houses and estates with parkland, estate woodlands, plantations and game coverts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The rivers <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">Aire</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">Calder</a> drain the area, flowing from west to east.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Green_belt">Green belt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Green belt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/South_and_West_Yorkshire_Green_Belt" title="South and West Yorkshire Green Belt">South and West Yorkshire Green Belt</a></div>
<p>West Yorkshire contains <a href="/wiki/Green_belt_(United_Kingdom)" title="Green belt (United Kingdom)">green belt</a> interspersed throughout the county, surrounding the West Yorkshire Urban Area. It was first drawn up in the 1950s. All the county's districts contain large portions of belt.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>West Yorkshire has an Oceanic climate, similar to other parts of the United Kingdom. West Yorkshire tends to be cooler than counties further south, due to inland location and high elevation (especially in western areas). Snow is common, as are sub-zero temperatures. In December 2010, many rivers in West Yorkshire froze over.
</p><p>Temperatures over the year usually remain between −1&#160;°C (30&#160;°F) and 20&#160;°C (68&#160;°F) with rare extremes near 30&#160;°C (86&#160;°F) and −16&#160;°C (3&#160;°F).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_civil_parishes_in_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="List of civil parishes in West Yorkshire">List of civil parishes in West Yorkshire</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_arms_of_West_Yorkshire_County_Council.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Coat_of_arms_of_West_Yorkshire_County_Council.png/220px-Coat_of_arms_of_West_Yorkshire_County_Council.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="224" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Coat_of_arms_of_West_Yorkshire_County_Council.png/330px-Coat_of_arms_of_West_Yorkshire_County_Council.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Coat_of_arms_of_West_Yorkshire_County_Council.png/440px-Coat_of_arms_of_West_Yorkshire_County_Council.png 2x" data-file-width="1500" data-file-height="1526" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Coat_of_arms_of_West_Yorkshire_County_Council.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The coat of arms of the former West Yorkshire County Council</div></div></div>
<p>In Parliament, 17 out of 22 of West Yorkshire's MPs are <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> and 5 are Conservative. At local level, the councils are generally divided, apart from the Wakefield district, which has long been one of the safest Labour councils in the country.
</p><p>Certain services are provided across the county by <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Joint_Services" title="West Yorkshire Joint Services">West Yorkshire Joint Services</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Police" title="West Yorkshire Police">West Yorkshire Police</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Fire_and_Rescue_Service" title="West Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service">West Yorkshire Fire and Rescue Service</a> are also county-wide.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industries">Industries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Industries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>West Yorkshire grew up around several industries. <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a> and <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> were grown through the development of <a href="/wiki/Woolen" title="Woolen">woollen</a> mills, Leeds' traditional industry was the manufacturing of cloth, while heavier engineering industries facilitated growth in South Leeds. Wakefield, Castleford, Pontefract and South and East Leeds were traditional <a href="/wiki/Coal_mining" title="Coal mining">coal mining</a> areas. The woollen and cloth industries declined throughout the twentieth century. Many of the coal mines in West Yorkshire closed during the <a href="/wiki/Alf_Robens" class="mw-redirect" title="Alf Robens">Robens</a> era in the 1960s, but mining was still a significant employer in the Wakefield district at the time of the 1984–85 strike. The last pit in West Yorkshire to close was Hay Royds Colliery at <a href="/wiki/Denby_Dale" title="Denby Dale">Denby Dale</a> in 2012 after a flood.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Leeds has since attracted investment from financial institutions, to become a recognised financial centre, with many <a href="/wiki/Bank" title="Bank">banks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Building_societies" class="mw-redirect" title="Building societies">building societies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Insurance_companies" class="mw-redirect" title="Insurance companies">insurance companies</a> having offices in the city.  Wakefield has also attracted many service-based industries, in particular <a href="/wiki/Call_centres" class="mw-redirect" title="Call centres">call centres</a>. Two of the big four supermarkets are from West Yorkshire. <a href="/wiki/Morrisons" title="Morrisons">Morrisons</a> is based in Bradford, while <a href="/wiki/Asda" title="Asda">Asda</a> is based in Leeds.  <a href="/wiki/Netto_(store)" title="Netto (store)">Netto</a> have their British headquarters in South Elmsall.
</p>
<dl><dt>Rhubarb</dt></dl>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Rhubarb_Triangle" title="Rhubarb Triangle">Rhubarb Triangle</a> is wholly in West Yorkshire and still produces the vegetable in considerable quantities. Twelve farmers who farm within the Rhubarb Triangle applied to have the name "Yorkshire forced rhubarb" added to the list of foods and drinks that have their names legally protected by the European Commission's Protected Food Name scheme.<sup id="cite_ref-Application_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Application-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The application was successful and the farmers in the Rhubarb Triangle were awarded Protected Designation of Origin status (PDO) in February 2010. Food protected status accesses European funding to promote the product and legal backing against other products made outside the area using the name. Other protected names include <a href="/wiki/Stilton_cheese" title="Stilton cheese">Stilton cheese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Champagne_(wine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Champagne (wine)">Champagne</a> and Parma Ham.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television_productions">Film and television productions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Film and television productions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Several films and television series have been filmed in West Yorkshire's historic areas, particularly around the town of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> For example, portions of the BBC television series <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_Valley_(TV_series)" title="Happy Valley (TV series)">Happy Valley</a></i> were filmed in Huddersfield; in addition to exteriors, some of the studio filming was done at North Light Film Studios at Brookes Mill, Huddersfield. As well, interiors for the BBC's <i><a href="/wiki/Jamaica_Inn_(2014_TV_series)" title="Jamaica Inn (2014 TV series)">Jamaica Inn</a></i>, for the BBC's <i><a href="/wiki/Remember_Me_(TV_series)" title="Remember Me (TV series)">Remember Me</a></i> and for ITV series <i><a href="/wiki/Black_Work" title="Black Work">Black Work</a></i>, were also filmed at the studios.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> More recently, many of the exteriors of the BBC series <i><a href="/wiki/Jericho_(2016_TV_series)" title="Jericho (2016 TV series)">Jericho</a></i> were filmed at the nearby Rockingstone Quarry and some interior work was done at North Light Film Studios.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="#Places_of_interest"> §&#160;Places of interest</a></div>
<p>Urban tourism varies.  National interest features include sporting stadia, museums, theatre and galleries. Royal Armouries is in Leeds, as is the West Yorkshire Playhouse, Opera North and The Grand Theatre. The <a href="/wiki/First_Direct_Arena" title="First Direct Arena">First Direct Arena</a> in Leeds seats around 15,000 people. <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Arena" title="Sheffield Arena">Sheffield Arena</a> is also popular, as is the <a href="/wiki/Alhambra_Theatre" class="mw-redirect" title="Alhambra Theatre">Alhambra Theatre</a>, St Georges Hall and the Media and Science Museum in Bradford.  Leeds is the most popular shopping destination in West Yorkshire, probably Yorkshire and rivals Manchester having claim to Briggate, the Headrow, <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Leeds" title="Trinity Leeds">Trinity Leeds</a>, Victoria Gate, the Victoria and Northern Quarters, the biggest indoor market in Europe and the <a href="/wiki/White_Rose_Centre" title="White Rose Centre">White Rose Centre</a>, as well as many 'first outside of London' labels such as <a href="/wiki/Harvey_Nichols" title="Harvey Nichols">Harvey Nichols</a> and <a href="/wiki/Victoria%27s_Secret" title="Victoria&#39;s Secret">Victoria's Secret</a>.  Leeds is also a popular nightlife destination domestically, which is not surprising given its accessibility and central location. All cities are well connected via rail and road, <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds railway station</a> is an important hub seeing 29.7 million passengers 2015–16, making it the fourth busiest station in the UK (London altogether being classed as number one) after London, <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_New_Street_railway_station" title="Birmingham New Street railway station">Birmingham New Street</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_Central_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Glasgow Central railway station">Glasgow Central</a>. It is the busiest in Northern England.
</p><p>Signposted walks follow rivers and the escarpment of the Pennines, which is scaled in meandering stages and tunnels by the recreational <a href="/wiki/Leeds-Liverpool_Canal" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds-Liverpool Canal">Leeds-Liverpool Canal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rochdale_Canal" title="Rochdale Canal">Rochdale Canal</a>, navigatable by barge, canoe or kayak.  Other tourism features include abbeys, castles, countryside walks, landscapes, picturesque villages, architecture, stately homes, <a href="/wiki/Tea_room" class="mw-redirect" title="Tea room">tea rooms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Real_ale" class="mw-redirect" title="Real ale">real ale</a> breweries, farmer's markets, restaurants and hiking in villages including <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Bridge" title="Hebden Bridge">Hebden Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ilkley" title="Ilkley">Ilkley</a> with its scenic riversides, cherry blossoms and suspension bridge and equally in Wharfedale, <a href="/wiki/Otley" title="Otley">Otley</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:LBIA_terminal_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/LBIA_terminal_1.jpg/220px-LBIA_terminal_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/LBIA_terminal_1.jpg/330px-LBIA_terminal_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/LBIA_terminal_1.jpg/440px-LBIA_terminal_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:LBIA_terminal_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_Airport" title="Leeds Bradford Airport">Leeds Bradford Airport</a></div></div></div>
<p>West Yorkshire lies in arguably the most strategic part of Yorkshire: the <a href="/wiki/M62_Motorway" class="mw-redirect" title="M62 Motorway">M62</a>, <a href="/wiki/M1_Motorway" class="mw-redirect" title="M1 Motorway">M1</a> and the <a href="/wiki/A1(M)" class="mw-redirect" title="A1(M)">A1(M)</a> pass through the county, as well as the internal urban motorways in <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>. West Yorkshire has two mainline railway stations, <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Westgate_railway_station" title="Wakefield Westgate railway station">Wakefield Westgate</a>. Leeds railway station is the only <a href="/wiki/Network_Rail" title="Network Rail">Network Rail</a> principal station in Yorkshire and <a href="/wiki/North_East_England" title="North East England">North East England</a>, and one of only three in the North of England along with <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Piccadilly_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Piccadilly railway station">Manchester Piccadilly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Lime_Street_railway_station" title="Liverpool Lime Street railway station">Liverpool Lime Street</a>. Other important railway stations in West Yorkshire include <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange" title="Bradford Interchange">Bradford Interchange</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Forster_Square_railway_station" title="Bradford Forster Square railway station">Bradford Forster Square</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax_railway_station_(West_Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Halifax railway station (West Yorkshire)">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_railway_station" title="Dewsbury railway station">Dewsbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keighley_railway_station" title="Keighley railway station">Keighley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shipley_railway_station" title="Shipley railway station">Shipley</a>. West Yorkshire also has Yorkshire's largest airport, <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_Airport" title="Leeds Bradford Airport">Leeds Bradford Airport</a>.
</p><p>Unlike South Yorkshire, West Yorkshire has no light transit system; the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Supertram" title="Leeds Supertram">Leeds Supertram</a> was proposed but was later cancelled after the withdrawal of government funding. Public transport is run under the authority of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Metro" title="West Yorkshire Metro">West Yorkshire Metro</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are three major football clubs in West Yorkshire:  <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_F.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Town F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a>, which is in the <a href="/wiki/EFL_Championship" title="EFL Championship">EFL Championship</a> of English football; <a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_A.F.C." title="Bradford City A.F.C.">Bradford City</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leeds_United_A.F.C." class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds United A.F.C.">Leeds United</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">Rugby league</a> is also big in West Yorkshire. The teams who are, or have been, in <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a> are <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Bulls" title="Bradford Bulls">Bradford Bulls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castleford_Tigers" title="Castleford Tigers">Castleford Tigers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax_R.L.F.C." title="Halifax R.L.F.C.">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Giants" title="Huddersfield Giants">Huddersfield Giants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Rhinos" title="Leeds Rhinos">Leeds Rhinos</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Trinity" title="Wakefield Trinity">Wakefield Trinity</a>. Other rugby league clubs in West Yorkshire are <a href="/wiki/Batley_Bulldogs" title="Batley Bulldogs">Batley Bulldogs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_Rams" title="Dewsbury Rams">Dewsbury Rams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Featherstone_Rovers" title="Featherstone Rovers">Featherstone Rovers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunslet_R.L.F.C." title="Hunslet R.L.F.C.">Hunslet Hawks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Keighley_Cougars" title="Keighley Cougars">Keighley Cougars</a>. Any combination of these teams playing against each other would be called a West Yorkshire derby even if the rivalry is not as great as other rivalries between teams in the area. The main rugby union club in the county is <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Carnegie" title="Yorkshire Carnegie">Yorkshire Carnegie</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Headingley_Stadium" title="Headingley Stadium">Headingley Stadium</a> is home of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Yorkshire County Cricket Club">Yorkshire County Cricket Club</a>, Leeds Rhinos and Yorkshire Carnegie, whereas the <a href="/wiki/John_Smith%27s_Stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="John Smith&#39;s Stadium">John Smith's Stadium</a> is home of Huddersfield Town and Huddersfield Giants. <a href="/wiki/Valley_Parade" title="Valley Parade">Valley Parade</a> is home of Bradford City, whereas the <a href="/wiki/Odsal_Stadium" title="Odsal Stadium">Odsal Stadium</a> is home of the Bradford Bulls.
</p><p>There are two racecourses in West Yorkshire: <a href="/wiki/Pontefract_Racecourse" title="Pontefract Racecourse">Pontefract</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Racecourse" title="Wetherby Racecourse">Wetherby</a>
</p><p>West Yorkshire also used to host regular <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_speedway" title="Motorcycle speedway">speedway</a> meetings, having the <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Dukes" title="Halifax Dukes">Halifax Dukes</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Dukes" title="Bradford Dukes">Bradford Dukes</a> teams. <a href="/wiki/Odsal_Stadium" title="Odsal Stadium">Odsal Stadium</a> used to host <a href="/wiki/BriSCA_Formula_1_Stock_Cars" title="BriSCA Formula 1 Stock Cars">BriSCA</a> stock cars. Leeds has a hill climb event at <a href="/wiki/Harewood_speed_Hillclimb" title="Harewood speed Hillclimb">Harewood speed Hillclimb</a>.
</p>
