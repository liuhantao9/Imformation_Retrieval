<p>The <b>Dutch government in exile</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">Nederlandse regering in ballingschap</i>), also known as the <b>London Cabinet</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">Londens kabinet</i>), was the <a href="/wiki/Government_in_exile" title="Government in exile">government in exile</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, headed by <a href="/wiki/Wilhelmina_of_the_Netherlands" title="Wilhelmina of the Netherlands">Queen Wilhelmina</a>, that evacuated to <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Netherlands" title="Battle of the Netherlands">German invasion</a> of the country during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> on 10 May 1940.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_exile">Background and exile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_government-in-exile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and exile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to 1940, the Netherlands was a neutral country, generally on good terms with Germany. On 10 May 1940, Germany invaded the Netherlands.  <a href="/wiki/Wilhelmina_of_the_Netherlands" title="Wilhelmina of the Netherlands">Queen Wilhelmina</a> fled the country by sea, arriving in London on 13 May.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Netherlands armed forces surrendered two days later as it had been unable to withstand the speed of Germany's blitzkrieg style attack. In London, the queen took charge of the Dutch government in exile, which was established at Stratton House in the <a href="/wiki/Piccadilly" title="Piccadilly">Piccadilly</a> area of London, opposite <a href="/wiki/Green_Park" title="Green Park">Green Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Initially, their hope was that <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> would regroup and liberate the country.  Although there was an attempt in this direction, it soon failed, because the Allied forces were surrounded and forced to evacuate at <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dunkirk" title="Battle of Dunkirk">Dunkirk</a>. The Dutch armed forces in the Netherlands, except for those occupying Zeeland, surrendered on 15 May 1940. In Britain, Queen Wilhelmina took charge of the Dutch government in exile.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>To safeguard the succession, the heir to the throne <a href="/wiki/Juliana_of_the_Netherlands" title="Juliana of the Netherlands">Princess Juliana</a> and her family were sent farther to Canada, where they spent the war.
</p><p>The government-in-exile was soon faced with a dilemma. After France had been defeated, the <a href="/wiki/Vichy_France" title="Vichy France">Vichy French</a> government came to power, proposing to <a href="/wiki/Hitler" class="mw-redirect" title="Hitler">Hitler</a> a policy of collaboration. This led to a conflict between Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Dirk_Jan_de_Geer" title="Dirk Jan de Geer">Dirk Jan de Geer</a> and the Queen. De Geer wanted to return to the Netherlands and collaborate as well. The government in exile was still in control of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a> with all its resources: it was the third largest oil producer at the time (after the US and the USSR). Wilhelmina realized that if the Dutch collaborated with Germany, the Dutch East Indies would be surrendered to <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, as <a href="/wiki/French_Indochina" title="French Indochina">French Indochina</a> was surrendered later by orders of the Vichy government.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Exile_in_London">Exile in London</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_government-in-exile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Exile in London">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Because the Netherlands' hope for liberation was now the entry of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">USSR</a> into the war, the Queen dismissed her prime minister, De Geer, and replaced him with <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Sjoerds_Gerbrandy" title="Pieter Sjoerds Gerbrandy">Pieter Sjoerds Gerbrandy</a>, who worked with Churchill and Roosevelt on ways to smooth the path for an American entry. <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao_and_Dependencies" title="Curaçao and Dependencies">Curaçao</a>, the then world-class exporting oil refineries, were important suppliers of refined products to the Allies. Aruba became a British protectorate from 1940 to 1942 and a US protectorate from 1942 to 1945. On November 23, 1941, under an agreement with the Netherlands government-in-exile, the United States occupied <a href="/wiki/Surinam_(Dutch_colony)" title="Surinam (Dutch colony)">Dutch Guiana</a> to protect the <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a> mines.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> An oil boycott was imposed on Japan, which helped to spark the <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">Pearl Harbor attack</a>.
</p><p>In September 1944, the Dutch, <a href="/wiki/Belgian_government_in_exile" title="Belgian government in exile">Belgian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luxembourgish_government-in-exile" class="mw-redirect" title="Luxembourgish government-in-exile">Luxembourgish governments in exile</a> began formulating an agreement over the creation of a <a href="/wiki/Benelux" title="Benelux">Benelux Customs Union</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walsh1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walsh1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The agreement was signed in the <a href="/wiki/London_Customs_Convention" title="London Customs Convention">London Customs Convention</a> on 5 September 1944.
</p><p>The Queen's unusual action was later ratified by the Dutch Parliament in 1946. Churchill called her "the only man in the Dutch government".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After World War II ended, Wilhelmina and her government returned from exile to re-establish a regime more democratic than ever before.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
