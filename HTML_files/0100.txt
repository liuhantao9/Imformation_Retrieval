<p><b>Sylvia "Jocelyn" Llewelyn Davies</b> (25 November 1866 – 27 August 1910), <i>née</i> <b>Sylvia du Maurier</b>, was the mother of <a href="/wiki/Llewelyn_Davies_boys" title="Llewelyn Davies boys">the boys</a> who were the inspiration for the stories of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Pan" title="Peter Pan">Peter Pan</a> by <a href="/wiki/J._M._Barrie" title="J. M. Barrie">J. M. Barrie</a>. She was the daughter of cartoonist and writer <a href="/wiki/George_du_Maurier" title="George du Maurier">George du Maurier</a> and his wife Emma Wightwick, the elder sister to actor <a href="/wiki/Gerald_du_Maurier" title="Gerald du Maurier">Gerald du Maurier</a>, the aunt of novelists <a href="/wiki/Angela_du_Maurier" title="Angela du Maurier">Angela</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daphne_du_Maurier" title="Daphne du Maurier">Daphne du Maurier</a> and a great-granddaughter of <a href="/wiki/Mary_Anne_Clarke" title="Mary Anne Clarke">Mary Anne Clarke</a>, royal mistress of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Frederick,_Duke_of_York_and_Albany" title="Prince Frederick, Duke of York and Albany">Prince Frederick, Duke of York and Albany</a>.
</p><p>She met the young barrister <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Llewelyn_Davies" title="Arthur Llewelyn Davies">Arthur Llewelyn Davies</a> at a dinner party in 1889 and they became engaged shortly thereafter.<sup id="cite_ref-Birkin_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Birkin-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> She married him in 1892, and they had five children, all boys: <a href="/wiki/George_Llewelyn_Davies" title="George Llewelyn Davies">George</a> (1893–1915), <a href="/wiki/Jack_Llewelyn_Davies" title="Jack Llewelyn Davies">Jack</a> (1894–1959), <a href="/wiki/Peter_Llewelyn_Davies" title="Peter Llewelyn Davies">Peter</a> (1897–1960), <a href="/wiki/Michael_Llewelyn_Davies" title="Michael Llewelyn Davies">Michael</a> (1900–1921), and <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Llewelyn_Davies" title="Nicholas Llewelyn Davies">Nicholas</a> (Nico) (1903–1980).
</p><p>In 1898, Llewelyn Davies met Barrie at a dinner party, discovering he was already friends with her three sons from their regular visits to <a href="/wiki/Kensington_Gardens" title="Kensington Gardens">Kensington Gardens</a>. She and Barrie became close (he adopted the pet name "Jocelyn" for her), with him spending considerable time at the Llewelyn Davies's home, and the family accompanying Barrie and his wife on holidays. She encouraged her boys' friendship with him.
</p><p>Her husband died in 1907 of a <a href="/wiki/Sarcoma" title="Sarcoma">sarcoma</a> in his cheek. Llewelyn Davies welcomed Barrie's financial and emotional support, both for herself and for her boys. Following Barrie's divorce, he and Sylvia remained close, but did not marry. She became ill with an inoperable cancer in her chest, and died in 1910. Shortly before her death, she wrote that she wanted her boys' nurse Mary Hodgson to continue caring for them, and that she knew Barrie would continue providing for them, which he did. She named him, along with her mother Emma du Maurier, her brother <a href="/wiki/Guy_du_Maurier" title="Guy du Maurier">Guy du Maurier</a>, and Arthur's brother Crompton Llewelyn Davies as their guardians. Barrie told the boys after her death that she had been engaged to him, but Jack and Peter later expressed scepticism of this report.
</p><p>Her son Peter was the publisher of her niece <a href="/wiki/Daphne_du_Maurier" title="Daphne du Maurier">Daphne du Maurier</a>'s book about their grandfather, <i>The Young George du Maurier, letters 1860–1867</i> (1951).
</p><p>In the 1978 <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> <a href="/wiki/Mini-series" class="mw-redirect" title="Mini-series">mini-series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lost_Boys_(docudrama)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Lost Boys (docudrama)">The Lost Boys</a></i>, she was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Ann_Bell" title="Ann Bell">Ann Bell</a>. She was played by <a href="/wiki/Kate_Winslet" title="Kate Winslet">Kate Winslet</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2004_in_film" title="2004 in film">2004</a> movie <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Neverland_(film)" title="Finding Neverland (film)">Finding Neverland</a></i>, and was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Laura_Michelle_Kelly" title="Laura Michelle Kelly">Laura Michelle Kelly</a> in the Broadway-musical adaptation of the same name, which ran until the 21st of August, 2016.
</p>
