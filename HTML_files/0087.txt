<p><b>Earl of Wharncliffe</b>, in the West Riding of the County of York, is a title in the <a href="/wiki/Peerage_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Peerage of the United Kingdom">Peerage of the United Kingdom</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_of_Wharncliffe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The title was created in 1876 for <a href="/wiki/Edward_Montagu-Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie,_1st_Earl_of_Wharncliffe" title="Edward Montagu-Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie, 1st Earl of Wharncliffe">Edward Montagu-Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie, 3rd Baron Wharncliffe</a>. He was a descendant of <a href="/wiki/Edward_Wortley_Montagu_(diplomat)" title="Edward Wortley Montagu (diplomat)">Edward Wortley Montagu</a> (grandson of <a href="/wiki/Edward_Montagu,_1st_Earl_of_Sandwich" title="Edward Montagu, 1st Earl of Sandwich">Edward Montagu, 1st Earl of Sandwich</a>,) and his wife, the author <a href="/wiki/Lady_Mary_Wortley_Montagu" title="Lady Mary Wortley Montagu">Lady Mary Wortley Montagu</a>. Their daughter, <a href="/wiki/Mary_Stuart,_Countess_of_Bute" title="Mary Stuart, Countess of Bute">Mary</a>, married the future Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/John_Stuart,_3rd_Earl_of_Bute" title="John Stuart, 3rd Earl of Bute">John Stuart, 3rd Earl of Bute</a> (see the <a href="/wiki/Marquess_of_Bute" title="Marquess of Bute">Marquess of Bute</a> for earlier history of the Stuart family). Their second son, the Hon. <a href="/wiki/James_Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie" title="James Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie">James</a>, succeeded to the Wortley estates in Yorkshire and Cornwall through his mother and assumed the additional surname of Wortley. In 1803 he also inherited the Scottish estates of his uncle <a href="/wiki/James_Stuart-Mackenzie" title="James Stuart-Mackenzie">James Stuart-Mackenzie</a>, and assumed the additional surname of Mackenzie. His second son <a href="/wiki/James_Stuart-Wortley,_1st_Baron_Wharncliffe" title="James Stuart-Wortley, 1st Baron Wharncliffe">James</a> was a soldier and prominent <a href="/wiki/British_Tory_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="British Tory Party">Tory</a> politician. In 1826 he was raised to the Peerage of the United Kingdom as <b>Baron Wharncliffe</b>, of <a href="/wiki/Wortley,_South_Yorkshire" title="Wortley, South Yorkshire">Wortley</a> in the <a href="/wiki/County_of_York" class="mw-redirect" title="County of York">County of York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was succeeded by his eldest son, John, the second Baron. He represented <a href="/wiki/Bossiney_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Bossiney (UK Parliament constituency)">Bossiney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perth_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Perth (UK Parliament constituency)">Perth</a> and the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="West Riding of Yorkshire (UK Parliament constituency)">West Riding of Yorkshire</a> in the <a href="/wiki/British_House_of_Commons" class="mw-redirect" title="British House of Commons">House of Commons</a>. On his death the title passed to his eldest son Edward, the third Baron. He was Chairman of the <a href="/wiki/Manchester,_Sheffield_and_Lincolnshire_Railway" title="Manchester, Sheffield and Lincolnshire Railway">Manchester, Sheffield and Lincolnshire Railway</a>, which under his leadership became the <a href="/wiki/Great_Central_Railway" title="Great Central Railway">Great Central Railway</a>. In 1876 he was created <b>Viscount Carlton</b>, of Carlton in the West Riding of the County of York, and <b>Earl of Wharncliffe</b>, in the West Riding of the County of York, with remainder to his younger brother the Hon. Francis Dudley Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie (1829–1893).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> These titles are also in the Peerage of the United Kingdom. In 1880 Lord Wharncliffe assumed the additional surname of Montagu. He was succeeded (in the viscountcy and earldom according to the special remainder) by his nephew Francis, the second Earl. He was the eldest son of the Hon. Francis Dudley Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie. This line of the family failed on the death of his grandson, the fourth Earl, in 1987. The late Earl was succeeded by his second cousin once removed, the fifth and present American born Earl, Richard Alan Montagu-Stuart-Wortley, of Portland, Maine. He is the elder son of Alan Ralph Montagu-Stuart-Wortley, only son of Ralph Montagu-Stuart-Wortley, only son of the Hon. Ralph Granville Montagu-Stuart-Wortley, younger brother of the second Earl. As a descendant of the third Earl of Bute he is also in remainder to the earldom of Bute and its subsidiary titles, now held by his kinsman <a href="/wiki/John_Crichton-Stuart,_7th_Marquess_of_Bute" title="John Crichton-Stuart, 7th Marquess of Bute">John Crichton-Stuart, 7th Marquess of Bute</a>.
</p><p>Several other members of this branch of the Stuart family have also gained distinction. <a href="/wiki/John_Stuart-Wortley" title="John Stuart-Wortley">John Stuart-Wortley</a>, younger brother of the first Baron, sat as <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for Bossiney. The Hon. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie" title="Charles Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie">Charles Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie</a>, second son of the first Baron, was also Member of Parliament for Bossiney. His daughter <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_Lady_Welby" title="Victoria, Lady Welby">Victoria, Lady Welby</a>, was a philosopher of language. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Stuart-Wortley,_1st_Baron_Stuart_of_Wortley" title="Charles Stuart-Wortley, 1st Baron Stuart of Wortley">Charles Stuart-Wortley, 1st Baron Stuart of Wortley</a>, was the son of the Hon. <a href="/wiki/James_Stuart-Wortley_(Liberal_politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="James Stuart-Wortley (Liberal politician)">James Stuart-Wortley</a>, third son of the first Baron (see the <a href="/wiki/Baron_Stuart_of_Wortley" class="mw-redirect" title="Baron Stuart of Wortley">Baron Stuart of Wortley</a> for more information on this branch of the family). The Hon. Edward James Montague-Stuart-Wortley (1857–1934), second son of the Hon. Francis, second son of the second Baron, was a <a href="/wiki/Major-General" class="mw-redirect" title="Major-General">Major-General</a> in the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>. The Hon. Sir (Alan) Richard Montagu-Stuart-Wortley (1868–1949), younger son of the Hon. Francis, second son of the second Baron, was a <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant-General" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant-General">Lieutenant-General</a> in the British Army. The Hon. <a href="/wiki/James_Stuart-Wortley_(New_Zealand_politician)" title="James Stuart-Wortley (New Zealand politician)">James Stuart-Wortley</a>, third son of the second Baron, was a member of the <a href="/wiki/1st_New_Zealand_Parliament" title="1st New Zealand Parliament">first Parliament of New Zealand</a>.
</p><p>The family seat was <a href="/wiki/Wortley_Hall" title="Wortley Hall">Wortley Hall</a> but the present Earl, Richard Wortley, is a distantly-related construction worker from <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>, USA. He did not inherit the estate, which had been reduced by death duties then passed to a different line of the family, namely to Lady Rowena Montagu Stuart Wortley Hunt, second and only surviving daughter of the 4th Earl; this was because the estate did not go with the title but was in the family before they became en-nobled.
</p>

<h2><span id="Earls_of_Wharncliffe_.281876.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Earls_of_Wharncliffe_(1876)">Earls of Wharncliffe (1876)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Earl_of_Wharncliffe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Earls of Wharncliffe (1876)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
