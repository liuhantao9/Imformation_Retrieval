<p><b>Hillary Lee Lindsey</b> is an American singer-songwriter born in Washington, Georgia. She has written songs with or for several artists including <a href="/wiki/Michelle_Branch" title="Michelle Branch">Michelle Branch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Faith_Hill" title="Faith Hill">Faith Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martina_McBride" title="Martina McBride">Martina McBride</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Antebellum" title="Lady Antebellum">Lady Antebellum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gary_Allan" title="Gary Allan">Gary Allan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sara_Evans" title="Sara Evans">Sara Evans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kellie_Pickler" title="Kellie Pickler">Kellie Pickler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bon_Jovi" title="Bon Jovi">Bon Jovi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tim_McGraw" title="Tim McGraw">Tim McGraw</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luke_Bryan" title="Luke Bryan">Luke Bryan</a>. In 2006 and 2016, respectively, Lindsey won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for Best Country Song for Carrie Underwood's "<a href="/wiki/Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel" title="Jesus, Take the Wheel">Jesus, Take the Wheel</a>" and for <a href="/wiki/Little_Big_Town" title="Little Big Town">Little Big Town</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Girl_Crush" title="Girl Crush">Girl Crush</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, Lindsey received an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> nomination for "Coming Home", recorded by <a href="/wiki/Gwyneth_Paltrow" title="Gwyneth Paltrow">Gwyneth Paltrow</a> for the soundtrack of <i><a href="/wiki/Country_Strong" title="Country Strong">Country Strong</a></i>, in the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Original_Song" title="Academy Award for Best Original Song">Best Original Song</a> category. "Coming Home" also received a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe</a> nomination that same year for Best Original Song along with "<a href="/wiki/There%27s_a_Place_for_Us_(song)" title="There&#39;s a Place for Us (song)">There's a Place for Us</a>", making Lindsey a double nominee in 2011. As of 2018<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hillary_Lindsey&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, she has had 20 number-one singles as a writer.
</p><p>She has been nominated twice for the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Song_of_the_Year" title="Grammy Award for Song of the Year">Grammy Award for Song of the Year</a> for her work on "Jesus Take the Wheel" and "<a href="/wiki/Girl_Crush" title="Girl Crush">Girl Crush</a>".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hillary_Lindsey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1994, Lindsey moved from Washington, Ga. to <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a> to enroll in <a href="/wiki/Belmont_University" title="Belmont University">Belmont University</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Belmont_University#Music_and_music_business_programs" title="Belmont University">music business school</a>. She tells the story<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> of a roommate who had an internship at a record label and took one of her tapes into work with her. The tape was passed around to publishers, and Lindsey was quickly signed to <a href="/wiki/Famous_Music_Publishing" class="mw-redirect" title="Famous Music Publishing">Famous Music Publishing</a>. In her first year as a writer she had eight cuts. She had her first number one song as a writer in 2002 with <a href="/wiki/Martina_McBride" title="Martina McBride">Martina McBride</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Blessed_(Martina_McBride_song)" title="Blessed (Martina McBride song)">Blessed</a>". Eleven of her number ones as a writer are <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> tracks including "<a href="/wiki/Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel" title="Jesus, Take the Wheel">Jesus Take The Wheel</a>", <a href="/wiki/Wasted_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Wasted (Carrie Underwood song)">"Wasted"</a>, "<a href="/wiki/So_Small" title="So Small">So Small</a>", <a href="/wiki/Just_a_Dream_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Just a Dream (Carrie Underwood song)">"Just A Dream"</a>, "<a href="/wiki/Last_Name_(song)" title="Last Name (song)">Last Name</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Two_Black_Cadillacs" title="Two Black Cadillacs">Two Black Cadillacs</a>". Thirty million records have been sold featuring her compositions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> She has had two songs featured on the ABC television series <i><a href="/wiki/Nashville_(2012_TV_series)" title="Nashville (2012 TV series)">Nashville</a></i>: "<a href="/wiki/Telescope_(song)" title="Telescope (song)">Telescope</a>" performed by <a href="/wiki/Hayden_Panettiere" title="Hayden Panettiere">Hayden Panettiere</a> and "Change Your Mind" performed by <a href="/wiki/Clare_Bowen" title="Clare Bowen">Clare Bowen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sam_Palladio" title="Sam Palladio">Sam Palladio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
