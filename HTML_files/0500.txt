<p><b>Oscar Roël Brandon</b> (born 8 August 1971) is a Surinamese badminton player, coach and Olympic team manager. He competed for Suriname at the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_1996_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_singles" title="Badminton at the 1996 Summer Olympics – Men&#39;s singles">1996 Summer Olympics</a> as a badminton player in the Men's singles event.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> And he was "chef de mission" (team manager) for Suriname at the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Suriname at the 2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics" title="Suriname at the 2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_Brandon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the age of six Oscar Brandon accompanied his dad to the badminton "Ismay van Wilgen sportshall" and when he turned 12 years old became a member of the SCVU (Sociaal Culturele Vereniging Uitvlugt) badminton club at the outskirts of the city of <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>. There he nurtured his talent under guidance and supervision of trainer and coach "Ma Lefi". He quickly became a junior champ and was then soon included in the National Juniors Squad of Suriname for centralized training.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_Brandon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His first big international success came in 1988 as a junior player winning the Carebaco juniors event in boys singles. In 1990 he reached the semi-finals in Men's Singles at the <a href="/wiki/1990_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="1990 Central American and Caribbean Games">1990 CACSO Games</a> in Mexico City. Unfortunately he lost the semi-final against experienced home player Ernesto de La Torre and then he lost the bronze medal match against Jamaican Robert Richards.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> After a short period of training and playing tournaments in the Netherlands, he decided to play a full year of badminton competition abroad in the Netherlands. There he was part of the famous BV van Zijderveld club in <a href="/wiki/Amstelveen" title="Amstelveen">Amstelveen</a>. He won a record of ten <a href="/wiki/Surinamese_National_Badminton_Championships" title="Surinamese National Badminton Championships">National</a> Men's Singles titles, the first in 1991 and then was undisputed from 1993 till 2001. In 1996 he received a wildcard to participate at the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_1996_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_singles" title="Badminton at the 1996 Summer Olympics – Men&#39;s singles">1996 Summer Olympics</a> a first in badminton for <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. At the Atlanta Olympics he would also become the first Olympic torch bearer for his country, participating in the <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics_torch_relay" title="1996 Summer Olympics torch relay">Olympic Torch Relay</a> in Miami. In the Men's Singles event at the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_1996_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_singles" title="Badminton at the 1996 Summer Olympics – Men&#39;s singles">1996 Summer Olympics</a> he lost his first match against <a href="/wiki/Jamie_Dawson" title="Jamie Dawson">Jamie Dawson</a> of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> 5-15, 4-15. In 1998 he became "Sportsman of the Year" in Suriname after reaching the 62nd place on the badminton world ranking, by winning the first edition of the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_International" title="Suriname International">Suriname International</a> and reaching the final of the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_International" title="Argentina International">Argentina International</a>. That year 1998 he also won both the <a href="/wiki/Argentina_International" title="Argentina International">Argentina International</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brazil_International" title="Brazil International">Brazil International São Paulo Cup 1998</a> in the mixed doubles event with <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adrienn_Kocsis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Adrienn Kocsis (page does not exist)">Adrienn Kocsis</a> a former Hungarian player playing for Peru.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 1999 Oscar Brandon participated in both the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_Pan_American_Games" title="Badminton at the Pan American Games">Pan Am Games</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1999_IBF_World_Championships" title="1999 IBF World Championships">World Championships</a>, a first for his country Suriname. Once again in 1999 he was chosen to be "Sportsman of the Year" in Suriname.  In 2001 he was crowned "Badminton player of the century" in his native Suriname.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-playing_career">Post-playing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oscar_Brandon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Post-playing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After his active career Oscar Brandon became a badminton coach,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  team manager and politician for his country.  In November 1998 he was chosen as a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(Suriname)" title="National Assembly (Suriname)">National Assembly</a>, the Parliament, representing the legislative branch of government in Suriname. He is also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_Olympic_Committee" title="Suriname Olympic Committee">Suriname Olympic Committee</a> and took up the "chef de mission" (team manager) role for Suriname at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="2010 Central American and Caribbean Games">2010 Central American and Caribbean Games</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2010_Summer_Youth_Olympics" title="2010 Summer Youth Olympics">2010 Summer Youth Olympics</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2010_South_American_Games" title="2010 South American Games">2010 South American Games</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Suriname at the 2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2014_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="2014 Central American and Caribbean Games">2014 Central American and Caribbean Games</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2014_South_American_Games" title="2014 South American Games">2014 South American Games</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_at_the_2014_Summer_Youth_Olympics" title="Suriname at the 2014 Summer Youth Olympics">2014 Summer Youth Olympics</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2015_Pan_American_Games" title="2015 Pan American Games">2015 Pan American Games</a>  and the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics" title="Suriname at the 2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
