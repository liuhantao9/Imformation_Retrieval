<p>"<b>Something in the Water</b>" is a song by American country music artist <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, from her first <a href="/wiki/Greatest_hits_album" title="Greatest hits album">greatest hits album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits:_Decade_Number_1" title="Greatest Hits: Decade Number 1">Greatest Hits: Decade #1</a></i>. The song was released on September 29, 2014, as the album's lead single through <a href="/wiki/Arista_Nashville" title="Arista Nashville">Arista Nashville</a>. It was written by Underwood, <a href="/wiki/Chris_DeStefano" title="Chris DeStefano">Chris DeStefano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brett_James" title="Brett James">Brett James</a>, and produced by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Bright_(record_producer)" title="Mark Bright (record producer)">Mark Bright</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Greatest_Hits_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greatest_Hits-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Crossover_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Crossover (music)">crossover</a> success, "Something in the Water" reached No. 1 on both the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hot_Christian_Songs" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Christian Songs">Hot Christian Songs</a> charts. It is Underwood's 14th career leader on Hot Country Songs and her second in 2014. It topped the former chart for seven weeks and the latter for 26 weeks, and also debuted at number 24 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_100" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 100">Hot 100</a>. It set and broke multiple <a href="/wiki/Airplay" title="Airplay">airplay</a> records on country radio in its first week.
</p><p>The song won the <a href="/wiki/57th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="57th Annual Grammy Awards">Grammy Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Solo_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Solo Performance">Best Country Solo Performance</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Something_in_the_Water_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:35%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">""Something in the Water" is so inspirational. It's so joyous!  I can't help but smile when I hear it. I hope it has the same effect on everyone who listens to it."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">—Underwood on the song <sup id="cite_ref-Greatest_Hits_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greatest_Hits-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The song was written by Underwood along with Chris DeStefano and Brett James, and produced by Underwood's longtime producer Mark Bright. It also has a sample of <a href="/wiki/Amazing_Grace" title="Amazing Grace">Amazing Grace</a> at the closing of the song, sung by Underwood.<sup id="cite_ref-Music_Times_September_2014_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Music_Times_September_2014-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>James told <i><a href="/wiki/Country_Weekly" class="mw-redirect" title="Country Weekly">Country Weekly</a></i> that, when he arrived at Underwood's cabin in <a href="/wiki/Leiper%27s_Fork,_Tennessee" title="Leiper&#39;s Fork, Tennessee">Leiper's Fork, Tennessee</a> for a songwriter's session, she and DeStefano already had a track ready, when Underwood suggested "Something in the Water" as a title. They then chose to make the song themed after a person whose life changes after <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptism</a>. James said that he found Underwood's demo of the song "emotional", and praised Underwood's singing and songwriting style. He later called it "one of the most perfect writing days I have had in my life".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Something_in_the_Water_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Something in the Water" was met with widespread critical acclaim. <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> praised the song stating that "Underwood sings in the soaring song showcasing her heavenly high vocal range" and called the song a "spiritual heartstring-tugger".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> praised the song with 4 out of 5 stars, stating: "Carrie Underwood offers a message of faith in times of trouble".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <i>Music Times</i> praised the song by saying that Underwood "pulls out all of her strongest, boldest notes for the religious anthem" and that the song "builds to some of the greatest heights Underwood has reached to date".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i>All Access Music</i> also praised the song, stating that "It's a welcome – and bold – move for Underwood that flies in the face of Country's recent direction. Her vocals soar and to no one's surprise she powerfully delivers the message".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/AXS_(ticket_merchant)" class="mw-redirect" title="AXS (ticket merchant)">AXS</a> called "Something in the Water" a "mighty offering" while praising Underwood for a "righteously powerful performance." <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Website <i><a href="/wiki/Taste_of_Country" class="mw-redirect" title="Taste of Country">Taste of Country</a></i> praised Underwood's vocals and the songs's lyrics and production, saying "Mark Bright creates a complex and rich tapestry that swirls between the rare breathes Underwood takes. Her vocal performance is typically stunning and personal" and also predicted the song "to clean up come 2015 awards season".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Website <i>Popdust</i> gave the song a rating of 4.5/5, saying, "assembled with an orchestral arrangement and a backing of a harmoniously sanctified choir, this track...is a game changer." The website also praised Underwood bringing faith back into her song.<sup id="cite_ref-Popdust_2014-09-28_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Popdust_2014-09-28-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 8, 2015, at the <a href="/wiki/57th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="57th Grammy Awards">57th Grammy Awards</a>, Underwood won <a href="/wiki/Best_Country_Solo_Performance" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Country Solo Performance">Best Country Solo Performance</a> for "Something in the Water", marking her seventh career Grammy Award.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Carrie_Underwood_official_Grammy_source_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carrie_Underwood_official_Grammy_source-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Something_in_the_Water_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Something in the Water" sold over 125,000 copies in its first week, becoming the biggest opening-week sales of any female country artist in 2014 and the singer's highest sales frame since 2009, when "<a href="/wiki/I_Told_You_So_(Randy_Travis_song)" title="I Told You So (Randy Travis song)">I Told You So</a>", featuring Randy Travis, sold 126,000 copies. It is also Underwood's best debut sales week for a digital song. It debuted at No. 4 on Billboard's <a href="/wiki/Digital_Songs" title="Digital Songs">Digital Songs</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The song's massive <a href="/wiki/Airplay" title="Airplay">airplay</a> in its first week prompted it to also debuted at No. 17 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart, achieving the highest debut for a solo artist in 2014 and surpassing Underwood's previous record of No. 20 with "<a href="/wiki/So_Small" title="So Small">So Small</a>" in 2007. Additionally, it set an all-time record by amassing 144 total stations on impact date on <a href="/wiki/Mediabase" title="Mediabase">Mediabase</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The song debuted at No. 1 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Christian_Songs" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Christian Songs">Hot Christian Songs</a>, becoming the singer's first song to top such a chart, and rose from No. 48 to number two on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> in its second week on the chart. It also debuted at No. 24 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In its seventh week on the charts, "Something in the Water" reached No.1 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a>, becoming Underwood's 14th career leader on the chart and her second in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Wade_Jessen_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wade_Jessen-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> It became her fastest climbing single on the Hot Country Songs chart. With 14 chart-toppers on such a chart, she expands her record as the female country artist with the most number one hits on the Hot Country Songs chart in the <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a> era (1991–present), breaking her own <i><a href="/wiki/Guinness_Book_of_World_Records" class="mw-redirect" title="Guinness Book of World Records">Guinness Book</a></i> record of ten, set in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-guinnessworldrecords1_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guinnessworldrecords1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> It has topped the Hot Country Songs for seven weeks, becoming her longest No.1 reign on the chart and the third longest No.1 reign among women in the chart's history.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The song has also topped the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Christian_Songs" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Christian Songs">Hot Christian Songs</a> for 26 consecutive weeks,setting a record for the longest reign on that chart by a woman and also the third longest running number one.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-billboardscalecharts_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboardscalecharts-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The song's record was broken by <a href="/wiki/Lauren_Daigle" title="Lauren Daigle">Lauren Daigle</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/You_Say" title="You Say">You Say</a>.".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The song was certified Platinum by the RIAA on February 25, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodSomething_in_the_water&#123;&#123;&#123;type&#125;&#125;&#125;CertRef_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodSomething_in_the_water{{{type}}}CertRef-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>  As of April, 2016, the song has sold over 1,100,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-Sales_2016_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sales_2016-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The song won Top Christian Song at the <a href="/wiki/2015_Billboard_Music_Awards" title="2015 Billboard Music Awards">2015 Billboard Music Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Something_in_the_Water_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:35%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">""We took it in a direction that was probably not what most people would think." "We made it joyful and happy and uplifting."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">—Underwood describing the video to <i>Entertainment Tonight</i> <sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The music video was directed by Raj Kapoor and premiered on November 4, 2014, on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> during the 48th Annual CMA Awards. Emmy nominated choreographer <a href="/wiki/Travis_Wall" title="Travis Wall">Travis Wall</a> choreographed the video.
</p><p>Underwood performed with dancers from the Shaping Sound dance academy dancing in the background and "required 16 choir members, 12 dancers, three costume changes and a custom-built tank of recycled water to create its dance spectacle."<sup id="cite_ref-Premiere_video_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Premiere_video-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Something_in_the_Water_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood performed the song at 2014 CMA Awards and on April 7, 2016 at the <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_15)" title="American Idol (season 15)">fifteenth season</a> finale of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The song is also performed as the encore of her "<a href="/wiki/Storyteller_Tour:_Stories_in_the_Round" title="Storyteller Tour: Stories in the Round">Storyteller Tour: Stories in the Round</a>".
</p>
