	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Lingua franca</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Lingua_franca_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Lingua franca (disambiguation)">Lingua franca (disambiguation)</a>.</div><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">languages used to facilitate trade between groups without a common native language</div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Trilingual_Chinese-Malay-English_text_from_1839.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Trilingual_Chinese-Malay-English_text_from_1839.jpg/220px-Trilingual_Chinese-Malay-English_text_from_1839.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="368" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Trilingual_Chinese-Malay-English_text_from_1839.jpg/330px-Trilingual_Chinese-Malay-English_text_from_1839.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/52/Trilingual_Chinese-Malay-English_text_from_1839.jpg 2x" data-file-width="384" data-file-height="642" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Trilingual_Chinese-Malay-English_text_from_1839.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1839 – Trilingual Chinese–Malay–English text – <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay</a> was the lingua franca across the <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Malacca" title="Strait of Malacca">Strait of Malacca</a>, including the coasts of the <a href="/wiki/Malay_Peninsula" title="Malay Peninsula">Malay Peninsula</a> (now in Malaysia) and the eastern coast of <a href="/wiki/Sumatra" title="Sumatra">Sumatra</a> (now in Indonesia), and has been established as a native language of part of western coastal <a href="/wiki/Sarawak" title="Sarawak">Sarawak</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Kalimantan" title="West Kalimantan">West Kalimantan</a> in <a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>A <b>lingua franca</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/ɪ/: &#39;i&#39; in &#39;kit&#39;">ɪ</span><span title="/ŋ/: &#39;ng&#39; in &#39;sing&#39;">ŋ</span><span title="/ɡ/: &#39;g&#39; in &#39;guy&#39;">ɡ</span><span title="&#39;w&#39; in &#39;wind&#39;">w</span><span title="/ə/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">ə</span></span> <wbr /><span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;f&#39; in &#39;find&#39;">f</span><span title="&#39;r&#39; in &#39;rye&#39;">r</span><span title="/æ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;bad&#39;">æ</span><span title="/ŋ/: &#39;ng&#39; in &#39;sing&#39;">ŋ</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span><span title="/ə/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;about&#39;">ə</span></span>/</a></span>&#32;<span class="nowrap" style="font-size:85%">(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:En-Lingua_franca-pronunciation.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" decoding="async" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a></span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c5/En-Lingua_franca-pronunciation.ogg" class="internal" title="En-Lingua franca-pronunciation.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</span></span>; <abbr title="literal translation">lit.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;">&#8201;Frankish tongue</span>),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> also known as a <b>bridge language</b>, <b>common language</b>, <b>trade language</b>, <b>auxiliary language, vehicular language</b>, or <b>link language</b> is a language or <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">dialect</a> systematically used to make communication possible between groups of people who do not share a <a href="/wiki/First_language" title="First language">native language</a> or dialect, particularly when it is a third language that is distinct from both of the speakers' native languages.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Lingua francas have developed around the world throughout human history, sometimes for commercial reasons (so-called "trade languages" facilitated trade), but also for cultural, religious, diplomatic and administrative convenience, and as a means of exchanging information between scientists and other scholars of different nationalities.<sup id="cite_ref-Nye_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nye-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gordin_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gordin-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The term is taken from the medieval <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Lingua_Franca" title="Mediterranean Lingua Franca">Mediterranean Lingua Franca</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Romance_languages" title="Romance languages">Romance</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Pidgin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Pidgin language">pidgin language</a> used (especially by traders and seamen) as a <a class="mw-selflink selflink">lingua franca</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Basin" title="Mediterranean Basin">Mediterranean Basin</a> from the 11th to the 19th century. A <a href="/wiki/World_language" title="World language">world language</a> – a language spoken internationally and by many people – is a language that may function as a global lingua franca.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lingua_franca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lingua franca refers to any language used for communication between people who do not share a native language.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It can refer to <a href="/wiki/Mixed_language" title="Mixed language">mixed languages</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Pidgin" title="Pidgin">pidgins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Creole_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Creole languages">creoles</a> used for communication between language groups. It can also refer to languages which are native to one nation (often a colonial power) but used as a <a href="/wiki/Second_language" title="Second language">second language</a> for communication between groups.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Lingua franca is a functional term, independent of any linguistic history or language structure.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lingua francas are often pre-existing languages with native speakers, but they can also be pidgin or creole languages developed for that specific region or context. Pidgin languages are rapidly developed and simplified combinations of two or more established languages, while creole languages are simply pidgins that evolve and are passed onto later generations.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Pre-existing lingua francas such as French are used to facilitate intercommunication in large-scale trade or political matters, while pidgins and creoles often arise out of colonial situations and a specific need for communication between colonists and indigenous peoples.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>&#160;Pre-existing lingua francas are generally widespread, highly developed languages with many native speakers. Conversely, pidgin languages are very simplified means of communication, containing loose structuring, few grammatical rules, and possessing no native speakers. Creole languages are more developed than their ancestral pidgins, utilizing more complex structure, grammar, and vocabulary, as well as having native speakers.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Whereas a <a href="/wiki/Vernacular" title="Vernacular">vernacular</a> language is the native language of a specific geographical community, a lingua franca is used beyond the boundaries of its original community, for trade, religious, political, or academic reasons. For example, English is a vernacular in the United Kingdom but is used as a lingua franca in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mandarin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Mandarin language">Mandarin Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hindustani_language" title="Hindustani language">Hindustani</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a> serve a similar purpose as industrial/educational lingua francas, across regional and national boundaries.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/International_auxiliary_language" title="International auxiliary language">International auxiliary languages</a> created with the purpose of being lingua francas such as <a href="/wiki/Esperanto" title="Esperanto">Esperanto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lingua_Franca_Nova" title="Lingua Franca Nova">Lingua Franca Nova</a> have not had a great degree of adoption globally so they cannot be described as global lingua francas.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lingua_franca&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term <i>lingua franca</i> derives from <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Lingua_Franca" title="Mediterranean Lingua Franca">Mediterranean Lingua Franca</a>, the pidgin language that people around the <a href="/wiki/Levant" title="Levant">Levant</a> and the eastern Mediterranean Sea used as the main language of commerce and diplomacy from late <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">medieval times</a>, especially during the <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_era" class="mw-redirect" title="Renaissance era">Renaissance era</a>, to the 18th century.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> At that time, Italian-speakers dominated seaborne commerce in the port cities of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> and a simplified version of Italian, including many <a href="/wiki/Loan_words" class="mw-redirect" title="Loan words">loan words</a> from <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>, <a href="/wiki/Old_French_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Old French language">Old French</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Occitan" class="mw-redirect" title="Occitan">Occitan</a>, and Spanish as well as <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a> came to be widely used as the "lingua franca" (in the generic sense) of the region.
</p><p>In Lingua Franca (the specific language), <i>lingua</i> means a language, as in Portuguese and Italian, and <i>franca</i> is related to <i>phrankoi</i> in Greek and <i>faranji</i> in Arabic as well as the equivalent Italian. In all three cases, the literal sense is "<a href="/wiki/Frankish_language" title="Frankish language">Frankish</a>", leading to the direct translation: "language of the Franks". During the late <a href="/wiki/Byzantine_Empire" title="Byzantine Empire">Byzantine Empire</a>, "Franks" was a term that applied to all <a href="/wiki/Franks" title="Franks">Western Europeans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HEL_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HEL-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Through changes of the term in literature, Lingua Franca has come to be interpreted as a general term for pidgins, creoles, and some or all forms of vehicular languages. This transition in meaning has been attributed to the idea that pidgin languages only became widely known from the 16th century on due to European colonization of continents such as The Americas, Africa, and Asia. During this time, the need for a term to address these pidgin languages arose, hence the shift in the meaning of Lingua Franca from a single proper noun to a common noun encompassing a large class of pidgin languages.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As recently as the late 20th century, some restricted the use of the generic term to mean only <a href="/wiki/Mixed_language" title="Mixed language">mixed languages</a> that are used as vehicular languages, its original meaning.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Douglas Harper Etymology Dictionary states that the term <i>Lingua Franca</i> (as the name of the particular language) was first recorded in English during the 1670s,<sup id="cite_ref-Harper_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harper-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> although an even earlier example of the use of <i>Lingua Franca</i> in English is attested from 1632, where it is also referred to as "Bastard Spanish".<sup id="cite_ref-Morgan_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Morgan-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The term is well established in its naturalization to English, which is why major dictionaries do not italicize it as a "foreign" term.<sup id="cite_ref-OxfordDictionaries_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OxfordDictionaries-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AHD_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHD-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MW_Collegiate_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MW_Collegiate-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Its plurals in English are <b>lingua francas</b> and <b>linguae francae</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-AHD_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHD-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MW_Collegiate_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MW_Collegiate-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> with the former being first-listed<sup id="cite_ref-AHD_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHD-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MW_Collegiate_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MW_Collegiate-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> or only-listed<sup id="cite_ref-OxfordDictionaries_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OxfordDictionaries-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> in major dictionaries.
</p>

<p>The use of lingua francas has existed since antiquity. <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Koine_Greek" title="Koine Greek">Koine Greek</a> were the lingua francas of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hellenistic" class="mw-redirect" title="Hellenistic">Hellenistic</a> culture. <a href="/wiki/Akkadian_language" title="Akkadian language">Akkadian</a> (died out during <a href="/wiki/Classical_antiquity" title="Classical antiquity">Classical antiquity</a>) and then <a href="/wiki/Aramaic" title="Aramaic">Aramaic</a> remained the common languages of a large part of Western Asia from several earlier empires.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Hindustani_language" title="Hindustani language">Hindustani language</a> (<a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a>-<a href="/wiki/Urdu" title="Urdu">Urdu</a>) is the lingua franca of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Northern_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern India">Northern India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-siddiqi1994_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-siddiqi1994-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Self-published_sources" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on a self-published source. (August 2018)">self-published source?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup><sup id="cite_ref-pulsipher2005_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pulsipher2005-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (August 2018)">page&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Many Indian states have adopted the <a href="/wiki/Three-language_formula" title="Three-language formula">Three-language formula</a> in which students in Hindi-speaking states are taught: "(a) Hindi (with Sanskrit as part of the composite course); (b) Urdu or any other modern Indian language and (c) English or any other modern European language." The order in non-Hindi speaking states is: "(a) the regional language; (b) Hindi; (c) Urdu or any other modern Indian language excluding (a) and (b); and (d) English or any other modern European language."<sup id="cite_ref-nic_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nic-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Hindi has also emerged as a lingua franca for the locals of <a href="/wiki/Arunachal_Pradesh" title="Arunachal Pradesh">Arunachal Pradesh</a>, a linguistically diverse state in Northeast India.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>It is estimated that 90 percent of the state's population knows Hindi.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Swahili_language" title="Swahili language">Swahili</a> developed as a lingua franca between several Bantu-speaking tribal groups on the east coast of Africa with heavy influence from Arabic.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The earliest examples of writing in Swahili are from 1711.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> In the early 1800's the use of Swahili as a lingua franca moved inland with the Arabic ivory and slave traders. It was eventually adopted by Europeans as well during periods of colonization in the area. German colonizers used it as the language of administration in <a href="/wiki/Tanganyika" title="Tanganyika">Tanganyika</a>, which influenced the choice to use it as a national language in what is now independent <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, the use of <a href="/wiki/English_as_a_lingua_franca" title="English as a lingua franca">English as a lingua franca</a> has led to the emergence of a new dialect called <a href="/wiki/Euro_English" title="Euro English">Euro English</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the United Kingdom became a colonial power, English served as the lingua franca of the colonies of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a>. In the post-colonial period, some of the newly created nations which had multiple <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_language" title="Indigenous language">indigenous languages</a> opted to continue using English as an official language .
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Francophone_Africa.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Francophone_Africa.png/170px-Francophone_Africa.png" decoding="async" width="170" height="183" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Francophone_Africa.png/255px-Francophone_Africa.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Francophone_Africa.png/340px-Francophone_Africa.png 2x" data-file-width="360" data-file-height="388" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Francophone_Africa.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/African_French" title="African French">Francophone Africa</a></div></div></div> 
<p><a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> is still a lingua franca in most Western and Central African countries and an official language of many, a remnant of <a href="/wiki/French_colonial_empire" title="French colonial empire">French</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belgian_colonial_empire" title="Belgian colonial empire">Belgian</a> <a href="/wiki/Colonialism" title="Colonialism">colonialism</a>. These African countries and others are members of the <a href="/wiki/Francophonie" title="Francophonie">Francophonie</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a> is in use and widely understood in Central Asia and the Caucasus, areas formerly part of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> and Soviet Union, and in much of Central and Eastern Europe. It remains the official language of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">Commonwealth of Independent States</a>. Russian is also one of the <a href="/wiki/Official_languages_of_the_United_Nations" title="Official languages of the United Nations">six official languages of the United Nations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-un.org_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-un.org-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Qatar, the medical community is primarily made up of workers from countries without English as a native language. In medical practices and hospitals, nurses typically communicate with other professionals in English as a lingua franca.<sup id="cite_ref-melf_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-melf-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> This occurrence has led to interest in researching the consequences and affordances of the medical community communicating in a lingua franca.<sup id="cite_ref-melf_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-melf-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Indonesia_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Indonesia language">Indonesian</a> – which originated from a <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">Malay language</a> variant spoken in <a href="/wiki/Riau-Lingga_Sultanate" title="Riau-Lingga Sultanate">Riau</a> – is the official language and a lingua franca in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, although <a href="/wiki/Javanese_language" title="Javanese language">Javanese</a> has more native speakers. Still, Indonesian is the sole official language and is spoken throughout the country. <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> is also the lingua franca of <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> and its national language.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hausa_language" title="Hausa language">Hausa</a> can also be seen as a lingua franca because it is the language of communication between speakers of different languages in Northern <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> and other <a href="/wiki/West_Africa" title="West Africa">West African</a> countries.
</p><p>The only documented <a href="/wiki/Sign_language" title="Sign language">sign language</a> used as a lingua franca is <a href="/wiki/Plains_Indian_Sign_Language" title="Plains Indian Sign Language">Plains Indian Sign Language</a>, used across much of North America. It was used as a second language across many indigenous peoples. Alongside or a derivation of Plains Indian Sign Language was <a href="/wiki/Plateau_Sign_Language" title="Plateau Sign Language">Plateau Sign Language</a>, now extinct. <a href="/wiki/Inuit_Sign_Language" title="Inuit Sign Language">Inuit Sign Language</a> could be a similar case in the Arctic among the <a href="/wiki/Inuit" title="Inuit">Inuit</a> for communication across oral language boundaries, but little research exists.
</p>
