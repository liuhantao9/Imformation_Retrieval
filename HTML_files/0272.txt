<p><b>Henck Alphonsus Eugène Arron</b> (25 April 1936 – 4 December 2000) was the first <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Prime Ministers of Suriname">Prime Minister of Suriname</a> after it gained independence in 1975. A member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Party_of_Suriname" title="National Party of Suriname">National Party of Suriname</a>, he served from 24 December 1973 with the transition government, to 25 February 1980. He was overthrown in a <a href="/wiki/1980_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="1980 Surinamese coup d&#39;état">coup d'état</a> by the military, led by <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a>. Released in 1981 after charges of corruption were dropped, he returned to banking, his previous career.
</p><p>In 1987, Arron was elected as Vice-president of Suriname and served until another coup in 1990 overthrew the government.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Henck_Arron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arron was born in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a> in 1936. He completed high school in 1956, and moved to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> for training in banking. Arron worked several years at an <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> bank. When he returned to <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, he worked in the financial system. In late 1963, he became deputy director of the  People's Credit Bank.
</p><p>In 1961, Arron became a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Party_of_Suriname" title="National Party of Suriname">National Party of Suriname</a> (NPS), the main <a href="/wiki/Creole_peoples" title="Creole peoples">Creole</a> party. In 1970, Arron became the leader of the NPS. In 1973, he created a coalition, including the pro-independence <a href="/wiki/Nationalist_Republican_Party_(Suriname)" title="Nationalist Republican Party (Suriname)">Nationalist Republican Party</a> (PNR), that won that year's <a href="/wiki/1973_Surinamese_general_election" title="1973 Surinamese general election">general election</a>.
</p><p>In February 1974, Arron announced that Suriname would be seeking independence before the end of 1975. Many observers were surprised, as Arron's NPS did not have a majority in favour of independence.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Netherlands granted Suriname independence on 25 November 1975. Surinamese politics was driven by ethnic polarisation and corruption; the NPS used Dutch aid money for partisan purposes. Its leaders were accused of fraud in the <a href="/wiki/1977_Surinamese_general_election" title="1977 Surinamese general election">1977 elections</a>, in which Arron won a further term, and the discontent was such that a large chunk of the population fled to the Netherlands, joining an already significant Surinamese community there.
</p><p>In 1980, Arron was accused of corruption by high-ranking military officers. He was overthrown and jailed in a coup by the <a href="/wiki/Military_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="Military of Suriname">military</a> led by <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a>. The Dutch government had previously advised Arron against the establishment of such a standing army. Arron bore the ill-treatment and humiliation with courage; the charges did not stick, and he was released in 1981. A year later, he was selected as managing director of the Surinamese People's Credit Bank.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1987, Arron was elected as <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_Suriname" title="Vice President of Suriname">Vice President of Suriname</a> (and therefore Chairman of the Council of Ministers), serving from 26 January 1988 to 24 December 1990. <a href="/wiki/Ramsewak_Shankar" title="Ramsewak Shankar">Ramsewak Shankar</a> was elected as president. Their government was overthrown in 1990 in another coup by Bouterse and the military.
</p><p>In December 2000, Arron was invited to the Netherlands to talk about 25 years of independence of Suriname. On the evening of December 4, he died in his hotel room as a result of cardiac arrest.
</p>
