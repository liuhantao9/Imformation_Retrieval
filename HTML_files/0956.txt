	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Bachelor of Arts</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For the R. K. Narayan novel, see <a href="/wiki/The_Bachelor_of_Arts" title="The Bachelor of Arts">The Bachelor of Arts</a>.</div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"B. A." redirects here. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/BA_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="BA (disambiguation)">BA (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"A. B." redirects here. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/AB_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="AB (disambiguation)">AB (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Bachelor's degree awarded for undergraduate study in liberal arts, the sciences or both</div>
<p>A <b>Bachelor of Arts</b> (<b>BA</b> or <b>AB</b>; from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">baccalaureus artium</i></i> or <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">artium baccalaureus</i></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">bachelor's degree</a> awarded for an <a href="/wiki/Undergraduate_education" title="Undergraduate education">undergraduate</a> course or program in either the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_arts_education" title="Liberal arts education">liberal arts</a>, sciences, or both. Bachelor of Arts programs generally take three to four years depending on the country, institution, and specific specializations, majors, or minors. The word <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">baccalaureus</i></i> (from the Latin <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">bacca</i></i>, a berry, and <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">laureus</i></i>, "of the <a href="/wiki/Bay_laurel" class="mw-redirect" title="Bay laurel">bay laurel</a>") should not be confused with <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">baccalaureatus</i></i> (translatable as "gold-plated <a href="/wiki/Sceptre" title="Sceptre">scepter</a>" by using the Latina <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">bacum</i></i> and <i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">aureatus</i></i>), which refers to the one- to two-year postgraduate <i>Bachelor of Arts <a href="/wiki/Honors_degree" class="mw-redirect" title="Honors degree">with Honors</a></i> degree (<i><i lang="la" title="Latin language text">Baccalaureatus in Artibus Cum Honore</i></i>) in some countries.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Diploma" title="Diploma">Diplomas</a> generally give the name of the institution, signatures of officials of the institution (generally the president or rector of the <a href="/wiki/University" title="University">university</a> as well as the secretary or dean of the component <a href="/wiki/College" title="College">college</a>), type of degree conferred, conferring authority, and location at which the degree is conferred. Diplomas generally are printed on high-quality <a href="/wiki/Paper" title="Paper">paper</a> or <a href="/wiki/Parchment" title="Parchment">parchment</a>; individual institutions set the preferred abbreviation for their degrees.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>The Bachelor of Arts is usually attained in four years in <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brunei" title="Brunei">Brunei</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latvia" title="Latvia">Latvia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, and most of the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Degree attainment generally takes three years in nearly all of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia and Herzegovina</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Caribbean" class="mw-redirect" title="The Caribbean">the Caribbean</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, and the Canadian province of <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, the Bachelor of Arts can also be attained within two years as an <i><a href="/wiki/External_degree" title="External degree">external degree</a>.</i></li></ul>

<h2><span id="Australia.2C_Canada.2C_India.2C_Nepal.2C_New_Zealand_and_South_Africa"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia,_Canada,_India,_Nepal,_New_Zealand_and_South_Africa">Australia, Canada, India, Nepal, New Zealand and South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_of_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Australia, Canada, India, Nepal, New Zealand and South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><span id="India"></span><span id="Nepal"></span><span id="Australia"></span><span id="New_Zealand"></span><span id="South_Africa"></span><span id="Canada"></span>
In colleges and universities in Australia, New Zealand, <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, and South Africa, the BA degree can be taken over three years of full-time study.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Students must pursue at least one <a href="/wiki/Academic_major" class="mw-redirect" title="Academic major">major</a> area of study, and units from that subject are usually studied in each year, though sometimes students may choose to complete upper-level classes in the same year and as a result, can leave space for elective subjects from a different field. At some universities students may choose to pursue a second major; alternatively, the remainder of the degree is taken up with a minor area of study (in the first two years) and other individual or stream-based subjects make up the degree.
</p><p>Unlike in other countries, students do not receive an overall grade for their Bachelor of Arts degree with varying levels of honours ("honours" is a distinction but not part of the degree itself). Qualified students may be admitted, after they have achieved their (general) Bachelor's program with a high overall grade point average, to a further one year Bachelor (with) Honours degree program (<i>(with) Honours</i> is part of the degree itself). Thus, to achieve a Bachelor (with) Honours degree (abbr. e.g. BA (Hons.) or BA hons.), an extra "<a href="/wiki/Postgraduate" class="mw-redirect" title="Postgraduate">postgraduate</a>" year and a high research honour's thesis must be completed; see <a href="/wiki/Honours_Degree" class="mw-redirect" title="Honours Degree">Honours Degrees</a>. A student who holds a (with) Honours degree is eligible for direct entry to either a Doctorate (Ph.D.) or a very high research master's degree program.
</p><p>Education in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> is controlled by the Provinces and can be very different depending on the province.  Canadian universities typically offer a four-year Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) degrees. In many universities and colleges, Bachelor of Arts degrees are differentiated either as Bachelors of Arts or as honours Bachelor of Arts degree. The term "Honours" is an academic distinction, which indicates that students must achieve their Bachelor of Arts degree with a sufficiently high overall grade point average; in addition, some programs may require more education than non-honours programs.
</p><p>The honours degrees are sometimes designated with the abbreviation in brackets of '(Hon(s))'. It should not be confused with the consecutive <a href="/wiki/Honours_degree" title="Honours degree">Bachelor of Arts degree "with Honours"</a>, Latin "Baccalaureatus in Artibus Cum Honore", abbr. 'BA hon.' <i>de jure</i> without brackets and with a dot. It is a "postgraduate" degree. Going back in history, a three-year Bachelor of Arts degree (also known e.g. in Québec as grade de bachelier ès arts) was also called a <i>pass degree</i> or <i>general degree</i>.
</p><p>A student who first achieves a general Bachelor of Arts degree with a sufficiently high overall average may be admitted to a "postgraduate" Baccalaureatus cum Honore degree in the same field; it requires a minimum of one year but may also take longer; it typically does not exceed two years. Students may be required to undertake a long high-quality research empirical thesis (Honours Seminar Thesis) combined with a selection of courses from the relevant field of studies. The consecutive B. cum Honore degree is essential if students ultimate goal is to study towards a two- or three-year very high research master's degree qualification. A student holding a Baccalaureatus Cum Honore degree also may choose to complete a Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) program without the requirement to first complete a master's degree. Over the years, in some universities certain Baccalaureatus cum Honore programs have been changed to corresponding master's degrees.
</p><p>In general, in all four countries, the B.A. degree is the standard required for entry into  a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts" title="Master of Arts">master's</a> programme. In science, a BA hons degree is generally a prerequisite for entrance to a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Ph.D</a> program or a very-high-research-activity master's programme. As such, for example the UK awarded <i>honours</i> are designed and should not be confused with Canadian, Australian, New Zealand and South African awarded BA hons/BA (Hons) degree.
</p><p>For further clarification, in other countries the so-called <i>honours</i> bachelor may not be an actual degree, but the name of a (vocational) programme which can be completed through specified course work, a high grade average, and the completion of possibly a short paper or project; students who completed such a <i>honours</i> BA programme sometimes style themselves by '(Hon)' after the degree abbreviation without a space, in parentheses and after the 'Hon(s)' without a dot, for example 'B.A.(Hon)'. The three and four year B.A. degree is also accepted for entry into graduate professional programmes such as <a href="/wiki/LLB#Structure_of_LL.B._programmes" class="mw-redirect" title="LLB">law</a> or a <a href="/wiki/M.B.A." class="mw-redirect" title="M.B.A.">Master of Business Administration</a> program.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Netherlands">Netherlands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_of_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Netherlands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, the Bachelor of Arts and Master of Arts degrees were introduced in 2002. Until then there was a single program which led to the <a href="/wiki/Doctorandus" title="Doctorandus">doctorandus</a> degree. This comprised the same course load as the Bachelor and Master programs combined. The title <i>doctorandus</i> was used in almost all fields of study; other titles were used for legal studies (<i>meester</i>, Dutch for master, abbreviated <i>Mr.</i>) and engineering (<i><a href="/wiki/Ingenieur" class="mw-redirect" title="Ingenieur">ingenieur</a></i>). Those who had already started the doctorandus program could, on completing it, opt for the doctorandus degree (entitling them to use "Drs." in front of their name), or could use the master's degree (post-nominal letters) in accordance with the new standard.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_of_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Germany, university-level education usually happens in either a <i>Universität</i> (plural: <i>Universitäten</i>) or a <i><a href="/wiki/Fachhochschule" title="Fachhochschule">Fachhochschule</a></i> (plural: <i>Fachhochschulen</i>); both can be referred to as a <i>Hochschule</i>, which is the generic term in Germany for all institutions awarding academic degrees. Fachhochschule is often translated as "University of Applied Sciences". Universitäten place greater emphasis on fundamental science and theoretical background, while Fachhochschulen are generally designed with a focus on teaching professional skills. Degrees earned at Universitäten and Fachhochschulen are legally equivalent.
</p><p>In Germany, the B.A. normally lasts between three and three and a half years – six or seven semesters – and is awarded after the student earns between one-hundred eighty and two-hundred ten <a href="/wiki/European_Credit_Transfer_and_Accumulation_System" title="European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System">ECTS</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="United_Kingdom_and_Ireland">United Kingdom and Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bachelor_of_Arts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United Kingdom and Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/British_undergraduate_degree_classification" title="British undergraduate degree classification">British undergraduate degree classification</a></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> (excluding <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>, the first degree course normally lasts three years, but nomenclature varies: 19th-century and later universities usually distinguish between arts and sciences subjects by awarding either a B.A. or B.Sc. degree. However, some older or <a href="/wiki/Ancient_universities" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancient universities">ancient universities</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">Oxford</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">Cambridge</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Dublin" title="University of Dublin">Dublin</a> traditionally award B.A.s to undergraduates having completed the final examinations, e.g. Part II <a href="/wiki/Tripos" title="Tripos">Tripos</a> (Cambridge), Final Honour Schools (Oxford), Moderatorship (Dublin), in most subjects including the sciences. Some new <a href="/wiki/Plate_glass_university" title="Plate glass university">plate glass universities</a> established in the 1960s, such as <a href="/wiki/University_of_York" title="University of York">York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lancaster_University" title="Lancaster University">Lancaster</a> originally followed the practice of Oxford and Cambridge by awarding B.A.s in all subjects, but have since changed to awarding B.Sc. degrees in science subjects. At Oxford, Cambridge and Dublin the degree of <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts_(Oxford,_Cambridge_and_Dublin)" class="mw-redirect" title="Master of Arts (Oxford, Cambridge and Dublin)">M.A.</a> can be claimed, usually twenty-one terms after <a href="/wiki/Matriculation" title="Matriculation">matriculation</a>. For many centuries, the bachelor's degree was an intermediate step and was awarded for much of the work carried out in later times at secondary schools. The names of the final secondary school exams in France and Spain (and increasingly in the UK—the <a href="/wiki/International_Baccalaureate" title="International Baccalaureate">International Baccalaureate</a>) come from this: le <a href="/wiki/Baccalaur%C3%A9at" title="Baccalauréat">Baccalauréat</a> and el <i>Bachillerato</i>, respectively.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Universities_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Ancient Universities of Scotland">Ancient Universities of Scotland</a> award a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts_(Scotland)" title="Master of Arts (Scotland)">Master of Arts</a> degree to humanities or arts graduates, but a B.Sc. to science graduates. This course takes four years for an honours degree and three for an ordinar.  In Scotland, it is possible to opt to take an ordinary degree rather than this simply ranking below a third class honours (for example, B.A. with distinction, merit or pass).
</p><p>A Bachelor of Arts is entitled to the designation B.A. for an ordinary/pass degree and B.A. (Hons) for an honours degree. Students who completed an honours B.A. sometimes style themselves by '(Hon)' or '(Hons)' after the degree abbreviation in parentheses. An honours degree is always awarded in one of four classes depending upon the marks gained in the final assessments and examinations. The top students are awarded a first-class degree, followed by an upper second-class degree (usually referred to as a 2:1), a lower second-class degree (usually referred to as a 2:2), and those with the lowest marks gain a third-class degree. An ordinary or unclassified degree (which does not give the graduate the right to add '(Hons)') may be awarded if a student has completed the full honours degree course but has not obtained the total required passes sufficient to merit a third-class honours degree. Typically these degrees lack the final year requirement of a dissertation.
</p>
