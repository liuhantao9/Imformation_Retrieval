padding-top:0.3em; border-top:2px solid #99CC33;"><ul><li><a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Suriname" title="Politics of Suriname">Politics of Suriname</a></li><li><a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Suriname" title="List of political parties in Suriname">Political parties</a></li><li><a href="/wiki/Elections_in_Suriname" title="Elections in Suriname">Elections</a></li></ul></div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The <b>National Party of Suriname</b> (<i>Nationale Partij Suriname</i>, NPS) is a <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a> in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, founded in 1946, and since June 2012 led by <a href="/wiki/Gregory_Rusland" title="Gregory Rusland">Gregory Rusland</a>. For a long time it was the largest ruling party in the country, and it has been in government for over 40 years. Of the 16 general elections held in Suriname, the party became first 11 times and lost 5 times.
</p><p>At the 2005 <a href="/wiki/2005_Surinamese_legislative_election" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Surinamese legislative election">legislative elections</a>, the party was part of the <a href="/wiki/New_Front_for_Democracy_and_Development" title="New Front for Democracy and Development">New Front for Democracy and Development</a> that won 41.2% of the popular vote and 23 out of 51 seats in the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="National Assembly of Suriname">National Assembly</a>.
</p><p>In June 2012 <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Venetiaan" title="Ronald Venetiaan">Ronald Venetiaan</a> stepped down from party leadership. Party elections were held for his successor with Gregory Rusland winning seven out of eleven districts, and <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Fernald" title="Ivan Fernald">Ivan Fernald</a> winning four.<sup id="cite_ref-star_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-star-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
