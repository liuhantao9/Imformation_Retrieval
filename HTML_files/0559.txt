<p>The <b>Jules Wijdenbosch Bridge</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i>Jules Wijdenboschbrug</i>), also called <b>Suriname bridge</b> and known locally as <b>Bosje Brug</b>, is a bridge over the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_River" title="Suriname River">Suriname River</a> between the capital city <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meerzorg" title="Meerzorg">Meerzorg</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Commewijne_District" title="Commewijne District">Commewijne District</a>. The bridge is part of the <a href="/wiki/East-West_Link_(Suriname)" title="East-West Link (Suriname)">East-West Link</a>, and is named after former president <a href="/wiki/Jules_Wijdenbosch" title="Jules Wijdenbosch">Jules Wijdenbosch</a>. Constructed by Dutch constructor Ballast-Nedam, the bridge has two lanes, is 1504 metres long, and was opened on 20 May 2000.
</p>
