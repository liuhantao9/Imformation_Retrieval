<p>"<b>Little Toy Guns</b>" is a song recorded by American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> for her first <a href="/wiki/Greatest_hits_album" title="Greatest hits album">greatest hits album</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits:_Decade_Number_1" title="Greatest Hits: Decade Number 1">Greatest Hits: Decade #1</a></i>. It was released on February 16, 2015 through <a href="/wiki/Arista_Nashville" title="Arista Nashville">Arista Nashville</a> as the album's second single.<sup id="cite_ref-CMT_new_single_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CMT_new_single-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was written by Underwood, <a href="/wiki/Chris_DeStefano" title="Chris DeStefano">Chris DeStefano</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, and produced by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Bright_(record_producer)" title="Mark Bright (record producer)">Mark Bright</a>.
</p><p>The song was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Best_Country_Solo_Performance" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Country Solo Performance">Best Country Solo Performance</a> at the <a href="/wiki/58th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="58th Grammy Awards">58th Grammy Awards</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_composition">Background and composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_Toy_Guns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background and composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:35%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I’ve seen it firsthand from different people in my life and seen how the tumultuous parental relationship [...] can affect a child. So that’s kind of what it’s about, but it’s a really tempo-driven song as well, so it’s kind of a bit of a juxtaposition as far as the story content and the music … It sounds rockin’, but it has a positive message."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">- Carrie Underwood on the inspiration behind the song.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The song was written by Underwood with frequent collaborators <a href="/wiki/Chris_DeStefano" title="Chris DeStefano">Chris DeStefano</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, and was produced by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Bright_(record_producer)" title="Mark Bright (record producer)">Mark Bright</a>. Drawing on <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> influences in its composition, "Little Toy Guns" features a "heavy and orchestral" arrangement with emotionally resonant lyrics and an "uplifting" melody.<sup id="cite_ref-TOC_review_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TOC_review-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RS_lyrics_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS_lyrics-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Critics have also called the song a <a href="/wiki/Prequel" title="Prequel">prequel</a> of sorts to her 2012 hit "<a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Blown Away (Carrie Underwood song)">Blown Away</a>" for the songs' similar thematic content and "stormy" sonic aesthetic.<sup id="cite_ref-TOC_review_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TOC_review-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RS_lyrics_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS_lyrics-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
Lyrically, the song tells a story of domestic violence revolving around a young child overhearing her parents fighting and wanting to escape from the pain.<sup id="cite_ref-CMT_new_single_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CMT_new_single-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TOC_review_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TOC_review-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Underwood narrates the chorus from the child's perspective, stating that she wishes the angry words were fake "like little toy guns" so they wouldn't hurt.<sup id="cite_ref-CMT_new_single_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CMT_new_single-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_Toy_Guns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song received mostly positive reviews with acclaim going to Underwood's vocals, production of the song, and the lyrics. Allmusic editor Stephen Thomas Erlewine called this song "bombastic."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> News OK called this song "meaningful" and added that it "sounds fresh enough to have fans craving another new studio collection sooner rather than later."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Pipy Ellwood concluded that the song is "a mid-tempo number that will appeal to fans of Underwood’s more uptempo moments."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Renowned_for_Sound" title="Renowned for Sound">Renowned for Sound</a></i> described that Underwood's "strong vocals create a vigorous atmosphere that fans old and new are going to love."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Digital Journals editor Markos Padatados agreed, stating that, "yet again, [Underwood] showcases her trademark pipes."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_Toy_Guns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Little Toy Guns" entered the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> at number 40 and peaked at number six. It debuted at number 99 on the Hot 100 chart and peaked at number 47.<sup id="cite_ref-country-update_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-country-update-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It peaked at number 2 on the Country Airplay Chart, number seven on the Canadian Country Chart, and number 70 on Canadian Hot 100. As of September 2015, the single has sold 437,000 copies in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-sales_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sales-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song was officially certified <a href="/wiki/Gold_certification" class="mw-redirect" title="Gold certification">Gold</a> by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> on August 10, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodLittle_Toy_GunssingleCertRef_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodLittle_Toy_GunssingleCertRef-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_Toy_Guns&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood posted a teaser video to her social media accounts on January 28, 2015 with the caption "Friday..." and the <a href="/wiki/Hashtag" title="Hashtag">hashtags</a> #LittleToyGuns and #CUX1 (Carrie Underwood Decade 1, an <a href="/wiki/Initialism" class="mw-redirect" title="Initialism">initialism</a> for her greatest hits album),<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> which was later confirmed as a preview for the "Little Toy Guns" video premiering via <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> on January 30, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-CMT_new_single_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CMT_new_single-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The video was directed by P.R. Brown, who has previously collaborated with Underwood's on her "<a href="/wiki/Two_Black_Cadillacs" title="Two Black Cadillacs">Two Black Cadillacs</a>" video. The video managed to reach 7.5 million <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> views on its debut week before being deleted off <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> and being officially released to <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a> and <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>.
The video received positive feedback and was nominated for "Female Video of the Year" at the 2015 CMT Music Awards; however, it lost to another of Carrie's videos, the one for her previous single "Something in the Water" (which also won "Video of the Year" at the ceremony).
</p><p>The video stars Grace Rundhaug, who starred alongside Underwood in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sound_of_Music_Live!" title="The Sound of Music Live!">The Sound of Music Live!</a></i> television special, as a girl who escapes her parents' fighting by mentally transporting herself to a "storybook land" where she plays a mythical hero and reunites her captive parents. Sara Antonio plays the mother and Kris Wente plays the father, who start the video with a "vicious fight (sans any music at all)."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Underwood can be seen in between, singing the song in various locations of the forest.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RS-video_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-video-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Beville Dunkerley at <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> noted that the end of the video provides a more peaceful resolution than the lyrics of the song.<sup id="cite_ref-RS-video_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-video-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
