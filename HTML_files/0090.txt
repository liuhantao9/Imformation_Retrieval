<p>The army rank of <b>captain</b> (from the French <i>capitaine</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Officer_(armed_forces)" title="Officer (armed forces)">commissioned officer</a> rank historically corresponding to the command of a <a href="/wiki/Company_(military_unit)" title="Company (military unit)">company</a> of soldiers. The rank is also used by some <a href="/wiki/Air_force" title="Air force">air forces</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marine_(armed_services)" class="mw-redirect" title="Marine (armed services)">marine forces</a>. Today, a captain is typically either the commander or <a href="/wiki/Second-in-command" title="Second-in-command">second-in-command</a> of a company or <a href="/wiki/Artillery_battery" title="Artillery battery">artillery battery</a> (or <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> <a href="/wiki/Armoured_cavalry" title="Armoured cavalry">cavalry</a> <a href="/wiki/Troop" title="Troop">troop</a> or <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> <a href="/wiki/Squadron_(army)" title="Squadron (army)">squadron</a>). In the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People&#39;s Republic of China">Chinese</a> <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People&#39;s Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a>, a captain may also command a <a href="/wiki/Company_(military_unit)" title="Company (military unit)">company</a>, or be the second-in-command of a <a href="/wiki/Battalion" title="Battalion">battalion</a>.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> countries, the rank of captain is described by the code OF-2 and is one rank above an OF-1 (<a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a> or <a href="/wiki/First_lieutenant" title="First lieutenant">first lieutenant</a>) and one below an OF-3 (<a href="/wiki/Major" title="Major">major</a> or <a href="/wiki/Commandant_(rank)" title="Commandant (rank)">commandant</a>). The rank of captain is generally considered to be the highest rank a soldier can achieve while remaining in the field.
</p><p>In some militaries, such as United States Army and Air Force and the British Army, captain is the entry-level rank for officer candidates possessing a professional degree, namely, most medical professionals (doctors, pharmacists, dentists) and lawyers. In the U.S.. Army, lawyers who are not already officers at captain rank or above enter as lieutenants during training, and are promoted to the rank of captain after completion of their training if they are in the active component, or after a certain amount of time, usually one year from their date of commission as a lieutenant, for the reserve components.
</p><p>The rank of captain should not be confused with the <a href="/wiki/Captain_(naval)" title="Captain (naval)">naval rank of captain</a> or with the UK-influenced air force rank of <a href="/wiki/Group_captain" title="Group captain">group captain</a>, both of which are equivalent to the army rank of <a href="/wiki/Colonel" title="Colonel">colonel</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_(armed_forces)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The term ultimately goes back to <a href="/wiki/Late_Latin" title="Late Latin">Late Latin</a> <i>capitaneus</i> meaning "chief, prominent"; in <a href="/wiki/Middle_English" title="Middle English">Middle English</a> adopted as <i>capitayn</i> in the 14th century, from <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i>capitaine</i>.
</p><p>The military rank of captain was in use from the 1560s, referring to an officer who commands a company. The naval sense, an officer who commands a man-of-war, is somewhat earlier, from the 1550s, later extended in meaning to "master or commander of any kind of vessel".
A captain in the period prior to the professionalization of the armed services of European nations subsequent to the <a href="/wiki/French_Revolution" title="French Revolution">French Revolution</a>, during the <a href="/wiki/Early_modern_warfare" title="Early modern warfare">early modern period</a>, was a nobleman who purchased the right to head a company from the previous holder of that right. He would in turn receive money from another nobleman to serve as his <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">lieutenant</a>. The funding to provide for the troops came from the monarch or his government; the captain had to be responsible for it. If he was not, or was otherwise court-martialed, he would be dismissed ("<a href="/wiki/Cashiering" title="Cashiering">cashiered</a>"), and the monarch would receive money from another nobleman to command the company. Otherwise, the only pension for the captain was selling the right to another nobleman when he was ready to retire.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Air_forces">Air forces</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Captain_(armed_forces)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Air forces">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many air forces, such as the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a>, use a rank structure and insignia similar to those of the army.
</p><p>However, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">Royal Air Force</a>, many other <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> air forces and a few non-Commonwealth air forces<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> use an air force-specific rank structure in which <a href="/wiki/Flight_lieutenant" title="Flight lieutenant">flight lieutenant</a> is OF-2. A <a href="/wiki/Group_captain" title="Group captain">group captain</a> is OF-5 and was derived from the naval rank of captain.
</p><p>In the unified system of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Forces" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian Forces">Canadian Forces</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Air_Force" title="Royal Canadian Air Force">air force</a> rank titles are pearl grey and increase from OF-1 to OF-5 in half strip increments.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
