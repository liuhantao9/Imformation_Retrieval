<p>"<b>Love Wins</b>" is a song by American singer <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>. The song was written by Underwood with <a href="/wiki/Brett_James" title="Brett James">Brett James</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Garcia_(musician)" title="David Garcia (musician)">David Garcia</a>, and produced by Garcia and Underwood. It was released on August 31, 2018, as the second single from her sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Cry_Pretty" title="Cry Pretty">Cry Pretty</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> "Love Wins" officially impacted country radio on September 17, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song was featured in the motion picture, <i><a href="/wiki/Breakthrough_(2019_film)" title="Breakthrough (2019 film)">Breakthrough</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_and_composition">Writing and composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Love_Wins_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Writing and composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song was written by Underwood, Brett James, and David Garcia.<sup id="cite_ref-stone_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stone-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Underwood said of the song, "I do think that we as humans are inherently good and we need to remember that. Because we're different, that doesn't make somebody else bad, it just makes us different. We wanted that song to be hopeful and to maybe make somebody stop and think about that."<sup id="cite_ref-stone_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stone-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song is composed in the key of <a href="/wiki/G_major" title="G major">G major</a> with a moderately fast tempo of approximately 132 <a href="/wiki/Beats_per_minute" class="mw-redirect" title="Beats per minute">beats per minute</a>. It follows the chord pattern C-G-Em7-D, and Underwood's vocals range from G<sub>3</sub> to D<sub>5</sub>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Love_Wins_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song has received mostly positive reviews upon its release. Taste of Country described it as an "anthemic, mid-tempo power ballad," and highlighted the use of a backing choir.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Robert Crawford, for <a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a> magazine, described it as "an open-armed anthem that calls for peace in a time of civil unrest". He continued, "Underwood is at her brazen best, filled with glory notes, gospel overtones and power-ballad gloss. She shies away from making direct references to politicians or unfair legislation, leaning instead on an "all you need is love" sentiment that goes down easily and inoffensively on both sides of the aisle".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Kyle’s Korner</i>  gave a more mixed review of the song, saying that it was “heavily reliant on Underwood’s gravitas to sell people on the sentiment that things need to change, and while she succeeds in doing so, it doesn’t give listeners any ideas as to what they can do to make things better, which is something that a lot of people are yearning for right now.”<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Love_Wins_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Love Wins" debuted at number 90 on the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Singles_Chart" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish Singles Chart">Scottish Singles Chart</a> for the week ending September 8, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-Scotland_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scotland-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In the United States, it debuted at number 30 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart for the week ending September 15, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-Country_Songs_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Country_Songs-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The following week, it debuted at number 34 on the <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-Country_Airplay_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Country_Airplay-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The song peaked at number 11 on the <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart and number 14 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart: becoming the first single of her career officially released to country radio to miss the top ten on either chart.
</p><p>As of March 2019, the song has sold 70,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-roughstock_single_mar2019_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-roughstock_single_mar2019-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The song was certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> on August 16, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Love_Wins_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The lyric video of the song was uploaded to Underwood's <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel on August 31, 2018. The official video premiered on <a href="/wiki/Apple_Music" title="Apple Music">Apple Music</a> on September 10, 2018, and was released to <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> the next day.<sup id="cite_ref-twitter_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twitter-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It was directed by <a href="/wiki/Shane_Drake" title="Shane Drake">Shane Drake</a>, whom Underwood had worked with previously on her video for "<a href="/wiki/Dirty_Laundry_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Dirty Laundry (Carrie Underwood song)">Dirty Laundry</a>". <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> writes "the video begins with a hopeless-looking group of people walking on a road in a post-apocalyptical setting as Underwood, in a goddess-like orange dress, emotionally leans into the track’s sentimental lyrics. Underwood looks radiant in her silk dress as she approaches the country’s critical political moment -- touching on themes of immigration and violence, while offering optimistic advice. As the crowd continues their journey, they find comfort in each other, arriving in some sort of promise land as Underwood chants the chorus. The video then shifts perspective as the group of people, now a community, rejoice in their newfound land while Underwood, now wearing a white long-sleeve butterfly gown and a face full of colorful make-up, joyfully sings to the crowd. As the loud clapping drums follow her powerful voice, the party turns into a vibrant celebration with colored powder being thrown everywhere, all thanks to the power of love and unity."<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Love_Wins_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On September 13, 2018, Underwood performed the single live as a musical guest on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a></i> at <a href="/wiki/Central_Park" title="Central Park">Central Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> She again performed the song on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a></i> on September 19, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On November 14, 2018, Underwood performed the song at the <a href="/wiki/52nd_Annual_Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="52nd Annual Country Music Association Awards">52nd Annual Country Music Association Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> It also served as the encore to the set list of Underwood's <a href="/wiki/Cry_Pretty_Tour_360" title="Cry Pretty Tour 360">Cry Pretty Tour 360</a>.
</p>
