<p>The <b>Central Suriname Nature Reserve</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">Centraal Suriname Natuurreservaat (CSNR)</i>) is a conservation unit in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>.
It preserves an area of tropical rainforest. The forest received material support from Microsoft co-founder Jeff Harbers.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Suriname_Nature_Reserve&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Central Suriname Nature Reserve was created in 1998 by <a href="/wiki/Conservation_International" title="Conservation International">Conservation International</a> and the government of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> from the fusion of three existing nature reserves: Ralleighvallen, Tafelberg and Eilerts de Haan gebergte.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was designated a UNESCO <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> in 2000 for its pristine tropical rainforest ecosystem.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Environment">Environment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Central_Suriname_Nature_Reserve&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Environment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The reserve is in the <a href="/wiki/Guianan_moist_forests" title="Guianan moist forests">Guianan moist forests</a> ecoregion.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
It contains 16,000 square kilometres (6,200&#160;sq&#160;mi) of both montane and lowland primary tropical forest including sections of the <a href="/wiki/Guyana_Highlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Guyana Highlands">Guyana Highlands</a>.
</p><p>It is known for its rapids and bird species, including the Guiana Cock of the Rock (<i><a href="/wiki/Rupicola_rupicola" class="mw-redirect" title="Rupicola rupicola">Rupicola rupicola</a></i>).<sup id="cite_ref-vis_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vis-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A research station is located at the foot of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Voltzberg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Voltzberg (page does not exist)">Voltzberg</a> and the area is tourist attraction.<sup id="cite_ref-vis_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vis-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some of the most outstanding features in Central Suriname Nature Reserve are several <a href="/wiki/Granite_dome" title="Granite dome">granite domes</a> - uplifted monoliths of granite rising high above the surrounding rainforest. Barren surface of dark-colored <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a> is exposed to impact of Sun thus creating unique <a href="/wiki/Xerophyte" title="Xerophyte">xerophytic</a> biotope which includes also endemic plant species. The best known granite dome is the 245 m high Voltzberg.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other attractions include the <a href="/wiki/Julianatop" title="Julianatop">Julianatop</a> (1230 m), the highest mountain in Suriname, the <a href="/wiki/Tafelberg,_Suriname" title="Tafelberg, Suriname">Tafelberg</a> (Table Mountain, 1026 meters), the Van Stockum Berg (360 m), and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Duivelsei&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Duivelsei (page does not exist)">Duivelsei</a> (<b>Devil's Egg</b>), a rock seemingly balanced on the edge of a mountain.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Eilerts de Haan Nature Park is named for <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Gijsbert_Willem_Jacobus_Eilerts_de_Haan" class="mw-redirect" title="Johannes Gijsbert Willem Jacobus Eilerts de Haan">Johannes Gijsbert Willem Jacobus Eilerts de Haan</a>, an explorer who died in Suriname's interior.
</p>
