<p><b>Nando's</b> is a South African <a href="/wiki/Restaurant_chain" class="mw-redirect" title="Restaurant chain">restaurant chain</a>, specialising in Portuguese food such as <a href="/wiki/Peri-peri" title="Peri-peri">peri-peri</a> style chicken dishes. Founded in <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a> in 1987, Nando's operates over 1,000 outlets in 35 countries.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nando%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The restaurant was founded in 1987 in the <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a> suburb <a href="/wiki/Rosettenville,_Gauteng" class="mw-redirect" title="Rosettenville, Gauteng">Rosettenville</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a>-born Fernando Duarte and <a href="/wiki/South_African" class="mw-redirect" title="South African">South African</a>-born Robert Brozin. Upon visiting a Portuguese takeaway named Chickenland, and trying the chicken with <a href="/wiki/Peri_peri" class="mw-redirect" title="Peri peri">peri peri</a>, they bought the restaurant for about R80,000 <a href="/wiki/South_African_rand" title="South African rand">rand</a> (equivalent of about <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">£</a>25,000 at the time).<sup id="cite_ref-DT_Brown_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DT_Brown-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> They renamed the restaurant Nando's after Fernando's first born son. By 1989, the restaurant had three outlets in Johannesburg and one in Portugal.<sup id="cite_ref-nandos_worldwide_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nandos_worldwide-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DT_Brown_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DT_Brown-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Capricorn Ventures International acquired the chain in 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As of 2017, there were over 1,000 Nando's branches in 35 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-usa-today-2017_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usa-today-2017-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010, <i><a href="/wiki/Advertising_Age" class="mw-redirect" title="Advertising Age">Advertising Age</a></i> magazine named Nando's one of the world's top 30 hottest marketing brands alongside <a href="/wiki/Tata_Nano" title="Tata Nano">Tata Nano</a>, <a href="/wiki/MTN_Group" title="MTN Group">MTN</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natura" class="mw-redirect" title="Natura">Natura</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As of July 2014 the Nando's restaurant group was ultimately owned by South African businessman <a href="/wiki/Dick_Enthoven" title="Dick Enthoven">Dick Enthoven</a> and his family. Enthoven's son, <a href="/wiki/Robby_Enthoven" title="Robby Enthoven">Robby Enthoven</a> who took over running the restaurants in 1993, was responsible for expanding the Nando's chain in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-Jersey_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jersey-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2018, American-based recruitment website <a href="/wiki/Indeed" title="Indeed">Indeed</a> named Nando's as the UK's sixth best private sector employer<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> based on millions of employee ratings and reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nando%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa">South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nando%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

<p>Fernando Duarte and his friend Robert Brozin are the founders of the first Nando's restaurant bought a joint previously called "ChickenLand" and renamed it Nando's. <p>Nando's opened its first UK restaurant in 1992 in the west London suburbs of <a href="/wiki/Ealing" title="Ealing">Ealing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Earls_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="Earls Court">Earls Court</a>, initially focusing on takeaway food.<sup id="cite_ref-DT_Brown_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DT_Brown-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The UK arm, owned by the Enthoven family via a private equity company, struggled until chairman Dick Enthoven put his son Robert in control. </p><p>Nando's claims to have the largest collection of South African art in the UK, with over 5,000 works displayed in restaurants; original artworks are commissioned by the company.<sup id="cite_ref-Observer_Sawyer_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Observer_Sawyer-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nando's also sells food to take away, delivering in some locations.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Advertising">Advertising</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nando%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Advertising">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Australian_refugee_advertisement">Australian refugee advertisement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nando%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Australian refugee advertisement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In Australia, Nando's ran an advertising campaign based around the 2002 political controversy regarding the <a href="/wiki/Immigration_detention_in_Australia" title="Immigration detention in Australia">mandatory detention of refugees</a>. The detainees had been waging a hunger strike campaign, even resorting to physically sewing their lips closed. Nando's adverts proclaimed that the strikers "decided to unsew their lips after hearing the news that with every Nando's quarter chicken combo, Nando's are giving away an extra quarter chicken free". Melbourne's Sphere Advertising said that the ad was designed to spark controversy, saying that they knew that "there's a section of our audience that's going to be uncomfortable... but we want to evoke a response".<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Malema_advertisement">Malema advertisement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nando%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Malema advertisement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During the South African national elections of 2009, Nando's made an advert lampooning <a href="/wiki/African_National_Congress_Youth_League" title="African National Congress Youth League">African National Congress Youth League</a> then president <a href="/wiki/Julius_Malema" title="Julius Malema">Julius Malema</a> by using a puppet that resembled him. Malema's lawyers sued Nando's and the original advert was removed. However, an altered version was released, with the puppet's face pixelated and the voice altered. The puppet used in the advert was later sold at auction for R100,000 which was donated to an educational charity.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Last_Dictator_advertisement">Last Dictator advertisement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nando%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Last Dictator advertisement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2011 Nando's launched a "Last dictators" advert in South Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-last_dictator_spoof_commercial_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-last_dictator_spoof_commercial-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The 60-second commercial shows a sad <a href="/wiki/Robert_Mugabe" title="Robert Mugabe">Robert Mugabe</a> dining alone at Christmas in a large mansion while he reminisces about "happier times" with former dictators, such as playing water tag with <a href="/wiki/Muammar_Gaddafi" title="Muammar Gaddafi">Muammar Gaddafi</a>, making snow angels in the sand with <a href="/wiki/Saddam_Hussein" title="Saddam Hussein">Saddam Hussein</a>, singing Karaoke with <a href="/wiki/Mao_Zedong" title="Mao Zedong">Mao Zedong</a>, pushing <a href="/wiki/P._W._Botha" title="P. W. Botha">P. W. Botha</a> on a swing set, and riding a British <a href="/wiki/Covenanter_tank" title="Covenanter tank">Covenanter cruiser tank</a> with <a href="/wiki/Idi_Amin" title="Idi Amin">Idi Amin</a> in a similar fashion to <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_DiCaprio" title="Leonardo DiCaprio">Leonardo DiCaprio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kate_Winslet" title="Kate Winslet">Kate Winslet</a> embracing each other from the movie <i><a href="/wiki/Titanic_(1997_film)" title="Titanic (1997 film)">Titanic</a>,</i> while the music from "<a href="/wiki/Those_Were_the_Days_(song)" title="Those Were the Days (song)">Those Were the Days</a>" is played.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Musekiwa Kumbula, holder of the Nando's franchise in Zimbabwe, said his group "strongly feels the advertisement is insensitive and in poor taste". The advert also offended Chipangano, a Zimbabwean youth militia loyal to Mugabe, who then demanded an apology from Nando's, threatened to boycott the South Africa-based chain, and demanded the advert be withdrawn or the restaurant face retribution.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Nando's South Africa subsequently withdrew the advert citing threats to its staff in Zimbabwe from a youth group.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporation_tax_bill">Corporation tax bill</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nando%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Corporation tax bill">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Both <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> newspaper (UK) and the American non-profit publication <i><a href="/wiki/ICIJ#International_Consortium_of_Investigative_Journalists" class="mw-redirect" title="ICIJ">ICIJ</a></i> received documents in July 2014 revealing the details of past and present offshore clients of wealth managers <a href="/wiki/Kleinwort_Benson" title="Kleinwort Benson">Kleinwort Benson</a>, including the Nando's restaurant group. <i>The Guardian</i> published its belief that, through the use of businesses in Malta, Guernsey and the Netherlands, Enthoven legally reduces the group's UK corporation tax bill by "up to a third". According to the British newspaper, Enthoven's profits eventually accumulate in the Kleinwort Benson-managed "Taro III Trust" that is based in <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a> and contains at least £750 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Jersey_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jersey-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While no member of the Enthoven family agreed to speak with <i>The Guardian</i>, a company representative explained that UK tax laws are not applicable to Enthoven, as "he is not resident in the US or the UK." The spokesperson also stated that, in the UK, Nando's paid corporation tax of £12.6 million on a profit of £58.2 million for the year ending February 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-Jersey_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jersey-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
