<p><b>Bronxville</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_New_York#Village" class="mw-redirect" title="Administrative divisions of New York">village</a> in <a href="/wiki/Westchester_County,_New_York" title="Westchester County, New York">Westchester County</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>, located about 15 miles (24&#160;km) north of midtown <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It is part of the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_divisions_of_New_York#town" class="mw-redirect" title="Administrative divisions of New York">town</a> of <a href="/wiki/Eastchester_(town),_New_York" title="Eastchester (town), New York">Eastchester</a>. The village comprises 1 square mile (2.5&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) of land in its entirety, approximately 20% of the town of Eastchester. As of the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 U.S. census</a>, Bronxville had a population of 6,323.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, Bronxville was rated by CNBC as the most expensive suburb of any of America's ten largest cities, with a median home value of $2.33 million.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It was ranked eighth in Bloomberg's "America's 100 Richest Places" in 2017 and 2018 and ninth in 2019.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bronxville,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The region that includes the contemporary village of Bronxville was deeded to British colonists in 1666, but first settled by Europeans in the early 18th century. The two founding inhabitants were the Underhill and Morgan families. The Underhills built a <a href="/wiki/Sawmill" title="Sawmill">sawmill</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Gristmill" title="Gristmill">gristmill</a>, which was the first factory in the area, on the <a href="/wiki/Bronx_River" title="Bronx River">Bronx River</a>. After they built a wooden bridge, the area became known as "Underhill's Crossing".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Millionaire real-estate and pharmaceutical mogul <a href="/wiki/William_Van_Duzer_Lawrence" title="William Van Duzer Lawrence">William Van Duzer Lawrence</a> sparked the development of Bronxville as an affluent suburb of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> by building grand homes in a rustic setting.<sup id="cite_ref-BSV_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BSV-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The area became "Bronxville" when the village was formally established. The population grew in the second half of the 19th century, when railroads allowed commuters from Westchester County to work in New York City.<sup id="cite_ref-BSV_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BSV-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Lawrence's influence can be seen throughout the community, including the historic <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Park_Historic_District" title="Lawrence Park Historic District">Lawrence Park</a> neighborhood, the Houlihan Lawrence Real Estate Corporation, and <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Hospital" title="Lawrence Hospital">Lawrence Hospital</a>. <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>, the future president of the United States, also lived here for a time.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The village was home to an <a href="/wiki/Art_colony" title="Art colony">arts colony</a> in the early 20th century, when many noteworthy houses were built by prominent and casual architects.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/Bronx_River_Parkway" title="Bronx River Parkway">Bronx River Parkway</a> was completed in 1925, the village expanded rapidly with the construction of several apartment buildings and townhouses, many of them built by the Lawrence family. As of 1959, the family continued to own or manage 97% of the rental market.<sup id="cite_ref-c59_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-c59-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In both rentals and ownership, the village discouraged and effectively prohibited Jewish residency, earning the name "Holy Square Mile".<sup id="cite_ref-c59_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-c59-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/James_W._Loewen" title="James W. Loewen">James W. Loewen</a> wrote about it in his book <i><a href="/wiki/Sundown_town" title="Sundown town">Sundown Towns</a></i>, quoting the <a href="/wiki/Anti-Defamation_League" title="Anti-Defamation League">Anti-Defamation League</a>'s 1959 comment:
</p>
<blockquote><p>The Incorporated Village of Bronxville in Westchester County has earned a reputation for admitting to its precincts as home-owners or -renters only those who profess to be Christian. According to informed observers, this mile-square village, with a population of 6500, does not have any known Jewish families residing within its boundaries.… Even in the apartment buildings located in Bronxville there are no known Jewish tenants.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>The Gramatan Hotel on Sunset Hill was a residence hotel in the late 19th century and early 20th century.<sup id="cite_ref-hotel_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotel-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Gramatan was the name of the chief of the local <a href="/wiki/Siwanoy" title="Siwanoy">Siwanoy</a> Indian tribe that was centered in the Gramatan Rock area above <a href="/wiki/Bronxville_(Metro-North_station)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bronxville (Metro-North station)">Bronxville Station</a>. Chief Gramatan sold the land to the settlers. The hotel was demolished in 1970, and a complex of townhouses was built on the site in 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-hotel_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hotel-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Elizabeth Clift Bacon, General <a href="/wiki/George_Armstrong_Custer" title="George Armstrong Custer">George Armstrong Custer</a>'s widow, lived in Bronxville, and her house still stands to this day.<sup id="cite_ref-Westchester_History_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Westchester_History-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Church_of_St._Joseph_(Bronxville)" class="mw-redirect" title="Church of St. Joseph (Bronxville)">St. Joseph's Catholic Church</a>, located in the downtown area, was attended by <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_family" title="Kennedy family">the Kennedys</a> when they were residents from 1929 to about 1938 before moving to London.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 1958, future senator <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Ted Kennedy</a> married Joan Bennett in St. Joseph's Church. In 1960, the Village voted 5:1 for <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">Nixon</a> over Kennedy.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Post_Office_(Bronxville,_New_York)" title="United States Post Office (Bronxville, New York)">US Post Office–Bronxville</a> was listed on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a> in 1988. Other sites on the National Register are the <a href="/wiki/Bronxville_Women%27s_Club" title="Bronxville Women&#39;s Club">Bronxville Women's Club</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Park_Historic_District" title="Lawrence Park Historic District">Lawrence Park Historic District</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Masterton-Dusenberry_House" title="Masterton-Dusenberry House">Masterton-Dusenberry House</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nris_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nris-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_census" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 United States census">2000 census</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GR2_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> there were 6,543 people, 2,312 households and 1,660 families residing in the village. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 6,869.3 per square mile (2,659.2/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 2,387 housing units, at an average density of 2,506.0 per square mile (970.1/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the village was 91.88% <a href="/wiki/White_Americans" title="White Americans">White</a>, 1.15% <a href="/wiki/African_American" class="mw-redirect" title="African American">African American</a>, 0.05% <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a>, 4.83% <a href="/wiki/Asian_Americans" title="Asian Americans">Asian</a>, 0.06% <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islands_Americans" title="Pacific Islands Americans">Pacific Islander</a>, 0.73% from <a href="/wiki/Multiracial_Americans" title="Multiracial Americans">other races</a>, and 1.30% from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_and_Latino_Americans" title="Hispanic and Latino Americans">Hispanic or Latinos</a> of any race were 2.93% of the population.
</p><p>There were 2,312 households, of which 40.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 64.4% were married couples living together, 6.2% had a female householder with no husband present, and 28.2% were non-families. In the village, 24.3% of all households were made up of individuals, and 11.4% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.71, and the average family size was 3.27.
</p><p>Age distribution was 29.1% under the age of 18, 7.3% from 18 to 24, 25.9% from 25 to 44, 25.6% from 45 to 64, and 12.2% 65 or older. The median age was 38 years. For every 100 females, there were 86.7 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 82.1 males.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Median_household_income" class="mw-redirect" title="Median household income">median household income</a> was $205,781, the average household income was $340,448, and the median per capita income was $116,698—making it one of the wealthiest and most affluent places with more than 1,000 households, or a population greater than 1,000, in the United States. Median income is currently ranked 16th highest in the country. Males had a median income of $100,000, versus $61,184 for females. The <a href="/wiki/Per_capita_income" title="Per capita income">per capita income</a> for the village was $116,698 and the average family income was $417,772. About 1.7% of families and 2.7% of the population were below the <a href="/wiki/Poverty_line" class="mw-redirect" title="Poverty line">poverty line</a>, including 1.6% of those under age 18 and 2.9% of those age 65 or over. In 2016, Forbes named it one of the ten most expensive suburbs of America's major cities.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Postal_code">Postal code</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bronxville,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Postal code">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bronxville's 10708 <a href="/wiki/ZIP_Code" title="ZIP Code">ZIP Code</a> covers the village of Bronxville proper, plus Chester Heights and other sections of Eastchester, parts of <a href="/wiki/Tuckahoe,_Westchester_County,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Tuckahoe, Westchester County, New York">Tuckahoe</a>, and Lawrence Park West, Cedar Knolls, <a href="/wiki/Armour_Villa,_Yonkers" title="Armour Villa, Yonkers">Armour Villa</a>, and other sections of <a href="/wiki/Yonkers,_New_York" title="Yonkers, New York">Yonkers</a>. These areas are collectively known as "Bronxville P.O." and are not located with-in the boundaries of the Village of Bronxville, therefore their residents are not considered residents of the village despite sharing the same zip code.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> This brings the ZIP code's population to 22,411 (2000 census), covering an area more than twice as large as the municipality of Bronxville itself and encompassing several notable institutions, such as <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Lawrence_College" title="Sarah Lawrence College">Sarah Lawrence College</a> in Yonkers.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In fact, there are more residents of Yonkers using a Bronxville mailing address than living in the village itself.<sup id="cite_ref-NY_Times_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NY_Times-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Post_Office_(Bronxville,_New_York)" title="United States Post Office (Bronxville, New York)">Bronxville Post Office</a> serves residents of the village.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bronxville,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bronxville_HS.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Bronxville_HS.jpg/220px-Bronxville_HS.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Bronxville_HS.jpg/330px-Bronxville_HS.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Bronxville_HS.jpg/440px-Bronxville_HS.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2400" data-file-height="1800" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bronxville_HS.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Bronxville School</div></div></div>
<p>Bronxville is home to <a href="/wiki/Concordia_College_(New_York)" title="Concordia College (New York)">Concordia College</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Liberal_arts_college" title="Liberal arts college">liberal arts college</a> operated by the <a href="/wiki/Lutheran_Church%E2%80%93Missouri_Synod" title="Lutheran Church–Missouri Synod">Lutheran Church–Missouri Synod</a>. Adjacent to the Concordia College campus is the Chapel School—a pre-K-8 school affiliated with the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Bronxville_Union_Free_School_District" title="Bronxville Union Free School District">Bronxville Public School</a> is known as The Bronxville School.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The school was started as a <a href="/wiki/Progressive_education" title="Progressive education">progressive educational</a> institution in the 1920s.
</p><p>St. Joseph School is a Catholic <a href="/wiki/Parochial_school" title="Parochial school">parochial school</a> run by <a href="/wiki/Church_of_St._Joseph_(Bronxville,_New_York)" title="Church of St. Joseph (Bronxville, New York)">St. Joseph's Church</a>. It was established in 1951, and schools children from kindergarten through eighth grade.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Parks_and_recreation">Parks and recreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bronxville,_New_York&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Parks and recreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bronx_River1.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Bronx_River1.JPG/220px-Bronx_River1.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Bronx_River1.JPG/330px-Bronx_River1.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Bronx_River1.JPG/440px-Bronx_River1.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2848" data-file-height="2136" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bronx_River1.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bronx River Parkway Reservation</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bville_river.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Bville_river.jpg/220px-Bville_river.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Bville_river.jpg/330px-Bville_river.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Bville_river.jpg/440px-Bville_river.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2400" data-file-height="1800" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bville_river.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Bronx River</div></div></div>
<p>The Village of Bronxville has more than 70 acres (280,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) of parkland including athletic fields, woodlands, and a very small part of the <a href="/wiki/Bronx_River" title="Bronx River">Bronx River</a> Parkway Reservation. The Reservation, Westchester’s oldest park, was created as an adjunct to the <a href="/wiki/Bronx_River_Parkway" title="Bronx River Parkway">Bronx River Parkway</a> that opened in 1925, and was the first linear park in the United States. The Reservation features ponds, wooden footbridges and hundreds of varieties of native trees and shrubs. The park is owned by Westchester County, and it is a favorite place for bicycling, walking, running, and nature study.  It is sometimes referred to by locals as "The Duck Pond."
</p><p>The Bronxville School's athletic fields contain a football field, three smaller fields used for various sports like field hockey and lacrosse, and a running track (which is only 380 meters in Lane 1 because of space issues). Bacon Woodlands, located on Kensington Road, is a natural rock outcropping which has been left in its natural state, the flatter portion of which is used as an informal play area by children. Scout Field, a Westchester County Park which is located predominantly in Yonkers and <a href="/wiki/Mount_Vernon,_New_York" title="Mount Vernon, New York">Mount Vernon</a> but is controlled by Bronxville, is heavily utilized by the Bronxville schools' soccer, football, baseball and cross-country running programs.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In 2006, Chambers Field was replaced with turf, which was funded by the community and parents of athletes in Bronxville.
</p><p>From April to June and September to October, a 7-mile (11&#160;km) stretch of the Bronx River Parkway (no part of the roadway of which is in Bronxville) from Scarsdale Road in Yonkers (north of Bronxville) to <a href="/wiki/White_Plains,_New_York" title="White Plains, New York">White Plains</a> closes to automobile traffic each Sunday (except on holiday weekends) between 10 a.m. and 2 p.m. During those times, the Westchester County Parks Department runs "Bicycle Sundays" along this stretch of the parkway.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
