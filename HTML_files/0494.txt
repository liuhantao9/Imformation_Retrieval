<p><b>Virgil Soeroredjo</b> (born 11 March 1985) is a former <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Surinamese</a> <a href="/wiki/Badminton" title="Badminton">badminton</a> player and now coach. He competed for Suriname at the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Badminton at the 2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He also competed for Suriname at 3 Pan Am Games: the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2003_Pan_American_Games" title="Badminton at the 2003 Pan American Games">2003 Pan American Games</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2007_Pan_American_Games" title="Badminton at the 2007 Pan American Games">2007 Pan American Games</a> &amp; the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2011_Pan_American_Games" title="Badminton at the 2011 Pan American Games">2011 Pan American Games</a>. As a young badminton player of the club <a href="/w/index.php?title=SCVU&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="SCVU (page does not exist)">SCVU</a> in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> Virgil Soeroredjo won numerous juniors titles and was soon selected to represent his country abroad winning several juniors medals at Caribbean, Central American and South American events. Lots of his success was with his doubles partner <a href="/wiki/Mitchel_Wongsodikromo" title="Mitchel Wongsodikromo">Mitchel Wongsodikromo</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Multiple_Caribbean_and_Pan_Am_Juniors_badminton_champion">Multiple Caribbean and Pan Am Juniors badminton champion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Virgil_Soeroredjo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Multiple Caribbean and Pan Am Juniors badminton champion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Together they grew up to become a badminton force for Suriname in the Pan Am region. In 2000 they won the Pan Am Boys Doubles U-17 juniors title at <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> after they had already won the Caribbean Boys Doubles titles U-17 &amp; U-19 the same year at <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>. At the 2000 Carebaco Games Virgil Soeroredjo won 5 Gold Medals plus a Silver Medal. Winning the triple in the U-17 category plus Boys Doubles in the U-19 category and the U-19 team event for Suriname and a second place in Mixed Doubles U-19. In 2001 Virgil Soeroredjo managed to successfully defend four of his five Carebaco juniors titles only losing his singles U-17 title in the final at <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a>. In 2001 Virgil &amp; Mitchel won Boys Doubles U-17 Gold at the South American Juniors Championships in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. That year they also won the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Regatas_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Regatas Cup (page does not exist)">Regatas Cup</a> for Boys Doubles U-17 in <a href="/wiki/Lima" title="Lima">Lima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>. In 2002 Virgil Soeroredjo &amp; Mitchel Wongsodikromo became Pan American Juniors Semi-Champions U-19 in <a href="/wiki/Orange_County,_California" title="Orange County, California">Orange County</a>, USA. At the same event Virgil Soeroredjo also took two Bronze Medals, one in the U-19 Mixed Doubles category and also in the team event, where the Surinamese juniors team surprisingly beat favorites Canada. In the 2002 Carebaco Games Virgil Soeroredjo won Gold at the Men's Singles and the Men's Doubles U-19 juniors categories at <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>. Together with compatriot <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carolyn_Davids&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carolyn Davids (page does not exist)">Carolyn Davids</a> he participated at the Pan American IBF World Academy training camp held in Lima, Peru 2002. In the 2003 Carebaco Games at <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_%26_Tobago" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinidad &amp; Tobago">Trinidad &amp; Tobago</a> Virgil Soeroredjo lost both the U-19 Boys Singles &amp; Mixed Doubles finals to his compatriot Mitchel Wongsodikromo. Together they eventually won the Boys Doubles U-19 title and the Carebaco Juniors 2003 team event.
<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_and_national_achievements">International and national achievements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Virgil_Soeroredjo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International and national achievements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2002 they both won a Men's Singles Bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/2002_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="2002 Central American and Caribbean Games">2002 Central American and Caribbean Games</a> (CACSO Games 2002) in San Salvador. At that time a special achievement since they were both still 17-year-old juniors. In 2003 Virgil Soeroredjo was part of the first Surinamese badminton team that participated at the <a href="/wiki/Sudirman_Cup" title="Sudirman Cup">Sudirman Cup</a> Mixed teams World Championships in <a href="/wiki/Eindhoven" title="Eindhoven">Eindhoven</a>, the Netherlands. In 2005 at the Carebaco Open Championships Virgil Soeroredjo managed to get in the semi-finals Men's Singles achieving a Bronze Medal as well as in the Team Event held in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a>.
</p><p>Virgil Soeroredjo gained six National Men's Singles titles in 2004, 2006, 2008, 2011, 2013 &amp; 2014. Internationally he was a triple champion at the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_International_Badminton_Tournament" class="mw-redirect" title="Suriname International Badminton Tournament">Suriname International</a> in 2008 winning all three categories Men's Singles, Men's Doubles and Mixed Doubles. In 2009 he won the Men's Singles and Men's Doubles titles at the Suriname International, in 2010 he won the Mixed Doubles title and in 2011 he won the Men's Doubles title at the Suriname International. 
In 2010 Virgil Soeroredjo &amp; Mitchel Wongsodikromo reached the final at the Bill Graham Miami International<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and in 2011 they reached the semi-final of that same event.<sup id="cite_ref-tournamentsoftware1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tournamentsoftware1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2011 Virgil Soeroredjo won both the Suriname International and the Carebaco International<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> in Men's Doubles. In March 2010 Virgil Soeroredjo was also part of the Suriname badminton team that won a Bronze medal <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2010_South_American_Games_%E2%80%93_Mixed_team" title="Badminton at the 2010 South American Games – Mixed team">Mixed Teams</a> <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2010_South_American_Games" title="Badminton at the 2010 South American Games">Badminton at the 2010 South American Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Medell%C3%ADn" title="Medellín">Medellín</a>. In July 2010 Virgil Soeroredjo &amp; Mitchel Wongsodikromo won the only medal, a Bronze, for Suriname at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="2010 Central American and Caribbean Games">2010 Central American and Caribbean Games</a> by reaching the semi-finales. In 2012 Virgil Soeroredjo was part of the first Suriname Men's badminton team that participated at the <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Cup" title="Thomas Cup">Thomas Cup</a> preliminaries in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, USA.
</p><p>In 2009 Virgil Soeroredjo moved to the Netherlands to improve his skills, training under coach <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mike_van_Daal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mike van Daal (page does not exist)">Mike van Daal</a>. He participated at the highest badminton club competition level (<a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a>) for <a href="/w/index.php?title=BC_Culemborg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="BC Culemborg (page does not exist)">BC Culemborg</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=BV_van_Zijderveld&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="BV van Zijderveld (page does not exist)">BV van Zijderveld</a>. At that time this badminton player started switching his focus on his sports management study at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Johan_Cruyff_University&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Johan Cruyff University (page does not exist)">Johan Cruyff University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>.
From 2012 the National Association, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinaamse_Badminton_Bond&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Surinaamse Badminton Bond (page does not exist)">Surinaamse Badminton Bond</a> was annually organizing a Top 8 Singles event in honor of Virgil Soeroredjo's participation at the 2012 London Olympics. In 2013 Virgil Soeroredjo was planning on attending this event in his home country held by the badminton club of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nieuw_Stenov&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nieuw Stenov (page does not exist)">Nieuw Stenov</a>, unfortunately he did not due to his busy study schedule.
In November 2013 Virgil attended the National Championships and 7th Suriname International in Paramaribo. In April 2014 he won the 3rd Assuria International Easter Badminton Tournament 2014 in Paramaribo beating young Jamaican talent Samuel Ricketts in the final. In November 2014 Virgil attended the National Championships and 8th Suriname International in Paramaribo. Due to a recurring knee injury Virgil decided to retire as an international player in December 2015.  He then followed the BWF Level 1 and Level 2 Badminton Coaches courses successfully.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Virgil_Soeroredjo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Virgil Soeroredjo and his wife Willeke have one daughter. Virgil Soeroredjo graduated in 2018 as sports marketeer from the Johan Cruijff University in the Netherlands and is nowadays assistant trainer/coach at the Dutch premier division club The Flying Shuttle Barendrecht.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
