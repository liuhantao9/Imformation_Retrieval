<p>"<b>Last Name</b>" is a song recorded by American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> and written by Underwood, <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luke_Laird" title="Luke Laird">Luke Laird</a>. It is the third single from Underwood's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Carnival_Ride" title="Carnival Ride">Carnival Ride</a></i>. It was released in the United States on April 7, 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> by which point the song had already charted. At the <a href="/wiki/51st_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="51st Grammy Awards">51st Grammy Awards</a>, the song won Underwood her third consecutive <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Female_Country_Vocal_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance">Grammy Award for Best Female Country Vocal Performance</a>. It has sold 1,300,000 copies to date.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Last_Name_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song is one of four tracks on the album co-written by Underwood, and the third consecutive one to be released as a single from the album. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Last_Name_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song is a moderate up-tempo describing a woman meeting a man at a club and later eloping with him in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Strip" title="Las Vegas Strip">Las Vegas</a> after having had too much to drink that night. She wakes up the next morning, "thinkin' 'bout Elvis somewhere in Vegas", to discover that she does not even know her last name (i.e., she married the man while she was intoxicated), and worries that her "mama would be so ashamed." The music video portrays the song as a <a href="/wiki/Prequel" title="Prequel">prequel</a> to her "<a href="/wiki/Before_He_Cheats" title="Before He Cheats">Before He Cheats</a>" song, even going so far as to hire the same actor to play the man in question.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Last_Name_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The single was generally met with mixed reviews. 
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Allmusic" class="mw-redirect" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a></i> picked the song as a "track pick", calling the song "<a href="/wiki/Miranda_Lambert" title="Miranda Lambert">Miranda Lambert</a> filtered through <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain" title="Shania Twain">Shania Twain</a>", and dubbed it a "one night stand anthem."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rolling Stone (magazine)">Rolling Stone</a></i> picked the track as their favorite, saying "the most fun is "Last Name," where she gets wasted and runs off to Vegas with a guy she doesn't know."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Blender_(magazine)" title="Blender (magazine)">Blender</a></i> awarded the song four out of five stars, describing the song as the "most irresponsible (and fun) moment on the new album involves one wild night, one too many shots of Cuervo and one unexpected ring."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> gave a positive review of the track, praising the lyrics: "It's a cleverly penned lyric that hilariously celebrates drunken debauchery" as well as summing it up as "a performance that combines soulful vocals, edgy intensity and sassy attitude into a delicious cocktail."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>However, it also received some negative reviews: <i>Engine 145</i> gave the song a 'thumbs down', describing the song as a "straight-laced performance" that just "places the focus on the vocal".<sup id="cite_ref-engine145_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-engine145-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant Magazine</a></i> labelled the single as a "bald-faced attempt at recreating the 'Before He Cheats' phenomenon".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Underwood performed the song live for the first time on television during the 43rd Annual <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music Awards</a> on May 18, 2008. She performed the song live at the <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_7)" title="American Idol (season 7)">seventh season</a> finale of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> on May 21, 2008. She performed the song at the <a href="/wiki/51st_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="51st Annual Grammy Awards">51st Annual Grammy Awards</a> on February 8, 2009.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Last_Name_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song is described in the video as what happened before the "Before He Cheats" video.
</p><p>The video starts with some segments from "Before He Cheats" and then goes into a scene three months earlier ("before he cheated", it says) with her in the club drinking with her friends. The same man from the "Before He Cheats" spots her and asks her for a dance. From this point on, while the guy is with Underwood, he is <a href="/wiki/Flirting" title="Flirting">flirting</a> with every other woman he sees. After the dance, Underwood leaves with the guy in his car with <a href="/wiki/Fuzzy_dice" title="Fuzzy dice">fuzzy dice</a> in the rear view mirror. They make it out to <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas,_Nevada" class="mw-redirect" title="Las Vegas, Nevada">Las Vegas</a> where they participate in a couple of gambling activities such as <a href="/wiki/Roulette" title="Roulette">roulette</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Big_Six_wheel" title="Big Six wheel">Wheel of Fortune</a> game where they win a new truck. Later they get married in a chapel with an <a href="/wiki/Elvis_impersonator" title="Elvis impersonator">Elvis impersonator</a> officiating. They run off in his new pickup truck (the one used in "Before He Cheats") and the video ends with a spark from the chapel sign lights.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Last_Name_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song debuted at number 46 on the U.S. <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart, nearly a month before the single's official release. For the week of June 21, 2008 it rose from number five to number one, to become her fifth straight number one on the <i>Billboard</i> chart, her seventh straight number one country single, and her eighth number one <i>Billboard</i> single overall. This marked Underwood as the first solo female artist to have five consecutive number one's on the country survey in almost 20 years, since <a href="/wiki/Rosanne_Cash" title="Rosanne Cash">Rosanne Cash</a> had a string of six number one titles between 1987 and 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It is the third country number one from <i>Carnival Ride</i> and reached the top of the chart faster than the first two singles, taking only 13 weeks. "Last Name" has sold 1,300,000 downloads as of November 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the <a href="/wiki/Hot_100" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 100">Hot 100</a> the song reached number 19, making it Underwood's fifth top 20 Hot 100 hit, and the second from <i>Carnival Ride</i>.
</p>
