<p><b>Dewsbury railway station</b> serves the town of <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. Situated 9.25 miles (15&#160;km) south west of <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_line" title="Huddersfield line">main line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_station_group" title="Manchester station group">Manchester</a>, the station was opened by the <a href="/wiki/London_and_North_Western_Railway" title="London and North Western Railway">London and North Western Railway</a> in 1848.
</p><p>The station is now managed by <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a>, who provide trains to <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Hull</a> and <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> also serve the station with trains on the <a href="/wiki/Calder_Valley_line" title="Calder Valley line">Calder Valley line</a>.
</p>

<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield,_Bradley_Wood,_Heaton_Lodge,_Thornhill,_Mirfield_%26_Dewsbury_RJD_36.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Huddersfield%2C_Bradley_Wood%2C_Heaton_Lodge%2C_Thornhill%2C_Mirfield_%26_Dewsbury_RJD_36.jpg/220px-Huddersfield%2C_Bradley_Wood%2C_Heaton_Lodge%2C_Thornhill%2C_Mirfield_%26_Dewsbury_RJD_36.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Huddersfield%2C_Bradley_Wood%2C_Heaton_Lodge%2C_Thornhill%2C_Mirfield_%26_Dewsbury_RJD_36.jpg/330px-Huddersfield%2C_Bradley_Wood%2C_Heaton_Lodge%2C_Thornhill%2C_Mirfield_%26_Dewsbury_RJD_36.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Huddersfield%2C_Bradley_Wood%2C_Heaton_Lodge%2C_Thornhill%2C_Mirfield_%26_Dewsbury_RJD_36.jpg/440px-Huddersfield%2C_Bradley_Wood%2C_Heaton_Lodge%2C_Thornhill%2C_Mirfield_%26_Dewsbury_RJD_36.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2861" data-file-height="1890" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield,_Bradley_Wood,_Heaton_Lodge,_Thornhill,_Mirfield_%26_Dewsbury_RJD_36.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A 1911 <a href="/wiki/Railway_Clearing_House" title="Railway Clearing House">Railway Clearing House</a> Junction Diagram showing railways in the vicinity of Dewsbury (upper right)</div></div></div>
<p>The line between Leeds and <a href="/wiki/Ravensthorpe_railway_station" title="Ravensthorpe railway station">Ravensthorpe</a> was built by the Leeds, Dewsbury and Manchester Railway, which was absorbed by the London and North Western Railway prior to opening.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Dewsbury railway station was opened on 18 September 1848 and was subsequently named <i>Dewsbury Wellington Road</i> from 2 June 1924 until 20 February 1969, when it reverted to the original name.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dewsbury was also served by three other stations which have since closed:
</p>
<ul><li>Dewsbury Central, served by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Northern_Railway_(Great_Britain)" title="Great Northern Railway (Great Britain)">Great Northern Railway</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Exchange_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradford Exchange railway station">Bradford Exchange</a> to Wakefield line, closed in 1964.</li>
<li>Dewsbury Market Place, built by the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_and_Leeds_Railway" title="Manchester and Leeds Railway">Manchester and Leeds Railway</a>, closed in 1930.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Thornhill_railway_station" title="Thornhill railway station">Thornhill</a>, also built by the Manchester and Leeds Railway, closed in 1961.</li></ul>
<p>No trace of Market Place station remains, but the façade of Dewsbury Central was incorporated into a bridge supporting the Dewsbury Ring Road in 1985.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The station is staffed through the day, with the ticket office on Platform 1. Self-service ticket machines are also provided in the booking hall.
</p><p>There are waiting rooms on each platform, along with digital display screens, customer help points, timetable poster boards and automated announcements to offer train running information. There are no toilets. Lifts integrated into the footbridge provide step-free access to both platforms. The footbridge is also a public right of way.
</p><p>Ticket barriers were installed in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In summer 2018, Kirklees Council started work on making improvements to the outside of the station. Plans included pedestrianizing the area directly outside the station and redesigning the car park entrances to improve traffic flow. The improvements are part of the "North Kirklees Growth Zone" initiative, intended to be a kick start to improvements to the town.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dewsbury_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
