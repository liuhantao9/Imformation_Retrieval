<p><b>Tommy Asinga</b> (born 20 November 1968 in <a href="/wiki/Moengo" title="Moengo">Moengo</a>) is a track star from <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>.
</p><p>Asinga trained in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, attending <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Michigan_University" title="Eastern Michigan University">Eastern Michigan University</a> from 1991–1994, where he won <a href="/wiki/All-American" class="mw-redirect" title="All-American">All-American</a> honors five times.
</p><p>Asinga competed at three Olympic Games, representing <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>: in <a href="/wiki/1988_Summer_Olympics" title="1988 Summer Olympics">1988</a> at <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992</a> at <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a> (where he was <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>'s flag bearer in the opening ceremonies) and in <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics" title="1996 Summer Olympics">1996</a> in <a href="/wiki/Atlanta,_Georgia" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlanta, Georgia">Atlanta</a>.
</p><p>Asinga won the bronze medal in the <a href="/wiki/800_metres" title="800 metres">800 metres</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1991_Pan_American_Games" title="1991 Pan American Games">1991 Pan American Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/Havana,_Cuba" class="mw-redirect" title="Havana, Cuba">Havana, Cuba</a>. That same year, he also took the silver medal <a href="/wiki/800_metres" title="800 metres">800 metres</a> run at the 1991 NCAA Outdoor Championships. Asinga placed sixth in the 1992 <a href="/wiki/NCAA" class="mw-redirect" title="NCAA">NCAA</a> Indoor Championships. In 1993, Asinga was the anchor leg on the winning 3,200-meter <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Michigan_University" title="Eastern Michigan University">Eastern Michigan University</a> relay team at the <a href="/wiki/NCAA" class="mw-redirect" title="NCAA">NCAA</a> Indoor Championships. Asinga also won the silver medal in the <a href="/wiki/Mid-American_Conference" title="Mid-American Conference">Mid-American Conference</a> outdoor <a href="/wiki/400_metres" title="400 metres">400 metres</a> in three consecutive years in 1992, 1993 and 1994.
</p><p>After graduating from <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Michigan_University" title="Eastern Michigan University">Eastern Michigan University</a>, Asinga went to <a href="/wiki/Tuskegee_University" title="Tuskegee University">Tuskegee University</a>'s School of Veterinary Medicine, and is now a practicing <a href="/wiki/Veterinarian" title="Veterinarian">veterinarian</a>. He is the husband of <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambian</a> former sprinter <a href="/wiki/Ngozi_Mwanamwambwa" title="Ngozi Mwanamwambwa">Ngozi Mwanamwambwa</a>.
</p>
