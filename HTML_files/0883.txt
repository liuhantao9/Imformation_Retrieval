<p>The <b>City of Leeds</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span><span title="/iː/: &#39;ee&#39; in &#39;fleece&#39;">iː</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span><span title="&#39;z&#39; in &#39;zoom&#39;">z</span></span>/</a></span></span> <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>) is a <a href="/wiki/Local_government_district" class="mw-redirect" title="Local government district">local government district</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England, governed by <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a>, with the status of a <a href="/wiki/City_status_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="City status in the United Kingdom">city</a> and <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a>. The metropolitan district includes the administrative centre <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and the ten towns of <a href="/wiki/Farsley" title="Farsley">Farsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garforth" title="Garforth">Garforth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guiseley" title="Guiseley">Guiseley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Horsforth" title="Horsforth">Horsforth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Morley, West Yorkshire">Morley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Otley" title="Otley">Otley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pudsey" title="Pudsey">Pudsey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rothwell,_West_Yorkshire" title="Rothwell, West Yorkshire">Rothwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wetherby" title="Wetherby">Wetherby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yeadon,_West_Yorkshire" title="Yeadon, West Yorkshire">Yeadon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It has a population of 789,194 (mid-2018&#160;est.), making it technically the second <a href="/wiki/List_of_English_districts_by_population" title="List of English districts by population">largest city</a> in England by population behind <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a>.
</p><p>The current city boundaries were set on 1 April 1974 by the provisions of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, as part a reform of <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_England" title="Local government in England">local government in England</a>. The city is a <a href="/wiki/Amalgamation_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Amalgamation (politics)">merger</a> of eleven former local government districts; the unitary <a href="/wiki/County_Borough_of_Leeds" title="County Borough of Leeds">City and County Borough of Leeds</a> combined with the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal boroughs</a> of <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Borough_of_Morley" title="Municipal Borough of Morley">Morley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Borough_of_Pudsey" title="Municipal Borough of Pudsey">Pudsey</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban districts</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aireborough_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Aireborough Urban District">Aireborough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garforth_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Garforth Urban District">Garforth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Horsforth_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Horsforth Urban District">Horsforth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Otley_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Otley Urban District">Otley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rothwell_Urban_District_(Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rothwell Urban District (Yorkshire)">Rothwell</a>, and parts of the <a href="/wiki/Rural_district" title="Rural district">rural districts</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tadcaster_Rural_District" title="Tadcaster Rural District">Tadcaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale_Rural_District" title="Wharfedale Rural District">Wharfedale</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Rural_District" title="Wetherby Rural District">Wetherby</a> from the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>.
</p><p>For its first 12 years the city had a two-tier system of local government; Leeds City Council shared power with the West Yorkshire County Council. Since the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1985" title="Local Government Act 1985">Local Government Act 1985</a> Leeds City Council has effectively been a <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authority</a>, serving as the sole <a href="/wiki/Executive_(government)" title="Executive (government)">executive</a>, <a href="/wiki/Deliberation" title="Deliberation">deliberative</a> and <a href="/wiki/Legislative" class="mw-redirect" title="Legislative">legislative</a> body responsible for local policy, setting <a href="/wiki/Council_tax" class="mw-redirect" title="Council tax">council tax</a>, and allocating budget in the city, and is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Region" title="Leeds City Region">Leeds City Region Partnership</a>. The City of Leeds is divided into 31 <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parishes</a> and a single <a href="/wiki/Unparished_area" title="Unparished area">unparished area</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Leeds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Leeds" title="History of Leeds">History of Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Borough_of_Leeds" title="County Borough of Leeds">County Borough of Leeds</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Leeds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/County_Borough_of_Leeds" title="County Borough of Leeds">Borough of Leeds</a> was created in 1207, when Maurice Paynel, lord of the manor, granted a charter covering a small area adjacent to a crossing of the River Aire, between the old settlement centred on Leeds Parish Church to the east and the manor house and mills to the west. In 1626 a charter was granted by Charles I, incorporating the entire parish as the <a href="/wiki/County_Borough_of_Leeds" title="County Borough of Leeds">Borough of Leeds</a>; it was reformed by the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Corporations_Act_1835" title="Municipal Corporations Act 1835">Municipal Corporations Act 1835</a>. The parish and borough included the chapelries of <a href="/wiki/Chapel_Allerton" title="Chapel Allerton">Chapel Allerton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armley" title="Armley">Armley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beeston,_Leeds" title="Beeston, Leeds">Beeston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beeston,_Leeds" title="Beeston, Leeds">Bramley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Farnley,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Farnley, West Yorkshire">Farnley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Headingley_cum_Burley" class="mw-redirect" title="Headingley cum Burley">Headingley cum Burley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holbeck" title="Holbeck">Holbeck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunslet" title="Hunslet">Hunslet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Potternewton" title="Potternewton">Potternewton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wortley,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Wortley, West Yorkshire">Wortley</a>. The borough was located in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding</a> of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> and gained <a href="/wiki/City_status_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="City status in the United Kingdom">city status</a> in 1893. When a county council was formed for the riding in 1889, Leeds was excluded from its area of responsibility and formed a <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county borough</a>. The borough made a significant number of territorial expansions, expanding from 21,593 acres (87.38&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) in 1911 to 40,612 acres (164.35&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) in 1961;<sup id="cite_ref-county_borough_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-county_borough-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> adding in stages the former area of the Roundhay, Seacroft, Shadwell and <a href="/wiki/Middleton,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Middleton, West Yorkshire">Middleton</a> parishes and gaining other parts of adjacent districts.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Leeds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A review of local government arrangements <a href="/wiki/Redcliffe-Maud_Report" title="Redcliffe-Maud Report">completed in 1969</a> proposed the creation of a new large district centred on Leeds, occupying 317,000 acres (1,280&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) and including 840,000 people. The proposed area was significantly reduced in a 1971 <a href="/wiki/White_paper" title="White paper">white paper</a>; and within a year every local authority to be incorporated into it protested or demonstrated.<sup id="cite_ref-fraser_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fraser-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The final proposal reduced the area further and following the enactment of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, the county borough was abolished on 1 April 1974 and its former area was combined with that of the municipal boroughs of <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Borough_of_Morley" title="Municipal Borough of Morley">Morley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Municipal_Borough_of_Pudsey" title="Municipal Borough of Pudsey">Pudsey</a>; the urban districts of <a href="/wiki/Aireborough_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Aireborough Urban District">Aireborough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Horsforth_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Horsforth Urban District">Horsforth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Otley_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Otley Urban District">Otley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garforth_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Garforth Urban District">Garforth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rothwell_Urban_District_(Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rothwell Urban District (Yorkshire)">Rothwell</a>; and parts of the rural districts of <a href="/wiki/Tadcaster_Rural_District" title="Tadcaster Rural District">Tadcaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wetherby_Rural_District" title="Wetherby Rural District">Wetherby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale_Rural_District" title="Wharfedale Rural District">Wharfedale</a>. The new district gained both borough and city status, as had been held by the county borough; and forms part of the county of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Leeds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:South_Leeds_aerial_photograph.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/South_Leeds_aerial_photograph.jpg/220px-South_Leeds_aerial_photograph.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/South_Leeds_aerial_photograph.jpg/330px-South_Leeds_aerial_photograph.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/South_Leeds_aerial_photograph.jpg/440px-South_Leeds_aerial_photograph.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:South_Leeds_aerial_photograph.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An aerial photograph of South Leeds, showing Beeston, Holbeck, Wortley, Elland Road stadium and the M621</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/List_of_places_in_Leeds" title="List of places in Leeds">The district and its settlements</a> are situated in the eastern foothills of the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a> astride the <a href="/wiki/River_Aire" title="River Aire">River Aire</a> whose valley, the Aire Gap, provides a road and rail corridor that facilitates communications with cities to the west of the Pennines. The district extends 15 miles (24&#160;km) from east to west and 13 miles (21&#160;km) from north to south; with over 65% covered with <a href="/wiki/Green_belt_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Green belt (UK)">green belt</a> land. The highest point, at 1,115&#160;feet (340&#160;m), is at its north western extremity on the eastern slopes of Rombalds Moor, better known as <a href="/wiki/Ilkley_Moor" title="Ilkley Moor">Ilkley Moor</a>, on the boundary with the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford" title="City of Bradford">City of Bradford</a>. The lowest points are at around 33&#160;feet (10&#160;m), in the east: where <a href="/wiki/River_Wharfe" title="River Wharfe">River Wharfe</a> crosses the boundary with <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a> south of <a href="/wiki/Thorp_Arch_Trading_Estate" title="Thorp Arch Trading Estate">Thorp Arch Trading Estate</a> and where the River Aire (at this point forming the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Wakefield" title="City of Wakefield">City of Wakefield</a> boundary) meets the North Yorkshire boundary near <a href="/wiki/Fairburn_Ings_RSPB_reserve" title="Fairburn Ings RSPB reserve">Fairburn Ings</a>. To the north and east Leeds is bordered by <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>: <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_(borough)" class="mw-redirect" title="Harrogate (borough)">Harrogate district</a> to the north and <a href="/wiki/Selby_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Selby (district)">Selby district</a> to the east. The remaining borders are with other districts of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>: <a href="/wiki/City_of_Wakefield" title="City of Wakefield">Wakefield</a> to the south, <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a> to the south west, and <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford" title="City of Bradford">Bradford</a> to the west.<sup id="cite_ref-city_region_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-city_region-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=City_of_Leeds&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a> and <a href="/wiki/History_of_local_government_in_Yorkshire" title="History of local government in Yorkshire">History of local government in Yorkshire</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Leeds_Civic_Hall_11_Sep_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Leeds_Civic_Hall_11_Sep_2016.jpg/250px-Leeds_Civic_Hall_11_Sep_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Leeds_Civic_Hall_11_Sep_2016.jpg/375px-Leeds_Civic_Hall_11_Sep_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Leeds_Civic_Hall_11_Sep_2016.jpg/500px-Leeds_Civic_Hall_11_Sep_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Leeds_Civic_Hall_11_Sep_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Leeds Civic hall, Millennium Square, meeting place of Leeds City Council</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a> is the local authority of the district. The council is composed of 99 councillors, three for each of the city's <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Wards of the United Kingdom">wards</a>. Elections are held three years out of four, on the first Thursday of May. One third of the councillors are elected, for a four-year term, in each election. 2004 saw all seats up for election due to boundary changes. It is currently run by a <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> administration. Before the 2011 election, the council had been under <a href="/wiki/No_overall_control" title="No overall control">no overall control</a> since 2004. The Chief Executive of Leeds City Council is Tom Riordan while the Leader of the Council is Councillor <a href="/wiki/Judith_Blake" title="Judith Blake">Judith Blake</a> of the Labour Party. As a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_county" title="Metropolitan county">metropolitan county</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> does not have a county council, so Leeds City Council is the primary provider of local government services. The district forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_and_the_Humber" title="Yorkshire and the Humber">Yorkshire and the Humber</a> region of England.
</p><p>Most of the district is an <a href="/wiki/Unparished_area" title="Unparished area">unparished area</a>, comprising Leeds itself (the area of the former county borough), Pudsey, Garforth, Rothwell and the area of the former urban district of Aireborough.  In the unparished area there is no lower tier of government. Outside the unparished area there are 31 <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parishes</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">parish councils</a>.  These form the lowest tier of local government<sup id="cite_ref-leeds_parish_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-leeds_parish-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and absorb some limited functions from Leeds City Council in their areas. The councils of the civil parishes of <a href="/wiki/Horsforth" title="Horsforth">Horsforth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Morley, West Yorkshire">Morley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Otley" title="Otley">Otley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wetherby" title="Wetherby">Wetherby</a> are town councils.<sup id="cite_ref-map_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-map-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  The 27 other civil parishes are:

<p>At the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001 UK census</a>, the district had a total population of 715,402.<sup id="cite_ref-Leeds_ethnic_group_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leeds_ethnic_group-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Of the 301,614 households in Leeds, 33.3% were married couples living together, 31.6% were one-person households, 9.0% were <a href="/wiki/Cohabitation" title="Cohabitation">co-habiting</a> couples and 9.8% were lone parents, following a similar trend to the rest of England.<sup id="cite_ref-household_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-household-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The population density was 1,967/km<sup>2</sup> (5,090/sq&#160;mi)<sup id="cite_ref-household_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-household-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and for every 100 females, there were 93.5 males. Of those aged 16–74, 30.9% had no <a href="/wiki/National_Qualifications_Framework" class="mw-redirect" title="National Qualifications Framework">academic qualifications</a>, higher than the 28.9% in all of England.<sup id="cite_ref-key_stats_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-key_stats-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Of the residents, 6.6% were born outside the United Kingdom, lower than the England average of 9.2%.<sup id="cite_ref-stats_birth_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats_birth-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The majority of people in Leeds identify themselves as <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Census_Data_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Census_Data-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The proportion of <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslims</a> is average for the country.<sup id="cite_ref-Census_Data_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Census_Data-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Leeds has the third-largest <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Jewish</a> community in the United Kingdom, after those of London and Manchester. The areas of <a href="/wiki/Alwoodley" title="Alwoodley">Alwoodley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moortown,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Moortown, West Yorkshire">Moortown</a> contain sizeable Jewish populations.<sup id="cite_ref-Freedman_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Freedman-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> 16.8% of Leeds residents in the 2001 census declared themselves as having "no religion", which is broadly in line with the figure for the whole of the UK (also 8.1% "religion not stated").
</p><p>The crime rate in Leeds is well above the national average, like many other English major cities.<sup id="cite_ref-crime_street_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crime_street-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-crime_stat_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crime_stat-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In July 2006, the <a href="/wiki/Think_tank" title="Think tank">think tank</a> <a href="/wiki/Reform_(think_tank)" title="Reform (think tank)">Reform</a> calculated rates of crime for different offences and has related this to populations of major urban areas (defined as towns over 100,000 population). Leeds was 11th in this rating (excluding London boroughs, 23rd including London boroughs).<sup id="cite_ref-reform_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reform-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The table below details the population of the current area of the district since 1801, including the percentage change since the last available census data.
</p>

In April 2011 Leeds City Council disbanded Education Leeds and has consolidated educational services into the Children's Services Department of the council itself.<sup id="cite_ref-edu_leeds_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-edu_leeds-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
