<p><b>Warley Town</b> is a town near <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a> in the <a href="/wiki/County" title="County">county</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England.<sup id="cite_ref-OS104_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OS104-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Warley is a ward of Calderdale. The population of this ward as taken at the 2011 Census was 12,215.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Warley_Town&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Warley Town was listed in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_book" class="mw-redirect" title="Domesday book">Domesday book</a> as <i>Werlafeslei</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Warley township, one of 23 townships in the ancient parish of Halifax, was also one of the biggest, stretching as far as <a href="/wiki/Luddenden" title="Luddenden">Luddenden</a> and what was to become <a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge" title="Sowerby Bridge">Sowerby Bridge</a>. The township consisted of many tiny settlements essentially based on the local farmsteads, places such as Lane End, Warley Edge, Winterburn Hill, Cliff Hill <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and Warley itself.
</p><p>Warley remained a small settlement until the beginning of the 18th century. Then the consolidation of the Cliff Hill estate into a major land holding, coupled with the establishment of the <a href="/wiki/Congregational_church" title="Congregational church">Congregational</a> chapel, formed the core, around which the present village grew. In 1977 the chapel was closed and converted into housing now called Chantry House.
</p><p>In front of the chapel, cottages were built for agricultural workers as well as larger houses for local businessmen who preferred to live in the country away from the <a href="/wiki/Smog" title="Smog">smog</a> of Halifax.
</p><p>Warley Town is home to the amateur football team Warley Rangers. <a href="/wiki/Wilfred_Pickles" title="Wilfred Pickles">Wilfred Pickles</a> and his wife Mabel were residents of Warley for many years, and Wilfred could occasionally be seen enjoying a pint of Webster's beer in the village pub, The Maypole. During the late 1960s, and 1970s The Maypole was run by <a href="/wiki/Jack_Wilkinson_(rugby_league)" title="Jack Wilkinson (rugby league)">Jack Wilkinson</a> and his wife June; Jack had previously been a well-known rugby league player and had played for <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Trinity" title="Wakefield Trinity">Wakefield Trinity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Halifax_R.L.F.C." title="Halifax R.L.F.C.">Halifax</a> rugby league football clubs. He also appeared as himself on the rugby pitch in the film <i><a href="/wiki/This_Sporting_Life" title="This Sporting Life">This Sporting Life</a></i>.
</p><p>The writer Dr <a href="/wiki/Phyllis_Bentley" title="Phyllis Bentley">Phyllis Bentley</a> was also a famous and long-term Warley resident.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Patrick_Bront%C3%AB" title="Patrick Brontë">Patrick Brontë</a>, father of the writers <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_Bront%C3%AB" title="Charlotte Brontë">Charlotte</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emily_Bront%C3%AB" title="Emily Brontë">Emily</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anne_Bront%C3%AB" title="Anne Brontë">Anne Brontë</a>, and of <a href="/wiki/Branwell_Bront%C3%AB" title="Branwell Brontë">Branwell Brontë</a>, lived for a time in Warley Town in a house called "The Grange" next to the congregational chapel. The <a href="/wiki/William_Swinden_Barber#Church_of_St_John_the_Evangelist,_Warley,_1877" title="William Swinden Barber">Church of St John the Evangelist</a> was completed in 1878.<sup id="cite_ref-EH_1259129_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EH_1259129-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
