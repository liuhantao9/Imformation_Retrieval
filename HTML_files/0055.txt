<p>The <b>National Library of Australia</b> (<b>NLA</b>) is the largest <a href="/wiki/Reference_library" class="mw-redirect" title="Reference library">reference library</a> in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, responsible under the terms of the <i>National Library Act</i> for "maintaining and developing a national collection of library material, including a comprehensive collection of library material relating to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Australians" title="Australians">Australian people</a>." In 2012–13, the National Library collection comprised 6,496,772 items, and an additional 15,506 metres (50,873&#160;ft) of manuscript material.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It is located in <a href="/wiki/Parkes,_Australian_Capital_Territory" title="Parkes, Australian Capital Territory">Parkes</a>, Canberra, <a href="/wiki/Australian_Capital_Territory" title="Australian Capital Territory">ACT</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Library_of_Australia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The National Library of Australia, while formally established by the passage of the <i>National Library Act 1960</i> (Cth), had been functioning as a <a href="/wiki/National_library" title="National library">national library</a> rather than strictly a Parliamentary Library, almost since its inception.
</p><p>In 1901 a <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_Library_of_Australia" title="Parliamentary Library of Australia">Commonwealth Parliamentary Library</a> was established to serve the newly formed Federal Parliament of Australia. From its inception the Commonwealth Parliamentary Library was driven to development of a truly national collection. In 1907 the Joint Parliamentary Library Committee under the Chairmanship of the Speaker, Sir <a href="/wiki/Frederick_William_Holder" class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick William Holder">Frederick William Holder</a> defined the objective of the Commonwealth Parliamentary Library in the following words:
</p>
<blockquote><p>The Library Committee is keeping before it the ideal of building up, for the time when Parliament shall be established in the Federal Capital, a great Public Library on the lines of the world-famed Library of Congress at Washington; such a library, indeed, as shall be worthy of the Australian Nation; the home of the literature, not of a State, or of a period, but of the world, and of all time.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>
<p>The present library building was opened on 15 August 1968 by Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/John_Gorton" title="John Gorton">John Gorton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The building was designed by the architectural firm of <a href="/wiki/Bunning_and_Madden" title="Bunning and Madden">Bunning and Madden</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Australian_non-residential_architectural_styles#Stripped_Classical" title="Australian non-residential architectural styles">Late Twentieth Century Stripped Classical</a> style. The foyer is decorated in marble, with stained-glass windows by <a href="/wiki/Leonard_French" title="Leonard French">Leonard French</a> and three tapestries by <a href="/wiki/Mathieu_Mat%C3%A9got" title="Mathieu Matégot">Mathieu Matégot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The building was listed on the Australian <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Heritage_List" title="Commonwealth Heritage List">Commonwealth Heritage List</a> on 22 June 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-AHD_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AHD-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Collections">Collections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Library_of_Australia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Collections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012–13 the Library collection comprised 6,496,772 items, with an estimated additional 2,325,900 items held in the manuscripts collection.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Library's collections of <a href="/wiki/Australiana" title="Australiana">Australiana</a> have developed into the nation's single most important resource of materials recording the Australian cultural heritage. <a href="/wiki/Category:Australian_writers" title="Category:Australian writers">Australian writers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Category:Australian_editors" title="Category:Australian editors">editors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Category:Australian_illustrators" title="Category:Australian illustrators">illustrators</a> are actively sought and well represented—whether published in Australia or overseas.
</p><p>The Library's collection includes all formats of material, from books, journals, websites and manuscripts to pictures, photographs, maps, music, oral history recordings, manuscript papers and ephemera.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Approximately 92.1% of the Library's collection has been catalogued<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and is discoverable through the online catalogue.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Library has <a href="/wiki/Digitizing" class="mw-redirect" title="Digitizing">digitized</a> over 174,000 items from its collection<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> (the 100,000th being "Feeding cotton seed to sheep, Bourke, New South Wales")<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and, where possible, delivers these directly across the Internet. The Library is a world leader in <a href="/wiki/Digital_preservation" title="Digital preservation">digital preservation</a> techniques,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and maintains an Internet-accessible archive of selected Australian websites called the <a href="/wiki/Pandora_Archive" title="Pandora Archive">Pandora Archive</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="Australian_.26_General_Collection"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Australian_&amp;_General_Collection">Australian &amp; General Collection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Library_of_Australia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Australian &amp; General Collection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Library collects material produced by Australians, for Australians or about the Australian experience in all formats—not just printed works—books, serials, newspapers, maps, posters, music and printed <a href="/wiki/Ephemera" title="Ephemera">ephemera</a>—but also online publications and unpublished material such as <a href="/wiki/Manuscript" title="Manuscript">manuscripts</a>, pictures and <a href="/wiki/Oral_history" title="Oral history">oral histories</a>. A core Australiana collection is that of <a href="/wiki/John_Alexander_Ferguson" title="John Alexander Ferguson">John A. Ferguson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>  The Library has particular collection strengths in the <a href="/wiki/Performing_art" class="mw-redirect" title="Performing art">performing arts</a>, including dance.
</p><p>The Library's considerable collections of general overseas and <a href="/wiki/Rare_book" class="mw-redirect" title="Rare book">rare book</a> materials, as well as world-class Asian and Pacific collections which augment the Australiana collections. The print collections are further supported by extensive <a href="/wiki/Microform" title="Microform">microform</a> holdings.
</p><p>The Library also maintains the National Reserve <a href="/wiki/Braille" title="Braille">Braille</a> Collection.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asian_Collections">Asian Collections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Library_of_Australia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Asian Collections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Library houses the largest and most actively developing <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">research</a> resource on <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> in Australia, and the largest Asian language collections in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_hemisphere" class="mw-redirect" title="Southern hemisphere">Southern hemisphere</a>, with over half a million volumes in the collection, as well as extensive <a href="/wiki/Online" class="mw-redirect" title="Online">online</a> and electronic resources. The Library collects resources about all Asian countries in <a href="/wiki/Western_world" title="Western world">Western</a> languages extensively, and resources in the following Asian languages: <a href="/wiki/Burmese_language" title="Burmese language">Burmese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Indonesian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khmer_language" title="Khmer language">Khmer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lao_language" title="Lao language">Lao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchu" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchu">Manchu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mongolian_language" title="Mongolian language">Mongolian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thai_language" title="Thai language">Thai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Languages_of_East_Timor" title="Languages of East Timor">Timorese</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_language" title="Vietnamese language">Vietnamese</a>.

<p>The National Library holds an extensive collection of pictures and manuscripts. The manuscript collection contains about 26 million separate items, covering in excess of 10,492 meters of shelf space (ACA Australian Archival Statistics, 1998). The collection relates predominantly to Australia, but there are also important holdings relating to <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> and the Pacific. The collection also holds a number of European and Asian manuscript collections or single items have been received as part of formed book collections.
</p><p>The Australian manuscript collections date from the period of maritime exploration and settlement in the 18th century until the present, with the greatest area of strength dating from the 1890s onwards. The collection includes a large number of outstanding single items, such as the 14th century <a href="/wiki/Chertsey" title="Chertsey">Chertsey</a> <a href="/wiki/Cartulary" title="Cartulary">Cartulary</a>, the journal of <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> on <a href="/wiki/HM_Bark_Endeavour" class="mw-redirect" title="HM Bark Endeavour">HM Bark Endeavour</a>, inscribed on the Memory of the World<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Register in 2001, the diaries of <a href="/wiki/Robert_O%27Hara_Burke" title="Robert O&#39;Hara Burke">Robert O'Hara Burke</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_John_Wills" title="William John Wills">William John Wills</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Burke_and_Wills_expedition" title="Burke and Wills expedition">Burke and Wills expedition</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Kingsford_Smith" title="Charles Kingsford Smith">Charles Kingsford Smith</a>'s and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Ulm" title="Charles Ulm">Charles Ulm</a>'s log of the Southern Cross.
</p><p>A wide range of individuals and families are represented in the collection, with special strength in the fields of politics, public administration, diplomacy, theatre, art, literature, the pastoral industry and religion. Examples are the papers of <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Deakin" title="Alfred Deakin">Alfred Deakin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sir_John_Latham" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir John Latham">Sir John Latham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sir_Keith_Murdoch" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Keith Murdoch">Sir Keith Murdoch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sir_Hans_Heysen" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Hans Heysen">Sir Hans Heysen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sir_John_Monash" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir John Monash">Sir John Monash</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vance_Palmer" title="Vance Palmer">Vance Palmer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nettie_Palmer" title="Nettie Palmer">Nettie Palmer</a>, <a href="/wiki/A.D._Hope" class="mw-redirect" title="A.D. Hope">A.D. Hope</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manning_Clark" title="Manning Clark">Manning Clark</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Williamson" title="David Williamson">David Williamson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billy_Hughes" title="Billy Hughes">W.M. Hughes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sir_Robert_Menzies" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Robert Menzies">Sir Robert Menzies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sir_William_McMahon" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir William McMahon">Sir William McMahon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lord_Casey" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Casey">Lord Casey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Dutton" title="Geoffrey Dutton">Geoffrey Dutton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sculthorpe" title="Peter Sculthorpe">Peter Sculthorpe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daisy_Bates_(Australia)" class="mw-redirect" title="Daisy Bates (Australia)">Daisy Bates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jessie_Street" title="Jessie Street">Jessie Street</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Mabo" title="Eddie Mabo">Eddie Mabo</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">James Cook</a> both of whose papers were inscribed on <a href="/wiki/UNESCO" title="UNESCO">UNESCO's</a> <a href="/wiki/Memory_of_the_World_Programme" title="Memory of the World Programme">Memory of the World Programme</a> Register in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-mowmabo_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mowmabo-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mowcook_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mowcook-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Library has also acquired the records of many national non-governmental organisations. They include the records of the Federal Secretariats of the Liberal party, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Labor_Party" title="Australian Labor Party">A.L.P</a>, the Democrats, the <a href="/wiki/Returned_and_Services_League_of_Australia" title="Returned and Services League of Australia">R.S.L.</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Inland_Mission" title="Australian Inland Mission">Australian Inland Mission</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Union_of_Students" title="Australian Union of Students">Australian Union of Students</a>, The <a href="/wiki/Australian_Ballet" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian Ballet">Australian Ballet</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Elizabethan_Theatre_Trust" title="Australian Elizabethan Theatre Trust">Australian Elizabethan Theatre Trust</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Australian_Institute_of_Urban_Studies&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Australian Institute of Urban Studies (page does not exist)">Australian Institute of Urban Studies</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Australian_Industries_Protection_League&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Australian Industries Protection League (page does not exist)">Australian Industries Protection League</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Conservation_Foundation" title="Australian Conservation Foundation">Australian Conservation Foundation</a>, and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Australian_Council_of_National_Trusts&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Australian Council of National Trusts (page does not exist)">Australian Council of National Trusts</a>. Finally, the Library holds about 37,000 reels of <a href="/wiki/Microfilm" class="mw-redirect" title="Microfilm">microfilm</a> of manuscripts and archival records, mostly acquired overseas and predominantly of Australian and Pacific interest.
</p><p>The National Library's Pictures collection focuses on Australian people, places and events, from European exploration of the South Pacific to contemporary events. Art works and photographs are acquired primarily for their informational value, and for their importance as historical documents.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Media represented in the collection include photographs, drawings, watercolours, oils, lithographs, engravings, etchings and sculpture/busts.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reading_rooms">Reading rooms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Library_of_Australia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reading rooms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The large National Library building is home to various reading rooms and collections. On the ground floor is the Main Reading Room — this is where the bulk of the Library's Internet access terminals are located, and where wireless internet access is available. Services are also delivered on-site from the Newspaper &amp; Family History zone on the ground floor, Special Collections Reading Room on the 1st floor, and Asian Collections on level 3.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Library_of_Australia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The National Library of Australia provides a national leadership role in developing and managing collaborative online services with the Australian library community, making it easier for users to find and access information resources at the national level.
</p>
<ul><li>Australian National Bibliographic Database<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> (ANBD) and offers free access through the <b>Libraries Australia</b><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> subscription based service which is also operated by the NLA. It is used for reference, collection development, cataloguing and interlibrary lending.</li>
<li>Prepublication Data Service,<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/ISSN" class="mw-redirect" title="ISSN">ISSNs</a> and <a href="/wiki/ISMN" class="mw-redirect" title="ISMN">ISMNs</a> for Australian publishers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/PANDORA" class="mw-redirect" title="PANDORA">PANDORA</a>, Australia's Web Archive. A collection of Australian online publications, established initially by the National Library of Australia in 1996, and now built in collaboration with nine other Australian libraries and cultural collecting organisations.</li>
<li>Trove, online library database aggregator</li></ul>
<p>The National Library of Australia maintains a catalogue of the resources in its own collection which are available to the general public.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Trove">Trove</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Library_of_Australia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Trove">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Trove_(NLA_website)_logo.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d8/Trove_%28NLA_website%29_logo.svg/220px-Trove_%28NLA_website%29_logo.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="51" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d8/Trove_%28NLA_website%29_logo.svg/330px-Trove_%28NLA_website%29_logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d8/Trove_%28NLA_website%29_logo.svg/440px-Trove_%28NLA_website%29_logo.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="654" data-file-height="153" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Trove_(NLA_website)_logo.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Trove logo</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Trove" title="Trove">Trove</a></div>
<p>Trove is an online library database aggregator, a centralised national service built with the collaboration of major libraries of Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Trove's most well known feature is the digitised collection of Australian newspapers. By June 2013 over 10 million digitised pages, or 100 million articles were accessible through Trove.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>  Many of the NLA's resource discovery services have been fully integrated with Trove—meaning that several (such as "music Australia", "pictures Australia" and "Australian newspapers") are now accessible only through the site. Others (such as PANDORA and the ANBD) use Trove as their primary means of public access. The service is able to locate resources about Australia and Australians, which reaches many locations otherwise unavailable to external search engines.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
