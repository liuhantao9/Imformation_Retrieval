<p>The <b>Surinamese dollar</b> (<a href="/wiki/ISO_4217" title="ISO 4217">ISO 4217</a> code <i>SRD</i>) has been the <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">currency</a> of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> since 2004. It is normally abbreviated with the <a href="/wiki/Dollar_sign" title="Dollar sign">dollar sign</a> <i>$</i>, or alternatively <i>Sr$</i> to distinguish it from other <a href="/wiki/Dollar" title="Dollar">dollar</a>-denominated currencies. It is divided into 100 <a href="/wiki/Cent_(currency)" title="Cent (currency)">cents</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinamese_dollar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The dollar replaced the <a href="/wiki/Surinamese_guilder" title="Surinamese guilder">Surinamese guilder</a> on 1 January 2004, with one dollar equal to 1,000 guilders<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>. Initially, only coins were available, with banknotes delayed until mid-February, reportedly due to a problem at the printer, the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Bank_Note_Company" title="Canadian Bank Note Company">Canadian Bank Note Company</a>.
</p><p>The old coins denominated in cents (i.e. &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>100</sub></span> guilder) were declared to be worth their face value in the new cents, negating the necessity of producing new coins. Thus, for example, an old 25-cent coin, previously worth &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>4</sub></span> guilder, was now worth &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>4</sub></span> dollar (equivalent to 250 guilders). The rebasing of coins explicitly did not apply to commemorative coins.
</p><p>Amendment 121 of <a href="/wiki/ISO_4217" title="ISO 4217">ISO 4217</a> gave the currency the code SRD replacing the Suriname guilder (SRG).
</p><p>The people of Suriname often refer to their currency as SRD to differentiate it from the US dollar, which is also used to quote prices for electronic goods, household furnishings and appliances, and automobiles.
</p><p>In January 2011, the SRD was fixed at 1 <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a> = 3.25 SRD.
</p><p>In November 2015, this was changed to a fixed rate of <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a> = 4.0 SRD and in April 2016 this was further changed.
</p><p>As of 23&#160;October&#160;2018<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Surinamese_dollar&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, 1 euro was equivalent to 8.54 Surinamese dollars.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coins">Coins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinamese_dollar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Coins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Coins in denominations of 1, 5, 10, 25, 100 and 250 cents from the previous currency are in circulation.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Banknotes">Banknotes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinamese_dollar&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Banknotes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Surinamese dollar replaced the Surinamese guilder on 1 January 2004, with one dollar equal to 1,000 guilders, prompting the issuance of notes denominated in the new currency. On the notes, the currency is expressed in the singular, as is the Dutch custom.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
