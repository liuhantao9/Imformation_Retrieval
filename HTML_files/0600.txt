<p>The <b>A629 road</b> is an inter-Yorkshire road that runs from <a href="/wiki/Skipton" title="Skipton">Skipton</a> to <a href="/wiki/Rotherham" title="Rotherham">Rotherham</a> through <a href="/wiki/Keighley" title="Keighley">Keighley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chapeltown,_South_Yorkshire" title="Chapeltown, South Yorkshire">Chapeltown</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, England. The road runs through North, West and South Yorkshire, but before 1974, the entire length of the road was wholly within the boundaries of the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>. It is designated as a primary route through most of its length.
</p><p>The road is part of the intended Doncaster to Kendal Trunk Route which was designated as a trunk road in 1946.
</p><p>Parts of the road are designated as a High Load Route by the UK Government although the section through <a href="/w/index.php?title=Burncross&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Burncross (page does not exist)">Burncross</a> to Chapeltown is designated as B road (B6546) because of a weight restriction.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Route">Route</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A629_road&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Route">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The very northern section of the route partially runs over the route of the <a href="/wiki/Keighley_and_Kendal_Turnpike" title="Keighley and Kendal Turnpike">Keighley and Kendal Turnpike</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Most parts have been bypassed such as the section between Snaygill to the south of Skipton onto the A65/A59 roundabout which opened in 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Southern parts of the route were formerly the Huddersfield to Penistone Turnpike and the Halifax to Sheffield Turnpike.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Along with the A65 and the A650, the A629 is part of the intended <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kendal" title="Kendal">Kendal</a> trunk route<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;note 1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> which was meant to provide a through route between the two towns and cut across the <a href="/wiki/Aire_Gap" title="Aire Gap">Aire Gap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> As the section of the A629 between Skipton and Keighley is designated as a trunk route, it is maintained by the <a href="/wiki/Highways_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Highways Agency">Highways Agency</a> as opposed to the local authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Additionally; the section between Kildwick roundabout and Keighley is designated as a Highways Agency <i>High Route</i> (IE one that high loads should take to avoid bridges).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From the north the route starts at the A65/A59 roundabout to the northeast of Skipton and heads southwards through the Aire Valley bypassing Skipton, <a href="/wiki/Farnhill" title="Farnhill">Farnhill</a> and Kildwick. Just before the Kildwick level crossing, the road takes a bypass around <a href="/wiki/Eastburn,_West_Yorkshire" title="Eastburn, West Yorkshire">Eastburn</a> and Steeton on a dual carriageway that was opened in 1989. At Keighley, the A629 diverts through the town whilst Aire Valley traffic takes the A650. Through and beyond Keighley to Halifax, the road is a single carriageway.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After going through Keighley town centre, the road heads due south but up onto the high ground through Ingrow, Cross Roads and Denholme. After Denholme the road crosses over into <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a> and goes through Illingworth,  Ovenden and Halifax.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The section of road between Halifax and Huddersfield is the main link between the two towns<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and incorporates a bad junction with the A6026 in <a href="/wiki/Salterhebble" title="Salterhebble">Salterhebble</a> and the Elland bypass that leads up towards a junction with the <a href="/wiki/M62_Motorway" class="mw-redirect" title="M62 Motorway">M62 Motorway</a>. The bypass at Elland is being  widened to four lanes and is expected to reduce journey times between the M62 and Halifax by 30% when opened in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Through Huddersfield the road is in a multiplex with the ring road and then heads off south east past Shepley and Penistone.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The route is broken on Burncross road through Chapeltown due to a 7.5-tonne (8.3-ton) weight restriction on the road,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> but was traditionally part of the A629.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Heavy goods traffic is expected to divert onto the <a href="/wiki/A61_road" title="A61 road">A61</a> north, use the <a href="/wiki/M1_motorway" title="M1 motorway">M1 motorway</a> south and then pick up the A629 at junction 35 for Chapeltown to access the rest of the route towards Rotherham.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents_and_safety">Incidents and safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A629_road&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Incidents and safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 2011, a section of the southbound Elland bypass was closed after boulders, remnants of a quarry that the bypass was built next to, came crashing down into the road.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the Eurorap safety rating, the A629 is a Medium High-Risk Road between Skipton and Huddersfield and a Medium-Risk Road between Huddersfield and Rotherham.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2017, a man was shot by police on the side of the A629 southbound just by junction 24 of the M62 Motorway.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The police announced it was a pre-planned operation as they had credible information that the man was carrying a gun.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Congestion">Congestion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A629_road&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Congestion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As the road passes through the major conurbations of Keighley, Halifax, Huddersfield and Rotherham, it is prone to some congestion especially during the morning and evening peak periods. The junction of the road with the A6026 and the B6112 in Saltherhebble, regularly sees queuing traffic for over a 1-mile (1.6&#160;km) southbound that takes between 12 and 15 minutes to get through.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kirklees Council have previously acknowledged that the A629 is one of the busiest roads in Kirklees.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
