<p><b>Lockwood</b> is an area of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Metropolitan Borough of Kirklees</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. It is 0.9 miles (1.4&#160;km) to the southwest of Huddersfield Town Centre, to the west of the <a href="/wiki/River_Holme" title="River Holme">River Holme</a>.
</p><p>Lockwood was originally called <i>North Crosland</i> and part of the Crosland family estate. However, it was taken over by the Lockwood family after a series of disputes between the dynasties. Parts of the area are still known as North Crosland.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lockwood_railway_station" title="Lockwood railway station">Lockwood railway station</a> is on the <a href="/wiki/Penistone_Line" title="Penistone Line">Penistone Line</a> between Huddersfield and Sheffield. It is situated in Swan Lane, just before the Grade II-listed, 32-arched Lockwood viaduct, which spans the valley and connects the line to <a href="/wiki/Berry_Brow" title="Berry Brow">Berry Brow</a>. Prior to the mid-1970s it had its own extensive goods yard, coal yard, sidings and <a href="/wiki/Station_master" title="Station master">station master</a>'s house.
</p><p>The goods yards were used to service and supply raw materials to the former engineering works of <a href="/wiki/David_Brown_Ltd." title="David Brown Ltd.">David Brown Ltd</a>. This particular division of David Brown's produced gearboxes for industrial machinery and hydraulic drives and some military armoured vehicles. The gear box that turns the top of the <a href="/wiki/Post_Office_Tower" class="mw-redirect" title="Post Office Tower">Post Office Tower</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> was designed and built there,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> whilst the electric motors that drives the gearing were manufactured by Brook (Electric) Motors, based in <a href="/wiki/Brockholes" title="Brockholes">Brockholes</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>A branch line from the station, just before the railway viaduct, went via <a href="/wiki/Armitage_Bridge" title="Armitage Bridge">Armitage Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Netherton,_Kirklees" title="Netherton, Kirklees">Netherton</a> to <a href="/wiki/Meltham" title="Meltham">Meltham</a>. This passed Meltham Mills, where David Brown Ltd. had a <a href="/wiki/Tractor" title="Tractor">tractor</a> manufacturing facility.  The former station master's house is now a private residence and the goods yards are part of a <a href="/wiki/Timber" class="mw-redirect" title="Timber">timber</a> <a href="/wiki/Merchant" title="Merchant">merchant</a>'s.
</p><p>Lockwood is the manufacturing base of locally renowned Dixon's Milk Ices, housed in the former Lockwood Town Hall.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Rugby_Union_Football_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Rugby Union Football Club">Huddersfield Rugby Union Football Club</a>, at Lockwood Park, is situated below and to both sides of the railway viaduct, in the former 'Bentley &amp; Shaws' Brewery.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lockwood_Brewery">Lockwood Brewery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lockwood,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lockwood Brewery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Timothy Bentley, founder of 'Bentley &amp; Shaws' Lockwood Brewery, is recognised as the inventor of the Stone Square system of brewing beer.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This method allowed high levels of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">carbon dioxide</a> to remain in the beer during fermentation, helping to give it a unique flavour and smoothness when served.  The stone used in the brewery came from <a href="/wiki/Quarry" title="Quarry">stone quarries</a> in <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a>.
</p><p>The brewery was taken over by <a href="/wiki/Bass_(beer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Bass (beer)">Bass Charrington</a> before it closed down in the late 1960s.
</p>
