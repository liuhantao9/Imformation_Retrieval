<p>The <b>American Campaign Medal</b> is a <a href="/wiki/United_States_service_medals_of_the_World_Wars" title="United States service medals of the World Wars">military award</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">United States Armed Forces</a> which was first created on November 6, 1942 by <a href="/wiki/Executive_order_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Executive order (United States)">Executive Order</a> <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Executive_Order_9265" class="extiw" title="s:Executive Order 9265">9265</a> issued by President <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-American_Campaign_Medal_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-American_Campaign_Medal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tioh_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tioh-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The medal was intended to recognize those military members who had performed military service in the <a href="/wiki/American_Theater_of_World_War_II" class="mw-redirect" title="American Theater of World War II">American Theater of Operations</a> during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tioh_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tioh-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  A similar medal, known as the <a href="/wiki/American_Defense_Service_Medal" title="American Defense Service Medal">American Defense Service Medal</a> was awarded for active duty service before the United States entry into World War II.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Campaign_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The American Campaign Medal was established per Executive Order 9265,6 November 1942, by President Franklin D. Roosevelt and announced in War Department Bulletin 56, 1942. The criteria were initially announced in Department of the Army (DA) Circular 1, dated 1 January 1943, so that the ribbon could be authorized prior to design of the medal. The criteria for the medal were announced in DA Circular 84, dated 25 March 1948 and subsequently published in Army Regulation 600-65, dated 22 September 1948. The American Campaign Medal was issued as a <a href="/wiki/Service_ribbon" title="Service ribbon">service ribbon</a> only during the Second World War, and was not issued as a full-sized medal until 1947.<sup id="cite_ref-tioh_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tioh-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first recipient of the American Campaign Medal was <a href="/wiki/General_of_the_Army_(United_States)" title="General of the Army (United States)">General of the Army</a> <a href="/wiki/George_Marshall" title="George Marshall">George C. Marshall, Jr.</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tioh_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tioh-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria">Criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Campaign_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The requirements for the American Campaign Medal were for service within the American Theater between 7 December
1941 and 2 March 1946 under any of the following conditions:<sup id="cite_ref-American_Campaign_Medal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-American_Campaign_Medal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ol><li>On permanent assignment outside the continental limits of the United States.</li>
<li>Permanently assigned as a member of a crew of a vessel sailing ocean waters for a period of 30 consecutive days or 60 nonconsecutive days.</li>
<li>Outside the continental limits of the United States in a passenger status or on temporary duty for 30 consecutive days or 60 nonconsecutive days.</li>
<li>In active combat against the enemy and was awarded a combat decoration or furnished a certificate by the commanding general of a corps, higher unit, or independent force that the Soldier actually participated in combat.</li>
<li>Within the continental limits of the United States for an aggregate period of 1 year.</li></ol>
<p>The boundaries of American Theater are as follows:
The eastern boundary is located from the North Pole, south along the 75th meridian west longitude to the 77th
parallel north latitude, thence southeast through Davis Strait to the intersection of the 40th parallel north latitude and
the 35th meridian west longitude, thence south along the meridian to the 10th parallel north latitude, thence southeast
to the intersection of the Equator and the 20th meridian west longitude, thence south along the 20th meridian west
longitude to the South Pole.
</p><p>The western boundary is located from the North Pole, south along the 141st meridian west longitude to the east
boundary of Alaska, thence south and southeast along the Alaska boundary to the Pacific Ocean, thence south along the
130th meridian to its intersection with the 30th parallel north latitude, thence southeast to the intersection of the
Equator and the 100th meridian west longitude, thence south to the South Pole.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearance">Appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Campaign_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="wikitable floatright" border="0">

<p>The medal, made of bronze, is <span class="frac nowrap">1<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>4</sub></span> inches (32&#160;mm) inches wide.  The <a href="/wiki/Obverse_and_reverse" title="Obverse and reverse">obverse</a> was designed by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Hudson_Jones" title="Thomas Hudson Jones">Thomas Hudson Jones</a>. It shows a Navy <a href="/wiki/Cruiser" title="Cruiser">cruiser</a> <a href="/wiki/Underway" title="Underway">underway</a> with a <a href="/wiki/Consolidated_B-24_Liberator" title="Consolidated B-24 Liberator">B-24 Liberator bomber</a> flying overhead.  In the foreground is a sinking enemy submarine, and in the background is the skyline of a city.  At the top of the medal are the words <i>AMERICAN CAMPAIGN</i>.  The reverse of the medal, designed by <a href="/wiki/Adolph_Alexander_Weinman" title="Adolph Alexander Weinman">Adolph Alexander Weinman</a>, is the same design as used on the reverse of both the <a href="/wiki/Asiatic-Pacific_Campaign_Medal" class="mw-redirect" title="Asiatic-Pacific Campaign Medal">Asiatic-Pacific Campaign Medal</a> and the <a href="/wiki/European-African-Middle_Eastern_Campaign_Medal" class="mw-redirect" title="European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal">European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal</a>.  It depicts an American <a href="/wiki/Bald_eagle" title="Bald eagle">bald eagle</a> between the dates <i>1941 - 1945</i> and the words <i>UNITED STATES OF AMERICA</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-tioh_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tioh-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The ribbon is <span class="frac nowrap">1<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>3</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span> inches (35&#160;mm) inches wide in oriental blue in the center is a <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>8</sub></span> inch (3.2&#160;mm) inch center stripe divided into thirds of old glory blue, white, and scarlet.  Between the center and the edges are stripes of <span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>16</sub></span> inch (1.6&#160;mm) inch in white, black, scarlet and white.  
The blue color represents the Americas; the central blue, white and red stripes, taken from the <a href="/wiki/American_Defense_Service_Medal" title="American Defense Service Medal">American Defense Service Medal</a> ribbon, represents the continuance of American defense after Pearl Harbor. The white and black stripes represent the German part of the conflict on the Atlantic Coast, while the red and white stripes are for the Japanese colors and refer to that part of the conflict on the Pacific Coast.<sup id="cite_ref-tioh_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tioh-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ribbon_devices">Ribbon devices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Campaign_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Ribbon devices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>3/16 inch <a href="/wiki/Service_stars" class="mw-redirect" title="Service stars">service stars</a> were authorized to service members who participated in combat with <a href="/wiki/Axis_powers" title="Axis powers">Axis</a> forces within the American Theater. This primarily applied to those service members whose units participated in anti-<a href="/wiki/U-Boat" class="mw-redirect" title="U-Boat">U-Boat</a> patrols (<a href="/wiki/Anti-submarine_warfare" title="Anti-submarine warfare">Anti-submarine warfare</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Atlantic" title="Battle of the Atlantic">Atlantic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaigns">Campaigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Campaign_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Campaigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Navy_campaigns">Navy campaigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Campaign_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Navy campaigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Participation in these escort, antisubmarine, armed guard, and special operations entitle recipients to one campaign star for each participation:<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Army_campaigns">Army campaigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Campaign_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Army campaigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A bronze service star is authorized for participation in the antisubmarine campaign.  To qualify individuals must have been assigned to or attached to, and present for duty with, a unit with antisubmarine campaign credit.<sup id="cite_ref-tioh_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tioh-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
