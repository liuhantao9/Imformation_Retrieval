<p><b>Darlington railway station</b> is on the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Main_Line" title="East Coast Main Line">East Coast Main Line</a> in the United Kingdom, serving the town of <a href="/wiki/Darlington" title="Darlington">Darlington</a>, County Durham. It is 232&#160;miles 50&#160;<a href="/wiki/Chain_(unit)" title="Chain (unit)">chains</a> (374.37&#160;km) north of <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a> and on the main line it is situated between <a href="/wiki/Northallerton_railway_station" title="Northallerton railway station">Northallerton</a> to the south and <a href="/wiki/Durham_railway_station" title="Durham railway station">Durham</a> to the north. Its three-letter station code is DAR.
</p><p>The station is well served, since it is an important stop for main line services, with trains being operated by <a href="/wiki/London_North_Eastern_Railway" title="London North Eastern Railway">London North Eastern Railway</a>, <a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a> and <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a>, and it is the interchange for <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> services to Bishop Auckland, <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_railway_station" title="Middlesbrough railway station">Middlesbrough</a> and Saltburn.
Darlington is the location of the first commercial steam railway: the <a href="/wiki/Stockton_and_Darlington_Railway" title="Stockton and Darlington Railway">Stockton and Darlington Railway</a>. The station building is a Grade II* <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed</a><sup id="cite_ref-Listing_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Listing-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Victorian_architecture" title="Victorian architecture">Victorian</a> structure and winner of the "Large Station of the Year" award in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darlington_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first railway to pass through the area now occupied by the station was built by the <a href="/wiki/Stockton_and_Darlington_Railway" title="Stockton and Darlington Railway">Stockton and Darlington Railway</a>, who opened their mineral branch from <i>Albert Hill Junction</i> on their main line to <a href="/wiki/Croft-on-Tees" title="Croft-on-Tees">Croft-on-Tees</a> on 27 October 1829. This branch line was subsequently purchased by the <a href="/wiki/Great_North_of_England_Railway" title="Great North of England Railway">Great North of England Railway</a> a decade later to incorporate into their new main line from <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York</a> which reached the town on 30 March 1841. A separate company, the <i>Newcastle &amp; Darlington Junction Railway</i> continued the new main line northwards towards <a href="/wiki/Ferryhill" title="Ferryhill">Ferryhill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle</a>, opening its route three years later on 19 June 1844.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This crossed the S&amp;D at <i>Parkgate Junction</i> by means of a <a href="/wiki/Flat_crossings" class="mw-redirect" title="Flat crossings">flat crossing</a> which would in future years become something of an operational headache for the <a href="/wiki/North_Eastern_Railway_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="North Eastern Railway (UK)">North Eastern Railway</a> and <a href="/wiki/London_and_North_Eastern_Railway" title="London and North Eastern Railway">LNER</a>. The original <i>Bank Top</i> station where the two routes met was a modest affair, which was rebuilt in 1860 to accommodate the expanding levels of traffic on the main line. By the mid-1880s even this replacement structure was deemed inadequate and so the NER embarked on a major upgrade to facilities in the area.  This included an ornate new station with an impressive three-span overall roof on the Bank Top site, new sidings and goods lines alongside it and a new connecting line from the south end of the station (<i>Polam Junction</i>) to meet the original S&amp;D line towards <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a> at <i>Oak Tree Junction</i> near <a href="/wiki/Dinsdale_railway_station" title="Dinsdale railway station">Dinsdale</a>. These improvements were completed on 1 July 1887, when the old route west of Oak Tree closed to passengers (although it remained in use for freight until 1967).  
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Darlington_Bank_Top_station_geograph-2259696-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Darlington_Bank_Top_station_geograph-2259696-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg/220px-Darlington_Bank_Top_station_geograph-2259696-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="130" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Darlington_Bank_Top_station_geograph-2259696-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg/330px-Darlington_Bank_Top_station_geograph-2259696-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Darlington_Bank_Top_station_geograph-2259696-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg/440px-Darlington_Bank_Top_station_geograph-2259696-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="606" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Darlington_Bank_Top_station_geograph-2259696-by-Ben-Brooksbank.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/LNER_Peppercorn_Class_A1#List_of_original_locomotives" title="LNER Peppercorn Class A1">60129 <i>Guy Mannering</i></a> entering the station in 1961</div></div></div>
<p>The new station, with its broad <a href="/wiki/Island_platform" title="Island platform">island platform</a> was designed by T.E. Harrison chief engineer, and William Bell, the architect of the <a href="/wiki/North_Eastern_Railway_(United_Kingdom)" title="North Eastern Railway (United Kingdom)">North Eastern Railway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and cost £81,000 (equivalent to £8,850,000&#32;in 2018)<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-UK_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UK-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> to construct<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and soon became a busy interchange on the main East Coast route, thanks to its rail links to <a href="/wiki/Richmond_railway_station_(North_Yorkshire)" title="Richmond railway station (North Yorkshire)">Richmond</a> (opened in 1846), <a href="/wiki/Barnard_Castle_railway_station" title="Barnard Castle railway station">Barnard Castle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penrith_railway_station" title="Penrith railway station">Penrith</a> (1862/5) and the <a href="/wiki/Tees_Valley_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Tees Valley Line">Tees Valley Line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Auckland_railway_station" title="Bishop Auckland railway station">Bishop Auckland</a> (1842) and <a href="/wiki/Saltburn_railway_station" title="Saltburn railway station">Saltburn</a> (1861).
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Darlington_Bank_Top_railway_station_geograph-2135041.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Darlington_Bank_Top_railway_station_geograph-2135041.jpg/220px-Darlington_Bank_Top_railway_station_geograph-2135041.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="134" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Darlington_Bank_Top_railway_station_geograph-2135041.jpg/330px-Darlington_Bank_Top_railway_station_geograph-2135041.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Darlington_Bank_Top_railway_station_geograph-2135041.jpg/440px-Darlington_Bank_Top_railway_station_geograph-2135041.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="624" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Darlington_Bank_Top_railway_station_geograph-2135041.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Down freight passing the station in 1961</div></div></div><p>The lines to Penrith (closed in 1962), Barnard Castle (1964)<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and Richmond (1969) have now gone (along with the bays at the northern end of the station, now used for car parking), but the main line (electrified in 1991) and the Tees Valley route remain busy.  It is also still possible to travel to <a href="/wiki/Catterick_Garrison" title="Catterick Garrison">Catterick Garrison</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_North_Yorkshire" title="Richmond, North Yorkshire">Richmond</a> from here, by means of the <a href="/wiki/Arriva_North_East" title="Arriva North East">Arriva North East</a>-operated X26 and X27 buses (which have through <a href="/wiki/National_Rail" title="National Rail">National Rail</a> ticketing arrangements). The same company also operated the <i>Sky Express</i> bus service to <a href="/wiki/Durham_Tees_Valley_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Durham Tees Valley Airport">Durham Tees Valley Airport</a> from the station, but this was withdrawn in January 2009 due to declining demand.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 1980s a replica set of Darlington Railway Station was built for an episode of Noel's Saturday Roadshow for the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darlington_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>On 16 November 1910, an express freight train overran signals and was involved in a rear-end collision with another freight train.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 27 June 1928, <a href="/wiki/Darlington_rail_crash" title="Darlington rail crash">a parcels train and an excursion train were involved in a head-on collision</a>. Twenty five people were killed and 45 were injured.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 11 December 1968, a Newcastle to Kings Cross express train was derailed at the south end of the station after passing a signal at danger.  No-one was hurt.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 16 February 1977, an express passenger train hauled by <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_55" title="British Rail Class 55">Class 55 locomotive</a> 55 008 collided with an empty stock train after failing to stop at Darlington. The guard of the express was slightly injured. The cause of the accident was that the brakes on the carriages had become isolated whilst the train was moving in a freak event. The train had struck an object on the track, which had caused a traction motor cover to come lose. This struck the handle of the brake isolating cock, closing it and thus separating the brakes between the locomotive and train. Following the collision, the train was diverted onto the <a href="/wiki/Tees_Valley_line" title="Tees Valley line">Tees Valley line</a>, where it was brought to a halt by the operation of the <a href="/wiki/Emergency_brake_(train)#United_Kingdom" title="Emergency brake (train)">communication cord</a> in one of the carriages.<sup id="cite_ref-Darlington77_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Darlington77-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 3 October 2009, a <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_142" title="British Rail Class 142">Class 142</a> unit, operated by <a href="/wiki/Northern_Rail_(Serco-Abellio)" title="Northern Rail (Serco-Abellio)">Northern Rail</a>, hit the rear end of a departing <a href="/wiki/National_Express_East_Coast" title="National Express East Coast">National Express East Coast</a> service. Three passengers from the Northern Rail train were taken to hospital with minor injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Exeter_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Exeter-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facilities">Facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darlington_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As noted previous, the station is fully staffed; the ticket office is open throughout the week (06:00-20:00/21:00 weekdays, 06:30-19:45 Saturdays, 07:45-20:00 Sundays). There is a waiting room and a first class Lounge on the platform, with the lounge open between 06:00 and 20:00 each day (except Sundays, when it opens at 08:00).  Self-service ticket machines are also provided for use outside the opening hours for the booking office and for collecting pre-paid tickets.  Various retail outlets are located in the main buildings, including a coffee shop, grocers and newsagents.  Vending machines, toilets, a photo booth, payphone and cash machines are also provided.  Train running information is offered via digital CIS displays, announcements and timetable posters.  Step-free access to all platforms is via ramps from the subway linking the platforms with the main entrance and car park.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darlington_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Darlington is well served by trains on the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Main_Line" title="East Coast Main Line">East Coast Main Line</a>, with regular trains southbound to <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a> via <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York</a> and northbound to <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_railway_station" title="Newcastle railway station">Newcastle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_Waverley_railway_station" title="Edinburgh Waverley railway station">Edinburgh Waverley</a> operated by <a href="/wiki/London_North_Eastern_Railway" title="London North Eastern Railway">London North Eastern Railway</a>. Two trains per hour run south to London and north to Newcastle for much of the day with hourly services to Edinburgh Waverley. There are also several daily services to <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen_railway_station" title="Aberdeen railway station">Aberdeen</a> and also daily direct services to <a href="/wiki/Stirling_railway_station,_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Stirling railway station, Scotland">Stirling</a> (two) and <a href="/wiki/Inverness_railway_station" title="Inverness railway station">Inverness</a> (one).<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> 
Due to the introduction of the new ECML timetable on 22 May 2011, LNER only now provide one daily direct service each way between <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_Central_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Glasgow Central railway station">Glasgow Central</a> which calls at Darlington. The northbound service to Glasgow departs Darlington at 18:09 and the southbound service from Glasgow arrives into Darlington at 10:00.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a> services between Edinburgh, Newcastle and <a href="/wiki/Birmingham_New_Street_railway_station" title="Birmingham New Street railway station">Birmingham New Street</a> and beyond to (<a href="/wiki/Reading_railway_station" title="Reading railway station">Reading</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southampton_Central_railway_station" title="Southampton Central railway station">Southampton Central</a> and to <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Temple_Meads_railway_station" title="Bristol Temple Meads railway station">Bristol Temple Meads</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exeter_St_Davids_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Exeter St Davids railway station">Exeter St Davids</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_railway_station" title="Plymouth railway station">Plymouth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penzance_railway_station" title="Penzance railway station">Penzance</a>) also call here twice each hour, along with <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> trains between Newcastle and <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Lime_Street" class="mw-redirect" title="Liverpool Lime Street">Liverpool Lime Street</a> or <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Airport railway station">Manchester Airport</a> via York, <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Victoria">Manchester Victoria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>  The Liverpool service was introduced at the May 2014 timetable change in place of the former route to/from Manchester Airport, but since the May 2018 timetable change trains now run to both destinations from here.  Certain CrossCountry trains extend beyond Edinburgh to Glasgow Central, Dundee or Aberdeen.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> run their Tees Valley line trains twice hourly to <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_railway_station" title="Middlesbrough railway station">Middlesbrough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Redcar_Central_railway_station" title="Redcar Central railway station">Redcar</a> and Saltburn (hourly on Sundays), whilst the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Auckland_railway_station" title="Bishop Auckland railway station">Bishop Auckland</a> branch has a service every hour (including Sundays).<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The company also operates two Sundays-only direct trains to/from Stockton and <a href="/wiki/Hartlepool_railway_station" title="Hartlepool railway station">Hartlepool</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Platforms">Platforms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darlington_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Platforms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Darlington_railway_station.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Darlington_railway_station.JPG/220px-Darlington_railway_station.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Darlington_railway_station.JPG/330px-Darlington_railway_station.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Darlington_railway_station.JPG/440px-Darlington_railway_station.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4176" data-file-height="3135" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Darlington_railway_station.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Platforms 1 and 2</div></div></div>
<p>Darlington railway station has five main platforms:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Platform 1:</b> This is the main southbound platform, with, in order of frequency, London North Eastern Railway services to York and London King's Cross, CrossCountry services to Reading and Southampton or Birmingham and Plymouth, via York and Leeds, TransPennine Express services to Manchester Piccadilly and Manchester Airport or Liverpool Lime Street, via York and Leeds, and Northern services to Saltburn via Middlesbrough, from Bishop Auckland.</li>
<li><b>Platforms 2 and 3:</b> These platforms are south-facing bays used exclusively by Northern services terminating at Darlington from Saltburn and Middlesbrough. Platform 2 is used most frequently.</li></ul>
<p>TransPennine Express trains also terminate in Platform 3 when there are delays in order to allow them to run their southbound services back on time.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Platform 4:</b> This is the main northbound platform, with, in order of frequency, London North Eastern Railway services to Newcastle, Edinburgh and Glasgow, CrossCountry services to Newcastle, Edinburgh and Glasgow, TransPennine Express services to Newcastle and Northern services to Bishop Auckland.</li>
<li><b>Platform 4a:</b> This is a southern extension of platform four catering for trains waiting at Darlington such that they can be bypassed by trains stopping at platform 4. It is the only platform that is not under the station roof. It is used predominantly by Northern services for Bishop Auckland.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future">Future</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darlington_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Future">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As part of the <a href="/wiki/Tees_Valley_Metro" title="Tees Valley Metro">Tees Valley Metro</a>, two new platforms were to be built on the eastern edge of the main station. There were to be a total of four trains per hour, to <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_railway_station" title="Middlesbrough railway station">Middlesbrough</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saltburn_railway_station" title="Saltburn railway station">Saltburn</a> via the <a href="/wiki/Tees_Valley_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Tees Valley Line">Tees Valley Line</a>, and trains would not have to cross the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Main_Line" title="East Coast Main Line">East Coast Main Line</a> when the new platforms would have been built. The Tees Valley Metro project was, however, cancelled.
</p><p>Services on the Bishop Auckland branch are also to be improved to hourly throughout the day, as part of the new Northern franchise (awarded to <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Rail_North" class="mw-redirect" title="Arriva Rail North">Arriva Rail North</a> in December 2015) from the December 2017 timetable change.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="High_Speed_2">High Speed 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Darlington_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: High Speed 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The new high speed rail project in the UK, <a href="/wiki/High_Speed_2" title="High Speed 2">High Speed 2</a>, is planned to run through Darlington once Phase 2b is complete and will run on the existing <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Main_Line" title="East Coast Main Line">East Coast Main Line</a> from <a href="/wiki/York_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="York Station">York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_Central_Station" class="mw-redirect" title="Newcastle Central Station">Newcastle</a>. Darlington Station will have two new platforms built for the HS2 trains on the Main Line, as the station is built just off the ECML to allow for freight services to pass through. 
</p><p>HS2 Phase 2b<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> is scheduled to start running in late 2033.
</p>
