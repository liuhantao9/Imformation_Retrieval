<p><b>Huddersfield railway station</b> serves the town of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.
</p><p>The station is managed by <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a>, which provides trains between <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> and <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a> in the <a href="/wiki/North_West_England" title="North West England">North West</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_upon_Tyne" title="Newcastle upon Tyne">Newcastle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a> in the <a href="/wiki/North_East_England" title="North East England">North East</a> and also to <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_North_Yorkshire" title="Scarborough, North Yorkshire">Scarborough</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Hull</a> via <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>. It is also served by local <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> trains on the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Line">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Penistone_Line" title="Penistone Line">Penistone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caldervale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Caldervale Line">Caldervale</a> lines, which between them provide service to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Westgate_railway_station" title="Wakefield Westgate railway station">Wakefield Westgate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Victoria railway station">Manchester Victoria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield railway station">Sheffield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brighouse_railway_station" title="Brighouse railway station">Brighouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax_railway_station_(West_Yorkshire)" class="mw-redirect" title="Halifax railway station (West Yorkshire)">Halifax</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange" title="Bradford Interchange">Bradford Interchange</a>.
</p><p>Huddersfield station is the second busiest station in West Yorkshire with Leeds being the first.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_station_building">The station building</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The station building">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield_railway_station_at_night.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Huddersfield_railway_station_at_night.jpg/220px-Huddersfield_railway_station_at_night.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Huddersfield_railway_station_at_night.jpg/330px-Huddersfield_railway_station_at_night.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Huddersfield_railway_station_at_night.jpg/440px-Huddersfield_railway_station_at_night.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield_railway_station_at_night.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Huddersfield railway station by night</div></div></div>
<p>Designed by the architect <a href="/wiki/James_Pigott_Pritchett" title="James Pigott Pritchett">James Pigott Pritchett</a> and built by the firm of Joseph Kaye in 1846&#8211;50<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> using the <a href="/wiki/Neoclassical_architecture" title="Neoclassical architecture">neo-classical style</a>, the station is well known in architectural circles for its classical-style <a href="/wiki/Facade" title="Facade">facade</a>, with a <a href="/wiki/Portico" title="Portico">portico</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Corinthian_order" title="Corinthian order">Corinthian order</a>, consisting of six columns in width and two in depth, which dominates St George's Square. It faces out towards Lion Buildings. It is a grade I listed building. The station frontage was described by <a href="/wiki/John_Betjeman" title="John Betjeman">John Betjeman</a> as the most splendid in England and by Sir <a href="/wiki/Nikolaus_Pevsner" title="Nikolaus Pevsner">Nikolaus Pevsner</a> as <i>one of the best early railway stations in England</i> and <i>the only important Victorian railway station [in the West Riding]</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Similarly, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Jenkins" title="Simon Jenkins">Simon Jenkins</a> reported it to be one of the best 100 stations in Britain.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two pubs are within the station frontage, to each side of the main entrance: <i>The Head Of Steam</i> and <i>The King's Head</i> (previously known as <i>The Station Tavern</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Both facilities are accessible from Platform 1. At the building's entrance, the booking office is to the left and to the right are the train timetables and a newsagent.  Platforms 4 to 8 are located via a lift or subway, accessed from Platform 1.  The public conveniences are located through this subway at the top of the steps to Platforms 4&#8211;8. The platforms are all covered by a large canopy. To the rear of the station are some <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_sidings" title="Huddersfield sidings">carriage sidings</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Freemasons%27_circular_regarding_the_laying_of_Huddersfield_Station_foundation_stone_-_1846.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Freemasons%27_circular_regarding_the_laying_of_Huddersfield_Station_foundation_stone_-_1846.jpg/220px-Freemasons%27_circular_regarding_the_laying_of_Huddersfield_Station_foundation_stone_-_1846.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="273" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Freemasons%27_circular_regarding_the_laying_of_Huddersfield_Station_foundation_stone_-_1846.jpg/330px-Freemasons%27_circular_regarding_the_laying_of_Huddersfield_Station_foundation_stone_-_1846.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Freemasons%27_circular_regarding_the_laying_of_Huddersfield_Station_foundation_stone_-_1846.jpg/440px-Freemasons%27_circular_regarding_the_laying_of_Huddersfield_Station_foundation_stone_-_1846.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1288" data-file-height="1600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Freemasons%27_circular_regarding_the_laying_of_Huddersfield_Station_foundation_stone_-_1846.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Freemason" class="mw-redirect" title="Freemason">Freemasons</a>' circular, dated 25 September 1846, regarding attendance at the laying of the station's foundation stone by the <a href="/wiki/William_Wentworth-Fitzwilliam,_6th_Earl_Fitzwilliam" title="William Wentworth-Fitzwilliam, 6th Earl Fitzwilliam">Earl Fitzwilliam</a></div></div></div>
<p>The station is staffed 24 hours a day, with booking office open from 05:45 to 20:00 Mondays to Saturdays and 07:45 to 20:00 on Sundays.  There are also 4 self-service ticket machines available in the ticket hall for use when the booking office is closed or for collecting pre-paid tickets. Automated train announcements, customer help points and digital display screens provide train running information on all platforms.  In addition to the aforementioned pubs, the station has a waiting room and buffet on platform 4 and a coffee kiosk on platform 1.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2018_at_Huddersfield_station_-_train_shed.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/2018_at_Huddersfield_station_-_train_shed.JPG/220px-2018_at_Huddersfield_station_-_train_shed.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/2018_at_Huddersfield_station_-_train_shed.JPG/330px-2018_at_Huddersfield_station_-_train_shed.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/2018_at_Huddersfield_station_-_train_shed.JPG/440px-2018_at_Huddersfield_station_-_train_shed.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1366" data-file-height="910" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2018_at_Huddersfield_station_-_train_shed.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The view from the south with platforms 1 and 2 on the right</div></div></div>
<p>The station is situated on St George's Square, which was refurbished in 2009. The square has been made a <a href="/wiki/Pedestrian_zone" title="Pedestrian zone">pedestrian zone</a>. No car parking is available in front of the station entrance, but it is nearby on Brook Street.
</p><p>The station is situated a short distance from <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_bus_station" title="Huddersfield bus station">Huddersfield bus station</a>, so interchange facilities are possible but limited. The Huddersfield <a href="/wiki/FreeCityBus" title="FreeCityBus">FreeCityBus</a> connects the railway station with the bus station, as well as the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Huddersfield" title="University of Huddersfield">University of Huddersfield</a> and other areas of the town centre.
</p><p><b>There are six platforms:</b>
</p>
<ul><li>Platform 1 &#8212; Express services to Manchester Piccadilly, Manchester Airport and Liverpool (via Manchester Victoria).</li>
<li>Platform 2 &#8212; Terminus platform for Penistone Line services to/from Sheffield.</li>
<li>Platform 4 &#8212; Stopping services to Leeds (4a) and Manchester Piccadilly (4b). (One evening train from Hull terminates at Platform 4a.)</li>
<li>Platforms 5 and 6 &#8212; Terminus platforms for local services to/from Leeds (via Brighouse, Halifax and Bradford) and Wakefield Kirkgate.</li>
<li>Platform 8 &#8212; Express services to Leeds, Hull, York, Scarborough, Middlesbrough and Newcastle.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2010, Network Rail and First TransPennine Express completed a series of improvements to the station in order to provide better access for passengers. This consisted of two new lifts, and a new staircase to the subway on Platform 1. The new staircase replaced the existing staircase inside the booking hall. As well as this each platform received new information screens.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2011, further improvement works were carried out to the concourse and waiting area. This phase of improvements was funded by the Railway Heritage Trust, Metro, Kirklees council and the National Station's Improvement Programme.  The main purpose of this was to reduce bottlenecks at peak times as well as general crowding. The redundant stable block on Platform 1 was also turned into a staff training centre and toilets.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2013, automatic ticket barriers were installed at the station.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Work is currently underway on Network Rail's <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hub" title="Northern Hub">Northern Hub</a> project which will see electrification of the Huddersfield Line by 2022, allowing many of the services through the station to switch to newer, faster electric rolling stock.  As part of this project the panel <a href="/wiki/Signal_box" class="mw-redirect" title="Signal box">signal box</a> on platform 4 is to be decommissioned, with its control area passing to the <a href="/wiki/York_Rail_Operating_Centre" title="York Rail Operating Centre">York Rail Operating Centre</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  The first portion of this work took place in early August 2017, with two further weekend closures planned for late October and January 2018.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield_-_TPE_185126_Manchester_Airport_service.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Huddersfield_-_TPE_185126_Manchester_Airport_service.JPG/220px-Huddersfield_-_TPE_185126_Manchester_Airport_service.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Huddersfield_-_TPE_185126_Manchester_Airport_service.JPG/330px-Huddersfield_-_TPE_185126_Manchester_Airport_service.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Huddersfield_-_TPE_185126_Manchester_Airport_service.JPG/440px-Huddersfield_-_TPE_185126_Manchester_Airport_service.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1366" data-file-height="820" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Huddersfield_-_TPE_185126_Manchester_Airport_service.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A westbound <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_185" title="British Rail Class 185">Class 185</a></div></div></div>
<p>During Monday to Friday daytimes, TransPennine Express operate services to <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_railway_station" title="Newcastle railway station">Newcastle</a> , <a href="/wiki/Hull_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Hull railway station">Hull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scarborough_railway_station" title="Scarborough railway station">Scarborough</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_railway_station" title="Middlesbrough railway station">Middlesbrough</a>, all of which call at <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>. A sixth service terminates at Leeds. Four of these are expresses (either non-stop or calling at Dewsbury only), one calls at Dewsbury and Batley and the other serves most local stops to Leeds.
</p><p>Westbound there are two trains per hour to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Piccadilly_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Piccadilly railway station">Manchester Piccadilly</a>, two to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Airport railway station">Manchester Airport</a> (via <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Victoria_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Victoria railway station">Manchester Victoria</a>) and two to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Lime_Street_railway_station" title="Liverpool Lime Street railway station">Liverpool Lime Street</a> via <a href="/wiki/Newton-le-Willows_railway_station" title="Newton-le-Willows railway station">Newton-le-Willows</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> operates hourly stopping services to <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield railway station">Sheffield</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Kirkgate_railway_station" title="Wakefield Kirkgate railway station">Wakefield Kirkgate</a> and to Leeds via <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange" title="Bradford Interchange">Bradford Interchange</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Future_services">Future services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Future services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/London_North_Eastern_Railway" title="London North Eastern Railway">London North Eastern Railway</a> plan to operate services from <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a> via <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a>, beginning in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Steam_trains">Steam trains</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Steam trains">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In keeping with the on-site Head of Steam railway pub, several steam trains still pass through Huddersfield station, including the <i>Cotton Mill Express</i> and the <i>Scarborough Flyer</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the disused side of Platform 2, an old carriage is bolted to the ground. Set in its window is a plaque commemorating 100 years of <a href="/wiki/Steamtrain_Hoorn_Medemblik" class="mw-redirect" title="Steamtrain Hoorn Medemblik">Steamtrain Hoorn Medemblik</a>, a Dutch steam train.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Station_cats">Station cats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Station cats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felix_the_station_cat.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Felix_the_station_cat.jpg/220px-Felix_the_station_cat.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Felix_the_station_cat.jpg/330px-Felix_the_station_cat.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Felix_the_station_cat.jpg/440px-Felix_the_station_cat.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4032" data-file-height="3024" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felix_the_station_cat.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Felix by the door of the station offices on Platform 1</div></div></div>
<p>The first station cat, Felix, joined the staff as a nine-week-old kitten in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Since then she has patrolled the station to keep it free from rodents, and even has her own cat-flap to bypass the ticket barriers.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, Felix was promoted to Senior Pest Controller and has her own hi-vis jacket and name badge.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, local artist Rob Martin painted a portrait of Felix which now hangs in the station.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second station cat, Bolt, joined the staff in September 2018 as an eight-week-old kitten.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
