<p><b>Sipaliwini</b> is the largest <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Suriname" title="Districts of Suriname">district</a> of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, located in the south. Sipaliwini does not have a regional capital as it is directly administered by the national government in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>. The main villages in the district are <a href="/wiki/Apetina" title="Apetina">Apetina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Apoera" title="Apoera">Apoera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bakhuys" class="mw-redirect" title="Bakhuys">Bakhuys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bitagron" title="Bitagron">Bitagron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pokigron" title="Pokigron">Pokigron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kajana,_Suriname" title="Kajana, Suriname">Kajana</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kamp_52&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kamp 52 (page does not exist)">Kamp 52</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pelelu_Tepu" title="Pelelu Tepu">Pelelu Tepu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cottica" title="Cottica">Cottica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anapaike" class="mw-redirect" title="Anapaike">Anapaike</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benzdorp" title="Benzdorp">Benzdorp</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kwamalasamutu" title="Kwamalasamutu">Kwamalasamutu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nieuw_Jacobkondre" title="Nieuw Jacobkondre">Nieuw Jacobkondre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aurora,_Suriname" title="Aurora, Suriname">Aurora</a>, <a href="/wiki/Botopasi" title="Botopasi">Botopasi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goddo,_Suriname" title="Goddo, Suriname">Goddo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Djumu" title="Djumu">Djumu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pontoetoe" title="Pontoetoe">Pontoetoe</a>.
</p><p>Sipaliwini has a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 37,065 and an <a href="/wiki/Area" title="Area">area</a> of 130,567&#160;km². The district is nearly 4 times as large as the other 9 districts of Suriname combined; however, most of the Sipaliwini is unused (it was specifically stated when the district was formed that it would encompass the unused land in Suriname's south) except for the north and the west.
</p><p>It is thought by <a href="/wiki/Archaeologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Archaeologist">archaeologists</a> that <a href="/wiki/Hunter-gatherer" title="Hunter-gatherer">hunter-gatherers</a> lived in what is today Sipaliwini district during the <a href="/wiki/Paleolithic" title="Paleolithic">Paleolithic</a> period.
</p><p>The region was largely left alone during the <a href="/wiki/Colonisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonisation">colonial</a> period, as the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> that controlled Suriname were fearful of the <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, and it was not until the 20th century that development projects began.
</p><p>Sipaliwini district has seen occasional fighting between <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyanese</a> and Surinamese troops over border disputes in the south-west <a href="/wiki/Tigri_Area" title="Tigri Area">Tigri Area</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Coeroeni" title="Coeroeni">Coeroeni</a> resort.
</p>
