<p>The <b>Kennedy family</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Political_family" title="Political family">political family</a> that has long been prominent in <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_the_United_States" title="Politics of the United States">American politics</a>, public service, entertainment and business. The first Kennedy elected to public office was <a href="/wiki/P._J._Kennedy" title="P. J. Kennedy">Patrick Joseph "P. J." Kennedy</a> in 1884, 35 years after the family's arrival from <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. He served in the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_state_legislature" class="mw-redirect" title="Massachusetts state legislature">Massachusetts state legislature</a> from 1884 to 1895. At least one Kennedy family member served in federal elective office in every year from 1947, when P.J. Kennedy's grandson, <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>, became a member of Congress from <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>; to 2011, when P.J. Kennedy's great-grandson, <a href="/wiki/Patrick_J._Kennedy" title="Patrick J. Kennedy">Patrick J. Kennedy</a>, retired as a member of Congress from <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, a span of 64 years.<sup id="cite_ref-bg021310_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bg021310-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The descendants of P.J.'s son, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_Sr." title="Joseph P. Kennedy Sr.">Joseph P. Kennedy Sr.</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rose_Fitzgerald_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Rose Fitzgerald Kennedy">Rose Fitzgerald Kennedy</a> include a <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">president of the United States</a> (who had also served in <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">both houses of Congress</a>), a <a href="/wiki/Attorney_General_of_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Attorney General of the United States">U.S. attorney general</a> (who later served in the U.S. Senate), four other members of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a> or <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">Senate</a>, and two U.S. ambassadors, a lieutenant governor, three state legislators (one of whom went on to the U.S. House of Representatives), and one mayor.
</p><p>In addition, Joseph Sr. and Rose's daughter, <a href="/wiki/Eunice_Kennedy_Shriver" title="Eunice Kennedy Shriver">Eunice</a>, founded the National Institute of Child Health and Human Development (a part of the <a href="/wiki/National_Institutes_of_Health" title="National Institutes of Health">National Institutes of Health</a>), and founded the <a href="/wiki/Special_Olympics" title="Special Olympics">Special Olympics</a>. Eunice's daughter <a href="/wiki/Maria_Shriver" title="Maria Shriver">Maria Shriver</a> served as First Lady of California.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Other descendants of Joseph and Rose Kennedy have been active as lawyers, authors, and activists on behalf of those with physical and intellectual challenges.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kennedy_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Patrick_Kennedy_(1823%E2%80%931858).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Patrick_Kennedy_%281823%E2%80%931858%29.jpg/220px-Patrick_Kennedy_%281823%E2%80%931858%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="283" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Patrick_Kennedy_%281823%E2%80%931858%29.jpg/330px-Patrick_Kennedy_%281823%E2%80%931858%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Patrick_Kennedy_%281823%E2%80%931858%29.jpg/440px-Patrick_Kennedy_%281823%E2%80%931858%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="736" data-file-height="946" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Patrick_Kennedy_(1823%E2%80%931858).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Black-and-white photograph of Patrick Kennedy, the progenitor of the Kennedy family.</div></div></div>
<p>According to genealogist Brian Kennedy in his work <i>JFK's Irish O'Kennedy Ancestors</i>, the Kennedys who would go on to play a significant role in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" class="mw-redirect" title="United States of America">United States of America</a> originated from the Ó Cinnéide Fionn (one of the three Irish Gaelic <a href="/wiki/%C3%93_Cinn%C3%A9ide" class="mw-redirect" title="Ó Cinnéide">Ó Cinnéide</a> clans who ruled the <a href="/wiki/Kings_of_Osraige" title="Kings of Osraige">kingdom of Ormond</a>, along with the Ó Cinnéide Donn and Ó Cinnéide Ruadh). Their progenitor, Diarmaid Ó Cinnéide Fionn, held Knight Castle close to what is today <a href="/wiki/Puckane" title="Puckane">Puckane</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Tipperary" title="County Tipperary">County Tipperary</a> in 1546. From there, having lost out to the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Irish_people" title="Anglo-Irish people">New English</a> order in the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Ireland" title="Kingdom of Ireland">Kingdom of Ireland</a>, they ended up in Dunganstown, <a href="/wiki/New_Ross" title="New Ross">New Ross</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Wexford" title="County Wexford">County Wexford</a> by 1740. Patrick Kennedy was born there in 1823.
</p><p>The first Kennedys to reside in the United States were Patrick Kennedy (1823–1858) and Bridget Murphy (1824–1888), who sailed from Ireland to <a href="/wiki/East_Boston" title="East Boston">East Boston</a> in 1849; Patrick worked in East Boston as a barrel maker, or cooper.<sup id="cite_ref-Maier2003_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maier2003-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Patrick and Bridget had five children: their youngest, <a href="/wiki/P._J._Kennedy" title="P. J. Kennedy">Patrick Joseph "P. J." Kennedy</a>, went into business and served in the state Legislature from 1884–1895.
</p><p>P.J. and Mary Augusta Hickey were the parents of four children. Their oldest was <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Patrick_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph Patrick Kennedy">Joseph Patrick "Joe" Kennedy Sr.</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> who amassed a fortune in banking and securities trading, which he further expanded by investing in other growing industries. Joseph Sr. was appointed by <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a> as the first chairman of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">Securities and Exchange Commission</a>, chairman of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Maritime_Commission" title="United States Maritime Commission">Maritime Commission</a>, and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom">U.S. Ambassador to the United Kingdom</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Causes_of_World_War_II" title="Causes of World War II">lead-up to World War II</a>. He served on The Hoover Commission, officially named the Commission on Organization of the Executive Branch of the Government, from 1947–1949; the commission was appointed by <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Harry_Truman" class="mw-redirect" title="Harry Truman">Harry Truman</a> to recommend administrative changes in the federal government.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Continued_public_service">Continued public service</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kennedy_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Continued public service">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Joseph Sr. and <a href="/wiki/Rose_Kennedy" title="Rose Kennedy">Rose Elizabeth Fitzgerald</a>, were the parents of nine children: <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_Jr." title="Joseph P. Kennedy Jr.">Joseph Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rosemary_Kennedy" title="Rosemary Kennedy">Rosemary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington" title="Kathleen Cavendish, Marchioness of Hartington">Kathleen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eunice_Kennedy_Shriver" title="Eunice Kennedy Shriver">Eunice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Kennedy_Lawford" title="Patricia Kennedy Lawford">Patricia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Kennedy_Smith" title="Jean Kennedy Smith">Jean</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Ted</a>. John served as the 35th <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>, while Robert and Ted both became prominent <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">senators</a>. Every Kennedy elected to public office has served as a <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrat</a>, while other members of the family have worked for the Democratic Party or held <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_the_United_States" title="Cabinet of the United States">Cabinet</a> posts in Democratic administrations. Many have attended <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>, and the family has contributed greatly to that university's <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_School_of_Government" title="John F. Kennedy School of Government">John F. Kennedy School of Government</a>.
</p><p>Joseph Sr. expected his eldest son, Joseph Jr., to go into politics and to ultimately be elected president. Joseph Jr. was elected as a delegate to the <a href="/wiki/1940_Democratic_National_Convention" title="1940 Democratic National Convention">1940 Democratic National Convention</a>, and enlisted in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">Navy</a> after the U.S. entered <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. Joseph Jr. was killed in 1944 when the bomber he was piloting exploded in flight. It then fell upon John, who had considered a career as a journalist — he had authored a book and did some reporting for Hearst Newspapers — to fulfill his father's desire to see the family involved in politics and government. After returning from Navy service, John served in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">U.S. House of Representatives</a> representing the <a href="/wiki/11th_congressional_district_of_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="11th congressional district of Massachusetts">11th congressional district of Massachusetts</a> from 1947 to 1953, and then as <a href="/wiki/List_of_United_States_Senators_from_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="List of United States Senators from Massachusetts">U.S. Senator</a> until his election as <a href="/wiki/1960_United_States_presidential_election" title="1960 United States presidential election">President</a> in 1960. 
</p><p>During <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_the_presidency_of_John_F._Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Timeline of the presidency of John F. Kennedy">John's administration</a>, Robert served as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Attorney_General" title="United States Attorney General">attorney general</a>; his brother-in-law <a href="/wiki/Sargent_Shriver" title="Sargent Shriver">Sargent Shriver</a> served as director of the new Peace Corps, and Ted <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate_special_election_in_Massachusetts,_1962" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Senate special election in Massachusetts, 1962">was elected to the Senate</a>. Among the Kennedy administration's accomplishments: the <a href="/wiki/Alliance_for_Progress" title="Alliance for Progress">Alliance for Progress</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Peace_Corps" title="Peace Corps">Peace Corps</a>, peaceful resolution to the <a href="/wiki/Cuban_Missile_Crisis" title="Cuban Missile Crisis">Cuban Missile Crisis</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_Test_Ban_Treaty" class="mw-redirect" title="Nuclear Test Ban Treaty">Nuclear Test Ban Treaty</a> of 1963, the <a href="/wiki/24th_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="24th Amendment to the United States Constitution">24th Amendment</a> ending the poll tax, and the <a href="/wiki/Civil_Rights_Act_of_1964" title="Civil Rights Act of 1964">Civil Rights Act of 1964</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The family was the subject of intense media coverage during and after the Kennedy presidency, often emphasizing their relative youth, allure, education, and future in politics. Ted served in the Senate with his brother Robert, and was serving in the Senate when his nephew, Joseph P. II, and son, Patrick J., served in the U.S. House of Representatives.
</p><p>In November 2012, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph P. Kennedy III">Joseph P. Kennedy III</a>, son of former Rep. Joseph P. Kennedy II and grandson of the late Sen. Robert F. Kennedy, was elected to the U.S. House of Representatives from Massachusetts' 4th Congressional District.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_incidents">Family incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kennedy_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Family incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_curse" title="Kennedy curse">Kennedy curse</a></div>
<p>Family incidents led Ted to question in a televised statement about the <a href="/wiki/Chappaquiddick_incident" title="Chappaquiddick incident">Chappaquiddick incident</a> in 1969, whether there was a "Kennedy curse". In 1941, Rosemary underwent a lobotomy intended to curb behavioral and emotional issues, but the operation left her incapacitated; Joseph Jr. died in 1944 when the Navy bomber he was piloting during World War II exploded in flight; Kathleen died in a plane crash in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> in 1948; and <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_John_F._Kennedy" title="Assassination of John F. Kennedy">John</a> and <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Assassination of Robert F. Kennedy">Robert</a> were both assassinated, in 1963 and 1968. In 1964, Ted was nearly killed in a plane crash in <a href="/wiki/Southampton,_Massachusetts" title="Southampton, Massachusetts">Southampton, Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-time062664_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-time062664-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Legislative aide Edward Moss and the pilot were killed.<sup id="cite_ref-misfortunes_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-misfortunes-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Ted was seriously injured and spent months in a hospital recovering from a severe back injury, a <a href="/wiki/Pneumothorax" title="Pneumothorax">punctured lung</a>, broken ribs and internal bleeding.<sup id="cite_ref-bg-series-2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bg-series-2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He suffered chronic back pain for the rest of his life as a result of the accident.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-time-rules_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-time-rules-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In later generations, <a href="/wiki/David_A._Kennedy" title="David A. Kennedy">David</a> died of a drug overdose in 1984; <a href="/wiki/Michael_LeMoyne_Kennedy" title="Michael LeMoyne Kennedy">Michael</a> died from injuries sustained in a skiing accident in 1997; <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Jr." title="John F. Kennedy Jr.">John Jr.</a> <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Jr._plane_crash" title="John F. Kennedy Jr. plane crash">died in a plane crash</a> in 1999; <a href="/wiki/Kara_Kennedy" title="Kara Kennedy">Kara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Kennedy_Lawford" class="mw-redirect" title="Christopher Kennedy Lawford">Christopher</a> died of heart attacks in 2011 and 2018; and Courtney's daughter Saoirse died of a drug overdose in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_offices_held">Political offices held</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kennedy_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Political offices held">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/P._J._Kennedy" title="P. J. Kennedy">Patrick Joseph Kennedy</a>: Member of the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_House_of_Representatives" title="Massachusetts House of Representatives">Massachusetts House of Representatives</a> 1884–1889; member of the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Senate" title="Massachusetts Senate">Massachusetts Senate</a>, 1889–1895.
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Joseph_Patrick_Kennedy_Sr." class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph Patrick Kennedy Sr.">Joseph Patrick Kennedy Sr.</a>: Chairman of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Securities_and_Exchange_Commission" title="U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission">U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission</a>, 1934–1935; chairman of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Maritime_Commission" title="United States Maritime Commission">United States Maritime Commission</a>, 1936–1938; <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom">United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom</a>, 1938–1940.
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="John Fitzgerald Kennedy">John Fitzgerald Kennedy</a>: Member of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a> from Massachusetts, 1947–1953; <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senator</a> from Massachusetts, 1953–1960; <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>, 1961–1963; married <a href="/wiki/Jacqueline_Kennedy_Onassis" title="Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis">Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis</a>: <a href="/wiki/First_Lady_of_the_United_States" title="First Lady of the United States">First Lady of the United States</a>, 1961–1963.
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Caroline_Kennedy" title="Caroline Kennedy">Caroline Kennedy</a>: <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_Japan" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to Japan">United States Ambassador to Japan</a>, 2013–2017.</li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eunice_Kennedy_Shriver" title="Eunice Kennedy Shriver">Eunice Kennedy Shriver</a>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Bobby_Shriver" title="Bobby Shriver">Bobby Shriver</a>: Member of the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Monica,_California" title="Santa Monica, California">Santa Monica, California</a> City Council, 2004–2012; Mayor of Santa Monica, 2010.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mark_Kennedy_Shriver" title="Mark Kennedy Shriver">Mark Kennedy Shriver</a>: Member of the <a href="/wiki/Maryland_House_of_Delegates" title="Maryland House of Delegates">Maryland House of Delegates</a>, 1995–2003.</li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Robert_Francis_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Francis Kennedy">Robert Francis Kennedy</a>: <a href="/wiki/United_States_Attorney_General" title="United States Attorney General">United States Attorney General</a> 1961–1964; United States Senator from New York, 1965–1968.
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Kennedy_Townsend" title="Kathleen Kennedy Townsend">Kathleen Kennedy Townsend</a>: <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_governor_of_Maryland" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant governor of Maryland">Lieutenant governor of Maryland</a>, 1995–2003.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_II" title="Joseph P. Kennedy II">Joseph P. Kennedy II</a>: Member of the United States House of Representatives from Massachusetts, 1987–1999.
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph P. Kennedy III">Joseph P. Kennedy III</a>: Member of the United States House of Representatives from Massachusetts, 2013–present.</li></ul></li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jean_Kennedy_Smith" title="Jean Kennedy Smith">Jean Kennedy Smith</a>: <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to Ireland">United States Ambassador to Ireland</a>, 1993–1998.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Edward_Moore_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward Moore Kennedy">Edward Moore Kennedy</a>: United States Senator from Massachusetts, 1962–2009.
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Edward_M._Kennedy_Jr." title="Edward M. Kennedy Jr.">Edward M. Kennedy Jr.</a>: Member of the <a href="/wiki/Connecticut_Senate" title="Connecticut Senate">Connecticut Senate</a>, 2015–2019.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Patrick_J._Kennedy" title="Patrick J. Kennedy">Patrick J. Kennedy</a>: Member of the <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_House_of_Representatives" title="Rhode Island House of Representatives">Rhode Island House of Representatives</a>, 1989–1993; member of the United States House of Representatives from Rhode Island, 1995–2011.</li></ul></li></ul></li></ul></li></ul>
<p>Since John F. Kennedy's election to the U.S. House of Representatives in 1946, there have been very few times in which a Kennedy was not holding public office — first from December 22, 1960 until January 20, 1961 (from Kennedy's resignation from the Senate to his taking office as President) and next from Patrick J. Kennedy's departure from the House on January 3, 2011 until Joseph P. Kennedy III's election to the House on January 3, 2013.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kennedy_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1961, John F. Kennedy was presented with a grant of <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">arms</a> for all the descendants of Patrick Kennedy (1823–1858) from the <a href="/wiki/Chief_Herald_of_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Herald of Ireland">Chief Herald of Ireland</a>. The design of the arms (three silver closed helmets on a black field)<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> strongly alludes to symbols in the coats of arms of the <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_(Ireland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kennedy (Ireland)">O'Kennedys</a> of Ormonde and the <a href="/wiki/Earl_of_Desmond" title="Earl of Desmond">FitzGeralds</a> of Desmond, from whom the family is believed to be descended. The crest is an armored hand holding four arrows between two olive branches, elements taken from the coat of arms of the United States of America and also symbolic of Kennedy and his brothers.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
