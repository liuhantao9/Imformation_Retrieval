<p>The <b>Massachusetts Senate</b> is the upper house of the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_General_Court" title="Massachusetts General Court">Massachusetts General Court</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Bicameral" class="mw-redirect" title="Bicameral">bicameral</a> state legislature of the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_(U.S._state)" title="Commonwealth (U.S. state)">Commonwealth</a> of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>. The Senate comprises 40 elected members from 40 single-member senatorial districts in the state. All but one of the districts are named for the counties in which they are located (the "Cape and Islands" district covers <a href="/wiki/Dukes_County,_Massachusetts" title="Dukes County, Massachusetts">Dukes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nantucket_County,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Nantucket County, Massachusetts">Nantucket</a>, and parts of <a href="/wiki/Barnstable_County,_Massachusetts" title="Barnstable County, Massachusetts">Barnstable</a> counties). Senators serve two-year terms, without <a href="/wiki/Term_limits_in_the_United_States" title="Term limits in the United States">term limits</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Senate convenes in the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_State_House" title="Massachusetts State House">Massachusetts State House</a>, in Boston.
</p><p>The current session is the 191st General Court, which convened January 2, 2019. It consists of 34 <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrats</a> and 6 <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republicans</a>. The <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Massachusetts_Senate" title="President of the Massachusetts Senate">President of the Senate</a> is <a href="/wiki/Karen_E._Spilka" class="mw-redirect" title="Karen E. Spilka">Karen E. Spilka</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ashland,_Massachusetts" title="Ashland, Massachusetts">Ashland</a>. The Senate <a href="/wiki/Minority_Leader" class="mw-redirect" title="Minority Leader">Minority Leader</a>, from the Republican Party, is <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Tarr" title="Bruce Tarr">Bruce Tarr</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gloucester,_Massachusetts" title="Gloucester, Massachusetts">Gloucester</a>. The last state general election was on November 6, 2018.
</p>
