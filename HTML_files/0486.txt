	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Mamas &amp; Papas</h1>

<p><b>Mamas &amp; Papas</b> is a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a>-based nursery <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">brand</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manufacturer" class="mw-redirect" title="Manufacturer">manufacturer</a> supplying <a href="/wiki/Baby_transport" title="Baby transport">prams, pushchairs</a>,  baby products, <a href="/wiki/Furniture" title="Furniture">furniture</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maternity_wear" class="mw-redirect" title="Maternity wear">maternity wear</a>. It was established in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> in 1981 by David and Luisa  Scacchetti, both of Italian ancestry, whose initial aim was to bring Italian design to the UK baby buggy market. They started the business with a range of coloured corduroy prams imported from Italy.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> They sold through one local shop of their own and through supplying other stores, until deciding to become a retail brand in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> For most of its existence, the business was privately owned.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By 2011, Mamas &amp; Papas was described as the "UK's bestselling nursery brand".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> At this time they were expanding the number of their overseas outlets.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, the business embarked on cost-cutting associated with a <a href="/wiki/Trading_while_insolvent#Company_voluntary_arrangement" title="Trading while insolvent">Company Voluntary Arrangement</a> (CVA).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Store closures were considered <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> but were avoided by the CVA.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Private_equity_firm" title="Private equity firm">private equity firm</a> BlueGem Capital Partners took a majority share in Mamas &amp; Papas in the summer of 2014, joining a portfolio that includes Liberty and Jack Wills. <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The company returned to profitability in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2016, the rejuvenated retailer opened a 5,000 sq ft flagship store at Westfield London. <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
