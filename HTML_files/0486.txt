<p><b>Cricket West Indies</b> (<b>CWI</b>), formerly known as <b>West Indies Cricket Board</b> (<b>WICB</b>), is the governing body for <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a> (a sporting confederation of over a dozen mainly English-speaking <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> countries and dependencies that once formed the <a href="/wiki/British_West_Indies" title="British West Indies">British West Indies</a>). It was originally formed in the early 1920s as the <b>West Indies Cricket Board of Control</b>, but changed its name to West Indies Cricket Board in 1996. In November 2015, the Board resolved to rename itself as Cricket West Indies as part of a restructuring exercise that would also see the creation of a separate commercial body. This rebranding formally occurred in May 2017.
</p><p>CWI has been a full member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Cricket_Council" title="International Cricket Council">International Cricket Council</a> (ICC) since 1926. It operates the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_cricket_team" title="West Indies cricket team">West Indies cricket team</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_A_cricket_team" title="West Indies A cricket team">West Indies A cricket team</a>, organising Test tours and one-day internationals with other teams. It also organises domestic cricket in West Indies, including the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Four_Day_Competition" title="Regional Four Day Competition">Regional Four Day Competition</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Super50" title="Regional Super50">Regional Super50</a> domestic one-day (List A) competition. The CWI has also collaborated with Sir <a href="/wiki/Allen_Stanford" title="Allen Stanford">Allen Stanford</a> in the organisation of the domestic <a href="/wiki/Stanford_20/20" title="Stanford 20/20">Stanford 20/20</a> competition for the Twenty20 format of cricket. Later they created their own Twenty20 league called <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_T20" class="mw-redirect" title="Caribbean T20">Caribbean Twenty20</a> after disbanding Stanford 20/20. In 2013 they Created <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Premier_League" title="Caribbean Premier League">Caribbean Premier League</a> , a Professional Twenty20 league.
</p><p>CWI's membership includes the six territorial cricket associations of the various countries and territories which contest the West Indies first-class and limited-overs competition in the Caribbean. Each provides two directors, in addition to a number of non-member directors. Two of these associations are themselves multi-national boards representing a number of countries and dependencies.
</p><p>The President of CWI is <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Skerritt" title="Ricky Skerritt">Ricky Skerritt</a>, who replaced Dave Cameron in 2019. Johnny Grave is the Chief Executive Officer.
</p><p>Since 2005, as per an ICC mandate, the <b>West Indies Women's Cricket Federation</b> (WIWCF) has been integrated with CWI. The President of the WIWCF is Carol Whilby-Maxwell and the Secretary is Michael Seepersaud.
</p><p>CWI is charged with aiding regional development of cricket in the Americas region, under the ICC's development program.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_West_Indies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:WestIndiesCricketFlagPre1999.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/WestIndiesCricketFlagPre1999.svg/200px-WestIndiesCricketFlagPre1999.svg.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="127" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/WestIndiesCricketFlagPre1999.svg/300px-WestIndiesCricketFlagPre1999.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/WestIndiesCricketFlagPre1999.svg/400px-WestIndiesCricketFlagPre1999.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="486" data-file-height="309" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:WestIndiesCricketFlagPre1999.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pre-1999 Flag of the West Indies Cricket Board and Team</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:West_indies_cricket_board_flag.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/65/West_indies_cricket_board_flag.png/200px-West_indies_cricket_board_flag.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="133" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/65/West_indies_cricket_board_flag.png/300px-West_indies_cricket_board_flag.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/6/65/West_indies_cricket_board_flag.png 2x" data-file-width="387" data-file-height="257" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:West_indies_cricket_board_flag.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Flag of the West Indies Cricket Board and Team used until mid-2017</div></div></div>
<p>From the 1880s onwards there had been no central body to co-ordinate the <a href="/wiki/Inter-Colonial_Tournament" title="Inter-Colonial Tournament">Inter-Colonial Tournaments</a> and matches, select composite West Indian XIs for tours and against touring sides and to organise the West Indian tours of England, Canada and the United States. Such organisation as there was, was done by an informal coalition of the major clubs in the region.<sup id="cite_ref-Patterson_Report_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Patterson_Report-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> West Indian cricketers however had felt the need for the establishment of such an organisation although geography and culture were to make such a task difficult. However, with the help of one influential <a href="/wiki/Marylebone_Cricket_Club" title="Marylebone Cricket Club">Marylebone Cricket Club</a> member, <a href="/w/index.php?title=R._H._Mallet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="R. H. Mallet (page does not exist)">R. H. Mallet</a> (the manager of the 1906 and 1923 West Indian tours and future manager of the 1930–31 West Indian tour to Australia), representatives of the various territories finally got together and ultimately formed the West Indies Cricket Board of Control. The preliminary meeting was in <a href="/wiki/Bridgetown" title="Bridgetown">Bridgetown</a>, Barbados, from which the informal <i>West Indies Cricket Conference</i> was founded in 1926. The West Indies Cricket Conference held its first official meeting on 22 January 1927 at the Bridgetown Club in Barbados attended by Mallett and representatives from the Windward Islands, Trinidad &amp; Tobago, Barbados and Demerara (British Guiana). Delegates from Jamaica and the Leeward Islands were invited by unfortunately were unable to attend. At this January meeting the delegates present decided to form the West Indies Cricket Board of Control (WICBC) which would be composed of a president, a secretary and two delegates each from Barbados, Demerara, Jamaica and Trinidad &amp; Tobago and one delegate each from the Leeward Islands and Windward Islands. The WICBC's first meeting was held on 17 and 18 June 1927 in <a href="/wiki/Port-of-Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Port-of-Spain">Port-of-Spain</a>, Trinidad with Harold Austin (the former captain of the 1906 and 1923 West Indian tours of England) serving as the first president.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Board as constituted was enlarged in 1982 when the Leeward and Windward Islands were given the same representation as the other full Members. The President was appointed by the Board at an Ordinary General Meeting and had to be a person resident in the West Indies who had shown a keen and active interest in West Indies cricket. His term of office was for two years (and eligible for re-election) or until his successor was appointed. Board members were also thenceforce to be appointed by their Cricket Associations although in British Guiana the Georgetown Cricket Club held this responsibility until 1943, and in Trinidad the Queens Park Oval Club until 1981 when their respective Cricket Associations were given recognition. There was a provision for Associate Members who would be entitled to attend Meetings but not to move resolutions or vote. <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_national_cricket_team" title="Bermuda national cricket team">Bermuda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belize_national_cricket_team" title="Belize national cricket team">Belize</a> had been the only members in this category prior to their cessation as Associate Members.<sup id="cite_ref-Patterson_Report_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Patterson_Report-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> According to Ram Hiralal,<sup id="cite_ref-Wisden:_Suriname_2002_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wisden:_Suriname_2002-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> former President of the <a href="/wiki/Surinaamse_Cricket_Bond" class="mw-redirect" title="Surinaamse Cricket Bond">Suriname Cricket Association</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Special_award_for_Ram_Hiralal_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Special_award_for_Ram_Hiralal-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Off_the_beaten_track:_Suriname_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Off_the_beaten_track:_Suriname-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> up until 2002 when <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_cricket_team" title="Suriname national cricket team">Suriname</a> applied for and gained affiliate membership of the ICC, it was also an associate member of the West Indies Cricket Board.<sup id="cite_ref-Wisden:_Suriname_2002_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wisden:_Suriname_2002-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic_competitions">Domestic competitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_West_Indies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Domestic competitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The West Indies' major domestic competitions are the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Four_Day_Competition" title="Regional Four Day Competition">Regional Four Day Competition</a> (<a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class</a> competition) and the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Super50" title="Regional Super50">Regional Super50</a> (<a href="/wiki/List_A" class="mw-redirect" title="List A">List A</a> one-day competition) and more recently the Hero <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Premier_League" title="Caribbean Premier League">Caribbean Premier League</a> (domestic <a href="/wiki/Twenty20" title="Twenty20">Twenty20</a> competition - replacing the Caribbean Twenty20, which in turn replaced the <a href="/wiki/Stanford_20/20" title="Stanford 20/20">Stanford 20/20</a> that had been financed and organised by <a href="/wiki/Allen_Stanford" title="Allen Stanford">Allen Stanford</a>).
</p><p>Other CWI domestic competitions include the <a href="/w/index.php?title=TCL_Under-19_West_Indies_Challenge&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="TCL Under-19 West Indies Challenge (page does not exist)">TCL Under-19 West Indies Challenge</a> (three-day first-class competition), <a href="/w/index.php?title=TCL_Under-19_West_Indies_Challenge_Limited_Overs_Series&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="TCL Under-19 West Indies Challenge Limited Overs Series (page does not exist)">TCL Under-19 West Indies Challenge Limited Overs Series</a> (one-day limited overs competition), <a href="/w/index.php?title=CLICO_West_Indies_Under-15_competition&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="CLICO West Indies Under-15 competition (page does not exist)">CLICO West Indies Under-15 competition</a> and the WIWCF <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_cricket" title="Women&#39;s cricket">Women's</a> Senior Tournament. One prominent former competition (not originally organized by the WICB) was the <a href="/wiki/Inter-Colonial_Tournament" title="Inter-Colonial Tournament">Inter-Colonial Tournament</a>.
</p><p>In the case of the Regional Four Day Competition and the Regional Super50 the following first-class domestic teams participate:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Barbados_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Barbados cricket team">Barbados cricket team</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Guyana_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Guyana cricket team">Guyana cricket team</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jamaica_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Jamaica cricket team">Jamaica cricket team</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands_cricket_team" title="Leeward Islands cricket team">Leeward Islands cricket team</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands_cricket_team" title="Windward Islands cricket team">Windward Islands cricket team</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Trinidad and Tobago cricket team">Trinidad and Tobago cricket team</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Combined_Campuses_and_Colleges_cricket_team" title="Combined Campuses and Colleges cricket team">Combined Campuses and Colleges cricket team</a></li></ul>
<p>In 1978 <a href="/wiki/Belize_national_cricket_team" title="Belize national cricket team">Belize</a> had requested (through the then <a href="/wiki/Belize_National_Cricket_Association" title="Belize National Cricket Association">Belize Cricket Association</a>) in a letter to the WICBC to participate in the Shell Shield and the List A competitions, but the WICBC was unable to entertain their request.<sup id="cite_ref-A_Letter_from_the_Belize_Cricket_Association_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-A_Letter_from_the_Belize_Cricket_Association-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For the TCL Under-19 West Indies Challenge (both the first-class and limited overs competitions) it is the Under-19 squads for these teams which participate, while for the CLICO Under-15 West Indies tournament it is the Under-15 squads for these teams which participate. In the 2004 TCL Under-19 Challenge the Under-19 <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Bermuda cricket team">Bermuda cricket team</a> and an Under-19 combined Americas cricket team also took part.
</p><p>In the women's WIWCF Senior Tournament and the Stanford 20/20 competition the separate components of the Leeward Islands and Windward Islands compete individually. Additionally for the Stanford 20/20 competition teams from outside the West Indies sporting confederation, but within the Caribbean, also compete including the <a href="/wiki/Bahamas_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahamas cricket team">Bahamas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Bermuda cricket team">Bermuda</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Cayman Islands cricket team">Cayman Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cuban_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Cuban cricket team">Cuba</a> (which was barred from competing in 2008 by the US embargo), the <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Turks and Caicos Islands cricket team">Turks and Caicos Islands</a> (both competing in 2008) as well as the Dominican Republic and Puerto Rico (announced for the 2009 edition of the Stanford 20/20).
</p><p>In the Caribbean Premier League there are franchise teams competing, with each franchise currently representing one of the six traditional cricketing territories in the West Indies:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Barbados_Tridents" title="Barbados Tridents">Tridents</a> - representing Barbados</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Guyana_Amazon_Warriors" title="Guyana Amazon Warriors">Amazon Warriors</a> - representing Guyana</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jamaica_Tallawahs" title="Jamaica Tallawahs">Tallawahs</a> - representing Jamaica</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/St_Kitts_and_Nevis_Patriots" class="mw-redirect" title="St Kitts and Nevis Patriots">Patriots</a> - representing St. Kitts and Nevis, and the rest of the Leeward Islands</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/St_Lucia_Zouks" title="St Lucia Zouks">Zouks</a> - representing St. Lucia and the rest of the Windward Islands</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Trinbago_Knight_Riders" title="Trinbago Knight Riders">Knight Riders</a> - representing Trinidad and Tobago</li></ul>
