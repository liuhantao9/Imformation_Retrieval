<p><i><b>Happy Valley</b></i> is a British crime drama television series filmed and set in the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">Calder Valley, West Yorkshire</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Northern_England" title="Northern England">Northern England</a>. The series, starring <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Lancashire" title="Sarah Lancashire">Sarah Lancashire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Siobhan_Finneran" title="Siobhan Finneran">Siobhan Finneran</a>, is written and created by <a href="/wiki/Sally_Wainwright" title="Sally Wainwright">Sally Wainwright</a>, and directed by Wainwright, <a href="/wiki/Euros_Lyn" title="Euros Lyn">Euros Lyn</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Fywell" title="Tim Fywell">Tim Fywell</a>. The first series debuted on <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> on 29 April 2014, and the second series debuted on 9 February 2016. In May 2015, Happy Valley won the BAFTA Award for Best Drama Series.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_1">Series 1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Happy_Valley_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Series 1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Catherine Cawood (<a href="/wiki/Sarah_Lancashire" title="Sarah Lancashire">Sarah Lancashire</a>) is a strong-willed police sergeant in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, still coming to terms with the suicide of her teenage daughter, Becky, eight years earlier. Cawood is now divorced from her husband and living with her sister, Clare (<a href="/wiki/Siobhan_Finneran" title="Siobhan Finneran">Siobhan Finneran</a>), a recovering alcoholic and heroin addict, who is helping her bring up Becky's young son, Ryan (Rhys Connah), the product of rape. Neither Catherine's ex-husband nor their adult son, Daniel, want anything to do with Ryan. Catherine hears that Tommy Lee Royce (<a href="/wiki/James_Norton_(actor)" title="James Norton (actor)">James Norton</a>), the man responsible for the brutal rape that impregnated Becky and drove her to suicide shortly after Ryan was born, is out of prison after serving eight years for drug charges. Catherine soon becomes obsessed with finding Royce, unaware that he is involved in the kidnapping of Ann Gallagher (<a href="/wiki/Charlie_Murphy_(actress)" title="Charlie Murphy (actress)">Charlie Murphy</a>), a plot instigated by Kevin Weatherill (<a href="/wiki/Steve_Pemberton" title="Steve Pemberton">Steve Pemberton</a>) and orchestrated by Ashley Cowgill (<a href="/wiki/Joe_Armstrong_(actor)" title="Joe Armstrong (actor)">Joe Armstrong</a>). Things quickly take a dark turn as the abductors scramble to keep the kidnapping secret, although Catherine is on to them.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_2">Series 2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Happy_Valley_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Series 2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Eighteen months after the events of the first series, Catherine Cawood (<a href="/wiki/Sarah_Lancashire" title="Sarah Lancashire">Sarah Lancashire</a>) is back at work and has won the <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Police_Medal" title="Queen&#39;s Police Medal">Queen's Police Medal</a> (QPM) for gallantry, for rescuing Ann Gallagher (<a href="/wiki/Charlie_Murphy_(actress)" title="Charlie Murphy (actress)">Charlie Murphy</a>) from Tommy Lee Royce (<a href="/wiki/James_Norton_(actor)" title="James Norton (actor)">James Norton</a>), who is serving a life sentence in prison. But when Royce's mother is killed, Catherine finds herself implicated in a string of murders. While trying to prove her innocence, Catherine is tasked with investigating a human trafficking operation linked to the serial killings. Meanwhile, senior HMIT officers Detective Superintendent Andy Shepard (<a href="/wiki/Vincent_Franklin" title="Vincent Franklin">Vincent Franklin</a>) and Detective Inspector Jodie Shackleton (<a href="/wiki/Katherine_Kelly_(actress)" title="Katherine Kelly (actress)">Katherine Kelly</a>) begin to suspect that the supposed fourth victim of the <a href="/wiki/Serial_killer" title="Serial killer">serial killer</a>, Victoria Fleming (<a href="/wiki/Amelia_Bullmore" title="Amelia Bullmore">Amelia Bullmore</a>), was in fact murdered by someone else.  Gradually their investigation starts to lead them towards Victoria's actual killer – police detective John Wadsworth (<a href="/wiki/Kevin_Doyle_(actor)" title="Kevin Doyle (actor)">Kevin Doyle</a>), who Fleming had been blackmailing. Catherine's grandson, Ryan, develops a friendship with a new teaching assistant, Miss Wealand (<a href="/wiki/Shirley_Henderson" title="Shirley Henderson">Shirley Henderson</a>), who is secretly a prison groupie infatuated with Royce. Royce, who the court has forbidden having any contact with Ryan, is using Wealand to try to build a relationship with Ryan and get revenge on Catherine. Ryan increasingly concerns his family by asking questions about his father and even suggests Royce should be forgiven.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Happy_Valley_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 22 November 2012, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Stephenson" title="Ben Stephenson">Ben Stephenson</a> announced the commissioning of <i>Happy Valley</i> for BBC One. The programme was written by <a href="/wiki/Sally_Wainwright" title="Sally Wainwright">Sally Wainwright</a>, produced by Karen Lewis, and directed by <a href="/wiki/Euros_Lyn" title="Euros Lyn">Euros Lyn</a>, Sally Wainwright, and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Fywell" title="Tim Fywell">Tim Fywell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Filming began in the <a href="/wiki/Calder_Valley" class="mw-redirect" title="Calder Valley">Calder Valley</a> in November 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Locations in the area included <a href="/wiki/Todmorden" title="Todmorden">Todmorden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luddenden" title="Luddenden">Luddenden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mytholmroyd" title="Mytholmroyd">Mytholmroyd</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keighley" title="Keighley">Keighley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge" title="Sowerby Bridge">Sowerby Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Bridge" title="Hebden Bridge">Hebden Bridge</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Heptonstall" title="Heptonstall">Heptonstall</a>. <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, and other <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> cities are mentioned, though not main filming locations. A former police station ( Station Road, Sowerby Bridge) was used for some scenes, and additional filming took place at North Light Film Studios at Brookes Mill, Huddersfield.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The name "Happy Valley" is what local police in the Calder Valley call the area because of its drug problem.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the series one premiere episode, Ryan points out to Catherine, who is visiting her daughter Becky's grave in the next row, that visitors have left pens at <a href="/wiki/Sylvia_Plath#Following_Plath&#39;s_death" title="Sylvia Plath">Sylvia Plath Hughes</a>' grave.
</p><p>A second series was commissioned on 18 August 2014. Filming began in August 2015, and the first episode was broadcast on 9 February 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-Happy_Valley_series_2_casting_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Happy_Valley_series_2_casting-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The second series was written by Wainwright, produced by Lewis, and directed by Lyn and Wainwright.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Catherine's workplace is a former police station in Sowerby Bridge, and her home and local pub (two other main filming locations) are based in <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Bridge" title="Hebden Bridge">Hebden Bridge</a>. The prison scenes were filmed at Oakham Enterprise Park in Rutland, which was Ashwell Prison until its closure.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Happy_Valley_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first episode aired on 29 April 2014 at 21:00. It garnered 8.64 million viewers, and it was the second most watched show of the week (commencing 28 April 2014) for BBC One.<sup id="cite_ref-2nd_most_watched_show_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2nd_most_watched_show-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The BBC reported that the show received an average consolidated audience of 8.21 million viewers, over six episodes, and an additional 8.1 million requests for the show on <a href="/wiki/BBC_iPlayer" title="BBC iPlayer">BBC iPlayer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Radio_Times" title="Radio Times">Radio Times</a></i> called <i>Happy Valley</i> a "word-of-mouth hit" which "steadily became a success outside the normal audience for the slot and channel."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After "Episode 1" aired, <a href="/wiki/Ofcom" title="Ofcom">Ofcom</a> received four complaints under the category "violence and dangerous behaviour", but they did not pursue the matter.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Reviews from the media have been overwhelmingly positive, and the show has received 100% rating critic review on <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> However, some reviewers have criticised the show for its graphic content, especially in "Episode 3" and "Episode 4". The <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mail" title="Daily Mail">Daily Mail</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s TV correspondent Alasdair Glennie questioned whether or not the BBC went "too far" in "Episode 4" with the brutal attack on police sergeant Cawood and the murder of one of her officers. The <i>Daily Mail</i> claimed that the BBC had received "15 complaints about ["Episode 4"], which aired between 9pm and 10pm after the watershed, while 45 viewers contacted the [BBC] corporation to say how much they enjoyed the show." Vivienne Pattison, a campaigner for stronger television regulations and a part of <a href="/wiki/Mediawatch-uk" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediawatch-uk">Mediawatch-uk</a>, declared that the violence "is part of a worrying trend in TV drama."<sup id="cite_ref-Happy_Valley_gone_too_far_-_Daily_Mail_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Happy_Valley_gone_too_far_-_Daily_Mail-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In response to the criticism, <i>Happy Valley</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s creator-writer, Wainwright, defended the show as "a quality, well-written drama" and stated, "Judging by the amount of email, texts, tweets I've had, I don't think anyone is asking me to apologise."<sup id="cite_ref-Series_2_discussion_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Series_2_discussion-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i>Radio Times</i>, Wainwright said the level of violence had been carefully considered and it was done responsibly, by showing the psychological and physical damage suffered by Catherine.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other critics have praised the show. Vicky Frost of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> wrote: "To get hung up on the violence of this BBC1 kidnap drama misses the point. It is beautifully written by Sally Wainwright, draws an astonishing performance from Sarah Lancashire—and between them, they have created something truly unmissable."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Another <i>Daily Mail</i> TV correspondent, Christopher Stevens, rated "Episode 4" with 5/5 stars, saying that "every instalment has been unmissable" and "BAFTA bosses might as well get next year's trophy inscribed now" for star Sarah Lancashire.<sup id="cite_ref-5/5_Stars_-_Ep4_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5/5_Stars_-_Ep4-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Gerard O'Donovan of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Telegraph</a></i> called <i>Happy Valley</i> "complex, thrilling and brilliantly written and acted", and "one of the best watches of 2014."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Happy_Valley_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In May 2015, <i>Happy Valley</i> won the BAFTA Award for Best Drama Series.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, it had won a TV Choice Award for Best New Drama; Sarah Lancashire was named Best Actress at the same time. The series was also voted the best TV programme of 2014 by readers of <i><a href="/wiki/Radio_Times" title="Radio Times">Radio Times</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Happy_Valley_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/BBC_Shop" title="BBC Shop">BBC Shop</a> released <i>Happy Valley</i> series one on DVD, in <a href="/wiki/DVD_region_code" title="DVD region code">regions</a> <a href="/wiki/DVD_region_code#Region_codes_and_countries" title="DVD region code">two and four</a>, on 16 June 2014. The DVD includes two discs, featuring 351 minutes worth of footage, and has an age certificate of 15.<sup id="cite_ref-DVD_release_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DVD_release-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> All six episodes of the series were released on <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>, both in standard and high definition.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 20 August 2014, the series was further released on <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> in Canada and the USA, marketed as a "Netflix Original".<sup id="cite_ref-Netflix_release_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Netflix_release-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the summer of 2016 Series 1 of Happy Valley was released on Netflix in the UK and is currently repeated on the channel 'W'. A year later Series 2 was released on Netflix in the UK.
</p>
