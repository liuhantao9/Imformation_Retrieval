<p>There are many kinds of people who deal with <a href="/wiki/Magic_(paranormal)" class="mw-redirect" title="Magic (paranormal)">magic</a>. They include <a href="/wiki/Magician_(paranormal)" class="mw-redirect" title="Magician (paranormal)">paranormal magicians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magician_(fantasy)" title="Magician (fantasy)">fantasy magicians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shaman" class="mw-redirect" title="Shaman">shamans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kalku" title="Kalku">kalku</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Magi" title="Magi">magi</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Shamanic_magic" class="mw-redirect" title="Shamanic magic">Shamanic magic</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Seid_(shamanic_magic)" class="mw-redirect" title="Seid (shamanic magic)">Seid</a> plays a role, as does the <a href="/wiki/Warlock" title="Warlock">Warlock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Witch" class="mw-redirect" title="Witch">Witch</a> in Paganism. In history, <a href="/wiki/Magic_in_the_Greco-Roman_world" class="mw-redirect" title="Magic in the Greco-Roman world">magic in the Greco-Roman world</a> was common. There are also the <a href="/wiki/Onmy%C5%8Dd%C5%8D" title="Onmyōdō">Onmyou Mystic</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Bomoh" title="Bomoh">Bomoh</a>.
</p>
