<p><b>Yorkshire Tiger</b><sup id="cite_ref-company6544272_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company6544272-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a bus company based in <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. Formed in May 2008 as Centrebus Holdings, in September 2013 it became a subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Arriva" title="Arriva">Arriva</a> and was rebranded Yorkshire Tiger.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stagecoach">Stagecoach</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Tiger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Stagecoach">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2005 <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_Yorkshire" title="Stagecoach Yorkshire">Stagecoach Yorkshire</a> purchased the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> operations and depot of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Traction" title="Yorkshire Traction">Yorkshire Traction</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2008 it was reported that <a href="/wiki/Arriva" title="Arriva">Arriva</a> were in discussions with Stagecoach to purchase their bus operations in Huddersfield. <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Yorkshire" title="Arriva Yorkshire">Arriva Yorkshire</a> already operated services in the town, so purchasing Stagecoach's operations would enlarge their presence. At the time Arriva registered a trading name of The Huddersfield Bus Company, registered on the <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Southern_Counties" title="Arriva Southern Counties">Arriva Southern Counties</a> licence.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2008 Centrebus Holdings<sup id="cite_ref-company6544272_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-company6544272-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> purchased the business. Centrebus Holdings was owned by three directors of <a href="/wiki/Centrebus" title="Centrebus">Centrebus</a> who together owned 56% and Arriva the remaining 44%.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Despite the common name and livery, Centrebus Holdings has never been owned by the Centrebus company.<sup id="cite_ref-BusesJun08_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BusesJun08-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hinckley_operations">Hinckley operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Tiger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hinckley operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2008 <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Midlands" title="Arriva Midlands">Arriva Midlands</a>' <a href="/wiki/Hinckley" title="Hinckley">Hinckley</a> depot was sold to Centrebus Holdings.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In September 2008 <a href="/wiki/Centrebus" title="Centrebus">Centrebus</a> purchased the <a href="/wiki/Leicester" title="Leicester">Leicester</a> bus operations of Woods Coaches and services 58 and 140 were transferred to Centrebus Holdings.
</p><p>Centrebus were given an emergency contract for route 148 in September which was previously operated by Thurmaston Bus, during December 2012. Wide Horizon Coaches of Hinckley ceased trading and Centrebus were awarded an emergency contract for their H1 service by <a href="/wiki/Leicestershire_County_Council" title="Leicestershire County Council">Leicestershire County Council</a>, for four months until a review of tendered bus services around the Hinckley area was carried out. Centrebus were originally awarded a new contract for a revised service 148 which would have covered services 5, 8 and H1. However, Centrebus Holdings took the commercial decision to merge services 8 and H1 into the current 148 which was expanded into Hinckley. Service 5 was kept on as a tendered service.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On 9 September 2013 the <a href="/wiki/Hinckley" title="Hinckley">Hinckley</a> based operations were rebranded as Hinckley Bus.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Centrebus_Holdings">Centrebus Holdings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Tiger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Centrebus Holdings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With the purchase of both Stagecoach and K-Line, Centrebus Holdings became the second largest bus operator in Huddersfield after <a href="/wiki/First_West_Yorkshire" title="First West Yorkshire">First West Yorkshire</a>. Centrebus decided to retain the K-Line brand, and operate the former Stagecoach services under the Huddersfield Bus Company name, although the Centrebus name was used for the rest of the West Yorkshire operations.
</p><p>In January 2010 Centrebus Holdings took over some contracted services previously run by <a href="/wiki/First_West_Yorkshire" title="First West Yorkshire">First West Yorkshire</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a> area, including <a href="/wiki/Greetland" title="Greetland">Greetland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ripponden" title="Ripponden">Ripponden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge" title="Sowerby Bridge">Sowerby Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stainland" title="Stainland">Stainland</a>. Unlike services in Huddersfield, most of the Calderdale services began to run under the Centrebus brand, while routes 536 and 537 were operated by Huddersfield Bus Company from their Waterloo depot and route 343 was operated by K-Line.<sup id="cite_ref-Metro_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metro-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result, a new depot was established in <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a> for the Calderdale operations.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The services that Centrebus Holdings obtained were:<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>343 Halifax-Huddersfield (operated by K-Line)</li>
<li>528 Halifax-Rochdale</li>
<li>531 Halifax-Ripponden via Copley</li>
<li>534 Halifax-Northowram</li>
<li>536/537 Halifax-Huddersfield (operated by Huddersfield Bus)</li>
<li>557 Halifax-Ripponden/Rishworth (Commons also on last run)</li>
<li>559 Halifax-Dean Head</li>
<li>560 Halifax-Commons (early morning only)</li>
<li>574 Halifax-Booth/Midgeley</li>
<li>577 Halifax-Hubberton</li>
<li>579 Halifax-Sowerby (evenings only; daytime services operated by First)</li>
<li>832 Halifax-West End</li></ul>
<p>In April 2010, Centrebus Holdings obtained another service under the Huddersfield Bus Company division, taking over routes 435/436 from <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Yorkshire" title="Arriva Yorkshire">Arriva Yorkshire</a>. They also extended further by obtaining three <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_Airport" title="Leeds Bradford Airport">Leeds Bradford Airport</a> bus services that were previously run by First, routes 737 and 747 to <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a> and 757 to Leeds.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Like the Calderdale operations, these services operated under the Centrebus name, with a depot on Kirkstall Road.
</p><p>The company would face competition as First continued to run the 757 service between the airport and <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds railway station</a>, while Centrebus ran to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_bus_station" title="Leeds City bus station">Leeds City bus station</a>. However, since July 2010 Centrebus have been the only operator of route 757 after First withdrew their service.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> At the same time, Centrebus began to operate many more services in the Bradford and Leeds areas, winning contracts from <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Passenger_Transport_Executive" class="mw-redirect" title="West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive">Metro</a> for services previously run by First, while some services are jointly operated by the two companies.
</p><p>In October 2010 <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Joint_Committee" title="Halifax Joint Committee">Halifax Joint Committee</a>'s routes 28A, 34 and 36 were taken over by Centrebus's Elland depot while K-Line took over route 31.<sup id="cite_ref-Firm_takes_on_bus_services_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Firm_takes_on_bus_services-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The A99 service, which runs during term time between the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Bradford" title="University of Bradford">University of Bradford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford_University_School_of_Management" title="Bradford University School of Management">Bradford University School of Management</a> campuses, was taken over by Centrebus in 2012 having previously been operated by First. At the same time the service was made free to use for university students.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Yorkshire_Tiger">Yorkshire Tiger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Tiger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Yorkshire Tiger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In September 2013 Arriva bought out its partners and rebranded the company Yorkshire Tiger.<sup id="cite_ref-Arriva090913_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arriva090913-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bus&amp;Coach120913_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bus&amp;Coach120913-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bus&amp;Coach9Oct13_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bus&amp;Coach9Oct13-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Its fleet is gradually being repainted with the new brand identity, consisting of overall orange with tiger prints. Many of its contracted services in Calderdale fall under a new sub-brand, 'The Calder Cubs'. These are all operated with Optare Solo buses, which all have free wifi and USB plug sockets.
</p><p>A fire at its Elland depot in December 2015 saw ten of its buses destroyed.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flying_Tiger">Flying Tiger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Tiger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Flying Tiger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:YJ14_BWW_at_Leeds_City_Sqaure_on_the_757_21_February_2018.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/YJ14_BWW_at_Leeds_City_Sqaure_on_the_757_21_February_2018.jpg/220px-YJ14_BWW_at_Leeds_City_Sqaure_on_the_757_21_February_2018.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/YJ14_BWW_at_Leeds_City_Sqaure_on_the_757_21_February_2018.jpg/330px-YJ14_BWW_at_Leeds_City_Sqaure_on_the_757_21_February_2018.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/YJ14_BWW_at_Leeds_City_Sqaure_on_the_757_21_February_2018.jpg/440px-YJ14_BWW_at_Leeds_City_Sqaure_on_the_757_21_February_2018.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4608" data-file-height="3456" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:YJ14_BWW_at_Leeds_City_Sqaure_on_the_757_21_February_2018.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>YJ14 BWW an <a href="/wiki/Optare_Versa" title="Optare Versa">Optare Versa</a>, in the old livery, at Leeds City Square on the 757 towards Leeds Bus Station. 21 February 2018</div></div></div>
<p>In April 2014, Yorkshire Tiger's services to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_Airport" title="Leeds Bradford Airport">Leeds Bradford Airport</a> (737, 747 and 757) began using the sub-brand <i>Flying Tiger</i>. For the 757 service from Leeds, new <a href="/wiki/Optare_Versa" title="Optare Versa">Optare Versa</a> buses in a blue livery have been used and the service frequency increased to every 20 minutes. Existing <a href="/wiki/Scania_OmniCity" title="Scania OmniCity">Scania OmniCity</a> buses used on routes 737 and 747 (<a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a> to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Bradford_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds Bradford International Airport"> the airport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a> to <a href="/wiki/Harrogate" title="Harrogate">Harrogate</a> respectively) were also refurbished and rebranded.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In 2017, new Enviro 200 MMC vehicles were purchased for routes 737 and 747 with two Scania Omnicities staying on as spares, a new livery was also introduced and will also be applied to the Versa vehicles.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tiger_Blue">Tiger Blue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Tiger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tiger Blue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Tiger Blue</b> (formerly known as <b>K-Line</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> is a bus operator based in <a href="/wiki/Honley" title="Honley">Honley</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. Tiger Blue operate a number of public bus services in the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Kirklees</a> area. The company is only second to <a href="/wiki/First_Calderdale_%26_Huddersfield" title="First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield">First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield</a> in terms of the number of services operated in the Huddersfield area.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The company also runs the <a href="/wiki/FreeCityBus#Huddersfield" title="FreeCityBus">FreeTownBus</a> service in Huddersfield town centre, which is a <a href="/wiki/Zero-fare_public_transport" class="mw-redirect" title="Zero-fare public transport">free</a> bus service running a circular route around the town centre.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="History_2">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Tiger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:KlineBusCompany.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8e/KlineBusCompany.svg/220px-KlineBusCompany.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="74" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8e/KlineBusCompany.svg/330px-KlineBusCompany.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8e/KlineBusCompany.svg/440px-KlineBusCompany.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="172" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:KlineBusCompany.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The former K-Line logo</div></div></div>
<p>K-Line was founded in 1980 initially operating out of a garage in Kingburton. With deregulation in 1986, it began operating commercial services in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and tendered services in <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a>. In November 1993, K-Line was purchased by Pride of the Road Group and in 1998 moved to <a href="/wiki/Honley" title="Honley">Honley</a>. In May 2008 K-Line was purchased by Centrebus Holdings.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> It continued to trade as a separate entity from Centrebus, however.
</p><p>In January 2010, Centrebus Holdings took over some contracted services previously run by <a href="/wiki/First_West_Yorkshire" title="First West Yorkshire">First West Yorkshire</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a> area including route 343 that was taken over by K-Line.<sup id="cite_ref-Metro_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metro-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In October 2010, K-Line took over route 31 from <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Joint_Committee" title="Halifax Joint Committee">Halifax Joint Committee</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Firm_takes_on_bus_services_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Firm_takes_on_bus_services-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After <a href="/wiki/Arriva" title="Arriva">Arriva</a> wholly purchased Centrebus Holdings in September 2013, it was decided to rebrand most of the business as Yorkshire Tiger while retaining K-Line as a separate brand.<sup id="cite_ref-Arriva090913_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arriva090913-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bus&amp;Coach120913_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bus&amp;Coach120913-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bus&amp;Coach9Oct13_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bus&amp;Coach9Oct13-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In October 2014, Arriva rebranded K-Line as Tiger Blue to bring it in line with the Yorkshire Tiger brand.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Tiger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hudds-ftb.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Hudds-ftb.jpg/220px-Hudds-ftb.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Hudds-ftb.jpg/330px-Hudds-ftb.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Hudds-ftb.jpg/440px-Hudds-ftb.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="1024" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hudds-ftb.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Optare_Solo" title="Optare Solo">Optare Solo</a> on the Huddersfield FreeTownBus</div></div></div>
<p>Tiger Blue provide several bus services in the Huddersfield area, running during the day Mondays to Saturdays, serving areas such as <a href="/wiki/Almondbury" title="Almondbury">Almondbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brackenhall" title="Brackenhall">Brackenhall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crosland_Moor" title="Crosland Moor">Crosland Moor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golcar" title="Golcar">Golcar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Royal_Infirmary" title="Huddersfield Royal Infirmary">Huddersfield Royal Infirmary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meltham" title="Meltham">Meltham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Milnsbridge" title="Milnsbridge">Milnsbridge</a>, Mount, <a href="/wiki/Netherton,_Kirklees" title="Netherton, Kirklees">Netherton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newsome" title="Newsome">Newsome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quarmby" title="Quarmby">Quarmby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scapegoat_Hill" title="Scapegoat Hill">Scapegoat Hill</a>. Buses also run to parts of <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hebden_Bridge" title="Hebden Bridge">Hebden Bridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ripponden" title="Ripponden">Ripponden</a>. Tiger Blue only run one service on Sundays, the 900 (and then only between Easter and the third Sunday in October) and do not run any services during the evening.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Tiger Blue also operates the <a href="/wiki/FreeCityBus" title="FreeCityBus">FreeTownBus</a> service in Huddersfield.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 29 September 2014, Tiger Blue took over operation of the Kirklees Access Bus contract, for <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Metro" title="West Yorkshire Metro">Metro</a>, with four new <a href="/wiki/Optare_Solo#SR" title="Optare Solo">Optare Solo SRs</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleet">Fleet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Tiger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Fleet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As at October 2014, the fleet consists of 144 buses (including Tiger Blue).<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
