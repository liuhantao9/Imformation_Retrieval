<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tafel_paepste.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Tafel_paepste.jpg/220px-Tafel_paepste.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="309" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Tafel_paepste.jpg/330px-Tafel_paepste.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Tafel_paepste.jpg/440px-Tafel_paepste.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1882" data-file-height="2643" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tafel_paepste.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Popes buried in <a href="/wiki/St._Peter%27s_Basilica" title="St. Peter&#39;s Basilica">St. Peter's</a></div></div></div>
<p>A <b>papal name</b> or <b>pontificial name</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Regnal_name" title="Regnal name">regnal name</a> taken by a <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">pope</a>. Both the head of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>, usually known as <i>the</i> Pope, and the <a href="/wiki/Pope_of_the_Coptic_Orthodox_Church_of_Alexandria" title="Pope of the Coptic Orthodox Church of Alexandria">Pope of the Coptic Orthodox Church of Alexandria</a> (Coptic Pope) choose papal names. As of 2013<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Papal_name&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Pope Francis</a> is the Catholic Pope, and <a href="/wiki/Pope_Tawadros_II_of_Alexandria" title="Pope Tawadros II of Alexandria">Tawadros II or Theodoros II</a> is the Coptic Pope. This article discusses and lists the names of Catholic Popes; another article has a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Coptic_Orthodox_Popes_of_Alexandria" title="List of Coptic Orthodox Popes of Alexandria">list of Coptic Orthodox Popes of Alexandria</a>.
</p><p>While popes in the early centuries retained their birth names after their accession to the papacy, later on <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">popes</a> began to adopt a <a href="/wiki/Name_change" title="Name change">new name</a> upon their accession. This first started in the sixth century and became customary in the 10th century. Since 1555, every pope has taken a papal name.
</p><p>The pontificial name is given in Latin by virtue of the Pope's status as Bishop of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See" title="Holy See">Holy See</a> of Rome and head of the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">universal church</a>. The Pope is also given an Italian name by virtue of his <a href="/wiki/Vatican_city" class="mw-redirect" title="Vatican city">Vatican</a> citizenship and because of his position as <a href="/wiki/Primate_of_Italy" class="mw-redirect" title="Primate of Italy">Primate of Italy</a>. However, it is customary when referring to popes to translate the regnal name into all local languages. Thus, for example, Papa Franciscus is Papa Francesco in Italian, Papa Francisco in his native Spanish, and Pope Francis in English.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Title_and_honorifics">Title and honorifics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Title and honorifics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Catholic">Catholic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Catholic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The official <a href="/wiki/Style_(manner_of_address)" title="Style (manner of address)">style</a> of the Catholic Pope in English is His Holiness Pope [papal name]. Holy Father is another honorific often used for popes.
</p><p>The full title, rarely used, of the Catholic Pope in English is: <a href="/wiki/His_Holiness" title="His Holiness">His Holiness</a> [papal name], <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Rome" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishop of Rome">Bishop of Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vicar_of_Christ" title="Vicar of Christ">Vicar of Jesus Christ</a>, Successor of the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Peter" title="Saint Peter">Prince of the Apostles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pontifex_Maximus" class="mw-redirect" title="Pontifex Maximus">Supreme</a> <a href="/wiki/Pontiff#Christianity" title="Pontiff">Pontiff</a> of the Universal Church, <a href="/wiki/Primate_(bishop)" title="Primate (bishop)">Primate</a> of Italy, <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_bishop" title="Metropolitan bishop">Archbishop and Metropolitan</a> of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_the_Roman_Catholic_dioceses_in_Italy#Ecclesiastical_Province_of_Rome" class="mw-redirect" title="List of the Roman Catholic dioceses in Italy">Roman</a> <a href="/wiki/Ecclesiastical_province#Catholic_Church" title="Ecclesiastical province">Province</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Sovereigns_of_the_Vatican_City_State" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Sovereigns of the Vatican City State">Sovereign</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Vatican_City" title="Vatican City">Vatican City State</a>, <a href="/wiki/Servant_of_the_servants_of_God" title="Servant of the servants of God">Servant of the servants of God</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coptic">Coptic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Coptic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Pope_of_the_Coptic_Orthodox_Church_of_Alexandria#Official_title" title="Pope of the Coptic Orthodox Church of Alexandria">official title of the leader of the Coptic Orthodox Church of Alexandria</a> is <i>Pope of Alexandria and Patriarch of all Africa on the <a href="/wiki/Holy_See_of_St._Mark" class="mw-redirect" title="Holy See of St. Mark">Holy See of St. Mark</a> the Apostle</i>. The Successor of  St. <a href="/wiki/Mark_the_Evangelist" title="Mark the Evangelist">Mark the Evangelist</a>, Holy Apostle and Martyr, on the Holy Apostolic Throne of the Great City of Alexandria.
</p><p>He is considered to be
</p>
<ul><li>Father of Fathers.</li>
<li>Shepherd of Shepherds.</li>
<li>Hierarch of all Hierarchs</li></ul>
<p>Honorary titles attributed to the Hierarch of the Alexandrine Throne are
</p>
<ul><li>The Pillar and Defender of the Holy, Catholic, Apostolic Church and of the Orthodox Faith.</li>
<li>The Dean of the <a href="/wiki/Alexandrian_school" title="Alexandrian school">Great Catechetical School of Theology of Alexandria</a>.</li>
<li>The Ecumenical (Universal) Judge (Arbitrator) of the Holy Apostolic and Catholic (Universal) Church.</li>
<li>The Thirteenth among the Holy <a href="/wiki/Twelve_Apostles" class="mw-redirect" title="Twelve Apostles">Apostles</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the first centuries of the church, the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Rome" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishop of Rome">bishops of Rome</a> continued to use their <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptismal</a> names after their elections. The custom of choosing a new name began in AD 533: Mercurius deemed it inappropriate for a pope to be named after the pagan Roman god <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(mythology)" title="Mercury (mythology)">Mercury</a>, and adopted the name <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_II" title="Pope John II">John II</a> in honor of his predecessor <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_I" title="Pope John I">John I</a>, who was venerated as a <a href="/wiki/Martyr" title="Martyr">martyr</a>. In the 10th century clerics from beyond the Alps, especially Germany and France, acceded to the papacy and replaced their foreign-sounding names with more traditional ones.
</p><p>The last pope to use his baptismal name was <a href="/wiki/Pope_Marcellus_II" title="Pope Marcellus II">Marcellus II</a> in 1555, a choice that was even then quite exceptional. Names are freely chosen by popes, and not based on any system. Names of immediate or distant predecessors, mentors, saints, or even family members—as was the case with <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_XXIII" title="Pope John XXIII">John XXIII</a>—have been adopted.
</p><p>In 1978 <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_(Catholicism)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cardinal (Catholicism)">Cardinal</a> <a href="/wiki/Albino_Luciani" class="mw-redirect" title="Albino Luciani">Albino Luciani</a> became the first pope to take a double name, John Paul I, to honour his two immediate predecessors, John XXIII and <a href="/wiki/Pope_Paul_VI" title="Pope Paul VI">Paul VI</a>; he had been elevated to bishop by John XXIII, then to <a href="/wiki/Patriarch_of_Venice" title="Patriarch of Venice">Patriarch of Venice</a> and the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Cardinals" title="College of Cardinals">College of Cardinals</a> by Paul VI. John Paul I was also the first pope in almost 1,100 years since <a href="/wiki/Pope_Lando" title="Pope Lando">Lando</a> in 913 to adopt a papal name that had not previously been used. After John Paul I's sudden death a month later, Cardinal <a href="/wiki/Karol_J%C3%B3zef_Wojty%C5%82a" class="mw-redirect" title="Karol Józef Wojtyła">Karol Józef Wojtyła</a> was elected and, wishing to continue his predecessor's work, became the second Pope to take a double name as <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">John Paul II</a>. In 2013, a new name was introduced into the lineage: on being elected Pope, Cardinal Jorge Mario Bergoglio selected the name <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Francis</a> to emphasize the spirit of poverty and peace embodied by Saint <a href="/wiki/Francis_of_Assisi" title="Francis of Assisi">Francis of Assisi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbolism">Symbolism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Symbolism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Often the new pontiff's choice of name upon being elected to the papacy is seen as a signal to the world of whom the new pope will emulate, what policies he will seek to enact, or even the length of his reign.  Such was the case with <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XVI" title="Pope Benedict XVI">Benedict XVI</a> – it was speculated that he chose the name because he wished to emulate <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_XV" title="Pope Benedict XV">Benedict XV</a>, and to also call attention to the fact that at 7.5 years, Benedict XV's reign was relatively short. Benedict XVI's own reign, which ended with his resignation on 28 February 2013, also lasted less than 8 years (he was already 78 when elected).
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Saint_Peter" title="Saint Peter">Saint Peter</a> was the first Pope; no Pope of Rome has chosen the name <a href="/wiki/Pope_Peter_II" title="Pope Peter II">Peter II</a>, although there is no prohibition against doing so. Since the 1970s <a href="/wiki/Conclavism#&quot;Mysticalists&quot;" title="Conclavism">some antipopes</a>, with only a minuscule following, took the name Pope Peter II.
</p><p>Probably because of the controversial fifteenth-century <a href="/wiki/Antipope" title="Antipope">antipope</a> known as <a href="/wiki/Antipope_John_XXIII" title="Antipope John XXIII">Pope John XXIII</a>, this name was avoided for over 500 years until the election in 1958 of Pope (of Rome) John XXIII.  Immediately after John XXIII's election as pope in 1958, it was not known if he would be John XXIII or XXIV; he decided that he would be known as John XXIII. The number used by an antipope is ignored if possible, but this is not possible if, by the time someone is reckoned as antipope, the name has since been used by one or more legitimate popes (e.g. Benedict X was later reckoned as antipope).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_practice">Current practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Current practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Immediately after a new pope is elected, and accepts the election, he is asked in <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> "By what name shall you be called?"† The new pope chooses the name by which he will be known from that point on. The senior Cardinal Deacon, or Cardinal Protodeacon, then appears on the balcony of Saint Peter's to proclaim the new pope by his birth name, and announce his papal name:
</p>
<blockquote lang="la" style="font-style:italic; width:42%; margin:1em 4%; float:left;"><div class="poem">
<p>Annuntio vobis gaudium magnum:<br />
<a href="/wiki/Habemus_Papam" class="mw-redirect" title="Habemus Papam">Habemus Papam</a>!<br />
Eminentissimum ac reverendissimum dominum,<br />
dominum [baptismal name],<br />
Sanctæ Romanæ Ecclesiæ Cardinalem [surname],<br />
qui sibi nomen imposuit [papal name].
</p>
</div></blockquote>
<blockquote style="width:42%; margin:1em 4%; float:left;"><div class="poem">
<p>I announce to you a great joy:<br />
We have a Pope!<br />
The Most Eminent and Most Reverend Father,<br />
Lord [baptismal name],<br />
Cardinal of the Holy Roman Church [surname],<br />
who takes to himself the name [papal name].
</p>
</div></blockquote>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<p>†Unless impeded, the <a href="/wiki/Dean_of_the_College_of_Cardinals" title="Dean of the College of Cardinals">Dean of the College of Cardinals</a> asks the newly elected pope if he accepts his election and what name he will use. In 2005, <a href="/wiki/Cardinal_Joseph_Ratzinger" class="mw-redirect" title="Cardinal Joseph Ratzinger">Cardinal Joseph Ratzinger</a>, the Dean, was himself elected pope, so these questions were asked by the <a href="/wiki/Dean_of_the_College_of_Cardinals" title="Dean of the College of Cardinals">subdean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angelo_Sodano" title="Angelo Sodano">Cardinal Angelo Sodano</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Papal_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>On average the papal name repeats 3.29 times.</li>
<li>The number of all popes to the present is 264. <a href="/wiki/Pope_Benedict_IX" title="Pope Benedict IX">Pope Benedict IX</a> was elected pope three times, therefore the number of pontificates is actually 266.</li>
<li>Data are taken from book: <a href="/wiki/A._J._O%27Brien" class="mw-redirect" title="A. J. O&#39;Brien">A. J. O'Brien</a>, <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Popes:_Twenty_Centuries_of_History&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="The Popes: Twenty Centuries of History (page does not exist)">The Popes: Twenty Centuries of History</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Libreria_Editrice_Vaticana" class="mw-redirect" title="Libreria Editrice Vaticana">Libreria Editrice Vaticana</a>, 2002.</li>
<li>It is stated that the fifteenth-century <a href="/wiki/Antipope_John_XXIII" title="Antipope John XXIII">John XXIII</a> did exist but was considered an antipope.</li></ul>
