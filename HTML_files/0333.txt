<p>This page introduces a list of <a href="/wiki/Proper_noun" title="Proper noun">proper names</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a>.
</p><p>Some of the names are given with a proposed etymological meaning. For further information on the names included on the list, the reader may consult the sources listed below in the References and External Links.
</p><p>Names play a variety of roles in the Bible. They sometimes relate to the nominee's role in a biblical narrative, as in the case of Nabal, a foolish man whose name means "fool."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Names in the Bible can represent human hopes, divine revelations, or are used to illustrate prophecies. See also <a href="/wiki/Theophory" class="mw-redirect" title="Theophory">Theophory</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="There are three references by Lockyer (February 2019)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
