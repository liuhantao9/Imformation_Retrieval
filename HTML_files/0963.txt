<p><b>Lieutenant (junior grade)</b>, commonly abbreviated as <b>LTJG</b> or, historically, <b>Lt.&#160;(j.g.)</b> (as well as variants of both abbreviations), is a junior <a href="/wiki/Commissioned_officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Commissioned officer">commissioned officer</a> <a href="/wiki/Military_rank" title="Military rank">rank</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Public_Health_Service_Commissioned_Corps" title="United States Public Health Service Commissioned Corps">United States Public Health Service Commissioned Corps</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration_Commissioned_Officer_Corps" class="mw-redirect" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration Commissioned Officer Corps">National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration Commissioned Officer Corps</a> (NOAA Corps). LTJG has a US military  <a href="/wiki/Uniformed_services_pay_grades_of_the_United_States#Officer_pay_grades" title="Uniformed services pay grades of the United States">pay grade of O-2</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-ranks_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ranks-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-paygrades_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paygrades-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/Ranks_and_insignia_of_NATO_navies%27_officers" title="Ranks and insignia of NATO navies&#39; officers">NATO rank code of OF-1a</a>. The rank is also used in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Maritime_Service" title="United States Maritime Service">United States Maritime Service</a>. The NOAA Corps's predecessors, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_and_Geodetic_Survey_Corps" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Coast and Geodetic Survey Corps">United States Coast and Geodetic Survey Corps</a> (1917–1965) and the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_Science_Services_Administration_Corps" class="mw-redirect" title="Environmental Science Services Administration Corps">Environmental Science Services Administration Corps</a> or ESSA Corps (1965–1970), also used the rank.
</p><p>Lieutenant, junior grade, ranks above <a href="/wiki/Ensign_(rank)" title="Ensign (rank)">ensign</a> and below <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_(navy)" title="Lieutenant (navy)">lieutenant</a> and is equivalent to a <a href="/wiki/First_lieutenant" title="First lieutenant">first lieutenant</a> in the other <a href="/wiki/Uniformed_services_of_the_United_States" title="Uniformed services of the United States">uniformed services</a> (the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">Army</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">Marine Corps</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">Air Force</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Sub-lieutenant" title="Sub-lieutenant">sub-lieutenant</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> and the navies of many <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth countries</a>.
</p><p>Promotion to LTJG is governed by Department of Defense policies derived from the <a href="/wiki/Defense_Officer_Personnel_Management_Act" title="Defense Officer Personnel Management Act">Defense Officer Personnel Management Act</a> of 1980. DOPMA guidelines suggest all "fully qualified" <a href="/wiki/Ensign_(rank)#United_States" title="Ensign (rank)">ensigns</a> should be promoted to LTJG. The time for promotion to LTJG is a minimum of two years after commissioning in the Navy or 18 months in the Coast Guard. Lieutenants, junior grade typically lead <a href="/wiki/Petty_officer" title="Petty officer">petty officers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Enlisted_rank" title="Enlisted rank">non-rated personnel</a>, unless assigned to small aircraft or on <a href="/wiki/Staff_officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Staff officer">staff duty</a>. A LTJG's usual shipboard billet is as a <a href="/wiki/Division_officer" title="Division officer">division officer</a>.
</p><p>Lieutenant, junior grade is often referred to colloquially as JG. Prior to March 3, 1883, this rank was known in the U.S. Navy as <a href="/wiki/Master_(naval)" title="Master (naval)">master</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
