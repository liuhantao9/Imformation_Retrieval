<p>The <b>flag of Suriname</b> is formed by five horizontal bands of green (top, double width), white, red (quadruple width), white, and green (double width). There is a large, yellow, five-pointed star centered in the red band. 
</p><p>The flag was adopted on 25 November 1975, upon the independence of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. The star represents the unity of all ethnic groups, the red stripe stands for progress and love,  the green for hope and fertility, and the white bands for peace and justice.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flag_before_1975">Flag before 1975</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Flag before 1975">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The pre-independence flag adopted in 1959 consisted of five colored stars connected by an ellipse. The colored stars represented the major ethnic groups that comprise the Surinamese population: the original <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_in_Suriname" title="Indigenous peoples in Suriname">Amerindians</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Surinamese" title="Dutch Surinamese">Dutch Surinamese</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Afro-Surinamese" title="Afro-Surinamese">Afro-Surinamese</a> descendants of Africans brought in as slaves to work in the plantations, and the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Surinamese" title="Indo-Surinamese">Indo-Surinamese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Javanese_Surinamese" title="Javanese Surinamese">Javanese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Surinamese" title="Chinese Surinamese">Chinese</a> whose ancestors came as indentured workers following the abolition of slavery. The ellipse represented the harmonious relationship amongst the groups.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> There was also a flag for use by the Governor, based on the national flag. 
</p>
