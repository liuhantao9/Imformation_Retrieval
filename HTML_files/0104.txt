<p><b>Julio José Iglesias de la Cueva</b> (<small>Spanish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Spanish" title="Help:IPA/Spanish">[ˈxuljo iˈɣlesjas]</a></span>; born 23 September 1943) is a Spanish singer, songwriter and former professional footballer. Iglesias is recognized as the most commercially successful Continental European singer in the world and one of the top ten record sellers in music history, having sold more than 200 million records worldwide in 14 languages. It is estimated that during his career he has performed in more than 5000 concerts, for over 60 million people in five continents. In April 2013, Iglesias was inducted into the Hall of Fame of Latin Composers.
</p><p>In 1983, he was celebrated as having recorded songs in the most languages in the world, and in 2013 for being the <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_Latin_music_artists" title="List of best-selling Latin music artists">Latin artist with the most records sold</a> in history. In April 2013 he was awarded in Beijing as the most popular international artist in China. In Brazil, France, Romania, Italy, and others, Iglesias is the most successful foreign record seller, while in his home country, Spain, he has sold the most records in history, with 23 million records.
</p><p>During his career, Iglesias has won many awards in the music industry, including the <a href="/wiki/Grammy" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy">Grammy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latin_Grammy" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin Grammy">Latin Grammy</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Music_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="World Music Award">World Music Award</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Music_Award" title="Billboard Music Award">Billboard Music Award</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="American Music Award">American Music Award</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lo_Nuestro_Awards" title="Lo Nuestro Awards">Lo Nuestro Award</a>. He has been awarded the Gold Medal for Merit in the Fine Arts of Spain and the <a href="/wiki/Legion_of_Honour" title="Legion of Honour">Legion of Honour</a> of France. <a href="/wiki/UNICEF" title="UNICEF">UNICEF</a> named him Special Ambassador for the Performing Arts in 1989. He has been a star on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a> since 1985.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Julio_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Iglesias was born in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> to <a href="/wiki/Julio_Iglesias_Sr." title="Julio Iglesias Sr.">Julio Iglesias Sr.</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Medical_doctor" class="mw-redirect" title="Medical doctor">medical doctor</a> from <a href="/wiki/Ourense" title="Ourense">Ourense</a> who became one of the youngest <a href="/wiki/Gynecologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Gynecologist">gynecologists</a> in the country, and María del Rosario de la Cueva y Perignat. Iglesias's paternal ancestry comes from <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> his paternal grandparents were named Manuela Puga Noguerol  and Ulpiano Iglesias Sarria. His maternal grandparents were José de la Cueva y Orejuela (1887–1955),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  and Dolores de Perignat y Ruiz de Benavides,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> who was a native of <a href="/wiki/Guayama,_Puerto_Rico" title="Guayama, Puerto Rico">Guayama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The name "Iglesias" translates as "churches;" likewise, Iglesias is of <a href="/wiki/Jew" class="mw-redirect" title="Jew">Jewish</a> ancestry from his maternal side, having mentioned that his mother's family name, "de la Cueva y Perignat," meaning literally "of the cave" and referring to Jewish people in hiding, is a very common Jewish name.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He alternated playing professional football with studying law at the <a href="/wiki/CEU_San_Pablo_University" class="mw-redirect" title="CEU San Pablo University">CEU San Pablo University</a> in Madrid. In the earliest years of his young adulthood, he was a goalkeeper for <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_Castilla" title="Real Madrid Castilla">Real Madrid Castilla</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Segunda_Divisi%C3%B3n" title="Segunda División">Segunda División</a>. His professional football career was ruined when he was involved in a serious automobile accident, due to which he was unable to walk for two years.<sup id="cite_ref-ElSoldeCuervanaca_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ElSoldeCuervanaca-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Afterwards, he said of those years, "I had more courage and attitude than talent." These were sorely tested when he was involved in that car crash—it smashed his lower spine; his legs were permanently weakened as a direct result, and they still required therapy several years later. During his hospitalization after the accident, a nurse named Eladio Magdaleno gave him a guitar so that he could recover the dexterity of his hands.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In learning to play, he discovered his musical talent.<sup id="cite_ref-People-1988_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-People-1988-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Julio-Enrique_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Julio-Enrique-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> After his rehabilitation, Iglesias studied for three months at <a href="/wiki/Bell_Educational_Trust" title="Bell Educational Trust">Bell Educational Trust's Language School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cambridge,_UK" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge, UK">Cambridge, UK</a>. After that, he went back to obtain his law degree at <a href="/wiki/Complutense_University_of_Madrid" title="Complutense University of Madrid">Complutense University of Madrid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In 1968, he won the <a href="/wiki/Benidorm_International_Song_Festival" title="Benidorm International Song Festival">Benidorm International Song Festival</a>, a songwriter's event in Spain, with the song <i>"La vida sigue igual"</i> (meaning "Life Goes On The Same") which was used in the film <i>La vida sigue igual,</i> about his own life. After this event he signed a deal with Discos Columbia, the Spanish branch of the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia Records</a> company, and released his first studio album, titled <i>Yo Canto,</i> or <i>I Sing.</i> The
album spent 15 weeks in the Spanish charts,
and peaked at #3.<sup id="cite_ref-Julio-Enrique_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Julio-Enrique-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He represented Spain in the 1970 <a href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest_1970" title="Eurovision Song Contest 1970">Eurovision Song Contest</a>, finishing in fourth place<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> behind Ireland's winning entry, performed by <a href="/wiki/Dana_Rosemary_Scallon" title="Dana Rosemary Scallon">Dana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> His entry was the song "<a href="/wiki/Gwendolyne" title="Gwendolyne">Gwendolyne</a>." Shortly after, he had a number one hit in many European countries with "Un Canto A Galicia," sung in <a href="/wiki/Galician_language" title="Galician language">Galician</a>, in honour of his father, who hailed from <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a>. That single sold 1 million copies in Germany. In 1975, he found success in the Italian market by recording a song exclusively in Italian, called "Se mi lasci non vale," or "If You Leave Me, It Can't Be." Notable albums from this decade are <i><a href="/wiki/A_flor_de_piel_(album)" title="A flor de piel (album)">A flor de piel</a></i> (1974, with the European hit "Manuela"), <i><a href="/wiki/El_amor_(Julio_Iglesias_album)" title="El amor (Julio Iglesias album)">El amor</a></i> (1975), and <i><a href="/wiki/Soy_(Julio_Iglesias_album)" title="Soy (Julio Iglesias album)">Soy</a></i> (1973). He also sang in French; one of his popular songs in this language became "Je n'ai pas changé."
</p><p>In 1979, he moved to <a href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" class="mw-redirect" title="Miami, Florida">Miami, Florida</a>, in the United States, signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/CBS_Corporation" title="CBS Corporation">CBS International</a>, and started singing in different languages such as English, French, Portuguese, German and other languages to his music. Two years later, in 1981, he released the album <i><a href="/wiki/De_ni%C3%B1a_a_mujer" title="De niña a mujer">De niña a mujer</a>,</i> which he dedicated to his daughter. (She shared the cover photo with him.) From it came the first English-language hit of his career, a Spanish cover of "<a href="/wiki/Begin_the_Beguine" title="Begin the Beguine">Begin the Beguine</a>" which became number 1 in the United Kingdom; he also released a collection, <i><a href="/wiki/Julio_(album)" title="Julio (album)">Julio</a></i> (1983). In 1984, he released <i><a href="/wiki/1100_Bel_Air_Place" title="1100 Bel Air Place">1100 Bel Air Place</a></i>, the hit album which established him as a star in the English-speaking entertainment industry. It sold over three million albums in the United States alone.<sup id="cite_ref-People-1988_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-People-1988-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The first single, "<a href="/wiki/To_All_the_Girls_I%27ve_Loved_Before" title="To All the Girls I&#39;ve Loved Before">To All the Girls I've Loved Before</a>," a duet with <a href="/wiki/Willie_Nelson" title="Willie Nelson">Willie Nelson</a>, hit #1 on the Country charts and went Top Five in the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It also featured "<a href="/wiki/All_of_You_(Julio_Iglesias_and_Diana_Ross_song)" title="All of You (Julio Iglesias and Diana Ross song)">All of You</a>," in vocal duet with <a href="/wiki/Diana_Ross" title="Diana Ross">Diana Ross</a>, a Top Twenty Pop hit, that climbed to #2 on the Adult Contemporary Chart with the help of a popular video.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1984, he had recorded and released the previously mentioned duets with <a href="/wiki/Diana_Ross" title="Diana Ross">Diana Ross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Willie_Nelson" title="Willie Nelson">Willie Nelson</a>. Iglesias won a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Latin_Pop_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Latin Pop Album">Grammy Award for Best Latin Pop Album</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Awards_of_1988" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammy Awards of 1988">1988 Grammy Awards</a> for the album <i><a href="/wiki/Un_hombre_solo" title="Un hombre solo">Un hombre solo</a></i> ("A Man Alone"). He recorded a duet with <a href="/wiki/Stevie_Wonder" title="Stevie Wonder">Stevie Wonder</a>, "<a href="/wiki/My_Love_(Julio_Iglesias_song)" title="My Love (Julio Iglesias song)">My Love</a>", in his <i><a href="/wiki/Non_Stop_(Julio_Iglesias_album)" title="Non Stop (Julio Iglesias album)">Non Stop</a></i> album, a crossover success in 1988. In the 1990s, Iglesias returned to his original Spanish melody in <i><a href="/wiki/Tango_(Julio_Iglesias_album)" title="Tango (Julio Iglesias album)">Tango</a></i> (1996), nominated for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Latin_Pop_Album" title="Grammy Award for Best Latin Pop Album">Best Latin Pop Album</a> at the 1998 Grammy Awards, losing to the <i><a href="/wiki/Romances_(Luis_Miguel_album)" title="Romances (Luis Miguel album)">Romances</a></i> album by Mexican singer, <a href="/wiki/Luis_Miguel" title="Luis Miguel">Luis Miguel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Iglesias made a cameo appearance as himself on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Golden_Girls" title="The Golden Girls">The Golden Girls</a></i> as <a href="/wiki/Sophia_Petrillo" title="Sophia Petrillo">Sophia Petrillo</a>'s date on St. Valentine's Day 1989. In 1995, Iglesias was the recipient of the <a href="/wiki/Lo_Nuestro_Excellence_Award" title="Lo Nuestro Excellence Award">Excellence Award</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Premio_Lo_Nuestro_1995" title="Premio Lo Nuestro 1995">1995 Lo Nuestro Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Winners-archive_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Winners-archive-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 2001, Iglesias was recognized as the <a href="/wiki/Latin_Recording_Academy_Person_of_the_Year" title="Latin Recording Academy Person of the Year">Person of the Year</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Latin_Recording_Academy" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin Recording Academy">Latin Recording Academy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Iglesias_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iglesias-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> A year later, Iglesias was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/International_Latin_Music_Hall_of_Fame" title="International Latin Music Hall of Fame">International Latin Music Hall of Fame</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2003, Julio released his album <i><a href="/wiki/Divorcio" title="Divorcio">Divorcio</a></i> ("Divorce"). In its first day of sales, <i>Divorcio</i> sold a record 350,000 albums in Spain, and reached the number 1 spot on the charts in Spain, Portugal, France, Italy, and Russia. In 2003 and 2004, aided by the success of his <i>Divorcio</i> album, Iglesias went on a ten-month world tour which took him from Europe to Asia and then on to North America, South America and Africa. More than half the shows on that tour sold out within days of going on sale. In December 2004, his Dutch girlfriend Miranda Rijnsburger and Iglesias himself recorded a duet of the Christmas song "<a href="/wiki/Silent_Night_(song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Silent Night (song)">Silent Night</a>". The song, which was not officially released, also included a voice message from Iglesias, Rijnsburger, and their 4 young children of that time. The song was released online through the singer's official website and a CD was included on their Christmas card as a holiday gift from the Iglesias family to their friends and fans around the world.
</p><p>In 2008, Iglesias recorded another song as a gift to his fans. The family recorded "The Little Drummer Boy" in Spanish and English and included it in the family's Christmas card. Iglesias also made investments in the Dominican Republic's eastern town of <a href="/wiki/Punta_Cana" title="Punta Cana">Punta Cana</a>, a major tourist destination, where he took to spending most of the year. Indeed, Iglesias became a Dominican citizen in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2006, Iglesias released a new English-language album, which he titled <i><a href="/wiki/Romantic_Classics" title="Romantic Classics">Romantic Classics</a>.</i> "I've chosen songs from the 1960s, 1970s and 1980s that I believe will come to be regarded as the new standards," Iglesias stated in the album's sleeve notes. The album featured his interpretations of <a href="/wiki/Foreigner_(band)" title="Foreigner (band)">Foreigner</a>'s "I Want to Know What Love Is," the <a href="/wiki/Wham!" title="Wham!">Wham!</a> selection "Careless Whisper," and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Marx" title="Richard Marx">Richard Marx</a>'s "Right Here Waiting." <i>Romantic Classics</i> was Iglesias's highest debut on the Billboard charts, entering at number 31 in the United States, 21 in Canada, 10 in Australia, and top spots across Europe and Asia. He returned to the studio to record songs in <a href="/wiki/Philippine_languages" title="Philippine languages">Filipino</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Indonesian</a> for his Asian releases of <i>Romantic Classics</i> which helped propel record sales in the Asian entertainment industry. Iglesias promoted <i>Romantic Classics</i> in 2006; it was seen all over the world on television shows. In the United States, for example, he appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_With_The_Stars" class="mw-redirect" title="Dancing With The Stars">Dancing With The Stars</a>,</i> where he sang his version of "I Want To Know What Love Is," <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_View_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The View (U.S. TV series)">The View</a>,</i> <i><a href="/wiki/Fox_and_Friends" class="mw-redirect" title="Fox and Friends">Fox and Friends</a>,</i> and <i>Martha Stewart.</i>
</p><p>In 2008, Iglesias made a music video with <a href="/wiki/Gulnara_Karimova" title="Gulnara Karimova">Gulnara Karimova</a>, the daughter of Uzbek dictator <a href="/wiki/Islam_Karimov" title="Islam Karimov">Islam Karimov</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pizano_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pizano-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2011, the artist launched a new studio album called <i><a href="/wiki/1_(Julio_Iglesias_album)" title="1 (Julio Iglesias album)">1</a></i> (or <i><a href="/wiki/1_(Julio_Iglesias_album)" title="1 (Julio Iglesias album)">Numero 1</a></i>).
</p><p>Iglesias's performance of the song <i>"<a href="/wiki/La_Mer_(song)" title="La Mer (song)">La Mer</a>"</i> ("The Sea") is featured in the soundtrack of the 2011 film <i><a href="/wiki/Tinker_Tailor_Soldier_Spy_(film)" title="Tinker Tailor Soldier Spy (film)">Tinker, Tailor, Soldier, Spy</a>.</i> The performance comes from a live album, which had gone out of print by the last days of December 2015, that he recorded live at the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Olympia" class="mw-redirect" title="Paris Olympia">Olympia</a> theater in Paris in 1976. At the beginning and the end of the recording, Iglesias introduces the song, and his backing musicians, in fluent French.
</p><p>In October 2012, Iglesias performed a concert in <a href="/wiki/Equatorial_Guinea" title="Equatorial Guinea">Equatorial Guinea</a>, where tickets were reportedly $1,000 each.<sup id="cite_ref-Pizano_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pizano-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 April 2013, in Beijing, he received two historic awards: <b>First &amp; Most Popular International Artist of All Time in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>,</b> an award given by Sony Music China and which was presented to Julio by the world-renowned Chinese artist <a href="/wiki/Lang_Lang" title="Lang Lang">Lang Lang</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Guinness_World_Records" title="Guinness World Records">Guinness World Records</a> for the Best-selling Male Latin Artist.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> As Iglesias is a composer and lyricist, some of his songs<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Avoid_weasel_words" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Avoid weasel words"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too vague attribution or weasel words. (December 2015)">which?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> being of his own authorship and composition, on 23 April 2013, he was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Latin_Songwriters_Hall_of_Fame" title="Latin Songwriters Hall of Fame">Latin Songwriters Hall of Fame</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Armando_Manzanero" title="Armando Manzanero">Armando Manzanero</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Feliciano" title="José Feliciano">José Feliciano</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, as was announced,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (December 2015)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Iglesias was slated to perform a concert (complete show) for the first time with his son <a href="/wiki/Julio_Iglesias_Jr." title="Julio Iglesias Jr.">Julio Iglesias Jr.</a> in a tour in Romania, on 22 May at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Polyvalent_Hall_(Cluj-Napoca)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Polyvalent Hall (Cluj-Napoca) (page does not exist)">Sala Polivalentă</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cluj-Napoca" title="Cluj-Napoca">Cluj-Napoca</a> and 2 July at <a href="/wiki/Sala_Palatului" title="Sala Palatului">Sala Palatului</a> in the capital city, <a href="/wiki/Bucharest" title="Bucharest">Bucharest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Berklee College of Music, the most important private university of music in the world, named Julio Iglesias Honorary Doctor, in Boston, on May 9, 2015, in recognition of his achievements and influence in music, and for his enduring contribution to American and international culture.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Julio_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 29 January 1971, Iglesias married <a href="/wiki/Isabel_Preysler" title="Isabel Preysler">Isabel Preysler</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Filipina</a> journalist and <a href="/wiki/Television_host" class="mw-redirect" title="Television host">television host</a>. Preysler, a <a href="/wiki/Filipino_people_of_Spanish_ancestry" title="Filipino people of Spanish ancestry">Filipino of Spanish ancestry</a>, was also a member of the wealthy and aristocratic Pérez de Tagle family. The couple had three children: <a href="/wiki/Chabeli_Iglesias" title="Chabeli Iglesias">Chábeli</a> (born 3 September 1971), a socialite; <a href="/wiki/Julio_Iglesias_Jr." title="Julio Iglesias Jr.">Julio Jr.</a> (born 25 February 1973), a singer; and <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Iglesias" title="Enrique Iglesias">Enrique</a> (born 8 May 1975), an internationally successful singer-songwriter, actor, and record producer. In the 1970s, Iglesias and his family were extensively depicted on the front pages of international newspapers and magazines. The marriage of Iglesias and Preysler ended in divorce in 1979.
</p><p>Whenever Iglesias was not on tour, he spent his time at his Miami residence, purchased in 1978 for $650,000. The mansion on the private <a href="/wiki/Indian_Creek,_Florida" title="Indian Creek, Florida">Indian Creek Island</a>, whose interior design was made by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Virginia_Sipl&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Virginia Sipl (page does not exist)">Virginia Sipl</a> "LA FLACA",  was placed on the market in 2006 for a quoted $28 million, making it one of "Ten Most Expensive Homes in the South" in 2006 according to <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After his divorce, Iglesias lived with Dutch model <a href="/w/index.php?title=Miranda_Rijnsburger&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Miranda Rijnsburger (page does not exist)">Miranda Rijnsburger</a> (b. 1965), 22 years his junior, whom he married on 26 August 2010 in a small church in <a href="/wiki/Marbella,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Marbella, Spain">Marbella, Spain</a>. They had five children: Miguel Alejandro Iglesias (born 7 September 1997), Rodrigo Iglesias (born 3 April 1999), twins Cristina and Victoria Iglesias (born 1 May 2001), and Guillermo Iglesias (born 5 May 2007). They took up residence in the Dominican Republic, where Iglesias had acquired several hotel complexes, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Punta_Cana_International_Airport" title="Punta Cana International Airport">Punta Cana International Airport</a>, which he acquired jointly with other investors, including fashion designer <a href="/wiki/Oscar_de_la_Renta" title="Oscar de la Renta">Oscar de la Renta</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ElSoldeCuervanaca_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ElSoldeCuervanaca-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 19 December 2005, Iglesias's father, Julio Iglesias Sr., died of a heart attack at the age of 90. A week before his death, it became known that the 42-year-old wife of Julio Iglesias Sr., Ronna Keith, was pregnant with their second child. Their first child, Jaime, was born on 18 May 2004. The second child, Ruth, was born on 26 July 2006.
</p><p>In 2008, after his house in Indian Creek did not sell at his asking price, he ordered it razed and said he planned to build another on the lot.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, he purchased the property next door for $15 million and announced that he planned to build a new home on the combined properties.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019 he was declared as the biological father of a 43 year old Spaniard after having a short affair with the mother. The ruling in Spain is a victory for former ballerina Maria Edite Santos and her son Javier Sánchez, 43, who began the paternity claim three decades ago.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Julio_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Punk band <a href="/wiki/The_Dead_Milkmen" title="The Dead Milkmen">The Dead Milkmen's</a> song "Now Everybody's Me" contains a reference to Julio, and the singer of the song claiming to have sang a duet with Julio entitled, "Me and Julio Down By the Crackhouse".</li>
<li>He appeared as the date of Sophia Petrillo (<a href="/wiki/Estelle_Getty" title="Estelle Getty">Estelle Getty</a>) for the Valentine's day episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Golden_Girls" title="The Golden Girls">The Golden Girls</a></i>.</li>
<li>He is mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Gary_Busey" title="Gary Busey">Gary Busey's</a> character Eddie Lomax in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Firm_(1993_film)" title="The Firm (1993 film)">The Firm</a></i>, when questioned who supplied him with the information against the titular firm, he sarcastically replies , "Julio Iglesias" before being shot.</li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Julio_Iglesias&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
