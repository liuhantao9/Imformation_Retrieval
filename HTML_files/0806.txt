<p><b>John Fitzgerald Kennedy Jr.</b> (November 25, 1960 – July 16, 1999) was an American lawyer, journalist, and magazine publisher. He was a son of President <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/First_Lady_of_the_United_States" title="First Lady of the United States">First Lady</a> <a href="/wiki/Jacqueline_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Jacqueline Kennedy">Jacqueline Kennedy</a>, and a younger brother of <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Kennedy" title="Caroline Kennedy">Caroline Kennedy</a>. His father <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_John_F._Kennedy" title="Assassination of John F. Kennedy">was assassinated</a> three days before his third birthday.
</p><p>Beyond his childhood years at the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a>, Kennedy was the subject of much media scrutiny, and became a popular social figure in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>. Trained as a lawyer, he worked as a New York City assistant district attorney for almost four years. In 1995, he launched <i><a href="/wiki/George_(magazine)" title="George (magazine)">George</a></i> magazine, using his political and celebrity status to publicize it. He <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Jr._plane_crash" title="John F. Kennedy Jr. plane crash">died in a plane crash</a> in 1999 at the age of 38.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Two_JFKs.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Two_JFKs.jpg/170px-Two_JFKs.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="249" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Two_JFKs.jpg/255px-Two_JFKs.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Two_JFKs.jpg/340px-Two_JFKs.jpg 2x" data-file-width="760" data-file-height="1111" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Two_JFKs.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>JFK Jr. with <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">his father</a> at the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a> at age two</div></div></div>
<p>John Fitzgerald Kennedy Jr. was born at <a href="/wiki/MedStar_Georgetown_University_Hospital" title="MedStar Georgetown University Hospital">MedStar Georgetown University Hospital</a> on November 25, 1960, two weeks after his father, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> Senator <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> was <a href="/wiki/1960_United_States_presidential_election" title="1960 United States presidential election">elected president</a>. His father took office exactly eight weeks after John Jr. was born. His parents had a stillborn daughter named Arabella four years before John Jr.'s birth. John Jr. had an older sister, <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Kennedy" title="Caroline Kennedy">Caroline</a>, and a younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Bouvier_Kennedy" title="Patrick Bouvier Kennedy">Patrick</a>, who died two days after his premature birth in 1963. His putative nickname, "John-John," came from a reporter who misheard JFK calling him "John" twice in quick succession; the name was not used by his family.<sup id="cite_ref-CNN_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>John Jr. lived in the <a href="/wiki/White_House" title="White House">White House</a> during the first three years of his life and remained in the public spotlight as a young adult. His father was <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_John_F._Kennedy" title="Assassination of John F. Kennedy">assassinated</a> on November 22, 1963, and the <a href="/wiki/State_funeral_of_John_F._Kennedy" title="State funeral of John F. Kennedy">state funeral</a> was held three days later, on John Jr.'s third birthday. In a moment that became an iconic image of the 1960s, John Jr. stepped forward and rendered a final salute as his father's flag-draped casket was carried out from <a href="/wiki/Cathedral_of_St._Matthew_the_Apostle" class="mw-redirect" title="Cathedral of St. Matthew the Apostle">St. Matthew's Cathedral</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FamousPictures_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FamousPictures-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The family continued with their plans for a birthday party to demonstrate that the Kennedys would go on despite the death of the president.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After President Kennedy was assassinated in 1963, Jacqueline Kennedy moved her family, after brief residency in the Georgetown area of Washington, to a luxury apartment on the <a href="/wiki/Upper_East_Side" title="Upper East Side">Upper East Side</a> of <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> in New York City, where Kennedy Jr. grew up. In 1967, his mother took him and Caroline on a six-week "sentimental journey" to Ireland, where they met <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ireland" title="President of Ireland">President</a> <a href="/wiki/%C3%89amon_de_Valera" title="Éamon de Valera">Éamon de Valera</a> and visited the Kennedy ancestral home in Dunganstown.<sup id="cite_ref-Heymann145146_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heymann145146-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Mother.27s_remarriage"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Mother's_remarriage">Mother's remarriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mother&#039;s remarriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After his uncle <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Assassination of Robert F. Kennedy">Robert was assassinated</a> in 1968, his mother took him and his sister out of the United States, saying: "If they're killing Kennedys, then my children are targets&#160;... I want to get out of this country."<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_obit_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_obit-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The same year, she married Greek shipping tycoon <a href="/wiki/Aristotle_Onassis" title="Aristotle Onassis">Aristotle Onassis</a>, and the family went to live on his private island of <a href="/wiki/Skorpios" title="Skorpios">Skorpios</a>. Kennedy is said to have considered his stepfather "a joke."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1971, Kennedy returned to the White House with his mother and sister for the first time since the assassination. President <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">Richard Nixon</a>'s daughters gave Kennedy a tour that included his old bedroom, and Nixon showed him the <a href="/wiki/Resolute_desk" title="Resolute desk"><i>Resolute</i> desk</a> under which his father had let him play. When Onassis died in 1975, he left Kennedy $25,000, though Jacqueline was able to renegotiate the will, and acquired $20 million for herself and her children.<sup id="cite_ref-BaltimoreSun_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BaltimoreSun-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kennedy attended private schools in Manhattan, starting at <a href="/wiki/Saint_David%27s_School_(New_York_City)" title="Saint David&#39;s School (New York City)">Saint David's School</a> and moving to <a href="/wiki/Collegiate_School_(New_York)" class="mw-redirect" title="Collegiate School (New York)">Collegiate School</a>, which he attended from third through tenth grade.<sup id="cite_ref-Heymann145146_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heymann145146-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He completed his high school education at <a href="/wiki/Phillips_Academy" title="Phillips Academy">Phillips Academy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Andover,_Massachusetts" title="Andover, Massachusetts">Andover, Massachusetts</a>. After graduating, he accompanied his mother on a trip to Africa. He rescued his group while on a pioneering course, which had gotten lost for two days without food or water, and won points for leadership.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1976, Kennedy and his cousin visited an earthquake disaster zone at <a href="/wiki/Rabinal" title="Rabinal">Rabinal</a> in Guatemala, helping with heavy building work and distributing food. The local priest said that they "ate what the people of Rabinal ate and dressed in Guatemalan clothes and slept in tents like most of the earthquake victims," adding that the two "did more for their country's image" in Guatemala "than a roomful of ambassadors."<sup id="cite_ref-Leigh195_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leigh195-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On his sixteenth birthday, Kennedy's Secret Service protection ended<sup id="cite_ref-Leigh137_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leigh137-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and he spent the summer of 1978 working as a wrangler in Wyoming.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  In 1979, the <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Presidential_Library_and_Museum" title="John F. Kennedy Presidential Library and Museum">John F. Kennedy Presidential Library and Museum</a> in Boston was dedicated, and Kennedy made his first major speech, reciting <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Spender" title="Stephen Spender">Stephen Spender</a>'s poem "I Think Continually of Those Who Were Truly Great."<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy attended <a href="/wiki/Brown_University" title="Brown University">Brown University</a>, where he majored in American studies.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> There, he co-founded a student discussion group that focused on contemporary issues such as <a href="/wiki/Apartheid" title="Apartheid">apartheid</a> in South Africa, gun control, and civil rights. Visiting South Africa during a summer break, he was appalled by apartheid, and arranged for U.N. ambassador <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Young" title="Andrew Young">Andrew Young</a> to speak about the topic at Brown.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> By his junior year at Brown, he had moved off campus to live with several other students in a shared house,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and spent time at Xenon, a club owned by <a href="/wiki/Howard_Stein" title="Howard Stein">Howard Stein</a>. Kennedy was initiated into Phi Psi, a local social fraternity that had been the Rhode Island Alpha Chapter of national <a href="/wiki/Phi_Kappa_Psi" title="Phi Kappa Psi">Phi Kappa Psi</a> fraternity until 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He graduated in 1983 with a bachelor's degree in American studies, and then took a break, traveling to India and spending some time at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Delhi" title="University of Delhi">University of Delhi</a>, where he met <a href="/wiki/Mother_Teresa" title="Mother Teresa">Mother Teresa</a>. He also worked with some of the Kennedy special interest projects, including the East Harlem School at Exodus House and Reaching Up.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the 1984 Democratic Convention in San Francisco, Kennedy returned to New York and earned $20,000 a year in a position at the Office of Business Development, where his boss reflected that he worked "in the same crummy cubbyhole as everybody else. I heaped on the work and was always pleased."<sup id="cite_ref-mgross.com_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mgross.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> From 1984 to 1986, he worked for the New York City Office of Business Development and served as deputy director of the 42nd Street Development Corporation in 1986,<sup id="cite_ref-Bly279_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bly279-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> conducting negotiations with developers and city agencies. In 1988, he became a summer associate at Manatt, Phelps, Rothenberg &amp; Phillips, a Los Angeles law firm with strong connections to the Democratic Party. There, Kennedy worked for Charlie Manatt, his uncle <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Ted Kennedy</a>'s law school roommate.<sup id="cite_ref-mgross.com_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mgross.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1989, Kennedy headed Reaching Up, a nonprofit group which provided educational and other opportunities for workers who helped people with disabilities. William Ebenstein, executive director of Reaching Up, said, "He was always concerned with the working poor, and his family always had an interest in helping them."
</p><p>In 1989, Kennedy earned a <a href="/wiki/Juris_Doctor" title="Juris Doctor">J.D.</a> degree from the <a href="/wiki/New_York_University_School_of_Law" title="New York University School of Law">New York University School of Law</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He then failed the New York <a href="/wiki/Bar_exam" class="mw-redirect" title="Bar exam">bar exam</a> twice, before passing on his third try in July 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> After failing the exam for a second time, Kennedy vowed that he would take it continuously until he was ninety-five years old or passed.<sup id="cite_ref-desertnews_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-desertnews-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> If he had failed a third time, he would have been ineligible to serve as a prosecutor in the <a href="/wiki/New_York_County_District_Attorney" title="New York County District Attorney">Manhattan District Attorney's Office</a>, where he worked for the next four years.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On August 29, 1991, Kennedy won his first case as a prosecutor.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the summer of 1992, he worked as a journalist and was commissioned by <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> to write an article about his kayaking expedition to the <a href="/wiki/%C3%85land_Archipelago" class="mw-redirect" title="Åland Archipelago">Åland Archipelago</a>, where he saved one of his friends from the water when his kayak capsized.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He then considered creating a magazine with his friend, public-relations magnate <a href="/wiki/Michael_J._Berman" title="Michael J. Berman">Michael J. Berman</a> – a plan which his mother thought too risky. In his 2000 book <i>The Day John Died</i>, Christopher Andersen wrote that Jacqueline had also worried that her son would die in a plane crash, and asked her longtime companion <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Tempelsman" title="Maurice Tempelsman">Maurice Tempelsman</a> "to do whatever it took to keep John from becoming a pilot".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting">Acting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Acting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Meanwhile, Kennedy had done a bit of acting, which was one of his passions (he had appeared in many plays while at Brown). He expressed interest in acting as a career, but his mother strongly disapproved of it, considering it an unsuitable profession.<sup id="cite_ref-A&amp;E_Biography_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-A&amp;E_Biography-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On August 4, 1985, Kennedy made his New York acting debut in front of an invitation-only audience at the Irish Theater on Manhattan's West Side. Executive director of the Irish Arts Center, Nye Heron, said that Kennedy was "one of the best young actors I've seen in years".<sup id="cite_ref-Bly279_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bly279-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy's director, Robin Saex, stated, "He has an earnestness that just shines through." Kennedy's largest acting role was playing a fictionalized version of himself in the season-eight episode of the sitcom <i><a href="/wiki/Murphy_Brown" title="Murphy Brown">Murphy Brown</a></i>, called "Altered States". In this episode, Kennedy visits Brown at her office, in order to promote a magazine he is publishing.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="George_magazine"><i>George</i> magazine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: George magazine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1995, Kennedy and Michael Berman founded <i><a href="/wiki/George_(magazine)" title="George (magazine)">George</a></i>, a glossy, politics-as-lifestyle and fashion monthly, with Kennedy controlling 50 percent of the shares.<sup id="cite_ref-A&amp;E_Biography_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-A&amp;E_Biography-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>  Kennedy officially launched the magazine at a news conference in Manhattan on September 8, and joked that he had not seen so many reporters in one place since he failed his first bar exam.<sup id="cite_ref-Landau117_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Landau117-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Each issue of the magazine contained an editor's column and interviews written by Kennedy,<sup id="cite_ref-Sumner_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sumner-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> who believed they could make politics "accessible by covering it in an entertaining and compelling way" which would allow "popular interest and involvement" to follow.<sup id="cite_ref-Landau100-102_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Landau100-102-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy did interviews with <a href="/wiki/Louis_Farrakhan" title="Louis Farrakhan">Louis Farrakhan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billy_Graham" title="Billy Graham">Billy Graham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Garth_Brooks" title="Garth Brooks">Garth Brooks</a>, and others.<sup id="cite_ref-Landau100-102_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Landau100-102-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first issue was criticized for its image of <a href="/wiki/Cindy_Crawford" title="Cindy Crawford">Cindy Crawford</a> posing as <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a> in a powdered wig and ruffled shirt. In defense of the cover, Kennedy stated that "political magazines should look like Mirabella."<sup id="cite_ref-Landau99_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Landau99-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 1997, <i><a href="/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine)" title="Vanity Fair (magazine)">Vanity Fair</a></i> had published a profile of New York City mayor <a href="/wiki/Rudy_Giuliani" title="Rudy Giuliani">Rudy Giuliani</a>, claiming that the mayor was sleeping with his press secretary (which both parties denied). Although tempted to follow up on this story, Kennedy decided against it.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
The same month, Kennedy wrote about meeting <a href="/wiki/Mother_Teresa" title="Mother Teresa">Mother Teresa</a>, declaring that the "three days I spent in her presence was the strongest evidence this struggling Catholic has ever had that God exists."<sup id="cite_ref-Sumner_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sumner-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:JFKJr2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/JFKJr2.jpg/220px-JFKJr2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="279" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/JFKJr2.jpg/330px-JFKJr2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/JFKJr2.jpg/440px-JFKJr2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="711" data-file-height="901" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:JFKJr2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kennedy in 1998</div></div></div>
<p>The September 1997 issue of <i>George</i> centered on temptation, and featured two of Kennedy's cousins, <a href="/wiki/Michael_LeMoyne_Kennedy" title="Michael LeMoyne Kennedy">Michael LeMoyne Kennedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_II" title="Joseph P. Kennedy II">Joseph P. Kennedy II</a>. Michael had been accused of having an affair with his children's underaged babysitter, while Joe had been accused by his ex-wife of having bullied her. John declared that both his cousins had become "poster boys for bad behaviour"  –  believed to be the first time a member of the Kennedy family had publicly attacked another Kennedy. He said he was trying to show that press coverage of the pair was unfair, due to them being Kennedys.<sup id="cite_ref-Andersen,_p._316_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Andersen,_p._316-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> But Joe paraphrased John's father by stating, "Ask not what you can do for your cousin, but what you can do for his magazine."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Decline">Decline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Decline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>By early 1997, Kennedy and Berman found themselves locked in a power struggle, which led to screaming matches, slammed doors, and even one physical altercation. Eventually Berman sold his share of the company, and Kennedy took on Berman's responsibilities himself. Though the magazine had already begun to decline in popularity before Berman left, his departure was followed by a rapid drop in sales.<sup id="cite_ref-Heymann438_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heymann438-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/David_Pecker" title="David Pecker">David Pecker</a>, CEO of Hachette Filipacchi Magazines who were partners in <i>George</i>, said the decline was because Kennedy refused to "take risks as an editor, despite the fact that he was an extraordinary risk taker in other areas of his life." Pecker said, "He understood that the target audience for <i>George</i> was the eighteen-to-thirty-four-year-old demographic, yet he would routinely turn down interviews that would appeal to this age group, like <a href="/wiki/Princess_Diana" class="mw-redirect" title="Princess Diana">Princess Diana</a> or <a href="/wiki/John_Gotti_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="John Gotti Jr.">John Gotti Jr.</a>, to interview subjects like <a href="/wiki/Dan_Rostenkowski" title="Dan Rostenkowski">Dan Rostenkowski</a> or <a href="/wiki/V%C3%B5_Nguy%C3%AAn_Gi%C3%A1p" title="Võ Nguyên Giáp">Võ Nguyên Giáp</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-Heymann438_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heymann438-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
Shortly before his death, Kennedy had been planning a series of online chats with the 2000 presidential candidates. <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> was to provide the technology and pay for it while receiving advertising in <i>George</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Blow274_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blow274-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> After his death, the magazine was bought out by Hachette,<sup id="cite_ref-media_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-media-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> but folded in early 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-cnn_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Later_life">Later life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Later life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_activity">Family activity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Family activity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:C30001-29.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/C30001-29.jpg/220px-C30001-29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/C30001-29.jpg/330px-C30001-29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/C30001-29.jpg/440px-C30001-29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="750" data-file-height="495" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:C30001-29.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A June 24, 1985 reception for the <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Presidential_Library_and_Museum" title="John F. Kennedy Presidential Library and Museum">John F. Kennedy Library Foundation</a> held at Senator Ted Kennedy's McLean, Virginia residence. Pictured are Ted Kennedy, President <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a>, First Lady <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Reagan" title="Nancy Reagan">Nancy Reagan</a>, Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis, <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Kennedy" title="Ethel Kennedy">Ethel Kennedy</a>, Caroline Kennedy and John F. Kennedy Jr</div></div></div>
<p>Kennedy addressed the 1988 Democratic National Convention, introducing his uncle, Senator Ted Kennedy. He invoked his father's inaugural address, calling "a generation to public service", and received a two-minute standing ovation.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTimesDeath_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimesDeath-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Republican consultant Richard Viguerie said he did not remember a word of the speech, but remembered "a good delivery" and added, "I think it was a plus for the Democrats and the boy. He is strikingly handsome."<sup id="cite_ref-Wadler1988_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wadler1988-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy participated in his cousin <a href="/wiki/Patrick_J._Kennedy" title="Patrick J. Kennedy">Patrick J. Kennedy</a>'s campaign for a seat in the Rhode Island House of Representatives by visiting the district.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> He sat outside the polling booth and had his picture taken with "would-be" voters. The polaroid ploy worked so well in the campaign that Patrick J. Kennedy used it again in 1994.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While attending <a href="/wiki/Brown_University" title="Brown University">Brown University</a>, Kennedy met Sally Munro, whom he dated for six years, and they visited India in 1983. While he was a student at Brown, he also met <a href="/wiki/Brooke_Shields" title="Brooke Shields">Brooke Shields</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> with whom he was later linked.
</p><p>Kennedy also dated models <a href="/wiki/Cindy_Crawford" title="Cindy Crawford">Cindy Crawford</a> and Julie Baker, as well as actress <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Jessica_Parker" title="Sarah Jessica Parker">Sarah Jessica Parker</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Landau9495_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Landau9495-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> who said she enjoyed dating Kennedy but realized he "was a public domain kind of a guy." Parker claimed to have no idea what "real fame" was until dating Kennedy and felt that she should "apologize for dating him" since it became the "defining factor in the person" she was.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy had known actress <a href="/wiki/Daryl_Hannah" title="Daryl Hannah">Daryl Hannah</a> since their two families had vacationed together in St. Maarten in the early '80s. After meeting again at the wedding of his aunt <a href="/wiki/Lee_Radziwill" title="Lee Radziwill">Lee Radziwill</a> in 1988, they dated for five and a half years, though their relationship was complicated by her feelings for singer <a href="/wiki/Jackson_Browne" title="Jackson Browne">Jackson Browne</a>, with whom she had lived for a time.
</p><p>Also during this time, Kennedy dated Christina Haag. They had known each other as children, and she also attended Brown University.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage">Marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Carolyn_and_John_Kennedy.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Carolyn_and_John_Kennedy.jpg/220px-Carolyn_and_John_Kennedy.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="275" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Carolyn_and_John_Kennedy.jpg/330px-Carolyn_and_John_Kennedy.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/db/Carolyn_and_John_Kennedy.jpg 2x" data-file-width="342" data-file-height="427" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Carolyn_and_John_Kennedy.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Carolyn and John Kennedy at the <a href="/wiki/White_House_Correspondents%27_Dinner" class="mw-redirect" title="White House Correspondents&#39; Dinner">White House Correspondents' Dinner</a>, 1997</div></div></div>
<p>After his relationship with Daryl Hannah ended, Kennedy <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cohabited&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cohabited (page does not exist)">cohabited</a> with <a href="/wiki/Carolyn_Bessette" class="mw-redirect" title="Carolyn Bessette">Carolyn Bessette</a>, who worked in the fashion industry. They were engaged for a year, though Kennedy consistently denied reports of this. On September 21, 1996, they married in a private ceremony on <a href="/wiki/Cumberland_Island" title="Cumberland Island">Cumberland Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> where his sister, Caroline, was <a href="/wiki/Matron_of_honor" class="mw-redirect" title="Matron of honor">matron of honor</a> and his cousin Anthony Radziwill was best man.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The next day, Kennedy's cousin Patrick revealed that the pair had married. When they returned to their Manhattan home, a mass of reporters was on the doorstep. One of them asked Kennedy if he had enjoyed his honeymoon, to which he responded: "Very much." He added "Getting married is a big adjustment for us, and for a private citizen like Carolyn even more so. I ask you to give her all the privacy and room you can."<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>But Carolyn was, in fact, badly disoriented by the constant attention from the paparazzi. The couple was permanently on show, both at fashionable Manhattan events, and on their travels to visit celebrities such as Mariuccia Mandelli and <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Versace" title="Gianni Versace">Gianni Versace</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> She also complained to her friend, journalist Jonathan Soroff, that she could not get a job without being accused of exploiting her fame.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
In June 2019 long time pal of John F. Kennedy Jr., Billy Noonan, released a video tape of the secret wedding that had taken place on the remote Georgian island.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Piloting">Piloting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Piloting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kennedy took flying lessons at the Flight Safety Academy in <a href="/wiki/Vero_Beach,_Florida" title="Vero Beach, Florida">Vero Beach, Florida</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Andersen,_p._316_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Andersen,_p._316-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> In April 1998, he received his pilot's license, which he had aspired to since he was a child.<sup id="cite_ref-Landau117_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Landau117-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The death of his cousin <a href="/wiki/Michael_LeMoyne_Kennedy" title="Michael LeMoyne Kennedy">Michael</a> in a skiing accident<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> prompted John to take a hiatus from his piloting lessons for three months. His sister Caroline hoped this would be permanent, but when he resumed, she did little to stop him.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Jr._plane_crash" title="John F. Kennedy Jr. plane crash">John F. Kennedy Jr. plane crash</a></div>
<p>On July 16, 1999, Kennedy departed from <a href="/wiki/Fairfield_Township,_Essex_County,_New_Jersey" title="Fairfield Township, Essex County, New Jersey">Fairfield</a>, New Jersey, at the controls of his <a href="/wiki/Piper_Saratoga" class="mw-redirect" title="Piper Saratoga">Piper Saratoga</a> light aircraft. He was traveling with his wife <a href="/wiki/Carolyn_Bessette-Kennedy" title="Carolyn Bessette-Kennedy">Carolyn</a> and sister-in-law Lauren Bessette to attend the wedding of his cousin <a href="/wiki/Rory_Kennedy" title="Rory Kennedy">Rory Kennedy</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hyannis_Port" class="mw-redirect" title="Hyannis Port">Hyannis Port</a>, <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> after first dropping Lauren off in Martha's Vineyard. He had purchased the plane on April 28, 1999, from Air Bound Aviation.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Carolyn and Lauren were passengers sitting in the second row of seats.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy had checked in with the <a href="/wiki/Control_tower" class="mw-redirect" title="Control tower">control tower</a> at the Martha's Vineyard Airport, but the plane was reported missing after it failed to arrive on schedule.<sup id="cite_ref-Heymann499_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heymann499-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Officials were not hopeful about finding survivors after aircraft debris and a black suitcase belonging to Bessette were recovered from the Atlantic Ocean.<sup id="cite_ref-WashingtonPost_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WashingtonPost-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a> gave his support to the <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_family" title="Kennedy family">Kennedy family</a> during the search for the three missing passengers.<sup id="cite_ref-WashingtonPost_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WashingtonPost-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 18, a Coast Guard admiral declared an end to hope that Kennedy, his wife and her sister could be found alive.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> On July 19, the fragments of Kennedy's plane were found by the NOAA vessel Rude using side-scan sonar. The next day, Navy divers descended into the 62&#160;°F (17&#160;°C) water. The divers found part of the shattered plane strewn over a broad area of seabed 120 feet (37 m) below the surface of the Atlantic Ocean.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> The search ended in the late afternoon of July 21, when the three bodies were recovered from the ocean floor by Navy divers and taken by motorcade to the county medical examiner's office.<sup id="cite_ref-washpo_timeline_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washpo_timeline-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> The discovery was made from high-resolution images of the ocean bottom.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Divers found Carolyn and Lauren's bodies near the twisted and broken fuselage while Kennedy's body was still strapped into the pilot's seat.<sup id="cite_ref-Heymann499_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heymann499-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Admiral Richard M. Larrabee of the Coast Guard said that all three bodies were "near and under" the fuselage, still strapped in.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) determined that pilot error was the probable cause of the crash: "Kennedy's failure to maintain control of the airplane during a descent over water at night, which was a result of <a href="/wiki/Spatial_disorientation" title="Spatial disorientation">spatial disorientation</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the evening of July 21, the bodies were autopsied at the county medical examiner's office; the findings revealed that the crash victims had died upon impact. At the same time, the Kennedy and Bessette families announced their plans for memorial services.<sup id="cite_ref-washpo_timeline_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washpo_timeline-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> On July 21, the three bodies were taken from <a href="/wiki/Hyannis,_Massachusetts" title="Hyannis, Massachusetts">Hyannis</a> to <a href="/wiki/Duxbury,_Massachusetts" title="Duxbury, Massachusetts">Duxbury, Massachusetts</a>, where they were cremated in the Mayflower Cemetery crematorium.<sup id="cite_ref-DuxburyClipper_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DuxburyClipper-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> Ted Kennedy favored a public service for John, while Caroline Kennedy insisted on family privacy.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> On the morning of July 22, their ashes were scattered at sea from the Navy <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">destroyer</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Briscoe_(DD-977)" title="USS Briscoe (DD-977)">USS&#160;<i>Briscoe</i></a> off the coast of Martha's Vineyard.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A memorial service was held for Kennedy on July 23, 1999, at the <a href="/wiki/St._Thomas_More_Church_(New_York_City)" title="St. Thomas More Church (New York City)">Church of St. Thomas More</a>, which was a parish that Kennedy had often attended with his mother and sister. The invitation-only service was attended by hundreds of mourners, including President Bill Clinton, who presented the family with photo albums of John and Carolyn on their visit to the White House from the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kennedy's last will and testament stipulated that his personal belongings, property, and holdings were to be "evenly distributed" among his sister Caroline Kennedy's three children, who were among fourteen beneficiaries in his will.<sup id="cite_ref-Heymann499_54-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heymann499-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_F._Kennedy_Jr.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:John_F._Kennedy_Jr._memorial_wall.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/John_F._Kennedy_Jr._memorial_wall.jpg/220px-John_F._Kennedy_Jr._memorial_wall.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="332" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/John_F._Kennedy_Jr._memorial_wall.jpg/330px-John_F._Kennedy_Jr._memorial_wall.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/John_F._Kennedy_Jr._memorial_wall.jpg/440px-John_F._Kennedy_Jr._memorial_wall.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2075" data-file-height="3130" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:John_F._Kennedy_Jr._memorial_wall.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A drawing of three-year-old JFK Jr. saluting his father's coffin, placed on a memorial wall for him shortly after his death</div></div></div>
<p>John F. Kennedy Jr. was the first child ever born to a <a href="/wiki/President-elect_of_the_United_States" title="President-elect of the United States">president-elect of the United States</a>. On the occasion of the 50th anniversary of the assassination of John Jr.'s father on November 22, 1963, the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Daily_News" title="New York Daily News">New York Daily News</a></i> re-ran the famous photograph of the three-year-old John Jr. saluting his father's coffin during the funeral procession. Photographer Dan Farrell, who took the photo, called it "the saddest thing I've ever seen in my whole life".<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2000, Reaching Up, the organization which Kennedy founded in 1989, joined with The City University of New York to establish the John F. Kennedy Jr. Institute.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> On September 19, 2003, the ARCO Forum at Harvard University's <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_School_of_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Kennedy School of Government">Kennedy School of Government</a> was renamed to the John F. Kennedy Jr. Forum of Public Affairs. An active participant in Forum events, Kennedy had been a member of the Senior Advisory Committee of Harvard's Institute of Politics for fifteen years. Kennedy's paternal uncle, Ted, said the renaming symbolically linked Kennedy and his father while his sister, Caroline, stated the renaming represented his love of discussing politics.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
