<p><b>General elections</b> were held in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> on 31 October 1977, the first after independence.<sup id="cite_ref-N1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-N1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The result was a victory for the National Party Combination (an alliance of the <a href="/wiki/National_Party_of_Suriname" title="National Party of Suriname">National Party of Suriname</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Renewed_Progressive_Party&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Renewed Progressive Party (page does not exist)">Renewed Progressive Party</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Party_for_National_Unity_and_Solidarity" title="Party for National Unity and Solidarity">Party for National Unity and Solidarity</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_Progressive_People%27s_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="Suriname Progressive People&#39;s Party">Suriname Progressive People's Party</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> which won 22 of the 39 seats. Voter turnout was 77.8%.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
