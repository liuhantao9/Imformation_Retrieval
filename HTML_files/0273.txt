<p><b>Ghanaian names</b> (or Personal names in Ghana) consists of several given names and surnames based on the language of ethnic groups in Ghana including <a href="/wiki/Akan_people" title="Akan people">Akan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dagomba_people" title="Dagomba people">Mole-Dagombas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ga_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Ga people">Ga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ewe_people" title="Ewe people">Ewe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nzema_people" title="Nzema people">Nzema</a>. Frequently, children are given a "day name" which corresponds to the day in the week they were born. These day names have further meanings concerning the soul and character of the person. Middle names have considerably more variety and can refer to their birth order, twin status, or an ancestor's middle name. These names are also used among Ghanaians living abroad and among Africans living in the diaspora who wish to identify with their ancestral homeland. During the 18th-19th Century, slaves from modern day Ghana in the Caribbean were referred to as <a href="/wiki/Coromantees" class="mw-redirect" title="Coromantees">Coromantees</a>. Most day names among the <a href="/wiki/Dagomba_people" title="Dagomba people">Mole-Dagombas</a> are usually given to the females and few are given to both sexes.
</p><p>Most <a href="/wiki/Ghanaian_people" title="Ghanaian people">Ghanaians</a> have at least one name from this system, even if they also have an Arabic or western name. Notable figures with day names include Ghana's first president <a href="/wiki/Kwame_Nkrumah" title="Kwame Nkrumah">Kwame Nkrumah</a> and former <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> Secretary General <a href="/wiki/Kofi_Annan" title="Kofi Annan">Kofi Annan</a>.
</p>

<p><b>Male</b>
</p>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Sunday" title="Sunday">Sunday</a></b>: Akwasi, Kwasi, Kwesi, Akwesi, Sisi, Kacely, Koshi</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Monday" title="Monday">Monday</a></b>: Kojo,  Kwadwo, Jojo, Joojo, Kujoe</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Tuesday" title="Tuesday">Tuesday</a></b>:Kobla, Kwabena, Kobe, Kobi, Ebo, Komla, Kobby, Kobena,  Kwabla</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Wednesday" title="Wednesday">Wednesday</a></b>: Kwaku, Abeiku, Kuuku, Kweku</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Thursday" title="Thursday">Thursday</a></b>: Yaw, Ekow, Yao</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Friday" title="Friday">Friday</a></b>:  Kofi, Fifi, Fiifi, Yoofi</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Saturday" title="Saturday">Saturday</a></b>: Kwame, Kwamena, Kwamina</li></ul>
<p><b>Female</b>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Sunday</b>: Koshiwa,Akosua, Akasi, Akos, Esi, Awesi, Awisi</li>
<li><b>Monday</b>: Adzo, Adwoa, Adjoa, Adzoa, Adwoma</li>
<li><b>Tuesday</b>:Abla, Abena, Araba, Abenayo</li>
<li><b>Wednesday</b>: Akua, Aku, Kukua, Akuma</li>
<li><b>Thursday</b>: Aba, Yaa, Yawa, Baaba, Awo</li>
<li><b>Friday</b>: Afua, Afia, Afi, Efua</li>
<li><b>Saturday</b>: Ama, Ami</li></ul>
