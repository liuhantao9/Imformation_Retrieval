<p>The <b>Guiana Shield</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is one of the three <a href="/wiki/Craton" title="Craton">cratons</a> of the <a href="/wiki/South_American_Plate" title="South American Plate">South American Plate</a>. It is a 1.7 billion-year-old <a href="/wiki/Precambrian" title="Precambrian">Precambrian</a> geological <a href="/wiki/Shield_(geology)" title="Shield (geology)">formation</a> in northeast <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> that forms a portion of the northern coast.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The higher elevations on the <a href="/wiki/Shield_(geology)" title="Shield (geology)">shield</a> are called the <b>Guiana Highlands</b>, which is where the table-like mountains called <a href="/wiki/Tepui" title="Tepui">tepuis</a> are found. The Guiana Highlands are also the source of some of the world's most spectacular waterfalls such as <a href="/wiki/Angel_Falls" title="Angel Falls">Angel Falls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kaieteur_Falls" title="Kaieteur Falls">Kaieteur Falls</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kuquenan_Falls" class="mw-redirect" title="Kuquenan Falls">Kuquenan Falls</a>.
</p><p>The Guiana Shield underlies <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> (previously British Guiana), <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> (previously Dutch Guiana) and <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a> (or Guyane), much of southern <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, as well as parts of <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. The rocks of the Guiana Shield consist of metasediments and metavolcanics (<a href="/wiki/Greenschist" title="Greenschist">greenstones</a>) overlain by sub-horizontal layers of <a href="/wiki/Sandstones" class="mw-redirect" title="Sandstones">sandstones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quartzites" class="mw-redirect" title="Quartzites">quartzites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shales" class="mw-redirect" title="Shales">shales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conglomerate_(geology)" title="Conglomerate (geology)">conglomerates</a> intruded by sills of younger mafic intrusives such as <a href="/wiki/Gabbro" title="Gabbro">gabbros</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guiana_Shield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The oldest rocks in the shield consist of <a href="/wiki/Archean" title="Archean">Archean</a> Imataca Complex, composed of a <a href="/wiki/Quartz" title="Quartz">quartz</a>-<a href="/wiki/Feldspar" title="Feldspar">feldspar</a> <a href="/wiki/Gneiss" title="Gneiss">gneiss</a> and subordinate <a href="/wiki/Mafic" title="Mafic">mafic</a> gneiss.  The Guri <a href="/wiki/Fault_(geology)" title="Fault (geology)">Fault</a> marks the southern boundary of the complex.  South of that fault are Early <a href="/wiki/Proterozoic" title="Proterozoic">Proterozoic</a> rocks consisting of the <a href="/wiki/Metavolcanic_rock" title="Metavolcanic rock">metavolcanic</a> Pastora <a href="/wiki/Geological_unit" class="mw-redirect" title="Geological unit">Supergroup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Granitic" class="mw-redirect" title="Granitic">granitic</a> <a href="/wiki/Plutonic" class="mw-redirect" title="Plutonic">plutonic</a> Supamo Complex.  The Cuchivero Group consists of <a href="/wiki/Ash_flow" class="mw-redirect" title="Ash flow">ash flow</a> <a href="/wiki/Tuff" title="Tuff">tuff</a> and granitic plutonic rocks.  The Early to Middle Proterozoic Roraima Group consists of continental <a href="/wiki/Clastic_sediment" class="mw-redirect" title="Clastic sediment">clastic sedimentary</a> rocks.  These <a href="/wiki/Precambrian" title="Precambrian">Precambrian</a> sediments include quartz <a href="/wiki/Sandstones" class="mw-redirect" title="Sandstones">sandstones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quartzite" title="Quartzite">quartzites</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Conglomerate_(geology)" title="Conglomerate (geology)">conglomerates</a> presumed to be 1.8 to 1.4 <a href="/wiki/Giga_annum" class="mw-redirect" title="Giga annum">Ga</a> in age.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geomorphology">Geomorphology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guiana_Shield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geomorphology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are three upland areas of the Guiana Shield:
</p>
<ol><li>The Guiana Highlands proper are in Venezuela east of the Orinoco and extend across much of west-central Guyana and into the northern <a href="/wiki/Roraima" title="Roraima">Roraima</a> state in Brazil.</li>
<li>The Tumucumaque Uplands which are a series of central massifs in an arc from the <a href="/wiki/Wilhelmina_Mountains" title="Wilhelmina Mountains">Wilhelmina Mountains</a> of south-central Suriname, along the southern boundary of Suriname and Guyana, forming the Acarai Mountains of Roraima state and the <a href="/wiki/Tumuc-Humac_Mountains" class="mw-redirect" title="Tumuc-Humac Mountains">Tumuc-Humac Mountains</a> of <a href="/wiki/Par%C3%A1" title="Pará">Pará</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amap%C3%A1" title="Amapá">Amapá</a> states of Brazil. From this arc, the southern uplands slope gently downwards towards the Amazon River and the northern uplands slope gently downwards toward the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlantic">Atlantic</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Chiribiquete_Plateau" class="mw-redirect" title="Chiribiquete Plateau">Chiribiquete Plateau</a> is a sandstone topped <a href="/wiki/Plateau" title="Plateau">plateau</a> with an elevation of 900&#160;m (2,953&#160;ft) that forms the western edge of the shield. The plateau is separated from the eastern <a href="/wiki/Andes" title="Andes">Andes</a> by the thick <a href="/wiki/Neogene" title="Neogene">Neogene</a> sediments of the Sub-Andean Trough that runs along the northern and western rim of the Guiana Shield.</li></ol>
<p>The north-central part of the Guiana Highlands is dominated by high flat-topped peaks called <a href="/wiki/Tepui" title="Tepui">tepuis</a>, of the Roraima supergroup and Quasi-Roraima formation, and the rounded granite peaks of the Parguaza and Imataca complexes to the north and southwestern edges of the area. The highest point in the shield is <a href="/wiki/Pico_da_Neblina" title="Pico da Neblina">Pico da Neblina</a> in Brazil at 2,995 metres (9,826&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-ibge2016_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ibge2016-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Pico da Neblina is the highest summit of the larger <a href="/wiki/Neblina_massif" class="mw-redirect" title="Neblina massif">Neblina massif</a>, a highly eroded sandstone plateau that straddles the Venezuela-Brazil border and that has lost the typical tabletop shape of the other tepuis in the region.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guiana_Shield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:H_chimantensis2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/H_chimantensis2.jpg/170px-H_chimantensis2.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/H_chimantensis2.jpg/255px-H_chimantensis2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/H_chimantensis2.jpg/340px-H_chimantensis2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="467" data-file-height="700" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:H_chimantensis2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i><a href="/wiki/Heliamphora_chimantensis" title="Heliamphora chimantensis">Heliamphora chimantensis</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Chimant%C3%A1_Massif" title="Chimantá Massif">Chimantá Massif</a> (a <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuelan</a> part of the Guiana Shield)</div></div></div>
<p>The Guiana Shield is one of the regions of highest <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a> in the world, and has many <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> species. The region houses has over 3000 <a href="/wiki/Vertebrate" title="Vertebrate">vertebrate</a> species: 1168 fresh water fish, 269 amphibians (54% endemics), 295 reptiles (29%), 1004 birds (7.7%), and 282 mammals (11%).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Diversity of invertebrates remains largely undocumented, but there are several species of endemic butterflies and dung beetles.<sup id="cite_ref-IndicativeResponse_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IndicativeResponse-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ViloriaPantepui_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ViloriaPantepui-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Plant life is equally rich and 13,367 species of <a href="/wiki/Vascular_plant" title="Vascular plant">vascular plants</a> have been found, approximately 40% of which is considered endemic.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The shield is overlain by the largest expanse of tropical forest on any Precambrian shield area in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Guianan rain forest is similar in nature to <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Basin" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon Basin">Amazonian</a> rain forest and known protected areas include the Iwokrama Forest of central Guyana, Kaieteur, <a href="/wiki/Kanuku" class="mw-redirect" title="Kanuku">Kanuku</a> National Park of southern Guyana, the UNESCO <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Site" title="World Heritage Site">World Heritage Site</a> <a href="/wiki/Central_Suriname_Nature_Reserve" title="Central Suriname Nature Reserve">Central Suriname Nature Reserve</a> of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Guiana_Amazonian_Park" title="Guiana Amazonian Park">Guiana Amazonian Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Tumucumaque_National_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Tumucumaque National Park">Tumucumaque National Park</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Amap%C3%A1" title="Amapá">Amapá</a> State of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. In Venezuela the forests are protected by <a href="/wiki/Canaima_National_Park" title="Canaima National Park">Canaima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parima_Tapirapec%C3%B3_National_Park" title="Parima Tapirapecó National Park">Parima-Tapirapeco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Serran%C3%ADa_de_la_Neblina_National_Park" title="Serranía de la Neblina National Park">Serranía de la Neblina</a> national parks. In 2014, the Government of Colombia designated a 250 hectare area of the Guaina Shield, as a Ramsar Wetland, thus becoming a protected area of international importance in accordance to the <a href="/wiki/Ramsar_Convention" title="Ramsar Convention">Ramsar Convention</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to recent researches, ecosystems of the Guayana Highlands are in danger because of non-native plant species (including "a well-known <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">invasive</a> plant elsewhere" <i><a href="/wiki/Poa_annua" title="Poa annua">Poa annua</a></i> and "one of the most aggressive weeds" <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Polypogon_elongatus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Polypogon elongatus (page does not exist)">Polypogon elongatus</a></i>) and infectious faecal bacteria <i><a href="/wiki/Helicobacter_pylori" title="Helicobacter pylori">Helicobacter pylori</a></i> introduced by tourists.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
