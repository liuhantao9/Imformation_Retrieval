<p>The <b>Secretariat for Multidimensional Security of the Organization of American States</b> is a part of the <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_American_States" title="Organization of American States">General Secretariat</a>, which is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The Secretariat for Multidimensional Security has a mandate to promote cooperation between <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="Member states of the Organization of American States">Organization's Member States</a>, Inter-American and international organizations, as well as with entities such as the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> and its subsidiaries, in order to analyze, prevent, confront and respond to security threats.
</p><p>The Secretariat is composed of three main directorates and one executive office:
</p>
<ul><li>the Department of Public Security (DPS)</li>
<li>the Executive Secretariat of the Inter-American Drug Abuse Control Commission (CICAD)</li>
<li>the Executive Secretariat of the Inter-American Committee against Terrorism</li>
<li>the Executive Office of the Secretary for Multidimensional Security</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States_Secretariat_for_Multidimensional_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Secretary General <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Miguel_Insulza" title="José Miguel Insulza">José Miguel Insulza</a> of the Organization of American States created the Secretariat for Multidimensional Security in 2005, as a direct response to the 2003 Declaration on Security in the Americas, adopted in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a>. This measure institutionalized the concept of <b>multidimensional security</b>, ensuring that the public and citizens were at the center of the Secretariat's policy discussion.<sup id="cite_ref-OAS_SMS_Secretaria_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OAS_SMS_Secretaria-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The SMS works in close coordination with the Meeting of Ministers Responsible for Public Security in the Americas (MISPA) and the Meeting of Justice Ministers and Attorneys General in the Americas (REMJA).
</p><p>The scope of activity of the SMS is defined by the Declaration on Security in the Americas and its conceptualization of hemispheric security as being multidimensional and comprising traditional threats and new threats, concerns, and challenges to the security of the states of the Hemisphere.
</p><p>The Secretariat's mandates stem from the following documents and political bodies:
</p>
<ul><li>Declaration on Security in the Americas</li>
<li>General Assembly Summit</li>
<li>17 Political and Technical Subsidiary Bodies</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission_statement">Mission statement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States_Secretariat_for_Multidimensional_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Mission statement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style=""><i>The mission of the Secretariat for Multidimensional Security (SMS) is to promote and coordinate cooperation among the OAS Member States and between them and the Inter-American system and other bodies in the international system, in order to assess, prevent, confront, and respond effectively to threats to security, with a view to being the leading point of reference in the Hemisphere for developing cooperation and capacity-building in the OAS Member States.</i></div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">—<b>Secretariat for Multidimensional Security, 2005</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Responsibilities">Responsibilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States_Secretariat_for_Multidimensional_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Responsibilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Secretariat is mandated, as from the 2003 Declaration on Security in the Americas, to coordinate technical assistance and cooperation in the following six areas:<sup id="cite_ref-OAS_SMS_Secretaria_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OAS_SMS_Secretaria-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ol><li>Laws;</li>
<li>Compliance with laws;</li>
<li>The prevention of delinquent activities and drug consumption;</li>
<li>Victims assistance;</li>
<li>Rehabilitation of criminal offenders;</li>
<li>The promotion of peace and security in the Hemisphere.<sup id="cite_ref-OAS_SMS_Secretaria_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OAS_SMS_Secretaria-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ol>
<p>The Secretariat of Multidimensional Security offers solutions founded upon the model of <i>Smart Security</i>. This model requires objective, evidence- based and systemic approaches to the development of proposals and solutions, which respond to national or regional necessities and capacities; from a multidimensional perspective.<sup id="cite_ref-OAS_SMS_Secretaria_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OAS_SMS_Secretaria-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organizational_structure">Organizational structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States_Secretariat_for_Multidimensional_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Organizational structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Secretariat_for_Multidimensional_Security">Secretariat for Multidimensional Security</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States_Secretariat_for_Multidimensional_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Secretariat for Multidimensional Security">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Secretary_for_Multidimensional_Security">Secretary for Multidimensional Security</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States_Secretariat_for_Multidimensional_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Secretary for Multidimensional Security">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The current Secretary for Multidimensional Security at the Organization of American States is Nobel peace prize nominee and former Attorney General of Guatemala Claudia Paz Y Paz. She was the first woman ever to assume to position of Attorney General in her country and has been invaluable developing the rule of law and transforming the justice sector.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="The_Department_of_Public_Security_.28DPS.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Department_of_Public_Security_(DPS)">The Department of Public Security (DPS)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States_Secretariat_for_Multidimensional_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: The Department of Public Security (DPS)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The DPS works with Member States to develop mechanisms to evaluate and strengthen their institutional capacity to address threats to public security. These efforts focus on the following areas: the strengthening of security legislation, the improvement of law enforcement coordination and practices, the prevention of crime and violence, victim assistance and the social reintegration of convicted criminals.<sup id="cite_ref-OAS_DPS_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OAS_DPS-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Department of Public Security's Director is Paulina do Carmo Arruda Vieira Duarte.
</p>
<h4><span id="Executive_Secretariat_of_the_Inter-American_Drug_Abuse_Control_Commission_.28CICAD.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_Secretariat_of_the_Inter-American_Drug_Abuse_Control_Commission_(CICAD)">Executive Secretariat of the Inter-American Drug Abuse Control Commission (CICAD)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States_Secretariat_for_Multidimensional_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Executive Secretariat of the Inter-American Drug Abuse Control Commission (CICAD)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:CICAD_Logo_en.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/CICAD_Logo_en.png/170px-CICAD_Logo_en.png" decoding="async" width="170" height="168" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/CICAD_Logo_en.png/255px-CICAD_Logo_en.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/CICAD_Logo_en.png/340px-CICAD_Logo_en.png 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:CICAD_Logo_en.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>CICAD was founded by the <a href="/wiki/General_Assembly_of_the_Organization_of_American_States" title="General Assembly of the Organization of American States">General Assembly of the Organization of American States</a> in 1986, in <a href="/wiki/Guatemala_City" title="Guatemala City">Guatemala City</a></div></div></div>
<p>The Inter-American Drug Abuse Control Commission (CICAD) is the Western Hemisphere's policy forum for dealing with the <a href="/wiki/Drug" title="Drug">drug</a> problem. The CICAD Executive Secretariat supports the Commission by strengthening the human and institutional capabilities and channeling the collective efforts of its member states to reduce the production, trafficking and use of illegal drugs. The Hemispheric Drug Strategy, approved in May 2010, expresses the firm commitment of Member States to deal with the consequences of drug trade, which poses a growing threat to health, economic development, social cohesion, and the rule of law.<sup id="cite_ref-OAS_CICAD_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OAS_CICAD-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>CICAD was established by the General Assembly of the Organization of American States (OAS) in 1986. Each member government appoints a high-ranking representative to the Commission, which meets twice a year.<sup id="cite_ref-OAS_CICAD_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OAS_CICAD-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>CICAD's Executive Secretary is <a href="/wiki/Paul_E._Simons" title="Paul E. Simons">Paul E. Simons</a>, an <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> diplomat and former <a href="/wiki/US_Ambassador_to_Chile" class="mw-redirect" title="US Ambassador to Chile">US Ambassador to Chile</a>.
</p>
<h4><span id="The_Secretariat_of_the_Inter-American_Committee_against_Terrorism_.28CICTE.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Secretariat_of_the_Inter-American_Committee_against_Terrorism_(CICTE)">The Secretariat of the Inter-American Committee against Terrorism (CICTE)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Organization_of_American_States_Secretariat_for_Multidimensional_Security&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: The Secretariat of the Inter-American Committee against Terrorism (CICTE)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Logo_CICTE.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Logo_CICTE.png/220px-Logo_CICTE.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="224" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Logo_CICTE.png/330px-Logo_CICTE.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Logo_CICTE.png/440px-Logo_CICTE.png 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="612" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Logo_CICTE.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>CICTE's programs include border control, cybersecurity, combating <a href="/wiki/Terrorism_financing" title="Terrorism financing">terrorism financing</a>, immigration, customs, among others.</div></div></div>
<p>The main purpose of the Inter-American Committee against Terrorism (CICTE) is to promote and develop cooperation among Member States to prevent, combat and eliminate <a href="/wiki/Terrorism" title="Terrorism">terrorism</a>, in accordance with the principles of the OAS Charter, with the Inter-American Convention Against Terrorism, and with full respect for the sovereignty of states, the rule of law, and international law, including international humanitarian law, international human rights law, and international refugee law.<sup id="cite_ref-OAS_CICTE_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OAS_CICTE-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>CICTE is composed of all Member States of the OAS and holds one regular session each year as a forum for discussion and decision-making on counter terrorism issues, measures, and cooperation. Member States designate a competent national authority, a principal representative, alternate representatives, and advisors. Member States also appoint one or more National Points of Contact with competence in the field of prevention and elimination of terrorism to serve as the principal liaison among governments of the Member States and with the CICTE Secretariat for developing cooperation programs between them and the CICTE Secretariat.<sup id="cite_ref-OAS_CICTE_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OAS_CICTE-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>CICTE's Executive Secretary is Neil Klopfenstein.
</p>
