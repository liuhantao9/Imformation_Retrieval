	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Beaumont Park</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the Huddersfield suburb. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Beaumont_Park_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Beaumont Park (disambiguation)">Beaumont Park (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Beaumont Park</b> is a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Metropolitan Borough of Kirklees</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> that is located between <a href="/wiki/Netherton,_Huddersfield" class="mw-redirect" title="Netherton, Huddersfield">Netherton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crosland_Moor" title="Crosland Moor">Crosland Moor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lockwood,_Huddersfield" title="Lockwood, Huddersfield">Lockwood</a>.
</p>
<div class="center"><div class="thumb tnone"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:702px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Butternab_%26_Beaumont_Park.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Butternab_%26_Beaumont_Park.jpg/700px-Butternab_%26_Beaumont_Park.jpg" decoding="async" width="700" height="240" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Butternab_%26_Beaumont_Park.jpg/1050px-Butternab_%26_Beaumont_Park.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/10/Butternab_%26_Beaumont_Park.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1400" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Butternab_%26_Beaumont_Park.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>View of the housing area of Beaumont Park, Huddersfield</div></div></div></div>
<p>The housing in this area is situated around the periphery of a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Woodland" title="Woodland">woodland</a> and recreational <a href="/wiki/Park" title="Park">park</a>, known as 'Dungeon Wood' that was bequeathed to the people of Huddersfield in 1879 by the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20111118135936/http://www.kirkheatononlineparishclerk.com/kirkheaton/beaumont.htm">'Beaumont's of Whitley' estate (Henry Fredrick Beaumont</a>, for leisure purposes. Adjacent to the park is Beagle Woods, which is a popular venue for walkers. The wood is home to a <a href="/wiki/Kennel" title="Kennel">kennels</a> with a working pack of English <a href="/wiki/Beagle" title="Beagle">Beagle</a> hounds.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_park">Public park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Beaumont_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Public park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The park was officially opened on 13 October 1883, by <a href="/wiki/Prince_Leopold,_Duke_of_Albany" title="Prince Leopold, Duke of Albany">Prince Leopold</a>, fourth son of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>, and his wife <a href="/wiki/Princess_Helena_of_Waldeck_and_Pyrmont" title="Princess Helena of Waldeck and Pyrmont">Princess Helena of Waldeck and Pyrmont</a> (The Duke and Duchess of Albany). It was Huddersfield's first public park and is a fine example of a <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian</a> park. The park is maintained by Kirklees Council with assistance from a group of volunteers interested in returning the grounds to their former glory, who have instigated many improvements to the park including a new bandstand and water cascades.
</p><p>In 2011 the park was awarded a first 'Green Flag Award' by the Environmental charity <a href="/wiki/Keep_Britain_Tidy" title="Keep Britain Tidy">Keep Britain Tidy</a> organisation and has retained it each subsequent year.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
