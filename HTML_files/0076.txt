<p><i><b>Blown Away</b></i> is the fourth studio album by American singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>. It was released in the United States and Canada on May 1, 2012 by <a href="/wiki/Arista_Nashville" title="Arista Nashville">Arista Nashville</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> After finishing her successful <a href="/wiki/Play_On_Tour" title="Play On Tour">Play On Tour</a> in December 2010, Underwood started working on the album, but took her time, as she "wanted to change things up", and needed to step away from the "celebrity bubble" to "have real things to write about and to sing about". It took her over one year to finish the album, which, according to Underwood, ended up having a "darker storyline" than her previous efforts.
</p><p>Met with mostly positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_critics" class="mw-redirect" title="Music critics">music critics</a>, the album became a commercial success. It debuted at number one on <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, with first-week sales of 267,000 copies, achieving the third-largest opening of 2012. With <i>Blown Away</i> reaching number one, Underwood became the third female to chart at number one on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 with three country albums, tying her with <a href="/wiki/Linda_Ronstadt" title="Linda Ronstadt">Linda Ronstadt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Faith_Hill" title="Faith Hill">Faith Hill</a>. Like her three previous albums, <i>Blown Away</i> debuted at the top of the <a href="/wiki/Top_Country_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Country Albums">Top Country Albums</a> chart. The album remained at number one for a second week on the <i>Billboard</i> 200, becoming her first album to spend more than one week at the top of that chart. It topped the "Top Country Albums" chart for seven weeks.
</p><p><i>Blown Away</i> was the seventh best selling album overall and second best-selling country album of 2012 in the US. It finished 2012 with over 1.4 million copies sold worldwide, according to the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_the_Phonographic_Industry" title="International Federation of the Phonographic Industry">International Federation of the Phonographic Industry</a>. <i>Blown Away</i> has since sold around 1.8 million copies in the US and over two million copies worldwide. In October 2016, the album was certified 2x-Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>.
</p><p>It spawned the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> Top 20 and <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> number-one singles "<a href="/wiki/Good_Girl_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Good Girl (Carrie Underwood song)">Good Girl</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Blown Away (Carrie Underwood song)">Blown Away</a>", along with the Country Airplay top-two hits "<a href="/wiki/Two_Black_Cadillacs" title="Two Black Cadillacs">Two Black Cadillacs</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/See_You_Again_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="See You Again (Carrie Underwood song)">See You Again</a>". All singles received multi-Platinum or Platinum certifications.
</p><p>In 2013, at the <a href="/wiki/55th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="55th Grammy Awards">55th Grammy Awards</a>, Underwood won the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Solo_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Solo Performance">Grammy Award for Best Country Solo Performance</a> for "Blown Away", which also won <a href="/wiki/Best_Country_Song" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Country Song">Best Country Song</a> for its songwriters. The album won the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="American Music Award">American Music Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Award_for_Favorite_Country_Album" title="American Music Award for Favorite Country Album">Favorite Country Album</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blown_Away_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Underwood's <a href="/wiki/Play_On_Tour" title="Play On Tour">Play On Tour</a> ended in December 2010, she started to work on her fourth studio album. Underwood took her time working on <i>Blown Away</i>. She stated that she "wanted to change things up", and she needed to step away from the "celebrity bubble" to "have real things to write about and real things to sing about."<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Nashville" title="Sony Music Nashville">Sony Music Nashville</a> chairman/CEO Gary Overton said that the singer "took nearly a year to compile and record the songs."<sup id="cite_ref-usainterview_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usainterview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Songwriters <a href="/wiki/Chris_Tompkins" title="Chris Tompkins">Chris Tompkins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Kear" title="Josh Kear">Josh Kear</a> wrote "<a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Blown Away (Carrie Underwood song)">Blown Away</a>" and sent it to the singer.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Underwood loved the song and recorded it, also naming her album <i>Blown Away</i> as it was the song that defined the album's darker direction.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She then started writing material with several songwriters, including <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a> and Kear. Together, the trio wrote "<a href="/wiki/Two_Black_Cadillacs" title="Two Black Cadillacs">Two Black Cadillacs</a>", which is about two women finding out that they both are involved in a relationship with the same man and set their differences aside to kill him. Underwood said that "it was so much fun to write and just be in that room" with Lindsey and Kear, as they "didn't really know what to expect or where we were headed or what we would end up with."<sup id="cite_ref-btsong_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-btsong-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Underwood has stated that the album would contain material that is different from her previous efforts, since stating that <i>Blown Away</i> features songs with a "darker storyline."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> She has stated that she isn't trying to impress people with what [she] can do" and she doesn't "have to sing to the moon with every single song" with this project. The lead single, "Good Girl," is said to be the album's most upbeat track, with the other tracks creating a much darker record.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
The album combines <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> genres.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><i>Blown Away</i> received mostly positive reviews from music critics. On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>,  which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream critics, the album received an average score of 70 based on 12 reviews, indicating "generally favorable reviews". Stephen Thomas Erlewine of <a href="/wiki/Allmusic" class="mw-redirect" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> gave a positive review to the album, awarding it with four stars out of five and said: naturally, this showstopping act suits a former American Idol winner but, better still, this exercise in turn-of-the-millennium nostalgia is executed with skill and savvy, offering the kind of larger-than-life power ballads and cheerful, clomping arena country. This is Carrie's wheelhouse—she's meant to sing these oversized ballads and hooks, she's meant to look as unattainable as she does on the cover. She's meant to be a superstar and she's never seemed as comfortable with her calling as she does on Blown Away. Deborah Evans Price of <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> praised the album, saying, "Underwood delivers her most adventurous album yet, tackling a variety of emotional topics, including abuse, infidelity, revenge and regret."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Ian Ewing of <i>Keepin' It Country</i>  rated the album 9.5 on a scale of 10, and highlighted the title track, saying, "Although there's nothing to mark it as a country song, it's a stunning piece of music that can't help but impress."<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Daryl Addison of <a href="/wiki/Great_American_Country" title="Great American Country">GAC</a> praised the album, saying, "Carrie doesn't shy away from complexities, and with a powerful voice unafraid to push the envelope, <i>Blown Away</i> could be just the album to show what's possible for pop-influenced country."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Jessica Nicholson of <a href="/wiki/Country_Weekly" class="mw-redirect" title="Country Weekly">Country Weekly</a> rated the album with four stars out of five and said: "This is easily Carrie’s most well-rounded and confident work to date". <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> gave a positive review to the album and said: Ms. Underwood enjoys rage; her huge voice, both naïve and muscular, is well suited to it. Her best songs have historically been in the range between fury and resentment. “Blown Away” is only her fourth album, but that number belies her concrete-hard place in the country firmament, with a combination of vocal ambition and toughness that recalls a younger Martina McBride. Eric Allen of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Songwriter" title="American Songwriter">American Songwriter</a></i> gave a positive review, awarding it with four stars out of five and said: Blown Away displays considerable artistic and creative growth in both Underwood’s songwriting and selection of material.  Is more thematic and unified, resulting in Underwood’s strongest effort to date, which easily leaves her previous releases twisting in the wind. Melissa Maerz of <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> gave the album a B+, and referred to <i>Blown Away</i> as Underwood's "most stadium-rock-friendly album yet.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> At <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i>, Elysa Garnder told that the release "both reaffirms her natural gifts and makes us continue to root for her to push beyond them."<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mikael Wood of the <i><a href="/wiki/LA_Times" class="mw-redirect" title="LA Times">LA Times</a></i> was less than enthusiastic about the album, giving it two stars out of four, and saying that much of the album "finds Underwood using her remarkable voice to deliver feel-good bromides like those in the lightly-reggae-inflected 'One Way Ticket.'"<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> While Johnathan Keefe of <i><a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant</a></i> magazine wasn't overly impressed with the album in his review, giving the album 3 stars, he did give Underwood credit for "at least tak[ing] far more creative risks than she ever has before and occasionally stray[ing] from a formula that had become stale and predictable."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Melissa Newman of <i>Hitfix</i> gave the album a B, but criticized the album's material, calling it "a mixed bag, with the songs running the gamut from ranking among her best to wondering who the hell let some of them on the album."<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blown_Away_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Blown Away</i> debuted at number one in the United States on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart with first-week sales of 267,000 copies. It is Carrie Underwood's third number-one album on the chart, following <i><a href="/wiki/Carnival_Ride" title="Carnival Ride">Carnival Ride</a></i> (2007) and <i><a href="/wiki/Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" title="Play On (Carrie Underwood album)">Play On</a></i> (2009). She is the third female to chart at number one on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 with three country albums, thus tying her with <a href="/wiki/Linda_Ronstadt" title="Linda Ronstadt">Linda Ronstadt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Faith_Hill" title="Faith Hill">Faith Hill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard200_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard200-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Like her three previous albums, <i>Blown Away</i> also debuted at the top of the <a href="/wiki/Top_Country_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Country Albums">Top Country Albums</a> chart, making Underwood only the second female to achieve that feat.<sup id="cite_ref-grein1_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grein1-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In its second week, <i>Blown Away</i> stayed at number one, being Underwood's first album to spend more than a week at the top, selling 120,000 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-billboard1_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-billboard1-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> <i>Blown Away</i> has topped the US <a href="/wiki/Top_Country_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Country Albums">Top Country Albums</a> chart for seven weeks, being Underwood's longest run at number one on the chart since her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Hearts_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Some Hearts (Carrie Underwood album)">Some Hearts</a></i>, logged 27 weeks on top.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The album was the seventh best selling album of 2012 in the US, being the second time Underwood has an album inside the top 10 albums since her debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Hearts_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Some Hearts (Carrie Underwood album)">Some Hearts</a></i> (2005) landed at number three in 2006. It was number two for country albums.<sup id="cite_ref-music.yahoo.com_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-music.yahoo.com-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> <i>Blown Away</i> finished 2012 with over 1,400,000 copies sold worldwide, according to the <a href="/wiki/International_Federation_of_the_Phonographic_Industry" title="International Federation of the Phonographic Industry">International Federation of the Phonographic Industry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> As of April 2017, <i>Blown Away</i> has sold 1,794,800 copies in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-sales_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sales-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> It has sold over two million copies worldwide, becoming Underwood's fourth consecutive album to sell at least two million copies.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On October 24, 2016, the album received a 2x-Platinum certification from the RIAA for 2 million units in sales and streams.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, the album debuted at number one, with first-week sales of 17,300 copies.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> It also debuted at number four on the ARIA Top 50 Albums, and at number one on the ARIA Top 20 Country Albums charts in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Her first-ever officially released album in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <i>Blown Away</i> debuted at number eleven on the UK Top 100 Albums, and at number one on the UK Top Country Albums.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> It has since sold 61,000 copies in the United Kingdom<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> and has been certified Silver by the <a href="/wiki/British_Phonographic_Industry" title="British Phonographic Industry">BPI</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blown_Away_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With the release of her highly anticipated fourth album, Underwood made numerous television, radio, and online appearances. On April 29, Underwood was featured on <i><a href="/wiki/Nightline" title="Nightline">Nightline</a></i>, and on April 30, <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i> aired an exclusive interview. Later that evening, Underwood performed "<a href="/wiki/Good_Girl_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Good Girl (Carrie Underwood song)">Good Girl</a>" on <i><a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">Late Show with David Letterman</a></i> as well as holding a concert on the <i>Live on Letterman</i> webcast concert series streaming live online. On May 1, Underwood returned to <i>Good Morning America</i> to perform a special live outdoor concert in Times Square, followed by an iHeartRadio Live performance streaming live online that evening. Underwood appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> on May 3 with a special performance of the title track from her album. Underwood also made appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a></i> on May 9, <i><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Kimmel_Live!" title="Jimmy Kimmel Live!">Jimmy Kimmel Live!</a></i> on May 10, and <i><a href="/wiki/Dancing_with_the_Stars_(U.S._TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Dancing with the Stars (U.S. TV series)">Dancing with the Stars</a></i> on May 15, followed by the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Music_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard Music Awards">Billboard Music Awards</a> on May 20.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> On May 26, she headlined at Bayou Country Superfest in <a href="/wiki/Baton_Rouge,_Louisiana" title="Baton Rouge, Louisiana">Baton Rouge, Louisiana</a>, performing several new songs from the album.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Underwood kicked off the <a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_Tour" title="Blown Away Tour">Blown Away Tour</a>  during the summer of 2012.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blown_Away_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first single of the album, "<a href="/wiki/Good_Girl_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Good Girl (Carrie Underwood song)">Good Girl</a>" was released on February 23, 2012 and it debuted at number thirty on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart and number twenty-four on <a href="/wiki/Hot_100" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 100">Hot 100</a>. It eventually climbed to a peak of eighteen on the Hot 100, number one on the Hot Country Songs, number twenty on the <a href="/wiki/Adult_Top_40" title="Adult Top 40">Adult Pop Songs</a> chart and number twenty-one on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a>. It has been certified 2x Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> and Platinum by <a href="/wiki/Music_Canada" title="Music Canada">Music Canada</a>.
</p><p>The second single of the album is "<a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Blown Away (Carrie Underwood song)">Blown Away</a>". Before the official release of the song, it charted at number 66 on the Hot 100 and number 49 on the Hot Country Songs, and has since reached a peak of number twenty and two, respectively. It became Underwood's 16th number one single when it topped the <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart. It is also her first song to chart in the UK Singles Chart where it has reached 155. It has been certified 3x Platinum by the RIAA and Platinum by the CRIA.
</p><p>The third single of the album is "<a href="/wiki/Two_Black_Cadillacs" title="Two Black Cadillacs">Two Black Cadillacs</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> It was released on November 18, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> The music video was released on January 23, 2013. The song peaked at number two on the Billboard Country Airplay chart and number four on Hot Country Songs and has been <a href="/wiki/Platinum_certification" class="mw-redirect" title="Platinum certification">certified Platinum</a> by the RIAA.
</p><p>On March 18, 2013, Underwood announced via <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> that "<a href="/wiki/See_You_Again_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="See You Again (Carrie Underwood song)">See You Again</a>" would be the fourth single off the album. "See You Again" impacted Country radio on April 15.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> It reached number two on the Country Airplay chart and number seven on Hot Country Songs. On August 10, 2015, "<a href="/wiki/See_You_Again_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="See You Again (Carrie Underwood song)">See You Again</a>" was certified Platinum by the RIAA.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tour">Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blown_Away_(album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The tour included an international run of shows during the summer of 2012, including her first-ever United Kingdom concert, taking place at the prestigious Royal Albert Hall in London on June 21. The Royal Albert Hall concert sold out within the first 90 minutes of public ticket sales.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The opening act for Underwood on the North American leg of the tour was country singer <a href="/wiki/Hunter_Hayes" title="Hunter Hayes">Hunter Hayes</a>. Underwood donated $1 from each ticket sold on the North American leg of the tour to support the Red Cross disaster relief. The first leg of The Blown Away Tour began in September 14 in <a href="/wiki/Manchester,_New_Hampshire" title="Manchester, New Hampshire">Manchester</a> and continued deep into December.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Due to positive reception and successful touring revenue from the first North American leg of the tour, a second North American leg of the tour was announced for the spring of 2013. The tour ultimately ran through May 2013 and played to over one million fans over the course of 112 shows.<sup id="cite_ref-cmt.com_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cmt.com-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A concert DVD, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Blown_Away_Tour:_Live" title="The Blown Away Tour: Live">The Blown Away Tour: Live</a></i>, was released on August 13, 2013. The footage was captured during Underwood's concert in <a href="/wiki/Ontario,_California" title="Ontario, California">Ontario, California</a> on March 3, 2013. The DVD includes more than twenty songs performed by Underwood on tour, as well as exclusive interviews with Underwood, tour director of The Blown Away Tour, Raj Kapoor, and other behind-the-scenes footage about the tour.<sup id="cite_ref-cmt.com_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cmt.com-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> The DVD has since been certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
