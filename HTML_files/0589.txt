<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Desi_Bouterse.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Desi_Bouterse.jpg/220px-Desi_Bouterse.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="393" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Desi_Bouterse.jpg/330px-Desi_Bouterse.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Desi_Bouterse.jpg/440px-Desi_Bouterse.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1015" data-file-height="1811" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Desi_Bouterse.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bouterse as military leader in 1985.</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>1980 Surinamese coup d'état</b>, usually referred to as the <b>Sergeants' Coup</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">De Seargentencoup</i>), was a military <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d&#39;état">coup</a> in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> which occurred on 25 February 1980, when a group of 16 sergeants (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">groep van zestien</i>, <small><a href="/wiki/Literal_translation" title="Literal translation">lit.</a>&#160;</small>&#39;group of sixteen&#39;) of the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_National_Army" title="Suriname National Army">Surinamese Armed Forces</a> (SKM) led by <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a> overthrew the government of Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Henck_Arron" title="Henck Arron">Henck Arron</a> with a violent <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d&#39;état">coup d'état</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This marked the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Military_dictatorship" title="Military dictatorship">military dictatorship</a> that dominated the country from 1980 until 1991. The dictatorship featured the presence of an evening <a href="/wiki/Curfew" title="Curfew">curfew</a>, the lack of <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_press" class="mw-redirect" title="Freedom of press">freedom of press</a>, a ban on <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political parties</a> (from 1985), a restriction on the <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_assembly" title="Freedom of assembly">freedom of assembly</a>, a high level of government corruption and the <a href="/wiki/Summary_execution" title="Summary execution">summary executions</a> of political opponents.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Developments">Developments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Developments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>President <a href="/wiki/Johan_Ferrier" title="Johan Ferrier">Johan Ferrier</a> was eventually forced out of office in August 1980, and several months after the coup d'état by Bouterse most of the political authority transferred to the military leadership. From then until 1988, the titular Presidents were essentially army-installed by  Bouterse, who ruled as a <i>de facto</i> leader with few practical <a href="/wiki/Checks_and_balances" class="mw-redirect" title="Checks and balances">checks on his power</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="December_murders_and_Moiwana_massacre">December murders and Moiwana massacre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: December murders and Moiwana massacre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 8 December 1982, a group of fifteen academics, journalists, lawyers, union leaders, and military officials, who opposed the military rule in Suriname were snatched from their beds and brought to <a href="/wiki/Fort_Zeelandia_(Paramaribo)" title="Fort Zeelandia (Paramaribo)">Fort Zeelandia</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a> where they were tortured and executed by Bouterse's soldiers. Fourteen of those executed were Surinamese; journalist <a href="/wiki/Frank_Wijngaarde" class="mw-redirect" title="Frank Wijngaarde">Frank Wijngaarde</a> was a Dutch national. The events are known as the <a href="/wiki/December_murders" title="December murders">December murders</a>.
</p><p>In 1986 Bouterse's soldiers killed at least 39 citizens, mostly children and women, of the Maroon village of <a href="/wiki/Moiwana" title="Moiwana">Moiwana</a>, as part of the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_Guerrilla_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Suriname Guerrilla War">Suriname Guerrilla War</a> which was fought between the soldiers of Bouterse and the <a href="/wiki/Jungle_Commando" title="Jungle Commando">Jungle Commando</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Brunswijk" title="Ronnie Brunswijk">Ronnie Brunswijk</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transition_to_democratic_rule">Transition to democratic rule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transition to democratic rule">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Elections were held in 1987 and a new constitution was adopted, which among other things allowed Bouterse to remain in charge of the army. Dissatisfied with the government, Bouterse overthrew them on 24 December 1990, by <a href="/wiki/1990_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="1990 Surinamese coup d&#39;état">another coup</a>. This event became popularly known as "the telephone coup". In 1991, elections returned to Suriname where the New Front party gained 41 of the 51 parliament seats. <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Venetiaan" title="Ronald Venetiaan">Ronald Venetiaan</a> (a fierce opponent of Bouterse) became president. In 1996, <a href="/wiki/Jules_Wijdenbosch" title="Jules Wijdenbosch">Jules Wijdenbosch</a> was elected as president of Suriname on behalf of Bouterse's party, the National Democratic Party (NDP). In 2000 and 2005 Ronald Venetiaan was elected as president of Suriname. Dési Bouterse returned to power as president in 2010.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_holiday">National holiday</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_Surinamese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: National holiday">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:%22De_Historische_Strijd%22_Herdenkingsmonument_revolutie_25_februari_1980_-_Nieuw-Amsterdam_-_20418126_-_RCE.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/%22De_Historische_Strijd%22_Herdenkingsmonument_revolutie_25_februari_1980_-_Nieuw-Amsterdam_-_20418126_-_RCE.jpg/220px-%22De_Historische_Strijd%22_Herdenkingsmonument_revolutie_25_februari_1980_-_Nieuw-Amsterdam_-_20418126_-_RCE.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="337" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/%22De_Historische_Strijd%22_Herdenkingsmonument_revolutie_25_februari_1980_-_Nieuw-Amsterdam_-_20418126_-_RCE.jpg/330px-%22De_Historische_Strijd%22_Herdenkingsmonument_revolutie_25_februari_1980_-_Nieuw-Amsterdam_-_20418126_-_RCE.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/%22De_Historische_Strijd%22_Herdenkingsmonument_revolutie_25_februari_1980_-_Nieuw-Amsterdam_-_20418126_-_RCE.jpg/440px-%22De_Historische_Strijd%22_Herdenkingsmonument_revolutie_25_februari_1980_-_Nieuw-Amsterdam_-_20418126_-_RCE.jpg 2x" data-file-width="783" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:%22De_Historische_Strijd%22_Herdenkingsmonument_revolutie_25_februari_1980_-_Nieuw-Amsterdam_-_20418126_-_RCE.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Monument commemorating the coup.</div></div></div>
<p>After becoming president of Suriname, Bouterse designated February 25, the anniversary of the day of the coup d’état, as a national holiday.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On the day of the coup, Bouterse's soldiers burned down the Central Police Station of Suriname. The remains of this building now form the monument of the Revolution where every year on 25 February the coup is commemorated.
</p>
