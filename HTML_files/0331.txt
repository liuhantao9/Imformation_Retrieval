<p>A <b>religious name</b> is a type of <a href="/wiki/Given_name" title="Given name">given name</a> bestowed for a religious purpose, and which is generally used in religious contexts.  Different types of religious names may be in use among  <a href="/wiki/Clergy" title="Clergy">clergy</a> of a religion, as well in some cases among the <a href="/wiki/Laity" title="Laity">laity</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Christianity">Christianity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Christianity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Catholic_Church">Catholic Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Catholic Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Baptism_name">Baptism name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Baptism name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholics</a> are given a <a href="/wiki/Christian_name" title="Christian name">Christian name</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> which should not be "foreign to Christian sentiment"<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and is often the name of a <a href="/wiki/Saint" title="Saint">saint</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In East Asia, in Africa and elsewhere, the baptismal name is distinct from the traditional-style given name.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Confirmation_name">Confirmation name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Confirmation name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In some countries, it is common to adopt a <a href="/wiki/Confirmation#Confirmation_name" title="Confirmation">confirmation name</a>, in addition to the baptismal name.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Monastic_and_papal_name">Monastic and papal name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Monastic and papal name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In some <a href="/wiki/Religious_institute" title="Religious institute">religious institutes</a>, a new member takes a <a href="/wiki/Religious_(Catholicism)" class="mw-redirect" title="Religious (Catholicism)">religious</a> name. In Italian, a religious figure is often referred to by their religious name and by their secular name or their <i><a href="https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/al_secolo" class="extiw" title="it:al secolo">it:al secolo</a></i> name. A <a href="/wiki/Papal_election" class="mw-redirect" title="Papal election">newly elected</a> <a href="/wiki/Pope" title="Pope">Pope</a> also takes on a new name, called his <a href="/wiki/Regnal_name" title="Regnal name">regnal name</a> or <a href="/wiki/Papal_name" title="Papal name">papal name</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_Church">Eastern Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Eastern Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Baptism_name_2">Baptism name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Baptism name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox Church</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Catholicism" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Catholicism">Eastern Catholicism</a> converts often take a new name at the time of their reception into the church, either by <a href="/wiki/Baptism" title="Baptism">baptism</a> or <a href="/wiki/Chrismation" title="Chrismation">chrismation</a>. A person's religious name is always the name of a <a href="/wiki/Saint" title="Saint">saint</a>, who then becomes that person's <a href="/wiki/Patron_saint" title="Patron saint">patron saint</a>. When deciding on a name for their child, Orthodox parents will often name the child after a <a href="/wiki/Saint" title="Saint">saint</a> whose <a href="/wiki/Feast_day" class="mw-redirect" title="Feast day">feast day</a> falls on either the day of the child's birth or the day of its baptism. Traditionally, Orthodox Christians celebrate their "<a href="/wiki/Name_day" title="Name day">name day</a>" (i.e., the feast day of their patron saint), rather than their birthday.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Monastic_name">Monastic name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Monastic name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Catholic and Orthodox <a href="/wiki/Monk" title="Monk">monks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nun" title="Nun">nuns</a> are often given a new monastic name at the time of their <a href="/wiki/Tonsure" title="Tonsure">tonsure</a> (i.e., when they take their <a href="/wiki/Monastic_vows" class="mw-redirect" title="Monastic vows">monastic vows</a>). A monastic name is usually the name of a <a href="/wiki/Prophet" title="Prophet">prophet</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Monastic" class="mw-redirect" title="Monastic">monastic</a> saint. Sometimes, the monastic name will begin with the same initial as the individual's baptismal name.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Judaism">Judaism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Judaism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Conversion_to_Judaism" title="Conversion to Judaism">Converts to Judaism</a> take a <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_name" title="Hebrew name">Hebrew name</a> upon conversion.  Born Jews generally have a <a href="/wiki/Patronymic" title="Patronymic">patronymic</a> Hebrew name which is used for religious purposes; this is frequently different from their <a href="/wiki/Legal_name" title="Legal name">legal name</a>, especially when the latter is of <a href="/wiki/Gentile" title="Gentile">gentile</a> or non-Hebrew origin.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Buddhism">Buddhism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Buddhism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Many <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhist</a> sects also practice this, with newly ordained <a href="/wiki/Sangha_(Buddhism)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sangha (Buddhism)">sangha</a> often given new names by their master. Lay Buddhists are also given <a href="/wiki/Dharma_name" title="Dharma name">Dharma names</a> during their <a href="/wiki/Refuge_(Buddhism)" title="Refuge (Buddhism)">refuge ceremony</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Others">Others</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Others">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gaudiya_Vaishnavism">Gaudiya Vaishnavism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Gaudiya Vaishnavism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Members of <a href="/wiki/International_Society_for_Krishna_Consciousness" title="International Society for Krishna Consciousness">ISKCON</a> and some other <a href="/wiki/Gaudiya_Vaishnavism" title="Gaudiya Vaishnavism">Gaudiya Vaishnava</a> organisations are given a "spiritual name" by their <a href="/wiki/Guru" title="Guru">guru</a> upon initiation. This name ends in "Das" or "Dasa" for men and "Dasi" for women (meaning "servant"), and generally begins with the same letter as the devotee's given name.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wicca">Wicca</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Wicca">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Wicca" title="Wicca">Wicca</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Craft_name" title="Craft name">craft name</a> is often used.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parody_religions">Parody religions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religious_name&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Parody religions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It is usual for a <a href="/wiki/Discordianism#Episkopos" title="Discordianism">Discordian Episkopos</a> to adopt a faux-mystical and bizarre name for the duration of the Episkoposity; such names are not immutable and are frequently adapted over time. The same is true for the clergy of the related <a href="/wiki/Church_of_the_SubGenius" title="Church of the SubGenius">Church of the SubGenius</a>.
</p>
