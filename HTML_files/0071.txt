<p>"<b>Cowboy Casanova</b>" is a <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country</a> song by American singer <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>. The song was written by <a href="/wiki/Mike_Elizondo" title="Mike Elizondo">Mike Elizondo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brett_James" title="Brett James">Brett James</a>, and co-written by Underwood herself. It was released on September 14, 2009, by <a href="/wiki/Arista_Nashville" title="Arista Nashville">Arista Nashville</a> as the lead single from her third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" title="Play On (Carrie Underwood album)">Play On</a></i>. The song sold 2,300,000 copies, making it the fourth-biggest single of her career behind "<a href="/wiki/Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel" title="Jesus, Take the Wheel">Jesus, Take the Wheel</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Before_He_Cheats" title="Before He Cheats">Before He Cheats</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Blown Away (Carrie Underwood song)">Blown Away</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-USsales_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USsales-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song received positive reviews from critics and became the fastest climbing country single of 2009. It also became Underwood's eighth number one country single in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. The single was certified 2x<a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" class="mw-redirect" title="Music recording sales certification">Platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cowboy_Casanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the end of her "Carnival Ride Tour", in February 2009, Underwood began writing songs for her new album <i>Play On</i>. She collaborated with a number of producers which included <a href="/wiki/Brett_James" title="Brett James">Brett James</a>, one of the three co-writers of her hit single "<a href="/wiki/Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel" title="Jesus, Take the Wheel">Jesus, Take the Wheel</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-weekly_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weekly-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Elizondo" title="Mike Elizondo">Mike Elizondo</a>, who had collaborated with <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dre" title="Dr. Dre">Dr. Dre</a>.  Elizondo came up with the main melody of the song, to which James and Underwood set the lyrics.<sup id="cite_ref-weekly_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weekly-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> James then suggested the title "Cowboy Casanova" off a list of potential titles that he had, and the  lyrics talk about a male character who is a "devil in disguise." In an interview with <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Country Music Television">CMT</a> Underwood said,
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>"I think to every woman, this song would be telling a story about someone they know or met or has tried to pick them up in a bar".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  "We're not trashing this guy, we're just warning these other girls about him."<sup id="cite_ref-weekly_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weekly-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>
<p>An unfinished version of the song leaked online on September 2, 2009 after receiving airplay from <a href="/wiki/KSWD_(FM)" title="KSWD (FM)">KMPS-FM</a> in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>. The label then rush-released the official version to Underwood's <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> channel on September 3, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It was officially made available for download on September 14, 2009.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cowboy_Casanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Cowboy Casanova" is a moderately paced country song. It incorporates elements of <a href="/wiki/Country_rock" title="Country rock">rock</a>-inspired <a href="/wiki/Guitar" title="Guitar">guitar</a> riffs and <a href="/wiki/Background_vocals" class="mw-redirect" title="Background vocals">background gang vocals</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Introduction_(music)" title="Introduction (music)">introduction</a> and verses, and also the violin and <a href="/wiki/Banjo" title="Banjo">banjo</a>-esque qualities which travel into the <a href="/wiki/Refrain" title="Refrain">refrain</a>. The song is composed in <a href="/wiki/D_minor" title="D minor">D minor</a>, set in <a href="/wiki/Common_time" class="mw-redirect" title="Common time">common time</a> with a tempo of 120 <a href="/wiki/Beats_per_minute" class="mw-redirect" title="Beats per minute">beats per minute</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sheet_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sheet-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Underwood's vocals span from A<sub>3</sub> to D<sub>5</sub>.<sup id="cite_ref-sheet_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sheet-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cowboy_Casanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song has been met with generally favorable reviews. <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard Magazine">Billboard</a></i> reviewed the song favorably, stating that it "gives [Underwood] the chance to display the sassy side of her musical persona, which made previous hit "<a href="/wiki/Before_He_Cheats" title="Before He Cheats">Before He Cheats</a>" such an impact record. Blessed with a versatile set of pipes that make the best of any tune, Underwood shines on this steamy upbeat number".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Matt Bjorke of <i>Roughstock</i> also gave a favorable review, saying, "Although the song borrows familiar melodic themes, the production is sparkling; Carrie sings the verses with rapid-fire precision and the hook is just infectious. This may very well be my favorite Carrie Underwood single yet."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Kevin Coyne of <i>Country Universe</i> said that on “Cowboy Casanova”, Underwood makes a strong attempt but doesn’t quite pull it off. We’re left with just a decent single that doesn’t feel quite country enough or quite pop enough to make it a keeper, therefore giving her a grade of B-.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_reception">Commercial reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cowboy_Casanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Cowboy Casanova" was released on September 14, 2009 for digital download. The song logged a first week sales of 110,236 copies in downloads. The song also made Underwood the only solo country artist in 2009 to debut a digital single above the 100,000.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The song also became the fastest climbing single in Underwood's career and also the fastest-climbing single of 2009 in the country music genre.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, the song gave Underwood the most Top 10 hits on the country chart this decade - 10 top 10 singles followed by <a href="/wiki/Faith_Hill" title="Faith Hill">Faith Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martina_McBride" title="Martina McBride">Martina McBride</a> each with nine.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of October 2015, 2,300,000 copies of "Cowboy Casanova" were sold in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-USsales_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USsales-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It has been certified 2× Platinum, being Underwood's fourth single to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cowboy_Casanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video was filmed on September 2, 2009, in <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans,_Louisiana" class="mw-redirect" title="New Orleans, Louisiana">New Orleans, Louisiana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It premiered during <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Country Music Television">CMT</a>'s Big New Music Weekend on October 2, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The video was directed by Theresa Wingert and it features Underwood and an entourage of attractive female dancers all wearing <a href="/wiki/Flapper" title="Flapper">flapper</a> party gowns.  She warns the video's audience, as well as her entourage, about smooth-talking attractive men and their true intentions.  An attractive man sporting a fancy outfit that consists of an <a href="/wiki/Overcoat" title="Overcoat">overcoat</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Fedora" title="Fedora">fedora</a> gazes at the scene with interest. The man, a well representation of the song's topic, seemingly has designs on Underwood and as the video progresses the dancers become dressed in revealing clothing, consisting of <a href="/wiki/Garter_(stockings)" class="mw-redirect" title="Garter (stockings)">garters</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corsets" class="mw-redirect" title="Corsets">corsets</a>. Eventually Underwood, along with her empowered entourage, have a showdown with the man in a back-alley and defiantly spurn his advances, leading him to give up and walk away. The video also became Underwood's first <a href="/wiki/Choreography" title="Choreography">choreographed</a> video.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cowboy_Casanova&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Cowboy Casanova" debuted at number 26 on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart for the week of September 19, 2009 and reached the number one spot in its tenth week, becoming Underwood's eighth number one on that chart and also her fastest climbing song at that time. "Cowboy Casanova" was also marked as Underwood's fastest climbing song on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 chart, climbing to number 11 only one week after its official release.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The song made its debut on the U.S. <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_100" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot 100">Hot 100</a> at number 96, making a jump to number 11 in its second week, becoming her sixth top 20 Hot 100 hit.
</p>
