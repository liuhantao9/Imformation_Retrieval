	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:TrescoAbbey.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/TrescoAbbey.jpg/220px-TrescoAbbey.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/TrescoAbbey.jpg/330px-TrescoAbbey.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/TrescoAbbey.jpg/440px-TrescoAbbey.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3008" data-file-height="2000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:TrescoAbbey.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Tresco Abbey, his home on <a href="/wiki/Tresco,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Tresco, Isles of Scilly">Tresco, Isles of Scilly</a></div></div></div>
<p>Lieutenant <b>Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith</b> JP, DL, (7 Feb 1846 – 6 Aug 1918) was <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly#Governors_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Lord Proprietor of the Isles of Scilly</a> from 1872 – 1918.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Algernon_Smith-Dorrien-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith was born on 7 February 1846 at <a href="/wiki/Berkhamsted" title="Berkhamsted">Berkhamsted</a> in Hertfordshire. He was the son of Robert Algernon Smith-Dorrien (died 8 October 1879) and Mary Ann Drever.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His younger brother was <a href="/wiki/Horace_Smith-Dorrien" title="Horace Smith-Dorrien">Horace Smith-Dorrien</a>. He married Edith Anna Maria Tower (d. 17 Jan 1892), daughter of Christopher Theron Tower and Lady Sophia Frances Cust, on 8 April 1875. The children of this marriage were: 
</p>
<ul><li>Major <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Algernon_Dorrien-Smith" title="Arthur Algernon Dorrien-Smith">Arthur Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith</a> (28 Jan 1876 – 30 May 1955) married on 11 May 1909 to Eleanor Salvin Bowlby, daughter of Edward Salvin Bowlby and Elizabeth Vans Agnew.</li>
<li>Mary Sophia Smith-Dorrien-Smith (9 Nov 1877 – 22 Dec 1948) married on 3 July 1902 to Townshend Evelyn Boscawen, son of Rev Hon John Townshend Boscawen and Mary Tremayne.</li>
<li>Major Edward Pendarves Smith-Dorrien-Smith (26 Feb 1878 – 12 Oct 1937) married on 2 June 1915 to Frances Amy Salvin Bowlby, daughter of Edward Salvin Bowlby and Elizabeth Vans Agnew.</li>
<li>Edith Innis Smith-Dorrien-Smith (b. 1 Jan 1881)</li>
<li>Cicely Frances Smith-Dorrien-Smith (b. 4 Nov 1882)</li>
<li>Gwendolen Smith-Dorrien-Smith (b. 7 Nov 1883)</li>
<li>Charlotte Smith-Dorrien-Smith  (b. c 1886)</li></ul>
<p>He was given the name of Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien at birth. In 1872 he changed his name by Royal Licence to Thomas Algernon Smith-Dorrien-Smith.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Thomas_Algernon_Smith-Dorrien-Smith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Harrow_School" title="Harrow School">Harrow School</a>. From 1864 to 1874 he served in the <a href="/wiki/10th_Royal_Hussars" title="10th Royal Hussars">10th Royal Hussars</a> achieving the rank of Lieutenant.
</p><p>He succeeded his uncle, <a href="/wiki/Augustus_Smith_(politician)" title="Augustus Smith (politician)">Augustus Smith</a> as <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly#Governors_of_Scilly_Lord" title="Isles of Scilly">Proprietor of the Isles of Scilly</a> in 1872, and continued the development of the <a href="/wiki/Tresco_Abbey_Gardens" title="Tresco Abbey Gardens">Tresco Abbey Gardens</a>. He designed <a href="/wiki/St_Nicholas%27s_Church,_Tresco" title="St Nicholas&#39;s Church, Tresco">St Nicholas's Church, Tresco</a> which was built between 1877 and 1879. He is considered to be the person who saw the potential for the export of flowers from the Islands,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> a trade which continues to this day. A week after his marriage, the <a href="/wiki/SS_Schiller" title="SS Schiller">SS Schiller</a> was wrecked in the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>. <a href="/wiki/William_I,_German_Emperor" title="William I, German Emperor">William I, German Emperor</a> awarded him the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Red_Eagle" title="Order of the Red Eagle">Order of the Red Eagle</a> and his wife a gold bracelet for their work with survivors.
</p><p>He was a <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_Peace" class="mw-redirect" title="Justice of the Peace">Justice of the Peace</a>, and later <a href="/wiki/Deputy_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Deputy Lieutenant">Deputy Lieutenant</a> of Cornwall. He was elected a Member of the <a href="/wiki/British_Ornithologists%27_Union" title="British Ornithologists&#39; Union">British Ornithologists' Union</a> in 1904.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> A friend, <a href="/wiki/Edward_Hearle_Rodd" title="Edward Hearle Rodd">Edward Rodd</a>, in his annual report to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Institution_of_Cornwall" title="Royal Institution of Cornwall">Royal Institution of Cornwall</a> records the number of birds shot by Lt Smith-Dorrien-Smith during the Isles of Scilly  shooting season. These were 545 <a href="/wiki/Common_snipe" title="Common snipe">common snipe</a> (<i>Gallinago gallinago</i>), 415 <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_woodcock" title="Eurasian woodcock">Eurasian woodcock</a> (<i>Scolopax rusticola</i>) and 42 <a href="/wiki/Jack_snipe" title="Jack snipe">jack snipe</a> (<i>Lymnocryptes minimus</i>). Rodd also received an immature <a href="/wiki/Purple_heron" title="Purple heron">purple heron</a> (<i>Ardea purpurea</i>) and a <a href="/wiki/Wood_sandpiper" title="Wood sandpiper">wood sandpiper</a> (<i>Tringa glareola</i>) for his collection.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
