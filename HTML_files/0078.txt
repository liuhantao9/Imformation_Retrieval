<p>"<b>Blown Away</b>" is a song by American recording artist <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, taken from her fourth studio album <a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(album)" title="Blown Away (album)">of the same name</a> (2012). The song served as the album's second single on July 9, 2012 through <a href="/wiki/Arista_Nashville" title="Arista Nashville">Arista Nashville</a>. Written by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Tompkins" title="Chris Tompkins">Chris Tompkins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Kear" title="Josh Kear">Josh Kear</a>, who previously wrote Underwood's single "<a href="/wiki/Before_He_Cheats" title="Before He Cheats">Before He Cheats</a>" (2007), "Blown Away" is a <a href="/wiki/Country_pop" title="Country pop">country pop</a> song with lyrics addressing the story of a daughter locking herself in a storm cellar while her alcoholic father is passed out on the couch in the path of a tornado. Producer <a href="/wiki/Mark_Bright_(record_producer)" title="Mark Bright (record producer)">Mark Bright</a> drew inspiration from 1980s music.
</p><p>Upon its release, "Blown Away" was met with positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>, who considered it to be the musical highlight of the album. The song's content and production received particular praise, as critics felt it confirmed the album's darker mood which Underwood had mentioned prior to its release. Commercially, "Blown Away" was successful. In the United States, it became her 13th number one hit on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart, and also reached number 20 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a>. It also charted in Canada and the United Kingdom. It won several awards, including two <a href="/wiki/55th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="55th Grammy Awards">Grammy Awards</a>, for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Song" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Song">Best Country Song</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Solo_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Solo Performance">Best Country Solo Performance</a>.
</p><p>The accompanying music video was directed by Randee St. Nicholas. Underwood said that when she heard first the song, she already had ideas of a possible video for it. She wanted it to be a dark <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wonderful_Wizard_of_Oz" title="The Wonderful Wizard of Oz">Wizard of Oz</a></i> in 2012. It earned her an award for Video of the Year at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Awards" title="CMT Music Awards">CMT Music Awards</a>. The video was nominated for Music Video of the Year at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association" title="Country Music Association">Country Music Association</a> Awards. Underwood has performed "Blown Away" in a number of live appearances, including at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Billboard_Music_Awards" title="2012 Billboard Music Awards">2012 Billboard Music Awards</a>, the <a href="/wiki/55th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="55th Annual Grammy Awards">55th Annual Grammy Awards</a>, and two years in a row at the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">Country Music Association Awards</a>. It was also performed as the encore of the <a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_Tour" title="Blown Away Tour">Blown Away Tour</a> (2012–13).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_and_composition">Writing and composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Writing and composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Underwood's <a href="/wiki/Play_On_Tour" title="Play On Tour">Play On Tour</a> wrapped in December 2010, she started to work on her fourth studio album, which was then untitled. <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Nashville" title="Sony Music Nashville">Sony Music Nashville</a> chairman/CEO Gary Overton said that the singer "took nearly a year to compile and record the songs."<sup id="cite_ref-usainterview_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usainterview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Songwriters <a href="/wiki/Chris_Tompkins" title="Chris Tompkins">Chris Tompkins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Kear" title="Josh Kear">Josh Kear</a>, who previously wrote "<a href="/wiki/Before_He_Cheats" title="Before He Cheats">Before He Cheats</a>" for Underwood's debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Hearts_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" class="mw-redirect" title="Some Hearts (Carrie Underwood album)">Some Hearts</a></i> (2005), worked on "Blown Away" initially without a specific artist in mind.<sup id="cite_ref-tasteofcountry_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tasteofcountry-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At first, they built the drums and the string parts that of the introduction and verses.<sup id="cite_ref-radiocom_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radiocom-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Tompkins started playing his keyboard to find sound effects for the introduction, and ended up using one that was a <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">thunderstorm</a>. "I think that's what kind of threw us into it. We just started writing it," he said.<sup id="cite_ref-tasteofcountry_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tasteofcountry-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The songwriters then came up with the first verse of the song: "Dry lighting cracks across the skies / Those storm clouds gather in her eyes / Her daddy was a mean old mister / Momma was an angel in the ground / The weatherman called for a twister / She prayed blow it down."<sup id="cite_ref-tasteofcountry_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tasteofcountry-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Kear commented that it was not the duo's intent or goal to make it a dark, revenge-themed song. As they wrote the pre-chorus line "not enough rain in <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>", both knew that it was a Carrie Underwood song, as the singer is from that state. "We knew if we stuck with that lyric ["Oklahoma"—which is Carrie's home state] it was Carrie's song or maybe no one would ever record it," Kear commented.<sup id="cite_ref-radiocom_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radiocom-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After the theme of the song had been established, the songwriters wanted to "dig up as much drama as [they] could."<sup id="cite_ref-radiocom_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radiocom-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Lyrically, "Blown Away" tells the story of a daughter locking herself in a storm cellar while her alcoholic father is passed out on the couch in the path of a tornado.<sup id="cite_ref-theboot_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theboot-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Underwood revealed that "Blown Away" was the song that defined the direction of the album, and recalled the first time she heard the demo:
</p>
<blockquote><p>"I listened to it on my crappy computer speakers and then I had to go find my headphones because as soon as I listened to a few bars, I had to listen more closely and I got chills. I remember where I was when I heard it and called my manager, Ann, and I was like, 'Do not let anyone else have this song! It’s my song.' In talking to Chris and Josh about it, they [told me], 'We said we're either writing a song for Carrie Underwood or this song is never going to see the light of day.' It made me feel so good that they were thinking of me when they wrote it."<sup id="cite_ref-theboot_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theboot-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>

<p>As noted by interviewer Kurt Wolff, "the melody and overall sound of the song also stand out as something fresh and very different."<sup id="cite_ref-radiocom_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radiocom-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Tompkins explained to Wolff that "Blown Away" was an "attempt at 'melodic pop' that was 'unique' yet still 'country.'"<sup id="cite_ref-radiocom_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radiocom-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Tompkins also commented that "Blown Away" is "not even mine and Josh’s song anymore; it's [Carrie's] song now. She's completely claimed it. The song is Carrie. It's not even a song that had a chance of being pitched to anybody else. It's a completely different type of song for Carrie. She's got a lot of pop elements, and I listen to a lot of pop, but I listen to everything from <a href="/wiki/Randy_Newman" title="Randy Newman">Randy Newman</a> to <a href="/wiki/Mozart" class="mw-redirect" title="Mozart">Mozart</a> to <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">Rihanna</a> to <a href="/wiki/Steely_Dan" title="Steely Dan">Steely Dan</a>. I think all that stuff shows up in my music, and I think it showed up in 'Blown Away.'"<sup id="cite_ref-radiocom_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radiocom-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The final version of the song was produced by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Bright_(record_producer)" title="Mark Bright (record producer)">Mark Bright</a>, who drew inspiration from 1980s music. Underwood recalled Bright adding an effect to her vocals similar to the ones used in <a href="/wiki/Def_Leppard" title="Def Leppard">Def Leppard</a> songs: "That was a big thing with [the band], all of that hollow vocal sound. And I liked it."<sup id="cite_ref-usainterview_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usainterview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Bright used a similar effect on "<a href="/wiki/See_You_Again_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="See You Again (Carrie Underwood song)">See You Again</a>", another song from <i><a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(album)" title="Blown Away (album)">Blown Away</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-usainterview_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usainterview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Melodically, "Blown Away" is written in the <a href="/wiki/Key_(music)" title="Key (music)">key</a> of <a href="/wiki/A_minor" title="A minor">A minor</a>, and is set in the common time with a tempo of 134 <a href="/wiki/Beats_per_minute" class="mw-redirect" title="Beats per minute">beats per minute</a>. The song follows the sequence of <a href="/wiki/A_minor" title="A minor">Am</a> - <a href="/wiki/C_(musical_note)" title="C (musical note)">C</a> - <a href="/wiki/G_(musical_note)" title="G (musical note)">G</a> as its <a href="/wiki/Chord_progression" title="Chord progression">chord progression</a>, and Underwood's voice spans from the low note of G<sup>3</sup> to the high note of <a href="/wiki/E_(musical_note)" title="E (musical note)">E</a><sup>5</sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Blown_Away_-_May_2013.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Blown_Away_-_May_2013.jpg/220px-Blown_Away_-_May_2013.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Blown_Away_-_May_2013.jpg/330px-Blown_Away_-_May_2013.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Blown_Away_-_May_2013.jpg/440px-Blown_Away_-_May_2013.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="3000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Blown_Away_-_May_2013.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Underwood performing "Blown Away" on the <a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_Tour" title="Blown Away Tour">Blown Away Tour</a> (2012-13).</div></div></div> 
<p>"Blown Away" received generally positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_journalism" title="Music journalism">music critics</a>. A reviewer for <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> thought that Mark Bright's "brooding, atmospheric" production and Underwood's vocal performance "elevate this cinematic tune to an instant classic." They further commented, "When the girl shuts herself in the storm cellar, leaving her alcoholic father passed out on the couch in the path of a twister, you can almost feel the wind."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Writing for the <i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i>, Mikael Wood thought that the song confirmed the description of the album as "a turn toward darkness from a singer who first topped the country chart with '<a href="/wiki/Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel" title="Jesus, Take the Wheel">Jesus, Take the Wheel</a>'."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> columnist <a href="/wiki/Brian_Mansfield" title="Brian Mansfield">Brian Mansfield</a> thought the song's "synthesizers, strings sounds, vocal overdubs and echoing guitars" combined dramatically, creating a "neo-80s feel - think an Oklahoma version of the <a href="/wiki/Eurythmics" title="Eurythmics">Eurythmics</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> "Blown Away" received a five-star rating from Billy Dukes of Taste of Country, who called it "dangerous, but irresistible." He also praised Underwood for recording darker material than her previous singles.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Also giving it five stars, Bobby Peacock of Roughstock called it "more grandiose" than Underwood's previous efforts, also saying that it "makes itself known by sounding like absolutely nothing else on radio."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Chris Richards of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i> gave the song a mixed review, deeming the lyrics as "gripping", but negatively comparing the instrumentation to the work of <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor Swift</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the release of the album, "Blown Away" debuted at number 22 on <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s <a href="/wiki/Digital_Songs" title="Digital Songs">Hot Digital Songs</a> chart, with 66,000 units sold.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It eventually peaked at number 15, staying at the chart for a total of 35 weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On the main <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a> chart, "Blown Away" peaked at number 20 and spent 22 weeks on the chart; it was ranked the 70th biggest song of 2012 there overall.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On the week of September 6, 2012, the song became Underwood's 16th top ten single on the <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> component chart, a record among women in the tally's 68-year history according to <i>Billboard</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The following month, on the week of October 15, it reached the top spot, becoming the singer's 13th number one.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> It stayed there for a second week, making Underwood the only female vocalist of 2012 to achieve three weeks at number one on the chart, as previous single "<a href="/wiki/Good_Girl_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Good Girl (Carrie Underwood song)">Good Girl</a>" had peaked at the same position.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The song was certified three-times Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) on August 10, 2015.  As of February 2016, "Blown Away" has sold 2,819,000 digital units in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-USsales_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-USsales-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"Blown Away" also peaked at number one in Canada Country chart, number 27 in Canadian Hot 100, and is Underwood's second song to chart in the United Kingdom, reaching number 155, despite not having a proper release in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-canada_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-canada-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-uk_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-uk-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The accompanying music video for "Blown Away" was directed by Randee St. Nicholas and produced by Brandon Bonfiglo for Randee St. Nicholas Photography.<sup id="cite_ref-premiere_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Underwood said that when she first heard the song, she already had ideas of a possible video for it, and wanted it to be a dark <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wonderful_Wizard_of_Oz" title="The Wonderful Wizard of Oz">Wizard of Oz</a></i> in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-videointerview_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-videointerview-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The singer deemed it "a visual song. You listen to it and you can see everything that is happening. It's so dramatic. I'm not a drama person, but when you can make a movie in song form in three-and-a-half minutes, it's surreal."<sup id="cite_ref-theboot_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theboot-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> A sneak peek of the video was released on June 11, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The music video premiered worldwide on July 30, during a 24-hour exclusive window domestically and internationally on <a href="/wiki/E!_News" title="E! News">E! News</a> at 7 and 11:30&#160;p.m. ET/PT and on the homepage of <a href="/wiki/E!_Online" class="mw-redirect" title="E! Online">E! Online</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-premiere_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Prior to the E! premiere, Underwood held a private screening of the video with country radio station KJ97 for over 200 of their listeners in <a href="/wiki/San_Antonio" title="San Antonio">San Antonio, Texas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-premiere_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-premiere-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Blown_Away.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bc/Blown_Away.png/300px-Blown_Away.png" decoding="async" width="300" height="125" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/bc/Blown_Away.png/450px-Blown_Away.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/bc/Blown_Away.png 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="208" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Blown_Away.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A scene of the video with Underwood as the daughter running back to her home, while a tornado approaches. The singer used subtle references to the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wonderful_Wizard_of_Oz" title="The Wonderful Wizard of Oz">Wizard of Oz</a></i> in the music video.<sup id="cite_ref-subtle_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-subtle-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>The video begins with a girl (a young Underwood) studying at home. Her abusive father arrives and asks her if she needs help. She declines his offer and starts gathering her things to leave the room, however; as she stands up, he grabs her arm. She manages to pull away and leave. Underwood stated that this scene had no script. She and the actor acted it out the way they thought it might have happened.<sup id="cite_ref-videointerview_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-videointerview-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Underwood revealed that she left the filming location with bruises on her arm- "I had finger marks on my arm when I left at the end of the day, so I was like, 'Wow.' ... It was intense. I wasn't just imagining it, it was really intense."<sup id="cite_ref-videointerview_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-videointerview-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> As the video continues, the girl is seen standing in the middle of a cemetery, walking around and staring at the clouds as a thunderstorm forms in the sky. She runs back to her house through an old, destroyed yellow-brick road, similar to the one of <i>Wizard of Oz</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-videointerview_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-videointerview-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> She enters the house to see her father sleeping on the couch, holding a bottle of alcohol. She sits next to him, recalling the times he let his drunkenness and anger get out of hand. She tries to wake him up but fails. She realizes that he has passed out. She looks through the window and notices the thunderstorm getting worse, so she leaves her father and runs to the cellar alone to protect herself. She's seen lying in an old bed and crying, as she hears the tornado coming closer, with her father still passed out. The next day, there are no traces of the house, and she calmly walks away.
</p><p>Regarding the <i>Wizard of Oz</i> references, Underwood commented that "it was all about having subtle references," such as the plaid shirt and red shoes she wore on the video.<sup id="cite_ref-subtle_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-subtle-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> When asked if her aim was to generate controversy with the storyline, the singer said that it was not what she were aiming for at all, adding: "I try to stay away from controversy in any form or fashion. It was just such a great story and such a mini-movie, listening to the song, and we really wanted to do it justice in the video."<sup id="cite_ref-videointerview_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-videointerview-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The music video won Video of the Year at the 2013 <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Awards" title="CMT Music Awards">CMT Music Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood performed "Blown Away" on <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> on May 3, 2012, the week of the album's release. Amy Sciarretto of Taste of Country summarized the performance, writing, "Underwood was elevated on steps as she performed, with storm clouds roaring on the screens behind her. She was bathed in light as smoke billowed at feet."<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The same month, she performed the song at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Billboard_Music_Awards" title="2012 Billboard Music Awards">2012 Billboard Music Awards</a>, dressed in a long, red gown.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The singer also performed it at the <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">2012 Country Music Association Awards</a>. Throughout the performance, wind machines were used while confetti flew through the air.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, Underwood performed "Blown Away" again at the same award show, this time in a medley with "Good Girl", "See You Again" and "<a href="/wiki/Two_Black_Cadillacs" title="Two Black Cadillacs">Two Black Cadillacs</a>", representing the ending of the <i>Blown Away</i> era.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> The same year, she performed an acoustic version of the song during the <a href="/wiki/55th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="55th Annual Grammy Awards">55th Annual Grammy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> "Blown Away" was performed as the encore of Underwood's <a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_Tour" title="Blown Away Tour">Blown Away Tour</a> (2012–13), along with "I Know You Won't".<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
