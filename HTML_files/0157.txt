<p><b>Anders Fogh Rasmussen</b> (<small>Danish pronunciation:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Danish" title="Help:IPA/Danish">[anɐs ˈfɔʊ̯ˀ ˈʁasmusn̩]</a></span>&#32;<span class="nowrap" style="font-size:85%">(<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Andersfogh_pronunciation.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" decoding="async" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a></span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9e/Andersfogh_pronunciation.ogg" class="internal" title="Andersfogh pronunciation.ogg">listen</a></span></span>)</span>; born 26 January 1953) is a <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> politician who was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Denmark" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Prime Ministers of Denmark">24th Prime Minister of Denmark</a> from November 2001 to April 2009 and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_NATO_Secretaries_General#Secretaries_General" class="mw-redirect" title="List of NATO Secretaries General">12th Secretary General</a> of <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> from August 2009 to October 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-secgen_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-secgen-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is now CEO of political consultancy <a href="/wiki/Rasmussen_Global" title="Rasmussen Global">Rasmussen Global</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and a senior advisor at <a href="/wiki/The_Boston_Consulting_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="The Boston Consulting Group">The Boston Consulting Group</a>'s Copenhagen office.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rasmussen was first elected to the <a href="/wiki/Folketing" title="Folketing">Folketing</a> in 1978 and served in various ministerial positions, including <a href="/wiki/Tax_Minister_of_Denmark" class="mw-redirect" title="Tax Minister of Denmark">Minister of Tax</a> (1987–1992) and Minister of Economic Affairs (1990–1992). In his early career, Rasmussen was a strident critic of the <a href="/wiki/Welfare_state" title="Welfare state">welfare state</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> writing the <a href="/wiki/Classical_liberalism" title="Classical liberalism">classical liberal</a> book <i><a href="/wiki/From_Social_State_to_Minimal_State" title="From Social State to Minimal State">From Social State to Minimal State</a></i> in 1993. However, his views moved towards the <a href="/wiki/Centrism" title="Centrism">political centre</a> through the 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He was elected the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_liberalism" title="Conservative liberalism">conservative-liberal</a> party <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a> in 1998 and headed a centre-right coalition with the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_People%27s_Party_(Denmark)" title="Conservative People&#39;s Party (Denmark)">Conservative People's Party</a> which took office in <a href="/wiki/2001_Danish_general_election" title="2001 Danish general election">November 2001</a> and won its second and third terms in <a href="/wiki/2005_Danish_general_election" title="2005 Danish general election">February 2005</a> and in <a href="/wiki/2007_Danish_general_election" title="2007 Danish general election">November 2007</a>. Rasmussen's government relied on the <a href="/wiki/Danish_People%27s_Party" title="Danish People&#39;s Party">Danish People's Party</a> for support, keeping with the Danish tradition of <a href="/wiki/Minority_government" title="Minority government">minority government</a>.
</p><p>His government introduced tougher limits on non-<a href="/wiki/European_Economic_Area" title="European Economic Area">EEA</a> immigration and a freeze on tax rates (<i>skattestoppet</i> in Danish). Certain taxes were lowered, but his coalition partners in the Conservative People's Party repeatedly argued for more tax cuts and a <a href="/wiki/Flat_tax" title="Flat tax">flat tax</a> rate at no higher than 50%. Rasmussen's government implemented an administrative reform reducing the number of municipalities (<i>kommuner</i>) and replacing the thirteen <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Denmark" title="Counties of Denmark">counties</a> (<i>amter</i>) with five <a href="/wiki/Regions_of_Denmark" title="Regions of Denmark">regions</a> which he referred to as "the biggest reform in thirty years". He authored several books about <a href="/wiki/Taxation" class="mw-redirect" title="Taxation">taxation</a> and government structure. He resigned as Prime Minister in April 2009 to become Secretary General of NATO. His term as Secretary General was to end in the summer of 2014, but the <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Council" title="North Atlantic Council">North Atlantic Council</a> extended his term on 11 December 2013 until 30 September 2014 in order to ensure the organisation of the 2014 NATO summit in <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Wales" title="Newport, Wales">Newport</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rasmussen was born in 1953 in <a href="/wiki/Ginnerup" title="Ginnerup">Ginnerup</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jutland" title="Jutland">Jutland</a>, to farmer Knud Rasmussen and Martha Rasmussen (née Fogh). His surname is Rasmussen, while Fogh, his mother's maiden name, is his <a href="/wiki/Middle_name#Scandinavia" title="Middle name">middle name</a> and not considered part of his last name. He is correctly referred to as Rasmussen (not Fogh Rasmussen), unless his full name (including his given name) is used. In Danish media and society, he has often been popularly referred to as Fogh Rasmussen, or merely Anders Fogh, when not referred to by his full name, mainly to distinguish him from other prominent politicians in the country with the same family name.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He matriculated in languages and social studies from Viborg Cathedral School, in 1969–1972.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and studied Economics at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Aarhus" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Aarhus">University of Aarhus</a>, graduating in 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He has been active in politics most of his life and has authored several books about taxation and government structure. He and his wife Anne-Mette (born 1958) married in 1978 and have three children: Henrik Fogh Rasmussen (born 1979), Maria (born 1981) and Christina (born 1984). Rasmussen also has six grandchildren.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As an amateur cyclist, Rasmussen completed part of the notorious <a href="/wiki/Alpe_d%27Huez" title="Alpe d&#39;Huez">Alpe d'Huez</a> stage of the <a href="/wiki/Tour_de_France_2008" class="mw-redirect" title="Tour de France 2008">2008 Tour de France</a> the day after the professional race took place.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> His attendance at <i>Le Tour</i> was at the invitation of Danish former cyclist <a href="/wiki/Bjarne_Riis" title="Bjarne Riis">Bjarne Riis</a>. Rasmussen is also an avid runner.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>He is of no relation to either his predecessor <a href="/wiki/Poul_Nyrup_Rasmussen" title="Poul Nyrup Rasmussen">Poul Nyrup Rasmussen</a>, nor his successor <a href="/wiki/Lars_L%C3%B8kke_Rasmussen" title="Lars Løkke Rasmussen">Lars Løkke Rasmussen</a> as Prime Minister of Denmark.
</p><p>He received the <i>America Award</i> of the <a href="/wiki/Italy-USA_Foundation" class="mw-redirect" title="Italy-USA Foundation">Italy-USA Foundation</a> in 2017.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_political_career">Early political career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early political career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He has held positions in government and opposition throughout his career, first winning a seat in the <a href="/wiki/Folketing" title="Folketing">Folketing</a> (Danish parliament) in 1978.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In general, Rasmussen is in favour of <a href="/wiki/Centralisation" title="Centralisation">centralisation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Privatisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Privatisation">privatisation</a> and limiting the size of government.
</p><p>Rasmussen wrote the book <i><a href="/wiki/From_Social_State_to_Minimal_State" title="From Social State to Minimal State">From Social State to Minimal State</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>: <i lang="da">Fra socialstat til minimalstat</i>) in 1993, in which he advocated an extensive reform of the Danish <a href="/wiki/Welfare_state" title="Welfare state">welfare system</a> along classic liberal lines. In particular, he favours lower taxes and less government interference in <a href="/wiki/Corporation" title="Corporation">corporate</a> and individual matters. In 1993 he was awarded the <a href="/wiki/Adam_Smith" title="Adam Smith">Adam Smith</a> award by the <a href="/wiki/Libertarianism" title="Libertarianism">libertarian</a> society <a href="/w/index.php?title=Libertas_(Denmark)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Libertas (Denmark) (page does not exist)">Libertas</a>, partly because of this book.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Resignation_as_Minister_of_Taxation">Resignation as Minister of Taxation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Resignation as Minister of Taxation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>From 1987 to 1990 he was Minister for Taxation and from 1990 Minister for Economy and Taxation in the Conservative-led <a href="/wiki/Poul_Schl%C3%BCter" title="Poul Schlüter">Poul Schlüter</a> government.
</p><p>In 1992 Rasmussen resigned from his ministerial posts after a report from a commission of inquiry had decided that he had provided the Folketing with inaccurate and incomplete information regarding his decision to postpone payment of several bills from <a href="/wiki/Regnecentralen" title="Regnecentralen">Regnecentralen</a> and <a href="/wiki/KMD_(company)" title="KMD (company)">Kommunedata</a> from one accounting year to the next. Rasmussen disagreed with the findings of the commission, but faced with the threat of a <a href="/wiki/Motion_of_no_confidence" title="Motion of no confidence">motion of no confidence</a>, he left his posts voluntarily.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2001_election">2001 election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2001 election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>His Liberal (<i><a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Venstre</a></i>) Party won power in the November <a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Danish_election&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2001 Danish election (page does not exist)">2001 election</a>, defeating the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democrats_(Denmark)" title="Social Democrats (Denmark)">Social Democratic</a> government of <a href="/wiki/Poul_Nyrup_Rasmussen" title="Poul Nyrup Rasmussen">Poul Nyrup Rasmussen</a> and enabling him to form <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabinet of Anders Fogh Rasmussen I">his first cabinet</a>. That election marked a dramatic change in Danish politics. It was the first time since 1920 that the Social Democratic Party lost its position as the largest party in the <a href="/wiki/Folketing" title="Folketing">Folketing</a> (parliament), mainly due to a loss of working class votes to <a href="/wiki/Dansk_Folkeparti" class="mw-redirect" title="Dansk Folkeparti">Dansk Folkeparti</a> (The Danish People's Party). The 2001 election also marked the emergence and solidification of far-right, anti-immigrant rhetoric within Danish politics.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prime_Minister_of_Denmark">Prime Minister of Denmark</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Prime Minister of Denmark">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the 2001 election, <i>Venstre</i> formed a government in a parliamentary coalition with the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_People%27s_Party_(Denmark)" title="Conservative People&#39;s Party (Denmark)">Conservative People's Party</a> to form a <a href="/wiki/Minority_government" title="Minority government">minority government</a> with the parliamentary support of Dansk Folkeparti. Together these three parties survived both the <a href="/wiki/2005_Danish_parliamentary_election" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Danish parliamentary election">2005 election</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2007_Danish_general_election" title="2007 Danish general election">2007 election</a>.
</p><p>After becoming Prime Minister, Rasmussen distanced himself from his earlier writings and announced the death of <a href="/wiki/Neoliberalism" title="Neoliberalism">neoliberalism</a> during the national elections of 2005. Commonly regarded to have been inspired by the success of <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">Tony Blair</a>, Rasmussen now seemed more in favour of the theories of <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Giddens" title="Anthony Giddens">Anthony Giddens</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Third_Way_(centrism)" class="mw-redirect" title="Third Way (centrism)">third way</a>. There was talk in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Libertas_(Denmark)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Libertas (Denmark) (page does not exist)">Libertas</a> of revoking Fogh Rasmussen's award as a result of this, though this never happened.
</p><p>His government enacted tough measures designed to limit the number of immigrants coming to Denmark, specifically as <a href="/wiki/Asylum_seeker" title="Asylum seeker">asylum seekers</a> or through arranged marriages. However, his governments depended on the support of <a href="/wiki/Dansk_Folkeparti" class="mw-redirect" title="Dansk Folkeparti">Dansk Folkeparti</a>, and it is impossible to draw a clear dividing line between his personal ideology and the required compromises with Dansk Folkeparti.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tax_reform">Tax reform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Tax reform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the 2001 elections, Venstre banned all tax increases. Venstre campaigned by claiming that taxes had been growing constantly during the previous eight years under the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democrats_(Denmark)" title="Social Democrats (Denmark)">Social Democrats</a>. While the overall tax burden was more or less unchanged from 1993 until 2001<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>, there was a shift from the taxation of income, both corporate and personal, to a personal consumption (especially through the "ecological taxes" (da. <i>grønne afgifter</i>)), which gave the average citizen the impression of rising taxes.
</p><p>This "tax stop" was criticised by left wing parties, allegedly for being "antisocial" and "only for the rich." Since the tax stop also froze the tax on real property (da. <i>ejendomsværdiskat</i>, 1%), it was beneficial to homeowners in densely populated regions that had experienced rising real estate values. The property tax was set at a nominal level&#160;— not at a relative level. While the rate was one percent when the tax stop was enacted, the rate is much less today when recent increases in property value (+20%/p.a. in large cities) are considered. The Danish Economic Council criticized this as unfairly benefiting current homeowners.
</p><p>Even though the total tax burden was marginally higher in 2005 than in 2001, the tax stop was popular among voters. Thus, in January 2005, the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democrats_(Denmark)" title="Social Democrats (Denmark)">Social Democrats</a> announced that they accepted the principle of a tax stop until at least one right-wing party was willing to participate in tax reform.
</p><p>The tax stop has, however, been ineffective, judging by Venstre's intentions. Its goal was to halt the growth of public expenditures (and halt the growth of taxes), but even with cuts in public spending (which were considered aggressive by the political left wing), overall spending continued to rise by approximately one percentage point above inflation each year.
</p><p>From 2004 and onwards, minor tax cuts came into effect, on two accounts:
</p>
<ol><li>People with jobs got a 3% tax reduction on the 5.5% "bottom tax" (da. <i>bundskat</i>).</li>
<li>An "employment deduction" (da. <i>beskæftigelsesfradrag</i>) was introduced. This initiative was to encourage people to get off welfare and take jobs instead.</li>
<li>The bottom limit of the "middle tax" (da. <i>mellemskat</i>) of 6%, was raised by 12.000 <a href="/wiki/Danish_krone" title="Danish krone">DKK</a> every year, over the next four years. This was supposed to limit the income stresses of middle incomes and families with children.</li></ol>
<p>In 2009 a major tax reform was implemented. The overall marginal tax rate was reduced by 7.5%. In the end, the top tax rate (topskatten) was not changed, but the income level at which it applied was raised. This had the effect of removing 350,000 Danes from the top tax bracket. The medium tax rate was eliminated, and the lowest was reduced by 1.5%. Various other tax reforms were enacted such as an increase in the old age pension, incentives for renovation, and various initiatives designed to improve energy efficiency.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Finance Minister, Lars Lokke Rasmussen, called it the biggest reduction in the marginal tax rate since the introduction of income tax in 1903.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2009 tax revenue was 777,375 million Krone. It had grown to 831,172 million in 2011, 901,001 million in 2013, 954,473 million in 2015, and 995,058 million in 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It's important to note that from 2011 to 2015 Social Democrat <a href="/wiki/Helle_Thorning-Schmidt" title="Helle Thorning-Schmidt">Helle Thorning-Schmidt</a> was the prime minister, however, she passed a tax-reform with support from the liberal-conservative opposition.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It raised the top tax threshold, effectively lowering tax rates for high income earners.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2002_EU_Presidency">2002 EU Presidency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2002 EU Presidency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rasmussen held the rotating presidency of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> from July to December 2002, proving his dedication to a pro-EU agenda and the guiding principles of the <a href="/wiki/Ellemann-Jensen_doctrine" class="mw-redirect" title="Ellemann-Jensen doctrine">Ellemann-Jensen doctrine</a>. He pursued this to its logical conclusion by publicly denouncing the <a href="/wiki/Danish_collaboration_policy" class="mw-redirect" title="Danish collaboration policy">Danish collaboration policy</a> during its second World War occupation, the first Danish prime minister ever to do so. While his predecessors may not have favoured the policy, they had all implicitly maintained that it was 'a good thing',<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> because it had saved Danish lives.
</p><p>During the EU presidency he was involved in a curious episode with then Italian Premier <a href="/wiki/Silvio_Berlusconi" title="Silvio Berlusconi">Silvio Berlusconi</a>. In a joint press conference on 4 October 2002 Berlusconi said: "Rasmussen is the most handsome prime minister in Europe. I think I will introduce him to my wife because he is even more handsome than Cacciari". <a href="/wiki/Massimo_Cacciari" title="Massimo Cacciari">Massimo Cacciari</a> was an Italian philosopher and centrist politician opposing Berlusconi, and some gossip tabloids had alleged an affair between him and Berlusconi's second wife <a href="/wiki/Veronica_Lario" title="Veronica Lario">Veronica Lario</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Rasmussen was puzzled by this remark and Berlusconi quickly told him he'd explain later.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="War_in_Iraq">War in Iraq</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: War in Iraq">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/The_letter_of_the_eight" title="The letter of the eight">The letter of the eight</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:President_George_W._Bush_and_Danish_Prime_Minister_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_hold_a_joint_press_conference.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/President_George_W._Bush_and_Danish_Prime_Minister_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_hold_a_joint_press_conference.jpg/250px-President_George_W._Bush_and_Danish_Prime_Minister_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_hold_a_joint_press_conference.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/President_George_W._Bush_and_Danish_Prime_Minister_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_hold_a_joint_press_conference.jpg/375px-President_George_W._Bush_and_Danish_Prime_Minister_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_hold_a_joint_press_conference.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/President_George_W._Bush_and_Danish_Prime_Minister_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_hold_a_joint_press_conference.jpg/500px-President_George_W._Bush_and_Danish_Prime_Minister_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_hold_a_joint_press_conference.jpg 2x" data-file-width="514" data-file-height="320" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:President_George_W._Bush_and_Danish_Prime_Minister_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_hold_a_joint_press_conference.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Under Rasmussen, Denmark supported American foreign policies.</div></div></div>
<p>As Prime Minister, Rasmussen strongly supported the <a href="/wiki/2003_invasion_of_Iraq" title="2003 invasion of Iraq">2003 Iraq War</a>. As in most European countries he faced considerable opposition, both in the parliament and in the general population. Subsequent opinion polls suggested the Danish population's opinion was split on the issue. One vocal protester managed to get into the Danish parliament during the period before the war, where he poured red paint on the prime minister while yelling "Du har blod på dine hænder" (literally: "You have blood on your hands"). A member of the Danish parliament for the socialist <a href="/wiki/Red-Green_Alliance_(Denmark)" class="mw-redirect" title="Red-Green Alliance (Denmark)">Red-Green Alliance</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pernille_Rosenkrantz-Theil" title="Pernille Rosenkrantz-Theil">Pernille Rosenkrantz-Theil</a>, stated that it was a reaction she might have made under the circumstances, although she later denounced such behaviour. Denmark was one of only five countries to take part in the actual invasion operations (the others being the <a href="/wiki/U.S" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S">U.S</a>, <a href="/wiki/UK" class="mw-redirect" title="UK">UK</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>) though the contingent mainly consisted of two minor warships and staff and radio units that were never involved in actual combat. In the months after the initial phase of the war, Danish troops participated in the <a href="/wiki/Post-invasion_Iraq,_2003%E2%80%93present" class="mw-redirect" title="Post-invasion Iraq, 2003–present">multi-national force stationed in Iraq</a>. Approximately 550 <a href="/wiki/Dancon/Irak" title="Dancon/Irak">Danish troops were stationed in Iraq</a> from 2004 and into 2007, first at "Camp Dannevang" and later at "Camp Einherjer", both near <a href="/wiki/Basra" title="Basra">Basra</a>. When the contingent of troops left around August 2007, it was not replaced and Denmark shifted its focus to non-military support around Baghdad. The official reason provided is that the Iraqi government should now be able to handle security in the Basra area. Critics of Rasmussen argued that the withdrawal was motivated by decreasing domestic support for the war.
</p><p>In 2004 Rasmussen's government came under attack based on questions of how much intelligence it had with regard to <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraqi</a> <a href="/wiki/Weapons_of_mass_destruction" class="mw-redirect" title="Weapons of mass destruction">weapons of mass destruction</a>. The government held hearings, and was forced to publish classified reports it had consulted about the likelihood of banned weapons existing in Iraq. While the Blair and Bush administrations became the subject of criticism for extended periods because of their reliance on questionable intelligence, Rasmussen stayed clear of this controversy. This is probably largely because the motion passed by parliament (Folketinget) authorising the deployment of Danish troops states as the reason for the deployment Iraq's continued refusal to cooperate with UN inspectors in violation of the UN Security Council's resolution. The Danish deployment of troops was thus not formally based on a claim that Iraq had WMD's.
</p>

<p>In March 2003, Rasmussen stated as one of the reasons to support a military intervention, "Irak har masseødelæggelsesvåben. Det er ikke noget vi tror. Vi ved det. Irak har selv indrømmet, at det har haft sennepsgas, nervegas, miltbrand, men Saddam vil ikke afregne. Han vil ikke fortælle os, hvor og hvordan de våben er blevet destrueret. Det ved vi fra FN's inspektører, så der er ingen tvivl i mit sind."<sup id="cite_ref-Iraq_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iraq-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In English, this translates to:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886047036">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Iraq has weapons of mass destruction. This is not something we think. We know it. Iraq has itself admitted that it had mustard gas, nerve gas, anthrax, but Saddam will not settle. He will not tell us where and how the weapons have been destroyed. We know from the UN inspectors, so there is no doubt in my mind.<sup id="cite_ref-Iraq_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iraq-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Forsvarets_Efterretningstjeneste" class="mw-redirect" title="Forsvarets Efterretningstjeneste">Danish Defence Intelligence Service</a> (<i>FE</i>) produced a classified report stating that it had no absolute proof of WMDs in Iraq. Rasmussen had access to this report and used it in other parts of his decision. Since the presence of substantial WMDs in Iraq was later refuted, Rasmussen has focused almost exclusively on the tyrannical nature of Saddam Hussein's regime.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> A former <i>FE</i> analyst, Major <a href="/wiki/Frank_Grevil" title="Frank Grevil">Frank Grevil</a>, was sentenced to four months in prison for leaking the information to the press. Grevil argued that Rasmussen either lied about or misunderstood the content of the secret reports in his presentations to parliament. During Rasmussen's administrations, Denmark deployed troops to <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina" title="Bosnia and Herzegovina">Bosnia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kosovo" title="Kosovo">Kosovo</a>. All three missions met only minor political opposition. Rasmussen as a prime minister rejected insistent demands from the opposition for investigations into Denmark's involvement in the Iraq war, similar to the ones in the United States and the UK, on the grounds that his government took arms against Saddam not because of possible arms possessed, but because of Saddam's failure to comply with UN resolutions.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gay_marriage">Gay marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Gay marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Civil_union" title="Civil union">Civil unions</a> between <a href="/wiki/Homosexuality" title="Homosexuality">gay</a> couples became legal in Denmark in 1989. In January 2004, Rasmussen stated his belief that homosexuals should be able to marry in religious ceremonies, which were not allowed at the time in the Evangelical Lutheran State <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Denmark" title="Church of Denmark">Church of Denmark</a>, but he has said it should be up to religious communities to decide whether to perform ceremonies for gay couples.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2005_election">2005 election</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2005 election">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 18 January 2005 Rasmussen called an <a href="/wiki/2005_Danish_parliamentary_election" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Danish parliamentary election">election</a> for 8 February 2005. He delayed the call by a couple of weeks because of the <a href="/wiki/2004_Indian_Ocean_earthquake" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 Indian Ocean earthquake">2004 Indian Ocean earthquake</a> which killed several Danes. His government was criticized for its allegedly slow response to that crisis, although a clear majority applauded the government's actions.
</p><p>Although his party's support was reduced from the 2001 election, costing it four seats, <i>Venstre</i> was able to maintain its coalition through gains by other parties, and on 18 February Rasmussen formed the <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_of_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Cabinet of Anders Fogh Rasmussen II">Cabinet of Anders Fogh Rasmussen II</a>.
</p><p>Rasmussen received the most "personal votes" ever of any politician in the Folketing (Denmark's Parliament) with 61,792.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Muhammad_cartoons_and_Danish_goods_boycott">Muhammad cartoons and Danish goods boycott</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Muhammad cartoons and Danish goods boycott">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Jyllands-Posten_Muhammad_cartoons_controversy" title="Jyllands-Posten Muhammad cartoons controversy">Jyllands-Posten Muhammad cartoons controversy</a></div>
<p>A major period of conflict in Rasmussen's political career concerned a set of cartoons printed in <i><a href="/wiki/Jyllands-Posten" title="Jyllands-Posten">Jyllands-Posten</a></i>, a major Danish newspaper. In September 2005 the newspaper printed a full page with 12 cartoons depicting various interpretations of Muhammad. Due to the cartoons portraying Muhammad as a terrorist, some Muslims found the cartoons offensive. Rasmussen denied a request from 11 ambassadors of Middle-Eastern countries to discuss the issue. Rasmussen described the controversy as Denmark's worst international crisis since World War II. Later he stated, that he "was deeply distressed that the cartoons were seen by some Muslims as an attempt by Denmark to mark and insult or behave disrespectfully towards Islam or Mohammed."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Municipal_reform">Municipal reform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Municipal reform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Denmark#Municipal_Reform_2007" title="Municipalities of Denmark">Municipalities of Denmark §&#160;Municipal Reform 2007</a></div>
<p>One of Rasmussen's main initiatives was the introduction of municipal reform, the aim of which was the geographic and administrative consolidation of smaller municipalities and the abolition of counties. Major areas of public services, such as the national health service, were consolidated into five regional bodies, while the number of municipalities was reduced from 271 to 98. The reform was ratified on 16 June 2005 and was effective as of 1 January 2007.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007_election_and_resignation">2007 election and resignation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2007 election and resignation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2007, Rasmussen called the <a href="/wiki/2007_Danish_general_election" title="2007 Danish general election">2007 general election</a>, which was held on 13 November.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> His official reason for doing so was to allow parliament to face important upcoming decisions without being distracted by a future election, with welfare reform being cited as an example. Initial polls had predicted that neither the incumbent alliance nor the left-wing opposition would win a majority, leaving the centrist <a href="/wiki/New_Alliance_(Denmark)" class="mw-redirect" title="New Alliance (Denmark)">New Alliance</a> with the balance of power.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rasmussen-Lula25042007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Rasmussen-Lula25042007.jpg/250px-Rasmussen-Lula25042007.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="189" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Rasmussen-Lula25042007.jpg/375px-Rasmussen-Lula25042007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Rasmussen-Lula25042007.jpg/500px-Rasmussen-Lula25042007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2598" data-file-height="1965" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rasmussen-Lula25042007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rasmussen in <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> with <a href="/wiki/Lula_da_Silva" class="mw-redirect" title="Lula da Silva">Lula da Silva</a>, 25 April 2007</div></div></div>
<p>At 11:30&#160;p.m. on the night of the election, Rasmussen claimed victory on the basis of almost-complete results.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> By the morning of 14 November 2007, after results came through from the <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">Faroe Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>, his centre-right coalition of the <a href="/wiki/Venstre_(Denmark)" title="Venstre (Denmark)">Liberals</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_People%27s_Party_(Denmark)" title="Conservative People&#39;s Party (Denmark)">Conservative People's Party</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Danish_People%27s_Party" title="Danish People&#39;s Party">Danish People's Party</a> secured 90 seats, the minimum number required for a majority.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Rasmussen went on to become the longest-ruling Liberal Prime Minister of Denmark.
</p><p>Shortly after his second reelection in 2007, rumours began to spread in the Danish media that Rasmussen was a candidate for high-profile international jobs. A first rumour was that he was informally one of the top candidates for the new position of <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_EU" class="mw-redirect" title="President of the EU">President of the EU</a> that could be created when the <a href="/wiki/Lisbon_Treaty" class="mw-redirect" title="Lisbon Treaty">Lisbon Treaty</a> would come into effect.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Following the Irish <a href="/wiki/Twenty-eighth_Amendment_of_the_Constitution_Bill,_2008_(Ireland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Twenty-eighth Amendment of the Constitution Bill, 2008 (Ireland)">rejection</a> of the treaty in June 2008, it became obvious that this position would not be created in the near future. Rumors then spread around Rasmussen's candidacy for Secretary General of NATO.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Rasmussen denied the rumors until a few days before the official announcement of his selection was made.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After he was confirmed as the NATO Secretary General, Rasmussen announced that he would resign as Prime Minister of Denmark on 5 April 2009.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="NATO_Secretary_General">NATO Secretary General</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: NATO Secretary General">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/NATO%E2%80%93Russia_relations" class="mw-redirect" title="NATO–Russia relations">NATO–Russia relations</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:NATO_Secretary_General_Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen,_center_right,_speaks_during_a_North_Atlantic_Council_meeting_at_NATO_headquarters_in_Brussels_June_4,_2013_130604-D-BW835-241.jpg" class="image"><img alt="An older man in a suit sits, speaking at a round table with dozens of other men and women. <p>Anders Fogh Rasmussen became the 12th <a href="/wiki/NATO" title="NATO">NATO</a> Secretary General on 1 August 2009, succeeding <a href="/wiki/Jaap_de_Hoop_Scheffer" title="Jaap de Hoop Scheffer">Jaap de Hoop Scheffer</a>, who held the post from 2004 until 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-secgen_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-secgen-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The announcement was made on 4 April 2009, at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Strasbourg%E2%80%93Kehl_summit" title="2009 Strasbourg–Kehl summit">2009 Strasbourg–Kehl summit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Strasbourg" title="Strasbourg">Strasbourg</a>. During the final selection process only one country, Turkey, remained opposed to Rasmussen's candidacy, partly because of his handling of the <a href="/wiki/Jyllands-Posten_Muhammad_cartoons_controversy" title="Jyllands-Posten Muhammad cartoons controversy">cartoon episode</a> in 2005, when the publication in some Danish newspapers of cartoons of Muhammad caused violent protests.<sup id="cite_ref-tw1_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tw1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tw2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tw2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Another major element of Turkey's opposition was Denmark's tolerance of <a href="/wiki/Roj_TV" title="Roj TV">Roj TV</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-tw1_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tw1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tw2_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tw2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> which is claimed by the Turkish government to be a mouthpiece for the <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Workers_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="Kurdistan Workers Party">Kurdistan Workers Party</a> (PKK). Eventually, Turkey withdrew its opposition to Rasmussen's appointment when it received assurances from US president <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> that Turkish officials would be appointed to three high NATO posts.
</p><p>After his accession on 1 August 2009, Rasmussen's first mission was a visit to <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, where he met with President <a href="/wiki/Hamid_Karzai" title="Hamid Karzai">Karzai</a> and senior Afghan ministers, including Minister for Foreign Affairs <a href="/wiki/Rangin_Dadfar_Spanta" title="Rangin Dadfar Spanta">Spanta</a>, Minister for Defence General <a href="/wiki/Abdul_Rahim_Wardak" title="Abdul Rahim Wardak">Wardak</a>, and Minister of Interior <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Hanif_Atmar" title="Mohammad Hanif Atmar">Atmar</a> to discuss the then impending <a href="/wiki/2009_Afghan_presidential_election" title="2009 Afghan presidential election">presidential and provincial council elections</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2011, in relation to the <a href="/wiki/2011_Libyan_civil_war" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Libyan civil war">2011 Libyan civil war</a> Rasmussen said that on the day <a href="/wiki/2011_military_intervention_in_Libya" title="2011 military intervention in Libya">NATO started</a> <a href="/wiki/Operation_Unified_Protector" title="Operation Unified Protector">taking command</a> of the mission under the U.N. mandate, the alliance ruled out arming the rebels. Rasmussen said the coalition under his control was clear about its mission. "We are not in Libya to arm people. We are in Libya to protect civilians against attacks" from loyalists of the country's <a href="/wiki/Muammar_Gaddafi" title="Muammar Gaddafi">Muammar Gaddafi</a> government, he said at that time.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Defense.gov_photo_essay_120520-D-TT977-036.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Defense.gov_photo_essay_120520-D-TT977-036.jpg/220px-Defense.gov_photo_essay_120520-D-TT977-036.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Defense.gov_photo_essay_120520-D-TT977-036.jpg/330px-Defense.gov_photo_essay_120520-D-TT977-036.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Defense.gov_photo_essay_120520-D-TT977-036.jpg/440px-Defense.gov_photo_essay_120520-D-TT977-036.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2500" data-file-height="1664" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Defense.gov_photo_essay_120520-D-TT977-036.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>U.S. President <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> thanks Rasmussen at the opening of the NATO summit in Chicago, 20 May 2012.</div></div></div>
<p>In October 2011, the intensive 7-month NATO intervention had "now moved much closer" to its end, according to Rasmussen. The last two major outposts of Gaddafi loyalists—Gaddafi's hometown of <a href="/wiki/Sirte" title="Sirte">Sirte</a> and the town of <a href="/wiki/Bani_Walid" title="Bani Walid">Bani Walid</a>—had fallen and the deposed leader <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Muammar_Gaddafi" title="Death of Muammar Gaddafi">had been killed</a> as he tried to flee from Sirte<sup id="cite_ref-LAT01_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT01-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> toward <a href="/wiki/Misrata" title="Misrata">Misrata</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Gaddafi's killing came with close NATO aerial support of Libyan ground forces.<sup id="cite_ref-LAT01_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LAT01-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Secretary General normally serves for a term of four years with a one-year extension option. On 3 October 2012, Fogh Rasmussens term was extended one year, so it ends on 31 July 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2013 in the first visit of a NATO Secretary General to Ireland for a meeting with EU defence ministers, he said NATO had an "open-door policy" towards membership of the organisation. "Our door remains open for European countries, European democracies that fulfil the necessary criteria and can contribute to Euro-Atlantic security, but of course it’s for individual partners to decide how they want to develop their relationship and partnership with NATO." Ireland is not a member of the organisation but ties through the <a href="/wiki/Partnership_for_Peace" title="Partnership for Peace">Partnership for Peace</a> Programme (PFP), a bilateral programme that allows for Irish forces to be used for peacekeeping and crisis management where there is a UN mandate and parliamentary approval.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Secretary_Kerry_Meets_with_Secretary_General_Rasmussen_(11189045534).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Secretary_Kerry_Meets_with_Secretary_General_Rasmussen_%2811189045534%29.jpg/250px-Secretary_Kerry_Meets_with_Secretary_General_Rasmussen_%2811189045534%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Secretary_Kerry_Meets_with_Secretary_General_Rasmussen_%2811189045534%29.jpg/375px-Secretary_Kerry_Meets_with_Secretary_General_Rasmussen_%2811189045534%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Secretary_Kerry_Meets_with_Secretary_General_Rasmussen_%2811189045534%29.jpg/500px-Secretary_Kerry_Meets_with_Secretary_General_Rasmussen_%2811189045534%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2469" data-file-height="1639" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Secretary_Kerry_Meets_with_Secretary_General_Rasmussen_(11189045534).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>U.S. Secretary of State <a href="/wiki/John_Kerry" title="John Kerry">John Kerry</a> with Rasmussen in Brussels, on 3 December 2013.</div></div></div>
<p>On 19 December 2013, Rasmussen was invited to speak at a periodic meeting of the <a href="/wiki/European_Council" title="European Council">European Council</a> by the Prime Minister of the UK, <a href="/wiki/David_Cameron" title="David Cameron">David Cameron</a>, in opposition to plans proposed by the <a href="/wiki/External_Action_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="External Action Service">External Action Service</a> of <a href="/wiki/High_Representative_of_the_Union_for_Foreign_Affairs_and_Security_Policy" title="High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy">HRUFASC</a> <a href="/wiki/Catherine_Ashton" title="Catherine Ashton">Catherine Ashton</a> to create a <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Air_Force&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="European Air Force (page does not exist)">European Air Force</a> composed of surveillance drones, heavy transport airplanes, and air-to-air refuelling planes.  This plan was supported by France, Spain, Italy, Poland and Germany who together have <a href="/wiki/Qualified_majority_voting" class="mw-redirect" title="Qualified majority voting">QMV</a> majority.<sup id="cite_ref-water_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-water-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>  Rasmussen's position was opposed to that of <a href="/wiki/European_Parliament" title="European Parliament">European Parliament</a> President <a href="/wiki/Martin_Schulz" title="Martin Schulz">Martin Schulz</a>, who made a presentation at the same meeting where he said that "If we wish to defend our values and interests, if we wish to maintain the security of our citizens, then a majority of <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_European_Parliament" title="Member of the European Parliament">MEPs</a> consider that we need a headquarters for civil and military missions in Brussels and deployable troops."  Rasmussen was satisfied with the role of NATO in European defence matters and saw every reason to maintain the status quo.<sup id="cite_ref-water_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-water-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 28 March 2014, <a href="/wiki/Jens_Stoltenberg" title="Jens Stoltenberg">Jens Stoltenberg</a> was appointed as Rasmussen's successor as Secretary General.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rasmussen wrote an <a href="/wiki/Op-ed" title="Op-ed">op-ed</a> piece in London's <a href="/wiki/Daily_Telegraph" class="mw-redirect" title="Daily Telegraph">Daily Telegraph</a> on 6 April 2014 to warn allies to invest in their armed forces, and to maintain that "Russia’s illegal aggression against Ukraine and its continued breach of international law" were clear.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Russian_Foreign_Ministry" class="mw-redirect" title="Russian Foreign Ministry">Russian Foreign Ministry</a> blamed Rasmussen for his "active employment of double standards."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2014, Rasmussen claimed that <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> "engaged actively with so-called non-governmental organisations - environmental organisations working against shale gas - to maintain European dependence on imported Russian gas", without providing evidence for this claim.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rasmussen_Global">Rasmussen Global</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anders_Fogh_Rasmussen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Rasmussen Global">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 1 October 2014 as Rasmussen was succeeded by <a href="/wiki/Jens_Stoltenberg" title="Jens Stoltenberg">Jens Stoltenberg</a>, the former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Norway" title="Prime Minister of Norway">Prime Minister of Norway</a>.,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> he declared the launch of <a href="/wiki/Rasmussen_Global" title="Rasmussen Global">Rasmussen Global</a> to provide support on issues regarding <a href="/wiki/Security_policy" title="Security policy">security policy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Transatlantic_relations" title="Transatlantic relations">Transatlantic relations</a>, the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brexit" title="Brexit">Brexit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">Economic development</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, in 2016 he published a book called 'The Will to Lead', giving his view that the USA should 'restore America's role as a global leader'<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 27 May 2016 Rasmussen became non-staff advisor to <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ukraine" title="President of Ukraine">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Petro_Poroshenko" title="Petro Poroshenko">Poroshenko</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Rasmussen also convened the Friends of Ukraine group<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> of sitting and former senior politicians and diplomats to raise international awareness of Ukraine and to keep domestic reform on the agenda.
</p>
