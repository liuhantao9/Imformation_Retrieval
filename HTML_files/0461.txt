<p><b>Aron Mohamed Winter</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:&#32;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Dutch" title="Help:IPA/Dutch">[ˈaːrɔn ˈʋɪntər]</a></span>; born 1 March 1967) is a retired Dutch <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> and current head coach of the <a href="/wiki/Jong_Ajax" title="Jong Ajax">Ajax</a> Under-19 squad.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He has played for <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sparta_Rotterdam" title="Sparta Rotterdam">Sparta Rotterdam</a> in the Netherlands, for Italian sides <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a> and <a href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano" class="mw-redirect" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano">Internazionale</a>, and for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands national team</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aron_Winter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ajax">Ajax</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aron_Winter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ajax">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He began his career with amateur club VV Unicum in <a href="/wiki/Lelystad" title="Lelystad">Lelystad</a>, moved on to SV Lelystad, and the same year joined <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a> at the age of 19. His first game for Ajax was a match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Utrecht" title="FC Utrecht">FC Utrecht</a> on 6 April 1986, which Ajax won 3–0. Winter won two <a href="/wiki/KNVB_Cup" title="KNVB Cup">KNVB Cups</a> (1987 and 1988), the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> title (1990), the <a href="/wiki/1986%E2%80%9387_European_Cup_Winners%27_Cup" title="1986–87 European Cup Winners&#39; Cup">UEFA Cup Winners' Cup</a> (1987) and the <a href="/wiki/1991%E2%80%9392_UEFA_Cup" title="1991–92 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> (1992)
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lazio">Lazio</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aron_Winter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lazio">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1992, he moved to the Roman team <a href="/wiki/S.S._Lazio" title="S.S. Lazio">Lazio</a>, playing his first match against <a href="/wiki/U.C._Sampdoria" title="U.C. Sampdoria">Sampdoria</a> on 6 September, which Lazio tied 3–3.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Inter_Milan">Inter Milan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aron_Winter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Inter Milan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1996, Winter joined <a href="/wiki/F.C._Internazionale_Milano" class="mw-redirect" title="F.C. Internazionale Milano">Inter Milan</a>, with whom he won the <a href="/wiki/1997%E2%80%9398_UEFA_Cup" title="1997–98 UEFA Cup">UEFA Cup</a> in 1998. He had also played in the <a href="/wiki/1997_UEFA_Cup_Final" title="1997 UEFA Cup Final">previous year's final</a>, with the game going to penalties. However, Winter missed his penalty as Inter lost to <a href="/wiki/Schalke_04" class="mw-redirect" title="Schalke 04">Schalke</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ajax_return_and_Sparta_Rotterdam_loan">Ajax return and Sparta Rotterdam loan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aron_Winter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ajax return and Sparta Rotterdam loan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Winter left Inter for his former club Ajax in 1999. In 2001, he was loaned out to <a href="/wiki/Sparta_Rotterdam" title="Sparta Rotterdam">Sparta Rotterdam</a>. He played 32 games for Sparta Rotterdam and scored one goal before returning to Ajax to finish his career, and where he chose to retire in 2003.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aron_Winter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Winter was a member of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Dutch national team</a> that won the <a href="/wiki/1988_European_Football_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1988 European Football Championship">1988 European Football Championship</a>, but made no appearances during the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_European_Championship#Final_tournament" title="UEFA European Championship">final tournament</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1994 FIFA World Cup">1994 FIFA World Cup</a>, he scored a goal against <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> in the quarter-finals, making him the only player of Indian descent ever to score in a World Cup.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was also selected for the Dutch national team for <a href="/wiki/1996_UEFA_European_Football_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1996 UEFA European Football Championship">Euro 96</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup" title="1998 FIFA World Cup">1998 FIFA World Cup</a> in France.
</p><p>Winter placed in Rijkaard's <a href="/wiki/2000_UEFA_European_Football_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 UEFA European Football Championship">Euro 2000</a> squad.
</p><p>Having represented his national team 84 times, scoring 6 goals, Winter is currently the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team#Records" title="Netherlands national football team">twelfth most capped player</a> for the Dutch national team.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Manager_career">Manager career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aron_Winter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Manager career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After three years as assistant coach for the Ajax first academy team, Winter signed a three-year contract with Canadian side <a href="/wiki/Toronto_FC" title="Toronto FC">Toronto FC</a> on 6 January 2011. Winter brought former colleague <a href="/wiki/Bob_de_Klerk" title="Bob de Klerk">Bob de Klerk</a> from Ajax to be his assistant coach, while <a href="/wiki/Paul_Mariner" title="Paul Mariner">Paul Mariner</a> was also brought in the same day as Director of Player Development.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After a disappointing season opener against <a href="/wiki/Vancouver_Whitecaps_FC" title="Vancouver Whitecaps FC">Vancouver Whitecaps</a> that ended in a 4–2 away defeat on 19 March, Winter won his first game as Toronto's head coach the following week against <a href="/wiki/Portland_Timbers" title="Portland Timbers">Portland Timbers</a> 2–0 in Toronto.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Winter won his first trophy with Toronto in early July as Toronto defeated Vancouver 3–2 on aggregate to capture its third consecutive <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Championship" title="Canadian Championship">Canadian Championship</a>, thereby earning a berth in the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_CONCACAF_Champions_League" title="2011–12 CONCACAF Champions League">2011–12 CONCACAF Champions League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  Winter then guided Toronto to the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_CONCACAF_Champions_League_Championship_Round" title="2011–12 CONCACAF Champions League Championship Round">Champions League semifinals</a>, the first time a Canadian club had progressed that far in the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Toronto eventually lost 7–3 on aggregate to <a href="/wiki/Santos_Laguna" title="Santos Laguna">Santos Laguna</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, Toronto began the 2012 MLS season with nine straight losses, the worst start to a season in the history of the MLS.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Toronto won its tenth match 1–0 versus Philadelphia on 26 May 2012, but it was not enough to save Winter's job, as the club announced on 7 June 2012 that he would be replaced by the director of player development, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Mariner" title="Paul Mariner">Paul Mariner</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aron_Winter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Winter was born in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname_(Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands)" title="Suriname (Kingdom of the Netherlands)">Suriname</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-introducing_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-introducing-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His cousin <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Winter" title="Ricardo Winter">Ricardo Winter</a> is also a former football player/manager who headed the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_football_team" title="Suriname national football team">Suriname national football team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
