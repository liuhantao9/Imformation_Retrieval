<p>The <b>Catholic Church in Suriname</b> is part of the worldwide <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>, and is under the spiritual leadership of the <a href="/wiki/Bishop_of_Rome" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishop of Rome">Bishop of Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pope_Francis" title="Pope Francis">Pope Francis</a>.
</p><p>There are 117,261 Catholics in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, 21.6% of the population, far lower than most of South America.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Church in Suriname consists of only one diocese, the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Paramaribo" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Paramaribo">Diocese of Paramaribo</a>. There are 22 priests in the Diocese, with a ratio of about 5,030 Catholics per priest. There are 31 Catholic parishes in the diocese. The seat of the Diocese of Paramaribo is <a href="/wiki/Saint_Peter_and_Paul_Cathedral,_Paramaribo" title="Saint Peter and Paul Cathedral, Paramaribo">The Cathedral of Sts. Peter and Paul</a> in Paramaribo.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catholic_Church_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first missionaries to come to Suriname were the Franciscans in 1683, but the harshness of the climate did not favor the arrival of other priests, so that up to 1786 the country was totally abandoned by the Catholic missions. Since 1786 some secular priests opened a missionary center, but soon had to flee to the opposition of the ministers of other Christian denominations. When in 1816 the territory passed into the hands of the Dutch, was guaranteed freedom of worship. This was the real starting point of Catholic evangelization of what today is called Suriname. In 1817 was erected the Apostolic Prefecture of Dutch Guyana, which became apostolic vicariate in 1842 and the mission was entrusted in a special way to the Redemptorists. On 7 May 1958 the apostolic vicariate was elevated to the rank of diocese, then <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Paramaribo" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Paramaribo">Diocese of Paramaribo</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecclesiastical_Organization">Ecclesiastical Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catholic_Church_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ecclesiastical Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Today in the country there is only one Catholic ecclesiastical district, the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Diocese_of_Paramaribo" title="Roman Catholic Diocese of Paramaribo">Diocese of Paramaribo</a>, suffragan of the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Port_of_Spain" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Port of Spain">Archdiocese of Port of Spain</a> in <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>.
</p><p>The local episcopate is <a href="/wiki/Antilles_Episcopal_Conference" title="Antilles Episcopal Conference">Antilles Episcopal Conference</a>'s officio member.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Apostolic_Nunciature">Apostolic Nunciature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Catholic_Church_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Apostolic Nunciature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Apostolic Nunciature of Suriname was established on 16 February 1994.
</p>
