<p>The <b>West Riding of Yorkshire</b> is one of the three historic subdivisions of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, England.  From 1889 to 1974 the <a href="/wiki/Administrative_county" title="Administrative county">administrative county</a>, <b>County of York, West Riding</b> (abbreviated: <span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span><b>County of York (WR)</b><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>) (the area under the control of West Riding County Council), was based closely on the historic boundaries.  The <a href="/wiki/Lord_Lieutenant_of_the_West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="Lord Lieutenant of the West Riding of Yorkshire">lieutenancy</a> at that time included the City of <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a> and as such was named <b>West Riding of the County of York and the County of the City of York</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Its boundaries roughly correspond to the present <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties_of_England" title="Ceremonial counties of England">ceremonial counties</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Craven" title="Craven">Craven</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_(borough)" class="mw-redirect" title="Harrogate (borough)">Harrogate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Selby_(district)" class="mw-redirect" title="Selby (district)">Selby</a> districts of <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>, along with smaller parts in <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> (for example, the parishes of Bracewell, Brogden and Salterforth became part of the Pendle district of Lancashire and the parishes of <a href="/wiki/Great_Mitton" title="Great Mitton">Great Mitton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newsholme,_Lancashire" title="Newsholme, Lancashire">Newsholme</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bowland_Forest_Low" title="Bowland Forest Low">Bowland Forest Low</a> became part of the Ribble Valley district also in Lancashire), <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a> and, since 1996, the unitary <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Riding of Yorkshire</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Riding_of_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The West Riding encompasses 1,771,562 acres (7,169&#160;km²) from <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> in the south to <a href="/wiki/Sedbergh" title="Sedbergh">Sedbergh</a> in the north and from <a href="/wiki/Dunsop_Bridge" title="Dunsop Bridge">Dunsop Bridge</a> in the west to <a href="/wiki/Adlingfleet" title="Adlingfleet">Adlingfleet</a> in the east.
</p><p>The southern industrial district, considered in the broadest application of the term, extended northward from Sheffield to <a href="/wiki/Skipton" title="Skipton">Skipton</a> and eastward from Sheffield to <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a>, covering less than one-half of the riding.  Within this district were <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Batley" title="Batley">Batley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brighouse" title="Brighouse">Brighouse</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keighley" title="Keighley">Keighley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Morley, West Yorkshire">Morley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ossett" title="Ossett">Ossett</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pontefract" title="Pontefract">Pontefract</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pudsey" title="Pudsey">Pudsey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rotherham" title="Rotherham">Rotherham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Todmorden" title="Todmorden">Todmorden</a> (partly in Lancashire until 1888, when fully incorporated into Yorkshire) and <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>. Major centres elsewhere in the riding included <a href="/wiki/Harrogate" title="Harrogate">Harrogate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ripon" title="Ripon">Ripon</a>.
</p><p>Within the industrial region, other urban districts included <a href="/wiki/Bingley" title="Bingley">Bingley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bolton_on_Dearne" class="mw-redirect" title="Bolton on Dearne">Bolton on Dearne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Castleford" title="Castleford">Castleford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cleckheaton" title="Cleckheaton">Cleckheaton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elland" title="Elland">Elland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Featherstone" title="Featherstone">Featherstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Handsworth,_South_Yorkshire" title="Handsworth, South Yorkshire">Handsworth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hoyland_Nether" class="mw-redirect" title="Hoyland Nether">Hoyland Nether</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liversedge" title="Liversedge">Liversedge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexborough" title="Mexborough">Mexborough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mirfield" title="Mirfield">Mirfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Normanton,_West_Yorkshire" title="Normanton, West Yorkshire">Normanton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rawmarsh" title="Rawmarsh">Rawmarsh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rothwell,_West_Yorkshire" title="Rothwell, West Yorkshire">Rothwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saddleworth" title="Saddleworth">Saddleworth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shipley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Shipley, West Yorkshire">Shipley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skipton" title="Skipton">Skipton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge" title="Sowerby Bridge">Sowerby Bridge</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stanley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Stanley, West Yorkshire">Stanley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swinton,_South_Yorkshire" title="Swinton, South Yorkshire">Swinton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thornhill,_West_Yorkshire" title="Thornhill, West Yorkshire">Thornhill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wath-upon-Dearne" class="mw-redirect" title="Wath-upon-Dearne">Wath-upon-Dearne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wombwell" title="Wombwell">Wombwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Worsborough" class="mw-redirect" title="Worsborough">Worsborough</a>. Outside the industrial region were <a href="/wiki/Goole" title="Goole">Goole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ilkley" title="Ilkley">Ilkley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Knaresborough" title="Knaresborough">Knaresborough</a> and <a href="/wiki/Selby" title="Selby">Selby</a>. The West Riding also contained a large rural area to the north including part of the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Dales_National_Park" title="Yorkshire Dales National Park">Yorkshire Dales National Park</a> (the remainder of the park being in the North Riding).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Riding_of_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The subdivision of Yorkshire into three <a href="/wiki/Riding_(country_subdivision)" title="Riding (country subdivision)">ridings</a> or "thirds" (<a href="/wiki/Old_Norse_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Old Norse language">Old Norse</a>: <i lang="non">Þriðungr</i>) is of Scandinavian origin.  The West Riding was first recorded (in the form <i>West Treding</i>) in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a> of 1086.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Unlike most English counties, Yorkshire, being so large, was divided first into the three ridings (<a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="North Riding of Yorkshire">North</a> and West) and, later, the city of <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a> (which lay within the <a href="/wiki/City_walls" class="mw-redirect" title="City walls">city walls</a> and was not part of any riding). Each riding was then divided into <a href="/wiki/Wapentakes" class="mw-redirect" title="Wapentakes">wapentakes</a>, a division comparable to the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_(country_subdivision)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hundred (country subdivision)">hundreds</a> of <a href="/wiki/Southern_England" title="Southern England">Southern England</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ward_(country_subdivision)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ward (country subdivision)">wards</a> of England's four northern-most historic counties. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wapentakes">Wapentakes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Riding_of_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wapentakes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_wapentakes_in_Yorkshire" title="List of wapentakes in Yorkshire">List of wapentakes in Yorkshire</a></div>
<p>Within the West Riding of Yorkshire there were ten wapentakes in total, four of which were split into two divisions, those were— <a href="/wiki/Claro_(wapentake)" class="mw-redirect" title="Claro (wapentake)">Claro (Upper and Lower)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Skyrack" title="Skyrack">Skyrack (Upper and Lower)</a>, <a href="/wiki/Strafforth_and_Tickhill" title="Strafforth and Tickhill">Strafforth and Tickhill (Upper and Lower)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Staincliffe_(wapentake)" class="mw-redirect" title="Staincliffe (wapentake)">Staincliffe (East and West)</a>. The wapentake of <a href="/wiki/Agbrigg_and_Morley" title="Agbrigg and Morley">Agbrigg and Morley</a> was created with two divisions but was later split into two separate wapentakes. A wapentake known as the <a href="/wiki/Ainsty" title="Ainsty">Ainsty</a> to the west of <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>, was until the 15th century a wapentake of the West Riding, but since then has come under the jurisdiction of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_York_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="City of York (UK Parliament constituency)">City of York</a>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_county">Administrative county</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Riding_of_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Administrative county">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The administrative county was formed in 1889 by the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1888" title="Local Government Act 1888">Local Government Act 1888</a>, and covered the historic West Riding except for the larger urban areas, which were <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county boroughs</a> with the powers of both a <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> and a <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county council</a>. Initially there were five in number: Bradford, Leeds, Huddersfield, Halifax, and Sheffield. The City of York (also a county borough) was included in the county for census and lieutenancy purposes. The number of county boroughs increased over the years; Rotherham gained this status in 1902, Barnsley and Dewsbury in 1913, Wakefield in 1915 and Doncaster in 1927. The boundaries of existing county boroughs were also widened.
</p><p>Beginning in 1898, the West Riding County Council was based at the <a href="/wiki/County_Hall,_Wakefield" title="County Hall, Wakefield">County Hall</a> in Wakefield, which was inherited by the West Yorkshire County Council in 1974.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Local Government Act 1888 included the entirety of Todmorden with the West Riding administrative county, and also in its lieutenancy area ("county"). Other boundary changes in the county included the expansion of the county borough of Sheffield southward in areas historically in <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Dore" title="Dore">Dore</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Signpost_near_Skirethorns_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1009850.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Signpost_near_Skirethorns_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1009850.jpg/220px-Signpost_near_Skirethorns_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1009850.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="367" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Signpost_near_Skirethorns_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1009850.jpg/330px-Signpost_near_Skirethorns_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1009850.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2e/Signpost_near_Skirethorns_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1009850.jpg 2x" data-file-width="384" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Signpost_near_Skirethorns_-_geograph.org.uk_-_1009850.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A typical West Riding fingerpost with grid reference</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Fingerpost" title="Fingerpost">Fingerposts</a> erected in the West Riding until the mid-1960s had a distinctive style. At the top of the post was a <a href="/wiki/Roundel" title="Roundel">roundel</a> in the form of a hollow circle with a horizontal line across the middle, displaying "Yorks W.R.", the name of the fingerpost's location, and a grid reference. Other counties, apart from <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> did not display a grid reference and did not have a horizontal bar through the roundel. From 1964, many fingerposts were replaced by ones in the modern style, but some of the old style still survive within the West Riding boundaries.
</p><p>By 1971 1,924,853 people (or 50.85% of the West Riding's population) lived in the administrative county, against 1,860,435 (or 49.15%) in the ten county boroughs.
</p>
