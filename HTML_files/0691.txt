<p>The <b>Moro National Liberation Front</b> (<b>MNLF</b>; <a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl">الجبهة الوطنية لتحرير مورو</span>&#8206;) is a political organization in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> that was founded in 1972.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It started as a splinter group of the <a href="/wiki/Muslim_Independence_Movement" title="Muslim Independence Movement">Muslim Independence Movement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The MNLF was the leading organization among Moro separatists for about two decades beginning from the 1970s.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1996, the MNLF signed a landmark <a href="/wiki/1996_Final_Peace_Agreement" title="1996 Final Peace Agreement">peace agreement</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Presidency_of_Fidel_Ramos" title="Presidency of Fidel Ramos">Philippine government</a> that saw the creation of <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_Region_of_Muslim_Mindanao" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous Region of Muslim Mindanao">Autonomous Region of Muslim Mindanao</a> (ARMM), an area composed of two mainland provinces and three island provinces in which the predominantly Muslim population enjoys a degree of self-rule.<sup id="cite_ref-fr_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fr-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nur_Misuari" title="Nur Misuari">Nur Misuari</a> was installed as the region's governor but his rule ended in violence when he led a failed rebellion against the Philippine government in November 2001,<sup id="cite_ref-fr_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fr-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and fled to <a href="/wiki/Sabah" title="Sabah">Sabah</a> before being deported back to the Philippines by the <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysian</a> authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The MNLF is internationally recognized by the <a href="/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation" title="Organisation of Islamic Cooperation">Organisation of Islamic Cooperation</a> (OIC) and its Parliamentary Union of OIC Member States (PUIC).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Since 1977, the MNLF has been an observer member of the OIC.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On January 30, 2012, MNLF became an observer member of the Parliamentary Union of Islamic Cooperation (PUIC), as approved during the 7th PUIC global session held in <a href="/wiki/Palembang" title="Palembang">Palembang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moro_National_Liberation_Front&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Jabidah_massacre" title="Jabidah massacre">Jabidah massacre</a></div>
<p>The Philippine government wanted to encourage migration of landless <a href="/wiki/Christianity_in_the_Philippines" title="Christianity in the Philippines">Christians</a> from other parts of the country in a so-called <i>Homestead Program</i> (1903–1973). There was no land titling system by the natives of Mindanao at that time, and the Christian settlers exploited the situation. <a href="/wiki/Lanao_(province)" title="Lanao (province)">Lanao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cotabato" title="Cotabato">Cotabato</a> received an influx of migrants from <a href="/wiki/Luzon" title="Luzon">Luzon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Visayas" title="Visayas">Visayas</a>. Tensions between Moros and Christians were caused by disputes about land ownership and disenfranchisement of Muslims. The <i>Homestead Program</i> is one of the root-causes of the <a href="/wiki/Moro_conflict" title="Moro conflict">Moro conflict</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-KingsburyKingsbury2015_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KingsburyKingsbury2015-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Poverty, grievances of the <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_the_Philippines" title="Islam in the Philippines">Muslim population</a>, weak <a href="/wiki/Rule_of_law" title="Rule of law">rule of law</a> and difficult terrain have made <a href="/wiki/Counterterrorism" class="mw-redirect" title="Counterterrorism">counterterrorism</a> challenging against insurgents in the Southern Philippines.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 18, 1968 there was an <a href="/wiki/Jabidah_massacre" title="Jabidah massacre">alleged massacre of Moro soldiers</a> in <a href="/wiki/Corregidor_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Corregidor Island">Corregidor Island</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sfof_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfof-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> There has been a long-standing allegation that <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> provided the initial training and arming of the first batch of MNLF cadres known as "Top 90" in 1969.<sup id="cite_ref-msia_aid_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-msia_aid-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Likewise, it has also been alleged that Malaysia was either seemingly ignorant or tolerated the illicit arms shipments, mainly from the Middle East, flowing into Mindanao that fueled the insurgency.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The founder and former leader of the MNLF is <a href="/wiki/Nur_Misuari" title="Nur Misuari">Nur Misuari</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The MNLF was founded as a splinter group of the <a href="/wiki/Muslim_Independence_Movement" title="Muslim Independence Movement">Muslim Independence Movement</a> on October 21, 1972.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>MNLF officially claims that its ideology is <a href="/wiki/Egalitarianism" title="Egalitarianism">egalitarianism</a>, and it is not a religious organization like its <a href="/wiki/Islamic" class="mw-redirect" title="Islamic">Islamic</a> splinter group the <a href="/wiki/Moro_Islamic_Liberation_Front" title="Moro Islamic Liberation Front">Moro Islamic Liberation Front</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AsiaTimes_2013_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AsiaTimes_2013-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leadership_and_splits">Leadership and splits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moro_National_Liberation_Front&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Leadership and splits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Moro_Islamic_Liberation_Front" title="Moro Islamic Liberation Front">Moro Islamic Liberation Front</a>, <a href="/wiki/Abu_Sayyaf" title="Abu Sayyaf">Abu Sayyaf</a>, and <a href="/wiki/MNLF_Executive_Council_of_15" title="MNLF Executive Council of 15">MNLF Executive Council of 15</a></div>
<p>The MNLF was the leading organization among Moro separatists for about two decades beginning from the 1970s. However, discontent on Nur Misuari's leadership caused the group to unravel especially after the 1996 peace agreement. Integration of former rebels in the society was also a factor in the weakening. At the present there are multiple competing factions. For instance, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hadja_Bainon_Karon&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hadja Bainon Karon (page does not exist)">Hadja Bainon Karon</a>'s faction supported peace deal in 2012, after <a href="/wiki/Nur_Misuari" title="Nur Misuari">Nur Misuari</a> had criticized it.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> There was also a case of defections of <a href="/wiki/Moro_Islamic_Liberation_Front" title="Moro Islamic Liberation Front">Moro Islamic Liberation Front</a> (MILF) fighters to MNLF.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> As of 14&#160;August&#160;2015<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Moro_National_Liberation_Front&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, the current chairman of the group is uncertain and the sources, including <a href="/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation" title="Organisation of Islamic Cooperation">Organisation of Islamic Cooperation</a> and Philippine Government, disagree. Nur Misuari still has support among some of the factions.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many splits followed tribal affiliation.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council" title="United Nations Security Council">United Nations Security Council</a> report stated in 2010 that the splinter groups Moro Islamic Liberation Front and <a href="/wiki/Abu_Sayyaf" title="Abu Sayyaf">Abu Sayyaf</a> recruit and use <a href="/wiki/Child_soldier" class="mw-redirect" title="Child soldier">child soldiers</a> in the conflict.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Peace_talks">Peace talks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moro_National_Liberation_Front&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Peace talks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/1996_Final_Peace_Agreement" title="1996 Final Peace Agreement">1996 Final Peace Agreement</a></div>
<p>Libyan and <a href="/wiki/Muammar_Gaddafi" title="Muammar Gaddafi">Muammar Gaddafi</a>'s mediation resulted in the Tripoli Agreement on December 23, 1976. It would have established an autonomous region and given Moros influence on foreign policy, military, education, courts, and finances. Areas such as <a href="/wiki/Basilan" title="Basilan">Basilan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Palawan" title="Palawan">Palawan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sulu" title="Sulu">Sulu</a> would have been included in the autonomous region. The plans failed due to president <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Marcos" title="Ferdinand Marcos">Ferdinand Marcos</a>' decision to hold a referendum on each area that was to be included in the autonomous region. Most of the regions did not have a Muslim majority. The referendums however did influence <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_Region_in_Muslim_Mindanao" title="Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao">Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao</a> by creating its predecessors. MNLF decided to continued armed struggle.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The MNLF shifted from demands of full independence to autonomy in the 1980s. In 1986 a ceasefire and attempts to have a peace agreement were made, but they failed.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao was established in 1989, despite opposition from the MNLF.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>OIC, Libya and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> mediated peace talks which were restarted in 1992. Statements of Understanding and Interim Agreements were made between 1992 and 1996. Jakarta Peace Agreement was signed in 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tensions between the Philippine Government and the MNLF have been fueled by mineral wealth sharing, problems of implementing the peace agreement and the <a href="/wiki/Nur_Misuari" title="Nur Misuari">Nur Misuari</a> faction's ongoing opposition of the peace agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-Stanford_3-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stanford-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015 Nur Misuari rejected reports on the MNLF involvement in the <a href="/wiki/North_Borneo_dispute" title="North Borneo dispute">North Borneo dispute</a> and said only the <a href="/wiki/Sultanate_of_Sulu" title="Sultanate of Sulu">Sultanate of Sulu</a> can pursue the negotiations for the <a href="/wiki/Sabah" title="Sabah">Sabah</a> claim with the <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysian</a> sides. The MNLF has asserted that their group are not involved in any part of the North Borneo dispute and stressing it is a non-issue as Sabah has become the "home-base for different tribal groupings of Muslims from different regions of Southeast Asia that have enjoyed peaceful and harmonious co-existence with the <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Chinese" title="Malaysian Chinese">Chinese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christianity_in_Malaysia" title="Christianity in Malaysia">Christian</a> populace in the area."<sup id="cite_ref-misuari_statement_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-misuari_statement-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>European Union and the United States do not use the classification of "terrorist" for the MNLF.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Zamboanga_City_crisis">Zamboanga City crisis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moro_National_Liberation_Front&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Zamboanga City crisis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Zamboanga_City_crisis" title="Zamboanga City crisis">Zamboanga City crisis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bangsamoro_Republik" title="Bangsamoro Republik">Bangsamoro Republik</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:ZAMBOANGA_CITY_Asia%27s_Latin_City_City_Hall_and_Plaza_Rizal_(Ayunamiento_y_Plaza_Rizal).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/ZAMBOANGA_CITY_Asia%27s_Latin_City_City_Hall_and_Plaza_Rizal_%28Ayunamiento_y_Plaza_Rizal%29.jpg/220px-ZAMBOANGA_CITY_Asia%27s_Latin_City_City_Hall_and_Plaza_Rizal_%28Ayunamiento_y_Plaza_Rizal%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/ZAMBOANGA_CITY_Asia%27s_Latin_City_City_Hall_and_Plaza_Rizal_%28Ayunamiento_y_Plaza_Rizal%29.jpg/330px-ZAMBOANGA_CITY_Asia%27s_Latin_City_City_Hall_and_Plaza_Rizal_%28Ayunamiento_y_Plaza_Rizal%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/ZAMBOANGA_CITY_Asia%27s_Latin_City_City_Hall_and_Plaza_Rizal_%28Ayunamiento_y_Plaza_Rizal%29.jpg/440px-ZAMBOANGA_CITY_Asia%27s_Latin_City_City_Hall_and_Plaza_Rizal_%28Ayunamiento_y_Plaza_Rizal%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="576" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:ZAMBOANGA_CITY_Asia%27s_Latin_City_City_Hall_and_Plaza_Rizal_(Ayunamiento_y_Plaza_Rizal).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Zamboanga City hall pictured</div></div></div>
<p>In 2013, the Nur Misuari faction of the MNLF declared independence for the <a href="/wiki/Bangsamoro_Republik" title="Bangsamoro Republik">Bangsamoro Republik</a> and attacked <a href="/wiki/Zamboanga_City" title="Zamboanga City">Zamboanga City</a>. During the MNLF <a href="/wiki/Zamboanga_City_crisis" title="Zamboanga City crisis">standoff</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Armed_Forces_of_the_Philippines" title="Armed Forces of the Philippines">Armed Forces of the Philippines</a>, the group was accused by the Philippines of using civilians as <a href="/wiki/Human_shield" title="Human shield">human shields</a>, thus leading the Philippine government to label them terrorists.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MNLF_terrorism_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MNLF_terrorism-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The State Department of the United States included a mention of the siege in its report on "East Asia and Pacific Overview".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Present">Present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moro_National_Liberation_Front&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PRRD_MISUARI3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/PRRD_MISUARI3.jpg/220px-PRRD_MISUARI3.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/PRRD_MISUARI3.jpg/330px-PRRD_MISUARI3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/PRRD_MISUARI3.jpg/440px-PRRD_MISUARI3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1350" data-file-height="900" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PRRD_MISUARI3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>President Rodrigo Duterte with Nur Misuari during their meeting at Davao City</div></div></div>
<p>Currently, the Moro National Liberation Front under Nur Misuari is still in talks with President <a href="/wiki/Rodrigo_Duterte" title="Rodrigo Duterte">Rodrigo Duterte</a>. Misuari have met with Duterte ever since after the signing of the <a href="/wiki/Bangsamoro_Organic_Law" title="Bangsamoro Organic Law">Bangsamoro Organic Law</a> (BOL) in <a href="/wiki/Davao_City" title="Davao City">Davao City</a>. Duterte is also considering autonomy to be given to Misuari.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Misuari also talked with Duterte about Federalism<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> according to Presidential Spokesperson <a href="/wiki/Harry_Roque" title="Harry Roque">Harry Roque</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Under Yusoph Jikiri and Muslimin Sema Group, they are part of the <a href="/wiki/Bangsamoro_Transition_Commission" title="Bangsamoro Transition Commission">Bangsamoro Transition Commission</a> with the Moro Islamic Liberation Front. Jikiri and Sema along with her wife Congresswoman Bai Sandra Sema supports the Bangsamoro Organic Law.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flag">Flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moro_National_Liberation_Front&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Flag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:MNLF_flag_(no_inscription).svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/MNLF_flag_%28no_inscription%29.svg/220px-MNLF_flag_%28no_inscription%29.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/MNLF_flag_%28no_inscription%29.svg/330px-MNLF_flag_%28no_inscription%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/MNLF_flag_%28no_inscription%29.svg/440px-MNLF_flag_%28no_inscription%29.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="361" data-file-height="217" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:MNLF_flag_(no_inscription).svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Flag of the MNLF without inscriptions</div></div></div>
<p>The Moro National Liberation Front makes use of a flag consist of a golden yellow star and crescent and a <a href="/wiki/Kris" title="Kris">kris</a> on a red field. The star represents Truthfulness, Fairness, Equality and Tolerance while the crescent moon symbolizes wisdom. The kris symbolizes strength. The red field represents the Bangsamoro activism, decisiveness, persistence, frugality, and sacrifices in pushing forward the revolutionary struggle for survival, self-determination, and success. The flag design is secular despite having a star and crescent, a symbol often associated with Islam. The flag has not been standardized and many variation exists regarding the scaling of elements in the flag. A variant, with a <a href="/wiki/Shahadah" class="mw-redirect" title="Shahadah">shahadah</a> on the star and crescent exists. The flag was also used for the <a href="/wiki/Bangsamoro_Republik" title="Bangsamoro Republik">Bangsamoro Republik</a>, a widely unrecognized state declared by the group.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
