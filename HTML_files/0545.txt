<p><b>Taylor Hill</b> is a semi rural/industrial <a href="/wiki/Urban_village" title="Urban village">urban village</a> of the town of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> in the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> county of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. It lies on a hill above the <a href="/wiki/A616_road" title="A616 road">A616 road</a> to <a href="/wiki/Honley" title="Honley">Honley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penistone" title="Penistone">Penistone</a> and the eastern bank of the <a href="/wiki/River_Holme" title="River Holme">River Holme</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley" title="Holme Valley">Holme Valley</a>, approximately 1.5 miles (2&#160;km) to the south of <a href="/wiki/Lockwood,_Huddersfield" title="Lockwood, Huddersfield">Lockwood</a>, west of <a href="/wiki/Newsome" title="Newsome">Newsome</a> and to the north of <a href="/wiki/Berry_Brow" title="Berry Brow">Berry Brow</a>.
</p><p>Administratively, Taylor Hill is in the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees#2006_Local_government_election" title="Kirklees">Newsome Ward</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Metropolitan Borough of Kirklees</a>, the latter of which includes all of Huddersfield and surrounding areas.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taylor_Hill,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Former_chapel,_Taylor_Hill_Road,_Taylor_Hill,_Almondbury_-_geograph.org.uk_-_392202.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Former_chapel%2C_Taylor_Hill_Road%2C_Taylor_Hill%2C_Almondbury_-_geograph.org.uk_-_392202.jpg/220px-Former_chapel%2C_Taylor_Hill_Road%2C_Taylor_Hill%2C_Almondbury_-_geograph.org.uk_-_392202.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Former_chapel%2C_Taylor_Hill_Road%2C_Taylor_Hill%2C_Almondbury_-_geograph.org.uk_-_392202.jpg/330px-Former_chapel%2C_Taylor_Hill_Road%2C_Taylor_Hill%2C_Almondbury_-_geograph.org.uk_-_392202.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Former_chapel%2C_Taylor_Hill_Road%2C_Taylor_Hill%2C_Almondbury_-_geograph.org.uk_-_392202.jpg/440px-Former_chapel%2C_Taylor_Hill_Road%2C_Taylor_Hill%2C_Almondbury_-_geograph.org.uk_-_392202.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Former_chapel,_Taylor_Hill_Road,_Taylor_Hill,_Almondbury_-_geograph.org.uk_-_392202.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The former chapel in Taylor Hill road</div></div></div>
<p>On 15 March 1812, Taylor Hill was the site of the first large-scale <a href="/wiki/Luddite" title="Luddite">Luddite</a> operation in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding" class="mw-redirect" title="West Riding">West Riding</a>, when the cloth-finishing shop of <a href="/wiki/Frank_Vickerman" title="Frank Vickerman">Frank Vickerman</a> was attacked. Vickerman had previously been involved in coordinating the military and civil response to the Luddite threat, and a warning letter had been thrown into his premises several days earlier. Despite a guard being set on the premises, the Luddites succeeded in destroying ten <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shearing_frame&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Shearing frame (page does not exist)">shearing frames</a>, 30 shears, a quantity of wool and every window in the building.<sup id="cite_ref-Binfield_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Binfield-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry">Industry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taylor_Hill,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Industry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The former <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vickerman_%26_Sons_Ltd&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vickerman &amp; Sons Ltd (page does not exist)">Vickerman &amp; Sons Ltd</a> <a href="/wiki/Woollen_mill" class="mw-redirect" title="Woollen mill">woollen mill</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> on Fairlea Road has been converted into several smaller industrial units, which include a <a href="/wiki/Carpet" title="Carpet">carpet manufacturers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commercial_photography" class="mw-redirect" title="Commercial photography">commercial</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Industry" title="Industry">industrial</a> <a href="/wiki/Photographer" title="Photographer">photographers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Printer_(publishing)" title="Printer (publishing)">printers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lithographer" class="mw-redirect" title="Lithographer">lithographers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wedding_cake" title="Wedding cake">wedding cake</a> bakery, <a href="/wiki/Wrought_iron" title="Wrought iron">wrought Ironwork</a> services, <a href="/wiki/Clothing" title="Clothing">clothing manufacturers</a> &amp; wholesalers and an independent <a href="/wiki/Domestic_energy_assessor" title="Domestic energy assessor">domestic energy assessment services</a>. The former Victorian village chapel has been converted into a <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_engineers" class="mw-redirect" title="Hydraulic engineers">Hydraulic engineers</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taylor_Hill,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There is a secondary school, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Newsome_High_School_and_Sports_College&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Newsome High School and Sports College (page does not exist)">Newsome High School and Sports College</a> that is 0.5 miles (0.8&#160;km) to the east; one of three secondary schools in the area governed by Kirklees to be recognised as a specialist sports college since the government's inception of the programme in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The school's assistant head teacher was named West Yorkshire Teacher of the Year 2006 for an award sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Pulse_1_radio" class="mw-redirect" title="Pulse 1 radio">Pulse 1 radio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The area is also home to <a href="/wiki/Kirklees_College" title="Kirklees College">Kirklees College</a>'s Taylor Hill Centre, on Close Hill Road. This provides full-time courses relating to animal care, land-based studies, conservation and countryside management.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Taylor_Hill,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Berry_Brow_railway_station" title="Berry Brow railway station">Berry Brow railway station</a> is the closest local railway station, being about 0.5 miles (0.8&#160;km) to the south-east. Trains on <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern's</a> <a href="/wiki/Penistone_Line" title="Penistone Line">Penistone Line</a> provide an hourly service (less frequent on Sundays) between <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sheffield railway station">Sheffield</a> stations, calling at Berry Brow. Huddersfield station provides interchange with direct services to <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> and other north of England destinations, whilst Sheffield station provides interchange with direct trains to <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bus routes 306 and 319, run respectively by <a href="/wiki/First_Calderdale_%26_Huddersfield" title="First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield">First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger#Tiger_Blue" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Tiger Blue</a>, pass through the village, link it to Huddersfield town centre several times an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The closest main road is the  <a href="/wiki/A616_road" title="A616 road">A616 road</a> to <a href="/wiki/Honley" title="Honley">Honley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penistone" title="Penistone">Penistone</a>.
</p>
