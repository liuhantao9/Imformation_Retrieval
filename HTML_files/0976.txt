<p><b>St. Columba's Chapel</b> in <a href="/wiki/Middletown,_RI" class="mw-redirect" title="Middletown, RI">Middletown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, is a parish church of the <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_Diocese_of_Rhode_Island" title="Episcopal Diocese of Rhode Island">Episcopal Diocese of Rhode Island</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Episcopal_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="The Episcopal Church">the Episcopal Church</a>. The church is located at 55 Vaucluse Avenue, <a href="/wiki/Middletown,_RI" class="mw-redirect" title="Middletown, RI">Middletown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>.  The chapel is named for the Irish-born missionary <a href="/wiki/St._Columba" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Columba">St. Columba</a>, renowned for his teaching, healing, and miracles in sixth-century <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St._Columba%27s_Chapel_(Middletown,_Rhode_Island)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eugene Sturtevant and his wife, Mary Clark Sturtevant, daughter of <a href="/wiki/Thomas_March_Clark" class="mw-redirect" title="Thomas March Clark">Thomas March Clark</a>, Bishop of Rhode Island and later presiding Episcopal Bishop of the U.S, donated an acre of land in 1882 for a chapel to serve the neighboring community. The church was originally known as The Berkeley Memorial Chapel in honor of <a href="/wiki/Bishop_George_Berkeley" class="mw-redirect" title="Bishop George Berkeley">Bishop George Berkeley</a> of <a href="/wiki/Derry" title="Derry">Derry</a>. The cornerstone of the chapel was laid on October 11, 1884. The first service was held on June 23, 1885, even though the chapel was not complete. The chapel was consecrated on August 31, 1886, as a mission of the <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_Diocese_of_Rhode_Island" title="Episcopal Diocese of Rhode Island">Episcopal Diocese of Rhode Island</a> by Bishop Clark.
</p><p>In its early history, the chapel had a close association with <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s_School,_Newport" title="St. George&#39;s School, Newport">Saint George’s School</a> in Middletown. Many of the ordained teachers officiated at services, and before the school’s own chapel was built, boys walked to St. Columba’s for Sunday worship.
</p><p>The chapel contains a collection of eleven stained-glass windows, ten by <a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Maitland_Armstrong&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="David Maitland Armstrong (page does not exist)">David Maitland Armstrong</a>, executed by <a href="/wiki/Tiffany_Studio" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiffany Studio">Tiffany Studio</a>. The eleventh window, by an unknown artist, was donated by actor <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Booth" title="Edwin Booth">Edwin Booth</a>, brother of <a href="/wiki/John_Wilkes_Booth" title="John Wilkes Booth">John Wilkes Booth</a>, and his daughter, Edwina, in memory of Edwina's mother, Mary Devlin. The churchyard is distinguished by an English <a href="/wiki/Lychgate" title="Lychgate">lychgate</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Churchyard">Churchyard</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=St._Columba%27s_Chapel_(Middletown,_Rhode_Island)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Churchyard">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<ul><li>United States Senator <a href="/wiki/Claiborne_Pell" title="Claiborne Pell">Claiborne Pell</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dorrance_Hill_Hamilton" title="Dorrance Hill Hamilton">Dorrance Hill Hamilton</a> - philanthropist</li>
<li>Prince <a href="/wiki/Mdivani" title="Mdivani">Serge Mdivani</a> - socialite</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Joseph_Washington_Frazier&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Joseph Washington Frazier (page does not exist)">Joseph Washington Frazier</a> - industrialist</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Frazier_Jelke&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Frazier Jelke (page does not exist)">Frazier Jelke</a> - oleomargarine magnate</li>
<li>Commander <a href="/wiki/Hugo_W._Koehler" title="Hugo W. Koehler">Hugo W. Koehler</a>, USN - international man of mystery</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_Russell_Pope" title="John Russell Pope">John Russell Pope</a> - architect</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Raphael_Pumpelly" title="Raphael Pumpelly">Raphael Pumpelly</a> - explorer and geologist</li>
<li>Dr. <a href="/wiki/Alexander_H._Rice_Jr." title="Alexander H. Rice Jr.">Alexander Hamilton Rice, Jr.</a> - surgeon, geologist and explorer</li>
<li>Brigadier General <a href="/wiki/James_Henry_Van_Alen" title="James Henry Van Alen">James Henry Van Alen</a>, USV - Civil War general</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Van_Alen" title="Jimmy Van Alen">Jimmy Van Alen</a> - founder of the <a href="/wiki/International_Tennis_Hall_of_Fame" title="International Tennis Hall of Fame">International Tennis Hall of Fame</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Varick_Frissell" title="Varick Frissell">Varick Frissell</a> - filmmaker</li>
<li>Admiral <a href="/wiki/Harry_E._Yarnell" title="Harry E. Yarnell">Harry E. Yarnell</a>, USN</li></ul>
