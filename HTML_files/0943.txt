<p>The <b>Embassy of the United States of America in London</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_mission" title="Diplomatic mission">diplomatic mission</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is the largest <a href="/wiki/List_of_diplomatic_missions_of_the_United_States" title="List of diplomatic missions of the United States">American embassy</a> in <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">Western Europe</a><sup id="cite_ref-moving_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-moving-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and the focal point for events relating to the United States held in the United Kingdom.
</p><p>The new chancery in <a href="/wiki/Nine_Elms" title="Nine Elms">Nine Elms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Battersea" title="Battersea">Battersea</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> has been open to the public since 13 December 2017, and was formally opened on 16 January 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Symbolically the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_United_States" title="Flag of the United States">Flag of the United States</a> was raised for the first time, in the new building, on 12 January 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  From 1960 to 2018, it was located in the London Chancery Building in <a href="/wiki/Grosvenor_Square" title="Grosvenor Square">Grosvenor Square</a>, <a href="/wiki/City_of_Westminster" title="City of Westminster">Westminster</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>.
</p>

<p>The American <a href="/wiki/Legation" title="Legation">legation</a> in London was first situated in <a href="/wiki/Great_Cumberland_Place" title="Great Cumberland Place">Great Cumberland Place</a>, later moving to <a href="/wiki/Piccadilly" title="Piccadilly">Piccadilly</a>, 98 <a href="/wiki/Portland_Place" title="Portland Place">Portland Place</a> (1863–1866),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and 123 Victoria Street in Westminster (1883–1893). The legation was upgraded to an embassy in 1893 and remained at Victoria Street until 1912, when it moved to 4 <a href="/wiki/Grosvenor_Gardens,_London" title="Grosvenor Gardens, London">Grosvenor Gardens</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1938, the embassy was moved to <a href="/wiki/Macdonald_House,_London" title="Macdonald House, London">1 Grosvenor Square</a> (which later housed part of the <a href="/wiki/High_Commission_of_Canada_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="High Commission of Canada in the United Kingdom">Canadian High Commission</a>). 
</p><p>During this time, Grosvenor Square began to accommodate many U.S. government offices, including the headquarters of General <a href="/wiki/Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Dwight D. Eisenhower">Dwight D. Eisenhower</a> and the European headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>. Following <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Grosvenor,_2nd_Duke_of_Westminster" title="Hugh Grosvenor, 2nd Duke of Westminster">Duke of Westminster</a> donated land for a memorial to wartime <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a>.
</p><p>The American Embassy London Chancery Building was designed by <a href="/wiki/Finnish_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Finnish American">Finnish American</a> <a href="/wiki/Modern_architecture" title="Modern architecture">modernist</a> architect <a href="/wiki/Eero_Saarinen" title="Eero Saarinen">Eero Saarinen</a> and constructed in the late 1950s, opening in 1960. The United States paid only a symbolic <a href="/wiki/Peppercorn_rent" class="mw-redirect" title="Peppercorn rent">peppercorn rent</a> to the Duke of Westminster for use of the land.<sup id="cite_ref-ft_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ft-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In response to an American offer to buy the site outright, the duke's trustee requested the return of ancestral lands confiscated following the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>, namely the city of Miami.<sup id="cite_ref-ft_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ft-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The building has nine storeys, three of which are below ground. A large gilded aluminum <a href="/wiki/Bald_eagle" title="Bald eagle">bald eagle</a> by <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Roszak_(artist)" title="Theodore Roszak (artist)">Theodore Roszak</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> with a wingspan of over 11 metres (35&#160;feet), is situated on the roof of the Chancery Building, making it a recognizable London landmark.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In October 2009, the building was granted <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">Grade II listed status</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Spoilt_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spoilt-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The building has been described as a modernist classic and architectural gem.<sup id="cite_ref-veconomist_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-veconomist-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demonstrations">Demonstrations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Embassy_of_the_United_States,_London&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demonstrations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 1968, a crowd of some 10,000 demonstrated at <a href="/wiki/Trafalgar_Square" title="Trafalgar Square">Trafalgar Square</a> against US involvement in the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a>, before marching to Grosvenor Square. The <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Police" class="mw-redirect" title="Metropolitan Police">Metropolitan Police</a> had attempted to cordon off part of the square nearest to the embassy and there was violence as the crowd broke through the police line. <a href="/wiki/Police_horse" class="mw-redirect" title="Police horse">Police horses</a> were used to regain control. 200 demonstrators were arrested and 50 people needed hospital treatment including 25 police officers, one of them with a serious spinal injury.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In October of the same year, during a demonstration organised by the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_Solidarity_Campaign" title="Vietnam Solidarity Campaign">Vietnam Solidarity Campaign</a>, a splinter group of 6,000 demonstrators returned to the square. A thousand police officers formed a cordon that the protesters failed to breach and remained relatively peaceful until the crowd began to disperse when there was disorder in the neighbouring streets.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Security_concerns">Security concerns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Embassy_of_the_United_States,_London&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Security concerns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Security at the former embassy was tightened in the 1980s and 1990s following successive <a href="/wiki/Terrorist" class="mw-redirect" title="Terrorist">terrorist</a> attacks on U.S. embassies and <a href="/wiki/Consulate" class="mw-redirect" title="Consulate">consulates</a> worldwide. However, it was after the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a> in 2001 that security was significantly increased. A massive security operation at the embassy has seen one side of Grosvenor Square closed to public access by car, and armed roadblocks are stationed outside the building. On August 29, 2002, <a href="/wiki/Kerim_Chatty" title="Kerim Chatty">Kerim Chatty</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Swedish</a> citizen of <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisian</a> descent, was arrested at <a href="/wiki/Stockholm-V%C3%A4ster%C3%A5s_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Stockholm-Västerås Airport">Stockholm-Västerås Airport</a> trying to board a <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> Flight 685 destined for <a href="/wiki/London_Stansted_Airport" title="London Stansted Airport">London Stansted Airport</a> with a loaded gun in his luggage. Anonymous intelligence sources cited in the media claimed that the man was planning to hijack the aircraft and crash it into the United States embassy in London, using the rooftop eagle to identify it from the air. <a href="/wiki/Swedish_Security_Service" title="Swedish Security Service">Sweden's Security Service</a>, Säpo, denied the claims and called the reports "false information".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The man was subsequently cleared of all <a href="/wiki/Terrorism" title="Terrorism">terrorism</a>-related charges.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The security threat against the embassy prompted the U.S. government to consider moving the embassy. Several British media outlets reported that the U.S. government had wished to use <a href="/wiki/Kensington_Palace" title="Kensington Palace">Kensington Palace</a> as their embassy, which allegedly had been vetoed by Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a>, as several members of the <a href="/wiki/British_Royal_Family" class="mw-redirect" title="British Royal Family">British Royal Family</a> have their residences there. The embassy "strenuously denied" the reports, and a spokesman for <a href="/wiki/Buckingham_Palace" title="Buckingham Palace">Buckingham Palace</a> reported that no formal request had been made.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCKensington_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCKensington-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Another possible option was <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Barracks" title="Chelsea Barracks">Chelsea Barracks</a>, for which the U.S. Embassy made an unsuccessful bid in February 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-TimesonlineChelseaBarracks_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TimesonlineChelseaBarracks-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_building">New building</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Embassy_of_the_United_States,_London&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: New building">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:During_construction_of_the_new_US_Embassy_in_London.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/During_construction_of_the_new_US_Embassy_in_London.jpg/220px-During_construction_of_the_new_US_Embassy_in_London.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="204" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/During_construction_of_the_new_US_Embassy_in_London.jpg/330px-During_construction_of_the_new_US_Embassy_in_London.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/During_construction_of_the_new_US_Embassy_in_London.jpg/440px-During_construction_of_the_new_US_Embassy_in_London.jpg 2x" data-file-width="570" data-file-height="529" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:During_construction_of_the_new_US_Embassy_in_London.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>During construction of the new embassy in <a href="/wiki/Nine_Elms" title="Nine Elms">Nine Elms</a></div></div></div>
<p>On 8 October 2008, the embassy announced a conditional agreement with the real estate developer <a href="/wiki/Ballymore_Group" title="Ballymore Group">Ballymore Group</a> to purchase property for a new embassy site on the <a href="/wiki/South_Bank" title="South Bank">South Bank</a> of the <a href="/wiki/River_Thames" title="River Thames">River Thames</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Nine_Elms" title="Nine Elms">Nine Elms</a> area of the <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_of_Wandsworth" title="London Borough of Wandsworth">London Borough of Wandsworth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-embassy-design_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-embassy-design-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The site lies within the Vauxhall/Nine Elms/Battersea Opportunity Area as set out in the <a href="/wiki/London_Plan" title="London Plan">London Plan</a>. The proposed plan would only go forward if approved by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a> and by the local planning authority.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Northern_line_extension_to_Battersea" title="Northern line extension to Battersea">Northern line extension to Battersea</a> will have new stations at Battersea and Nine Elms—combined with major local development. The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_State" title="United States Department of State">United States Department of State</a> announced in January 2009 that it was choosing among nine architectural firms, all "modern" and "upmarket", to replace the ageing embassy headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-embassy_design_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-embassy_design-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In March 2009, the U.S. Department of State's Bureau of Overseas Buildings Operations announced that four architectural firms had been selected for the final phase of the design competition.<sup id="cite_ref-embassy_design_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-embassy_design-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> By law, the architect for a U.S. embassy must be an American firm with "numerous <a href="/wiki/Security_clearance" title="Security clearance">security clearances</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-embassy_design_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-embassy_design-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Embassy_of_the_United_States_of_America_in_Battersea_Nine_Elms,_London,_seen_from_Pimlico.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/New_Embassy_of_the_United_States_of_America_in_Battersea_Nine_Elms%2C_London%2C_seen_from_Pimlico.jpg/220px-New_Embassy_of_the_United_States_of_America_in_Battersea_Nine_Elms%2C_London%2C_seen_from_Pimlico.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/New_Embassy_of_the_United_States_of_America_in_Battersea_Nine_Elms%2C_London%2C_seen_from_Pimlico.jpg/330px-New_Embassy_of_the_United_States_of_America_in_Battersea_Nine_Elms%2C_London%2C_seen_from_Pimlico.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/New_Embassy_of_the_United_States_of_America_in_Battersea_Nine_Elms%2C_London%2C_seen_from_Pimlico.jpg/440px-New_Embassy_of_the_United_States_of_America_in_Battersea_Nine_Elms%2C_London%2C_seen_from_Pimlico.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1440" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Embassy_of_the_United_States_of_America_in_Battersea_Nine_Elms,_London,_seen_from_Pimlico.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The new embassy in <a href="/wiki/Nine_Elms" title="Nine Elms">Nine Elms</a> with construction work complete</div></div></div>
<p>In November 2009, the U.S. government conditionally agreed to sell the lease of the Grosvenor Square Chancery Building to <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatari</a> real-estate investment firm <a href="/wiki/Qatar_Investment_Authority" title="Qatar Investment Authority">Qatari Diar</a>, which in 2007 purchased the <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Barracks" title="Chelsea Barracks">Chelsea Barracks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TimesQatar_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TimesQatar-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Though the price was undisclosed, the lease's worth was estimated at <a href="/wiki/%C2%A3" class="mw-redirect" title="£">£</a>500 million in July 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-BloombergQatar_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BloombergQatar-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The development value of the property was reduced when the building was given <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">Grade II listed status</a>, requiring developers to maintain its current design. The building is now one of Mayfair's 238 listed buildings and monuments.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBCQatar_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCQatar-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, plans were approved for the conversion of the building into a hotel.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 23 February 2010, the U.S. government announced that a team led by the firm of <a href="/wiki/KieranTimberlake" title="KieranTimberlake">KieranTimberlake</a> had won the competition to design the new embassy building and surrounding green spaces.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The winning design resembles a crystalline cube, with a semi-circular pond on one side (called a "moat" by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i>)<sup id="cite_ref-TimesOnline-Battersea_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TimesOnline-Battersea-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and surrounded by extensive public green spaces<sup id="cite_ref-KieranTimberlake_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KieranTimberlake-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Embassy_Gardens" title="Embassy Gardens">Embassy Gardens</a> housing development.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Groundbreaking" title="Groundbreaking">Ground was broken</a> on 13 November 2013, and the building opened to the public on 13 December 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CNNQatar_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNNQatar-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">US President</a> <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> had been expected to visit in February 2018 to undertake the official opening of the new embassy, but in January 2018 announced he would not make the trip. Trump publicly criticized the cost of the new embassy and its location, as well as the apparent price received for the sale of the lease of the building in Grosvenor Square, blaming the administration of his predecessor, <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>, for making what he referred to as a "bad deal".<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> However, the decision to move the embassy was made before the Obama administration.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom">United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom</a> is <a href="/wiki/Woody_Johnson" title="Woody Johnson">Woody Johnson</a>, who was confirmed by the Senate on 3 August 2017. He was sworn in by Vice President <a href="/wiki/Mike_Pence" title="Mike Pence">Mike Pence</a> on 21 August 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
