<p><b>Saramacca</b> is a district of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, in the north. Saramacca's <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital city</a> is <a href="/wiki/Groningen,_Suriname" title="Groningen, Suriname">Groningen</a>, with other towns and cities including <a href="/wiki/Batavia,_Suriname" title="Batavia, Suriname">Batavia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kampong_Baroe" title="Kampong Baroe">Kampong Baroe</a>, Uitkijk, Maho and <a href="/wiki/Boskamp,_Suriname" title="Boskamp, Suriname">Boskamp</a>
</p><p>Saramacca has a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 17,480 and an <a href="/wiki/Area" title="Area">area</a> of 3,636&#160;km².
</p><p>The district has traditionally been the site of dozens of small, family owned <a href="/wiki/Farming" class="mw-redirect" title="Farming">farming</a> communities, and it has only been recently that large agricultural projects have begun to emerge, primarily geared to the production of <a href="/wiki/Banana" title="Banana">bananas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>.
</p><p>The district is known for its <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Ornithology" title="Ornithology">ornithologists</a> and <a href="/wiki/Birdwatching" title="Birdwatching">birdwatchers</a> coming from all over the world to study and admire Saramacca's <a href="/wiki/Toucan" title="Toucan">toucans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Parrot" title="Parrot">parrots</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cock-of-the-rock" title="Cock-of-the-rock">cocks-of-the-rock</a>.
</p><p>Saramacca is also the name of a group of <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">Maroons</a> who established communities along the <a href="/wiki/Saramacca_River" title="Saramacca River">Saramacca River</a> having fled <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a>.  
</p>
