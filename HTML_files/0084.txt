<p>"<b>Smoke Break</b>" is a song recorded by American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> artist <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> from her fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Storyteller_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" title="Storyteller (Carrie Underwood album)">Storyteller</a></i>. It was released on August 21, 2015, through <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Nashville" title="Sony Music Nashville">Sony Music Nashville</a>, as the album's lead single. It was written by Underwood, <a href="/wiki/Chris_DeStefano" title="Chris DeStefano">Chris DeStefano</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, and was produced by <a href="/wiki/Jay_Joyce" title="Jay Joyce">Jay Joyce</a>. Musically, it has lyrics about escaping life's many stresses.
</p><p>"Smoke Break" was met with critical acclaim, with music critics describing it as "a homage to hard working people in need of a breather". It quickly became a commercial success, setting an all-time record for the largest first-week adds at <a href="/wiki/Country_radio" title="Country radio">country radio</a>. It went on to reach number one on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Canada_Country" class="mw-redirect" title="Canada Country">Canada Country</a> chart and number two on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart in the US, becoming her 20th top-two single on the chart.
</p><p>The song's accompanying music video was directed by Randee St. Nicholas and won the 2016 <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="CMT Music Award">CMT Music Award</a> for Female Video of the Year. The song received its first televised performance at <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tonight_Show_Starring_Jimmy_Fallon" title="The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon">The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon</a></i>, on September 10, 2015.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Smoke_Break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Smoke Break" is a <a href="/wiki/Country_rock" title="Country rock">country rock</a> song that critics have described as "a homage to hard working people in need of a breather".<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It has lyrics about escaping life's many stresses.<sup id="cite_ref-Smoke_Break_lyrics_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smoke_Break_lyrics-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> About the writing process, Underwood stated that she, Chris DeStefano and Hillary Lindsey "were writing and kept taking breaks to go outside because we were getting a little stuck on a song we were already working on. It was so beautiful outside that we had a hard time focusing, so we decided to write a song about taking breaks! 'Smoke Break' seemed like a great title, so we ran with it!"<sup id="cite_ref-Smoke_Break_lyrics_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Smoke_Break_lyrics-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Smoke_Break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Smoke Break" has received rave reviews from music critics. Grady Smith of UK's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> acclaimed it, by stating "among all the great music we've been treated to this summer, no song has carried more significance than Carrie Underwood’s excellent new single 'Smoke Break'. Here we have an <a href="/wiki/A-list" title="A-list">A-list</a> star releasing an unabashed <a href="/wiki/Country_rock" title="Country rock">country rock</a> song that isn't trying to chase any sonic trends". He also added that Underwood "has emerged as the leader of country music".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Denise Quan of <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> praised the song, saying that the singer "pays homage to hard working people in need of a breather" and adding that Underwood puts "a bit more twang in her vocals than usual — a welcome dose of laid-back country cool after a string of glossy, high-octane arena rockers and soaring ballads".<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Jason Lipshutz of <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> awarded it 3.5 starts out of a possible five, noting Underwood's departure from previous material and stating that "the normally overpolished <i>American Idol</i> winner wears the song's blue-collar country-rock vibe well".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Hugh McIntyre of <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i> praised it, saying its lines evokes "some pretty sentimental themes that likely connect with a lot of people" and the song "has the makings of another runaway hit on radio".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dee Lockett of <i><a href="/wiki/Vulture.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Vulture.com">Vulture</a></i> praised it, pointing out that "just as songs about getting trashed will remain a rite of passage in country music, its superstars like Carrie Underwood will continue to find ways to keep them meaningful".<sup id="cite_ref-Vulture_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vulture-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Kevin John of <i>Country Universe</i> praised Underwood's songwriting by saying that "she has the finest singing voice of her generation, but her greatest gift is her ability to express empathy for others through song".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Taste_of_Country" class="mw-redirect" title="Taste of Country">Taste of Country</a></i> praised the song as well, stating that "it's a song everyone can relate to" and "is easy to wear, and adaptable to life's changing circumstances", and praised Underwood's delivery, saying "she starts soft before soaring into the chorus".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Christgau" title="Robert Christgau">Robert Christgau</a> deemed it as <i>Storyteller</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s most impressive song, "cross-regional, cross-gender, class-conscious ... in which neither the mother of four working three jobs nor the farm family's first college man can do without the occasional drag or drink, sincere Christians though they be".<sup id="cite_ref-Christgau_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Christgau-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/WSM-FM" title="WSM-FM">WSM-FM</a> and <a href="/wiki/WKDF" title="WKDF">WKDF</a> Nashville Program Director Charlie Cook praised the song as well, saying "the lyrics
address the hectic lives everyone now lives, and the production stands out as a testament that she is willing to take chances, yet play within what her fans expect". <a href="/wiki/IHeartMedia" title="IHeartMedia">iHeartMedia</a> Senior VP of Programming Gator Harrison said the song is “an anthem for the overwhelmed, something we can all relate to, and amen. It's a smash".<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Smoke_Break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"Smoke Break" garnered some controversy upon its release, after some listeners accused the song of "trying to promote <a href="/wiki/Smoking" title="Smoking">smoking</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-Vulture_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vulture-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Journalists for vehicles such as <i>Vulture</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">USA Today</a></i> went on to defend the song. Dee Lockett of <i>Vulture</i> stated that the accusation "misses the point" and defended it by saying that "like many of Carrie's narrative-driven songs, though, she's making a larger point. A 'stiff drink' and a 'long drag' are just <a href="/wiki/Placeholder_name" title="Placeholder name">placeholders</a> for any vice the overworked might depend on to survive. The concern isn't how Americans cope with their blue-collar lives (though she's by no means undermining the severity of addiction); it's the culture of stress that suffocates everyone that should piss people off".<sup id="cite_ref-Vulture_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vulture-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Maeve McDermott of <i>USA Today</i> says "the song is less a pro-cigarette anthem as it is a tribute to sneaking away from your responsibilities and taking a minute to breathe".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Smoke_Break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Smoke Break" set, according to <i>Taste of Country</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s Sterling Whitaker, "an all-time record for the largest first-week radio adds in the history of <i>Country Aircheck</i>, with 145 <a href="/wiki/Mediabase" title="Mediabase">Mediabase</a> adds and 159 total <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Billboard</a></i> and <i>Country Aircheck</i> reporting stations lined up for the song’s official airplay impact date".<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, the single had opening week sales of 56,000 in the United States, debuting at the top of the <i>Billboard</i> Country Digital Singles chart – her fifth leader on the chart – and moving from number 36 to number five on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> in its second week on the chart; it eventually peaked on <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> at number four on November 24, 2015. It peaked at number one on <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Canada_Country" class="mw-redirect" title="Canada Country">Canada Country</a> chart, becoming her second single to top that chart, the other being "<a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Blown Away (Carrie Underwood song)">Blown Away</a>". The song peaked at number two on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart and both debuted and peaked at number 43 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a> chart.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song was certified Gold by the RIAA on April 29, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodSmoke_BreaksingleCertRef_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-United_StatesCarrie_UnderwoodSmoke_BreaksingleCertRef-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> As of May 2016, it has sold 447,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-us_sales_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-us_sales-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Smoke_Break&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The accompanying music video was directed by Randee St. Nicholas, who directed Underwood's "<a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Blown Away (Carrie Underwood song)">Blown Away</a>" music video in 2012, and was shot over two days.<sup id="cite_ref-music_video_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-music_video-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> It premiered on <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Tonight" title="Entertainment Tonight">Entertainment Tonight</a></i> on August 24, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Underwood cast "gorgeous, strong women" to play a horse trainer, a truck driver and a waitress. "It just tells a story," Underwood said of featuring blue collar characters. "These are everyday jobs, and we need a break."<sup id="cite_ref-music_video_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-music_video-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The video begins with Underwood driving through the dusty, <a href="/wiki/Sepia_(color)" title="Sepia (color)">sepia</a>-washed desert in a vintage <a href="/wiki/Ford_trucks" class="mw-redirect" title="Ford trucks">Ford truck</a>. She passes a busy waitress in a <a href="/wiki/Diner" title="Diner">diner</a>, and a businessman struggling with his responsibilities. Then her truck overheats, and she has to hoof it, while a patient <a href="/wiki/Horse_trainer" title="Horse trainer">horse trainer</a> and weary welder toil nearby. Underwood hitchhikes the rest of the way to her gig at a neighborhood bar. She takes the stage in front of a small crowd of patrons that includes all the local characters met earlier who were working hard and could use a break. She sings their life story, then moves on to the next dusty town, "like an angel of mercy in <a href="/wiki/Daisy_Dukes_(clothing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Daisy Dukes (clothing)">Daisy Dukes</a>", <i>Rolling Stone</i> describes.<sup id="cite_ref-Rolling_Stone_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rolling_Stone-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The video won Female Video of the Year at the 2016 <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Awards" title="CMT Music Awards">CMT Music Awards</a>, and was also nominated for Video of the Year.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
