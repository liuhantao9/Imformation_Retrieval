	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Music genre</h1>

<p>A <b>music genre</b> is a conventional category that identifies some pieces of <a href="/wiki/Music" title="Music">music</a> as belonging to a shared tradition or set of conventions.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is to be distinguished from <i><a href="/wiki/Musical_form" title="Musical form">musical form</a></i> and <i>musical style</i>, although in practice these terms are sometimes used interchangeably.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (December 2014)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Academics have argued that categorizing music by genre is inaccurate and outdated.<sup id="cite_ref-ECON_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECON-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Music can be divided into different <a href="/wiki/Genre" title="Genre">genres</a> in many different ways. The artistic nature of music means that these classifications are often subjective and controversial, and some genres may overlap. Academic definitions of the term <i>genre </i>itself vary. In his book <i>Form in Tonal Music</i>, Douglass M. Green distinguishes between genre and <a href="/wiki/Musical_form" title="Musical form">form</a>. He lists <a href="/wiki/Madrigal_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Madrigal (music)">madrigal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Motet" title="Motet">motet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canzona" title="Canzona">canzona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ricercar" title="Ricercar">ricercar</a>, and dance as examples of genres from the <a href="/wiki/Renaissance_music" title="Renaissance music">Renaissance</a> period. To further clarify the meaning of <i>genre</i>, Green writes, "Beethoven's Op. 61 and Mendelssohn's Op. 64 are identical in genre—both are violin concertos—but different in form. However, Mozart's Rondo for Piano, K. 511, and the <i>Agnus Dei</i> from his Mass, K. 317, are quite different in genre but happen to be similar in form."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Some, like <a href="/wiki/Peter_van_der_Merwe_(musicologist)" title="Peter van der Merwe (musicologist)">Peter van der Merwe</a>, treat the terms <i>genre</i> and <i>style</i> as the same, saying that <i>genre</i> should be defined as pieces of music that share a certain style or "basic musical language."<sup id="cite_ref-Pete_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pete-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Others, such as Allan F. Moore, state that <i>genre</i> and <i>style</i> are two separate terms, and that secondary characteristics such as subject matter can also differentiate between genres.<sup id="cite_ref-Moore_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moore-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> A music genre or subgenre may also be defined by the <a href="/wiki/Musical_technique" title="Musical technique">musical techniques</a>, the cultural context, and the content and spirit of the themes. Geographical origin is sometimes used to identify a music genre, though a single geographical category will often include a wide variety of subgenres. Timothy Laurie argues that, since the early 1980s, "genre has graduated from being a subset of popular music studies to being an almost ubiquitous framework for constituting and evaluating musical research objects".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Musicologists have sometimes classified music according to a trichotomous distinction such as Philip Tagg's "axiomatic triangle consisting of 'folk', 'art' and 'popular' musics".<sup id="cite_ref-Analysing_Popular_Music_1982,_p._41_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Analysing_Popular_Music_1982,_p._41-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He explains that each of these three is distinguishable from the others according to certain criteria.<sup id="cite_ref-Analysing_Popular_Music_1982,_p._41_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Analysing_Popular_Music_1982,_p._41-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alternatively, music can be assessed on the three dimensions of "arousal", "valence", and "depth".<sup id="cite_ref-ECON_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECON-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Arousal reflects physiological processes such as stimulation and relaxation (intense, forceful, abrasive, thrilling vs. gentle, calming, mellow), valence reflects emotion and mood processes (fun, happy, lively, enthusiastic, joyful vs. depressing, sad), and depth reflects cognitive processes (intelligent, sophisticated, inspiring, complex, poetic, deep, emotional, thoughtful vs. party music, danceable).<sup id="cite_ref-ECON_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECON-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> These help explain why many people like similar songs from different traditionally segregated genres.<sup id="cite_ref-ECON_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ECON-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><br />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Art_music">Art music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_genre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Art music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Art_music" title="Art music">Art music</a></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_art_music_traditions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of art music traditions">List of art music traditions</a></div>
<p>Art music primarily includes classical traditions, including both <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_classical_music" title="Contemporary classical music">contemporary</a> and historical classical music forms. Art music exists in many parts of the world. It emphasizes formal styles that invite technical and detailed deconstruction<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and criticism, and demand focused attention from the listener. In Western practice, art music is considered primarily a written musical tradition,<sup id="cite_ref-Arnold,_Denis_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arnold,_Denis-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> preserved in some form of <a href="/wiki/Musical_notation" title="Musical notation">music notation</a> rather than being transmitted orally, by rote, or in recordings, as <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" title="Popular music">popular</a> and <a href="/wiki/Traditional_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Traditional music">traditional music</a> usually are.<sup id="cite_ref-Arnold,_Denis_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arnold,_Denis-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Historically, most western art music has been written down using the standard forms of music notation that evolved in Europe, beginning well before the Renaissance and reaching its maturity in the <a href="/wiki/Romantic_music" title="Romantic music">Romantic</a> period. The identity of a "work" or "piece" of art music is usually defined by the notated version rather than by a particular performance, and is primarily associated with the composer rather than the performer (though composers may leave performers with some opportunity for interpretation or improvisation). This is so particularly in the case of western classical music. Art music may include certain forms of <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, though some feel that jazz is primarily a form of popular music. The 1960s saw a wave of avant-garde experimentation in free <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a>, represented by artists such as <a href="/wiki/Ornette_Coleman" title="Ornette Coleman">Ornette Coleman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sun_Ra" title="Sun Ra">Sun Ra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albert_Ayler" title="Albert Ayler">Albert Ayler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Archie_Shepp" title="Archie Shepp">Archie Shepp</a> and Don Cherry.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> And avant-garde rock artists such as <a href="/wiki/Frank_Zappa" title="Frank Zappa">Frank Zappa</a>, Captain Beefheart, and The Residents released art music albums.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_music">Popular music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_genre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Popular music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" title="Popular music">Popular music</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jennifer_Lopez_-_Pop_Music_Festival_(35).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Jennifer_Lopez_-_Pop_Music_Festival_%2835%29.jpg/220px-Jennifer_Lopez_-_Pop_Music_Festival_%2835%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Jennifer_Lopez_-_Pop_Music_Festival_%2835%29.jpg/330px-Jennifer_Lopez_-_Pop_Music_Festival_%2835%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Jennifer_Lopez_-_Pop_Music_Festival_%2835%29.jpg/440px-Jennifer_Lopez_-_Pop_Music_Festival_%2835%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1063" data-file-height="597" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jennifer_Lopez_-_Pop_Music_Festival_(35).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Jennifer Lopez performing at a pop music festival</div></div></div>
<p>Popular music is any musical style accessible to the general public and disseminated by the <a href="/wiki/Mass_media" title="Mass media">mass media</a>. Musicologist and popular music specialist Philip Tagg defined the notion in the light of sociocultural and economical aspects:
</p>
<blockquote><p>Popular music, unlike art music, is (1) conceived for mass distribution to large and often socioculturally heterogeneous groups of listeners, (2) stored and distributed in non-written form, (3) only possible in an industrial monetary economy where it becomes a commodity and (4) in capitalist societies, subject to the laws of 'free' enterprise&#160;... it should ideally sell as much as possible.<sup id="cite_ref-Analysing_Popular_Music_1982,_p._41_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Analysing_Popular_Music_1982,_p._41-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Popular music is found on most commercial and <a href="/wiki/Public_service" title="Public service">public service</a> radio stations, in most commercial music retailers and department stores, and in movie and television soundtracks. It is noted on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts"><i>Billboard</i> charts</a> and, in addition to singer-songwriters and composers, it involves music producers more than other genres do.
</p><p>The distinction between classical and popular music has sometimes been blurred in marginal areas<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> such as <a href="/wiki/Minimalist_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Minimalist music">minimalist music</a> and light classics. Background music for films/movies often draws on both traditions. In this respect, music is like fiction, which likewise draws a distinction between <a href="/wiki/Literary_fiction" title="Literary fiction">literary fiction</a> and <a href="/wiki/Popular_fiction" class="mw-redirect" title="Popular fiction">popular fiction</a> that is not always precise.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rock_music">Rock music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_genre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rock music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">Rock music</a></div>
<p>Rock music is a broad genre of popular music that originated as "<a href="/wiki/Rock_and_roll" title="Rock and roll">rock and roll</a>" in the United States in the early 1950s, and developed into a range of different styles in the 1960s and later, particularly in the United Kingdom and in the United States.
</p>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip hop music</a></div>
<p>Hip Hop music, also referred to as <i>hip hop</i> or <i>rap music</i>, is a genre of music that was started in the United States, specifically the South Bronx in the New York City by African-American youth from the inner cities during the 1970s. It can be broadly defined as a stylized rhythmic music that commonly accompanies <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> a rhythmic and rhyming speech that is chanted.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Hip hop music derives from the hip hop culture itself, including four key elements: emceeing (<a href="/wiki/Master_of_ceremonies" title="Master of ceremonies">MCing</a>)/<a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapping</a>, Disc jockeying (<a href="/wiki/Disc_jockey" title="Disc jockey">DJing</a>) with <a href="/wiki/Turntablism" title="Turntablism">turntablism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Breakdancing" title="Breakdancing">breakdancing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Graffiti" title="Graffiti">graffiti art</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traditional_and_folk_music">Traditional and folk music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_genre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Traditional and folk music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Traditional and Folk music are very similar categories. Although the traditional music is a very broad category and can include several different genres, it is widely accepted that traditional music encompasses folk music.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/International_Council_for_Traditional_Music" title="International Council for Traditional Music">ICTM</a> (International Council for Traditional Music), traditional music are songs and tunes that have been performed over a long period of time (usually several generations)<b>.</b> <sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">Folk music</a> genre is classified as the music that is orally passed from one generation to another. Usually the artist is unknown, and there are several different versions of the same song.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The genre is transmitted by singing, listening and dancing to popular songs. This type of communication allows culture to transmit the styles (pitches and cadences) as well as the context it was developed.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Culturally transmitting folk songs maintain rich evidence about the period of history when they were created and the social class in which they developed.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Some examples of the Folk Genre can be seen in the <a href="/wiki/English_folk_music" title="English folk music">Folk music of England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkish_folk_music" title="Turkish folk music">Turkish Folk music</a>. English folk music has developed since the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">medieval period</a> and has been transmitted from that time until today. Similarly, Turkish Folk music relates to all the civilizations that once passed thorough Turkey, thereby being a world reference since the East-West tensions during the <a href="/wiki/Early_Modern_history" class="mw-redirect" title="Early Modern history">Early Modern Period</a>.
</p><p>Traditional Folk music usually refers to songs composed in the 20th century, which tend to be written as universal truths and big issues of the time they were composed.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Great names such as <a href="/wiki/Bob_Dylan" title="Bob Dylan">Bob Dylan</a>; <a href="/wiki/Peter,_Paul_and_Mary" title="Peter, Paul and Mary">Peter, Paul and Mary</a>; <a href="/wiki/James_Taylor" title="James Taylor">James Taylor</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Leonard_Cohen" title="Leonard Cohen">Leonard Cohen</a> transformed Folk music to what it is known today. Newer composers such as <a href="/wiki/Ed_Sheeran" title="Ed Sheeran">Ed Sheeran</a> (Pop Folk) and <a href="/wiki/The_Lumineers" title="The Lumineers">The Lumineers</a> (American Folk) are examples of <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_folk_music" title="Contemporary folk music">Contemporary Folk music,</a> which has been recorded and adapted to the new way of listening to music (online)—unlike the traditional way of orally transmitting music.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Each country in the world, in some cases each region, district and community, has its own Folk Music style. The different sub-divisions of Folk genre are developed by each place, cultural identity and history.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Because the music is developed in different places, a lot of the instruments are characteristic to location and population—but some are used everywhere: <a href="/wiki/Button_accordion" title="Button accordion">Button</a> or <a href="/wiki/Piano_accordion" title="Piano accordion">Piano Accordion</a>, different types of <a href="/wiki/Flute" title="Flute">flutes</a> or <a href="/wiki/Trumpet" title="Trumpet">trumpets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Banjo" title="Banjo">Banjo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ukulele" title="Ukulele">Ukulele</a>. Both French and Scottish folk music use related instruments such as the Fiddle, the harp and variations of bagpipes.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><br />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Emergence_of_new_genres_and_sub-genres">Emergence of new genres and sub-genres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_genre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Emergence of new genres and sub-genres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable relarticle mainarticle">For more information, see <b><a href="/wiki/Fusion_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fusion (music)">Fusion (music)</a></b>.</div>
<p>New genres can arise by the development of new forms and styles of music and also simply by creating a new categorization. Although it is conceivable to create a musical style with no relation to existing genres, new styles usually appear under the influence of pre-existing genres. The <a href="/wiki/Genealogy_of_musical_genres" title="Genealogy of musical genres">genealogy of musical genres</a> expresses, often in the form of a written chart, the way in which new genres have developed under the influence of older ones. If two or more existing genres influence the emergence of a new one, a fusion between them can be said to have taken place. The proliferation of popular music in the 20th century has led to over 1,200 definable sub-genres of music.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Social_influences_on_music_selection">Social influences on music selection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_genre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Social influences on music selection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since music has become more easily accessible (<a href="/wiki/Spotify" title="Spotify">Spotify</a>, <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>, <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, etc.), more people have begun listening to a broader and wider range of music styles.<sup id="cite_ref-:02_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> In addition, social identity also plays a large role in music preference. Personality is a key contributor for music selection. Someone who considers themselves to be a "rebel" will tend to choose heavier music styles like <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">Heavy Metal</a> or <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">Hard Rock</a>, while someone who considers themselves to be more "relaxed" or "laid back" will tend to choose lighter music styles like <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">Jazz</a> or <a href="/wiki/Classical_music" title="Classical music">Classical</a> music.<sup id="cite_ref-:02_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:02-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> There are five main factors that exist that underlie music preferences that are genre-free,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Category:Articles_contradicting_other_articles" title="Category:Articles contradicting other articles"><span title=" (date=September 2019)">contradictory</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and reflect emotional/affective responses.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> These five factors are:
</p>
<ol><li>A Mellow factor consisting of smooth and relaxing styles (Jazz, Classical, etc.).</li>
<li>An Urban factor defined largely by rhythmic and percussive music (Rap, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">Hip-Hop</a>, <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">Funk</a>, etc.).</li>
<li>A Sophisticated factor (<a href="/wiki/Opera_music" class="mw-redirect" title="Opera music">Operatic</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_music" title="World music">World</a>, etc.)</li>
<li>An Intensity factor that is defined by forceful, loud, and energetic music (Rock, Metal, etc.).</li>
<li>A campestral factor, which refers to singer-songwriter genres and country.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></li></ol>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Individual_and_situational_influences">Individual and situational influences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_genre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Individual and situational influences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Gender">Gender</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_genre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Gender">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Studies have shown that while women prefer more treble oriented music, men prefer to listen to bass heavy music. This is sometimes paired with <a href="/wiki/Borderline_personality_disorder" title="Borderline personality disorder">borderline</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antisocial_personality_disorder" title="Antisocial personality disorder">antisocial</a> personalities.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Age">Age</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_genre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Age">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Age is another strong factor that contributes to musical preference. Evidence is available that shows that music preference can change as one gets older.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> A Canadian study showed that adolescents show greater interest in pop music artists while adults and the elderly population prefer classic genres such as <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">Rock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">Opera</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">Jazz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
