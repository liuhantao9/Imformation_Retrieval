<p>The <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Suriname" title="Districts of Suriname">ten districts</a> of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> are divided into 62 <b>resorts</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i>ressorten</i>). Within the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, a resort entails a neighbourhood, in other cases it is more akin to a municipality, consisting of a central place with a few settlements around it. The resorts in the <a href="/wiki/Sipaliwini_District" title="Sipaliwini District">Sipaliwini District</a> are especially large, since the inlands of Suriname are sparsely inhabited.
</p><p>The average resort is about 2600&#160;km² and has almost 8,000 inhabitants. According to article 161 of the <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Suriname" title="Constitution of Suriname">Constitution of Suriname</a>, the highest political body of the resort is the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Resort_council&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Resort council (page does not exist)">resort council</a>. Elections for the resort council are held every five years and are usually at the same time as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_general_election&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Suriname general election (page does not exist)">Suriname general elections</a>.
</p>
