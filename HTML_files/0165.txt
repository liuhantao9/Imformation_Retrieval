<p><b>Cahit Sıtkı Tarancı</b> (born <b>Hüseyin Cahit</b>; October 4, 1910 &#8211; October 13, 1956) was a <a href="/wiki/Turkish_people" title="Turkish people">Turkish</a> <a href="/wiki/Poet" title="Poet">poet</a> and author.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cahit_S%C4%B1tk%C4%B1_Taranc%C4%B1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tarancı belonged to a well known clan family of Diyarbekir (present day: Diyarbakır) like his father <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pirin%C3%A7%C3%A7iz%C3%A2de_Bekir_S%C4%B1dk%C4%B1&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pirinççizâde Bekir Sıdkı (page does not exist)">Pirinççizâde Bekir Sıdkı</a> and his uncle <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pirin%C3%A7%C3%A7iz%C3%A2de_Aziz_Feyzi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pirinççizâde Aziz Feyzi (page does not exist)">Pirinççizâde Aziz Feyzi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ndgr_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ndgr-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tarancı finished his secondary education in <a href="/wiki/St._Joseph_High_School_(Istanbul)" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Joseph High School (Istanbul)">St. Joseph High School</a>, then graduated from <a href="/wiki/Galatasaray_High_School" title="Galatasaray High School">Galatasaray High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>. After Tarancı finished high school, he continued his education in the School of Political Sciences in Istanbul between the years 1931 and 1935. Then he left for <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, to study in the <a href="/wiki/Institut_d%27%C3%89tudes_Politiques_de_Paris" class="mw-redirect" title="Institut d&#39;Études Politiques de Paris">Institut d'Études Politiques de Paris</a>, but he had to return to <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> without completing his education in the wake of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> in 1940.
</p><p>From 1944 on, he worked as a translator in the state-owned news agency <a href="/wiki/Anadolu_Agency" title="Anadolu Agency">Anadolu Ajansı</a>, the Turkish Grain Board (TMO) and the Ministry of Labor.<sup id="cite_ref-k_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-k-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1951, he married Cavidan Tınaz. Following a severe illness in 1954, he became paralyzed. As the treatment of his health problem did not succeed in Turkey, he was taken to <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. He died on October 13, 1956 in a hospital there. His body was brought to Turkey and was laid to rest at the <a href="/wiki/Cebeci_Asri_Cemetery" title="Cebeci Asri Cemetery">Cebeci Asri Cemetery</a> in Ankara.<sup id="cite_ref-k_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-k-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
