	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">List of sovereign states and dependent territories in South America</h1>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:South_America_(orthographic_projection).svg" class="image"><img alt="South America (orthographic projection).svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/South_America_%28orthographic_projection%29.svg/350px-South_America_%28orthographic_projection%29.svg.png" decoding="async" width="350" height="350" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/South_America_%28orthographic_projection%29.svg/525px-South_America_%28orthographic_projection%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/South_America_%28orthographic_projection%29.svg/700px-South_America_%28orthographic_projection%29.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="541" data-file-height="541" /></a></div>
<p>This is a <b>list of <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign states</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dependent_territory" title="Dependent territory">dependent territories</a> in South America</b>. It includes both fully recognized states and dependent territories of both South American and non-South American states. It lists 12 sovereign states and 3 non-sovereign territories.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> is bordered on the west by the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> and on the north and east by the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a> lie to the northwest. South America has an <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_and_outlying_territories_by_total_area" class="mw-redirect" title="List of countries and outlying territories by total area">area</a> of approximately 17,840,000 square kilometres (6,890,000 sq mi), or almost 3.5% of the <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">Earth</a>'s surface. As of 2008, its <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> is more than 380 million, according to estimates of population in <i><a href="/wiki/The_World_Factbook" title="The World Factbook">The World Factbook</a></i>. South America <a href="/wiki/Continent#Area_and_population" title="Continent">ranks fourth</a> among all continents in area (after <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and North America) and <a href="/wiki/Continent#Area_and_population" title="Continent">fifth in population</a> (after Asia, Africa, Europe, and North America).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The division between North and South America is unclear, lying somewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Panama" title="Isthmus of Panama">Isthmus of Panama</a>. However, <a href="/wiki/Panama" title="Panama">Panama</a> is not considered <a href="/wiki/List_of_transcontinental_countries" title="List of transcontinental countries">transcontinental</a>, and is generally considered to be wholly a part of North America.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sovereign_states_having_the_largest_part_of_their_territories_in_South_America">Sovereign states having the largest part of their territories in South America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_sovereign_states_and_dependent_territories_in_South_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Sovereign states having the largest part of their territories in South America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">Sovereign state</a></div>
<p>A sovereign state is a political association with effective <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">sovereignty</a> over a population for whom it makes decisions in the national interest.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/Montevideo_convention" class="mw-redirect" title="Montevideo convention">Montevideo convention</a>, a state must have a permanent population, a defined territory, a government, and the capacity to enter into relations with other states.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The following states are all <a href="/wiki/List_of_United_Nations_member_states" class="mw-redirect" title="List of United Nations member states">members</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_Union_of_South_American_Nations" title="Member states of the Union of South American Nations">members</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_South_American_Nations" title="Union of South American Nations">Union of South American Nations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
