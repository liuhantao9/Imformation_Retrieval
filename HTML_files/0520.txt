<p><b>Surinam Airways</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">Surinaamse Luchtvaart Maatschappij</i>), also known by its initials <i>SLM</i>, is the <a href="/wiki/Flag_carrier" title="Flag carrier">flag carrier</a> of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Putting_Surinam_on_the_map_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Putting_Surinam_on_the_map-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> based in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It operates regional and long-haul scheduled passenger services. Its hub is at <a href="/wiki/Johan_Adolf_Pengel_International_Airport" title="Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport">Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport</a> (Zanderij). Surinam Airways is wholly owned by the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="Government of Suriname">Government of Suriname</a>, although plans have been made in the past to sell it.<sup id="cite_ref-Suriname_state_oil_company_considers_share_offering_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suriname_state_oil_company_considers_share_offering-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinam_Airways&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The airline was established in 1953 by private entrepreneurs Ronald <i>Rudi</i> Kappel and Herman van Eyck as the Kappel-Van Eyck Aviation Company (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">Luchtvaartbedrijf Kappel-Van Eyck</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-issuu.com_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-issuu.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>18</span></sup> aimed at operating feeder flights from a domestic network.<sup id="cite_ref-FI1979_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI1979-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Scheduled operations started in January 1955,<sup id="cite_ref-issuu.com_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-issuu.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> initially serving the Paramaribo–<a href="/wiki/Moengo" title="Moengo">Moengo</a> route.<sup id="cite_ref-FI1999_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI1999-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On 30 August 1962, the company was taken over by the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_(Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands)" title="Suriname (Kingdom of the Netherlands)">Surinamese government</a> and renamed <i>SLM&#160;&#8211;&#32;Surinaamse Luchtvaart Maatschappij</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-FI1979_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI1979-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FI1983_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI1983-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From 1964 Surinam Airways started scheduled international operations to <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a> together with <a href="/wiki/ALM_Antillean_Airlines" title="ALM Antillean Airlines">ALM Antillean Airlines</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-issuu.com_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-issuu.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Upon the country's independence in November&#160;1975<span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="bday dtstart published updated">1975-11</span>)</span>, the carrier was appointed as the national airline of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Suriname">Republic of Suriname</a>, and it also started services to <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> using a <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-8-63" class="mw-redirect" title="Douglas DC-8-63">Douglas DC-8-63</a> that was leased from <a href="/wiki/KLM" title="KLM">KLM</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FI1976_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI1976-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At March&#160;1980<span style="display:none">&#160;(<span class="bday dtstart published updated">1980-03</span>)</span>, the carrier had 400 employees. At this time, the fleet consisted of a Douglas DC-8-63, a <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-8#Series_50" title="Douglas DC-8">Douglas DC-8-50F</a> and three <a href="/wiki/Twin_Otter" class="mw-redirect" title="Twin Otter">Twin Otters</a>. It operated international routes to Amsterdam, <a href="/wiki/Belem" class="mw-redirect" title="Belem">Belem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Curacao" class="mw-redirect" title="Curacao">Curacao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgetown,_Guyana" title="Georgetown, Guyana">Georgetown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manaus" title="Manaus">Manaus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a> and <a href="/wiki/Panama_City" title="Panama City">Panama City</a>, and domestic services to <a href="/wiki/Apoera" title="Apoera">Apoera</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Avanavero&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Avanavero (page does not exist)">Avanavero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bakhuys" class="mw-redirect" title="Bakhuys">Bakhuys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Djoemoe" class="mw-redirect" title="Djoemoe">Djoemoe</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ladouanie&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ladouanie (page does not exist)">Ladouanie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moengo" title="Moengo">Moengo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nieuw_Nickerie" title="Nieuw Nickerie">Nieuw Nickerie</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FI1980-358_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI1980-358-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> From 1955 til 2005 Surinam Airways operated an extensive domestic network.<sup id="cite_ref-issuu.com_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-issuu.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Now, since 2013 <a href="/wiki/Caricom_Airways" title="Caricom Airways">Caricom Airways</a> is operating as a feeder commuter airline from the hinterland of Suriname, using two <a href="/wiki/Britten_Norman_Islander" class="mw-redirect" title="Britten Norman Islander">Britten Norman BN2 Islanders</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Cessna_206" title="Cessna 206">Cessna 206</a>, as <i>Surinam Airways Commuter</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On 7 June 1989, a Douglas DC-8-62 crashed on approach to <a href="/wiki/Johan_Adolf_Pengel_International_Airport" title="Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport">Zanderij Airport</a>, killing 175 occupants on board.<sup id="cite_ref-FI1990-12_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI1990-12-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At March 2000<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Surinam_Airways&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, the airline had 543 employees.<sup id="cite_ref-FI2000_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FI2000-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2009, Surinam Airways ordered two <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737-300" class="mw-redirect" title="Boeing 737-300">Boeing 737-300</a> aircraft to replace the <a href="/wiki/McDonnell_Douglas_MD-82" class="mw-redirect" title="McDonnell Douglas MD-82">McDonnell Douglas MD-82</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Putting_Surinam_on_the_map_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Putting_Surinam_on_the_map-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In November the same year, the carrier retired the 2011-bought <a href="/wiki/Boeing_747-300" class="mw-redirect" title="Boeing 747-300">Boeing 747-300</a> from service. It was replaced by the end of 2009 with a 317-seater <a href="/wiki/Airbus_A340" title="Airbus A340">Airbus A340</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Suriname_Jumboloos_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suriname_Jumboloos-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> A new <a href="/wiki/Boeing_737-700" class="mw-redirect" title="Boeing 737-700">Boeing 737-700</a> was ordered on lease from <a href="/wiki/Dubai_Aerospace_Enterprise" title="Dubai Aerospace Enterprise">DAE Capital</a> and arrived on 30 April 2018 in Suriname <sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> this plane was also formerly flown by <a href="/wiki/Air_China" title="Air China">Air China</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_affairs">Corporate affairs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinam_Airways&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Corporate affairs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At one time the company had its head office at <i>Mr. Jagernath Lachmonstraat 136</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> As of June&#160;2013<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Surinam_Airways&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, Surinam Airways was the owner of both the only terminal in <a href="/wiki/Johan_Adolf_Pengel_International_Airport" title="Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport">Zanderij Airport</a> and the only <a href="/wiki/Ground_handling" class="mw-redirect" title="Ground handling">ground handling</a> company in that airport.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accidents_and_incidents">Accidents and incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Surinam_Airways&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Accidents and incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>On 5 May 1978, a <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-6" title="Douglas DC-6">Douglas DC-6</a> from the Surinaamse Luchtvaart Maatschappij, registered N3493F was damaged beyond repair while landing at <a href="/wiki/Johan_Adolf_Pengel_International_Airport" title="Johan Adolf Pengel International Airport">Paramaribo-Zanderij International Airport</a> on a cargo flight from <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao_International_Airport" title="Curaçao International Airport">Curaçao Hato International Airport</a>. All 3 occupants survived.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 7 June 1989, <a href="/wiki/Surinam_Airways_Flight_764" title="Surinam Airways Flight 764">Flight 764</a>, a <a href="/wiki/US" class="mw-redirect" title="US">US</a>-<a href="/wiki/Aircraft_registration" title="Aircraft registration">registered</a> <a href="/wiki/Douglas_DC-8" title="Douglas DC-8">Douglas DC-8</a>, crashed 3 kilometres (1.9&#160;mi) west of <a href="/wiki/Zanderij_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Zanderij Airport">Zanderij Airport</a>, on approach, due to a <a href="/wiki/Pilot_error" title="Pilot error">pilot error</a>. Out of 176 occupants on board, only 11 people survived the accident, which remains the worst in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>'s history.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
