<p><b>Hyannis Port</b> (or <b>Hyannisport</b>) is a small residential village located in <a href="/wiki/Barnstable,_Massachusetts" title="Barnstable, Massachusetts">Barnstable, Massachusetts</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It is an affluent summer community on Hyannis Harbor, 1.4 miles to the south-southwest of <a href="/wiki/Hyannis,_Massachusetts" title="Hyannis, Massachusetts">Hyannis</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hyannis_Port,_Massachusetts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It has a small <a href="/wiki/United_States_Postal_Service" title="United States Postal Service">post office</a> (ZIP code 02647) next door to a small seasonal <a href="/wiki/Convenience_store" title="Convenience store">convenience store</a>, The News Shop and Gallery. It has one of the premier golf courses on Cape Cod, the <a href="/wiki/Hyannisport_Club" title="Hyannisport Club">Hyannisport Club</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and is also home to the West Beach Club and the Hyannis Port Yacht Club.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> St. Andrew's-by-the-Sea Episcopal Church<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and the Union Chapel<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> conduct Sunday services in the summer.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kennedy_residences">Kennedy residences</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hyannis_Port,_Massachusetts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Kennedy residences">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hyannis Port is the location of the <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_compound" class="mw-redirect" title="Kennedy compound">Kennedy compound</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and other <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_political_family" class="mw-redirect" title="Kennedy political family">Kennedy family</a> residences and, as such, is included in the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>. There are three Kennedy houses on the compound: Rose's, Jack's, and Ethel's. (Ted's house was nearby but not in the compound itself.) <a href="/wiki/Rose_Fitzgerald_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Rose Fitzgerald Kennedy">Rose Fitzgerald Kennedy</a> was living in Hyannis Port and was the oldest resident in the town of Barnstable when she died at age 104.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hyannis_Port,_Massachusetts&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the 2000 <a href="/wiki/U.S._Census" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Census">U.S. Census</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> on April 1, it had 193 housing units with a resident population of 115 persons living in 46 housing units. There were 147 vacant housing units (76%), 144 of which were for seasonal, recreational, or occasional use. Based on the average family size of 2.97 persons, the summer population is at least 573.
</p>
