<p><b>Kirklees</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Local_government_district" class="mw-redirect" title="Local government district">local government district</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England, governed by <a href="/wiki/Kirklees_Council" title="Kirklees Council">Kirklees Council</a> with the status of a <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a>. The largest town and <a href="/wiki/Administrative_centre" title="Administrative centre">administrative centre</a> of Kirklees is <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, and the district also includes <a href="/wiki/Batley" title="Batley">Batley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birstall,_West_Yorkshire" title="Birstall, West Yorkshire">Birstall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cleckheaton" title="Cleckheaton">Cleckheaton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denby_Dale" title="Denby Dale">Denby Dale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury" title="Dewsbury">Dewsbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heckmondwike" title="Heckmondwike">Heckmondwike</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth" title="Holmfirth">Holmfirth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kirkburton" title="Kirkburton">Kirkburton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marsden,_West_Yorkshire" title="Marsden, West Yorkshire">Marsden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meltham" title="Meltham">Meltham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mirfield" title="Mirfield">Mirfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slaithwaite" title="Slaithwaite">Slaithwaite</a>. Kirklees had a population of 422,500 in 2011 and is therefore the most <a href="/wiki/List_of_English_districts_by_population" title="List of English districts by population">populous</a> borough in England that is not a <a href="/wiki/City_status_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="City status in the United Kingdom">city</a>; it is also the third largest metropolitan district by <a href="/wiki/List_of_English_districts_by_area" title="List of English districts by area">area</a> behind <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Doncaster" title="Metropolitan Borough of Doncaster">Doncaster</a> and <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">Leeds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The borough was formed on 1 April 1974 by the provisions of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a> as part of a reform of <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_England" title="Local government in England">local government in England</a>. Eleven former local government districts were <a href="/wiki/Amalgamation_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Amalgamation (politics)">merged</a>: the <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county boroughs</a> of Huddersfield and Dewsbury, the <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal boroughs</a> of Batley and <a href="/wiki/Spenborough" title="Spenborough">Spenborough</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban districts</a> of <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley" title="Colne Valley">Colne Valley</a>, Denby Dale, Heckmondwike, <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley" title="Holme Valley">Holme Valley</a>, Kirkburton, Meltham and Mirfield.
</p><p>The name <i>Kirklees</i> was chosen by the merging councils from more than fifty suggestions, including <i><a href="/wiki/Agbrigg_and_Morley" title="Agbrigg and Morley">Upper Agbrigg</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Brigantes" title="Brigantes">Brigantia</a></i> and <i>Wooldale</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-times1974_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times1974-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was named after <a href="/wiki/Kirklees_Priory" title="Kirklees Priory">Kirklees Priory</a>, legendary burial place of <a href="/wiki/Robin_Hood" title="Robin Hood">Robin Hood</a>, situated midway between Huddersfield and Dewsbury. The priory was located within the present-day Kirklees Park estate, most of which actually lies in the neighbouring borough of <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-times1974_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times1974-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Under the original draft of the Act, the district would have included <a href="/wiki/Ossett" title="Ossett">Ossett</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Dewsbury (UK Parliament constituency)">Dewsbury Parliamentary constituency</a> at that time. It was eventually decided that Ossett was too remote to be governed from Huddersfield and the town was included within the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Wakefield_Metropolitan_District_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="City of Wakefield Metropolitan District Council">Wakefield</a> district instead.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The principal settlements of Kirklees are <a href="/wiki/Mill_town#United_Kingdom" title="Mill town">mill towns</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley" title="Colne Valley">Colne Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley" title="Holme Valley">Holme Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">Calder Valley</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Spen" title="River Spen">Spen Valley</a>. Those areas of the district with a more urban character bound <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a> to the west, <a href="/wiki/City_of_Bradford" title="City of Bradford">Bradford</a> to the northwest, <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">Leeds</a> to the northeast and <a href="/wiki/City_of_Wakefield" title="City of Wakefield">Wakefield</a> to the east.
</p><p>The district also includes several rural villages, with the largest rural area extending from the south of Huddersfield. The Pennine countryside to the southwest of Meltham and <a href="/wiki/Holme,_West_Yorkshire" title="Holme, West Yorkshire">Holme</a> lies within the <a href="/wiki/Peak_District" title="Peak District">Peak District</a> National Park.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This moorland area mostly bounds <a href="/wiki/Saddleworth" title="Saddleworth">Saddleworth</a>, a traditional part of Yorkshire but now locally governed from <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Oldham" title="Metropolitan Borough of Oldham">Oldham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a>. There is then a relatively short border with the <a href="/wiki/High_Peak,_Derbyshire" title="High Peak, Derbyshire">High Peak</a> district of <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a> running across the summit of <a href="/wiki/Black_Hill_(Peak_District)" title="Black Hill (Peak District)">Black Hill</a>, and then the main border to the south of Kirklees is with <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Barnsley" title="Metropolitan Borough of Barnsley">Barnsley</a>.
</p><p>The inclusion of two county boroughs resulted in a district without an obvious centre. Over the years there have been suggestions of splitting the district into two, administered from Huddersfield and Dewsbury.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Graham_Riddick" title="Graham Riddick">Graham Riddick</a>, as MP for <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Colne Valley (UK Parliament constituency)">Colne Valley</a>, campaigned for a split in the early 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> A similar ambition was mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Peacock" title="Elizabeth Peacock">Elizabeth Peacock</a>, MP for <a href="/wiki/Batley_and_Spen_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Batley and Spen (UK Parliament constituency)">Batley and Spen</a> in 1991.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The boundaries of metropolitan boroughs were outside the remit of the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Commission_for_England_(1992)" title="Local Government Commission for England (1992)">Banham Commission</a> appointed to review local government structures in 1992 or its successors, and only minor boundary changes were made with neighbouring districts in 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The district includes parts of three postcode areas. Huddersfield and the rural areas to the south have <a href="/wiki/HD_postcode_area" title="HD postcode area">HD</a> postcodes, <a href="/wiki/Birkenshaw,_West_Yorkshire" title="Birkenshaw, West Yorkshire">Birkenshaw</a>, Cleckheaton and <a href="/wiki/Gomersal" title="Gomersal">Gomersal</a> have <a href="/wiki/BD_postcode_area" title="BD postcode area">BD</a> postcodes, and the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Heavy_Woollen_District" title="Heavy Woollen District">Heavy Woollen</a> area has <a href="/wiki/WF_postcode_area" title="WF postcode area">WF</a> postcodes. Similarly the district is split between several telephone dialling codes, with most residents in the 01484 (Huddersfield), 01274 (Bradford) and 01924 (Wakefield) codes.  A small number of residents in Birchencliffe and Birkenshaw villages fall within the 01422 (Halifax) and 0113 (Leeds) codes respectively.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Public transport information is provided by <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Metro" title="West Yorkshire Metro">Metro</a>, as is the case across West Yorkshire.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kirklees lies along the core <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_line" title="Huddersfield line">Huddersfield Line</a> of the <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a> network, with services calling at <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_railway_station" title="Huddersfield railway station">Huddersfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_railway_station" title="Dewsbury railway station">Dewsbury</a>. Direct <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a> services to <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London King's Cross</a> call at <a href="/wiki/Mirfield_railway_station" title="Mirfield railway station">Mirfield</a>. Other railway stations in the district on these routes and on the <a href="/wiki/Penistone_Line" title="Penistone Line">Penistone Line</a> have local <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> services. Some towns in Kirklees have not been served by rail transport since the <a href="/wiki/Beeching_cuts" title="Beeching cuts">Beeching cuts</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most bus services in the Huddersfield area are operated by <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Yorkshire Tiger</a> and <a href="/wiki/First_West_Yorkshire" title="First West Yorkshire">First</a>, and most bus services in the Heavy Woollen area are operated by <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Yorkshire" title="Arriva Yorkshire">Arriva</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Road">Road</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Road">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The urban areas of Kirklees are served by the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62</a> and <a href="/wiki/M1_motorway" title="M1 motorway">M1</a> motorways. Parts of the local road network are considered to require improvement, such as the main route from Huddersfield to the southbound M1 which narrows as it passes through <a href="/wiki/Flockton" title="Flockton">Flockton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bicycle">Bicycle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Bicycle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kirklees Council has developed a number of traffic-free cycle paths called Greenways in partnership with <a href="/wiki/Sustrans" title="Sustrans">Sustrans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</p><p>Tourist information in Kirklees can be obtained from major libraries.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a> play <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">football</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> as of the 2017-18 season. They were the first English club to win three successive league titles.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/History_of_rugby_league" title="History of rugby league">birthplace of rugby league</a> was at the <a href="/wiki/George_Hotel,_Huddersfield" title="George Hotel, Huddersfield">George Hotel, Huddersfield</a>; local clubs include <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a> side <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Giants" title="Huddersfield Giants">Huddersfield Giants</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Batley_Bulldogs" title="Batley Bulldogs">Batley Bulldogs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dewsbury_Rams" title="Dewsbury Rams">Dewsbury Rams</a> who both play in the <a href="/wiki/Championship_(rugby_league)" class="mw-redirect" title="Championship (rugby league)">Championship</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Council">Council</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Council">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p><a href="/wiki/Kirklees_Council" title="Kirklees Council">Kirklees Council</a> is the local authority of the district. The council is composed of 69 councillors, three for each of the borough's 23 <a href="/wiki/Wards_and_electoral_divisions_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Wards and electoral divisions of the United Kingdom">wards</a>. Elections are held three years out of four, on the first Thursday of May. One third of the councillors are elected, for a four-year term, in each election. The council is currently led by a <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> executive.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Borough_status_and_mayoralty">Borough status and mayoralty</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Borough status and mayoralty">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The shadow Kirklees District Council petitioned the <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Most_Honourable_Privy_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Her Majesty&#39;s Most Honourable Privy Council">privy council</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Royal_charter" title="Royal charter">royal charter</a> under section 245 of the Local Government Act 1972 granting the <a href="/wiki/Borough_status_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Borough status in the United Kingdom">status of a borough</a> from 1 April 1974.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The grant of borough status entitled the chairman of the council to the title of "<a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a>", effectively continuing the mayoralties of the former boroughs of Dewsbury (1862), Huddersfield (1898), Batley (1869) and Spenborough (1955).<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The mayor is elected from among the councillors for a one-year term (the "civic year") at the council's annual meeting.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kirklees is the most populated borough or district in England not to have <a href="/wiki/List_of_cities_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of cities in the United Kingdom">city status</a>. In 2001 it was announced that a grant of city status was to be made to an English town to mark the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Jubilee_of_Elizabeth_II" title="Golden Jubilee of Elizabeth II">Golden Jubilee of Elizabeth II</a>, and Kirklees council indicated that it was considering applying on behalf of Huddersfield. An unofficial telephone poll by the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Examiner" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Examiner">Huddersfield Examiner</a> found a slim majority against the proposal, and the council did not proceed with the application.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coat_of_arms">Coat of arms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Coat of arms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kirklees Borough Council was granted <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">armorial bearings</a> by the <a href="/wiki/College_of_Arms" title="College of Arms">College of Arms</a> by <a href="/wiki/Letters_patent" title="Letters patent">letters patent</a> dated 24 June 1974. the <a href="/wiki/Blazon" title="Blazon">blazon</a> of the arms is as follows:
</p>
<blockquote><p>Vert on a bend Argent a bendlet wavy azure on a chief Or a pale between two cog-wheels azure on the pale a Paschal Lamb supporting a staff of the fourth flying therefrom a forked pennon argent charged with a cross gules; <i>and for a Crest</i>, On a wreath of the colours a ram's head affronty couped argent armed Or gorged with a mural crown sable masoned argent.
</p><p><i>Supporters</i>: On either side a lion guardant purpure resting the inner hind leg on a cross crosslet Or embellished in each of the four angles with a fleur de lis azure. <i>Badge or device</i>: A roundel purpure charged with a Lacy Knot Or all within a circle of eleven roses argent barbed and seeded proper.<sup id="cite_ref-civic_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-civic-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The green colouring of the shield represents the fields, woods and moorland of the borough. The white stripe or <i>bend</i> represents the <a href="/wiki/M62_motorway" title="M62 motorway">M62 motorway</a>, while the blue wave upon it is for the many waterways of the area. On the <i>chief</i> or upper third of the shield is a <a href="/wiki/Korban_Pesach" class="mw-redirect" title="Korban Pesach">paschal lamb</a>, symbol of <a href="/wiki/St_John_the_Baptist" class="mw-redirect" title="St John the Baptist">St John the Baptist</a>. John was the patron saint of woolworkers, and the inclusion of the emblem represents the historic woollen industry. The cogwheels are for the modern engineering industries. The crest is a ram's head, found in the arms of the County Borough of Huddersfield and the Mirfield Urban District Council. The black mural crown stands for the district's status as a borough, recalling a city wall. The <a href="/wiki/Supporters" class="mw-redirect" title="Supporters">supporters</a> are purple lions from the arms of the <a href="/wiki/De_Lacy" title="De Lacy">de Laci</a> family, medieval lords of Huddersfield. For heraldic "difference" from other lion supporters a distinctive cross has been placed below their inner feet. This device, combining the symbols of Christ and the Virgin Mary, represents the priory from which the borough took its name.<sup id="cite_ref-civic_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-civic-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Parliamentary_representation">Parliamentary representation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Parliamentary representation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1997_to_date">1997 to date</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: 1997 to date">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Since 1997 Kirklees has been divided into five constituencies: four being entirely within the borough, while one ward (Wakefield) is included in the Wakefield Council borough.
</p><p>The boundaries of two of the Colne Valley and Huddersfield constituencies were virtually unchanged from those defined in 1983. Denby Dale and Kirkburton wards were transferred from Dewsbury to Wakefield, with the former constituency receiving Heckmondwike ward from Batley and Spen.
</p><p>The constituencies were first used at the <a href="/wiki/1997_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1997 United Kingdom general election">1997 general election</a>, when the Labour Party came to power in a <a href="/wiki/List_of_landslide_victories" class="mw-redirect" title="List of landslide victories">landslide</a>, gaining all the seats in the borough. The party held the seats at the subsequent elections of <a href="/wiki/2001_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2001 United Kingdom general election">2001</a> and <a href="/wiki/2005_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2005 United Kingdom general election">2005</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1995order_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1995order-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The incumbent MP for <a href="/wiki/Batley_and_Spen_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Batley and Spen (UK Parliament constituency)">Batley and Spen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jo_Cox" title="Jo Cox">Jo Cox</a>, was murdered on 16 June 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/2016_Batley_and_Spen_by-election" title="2016 Batley and Spen by-election">constituency by-election</a> took place on 20 October 2016 and <a href="/wiki/Tracy_Brabin" title="Tracy Brabin">Tracy Brabin</a> was elected.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1983_to_1997">1983 to 1997</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: 1983 to 1997">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/1983_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1983 United Kingdom general election">1983 general election</a> was the first at which constituencies based on the administrative areas created in 1974 were used. Kirklees was divided into four constituencies.<sup id="cite_ref-1983order_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1983order-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The Conservative Party polled well in the 1983 election, and took two of the borough's constituencies. Labour held Huddersfield, while the Liberals, running in an <a href="/wiki/SDP-Liberal_Alliance" class="mw-redirect" title="SDP-Liberal Alliance">alliance</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_(UK)" title="Social Democratic Party (UK)">Social Democrats</a>, held Colne Valley. In the following <a href="/wiki/1987_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1987 United Kingdom general election">election in 1987</a> the Labour vote increased slightly, and they gained Dewsbury from the Conservatives. At the same time the Alliance vote fell, and the Conservatives took Colne Valley. The four MPs elected in 1992 were all returned in 1997.

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="1974_to_1983">1974 to 1983</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kirklees&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: 1974 to 1983">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Parliamentary constituencies in England and Wales continued to be defined in terms of the boroughs and districts abolished in 1974 until a general redistribution of seats in 1983. Accordingly, Kirklees was divided between seven constituencies, which had first been used in the <a href="/wiki/1950_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1950 United Kingdom general election">1950 general election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
