<p><b>Crystal Leefmans</b> (born 6 January 1995) is a <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Surinamese</a> <a href="/wiki/Badminton" title="Badminton">badminton</a> player.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, she won the bronze medals at the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2010_South_American_Games" title="Badminton at the 2010 South American Games">Medellín South American Games</a> in the mixed doubles and team event.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, she won the mixed doubles title at the <a href="/wiki/Carebaco_International" title="Carebaco International">Carebaco International</a> tournament in the individuals event of the Carebaco Games partnered with <a href="/wiki/Mitchel_Wongsodikromo" title="Mitchel Wongsodikromo">Mitchel Wongsodikromo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Leefmans was also the champion at the <a href="/wiki/Suriname_International" title="Suriname International">Suriname International</a> tournament in 2012 and 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2018 she won the mixed doubles <a href="/wiki/Carebaco_International" title="Carebaco International">Carebaco International</a> title again at home, this time partnering compatriot <a href="/wiki/Dylan_Darmohoetomo" title="Dylan Darmohoetomo">Dylan Darmohoetomo</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crystal_Leefmans&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Crystal Leefmans, who originates from Badminton Club Lelydorp and switched to club TNF after moving to the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, was voted Female Badminton Player of the year in Suriname in 2010, 2012, 2013, 2014 &amp; 2015.
After winning many National juniors titles, Crystal won her first of a total of five National Women's singles titles in 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Surinamese_National_Badminton_Championships" title="Surinamese National Badminton Championships">Surinamese National Badminton Championships</a>. She held on to her title in 2010, then lost it to Danielle Melchiot the following year, but regained her singles title in 2012 to lose it again to Melchiot the following year 2013. In 2014 she took her fourth singles title and defended this one successfully in 2015. Crystal Leefmans also holds five National Women's doubles titles (2009, 2012, 2014, 2015 and 2017) and three National Mixed doubles titles (2010, 2012 &amp; 2014). With these achievements she is holder of two National Triple crowns in 2012 and 2014.
</p>
