<p><b>Marsden</b> is a large village within the <a href="/wiki/Kirklees" title="Kirklees">Metropolitan Borough of Kirklees</a> district, in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. It is in the southern edge of the <a href="/wiki/South_Pennines" title="South Pennines">South Pennines</a> and is fringed by the <a href="/wiki/Peak_District" title="Peak District">Peak District</a> to the south. The village is 7 miles (11&#160;km) west of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and located at the confluence of the <a href="/wiki/River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Colne, West Yorkshire">River Colne</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Wessenden_Valley" title="Wessenden Valley">Wessenden Brook</a>. It was an important centre for the production of woollen cloth, focused at Bank Bottom Mill, which closed in 2003. According to a 2008 mid-year estimate the village has a population of 4,440.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marsden,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marsden grew wealthy in the 19th century from the production of woollen cloth. It is still home to <a href="/wiki/Bank_Bottom_Mill" title="Bank Bottom Mill">Bank Bottom Mill</a>, later known as Marsden Mill, and home to <a href="/wiki/John_Edward_Crowther" title="John Edward Crowther">John Edward Crowther Ltd</a>, formerly one of the largest mills in Yorkshire. The Crowthers moved to Marsden in 1876, beginning a long and profitable association with cloth manufacturing in the town.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the 1930s Bank Bottom Mill covered an area of 14 acres, employed 680 looms, and provided employment for 1,900 workers.<sup id="cite_ref-View_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-View-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Church of <a href="/wiki/Bartholomew_the_Apostle" title="Bartholomew the Apostle">St Bartholomew</a> was completed in 1899, although the <a href="/wiki/Nave" title="Nave">nave</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aisle#Architecture" title="Aisle">aisle</a> had been in use from 1895, when the previous chapel was demolished. The tower was built in 1911, and the Parochial Hall in 1924 (with an extension in 1978). The church has a <a href="/wiki/Ring_of_bells" title="Ring of bells">peal</a> of ten bells.<sup id="cite_ref-mlhgcc_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mlhgcc-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Production of woollen cloth at Bank Bottom Mill ceased in 2003, with the loss of 244 jobs.<sup id="cite_ref-View_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-View-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marsden,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Bartholomew,_Marsden_1_(3272797404).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/St_Bartholomew%2C_Marsden_1_%283272797404%29.jpg/220px-St_Bartholomew%2C_Marsden_1_%283272797404%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="177" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/St_Bartholomew%2C_Marsden_1_%283272797404%29.jpg/330px-St_Bartholomew%2C_Marsden_1_%283272797404%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/St_Bartholomew%2C_Marsden_1_%283272797404%29.jpg/440px-St_Bartholomew%2C_Marsden_1_%283272797404%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2288" data-file-height="1839" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Bartholomew,_Marsden_1_(3272797404).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St Bartholomew's Church</div></div></div>
<p>Marsden is the last significant settlement on the West Yorkshire side of the <a href="/wiki/Standedge" title="Standedge">Standedge</a> <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennine</a> crossing into <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a>. The village is in the southern edge of the <a href="/wiki/South_Pennines" title="South Pennines">South Pennines</a> with the boundary of the <a href="/wiki/Peak_District" title="Peak District">Peak District</a> National Park to the south. It is surrounded on three sides by the <a href="/wiki/Moorland" title="Moorland">moorland</a> of Marsden and Meltham Moors with <a href="/wiki/Saddleworth_Moor" title="Saddleworth Moor">Saddleworth Moor</a> nearby. Marsden has low level access only from the east along the <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley" title="Colne Valley">Colne Valley</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Marsden_Moor_Estate" title="Marsden Moor Estate">Marsden Moor Estate</a>, which surrounds Marsden to the west and south, includes several reservoirs, and is in the care of the <a href="/wiki/National_Trust_for_Places_of_Historic_Interest_or_Natural_Beauty" title="National Trust for Places of Historic Interest or Natural Beauty">National Trust</a>. The trust is developing techniques to  rehabilitate the moor.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  Butterley Reservoir with its distinctive <a href="/wiki/Spillway" title="Spillway">spillway</a> is near Marsden inside the Peak District National Park.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Chronostratigraphy" title="Chronostratigraphy">chronostratigraphy</a>, the British <a href="/wiki/Stage_(stratigraphy)" title="Stage (stratigraphy)">sub-stage</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Carboniferous" title="Carboniferous">Carboniferous</a> period, the <a href="/wiki/List_of_geochronologic_names" title="List of geochronologic names">Marsdenian</a> derives its name from Marsden.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marsden,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Several generations of tracks and roads have crossed the moors near Marsden. Mellor Bridge by the church, and Close Gate Bridge at the edge of the moor to the east of the village are both <a href="/wiki/Packhorse_bridges" class="mw-redirect" title="Packhorse bridges">packhorse bridges</a>. The <a href="/wiki/A62_road" title="A62 road">A62 road</a> between Huddersfield and Oldham passes through the village and the Standedge cutting some 2.5 miles (4&#160;km) to the west. The road between Oldham and Huddersfield, in particular the stretch between Marsden and <a href="/wiki/Diggle,_Greater_Manchester" title="Diggle, Greater Manchester">Diggle</a> was named the fourth dangerous road in Britain in 2003-2005.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marsden,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/First_West_Yorkshire" title="First West Yorkshire">First West Yorkshire</a> operates bus services between Huddersfield and Marsden. A local service run by JRT, runs around the Marsden area, before continuing to Slaithwaite. A <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester_bus_route_184" title="Greater Manchester bus route 184">Trans-Pennine service</a> between Huddersfield and <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, jointly operated by <a href="/wiki/First_Greater_Manchester" title="First Greater Manchester">First Greater Manchester</a> and First West Yorkshire, passes through the village. Until 1963 it was a <a href="/wiki/Trolleybuses_in_Huddersfield" title="Trolleybuses in Huddersfield">Huddersfield trolleybus</a> terminus.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail_and_canal">Rail and canal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marsden,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rail and canal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Narrow_Canal" title="Huddersfield Narrow Canal">Huddersfield Narrow Canal</a> and the Huddersfield to <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> railway enter the parallel rail and canal <a href="/wiki/Standedge_Tunnels" title="Standedge Tunnels">Standedge Tunnels</a> about half a mile (0.8&#160;km) to the west of the town centre. <a href="/wiki/Marsden_railway_station" title="Marsden railway station">Marsden railway station</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_line" title="Huddersfield line">Huddersfield line</a> is operated by <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a> provides services to Huddersfield, Manchester Piccadilly railway station and <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mountain_rescue">Mountain rescue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marsden,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Mountain rescue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Packhorse_bridge_in_Marsden,_West_Yorkshire.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Packhorse_bridge_in_Marsden%2C_West_Yorkshire.jpg/170px-Packhorse_bridge_in_Marsden%2C_West_Yorkshire.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Packhorse_bridge_in_Marsden%2C_West_Yorkshire.jpg/255px-Packhorse_bridge_in_Marsden%2C_West_Yorkshire.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Packhorse_bridge_in_Marsden%2C_West_Yorkshire.jpg/340px-Packhorse_bridge_in_Marsden%2C_West_Yorkshire.jpg 2x" data-file-width="429" data-file-height="643" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Packhorse_bridge_in_Marsden,_West_Yorkshire.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mellor Bridge, one of Marsden's two packhorse bridges, with St Bartholomew's Church in the background</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley_Mountain_Rescue_Team" title="Holme Valley Mountain Rescue Team">Holme Valley Mountain Rescue Team</a> has its headquarters at Marsden Fire Station from where the volunteer team provides rescue cover for surrounding moorland areas and assists West Yorkshire Police with searches for missing people. The team was founded in 1965 and was based in <a href="/wiki/Meltham" title="Meltham">Meltham</a> before relocating in 2005.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marsden,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marsden football club, Marsden AFC, play their home matches at the Fall Lane ground. In its centenary year the 1st team were promoted from the West Riding County Amateur League Division 1, and played in the West Riding County Amateur Premier Division for the 2008–09 season.
</p><p>Above the village at Hemplow, on Mount Road is a sports ground that hosts Marsden's cricket,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> golf and tennis clubs, as well as Hemplow Bowling Club. The cricket club, formed in 1865, runs two teams in the Drake's Huddersfield Cricket League<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and teams in five age groups in the Huddersfield Junior Cricket League.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2010 Marsden gained Walkers are Welcome status in recognition of its well-maintained footpaths, facilities and information for walkers and ramblers.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marsden,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marsden Silver Prize Band is the local <a href="/wiki/Brass_band_(British_style)#Silver_band" class="mw-redirect" title="Brass band (British style)">silver band</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The village hosts festivals and cultural events throughout the year. Marsden Cuckoo Day, a day-long festival held annually in Spring (April), holds <a href="/wiki/Clog_dancing" title="Clog dancing">clog dancing</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Rubber_duck#United_Kingdom" title="Rubber duck">duck race</a>, music, a procession and a "cuckoo walk". The Marsden Jazz Festival<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> is held every October, and the winter <a href="/wiki/Imbolc" title="Imbolc">Imbolc</a> Festival, in which the 'triumph of the <a href="/wiki/Green_Man" title="Green Man">Green Man</a>' (who represents the coming spring), over Jack Frost (the winter) is celebrated with fire juggling and giant puppets.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Marsden is the home of <a href="/wiki/Mikron_Theatre_Company" title="Mikron Theatre Company">Mikron Theatre Company</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> the world's only professional theatre company to tour by Narrowboat.
</p><p>Marsden's 'Cuckoo Day festival' is named after a local legend of the Marsden Cuckoo:
</p>
<dl><dd>"Many years ago the people of Marsden were aware that when the cuckoo arrived, so did the Spring and sunshine. They tried to keep Spring forever, by building a tower around the Cuckoo. Unfortunately, as the last stones were about to be laid, away flew the cuckoo. If only they'd built the tower one layer higher. As the legend says, it 'were nobbut just wun course too low'."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_people">Notable people</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marsden,_West_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Notable people">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Marsden was the birthplace of Henrietta Thompson, the mother of General <a href="/wiki/James_Wolfe" title="James Wolfe">James Wolfe</a> who took <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> from the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> in 1759.
</p><p>Marsden is also where Enoch Taylor was buried. Enoch Taylor was the blacksmith who built the first automatic croppers. The name Enoch was used for the hammers that the Luddites used to smash them. The Luddites used the slogan "Enoch made them, and Enoch shall break them."<sup id="cite_ref-luddites_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-luddites-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Simon_Armitage" title="Simon Armitage">Simon Armitage</a>, born 1963, British Poet Laureate and playwright.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Samuel_Laycock" title="Samuel Laycock">Samuel Laycock</a>, 1826–1893, dialect poet.</li></ul>
