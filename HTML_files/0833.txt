<p>The <b>1960 Democratic presidential primaries</b> were the selection process by which voters of the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democratic Party</a> chose its nominee for <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1960_United_States_presidential_election" title="1960 United States presidential election">1960 U.S. presidential election</a>. <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">Senator</a> <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> was selected as the nominee through a series of <a href="/wiki/Primary_election" title="Primary election">primary elections</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caucus" title="Caucus">caucuses</a> culminating in the <a href="/wiki/1960_Democratic_National_Convention" title="1960 Democratic National Convention">1960 Democratic National Convention</a> held from July 11 to July 15, 1960, in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Primary_race">Primary race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1960_Democratic_Party_presidential_primaries&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Primary race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Recalling the experience of 1928 <a href="/wiki/Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic">Catholic</a> Democratic presidential nominee <a href="/wiki/Al_Smith" title="Al Smith">Al Smith</a>, many wondered if <a href="/wiki/Anti-Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Anti-Catholic">anti-Catholic</a> prejudice would affect Kennedy's chances of winning the nomination and the election in November. To prove his vote-getting ability, Kennedy challenged <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a> Senator <a href="/wiki/Hubert_Humphrey" title="Hubert Humphrey">Hubert Humphrey</a>, a liberal, in the <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a> primary. Although Kennedy defeated Humphrey in <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">Wisconsin</a>, the fact that his margin of victory came mostly from heavily Catholic areas left many party bosses unconvinced of Kennedy's appeal to non-Catholic voters. Kennedy next faced Humphrey in the heavily <a href="/wiki/Protestant" class="mw-redirect" title="Protestant">Protestant</a> state of <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">West Virginia</a>, where anti-Catholic <a href="/wiki/Bigotry" class="mw-redirect" title="Bigotry">bigotry</a> was said to be widespread. Humphrey's campaign was low on money and could not compete with the well-organized, well-financed Kennedy team. Kennedy's attractive sisters and brothers combed the state looking for votes, leading Humphrey to complain that he "felt like an independent merchant running against a chain store."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  On primary day, Kennedy crushed Humphrey with over 60% of the vote. Humphrey withdrew from the race and Kennedy had gained the victory he needed to prove to the party's bosses that a Catholic could win in a non-Catholic state. In the months leading up to the Democratic Convention Kennedy traveled around the nation persuading delegates from various states to support him. However, as the Convention opened, Kennedy was still a few dozen votes short of victory.
</p><p>Although Kennedy won primaries by comfortable margin, his main opponent, <a href="/wiki/Senate_Majority_Leader" class="mw-redirect" title="Senate Majority Leader">Senate Majority Leader</a> <a href="/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson" title="Lyndon B. Johnson">Lyndon B. Johnson</a>, who did not participate in primaries, had a very strong base in party establishment and gained many delegates. Johnson did not join any primary, but was a write-in.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
