<p><b>Richard Oliver Earnshaw</b> (8 January 1939 &#8211; 28 July 1963) was an <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> first-class cricketer.
</p><p>While undertaking his <a href="/wiki/National_Service_Act_1948" title="National Service Act 1948">National Service</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Electrical_and_Mechanical_Engineers" title="Royal Electrical and Mechanical Engineers">Royal Electrical and Mechanical Engineers</a>, Earnshaw played <a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class cricket</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Combined_Services_cricket_team" title="Combined Services cricket team">Combined Services cricket team</a>, making his debut against the touring <a href="/wiki/South_Africa_national_cricket_team" title="South Africa national cricket team">South Africans</a> at <a href="/wiki/United_Services_Recreation_Ground" title="United Services Recreation Ground">Portsmouth</a> in 1960.<sup id="cite_ref-FCM_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FCM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He made a second first-class appearance in 1961, against <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Northamptonshire County Cricket Club">Northamptonshire</a> at <a href="/wiki/County_Ground,_Northampton" class="mw-redirect" title="County Ground, Northampton">Northampton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FCM_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FCM-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He scored 11 runs across his two matches,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> as well as bowling 67 wicketless <a href="/wiki/Over_(cricket)" title="Over (cricket)">overs</a> with his right-arm <a href="/wiki/Fast_bowling" class="mw-redirect" title="Fast bowling">fast-medium</a> bowling, conceding 211 runs.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He died at <a href="/wiki/Westminster" title="Westminster">Westminster</a> in July 1963, aged just 24.
</p>
