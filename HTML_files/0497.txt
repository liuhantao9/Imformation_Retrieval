<p>The <b>River Holme</b> is a <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Holme_Valley" title="Holme Valley">Holme Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and is a tributary of the <a href="/wiki/River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Colne, West Yorkshire">River Colne</a>.  It starts from Digley reservoir and is then fed firstly by the run-off stream from Brownhill Reservoir, then by Dobbs Dike. Most of the banks of the upper part of the river are urbanised and are part of the Holme Valley <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_West_Yorkshire" title="Civil parishes in West Yorkshire">civil parish</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Course">Course</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Holme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Course">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From Digley reservoir, the river flows north east through <a href="/wiki/Holmbridge" title="Holmbridge">Holmbridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth" title="Holmfirth">Holmfirth</a>. It flows north-north-east to <a href="/wiki/Thongsbridge" title="Thongsbridge">Thongsbridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brockholes" title="Brockholes">Brockholes</a> before turning north and reaching <a href="/wiki/Honley" title="Honley">Honley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berry_Brow" title="Berry Brow">Berry Brow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lockwood,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Lockwood, West Yorkshire">Lockwood</a>. It then proceeds northwards and joins the <a href="/wiki/River_Colne,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Colne, West Yorkshire">River Colne</a> just south of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> town centre at Folly Hall.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Environment_Agency" title="Environment Agency">Environment Agency</a> have a gauging station at Queen's Mill in Huddersfield where the recorded average low river level is 0.25 metres (0.82&#160;ft) and the high river level is 1.2 metres (3.9&#160;ft). The record highest level recorded was 2.5 metres (8.2&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flooding">Flooding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Holme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Flooding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth_floods" title="Holmfirth floods">Holmfirth floods</a></div>
<p>The river was prone to flooding with the earliest recorded in 1738. In 1840 Bilberry Reservoir was built over an existing stream, but the work had not been carried out properly and the stream not correctly redirected. The result was that, in February 1852, the reservoir broke its confines and flooded the Holme Valley as far as Holmfirth. It resulted in 81 fatalities and the destruction of many homes and businesses.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=River_Holme&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The start of the river valley is surrounded by the high hills of <a href="/wiki/Holme_Moss" title="Holme Moss">Holme Moss</a>, Harden Moss and Cartworth Moor. The underlying bedrock was laid down in the <a href="/wiki/Carboniferous" title="Carboniferous">Upper Carboniferous</a> period and consists primarily of <a href="/wiki/Millstone_Grit" title="Millstone Grit">Millstone Grit</a> with some <a href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">sandstone</a> interspersed with thin <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a> seams.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
