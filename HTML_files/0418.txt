<p><b>Lelydorp</b> is the capital city of <a href="/wiki/Wanica_District" title="Wanica District">Wanica District</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. With a population of 18,663 (2012), it is the second largest city in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, after <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is located at around <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Lelydorp&amp;params=5_42_N_55_14_W_region:SR_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">5°42′N</span> <span class="longitude">55°14′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">5.700°N 55.233°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">5.700; -55.233</span></span></span></a></span><span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Lelydorp&amp;params=5_42_N_55_14_W_region:SR_type:city"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">5°42′N</span> <span class="longitude">55°14′W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">5.700°N 55.233°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">5.700; -55.233</span></span></span></a></span></span></span>.
</p><p>It was originally called <i>Kofi Djompo</i> but was renamed by <a href="/wiki/Cornelis_Lely" title="Cornelis Lely">Cornelis Lely</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_colonial_heads_of_Suriname_(Netherlands_Guiana)" class="mw-redirect" title="List of colonial heads of Suriname (Netherlands Guiana)">governor</a> of Suriname in 1905) and since then it is known as <i>Lelydorp</i>. Lely was responsible for many large water construction projects in the Netherlands.
</p><p>The original name <i>Kofi Djompo</i> is said to refer to a <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">maroon</a> rebel leader called Kofi, who was captured by the Dutch and then decapitated. The Dutch took his head on a boat and attached it to a stick as a warning to the other slaves who had escaped and were wandering in the forest. It is said that when the boat reached the middle of the river, the head of Kofi jumped out the boat and disappeared. Kofi means "born on Friday" (<a href="/wiki/Kofi_Annan" title="Kofi Annan">Kofi Annan</a>, for example, was born on a Friday). Djompo means "jump". 
</p><p>Lelydorp is more like a big village instead of a city. <a href="/wiki/Wanica" class="mw-redirect" title="Wanica">Wanica</a> has a population of about 80,000 and an area of about 440&#160;km². With a population of this size, Wanica is one of Suriname's most populated and most urbanised districts. Wanica is divided into resorts: De Nieuwe Grond, Domburg, Houttuin, Koewarasan, Kwatta, Lelydorp, Saramacca Polder. In the 1950s, a test was made in Lelydorp with a medical center that also had to function as mini-hospital. 
</p><p>Lelydorp is also the most important halfway stopping point between Paramaribo and Zanderij, where the <a href="/wiki/Johan_Adolf_Pengel_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Johan Adolf Pengel Airport">Johan Adolf Pengel Airport</a> is located. Its population consists of a mixture of <a href="/wiki/Javanese_people" title="Javanese people">Javanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indian_people" title="Indian people">East Indian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Surinamese_Creole_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Surinamese Creole people">Creoles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europeans" class="mw-redirect" title="Europeans">Europeans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Jews" class="mw-redirect" title="Portuguese Jews">Portuguese Jews</a>. Names of roads and streets in Lelydorp, like Dessa <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.indexmundi.com/zp/id/42360.htm">Sidodadi</a> and Tawajari, are typical original names from <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. The small river known as "Tawajari Creek" divides Lelydorp from <a href="/wiki/Saramacca_District" title="Saramacca District">Saramacca</a>.
</p>
