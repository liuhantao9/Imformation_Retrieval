<p><b>Fort Zeelandia</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Fortress" class="mw-redirect" title="Fortress">fortress</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. In 1640 the French built a wooden fort on the spot, which during the British colonial days was reinforced and became <b>Fort Willoughby</b>. It was taken by the Dutch in 1667 and renamed Fort Zeelandia.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fort_Zeelandia_(Paramaribo)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1651 a small trading post was created by the Dutch. The Dutch presence increased and strengthened. In 1667 the Dutch Admiral <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Crijnssen" title="Abraham Crijnssen">Abraham Crijnssen</a> took Paramaribo and recaptured the Essequibo-Pomeroon Colony.  Byam, the British commander of Fort Willoughby defended the fort in a battle which lasted only three hours as British munitions were exhausted.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Crijnssen renamed the fort to Zeelandia.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the Surinamese independence in 1975, during the military government of <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a> in the 1980s, Fort Zeelandia was the location of the "<a href="/wiki/December_murders" title="December murders">December murders</a>" of 1982 and was used to hold and <a href="/wiki/Torture" title="Torture">torture</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_prisoner" title="Political prisoner">political prisoners</a>.
</p>
