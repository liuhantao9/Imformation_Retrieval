<p>"<b>Some Hearts</b>" is a song written by <a href="/wiki/Diane_Warren" title="Diane Warren">Diane Warren</a>. The track was originally written for <a href="/wiki/Belinda_Carlisle" title="Belinda Carlisle">Belinda Carlisle</a>, who recorded it as a demo for her 1987 <i><a href="/wiki/Heaven_on_Earth_(Belinda_Carlisle_album)" title="Heaven on Earth (Belinda Carlisle album)">Heaven on Earth</a></i> album, but it was not included on the album. It was released as a single by <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Crenshaw" title="Marshall Crenshaw">Marshall Crenshaw</a> from his 1989 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Evening_(album)" title="Good Evening (album)">Good Evening</a>,</i> but it failed to chart. Singers that have covered the song include Kelly Levesque, featured in the 2001 film <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Sweethearts" title="America&#39;s Sweethearts">America's Sweethearts</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Maria_Arredondo" title="Maria Arredondo">Maria Arredondo</a> for her 2004 album <i>Not Going Under</i>, and <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> for her <a href="/wiki/Some_Hearts" title="Some Hearts">debut album of the same name</a>.
</p>

<p>In 2005, it was recorded by <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> as the title track of her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Hearts" title="Some Hearts">Some Hearts</a></i>, and was the album's third single. It was only released to <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adult_contemporary" class="mw-redirect" title="Adult contemporary">adult contemporary</a> radio in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in November 2005, around the same time that "<a href="/wiki/Jesus,_Take_the_Wheel" title="Jesus, Take the Wheel">Jesus, Take the Wheel</a>" was released to country radio. "Some Hearts" peaked at number 12 on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Adult_Contemporary_Tracks" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Adult Contemporary Tracks">Adult Contemporary</a> chart and reached number 22 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Adult_Top_40_Tracks" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Adult Top 40 Tracks">Adult Top 40</a> chart. The song had no accompanying music video and was not released to country radio. It has sold over 207,000 copies in US as of February 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Underwood performed the song at the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Music_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="Billboard Music Awards"><i>Billboard</i> Music Awards</a>, the half-time of the <a href="/wiki/2006_NBA_All-Star_Game" title="2006 NBA All-Star Game">2006 NBA All-Star Game</a> in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>, on an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a></i> and during her promotional tour for the album's release. The song was used in early commercials for <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i>'s website during <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_6)" title="American Idol (season 6)">the sixth season</a> of the show, until <a href="/wiki/Daughtry_(band)" title="Daughtry (band)">Daughtry</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Home_(Daughtry_Song)" class="mw-redirect" title="Home (Daughtry Song)">Home</a>" was used after Hollywood week.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Some_Hearts_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Scott Shetler of <i><a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant Magazine</a></i> wrote in his review of the album that " Underwood is likely to become a fixture on the country charts for the next year with songs like the uptempo title track, a smash hit in the making that is equal parts <a href="/wiki/Jo_Dee_Messina" title="Jo Dee Messina">Jo Dee Messina</a> and <a href="/wiki/SHeDAISY" title="SHeDAISY">SHeDAISY</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_Erlewine" title="Stephen Thomas Erlewine">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</a> wrote that Underwood sounds equally convincing on such sentimental fare as "Jesus, Take the Wheel" as on the soaring pop "Some Hearts".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
