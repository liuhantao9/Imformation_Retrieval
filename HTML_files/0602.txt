	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Education in Suriname</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> has an extensive educational system with free schooling compulsory until age 12. The Government and the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic">Roman Catholic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moravian_Church" title="Moravian Church">Moravian Churches</a> provide education for <a href="/wiki/Kindergarten" title="Kindergarten">kindergarten</a> through <a href="/wiki/Secondary_school" title="Secondary school">secondary school</a>. As a rule, all instructions are in <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>. The four exceptions to this rule are the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iasuriname.org">International Academy of Suriname</a>, administered by a local Christian foundation, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_Liberty_Academy,_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Christian Liberty Academy, Suriname (page does not exist)">Christian Liberty Academy</a>, administered by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Caribbean_Christian_Ministries&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Caribbean Christian Ministries (page does not exist)">Caribbean Christian Ministries</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/AlphaMax_Academy" title="AlphaMax Academy">AlphaMax Academy</a>, a private nonsectarian school administered by the AlphaMax Foundation, and since 2011 Suriname International School, which provides k12 online school for high school students.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Adult_literacy" class="mw-redirect" title="Adult literacy">adult literacy</a> rate is approximately 89.6%. Teacher training institutes, secondary schools, and technical schools provide terminal degrees. Nurses and dental technicians are trained in conjunction with the medical faculty, but standards do not equal those found in more developed countries. The <a href="/wiki/Anton_de_Kom_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Anton de Kom University">Anton de Kom University</a> in Paramaribo has faculties of medicine, law, natural resources, and social and technical sciences. However, transfer of individual course credits to and from the U.S. is difficult, if not unlikely. Enrollment is very difficult for non-Dutch speaking persons as well. Many students still attend high schools and universities in the Netherlands. A growing number study in U.S. universities.
</p>
