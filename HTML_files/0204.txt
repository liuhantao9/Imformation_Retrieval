<p>The <b>United Provinces of the Netherlands</b>, or simply <b>United Provinces</b> (officially the <b>Republic of the Seven United Netherlands</b>), and commonly referred to historiographically as the <b>Dutch Republic</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Confederation" title="Confederation">confederal republic</a> formally established from the formal creation of a confederacy in 1581 by several Dutch provinces—<a href="/wiki/Dutch_Revolt" title="Dutch Revolt">seceded</a> from <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Netherlands" title="Spanish Netherlands">Spanish rule</a>—until the <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Revolution" title="Batavian Revolution">Batavian Revolution</a> of 1795. It was a <a href="/wiki/Predecessor_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Predecessor state">predecessor state</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and the first fully independent <a href="/wiki/Dutch_people" title="Dutch people">Dutch</a> <a href="/wiki/Nation_state" title="Nation state">nation state</a>.
</p><p>The small republic of around 1.5 million inhabitants distinguished itself by world trade through the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company (VOC)</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_West_India_Company" title="Dutch West India Company">Dutch West India Company (WIC)</a>, by its great military successes against ostensibly stronger countries such as <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> and <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, by its huge fleet (with 2000 ships larger than that of England and France combined), by the flourishing of arts (painters such as <a href="/wiki/Rembrandt" title="Rembrandt">Rembrandt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Vermeer" title="Johannes Vermeer">Johannes Vermeer</a> and many others) and sciences (including <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Grotius" title="Hugo Grotius">Hugo Grotius</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antonie_van_Leeuwenhoek" title="Antonie van Leeuwenhoek">Antonie van Leeuwenhoek</a>), and by its relatively tolerant atmosphere towards different religions and ideas (<a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_thought" title="Freedom of thought">freedom of thought</a>). Because Dutch trade, science, military, and art were among the most acclaimed in the world during much of the 17th century, this period became known in <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Netherlands" title="History of the Netherlands">Dutch history</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Golden_Age" title="Dutch Golden Age">Dutch Golden Age</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Until the 16th century, the <a href="/wiki/Low_Countries" title="Low Countries">Low Countries</a>—corresponding roughly to the present-day <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg" title="Luxembourg">Luxembourg</a>—consisted of a number of <a href="/wiki/Duchy" title="Duchy">duchies</a>, counties, and <a href="/wiki/Prince-bishop" title="Prince-bishop">prince-bishoprics</a>, almost all of which were under the supremacy of the <a href="/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire" title="Holy Roman Empire">Holy Roman Empire</a>, with the exception of the county of Flanders, most of which was under the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">Kingdom of France</a>.
</p><p>Most of the Low Countries had come under the rule of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Valois-Burgundy" title="House of Valois-Burgundy">House of Burgundy</a> and subsequently the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Habsburg" title="House of Habsburg">House of Habsburg</a>. In 1549 <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Holy Roman Emperor Charles V</a> issued the <a href="/wiki/Pragmatic_Sanction_of_1549" title="Pragmatic Sanction of 1549">Pragmatic Sanction</a>, which further unified the Seven Provinces under his rule. Charles was succeeded by his son, King <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">Philip II of Spain</a>. In 1568 the Netherlands, led by <a href="/wiki/William_I_of_Orange" class="mw-redirect" title="William I of Orange">William I of Orange</a>, revolted against Philip II because of high taxes, persecution of Protestants by the government, and Philip's efforts to modernize and centralize the devolved-medieval government structures of the provinces.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This was the start of the <a href="/wiki/Eighty_Years%27_War" title="Eighty Years&#39; War">Eighty Years' War</a>.
</p><p>In 1579, a number of the northern provinces of the Low Countries signed the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Utrecht" title="Union of Utrecht">Union of Utrecht</a>, in which they promised to support each other in their defence against the Spanish army. This was followed in 1581 by the <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Abjuration" title="Act of Abjuration">Act of Abjuration</a>, the declaration of independence of the provinces from Philip II.
</p><p>In 1582, the United Provinces invited <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois,_Duke_of_Anjou" class="mw-redirect" title="François, Duke of Anjou">Francis, Duke of Anjou</a> to lead them; but after a <a href="/wiki/French_Fury" title="French Fury">failed attempt to take Antwerp</a> in 1583, the duke left the Netherlands again. After the assassination of <a href="/wiki/William_the_Silent" title="William the Silent">William of Orange</a> on 10 July 1584, both <a href="/wiki/Henry_III_of_France" title="Henry III of France">Henry III of France</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_I_of_England" title="Elizabeth I of England">Elizabeth I of England</a> declined offers of sovereignty. However, the latter agreed to turn the United Provinces into a <a href="/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">protectorate</a> of England (<a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Nonsuch" title="Treaty of Nonsuch">Treaty of Nonsuch</a>, 1585), and sent the <a href="/wiki/Robert_Dudley,_Earl_of_Leicester" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Dudley, Earl of Leicester">Earl of Leicester</a> as governor-general. This was unsuccessful and in 1588 the provinces became a <a href="/wiki/Confederation" title="Confederation">confederacy</a>. The Union of Utrecht is regarded as the foundation of the Republic of the Seven United Provinces, which was not recognized by the Spanish Empire until the <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Westphalia" title="Peace of Westphalia">Peace of Westphalia</a> in 1648.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-French_War_(1778)" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo-French War (1778)">Anglo-French War (1778)</a>, the internal territory was divided into two groups: the Patriots, who were pro-French and pro-American, and the Orangists, who were pro-British.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEErtl2008217_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEErtl2008217-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Republic of the United Provinces faced a series of republican revolutions in 1783–1787. During this period, <a href="/wiki/Republicanism_in_the_Netherlands" title="Republicanism in the Netherlands">republican forces</a> occupied several major Dutch cities. Initially on the defence, the Orangist forces received aid from Prussian troops and <a href="/wiki/Prussian_invasion_of_Holland" title="Prussian invasion of Holland">retook the Netherlands</a> in 1787. The republican forces fled to France, but then <a href="/wiki/Flanders_Campaign" class="mw-redirect" title="Flanders Campaign">successfully re-invaded</a> alongside the army of the French Republic (1793–95), ousting stadtholder <a href="/wiki/William_V,_Prince_of_Orange" title="William V, Prince of Orange">William V</a>, abolishing the Dutch Republic, and replacing it with the <a href="/wiki/Batavian_Republic" title="Batavian Republic">Batavian Republic</a> (1795–1806). After the French Republic became the French Empire under <a href="/wiki/Napoleon" title="Napoleon">Napoleon</a>, the Batavian Republic was replaced by the Napoleonic <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Holland" title="Kingdom of Holland">Kingdom of Holland</a> (1806–1810).
</p><p>The Netherlands regained independence from France in 1813. In the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Dutch_Treaty_of_1814" title="Anglo-Dutch Treaty of 1814">Anglo-Dutch Treaty of 1814</a> the names "United Provinces of the Netherlands" and "United Netherlands" were used. In 1815, it was rejoined with the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Netherlands" title="Austrian Netherlands">Austrian Netherlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prince-Bishopric_of_Li%C3%A8ge" title="Prince-Bishopric of Liège">Liège</a> (the "Southern provinces") to become the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>, informally known as the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="United Kingdom of the Netherlands">United Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>, to create a strong <a href="/wiki/Buffer_state" title="Buffer state">buffer state</a> north of France. On 16 March 1815, the son of stadtholder William V crowned himself King <a href="/wiki/William_I_of_the_Netherlands" title="William I of the Netherlands">William I of the Netherlands</a>. Between 1815 and 1890, the King of the Netherlands was also in a <a href="/wiki/Personal_union" title="Personal union">personal union</a> the Grand Duke of the sovereign Grand Duchy of Luxembourg. After Belgium <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Revolution" title="Belgian Revolution">gained its independence</a> in 1830, the state became unequivocally known as the "Kingdom of the Netherlands", as it remains today.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Economic_history_of_the_Netherlands_(1500%E2%80%931815)" title="Economic history of the Netherlands (1500–1815)">Economic history of the Netherlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Financial_history_of_the_Dutch_Republic" title="Financial history of the Dutch Republic">financial history of the Dutch Republic</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Emanuel_de_Witte_-_De_binnenplaats_van_de_beurs_te_Amsterdam.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Emanuel_de_Witte_-_De_binnenplaats_van_de_beurs_te_Amsterdam.jpg/170px-Emanuel_de_Witte_-_De_binnenplaats_van_de_beurs_te_Amsterdam.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="179" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Emanuel_de_Witte_-_De_binnenplaats_van_de_beurs_te_Amsterdam.jpg/255px-Emanuel_de_Witte_-_De_binnenplaats_van_de_beurs_te_Amsterdam.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Emanuel_de_Witte_-_De_binnenplaats_van_de_beurs_te_Amsterdam.jpg/340px-Emanuel_de_Witte_-_De_binnenplaats_van_de_beurs_te_Amsterdam.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="1051" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Emanuel_de_Witte_-_De_binnenplaats_van_de_beurs_te_Amsterdam.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Amsterdam_Stock_Exchange" class="mw-redirect" title="Amsterdam Stock Exchange">Amsterdam Stock Exchange</a> courtyard, 1653</div></div></div>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Golden_Age" title="Dutch Golden Age">Dutch Golden Age</a> in the late-16th and 17th centuries, the Dutch Republic dominated <a href="/wiki/History_of_international_trade" class="mw-redirect" title="History of international trade">world trade</a>, conquering a vast <a href="/wiki/Dutch_colonial_empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Dutch colonial empire">colonial empire</a> and operating the largest fleet of <a href="/wiki/Cargo_ship" title="Cargo ship">merchantmen</a> of any nation. The <a href="/wiki/County_of_Holland" title="County of Holland">County of Holland</a> was the wealthiest and most urbanized region in the world. In 1650 the <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban population</a> of the Dutch Republic as a percentage of total population was 31.7&#160;percent, while that of the Spanish Netherlands was 20.8&#160;percent, of Portugal 16.6&#160;percent, and of Italy 14&#160;percent.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1675 the urban population density of Holland alone was 61&#160;percent, that of the rest of the Dutch Republic 27&#160;percent.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The free trade spirit of the time was augmented by the development of a modern, effective <a href="/wiki/Stock_market" title="Stock market">stock market</a> in the Low Countries.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The Netherlands has the oldest stock exchange in the world, founded in 1602 by the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a> has the oldest bourse in the Netherlands. The Dutch East-India Company exchange went public in six different cities. Later, a court ruled that the company had to reside legally in a single city, so <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> is recognized as the oldest such institution based on modern trading principles. While the banking system evolved in the Low Countries, it was quickly incorporated by the well-connected English, stimulating English economic output.
</p><p>During the period of <a href="/wiki/Proto-industrialization" title="Proto-industrialization">Proto-industrialization</a>, the empire received 50% of textiles and 80% of silks import from the India's <a href="/wiki/Mughal_Empire" title="Mughal Empire">Mughal Empire</a>, chiefly from its most developed region known as <a href="/wiki/Bengal_Subah" title="Bengal Subah">Bengal Subah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tong_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tong-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-esposito_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-esposito-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Prakash_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Prakash-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics">Politics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Politics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Politics_and_government_of_the_Dutch_Republic" title="Politics and government of the Dutch Republic">Politics and government of the Dutch Republic</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Republiek_der_Zeven_Verenigde_Nederlanden.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Republiek_der_Zeven_Verenigde_Nederlanden.svg/220px-Republiek_der_Zeven_Verenigde_Nederlanden.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="249" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Republiek_der_Zeven_Verenigde_Nederlanden.svg/330px-Republiek_der_Zeven_Verenigde_Nederlanden.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Republiek_der_Zeven_Verenigde_Nederlanden.svg/440px-Republiek_der_Zeven_Verenigde_Nederlanden.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="2870" data-file-height="3250" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Republiek_der_Zeven_Verenigde_Nederlanden.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The united provinces, with <a href="/wiki/Drenthe" title="Drenthe">Drenthe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Generality_Lands" title="Generality Lands">Generality Lands</a></div></div></div>
<p>The republic was a <a href="/wiki/Confederation" title="Confederation">confederation</a> of seven provinces, which had their own governments and were very independent, and a number of so-called <a href="/wiki/Generality_Lands" title="Generality Lands">Generality Lands</a>. The latter were governed directly by the <a href="/wiki/States_General_of_the_Netherlands#Dutch_Republic" title="States General of the Netherlands">States General</a>, the federal government. The States General were seated in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a> and consisted of representatives of each of the seven provinces. The provinces of the republic were, in official feudal order:
</p>
<ol><li><a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Guelders" class="mw-redirect" title="Duchy of Guelders">Duchy of Guelders</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/County_of_Holland" title="County of Holland">County of Holland</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/County_of_Zeeland" title="County of Zeeland">County of Zeeland</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lordship_of_Utrecht" title="Lordship of Utrecht">Lordship of Utrecht</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lordship_of_Overijssel" title="Lordship of Overijssel">Lordship of Overijssel</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lordship_of_Frisia" title="Lordship of Frisia">Lordship of Frisia</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lordship_of_Groningen" title="Lordship of Groningen">Lordship of Groningen</a></li></ol>
<p>There was an eighth province, the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Drenthe" title="County of Drenthe">County of Drenthe</a>, but this area was so poor it was exempt from paying federal taxes and as a consequence was denied representation in the States General. Each province was governed by the Provincial States, the main executive official (though not the official head of state) was a <i><a href="/wiki/Raadspensionaris" class="mw-redirect" title="Raadspensionaris">raadspensionaris</a></i>. In times of war, the <a href="/wiki/Stadtholder" title="Stadtholder">stadtholder</a>, who commanded the army, would have more power than the <i>raadspensionaris</i>.
</p><p>In theory, the stadtholders were freely appointed by and subordinate to the states of each province. However, in practice the <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Orange" title="Prince of Orange">princes of Orange</a> of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Orange-Nassau" title="House of Orange-Nassau">House of Orange-Nassau</a>, beginning with <a href="/wiki/William_the_Silent" title="William the Silent">William the Silent</a>, were always chosen as stadtholders of most of the provinces. Zeeland and usually Utrecht had the same stadtholder as Holland. There was a constant power struggle between the Orangists, who supported the stadtholders and specifically the princes of Orange, and the Republicans, who supported the States General and hoped to replace the semi-hereditary nature of the stadtholdership with a true republican structure.
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/Peace_of_Westphalia" title="Peace of Westphalia">Peace of Westphalia</a>, several border territories were assigned to the United Provinces. They were federally governed <a href="/wiki/Generality_Lands" title="Generality Lands">Generality Lands</a>. They were <a href="/wiki/Staats-Brabant" class="mw-redirect" title="Staats-Brabant">Staats-Brabant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Staats-Vlaanderen" class="mw-redirect" title="Staats-Vlaanderen">Staats-Vlaanderen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Staats-Limburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Staats-Limburg">Staats-Limburg</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Staats-Oppergelre" class="mw-redirect" title="Staats-Oppergelre">Staats-Oppergelre</a>. The States General of the United Provinces were in control of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_West_India_Company" title="Dutch West India Company">Dutch West India Company</a>, but some shipping expeditions were initiated by some of the provinces, mostly Holland and Zeeland.
</p><p>The framers of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Constitution">U.S. Constitution</a> were influenced by the Constitution of the Republic of the United Provinces, as <a href="/wiki/Federalist_No._20" title="Federalist No. 20">Federalist No. 20</a>, by <a href="/wiki/James_Madison" title="James Madison">James Madison</a>, shows.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Such influence appears, however, to have been of a negative nature, as Madison describes the Dutch confederacy as exhibiting "Imbecility in the government; discord among the provinces; foreign influence and indignities; a precarious existence in peace, and peculiar calamities from war." Apart from this, the <a href="/wiki/American_Declaration_of_Independence" class="mw-redirect" title="American Declaration of Independence">American Declaration of Independence</a> is similar to the <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Abjuration" title="Act of Abjuration">Act of Abjuration</a>, essentially the declaration of independence of the United Provinces,<sup id="cite_ref-UWM06291998_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UWM06291998-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> but concrete evidence that the latter directly influenced the former is absent.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religion">Religion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Religion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Arminianism" title="Arminianism">Arminianism</a>, <a href="/wiki/Remonstrants" title="Remonstrants">Remonstrants</a>, and <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Netherlands#Refugees" title="History of the Netherlands">History of the Netherlands §&#160;Refugees</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Emanuel_de_Witte_-_Interior_of_the_Oude_Kerk_at_Delft_during_a_Sermon_-_WGA25811.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Emanuel_de_Witte_-_Interior_of_the_Oude_Kerk_at_Delft_during_a_Sermon_-_WGA25811.jpg/170px-Emanuel_de_Witte_-_Interior_of_the_Oude_Kerk_at_Delft_during_a_Sermon_-_WGA25811.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="236" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Emanuel_de_Witte_-_Interior_of_the_Oude_Kerk_at_Delft_during_a_Sermon_-_WGA25811.jpg/255px-Emanuel_de_Witte_-_Interior_of_the_Oude_Kerk_at_Delft_during_a_Sermon_-_WGA25811.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Emanuel_de_Witte_-_Interior_of_the_Oude_Kerk_at_Delft_during_a_Sermon_-_WGA25811.jpg/340px-Emanuel_de_Witte_-_Interior_of_the_Oude_Kerk_at_Delft_during_a_Sermon_-_WGA25811.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="1387" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Emanuel_de_Witte_-_Interior_of_the_Oude_Kerk_at_Delft_during_a_Sermon_-_WGA25811.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sermon at the <a href="/wiki/Oude_Kerk_(Delft)" title="Oude Kerk (Delft)">Oude Kerk at Delft</a>, 1651</div></div></div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_Utrecht" title="Union of Utrecht">Union of Utrecht</a> of 20 January 1579, Holland and Zeeland were granted the right to accept only one religion (in practice, <a href="/wiki/Calvinism" title="Calvinism">Calvinism</a>). Every other province had the freedom to regulate the religious question as it wished, although the Union stated every person should be free in the choice of personal religion and that no person should be prosecuted based on religious choice.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> William of Orange had been a strong supporter of public and personal freedom of religion and hoped to unite Protestants and Catholics in the new union, and, for him, the Union was a defeat. In practice, Catholic services in all provinces were quickly forbidden, and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Reformed_Church" title="Dutch Reformed Church">Dutch Reformed Church</a> became the "public" or "privileged" church in the Republic.<sup id="cite_ref-Israels_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Israels-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the Republic, any person who wished to hold public office had to conform to the Reformed Church and take an oath to this effect. The extent to which different religions or denominations were persecuted depended much on the time period and regional or city leaders. In the beginning, this was especially focused on Roman Catholics, being the religion of the enemy. In 17th-century <a href="/wiki/Leiden" title="Leiden">Leiden</a>, for instance, people opening their homes to services could be fined 200 guilders (a year's wage for a skilled <a href="/wiki/Tradesman" title="Tradesman">tradesman</a>) and banned from the city.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Throughout this, however, personal freedom of religion existed and was one factor—along with economic reasons—in causing large immigration of religious refugees from other parts of Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-Israels_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Israels-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the first years of the Republic, controversy arose within the Reformed Church, mainly around the subject of <a href="/wiki/Predestination" title="Predestination">predestination</a>. This has become known as the struggle between <a href="/wiki/Arminianism" title="Arminianism">Arminianism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gomarism" class="mw-redirect" title="Gomarism">Gomarism</a>, or between <a href="/wiki/Remonstrants" title="Remonstrants">Remonstrants</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contra-Remonstrants" class="mw-redirect" title="Contra-Remonstrants">Contra-Remonstrants</a>. In 1618, the <a href="/wiki/Synod_of_Dort" title="Synod of Dort">Synod of Dort</a> tackled this issue, which led to the banning of the Remonstrant faith.
</p><p>Beginning in the 18th century, the situation changed from more or less active persecution of religious services to a state of restricted toleration of other religions, as long as their services took place secretly in <a href="/wiki/Schuilkerk" class="mw-redirect" title="Schuilkerk">private churches</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Decline">Decline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Republic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Decline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:De_handelsloge_van_de_VOC_in_Hougly_in_Bengalen_Rijksmuseum_SK-A-4282.jpeg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/De_handelsloge_van_de_VOC_in_Hougly_in_Bengalen_Rijksmuseum_SK-A-4282.jpeg/260px-De_handelsloge_van_de_VOC_in_Hougly_in_Bengalen_Rijksmuseum_SK-A-4282.jpeg" decoding="async" width="260" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/De_handelsloge_van_de_VOC_in_Hougly_in_Bengalen_Rijksmuseum_SK-A-4282.jpeg/390px-De_handelsloge_van_de_VOC_in_Hougly_in_Bengalen_Rijksmuseum_SK-A-4282.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/De_handelsloge_van_de_VOC_in_Hougly_in_Bengalen_Rijksmuseum_SK-A-4282.jpeg/520px-De_handelsloge_van_de_VOC_in_Hougly_in_Bengalen_Rijksmuseum_SK-A-4282.jpeg 2x" data-file-width="5972" data-file-height="3808" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:De_handelsloge_van_de_VOC_in_Hougly_in_Bengalen_Rijksmuseum_SK-A-4282.jpeg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company" title="Dutch East India Company">Dutch East India Company</a> factory in <a href="/wiki/Hugli-Chuchura" title="Hugli-Chuchura">Hugli-Chuchura</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mughal_Bengal" class="mw-redirect" title="Mughal Bengal">Mughal Bengal</a>. <i>Hendrik van Schuylenburgh, 1665</i></div></div></div>
<p>Long-term rivalry between the two main factions in Dutch society, the <i>Staatsgezinden</i> (Republicans) and the <i>Prinsgezinden</i> (Royalists or <a href="/wiki/Orangism_(Netherlands)" class="mw-redirect" title="Orangism (Netherlands)">Orangists</a>), sapped the strength and unity of the country. <a href="/wiki/Johan_de_Witt" title="Johan de Witt">Johan de Witt</a> and the Republicans did reign supreme for a time at the middle of the 17th century (the <a href="/wiki/First_Stadtholderless_Period" class="mw-redirect" title="First Stadtholderless Period">First Stadtholderless Period</a>) until his overthrow and murder in 1672. Subsequently, <a href="/wiki/William_III_of_England" title="William III of England">William III of Orange</a> became stadtholder. After a 22-year stadtholderless era, the Orangists regained power, and his first problem was to survive the <a href="/wiki/Franco-Dutch_War" title="Franco-Dutch War">Franco-Dutch War</a> (with the derivative <a href="/wiki/Third_Anglo-Dutch_war" class="mw-redirect" title="Third Anglo-Dutch war">Third Anglo-Dutch war</a>), when France, England, <a href="/wiki/Prince-Bishopric_of_M%C3%BCnster" title="Prince-Bishopric of Münster">Münster</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Archbishopric_of_Cologne" class="mw-redirect" title="Archbishopric of Cologne">Cologne</a> united against this country.
</p><p>Wars to contain the expansionist policies of France in various coalitions after the <a href="/wiki/Glorious_Revolution" title="Glorious Revolution">Glorious Revolution</a>, mostly including England and Scotland—after 1707, <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Great Britain</a>—burdened the republic with huge debts, although little of the fighting after 1673 took place on its own territory. The necessity to maintain a vast army against France meant that less money could be spent on the navy, weakening the Republic's economy. After William III's death in 1702 the <a href="/wiki/Second_Stadtholderless_Period" class="mw-redirect" title="Second Stadtholderless Period">Second Stadtholderless Period</a> was inaugurated. Despite having contributed much in the <a href="/wiki/War_of_Spanish_Succession" class="mw-redirect" title="War of Spanish Succession">War of Spanish Succession</a>, the Dutch Republic gained little from the peace talks in <a href="/wiki/Utrecht" title="Utrecht">Utrecht</a> (1713). The end of the <a href="/wiki/War_of_Austrian_Succession" class="mw-redirect" title="War of Austrian Succession">War of Austrian Succession</a> in 1748, and Austria becoming allies with France against Prussia, marked the end of the republic as a major military power.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fierce competition for trade and colonies, especially from France and England, furthered the economic downturn of the country. The three Anglo-Dutch Wars and the rise of <a href="/wiki/Mercantilism" title="Mercantilism">mercantilism</a> had a negative effect on Dutch shipping and commerce.
</p>
