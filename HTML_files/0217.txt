	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Suriname River</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:G.P.H._Zimmerman_-_Kaart_van_Suriname_(1877).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/G.P.H._Zimmerman_-_Kaart_van_Suriname_%281877%29.jpg/220px-G.P.H._Zimmerman_-_Kaart_van_Suriname_%281877%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="346" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/G.P.H._Zimmerman_-_Kaart_van_Suriname_%281877%29.jpg/330px-G.P.H._Zimmerman_-_Kaart_van_Suriname_%281877%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/G.P.H._Zimmerman_-_Kaart_van_Suriname_%281877%29.jpg/440px-G.P.H._Zimmerman_-_Kaart_van_Suriname_%281877%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3501" data-file-height="5500" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:G.P.H._Zimmerman_-_Kaart_van_Suriname_(1877).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map of the Suriname River in 1877 by G.P.H. Zimmerman.</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Suriname River</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i>Surinamerivier</i>) is 480&#160;km long and flows through the country <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. Its sources are located in the <a href="/wiki/Guiana_Highlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Guiana Highlands">Guiana Highlands</a> on the border between the <a href="/wiki/Wilhelminagebergte" class="mw-redirect" title="Wilhelminagebergte">Wilhelmina Mountains</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Eilerts_de_Haan_Mountains" title="Eilerts de Haan Mountains">Eilerts de Haan Mountains</a> (where it is known as the <a href="/wiki/Gran_Rio" title="Gran Rio">Gran Rio</a>). The river flows below the reservoir along <a href="/wiki/Brokopondo" title="Brokopondo">Brokopondo</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Berg_en_Dal_(Suriname)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Berg en Dal (Suriname) (page does not exist)">Berg en Dal</a>, the migrant communities <a href="/wiki/Klaaskreek" title="Klaaskreek">Klaaskreek</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nieuw-Lomb%C3%A9&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nieuw-Lombé (page does not exist)">Nieuw-Lombé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jodensavanne" title="Jodensavanne">Jodensavanne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Suriname" title="Carolina, Suriname">Carolina</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ornamibo&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ornamibo (page does not exist)">Ornamibo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Domburg,_Suriname" title="Domburg, Suriname">Domburg</a>, before reaching the capital <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a> on the left bank and <a href="/wiki/Meerzorg" title="Meerzorg">Meerzorg</a> on the right bank. At <a href="/wiki/Nieuw_Amsterdam,_Suriname" title="Nieuw Amsterdam, Suriname">Nieuw-Amsterdam</a> it is joined by the <a href="/wiki/Commewijne_River" title="Commewijne River">Commewijne</a> and immediately thereafter at the sandspit <a href="/w/index.php?title=Braamspunt&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Braamspunt (page does not exist)">Braamspunt</a> it flows into the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>.
</p><p>The river has several sets of rapids as well as a few dams, the largest of which is the Afobaka Dam.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The river's flow is interrupted by the <a href="/wiki/Brokopondo_Reservoir" title="Brokopondo Reservoir">Brokopondo Reservoir</a>, which therefore divides the river into two sections. The upstream section runs almost entirely through the <a href="/wiki/Sipaliwini_District" title="Sipaliwini District">Sipaliwini district</a>, and the downstream section runs through the <a href="/wiki/Brokopondo_District" title="Brokopondo District">Brokopondo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Para_District" title="Para District">Para</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commewijne_District" title="Commewijne District">Commewijne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wanica_District" title="Wanica District">Wanica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo_District" title="Paramaribo District">Paramaribo</a> districts.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Navigation">Navigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Navigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For <a href="/wiki/Import" title="Import">import</a> and <a href="/wiki/Export" title="Export">export</a>, the Suriname is by far the most important river in the country. Not only do all vessels carrying <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aluminum_oxide" class="mw-redirect" title="Aluminum oxide">aluminum oxide</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aluminum" class="mw-redirect" title="Aluminum">aluminum</a> depart from Suriname via the river, nearly all vessels carrying materials vital for Suriname enter the country at Braamspunt in order to offload their cargoes in the harbours of Paramaribo (immediately south of the city centre), or the harbours of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Smalkalden&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Smalkalden (page does not exist)">Smalkalden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paranam" title="Paranam">Paranam</a> – approximately 30&#160;km from Paramaribo – which are vital for the aluminum industry. During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">the Second World War</a>, the 6,000 tonne <a href="/wiki/Hapag_Lloyd" class="mw-redirect" title="Hapag Lloyd">North German Lloyd</a> cargo ship <a href="/w/index.php?title=Goslar_(ship)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Goslar (ship) (page does not exist)">Goslar</a> was scuttled in the Suriname in order to prevent it from falling into <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> hands. The wreck has never been removed and remains visible in the middle of the river. <span style="font-size: small;"><span id="coordinates"><a href="/wiki/Geographic_coordinate_system" title="Geographic coordinate system">Coordinates</a>: <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Suriname_River&amp;params=5_49_05.32_N_55_09_32.84_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">5°49′05.32″N</span> <span class="longitude">55°09′32.84″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">5.8181444°N 55.1591222°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">5.8181444; -55.1591222</span></span></span></a></span></span></span>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Bridges">Bridges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Bridges">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2000, the <a href="/wiki/Jules_Wijdenbosch_Bridge" title="Jules Wijdenbosch Bridge">Jules Wijdenbosch Bridge</a> at Paramaribo was officially opened by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Suriname" title="President of Suriname">President</a>, who had commissioned construction of the bridge and after whom it is named. The bridge permits access to the eastern part of the country.
At <a href="/wiki/Carolina,_Suriname" title="Carolina, Suriname">Carolina</a>, approximately 50&#160;km South of Paramaribo, a wooden bridge crosses the river.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recreation">Recreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Suriname_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Recreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Near Domburg, an artificial white sand <a href="/wiki/Beach" title="Beach">beach</a> has been created along the Suriname River. The adjoining strip of water has been protected with nets to allow swimming in the <a href="/wiki/Piranha" title="Piranha">piranha</a>-infested waters. As a result of this and associated recreation facilities, White Beach is a popular weekend destination.
</p>
