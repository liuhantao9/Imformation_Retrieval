<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission_and_objectives">Mission and objectives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWF_Guianas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mission and objectives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature" title="World Wide Fund for Nature">WWF</a> main mission is "to stop the degradation of the planet’s natural environment and to build a future in which humans live in harmony with nature."
The regional office of WWF Guianas is also involved in a plurality of site specific objectives, often in partnership with civil society organizations and government institutions such as <a href="/wiki/Conservation_International" title="Conservation International">Conservation International</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iwokrama_International_Centre_for_Rain_Forest_Conservation_and_Development" title="Iwokrama International Centre for Rain Forest Conservation and Development">Iwokrama International Centre for Rain Forest Conservation and Development</a>. These range from the protection and management of ecological landscapes, to educational and awareness campaigns on <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">climate change</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">Biodiversity</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conservation_(ethic)" title="Conservation (ethic)">conservation</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Guianas_Conservation_Significance">The Guianas Conservation Significance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWF_Guianas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Guianas Conservation Significance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 3 <a href="/wiki/Guianas" class="mw-redirect" title="Guianas">Guianas</a> (<a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>) have between 80-98% pristine forest cover and are part of the complex ecosystem of The <a href="/wiki/Guiana_Shield" title="Guiana Shield">Guiana Shield</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_freshwater_ecoregions_(WWF)" title="List of freshwater ecoregions (WWF)">freshwater ecoregion</a>, which provides 15% of the world’s freshwater reserves. 
The forests, wetlands, savannah, waters and <a href="/wiki/Biodiversity" title="Biodiversity">biodiversity</a> make the Guiana Shield “essential to enriching and replenishing the world’s biodiversity and, consequently, essential to the planet’s survival” as <a href="/wiki/David_A._Granger" title="David A. Granger">Brigadier David Granger</a> - President of the <a href="/wiki/Cooperative_Republic_of_Guyana" class="mw-redirect" title="Cooperative Republic of Guyana">Cooperative Republic of Guyana</a> pointed out<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> during its speech at the Opening Ceremony of the IVth International Congress on Biodiversity of the Guiana Shield.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
It is also the home of incredible wildlife creatures such as <a href="/wiki/Jaguar" title="Jaguar">jaguars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anteater" title="Anteater">anteaters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anaconda" title="Anaconda">anacondas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caiman" title="Caiman">caimans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bat" title="Bat">bats</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sea_turtle" title="Sea turtle">sea turtles</a>. A phenomenal variety of fishes populate its waters, and a prodigious amount of different bird species inhabits its trees and skies.
The coasts of the Guianas are home to one of the largest populations of the endangered <a href="/wiki/Leatherback_Turtle" class="mw-redirect" title="Leatherback Turtle">Leatherback turtle</a> in the world and hosts millions of migratory birds from North America. Typical Guiana birds include the <a href="/wiki/Ibis" title="Ibis">Red Ibis</a> and the Cock-of-the-Rock.
Multiple <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_communities" class="mw-redirect" title="Indigenous communities">Indigenous communities</a> live in key parts of the Shield such as the <a href="/wiki/Iwokrama_Forest" title="Iwokrama Forest">Iwokrama forest</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Rupununi" title="Rupununi">Rupununi</a> region.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Offices_and_staff">Offices and staff</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WWF_Guianas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Offices and staff">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Regional Office of <b>WWF Guianas</b> is located in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. Country offices are also located in <a href="/wiki/Georgetown,_Guyana" title="Georgetown, Guyana">Georgetown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cayenne" title="Cayenne">Cayenne</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>. 
Laurens Gomes is the Regional Representative of WWF Guianas, Aiesha Williams runs the WWF Guyana Country Office and Laurent Kelle the WWF French Guiana Country Office.
</p>
