<p>The <b>John Fitzgerald Kennedy National Historic Site</b> is the birthplace and childhood home of <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>, the 35th <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>. The house is at 83 Beals Street in the <a href="/wiki/Coolidge_Corner" title="Coolidge Corner">Coolidge Corner</a> neighborhood of <a href="/wiki/Brookline,_Massachusetts" title="Brookline, Massachusetts">Brookline, Massachusetts</a>. The property is now owned by the <a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">National Park Service</a>; tours of the house are offered, and a film is presented.
</p><p>The Kennedy home was designated a <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Landmark" title="National Historic Landmark">National Historic Landmark</a> in 1964, and was established as a National Historic Site on May 26, 1967.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy_National_Historic_Site&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kennedyhouse.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Kennedyhouse.jpg/220px-Kennedyhouse.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="321" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/14/Kennedyhouse.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="300" data-file-height="438" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kennedyhouse.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The house in 1974.</div></div></div>
<p>The house was purchased by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Patrick_Kennedy,_Sr." class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph Patrick Kennedy, Sr.">Joseph Patrick Kennedy, Sr.</a> on 20 August 1914 in preparation for his marriage to <a href="/wiki/Rose_Kennedy" title="Rose Kennedy">Rose Elizabeth Fitzgerald</a> on 7 October 1914.
</p><p>John and his sisters <a href="/wiki/Rosemary_Kennedy" title="Rosemary Kennedy">Rosemary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington" title="Kathleen Cavendish, Marchioness of Hartington">Kathleen</a> were born in the upstairs master bedroom. The family lived there until 1920, when the growth of the family motivated the Kennedys to move to a larger home just a few blocks away on the corner of Abbottsford and Naples Roads. The Kennedys sold the Beals Street house to Joe Kennedy's advisor Edward Moore and his family. The Kennedys lived in the Abbottsford Road home until 1927, when Joe Kennedy's business interests prompted the family's move to <a href="/wiki/Riverdale,_Bronx" title="Riverdale, Bronx">Riverdale, Bronx</a>, New York.
</p><p>The Kennedy family was the third owner of the house on Beals Street. It was built in 1909, a period of rapid growth in Brookline. The Kennedys moved into the home, then the last on the street, after returning from their honeymoon in 1914. A few months after they were married, Joe Kennedy purchased a new <a href="/wiki/Model_T_Ford" class="mw-redirect" title="Model T Ford">Model T Ford</a> that he used to commute to downtown, where he worked as the president of the Columbia Trust Bank. The "T" line to Coolidge Corner existed when the Kennedys were living in Brookline. Rose Kennedy would often walk from the Beals Street house down to the shopping district in Coolidge Corner, where there was a <a href="/wiki/S.S._Pierce" title="S.S. Pierce">S.S. Pierce</a> store—which replaced the Coolidge &amp; Brothers General Store—among the other shops. Brookline was a rapidly growing suburb of Boston, and it appealed to the growing Kennedy family because the suburbs would have more space for the children to play than if they were raised in the crowded inner city of Boston.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1966, the Kennedy family repurchased the home. From 1966 to 1969, Rose Kennedy restored it to her recollection of its 1917 appearance. She wanted to restore the home to the hour of John's birth, but the home really paints a picture of a typical American home 1914–1920. About 19% of the artifacts in the home are original to the Kennedy family, either used in the Beals Street residence or in later homes and returned to Beals Street during the restoration. Rose Kennedy worked with an interior designer named Robert Luddington as she restored the home, and he was largely in charge of procuring the rest of the items in the home, which are either period antiques or reproductions.
</p><p>Rose Kennedy donated the home to the <a href="/wiki/National_Park_Service" title="National Park Service">National Park Service</a> in 1967 as a memorial to her son. It is open to the public and visitors can take a ranger-guided tour or self-guided tours through the home.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy_National_Historic_Site&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>The house:</b><sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
Visitors can tour each of these rooms by both the ranger led and the self-guided tours.
</p><p><b>The basement</b> is the visitors' entrance and National Park visitor center. There is a public restroom and a small Eastern National retail shop. Visitors can view the exhibits and photographs and watch a film.
</p><p><b>Living room</b>  
Visitors to the Kennedy home would have been shown into the living room, or parlor as it would have been called when the family lived there. Here, visitors can see both a space for formal entertaining within the home, but also a space where the family would relax in the evenings when the children were getting ready for bed and when Joe Kennedy returned from his office in Boston.  The piano dominating the parlor belonged to Rose Kennedy and is one of the original pieces in the home. It was a wedding gift from two of her uncles.
</p><p><b>Dining room</b><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
The dining room has the home's most complete collection of Kennedy artifacts. The china was Rose's wedding china. The table would not have been set with a formal place setting for day to day meals. Rose Kennedy sets the table this way because as she is turning the house into a museum, she is expecting company. Meals were a time for the family to have lively discussions about topics that ranged from history, to politics, current events and religion. Rose was fond of saying "history is shaped by those who get their ideas across"<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and mealtime discussions were a staple in the Kennedy home. Meals were a formal affair, with the family assembling in the dining room and the food brought from the kitchen by a uniformed maid.
</p><p><b>Master bedroom</b>
The master bedroom is where Jack, Rosemary and Kathleen Kennedy were born. Jack Kennedy was born at 3:00pm on May 29, 1917.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  Rose Kennedy had seven of her nine children at home, and the same doctor, Dr. Good, delivered all of them, as well as the final two who were born in a hospital. The bedroom has several photographs, including the 6 month old baby pictures of Joe Jr., Jack, Rosemary, and Kathleen.
</p><p><b>Nursery</b>
The nursery has a bassinet that will eventually hold each of the nine Kennedy children, as well as a christening cap, gown, and a number of books and toys Joe Jr. and Jack would have played with. Visitors can see Jack's two favorite books: "King Arthur and his Knights" and "Billy Whiskers and His Kids."
</p><p><b>Guest bedroom</b>
The guest bedroom showcases linens bearing Rose's initials - REF - and Rosemary Kennedy's toiletry set. The guest room was converted to a girl's bedroom as soon as Rosemary and Kathleen were born.
</p><p><b>Boudoir</b>
The boudoir was Rose's private space as well as her office space. She would use the desk to keep track of the accounts of the household and to do her correspondence.  The desk showcases a card file. Rose kept an index card for each of her children where she would list important milestones in their medical history - things like vaccinations, major illnesses and hospital stays, as well as birthdays and confirmation dates. Jack Kennedy's card is on the desk.
</p><p><b>Kitchen</b>
The kitchen would have been the domain of the household's servants. It is modern for the time; the stove is a combination coal and gas, the family has an electric toaster as well as a large icebox.  Servants would have frequented the kitchen and the basement, which at the time would have been used for laundry.
</p><p><b>3rd floor</b>   
The third floor is not included on the tours. It has two attic rooms the servants used. Rose Kennedy hired both a French and an Irish girl, Alice Michelin and Mary O'Donahue<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> as the maid-of-all-work and nanny in the house. Their bedrooms were on the third floor. More information on domestic servants is on the park's <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.nps.gov/jofi">website</a>. The third floor was not restored by Rose Kennedy along with the rest of the house in the 1960s. This was due to the fact she did not have a complete memory of those rooms. Today, they are the park's administrative offices.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Visiting_information">Visiting information</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy_National_Historic_Site&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Visiting information">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the 2017 season (May–October), John Fitzgerald Kennedy National Historic Site offers daily guided tours. Ranger-guided tours are available every half hour from 10:00–11:30 and 1:00–3:30. The tours are 30 minutes long. Self-guided tours are available from 12:00–1:00 and 4:00–5:00. Visitors can use audio guides or to ask Rangers questions. On most days, rangers also lead neighborhood walks that leave at 2:15 and run for 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
