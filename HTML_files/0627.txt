<p><b>Middlesbrough railway station</b> serves the large town of <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a>, England and is managed by <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a>.
</p><p>The main <a href="/wiki/Railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Railway station">station</a> layout consists of an entrance hall with ticket office, large concourse/waiting area and two covered platforms that are each subdivided into two sections i.e. platform 1 (1a and 1b) and platform 2 (2a and 2b). Two freight lines bypass to the north of the station.
</p><p>The station is staffed and has a range of facilities including a cafe / bar, newsagent, cycle storage, toilets and lifts.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Various screens throughout the station give information on train arrivals and departures.
</p><p>Car parking is situated just to the east of the station and can be accessed via Exchange Square and Wood Street and by footpath directly to the station. A drop-off point is located at the front of the station close to the main entrance.
</p><p>According to the Office of Rail and Road statistics, Middlesbrough railway station is the fourth busiest in the North East region, with 1,312,916 total entries and exits (2017–18 period).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Middlesbrough_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>The first railway was built in the area as long ago as 1830 as an extension of the <a href="/wiki/Stockton_and_Darlington_Railway" title="Stockton and Darlington Railway">Stockton and Darlington Railway</a> to connect with the port of the then new town of Middlesbrough. A branch off this, passing just south of the new town and extending eastwards to <a href="/wiki/Redcar_Central_railway_station" title="Redcar Central railway station">Redcar</a> was opened in June 1846 by the Middlesbrough and Redcar Railway Company.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Situated on the southern edge of the new town on the Redcar branch line, Middlesbrough's first passenger station was designed by <a href="/wiki/John_Middleton_(architect)" title="John Middleton (architect)">John Middleton</a> and opened on 26 July 1847. As the town expanded rapidly during the second half of the 19th Century however, the station was unable to cope with the traffic growth. Due to the design of the station not lending itself to enlargement it was therefore demolished in 1874 and replaced by the current station, opened in December 1877.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The current station was designed by the <a href="/wiki/North_Eastern_Railway_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="North Eastern Railway (UK)">North Eastern Railway's</a> chief architect, <a href="/wiki/William_Peachey" title="William Peachey">William Peachey</a>, with an ornate <a href="/wiki/Gothic_architecture" title="Gothic architecture">Gothic</a> style frontage. Behind this an overall roof of <a href="/wiki/Elliptical" class="mw-redirect" title="Elliptical">elliptical</a> design once existed. Constructed out of <a href="/wiki/Wrought_iron" title="Wrought iron">wrought iron</a> of lattice design, with glass covering the middle half and timber (inside)/slate (outside) covering the outer quarters. The two end screens were glazed with timber cladding around the outer edges. The roof was high in relation to its width.<sup id="cite_ref-GazetteThenNow_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GazetteThenNow-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Middlesbrough_7_77438_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Middlesbrough_7_77438_1.jpg/220px-Middlesbrough_7_77438_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="142" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Middlesbrough_7_77438_1.jpg/330px-Middlesbrough_7_77438_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Middlesbrough_7_77438_1.jpg/440px-Middlesbrough_7_77438_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1594" data-file-height="1032" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Middlesbrough_7_77438_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Middlesbrough station, July 1977</div></div></div>
<p>The elliptical roof was severely damaged in a German daylight air raid in the afternoon of 3 August 1942<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and eventually removed in 1954, to be replaced by the current design over the concourse and platforms.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A major refurbishment of the station took place during 2017 and 2018 with repairs carried out to the station's roof and stonework and upgrading of the Wood Street car park.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> New information screens were also installed as part of the refurbishment.
</p><p>A new £20m station masterplan was approved in November 2018 by the Tees Valley Combined Authority. The plans include opening a third platform with accompanying glass frontage and concourse facing out onto Bridge Street West, giving the station increased capacity to accommodate more trains to <a href="/wiki/Whitby_railway_station" title="Whitby railway station">Whitby</a> and <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_railway_station" title="Newcastle railway station">Newcastle</a> and the re-introduction of direct trains from Middlesbrough to <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">London</a> in 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Middlesbrough_railway_station&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Middlesbrough_railway_station_July2014.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Middlesbrough_railway_station_July2014.jpg/220px-Middlesbrough_railway_station_July2014.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Middlesbrough_railway_station_July2014.jpg/330px-Middlesbrough_railway_station_July2014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Middlesbrough_railway_station_July2014.jpg/440px-Middlesbrough_railway_station_July2014.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="1552" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Middlesbrough_railway_station_July2014.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Middlesbrough station, July 2014</div></div></div>
<p>The station is served by a number of routes, all of which are operated by <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a>, except those to <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Manchester Airport railway station">Manchester</a> that are operated by <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Tees_Valley_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Tees Valley Line">Tees Valley Line</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bishop_Auckland_railway_station" title="Bishop Auckland railway station">Bishop Auckland</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Darlington_railway_station" title="Darlington railway station">Darlington</a> to <a href="/wiki/Saltburn_railway_station" title="Saltburn railway station">Saltburn</a>, (usually a British Rail <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_156" title="British Rail Class 156">Class 156 Super Sprinter</a> or <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_158" title="British Rail Class 158">Class 158 Express Sprinter</a>). Monday to Saturday daytimes there is a service every 30 minutes to Darlington &amp; Saltburn and every hour to Bishop Auckland. There are also three a.m peak services to Newcastle via <a href="/wiki/Durham_railway_station" title="Durham railway station">Durham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NRT_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NRT-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>  Sundays see an hourly service to Darlington/Saltburn and a two-hourly service to Bishop Auckland.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Esk_Valley_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Esk Valley Line">Esk Valley Line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Whitby_railway_station" title="Whitby railway station">Whitby</a> via <a href="/wiki/Nunthorpe_railway_station" title="Nunthorpe railway station">Nunthorpe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grosmont_railway_station" title="Grosmont railway station">Grosmont</a>, (usually a British Rail Class 156 Super Sprinter or Class 158 Express Sprinter). The hourly service runs up to seventeen trains a day to <a href="/wiki/Nunthorpe_railway_station" title="Nunthorpe railway station">Nunthorpe</a> with four of the trains continuing to the terminus at Whitby, one running as far as <a href="/wiki/Danby_railway_station" title="Danby railway station">Danby</a> and a sixth as far as <a href="/wiki/Battersby_railway_station" title="Battersby railway station">Battersby</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>. Sunday services (four each way, some running through to/from Newcastle) operate throughout the year. A new <a href="/wiki/James_Cook_railway_station" title="James Cook railway station">station</a> on the Esk Valley Line to serve the <a href="/wiki/James_Cook_University_Hospital" title="James Cook University Hospital">James Cook University Hospital</a> (between Middlesbrough and <a href="/wiki/Marton_railway_station_(North_Yorkshire)" title="Marton railway station (North Yorkshire)">Marton</a> railway stations) was opened on 18 May 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Durham_Coast_Line" title="Durham Coast Line">Durham Coast Line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_railway_station" title="Newcastle railway station">Newcastle Central</a> via <a href="/wiki/Hartlepool_railway_station" title="Hartlepool railway station">Hartlepool</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sunderland_railway_station" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunderland railway station">Sunderland</a> (usually a British Rail <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_142" title="British Rail Class 142">Class 142 Pacer</a> (to be phased out at the end of December 2019) or Class 156 Super Sprinter).<sup id="cite_ref-NRT_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NRT-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> There is a train every hour to Newcastle (most of which continue along the <a href="/wiki/Tyne_Valley_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Tyne Valley Line">Tyne Valley Line</a> to <a href="/wiki/Hexham_railway_station" title="Hexham railway station">Hexham</a> or Carlisle), with many of these running to/from Nunthorpe.  Sundays see an hourly service through to/from the <a href="/wiki/MetroCentre_railway_station" title="MetroCentre railway station">MetroCentre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carlisle_railway_station" title="Carlisle railway station">Carlisle</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/North_TransPennine" class="mw-redirect" title="North TransPennine">North Trans-Pennine line</a> to Manchester Airport via <a href="/wiki/York_railway_station" title="York railway station">York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leeds_railway_station" title="Leeds railway station">Leeds</a> (using a British Rail <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_185" title="British Rail Class 185">Class 185 Desiro</a>). Monday to Saturdays there is an hourly service to Manchester Airport and every two hours on Sundays. TransPennine Express also operate one daily return service to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Lime_Street_railway_station" title="Liverpool Lime Street railway station">Liverpool</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Under the new Northern franchise awarded to <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Rail_North" class="mw-redirect" title="Arriva Rail North">Arriva Rail North</a> in December 2015 (commenced 1 April 2016), service frequencies on several routes are to be improved and rolling stock upgraded.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 27 November 2014, it was announced that, as part of the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Trains_East_Coast" title="Virgin Trains East Coast">Virgin Trains East Coast</a> franchise, direct rail services from Middlesbrough to London would be re-introduced.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/London_North_Eastern_Railway" title="London North Eastern Railway">LNER</a>, the current operator of the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Main_Line" title="East Coast Main Line">East Coast Main Line</a>, is expected to run the new Hitachi manufactured <a href="/wiki/British_Rail_Class_800" title="British Rail Class 800">Class 800 Azuma</a> trains to Middlesbrough by 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
