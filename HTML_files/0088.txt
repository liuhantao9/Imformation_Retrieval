<p><i><b>Cry Pretty</b></i> is the sixth studio album by American country music singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>. The album was released on September 14, 2018, as Underwood's first album with her new record label, <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records_Nashville" title="Capitol Records Nashville">Capitol Records Nashville</a> after signing a global deal with <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a> at the start of 2017. The album marked the first co-producing effort by Underwood, who partnered with <a href="/wiki/David_Garcia_(musician)" title="David Garcia (musician)">David Garcia</a> for the record.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The album was met with mostly positive reviews from <a href="/wiki/Music_critics" class="mw-redirect" title="Music critics">music critics</a>, and debuted at number one on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>, making Underwood the first woman to hit the top of the <i>Billboard</i> 200 chart with four country albums. It also achieved the biggest sales week for a country album in more than three years, as well as the biggest sales week for a female artist in 2018. It also debuted at number one in Canada, number four in Australia and charted in several other markets.
</p><p>The album has so far been supported by three singles: "<a href="/wiki/Cry_Pretty_(song)" title="Cry Pretty (song)">Cry Pretty</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Love_Wins_(song)" title="Love Wins (song)">Love Wins</a>" and <a href="/wiki/Southbound_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Southbound (Carrie Underwood song)">“Southbound</a>”. To further promote it, Underwood embarked on <a href="/wiki/The_Cry_Pretty_Tour_360" class="mw-redirect" title="The Cry Pretty Tour 360">The Cry Pretty Tour 360</a>, which started in May 2019.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cry_Pretty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On April 18, 2018, Underwood released a statement on the album, saying, "At this point in my career, I feel stronger and more creative than ever. I think you can hear that in this new album. It's emotional, it's soulful, it's real, and we also have some fun on there too. I hope everyone loves it as much as I have loved making it."<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Songwriter and producer <a href="/wiki/David_Garcia_(musician)" title="David Garcia (musician)">David Garcia</a> was selected to co-produce the album with Underwood. Additionally, <i>Cry Pretty</i> marks Underwood's first effort as producer.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">Promotion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cry_Pretty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Promotion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood performed the title track for the first time at the 53rd Academy of Country Music Awards on April 15, 2018, which was well-received by critics.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> She also performed "Spinning Bottles" at the <a href="/wiki/American_Music_Awards_of_2018" title="American Music Awards of 2018">American Music Awards</a> on October 9, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She gave her first live performance of “Southbound” at the 54th <a href="/wiki/Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="Academy of Country Music Awards">Academy of Country Music Awards</a> on April 7, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">Singles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cry_Pretty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Singles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"<a href="/wiki/Cry_Pretty_(song)" title="Cry Pretty (song)">Cry Pretty</a>", the first single from the album was released on April 11, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It debuted at number 20 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart, and was the most added song to Country Radio that week.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The song topped the <a href="/wiki/Digital_Songs" title="Digital Songs">Digital Songs</a> chart, becoming Underwood's first song to do so and also debuted at number 48 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It peaked at number nine on the <i>Billboard</i> Country Airplay chart and number five on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart. "<a href="/wiki/Love_Wins_(song)" title="Love Wins (song)">Love Wins</a>" was released as the second single from the album on August 31, 2018, alongside a lyric video.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It debuted at number 90 on the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Singles_Chart" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish Singles Chart">Scottish Singles Chart</a> for the week of September 8, 2018, and number 30 on the US Hot Country Songs chart for the week of September 15, 2018. On September 7, 2018, "End Up with You" was released as a promotional single.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Southbound_(Carrie_Underwood_song)" title="Southbound (Carrie Underwood song)">“Southbound</a>” was released as the third single from the album, impacting radio on April 29, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tour">Tour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cry_Pretty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 8, 2018, Underwood announced <a href="/wiki/The_Cry_Pretty_Tour_360" class="mw-redirect" title="The Cry Pretty Tour 360">The Cry Pretty Tour 360</a> in support of the album, scheduled to begin on May 1, 2019, in <a href="/wiki/Greensboro,_North_Carolina" title="Greensboro, North Carolina">Greensboro, North Carolina</a> and conclude on October 31 in <a href="/wiki/Detroit,_Michigan" class="mw-redirect" title="Detroit, Michigan">Detroit, Michigan</a>, playing 54 shows.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Runaway_June" title="Runaway June">Runaway June</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maddie_%26_Tae" title="Maddie &amp; Tae">Maddie &amp; Tae</a> are the supporting acts of the tour.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><i>Cry Pretty</i> received mostly positive reviews from critics. On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a normalized rating out of 100 to reviews from mainstream publications, the album has an <a href="/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean" title="Weighted arithmetic mean">average</a> score of 69, based on eleven reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-MC_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MC-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Giving a rating of three and a half stars out of five, <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> called <i>Cry Pretty</i> a "modern country album pivoting into pop and R&amp;B without going full <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Swift" title="Taylor Swift">Taylor</a>, while also showing the kind of character more mega-stars should aspire to". The songwriting was referred to as "grade-A" and Underwood's voice was deemed "mighty" and "selling even the lesser [songs]". The review also praised Underwood for raising the topic of gun violence in "The Bullet" and "Love Wins", noting that "if Underwood isn't going there, she's at least got the balls to engage the subject in the mainstream, where the conversation needs to happen — while her male peers, no doubt worried about their market share, seem scared to make a peep". Additionally, the review draws favourable similarities to the music of <a href="/wiki/Adele" title="Adele">Adele</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kate_Bush" title="Kate Bush">Kate Bush</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brad_Paisley" title="Brad Paisley">Brad Paisley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alison_Krauss" title="Alison Krauss">Alison Krauss</a> but highlights <a href="/wiki/Sam_Hunt" title="Sam Hunt">Sam Hunt</a>, explaining that the "architecture [of the album] is R&amp;B at its core".<sup id="cite_ref-RS_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RS-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Markos Papadatos of the <i>Digital Journal</i> praised the album stating "the control Underwood maintains over her voice throughout the album is impeccable. Overall, Carrie Underwood excels on this album as a singer, songwriter, and producer. <i>Cry Pretty</i> is a superb studio effort, and it garners an A rating."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Diamondback" title="The Diamondback">The Diamondback</a></i> reviewed the album favorably, writing "poised and mature, her sixth studio album effortlessly differentiates itself through the masterful lyricism and savvy falsettos that trademark Underwood's undeniable talent, even over a decade after her <i>American Idol</i> win. The organic sense of vulnerability conveyed throughout the entire album leaves the listener with the impression of having just sat down and talked for hours with Underwood herself." The review concluded with the statement "Underwood proves on <i>Cry Pretty</i> that she has strategically maintained her stronghold of country regality, as this album portrays her dynamic evolution not only as a musician, but as a person as well."<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cry_Pretty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United States, <i>Cry Pretty</i> debuted on top of the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> with 266,000 <a href="/wiki/Album-equivalent_unit" title="Album-equivalent unit">album-equivalent units</a>, including 251,000 pure album sales, giving Underwood her fourth number-one album, and making her the first woman to have four number-one country albums on the chart.<sup id="cite_ref-BB200_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB200-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> It is the largest sales week for a country album since <a href="/wiki/Luke_Bryan" title="Luke Bryan">Luke Bryan</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Kill_the_Lights_(Luke_Bryan_album)" title="Kill the Lights (Luke Bryan album)">Kill the Lights</a></i> in 2015, and the biggest sales week for a female artist in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-BB200_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB200-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> It also debuted atop the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts">Top Country Albums</a> chart, becoming her seventh consecutive number one album on that chart. The debut of <i>Cry Pretty</i> at number one on the <i>Billboard 200</i> prompted Underwood to rise from number sixty-one to the top of <a href="/wiki/List_of_number-one_artists_on_the_Billboard_Artist_100" title="List of number-one artists on the Billboard Artist 100"><i>Billboard</i> Artist 100</a>. It made her the first female country artist to top that chart.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <i>Cry Pretty</i> was certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> on October 23, 2018 for 500,000 units including streams and track sales in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-RIAA_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> It was the seventh best-selling album of 2018 in the United States, with 401,000 copies sold that year.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> It has sold 499,100 copies in the United States as of September 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-sales_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sales-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The album also debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Albums_Chart" title="Canadian Albums Chart">Canadian Albums Chart</a> with 28,000 album-equivalent units, giving Underwood her third number-one album in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-FYI_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FYI-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <i>Cry Pretty</i> opened at number four on the Australian <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">ARIA Albums Chart</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-ARIA_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ARIA-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> becoming her third top five album there, while also debuting at number one on the country component chart.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> It also debuted at number 12 in <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Singles_and_Albums_Charts" title="Scottish Singles and Albums Charts">Scotland</a> and number sixteen on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a>—becoming her third top twenty album in the region.
</p>
