<p>The <b>Rugby Football League Championship First Division</b> was the top division of rugby league in England between 1895 and 1996, when it was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League_Championship_First_Division&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1895.E2.80.931904:_Foundations"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1895–1904:_Foundations">1895–1904: Foundations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League_Championship_First_Division&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1895–1904: Foundations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_rugby_league" title="History of rugby league">History of rugby league</a></div>
<p>The first season of <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> (1895–96) saw all the breakaway clubs play in a single league competition. The addition of new teams and the problems of travelling led to the league being split in two for the following season; into the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_county_leagues" title="Rugby league county leagues">Yorkshire League</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_county_leagues" title="Rugby league county leagues">Lancashire League</a>. This arrangement lasted until the 1901–02 season, when the top clubs from each league resigned and formed a single new competition. The following season the remaining clubs in the Yorkshire and Lancashire Leagues were re-organised to form a <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_League_Championship_Second_Division" title="Rugby Football League Championship Second Division">second division</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="1905.E2.80.931970:_Restructure"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1905–1970:_Restructure">1905–1970: Restructure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League_Championship_First_Division&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1905–1970: Restructure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1905–06 the two divisions were re-combined into a single competition. Clubs played all the teams in their own county on a home-and-away basis, results counting towards the re-formed Yorkshire and Lancashire Leagues. They also organised inter-county fixtures on an individual basis; all results were collated into a single table for the Championship. In order to even up the competition a top-four play-off series was used to determine the Championship.
</p><p>Apart from the interventions of the two <a href="/wiki/World_war" title="World war">world wars</a>, this system was retained until the 1962–63 season, when the league briefly returned to a two divisional system. This lasted only two years, and in the 1964–65 season they returned to one large division subdivided into county leagues. The play-offs were expanded to the top 16 teams and the <a href="/wiki/Harry_Sunderland_Trophy" title="Harry Sunderland Trophy">Harry Sunderland Trophy</a> was introduced as the man-of-the-match award for the decider.
</p>
<h3><span id="1970.E2.80.931995:_Premiership_and_reintroducing_Second_Division"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1970–1995:_Premiership_and_reintroducing_Second_Division">1970–1995: Premiership and reintroducing Second Division</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League_Championship_First_Division&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1970–1995: Premiership and reintroducing Second Division">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the 1973–74 season they once again went back to two divisions. The play-off and the Yorkshire and Lancashire League were abandoned, and a new play-off type competition, the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_Premiership" title="Rugby League Premiership">Premiership</a>, was introduced however the team finishing top of the Championship were crowned champions. During this period in the late 1980s and early 1990s <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Warriors" title="Wigan Warriors">Wigan</a> dominated both the Championship and <a href="/wiki/Challenge_Cup" title="Challenge Cup">Challenge Cup</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="1996.E2.80.93present:_Super_League"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1996–present:_Super_League">1996–present: Super League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League_Championship_First_Division&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1996–present: Super League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1996 the championship was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a>. The remaining clubs later went on to form the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_Championships" title="Rugby League Championships">Rugby League Championships</a>.
</p>
