	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Miss Woodford Stakes</h1>

<p>The <b>Miss Woodford Stakes</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Thoroughbred_horse_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Thoroughbred horse race">Thoroughbred horse race</a> usually run each year in August at <a href="/wiki/Monmouth_Park_Racetrack" title="Monmouth Park Racetrack">Monmouth Park Racetrack</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oceanport,_New_Jersey" title="Oceanport, New Jersey">Oceanport, New Jersey</a> open to three-year-old <a href="/wiki/Filly" title="Filly">fillies</a>.  An <a href="/wiki/Graded_stakes_race" title="Graded stakes race">ungraded</a> stakes currently offering a current purse of $75,000, it is a <a href="/wiki/Sprint_(horse_racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sprint (horse racing)">sprint race</a> contested over a distance of six <a href="/wiki/Furlong" title="Furlong">furlongs</a> on the dirt.
</p><p>Added to Monmouth Park's stakes schedule in 1952, the race is named for the great racing mare <a href="/wiki/Miss_Woodford" title="Miss Woodford">Miss Woodford</a> of the late 19th century who was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/National_Museum_of_Racing_and_Hall_of_Fame" title="National Museum of Racing and Hall of Fame">National Museum of Racing and Hall of Fame</a> in 1967. It was at Monmouth in the old <a href="/wiki/Monmouth_Oaks" title="Monmouth Oaks">Monmouth Oaks</a> that Miss Woodford ran the race that made her the highest earning racehorse of her time. Fellow U.S. Racing Hall of Fame inductees <a href="/wiki/Tosmah" title="Tosmah">Tosmah</a> (1964) and  <a href="/wiki/Ta_Wee" title="Ta Wee">Ta Wee</a> (1969) won this race. 
</p><p>The Miss Woodford stakes was run in two divisions in 1967 and again in 1986.
</p>
