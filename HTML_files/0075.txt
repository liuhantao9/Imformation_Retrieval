<p>"<b>Home Sweet Home</b>" is a song by American <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metal_music" title="Heavy metal music">heavy metal</a> band <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe" title="Mötley Crüe">Mötley Crüe</a>. It was originally released in 1985 on the album <i><a href="/wiki/Theatre_of_Pain" title="Theatre of Pain">Theatre of Pain</a></i>, and again in 1991 for the <i><a href="/wiki/Decade_of_Decadence_81-91" title="Decade of Decadence 81-91">Decade of Decadence 81-91</a></i> compilation album. It has been recorded as a <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover version</a> by several artists, most notably country singer <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, who released her version as a single in 2009.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_Sweet_Home_(M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally released on the band's 1985 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Theatre_of_Pain" title="Theatre of Pain">Theatre of Pain</a></i>, the song was accompanied by a music video which documented the band's undertakings over the course of one or several concerts. Some of the original video was shot in Houston, Texas live at <a href="/wiki/The_Summit_(Houston)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Summit (Houston)">The Summit</a> during the 1985 Theatre of Pain tour. They performed the song twice that night apparently to get more video footage. "Home Sweet Home" was released &amp; remixed twice: once for the original promotion for the single in 1985. A radio only promo 12" with the remix was sent to stations, but not released commercially until the 1988 Japan-only <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">EP</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Raw_Tracks" title="Raw Tracks">Raw Tracks</a>. </i>The song was remixed again in 1991 and had some instruments overdubbed. Now called "Home Sweet Home '91", it was released as a single with a new video and included on the <i><a href="/wiki/Decade_of_Decadence" class="mw-redirect" title="Decade of Decadence">Decade of Decadence</a></i> compilation. The song is often referred to as a <a href="/wiki/Power_ballad" class="mw-redirect" title="Power ballad">power ballad</a>, and its success was a prelude to similar marketing formula for other hair bands in the late 1980s.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The song ranks number 12 on VH1's chart of the greatest power ballads of all time.
Drummer <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Lee" title="Tommy Lee">Tommy Lee</a> re-recorded the song for Season 4 of the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Californication_(TV_series)" title="Californication (TV series)">Californication</a></i>, and has a cameo in "Lights, Camera, Asshole" performing the song on piano in a bar at the end of the episode. Also, the third episode in the show's fourth season was named after this song.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_Sweet_Home_(M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The video (directed by <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Isham" title="Wayne Isham">Wayne Isham</a> and guest-starring actor <a href="/wiki/Michael_Berryman" title="Michael Berryman">Michael Berryman</a>) depicts each band member receiving a phone call home, and replying "I'm on my way!", Vince Neil on a beach, Mick Mars on a throne in a haunted house, Nikki Sixx at a bar, and Tommy Lee at a wild party. The piano intro begins as a <a href="/wiki/Tour_bus" class="mw-redirect" title="Tour bus">tour bus</a> driving by is shown. The rest of the video shows the band pre-concert and performing on stage, shot at <a href="/wiki/The_Summit_(Houston)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Summit (Houston)">The Summit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a> (concert footage) and <a href="/wiki/Reunion_Arena" title="Reunion Arena">Reunion Arena</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dallas,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Dallas, Texas">Dallas, Texas</a> (exterior). The video is notable for its long stay on top of the MTV daily request chart, lasting over three months until MTV invoked the unwritten "Crue Rule", dropping videos from eligibility on their request line after 30 days. The end of the video shows the same <a href="/wiki/Tour_bus" class="mw-redirect" title="Tour bus">tour bus</a> from the beginning with the words "Rockin 'N' Rollin".
</p><p>A parody of this video was used for the end credits of the 2010 film <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_Tub_Time_Machine" title="Hot Tub Time Machine">Hot Tub Time Machine</a></i>, with <a href="/wiki/Rob_Corddry" title="Rob Corddry">Rob Corddry</a>'s character Lou "Violator" Dorchen disguised as <a href="/wiki/Vince_Neil" title="Vince Neil">Vince Neil</a> and the band's name known as "Mötley Lüe". It also reuses the same clips from the original video.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_positions">Chart positions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_Sweet_Home_(M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chart positions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The original release of "Home Sweet Home" charted at number 89 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, and "Home Sweet Home '91" peaked at number 37 on the same chart in 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> To date, "Home Sweet Home '91" is the last Mötley Crüe song to chart in the American Billboard Top 40.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cover_versions">Cover versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_Sweet_Home_(M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cover versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The song was re-recorded by <a href="/wiki/Linkin_Park" title="Linkin Park">Linkin Park</a> vocalist <a href="/wiki/Chester_Bennington" title="Chester Bennington">Chester Bennington</a> on co-lead vocals along with Mötley Crüe in the wake of <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">Hurricane Katrina</a>. The music video for the song shows videos of Katrina rescues, along with a performance from the band.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/30_Foot_Fall" title="30 Foot Fall">30 Foot Fall</a> included a cover version of the song as a bonus track on their album <i>Ever Revolving, Never Evolving</i>.</li>
<li>It was also recorded by <a href="/wiki/Limp_Bizkit" title="Limp Bizkit">Limp Bizkit</a> for their <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hitz" class="mw-redirect" title="Greatest Hitz">Greatest Hitz</a></i> album, and is joined by a remake of "<a href="/wiki/Bitter_Sweet_Symphony" title="Bitter Sweet Symphony">Bitter Sweet Symphony</a>" by <a href="/wiki/The_Verve" title="The Verve">The Verve</a>. It is often referred to as "Bittersweet Home".</li>
<li>The band Radio Cult released a cover of the song on their album "Retroactive" in 2007.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rob_Corddry" title="Rob Corddry">Rob Corddry</a> did a cover version for the 2010 film, <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_Tub_Time_Machine" title="Hot Tub Time Machine">Hot Tub Time Machine</a></i>.</li>
<li>Tommy Lee performed the song on piano when he guest starred as a singer on the TV series <i><a href="/wiki/Californication_(TV_series)" title="Californication (TV series)">Californication</a></i> at the end of the episode of "Lights, Camera, Asshole". This version also appears on the show's Season 4 soundtrack.</li>
<li>In 2013, former <a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a> contestant Todrick Hall made a version of the song in a medley video in tribute to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wizard_of_Oz_(1939_film)" title="The Wizard of Oz (1939 film)">The Wizard of Oz</a></i>.</li></ul>
