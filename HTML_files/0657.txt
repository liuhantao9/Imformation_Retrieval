<p>The <b>Rugby Football Union</b> (<b>RFU</b>) is the governing body for <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> in England. It was founded in 1871, and was the sport's international governing body prior to the formation of what is now known as <a href="/wiki/World_Rugby" title="World Rugby">World Rugby</a> (WR) in 1886. It promotes and runs the sport, organises international matches for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_union_team" title="England national rugby union team">England national team</a>, and educates and trains players and officials.
</p><p>The RFU is an <a href="/wiki/Industrial_and_provident_society" title="Industrial and provident society">industrial and provident society</a> owned by over 2,000 member clubs,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> representing over 2.5 million registered players,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and forms the largest rugby union society in the world, and one of the largest sports organisations in England. It is based at <a href="/wiki/Twickenham_Stadium" title="Twickenham Stadium">Twickenham Stadium</a>, London.
</p><p>In September 2010 the equivalent women's rugby body, the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_Union_for_Women" title="Rugby Football Union for Women">Rugby Football Union for Women</a> (RFUW), was able to nominate a member to the RFU Council to represent women and girls rugby. The RFUW  was integrated into the RFU in July 2012.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<td class="mbox-image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Wikisource-logo.svg/38px-Wikisource-logo.svg.png" decoding="async" width="38" height="40" class="noviewer" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Wikisource-logo.svg/57px-Wikisource-logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Wikisource-logo.svg/76px-Wikisource-logo.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="410" data-file-height="430" /></td>

<p>On 4 December 1870, Edwin Ash of <a href="/wiki/Richmond_F.C." title="Richmond F.C.">Richmond</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Burns" title="Benjamin Burns">Benjamin Burns</a> of <a href="/wiki/Blackheath_F.C." title="Blackheath F.C.">Blackheath</a> published a letter in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> suggesting that "those who play the rugby-type game should meet to form a code of practice as various clubs play to rules which differ from others, which makes the game difficult to play." On 26 January 1871 a meeting attended by representatives from 21 clubs was held in London at the <a href="/wiki/Pall_Mall_Restaurant" title="Pall Mall Restaurant">Pall Mall Restaurant</a> on <a href="/wiki/Regent_Street" title="Regent Street">Regent Street</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a href="/wiki/File:RFU-foundation-plaque.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/RFU-foundation-plaque.jpg/160px-RFU-foundation-plaque.jpg" decoding="async" width="160" height="159" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/RFU-foundation-plaque.jpg/240px-RFU-foundation-plaque.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/RFU-foundation-plaque.jpg/320px-RFU-foundation-plaque.jpg 2x" data-file-width="891" data-file-height="887" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:RFU-foundation-plaque.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Plaque marking the foundation location of the RFU</div></div></div>
<p>The 21 clubs present at the meeting were: <a href="/wiki/Blackheath_F.C." title="Blackheath F.C.">Blackheath</a> (represented by Burns and <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Stokes_(rugby_player)" class="mw-redirect" title="Frederick Stokes (rugby player)">Frederick Stokes</a>, the latter becoming the first captain of <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_union_team" title="England national rugby union team">England</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Richmond_F.C." title="Richmond F.C.">Richmond</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ravenscourt_Park_Football_Club" title="Ravenscourt Park Football Club">Ravenscourt Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Kent_Football_Club" title="West Kent Football Club">West Kent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marlborough_Nomads" title="Marlborough Nomads">Marlborough Nomads</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wimbledon_RFC" title="Wimbledon RFC">Wimbledon Hornets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gipsies_Football_Club" title="Gipsies Football Club">Gipsies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Civil_Service_FC" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil Service FC">Civil Service</a>, <a href="/wiki/Law_FC" title="Law FC">The Law Club</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wellington_College,_Berkshire" title="Wellington College, Berkshire">Wellington College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guy%27s,_Kings_and_St._Thomas%27_Rugby_Football_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Guy&#39;s, Kings and St. Thomas&#39; Rugby Football Club">Guy's Hospital</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flamingoes_(Rugby_Football_Club)" class="mw-redirect" title="Flamingoes (Rugby Football Club)">Flamingoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clapham_Rovers" class="mw-redirect" title="Clapham Rovers">Clapham Rovers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harlequin_F.C." title="Harlequin F.C.">Harlequin F.C.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kings_College_Hospital_RFC" class="mw-redirect" title="Kings College Hospital RFC">King's College Hospital</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Paul%27s_School_(London)" class="mw-redirect" title="St Paul&#39;s School (London)">St Paul's</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_House_Football_Club" title="Queen&#39;s House Football Club">Queen's House</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lausanne_Football_Club_(Dulwich)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lausanne Football Club (Dulwich)">Lausanne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Addison_Football_Club" title="Addison Football Club">Addison</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mohicans_Football_Club" title="Mohicans Football Club">Mohicans</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Belsize_Park_Football_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="Belsize Park Football Club">Belsize Park</a>. The one notable omission was the <a href="/wiki/Wasps_RFC" title="Wasps RFC">Wasps</a>. According to one version, a Wasps' representative was sent to attend the meeting, but owing to a misunderstanding was sent to the wrong venue at the wrong time on the wrong day; another version is that he went to a venue of the same name where, after consuming a number of drinks, he realised his mistake but was too drunk to make his way to the correct venue. Ealing Rugby Club (now Ealing Trailfinders) also received an invitation, but their representative stopped in a public house and also missed the meeting.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Re-excommunication_-_JM_Staniforth.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Re-excommunication_-_JM_Staniforth.png/160px-Re-excommunication_-_JM_Staniforth.png" decoding="async" width="160" height="254" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Re-excommunication_-_JM_Staniforth.png/240px-Re-excommunication_-_JM_Staniforth.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Re-excommunication_-_JM_Staniforth.png/320px-Re-excommunication_-_JM_Staniforth.png 2x" data-file-width="463" data-file-height="735" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Re-excommunication_-_JM_Staniforth.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Re-excommunication</i>: cartoon by <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Morewood_Staniforth" title="Joseph Morewood Staniforth">J. M. Staniforth</a>. The RFU is represented as a religious cabal, expelling <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Gould_(rugby_union)" title="Arthur Gould (rugby union)">Arthur "Monkey" Gould</a> from their "church" over the "Gould Affair". Gould, in his Newport jersey, appears unconcerned</div></div></div>
<p>As a result of this meeting the Rugby Football Union (RFU) was founded. Algernon Rutter was elected as the first president of the RFU, and Edwin Ash was elected as treasurer. Three lawyers who were <a href="/wiki/Rugby_School" title="Rugby School">Rugby School</a> alumni (Rutter, Holmes and L.J. Maton) drew up the first laws of the game, which were approved in June 1871.
</p><p>Although similar unions were organised during the next few years in Ireland, Wales, Scotland, New Zealand, Australia, France, Canada, South Africa, and the United States, the RFU was the first and therefore had no need to distinguish itself from others by calling itself the English RFU.
</p>
<h3><span id="Northern_clubs_secede_.E2.80.93_Rugby_League"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_clubs_secede_–_Rugby_League">Northern clubs secede – Rugby League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Northern clubs secede – Rugby League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Twenty-two rugby clubs from across the north of England met on 29 August 1895 in the <a href="/wiki/George_Hotel,_Huddersfield" title="George Hotel, Huddersfield">George Hotel</a> in Huddersfield, where they voted to secede from the Rugby Football Union<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The reason for this is unclear. (November 2017)">why?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and set up the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_League" title="Rugby Football League">Northern Rugby Football Union</a> (later renamed the Rugby Football League). The RFU took strong action against the clubs involved in the formation of the NRFU, all of whom were deemed to have forfeited their amateur status and therefore to have left the RFU. A similar interpretation was applied to all players who played either for or against such clubs, whether or not they received any compensation. These players were barred indefinitely from any involvement in organised rugby union. These comprehensive and enduring sanctions, combined with the very localised nature of most rugby competition, meant that most northern clubs had little practical option but to affiliate with the NRFU in the first few years of its existence.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="The_modern_era_.281970_.E2.80.93_present.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_modern_era_(1970_–_present)">The modern era (1970 – present)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The modern era (1970 – present)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_Rugby_Union#Professionalism" class="mw-redirect" title="History of Rugby Union">History of Rugby Union §&#160;Professionalism</a></div>
<p>The RFU long resisted competitions and leagues fearing that they would encourage foul play and professionalism. The first club competition, then known as the R.F.U. Club Competition, took place in 1972. Following a sponsorship agreement it became known as the John Player Cup in 1976.
</p><p>The RFU agreed to the formation of a league pyramid in 1987.
</p><p>In 2005 the RFU began talks about a merger with the governing body for women's rugby union the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_Union_for_Women" title="Rugby Football Union for Women">RFUW</a>. In September 2010 the RFUW was able to nominate a member to the RFU Council to represent women and girls rugby. The RFUW  was integrated into the RFU in July 2012.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">Structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:102px;"><a href="/wiki/File:England_national_rugby_union_team_(emblem).svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a1/England_national_rugby_union_team_%28emblem%29.svg/100px-England_national_rugby_union_team_%28emblem%29.svg.png" decoding="async" width="100" height="151" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a1/England_national_rugby_union_team_%28emblem%29.svg/150px-England_national_rugby_union_team_%28emblem%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/a/a1/England_national_rugby_union_team_%28emblem%29.svg/200px-England_national_rugby_union_team_%28emblem%29.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="117" data-file-height="177" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:England_national_rugby_union_team_(emblem).svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Former logo of the RFU</div></div></div>
<p>In response to the faltering results of the England national team, <a href="/wiki/Rob_Andrew" title="Rob Andrew">Rob Andrew</a> was appointed on 18 August 2006 by the RFU to the post of Director of Elite Rugby, to oversee all aspects of representative rugby in England from the regional academies to the full senior side, including senior team selection powers and the power to hire and fire coaches at all levels of English rugby. Andrew also had the task of building bridges with the premiership clubs and the RFU in terms of players withdrawal from their club duties for international duties. On 6 January 2011 his role of Director of Elite Rugby was scrapped in an overhaul of the organisation's structure.
</p><p>Chief executive John Steele opted to create a single rugby department divided into the areas of performance, operations and development with the emphasis on "delivering rugby at all levels", with each area having its own director.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_teams">National teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: National teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Men.27s_team"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Men's_team">Men's team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Men&#039;s team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_union_team" title="England national rugby union team">England national rugby union team</a></div>
<p>The England national rugby union team competes in the annual <a href="/wiki/Six_Nations_Championship" title="Six Nations Championship">Six Nations Championship</a> with <a href="/wiki/France_national_rugby_union_team" title="France national rugby union team">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland_national_rugby_union_team" title="Ireland national rugby union team">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_rugby_union_team" title="Scotland national rugby union team">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_rugby_union_team" title="Italy national rugby union team">Italy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_rugby_union_team" title="Wales national rugby union team">Wales</a>. They have won this championship outright on a total of 28 occasions (with the addition of 10 shared victories), 13 times winning the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Slam_(rugby_union)" title="Grand Slam (rugby union)">Grand Slam</a> and 25 times winning the <a href="/wiki/Triple_Crown_(rugby_union)" title="Triple Crown (rugby union)">Triple Crown</a>, making them the most successful team in the tournament's history. England are to date the only team from the northern hemisphere to win the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_World_Cup" title="Rugby World Cup">Rugby World Cup</a>, when they won the tournament back in <a href="/wiki/2003_Rugby_World_Cup" title="2003 Rugby World Cup">2003</a>. They were also runners-up in <a href="/wiki/1991_Rugby_World_Cup" title="1991 Rugby World Cup">1991</a> and <a href="/wiki/2007_Rugby_World_Cup" title="2007 Rugby World Cup">2007</a>. They are currently <a href="/wiki/IRB_World_Rankings" class="mw-redirect" title="IRB World Rankings">ranked</a> fourth in the world by the <a href="/wiki/International_Rugby_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="International Rugby Board">International Rugby Board</a> as of 19 November 2018.
</p>
<h3><span id="Women.27s_national_team"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Women's_national_team">Women's national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Women&#039;s national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/England_women%27s_national_rugby_union_team" title="England women&#39;s national rugby union team">England women's national rugby union team</a></div>
<p>The England women's national rugby union team first played in 1982.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> England have taken part in every <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Rugby_World_Cup" title="Women&#39;s Rugby World Cup">Women's Rugby World Cup</a> competition. They won the competition in 1994 by defeating the United States 38–23 in the final, and again in 2014 by beating Canada 21–9 in the final. They finished as runner-up on four other occasions.  Their coach is <a href="/wiki/Simon_Middleton" title="Simon Middleton">Simon Middleton</a> after their coach <a href="/wiki/Gary_Street" title="Gary Street">Gary Street</a>, who had been head coach since 2006, retired in 2015.
</p>
<h3><span id="Men.27s_national_sevens_team"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Men's_national_sevens_team">Men's national sevens team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Men&#039;s national sevens team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_sevens_team" title="England national rugby sevens team">England national rugby sevens team</a></div>
<p>The England national rugby sevens team competes in the <a href="/wiki/World_Rugby_Sevens_Series" title="World Rugby Sevens Series">World Rugby Sevens Series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rugby_World_Cup_Sevens" title="Rugby World Cup Sevens">Rugby World Cup Sevens</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Games" title="Commonwealth Games">Commonwealth Games</a>. England's best finish in the Sevens Series is second place, which they have achieved four times, most recently in the 2016-17 season.
The England Sevens team has generated several notable sevens players. <a href="/wiki/Ben_Gollings" title="Ben Gollings">Ben Gollings</a> holds the record for points scored on the Sevens Series with 2,652 points. <a href="/wiki/Dan_Norton" title="Dan Norton">Dan Norton</a> holds the record for tries scored on the Sevens Series with 261 tries as of June 2017. England's <a href="/wiki/Simon_Amor" title="Simon Amor">Simon Amor</a> (2004) and <a href="/wiki/Ollie_Phillips_(rugby_union)" title="Ollie Phillips (rugby union)">Ollie Phillips</a> (2009) have each won a <a href="/wiki/World_Rugby_Sevens_Player_of_the_Year" title="World Rugby Sevens Player of the Year">World Rugby Sevens Player of the Year</a> award.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic_high-level_competitions">Domestic high-level competitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Domestic high-level competitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Premiership">Premiership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Premiership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Premiership_Rugby" title="Premiership Rugby">Premiership Rugby</a> is an English professional <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a> competition. The Premiership consists of twelve clubs, and is the top division of the <a href="/wiki/English_rugby_union_system" title="English rugby union system">English rugby union system</a>. Premiership clubs qualify for Europe's two main club competitions, the <a href="/wiki/European_Rugby_Champions_Cup" title="European Rugby Champions Cup">European Rugby Champions Cup</a> and the <a href="/wiki/European_Rugby_Challenge_Cup" title="European Rugby Challenge Cup">European Rugby Challenge Cup</a>. The team finishing at the bottom of the Premiership each season is relegated to the second-division <a href="/wiki/RFU_Championship" title="RFU Championship">RFU Championship</a>, and the winner of the Championship is promoted to the Premiership. The competition has been played since 1987, and has evolved into the current Premiership system. The current champions are Saracens. The most recently promoted side are the newly named Bristol Bears.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship">Championship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Championship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The RFU Championship is the second tier of the <a href="/wiki/English_rugby_union_system" title="English rugby union system">English rugby union league system</a> and was founded in September 1987. The league was previously known as National Division One and in 2009 changed from a league consisting of <a href="/wiki/Semi-professional" class="mw-redirect" title="Semi-professional">semi-professional</a> clubs to one that is now fully <a href="/wiki/Professional" title="Professional">professional</a>. The current champions are <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Bears" title="Bristol Bears">Bristol Bears</a> who won promotion to the <a href="/wiki/Premiership_Rugby" title="Premiership Rugby">English Premiership</a> after beating the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Carnegie" title="Yorkshire Carnegie">Yorkshire Carnegie</a> in a two-legged play-off, having finished first in the league during the regular season.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Premier_15s">Premier 15s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Premier 15s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Premier_15s" title="Premier 15s">Premier 15s</a> is the top tier of the women's English rugby union domestic league system run by the Rugby Football Union. The league was created mainly from teams in the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Premiership" title="Women&#39;s Premiership">Women's Premiership</a>. Its first season began on 16 September 2017. The current champions are Saracens, with the runners up being Harlequins.
</p>
