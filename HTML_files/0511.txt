<p><b>Regian Eersel</b> (born 16 December 1992) is a Surinamese-Dutch <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxer</a>. He is the current <a href="/wiki/ONE_Championship" title="ONE Championship">ONE Lightweight Kickboxing World Champion</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Regian_Eersel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Eersel was born in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, Suriname. He moved to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> with his family when he was four years old. At the age of 15, he started to do martial arts.
His interest for <a href="/wiki/K-1" title="K-1">K-1</a> started watching examples like <a href="/wiki/Remy_Bonjasky" title="Remy Bonjasky">Remy Bonjasky</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyrone_Spong" title="Tyrone Spong">Tyrone Spong</a> on television. He googled the most nearby gym in <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> and it so happened that he signed in for classes at Sityodtong Amsterdam. One year later he entered the ring for the first time. He won.
His nickname, The Immortal, was given to Eersel in this early amateur era. His teammates gave it to him, as he never used the break time between the rounds to sit and regain breath.
As it was not easy to find competitor in Holland after becoming a professional athlete in his weightclass, he tried his luck abroad. In Baku, Azerbeidzjan, Eersel took part of a K1-tournament, where he qualified for the finals. He did not win the finals, but the hunger for more had started. 
After this Eersel was invited for competitions in his country of birth Surinam, Russia (W5), Greece, Germany, Turkey (Mix Fight Gala), the US (Legacy and Lion Fight) and China (Wu Lin Feng, Glory of Heroes).
</p><p>On May 17, 2019, he won the <a href="/wiki/ONE_Championship" title="ONE Championship">ONE Lightweight Kickboxing World Championship</a> by beating <a href="/wiki/Nieky_Holzken" title="Nieky Holzken">Nieky Holzken</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
