<p><a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a> is the second-largest religion in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. According to the 2012 census of Suriname, Hindus constitute 22.3% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Suriname has the second largest percentage of Hindus in the Western Hemisphere, after <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> (24.8%).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hinduism_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:KITLV_-_33936_-_Hindu_woman,_festively_dressed,_in_Surinam_-_circa_1900.tif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/KITLV_-_33936_-_Hindu_woman%2C_festively_dressed%2C_in_Surinam_-_circa_1900.tif/lossy-page1-220px-KITLV_-_33936_-_Hindu_woman%2C_festively_dressed%2C_in_Surinam_-_circa_1900.tif.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="290" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/KITLV_-_33936_-_Hindu_woman%2C_festively_dressed%2C_in_Surinam_-_circa_1900.tif/lossy-page1-330px-KITLV_-_33936_-_Hindu_woman%2C_festively_dressed%2C_in_Surinam_-_circa_1900.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/KITLV_-_33936_-_Hindu_woman%2C_festively_dressed%2C_in_Surinam_-_circa_1900.tif/lossy-page1-440px-KITLV_-_33936_-_Hindu_woman%2C_festively_dressed%2C_in_Surinam_-_circa_1900.tif.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1955" data-file-height="2579" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:KITLV_-_33936_-_Hindu_woman,_festively_dressed,_in_Surinam_-_circa_1900.tif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hindu woman festively dressed in Suriname in 1900</div></div></div>
<p>The story of Hindus in Suriname is broadly parallel to that in <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>. <a href="/wiki/Indian_indenture_system" title="Indian indenture system">Indian indentured labourers</a> were sent to colonial <a href="/wiki/Dutch_colonisation_of_the_Guianas" title="Dutch colonisation of the Guianas">Dutch Guiana</a> by special arrangement between the Dutch and British.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The difference is that the Netherlands' more liberal policy toward Hinduism allowed a stronger culture to develop. Examples are the lack of a rigid caste system and the almost universal reading of the <a href="/wiki/Gita" class="mw-redirect" title="Gita">Gita</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ramayana" title="Ramayana">Ramayana</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Demographics">Demographics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hinduism_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Demographics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the 2012 census,there are 120,623 Hindus in Suriname,constituting 22.3% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The percentage of Hindus increased initially (1900 – 1930s), fluctuated slightly between the 1930s and 1980s, and remained stable in the 20s (20%).The demographic changes in the religious population in the first half of the 20th century can be explained by migration. In the second half of the 20th century, in particular after 1970s the decline of Hindus might be explained by large migration to the Netherlands during the independence (1975) and the military regime in the 1980-1987.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hindu_denominations">Hindu denominations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hinduism_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hindu denominations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Arya_Samaj_in_Suriname" title="Arya Samaj in Suriname">Arya Samaj in Suriname</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hindoe_temple_in_Nickerie_(31125297781).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Hindoe_temple_in_Nickerie_%2831125297781%29.jpg/220px-Hindoe_temple_in_Nickerie_%2831125297781%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Hindoe_temple_in_Nickerie_%2831125297781%29.jpg/330px-Hindoe_temple_in_Nickerie_%2831125297781%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Hindoe_temple_in_Nickerie_%2831125297781%29.jpg/440px-Hindoe_temple_in_Nickerie_%2831125297781%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4734" data-file-height="2661" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hindoe_temple_in_Nickerie_(31125297781).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hindu temple in <a href="/wiki/Nickerie_District" title="Nickerie District">Nickerie</a></div></div></div>
<p>According to the 2012 census, 18% of Surinamese are <a href="/wiki/Sanatani" class="mw-redirect" title="Sanatani">Sanatani</a> Hindus, 3.1% are <a href="/wiki/Arya_Samaj" title="Arya Samaj">Arya Samaj</a>, and the remaining 1.2% followed other forms of Hinduism.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/ISKCON" class="mw-redirect" title="ISKCON">ISKCON</a> also have a presence in Suriname.The first Hare Krishna devotees to visit Suriname were devotees from Guyana way back in the early 1980’s. The first Center was established about two decades ago, and now there is a vibrant preaching center in the country’s second city, New Nickerie. <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Majority of the Hindus in Suriname are <a href="/wiki/East_Indians" title="East Indians">East Indians</a> both in absolute terms and in percentage.Hinduism has a considerable following among Mixed ethnic people(3210 people) and <a href="/wiki/Javanese_Surinamese" title="Javanese Surinamese">Javanese Surinamese</a>(915 people).Hinduism is also practised among <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Surinamese" title="Chinese Surinamese">Chinese Surinamese</a>(157 people),Creole (142 people),<a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">Maroon</a>(84 people),<a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_in_Suriname" title="Indigenous peoples in Suriname">Indigenous people</a>(83 people) and <a href="/wiki/Afro-Surinamese" title="Afro-Surinamese">Afro-Surinamese</a>(59 people)<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Language">Language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hinduism_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In contrast to the neighbouring Guyanese Hindus who speak English,most of the Surinamese Hindus speak <a href="/wiki/Sarnami_Hindustani" class="mw-redirect" title="Sarnami Hindustani">Sarnami Hindustani</a>, a dialect of <a href="/wiki/Bhojpuri" class="mw-redirect" title="Bhojpuri">Bhojpuri</a>.This is largely due to the fact that the Dutch did not force the Indo-Caribbean population to abandon their native languages, unlike in British colonies like <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">Trinidad</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>, where English was imposed as a means of attempting to erase cultural and religious traditions.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Festivals">Festivals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hinduism_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Festivals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Deepavali" class="mw-redirect" title="Deepavali">Deepavali</a> and <a href="/wiki/Holi" title="Holi">Holi</a> are national holidays in Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
