<p>The <b>British Computer Society</b> (<b>BCS</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Professional_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Professional body">professional body</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Learned_society" title="Learned society">learned society</a> that represents those working in <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a> (IT) and <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a>, both in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and internationally. Founded in 1956, BCS has played an important role in educating and nurturing IT professionals, computer scientists, computer engineers, upholding the profession, accrediting chartered IT professional status, and creating a global community active in promoting and furthering the field and practice of computing.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Computer_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With a worldwide membership of over 68,000 members in over 150 countries, BCS is a registered <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organization" title="Charitable organization">charity</a> and was incorporated by <a href="/wiki/Royal_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Charter">Royal Charter</a> in 1984. Its objectives are to promote the study and application of communications technology and computing technology and to advance knowledge of education in ICT for the benefit of professional practitioners and the general public.
</p><p>BCS is a member institution of <a href="/wiki/Engineering_Council" title="Engineering Council">Engineering Council</a>, through which it is licensed to award the designation of <a href="/wiki/Incorporated_Engineer" class="mw-redirect" title="Incorporated Engineer">Incorporated Engineer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Engineer" class="mw-redirect" title="Chartered Engineer">Chartered Engineer</a> and therefore is responsible for regulation of ICT and computer science fields within the UK. The BCS is also a member of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=CEPIS&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="CEPIS (page does not exist)">Council of European Professional Informatics Societies</a> (CEPIS) and the <a href="/wiki/Seoul_Accord" title="Seoul Accord">Seoul Accord</a> for international tertiary degree recognition. BCS was previously a member organisation of the  <a href="/wiki/Science_Council" title="Science Council">Science Council</a> through which it was licensed to award the designation of <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Scientist" title="Chartered Scientist">Chartered Scientist</a>. 
</p><p>BCS has offices off the <a href="/wiki/Strand,_London" title="Strand, London">Strand</a> in <a href="/wiki/Southampton_Street,_London" title="Southampton Street, London">Southampton Street</a>, south of <a href="/wiki/Covent_Garden" title="Covent Garden">Covent Garden</a> in central <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. The main administrative offices are in <a href="/wiki/Swindon" title="Swindon">Swindon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiltshire" title="Wiltshire">Wiltshire</a>, west of London. It also has two overseas offices in <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>.
</p><p>Members are sent the quarterly IT professional magazine <i><a href="/wiki/ITNOW" title="ITNOW">ITNOW</a></i> (formerly <i>The Computer Bulletin</i>).
</p><p>BCS is a member organization of the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Enterprise_Architecture_Professional_Organizations" title="Federation of Enterprise Architecture Professional Organizations">Federation of Enterprise Architecture Professional Organizations</a> (FEAPO), a worldwide association of professional organizations which have come together to provide a forum to standardize, professionalize, and otherwise advance the discipline of <a href="/wiki/Enterprise_Architecture" class="mw-redirect" title="Enterprise Architecture">Enterprise Architecture</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Computer_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The forerunner of BCS was the "London Computer Group" (LCG), founded in 1956. BCS was formed a year later from the merger of the LCG and an unincorporated association of scientists into an unincorporated club. In October 1957, BCS was incorporated, by <a href="/wiki/Articles_of_Association_(law)" class="mw-redirect" title="Articles of Association (law)">Articles of Association</a>, as "The British Computer Society Ltd": the first President of BCS was Sir <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Wilkes" title="Maurice Wilkes">Maurice Wilkes</a> (1913–2010), <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Fellow of the Royal Society">FRS</a>.
</p><p>In 1966, the BCS was granted charitable status and in 1970, the BCS was given <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">Armorial Bearings</a> including the shield and crest.
The major ethical responsibilities of BCS are emphasized by the leopard's face, surmounting the whole crest and depicting eternal vigilance over the integrity of the Society and its members.
</p><p>The BCS patron is <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward,_Duke_of_Kent" title="Prince Edward, Duke of Kent">The Duke of Kent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Garter" title="Order of the Garter">KG</a>. He became patron in December 1976 and has been actively involved in BCS activities, particularly having been President in the <a href="/wiki/Silver_Jubilee" class="mw-redirect" title="Silver Jubilee">Silver Jubilee</a> Year in 1982–1983.
</p><p>In 2007, BCS launched <i>BCSrecruit.com</i> — a job site specifically aimed at IT professionals.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2008 the BCS was labelled "irrelevant" by an IT training company, in connection with claims it made that nine out of ten IT professionals were "unaware" of the BCS's Chartered accreditation scheme.<sup id="cite_ref-v3_williams_2008_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-v3_williams_2008-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 21 September 2009, the British Computer Society went through a transformation and re-branded itself as "BCS — The Chartered Institute for IT".<sup id="cite_ref-rebrand_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rebrand-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, an <a href="/wiki/Extraordinary_General_Meeting" class="mw-redirect" title="Extraordinary General Meeting">Extraordinary General Meeting</a> was called to discuss the direction of the BCS.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The debate has been covered by the computing press.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Computer_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>BCS is governed by a <a href="/wiki/Trustee" title="Trustee">Trustee</a> Board comprising the President, the Deputy President, the immediate past President, up to nine Vice Presidents (including Vice-President Finance), and five Professional Members elected by the advisory Council.<sup id="cite_ref-Boards_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boards-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Sir <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Wilkes" title="Maurice Wilkes">Maurice Wilkes</a>, Professor of Computer Science at Cambridge University, served as its first president. Each president serves for a 2-year term. A <a href="/wiki/List_of_presidents_of_the_British_Computer_Society" title="List of presidents of the British Computer Society">list of presidents of the British Computer Society</a> can be found at BCS web site <sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>. 
</p><p>The BCS advisory Council elects the Honorary Officers — the President, the Deputy President and up to nine Vice-Presidents, together with the immediate past President and five members of Council.<sup id="cite_ref-Council_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Council-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  Lists of Trust Board and Advisory Council members are maintained online.<sup id="cite_ref-Boards_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boards-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The advisory Council provides advice to the Trustee Board on the direction and operation of BCS; in particular it is consulted on strategic plans and the annual budget. The Council is a representative body of the membership, with members elected directly by the professional membership, and by the Branches, Groups and Forums.<sup id="cite_ref-Council_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Council-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Fellow_British_Computer_Society_.28FBCS.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Fellow_British_Computer_Society_(FBCS)">Fellow British Computer Society (FBCS)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Computer_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Fellow British Computer Society (FBCS)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>Fellow of BCS (FBCS)</b><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> title is conferred to individuals to recognize their outstanding achievements and contributions to Information Technology. Fellows are expected to give something back to the profession, by promoting and evangelizing the profession to the
public and society, and contributing to debates in conferences, panels, meetings, etc. 
Fellows are nominated to the society each year and have to be supported by one or more existing fellows. Criteria<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> for election to fellow include:
</p>
<ul><li>Demonstrate leadership in the profession</li>
<li>Wide acknowledgement of specific IT expertise</li>
<li>Contribution to advancement of knowledge</li>
<li>Eminent individual</li>
<li>Authority and seniority, including leading major projects and managing teams</li></ul>
<p><a href="/wiki/Category:Fellows_of_the_British_Computer_Society" title="Category:Fellows of the British Computer Society">Current fellows</a><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
include distinguished individuals from industries and universities. Some of the prominent fellows include:
</p>
<ul><li>Dame <a href="/wiki/Wendy_Hall" title="Wendy Hall">Wendy Hall</a>, FBCS - ex-President of BCS</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Andy_Harter" title="Andy Harter">Andy Harter</a>, FBCS - CEO of RealVNC</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tony_Hey" title="Tony Hey">Tony Hey</a>, FBCS - ex-VP of Microsoft Research</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hermann_Hauser" title="Hermann Hauser">Hermann Hauser</a>, Distinguished FBCS - founder of ARM Ltd.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chartered_IT_Professional">Chartered IT Professional</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Computer_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Chartered IT Professional">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Chartered_IT_Professional" title="Chartered IT Professional">Chartered IT Professional</a></div>
<p>The BCS is the only professional body in the United Kingdom with the ability to grant chartered status to IT professionals under its <a href="/wiki/Royal_Charter" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Charter">Royal Charter</a>, granted to them by the <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">Privy Council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Thus having the ability to grant <a href="/wiki/Chartered_(Professional)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chartered (Professional)">Chartered (Professional)</a> status to both its <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">Fellows</a> and Professional members. Known as <b>Chartered IT Professional</b>, they are entitled to use the <a href="/wiki/Post-nominal_letters" title="Post-nominal letters">suffix</a> <b>CITP</b>. The BCS keeps a register of current Chartered Members and Fellows.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other Professional membership bodies apply to the BCS for a licence that enables them to award CITP to their eligible members. The <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Engineering_and_Technology_(professional_society)" class="mw-redirect" title="Institution of Engineering and Technology (professional society)">Institution of Engineering and Technology</a> (IET) is the first such membership body licensed to award CITP.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grades_of_Membership">Grades of Membership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Computer_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Grades of Membership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>BCS has different grades of membership:
</p>
<dl><dt>Honorary grades</dt></dl>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/DFBCS" class="mw-redirect" title="DFBCS">Distinguished Fellow</a> (Only 24 awards since 1971<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>)</li>
<li>Honorary Fellowship (Hon FBCS)<sup id="cite_ref-Honorary_Fellowship_of_BCS_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Honorary_Fellowship_of_BCS-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> (Only 104 awarded to date)</li></ul>
<dl><dt>Professional grades</dt></dl>
<ul><li>Fellow (<a href="/wiki/FBCS" class="mw-redirect" title="FBCS">FBCS</a>)</li>
<li>Member (MBCS)</li></ul>
<dl><dt>Ordinary grades</dt></dl>
<ul><li>Associate Member (AMBCS)</li>
<li>Student Member</li></ul>
<dl><dt>Group, corporate and other membership categories</dt></dl>
<ul><li>Affiliate: for those with an interest in IT but not yet employed in an IT role.</li>
<li>Group membership: nearly 200 organisations now encourage their IT professionals to join the Society through its Group Membership Scheme.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Education affiliates: education intuitions can also be accredited by BCS.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<dl><dt>Other Chartered designations</dt></dl>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Engineering_Council_UK" class="mw-redirect" title="Engineering Council UK">Engineering Council UK</a> has licensed the BCS to award <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Engineer_(UK)" title="Chartered Engineer (UK)">Chartered Engineer</a> status (<i>CEng</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Incorporated_Engineer" class="mw-redirect" title="Incorporated Engineer">Incorporated Engineer</a> status (<i>IEng</i>).</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Science_Council" title="Science Council">Science Council</a> formerly licensed the BCS to award <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Scientist" title="Chartered Scientist">Chartered Scientist</a> status (<i>CSci</i>). However, the BCS no longer offers <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Scientist" title="Chartered Scientist">Chartered Scientist</a> status (<i>CSci</i>)</li>
<li>Members may also apply through BCS to the <a href="/wiki/European_Federation_of_National_Engineering_Associations" title="European Federation of National Engineering Associations">European Federation of National Engineering Associations</a> (FEANI) for <a href="/wiki/European_Engineer" title="European Engineer">European Engineer</a> (<i>Eur Ing</i>) status.</li></ul>
<dl><dt>Designatory (post-nominal) letters</dt></dl>
<p>Members are encouraged to display the designatory letters to which they are entitled whenever appropriate. The order of designatory (post-nominal) letters is complex and open to a certain amount of interpretation. The accepted authority on this subject is <i>Debrett’s Correct Form</i>. Normally these should appear after decorations, degrees and chartered letters. Members holding CEng should also display the designatory letters of the institution through which they are registered immediately after the CEng. Conventionally, members holding Chartered status (<a href="/wiki/Chartered_IT_Professional" title="Chartered IT Professional">CITP</a>) display this immediately after their membership letters (e.g., FBCS CITP or MBCS CITP). However, as CITP may now be awarded by other organisations it may also be displayed separately, following that of the awarding institution.
</p><p>Some examples of BCS-related post-nominals:
</p>
<ul><li>Mr Frank James <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">MBE</a>, FBCS.</li>
<li>Mr Frank James MBE, <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">MSc</a>, <a href="/wiki/CEng" class="mw-redirect" title="CEng">CEng</a>, MBCS, MIET.</li>
<li>Mr Frank James MBE, <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">BSc</a> (<a href="/wiki/Honours_degree" title="Honours degree">Hons</a>), MBCS, CITP.</li>
<li>Mr Frank James MBE, MSc, <a href="/wiki/Chartered_Scientist" title="Chartered Scientist">CSci</a>, MIET, CITP.</li>
<li>Mr Frank James MBE, <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Science" title="Master of Science">MSc</a>, MCGI, <a href="/wiki/CEng" class="mw-redirect" title="CEng">CEng</a>, MBCS, FEDIPAdvPra.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifications">Qualifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Computer_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Qualifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>BCS provides a range of qualifications both for users of computers and <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">IT</a> professionals.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="BCS_IT_User_Qualifications">BCS IT User Qualifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Computer_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: BCS IT User Qualifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>BCS offers qualifications that cover all areas of IT, including understanding Spreadsheets and Presentation Software, Animation, Video Editing and Social Networking safety.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The current IT user qualifications are:
</p>
<div class="floatright"><a href="/wiki/File:ECDL_ft_rgb2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="ECDL ft rgb2.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c9/ECDL_ft_rgb2.jpg/200px-ECDL_ft_rgb2.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="90" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/c/c9/ECDL_ft_rgb2.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="261" data-file-height="118" /></a></div>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/European_Computer_Driving_Licence" title="European Computer Driving Licence">European Computer Driving Licence</a> (ECDL) - BCS is the only organisation licensed to offer ECDL qualifications in the UK.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/European_Computer_Driving_Licence#ECDL_Advanced" title="European Computer Driving Licence">Advanced ECDL</a> - the advanced course of ECDL ("Advanced ECDL") has four sections, each a qualification in its own right. Upon achieving all four advanced qualifications, the individual will receive a qualification as an "ECDL Expert" — in the UK, this confers upon the person Associate Membership of The British Computer Society, should that person wish to sign up to a code of conduct and join BCS.</li></ul>
<h3><span id="BCS_Higher_Education_Qualifications_.28HEQs.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="BCS_Higher_Education_Qualifications_(HEQs)">BCS Higher Education Qualifications (HEQs)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Computer_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: BCS Higher Education Qualifications (HEQs)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>BCS conducts its own BCS Higher Education Qualifications<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> in many countries. It was formerly known as BCS Professional Examinations which consisted of Parts 1 and 2 of which passing of Part 2 with the professional project was equivalent to a British honours degree. These programs had an early history of success, with participants coming from all parts of the world, including Asia. Many private computing schools outside the UK have hosted students in preparation for BCS Part 1 and 2 examinations. The level of current qualifications are:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Academic_certificate" title="Academic certificate">Certificate</a> in <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">IT</a> (equivalent to the first year of an honours university degree)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Diploma" title="Diploma">Diploma</a> in <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">IT</a> (equivalent to the second year of an honours university degree)</li>
<li>Professional Graduate Diploma in IT (equivalent to a <a href="/wiki/Honours_graduate" class="mw-redirect" title="Honours graduate">British honours university degree</a>)</li></ul>
<dl><dt>e-type</dt></dl>
<p>e-type is a qualification that allows individuals to improve and certify their typing skills. The average user can save up to 21 days a year by improving their typing speed as well as preventing repetitive strain injury (RSI). e-type comes with full support materials and computer-based courseware before allowing the user to assess their skills using a simple online test.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dt>Digital Creator</dt></dl>
<p>Digital Creator is a set of engaging qualifications that teach digital media skills through creative projects. They are designed for all types and ages of learners - in schools from Key Stage 2 to Key Stage 4 and in all areas of adult learning.
</p>
<dl><dt>ITQ - The Flexible IT qualification</dt></dl>
<p>The BCS ITQ is a range of IT user qualifications made up of a combination of units available on the ITQ framework.
</p><p>The framework consists of a wide range of units covering all aspects of IT user application including word processing, spreadsheets, the internet, multimedia software and design software.
</p>
<dl><dt></dt></dl>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_certifications">Other certifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Computer_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Other certifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl><dt><a href="/wiki/ISEB" class="mw-redirect" title="ISEB">ISEB</a></dt></dl>
<p>BCS also offers professional qualifications via its Professional Certifications board, formerly known as ISEB (<a href="/wiki/Information_Systems_Examination_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="Information Systems Examination Board">Information Systems Examination Board</a>).
</p><p>Professional Certifications (ISEB) provides a wide range of qualifications for IT professionals covering major areas including Management, Development, Service Delivery and Quality.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dt><big>Informatics Professional</big></dt></dl>
<p>BCS via FEDIP<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
provides 4 different professional registration levels for health and care informatics professionals:
Practitioner, Senior Practitioner, Advanced Practitioner, Leading Practitioner. 
</p><p>FEDIPAdvPra - post-nominals for Advanced Practitioner.
</p><p>FEDIP is the Federation for Informatics Professionals in Health and Social Care, a collaboration between the leading professional bodies in health and care informatics supporting the development of the informatics profession.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Retired_qualifications">Retired qualifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=British_Computer_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Retired qualifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl><dt>e-Citizen</dt></dl>
<p>The e-Citizen qualification allows beginners to get online and start using the Internet. The qualification has been designed to provide a basic understanding of the Internet and to start using the web safely, from reading email to shopping online.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<dl><dt>MoR (Management of Risk)</dt></dl>
<p>M_o_R Foundation is suitable for any organization or individual seeing the need for guidance on a controlled approach to identification, assessment and control risk at strategic, programme, project and operational perspectives.
</p>
