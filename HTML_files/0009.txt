<p><b>Fellowship of the British Academy</b> (<b>FBA</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Award">award</a> granted by the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy" title="British Academy">British Academy</a> to leading academics for their distinction<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> in the humanities and social sciences.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> There are three kinds of fellowship:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ol><li>Fellows, for scholars resident in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a></li>
<li>Corresponding Fellows, for scholars not resident in the UK</li>
<li>Honorary Fellows, an <a href="/wiki/Honorary_title_(academic)" title="Honorary title (academic)">honorary academic title</a></li></ol>
<p>The award of fellowship is evidenced by published work and fellows may use the <a href="/wiki/Post-nominal_letters" title="Post-nominal letters">post-nominal letters</a> <i>FBA</i>. Examples of fellows include <a href="/wiki/Mary_Beard_(classicist)" title="Mary Beard (classicist)">Mary Beard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Stern,_Baron_Stern_of_Brentford" title="Nicholas Stern, Baron Stern of Brentford">Nicholas Stern, Baron Stern of Brentford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Horder" title="Jeremy Horder">Jeremy Horder</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Lobban" title="Michael Lobban">Michael Lobban</a>, <a href="/wiki/M._R._James" title="M. R. James">M.&#160;R. James</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rowan_Williams" title="Rowan Williams">Rowan Williams</a>.
</p>
