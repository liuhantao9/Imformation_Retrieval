<p><b>Greenhead College</b> is a former <a href="/wiki/Grammar_school" title="Grammar school">grammar school</a> and current <a href="/wiki/Sixth_form_college" title="Sixth form college">sixth form college</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, in the county of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. The current principal is Simon Lett. With over 2300 students, it is a large sixth form college, attracting students from as far afield as <a href="/wiki/Wakefield" title="Wakefield">Wakefield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wetherby" title="Wetherby">Wetherby</a> and even <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>. It is located next to <a href="/wiki/Greenhead_Park" title="Greenhead Park">Greenhead Park</a> which is one of the largest parks in Huddersfield.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reputation">Reputation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhead_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reputation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The college topped both <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent</a>'</i>s <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Level_(UK)" class="mw-redirect" title="Advanced Level (UK)">A level</a> college league tables in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It came fourth in 2007<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (June 2012)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and sixth in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). (June 2012)">failed verification</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> It was also the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sunday_Times" title="The Sunday Times">Sunday Times</a></i>'s Sixth Form College of the Year, 2014-15.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
2019 will see 45 students heading to Oxford or Cambridge University<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subject_choices">Subject choices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhead_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subject choices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Students usually study 3 subjects at A level. The college is quite flexible with respect to courses being studied, with a proportion of students studying 4 A Levels as long as they meet the required criteria (an average of an A grade at GCSE).  There are over 30 different subjects for students to study.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Admissions">Admissions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhead_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Admissions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Students from partnership feeder schools are given priority for places at the college, and are required to obtain a minimum of GCSE grades 4 in Maths and English language in addition to three GCSE grade 6s to gain a place at the college. Students usually need to get a B grade in the subjects they are wanting to take.<sup id="cite_ref-entry_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entry-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Current partner schools are <a href="/wiki/All_Saints_Catholic_College,_Huddersfield" title="All Saints Catholic College, Huddersfield">All Saints Catholic High School</a>, Newsome High School, <a href="/wiki/Almondbury_High_School_and_Language_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Almondbury High School and Language College">Almondbury High School and Language College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colne_Valley_High_School" title="Colne Valley High School">Colne Valley High School</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Huddersfield_Trust_School" title="North Huddersfield Trust School">North Huddersfield Trust School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth_High_School" title="Holmfirth High School">Holmfirth High School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honley_High_School" title="Honley High School">Honley High School</a>, <a href="/wiki/King_James%27s_Grammar_School,_Almondbury" class="mw-redirect" title="King James&#39;s Grammar School, Almondbury">King James's School</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moor_End_Technology_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Moor End Technology College">Moor End Technology College</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherhall_Learning_Campus" title="Netherhall Learning Campus">Netherhall Learning Campus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Royds_Hall_School" title="Royds Hall School">Royds Hall School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Salendine_Nook_High_School" title="Salendine Nook High School">Salendine Nook High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Students from other schools are considered based on their mock GCSE results, a one-to-one interview and the availability of subject places, this after partner school students have been taken into consideration. Students from the <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a> secondary schools, including <a href="/wiki/The_Brooksbank_School" title="The Brooksbank School">The Brooksbank School</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Crossley_Heath_School" title="The Crossley Heath School">The Crossley Heath School</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Halifax_Grammar_School" title="North Halifax Grammar School">North Halifax Grammar School</a>, can be accepted through the application process. In 2005, then-principal Martin Rostron said he believed the college has been criticised for selecting only the best students, which he denied, saying that Greenhead took those of all abilities.<sup id="cite_ref-entry_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entry-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campus">Campus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhead_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Campus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Greenhead_walk_on_walls.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Greenhead_walk_on_walls.jpg/220px-Greenhead_walk_on_walls.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Greenhead_walk_on_walls.jpg/330px-Greenhead_walk_on_walls.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Greenhead_walk_on_walls.jpg/440px-Greenhead_walk_on_walls.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="425" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Greenhead_walk_on_walls.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The wall art of the science block at Greenhead College</div></div></div>
<p>The Greenhead College campus is located on one site, near the centre of Huddersfield, directly next to Greenhead park. The campus is fairly small in comparison with the number of students enrolled - however, it manages to effectively provide provision for a multitude of subjects taught.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The college has approximately 8 main buildings, all of which are internally linked. Each building represents a subject or a group of similar subjects. On 26 May 2004, the college officially opened a new building on the campus, the Conway Building. The building was dedicated to the former principal, Dr Kevin Conway.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The college has a small football field on site and a small hockey field. Two tennis courts were replaced in early 2012 to make way for a new maths and science building, opened by the Duke of Kent. Many enrichments<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> (extra curricular activities) offered by the college are done off campus due to the lack of outdoor space owing to the proximity to <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> town centre.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The infamous statue situated on the side of the Science Building, in the adjacent picture, has recently changed location to outside the newly constructed Rostrom Building. College legend has it that on the eve of results day 2015 the metal woman, also known as The Silent Student, moved on her own free will. Her rusty feet remain on the side of the Science Building; she has since regenerated new feet. By 2025 she is expected to move once again.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Redevelopment">Redevelopment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhead_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Redevelopment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2008 Ryder Architecture were commissioned to draw up plan for a new project with would provide a series of new buildings which were linked to the existing buildings via a central atrium hub. These buildings provided educational classrooms as well as various support and ancillary accommodation. The existing site was constrained with several existing buildings which would have caused a complex phased construction process. It was estimated that the project would cost approximately £15 million.
</p><p>In December 2008 a report examining the feasibility of the project was approved by the College. An application was submitted to the <a href="/wiki/Learning_and_Skills_Council" title="Learning and Skills Council">Learning and Skills Council</a> in July 2009 with construction works commencing January 2010 for a duration of 2 years. Unfortunately LSC funding was not received and the project was placed on hold until alternative funding could be sourced.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Staff">Staff</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhead_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Staff">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Extracurricular_activities">Extracurricular activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greenhead_College&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Extracurricular activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Greenhead is well praised for its enrichment programmes which encourages pupils to partake in extracurricular activities designed to build students character, their personal, sporting and social interests and prepares them more effectively both for higher education and future employment. It was first introduced in 1990. Its most recent praise was from Ofsted in 2011 from their spot check report.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Enrichment is mandatory for student to undertake, this encourages pupils to partake supporting the programs success. There are a huge variety of courses for students to choose form including <a href="/wiki/The_Duke_of_Edinburgh%27s_Award" title="The Duke of Edinburgh&#39;s Award">The Duke of Edinburgh's Award</a>, sports, music and drama, information technology, voluntary services and <a href="/wiki/The_World_Challenge" title="The World Challenge">The World Challenge</a>; additionally students are free to start new courses on the proviso they can gain enough support for them. In 2011 a total of 79 different courses were available to students including 22 different sporting activates such as badminton, basketball, cycling, football (men and women’s), rugby (men and women’s), judo and squash and 9 different music groups.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Taster sessions are provided so that students can make informed decisions.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As part of the enrichment program, pupils must undertake one week of PaWS (Projects and Work Shadowing) in their first year. Work shadowing placements include medical, political, scientific and language based jobs, usually at a senior level within the respective companies. If a pupil does not wish to do work shadowing, many projects, from circus skills to mathematical art, are available.
</p>
