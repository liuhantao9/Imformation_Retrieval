<p>The <a href="/wiki/Badminton" title="Badminton">badminton</a> tournaments at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Olympic Games</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> took place between 28 July and 5 August at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Arena" title="Wembley Arena">Wembley Arena</a>.
</p><p>A total of 172 athletes competed in five events: <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_singles" title="Badminton at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men&#39;s singles">men's singles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_singles" title="Badminton at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women&#39;s singles">women's singles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_doubles" title="Badminton at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men&#39;s doubles">men's doubles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_doubles" title="Badminton at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women&#39;s doubles">women's doubles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Mixed_doubles" title="Badminton at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Mixed doubles">mixed doubles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-International_Badminton_Federation_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-International_Badminton_Federation-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The women's doubles tournament was marred by several disqualifications during the group stage for unethical play.
</p><p>All of the gold medals were won by players from <a href="/wiki/China_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="China at the 2012 Summer Olympics">China</a>. The Chinese team also collected two silvers and one bronze, to top the medal table with eight in total. <a href="/wiki/Denmark_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Denmark at the 2012 Summer Olympics">Denmark</a> finished in second place, with one silver and one bronze won.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Competition_format">Competition format</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Badminton_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Competition format">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For the first time there was a combination of group play and knockout stages in the Olympic badminton tournament, following its introduction at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Youth_Summer_Olympics" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Youth Summer Olympics">2010 Youth Summer Olympics</a> in Singapore.
</p><p>All matches were the best of three games, with each game won by the first player to reach 21 points. If the score reached 20–20, the winner was the first player to lead by two points. If the score reached 29–29, the player who won the next point won the match.
</p><p>The draw for the groups was held on 23 July 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualification">Qualification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Badminton_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Qualification" title="Badminton at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Qualification">Badminton at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Qualification</a></div>
<p>The Olympic qualification period was between 2 May 2011 and 29 April 2012, and the <a href="/wiki/Badminton_World_Federation" title="Badminton World Federation">Badminton World Federation</a> rankings list, published on 3 May 2012, was used to allocate spots.<sup id="cite_ref-International_Badminton_Federation_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-International_Badminton_Federation-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Nations could enter a maximum of three players. Three quota places if three players are ranked four or above, two if two players are ranked 16 or above and otherwise one quota place until the quota contingent of 38 is filled.
</p><p>For each male player who qualifies in more than one discipline, an additional quota place in the men's singles becomes free. If no player from one continent can qualify, the best ranked player from this continent gets a quota place.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Women.27s_doubles_disqualifications"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Women's_doubles_disqualifications">Women's doubles disqualifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Badminton_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Women&#039;s doubles disqualifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Badminton_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Women%27s_doubles" title="Badminton at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women&#39;s doubles">Badminton at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Women's doubles</a></div>
<p>On 1 August 2012, four teams were ejected from the competition (<a href="/wiki/Wang_Xiaoli" title="Wang Xiaoli">Wang Xiaoli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yu_Yang_(badminton)" title="Yu Yang (badminton)">Yu Yang</a> of <a href="/wiki/China_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="China at the 2012 Summer Olympics">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jung_Kyung-eun" title="Jung Kyung-eun">Jung Kyung-eun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Ha-na" title="Kim Ha-na">Kim Ha-na</a> with <a href="/wiki/Ha_Jung-eun" title="Ha Jung-eun">Ha Jung-eun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kim_Min-jung_(badminton)" title="Kim Min-jung (badminton)">Kim Min-jung</a>, both playing for <a href="/wiki/South_Korea_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="South Korea at the 2012 Summer Olympics">South Korea</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Meiliana_Jauhari" title="Meiliana Jauhari">Meiliana Jauhari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greysia_Polii" title="Greysia Polii">Greysia Polii</a> of <a href="/wiki/Indonesia_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics" title="Indonesia at the 2012 Summer Olympics">Indonesia</a>) for "not using one's best efforts to win a match" and "conducting oneself in a manner that is clearly abusive or detrimental to the sport" following round-robin matches the previous evening, during which the teams were accused of trying to lose in order to manipulate the draw.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
