<p>The <b>Raid on Choiseul</b> was a small unit engagement that occurred from October 28 to November 3, 1943, during the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands_campaign" title="Solomon Islands campaign">Solomon Islands campaign</a>, and was launched to divert Japanese from the Allied <a href="/wiki/Landings_at_Cape_Torokina" title="Landings at Cape Torokina">landings at Cape Torokina</a>. During the raid, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marines" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Marines">United States Marines</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Paramarines" title="Paramarines">2nd Parachute Battalion</a>, led by Lt Col. <a href="/wiki/Victor_H._Krulak" title="Victor H. Krulak">Victor "Brute" Krulak</a>, landed on Japanese occupied <a href="/wiki/Choiseul_Island" title="Choiseul Island">Choiseul</a> in the northern <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a> and carried out raids on Japanese <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">army</a> and <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Navy" title="Imperial Japanese Navy">navy</a> forces over a 40 kilometer (25&#160;mi) area over the course of seven days.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Mission">The Mission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Raid_on_Choiseul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The Mission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The raid was meant to divert Japanese attention from the planned <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allied</a> landings on the west coast of <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bougainville" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Bougainville">Bougainville</a> at <a href="/wiki/Landings_at_Cape_Torokina" title="Landings at Cape Torokina">Cape Torokina</a>.  Instead, the Allies hoped that the raid would cause the Japanese to believe the landings would be on the east side of <a href="/wiki/Bougainville_Island" title="Bougainville Island">Bougainville</a>.
</p><p>During the course of the operation, Krulak's 650-man battalion, assisted by an Australian <a href="/wiki/Coastwatcher" class="mw-redirect" title="Coastwatcher">coastwatcher</a> and native Choiseul islanders, killed 143 Japanese troops, losing 14&#160;Marines (12 killed in action, two missing), in actions later described by Major General <a href="/wiki/Roy_Geiger" title="Roy Geiger">Roy Geiger</a> as, "a series of short right jabs designed to throw the enemy off balance and conceal the real power of the left hook to his midriff at Empress Augusta Bay."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 2, the raid was momentarily stalled when a Japanese ambush trapped between 40 and 50 marines. Three marines were severely wounded, one of them fatally. Ten of the marines were picked up and rescued by the <a href="/wiki/Motor_torpedo_boat" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor torpedo boat">motor torpedo boat</a> <i><a href="/wiki/PT-59" class="mw-redirect" title="PT-59">PT-59</a></i>, under the command of <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant" title="Lieutenant">Lieutenant</a> <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>, though fire from the <i>59</i> allowed the Navy time to rescue many other surviving marines using other PT's and Navy vessels.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The ultimate impact of the raid on the Japanese response to the Allied Bougainville landings is unclear,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> although following the raid the Japanese moved thousands of reinforcements to Choiseul.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
