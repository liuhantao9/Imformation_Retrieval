<p><b>Kolombangara</b> (sometimes spelled <i>Kulambangara</i>) is an island in the <a href="/wiki/New_Georgia_Islands" title="New Georgia Islands">New Georgia Islands</a> group of the nation state of <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a> in the southwestern Pacific Ocean. The name is from a local language, a rough translation of its meaning is "Water Lord" with approximately 80 rivers and streams running down its flanks.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kolombangara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Almost perfectly round in shape and about 30&#160;km (19 miles) across, the island is a <a href="/wiki/Stratovolcano" title="Stratovolcano">stratovolcano</a> that reaches an altitude of 1,770 metres (5,807&#160;ft) at <a href="/wiki/Mount_Veve" title="Mount Veve">Mount Veve</a>. The island forms part of the southern boundary of the <a href="/wiki/New_Georgia_Sound" title="New Georgia Sound">New Georgia Sound</a>; to the northwest the <a href="/wiki/Vella_Gulf" title="Vella Gulf">Vella Gulf</a> separates it from <a href="/wiki/Vella_Lavella" title="Vella Lavella">Vella Lavella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gizo,_Solomon_Islands" title="Gizo, Solomon Islands">Gizo</a>, while to the southeast <a href="/wiki/New_Georgia" title="New Georgia">New Georgia</a> lies across the <a href="/wiki/Kula_Gulf" title="Kula Gulf">Kula Gulf</a>.   West-southwest of Kolombangara is <a href="/wiki/Ghizo_Island" title="Ghizo Island">Ghizo Island</a>, upon which the Western provincial capital <a href="/wiki/Gizo,_Solomon_Islands" title="Gizo, Solomon Islands">Gizo</a> is located.
</p><p>Kolombangara is heavily forested, with few inhabitants.   Kolombangara has two notable settlements, Ringgi and Mongga, the former being the larger.  The most significant industry on the island at this time is logging, with the largest of these based at Poitete.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kolombangara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first recorded sighting of Kolombangara by western explorers was in 1568 by the Spanish explorer <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_de_Menda%C3%B1a_de_Neira" title="Álvaro de Mendaña de Neira">Álvaro de Mendaña de Neira</a>. On March 15, 1893 Kolombangara was declared part of the <a href="/wiki/British_Solomon_Islands" title="British Solomon Islands">British Solomon Islands</a> <a href="/wiki/Protectorate" title="Protectorate">protectorate</a>. The island was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Empire of Japan</a> in the early stages of the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_War" title="Pacific War">Pacific War</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kolombangara&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> the island and the waters around it were the scene of much fighting. The <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army" title="Imperial Japanese Army">Imperial Japanese Army</a> used an airstrip on some flat ground at <a href="/wiki/Vila,_Solomon_Islands" title="Vila, Solomon Islands">Vila</a> on the south shore of the island, and in May 1943 based several military units with over ten thousand troops garrisoned on the southeast side of the island under the command of Major General <a href="/wiki/Minoru_Sasaki" title="Minoru Sasaki">Minoru Sasaki</a>, in an attempt to establish a defense line through the Central Solomons. Naval battles nearby included the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kula_Gulf" title="Battle of Kula Gulf">Battle of Kula Gulf</a> and <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Kolombangara" title="Battle of Kolombangara">Battle of Kolombangara</a>.
</p><p>The most famous and bloody  battle was the mission to intercept the "<a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Express" title="Tokyo Express">Tokyo Express</a>" supply convoy which resulted in the ramming and explosion of U.S. torpedo boat <a href="/wiki/Motor_Torpedo_Boat_PT-109" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor Torpedo Boat PT-109">PT-109</a>, manned by <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> and his crew.  Australian <a href="/wiki/Coastwatchers" title="Coastwatchers">coastwatcher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sub_Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Sub Lieutenant">Sub Lieutenant</a> <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Reginald_Evans" title="Arthur Reginald Evans">Arthur Reginald Evans</a>, who manned a secret observation post at the top of the island's Mount Veve volcano, spotted the explosion. After decoding news that the explosion he had witnessed was probably from the lost <i>PT-109</i> he dispatched <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islanders</a> <a href="/wiki/Biuku_Gasa_and_Eroni_Kumana" title="Biuku Gasa and Eroni Kumana">Biuku Gasa and Eroni Kumana</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Dugout_canoe" title="Dugout canoe">dugout canoe</a> to look for possible survivors. Their efforts led to the subsequent discovery and rescue of John F. Kennedy and the surviving crew.
</p><p>After destroyers succeeded in sinking the supply ships three nights later and isolating the garrison of 12,400 there, US forces were able to "leapfrog" Kolombangara to land on <a href="/wiki/Vella_Lavella" title="Vella Lavella">Vella Lavella</a> to the west. The Japanese evacuated Kolombangara between September 23 and October 4, 1943.
</p><p>In January 1944 a detachment of 1 officer and 6 enlisted men from the 350th Engineer General Service Regiment stationed at Munda, established a vegetable farm on the abandoned Japanese airstrip at Vila. The British government furnished 16 male natives to help with the project. With seeds acquired through the <a href="/wiki/International_Red_Cross" class="mw-redirect" title="International Red Cross">International Red Cross</a>, many vegetables were sent back to the base hospital to supplement the dehydrated meals served the recuperating veterans. The main item was <a href="/wiki/Watermelon" title="Watermelon">watermelons</a>.
</p><p>Since 1978, the island has been part of the independent state of the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a>.
</p>
