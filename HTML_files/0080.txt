<p><b>See You Again</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Power_ballad" class="mw-redirect" title="Power ballad">power ballad</a> song released by American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> recording artist <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>. It was written by Underwood, <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/David_Hodges" title="David Hodges">David Hodges</a>. It was released as the fourth and final single from Underwood's fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(album)" title="Blown Away (album)">Blown Away</a></i>, on April 15, 2013. Underwood announced the single on her official Twitter account.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Underwood debuted the song on the <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_12)" title="American Idol (season 12)">twelfth season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> on April 4, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-countrymusicislove1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-countrymusicislove1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> "See You Again" is Underwood's eighteenth consecutive Top 10 single on the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart and is also her seventeenth single to be certified at least Gold by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a>. As of December 2013, the single has sold 740,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-Ask_Billboard_2013_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ask_Billboard_2013-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On August 10, 2015, "See You Again" was officially certified as <a href="/wiki/Platinum_certification" class="mw-redirect" title="Platinum certification">Platinum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-riaa.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riaa.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_You_Again_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"See You Again" tells the story of moving onwards after experiencing the death of a loved one in life. The lyrics provide hope and promise through Underwood's belief that one will reunite with every fallen loved one in the afterlife. The song's opening consists of a solo piano, followed by a backing chorus and band kicking in shortly after.
</p><p>"See You Again" was originally one of three songs written for the 2010 feature film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Chronicles_of_Narnia:_The_Voyage_of_the_Dawn_Treader" title="The Chronicles of Narnia: The Voyage of the Dawn Treader">The Chronicles of Narnia: The Voyage of the Dawn Treader</a></i>. Of the three songs that Underwood, Hodges, and Lindsey co-wrote for the film, the workers behind the project opted to choose "<a href="/wiki/There%27s_a_Place_for_Us_(song)" title="There&#39;s a Place for Us (song)">There's a Place For Us</a>." Underwood pushed to include "See You Again" on her fourth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(album)" title="Blown Away (album)">Blown Away</a></i>, during the writing process of the album.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">Composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_You_Again_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>See You Again is written in the key of <a href="/wiki/A-flat_major" title="A-flat major">A-flat major</a>, with a vocal range from F3 to D<span class="music-symbol" style="font-family: Arial Unicode MS, Lucida Sans Unicode;"><span class="music-flat">&#x266d;</span></span>5.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_You_Again_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song has received positive reviews from music critics. Billboard.com described the song to be "truly a taste of heaven."<sup id="cite_ref-countrymusicislove1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-countrymusicislove1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Kevin John Coyne of Country Universe included the song in his personal favorite tracks from the <i>Blown Away</i> studio album in the website's 2012 year-end countdown of editors' favorite Country albums of the year,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> as did Matt Bjorke of Roughstock for their album countdown.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Liv Carter of Urban Country News gave the song a "thumbs up", adding "what pulls it over the line however, and presumably why it has become a fan-favorite, is its sincerity in wishing to bring comfort and Underwood’s confident performance."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Jonathan Keefe of <i><a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant Magazine</a></i> was less favorable, saying that "the chorus of power ballad "See You Again" is marred by dated, campy arena-rock clichés."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_You_Again_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song debuted at No. 60 on the US <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart for the week of April 13, 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and peaked at No. 2 for the chart dated September 7, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  It entered the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> at No. 90 for the chart dated May 18, 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and peaked at No. 34 for the chart of August 31, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  It also peaked at No.7 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart on August 24, 2013.  As of December 2013, the single has sold 740,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-Ask_Billboard_2013_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ask_Billboard_2013-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 10, 2015 "See You Again" was officially certified as <a href="/wiki/Platinum_certification" class="mw-redirect" title="Platinum certification">Platinum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-riaa.com_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riaa.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=See_You_Again_(Carrie_Underwood_song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On June 7, 2013, <i><a href="/wiki/Good_Morning_America" title="Good Morning America">Good Morning America</a></i> gave a first look for the music video for "See You Again". The full video premiered soon after on ABCNews.com.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The music video was directed by Eric Welch.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It showed the aftermaths of the <a href="/wiki/Sandy_Hook_Elementary_School_shooting" title="Sandy Hook Elementary School shooting">Sandy Hook Elementary School shooting</a>, the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2013_Moore_tornado" title="2013 Moore tornado">2013 Moore, Oklahoma, tornado</a>, along with various reunions.
