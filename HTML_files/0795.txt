<p>The <b>Yorkshire Regiment (14th/15th, 19th and 33rd/76th Foot)</b> (abbreviated <small>YORKS</small>) is an <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">infantry</a> <a href="/wiki/Regiment" title="Regiment">regiment</a> of the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>, created by the amalgamation of three historic regiments in 2006. It is currently the only line infantry or rifles unit to represent a single geographical <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_England" title="Counties of England">county</a> in the new infantry structure, serving as the county regiment of <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>. It lost one battalion as part of the <a href="/wiki/Future_of_the_British_Army_(Army_2020)" class="mw-redirect" title="Future of the British Army (Army 2020)">Army 2020</a> defence review.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The regiment's <a href="/wiki/Infantry_of_the_British_Army#Recruitment" title="Infantry of the British Army">recruitment area</a> today covers almost all the <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">historic county</a> (the three <a href="/wiki/Riding_(country_subdivision)" title="Riding (country subdivision)">ridings</a> of the county: <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Riding of Yorkshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="North Riding of Yorkshire">North Riding of Yorkshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>) except for the eastern half of <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire" title="South Yorkshire">South Yorkshire</a> and the southeast of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, which is a recruitment area for <a href="/wiki/The_Rifles" title="The Rifles">the Rifles</a>, and the part of the West Riding that is now in <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a>. The recruitment area covers all of the present-day <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_county" class="mw-redirect" title="Ceremonial county">ceremonial counties</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire" title="North Yorkshire">North Yorkshire</a> (which extends to the northern border of historic Yorkshire — the <a href="/wiki/River_Tees" title="River Tees">River Tees</a>) and East Riding of Yorkshire. Recruitment however is open to those from outside the formal recruitment area, with the regiment in particular recruiting from <a href="/wiki/North_East_England" title="North East England">North East England</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth</a> countries.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The regiment's antecedent units also recruited in areas that are now part of <a href="/wiki/Northumberland" title="Northumberland">Northumberland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyne_and_Wear" title="Tyne and Wear">Tyne and Wear</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cumbria" title="Cumbria">Cumbria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">Formation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Formation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The regiment's formation was announced on 16 December 2004 by <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Hoon" title="Geoff Hoon">Geoff Hoon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Jackson_(British_Army_officer)" title="Mike Jackson (British Army officer)">General Sir Mike Jackson</a> as part of the restructuring of the <a href="/wiki/British_Army_Infantry" class="mw-redirect" title="British Army Infantry">infantry</a> – it was formed from the merger of three regular battalions, plus a reserve battalion:<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b>1st Battalion, The Yorkshire Regiment (Prince of Wales's Own)</b> (previously: 1st Battalion, <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales%27s_Own_Regiment_of_Yorkshire" title="Prince of Wales&#39;s Own Regiment of Yorkshire">Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire</a>)</li>
<li><b>2nd Battalion, The Yorkshire Regiment (Green Howards)</b> (previously: 1st Battalion, <a href="/wiki/Green_Howards" title="Green Howards">Green Howards</a>)</li>
<li><b>3rd Battalion, The Yorkshire Regiment (Duke of Wellington's)</b> (previously: 1st Battalion, <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Wellington%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Wellington&#39;s Regiment">The Duke of Wellington's Regiment (West Riding)</a></li>

<p>The regiment was officially formed on 6 June 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The reserve "<a href="/wiki/Territorial_Army_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="Territorial Army (United Kingdom)">Territorial Army</a>" (as it was then known) battalion re-badged in TA Centres on the same day, but later paraded publicly to re-badge in York on 10 June 2006 in the Museum Gardens, York.  That same afternoon representatives of the affiliated Army Cadet Forces and Combined Cadet Forces detachments (approx 50) rebadged in <a href="/wiki/Imphal_Barracks" title="Imphal Barracks">Imphal Barracks</a>, <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Prince_Andrew,_Duke_of_York" title="Prince Andrew, Duke of York">The Duke of York KG</a> was appointed Colonel-in-Chief and <a href="/wiki/Valerian_Wellesley,_8th_Duke_of_Wellington" title="Valerian Wellesley, 8th Duke of Wellington">the 8th Duke of Wellington KG LVO OBE MC DL</a> the Deputy Colonel-in-Chief, by <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom">the Queen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The four battalions of the regiment were presented with new Queens and Regimental Colours at a parade in Imphal Barracks, York, to replace the antecedent regiments former colours, on 18 June 2010. The 'Honorary' set of Colours carried by the 3rd Battalion (Duke of Wellington's) was paraded by the battalion. The new colours of all four battalions were later paraded through the streets of York from <a href="/wiki/Cliffords_Tower" class="mw-redirect" title="Cliffords Tower">Cliffords Tower</a> to <a href="/wiki/York_Minster" title="York Minster">York Minster</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As part of the <a href="/wiki/Future_of_the_British_Army_(Army_2020)" class="mw-redirect" title="Future of the British Army (Army 2020)">Army 2020</a> reorganisation, in July 2012 an announcement was made that the Yorkshire Regiment would be reduced to two regular battalions, with the 2nd Battalion (Green Howards) disbanded and its soldiers dispersed to the remainder of the regiment on completion of their Cyprus tour in the autumn of 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, on 25 January 2013 the Colonel of the Regiment, <a href="/wiki/Graham_Binns" title="Graham Binns">Major General Graham Binns CBE DSO MC</a>, announced instead a change in the order of battle as follows: The 2nd Battalion was merged into the 1st &amp; 3rd Battalions. The 1st Battalion was then renumbered as the 2nd Battalion and the 3rd Battalion was renumbered as the 1st Battalion. He also announced that they would dispense with the antecedent regiments' historical titles.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lineage">Lineage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lineage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Volunteers" title="Yorkshire Volunteers">Yorkshire Volunteers</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_and_West_Riding_Regiment" title="East and West Riding Regiment">East and West Riding Regiment</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tyne-Tees_Regiment" title="Tyne-Tees Regiment">Tyne-Tees Regiment</a></div>
<p>The history of the 4th Battalion clearly shows the changing attitudes over the last 40 years, toward the links between regular army battalions and Territorial Army (TA) battalions. The <a href="/wiki/1966_Defence_White_Paper" title="1966 Defence White Paper">1966 Defence White Paper</a> dramatically reduced the number of TA units. The effect of this in Yorkshire was the amalgamation of the territorial battalions of the Yorkshire infantry regiments into a single regiment, the <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Volunteers" title="Yorkshire Volunteers">Yorkshire Volunteers</a>, created in 1967, that was no longer affiliated with any of the regular army regiments. The Yorkshire Volunteers continued in existence until 1993 when it was disbanded. The driver for disbandment was the 1990 defence review, <a href="/wiki/Options_for_Change" title="Options for Change">Options for Change</a> which recommended that the link between TA units and regular units be reintroduced.<sup id="cite_ref-4YorksHistory_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4YorksHistory-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 1997 <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Defence_Review" title="Strategic Defence Review">Strategic Defence Review</a> again cut the size of the TA. As far as the Yorkshire regiments were concerned this involved not only the Yorkshire TA infantry battalions but the TA battalions of the Kings Own Yorkshire Yeomanry and the Royal Regiment of Fusiliers. Too big to be amalgamated into one unit, they were instead split into two units; The <a href="/wiki/East_and_West_Riding_Regiment" title="East and West Riding Regiment">East and West Riding Regiment</a> covering those regiments covering the historical geographic areas of York, and the East and West Ridings of Yorkshire,<sup id="cite_ref-4YorksHistory_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4YorksHistory-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barely five years later a further defence review <a href="/wiki/Delivering_Security_in_a_Changing_World" title="Delivering Security in a Changing World">Delivering Security in a Changing World</a> concluded that a new infantry structure, that was geographically based and included both regular and territorial units in the same structure, was required.<sup id="cite_ref-FutureCapabilities_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FutureCapabilities-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Iraq_2006.E2.80.932007"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Iraq_2006–2007">Iraq 2006–2007</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Iraq 2006–2007">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The deployment of 1 <small>YORKS</small> (Prince of Wales's Own) Battle Group to <a href="/wiki/Basra" title="Basra">Basra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> in November 2006 was the first major operational deployment for the Yorkshire Regiment.  A Company, under command of Major Richard Hall were based initially in Basra Palace with responsibility from Abu Al Kasib to the whole <a href="/wiki/Al-Faw_Peninsula" title="Al-Faw Peninsula">Al-Faw Peninsula</a>, before moving to Shaibah Logistics Base and being charged with handing over security responsibilities of the base to the Iraqi Army. B Company, under command of Major Dan Bradbury, were based in Shiaba Logistics Base and had responsibility for Az Zubayr, whilst C Company were detached to 2nd Battalion, <a href="/wiki/Duke_of_Lancaster%27s_Regiment" title="Duke of Lancaster&#39;s Regiment">Duke of Lancaster's Regiment</a> in the COB. During <a href="/wiki/Operation_Telic_order_of_battle#8th_Roulement_(Operation_TELIC_IX)" title="Operation Telic order of battle">Op Telic 9</a> Private Luke Simpson of A Company, from <a href="/wiki/Howden" title="Howden">Howden</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="East Riding of Yorkshire">East Riding of Yorkshire</a>, was killed, becoming the 1st Battalion (Prince of Wales's Own) and the Regiment's first operational fatality.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  Two members of the 1st Battalion (Prince of Wales's Own), Major Ian Crowley of C Company,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> of <a href="/wiki/Beverley" title="Beverley">Beverley</a>, East Riding of Yorkshire and Captain Ibrar Ali of A Company, were awarded the Military Cross.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The 2nd Battalion (Green Howards) of the regiment were deployed to the <a href="/wiki/Helmand_Province" title="Helmand Province">Helmand Province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> in September, 2007 in an '<a href="/wiki/Operational_Mentoring_and_Liaison_Team" title="Operational Mentoring and Liaison Team">Operational Mentoring and Liaison</a>' role with the <a href="/wiki/Afghan_National_Army" title="Afghan National Army">Afghan National Army</a>. Whilst there they took part in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Musa_Qala" title="Battle of Musa Qala">Battle of Musa Qala</a> during which Sgt Lee Johnson was killed shortly after 10am on 8 December 2007, in the lead up to the operation to retake the town from the <a href="/wiki/Taliban" title="Taliban">Taliban</a> forces, after his Vector armoured vehicle ran over a landmine.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Sgt Johnson had previously received a <a href="/wiki/Mentioned_in_Despatches" class="mw-redirect" title="Mentioned in Despatches">MID</a> for assisting his company commander, Major Jake Little, in extracting soldiers who were surrounded by Taliban fighters. Major Little was later awarded a <a href="/wiki/Military_Cross" title="Military Cross">Military Cross</a> for his "inspirational leadership" to extract his men.<sup id="cite_ref-gazette58776_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gazette58776-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>  Acting Sergeant John Cockburn was also awarded a Military Cross for his actions in the same tour of duty.<sup id="cite_ref-gazette58776_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gazette58776-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 2nd Battalion (Green Howards) returned to their barracks in <a href="/wiki/Weeton,_Lancashire" class="mw-redirect" title="Weeton, Lancashire">Weeton, Lancashire</a> during April, 2008 and were presented with their service medals at a parade there on 30 April 2008 by <a href="/wiki/Major_General" class="mw-redirect" title="Major General">Major General</a> Andrew Farquhar, Honorary Colonel of the Battalion, along with <a href="/wiki/Brigadier" title="Brigadier">Brigadier</a> Andrew Mackay, of <a href="/wiki/52nd_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="52nd Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">52 Infantry Brigade</a>, and Brigadier Mike Griffiths, of <a href="/wiki/42nd_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="42nd Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">42 (North West) Brigade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Kosovo_2008.E2.80.932009"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Kosovo_2008–2009">Kosovo 2008–2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Kosovo 2008–2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>B Company Group of 1st Battalion (Prince of Wales Own) deployed for six-months on Op OCULUS(K) as the last UK Intelligence, Surveillance &amp; Reconnaissance Task Force (ISR TF).  They presided over the immediate aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Kosovo_declaration_of_independence" title="2008 Kosovo declaration of independence">Kosovo's declaration of independence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Iraq_2008.E2.80.932009"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Iraq_2008–2009">Iraq 2008–2009</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Iraq 2008–2009">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2008, elements of the 1st Battalion (Prince of Wales's Own) redeployed to <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> on <a href="/wiki/Operation_Telic_order_of_battle#12th_Roulement_(Operation_TELIC_XIII)" title="Operation Telic order of battle">Op Telic 13</a>.  In May 2009, 'A Company' of the 1st Battalion (Prince of Wales Own) handed over their Iraq British military HQ protection duties, in Baghdad, to Alma Company of the 3rd Battalion (Duke of Wellington's). Alma Company were subsequently amongst the last British soldiers to leave Iraq at the end of July 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Afghanistan_2009.E2.80.932010"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Afghanistan_2009–2010">Afghanistan 2009–2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Afghanistan 2009–2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Over 90 members of the 3rd Battalion (Duke of Wellington's) deployed in August 2009 as Battle Casualty Replacements to various other British regiments in Helmand Province. Burma Company of the 3rd Battalion (Duke of Wellington's) deployed to Afghanistan in August 2009 and were then followed by members of Corunna Company. 18-year-old Pte Jonathon Young, of Burma Company, who was attached to the 2nd Battalion of <a href="/wiki/The_Rifles" title="The Rifles">The Rifles</a>, was killed by an <a href="/wiki/Improvised_explosive_device" title="Improvised explosive device">Improvised explosive device</a> in Sangin, on Thursday 20 August, three weeks after arriving in Afghanistan. A <a href="/wiki/Sergeant#Army_13" title="Sergeant">serjeant</a>, from the 2nd Battalion The Rifles, 29-year-old Serjeant Paul McAleese, was killed when he went to the assistance of Pte Young.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 February 2010, 22-year-old Corporal Liam Riley, of <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, and 27-year-old Lance Corporal Graham Shaw, of <a href="/wiki/Golcar" title="Golcar">Golcar</a> in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> were killed by separate IED's, <a href="/wiki/Malgir" title="Malgir">Malgir</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Gereshk" class="mw-redirect" title="Gereshk">Gereshk</a> in Helmand Province, whilst on base security foot patrols. Both soldiers, from Corunna Company of the 3rd Battalion (Duke of Wellington's), were attached to the 1st Battalion <a href="/wiki/Coldstream_Guards" title="Coldstream Guards">Coldstream Guards</a>, as Battle Casualty Replacements.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Prince_Harry" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Harry">Prince Harry</a> had trained with Cpl Riley at <a href="/wiki/CFB_Suffield" title="CFB Suffield">Suffield</a> in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and paid tribute to him in a news release.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 2nd Battalion (Green Howards), plus 60 members of the 4th Battalion, deployed with <a href="/wiki/11th_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="11th Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">11th Light Brigade</a> in September 2009 for a seven-month posting in <a href="/wiki/Operation_Herrick_order_of_battle" title="Operation Herrick order of battle">Op Herrick XI</a> as the <a href="/wiki/OMLT" class="mw-redirect" title="OMLT">OMLT</a> Battle Group.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>  Their actions featured in a series of <a href="/wiki/Newsnight" title="Newsnight">Newsnight</a> programmes shown both during the deployment and after their return, which focused on OMLT 2 in <a href="/wiki/Sangin" title="Sangin">Sangin</a> under Major Rob Palfrey. Two members of the Battalion were awarded the Military Cross, Lieutenant Colin Lunn of <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a>, and Acting Corporal Andrew Wardle of <a href="/wiki/Hetton-le-Hole" title="Hetton-le-Hole">Hetton-le-Hole</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sunderland,_Tyne_and_Wear" class="mw-redirect" title="Sunderland, Tyne and Wear">Sunderland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In addition, Sergeant John Swithenbank and Corporal John Hardman received the <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Gallantry_Medal" title="Queen&#39;s Gallantry Medal">Queen's Gallantry Medal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 23 June 2010, 26-year-old Lance Corporal David Ramsden of <a href="/wiki/Otley" title="Otley">Otley</a>, who was serving with the 1st Battalion <a href="/wiki/Mercian_Regiment" title="Mercian Regiment">The Mercian Regiment</a>, was killed. The NCO from 1st Battalion (Prince of Wales Own) was serving as part of the Police Advisory Team which was tasked to respond to an incident at a checkpoint near <a href="/wiki/Gereshk" class="mw-redirect" title="Gereshk">Gereshk</a> in Helmand Province, when the Ridgeback armoured vehicle he was travelling in rolled into a waterway. He was killed alongside colleagues Colour Sergeant Martyn Simon Horton, Private Douglas Niall Halliday and Private Alex Isaac.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Afghanistan_2011.E2.80.932012"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Afghanistan_2011–2012">Afghanistan 2011–2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Afghanistan 2011–2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2011, 500 members of the 1st Battalion (Prince of Wales Own) were deployed to Helmand province in Afghanistan for a six-month tour in support of the 20th Armoured Brigade, their first deployment to Afghanistan as a complete battalion.<sup id="cite_ref-gov.uk_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gov.uk-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The battalion was split up for the deployment, with A Company working with 3rd Battalion The Royal Regiment of Scotland, B and C Companies with the Danish Army, and the Brigade Headquarters providing staff for Headquarters Task Force Helmand. 1 <small>YORKS</small> soldiers formed the Brigade Reconnaissance Force with members of <a href="/wiki/1st_The_Queen%27s_Dragoon_Guards" title="1st The Queen&#39;s Dragoon Guards">1st The Queen's Dragoon Guards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gov.uk_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gov.uk-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Soldiers from 1st Battalion were reported to have been involved in 'high tempo' patrol operations alongside the Afghanistan police force.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> B Company serving as part of the Danish Battle Group in the Nahr-e Saraj (North) area of operations, patrolled aggressively into several contested areas. On 1 December 2011 the Battalion was involved in a dawn raid on a suspected IED factory.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On 1 January 2012 Private John King was killed in an explosion while on a foot patrol in Nahr-e Saraj,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> on 27 January Lance Corporal Gajbahadur Gurung of the Royal Gurkha Rifles was killed while serving with 1 <small>YORKS</small> in the Nahr-e Saraj district.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 6 March 2012, six members of Corunna company, 3rd Battalion (Duke of Wellington's) were killed in the Lashkar Gah Durai region after their Warrior Armoured vehicle was struck by an improvised explosive device.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 29 April 2012, the 3 <small>YORKS</small> Battle Group took over responsibility of Nahr-e Saraj (South), Helmand province from the 5th Battalion, the Rifles. On the 3 June 2012 Private Gregg Thomas Stone of Burma Company, 3 <small>YORKS</small> was killed in Nahr-e Saraj during an operation to apprehend a group of insurgents who had abducted a member of the Afghan Police.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On 16 September 2012, Sergeant Gareth Thursby and Private Thomas Wroe of the 3rd Battalion were killed in Nahr-e Saraj by a rogue Afghan police officer.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kenya_2013">Kenya 2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Kenya 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2013, 16 soldiers serving with the <a href="/wiki/Reconnaissance" title="Reconnaissance">reconnaissance</a> platoon of 1st Battalion (Prince of Wales Own), staged a mutiny whilst on a training exercise in <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>. Led by Corporal Anthony Brown, they staged a sit down strike in front of 1,000 people. Each soldier refused to stand up when individually commanded to do so. As they went through their drills, a ringleader shouted 'sit down' and the dissenters dropped to ground. Commanders ordered each man in turn to stand up but were ignored. The soldiers had clashed with their newly appointed commanding officers whom they accused of excessive drinking, complaining they were "being led by muppets".<sup id="cite_ref-Drury_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Drury-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/Court-martial" title="Court-martial">courts martial</a>, in December 2013, the soldiers pleaded guilty to disobeying a lawful command. Cpl Brown was sentenced to 60 days imprisonment and a dishonourable discharge. The prosecutor, Col. Clive Whitwham, said: 'There was a feeling they were not being appreciated, that they were not being well managed. 'The platoon was said to have worked well in Afghanistan. There was said to have been a perception that they had got a little too big for their boots.'
The soldiers defence Jonathan Lynch, said: 'This is not a case of inflated egos but a case of mismanagement.'<sup id="cite_ref-Drury_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Drury-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Two lance corporals were reduced to the rank of private, with 60 days imprisonment and the Private soldiers were each given 40 days imprisonment.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Regimental_Battalion_changes.2C_July_2013"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Regimental_Battalion_changes,_July_2013">Regimental Battalion changes, July 2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Regimental Battalion changes, July 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 3rd Battalion (Duke of Wellington's) laid up its Regulation Colours on 20 July 2013, at <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Minster" title="Halifax Minster">Halifax Minster</a>, following a series of 'Freedom Parades' during the preceding week.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On 25 July there was a parade at the 3rd Battalion barracks, in Warminster, the Colonel in Chief, the Duke of York, exchanged the Colours between the Battalions. The 1st Battalion colours were handed over to the Warminster battalion (1 <small>YORKS</small>). The honorary colours formerly belonging to 3rd Battalion (<a href="/wiki/76th_Regiment_of_Foot" title="76th Regiment of Foot">76th Regiment of Foot</a>) were to be rotated annually between the remaining battalions.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

The battalion is an armoured infantry battalion deployed as part of <a href="/wiki/12th_Armoured_Infantry_Brigade_(United_Kingdom)" title="12th Armoured Infantry Brigade (United Kingdom)">12th Armoured Infantry Brigade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1&amp;2_Yorks_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1&amp;2_Yorks-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>

<p>The 2nd Battalion, is based at Somme Barracks at <a href="/wiki/Catterick_Garrison" title="Catterick Garrison">Catterick Garrison</a>. The battalion is a light mechanised infantry battalion deployed as part of <a href="/wiki/4th_Infantry_Brigade_and_Headquarters_North_East" title="4th Infantry Brigade and Headquarters North East">4th Infantry Brigade and Headquarters North East</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1&amp;2_Yorks_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1&amp;2_Yorks-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span id="4th_Battalion_.28abbreviated_4_YORKS.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="4th_Battalion_(abbreviated_4_YORKS)">4th Battalion (abbreviated 4 <small>YORKS</small>)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yorkshire_Regiment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 4th Battalion (abbreviated 4 YORKS)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 4th Battalion, serving as <a href="/wiki/Military_reserve_force" title="Military reserve force">reserve infantry</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Army_Reserve(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="Army Reserve(United Kingdom)">Army Reserve</a> battalion of the Yorkshire Regiment. The battalion headquarters is in Worsley Barracks, York and forms part of <a href="/wiki/4th_Infantry_Brigade_and_Headquarters_North_East" title="4th Infantry Brigade and Headquarters North East">4th Infantry Brigade and Headquarters North East</a>. The battalion operates from Army Reserve Centres across the regimental footprint with companies dispersed in <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beverley" title="Beverley">Beverley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kingston_upon_Hull" title="Kingston upon Hull">Hull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough" title="Middlesbrough">Middlesbrough</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> and York.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Freedoms awarded to the Yorkshire Regiment including those carried through from the antecedent regiments include the following:<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Cities:</b> Bradford, Kingston-upon-Hull, Leeds, Sheffield, York and Wakefield.</li>
<li><b>Towns and districts:</b> Barnsley, Beverley (East Riding of Yorkshire), Bridlington (East Riding of Yorkshire), Halifax (Calderdale), Harrogate, Huddersfield (Kirklees), Middlesbrough, Redcar and Cleveland, Richmond, Rotherham, Scarborough, Skipton (Craven District), Stockton on Tees and Warminster</li></ul>
<p>The regiment also holds the distinction of having the freedom of <a href="/wiki/Erquinghem-Lys" title="Erquinghem-Lys">Erquinghem-Lys</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. This was passed on from the freedom awarded to the 3rd Battalion (Duke of Wellington's) in November 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> The Duke of Wellington's Regiment (West Riding) were also awarded the Freedom of <a href="/wiki/Mossley" title="Mossley">Mossley</a>, on 8 July 1967.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 3 August 2009 Rotherham became the first town to bestow the 'Freedom of the Borough' on the Yorkshire Regiment. This gives the regiment the right to march through the town with 'flags flying, bands playing and bayonets fixed'.  At a ceremony outside the Town Hall the Regiment paraded two Guards of soldiers, who had recently returned from Iraq and the <a href="/wiki/Colours,_standards_and_guidons" class="mw-redirect" title="Colours, standards and guidons">Colours</a> of the 3rd Battalion Yorkshire Regiment (Duke of Wellington's), led by the Kings Division Band, under the command of Lieutenant Colonel Vallings, the Battalion <a href="/wiki/Commanding_officer" title="Commanding officer">Commanding officer</a>. The Mayor of Rotherham, Councillor Shaukat Ali, on behalf of the Borough, presented the Freedom Scroll to Colonel Simon Newton, who accepted the honour for the Regiment. The regiment was the first military unit to become 'Honorary Freemen of the Borough.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 8 September 2013 the regiment was given the Freedom of <a href="/wiki/Barnoldswick" title="Barnoldswick">Barnoldswick</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Lancashire" title="Lancashire">Lancashire</a> during a rededication ceremony of the town War Memorial, when 46 new names from the two world wars were added.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Yorkshire Regiment was awarded the Freedom of Warminster on its return from Afghanistan following their Homecoming Parade on 30 November 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Infantry regiments are permitted to display 43 battle honours from the two world wars on the Queen's Colour and 46 honours from other conflicts on the Regimental Colour. Upon amalgamation, the Yorkshire Regiment had to choose from the total list of honours of its three antecedents which honours would be displayed on its new colours. The chosen honours were:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Queen's Colour</b>
<ul><li>Mons; Marne 1914, 18; Aisne 1914, 18; Armentieres 1914; Ypres 1914, 15, 17, 18; Hill 60; Loos; Somme 1916, 18; Arras 1917, 18; Cambrai 1917, 18; Lys; Tardenois; Selle; Valenciennes; Piave; Vittoria Veneto; Doiran 1917; Suvla; Gallipoli 1915; Norway 1940; Dunkirk; St Valery en Caux; Normandy Landing; Odon; Fontenay le Pesnil; Schaddenhof; NW Europe 1940, 44–45; Keren; Gazala; El Alamein; Mareth; Akarit; Djebel Bou Aoukaz 1943; Sicily 1943; Minturno; Anzio; Monte Ceco; Sittang 1942, 45; Pegu 1942; Defence of Sinweya; Imphal; Meiktila; Burma 1942–45</li></ul></li>
<li><b>Regimental Colour</b>
<ul><li>Namur 1695; Blenheim; Ramillies; Oudenarde; Malplaquet; Dettingen; Louisburg; Quebec 1759; Bellisle; Martinique 1762; Havannah; St Lucia 1778; Martinique 1794, 1809; Tournay; Mysore; Seringapatam; Ally Ghur; Delhi 1803; Leswarree; Dieg; Corunna; Guadaloupe 1810; Java; Nive; Peninsula; Waterloo; Bhurtpore; Alma; Inkerman; Sevastopol; New Zealand; Abyssinia; Afghanistan 1879–80; Tirah; Relief of Kimberley; Paardeburg; Relief of Ladysmith; South Africa 1899–1902; Afghanistan 1919; Korea 1952–53; The Hook 1953; Iraq 2003</li></ul></li></ul>
<p>In addition to the displayed honours, the regimental colours also display four emblems from the antecedents regiments:
</p>
<ul><li>White Horse of Hanover – displayed top right; from the Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Brunswick_star" title="Brunswick star">Star of Brunswick</a> – displayed bottom left; from Prince of Wales's Own Regiment of Yorkshire</li>
<li>Dannebrog Cross – displayed bottom right; from the Green Howards</li>
<li>Elephant &amp; Howdah – displayed bottom centre; from the Duke of Wellington's Regiment</li></ul>
<p>The Battalion's <a href="/wiki/Roman_Numerals" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Numerals">Roman Numeral</a> is in the top left corner of each flag.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> The use of the Cross of St George as the background is unique to the Yorkshire Regiment.
