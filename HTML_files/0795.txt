<p><b>Massachusetts Congressional District 11</b> is an <b>obsolete</b> congressional district in eastern <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>.  It was eliminated in 1993 after the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census,_1990" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Census, 1990">1990 U.S. Census</a>.  Its last <a href="/wiki/Congressman" class="mw-redirect" title="Congressman">Congressman</a> was <a href="/wiki/Brian_J._Donnelly" title="Brian J. Donnelly">Brian Donnelly</a>; its most notable were <a href="/wiki/John_Quincy_Adams" title="John Quincy Adams">John Quincy Adams</a> following his term as president, eventual president <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> and Speaker of the House <a href="/wiki/Tip_O%27Neill" title="Tip O&#39;Neill">Tip O'Neill</a>.
</p>
