<p><b>Andy Ristie</b> (born March 17, 1982) is a <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Surinamese</a>-<a href="/wiki/Dutch_people" title="Dutch people">Dutch</a> <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxer</a> who competes in the <a href="/wiki/Lightweight" title="Lightweight">lightweight</a> division. After an unremarkable beginning to his career, he came to prominence with a lengthy and entertaining winning streak in the <a href="/wiki/It%27s_Showtime_(kickboxing)" title="It&#39;s Showtime (kickboxing)">It's Showtime</a> promotion in 2011 and 2012. This earned him an invite to fight amongst the 70&#160;kg/154&#160;lb division's elite, competing in the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_MAX_2012_World_Championship_Tournament_Final" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World MAX 2012 World Championship Tournament Final">K-1 World MAX 2012 Tournament</a> and debuting in <a href="/wiki/Glory_(kickboxing)" title="Glory (kickboxing)">Glory</a> shortly after.
</p><p>Standing at 1.82&#160;m (5&#160;ft <span class="frac nowrap">11<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>2</sub></span>&#160;in), Ristie is tall for his weight class and is known to look for <a href="/wiki/Knockout" title="Knockout">knockouts</a> from the first bell. He utilizes devastating <a href="/wiki/Knee_(strike)" title="Knee (strike)">knee strikes</a> to the body and possesses considerable <a href="/wiki/Punching_power" title="Punching power">punching power</a>, especially in his left <a href="/wiki/Hook_(boxing)" title="Hook (boxing)">hook</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As of 2 November 2015, he is ranked the #2 lightweight in the world by <a href="/wiki/Glory_(kickboxing)" title="Glory (kickboxing)">GLORY</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Ristie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Early_career_.282002.E2.80.932011.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career_(2002–2011)">Early career (2002–2011)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Ristie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early career (2002–2011)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ristie was born in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> in 1982 and relocated to <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> where he is trained by Lucien Carbin at Fight Club Carbin. He began training <a href="/wiki/Muay_Thai" title="Muay Thai">Muay Thai</a> around age of 20 and while he won the <a href="/wiki/World_Kickboxing_Network" title="World Kickboxing Network">World Kickboxing Network</a> (WKN) Intercontinental Super Welterweight (-72.6/160.1&#160;lb) Championship as an amateur, the beginnings of his professional career were fought in relative obscurity. Having built up an extensive record with an impressive <a href="/wiki/Knockout" title="Knockout">knockout</a> ratio fighting in smaller events in Suriname and the Netherlands, he was recruited by the <a href="/wiki/It%27s_Showtime_(kickboxing)" title="It&#39;s Showtime (kickboxing)">It's Showtime</a> promotion at twenty-nine years of age. He debuted with a first-round KO of Nick Beljaards at <i><a href="/wiki/BFN_Group_presents:_It%27s_Showtime_Brussels" class="mw-redirect" title="BFN Group presents: It&#39;s Showtime Brussels">BFN Group presents: It's Showtime Brussels</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> on March 26, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On September 25, 2011, Ristie defeated <a href="/w/index.php?title=William_Diender&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="William Diender (page does not exist)">William Diender</a> via <a href="/wiki/Unanimous_decision" title="Unanimous decision">unanimous decision</a> in the tournament reserve bout of the <i><a href="/wiki/BFN_Group_%26_Music_Hall_presents:_It%27s_Showtime_%22Fast_%26_Furious_70MAX%22" class="mw-redirect" title="BFN Group &amp; Music Hall presents: It&#39;s Showtime &quot;Fast &amp; Furious 70MAX&quot;">BFN Group &amp; Music Hall presents: It's Showtime "Fast &amp; Furious 70MAX"</a></i> in Brussels.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He followed this up with a <a href="/wiki/Technical_knockout" class="mw-redirect" title="Technical knockout">technical knockout</a> defeat of Jonay Risco at <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Culture,_Fight_Club_Group_%26_Canary_Kickboxing_Federation_presents:_It%27s_Showtime_53" class="mw-redirect" title="Street Culture, Fight Club Group &amp; Canary Kickboxing Federation presents: It&#39;s Showtime 53">Street Culture, Fight Club Group &amp; Canary Kickboxing Federation presents: It's Showtime 53</a></i> on November 12, 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">Tenerife</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Having dominated Risco throughout the fight, Ristie floored the Spaniard with a left <a href="/wiki/Hook_(boxing)" title="Hook (boxing)">hook</a> in round three and, while he was able to make it back to his feet, referee Joop Ubeda called a halt to the contest.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2012.E2.80.93present"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012–present">2012–present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andy_Ristie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2012–present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He stopped <a href="/wiki/Hinata_Watanabe" class="mw-redirect" title="Hinata Watanabe">Hinata Watanabe</a> in the first round of their contest at <i><a href="/wiki/It%27s_Showtime_2012_in_Leeuwarden" title="It&#39;s Showtime 2012 in Leeuwarden">It's Showtime 2012 in Leeuwarden</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Leeuwarden" title="Leeuwarden">Leeuwarden</a>, Netherlands, on January 28, 2012. After dropping his opponent in ten seconds, he sent Hinata to the canvas twice more, forcing a referee stoppage.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> This win helped establish Ristie as a force on the world stage and earned him a contract with <a href="/wiki/K-1" title="K-1">K-1</a>. He was invited to compete alongside fifteen of the world's other top 70&#160;kg/154&#160;lb kickfighters in the K-1 World MAX 2012 World Championship Tournament, and faced <a href="/wiki/Gago_Drago" title="Gago Drago">Gago Drago</a> in the opening stage at the <i><a href="/wiki/K-1_World_MAX_2012_World_Championship_Tournament_Final_16" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World MAX 2012 World Championship Tournament Final 16">K-1 World MAX 2012 World Championship Tournament Final 16</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> on May 27, 2012. He began the fight by battering Drago with <a href="/wiki/Knee_(strike)" title="Knee (strike)">knees</a> to the head and body and continued to put a one-sided beating on the Armenian throughout, winning a wide unanimous decision.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 30, 2012, he KO'd Samir Djabba with a left hook in the first round, after 32 seconds, <i><a href="/wiki/Music_Hall_%26_BFN_Group_present:_It%27s_Showtime_57_%26_58" class="mw-redirect" title="Music Hall &amp; BFN Group present: It&#39;s Showtime 57 &amp; 58">Music Hall &amp; BFN Group present: It's Showtime 57 &amp; 58</a></i> in Brussels.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Then, in his third fight in as many months, he stopped David Calvo with a knee to the body in under a minute at <i><a href="/wiki/Street_Culture,_Federaci%C3%B3n_Canaria_de_Kickboxing_%26_Fightclub_Group_present:_It%27s_Showtime_59" class="mw-redirect" title="Street Culture, Federación Canaria de Kickboxing &amp; Fightclub Group present: It&#39;s Showtime 59">Street Culture, Federación Canaria de Kickboxing &amp; Fightclub Group present: It's Showtime 59</a></i> in Tenerife on July 21, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He had originally been set to face <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Roque%C3%B1i" title="Abraham Roqueñi">Abraham Roqueñi</a> at the event but he withdrew due to injury a week before.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> After these victories, he broke the top ten in the world rankings for the first time in June 2012, coming in at #8.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was expected to fight on an <i>It's Showtime</i> card in <a href="/wiki/Yokohama" title="Yokohama">Yokohama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, on November 17, 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> but the event was cancelled following the promotion's acquisition by <a href="/wiki/Glory_(kickboxing)" title="Glory (kickboxing)">Glory</a><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and so he was transferred to <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_2:_Brussels" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 2: Brussels">Glory 2: Brussels</a></i> in Belgium and put up against Nordin Benmoh. Once again, he stopped his opponent with a left hook.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> A rematch between Ristie and Hinata Watanabe was booked as the reserve fight for the <i><a href="/wiki/Shoot_Boxing_World_Tournament_2012" class="mw-redirect" title="Shoot Boxing World Tournament 2012">Shoot Boxing World Tournament 2012</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, Japan on November 17, 2012, and ended like the pair's first match ten months earlier: Hinata was <a href="/wiki/Cut_(wound)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cut (wound)">cut</a> early on and dropped with <a href="/wiki/Punch_(combat)" title="Punch (combat)">punches</a> twice shortly after, resulting in a referee stoppage and a first-round <a href="/wiki/Technical_knockout" class="mw-redirect" title="Technical knockout">technical knockout</a> win for Ristie.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ristie was drawn against <a href="/wiki/Andy_Souwer" title="Andy Souwer">Andy Souwer</a> in the quarter-finals of the <i><a href="/wiki/K-1_World_MAX_2012_World_Championship_Tournament_Final" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World MAX 2012 World Championship Tournament Final">K-1 World MAX 2012 World Championship Tournament Final</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> on December 15, 2013, but lost after a unanimous decision.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Despite ending the year with a loss, he went 6–1 over the course of the twelve months, breaking into the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For his first match in 2013, Ristie was set to face four-time world champion and original K-1 World MAX winner <a href="/wiki/Albert_Kraus" title="Albert Kraus">Albert Kraus</a> at <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_5:_London" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 5: London">Glory 5: London</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> on March 23<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> but was unable to fight due to visa issues and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Warren_Stevelmans" title="Warren Stevelmans">Warren Stevelmans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He was then transferred onto the <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_6:_Istanbul" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 6: Istanbul">Glory 6: Istanbul</a></i> card in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> on April 6 to face <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Campagna_(kickboxer)" title="Alessandro Campagna (kickboxer)">Alessandro Campagna</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Kraus-Ristie fight was rescheduled for <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_8:_Tokyo_-_2013_65kg_Slam" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 8: Tokyo - 2013 65kg Slam">Glory 8: Tokyo - 2013 65kg Slam</a></i> on May 3, 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> and he won with a knee KO in round two.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was to fight <a href="/wiki/Sanny_Dahlbeck" title="Sanny Dahlbeck">Sanny Dahlbeck</a> at <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_10:_Los_Angeles_-_Middleweight_World_Championship_Tournament" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 10: Los Angeles - Middleweight World Championship Tournament">Glory 10: Los Angeles - Middleweight World Championship Tournament</a></i> in Ontario, California on September 28, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> However, the Swede withdrew for undisclosed reasons and was replaced by Niclas Larsen.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> He defeated Larsen via unanimous decision.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ristie caused one of the biggest upsets in kickboxing history when he knocked out both <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Petrosyan" title="Giorgio Petrosyan">Giorgio Petrosyan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robin_van_Roosmalen" title="Robin van Roosmalen">Robin van Roosmalen</a> to win the <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_12:_New_York_-_Lightweight_World_Championship_Tournament" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 12: New York - Lightweight World Championship Tournament">Glory 12: New York - Lightweight World Championship Tournament</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="New York City, New York">New York City, New York</a>, US on November 23, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Against Giorgio Petrosyan in the semi-finals, Ristie applied a lot of pressure and caused the Italian problems in finding his rhythm, but Petrosyan controlled the first two rounds nonetheless. Going into the third in need of a knockout, Ristie managed just that when he landed a huge right hand that had Petrosyan stunned only for him to follow it up with a combination of a right, left and another left that finished it, becoming the first man to stop Petrosyan and ending his forty-two fight, six year undefeated streak.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Riding the wave of momentum that saw him dethrone kickboxing's all-time greatest, Ristie continued his rich vein of form in the final against Robin van Roosmalen, dropping the Dutchman towards the tail end of round one before putting him away in two.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Ristie had some problems with his camp in the weeks prior to the tournament, leaving his longtime trainer Lucien Carbin to form his own Team Ristie.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was to fight for the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Glory_(kickboxing)#Glory_Lightweight_Championship" title="Glory (kickboxing)">Glory Lightweight Championship</a> against <a href="/wiki/Ky_Hollenbeck" title="Ky Hollenbeck">Ky Hollenbeck</a> at <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_14:_Zagreb" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 14: Zagreb">Glory 14: Zagreb</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> on March 8, 2014<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> but Hollenbeck withdrew and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Davit_Kiria" title="Davit Kiria">Davit Kiria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> He dominated the Georgian for the first three rounds and scored a knockdown with a step knee in the second, but faded in the championship rounds and was stopped in the fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Hollenbeck-Ristie fight was then scheduled for <i><a href="/wiki/Glory_17:_Los_Angeles" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 17: Los Angeles">Glory 17: Los Angeles</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/Inglewood,_California" title="Inglewood, California">Inglewood, California</a>, US on June 21, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> Andy Ristie knocked out Ky Hollenbeck thirty-five seconds into round one, stunning his American opponent with a <a href="/wiki/Jab" title="Jab">jab</a> before putting him away with a left hook.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the February 6, 2015, Ristie fought <a href="/wiki/Steve_Moxon" title="Steve Moxon">Steve Moxon</a> in a Superfight on <a href="/wiki/Glory_19:_USA" class="mw-redirect" title="Glory 19: USA">Glory 19: USA</a> in Hampton, Virginia, USA. He won the fight via TKO in the first round.
</p>
