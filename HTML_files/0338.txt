	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">List of titles</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>This is a list of personal <b><a href="/wiki/Title" title="Title">titles</a></b> arranged in a sortable table. They can be sorted:
</p>
<ul><li>Alphabetically</li>
<li>By language, nation, or tradition of origin</li>
<li>By function. See <a href="/wiki/Separation_of_powers" title="Separation of powers">Separation of powers</a> for a description of the <b>Executive</b>, <b>Judicial</b>, and <b>Legislative</b> functions as they are generally understood today. Titles that bestow functional authority with minimal executive, judicial, or legislative power are listed as <b>Administrative</b>. Titles that bestow political or spiritual authority with minimal executive, judicial, or legislative power are listed as <b>Ceremonial</b>.</li>
<li>By manner in which the title is usually achieved. Most title-holders are <b>Appointed</b> to their rank by someone higher in the system or <b>Elected</b> by people equal in the system. Historically, many titles were achieved through <b>Hereditary</b> birthright. A few historical titles have been randomly <b>Chosen By Lot</b> or <b>Purchased</b> outright. For those unofficial titles granted as a sign of respect, such as <a href="/wiki/Mr." title="Mr.">Mister</a> or <a href="/wiki/Prophet" title="Prophet">Prophet</a>, the word <b>Identified</b> is used here.</li>
<li>By scope of authority. Especially with historical titles, this scope may have changed over time, but the most usual understanding of the title today is implied. <b>Personal</b> titles are not part of a governing body, and have authority only over those who individually choose to follow the title-holder. <b>Tribal</b> titles give the title-holder authority over a bloodline rather than a physical geography. <b>Institutional</b> titles are mostly confined to a specific <a href="/wiki/Campus" title="Campus">campus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corporation" title="Corporation">corporation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Temple" title="Temple">temple</a>, or other private or semi-public institution. <b>Divisional</b> is applied to most <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Police_ranks" class="mw-redirect" title="Police ranks">police ranks</a>, with the number of people under that rank's command listed when known. <b>Local</b> titles are those with authority in a metropolitan or similar area, such as a <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a>. <b>Provincial</b> titles are those with authority over a <a href="/wiki/Constituent_state" title="Constituent state">constituent state</a>, such as a United States <a href="/wiki/Governor" title="Governor">governor</a>. <b>Regional</b> titles are those with authority over multiple constituent states, such as a <a href="/wiki/Federal_judge" title="Federal judge">federal judge</a>. <b>Courtly</b> titles have no sovereign power of their own but are granted high prestige by, and are possibly able to exert influence over, a head of state. Similarly, <b>Diplomatic</b> titles bear no <a href="/wiki/Sovereignty" title="Sovereignty">sovereignty</a> of their own, but are vested with the equivalent of sovereignty by a head of state. <b>National</b> titles are at the level of a <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a> or <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a>, with authority over a sovereign nation. <b>Supranational</b> titles are those with authority over multiple sovereign nations. <b>Supernatural</b> titles are those applied solely to deceased figures, such as saints, or to <a href="/wiki/Superhuman" title="Superhuman">superhuman</a> beings, such as angels and gods.</li></ul>
<p>For in-depth information please follow the links to individual titles.
</p>
