<p>"<b>Mama's Song</b>" is a song recorded by American country music singer <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>, co-written by her along with <a href="/wiki/Kara_DioGuardi" title="Kara DioGuardi">Kara DioGuardi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marti_Frederiksen" title="Marti Frederiksen">Marti Frederiksen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Luke_Laird" title="Luke Laird">Luke Laird</a>. It was released in November 2010 as the fourth and final single from her third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" title="Play On (Carrie Underwood album)">Play On</a></i>. These songwriters also wrote her previous single, "<a href="/wiki/Undo_It" title="Undo It">Undo It</a>". 
</p><p>Underwood performed this song on the 44th <a href="/wiki/Country_Music_Association_Awards" title="Country Music Association Awards">CMA Awards</a> on November 10, 2010. The song was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Best_Country_Solo_Performance" class="mw-redirect" title="Best Country Solo Performance">Best Country Solo Performance</a> at the <a href="/wiki/54th_Grammy_Awards" class="mw-redirect" title="54th Grammy Awards">54th Grammy Awards</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Content">Content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mama%27s_Song&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song is a ballad where the female narrator sings to her mother, telling her to let her go and not to worry as the daughter moves out and begins her own life as the bride of the man she loves.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mama%27s_Song&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_Erlewine" title="Stephen Thomas Erlewine">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</a> of <a href="/wiki/Allmusic" class="mw-redirect" title="Allmusic">Allmusic</a> described the song as "sticky" and "tacky."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Sean Daly of the <i><a href="/wiki/St._Petersburg_Times" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Petersburg Times">St. Petersburg Times</a></i> compared it unfavorably to "Change" on the same album, saying that both were "too precious, too preachy."<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Slant_Magazine" title="Slant Magazine">Slant Magazine</a></i> reviewer Jonathan Keefe described the song more favorably in his review of the album, saying that it had cliché lyrics but praising Underwood's "understated vocal" as well as the comparatively softer production.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In a more positive review, Bobby Peacock of Roughstock gave it three-and-a-half stars out of five, saying that she "proves yet again that she can just sing without having to reach for the rafters on every note, and the production is agreeably mellow and acoustic."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mama%27s_Song&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The official music video for "Mama's Song" premiered on <a href="/wiki/VEVO" class="mw-redirect" title="VEVO">VEVO</a> on September 24, 2010. It features both Underwood's husband <a href="/wiki/Mike_Fisher_(ice_hockey)" title="Mike Fisher (ice hockey)">Mike Fisher</a>, and her mother Carole. It was filmed in <a href="/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee" title="Nashville, Tennessee">Nashville</a>, and was directed by <a href="/wiki/Shaun_Silva" title="Shaun Silva">Shaun Silva</a>. It tells the story of a woman who meets her true love and moves away from her mom.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mama%27s_Song&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In October 2009, in advance of the album's release, "Mama's Song" charted at number 77 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and number 68 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a>. It was the first of three promotional singles from the album.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> On the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> charts dated for the week ending September 4, 2010, it entered at number 55.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It has also re-entered the <i>Billboard</i> Hot 100 at number 87 upon its release as an official single and has reached a new peak of 56. It is so far the only one of Underwood's singles to reach the Hot 100 that has charted outside the top 50. On the week of January 17, 2011 it peaked at number two on Hot Country Songs and became Underwood's third single to peak at number two.
</p>
