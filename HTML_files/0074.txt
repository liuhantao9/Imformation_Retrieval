	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe baronets</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe Baronetcy</b>, of Elvetham Hall in <a href="/wiki/Hartley_Wintney#Elvetham" title="Hartley Wintney">Elvetham</a> in the County of <a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a>, is a title in the <a href="/wiki/Baronetage_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Baronetage of the United Kingdom">Baronetage of the United Kingdom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was created on 1 July 1929 for Fitzroy Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe. Born Fitzroy Hamilton Niall Lloyd-Anstruther, he was the son of <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant-Colonel" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant-Colonel">Lieutenant-Colonel</a> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Hamilton_Lloyd-Anstruther" title="Robert Hamilton Lloyd-Anstruther">Robert Hamilton Lloyd-Anstruther</a>, <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> for <a href="/wiki/Woodbridge_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Woodbridge (UK Parliament constituency)">Woodbridge</a>, son of  Colonel James Hamilton Lloyd-Anstruther, son of <a href="/wiki/Brigadier-General" class="mw-redirect" title="Brigadier-General">Brigadier-General</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Robert_Anstruther_(soldier)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Robert Anstruther (soldier) (page does not exist)">Robert Anstruther</a>, son of Sir Robert Anstruther, 3rd Baronet, of Wrae (see <a href="/wiki/Anstruther_baronets" title="Anstruther baronets">Anstruther baronets</a>). In 1910, he assumed by Royal licence the surname of Anstruther only. Later the same year he assumed for himself and issue the surnames of Gough-Calthorpe in addition to that of Anstruther, having married the Hon. Rachel, eldest daughter and co-heir of Augustus Cholmondeley Gough-Calthorpe, 6th Baron Calthorpe (see the <a href="/wiki/Baron_Calthorpe" title="Baron Calthorpe">Baron Calthorpe</a>). The first Baronet was succeeded by his son, the second Baronet. He was a <a href="/wiki/Brigadier" title="Brigadier">Brigadier</a> in the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> and a member of the <a href="/wiki/Hampshire_County_Council" title="Hampshire County Council">Hampshire County Council</a>. As of 2014, the title is held by his grandson, the third Baronet, who succeeded in 1985. He is the eldest son of Niall Hamilton Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe (1940-1970), eldest son of the second Baronet. As a descendant of the third Baronet of Wrae he is also in remainder to this title.
</p>
<h2><span id="Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe_baronets.2C_of_Elvetham_Hall_.281929.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe_baronets,_of_Elvetham_Hall_(1929)">Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe baronets, of Elvetham Hall (1929)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe_baronets&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe baronets, of Elvetham Hall (1929)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Sir_Fitzroy_Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe,_1st_Baronet" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Fitzroy Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe, 1st Baronet">Sir Fitzroy Hamilton Niall Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe, 1st Baronet</a> (1872–1957)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sir_Richard_Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe,_2nd_Baronet" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Richard Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe, 2nd Baronet">Sir Richard Hamilton Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe, 2nd Baronet</a> (1908–1985)</li>
<li><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sir_Euan_Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe,_3rd_Baronet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sir Euan Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe, 3rd Baronet (page does not exist)">Sir Euan Hamilton Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe, 3rd Baronet</a> (b. 1966)</li></ul>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Heir_apparent" title="Heir apparent">heir apparent</a> is the present holder's only son, Barnaby Charles Anstruther-Gough-Calthorpe (b. 2005).
</p>
