	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Borders of Suriname</h1>

<p>The <b>borders of Suriname</b> are subject to longstanding disputes between <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> and its neighbouring countries <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> (via <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a>). Only the border with Brazil has been <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Limits_(Brazil%E2%80%93Netherlands)" title="Treaty of Limits (Brazil–Netherlands)">uncontroversial since 1906</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_border">Eastern border</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borders_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Eastern border">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:KAARTEN_SGD_-_Kaart_van_de_Marowijne_van_af_de_samenvloeiing_der_Lawa_en_Tapanahoni_tot_Albina_met_de_grensregeling_tusschen_Suriname_en_Fransch-Guyana.jpeg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/KAARTEN_SGD_-_Kaart_van_de_Marowijne_van_af_de_samenvloeiing_der_Lawa_en_Tapanahoni_tot_Albina_met_de_grensregeling_tusschen_Suriname_en_Fransch-Guyana.jpeg/250px-KAARTEN_SGD_-_Kaart_van_de_Marowijne_van_af_de_samenvloeiing_der_Lawa_en_Tapanahoni_tot_Albina_met_de_grensregeling_tusschen_Suriname_en_Fransch-Guyana.jpeg" decoding="async" width="250" height="581" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/KAARTEN_SGD_-_Kaart_van_de_Marowijne_van_af_de_samenvloeiing_der_Lawa_en_Tapanahoni_tot_Albina_met_de_grensregeling_tusschen_Suriname_en_Fransch-Guyana.jpeg/375px-KAARTEN_SGD_-_Kaart_van_de_Marowijne_van_af_de_samenvloeiing_der_Lawa_en_Tapanahoni_tot_Albina_met_de_grensregeling_tusschen_Suriname_en_Fransch-Guyana.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/KAARTEN_SGD_-_Kaart_van_de_Marowijne_van_af_de_samenvloeiing_der_Lawa_en_Tapanahoni_tot_Albina_met_de_grensregeling_tusschen_Suriname_en_Fransch-Guyana.jpeg/500px-KAARTEN_SGD_-_Kaart_van_de_Marowijne_van_af_de_samenvloeiing_der_Lawa_en_Tapanahoni_tot_Albina_met_de_grensregeling_tusschen_Suriname_en_Fransch-Guyana.jpeg 2x" data-file-width="1909" data-file-height="4440" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:KAARTEN_SGD_-_Kaart_van_de_Marowijne_van_af_de_samenvloeiing_der_Lawa_en_Tapanahoni_tot_Albina_met_de_grensregeling_tusschen_Suriname_en_Fransch-Guyana.jpeg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The section of the Maroni River on which the Netherlands and France concluded a border treaty in 1915.</div></div></div>
<p>In 1860, the question was posed from the French side, which of the two tributary rivers of the <a href="/wiki/Maroni_(river)" title="Maroni (river)">Marowijne River</a> (also called Maroni and Marowini) was the <a href="/wiki/Source_(river_or_stream)" class="mw-redirect" title="Source (river or stream)">headwater</a>, and thus the border. A joint French-Dutch commission was appointed to review the issue. The Dutch side of the commission consisted of J.H. Baron van Heerdt tot Eversberg, J.F.A. Cateau van Rosevelt and August Kappler. Luits Vidal, Ronmy, Boudet and Dr. Rech composed the French side. In 1861 measurements were taken, which produced the following result: the <a href="/wiki/Lawa_River_(South_America)" title="Lawa River (South America)">Lawa</a> had a <a href="/wiki/Discharge_(hydrology)" title="Discharge (hydrology)">flow rate</a> of 35,960 m<sup>3</sup>/minute at a width of 436&#160;m; the Tapanahony had a flow rate of 20,291 m<sup>3</sup>/minute at a width of 285&#160; m. Thus, the Lawa River was the headwater of the Maroni River.
</p><p>There were no problems with this decision until 1885, when the discovery of gold in the area between the Lawa and the Tapanahony created a new border conflict. On November 29, 1888, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> reached an agreement that the conflict should be subject to arbitration. Czar <a href="/wiki/Alexander_III_of_Russia" title="Alexander III of Russia">Alexander III of Russia</a>, acting as the arbitrator, decided that the Lawa was the headwater of the Maroni, and thus should be considered the border. The Netherlands and France concluded a border treaty on this section of the river on 30 September 1915.
</p><p>However, this decision created another issue as to which river is the source of the Lawa. The Netherlands considered the Marowini (the eastern tributary river) to be the source of the Lawa; the French considered the <a href="/wiki/Litani_River_(South_America)" class="mw-redirect" title="Litani River (South America)">Litani</a>, located further to the west, to be the source of the Lawa. This issue has still not been resolved.
</p><p>The <i>Litani</i> originates in the Tumuk Humak Mountains at approximately 2½°&#160;N 55°&#160;W; along its path it is fed by <i>Koele Koelekreek</i>, the <i>Lokekreek</i>, the <i>Mapaonikreek</i> and the <i>Oelemari</i>.
</p><p>The <i>Marowini</i> also has its source in the Tumuk Humak Mountains, at approximately 2°&#160;N, 54°&#160;W; it also absorbs the <i>Koelebreek</i>, among others.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Western_border">Western border</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borders_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Western border">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Boundary_lines_of_British_Guiana_1896.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Boundary_lines_of_British_Guiana_1896.jpg/250px-Boundary_lines_of_British_Guiana_1896.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="375" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Boundary_lines_of_British_Guiana_1896.jpg/375px-Boundary_lines_of_British_Guiana_1896.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Boundary_lines_of_British_Guiana_1896.jpg/500px-Boundary_lines_of_British_Guiana_1896.jpg 2x" data-file-width="925" data-file-height="1388" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Boundary_lines_of_British_Guiana_1896.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The boundaries of British Guiana according to the Schomburgk survey.</div></div></div>
<p>According to an agreement between Suriname governor <a href="/wiki/Cornelis_van_Aerssen_van_Sommelsdijck" title="Cornelis van Aerssen van Sommelsdijck">Cornelis van Aerssen van Sommelsdijck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Berbice" title="Berbice">Berbice</a> governor <a href="/wiki/Abraham_van_Pere" class="mw-redirect" title="Abraham van Pere">Abraham van Pere</a>&#8212;both were Dutch colonies at the time&#8212;the border between the two colonies was located at Devil Creek, between the <a href="/wiki/Berbice_River" title="Berbice River">Berbice River</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Courantyne_River" title="Courantyne River">Courantyne River</a>. In 1799, however, Berbice governor <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Jacob_van_Imbijze_van_Batenburg" title="Abraham Jacob van Imbijze van Batenburg">Abraham Jacob van Imbijze van Batenburg</a> and Suriname governor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jurriaan_Fran%C3%A7ois_de_Friderici&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jurriaan François de Friderici (page does not exist)">Jurriaan François de Friderici</a> signed an agreement in which the western <a href="/wiki/Bank_(geography)" title="Bank (geography)">bank</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Courantyne_River" title="Courantyne River">Courantyne River</a> was demarcated as the boundary. All islands in the Courantyne, as well as the post at <a href="/wiki/Orealla" title="Orealla">Orealla</a>, were deemed to be in Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As the <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Dutch_Treaty_of_1814" title="Anglo-Dutch Treaty of 1814">Anglo-Dutch Treaty of 1814</a> returned Suriname to Dutch rule in the boundaries of 1 January 1803, the Courantyne became the new boundary between <a href="/wiki/British_Guiana" title="British Guiana">British Guiana</a> and Suriname.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_River_Triangle">New River Triangle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borders_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: New River Triangle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Tigri_Area" title="Tigri Area">Tigri Area</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Robert_Schomburgk" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Schomburgk">Robert Schomburgk</a> surveyed <a href="/wiki/British_Guiana" title="British Guiana">British Guiana</a>'s borders in 1840. Taking the <a href="/wiki/Courantyne_River" title="Courantyne River">Courantyne River</a> as the border, he sailed up to which he deemed its source, the <a href="/wiki/Kutari_River" title="Kutari River">Kutari River</a>, in order to delineate the boundary. In 1871, however, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Barrington_Brown" title="Charles Barrington Brown">Charles Barrington Brown</a> discovered the <a href="/wiki/New_River_(Guyana)" class="mw-redirect" title="New River (Guyana)">New River</a> or Upper Courantyne, which is the source of the Courantyne. Thus the New River Triangle dispute was born.
</p><p>The tribunal which dealt with the <a href="/wiki/Venezuela_Crisis_of_1895" class="mw-redirect" title="Venezuela Crisis of 1895">Venezuela Crisis of 1895</a> also illegally awarded the New River Triangle to British Guiana. The Netherlands, however, raised a diplomatic protest, claiming that the New River, and not the Kutari, was to be regarded as the source of the Courantyne and the boundary. The British government in 1900 replied that the issue was already settled by the long acceptance of the Kutari as the boundary.
</p><p>In 1936, a Mixed Commission established by the British and Dutch government agreed to award the full width of the Courantyne River to Suriname, as per the 1799 agreement. The territorial sea boundary was deemed to prolongate 10° from Point No. 61, 6&#160;km (3&#160;nmi) from the <a href="/wiki/Shore" title="Shore">shore</a>. The New River Triangle, however, was completely awarded to Guyana. The treaty putting this agreement into law was never ratified, because of the outbreak of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1936, the Dutch representative <a href="/w/index.php?title=Conrad_Carel_K%C3%A4yser&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Conrad Carel Käyser (page does not exist)">Conrad Carel Käyser</a> signed an agreement with British and Brazilian representatives, placing the tri-point junction near the source of the Kutari River.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Desiring to put the border issue to a closure before <a href="/wiki/British_Guiana" title="British Guiana">British Guiana</a> would gain independence, the British government restarted negotiations in 1961. The British position asserted "Dutch sovereignty over the Corentyne River, a 10° line dividing the territorial sea, and British control over the New River Triangle."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Netherlands replied with a formal claim to the New River Triangle, but with a <i><a href="/wiki/Thalweg" title="Thalweg">thalweg</a></i> boundary in the Courantyne River (the latter position has never been repeated by any Surinamese government). No agreements were made and Guyana became independent with its borders unresolved. In 1969 border skirmishes occurred between Guyanese forces and Surinamese militias. In 1971, the Surinamese and Guyanese government agreed in Trinidad to withdraw military forces from the Triangle. Guyana still maintains a portion of Suriname’s territory in the southwest of the country occupied and performs management actions from there.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Maritime_dispute">Maritime dispute</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borders_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Maritime dispute">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The maritime boundary has long been disputed between Guyana and Suriname as well, and led in 2000 to skirmishes between Guyanese oil explorers and Surinamese coast guards.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Guyana claimed a <i>thalweg</i> boundary of the <a href="/wiki/Courantyne_River" title="Courantyne River">Courantyne River</a> (probably inspired by the 1962 Dutch position), and a 35° line from the true North, from the mouth of the river. Suriname claimed sovereignty over the full width of the Courantyne and a 10° dividing line. Eventually a five-member tribunal was convened under the rules set out in <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea/Annex_VII" class="extiw" title="s:United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea/Annex VII">Annex VII</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Convention_on_the_Law_of_the_Sea" title="United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea">United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea</a>, which in 2007 set out its own boundary that differed from both parties' claims. The maritime border was finally settled by international arbitration.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The tribunal did award sovereignty over the full width of the Courantyne to Suriname, and also awarded Suriname with a 10° territorial sea boundary 6&#160;km (3&#160;nmi) from the shore, according to the 1936 agreement. The rest of the territorial sea boundary, which extends 22&#160;km (12&#160;nmi) from the shore under modern international law, and the boundary separating the <a href="/wiki/Exclusive_Economic_Zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Exclusive Economic Zone">Exclusive Economic Zones</a> of both countries, was awarded according to the <a href="/wiki/Equidistance_principle" title="Equidistance principle">principle of equidistance</a>, however.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Southern_border">Southern border</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Borders_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Southern border">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The southern border with <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> is described in the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Limits_(Brazil%E2%80%93Netherlands)" title="Treaty of Limits (Brazil–Netherlands)">Treaty of Limits</a> of 1906 as "formed from the French border <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a> to the British border <a href="/wiki/British_Guiana" title="British Guiana">British Guiana</a>, the line of the <a href="/wiki/Drainage_divide" title="Drainage divide">drainage divide</a> (watershed) between the Amazon basin to the south, and the basins of the rivers flowing into north to the Atlantic Ocean." The border described in the treaty was the result of an <a href="/wiki/Arbitration" title="Arbitration">arbitration</a> process that was headed by King <a href="/wiki/Victor_Emmanuel_III_of_Italy" title="Victor Emmanuel III of Italy">Victor Emmanuel III</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">Italy</a>. The treaty was signed in <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a> on May 5, 1906. Brazil and the Netherlands both ratified the treaty in 1908.
</p>
