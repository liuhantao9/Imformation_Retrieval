</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Introduction">Introduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_Literature_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Introduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The festival runs author talks, creative writing classes and competitions, multi-arts performances, LGBT events, poetry nights and open mic events, plus a Literary Afternoon Tea. 
</p><p>Events take place at a number of venues around Huddersfield, including <a href="/wiki/The_University_of_Huddersfield" class="mw-redirect" title="The University of Huddersfield">The University of Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Batley_Theatre" title="Lawrence Batley Theatre">Lawrence Batley Theatre</a>, Huddersfield Town Hall, <a href="/wiki/The_Media_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="The Media Centre">The Media Centre</a>, libraries and local bars/cafes.
</p><p>The Festival is committed to showcasing BAME and LGBT performers as well as those with disabilities. It provides Access Guides to key venues and includes several subtitled events every year. Many events are free or low cost.
</p><p>Festival patrons are <a href="/wiki/Lemn_Sissay" title="Lemn Sissay">Lemn Sissay</a> MBE and Sir <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Stewart" title="Patrick Stewart">Patrick Stewart</a> OBE.
</p><p>In 2016 and 2017, the Festival was chosen as a finalist in <a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_Yorkshire" title="Welcome to Yorkshire">Welcome to Yorkshire</a>'s White Rose Awards and in May 2017 it won a <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Daily_Examiner" title="Huddersfield Daily Examiner">Huddersfield Examiner</a> Community Award (Arts category).
</p><p>The next Huddersfield Literature Festival is scheduled to run in March 2019.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Huddersfield_Literature_Festival&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally conceived in 2006 the festival is run as a not-for-profit organisation, with funding from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Huddersfield" title="University of Huddersfield">University of Huddersfield</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Arts_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Arts Council">Arts Council</a>, with business sponsorship from local firms.
</p><p>From 2009-2011 the festival was headed up by creative writing tutor and author Michael Stewart. In 2012 it took a break, but was relaunched in 2013 with a new Festival Director Michelle Hodgson of <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://key-words.co.uk/">Key Words</a>. In April 2013, Michelle featured in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent on Sunday</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s <a href="/wiki/Happy_List" title="Happy List">Happy List</a> for her work with the festival.
</p><p>Over the years, the festival has welcomed a diverse range of authors, poets and performers (see below). 
</p>
