<p><b>Michael Ashwin Satyandre Adhin</b> (born 10 June 1980)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a Surinamese educator, politician, and the <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_Suriname" title="Vice President of Suriname">Vice President of Suriname</a> since 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He is a member of the <a href="/wiki/National_Democratic_Party_(Suriname)" title="National Democratic Party (Suriname)">National Democratic Party</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bndestem_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bndestem-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At the age of 36, he became the youngest Vice President in the history of Suriname to lead the Council of Ministers.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Adhin is a <a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> of <a href="/wiki/Indo-Surinamese" title="Indo-Surinamese">Indian</a> descent.
</p><p>In July 2013 Adhin became Minister of Education in the cabinet of President <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a>, replacing Shirley Sitaldin.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After the <a href="/wiki/2015_Surinamese_general_election" title="2015 Surinamese general election">2015 Surinamese general election</a>, in which Adhin was elected to the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(Suriname)" title="National Assembly (Suriname)">National Assembly</a> for the electoral district of Paramaribo, he was inaugurated as Vice President on 12 August 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-bndestem_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bndestem-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_career">Early life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashwin_Adhin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashwin_Adhin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Adhin obtained a <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science" title="Bachelor of Science">BSc</a> degree in  in Electrical Engineering (Information Technology) from the <a href="/wiki/Anton_de_Kom_University_of_Suriname" title="Anton de Kom University of Suriname">Anton de Kom University of Suriname</a> (AdeKUS) in 2001, and subsequently a Dutch <a href="/wiki/Engineer%27s_degree#The_Netherlands" title="Engineer&#39;s degree">Engineers's degree</a> (Ir.) in Electrical Engineering (Telecommunications) from the <a href="/wiki/Delft_University_of_Technology" title="Delft University of Technology">Delft University of Technology</a> in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-srherald.com_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-srherald.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-a_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-a-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before embarking on a political career, he served as an educator at the <a href="/wiki/Anton_de_Kom_University_of_Suriname" title="Anton de Kom University of Suriname">Anton de Kom University of Suriname</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Chair_Cultural_Union_Suriname_.28CUS.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Chair_Cultural_Union_Suriname_(CUS)">Chair Cultural Union Suriname (CUS)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashwin_Adhin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chair Cultural Union Suriname (CUS)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In May 2011 Adhin was appointed the new chairman of the Cultural Union Suriname and remained onboard <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.starnieuws.com/index.php/welcome/index/nieuwsitem/21482">until February 2014</a>. Adhin who has been active within social cultural organizations for the past 13 years, remains to place emphasis on the importance of the role of culture for development, consciousness creation, appreciation of one's heritage, and for stimulating the achievement of common goals through active and collective participation of individuals.<sup id="cite_ref-starnieuws.com_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-starnieuws.com-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It was his leadership qualities during the preparation of the 140 years Indian Immigration national festivities, which caught the attention of <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">President Bouterse</a>, who later on appointed him Minister of Education and Community Development in July 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-srherald.com_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-srherald.com-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Adhin has also been an active member of the <a href="/wiki/Vishva_Hindu_Parishad" title="Vishva Hindu Parishad">Vishva Hindu Parishad</a> Suriname and foundation Mata Gauri.<sup id="cite_ref-starnieuws.com_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-starnieuws.com-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Minister_of_Education_and_Community_Development">Minister of Education and Community Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashwin_Adhin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Minister of Education and Community Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 2013 to 2015 Adhin served as Minister of Education and Community Development. He announced a total restructuring of the Ministry to improve the results within the education sector in Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-b_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-b-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Suriname_National_Training_Authority">Suriname National Training Authority</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashwin_Adhin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Suriname National Training Authority">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August 2013 Adhin launched the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Technical_Vocational_Education_and_Training&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Technical Vocational Education and Training (page does not exist)">Technical Vocational Education and Training</a> (TVET) method of education in Suriname. A taskforce was installed on November 1, 2013, to work towards the establishment of a National Training Authority making use of an existing Caricom strategy.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> This national authority was to be responsible for closing the gap between the education system and the demand of the private sector labor markets, the certification of various professions as well as the quality assurance of courses within the Suriname education system.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ICT_in_Education">ICT in Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashwin_Adhin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: ICT in Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2014 Adhin introduced two ICT in Education courses, amongst which a 3 years course leading to the Bachelor in Education (B.ed) degree. The courses are meant to target educators and are in line with Adhin's policy priorities to upgrade and professionalize the sector and to prepare for technological developments and challenges within education in general. He brought international professionals on board to realize the plans within a period of 4 months.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-College_Bridging_Institute_and_Evening_School">Pre-College Bridging Institute and Evening School</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashwin_Adhin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Pre-College Bridging Institute and Evening School">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In April 2015 Adhin officially launched the opening of Suriname's first Pre-college Bridging Institute increasing the opportunities for more students to attend pre-college. The opening and launch of the Evening Pre-College also served the same purpose.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Young_Entrepreneurship">Young Entrepreneurship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashwin_Adhin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Young Entrepreneurship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As minister of education Adhin created opportunities for young entrepreneurs to invest in themselves, with the establishment of the Suriname National Training Authority, as well as several ICT training courses.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vice_President_of_Suriname">Vice President of Suriname</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashwin_Adhin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Vice President of Suriname">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_policy">Foreign policy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashwin_Adhin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Foreign policy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Relations_with_India">Relations with India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ashwin_Adhin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Relations with India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On January 9, 2017, Vice President Adhin met with the Prime Minister of India, <a href="/wiki/Narendra_Modi" title="Narendra Modi">Narendra Modi</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Bangalore" title="Bangalore">Bengaluru</a>, India, where they discussed strengthening the economic and technological bilateral cooperation between both states. Adhin emphasized that Suriname has a lot to of potential in the areas of agriculture, animal husbandry, palm oil, wood processing, besides those in the extractive industries (gold, crude oil and bauxite).<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Adhin's plans are to set up a chair on Ayurveda at the University of Suriname and National Institute for Natural and Holistic Therapies in collaboration with India. His plans include setting up medicinal plantations, research laboratories, factories to manufacture Ayurveda products for the Surinamese market and expanding to also cover the Latin American countries and Caribbean Islands . This vision of Adhin supports efforts to diversify the Surinamese economy but also the governments approach to make healthcare the most important priority. This latter is also emphasized by the Governments Health in All Policies (HIAP) programme.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a young accomplished individual of Indian origin, Adhin was invited to India as keynote speaker at the Youth Privasi Bharatiya Divas Convention held on January 7, 2016, which was part of the annual <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://pbdindia.gov.in/introduction">Privasi Bharatiya Divas (PBD) Convention</a>, a global platform for Indian Diaspora held from 8–9 January 2017 in Bengaluru, the capital of the state of <a href="/wiki/Karnataka" title="Karnataka">Karnataka</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-a_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-a-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
