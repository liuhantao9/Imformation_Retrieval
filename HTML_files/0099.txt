<p>"<b>I'll Stand by You</b>" is a song recorded by <a href="/wiki/The_Pretenders" title="The Pretenders">the Pretenders</a> from their sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Last_of_the_Independents" title="Last of the Independents">Last of the Independents</a></i> (1994). The song was written by <a href="/wiki/Chrissie_Hynde" title="Chrissie Hynde">Chrissie Hynde</a> and the songwriting team of <a href="/wiki/Tom_Kelly_(musician)" title="Tom Kelly (musician)">Tom Kelly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Billy_Steinberg" title="Billy Steinberg">Billy Steinberg</a>, and produced by <a href="/wiki/Ian_Stanley" title="Ian Stanley">Ian Stanley</a>. The song pledges love and faithful assistance in times of personal darkness.
</p><p>Released on July 21, 1994, "I'll Stand by You" reached the top 20 in several countries, including Australia, Belgium, Canada, Iceland, the United Kingdom and the United States. It also became a major hit for British girl group <a href="/wiki/Girls_Aloud" title="Girls Aloud">Girls Aloud</a> in 2004 and American country singer <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> in 2007, both times recorded as a <a href="/wiki/Charity_single" class="mw-redirect" title="Charity single">charity single</a>. Colombian singer <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a> performed the song for the live charity telethon <i><a href="/wiki/Hope_for_Haiti_Now" title="Hope for Haiti Now">Hope for Haiti Now</a></i> in 2010; her cover version was released on the charity benefit <a href="/wiki/Hope_for_Haiti_Now_(album)" title="Hope for Haiti Now (album)">album of the same name</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release_and_reception">Release and reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I%27ll_Stand_by_You&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Release and reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"I'll Stand by You" was released as the second single from the album <i><a href="/wiki/Last_of_the_Independents" title="Last of the Independents">Last of the Independents</a></i> (1994), and it reached number 16 on the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_charts" title="Billboard charts"><i>Billboard</i></a> <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100">Hot 100</a>, number 21 on <i>Billboard</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s <a href="/wiki/Modern_Rock_Tracks" class="mw-redirect" title="Modern Rock Tracks">Modern Rock Tracks</a> chart, and number 10 in the United Kingdom. It charted higher in the UK and the US than "Night in My Veins", the album's first single. The <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> for "I'll Stand by You" was released in 1994 and features Chrissie Hynde caring for an ill man.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
On the long-running BBC program Songwriters Circle, Hynde mentions her embarrassment at having setout to write a “hit.” She goes on to say that she felt better about the song after Noel Gallagher said “he wished he’d written it.” 
It was later featured in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Dawson%27s_Creek_episodes#Season_1:_1998" title="List of Dawson&#39;s Creek episodes">pilot episode</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Dawson%27s_Creek" title="Dawson&#39;s Creek">Dawson's Creek</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Since 2010 the song has been used in an appeal advert for the <a href="/wiki/NSPCC" class="mw-redirect" title="NSPCC">NSPCC</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and also the Girls Aloud version was used in an advert for Aptamil follow-on milk.
</p>

<p>In 2004, the British-Irish all-female <a href="/wiki/Pop_music" title="Pop music">pop</a> group <a href="/wiki/Girls_Aloud" title="Girls Aloud">Girls Aloud</a> recorded a cover of "I'll Stand by You" as the official <a href="/wiki/Charity_single" class="mw-redirect" title="Charity single">charity single</a> for the 2004 <a href="/wiki/Children_in_Need_2004" title="Children in Need 2004">Children in Need</a> fundraising campaign. Their version was produced by <a href="/wiki/Brian_Higgins_(producer)" title="Brian Higgins (producer)">Brian Higgins</a> and his production team <a href="/wiki/Xenomania" title="Xenomania">Xenomania</a>. The track was announced a single just ten days before its 15 November 2004 release. It became Girls Aloud's second number one on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a>, spending two weeks at the top position and receiving a silver certification from the <a href="/wiki/British_Phonographic_Industry" title="British Phonographic Industry">British Phonographic Industry</a>. The song appears on Girls Aloud's second studio album <i><a href="/wiki/What_Will_the_Neighbours_Say%3F" title="What Will the Neighbours Say?">What Will the Neighbours Say?</a></i> (2004).
</p><p>The music video, directed by <a href="/wiki/Trudy_Bellinger" title="Trudy Bellinger">Trudy Bellinger</a>, saw Girls Aloud stranded in a <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> as they "stand by" each other. "I'll Stand by You" was promoted through various live appearances and has since been performed on a number of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Girls_Aloud_concert_tours" title="List of Girls Aloud concert tours">the group's concert tours</a>. Despite its success, Girls Aloud's cover of the song was largely panned by contemporary music critics.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background_and_release">Background and release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I%27ll_Stand_by_You&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Background and release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first version of "I'll Stand by You" that Xenomania created was described as a "weird, modernist breakbeat version", which was scrapped upon deciding an updated version of the original song would be better suited for Children in Need.<sup id="cite_ref-boxset_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boxset-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The track's release was announced a single just ten days before its release date.<sup id="cite_ref-cbbc_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbbc-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Roberts" title="Nicola Roberts">Nicola Roberts</a> said, "Hopefully if our single does well it's a lot of money going to the charity."<sup id="cite_ref-cbbc_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbbc-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The single was released on 15 November 2004 in the UK, the week of the <a href="/wiki/Children_in_Need_2004" title="Children in Need 2004">Children in Need 2004</a> telethon. It was available on two different <a href="/wiki/CD_single" title="CD single">CD singles</a>. The first disc includes "Real Life", a song taken from Girls Aloud's second album <i><a href="/wiki/What_Will_the_Neighbours_Say%3F" title="What Will the Neighbours Say?">What Will the Neighbours Say?</a></i>. The second CD format featured a <a href="/wiki/Medley_(music)" class="mw-redirect" title="Medley (music)">medley</a> of songs taken from the album, as well as the Tony Lamezma's Club Romp remix of the track. The Gravitas Vocal Dub Mix Edit of "I'll Stand by You" appears on the "<a href="/wiki/Wake_Me_Up_(Girls_Aloud_song)" title="Wake Me Up (Girls Aloud song)">Wake Me Up</a>" single.
</p><p>In order to promote the release, Girls Aloud toured radio stations across the United Kingdom and performed an <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_music" title="Acoustic music">acoustic</a> arrangement of "I'll Stand by You" that they arranged themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-boxset_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boxset-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> They also visited <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_hospital" title="Children&#39;s hospital">children's hospitals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-cbbc_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cbbc-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I%27ll_Stand_by_You&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Girls Aloud's version of "I'll Stand By You" received negative reviews from music critics. It was called "a particular soggy area" on Girls Aloud's album.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Alexis Petridis of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i>, who praised Girls Aloud's album, said that "I'll Stand by You" was "a thing of death-dealing tedium."<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BBC_Music" title="BBC Music">BBC Music</a> felt it was "arguable whether this cover adds much to the Pretenders' original."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Sam Shepherd of <a href="/wiki/MusicOMH" title="MusicOMH">musicOMH</a> bluntly stated "perhaps the children most in need, are those that part with their pocket money for this drivel [...] This is a sub-karaoke version, limp, tired, and uninspired."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, <a href="/wiki/Stylus_Magazine" title="Stylus Magazine">Stylus Magazine</a> actually commended the song, comparing it to <a href="/wiki/Shakespears_Sister" title="Shakespears Sister">Shakespears Sister</a> and calling it "far, far better than anyone gives it credit for."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I%27ll_Stand_by_You&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>"I'll Stand by You" debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">UK Singles Chart</a> on 21 November 2004, becoming Girls Aloud's second single to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Despite competition from four new top ten entries, the song also remained at the top for a second week.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> It slipped to number four in its third week, making way for <a href="/wiki/Band_Aid_20" title="Band Aid 20">Band Aid 20</a>'s take on "<a href="/wiki/Do_They_Know_It%27s_Christmas%3F" title="Do They Know It&#39;s Christmas?">Do They Know It's Christmas?</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Girls Aloud spent a fourth week in the top five.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> The single spent two further weeks in the top ten, holding on at number nine.<sup id="cite_ref-chartstats_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chartstats-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Overall, "I'll Stand by You" spent fourteen weeks in the top 75, including nine weeks in the top forty.<sup id="cite_ref-chartstats_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chartstats-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-occ_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-occ-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The single was ranked thirty-fourth on the year-end chart (see <a href="/wiki/2004_in_British_music#Top_40_singles_of_2004" title="2004 in British music">2004 in British music</a>). It was certified silver by the <a href="/wiki/British_Phonographic_Industry" title="British Phonographic Industry">British Phonographic Industry</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bpi_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bpi-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song entered the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Singles_Chart" title="Irish Singles Chart">Irish Singles Chart</a> at number four.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> It spent a second week at number four,<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> before rising to number three.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> It spent three more consecutive weeks at number three.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> It then slipped back down to number four,<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> and spent two more weeks in the top five.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I%27ll_Stand_by_You&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">video</a> for Girls Aloud's version of "I'll Stand by You" was directed by <a href="/wiki/Trudy_Bellinger" title="Trudy Bellinger">Trudy Bellinger</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> It begins with each member of the group lying or sitting motionless in a parched desert. At the song's climax, a thunderstorm begins, and the members of Girls Aloud come together and stand in a circle. A special Children in Need version of the video depicts the group members holding "Pudsey" <a href="/wiki/Teddy_bear" title="Teddy bear">teddy bears</a>, the mascot of the organisation, towards the end of the video.
</p>
<div style="clear:left;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I%27ll_Stand_by_You&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Girls Aloud toured radio stations across the United Kingdom and performed an <a href="/wiki/Acoustic_music" title="Acoustic music">acoustic</a> arrangement of "I'll Stand by You" that they arranged themselves.<sup id="cite_ref-boxset_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boxset-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> As the charity's official single of the year, the song was showcased as the <a href="/wiki/Children_in_Need_2004" title="Children in Need 2004">Children in Need 2004</a> telethon. They also performed the song on television programmes such as <i><a href="/wiki/Blue_Peter" title="Blue Peter">Blue Peter</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/CD:UK" title="CD:UK">CD:UK</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Countdown_(game_show)" title="Countdown (game show)">Countdown</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_GMTV_programmes#Diggit/Diggin&#39;_It" title="List of GMTV programmes">Diggin' It</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/GMTV" title="GMTV">GMTV</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Holly_%26_Stephen%27s_Saturday_Showdown" title="Holly &amp; Stephen&#39;s Saturday Showdown">Ministry of Mayhem</a></i>, <i>Popbusters</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Top_of_the_Pops" title="Top of the Pops">Top of the Pops</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Xchange_(TV_series)" title="Xchange (TV series)">Xchange</a></i>. In 2005, Girls Aloud performed "I'll Stand by You" at <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>'s <a href="/wiki/ChildLine_Concert" title="ChildLine Concert">ChildLine Concert</a> and on the TV show <i>All Time Greatest Love Songs</i>. In 2006, they performed the song on Australia's <i><a href="/wiki/Sunrise_(Australian_TV_program)" title="Sunrise (Australian TV program)">Sunrise</a></i>. The group also performed the cover version on their 2005 MTV special (as seen on the <i><a href="/wiki/Girls_on_Film_(DVD)" class="mw-redirect" title="Girls on Film (DVD)">Girls on Film</a></i> DVD) and <a href="/wiki/ITV1" class="mw-redirect" title="ITV1">ITV1</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Girls_Aloud_Party" title="The Girls Aloud Party">The Girls Aloud Party</a></i> (2008).
</p><p>"I'll Stand by You" has been performed by Girls Aloud on most of their concert tours. For 2005's <a href="/wiki/What_Will_the_Neighbours_Say%3F_Live" class="mw-redirect" title="What Will the Neighbours Say? Live">What Will the Neighbours Say? Live</a>, Girls Aloud's first tour, they sang the song in colourful, simple <a href="/wiki/Evening_gown" title="Evening gown">evening gowns</a>. It was also included on 2006's <a href="/wiki/Chemistry_Tour" class="mw-redirect" title="Chemistry Tour">Chemistry Tour</a> and 2007's <a href="/wiki/The_Sound_of_Girls_Aloud:_The_Greatest_Hits_Tour" class="mw-redirect" title="The Sound of Girls Aloud: The Greatest Hits Tour">The Sound of Girls Aloud: The Greatest Hits Tour</a>. "I'll Stand by You" was included on a ballad section on 2008's <a href="/wiki/Tangled_Up_Tour" title="Tangled Up Tour">Tangled Up Tour</a>, in which Girls Aloud walk along a <a href="/wiki/Catwalk" class="mw-redirect" title="Catwalk">catwalk</a> which reaches the middle of the crowd.
</p>

<p>"<b>I'll Stand by You</b>" was covered in 2007 by <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> as a charity single, recorded for the <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> special, <i><a href="/wiki/Idol_Gives_Back" title="Idol Gives Back">Idol Gives Back</a></i> which aired on April 25, 2007.
</p><p>Carrie Underwood performed the song during her visit to <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, which was used as soundtrack for a video vignette, where Underwood is seen singing to children and doing different activities with them. The single and the video were released on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a> on April 26, 2007. <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Seacrest" title="Ryan Seacrest">Ryan Seacrest</a> announced that all money made on <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital downloads</a> would be donated to charity to help millions of poor children at the time.
</p><p>On its first few days of release, the song sold 124,496 <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital downloads</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> prompting a debut on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> at number six,<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> Underwood's highest Hot 100 position since her 2005 debut single "<a href="/wiki/Inside_Your_Heaven" title="Inside Your Heaven">Inside Your Heaven</a>", which debuted at number one.
</p><p>It was eventually removed from <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">iTunes</a>, leading the single to have one of the biggest falls in Hot 100 history. The single collected over 300,000 digital downloads in the time period it was available for download. As announced, all profits from the sale of the single were donated to charity. The single also received unsolicited airplay on U.S. <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> radio stations, despite not being officially released to country radio.
</p><p>During one of the elimination episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(Season_6)" class="mw-redirect" title="American Idol (Season 6)">Season Six</a>, the song was used as the send-off song for contestant <a href="/wiki/Phil_Stacey" title="Phil Stacey">Phil Stacey</a> (the first of two eliminated contestants that week) in lieu of <a href="/wiki/Daughtry_(band)" title="Daughtry (band)">Daughtry's</a> "<a href="/wiki/Home_(Daughtry_song)" title="Home (Daughtry song)">Home</a>," the show's main send-off song that season. Underwood performed the song during the Idol Finale on May 23, 2007.
</p><p>Underwood's version was used in a commercial for the <a href="/wiki/ASPCA" class="mw-redirect" title="ASPCA">ASPCA</a> in 2019.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video_2">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I%27ll_Stand_by_You&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Music_video" title="Music video">music video</a> was filmed in Africa. Underwood is seen singing to young children and helping them with different art projects, such as painting. At one point in the video, she is seen walking into a <a href="/wiki/Cemetery" title="Cemetery">cemetery</a> with a girl on her arms, and a boy by her hand who is carrying flowers. Underwood is also seen crying towards the end of the video, when she is helping an ill person. This music video was made available for download on the U.S. iTunes Store.
</p>

<p>"<b>I'll Stand by You</b>" is a cover in 2010 by the <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombian</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a> as a promotional charity single for the <a href="/wiki/Hope_for_Haiti_Now" title="Hope for Haiti Now">Hope for Haiti Now</a> campaign<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> and was released on 23 January 2010, in the wake of the devastating <a href="/wiki/2010_Haiti_earthquake" title="2010 Haiti earthquake">2010 Haiti earthquake</a>. Recorded live on the charity <a href="/wiki/Telethon" title="Telethon">telethon</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hope_for_Haiti_Now:_A_Global_Benefit_for_Earthquake_Relief" class="mw-redirect" title="Hope for Haiti Now: A Global Benefit for Earthquake Relief">Hope for Haiti Now: A Global Benefit for Earthquake Relief</a></i> on January 22, 2010 in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. She performed the song during the program, exactly before <a href="/wiki/Wyclef_Jean" title="Wyclef Jean">Wyclef Jean</a>'s speech and after <a href="/wiki/Reese_Witherspoon" title="Reese Witherspoon">Reese Witherspoon</a>'s call on the phone bank. The song is found on the charity benefit album, <a href="/wiki/Hope_for_Haiti_Now_(album)" title="Hope for Haiti Now (album)"><i>Hope for Haiti Now</i></a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_notable_cover_versions">Other notable cover versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=I%27ll_Stand_by_You&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Other notable cover versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Patti_LaBelle" title="Patti LaBelle">Patti LaBelle</a> covered the song for her 2005 album <i><a href="/wiki/Classic_Moments" title="Classic Moments">Classic Moments</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rod_Stewart" title="Rod Stewart">Rod Stewart</a> covered it for his 2006 album <i><a href="/wiki/Still_the_Same..._Great_Rock_Classics_of_Our_Time" title="Still the Same... Great Rock Classics of Our Time">Still the Same... Great Rock Classics of Our Time</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> The <i><a href="/wiki/Glee_(TV_series)" title="Glee (TV series)">Glee</a></i> cast performed the song in an episode "<a href="/wiki/The_Quarterback_(Glee)" title="The Quarterback (Glee)">The Quarterback</a>" (2013), which pays tribute to the deceased character <a href="/wiki/Finn_Hudson" title="Finn Hudson">Finn Hudson</a>, portrayed by the late <a href="/wiki/Cory_Monteith" title="Cory Monteith">Cory Monteith</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The contestants on <a href="/wiki/The_Voice_(American_TV_series)" title="The Voice (American TV series)">US version of <i>The Voice</i></a> individually performed the song for one-on-one competitions. A contestant <a href="/wiki/Xenia_(singer)" title="Xenia (singer)">Xenia</a> won one round over Sara Oromchi in the <a href="/wiki/The_Voice_(American_season_1)" title="The Voice (American season 1)">first season</a> (2011).<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> A contestant Bria Kelly won one round over Madilyn Paige in the <a href="/wiki/The_Voice_(American_season_6)" title="The Voice (American season 6)">sixth season</a> (2014).<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup>
