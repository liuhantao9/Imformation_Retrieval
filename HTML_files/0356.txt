	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Brownsberg Nature Park</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Brownsberg Nature Park</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Nature_reserve" title="Nature reserve">nature reserve</a> located in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. The site measures 12,000 ha and is located in the district of <a href="/wiki/Brokopondo_District" title="Brokopondo District">Brokopondo</a> around 130&#160;km south from the capital city <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>. The 500 meter high Brownsberg is the central point of the park. The site is located near the <a href="/wiki/Brokopondo_Reservoir" title="Brokopondo Reservoir">Brokopondo Reservoir</a>. The park is managed by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stichting_Natuurbehoud_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stichting Natuurbehoud Suriname (page does not exist)">STINASU</a>. Often the people of Surinam use the word <b>Brownsberg</b> as simple variant of <b>Brownsberg Nature Park</b>. Local people also write it as <b>Bruijnsberg</b>, pronounced as <b>Braynsberg</b>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brownsberg_Nature_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Leo_val_brownsberg.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Leo_val_brownsberg.JPG/220px-Leo_val_brownsberg.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="331" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Leo_val_brownsberg.JPG/330px-Leo_val_brownsberg.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Leo_val_brownsberg.JPG/440px-Leo_val_brownsberg.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2000" data-file-height="3008" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Leo_val_brownsberg.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Leo waterfall</div></div></div>
<p>At the end of the 19th century gold miners entered the area now known as Brownsberg Nature Park. One of the first was John Brown after whom both the park as well as the mountain (<i>berg</i> meaning mountain in <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>) are named. After the miners left there was a failed attempt to mine <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a>.
Since 1969 the site has been a nature reserve and has been managed by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Stichting_Natuurbehoud_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Stichting Natuurbehoud Suriname (page does not exist)">STINASU</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-zuidame_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zuidame-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Gold mining is known to have occurred again since 1999. Action against the illegal miners was taken periodically, but high gold prices have led them to return.<sup id="cite_ref-wwf_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwf-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brownsberg_Nature_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The geomorphological history of the area led to the current biodiversity of the park. It also left the gold deposits.<sup id="cite_ref-wwf_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwf-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Brownsberg, located on the Mazaroni plateau, overlooks the <a href="/wiki/Brokopondo_Reservoir" title="Brokopondo Reservoir">Brokopondo Reservoir</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flora_and_fauna">Flora and fauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brownsberg_Nature_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Flora and fauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The park is in the <a href="/wiki/Guianan_moist_forests" title="Guianan moist forests">Guianan moist forests</a> ecoregion.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
It has an estimated 1450 species of plants and 350 species of birds. Amongst the species present are <a href="/wiki/Howler_monkey" title="Howler monkey">howler monkeys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grey-winged_trumpeter" title="Grey-winged trumpeter">grey-winged trumpeters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toucan" title="Toucan">toucans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Red-rumped_agouti" title="Red-rumped agouti">red-rumped agoutis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-zuidame_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zuidame-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Threats">Threats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brownsberg_Nature_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Threats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2012 the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature" title="World Wide Fund for Nature">World Wide Fund for Nature</a> published a report on the nature park which stated that it was severely threatened by illegal <a href="/wiki/Gold_mining" title="Gold mining">gold mining</a>. The organisation found fifty illegal sites of gold miners in three weeks. The number of illegal gold miners was estimated between 1500 and 2000 in the immediate years preceding 2012 by the head of the committee of Gold Mining Regulation. The number has risen due to higher gold prices.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mercury_(element)" title="Mercury (element)">Mercury</a> also attained the soil of the park.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tourism">Tourism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brownsberg_Nature_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tourism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The park receives around 20,000 visitors each year.<sup id="cite_ref-wwf_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wwf-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Near the mountain there are several sleeping accommodations, an information center and restaurant. Starting at the mountain there are several paths leading to waterfalls and other waters.<sup id="cite_ref-zuidame_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zuidame-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
