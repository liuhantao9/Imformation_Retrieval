<p><b>Wilko Retail Ltd.,</b> formerly <b>Wilkinson Cash Stores</b> (1930 - 1941) and <b>Wilkinson Hardware Stores</b> (1941 - 2014) is a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> <a href="/wiki/High_Street" title="High Street">high-street</a> <a href="/wiki/Chain_store" title="Chain store">chain</a> which sells homewares and household goods. The company was founded in <a href="/wiki/Leicester" title="Leicester">Leicester</a> by <a href="/wiki/James_Kemsey_Wilkinson" title="James Kemsey Wilkinson">James Kemsey Wilkinson</a> in 1930, and remains in the hands of the founding family today.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The founder's son <a href="/wiki/Tony_Wilkinson_(businessman)" title="Tony Wilkinson (businessman)">Tony Wilkinson</a> joined the company as a Branch Manager in 1960 and succeeded his father as <a href="/wiki/Chairman" class="mw-redirect" title="Chairman">chairman</a> in 1972, retiring in June 2005. He was replaced by his daughter, Lisa Wilkinson, and his niece, Karin Swann.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, Karin Swann sold her family's 50% holding in the business to Lisa Wilkinson.<sup id="cite_ref-Ruddick_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ruddick-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Product_range">Product range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilko_(retailer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Product range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Wilko product range concentrates on household essentials, including homewares, textiles, <a href="/wiki/DIY" class="mw-redirect" title="DIY">DIY</a>, cleaning products, health and beauty lines, stationery, confectionery, pet products and kitchen and bathroom goods. A large proportion of the range is made up of <a href="/wiki/Store_brand" class="mw-redirect" title="Store brand">own-label</a> products sold under the <i>Wilko</i> brand, but like other retailers, Wilko has started to use <a href="/wiki/Private_label" title="Private label">private label</a> brands. Wilko sells seasonal lines such as gardening products in the summer, as well as Christmas decorations and an expanded toys and games range from September to January.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilko_(retailer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wilko,_Kings_Mall,_Hammersmith_01.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Wilko%2C_Kings_Mall%2C_Hammersmith_01.jpg/220px-Wilko%2C_Kings_Mall%2C_Hammersmith_01.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Wilko%2C_Kings_Mall%2C_Hammersmith_01.jpg/330px-Wilko%2C_Kings_Mall%2C_Hammersmith_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Wilko%2C_Kings_Mall%2C_Hammersmith_01.jpg/440px-Wilko%2C_Kings_Mall%2C_Hammersmith_01.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4608" data-file-height="3456" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wilko,_Kings_Mall,_Hammersmith_01.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>2015 - present logo</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Now_called_Wilkinsons_-_geograph.org.uk_-_2128397.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Now_called_Wilkinsons_-_geograph.org.uk_-_2128397.jpg/220px-Now_called_Wilkinsons_-_geograph.org.uk_-_2128397.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Now_called_Wilkinsons_-_geograph.org.uk_-_2128397.jpg/330px-Now_called_Wilkinsons_-_geograph.org.uk_-_2128397.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Now_called_Wilkinsons_-_geograph.org.uk_-_2128397.jpg/440px-Now_called_Wilkinsons_-_geograph.org.uk_-_2128397.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Now_called_Wilkinsons_-_geograph.org.uk_-_2128397.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>2009 - 2014 logo</div></div></div>
<p>The first Wilkinson store was opened by James Kemsey and his <a href="/wiki/Fiancee" class="mw-redirect" title="Fiancee">fiancee</a> Mary Cooper at 151 Charnwood Street, Leicester, in 1930, and a second store was opened in <a href="/wiki/Wigston_Magna" class="mw-redirect" title="Wigston Magna">Wigston Magna</a>, also in Leicester, in 1932. A total of nine branches had been opened by 1939.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Beaconsfield" title="Beaconsfield">Beaconsfield</a> store can be seen in the background in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Brief_Encounter" title="Brief Encounter">Brief Encounter</a></i> (1945). By the end of the 1980s, the Wilkinson chain had a total of 78 stores, increasing to over 150 by the time of the founder's death, in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wilkinson opened its Scottish outlet in <a href="/wiki/Castle_Douglas" title="Castle Douglas">Castle Douglas</a> on 23 January 2009, in a store formerly occupied by <a href="/wiki/The_Co-operative_Group" title="The Co-operative Group">The Co-operative Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Stores were subsequently opened in Scotland that same year in <a href="/wiki/Motherwell" title="Motherwell">Motherwell</a> on 14 July, in <a href="/wiki/Irvine,_North_Ayrshire" title="Irvine, North Ayrshire">Irvine</a> in November and in <a href="/wiki/Clydebank" title="Clydebank">Clydebank</a> on 2 December. In 2010, the stores planned for Scotland to open were <a href="/wiki/Hamilton,_South_Lanarkshire" title="Hamilton, South Lanarkshire">Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kilmarnock" title="Kilmarnock">Kilmarnock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greenock" title="Greenock">Greenock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Livingston,_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Livingston, Scotland">Livingston</a>.
</p><p>Wilkinson has a trend of taking over redundant former stores rather than building new ones, particularly in the <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(county)" title="West Midlands (county)">West Midlands</a>; examples have included the former <a href="/wiki/Kwik_Save" title="Kwik Save">Kwik Save</a> store in <a href="/wiki/Great_Bridge,_West_Midlands" title="Great Bridge, West Midlands">Great Bridge</a> (May 2008),<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> the former <a href="/wiki/Safeway_(UK)" title="Safeway (UK)">Safeway</a> store in <a href="/wiki/Halesowen" title="Halesowen">Halesowen</a> (November 2006)<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and the former <a href="/wiki/Marks_%26_Spencer" title="Marks &amp; Spencer">Marks &amp; Spencer</a> store in <a href="/wiki/Dudley" title="Dudley">Dudley</a> (July 1991). The opening of a store in Dudley was an example of a trend of the retailer to take over large units in town centres left vacant by the relocation of big retail names to out of town locations, as Marks &amp; Spencer had closed its Dudley store in 1990, in favour of a new store at the nearby <a href="/wiki/Merry_Hill_Shopping_Centre" title="Merry Hill Shopping Centre">Merry Hill Shopping Centre</a>.
</p><p>The Dudley store was so successful that a £250,000 expansion to the upper level of the building (initially, only the ground floor was used) was completed three years after its opening. By then, Wilkinson was one of Britain's fastest growing retailers.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, Wilkinson began rebranding its stores as Wilko, after its own brand products marketed under the <i>Wilko</i> name, and by 2014, most stores had been rebranded.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>One side of the Wilkinson family, Karin Swann, sold her 50 percent share of the business  to the other side of the family in 2014, leaving Tony Wilkinson, his wife Christine and Lisa Wilkinson the sole owners of the company and Lisa Wilkinson the sole chairman. Swann wanted to pursue other business interests and the decision did not represent a falling out of the family.<sup id="cite_ref-Ruddick_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ruddick-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2017, Wilko began negotiations with <a href="/wiki/GMB_(trade_union)" title="GMB (trade union)">GMB</a> over their plan to remove over 4,000 positions. This was contrasted unfavourably with reports that Lisa Wilkinson had just received a £3m dividend.<sup id="cite_ref-ftwilkolooksatcuttingmorethan_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ftwilkolooksatcuttingmorethan-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warehouses_and_distribution">Warehouses and distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilko_(retailer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Warehouses and distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company's first <a href="/wiki/Warehouse" title="Warehouse">warehouse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Office" title="Office">offices</a> opened in 1938 and were located in existing premises in <a href="/wiki/Syston" title="Syston">Syston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charnwood_(borough)" class="mw-redirect" title="Charnwood (borough)">Charnwood</a>. Wilko's first purpose-built warehouse on Brighton Road, Leicester, opened in 1937. In 1970 the head office and warehouse were relocated to <a href="/wiki/Carlton_in_Lindrick" title="Carlton in Lindrick">Carlton-in-Lindrick</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nottinghamshire" title="Nottinghamshire">Nottinghamshire</a>, and a 'high-bay' stacking system was installed. Wilko's current main <a href="/wiki/Distribution_center" title="Distribution center">distribution centre</a> (DC1) opened in 1995 and is located in Manton Wood, <a href="/wiki/Worksop" title="Worksop">Worksop</a>, Nottinghamshire. At the time of opening, it had the fastest sorting system in the UK. A second distribution centre (DC2) was opened in <a href="/wiki/Magor,_Monmouthshire" title="Magor, Monmouthshire">Magor</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Wales" title="South Wales">South Wales</a> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The company awarded a five-year logistics contract to <a href="/wiki/Wincanton_plc" title="Wincanton plc">Wincanton plc</a> in March 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing_and_brand">Marketing and brand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilko_(retailer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Marketing and brand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Advertising is concentrated in the press, such as inserts included with local newspapers. Advertising emphasises value for money, with in-store promotion encouraging customers to purchase more than one item when they visit the store.
</p><p>A revamped version of the brand was unveiled in December 2008. Designed by Jupiter Creative,<sup id="cite_ref-jupiter_creative_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jupiter_creative-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> the brand was showcased in new format stores in <a href="/wiki/Thornaby" class="mw-redirect" title="Thornaby">Thornaby</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northallerton" title="Northallerton">Northallerton</a> (which replaced a former Woolworths store), <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leicester" title="Leicester">Leicester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Newton_Aycliffe" title="Newton Aycliffe">Newton Aycliffe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walton-on-Thames" title="Walton-on-Thames">Walton-on-Thames</a> as well as the <a href="/wiki/Castle_Douglas" title="Castle Douglas">Castle Douglas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Motherwell" title="Motherwell">Motherwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clydebank" title="Clydebank">Clydebank</a> stores in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.
</p>
