<p><b>Metro</b> is the passenger information brand used by the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Combined_Authority" title="West Yorkshire Combined Authority">West Yorkshire Combined Authority</a> in England. It was formed on 1 April 1974 as the <b>West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive</b> (<b>WYPTE</b>) at the same time as the metropolitan county of West Yorkshire.<sup id="cite_ref-tenyears_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tenyears-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <i>Metro</i> brand has been used from the outset, and since the formal abolition of the WYPTE on 1 April 2014, it has been the public facing name of the organisation. The transport authority of West Yorkshire, responsible for setting transport policy, is the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Combined_Authority" title="West Yorkshire Combined Authority">West Yorkshire Combined Authority</a>. The WYCA is also responsible for delivery of transport policies.
</p>

<p>Metro is a public transport brand of the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Combined_Authority" title="West Yorkshire Combined Authority">West Yorkshire Combined Authority</a> which is, through its transport committee, the transport authority for <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>. It replaced the West Yorkshire Integrated Transport Authority on 1 April 2014. The <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_County_Council" title="West Yorkshire County Council">West Yorkshire County Council</a> was the transport authority from 1 April 1974 until 1 April 1986. It was replaced by the West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Authority that was made up of elected councillors from the districts of West Yorkshire. The West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Authority was renamed the West Yorkshire Integrated Transport Authority following the <a href="/wiki/Local_Transport_Act_2008" title="Local Transport Act 2008">Local Transport Act 2008</a>. The Metro brand was adopted in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="MetroBus_History">MetroBus History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: MetroBus History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Buses are operated by private companies, with early morning, late evening, Sunday and rural services often supported by Metro. There is a special rural bus section, which promotes a combination of minor local links and major long distance routes.
</p><p>On 1 April 1974, the West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Executive was created by merging the municipal bus fleets of Bradford City Transport, Leeds City Transport, Huddersfield Joint Omnibus Committee and Halifax Joint Omnibus Committee, which earlier in the 1970s swallowed up Todmorden Joint Omnibus Committee. The operation was divided into four districts and a new livery of cream and verona green replaced the Bradford light blue &amp; cream, Huddersfield red &amp; cream, Leeds two-tone green and Halifax &amp; Calderdale orange, green &amp; cream.                                                                                                                            Created following the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> the Executive had to operate within the policy guidelines of the County Council Public Transport Committee, coordinating the operation of all public transport in the county. The Executive inherited approximately 1,500 buses along with 6,000 staff and the associated garages and street furniture.
</p><p>The Executive relinquished ownership of local buses following the <a href="/wiki/Transport_Act_1985" title="Transport Act 1985">Transport Act 1985</a>, creating arms-length operating companies. It continued to coordinate public transport as the West Yorkshire Passenger Transport Authority when the metropolitan county was abolished in 1986.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
New buses were purchased in large numbers at the outset. In 1976 Baddeley Brothers of <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth" title="Holmfirth">Holmfirth</a> was purchased providing the PTE with additional coaching and stage-carriage duties. In 1980 the Baddeley Brothers business was also disposed of, although the Metrocoach operation was retained.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleet_livery">Fleet livery</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fleet livery">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1976 modifications were made to the livery. Originally there were three stripes at the sides of the destination box, which wrapped round to the sides and swept down. This took time to apply, and a trial was made with one thin line. In 1977 the lines were removed and the green area at the skirting of was raised up, so there was slightly more green. The other change was the fleet name to MetroBus in 1976, removing the district names.
</p><p>On 25 April 1977, the PTE acquired the old-established <a href="/wiki/Kinsley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Kinsley, West Yorkshire">Kinsley</a> based United Services from WR &amp; P Bingley. As well as providing the PTE with more coaching operations, this took it into an area of West Yorkshire where it had previously had no presence. United Services was maintained as a separate subsidiary and retained its distinctive blue livery, whilst a new livery of red &amp; ivory was adopted for the PTE's coaches, which operated under the "Metrocoach" banner, with brown added for "Metrocoach Executive". Later Bingley's depot received double-deckers transferred from the Leeds District. In early 1981 a reorganisation of operating districts was implemented with the East District becoming responsible for the <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> depots and United Services, whilst the West District took control of <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Todmorden" title="Todmorden">Todmorden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>. Three new <a href="/wiki/Leyland_National" title="Leyland National">Leyland National</a> 2s were acquired for United Services, in blue livery.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_integrated_bus_system">New integrated bus system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: New integrated bus system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In July 1981, MetroBus and the <a href="/wiki/National_Bus_Company_(UK)" title="National Bus Company (UK)">National Bus Company</a> (NBC) formed a new integrated transport system known as the "Metro-National Transport Company Limited". All PTE and NBC buses began to appear with a new emblem, which consisted of the MetroBus WY's in one box and the NBC "double N" or "N-blem" appearing in another to the right of the PTE emblem, and slightly lower. The boxes were linked to show the integration. They also appeared with MetroBus fleetnames with "<i>The easy way from here to there in West Yorkshire</i>". The new "Metrobus" fleetname being applied not only to PTE owned vehicles on which WYPTE lettering was carried beneath the fleet name, but also buses of NBC subsidiaries <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Road_Car_Company" title="West Yorkshire Road Car Company">West Yorkshire Road Car Company</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_Automobile_Company" title="West Riding Automobile Company">West Riding Automobile Company</a>, Yorkshire Woollen Transport Company and <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Traction" title="Yorkshire Traction">Yorkshire Traction</a>, carrying "West Yorkshire", "West Riding", "Yorkshire" and "Yorkshire Traction" names below the Metrobus name. Some years later some of those buses (excluding Yorkshire Traction) were repainted into the PTEs verona cream and buttermilk livery so as to present a corporate image. From this date the "WY" logo on the front of buses was replaced by the "Metro-National" emblem in mid-1983, to celebrate 100 years of public transport in Huddersfield, MetroBus paint two vehicles in old liveries: <a href="/wiki/Leyland_Atlantean" title="Leyland Atlantean">Leyland Atlanteans</a> carried Huddersfield Corporation red livery and <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Corporation_Tramways" title="Huddersfield Corporation Tramways">Huddersfield Corporation Tramways</a> livery. They became "Building on a Great Tradition" vehicles and were in those liveries until the late 1990s.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Deregulation">Deregulation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Deregulation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Yorkshire_Rider_bus_5507_(C507_KBT),_TYPG_2009_Leeds_bus_rally.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Yorkshire_Rider_bus_5507_%28C507_KBT%29%2C_TYPG_2009_Leeds_bus_rally.jpg/220px-Yorkshire_Rider_bus_5507_%28C507_KBT%29%2C_TYPG_2009_Leeds_bus_rally.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Yorkshire_Rider_bus_5507_%28C507_KBT%29%2C_TYPG_2009_Leeds_bus_rally.jpg/330px-Yorkshire_Rider_bus_5507_%28C507_KBT%29%2C_TYPG_2009_Leeds_bus_rally.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Yorkshire_Rider_bus_5507_%28C507_KBT%29%2C_TYPG_2009_Leeds_bus_rally.jpg/440px-Yorkshire_Rider_bus_5507_%28C507_KBT%29%2C_TYPG_2009_Leeds_bus_rally.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Yorkshire_Rider_bus_5507_(C507_KBT),_TYPG_2009_Leeds_bus_rally.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Preserved <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Rider" title="Yorkshire Rider">Yorkshire Rider</a> <a href="/wiki/Leyland_Olympian" title="Leyland Olympian">Leyland Olympian</a> in <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> in July 2009</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bus_deregulation_in_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Bus deregulation in the United Kingdom">Deregulation</a> occurred on 26 October 1986. The WYPTE bus division was renamed <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Rider" title="Yorkshire Rider">Yorkshire Rider</a> and with it a new livery of dark olive green and cream and a stylised "YR" emblem. Five double-decker buses were operated in each of the municipal council's colours (already included were the two Huddersfield buses), with words on the sides between the decks saying "Building on a Great Tradition". The bus services and fare/bus pass/timetables division was renamed Metro.
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Bank_Holidays">Bank Holidays</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Bank Holidays">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Generally speaking:
</p>
<ul><li>Christmas Day, Boxing Day, New Year's Day – no service.  Some free services are provided by volunteers using buses from the Keighley Bus Museum Trust during Christmas Day daytime.  In 2007 Metro have agreed to provide trial services on main routes in Huddersfield and Leeds on <a href="/wiki/Boxing_Day" title="Boxing Day">Boxing Day</a> 2007.</li>
<li>Christmas and New Year's Eves – Saturday service (or Sunday service if these days fall on a Sunday), but with early finish.</li>
<li>Weekdays between Christmas and New Year – Saturday service. Any Bank Holidays in lieu of Christmas Day falling on a weekend will have a Sunday service</li>
<li>Bank Holiday Mondays – Sunday service (except Saturday service on Harrogate and District, and where otherwise stated).</li>
<li>Good Friday – normal service.</li></ul>
<p>In August Metro start to Issue A ticket called Metro Active. This ticket is Available after 09:30 and is used by children (under 11) with parents, children aged 11–16 with a young persons photocard and teenagers aged 17–19 with a Scholars Photocard. The cost of the ticket is just £1 and can be used on all operators services within West Yorkshire (Also includes Barnsley and Doncaster).
</p>
<h3><span id=".27My_bus.27_school_bus_services"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="'My_bus'_school_bus_services">'My bus' school bus services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: &#039;My bus&#039; school bus services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:My_bus_at_Bingley.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/My_bus_at_Bingley.jpg/220px-My_bus_at_Bingley.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/My_bus_at_Bingley.jpg/330px-My_bus_at_Bingley.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/My_bus_at_Bingley.jpg/440px-My_bus_at_Bingley.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3494" data-file-height="2330" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:My_bus_at_Bingley.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>My bus</i> <a href="/wiki/School_bus" title="School bus">school bus</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bingley" title="Bingley">Bingley</a></div></div></div>
<p><b>My bus</b> is a <a href="/wiki/School_bus" title="School bus">school bus</a> service provided by West Yorkshire Metro with certain features which set it apart from normal <a href="/wiki/Student_transport#United_Kingdom" title="Student transport">school transport services in the United Kingdom</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-howworks_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-howworks-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Mybus_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mybus-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>a dedicated fleet of <a href="/wiki/School_bus" title="School bus">school buses</a> (rather than ordinary <a href="/wiki/Transit_bus" title="Transit bus">transit buses</a> or <a href="/wiki/Coach_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Coach (vehicle)">coaches</a> borrowed from other duties); additional use of these vehicles is limited to school and young-person focussed activities and all have <a href="/wiki/Low_floor" class="mw-redirect" title="Low floor">low floors</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seat_belt" title="Seat belt">seat belts</a></li>
<li>drivers with enhanced <a href="/wiki/Disclosure_and_Barring_Service" title="Disclosure and Barring Service">DBS</a> checks, who permanently assigned to each route, assisted on some routes by <a href="/wiki/Volunteering" title="Volunteering">volunteer</a> escorts</li>
<li>a bus seat is permanently assigned to each student</li>
<li>in-school education campaign to support the service</li></ul>
<p>The service, using buses painted yellow with coordinated <i>My bus</i> <a href="/wiki/Brand" title="Brand">branding</a> gained significant mode shift: 64% of primary school users were previously driven by car. Under this scheme, these buses were not allowed to be used for non-school purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-Mybus_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mybus-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>West Yorkshire Metro claims benefits from 'My bus' ranging from reductions in car use, <a href="/wiki/Traffic_congestion" title="Traffic congestion">traffic congestion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Air_pollution" title="Air pollution">air pollution</a>, <a href="/wiki/Traffic_accident" class="mw-redirect" title="Traffic accident">traffic accidents</a>, <a href="/wiki/Social_exclusion" title="Social exclusion">social exclusion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Truancy" title="Truancy">truancy</a> and late student arrivals and improvements to <a href="/wiki/Education" title="Education">education</a>, safety for pedestrians and cyclists, <a href="/wiki/Mainstreaming_(education)" title="Mainstreaming (education)">integration</a> of people with <a href="/wiki/Special_needs" title="Special needs">special needs</a> and children's experiences of <a href="/wiki/Public_transport" title="Public transport">public transport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-howworks_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-howworks-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><b>My bus contractors:</b> City Travel, <a href="/wiki/HCT_Group" title="HCT Group">CT Plus</a>, <a href="/wiki/First_Student_UK" title="First Student UK">First Student UK</a> (<a href="/wiki/First_Calderdale_%26_Huddersfield" title="First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield">First Calderdale &amp; Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/First_Leeds" title="First Leeds">First Leeds</a>), <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Joint_Committee" title="Halifax Joint Committee">Halifax Joint Committee</a>, Rollinson Safeway, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger#Tiger_Blue" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Tiger Blue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Keighley_Bus_Company" title="Keighley Bus Company">Keighley Bus Company</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Routes">Routes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Routes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There are 12 commuter railway lines serving West Yorkshire: <a href="/wiki/Airedale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Airedale Line">Airedale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calder_Valley_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Calder Valley Line">Calder Valley Line</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dearne_Valley_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Dearne Valley Line">Dearne Valley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hallam_Line" title="Hallam Line">Hallam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harrogate_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Harrogate Line">Harrogate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Huddersfield Line">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds-Bradford_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds-Bradford Line">Leeds-Bradford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Penistone_Line" title="Penistone Line">Penistone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pontefract_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Pontefract Line">Pontefract</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wakefield_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Wakefield Line">Wakefield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wharfedale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Wharfedale Line">Wharfedale</a> and <a href="/wiki/York_and_Selby_Lines" title="York and Selby Lines">York &amp; Selby</a>.
</p><p>The majority of these lines run into Leeds and most continue into neighbouring areas, serving towns and cities such as <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blackpool" title="Blackpool">Blackpool</a>, <a href="/wiki/Doncaster" title="Doncaster">Doncaster</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harrogate" title="Harrogate">Harrogate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nottingham" title="Nottingham">Nottingham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Preston,_Lancashire" title="Preston, Lancashire">Preston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a> and <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Airport" title="Manchester Airport">Manchester Airport</a>. Some lines overlap each other, which means that stations such as Castleford, Huddersfield and Wakefield Westgate are served by more than one line.
</p><p>Most lines have frequent services, but the Dearne Valley line has a limited service of two trains a day in each direction.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Operators">Operators</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Operators">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most local services are run by <a href="/wiki/Northern_(train_operating_company)" title="Northern (train operating company)">Northern</a>, and longer-distance routes are served by <a href="/wiki/TransPennine_Express" title="TransPennine Express">TransPennine Express</a>, <a href="/wiki/London_North_Eastern_Railway" title="London North Eastern Railway">London North Eastern Railway</a>, <a href="/wiki/CrossCountry" title="CrossCountry">CrossCountry</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Midlands_Railway" title="East Midlands Railway">East Midlands Railway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grand_Central_(train_operating_company)" title="Grand Central (train operating company)">Grand Central</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Leeds_Supertram">Leeds Supertram</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Metro&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Leeds Supertram">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2001, Metro came up with <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Supertram" title="Leeds Supertram">Leeds Supertram</a>, with the idea of bringing back a tram network for Leeds. After the original scheme exceeded its budget, the Government asked Metro to look at ways of reducing costs. In spring 2005 Metro went back to the Government with a re-costed plan but the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Transport" title="Secretary of State for Transport">Secretary of State for Transport</a> <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Darling" title="Alistair Darling">Alistair Darling</a> rejected the proposal after the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="2010 United Kingdom general election">2005 general election</a>. Darling responded by pointing to plans by a private bus operator for a bus system where the vehicles were designed to look like trams.
</p><p>This is not the first time such a scheme has been unsuccessfully pursued. In the mid-1980s the PTE was interested in bringing back <a href="/wiki/Trolleybuses_in_Bradford" title="Trolleybuses in Bradford">trolleybuses in Bradford</a> with <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Rider" title="Yorkshire Rider">Yorkshire Rider</a> awarded a contract to operate, but nothing came of it.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
