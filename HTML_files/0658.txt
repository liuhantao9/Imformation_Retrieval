<p>The <b>Rugby Football League</b> is the governing body for professional <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league_in_England" title="Rugby league in England">rugby league in England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The name Rugby Football League previously also referred to the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_League_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="Rugby Football League Championship">main league competition</a> run by the organisation. This has since been supplanted by <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Championship_(rugby_league)" class="mw-redirect" title="Championship (rugby league)">Championship</a> and <a href="/wiki/League_1_(rugby_league)" title="League 1 (rugby league)">League 1</a>.
</p><p>The Patron of the Rugby Football League is <a href="/wiki/Prince_Harry,_Duke_of_Sussex" title="Prince Harry, Duke of Sussex">Prince Harry, Duke of Sussex</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>.
</p><p>Based at Red Hall in <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>, it administers the <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_league_team" title="England national rugby league team">England national rugby league team</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Challenge_Cup" title="Challenge Cup">Challenge Cup</a>, <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_Championships" title="Rugby League Championships">Rugby League Championships</a>. The social and junior game is administered in association with the <a href="/wiki/British_Amateur_Rugby_League_Association" title="British Amateur Rugby League Association">British Amateur Rugby League Association</a> (BARLA). The Rugby Football League is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_European_Federation" title="Rugby League European Federation">Rugby League European Federation</a> and as a senior Full Member has a combined veto power over the Council with France. The RFL is part of the Community Board, which also has representatives from BARLA, Combined Services, English Schools Rugby League and <a href="/wiki/Student_Rugby_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Student Rugby League">Student Rugby League</a>.
<a href="/wiki/Tony_Adams" title="Tony Adams">Tony Adams</a> will take over as the president in 2019, taking over from <a href="/wiki/Andy_Burnham" title="Andy Burnham">Andy Burnham</a>.
</p><p>Established as the <b>Northern Rugby Football Union</b> (often shortened to <b>Northern Union</b>) in August 1895 by representatives of twenty-one <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_Union" title="Rugby Football Union">Rugby Football Union</a> clubs at a meeting at the <a href="/wiki/George_Hotel,_Huddersfield" title="George Hotel, Huddersfield">George Hotel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, it changed its name in 1922 to the <b>Rugby Football League</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> mirroring its sister organisations overseas, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Rugby_Football_League" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian Rugby Football League">Australian Rugby Football League</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Rugby_Football_League" class="mw-redirect" title="New Zealand Rugby Football League">New Zealand Rugby Football League</a>.
</p><p>The turnover of the RFL was reported as £27m in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-RFL2011_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RFL2011-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBC_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_rugby_league" title="History of rugby league">History of rugby league</a></div>
<p>On Tuesday 27 August 1895, as a result of an emergency meeting in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, prominent Lancashire rugby clubs Broughton Rangers, Leigh, Oldham, Rochdale Hornets, St Helens, Tyldesley, Warrington, Widnes and Wigan declared that they would support their Yorkshire colleagues in their proposal to form a Northern Union.
</p><p>Two days later, on Thursday 29 August 1895, representatives of 21 clubs met in the <a href="/wiki/George_Hotel,_Huddersfield" title="George Hotel, Huddersfield">George Hotel</a>, Huddersfield to form the "Northern Rugby Football Union" (usually termed Northern Union or NU). Twenty clubs agreed to resign from the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_Union" title="Rugby Football Union">Rugby Football Union</a>, but Dewsbury felt unable to comply with the decision. The <a href="/wiki/Cheshire" title="Cheshire">Cheshire</a> club, Stockport, had telegraphed the meeting requesting admission to the new organisation and was duly accepted with a second Cheshire club, Runcorn, admitted at the next meeting.

<p>In 1908 the Northern Union's brand of rugby was taken up in Australia and New Zealand. The Union hosted touring sides from both countries before assembling a <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_national_rugby_league_team" title="Great Britain national rugby league team">Great Britain representative team</a> for a <a href="/wiki/1910_Great_Britain_Lions_tour_of_Australia_and_New_Zealand" title="1910 Great Britain Lions tour of Australia and New Zealand">1910 tour of Australia and New Zealand</a>. These nations, particularly Australia, would go on to excel in the sport and gain significant influence over it over the following century.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/British_Amateur_Rugby_League_Association" title="British Amateur Rugby League Association">British Amateur Rugby League Association</a> (BARLA) was created in 1973 in Huddersfield by a group of enthusiasts concerned about the dramatic disappearance of many amateur leagues and clubs. Fewer than 150 amateur teams remained with a mere 30 youth rugby league teams. The 'breakaway' from the RFL was acrimonious and was strongly contested, with a vote 29-1 against recognising BARLA. Thanks to Tom Mitchell, this changed to a unanimous vote of approval for BARLA within 12 months.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Maurice_Lindsay_(Rugby_League)" class="mw-redirect" title="Maurice Lindsay (Rugby League)">Maurice Lindsay</a> became the Chief Executive of the RFL in 1992, proposing the <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a>, which replaced <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_League_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="Rugby Football League Championship">Championship</a> as the sport's premier league competition from 1996 onwards. Lindsay returned to Wigan in 1999 for his second stint at the club after <a href="/wiki/Rodney_Walker_(rugby_league)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rodney Walker (rugby league)">Sir Rodney Walker</a>, then chairman of the RFL, sacked him after a campaign to unseat him failed.<sup id="cite_ref-League&#39;s_cease-fire_is_over_as_superpowers_prepare_for_War_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-League&#39;s_cease-fire_is_over_as_superpowers_prepare_for_War-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The RFL accumulated losses of £1.9 million at the end of 2001, shortly before a major restructuring of the governing body and the appointment of <a href="/wiki/Richard_Lewis_(tennis_and_rugby_league)" title="Richard Lewis (tennis and rugby league)">Richard Lewis</a> as executive chairman in May 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Within a year of joining the RFL, he oversaw reunification with BARLA after nearly 30 years of division.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  Lewis left in 2012 to become Chief Executive of the <a href="/wiki/All_England_Lawn_Tennis_and_Croquet_Club" title="All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club">All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-web2012_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-web2012-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  The RFL net value has been positive every year since 2004, being £1.7M in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-RFL2011_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RFL2011-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011 a major change to the game was agreed, changing from a winter to a summer game, starting in 2012 with a playing season from March to November, aligning with the <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a>, which has played this way since 1996. The regional leagues may include winter competitions in addition.<sup id="cite_ref-RFL2011_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RFL2011-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, the Rugby Football League were awarded the <a href="/wiki/Stonewall_Awards" title="Stonewall Awards">Stonewall Sport Award</a> in recognition of their work in embracing inclusivity and tackling homophobia.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> They also became the first UK sporting organisation to make the top 100 employers in the Stonewall Index that measures attitudes towards lesbian, gay and bisexual staff.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Competitions">Competitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Competitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/British_rugby_league_system" title="British rugby league system">British rugby league system</a></div>
<p>The RFL operates a five-tier system and is responsible for running the top three professional divisions as well as the <a href="/wiki/National_Conference_League" title="National Conference League">National Conference League</a> and various regional leagues below that. The RFL also runs two cup competitions for professional clubs and is involved with the organization of the <a href="/wiki/World_Club_Challenge" title="World Club Challenge">World Club Challenge</a> and <a href="/wiki/World_Club_Series" title="World Club Series">World Club Series</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Teams">Teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="English_national_team">English national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: English national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_league_team" title="England national rugby league team">England national rugby league team</a></div>
<p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p><p>The England national rugby league team represent England in international <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> football tournaments. The team has now seen a revival, having largely formed from the <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_national_rugby_league_team" title="Great Britain national rugby league team">Great Britain team</a>, who also represented <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_rugby_league_team" title="Wales national rugby league team">Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_rugby_league_team" title="Scotland national rugby league team">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland_national_rugby_league_team" title="Ireland national rugby league team">Ireland</a>. The team is run under the auspices of the Rugby Football League. As of 2008, the team now participates in all <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="Rugby League World Cup">World Cups</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_Four_Nations" title="Rugby League Four Nations">Four Nations</a>, and Test matches.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The team dates back to 1904 when they played against a mixture of Welsh and Scottish players in <a href="/wiki/Wigan" title="Wigan">Wigan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Since then, and right up until the 1950s, they regularly toured Australia and New Zealand and played both home and away matches against neighbours Wales and France. But when it was decided that Great Britain would tour the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a> instead of England, France and Wales became the only regular opponents. Even then though, there are some long periods where England barely played any matches. Their first appearance in the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="Rugby League World Cup">Rugby League World Cup</a> was in 1975, and since then they have become runners-up in <a href="/wiki/1975_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="1975 Rugby League World Cup">1975</a> and <a href="/wiki/1995_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="1995 Rugby League World Cup">1995</a>, the latter tournament being held in England. In 2008 they competed in the <a href="/wiki/2008_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="2008 Rugby League World Cup">2008 World Cup</a> in Australia. For many years England also competed in the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_European_Nations_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="Rugby League European Nations Cup">European Nations Cup</a> and in 2006, an England 'A' team, competed for the <a href="/wiki/Federation_Shield" title="Federation Shield">Federation Shield</a>. In the past England's main rivals have been <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_rugby_league_team" title="Wales national rugby league team">Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/France_national_rugby_league_team" title="France national rugby league team">France</a>, with the rivalry stretching back to 1908 and 1934 respectively. However, England's main rivals would now be <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_rugby_league_team" title="Australia national rugby league team">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_national_rugby_league_team" title="New Zealand national rugby league team">New Zealand</a> and, to a lesser extent, <a href="/wiki/France_national_rugby_league_team" title="France national rugby league team">France</a>.
</p><p>Traditionally a predominantly white kit is worn including white shorts and socks. However the shirt usually features some form of red, like red stripes, crosses or chevrons. These colours are similar to other English sporting teams and are the colours used on the national flag. In 2008 a new kit was introduced featuring a red cross on the front and red strips down the sides of the shirt, shorts and socks were white too with red strips.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Also in 2008 the Rugby Football League chose to abandon the traditional English lion on the badge in favour of a much simpler shield and <a href="/wiki/St_George_cross" class="mw-redirect" title="St George cross">cross</a> design,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> nevertheless the team will still be known as "The Lions".
</p><p>Currently the team is ranked third in the world, behind <a href="/wiki/Australia_national_rugby_league_team" title="Australia national rugby league team">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_national_rugby_league_team" title="New Zealand national rugby league team">New Zealand</a>. <a href="/wiki/Steve_McNamara" title="Steve McNamara">Steve McNamara</a> became head coach leaving <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Bulls" title="Bradford Bulls">Bradford</a> to take the national job and <a href="/wiki/Sean_O%27Loughlin" title="Sean O&#39;Loughlin">Sean O'Loughlin</a> is the current captain.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Great_Britain_national_team">Great Britain national team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Great Britain national team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_national_rugby_league_team" title="Great Britain national rugby league team">Great Britain national rugby league team</a></div>
<p>The Great Britain national rugby league team represents Great Britain in <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> football. Administered by the Rugby Football League (RFL), the team is nicknamed the "Great Britain Lions", or simply "The Lions".
</p><p>For most of the 20th century the Great Britain team was assembled to go on tours overseas, and to play against foreign touring teams, as well as competing in <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="Rugby League World Cup">Rugby League World Cup</a> tournaments. They were one of the strongest teams in rugby league, though usually playing second fiddle to <a href="/wiki/Australian_Kangaroos" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian Kangaroos">Australia</a>. They won the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="Rugby League World Cup">Rugby League World Cup</a> on three occasions: <a href="/wiki/1954_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="1954 Rugby League World Cup">1954</a>, <a href="/wiki/1960_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="1960 Rugby League World Cup">1960</a> and <a href="/wiki/1972_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="1972 Rugby League World Cup">1972</a>.
</p><p>Since <a href="/wiki/1995_Rugby_League_World_Cup" title="1995 Rugby League World Cup">1995</a> the RFL have pretended to send the <a href="/wiki/Home_nations" class="mw-redirect" title="Home nations">home nations</a> as separate teams for World Cup purposes. Great Britain continued to compete as a test playing nation both home and away. They competed against Australia for <a href="/wiki/The_Ashes_(rugby_league)" title="The Ashes (rugby league)">the Ashes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_national_rugby_league_team" title="New Zealand national rugby league team">New Zealand</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Baskerville_Shield" title="Baskerville Shield">Baskerville Shield</a>, as well the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_Tri-Nations" title="Rugby League Tri-Nations">Tri-Nations</a> series with both Australia and New Zealand. Great Britain also played in series and tours against other nations such as <a href="/wiki/France_national_rugby_league_team" title="France national rugby league team">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea_national_rugby_league_team" title="Papua New Guinea national rugby league team">Papua New Guinea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fiji_national_rugby_league_team" title="Fiji national rugby league team">Fiji</a>.
</p><p>In 2006, the RFL announced that after the <a href="/wiki/2007_All_Golds_Tour" title="2007 All Golds Tour">2007 All Golds Tour</a><sup id="cite_ref-RLSL_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RLSL-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> the Great Britain team would no longer compete on a regular basis, and that players would be able to represent <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_league_team" title="England national rugby league team">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_rugby_league_team" title="Wales national rugby league team">Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_rugby_league_team" title="Scotland national rugby league team">Scotland</a> at Test level. It is planned that the Great Britain team will come together in future only for occasional tours, similar to the <a href="/wiki/British_and_Irish_Lions" title="British and Irish Lions">British and Irish Lions</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby union</a>.
</p>

<h3><span id="Young_People.27s_Advisory_Panel"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Young_People's_Advisory_Panel">Young People's Advisory Panel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Young People&#039;s Advisory Panel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The RFL launched the Young People's Advisory Panel in 2010, a group consisting of young people aged 16–25 from across England. The national panel meet at least three times a year at the RFL's Red Hall headquarters to discuss and debate the following:
</p>
<ul><li>Changes in the structure of youth rugby;</li>
<li>Communications between young rugby league enthusiasts and the RFL;</li>
<li>RFL policies which impact on young people.</li></ul>
<p>Two nominated members will also sit on the youth &amp; junior forum, a key device used to advance youth rugby league.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Logo">Logo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Logo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first logo used by the RFL was an oval shape, representing the ball with XIII and 13 over it and <i>The Rugby Football League</i> around it. The logo was also seen on the sleeve of teams shirts.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rugby_Football_League_2005_logo.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d7/Rugby_Football_League_2005_logo.svg/150px-Rugby_Football_League_2005_logo.svg.png" decoding="async" width="150" height="61" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d7/Rugby_Football_League_2005_logo.svg/225px-Rugby_Football_League_2005_logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/d7/Rugby_Football_League_2005_logo.svg/300px-Rugby_Football_League_2005_logo.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="300" data-file-height="121" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rugby_Football_League_2005_logo.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>RFL logo used until 2017 rebrand</div></div></div>
<p>In the late 1990s the logo was changed to a more simplistic design to the old one. It had a rugby ball shape with three small lines, representing 13 players, and two long lines, representing goalposts, arranged on a rugby ball so as to suggest a hand carrying or passing it. The <i>Rugby Football League</i> was abbreviated to <i>RFL</i>.
</p><p>In 2017 the RFL had its most radical rebrand since the formation of <a href="/wiki/Super_League" title="Super League">Super League</a>. The new logo was a rectangular background meant to represent The George Hotel in Huddersfield, where rugby league was founded and 1895 the year it was founded. Thirteen stripes inside it represent thirteen players. The oval on top represents the ball and the appreciation RFL has been replaced with <i>Rugby Football League</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Headquarters">Headquarters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rugby_Football_League&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Headquarters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Red_Hall_Leeds_Sep_2012.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Red_Hall_Leeds_Sep_2012.jpg/220px-Red_Hall_Leeds_Sep_2012.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Red_Hall_Leeds_Sep_2012.jpg/330px-Red_Hall_Leeds_Sep_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Red_Hall_Leeds_Sep_2012.jpg/440px-Red_Hall_Leeds_Sep_2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1632" data-file-height="1224" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Red_Hall_Leeds_Sep_2012.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Red Hall, RFL HQ</div></div></div>
<p>The RFL moved into permanent headquarters in 1922 at 180 Chapeltown Road, Leeds, where it stayed for 73 years before leaving in 1995 to its current HQ at Red Hall in Leeds, a <a href="/wiki/Grade_II_listed" class="mw-redirect" title="Grade II listed">Grade II listed</a> brick building dating from 1642.<sup id="cite_ref-Britlist_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Britlist-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RedHall_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RedHall-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, some departments including Super League, moved to offices at Quay West in Trafford Wharf, <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester" title="Greater Manchester">Greater Manchester</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
