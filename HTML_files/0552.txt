	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Coppename River</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Coppename_River_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Coppename_River_2.jpg/350px-Coppename_River_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="350" height="212" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Coppename_River_2.jpg/525px-Coppename_River_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Coppename_River_2.jpg/700px-Coppename_River_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3456" data-file-height="2092" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Coppename_River_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Coppename River</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Coppename</b> is a river in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> (South America) in the district of <a href="/wiki/Sipaliwini" title="Sipaliwini">Sipaliwini</a>, forming part of the boundary between the districts of <a href="/wiki/Coronie" class="mw-redirect" title="Coronie">Coronie</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saramacca_District" title="Saramacca District">Saramacca</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Course">Course</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Coppename_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Course">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bailey-Coppename_River.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Bailey-Coppename_River.jpg/350px-Bailey-Coppename_River.jpg" decoding="async" width="350" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Bailey-Coppename_River.jpg/525px-Bailey-Coppename_River.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Bailey-Coppename_River.jpg/700px-Bailey-Coppename_River.jpg 2x" data-file-width="875" data-file-height="390" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bailey-Coppename_River.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Bailey_bridge" title="Bailey bridge">Bailey bridge</a> over the Coppename river at Bitagron</div></div></div>
<p>The Coppename river begins in the <a href="/wiki/Wilhelmina_Mountains" title="Wilhelmina Mountains">Wilhelmina Mountains</a>. Its tributaries are the Right Coppename (which originates on the northeast slope of the Wilhelmina range, <a href="/wiki/Tafelberg,_Suriname" title="Tafelberg, Suriname">Tafelberg</a>, and the western parts of the Emmakette), the Left Coppename, and the middle Coppename, which has its sources in the western and central parts of the north side of the Wilhelmina mountains and from the south east region of the <a href="/wiki/Bakhuys_Mountains" class="mw-redirect" title="Bakhuys Mountains">Bakhuys Mountains</a>.
</p><p>The three branches of the Coppename unite above the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tonckens_Falls&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tonckens Falls (page does not exist)">Tonckens Falls</a> and then flows past Hebiweri Mountain. Below the Sidonkrutu-rapids it joins with the Adampada, which drains most of the eastern slope of the Bakhuys range. Below the Langa rapids, the river bends to the east and above the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Raleigh_Falls&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Raleigh Falls (page does not exist)">Raleigh Falls</a> is joined by the Tangimama, which rises in the northern foothills of the Emmaketen.
</p><p>The Raleigh Falls, together with the nearby Voltz Mountain and the Van Stockum range, are a tourist attraction, which can be reached by a small airplanes.
</p><p>After being joined by the Kwama, the Coppename flows further north and passes the <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">Maroon</a> villages of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kaaimanston&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kaaimanston (page does not exist)">Kaaimanston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bitagron" title="Bitagron">Bitagron</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=H%C3%A9doti&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hédoti (page does not exist)">Hédoti</a>. Once in the old coastal plain, the Coppename is joined by the <a href="/wiki/Wayambo" class="mw-redirect" title="Wayambo">Wayambo</a>, which forms a <a href="/wiki/River_bifurcation" title="River bifurcation">bifurcation</a> between the Coppename and the <a href="/wiki/Nickerie_River" title="Nickerie River">Nickerie Rivers</a>.
</p><p>Finally, the <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">tributaries</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tibiti&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tibiti (page does not exist)">Tibiti</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Coesewijne&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Coesewijne (page does not exist)">Coesewijne</a> join it. The Coppename and <a href="/wiki/Saramacca_River" title="Saramacca River">Saramacca Rivers</a> unite and flow together into the Atlantic.
</p>
