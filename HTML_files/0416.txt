<p><b>Paramaribo</b> is a district of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, encompassing the city of Paramaribo and the surrounding area.
</p><p>Paramaribo district has a <a href="/wiki/Population" title="Population">population</a> of 240,924, almost half the population of the entire country, and an <a href="/wiki/Area" title="Area">area</a> of 182&#160;km².<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The area was first <a href="/wiki/Colonisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Colonisation">colonised</a> by the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> in the 17th century with the construction of <a href="/wiki/Fort_Willoughby" class="mw-redirect" title="Fort Willoughby">Fort Willoughby</a>. This fort was later taken by the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and renamed <a href="/wiki/Fort_Zeelandia_(Paramaribo)" title="Fort Zeelandia (Paramaribo)">Fort Zeelandia</a>. The area, and the city of Paramaribo, switched between Dutch and British control until the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Breda_(1667)" title="Treaty of Breda (1667)">Treaty of Breda</a> at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Anglo-Dutch_War" title="Second Anglo-Dutch War">Second Anglo-Dutch War</a> ceded all of Suriname to the Dutch.
</p>
