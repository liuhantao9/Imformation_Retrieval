<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Foundation">Foundation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Foundation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Arya Samaj was established in <a href="/wiki/Bombay" class="mw-redirect" title="Bombay">Bombay</a> on 7 April 1875 by <a href="/wiki/Dayananda_Saraswati" title="Dayananda Saraswati">Dayananda Saraswati</a> (born "Mool Shankar" in <a href="/wiki/Kathiawar" title="Kathiawar">Kathiawar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gujarat" title="Gujarat">Gujarat</a> 1824 â died <a href="/wiki/Ajmer" title="Ajmer">Ajmer</a>, 1883)<sup id="cite_ref-aryasamaj_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aryasamaj-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>An alternative date for the foundation of the samaj is 24 June 1877 because it was then, in <a href="/wiki/Lahore" title="Lahore">Lahore</a> when the samaj became more than just a regional movement based in <a href="/wiki/Punjab" title="Punjab">Punjab</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vedic_schools">Vedic schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Vedic schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Between 1869 and 1873, Dayanand began his efforts to reform orthodox <a href="/wiki/Hinduism" title="Hinduism">Hinduism</a> in India. He established <i><a href="/wiki/Gurukul" class="mw-redirect" title="Gurukul">Gurukul</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Vedas" title="Vedas">Vedic</a> schools) which emphasised Vedic values, culture, <i><a href="/wiki/Satya" title="Satya">Satya</a></i> (virtue) and <i><a href="/wiki/Sanatana_Dharma" class="mw-redirect" title="Sanatana Dharma">Sanatana Dharma</a></i> (the essence of living). The schools gave separate educations to boys and girls based on ancient Vedic principles. The Vedic school system was also to relieve Indians from the pattern of a <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British</a> education.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first Vedic school was established at <a href="/wiki/Farrukhabad" title="Farrukhabad">Farrukhabad</a> in 1869.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Fifty students were enrolled in its first year. This success led to the founding of schools at <a href="/wiki/Mirzapur" title="Mirzapur">Mirzapur</a> (1870), <a href="/wiki/Kasganj" title="Kasganj">Kasganj</a> (1870), <a href="/wiki/Chhalesar" title="Chhalesar">Chhalesar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Aligarh" title="Aligarh">Aligarh</a>) (1870) and <a href="/wiki/Varanasi" title="Varanasi">Varanasi</a> (1873).
</p><p>At the schools, students received all meals, lodging, clothing and books free of charge. Discipline was strict. Students were not allowed to perform <i><a href="/wiki/Murti" title="Murti">murti</a> <a href="/wiki/Puja_(Hinduism)" title="Puja (Hinduism)">puja</a></i> (worship of sculpted stone idols). Rather, they performed <i><a href="/wiki/Sandhyavandanam" title="Sandhyavandanam">Sandhyavandanam</a></i> (meditative prayer using Vedic mantras with divine sound) and <i><a href="/wiki/Agnihotra" title="Agnihotra">agnihotra</a></i> (making a heated milk offering twice daily).
</p><p>The study of <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a> scriptural texts which accepted the authority of the Vedas were taught. They included the <i><a href="/wiki/Vedas" title="Vedas">Vedas</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Upanishads" title="Upanishads">Upanishads</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Aranyaka" title="Aranyaka">Aranyaka</a></i>, <i>Kashika</i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Nirukta" title="Nirukta">Nirukta</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mahabhasya" class="mw-redirect" title="Mahabhasya">Mahabhasya</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/P%C4%81%E1%B9%87ini" title="PÄá¹ini">Ashtadhyayi</a></i>, <i>Darshanas</i>. The teaching was open to girls and to children who were not of the <a href="/wiki/Brahmins" class="mw-redirect" title="Brahmins">Brahmins</a> class.
</p><p>Dayanand had difficulty finding qualified teachers who agreed with his views on religious reform, since, he was criticized for plagiarising Abrahamic texts and juxtaposing Hindu philosophy in them. There were few textbooks which he considered suitable. Funding was sporadic, attendance fluctuated and students did not achieve desired standards and so some schools closed soon after opening. The last remaining school at Farrukhabad closed in 1876.
</p>

<p>After visiting <a href="/wiki/Calcutta" class="mw-redirect" title="Calcutta">Calcutta</a>, Dayanand's work changed. He began lecturing in <a href="/wiki/Hindi" title="Hindi">Hindi</a> rather than in <a href="/wiki/Sanskrit" title="Sanskrit">Sanskrit</a>. Although Sanskrit garnered respect, in Hindi, Dayanand reached a much larger audience. His ideas of reform began to reach the poorest people.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Varanasi" title="Varanasi">Varanasi</a>, after hearing Dayanand speak, a local government official called Jaikishen Das encouraged Dayanand to publish a book about his ideas. From June to September 1874, Dayanand dictated a series of lectures to his scribe, Bhimsen Sharma. The lectures recorded Dayanand's views on a wide range of subjects. They were published in 1875 in Varanasi with the title <i><a href="/wiki/Satyarth_Prakash" title="Satyarth Prakash">Satyarth Prakash</a></i> ("the light of truth").
</p>

<p>While his manuscript for <i>Satyarth Prakash</i> was being edited in Varanasi, Dayanand received an invitation to travel to <a href="/wiki/Bombay" class="mw-redirect" title="Bombay">Bombay</a>. There, he was to debate representatives of the <a href="/wiki/Vallabhacharya" class="mw-redirect" title="Vallabhacharya">Vallabhacharya</a> sect. On 20 October 1874, Dayanand arrived in Bombay. The debate, though well publicized, never took place. Nonetheless, two members of the <a href="/wiki/Prarthana_Samaj" title="Prarthana Samaj">Prarthana Samaj</a> approached Dayanand and invited him to speak at one of their gatherings. He did so and was well received. They recognized Dayanand's desire to uplift the Hindu community and protect Hindus from the pressures to convert to <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a> or <a href="/wiki/Islamism" title="Islamism">Islam</a>. Dayanand spent over one month in Bombay and attracted sixty people to his cause. They proposed founding a new samaj with Dayanand's ideas as its spiritual and intellectual basis.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ahmedabad_debates">Ahmedabad debates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ahmedabad debates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 December 1874, Dayanand arrived in <a href="/wiki/Ahmedabad" title="Ahmedabad">Ahmedabad</a>, Gujarat on the invitation of <a href="/wiki/Gopal_Hari_Deshmukh" title="Gopal Hari Deshmukh">Gopal Hari Deshmukh</a>. There, he debated with interested parties.
</p>

<p>On 31 December 1874, Dayanand arrived in <a href="/wiki/Rajkot" title="Rajkot">Rajkot</a>, Gujarat, on the invitation of Hargovind Das Dvarkadas, the secretary of the local Prarthana Samaj. He invited topics of discourse from the audience and spoke on eight. Again, Dayanand was well received and the Rajkot group elected to join his cause. The Samaj was renamed <i>Arya Samaj</i> (Society of Nobles). Dayanand published a list of twenty-eight rules and regulations for the followers. After leaving Rajkot, Dayanand went to <a href="/wiki/Ahmedabad" title="Ahmedabad">Ahmedabad</a> but his audience at a meeting on 27 January 1875, did not elect to form a new Arya Samaj. Meanwhile, the Rajkot group had become in a political row.
</p>

<p>On his return to Bombay, Dayanand began a membership drive for a local Arya samaj and received one hundred enrollees. 
On 7 April 1875, the Bombay Arya Samaj was established. Dayanand himself enrolled as a member rather than the leader of the Bombay group. The Samaj began to grow.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_Dayanand">After Dayanand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: After Dayanand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dayanand died in 1883. The Arya Samaj continued to grow, especially in Punjab. The early leaders of the Samaj were <a href="/wiki/Pandit_Lekh_Ram" title="Pandit Lekh Ram">Pandit Lekh Ram</a> (1858 â 1897) and <a href="/wiki/Swami_Shraddhanand" title="Swami Shraddhanand">Swami Shraddhanand</a> (Mahatma Munshi Ram Vij) (1856 â 1926). Some authors claim that the activities of the Samaj led to increased antagonism between Muslims and Hindus.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Shraddhanand led the <a href="/wiki/Shuddhi" title="Shuddhi">Shuddhi</a> movement that aimed to bring Hindus who had converted to other religions back to Hinduism.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1893, the Arya Samaj members of Punjab were divided on the question of <a href="/wiki/Vegetarianism" title="Vegetarianism">vegetarianism</a>. The group that refrained from eating meat were called the <i>"Mahatma"</i> group and the other group, the "Cultured Party".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the early 1900s, the Samaj (or organizations inspired by it such as <i>Jat Pat Todak Mandal</i>) campaigned against caste discrimination.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> They also campaigned for <a href="/wiki/Widow_remarriage" class="mw-redirect" title="Widow remarriage">widow remarriage</a> and <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_education" class="mw-redirect" title="Women&#39;s education">women's education</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The samaj also established chapters in <a href="/wiki/British_colonies" class="mw-redirect" title="British colonies">British colonies</a> with an <a href="/wiki/Non-resident_Indian_and_person_of_Indian_origin" title="Non-resident Indian and person of Indian origin">Indian diaspora</a> such as <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prominent Indian Nationalists such as <a href="/wiki/Lala_Lajpat_Rai" title="Lala Lajpat Rai">Lala Lajpat Rai</a> belonged to Arya Samaj and were active in its campaigning.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The British colonial government in the early part of 20th century viewed the Samaj as a political body. Some Samajis in government service were dismissed for belonging to the Samaj<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 1930s, when the Hindu Nationalist group, the <a href="/wiki/Rashtriya_Swayamsevak_Sangh" title="Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh">Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh</a> grew in prominence in Northern India, they found support in the Arya Samaj of Punjab.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arya_Samaj_in_Punjab">Arya Samaj in Punjab</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Arya Samaj in Punjab">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Punjab, the Arya Samaj was opposed by the <a href="/wiki/Ahmadiyya" title="Ahmadiyya">Ahmadiyya</a> movement which provided the Samaj one of its most aggressive opponenets from among the various Muslim groups and whose founder <a href="/wiki/Mirza_Ghulam_Ahmad" title="Mirza Ghulam Ahmad">Mirza Ghulam Ahmad</a> was extensively involved in theological disputations with Samaj leaders, most notably with <a href="/wiki/Pandit_Lekh_Ram" title="Pandit Lekh Ram">Pandit Lekh Ram</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> It was also opposed by the <a href="/wiki/Sikhism" title="Sikhism">Sikh</a> dominated <a href="/wiki/Singh_Sabha" class="mw-redirect" title="Singh Sabha">Singh Sabha</a>, the forerunner of the <a href="/wiki/Akali_Dal" class="mw-redirect" title="Akali Dal">Akali Dal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arya_Samaj_in_Gujarat">Arya Samaj in Gujarat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Arya Samaj in Gujarat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Arya Samaj of Gujarat members were missionaries from Punjab who had been encouraged to move to Gujarat to carry out educational work amongst the <a href="/wiki/Untouchability" title="Untouchability">untouchable castes</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Maharaja" title="Maharaja">maharaja</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sayajirao_Gaekwad_III" title="Sayajirao Gaekwad III">Sayajirao Gaekwad III</a>. The Gujarat Samaj opened orphanages. In 1915, the samaj lost its following to <a href="/wiki/Mahatma_Gandhi" title="Mahatma Gandhi">Mahatma Gandhi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reconversion_in_Malabar">Reconversion in Malabar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reconversion in Malabar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1921, during a rebellion by the Muslim <a href="/wiki/Moplah" class="mw-redirect" title="Moplah">Moplah</a> community of <a href="/wiki/Malabar_region" title="Malabar region">
Malabar</a> Indian newspapers reported that a number of Hindus were forcibly converted to Islam. The Arya Samaj extended its efforts to the region to reconvert these people back to Hinduism through <a href="/wiki/Shaucha" title="Shaucha">Shuddhi</a> ceremonies.
<sup id="cite_ref-Thursby_1975_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thursby_1975-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>p.141â152</span></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Views_of_Orthodox_Hindu_on_the_Samaj">Views of Orthodox Hindu on the Samaj</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Views of Orthodox Hindu on the Samaj">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The then <a href="/wiki/Shankaracharya" title="Shankaracharya">Shankaracharya</a> of Badrinath math in 1939 in a letter to the archbishop of Canterbury, called Arya Samajis Un-Hindu. He also criticized the samaj efforts at converting Christians and Muslims.<sup id="cite_ref-Benichou_2000_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Benichou_2000-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arya_Samaj_in_Hyderabad_state">Arya Samaj in Hyderabad state</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Arya Samaj in Hyderabad state">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A branch of Arya Samaj was established at Dharur in Beed district of <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Hyderabad state">Hyderabad state</a>, the largest princely state during British colonial rule. Keshavrao Koratkar was the president of the organization until 1932. During his tenure, the Samaj, established schools and libraries throughout the state. Although a social and religious organization, the Samaj activities assumed a great political role in resisting the government of the Nizam during 1930s. In 1938-1939, Arya Samaj teamed up with the Hindu Mahasabha to resist the government through Satyagraha. The Nizam government responded by raiding and desecrating Arya samaj mandirs. The Samaj, in turn, criticized Islam and the Islamic rulers of the state. This widely increased the gulf between the Hindu and Muslim population of the state.<sup id="cite_ref-Arya_Samaj_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arya_Samaj-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Benichou-2000_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Benichou-2000-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Language_issue">Language issue</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Language issue">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Arya Samaj promoted the use of Hindi in Punjab and discouraged the use of <a href="/wiki/Punjabi_language" title="Punjabi language">Punjabi</a>. This was a serious point of difference between the Sikhs, represented by the <a href="/wiki/Shiromani_Akali_Dal" title="Shiromani Akali Dal">Shiromani Akali Dal</a> group and the Arya Samaj. The difference was marked during the period immediately following the <a href="/wiki/Independence_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Independence of India">independence of India</a> and the time of the <a href="/wiki/Punjabi_Suba_movement" title="Punjabi Suba movement">Punjabi Suba movement</a> (demand for a Punjabi speaking state).<sup id="cite_ref-google_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-google-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-google2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-google2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-google3_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-google3-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Humanitarian_efforts">Humanitarian efforts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Humanitarian efforts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Arya Samaj was a charitable organisation. For example, donations were made to victims of the <a href="/wiki/1905_Kangra_earthquake" title="1905 Kangra earthquake">1905 Kangra earthquake</a>. The samaj campaigned for <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_right_to_vote" class="mw-redirect" title="Women&#39;s right to vote">women's right to vote</a>, and for the protection of widows.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Contemporary_Arya_Samaj">Contemporary Arya Samaj</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Contemporary Arya Samaj">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arya_Samaj_in_India">Arya Samaj in India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Arya Samaj in India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Arya Samaj schools and <a href="/wiki/Temple" title="Temple">temples</a> are found in almost all major cities and as well as in rural areas (esp in North region) of India. Some are authorised to conduct weddings. The Samaj is associated with the <a href="/wiki/Dayanand_Anglo_Vedic" class="mw-redirect" title="Dayanand Anglo Vedic">Dayanand Anglo Vedic</a> (DAV) schools which number over two hundred.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The former Indian prime minister <a href="/wiki/Charan_Singh" title="Charan Singh">Charan Singh</a>, as a young man, was a member of Arya Samaj in <a href="/wiki/Ghaziabad,_Uttar_Pradesh" class="mw-redirect" title="Ghaziabad, Uttar Pradesh">Ghaziabad</a>.
</p><p>A branch of Arya Samaj was established in 2015 in <a href="/wiki/Angul_district" title="Angul district">Angul district</a> in the state of Odisha<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arya_Samaj_around_the_world">Arya Samaj around the world</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Arya Samaj around the world">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Arya Samaj is active in countries including <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-aryasamajqueensland_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aryasamajqueensland-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> and other countries where a significant <a href="/wiki/Hindu_diaspora" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu diaspora">Hindu diaspora</a> is present.
</p><p>Immigrants to <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> from <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Africa" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastern Africa">Eastern Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> countries form Arya Samaj temples in their communities.<sup id="cite_ref-Vancouver_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vancouver-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Most major metropolitan areas of United States have chapters of Arya Samaj.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Members of the Arya Samaj believe in one almighty creator referred to with the syllable <i>Aum</i> as mentioned in the <a href="/wiki/Yajur_Veda" class="mw-redirect" title="Yajur Veda">Yajur Veda</a> (40:17). They believe the Vedas is an infallible authority. The Arya Samaj members reject other Hindu religious texts because they are not "revealed" works. For instance, they believe books like the <i><a href="/wiki/Ramayana" title="Ramayana">Ramayana</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Mahabharata" title="Mahabharata">Mahabharata</a></i> are legends of historical figures, and rejects them as reference to <a href="/wiki/God" title="God">supreme beings</a> and <a href="/wiki/Avatars" class="mw-redirect" title="Avatars">avatars</a>. The members of Arya Samaj reject other scriptural works such as the Puranas, The Upanishads, the <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Quran</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Jones1976_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jones1976-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> They reject the worship of <a href="/wiki/Cult_image" title="Cult image">idols</a>. The Arya Samaj promotes the equality of all human beings and the empowerment of women.
</p><p>The core beliefs of Arya Samaj are postulated below:<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ol><li>The primeval cause of all genuine knowledge and all that is known by means of knowledge is God.</li>
<li>God is Truth-consciousness â Bliss personified, Formless, Omnipotent, Just, Merciful, Unborn, Infinite, Unchangeable, Beginningless, Incomparable, Support of all, Lord of all, Omnipresent, Internal, the regulator of all, Undecaying, Immortal, Fearless, Eternal, Holy, and creator of the Universe. He alone deserves worship.</li>
<li>The Vedas are repositories of all of true knowledge. It is the paramount duty of all Aryas to study and teach and to propound the Veda.</li>
<li>We should be ever ready to imbibe truth and forsake untruth.</li>
<li>All acts should be done in accordance with Dharma, i.e. after deliberating upon what is truth and untruth.</li>
<li>The prime object of Arya Samaj is to do good to the whole world, i.e. to achieve physical, spiritual and social prosperity for all.</li>
<li>Our conduct towards all should be guided by love, by injunctions of Dharma and according to their respective positions.</li>
<li>One should dispel ignorance and promote knowledge.</li>
<li>One should not be content with one's own prosperity only, but should consider the prosperity of all as his own prosperity.</li>
<li>All human beings should abide by the rules concerning social or everyone's benefit, while everyone should be free to follow any rule beneficial for him/her.</li></ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practices">Practices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Practices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Arya_samaj_Mandir_hawan_kaksh.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Arya_samaj_Mandir_hawan_kaksh.jpg/220px-Arya_samaj_Mandir_hawan_kaksh.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="206" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Arya_samaj_Mandir_hawan_kaksh.jpg/330px-Arya_samaj_Mandir_hawan_kaksh.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Arya_samaj_Mandir_hawan_kaksh.jpg/440px-Arya_samaj_Mandir_hawan_kaksh.jpg 2x" data-file-width="920" data-file-height="860" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Arya_samaj_Mandir_hawan_kaksh.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Agnihotra" title="Agnihotra">Agnihotra</a> by <i>Arya Samaj</i>.</div></div></div>
<p>The Arya Samaj members consider the <a href="/wiki/Gayatri_Mantra" title="Gayatri Mantra">Gayatri Mantra</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> as the most holy mantra and chants it peridically, do the meditation known as 'Sandhya' and make offering to the holy fire '('<a href="/wiki/Havan" class="mw-redirect" title="Havan">havan</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The havan can be performed with a priest for special occasions or without a priest for personal worship. The havan is performed as per the havan pustika, usually a simplified guide to do havan, having mantras for general or special occasions. The priest is generally a Vedic scholar from the local Arya Samaj Mandir or Gurukul. Sometimes elder members of family or neighbours can also perform the havan acting as a purohit. The host is known as the 'Yajmana'. The priest can be called an 'Acharya', 'Swami ji' or 'Pandit Ji' depending upon his scholer status and local reputation. It is customary to give a nominal 'dakshina' to the priest after havan, although in Arya Samaj it is more symbolic and the priest doesn't state any sum. The sum is decided by the host's capability and status but is still a small amount.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Your explanation here (April 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Members celebrate <i><a href="/wiki/Holi" title="Holi">Holi</a></i> (the start of spring) and <i><a href="/wiki/Diwali" title="Diwali">Diwali</a></i> (a harvest festival and the victory of good over evil).
</p><p>Arya Samaj advocates a <a href="/wiki/Lacto-vegetarian" class="mw-redirect" title="Lacto-vegetarian">lacto-vegetarian</a> diet and in particular, the eating of beef is strictly prohibited.
</p><p>After a death, Arya Samaj will often conduct a haven and collect the ashes on the fourth day.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Diwali">Diwali</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Diwali">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dia_one_wick_divali_20151111.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Dia_one_wick_divali_20151111.jpg/170px-Dia_one_wick_divali_20151111.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="96" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Dia_one_wick_divali_20151111.jpg/255px-Dia_one_wick_divali_20151111.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Dia_one_wick_divali_20151111.jpg/340px-Dia_one_wick_divali_20151111.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="1836" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dia_one_wick_divali_20151111.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Diya with one wick.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dia_two_wicks_divali_20151111.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Dia_two_wicks_divali_20151111.jpg/170px-Dia_two_wicks_divali_20151111.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="96" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Dia_two_wicks_divali_20151111.jpg/255px-Dia_two_wicks_divali_20151111.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Dia_two_wicks_divali_20151111.jpg/340px-Dia_two_wicks_divali_20151111.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="1836" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dia_two_wicks_divali_20151111.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Diya with four wicks, pointing in each direction (N, W, S, E).</div></div></div>
<p>The Arya Samaj celebration of <i>Diwali</i> is typified by the celebration in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. The festival celebrates the victory of good over evil. A vegetarian fast is kept. The Gayatri mantra is spoken while oil lamps are lit. One <a href="/wiki/Diya_(lamp)" title="Diya (lamp)">Diya</a> lamp, which is of larger size has two wicks crossed to produce four lights, one in each direction and is lit first. The smaller lamp has one wick. The recitation of the Gayatri mantra occurs in front of a fire altar lit with sandalwood. A lamp is kept in every room except the bathroom and restroom. More lamps can be lit, which can be placed arbitrarily in the yard, living room and so on.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Holi">Holi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arya_Samaj&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Holi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Holi is celebrated as the conclusion of winter and the start of spring to sow the land and hope for a good harvest. This day is marked by colors and song (<i>Chautal</i>). It does not require specific prayer or fasting, however, some people keep a vegetarian fast on this day. The Arya Samaj does not associate Holi with a particular deity such as Vishnu or Shiva and in comparison to some interpretations of the festival, the Arya Samaj version in more sober and is as per the 4 Vedas.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
