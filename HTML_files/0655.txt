	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">George Hotel, Huddersfield</h1>

<p>The <b>George Hotel</b> in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England, is a <a href="/wiki/Grade_II#England_and_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="Grade II">Grade II</a> listed building famous as the birthplace of <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">rugby league</a> football in 1895.<sup id="cite_ref-George_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-George-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The 60 bed hotel was built in 1851 and closed in January 2013, with the <a href="/wiki/Receivership" title="Receivership">receivers</a> looking for a new buyer.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The three-star rated George Hotel, which has an <a href="/wiki/Italianate_architecture" title="Italianate architecture">Italianate façade</a>, was designed by William Walker.<sup id="cite_ref-Direct_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Direct-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Expedia_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Expedia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBCa_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCa-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The Victorian era hotel was built around 1851.<sup id="cite_ref-Expedia_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Expedia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Birth_of_Rugby_League">Birth of Rugby League</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hotel,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Birth of Rugby League">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/History_of_rugby_league" title="History of rugby league">History of rugby league</a></div>
<p>It was in the George Hotel, Huddersfield on 29 August 1895 that 21 Lancashire and Yorkshire clubs held a meeting and by a majority of 20 to 1 voted to <a href="/wiki/History_of_rugby_league" title="History of rugby league">secede</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_Union" title="Rugby Football Union">Rugby Football Union</a> to set up their own <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_League" title="Rugby Football League">Northern Rugby Football Union</a>. In 1922 this became the <a href="/wiki/Rugby_Football_League" title="Rugby Football League">Rugby Football League</a>.
</p><p>Stockport was also accepted into the league via telephone to the hotel.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCb_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCb-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Memorabilia recalling the meeting can be found throughout the hotel as well as in the Heritage Centre.<sup id="cite_ref-Expedia_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Expedia-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="British_Amateur_Rugby_League_Association">British Amateur Rugby League Association</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hotel,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: British Amateur Rugby League Association">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/British_Amateur_Rugby_League_Association" title="British Amateur Rugby League Association">British Amateur Rugby League Association</a> (BARLA) was also founded at the George Hotel in 1973.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby_League_Heritage_Centre">Rugby League Heritage Centre</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=George_Hotel,_Huddersfield&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rugby League Heritage Centre">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rugby_League_Heritage_Centre" title="Rugby League Heritage Centre">Rugby League Heritage Centre</a></div>
<p>The Rugby League Heritage Centre was located in the basement of the George. It was the UK’s only rugby league heritage museum. It was the brainchild of sports presenter <a href="/wiki/Mike_Stephenson" title="Mike Stephenson">Mike Stephenson</a>.
</p><p>The Rugby League Heritage centre was opened on 30 August 2005 by former players <a href="/wiki/Billy_Boston" title="Billy Boston">Billy Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Neil_Fox_(rugby_league)" title="Neil Fox (rugby league)">Neil Fox</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mick_Sullivan" title="Mick Sullivan">Mick Sullivan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rlheritage_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rlheritage-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Within the centre were displays of memorabilia, including rare jerseys, medals, caps, programmes and photographs. There was also footage played on several plasma screens. The <a href="/wiki/British_Rugby_League_Hall_of_Fame" class="mw-redirect" title="British Rugby League Hall of Fame">British Rugby League Hall of Fame</a> is now located in the Heritage Centre.
</p>
