	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en"><i>Why England Slept</i></h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sleeping_england.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/42/Sleeping_england.jpg/220px-Sleeping_england.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="310" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/4/42/Sleeping_england.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="238" data-file-height="335" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sleeping_england.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>First edition (publ. <a href="/wiki/Wilfred_Funk" class="mw-redirect" title="Wilfred Funk">Wilfred Funk</a>)</div></div></div>
<p><i><b>Why England Slept</b></i> is the published version of a <a href="/wiki/Thesis" title="Thesis">thesis</a> written by <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> while in his senior year at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_College" title="Harvard College">Harvard College</a>. Its title is an allusion to <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a>'s 1938 book <i><a href="/wiki/While_England_Slept" class="mw-redirect" title="While England Slept">While England Slept</a>,</i> which also examined the buildup of German power. Published in 1940,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Kennedy's book examines the failures of the <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Her Majesty&#39;s Government">British government</a> to take steps to prevent <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, and its initial lack of response to <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>'s threats of war.
</p><p>It is notable for its uncommon stance of suggesting instead that an earlier confrontation between the United Kingdom and <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> could well have been more disastrous in the long run, rather than castigating the <a href="/wiki/Appeasement_policy" class="mw-redirect" title="Appeasement policy">appeasement policy</a> which the British government pursued at the time.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publication">Publication</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Why_England_Slept&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Publication">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The book was originally intended to be no more than a college thesis. It was rated <i><a href="/wiki/Magna_cum_laude" class="mw-redirect" title="Magna cum laude">magna cum laude</a></i> by Professor Henry A. Yeomans and <i>cum laude plus</i> by Professor <a href="/wiki/Carl_J._Friedrich" class="mw-redirect" title="Carl J. Friedrich">Carl J. Friedrich</a>. However, Kennedy's father, <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy,_Sr." class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph P. Kennedy, Sr.">Joseph P. Kennedy, Sr.</a>, keen to elevate his son's reputation, encouraged Kennedy to convert the thesis into book form and publish it. He then enlisted <a href="/wiki/Henry_Luce" title="Henry Luce">Henry Luce</a> to write the foreword,<sup id="cite_ref-OBrien_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OBrien-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and his friend <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Krock" title="Arthur Krock">Arthur Krock</a>, former bureau chief of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a>,</i> to assist in redrafting the thesis, which had originally been titled "Appeasement At Munich".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The writer <a href="/wiki/Garry_Wills" title="Garry Wills">Garry Wills</a> claims that this assistance amounted to rewriting and retitling the manuscript, as well as finding an agent for its publication.<sup id="cite_ref-wills_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wills-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As <a href="/wiki/US_ambassador_to_Britain" class="mw-redirect" title="US ambassador to Britain">ambassador to Britain</a>, Kennedy, Sr. supported Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain" title="Neville Chamberlain">Neville Chamberlain</a>'s policy of appeasement during the late 1930s. John F. Kennedy lived with his father in Britain at that time and as such witnessed the Luftwaffe's bombings of Britain first-hand.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Why_England_Slept&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After publication in 1940, the book sold 80,000 copies in the United Kingdom and the United States, collecting US$40,000 in royalties for Kennedy. Those from the British sales were donated to <a href="/wiki/Plymouth,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Plymouth, England">Plymouth, England</a>, recently <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Blitz" title="Plymouth Blitz">bombed by the Luftwaffe</a>, while Kennedy bought a <a href="/wiki/Buick" title="Buick">Buick</a> <a href="/wiki/Convertible" title="Convertible">convertible</a> with his income from the book's North-American sales.<sup id="cite_ref-OBrien_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OBrien-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Joseph Kennedy had initially approached <a href="/wiki/Harold_Laski" title="Harold Laski">Harold Laski</a> to write the book's foreword, but Laski declined, feeling that it was "the book of an immature mind; that if it hadn't been written by the son of a very rich man, he wouldn't have found a publisher."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
