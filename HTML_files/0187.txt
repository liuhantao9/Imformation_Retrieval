<p><a href="/wiki/Oba_(ruler)" title="Oba (ruler)">Oba</a> <a href="/wiki/Sir" title="Sir">Sir</a> <b>Musendiku Buraimoh Adeniji Adele II</b>, <a href="/wiki/KBE" class="mw-redirect" title="KBE">KBE</a> (November 13, 1893 - July 12, 1964) was the <a href="/wiki/Oba_of_Lagos" title="Oba of Lagos">Oba (King) of Lagos</a> from October 1, 1949 to July 12, 1964.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adeniji_Adele&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adele was born in Lagos in 1893 to Buraimoh Adele and Moriamo Lalugbi. His grandfather was <a href="/wiki/Oba_(king)" class="mw-redirect" title="Oba (king)">Oba</a> <a href="/wiki/Adele_Ajosun" title="Adele Ajosun">Adele Ajosun</a>. He studied at Holy Trinity Primary School, Ebutte-Ero and then at <a href="/wiki/CMS_Grammar_School,_Lagos" title="CMS Grammar School, Lagos">CMS Grammar School, Lagos</a>. After his secondary education, he joined the colonial service as a trainee surveyor, after completing his training, he was posted to <a href="/wiki/Kano" title="Kano">Kano</a> as a surveyor. He served as a land surveyor with the Cameroon Expeditionary Force during <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. Adele later worked with the treasury department and became a chief clerk in 1937.
</p><p>He was awarded the titles of Commander of the Order of the British Empire (1956) and Knight of the Order of the British Empire (1962) by the <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Queen of England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Politics_.26_Legal_Challenge"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Politics_&amp;_Legal_Challenge">Politics &amp; Legal Challenge</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Adeniji_Adele&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Politics &amp; Legal Challenge">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Oba Adele II was a supporter of the Nigerian Youth Movement and a member of <a href="/wiki/Egbe_Omo_Oduduwa" title="Egbe Omo Oduduwa">Egbe Omo Oduduwa</a> led by <a href="/wiki/Obafemi_Awolowo" title="Obafemi Awolowo">Obafemi Awolowo</a>. His political affiliation was therefore in opposition to the ruling <a href="/wiki/National_Council_of_Nigeria_and_the_Cameroons" title="National Council of Nigeria and the Cameroons">NCNC</a>/<a href="/wiki/Nigerian_National_Democratic_Party" title="Nigerian National Democratic Party">NNDP</a> (whose political bedrock was the House of <a href="/wiki/Dosunmu" title="Dosunmu">Docemo</a>) founded by <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Macaulay" title="Herbert Macaulay">Herbert Macaulay</a> and later led by <a href="/wiki/Nnamdi_Azikiwe" title="Nnamdi Azikiwe">Nnamdi Azikiwe</a>. NNDP was opposed to Oba Adele's ascension, him not being descended from <a href="/wiki/Dosunmu" title="Dosunmu">Dosunmu</a>, and filed legal challenges to thwart his coronation. Oba Adele's right to the throne was finally sustained by the <a href="/wiki/Judicial_Committee_of_the_Privy_Council" title="Judicial Committee of the Privy Council">Judicial Committee of the Privy Council</a> in England in 1957.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
