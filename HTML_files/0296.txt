</ol></div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The <b>Community of Latin American and Caribbean States</b> (Spanish: <i lang="es">Comunidad de Estados Latinoamericanos y Caribeños, CELAC</i>; <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>: <i lang="pt">Comunidade de Estados Latino-Americanos e Caribenhos</i>; French: <i lang="fr">Communauté des États latino-américains et caribéens</i>; <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <i lang="nl">Gemeenschap van Latijns-Amerikaanse en Caraïbische Staten</i>) is a regional bloc of Latin American and <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> states thought out on February 23, 2010, at the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Group" title="Rio Group">Rio Group</a>–<a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Community" title="Caribbean Community">Caribbean Community</a> <i>Unity Summit</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and created on December 3, 2011, in <a href="/wiki/Caracas" title="Caracas">Caracas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, with the signature of The Declaration of Caracas.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It consists of 33 sovereign countries in the Americas representing roughly 600 million people. Due to the focus of the organization on Latin American and Caribbean countries, other countries and territories in the Americas, Canada and the United States, as well as the overseas territories in the Americas of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> (<a href="/wiki/Overseas_departments_and_territories_of_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Overseas departments and territories of France">Overseas departments and territories of France</a>), the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_Caribbean" title="Dutch Caribbean">Dutch Caribbean</a>), <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> (<a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> (<a href="/wiki/British_Overseas_Territories" title="British Overseas Territories">British Overseas Territories</a>) are not included.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>CELAC is an example of a decade-long push for deeper integration within Latin America.<sup id="cite_ref-en.mercopress.com_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-en.mercopress.com-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> CELAC was created to deepen <a href="/wiki/Latin_American_integration" title="Latin American integration">Latin American integration</a> and by some to reduce the significant influence of the United States on the politics and economics of Latin America. It is seen as an alternative to the <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_American_States" title="Organization of American States">Organization of American States</a> (OAS), the regional body that was founded by United States and 21 other Latin American nations originally as a countermeasure to potential Soviet influence in the region.<sup id="cite_ref-en.mercopress.com_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-en.mercopress.com-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-telesurtv.net_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telesurtv.net-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>CELAC is the successor of the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Group" title="Rio Group">Rio Group</a> and the <i>Latin American and Caribbean Summit on Integration and Development</i> (CALC).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In July 2010, CELAC selected <a href="/wiki/President_of_Venezuela" title="President of Venezuela">President of Venezuela</a> <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Ch%C3%A1vez" title="Hugo Chávez">Hugo Chávez</a> and <a href="/wiki/President_of_Chile" title="President of Chile">President of Chile</a> <a href="/wiki/Sebasti%C3%A1n_Pi%C3%B1era" title="Sebastián Piñera">Sebastián Piñera</a>, as co-chairs of the forum to draft statutes for the organization.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2008.E2.80.932010:_Brazil_and_Mexico_initiatives"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2008–2010:_Brazil_and_Mexico_initiatives">2008–2010: Brazil and Mexico initiatives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2008–2010: Brazil and Mexico initiatives">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Rio_Group" title="Rio Group">Rio Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/I_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_Summit_on_Integration_and_Development" title="I Latin American and Caribbean Summit on Integration and Development">I Latin American and Caribbean Summit on Integration and Development</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Latin_American_and_Caribbean_Unity_Summit" title="Latin American and Caribbean Unity Summit">Latin American and Caribbean Unity Summit</a></div>
<p>The immediate predecessor of the CELAC is the <a href="/wiki/Rio_Group" title="Rio Group">Rio Group</a>. Formed in 1986, it gathered 24 Latin American and Caribbean countries around summits to cooperate regional policy issue independently of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERojas_AravenaAltmann_BorbónBeirute_Brealey201225-27_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERojas_AravenaAltmann_BorbónBeirute_Brealey201225-27-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 16–17 December 2008, the <a href="/wiki/I_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_Summit_on_Integration_and_Development" title="I Latin American and Caribbean Summit on Integration and Development">I Latin American and Caribbean Summit on Integration and Development</a> (CALC) took place in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Costa_do_Sauipe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Costa do Sauipe (page does not exist)">Costa do Sauipe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bahia" title="Bahia">Bahia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. It was organized at the initiative of the <a href="/wiki/Luiz_In%C3%A1cio_Lula_da_Silva" title="Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva">Lula</a> administration with the goal of building cooperation mechanism with greater autonomy from the United States and Canada. Most heads of state from Latin America and the Caribbean states attended, with the exception of <a href="/wiki/President_of_Colombia" title="President of Colombia">President of Colombia</a> <a href="/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Uribe" title="Álvaro Uribe">Álvaro Uribe</a> and <a href="/wiki/President_of_Peru" title="President of Peru">President of Peru</a> <a href="/wiki/Alan_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Alan García">Alan García</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERojas_AravenaAltmann_BorbónBeirute_Brealey201227_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERojas_AravenaAltmann_BorbónBeirute_Brealey201227-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The summit finished with the signing of the <i>Bahia Declaration</i>, a common agenda establishing the following priorities: cooperation between mechanism of regional and subregional integration, the <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007%E2%80%932008" title="Financial crisis of 2007–2008">global financial crisis</a>, energy, infrastructures, social development and eradication of hunger and poverty, <a href="/wiki/Food_security" title="Food security">food security</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_development" title="Sustainable development">sustainable development</a>, <a href="/wiki/Natural_disaster" title="Natural disaster">natural disasters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a> promotion, <a href="/wiki/International_migration" title="International migration">migration</a>, <a href="/wiki/South%E2%80%93South_cooperation" title="South–South cooperation">South–South cooperation</a> and Latin America and Caribbean projection.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERojas_AravenaAltmann_BorbónBeirute_Brealey201228_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERojas_AravenaAltmann_BorbónBeirute_Brealey201228-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, the <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Calder%C3%B3n_Hinojosa" class="mw-redirect" title="Felipe Calderón Hinojosa">Calderón</a> administration of <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a> proposed the creation of the Latin American and the Caribbean Union (Spanish: <i>Unión Latinoamericana y del Caribe</i>, ULC). The proposal was formalized on 27 March 2009 at <a href="/wiki/Rio_Group" title="Rio Group">Rio Group</a> meeting. At the initiative of Mexico, the XXI Rio Summit and the II CALC summit were held together on 22–23 February 2010 in <a href="/wiki/Playa_del_Carmen" title="Playa del Carmen">Playa del Carmen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>. The joint summit was named the <a href="/wiki/Latin_American_and_Caribbean_Unity_Summit" title="Latin American and Caribbean Unity Summit">Latin American and Caribbean Unity Summit</a> and the 32 attending states decided to create the Community of Latin American and Caribbean States (CELAC), which would be formally established in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERojas_AravenaAltmann_BorbónBeirute_Brealey201229-30_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTERojas_AravenaAltmann_BorbónBeirute_Brealey201229-30-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prominent left-wing leaders in the bloc praised the founding of the organization as a step towards separating Latin America from the United States with <a href="/wiki/Evo_Morales" title="Evo Morales">Evo Morales</a>, President of Bolivia saying "A union of Latin American countries is the weapon against imperialism. It is necessary to create a regional body that excludes the United States and Canada. ... it is the best time for prime ministers of Latin America and the Caribbean to gestate this great new organization without the United States to free our peoples in Latin America and the Caribbean."<sup id="cite_ref-telesurtv.net_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telesurtv.net-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Ch%C3%A1vez" title="Hugo Chávez">Hugo Chávez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luiz_In%C3%A1cio_Lula_da_Silva" title="Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva">Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Correa" title="Rafael Correa">Rafael Correa</a> were among the other prominent left-wing leaders who praised the creation of CELAC <sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-estadao_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-estadao-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The announcement prompted debate and discussion across Latin America and the Caribbean about whether it was more beneficial to have close ties with U.S. and Canada or to work independently.<sup id="cite_ref-rueda_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rueda-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ptv_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ptv-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Raúl Zibechi, writing for Mexico's center-left <i><a href="/wiki/La_Jornada" title="La Jornada">La Jornada</a></i> newspaper said, "The creation of the Community of Latin American and Caribbean States is part of a global and continental shift, characterized by the decline of U.S. hegemony and the rise of a group of regional blocs that form part of the new global balance."<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>An editorial in Brazil's conservative <i><a href="/wiki/O_Estado_de_S._Paulo" title="O Estado de S. Paulo">Estadão</a></i> newspaper said, "CELAC reflects the disorientation of the region's governments in relation to its problematic environment and its lack of foreign policy direction, locked as it is into the illusion that snubbing the United States will do for Latin American integration what 200 years of history failed to do."<sup id="cite_ref-estadao_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-estadao-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011:_Founding">2011: Founding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2011: Founding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2011_CELAC_summit" title="2011 CELAC summit">2011 CELAC summit</a></div>
<p>CELAC's inaugural summit was due to be held in mid-2011, but was postponed because of the ill-health of <a href="/wiki/Hugo_Ch%C3%A1vez" title="Hugo Chávez">Hugo Chávez</a>, president of the host nation, Venezuela. The summit was instead held on December 2 and 3, 2011 in <a href="/wiki/Caracas" title="Caracas">Caracas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> It primarily focused on the global economic crisis and its effects on the region. Several leaders, including presidents <a href="/wiki/Cristina_Fern%C3%A1ndez_de_Kirchner" title="Cristina Fernández de Kirchner">Cristina Fernández de Kirchner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dilma_Rousseff" title="Dilma Rousseff">Dilma Rousseff</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Santos" title="Juan Manuel Santos">Juan Manuel Santos</a>, encouraged an increase in regional trade, economic development, and further economic cooperation among members in order to defend their growing economies.<sup id="cite_ref-rueda_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rueda-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ptv_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ptv-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Chavez, and other leaders such as <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Correa" title="Rafael Correa">Rafael Correa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ortega" title="Daniel Ortega">Daniel Ortega</a>, expressed hope that the bloc would work to further <a href="/wiki/Latin_American_integration" title="Latin American integration">Latin American integration</a>, end U.S. hegemony and consolidate control over regional affairs.<sup id="cite_ref-rueda_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rueda-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Chavez, citing the <a href="/wiki/Monroe_Doctrine" title="Monroe Doctrine">Monroe Doctrine</a> as the original confirmation of U.S. interference in the region, openly called for CELAC to replace the OAS: "As the years go by, CELAC is going to leave behind the old and worn-out OAS."<sup id="cite_ref-ptv_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ptv-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Correa called for a new human rights commission to replace the <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Commission_on_Human_Rights" title="Inter-American Commission on Human Rights">Inter-American Commission on Human Rights</a>. Other leaders argued that the organisation should be used as a tool to resolve regional disagreements and uphold democratic values, but not as a replacement of the OAS.<sup id="cite_ref-rueda_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rueda-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ptv_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ptv-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Santos stated that he would like to see dialogue within the group over whether existing counter-drug regulations should be revised.<sup id="cite_ref-rueda_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rueda-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The president of the <a href="/wiki/Latin_American_Parliament" title="Latin American Parliament">Latin American Parliament</a> (Parlatino) said he expects that Parlatino will become the main legislative institution of CELAC.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Amongst the key issues on the agenda were the creation of a "new financial architecture," sanction for maintaining the legal status of <a href="/wiki/Coca" title="Coca">coca</a> in Bolivia and the rejection of the <a href="/wiki/Cuban_embargo" class="mw-redirect" title="Cuban embargo">Cuban embargo</a> by the U.S.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>United States President Barack Obama's senior adviser on Latin America, Daniel Restrepo, informed reporters from Miami that the U.S. government would "watch and see what direction CELAC takes".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2013_Summit_.E2.80.93_Chile"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_Summit_–_Chile">2013 Summit – Chile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2013 Summit – Chile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</p>
<h3><span id="2014_Summit_.E2.80.93_Cuba"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014_Summit_–_Cuba">2014 Summit – Cuba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2014 Summit – Cuba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2014_CELAC_summit" title="2014 CELAC summit">2014 CELAC summit</a></div>
<p>During the summit, the region was declared a "peace zone". After three days and with the approval of participating representatives, a document with 83 focus points was created. It emphasized that, despite cultural and regional differences, unity between the participating countries is necessary in order to create progress. "Unity and the integration of our region must be gradually constructed, with flexibility, with respect to differences, diversity, and the sovereign right of each of our countries to choose our own forms of political and economic organization" stated the document. It also states which countries have been developing the best and how they are doing it in order for them to be a model for other countries.
</p><p>The issue of poverty was widely discussed. Cuba's Raul Castro pointing out that throughout Latin America and the Caribbean, people want a fairer distribution of wealth, access to affordable education, employment, better salaries, and the eradication of illiteracy. He argued that CELAC countries can work together, support each other, to create new plans and solutions for these problems.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015_Summit_.E2.80.93_Costa_Rica"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015_Summit_–_Costa_Rica">2015 Summit – Costa Rica</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2015 Summit – Costa Rica">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2015_CELAC_summit" title="2015 CELAC summit">2015 CELAC summit</a></div>
<p>Countries discussed plans to eradicate hunger by 2025. Venezuela would host a follow up meeting in late 2015 to review the United Nations Food and Agricultural Organizations plan for their region. They called for the U.S to stop meddling in regional affairs, for Puerto Rico to be able to participate in future summits, and for the embargo against Cuba to be lifted. Statements in support of Cuba and Venezuela were widely heard. CELAC rejected U.S. sanctions against Venezuela. It was decided that a monument would be built in memory of victims of the slave trade. Member countries also supported Argentina's right to the Falkland Islands and peace negotiations in Colombia.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2017_CELAC_summit" title="2017 CELAC summit">2017 CELAC summit</a></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organization">Organization</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Community_of_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_States&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Organization">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Pro_tempore_presidency_of_the_Community_of_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_States" title="Pro tempore presidency of the Community of Latin American and Caribbean States">Pro tempore presidency of the Community of Latin American and Caribbean States</a></div>
<p>The CELAC has six organs:<sup id="cite_ref-bernal_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bernal-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>The summit of <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">Heads of State</a> and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">Government</a>.</li>
<li>The meeting of <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs" title="Ministry of Foreign Affairs">Ministers of Foreign Affairs</a>.</li>
<li>The meeting of National Coordinators.</li>
<li>The <i>pro tempore</i> presidency.</li>
<li>The Troika.</li></ul>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Pro_tempore_presidency_of_the_Community_of_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_States" title="Pro tempore presidency of the Community of Latin American and Caribbean States"><i>pro tempore</i> presidency</a> is the main representative of the CELAC. The troika is composed by the current <i>pro tempore</i> presidency, its predecessor, its successor and the presidency of the <a href="/wiki/CARICOM" class="mw-redirect" title="CARICOM">CARICOM</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bernal_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bernal-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
