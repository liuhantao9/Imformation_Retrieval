<p><b>ICC Americas</b> is an international body which oversees cricket in countries in North and South America, and the Caribbean islands. It is a subordinate body to the <a href="/wiki/International_Cricket_Council" title="International Cricket Council">International Cricket Council</a>. The organisation currently has 17 members, located in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, and is responsible for the development, promotion and administration of the game in the above regions.
</p><p>The body is also responsible for the <a href="/wiki/ICC_Americas_Championship" title="ICC Americas Championship">ICC Americas Championship</a>, which is the premier international competition in the region, and allows teams to compete for <a href="/wiki/ICC_Cricket_World_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC Cricket World Cup">World Cup</a> qualification. The World Cup itself has only been held in the region on a single occasion, when the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2007 Cricket World Cup">2007 World Cup</a> was hosted by the West Indies. The organisation is also responsible for hosting the <a href="/wiki/ICC_Americas_Under-19_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC Americas Under-19 Championship">ICC Americas Under-19 Championship</a>, which fills a similar role in qualification for the <a href="/wiki/Under-19_Cricket_World_Cup" title="Under-19 Cricket World Cup">Under-19 World Cup</a>. A separate <a href="/wiki/South_American_Cricket_Championship" title="South American Cricket Championship">South American Cricket Championship</a> exists, but is not organised by the ICC.
</p><p>Previously the regional office was located in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>, but it was relocated to <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Springs,_Colorado" title="Colorado Springs, Colorado">Colorado Springs, Colorado</a> in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-Colorado_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Colorado-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_Members">Former Members</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICC_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Former Members">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><span class="flagicon"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Flag_of_Cuba.svg/23px-Flag_of_Cuba.svg.png" decoding="async" width="23" height="12" class="thumbborder" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Flag_of_Cuba.svg/35px-Flag_of_Cuba.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Flag_of_Cuba.svg/46px-Flag_of_Cuba.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="600" />&#160;</span><a href="/wiki/Cuba_national_cricket_team" title="Cuba national cricket team">Cuba</a></li></ul>
<p>Cuba, which had its ICC membership revoked in 2012, was invited to participate in the <a href="/wiki/2010_ICC_Americas_Championship_Division_Four" title="2010 ICC Americas Championship Division Four">2010 ICC Americas Championship Division Four</a>, but withdrew due to travel restrictions.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is unclear if Cuba remains a formal member of ICC Americas.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Related_organisations">Related organisations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICC_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Related organisations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The short-lived <b>Cricket Council of the Americas</b> (CCAM) was formed in April 2001, after delegates from fifteen countries met in <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a>. The organisation was patterned after the three existing regional cricket councils at the time,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup> and it was noted that one of its primary reasons for existence was "to deliver more effectively and efficiently the ICC development programme". It was stated that, as a result of the council's establishment, "the region [would] now be responsible for the strategy and the delivery of its own development programmes, as well as tournament management and funding arrangements".<sup id="cite_ref-nicholas_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nicholas-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The council had fifteen members at its peak, listed below.<sup id="cite_ref-Chile_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chile-5">&#91;b&#93;</a></sup> The inaugural meeting was also attended by delegates from two other countries, <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">Guadeloupe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, but those countries were not given membership.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Representative_teams">Representative teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICC_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Representative teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Senior_men.27s_team"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior_men's_team">Senior men's team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICC_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Senior men&#039;s team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In June 2015, it was announced that a combined team from the Americas development region would participate in the 2015–16 season of the <a href="/wiki/Regional_Super50" title="Regional Super50">Regional Super50</a>, the West Indian domestic limited-overs competition.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> To select the squad for the tournament, an open combine was held in September 2015 at the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_World_Sports_Park" title="Indianapolis World Sports Park">Indianapolis World Sports Park</a>, divided into two sections. The first part of the combine featured 83 invitees from five countries, although the vast majority (65, or 78 percent) were from the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Another 21 players were fast-tracked to the second part of the combine, where they were eventually joined by 12 of the first-section participants.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The final squad featured nine Americans and six Canadians, with no other countries represented.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Senior_women.27s_team"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior_women's_team">Senior women's team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICC_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Senior women&#039;s team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the 2007 Women's Americas Championship in <a href="/wiki/King_City,_Ontario" title="King City, Ontario">King City, Ontario</a>, an Americas Select XI was selected from the three participating teams (<a href="/wiki/Argentina_women%27s_national_cricket_team" title="Argentina women&#39;s national cricket team">Argentina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_women%27s_national_cricket_team" title="Bermuda women&#39;s national cricket team">Bermuda</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Canada_women%27s_national_cricket_team" title="Canada women&#39;s national cricket team">Canada</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Captained by Ave Mogan, a Canadian, the team played a one-off match against the <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago_women%27s_national_cricket_team" title="Trinidad and Tobago women&#39;s national cricket team">Trinidad and Tobago</a> national women's under-17 side, which it lost by five wickets.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The exercise was repeated after the 2009 Americas Championship in <a href="/wiki/Fort_Lauderdale" class="mw-redirect" title="Fort Lauderdale">Fort Lauderdale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, which featured two new teams (<a href="/wiki/Brazil_women%27s_national_cricket_team" title="Brazil women&#39;s national cricket team">Brazil</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_women%27s_national_cricket_team" title="United States women&#39;s national cricket team">United States</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> With U.S. player <a href="/wiki/Roselyn_Emmanuel" title="Roselyn Emmanuel">Roselyn Emmanuel</a> serving as captain, the team's opponent was a Trinidad and Tobago development XI, which went on to win by 29 runs in a rain-interrupted match at <a href="/wiki/Central_Broward_Regional_Park" title="Central Broward Regional Park">Central Broward Regional Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Age-group_teams">Age-group teams</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=ICC_Americas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Age-group teams">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/2000_ICC_Under-19_Cricket_World_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup">2000 Under-19 World Cup</a> in Sri Lanka, the Americas development region was represented by a combined team, captained by future Canadian international <a href="/wiki/Ashish_Bagai" title="Ashish Bagai">Ashish Bagai</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-u19odi_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-u19odi-16">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The team failed to win a single match (a fate it shared with <a href="/wiki/Namibia_national_under-19_cricket_team" title="Namibia national under-19 cricket team">Namibia</a>), with its closest game being a seven-run loss to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-19_cricket_team" title="Netherlands national under-19 cricket team">Netherlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Four countries were represented in the 14-man squad, which featured three Americans, three Argentines, four Bermudians, and four Canadians.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> All matches at the World Cup held under-19 <a href="/wiki/One_Day_International" title="One Day International">One Day International</a> (ODI) status.<sup id="cite_ref-u19odi_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-u19odi-16">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A combined Americas under-19 team also played at the 2002, 2003, and 2004 editions of the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_Cricket_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="West Indies Cricket Board">West Indies Cricket Board</a>'s under-19 tournament. The 2002 event featured both three-day and one-day tournaments, with the latter played as a knock-out cup. However, the team, which featured <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Caymanian</a> players for the first time, did not win a match in either format.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> That performance was repeated at both the 2003 and 2004 events.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, it was announced that the Americas development region would field a combined team in the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_Cricket_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="West Indies Cricket Board">WICB</a> regional under-19 tournament, although it would have the age restriction loosened by one year compared to the other teams.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The 2012 tournament was interrupted by rain, but the Americas under-20s side lost all three of its six matches where a result was possible.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The team won its first ever match the following year, defeating the <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands_cricket_team" title="Leeward Islands cricket team">Leeward Islands</a> by three wickets,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> but were once again winless at the 2014 event, and were not invited back in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
