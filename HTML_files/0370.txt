<p><b>Javanese Surinamese people</b> are an ethnic group of <a href="/wiki/Javanese_people" title="Javanese people">Javanese</a> descent in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. They have been present since the late 19th century, when their first members were selected by the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Empire" title="Dutch Empire">Dutch colonizers</a> from the former <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Javanese_Surinamese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:COLLECTIE_TROPENMUSEUM_Suriname_immigranten_afkomstig_uit_Nederlands-Indi%C3%AB_de_vrouw_rechts_draagt_een_peniti_tak_broche_TMnr_60008927.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/COLLECTIE_TROPENMUSEUM_Suriname_immigranten_afkomstig_uit_Nederlands-Indi%C3%AB_de_vrouw_rechts_draagt_een_peniti_tak_broche_TMnr_60008927.jpg/200px-COLLECTIE_TROPENMUSEUM_Suriname_immigranten_afkomstig_uit_Nederlands-Indi%C3%AB_de_vrouw_rechts_draagt_een_peniti_tak_broche_TMnr_60008927.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/COLLECTIE_TROPENMUSEUM_Suriname_immigranten_afkomstig_uit_Nederlands-Indi%C3%AB_de_vrouw_rechts_draagt_een_peniti_tak_broche_TMnr_60008927.jpg/300px-COLLECTIE_TROPENMUSEUM_Suriname_immigranten_afkomstig_uit_Nederlands-Indi%C3%AB_de_vrouw_rechts_draagt_een_peniti_tak_broche_TMnr_60008927.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/COLLECTIE_TROPENMUSEUM_Suriname_immigranten_afkomstig_uit_Nederlands-Indi%C3%AB_de_vrouw_rechts_draagt_een_peniti_tak_broche_TMnr_60008927.jpg/400px-COLLECTIE_TROPENMUSEUM_Suriname_immigranten_afkomstig_uit_Nederlands-Indi%C3%AB_de_vrouw_rechts_draagt_een_peniti_tak_broche_TMnr_60008927.jpg 2x" data-file-width="700" data-file-height="548" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:COLLECTIE_TROPENMUSEUM_Suriname_immigranten_afkomstig_uit_Nederlands-Indi%C3%AB_de_vrouw_rechts_draagt_een_peniti_tak_broche_TMnr_60008927.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Javanese_people" title="Javanese people">Javanese</a> immigrants from the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a>, picture taken between 1880-1900.</div></div></div>
<p>After the abolition of slavery, the plantations in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> needed a new source of labor. In 1890, the influential <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Trading_Society" title="Netherlands Trading Society">Netherlands Trading Society</a>, owner of the plantation <a href="/wiki/Mari%C3%ABnburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Mariënburg">Mariënburg</a> in Suriname, undertook a test to attract <a href="/wiki/Javanese_people" title="Javanese people">Javanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Labor_contract" class="mw-redirect" title="Labor contract">contract workers</a> from the Dutch East Indies. Until then, primarily <a href="/wiki/Indo-Surinamese" title="Indo-Surinamese">Hindustani</a> contract workers from <a href="/wiki/British_Raj" title="British Raj">British India</a> worked at the Suriname plantations as field and factory workers. 
On 9 August, the first <a href="/wiki/Java" title="Java">Javanese</a> arrived in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>. The test was considered successful and by 1894 the colonial government took over the task of recruiting Javanese hands. They came in small groups from the Dutch East Indies to the Netherlands, and from there to Paramaribo. The transport of Javanese immigrants continued until 1914 (except 1894) in two stages through Amsterdam.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Javanese_immigration_memorial,_Paramaribo,_Suriname.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Javanese_immigration_memorial%2C_Paramaribo%2C_Suriname.jpg/220px-Javanese_immigration_memorial%2C_Paramaribo%2C_Suriname.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="391" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Javanese_immigration_memorial%2C_Paramaribo%2C_Suriname.jpg/330px-Javanese_immigration_memorial%2C_Paramaribo%2C_Suriname.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Javanese_immigration_memorial%2C_Paramaribo%2C_Suriname.jpg/440px-Javanese_immigration_memorial%2C_Paramaribo%2C_Suriname.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2988" data-file-height="5312" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Javanese_immigration_memorial,_Paramaribo,_Suriname.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Monument commemorating the 100 years (1890-1990) of Javanese presence in Suriname. Sana Budaya, Paramaribo, Suriname.</div></div></div>
<p>The workers came from villages in <a href="/wiki/Central_Java" title="Central Java">Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/East_Java" title="East Java">East Java</a>. Departure points were <a href="/wiki/Batavia_(Dutch_East_Indies)" class="mw-redirect" title="Batavia (Dutch East Indies)">Batavia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Semarang" title="Semarang">Semarang</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tanjung_Priok" title="Tanjung Priok">Tandjong Priok</a>. The recruited workers and their families awaited their departure in a depot, where they were inspected and registered and where they signed their contract.
</p><p>The immigrants were recruited to work on the <a href="/wiki/Plantation" title="Plantation">plantations</a>. The exception was a group in 1904, when 77 Javanese were recruited specifically to work at the Colonial Railways. From World War I Javanese also worked at the Suriname Bauxite Company in <a href="/wiki/Moengo" title="Moengo">Moengo</a>. Immigration continued until 13 December 1939. The outbreak of World War Two ended transplantation schemes.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Javanese_Surinamese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A total of 32,965 Javanese immigrants went to Suriname. In 1954, 8,684 Javanese returned to <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, with the rest remaining in Suriname. The census of 1972 counted 57,688 Javanese in Suriname, and in 2004 there were 71,879. In addition, in 2004 more than 60,000 people of mixed descent were recorded, with an unknown number of part Javanese descent.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diaspora">Diaspora</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Javanese_Surinamese&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Diaspora">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1953, a large group of 300 families (1,200 people), led by <a href="/wiki/Salikin_Hardjo" title="Salikin Hardjo">Salikin Hardjo</a>, went back to Indonesia on the ship <i>Langkuas</i> of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Royal_Rotterdam_Lloyd&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Royal Rotterdam Lloyd (page does not exist)">Royal Rotterdam Lloyd</a>. They intended to settle in <a href="/wiki/Java" title="Java">Java</a> or <a href="/wiki/Lampung" title="Lampung">Lampung</a>, but their request was not approved by the Indonesian government, and instead they were sent to <a href="/wiki/West_Sumatra" title="West Sumatra">West Sumatra</a>. They established the village of <i>Tongar</i>, also referred to as Tongass in <a href="/wiki/Kabupaten" class="mw-redirect" title="Kabupaten">Kabupaten</a> <a href="/wiki/Pasaman" class="mw-redirect" title="Pasaman">Pasaman</a>, north of  <a href="/wiki/Padang,_Indonesia" class="mw-redirect" title="Padang, Indonesia">Padang</a>, clearing land and building new houses. They integrated smoothly with the <a href="/wiki/Minangkabau_people" title="Minangkabau people">Minangkabau</a> community, despite the fact that most of the Javanese were Christian. Marriages with the mainly Muslim Minangkabau were common. The current generation is said to identify more as Indonesian than Surinamese, but still maintain contacts with family and friends in Suriname and the Netherlands, sometimes traveling to those countries.
</p><p>In the 1970s, 20-25,000 Javanese Surinamese went to the Netherlands. They settled mainly in and around cities such as <a href="/wiki/Groningen_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="Groningen (city)">Groningen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Hague" title="The Hague">The Hague</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zoetermeer" title="Zoetermeer">Zoetermeer</a>. They are well integrated into Dutch society, but preserve their Javanese identity through associations and regularly organized meetings. Most still have relatives in Suriname and send  <a href="/wiki/Remittance" title="Remittance">remittances</a>, and regularly visit Suriname.
</p>
