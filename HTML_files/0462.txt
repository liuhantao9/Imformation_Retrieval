<p><b>Georginio Gregion Emile Wijnaldum</b> (<small>Dutch pronunciation:&#32;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Dutch" title="Help:IPA/Dutch">[ɟɔrˈɟiɲoː ʋɛiˈnɑldʏm]</a></span>; born 11 November 1990) is a Dutch professional <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">footballer</a> who plays as a <a href="/wiki/Midfielder" title="Midfielder">midfielder</a> for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands national team</a>.
</p><p>A youth product of <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> side <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a>, Wijnaldum became the youngest player ever to represent the club when he made his debut in 2007, and went on to play 134 matches over the course of a five-year spell. Following his departure from Feyenoord, Wijnaldum spent four seasons at <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV Eindhoven</a> where he won the <a href="/wiki/KNVB_Cup" title="KNVB Cup">KNVB Cup</a> in the first season and the Eredivisie in his last. He also earned the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Footballer_of_the_Year" title="Dutch Footballer of the Year">Dutch Footballer of the Year</a> award during his time with PSV. 
</p><p>In 2015, Wijnaldum joined Premier League side <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> for £14.5&#160;million, before departing following the club's relegation a year later to join Liverpool in a £23&#160;million deal. In the 2018–19 season, Wijnaldum scored twice against Barcelona in the Champions League semi-final second leg comeback as Liverpool went through 4–3 on aggregate, and he started as the club were victorious in the <a href="/wiki/2019_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2019 UEFA Champions League Final">2019 UEFA Champions League Final</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wijnaldum is also a full Dutch international, having earned more than 50 caps since making his debut in 2011, and was a member of the squad which came third at the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wijnaldum was born and raised in <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Holland" title="South Holland">South Holland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Liverpool_transfer_target_Georginio_Wijnaldum:_10_things_you_need_to_know!_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Liverpool_transfer_target_Georginio_Wijnaldum:_10_things_you_need_to_know!-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> When he was six years old, his parents, who are both of <a href="/wiki/Afro-Surinamese" title="Afro-Surinamese">Afro-Surinamese</a> descent, went through a divorce resulting in his mother moving to <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Liverpool_transfer_target_Georginio_Wijnaldum:_10_things_you_need_to_know!_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Liverpool_transfer_target_Georginio_Wijnaldum:_10_things_you_need_to_know!-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> However, Wijnaldum decided to stay in Rotterdam and moved in with his grandmother, where he lived for the remainder of his childhood.<sup id="cite_ref-10_things_you_need_to_know_about_Georginio_Wijnaldum_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10_things_you_need_to_know_about_Georginio_Wijnaldum-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Wijnaldum's younger years, he never showed interest in football. He never played with a ball or watched football on TV. His ambition was to become a gymnast or acrobat. This changed when Wijnaldum's cousin asked him to come to <a href="/wiki/Sparta_Rotterdam" title="Sparta Rotterdam">Sparta Rotterdam</a>'s opening day with him; then he was invited to the Sparta Rotterdam youth academy and his love for football started to grow slowly.<sup id="cite_ref-kameraadjes_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kameraadjes-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wijnaldum has two younger brothers, one of whom, <a href="/wiki/Giliano_Wijnaldum" title="Giliano Wijnaldum">Giliano Wijnaldum</a>, currently plays for Sparta Rotterdam, as well as a half-brother, <a href="/wiki/Rajiv_van_La_Parra" title="Rajiv van La Parra">Rajiv van La Parra</a>, who currently plays for <a href="/wiki/Red_Star_Belgrade" title="Red Star Belgrade">Red Star</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Wijnaldum was formerly known as Georginio Boateng, but following his mother's divorce, he took her maiden name (Wijnaldum).<sup id="cite_ref-ad_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ad-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Club_career">Club career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Club career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At age six, Wijnaldum developed himself rapidly at Sparta Rotterdam, winning two championship titles in his first two seasons. Soon <a href="/wiki/AFC_Ajax" title="AFC Ajax">Ajax</a>, <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a> and <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a> showed interest in the youngster, but Wijnaldum declined all offers: "I didn't watch football on TV and I didn't know any of the first team players of Sparta or any of the top clubs. I only knew the real famous players of <i>Oranje</i>, so the offers didn't impress me much. I had a good time at Sparta, I wanted to stay." After playing for Sparta Rotterdam for seven seasons and representing the Netherlands at various youth levels, Wijnaldum decided to accept a new Feyenoord offer. He was convinced playing for Feyenoord was better for his development as a football player and believed in Feyenoord's vision.<sup id="cite_ref-kameraadjes_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kameraadjes-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At Feyenoord, Wijnaldum joined a successful generation with the likes of <a href="/wiki/Leroy_Fer" title="Leroy Fer">Leroy Fer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_Pedro_(footballer,_born_1990)" title="Luís Pedro (footballer, born 1990)">Luís Pedro</a>. Wijnaldum stood out as an exceptional talent. In January 2007, a few weeks after turning 16, Wijnaldum was invited to the first team's training camp in <a href="/wiki/Belek" title="Belek">Belek</a>, Turkey, by Feyenoord manager <a href="/wiki/Erwin_Koeman" title="Erwin Koeman">Erwin Koeman</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Feyenoord">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Georginio_Wijnaldum_001.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Georginio_Wijnaldum_001.jpg/200px-Georginio_Wijnaldum_001.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="300" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Georginio_Wijnaldum_001.jpg/300px-Georginio_Wijnaldum_001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Georginio_Wijnaldum_001.jpg/400px-Georginio_Wijnaldum_001.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="900" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Georginio_Wijnaldum_001.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Wijnaldum with <a href="/wiki/Feyenoord" title="Feyenoord">Feyenoord</a> in 2007.</div></div></div>
<p>On 8 April 2007, Wijnaldum made his official debut in Feyenoord's starting line-up in the <a href="/wiki/Eredivisie" title="Eredivisie">Eredivisie</a> home match against <a href="/wiki/FC_Groningen" title="FC Groningen">Groningen</a> (0–4).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> At the age of 16 and 148 days, Wijnaldum became the youngest player ever to play in Feyenoord's first team and was chosen Feyenoord's man of the match. On 2 December 2007, Wijnaldum scored his first Eredivisie goal for Feyenoord, against <a href="/wiki/Heracles_Almelo" title="Heracles Almelo">Heracles Almelo</a> in a 6–0 home win.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the 2008–09 season, Wijnaldum made his official European debut. On 18 September 2008, he was named in the starting line-up in the <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Europa_League" title="UEFA Europa League">UEFA Cup</a> <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_UEFA_Cup_first_round" title="2008–09 UEFA Cup first round">home match</a> against <a href="/wiki/Kalmar_FF" title="Kalmar FF">Kalmar FF</a> (0–1).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> On 2 October 2008, he scored his first European goal for Feyenoord in the return match against Kalmar FF, which resulted in a 1–2 win and a place in the <a href="/wiki/2008%E2%80%9309_UEFA_Cup_group_stage" title="2008–09 UEFA Cup group stage">competition's group stage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 6 March 2009, Wijnaldum signed a new contract at Feyenoord which would have kept him at <a href="/wiki/De_Kuip" title="De Kuip">De Kuip</a> until summer 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On 27 February 2011, he helped his struggling Feyenoord to a 5–1 demolition of Groningen, scoring four goals. He scored two goals from open play in the first half and two more from the penalty spot to add his total for <a href="/wiki/2010%E2%80%9311_Eredivisie" title="2010–11 Eredivisie">the season</a> to eight goals.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PSV">PSV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: PSV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:226px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dinamo-PSV_(7).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Dinamo-PSV_%287%29.jpg/224px-Dinamo-PSV_%287%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="224" height="272" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Dinamo-PSV_%287%29.jpg/336px-Dinamo-PSV_%287%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d3/Dinamo-PSV_%287%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="355" data-file-height="431" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dinamo-PSV_(7).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Wijnaldum with <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a> in 2014.</div></div></div>
<p>On 29 June 2011, the technical director of Feyenoord announced the club had reached a transfer deal for Wijnaldum reported to be worth €5&#160;million with <a href="/wiki/PSV_Eindhoven" title="PSV Eindhoven">PSV</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> On the opening day of the <a href="/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Eredivisie" title="2011–12 Eredivisie">2011–12 Eredivisie season</a>, Wijnaldum made his debut for PSV in a 3–1 loss against <a href="/wiki/AZ_Alkmaar" title="AZ Alkmaar">AZ</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> On 21 August 2011, Wijnaldum scored his first goal for PSV in a 3–0 victory against <a href="/wiki/ADO_Den_Haag" title="ADO Den Haag">ADO Den Haag</a>. Since joining the club, Wijnaldum established himself in the starting 11 as he scored goals and developed assists from his attacking role in midfield in Eredivisie, the KNVB Cup and the UEFA Europa League. After the retirement of <a href="/wiki/Mark_van_Bommel" title="Mark van Bommel">Mark van Bommel</a> and the departure of <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Strootman" title="Kevin Strootman">Kevin Strootman</a> in the summer of 2013, Wijnaldum was named PSV's captain for the <a href="/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_Eredivisie" title="2013–14 Eredivisie">2013–14 Eredivisie</a> season. However, he played just 11 matches that year, scoring four goals, due to a back injury. For the <a href="/wiki/2014%E2%80%9315_Eredivisie" title="2014–15 Eredivisie">2014–15 Eredivisie</a> season, Wijnaldum returned to full fitness and captained PSV to their first Eredivisie title since <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Eredivisie" title="2007–08 Eredivisie">2008</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Newcastle_United">Newcastle United</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Newcastle United">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 11 July 2015, Wijnaldum joined English <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> club <a href="/wiki/Newcastle_United_F.C." title="Newcastle United F.C.">Newcastle United</a> on a five-year contract, for a reported transfer fee of £14.5&#160;million, making him the most expensive signing of <a href="/wiki/Mike_Ashley_(businessman)" title="Mike Ashley (businessman)">Mike Ashley</a>'s ownership.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Wijnaldum made his debut on 9 August as Newcastle began the season with a 2–2 home draw against <a href="/wiki/Southampton_F.C" class="mw-redirect" title="Southampton F.C">Southampton</a>, heading in a goal from <a href="/wiki/Gabriel_Obertan" title="Gabriel Obertan">Gabriel Obertan</a>'s cross.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He would score his second Newcastle goal in a 2–2 draw with <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_F.C." title="Chelsea F.C.">Chelsea</a> on 26 September.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On 18 October, Wijnaldum scored four goals in a 6–2 home win over <a href="/wiki/Norwich_City_F.C." title="Norwich City F.C.">Norwich City</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> becoming only the second Newcastle player to score more than three goals in a Premier League match.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Wijnaldum was considered the player of the match in a game against <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_F.C." title="Liverpool F.C.">Liverpool</a> when he forced <a href="/wiki/Martin_%C5%A0krtel" title="Martin Škrtel">Martin Škrtel</a> into an own goal as well as scoring himself in a 2–0 win.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He scored against <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> in a 3–3 draw on 12 January 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and again four days later in a 2–1 win against <a href="/wiki/West_Ham_United_F.C." title="West Ham United F.C.">West Ham United</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and later scored twice against <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a> in a 5–1 win on the final day of the season, finishing the season as Newcastle's top goalscorer.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> However, after the season, Newcastle were relegated to the <a href="/wiki/EFL_Championship" title="EFL Championship">Championship</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Liverpool">Liverpool</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Liverpool">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 July 2016, Wijnaldum returned to the Premier League, signing for Liverpool on a five-year contract, for an initial £23&#160;million with a further £2&#160;million in conditional add-ons. He was given the number 5 shirt.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2016.E2.80.9317_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016–17_season">2016–17 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2016–17 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Wijnaldum made his Premier League debut for Liverpool against <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> on 14 August 2016, where he played 80 minutes and provided an assist for <a href="/wiki/Adam_Lallana" title="Adam Lallana">Adam Lallana</a> before being replaced by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Stewart_(footballer)" title="Kevin Stewart (footballer)">Kevin Stewart</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He scored his first goal for the club in a 6–1 thrashing of <a href="/wiki/Watford_F.C." title="Watford F.C.">Watford</a> on 6 November.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On 31 December, he scored the match's only goal with a powerful header in Liverpool's 1–0 win over <a href="/wiki/Manchester_City_F.C." title="Manchester City F.C.">Manchester City</a> to take Liverpool four points clear in second. He was highly praised for his goal.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Wijnaldum sealed Liverpool's 3–1 win over Arsenal on 4 March 2017 with a close-range finish in stoppage time.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> In the final match of the <a href="/wiki/2016%E2%80%9317_Premier_League" title="2016–17 Premier League">2016–17 Premier League</a> season, with Liverpool needing a win over <a href="/wiki/Middlesbrough_F.C." title="Middlesbrough F.C.">Middlesbrough</a> to secure a top-four finish and a <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2017–18 UEFA Champions League">2017–18 UEFA Champions League</a> berth, Wijnaldum opened the scoring with a first-half stoppage time goal as Liverpool won 3–0.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span id="2017.E2.80.9318_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017–18_season">2017–18 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2017–18 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 28 October 2017, Wijnaldum scored his first goal of the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_Liverpool_F.C._season" title="2017–18 Liverpool F.C. season">2017–18 season</a>, scoring the third goal in Liverpool's 3–0 win over newly-promoted <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield_Town_A.F.C." title="Huddersfield Town A.F.C.">Huddersfield Town</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On 2 May 2018, Wijnaldum scored his first away goal since May 2015, and his first for an English club, when he netted in a 7–6 aggregate victory over <a href="/wiki/AS_Roma" class="mw-redirect" title="AS Roma">Roma</a> to help Liverpool progress to the final of the <a href="/wiki/2017%E2%80%9318_UEFA_Champions_League" title="2017–18 UEFA Champions League">2017–18 Champions League</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> His goal also saw Liverpool break the competition's record for the most goals scored in a single campaign, with the club's tally of 46 surpassing the record of 45 previously held by <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">Barcelona</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Wijnaldum played for Liverpool in the <a href="/wiki/2018_UEFA_Champions_League_Final" title="2018 UEFA Champions League Final">2018 UEFA Champions League Final</a> against <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid_C.F." class="mw-redirect" title="Real Madrid C.F.">Real Madrid</a>, playing the full 90 minutes as Liverpool lost 1–3.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="2018.E2.80.9319_season"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018–19_season">2018–19 season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2018–19 season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On 15 September 2018, Wijnaldum scored his first ever Premier League away goal,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> scoring the opening goal in a 2–1 win over <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur_F.C." title="Tottenham Hotspur F.C.">Tottenham Hotspur</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> He made his 100th appearance for the club on 29 September in a 1–1 Premier League draw with Chelsea.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On 14 April 2019, and against the same opposition, he made his 100th Premier League appearance for Liverpool.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Notably coming on as a substitute in the second leg of the Champions League semifinal against Barcelona, Wijnaldum scored twice in two minutes to tie Barcelona on aggregate (3–3) before <a href="/wiki/Divock_Origi" title="Divock Origi">Divock Origi</a>'s second goal of the night sent Liverpool to the final.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> In the final, Liverpool went on to beat <a href="/wiki/Tottenham_Hotspur" class="mw-redirect" title="Tottenham Hotspur">Tottenham Hotspur</a> 2-0. <sup id="cite_ref-MOTM_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MOTM-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_career">International career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: International career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth">Youth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Youth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Wijnaldum was one of the key players of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-17_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-17 football team">Netherlands under-17 squad</a> on the <a href="/wiki/2007_UEFA_European_Under-17_Championship" title="2007 UEFA European Under-17 Championship">2007 UEFA European Under-17 Championship</a> in Belgium. However, the team finished third in their group behind England and Belgium, failing to qualify for the knockout stage. Quickly after the tournament, Wijnaldum was selected for the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-19_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-19 football team">Netherlands under-19 squad</a>. Despite excellent individual performances, the team underachieved and failed to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2008_UEFA_European_Under-19_Championship" title="2008 UEFA European Under-19 Championship">2008 UEFA European Under-19 Championship</a> in the Czech Republic and <a href="/wiki/2009_UEFA_European_Under-19_Championship" title="2009 UEFA European Under-19 Championship">2009 UEFA European Under-19 Championship</a> in Ukraine.
</p><p>On 12 November 2008, the young midfielder got invited to the Netherlands B by manager <a href="/wiki/Johan_Neeskens" title="Johan Neeskens">Johan Neeskens</a>. Wijnaldum had to deny the invitation due to an injury.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wijnaldum had to deny his first <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_under-21_football_team" title="Netherlands national under-21 football team">Netherlands under-21</a> invitation on 9 August 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He received an invitation for the friendly match against <a href="/wiki/England_national_under-21_football_team" title="England national under-21 football team">England</a> by manager <a href="/wiki/Cor_Pot" title="Cor Pot">Cor Pot</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Wijnaldum made his official Netherlands U21 debut on 4 September 2009, in the <a href="/wiki/2011_UEFA_European_Under-21_Championship" title="2011 UEFA European Under-21 Championship">2011 UEFA European Under-21 Championship</a> qualification match against <a href="/wiki/Finland_national_under-21_football_team" title="Finland national under-21 football team">Finland</a> (2–0).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Senior">Senior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Georginio_Wijnaldum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Senior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:De_Guzman_Wijnaldum_NL_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/De_Guzman_Wijnaldum_NL_2015.jpg/220px-De_Guzman_Wijnaldum_NL_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/De_Guzman_Wijnaldum_NL_2015.jpg/330px-De_Guzman_Wijnaldum_NL_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/De_Guzman_Wijnaldum_NL_2015.jpg/440px-De_Guzman_Wijnaldum_NL_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1560" data-file-height="1170" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:De_Guzman_Wijnaldum_NL_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Wijnaldum (right) training alongside <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_de_Guzm%C3%A1n" title="Jonathan de Guzmán">Jonathan de Guzman</a> (left) with <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">the Netherlands</a> in 2015.</div></div></div>
<p>On 30 May 2011, Wijnaldum was named in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">Netherlands senior squad</a> for friendlies against <a href="/wiki/Brazil_national_football_team" title="Brazil national football team">Brazil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uruguay_national_football_team" title="Uruguay national football team">Uruguay</a>, but did not play in either match.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> He made his international debut in the match against <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_national_football_team" title="San Marino national football team">San Marino</a> on 2 September 2011, appearing as a substitute in the 86th minute and promptly scoring the final goal in an 11–0 win.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wijnaldum was a member of the Netherlands squad for the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2014 FIFA World Cup">2014 FIFA World Cup</a>, and scored his second goal for the nation in a 3–0 win against Brazil in the third-place play-off.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 9 September 2018, he made his 50th appearance for the Netherlands in a 2–1 <a href="/wiki/UEFA_Nations_League" title="UEFA Nations League">UEFA Nations League</a> defeat to <a href="/wiki/France_national_football_team" title="France national football team">France</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
