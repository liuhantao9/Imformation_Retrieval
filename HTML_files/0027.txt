<p><b>AllMusic</b> (previously known as <b>All Music Guide</b> and <b>AMG</b>) is an <a href="/wiki/Online_database" title="Online database">online music database</a>. It catalogs more than 3 million <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">album</a> entries and 30 million tracks, as well as information on <a href="/wiki/Musical_artist" class="mw-redirect" title="Musical artist">musical artists</a> and <a href="/wiki/Musical_ensemble" title="Musical ensemble">bands</a>. It launched in 1991, predating the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">World Wide Web</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Wired_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wired-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ernie_smith_vice_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ernie_smith_vice-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As of 2015, AllMusic is owned by <a href="/wiki/RhythmOne" title="RhythmOne">RhythmOne</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AllMusic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>AllMusic was launched as <i>All Music Guide</i> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Erlewine" title="Michael Erlewine">Michael Erlewine</a>, a "compulsive archivist, noted astrologer,  Buddhist scholar and musician".  He  became interested in using computers for his astrological work in the mid-'70s and founded a software company, Matrix, in 1977. In the early '90s, as CDs replaced vinyl as the dominant format for recorded music, Erlewine purchased what he thought was a CD of early recordings by <a href="/wiki/Little_Richard" title="Little Richard">Little Richard</a>. After buying it he discovered it was a "flaccid latter-day rehash".<sup id="cite_ref-Metro_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metro-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Frustrated with the labeling, he researched using <a href="/wiki/Meta_data" class="mw-redirect" title="Meta data">meta data</a> to create a music guide.<sup id="cite_ref-Videoland_book_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Videoland_book-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1990, in Big Rapids, Michigan, he founded All Music Guide with a goal to create an open access database that included every recording "since <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Caruso" title="Enrico Caruso">Enrico Caruso</a> gave the industry its first big boost".<sup id="cite_ref-Wired_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wired-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first <i>All Music Guide</i> was a 1,200-page reference book, packaged with a CD-ROM,  titled <i>All Music Guide: The Best CDs, Albums &amp; Tapes: The Expert's Guide to the Best Releases from Thousands of Artists in All Types of Music</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-world_cat_first_ed_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-world_cat_first_ed-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Its first digital iteration, in 1991, was a text-based <a href="/wiki/Gopher_(protocol)" title="Gopher (protocol)">Gopher</a> site.<sup id="cite_ref-Vice_history_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vice_history-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It moved to the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">World Wide Web</a> as <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">web browsers</a> became more user-friendly.<sup id="cite_ref-Metro_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metro-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Erlewine hired a database engineer, Vladimir Bogdanov, to design the All Music Guide framework, and recruited his nephew, writer <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Thomas_Erlewine" title="Stephen Thomas Erlewine">Stephen Thomas Erlewine</a>, to develop editorial content. In 1993, Chris  Woodstra joined the staff as an engineer.  A "record geek" who had written for alternative weeklies and fanzines, his main qualification was an "encyclopedic knowledge of music".<sup id="cite_ref-Metro_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metro-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 1,400 subgenres of music were created, a feature which became central to the site's utility.  In a 2016 article in <i>Tedium</i>, Ernie Smith wrote: "AllMusic may have been one of most ambitious sites of the early-internet era—and it’s one that is fundamental to our understanding of pop culture. Because, the thing is, it doesn’t just track reviews or albums. It tracks styles, genres, and subgenres, along with the tone of the music and the platforms on which the music is sold. It then connects that data together, in a way that can intelligently tell you about an entire type of music, whether a massive genre like classical, or a tiny one like sadcore."<sup id="cite_ref-big_data_smith_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-big_data_smith-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1996, seeking to further develop its web-based businesses, Alliance Entertainment Corp. bought All Music from Erlewine for a reported $3.5 million. He left the company after its sale.<sup id="cite_ref-Metro_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metro-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Alliance filed for bankruptcy in 1999, and its assets were acquired by <a href="/wiki/Ron_Burkle" class="mw-redirect" title="Ron Burkle">Ron Burkle</a>'s Yucaipa Equity Fund.<sup id="cite_ref-Videoland_book_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Videoland_book-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1999, All Music relocated from Big Rapids to Ann Arbor, where the staff expanded from 12 to 100 people.<sup id="cite_ref-Metro_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metro-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
By February of that year, 350,000 albums and 2 million tracks had been cataloged. All Music had published biographies of 30,000 artists, 120,000 record reviews and 300 essays written by "a hybrid of historians, critics and passionate collectors".<sup id="cite_ref-Weisbard_Voice_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Weisbard_Voice-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NPR_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NPR-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late 2007, AllMusic was purchased for $72 million by <a href="/wiki/TiVo_Corporation" title="TiVo Corporation">TiVo Corporation</a> (known as Macrovision at the time of the sale, and as Rovi from 2009 until 2016).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, AllMusic removed all of Bryan Adams' info from the site as per a request from the artist<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>.
</p><p><br />
In 2015, AllMusic was purchased by BlinkX (later known as <a href="/wiki/RhythmOne" title="RhythmOne">RhythmOne</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The AllMusic database is powered by a combination of <a href="/wiki/MySQL" title="MySQL">MySQL</a> and <a href="/wiki/MongoDB" title="MongoDB">MongoDB</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ernie_smith_vice_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ernie_smith_vice-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_All_Music_Guide_series">The All Music Guide series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AllMusic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The All Music Guide series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The All Media Network also produced the AllMusic guide series, which includes the <i>AllMusic Guide to Rock</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> the <i><a href="/wiki/All_Music_Guide_to_Jazz" title="All Music Guide to Jazz">All Music Guide to Jazz</a></i> and the <i><a href="/wiki/All_Music_Guide_to_the_Blues" title="All Music Guide to the Blues">All Music Guide to the Blues</a></i>. <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Bogdanov_(editor)" title="Vladimir Bogdanov (editor)">Vladimir Bogdanov</a> is the president of the series.<sup id="cite_ref-Billboard_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Metro_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metro-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AllMusic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
