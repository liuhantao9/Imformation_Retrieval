<p><b>West Yorkshire Police</b> is the <a href="/wiki/Territorial_police_force" title="Territorial police force">territorial police force</a> responsible for policing <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a> in England.  It is the fourth largest force in England and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> by number of officers, with 5,671 officers.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Police&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>West Yorkshire Police was formed in 1974, when part of the <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire_Constabulary" title="West Yorkshire Constabulary">West Yorkshire Constabulary</a> (itself created in 1968, and covering a much larger area) was amalgamated with the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Police" title="Leeds City Police">Leeds City Police</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford_City_Police" title="Bradford City Police">Bradford City Police</a>, under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>.  The force was originally known as the <b>West Yorkshire Metropolitan Police</b>. Some older signs around the Force area, such as the one in the reception of Millgarth Police Station in Leeds city centre read 'West Yorkshire Metropolitan Police'. The 'Metropolitan' from the police title was dropped in 1986 when the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_counties" class="mw-redirect" title="Metropolitan counties">Metropolitan counties</a> were abolished.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
Proposals made by the <a href="/wiki/Home_Secretary" title="Home Secretary">Home Secretary</a> on 21 March 2006 would see the force merge with <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire_Police" title="North Yorkshire Police">North Yorkshire Police</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Police" title="South Yorkshire Police">South Yorkshire Police</a> and <a href="/wiki/Humberside_Police" title="Humberside Police">Humberside Police</a> to form a strategic police force for the entire region. These plans are currently<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (February 2018)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> under review and not expected to take place in the foreseeable future.
</p><p>On 12 December 2006, <a href="/wiki/Norman_Bettison" title="Norman Bettison">Sir Norman Bettison</a> was announced as the new Chief Constable, replacing <a href="/wiki/Colin_Cramphorn" title="Colin Cramphorn">Colin Cramphorn</a> and resigned from his post on 24 October 2012. He was replaced by Temporary Chief Constable John Parkinson until the appointment of Mark Gilmore as Chief Constable on 1 February 2013.
</p><p>In 2018 it was reckoned West Yorkshire Police would lose 400 officers from its 4,800 officers due to <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_government_austerity_programme#Effects" title="United Kingdom government austerity programme">austerity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operational_structure">Operational structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Police&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Operational structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For operational purposes West Yorkshire Police is divided into five geographic <a href="/wiki/Police_division" title="Police division">divisions</a> known within the force as ‘policing districts’. The change in nomenclature reflects that of April 2014 the alignment with council boundaries for policing districts and the reduction of divisions in Leeds (which had three) and Bradford (which had two) so that each policing district was conterminous with its respective local authority boundaries. Each district is made up of <b>Partnership Working Areas</b> (PWA) which consist of an Inspector and three teams of sergeants, police constables, special constables and PCSOs.  The first single police commander of the Bradford district, Chief Superintendent Simon Atkin, was appointed in October 2013 as part of ongoing moves to merge the district’s two policing divisions, while Chief Superintendent Angela Williams was appointed in Calderdale.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>

</p><p>The Sir Alec Jeffreys Building in the Calder Park Business Estate (at Junction 39 of the M1 motorway) houses the Yorkshire and The Humber Scientific Support Service and was opened in May 2012 by Sir <a href="/wiki/Alec_Jeffreys" title="Alec Jeffreys">Alec Jeffreys</a> himself.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> West Yorkshire Police is the 'lead force' for scientific support and provides such services for <a href="/wiki/North_Yorkshire_Police" title="North Yorkshire Police">North Yorkshire Police</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Yorkshire_Police" title="South Yorkshire Police">South Yorkshire Police</a> and <a href="/wiki/Humberside_Police" title="Humberside Police">Humberside Police</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Changing_estate_of_West_Yorkshire_Police">Changing estate of West Yorkshire Police</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Police&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Changing estate of West Yorkshire Police">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:West_Yorkshire_Police_-_Bishopgarth.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/West_Yorkshire_Police_-_Bishopgarth.jpg/220px-West_Yorkshire_Police_-_Bishopgarth.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/West_Yorkshire_Police_-_Bishopgarth.jpg/330px-West_Yorkshire_Police_-_Bishopgarth.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/West_Yorkshire_Police_-_Bishopgarth.jpg/440px-West_Yorkshire_Police_-_Bishopgarth.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="1836" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:West_Yorkshire_Police_-_Bishopgarth.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sign outside of the former West Yorkshire Police Training and Development Centre, Bishopgarth, Wakefield.</div></div></div>
<p>The current estate of police stations and other buildings is changing with certain buildings closing and new buildings opening. As of 2014 there are three <a href="/wiki/Private_finance_initiative" title="Private finance initiative">PFI</a> projects completed and as a result of these new buildings a number of police stations have closed and been sold.<sup id="cite_ref-PFI_projects_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PFI_projects-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wakefield_District_Headquarters">Wakefield District Headquarters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Police&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wakefield District Headquarters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Wakefield District police headquarters is now located on Havertop Lane, <a href="/wiki/Normanton,_West_Yorkshire" title="Normanton, West Yorkshire">Normanton</a>. The total area is 11,500 metres squared and provides office accommodation as well as a 35-cell custody suite.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As it's now operational, the following Police stations are in the process of being closed and/or sold.
</p>
<ul><li><b>Wood Street Police Station</b>, Wakefield.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Normanton Police Station</b>,<b> </b>Normanton.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Castleford Police Station</b>, Castleford<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Leeds_District_Headquarters">Leeds District Headquarters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Police&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Leeds District Headquarters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The new headquarters for the newly formed Leeds District is operational on Elland Road, <a href="/wiki/Beeston,_Leeds" title="Beeston, Leeds">Beeston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>. The total area is 12,500 metres squared and provides office accommodation as well as a 40-cell custody suite.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Now operational, the Leeds District HQ will replace the following stations:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Millgarth Police Station</b>, Leeds city centre.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Holbeck Police Station</b>, <a href="/wiki/Holbeck" title="Holbeck">Holbeck</a>, Leeds.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Carr_Gate_Special_Operational_Training_Facility">Carr Gate Special Operational Training Facility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Yorkshire_Police&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Carr Gate Special Operational Training Facility">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The existing Operational Support facilities at Carr Gate, Wakefield have been expanded and new buildings constructed which has centralised specialist training for the force in one location. The new facility, which totals 20,000 metres squared includes the following:
</p>
<ul><li>Training hub facility (reception, classrooms, 3 gyms, storage and offices etc.)</li>
<li>Public Order training facility (storage areas, breakout areas, large briefing room, classroom, training arena, etc.)</li>
<li>Driver training facility</li>
<li>Firearms training facility (100m and two 50m firing ranges, armoury, etc.)</li>
<li>Method of Entry area (approximately 400 metres squared)</li></ul>
