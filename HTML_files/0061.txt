	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Carrie Underwood discography</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox" style="width:22em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold"><span class="fn"><a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> discography</span></th></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center"><a href="/wiki/File:Carrie_Underwood_2012.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Carrie Underwood 2012.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Carrie_Underwood_2012.jpg/220px-Carrie_Underwood_2012.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="356" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Carrie_Underwood_2012.jpg/330px-Carrie_Underwood_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Carrie_Underwood_2012.jpg/440px-Carrie_Underwood_2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2001" data-file-height="3234" /></a><div>Underwood performing at <a href="/wiki/Times_Square" title="Times Square">Times Square</a> in May 2012</div></td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="background:#DADCED;">Studio albums</th><td style="background:#E6E8FA; text-align:center;">6</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="background:#DADCED;">Compilation albums</th><td style="background:#E6E8FA; text-align:center;">1</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="background:#DADCED;">Video albums</th><td style="background:#E6E8FA; text-align:center;">1</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="background:#DADCED;">Music videos</th><td style="background:#E6E8FA; text-align:center;">34</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" style="background:#DADCED;">Singles</th><td style="background:#E6E8FA; text-align:center;">31</td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>American singer and songwriter <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a> has released six <a href="/wiki/Studio_album" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio album">studio albums</a> and 31 <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">singles</a>. Underwood rose to fame after winning the <a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_4)" title="American Idol (season 4)">fourth season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> in 2005. Her debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Some_Hearts" title="Some Hearts">Some Hearts</a></i>, was released in 2005 and is the fastest-selling debut country album in <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_SoundScan" title="Nielsen SoundScan">Nielsen SoundScan</a> history.<sup id="cite_ref-cst_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cst-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It also became the best-selling solo female country debut in <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) history, as well as the top-selling debut album of any <i>American Idol</i> contestant in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Her second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Carnival_Ride" title="Carnival Ride">Carnival Ride</a></i>, was released in 2007 and debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> charts. During the first week, it sold 527,000 copies, which at the time was the best sales week by a female solo country artist since <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain" title="Shania Twain">Shania Twain</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Shania_Twain_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Shania Twain album)">Greatest Hits</a></i> sold 530,000 copies in its first week back in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The album has since been certified 4x Platinum.
</p><p>Underwood released her third studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Play_On_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" title="Play On (Carrie Underwood album)">Play On</a></i>, on November 3, 2009. It debuted at number one on the Top Country Albums chart and the all-genre <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. It features the number one singles "<a href="/wiki/Cowboy_Casanova" title="Cowboy Casanova">Cowboy Casanova</a>", "<a href="/wiki/Temporary_Home" title="Temporary Home">Temporary Home</a>", and "<a href="/wiki/Undo_It" title="Undo It">Undo It</a>", as well as the number two single, "<a href="/wiki/Mama%27s_Song" title="Mama&#39;s Song">Mama's Song</a>". The album has since been certified 3× Platinum in the US, Platinum in Canada, and Gold in Australia. On June 2011, the album re-entered the <a href="/wiki/ARIA_Charts" title="ARIA Charts">Australian Albums Charts</a> for both the ARIA National Top 50 and the Country Top 20 albums at number 14 and number one, respectively, giving Underwood her first number one album in Australia. The album was eventually certified Gold in Australia.
</p><p>On May 1, 2012, Underwood released her fourth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Blown_Away_(album)" title="Blown Away (album)">Blown Away</a></i>. In the United States, it sold 267,000 albums in its first week and debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and the Top Country Albums charts. It debuted at number one in Canada's Top 200 all-genre albums chart and Top 100 Country albums chart, being immediately certified Gold. The album also debuted at number one on Australia's Top 20 Country Albums and number four on the all-genre Top 50 Albums.<sup id="cite_ref-carrieunderwoodofficial1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-carrieunderwoodofficial1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The album has been certified 2x Platinum in the United States and Canada.
</p><p>On December 9, 2014, Underwood released her first greatest hits album, <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits:_Decade_Number_1" title="Greatest Hits: Decade Number 1">Greatest Hits: Decade #1</a></i>. The compilation debuted at number four on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> and at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Top_Country_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Country Albums">Top Country Albums</a> charts, and set several records upon release, including having the biggest sales debut for a hits collection in any genre of music in more than six years and the biggest first-week sales for a female hits album in any genre in over nine years. The album was certified Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> on January 16, 2015.
</p><p>Underwood's fifth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Storyteller_(Carrie_Underwood_album)" title="Storyteller (Carrie Underwood album)">Storyteller</a></i> was released on October 23, 2015. The album debuted at number 2 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. This made Underwood the only country artist to have his or her first five studio albums debut at number one or number two on the <i>Billboard</i> 200 chart. It also debuted atop the <a href="/wiki/Top_Country_Albums" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Country Albums">Top Country Albums</a> chart, earning Underwood another record as the only artist to score six consecutive number one albums on that chart. The album was certified Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> on October 24, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-riaaAlbums_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-riaaAlbums-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Underwood released her sixth studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Cry_Pretty" title="Cry Pretty">Cry Pretty</a>,</i> on September 14, 2018. The album debuted at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a> chart, making Underwood the first woman to have four number one country albums on that chart. It sold 266,000 album-equivalent units in its first week, out of which 251,000 copies were pure sales, giving Underwood the largest sales week for a female artist in 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Underwood has sold in excess of 65 million records globally.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the United States, she remains the best-selling album artist from <i>American Idol</i> franchise with a total of 16.3 million albums sold.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> She is the top country artist and the fifth highest-certified female artist on the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">RIAA</a> Top Artists (Digital Singles) Ranking.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> She is also the fourth biggest album seller among artists who have debuted the past 13 years.<sup id="cite_ref-music.yahoo.com_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-music.yahoo.com-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Underwood has accumulated 85 <i>Billboard</i> number-ones.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> She has had 15 singles top the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and 14 singles top the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Underwood has the longest streak of top 10 promoted singles from a career's start on the Hot Country Songs chart at 27.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
