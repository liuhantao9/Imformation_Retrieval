<p><b>Green Spain</b> (direct translation into English of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> <i>España Verde</i>) is the name given to a lush <a href="/wiki/Natural_region" title="Natural region">natural region</a> in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Northern Spain">Northern Spain</a>, stretching along the Atlantic coast from the <a href="/wiki/Portugal%E2%80%93Spain_border" title="Portugal–Spain border">border with Portugal</a> to the <a href="/wiki/France%E2%80%93Spain_border" title="France–Spain border">border with France</a>.
</p><p>The region includes nearly all of <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Spain)" title="Galicia (Spain)">Galicia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asturias" title="Asturias">Asturias</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cantabria" title="Cantabria">Cantabria</a> plus the northern parts of the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque Country</a>, as well as a minor portion of <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate_and_landscape">Climate and landscape</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Climate and landscape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is called <i>green</i> because its wet and <a href="/wiki/Temperate_climate" title="Temperate climate">temperate</a> <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">oceanic climate</a> helps lush pastures and forests thrive, providing a landscape similar to that of <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, and the west coast of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.
</p><p>The climate and landscape are determined by the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> winds whose moisture gets trapped by the mountains circumventing the Spanish Atlantic façade.
</p><p>Because of the <a href="/wiki/Foehn_effect" class="mw-redirect" title="Foehn effect">Foehn effect</a>, the southern slopes fall inside the <a href="/wiki/Rain_shadow" title="Rain shadow">rain shadow</a> zone and so Green Spain contrasts starkly with the Spanish drier central plateau. 
Conversely, in those brief episodes when the southwestern winds blow through the mountains (especially during October–November<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>), the effect reverses, the northern coast gets inside the Foehn winds and is dry and much warmer than the inner plateau, where rain is present.
</p><p>The average precipitation is about 1,200&#160;mm, higher than in most areas in inland central Europe, and wetter than almost anywhere in Spain, a country generally considered dry (the main exception to this northernly rainfall trend is the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_de_Grazalema_Natural_Park" title="Sierra de Grazalema Natural Park">Sierra de Grazalema</a>, in the southern province of <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Cadiz" class="mw-redirect" title="Province of Cadiz">Cadiz</a>, with mountains that block the Atlantic moisture-carrying winds and which is, indeed, the most rainy place in Spain). Asturias has an average summer temperature of 20-22 ºC, being one of the mildest climates in Europe.
</p><p>Most of the rain comes from the Atlantic through Galicia, the western part of Green Spain.
Depending on the latitude of entry, this wet air can drop to the south, or more likely stay and run through the north stretch of land, pushed north by the Cantabrian mountains.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Native_species">Native species</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_Spain&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Native species">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main native tree species of <a href="/wiki/Temperate_broadleaf_and_mixed_forests" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate broadleaf and mixed forests">this biome</a> are <a href="/wiki/Beeches" class="mw-redirect" title="Beeches">beeches</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oaks</a>. However, since the second half of the 20th century, in some areas (especially in coastal areas), native forests have been replaced by <a href="/wiki/Plantations" class="mw-redirect" title="Plantations">plantations</a> of <a href="/wiki/Eucalyptus" title="Eucalyptus">eucalyptus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Monterey_Pine" class="mw-redirect" title="Monterey Pine">Monterey Pines</a> for its commercial exploitation in the <a href="/wiki/Paper_industry" class="mw-redirect" title="Paper industry">paper industry</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Pyrenees" title="Pyrenees">Pyrenees</a>, which sometimes are considered in the same geologic system as the Cantabrian Mountains, were once included in Green Spain, even though the rainfall there has different patterns and the general landscape is more <a href="/wiki/Alpine_climate" title="Alpine climate">alpine</a> rather than genuinely oceanic.
</p>
