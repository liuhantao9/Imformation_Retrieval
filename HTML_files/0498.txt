<p>The <b>South American Games</b> (also known as <b>ODESUR Games</b>; Spanish: <i>Juegos Sudamericanos</i>; Portuguese: <i>Jogos Sul-Americanos</i>), formerly the <b>Southern Cross Games</b> (Spanish: <i>Juegos Cruz del Sur</i>) is a regional <a href="/wiki/Multi-sport_event" title="Multi-sport event">multi-sport event</a> held between nations from <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, organized by the <a href="/wiki/South_American_Sports_Organization" class="mw-redirect" title="South American Sports Organization">South American Sports Organization</a> (<i>Organización Deportiva Sudamericana</i>, ODESUR).<sup id="cite_ref-odesur_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-odesur-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first Games were held in 1978 in <a href="/wiki/La_Paz" title="La Paz">La Paz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>. They have since been held every four years, with the most recent edition in 2018 in <a href="/wiki/Cochabamba" title="Cochabamba">Cochabamba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>. The Games have had an equivalent to the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Flame" class="mw-redirect" title="Olympic Flame">Olympic Flame</a> since their inception: the <a href="/wiki/South_American" class="mw-redirect" title="South American">South American</a> Flame, which is relayed from <a href="/wiki/Tiahuanaco" class="mw-redirect" title="Tiahuanaco">Tiahuanaco</a>, Bolivia, to the host city.<sup id="cite_ref-flame_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-flame-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For the XI edition in 2018 there were two bids: <a href="/wiki/Cochabamba,_Bolivia" class="mw-redirect" title="Cochabamba, Bolivia">Cochabamba, Bolivia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Barquisimeto" title="Barquisimeto">Barquisimeto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>, with the final hosting decision in favour of Cochabamba in 2011. Starting with the 2014 edition, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=South_American_Para_Games&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="South American Para Games (page does not exist)">South American Para Games</a> are held for South American Paralympic athletes. Just like the <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a>, the host city for the South American Games is also the host for Para-South American Games.
</p><p>The detailed history of the South American Games together with an extensive list of medal winners was published in a book written (in Spanish) by Argentinian journalist Ernesto Rodríguez III with support of the <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Olympic_Committee" title="Argentine Olympic Committee">Argentine Olympic Committee</a> under the auspices of the Ministry of Education in collaboration with the Sports Secretary of Argentina.<sup id="cite_ref-cada_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cada-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
