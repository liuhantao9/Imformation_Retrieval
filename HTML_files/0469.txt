<p><b>Letitia Alma Vriesde</b> (born 5 October 1964) is a female former track and field athlete from <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, who specialised in the 800 metres but was also successful over 1500 metres. She is the first (and as of 2015, only) sportsperson from Suriname to compete at five Olympic Games.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> She won a silver medal at the 1995 World Championships and a bronze medal at the 2001 World Championships. Vriesde holds the South American records for 800m, 1000m and 1500m (indoors and outdoors) and also for 3000m (indoors).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Letitia_Vriesde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vriesde started running in Suriname, coached by Luiz de Oliveira. She left Suriname after failing to be selected for the 1984 Olympics to train in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. She competed in the <a href="/wiki/Athletics_at_the_1988_Summer_Olympics_-_Women%27s_800_metres" class="mw-redirect" title="Athletics at the 1988 Summer Olympics - Women&#39;s 800 metres">800 metres</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1988_Summer_Olympics" title="1988 Summer Olympics">1988 Summer Olympics</a>, and broke into the highest echelons of the sport in 1991, when she reached the finals of both 800m and 1500m at the <a href="/wiki/IAAF_World_Championships_in_Athletics" class="mw-redirect" title="IAAF World Championships in Athletics">IAAF World Championships in Athletics</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, finishing in fifth and ninth places respectively. At the <a href="/wiki/1992_Summer_Olympics" title="1992 Summer Olympics">1992 Summer Olympics</a> she set a record of sorts by recording the fastest ever non-qualifying time (1:58.28) in an 800m semi-final.
</p><p>Vriesde won a bronze medal in the 800m at the <a href="/wiki/1995_IAAF_World_Indoor_Championships" title="1995 IAAF World Indoor Championships">1995 IAAF World Indoor Championships</a>, before going on to win a silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/1995_World_Championships_in_Athletics" title="1995 World Championships in Athletics">1995 World Championships in Athletics</a> behind Cuba's <a href="/wiki/Ana_Quirot" class="mw-redirect" title="Ana Quirot">Ana Quirot</a>. A year later, she missed the finals at the <a href="/wiki/1996_Summer_Olympics" title="1996 Summer Olympics">1996 Summer Olympics</a>, running a nearly identical time to her 1992 Olympic performance (1:58.29), again placing fifth in her semifinal. She has also won many medals at the <a href="/wiki/Pan_American_Games" title="Pan American Games">Pan American Games</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_American_and_Caribbean_Games" title="Central American and Caribbean Games">Central American and Caribbean Games</a>.
</p><p>Vriesde was disqualified and stripped of her gold medal at the <a href="/wiki/2003_Pan_American_Games" title="2003 Pan American Games">2003 Pan American Games</a> after testing positive for excessive caffeine levels. She was said to have the equivalent of five gallons of coffee in her system, and admitted her guilt. She was not banned however and went on to compete at that year's World Championships. Drinking too much coffee or taking a common cold tablet would no longer get athletes disqualified after a new global list of banned substances was drawn up by the World Anti-Doping Agency and applied from 1 January 2004.
</p><p>Vriesde retired soon after competing at her seventh World Championships in 2005. In Paramaribo a street formerly known as <i>Cultuurtuinlaan</i> was changed into <i>Letitia Vriesdelaan</i>. The Surinamese Government also gave her a piece of land in appreciation of her achievements.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Letitia_Vriesde&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vriesde married  Bas van Veen on 9 September 2005 in <a href="/wiki/Middelburg_(Zeeland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Middelburg (Zeeland)">Middelburg</a>, Holland. Her daughter Joi Vienna was born on 19 March 2007.
</p>
