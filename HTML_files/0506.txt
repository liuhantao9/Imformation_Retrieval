<p><b>Almondbury</b> is a village 2 miles (3.2&#160;km)  south-east of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> town centre in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. The population of Almondbury in 2001 was 7,368<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> increasing to 18,346 at the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Almondbury appears in the <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i> as "Almondeberie". After the <a href="/wiki/Norman_Conquest" class="mw-redirect" title="Norman Conquest">Norman Conquest</a>, the land around the village was held by the powerful De Lacy family, who gave their name to De Lacy Avenue.
</p><p>For 300 years until the 17th century, the village's Monday Market was the most important in the area. Almondbury was the hub of parish activity and in its early history was a more important centre than the town of Huddersfield. The villages of <a href="/wiki/Linthwaite" title="Linthwaite">Linthwaite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lockwood,_West_Yorkshire" class="mw-redirect" title="Lockwood, West Yorkshire">Lockwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honley" title="Honley">Honley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth" title="Holmfirth">Holmfirth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Meltham" title="Meltham">Meltham</a> were all part of the Almondbury parish area.
</p><p>The village is close to <a href="/wiki/Castle_Hill,_Huddersfield" title="Castle Hill, Huddersfield">Castle Hill</a>, Huddersfield's most prominent landmark. Almondbury has several notable buildings including the 16th-century Wormald's Hall, now the village <a href="/wiki/Conservative_club" class="mw-redirect" title="Conservative club">Conservative club</a>, and the Grade I listed <a href="/w/index.php?title=All_Hallows_Church,_Almondbury&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="All Hallows Church, Almondbury (page does not exist)">All Hallows</a> Church.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Almondbury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1547 the people of Almondbury were faced with the possible <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Monasteries" title="Dissolution of the Monasteries">dissolution</a> of its Chantry Chapel.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> By "concent of the parishe", Arthur Kay of <a href="/wiki/Woodsome_Hall" title="Woodsome Hall">Woodsome Hall</a> and his son John "dyd shifte yt" stone by stone, along St Helen's Gate, to be reconstructed as a school house. A <a href="/wiki/Royal_charter" title="Royal charter">royal charter</a>, formally called the Letters Patent,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> was granted by <a href="/wiki/James_I_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="James I of England">James I</a> on 24 November 1608 and the school became a <a href="/wiki/Grammar_school" title="Grammar school">grammar school</a>. The school has had various names (Almondbury Grammar School, King James's Grammar School) and today is called <a href="/wiki/King_James%27s_School,_Almondbury" title="King James&#39;s School, Almondbury">King James's School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Harry Taylor Trust was established in 1987 in memory of Harry Taylor, former <a href="/wiki/Headmaster" class="mw-redirect" title="Headmaster">headmaster</a> of King James's Grammar School (1952-1973), to benefit pupils at the school and young people in the village of Almondbury.
</p><p>There is also <a href="/wiki/Almondbury_Community_School" title="Almondbury Community School">Almondbury Community School</a> on Fernside Avenue and Almond House Nursery on Forest Road. The Community School made headlines when it was revealed that white pupils were <a href="/wiki/Almondbury_Community_School_bullying_incident#Assault_on_Syrian_refugees" title="Almondbury Community School bullying incident">bullying a Syrian refugee</a> pupil, including waterboarding. An incident that occurred in October was finally investigated by the school and police a month later.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-washington-Almondbury-assault_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washington-Almondbury-assault-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
