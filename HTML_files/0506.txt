<p><b>Ernesto Fritz Hoost</b> (born 11 July 1965) is a Dutch retired <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxer</a>. A four-time <a href="/wiki/List_of_K-1_champions#K-1_World_Grand_Prix_Champions" title="List of K-1 champions">K-1 World Champion</a>, Hoost is considered to be one of the greatest kickboxers of all time. Debuting in 1993 at the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix_1993" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World Grand Prix 1993">K-1 World Grand Prix 1993</a>, where he came just one win short of the world title, Hoost announced his retirement thirteen years later on 2 December 2006 after the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_GP_2006_Final" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World GP 2006 Final">K-1 World GP Final</a> tournament in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Dome" title="Tokyo Dome">Tokyo Dome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ernesto_Hoost&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hoost was born in <a href="/wiki/Heemskerk" title="Heemskerk">Heemskerk</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Holland" title="North Holland">North Holland</a>, to parents from <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/K-1_Grand_Prix_%2793" title="K-1 Grand Prix &#39;93">K-1 Grand Prix '93</a>, the inaugural K-1 World Grand Prix, Hoost defeated <a href="/wiki/Peter_Aerts" title="Peter Aerts">Peter Aerts</a> by decision in the quarterfinals, knocked out <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Smith_(kickboxer)" title="Maurice Smith (kickboxer)">Maurice Smith</a> in the semifinals, and advanced to the tournament final where he was knocked out by <a href="/wiki/Branko_Cikatic" class="mw-redirect" title="Branko Cikatic">Branko Cikatic</a>. Hoost got another shot at a title on 19 December 1993, when he won the K-2 World Championship, knocking out <a href="/wiki/Changpeuk_Kiatsonglit" class="mw-redirect" title="Changpeuk Kiatsonglit">Changpuek Kiatsongrit</a> in four rounds. This was the only time the K-1 organisation held a K-2 tournament.
</p><p>Hoost reached the K-1 World Grand Prix Finals again in 1995, but lost to <a href="/wiki/Peter_Aerts" title="Peter Aerts">Peter Aerts</a> by a four-round decision. He went on to win every fight the remainder of that year. In 1996, he lost at the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix_1996" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World Grand Prix 1996">K-1 World Grand Prix 1996</a> finals to <a href="/wiki/Andy_Hug" title="Andy Hug">Andy Hug</a> by a five-round split decision. He finally became K-1 World Champion in 1997 when he beat Hug by a three-round unanimous decision.
</p><p>Hoost was unable to defend his title at the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix_1998" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World Grand Prix 1998">K-1 World Grand Prix 1998</a> tournament, being technically knocked out in the quarterfinals by Australian <a href="/wiki/Sam_Greco" title="Sam Greco">Sam Greco</a> due to being unable to start the 3rd round after a cut above his left eye; he was mostly dominated in the fight by Greco.
</p><p>In 1999, Hoost won his second K-1 World Grand Prix title, beating <a href="/wiki/Mirko_Cro_Cop" title="Mirko Cro Cop">Mirko Cro Cop</a> by technical knockout in the third round. On 23 April 2000, Hoost avenged his loss to Greco when he beat him by a <a href="/wiki/Technical_knockout" class="mw-redirect" title="Technical knockout">technical knockout</a>.
</p><p>Hoost retained the K-1 World Grand Prix Championship title for third time in 2000 by defeating <a href="/wiki/Ray_Sefo" title="Ray Sefo">Ray Sefo</a>. By then, many K-1 fans were hoping for a meeting between Hoost and <a href="/wiki/Bob_Sapp" title="Bob Sapp">Bob Sapp</a>. Hoost returned to defend his crown in 2001 defeating <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Leko" title="Stefan Leko">Stefan Leko</a>. However, he was forced to retire from the tournament due to an injured shin before the semi-finals.
</p><p>The highly anticipated fight with Bob Sapp came at the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix_2002_Final_Elimination" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World Grand Prix 2002 Final Elimination">K-1 World Grand Prix 2002 Final Elimination</a>. Sapp won by a first-round knockout after the doctor stopped the match on cuts. Despite the loss he was again matched up with Sapp in the quarter finals of the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix_2002" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World Grand Prix 2002">K-1 World Grand Prix 2002</a>. After knocking Sapp down in first round, Hoost lost to Sapp again in a wild slugfest when referee <a href="/wiki/Nobuaki_Kakuda" title="Nobuaki Kakuda">Nobuaki Kakuda</a> declared a KO while Hoost was still standing, but after the fight, Sapp turned out to have broken his hand and suffered four cracked ribs, and wasn't able to continue, allowing Hoost to replace him in the semi-finals. Hoost beat Ray Sefo in first round by TKO, after Sefo damaged his shin against Hoost's kneecap. Hoost proceeded to his fifth K-1 Finals, and was matched up against <a href="/wiki/Jerome_Le_Banner" class="mw-redirect" title="Jerome Le Banner">Jerome Le Banner</a>. The fight was clearly in Le Banner's favour up until the third and final round when Le Banner injured his arm blocking Hoost's kick. Hoost aggressively attacked the arm again, forcing Le Banner down with only 94 seconds left in the match, winning by TKO and his fourth Grand Prix Championship. Le Banner suffered a severe <a href="/wiki/Compound_fracture" class="mw-redirect" title="Compound fracture">compound fracture</a>, putting him out of competition for over a year.
</p><p>In 2003, Hoost would continue his feud with Bob Sapp in another arena of combat sports, <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WRESTLE-1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WRESTLE-1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> At <a href="/wiki/AJPW" class="mw-redirect" title="AJPW">AJPW</a>'s 2nd WRESTLE-1 event held in the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Dome" title="Tokyo Dome">Tokyo Dome</a>, Hoost would defeat Sapp after delivering a chair shot and slap to the back of the head which led to a schoolboy pin.<sup id="cite_ref-WRESTLE-1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WRESTLE-1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to his Grand Prix titles Ernesto Hoost fought a number of Super fights. In 2004 he was again in the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix_2004" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World Grand Prix 2004">K-1 World Grand Prix 2004</a> finals, in which he lost to the eventual Grand Prix champion <a href="/wiki/Remy_Bonjasky" title="Remy Bonjasky">Remy Bonjasky</a>.
</p><p>In 2006, Hoost declared that he would fight his last tournament in K-1. In the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix_2006" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World Grand Prix 2006">K-1 World Grand Prix 2006</a>, Hoost was defeated in the semi-finals by <a href="/wiki/Semmy_Schilt" title="Semmy Schilt">Semmy Schilt</a>. An emotional Hoost was met with a standing ovation from the audience as he left the arena.
</p><p>Hoost is well known for training "Knees of Fury" fighters <a href="/wiki/Paul_Slowinski" title="Paul Slowinski">Paul Slowinski</a> (whom he is currently still training). Under his guidance Slowinski has won the K-1 tournament 2007 in Amsterdam.  He has also trained leg strikes, wrestling, and other skills with <a href="/wiki/Pride_Fighting_Championships" title="Pride Fighting Championships">PRIDE</a> Champion <a href="/wiki/Fedor_Emelianenko" title="Fedor Emelianenko">Fedor Emelianenko</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hoost was also present as a cornerman for UFC fighter <a href="/wiki/Antoni_Hardonk" title="Antoni Hardonk">Antoni Hardonk</a> in Hardonk's <a href="/wiki/UFC_85" title="UFC 85">UFC 85</a> bout with <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Sanchez" title="Eddie Sanchez">Eddie Sanchez</a>, his <a href="/wiki/UFC_92" title="UFC 92">UFC 92</a> win over <a href="/wiki/Mike_Wessel" title="Mike Wessel">Mike Wessel</a>, and his <a href="/wiki/UFC_97" title="UFC 97">UFC 97</a> loss to <a href="/wiki/Cheick_Kongo" title="Cheick Kongo">Cheick Kongo</a>.
</p><p>In 2012, Hoost was invited by the Katana Fighting Series to be guest of honour at their Katana 6 'Rebellion' show.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hoost made a comeback aged 48. In his first fight in over eight years, he scored two knockdowns en route to a unanimous decision victory over Thomas Stanley at <i>Hoost Cup: Legend</i> in Nagoya, Japan on 23 March 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He defeated Peter Aerts in their sixth meeting via unanimous decision on 19 October 2014 in Osaka to win the vacant WKO World Heavyweight Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ernesto_Hoost&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hoost currently lives in the town of <a href="/wiki/Hoorn" title="Hoorn">Hoorn</a>, together with his wife and children.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Titles">Titles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ernesto_Hoost&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Titles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>2014 WKO World Heavyweight Champion</li>
<li>2002 <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix_2002_Final" title="K-1 World Grand Prix 2002 Final">K-1 World Grand Prix 2002</a> champion in Tokyo, Japan</li>
