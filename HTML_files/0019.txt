	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Alvey</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For the roller coaster enthusiast and video game producer, see <a href="/wiki/Robb_Alvey" title="Robb Alvey">Robb Alvey</a>.</div>
<p>The <b>Alvey</b> Programme was a <a href="/wiki/British_government" class="mw-redirect" title="British government">British government</a> sponsored research program in <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information technology</a> that ran from 1983 to 1987. The program was a reaction to the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Fifth_generation_computer" title="Fifth generation computer">Fifth Generation project</a>, which aimed to create a computer using <a href="/wiki/Massively_parallel_computing" class="mw-redirect" title="Massively parallel computing">massively parallel computing</a>/processing. The program is not focused any specific technology such as robotics but it supports research in knowledge engineering in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It has been likened in operations to the <a href="/wiki/DARPA" title="DARPA">U.S. Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA)</a> and Japan's ICOT. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alvey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the early 1980s, Japan invited the United Kingdom to become a part of the Fifth Generation Project. In response, a committee was created and was chaired by John Alvey, a technology director at BT. The report generated proposed a different course of action to the Japanese initiative and became the basis for the UK's rejection of the Fifth Generation and the creation of its own Alvey Programme.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The program's fundamental goal was the improvement of the advanced information technology in the UK to address the declining performance of this sector.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It operated in 1984 until 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alvey was not involved in the program itself <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Focus areas for the Alvey Programme included:
</p>
<ul><li>VLSI (<a href="/wiki/Very-large-scale_integration" class="mw-redirect" title="Very-large-scale integration">very large scale integration</a>) technology for microelectronics</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Knowledge-based_systems" title="Knowledge-based systems">Intelligent Knowledge Based Systems</a> (IKBS) or <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">Artificial Intelligence</a> (AI)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Software_Engineering" class="mw-redirect" title="Software Engineering">Software Engineering</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Man-Machine_Interface" class="mw-redirect" title="Man-Machine Interface">Man-Machine Interface</a> (included <a href="/wiki/Natural_language_processing" title="Natural language processing">Natural Language Processing</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Systems_architecture" title="Systems architecture">Systems Architecture</a> (for <a href="/wiki/Parallel_computing" title="Parallel computing">parallel processing</a>)</li></ul>
