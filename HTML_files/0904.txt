<p><b>Kathleen Agnes Cavendish, Marchioness of Hartington</b> (<i>née</i> <b>Kennedy</b>; February 20, 1920 – May 13, 1948), also known as <b>"Kick" Kennedy</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was an <a href="/wiki/Americans" title="Americans">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Socialite" title="Socialite">socialite</a>. She was the daughter of <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy,_Sr." class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph P. Kennedy, Sr.">Joseph P. Kennedy, Sr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rose_Kennedy" title="Rose Kennedy">Rose Kennedy</a>, sister of future U.S. President <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>, and wife of the <a href="/wiki/William_Cavendish,_Marquess_of_Hartington" title="William Cavendish, Marquess of Hartington">Marquess of Hartington</a>, heir apparent to the <a href="/wiki/10th_Duke_of_Devonshire" class="mw-redirect" title="10th Duke of Devonshire">10th Duke of Devonshire</a>.
</p><p>When her father was serving as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom">United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom</a>, Kathleen made many friends in London and was the "<a href="/wiki/Debutante" title="Debutante">debutante</a> of 1938." Working with the <a href="/wiki/Red_Cross" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Cross">Red Cross</a>, she began a romantic relationship with Lord Hartington, whom she married in May 1944. He was killed on active service in Belgium only four months later. Kick died in a plane crash in 1948, flying to the south of France while on vacation with her new partner, the <a href="/wiki/Peter_Wentworth-Fitzwilliam,_8th_Earl_Fitzwilliam" title="Peter Wentworth-Fitzwilliam, 8th Earl Fitzwilliam">8th Earl Fitzwilliam</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kathleen Agnes Kennedy was born in <a href="/wiki/Brookline,_Massachusetts" title="Brookline, Massachusetts">Brookline, Massachusetts</a>, the fourth child and second daughter of <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy,_Sr." class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph P. Kennedy, Sr.">Joseph P. Kennedy, Sr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rose_Kennedy" title="Rose Kennedy">Rose Kennedy</a>. She was nicknamed "Kick" because of her "irrepressible nature." Kathleen was especially close to her older brother, <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>, known as "Jack". Her other siblings included <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy_Jr." title="Joseph P. Kennedy Jr.">Joseph P. Kennedy Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rosemary_Kennedy" title="Rosemary Kennedy">Rosemary Kennedy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eunice_Kennedy_Shriver" title="Eunice Kennedy Shriver">Eunice Kennedy Shriver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patricia_Kennedy_Lawford" title="Patricia Kennedy Lawford">Patricia Kennedy Lawford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kennedy" title="Ted Kennedy">Ted Kennedy</a>, Robert Kennedy and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Kennedy_Smith" title="Jean Kennedy Smith">Jean Kennedy Smith</a>.
</p><p>Kathleen was educated at <a href="/wiki/Riverdale_Country_School" title="Riverdale Country School">Riverdale Country School</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Riverdale,_The_Bronx" class="mw-redirect" title="Riverdale, The Bronx">Riverdale</a> section of <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. She also attended Noroton Convent of the Sacred Heart in <a href="/wiki/Darien,_Connecticut#Sections_of_the_town" title="Darien, Connecticut">Noroton, Connecticut</a> and the Holy Child Convent in <a href="/wiki/Neuilly" title="Neuilly">Neuilly</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-jfklibrary_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jfklibrary-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> While the Kennedy daughters were not raised to have political ambitions like their brothers, they were nonetheless provided with many of the same educational and social opportunities owing to their father's powerful financial and political connections and influence. This was particularly the case when President <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a> appointed Joseph as <a href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom">United States Ambassador to the United Kingdom</a> in 1938.
</p><p>As a child, Kathleen was very athletic and played <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">football</a> with her brothers. Her optimism and high spirits attracted many suitors, some of whom were Jack's closest friends. When Kathleen attended the Riverdale Country School, her mother did not approve of the male attention she attracted and sent her to the all-girls Noroton Convent of the Sacred Heart. Eventually, Kathleen started to date and had her first serious relationship, with <a href="/wiki/J._Peter_Grace" title="J. Peter Grace">Peter Grace</a>, an heir to <a href="/wiki/W._R._Grace_and_Company" title="W. R. Grace and Company">W. R. Grace and Company</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Britain">Britain</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Britain">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kick's time in Britain during her father's term as Ambassador would dramatically influence the remainder of her life. While living in England, she was educated in London at <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_College,_London" title="Queen&#39;s College, London">Queen's College</a> and quickly cultivated a wide circle of friends, both male and female, in British high society. She dated <a href="/wiki/David_Rockefeller" title="David Rockefeller">David Rockefeller</a> and was declared the "debutante of 1938" by the English media.
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_Poland" title="Invasion of Poland">invasion of Poland</a> and the outbreak of <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> in September 1939, the entire Kennedy family, save for Joseph and daughter <a href="/wiki/Rosemary_Kennedy" title="Rosemary Kennedy">Rosemary</a>, returned to the United States. Kathleen, having become very fond of England and the many friends she had made during her two years there, petitioned her parents to remain in London in spite of the coming danger. However, she was overruled by her father and sailed back home in the early fall of 1939.
</p><p>After returning to the U.S., Kennedy enrolled at the <a href="/wiki/Finch_College" title="Finch College">Finch School</a> for a time and then attended Florida Commercial College. In addition to her studies, she also began doing volunteer work for the <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">American Red Cross</a>. In 1941, she decided to leave school and began working as a research assistant for Frank Waldrop, the executive editor for the <i><a href="/wiki/Washington_Times-Herald" title="Washington Times-Herald">Washington Times-Herald</a></i>. She later teamed with <a href="/wiki/Inga_Arvad" title="Inga Arvad">Inga Arvad</a>, who wrote the "Did You Happen to See....." column and was eventually given her own column where she reviewed films and plays.<sup id="cite_ref-jfklibrary_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jfklibrary-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage">Marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lord_Cavendish.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Lord_Cavendish.jpg/220px-Lord_Cavendish.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Lord_Cavendish.jpg/330px-Lord_Cavendish.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Lord_Cavendish.jpg/440px-Lord_Cavendish.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="607" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lord_Cavendish.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kathleen on her wedding day in 1944</div></div></div>
<p>In 1943, seeking a way to return to England, Kathleen signed up to work in a center for servicemen set up by the Red Cross. During her time in England, both before and particularly during the war, she gradually but increasingly grew more independent from her family and the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Roman Catholic Church</a> they belonged to. During this time, Kick began a romantic relationship with politician <a href="/wiki/William_Cavendish,_Marquess_of_Hartington" title="William Cavendish, Marquess of Hartington">William Cavendish, Marquess of Hartington</a> (usually known to his family and friends as Billy Hartington). He was the eldest son and <a href="/wiki/Heir_apparent" title="Heir apparent">heir apparent</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Edward_Cavendish,_10th_Duke_of_Devonshire" title="Edward Cavendish, 10th Duke of Devonshire">10th Duke of Devonshire</a>.
</p><p>The two had met and begun a friendship when she moved to England when her father was appointed American Ambassador. Despite objections from her mother, Kennedy and Lord Hartington reunited upon her return to England.<sup id="cite_ref-buck_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buck-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Rose especially rejected their relationship because she saw that their marriage would break the laws of the Roman Catholic Church by allowing Kathleen's children to be raised in the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_England" title="Church of England">Church of England</a> (<a href="/wiki/Anglican_Communion" title="Anglican Communion">Anglican Communion</a>) rather than the Roman Catholic Church.  Rose even tried to manipulate their relationship by keeping Kathleen away from Hartington and postponing a possible wedding. Regardless, Kathleen stubbornly married Hartington on May 6, 1944, in a civil ceremony at the <a href="/wiki/Caxton_Hall" title="Caxton Hall">Caxton Hall</a> <a href="/wiki/Register_office" class="mw-redirect" title="Register office">Registry Office</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pittsburgh_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pittsburgh-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Kick's eldest brother <a href="/wiki/Joseph_P._Kennedy,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Joseph P. Kennedy, Jr.">Joseph P. Kennedy, Jr.</a>, an officer in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>, to whom she had grown close during the last year of his life as he was serving in Britain in the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Air_Forces" title="United States Army Air Forces">United States Army Air Forces</a> during the war, was the only member of the family to attend the ceremony. Her second eldest brother, Jack, was still hospitalized due to a back injury incurred on the motor torpedo patrol boat <a href="/wiki/Motor_Torpedo_Boat_PT-109" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor Torpedo Boat PT-109"><i>PT-109</i></a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">South Pacific Ocean</a>, while her younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Robert_F._Kennedy" title="Robert F. Kennedy">Robert F. Kennedy</a>, was in naval training. On August 12, 1944, Joe Jr. was killed when his plane exploded over the <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a> during a top-secret bombing mission in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-pittsburgh_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pittsburgh-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Widowhood">Widowhood</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Widowhood">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kathleen, now <a href="/wiki/Marchioness" class="mw-redirect" title="Marchioness">Marchioness</a> of Hartington, and Lord Hartington spent less than five weeks together before he went out to fight in France. Four months after their marriage, and less than a month after Joe Jr. was killed, Hartington was killed by a sniper during a battle with the Germans in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. With his family's blessing, he was buried close to where he fell. His younger brother <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Cavendish,_11th_Duke_of_Devonshire" title="Andrew Cavendish, 11th Duke of Devonshire">Lord Andrew Cavendish</a>, who was married to <a href="/wiki/Deborah_Cavendish,_Duchess_of_Devonshire" title="Deborah Cavendish, Duchess of Devonshire">Debo Mitford</a> of the famous <a href="/wiki/Mitford_family" title="Mitford family">Mitford sisters</a>, thus became the <a href="/wiki/Heir_apparent" title="Heir apparent">heir apparent</a> to the dukedom as Billy Hartington had left no heirs.
</p><p>Popular on the London social circuit and admired by many for her high spirits and wit, Lady Hartington eventually became romantically involved with the <a href="/wiki/Peter_Wentworth-Fitzwilliam,_8th_Earl_Fitzwilliam" title="Peter Wentworth-Fitzwilliam, 8th Earl Fitzwilliam">8th Earl Fitzwilliam</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> who was in the process of divorcing his wife. Once again, Rose Kennedy expressed her disapproval of her daughter's suitor and warned Kick that she would be disowned and cut off financially if she married Lord Fitzwilliam. In May 1948, Kathleen learned that her father would be traveling to Paris. In an effort to gain his consent for her upcoming plans to marry Fitzwilliam, she decided to fly to Paris to meet with her father.<sup id="cite_ref-hilty_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hilty-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Peter%27s_Churchyard,_Edensor_-_grave_of_Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington_(n%C3%A9e_Kennedy,_1920%E2%80%931948).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/St_Peter%27s_Churchyard%2C_Edensor_-_grave_of_Kathleen_Cavendish%2C_Marchioness_of_Hartington_%28n%C3%A9e_Kennedy%2C_1920%E2%80%931948%29.JPG/220px-St_Peter%27s_Churchyard%2C_Edensor_-_grave_of_Kathleen_Cavendish%2C_Marchioness_of_Hartington_%28n%C3%A9e_Kennedy%2C_1920%E2%80%931948%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/St_Peter%27s_Churchyard%2C_Edensor_-_grave_of_Kathleen_Cavendish%2C_Marchioness_of_Hartington_%28n%C3%A9e_Kennedy%2C_1920%E2%80%931948%29.JPG/330px-St_Peter%27s_Churchyard%2C_Edensor_-_grave_of_Kathleen_Cavendish%2C_Marchioness_of_Hartington_%28n%C3%A9e_Kennedy%2C_1920%E2%80%931948%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/St_Peter%27s_Churchyard%2C_Edensor_-_grave_of_Kathleen_Cavendish%2C_Marchioness_of_Hartington_%28n%C3%A9e_Kennedy%2C_1920%E2%80%931948%29.JPG/440px-St_Peter%27s_Churchyard%2C_Edensor_-_grave_of_Kathleen_Cavendish%2C_Marchioness_of_Hartington_%28n%C3%A9e_Kennedy%2C_1920%E2%80%931948%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1488" data-file-height="1984" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:St_Peter%27s_Churchyard,_Edensor_-_grave_of_Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington_(n%C3%A9e_Kennedy,_1920%E2%80%931948).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/St_Peter%27s_Church,_Edensor" title="St Peter&#39;s Church, Edensor">St Peter's Churchyard, Edensor</a> - grave of Kathleen Cavendish, Marchioness of Hartington (née Kennedy, 1920–1948). Her grave is marked with a headstone and a plaque in the ground commemorating the visit of 35th U.S. President <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> at the gravesite. On her gravestone, it says "Joy she gave joy she has found"</div></div></div>
<p>On May 13, 1948, Lady Hartington and Lord Fitzwilliam were flying from Paris to the <a href="/wiki/French_Riviera" title="French Riviera">French Riviera</a> for a vacation<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> aboard a <a href="/wiki/De_Havilland_DH.104_Dove" class="mw-redirect" title="De Havilland DH.104 Dove">de Havilland DH.104 Dove</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> At 3:30 in the afternoon, their plane took off, reaching an altitude of 10,000 feet (3,000 m). Approximately one hour into the flight, radio contact was lost with the plane when it entered the region near Vienne which was also close to the center of a storm. The plane's four occupants endured twenty minutes of severe turbulence which bounced their small plane up and down as much as several thousand feet at a time.
</p><p>When they finally cleared the clouds, they instantly discovered the plane was in a dive and moments away from impact, and they attempted to pull up. The stress of the turbulence coupled with the sudden change of direction tore loose one of the wings, followed by both engines and finally the tail. The plane's <a href="/wiki/Fuselage" title="Fuselage">fuselage</a> then spun into the ground seconds later, coming to rest nose down in a ravine after striking terrain  at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Plateau_du_Coiron&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Plateau du Coiron (page does not exist)">Plateau du Coiron</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Saint-Bauzile,_Ard%C3%A8che" title="Saint-Bauzile, Ardèche">Saint-Bauzile, Ardèche</a>, France. Lady Hartington was instantly killed along with Fitzwilliam, pilot Peter Townshend, and navigator Arthur Freeman.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Her father was the only family member to attend the funeral, arranged by the Cavendishes. Rose Kennedy refused to attend her daughter's memorial service, instead, it was claimed, choosing to enter a hospital for routine medical tests.<sup id="cite_ref-hilty_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hilty-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Rose later said her daughter's death was "God pointing a finger."
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_culture">Popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kathleen_Cavendish,_Marchioness_of_Hartington&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Kennedy Debutante</i>, a novelization of Kennedy's life, was published by <a href="/wiki/Berkley_Books" title="Berkley Books">Berkley Books</a> in 2018. Written by Kerri Maher, it was well received, including being named a "Best Book of the Week" by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Post" title="New York Post">New York Post</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kick Kennedy is portrayed in the 1993 movie <i><a href="/wiki/JFK:_Reckless_Youth" title="JFK: Reckless Youth">JFK: Reckless Youth</a></i>.
</p>
