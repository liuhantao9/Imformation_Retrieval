<p>The <b>Dutch Language Union</b> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>: <span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap;margin-right:.25em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nl-Nederlandse_Taalunie.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" decoding="async" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a></span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/7f/Nl-Nederlandse_Taalunie.ogg" class="internal" title="Nl-Nederlandse Taalunie.ogg"><i>Nederlandse Taalunie</i></a></span>&#160;<small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Nl-Nederlandse_Taalunie.ogg" title="File:Nl-Nederlandse Taalunie.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span>, NTU) is an international regulatory institution that governs issues regarding the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch language</a>. It is best known for its spelling reforms which are promulgated by member states, grammar books, the <a href="/wiki/Green_Booklet" class="mw-redirect" title="Green Booklet">Green Booklet</a> and its support of Dutch language courses and studies worldwide. It was founded on 9 September 1980 by the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> (in respect of the <a href="/wiki/Flemish_Community" title="Flemish Community">Flemish Community</a>). <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> has been an associate member of the <i>Taalunie</i> since 2004.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Language_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:292px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_Dutch_World_scris.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Map_Dutch_World_scris.png/290px-Map_Dutch_World_scris.png" decoding="async" width="290" height="127" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Map_Dutch_World_scris.png/435px-Map_Dutch_World_scris.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Map_Dutch_World_scris.png/580px-Map_Dutch_World_scris.png 2x" data-file-width="1425" data-file-height="625" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Map_Dutch_World_scris.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Dutch-speaking world.</div></div></div>
<p>The Dutch Language Union was established by a treaty between Belgium and the Netherlands, signed on 9 September 1980 in Brussels. It succeeded the "Cultural Agreement" (governing more than just language) between the two countries signed just after the Second World War. This agreement was redone in 1995, after the <a href="/wiki/State_reform_in_Belgium" title="State reform in Belgium">federalization of Belgium</a>, and a new treaty was signed between the Netherlands and <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Willemijns176_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Willemijns176-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> On 12 December 2003, president of the Committee of Ministers of the Dutch Language Union <a href="/wiki/Medy_van_der_Laan" title="Medy van der Laan">Medy van der Laan</a> and Minister of Education of Suriname <a href="/w/index.php?title=Walter_Sandriman&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Walter Sandriman (page does not exist)">Walter Sandriman</a> signed the agreement to the accession of Suriname to the union. The accession was ratified by the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(Suriname)" title="National Assembly (Suriname)">National Assembly of Suriname</a> in 2004 and came into force in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-Suriname_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suriname-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Standard Dutch (<i>Algemeen Nederlands</i>, often abbreviated to <i>AN</i>) is the <a href="/wiki/Standard_language" title="Standard language">standard language</a> as it is taught in schools and used by authorities in the Netherlands, <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a>, Suriname, and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Antilles" title="Netherlands Antilles">Dutch Caribbean</a>. The Dutch Language Union defines what is <i>AN</i> and what is not. Since efforts to "uplift" people had come to be considered rather presumptuous, the earlier name <i>Algemeen Beschaafd Nederlands</i> ("Common Civilised Dutch") and its abbreviation, <i>ABN</i>, were replaced with <i>Algemeen Nederlands</i> (<i>AN</i>). The implication that <a href="/w/index.php?title=Non-standard_variety&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Non-standard variety (page does not exist)">non-standard varieties</a> are not civilised was thus removed.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Word_list">Word list</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Language_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Word list">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Groene_Boekje_1954.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Groene_Boekje_1954.png/220px-Groene_Boekje_1954.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="264" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/eb/Groene_Boekje_1954.png 1.5x" data-file-width="234" data-file-height="281" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Groene_Boekje_1954.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Groene Boekje</i> (1954)</div></div></div>
<p>An important change that was carried out by the Dutch Language Union was the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Dutch_orthography#The_spelling_reform_of_1996_.28The_Netherlands_and_Flanders.29" title="History of Dutch orthography">modification</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dutch_orthography" title="Dutch orthography">Dutch orthography</a> in 1995, regarding in particular the writing of the <a href="/wiki/Interfix" title="Interfix">interfix</a> <i>-n</i> in many <a href="/wiki/Compound_(linguistics)" title="Compound (linguistics)">compounds</a>. Among the Union’s publications is the well-known <a href="/wiki/Word_list_of_the_Dutch_language" title="Word list of the Dutch language">Word list of the Dutch language</a> (<i>Woordenlijst Nederlandse taal</i>), commonly known as the “<i>Groene boekje</i>” ("<i>Green booklet</i>", because of its distinctive green colour). The green booklet is the official orthographic and grammatical reference of the Dutch language. It is laid out like a vocabulary, including plural forms and spelling but without actual word definitions.
</p><p>The most recent version of the Green Booklet appeared on 13 October 2015. Its content does not differ from the previous version of 2005, which included a somewhat controversial spelling reform which was not received well in general because a part of the spelling reforms of 1996 was changed again. In December 2005, most of the Dutch mainstream media announced a boycott. In August 2006, they released a 'White book' as their own, subtly different guideline. Currently these two spellings are both in use, sometimes confusing people; the 'green' one is used by schools and officials, the 'white' one by papers, magazines and television stations. 
</p><p>In Belgium, on the other hand, the spelling reform was generally accepted without protest.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dictionary">Dictionary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Language_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Dictionary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Van_Dale" title="Van Dale">Van Dale</a> dictionary is commonly accepted as the most authoritative <a href="/wiki/Dutch_dictionary" class="mw-redirect" title="Dutch dictionary">Dutch dictionary</a>. Commonly named the <a href="/wiki/Dikke_Van_Dale" class="mw-redirect" title="Dikke Van Dale">Dikke Van Dale</a> ("fat Van Dale" due to its size) the dictionary is split into three tomes (A–I, J–R, S–Z) and is usually updated every 7–8 years. The 15th edition was published on 23 September 2015.
</p><p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Woordenboek_der_Nederlandsche_Taal" title="Woordenboek der Nederlandsche Taal">Woordenboek der Nederlandsche Taal</a></i> (<i><b>WNT</b></i>) is a historic dictionary that includes all words used from 1500 to the present day.
</p><p>Work began in 1864 and was finished in 1998 when the last volume was published. It has 43 volumes, needing about 3m if placed on a shelf, with about 400,000 words on 49,255 pages. In 2001, three additional volumes were published containing mostly words from the 20th century that were not included in the previous 43 volumes. This dictionary is published by Instituut voor Nederlandse Lexicologie and can be found online.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dutch_as_a_foreign_language">Dutch as a foreign language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Language_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dutch as a foreign language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Dutch Language Union supports the teaching of Dutch in primary and secondary schools in the neighbouring regions and countries. It concerns <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Wallonia</a>; 350,000 learners), <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lower_Saxony" title="Lower Saxony">Lower Saxony</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">North Rhine-Westphalia</a>, 40,000 learners) and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> (<a href="/wiki/Nord-Pas-de-Calais" title="Nord-Pas-de-Calais">Nord-Pas-de-Calais</a>, 8,000 learners). 
</p><p>The Union also supports the study of <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch language</a> and <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_the_Netherlands" title="Culture of the Netherlands">culture</a> at universities and schools worldwide. Approximately 14,000 people study the Dutch language and Dutch literature at 140 institutions.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">Organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Language_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Internal_organisation">Internal organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Language_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Internal organisation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The NTU has offices in The Hague and Brussels, and it comprises four institutions:
</p>
<ul><li>The Secretariat-General, responsible for preparing and implementing policy, headed by Hans Bennis since 2017</li>
<li>The Committee of Ministers, containing the ministers of education and culture from the Netherlands and <a href="/wiki/Flanders" title="Flanders">Flanders</a>;</li>
<li>The Interparliamentary Commission, made up of members of the Dutch and Flemish parliaments;</li>
<li>The Council for Dutch Language and Literature.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Instituut_voor_de_Nederlandse_Taal"><span id="Instituut_voor_de_Nederlandse_Taal"></span><i>Instituut voor de Nederlandse Taal</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Language_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Instituut voor de Nederlandse Taal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The binational (Belgium and the Netherlands) <i>Instituut voor de Nederlandse Taal</i> ("Institute for the Dutch Language") in Leiden, formerly <i>Instituut voor Nederlandse Lexicologie</i>, works under the auspices of the Dutch Language Union, and is responsible for the <i><a href="/wiki/Woordenboek_der_Nederlandsche_Taal" title="Woordenboek der Nederlandsche Taal">Woordenboek der Nederlandsche Taal</a></i> (Dictionary of the Dutch language).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In addition, the institute publishes a number of dictionaries, including the <i>Old Dutch Dictionary</i>, the <i>Early Middle Dutch Dictionary</i>, and an etymological dictionary; and databases, including databases indexing various newspapers and magazines, and legal documents. The <i>Spoken Dutch Corpus</i> has contemporary Dutch as spoken in the Netherlands and Flanders, including audio recordings of Standard Dutch.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Royal_Academy_of_Dutch_language_and_literature">Royal Academy of Dutch language and literature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Language_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Royal Academy of Dutch language and literature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Dutch_language_and_literature" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Academy of Dutch language and literature">Royal Academy of Dutch language and literature</a> was founded in Belgium in 1886 to stimulate the culture and literature of Flanders. It is the official advisory board to the Flanders government, and publishes the <i>Materiaal Willems</i> database, based on the dialect surveys done by Pieter Willems, containing material from dialects in Flanders, the Netherlands, and <a href="/wiki/French_Flanders" title="French Flanders">French Flanders</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="External_connections">External connections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Language_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: External connections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The organisation is also responsible for the external linguistic policy of the Netherlands and Flanders and is active in <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, two countries with historic links with the Dutch language. Nevertheless, cooperation with South Africa is not limited to the <a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a> language but aimed also at fostering multilingualism.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The purposes of the organisation are limited to Dutch language and Dutch-language literature, and are hence not as wide as those of the <a href="/wiki/Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries" title="Community of Portuguese Language Countries">Community of Portuguese Language Countries</a>, <a href="/wiki/La_Francophonie" class="mw-redirect" title="La Francophonie">La Francophonie</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a>.
</p><p>The Treaty on the Dutch Language Union foresees the possibility that the Kingdom of the Netherlands extends application to NTU member's parts outside Europe. The organs of the NTU are its Committee of Ministers (composed of the ministers of education and culture of the Dutch and Flemish Governments), its Secretariat-General, an Interparliamentary Committee (composed of members of the <a href="/wiki/States_General_of_the_Netherlands" title="States General of the Netherlands">States General of the Netherlands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Flemish_Parliament" title="Flemish Parliament">Flemish Parliament</a>) and a Dutch Language and Literature Council (composed of twelve independent experts). There are specific arrangements for the participation of Suriname in the organisation's inner workings.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Member_states">Member states</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Language_Union&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Member states">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the basis of the Taalunie is a treaty between the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Belgium" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom of Belgium">Kingdom of Belgium</a>, the two sovereign states that constitute the Union. For the Kingdom of the Netherlands, the treaty is only applicable to its <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">European territory</a>.
The treaty allows for two types of extensions:
</p>
<ul><li>membership of other sovereign states through an "association membership"<sup id="cite_ref-verdrag_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-verdrag-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>extension to other territories of the Kingdom of the Netherlands (presently: <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sint-Maarten" class="mw-redirect" title="Sint-Maarten">Sint-Maarten</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Netherlands" title="Caribbean Netherlands">Caribbean Netherlands</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-verdrag_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-verdrag-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>In 2004, Suriname signed an "associative treaty" with the Taalunie.<sup id="cite_ref-Suriname_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Suriname-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> From 27 November 2013 the treaty also applies to the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Netherlands" title="Caribbean Netherlands">Caribbean Netherlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
The three autonomous Caribbean countries of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">Aruba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Curaçao">Curaçao</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a>, are designated as candidate member states.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> are considered "special partners" of the Dutch Language Union.
</p>
