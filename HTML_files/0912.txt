<p>The <b>Noble and Greenough School</b>, commonly known as <b>Nobles</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Coeducation" class="mw-redirect" title="Coeducation">coeducational</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nonsectarian" title="Nonsectarian">nonsectarian</a> day and five-day boarding school for students in grades seven through twelve. It is near Boston on a 187-acre (0.76&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) campus that borders the <a href="/wiki/Charles_River" title="Charles River">Charles River</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dedham,_Massachusetts" title="Dedham, Massachusetts">Dedham, Massachusetts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-glance_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-glance-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The current enrollment of 614 students includes a balance of boys and girls.<sup id="cite_ref-glance_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-glance-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The boarding program hosts 45 students who live on campus five days a week. The majority of students are from <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, neighboring states and occasionally from abroad. In recent history, all members of the senior class go on to accredited four-year colleges and universities. In 2010, Nobles was ranked as the 18th best prep school in the United States by <i><a href="/wiki/Forbes" title="Forbes">Forbes</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Nobles has 134 faculty members, with a student to faculty ratio of approximately 6:1. The average class size is 12. Tuition for the 2018–2019 academic year is $50,200 for day students and $56,000 for five-day boarding students.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Nobles' historic athletic rival is <a href="/wiki/Milton_Academy" title="Milton Academy">Milton Academy</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_and_Greenough_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nobles was founded in 1866 by George Washington Copp Noble, in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, Massachusetts, as an all-boys preparatory school for <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>. It became known as Noble &amp; Greenough in 1892. During World War I, the school merged with Boston-based Volkman School, which had faced a drastically declining student population due to the headmaster's German origins. There is a monument to the Volkman School on the Nobles campus. In 1922, the school moved from Boston to its current location in <a href="/wiki/Dedham,_Massachusetts" title="Dedham, Massachusetts">Dedham</a>. The property had been a family's estate. The grounds were designed by <a href="/wiki/Frederick_Law_Olmsted" title="Frederick Law Olmsted">Frederick Law Olmsted</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The school discontinued the  lower school at this time, which caused parents to start the <a href="/wiki/Dexter_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Dexter School">Dexter School</a>, to fill the gap created. In 1975, Nobles began admitting girls.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Facts">Facts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_and_Greenough_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Facts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Noble and Greenough School includes grades 7–12. Students in grade 12 are known as members of Class I, this continues on down to seventh grade students who are known as members of Class VI, or more informally as the "sixies". The eighth grade students, members of class V, are also informally known as "fifthies", The Middle school is located in the Pratt Middle School building. The Upper School, grades 9–12, is in the main building, known as the Shattuck Schoolhouse.
</p><p>The school covers 187 acres (0.76&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) in Dedham, Massachusetts. The grounds include nine athletic fields, two gyms, and a sizable length of the <a href="/wiki/Charles_River" title="Charles River">Charles River</a>.
</p><p>Nobles alumni have been prominently featured in recent news coverage. <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Parsons" title="Sarah Parsons">Sarah Parsons</a> '05 scored four goals in the 2006 <a href="/wiki/Winter_Olympic_Games" title="Winter Olympic Games">Winter Olympic Games</a> for the USA's Olympic women's hockey team. <a href="/wiki/Helen_Resor_(ice_hockey)" title="Helen Resor (ice hockey)">Helen Resor</a> '04 was also on this team. <a href="/wiki/Ayla_Brown" title="Ayla Brown">Ayla Brown</a> '06 was on the American television show <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol" title="American Idol">American Idol</a></i> as one of the final 13 contestants. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Andrea_Ross&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Andrea Ross (page does not exist)">Andrea Ross</a> '09 sang in the Concert for Diana and is currently on tour performing in a musical produced by <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Lloyd_Webber" title="Andrew Lloyd Webber">Andrew Lloyd Webber</a>. Michelle Picard '11 is on the USA's Olympic women's hockey team and competed in the 2014 Sochi Olympics. <a href="/wiki/John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="John Fitzgerald Kennedy">John Fitzgerald Kennedy</a> (John F. Kennedy) also attended Noble and Greenough School when he was in 7th and 8th Grade but could not graduate.
</p><p>In September 2006, Nobles completed a state of the art, $20 million, 40,000-square-foot (3,700&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) Arts Center, which houses theatrical productions as well as musical performances.  The theatre was funded by the Viniks; thus the new venue was named the Vinik Theatre.  This was part of an $86 million capital campaign ending in 2008 that significantly improved faculty compensation as well as other aspects of the school. Additional theaters in the school include: Lawrence Auditorium in the main building, Vinik 
Theatre in the arts building, and Towles Theater in the Baker science building. In 2012, Nobles approved the construction of a dance studio adjacent to the Arts Center. Construction started in June 2013 and was completed in January 2014.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Noble_and_Greenough_Middle_School">The Noble and Greenough Middle School</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_and_Greenough_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Noble and Greenough Middle School">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Noble and Greenough Middle School consists of exactly 122 students in the 7th and 8th grades, with approximately 60 students in each grade. The Middle School has a different afternoon activities program from the Upper School. Not all students start in the Middle School at Nobles.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Athletics_at_Nobles">Athletics at Nobles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_and_Greenough_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Athletics at Nobles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nobles is a member of the highly competitive <a href="/wiki/Independent_School_League_(Boston_Area)" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent School League (Boston Area)">Independent School League</a>. The school has 25 varsity teams. Boys and girls participate in soccer, cross-country, hockey, basketball, squash, skiing, golf, lacrosse, tennis, crew, and the newly formed ultimate frisbee team. Boys also participate in football, wrestling, and baseball, while girls participate in volleyball, field hockey and softball.
</p><p>Former <a href="/wiki/Boston_College" title="Boston College">Boston College</a> hockey players, goalie, <a href="/wiki/John_Muse" title="John Muse">John Muse</a> (<a href="/wiki/American_Hockey_League" title="American Hockey League">AHL</a>), and forwards, <a href="/wiki/Miles_Wood" title="Miles Wood">Miles Wood</a> (<a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">NHL</a>), <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Hayes_(ice_hockey)" title="Jimmy Hayes (ice hockey)">Jimmy Hayes</a> (NHL) and younger brother <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Hayes_(ice_hockey)" title="Kevin Hayes (ice hockey)">Kevin Hayes</a> (NHL), attended Nobles.
</p><p>Nobles and <a href="/wiki/Milton_Academy" title="Milton Academy">Milton Academy</a> historically have a Nobles/Milton Day each athletic season. On this day, usually nearing, or on the last game of the season, the two schools compete in almost every sport. Students are known to "get psyched" by face-painting, reciting chants, and wearing team colors.
</p><p>Over the past seven years (2006–2013):<sup id="cite_ref-glance_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-glance-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Visual_arts">Visual arts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_and_Greenough_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Visual arts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All Nobles students take at least one semester of introductory-level visual arts as a graduation requirement. Students are instructed in photography (darkroom and digital), painting, drawing, ceramics, and digital design.
</p><p>Students' art can be found on display in the Dawson Gallery, Schmid Gallery, and on <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://nobles.edu/home/content.asp?id=2124&amp;pointID=2139&amp;zzSec=school">Exposure</a>, Nobles' visual arts website. Additionally, the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.fostergallery.org/">Foster Gallery</a> showcases the work of external, regional artists. Finally, Calliopé, a student-run literary and arts magazine, produces two to three issues each year.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_and_Greenough_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are several publications at the Noble and Greenough School. The official school newspaper, <i>The Nobleman</i>, is student-run, overseen by faculty advisors, and funded by the school. In typically 20-page issues, the paper covers a range of topics from local school events to global issues. There are also many smaller publications such as Calliope (arts), Nobellium (science), and Cogito (international affairs).
</p><p>There is also <i>Nobles</i> magazine, published three times a year for graduates, past and current parents and grandparents, students and supporters of the Noble and Greenough School.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Clubs_and_organizations">Clubs and organizations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_and_Greenough_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Clubs and organizations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A variety of student-run clubs and organizations at Nobles meet both during and after regular school hours. These groups include a Chess, Weiqi, Debate, Outing club, computer science club, and more. As well as student boards for community service and multicultural students. The Nobleman, a student newspaper, and the Nobles Theatre Collective reflect student-generated interest in writing and the arts. Many take place during "X - Block", "M - Block", and in after-school meetings.
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Outing_club">Outing club</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_and_Greenough_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Outing club">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>The Nobles outing club is run by math teacher, senior master, and avid outdoorsman, Richard Nickerson. The club sponsors hiking trips and rock climbing trips throughout the year on the weekends.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traditions">Traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Noble_and_Greenough_School&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Traditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Morning Assembly:</b> Every morning Noble's students begin the day with an all-school assembly. Every other week, on a Wednesday, is a 'long assembly' that features distinguished speakers or performers. 
</p><p><b>Christmas Carol:</b> Every year before the winter break, the sixies (first year middle-schoolers) put on a rendition of Charles Dickens' <i>Christmas Carol</i>.
</p><p>"<b>Power of Light</b>" Along with the "Christmas Carol" part of the sixie students perform the "Power of Light" a story about two  Jewish children escaping from the Nazis in World War II.*
</p>
<ul><li>In 2014 and 2018, a rendition of a Yiddish folk tale, "Things Could Always Be Worse." was put on instead.</li></ul>
<p><b>The Nobleman:</b> <i>The Nobleman</i> is the school newspaper, run mostly by first class students.
</p><p><b>Castle Secret Passageways:</b> There are rumored to be many secret passages in the Castle. When the building served as the boys' dormitory, many would sneak out in the night to explore the building. The legend that surrounds this tradition is that there is a passageway that has never been found by anyone, but is supposed to extend from the castle, underground, to the other side of the Charles River. The student who finds this passage is supposed to be granted free tuition to the school until they graduate.
</p><p><b>Rivalry:</b> The Noble and Greenough School has a rivalry with <a href="/wiki/Milton_Academy" title="Milton Academy">Milton Academy</a> (also a member of the ISL) which becomes most prominent every fall during Nobles/Milton Weekend. Athletic teams from both schools play each other on the same campus (alternating yearly) creating a festive and heated environment. Courtesy of the 1975 Nobles Dictionary, "Milton: Nobles' satanic alter-ego." ... "Milton Game: the fame that determines the success of the season, as well as the fund drive." The Nobles/Milton football game is one of the oldest annual rivalries in the nation, beginning in 1886. The rivalry continues at the graduate level. Beginning in 1984, the graduate soccer teams from the two schools have met on the same afternoon as the school varsity teams play. As far as is known, this is the only rivalry of this nature that endures to present time.
</p><p><b>The Classics:</b> The school was started as a preparatory school for Harvard University. At the time one of the requirements for admission to Harvard was a thorough knowledge of Latin and Greek. Therefore, the students at Noble and Greenough primarily studied the Classics. This is still reflected in the middle school, which requires Latin in the seventh grade via a hybrid English-Latin class called English Via Latin. After the required seventh grade English Via Latin class, Latin is available as a course throughout middle and high school.
</p><p><b>Wiggins Papers:</b> Students of the junior class submit a portfolio of writing every year in expository, creative, and personal narrative styles. The most impressive are chosen for the Wiggins Prize.
</p><p><b>Senior Projects:</b> In the spring of their senior year, students have the option of dropping classes to undertake a project of their own design.
</p>
