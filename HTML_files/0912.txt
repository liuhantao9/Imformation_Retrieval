<p><b>South Elmsall</b> is a small town and <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> which lies to the east of <a href="/wiki/Hemsworth" title="Hemsworth">Hemsworth</a> in the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Wakefield" title="City of Wakefield">City of Wakefield</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. The town had a population in 2001 of 6,107,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> increasing to 6,519 at the 2011 Census.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Elmsall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Former_Corn_Mill_South_Elmsall.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Former_Corn_Mill_South_Elmsall.jpg/220px-Former_Corn_Mill_South_Elmsall.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="129" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Former_Corn_Mill_South_Elmsall.jpg/330px-Former_Corn_Mill_South_Elmsall.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Former_Corn_Mill_South_Elmsall.jpg/440px-Former_Corn_Mill_South_Elmsall.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="936" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Former_Corn_Mill_South_Elmsall.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The former mill, now modern housing</div></div></div>
<p>The town was largely a small farming settlement until the industrial revolution and the sinking of collieries caused a boom in population and a need for modern housing for the workforce. This has left a town with a mixture of stone and brick buildings. The town and its neighbours were mentioned in the <i><a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a></i>. Other industries such as quarrying for stone, agriculture and brick manufacture were also known at different periods of the town's history, with many of the former buildings and sites associated with them still existing.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_quarry_site">Former quarry site</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Elmsall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Former quarry site">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Eastern_Face_Former_South_Elmsall_Quarry.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Eastern_Face_Former_South_Elmsall_Quarry.jpg/220px-Eastern_Face_Former_South_Elmsall_Quarry.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Eastern_Face_Former_South_Elmsall_Quarry.jpg/330px-Eastern_Face_Former_South_Elmsall_Quarry.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Eastern_Face_Former_South_Elmsall_Quarry.jpg/440px-Eastern_Face_Former_South_Elmsall_Quarry.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Eastern_Face_Former_South_Elmsall_Quarry.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The former quarry, a nationally important site</div></div></div>
<p>The former South Elmsall quarry was deemed a site of national importance, by <a href="/wiki/Defra" class="mw-redirect" title="Defra">Defra</a>, due to the visible section of an unusually complete patch coral reef.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Coal_mining">Coal mining</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Elmsall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Coal mining">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is most famous for its coal-mining past; it was the site of <a href="/wiki/Frickley_Colliery" title="Frickley Colliery">Frickley Colliery</a>, which was one of the largest deep coal mines in the United Kingdom.  The colliery invested in several improvements that were of benefit to the area, including the building of the Warde Aldam Hospital, as well as swimming baths and sports facilities.
</p><p>The colliery became a key source of <a href="/wiki/National_Union_of_Mineworkers_(Great_Britain)" title="National Union of Mineworkers (Great Britain)">union radicalism</a>. During the <a href="/wiki/UK_Miners%27_Strike_(1984-1985)" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Miners&#39; Strike (1984-1985)">UK Miners' Strike</a>, there were numerous disturbances in the area. It was one of the last pits to return to work after the strike, seeing as it was picketed by a group of hardliners from <a href="/wiki/Kent_Miners%27_Association" title="Kent Miners&#39; Association">Kent</a> who continued to picket Frickley after the NUM had called off the strike.  The Frickley miners refused to cross the hardliners' picket.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The colliery was closed on 26 November 1993 following closures of nearby pits like Ferrymoor-Riddings (1985), Kinsley (1986), South Kirkby (1988) and <a href="/wiki/Grimethorpe" title="Grimethorpe">Grimethorpe</a> (1992). In 1998 the UK Governments "Social Exclusion Unit" published a series of "Policy Action Team" reports looking at issues of social exclusion. The Skills report used South Elmsall and surrounding towns as a case study and stated "The local communities still suffer from serious
environmental, social and economic problems - and a crisis of morale - as a result of the loss of jobs in the mining industry". <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warde-Aldam_Hospital">Warde-Aldam Hospital</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Elmsall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Warde-Aldam Hospital">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Warde-Aldam Hospital was a hospital situated in South Elmsall, Yorkshire, England.  It was built in 1911 by Carlton Main Colliery Ltd and was used as a general, voluntary hospital until 1948.  It was then taken over by the NHS and provided acute hospital care until 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Elmsall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is home to <a href="/wiki/Carlton_Main_Frickley_Colliery_Band" title="Carlton Main Frickley Colliery Band">Carlton Main Frickley Colliery Band</a>, a successful brass band who have previously been British and European champions.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Frickley_Country_Park">Frickley Country Park</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Elmsall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Frickley Country Park">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Frickley Country Park officially opened in September 2009, after a long period of land reclamation, environmental cleaning and landscaping of the former <a href="/wiki/Frickley_Colliery" title="Frickley Colliery">Frickley Colliery</a> site was finished. The site offers walks and play areas as well as space for new housing development as part of the wider regeneration scheme. Nearby is located the Frickley Off-road Centre, a specialist facility for motorsports.
</p>

<p>The large, traditional market is a central feature of the shopping area of the town, with approximately 105 trading stalls. On the main high street there are shops featuring many household names such as <a href="/wiki/Post_Office_Ltd" title="Post Office Ltd">The Post Office</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hallmark_Cards" title="Hallmark Cards">Hallmark Cards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Specsavers" title="Specsavers">Specsavers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Boots_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Boots Group">Boots Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greggs" title="Greggs">Greggs</a> alongside many other independent shops offering a range of goods and services such as travel, boutique fashion, wedding dress hire, sportswear and much more.
</p><p>On the outskirts of the town is the Dale Lane Industrial Estate which is home to a number of large, internationally recognised companies. For example, there are a number of <a href="/wiki/Next_plc" title="Next plc">Next Distribution</a> warehouses. The estate itself reflects the change in industry of the area, being one of the key expansion areas since the closure of the local collieries.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Elmsall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Carlton, Moorthorpe, Northfield and Ash Grove are the primary education schools in South Elmsall. The main source of secondary and further education in the town is <a href="/wiki/Minsthorpe_Community_College" title="Minsthorpe Community College">Minsthorpe Community College</a>, which also provides places to residents of South Kirkby and Upton. In 2008 Minsthorpe Community College gained its best ever results at all levels – KS3/GCSE/A-Level, the college GCSE results are higher than the local and national averages.
</p><p>The school also hosts a number of other facilities such as a sports and fitness suite, all weather floodlit sports pitches, a gym and an upcoming public swimming pool. The school and its science and sporting facilities were upgraded as one of the host training facilities for the London 2012 <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a>.
</p><p>Among a number of well known people who have an association with Minsthorpe include playwright <a href="/wiki/John_Godber" title="John Godber">John Godber</a>, actors Adrian Hood and Chris Walker and footballers <a href="/wiki/Jamie_McCombe" title="Jamie McCombe">Jamie</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_McCombe" title="John McCombe">John McCombe</a>.
</p>

</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Elmsall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Westfield_Lane_Stadium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Westfield_Lane_Stadium.jpg/220px-Westfield_Lane_Stadium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Westfield_Lane_Stadium.jpg/330px-Westfield_Lane_Stadium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Westfield_Lane_Stadium.jpg/440px-Westfield_Lane_Stadium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Westfield_Lane_Stadium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Westfield Lane Stadium</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Frickley_Athletic_F.C." title="Frickley Athletic F.C.">Frickley Athletic</a> are based in South Elmsall. Frickley play their home games at Westfield Lane Stadium.
</p><p>Other sports clubs include The South Elmsall Social Cycling Club, which was officially founded in 1934.
</p><p>Minsthorpe Marlins were based at South Elmsall but were left homeless when the public swimming baths were closed. A new facility is planned to open in early 2018.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_residents">Notable residents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=South_Elmsall&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Notable residents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

He won an award at the British Soap Awards in 2010 for his role, with on-screen wife, actress <a href="/wiki/Jan_Pearson" title="Jan Pearson">Jan Pearson</a>.</li>
<li>Footballer <a href="/wiki/George_Gibson_(footballer_born_1914)" class="mw-redirect" title="George Gibson (footballer born 1914)">George Gibson</a> played for South Elmsall-based <a href="/wiki/Frickley_Athletic_F.C." title="Frickley Athletic F.C.">Frickley Athletic</a> before becoming one of the early English footballers to play abroad.</li></ul>
