<p><b>Pudsey</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Market_town" title="Market town">market town</a> in <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>, England. Once independent, it was incorporated into the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a> <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a> in 1974. It is located midway between <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford city centre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Leeds_city_centre" title="Leeds city centre">Leeds city centre</a>. <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">Historically</a> in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a>, it has a population of 22,408.<sup id="cite_ref-2011_census_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2011_census-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It also lends its name and sits in the local <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a> <a href="/wiki/Electoral_ward" class="mw-redirect" title="Electoral ward">ward</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pudsey_(ward)" title="Pudsey (ward)">Pudsey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pudsey_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Pudsey (UK Parliament constituency)">Pudsey</a> <a href="/wiki/UK_Parliament_constituencies" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Parliament constituencies">parliamentary constituency</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pudsey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The place-name <i>Pudsey</i> is first recorded in 1086 in the <a href="/wiki/Domesday_Book" title="Domesday Book">Domesday Book</a> as <i>Podechesai(e)</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Its etymology is rather uncertain: it seems most likely to derive from a putative personal name *<i>Pudoc</i> and the word <i>ēg</i> meaning 'island' but here presumably referring metaphorically to an 'island' of good ground in moorland. Thus the name would mean 'Pudoc's island'. Other possibilities have been suggested, however.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the early sixth century the district was in the Kingdom of <a href="/wiki/Elmet" title="Elmet">Elmet</a>, which seems to have retained its Celtic character for perhaps as many as two centuries after other neighbouring kingdoms had adopted the cultural identity of the <a href="/wiki/Angles" title="Angles">Angles</a>.
</p><p>Around 1775 a cache of a 100 silver Roman coins, many predating the time of <a href="/wiki/Julius_Caesar" title="Julius Caesar">Julius Caesar</a>, was found on Pudsey Common at a place known as "King Alfred's Camp" by Benjamin Scholfield of Pudsey.
</p><p>The town was famous in the 18th and 19th centuries for <a href="/wiki/Wool" title="Wool">wool</a> manufacture, and, from the 19th century, for <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a>. Yorkshire and England cricketers <a href="/wiki/Len_Hutton" title="Len Hutton">Sir Len Hutton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Sutcliffe" title="Herbert Sutcliffe">Herbert Sutcliffe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ray_Illingworth" title="Ray Illingworth">Ray Illingworth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Hoggard" title="Matthew Hoggard">Matthew Hoggard</a> all learned to play in Pudsey. A 19th century Yorkshire cricketer, <a href="/wiki/John_Tunnicliffe" title="John Tunnicliffe">John Tunnicliffe</a>, was born in Lowtown.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>, Pudsey was one of the most polluted areas of the UK due to its position in a slight valley between the two industrial cities of <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a>. As a result, whichever way the wind blew Pudsey became covered in thick soot. The <a href="/wiki/Temperature_inversion" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperature inversion">temperature inversion</a> created by the valley led to the soot becoming trapped leading to dense smogs. This is believed to have led to jokes that pigeons in <a href="/wiki/Pudsey_Park" title="Pudsey Park">Pudsey Park</a> flew backwards in order to keep the soot out of their eyes.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pudsey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Formerly within the <a href="/wiki/Wapentake" class="mw-redirect" title="Wapentake">wapentake</a> of <a href="/wiki/Morley,_West_Yorkshire" title="Morley, West Yorkshire">Morley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Calverley" title="Calverley">Calverley</a> Parish, <a href="/wiki/Pudsey_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Pudsey Urban District">Pudsey Urban District</a> was formed in 1894; it gained <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">municipal borough</a> status c1900.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> For many years, despite being joined to the Leeds conurbation, it avoided being made part of the <a href="/wiki/County_Borough_of_Leeds" title="County Borough of Leeds">County Borough of Leeds</a>. In 1937 the <a href="/wiki/Farsley_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Farsley Urban District">Farsley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Calverley_Urban_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Calverley Urban District">Calverley</a> <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban districts</a> were added to Pudsey. In 1974, under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_Act_1972" title="Local Government Act 1972">Local Government Act 1972</a>, it became part of the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough</a> of the <a href="/wiki/City_of_Leeds" title="City of Leeds">City of Leeds</a>. It sits in the <a href="/wiki/Leeds_City_Council" title="Leeds City Council">Leeds City Council</a> <a href="/wiki/Electoral_ward" class="mw-redirect" title="Electoral ward">ward</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pudsey_(ward)" title="Pudsey (ward)">Pudsey</a>.
</p><p>Pudsey forms part of the <a href="/wiki/Pudsey_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Pudsey (UK Parliament constituency)">Pudsey parliamentary constituency</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Farsley" title="Farsley">Farsley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Calverley" title="Calverley">Calverley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Horsforth" title="Horsforth">Horsforth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guiseley" title="Guiseley">Guiseley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  The <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">MP</a>  is <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Andrew" title="Stuart Andrew">Stuart Andrew</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Present">Present</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pudsey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Present">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_World%27s_End,_Pudsey.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/The_World%27s_End%2C_Pudsey.jpg/220px-The_World%27s_End%2C_Pudsey.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/The_World%27s_End%2C_Pudsey.jpg/330px-The_World%27s_End%2C_Pudsey.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/The_World%27s_End%2C_Pudsey.jpg/440px-The_World%27s_End%2C_Pudsey.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_World%27s_End,_Pudsey.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The World's End public house</div></div></div>
<p>There are recreational parks in Pudsey, the largest of which is <a href="/wiki/Pudsey_Park" title="Pudsey Park">Pudsey Park</a>. Queens Park is where the Pudsey carnival is held once a year.
</p><p>Pudsey's market operates on Tuesday, Friday and Saturday<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and has recently been refurbished.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Pudsey has a monthly <a href="/wiki/Farmers%27_market" title="Farmers&#39; market">farmers' market</a> with stalls selling meat, fish, dairy produce, organic fruit and vegetables, delicatessen and craft-ware.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pudsey_Parish_Church_04_2_September_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Pudsey_Parish_Church_04_2_September_2017.jpg/200px-Pudsey_Parish_Church_04_2_September_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="267" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Pudsey_Parish_Church_04_2_September_2017.jpg/300px-Pudsey_Parish_Church_04_2_September_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Pudsey_Parish_Church_04_2_September_2017.jpg/400px-Pudsey_Parish_Church_04_2_September_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2304" data-file-height="3072" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pudsey_Parish_Church_04_2_September_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pudsey Parish Church</div></div></div>
<p>Pudsey town centre has amenities including high street chain stores and independent retailers, and there are several branches of banks and estate agents. Following the closure of <a href="/wiki/Kwik_Save" title="Kwik Save">Kwik Save</a> <a href="/wiki/Supermarkets" class="mw-redirect" title="Supermarkets">supermarkets</a> across the country, Pudsey's store was bought by <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury&#39;s">Sainsbury's</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  Until the administration of the group, Pudsey had a <a href="/wiki/Woolworths_Group" title="Woolworths Group">Woolworths</a> on Church Lane.  It is now a <a href="/wiki/B_%26_M" title="B &amp; M">B&amp;M Home Bargains</a> store.
</p><p>There are three secondary schools situated within or near Pudsey: <a href="/wiki/Crawshaw_Academy" title="Crawshaw Academy">Crawshaw Academy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Priesthorpe_School" title="Priesthorpe School">Priesthorpe School</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pudsey_Grangefield_School" title="Pudsey Grangefield School">Pudsey Grangefield School</a>. The latter has been rebuilt under an extensive redevelopment programme adjacent to the current site. The old grammar school building facing Richardshaw Lane, which opened in 1911<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and a prominent landmark of Pudsey, will unlike the rest of the old school buildings not be demolished due to its <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a> status, but has since been converted into flats.
</p><p>Pudsey's historic town hall benefits from a new, energy-efficient lighting project to highlight its most interesting features. The multi-coloured lights can be changed to offer 255 different scenes.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the Easter weekend 2009, the Pudsey Business Forum launched the Pudsey Shop Local campaign. The campaign is to encourage local residents to shop more in Pudsey Town Centre. As part of this campaign they have launched a directory of all local shops.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
<i>Pudsey in Bloom</i> was established in 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pudsey_Park_02_2_September_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Pudsey_Park_02_2_September_2017.jpg/220px-Pudsey_Park_02_2_September_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Pudsey_Park_02_2_September_2017.jpg/330px-Pudsey_Park_02_2_September_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Pudsey_Park_02_2_September_2017.jpg/440px-Pudsey_Park_02_2_September_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pudsey_Park_02_2_September_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pudsey Park - opened in October 1889</div></div></div>
<p>Pudsey is home to an established Indian <a href="/wiki/Sikh" class="mw-redirect" title="Sikh">Sikh</a> and minority <a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> community.
</p><p>Pudsey's business community introduced a Loyalty Scheme, aimed at local shoppers. The loyalty scheme, encourages shoppers to collect stamps from local shops within the scheme. Once enough stamps are collected on a card it can be submitted for a monthly draw, where the winner receives vouchers that can be spent in any of the participating shops. 50 retailers took part in the scheme.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There are several pubs and clubs in and around the town centre including the Butcher's Arms, The Manor Inn, the Mason's Arms, the Shamrock Inn (now closed), the Crossed Shuttle, the World's End pub, the Britannia pub and (outside the town centre) the Troydale Recreational Club.
</p><p>Pudsey has a Masonic community, there are Lodges which meet at Pudsey Masonic Hall on Church Street. Locally these lodges support activities within the local community.
</p><p>Next to the Masonic Hall on Church Street is the former <a href="/wiki/Unitarianism" title="Unitarianism">Unitarian</a> Church, now a private residence known as Churchfield House. <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Public_transport">Public transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pudsey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Public transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Pudsey_Stn_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/New_Pudsey_Stn_2.jpg/220px-New_Pudsey_Stn_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/New_Pudsey_Stn_2.jpg/330px-New_Pudsey_Stn_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/New_Pudsey_Stn_2.jpg/440px-New_Pudsey_Stn_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2304" data-file-height="1728" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Pudsey_Stn_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>New Pudsey railway station</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/New_Pudsey_railway_station" title="New Pudsey railway station">New Pudsey railway station</a> is on the <a href="/wiki/Caldervale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Caldervale Line">Caldervale Line</a> between Leeds and <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Interchange" title="Bradford Interchange">Bradford Interchange</a>. It was built as a "parkway" station, and is situated over a mile away from the town centre. The frequent no. 16 bus service between Pudsey and Farsley (continuing to Leeds) passes close to the station.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The location of the station near to the junction of the main Leeds to Bradford road with the ring road provides easy access for those travelling to the station by car. There is a large car park adjacent to the station.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pudsey_Bus_Station_2_September_2017.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Pudsey_Bus_Station_2_September_2017.jpg/250px-Pudsey_Bus_Station_2_September_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Pudsey_Bus_Station_2_September_2017.jpg/375px-Pudsey_Bus_Station_2_September_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Pudsey_Bus_Station_2_September_2017.jpg/500px-Pudsey_Bus_Station_2_September_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2304" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pudsey_Bus_Station_2_September_2017.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pudsey bus station</div></div></div><p>Pudsey is now served by a number of <a href="/wiki/First_Leeds" title="First Leeds">First</a>, <a href="/wiki/Centrebus" title="Centrebus">Centrebus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arriva_Yorkshire" title="Arriva Yorkshire">Arriva</a> bus services that run through to <a href="/wiki/Leeds_city_centre" title="Leeds city centre">Leeds city centre</a> and other areas of the city, Bradford and other surrounding towns. The 4 bus service runs regularly to the Leeds city centre from Highfield Green via the town centre and <a href="/wiki/Swinnow" title="Swinnow">Swinnow</a>. There is also a No.72 bus service that runs between Leeds and Bradford along <a href="/wiki/Stanningley" title="Stanningley">Stanningley</a> Town Street and Bradford Road, which is 10 minutes walk from Pudsey town centre. The No.508 bus service to <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a> is a further 5 minutes walk up Bradford Road, to near the junction with Old Road.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sport">Sport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pudsey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/English_national_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="English national cricket team">England</a> cricket captain Sir <a href="/wiki/Len_Hutton" title="Len Hutton">Len Hutton</a> was born in nearby <a href="/wiki/Fulneck_Moravian_Settlement" title="Fulneck Moravian Settlement">Fulneck</a> and was called "the man from Pudsey".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Illingworth" class="mw-redirect" title="Raymond Illingworth">Raymond Illingworth</a>, another former England cricket captain, was born in Pudsey as was the England fast bowler <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Hoggard" title="Matthew Hoggard">Matthew Hoggard</a> and the snooker player <a href="/wiki/Danny_Fowler" title="Danny Fowler">Danny Fowler</a>. For over a hundred years the Yorkshire County Cricket Club had at least one player who came from the old Borough of Pudsey. The England opening batsman <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Sutcliffe" title="Herbert Sutcliffe">Herbert Sutcliffe</a> attended Pudsey School and learnt his cricket with the <a href="/wiki/Pudsey_St_Lawrence_Cricket_Club" title="Pudsey St Lawrence Cricket Club">Pudsey St Lawrence</a> and Pudsey Britannia cricket clubs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Pudsey Runners running club was formed in 2013 and meet on Tuesdays and Thursdays at the Bankhouse Inn. The aim of the club is to get non-runners into running and improve their fitness as well as developing the abilities of experienced runners. Pudsey Pacers Running Club was established in 1991 and focuses on road, fell and trail running for all abilities. They also host the annual Pudsey 10K Challenge, a demanding and popular mixed surface race, incorporating on and off road sections.  <sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pudsey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cultural references">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In <i><a href="/wiki/The_Meaning_of_Liff" title="The Meaning of Liff">The Meaning of Liff</a></i> a Pudsey is defined as "The curious-shaped flat wads of dough left on a kitchen table after someone has been cutting scones out of it."<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
Toward the beginning of the Monty Python episode "<a href="/wiki/You%27re_No_Fun_Anymore" class="mw-redirect" title="You&#39;re No Fun Anymore">You're No Fun Anymore</a>", the two characters, Mr and Mrs Samuel Brainsample can be seen walking along the platform of <a href="/wiki/New_Pudsey_railway_station#New_Pudsey_Station_on_television" title="New Pudsey railway station">New Pudsey railway station</a>. Pudsey is also mentioned frequently in the <a href="/wiki/Michael_Palin" title="Michael Palin">Michael Palin</a> TV series Ripping Yarns.
</p><p>Pudsey has given its name to "<a href="/wiki/Children_in_Need#Mascot" title="Children in Need">Pudsey Bear</a>", the mascot of the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>'s annual fundraising marathon <i><a href="/wiki/Children_in_Need" title="Children in Need">Children in Need</a></i>, as this was where Pudsey logo designer Joanna Lane's grandfather was mayor.<sup id="cite_ref-Potts_BBC_News_14_November_2014_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Potts_BBC_News_14_November_2014-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
