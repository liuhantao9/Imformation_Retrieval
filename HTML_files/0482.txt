	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Cricket in the Netherlands</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p><a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">Cricket</a> has been played in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> since at least the 19th century, and in the 1860s was considered a major sport in the country. The sport is governed by the <a href="/wiki/Koninklijke_Nederlandse_Cricket_Bond" title="Koninklijke Nederlandse Cricket Bond">Koninklijke Nederlandse Cricket Bond </a> (KNCB).
</p><p>Other sports (notably <a href="/wiki/Football_(soccer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Football (soccer)">football</a>) have long since surpassed cricket in popularity amongst the Dutch but today there are around 6,500 cricketers in the Netherlands and recent developments show that <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">cricket</a> is growing in Netherlands. The first national association, the forerunner of today's KNCB, was formed in 1883, and the Netherlands achieved <a href="/wiki/Associate_membership_of_the_ICC" class="mw-redirect" title="Associate membership of the ICC">associate membership of the ICC</a> in 1966.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_cricket_team" title="Netherlands national cricket team">Dutch national side</a> has qualified for the <a href="/wiki/Cricket_World_Cup" title="Cricket World Cup">World Cup</a> on four occasions (1996, 2003, 2007, and 2011) and the <a href="/wiki/ICC_World_Twenty20" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC World Twenty20">ICC World Twenty20</a> on three occasions (2009, 2014 and 2016). The team previously had <a href="/wiki/One_Day_International" title="One Day International">One Day International</a> (ODI) status, but lost its status at the <a href="/wiki/2014_Cricket_World_Cup_Qualifier" title="2014 Cricket World Cup Qualifier">2014 World Cup Qualifier</a>, at which at failed to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2015 Cricket World Cup">2015 World Cup</a>.
</p><p>The popularity of <b>cricket in the Netherlands</b> has also influenced the sport's development in both former Dutch colonies and current members of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>. <a href="/wiki/Cricket_in_Indonesia" title="Cricket in Indonesia">Cricket in Indonesia</a> was introduced by the Dutch, <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten_national_cricket_team" title="Sint Maarten national cricket team">Sint Maarten</a> is a member of the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_Cricket_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="West Indies Cricket Board">West Indies Cricket Board</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_cricket_team" title="Suriname national cricket team">Suriname</a> is an associate member of the ICC.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The popularity of cricket in the Netherlands has also influenced the sport's development in former Dutch colonies, including current members of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Netherlands" title="Kingdom of the Netherlands">Kingdom of the Netherlands</a>. <a href="/wiki/Suriname_national_cricket_team" title="Suriname national cricket team">Suriname</a> is an associate member of the ICC, and <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">Sint Maarten</a> is a member of the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_Cricket_Board" class="mw-redirect" title="West Indies Cricket Board">West Indies Cricket Board</a>, which has full membership.
</p><p>The Dutch team has taken part in all eight <a href="/wiki/ICC_Trophy" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC Trophy">ICC Trophy</a> tournaments, winning the competition in Canada in 2001 and finishing as runners-up twice (in 1986 and 1990). The Netherlands also participated in the 1996, 2003, 2007 and 2011 <a href="/wiki/Cricket_World_Cup" title="Cricket World Cup">Cricket World Cups</a>, and from 1996 onwards the national team entered the English domestic <a href="/wiki/NatWest_Trophy" class="mw-redirect" title="NatWest Trophy">NatWest Trophy</a> competition (and its successor, the <a href="/wiki/C%26G_Trophy" class="mw-redirect" title="C&amp;G Trophy">C&amp;G Trophy</a>). In 2004 they played <a href="/wiki/First-class_cricket" title="First-class cricket">first-class cricket</a> in the <a href="/wiki/ICC_Intercontinental_Cup" title="ICC Intercontinental Cup">ICC Intercontinental Cup</a>, drawing with <a href="/wiki/Scottish_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish cricket team">Scotland</a> in Aberdeen and then going down to an <a href="/wiki/The_result_in_cricket" class="mw-redirect" title="The result in cricket">innings defeat</a> against <a href="/wiki/Irish_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish cricket team">Ireland</a> in <a href="/wiki/Deventer" title="Deventer">Deventer</a>.
</p><p>In 2005 the Dutch team beat <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="United Arab Emirates cricket team">the UAE</a> to finish fifth in the <a href="/wiki/ICC_Trophy" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC Trophy">ICC Trophy</a>, a slightly disappointing result but one which qualified them for the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2007 Cricket World Cup">2007 World Cup</a>; they enjoyed full <a href="/wiki/One_Day_International" title="One Day International">One Day International</a> status from 1 January 2006 until the 2009 ICC Trophy.
</p><p>In 2001 finally saw the Netherlands win the ICC Trophy, beating <a href="/wiki/Namibian_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Namibian cricket team">Namibia</a> in the final in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a>. They thus qualified for the <a href="/wiki/2003_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2003 Cricket World Cup">2003 World Cup</a>. They again failed to progress beyond the first round in the tournament, but recorded their first one-day international win over Namibia during the tournament. <a href="/wiki/Feiko_Kloppenburg" title="Feiko Kloppenburg">Feiko Kloppenburg</a> (with 121) and <a href="/wiki/Klaas-Jan_van_Noortwijk" title="Klaas-Jan van Noortwijk">Klaas-Jan van Noortwijk</a> (134 not out) scored the first two One Day International centuries in the side's history.<sup id="cite_ref-justlanded1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-justlanded1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/2005_ICC_Trophy" title="2005 ICC Trophy">2005 ICC Trophy</a>, the Netherlands finished 5th, qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2007 Cricket World Cup">2007 Cricket World Cup</a>, and gaining one-day International status until the <a href="/wiki/2009_ICC_World_Cup_Qualifier" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 ICC World Cup Qualifier">2009 ICC World Cup Qualifier</a>. Their first one-day international with this new status was scheduled to be against <a href="/wiki/Kenyan_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Kenyan cricket team">Kenya</a> in March 2006; however this match was cancelled due to a Kenyan tour of <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>. Instead their first ODI with this status (and their twelfth overall) came against <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lankan_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Sri Lankan cricket team">Sri Lanka</a>; this was their first ODI at home. However Sri Lanka won the two match series 2–0, with a record ODI score of 443–9.<sup id="cite_ref-justlanded1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-justlanded1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Dutch played their first Intercontinental Cup match of 2006 against <a href="/wiki/Kenyan_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Kenyan cricket team">Kenya</a> in Nairobi in March. The game was drawn, but the Netherlands gained six points for a first innings lead.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In August, the Netherlands competed in Division One of the <a href="/wiki/European_Cricket_Championship" title="European Cricket Championship">European Championship</a>. They beat <a href="/wiki/Danish_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Danish cricket team">Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italian_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Italian cricket team">Italy</a>, but lost to <a href="/wiki/Scottish_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish cricket team">Scotland</a> and their game against <a href="/wiki/Irish_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish cricket team">Ireland</a> was rained off. They finished third in the tournament.
</p><p>In November, the Dutch travelled to South Africa. They first played an Intercontinental Cup match against <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_national_cricket_team" title="Bermuda national cricket team">Bermuda</a>:   <a href="/wiki/David_Hemp" title="David Hemp">David Hemp</a> achieved what was then a competition record score of 247 <a href="/wiki/Not_out" title="Not out">not out</a> in the drawn match.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This was followed by a triangular series against Bermuda and <a href="/wiki/Canada_national_cricket_team" title="Canada national cricket team">Canada</a>, which they won.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Their final game of 2006, also in South Africa, was an Intercontinental Cup game against Canada. They won the match by 7 wickets, with <a href="/wiki/Ryan_ten_Doeschate" title="Ryan ten Doeschate">Ryan ten Doeschate</a> setting a new competition record individual score of 259 not out.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2007, they travelled to <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a>, Kenya to take part in <a href="/wiki/2007_ICC_World_Cricket_League_Division_One" title="2007 ICC World Cricket League Division One">Division One</a> of the <a href="/wiki/World_Cricket_League" title="World Cricket League">World Cricket League</a>, finishing third out of six.<sup id="cite_ref-2007WCL1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2007WCL1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This was followed by the <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2007 Cricket World Cup">2007 World Cup</a> in the West Indies, where they were eliminated in the first round, though they did beat <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_cricket_team" title="Scotland national cricket team">Scotland</a> along the way.<sup id="cite_ref-2007WC_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2007WC-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the World Cup, they underwent a period of transformation. Captain <a href="/wiki/Luuk_van_Troost" title="Luuk van Troost">Luuk van Troost</a> retired, as did <a href="/wiki/Tim_de_Leede" title="Tim de Leede">Tim de Leede</a> and their coach Peter Cantrell. <a href="/wiki/Daan_van_Bunge" title="Daan van Bunge">Daan van Bunge</a> also opted to take a break from international cricket, and the new coach opted not to retain the services of bowling coach <a href="/wiki/Ian_Pont" title="Ian Pont">Ian Pont</a>.
</p><p>In June 2007, they visited Canada, first winning an Intercontinental Cup match against Canada in <a href="/wiki/King_City,_Ontario" title="King City, Ontario">King City, Ontario</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> They then won the first ODI by 117 runs,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> with the second one being abandoned.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> They then played a quadrangular series in Ireland, losing by ten wickets to the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies_cricket_team" title="West Indies cricket team">West Indies</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and by one run to <a href="/wiki/Ireland_cricket_team" title="Ireland cricket team">Ireland</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> with the game against Scotland being abandoned due to rain.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2008, The Netherlands participated in the <a href="/wiki/2009_ICC_World_Twenty20_Qualifier" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 ICC World Twenty20 Qualifier">2009 ICC World Twenty20 Qualifier</a>. This was their debut playing <a href="/wiki/Twenty20_International" title="Twenty20 International">Twenty20 International</a> matches.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> They finished in first place in Group B, based on their run-rate.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> After beating Scotland in the Semi-Finals,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> the final was abandoned due to rain and the trophy was shared between The Netherlands and Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Netherlands caused a sensation in the cricketing world by beating England<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> in the opening match of the <a href="/wiki/ICC_World_Twenty20_2009" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC World Twenty20 2009">ICC World Twenty20 2009</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> whilst being 500/1 outsiders.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> They lost their second match to the eventual winners Pakistan<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and did not qualify for the super 8 stage based on run rate.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Netherlands_cricket_team.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Netherlands_cricket_team.jpg/220px-Netherlands_cricket_team.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="140" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Netherlands_cricket_team.jpg/330px-Netherlands_cricket_team.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Netherlands_cricket_team.jpg/440px-Netherlands_cricket_team.jpg 2x" data-file-width="668" data-file-height="425" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Netherlands_cricket_team.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Netherlands national cricket team at <a href="/wiki/Rotterdam" title="Rotterdam">Rotterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/2010_ICC_World_Cricket_League_Division_One" title="2010 ICC World Cricket League Division One">ICC WCL Division One in 2010</a></div></div></div>
<p>In July 2010, The Netherlands beat a full-member nation for the first time in an ODI. In a one-off match shortened by rain to 30 overs a side, they beat Bangladesh by 6 wickets.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The win in combination with their winning percentage against other associate and affiliate nations resulted in The Netherlands being included in ICC's official ODI-rankings.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2011, The Netherlands posted their highest ever total against a full-member nation, scoring 292 against England, batting first at the <a href="/wiki/2011_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2011 Cricket World Cup">2011 Cricket World Cup</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Ten_Doeschate" class="mw-redirect" title="Ryan Ten Doeschate">Ryan Ten Doeschate</a> top scored 119 from 110 balls. However, the Netherlands were unable to defend their strong total and failed to pull off a huge shock, England winning by 6 wickets with 2 overs to spare.They eventually failed to win any of their group matches and were last in their Group.
</p><p>In September 2011, Netherlands whitewashed <a href="/wiki/Kenya_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Kenya cricket team">Kenya</a> in a short two-match ODI series held at home.<sup id="cite_ref-blogspot1_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blogspot1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2014, <a href="/wiki/2014_Cricket_World_Cup_Qualifier" title="2014 Cricket World Cup Qualifier">World Cup Qualifier</a> saw Netherlands failed to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2015 Cricket World Cup">World Cup</a> and lose their <a href="/wiki/One_Day_International" title="One Day International">ODI</a> status till 2018 although the Netherlands did qualify for <a href="/wiki/2014_ICC_World_Twenty20" title="2014 ICC World Twenty20">2014 ICC World Twenty20</a> instead of <a href="/wiki/Scotland_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Scotland cricket team">Scotland</a>. It gained their ODI status back after winning the <a href="/w/index.php?title=2015-17_ICC_World_Cricket_League_Championship.&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2015-17 ICC World Cricket League Championship. (page does not exist)">2015-17 ICC World Cricket League Championship.</a>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/2014_ICC_World_Twenty20" title="2014 ICC World Twenty20">2014 ICC World Twenty20</a>, Netherlands had one win and one loss before their final game. In order to go through on net run rate against <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Zimbabwe cricket team">Zimbabwe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland_national_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Ireland national cricket team">Ireland</a>, they needed to chase down Ireland's score in 14.2 overs or less. As Ireland scored 189, this seemed unlikely.
</p><p>However, strong and aggressive batting enabled them to score 193/4 in 13.5 overs, securing passage to the <a href="/wiki/2014_ICC_World_Twenty20_Super_10s" title="2014 ICC World Twenty20 Super 10s">2014 ICC World Twenty20 Super 10s</a>. Although they lost their first three games in the group, including a 39/10 against <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka_national_cricket_team" title="Sri Lanka national cricket team">Sri Lanka</a>, they pulled off an upset against <a href="/wiki/England_national_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="England national cricket team">England</a> in their last game.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governing_body">Governing body</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Governing body">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Koninklijke_Nederlandse_Cricket_Bond" title="Koninklijke Nederlandse Cricket Bond">Koninklijke Nederlandse Cricket Bond</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Koninklijke_Nederlandse_Cricket_Bond" title="Koninklijke Nederlandse Cricket Bond">Koninklijke Nederlandse Cricket Bond</a> (Royal Dutch Cricket Board) is the governing body of cricket in the Netherlands. It was formed in 1883 and received a Royal charter in 1958. The Netherlands achieved Associate Membership of the <a href="/wiki/International_Cricket_Council" title="International Cricket Council">ICC</a> in 1966.
</p><p>The KNCB has been an associate member of the <a href="/wiki/International_Cricket_Council" title="International Cricket Council">International Cricket Council</a> since 1966.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> There are a few cricket grounds in the Netherlands which are officially sanctioned by the ICC to host ODIs such as <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amstelveen" title="Amstelveen">Amstelveen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Voorburg" title="Voorburg">Voorburg</a>. It hosted some of the matches of the <a href="/wiki/1999_Cricket_World_Cup" title="1999 Cricket World Cup">1999 Cricket World Cup</a>, though the Dutch did not participate in that tournament.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Dutch participated in the <a href="/wiki/1996_Cricket_World_Cup" title="1996 Cricket World Cup">1996 Cricket World Cup</a>, <a href="/wiki/2003_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2003 Cricket World Cup">2003 Cricket World Cup</a>, <a href="/wiki/2007_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2007 Cricket World Cup">2007 Cricket World Cup</a>, and <a href="/wiki/2011_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2011 Cricket World Cup">2011 Cricket World Cup</a>. In their debut campaign, they lost all of their matches barring a respectful performance against England. They qualified for the 2003 edition after winning the <a href="/wiki/2001_ICC_Trophy" title="2001 ICC Trophy">2001 ICC Trophy</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> with their only win of the World Cup tournament coming against fellow qualifier Namibia. It was around this time that stars and excellent cricketers like <a href="/wiki/Roland_Lefebvre" title="Roland Lefebvre">Roland Lefebvre</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ryan_ten_Doeschate" title="Ryan ten Doeschate">Ryan ten Doeschate</a> started emerging to make Dutch cricket much more strong.
</p><p>Before 1958 it was known as the Nederlandse Cricket Bond or the Dutch Cricket Board. After receiving a Royal charter in 1958, a "Royal" was added before the board's name known simply as <a href="/wiki/Koninklijk" title="Koninklijk">Koninklijke</a> in Dutch. The following names have been for the board in their history:-
</p>
<ul><li>Nederlandse Cricket Bond (Dutch Cricket Board)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Koninklijk" title="Koninklijk">Koninklijke</a> Nederlandse Cricket Bond (Royal Dutch Cricket Board)</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domestic_competitions">Domestic competitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Domestic competitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main cricket division is called <i><a href="/wiki/Topklasse_(cricket)" title="Topklasse (cricket)">Topklasse</a></i>, in which eight teams compete for the national championships.<br />
Other important competitions are: <i><a href="/wiki/Hoofdklasse_(cricket)" title="Hoofdklasse (cricket)">Hoofdklasse</a></i>, Eerste Klasse, and the <i><a href="/wiki/Dutch_Twenty20_Cup" title="Dutch Twenty20 Cup">Twenty20 Cup</a></i>.
</p>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Topklasse_(cricket)" title="Topklasse (cricket)">Topklasse</a></i>,</li></ul>
<p>Topklasse known before as <b>Hoofdklasse</b> is the highest domestic cricket competition in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<br />
Eight teams are playing in the regular competition; the finalists being decided during a play-off stage.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="National_team">National team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: National team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_cricket_team" title="Netherlands national cricket team">Netherlands national cricket team</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_cricket_team" title="Netherlands national cricket team">Netherlands national cricket team</a> qualified for the <a href="/wiki/Cricket_World_Cup" title="Cricket World Cup">Cricket World Cup</a> on 4 occasions; 1996, 2003, 2007 and <a href="/wiki/2011_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2011 Cricket World Cup">2011</a>, Also qualified for <a href="/wiki/2009_ICC_World_Twenty20" title="2009 ICC World Twenty20">2009 ICC World Twenty20</a>. The country has been considered one of the stronger "minnow" nations for some time. Netherlands beat <a href="/wiki/England_cricket_team" title="England cricket team">England</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2009_ICC_World_Twenty20" title="2009 ICC World Twenty20">2009 ICC World Twenty20</a> which is one of the biggest wins in Dutch cricket history.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> One year later they recorded their first ODI win against a full ICC member when they beat Bangladesh.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Although the sport is sustained by a small player base, some have won professional contracts in England. Netherlands is ranked within the top 15 teams in the world and has co-hosted the <a href="/wiki/ICC_Cricket_World_Cup" class="mw-redirect" title="ICC Cricket World Cup">ICC Cricket World Cup</a> in 1999.
</p><p>In February 2011, The Netherlands posted their highest ever total against a full-member nation, scoring 292 against England, batting first at the <a href="/wiki/2011_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2011 Cricket World Cup">2011 Cricket World Cup</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Ten_Doeschate" class="mw-redirect" title="Ryan Ten Doeschate">Ryan Ten Doeschate</a> top scored 119 from 110 balls. However, the Netherlands were unable to defend their strong total and failed to pull off a huge shock, England winning by 6 wickets with 2 overs to spare.They eventually failed to win any of their group matches and were last in their Group.
</p><p>In September 2011, Netherlands whitewashed <a href="/wiki/Kenya_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Kenya cricket team">Kenya</a> in a short two-match ODI series held at home.<sup id="cite_ref-blogspot1_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blogspot1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2014, <a href="/wiki/2014_Cricket_World_Cup_Qualifier" title="2014 Cricket World Cup Qualifier">World Cup Qualifier</a> saw Netherlands failed to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Cricket_World_Cup" title="2015 Cricket World Cup">World Cup</a> and lose their <a href="/wiki/One_Day_International" title="One Day International">ODI</a> status till 2018 although the Netherlands did qualify for <a href="/wiki/2014_ICC_World_Twenty20" title="2014 ICC World Twenty20">2014 ICC World Twenty20</a> instead of <a href="/wiki/Scotland_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Scotland cricket team">Scotland</a>.
</p>
<h2><span id="National_women.27s_team"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="National_women's_team">National women's team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: National women&#039;s team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_women%27s_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Netherlands national women&#39;s cricket team">Netherlands national women's cricket team</a></div>
<p>The Dutch women's cricket team represents the Netherlands in international women's cricket matches. Unlike their male counterparts, they currently have both Test and ODI status.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Under-19_cricket_team">Under-19 cricket team</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cricket_in_the_Netherlands&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Under-19 cricket team">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_Under-19_cricket_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Netherlands Under-19 cricket team">Netherlands Under-19 cricket team</a></div>
<p>Netherlands Under-19 cricket team represents the Netherlands in Under-19 international cricket.
</p><p>The team has been playing in international youth tournaments since 1979 but have only participated in one U-19 World Cup in 2000. They most recently participated in 2010 European U-19 Championship where they finished fourth, thus missing out on a chance for World Cup qualification.
</p>
