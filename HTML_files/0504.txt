<p><b>K-1</b> began in 1993 and is a <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxing</a> organisation and martial arts brand well-known worldwide mainly for its heavyweight division fights.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In January 2012, K-1 Global Holdings Limited, a company registered in Hong Kong, acquired the rights to K-1, and is the current organizer of K-1 events worldwide.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The letter <a href="/wiki/K" title="K">K</a> in K-1 is officially designated as a representation of words <a href="/wiki/Karate" title="Karate">karate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kickboxing" title="Kickboxing">kickboxing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kung_fu" class="mw-redirect" title="Kung fu">kung fu</a>. Nevertheless, some reports suggest that it represents the initial K found in competing disciplines such as karate, kickboxing, kung fu, <a href="/wiki/Kempo" class="mw-redirect" title="Kempo">kempo</a>, <i>kakutougi</i> (the generic Japanese term for "combat sports"), and <a href="/wiki/Taekwondo" title="Taekwondo">[tae] kwon do</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TashiroTyrangiel2001_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TashiroTyrangiel2001-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Yet another theory claims that the K simply comes from <i>kakutougi</i> and the "1" component pertains to the single weight division (in earlier competition) and the champion's unique position.<sup id="cite_ref-Maylam2001_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maylam2001-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Nevertheless, the promotion held several tournaments under K-2 and K-3 banners from 1993 to 1995.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_K-1" title="History of K-1">History of K-1</a></div>
<p>K-1's predecessor <a href="/wiki/Seidokaikan" title="Seidokaikan">Seidokaikan</a> Karate was formed in 1980 by <a href="/wiki/Kazuyoshi_Ishii" title="Kazuyoshi Ishii">Kazuyoshi Ishii</a>, a former Kyokushin karate practitioner who had formed his own organization to help promote the best stand-up martial artists.<sup id="cite_ref-fightmagazine_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fightmagazine-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Seidokaikan arranged several successful challenge events against other martial arts organizations, originally using rules based on the Kyokushin Knockdown karate rules, but gradually adapting and changing closer to kickboxing rules. In 1993, Mr. Ishii founded the K-1 organization exclusively as a kickboxing organization, closely cooperating with, but independent from Seidokaikan.<sup id="cite_ref-Maylam2001_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maylam2001-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_problems">Financial problems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Financial problems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Starting in 2010, multiple stories began to surface regarding the financial troubles of K-1 and their parent company FEG.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Simon Rutz, the owner of the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a>-based kickboxing promotion <a href="/wiki/It%27s_Showtime_(kickboxing)" title="It&#39;s Showtime (kickboxing)">It's Showtime</a>, claimed in January 2011 that some fighters from It's Showtime had not been paid for fights in K-1.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2011, <a href="/wiki/Fighting_and_Entertainment_Group" title="Fighting and Entertainment Group">FEG</a> publicly announced that they were facing financial problems and that the organization would take some months off to restructure. Many fighters and managers spoke out against FEG due to unpaid fight purses and objections to the direction that its owner was taking K-1. It appeared that financial problems were severe, and that FEG could potentially lose ownership of K-1.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The entire K-1 brand, along with most of its trademarks, with the exception of, 'K-1 Koshien', 'K-1 MAX' and '<a href="/wiki/Dream_(mixed_martial_arts)" title="Dream (mixed martial arts)">Dream</a>', were sold to  Japanese real estate firm, Barbizon Corporation Limited, on July 28, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 1, 2012 EMCOM Entertainment Inc. purchased K-1 from Barbizon.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="K-1_Global">K-1 Global</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: K-1 Global">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In March 2012, It's Showtime announced that EMCOM Entertainment established a new company K-1 Global Holdings Ltd. in <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>. K-1 Global Holdings, Ltd., became the new official owner of the K-1 brand. K-1 Global's agreement with promotion required that certain fighters signed under It's Showtime appear in upcoming K-1 Global events.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A number of events were already planned and scheduled for the 2012 calendar before new K-1 ownership took complete control of the company. It's Showtime was the promoter of the first event presented by K-1 Global Holdings Ltd., the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_MAX_2012_World_Championship_Tournament_Final_16#K-1_World_MAX_2012_World_Championship_Tournament_Final_16" class="mw-redirect" title="K-1 World MAX 2012 World Championship Tournament Final 16">K-1 World MAX Final 16</a>. It took place on May 27, 2012 at the Palacio Vistalegre in Madrid, Spain.
</p><p>After the event, controversy and rumors circulated over the fact that once again, fighters were not paid for their performances. Problems were compounded due to unpaid financial obligations of FEG, and the complete transfer of ownership of the K-1 name was not yet complete. As a result, the K-1 World Grand Prix scheduled for 2012 would be cancelled if these issues were not resolved.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ultimately, K-1 Global Holdings, Ltd. was blamed for the failure of an event they did not promote. They were also accused of not paying fighters when in fact, the promoter and FEG shared responsibilities in ensuring fighters were paid. The Chairman of the new K-1 resolved the matter by paying fighters what was owed and including a 50% bonus.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was announced in June 2012 that It's Showtime was purchased by Glory Sports International, eventually to be merged in their new promotion <a href="/wiki/Glory_(kickboxing)" title="Glory (kickboxing)">GLORY</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 10, 2012 K-1's co-promotion agreement with <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romanian</a>-based kickboxing promotion SUPERKOMBAT was announced.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Eventually it was ended. Early 2013 SK president <a href="/wiki/Eduard_Irimia" title="Eduard Irimia">Eduard Irimia</a> stated that its company will be independent in 2013 but will still continue to have cooperation with any interested promotion including K-1 by offering fighters.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="K-1_Grand_Prix">K-1 Grand Prix</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: K-1 Grand Prix">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix" title="K-1 World Grand Prix">K-1 World Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>Original K-1 Grand Prix was a single event tournament held in Japan where competitors participated on invitation. By 1998, K-1 introduced the <a href="/wiki/K-1_World_Grand_Prix" title="K-1 World Grand Prix">K-1 World Grand Prix</a> format composed of  K-1 Regional Elimination Tournaments (theoretically amounting to six), which qualify fighters for the K-1 World Grand Prix Final, along with licensed K-1 Fighting Network events designed to hold national preliminaries for regional qualification. However, given the fact that K-1's popularity differs greatly among six K-1 regions, which may limit the number of actual elimination tournaments or change locations. For example, K-1 attempted to gain popularity in the United States by holding two GPs, however only a few Americans have ever qualified for the Finals. In 2006 one of the American GPs was relocated to <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a>. Additionally the K-1 <a href="/wiki/Paris,_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Paris, France">Paris</a> GP lost its qualifying right in favor of <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam,_Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Amsterdam, Netherlands">Amsterdam</a>. Eventually Amsterdam lost it in favor of <a href="/wiki/%C5%81%C3%B3d%C5%BA,_Poland" class="mw-redirect" title="Łódź, Poland">Łódź</a>, and then Łódź in favor of <a href="/wiki/Bucharest,_Romania" class="mw-redirect" title="Bucharest, Romania">Bucharest</a>.
</p><p>K-1 World Grand Prix Final Eliminator ("Final 16") is an event where 16 participants compete for the final eight spots in the Final ("Final 8"). Eight participants from the Final Eliminator meet at the K-1 World Grand Prix Final that was traditionally held at <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Dome" title="Tokyo Dome">Tokyo Dome</a>. Lesser elements in the tournament format have been significantly modified in years. The 2012 final took place for the first time in history outside Japan, in <a href="/wiki/Zagreb,_Croatia" class="mw-redirect" title="Zagreb, Croatia">Zagreb, Croatia</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="K-1_World_MAX_and_other">K-1 World MAX and other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: K-1 World MAX and other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By 2002, K-1 started the K-1 World MAX ("Middleweight Artistic Xtreme") tournament for 70&#160;kg (154&#160;lb) Middleweight division, following a similar scheme to K-1 World Grand Prix (with theoretically four regional eliminators). In 2007, K-1 introduced two new title belts separate from K-1 World GP Champions, Super Heavyweight World Title for fighters over 100&#160;kg/220&#160;lbs and Heavyweight World Title for fighters under 100&#160;kg/156–220&#160;lbs.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rules">Rules</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Rules">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Unreferenced_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=K-1&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing_with_Wiki_Markup/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing with Wiki Markup/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Burden_of_evidence" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">removed</a>.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">December 2012</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<ul><li>Each match is three or five rounds in duration, with each round lasting three minutes.</li>
<li>The match can end by Knockout, Technical Knockout, Decision, Disqualification, Draw or No Contest.</li>
<li>Both the referee and the ring doctor have full authority to stop the fight.</li>
<li>The fight is scored by three judges on a ten-point must system (The winner of each round receives ten points, and the loser receives nine or less. If the round is even, both competitors receive ten points).</li>
<li>If there is a draw after three rounds, the judges' scores are thrown out and one or two extra three-minute rounds are contested. The judges' decision will then come from the scoring of each extra round only. If, after the extra round(s), there is still a draw, the judges will decide a winner based on the flow of the entire match, considering even the slightest difference. A fight can only end in a draw if both fighters go down at the same time and cannot get up, or in the case of accidental injury in the late stages of the contest.</li>
<li>Three knockdowns in a round ends the contest, declared a <a href="/wiki/Technical_knockout" class="mw-redirect" title="Technical knockout">technical knockout</a>.</li>
<li>The mandatory eight count is in effect (regardless when the fallen fighter rises, the referee must count to at least "eight" on all knockdowns).</li>
<li>The standing eight count is in effect (the referee has the right to declare a knockdown on a fighter who appears to be in a dangerous condition to continue in the match).</li>
<li>A fighter can be saved by the bell only in the last round.</li></ul>
<p>In K-1 single elimination tournament matches:
</p>
<ul><li>Each match is three rounds in duration.</li>
<li>Except in the final, two knockdowns in a round ends the contest, declared a <a href="/wiki/Technical_knockout" class="mw-redirect" title="Technical knockout">technical knockout</a>.</li>
<li>One or two reserve fights are held prior to the single elimination matches. If for any reason a fighter who wins and advances through the brackets is unable to continue, a reserve match competitor, or the fighter's opponent from the most recent match, takes his place. There are certain exceptions to this rule (i.e. a fighter who lost a match by knockout might not be eligible to replace another fighter).</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fouls">Fouls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Fouls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The following actions in K-1 are considered fouls:<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Using the head or elbow to deliver a blow</li>
<li>Attacking the opponent in the groin</li>
<li>Delivering wrestling or judo throwing or submission techniques</li>
<li>Thumbing, choking or biting the opponent</li>
<li>Punching the opponent in the throat</li>
<li>Attacking the opponent while he is down or in the process of getting up</li>
<li>Attacking the opponent after the referee calls a break</li>
<li>Holding the ropes</li>
<li>Using offensive language to the referee</li>
<li>Attacking the back of the head with a punch</li>
<li>Attempting to cause the opponent to fall out of the ring</li>
<li>Voluntarily exiting the ring during the course of a match</li>
<li>Attacking an opponent who turns around and shows his back (if the opponent loses his will to fight)</li>
<li>Delivering a backspin blow in an unauthorized area</li>
<li>Charging inside the opponent's arms with the head held low (inducing a head-butt)</li>
<li>Fighting in a passive manner (without attacking), including continuous holding and clinching</li>
<li>Attacking more than once while holding the opponent's kicking leg, or while holding the opponent's neck with both hands</li></ul>
<p>A fighter is penalized as follows:
</p>
<ul><li>Caution&#160;– verbal reprimand by the referee</li>
<li>Warning&#160;– fighter is shown a yellow card</li>
<li>Point Deduction&#160;– fighter is shown a red card</li></ul>
<p>Two cautions result in one warning. Two warnings result in a point deduction, and three point deductions in one round can result in a disqualification.
</p><p>A red card is shown automatically if a fighter commits a foul with malicious intent.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=K-1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kickboxing is very popular in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> in countries such as the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> but recently enjoys only limited popularity in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.
</p><p>In the past the events were frequently shown on <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Broadcasting_System" title="Tokyo Broadcasting System">Tokyo Broadcasting System</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Fuji Television">Fuji TV</a><sup id="cite_ref-headkicklegend_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-headkicklegend-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> in Japan, XTM in South Korea, Combate and <a href="/wiki/SporTV" title="SporTV">SporTV</a> in Brazil, <a href="/wiki/HDNet_Fights" title="HDNet Fights">HDNet Fights</a><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> in the United States and on <a href="/wiki/Eurosport" title="Eurosport">Eurosport</a> in Europe.
</p>
