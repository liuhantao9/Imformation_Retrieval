<p>The <i><b>Historical Dictionary of Switzerland</b></i> is an <a href="/wiki/Encyclopedia" title="Encyclopedia">encyclopedia</a> on the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Switzerland" title="History of Switzerland">history of Switzerland</a> that aims to take into account the results of modern historical research in a manner accessible to a broader audience.
</p><p>The encyclopedia is published by a <a href="/wiki/Foundation_(charity)" class="mw-redirect" title="Foundation (charity)">foundation</a> under the patronage of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiss_Academy_of_Humanities_and_Social_Sciences&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Swiss Academy of Humanities and Social Sciences (page does not exist)">Swiss Academy of Humanities and Social Sciences</a> (SAGW/ASSH) and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Swiss_Historical_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Swiss Historical Society (page does not exist)">Swiss Historical Society</a> (SGG-SHH) and is financed by national research grants. Besides a staff of 35 at the central offices, the contributors include 100 academic advisors, 2500 historians and 100 translators.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Print_edition">Print edition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historical_Dictionary_of_Switzerland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Print edition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DHS-fr-page.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/DHS-fr-page.jpg/220px-DHS-fr-page.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="309" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/DHS-fr-page.jpg/330px-DHS-fr-page.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/DHS-fr-page.jpg/440px-DHS-fr-page.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1257" data-file-height="1766" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DHS-fr-page.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Example of one page (French version, volume 3, page 506).</div></div></div>
<p>The encyclopedia is being edited simultaneously in three national languages of Switzerland: <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> (<i>Historisches Lexikon der Schweiz</i>, HLS, in red), <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a> (<i>Dictionnaire Historique de la Suisse</i>, DHS, in blue) and <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> (<i>Dizionario Storico della Svizzera</i>, DSS, in yellow). The first of 13 volumes was published in 2002. The last volume was published in 2014.
</p><p>The 36,000 headings are grouped in:
</p>
<ol><li>Biographies (35%)</li>
<li>Articles on families and genealogy (10%)</li>
<li>Articles on places (municipalities, cantons, other nation states, fortresses, signories, abbeys, archeological sites) (30%)</li>
<li>Subject articles (historical phenomena and terms, institutions, events) (25%)</li></ol>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="On-line_edition">On-line edition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historical_Dictionary_of_Switzerland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: On-line edition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The on-line edition has been available since 1998. It makes accessible, for free, all articles ready for publication in print, but no illustrations. It also lists all 36,000 topics that are to be covered.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lexicon_Istoric_Retic"><i>Lexicon Istoric Retic</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historical_Dictionary_of_Switzerland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lexicon Istoric Retic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Lexicon Istoric Retic</i> (LIR) is a two volume version with a selection of articles published in <a href="/wiki/Romansh_language" title="Romansh language">Romansh</a>. It includes articles not available in the other languages. The first volume was published in 2010, the second in 2012. An on-line version is also available.
</p>
