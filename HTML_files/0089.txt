<p>"<b>Cry Pretty</b>" is a song co-written and recorded by American <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> singer <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>. It was released on April 11, 2018, as the lead single from her <a href="/wiki/Cry_Pretty" title="Cry Pretty">sixth studio album of the same name.</a>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Writing_and_composition">Writing and composition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cry_Pretty_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Writing and composition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Cry Pretty" was written by Underwood, <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Lindsey" title="Hillary Lindsey">Hillary Lindsey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liz_Rose" title="Liz Rose">Liz Rose</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lori_McKenna" title="Lori McKenna">Lori McKenna</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Carrie&#39;s_Letter_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carrie&#39;s_Letter-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> About the song, Underwood said:
</p>
<blockquote><p>"The title refers to when emotions take over and you just can't hold them back. It really speaks to a lot of things that have happened in the past year and I hope when you hear it, you can relate those feelings to those times in your life. It's emotional. It's real. And it rocks!"<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cry_Pretty_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Cry Pretty" received positive reviews upon its release. <i>Billboard</i> praised its subject matter, writing, "The four-minute song digs deep into the difficulty of "dressing up" heartache with fancy rhinestones and lace when all you want to do is break down and cry, building to a sky-high crescendo in the final minute, where Underwood unleashes the full, soulful power of her voice."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <i>NPR</i> praised Underwood's delivery, writing that her "performance communicates the same weary rage these classics do, the injustice of being expected to keep it together so that the world is a little better-looking, a version of country's tragic punchlines that could only be sung by a woman."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <i>USA Today</i> praised the song, writing "The track is a promising return for Underwood, featuring an enduring melody that's instantly more memorable than "The Champion", her Super Bowl theme she released in January. For country music's unshakable sweetheart, "Cry Pretty" shows Underwood in full empowerment mode, even if she's not always in control of her emotions."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cry_Pretty_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song debuted at number 20 on the <i>Billboard</i> <a href="/wiki/Country_Airplay" title="Country Airplay">Country Airplay</a> chart for the week ending April 21, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It peaked at number 9 on that chart, becoming Underwood's 27th consecutive top ten single and extending the record for most consecutive top ten singles from the start of a career.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The following week, the song topped the <a href="/wiki/Digital_Songs" title="Digital Songs">Digital Songs</a> chart with 54,000 sold, becoming the first country song to top that chart since 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It also became Underwood's first ever number one on the Digital Songs chart and reached number 5 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs">Hot Country Songs</a> chart, becoming her 27th consecutive top ten single. It also debuted at number 48 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Canada, the song debuted at number 83 on the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hot_100" title="Canadian Hot 100">Canadian Hot 100</a> chart. On the Canadian Country Songs chart, it debuted at number 35 and has reached number 8.
</p><p>"Cry Pretty" debuted at number 94 on the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Singles_Chart" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish Singles Chart">Scottish Singles Chart</a> before moving to number 75 in its second week.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of September 2018, the single has sold 189,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The song was certified Gold by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a> on November 7, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cry_Pretty_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood first posted a short teaser clip for the official music video of the song on May 3, 2018, with a note at the end of the clip saying "official video coming soon". The video premiered during the May 6 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_16)" title="American Idol (season 16)">American Idol</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It was directed by Randee St. Nicholas.
</p><p>The video begins with Underwood in the shower, then shows her walking onstage getting ready to perform the song. This is intercut with scenes of her in her dressing room, bedroom, shower and limousine, where she is seen crying. It was filmed over two days at the <a href="/wiki/Nashville_Municipal_Auditorium" title="Nashville Municipal Auditorium">Nashville Municipal Auditorium</a> and at the Noel Hotel in Nashville. <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_performances">Live performances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cry_Pretty_(song)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Live performances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Underwood performed the single live for the first time at the <a href="/wiki/53rd_Academy_of_Country_Music_Awards" title="53rd Academy of Country Music Awards">53rd Academy of Country Music Awards</a> on April 15, 2018, marking her first public appearance since November 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The performance was very well received and she received a standing ovation from the crowd.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <i>Billboard</i> ranked it as the best performance of the night.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 13, 2018, Underwood performed the song live during the semi-final of <i><a href="/wiki/American_Idol_(season_16)" title="American Idol (season 16)">American Idol</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On May 11, she performed the song during her ten-year <a href="/wiki/Grand_Ole_Opry" title="Grand Ole Opry">Grand Ole Opry</a> anniversary celebration,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and at the 2018 <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Awards" title="CMT Music Awards">CMT Music Awards</a> on June 6, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Underwood's performance of the song was shown during the <i>CMA Music Fest</i> on August 8.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Underwood performed this song at 2018's <i>CMT Artist of the Year</i> in the ending of her performance along with  <a href="/wiki/Tammy_Wynette" title="Tammy Wynette">Tammy Wynette</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Stand_by_Your_Man" title="Stand by Your Man">Stand by Your Man</a>." From there, her 2019 tour mates <a href="/wiki/Maddie_%26_Tae" title="Maddie &amp; Tae">Maddie &amp; Tae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Runaway_June" title="Runaway June">Runaway June</a> joined her on the next seven songs: <a href="/wiki/Dolly_Parton" title="Dolly Parton">Dolly Parton</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/9_to_5_(Dolly_Parton_song)" title="9 to 5 (Dolly Parton song)">9 to 5</a>," <a href="/wiki/The_Judds" title="The Judds">The Judds</a> "<a href="/wiki/Rockin%27_with_the_Rhythm_of_the_Rain" title="Rockin&#39; with the Rhythm of the Rain">Rockin' with the Rhythm of the Rain</a>," <a href="/wiki/Martina_McBride" title="Martina McBride">Martina McBride</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Martina_McBride_song)" title="Independence Day (Martina McBride song)">Independence Day</a>," <a href="/wiki/Faith_Hill" title="Faith Hill">Faith Hill</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Wild_One_(Faith_Hill_song)" title="Wild One (Faith Hill song)">Wild One</a>," <a href="/wiki/Reba_McEntire" title="Reba McEntire">Reba McEntire</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Why_Haven%27t_I_Heard_from_You" title="Why Haven&#39;t I Heard from You">Why Haven't I Heard from You</a>," and <a href="/wiki/Shania_Twain" title="Shania Twain">Shania Twain</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Man!_I_Feel_Like_a_Woman!" title="Man!
