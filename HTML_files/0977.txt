<p><b>Fellowship of the Royal Society</b> (<b>FRS</b>, <b>ForMemRS</b> and <b>HonFRS</b>) is an award granted to individuals that the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a> of London judges to have made a 'substantial contribution to the improvement of <a href="/wiki/Natural_science" title="Natural science">natural knowledge</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Engineering science">engineering science</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Medical_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Medical science">medical science</a>'.<sup id="cite_ref-elections_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elections-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GodfreyKneller-IsaacNewton-1689.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/GodfreyKneller-IsaacNewton-1689.jpg/220px-GodfreyKneller-IsaacNewton-1689.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="302" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/GodfreyKneller-IsaacNewton-1689.jpg/330px-GodfreyKneller-IsaacNewton-1689.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/39/GodfreyKneller-IsaacNewton-1689.jpg 2x" data-file-width="407" data-file-height="559" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GodfreyKneller-IsaacNewton-1689.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Isaac_Newton" title="Isaac Newton">Isaac Newton</a> was one of the earliest Fellows of the Royal Society, elected in 1672</div></div></div>
<p>Fellowship of the Society, the oldest scientific academy in continuous existence, is a significant honour which has been awarded to many eminent scientists from history including <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Newton" title="Isaac Newton">Isaac Newton</a> (1672),<sup id="cite_ref-allfrs_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allfrs-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a> (1839),<sup id="cite_ref-allfrs_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allfrs-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Michael_Faraday" title="Michael Faraday">Michael Faraday</a> (1824),<sup id="cite_ref-allfrs_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allfrs-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Rutherford" title="Ernest Rutherford">Ernest Rutherford</a> (1903),<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Srinivasa_Ramanujan" title="Srinivasa Ramanujan">Srinivasa Ramanujan</a> (1918),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">Albert Einstein</a> (1921),<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> (1941), <a href="/wiki/Subrahmanyan_Chandrasekhar" title="Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar">Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar</a> (1944),<sup id="cite_ref-subra_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-subra-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Dorothy_Hodgkin" title="Dorothy Hodgkin">Dorothy Hodgkin</a> (1947),<sup id="cite_ref-dorothyfrs_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dorothyfrs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alan_Turing" title="Alan Turing">Alan Turing</a> (1951)<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Francis_Crick" title="Francis Crick">Francis Crick</a> (1959).<sup id="cite_ref-crickrsbm_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crickrsbm-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Obituary_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Obituary-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> More recently, fellowship has been awarded to  <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Hawking" title="Stephen Hawking">Stephen Hawking</a>  (1974), <a href="/wiki/Tim_Hunt" title="Tim Hunt">Tim Hunt</a> (1991), <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Blackburn" title="Elizabeth Blackburn">Elizabeth Blackburn</a> (1992),  <a href="/wiki/Tim_Berners-Lee" title="Tim Berners-Lee">Tim Berners-Lee</a> (2001), <a href="/wiki/Venkatraman_Ramakrishnan" title="Venkatraman Ramakrishnan">Venkatraman Ramakrishnan</a> (2003), <a href="/wiki/Atta-ur-Rahman_(chemist)" title="Atta-ur-Rahman (chemist)">Atta-ur Rahman</a> (2006),<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Andre_Geim" title="Andre Geim">Andre Geim</a> (2007),<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/James_Dyson" title="James Dyson">James Dyson</a> (2015), <a href="/wiki/Ajay_Kumar_Sood" class="mw-redirect" title="Ajay Kumar Sood">Ajay Kumar Sood</a> (2015), <a href="/wiki/Subhash_Khot" title="Subhash Khot">Subhash Khot</a> (2017), <a href="/wiki/Elon_Musk" title="Elon Musk">Elon Musk</a> (2018),<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Category:Fellows_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Category:Fellows of the Royal Society">around 8,000 others</a> in total,<sup id="cite_ref-allfrs_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allfrs-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> including over 280 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Nobel_laureates" title="List of Nobel laureates">Nobel Laureates</a> since 1900. As of October&#160;2018<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, there are approximately 1689 living Fellows, Foreign and Honorary Members, of which over 60 are Nobel Laureates.<sup id="cite_ref-alive_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alive-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-window_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-window-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fellowship of the Royal Society has been described by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> newspaper as “the equivalent of a lifetime achievement <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Oscar</a>”<sup id="cite_ref-oscar_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oscar-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> with several institutions celebrating their announcement each year.<sup id="cite_ref-icr_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-icr-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-crickinstitute_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crickinstitute-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-manchester_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-manchester-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-imperial_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-imperial-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-aberdeen_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aberdeen-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cam_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cam-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Main_Fellowships">Main Fellowships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Main Fellowships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Up to 60 new Fellows (FRS), honorary (HonFRS) and foreign members (ForMemRS) are elected  annually in late April or early May, from a pool of around 700 proposed candidates each year.<sup id="cite_ref-Keeler2011_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Keeler2011-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> New Fellows can only be nominated by existing <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">Fellows</a> for one of the fellowships described below:
</p>

<p>Every year, up to 52 new Fellows are elected from the  <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Commonwealth of Nations">Commonwealth of Nations</a> which make up around 90% of the society.<sup id="cite_ref-elections_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elections-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Each candidate is considered on their merits and can be proposed from any sector of the scientific community. Fellows are elected for life on the basis of excellence in science and are entitled to use the <a href="/wiki/Post-nominal_letters" title="Post-nominal letters">post-nominal letters</a> FRS.<sup id="cite_ref-elections_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elections-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> See <a href="/wiki/Category:Fellows_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Category:Fellows of the Royal Society">Category:Fellows of the Royal Society</a> and <a href="/wiki/Category:Female_Fellows_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Category:Female Fellows of the Royal Society">Category:Female Fellows of the Royal Society</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="Foreign_Member_of_the_Royal_Society_.28ForMemRS.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_Member_of_the_Royal_Society_(ForMemRS)">Foreign Member of the Royal Society (ForMemRS)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Foreign Member of the Royal Society (ForMemRS)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Professor_Jennifer_Doudna_ForMemRS.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Professor_Jennifer_Doudna_ForMemRS.jpg/220px-Professor_Jennifer_Doudna_ForMemRS.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Professor_Jennifer_Doudna_ForMemRS.jpg/330px-Professor_Jennifer_Doudna_ForMemRS.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Professor_Jennifer_Doudna_ForMemRS.jpg/440px-Professor_Jennifer_Doudna_ForMemRS.jpg 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="1440" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Professor_Jennifer_Doudna_ForMemRS.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Doudna" title="Jennifer Doudna">Jennifer Doudna</a>, a professor of chemistry, was elected a Foreign Member of the Royal Society (ForMemRS) in 2016</div></div></div>
<p>Every year, Fellows elect up to ten new Foreign Members. Like Fellows, Foreign Members are elected for life through <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer review</a> on the basis of excellence in science. As of 2016<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> there are around 165 Foreign Members, who are entitled to use the post-nominal ForMemRS.<sup id="cite_ref-GulyasSomogyi2012_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GulyasSomogyi2012-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> See <a href="/wiki/Category:Foreign_Members_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Category:Foreign Members of the Royal Society">Category:Foreign Members of the Royal Society</a>.
</p>
<h3><span id="Honorary_Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society_.28HonFRS.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Honorary_Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society_(HonFRS)">Honorary Fellow of the Royal Society (HonFRS)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Honorary Fellow of the Royal Society (HonFRS)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bill_Bryson_edit.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Bill_Bryson_edit.jpg/220px-Bill_Bryson_edit.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/ea/Bill_Bryson_edit.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="256" data-file-height="341" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bill_Bryson_edit.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Bill_Bryson" title="Bill Bryson">Bill Bryson</a> was elected an Honorary Member of the Royal Society in 2013</div></div></div>
<p>Honorary Fellowship is an <a href="/wiki/Honorary_title_(academic)" title="Honorary title (academic)">honorary academic title</a> awarded to candidates who have given distinguished service to the cause of science, but do not have the kind of scientific achievements required of Fellows or Foreign Members. Honorary Fellows include <a href="/wiki/Bill_Bryson" title="Bill Bryson">Bill Bryson</a> (2013), <a href="/wiki/Melvyn_Bragg" title="Melvyn Bragg">Melvyn Bragg</a> (2010), <a href="/wiki/Robin_Saxby" title="Robin Saxby">Robin Saxby</a> (2015), <a href="/wiki/David_Sainsbury,_Baron_Sainsbury_of_Turville" title="David Sainsbury, Baron Sainsbury of Turville">David Sainsbury, Baron Sainsbury of Turville</a> (2008) and  <a href="/wiki/Onora_O%27Neill" title="Onora O&#39;Neill">Onora O'Neill</a> (2007). Honorary Fellows are entitled to use the post nominal letters FRS.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Others including <a href="/wiki/John_Maddox" title="John Maddox">John Maddox</a> (2000),<sup id="cite_ref-maddox_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maddox-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Moore" title="Patrick Moore">Patrick Moore</a> (2001) and <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Jardine" title="Lisa Jardine">Lisa Jardine</a> (2015)<sup id="cite_ref-jarsbm_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jarsbm-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-jardine_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jardine-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> were elected as honorary fellows, see <a href="/wiki/Category:Honorary_Fellows_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Category:Honorary Fellows of the Royal Society">Category:Honorary Fellows of the Royal Society</a>.
</p>

<p>Statute 12 is a legacy mechanism for electing members before official honorary membership existed in 1997.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. </p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Royal_Fellows_of_the_Royal_Society">Royal Fellows of the Royal Society</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Royal Fellows of the Royal Society">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Council of the Royal Society can recommend members of the <a href="/wiki/British_Royal_Family" class="mw-redirect" title="British Royal Family">British Royal Family</a> for election as <a href="/wiki/Royal_Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Royal Fellow of the Royal Society">Royal Fellows of the Royal Society</a>. As of 2016<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> there are five royal fellows:  
</p>
<ol><li><a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Charles, Prince of Wales</a> elected 1978<sup id="cite_ref-frscharles_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-frscharles-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anne,_Princess_Royal" title="Anne, Princess Royal">Anne, Princess Royal</a> elected 1987<sup id="cite_ref-frsanne_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-frsanne-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward,_Duke_of_Kent" title="Prince Edward, Duke of Kent">Prince Edward, Duke of Kent</a> elected 1990<sup id="cite_ref-frsedwards_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-frsedwards-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup></li>

</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Election_of_new_fellows">Election of new fellows</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Election of new fellows">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The election of new fellows is announced annually in May, after their nomination and a period of <a href="/wiki/Peer-review" class="mw-redirect" title="Peer-review">peer-reviewed</a> selection.<sup id="cite_ref-elections_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elections-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Nomination">Nomination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Nomination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Each candidate for Fellowship or Foreign Membership is nominated by two Fellows of the Royal Society (a proposer and a seconder), who sign a certificate of proposal.<sup id="cite_ref-occamten_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-occamten-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Previously, nominations required at least five fellows to support each nomination by the proposer,<sup id="cite_ref-occamten_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-occamten-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> which was criticised for supposedly establishing an <a href="/wiki/Old-boy_network" class="mw-redirect" title="Old-boy network">old-boy network</a> and elitist <a href="/wiki/Gentlemen%27s_club" title="Gentlemen&#39;s club">gentlemen's club</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gender_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gender-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cronysim_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cronysim-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-notenoughwomen_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-notenoughwomen-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The certificate of election (see for example<sup id="cite_ref-geimfrs_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-geimfrs-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>) includes a statement of the principal grounds on which the proposal is being made. There is no limit on the number of nominations made each year. In 2015, there were 654 candidates for election as Fellows and 106 candidates for Foreign Membership.<sup id="cite_ref-elections_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elections-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Selection">Selection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Selection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Council of the Royal Society oversees the selection process and appoints 10 subject area committees, known as Sectional Committees,<sup id="cite_ref-window_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-window-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> to recommend the strongest candidates for election to Fellowship. The final list of up to 52 Fellowship candidates and up to 10 Foreign Membership candidates is confirmed by the Council in April and a <a href="/wiki/Secret_ballot" title="Secret ballot">secret ballot</a> of Fellows is held at a meeting in May. A candidate is elected if he or she secures two-thirds of votes of those Fellows present and voting.
</p><p>A maximum of 18 Fellowships can be allocated to candidates from Physical Sciences and Biological Sciences; and up to 10 from Applied Sciences, Human Sciences and Joint Physical and Biological Sciences. A further maximum of 6 can be ‘Honorary’, ‘General’ or ‘Royal’ Fellows. Nominations for Fellowship are <a href="/wiki/Peer_review" title="Peer review">peer reviewed</a> by sectional committees, each with 15 members and a chair. Members of the 10 sectional committees change every 3 years to mitigate <a href="/wiki/In-group_favoritism" title="In-group favoritism">in-group bias</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-occamten_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-occamten-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> each group covers different specialist areas including:
</p>
<ol><li><a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">Mathematics</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Astronomy" title="Astronomy">Astronomy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Physics" title="Physics">physics</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chemistry" title="Chemistry">Chemistry</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">Engineering</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Earth_science" title="Earth science">Earth science</a> and <a href="/wiki/Environmental_science" title="Environmental science">environmental science</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Biochemistry" title="Biochemistry">Biochemistry</a> and molecular <a href="/wiki/Cell_biology" title="Cell biology">cell biology</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Microbiology" title="Microbiology">Microbiology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Immunology" title="Immunology">immunology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Developmental_biology" title="Developmental biology">developmental biology</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anatomy" title="Anatomy">Anatomy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Physiology" title="Physiology">physiology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neuroscience" title="Neuroscience">neuroscience</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Biology" title="Biology">Organismal biology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Evolution" title="Evolution">evolution</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecology</a></li>
<li>Health and <a href="/wiki/Human_science" title="Human science">human sciences</a><sup id="cite_ref-sectional_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sectional-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup></li></ol>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Admission">Admission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Admission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>New Fellows are admitted to the Society at a formal admissions day ceremony held annually in July,<sup id="cite_ref-admissions_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-admissions-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> when they sign the <a href="/wiki/Charter" title="Charter">Charter</a> Book and the Obligation which reads:
"We who have hereunto subscribed, do hereby promise, that we will endeavour to promote the good of the Royal Society of London for Improving Natural Knowledge, and to pursue the ends for which the same was founded; that we will carry out, as far as we are able, those actions requested of us in the name of the Council; and that we will observe the Statutes and Standing Orders of the said Society. Provided that, whensoever any of us shall signify to the President under our hands, that we desire to withdraw from the Society, we shall be free from this Obligation for the future".<sup id="cite_ref-elections_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-elections-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2014, portraits of Fellows at the admissions ceremony have been published without <a href="/wiki/Copyright" title="Copyright">copyright</a> restrictions in <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Commons" title="Wikimedia Commons">Wikimedia Commons</a> under a more permissive <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons_license" title="Creative Commons license">Creative Commons license</a> which allows wider re-use.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wikimediablog_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikimediablog-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_Fellowships_and_other_awards">Research Fellowships and other awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Research Fellowships and other awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Professor_Brian_Cox_OBE_FRS.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Professor_Brian_Cox_OBE_FRS.jpg/220px-Professor_Brian_Cox_OBE_FRS.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Professor_Brian_Cox_OBE_FRS.jpg/330px-Professor_Brian_Cox_OBE_FRS.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Professor_Brian_Cox_OBE_FRS.jpg/440px-Professor_Brian_Cox_OBE_FRS.jpg 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="1440" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Professor_Brian_Cox_OBE_FRS.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Brian_Cox_(physicist)" title="Brian Cox (physicist)">Brian Cox</a>, a professor of physics, was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society in 2016 having previously held a <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_University_Research_Fellowship" title="Royal Society University Research Fellowship">Royal Society University Research Fellowship</a> (URF) from 2005 to 2013<sup id="cite_ref-amazin_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-amazin-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>In addition to the main Fellowships of the Royal Society (FRS, ForMemRS &amp; HonFRS), other fellowships are available which are applied for by individuals, rather than through election. Holders of these fellowships are known as Royal Society <a href="/wiki/Research_fellow" title="Research fellow">Research Fellows</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b>University Research Fellowships</b> (URFs) <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_University_Research_Fellowship" title="Royal Society University Research Fellowship">Royal Society University Research Fellowships</a> are for outstanding scientists in the UK who are in the early stages of their research career and have the potential to become leaders in their field.<sup id="cite_ref-urf_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urf-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Previous holders of URFs have been elected FRS at a later date including <a href="/wiki/Richard_Borcherds" title="Richard Borcherds">Richard Borcherds</a> (1994), <a href="/wiki/Jean_Beggs" title="Jean Beggs">Jean Beggs</a> (1998), <a href="/wiki/Frances_Ashcroft" title="Frances Ashcroft">Frances Ashcroft</a> (1999), <a href="/wiki/Athene_Donald" title="Athene Donald">Athene Donald</a> (1999) and <a href="/wiki/John_Pethica" title="John Pethica">John Pethica</a> (1999).<sup id="cite_ref-urfs_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-urfs-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> More recent awardees include <a href="/wiki/Terri_Attwood" title="Terri Attwood">Terri Attwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarah-Jayne_Blakemore" title="Sarah-Jayne Blakemore">Sarah-Jayne Blakemore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Cox_(physicist)" title="Brian Cox (physicist)">Brian Cox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Bridle" title="Sarah Bridle">Sarah Bridle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shahn_Majid" title="Shahn Majid">Shahn Majid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tanya_Monro" title="Tanya Monro">Tanya Monro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beth_Shapiro" title="Beth Shapiro">Beth Shapiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_J._Wales" title="David J. Wales">David J. Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kathy_Willis" title="Kathy Willis">Kathy Willis</a></li>
<li><b>Royal Society Leverhulme Trust Senior Research Fellowships</b> are for scientists who would benefit from a period of full-time research without teaching and administrative duties, supported by the <a href="/wiki/Leverhulme_Trust" title="Leverhulme Trust">Leverhulme Trust</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-leverhulme_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-leverhulme-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Newton Advanced Fellowships</b> provide established international researchers with an opportunity to develop the research strengths and capabilities of their research group. Provided by the Newton Fund via the <a href="/wiki/Official_development_assistance" title="Official development assistance">official development assistance</a><sup id="cite_ref-newtonfund_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newtonfund-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Industry Fellowships</b> are for academic scientists who want to work on a collaborative project with industry and for scientists in industry who want to work on a collaborative project with an academic organisation.<sup id="cite_ref-industry_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-industry-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Dorothy Hodgkin Fellowships</b> are for outstanding scientists in the UK at an early stage of their research career who require a flexible working pattern due to personal circumstances. These fellowships are named after <a href="/wiki/Dorothy_Hodgkin" title="Dorothy Hodgkin">Dorothy Hodgkin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-dorothy_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dorothy-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>In addition to the award of <a href="/wiki/Fellow" title="Fellow">Fellowship</a> (FRS, HonFRS &amp; ForMemRS) and the <a href="/wiki/Research_fellow" title="Research fellow">Research Fellowships</a> described above, several other <a href="/wiki/Awards,_lectures_and_medals_of_the_Royal_Society" title="Awards, lectures and medals of the Royal Society">awards, lectures and medals of the Royal Society</a> are also given.
</p>
