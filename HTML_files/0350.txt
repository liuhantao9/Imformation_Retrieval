<p>The<b> wet season</b> (sometimes called the <b>rainy season</b>) is the time of year when most of a region's average annual <a href="/wiki/Rainfall" class="mw-redirect" title="Rainfall">rainfall</a> occurs. Generally the season lasts at least a month.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The term '<b>green season'</b> is also sometimes used as a <a href="/wiki/Euphemism" title="Euphemism">euphemism</a> by tourist authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Areas with wet seasons are dispersed across portions of the <a href="/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">tropics</a> and <a href="/wiki/Subtropics" title="Subtropics">subtropics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Under the <a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen climate classification</a>, for <a href="/wiki/Tropical_climate" title="Tropical climate">tropical climates</a>, a wet season month is defined as a month where average precipitation is 60 millimetres (2.4&#160;in) or more.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In contrast to areas with <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a> climates and <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">monsoon</a> regimes, <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">Mediterranean climates</a> have wet winters and dry summers. Dry and rainy months are characteristic of <a href="/wiki/Tropical_seasonal_forest" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical seasonal forest">tropical seasonal forests</a>: in contrast to <a href="/wiki/Tropical_rainforest" title="Tropical rainforest">tropical rainforests</a>, which do not have dry or wet seasons, since their rainfall is equally distributed throughout the year.<sup id="cite_ref-Hyde_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hyde-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Some areas with pronounced rainy seasons will see a break in rainfall mid-season, when the <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_convergence_zone" class="mw-redirect" title="Intertropical convergence zone">intertropical convergence zone</a> or <a href="/wiki/Monsoon_trough" title="Monsoon trough">monsoon trough</a> moves to higher latitudes in the middle of the warm season.<sup id="cite_ref-JS_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JS-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the wet season occurs during a warm season, or <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">summer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">precipitation</a> falls mainly during the late afternoon and early evening. In the wet season, <a href="/wiki/Air_quality" class="mw-redirect" title="Air quality">air quality</a> improves, fresh water quality improves, and vegetation grows substantially, leading to crop yields late in the season. Rivers overflow their banks, and some animals retreat to higher ground. <a href="/wiki/Soil" title="Soil">Soil</a> nutrients diminish and erosion increases. The incidence of <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">malaria</a> increases in areas where the rainy season coincides with high temperatures, particularly in tropical areas.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Some animals have adaptation and survival strategies for the wet season. Often, the previous dry season leads to food shortages in the wet season, as the crops have yet to mature.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Character_of_the_rainfall">Character of the rainfall</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wet_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Character of the rainfall">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Darwin_1824.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Darwin_1824.jpg/220px-Darwin_1824.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Darwin_1824.jpg/330px-Darwin_1824.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Darwin_1824.jpg/440px-Darwin_1824.jpg 2x" data-file-width="700" data-file-height="500" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Darwin_1824.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Wet season storm at night in <a href="/wiki/Darwin,_Northern_Territory" title="Darwin, Northern Territory">Darwin</a>, Australia.</div></div></div>
<p>In areas where the heavy rainfall is associated with a wind shift, the wet season is known as the monsoon. Many tropical and subtropical climates experience monsoon rainfall patterns. <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Rainfall in the wet season is mainly due to daytime heating which leads to diurnal thunderstorm activity within a pre-existing moist airmass, so the rain mainly falls in late afternoon and early evening in savannah and monsoon regions. Further, much of the total rainfall each day occurs in the first minutes of the downpour,<sup id="cite_ref-JS_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JS-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> before the storms mature into their stratiform stage.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Most places have only one wet season, but areas of the tropics can have two wet seasons, because the monsoon trough, or <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">Intertropical Convergence Zone</a>, can pass over locations in the tropics twice per year. However, since rain forests have rainfall spread evenly through the year, they do not have a wet season. <sup id="cite_ref-Hyde_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hyde-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Areas_affected">Areas affected</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wet_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Areas affected">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Areas with a <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a> climate in <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Sub-Saharan Africa</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Ghana" title="Ghana">Ghana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Darfur" title="Darfur">Darfur</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a> have a distinct rainy season.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  Also subtropical areas like <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Texas" title="South Texas">South</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Texas" title="Southeast Texas">Southeast</a> Texas, and southern <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a> have a rainy season. <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Monsoon regions include the <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">Indian subcontinent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>),<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> northern sections of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>'s North,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Polynesia" title="Polynesia">Polynesia</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> western and southern <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> the Desert Southwest of the United States,<sup id="cite_ref-JHorel_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JHorel-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> southern <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-GUY_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GUY-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> portions of northeast <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Brazil_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brazil-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Northern <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> has two wet seasons: one in early spring and the other in early winter.<sup id="cite_ref-GUY_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GUY-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In western Africa, there are two rainy seasons across southern sections, but only one across the north.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Within the Mediterranean climate regime, the west coast of the United States and the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> coastline of Italy, Greece,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and Turkey experience a wet season in the winter months.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>  Similarly, the wet season in the <a href="/wiki/Negev_desert" class="mw-redirect" title="Negev desert">Negev desert</a> of <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> extends from October through May.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>  At the boundary between the Mediterranean and monsoon climates lies the <a href="/wiki/Sonoran_desert" class="mw-redirect" title="Sonoran desert">Sonoran desert</a>, which receives the two rainy seasons associated with each climate regime.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The wet season is known by many different local names throughout the world. For example, in Mexico it is known as "storm season". Different names are given to the various short "seasons" of the year by the Aboriginal tribes of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Australia" title="Northern Australia">Northern Australia</a>: the wet season typically experienced there from December to March is called <i>Gudjewg</i>. The precise meaning of the word is disputed, although it is widely accepted to relate to the severe thunderstorms, flooding, and abundant vegetation growth commonly experienced at this time.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Effects">Effects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wet_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Effects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vindhya.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Vindhya.jpg/250px-Vindhya.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Vindhya.jpg/375px-Vindhya.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Vindhya.jpg/500px-Vindhya.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="500" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vindhya.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Monsoon in the <a href="/wiki/Vindhya" class="mw-redirect" title="Vindhya">Vindhya</a> mountain range, central <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>In tropical areas, when the monsoon arrives, high daytime high temperatures drop and overnight low temperatures increase, thus reducing <a href="/wiki/Diurnal_temperature_variation" title="Diurnal temperature variation">diurnal temperature variation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>  During the wet season, a combination of heavy rainfall and, in some places such as <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, an onshore wind, improve <a href="/wiki/Air_quality" class="mw-redirect" title="Air quality">air quality</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In Brazil, the wet season is correlated with weaker <a href="/wiki/Trade_winds" title="Trade winds">trade winds</a> off the ocean.<sup id="cite_ref-Brazil_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brazil-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>  The <a href="/wiki/PH" title="PH">pH</a> level of water becomes more balanced due to the charging of local <a href="/wiki/Aquifer" title="Aquifer">aquifers</a> during the wet season.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Water also softens, as the concentration of dissolved materials reduces during the rainy season.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Erosion is also increased during rainy periods.<sup id="cite_ref-JS_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JS-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Arroyo_(creek)" title="Arroyo (creek)">Arroyos</a> that are dry at other times of the year fill with runoff, in some cases with water as deep as 10 feet (3.0&#160;m).<sup id="cite_ref-Road_Notes,_Cuba._1909_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Road_Notes,_Cuba._1909-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Leaching of soils during periods of heavy rainfall depletes nutrients.<sup id="cite_ref-Road_Notes,_Cuba._1909_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Road_Notes,_Cuba._1909-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> The higher runoff from land masses affects nearby ocean areas, which are more <a href="/wiki/Stratification_(water)" title="Stratification (water)">stratified</a>, or less mixed, due to stronger surface currents forced by the heavy rainfall runoff.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Floods">Floods</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wet_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Floods">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>High rainfall can cause widespread flooding,<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> which can lead to landslides and mudflows in mountainous areas.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Such floods cause rivers to burst their banks and submerge homes.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Ghaggar-Hakra_River" title="Ghaggar-Hakra River">Ghaggar-Hakra River</a>, which only flows during India's monsoon season, can flood and severely damage local crops.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Floods can be exacerbated by fires that occurred during the previous dry season, which cause soils which are sandy or composed of <a href="/wiki/Loam" title="Loam">loam</a> to become hydrophobic, or water repellent.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In various ways governments may help people deal with wet season floods. Flood plain mapping identifies which areas are more prone to flooding.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Instructions on controlling erosion through outreach<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (August 2015)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> are also provided by telephone or the internet.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_adaptations">Life adaptations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wet_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Life adaptations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:East_-_Guinean_Savanna_001.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/East_-_Guinean_Savanna_001.jpg/250px-East_-_Guinean_Savanna_001.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/8d/East_-_Guinean_Savanna_001.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="320" data-file-height="185" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:East_-_Guinean_Savanna_001.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Equatorial <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savanna</a> in the <a href="/wiki/East_Province,_Cameroon" class="mw-redirect" title="East Province, Cameroon">East Province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>.</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Humans">Humans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wet_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Humans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The wet season is the main period of vegetation growth within the Savanna climate regime.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> However, this also means that wet season is a time for food shortages before crops reach their full maturity.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> This causes seasonal weight changes for people in developing countries, with a drop occurring during the wet season until the time of the first harvest, when weights rebound.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Malaria incidence increases during periods of high temperature and heavy rainfall.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Animals">Animals</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wet_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Animals">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Cows calve, or give birth, at the beginning of the wet season.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>  The onset of the rainy season signals the departure of the <a href="/wiki/Monarch_butterfly" title="Monarch butterfly">monarch butterfly</a> from Mexico.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>  Tropical species of <a href="/wiki/Butterfly" title="Butterfly">butterflies</a> show larger dot markings on their wings to fend off possible predators and are more active during the wet season than the <a href="/wiki/Dry_season" title="Dry season">dry season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>  Within the tropics and warmer areas of the subtropics, decreased salinity of near shore wetlands due to the rains causes an increase in crocodile nesting.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>  Other species, such as the <a href="/wiki/Arroyo_toad" title="Arroyo toad">arroyo toad</a>, spawn within the couple of months after the seasonal rains.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Armadillo" title="Armadillo">Armadillos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rattlesnake" title="Rattlesnake">rattlesnakes</a> seek higher ground.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
