<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Border_disputes">Border disputes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Foreign_relations_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Border disputes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Borders_of_Suriname" title="Borders of Suriname">Borders of Suriname</a></div>
<p>The country claims an area in <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a> between <a href="/wiki/Litani_River,_South_America" class="mw-redirect" title="Litani River, South America">Litani River</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marouini_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Marouini River">Marouini River</a> (both headwaters of the Lawa). Suriname also claims an area in <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> between New (Upper Courantyne) and Courantyne/Koetari Rivers (all headwaters of the <a href="/wiki/Courantyne_River" title="Courantyne River">Courantyne</a>).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="International_organization_participation">International organization participation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Foreign_relations_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: International organization participation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Suriname is a member of numerous international organizations. Among them, since gaining independence, Suriname has become a member of the UN, the <a href="/wiki/Organization_of_American_States" title="Organization of American States">OAS</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement" title="Non-Aligned Movement">Non-Aligned Movement</a>. Suriname is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Community_and_Common_Market" class="mw-redirect" title="Caribbean Community and Common Market">Caribbean Community and Common Market</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_Caribbean_States" title="Association of Caribbean States">Association of Caribbean States</a>. It is associated with the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Lome_Convention" class="mw-redirect" title="Lome Convention">Lome Convention</a>. Suriname participates in the Amazonian Pact, a grouping of the countries of the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Basin" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon Basin">Amazon Basin</a> that focuses on protection of the Amazon region's natural resources from environmental degradation. 
</p><p>Reflecting its status as a major <a href="/wiki/Bauxite" title="Bauxite">bauxite</a> producer, Suriname is a member of the International Bauxite Association. The country also belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Economic_Commission_for_Latin_America" class="mw-redirect" title="Economic Commission for Latin America">Economic Commission for Latin America</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Inter-American_Development_Bank" title="Inter-American Development Bank">Inter-American Development Bank</a>, the <a href="/wiki/International_Finance_Corporation" title="International Finance Corporation">International Finance Corporation</a>, the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund" title="International Monetary Fund">International Monetary Fund</a>. Suriname became a member of the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Development_Bank" title="Islamic Development Bank">Islamic Development Bank</a> in 1998, under the <a href="/wiki/Wijdenbosch" class="mw-redirect" title="Wijdenbosch">Wijdenbosch</a> government. In 2003, Suriname joined the <a href="/wiki/Nederlandse_Taalunie" class="mw-redirect" title="Nederlandse Taalunie">Nederlandse Taalunie</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dutch_Language" class="mw-redirect" title="Dutch Language">Dutch Language</a> union).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_and_international_agreements">Regional and international agreements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Foreign_relations_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Regional and international agreements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bilateral agreements with several countries of the region, covering diverse areas of cooperation, have underscored the government's interest in strengthening regional ties. The return to Suriname from <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">French Guiana</a> of about 8,000 refugees of the 1986–91 civil war between the military and domestic insurgents has improved relations with French authorities. Longstanding border disputes with <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> and French Guiana remain unresolved. Negotiations with the Government of Guyana brokered by the Jamaican Prime Minister in 2000 did not produce an agreement but the countries agreed to restart talks after Guyanese national elections in 2001. In January 2002 the presidents of Suriname and Guyana met in Suriname and agreed to resume negotiations, establishing the Suriname-Guyana border commission to begin meeting in May 2002. An earlier dispute with <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> ended amicably after formal demarcation of the border.
</p><p>In May 1997, then-President Wijdenbosch joined US <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">President Clinton</a> and 14 other <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> leaders during the first-ever US-regional summit in <a href="/wiki/Bridgetown" title="Bridgetown">Bridgetown</a>, Barbados. The summit strengthened the basis for regional cooperation on justice and counter narcotics issues, finance, development, and trade.
</p>
