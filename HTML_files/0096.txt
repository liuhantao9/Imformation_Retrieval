<p>"<b>Somethin' Bad</b>" is a song recorded as a duet by American country music artists <a href="/wiki/Miranda_Lambert" title="Miranda Lambert">Miranda Lambert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carrie_Underwood" title="Carrie Underwood">Carrie Underwood</a>.  The song was written by <a href="/wiki/Chris_DeStefano" title="Chris DeStefano">Chris DeStefano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brett_James" title="Brett James">Brett James</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Priscilla_Renea" title="Priscilla Renea">Priscilla Renea</a>. It was released as the second single from Lambert's fifth studio album <i><a href="/wiki/Platinum_(Miranda_Lambert_album)" title="Platinum (Miranda Lambert album)">Platinum</a></i> in advance of the album's release two weeks later. The song premiered at the <a href="/wiki/2014_Billboard_Music_Awards" title="2014 Billboard Music Awards">2014 Billboard Music Awards</a> on May 18, 2014, and was released for sale the next day.
</p><p>"Somethin' Bad" peaked at number one on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs"><i>Billboard</i> Hot Country Songs</a>, becoming Underwood's thirteenth number one on the chart and Lambert's fifth. The song is the first number one by teamed-up solo women in more than two decades on this chart.
</p><p>On December 5, 2014, the collaboration received a <a href="/wiki/57th_Annual_Grammy_Awards" title="57th Annual Grammy Awards">Grammy nomination</a> for <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Duo/Group_Performance" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Duo/Group Performance">Best Country Duo/Group Performance</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Somethin%27_Bad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song was written by Chris DeStefano and Brett James along with Priscilla Renea while they were at a writing retreat in France for <a href="/wiki/ASCAP" class="mw-redirect" title="ASCAP">ASCAP</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The song was originally intended to be a male/female duet, but was later tweaked so that it could be performed by two females.<sup id="cite_ref-tennessean_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tennessean-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song is seen as a female take on the <a href="/wiki/Bro-country" title="Bro-country">bro-country</a> genre.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> According to Lambert, when she heard "Somethin’ Bad", "the song just popped," and it "really struck a chord."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  Lambert said she had wanted to do a duet with Carrie Underwood, and that "It's been too long since two girls in our genre have come together like that, especially on a song that's kind of in your face."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> She sent Underwood an email to request a collaboration, which was accepted as they had wanted to sing together for some time.<sup id="cite_ref-tennessean_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tennessean-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song made its debut on the 2014 Billboard Music Awards on May 18, 2014 with a performance by Lambert and Underwood.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> They performed the song again at the <a href="/wiki/CMT_Music_Awards" title="CMT Music Awards">CMT Music Awards</a> on June 4, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Somethin%27_Bad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song received generally favorable response from the critics.  Matt Bjorke of <i>Roughstock</i> thought the song playful, fun, and "sure to be a fast-selling hit."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Jason Scott of AXS called it "a fun, beat-driven summer anthem" and rated it four stars out of five.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <i>Taste of Country</i> said that the song's "ready-for-anything lyrics and the way that Lambert and Underwood belt them out with such power, make ‘Somethin’ Bad’ a catchy girl-power anthem."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercial_performance">Commercial performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Somethin%27_Bad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Commercial performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>"Somethin' Bad" debuted at No. 39 on the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> chart and at No. 5 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Country_Songs" title="Hot Country Songs"><i>Billboard</i> Hot Country Songs</a> chart following its release for sales on May 19, 2014 (chart dated June 7, 2014).<sup id="cite_ref-trust_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trust-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>  The song debuted at No. 48 on the <i>Billboard</i> Country Airplay chart for the same week, before it was officially released to radio on June 16, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The song also debuted at No. 1 on the <i>Billboard</i> Country Digital Songs chart, with 107,000 downloads sold in its first week.<sup id="cite_ref-trust_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trust-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> "Somethin' Bad" peaked at No. 1 on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot Country Songs in its sixth week, becoming Underwood's 13th number one on the chart and Lambert's fifth. The same week, the song jumped to No. 19 on the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, becoming Lambert's highest peaking song to date on that chart. It is the first No. 1 by teamed-up solo women in more than two decades on the <i>Billboard</i> Hot Country Songs chart.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> As of April 2015, the song has sold 1,080,000 copies in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-sales_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sales-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Canada, the song debuted at No. 33 on the Canadian Hot 100 chart following its first week of release.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_video">Music video</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Somethin%27_Bad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Music video">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The music video was directed by <a href="/wiki/Trey_Fanjoy" title="Trey Fanjoy">Trey Fanjoy</a> and released on June 25, 2014. It features Lambert as Belle Boyd and Underwood as  Priscilla Parker, a pair of jewel thieves.  The video is presented as a trailer for a film where the pair play poker, ride Indian motorcycles and engage in a jewel heist in which they flee in a luxury Bell Helicopter.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Miranda purchased the two Indian Motorcycles keeping one for herself, while giving the other to Carrie as a "thank you" for doing the song with her. Carrie tweeted a pic of her on the bike. The bikes and the helicopter were part of a product placement arrangement organized by CMS Nashville.
</p><p>As of April 2018, the music video has accumulated over 86 million views on <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a> and was nominated for a 2014 CMA for "Video of the Year."
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sunday_Night_Football_2016_theme"><i>Sunday Night Football</i> 2016 theme</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Somethin%27_Bad&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sunday Night Football 2016 theme">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In June 2016, <a href="/wiki/NBC_Sports" title="NBC Sports">NBC Sports</a> announced that "Somethin' Bad" would form the basis of Underwood's new theme for <i><a href="/wiki/NBC_Sunday_Night_Football" title="NBC Sunday Night Football">NBC Sunday Night Football</a></i> starting with the <a href="/wiki/2016_NFL_season" title="2016 NFL season">2016 NFL season</a>. Titled "Oh, Sunday Night", it replaced "Waiting All Day for Sunday Night", itself inspired by an existing song (<a href="/wiki/Joan_Jett" title="Joan Jett">Joan Jett</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/I_Hate_Myself_for_Loving_You" title="I Hate Myself for Loving You">I Hate Myself for Loving You</a>").<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
