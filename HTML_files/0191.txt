	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">List of double placenames</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/List_of_reduplicated_place_names" title="List of reduplicated place names">List of reduplicated place names</a>.</div>
<p><b>Double placenames</b> prominently feature the <a href="/wiki/Placename" class="mw-redirect" title="Placename">placenames</a> of two or more constituent geopolitical entities.
</p><p>Such placenames are often created when two cities, provinces or other territories are <a href="/wiki/Amalgamation_(politics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Amalgamation (politics)">amalgamated</a> or merged, and a decision is taken to preserve the old names in <a href="/wiki/Double-barrelled_name" title="Double-barrelled name">double-barrelled</a> form rather than invent a new name. This is often out of consideration for local sensitivities, since the smaller entity may resent its takeover, and may demand its symbolic perpetuation within an amalgamated name so as to propagate the impression of a merger between equals.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Styles">Styles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_double_placenames&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Styles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In their English forms, the conjoined names may have the following patterns:
</p>
<ul><li>concatenation, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Grammatical_conjunction" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammatical conjunction">grammatical conjunction</a>, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago" title="Trinidad and Tobago">Trinidad and Tobago</a></li></ul>
<p>The punctuation and capitalization practices in written English vary:
</p>
<ul><li>merging into one word without an intermediate space, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a></li>
<li>standing apart, e.g. <a href="/wiki/Papua_New_Guinea" title="Papua New Guinea">Papua New Guinea</a></li>
<li>conjunction by <a href="/wiki/Hyphen" title="Hyphen">hyphenation</a>. While English-speakers are relaxed about using a hyphen or not, this punctuation once caused <a href="/wiki/Hyphen_War" title="Hyphen War">controversy</a> between Czechs and Slovaks</li>
<li>conjunction with an <a href="/wiki/En_dash" class="mw-redirect" title="En dash">en dash</a>, typically when the usage is associative, attributive or is a juxtaposition of two independent entities.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/CamelCase" class="mw-redirect" title="CamelCase">CamelCase</a> may sometimes be attempted, but many style guides recommend against this in formal English-language use.</li></ul>
<table class="box-Original_research plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Original_research" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/40px-Ambox_important.svg.png" decoding="async" width="40" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/60px-Ambox_important.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/80px-Ambox_important.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="40" data-file-height="40" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>possibly contains <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:No_original_research" title="Wikipedia:No original research">original research</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=List_of_double_placenames&amp;action=edit">improve it</a> by <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verifying</a> the claims made and adding <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#Inline_citations" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">inline citations</a>. <p>Three-word names for two-part entities are often ambiguous. For example, it may not be clear whether North Rhine-Westphalia is an amalgamation between the north part of the <a href="/wiki/Rhine_Province" title="Rhine Province">Rhine Province</a> on the one hand and <a href="/wiki/Westphalia" title="Westphalia">Westphalia</a> on the other (true) or the northern division of some pre-existing place called Rhine-Westphalia (false). While this problem does not arise in German, no entirely satisfactory punctuation of such names has been established in English. In the above case, the hyphen is often omitted because it is misleading. It has been proposed that this state's name be punctuated "North-Rhine/Westphalia" in English, but the solidus or forward slash is also ambiguous.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Neologisms">Neologisms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_double_placenames&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Neologisms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Some names have been merged and modified as an alternative to using <a href="/wiki/Hyphen" title="Hyphen">hyphenation</a> or <a href="/wiki/Grammatical_conjunction" class="mw-redirect" title="Grammatical conjunction">grammatical conjunction</a>:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/BosWash" title="BosWash">BosWash</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Megalopolis_(city_type)" class="mw-redirect" title="Megalopolis (city type)">megalopolis</a> extending from <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> to Washington, D.C., <a href="/wiki/CamelCase" class="mw-redirect" title="CamelCase">CamelCase</a> example (extended in fiction into a <a href="/wiki/Boston%E2%80%93Atlanta_Metropolitan_Axis" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston–Atlanta Metropolitan Axis">Boston–Atlanta Metropolitan Axis</a> or BAMA covering most of the US East Coast)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/SeaTac,_Washington" title="SeaTac, Washington">SeaTac</a>: another example of CamelCase (<a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tacoma,_Washington" title="Tacoma, Washington">Tacoma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Washington_(state)" title="Washington (state)">Washington</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Czech_lands" title="Czech lands">Czech lands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Slovakia" title="Slovakia">Slovakia</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Senegambia" title="Senegambia">Senegambia</a>: <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gambia" class="mw-redirect" title="Gambia">Gambia</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>: <a href="/wiki/Tanganyika" title="Tanganyika">Tanganyika</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zanzibar" title="Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a></li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="False_double_placenames">False double placenames</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_double_placenames&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: False double placenames">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Binomial_nomenclature" title="Binomial nomenclature">Binomial</a> placenames are not true double placenames, but elements in a <a href="/wiki/Hierarchical" class="mw-redirect" title="Hierarchical">hierarchical</a> naming system.  They are a means of distinguishing two entities which share a parent geographic feature. Examples:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Guinea-Bissau" title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a> (official name of the country with capital <a href="/wiki/Bissau" title="Bissau">Bissau</a>, as distinct from <a href="/wiki/Guinea" title="Guinea">Guinea</a>, with capital <a href="/wiki/Conakry" title="Conakry">Conakry</a>)</li>
<li>Congo-<a href="/wiki/Brazzaville" title="Brazzaville">Brazzaville</a> and Congo-<a href="/wiki/Kinshasa" title="Kinshasa">Kinshasa</a> (from the respective capitals of what are officially <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of the Congo">Democratic Republic of the Congo</a>)</li></ul>
<p>They are often used for railway stations and airports:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/King%27s_Cross_St_Pancras_tube_station" title="King&#39;s Cross St Pancras tube station">King's Cross St Pancras</a> This is the <a href="/wiki/London_Underground" title="London Underground">London Underground</a> station serving two separate London train terminals, <a href="/wiki/London_King%27s_Cross_railway_station" title="London King&#39;s Cross railway station">King's Cross</a> and <a href="/wiki/St_Pancras_railway_station" title="St Pancras railway station">St Pancras</a> train stations.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Paris_-_Orly_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Paris - Orly Airport">Paris - Orly Airport</a> (one of two Paris airports)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Trenton%E2%80%93Mercer_Airport" title="Trenton–Mercer Airport">Trenton–Mercer Airport</a> (actually located in <a href="/wiki/Ewing,_New_Jersey" class="mw-redirect" title="Ewing, New Jersey">Ewing</a>, outside <a href="/wiki/Trenton,_New_Jersey" title="Trenton, New Jersey">Trenton</a>, both in <a href="/wiki/Mercer_County,_New_Jersey" title="Mercer County, New Jersey">Mercer County</a>)</li></ul>
<p>Trenton–Mercer is an example of a marketing decision in which a small airport tries to associate itself with a larger city. <a href="/wiki/Ryanair" title="Ryanair">Ryanair</a> has been criticized for promoting names for airports unusually far from the city from which they are named, such as <a href="/wiki/Paris_Beauvais_Till%C3%A9_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Paris Beauvais Tillé Airport">Paris Beauvais Tillé Airport</a> (a triple name) and <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt-Hahn_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Frankfurt-Hahn Airport">Frankfurt-Hahn Airport</a>.
</p><p>Binomial names may be seen in German-language texts to denominate parts of towns:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Belsen_(Bergen)" title="Belsen (Bergen)">Bergen-Belsen</a> (the Belsen section within the municipality of Bergen: this form is now fixed in English when referring to the Nazi concentration camp and the present memorial there)</li>
<li><i>Berlin-Charlottenburg</i>: the district of <a href="/wiki/Charlottenburg" title="Charlottenburg">Charlottenburg</a>, Berlin</li></ul>
<p>The word "and" in its name does not always signify the union of two distinct territories:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Sala_y_G%C3%B3mez" class="mw-redirect" title="Sala y Gómez">Sala y Gómez</a>: one island named for two people</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lewis_and_Clark_County,_Montana" title="Lewis and Clark County, Montana">Lewis and Clark County, Montana</a>: named for <a href="/wiki/Meriwether_Lewis" title="Meriwether Lewis">Meriwether Lewis</a> and <a href="/wiki/William_Clark_(explorer)" class="mw-redirect" title="William Clark (explorer)">William Clark</a></li></ul>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Dual_naming" title="Dual naming">dual naming</a>, words in two different languages have been joined by a hyphen or a slash to become the community's (or geographic feature's) official name, often because of <a href="/wiki/Language_politics" title="Language politics">language politics</a>:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Vitoria-Gasteiz" title="Vitoria-Gasteiz">Vitoria-Gasteiz</a>: the combination of this city's Spanish name of <i>Vitoria</i> and <a href="/wiki/Basque_language" title="Basque language">Basque</a> name of <i>Gasteiz</i></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dingle/Daingean_U%C3%AD_Ch%C3%BAis" class="mw-redirect" title="Dingle/Daingean Uí Chúis">Dingle/Daingean Uí Chúis</a>: proposed official name (combining English and Irish-language names) of a town in the <a href="/wiki/County_Kerry" title="County Kerry">County Kerry</a> <a href="/wiki/Gaeltacht" title="Gaeltacht">Gaeltacht</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Aoraki_/_Mount_Cook" title="Aoraki / Mount Cook">Aoraki / Mount Cook</a>: mountain in New Zealand with <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_language" title="Māori language">Māori</a> and English names combined. Many geographic features of New Zealand are officially designated in a similar way (and the country as a whole is sometimes unofficially referred to as "<a href="/wiki/Aotearoa" title="Aotearoa">Aotearoa</a> New Zealand").</li></ul>
<p>Similarly, places may simply have an official name which consists of two names, such as the Australian territory of the <a href="/wiki/Cocos_(Keeling)_Islands" title="Cocos (Keeling) Islands">Cocos (Keeling) Islands</a>, which consists of the North Keeling Island and the South Keeling Islands.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transitional_names">Transitional names</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_double_placenames&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transitional names">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sometimes names will be concatenated during a name change.  <a href="/wiki/Zimbabwe_Rhodesia" title="Zimbabwe Rhodesia">Zimbabwe Rhodesia</a> was the name of the former Rhodesia and future Zimbabwe from June 1 to December 12, 1979.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Polycentric_metropolitan_areas">Polycentric metropolitan areas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_double_placenames&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Polycentric metropolitan areas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_area" title="Metropolitan area">Metropolitan areas</a> composed of multiple cities and shared facilities are often collectively named or referred to with the names of the major cities that comprise them. These are conjoined with an unspaced <a href="/wiki/En_dash" class="mw-redirect" title="En dash">en dash</a> in formal writing, though not journalism, which hyphenates. Some examples include:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Adana-Mersin_Metropolitan_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Adana-Mersin Metropolitan Area">Adana-Mersin Metropolitan Area</a>, Turkey</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Randstad" title="Randstad">Amsterdam–The Hague–Rotterdam</a>, Netherlands (also known collectively as the Randstad)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Baltimore%E2%80%93Washington_metropolitan_area" title="Baltimore–Washington metropolitan area">Baltimore–Washington metropolitan area</a>, Maryland and DC, United States</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Champaign%E2%80%93Urbana_metropolitan_area" title="Champaign–Urbana metropolitan area">Champaign–Urbana</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_Illinois_at_Urbana%E2%80%93Champaign" title="University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign">University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign</a>, US</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dallas%E2%80%93Fort_Worth_metroplex" title="Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex">Dallas–Fort Worth</a>, Texas, US</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gulfport%E2%80%93Biloxi_metropolitan_area" title="Gulfport–Biloxi metropolitan area">Gulfport–Biloxi</a>, Mississippi, US</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Minneapolis%E2%80%93Saint_Paul" title="Minneapolis–Saint Paul">Minneapolis–St. Paul</a>, Minnesota, US</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Keihanshin" title="Keihanshin">Osaka–Kobe–Kyoto</a>, Japan (a.k.a. Keihanshin)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Research_Triangle" title="Research Triangle">Raleigh–Durham–Chapel Hill</a>, North Carolina, US (a.k.a. the Research Triangle)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Seattle_metropolitan_area" title="Seattle metropolitan area">Seattle–Tacoma</a>, Washington (state), US</li></ul>
<p>Some may even be international <a href="/wiki/Conurbation" title="Conurbation">conurbations</a>, and do not exist as geopolitical entities:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Detroit%E2%80%93Windsor" title="Detroit–Windsor">Detroit–Windsor</a>, US and Canada</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/San_Diego%E2%80%93Tijuana" title="San Diego–Tijuana">San Diego–Tijuana</a>, US and Mexico</li></ul>
<p>In cases where one of the cities in the metropolitan area is itself conjoined, some other form of punctuation may be used to separate them, e.g <a href="/wiki/Scranton/Wilkes-Barre" class="mw-redirect" title="Scranton/Wilkes-Barre">Scranton/Wilkes-Barre</a>, consisting of the cities of <a href="/wiki/Scranton,_Pennsylvania" title="Scranton, Pennsylvania">Scranton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wilkes-Barre,_Pennsylvania" title="Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania">Wilkes-Barre</a>, Pennsylvania. 
</p>

<li><a href="/wiki/Matanuska-Susitna_Borough,_Alaska" title="Matanuska-Susitna Borough, Alaska">Matanuska-Susitna Borough, Alaska</a>: Named for <a href="/wiki/Matanuska_River" title="Matanuska River">Matanuska River</a> and the town of <a href="/wiki/Susitna,_Alaska" title="Susitna, Alaska">Susitna</a>.</li>
<li>More than half of the land area of <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> is within the <a href="/wiki/Unorganized_Borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Unorganized Borough">Unorganized Borough</a> which is administered directly by the state.  Therefore, the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Government">United States Government</a> considers the census areas within the Unorganized Borough to be <a href="/wiki/County-equivalent" class="mw-redirect" title="County-equivalent">county-equivalent</a> entities. <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.itl.nist.gov/fipspubs/co-codes/ak.txt">[1]</a>  Four of these have double (or triple) names:
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Wales%E2%80%93Hyder_Census_Area,_Alaska" title="Prince of Wales–Hyder Census Area, Alaska">Prince of Wales – Hyder Census Area</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hoonah%E2%80%93Angoon_Census_Area,_Alaska" title="Hoonah–Angoon Census Area, Alaska">Hoonah–Angoon Census Area</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Valdez%E2%80%93Cordova_Census_Area,_Alaska" title="Valdez–Cordova Census Area, Alaska">Valdez–Cordova Census Area</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Yukon%E2%80%93Koyukuk_Census_Area,_Alaska" title="Yukon–Koyukuk Census Area, Alaska">Yukon–Koyukuk Census Area</a></li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Norwood_Young_America,_Minnesota" title="Norwood Young America, Minnesota">Norwood Young America, Minnesota</a>, formed in 1997 when the cities of Norwood and Young America merged.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sedro-Woolley,_Washington" title="Sedro-Woolley, Washington">Sedro-Woolley, Washington</a>, formed in 1898 from towns Sedro and Woolley</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Soddy-Daisy,_Tennessee" title="Soddy-Daisy, Tennessee">Soddy-Daisy, Tennessee</a>, created in 1969 by the incorporation of the former communities of Soddy and Daisy, plus some surrounding areas</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Texarkana" class="mw-redirect" title="Texarkana">Texarkana</a>, on the border between <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arkansas" title="Arkansas">Arkansas</a>, and near the triple point of those two states with <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">Louisiana</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Winston-Salem,_North_Carolina" title="Winston-Salem, North Carolina">Winston-Salem, North Carolina</a>, created by the 1913 merger of the towns of Winston and Salem.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Wilkes-Barre,_Pennsylvania" title="Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania">Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania</a>, named after British Parliamentarians <a href="/wiki/John_Wilkes" title="John Wilkes">John Wilkes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isaac_Barr%C3%A9" title="Isaac Barré">Isaac Barré</a> who were sympathetic to colonial concerns</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_countries">Other countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_double_placenames&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Other countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Albury%E2%80%93Wodonga" title="Albury–Wodonga">Albury–Wodonga</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>: incorporating the twin cities of <a href="/wiki/Albury,_New_South_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="Albury, New South Wales">Albury</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wodonga,_Victoria" class="mw-redirect" title="Wodonga, Victoria">Wodonga</a>, divided by the <a href="/wiki/Murray_River" title="Murray River">Murray River</a> which marks the border between states</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Brand%C3%BDs_nad_Labem-Star%C3%A1_Boleslav" title="Brandýs nad Labem-Stará Boleslav">Brandýs nad Labem-Stará Boleslav</a>, <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>: created in 1960 from the merger of two towns (see also <a href="https://cs.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kategorie:Obce_v_%C4%8Cesku_se_slo%C5%BEen%C3%BDm_n%C3%A1zvem" class="extiw" title="cs:Kategorie:Obce v Česku se složeným názvem">other Czech municipalities with hyphenated names</a>)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/D%C3%BAn_Laoghaire%E2%80%93Rathdown" title="Dún Laoghaire–Rathdown">Dún Laoghaire–Rathdown</a>: borough of <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BAn_Laoghaire" title="Dún Laoghaire">Dún Laoghaire</a> and barony of <a href="/wiki/Rathdown_(County_Wicklow_barony)" title="Rathdown (County Wicklow barony)">Rathdown</a> in Ireland</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Dysart_et_al,_Ontario" title="Dysart et al, Ontario">Corporation of the United Townships of Dysart, Dudley, Harcourt, Guilford, Harburn, Bruton, Havelock, Eyre and Clyde</a>: official name of a municipality in <a href="/wiki/Central_Ontario" title="Central Ontario">central</a> <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, Canada formed by the merger of nine smaller communities; more commonly known as "Dysart et al"</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Manawatu-Wanganui" title="Manawatu-Wanganui">Manawatu-Wanganui</a>: Region of New Zealand, combining the regions of Manawatu and Whanganui river catchments</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_and_Providencia" class="mw-redirect" title="San Andrés and Providencia">San Andrés and Providencia</a>: <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_(island)" title="San Andrés (island)">San Andrés Island</a> and <a href="/wiki/Providencia_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Providencia Island">Providencia Island</a> in Colombia</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Skan%C3%B6r_med_Falsterbo" title="Skanör med Falsterbo">Skanör med Falsterbo</a>: a city formed from the conurbation of the two previous cities Skanör and Falsterbo in southwesternmost Sweden.</li></ul>
