<p><b>Mirella Lapata</b> <a href="/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society_of_Edinburgh" class="mw-redirect" title="Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh">FRSE</a> is a computer scientist and Professor in the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh_School_of_Informatics" title="University of Edinburgh School of Informatics">School of Informatics</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Edinburgh" title="University of Edinburgh">University of Edinburgh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Working on the general problem of extracting semantic information from large bodies of text, Lapata develops computer algorithms and models in the field of <a href="/wiki/Natural_language_processing" title="Natural language processing">natural language processing</a> (NLP).<sup id="cite_ref-gs_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gs-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirella_Lapata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lapata obtained an <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts" title="Master of Arts">Master of Arts</a> (MA) degree from <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">Carnegie Mellon University</a> and subsequently earned a doctorate from the University of Edinburgh.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Lapata's doctoral research investigated the acquisition of information from <a href="/wiki/Polysemous" class="mw-redirect" title="Polysemous">polysemous</a> linguistic units using <a href="/wiki/Probabilistic_method" title="Probabilistic method">probabilistic methods</a> supervised by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Lascarides&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alex Lascarides (page does not exist)">Alex Lascarides</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://www.wikidata.org/wiki/Q62022331#sitelinks-wikipedia" class="extiw" title="d:Q62022331"><span title="&quot;Alex Lascarides&quot; in other languages">Wikidata</span></a>&#93;</span>, Chris Brew and Steve Finch.<sup id="cite_ref-mphd_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mphd-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career_and_research">Career and research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirella_Lapata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career and research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After her doctorate, Lapata assumed academic positions at <a href="/wiki/Saarland_University" title="Saarland University">Saarland University</a> and at the Department of Computer Science at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Sheffield" title="University of Sheffield">University of Sheffield</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> At the University of Edinburgh she became a <a href="/wiki/Reader_(academic_rank)" title="Reader (academic rank)">reader</a> in the School of Informatics where she is a full Professor and holds a personal chair in natural language processing.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Lapata is a member of the Human Communication Research Center and Institute for Language, Cognition and Computation, both in Edinburgh.<sup id="cite_ref-MitchellLapata2010_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MitchellLapata2010-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Between 2015 and 2017, Lapata served as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society" title="Royal Society">Royal Society</a> Machine Learning Working Group.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Recently<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (March 2019)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Lapata was granted a <a href="/wiki/European_Research_Council" title="European Research Council">European Research Council</a> (ERC) Consolidator Grant worth €1.9M to fund five years of her project, TransModal: Translating from Multiple Modalities into Text.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_honors">Awards and honors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mirella_Lapata&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Awards and honors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>In 2009 Lapata became the first recipient of the <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> <a href="/wiki/British_Computer_Society" title="British Computer Society">British Computer Society</a> (BCS)/BCS IRSG Karen Spärck Jones Award.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The award recognizes achievement in furthering the progress in <a href="/wiki/Information_retrieval" title="Information retrieval">information retrieval</a> and natural language processing; the award commemorates the life and work of <a href="/wiki/Karen_Sp%C3%A4rck_Jones" title="Karen Spärck Jones">Karen Spärck Jones</a>.</li>
<li>In 2012 Lapata won an <a href="/wiki/Empirical_Methods_in_Natural_Language_Processing" title="Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing">Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing</a> (EMNLP)-CoNLL 2012 Best Reviewer Award.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In 2016 Lapata, with Eneko Agirre and Sebastian Riedel, won the EMNLP Best Data Set Paper Award.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In 2018 Lapata was awarded, alongside Li Dong, an <a href="/wiki/Association_for_Computational_Linguistics" title="Association for Computational Linguistics">Association for Computational Linguistics</a> (ACL) Best Paper Honorable Mention.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In 2019 Lapata was elected a Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Society_of_Edinburgh" title="Royal Society of Edinburgh">Royal Society of Edinburgh</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
