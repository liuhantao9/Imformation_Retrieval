	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Joseph Woodhead</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		
		
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>

<b>Joseph Woodhead</b> (1824 - 21 May 1913) was an English newspaper proprietor and editor and a <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal Party</a> politician.
</p><p>Woodhead was the youngest son of Godfrey Woodhead, a currier and leather merchant of <a href="/wiki/Holmfirth" title="Holmfirth">Holmfirth</a>. He was educated at private schools but grew up in a home where books and reading were valued. At fifteen he was apprenticed to a woollen manufacturer working all day and studying until late at night. He was a teetotaller and spoke at temperance meeting. In 1851 with a group of like-minded local Liberals he founded the <i>Huddersfield Examiner</i> newspaper<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> which was  produced in a room over a shop in Kirkgate.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  He made it into a leading exponent of non-conformist liberalism and in 1871 it became a daily evening paper.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Woodhead also established the <i>Dewsbury Reporter</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1868, he was elected to Huddersfield Town Council and became an Alderman of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> and was twice Mayor of Huddersfield in 1876 and 1877.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Huddersfield Town Hall, known as a concert venue, was sited opposite his newspaper offices and in 1885 he was president of the Glee and Madrigal Society - now the Huddersfield Singers.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  He was also a <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_Peace" class="mw-redirect" title="Justice of the Peace">J. P.</a> <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/1885_United_Kingdom_general_election" title="1885 United Kingdom general election">1885 general election</a> Woodhead was elected as the <a href="/wiki/Member_of_Parliament" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of Parliament">Member of Parliament</a> (MP) for <a href="/wiki/Spen_Valley_(UK_Parliament_constituency)" title="Spen Valley (UK Parliament constituency)">Spen Valley</a>. He held the seat until 1892.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He is said to have turned down a baronetcy as he had no wish to be known as other than Joe Woodhead.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 1898 he was awarded Freedom of the City of Huddersfield.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Census_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Census in the United Kingdom">1891 Census of Huddersfield</a> Woodhead is listed as a 66-year-old Newspaper proprietor, Justice of the Peace and Member of Parliament living at Longdenholme, West Hill, Huddersfield with his four sons.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Woodhead lived at Longdenholme, Huddersfield and at Preswylfa, Conway. He died in Huddersfield at the age of 89.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Woodhead married Catherine Woodhead in 1853. Their son <a href="/wiki/German_Sims_Woodhead" title="German Sims Woodhead">Sir German Sims Woodhead</a> became an eminent pathologist.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Another son Ernest Woodhead played rugby for England.
</p>
