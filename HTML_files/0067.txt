<p>The <b>Rolls family</b> were substantial landowners and benefactors in and around <a href="/wiki/Monmouth" title="Monmouth">Monmouth</a> in south east <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>.  <a href="/wiki/Charles_Stewart_Rolls" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles Stewart Rolls">Charles Stewart Rolls</a> was the co-founder of the <a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_Limited" title="Rolls-Royce Limited">Rolls-Royce</a> company.  The ascent of the family to the aristocracy was through marriage.
</p><p>The family's <a href="/wiki/Coat_of_arms" title="Coat of arms">arms</a> were described in 1852 as:<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<blockquote><p>Quarterly: 1st and 4th, or, on a fesse, dancett√©e, with plain cotises between three billets, sa., each charged with a lion, rampant, of the field, as many bezants; 2nd, gu., an eagle, displayed, barry of six, erminois and az.; 3rd, or, a saltier, sa., in chief, a leopard's face, of the second.</p></blockquote>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="James_James_and_his_descendants">James James and his descendants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolls_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: James James and his descendants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>James James of the parish of <a href="/wiki/Llanvihangel-Ystern-Llewern" title="Llanvihangel-Ystern-Llewern">Llanvihangel-Ystern-Llewern</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monmouthshire" title="Monmouthshire">Monmouthshire</a>, son of John James the Elder, purchased several farms and parcels of land in Llanvihangel-Ystern-Llewern and <a href="/wiki/Llangattock-Vibon-Avel" title="Llangattock-Vibon-Avel">Llangattock-Vibon-Avel</a> between 1639 and 1648.  He settled in London and acquired a valuable estate in along the area of <a href="/wiki/Old_Kent_Road" title="Old Kent Road">Old Kent Road</a> between the parishes of Bermondsey and Camberwell (both now part of Southwark) Surrey.<sup id="cite_ref-Archive_Wales_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Archive_Wales-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In his will published in 1677 he left his estate to his only surviving child, Sarah, who was the wife of Dr Elisha Coysh, a physician from London.<sup id="cite_ref-Archive_Wales_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Archive_Wales-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Their daughter married William Allen, who also bought land in Monmouthshire.<sup id="cite_ref-Archive_Wales_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Archive_Wales-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> William Allen's daughter and heir married her cousin Thomas Coysh.<sup id="cite_ref-Archive_Wales_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Archive_Wales-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><br />

<p>They were succeeded by their son Richard, who was succeeded by his sister <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Coysh" title="Sarah Coysh">Sarah Coysh</a> (d. 1801), the eventual sole heir of the families of Coysh, Allen and James. Sarah married <a href="/wiki/John_Rolls" title="John Rolls">John Rolls</a> (1735‚Äì1801) of the Grange, <a href="/wiki/Bermondsey" title="Bermondsey">Bermondsey</a>, and of the Hendre, Monmouthshire, <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_Monmouthshire" class="mw-redirect" title="High Sheriff of Monmouthshire">High Sheriff of Monmouthshire</a> in 1794, bringing him much property both in Monmouthshire and London. John Rolls died the day after his wife.

<h2><span id="John_Rolls_and_.22The_Hendre.22"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="John_Rolls_and_&quot;The_Hendre&quot;">John Rolls and "The Hendre"</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolls_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: John Rolls and &quot;The Hendre&quot;">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Hendre" title="The Hendre">The Hendre</a></div>
<p>John was succeeded by his son <a href="/wiki/John_Rolls_of_The_Hendre" title="John Rolls of The Hendre">John Rolls</a> (1776‚Äì1837) who, with his wife Martha, built the finest country estate in Monmouthshire: The Hendre (<a href="/wiki/Welsh_language" title="Welsh language">Welsh</a>: <i lang="cy">Yr Hendre</i>). It was a shooting lodge that was expanded throughout the next one hundred years.  It is Monmouthshire's only full-scale <a href="/wiki/Victorian_era" title="Victorian era">Victorian</a> <a href="/wiki/Country_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Country house">country house</a>, constructed in the <a href="/wiki/Victorian_Gothic" class="mw-redirect" title="Victorian Gothic">Victorian Gothic</a> style.  It is located in the <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">parish</a> of Llangattock-Vibon-Avel, some 4 miles (6.4&#160;km) north-west of the town of Monmouth. The first of three expansions by the Rolls family began with the architect <a href="/wiki/George_Vaughan_Maddox" title="George Vaughan Maddox">George Vaughan Maddox</a> who rebuilt parts of the south wing in 1830. John Rolls' successor, John Etherington Welch Rolls, continued the mansion's development, using <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Henry_Wyatt" title="Thomas Henry Wyatt">Thomas Henry Wyatt</a> as his architect.  
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="John_Etherington_Welch_Rolls">John Etherington Welch Rolls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolls_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: John Etherington Welch Rolls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/John_Etherington_Welch_Rolls" title="John Etherington Welch Rolls">John Etherington Welch Rolls</a></div>

<p>John was succeeded by his son John Etherington Welch Rolls (4 May 1807 ‚Äì 27 May 1870), <a href="/wiki/High_Sheriff_of_Monmouthshire" class="mw-redirect" title="High Sheriff of Monmouthshire">sheriff of Monmouthshire</a>. In 1842 he married Elizabeth Mary Long, granddaughter of <a href="/wiki/William_Carnegie,_7th_Earl_of_Northesk" title="William Carnegie, 7th Earl of Northesk">William Carnegie, 7th Earl of Northesk</a>. He was succeeded by his son, also named John.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="John_Allan_Rolls">John Allan Rolls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolls_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: John Allan Rolls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/John_Rolls,_1st_Baron_Llangattock" title="John Rolls, 1st Baron Llangattock">John Rolls, 1st Baron Llangattock</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Monmouth_Library_First_Baron_Llangattock.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Monmouth_Library_First_Baron_Llangattock.jpg/220px-Monmouth_Library_First_Baron_Llangattock.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="347" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Monmouth_Library_First_Baron_Llangattock.jpg/330px-Monmouth_Library_First_Baron_Llangattock.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Monmouth_Library_First_Baron_Llangattock.jpg/440px-Monmouth_Library_First_Baron_Llangattock.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1080" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Monmouth_Library_First_Baron_Llangattock.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/John_Rolls,_1st_Baron_Llangattock" title="John Rolls, 1st Baron Llangattock">John Rolls, 1st Baron Llangattock</a></div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/John_Rolls,_1st_Baron_Llangattock" title="John Rolls, 1st Baron Llangattock">John Allan Rolls</a> (19 February 1837 ‚Äì 24 September 1912), sheriff in 1875 and MP for Monmouthshire, 1880‚Äì1885, created <a href="/wiki/Baron_Llangattock" title="Baron Llangattock">Baron Llangattock</a> of the Hendre, 1892.
</p><p>John Allan Rolls's ennoblement brought the family, and the house, to its social apogee, culminating in a visit from the Duke and Duchess of York (later <a href="/wiki/George_V_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="George V of England">King George V</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mary_of_Teck" title="Mary of Teck">Queen Mary</a>), who stayed with Lord and <a href="/wiki/Lady_Llangattock" class="mw-redirect" title="Lady Llangattock">Lady Llangattock</a> at the Hendre in late October&#160;‚Äì early November 1900. The Duke and Duchess were taken on motor car excursions by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Rolls" title="Charles Rolls">Charles Rolls</a>, probably the first time that the royal couple had been in a car. This was an important event in the family's social history, confirming their elevation to the upper echelons of society.
</p><p>Of John Allan's three sons, the most famous was Charles, the co-founder of Rolls Royce. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charles_Rolls">Charles Rolls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolls_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Charles Rolls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Charles_Rolls" title="Charles Rolls">Charles Rolls</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Charles-Rolls.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Charles-Rolls.jpg/220px-Charles-Rolls.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="240" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Charles-Rolls.jpg/330px-Charles-Rolls.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0a/Charles-Rolls.jpg 2x" data-file-width="413" data-file-height="450" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Charles-Rolls.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Charles_Stewart_Rolls" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles Stewart Rolls">Charles Stewart Rolls</a></div></div></div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Charles_Stewart_Rolls" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles Stewart Rolls">Charles Stewart Rolls</a> (27 August 1877 - 12 July 1910) was a motoring and aviation pioneer. Together with <a href="/wiki/Henry_Royce" title="Henry Royce">Frederick Henry Royce</a> he co-founded the <a href="/wiki/Rolls-Royce_Limited" title="Rolls-Royce Limited">Rolls-Royce</a> car manufacturing firm. He was the first Briton to be killed in a flying accident, when the tail of his <a href="/wiki/Wright_Flyer" title="Wright Flyer">Wright Flyer</a> broke off during a flying display near <a href="/wiki/Bournemouth" title="Bournemouth">Bournemouth</a>, England. He was aged 32.
As well as his interests in cars and aeroplanes, Charles was a keen balloonist, the Flight Magazine of January 1909 recording a flight from Monmouth:
</p>
<blockquote><p><i>Ballooning Home</i>. On Saturday last the Hon. C. S. Rolls gave an exhibition of the possibilities of ballooning by taking his mother, Lady Llangattock, home by balloon. The ascent was made at Monmouth in the balloon " Mercury," the occupants of the basket being Lady Llangattock, Hon. C. S. Rolls, Hon. Mrs. Assheton&#160;‚Äì Harbord, Mr. Claud Crompton, and Mr. Charles Freeman, and the balloon landed on the lawn in front of Lord Llangattock's house, The Hendre.</p></blockquote>
<p>In July 1910, Charles Rolls was killed when his plane crashed during a flight display at Bournemouth, the first Englishman to be killed in a flying accident.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="John_Maclean_Rolls">John Maclean Rolls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolls_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: John Maclean Rolls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lord Llangattock died in 1912.  His heir was his first son <a href="/wiki/John_Maclean_Rolls,_2nd_Baron_Llangattock" class="mw-redirect" title="John Maclean Rolls, 2nd Baron Llangattock">John Maclean Rolls</a> (1870‚Äì1916, dsp.), 2nd Baron Llangattock. He died at Boulogne in 1916 from wounds received at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Somme" title="Battle of the Somme">Battle of the Somme</a>.  With his younger brother, Henry Alan, having died four months previously, and none of Lord Llangattock's three sons having had children, the direct male line ended. John Maclean Rolls was succeeded by his sister Eleanor Georgiana (d. 1961), wife of John Courtown Edward Shelley.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Harding-Rolls">Harding-Rolls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rolls_family&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Harding-Rolls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With the death of Charles Rolls, Henry Allen Rolls and John Maclean Rolls and the extinction of the barony and surname in the male line, the estate passed back up the family through the closest member of the family with surviving descendants, Patricia Rolls, sister of John Allan Rolls. She had married John Taylor Harding of Pentwyn, vicar of Rockfield and Canon of Llandaff, son of John Harding of Henbury and Jane Morgan, and they had four children (John Reginald Harding, Charles Allan Harding, Francis Henry Harding and George Valentine Harding). John Reginald Harding in turn married Elizabeth Margaret Saunders (daughter of Captain John Saunders of <a href="/wiki/Fuzhou" title="Fuzhou">Fuzhou</a>, China) and had five children. Upon the extinction of the Rolls branch, the estate came down to his son, John Charles Etherington Harding (born 1898, <a href="/wiki/Xiamen" title="Xiamen">Xiamen</a>), the first cousin once removed of the 2nd Baron Llangattock, who inherited the house, estate and surrounding farmland, changing his family name to Harding-Rolls for this purpose.
</p>

<p>The Harding-Rolls branch of the family continued to live at The Hendre until 30 August 1984 when, following a failed <a href="/wiki/Time-share" class="mw-redirect" title="Time-share">time-share</a> operation, it was sold to Effold Properties Limited. The mansion is now the focal point of the Rolls of Monmouth golf course.
</p>
