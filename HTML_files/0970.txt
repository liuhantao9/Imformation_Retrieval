<p>The <b>Navy Cross</b> is the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> military's second-highest <a href="/wiki/Military_decoration" class="mw-redirect" title="Military decoration">decoration</a> awarded for <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/valor" class="extiw" title="wikt:valor">valor</a> in combat. The Navy Cross is awarded primarily to a member of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">Marine Corps</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">Coast Guard</a> (when operating under the Department of the Navy) for extraordinary heroism.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The medal is equivalent to the Army <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Cross_(United_States)" title="Distinguished Service Cross (United States)">Distinguished Service Cross</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Air_Force_Cross_(United_States)" title="Air Force Cross (United States)">Air Force Cross</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Coast_Guard_Cross" title="Coast Guard Cross">Coast Guard Cross</a>.
</p><p>The Navy Cross is bestowed by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_the_Navy" title="United States Secretary of the Navy">Secretary of the Navy</a> and may also be awarded to members of the other armed services, and to foreign military personnel while serving with the U.S. naval services. The Navy Cross was established by Act of Congress (<a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/United_States_Statutes_at_Large/Volume_40/65th_Congress/3rd_Session/Chapter_14" class="extiw" title="s:United States Statutes at Large/Volume 40/65th Congress/3rd Session/Chapter 14">Public Law 65-253</a>) and approved on February 4, 1919.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navy_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Navy Cross was instituted in part due to the entrance of the United States into <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>. Many European nations had the custom of decorating heroes from other nations, but the <a href="/wiki/Medal_of_Honor" title="Medal of Honor">Medal of Honor</a> was the sole U.S. award for valor at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-NHC_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NHC-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Army instituted the <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Cross_(United_States)" title="Distinguished Service Cross (United States)">Distinguished Service Cross</a> and <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Medal_(U.S._Army)" title="Distinguished Service Medal (U.S. Army)">Distinguished Service Medal</a> in 1918, while the Navy followed suit in 1919, retroactive to 6 April 1917. Originally, the Navy Cross was lower in precedence than the Medal of Honor and the <a href="/wiki/Navy_Distinguished_Service_Medal" class="mw-redirect" title="Navy Distinguished Service Medal">Navy Distinguished Service Medal</a>, because it was awarded for both combat heroism and for "other distinguished service."<sup id="cite_ref-NHC_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NHC-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Congress revised this on 7 August 1942, making the Navy Cross a combat-only decoration that follows the Medal of Honor in order of precedence. Since the medal was established, it has been awarded more than 6,300 times.<sup id="cite_ref-NHC_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NHC-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It was designed by <a href="/wiki/James_Earle_Fraser_(sculptor)" title="James Earle Fraser (sculptor)">James Earle Fraser</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NHC_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NHC-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Since the <a href="/wiki/11_September_attacks" class="mw-redirect" title="11 September attacks">11 September attacks</a> the Navy Cross has been awarded 47 times, with two of them having the name of the recipient held in secret.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> One of those secret awardings was due to actions during the <a href="/wiki/2012_Benghazi_attack" title="2012 Benghazi attack">2012 Benghazi attack</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first actual recipient of the Navy Cross is unknown because initial awards were made from a lengthy list published after <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria">Criteria</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navy_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Criteria">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Navy Cross may be awarded to any member of the U.S. Armed Forces while serving with the Navy, Marine Corps, or Coast Guard (when under the Department of the Navy) who distinguishes himself or herself in action by extraordinary heroism not justifying an award of the <a href="/wiki/Medal_of_Honor" title="Medal of Honor">Medal of Honor</a>. The action must take place under one of three circumstances:
</p>
<ol><li>In combat action while engaged against an enemy of the United States; or,</li>
<li>In combat action while engaged in military operations involving conflict with an opposing foreign force; or,</li>
<li>In combat action while serving with friendly foreign forces, who are engaged in armed conflict in which the United States is not a belligerent party.</li></ol>
<p>The act(s) to be commended must be performed in the presence of great danger, or at great personal risk, and must be performed in such a manner as to render the individual's action(s) highly conspicuous among others of equal grade, rate, experience, or position of responsibility. An accumulation of minor acts of heroism does not justify an award of the Navy Cross.  As originally authorized, the Navy Cross could be awarded for distinguished non-combat acts, but legislation of 7 August 1942 limited the award to acts of combat heroism.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wear">Wear</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Navy_Cross&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wear">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Navy Cross originally was the Navy's third-highest decoration, after the <a href="/wiki/Medal_of_Honor" title="Medal of Honor">Medal of Honor</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Navy_Distinguished_Service_Medal" class="mw-redirect" title="Navy Distinguished Service Medal">Navy Distinguished Service Medal</a>. On 7 August 1942, Congress revised the order of precedence, placing the Navy Cross above the Distinguished Service Medal in precedence. Since that time, the Navy Cross has been worn after the Medal of Honor and before all other awards.
</p><p>Additional awards of the Navy Cross are denoted by gold or silver <a href="/wiki/5/16_inch_star" title="5/16 inch star">&#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>5</sup>&#8260;<sub>16</sub></span> inch stars</a> affixed to the suspension and <a href="/wiki/Service_ribbon" title="Service ribbon">service ribbon</a> of the medal. A gold star would be issued for each of the second through fifth awards, to be replaced by a silver star which would indicate a sixth award.  To date no one has received more than five awards.
</p>

<dl><dt>Medal</dt></dl>
<p>The earliest version of the Navy Cross (1919–1928) featured a more narrow strip of white, while the so-called "Black Widow" medals awarded from 1941 to 1942 were notable for the dark color due to over-<a href="/wiki/Anodizing" title="Anodizing">anodized</a> finish. The medal is similar in appearance to the British <a href="/wiki/Distinguished_Service_Cross_(United_Kingdom)" title="Distinguished Service Cross (United Kingdom)">Distinguished Service Cross</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NHC_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NHC-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Obverse:
The medal is a modified <a href="/wiki/Cross_patt%C3%A9e" title="Cross pattée">cross pattée</a> one and a half inches wide. The ends of its arms are rounded whereas a conventional cross patée has arms that are straight on the end. There are four laurel leaves with berries in each of the re-entrant arms of the cross. In the center of the cross a sailing vessel is depicted on waves, sailing to the viewer's left. The vessel is a symbolic <a href="/wiki/Caravel" title="Caravel">caravel</a> of the type used between 1480 and 1500. Fraser selected the caravel because it was a symbol often used by the Naval Academy and because it represented both naval service and the tradition of the sea. The laurel leaves with berries refer to achievement.
</p><p>Reverse:
In the center of the medal, a bronze cross pattée, one and a half inches wide, are crossed anchors from the pre-1850 period, with cables attached. The letters USN are evident amid the anchors.
</p>
<dl><dt>Service Ribbon</dt></dl>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Service_ribbon" title="Service ribbon">service ribbon</a> is <a href="/wiki/Navy_blue" title="Navy blue">navy blue</a> with a center stripe of <a href="/wiki/White" title="White">white</a> identical to the suspension ribbon of the medal. The blue alludes to naval service; the white represents the purity of selflessness.
</p>
