<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sowerby_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town was originally a fording point over the once much-wider <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a> where it joins the <a href="/wiki/River_Ryburn" title="River Ryburn">River Ryburn</a>. The town takes its name from the historic bridge which spans the river in the town centre. Before the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a> the area was divided between the parishes of Sowerby, Norland, Skircoat and Warley. The boundaries between them being the rivers Calder and <a href="/wiki/River_Ryburn" title="River Ryburn">Ryburn</a> and Warley Clough, which is now largely culverted. Textiles and engineering industry grew up around the bridge. By the mid-19th century the population had grown and the settlement became an urban district in the <a href="/wiki/West_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="West Riding of Yorkshire">West Riding of Yorkshire</a> in 1894.
</p><p>From 1892 to 1930 Pollit &amp; Wigzell manufactured stationary <a href="/wiki/Steam_engines" class="mw-redirect" title="Steam engines">steam engines</a> for the cotton and woollen mills of Yorkshire, Lancashire and India. Wood Brothers, an engineering and <a href="/wiki/Millwright" title="Millwright">millwright</a> company, also produced engines from its Valley Iron Works. The <a href="/wiki/Markfield_Beam_Engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Markfield Beam Engine">Markfield Beam Engine</a> in north <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> is an example of its work.
</p><p>Council buildings on Hollins Mill Lane, the old swimming pool, council offices and fire station will be transferred to a community group, Sowerby Bridge Fire and Water, and will be renovated for community use.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Christ_Church">Christ Church</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sowerby_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Christ Church">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Anglican <a href="/wiki/Parish_church" title="Parish church">parish church</a>, Christ Church, situated on Wharf Street, is a Grade II <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed building</a>. It was built in 1819 by John Oates. The <a href="/wiki/Chancel" title="Chancel">chancel</a> was rebuilt in 1873–74 and the church was re-roofed 1894.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The church is still active and the vicar is Revd. Canon Angela Dick.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first organ, built by John Ward of <a href="/wiki/York" title="York">York</a>, was installed in 1825, four years after the church opened. It was situated in the gallery above what is now the parish room. Ward also built the organ in the <a href="/wiki/Square_Chapel" title="Square Chapel">Halifax Square Chapel</a> in 1821. This organ was dismantled in 1865 by <a href="/wiki/Conacher_and_Co" title="Conacher and Co">Conacher</a> of <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>. Conacher installed a replacement at a cost of £266 (equivalent to £25,000 in 2018).<sup id="cite_ref-inflation-UK_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-UK-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 1873–74 the church was extended and the organ was moved to its present location. In 1894, however, a major fire started from a candle in the organ and destroyed most of the chancel and nave roof, as well as the organ itself.<sup id="cite_ref-chrstchurch_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chrstchurch-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As part of the church reconstruction a new and considerably larger organ was provided by <a href="/wiki/Abbott_and_Smith" title="Abbott and Smith">Abbott and Smith</a> of <a href="/wiki/Leeds" title="Leeds">Leeds</a>. The organ saw no repairs until 1979, by when the church was unable to afford any restoration. Between 1979 and 1983 a further replacement organ, of some quality, was moved from the now-closed Bolton Brow <a href="/wiki/Methodist_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Methodist Church">Methodist Church</a>. This had been originally built by <a href="/wiki/James_Jepson_Binns" title="James Jepson Binns">James Jepson Binns</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bramley,_Leeds" title="Bramley, Leeds">Bramley, Leeds</a>. Its bellows had originally been pumped by a water engine connected to the <a href="/wiki/Rochdale_Canal" title="Rochdale Canal">Rochdale Canal</a> below. The present organ retains the fine oak case and four stops of pipes from the Abbott and Smith organ and incorporates three stops from the organ at <a href="/wiki/Warley_Town" title="Warley Town">Warley</a> <a href="/wiki/Congregational_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Congregational Church">Congregational Church</a>. The instrument was converted to electric control between the keyboards and pipes. Some restoration work was completed in 2006 and a recent bequest, from the late Fred Seed, has been used to cover the cost of further work.<sup id="cite_ref-chrstchurch_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chrstchurch-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Governance">Governance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sowerby_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Governance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is part of the Sowerby Bridge <a href="/wiki/Wards_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Wards of the United Kingdom">ward</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">Metropolitan borough</a> of <a href="/wiki/Calderdale" title="Calderdale">Calderdale</a>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_county" title="Metropolitan county">Metropolitan county</a> of <a href="/wiki/West_Yorkshire" title="West Yorkshire">West Yorkshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sowerby_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sowerby Bridge is situated about 3 miles (4.8&#160;km) from <a href="/wiki/Halifax,_West_Yorkshire" title="Halifax, West Yorkshire">Halifax</a> town centre. It is at the confluence of the <a href="/wiki/River_Calder,_West_Yorkshire" title="River Calder, West Yorkshire">River Calder</a> and <a href="/wiki/River_Ryburn" title="River Ryburn">River Ryburn</a>, and the name Sowerby Bridge references its situation as a crossing point over the River Calder to the older settlement at <a href="/wiki/Sowerby,_West_Yorkshire" title="Sowerby, West Yorkshire">Sowerby</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sowerby_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are two high schools in Sowerby Bridge.
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Ryburn_Valley_High_School" title="Ryburn Valley High School">Ryburn Valley High School</a> on St Peter's Avenue</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge_High_School" class="mw-redirect" title="Sowerby Bridge High School">Sowerby Bridge High School</a> on Albert Road</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sowerby_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is served by <a href="/wiki/Sowerby_Bridge_railway_station" title="Sowerby Bridge railway station">Sowerby Bridge railway station</a>, which sees a regular service to Manchester, Bradford and Leeds on the <a href="/wiki/Caldervale_Line" class="mw-redirect" title="Caldervale Line">Caldervale Line</a>. Unfortunately this station has no public toilets although there are many interesting information panels about the local area.
</p><p>The town is at the junction of the <a href="/wiki/Calder_and_Hebble_Navigation" title="Calder and Hebble Navigation">Calder and Hebble Navigation</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rochdale_Canal" title="Rochdale Canal">Rochdale Canal</a>; <a href="/wiki/Tuel_Lane_Lock" title="Tuel Lane Lock">Tuel Lane Lock</a> on the Rochdale Canal is the deepest lock in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-PW_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PW-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The canal basin and warehouses where the canals meet, Sowerby Bridge Wharf, are <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed buildings</a> and house the Moorings Bar and Restaurant, 12-04 Restaurant and Temujin Mongolian Restaurant. The basin is the headquarters of the 12th Halifax <a href="/wiki/Sea_Scouts" class="mw-redirect" title="Sea Scouts">Sea Scouts</a> (M.o.D. No. 54 Royal Navy recognised) where <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">Prince Charles</a> opened the William Andrew Memorial Headquarters. Shire Cruisers run holiday hire canal barges, build narrow boats and provide mooring facilities.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sowerby_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sowerby Bridge features in <a href="/wiki/George_Gissing" title="George Gissing">George Gissing</a>'s 1890 novel <i>The Emancipated</i> as the hometown of the protagonist Ross Mallard.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The canal basin was used as a filming location for the <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> comedy-drama <i><a href="/wiki/Stay_Lucky" title="Stay Lucky">Stay Lucky</a></i>, and the ITV drama <i><a href="/wiki/Dead_Clever" title="Dead Clever">Dead Clever</a></i> was set in the town. The 2014 <a href="/wiki/BBC_One" title="BBC One">BBC One</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/Happy_Valley_(TV_series)" title="Happy Valley (TV series)">Happy Valley</a></i> was set in Sowerby Bridge and parts of the series were filmed in and around the town.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Catherine's workplace is a former police station. Series writer and director <a href="/wiki/Sally_Wainwright" title="Sally Wainwright">Sally Wainwright</a> grew up in Sowerby Bridge.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traditions">Traditions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sowerby_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Traditions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Boxing_Day_floods_2015">Boxing Day floods 2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sowerby_Bridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Boxing Day floods 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Local river monitoring stations recorded a level of 3.55m between 12&#160;pm and 6&#160;pm, surpassing previous recorded highs of 2.43m (23 June 2012). Known flood plains, such as Dixie Woods and Sowerby Bridge Cricket Club, were submerged to an estimated depth of 2 feet (61&#160;cm). The floods were reported as the worst in the area since 1968.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
