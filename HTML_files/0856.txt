<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_United_States">In the United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_memorials_to_John_F._Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In the United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy_Memorial" title="John Fitzgerald Kennedy Memorial">John Fitzgerald Kennedy Memorial</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Eternal_Flame" title="John F. Kennedy Eternal Flame">The Eternal Flame</a>, JFK's final resting place at <a href="/wiki/Arlington_National_Cemetery" title="Arlington National Cemetery">Arlington National Cemetery</a> just outside Washington, D.C..</li>
<li>A bust of President Kennedy was dedicated on May 31, 1965 at <a href="/wiki/Grand_Army_Plaza" title="Grand Army Plaza">Grand Army Plaza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. The bust was removed in 2003 for restoration, and reinstalled in 2010.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jacques_Lipchitz" title="Jacques Lipchitz">Jacques Lipchitz</a>'s bust of Kennedy at the <a href="/wiki/Military_Park_(Newark)" title="Military Park (Newark)">Military Park</a> in Newark, New Jersey was dedicated on November 11, 1965.</li>
<li>A life size statue of President John F. Kennedy in the presidential statues across from the Puerto Rico Capitol in San Juan, Puerto Rico.</li>
<li>A bust of President John F. Kennedy by Felix de Weldon at Kennedy Library, Columbia Point, <a href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Boston, Massachusetts">Boston, Massachusetts</a></li>
<li>A bronze statue of John F. Kennedy by Isabel McIlvain on the grounds of the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_State_House" title="Massachusetts State House">Massachusetts State House</a> was dedicated on May 29, 1990.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy_Memorial_(Portland,_Oregon)" title="John Fitzgerald Kennedy Memorial (Portland, Oregon)">John Fitzgerald Kennedy Memorial (Portland, Oregon)</a></li>
<li>A bust of John F. Kennedy in downtown <a href="/wiki/Nashua,_New_Hampshire" title="Nashua, New Hampshire">Nashua, New Hampshire</a> commemorates January 25, 1960 as the first campaign stop in Kennedy's bid for the presidency.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Schools">Schools</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_memorials_to_John_F._Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Schools">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>At Harvard University:
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/Harvard_Institute_of_Politics" title="Harvard Institute of Politics">Harvard Institute of Politics</a> serves as a living memorial which promotes public service in his name.</li>
<li>The School of Government is known as the <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_School_of_Government" title="John F. Kennedy School of Government">John F. Kennedy School of Government</a>.</li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_University" title="John F. Kennedy University">John F. Kennedy University</a> opened in <a href="/wiki/Pleasant_Hill,_California" title="Pleasant Hill, California">Pleasant Hill, California</a>, in 1964 as a school for adult education.</li>
<li>Hundreds of schools across the US were named in Kennedy's honor. The first school in the United States named after him, while he was alive, had been the <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Middle_School_(Cupertino,_California)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kennedy Middle School (Cupertino, California)">Kennedy Middle School</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Cupertino,_California" title="Cupertino, California">Cupertino, California</a>. In the week after Kennedy's death, the first schools renamed for him were the Kennedy Elementary School in <a href="/wiki/Butte,_Montana" title="Butte, Montana">Butte, Montana</a> and the John F. Kennedy Middle School on Long Island in <a href="/wiki/Bethpage,_New_York" title="Bethpage, New York">Bethpage, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Both schools held Board Meetings on November 26, at which time the new names were adopted.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_High_School_(Cedar_Rapids,_Iowa)" title="John F. Kennedy High School (Cedar Rapids, Iowa)">John F. Kennedy High School</a> in Cedar Rapids, Iowa.</li>

<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Library" class="mw-redirect" title="John F. Kennedy Library">John F. Kennedy Presidential Library and Museum</a> on <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Point_(Boston)" class="mw-redirect" title="Columbia Point (Boston)">Columbia Point</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dorchester,_Boston" title="Dorchester, Boston">Dorchester, Massachusetts</a> opened in 1979 as Kennedy's official <a href="/wiki/Presidential_library" title="Presidential library">presidential library</a>.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Center_for_the_Performing_Arts" title="John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts">John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts</a> opened in 1971 in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_DC" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington, DC">Washington, DC</a></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Government_Center,_Boston#John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy_Federal_Building" title="Government Center, Boston">John Fitzgerald Kennedy Federal Building</a> in <a href="/wiki/Government_Center,_Boston" title="Government Center, Boston">Government Center, Boston</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/JFK_Stadium_(Springfield,_Missouri)" title="JFK Stadium (Springfield, Missouri)">JFK Stadium (Springfield, Missouri)</a>, Springfield, Missouri, United States.</li>
<li>One of the five residential towers at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Massachusetts_Amherst" title="University of Massachusetts Amherst">University of Massachusetts Amherst</a> is named Kennedy Tower in his honor.</li>
<li>The student union at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Dayton" title="University of Dayton">University of Dayton</a> is named the John F. Kennedy Memorial Union, which opened in 1964.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>A bust of JFK and a building on science campus of the French-language university <i><a href="/wiki/Universit%C3%A9_du_Qu%C3%A9bec_%C3%A0_Montr%C3%A9al" title="Université du Québec à Montréal">Université du Québec à Montréal</a></i> named the pavilion Président-Kennedy are located at President Kennedy Street's western end in Montreal.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Cumberland,_Maryland" title="Cumberland, Maryland">Cumberland, Maryland</a> a low income residential apartment is named the "John F Kennedy Tower". It was dedicated by Maryland native and late brother-in-law of Kennedy, <a href="/wiki/Sargent_Shriver" title="Sargent Shriver">Sargent Shriver</a>, in 1967.</li>
<li>Philadelphia Municipal Stadium was renamed <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Stadium_(Philadelphia)" title="John F. Kennedy Stadium (Philadelphia)">John F. Kennedy Stadium</a> in 1964. It was razed in 1992 and its former location is now occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Wells_Fargo_Center_(Philadelphia)" title="Wells Fargo Center (Philadelphia)">Wells Fargo Center</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation_facilities">Transportation facilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_memorials_to_John_F._Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transportation facilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>'s Launch Operations Center at <a href="/wiki/Cape_Canaveral" title="Cape Canaveral">Cape Canaveral</a> was renamed the <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Space_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="John F. Kennedy Space Center">John F. Kennedy Space Center</a>. <li><a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_International_Airport" title="John F. Kennedy International Airport">John F. Kennedy International Airport</a>, New York City. <li><a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Memorial_Airport" title="John F. Kennedy Memorial Airport">John F. Kennedy Memorial Airport</a>, Ashland, Wisconsin, United States.</li>

Kennedy Memorial Bridge</a></b>, carries <a href="/wiki/Interstate_65" title="Interstate 65">Interstate 65</a> across the <a href="/wiki/Ohio_River" title="Ohio River">Ohio River</a> between <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville, Kentucky</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jeffersonville,_Indiana" title="Jeffersonville, Indiana">Jeffersonville, Indiana</a>. <h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Maine"><a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_memorials_to_John_F._Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=53" title="Edit section: Maine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<ul><li><b>Kennedy Memorial Drive</b>, formerly known as the Oakland Road, runs from the end of Silver Street in <a href="/wiki/Waterville,_Maine" title="Waterville, Maine">Waterville, Maine</a> to the center of town in <a href="/wiki/Oakland,_Maine" title="Oakland, Maine">Oakland, Maine</a>. The roadway was under reconstruction from two lanes to four during the time of Kennedy's assassination. The upgraded roadway was dedicated in his honor.</li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Songs">Songs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_memorials_to_John_F._Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=104" title="Edit section: Songs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>"<a href="/wiki/Abraham,_Martin_and_John" title="Abraham, Martin and John">Abraham, Martin and John</a>", written by <a href="/wiki/Dick_Holler" title="Dick Holler">Dick Holler</a> and recorded first by <a href="/wiki/Dion_DiMucci" title="Dion DiMucci">Dion</a>; the title refers to assassination victims <a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Luther_King,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Martin Luther King, Jr.">Martin Luther King, Jr.</a> and John F. Kennedy. In the song, the lyrics mention Robert F. Kennedy ("Bobby"), as well.</li>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/He_Was_a_Friend_of_Mine" title="He Was a Friend of Mine">He Was a Friend of Mine</a>", recorded by <a href="/wiki/The_Byrds" title="The Byrds">The Byrds</a>, was included on their <a href="/wiki/1965_in_music" title="1965 in music">1965</a> album, <i><a href="/wiki/Turn!_Turn!_Turn!_(album)" title="Turn! Turn! Turn! (album)">Turn! Turn! Turn!</a></i>. In The Byrds' version, the song's melody is altered and the lyrics are changed to lament John Kennedy's assassination.<sup id="cite_ref-David_Fricke_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-David_Fricke-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Jazz composer and arranger <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Nelson" title="Oliver Nelson">Oliver Nelson</a> recorded a tribute album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Kennedy_Dream" title="The Kennedy Dream">The Kennedy Dream</a></i> which used excerpts from Kennedy's speeches in 1967.</li>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/In_the_Summer_of_His_Years_(song)" title="In the Summer of His Years (song)">In the Summer of His Years</a>"</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_memorials_to_John_F._Kennedy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=105" title="Edit section: Other">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:John_F_Kennedy_1964_Issue-5c.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/John_F_Kennedy_1964_Issue-5c.jpg/220px-John_F_Kennedy_1964_Issue-5c.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/John_F_Kennedy_1964_Issue-5c.jpg/330px-John_F_Kennedy_1964_Issue-5c.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/John_F_Kennedy_1964_Issue-5c.jpg/440px-John_F_Kennedy_1964_Issue-5c.jpg 2x" data-file-width="839" data-file-height="550" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:John_F_Kennedy_1964_Issue-5c.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Five_cents_John_Kennedy" title="Five cents John Kennedy">John F. Kennedy memorial issue</a> of 1964</div></div></div>
<ul><li>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Postal_Service" title="United States Postal Service">United States Postal Service</a> honored Kennedy with two <a href="/wiki/Postage_stamp" title="Postage stamp">postage stamps</a>, a 5¢ issued in 1964, and a <a href="/wiki/Prominent_Americans_series" title="Prominent Americans series">Prominent Americans series</a> (1965–1978) 13¢.</li>
<li>The US Navy <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">aircraft carrier</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_John_F._Kennedy_(CV-67)" title="USS John F. Kennedy (CV-67)">USS <i>John F. Kennedy</i> (CV-67)</a> was named on April 30, 1964, and served until March 23, 2007. A <a href="/wiki/USS_John_F._Kennedy_(CVN-79)" title="USS John F. Kennedy (CVN-79)">second USS <i>John F. Kennedy</i></a> is due to start construction in 2012, to be commissioned in 2018.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy_National_Historic_Site" title="John Fitzgerald Kennedy National Historic Site">John Fitzgerald Kennedy National Historic Site</a> is a <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Sites_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="National Historic Sites (United States)">National Historic Site</a> that includes Kennedy's birthplace and childhood home in <a href="/wiki/Brookline,_Massachusetts" title="Brookline, Massachusetts">Brookline, Massachusetts</a> and is open to the public.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Hyannis_Museum" title="John F. Kennedy Hyannis Museum">John F. Kennedy Hyannis Museum</a> is a museum located in Hyannis, Massachusetts. It consists of a collection of photographs relating to the Kennedys and the times they spent vacationing on the Hyannis Port. It includes a video on the Kennedys, a Kennedy Family Tree, and a statue, "What Could Have Been", portraying John F. Kennedy walking on the beach with his adult son, John F. Kennedy, Jr..</li>
<li>The main house of the historic <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Compound" title="Kennedy Compound">Kennedy Compound</a> in which the Kennedy family would spend their summers and which was Senator John F. Kennedy's home during his senator years (1953-1956) and as well as his main base during his successful 1960 presidential campaign, a place for meetings and interviews and a presidential retreat and summer White House until his assassination, was donated in 2012 to the <a href="/wiki/Edward_M._Kennedy_Institute_for_the_United_States_Senate" title="Edward M. Kennedy Institute for the United States Senate">Edward M. Kennedy Institute for the United States Senate</a> in accordance with the promise <a href="/wiki/Edward_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward Kennedy">Ted Kennedy</a> made to <a href="/wiki/Rose_Kennedy" title="Rose Kennedy">Rose</a> that the house be preserved and used for charity. The institute announced that the house would host educational seminars and that will be open to the general public at specific times as a museum, dedicated to the Kennedy family.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> As of 2014, the house is undergoing a complete restoration in order to appear exactly as it was in 1960.</li>
<li>The Kennedy Homestead, birthplace of President John F. Kennedy's great-grandfather Patrick Kennedy, is a cultural museum in Ireland that is dedicated to the Kennedy family and plays an important role in the continued preservation of the legacy of the Kennedys in Ireland.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Hyannis_Armory" title="Hyannis Armory">Hyannis Armory</a> which was the site of Kennedy's presidential acceptance speech is now owned by the town of Barnstable, Massachusetts and is open to the public.</li>
<li>Kennedy was posthumously awarded the <a href="/wiki/Presidential_Medal_of_Freedom" title="Presidential Medal of Freedom">Presidential Medal of Freedom</a> in 1963.</li>
<li>Since 1964, Kennedy's portrait has appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Half_dollar_(United_States_coin)" title="Half dollar (United States coin)">half-dollar coin</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Franklin" title="Benjamin Franklin">Benjamin Franklin</a>.</li>
<li>John F. Kennedy Medical Center is a hospital located in <a href="/wiki/Edison,_New_Jersey" title="Edison, New Jersey">Edison, New Jersey</a>.</li>
<li>One of the <a href="/wiki/Solomon_Islands" title="Solomon Islands">Solomon Islands</a> is named <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Island" title="Kennedy Island">Kennedy Island</a></li>
<li>The city of <a href="/wiki/Evansville,_Indiana" title="Evansville, Indiana">Evansville, Indiana</a> observed John F. Kennedy Day on November 22, 2003, to mark the 40th anniversary of his death.</li>
<li>In February 2007, Kennedy's name, along with his wife's, was included on a list taken aboard the Japanese <i><a href="/wiki/SELENE" title="SELENE">Kaguya</a></i> spacecraft to the <a href="/wiki/Moon" title="Moon">Moon</a>, as part of <a href="/wiki/The_Planetary_Society" title="The Planetary Society">The Planetary Society</a>'s "Wish Upon the Moon" campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In addition, they are included on the list onboard <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lunar_Reconnaissance_Orbiter" title="Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter">Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter</a> mission.</li>
<li>The US Army's <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Special_Warfare_Center_and_School" title="John F. Kennedy Special Warfare Center and School">John F. Kennedy Special Warfare Center and School</a> is named after the president because of his support for the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Rangers" title="United States Army Rangers">Army Rangers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Special_Forces_(United_States_Army)" class="mw-redirect" title="Special Forces (United States Army)">Special Forces</a>.</li>
<li>John F. Kennedy Park in the <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a> suburb of <a href="/wiki/Castor_Bay" title="Castor Bay">Castor Bay</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a></li>
<li>The exterior of <a href="/wiki/14_Prince%27s_Gate,_London" title="14 Prince&#39;s Gate, London">14 Prince's Gate</a>, formerly the <a href="/wiki/Royal_College_of_General_Practitioners" title="Royal College of General Practitioners">Royal College of General Practitioners</a> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>,  bears a plaque noting that Kennedy lived there as a boy when his father was US Ambassador to the United Kingdom from 1938 to 1940.</li>
<li>The Historic Auto Attractions Museum, in <a href="/wiki/Roscoe,_Illinois" title="Roscoe, Illinois">Roscoe, Illinois</a>, houses a large display dedicated to the president.  Items on display include the 1956 <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secret_Service" title="United States Secret Service">Secret Service</a> <a href="/wiki/Cadillac" title="Cadillac">Cadillac</a> which followed the car in which the President, Mrs. Kennedy, Governor and Mrs. Connally were riding; a replica of the 1963 <a href="/wiki/Lincoln_Motor_Company#Presidential_Cars" title="Lincoln Motor Company">Lincoln Continental</a> used by the Kennedy; and several other items related to the Kennedy family, <a href="/wiki/Lee_Harvey_Oswald" title="Lee Harvey Oswald">Lee Harvey Oswald</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Ruby" title="Jack Ruby">Jack Ruby</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-museum_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-museum-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
