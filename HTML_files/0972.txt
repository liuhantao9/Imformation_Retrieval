<p><b>Middletown</b> is a <a href="/wiki/New_England_town" title="New England town">town</a> in <a href="/wiki/Newport_County,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport County, Rhode Island">Newport County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island" title="Rhode Island">Rhode Island</a>, United States. The population was 16,150 at the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 census</a>. It lies to the south of <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_Rhode_Island" title="Portsmouth, Rhode Island">Portsmouth</a> and to the north of <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport, Rhode Island">Newport</a> on <a href="/wiki/Aquidneck_Island" title="Aquidneck Island">Aquidneck Island</a>, hence the name "Middletown".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Middletown,_Rhode_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Various issues including unjust taxation and a growing population caused the freeholders living in the northern section of Newport to petition the general assembly for independence. As a result of the petition, the land that Middletown occupies was set apart in 1731. The town was incorporated in 1743.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the 1980s, large sections of East Main Road and West Main Road running through Middletown began to be commercialized, and by the late 1990s, the area had become Aquidneck Island's central business district.
</p>

<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the town has a total area of 14.9&#160;square miles (38.7&#160;km²), of which 13.0&#160;square miles (33.6&#160;km²) is land and 2.0&#160;square miles (5.1&#160;km²; 13.18%) is water. Middletown was known as the "farming community" of Aquidneck Island. Today most of the developed land is located towards the western part of the town, while what is left of its rural heritage is primarily towards the east. Middletown also has several beaches.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Middletown,_Rhode_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The town is governed by a seven-member <a href="/wiki/Town_council" title="Town council">town council</a>, elected <a href="/wiki/At-large" title="At-large">at-large</a> in partisan elections. Executive authority is vested in an appointed <a href="/wiki/Town_administrator" class="mw-redirect" title="Town administrator">town administrator</a> (on the <a href="/wiki/Council%E2%80%93manager_government" title="Council–manager government">council–manager</a> model). The town also elects a non-partisan <a href="/wiki/School_committee" class="mw-redirect" title="School committee">school committee</a>.
</p><p>Middletown forms part of <span data-sort-value="Rhode Island01&#160;!"><a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island%27s_1st_congressional_district" title="Rhode Island&#39;s 1st congressional district">Rhode Island's 1st congressional district</a></span>, represented by <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrat</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Cicilline" title="David Cicilline">David Cicilline</a>. At the state level, Middletown is part of one state senate and three state house districts. The 12th <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_Senate" title="Rhode Island Senate">Rhode Island Senate</a> district, which also includes parts of Newport, <a href="/wiki/Little_Compton,_Rhode_Island" title="Little Compton, Rhode Island">Little Compton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tiverton,_Rhode_Island" title="Tiverton, Rhode Island">Tiverton</a>, is held by Democrat <a href="/w/index.php?title=Louis_P._DiPalma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Louis P. DiPalma (page does not exist)">Louis P. DiPalma</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_House_of_Representatives" title="Rhode Island House of Representatives">Rhode Island House of Representatives</a>, Middletown forms part of the 72nd, 73rd, and 74th districts. The 72nd, which also includes portions of Newport and Portsmouth, is represented by <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Daniel_P._Reilly" title="Daniel P. Reilly">Daniel Reilly</a>. The 73rd, which is predominantly Newport, is held by Democrat Russell Jackson. The 74th, shared between Middletown and <a href="/wiki/Jamestown,_Rhode_Island" title="Jamestown, Rhode Island">Jamestown</a>, is represented by Democrat <a href="/wiki/Deb_Ruggiero" title="Deb Ruggiero">Deb Ruggiero</a>.
</p>

<p>At the 2000 census,<sup id="cite_ref-GR2_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GR2-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> there were 17,334 people, 6,993 households and 4,643 families residing in the town. The population density was 1,335.4 per square mile (515.6/km²). There were 7,603 housing units at an average density of 585.7 per square mile (226.2/km²). The racial makeup of the town was 89.12% <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 2.72% <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 2.36% <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 1.18% <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 1.11% <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 1.07% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">other races</a>, and 2.43% from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 2.93% of the population.
</p><p>There were 6,993 households of which 32.9% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 53.9% were married couples living together, 9.8% had a female householder with no husband present, and 33.6% were non-families. Of all households 28.7% were made up of individuals and 10.9% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.43 and the average family size was 3.01.
</p><p>25.0% of the population were under the age of 18, 6.6% from 18 to 24, 30.9% from 25 to 44, 22.6% from 45 to 64, and 14.9% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 38 years. For every 100 females, there were 94.8 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 89.7 males.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Median_household_income" class="mw-redirect" title="Median household income">median household income</a> was $51,075 and the median family income was $57,322. Males had a median income of $41,778 and females $27,229. The per capita income for the town was $25,857. About 3.7% of families and 5.0% of the population were below the poverty line, including 6.2% of those under age 18 and 4.7% of those age 65 or over.
</p>

<p>As of September 2009, the <a href="/wiki/Middletown_Public_Schools_(Rhode_Island)" title="Middletown Public Schools (Rhode Island)">Middletown Public School District</a> consists of four schools serving pre-kindergarten through 12th grade. They are Aquidneck Elementary School (grades pre-K to 3), Forest Avenue Elementary School (grades K–3), Joseph H. Gaudet Middle School (grades 4–8) and <a href="/wiki/Middletown_High_School_(Rhode_Island)" title="Middletown High School (Rhode Island)">Middletown High School</a> (grades 9–12). Starting in September 2009, all fourth grade students will attend Joseph H. Gaudet Middle School. John F. Kennedy, former elementary school, will be closed at the end of the 2008–2009 school year due to budget cuts.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
