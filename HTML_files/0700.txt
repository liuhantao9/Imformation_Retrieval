	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">2010 South American Summit</h1>

<p>The <b>2010 South American summit</b> (officially the 4th Ordinary Meeting of the Council of Heads of State and Government of the Union of South American Nations), took place in <a href="/wiki/Georgetown,_Guyana" title="Georgetown, Guyana">Georgetown</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a> on November 26, 2010. Eight heads of state and four foreign ministers of the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_South_American_Nations" title="Union of South American Nations">Union of South American Nations</a> attended the summit. During the summit, the leaders signed an additional protocol to the <a href="/wiki/UNASUR_Constitutive_Treaty" title="UNASUR Constitutive Treaty">Constitutive Treaty</a>, adding a democratic clause to the charter of the organization.<sup id="cite_ref-LatAm_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LatAm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Georgetown summit ended with the <a href="/wiki/President_of_Ecuador" title="President of Ecuador">Ecuadorian president</a> handing the UNASUR <a href="/wiki/President_pro_tempore_of_UNASUR" class="mw-redirect" title="President pro tempore of UNASUR">pro-tempore presidency</a> for the next twelve months to his Guyanese counterpart, <a href="/wiki/Bharrat_Jagdeo" title="Bharrat Jagdeo">Bharrat Jagdeo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Xinhua_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foreign_ministers">Foreign ministers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_South_American_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Foreign ministers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The foreign ministers of <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> (<a href="/wiki/David_Choquehuanca" title="David Choquehuanca">David Choquehuanca</a>), <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> (Alfredo Moreno), <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Garc%C3%ADa_Bela%C3%BAnde" title="José García Belaúnde">José García Belaúnde</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a> (Luis Almagro) represented their respective governments.<sup id="cite_ref-Xinhua_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Issues">Issues</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_South_American_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Issues">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regional_integration">Regional integration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_South_American_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Regional integration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/President_of_Brazil" title="President of Brazil">Brazilian President</a> <a href="/wiki/Luiz_In%C3%A1cio_Lula_da_Silva" title="Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva">Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva</a> advocated for the elimination of the asymmetries stopping the integration of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, in his speech during the summit. "None of our countries will really be prosperous, without all of us being also prosperous," said the Brazilian president.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Secretary-General">Secretary-General</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_South_American_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Secretary-General">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The name of the new secretary-general of the organization will remain unknown until the next presidential meeting in <a href="/wiki/Mar_del_Plata" title="Mar del Plata">Mar del Plata</a>, Argentina.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guyanese_presidency">Guyanese presidency</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_South_American_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Guyanese presidency">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>, which took over the Unasur rotating presidency from Ecuador, is one of <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>'s poorest countries and for this reason, President Lula has already announced that Brazil will provide assistance for Georgetown's efforts in presiding over the regional bloc.<sup id="cite_ref-LatAm_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LatAm-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Falklands.2FMalvinas"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Falklands/Malvinas">Falklands/Malvinas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_South_American_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Falklands/Malvinas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The summit addressed the <a href="/wiki/Falkland_Islands_sovereignty_dispute" title="Falkland Islands sovereignty dispute">Falkland Islands sovereignty dispute</a> between Argentina and the United Kingdom. Several articles of the final declaration state that all Unasur ports will be closed to vessels operating under the "illegal flag of Malvinas (Falkland Islands)".<sup id="cite_ref-MercoPress_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MercoPress-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Accomplishments">Accomplishments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_South_American_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Accomplishments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Democratic_clause">Democratic clause</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_South_American_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Democratic clause">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The leaders adopted an additional protocol to the organization's Constitutive Treaty, which added a <a href="/wiki/Democracy" title="Democracy">democratic</a> clause to the <a href="/wiki/Charter" title="Charter">charter</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The democratic clause imposes <a href="/wiki/International_sanctions" title="International sanctions">sanctions</a> on any member country of UNASUR that breaks or attempts to break <a href="/wiki/Constitutionality" title="Constitutionality">constitutional rule</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic system">democratic system</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MercoPress_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MercoPress-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The clause establishes sanctions, such as shutting down borders and the suspension of trade, against the country that suffers an attempted coup. The decision to include a democratic clause was made after the recent <a href="/wiki/2010_Ecuador_crisis" title="2010 Ecuador crisis">upheaval in Ecuador</a> that briefly threatened the administration of President, <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Correa" title="Rafael Correa">Rafael Correa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Georgetown_Declaration">Georgetown Declaration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_South_American_Summit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Georgetown Declaration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the end of the summit, the heads of state and foreign ministers issued the "Georgetown Declaration". In the declaration, the leaders reiterated their commitment expressed in the “Declaration of Bariloche” of August 28, 2009, to strengthen South America as a zone of peace, upholding the decision to refrain from resorting to the threat or use of force against the territorial integrity of another UNASUR State.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The leaders expressed their willingness to continue working toward the consolidation of a common space for the political, economic, social, cultural, energy, environmental and infrastructure integration of the region, in order to achieve sustainable development.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
