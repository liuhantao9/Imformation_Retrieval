<p><b>Politics of Suriname</b> take place in a framework of a  <a href="/wiki/Representative_democracy" title="Representative democracy">representative democratic</a> <a href="/wiki/Republic" title="Republic">republic</a>, whereby the president of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a>, and of a <a href="/wiki/Plurality_voting_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Plurality voting system">pluriform</a> <a href="/wiki/Multi-party_system" title="Multi-party system">multi-party system</a>. <a href="/wiki/Executive_power" class="mw-redirect" title="Executive power">Executive power</a> is exercised by the <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a>. The executive power is dependent on the Parliament in theory, but in reality is not. <a href="/wiki/Legislative_power" class="mw-redirect" title="Legislative power">Legislative power</a> is vested in both the government and the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="National Assembly of Suriname">National Assembly</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Judiciary" title="Judiciary">judiciary</a> is independent of the executive and the legislature.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Executive_branch">Executive branch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Politics_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Executive branch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The executive branch is headed by the <a href="/wiki/President_(government_title)" title="President (government title)">president</a>, who is elected by a two-thirds majority of the <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="National Assembly of Suriname">National Assembly</a> or, failing that twice, by a majority of the People's Assembly for a 5-year term. If at least two-thirds of the National Assembly cannot agree to vote for one presidential candidate, a People's Assembly is formed from all National Assembly delegates and regional and municipal <a href="/wiki/Legislator" title="Legislator">representatives</a> who were elected by popular vote in the most recent national election. The <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_Suriname" title="Vice President of Suriname">Vice President</a>, normally elected at the same time as the president, is elected for a 5-year term, the same way as the president.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As head of government, the president appoints a cabinet of ministers who are led in their day-to-day activities by the Vice President. There is no constitutional provision for removal or replacement of the president unless he resigns.
</p><p>A 15-member <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Privy Council">State Advisory Council</a> advises the president in the conduct of <a href="/wiki/Policy" title="Policy">policy</a>. Eleven of the 15 council seats are allotted by proportional representation of all political parties represented in the National Assembly. The president chairs the council, and two seats are allotted to representatives of labor, and two are to employers' organizations. The Cabinet of Ministers appointed by the president.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legislative_branch">Legislative branch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Politics_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legislative branch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_(Suriname)" title="National Assembly (Suriname)">National Assembly (Suriname)</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AssembleeSurinaam.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/AssembleeSurinaam.jpg/220px-AssembleeSurinaam.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="168" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/AssembleeSurinaam.jpg/330px-AssembleeSurinaam.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/AssembleeSurinaam.jpg/440px-AssembleeSurinaam.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2499" data-file-height="1905" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AssembleeSurinaam.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="National Assembly of Suriname">National Assembly of Suriname</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The legislative branch of <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">government</a> consists of a 51-member unicameral National Assembly, simultaneously and popularly elected for a 5-year term.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_parties_and_elections">Political parties and elections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Politics_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political parties and elections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For other political parties, see <a href="/wiki/List_of_political_parties_in_Suriname" title="List of political parties in Suriname">List of political parties in Suriname</a>. An overview on elections and election results is included in <a href="/wiki/Elections_in_Suriname" title="Elections in Suriname">Elections in Suriname</a>.</div>
<p>Suriname elects on national level a <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> - the <a href="/wiki/President_(government_title)" title="President (government title)">president</a> -  and a <a href="/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">legislature</a>. The president is elected for a five-year term by an electoral college based on the parliament. The <b><a href="/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="National Assembly of Suriname">National Assembly</a></b> (<i>Nationale Assemblée</i>) has 51 members, elected every five years by <a href="/wiki/Proportional_representation" title="Proportional representation">proportional representation</a> per district.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Judicial_branch">Judicial branch</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Politics_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Judicial branch">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Judiciary" title="Judiciary">judiciary</a> is headed by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Court_of_Justice_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Court of Justice of Suriname (page does not exist)">Court of Justice</a> (<a href="/wiki/Supreme_court" title="Supreme court">Supreme Court</a>). This court supervises the magistrate courts. Members are appointed for life by the president in consultation with the National Assembly, the State Advisory Council, and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Order_of_Private_Attorneys&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="National Order of Private Attorneys (page does not exist)">National Order of Private Attorneys</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrative_divisions">Administrative divisions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Politics_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Administrative divisions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Suriname" title="Districts of Suriname">Districts of Suriname</a> and <a href="/wiki/Resorts_of_Suriname" title="Resorts of Suriname">Resorts of Suriname</a></div>
<p>The country is divided into 10 administrative <a href="/wiki/Districts" class="mw-redirect" title="Districts">districts</a>, each headed by a <a href="/w/index.php?title=District_Commissioner_(Suriname)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="District Commissioner (Suriname) (page does not exist)">district commissioner</a> appointed by the president. The commissioner serves at the president's pleasure.
The 10 districts (distrikten, singular - distrikt) are&#160;; <a href="/wiki/Brokopondo" title="Brokopondo">Brokopondo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commewijne_District" title="Commewijne District">Commewijne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coronie" class="mw-redirect" title="Coronie">Coronie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marowijne_District" title="Marowijne District">Marowijne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nickerie_District" title="Nickerie District">Nickerie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Para_District" title="Para District">Para</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo_District" title="Paramaribo District">Paramaribo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saramacca_District" title="Saramacca District">Saramacca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sipaliwini" title="Sipaliwini">Sipaliwini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wanica" class="mw-redirect" title="Wanica">Wanica</a>
</p>
