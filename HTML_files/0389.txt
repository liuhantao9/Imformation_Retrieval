<p><b><a href="/wiki/Religion" title="Religion">Religion</a> in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a></b> is characterized by a range of religious beliefs and practices due to its <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="Ethnic groups in Suriname">ethnic diversity</a>. The government is vocally supportive of religious diversity and tolerance, and these attitudes are present in general society as well. According to the most recent census (2012), 48.4 percent of the population is <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christian</a> (the largest groups being <a href="/wiki/Pentecostalism" title="Pentecostalism">Pentecostalism</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Moravian_Church" title="Moravian Church">Moravian Church</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church" title="Catholic Church">Catholic Church</a>) 22.3 percent is <a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">Hindu</a>, 13.9 percent is <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a>, 1.8 percent follows <a href="/wiki/Winti" title="Winti">Winti</a>, and 0.8 percent is <a href="/wiki/Javanism" class="mw-redirect" title="Javanism">Javanist</a>. In addition 2.1 percent of the population follows other faiths (including <a href="/wiki/Jehovah%27s_Witnesses" title="Jehovah&#39;s Witnesses">Jehovah's Witnesses</a>), 7.5 percent are <a href="/wiki/Atheist" class="mw-redirect" title="Atheist">atheist</a> or <a href="/wiki/Agnostic" class="mw-redirect" title="Agnostic">agnostic</a>, and 3.2 percent did not answer the question about their religion.<sup id="cite_ref-CENSUS2012_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CENSUS2012-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Indigenous religions are practiced by the Amerindian and Afro-descendant Maroon populations. <a href="/wiki/Amerindian" class="mw-redirect" title="Amerindian">Amerindians</a>, found principally in the interior and to a lesser extent in coastal areas, practice <a href="/wiki/Shamanism" title="Shamanism">shamanism</a>, worship of all living things, and their rites are led by <a href="/wiki/Medicine_man" title="Medicine man">medicine men</a>, or <i>piaiman</i>. <a href="/wiki/Maroon_(people)" title="Maroon (people)">Maroons</a>, who inhabit the interior, worship nature through a practice that has no special name, and they also worship their ancestors through a rite called Winti. Citizens of Amerindian and Maroon origin who classify themselves as Christian often simultaneously follow indigenous religious customs, with the acknowledgment of their Christian church leaders.
</p><p>The negligible <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Jewish</a> community numbers 181,<sup id="cite_ref-CENSUS2012_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CENSUS2012-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and there are also small numbers of <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_faith" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahá&#39;í faith">Bahá'ís</a> and <a href="/wiki/Buddhism" title="Buddhism">Buddhists</a>. Other groups include <a href="/wiki/The_Church_of_Jesus_Christ_of_Latter-day_Saints" title="The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints">The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints</a> (LDS Church), the <a href="/wiki/Ahmadiyya" title="Ahmadiyya">Ahmadiyya Muslim Community</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_Islamic_Call_Society" title="World Islamic Call Society">World Islamic Call Society</a>. "No religion in Suriname has any problem with any other religion", quips Guido Robles, a prominent Jewish businessman in Paramaribo. "All the problems are caused by the politicians." <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many <a href="/wiki/Political_parties" class="mw-redirect" title="Political parties">political parties</a>, including six of the eight governing coalition parties, have strong ethnic ties, and members tend to adhere to or practice one faith. For example, within the governing coalition, the majority of members of the mostly ethnic-<a href="/wiki/National_Party_of_Suriname" title="National Party of Suriname">National Party of Suriname</a> (NPS) is Moravian, most members of the mostly ethnic-Indian <a href="/wiki/Progressive_Reform_Party_(Suriname)" title="Progressive Reform Party (Suriname)">Progressive Reform Party</a> are <a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">Hindu</a>, and those of the mostly ethnic-Javanese <a href="/wiki/Pertjajah_Luhur" title="Pertjajah Luhur">Pertjaja Luhur Party</a> tend to be Muslim.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However, parties have no requirement that political party leaders or members adhere to a particular religion.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Christianity">Christianity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Christianity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Catholic_Church_in_Suriname" title="Catholic Church in Suriname">Catholic Church in Suriname</a></div>
<p>The dominant religion in Suriname is Christianity, both in the form of <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholicism_in_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholicism in Suriname">Roman Catholicism</a> and various denominations of <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestantism</a>, the Anglican Church being the oldest.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> According to the 2012 census data 48.4% of the population of Suriname is Christian<sup id="cite_ref-CENSUS2012_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CENSUS2012-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/Pentecostalism" title="Pentecostalism">Pentecostal</a> churches are the largest Protestant denomination, closely followed by <a href="/wiki/Moravian_Church" title="Moravian Church">Moravians</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CENSUS2012_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CENSUS2012-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hinduism">Hinduism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Hinduism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hinduism_in_Suriname" title="Hinduism in Suriname">Hinduism in Suriname</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Suriname_Hindu.PNG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Suriname_Hindu.PNG/150px-Suriname_Hindu.PNG" decoding="async" width="150" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Suriname_Hindu.PNG/225px-Suriname_Hindu.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Suriname_Hindu.PNG/300px-Suriname_Hindu.PNG 2x" data-file-width="716" data-file-height="709" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Suriname_Hindu.PNG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Distribution of Hindus by district in Suriname.</div></div></div>
<p>The story of Hinduism in Suriname is broadly parallel to that in <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>. <a href="/wiki/Indian_indenture_system" title="Indian indenture system">Indian indentured labourers</a> were sent to colonial <a href="/wiki/Dutch_colonisation_of_the_Guianas" title="Dutch colonisation of the Guianas">Dutch Guiana</a> by special arrangement between the Dutch and British.  
The difference is that the Netherlands' more liberal policy toward Hinduism allowed the culture to develop stronger. Examples are the lack of a rigid caste system and the almost universal reading of <a href="/wiki/Gita" class="mw-redirect" title="Gita">Gita</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ramayan" class="mw-redirect" title="Ramayan">Ramayan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>According to the 2012 census of Suriname, Hindus constitute 22.3% of the population.<sup id="cite_ref-CENSUS2012_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CENSUS2012-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Hindus are mostly concentrated in Northern coastal regions of Suriname: <a href="/wiki/Nickerie_District" title="Nickerie District">Nickerie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wanica_District" title="Wanica District">Wanica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saramacca_District" title="Saramacca District">Saramacca</a>, where they constitute the largest religious group.  There are several <a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">hindu</a> temples in Suriname.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Islam">Islam</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Islam">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_Suriname" title="Islam in Suriname">Islam in Suriname</a></div>
<p>According to the most recent census, the <a href="/wiki/Muslim" class="mw-redirect" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> population of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> represents about 13.9%<sup id="cite_ref-CENSUS2012_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CENSUS2012-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> of the country's total population, giving the country the highest proportion of Muslims on the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">American continent</a>.
</p><p>Muslims that first came to Suriname consisted of indentured laborers from <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, from whom today most Muslims in Suriname are descended.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Javanism">Javanism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Javanism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Javanism is followed by 0.8% of the Suriname population. It is followed mainly by the Javanese people in Suriname.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Judaism">Judaism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Judaism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Suriname" title="History of the Jews in Suriname">History of the Jews in Suriname</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Paramaribo,_German_Synagogue.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Paramaribo%2C_German_Synagogue.JPG/150px-Paramaribo%2C_German_Synagogue.JPG" decoding="async" width="150" height="113" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Paramaribo%2C_German_Synagogue.JPG/225px-Paramaribo%2C_German_Synagogue.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Paramaribo%2C_German_Synagogue.JPG/300px-Paramaribo%2C_German_Synagogue.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Paramaribo,_German_Synagogue.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Neveh_Shalom_Synagogue" title="Neveh Shalom Synagogue">Neveh Shalom Synagogue</a> in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>There has been a Jewish community in Suriname since 1639, when the English government allowed <a href="/wiki/Sephardi_Jews" title="Sephardi Jews">Sephardi Jews</a> to settle the region.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The Jewish community is currently struggling due to dwindling funds and membership.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Religious_freedom">Religious freedom</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Religion_in_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Religious freedom">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Suriname" title="Constitution of Suriname">constitution</a> of Suriname establishes the freedom of religion and outlaws discrimination along religious lines. "Instigating religious hatred" is punishable by fines, and in some cases prison.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Religious groups may register with the government in order to receive financial support. Most groups are registered.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Religious instruction is not allowed in public schools. Private religious schools are allowed, and comprise roughly half of the primary and secondary schools in Suriname. These private schools are partially subsidized by the government. Parents are not allowed to homeschool children for religious reasons<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The government engages in vocal support of religious diversity and tolerance, and recognizes the holidays of various religious traditions present in the country as national holidays. The armed forces have chaplains for the Hindu, Muslim, Catholic, and Protestant faiths.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
