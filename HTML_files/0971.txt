<p><b>Newport</b> is a seaside city on <a href="/wiki/Aquidneck_Island" title="Aquidneck Island">Aquidneck Island</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Newport_County,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport County, Rhode Island">Newport County, Rhode Island</a>, located approximately 33 miles (53&#160;km) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence, Rhode Island</a>, 20 miles (32&#160;km) south of <a href="/wiki/Fall_River,_Massachusetts" title="Fall River, Massachusetts">Fall River, Massachusetts</a>, 73 miles (117&#160;km) south of Boston, and 180 miles (290&#160;km) northeast of New York City. It is known as a New England summer resort and is famous for its historic <a href="/wiki/Newport_Mansions" class="mw-redirect" title="Newport Mansions">mansions</a> and its rich sailing history. It was the location of the first U.S. Open tournaments in both <a href="/wiki/US_Open_(tennis)" title="US Open (tennis)">tennis</a> and <a href="/wiki/US_Open_(golf)" class="mw-redirect" title="US Open (golf)">golf</a>, as well as every challenge to the <a href="/wiki/America%27s_Cup" title="America&#39;s Cup">America's Cup</a> between 1930 and 1983. It is also the home of <a href="/wiki/Salve_Regina_University" title="Salve Regina University">Salve Regina University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Naval_Station_Newport" title="Naval Station Newport">Naval Station Newport</a>, which houses the United States <a href="/wiki/Naval_War_College" title="Naval War College">Naval War College</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Undersea_Warfare_Center" title="Naval Undersea Warfare Center">Naval Undersea Warfare Center</a>, and an important Navy training center. It was a major 18th-century port city and also contains a high number of buildings from the <a href="/wiki/Colonial_history_of_the_United_States" title="Colonial history of the United States">Colonial</a> era.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The city is the county seat of Newport County, which has no governmental functions other than court administrative and sheriff corrections boundaries. It was known for being the location of the "<a href="/wiki/Summer_White_Houses" class="mw-redirect" title="Summer White Houses">Summer White Houses</a>" during the administrations of Presidents <a href="/wiki/Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Dwight D. Eisenhower">Dwight D. Eisenhower</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a>. The population was 24,027 as of 2013.
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Newport,_R.I._in_1730_(NYPL_Hades-254162-478731).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Newport%2C_R.I._in_1730_%28NYPL_Hades-254162-478731%29.jpg/220px-Newport%2C_R.I._in_1730_%28NYPL_Hades-254162-478731%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="161" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Newport%2C_R.I._in_1730_%28NYPL_Hades-254162-478731%29.jpg/330px-Newport%2C_R.I._in_1730_%28NYPL_Hades-254162-478731%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Newport%2C_R.I._in_1730_%28NYPL_Hades-254162-478731%29.jpg/440px-Newport%2C_R.I._in_1730_%28NYPL_Hades-254162-478731%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4284" data-file-height="3128" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Newport,_R.I._in_1730_(NYPL_Hades-254162-478731).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Newport, R.I. in 1730</i>, <a class="external text" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Newport,_R.I._in_1730_(NYPL_Hades-254162-478731).jpg">New York Public Library</a></div></div></div>
<p>Newport was founded in 1639 on <a href="/wiki/Aquidneck_Island" title="Aquidneck Island">Aquidneck Island</a>, which was called Rhode Island at the time. Its eight founders and first officers were <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Easton" title="Nicholas Easton">Nicholas Easton</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Coddington" title="William Coddington">William Coddington</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Clarke_(Baptist_minister)" title="John Clarke (Baptist minister)">John Clarke</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Coggeshall" title="John Coggeshall">John Coggeshall</a>, <a href="/wiki/William_Brenton" title="William Brenton">William Brenton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Clarke_(Governor)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jeremy Clarke (Governor)">Jeremy Clark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Hazard" title="Thomas Hazard">Thomas Hazard</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Henry_Bull_(Governor)" class="mw-redirect" title="Henry Bull (Governor)">Henry Bull</a>. Many of these people had been part of the settlement at <a href="/wiki/Portsmouth,_Rhode_Island" title="Portsmouth, Rhode Island">Portsmouth</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Anne_Hutchinson" title="Anne Hutchinson">Anne Hutchinson</a> and her followers. They separated within a year of that settlement, however, and Coddington and others began the settlement of Newport on the southern side of the island.
</p><p>Newport grew to be the largest of the four original settlements which became the <a href="/wiki/Colony_of_Rhode_Island_and_Providence_Plantations" title="Colony of Rhode Island and Providence Plantations">Colony of Rhode Island and Providence Plantations</a>, which also included <a href="/wiki/Providence_Plantations" title="Providence Plantations">Providence Plantations</a> and <a href="/wiki/Warwick,_Rhode_Island" title="Warwick, Rhode Island">Shawomett</a>. Many of the first colonists in Newport became <a href="/wiki/Baptists" title="Baptists">Baptists</a>, and the second Baptist congregation in Rhode Island was formed in 1640 under the leadership of John Clarke.  In 1658, a group of Jews were welcomed to settle in Newport; they were fleeing the <a href="/wiki/Inquisition" title="Inquisition">Inquisition</a> in Spain and Portugal but had not been permitted to settle elsewhere. The Newport congregation is now referred to as Congregation Jeshuat Israel and is the second-oldest Jewish congregation in the United States. It meets in <a href="/wiki/Touro_Synagogue" title="Touro Synagogue">Touro Synagogue</a>, the oldest synagogue in the United States.
</p><p>The Colony of Rhode Island and Providence Plantations received its royal charter in 1663, and <a href="/wiki/Benedict_Arnold_(governor)" title="Benedict Arnold (governor)">Benedict Arnold</a> was elected as its first governor at Newport. The <a href="/wiki/Old_Colony_House" title="Old Colony House">Old Colony House</a> served as a seat of Rhode Island's government upon its completion in 1741 at the head of <a href="/wiki/Washington_Square_(Newport,_Rhode_Island)" title="Washington Square (Newport, Rhode Island)">Washington Square</a>, until the current <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_State_House" title="Rhode Island State House">Rhode Island State House</a> in Providence was completed in 1904 and Providence became the state's sole capital city. Newport became the most important port in colonial Rhode Island, and a public school was established in 1640.
</p><p>The commercial activity which raised Newport to its fame as a rich port was begun by a second wave of Portuguese Jews who settled there around the middle of the 18th century. They had been practicing Judaism in secret for 300 years in Portugal, and they were attracted to Rhode Island because of the freedom of worship there. They brought with them commercial experience and connections, capital, and a spirit of enterprise. Most prominent among those were Jacob Rodrigues Rivera, who arrived in 1745 (died 1789) and <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Lopez" title="Aaron Lopez">Aaron Lopez</a>, who came in 1752 (died May 28, 1782). Rivera introduced the manufacture of <a href="/wiki/Sperm_oil" title="Sperm oil">sperm oil</a> which became one of Newport's leading industries and made the town rich. Newport developed 17 manufactories of oil and candles and enjoyed a practical monopoly of this trade until the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>.
</p><p>Aaron Lopez is credited with making Newport an important center of trade.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He encouraged 40 Portuguese Jewish families to settle there, and Newport had 150 vessels engaged in trade within 14 years of his activity.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He was involved in the slave trade and manufactured spermaceti candles, ships, barrels, rum, chocolate, textiles, clothes, shoes, hats, and bottles.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  He became the wealthiest man in Newport but was denied citizenship on religious grounds, even though British law protected the rights of Jews to become citizens.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He appealed to the Rhode Island legislature for redress and was refused with this ruling: "Inasmuch as the said Aaron Lopez hath declared himself by religion a Jew, this Assembly doth not admit himself nor any other of that religion to the full freedom of this Colony. So that the said Aaron Lopez nor any other of said religion is not liable to be chosen into any office in this colony nor allowed to give vote as a free man in choosing others."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Lopez persisted by applying for citizenship in Massachusetts, where it was granted.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>From the mid-17th century, the religious tolerance in Newport attracted numbers of <a href="/wiki/Quakers" title="Quakers">Quakers</a>, known also as the <i>Society of Friends</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Great_Friends_Meeting_House" title="Great Friends Meeting House">Great Friends Meeting House</a> in Newport (1699) is the oldest existing structure of worship in Rhode Island.
</p><p>In 1727, <a href="/wiki/James_Franklin_(printer)" title="James Franklin (printer)">James Franklin</a> (brother of <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Franklin" title="Benjamin Franklin">Benjamin</a>) printed the <i>Rhode-Island Almanack</i> in Newport. In 1732, he published the first newspaper, the <i>Rhode Island Gazette</i>. In 1758, his son James founded the weekly newspaper <i>Mercury</i>. The famous 18th century <a href="/wiki/Goddard_and_Townsend" title="Goddard and Townsend">Goddard and Townsend</a> furniture was also made in Newport.
</p><p>Throughout the 18th century, Newport suffered from an imbalance of trade with the largest colonial ports.  As a result, Newport merchants were forced to develop alternatives to conventional exports.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In the 1720s, Colonial leaders arrested many pirates, acting under pressure from the British government. Many were hanged in Newport and were buried on <a href="/wiki/Goat_Island,_Rhode_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Goat Island, Rhode Island">Goat Island</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Slave_trade">Slave trade</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newport,_Rhode_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Slave trade">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Newport was a major center of the <a href="/wiki/Slave_trade" class="mw-redirect" title="Slave trade">slave trade</a> in colonial and early America, active in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_slave_trade#Triangular_trade" title="Atlantic slave trade">"triangle trade"</a> in which slave-produced sugar and molasses from the Caribbean were carried to Rhode Island and distilled into <a href="/wiki/Rum" title="Rum">rum</a>, which was then carried to West Africa and exchanged for captives. In 1764, Rhode Island had about 30 rum distilleries, 22 in Newport alone. The <a href="/wiki/Common_Burial_Ground" class="mw-redirect" title="Common Burial Ground">Common Burial Ground</a> on Farewell Street was where most of the slaves were buried.
</p><p>Sixty percent of slave-trading voyages launched from North America issued from tiny Rhode Island, in some years more than 90%, and many from Newport. Almost half were trafficked illegally, breaking a 1787 state law prohibiting residents of the state from trading in slaves. Slave traders were also breaking federal statutes of 1794 and 1800 barring Americans from carrying slaves to ports outside the United States, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Act_Prohibiting_Importation_of_Slaves" title="Act Prohibiting Importation of Slaves">1807 Congressional act</a> abolishing the transatlantic slave trade. A few Rhode Island families made substantial fortunes in the trade. <a href="/wiki/William_Vernon" title="William Vernon">William</a> and Samuel Vernon were Newport merchants who later played an important role in financing the creation of the United States Navy; they sponsored 30 African slaving ventures. However, it was the DeWolfs of <a href="/wiki/Bristol,_Rhode_Island" title="Bristol, Rhode Island">Bristol, Rhode Island</a>, and most notably <a href="/wiki/James_De_Wolf" class="mw-redirect" title="James De Wolf">James De Wolf</a>, who were the largest slave-trading family in all of North America, mounting more than 80 transatlantic voyages, most of them illegal. The Rhode Island slave trade was broadly based. Seven hundred Rhode Islanders owned or captained slave ships, including most substantial merchants, and many ordinary shopkeepers and tradesmen who purchased shares in slaving voyages.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to being one of America's most active slave ports, Newport was also home to a small community of abolitionists and free blacks. Reverend <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Hopkins_(theologian)" title="Samuel Hopkins (theologian)">Samuel Hopkins</a>, minister at Newport's <a href="/wiki/United_Congregational_Church_(Newport,_Rhode_Island)" title="United Congregational Church (Newport, Rhode Island)">First Congregational Church</a>, has been called "America's first abolitionist".<sup id="cite_ref-ProJo20171219_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ProJo20171219-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Among subscribers to Hopkins' writings were 17 free black subscribers, most of whom lived in Newport.<sup id="cite_ref-ProJo20171219_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ProJo20171219-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> This community of free blacks, including <a href="/wiki/Newport_Gardner" title="Newport Gardner">Newport Gardner</a>, founded the <a href="/wiki/Free_African_Union_Society" title="Free African Union Society">Free African Union Society</a> in 1780, the first African <a href="/wiki/Benefit_society" title="Benefit society">mutual aid society</a> in America.<sup id="cite_ref-1696Heritage_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1696Heritage-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="American_Revolutionary_era">American Revolutionary era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newport,_Rhode_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: American Revolutionary era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:W-RSimpleMap.gif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/W-RSimpleMap.gif/250px-W-RSimpleMap.gif" decoding="async" width="250" height="347" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/W-RSimpleMap.gif/375px-W-RSimpleMap.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/W-RSimpleMap.gif/500px-W-RSimpleMap.gif 2x" data-file-width="1960" data-file-height="2718" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:W-RSimpleMap.gif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>NPS map of the <a href="/wiki/Washington-Rochambeau_Revolutionary_Route" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington-Rochambeau Revolutionary Route">W3R Route</a></div></div></div>
<p>Newport was the scene of much activity during the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>. <a href="/wiki/William_Ellery" title="William Ellery">William Ellery</a> came from Newport, one of the signers of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Declaration_of_Independence" title="United States Declaration of Independence">Declaration of Independence</a>. He later served on the Naval Committee.
</p><p>In the winter of 1775 and 1776, the <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_Legislature" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhode Island Legislature">Rhode Island Legislature</a> put militia General <a href="/wiki/William_West_(Rhode_Island_politician)" title="William West (Rhode Island politician)">William West</a> in charge of rooting out <a href="/wiki/Loyalist_(American_Revolution)" title="Loyalist (American Revolution)">loyalists</a> in Newport, and several notable individuals were exiled to the northern part of the state, such as <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Wanton" title="Joseph Wanton">Joseph Wanton</a> and Thomas Vernon.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In the fall of 1776, the British saw that Newport could be used as a naval base to attack New York (which they had recently occupied), so they took over the city.  The population of Newport had divided loyalties; many pro–independence Patriots left town, while loyalist Tories remained.  Newport was a British stronghold for the next three years.
</p><p>In the summer of 1778, the Americans began the campaign known as the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Rhode_Island" title="Battle of Rhode Island">Battle of Rhode Island</a>. This was the first joint operation between the Americans and the French after the signing of the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Alliance_(1778)" title="Treaty of Alliance (1778)">Treaty of Alliance</a>. The Americans based in <a href="/wiki/Tiverton,_Rhode_Island" title="Tiverton, Rhode Island">Tiverton</a> planned a formal <a href="/wiki/Siege" title="Siege">siege</a> of the town. However, the French refused to take part in it, wanting a frontal assault.  This weakened the American position, and the British were able to expel the Americans from the island. The following year, the British abandoned Newport, wanting to concentrate their forces in New York.
</p><p>On July 10, 1780, <a href="/wiki/Exp%C3%A9dition_Particuli%C3%A8re" title="Expédition Particulière">a French expedition</a> arrived in <a href="/wiki/Narragansett_Bay" title="Narragansett Bay">Narragansett Bay</a> off Newport with an army of 450 officers and 5,300 men, sent by <a href="/wiki/King_Louis_XVI" class="mw-redirect" title="King Louis XVI">King Louis XVI</a> and commanded by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Baptiste_Donatien_de_Vimeur,_comte_de_Rochambeau" title="Jean-Baptiste Donatien de Vimeur, comte de Rochambeau">Rochambeau</a>.  For the rest of the war, Newport was the base of the French forces in the United States.  In July 1781, Rochambeau was finally able to leave Newport for <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">Providence</a> to begin the <a href="/wiki/Washington%E2%80%93Rochambeau_Revolutionary_Route" title="Washington–Rochambeau Revolutionary Route">decisive march</a> to <a href="/wiki/Yorktown,_Virginia" title="Yorktown, Virginia">Yorktown, Virginia</a>, along with General <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">George Washington</a>. The first Roman <a href="/wiki/Catholic_mass" class="mw-redirect" title="Catholic mass">Catholic mass</a> in Rhode Island was said in Newport during this time. The <a href="/wiki/Rochambeau_Monument_(Newport,_RI)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rochambeau Monument (Newport, RI)">Rochambeau Monument</a> in Kings Park on Wellington Avenue along Newport Harbor commemorates Rochambeau's contributions to the Revolutionary War and to Newport's history.
</p><p>Newport's population had fallen from over 9,000 (according to the census of 1774) to fewer than 4,000 by the time that the war ended (1783). Over 200 abandoned buildings were torn down in the 1780s. Also, the war destroyed Newport's economic wealth, as years of military occupation closed the city to any form of trade. The Newport merchants moved away, some to Providence, others to <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> and New York.
</p><p>It was in Newport that the <a href="/wiki/Rhode_Island_General_Assembly" title="Rhode Island General Assembly">Rhode Island General Assembly</a> voted to ratify the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Constitution" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Constitution">Constitution</a> in 1790 and become the 13th state, acting under pressure from the merchant community of Providence.
</p><p>The city was the last residence of Commodore <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Hazard_Perry" title="Oliver Hazard Perry">Oliver Hazard Perry</a> and the birthplace of Commodore <a href="/wiki/Matthew_C._Perry" title="Matthew C. Perry">Matthew C. Perry</a> and the Reverend <a href="/wiki/William_Ellery_Channing" title="William Ellery Channing">William Ellery Channing</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gilded_Age">Gilded Age</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newport,_Rhode_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Gilded Age">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Beginning in the mid-nineteenth century, wealthy southern planters seeking to escape the heat began to build summer cottages on Bellevue Avenue, such as <a href="/wiki/Kingscote_(mansion)" title="Kingscote (mansion)">Kingscote</a> (1839).<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>  Around the middle of the century, wealthy <a href="/wiki/Yankee" title="Yankee">Yankees</a>, such as the Wetmore family, also began constructing larger mansions, such as <a href="/wiki/Chateau-sur-Mer" title="Chateau-sur-Mer">Chateau-sur-Mer</a> (1852) nearby.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Most of these early families made a substantial part of their fortunes in the <a href="/wiki/Old_China_Trade" title="Old China Trade">Old China Trade</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By the turn of the 20th century, many of the nation's wealthiest families were summering in Newport, including the <a href="/wiki/Vanderbilt_family" title="Vanderbilt family">Vanderbilts</a>, <a href="/wiki/Astor_family" title="Astor family">Astors</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Widener_family" title="Widener family">Widener family</a>, who constructed the largest "cottages", such as <a href="/wiki/The_Breakers" title="The Breakers">The Breakers</a> (1895) and <a href="/wiki/Miramar_(mansion)" title="Miramar (mansion)">Miramar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  They resided for a brief summer social season in grand, gilded mansions with elaborate receiving rooms, dining rooms, music rooms, and ballrooms—but with few bedrooms, since the guests were expected to have "cottages" of their own. Many of the homes were designed by New York architect <a href="/wiki/Richard_Morris_Hunt" title="Richard Morris Hunt">Richard Morris Hunt</a>, who kept a house in Newport himself.
</p><p>The social scene at Newport is described in <a href="/wiki/Edith_Wharton" title="Edith Wharton">Edith Wharton</a>'s novel <i><a href="/wiki/The_Age_of_Innocence" title="The Age of Innocence">The Age of Innocence</a></i>. Wharton's own Newport "cottage" was called <i>Land's End.</i> Today, many mansions continue in private use. <a href="/wiki/Hammersmith_Farm" title="Hammersmith Farm">Hammersmith Farm</a> is the mansion where <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">John F. Kennedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="Jackie Kennedy">Jackie Kennedy</a> held their wedding reception; it was open to tourists as a "house museum", but has since been purchased and reconverted into a private residence. Many other mansions are open to tourists; still others were converted into academic buildings for <a href="/wiki/Salve_Regina_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Salve Regina College">Salve Regina College</a> in the 1930s, when the owners could no longer afford their tax bills.
</p><p>In the mid-19th century, a large number of <a href="/wiki/Irish_immigrants" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish immigrants">Irish immigrants</a> settled in Newport. The Fifth Ward of Newport in the southern part of the city became a staunch Irish neighborhood for many generations. To this day, St. Patrick's Day is an important day of pride and celebration in Newport, with a large parade going down Thames Street.
</p><p>The oldest Catholic parish in Rhode Island is <a href="/wiki/St._Mary%27s_Church_Complex_(Newport,_Rhode_Island)" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Mary&#39;s Church Complex (Newport, Rhode Island)">St. Mary's</a>, located on Spring Street—though the current building is not the original one.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="20th_century_and_beyond">20th century and beyond</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newport,_Rhode_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 20th century and beyond">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rhode Island did not have a fixed capital during and after the colonial era but rotated its legislative sessions among Providence, Newport, Bristol, East Greenwich, and South Kingstown. In 1854, the sessions were eliminated in the cities other than Providence and Newport, and Newport was finally dropped in 1900. A constitutional amendment that year restricted the meetings of the legislature to Providence.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/John_Fitzgerald_Kennedy" class="mw-redirect" title="John Fitzgerald Kennedy">John Fitzgerald Kennedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacqueline_Kennedy_Onassis" title="Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis">Jacqueline Bouvier</a> were married in St. Mary's Church in Newport on September 12, 1953.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Presidents <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">Kennedy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dwight_Eisenhower" class="mw-redirect" title="Dwight Eisenhower">Eisenhower</a> both made Newport the sites of their "<a href="/wiki/Summer_White_Houses" class="mw-redirect" title="Summer White Houses">Summer White Houses</a>" during their years in office. Eisenhower stayed at Quarters A at the <a href="/wiki/Naval_War_College" title="Naval War College">Naval War College</a> and at what became known as the <a href="/wiki/Eisenhower_House" title="Eisenhower House">Eisenhower House</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> while Kennedy used <a href="/wiki/Hammersmith_Farm" title="Hammersmith Farm">Hammersmith Farm</a> next door.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:262px;"><a href="/wiki/File:NavalWarCollege.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/NavalWarCollege.jpg/260px-NavalWarCollege.jpg" decoding="async" width="260" height="68" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/NavalWarCollege.jpg/390px-NavalWarCollege.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/NavalWarCollege.jpg/520px-NavalWarCollege.jpg 2x" data-file-width="867" data-file-height="227" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:NavalWarCollege.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Naval_War_College" title="Naval War College">Naval War College</a></div></div></div>
<p>The city has long been entwined with the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">United States Navy</a>. It held the campus of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Naval_Academy" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Naval Academy">U.S. Naval Academy</a> during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> (1861–65) when the undergraduate officer training school was temporarily moved north from <a href="/wiki/Annapolis,_Maryland" title="Annapolis, Maryland">Annapolis, Maryland</a>. From 1952 to 1973, it hosted the Cruiser-Destroyer Force of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Fleet_Forces_Command" title="United States Fleet Forces Command">U.S. Atlantic Fleet</a>, and subsequently it has hosted smaller numbers of warships from time to time. Today it hosts the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Station_Newport" title="Naval Station Newport">Naval Station Newport</a> (NAVSTA Newport) and remains home to the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Naval_War_College" title="Naval War College">Naval War College</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Education_and_Training_Command" title="Naval Education and Training Command">Naval Education and Training Command</a> (NETC), the center for <a href="/wiki/Surface_Warfare_Officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Surface Warfare Officer">Surface Warfare Officer</a> training, numerous other schools, and the headquarters of the <a href="/wiki/Naval_Undersea_Warfare_Center" title="Naval Undersea Warfare Center">Naval Undersea Warfare Center</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Ship_decommissioning" class="mw-redirect" title="Ship decommissioning">decommissioned</a> aircraft carrier <a href="/wiki/USS_Saratoga_(CV-60)" title="USS Saratoga (CV-60)">USS&#160;<i>Saratoga</i>&#160;<span class="nowrap">(CV-60)</span></a> was moored in an inactive status at the docks previously used by the Cruiser-Destroyer Force, until it was towed to Brownsville, Texas in August–September 2014 to be dismantled. The <a href="/wiki/USS_Forrestal_(CV-59)" title="USS Forrestal (CV-59)">USS&#160;<i>Forrestal</i>&#160;<span class="nowrap">(CV-59)</span></a> shared the pier until June 2010.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The departure of the Cruiser-Destroyer fleet from Newport and the closure of nearby <a href="/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_Quonset_Point" class="mw-redirect" title="Naval Air Station Quonset Point">Naval Air Station Quonset Point</a> in 1973 were devastating to the local economy. The population of Newport decreased, businesses closed, and property values plummeted. However, in the late 1960s, the city began revitalizing the downtown area with the construction of America's Cup Avenue, malls of stores and condominiums, and upscale hotels. Construction was completed on the <a href="/wiki/Claiborne_Pell_Newport_Bridge" title="Claiborne Pell Newport Bridge">Newport Bridge</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Preservation_Society_of_Newport_County" title="Preservation Society of Newport County">Preservation Society of Newport County</a> began opening Newport's historic mansions to the public, and the tourist industry became Newport's primary commercial enterprise over the subsequent years.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography_and_climate">Geography and climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newport,_Rhode_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Geography and climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Newport_shoreline_of_Easton_Bay_looking_south_from_cliffside_overlook_at_east_end_of_Narragansett_Ave.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Newport_shoreline_of_Easton_Bay_looking_south_from_cliffside_overlook_at_east_end_of_Narragansett_Ave.JPG/220px-Newport_shoreline_of_Easton_Bay_looking_south_from_cliffside_overlook_at_east_end_of_Narragansett_Ave.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Newport_shoreline_of_Easton_Bay_looking_south_from_cliffside_overlook_at_east_end_of_Narragansett_Ave.JPG/330px-Newport_shoreline_of_Easton_Bay_looking_south_from_cliffside_overlook_at_east_end_of_Narragansett_Ave.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Newport_shoreline_of_Easton_Bay_looking_south_from_cliffside_overlook_at_east_end_of_Narragansett_Ave.JPG/440px-Newport_shoreline_of_Easton_Bay_looking_south_from_cliffside_overlook_at_east_end_of_Narragansett_Ave.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="2256" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Newport_shoreline_of_Easton_Bay_looking_south_from_cliffside_overlook_at_east_end_of_Narragansett_Ave.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Shoreline of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Easton_Bay&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Easton Bay (page does not exist)">Easton Bay</a> looking south from cliffside at east end of Narragansett Ave.</div></div></div>
<p>Newport is located at <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion"><a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Newport,_Rhode_Island&amp;params=41_29_17_N_71_18_45_W_"><span class="geo-default"><span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location"><span class="latitude">41°29′17″N</span> <span class="longitude">71°18′45″W</span></span></span><span class="geo-multi-punct">&#xfeff; / &#xfeff;</span><span class="geo-nondefault"><span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">41.48806°N 71.31250°W</span><span style="display:none">&#xfeff; / <span class="geo">41.48806; -71.31250</span></span></span></a></span>. It is the most populous municipality on <a href="/wiki/Aquidneck_Island" title="Aquidneck Island">Aquidneck Island</a> in <a href="/wiki/Narragansett_Bay" title="Narragansett Bay">Narragansett Bay</a>. According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 11.4 square miles (29.5&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), of which 7.7 square miles (19.9&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) is land and 3.7 square miles (9.6&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), or 32.64%, is water.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Claiborne_Pell_Newport_Bridge" title="Claiborne Pell Newport Bridge">Newport Bridge</a>, the longest suspension bridge in New England, connects Newport to neighboring <a href="/wiki/Conanicut_Island" title="Conanicut Island">Conanicut Island</a> across the East Passage of the Narragansett.
</p>

<p>As of 2013, there were 24,027 people, 10,616 households, and 4,933 families residing in the city. The population density was 3,204.2 people per square mile (1,239.8/km²). There were 13,069 housing units at an average density of 1,697.3 per square mile (656.7/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 82.5% <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 6.9% <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.8% <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 1.4% <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a>, 0.1% <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific Islander (U.S. Census)">Pacific Islander</a>, 3.1% <a href="/wiki/Race_(United_States_Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (United States Census)">some other race</a>, and 5.2% from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 8.4% of the population (3.3% <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rican_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Puerto Rican American">Puerto Rican</a>, 1.2% <a href="/wiki/Guatemalan_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Guatemalan American">Guatemalan</a>, 1.1% <a href="/wiki/Mexican_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Mexican American">Mexican</a><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>).<sup id="cite_ref-Census_2010_DP_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010_DP-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were 10,616 households, out of which 21.2% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 30.9% were headed by married couples living together, 12.4% had a female householder with no husband present, and 53.5% were non-families. 41.4% of all households were made up of individuals, and 12.7% were someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.05, and the average family size was 2.82.<sup id="cite_ref-Census_2010_DP_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010_DP-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The age distribution was 16.5% under the age of 18, 16.3% from 18 to 24, 28.1% from 25 to 44, 24.9% from 45 to 64, and 14.2% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 36.4 years. For every 100 females, there were 95.8 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 94.3 males.<sup id="cite_ref-Census_2010_DP_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Census_2010_DP-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For the period 2009-11, the estimated median annual income for a household in the city was $59,388, and the median income for a family was $83,880. Male full-time workers had a median income of $52,221 versus $41,679 for females. The per capita income for the city was $35,644. About 10.7% of the population were below the poverty line.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">Culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newport,_Rhode_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Newport has one of the highest concentrations of colonial homes in the nation in the downtown <a href="/wiki/Newport_Historic_District_(Rhode_Island)" title="Newport Historic District (Rhode Island)">Newport Historic District</a>, one of three <a href="/wiki/National_Historic_Landmark_District" class="mw-redirect" title="National Historic Landmark District">National Historic Landmark Districts</a> in the city, and Newport's colonial heritage is well preserved and documented at the <a href="/wiki/Newport_Historical_Society" title="Newport Historical Society">Newport Historical Society</a>.  In addition to the colonial architecture, the city is known for its <a href="/wiki/Gilded_Age" title="Gilded Age">Gilded Age</a> mansions, summer "cottages" built in varying styles copied from the royal palaces of Europe.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/White_Horse_Tavern_(Rhode_Island)" class="mw-redirect" title="White Horse Tavern (Rhode Island)">White Horse Tavern</a> was built prior to 1673 and is one of the oldest taverns in the US.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Newport is also home to the <a href="/wiki/Touro_Synagogue" title="Touro Synagogue">Touro Synagogue</a>, one of the oldest Jewish houses of worship in the Western hemisphere, and to the <a href="/wiki/Newport_Public_Library" title="Newport Public Library">Newport Public Library</a> and <a href="/wiki/Redwood_Library_and_Athenaeum" title="Redwood Library and Athenaeum">Redwood Library and Athenaeum</a>, one of the nation's oldest lending libraries.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Outdoor_activities">Outdoor activities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newport,_Rhode_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Outdoor activities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Newport_sailing_2009b.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Newport_sailing_2009b.JPG/220px-Newport_sailing_2009b.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="115" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Newport_sailing_2009b.JPG/330px-Newport_sailing_2009b.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Newport_sailing_2009b.JPG/440px-Newport_sailing_2009b.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4288" data-file-height="2236" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Newport_sailing_2009b.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sailing</div></div></div>
<p>Aquidneck Island has many beaches, both public and private. The largest public beach is Easton's beach or First Beach, which has a view of the <a href="/wiki/Newport_Cliff_Walk" title="Newport Cliff Walk">Newport Cliff Walk</a>. Sachuest Beach, or Second Beach, in Middletown is the second largest beach in the area. <a href="/wiki/Gooseberry_Beach" title="Gooseberry Beach">Gooseberry Beach</a> is a private beach but is open to the public on certain days of the year; it is located on Ocean Drive, along with the private <a href="/wiki/Bailey%27s_Beach" title="Bailey&#39;s Beach">Bailey's Beach</a> and Hazard's Beach. The Cliff Walk is one of the most popular attractions in the city. It is a 3.5-mile (5.6&#160;km) public access walkway bordering the shoreline and has been designated a <a href="/wiki/National_Recreation_Trail" title="National Recreation Trail">National Recreation Trail</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Brenton_Point_State_Park" title="Brenton Point State Park">Brenton Point State Park</a> is the site of the annual Brenton Point Kite Festival. Newport is also home to the Newport Country Club, which hosted the 2007 Women's US Open and the 1995 Men's US Amateurs. <a href="/wiki/Fort_Adams" title="Fort Adams">Fort Adams</a> dates back to the War of 1812; it houses the Museum of Yachting and hosts both the <a href="/wiki/Newport_Folk_Festival" title="Newport Folk Festival">Newport Folk Festival</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Newport_Jazz_Festival" title="Newport Jazz Festival">Newport Jazz Festival</a>.
</p><p>As of Fall 2013, Newport has been designated a Bronze Bicycle Friendly Community by the <a href="/wiki/League_of_American_Bicyclists" title="League of American Bicyclists">League of American Bicyclists</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/International_Tennis_Hall_of_Fame" title="International Tennis Hall of Fame">International Tennis Hall of Fame</a> is also located in Newport. The Campbell's <a href="/wiki/Hall_of_Fame_Tennis_Championships" class="mw-redirect" title="Hall of Fame Tennis Championships">Hall of Fame Tennis Championships</a> is held every year in early July, the week following <a href="/wiki/The_Championships,_Wimbledon" title="The Championships, Wimbledon">Wimbledon</a>. The week also includes annual enshrinements into the Hall of Fame. The annual Citizens Bank Pell Bridge Run is held every to raise money for local charities.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Newport,_Rhode_Island&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Newport Public Schools operates Claiborne Pell Elementary School, Thompson Middle School, <a href="/wiki/Rogers_High_School_(Newport,_Rhode_Island)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rogers High School (Newport, Rhode Island)">Rogers High School</a>, Newport Area Career and Technical Center, and Aquidneck Island Adult Learning Center.<sup id="cite_ref-Borgoverc_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Borgoverc-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> St. Michael's Country Day School is a private elementary school.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Post-secondary schools include the Naval Academy Preparatory School, <a href="/wiki/Salve_Regina_University" title="Salve Regina University">Salve Regina University</a>, <a href="/wiki/Naval_War_College" title="Naval War College">Naval War College</a>, International Yacht Restoration School, and the <a href="/wiki/Community_College_of_Rhode_Island" title="Community College of Rhode Island">Community College of Rhode Island</a> Newport Campus.
</p>
