	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Wilfred Hawker</h1>
	
	<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Wilfred Hawker</b> (1955 – 13 March 1982) was a sergeant-major in the <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Surinamese</a> <a href="/wiki/Military_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="Military of Suriname">military</a> who was involved in two unsuccessful <a href="/wiki/Coups_d%27%C3%A9tat" class="mw-redirect" title="Coups d&#39;état">coups d'état</a> in the early 1980s. Hawker was <a href="/wiki/Capital_punishment" title="Capital punishment">executed</a> by the ruling military government for his role in the plots. He was the last person to be legally executed by <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_Hawker&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 1980, <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a> gained control of Suriname after staging a successful military coup, which he led with fifteen other sergeants. Hawker was a member of the group of sixteen who overthrew the elected government. In August, Bouterse dissolved <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Suriname" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliament of Suriname">parliament</a> and declared a <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">state of emergency</a>. 
</p><p>Hawker disagreed with these actions and he led a <a href="/wiki/Right-wing" class="mw-redirect" title="Right-wing">right-wing</a>-inspired uprising against Bouterse's régime, which began on 15 March 1981. The coup failed and Hawker was imprisoned.
</p><p>On 11 March 1982, Hawker escaped from prison in the midst of another failed coup, this one led by <a href="/wiki/Surendre_Rambocus" class="mw-redirect" title="Surendre Rambocus">Surendre Rambocus</a>. Hawker was injured the next day and was taken to a military hospital. Bouterse's soldiers arrested Hawker in the hospital and took him by <a href="/wiki/Stretcher" title="Stretcher">stretcher</a> to <a href="/wiki/Fort_Zeelandia_(Paramaribo)" title="Fort Zeelandia (Paramaribo)">Fort Zeelandia</a>. There he was summarily tried and condemned to death for <a href="/wiki/Treason" title="Treason">treason</a>. Hawker was executed by <a href="/wiki/Firing_squad" class="mw-redirect" title="Firing squad">firing squad</a>, which was shown on a live Surinamese television broadcast.
</p><p>In November 2007, Surinamese military judge <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dhanoesdharie_Nankoesing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dhanoesdharie Nankoesing (page does not exist)">Dhanoesdharie Nankoesing</a> was pressured to resign after the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> periodical <i>Obsession</i> published a <a href="/wiki/Photograph" title="Photograph">photograph</a> of Nankoesing holding a <a href="/wiki/Rifle" title="Rifle">rifle</a> and speaking with Hawker on the stretcher in March 1982 at Fort Zeelandia.<sup id="cite_ref-cairo_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cairo-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Nankoesing denied any involvement in Hawker's trial or executions. He said that he was a Surinamese soldier who carried a weapon as others did.<sup id="cite_ref-cairo_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cairo-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
