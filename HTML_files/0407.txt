<p>The <b>Paramaccan</b> are a <a href="/wiki/Bushinengue" title="Bushinengue">Bushinengue</a> ethnic group living in the forested interior of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, mainly in the <a href="/wiki/Marowijne_District" title="Marowijne District">Marowijne District</a>.
</p><p>It is also the eponymous term for their language, which is <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>-based with influences from <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a> and other languages. It is similar to the languages spoken by the <a href="/wiki/Aluku" title="Aluku">Aluku</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kwinti" class="mw-redirect" title="Kwinti">Kwinti</a>.
</p><p>It has less than 1,000 speakers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
