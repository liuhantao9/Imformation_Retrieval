<p>The <b>National Assembly</b> (<i>De Nationale Assemblée</i>, <i>The Assembly</i>, commonly abbreviated "DNA") is the Parliament, representing the <a href="/wiki/Legislature" title="Legislature">legislative branch</a> of government in <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Unicameral" class="mw-redirect" title="Unicameral">unicameral</a> legislature. The assembly is situated at the Independence Square in Paramaribo, after a fire completely destroyed the old building of representation on August 1, 1996.
</p><p>The 51 members of parliament are elected every five years by <a href="/wiki/Proportional_representation" title="Proportional representation">proportional representation</a> on the basis of the country's component <a href="/wiki/Districts_of_Suriname" title="Districts of Suriname">districts</a>. The most recent elections were held on May 25, 2010. On May 30, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Simons" title="Jennifer Simons">Jennifer Simons</a> was appointed as Chair of the assembly. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruth_Wijdenbosch&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ruth Wijdenbosch (page does not exist)">Ruth Wijdenbosch</a> was the first woman to be appointed as Vice Chair.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Assembly_(Suriname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AssembleeSurinaam.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/AssembleeSurinaam.jpg/220px-AssembleeSurinaam.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="168" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/AssembleeSurinaam.jpg/330px-AssembleeSurinaam.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/AssembleeSurinaam.jpg/440px-AssembleeSurinaam.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2499" data-file-height="1905" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AssembleeSurinaam.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The National Assembly in <a href="/wiki/Paramaribo" title="Paramaribo">Paramaribo</a></div></div></div>
<p>The first representation was formed by the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Colonial_States&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Colonial States (page does not exist)">Colonial States</a>, from 1866. The name was changed to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Estates_of_Suriname&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Estates of Suriname (page does not exist)">Estates of Suriname</a> in 1936. When Suriname became an independent republic on November 25, 1975, the representation was named Parliament of the Republic of Suriname. This Parliament was made inoperative during the <a href="/wiki/Coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="Coup d&#39;état">coup d'état</a> of 1980. In 1985, the Parliament was replaced by an appointed Assembly. The National Assembly, in its current form, dates from 1987. In that year, democracy was reestablished after the coup and a new constitution was adopted, organizing the Assembly, so new elections could be held on November 25, 1987. From here on, elections were held in 1991, 1996, 2000, 2005 and 2010.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chairmen_since_1975">Chairmen since 1975</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_Assembly_(Suriname)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Chairmen since 1975">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From December 1973, <a href="/wiki/Emile_Wijntuin" title="Emile Wijntuin">Emile Wijntuin</a> was the Chairman of the States of Suriname, and remained Chairman of Parliament after Independence, until it was dissolved in August 1980.
</p><p>After the elections in 1987, <a href="/wiki/Jagernath_Lachmon" title="Jagernath Lachmon">Jagernath Lachmon</a> (<a href="/wiki/Progressive_Reform_Party_(Suriname)" title="Progressive Reform Party (Suriname)">VHP</a>) became Chairman, an office which he already had taken twice in the States of Suriname. Lachmon resigned in 1996, because he could not agree with the <a href="/wiki/Jules_Wijdenbosch" title="Jules Wijdenbosch">Wijdenbosch</a> government.
</p><p>On October 10, 1996, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Marijke_Djwalapersad&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Marijke Djwalapersad (page does not exist)">Marijke Djwalapersad</a> (<a href="/wiki/Basic_Party_for_Renewal_and_Democracy" title="Basic Party for Renewal and Democracy">BVD</a>) was elected as Chair, becoming the first woman in Suriname's history to assume this office. On July 24, 2000, Djwalapersad was succeeded by Jagernath Lachmon, who remained in office until his death in 2001. His fellow party man <a href="/wiki/Ramdien_Sardjoe" class="mw-redirect" title="Ramdien Sardjoe">Ramdien Sardjoe</a> took his place.
</p><p>After the elections of 2005, <a href="/wiki/Paul_Somohardjo" title="Paul Somohardjo">Paul Somohardjo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pertjajah_Luhur" title="Pertjajah Luhur">Pertjajah Luhur</a> (a party that was part of the <a href="/wiki/New_Front_for_Democracy_and_Development" title="New Front for Democracy and Development">New Front</a> combination) wanted to become <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_Suriname" title="Vice President of Suriname">Vice President of Suriname</a>. This did not seem feasible, since he was convicted in August 2003 for defamation, which resulted in a suspended two-month sentence and his removal as minister. Within the coalition was decided to make <a href="/wiki/Ramdien_Sardjoe" class="mw-redirect" title="Ramdien Sardjoe">Ramdien Sardjoe</a> vice president and Somohardjo Chairman of the National Assembly.
</p><p>On June 30, 2005, Somohardjo was elected to the proposed office with 29 out of 50 votes. <a href="/w/index.php?title=Caprino_Allendy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Caprino Allendy (page does not exist)">Caprino Allendy</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brotherhood_and_Unity_in_Politics" title="Brotherhood and Unity in Politics">BEP</a>/<a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Combination&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="A Combination (page does not exist)">A Combination</a>) was elected as vice-Chairman, with the same number of votes.
</p><p>On June 30, 2010, <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Simons" title="Jennifer Simons">Jennifer Simons</a> of <a href="/wiki/D%C3%A9si_Bouterse" title="Dési Bouterse">Dési Bouterse</a>'s <a href="/wiki/National_Democratic_Party_(Suriname)" title="National Democratic Party (Suriname)">NDP</a>/<a href="/wiki/Mega_Combination" class="mw-redirect" title="Mega Combination">MC</a> surprisingly won the office from Somohardjo, with 26 votes against 24 votes, and became the incumbent Chair of the Assembly. Ruth Wijdenbosch was elected as Vice Chair with 25 votes, one more than her rival, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anton_Paal&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Anton Paal (page does not exist)">Anton Paal</a> (<a href="/wiki/Progressive_Workers%27_and_Farmers%27_Union" title="Progressive Workers&#39; and Farmers&#39; Union">PALU</a>/<a href="/wiki/Mega_Combination" class="mw-redirect" title="Mega Combination">MC</a>). Remarkable is the fact that this is the first time that these offices are held by opposing politicians. It is also the first time that both offices are held by women.
</p>
