<p><b>Historic England</b> (officially the <b>Historic Buildings and Monuments Commission for England</b>) is an executive <a href="/wiki/Non-departmental_public_body" title="Non-departmental public body">non-departmental public body</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Her Majesty&#39;s Government">British Government</a> sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Culture,_Media_and_Sport" class="mw-redirect" title="Department for Culture, Media and Sport">Department for Culture, Media and Sport</a> (DCMS). It is tasked with protecting the historical environment of England by preserving and <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listing historic buildings</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scheduled_ancient_monument" class="mw-redirect" title="Scheduled ancient monument">ancient monuments</a> and by advising central and local government.
</p><p>The body was officially created by the <a href="/wiki/National_Heritage_Acts#National_Heritage_Act_1983" class="mw-redirect" title="National Heritage Acts">National Heritage Act 1983</a>, and operated from April 1984 to April 2015 under the name of <a href="/wiki/English_Heritage" title="English Heritage">English Heritage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HE_Role_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HE_Role-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, following the changes to English Heritage's structure that moved the protection of the National Heritage Collection into the <a href="/wiki/Voluntary_sector" title="Voluntary sector">voluntary sector</a> in the <a href="/wiki/English_Heritage" title="English Heritage">English Heritage Trust</a>, the body that remained was rebranded as <b>Historic England</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-New_Era_EH_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_Era_EH-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Historic England has a similar remit to and complements the work of <a href="/wiki/Natural_England" title="Natural England">Natural England</a>, which aims to protect the <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">natural environment</a>.
</p><p>The body also inherited the <a href="/wiki/Historic_England_Archive" title="Historic England Archive">Historic England Archive</a> from the old English Heritage, and projects linked to the archive such as Britain from Above, which saw the archive work with the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Commission_on_the_Ancient_and_Historical_Monuments_of_Wales" title="Royal Commission on the Ancient and Historical Monuments of Wales">Royal Commission on the Ancient and Historical Monuments of Wales</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Commission_on_the_Ancient_and_Historical_Monuments_of_Scotland" title="Royal Commission on the Ancient and Historical Monuments of Scotland">Royal Commission on the Ancient and Historical Monuments of Scotland</a> to digitise, catalogue and put online 96,000 of the oldest <a href="/wiki/Aerofilms" title="Aerofilms">Aerofilms</a> images. The archive also holds various nationally important collections and the results of older projects such as the work of the National Buildings Record, later absorbed by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Commission_on_the_Historical_Monuments_of_England" title="Royal Commission on the Historical Monuments of England">Royal Commission on the Historical Monuments of England</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Images_of_England" title="Images of England">Images of England</a> project which set out to create a freely accessible online database of the 370,000 listed properties in England as a snapshot in time at the turn of the millennium.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Remit">Remit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Historic_England&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Remit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:English_Heritage_-_National_Monuments_record_centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_309655.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/English_Heritage_-_National_Monuments_record_centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_309655.jpg/220px-English_Heritage_-_National_Monuments_record_centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_309655.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/English_Heritage_-_National_Monuments_record_centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_309655.jpg/330px-English_Heritage_-_National_Monuments_record_centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_309655.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/English_Heritage_-_National_Monuments_record_centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_309655.jpg/440px-English_Heritage_-_National_Monuments_record_centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_309655.jpg 2x" data-file-width="619" data-file-height="413" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:English_Heritage_-_National_Monuments_record_centre_-_geograph.org.uk_-_309655.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Historic England's <a href="/wiki/Swindon" title="Swindon">Swindon</a> office, home to their Archives</div></div></div>

<p>Historic England inherits English Heritage's position as the UK government's statutory adviser and a statutory consultee on all aspects of the historic environment and its heritage assets.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This includes archaeology on land and underwater, historic buildings sites and areas, designated landscapes and the historic elements of the wider landscape.<sup id="cite_ref-Funding_of_the_Arts_and_Heritage_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Funding_of_the_Arts_and_Heritage-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It monitors and reports on the state of England's heritage and publishes the annual <a href="/wiki/Heritage_at_Risk" class="mw-redirect" title="Heritage at Risk">Heritage at Risk</a> survey which is one of the UK Government's <a href="/wiki/Official_statistics" title="Official statistics">Official statistics</a>. It is tasked to secure the preservation and enhancement of the man-made heritage of England for the benefit of future generations.<sup id="cite_ref-Funding_of_the_Arts_and_Heritage_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Funding_of_the_Arts_and_Heritage-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Its remit involves:
</p>
<ul><li>Caring for nationally important archive collections of photographs, drawings and other records which document the historic environment of England and date from the eighteenth century onwards.</li>
<li>Giving grants national and local organisations for the conservation of historic buildings, monuments and landscapes. In 2013/14 over £13 million worth of grants were made to support heritage buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-EH_Report_13/14_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EH_Report_13/14-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Advising central UK government on which English heritage assets are nationally important and should be protected by designation (i.e. listing, scheduling, etc.).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Administering and maintaining the register of England's <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed buildings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scheduled_monument" title="Scheduled monument">scheduled monuments</a>, registered battlefields, <a href="/wiki/World_Heritage_Sites" class="mw-redirect" title="World Heritage Sites">World Heritage Sites</a> and protected parks and gardens.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> This is published as an online resource as '<a href="/wiki/The_National_Heritage_List_for_England" class="mw-redirect" title="The National Heritage List for England">The National Heritage List for England</a>'.</li>
<li>Advising local authorities on managing changes to the most important parts of heritage.</li>
<li>Providing expertise through advice, training and guidance to improve the standards and skills of people working in heritage, practical conservation and access to resources. In 2009–2010 it trained around 200 professionals working in local authorities and the wider sector.<sup id="cite_ref-EH_Report_13/14_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EH_Report_13/14-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Consulting and collaborating with other heritage bodies, local and national planning organisations e.g. the preparation of Planning Policy statement for the Historic Environment (PPS5)<sup id="cite_ref-pps5_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pps5-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Commissioning and conducting archaeological research, including the publication of 'Heritage Counts' and ‘<a href="/wiki/Heritage_at_Risk" class="mw-redirect" title="Heritage at Risk">Heritage at Risk</a>’ on behalf of the heritage sector which are the annual research surveys into the state of England's heritage.</li></ul>
<p>It is not responsible for approving alterations to <a href="/wiki/Listed_building" title="Listed building">listed buildings</a>. The management of listed buildings is the responsibility of local planning authorities and the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Communities_and_Local_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Department for Communities and Local Government">Department for Communities and Local Government</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pps5_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pps5-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It also owns the National Heritage Collection of nationally important historic sites, currently in public care. However, they do not run these sites as this function is instead carried out by the <a href="/wiki/English_Heritage" title="English Heritage">English Heritage Trust</a> under licence until 2023.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
