<p><b>Stagecoach Yorkshire</b> is an operating division of <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_Group" title="Stagecoach Group">Stagecoach UK Bus</a>.
</p><p>It was formed in 2005 to take over the former Traction Group fleets in Yorkshire by <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_Group" title="Stagecoach Group">Stagecoach Group</a>, which took over Traction from Frank Carter on 14 December 2005; <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Traction" title="Yorkshire Traction">Yorkshire Traction</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Terrier_(bus_company)" class="mw-redirect" title="Yorkshire Terrier (bus company)">Yorkshire Terrier</a> and Barnsley &amp; District.  Since then the geographical coverage of the division has changed, with the divestment of services in the <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a> area, and the addition of Derbyshire operations from <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_East_Midlands" title="Stagecoach East Midlands">Stagecoach East Midlands</a>.  The former Yorkshire Traction and Barnsley &amp; District operations are now branded as Stagecoach Yorkshire, whilst services in Yorkshire Terrier's former area are operated as <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_Sheffield" title="Stagecoach Sheffield">Stagecoach Sheffield</a>, and the Derbyshire operation continues as <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_in_Chesterfield" title="Stagecoach in Chesterfield">Stagecoach in Chesterfield</a>.  However, following rationalisation, services throughout the division are now registered to Yorkshire Traction.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stagecoach_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Traction" title="Yorkshire Traction">Traction Group</a> was formed in 1986, when <a href="/wiki/Barnsley" title="Barnsley">Barnsley</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Traction" title="Yorkshire Traction">Yorkshire Traction</a> was sold to its management and employees, led by Frank Carter. In 2000 Sheffield-based Yorkshire Terrier was taken over along with the operations of Andrews, South Riding, Sheffield Omnibus, and Kingsman, all in Sheffield, which all competed with <a href="/wiki/First_South_Yorkshire" title="First South Yorkshire">Mainline</a> in the city.
</p><p>Barnsley &amp; District was formed in July 1990 when Traction bought Tom Jowitt Travel of <a href="/wiki/Tankersley,_South_Yorkshire" title="Tankersley, South Yorkshire">Tankersley</a>. It was enlarged in 1992 when Pride of The Road, Royston was purchased, and in 1995, when the bus operations of Globe of Barnsley were taken over.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/First_South_Yorkshire" title="First South Yorkshire">Mainline</a> was sold to <a href="/wiki/FirstGroup" title="FirstGroup">FirstGroup</a>, became First Mainline and then <a href="/wiki/First_South_Yorkshire" title="First South Yorkshire">First South Yorkshire</a>. It still competes with Stagecoach Yorkshire and <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_Sheffield" title="Stagecoach Sheffield">Stagecoach Sheffield</a> today, which brings Britain's largest two bus groups into competition with each other.
</p><p>Following the takeover by Stagecoach, Barnsley &amp; District was closed and absorbed into Yorkshire Traction in July 2006, and three months later the <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_in_Chesterfield" title="Stagecoach in Chesterfield">Stagecoach in Chesterfield</a> operation was transferred into Stagecoach Yorkshire from <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_East_Midlands" title="Stagecoach East Midlands">Stagecoach East Midlands</a>.
</p><p>From Sunday 20 May 2008 the company is operating a brand new bus service across Barnsley and South Yorkshire. There have been a number of changes, presented as a bid to improve the service but really major service reductions. Many cross-border services into West Yorkshire have been withdrawn, especially between Barnsley and the Huddersfield areas, a former Yorkshire Traction stronghold.
</p><p>In May 2008, following rumours that <a href="/wiki/Arriva" title="Arriva">Arriva</a> would purchase Stagecoach's Huddersfield operations, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Centrebus Holdings</a> would buy the division. That company was a partnership between the <a href="/wiki/Centrebus" title="Centrebus">Centrebus</a> owner and Arriva, which held a 40% stake. It is now <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_Tiger" title="Yorkshire Tiger">Yorkshire Tiger</a>, wholly owned by Arriva.
</p><p>Stagecoach Yorkshire division's first <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach_Gold" title="Stagecoach Gold">Stagecoach Gold</a> service commenced on Monday 10 November 2014, operated by Stagecoach in Chesterfield and running between Sheffield, Chesterfield and Matlock, more recently extended from Sheffield to Meadowhall and Barnsley.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fleet">Fleet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stagecoach_Yorkshire&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Fleet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Stagecoach_Yorkshire_59205_FJ56_OBR.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Stagecoach_Yorkshire_59205_FJ56_OBR.JPG/220px-Stagecoach_Yorkshire_59205_FJ56_OBR.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Stagecoach_Yorkshire_59205_FJ56_OBR.JPG/330px-Stagecoach_Yorkshire_59205_FJ56_OBR.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Stagecoach_Yorkshire_59205_FJ56_OBR.JPG/440px-Stagecoach_Yorkshire_59205_FJ56_OBR.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3712" data-file-height="2088" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Stagecoach_Yorkshire_59205_FJ56_OBR.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Stagecoach Yorkshire run coaches on behalf of <a href="/wiki/National_Express_Coaches" title="National Express Coaches">National Express</a>. This is <a href="/wiki/Caetano_Levante" title="Caetano Levante">Caetano Levante</a> 59205, seen in <a href="/wiki/Southampton" title="Southampton">Southampton</a> coach station, on route 310 to Bradford.</div></div></div>
<p>Stagecoach Yorkshire's vehicles are varied. Traction Group was known for a lot of variety among its bus fleet; Yorkshire Traction's fleet included the unique KIRN Mogul with East Lancashire Flyte body, a large fleet of <a href="/wiki/Scania_AB" title="Scania AB">Scania</a> buses dating back to the 1990s, which were relatively rare then, and Traction has been the only major buyer of <a href="/w/index.php?title=MAN_14.220&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="MAN 14.220 (page does not exist)">MAN 14.220s</a>, all with <a href="/wiki/East_Lancs_Myllennium" title="East Lancs Myllennium">East Lancs Myllennium</a> bodies. Many more rare vehicles operate in the fleet, as well as more familiar vehicles, such as <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Dart" title="Dennis Dart">Dennis Darts</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Plaxton" title="Plaxton">Plaxton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walter_Alexander_Coachbuilders" title="Walter Alexander Coachbuilders">Alexander</a> bodies, low-floor <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Dart_SLF" class="mw-redirect" title="Dennis Dart SLF">Dart SLFs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volvo_B6BLE" title="Volvo B6BLE">Volvo B6BLEs</a> with East Lancashire Spryte bodies, plus some ex-Blazefield <a href="/wiki/Volvo_B10B" title="Volvo B10B">Volvo B10Bs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volvo_B10BLE" title="Volvo B10BLE">B10BLEs</a> with Wright bodies and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_ALX200" title="Alexander ALX200">Alexander ALX200</a>-bodied <a href="/wiki/Volvo_B6" title="Volvo B6">Volvo B6</a>LEs.
</p><p>The company's last <a href="/wiki/MCW_Metrobus" title="MCW Metrobus">MCW Metrobuses</a> were withdrawn in the first quarter of 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Terrier's fleet was more standardised, with East Lancashire Spryte-bodied Dennis Darts and Volvo B6BLEs, East Lancashire Myllennium-bodied <a href="/wiki/DAF_SB220" title="DAF SB220">DAF SB220s</a>, a single low-floor <a href="/wiki/Volvo_B6" title="Volvo B6">Volvo B6</a> with <a href="/wiki/Plaxton_Pointer" title="Plaxton Pointer">Plaxton Pointer</a> bodywork, and 10 Alexander Dennis Pointer Darts. More recently, 25 <a href="/wiki/MAN_18.240" class="mw-redirect" title="MAN 18.240">MAN 18.240s</a> with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_ALX300" title="Alexander ALX300">Alexander ALX300</a> bodywork joined the fleet, replacing all the remaining <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Dash" title="Alexander Dash">Alexander Dash</a>-bodied <a href="/wiki/Volvo_B6" title="Volvo B6">Volvo B6s</a>, and allowing the single low-floor <a href="/wiki/Plaxton_Pointer" title="Plaxton Pointer">Plaxton Pointer</a> example to be cascaded (first to Doncaster, then to Barnsley where it currently resides).
</p><p>Since the Stagecoach takeover, 20 new Alexander Dennis Pointer Darts have entered service at Rawmarsh and Ecclesfield, of which 17 were branded for routes 88 and 108/9, along with seven MCV Evolutions at Rawmarsh and 30 <a href="/wiki/Alexander_ALX300" title="Alexander ALX300">Alexander ALX300</a>-bodied <a href="/wiki/MAN_18.240" class="mw-redirect" title="MAN 18.240">MAN 18.240s</a> split between Sheffield and Barnsley depots. In 2009, Barnsley received a number of Enviro400 vehicles, branded for service 66 (Barnsley – Elsecar).
</p><p>In 2010 Sheffield received fifteen brand new Enviro 400s branded for service 88 to replace the previous buses on this route, which moved on to new route 87 (Lowedges – High Green). More ALX 300 were transferred from Oxford and were placed into service on new service 79 (Batemoor – Ecclesfield). Also three Optare solos were transferred in from <a href="/wiki/Preston_Bus" title="Preston Bus">Preston Bus</a> to operate new service SL2, linking the Stannington estate with the tram at Malin Bridge. Rawmarsh and Barnsley received seven Enviro 200s each.
</p><p>In 2011, Holbrook depot received 21 Alexander Dennis Enviro 400 Hybrids for service 120 (Halfway/Crystal Peaks – Royal Hallamshire Hospital/Fulwood), which were later followed with another 19 for service 52 in 2012. Ecclesfield Depot received six Enviro 200s for the enhanced <a href="/wiki/Sheffield_Supertram" title="Sheffield Supertram">Supertram</a> Link service between Stocksbridge and Middlewood. More Enviro 200s followed in 2012; Barnsley received another six vehicles for the Townlines 1 service, displacing the existing Optare Solos to Holbrook for a new Supertram link service, whilst Holbrook received eight to upgrade service 25.
</p><p>In October 2017, three Volvo B9R Plaxton Elites were added for the new X10 route between Barnsley and Leeds. These were transferred from National Express and sport leather seating and USB charging.
</p>
