<p><b>Foynes</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;f&#39; in &#39;find&#39;">f</span><span title="/ɔɪ/: &#39;oi&#39; in &#39;choice&#39;">ɔɪ</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span><span title="&#39;z&#39; in &#39;zoom&#39;">z</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a>: <i lang="ga">Faing</i>) is a town and major port in <a href="/wiki/County_Limerick" title="County Limerick">County Limerick</a> in the midwest of <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Ireland</a>, located at the edge of hilly land on the southern bank of  the <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Estuary" title="Shannon Estuary">Shannon Estuary</a>. The population of the town was 542 as of the 2011 census.<sup id="cite_ref-cso2011_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cso2011-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Foynes.27s_role_as_seaport"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Foynes's_role_as_seaport">Foynes's role as seaport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Foynes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Foynes&#039;s role as seaport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Foynes as a port has a longer history, being first surveyed in 1837, and is now the location of a <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Foynes_Port" title="Shannon Foynes Port">major deep water seaport</a> operated by the Shannon Foynes Port Company, an amalgamation under the Harbours Act 2000 of the agencies operating the ports of Limerick and Foynes. It is anticipated that more traffic from the upstream Limerick Port will ultimately be diverted there as the latter portlands are redeveloped as commercial and residential properties. A railway line to Limerick via <a href="/wiki/Patrickswell" title="Patrickswell">Patrickswell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Raheen,_County_Limerick" title="Raheen, County Limerick">Raheen</a> is in existence but not operational. <a href="/wiki/Iarnr%C3%B3d_%C3%89ireann" title="Iarnród Éireann">Iarnród Éireann</a>'s policy of concentrating on what it sees as more profitable passenger operations means the Port's traffic is entirely served by road.
</p><p>Foynes Port Company was amalgamated with the Shannon Estuary Ports Company (which had responsibility for the other areas of the Estuary) in 2000, to form Shannon Foynes Port Company (SFPC). SFPC is the second largest port facility in Ireland, handling over 10 million tonnes of cargo annually through the six terminals currently operational. Planning permission has been granted for an <a href="/wiki/LNG" class="mw-redirect" title="LNG">LNG</a> import terminal at <a href="/wiki/Ballylongford" title="Ballylongford">Ballylongford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Co._Kerry" class="mw-redirect" title="Co. Kerry">Co. Kerry</a>, within SFPC's jurisdiction; once constructed it will add considerably to throughput.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Foynes.27s_role_in_aviation"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Foynes's_role_in_aviation">Foynes's role in aviation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Foynes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Foynes&#039;s role in aviation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Seaplanes_at_Foynes.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Seaplanes_at_Foynes.jpg/220px-Seaplanes_at_Foynes.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Seaplanes_at_Foynes.jpg/330px-Seaplanes_at_Foynes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Seaplanes_at_Foynes.jpg/440px-Seaplanes_at_Foynes.jpg 2x" data-file-width="580" data-file-height="437" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Seaplanes_at_Foynes.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Seaplanes at Foynes, July 1938</div></div></div>
<p>During the late 1930s and early 1940s, land-based planes lacked sufficient flying range for <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> crossings. Foynes was the last port of call on its eastern shore for <a href="/wiki/Seaplane" title="Seaplane">seaplanes</a>.  As a result, Foynes would become one of the biggest civilian airports in Europe during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>.  Surveying flights for <a href="/wiki/Flying_boat" title="Flying boat">flying boat</a> operations were made by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Lindbergh" title="Charles Lindbergh">Charles Lindbergh</a> in 1933 and a terminal was begun in 1935.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The first transatlantic proving flights were operated on July 5, 1937 with a <a href="/wiki/Pan_Am" class="mw-redirect" title="Pan Am">Pan Am</a> <a href="/wiki/Sikorsky_S-42" title="Sikorsky S-42">Sikorsky S-42</a> service from <a href="/wiki/Botwood" title="Botwood">Botwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_Newfoundland" title="Dominion of Newfoundland">Newfoundland</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Exploits" title="Bay of Exploits">Bay of Exploits</a> and a <a href="/wiki/BOAC" class="mw-redirect" title="BOAC">BOAC</a> <a href="/wiki/Short_Empire" title="Short Empire">Short Empire</a> service from Foynes with successful transits of twelve and fifteen-and-a-quarter hours respectively. Services to <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southampton" title="Southampton">Southampton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poole" title="Poole">Poole</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> followed, the first non-stop New York service operating on June 22, 1942 in 25 hours 40 minutes.<br />
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Foynes_Flying_Boat_Museum">Foynes Flying Boat Museum</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Foynes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Foynes Flying Boat Museum">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:CountyLimerick_FoynesFlyingBoatMuseum.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ef/CountyLimerick_FoynesFlyingBoatMuseum.jpg/220px-CountyLimerick_FoynesFlyingBoatMuseum.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ef/CountyLimerick_FoynesFlyingBoatMuseum.jpg/330px-CountyLimerick_FoynesFlyingBoatMuseum.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/ef/CountyLimerick_FoynesFlyingBoatMuseum.jpg/440px-CountyLimerick_FoynesFlyingBoatMuseum.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:CountyLimerick_FoynesFlyingBoatMuseum.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A full-size replica of a <a href="/wiki/Boeing_314" class="mw-redirect" title="Boeing 314">Boeing 314</a> flying boat.</div></div></div>
<p>All of this changed following the construction and opening in 1942 of <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Airport" title="Shannon Airport">Shannon Airport</a> on flat <a href="/wiki/Bogland" class="mw-redirect" title="Bogland">bogland</a> on the northern bank of the <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">Estuary</a>. Foynes flying-boat station closed in 1946. A college for the learning of the <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish language</a> was opened in the former terminal in 1954. The Port Trustees purchased the building in 1980 and the Foynes Flying Boat Museum leased a portion in 1988.
</p><p>One of Foynes's main claims to fame is the invention there of <a href="/wiki/Irish_Coffee" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish Coffee">Irish Coffee</a>. This came about, it is said, in order to alleviate the suffering of cold and wet passengers during its aviation days in the 1930s and early '40s. <a href="/wiki/Brendan_O%27Regan" title="Brendan O&#39;Regan">Brendan O'Regan</a> was Catering Comptroller between 1943-1945 and after had the same position at Shannon Airport where in established the world's first <a href="/wiki/Duty-free_shop" title="Duty-free shop">duty-free shop</a> amongst his innovations. The Foynes Flying Boat Museum contains much memorabilia from that era, including the original radio and weather room, along with its equipment and a full-size replica <a href="/wiki/Boeing_314" class="mw-redirect" title="Boeing 314">Boeing 314</a> flying boat.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Foynes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Rail">Rail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Foynes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Rail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Foynes railway station was opened on 29 April 1858, but closed to passenger traffic on 4 February 1963.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Freight traffic declined until the final revenue movement - a train of molasses on 30 October 2000. The line was "mothballed" at the end of 2001. On 7 May 2002 the annual Irish Rail weed spray train visited the line <sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and the last known movement on the line was on 9 January 2003 when a permanent way inspection railcar traversed the line. Although the line is not currently operational it has not been formally abandoned. The Shannon Foynes Port Company and others maintain contact with Irish Rail to review opportunities for reopening it for future bulk cargo projects.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In an interview on Limerick's Live 95 fm on April 18, 2011, Kay McGuinness Chairperson of Shannon Foynes Port Company said that they are confident that the rail link could be reopened for €7 million, which was considerably less than initially quoted price of €30 million by Irish Rail following the involvement of consultants and it was now a do-able project.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 10 February 2015, Irish Rail wrote to lineside neighbours informing them of plans over the next 6 weeks, to clear the line of vegetation in order to allow a condition survey and inspection of structures to take place. This to inform a study that they are undertaking on behalf of the Shannon Foynes Port Company into the re-establishment of rail freight traffic on the line.
</p><p>On 29 June 2015,  it was announced that as part of the EU Infrastructure - TEN-T - Connecting Europe funding that the Shannon Foynes Port Company was successful in  securing €800,000 for a study to develop the business case to reopen the line. The project is called Connecting International Sea Cargo to the Irish Rail Network (CISCIRN).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Bus">Bus</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Foynes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bus_%C3%89ireann" title="Bus Éireann">Bus Éireann</a> route <b>314</b> provides a few journeys a day to <a href="/wiki/Limerick" title="Limerick">Limerick</a> via <a href="/wiki/Askeaton" title="Askeaton">Askeaton</a>. In the opposite direction there are buses to <a href="/wiki/Glin,_County_Limerick" title="Glin, County Limerick">Glin</a> with a weekend service to <a href="/wiki/Tralee" title="Tralee">Tralee</a> and a Summer service to <a href="/wiki/Ballybunion" title="Ballybunion">Ballybunion</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Places_of_interest">Places of interest</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Foynes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Places of interest">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In addition to the Foynes Flying Boat Museum, the Knockpatrick Gardens, an award-winning 3-acre (12,000&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) garden overlooking the Shannon Estuary, is located 2&#160;km (1&#160;mi) from Foynes.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Foynes is near to <a href="/wiki/Adare" title="Adare">Adare</a>, a national heritage town, and the city of <a href="/wiki/Limerick" title="Limerick">Limerick</a>. The main gateway to the region is <a href="/wiki/Shannon_Airport" title="Shannon Airport">Shannon Airport</a>. Foynes is located on the <a href="/wiki/N69_road_(Ireland)" title="N69 road (Ireland)">N69</a> "coast road" to <a href="/wiki/Tarbert,_Kerry" class="mw-redirect" title="Tarbert, Kerry">Tarbert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tralee" title="Tralee">Tralee</a> in <a href="/wiki/County_Kerry" title="County Kerry">County Kerry</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Foynes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Senan%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Saint Senan&#39;s (page does not exist)">Saint Senan's</a> GAA club draws its players from the parish centred on Foynes and the neighbouring town of Shanagolden. Although it has fielded <a href="/wiki/Hurling" title="Hurling">hurling</a> teams on occasions, it is primarily a <a href="/wiki/Gaelic_football" title="Gaelic football">Gaelic football</a> club. After winning the intermediate championship in 2003, Saint Senan's were narrowly beaten in the final of the 2006 <a href="/wiki/Limerick_Senior_Football_Championship" title="Limerick Senior Football Championship">Limerick Senior Football Championship</a>. Its predecessor, the Foynes team, won its only senior championship in 1907.
</p>
