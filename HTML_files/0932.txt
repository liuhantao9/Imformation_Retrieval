<p><b>Rochester</b> is a city founded in 1854 in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S.</a> <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">State</a> of <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a> and is the <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">county seat</a> of <a href="/wiki/Olmsted_County,_Minnesota" title="Olmsted County, Minnesota">Olmsted County</a> located on the <a href="/wiki/Zumbro_River" title="Zumbro River">Zumbro River</a>'s south fork in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Minnesota" title="Southeast Minnesota">Southeast Minnesota</a>. It is Minnesota's third-largest city and the largest city located outside the <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis%E2%80%93Saint_Paul" title="Minneapolis–Saint Paul">Minneapolis-St. Paul Metropolitan Statistical Area</a>. As of 2018, the Rochester metropolitan area had a population estimated at 219,802.<sup id="cite_ref-2018_Census_Estimates_-_Metropolitan_areas_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2018_Census_Estimates_-_Metropolitan_areas-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">2010 United States Census</a> the city had a population of 106,769.<sup id="cite_ref-2010_Census_(City)_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010_Census_(City)-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The U.S. Census Bureau estimated that the 2018 population was 116,961. It is the home of the <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic" title="Mayo Clinic">Mayo Clinic</a> and an <a href="/wiki/IBM_Rochester" title="IBM Rochester">IBM facility</a>, formerly one of the company's largest. The city has long been rated as one of the best places to live in the United States by multiple publications such as <i><a href="/wiki/Money_(magazine)" title="Money (magazine)">Money</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The area developed as a <a href="/wiki/Stagecoach" title="Stagecoach">stagecoach</a> stop between <a href="/wiki/Saint_Paul,_Minnesota" title="Saint Paul, Minnesota">Saint Paul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">Minnesota</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Dubuque,_Iowa" title="Dubuque, Iowa">Dubuque</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a> near the Zumbro River. The community was founded by George Head and his wife Henrietta who built log cabin Head's Tavern in 1854 and named the city after his hometown of <a href="/wiki/Rochester,_New_York" title="Rochester, New York">Rochester</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">New York</a>. When the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_and_North_Western_Transportation_Company" title="Chicago and North Western Transportation Company">Winona and St Peter Railroad</a> initiated service in October 1864, it brought new residents and business opportunities further spurring growth and expansion. In 1863, Dr. <a href="/wiki/William_W._Mayo" class="mw-redirect" title="William W. Mayo">William W. Mayo</a> arrived as the examining surgeon for <a href="/wiki/Union_(American_Civil_War)" title="Union (American Civil War)">Union</a> draftees in the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">Civil War</a>. Rochester celebrated its sesquicentennial in 2004.
</p>
<h3><span id="St._Mary.27s_Hospital"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="St._Mary's_Hospital">St. Mary's Hospital</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: St. Mary&#039;s Hospital">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg/220px-1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="118" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg/330px-1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg/440px-1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1182" data-file-height="633" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1910StMarysHospitalRochesterMNcopyrightCarlAHolland.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>St. Mary's Hospital (1910)</div></div></div>
<p>On August 21, 1883, the <a href="/wiki/1883_Rochester_Tornado" class="mw-redirect" title="1883 Rochester Tornado">Great Tornado</a> demolished much of Rochester, leaving 37 dead and approximately 200 injured. As there was no medical facility in the immediate area at the time, Dr. Mayo and his <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic#Early_years" title="Mayo Clinic">two sons</a> worked together to care for the wounded. Donations of US$60,000 (US$1,567,054 in 2016 accounting for inflation) were collected and the <a href="/wiki/Sisters_of_Saint_Francis_of_Rochester,_Minnesota" title="Sisters of Saint Francis of Rochester, Minnesota">Sisters of St. Francis</a>, assisted by Mayo, opened a new facility named <a href="/wiki/Saint_Marys_Hospital_(Rochester)" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Marys Hospital (Rochester)">St. Marys Hospital</a> in 1889.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mayo_Clinic">Mayo Clinic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mayo Clinic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Mayo practice grew and is today among the largest and most well-respected medical facilities in the world and it has been consistently ranked No. 1 Best hospital in the United States. Many notable people from around the world, including former <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">Presidents</a> <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Harry_S._Truman" title="Harry S. Truman">Harry S. Truman</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_H._W._Bush" title="George H. W. Bush">George H. W. Bush</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gerald_Ford" title="Gerald Ford">Gerald Ford</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a>, have visited Rochester as patients of the <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic" title="Mayo Clinic">Mayo Clinic</a>. International patients include King <a href="/wiki/Hussein_of_Jordan" title="Hussein of Jordan">Hussein of Jordan</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">Geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:SkylineRochesterMNreflectected.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/SkylineRochesterMNreflectected.JPG/220px-SkylineRochesterMNreflectected.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="111" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/SkylineRochesterMNreflectected.JPG/330px-SkylineRochesterMNreflectected.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/SkylineRochesterMNreflectected.JPG/440px-SkylineRochesterMNreflectected.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3324" data-file-height="1673" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:SkylineRochesterMNreflectected.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Zumbro River through Downtown Rochester</div></div></div>
<p>Rochester lies alongside the South Fork of the Zumbro River which is 57.6 miles long<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and is ringed by gentle hills and largely surrounded by farmland within a <a href="/wiki/Deciduous_forest" class="mw-redirect" title="Deciduous forest">deciduous forest</a> <a href="/wiki/Biome" title="Biome">biome</a>. The Zumbro Watershed flows through 1,422 square miles<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> of agricultural and urban lands. Located in southeast Minnesota, the City of Rochester falls within the <a href="/wiki/Driftless_Area" title="Driftless Area">Driftless Area</a>: the only region in North America that was never glaciated and contains deeply-carved river valleys. The rugged terrain is due both to the lack of glacial deposits, or drift, and to the incision of the upper Mississippi River and its tributaries into bedrock. According to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">United States Census Bureau</a>, the city has a total area of 54.75 square miles (141.80&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), of which 54.59 square miles (141.39&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) of it is land and 0.16 square miles (0.41&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) is water.<sup id="cite_ref-Gazetteer_files_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gazetteer_files-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The city is located 85 miles (137&#160;km) southeast of <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis-St._Paul" class="mw-redirect" title="Minneapolis-St. Paul">Minneapolis-St. Paul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Faruquep1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Faruquep1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rochester is in <a href="/wiki/Olmsted_County,_Minnesota" title="Olmsted County, Minnesota">Olmsted County</a>, one of only four counties in Minnesota without a natural lake. <a href="/wiki/Artificial_lake" class="mw-redirect" title="Artificial lake">Artificial lakes</a> exist in the area, including <a href="/wiki/Silver_Lake_(Rochester,_Minnesota)" title="Silver Lake (Rochester, Minnesota)">Silver Lake</a>, a dammed portion of the South Fork Zumbro River just below the convergence with Silver Creek near the city center. Silver Lake was once used as a <a href="/wiki/Cooling_pond" title="Cooling pond">cooling pond</a> when the coal-burning <a href="/wiki/Power_plant" class="mw-redirect" title="Power plant">power plant</a> was operated by Rochester Public Utilities at the lake.  When operational, the RPU coal plant's heated water output prevented the lake from generally freezing over during the winter months; attracting large numbers of <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrating</a> <a href="/wiki/Giant_Canada_goose" title="Giant Canada goose">giant Canada geese</a>.
</p><p>Rochester has an extensive parks system, the largest of which are Silver Lake and Soldiers Field in the central part of the city.  A major flood in 1978 led the city to embark on an expensive and successful flood-control project that involved altering many nearby rivers and streams. The Zumbro river flowing through the center of the city is presently being readdressed for increased development and use as part of city planning in conjunction with funding from the Destination Medical Center project.
</p>
<div class="thumb tnone" style="margin:0 auto;overflow:hidden;width:auto;max-width:808px"><div class="thumbinner"><div class="noresize" style="overflow:auto"><a href="/wiki/File:Roch_qhill.JPG" class="image" title="Westward View of Downtown Rochester from Quarry Hill Park (2010)."><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Roch_qhill.JPG/800px-Roch_qhill.JPG" decoding="async" width="800" height="195" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Roch_qhill.JPG/1200px-Roch_qhill.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Roch_qhill.JPG/1600px-Roch_qhill.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2808" data-file-height="684" /></a></div><div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Roch_qhill.JPG" title="File:Roch qhill.JPG"> </a></div>Westward View of Downtown Rochester from Quarry Hill Park (2010).</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With a slightly higher altitude and without the <a href="/wiki/Urban_heat_island" title="Urban heat island">UHI</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Minneapolis%E2%80%93Saint_Paul" title="Minneapolis–Saint Paul">Twin Cities</a>, the climate is <a href="/wiki/Humid_continental_climate" title="Humid continental climate">warm-summer humid continental climate</a> (<a href="/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification" title="Köppen climate classification">Köppen</a>: <i>Dfb</i>) even being further south, with hot summers and very cold winters.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The city features four distinct seasons. Rochester sees on average 30 inches (760&#160;mm) of rainfall and 48 inches (120&#160;cm) of snowfall per year. Significant snow accumulation is common during the winter months. Spring and fall are transitional seasons, with a general warming trend during the spring and a general cooling trend during the fall. However, it is not uncommon to see some snowfall during the early months of spring and the later months of fall.<sup id="cite_ref-NOWDataARX_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NOWDataARX-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Rochester is the second windiest city in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, with wind speeds averaging 12.6&#160;mph.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> January to April are the windiest months on average, according to The Weather Channel. Rochester has been hit by two <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">F4</a> tornadoes since 1950 (the first on May 10, 1953, and the other on September 16, 1962).<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The city has also been hit by two tornadoes in the past decade. On June 17, 2010, a tornado hit the city's northwest side, damaging or destroying several homes and businesses around the Lincolnshire neighborhood.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Another tornado struck the city on June 4, 2019, causing damage to parts of Southwest Rochester.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>As of the <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">census</a><sup id="cite_ref-FactFinder_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FactFinder-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> of 2010, there were 106,769 people, 43,025 households, and 26,853 families residing in the city. The <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> was 1,955.8 inhabitants per square mile (755.1/km<sup>2</sup>). There were 45,683 housing units at an average density of 836.8 per square mile (323.1/km<sup>2</sup>). The racial makeup of the city was 82.0% <a href="/wiki/White_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="White (U.S. Census)">White</a>, 6.3% <a href="/wiki/African_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="African American (U.S. Census)">African American</a>, 0.3% <a href="/wiki/Native_American_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Native American (U.S. Census)">Native American</a>, 6.8% <a href="/wiki/Asian_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asian (U.S. Census)">Asian</a> (1.3% Indian, 1.2% Cambodian, 1.0% Chinese, 0.9% Vietnamese, 0.6% Laotian, 0.4% Korean, 0.4% Filipino, 0.2% Hmong, 0.1% Japanese, 0.1% Thai, 0.1% Pakistani), 2.0% from <a href="/wiki/Race_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Race (U.S. Census)">other races</a>, and 2.6% from two or more races. <a href="/wiki/Hispanic_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispanic (U.S. Census)">Hispanic</a> or <a href="/wiki/Latino_(U.S._Census)" class="mw-redirect" title="Latino (U.S. Census)">Latino</a> of any race were 7.2% of the population (3.7% Mexican, 1.3% Puerto Rican, 2.0% Other).
</p><p>In terms of ancestry, the 2005–2007 American Community Survey found <a href="/wiki/German_American" class="mw-redirect" title="German American">German Americans</a> to be the largest single ethnic group in Rochester, making up 35.5% of the city's population. <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Norwegian American">Norwegian Americans</a> made up 15.9%, while <a href="/wiki/Irish_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Irish American">Irish Americans</a> contributed to 11.6% of the city's populace. <a href="/wiki/English_American" class="mw-redirect" title="English American">English Americans</a> made up 8.2% of the population and <a href="/wiki/Swedish_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Swedish American">Swedish Americans</a> were 5.0% of the city's population. In the mid-1980s Rochester had fewer than 40 Hmong persons.<sup id="cite_ref-Faruquep1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Faruquep1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/1990_United_States_Census" title="1990 United States Census">1990 United States Census</a> counted 200 Hmong persons in Rochester. This increased to 300 by 1998. Cathleen Jo Faruque, author of "Migration of Hmong to Rochester, Minnesota: Life in the Midwest," wrote in 2003 that there was "every indication that this trend will continue".<sup id="cite_ref-Faruquep2_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Faruquep2-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were 43,025 households of which 31.9% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 49.4% were <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">married couples</a> living together, 9.5% had a female householder with no husband present, 3.5% had a male householder with no wife present, and 37.6% were non-families. 30.1% of all households were made up of individuals and 8.8% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.42 and the average family size was 3.04.
</p><p>The median age in the city was 35 years. 24.8% of residents were under the age of 18; 8.3% were between the ages of 18 and 24; 29.4% were from 25 to 44; 24.8% were from 45 to 64; and 12.7% were 65 years of age or older. The gender makeup of the city was 48.4% male and 51.6% female.
</p><p>As of the 2011–2015 American Community Survey, the median household income was $68,023.<sup id="cite_ref-census.gov_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-census.gov-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Government">Government</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Government">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_Rochester,_Minnesota" title="List of mayors of Rochester, Minnesota">List of mayors of Rochester, Minnesota</a></div>
<p>Rochester is governed by a <a href="/wiki/Mayor-council_government" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayor-council government">mayor-council government</a> with a seven-member <a href="/wiki/City_council" title="City council">city council</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The current mayor is <a href="/wiki/Kim_Norton" title="Kim Norton">Kim Norton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> As of January 2019,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> the city council comprises: Randy Staver, City Council President; Patrick Keane, 1st Ward; Michael Wojcik, 2nd Ward; Nick Campion, 3rd Ward; Mark Bilderback, 4th Ward; Shaun Palmer, 5th Ward; and Annalissa Johnson, 6th Ward.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2009-0528-MN-Rochester-oldCH.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/2009-0528-MN-Rochester-oldCH.jpg/220px-2009-0528-MN-Rochester-oldCH.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="173" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/2009-0528-MN-Rochester-oldCH.jpg/330px-2009-0528-MN-Rochester-oldCH.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/2009-0528-MN-Rochester-oldCH.jpg/440px-2009-0528-MN-Rochester-oldCH.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3152" data-file-height="2472" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2009-0528-MN-Rochester-oldCH.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Old City Hall</div></div></div>
<p>Rochester is the seat of <a href="/wiki/Olmsted_County,_Minnesota" title="Olmsted County, Minnesota">Olmsted County,</a> run by a seven-member elected county board, each representing district residents.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The County Board of Commissioners oversee county operations and address citizen concerns. When a commissioner is elected, they are elected to serve a 4-year term. When that term ends, the commissioner can run for re-election if they so desire. The County Attorney and the County Sheriff are also elected to 4-year terms and can run for re-election when the term expires if they choose. Each of these elected officials acts as the director for his/her office, and are accountable to the residents of Olmsted County.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:OlmstedGovtCenter.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/OlmstedGovtCenter.JPG/220px-OlmstedGovtCenter.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="138" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/OlmstedGovtCenter.JPG/330px-OlmstedGovtCenter.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/OlmstedGovtCenter.JPG/440px-OlmstedGovtCenter.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3342" data-file-height="2091" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:OlmstedGovtCenter.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Government Center Building</div></div></div>
<p>Rochester falls under the Olmsted County District Court within the Third Judicial District of the State of Minnesota.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The city includes parts of <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Legislature" title="Minnesota Legislature">Minnesota state legislative</a> districts 25 and 26.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_House_of_Representatives" title="Minnesota House of Representatives">Minnesota House of Representatives</a>, District 25A is represented by <a href="/wiki/Duane_Quam" title="Duane Quam">Duane Quam</a> <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">(R)</a>, District 25B is represented by <a href="/wiki/Duane_Sauke" title="Duane Sauke">Duane Sauke</a> (<a href="/wiki/DFL" class="mw-redirect" title="DFL">DFL</a>), District 26A is represented by <a href="/wiki/Tina_Liebling" title="Tina Liebling">Tina Liebling</a> (DFL), and District 26B is represented by <a href="/wiki/Nels_Pierson" title="Nels Pierson">Nels Pierson</a> (R). In the <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_Senate" title="Minnesota Senate">Minnesota Senate</a>, Rochester is represented by <a href="/wiki/Dave_Senjem" title="Dave Senjem">Dave Senjem</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carla_Nelson" title="Carla Nelson">Carla Nelson</a>, both <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republicans</a>.  Rochester is located in <a href="/wiki/Minnesota%27s_1st_congressional_district" title="Minnesota&#39;s 1st congressional district">Minnesota's 1st congressional district</a>, represented by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Hagedorn" title="Jim Hagedorn">Jim Hagedorn</a>, a member of the Republican Party.
</p>

<p>Rochester Public Schools enroll 16,300 students in 23 public <a href="/wiki/Primary_education" title="Primary education">primary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Secondary_education" title="Secondary education">secondary</a> schools.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>  The city is divided into three public high school attendance zones: <a href="/wiki/John_Marshall_High_School_(Minnesota)" title="John Marshall High School (Minnesota)">John Marshall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayo_High_School" title="Mayo High School">Mayo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Century_High_School_(Rochester,_Minnesota)" title="Century High School (Rochester, Minnesota)">Century</a>. Private schools in the city include <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Lourdes_High_School" title="Rochester Lourdes High School">Lourdes</a>, Schaeffer Academy, and Rochester Central Lutheran School amongst various smaller private religious schools. Studio Academy, a fine arts-focused <a href="/wiki/Charter_school" title="Charter school">charter school</a> operated for 10 years in Rochester and closed its doors in 2011 upon losing its charter.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The Rochester STEM Academy opened in 2011, occupying the former Studio Academy building.
</p><p>Higher education in Rochester had been concentrated around the former <a href="/wiki/University_Center_Rochester" title="University Center Rochester">University Center Rochester</a> in the city's southeast outskirts, where <a href="/wiki/Rochester_Community_and_Technical_College" title="Rochester Community and Technical College">Rochester Community and Technical College</a> shares a campus with a branch of <a href="/wiki/Winona_State_University" title="Winona State University">Winona State University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>  The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Minnesota" title="University of Minnesota">University of Minnesota</a> offered degrees through UCR until 2007, when the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Minnesota_Rochester" title="University of Minnesota Rochester">University of Minnesota Rochester</a> was established downtown finally establishing the presence of major university in the city somewhat in hand with the development comprehensive city planning and investment under the Destination Medical Center project.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Branches of <a href="/wiki/Augsburg_University" title="Augsburg University">Augsburg University</a> and <a href="/wiki/College_of_St._Scholastica" title="College of St. Scholastica">College of St. Scholastica</a> are also in Rochester, as are branches of <a href="/wiki/Winona_State_University" title="Winona State University">Winona State University</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saint_Mary%27s_University_of_Minnesota" title="Saint Mary&#39;s University of Minnesota">St. Mary's University</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic" title="Mayo Clinic">Mayo Clinic</a> offers graduate medical education and research programs through the <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_School_of_Medicine" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayo Clinic School of Medicine">Mayo Clinic School of Medicine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_Graduate_School_of_Biomedical_Sciences" title="Mayo Clinic Graduate School of Biomedical Sciences">Mayo Clinic Graduate School of Biomedical Sciences</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic_School_of_Health_Sciences" title="Mayo Clinic School of Health Sciences">Mayo Clinic School of Health Sciences</a>.
</p><p>According to the United States Census Bureau 2011–2015, the number of high school graduate or higher, percent of persons age 25 years+ was 94.1%. The number of bachelor's degree or higher percent of persons age 25+ was 41.3%.<sup id="cite_ref-census.gov_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-census.gov-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economy">Economy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Economy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mayo_Clinic" title="Mayo Clinic">Mayo Clinic</a> forms the core of Rochester's economy.  As of 2016, it employs 34,180 people and draws more than 2 million visitors annually.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>  The clinic's many facilities, along with hotels, restaurants and retail stores, comprise nearly all of the city's downtown. Excluding the state government, Mayo Clinic is the largest employer in Minnesota.<sup id="cite_ref-MinnesotasLargestEmployers_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MinnesotasLargestEmployers-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Other care providers, including the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Medical_Center,_Rochester" title="Federal Medical Center, Rochester">Rochester Federal Medical Center</a>, are significant employers.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a>'s <a href="/wiki/IBM_Rochester" title="IBM Rochester">Rochester campus</a> is one of the company's most important R&amp;D and manufacturing centers.  It has produced the <a href="/wiki/IBM_System_i" title="IBM System i">System i</a> series,<sup id="cite_ref-IBMRochester_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IBMRochester-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> been home to the first <a href="/wiki/Blue_Gene" class="mw-redirect" title="Blue Gene">Blue Gene</a> prototype, and contributed the servers for <a href="/wiki/IBM_Roadrunner" class="mw-redirect" title="IBM Roadrunner">Roadrunner</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Seven employees at the Rochester IBM campus created IBM Employees Credit Union, which is now <a href="/wiki/Think_Mutual_Bank" title="Think Mutual Bank">Think Mutual Bank</a>, a chain of banks in the Rochester and <a href="/wiki/Twin_Cities" class="mw-redirect" title="Twin Cities">Twin Cities</a> metropolitan areas.
</p><p>The economy of Rochester is also influenced by the agricultural nature of the region.  There are multiple dairy producers such as <a href="/wiki/Kemps_(company)" title="Kemps (company)">Kemps</a> that are active in the area. In addition, Kerry Flavours and Ingredients, a subsidiary of the global Irish company called <a href="/wiki/Kerry_Group" title="Kerry Group">Kerry Group</a>, maintains a production plant in Rochester that specializes in fermented ingredients, found in breads, meats and other processed foods.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Destination_Medical_Center">Destination Medical Center</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Destination Medical Center">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:RochMN-Hilton.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/RochMN-Hilton.jpg/170px-RochMN-Hilton.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="173" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/RochMN-Hilton.jpg/255px-RochMN-Hilton.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/RochMN-Hilton.jpg/340px-RochMN-Hilton.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2345" data-file-height="2387" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:RochMN-Hilton.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Downtown Hilton, completed in 2019, is one of the first major DMC projects.</div></div></div>
<p>In 2013, Governor Mark Dayton signed a bill known as Destination Medical Center (DMC). It will also increase Mayo Clinic's tax base, business and economic growth throughout the state of Minnesota.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Because of this legislation, The Mayo Clinic will invest in $3.5 billion to upgrade patient rooms, add additional work space, and build research and administrative buildings.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The main developments of this plan will be in the DMC sub-districts. The <i>Downtown Waterfront</i> district will cover The Government Center, The Civic Center, and most areas near the Zumbro River. The <i>Central Station</i> district includes Central Park and most of Civic Center Drive, aimed to become a transportation hub of the city. <i>St. Marys Place</i> will cover St. Marys hospital and St. Marys Park, as well as most of 2nd Street. The <i>Heart of the City</i> will feature the Mayo Clinic campus and major residential, commercial, and retail companies. The <i>Discovery Square</i> area will include scientific and technology research facilities. Lastly, The <i>UMR and Recreation</i> district will feature Soldier's Field and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Minnesota_Rochester" title="University of Minnesota Rochester">University of Minnesota Rochester</a> campus.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arts_and_culture">Arts and culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Arts and culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A number of Rochester buildings are on the <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">National Register of Historic Places</a>, including the historic <a href="/wiki/Chateau_Theatre" title="Chateau Theatre">Chateau Theatre</a>, which in 2015 was bought by the city for preservation,<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Avalon_Music" title="Avalon Music">Avalon Music</a>, formerly a hotel important in the local <a href="/wiki/Civil_rights_movement" title="Civil rights movement">civil rights movement</a>. In 2019, after renovations and a public RFP, the Chateau Theatre is re-opening as an exhibition venue.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Founded in 1946, the Rochester Art Center is located downtown, just south of the Mayo Civic Center. In 2017, the Rochester Art Center's financial troubles <sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> combined with dwindling arts and gallery space in Rochester due to the departure of Southeastern Minnesota Visual Artists ( SEMVA) from their Peace Plaza location, citing rising rental costs, fire damage to 535 Gallery shortly after its launch, and the closure of C4 Salon, a proving ground for young artists, brought a renewed focus on the need for both formal and informal arts spaces in the city.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the summer from June to the end of August, every Thursday the city puts on Thursdays on First<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> where local restaurants and artists can set up booths all along First Avenue downtown Rochester. There are two stages where bands perform and provide entertainment. There are also a number of street musicians sitting on walls or standing on the sidewalks.
</p><p>The oldest cultural arts institution in the community, Rochester Symphony Orchestra &amp; Chorale was founded in 1919 as a professional performing arts organization called the Rochester Orchestra. Its earliest ensemble — the Lawler-Dodge Orchestra — was founded in 1912 as a volunteer orchestra, driven by Daisy Plummer, wife of world-famous Mayo Clinic physician, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Stanley_Plummer" title="Henry Stanley Plummer">Dr. Henry Plummer</a>, and directed by Harold Cooke. In early years, the Orchestra appeared in the former <a href="/wiki/Chateau_Theatre" title="Chateau Theatre">Chateau Theatre</a> where they performed background music for silent movies.
</p><p>In 2019, entrepreneurs Andy and Anna Smith opened the Gray Duck Theater, an <a href="/wiki/Independent_movie_theater" title="Independent movie theater">independent movie theater</a> meant to bring independent and art house films to the city and help build up the area's film community.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Parks_and_recreation">Parks and recreation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Parks and recreation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:RochesterMNbikepath2006-06-03.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/RochesterMNbikepath2006-06-03.JPG/220px-RochesterMNbikepath2006-06-03.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="120" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/RochesterMNbikepath2006-06-03.JPG/330px-RochesterMNbikepath2006-06-03.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/RochesterMNbikepath2006-06-03.JPG/440px-RochesterMNbikepath2006-06-03.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2443" data-file-height="1328" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:RochesterMNbikepath2006-06-03.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Rochester has an extensive network of bike and pedestrian paths.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lake_Pepin_Golf.jpeg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c4/Lake_Pepin_Golf.jpeg/220px-Lake_Pepin_Golf.jpeg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c4/Lake_Pepin_Golf.jpeg/330px-Lake_Pepin_Golf.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/c/c4/Lake_Pepin_Golf.jpeg/440px-Lake_Pepin_Golf.jpeg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lake_Pepin_Golf.jpeg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lake Pepin Golf Club North of Rochester</div></div></div>
<p>Minnesota has one of the most extensive state park systems in the nation. Seventy-two state parks are used year-round for hunting, camping, and hiking. In addition, Minnesota has more miles of bike trails than any other state, and a growing network of hiking trails.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Likewise, Rochester's city park system is large, with more than 100 sites covering 5 square miles (13&#160;km<sup>2</sup>). The city also maintains 85 miles (137&#160;km) of paved trails<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> in addition to state trails such as the <a href="/wiki/Douglas_State_Trail" title="Douglas State Trail">Douglas State Trail</a>. The nearest state park is <a href="/wiki/Whitewater_State_Park" title="Whitewater State Park">Whitewater State Park</a>.
</p><p>In addition, Rochester has provided educational and recreation opportunities through Quarry Hill Nature Center, a cooperative effort between the Rochester Parks &amp; Recreation Department, Rochester Public School District and Friends of Quarry Hill Nature Center. Quarry Hill Park was originally a part of the former Rochester State Hospital farm and was purchased from the state by the City of Rochester in 1965. The Nature Center opened in 1973 as a cooperative effort between the Rochester Park Department and the Rochester Public School District. In September 1990, work was completed on a major addition to the existing nature center, effectively tripling the main floor area and enabling Quarry Hill to meet the increasing needs of both the general public and school. Additional facilities were completed in 2017 in addition to <a href="/wiki/Nordic_skiing" title="Nordic skiing">nordic skiing</a> trails in 2014. These facilities include new educational facilities and a 19th-century prairie style home while guests can rent skis and snow shoes on site to enjoy the trails in winter.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Oxbow_Park_and_Zollman_Zoo" title="Oxbow Park and Zollman Zoo">Oxbow Park and Zollman Zoo</a> located on the Zumbro River in nearby <a href="/wiki/Byron,_Minnesota" title="Byron, Minnesota">Byron, Minnesota</a>, offers hiking, camping, fishing, and extensive trails in addition to the Zollman Zoo which houses over 30 species of animals, most of which have injuries that would prevent them from surviving in the wild. All of the animals are native to Minnesota. The zoo is named after Dr. Paul E. Zollman. The most popular animals include a wolf, bald eagle, white-tailed deer, coyotes, bobcat, river otters, bison and a cougar. The zoo is open year-round.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The city also maintains four public golf courses: Northern Hills, Eastwood Golf Club, Hadley Creek (9 holes), and Soldiers Field Golf Course located downtown.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> In addition, a number of private courses are located in the Rochester area, including Willow Creek Golf Club, Oak Summit Golf Club, Maple Valley Golf Club, <a href="/wiki/Somerby_Golf_Club_and_Community" title="Somerby Golf Club and Community">Somerby Golf Club</a>, and the Rochester Golf and Country Club along with a number of courses in smaller surrounding communities. Minnesota has one of the highest number of golf courses per capita in the country,<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> generally found to be of much higher quality than public courses located in the Northeast and elsewhere.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> Extensive fishing is available in the area,<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> including high quality trout runs.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> Along with parks and trails, there are various nearby farms in Rochester notably a Aplaca farm near Eastwood Golf Club which has 20 <a href="/wiki/Alpaca" title="Alpaca">Alpaca</a> native of <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> and other farms include <a href="/wiki/Apple" title="Apple">Apple</a> <a href="/wiki/Orchard" title="Orchard">Orchard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Berry" title="Berry">Berry</a> farms
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media">Media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Media_in_Rochester,_Minnesota" title="Media in Rochester, Minnesota">Media in Rochester, Minnesota</a></div>
<p>The city newspaper is the <i><a href="/wiki/Post-Bulletin" title="Post-Bulletin">Post-Bulletin</a></i>, an afternoon paper which publishes Monday through Saturday.  The Post-Bulletin company also publishes <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://rochestermn.com/magazine/rochestermagazine/">Rochester Magazine</a>, a monthly features periodical.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There are three television stations based in Rochester: <a href="/wiki/KTTC" title="KTTC">KTTC</a> channel&#160;10.1 (<a href="/wiki/NBC" title="NBC">NBC</a>), <a href="/wiki/KTTC" title="KTTC">KTTC</a>-(<a href="/wiki/The_CW_Television_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="The CW Television Network">CW</a>) channel 10.2, and <a href="/wiki/KXLT-TV" title="KXLT-TV">KXLT-TV</a> channel&#160;47 (<a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a>) are a duopoly. The stations share studios as part of a special agreement between Quincy Newspapers and Segamorehill Broadcasting. <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> affiliate <a href="/wiki/KAAL" title="KAAL">KAAL</a> is also based in southwest Rochester. <a href="/wiki/KIMT" title="KIMT">KIMT</a> channel&#160;3 (<a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Mason_City,_Iowa" title="Mason City, Iowa">Mason City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a>, channel&#160;15 <a href="/wiki/KSMQ-TV" title="KSMQ-TV">KSMQ</a>  (<a href="/wiki/Public_Broadcasting_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Broadcasting Service">PBS</a>) in Austin and channel&#160;24 <a href="/wiki/KYIN" class="mw-redirect" title="KYIN">KYIN</a> (<a href="/wiki/Public_Broadcasting_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="Public Broadcasting Service">PBS</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Mason_City,_Iowa" title="Mason City, Iowa">Mason City</a> are among the stations that serve the market. KAAL is licensed to Austin, but has a studio in Rochester. KIMT opened a studio in Rochester in late 2017, which now serves as the main setting for their newscasts.
</p><p>The Rochester area is provided cable service by <a href="/wiki/Charter_Communications" title="Charter Communications">Charter Communications</a>, which holds monopoly in the area. Indiana-based Metronet is currently in the process of developing the network to provide cable service to Rochester.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rochester is served by three <a href="/wiki/U.S._highway" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. highway">U.S. highways</a> (<a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_14" title="U.S. Route 14">U.S. 14</a>, <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_52_in_Minnesota" title="U.S. Route 52 in Minnesota">U.S. 52</a>, and <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_63_in_Minnesota" title="U.S. Route 63 in Minnesota">U.S. 63</a>), and the southern edge of Rochester is skirted by <a href="/wiki/Interstate_90" title="Interstate 90">Interstate Highway 90</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minnesota_State_Highway_30" title="Minnesota State Highway 30">State Highway 30</a>.  Olmsted County Highway 22 is also a main highway in the city because it circles most of Rochester. A combination of <a href="/wiki/Skyway" title="Skyway">skyways</a> and subterranean walkways, subways, link most downtown buildings, which residents often use to avoid harsh winter weather conditions. Public bus transit is run by Rochester Public Transit.  Its operations are carried out by <a href="/wiki/First_Transit" title="First Transit">First Transit</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Rochester_International_Airport" title="Rochester International Airport">Rochester International Airport</a> is located seven miles south of downtown. The airport is the second busiest commercial airport in Minnesota <sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>  It has direct flights to Atlanta, Chicago, and Minneapolis, with occasional charter flights servicing Riverside Resort in <a href="/wiki/Laughlin,_Nevada" title="Laughlin, Nevada">Laughlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nevada" title="Nevada">Nevada</a><sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>.  Beginning in July 2018, service to <a href="/wiki/St._Augustine,_Florida" title="St. Augustine, Florida">St. Augustine, Florida</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix</a> was provided by <a href="/wiki/Elite_Airways" title="Elite Airways">Elite Airways</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-PB-Elite_70-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PB-Elite-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> However, in August of 2018 Elite Airways announced the cancellation of its air service from Rochester that was scheduled to commence in October. Low bookings were reported as the reason for the cancellation of the service.<sup id="cite_ref-Postbul_71-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Postbul-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rochester has a shuttle service connecting to the Minneapolis St. Paul International airport by Rochester Shuttle Service and Groome Transportation (formerly Go Rochester Direct).
</p><p>Rochester's last passenger rail service, to Chicago to the southeast and <a href="/wiki/Rapid_City,_South_Dakota" title="Rapid City, South Dakota">Rapid City, South Dakota</a> to the west, ended when the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_and_North_Western_Railway" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago and North Western Railway">Chicago and North Western Railway</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Rochester_400" class="mw-redirect" title="Rochester 400">Rochester 400</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Streamliner" title="Streamliner">streamliner</a> ended service in 1963.  The closest <a href="/wiki/Amtrak" title="Amtrak">Amtrak</a> station is at <a href="/wiki/Winona,_Minnesota" title="Winona, Minnesota">Winona, Minnesota</a>, 45 miles to the east.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_rankings">Awards and rankings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rochester,_Minnesota&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Awards and rankings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The city had long been a fixture on <i><a href="/wiki/Money_(magazine)" title="Money (magazine)">Money</a></i> magazine's "Best Places to Live" index, and was ranked number 67 on the 2006 list,<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> and in the top 3, including number one multiple times, from 1993 to 1997.
</p><p>Rochester ranked second in Quality of Life by <i>American City Business Journal</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rochester ranked sixth in <i><a href="/wiki/Kiplinger%27s_Personal_Finance" title="Kiplinger&#39;s Personal Finance">Kiplinger's Personal Finance</a></i> magazine's <i>10 Best Cities for the Next Decade.</i>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Golf_Digest" title="Golf Digest">Golf Digest</a></i> and <i>Golf for Women</i> both ranked Rochester as the fifth best golf market in the midwest in 2006.
</p><p>In 2009, <i><a href="/wiki/US_News_and_World_Report" class="mw-redirect" title="US News and World Report">US News and World Report</a></i> ranked Rochester in the Top Ten Best Places to Grow Up and ninth for Best Cities for job seeking retirees.
</p><p>Rochester was ranked the 5th best city to retire in by the <a href="/wiki/Milken_Institute" title="Milken Institute">Milken Institute</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">&#91;78&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
