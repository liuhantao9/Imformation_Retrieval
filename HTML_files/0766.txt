	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">List of Dutch dictionaries</h1>

		<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>Notable <a href="/wiki/Dictionaries" class="mw-redirect" title="Dictionaries">dictionaries</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch language</a> include:
</p>
<ul><li><i>Etymologicum teutonicae linguae</i>, the first known Dutch dictionary published by <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Kiliaan" class="mw-redirect" title="Cornelius Kiliaan">Cornelius Kiliaan</a> in 1599. It continues to be a unique source of obsolete words today.</li>
<li><i> 't Nieuw Woorden-Boeck der Regten</i> ("The New Dictionary of Rights"), published by <a href="/wiki/Adriaan_Koerbagh" title="Adriaan Koerbagh">Adriaan Koerbagh</a> in 1664</li>
<li><i>Een Bloemhof van allerley lieflijkheyd</i> ("A Flower Garden of All Sorts of Delights"), written by Koerbagh under the pseudonym Vreederijk Waarmond in 1668. This book explained various foreign words and caused a great religious opposition that forced him to flee to <a href="/wiki/Leiden" title="Leiden">Leiden</a>.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Woordenboek_der_Nederlandsche_Taal" title="Woordenboek der Nederlandsche Taal">Woordenboek der Nederlandsche Taal</a></i>, the comprehensive academic dictionary of Dutch begun in 1863 and finished in 1998, listing all words in Dutch used since 1500.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Van_Dale" title="Van Dale">Van Dale Groot woordenboek van de Nederlandse taal</a></i>, first published in 1874 and today in its 14th edition, is the most well-known Dutch language dictionary.</li></ul>
<p>There are also two notable Dutch word lists:
</p>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Word_list_of_the_Dutch_language" title="Word list of the Dutch language">het Groene Boekje</a></i>, the "Green Booklet", the official Dutch orthography published by the Dutch Language Union since 1954</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/White_Booklet" title="White Booklet">het Witte Boekje</a></i>, the "White Booklet", published since 1998 and offers alternative Dutch spellings</li></ul>
