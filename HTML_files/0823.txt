<p>The <b>Asiatic–Pacific Campaign Medal</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_service_medals_of_the_World_Wars" title="United States service medals of the World Wars">military award</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>, which was awarded to any member of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">United States Armed Forces</a> who served in the <a href="/wiki/Asiatic-Pacific_Theater" title="Asiatic-Pacific Theater">Asiatic-Pacific Theater</a> from 1941 to 1945. The medal was created on November 6, 1942 by <a href="/wiki/Executive_order_(United_States)" class="mw-redirect" title="Executive order (United States)">Executive Order</a> <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Executive_Order_9265" class="extiw" title="s:Executive Order 9265">9265</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> issued by President <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a>. The medal was designed by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Hudson_Jones" title="Thomas Hudson Jones">Thomas Hudson Jones</a>; the reverse side was designed by <a href="/wiki/Adolph_Alexander_Weinman" title="Adolph Alexander Weinman">Adolph Alexander Weinman</a> which is the same design as used on the reverse of the <a href="/wiki/American_Campaign_Medal" title="American Campaign Medal">American Campaign Medal</a> and <a href="/wiki/European-African-Middle_Eastern_Campaign_Medal" class="mw-redirect" title="European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal">European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal</a>. 
</p><p>There were 21 Army and 48 Navy-Marine Corps official campaigns of the Pacific Theater, denoted on the suspension and <a href="/wiki/Service_ribbon" title="Service ribbon">service ribbon</a> of the medal by <a href="/wiki/Service_star" title="Service star">service stars</a> which also were called "battle stars"; some <a href="/wiki/Seabee_(US_Navy)" class="mw-redirect" title="Seabee (US Navy)">Navy construction battalion</a> units issued the medal with <a href="/wiki/Arabic_numeral" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic numeral">Arabic numerals</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/Arrowhead_device" title="Arrowhead device">Arrowhead device</a> is authorized for those campaigns which involved participation in amphibious assault landings. The <a href="/wiki/Fleet_Marine_Force_Combat_Operation_Insignia" title="Fleet Marine Force Combat Operation Insignia">Fleet Marine Force Combat Operation Insignia</a> is also authorized for wear on the medal for Navy service members who participated in combat while assigned to a Marine Corps unit. The flag colors of the United States and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> are visible in the ribbon.
</p><p>The Asiatic–Pacific Campaign Medal was first issued as a service ribbon in 1942. A full medal was authorized in 1947, the first of which was presented to <a href="/wiki/General_of_the_Army_(United_States)" title="General of the Army (United States)">General of the Army</a> <a href="/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur" title="Douglas MacArthur">Douglas MacArthur</a>.  The <a href="/wiki/European_Theater" class="mw-redirect" title="European Theater">European Theater</a> equivalent of the medal was known as the European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal.
</p><p>Boundaries of Asiatic-Pacific Theater.
(1) The eastern boundary is coincident with the western boundary of the <a href="/wiki/American_Theater_of_World_War_II" class="mw-redirect" title="American Theater of World War II">American Theater</a>.
(2) The western boundary is from the North Pole south along the 60th meridian east longitude to its intersection with the east boundary of Iran, then south along the Iran boundary to the Gulf of Oman and the intersection of the 60th meridian east longitude, then south along the 60th meridian east longitude to the South Pole.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_campaigns">Other campaigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Asiatic%E2%80%93Pacific_Campaign_Medal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Other campaigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For members of the U.S. military who did not receive campaign credit, but still served on active duty in the Pacific Theater, the following “blanket” campaigns are authorized for which the Asiatic-Pacific Campaign Medal is awarded without service stars.
</p>
<ul><li>Antisubmarine December 7, 1941 – September 2, 1945</li>
<li>Ground Combat: December 7, 1941 – September 2, 1945</li>
<li>Air Combat: December 7, 1941 – September 2, 1945</li></ul>
