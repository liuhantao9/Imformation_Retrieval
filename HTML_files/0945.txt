<p><b>Aldreth</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Hamlet_(place)" title="Hamlet (place)">hamlet</a> in Cambridgeshire with about 260 residents (2001 census). It is located near the larger village of <a href="/wiki/Haddenham,_Cambridgeshire" title="Haddenham, Cambridgeshire">Haddenham</a> (where the population is listed) and falls under the same <a href="/wiki/Parish_councils_in_England" title="Parish councils in England">Parish council</a>. Aldreth is surrounded by fenland on all sides and is close to the <a href="/wiki/River_Great_Ouse" title="River Great Ouse">River Great Ouse</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aldreth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toponymy">Toponymy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aldreth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Toponymy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The name "Aldreth" occurs as <i>Alreheða</i> in the 1170 <a href="/wiki/Pipe_rolls" title="Pipe rolls">Pipe rolls</a>, and means "landing-place by the <a href="/wiki/Alder" title="Alder">alders</a>", from a combination of the <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> words for "alder" and "<a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/hythe" class="extiw" title="wikt:hythe">hythe</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The name also occurs a number of times in the text of the 12th century <i><a href="/wiki/Liber_Eliensis" title="Liber Eliensis">Liber Eliensis</a></i>, as <i>Alreheðe</i>, with one variant as <i>Alhereðe</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Battles">Battles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aldreth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Battles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hereward_the_Wake" title="Hereward the Wake">Hereward the Wake</a></div>
<p>Aldreth may have been the site of two battles<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  between <a href="/wiki/Hereward_the_Wake" title="Hereward the Wake">Hereward the Wake</a> (<a href="/wiki/Anglo-Saxons" title="Anglo-Saxons">Anglo-Saxons</a>) and <a href="/wiki/William_the_Conqueror" title="William the Conqueror">William the Conqueror</a> (<a href="/wiki/Normans" title="Normans">Normans</a>). Aldreth was one of three routes, or <a href="/wiki/Causeway" title="Causeway">causeways</a>, into the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Ely" title="Isle of Ely">Isle of Ely</a> at that time; <a href="/wiki/Stuntney" title="Stuntney">Stuntney</a> Causeway 2.25 miles (3.62&#160;km) to the south-east, the <a href="/wiki/Earith" title="Earith">Earith</a> Causeway 10 miles (16&#160;km) to the west-south-west and the Aldreth Causeway 7 miles (11&#160;km) south-west of the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Ely" title="Isle of Ely">Isle of Ely</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology">Geology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aldreth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Geology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The village is on an east-west running <a href="/wiki/Boulder_clay" title="Boulder clay">boulder clay</a> (middle-<a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a> <a href="/wiki/Till" title="Till">till</a>) <a href="/wiki/Ridge" title="Ridge">ridge</a> sitting on a belt of mainly <a href="/wiki/Jurassic" title="Jurassic">Jurassic</a> <a href="/wiki/Kimmeridge_Clay" title="Kimmeridge Clay">Kimmerigian</a> <a href="/wiki/Clay" title="Clay">clays</a> running south-west from <a href="/wiki/The_Wash" title="The Wash">The Wash</a>.  To the east is a north-south running belt of geologically more recent Upper-<a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a> <a href="/wiki/Lower_Greensand" class="mw-redirect" title="Lower Greensand">Lower Greensand</a> capped by Lower-Cretaceous <a href="/wiki/Gault_Clay" class="mw-redirect" title="Gault Clay">Gault Clay</a>; the whole area is surrounded by even more recent <a href="/wiki/Fen" title="Fen">fen</a> deposits. To the west, again running north-east—south-west, is a <a href="/wiki/Fault_scarp" title="Fault scarp">scarp</a> belt of middle-Jurassic <a href="/wiki/Sedimentary_rock" title="Sedimentary rock">sedimentary rocks</a> including <a href="/wiki/Limestone" title="Limestone">limestone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">sandstone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The flat fenland countryside around the village, typical for this part of the region, lies about 16 feet (5&#160;m) above sea-level. The highest point in the village is 23 feet (7&#160;m) above sea-level and the highest point in the area is 85 feet (26&#160;m) at Ely, seven-mile (11&#160;km) north-east.<sup id="cite_ref-boundary_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boundary-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In contrast, the highest point in Cambridgeshire, 479 feet (146&#160;m) above sea-level, is at <a href="/wiki/Great_Chishill" title="Great Chishill">Great Chishill</a>, 21 miles (34&#160;km) almost due south. <a href="/wiki/Holme,_Cambridgeshire" title="Holme, Cambridgeshire">Holme</a> at nine&#160;feet (2.75&#160;m) below sea-level is East Cambridgeshire's (and the United Kingdom's) lowest point, and is 18 miles (29&#160;km) north-west.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Community">Community</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Aldreth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Community">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Aldreth shares an annual village open day, Blossoms &amp; Bygones, with neighbouring village Haddenham. The event, which includes tractor rides, vintage car and tractor displays, and open gardens, held its 40th Anniversary in 2011 with a VE Day theme with villagers dressed in 1940s costume.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
