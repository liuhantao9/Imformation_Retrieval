<p>The <b>UNASUR Constitutive Treaty</b>, officially the <b>Constitutive Treaty of the Union of South American Nations</b>, was signed on May 23, 2008 during the extraordinary summit of heads of state and government of the Union of South American Nations (UNASUR) held in <a href="/wiki/Bras%C3%ADlia" title="Brasília">Brasília</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It officially established the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_South_American_Nations" title="Union of South American Nations">Union of South American Nations</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Intergovernmental_organization" title="Intergovernmental organization">intergovernmental</a> <a href="/wiki/Continental_union" title="Continental union">continental union</a> of twelve <a href="/wiki/South_American" class="mw-redirect" title="South American">South American</a> nations.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ratification">Ratification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UNASUR_Constitutive_Treaty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ratification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Constitutive Treaty of the Union of South American Nations came <a href="/wiki/Force_(law)" title="Force (law)">into force</a> on March 11, 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> thirty days after the date of receipt<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> of the ninth instrument of <a href="/wiki/Ratification" title="Ratification">ratification</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Treaty_content">Treaty content</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UNASUR_Constitutive_Treaty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Treaty content">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The treaty consists of 27 relatively short sections, and is the constitution of the new union. It outlines its structure and organs, and assumes that additional documents fill in the details.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UNASUR_Constitutive_Treaty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The treaty declares the establishment and objects of the Union (in §§&#160;1-3), its organs (§§&#160;4-10 and 17), juridical foundation (§§&#160;11-13 and 22-27), and financial foundation (§&#160;16). It regulates the acceptance of new associate or full members and the right of cessation from the union in §§&#160;19, 20, 24, and 26, and the rules for adopting amendments to the treaty in §&#160;25. Finally, §§&#160;14, 15, 18, and 21 declare the intent to employ dialogue among the member states, with its citizens, with third parties, and as the means for conflict resolution.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Membership">Membership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UNASUR_Constitutive_Treaty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Membership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 12 original signatories of the document have presented documentation of ratification to the Ecuadorian government. Other <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Latin American</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> states may be admitted as associated members. An associated member may apply for and be granted full membership, but only after having been associated for at least four years, and only after five years have elapsed since the treaty came into force. This implies that no new full members outside the original twelve can be admitted before March 11, 2016.
</p><p>A full or associated member state may unilaterally withdraw from the union. To do so, the state must deposit their cessation documentation in a similar manner as the ratification, and the cessation will take effect six months after the deposition.  However, cessation of membership will not free states from any financial debts to the Union for unpaid membership fees or otherwise.
</p><p>At the time the treaty came into force on March 11, 2011, it had been ratified by <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guyana" title="Guyana">Guyana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuela</a>. The two other original signatories, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a>, had not yet completed the ratification process by that date.  By December 14, 2011, when Colombia deposited its instruments of ratification of the Constitutive Treaty with the Government of the Republic of Ecuador, the process was complete.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Amendments">Amendments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UNASUR_Constitutive_Treaty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Amendments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Any member state may suggest amendments to the constitutional treaty. In order to be adopted, an amendment must be approved by the Council of Heads of State and Government, and then ratified by at least nine member states.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Additional_Protocol">Additional Protocol</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UNASUR_Constitutive_Treaty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Additional Protocol">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table role="presentation" class="mbox-small plainlinks sistersitebox" style="background-color:#f9f9f9;border:1px solid #aaa;color:#000">
<tbody><tr>
<td class="mbox-image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Wikisource-logo.svg/38px-Wikisource-logo.svg.png" decoding="async" width="38" height="40" class="noviewer" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Wikisource-logo.svg/57px-Wikisource-logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Wikisource-logo.svg/76px-Wikisource-logo.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="410" data-file-height="430" /></td>
<td class="mbox-text plainlist"><a href="/wiki/Wikisource" title="Wikisource">Wikisource</a> has original text related to this article:
<div style="margin-left: 10px;"><b><a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Additional_Protocol_to_the_Constitutive_Treaty_of_the_Union_of_South_American_Nations_on_Commitment_to_Democracy" class="extiw" title="wikisource:Additional Protocol to the Constitutive Treaty of the Union of South American Nations on Commitment to Democracy">Additional Protocol to the Constitutive Treaty of the Union of South American Nations on Commitment to Democracy</a></b></div></td></tr>

<p>On November 26, 2010, during the <a href="/wiki/2010_South_American_Summit" title="2010 South American Summit">2010 South American Summit</a>, representatives introduced a democratic clause to the Constitutive Treaty of the Union of South American Nations. The <a href="/wiki/Amend_(motion)" title="Amend (motion)">amendment</a> specifies measures to be taken against member-states whose political processes are not respected. The clause establishes sanctions, such as shutting down borders and the suspension of trade against the country that suffers an attempted coup.
</p><p>The decision to include a democratic clause was made after the recent <a href="/wiki/2010_Ecuador_crisis" title="2010 Ecuador crisis">upheaval in Ecuador</a> that briefly threatened the administration of President, <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Correa" title="Rafael Correa">Rafael Correa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The additional protocol was signed by all member-states of UNASUR.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Entering_into_force">Entering into force</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=UNASUR_Constitutive_Treaty&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Entering into force">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On March 11, 2011, when the Constitutive Treaty entered into legal force, establishing the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_South_American_Nations" title="Union of South American Nations">Union of South American Nations</a> as an international <a href="/wiki/Legal_personality" class="mw-redirect" title="Legal personality">legal personality</a>, the Foreign Ministers of the <a href="/wiki/Member_State_of_the_Union_of_South_American_Nations" class="mw-redirect" title="Member State of the Union of South American Nations">UNASUR member states</a> met at <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_Mitad_del_Mundo" title="Ciudad Mitad del Mundo">Ciudad Mitad del Mundo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecuador" title="Ecuador">Ecuador</a>, to celebrate the event and lay the foundation stone of the UNASUR Secretariat headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
