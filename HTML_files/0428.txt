	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">World Religion Day</h1>

<p><b>World Religion Day</b> is an observance initiated in 1950 by the National <a href="/wiki/Spiritual_Assembly" title="Spiritual Assembly">Spiritual Assembly</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith_in_the_United_States" title="Bahá&#39;í Faith in the United States">Bahá'ís of the United States</a>, celebrated worldwide on the third Sunday in January each year. Though initiated in the United States, World Religion Day has come to be celebrated internationally.<sup id="cite_ref-buck2011_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buck2011-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Described as a "<a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahá&#39;í">Bahá'í</a>-inspired idea that has taken on a life of its own",<sup id="cite_ref-rotw_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rotw-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> the origins of World Religion Day lie in the Bahá'í principles of the <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith_and_the_unity_of_religion" title="Bahá&#39;í Faith and the unity of religion">oneness of religion</a> and of <a href="/wiki/Progressive_revelation_(Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD)" title="Progressive revelation (Bahá&#39;í)">progressive revelation</a>, which describe religion as evolving continuously throughout the history of humanity.<sup id="cite_ref-oneworld_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oneworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-interfaith_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-interfaith-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The purpose of World Religion Day is to highlight the ideas that the spiritual principles underlying the world's religions are harmonious, and that religions play a significant role in unifying humanity.<sup id="cite_ref-buck2011_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buck2011-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rotw_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rotw-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose">Purpose</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Religion_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Purpose">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Initially a Bahá'í observance, World Religion Day was inspired by the Bahá'í principles of the <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith_and_the_unity_of_religion" title="Bahá&#39;í Faith and the unity of religion">oneness of religion</a> and of <a href="/wiki/Progressive_revelation_(Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD)" title="Progressive revelation (Bahá&#39;í)">progressive revelation</a>, which describe religion as evolving continuously throughout the history of humanity.<sup id="cite_ref-oneworld_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oneworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-interfaith_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-interfaith-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It promotes these principles by highlighting the ideas that the spiritual principles underlying the world's religions are harmonious, and that religions play a significant role in unifying humanity.<sup id="cite_ref-buck2011_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buck2011-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rotw_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rotw-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a means of clarifying the nature and purpose of World Religion Day, the <a href="/wiki/Universal_House_of_Justice" title="Universal House of Justice">Universal House of Justice</a>, the elected council that serves as the head of the Bahá'í Faith noted in a 1968 message that, rather than providing a "platform for all religions and their emergent <a href="/wiki/Ecumenism" title="Ecumenism">ecumenical</a> ideas," the observance serves as "a celebration of the need for and the coming of a world religion for mankind, the <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith" title="Bahá&#39;í Faith">Bahá'í Faith</a> itself."<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2002, the Universal House of Justice published a letter, "To the World's Religious Leaders", in which it stated:
</p>
<blockquote><p>...interfaith discourse, if it is to contribute meaningfully to healing the ills that afflict a desperate humanity, must now address honestly ... the implications of the over-arching truth ... that God is one and that, beyond all diversity of cultural expression and human interpretation, religion is likewise one.<sup id="cite_ref-Buck_2011_937_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Buck_2011_937-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>World Religion Day has been described as a "<a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahá&#39;í">Bahá'í</a>-inspired idea that has taken on a life of its own",<sup id="cite_ref-rotw_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rotw-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> because its observance is no longer confined to the Bahá'í community, where it originally took shape. Although observances of World Religion Day are still sponsored and supported by Bahá'í communities worldwide, Baha'i institutions such as the Universal House of Justice or the National Spiritual Assembly of the Bahá'ís of the United States no longer play active roles in the promotion of events, apart from reporting on them. Instead, an increasing number of observances are independently organized by interfaith or multi-faith coalitions.<sup id="cite_ref-buck2011_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buck2011-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rotw_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rotw-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Religion_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The earliest observation entitled "World Peace Through World Religion" was in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Maine" title="Portland, Maine">Portland, Maine</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Eastland_Park_Hotel" class="mw-redirect" title="Eastland Park Hotel">Eastland Park Hotel</a> in October 1947 with a talk by <a href="/wiki/Firuz_Kazemzadeh" title="Firuz Kazemzadeh">Firuz Kazemzadeh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 1949 observances in various communities in the United States made the local newspapers in December called "World Religion Day".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It was standardized across the United States by the National Spiritual Assembly of the Bahá'ís of the United States in December 1949 to be held January 15, 1950.<sup id="cite_ref-Buck_2011_937_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Buck_2011_937-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It also began to be observed internationally starting as early as in <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith_in_Australia" title="Bahá&#39;í Faith in Australia">Australia</a> in 1950 in two cities<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith_in_Bolivia" title="Bahá&#39;í Faith in Bolivia">Bolivia</a> in 1951.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> By 1958 Bahá'ís had gathered notices of events in a number of countries—sometimes attracting hundreds of people and sometimes overlapping with race amity priorities.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith_in_Laos" title="Bahá&#39;í Faith in Laos">Laos</a>, for example, meetings were noted in 1958,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> 1959,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and 1960, among many countries activities.<sup id="cite_ref-mins_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mins-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith_in_the_Netherlands" title="Bahá&#39;í Faith in the Netherlands">the Netherlands</a> in 1962 it was noted in several cities.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="More_pronounced_awareness">More pronounced awareness</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Religion_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: More pronounced awareness">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The observance has grown in some scale of recognition beginning in the 1950s.
</p><p>It was noted on various <a href="/wiki/AM_radio" class="mw-redirect" title="AM radio">AM radio</a> stations in the 1950s and 1960s:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/KNBR" title="KNBR">(formerly) KVSM</a> (1951, San Francisco Bay Area)<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>

</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Stamps">Stamps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Religion_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Stamps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1985 <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a> issued the first World Religion Day <a href="/wiki/Postage_stamp" title="Postage stamp">postage stamp</a>. This was followed by a stamp issued by the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_the_Congo" title="Republic of the Congo">Republic of the Congo</a> in 2007. The Congo stamp showed a globe with the symbols of 11 religions surrounding it, and the text (in French) read, "God is the source of all religions."<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_observance">Modern observance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=World_Religion_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Modern observance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There is a long tradition of hosting panels and symposia with representatives of many religions at World Religion Day observances.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After years of activity since 2000<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>1:02min</span></sup> in 2011 Ottawa city government hosted an event that was video taped.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> It was subsequently noted in 2014 on <a href="/wiki/CTV_Television_Network" title="CTV Television Network">CTV Television Network</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Since 2013 participants have gathered at a virtual presentation in <a href="/wiki/Second_Life" title="Second Life">Second Life</a><sup id="cite_ref-SL2015_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SL2015-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:<span>1:44min</span></sup> at the UUtopia Center for an observance.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> The 2014 observance had screenshots taken.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> The 2015 event<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> of talks of a panel of speakers was recorded.<sup id="cite_ref-SL2015_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SL2015-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013 the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Religions" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliament of Religions">Parliament of Religions</a> noted it.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Press" title="Oxford University Press">Oxford University Press</a>' blog noted it in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
